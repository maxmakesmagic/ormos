A Different Reanimator
======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 26, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Junk Reanimator has become the dominant deck in Standard, and it's well known enough that I wouldn't write about it, so I've become used to seeing [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and skimming past to the next deck. Matsumoto Yuuki has taken his deck in a different direction, though. 


![Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Unburial+Rites)
 His use of a red splash for [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), which he can return with [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise), may look like a throwback to the old Human Reanimator decks like the deck we saw Tzu-Ching Kuo take to the finals of [Grand Prix Quebec City](/en/events/coverage/turn-three-take-fourteen-christiansen-mauls-quebec-city) earlier this year. However, unlike previous [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) decks, Matsumoto has no infinite combo; he's just putting more creatures into play and passing the turn. 


 Why would he do that? Well, he doesn't have to play any weaker support cards that just enable the combo (I'm not going to sit here and tell you that [Cathedral Sanctifier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Sanctifier) isn't a weak support card, but it still doesn't take the full dedication the combo does, and if the benchmark is [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer), this really isn't much worse). 


 Matsumoto's deck essentially looks like the deck that Human Reanimator sides into, bringing [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), and [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) to play a fair game. 


 This leaves Matsumoto with space to actually have a sideboard with removal, even more diverse threats, and [Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) (to accompany [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime)) to beat other [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) or control players.
