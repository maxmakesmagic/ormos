Modern Black-Green Death Cloud
==============================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on December 7, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Today we'll be featuring a Modern deck built around one of the most punishing cards recently printed. [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) has a pretty brutal effect on the game. It forces all players (including you) to sacrifice lands and creatures, discard cards, and lose life. However, the caster of the [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) has a lot of power in what the card actually does. Today's list was piloted by Osmanozguney to a 5-0 finish in the last *Magic Online* Modern League.


One of the reasons why [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) is not very popular in competitive tournaments is that it's a very symmetrical effect. When most symmetrical effects are cast, the caster is the one who loses out on the deal, as they had to use a card in their hand to play the spell in the first place. When building [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) decks, you have to consider how to make the [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) have as little symmetry as possible so that you, the caster of the [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud), come out ahead every time.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Death+Cloud)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud)
The best way to do this is to play permanents that you don't have to sacrifice to your own [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud). Obviously planeswalkers will do the trick here, but another important card that accomplishes this goal in this list is [Utopia Sprawl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Sprawl). Many players think of [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) or [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) when deciding what mana ramp to play in their decks, but [Utopia Sprawl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Sprawl) is a spell that accelerates you at the same rate.


Another way to maximize the value received from [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) is to play creatures or spells that give you card advantage. Most [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) decks that I've seen have played [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime). This is a great way of stripping your opponent's hand at a very low cost. However, this [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) deck is doing things a little differently. Most of the creatures gain you an advantage in some way, such as [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) or [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks). You don't mind losing these creatures to [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud), as they have already done their jobs.


Overall, the [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) strategy is very resilient and plays some of the strongest cards in Modern. I'm glad it achieved a 5-0 finish in the League and I look forward to seeing more of it.
