Team Round 2: The Netherlands vs. Germany
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 16, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TobyWachter.jpg)
By Toby Wachter











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a639.jpg)Without a doubt, this is the most important match of the day. The Dutch and German teams were the favorites coming into Worlds, and so far that prediction has come true. After the first round today, Germany is in first and the Dutch are in second. This match will not only put one team further ahead, but will put the other favored team further behind, so it's twice as important. Regardless of the outcome, at least one of these teams is likely to play Sunday, and if that is the case, the Team World Championship may leave North America for the first time and come to Europe.

Budde's [Skywing Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) and [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) came out fast, but Kai missed his fourth land drop. Boeken had [Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valor) to hold off some of the assault, but Kai used [Rites of Spring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Spring) to discard [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and fix his mana situation. Both players kept adding to the board, keeping it even. Boeken had [Balshan Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balshan+Griffin), and Budde summoned [Dreamwinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamwinder). Two copies of [Lost in Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+Thought) slowed down Budde's game a bit, and [Ghostly Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Wings) on [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) didn't happen thanks to a [Sonic Seizure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sonic+Seizure) that discarded [Prismatic Strands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Strands). Boeken now had the board slightly to his advantage thanks to [Aven Windreader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader), but it died after Budde dug for [Toxic Stench](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toxic+Stench) with the help of [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough). Budde then played [Arcane Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Teachings) on his [Gloomdrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gloomdrifter) to achieve board advantage, and cast [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). Boeken was forced to break it, and Budde's board advantage combined with an Ancestral Recall proved to be too much.

Schneiders' [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad) matched Cornelissen's [Krosan Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Avenger), which soon became big thanks to [Seton's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seton%27s+Desire). Schneiders' Looter went to work, and there wasn't much Cornelissen's blue/green deck could do about it. To make matters worse, his [Cephalid Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Aristocrat) and 5/3 Avenger were being held off by [Pardic Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pardic+Lancer) while Schneiders' Looter provided more of an advantage with each passing turn. Once [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) hit the graveyard, things went from bad to horrible. From that position, it was easy for Schneiders to take the first game.

Van de Logt's [Treacherous Werewolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treacherous+Werewolf) and [Gravegouger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravegouger) seemed threatening until Ziegner brought out [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast) and [Barbarian Bully](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Bully). The race was on, and Van de Logt got ahead thanks to [Vivify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivify) making a land attack along with two 2/2s. [Crippling Fatigue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue) then killed Ziegner's [Barbarian Bully](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Bully), leaving his [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast) useless. Unfortunately for the World Champion, Ziegner had [Violent Eruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Eruption), and wiped out both of Van de Logt's creatures. The champ came back with [Centaur Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Veteran), but Ziegner had [Dirty Wererat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dirty+Wererat) and a graveyard full of options such as [Morgue Theft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morgue+Theft), [Crippling Fatigue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue) and [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart). A few turns later, and it was off to Game 2. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a638.jpg)So far, the Germans were up 3-0 in games.

Schneiders had [Battle Screech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Screech) in Game 2, but it was less impressive thanks to Cornelissen's [Psionic Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Gift)ed Squirrel. [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger) took care of that problem before it killed off too many birds, but Cornelissen had [Scalpelexis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis) to make things somewhat scary. Still, the Dutchman was at seven life points, and Schneiders had a full, aggressive board. [Scalpelexis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis) tried to outrace the German, but a threshold [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger) took the first match for Germany.

Ziegner had a strong start Game 2 with second turn Boneyard Slasher, third turn [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast). Van de Logt played [Dusk Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Imp), and it traded with the Slasher. Ziegner then played [Whispering Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade) to help his [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast) keep attacking, and it would have been dominating if not for Van de Logt's [Crippling Fatigue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue). Regardless, Ziegner had [Pardic Firecat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pardic+Firecat) and kept the pressure on. Van de Logt tried to slow things down with [Twigwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twigwalker) but Ziegner had [Toxic Stench](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toxic+Stench), Just when things started to look bad, Van de Logt turned the game around with [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher). Ziegner went to the empty air with [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge), so Van de Logt flashed back [Crippling Fatigue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue) to kill it. [Centaur Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Veteran) improved Van de Logt's side as he started to stabilize, but [Pyromania](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromania) dealt the final four points of damage to finish off the job in time and take the win for the Germans.

Budde's opening draw for Game 2 had all four colors of mana he needed, thanks in part to [Mossfire Valley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mossfire+Valley). Boeken wasn't looking back either, thanks to a pair of [Skywing Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven)s. [Rites of Spring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Spring) and [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) got Budde to threshold very easily, which made his [Anurid Barkripper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Barkripper) and [Krosan Restorer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Restorer) much better. Adding to Budde's offense was a [Wormfang Turtle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Turtle) with [Ghostly Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Wings). [Bomb Squad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomb+Squad) made things even better, but Boeken had [Churning Eddy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Churning+Eddy) to bounce back the Turtle that had been holding back his flying army. This allowed him to come through the air, and head to a third game that would count for tiebreakers.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a637.jpg)Budde's [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) traded with Boeken's [Mystic Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Familiar) when Kai discarded two cards, which puzzled Boeken until Budde played [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). Boeken was forced to break it with [Cephalid Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Looter), and Budde played [Rites of Spring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Spring) for three, followed by [Ghostly Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Wings) on [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba). Boeken came back with [Resilient Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resilient+Wanderer), and Budde dropped [Bomb Squad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomb+Squad). [Skywing Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) made Boeken's side impressive, but if he didn't find a way to deal with [Bomb Squad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomb+Squad) soon he was going to lose. [Mirror Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Wall) was countered with [Rites of Refusal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Refusal), giving Budde threshold and making his Barkripper 4/4. The resulting attack saw Budde discard a card to [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) to deal eight damage total, and [Bomb Squad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomb+Squad) finished off the Looter to deal another four. From there it was just a matter of time, and it was a 3-0 sweep for Germany.
