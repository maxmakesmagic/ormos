German National Championship 2006
---------------------------------


Ninjas Invade Germany!
======================




August 11-12, 2006











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gernat06/champ.jpg)The German Nationals 2006 are over, and just like last year, it's Ninjas at the top! Maximilian Bracht, Germany's rising star, has taken top honors. His new Erayo-Ninja deck proved to be strong, although Bracht claims that his worst matchup is Solar Flare - the most played deck at this Nationals, and that he had good luck in the Swiss. Joining him on the National team are Frenchman Amiel Tenenbaum and Jörg Unfried, who defeated Tobias Henke in the playoff for third place. They made their way through a very strong top 8, which also featured Holger Althues, who made his third Nationals top 8 at this event.


The whole event showed off German Magic at its best. 158 Nationals competitors, 137 Legacy competitors, close to 40 JSS competitors and the best the community has to offer made the whole weekend a great one. The players party on Saturday night offered respite from the Magical cards, but like players all over the world, even then some Magic enthusiasts couldn't keep away from their decks to enjoy bowling and pool billiard!


I hope you enjoyed the coverage here and over at [PlanetMTG.de](http://www.planetmtg.de/events/coverage.html?id=19) . Now that the tournament is over, I can finally go celebrate my birthday! You can return to magicthegathering.com for more coverage of premier tournaments around the world next weekend for GP Hiroshima and the week after that for Japanese Nationals. So long and auf Wiedersehen from Aschaffenburg!
