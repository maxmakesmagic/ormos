=============================



 Posted in **Top Decks**

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











*A not-exhaustive look at two formats.*

Here at Top Decks, we spend a lot of time talking about the Standard format. Heck, last week we talked about circa *sixteen* Standard formats (or at least some of their bad guys and bogeymen)!

In light of this weekend's upcoming [World **Magic** Cup](/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup)—which in addition to Standard embraces the formats of Modern and *Innistrad*-*Avacyn Restored* Block Constructed—I thought we'd touch on some of those other formats for a little World **Magic** Cup sneak preview action.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td208_restoration_angel.jpg)[Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) | Art by [Johannes Voss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Johannes+Voss%22%5D)

Modern was—and is—a big idea and a reasonably big format; broader than Extended, but pruned ahead of time to defend the fun factor. No [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor); no [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic). Well, Estratti's Exarch Twin, too, has been similarly de-toothed since the Philadelphia win. Now gone are his one-drop consistency-makers, [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)... setting up [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) + [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) or [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) + [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) (either combo would give Estratti an engine for essentially limitless power by untapping either a [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin)-wearing blue guy or the machine gun legendary Goblin) has become a little less effortless.

The Modern hybrid [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) strategy is unambiguously potent; playing against it is reminiscent of fighting LSV-era full-on Elves Combo with [Glimpse of Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature). The deck is a creature deck. It can go [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) into second-turn [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and beat you up (certainly, other builds actually have the full four [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Or it can go [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and then chain up... [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) (draw a card), [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) (leave a body), [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) (reset, etc.).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |
|  |  |

...but like Estratti's deck, this one can win (typically wins?) on an unlimited combo. Four copies of [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker); four copies of [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel); a [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) for good measure. You can aim [Kiki-Jiki](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki) at [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), blink the Legendary Goblin, do it again... over and over... literally *ad infinitum*. Send as many 3/4 creatures as you like.

To say that Modern is a "diverse" format might be a bit of an understatement. It has the twin assets of being huge and not-particularly-developed (no StarCityGames Open Series grinding it to crystal distillation, weekly and on camera). In addition to two-card infinite combo decks and the [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)/creature-based take on the same, it is a format full of Blue-Red Urzatron decks, White-Blue Urzatron decks, Red-Green Urzatron decks... and regular-old "good stuff" decks.

This All-American beatdown deck in Modern is basically a Standard Delver deck... but with awesome burn spells like [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)! This deck is the—*ahem*—"modern" realization of the "critical mass of great beatdown creatures" and "critical mass of great burn spells" we have seen in select formats passed. [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is an all-star in Legacy as well as Standard, so acquits itself swimmingly in Modern. Joined at the one by high-reward fast attacker [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), this deck can hammer an opponent with [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). It can also circumvent the red zone with its high-value red spells [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)... and do it all over again with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage).

The above is a finals deck from this past May, and might be of particular interest as Siow is one of the World **Magic** Cup participants (for Canada). This deck in particular is a different take on the red-white-blue concept, with [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) as the undercosted heavy-hitter, and a bit of a more mana-intensive mid- and late-game.

Lucas played with recurring threat-answer [Vedalken Shackles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) (not particularly mana intensive, but requiring a fair number of [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s in play to do much going long) and [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) (which costs four mana).

|  |  |

Both Lucas's Delver deck and the All-American beatdown play cards like [Serum Visions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) where Standard aficionados might expect a [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)... not just to help dig for lands early (and regulate draws overall)... but to help flip up [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) where a moment ago we had the regular old [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). These decks explode in three different directions on a Modern battlefield: They can play the aggressor, close like burn decks (or push supposed blockers out of the way), or slow down/disrupt/out-race combo decks with their light suite of counterspells.

Both [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) and [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) offer opportunities to draw a card on the opponent's turn, setting up more miracles opportunities. At least in Hayne's build, [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) can be used in combination with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to set up and re-buy key spells. Remember: You don't *have* to [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) yourself! You can [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) the opponent if you think your top cards are so valuable... or if the opponent gives you reason to do other (say he or she reveals a game-breaking [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) with a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) on upkeep), you can go ahead and ruin that plan.

This is a deck of heavy fists. [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) does work, but not as much damage as a hasty [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) or [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts). Conscripts, by the by, doesn't just hit for 3, doesn't just steal the opponent's best creature... it can also steal an ultimate (to be) Planeswalker a turn before it is about to go off.

Kibler's deck has all manner of bombs. In addition to his heavy-fisted creatures, Brian played [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) as a small-creature dominator as well as [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned). Because, hey... when you one-way [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) the opponent, you also want to clear off that pesky life total.
