Ding, Dong, Jund Is Dead In Kuala Lumpur
========================================




March 13-14, 2010












Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur 2010
----------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl10/end_blurb.jpg)


With six Jund decks in the top 8, you’d have thought Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur was all but cut and dried. But there was one thing they hadn’t counted on, Ding Leong. Like some B-grade 80’s revenge flick, Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur 2002 Champion Ding Leong utterly annihilated three Jund opponents in a row, proving that even the ‘best’ deck has its Achilles Heel. If a bunch of Jund decklists isn’t your thing, take a look at the rest of the day two field, the metagame is much more wide open than this top 8 will have you believe. Congratulations to Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur Champion Ding Leong! Tune in next week as the Grand Prix circuit continues on to Yokohama for some hot, hot Extended action.

Sunday, March 15: 4:56 p.m. – Quarterfinal: Shouta Yasooka vs Shingo Fukuta
---------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

Shingo Fukuta won the roll and lead with a [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) , a card important enough in the mirror that Shouta Yasooka had one of his own. The Thrinax’s traded, and Fukuta took [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) and [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) from Yasooka’s hand with a [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) , but missed a land drop. Saprolings traded, and Yasooka summoned a second Thrinax. Another [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) from Fukuta nabbed [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) , while Yasooka attacked back with his Thrinax. Fukuta then won the lottery, with a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) hitting a third [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) . Yasooka frowned before [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) ing his own Thrinax, finding another one, and binned a [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) . Yasooka attacked, the Elf trading with the Thrinax, both players now on 10 life. Fukuta cleared the Saprolings from the battlefield with a [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) , and Yasooka struck back with a [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) of his own. Fukuta slammed a [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander) off the top, trading two Goblins for Yasooka’s incoming [Lavaclaw Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches) . Yasooka summoned a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) , but could only snort as Fukuta killed it with a [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) , hitting a [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) . Yasooka thought about it for around half a second, and scooped up his cards.


Fukuta 1 – Yasooka 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl10/QF1_YF.jpg)Yasooka kept his hand Game 2, but Fukuta threw his back. The first play of the game was once again a [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) , but this time from Yasooka. Fukuta could only play a tapped [Dragonskull Summit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonskull+Summit) on his third turn, and watch in horror as Yasooka cascaded into a [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) with [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) . Fukuta discarded another Summit and a [Goblin Ruinblaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ruinblaster) , [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) ing the Elf, and then discarding a [Verdant Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) to Yasooka’s brand new [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess) . A turn later with no answer to the Planeswalker, and it was Fukuta’s turn to pack it in.


Fukuta 1 – Yasooka 1


Third turn Thrinax again hit the battlefield for Fukuta, but Yasooka Bolt’d it, then mopped up the rest with a [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) . Fukuta naturally followed it up with another Thrinax, and then another, causing Yasooka to shake his head with a smile. Fukuta added a [Great Sable Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag) to his team, but it died to another Bolt, before Yasooka Summoned a [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) . The token died to a [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) , and Fukuta sent his Thrinaxes and a [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) in to battle the Dragon. A Ruinblaster from Yasooka took care of the Ravine, but Fukuta had another waiting and ready. [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) shot down the remaining Dragon, and cascaded up a Ruinblaster. Fukuta turned his team sideways, dropping Yasooka to 1. After the smoke had cleared Yasooka had no creatures, and Fukuta had six Saprolings. He looked Yasooka in the eye for a moment, and pushed them across the table at him. Yasooka chuckled and scooped up his cards.


Shingo Fukuta defeats Shouta Yasooka 2 – 1


 

Sunday, March 15: 4:56 p.m. – Quarterfinal: Raffy Sarto vs Xue Tong Du
----------------------------------------------------------------------


*by QJ Wong*

Du won the dice roll, chose to start and with a quick "Good luck" from Sarto, the players drew their opening hands and got the quarterfinals started.


A [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) and a [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) quickly took Du to 14 through a [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) while all Du had were tapped lands. An [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) joined Du’s side of the battlefield but was quickly removed by Sarto’s [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) which made way for Sarto’s creatures to take another 4 off Du, but gave Du a [Savage Lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands) in the process.
