Semifinals: Keith McLaughlin vs. Marco Blume
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 31, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_595.png)
By John Stephens











Despite all of his accomplishments in Magic, Marco has never won a Grand Prix. Today, he's two matches away from winning his first. It would also make him the first Non-North American to win a U.S. Grand Prix. He's playing his trusty Wake deck. Keith lives in Brandon, FL. He's been playing Magic since Mirage, but stopped during Urza's and started playing competitive Magic since *Torment* or so. This was the first time he ever was playing in the Top 8 of a major tournament. He's with the tournament's other heavy-hitter: Goblins.

### Game 1

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpatl03/a886.jpg)Keith opened up with a [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder), and the beatings were on. A turn two Piledriver and turn three [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) accelerated the beatings a little more. Turn three came and went, as did turn four. Marco looked dejected each time he looked down at the table. 

"I'm on two lands too," Keith said. "Don't worry about it."

Marco was stuck with an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) in play, nowhere near enough mana to make his Wake deck work efficiently. Keith on the other hand was good with just his two mountains on the table.

Marco used [Moment's Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace), then flashed it back a turn later, but all he was doing was delaying his impending death. Unable to [Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) into anything useful Marco packed up his cards and prepared for Game 2.

Marco Blume 0 - Keith McLaughlin 1 

Marco started off the second game with good mana. He was able to Leak a [Goblin Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief), gaining valuable tempo. Keith tried to climb back into the game, casting several goblins but never being allowed anything that might turn the game around. Each time that a Warchief came up Marco had the counter for it.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpatl03/a887.jpg)Marco also had brought in some of the ultimate anti-goblin tech: Brushhoppers. Once Marco played out his first one the game became doubtful for Keith. When Marco played out his second one, the game was over. 

He tried to burn one Brushhopper off the table, only to have Marco discard two cards to it. The second one he also tried to burn off the table, but Marco cast [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) on the [Violent Eruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Eruption).

Keith was able to laugh about it a little though, even remarking that it's good to make your play mistakes when they don't matter.

Marco Blume 1 â€“ Keith McLaughlin 1

### Game 2

Keith started quickly, getting a turn two Warchief onto the table, and then trying to add a turn three Piledriver. He got away with the Warchief, but by the time he tried the Piledriver Marco had enough land in play to cast [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) on it.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpatl03/a888.jpg)Nothing else went Keith's way the rest of the match. He was unable to find anything to help him, so when Marco cycled a [Decree of Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Justice) he was able to just laugh about it.

"That's my conciliation prize, right?"

Marco's tokens looked like they came from some German trading card company specializing in models.

Keith watched as Marco then placed an [Anurid Brushhopper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Brushhopper) next to the tokens and started swinging.

Two turns later Keith was done, this tournament was over him.

**Final Result:** Marco Blume 2 - Keith McLaughlin 1
