Sunday, 9:52 a.m. – Waiting for the Egg to Boil
-----------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

Second Breakfast was responsible for a lot of overtime yesterday. In tournaments like Grands Prix, the players get 5 additional turns to finish their match after time is called on a round. Typically, those 5 turns are over quickly. But if one of those turns is a huge Second Breakfast combo turn, it can take a while. It has happened on multiple occasions this weekend that 1000+ players were waiting for a single Second Breakfast player to break all of his Eggs and loop [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb) before the next round could start.


"A large part of the delays have been caused by Eggs," Head Judge Gijsbert Hoogendijk mentioned. "And some of the worst delays were caused by Eggs mirror matches, with one player trying to go off in response to the other."



![Second Sunrise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Second+Sunrise)

Gijsbert was referring to how an Egg player could piggyback off the [Second Sunrise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) of his opponent. While [Faith's Reward](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Reward) only refers to "your graveyard", [Second Sunrise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) affects both players equally. So if you have a bunch of Eggs in play and your opponent recklessly casts [Second Sunrise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) , you can sacrifice all of your artifacts and hope to draw more [Faith's Reward](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Reward) than your opponent.


This gives me headaches even thinking about it. You know, it would all become much easier if a [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) gets resolved at some point in the game. But then your opponent might try to go off in response to that [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) ... Maybe you should try to time the [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) in *response* to an opposing [Faith's Reward](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Reward) when the opponent doesn't have mana available? Argh, this is getting horribly complicated.


I just want to give one piece of advice to any player considering Eggs for a Modern tournament at some point in the future: Test the mirror match. The rest of the tournament will thank you for not having to figure out the interactions on the fly in the extra turns. And a player who knows what's going on in this particular mirror matchup will have a huge egg -- I mean edge.

Sunday, 10:10 a.m. – Yesterday's Undefeated
-------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Four players escaped yesterday's carnage with unblemished records. Nine wins, zero losses—considering the considerable competition, that's quite an accomplishment.


Kevin Chiche and Peter Dun both used [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) to create arbitrary numbers of hasty copies of [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) , [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) , or [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) , but their decks almost couldn't be more different. Chiche ran the combo as the killer in his red-green-white(-blue) [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck, whereas Dun had it more traditional in blue and red (and a tiny splash of black) with [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) . Meanwhile, Arnaud Duval and Mario Zuñiga both used aggressive beatdown strategies with an almost combo-like component for their respective 9-0s, Robots for Duval, Infect for Zuñiga. That's four different archetypes and no Jund ...


 






#### Kevin Chiche, 9-0

Round 10 Feature Match - Daniel Royde (Jund) vs. Timothée Simonot (Robots)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Englishman Daniel Royde already has one Grand Prix win under his belt and, sitting here at 8-1, was clearly looking for more Grand Prix glory. In his way, however, stood Frenchman Timothée Simonot, who wasn't willing to give up his own shot at glory easily.


### Game 1


Simonot, on the play, kept [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel) , [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) , [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) , [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) , [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) , [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) , and [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) , played the Citadel and lost the [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) to [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) . Turn two he made [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) and [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) , while Royde only had a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) .


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplyo12/fm10_simonot.jpg)
Simonot played a second [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) and cast [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) . Royde responded with [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) on [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) . In response, Simonot activated the other [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) , used [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) on his team, then [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) on his Overseer.


Another Bolt and [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) cleared the board anyway, except for the two [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) that nibbled away at Royde's life total. When those died later, [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) and [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) took over. Meanwhile, Royde couldn't for the life of him draw a creature of his own (and his life really did depend on it). He tried to make do with two [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) s, but the constant drain on his mana meant he couldn't both race and remove Simonot's creatures. He had to take care of some additional ones, quickly abandoned his racing ambitions, and in the end couldn't deal with all of them.


Daniel Royde 0-1 Timothée Simonot


### Game 2


This time, Simonot kept two [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel) s, [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) , [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) , and [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) —a five-card hand thanks to double mulligan. Royde's [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) made that four, getting rid of [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) .


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplyo12/fm10_royde.jpg)
A second [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Inquisition%2Bof%2BKozilek) missed, while Simonot played a second [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) , and now Royde had a creature himself, a sizeable 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) , that grew to 5/6 when [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) killed [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) . The race was on: [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) brought Simonot to 15, the two [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) gave Royde two poison counters; Simonot 10, Royde at four poison. At this rate, Simonot was clearly going to lose this game. Something had to be done.


That something was [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) , but Simonot fumbled with his mana (which by now involved a [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) as well as a [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) ) and ended up not being able to use it. That didn't really matter, though, because Royde had [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) anyway, destroying [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) and a Nexus. On his turn, he brought Simonot to 5 and tapped out for [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) .


Simonot had one topdeck and it needed to be a good one. It was. Another [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) . And this time Simonot managed his mana flawlessly, activated and equipped his one remaining [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) , and delivered the final points of poison.


Daniel Royde 0-2 Timothée Simonot




Round 12 Feature Match - Marijn Lybaert (Infect) vs. Mathieu Deloly (Infect)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

With 10-2 records, both Marijn Lybaert from Belgium and Mathieu Deloly from France still have their sights set on the Top 8, but in order to make the cut they're (likely) going to need to go undefeated from this point forward. Marijn is an accomplished player with several Pro Tour Top 8s to his name. Mathieu Deloly is less experienced -- in fact, he had just started playing again after a three-year hiatus. (See, no one ever quits!) Both players came to battle with an Infect deck.


### Game 1


Marijn won the die roll, drew his opening hand, and tanked for a minute before finally saying "keep".



![Breeding Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Breeding+Pool)

His 7 cards were [Breeding Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pool) , 2 [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) , [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) , [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) , [Mutagenic Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) , and [Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood) .


Now, this is a perfectly fine hand, except possibly when playing against Jund. Marijn wasn't only thinking about whether or not he would keep the hand, but rather which card he would play on turn 1: [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) or [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) . He could, of course, have said "keep" right away and then think for a minute on which card he would want to lead off with, but that would've given away a lot of information on his hand. Specifically, it would've given away that he had a choice between multiple one drops. By deciding on his turn 1 play before saying "keep", he left his opponent in the dark about what he was actually thinking about.


Marijn eventually decided to go for the turn 1 [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) , and [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) ed up his [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) on his second turn.


Mathieu, in the meantime, played [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) , [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) , and [Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent) over the course of his first two turns.


Marijn, on his third turn, played his second [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and attacked with his 6/2 Trampling [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) (+4/+0 from double [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) , +1/+1 due to [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) ). Mathieu blocked it with his own [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) . At first glance, it might seem awkward to block a 6/2 trampler with a 1/1 creature. But [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) deals damage to creatures in the form of -1/-1 counters, so Marijn's [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) would immediately die once the Exalted bonus would wear off. But before his Infect creature died, Marijn cast [Groundswell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundswell) and [Mutagenic Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) to put his opponent at 9 poison counters. Trample on the [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) was being really pivotal here.


After that exchange, a second [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) came down for Marijn. A [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and some pump spells later, the players were shuffling up for the next game.


Marijn Lybaert 1 - Mathieu Deloly 0


### Game 2


Mathieu got to play first and started with [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb) , [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) . Marijn, on a mulligan to 6, started with a turn 1 [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) . Deloly revealed the following hand:


[Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) , [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) , [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) , [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) , and [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) .


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplyo12/Marijn-Lybaert.jpg)
Lybaert, at this point, had [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) , [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) , [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) , [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) , and [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) in hand. Which card would you take here if you were Marijn?


The Belgian eventually chose [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) , one of the more fearsome cards against Infect as it can swallow up all those pump spells.


Next turn, it was time for a another [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) . Deloly revealed that he had drawn [Might of Old Krosa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Old+Krosa) . After some calculations, Marijn picked [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) .


A few turns later, Deloly cast a topdecked [Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood) on his [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) in response to Lybaert's [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) to take the game.


Marijn Lybaert 1 - Mathieu Deloly 1


### Game 3


Lybaert: "I mulligan."


Deloly: "Me too."


Lybaert: "Let's keep it fair this time."


Deloly: "The first two games were just about drawing the right cards at the right time."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplyo12/Mathieu-Deloly.jpg)
Lybaert got close to winning on [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) damage (not poison counters) this game, as Deloly had dealt a ton of damage to himself with [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) and lands, but eventually fell 1 damage short.


A well-timed [Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood) again allowed Deloly to take a game, and the match.


Marijn Lybaert 1 - Mathieu Deloly 2

This really is one sweet build. I especially like the fact that it fits the explosiveness of [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) into such an aggressive shell and seamlessly integrates this much disruption in the form of discard and [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) . Meanwhile, the team-up of discard-outlets [Lotleth Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll) and [Oona's Prowler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oona%27s+Prowler) provide extra consistency, and [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) speeds things up nicely.

[Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) may be pretty standard fare by now, but who would have expected [Burning Inquiry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Inquiry) or [Goblin Lore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lore) ? With those, Lévy fills the graveyard, then he dredges to bin even more stuff, unearths [Dregscape Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie) to allow him to cast two [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) s, which in turn reanimate all [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) s, with some +1/+1 counters via [Slitherhead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slitherhead) thrown in for good measure. Sunday, 3:33 a.m. – Interesting Sideboard Cards Seeing Play Today
-----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

While watching some of the games at the Grand Prix and while going through the decklists earlier today, my eye caught several interesting sideboard cards that might be excellent choices for the current metagame or just downright baffling. Let me highlight a couple of them.




---


![Bitter Ordeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bitter+Ordeal)

Seen in the sideboard of several Jund and Robots decks. It's nice against Eggs and [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) , and one mana less than [Slaughter Games](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) . Another draw is that [Bitter Ordeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Ordeal) can remove [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) , whereas [Slaughter Games](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) can only take nonland cards.




---


![Ground Seal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ground+Seal)

It shuts down [Conjurer's Bauble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conjurer%27s+Bauble) , preventing a Second Breakfast deck from looping [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb) .




---


![Darkblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Darkblast)

Seen in the sideboard of many decks today. It's good against both Infect and Affinity.




---


![Pollen Lullaby](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pollen+Lullaby)

Seen in the sideboard of a Mono Red Burn deck alongside a [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) . Why is it there? Maybe against Infect? Or against the [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) / [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) combo? Who knows...




---


![Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dryad+Arbor)

Frequently played as a one-of in the sideboards of Infect decks. I'm guessing it is there so you can fetch it with [Verdant Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) when playing against [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) ?



![Grave Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grave+Pact)

It's pretty good in the Jund mirror match!



![Grim Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grim+Poppet)

Some GR Tron decks have it in their sideboards today. It looks pretty good against Infect and Robots.


Hopefully this list has provided you with some additional ideas!




Round 14 Feature Match: Kenny Öberg vs. Timothée Simonot
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

Earlier today, Timothée Simonot and his Robots dispatched former GP champ Daniel Royde, now he was up against former GP champ Kenny Öberg and his Storm deck.


### Game 1


After mulligans for both players, Öberg started on [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) and [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) , while Simonot had [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) , [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) , [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) , [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) , and [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) on turn one, followed by [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) on turn two.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplyo12/fm14_Timothe-Simonot.jpg)*Timothée Simonot*
Turn three, Öberg went off: [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) was followed by [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) and another [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) , leaving him with a total of nine mana thanks to [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) . [Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) restocked Öberg's hand, [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) number three increased his mana pool by another three, and then it was time for [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames) . With every card in his graveyard ready to be flashed back, winning was mostly a formality now. When Öberg flashed back [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) and left both cards on top, Simonot knew he was in trouble.


A flurry of rituals ended in [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) and that was that.


Kenny Öberg 1-0 Timothée Simonot


### Game 2


Things went at a much more sedate pace this time around. Simonot spent his first turn taking a peek at (and a [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) from) Öberg's one-land hand via [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) . Öberg risked a glance as well with [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) .


Turn two, Simonot had [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel) , [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) (which ate [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) ), and another [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) that allowed him to cast [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) . Meanwhile Öberg was stuck on [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) as his only land, but made the most out of it with [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) directed at [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) . Simonot sacrificed his Mox and Citadel to save it.


Two turns later, Simonot had rebuild his board and had Öberg down to 6, when Öberg finally found a second land. He cast [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual) , [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) , and [Shatterstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatterstorm) , leaving Simonot with one [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) on the battlefield and one card in hand.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplyo12/fm14_Kenny-berg.jpg)*Kenny Öberg*
Simonot went back on offense with [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) , then [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) , and Öberg was back on the clock, even if it wasn't the fastest clock. He spent two turns on [Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) and [Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) flashback, then he had accumulated enough to attempt to go off.


A number of rituals and [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) for a little additional card drawing ended in [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames) , yet more of the same (that is, a number of rituals and [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) for a little additional card drawing), and finally: [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) . Or rather: [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) , [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames) flashback, and [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) again.


Kenny Öberg 2-0 Timothée Simonot
