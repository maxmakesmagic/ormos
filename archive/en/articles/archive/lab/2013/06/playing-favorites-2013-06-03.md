Playing Favorites
=================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on June 3, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







Hello! Come on in, take a seat. The show is about to begin.


I asked all of you to send me some cool decks built around your favorite *Dragon's Maze* cards. I received quite a number of responses, and paring all the great submissions down to just a few decks was tough, but in the end, there could only be so many winners. So, here they are, the best of the bunch, my favorite decks of the favorite decks.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl250_favoritey.jpg)### Vorelocutors


The first deck that caught my eye was sent in by Elliott Dent. Elliot took [Vorel of the Hull Clade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vorel+of+the+Hull+Clade), a Johnny-tastic card I've been eager to put to work, and combined him with cards like [Darksteel Reactor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Reactor) and [Azor's Elocutors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azor%27s+Elocutors) that simply win the game once you have enough counters. Vorel will drastically shorten the time it takes to reach that threshold, and [Paradox Haze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Haze) will give you two upkeeps, giving you an extra counter every turn for free. [Paradox Haze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Haze) is one of my favorite oddball enchantments, and it never ceases to amaze me how many times it comes in handy. Elliott also included [Illusionist's Bracers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionist%27s+Bracers), a card that will always be near to my heart, to double Vorel's doubling ability.

[Enlightened Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) and [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor) help you find your combo pieces. [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor) can search for either Vorel or [Azor's Elocutors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azor%27s+Elocutors), and [Enlightened Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) will dig up your choice of [Paradox Haze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Haze) or [Darksteel Reactor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Reactor). It can even grab [Illusionist's Bracers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionist%27s+Bracers) if the other pieces are already in place.

[Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) is an easy way to protect your combo from disruption, only requiring you to leave up a single blue mana. [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) can also be used to stop removal spells, but where it really shines is in its ability to counter your opponent's big spells, slowing down his or her game plan and giving you time to execute yours.


The deck gains a lot of flexibility from having both [Darksteel Reactor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Reactor) and [Azor's Elocutors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azor%27s+Elocutors) as potential win conditions. [Darksteel Reactor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Reactor) is slow and steady. Being indestructible makes it difficult to get rid of through normal means, and there's no built-in way for your opponent to remove counters from it. Although it requires a massive twenty counters to win the game, Vorel can usually make that happen in about four turns, or half that time with [Illusionist's Bracers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionist%27s+Bracers). [Azor's Elocutors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azor%27s+Elocutors), on the other hand, is much more vulnerable, but makes up for it with a great increase in speed. It only needs five counters to win the game, so Vorel can get it there in two turns. If you have [Paradox Haze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Haze) and Vorel equipped with [Illusionist's Bracers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionist%27s+Bracers) on the battlefield, you can win the game the turn after you play it, putting it straight from zero to five without making you worry about whether or not your opponent can deal damage to you.

### Millions of Voices


Our next deck comes from Benjamin Whiteman. He found a creative way to take advantage of [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), using the classic combo-enabler [Phyrexian Altar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar), as well as a Beast from *Conflux* that's usually found in Timmy decks with hoards of giant creatures, not at the center of an infinite combo. The idea of creating a combo with cards usually played for their value intrigued me, and Benjamin gets bonus points for finding a combo I had not yet considered.

Here's how it works. You'll need [Phyrexian Altar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar), [Parallel Lives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Lives), [Paleoloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paleoloth), and [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) on the battlefield. Sacrifice [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) to [Phyrexian Altar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar) to make one green mana. Bring [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) back to your hand, then sacrifice one of the Elementals for white mana. Cast [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and repeat for millions of Elemental tokens. As a bonus, your millions of tokens also each have millions of power and toughness, allowing you to deal trillions of damage to your opponent.

There are also some other tricksy things you can do with this deck. [Spikeshot Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Elder) can deal damage equal to its power to any creature or player, and although it loses this ability when you cast [Dragonshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonshift), there is a way to get around that. If you activate [Spikeshot Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Elder)'s ability a time or two, then cast [Dragonshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonshift) with it still on the stack, the Goblin will become a 4/4 before its ability resolves, making it deal 4 damage instead of 1. You can also use [Bioshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioshift) to give [Spikeshot Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Elder) all five of the +1/+1 counters from [Vigean Hydropon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigean+Hydropon), letting you kill just about anything. [Bioshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioshift) can also put the five counters on a [Slumbering Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slumbering+Dragon), waking it up immediately and allowing you to attack with your new 8/8.

### Humble Dragon


The other [Dragonshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonshift) deck I'll be highlighting comes from Avery Garon, who decided that his opponent's creatures should bow before the might of his Dragons. With [Humility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Humility) on the battlefield, every creature is just a 1/1 with no abilities. Overload [Dragonshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonshift), however, and everything changes. Suddenly your 1/1s turn into flying 4/4s, and you're free to soar right over your opponent's puny minions.

With [Humility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Humility) on the battlefield, tokens such as those from [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) and [Increasing Devotion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Devotion) stay exactly as they are, and living weapons like [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) and [Flayer Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+Husk) become even larger! [Electrickery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrickery) turns into a two-mana [Plague Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Wind), wiping out your opponent's entire army of 1/1 creatures, and [Glory of Warfare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory+of+Warfare) makes sure even your 1/1s are larger than your opponent's.
