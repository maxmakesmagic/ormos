The Modern Win Rates and Spiciest Decks at MC IV
================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on July 28, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











The Mythic Championship rewards performance in both Limited and Modern, but it's always interesting to see what had the best performance during the Modern rounds specifically.


Here are the Modern match win rates in non-mirror matches during the Swiss rounds for all archetypes with two or more pilots.

Hogaak and Hogaak Dredge had the highest Modern win rates of all major archetypes. (To give a rough indication of significance based on the sample size: the 95% Clopper-Pearson confidence interval for Hogaak's win rate ranged from 52.1% to 60.1%, and the corresponding interval for Hogaak Dredge ranged from 46.0% to 73.5%.)


To give another statistic, over half of the [players who went 8-2 or better in Modern](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2019MC4/24-27-modern-match-point-decklists-2019-07-28) were playing Hogaak or Hogaak Dredge. Given this, it's surprising that only one deck featuring Hogaak advanced to the Top 8.


The reason is that the Hogaak players with the best Modern records did poorly in draft. Even though Hogaak players as a group had an above-average win rate in draft collectively, the Hogaak players with excellent draft records did poorly in Modern, and vice versa. The exception was Top 8 competitor Martin Müller—the only Hogaak player to excel in both Limited and Constructed.


Hogaak Dredge Was the Deck of the Tournament
============================================


Purely based on Modern match win rates, Hogaak Dredge had the best performance of all major archetypes. This was a team deck played by Roshen Eapen, Ben Friedman, Marcus Luong, Ondrej Strasky, Oliver Tiu, Oliver Tomajko, and Noah Walker.

Their list was basically a Dredge deck, but with 3 copies of [Hogaak, Arisen Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis) (instead of several [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba)) and 4 [Stitcher's Supplier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier) (instead of [Shriekhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekhorn)) to enable it. Another important inclusion is the fetchable [Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor), which can help convoke Hogaak.


Although their list lacks the explosive element of [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), it is better in a damage race due to [Creeping Chill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Chill). This burn spell, along with [Conflagrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conflagrate), also helps beat [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) in Game 1, which is not to be underestimated.


There Are Several New Decks to Watch
====================================


*War of the Spark* and *Modern Horizons* enabled several new decks, most notably. Urza ThopterSword. This archetype had an excellent performance with a 55.3% match win rate. It even went 15-12 against Hogaak. So making infinite Thopters or stopping 8/8s with [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) is a solid plan against the boogeyman of the format, and Manuel Lenz piloted the deck to a [Top 8](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2019MC4/top-8-decklists-2019-07-27) finish.


But I wanted to highlight three other new decks, played in lesser numbers, to keep an eye on.

In the main deck, you see the infinite-mana combo of [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) and [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies). A new addition is [Giver of Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giver+of+Runes), which can protect the combo or be sacrificed to [Neoform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neoform) to find the pieces. But except for the somewhat unusual choice of [Eldritch Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldritch+Evolution) over [Finale of Devastation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finale+of+Devastation), the main deck looks fairly normal.


The surprise lurks in the sideboard. When opponents bring in removal spells for small creatures, Kihara can transform into a deck that aims to sacrifice [Allosaurus Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Allosaurus+Rider) to get [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand). Genius deck building.

The deck formerly known as Mardu Pyromancer got an upgrade in [Seasoned Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasoned+Pyromancer), so now it's fittingly called Mardu Pyromancers. [Seasoned Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasoned+Pyromancer) is particularly good when you can discard [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) or [Smiting Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smiting+Helix) for value, so it's a great fit.


Another new element in this deck is [Lightning Skelemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Skelemental), which can be returned via [Unearth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unearth) or [Thunderkin Awakener](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderkin+Awakener).

The reprintings of [Goblin Matron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Matron) and [Goblin Ringleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ringleader) have brought back the core that has made the Goblin tribe so successful in past formats. [Munitions Expert](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Munitions+Expert) and [Sling-Gang Lieutenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sling-Gang+Lieutenant) are also major additions to the deck.


There were two Goblins players in the event, and both did reasonably well. Jim Davis went 5-5 with [Skirk Prospector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector) as his one-drop, and Nicholas Montquila went 6-4 with [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) as his turn-one play. Together, they showed that Goblins is a real, competitive Modern deck.

The ideal draw for this deck starts with [Quest for the Holy Relic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Holy+Relic) on turn one, followed by [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) to get the quest counters going. Then on turn two, you might play [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk), return and recast [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), convoke [Martyr's Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr%27s+Soul), and finally sacrifice [Quest for the Holy Relic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Holy+Relic). This allows you trigger [Argentum Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argentum+Armor) as early as turn two, which should be enough to carry you to victory.


All in all, the Mythic Championship showed that [Hogaak, Arisen Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis) is dominating, but there is still a diversity of sweet decks possible in Modern.
