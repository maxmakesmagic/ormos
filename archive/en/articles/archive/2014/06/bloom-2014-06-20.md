In Bloom
========



 Posted in [NEWS](/en/articles)
 on June 20, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mana+Bloom)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom)
Many times, a card from a previous block becomes much more powerful once a future block's cards become Standard-legal. Sometimes, Magic R&D [does this intentionally](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ld/280). [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) is one such card. Prior to the release of *Journey into Nyx*, [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) was not a very powerful card. When used only to accelerate your mana, [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) is relatively poor. However, with the constellation mechanic, [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) becomes one of the most powerful enablers, by returning to your hand as often as you want.



This deck takes advantage of two of the most powerful constellation cardsâ€”[Eidolon of Blossoms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Blossoms) and [Doomwake Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomwake+Giant). One common play is to play [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) for X=1 on your second turn, then play [Eidolon of Blossoms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Blossoms) on your third turn, returning [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) on your fourth turn to get an extra enchantment to play each turn. If you want, you can set [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) to X=0 each turn so you have a constant source of constellation triggers.



The rest of the deck contains some powerful enchantments that help provide board control. [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) and [Courser of Kruphix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix) provide some extra synergy and card advantage. [Banishing Light](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banishing+Light) and [Brain Maggot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Maggot) provide some disruption for your opponent. These cards do this all while being enchantments, further strengthening your constellation strategy.
