Finding Some Inspiration
========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 12, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Sometimes you look at a decklist and you can't figure out exactly what's going on. If it were a flash in the pan, I might be able to move on, but lists like these have been popping up with multiple 3–1 and 4–0 finishes in **Magic Online** Daily Events. 

![Inspiration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inspiration) It's clear that we've got a control deck on our hands. However, the use of cards like [Mana Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom), [Sepulchral Primordial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sepulchral+Primordial), [Reap Intellect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reap+Intellect), and multiple copies of [Cyclonic Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift) leave me scratching my head. Clearly, the [Cyclonic Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift)s and [Reap Intellect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reap+Intellect)s combo well together, but is that enough to shape a brand-new archetype? 

 Acceleration, removal, card drawing, and big finishers is often a recipe for success, but I'm still confused. I'm very interested to see how this deck does in the coming weeks. If nothing else, this deck shows us why **Magic** is such a great game. You can be creative, use cards in an unconventional manner, and still crush people.
