*From FFL playtester Jay Schneider:*


At the start of every new format I begin designing decks from my comfort zone. Thus, my first deck in a format is always a Sligh deck. There are a few *Worldwake* cards that are exceptional for Sligh, most importantly [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) due to its amazing tempo. Any red one-drop that stands out is an obvious inclusion, so [Dragonmaster Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonmaster+Outcast) went in next. [Smoldering Spires](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smoldering+Spires) is also an interesting cardâ€”the ability to sneak through a key creature like a [Warren Instigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warren+Instigator) merits testing. Finally, Sligh decks really like having lands that turn into creatures, and while [Lavaclaw Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches) is very strong, [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) seems a little more efficient and provides access to [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). The rest of the deck rounds the curve out with cards that provide synergy.
