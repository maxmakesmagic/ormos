Round 2: Sextuple Grapeshot or Double Gifts?
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 1, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 





Hall of Famer Frank Karsten certainly needs no introduction, but his reason for being in Philadelphia probably does. He had not planned to attend the Pro Tour when it was scheduled to be Extended, but changed his mind as soon as the announcement was made that the format had been changed to Modern.


"It seemed like a deck builder's paradise, and I figured there might be some opportunity to break it," Karsten said.


To almost no one's surprise, Karsten brought his trusty [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) to the table, alongside [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), in a blue-red combo deck unlike many of the other Izzet-colored combo decks in the room. That attraction?


"Double Gifts," said Karsten, pointing to [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) with a [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven).




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptphi11/fm2_karsten_vidugiris.jpg)

*Gaudenis Vidugiris and Frank Karsten: both great players, both blue-red, but with very different pedigrees.*

A very different [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) deck sat across from the Dutch player in Round 2 as Gaudenis Vidugiris was sporting some Pyromancer tech of his own in [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath), once part of the briefly popular "Aussie Storm" deck. Vidugiris didn't quite have the pedigree of Karsten—few do!—but he is coming off a Top 8 performance at the last Pro Tour in Nagoya and has multiple Grand Prix titles to his name.



### Game One



Both players kept for the first game, which should have clued everyone watching in that it could be over quickly.


Vidugiris, on the play, broke a [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) for an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), casting [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions). He left one card on top, which signaled a pretty strong position for the Grand Prix Denver champion. That position was confirmed when Karsten paid 2 life to cast [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), revealing two [Rite of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame), two [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual), and [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath).


"That's a lot of rituals," Karsten said.


All Gaudenis needed was a [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) to win on turn two, considering Karsten had paid 2 life for [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) and 1 more to use [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) to find an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island).


Karsten used [Noxious Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Revival) to put [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) on the top of Vidugiris' deck—yes, it can do that—giving him one blank draw and slowing down the [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat)h long enough that the [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) in his hand might matter. Vidugiris simply played a tapped [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) and passed back.




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptphi11/fm2_karsten.jpg)

*Sometimes it takes a Hall of Famer to point out what [Noxious Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Revival) can really do.*

Unfortunately, with little other action, the Hall of Famer tapped out for [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension). That was all the window Vidugiris needed, as the top card was indeed the [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) he needed for the kill.


Vidugiris 1, Karsten 0



### Game Two



Karsten mulled to six on the play and led with [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), keeping one spell on top. Vidugiris matched with his one blue cantrip on a [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and elected not to shuffle. Could he have another turn-two win waiting in the wings?


Another [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) revealed a hand full of Rituals, [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot), and [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath). Depending on the top of his deck, it could be yet another potential turn-two kill. Karsten simply played a land and passed back with [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) at the ready.


Vidugiris, mindful of Karsten's untapped mana, was content to play a [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) tapped and pass.


A [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) from the Dutch player led to a shuffle and a [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) in hand, which would be necessary to cast the [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) after he passed with only [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) up.


Vidugiris played [Shivan Reef](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Reef) and tapped out to cast [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song), which Karsten let resolve. A second [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) gave Karsten pause as he did the math on what choke points he could fight over this turn. He let the second resolve as well, giving Vidugiris seven red mana and a storm count of two.


Vidugiris then used three of those to cast [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath), [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose), and a [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual), all of which resolved. Finally, as the sixth spell of the turn, he went for the kill with a [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) for 18, exactly Karsten's life total.




![](https://web.archive.org/web/20151123132305im_/http://archive.wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/events/ptphi11/fm2_Vidugiris.jpg)

*If you listen closely, you can hear the sound of grapeshot.*

Karsten, however, used [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) to counter one of the copies. All told, Karsten took 15 damage, but was very much still alive at 3 life. Thanks to the [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat)h, Vidugiris discarded his hand, but only a single copy of [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot), or any other burn spell, off the top would be enough to win.


Karsten went to work after surviving the turn, casting [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s for two turns while Vidugiris drew blanks and discarded them to [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat)h's trigger.


[Noxious Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Revival) brought back a [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), which still failed to find Karsten the action he needed to complete one of his combos. Instead, he settled with using another Revival to [Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) Vidugiris again.


A flurry of [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)s, [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)s, and a [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) followed until Karsten finally found a [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven). Running through the scenarios in his head, he decided he needed one more turn, so he passed back to what would likely be Vidugiris's final turn.


Fortunately for him, but unfortunately for fans of [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), Vidugiris made good use of that turn. [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) found [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot), and when [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) failed to find a way to stop both copies, Karsten extended his hand.


**Vidugiris 2, Karsten 0**
