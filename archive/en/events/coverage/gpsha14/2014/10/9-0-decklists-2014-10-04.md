9-0 DECKLISTS
=============



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX SHANGHAI 2014 - COVERAGE - EVENTS](/en/events/coverage/gpsha14)
 on October 4, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman_icon_0.jpg)
By Chapman Sim










Three players achieved the perfect 9-0 score yesterday. 



 Han Bing chose to build a hyper-aggressive deck, with a bunch of two drops, removal spells and tempo cards. As evidenced by cards like [Valley Dasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valley+Dasher) and [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason), he hoped to punish slower decks and achieve victory before they could stabilize. Double [Mardu Roughrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mardu+Roughrider) and [Butcher of the Horde](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+of+the+Horde) usually end games soon enough.




 Katsuhisa Saeki went with the Jeskai Way yesterday. He was fortunate to receive 4 on-color tapped duals, resulting in a great manabase. Double [Warden of the Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warden+of+the+Eye) along with [Jeskai Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Charm), [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) and [Arrow Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrow+Storm) proved to be great finishes in his aggressive deck.




 Lastly, Charles Lai opted to play a midrange-ish 4-color control deck, with highlights such as [Wingmate Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingmate+Roc) and [Ghostfire Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostfire+Blade). Sometimes, being greedy does pay off!
