The deck begins with a ton of mana acceleration. [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement) is a tough-to-kill guarantee of four mana on turn three. [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) also gets you there. So does [Explore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore), presuming you have enough land to make that happen. That's phase one of the plan, so now we're at turn three with four mana to play with.

There are broadly two options at this point. The most common is to lay a land, commit all four mana to an [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) with two counters on it, and then tap that Chalice for two more mana to create a second [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) with one counter on it. That takes you to a potential eight mana on turn four!


Alternatively - and this is the new innovation in the deck, courtesy of *M12* - spend the four mana on [Quicksilver Amulet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Amulet). Historically it has been a little awkward if you end up with huge casting cost spells you can't cast, and there has always been a trade off between having enough Emrakuls and Ulamogs and [Terastodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon)s and [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)s to find them with your [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap)s, and not having your hand swamped with them.




![Quicksilver Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Quicksilver%20Amulet&options=)

With the [Quicksilver Amulet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Amulet), that's no longer a problem. Assuming that you get to untap on turn five, you can activate the Amulet and drop one of your big guys into play. You won't get the extra turn from Emrakul, because you didn't cast it, but let's not pretend that a turn five Emrakul isn't going to give opponents plenty of problems anyway...


Assuming that you've gone the more conventional turn three route of further mana acceleration, you now have a slew of options on turn four. Six mana allows you to hard cast your [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine). Although that's one of your smaller threats, it's still a very big deal, especially that early in the game. With a couple of [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement)s, it's easily possible to reach [Terastodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) territory on turn four. Or, with a more 'reasonable' six mana on turn four you can use [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) to go hunt one of your fatties out of the top seven. Of course, this is still something of a fishing expedition, but you do have ten creatures in your deck, not including the 'please don't let this be what I have to take' [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement).




![Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Spellskite&options=)

Another neat card comes in the form of [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) out of the sideboard. Once your opponents have fallen victim to a [Quicksilver Amulet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Amulet) in games one or two, it's a good bet that they'll try to do something about it. That might mean [Manic Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal), or it might mean [Nature's Claim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Claim). In either case, getting [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) out as part of your turn four package makes it very tough to deal with [Quicksilver Amulet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Amulet) before it comes on line the following turn.


It looks as if Standard is wide open right now, and Eldrazi Green just got another shot in the arm. If you're playing Standard at *Friday Night Magic* this week, it's something you should take a look at. It's a lot of fun, and in the hands of Toshiyuki Kadooka at least, it's very good.
