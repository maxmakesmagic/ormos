Golgari
=======



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 5, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







An attentive reader named Jay pointed me to Kevin Florio's Golgari deck that won a Standard Grand Prix Trial at [Grand Prix Pittsburgh](/en/events/coverage/parker-makes-proud-return-pittsburgh). I'm always on the lookout for something new and exciting, and this is definitely the kind of deck I can get into. 


![Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sign+in+Blood)
 Several months ago, before *Return to Ravnica* had even come out, I was excited for the future of Standard, where I imagined a world dominated by sacrificing [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) to [Disciple of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Bolas) to down the opponent in life and card advantage. 


 Then [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) was printed, and I learned that we wouldn't have to work anywhere near that hard for our life and card advantage, and I forgot all about my old dream. 


Well, Kevin didn't, and he earned three byes for his efforts with this spicy midrange concoction.



[Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) provides the bulk of the card draw, but it's supported by [Underworld Connections](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) and [Disciple of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Bolas), who's amazing with the four [Desecration Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon)s in this deck. Speaking of amazing with [Desecration Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon)—how about [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)? This one-sided wrath doesn't even care about cards like [Boros Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) that some aggressive decks use to fight [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). 


 The sideboard has all the best hate—[Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) for Reanimator, [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) for control decks, [Gloom Surgeon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gloom+Surgeon) for aggro, and a good mix of other support cards to tune the deck. 


 My compliments to Kevin, as I like seeing people win with less-common decks, especially if those decks contain [Disciple of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Bolas).
