I'll Throw a Grenade for You
============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 9, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







When I first started playing in PTQs, back in the days of *Onslaught* Block Constructed, Goblins was definitely the boogeyman. The Goblin tribe was the fastest and most resilient of them all, and figuring out how to beat a tide of [Goblin Piledriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piledriver)s, [Goblin Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief)s, and [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander)s was first and foremost on everyone's list, both in Block and Standard. It's been some time since Goblins was so feared, and even in Legacy it's not the Tier 1 staple it used to be. 

![Goblin Grenade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Grenade) It looks like Modern may be where the goblin warrens are located these days, as this list from **Magic Online** indicates. The curve on this deck is lower than Goblin decks of years past, with only [Goblin Chieftain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chieftain) at three mana, and nothing more expensive than that. Instead, the deck overloads on one-drops, aiming to have three in play by turn two. Seeing as how [Goblin Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) and [Legion Loyalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist) have haste, and [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker) grants it, it won't be surprising to attack for 5 or more damage on the second turn! 

 The deck even has a token backup plan, which is nice. Having some resilience in the event of a [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) is smart, because one-drops aren't known for their longevity. The token backup plan happens to be just that, with [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal), [Dragon Fodder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder), and [Krenko's Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command) all putting multiple Goblins into play. Those play nicely with [Goblin Chieftain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chieftain) and [Shared Animosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shared+Animosity), both of which heavily reward you for having a ton of 1/1s in play (that soon become 5/1s or more). 

 If opponents are still standing after a million Goblins have been thrown their way, [Goblin Grenade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Grenade) and [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) are there to knock them down. [Goblin Grenade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Grenade) does a lot of damage for a small amount of mana, and finding a Goblin willing to deliver it is usually not very difficult.
