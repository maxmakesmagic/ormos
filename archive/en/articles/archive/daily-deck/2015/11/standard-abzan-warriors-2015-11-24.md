Standard Abzan Warriors
=======================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on November 24, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






You won't find any [Siege Rhino](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino)s in today's Abzan decklist. *Magic Online*'s Icculous piloted an Abzan Warrior tribal deck to a 4-0 finish in a recent Daily Event. This list is one of the more aggressive decks you can play in today's Standard environment.


The deck plays eleven one-drop creatures that attack with either 2 or 3 power, allowing you to get the beats in early. [Chief of the Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Edge) acts as a "[lord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+Atlantis)" for your Warriors and can come down as early as turn two. [Arashin Foremost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Foremost) gives the deck the reach it needs to attack for large chunks of damage. Finally, [Anafenza, the Foremost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+the+Foremost) is just a solid, well-rounded creature that attacks for a lot and hoses strategies like [Rally the Ancestors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors). It can even slow down the transformation of [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy).


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chief+of+the+Edge)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Edge) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Anafenza%2C+the+Foremost)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+the+Foremost)
[Collected Company](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) is excellent in this deck. While its main purpose is to gain you card advantage, it's capable of greatly affecting combat in your favor. Imagine attacking with a [Blood-Chin Rager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood-Chin+Rager), your only creature on the battlefield. Your opponent doesn't block, not fearing an innocent-looking 2/2. You cast [Collected Company](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and find [Chief of the Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Edge) and [Arashin Foremost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Foremost). Your 2/2 [Blood-Chin Rager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood-Chin+Rager) is suddenly a 3/2 with double strike. Suddenly you're attacking for 6 damage! [Collected Company](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) can also help transform [Kytheon, Hero of Akros](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kytheon%2C+Hero+of+Akros) into [Gideon, Battle-Forged](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Battle-Forged) by ensuring that you have at least three creatures on the battlefield to attack with.


One of the cards I found the most interesting in this deck is a sideboard card, [Abzan Ascendancy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Ascendancy). I've always liked this card, but it was really hard to find a home for it in Standard. This card acts as a one-shot [Glorious Anthem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Anthem), and every creature that dies leaves behind a creature with evasion. Abzan Ascendency makes you less vulnerable to sweeper effects and also helps you break through ground stalls. That's a lot for a three-mana enchantment.
