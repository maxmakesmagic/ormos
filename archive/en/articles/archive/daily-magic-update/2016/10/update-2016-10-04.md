The October 4, 2016 Update
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on October 4, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on October 4, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by winning the best way.


Today's Must
============


1. [The Best Way to Win](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/the-best-way-to-win/) | ChannelFireball | Will Jonathan

The best way to win in my book is with a pile of cards still in hand and a [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) (or [Cloudblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer)) charging in for those last 2 points of damage. But that's not what Jonathan is talking about. He's talking about having a healthy mindset that not only makes winning itself less important than your performance, but also puts you in a space where winning is more likely.


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [The *MTGO* Economy: It's About Trust](https://www.cardhoarder.com/content/the-mtgo-economy-it-s-about-trust-215) | Cardhoarder

Cardhoarder breaks down many of the community's larger issues with the recent [*Magic Online* announcements](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/magic-digital/magic-online-treasure-chests-and-pricing-changes-2016-09-29). Some of those issues are addressed in [today's update on Treasure Chests here](http://magic.wizards.com/en/MTGO/articles/archive/magic-online/treasure-chest-information-2016-10-03).

The Professor's "Is it worth it" series looks at who a product is best for, who would do better buying other products instead, and how well the product does its job, how well it's made, and whether it will bake you cookies. (Hint: It will not—also, he doesn't actually talk about that. We long ago instituted a policy against cookie-baking card products.)


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/k7GhLoTfva4>


1. [Option Two](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/alexullman-10042016-option-two/) | Gathering Magic | Alex Ullman

Ullman is still a bit shell-shocked that [Peregrine Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peregrine+Drake) was not banned in Pauper, but that doesn't mean he's not ready to attack a Drake-aligned format. Plus, he gives love to [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), so of course he caught my attention.


1. [Dear Azami: Your Deck Is Mine!](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33791_Dear-Azami-Your-Deck-Is-Mine.html) | StarCityGames.com | Levi Byrne

This is one of two Commander articles looking at building around [Gonti, Lord of Luxury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gonti%2C+Lord+of+Luxury); [the other is on MTGMintcard.com](http://www.mtgmintcard.com/articles/writers/prophet-of-kruphix/rhystic-study-gonti-lord-of-luxury). I can understand the appeal. By playing Gonti in your mono-black deck, you have a chance to cast actual good cards—like [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) or [Cloudblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer).


Yeah, I'm way over my limit on [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)/[Cloudblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer) references today.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Temur Aetherworks](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=108084)

One of the coolest strategies we saw on stream at the StarCityGames.com Indianapolis Open this past weekend was this Temur Aetherworks deck that Daniel Weiser managed to grab 25th place with, casting early [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger)s and [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End)s all day. The deck uses energy-producing Puzzleknots—[Glassblower's Puzzleknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glassblower%27s+Puzzleknot) and [Woodweaver's Puzzleknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodweaver%27s+Puzzleknot)—plus [Attune with Aether](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attune+with+Aether) and [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning) to generate more energy. The rest of the deck consists of ways to find [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) quickly, letting Weiser cast giant Eldrazi quickly and efficiently.


This type of strategy is certainly very powerful, and Weiser has proved the shell of the deck can work. I can just hear the sound of some team somewhere testing this, determined to break it before [Pro Tour *Kaladesh*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/premierplay/protour/ptkld). And it sounds marvelous.
