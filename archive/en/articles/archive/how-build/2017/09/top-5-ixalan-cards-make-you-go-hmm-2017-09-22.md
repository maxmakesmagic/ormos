Top 5 Ixalan Cards That Make You Go Hmm
=======================================



 Posted in **How to Build**
 on September 22, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






***Ixalan*** **Prerelease Sept. 23–24!**


There are plenty of wild and exciting new cards to discover in *Ixalan*! Chart a course for a [store near you](http://locator.wizards.com/) this weekend to experience the new set for yourself!


I'll be honest: *Ixalan* hooked me when I heard Pirates were going to be fighting with Dinosaurs. And with Vampires. And with Merfolk. And with other Pirates. And there was going to be *real Treasure*, in token form, hanging around too.



![Prosperous Pirates](https://media.wizards.com/2017/images/daily/cardart_XLN_Prosperous-Pirates.jpg)[Prosperous Pirates](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prosperous+Pirates) | Art by [Joss Hass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5b%22Josh+Hass%22%5d)
Getting into Pirates is something for another day as it's the *everything else* in *Ixalan* that I'm digging through. For example, both the [Growing Rites of Itlimoc](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growing+Rites+of+Itlimoc) and [Legion's Landing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Landing) will be making it into my [Rhys the Redeemed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) Commander deck soon. Finding the obvious upgrades and additions is always a treat with a new set—as are the cards I'm not quite so sure about where they'll go but I'm positive they'll find a home.


Here's five that I'm still puzzling out.


Tocatli Honor Guard
===================


When *Amonkhet* arrived, it brought a humble little two-mana creature: [Vizier of Remedies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies). The tiny little text of reducing the -1/-1 countered placed on creatures opened up significant options for Modern Abzan [Collected Company](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) decks.


[Tocatli Honor Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tocatli+Honor+Guard) feels like it hits similar notes:


* Hosing [enters-the-battlefield effects](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/play-design/m-files-hour-devastation-part-1-2017-07-14#enters-the-battlefield-effects), like [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb), turns off everything from [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan).
* Unlike [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb), [Tocatli Honor Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tocatli+Honor+Guard) is a creature that fits neatly into existing "[hate bears](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/play-design/m-files-hour-devastation-part-1-2017-07-14#hate-card)" and [Collected Company](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) decks. Slipping this into play at the right moment can make, or more likely break, someone's plans.
* It even specifically counters Abzan [Collected Company](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) decks, forcing them to fight "fair" on the ground.

Vanquisher's Banner
===================


A long time ago in a *Magic* set far, far away, I previewed a card called [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton). In [that article](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/i-one-welcome-our-new-robot-overlord-2011-07-05), I created a Jellyfish tribal deck that, surprisingly, mostly worked. (Umbra Mantle was involved. It was a very strange time.)


[Vanquisher's Banner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vanquisher%27s+Banner) is another card I'm scratching my head over. While more limited than its Modern-banned forerunner, [Glimpse of Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature), casting creatures spells to draw cards is usually a good deal for tribal decks. Pumping an army, any army you choose, is also a good bet.


But where does it end up?


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Edgar+Markov)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edgar+Markov)
A set filled with new Vampires is good for a Vampire deck of any stripe, but [Edgar Markov](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edgar+Markov) wants to see plenty of Vampires get cast, and [Vanquisher's Banner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vanquisher%27s+Banner) draws cards when you cast Vampires.


Elsewhere:


* Kobolds have always been a fun little type of creature, and so are [Memnite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)s and [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter)s. Maybe [Arcane Adaptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Adaptation) could help?
* [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio) has been part of [Glimpse of Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) Shenanigans in the past. Perhaps a colorless combo deck exists?
* Elves have always been cheeky about cheating themselves into play. Slipping [Vanquisher's Banner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vanquisher%27s+Banner) into Modern might be as easy as little green Forest friends.

No, there's no red here. But did you know if you control [Sunbird's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunbird%27s+Invocation), cast [Approach of the Second Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Approach+of+the+Second+Sun) from your hand, then find another copy of [Approach of the Second Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Approach+of+the+Second+Sun) while resolving [Sunbird's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunbird%27s+Invocation)'s trigger, you win the game when that first cast of [Approach of the Second Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Approach+of+the+Second+Sun) finally resolves?


Is [Sunbird's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunbird%27s+Invocation) a Standard deck? I don't know, but that two-card-and-a-little-luck combo won't go unnoticed or untried. Going back further, Modern's array of spells set up all sorts of hilarious opportunities. [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) may be banned, but giving all of your spells a flavor of cascade turns anything with haste into a threat—assuming you get to the point where you can cast a six-mana enchantment happens.


Commander is the biggest opportunity to [Sunbird's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunbird%27s+Invocation). Imagine casting [Insurrection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insurrection) into [Boldwyr Heavyweights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boldwyr+Heavyweights) or [Razaketh, the Foulblooded](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razaketh%2C+the+Foulblooded). Add in some library manipulation or thoughtful deck construction, and [Sunbird's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunbird%27s+Invocation) becomes a must-stop threat for opponents in a color they weren't expecting.


Dire Fleet Ravager
==================


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dire+Fleet+Ravager)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dire+Fleet+Ravager)
I have a confession: I'm a fan of [Pox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pox) and [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox). While [Dire Fleet Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dire+Fleet+Captain) isn't either of those spells, the ability to rip away a third of opponents' life totals in one convenient package shouldn't be overlooked.


Figuring out where it ends up is a bit harder.


Standard has some options to make it easier to hit multiple times, such as [Acrobatic Maneuver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acrobatic+Maneuver) and [Fairgrounds Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden), but pairing it up with burn spells is the more obvious angle. Menace and deathtouch make it a threat all on its own too, but I can't shake trying to see how to make its enters the battlefield trigger work harder (maybe outside Standard):


* [Exquisite Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exquisite+Blood) is one way to turn your frown upside down.
* [Gonti's Machinations](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gonti%27s+Machinations) has been looking for a home since it arrived in *Aether Revolt*.
* [Mindcrank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank) and [Bloodchief Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) have been buddies in life loss for a long time.

Of course, the format you play [Dire Fleet Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dire+Fleet+Ravager) in matters more than card legality—losing a third of your life in Commander hits much hard than in Standard. I'm just not finding the best way to make entering the battlefield that scary.


Vance's Blasting Cannons
========================


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vance%27s+Blasting+Cannons)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vance%27s+Blasting+Cannons)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spitfire+Bastion)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitfire+Bastion)Transform
When *Fate Reforged* was in Standard, [Outpost Siege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege) was a standout staple for red decks. Choosing the khans mode meant you got an extra, free card to use every turn. [Vance's Blasting Cannons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vance%27s+Blasting+Cannons) brings that effect back (for the most part), but now asks if you can cast three spells in a turn.


If you do, you get [Open Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Open+Fire) repeatable on a land: [Spitfire Bastion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitfire+Bastion).


The dream of playing a burn deck that just keeps on burning is amazing, but so is an enchantment that could not only help set up storm combo in Modern, but also gives your deck a little extra edge if you whiff.
