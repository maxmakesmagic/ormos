MAGIC 2015 SAMPLE SEALED POOL #1
================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX SALT LAKE CITY 2014 - COVERAGE - EVENTS](/en/events/coverage/gpslc14) 
 on September 6, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







 Whether you're a veteran of *Magic 2015* Sealed or you've just started opening packs, building the best Limited deck from just six packs of cards is a daunting challenge. Taking time to build decks and explore options is one of the ways any player can improve in one of the trickiest formats.




 Here is a sample pool from six packs of *Magic 2015*, and it's a good example of the types of hard decisions the Sealed format entails. With [Hornet Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen) headlining green, it'd be easy to just pile up Forests and find something to pair with it. However, green is lacking in early drops. Similarly, colors like blue and black have early action, evasion, and removal, but lack the splashy bomb finishers that can threaten to end games on their own. Powerful tricks like [Polymorphist's Jest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorphist%27s+Jest), [Sanctified Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanctified+Charge), [Festergloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festergloom), and [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) pull us in different directions, but it's up to you to find the deck you'd choose to play.
