Prowess in Legacy
=================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on December 24, 2014

While it is to be expected that new cards grab the spotlight at the Pro Tour, the shockwave generated by the arrival of *Khans of Tarkir* is reaching beyond just Standard.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Monastery%20Swiftspear)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery%20Swiftspear)
"Prowess." The Jeskai keyword ability gives a small temporary bonus to creatures whenever you cast a noncreature spell. This ability truly comes into its own in an environment where there is a rich variety of cheap draw spells.


An environment like, oh, Legacy.


An environment with what is arguably the supreme draw spell, Brainstorm. An environment with [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), spells so powerful they were banned in Modern. Oh, and let's not forget the zero-mana [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), or [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), which let you pad your graveyard as well.


With a veritable constellation to choose from, it's easy to set up a cascade of draw spells. This is what leads us to [Bob Huang's UR combo deck here](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/cruising-to-victory-in-new-jersey/), which looks to win with a single, decisive stroke.


In the past, this deck would have looked to green for an extra punch, but with the expansion of the one-mana stratum through the addition of [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Monastery Swiftspear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery+Swiftspear), it can streamline down to two colors and improve its reliability.
