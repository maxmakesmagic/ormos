We Three...
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 10, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







Last week, in [L.A. in Translation](/en/articles/archive/la-translation-2005-11-03), we set up the gauntlet for the current Extended PTQs. In that article, we said that a good gauntlet would be made up of [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) decks (both Dredge-inclusive and [Mental Note](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Note)), Boros Deck Wins, Heartbeat combo, Billy Moreno's new version of Madness, and perhaps some non-Top 8 scoring Affinity or Goblin decks. With three, Three, *THREE* Grand Prix Top 8s to look at this week, it is interesting to see how preparing for the metagame in such a way would have served us on the grander scale.

![James Zhang sports some new hardware.](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmelb05/win.jpg)*James Zhang sports some new hardware.* So far, our metagame preparation would have reasonably predicted the Top 8. I think it's kind of odd to see neither Heartbeat combo in the elimination rounds nor Billy's three color deck, but most everything else seems more or less on the radar. Raphael Levy spent the first seven rounds of Pro Tour L.A. undefeated, and only barely missed Top 8, in contention until the final round of the Swiss. A reasonable facsimile of his deck, represented by Hugh Glanville in the Top 8 of Grand Prix Melbourne, basically swaps a [Windswept Heath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windswept+Heath) for a [Polluted Delta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) (irrelevant for the purposes of finding an [Overgrown Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb)) and exchanges the positions of [Withered Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch) versus [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) between main and side.

![Balancing Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Balancing+Act)

Balancing Tings is based on playing [Balancing Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balancing+Act) and blowing up all its own lands in the same turn (check out how they can all make themselves disappear). Typically the Balancing Tings player will cast [Balancing Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balancing+Act) with one card in hand (floating three mana) or with one remaining permanent in play (ideally a [Terravore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore)). The theory is that all the land on both sides of the table will go away, leaving a [Terravore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) and two participants playing off the tops of their decks. [Terravore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) wins pretty quickly, generally in 1-2 strikes against a helpless opponent. Before the advent of [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) in Standard, Balancing Tings was held down with such cards as [Innocent Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood) (good also against the dominant [Desolation Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolation+Angel) decks of the day). However, and in light of Dimir week, the Black-Blue best creature ever printed *did* follow Balancing Tings into Standard; in the same way, Psychatog's recent successes in Extended are the primary reason I thought Balancing Tings would fall off the metagame charts.


The *card*  [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) is of course the centerpiece of the classic Psychatog archetype ([Mental Note](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Note)s or no), the finisher in the new Dredge decks with [Life From the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+From+the+Loam), and even helps out [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) and [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) in U/G Madness as a splash. Now all [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) decks pack counters, meaning that a [Balancing Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balancing+Act) player might not want to discard all his cards and blow up all his lands… but even when the counters don't cut it, [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) can trump [Balancing Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balancing+Act) and/or [Terravore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore). If the [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) is in play when Balancing Tings tries to play [Balancing Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balancing+Act) without [Terravore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) in play, [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)'s ability allows it to dump its hand and ruin the initial setup (forcing an in-hand Terravore into the graveyard); on the other hand, if it comes down to a brawl between three drop finishers, [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) can give itself +1/+1 and [Terravore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) -2/-2 at will by removing two lands from graveyard. If Psychatog continues to perform well – and this weekend says that it will – I would not recommend Balancing Tings, though perhaps the finishes in Copenhagen and Kitakyuushuu mean that it should re-earn its spot on the playtest gauntlet.


The Boros Deck Wins decks in Copenhagen were reasonably close to Tsuyoshi Fujita's Top 8 deck from Los Angeles, but substitute the [Meloku](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meloku)-killing [Char](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char) for [Pulse of the Forge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Forge). As for Ravager decks, there were three... *just like the number of [Arcbound Ravagers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravagers)* (and [Scale of Chiss-Goria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Scale%2Bof%2BChiss-Goria)!) in Olivier Ruel's deck. No, I don't get it either.

This version of NO Stick has a lot of things going for it. The singleton [Eternal Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Dragon) gives the deck a counter-resistant source of card advantage (that can grab [Sacred Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry), no less) and also a long game win condition of some merit after an attrition war. The lone [Orim's Chant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant) main deck seems odd... I'm sure Kaji's opponents played as if he had more copies, which was probably to the eventual champion's advantage. [Isochron Scepter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) + [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) just seems cruel to beatdown decks.


The new deck from Kitakyuushuu is Makihito Mihara's The "CAL."

In honor of Dimir Week, it seems that the Extended gods have bent the winners in favor of the Black-Blue [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Psychatog) (no surprises there).


So what should you play? As far as Grand Prix Top 8 data goes, it looks like Heartbeat Combo is a good choice. One reason is that Heartbeat is the most powerful known deck in the Extended format. More importantly, Heartbeat Combo's absence in the aggregate Top 8s means that it will be under prepared-for in at least the short term. Most of all, Heartbeat matches up well against the kinds of decks that are showing up at the top of the standings. Boros Deck Wins has very little it can do about Heartbeat's core plan. Control decks are historically the best weapons against combo, but the version we saw from the Pro Tour was filled with card drawing. [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) into [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) into [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) backed up by [Cunning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) can be too much for certain control decks, especially those with only 3-4 [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) total fueling their reactive elements. Though Heartbeat likes to win with [Mind's Desire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind%27s+Desire), Chris McDaniel actually beat NO Stick standout Ryuchi Arita in the L.A. Swiss *through* an [Isochron Scepter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) + [Orim's Chant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant) and *TWO* (!!!) [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s… defeating the Columbus repeat quarterfinalist *on Arita's own upkeep!*


That's power.


I hate that dirty combo feeling as much as the next non-Johnny guy, but my suggestion is that if you are interested in a Honolulu Blue Envelope, take another look at the Star Wars Kid:
