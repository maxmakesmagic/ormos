Round 13:  Plasms and Plans Captured
====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 18, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 







[Kai Budde (Esper Control) vs. Robert Jurkovic (Esper Control)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdgm13/fm12)


Patrick Chapin (Bant Control) vs. Gabriel Nassif (Bant Control)
---------------------------------------------------------------


[Reid Duke (Bant Control) vs. Josh Utter-Leyton (Boros Blitz)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdgm13/fm14)




---

Dueling [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling)s helped split the first two games in the Patrick Chapin and Gabriel Nassif Bant Control mirror match, but it was a spot of bad luck and a particularly potent pachyderm that gave the French Hall of famer his tenth win on the weekend.


And yet Nassif downplayed the role of [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) in a match that was far more complex than it appeared on the surface.


"[Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) is very good, but sometimes you can beat it if you get ahead on the board with Voices and [Smite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite)rs and stuff," he said. "I think you want to be the aggressor, and sometimes it's too slow."


While the third and final game came down to Chapin missing his third land for several turns while a [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) beat down, the first two games were all about [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/r13_chapin_nassif.jpg)  
*Patrick Chapin and Gabriel Nassif clashed in a Bant Control mirror match, with Nassif coming out on top when Chapin's deck fumbled on lands in the third game.* 
Sure. Other things happened. [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence)s supplied early pressure and made countermagic worse late, [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter)s slew [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought)s, [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) drew cards and [Gatecreeper Vine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine)s found lands. But each and every move was an attempt to set up [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) to seal the deal.


Chapin set himself up in the first game partially on the strength of early mana troubles from Nassif, the man known as Yellow Hat. A pair of [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) pressured Nassif into making some early moves to stay alive, and Chapin used the opportunity to land several Jace, Architects of Thought before [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) was able to steal the deal.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/r13_chapin.jpg)  
*Chapin keeps up the pressure, giving himself the time to resolve both Jace and [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) for the win in the first game.* 
Ever mindful of the clock, Nassif was almost disappointed he nearly pulled out of his early mana screw.


"It kind of sucks. I started drawing well in the first game and dragged it on," Nassif said.


"Drawing just good enough to keep it interesting, to keep it close? It's alright. Now you get to go first and win," Chapin offered back.


Turns out, he was correct.


The second game revolved around [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) when a sequence started by Chapin's attempt at [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) ended in Nassif Capturing more than enough Plasm (courtesy of [Plasm Capture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plasm+Capture)) to easily resolve [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling). Chapin went for a [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) with [Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) backup, but Nassif was flush with both [Plasm Capture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plasm+Capture) and multiple counterspells. Chapin simply scooped to the resolved [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling).


What was most interesting, however, was Nassif's sideboard strategy. He sided out his [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought)s in order to keep the full amount of [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere)s in the deck. While he said it was a mistake to side them all out on the play—a turn four Jace is still a solid play in his mind—he realized they didn't do all that much against [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter)s and Voices if things went long.


"It's not like [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)," he said. "You can actually activate it twice and still lose."


Instead, Nassif relied on [Smite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite)rs and Voices to provide early pressure and open his window to [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/r13_nassif.jpg)  
*Nassif's Bant Control deck is capable of switching to an aggressively minded strategy that is less reliant on [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought).* 
"You need them," he said in regards the cheap beaters. "It's so huge to put on pressure and deal with Jace and to be the one to sit on the counters."


Chapin had a different strategy, it appeared, as his Jaces stayed in. But the [Plasm Capture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plasm+Capture) sequence and missing his third land for several turns in a row were what really sunk Chapin.


In fact, the first two games were so interesting, Chapin lamented the finish to the final game of an otherwise intriguing matchup.


"Not like this," he said, smiling but sinking in his chair a bit. "Not like this."


**Match notes:**


* Nassif still has a shot at coming out of this Pro Tour as the top French Pro for the season. He needs to beat Raphael Levy by 9 points and Jeremy Dezani by just 1 point. He said that, as a Hall of Famer who doesn't travel to Grand Prix, he had no idea he was even in the running until today.
* Before the match, the pair did something I haven't seen anyone do at this level outside a Top 8: they shared their decks with each other. It turned out that, having tested and shared ideas together, they had decided before the tournament they would do so to keep things fair.
* Nassif and Chapin played in the semifinals of the World Championship in New York in 2007 in the famous [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) mirror match, one that people certainly pointed to when Chapin was inducted into the Hall of Fame this past year. Oddly enough, the winner of that tournament, Uri Peleg, returned to the Pro Tour this weekend in San Diego.
