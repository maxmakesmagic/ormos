Also, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
=====================================


1. [Pro Tour *Ixalan*: By the Numbers](https://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/pro-tour-ixalan-by-the-numbers?src=wizards) | MTGGoldfish | SaffronOlive

As far as summaries of the Pro Tour metagame go, this one is pretty thorough. Would you expect any less from Mr. Olive?


He does raise questions about the number of Temur/Four-Color Energy decks played at the Pro Tour and whether the representative numbers are too high for a healthy format. One thing Saffron doesn’t point out is how this Pro Tour is different from comparable Pro Tours where [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) were slightly less played as a part of the whole: this Pro Tour happened much later in the season. The best versions of these decks don’t really coalesce until later in the season, when they start making up larger portions of the metagame. Adam Prosak [talked a bit about this the other week](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/play-design/solving-strength-2017-10-27), and it makes a late-season Pro Tour a different breed.


1. [The Intriguing Possibilities Of Planeswalker Angrath](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/36122_The-Intriguing-Possibilities-Of-Planeswalker-Angrath.html) | StarCityGames | John Dale Beety

Ever since we published the fact that Angrath was just a dad angry he couldn’t get home to his two daughters, the internet has adopted him as their new favorite red-black character. But where can that story go? JDB looks into it.
