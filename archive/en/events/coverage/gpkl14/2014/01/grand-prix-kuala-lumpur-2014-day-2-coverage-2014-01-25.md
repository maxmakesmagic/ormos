Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur 2014 Day 2 Coverage
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 25, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl14/day2_opening.jpg)1,006 planeswalkers converged in Kuala Lumpur in hopes of hoisting the trophy. After nine rounds of Sealed Deck, only 128 remain. Of these, three players retain untarnished records: Jack Teo, Shouta Yasooka and Yusuka Iwasaki are excellently positioned for a spot in the Top 8. Hot on their heels are Pro Tour Veterans Makihito Mihara, Kelvin Chew and Hironobu Sugaya. Stay tuned to coverage of Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur to watch all the action unfold!

Sunday, 11:03 a.m. – Drafting with Kelvin Chew
----------------------------------------------


*by Noel Neo*

 

Kelvin Chew made his mark with a top 8 at Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*, but has had a bit of a dry spell since then. Here at Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur, he is sitting on 8-1, very nicely placed for a run at the top 8. How would he fare?


Kelvin was passed a clear signal UG was open in pack 1. However, this also meant he had to pass some strong blue cards himself. This would come back to haunt him in his second pack.


Below lists the key cards Kelvin considered in each pack, with his picks depicted as the leftmost card.

Reflecting on the draft, Kelvin lamented passing [Hammer of Purphoros](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer+of+Purphoros) in his booster one first pick. The draft would have gone very differently if he had – while red is widely viewed as underpowered in *Theros* draft, this also means players are prejudiced to the colour and it turned out under drafted. Some very strong red cards had tabled, including a pair of [Kragma Warcaller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kragma+Warcaller) that would have been absolutely brutal in a minotaur deck.


**Deckbuilding**


The draft's done and dusted, however, and Kelvin was left with solid green, some powerful blue and white spells, a possible black splash for [Pharika's Mender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%27s+Mender) and a smattering of red.


He chose to build his deck around a UG evasion core enhanced by a pair of powerful white creatures. He runs [Temple of Abandon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Abandon) simply for the scry ability and cut [Thassa's Bounty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Bounty) as he already has sufficient late game spells.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl14/dr_kelvin.jpg)For this strategy to work, his opponents need to be unable to close the game quickly so Kelvin's large evasion creatures can take chucks out of their life totals. He is possibly able to race with the help of [Hopeful Eidolon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hopeful+Eidolon).

Sunday, 11:08 a.m. – Drafting With Shouta Yasooka
-------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

One of only 3 undefeated players coming in to the final day of Grand Prix: Kuala Lumpur, Shouta Yasooka is no stranger to the top draft tables. Going into a *Theros* draft, his natural preference is for more controlling, U/x decks, with a preference for a removal-heavy, evasion-creature-laden U/B deck if he can assemble the necessary pieces.


Yasooka's draft table held the other two undefeated players, Yuusuke Iwasaki and Jack Teo, as well as his long-time drafting compatriot, Makihito Mihara. With a challenging draft ahead of him, Yasooka cracked open his first pack.


**Pack 1**


Xenagos, The Reveler stared out at him for his first choice, facing off against a [Wingsteed Rider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingsteed+Rider) and some scattered removal. The Planeswalker was a relatively straightforward first pick, but Yasooka took his time in the second, eventually settling for an [Ordeal of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordeal+of+Thassa) over some mediocre R/G cards.



![Xenagos, The Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Xenagos%2C+the+Reveler)

Yasooka's third pick was more interesting, having to choose between a [Leafcrown Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leafcrown+Dryad), [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet), and a couple of aggressive Red creatures. The [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet) won out, and Yasooka's next picks of [Dragon Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Mantle), a [Leafcrown Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leafcrown+Dryad) to replace the one he'd passed, and a [Satyr Rambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Satyr+Rambler) started to push him down the road of an aggressive Red-based deck.


**Pack 2**


Yasooka opened a second pack that was, on the surface, a little underwhelming. A [Thassa's Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Emissary), a [Rageblood Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rageblood+Shaman), a Feral invocation and a [Nylea's Disciple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%27s+Disciple) stared back out at him. Yasooka opted for straight-out power and chose the Emissary, clearly willing to move back into Blue if necessary at this early stage in the draft.



![Thassa's Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thassa%27s+Emissary)

The next few packs yielded an [Ill-Tempered Cyclops](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ill-Tempered+Cyclops), a [Wavecrash Triton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wavecrash+Triton), and a [Pharika's Cure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%27s+Cure). Then, just as Yasooka's preferred colours seemed to open up, he was forced back to his original R/G build, picking up a bunch of low-cost favourites like [Sedge Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedge+Scorpion), [Akroan Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroan+Crusader), and [Two-Headed Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Two-Headed+Cerberus). Yasooka managed to wheel his [Feral Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Invocation), upping the power of his aggressive and heroic smaller drops.


At the end of the second pack, Yasooka was firmly committed to Red, but looked undecided between a potential Blue splash and a bunch of solid dudes in Green.


**Pack 3**


Another disappointing pack, with a bevy of powerful Black and White cards, but nothing in Yasooka's colours more impressive than a [Nessian Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nessian+Courser). The second pick had no R/G cards at all, Yasooka shaking his head and picking up a [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin).



![Spearpoint Oread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spearpoint+Oread)

The next few packs saw him nab a [Leafcrown Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leafcrown+Dryad), another [Dragon Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Mantle), and a [Spearpoint Oread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spearpoint+Oread). The wheeling packs, again, held no particularly interesting choices, with Yasooka spending his last picks rounding out a solid if low-curved creature base out of boosters that seemed determined to punish his colour choices in Pack 1.


**Building the deck**


It's always hard to measure the thoughts of Japanese Pro's, who tend to combine heroic levels of stoicism with an inscrutability that borders on the Buddha-like. Nevertheless, Yasooka seemed less than pleased with the result of the draft.  
  

After tossing up whether it could be worth splashing for a third colour, Yasooka instead opted for a tightly focussed, if not particularly powerful, aggressive R/G deck. His strategy would be to try and run out games as quickly as possible with his aggressive low-costing creatures, and then strategically pump them with [Dragon Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Mantle)s and [Feral Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Invocation)s to push through the remaining points of damage. Without creatures with natural evasion, it looked like an uphill struggle for the former Player of the Year.

Round 11 Feature Match – Kelvin Chew (UGW) vs Ayato Imai (UB)
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Noel Neo*

 

Both players have clocked up two losses and are now battling to remain in contention for top 8.


The first game ended quickly as Ayato's crucial [Lash of the Whip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lash+of+the+Whip) Hopes were Stymied by Kelvin. This allowed [Fleecemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleecemane+Lion) to gain indestructible and hexproof, and the Lion bestowed with [Nylea's Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%27s+Emissary) ended the game in short order. Ayato also took quite a bit of early damage as his only creature for the first five turns was [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl14/r11_kelvin.jpg)*Kelvin's offensive after Stymieing Lash of the Whip*
Ayato took the early lead in the second game with an air force that included [Insatiable Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insatiable+Harpy), [Blood-Toll Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood-Toll+Harpy) and [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin). At 12 life, [Fleecemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleecemane+Lion) again came to Kelvin's rescue with the favour of [Nimbus Naiad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimbus+Naiad).


After Kelvin summoned [Vulpine Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulpine+Goliath), Ayato made the lethal error of bestowing [Cavern Lampad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Lampad) on [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin). Ayato was holding Lash of Whip in hand, but chose not to first deal with [Vulpine Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulpine+Goliath). Neither did he bestow the Lampad on lifelinking [Insatiable Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insatiable+Harpy) for fear of Kelvin having a removal spell.


This mistake cost Ayato dearly, for Kelvin was then able to bestow [Hopeful Eidolon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hopeful+Eidolon) on the Lion for his own 7/7 flying, lifelink, hexproof and indestructible creature and attack with Lion and Fox to turn the race in his favour with life totals at 15-6.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl14/r11_ayato.jpg)*Ayato trying to find an out against a 7/7 lifelink flying hexproof Lion*
Ayato attempted to remedy his error by bestowing [Thassa's Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Emissary) on [Insatiable Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insatiable+Harpy), but it was too little too late as Kelvin simply bestowed [Nylea's Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%27s+Emissary) on [Fleecemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleecemane+Lion) to seal the game.


**Kelvin Chew wins 2-0 and remains in contention for top 8.**

Round 11 Feature Match – Shouta Yasooka (R/G) vs Jack Teo (U/W)
---------------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

The last two undefeated players of the tournament, Yasooka and Teo, stared each other down across the feature match table. Yasooka led, but Teo had the first action with a [Favored Hoplite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Favored+Hoplite). Yasooka summoned a [Sedge Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedge+Scorpion) on his third turn, threatening shenanigans on his block, but seeming happy to let his creatures trade. Teo followed up with a [Triton Shorethief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triton+Shorethief), while Yasooka had a an [Arena Athlete](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena+Athlete). A [Leonin Snarecaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Snarecaster) tapped down the Athlete for at turn, but when Teo rumbled in to attack Yasooka had a [Feral Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Invocation). The tempo swing from Yasooka's trick and follow-up bestowed [Leafcrown Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leafcrown+Dryad) proved too much for Teo's aggressive-but-low-curve deck to keep up with, and both players moved to their sideboards in short order.


It says something about the blistering pace of the first game that both players had sideboarded, shuffled and presented their decks before 5 minutes had elapsed in the round. Impressive!


**Shouta Yasooka 1 - Jack Teo 0**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl14/r11_yasooka.jpg)The second game started out with both players matching small creatures, until Teo began to pull ahead with a [Daxos of Meletis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daxos+of+Meletis). He was able to negotiate his way around Yasooka's growing ground army with some assistance from an Ephera's Warden. After a few turns of careful growth on both sides and attacks from Teo, Yasooka found and summoned a [Stoneshock Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneshock+Giant), threatening to blow out Teo's hard-won advantage with a massive attack.


Both players stalled out, Yasooka hunting for his final land to activate the Giant's monstrous ability, and Teo for some more ways to punch through damage before it was too late. Both players grew their armies, but Teo pulled ahead with a [Triton Tactics](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triton+Tactics) on a pair of [Battlewise Hoplite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Hoplite)s on a full-team strike. Yasooka still couldn't find his final land and was unable to race back effectively before Teo took the second game.


**Jack Teo 1 - Shouta Yasooka 1**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl14/r11_teo.jpg)**Game 3**


Teo started things off with a [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin) and an [Ordeal of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordeal+of+Thassa), signaling his aggressive intent from the get-go. Yasooka had a satyr Rambler and a [Nessian Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nessian+Courser), and, facing down an increasingly huge [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin), decided to suit up his Satyr with a Leafcrown Druid and attacking, turning the final game into a straight-out race.


Yasooka decided to make things more challenging for Teo by summoning [Xenagos, the Reveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+the+Reveler), who promptly helped him to clog the board. Teo, however, found an [Ordeal of Heliod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordeal+of+Heliod) for his [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin), instantly trading in the enchantment for a +1/+1 counter and a valuable life buffer. Yasooka, unable to deal with the [Vaporkin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaporkin), was unable to get through for the damage he needed and scooped up his cards.


Jack Teo advances to Round 12 as the only undefeated player left at Grand Prix: Kuala Lumpur!


**Jack Teo 2 - Shouta Yasooka 1**
