Deck Tech: Bant Mastery with Craig Wescoe
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on April 18, 2015 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Olle Rade










Last weekend we saw the debut of Dragons of Tarkir in Standard and some decks really stood out and raised a few eyebrows. The field was filled with aggressive mono red decks and Dragonlords of all possible colors. One deck that caught the eyes of both players and coverage staff was Craig Wescoe's innovative take on White/Green/Blue, or Bant as the shard is more known as. Wescoe piloted the deck to an 8-2 record and a 10th place for his efforts, and he's in Krakow this weekend with the same deck, with some quite interesting changes.  



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpkra15/Craig-Wescoe.jpg)



The core of the deck is a green-white aggressive build with cheap and efficient threats like [Fleecemane Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleecemane+Lion), [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor), [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix) and [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector). In Brussels Wescoe also played the powerful [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai), but where most people seem to have added more dragons to their decks, Wescoe has opted to cut his altogether.  





Normally you would expect that a deck with cheap green creatures will run out of steam in the middle or late game, and be powerless against the “larger” decks' more expensive threats. Not to mention sweepers like [Crux of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crux+of+Fate) or [Perilous Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Vault). But according to Wescoe his deck actually wants to get to the late game.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpkra15/Boardstate.jpg)


“Once you get to the late game, the deck is so versatile and you have so many options with your spells. And creatures like [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor) and [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) work really well together,” he says.
