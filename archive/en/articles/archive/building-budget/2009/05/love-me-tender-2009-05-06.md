Love Me Tender
==============



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on May 6, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Hello, and welcome to another edition of Building on a Budget. I'm glad last week's article was so well received. I will make a conscious effort to keep the columns fun, interesting, and innovative from this point forward.

Remember those cards I [previewed a few weeks back](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/post-fixing-2009-04-16)? The mana-fixing artifacts? Yeah, these things.

| --- | --- |
| Mistvein Borderpost | Wildfield Borderpost |

I've been working on a pretty nice budget deck the past few weeks and I'm proud to share it with all of you. The Borderposts are an exciting new way to fix and accelerate our mana. When I previewed them initially I had discussed using them in a deck that included [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender). Having a single nongreen Borderpost in play makes [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) tap for three mana. The Borderposts even make infinite mana on turn three a possibility. (Turn one [Veinfire Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veinfire+Borderpost), turn two [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender), turn three tap the land to play [Fieldmist Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fieldmist+Borderpost), tap [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) to play [Umbral Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle), equip the Mantle, and use the two mana left over and the [Veinfire Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veinfire+Borderpost) to untap [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender). Repeat to make infinite mana.)



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Bloom Tender | Umbral Mantle |

One of the big problems with the list I talked about a few weeks ago was its ability to actually draw a [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender). I had included [Elvish Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Harbinger) to find the [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) and help fix my mana, but most people thought that was too clunky. (I agree.)

*Alara Reborn* bailed me out, though. [Violent Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Outburst) is a card that caught my attention quickly.

You can abuse the cascade spells that costs three to play in a lot of ways. Simply build a deck that only runs one playset of cards that costs less than three.

In formats like Extended you can guarantee that you play a [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis). (You can even accelerate to your cascade spell with [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide)) You've presumably built your deck around this interaction, and the resolved [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) will assuredly win you the game right away. (Piles of Akromas, Dragons, and Demons tend to do that.)



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Hypergenesis | Simian Spirit Guide |

In Standard we can use them to guarantee putting a [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) into play. It isn't as exciting as [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis), but [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) seems to be sold out everywhere.

![Lavalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lavalanche)  
Another card from the new set that makes this deck a lot better is [Lavalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavalanche). We don't want to play cards like [Mind Spring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) or [Mind Shatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Shatter) anymore because their converted mana cost is less than three. I don't want to whiff when I play a [Violent Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Outburst). [Lavalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavalanche) replaces these cards as a win condition quite nicely.

I'll start by explaining each of the cards in the deck.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab37_cross1.jpg)  
**[Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender):** The first combo piece. [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) can create infinite mana or just attack for infinite damage. It's very important to play a full four of these to maximize your chances of drawing one.

**[Umbral Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle):** The next piece of the puzzle. This piece of equipment lets your [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) do very scary things. Don't leave home without a full four.

"I have infinite mana now. What am I going to do with it?"

**[Lavalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavalanche):** I like dealing arbitrarily large amounts of damage to my opponent. What's even better is dealing that damage to all their creatures too. It's important to note that [Lavalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavalanche) is actually quite good on its own. If you have five mana at your disposal you can cast a one-sided [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm). Sometimes you just need to pay four and kill all of your opponent's 1/1 tokens. This is a very powerful card that I expect to increase in value steadily once people realize how good it is. I'd pick up three or four while they're still cheap.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab37_cross2.jpg)  
**[Violent Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Outburst):** In our deck, it's an instant-speed [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender).

**[Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor):** Sometimes you just need to find something. Whether it's an [Umbral Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle) or a [Lavalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavalanche), you're always happy to have a tutor.

**[Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks):** We need a way to survive until we combo. [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) is a very good way to slow our opponents down.

**[Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk):** See [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)' explanation.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab37_cross3.jpg)  
**[Jund Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm):** Sometimes you just need another board sweeper.

**Borderposts:** These power up our [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) and fix our mana.

**Vivid lands:** The Borderposts are great, but this deck needs to make a lot of different mana symbols.

If you do the math, the deck has:

13 green sources  
 10 blue sources  
 13 black sources  
 12 red sources  
 12 white sources

Pretty good, eh?

I was lucky enough to get accepted into *Alara Reborn* Beta on **Magic Online**. So I took the deck for a test drive in the casual room there.

I didn't get a chance to build a sideboard for the deck for a two reasons: First, we don't know what the metagame will look like after *Alara Reborn*. Second, we don't want to use cards that cost two or less because it will mess with our [Violent Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Outburst) plan, so deciding on sideboard cards is extraordinarily difficult. Instead of giving you a bad sideboard, I'd rather leave the sideboard as an open-ended question for you guys to sort out depending on your given metagame.

### Round 1 vs. Red-Green Elves

**Game 1**

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab37_wrens.jpg)I win the die roll and keep [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), [Veinfire Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veinfire+Borderpost), [Firewild Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firewild+Borderpost), [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), [Violent Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Outburst), and [Umbral Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle). I play my [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and a [Veinfire Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veinfire+Borderpost) and pass the turn. My opponent plays a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and passes the turn back. I replay my [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), and bounce it again for a freshly drawn [Wildfield Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildfield+Borderpost). My opponent attacks for 1 and plays a [Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher) revealing [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). I draw [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), play my [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) again, and tap out for [Umbral Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle). My opponent attacks for 4 and plays another [Vanquish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vanquish)er showing me the same card. I draw a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), play a land, and bounce the [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) for the other Borderpost before passing. My opponent draws a third land and taps [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and three lands for [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). It cascades into [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect), and he attacks me for 12 and passes the turn with me at 3 life. I play [Violent Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Outburst) on his end step to put [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) into play.
