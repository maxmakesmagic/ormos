Round 5 Feature Match: Lau, Sam Lei Kang vs. Onoda, Michihisa
=============================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**







![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Jun-Wei-2.jpg)
By Jun-Wei Hew











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPSING01/970.jpg)Sam Lau is one of Singapore's more accomplished players, having won the Singapore National Championships back in 1999, and finishing second at this year's Nationals. Incidentally, he is also the Grand Prix-Singapore defending champion, after his Trix deck took out Jonathan Chan's Sped Red last year. He faces Michihisa Onoda of Japan, former Japanese National Champion and current high school student.


Winning the die roll, Onoda chose to play first. Starting the game with an [Urborg Volcano](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Volcano), it looked like he was playing one of the UBR Void or Crosis-Go decks. However, as it turned out, he was playing an R-B-g deck, given away by his [Llanowar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes). Onoda played out a [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) on his second turn. 


On Lau's turn, he thought for a bit between Firing the [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) while Onoda couldn't regenerate, or playing a [Gaea's Skyfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Skyfolk). In the end, he decided to cast a [Gaea's Skyfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Skyfolk), which met up with a [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate). The [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) visited for a point of damage. It didn't help that out of the four lands Lau had in play, three of them were of the *Apocalypse* painland variety. Thus, Lau took two more points of damage from his [Yavimaya Coast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Coast)s in order to counter Onoda's [Crypt Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Angel) with a [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake).


Deciding that now was the time to rid himself of the [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), Lau Fired the Familiar and Onoda, and attacked with the Snake, which soon met up with a second [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) from Onoda while Lau did not have any free mana. An end step [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) gave Lau the option of either [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) and [Blurred Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blurred+Mongoose), or [Kavu Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kavu+Titan), [Yavimaya Coast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Coast) and Island.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPSING01/971.jpg)Choosing the bigger pile, Lau then decided to go for it, and played the [Kavu Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kavu+Titan) with kicker. Onoda drew, and tried to steal back the game with a [Yawgmoth's Agenda](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Agenda), giving him access to his two [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate)s- an advantage, thanks to his depleted hand. During Lau's upkeep, Onoda tried to [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) the [Kavu Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kavu+Titan), but Lau had a [Repulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) ready. A [Thunderscape Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderscape+Battlemage) with the black kicker stole back the card advantage Lau had garnered from the [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), which was soon Fired away. 


Lau then replayed his [Kavu Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kavu+Titan), once again with the kicker. This time, the second [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) took care of it for good. A [Crypt Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Angel) brought back no companionship from the graveyard, but it was a threat nonetheless, since there were only two [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar)s in the graveyard. Lau had another [Kavu Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kavu+Titan) at the ready, and it soon entered play.


A second [Thunderscape Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderscape+Battlemage) proved its worth, taking away the remaining two cards from Lau's hand ([Repulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) and [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt)), and destroying the [Yawgmoth's Agenda](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Agenda) via [Llanowar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes), freeing Onoda of its one spell a turn drawback. Lau went into topdeck mode, peeling a [Blurred Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blurred+Mongoose), which blocked the [Thunderscape Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderscape+Battlemage). However, another [Crypt Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Angel) arrived, and revived the Battlemage, ready and waiting to tear out even more cards from Lau's hand. 


Onoda attacked with the Angel and a [Phyrexian Rager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Rager). At this point, Lau had the kicked Titan and a [Gaea's Skyfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Skyfolk) in play. The life totals stood at 11 to 7 in Lau's favor. Sensing the means to remove the Titan, Lau decided not to block. His life total fell down to 6. Onoda then revealed the two remaining cards in his card: [Thunderscape Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderscape+Battlemage) and [Shivan Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Zombie). When Lau attacked with both his creatures, the Titan was chump-blocked by the Battlemage. The trample damage, in combination with the [Gaea's Skyfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Skyfolk) dealt only 5 damage to Onoda. Seeing no way out of the situation, Lau conceded the first game.


Onoda 1 - Lau 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPSING01/972.jpg)In a ritual well known to all tournament players, both players instinctively reached for their sideboards to further hone and tune their deck against the other. Michihisa brought out two [Thunderscape Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderscape+Battlemage)s, one [Phyrexian Rager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Rager) and one [Crypt Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Angel), bringing in a pair of [Slay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slay)s and a pair of [Flametongue Kavu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu)s. Lau took out two [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), one [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt), one [Blurred Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blurred+Mongoose) and one Fire/Ice. He replaced those cards with [Flametongue Kavu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu)s and [Jungle Barrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Barrier)s.


Lau was forced to take a Paris mulligan in the second game, which he did with no hesitation. His second hand was not much better, lacking any two drops that are so important in this format, but having two lands, a [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) and a [Flametongue Kavu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu). Lau chose to keep this hand. Onoda, on the other hand, had a two drop of his own, a [Shivan Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Zombie). It was eventually [Repulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)d, then [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude)d. 


However, Lau's fourth land proved to be very evasive. With his land mix consisting of Forest, [Yavimaya Coast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Coast) and [Shivan Reef](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Reef), not only was Lau limited to his reaction capabilities, but his painlands were slowly killing him each and every time he removed one of Onoda's threats. This included Excluding the aforementioned [Shivan Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Zombie), or countering a [Crypt Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Angel) with a [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake). In total, the lands dealt some 11 damage to Lau! 


[Skizzik](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skizzik) came into play while Lau was caught tapped out, and reduced Lau's life total down to just three. When Lau [Urza's Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Rage)d the [Skizzik](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skizzik), his life total dropped to two. When a [Pyre Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyre+Zombie) was returned from the graveyard and replayed, a [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt) on it cost Lau another life point. In the end, Onoda reached the magical nine-land barrier and sacrificed the [Pyre Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyre+Zombie) to finish the job.


Final Result: Onoda 2 - Lau 0
