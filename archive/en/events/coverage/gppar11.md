Sharfman Triumphs in Paris
==========================




February 11-12, 2011












Grand Prix Paris 2011
---------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppar11/trophy_shot.jpg)It was the biggest weekend in **Magic** history! First, the unfinished business of last year's Player of the Year race was finally finished, when Brad Nelson emerged victorious against Guillaume Matignon to take the title. Then there was the Pro Tour where after three days of fierce competition and in a star-studded Top 8 Ben Stark defeated Paul Rietzl to hoist the trophy.


And finally there was the Grand Prix … Grand Prix Paris 2011 was the largest Limited tournament of all time. 2,182 players started the tournament on Saturday morning and fought through nine grueling rounds of Sealed play. 279 made the cut, played the extra round on Saturday and returned on Sunday for *Mirrodin Besieged / Scars of M*irrodin booster draft. And once again, the cream rose to the top, highlighted by German Juggernaut Kai Budde who made his fifteenth (!) GP Top 8. But that was all he made. This weekend belonged to the Americans. All three trophies went to the US.


In the GP it was first-time Top-8er David Sharfman from Florida who left his mark on **Magic** history. He drafted a strong blue-green deck with [Thopter Assembly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) as his only bomb, but with two [Volition Reins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volition+Reins) to grab his opponents' big cards. He also used three bounce spells, two [Quicksilver Geyser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Geyser) and one [Disperse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disperse) , to take full advantage of his [Viridian Corrupter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Corrupter) . This strategy proved succesful in crushing his opposition: 2-0 followed 2-0 and only in the finals did he lose his first game, though he still came out on top.


Congratulations to David Sharfman who bested 2,181 players to become the champion of Grand Prix–Paris 2011!

Feature – Kai Budde Drafts for Top 8
------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe* 

Kai Budde has not shown many moments of vulnerability in, well, in his entire **Magic** career – but the first draft here in Grand Prix Paris was one of them. Having emerged on 10-0 from the first day's play Kai fell to an immediate 0-2 record and was a match away from being dumped out of the tournament. The best players thrive under pressure, and Kai turned it around to finish his first pod 1-2, then roared on to a 3-0 win his second draft pod.


As we entered the Top 8 draft there was no doubt that the eyes were on Kai, but the question was which Budde would turn up – the one who struggled through his first draft, or the one who went 3-0 in his second?


**Pack One**


Kai began with a statement of intent, taking the first card he saw...


[Kuldotha Flamefiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Flamefiend) , which he chose over [Burn the Impure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burn+the+Impure)  



Kai's intention was clear – Green-Red, lots of muscle, lots of raw power. The German [Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juggernaut) planned on bulldozing a path to the title.


[Mirran Mettle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Mettle)  
[Glissa's Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glissa%27s+Courier)  
[Dross Ripper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Ripper)  
[Pistus Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pistus+Strike)  
[Unnatural Predation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unnatural+Predation)  
[Concussive Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concussive+Bolt)  
[Frantic Search](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frantic+Search)

**Pack Two**


[Strata Scythe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strata+Scythe) was a strong pick from a pack that had a [Barrage Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barrage+Ogre) , [Rust Tick](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rust+Tick) , and [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest)

[Alpha Tyrranax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alpha+Tyrranax) – not often a fourth pick, but the only playable in green or red.  
[Panic Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panic+Spellbomb)

**Pack Three**


[Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine) – the bomb removal rare that can make grown men cry. Slam!  
[Horizon Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Spellbomb) – perhaps suprisingly preferred to a [Darksteel Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Axe) . For a second time there were three Infect creatures in a single booster.

[Ichorclaw Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr)

[Turn Aside](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+Aside)


And that was a wrap... as Kai built his deck the strengths and weaknesses became clear. The strength was, well... it was strength. Pure muscle power – bunching around four, five and six mana he had beatsticks aplenty. The weakness – what happened before turn four, which quite possibly wasn't much.


"I have plenty of quality cards, but my curve is terrible – it's not my fault though I just couldn't have done anything to fix it. There were no Myrs, nothing like that. It's actually not that bad I dont think. I have lots of random combinations that are quite powerful... a lot of synergies that could be pretty strong... [Molder Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Beast) and Firelord. If you look at all my draft decks they really clustered around 4 and 5 mana, but its not deliberate – I'm first-picking things that cost 3".


The good cards for me seemed bunched as well, like I got a first pick [Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine) from the third pack, then nothing, then a [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) and [Spikeshot Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Elder) at the same time, so I kept having to pass good cards on. The first pack first pick was the same, I passed on something like 4 or 5 good cards to everyone else.


I just have to hope my opponents don't come out quickly – if we get into the late game my guys should be bigger."

Quarterfinals – Dimitris Davios vs. David Sharfman
--------------------------------------------------


*by Marc Calderaro* 

Floridian David Sharfman has been around this block before. Though it's his first Grand Prix Top 8, he's finished in the Top 16 multiple times, and almost got there at US Nationals. He's drafted a fun, fun blue-green build with multiple [Volition Reins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volition+Reins) , [Bellowing Tanglewurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bellowing+Tanglewurm) s, [Quicksilver Geyser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Geyser) s and the pièce de résistance, a [Thopter Assembly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) . His only problem? "I have no mana myr. At all." Looking at his deck, 2-drop acceleration was indeed a glaring omission. "I passed three [Plague Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Stinger) s pack three. I don't want to face that guy."


Dimitris Davios, hailing from Athens, Greece, may not have all three [Plague Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Stinger) s, but he's got one. He didn't want to go infect, but the cards just kept coming. And when a late second-pack [Putrefax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefax) was gifted to him, he had little choice in the matter. It's Davios' second Grand Prix and his first time in the lights. He told me this is only his second time drafting infect, so it'll be different for him, but his cards certainly have the ability to punish Sharfman's lack of early plays.


 


### Game 1


Davios led off with [Fume Spitter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fume+Spitter) , [Ichorclaw Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr) , and an [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest) on Sharfman's [Perilous Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr) . The American returned in kind with a [Viridian Corrupter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Corrupter) aimed right at the Ichorclaw's icky claws. When the [Viridian Corrupter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Viridian%2BCorrupter)er was sent into the red zone the next turn, Davios blocked, then sacrificed the [Fume Spitter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fume+Spitter) and tried a [Virulent Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Virulent+Wound) to finish off the twisted Elf. But Sharfman had a [Disperse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disperse) to not only prevent the poison counter, but also allow him to re-use the powerful green uncommon.


[Sky-Eel School](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky-Eel+School) came down and attacked against Davios' empty board netting his first damage, then added insult to injury with his big ol' [Thopter Assembly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) . Davios was already low on gas and merely added a [Plague Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Myr) to respond to the flying threats.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppar11/sharfman_2.jpg)*David Sharfman*
After making his first five flyers, Sharfman simply recast the Assembly. Davios untapped and drew. He thought long and hard, and then he scooped into Game 2.


David Sharfman 1, Dimitris Davios 0


Sharfman thought it ok to strain his mana base for the [Arc Trail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail) he had originally cut, and asked the judge for a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) . The Trail is so good against infect, Sharfman knew it was worth it. And plus, he had the [Horizon Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Spellbomb) for additional virtual copies of the land.


 


### Game 2


Davios thought it best to have the extra card, so he made Sharfman play first. However, it was the Grecian who cast the first two spells of the game – an [Origin Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Origin+Spellbomb) and [Ichorclaw Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr) . It seems Sharfman's [Viridian Corrupter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Corrupter) had something against that clawed Myr, as it pointed directly at the Myr again and sent it packing.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppar11/davios_2.jpg)*Dimitris Davios*
Sharfman continued the infect route with a [Tel-Jilad Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tel-Jilad+Fallen) , and swung in to get Davios to 5 poison counters. The Corrupter traded with a new [Corpse Cur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Cur) in the process, but Sharfman replaced it with a [Blight Mamba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blight+Mamba) . This was hilarious only to Sharfman, because he had drawn every single infect creature in his deck. But Davios had no way of knowing that. More blockers were made for Davios and more blockers died with -1/-1 counters. The [Origin Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Origin+Spellbomb) token, and a [Plague Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Myr) binned; they were replaced by a [Plague Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Stinger) .


Sharfman threatened with another [Thopter Assembly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) , but this time Davios was ready with a timely [Revoke Existence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence) . The board was looking to settle with Davios at 8 poison counters, but no way for Sharfman to get his 3/1 protection from artifacts through. That is, until he cast his [Bellowing Tanglewurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bellowing+Tanglewurm) . The Wurm roared loudly into the night, ending Davios' Top 8 run.


David Sharfman 2, Dimitris Davios 0


David Sharfman advances to the Semifinals!

Huttenberger's only hope was to find creatures that ar least had some toughness to them – a [Strandwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strandwalker) , and [Kuldotha Forgemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Forgemaster) would suffice.

Finals – William Lowry vs. David Sharfman
-----------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe* 

An All-American finish to Grand Prix Paris, after an All-American finish to Pro Tour Paris and an American winner of the Player of the Year showdown – this **Magic** Weekend was a resounding success for Team USA, whoever proved the victor in this showdown, and they would be returning with every piece of silverware available. All that was left to be decided was in whose hands the Grand Prix trophy would be flying back across the Atlantic


William Lowry took the first turn, but it began without much ceremony – a [Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flight) Spellbomnb and Throne Gex were hardly killer plays. Sharfman had no responses to this so Lowry followed up with his [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) , which Sharfman took the chance to read. Lowry could only hope that Sharfman didn't realise quite how power the 'Plasm had been so far in the Top 8 – it was only masquerading as a 2/2 [Gray Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Ogre) for the time being.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppar11/final1.jpg)[Glissa's Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glissa%27s+Courier) was the first outing from Sharfman's deck, which the [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) duly copied, gaining a [Strider Harness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strider+Harness) to attack to four, before Sharfman played his next creature – a [Tel-Jilad Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tel-Jilad+Fallen) . Lowry steadied his board with a [Clone Shell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone+Shell) , then when Sharfman played a [Sky-Eel School](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky-Eel+School) Lowry simply upgraded his [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) once again. The combination of [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) and [Strider Harness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strider+Harness) was a powerful one – whatever the best creature was in play, the [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) was a copy of that creature but with +1/+1!


Just a second – was that a copy of a copy? No – William Lowry actually had two [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) s – that was two [Sky-Eel School](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky-Eel+School) s. This was just brutal – the harder Sharfman fought to defend himself, the better he made Lowry's deck... TIMES TWO! The duplicate Eels struck home, putting Lowry to 6 life, while Lowry still sat healthily on 14.


A [Quicksilver Geyser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Geyser) offered Sharfman hope, returning both the [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) s to hand – that cleared the way for his creatures to attack and he knocked Lowry down to 9, adding a [Thopter Assembly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) . The [Quicksilver Geyser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Geyser) has turned the tide of the match – Lowry couldn't rely on his [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) s to copy the [Thopter Assembly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) . He played a [Palladium Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palladium+Myr) and equipped it with the [Strider Harness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strider+Harness) , but a turn later succumbed to the swarm of Thopters that headed across the table to him


William Lowry 0 – 1 David Sharfman

A game down despite drawing both his [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) s, Lowry raced onto the offensive in a hurry to get back into the match. An [Embersmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Embersmith) was joined by an [Iron Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr) and [Rusted Relic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rusted+Relic) while across the table David Sharfman watched impassively behind a row of land and nothing else. William Lowry 1 – 1 David Sharfman

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppar11/f_william_lowry.jpg)*William Lowry*
