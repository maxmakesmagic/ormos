![Grizzly Bears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grizzly+Bears)*Shards of Alara* sees a return of one of **Magic**'s most favorite creatures: [Grizzly Bears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears)! Of course, a quick review of [Gatherer](http://ww2.wizards.com/gatherer/index.aspx?) will reveal there is no actual card named [Grizzly Bears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears) in the set, so what's the deal? The phrase "Grizzly Bears" is actually a term of endearment for any 2/2 creature that costs 1C, a generic mana and a specific colored mana. The term dates back all the way to Alpha where the actual [Grizzly Bears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears) saw print; from that point onwards any creature that was functionally identical to the Bears was referred to by the same name. Players often use [Gray Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Ogre) and [Hill Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hill+Giant) to refer to 2/2s for 2C and 3/3s for 3C in a similar manner. So, welcome to [Cylian Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cylian+Elf), *Shards of Alara*'s very own [Grizzly Bears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears), and enjoy this deck which features every "Grizzly Bear" the game has ever seen!
