Junk Walkers
============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 14, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











Planeswalkers have lent themselves to interesting strategies ever since they were printed. They have such a unique impact on the game and survive so many sweepers that there are a lot of ways to abuse them. One of the popular strategies for Planeswalkers has always been to team a bunch of them up and try to get multiple going at the same time. Each Planeswalker has an ability that profits something equal to a card each turn, so when multiple walkers are activating every turn, things can get out of hand. We have seen a little bit of Esper Walkers from time to time, but nothing with green in it...

![Ajani, Caller of the Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ajani%2C+Caller+of+the+Pride)Of course, someone had to change that, and Tacks decided to be that person. Putting up a perfect record in a recent Daily Event on **Magic Online**, Tacks decided to make sure green got its dues. But first, a walker list wouldn't be anything without walkers. Tacks has a package that includes [Ajani, Caller of the Pride](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Caller+of+the+Pride); [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless); and [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad). None of them appear in particularly high numbers, but that is because Tacks had to make room for all kinds of nuts and bolts that really give this deck a unique feel.

Harmless cards like [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary), which will usually just draw a card and chump block something, become a lot scarier when [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) begins returning it each turn or Ajani starts ticking up its size. Other synergies push the deck over the top in the late game as well. [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) plus [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) can be just dirty in most situations. [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) turns most of your walkers into machine guns, adding to the versatility of them even further. This deck can do a little bit of just about everything, making it an awesome pick for a more open metagame like Friday Night **Magic**. Because honestly, how long do you think they could really keep Garruk away from the party?
