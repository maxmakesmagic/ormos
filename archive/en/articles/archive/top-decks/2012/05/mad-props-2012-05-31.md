Mad Props
=========



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 31, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






This week on Top Decks, we will talk about a couple of different tournaments... and give mad props (up to and including mad props given by other props-granting entities or Pro Tour Champions) as we examine the further development of *Avacyn Restored* in both small and Standard format sizes.

First up, [Grand Prix Anaheim](/en/events/coverage/x-marcs-spot) and *Innistrad* Block Constructed. Let's look at how the Top 8 shook out; or, these are the archetypes to which we will apply mad props:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td197_lalague.jpg)*Marc Lalague*Now on the subject of mad props, the Tournament Center coverage for Grand Prix Anaheim had Lalague's finals opponent Paul Rietzl complimenting the eventual winner's straight green-red as the best deck of the tournament. No small praise coming from one of the Pro Tour's most successful beatdown success stories... and a man who came within inches of taking this tournament as well.

So what made Marc Lalague's "best deck" big winner tick, exactly?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td197_daw.jpg)[Dawntreader Elk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk) | Art by [John Avon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22John+Avon%22%5D)

This is a relatively straightforward attack deck. We have some haste between [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider), set up to put the opponent on his or her heels.

And while many have made mention of Lalague's two-colorness, the fact is, his mana consistency seems to be among the best in the *Innistrad* Block Constructed format. [Abundant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth) on one, [Dawntreader Elk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk) on two, [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) on three... and [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) on no mana at all could help the Anaheim champion line up whatever color was needed.

| --- | --- |

In terms of the powerhouse cards—your [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned), or [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts), or even [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=247419)—these are cards that we have, for the most part, seen helping various other multicolor red decks succeed in *Innistrad* Block; Marc just had them more mana-consistently and possibly bigger, due to his ramp sub-theme.

That said, he had one more big thing going for his big winner: [Dawntreader Elk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk), both as a defender and a catalyst.

If you review the semifinal coverage, Lalague's opponent Noah Koessel lamented his drawing three Elks in the first game. If you are a beatdown deck, a halfway mana-efficient 2/2 for two that can block—even chump block—with no loss of card advantage is exactly the kind of card you *don't* want to see on the other side of the table. It is kind of like a [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) that can also get its beat on.



|  |  |

Now, there is one other thing that [Dawntreader Elk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk) provided that [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) never had the opportunity to: morbid.

[Dawntreader Elk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk) is an awesome way to set up a 5-point [Brimstone Volley](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley). Nothing like getting exactly what you want... and profiting a little bit extra along the way.

They just had their cards in different places; for example, all of Kibler's [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) were in his sideboard, whereas Rosellini played all of his in the main.

Jund in *Innistrad* Block—these incarnations in any case—is a con-fusion of powerful cards and high-quality midrange creatures. Both Rosellini and Kibler used [Scorned Villager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorned+Villager) to accelerate from two to four and had a fairly heavy four-point on the curve... [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat), and [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) (Kibler played all four).

How can we sum up the Jund? It seems like a deck that just has tons and tons of the best cards in the format... [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned), [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) (Jason played four copies of both of these in his main), plus tons of removal. Rosellini could even win with [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run).

Both of them could pay the "iron price" for the opponent's permanents, stealing wins as they stole permanents with [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td197_froehlich.jpg)*Eric Froehlich*Froehlich played an update to Alexander "Insane" Hayne's White-Blue Miracles deck. As some mages predicted for the evolution of the format post-Pro Tour, the Miracles deck went red.

I think if you posed such a switch to most players, they would have automatically assumed [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) as a four-of; not only did Froehlich run just the one, his red splashes where relatively sedate. Sure, he could get much faster on defense with sideboarded Pillars of Flame, but his main functionality improvements (from red) weren't even based on miracles themselves!

**[Rolling Temblor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor)**

While the front side of [Rolling Temblor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) is both sub-[Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) and sub-[Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake), flashback is an extremely powerful mechanic. This card gives the miracles shell even more tools for fighting off the early game beatdown. Remember, the late game basically *belongs* to miracles!

**[Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight)**

Big. Different. Finisher. Other teams actually built their whole strategies around this creature (main deck). Running Gisela out of the board when you are a creatureless deck starting is safer against [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) decks (they are not automatically prompted to side in their all-star fives). And when she is on? It's often quickly over.

Interesting things of note:


> 
> * Froehlich maintained Hayne's twenty-seven-land count and added a pair of accelerators.
> * Even with all the new bells and whistles (and actual ways to kill creatures via red cards), Froehlich still played four copies of [Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread).
> * Hayne's sideboarded [Cathedral Sanctifier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Sanctifier)s, though, did not make the update's cut.

So what's different between this deck and the one we saw in Barcelona?

[Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212) is a substantial addition, especially for a deck that wants to hit [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) and might need a solo ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) for the back half of [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212) essentially gobbles up the main deck slot once held by [Cathedral Sanctifier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Sanctifier).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td197_ang.jpg)[Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) | Art by [James Ryman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22James+Ryman%22%5D)

And instead of [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) (big and busty finisher), Hopkins played only [Disciple of Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Griselbrand)! Michael's big reanimator target was therefore limited to [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise); [Disciple of Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Griselbrand) let him sacrifice creatures to set up a bigger [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) trigger, plus generate a huge life advantage at the same time.

| --- | --- |
| Angel of Glory's Rise | Champion of the Parish |

Michael had lots of stuff going on linked to the number of creatures he had in play. The solo [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) can benefit from having lots of humans come into play at the same time. [Geist-Honored Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk) might not look like a big threat by its lonesome, but in this deck, it can certainly masquerade as playing one on TV. Finally, [Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) is potentially cheap (Michael can have lots of little guys in play) and painless ([Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) is just going to get his side of the table back).

All in all, a powerful and fun combo-like deck!

Superficially... a Delver deck. It has the potential speed of a fast beatdown deck (play a flying [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl+) on the first turn), but can play a cerebral tempo/control-ish game. Gerry seems to eschew the [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) + [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681) model, instead bringing back [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine)!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td197_res.jpg)[Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) | Art by [Johannes Voss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Johannes+Voss%22%5D)

Gerry's choice in a non-white/non-blue Sword seems not to have failed him, as he cut through several other Delver decks along the way to the Open win.

There are no substantial token-production cards in this deck; rather, a switch to [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), plus the return of [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) to the big tables.
