Doeszzet Resolve?
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 25, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Oddly enough, there aren't nearly enough cards in Standard that deal with both [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) and [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections). At least, there aren't enough obvious ones. Sometimes you have to dig a little deeper and come out with something like [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm). 

![Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Izzet+Charm) Chris Yarbrough is well known to his friends as a control player, so it wouldn't surprise me if finding [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) wasn't exactly an "aha!" moment and instead he played the deck and card just because it's versatile and he likes it. Then again, he has a pair of [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion)s main deck alongside a [Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence), so it looked like he was being particularly hateful toward Mono-Black Devotion. Either way, [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) complements that plan nicely. 

 One thing I love about Chris's deck is the use of [Brimaz, King of Oreskos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos) alongside [Stormbreath Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbreath+Dragon) in the sideboard. Siding in Brimaz against the control mirror is all right, but when you're on that plan, you really want more than three or four threats total. If your opponent finally deals with your first threat when low on life, you're going to want a follow-up. 

 As far as WUx Control decks go, this version looks to be among the best in *Born of the Gods* Standard.
