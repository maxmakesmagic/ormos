Like playing gigantic red monsters far earlier than you should be able to? Then try James Mink's Dragon Stompy build! He attempts to do exactly that, using [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song), [City of Traitors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors), and [Ancient Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb) (amongst others) to drop [Arc-Slogger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc-Slogger) on the first or second turn. Of course, when that plan fails, he's happy to just plop a disruptive spell like [Magus of the Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Moon), [Blood Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon), or [Trinisphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinisphere) into play to disrupt his opponent until he *can*. To read more about James and the Legacy format, [visit the official web coverage from Grand Prix-Chicago](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/coverage-grand-prix-chicago-2009-03-07).
