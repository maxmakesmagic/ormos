TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------


Huang Hao-Shan has successfully kept the trophy on local soil and is your Grand Prix Taipei 2014 Champion! 


The Team MTG Mint Card Pro powered through the Top 8 with his White Blue deck featuring Soul of Ravnica, Spirit Bonds and Haunted Plate Mail to defeat finalist Lyu Li Ciang, who put up a valiant fight in the finals.


Not to be forgotten are also semifinalists, Grand Prix Bangkok Top 8 competitor Ryan Luo from Singapore and World *Magic* Cup 2012 Champion Cheng Tung-Yi.


The rest of the top 8 was well-represented by players from various regions around Asia, comprising Steven Dirga Setya of Indonesia, Takimura Kazuyuki of Japan, Joe Soh of Malaysia and Shair Chung Ngao of Hong Kong. All-in-all, it was a great ending to an awesome tournament, seeing so many nationalities represented in the Top 8. 


Finally advancing past the quarterfinals match in his 5th lifetime Grand Prix Top 8, it was a sweet ending for Taiwan. Once again, hearty congratulations to Huang Hao-Shan!


We'll see you next week at Pro Tour *Magic 2015* in Portland, where we will see more M15 cards in action.

DAY 1 COVERAGE
--------------


While it was great that a record breaking 852 players came out to play in Taipei today, we just can't have that many people playing on Day 2. 128 is a much nicer number for forming draft pods, otherwise it's all fractions and high school math was soooo long ago for me.


Leading the pack on 9-0 are the Triplicate Spirits known as Kazuyuki Takimura from Japan, Steven Setya from Indonesia, and Chye Yian Hsiang from Malaysia. They're not really Triplicate Spirits, guys, but there are three of them, and there were three copies of Triplicate Spirits between their decks, and the pro players we spoke to earlier in the day were almost unanimous in their opinions that Triplicate Spirits is the best common in *Magic 2015*, so like, gimme a break, ok?


Anyway, snapping at their heels (do Spirits have heels?) and also playing on Day 2 will be Fabian Li, Justin Cheung, Joe Soh, Kelvin Chew, Shota Yasooka, Naoki Shimizu, Yuuta Takahashi, Sherwin Pu, Simon Harnden, Weng Heng Soh, Jun Young Park, Sung-Wook Nam, Hao-Shan Huang, and Tzu-Ching Kuo to name a few. Just missing the cut at 129th is Makahito Mihara. That poor guy just can't catch a break.


So join us tomorrow as we draft up a *Magic 2015* storm! Who will win? We shall see!
