Spotlight Cube Series – Emma Handy's Proliferate Cube
=====================================================



 Posted in **Magic Online**
 on October 13, 2020 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/hero/2018_Daily_WOTC_icon_0.jpg)
By Emma Handy











Howdy, y'all! My name is Emma Handy, and you might know me from the Rivals League, broadcasting and writing for [StarCityGames.com](https://starcitygames.com/), script supervision for Tolarian Community College, or my stream. I wear a ton of hats in the *Magic* community and consume the game in a variety of ways as a result. Cube's no exception, and I'm excited to bring you my latest project: *Proliferate Cube*!


Part of the fun of designing your own cube is getting to cultivate a specific experience. With this cube, my goal was to create high-complexity synergies while placing an emphasis on combat math. How was this accomplished? Every kind of counter under the sun. There are even a few incidental combos thrown in for those who enjoy such effects. Those who are just looking to sink their teeth into the cube list can find the full card list at the bottom of this article. For everyone else, there's a ton going on, so let's dive in!


Archetypes
==========


The archetypes in this cube aren't as set in stone as a theme like tribal, but there are a ton of cards that play much better in specific decks. Rather than encouraging "midrange soup" decks, a major goal of this cube is to have decks that are greater than the sum of their parts. This leads to a bunch of thematic pieces within colors that have a host of applications across archetypes. Looking at what each color is about makes it easier to visualize how the themes can play together and what kinds of combinations can create some seriously messed-up Cube decks with cards that might not look as powerful on paper.


Green Ramp
----------


* [![Avenger of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Avenger+of+Zendikar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar)
* [![Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Joraga+Treespeaker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker)
* [![Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Devoted+Druid)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid)
* [![Genesis Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Genesis+Hydra)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra)

Ramp is one of the archetypes in the cube that people are going to be the most familiar with. Cube all-stars like [Avenger of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) and [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker) are going to make the deck feel like an old friend. The twist? Rather than ramping into [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) and [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), Hydras of all sorts are on the menu. They scale well with the beatdown decks available, and with enough mana, cards like [Genesis Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra) and [Lifeblood Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifeblood+Hydra) get completely out of control.


Red Aggro
---------


* [![Hellspark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hellspark+Elemental)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental)
* [![Bomat Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bomat+Courier)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier)
* [![Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rift+Bolt)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt)
* [![Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Koth+of+the+Hammer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer)

While designing the cube, it was great how many Red Deck Wins all-stars naturally slotted into the themes of the cube without feeling forced. Seriously, if you're trying to get your feet wet, just play a normal red deck. If you wanna lean into synergies, the pool goes so, so deep. [Hellspark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental) sacrificed post-combat to put a counter on [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder)? Nice! [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders) has counters *and* is a beatdown favorite? Same with [Stromkirk Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble)? And [Stormblood Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormblood+Berserker)? And [Soul-Scar Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul-Scar+Mage)? And—the list goes on.


Black Sacrifice
---------------


* [![Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blood+Artist)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist)
* [![Liliana, Heretical Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Liliana%2C+Heretical+Healer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+Heretical+Healer)
* [![Woe Strider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Woe+Strider)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woe+Strider)
* [![Elenda, the Dusk Rose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elenda%2C+the+Dusk+Rose)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elenda%2C+the+Dusk+Rose)

One of the common themes throughout the cube is leveraging on-battlefield advantages, especially sacrificing cards for short-term gains. Interestingly enough, with all the escape, persist, undying, and other recursive creatures available, it's hard to tell if anything of consequence actually ever stays dead. Black's place is an Aristocrats-esque theme that can play best with red and white but has enough cards to stand on its own as a monocolor deck at the right table.


Red Sacrifice
-------------


* [![Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Greater+Gargadon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon)
* [![Mogg War Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mogg+War+Marshal)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal)
* [![Mask of Immolation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mask+of+Immolation)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+Immolation)
* [![The Akroan War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=The+Akroan+War)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Akroan+War)

While red's sacrifice synergies aren't as strong as black's, there are a bunch of cards that are very into the idea of sacrificing raw (digital) cardboard for profitable damage exchanges. Having a handful of [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason)-like effects allows the archetype to play similarly to its Standard counterpart. Enough of the red creatures are going to die anyways, so having ways to get value out of [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders), [Hellspark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental), and so on is gravy.


White Weenie
------------


* [![Grateful Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grateful+Apparition)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grateful+Apparition)
* [![Venerated Loxodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Venerated+Loxodon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venerated+Loxodon)
* [![Basri's Solidarity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Basri%27s+Solidarity)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basri%27s+Solidarity)
* [![Topan Freeblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Topan+Freeblade)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Topan+Freeblade)

White Weenie operates similarly to the [Hardened Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales) archetype, but it goes much, much wider. With a minor token theme, cards like [Basri's Solidarity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basri%27s+Solidarity) are absolutely bananas, and proliferate spells do a great job of upgrading the entire team's power and toughness. [Evolution Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Sage) with fetch lands and white creatures ends the game *fast*.


Green Stompy
------------


* [![Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Experiment+One)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One)
* [![Avatar of the Resolute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Avatar+of+the+Resolute)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+the+Resolute)
* [![Wildborn Preserver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wildborn+Preserver)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildborn+Preserver)
* [![Kalonian Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kalonian+Hydra)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Hydra)

Ramp isn't the only way to play a base-green deck. If a traditional aggro deck is more your speed, there's a whole curve of above-rate green creatures that can beat down. The biggest thing to be cognizant of while drafting this deck is that it's important to find game-ending cards that work early and on into the late game. [Kalonian Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Hydra), [Verdurous Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdurous+Gearhulk), and [Hadana's Climb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hadana%27s+Climb) are great ways to make sure you can get across the finish line against a well-established synergy deck.


Blue Counters
-------------


* [![Thrummingbird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thrummingbird)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrummingbird)
* [![Clockspinning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Clockspinning)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning)
* [![Fate Transfer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fate+Transfer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Transfer)
* [![Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Simic+Manipulator)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator)

The central theme of this cube is counters, and while every other color has an incredibly proactive way to use them, blue is all about messing with counters and weaving an advantage through those interactions. There are ways to spend and steal counters, and there are even counter-centric counterspells in [Ertai's Meddling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ertai%27s+Meddling) and [Delay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delay). Most of blue's counter-manipulation cards aren't going to close a game by themselves, but as a support color, blue is able to play in several different directions. This customizability lends itself to letting players play at their pace and even steal some of the opponent's game pieces along the way.

In the interest of combatting the complexity of the interactions in the cube, blue has access to a traditional Skies archetype that is exactly what it looks like: efficient, evasive creatures with some room for synergies.

It wouldn't be much of a "counters" cube without one of the most iconic +1/+1 counter archetypes in recent history. This Stompy archetype is one of the stronger ones in the cube, easily producing a bunch of 4/4s in short order.


Artifact Aggro
--------------


* [![Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Arcbound+Ravager)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager)
* [![Unlicensed Disintegration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Unlicensed+Disintegration)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration)
* [![Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Master+of+Etherium)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium)
* [![Tezzeret's Touch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tezzeret%27s+Touch)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Touch)

On the topic of iconic aggro strategies, finding a way to incorporate [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) and friends was one of the primary challenges in the cube. The way to go with this is to try and stay open for a little bit before falling into a color pair within Grixis, usually base blue. There's enough mana fixing in artifacts (that can be upgraded by [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) and company!) that it isn't too difficult to base around a single color and lightly touch another color or two to round out a full deck. Red and black's biggest strengths for the archetype are going to be in sacrificing artifacts for bursts of damage via cards like [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) and [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). Their sacrifice cards also play beautifully with the modular mechanic for some tricky mid-combat plays. Make sure to prioritize the more ubiquitous artifact creatures (like [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker)) in pick orders because they go fast!


Tips and Tricks for Drafting
============================


A lot of what's going on in this cube isn't exactly what people are accustomed to when it comes to a normal cubing experience, and it feels appropriate to mention a few points that might be unintuitive to someone who hasn't played with the cards before.


The Mana
--------


* [![Badlands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Badlands)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Badlands)
* [![Blood Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blood+Crypt)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt)
* [![Graven Cairns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Graven+Cairns)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graven+Cairns)
* [![Lavaclaw Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lavaclaw+Reaches)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches)
* [![Bloodstained Mire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodstained+Mire)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodstained+Mire)

For mana, you'll find the usual suspects—dual lands, shock lands, fetch lands, and creature lands, with filter lands thrown into the mix. There are enough impactful cards with multiple pips of the same color in their mana cost that the filter lands are *much* better in this format than they normally are. There are several instances where I would take a filter land over a dual or creature land of the same color.


Proliferate
-----------


* [![Steady Progress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Steady+Progress)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steady+Progress)
* [![Volt Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Volt+Charge)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volt+Charge)
* [![Guildpact Informant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Guildpact+Informant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guildpact+Informant)
* [![Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Contagion+Engine)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine)

In case the name of the cube doesn't beat you over the head with it hard enough, proliferate is the name of the game. The original idea for the cube was "*War of the Spark* Expanded," with sweet proliferate-able mechanics like infect and energy. The size of the cube made those mechanics and planeswalkers hard to balance against, and some of the planeswalker fights felt too close to the *Modern Cube*. Even though the details may have changed, the primary focus on counters, more specifically, mechanics that work with proliferate, hasn't changed.


By the same token, most counters-matter cards have significantly more applications than they normally do in the same way that artifact hate was relevant in *Double Masters*. [Parting Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parting+Thoughts)? Easy Pack 1, Pick 1. Same with [Ion Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ion+Storm).


Combos
------


There are more combos than you think. Many of which are cards that happen to be incidentally good on their own.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Devoted+Druid)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Quillspike)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quillspike) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vizier+of+Remedies)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies)
[Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) has combos with [Quillspike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quillspike) and [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies). Both the latter cards are also just great with persist creatures and get sucked up early as a result. This makes it harder to assemble a ton of combos, but so rewarding when it comes together.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kitchen+Finks)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)
[Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) may be the easiest Pack 1, Pick 1 in the cube, simply because it's easy to build combos with cheap persist creatures. [Wingrattle Scarecrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingrattle+Scarecrow) is much better than it looks for the same reason. Simply find something like [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies) or [Anafenza, Kin-Tree Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit) to pair with a sacrifice outlet, and you're off to the races.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian+Unlife)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Unlife)
Can't lose the game if you can't take infect damage. Just make sure you find a way to end the game before they find a way to kill a [Grizzly Bears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears) or an enchantment.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Power+Conduit)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=History+of+Benalia)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=History+of+Benalia) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elspeth+Conquers+Death)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth+Conquers+Death)
On top of being a low-cost way to constantly move counters around, [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) allows you to repeat chapters on Sagas.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spike+Feeder)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Archangel+of+Thune)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune)
If there's a true "[Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin)" in this format, it's this. Gaining life and making all your creatures bigger for each set of clicks, as many times as you want, is no joke.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Heliod%2C+Sun-Crowned)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%2C+Sun-Crowned) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spike+Feeder)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Walking+Ballista)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista)
Heliod combos in the same ways that he does in Modern and Pioneer. Who knew?


Overall, despite there being a good number of combos in the cube, the fact that they aren't niche cards a la [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) means it's harder to actually see each piece of a combo in a draft. Coupled with the fact that this is a 600-card cube, not a 360 one, these combos will be novelties more frequently rather than format-ruining mainstays of an otherwise mid-power format.


Get Out What You Put In
=======================


The intricacies of this cube go miles deep, and there's so much to learn. Which means that the more reps you get, the more you're going to find and try. The best advice I can offer is this: always work toward ending the game. The threats are strong, and without a ton of sweepers, synergies will overwhelm most removal-heavy decks.


If you like attacking and doing things so whacky they're generally saved for Mental *Magic* and other made-up formats, you're going to love this cube. I know I do, and I couldn't be more excited to share it with the world.
