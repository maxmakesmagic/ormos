A Vault Unleashed
=================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on April 3, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






In my continuing quest to categorize all of [Serious Fun's treasure trove of ways to play](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Archive.aspx?tag=Serious%20Fun&description=Serious%20Fun), I've been reading a lot of [Anthony Alongi](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Anthony%20Alongi). He holds a prominent place in my own **Magic** history, having kept me interested in trying new things and exploring the great **Magic** unknown throughout the years.

He was also a mighty fine writer, so you'll have to excuse me for borrowing some of his efforts. I plan to *Steal Like an Artist*, as Austin Kleon wrote. (It's a short but engaging book.)

This week is [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) Week, a theme dedicated to the namesake's importance to the plane of Innistrad. You can find a lot of awesome information about what the [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) means in [Doug Beyer's background on it](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/stf/178). You'll also catch a rebuilt deck involving it in today's other article, [Gavin Verhey's ReConstructed](/en/articles/archive/helvault-challenge-2012-04-03). And that's just the tip of the silver prison.

I'd like to start tabula rasa, looking at creating a new Commander deck from the floor up. This isn't an exercise in everyone voting, but a deliberate demonstration of the process I use to build decks. As you might already be aware, I don't build my decks to be as *empirically* powerful as possible. I build with instinct, desire, and ample moments of bubbling joy.

And I want you to see exactly how it happens. Let's crack open what's trapped in my mind.

### Helvaults: How Do They Work?

![Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Helvault)  
While the humans of Innistrad probably feel the return of Avacyn is a genuine miracle, the [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault)'s inner workings aren't so mysterious.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf189_name2.jpg)  
The [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault)...

* is an easy-to-cast artifact
* is a cheap way to rescue, seal away, or hide your own creatures
* is an expensive way to rescue, seal away, or hide your opponents' creatures
* asks for a way to be destroyed so all the creatures sent away can be returned

While many of you might have been surprised by the abilities, I found the [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) to be a compelling update to a card long forgotten: [Cold Storage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cold+Storage).

![Cold Storage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cold+Storage)  
[Cold Storage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cold+Storage)...

* is an easy-to-cast artifact
* is a cheap way to rescue, seal away, or hide your own creatures
* gives you a way to return all the creatures sent away

The similarities are striking. (For full disclosure, [Cold Storage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cold+Storage) looks like a "fixed" version of the even *older* land, [Safe Haven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Haven)!) Thanks to my ongoing efforts at building my [Commander Box](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/boxing-day-2011-11-08-0) I happened to have a copy to boot.

![Safe Haven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Safe+Haven)  
Of course, having some cards isn't the same as having an idea. What both [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) and [Cold Storage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cold+Storage) inspired me to do is take advantage of creatures that like to enter the battlefield. These "ETB" creatures are all over the place—from [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) and [Sylvan Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger) to [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) and [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum)—and are usually ones you like to cast anyway. If I'm getting the chance to put them into play again, getting an additional benefit seems awesome.

Fortunately, doubling up on triggers is almost as old as [Cold Storage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cold+Storage). "Flicker" effects are aptly named by their progenitor, [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker), and come in plenty of Modern flavors:

* [Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp)
* [Ghostway](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostway)
* [Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink)
* [Otherworldly Journey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Otherworldly+Journey)
* [Sudden Disappearance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Disappearance)
* [Galepowder Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galepowder+Mage)

[Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) comes with a flashback cost in blue, which reminded me that there's another way to recycle creatures with triggers: "bounce" effects.

* [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept)
* [Man-o'-War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Man-o%27-War)
* [Part the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Part+the+Veil)
* [Evacuation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evacuation)
* [Whitemane Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf189_gra.jpg)[Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) | Art by [Zoltan Boros & Gabor Szikszai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Gabor%22%5D+%5B%22Zoltan%22%5D)

I hadn't played white-blue as a color combination for Commander in quite some time, so getting to dive into it feels good. I also wanted to provide a "Web Redemption," *a la* Tosh.0, to one specific white-blue legend: [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV).

![Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV)  
He speeds a white-blue deck up while taxing (slowing) everyone else, regardless of their colors. I've found him nothing short of annoying, and oftentimes *very* aggravating in conjunction with other taxing effects and an abundance of countermagic.

So how does he get any better here? Easy: [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault), [Cold Storage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cold+Storage), and many of the bounce and flicker effects can excuse opponents from his effects. Basically, instead of helping me *and* hurting opponents, I plan to use him to help me... then send him away to avoid receiving a return volley from angry enemies. Sidestepping a political quagmire might not be easy (or entirely convincing) to others, but like my previous attempt at redemption in the [Mono-You](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/tutored-worldly-2011-11-22) [Sakashima the Impostor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakashima+the+Impostor) deck, it doesn't take long to show your friends you really are sincere about your intentions.

**Trigger All the Things!**

*Now* we have a firm idea to work with: a white-blue Commander deck that packs plenty of creatures with enters-the-battlefield abilities, and ways to make them trigger multiple times. The first thing we're going to need is creatures.

All of these options provide something upon entering the battlefield, and many of them are classics you see consistently in Commander. A few of the more obtuse choices are the diamonds in the rough here:

* [Pentarch Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentarch+Paladin) provides a powerful ability (destroying a permanent of the chosen color) that will surprise opponents.
* [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid) (and the more familiar [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum)) keeps us up on our land count.
* [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) and [Phyrexian Ingester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Ingester) combine well with straight recycling, but are particularly brutal if we use a flicker effect while their ETB trigger is still on the stack! (The target doesn't come back at all!)
* Having all the [Clone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) guys seems smart. Need to copy something else? We're already packing ways to do that!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf189_phy.jpg)[Phyrexian Ingester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Ingester) | Art by [Chris Rahn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Chris+Rahn%22%5D)

Are there more options out there? Absolutely! These are just what I had handy, and otherwise not locked up in a deck. Our [community-built](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/choose-our-own-deck%E2%80%94-adventure-2012-02-14) [Ghave, Guru of Spores](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) deck currently has blocked off some notable treasures, specifically [Karmic Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karmic+Guide), [Precursor Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precursor+Golem), and a bucket of other token-producing creatures. This means today's deck is *completely different* from that.

I like a little variety in my high-variance format!

With our creatures at hand, it's probably worth a look at things that help them out.

We've touched on many of these, and the rest are fairly self-explanatory. Granting haste, playing them as instants, and pulling them back from the graveyard all feel good and provide more triggers. Things are shaping up nicely, but there's one point I've missed so far: we need to actually destroy the [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) to get our guys back!

There are some clever ways to do this.

A little targeted destruction gives us ways to take out an opponent's piece if we wanted, but the board-wiping power of [Nevinyrral's Disk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk), [Akroma's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Vengeance), and friends probably has us covered.

No introductions are needed for these. They all do their jobs well, and are among the most common cards included in Commander decks. But we did run into a different problem: if you count up every card mentioned so far today the total is well in excess of one hundred *without adding any basic lands*.

**The Line is Drawn Here!**

In my excitement, I left my options uncapped. This doesn't work for Commander: the ninety-nine card requirement isn't a suggestion or minimum. So how do I trim the excess? I have a few simple ideas to help me:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf189_gil.jpg)[Gilded Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake) | Art by [Bob Eggleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Bob+Eggleton%22%5D)

* *Less is more. Are the more expensive cards doing something an easier-to-cast option already is? For example: "[Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) is efficient. [Rout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rout) seems very sharp. [Kirtar's Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Wrath) feels a little too redundant."*
* *Am I coming out swinging? I'm always sorely tempted to just pile on everything big and bold with hefty mana requirements. Glancing at how much early action is available keeps my decks from always sputtering, as well as protecting my sanity in games. For example: "[Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) and [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid) are excellent early. [Reya Dawnbringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reya+Dawnbringer) might be an excessive addition in light of the Titans and other fatties already packed in."*
* *Is this too much on plan? While making sure you have cards working together, it's easy to get carried away and add things simply because they do something. It's perfectly fine to keep room for cards you love but aren't necessary dovetailed to the theme. For example: "Without an abundance of instants and sorceries, [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) feels anemic here."*

### A Map Quest

Last week's poll asked a fairly straightforward question. Here's how you answered.



|  |
| --- |
| **How often, on average, do you visit a local game store to play non-competitive **Magic**?** |
| Never | 699 | 34.8% |
| Less than once each month | 502 | 25.0% |
| Once or twice a month | 405 | 20.2% |
| Once or twice a week | 402 | 20.0% |
| **Total** | **2008** | **100.0%** |

  
While I've always been keen to frequent the local shop for friends and fun, it looks like many of you aren't as interested, or don't have the convenience of a local store. I firmly believe you will find new friends, new ways to play, and all sorts of new fun by heading out to the places nearby. So here's a challenge for you:

* Use [the Wizards Store and Event Locator](http://locator.wizards.com/) to find a place nearby with an Avacyn Restored Prerelease.
* Check in to find out what casual events or meet-ups are offered.
* Find out if they're taking [preregistration for a](/en/node/662906) [Helvault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) event Prerelease, and plan to join in that store's fun.
* After giving the place a chance, through the Prerelease or a few casual nights, share your story with me.

If you're up to the task, I'm up for understanding your experience. I already know some of you might end up disappointed, but I'm supremely confident that many of you will begin to experience the awesome power of a local **Magic** community.

And, as a final note, if there *aren't* casual events or open playing times at your local store, find out if they'd consider adding them! With a little more legwork, and some local networking, you might be the budding start to a whole new way for **Magic** to be played in your town!

My local troupe of 30–40 (and often more!) players started as a small group meeting in one guy's house. He decided to see if a local store was willing to let them come play every week. It's become one of the most important aspects of the local **Magic** community, serving as the first stop for new and returning players. Everyone is *always* welcome, and every area should have local players ready, willing, and enjoying games of all types.

And with that, we're going to skip a poll since next week is the first week of *Avacyn Restored* previews. I hope you're ready to see what's inside!
