Young Electromancer
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 8, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I always like seeing [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) in decklists, especially "fair" ones that don't involve casting a giant [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot). Playing with [Sapphire Medallion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Medallion) was just a ton of fun, and Electromancer is basically a [Sapphire Medallion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Medallion) with legs. Today's deck combines the cost reduction of Electromancer with the payoff of [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer), all wrapped up in a RWU midrange shell. As you can imagine, I'm a fan of all of those things. 


![Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Electromancer)
 The main engine here is the creature package, with Electromancer and Pyromancer enabling each other and rewarding you for keeping up a steady flow of spells. [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm), [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate), [Thoughtflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtflare), [Lightning Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike), and [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) all impact the board, which is another hallmark of midrange decks. Side note: I love that Standard has viable midrange decks. It contributes to the overall variety, and makes it a much more fun format than when it's all one-drop aggro and super-controlling decks. 



[Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner); [Chandra, Pyromaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Pyromaster); [Ral Zarek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek); and [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion) firmly ensconce this deck in the midrange category, much like [the BUG decklist from earlier this week](/en/articles/archive/bug-smash-2013-11-05). The recipe of Planeswalkers plus solid midrange creatures plus removal is a good one, and all of these cards do a fine job on both defense and offense. The creatures fight against aggro decks and the Planeswalkers fight against control, with both categories being useful in all matchups to some degree. Plus, I just want to flip coins and take extra turns, in that order. 


 The sideboard is incredibly funny to me, since it's not every day that you see [Hammer of Purphoros](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer+of+Purphoros) and [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) fighting side by side. A hasted [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) does sound awesome, and as both cards come in against control, it might actually happen. Past that, the deck gets more removal spells and some specific answers like [Glare of Heresy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Heresy) and [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle), allowing it to cover its bases against any and every threat it might face.
