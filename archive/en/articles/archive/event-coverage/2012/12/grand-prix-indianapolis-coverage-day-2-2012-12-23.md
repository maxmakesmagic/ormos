Sunday, 10:40 a.m. – The Super Sunday Series
--------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*

 

For 128 players here this weekend, Sunday consists of six rounds of *Return to Ravnica* Booster Draft action. The rest, however, will get a chance to play in a unique and exciting event series that will be making its premiere this weekend.


The Super Sunday Series is a new event that will take place on Sundays at various Grand Prix weekends in North America, starting with today here at Grand Prix Indianapolis. It gives players a chance to compete in an event that offers some great prizes, including one that is among one of the most unique offered.


Players can compete in one of two events: a Sealed tournament in the morning, or a Standard event at noon. The Top 4 from these two events will compete later in the day in a Booster Draft to determine the Super Sunday event winner. The Top 4 finishers at each Super Sunday Series event will win a special messenger bag. The winner will qualify for a final event in Seattle next year with $20,000 in cash and prizes on the line.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/supersundayseries.jpg) *The messenger bag isn't the only prize given out to the top finishers of the Super Sunday Series. In fact, the winner will be getting a whole lot more.* 
Oh, and the prize for winning a Super Sunday Series event also includes flight and airfare to Seattle so that the winners of the Super Sunday event can visit Wizards of the Coast HQ, play against R&amp;D and have dinner with the staff.


"This is one of the most unique tournament series that we've ever run," said Alan Hochman of Pastimes. "You don't often get a chance to play and have dinner with Wizards of the Coast R&amp;D. And I think that this event, for who it's for – those who aren't the usual pro players – makes it extra special. This is for your everyday **Magic** fan."


As for the championship event in Seattle, details are still being finalized. "The format for the championship event has yet to be announced," Alan noted. "But it's sure to be fun and interesting."


The Super Sunday Series offers a unique prize and a nice incentive for players who missed out on Day 2 of the Grand Prix to compete for something special. And it's going to be seen at a lot of Grand Prix events next year. Take a look below for the tentative schedule of where else this event series will be taking place:


* Grand Prix Denver - January 6, 2013
* Grand Prix Atlantic - City January 13, 2013
* Grand Prix Quebec - City February 24, 2013
* Grand Prix San Diego - March 17, 2013
* Grand Prix Pittsburgh - March 24, 2013
* Grand Prix Portland - May 12, 2013
* Grand Prix Providence - June 9, 2013
* Grand Prix Houston - June 16, 2013
* Grand Prix Las Vegas - June 23, 2013
* Grand Prix Kansas City - July 7, 2013
* Grand Prix Calgary - July 28, 2013
* Grand Prix Oakland - August 25, 2013
* Grand Prix Detroit - September 15, 2013
* Grand Prix Oklahoma City - October 6, 2013
* Grand Prix Louisville - October 20, 2013
* Grand Prix Washington D.C. - November 17, 2013
* Grand Prix Albuquerque - November 24, 2013
* Grand Prix Toronto - December 1, 2013

The schedule for now is tentative, as it's possible more locations will be added. "We are working to bring this to as many people as possible," Alan said, so more updates will be on their way soon!

Sunday, 11:00 a.m. – Drafting Izzet with Adam Yurchick
------------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*

 

Adam Yurchick's Sealed pool yesterday served him well, despite being fairly straight-forward and lacking any sort of powerful rares and mythics. In fact, his undefeated record was earned by a Rakdos deck splashing blue that contained zero rares or mythics in his main deck.


As he sat down for the first Booster Draft of the day, he remained quiet. His preference for *Return to Ravnica* Booster Draft was to remain open, but it wouldn't be too surprising to see him favoring yesterday's guild affiliation over the others.


Yurchick started off the draft by opening a pack that featured [Teleportal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teleportal) and [Slum Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slum+Reaper) along with Courser's Accord and [Axebane Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian). Yurchick showed respect to the powerful Izzet uncommon and took it for his first pick. He followed with [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler), passing on [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) and [Centaur's Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur%27s+Herald), preferring the powerful unleash creature, as Yurchick kept yesterday's Rakdos preference in mind. A third pick [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) showed an interest in Izzet, and was followed by another copy of the blue counter as a fourth pick. [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug) further solidified Yurchick's guild affiliation, as he cemented himself firmly into Izzet throughout the rest of the first pack, scoring big hits such as [Street Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm), [Pursuit of Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pursuit+of+Flight), [Izzet Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Guildgate), and [Inspiration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiration). It would take some pretty powerful cards to move Yurchick to Rakdos at this point.


The second pack, however, opened up some options for Yurchick, and kept a splash of Rakdos alive for him, as a [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) was staring back at him in his first pick. He took the powerful removal enchantment, and was promptly rewarded for his respect for black with [Carnival Hellsteed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnival+Hellsteed), making the Rakdos splash with Izzet a reality. The rest of the pack filled out his creature curve, with multiple [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer)s, another [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug), and a few other creatures that can fly high with a [Pursuit of Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pursuit+of+Flight).


The third and final pack earned a shocked reaction from Yurchick, as a second [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) made its way into his pile of cards. This was followed with big rare #2: [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps). Yurchick took the powerful flying creature, along with a [Pursuit of Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pursuit+of+Flight) in the next pick. [Rakdos Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildgate) and [Transguild Promenade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transguild+Promenade) made splashing a breeze, and an eighth pick [Chromatic Lantern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern) made mana requirements an afterthought.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/yurchick_draft.jpg) *Adam Yurchick debates over the final version of his Izzet-fueled draft deck.* 
"That went pretty well," Yurchick said, after he finished building. "My preference is to be open. I prefer green or black, and then will often take whatever guild is open with those." He admitted that [Axebane Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian), which was in his first pact along with [Teleportal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teleportal), was an option. "If I had taken that [Axebane Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian), I'd of picked up Courser's Accords and gone white," he said. "But I took [Teleportal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teleportal), so I saw myself being Rakdos splashing for it once I took [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler), but it also let me go Izzet."


What resulted from the draft was a solid Izzet deck that had access to multiple [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound)s and the Carnival Hellsteed, which ended up being the only black mana cards to make his final deck. "I'm a little creature light," he noted, but given the power level of his removal and the creatures he did have, it was hard to argue with Yurchick's chances. The final deck had a ton of ways to stop what an opponent was planning to do, and what creatures Yurchick did have were capable of a lot.


With a deck like this, it's possible that Yurchick would continue his winning streak for the weekend. As he headed over to his round 10 feature match against Bobby Bruggeman, one of the other remaining undefeated players, Yurchick could certainly not be happier with his deck and his chances for this first draft.

Round 10 Feature Match – Adam Yurchick vs. Bobby Bruggeman
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

 

Both of these Ohio-born players were perfect on Day One. Cincinnati native Bobby Bruggeman rode to victory on the wings of his [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) with some help from [Collective Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing). This was the first time he had broken the Day Two ceiling at a Grand Prix and his friends -- who were all relegated to side events on Sunday -- were doing their best to avoid nailbiting on the sidelines.


Sitting across from him was Cleveland-born Adam Yurchick who has five Grand Prix Top 8s including his win in Houston. He has had a quiet year **Magic**-wise but does have a Grand Prix Top 8 in this very same hall earlier this year playing Legacy. Yurchick, who has gotten as high as 9th at a PT is widely considered to be among the best players without that Sunday appearance in the game.


Yurchick, who has been healing from a broken leg and hopping around in crutches this weekend, has had plenty of time to dedicate to **Magic Online** lately and came into this event with a lot of confidence in his ability to navigate this Limited format. His 9-0 finish on Saturday came without the benefit of playing any of his rares.


### Game 1


Bruggeman led off and Yurchick debated before keeping a slow hand. It did allow him to [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) a [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) on turn three and then turtle up behind a [Hover Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hover+Barrier).


Bruggeman [Soul Tithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Tithe)d the wall and played [Concordia Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concordia+Pegasus). Adam paid the [Tithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe) and played a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). Bobby passed on turn four only to play [Eyes in the Skies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyes+in+the+Skies) at the end of Adam's turn. Adam continued to pay for his wall and took four when Bobby played [Common Bond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Common+Bond) on his two unblocked tokens.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/r10_bruggeman.jpg) *Bobby Bruggeman* 
Adam snuck in two points with Rakdos Shred Freak and took three on the crack back from Bruggeman who added [Selesnya Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Keyrune) to his board. Adam continued to pay for the flying wall but was on six mana and had a little room to maneuver -- maybe more thanks to [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer).


Courser's Accord gave Bruggeman a burgeoning army to go with his air forces. Yurchick played [Pursuit of Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pursuit+of+Flight) on the Goblin with an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) up and looked for a way back into the game. He ate one of the Centaurs with the Goblin and chump blocked another with the Shred-Freak but took 5 from the fliers. Bruggeman liked the way that played out and cast another Courser's Accord but considered his populate and eventually added a bird instead of a Centaur.


Adam nodded and they moved onto game two.


Adam Yurchick - 0 Bobby Bruggeman - 1



![Rakdos Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rakdos+Guildgate)

### Game 2


Adam kept and Bobby sent his hand back and both players opened on Guildgates. There was no third land for Yurchick who ended up discarding [Rakdos Shred-Freak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Shred-Freak) which he could not cast off of [Rakdos Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildgate) and [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). A turn later he found a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and was able to unleash a [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug). Bruggeman made two fliers at the end of that turn.


Bruggeman detained the Human Warrior with [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction) and flew over for two. Yurchick was still light on lands and tapped out for [Hover Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hover+Barrier) while Bruggeman played land six. [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction) kept the Thug at bay for a second turn and [Soul Tithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Tithe) taxed the wall. This time Yurchick let it go -- Bruggeman also played a Pegasus.


Chromantic Lantern and land for Yurchick left him with two mana and just out of range of being able to [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) a [Rubbleback Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rubbleback+Rhino) from Bruggeman who was on seven lands. Carnival Hellstead came into play tapped and attacking for Yurchick -- although it was not unleashed. Bruggeman attacked back to drop Yurchick to 8 and then played Courser's Accord.


Yurchick played [Rakdos Shred-Freak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Shred-Freak) and enchanted his [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug) with [Pursuit of Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pursuit+of+Flight). He crashed in for 5 and Bruggeman did not block. The score was 10 to 8 in Bruggeman's favor. Bruggeman attacked with everyone and Yurchick took six when he blocked a token with his Hellstead and threw a Shred-Freak under the paws of the Rhino.


Yurchick untapped and gave his his Thug flying and then showed Bruggeman the [Teleportal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teleportal) -- underloaded -- and still had mana left over for a [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) should Bruggeman have a [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment).


"Did you have enough to overload last turn?" asked Bruggeman perhaps plumbing for information.


"That was a game," exhaled Yurchick showing the first sign of any emotion during the match.


Adam Yurchick - 1 Bobby Bruggeman - 1


### Game 3


Both players mulliganed and kept their six card hands. Bruggeman led off with [Concordia Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concordia+Pegasus) while Yurchick played [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer). Bruggeman flew in for one and played [Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul). Yurchick attacked into it -- no blocks -- and unleashed [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug).


Bruggeman flew over for three and cast [Soul Tithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Tithe) enchanting the Thug. He also played [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition).


"How many cards do you have?" asked Yurchick as he considered paying for the Thug and potentially racing. Bruggeman only had one card left. Yurchick thought for awhile before ultimately letting his Thug go. He rummaged away a [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) he could not cast with his [Viashino Racketeer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Racketeer) and passed the turn with an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) up.


[Common Bond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Common+Bond) meant that Bruggeman was now flying in for five -- he had four mana so he was able to dodge the Electromantically powered Suncopate.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/r10_yurchick.jpg) *Adam Yurchick* 
Adam frowned at the board and calculated how many possessions he had left in this game. His [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) hit the board but was not going to be able to deal with either of the counter-enchanced fliers. Yurchick fell to 6 from Bruggeman's -- who was at 14 -- next attack. Yurchick just sent in his Electromancer. Then played his sixth land and tapped out for a leashed [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps). Bruggeman sent the turn back with no play and no attack.


[Carnival Hellsteed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnival+Hellsteed) was up next and it was unleashed. Bruggeman threw his Apparition in the way. Courser's Accord yielded two centaur's for the Hellsteed to slaughter over the next two turns. Yurchick played pursuit of [Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flight) on his Goblin. He was content to play this game and continued to just send in his four legged Abyss each turn while Bruggeman's team was whittled down to just his two fliers.


Bruggeman tried [Collective Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing) but Yurchick finally was able to [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) something and one all out attack later and Bruggeman was extending his hand to congratulate Yurchick on the 10-0 start to his tournament.


Final Result: Adam Yurchick - 2 Bobby Bruggeman - 1

Round 11 Feature Match – Chris Fennell vs. Adam Yurchick
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*

 

The two remaining undefeated players of Grand Prix Indianapolis, Chris Fennell and Adam Yurchick, quickly got ready for their match to see who will continue on without a single loss. Yurchick's Izzet deck splashing black, as seen from the round 10 feature match, can pack a punch, but Chris Fennell was more than happy with the results of this draft, as he sat down with a deck featuring an assortment of powerful Selesnya cards... along with a splash for the powerful [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat).


### Game 1


Fennell won the die roll and snap-kept his seven cards, while Yurchick sent his opening hand back for six new cards. "So bad..." Yurchick said, as he sent his less than optimal six card hand back for five... and those five were immediately sent back to four.


"This is pretty anticlimactic, Adam," Fennell said, as Yurchick kept his four. Fennell's turns started off with guildgates into [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer), while Yurchick had a third-turn [Lobber Crew](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew). Fennell played [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile), intent on pushing some damage through on the next turn, while Yurchick ensured that he'd have no mana troubles with [Chromatic Lantern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/r11_yurchick.jpg)*Adam Yurchick fights back after a mulligan to four.*
Both of Fennell's creatures were sent in, dropping Yurchick to 17, as [Selesnya Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Keyrune) ramped him up. Yurchick played another land and a [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) on Centaur's [Heal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heal)er. Fennell sent in his creatures and followed with Courser's Accord. When Yurchick's [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps) met [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment), and as the centaur army grew in size, Yurchick quickly scooped up his cards for the second game.


Fennell 1 - Yurchick 0


### Game 2


Yurchick was content with his seven card hand this time around, but that content turned to desperation as he lacked an answer for Fennell's second turn [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat). Yurchick unleashed a [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug), resigned to racing the [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) army. Fennell's [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) did its thing, attacking for 2 on the next turn after Fennell made a copy. Yurchick unleashed another [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug), while Fennell made another [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) token and sent in his other rats, bringing Yurchick to 12.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/r11_fennell.jpg) *Fennell's [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat)s quickly multiply, as [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat)s typically do.*

Sunday, 3:10 p.m. – Paying the Price of Progress
------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*

 

When a Grand Prix takes place in your hometown, it's hard to resist the call to play. For Nate Price, coverage reporter and writer for DailyMTG.com's aptly named '[Price of Progress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Price+of+Progress)' column, Grand Prix Indianapolis represented one of his first times to play in a major event since... well, a long time.


"The last Grand Prix I played in was about three years ago," Nate said. Between then and now, Nate has gone to lots of Grand Prixs and Pro Tour events... but his role was reserved to behind-the-scenes, as the man looking over a laptop as players battle it out in the feature match area.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/nate_price.jpg) *Nate Price, author of DailyMTG.com's '[Price of Progress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Price+of+Progress)' column, has proven that he knows how to build a Limited deck or two this weekend.* 
Nate is no stranger to the Limited format for *Return to Ravnica*. As a coverage reporter, he has learned a lot by talking to some of the greatest of the game. Watching them draft, play, and discuss can be a great way to learn what to do, and what to avoid.


That, and the hotel lobby Booster Drafts that are commonly seen at Pro Tour stops certainly don't hurt. I've got a couple memories of Nate promptly destroying me in coverage drafts from this year.


His experience with the fundamentals of Sealed Pack and his experience with the game itself for over seven years has also served him well this weekend. After all, as the author to a Sealed Pack column on DailyMTG.com, Nate knows more than a few things about what to do with the 84 cards you are given in a Sealed Pack event.


Nate tackled Day 1 by piloting an Izzet deck splashing white for [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment), [Dramatic Rescue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dramatic+Rescue), and [Skymark Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skymark+Roc). "My Sealed pool was fairly average," he said, noting that [Mercurial Chemister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mercurial+Chemister) was his only solid bomb. "I splashed white because I needed to be able to race, and these cards all helped me do that."



![Daggerdrome Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Daggerdrome+Imp)

Nate navigated his Izzet deck through nine rounds during Day 1, earning himself a 7-2 record by the end, which was good enough to get him into Sunday's drafts.


For the first draft of the day, Nate went in with a preference for the Golgari archetype. He sat down intent on the green-black guild as his goal. The result was a solid green-black deck that featured multiple [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) effects, a couple of solid scavenge creatures, and a whopping four [Daggerdrome Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp)s to be targets for these cards.


The Golgari guild served him well in the first draft, but fell a tad short in the final game of his third match, where his deck ultimately sputtered out, unable to provide him with a spell to fight back against Corey Hennings in the twelfth round. He headed into the second Booster Draft at 9-3, and will need to win out for a shot at the Top 16.


While the dream of a Top 8 at a local Grand Prix might be over for the veteran coverage reporter, he isn't ready to give up yet. With three rounds left to play at Grand Prix Indianapolis, Nate Price continues to progress through the tournament, undaunted by his one loss for the day and ready to tackle the second Booster Draft.

Sunday, 3:50 p.m. – Drafting with Ben Stark
-------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

 

Pod one of the penultimate draft of GP Indy was pretty impressive with Sam Kuprewicz being the only relative unknown sitting around a table with the likes Luis Scott-Vargas, Josh Utter-Leyton, Ben Stark, Adam Yurchick, Chris Fennell, Caleb Durward, and Brian Demars -- all of whom have found success to varying degrees at the Grand Prix level.


It was also tough to pick who to sit behind for draft coverage. LSV is always a crowd pleaser; Josh Utter-Leyton simply deserves more attention for his impeccable play; and Chris Fennell was the only undefeated player riding a 14 match winning streak between the Trial he won and rounds played in this tournament. I could have picked anyone at this table but in the end decided upon Ben Stark. We had been talking about draft earlier and I wanted to see him action and get a sense of what he values in this format.


The pod was seated as follows:


1. Sam Kuprewicz
2. Caleb Durward
3. Ben Stark
4. Brian Demars
5. Chris Fennell
6. Josh Utter-Leyton
7. Adam Yurchick
8. Luis Scott-Vargas

"Super easy," said Stark of his first pick of [Soulsworn Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulsworn+Spirit) over a handful of less-likely first picks that included [Lobber Crew](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew), [Launch Party](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party), [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion), [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile), Isperia Skywatch, and [Nivmagus Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nivmagus+Elemental). "The rest of the cards in that pack were just replacement level cards while [Soulsworn Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulsworn+Spirit) is a difference maker."


His second pick offered up a choice between [Street Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm), [Auger Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree), and [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug) and possibly an [Ogre Jailbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Jailbreaker) but Stark was only looking at the instants. He chose the monocolored removal spell to keep himself open.


"You don't want to take a red-black card that is about equal in power to a red card when you have just first picked a blue card. I don't know what I am going to end up playing yet -- if I was red-black aggro I would take the [Auger Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree) but if I am red-blue I would much rather have a [Street Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm)."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/r9_stark.jpg) *Ben Stark* 
His third pick -- one he considers pivotal to the success or failure of a draft -- ended up having major repercussions for Stark even though the [Lyev Skyknight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyev+Skyknight) did not make it into his final deck.


"I am just king the best card in the pack at that point," said Stark who could have taken [Nivix Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nivix+Guildmage) or [Skyline Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyline+Predator). "Once you get to a third or fourth pick that is where drafts are really decided. You should always be willing to throw away your first two picks and not even play them. Pick three, pick four, and pack five is where you are really trying to read what you are really seeing and where you are going to go. Skyknight was so much better than the other cards. I was definitely going to take that out of that pack."


His next two picks were back to back layups with [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire)s. When looking back at those picks it just made the Pro Tour Paris Champion sigh. He took a [Cobblebrute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobblebrute) out of a weak looking pack with his next pick but there was the potential for something better lurking further back.



![Cobblebrute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cobblebrute)

"This is where I messed up," he explained. Not a typical reaction to a draft walkthrough which just saw you get two mid-pack removal spells. At this point he was locked into red and could play red-blue splashing black or white or red-black splashing blue. "Because I already had the Skyknight I did not really see the [Rakdos Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildgate) in that pack and took [Cobblebrute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobblebrute). He is so meh -- although he does get better if you have two Annihilating Fire. It was a tough pack but considering that I had already seen an [Auger Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree) -- and the black stuff is so splashable -- that pick was wrong. That is not just being results oriented either. If I had the draft to do over again with random packs after that point I would take the Guildgate. I am committed to red at that point but everything else should be completely open. That was just a bad pick."


His Isperia Skywatch from the opening pack tabled around to him and when pack one came to a close he had a handful of good instant speed removal but not the rank and file creatures you might want for a game of Limited **Magic** -- or so I thought anyway.


"If you are not playing a green deck with pump spells and creature enchantments you don't need you creature count to be that high," Stark explained. "People overrate that. You need to have like 15 or 16 creatures if you are playing [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) but if you are playing a bunch of board sweepers and [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire)s you would always want to have [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire) over a creature. It is still one for one. That was not a really big concern. I would be happy with a deck with 8 creatures and good removal."


He continued on that theme and took [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) out of his opening pack over [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) and [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot).


"[Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) is just so much better than [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot). You counter cards like [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment) and Courser's Accord -- [Collective Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing). You can get them in combat when they use their trick like [Common Bond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Common+Bond). You can do two to a Guildmage. It is not a bomb but it is nice flexible removal. It just does everything you want at all times."



![Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Voidwielder)

To this point he was still base red and open to playing his deck in any number of color configurations. Even his second pick [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) still only committed him to red cards. When did he commit to playing blue as well?


"When I get the [Mercurial Chemister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mercurial+Chemister) third," laughed Stark. "It is one of the best cards in the set. It is a very good card, a first pick quality card. On the scale of 1-10 it is a 9."


He followed that up with another [Street Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm) and then a [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) -- a pretty good indication that he had positioned himself perfectly for the Izzet goodies to flow in pack two.


[Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) over [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) led off his third set of packs and that was a no-brainer in Stark's book hardly worthy of comment. It was clear that he was still able to get red but the packs were a little dry for his colors. He shook his head as had to take [Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact) with one of his early picks and he did get a third [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire). But he could only mourn for the deck that could have been.


"[Clear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clear)ly I know my read is right when I get passed a fourth pick [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire). I just would have liked to get something like a [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) over an [Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact)," said Stark giving his deck a rating of 7. He felt it could have been a 9 if he had been more open to drafting black and taken the [Rakdos Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildgate)s. Possibly being able to splash [Auger Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree) and a Rakdos Ragematt that went by late in the last two sets of packs. "I would have had perfect mana..."


His deck still looked pretty good to me. You can judge for yourself.

Round 13 Feature Match – Caleb Durward vs. Ben Stark
----------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*

 

"Good draft, huh," said Ben Stark to Caleb Durward, as the two discussed how their second Booster Draft went. "Yeah," Durward said. "I typically do better in Booster Drafts with a weaker card pool," he said, "But in drafts like this, it's harder to say," Durward said. "I'm not too good at this format," he followed.


Durward, who drafted a solid Golgari deck, was facing off against Stark's Izzet deck, featuring plenty of powerful removal spells and a solid line-up of great commons and uncommons.


### Game 1


Durward debated on his opening seven, but opted for a mulligan, while Stark was content with his first hand. Stark was first on the board with [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird), which halted Durward's third-turn [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler). Stark kept his curve going with [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake), and on the fourth turn, matched Durward's [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion) with an unleashed [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant), sending in the [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) for 2.


Durard sent the [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) in on the fifth turn after playing Kurozda Guildmage, which Stark did not block, instead going to 17. Stark continued his curve with [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) to bounce Durward's [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion), allowing the [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) to start brawling.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/r13_stark.jpg)*Ben Stark's Izzet deck had loads of great blue-red goodies.*
Durward attacked with the [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler), which took out Stark's [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) after Kurozda Guildmage pumped it up, and [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile) soon followed. However, Stark had the powerful [Mercurial Chemister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mercurial+Chemister) on the next turn, as the [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) continued to crash in with the [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake), sending Durward to 4.


Durward played [Golgari Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Keyrune) and passed with mana open for Kurozda Guildmage's effects, but with Stark's Chemister active, things were going to be tough. Stark sent the [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) and [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) in, and Durward threw the [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile) and [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) in the Giant's path, going to 2. Durward had [Launch Party](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party), sacrificing the guildmage, to take out Stark's air forces, but when Durward's only follow-up was [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion) once more, the [Mercurial Chemister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mercurial+Chemister) drew some cards, and [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) earned the concession from Durward.


Durward 0 - Stark 1



![Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stealer+of+Secrets)

### Game 2


Durward drew his opening hand, let out a simple "Nope," and shuffled up for a new hand. Stark looked at his seven and quickly chose to keep. Durward led off with [Drudge Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Beetle), while Stark deployed a [Doorkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doorkeeper) to put any sort of aggression at a standstill. Durward followed with a leashed [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler). "Good choice," Stark said, as he played [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets).


Durward promptly invited Stark's fourth turn [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) to a [Launch Party](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party), costing Durward his [Drudge Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Beetle). Another [Launch Party](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party) was used in response to an [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire) from Stark, taking out his [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets). Durward finished up with his last card in hand, a [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion), as Stark deployed another [Doorkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doorkeeper) and [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets). "If at first you don't succeed," Stark said, aiming a second [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire) at the [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion), as the [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets) began to draw Stark cards.


Durward found a Kurozda Guildmage waiting on top, giving him a blocker for [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets). Stark, not content with losing his card drawing creature, passed back after playing [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake). [Trestle Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trestle+Troll) gave Durward a blocker, as Stark drew and passed.


Durward found and played [Golgari Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Keyrune), and then activated Kurozda Guildmage, earning an [Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact) from Stark on the guildmage. When Stark drew [Street Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm), he shrugged, playing it on the [Trestle Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trestle+Troll) while Durward didn't have the mana to regenerate it, clearing the path for the stealer of Secrets once again.


[Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) from Stark locked down Durward's freshly played [Perilous Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Shadow)s. With Stark's airforce now a sizable threat, and Durward working off the top of his deck, the game was all but over, as the flying forces attacked each turn. Two turns later, Durward offered the handshake.


Durward 0 - Stark 2

Demars double pumped the Weird and Utter-Leyton smacked himself in the head when he forgot to use his [Security Blockade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Security+Blockade) -- see it happens to the best of them folks.


[Eyes in the Skies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyes+in+the+Skies) made another 2/2 and one flier during his own main phase when Demars couldn't punish Utter-Leyton with untapped mana and a myriad of potential removal spells.


Demars had no play and Utter-Leyton began to attack for two in the air. Demars drew a [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) for the Pegasus and played a second copy of [Tavern Swindler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tavern+Swindler). Utter-Leyton had another [Eyes in the Skies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyes+in+the+Skies) at the end of the turn to make a third Knight and a second bird.


Josh swung in with all three knights and his two fliers. Demars -- who was tapped out -- lined up his three creatures hoping to trade two Swindlers for two knights and have the [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) bounce with one of them. [Common Bond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Common+Bond) made things much worse for him.


He played [Auger Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree) on one of the enhanced tokens and passed the turn back to Utter-Leyton who used [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) as a disenchant and attacked with his Pegasus, two fliers and just his 3/3 token. Demars traded his Frostborn Weird for it. Utter-Leyton added [Selesnya Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Sentry) to his team.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/r15_utterleyton.jpg)*Josh Utter-Leyton*
[Zanikev Locust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zanikev+Locust) could only look on in lonely futility as Utter-Leyton attacked for three with his Sentry with the requisite six mana available for regeneration. Utter-Leyton added Centar's Herald to the board. Demars mustered a leashed [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler)s but as soon as Utter-Leyton began preparing for an all-out attack he picked up his cards and asked for a draw or concession from Utter-Leyton.


Demars was pretty confidant he would be ninth with the loss -- his second such finish this season and the despair on his face seemed to waver Utter-Leyton briefly but with Pro Points being so precious in terms of the Player's Championship, World **Magic** Cup and the newly re-announced Player of the Year chase he could not afford to let a Top 16 finish slip from his grasp.


Final Result: Josh Utter-Leyton - 2 Brian Demars - 0
