Color Breakdown
===============


From among the 58 decks, most of them were two-colored. Splashes for a third color were uncommon, and we'll only take into account the two primary colors for each deck.


Except for two Grixis Pirate decks and a small handful who splashed a third color for a couple of cards, most of the decks were two-colored.




| **Color** | **Number of 3-0 Decks** |
| --- | --- |
| White | 23 |
| Blue | 26 |
| Black | 26 |
| Red | 22 |
| Green | 21 |

Based on these numbers, blue and black were the best-performing colors, though not necessarily together. Despite that, white, red, and green were not far behind, indicating that all colors were capable of shoring up great results.  
  

However, that's not quite enough information, so the next thing we're going to share is the color combinations of all 58 3-0 decks!




| **Color** | **Number of 3-0 Decks** |
| --- | --- |
| White-Black | 15 |
| Green-Blue | 13 |
| Red-Green | 6 |
| Black-Red | 5 |
| Blue-Red | 5 |
| Red-White | 4 |
| Blue-Black | 3 |
| White-Blue | 3 |
| Green-White | 1 |
| Black-Green | 1 |
| Grixis | 2 |
| **Total** | **58** |

Most of the White-Black decks were dedicated Vampire decks, while most of the Green-Blue decks centered around Merfolk. This was a reasonable assumption since many white and black creatures were Vampires, and many blue and green creatures were Merfolk.  
  

You could technically end up with a White-Black deck with half Dinosaurs, half Pirates, and zero Vampires, but that was not the case this weekend. Most players wisely took advantage of tribal synergies as much as they possibly could.  
  

If we also operate under the assumption that most of the Green-White, Red-White, and Red-Green decks were Dinosaur-centric, and that most of the Blue-Black, Blue-Red, and Black-Red decks were filled with Pirates, here was how each tribe's performance looked like side by side.




| **Tribe** | **Number of 3-0 Decks** |
| --- | --- |
| Vampires | 15 |
| Pirates | 15 |
| Merfolk | 13 |
| Dinosaurs | 11 |
| Others | 4 |
| **Total** | **58** |

Vampires and Pirates had the best results, while Dinosaurs were the “worst-performing” of the lot, but not by far. Even if you did not draft around a particular tribe (I'm looking at you, White-Blue and Black-Green), it was still possible for any combination to achieve the clean sweep at your draft table.

The innocuous-looking 1/1 played a central role in his White-Black Vampires deck. It provided an early body ready to be enhanced by [Voracious Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voracious+Vampire) and [Sanguine Glorifier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanguine+Glorifier), and also contributed towards the Vampire count for [Champion of Dusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Dusk). Edgar Magalhaes demonstrated in Pod 5 how to draft a great Dinosaur deck. Starting with "ramp" spells such as [Thunderherd Migration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderherd+Migration), [Atzocan Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atzocan+Seer), [New Horizons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=New+Horizons), and double [Knight of the Stampede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Stampede), his opponents were in for numerous nasty surprises. With eleven Dinosaurs in his deck, Frank Karsten was "almost certain" that Magalhaes could always reliably cast [Thunderherd Migration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderherd+Migration) on turn two, which could result in a turn three [Knight of the Stampede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Stampede). To deal with pesky creatures, Magalhaes had [Bombard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bombard) and four [Hunt the Weaks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunt+the+Weaks), which worked splendidly with his angry dinos!


Most importantly, Magalhaes was *actually* able to create an infinite amount of [Polyraptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polyraptor)s! If he managed to have both [Bellowing Aegisaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bellowing+Aegisaur) and [Forerunner of the Empire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forerunner+of+the+Empire) on the battlefield, not only will his creatures not die to the damage triggers, they also become arbitrarily huge. You could just attack with a 1000/1000 creature and have 1,000 [Polyraptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polyraptor)s as blockers!

Last but not least, let's take a look at a White-Blue deck with an ascend theme. Collin Rountree cleaned out Pod 49 by unlocking the [Secrets of the Golden City](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Secrets+of+the+Golden+City), finding the [Orazca Relic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orazca+Relic), and took to the skies with [Resplendent Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resplendent+Griffin) and friends.


Conveniently, [Luminous Bonds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminous+Bonds) and [Waterknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waterknot) served as removal spells while contributing to his permanent count, while [Squire's Devotion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squire%27s+Devotion) not only helped Rountree pull ahead in the damage race but also get close to ascend. He also made sure to draft sturdy creatures such as [Skyblade of the Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyblade+of+the+Legion), [Everdawn Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everdawn+Champion), [Sun-Crested Pterodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun-Crested+Pterodon), and [Exultant Skymarcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exultant+Skymarcher) such that they stick around longer! Rountree knew *exactly* what to do to gain the city's blessing quickly!


After reviewing some statistics and learning a thing or two from these esteemed drafters, are you ready for your next *Rivals of Ixalan* booster draft?
