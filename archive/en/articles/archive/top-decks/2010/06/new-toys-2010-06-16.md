This particular new toy (or comeback toy, kind of) was largely developed in response to the success of Naya [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) around the  [2010 Nationals Qualifiers](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/events/nationalqualifiers%20) and the mighty [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage). [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage)—hasty to begin with—is fast enough to take apart [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), but along with [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) (a handy bullet find with [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)) [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage) can take down anything and everything. So basically, if you want to protect an [Eldrazi Conscription](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Conscription) from Sparkmage mastery, Linvala is a fine play.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence)  
Linvala is pretty cool at proactive disruption, as well. She can stop [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), and unlike almost every other similar effect in the game, Linvala will *also* stop [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) from coming online. Against some draws, that can be tantamount to mana-screw.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td95_master.jpg)  
The good Master was a card that saw heavy play around the time of the  [2009 World Championships](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/worlds09/welcome%20) , first in Eldrazi Green and later in Jund ... Well, now it's back!

A few weeks ago, coming back from Pro Tour–San Juan, Brian David-Marshall told me he wanted to play with [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), [Oracle of Mul Daya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya), and [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra). The three cards in combination are just silly. [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) lets you put two cards from your hand on top of your deck (or just rearrange other cards); [Oracle of Mul Daya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) can of course play lands that are on the top of your deck.

See where this is going?

The two fours put next to one another are absolutely dominating (providing they have enough time)—[Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) just makes the system faster. 

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130606034801im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td95_3Cards1.jpg)  
When you add all three cards together you have the fast [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp)s, the big [Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring)s. LSV's deck, in particular, won with a quartet of  [Avengers of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Avengers+of+Zendikar). This is in contrast to the Eldrazi giants we have seen in the past.

When four copies of  [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) just aren't enough for you ... you can kick it old school with [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp). 

The plan here is to play into [Brilliant Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brilliant+Ultimatum), setting up with [Liliana Vess](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess). Again, [Brilliant Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brilliant+Ultimatum) doesn't just put cards onto the battlefield: it actually *plays* them. That means that when you Ultimatum up  [Emrakul, the Aeon's Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Emrakul%2C+the+Aeon%27s+Torn), you are getting a [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) already. The addition of [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) means that when you go attacking, they never get another turn. Annihilator 6, [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp), untap, annihilator 6, -10 for you. Thanks.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td95_3cards2.jpg)  
These [Brilliant Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brilliant+Ultimatum) decks are *also* 80% there as regular White-Blue-based control decks. You have your [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment); you have your [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) ... but when the split is coming, no matter how juicy the four-pile is, it's probably not juicy enough to *not* take Emrakul!

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td95_angel.jpg)  
We have the luck this time around of getting some wonderful week-by-week tips and commentary from Shaheen Soorani on how to tune and play the classic White-Blue deck. Long-time readers of [Top Decks](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?tag=TopDecksamp;description=Swimming%20With%20Sharks%20) and its predecessor Swimming With Sharks know Shaheen's name. We have been following him via Top 8 deck lists analysis since the first time he bought a Blue Envelope with a [Rewind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind).

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Emeria+Angel)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel)  
At States 2009 he gave us the model for the White-Blue deck that would carry into largely our present "Tap-Out" understanding. Shaheen actually made *five* Top 8s (the last four of them Pro Tour Qualifier Top 8s)! He lost in the first four, but tuned and changed week to week (never straying from his beloved White-Blue color combination) until he once again waltzed off with the Pro Tour invitation. Let's check out what he was thinking along the way.

At this point in the metagame, Shaheen was still aiming for Jund. He played it five times in the Swiss (going 4-1) before losing to Jund in the quarterfinals.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130606052710im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td95_4Negates.jpg)  
The logic behind 4 [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)s main deck was that White-Blue can handle "any of the dudes that will be played" and [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) was there to handle the "problem spells"—removal for planeswalkers, removal for [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), card drawing, etc. 

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Luminarch+Ascension)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension)  
Shaheen Says:   
 "The MVP in the sideboard that won and will win nearly every mirror match is [Luminarch Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension). I call control decks *absurd* that go with other options like [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) and [Kor Sanctifier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Sanctifier). [Luminarch Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension) is one of the most unfair cards I have seen in seven years of competitive **Magic** (almost as unfair as [Sphere of Law](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Law) against Goblins in Extended), and people should not opt out of the 'turn two win' if you untap with it in play."

**Shaheen's Notes on "Terrible Cards":**

**[Martial Coup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup)**: Seven mana for five tokens isn't worth it. [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) is far superior ... clear the board then get to business; the key to victory is low curve answers, not high curve threats.   
**[Celestial Purge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Purge)**: [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) will be bumped to four copies. White-Blue cannot afford to go 1-for-1 or 1-for-0 on man lands ... it is time to conform to [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas).   
**[Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring)**: Have you ever drawn two of these in your opener? Two (total) is the right number.   
**[Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)**: I still stick to my guns about the power of [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), however with the rise of Mythic, sitting there with a [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) as the opponent plays a [Sovereigns of Lost Alara](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sovereigns+of+Lost+Alara) makes you reflect more on your decisions. Time to make an "answer all" with [Deprive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive).

For the first time during this amazing run, Shaheen conformed to playing [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) (i.e. the best blue card in Standard) as an out to not just a quick Jund opener, but opposing [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade)s and the [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)s.

"The common sense list"—four [Baneslayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer)s main deck. Let's stop beating around the bush and play the best creature in Standard.

Shaheen is most proud of his sideboard, in particular the addition of new toy [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel):

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td95_shaheen.jpg)  
"I thought this guy was amazing against Next Level Bant ... it protects planeswalkers, it's a win condition, it can't be countered by [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), it defends against [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) more than anything else ... literally the best sideboard card against Next Level Bant, but it turns out it's the best card in the sideboard against almost anything.   
 The mirror boards out a good amount of removal; I board in that creature; if it is not Path'd immediately, no Planeswalker can survive, [Luminarch Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension) is permanently protected, *and so much more*. I have boarded him in every matchup; it begs the question as to why it's not main deck. [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) is nuts."

In order to win a PTQ, Soorani had to figure out how to beat hot new —and hot new control killing deck—Next Level Bant. He has a list of tips that he says turn the match-up from 40-60 in favor of the new kid to 60-40 in favor of [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) and pals.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td95_dayoverbirds.jpg)  
1. In Game 1, turn four [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) no matter what. They don't have [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate); it doesn't matter what 1/3 you kill along with their [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Birds+of+Paradise)—turn four [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment). Play to remove their mana advantage; [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) is ineffective against [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) anyway.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130606051247im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td95_pathOverVengevine.jpg)  
2. Never [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) anything but [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Vengevine). Just don't do it. Use [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) on their man lands.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td95_spreadingoverraging.jpg)  
3. Use all resources you can to remove planeswalkers *besides playing your own* if you are able. Winning this match-up is about having your planeswalkers stick, followed by [Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) for 100 to [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) on Vengevine.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td95_sideboard.jpg)  
4. Sideboard in 4 [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) and 2 [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) for 1 [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas), 3 [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens), and 2 [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment). Game 2 is very different; you don't want to walk into [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) or [Deprive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive). They only have two [Deprive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive)s so your creatures can go on a run; but hold your planeswalkers until you feel like you can keep them. [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) will win it making one token per turn.
