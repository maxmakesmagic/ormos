Orzhova Good Time
=================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on March 25, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







A few weeks ago, I presented you all with a challenge: Create an Orzhov deck worthy of being shown off here at the Lab. I received dozens of submissions, most of which were quite good, but in the end I had to narrow it down to just a few decks. Although many great decks fell along the way, only the best of the best made it here, the ones that most piqued my interest or tickled my fancy. And now the time has come to tear off the big white sheet and see our winners.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl240_teysa.jpg)[Teysa, Orzhov Scion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teysa%2C+Orzhov+Scion) | Art by [Todd Lockwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Todd%20Lockwood%22%5D)


**The Darkest Scion**


The most popular combo by far among the decks I received was [Teysa, Orzhov Scion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teysa%2C+Orzhov+Scion) combined with cards like [Painter’s Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%E2%80%99s+Servant) and [Darkest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkest+Hour). Here are a few of the submissions I received using this combo.

> 
> The idea focuses on getting [Teysa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teysa) out with (1) [Painter’s Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%E2%80%99s+Servant) set to black or [Darkest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkest+Hour), (2) [Altar of Dementia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar+of+Dementia) or [Blasting Station](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) (or [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) if need be), and (3) any creature. The creature, which is always black thanks to the Servant or Hour, is sacrificed to one of the sac outlets, triggering Teysa’s ability and netting you a (now black) Spirit. Since every time a creature is sacrificed a Spirit is created, this gives you an infinite number of creatures to sacrifice to either mill out your opponent with the Altar or ping to death with the Station. [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood), [Enlightened Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor), and [Plunge Into Darkness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plunge+Into+Darkness) all help find whatever combo piece may be missing. These, as well as the redundancy of so many pieces, make it quite easy to assemble the combo.

> 
> If we make everything black with the [Painter’s Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%E2%80%99s+Servant), the tokens made by Teysa will be both black and white, so whenever one of them dies, we will get another one: that means that with three tokens we can exile every creature the opponent has over and over again, setting up for a lethal attack with a huge [Bloodthrone Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire).
> 
> 
> [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) can leave the missing finisher on the top of our library once we assemble the combo, and [Diabolic Intent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Intent) (with all the token producers) can help us assemble it in the first place.

We tried to keep the deck as Orzhovian as possible so we have lots of WB goodies and avoided certain cards, but if you’re looking to make the deck more competitive also consider [Blasting Station](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) and/or [Altar of Dementia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar+of+Dementia).”

Another Laboratorian went in a slightly different direction, using [Heartless Summoning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) instead of a sacrifice outlet. This creates an unstoppable infinite loop of dying Spirit tokens that causes the game to end in a draw. Well, unless your opponent dies somewhere in there. [Moonlit Wake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlit+Wake) allows you to gain 1 life on each iteration of the cycle, and when combined with [Sanguine Bond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanguine+Bond) or [Vizkopa Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizkopa+Guildmage), your opponent will lose 1 life as well. Repeat this process an infinite number of times and your opponent will undoubtedly die somewhere in there.

My favorite part of this deck is the clever use of [Parallax Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave) to enable even more crazy shenanigans. I’ll let Ross give you the rundown.



> 
> If you do get stuck in a long game, you also have a *coup de grâce*.
> 
> 
> * Use [Parallax Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave) to exile [Gwyllion Hedge-Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gwyllion+Hedge-Mage) and [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter).
> * Use [Leonin Relic-Warder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder) to exile [Parallax Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave), returning the creatures [Parallax Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave) exiled.
> * Get a token and place a -1/-1 counter with [Gwyllion Hedge-Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gwyllion+Hedge-Mage). Use [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) to exile [Relic Warder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+Warder).
> * Repeat Steps 1–3 until you have thousands of tokens and your opponent’s creatures are all dead.
> 
> If you have certain extra creatures during this combo, you can draw any number of cards ([Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens)), exile all of your opponent’s hand ([Mesmeric Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mesmeric+Fiend)/[Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler)), return all of your enchantments and/or creatures from your graveyard to your hand ([Auramancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer)/[Cadaver Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cadaver+Imp)), or return anything with 2 or less power from your graveyard to the battlefield ([Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)). If you have [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) and [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) as you go off with [Parallax Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave), you can give your [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) thousands of power by repeatedly sacrificing whatever [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) returns.
>

**Death and Undeath**


Callum Logan started off with the staple Commander combo of [Vish Kal, Blood Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vish+Kal%2C+Blood+Arbiter) and [Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed). Then he decided that wasn’t cool enough, and added a few more things to make it even more absurd. That’s exactly the kind of thinking I like to see here at the Lab.
