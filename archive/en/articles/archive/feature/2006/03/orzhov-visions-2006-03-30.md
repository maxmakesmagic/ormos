Orzhov Visions
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 30, 2006 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author_pic_bennie_smith.jpg)
By Bennie Smith




Bennie Smith began playing Magic in 1994 and started writing about it shortly after. A Virginia State Champion, he enjoys few things better than winning at tournaments with home brews. Bennie has a weekly column on StarCityGames.com. He also recently published The Complete Commander. Follow him on Twitter, on Facebook, and the occasional Commander games on Magic Online under the handle "blairwitchgreen." 






In honor of Orzhov Week and the fact that *Visions* is going to be released on ***Magic** Online* very very soon (tune in next week for details!), I thought I'd take a peek at *Visions* through a lens Dark and Light.


### *VISIONS* BLACK


***Rares** - [Aku Djinn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aku+Djinn), [Forbidden Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Ritual), [Kaervek's Spite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaervek%27s+Spite), [Necrosavant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrosavant), [Pillar Tombs of Aku](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+Tombs+of+Aku), [Vampiric Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampiric+Tutor)*


[Vampiric Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampiric+Tutor) obviously jumps out of this list as one of the best Tutor cards ever printed; its instant speed and cheap cost can arguably make the case for it surpassing the original classic [Demonic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Tutor). *Mirage* had [Enlightened Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor), [Mystical Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Tutor), and [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor); after *Visions* and [Vampiric Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampiric+Tutor), speculation was high about what the Red tutor card would be like in *Weatherlight*. Would it fetch only lands? Maybe it would be like Vampiric, but at sorcery speed. Of course, the joke was that the Red tutor randomly decided not to show up... until [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) in *Urza's Saga* years later. [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) did turn out to be the perfect Red Tutor card.


![Kaervek's Spite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kaervek%27s+Spite)For long-time players who devoured tournament reports on Usenet and The Magic Dojo, another card on the list stands out. Ah, [Kaervek's Spite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaervek%27s+Spite)! Mike Flores' old crew Team Discovery Channel coined a funny little catchphrase for this highly risky card that quickly caught on as **Magic** lingo (as did so many other things from Flores and Company). From [The Tao of TDC](http://classicdojo.org/b981/bou.980301chw.txt), a very funny read by Charles "Tuna" Hwa that might be a bit dated, but is bound to elicit chuckles from old-timers:
 
> 
> *1) You are at 4 life, your opponent is at 5. You are playing a monoblack deck, while you opponent is playing PT jank. Even though you know your deck is superior to his, since black is, well, strictly better than white, you have no cards in hand, while your opponent has 2 [White Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=White+Knight)s. You draw [Demonic Consultation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Consultation), but have only 10 cards left in your library. Knowing you cannot drain for more than 4, You:*
> 
> 
> *a) Consult for one of your four [Steel Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Golem)'s you have left to draw.  
>  b) Consult for one of your four [Drain Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drain+Life)'s anyways to stay alive.  
>  c) Consult for your one [Kaervek's Spite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaervek%27s+Spite).  
>  d) Consult for your one [Kaervek's Spite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaervek%27s+Spite) while chanting "Spring forth, my Burly Protector, and save me!"*
> 
> 
> 


The answer, of course, is d. Now, MTGO players will be able to get their hands on their own Burly Protector, going all in for that instant-speed five points of damage! What more could you possibly need?


Another old favorite is [Necrosavant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrosavant), who has quietly undergone a metamorphosis recently, changing from *Summon Necrosavant* to *Creature — Zombie Giant*. I suppose being the only [Necrosavant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrosavant) in existence (other than [Mistform Ultimus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus)) was a little bit lonely, even for a dead guy. *Visions* will be legal for Tribal Wars, and Zombies aren't a bad little tribe to join. 


***Uncommons** - [Blanket of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blanket+of+Night), [Brood of Cockroaches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brood+of+Cockroaches), [Crypt Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Rats), [Desolation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolation), [Necromancy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromancy), [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal), [Suq'Ata Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suq%27Ata+Assassin), [Vampirism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampirism)*


Two of the best cards here, [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) and [Crypt Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Rats), have already seen play on MTGO for those who want 'em, but it will be nice to have the original black-bordered versions available. [Desolation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolation) is a nasty bit of disruption that can complement a beatdown strategy, and [Suq'Ata Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suq%27Ata+Assassin) gives poison aficionados another weapon, this time with Fear. Accumulate those poison counters, sir!


![Necromancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Necromancy)[Necromancy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromancy) is a fun and flexible reanimator card that also happens to combo with [Worldgorger Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldgorger+Dragon) to give you infinite mana. You have been warned!
 One thing I stumbled across when checking [Necromancy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromancy) on Gatherer is something called *substance*. Have you ever heard of it? Neither had I. Here's what it said (with italics added by me):



> 
> **Rules Text (Oracle)**:You may play Necromancy any time you could play an instant. If it was played any time a sorcery couldn't have been played, it gains *substance* until end of turn and when it loses *substance*, sacrifice it.
> 
> 
> When Necromancy comes into play, if it's in play, it becomes an Aura with enchant creature. Put target creature card from a graveyard into play under your control and attach Necromancy to it.
> 
> 
> When Necromancy leaves play, destroy enchanted creature. It can't be regenerated.
> 
> 
> 


Substance appears to be a property exclusive to the *Mirage/Visions* “instantments,” enchantments that can be played at instant-speed but end up being sacrificed at the end of the turn if you do so. The [Comprehensive Rulebook](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=magic/rules/tourneyplayer) mentions Substance twice:



> 
> 502.49. Substance  
>  502.49a Substance is a static ability with no effect.
> 
> 
> Substance  
>  Substance is a static ability with no effect. Certain older cards have received errata that give them substance for a brief period of time.
> 
> 
> 


Weird, huh? Brings to mind The Bard, William Shakespeare who said, “The very substance of the ambitious is merely the shadow of a dream.”

[Fallen Askari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Askari) saw some play in beatdown decks, and [Funeral Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Funeral+Charm) is a great little package of options: weenie creature-kill, boost your larger creature's power or let him swampwalk in, or take advantage of the very rare instant-speed discard spell. I remember getting a lot of mileage from [Funeral Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Funeral+Charm) in an old Extended [Pox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pox) deck that fought the [High Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Tide) menace back in 1999:

[Tithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe) is an incredible powerhouse card that saw a ton of play in formats where dual lands were legal, fetching two for one card advantage and enabling White/X color combinations, all at instant speed. [Tithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe) gets a shot in the arm with *Ravnica* - not only are there new duals now that count as Plains ([Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden), [Sacred Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry), [Godless Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine)), but the “Karoo” lands also help get your land count down in order to maximize [Tithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe)'s Plains-tutoring power. [Tithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe) played an important role in “Pro Tour Jank” or “Richmond Gun” decks that employed a strategy way back in the day reminiscent of modern day Boros. ### *Ravnica* Block Constructed 4x Open (715154) Recap


*Saturday, March 25 2006*


100 players mustered out for the event that was both a 4x payout and a qualifying tournament for the E3 Championship this weekend. The unholy alliance of Golgari and Orzhov seems to hold sway over the format currently, with powerful early removal spells ramping up to late game powerhouses like [Skeletal Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Vampire), [Angel of Despair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair) and [Debtors' Knell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Debtors%27+Knell). Cephalid\_PL's aggro deck had some success pushing through enough damage to win before control's inevitability set in, using efficient damage dealers from the Gruul and Boros guilds. way2Sleazy4u went for a more focused Orzhov strategy, utilizing Teysa, [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Skeletal Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Vampire)s and the slow drain of Orzhova. AlexH and zyklone went nuts with the mana fixing, utilizing cards like [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) and [Civic Wayfinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder) to assemble four-color behemoths of good cards like [Firemane Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Angel)s, [Loxodon Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch)s, and [Ribbons of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ribbons+of+Night). AnssiA's deck had similar elements to the G/B/W control decks, but ran [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu) for some early beatdown.

Kirushi:



> 
>  “When we started testing Ravnica block after Guildpact was released, it became clear that the format, at least for now, could be split up into G/W/B control decks and decks that lose to G/W/B control decks. Knowing this, I built the deck with the intention of focusing on the mirror as much as possible. 
> 
> 
> ![Rolling Spoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rolling+Spoil)“The mirror match, generally speaking, is won by the player who gets to seven mana and starts casting [Angel of Despair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair) first, but when it comes down to it, it's really more who gets to four mana and starts casting [Rolling Spoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Spoil) first, as they're going to get to seven first too. As a result, we played a full 4 [Golgari Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Signet)s on top of 4 [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) to power out [Rolling Spoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Spoil), the card that really defines the format being a strong weapon against both control and aggro. Also, we played [Congregation at Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Congregation+at+Dawn) to line up the Angels when you reach seven mana. Finally for some protection against removal, along with a great way to end a board stalemate, we played a few copies of [Ghostway](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostway) to go along with all the comes into play effects.
>  “Between myself and clanmate ubermike who played the same maindeck, the deck was 14-3-1 in matches.
> 
> 
> “The name Got Rice comes from a spoof song that circulated on the internet several years ago. For MTGO, my clan uses the more family friendly name Got Rice Barn, and Bat seemed to fit naturally. If that's inappropriate you can just call it Ghostway.dec or [Ghostway](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostway) Despair Control or something.
> 
> 
> “Since Ravnica Block Constructed isn't used in tournaments in the paper Magic world (and therefore there is less strategy writing and netdecks), you see a lot of different decks from people just trying things out like [Storm Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Storm+Herd) or Savra or [Mindmoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil) with Niv-Mizzet. The dominant archetype so far is GWB [Angel of Despair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair)-based control with many different variations sometimes including Red for [Firemane Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Angel), or splashing for the Blue costs in [Dimir Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildmage) or [Ribbons of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ribbons+of+Night). Right now it seems like the aggressive decks are still finding out what colors they want to be, and how to deal with the [Rolling Spoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Spoil), [Seize the Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seize+the+Soul), [Culling Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+Sun), [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify), [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) and [Skeletal Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Vampire)s being constantly thrown at them along with several good life gain cards. I'm sure no matter what colors the best aggro deck ends up playing, it will play [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and it probably won't play [Rumbling Slum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rumbling+Slum).”
> 
> 
> 


Mike Laviolette (ubermike) had this to add: 



> 
> “My team knows for a fact that hitting four mana (for [Rolling Spoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Spoil)) and seven mana (for [Angel of Despair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair)) quickly is the key to winning, hence the mad acceleration. The Caryatids work amazingly with [Ghostway](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostway) and they also buy me time vs. aggro decks, and [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify) is a very flexible removal spell.
> 
> 
> “[Ghostway](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostway) is the heart of the deck, swinging stalemates dramatically in my favor. In round 4 I cast a [Ghostway](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostway) with 2 Caryatids and 3 Angels in play with damage on the stack, saving them all and then End of Turn draw two cards and destroying three permanents, for just three mana...ouch!
> 
> 
> “[Congregation at Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Congregation+at+Dawn) is another big game winner, setting up turn after turn of Angels or Hierarchs, or fetching the [Helldozer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helldozer).
> 
> 
> “In the sideboard, the [Trophy Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trophy+Hunter) as a Congregation target used vs. birds/bats has come in handy a few times. 
> 
> 
> “Kirushi and myself decided to invest in RBC right after it came out and together we built a Glare deck splashing Black. After that it was all uphill! Every time one of us won a Premier Event, we would change decks to beat the metagame we helped set. Right now, there is a whole lot of Black/White/Green control, with a tiny bit of aggro mixed in.”

### Tribal Wars 4x Open (706922) Recap


*Sunday, March 26 2006*


90 players came out for this event that was also both a 4x payout and a qualifying tournament for the E3 Championship this weekend. The old standard powerhouse, [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail), continues to dominate in the hands of the Shaman tribe, but it was the Human tribe's [Auriok Salvagers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Salvagers) combo deck that took the brass ring, turbo-charged by *Mirage's* [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond). I'm not sure what Geoster's Human deck was trying to accomplish, I saw [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault), [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) and [Aether Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Spellbomb). It may have included the Salvagers combo too and used Disciples as “burn.” Knights, Goblins and Clerics rounded out the rest of the Top 8. 


Here were the final standings:  
 1st Place: Brusher, Human Salvager Combo  
 2nd Place: tankong, Shaman Tooth and Nail  
 4th Place: yorick, Knights  
 4th Place: Monbailly Olivier, Shaman Tooth and Nail  
 8th Place: Gula, Shaman Tooth and Nail  
 8th Place: tcarmody, Goblins  
 8th Place: Yeufann, Clerics  
 8th Place: Geoster, Humans (artifacts)

Mon Lee (yorick) had this to say: 



> 
> “The deck is a straight forward White Weenie deck backed by burn to clear a path and finish the game. Efficient creatures and cheap burn have a good history. I had played knights in the first 4x tribal event and had some minor success. A few tweaks and some changes to the creature base got it to the current form. [Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) is fantastic when almost all my creatures have protection from red or have flying. The current batch of top decks would either be one combo (Salvagers) or the other combo ([Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail)) deck.”
