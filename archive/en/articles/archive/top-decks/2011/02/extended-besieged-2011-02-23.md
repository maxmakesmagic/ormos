Extended Besieged!
==================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on February 24, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











We've limited most of our *Mirrodin Besieged* discussions up to this point to Standard ... but there is a PTQ season going on!

So this week we are going to look at how the new set has impacted Extended, the present PTQ season format.

Sword of Feast and Famine
The most impressive card for Extended so far—and maybe the most impressive card from *Mirrodin Besieged* over all—is [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine). Before we get into specific decks, I thought it might be interesting to think about just how good [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) is.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td131_sword.jpg)  
Last week on Twitter there was some discussion around [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) versus [Sword of Body and Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Body+and+Mind). Personally, I don't think there is much debate here. What does [Sword of Body and Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Body+and+Mind) do? It makes a Wolf token and flips over ten of your opponent's cards. I think Conrad Kolos was the first person to look at it this way, but... *what should that cost?*

Personally, I think that Millstone-Wolf should cost about two mana. Maybe three. But no one would play it for three mana. Is flipping over ten cards good? It can be, and there are situations, like when you have a [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) or a [Survival of the Fittest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest), where drawing multiples can be degenerate. But in general? *Just* flipping over ten cards isn't much to write home about. Case in point: many players were skeptical about playing [Broken Ambitions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broken+Ambitions), because if you *won* the clash, you might be improving the opponent's draw.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Now how would you cost the ability on [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine)? How much should forcing your opponent to discard a card—but untapping all of your lands as well—cost? [Turnabout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turnabout) broke Extended at four mana, so I'm guessing some amount of mana greater than four!

Here is a different way of looking at [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) that Steve Sadin and I came up with last week: What is a turn? Brian Weissman used to say a turn could mostly be boiled down to 1) drawing a card, and 2) playing a land. You can also attack... but it's mostly mana and cards.

And that's what [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) does ya for.

By forcing the opponent to discard a card, [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) kind of takes away half a turn. And while you don't get a land *drop*, the "untap all your lands" ability on the new Sword is very reminiscent of the other half.

Back in 1997, we used to have a saying "Everything is a [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk)," and attributed that to anything from a [Man-o'-War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Man-o%27-War) to a [Memory Lapse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Lapse). Well, [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) seems quite [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk)-ish to me.

While, as an artifact, [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) can go into a good number of different decks, there are two main styles that seem perfect for it: White-Blue and Faeries.

You can tell how important Tanahashi's change was because so many other Faeries decks have taken up the banner since Masayasu's 2nd place in Paris.

[Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine), here, is taking the place vacated by [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte). The Sword only costs one more mana than [Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jitte) did, and is arguably more synergistic in Faeries. You see, Faeries is a deck that is mostly interested in playing spells on the opponent's turn: [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) during the upkeep, [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) in response to a threat, or [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) at the end of turn (or whenever seems the best opportunity).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td131_3Cards1.jpg)  
Though Faeries certainly has some spells it has to play on its own turn, after you've got the second-turn [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) down, those cards are mostly limited to one-mana [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) or [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek).

... So what's the deal with [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) in this deck?

Yes, you have to invest three on your own turn (once), and then any time you want to equip your creatures, you have to invest two mana on your own turn. However, untapping all your lands is frankly awesome for a deck that wants to play mostly on the opponent's turn! And though Faeries doesn't play a whole heck of a lot of creatures, [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) gives you a never-ending stream of potential swordsmen. Making them Protection from Black and Protection from Green is just fantastic in this format.

Do you know what is a total disaster (at least for the opponent)? This:

Another existing deck that has benefited from new options in *Mirrodin Besieged* is Steel Artifact.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td131_signal.jpg)  
[Signal Pest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) fits right into this deck, which was already focused on playing multiple artifact creatures and attacking in a gang with big ups from [Master of Etherium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium) and [Tempered Steel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel). Not only does [Signal Pest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) also get the same bonuses, but shares the love—or in this case the hateful, hateful, violence—with its fellow attackers.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td131_3Cards2.jpg)  
But what really classes up the joint is [Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl128_tezupclose.jpg)  
Tezzeret gives Steel Artifact yet another big threat; but unlike [Master of Etherium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium) and [Tempered Steel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel), a way to win that does not require the red zone. Tezzeret's "[Impulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse)"-like ability has a very high batting average in this deck (Schieffer's Steel Artifact is greater than 50% artifacts), and while all three abilities shine in Steel Artifact, the speed and savagery of his ultimate are unprecedented.

Coffey waded through a sea of Faeries, Jund, and [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) to take home Mobile's Blue Envelope with what looks like a Standard deck!

This is a Kuldotha Red swarm deck, using [Signal Pest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) and [Contested War Zone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone) to deal a ton of damage very quickly. With an optimal draw, Coffey can actually win on turn two:

**Turn One:**

The addition of battle cry makes for an interesting tension. On the one hand, you have a strategy traditionally based on creatures attacking alone. [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) showcase the Bant signature mechanic early, then [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour), the ultimate in exalted, tops things off as a finisher.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td131_hero.jpg)  
But with [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold), you have a creature that not only doesn't attack alone, but never attacks alone!

... Except when it does.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

You can attack with [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold), put exalted (and even [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour)'s other triggered ability) on the stack, get in for bonus damage, line up the little Soldiers, get exalted, give away battle cry, and basically break more rules than Ric Flair in the 80s.

Cute.

And like Hit-Girl from *Kick-Ass*, cute and *deadly*.

Say all you did was accelerate into [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) with [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), then cast a fourth-turn [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour) and attacked solo with the Hero. This is what would happen:


> 1. [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) attacks.
> 2. Stack two exalted triggers, [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour)'s other trigger, battle cry, and [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold)'s token trigger.
> 3. [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) makes two little guys, which then get battle cry bonuses (2 and 2).
> 4. [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) untaps thanks to [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour), with an additional combat phase on deck.
> 5. [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) gets two exalted bonuses.
> 6. You are now attacking with [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) (5), and two Soldiers (4)—9 damage.
> 7. Unbelievably (remember, we are the captain of the Four Horsemen here), you get *another* attack! [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) goes in again, this time starting at 5 power. (The two tokens are still tapped, and don't have haste anyway, so they stay home.)
> 8. Stack two exalted triggers, battle cry, and [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold)'s token trigger.
> 9. [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) makes two more little guys, who get battle cry bonuses (2 and 2).
> 10. [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) goes from 5 to 7 power thanks to exalted.
> 11. You are now attacking with [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) (7) and two Soldier tokens (4), for 11 total. With your initial 9, that's lethal!

Azulah finished just out of the Top 8 of the recent **Magic Online** PTQ. I just wanted to highlight the same potential combinations in Naya.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td131_mirranCrusader.jpg)  
For a moment I got super excited about [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) in this deck, which can set up [Behemoth Sledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge). Unfortunately, that particular equipment is a non-bo with [Mirran Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader)'s protection from green ability. That's not to say that there aren't savage possibilities in this deck, just that we won't soon see a marriage between these two three-drops.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mirran+Crusader)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader)  
As we can see, *Mirrodin Besieged* has a ton to contribute to the PTQ player. Even the most established, linear deck in the format can benefit from its premier Equipment, and pretty much everyone from Naya to Steel Artifact can get a boost. Standard decks are graduating and claiming Blue Envelopes... and they are probably just the beginning.
