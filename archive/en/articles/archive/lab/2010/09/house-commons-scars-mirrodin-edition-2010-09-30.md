House of Commons: Scars of Mirrodin Edition!
============================================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on September 30, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hi all, and welcome back to the first post-*Scars of Mirrodin* Prerelease edition of From the Lab. Every time a new large set is released, I like to complete a traditional puzzle called House of Commons. Created by Chris Millar, House of Commons is essentially a deck-building exercise that uses every common from the latest large set (in this case, *Scars of Mirrodin*.)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl110_houseOfCommonsLogo.jpg)  
Here's a quick rundown of the guidelines. I had to use four copies of every common to build a lot of decks. So, for example, I couldn't put four copies of [Galvanic Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) in all my red decks, and I'd have to use four copies of [Whitesun's Passage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitesun%27s+Passage) at some point. The resulting array of decks (eleven, in this case) is spread out across all five colors in a (hopefully) balanced fashion.

Whenever I attempt this puzzle, I always accidentally save some of the best commons for last, making that deck pretty unfair. So I split them up again, leading to some strange results. The four copies of [Instill Infection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Instill+Infection) (which, by the way, I witnessed being cast on a [Precursor Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precursor+Golem) at the Prerelease) became four singletons in four different black decks, which I thought was weird. [Melt Terrain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melt+Terrain) and [Blunt the Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blunt+the+Assault) were other odd placements.

I also am interested in what color combinations I end up with. Green white, black green, and black blue led the pack with two decks each. Most of the rest contained red, which was strangely difficult to split up. Oh well. Let's see what I came up with!

Today's first deck is a mostly red weenie deck, with a slight green splash. [Oxidda Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidda+Daredevil) and [Vulshok Heartstoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Heartstoker) bring the heat early on, while [Flameborn Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flameborn+Hellion) cleans up in the late game. There's lots of equipment for [Goblin Gaveleer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Gaveleer), as well as some combat tricks like [Assault Strobe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Strobe), [Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter), and [Tel-Jilad Defiance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tel-Jilad+Defiance).

This next deck is a green and white look at the metalcraft ability. Cheap artifacts, like the Replicas and [Accorder's Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accorder%27s+Shield), should pump up [Carapace Forger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carapace+Forger), [Auriok Sunchaser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Sunchaser), and [Ghalma's Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghalma%27s+Warden) for some solid beats. [Arrest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest) acts as general creature shutdown.
