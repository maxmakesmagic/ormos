The Undead Grass is Always Greener
==================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 26, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods












With *Return to Ravnica* putting both Rakdos and Golgari on display, it was not difficult to figure out that Zombies—Jund, BR, or BG—was going to be pretty good in the new metagame. We have taken a look at [the Jund variety](/en/articles/archive/artistic-zombie-aristocrats-2012-10-15) already and black-red has been the standard for quite awhile. But while the rumors of black-green have swirled around, we really have not had a good example of the list performing. Well, noted deck builder Gerry Thompson figured it was time to end that at the 2012s event on Sunday of [Grand Prix San Jose](/en/events/coverage/sperling-williams-and-rietzl-win-san-jose). 

![Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dreg+Mangler) Gerry put away the [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat)s and picked up [Lotleth Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll)s, securing him a Top 8 finish in the event. [Lotleth Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll) put on quite the show at [Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*](/en/events/coverage/cifka-savors-pro-tour-breakfast-champions), but his appearance in Standard really shows how powerful and versatile the card can be. Imagine this scenario: Your opponent leads with a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and a [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul). Next turn, he or she follows that up with an innocent-looking [Lotleth Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll). Cute, you think. Next turn,your opponent attacks with the pair of 2-power creatures, but before anything happens, two [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)s are discarded to the Troll and then cast out of the graveyard, leaving regeneration mana up the whole time! 

 Of course, [Lotleth Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll) is not the only green card in the deck. [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) gives the list another scary three-drop that is also a Zombie. [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) has always been powerful, but now there are two threats that present immediate damage once turn three shows up. And, of course, longtime beatdown staple [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) gives back the reach the deck lost when red left it. This list is just another deadly way to take your Zombies to FNM, so be prepared for a whole lot of walking dead!
