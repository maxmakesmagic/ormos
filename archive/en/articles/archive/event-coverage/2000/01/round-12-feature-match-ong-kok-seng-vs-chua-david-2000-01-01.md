Round 12 Feature Match: Ong, Kok Seng vs. Chua, David
=====================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**







![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Jun-Wei-2.jpg)
By Jun-Wei Hew











For those who remember, Ong had managed to fight his way through the single-elimination last minute trials in order to earn his three byes with his trusted GoMar deck. Today, he is using exactly the same deck, card for card, and is now standing at 8-3 and on the bubble for the Top 8. His opponent, David Chua is well known to be just a casual player, who had only placed in one Top 8 in the Trials that were held over the past two months. Suddenly, the casual player now found himself mired in pressure as he too is playing to stay in contention for the Top 8. Nonetheless, his relaxed composure never fell as he found the time to tease and torment this poor reporter.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPSING01/934.jpg)Ong kicked off the first game with a second turn [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage), naming Fire/Ice. Chua then put a [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) in front of it, and Ong [Vindicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vindicate)d it before Chua had the opportunity to regenerate it. A fourth turn [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) gave Chua the opportunity to split [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt), [Absorb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb), [Spectral Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Lynx) and two [Coastal Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coastal+Tower)s. Ong chose to take the two Towers and the [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt). Obviously forgetting the fact that he had just given Ong a [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt), Chua attempted his own fourth turn [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Needless to say, Ong [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)ed it.


Meanwhile, the [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) had already paid four visits to the red zone, each time scoring a hit. Chua held a [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt) in his hand, but couldn't draw the fifth land needed to cast it. When he finally drew the much-needed land, Ong had almost enough land to threaten three counterspells. He still tried for the [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt), which Ong [Undermine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undermine)d. Chua dropped down to five life.


Now the lands started to come. Drawing a mountain, Chua's hand was beautiful: three [Undermine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undermine)s, two Fire/Ice and an [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude). Unfortunately, he had no way to rid himself of the [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage), which had so conveniently named "Fire/Ice".


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPSING01/933.jpg)The [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) visited for two more points. Chua dropped down to three.


Chua attempted a newly drawn [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), which Ong tried to [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude) away. Still thinking on whether to use one of his three [Undermine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undermine)s, Chua eventually decided against it, and [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude)d his own [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), both to dig deeper into his deck for answers, and to deprive Ong of the card he'd draw from his own [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude). However, when another end step [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) from Ong revealed a [Spectral Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Lynx) and two [Dromar's Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromar%27s+Charm)s, Chua decided that the best decision was the split the piles into two [Dromar's Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromar%27s+Charm)s and [Spectral Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Lynx)-[Dromar's Cavern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromar%27s+Cavern) and [Tsabo's Decree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tsabo%27s+Decree). For obvious reasons, Ong took both Charms.


Next turn, [Dromar, the Banisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromar%2C+the+Banisher) in person (or is that in dragon?) finally convinced Chua that the game was over.


Ong 1 - Chua 0


Ong had a plan for the next game. He rotated his creatures, taking out the Dromar, and all four [Spectral Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Lynx), in addition to two [Vindicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vindicate)s, two [Tsabo's Decree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tsabo%27s+Decree) and a [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) in exchange for 4 [Galina's Knights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galina%27s+Knights), 2 [Urborg Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Shambler)s and 3 [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s. Chua took out a [Lobotomy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobotomy), an [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude), an [Urza's Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Rage) and two [Recoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil)s. He brought in four [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s and one [Obliterate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obliterate).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPSING01/932.jpg)Chua started the second game with two nonblack lands. On his third turn, it became apparent that he suffered the fate that many players face in *Invasion* Block Constructed- manascrew. Ong capitalized on Chua's misfortune with a fourth turn [Urborg Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Shambler). When [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) tried to come into play, Chua held a [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay), but Ong called upon Dromar's charming tricks and the [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) came into play, naming [Urza's Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Rage). Chua's only option to buy some time while Ong was tapped out was to [Repulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) the [Urborg Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Shambler), which reentered play on Ong's next turn. When Chua then tried to [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt) the [Urborg Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Shambler), Ong Disrupted it, and then followed up with a [Galina's Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galina%27s+Knight).


Now Ong has [Galina's Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galina%27s+Knight), [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) and [Urborg Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Shambler) in play, facing absolutely no opposition from an opponent who's at three life. There aren't many cards in this format that would get Chua out of this sticky mess, although Balance with triple Pyroblast backup would just about do the trick.


Final Result: Ong 2 - Chua 0
