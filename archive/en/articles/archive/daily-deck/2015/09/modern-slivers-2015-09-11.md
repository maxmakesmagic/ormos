Modern Slivers
==============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on September 11, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Just when I thought Modern was solved, Adam Bowman made Top 8 of the SCG Open in Cincinnati with an innovative Slivers deck. Slivers is a mechanic that has reappeared three times since its initial arrival in *Tempest* block. It was the backbone of a pretty strong deck, Counterslivers, but since then Slivers really hasn't been anything but fringe.


The cards that make the deck are definitely the Sliver lords, [Sinew Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sinew+Sliver) and [Predatory Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predatory+Sliver). [Manaweft Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manaweft+Sliver) also plays an important role, and when it's combined with [Sentinel Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel+Sliver), you are able to attack while still adding mana and casting spells post-combat. [Galerider Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galerider+Sliver) ties everything together by giving your team flying, and similar to Merfolk, eventually your creatures will become too big to handle.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Predatory+Sliver)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predatory+Sliver)
This deck is capable of some really cool plays, and it was fun seeing it in action in the coverage last weekend. I remember Adam played a [Predatory Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predatory+Sliver) on turn two and then passed his turn three without attacking and with three mana up. The commentators were a bit confused and wondered if he missed his attack, but on his opponent's turn, he [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial)ed in a [Manaweft Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manaweft+Sliver), tapped his [Predatory Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predatory+Sliver) for mana, and then cast [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), gaining an overwhelming board presence.


The Slivers deck also has some amazing utility in [Necrotic Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Sliver) and [Harmonic Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonic+Sliver). [Necrotic Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Sliver) in particular can take a little while to set up, but with an active [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial), you can really disrupt your opponent's attempts to get anything going. It's especially powerful to attack with an army of Slivers and then, after your opponent makes a double block, Vial in a Sliver to blow up one of the blockers. [Sliver Hive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Hive) also does a pretty good job of giving you instant Slivers that gain the powers of all your other creatures.


Congrats to Adam for taking a unique strategy all the way to the Top 8 of a 651-player tournament!
