TOP 8 COVERAGE




 Hitomi Masaaki has etched his name in the history books to become the ninth Grand Prix Singapore Champion. After only just making the cut to day 2, Masaaki rocked ‘em and socked ‘em left and right to cruise into the Top 8 as 4th seed.



With Affinity absent from the Top 8 at Grand Prix Copenhagen last weekend, Masaaki felt the time for Robots was now, and after besting Yuta Takahashi’s Temur Twin in the quarterfinals, he then battled through Keita Kawasaki’s Jund deck to face Steven Tan in the finals in the Affinity Mirror. Clearly aware Affinity was going to be the deck to beat, Masaaki came equipped with 3 Ancient Grudge in his sideboard, and after Tan mulliganed to 5 in the final game, Masaaki emerged the victor.



 Also in the Top 8, Reid Duke added another 4 Pro Points to his total, getting him ever closer to Platinum, while Jason Chung, who lost his win-and-in match, still managed to lock up Platinum for this season, and overtook Shota Yasooka for the 3rd Asia-Pacific slot for the World Championships later this year.




 Join us next weekend for Grand Prix events in both Montréal and Lille, as we get closer and closer to the end of this season at Pro Tour *Magic Origins* at the end of July.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



The sun has already set here in Singapore and after 9 rounds of intense competition, the field of 1108 has been boiled down to just 132.


Grand Prix Manila 2010 finalist Taufik Indrakesuma, Chen Sijin and Keita Kawsaki lead the pack, surviving the entire day without a single scratch. With perfect 9-0 records, these three players can reward themselves with a good night's rest before returning tomorrow for six more rounds of Modern.


Hot on their heels are former Indonesian National Champion Benny Soewanda, Pro Tour Dragons of Tarkir Top 8 competitor Jason Chung, two-time Grand Prix Top 8 competitor and former Singapore National Champion Steven Tan, three-time Grand Prix Champion are all at 8-1 records. Superstars No.4 Lee Shi Tian, No. 25 Reid Duke, Martin Jůza are all also at 24 points.


Nicholas Wong, Sugaya Hironobu, Ken Yukuhiro, Nam Sung Wook, Shota Yasooka, Kentaro Yamamoto, Yuuki Ichikawa, Leong Ding Yuan will also be returning tomorrow, as is Alexander Hayne who is looking to further his lead in the Grand Prix Race.


While the curtains are being drawn here in Singapore, the action is just getting started at Grand Prix Buenos Aires! Why don't you head over and check out the action on the other side of the world and we'll see you back here tomorrow?


It's a date!
