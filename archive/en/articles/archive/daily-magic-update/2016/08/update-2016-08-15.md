What You Might Have Missed This Weekend
=======================================


1. [A Meeting of Mages: Peeking into the Planeswalker Summit](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppor16/a-meeting-of-mages-planeswalker-summit-2016-08-14) | Wizards of the Coast | Meghan Wolff

Popular cosplayer MJ Scott ([@moxymtg](https://twitter.com/moxymtg) on Twitter) organized a massive cosplayer summit at [Grand Prix Portland](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppor16/). Read on to see what happened (hint: there was a lot of posing for photos).


1. [StarCityGames.com Syracuse Open](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/130816_syracuse.html)

Modern was the main name of the game on the plains of—New York. Okay, I could only keep that going so long, but there was a lot of Modern going on in Syracuse while the Grand Prix handled Standard. Ross Merriam won the whole thing with Dredge (uh, spoiler alert?), but the [Top 32 decklists](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/deckshow.php?&t%5BC1%5D=28&start_date=08/13/2016&end_date=08/13/2016&start=1&finish=32&event_ID=47&city=SYracuse&limit=50) offer a variety of interesting strategies, and the [play-by-play](https://www.twitch.tv/scglive/v/83567546) is worth watching if you have a few hours.


1. Top 5 Grand Prix Moments

Both Rimini and Portland highlighted their Top 5 Moments, as we do. Frank Karsten's [highlights from Rimini](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gprim16/top-5-moments-2016-08-14) includes some tech, the rise of a new Standard powerhouse, and the top stories of the weekend. [Over in Portland](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppor16/top-5-moments-2016-08-14), Marc Calderaro and Meghan Wolff covered adorable kittens, tough-looking cosplayers, and the second generation of *Magic* Micks.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Cat Pact](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppor16/top-8-decklists-2016-08-14)

Like this isn't going to be the same deck that everyone features today. Sure, Bant Company had a heck of a weekend (taking six of the Top 8 spots in Grand Prix Rimini and another three in Portland), but no one had a better weekend than Chris Botelho.


Botelho came with that deck your buddy went 3-1 with at FNM that one time, tuned it, souped it up, and wrecked the field at Grand Prix Portland, where he was the first overall seed in the Top 8 before falling in the quarterfinals. The deck? Lovingly called "Cat Pact," it's a [Demonic Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Pact)-[Harmless Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmless+Offering) deck.


The deck is essentially a Grixis control deck that looks to use [Demonic Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Pact) as both an advantage engine *and* a combo finish. Once [Demonic Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Pact) has only one option left, donating it to your opponent with [Harmless Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmless+Offering) causes them to lose the game on their next upkeep. The deck also plays a suite of Oaths—[Jace's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=407570) and [Chandra's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=407623)—to protect [Demonic Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Pact) from [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command), but the rest of the deck is made up of control elements, ways to mitigate [Demonic Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Pact), and some tutors to find the combo elements.


The deck looks to be the real deal (I took it for a spin on *Magic Online* while the Top 8 was going on), so don't be surprised if more people than your funny friend decide to bring this or something like it to an FNM near you.
