Wizards Asks: 02/14/2012
========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 14, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











*What's your favorite Standard deck from [Pro Tour Dark Ascension](/en/events/coverage/kibler-triumphs-epic-pro-tour)?*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/authorpics/authorpic_kellydigges.jpg)**Kelly Digges:** It's always fun to see what the pros brew up in a new Constructed format, especially in the innovation-friendly Standard format. The finals featured two Wolf Run Ramp decks that look pretty similar to last season's (with the hugely important addition of [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells)), but the rest of the field took a lot of fresh looks at the format: white-based weenie, various flavors of control, mono-green, mono-black, white-black token decks, and all kinds of other stuff.

My favorite decks from this Pro Tour are the [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks. Pod decks comes in a variety of flavors, with black-green and green-white-blue being the usual flavors. This tournament, though, saw the emergence of red-green-white Pod decks, exemplified by Lukas Blohon's Top 8 deck:
