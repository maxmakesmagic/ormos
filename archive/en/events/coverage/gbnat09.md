DG says GG for GB
=================












2009 Great Britain National Championship
----------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat09/blurb_end.jpg)And so we've reached the end of the line. With Great Britain Nationals coming at the end of the season, it looked as if there might not be much room for innovative deckbuilding. Perhaps Limited would hold the key to the title? Not so, as the hard work of Dan Gardner paid the highest of dividends. After twelve rounds of Swiss action, he bested Mark Glenister, Mick Edwards, and finally Chris Rossiter, to take the title. He's joined on the official GB Team by Rossiter and Peter Mottram, who defeated Edwards in the 3rd/4th Playoff. Edwards' consolation prize is a spot in the Worlds starting lineup. Congratulations to Edwards, Mottram, Rossiter, and of course to Dan Gardner, the Great Britain Champion of 2009!

Quarterfinals - Danny Gardner v Mark Glenister
----------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin*

Danny has been at the top of the standings for the entire weekend, picking people apart in Standard with his Baneslayer control deck that he worked on along side the Orsini Jones brothers and limited with powerful aggressive decks.


Mark, fresh off a 16th place finish at Pro Tour – Honolulu, was just barely able to squeak into the top 8 with 3 losses. Mark also brought an original deck with him to Nationals, a lightning fast Red-Green beatdown deck featuring [Colossal Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colossal+Might).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat09/QF%20Glenister.jpg)*Mark Glenister*While the players were poring over each others decklists began feeling pretty grim about his chances in the quarterfinals.


“I have so many mediocre cards against you. If you can figure out how I should sideboard please let me know,” said Mark.


“When I figure it out I’ll be sure to tell you,” quipped Danny.


Mark won the flip and got things started fast with a [Tattermunge Maniac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattermunge+Maniac) and a [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny). Danny’s first play was an evoked [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter). Mark cast a [Colossal Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colossal+Might) to knock Danny down to 6.


Danny again did nothing to improve his board on his fourth turn, merely playing two [Fieldmist Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fieldmist+Borderpost)s and passing the turn. Mark made a [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) threatening lethal damage, but Danny had a [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to keep himself in the game at a mere two life.


Danny briefly hoped that his [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) might allow him to take over, but Mark had a [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) to deal the final two points of damage.


**Glenister 1 – Gardner 0**


“Alright, let’s see if I can find 11 cards to board out,” said Mark who was clearly happy to have started the match up a game.


**Game 2**  

Danny started off with a mulligan prompting Mark, who was agonizing about his hand, to say “I wish I knew how low you were going to go...” before deciding to keep.


“Risky business,” said Danny who then drew a two borderpost, no land hand forcing him to go down to 5 cards.


Danny had a turn two [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid) and evoked [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) on turn three to get himself on the road to recovery.


Mark’s first play was a turn three [Great Sable Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag). Danny passed turn four without a play allowing him to [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) Mark’s [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) (which flipped an [Unwilling Recruit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unwilling+Recruit)), drawing a card in the process.


Danny [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile)d Mark’s [Great Sable Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag) on his next attack. After combat Mark cast a [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny) and a [Manabarbs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs). In response to the [Manabarbs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs), Danny used another [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to get rid of Mark’s freshly cast Figure.


Danny attacked, knocking Mark to 14 and added a [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) to his team. Mark had another [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny), but pumping it would come at quite a price in the face of his own [Manabarbs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs).


When Danny attacked with both of his creatures Mark blocked the [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid) with his Figure and took four damage to make it a 4/4. Danny was ready with the [Celestial Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Purge) leaving Mark, who was on a mere 8 after the attack, with little room to maneuver.


“I’m boarding [Manabarbs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs) out for the next game. This card is rubbish against you”


A couple of attacks later and they were off to Game 3.


Danny made no changes to his sideboard while Mark swapped out his two [Manabarbs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs) face up for a couple more burn spells.


**Glenister 1 – Gardner 1**


**Game 3**  

Danny was again forced to mulligan in game 3, but this time he was able to stay on 6 cards.


Mark started off with a [Tattermunge Maniac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattermunge+Maniac) while Danny had the combo of [Fieldmist Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fieldmist+Borderpost) plus [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid) to put himself in pretty good shape moving forward.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat09/QF%20Gardner.jpg)*Danny Gardner*Mark cast a [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) and was then forced to attack his [Tattermunge Maniac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattermunge+Maniac) into his opponent’s 2/2 first striker.


A [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) from Danny was met with a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) from Mark. The cascader flipped up [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose), which was then used to cast [Jund Hackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Hackblade). But because of the first striking [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid), Mark chose to attack with only his [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang).


Danny passed with no play and Mark cast a second [Jund Hackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Hackblade) before attacking with all four of his three power creatures. Danny, who was on 12, thought for a bit before Path to Exiling the [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang), blocking a [Jund Hackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Hackblade) with a [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) and [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) with [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid).


With [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage)’s persist on the stack Mark [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)ed the [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid).


Danny untapped, attacked for one and slammed down a [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel).


One attack phase and one more [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) later and the players were off to Game 4.


**Glenister 1 – Gardner 2**


**Game 4**  

In Game 4 Mark had to start things off with a mulligan.


And another mulligan.


And yet another mulligan.


“Keep,” shrugged Mark.


Mark played a [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny), which he pumped on turn two, but he failed to follow that play up with a second land.


The second land also evaded him on turn three.


Mark did play a land on his fourth turn, but he didn’t have a play until his fifth turn when his attempt at a [Jund Hackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Hackblade) got [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command)ed.


A [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) from Danny followed by a [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) was more than enough for Danny to advance to the semifinals.


**Final Result**  
**Glenister 1 – Gardner 3**


**Danny Gardner advances to the Semifinals!**




Quarterfinals - Mick Edwards v Gregory Squire
---------------------------------------------


*by Richard Coates*

**Game 1**


Greg hails from Reading where he plays alongside such names as Calum Stephenson who made the finals of English Nationals in 2006, and has made the Top 8 today despite incurring two game losses from the Judging Team. “To even the field”, he boasts as he sat down to play his Doran aggro deck against Mick. Though he lives in Wolverhampton, Mick plays most of his Magic with Team Leeds, the stomping ground of Craig Stevenson, Callum’s vanquisher in the 2006 final.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat09/QF%20Squire.jpg)*Gregory Squire*Greg, the reigning Cube Champion of Reading, wanted to decide who goes first by Rock-Paper-Scissors, but Mick protested, and the die was brought out. This was in Mick’s favour, as he won, and decided to play. Mick led with [Vivid Meadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Meadow), after Greg reluctantly mulliganed twice, and Greg responded with a tapped land of his own, in the form of Murmering Bosk. A 2/2 [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny) followed for Mick, with a [Wren’s Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%E2%80%99s+Run+Vanquisher) for Greg. The Figure attacked, and was joined by a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) after Greg declined to block.


After the Finks was led down the [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), the Figure was hit by Greg’s [Nameless Inversion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion). [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant) entered the Battlefield to destroy Greg’s elf, who then responded with a [Gaddock Teeg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg), preventing more high-cost hijinks.


[Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) cascaded into [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) for Mick, and then attacked twice, blocked the second time by Greg’s [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech). After they dueled, and the Leech was pumped, [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) took out the Leech, and [Wilt-Leaf Liege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) sufferd a similar fate at the hands of [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) and [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm). Damage from multiple [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant) activations sealed the first game, and Mick took an early lead.


**Mick Edwards 1 – Gregory Squire 0**


**Game 2**


After extensive sideboarding from Mick, who brought in his “Doran Plan” of [Austere Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command)s, [Firespout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout)s, a [Call the Skybreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+the+Skybreaker) and the ‘Sideboard card of choice’, [Great Sable Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag), we moved on to game 2, where Greg was on the play. Both players kept their seven, and [Treefolk Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treefolk+Harbinger) fetched up a [Murmuring Bosk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murmuring+Bosk) to join the one played on Greg’s first turn. A second Harbinger tutored up a [Doran, the Siege Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower), and Mick could only respond with [Vivid Meadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Meadow) and [Reflecting Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool).

Finals - Chris Rossiter v Dan Gardner
-------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat09/The%20Final.JPG)*Chris Rossiter v Dan Gardner*


Across the feature match area, Peter Mottram and Mick Edwards are set to do battle for the 3rd spot on the GB Team for Worlds. Here, only one issue remains. Which of these two worthy contenders will be crowned the Champion? With UW [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark), Dan Gardner has powered his way to a 7-1 Standard record so far, while Combo Elves has been the weapon of choice for Chris Rossiter, who has had two losses so far in the tournament – both to Gardner...


Gardner won the toss, and opened the Final with [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress). [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) led to [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) for Chris, who added [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) on turn two. With the Sentinel on the stack, Gardner took the chance to aim [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) at the [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), allowing Chris to search out a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). With no mana engine available, Chris attacked for one, and passed.


Gardner also passed, but cast [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) in Rossiter’s upkeep, finding [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command), [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) and [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) in hand. The [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) went away, and Dan must have been hoping that Chris got less good fortune than Mick Edwards earlier in the Top 8, who’d found multiple Planeswalkers off the “Vendilion gamble”. Chris laid [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) and [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), and passed back to Dan, who Evoked [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) before sending the Clique through the air.


Once again, he had effects in Rossiter’s upkeep, this time using [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to remove the [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) from the game. He quickly spotted that he could have waited until Rossiter had drawn for the turn, marginally improving the chances of Rossiter finding a land. Chris added [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender), and [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) was next for Dan, who was making a good fist of an opening game that was meant to go the way of the Elves.


Five more points came across the red zone unopposed, and [Knight of the White Orchid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid) allowed Dan to thin his deck just a little more, searching out a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). [Fieldmist Borderpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fieldmist+Borderpost) completed the turn, with Chris at 9, who had no action.


Again, five damage piled in, and when Gardner laid a second [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage), Rossiter swept up his permanents.


Gardner 1 – Rossiter 0.


Both players were cagey about their Sideboard plans, largely revolving around whether or not Rossiter would actively switch from his Combo plan, or stick to his guns. Given that at least one of the players can possibly glimpse this screen, let’s just find out as we go. OK with you? Thanks.


[Great Sable Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag) was the first play of the game from Rossiter, and Gardner Evoked [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) on his third turn. With eight cards in hand, he was forced to discard a second [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter). [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) was next for Rossiter, and the Stag attacked for 3. The following turn, the [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) joined the fray, and Gardner was down to 12. Rossiter added a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), but at the end of turn Gardner had [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) to bounce the [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), drawing him a card.


[Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) set to [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) ensured that the re-appearance of the M10 Rare would be, at least, delayed. He added [Ethersworn Canonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist), precluding the possibility of a full-on Combo splurge (that being the technical term, clearly.) The [Great Sable Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag) dropped Gardner to 9, while [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) was Rossiter’s solitary (non-artifact) spell for the turn.


After [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) dealt the first couple of points to Rossiter, Gardner dropped his first mighty monster, the [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). That lasted until just the end of turn though, as Rossiter fired it out of the game with [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). Now the Stag put Gardner to 6, and a second copy of the super-popular M10 card was steadily loosening Gardner’s grip. His response? A second [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), and this time Rossiter didn’t immediately have the [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) as the perfect answer. What he did have, however, was [Oversoul of Dusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversoul+of+Dusk), which lasted less long than this sentence took to type, as Gardner’s [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) bid it a swift goodbye.


In came the Baneslayer for Gardner, creating a massive ten point life swing in his favor. [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) took Rossiter one card nearer to a much-needed [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), but another turn went by without the white Instant, and now Rossiter was in trouble. He attempted an [Oversoul of Dusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversoul+of+Dusk), but Gardner had the answer, using [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) to counter the massive monster, and tapping the Rossiter Elf squad. Gardner 2 – Rossiter 0.


[Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) was the perfect opening for Rossiter, who added [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) on turn two, but failed to play a land. Gardner cast [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) set to [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command), and Rossiter found the land he was missing. With a counter on the [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), he was able to run out an [Oversoul of Dusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversoul+of+Dusk). [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) on the following upkeep showed a second [Oversoul of Dusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversoul+of+Dusk), two Gaddock Teag, [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) and [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel). The Clique sent the Oversoul away, and we were back to wondering whether the Clique might conceivably misfire. [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) and [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) came down, and Gardner stood at 15.


An Evoked [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) followed, with the [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) turning sideways to bring Rossiter to 17. This time Rossiter held nothing back, sending [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), and [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel), all in support of the mighty [Oversoul of Dusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversoul+of+Dusk). [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) offed the Archdruid, reducing the killing power of the other Elves, allowing [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) to tangle with [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), and live. Even so, Gardner took seven, and fell to 8.


Another [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) was Evoked – Gardner had not choice, sitting at four mana – and his [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) reduced Rossiter to 14. Gaddock Teag resolved, posing a problem for any [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command)s, but Gardner had another [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) for the Oversoul that was threatening to overwhelm him.


In came the Clique yet again, and then the flying 3/1 was joined by [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). Were we on the brink of a famous sweep to victory? Rossiter offered [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) and [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), before passing. Gardner piled in with his flyers, and stood on the brink at 13-3 ahead.


Rossiter drew....


and extended the hand. He would go to Worlds in Rome as part of the GB Team, but, by three games to none, Dan Gardner was the Great Britain National Champion 2009!
