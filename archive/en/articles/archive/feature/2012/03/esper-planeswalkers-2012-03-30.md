Esper Planeswalkers
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 30, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






At the StarCityGames.com Invitational event last weekend, numerous notable pros battled it out in both Standard and Legacy. **Magic** deck builder Shaheen Soorani brought a new take on the old Solar Flare archetype: Esper Planeswalkers. Carrying him all the way to 3rd place, Soorani's new deck is shaping up to be a major force in Standard.


Named after the white-blue-black shard of Esper from *Shards of Alara* and featuring several of the cards you would expect from that color combination, the big focus of Soorani's deck is on its planeswalkers! [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura); [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil); and [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad) come together to pack a tag-team punch. Serving as the deck's primary route to victory, this trio of planeswalkers helps shut down creature-based assaults on the ground while also being proactive against control decks. If the planeswalker plan doesn't work out, [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) and [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) can help close out the game.


To back it up, the deck features many of the control elements you would expect to show up in Standard. Three [Curse of Death's Hold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) help shut down the token-based decks, and a plethora of removal helps fight any other creatures that pop up.


Most noteworthy, though, might be Soorani's copies of [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls)! By using the tokens primarily to buy time, the deck can stutter the opponent's attacks for long enough to find a [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment)—or build up a planeswalker army!


Check out the decklist below, and keep it in mind for Friday Night **Magic** tonight!
