Judging from the mana base, he looks like he's playing a traditional Abzan Company deck, until you get to creature base.


In a high-powered format like Modern, it is unexpected that cards such as [Tukatongue Thallid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tukatongue+Thallid) and [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) could become the cornerstone of one's strategy.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tukatongue+Thallid)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tukatongue+Thallid)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Doomed+Traveler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler)
[Abzan Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Ascendancy) turns these little dorks and [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) into a more decent size, while providing double the tokens upon death. This is probably why Yukuhiro likes the midrange and control matchup so much.


Yukuhiro cited [Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) and [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) as one of the primary routes to a fast victory, doing an impression that is reminiscent of [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) and [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault).


"I don't have an infinite combo, but I have a way to win fast."





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blood+Artist)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Viscera+Seer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer)
Eschewing [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) in favor of [Return to the Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Return+to+the+Ranks), it was bound to give any decks that relied on spot removal a splitting headache. The interaction between [Return to the Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Return+to+the+Ranks) and his creature base is probably what makes the deck tick.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Return+to+the+Ranks)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Return+to+the+Ranks)
"I can sacrifice all my creatures to [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) and get a lot of tokens. Then I can use those tokens to convoke [Return to the Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Return+to+the+Ranks) and get it all back."


However, Yukuhiro's deck is not without weakness.


While he built his deck to beat up against all the other aggro and midrange decks, he had to sacrifice the combo matchup which he professes is beyond terrible. His only line of defense in the maindeck was [Tidehollow Sculler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) and could sometimes even be dead even before he gets to cast it.


"I really don't want to play against any Grishoalbrand or Amulet Bloom today! I cannot kill them just a bunch of 1/1s!"


This is probably why Yukuhiro's running three copies of [Stain the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stain+the+Mind), which can single-handedly cripple decks that rely heavily on a particular card.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stain+the+Mind)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stain+the+Mind)
"I have removed [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand)s yesterday and also [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin). Once I do that, they cannot win as fast and I have more time to attack."


"I also have no removal in the entire deck at first, so I added [Teysa, Orzhov Scion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teysa%2C+Orzhov+Scion). I did not want to randomly lose to cards like [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) and wanted a solution in the maindeck."


Of course, with [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) in his deck, he needed to find a creature that could achieve that and with all the tokens in his deck, Yukuhiro should have no problem finding sacrificial fodder.


Regardless, Yukuhiro seems to be having a lot of fun with his concoction today and would need to rattle off at least five wins today for a shot at the Top 8.


Will he get there with [Tukatongue Thallid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tukatongue+Thallid)? I look forward to it!
