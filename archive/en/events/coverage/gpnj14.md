TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------


Saturday morning, 4003 players came to New Jersey to battle through the largest Legacy field in history with hopes of being crowned Grand Prix New Jersey Champion. 


After nine rounds of swiss on Saturday, 446 players returned to continue their quest for glory. 


Another six rounds of Legacy action unfolded on Sunday, and only eight remained. Tom Ross, Brian Braun-Duin, Royce Walter, Philipp Schönegger, Phillip Braverman, Daniel Jordan, Joseph Santomassino, and Lam Phan stood atop a field of over four thousand players. 


After two elimination rounds, Braun-Duin and Ross, two friends that are sharing a hotel room this weekend, found themselves pitted against one another in the finals. Braun-Duin played beautifully and, in two games, secured his place in history as the Grand Prix New Jersey Champion!

DAY 2 COVERAGE
--------------


Welcome back to Grand Prix New Jersey! Yesterday, 4003 players descended on the Garden State with hopes of securing their place in history as the Grand Prix New Jersey Champion. After nine rounds of swiss, 466 players have returned to battle in the second day of competition. Of those, there are still a number of players with untarnished records: Craig Wescoe, Crispin Allen, Brian Braun-Duin, Samuel Friedman, Nick Hess, Michael Majors, Korey McDuffie, Lam Phan, James Pogue, Joseph Santomassino, Oren Shoemaker, and Denis Ulanov. Blue/Red Delver seems to be the most successful deck after nine rounds, but that could all change in the blink of an eye. Will Blue/Red Delver continue its dominance here in New Jersey, or will a new format boogieman emerge? Stay tuned to continuing coverage of Grand Prix New Jersey!

DAY 1 COVERAGE
--------------


4,003 isn’t just another number.


There are number numbers like this: 4,492 at Grand Prix Las Vegas; 4,303 at Grand Prix Richmond; 2,672 at Grand Prix Charlotte; 2,394 at Grand Prix Boston. 4,003 is the number of players that came to battle Legacy at Grand Prix New Jersey, and it’s now scored into the records as the third largest Grand Prix in Magic history.


The day was filled with the sounds of shuffling as many of the game’s greats joined in the historic events. Owen Turtenwald, Reid Duke, Ari Lax, William Jensen, Jacob Wilson, Tom Martell, Sam Black, Alexander Hayne, Brad Nelson, Mike Sigrist, and Andrew Cuneo – the Nos. 1-, 4-, 6-,10-, 13-, 15-, 17-, 18-, 23-, 24-, and 25-ranked players respectively – all made their presence known throughout Day 1. In a spectacular last round showdown Eric Froehlich, the No. 8-ranked player, managed to stay alive for Day 2 by defeating the paired-down Nathaniel Holt, better known was one half of the wizard duo behind Walking the Planes.


The excitement stretched into every corner of the massive venue.


Of course, there was plenty of action that wasn’t surprising. The impact of *Khans of Tarkir* on Legacy continued to be felt across the metagame. Players picked up Blue-Red Delver in surprising numbers, putting the plan of Treasure Cruise to work. Effectively casting Ancestral Recall was one part of the allure: Monastery Swiftspear joined Young Pyromancer and Delver of Secrets to create an aggressive strategy that could refuel quickly. Jeskai Miracles and blue-based combo decks alike leaned on Dig Through Time to find all the answers they needed. And nearly every other deck in the format appreciated the reintroduction of the fetch lands to Standard, a trickle down effect of making the mana bases of eternal decks easier to assemble.


At the end of it all a whopping 446 players earned the record they needed to return for Day 2. There were five undefeated with records of 8-0-1: Ralph Betesh, Justin Bundens, Bert Phillips, Arya Roohi, and Craig Wescoe. Edging them out with perfect sets of 9-0 points were a stunning eleven players: Cripsin Allen, Brian Braun-Duin, Samuel Friedmas, Nick Hess, Michael Majors, Korey Mcduffie, Lam Phan, James Pogue, Joseph Santomassino, Oren Shoemaker, and Denis Ulanov.


It’s going to be a tight race to the Top 8 here in Edison. Join us at 9 AM local time to see which of these perfect players can parley into the final playoff of Grand Prix New Jersey.
