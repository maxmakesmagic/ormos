Standard Blue-Red Ensoul Artifact
=================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on August 6, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a member of the Wizards of the Coast coverage team. 






2015 Player of the Year Mike Sigrist took a unique strategy to Pro Tour *Magic Origins* last weekend. Blue-Red [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) has been a fringe deck since *Magic 2015* was released, but *Magic Origins* gave the deck what it needed to push it over the top.



I remember testing for last year's Pro Tour, and [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) was one of our top decks. But we decided it was too fragile and inconsistent to take to the Pro Tour. So what changed in the past year? The biggest chance is the addition of [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker), a very powerful card in this deckâ€”and in Standard period, for that matter. This card is now a staple found in many different Standard strategies, and of course it shines in a deck that's based around artifacts.



Another card that's excellent in this deck is [Chief of the Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Foundry). I remember when I first saw this card, and I immediately compared it to [Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium). [Chief of the Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Foundry) is only a 2/3, whereas the Master has the potential to be gigantic. When I played it in Affinity, it was pretty common for the card to be a 10/10 or larger. 2/3 is pretty tiny in comparison, so I thought [Chief of the Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Foundry) was unplayable. Boy was I wrong. The synergy he has with [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker) alone is more than enough to warrant a slot in this deck.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chief+of+the+Foundry)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Foundry) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ensoul+Artifact)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact)

Blue-Red [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) is the fastest deck in Standard right now. With an opening hand of [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum), [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel), and Island, you can begin attacking for 5 on turn two. [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) allows you to win by turn four. Most decks don't have the resources to stop a 5/5 indestructible creature that early in the game. I watched this happened at Pro Tour *Magic Origins*, as Mike was able to win most of his games before his opponent had enough lands on the battlefield to play any answer to his threats.
