Standard Temur Unwritten
========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on October 5, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Welcome to *Battle for Zendikar* Release week! It's the first week of the brand new Standard format and there haven't been any tournament results as of yet, so this week I'll be showcasing new decks from *Magic* content providers from around the web. Today's deck was built by [TCGPlayer.com](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=12846&writer=Ali+Aintrazi) writer Ali Aintrazi. Ali built a green-based ramp deck that is trying to put large creatures into play as early as possible.


Ali plays a variety of small creatures that ramp your mana, such as [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic) and [Shaman of Forgotten Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaman+of+Forgotten+Ways). The Shaman is a card that we haven't seen much of in Standard, but in this new format it seems great when you're trying to cast big Eldrazi. Ali is also using [See the Unwritten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+the+Unwritten) to get up to two creatures onto the battlefield for only six mana. That's a pretty good deal when we're getting things such as [Dragonlord Atarka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Atarka) or [Bane of Bala Ged](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bane+of+Bala+Ged).


The planeswalkers in this deck both do similar things: They either ramp your mana or gain you card advantage. [Kiora, Master of the Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiora%2C+Master+of+the+Depths) is especially good at ramping, as it can untap both a creature *and* a land. If that creature happens to be [Shaman of Forgotten Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaman+of+Forgotten+Ways), you can produce up to ten mana on turn five (four from Shaman, five from your lands on the battlefield, and one from the land you untapped). That's a lot!


There are a lot of really interesting choices for finishers. Ali could have played powerful Eldrazi such as [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) or [Desolation Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolation+Twin), but instead he's playing a little more of a fair game. [Woodland Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Wanderer) is a card that is capable of being a 6/6 creature when you factor in that [Sarkhan Unbroken](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Unbroken) and [Shaman of Forgotten Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaman+of+Forgotten+Ways) can add a mana of any color, but it doesn't interact so well with [See the Unwritten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+the+Unwritten). [Greenwarden of Murasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenwarden+of+Murasa) is another great new card from *Battle for Zendikar* that can get back any creature that may have died in earlier turns. Ali's deck is a great example of a ramp deck with powerful effects that rarely runs out of things to do.
