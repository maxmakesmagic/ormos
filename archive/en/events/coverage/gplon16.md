Carvalho Crushes Kaladesh
=========================




Going 9-0 in draft matches, World Championship finalist and Draft Master Márcio Carvalho crushed all opposition, including Gabor Kocsis in the final to become champion of Grand Prix London 2016, the first premier event to feature *Kaladesh* Limited.

Top 8 Coverage



2,566 players made their way to London to take part in the first Grand Prix to feature Kaladesh Limited, a massive turnout rivaled by few European tournaments in the history of the game. After nine grueling rounds of Sealed Deck, 722 of them returned for the Booster Draft portion.


Here it was Platinum pro and 2016 World Championship finalist Márcio Carvalho who went on a tear and once again showed why he was the 2015–2016 Pro Tour Season's Draft Master. Playing two white-black decks to a perfect draft record, he joined seven others in the Top 8, the twelfth of his career. This time, he drafted an aggressive red-green energy deck but proved unstoppable just the same. Even his finals opponent, two-time World Magic Cup Top 4 competitor Gabor Kocsis, and his almost equally aggressive red-white deck stood no chance.


Congratulations to Márcio Carvalho, champion of Grand Prix London 2016!

Day 2 Coverage



A whopping 2,566 players had entered the tournament on Saturday, with high hopes of fame and fortune, but after nine grueling rounds of *Kaladesh* Sealed Deck a mere 722 remained. Now these players, everyone with a record of 6-3 or better, were back for six more rounds, for the first *Kaladesh* Booster Drafts at the Grand Prix Level, for their shot at a Top 8 berth.


Overnight, eight players sat atop the standings with perfect records of 9-0, including two-time World Magic Cup Top 4 competitor Gabor Kocsis, two-time Grand Prix Top 8er Federico Del Basso, and the 2015 Magic Online Championship competitor Bernardo Santos. They had secured a head start, but the people chasing them weren't far behind, including Pro Tour *Eldritch Moon* champion (3) Lukas Blohon at 8-1.

Day 1 Coverage



Start your engines! The new set, Kaladesh, is here, and while it might look like a colorful world of invention and shiny new artifacts, the reality is the train… the train of pain. Before the tournament started the word was that Renegade Freighter was the common to watch and many players have had fun driving that train over their opponents this weekend.


The attendance at the ExCel here in London was a whopping 2,566 players. After the dust and shattered bits of machinery settled, eight players emerged from the first day with perfect 9-0 records. They were Domantas Zavadzkis, Matt Brown, Gabor Kocsis, Marek Prokopovic, Jonathan Danz, Bernardo Santos, Andrew Brewer, and Federico Del Basso. Tomorrow the format switches to Booster Draft and the 722 players who finished with a 6-3 record or better will battle it out for glory.


This is not the only Grand Prix taking place this weekend. If you’re hungry for more vehicular carnage, don’t forget to check out the live video coverage from Grand Prix Atlanta!
