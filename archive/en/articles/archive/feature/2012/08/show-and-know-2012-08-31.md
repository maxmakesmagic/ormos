Show and Know
=============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 31, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











I don't know if any name has officially been given to today's deck, but I hope that one sticks! Last weekend, while nearly 1,900 players were slinging [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor)s and [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)s over in Boston, a pair of Constructed tournaments were going on in Denver. We have showed you a Standard deck [already this week](/en/articles/archive/mile-high-midrange-2012-08-29), but I figured it was time to show some love to Legacy as well. With the release of ***Magic** 2013*, the format got a new tool to work with that has been generating a lot of buzz.


![Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Omniscience)That card happens to be a ten-mana enchantment named [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience). [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) has a hugely powerful effect on the game, allowing you to cast literally everything for free. The problem, of course, comes in that ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170424152843im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless7.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) cost, as that is a lofty goal for just about anyone. In Legacy, however, that not need be a requirement at all, as the format has a few sneaky things up its sleeve. In this case, Michael Svien decided to feature two different ways to get around that prohibitive mana cost. The first involves the classic [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell).


Ever since [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul) decided to burst out of the ground in *Rise of the Eldrazi*, [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) has been a legitimate strategy in Legacy. It would eventually incorporate [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) into the list as another amazing fatty. With [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), though, you get staying power and a larger burst than with the other two options. [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) might leave you with a bunch of cards in hand, but [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) leaves you with a bunch of spells cast. In addition to [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), Michael also played [Academy Rector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector), which has seen his way around an enchantment or two. With [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) or [Phyrexian Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Tower) out of his [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) package, Michael can sacrifice the four-mana 1/2 and immediately fetch up [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), adding consistency to the combo.


This deck looks to be a big part of the new Legacy format, so keep your eye on the list as it will be sure to evolve quickly!
