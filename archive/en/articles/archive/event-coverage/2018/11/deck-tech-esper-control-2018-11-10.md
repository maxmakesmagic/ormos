Deck Tech: Esper Control
========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 10, 2018 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 










[![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/Arena_Banner_850x90_1.jpg)![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/Arena_Banner_300x90_0.jpg)![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/Arena_Banner_748x90_0.jpg)](https://magic.wizards.com/en/mtgarena?utm_source=MTGArena&utm_medium=Banner&utm_campaign=1358606)

"Some relationships need work. Just because Esper doesn't have the mana doesn't mean we can't make it work."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/ptgrn/PT2GRN-20181110-048.jpg)  
*Shaheen Soorani*


Esper is in an odd spot for *Guilds of Ravnica* Standard. Jeskai gets both [Sacred Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) and [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) to set up a solid mana base, compared to Esper which just gets [Watery Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave) until *Ravnica Allegiance* arrives next year. But Soorani says it's worth it now.


* [![Drowned Catacomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drowned+Catacomb)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowned+Catacomb)
* [![Watery Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Watery+Grave)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave)
* [![Glacial Fortress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glacial+Fortress)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress)
* [![Isolated Chapel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Isolated+Chapel)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isolated+Chapel)

"The twelve buddy lands [from *Ixalan* and *Dominaria*] with [Watery Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave) make it unlikely my lands will come into play untapped," he explained. "Island, Swamp, and buddy lands is fine. The Dimir surveil cards makes the mana even more smooth. [Search for Azcanta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Azcanta) is very good. The mana base is workable—not as good as Jeskai—but the black cards are just too good to pass up."


This echoed the thoughts of Corey Burkhart on [bringing Grixis Control to the Atlanta melee.](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptgrn/grixis-control-2018-11-09)


"When you look at Jeskai you don't see a lot of the new Jeskai cards in there. There's [Deafening Clarion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deafening+Clarion), but not really anything else. My deck is [Thought Erasure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Erasure), [Notion Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Rain), [Ritual of Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ritual+of+Soot)—a ton of the new cards since they're so good."


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thought+Erasure)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Erasure) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Notion+Rain)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Rain) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ritual+of+Soot)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ritual+of+Soot)
"[Eli Kassis won the Grand Prix with Jeskai](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpnj18)," Soorani said grinning, pointing to Kassis seated behind him. "Multiple teams we worked with tested Jeskai and berated us for 'wasting days of their time' testing it. Guillaume Wafo-Tapa said it's much weaker than a Grand Prix win shows."


So what positions Esper Control apart from and better than Jeskai—aside from leaning on black answers and surveil smoothing?


"The white is really just for [Teferi, Hero of Dominaria](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Hero+of+Dominaria)," Soorani said. "Teferi is still the best control option—a three-mana planeswalker, effectively. It's easy to build a deck around it. We had to return to the old tap-out style of play when [Torrential Gearhulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) left. You have a [Thought Erasure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Erasure) early and take the best card out of their hand that will cause Teferi issues. Then you counter or kill and keep the board clear for Teferi on a smooth turn five. Jeskai can't do that since [Deafening Clarion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deafening+Clarion) is much worse than [Golden Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golden+Demise); there's creatures like [Adanto Vanguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adanto+Vanguard) that are indestructible and Clarion bricks. Demise actually kills them all."


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Teferi%2C+Hero+of+Dominaria)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Hero+of+Dominaria) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deafening+Clarion)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deafening+Clarion) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Golden+Demise)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golden+Demise)
"The Dimir cards are underrepresented here at the Pro Tour," explained Soorani. "I know a few teams tested with Dimir decks and decided on something else, but [Thought Erasure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Erasure) is the best thing you can do on turn two. [Notion Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Rain) is better than [Chemister's Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chemister%27s+Insight). Insight is a trap that has the illusion of being the control deck card draw spell, but [Notion Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Rain) is the rebirth of [Read the Bones](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Bones)—the bread and butter of control decks of its time. People who played [Chemister's Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chemister%27s+Insight) and tested it know how mediocre it is, but people should know it's much weaker than its face value."


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chemister%27s+Insight)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chemister%27s+Insight) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Notion+Rain)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Rain)
Of course, every deck has its weaknesses. For Soorani, it's a familiar story. "Mono-Red Aggro with max [Risk Factor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Risk+Factor). [Experimental Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experimental+Frenzy) is rough too, and four [Risk Factor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Risk+Factor)s is hard to beat. They get to cast it for max value and jump-start it. But everyone knows mono-red is always the must-avoid for decks I play anyway."


Soorani, playing Esper at three of the last four Pro Tours, wouldn't have it any other way.
