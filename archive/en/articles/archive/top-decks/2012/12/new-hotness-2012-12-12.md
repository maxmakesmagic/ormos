The New Hotness
===============



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on December 13, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











Last weekend saw two, Two, TWO Constructed Grand Prix events—one in [Japan](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpnag12/welcome) and one in [Canada](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gptor12/welcome). Two formats. Two winning decks that put new spins on already good ideas. Modern might (still) largely be about Jund, but Grand Prix Toronto just taught us that "Jund" can be a lot more than four letters implying a [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). For its part, the current Standard looks to be one of the most dynamic formats in memory; nary a week goes by without the unexpected appearance of a [Door to Nothingness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+to+Nothingness), hard-cast [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), or... apparently [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding). [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding)!

It's always nice to see the hot new thing from a tournament *actually winning it*. In a Top 8 featuring five hasty Rakdos Aggro decks, the big winner was a Reanimator deck built on a completely different paradigm than we have seen previously.

 The deck actually does quite a few different things, but the card that really sticks out like a sore thumb is [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding). Okita could put [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding) on one of his lands on the second turn... and then would see lots and lots of cards in his graveyard afterward. Every time you tap the land that is enchanted by [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding), you put three cards into your graveyard; so if you just tap the enchanted land on turns three, four, and five (say) doing regular stuff—the typical [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) or [Tracker's Instincts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker%27s+Instincts), an [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) or [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) to defend yourself, the [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) to gum up the battlefield—you are going to put nine or so cards into your graveyard "incidentally." And that can leave you with quite the bounty down below once you decide you want to play [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) (an [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) you might never have drawn, natch!).

 Now it's not *just* [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding) that makes this deck so different and exciting. Okita's deck actually hybridizes the [Nightshade Peddler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler)/[Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) two-card Humans combo we have seen before (pair [Nightshade Peddler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) with [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster)... kill everybody), a more general Humans sub-theme, and a powerful Reanimator strategy. It's important to note, as well, what is *not* present. This deck taps all kinds of green, has five-mana threats it is willing to play (e.g., [Geist-Honored Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk)), but there is nary a [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) to be seen! 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 Okita's favored [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) target is [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise). When you have just filled your graveyard with lots of stuff with [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding) (including many Humans, hopefully), [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) is not just one target, but brings along with it any Humans who have either been milled by [Chronic Flooding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronic+Flooding), [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), or [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) or who have valiantly given their lives attacking and blocking, as per normal. 

 In short, one such [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) can give you a veritable army! 

 That is where [Goldnight Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldnight+Commander) comes in. There is only one in the main deck (although some more action in the sideboard), but [Goldnight Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldnight+Commander) ends up being great friends with the aforementioned [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) at the [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) party. [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) is great for suppressing random opposing Humans, Spirit tokens, and so on; gets really wild at murdering expensive stuff when paired with [Nightshade Peddler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler); and always has haste. The problem? [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) starts out with 0 power. 

 But when [Goldnight Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldnight+Commander) comes into play with a whole gaggle of additional Humans thanks to the Angel? Any [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster)s in play (or coming into play) will not only be able to attack, but might just have quite a bit of positive power!

The great Kenji Tsumura put on a show with a more conventional Junk Reanimator deck. Perhaps lacking some of the new bells and whistles of Okita's deck, Kenji's nevertheless proved the continued strength and flexibility of the traditional three-color build.

 The guts of this strategy is that it can just play fair. Ho hum [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) into [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer); [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) for the block-and-blink; [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), achievement unlocked with another [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) (maybe); all four copies of [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) at the top of the curve... Most decks are going to have to respect your life total and battlefield position on that sequence.

But that's not all, of course.

 This is a Reanimator deck, so you might instead go [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch)/[Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) on turn two to start the ball rolling down the [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) route. Or you might use [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) to play a surprisingly explosive grinding game, building value and free cards on top of value and free cards. 

 Kenji biased his deck toward taking a bit more advantage of [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) than many, although it is not really a dedicated [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) deck. He played not only the full eight-pack of little accelerators (quite friendly with [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth), of course), he ran even more mana guys and even a couple of lands in the sideboard!

 Kawaguchi's deck has most of the expected trappings of this successful archetype: eight-pack of 2-power one-drops, [Knight of Infamy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy) on the two, [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) as a big threat on three. The relatively thin Zombies count impairs [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) re-buys somewhat, but the deck still gets a lot of value out of playing four copies of [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls).

 Kawaguchi's top end includes [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) (great with [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) much of the time and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) most of the time) and [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) as its biggest finisher. He did not play the double-red [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider), instead moving two copies of [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) (an important mirror breaker and beater-upper of green decks) to the main.

Speaking of red, that is what Murae's deck was all about.

The Rakdos Red deck has a lot of the same stuff as the Rakdos Black. If you think what is defining or important about this strategy is the top end, then know that this deck also finishes on the same trio of big and hasty threats. The difference is primarily in the mana base, supporting different early creatures.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 Gone are [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul), and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger). They are replaced by [Stromkirk Noble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble) and [Rakdos Cackler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) at one; the haste starts on turn two with [Ash Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot). Attacks are declared. Creatures die. The coolest card of all is [Volcanic Strength](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength), a card I assume Dave Price could get behind. Price, who once won a Pro Tour by playing a first-turn [Mogg Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Conscripts), putting [Giant Strength](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Strength) on it, *and then being unable to attack* would probably appreciate a [Giant Strength](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Strength) ready-built for winning the mirror.

 Nagai went pretty heavy on his five-drops—both [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) here—but had both [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) for setup. It's hard to overrate Nagai's removal suite (despite his only playing eight non-mana spells). [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm) continues to impress. It smashes opposing [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run)s, stops [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) from getting too greedy, and helps break the [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) parity. [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) is... [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned). In a format with so many powerful strategies, [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) is a toehold that helps fair decks like this Naya play with a bit of power.

Every time Top Decks touches on Modern, it seems we are talking about Jund... Jund being the most popular deck, additions to Jund, so on, so forth. In this case we have Pro Tour veteran Willy Edel doing some actually off-the-wall stuff with his Jund deck, mixing it up with highly unique one-ofs and two-ofs... and lots of them.

 Many of these cards speak for themselves, so I will just jump to onetime [Top Decks preview card](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/td/56) [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) (a two-of that gobbled up two of Bob Maher's traditional Jund-slots), and what it does in this deck. 

 For one, [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) can help make white mana. Jund is really black-red-green, and since the last big event we call a deck "Jund" that has started to splash [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). Every little bit counts! You can also get a little extra value out of a [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) with [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra)... "Free" [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) or [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), as it were. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 But I think the most eye-opening interaction here is [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) plus the other big unique card, [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) (popular in Standard, of course, but brand new in Modern). [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) on two, followed by [Marsh Flats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats)... and all of a sudden you are attacking with a 5/5 on turn three, having executed the bejeezus out of your opponent's [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens.

 The general idea of the RUG [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) strategy is to use your land-search accelerators ([Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) on one, [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) to block, two-for-one [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate), whatever you have) to put yourself into a position with several lands in play. Then, ka-pow! You play [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) to put a lethal number of Mountains in play in concert with (ideally) both copies of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle).
