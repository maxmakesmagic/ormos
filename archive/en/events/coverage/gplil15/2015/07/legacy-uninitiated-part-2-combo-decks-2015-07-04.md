Legacy for the Uninitiated, Part 2: Combo Decks
===============================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX LILLE 2015](/en/events/coverage/gplil15)
 on July 4, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Craig-Jones-Author-Photo-150x150_0.jpg)
By Craig Jones











As would be expected of a format spanning twenty-plus years, Legacy has a lot of viable deck archetypes. Legacy is also a format that really rewards a player for really knowing their deck and all of its possible matchups. In a format this diverse, picking the deck you're most familiar with is a valid strategy.


We've already gone through the core decks that are likely to comprise most of the field. Let's take a look at some of the other decks, starting with the really degenerate ones: the combo decks. These aim to goldfish a fast kill, preferably without any interaction from an opponent. We've already seen the [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) deck, but there are others capable of ending the game before their opponents have even begun.

This is basically a storm deck. What it's aiming to do is to chain enough mana production spells in the same turn to create a big enough storm trigger to produce enough copies of [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) to kill the opponent in one go. Ad Nauseum is the mass draw to stock the hand up full of spells to ramp up the spell count.

The Elf deck can play as a combo deck, or it can just summon a lot of elves and run the opponent over the usual way. We don't need your colored mana here. The MUD decks use "post" lands and [Metalworker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker) to power out powerful artifact threats like [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine).


Despite the explosiveness of the fast artifact mana, the deck is not going to be as fast as out-and-out combo decks, so the usual strategy is more in keeping with the "Spoiler" strategies I'll cover in part three. Cards like [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void), [Trinisphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinisphere) and [Lodestone Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Golem) slow down opposing decks and drag them down to where the powerful artifact creatures have time to land their haymaker blows.


Again powerful. Again vulnerable to certain hate cards.


Going from a deck focused on artifacts, we now have a deck focused around lands ...

The Lands deck is a mixture of spoiler and combo. [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port) can tie opposing lands up, while [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s blow them up. [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) means the [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s never run out. Against "fairer" creature strategies, The Tabernacle of Pendrell Vale can make creature swarms costly to maintain while [Maze of Ith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith) keeps big guys occupied.


On the combo side there's the potential for a quick kill with [Thespian's Stage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage) and [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths). As the Stage doesn't enter the battlefield with any ice counters, copying [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) immediately summons Marit Lage, the 20/20 flying, indestructible creature, to bring the game to a quick end.


The deck has a lot of endurance in the form of [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) (with the help of [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows)) recursion. The [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire)/[Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) combo also shows up in other, creature-oriented decks, and that's what I'll move onto in part three.
