Exhausting Possibilities
========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 28, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I've been getting a lot of reader submissions and requests about building a Standard deck that combines [Possibility Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possibility+Storm) and [Curse of Exhaustion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Exhaustion) to lock the opponent out of casting spells. Today, I'm going to look at a reader submission based around that combo. 

![Possibility Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Possibility+Storm)  
 This deck was submitted by Zev Chasan, and I like it because it uses [Possibility Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possibility+Storm) to set up the combo—with enough cheap enchantments, you can cast several the turn after you play [Possibility Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possibility+Storm) to try to find a [Curse of Exhaustion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Exhaustion)—and because it simply embraces the randomness rather than trying to build to minimize it, like some lists do. I mean, really, if you're going to build a deck around [Possibility Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possibility+Storm), you might as well at least have fun with it. 

The deck was presented without a sideboard, and there are a few changes I would suggest for it. Zev sent in the following:

 I would like [Abundant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth) as a cheap enchantment that plays well with [Sphere of Safety](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Safety), and I don't see any reason to play [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern) over [Verdant Haven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Haven). This deck's only win condition is [Blind Obedience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Obedience), and I think I kind of like that approach in the main deck, especially since extort has the cute interaction with [Possibility Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possibility+Storm) that you can do it twice when you cast something. 

 I've also swapped [Runner's Bane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runner%27s+Bane) for more [Agoraphobia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agoraphobia)s because I think it's a better card.
