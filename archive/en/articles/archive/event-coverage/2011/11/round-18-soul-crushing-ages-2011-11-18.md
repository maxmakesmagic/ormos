Round 18: A Soul Crushing Of The Ages
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 17, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/walkinshaw.jpg)
By Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw












[![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/arrowL.png)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/worlds11/fm17)
  

Nico Bohny  
(Death Cloud)  

vs.   
Josh Utter-Leyton   
(CounterCat)

  

 


Nico Bohny and Josh Utter-Leyton had already battled in front of my laptop this weekend, yesterday, in round 10, just after lunch. Bohny took game one, but Utter-Leyton emerged victorious in the end. All of the rounds later, and here they are again, each a single win away from the top 8.




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/r18_bohny_vs_utter-leyton.jpg)

*Can Nico Bohny get revenge for his round 10 loss against Josh Utter-Leyton in order to get into the Top 8?* 


And while it goes without saying, I'ma say it anyway: a single loss away from a Soul Crushing of the Ages.


  

 


### Game 1


Utter-Leyton won the die roll, but mulliganed to five on the play. His turn-one [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) gained very little traction as he missed his third land drop, while Bohny summoned [Sakura-Tribe Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder), into [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker), into [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) for Utter-Leyton's everything (okay, he got to keep 2 cards in hand), followed by [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil).




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/r18_bohny.jpg)

*No, Utter-Leyton did not last long after that.* 


Bohny 1, Utter-Leyton 0


  

 


### Game 2


Keeping the full seven in game two, this time Utter-Leyton came screaming out of the gates with a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) and a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), beating Bohny down to 11 life before he'd even had his third turn. [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) ate the Nacatl, but Utter-Leyton correctly ignored her, attacking and soon finishing Bohny off with a [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) for 5.


Bohny 1, Utter-Leyton 1


  

 


### Game 3


It was Bohny's turn to mulligan to five in the decider, leading with a tapped [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb). Utter-Leyton, of course, promptly dropped to 17 to fetch out a [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground), and he summoned a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl). Bohny soaked up the first attack with [Sakura-Tribe Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder), and Utter-Leyton passed back the turn with no second creature. Bohny, making a mulligan to five look easy, cast [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil).


Like, seriously. A mulligan to five, your Zoo opponent didn't play a second creature, and you're about to land a turn-three [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)? How sweet is that?! Well, Utter-Leyton had two words for Bohny.


 [Spell. Pierce.](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Spell.+Pierce.)




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/r18_utter-leyton.jpg)

*Stone cold.* 

  

 


Utter-Leyton followed up with a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), only to see Bohny summon two of his own. Many turns passed, as each player took turns playing more and more creatures, gumming up the table in a sea of (mostly) green. Eventually, each player had three [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s apiece, and a whole slew of veritable hangers on. Thankfully, hordes of rabid binder-carrying traders were held back by the barriers surrounding the feature match area.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Knight of the Reliquary)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight of the Reliquary)
At this point, I'm sure Utter-Leyton was wishing he was running an [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) somewhere between his deck and sideboard to break a ground stall like this, but spoiler alert: he wasn't. Instead, he searched up some [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx)es with a [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), and started laying out some un-cracked fetchlands.


If Bohny was concerned, he didn't show it, waiting until Utter-Leyton finally added a [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) to the table (no doubt able to search out a stall-breaking [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run)) before pulling the trigger on a [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation). Utter-Leyton swept his army into the graveyard with barely a frown; a lowly [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) left to wonder where everyone went.


Utter-Leyton recovered with another [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), while Bohny could only draw individual removal spells. Two [Slaughter Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact)s could only do so much when Utter-Leyton's creatures came with gate-crashers, and a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) sealed the deal, flashing back a [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) to send Josh Utter-Leyton through to the Top 8.


Bohny 1, Utter-Leyton 2
