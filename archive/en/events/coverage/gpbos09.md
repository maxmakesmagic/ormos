![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/day2-endblurb.jpg)


Congratulations to 2009 Grand Prix–Boston champion Marlon Egolf!


More than 1500 players descended on the Hines Convention Center in Boston this weekend to try their hand at ***Magic** 2010* Limited in the largest Grand Prix ever held on U.S. soil. After 15 rounds of Swiss and two rounds of elimination only two players were left standing; two-time Pro Tour Top 8 competitor Ben Stark and Marlon Egolf, a local player taking part in his first individual Grand Prix and bound for his first taste of the Pro Tour in Austin. Ben’s black-red deck did not perform up to its previous showings in the quarter and semifinals but it seemed doubtful that his army of Sparkmages would have been up to the task of defeating [Ant Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ant+Queen), [Shivan Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon), [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake), and an [Oakenform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oakenform)ed [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll).


Players who came to the event expecting a traditionally basic core-set draft format were surprised. Those who put in the work found a surprising amount of depth and balance. You can follow more M10 action next weekend as the Limited Grand Prix bandwagon rolls into Brighton, UK.

Quarterfinals - Brad Wojceshonek vs Ben Stark
---------------------------------------------


*by Josh Bennett*

“Good luck.” - Brad Wojceshonek


“You too. Hope we have a good match.” - Ben Stark


Stark mulliganed once, and they were off. Wojceshonek had a second-turn [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night), but [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) sent it packing. He replaced it with [Looming Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade). Stark had a [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat), and opted to trade it and the [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) for Wojceshonek’s Shade and third turn. He untapped and picked it back up with [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger).


Wojceshonek dropped a fifth-turn [Bog Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith). Stark hit him with [Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) and caught Doom Blad and [Dread Warlock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Warlock). He replayed his Aristocrat. Wojceshonek had [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) and hit for three. Stark went one better with a [Vampire Nocturnus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nocturnus). He eagerly flipped his top card: Mountain.


“So depressing knowing a land is coming.”


He swung all out, and Wojceshonek spoiled his day with [Harm’s Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%E2%80%99s+Way), helped along by Stark’s forgetting what [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat)’s ability was. [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) put the Nocturnus on ice, and the [Bog Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith) continued to bash.


[Capricious Efreet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Capricious+Efreet) smiled back at Stark from the top of his deck, and he fell to eight waiting for it to show up. His next card was [Goblin Artillery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Artillery). Wojceshonek’s deck stubbornly refused to help him. After another hit Stark was at five, Wojceshonek at nine.


The Efreet targetted Nocturnus, Skeletons and [Bog Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith), and spectator Dave Irvine raised an eyebrow. Luck was with Stark, though, and the die chose the [Bog Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith). With nothing more coming from Wojceshonek’s deck, the game was Stark’s, though he had another turn to misplay his Efreet both in not choosing the [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) as a target, and not sacrificing the monster on his side to his Aristocrat.


Stark 1 - Wojceshonek 0


Game 2 was Wojceshonek’s turn to misplay for the crowd. He started with [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) and attacked past Stark’s [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night). He added an Aristocrat to his team, and promptly forgot its game text when Stark played [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) on it. When Stark sacrificed it after attacking, Wojceshonek could only frown.


He had [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) for Stark’s Artillery and played out a [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon). Stark had [Stone Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Giant) but was under intense pressure from [Looming Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade), the threat of [Harm’s Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%E2%80%99s+Way) and a stack of swamps. He got back his Artillery with Digger and took five from the Shade.


Parke played a [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath), which was promptly Pacified. Wojceshonek swung in with Mastodon and Shade. He had plains and four swamps open.


“I just want to verify with a judge before I throw my whole game away.” - Ben Stark.


He checked that [Harm’s Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%E2%80%99s+Way) worked the way he thought and triple-blocked the Shade. [Divine Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Verdict) took out the Giant and the [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night) traded for the Shade. Now things were back in his favor. He swung in with [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) and Wojceshonek reflexively blocked with his skeletons and regenerated. That left him without a swamp so [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) could finish it off. Parke played out his Artillery.


Wojceshonek was forced to play defensive. Stark’s life total was low enough that he couldn’t go nuts with his Artillery. Veteran Weaponsmith fortified his defenses, and then another. Stark swung in with a pair of [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger)s and finished off the blocking Mastodon with [Seismic Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Strike).


Stark’s card advantage continued to overwhelm Wojceshonek’s deck, and in short order the match was Stark’s.


Ben Stark defeats Brad Wojceshonek 2-0




Quarterfinals - Brian Lynch vs Jason Lundberg
---------------------------------------------


*by Duncan McGregor*

Jason started on the attack with [Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) and [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat), while Brian set up his mana with [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and then [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger), leaving a Mountain untapped. Jason eyed the Mountain warily when Brian blocked, and allowed the 2/2s to trade, refilling on Grey Ogres with a [Merfolk Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Sovereign). Brian’s [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) met with a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), and he opted to use the [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) he’d telegraphed on the fish lord, going to 17. Jason added a Drudge Skeleton before passing the turn back.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/qf-lundberg.jpg)*Jason Lundberg*Brian had land but no other play, showing signs of being mana-flooded. Jason could only attack him for two, though, and Brian finally got some board presence the next turn with a [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul). He used [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) to off the Skeletons and enable an attack, but Jason killed the Zombie with [Assassinate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate), continuing to ping away with the Owl.


Brian continued to draw into action, killing Jason’s [Drowned Catacomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowned+Catacomb)s with [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime), and following that up the next turn with [Centaur Courser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser). The Slime, after some debate, met with an [Assassinate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate), and turned traitor (and Zombie) the next turn thanks to [Rise from the Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+from+the+Grave), killing Brian’s only Mountain. Brian responded with [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), swinging with his Centaur. Jason blocked with the Slime, but a [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) let it trample over for seven, knocking Jason to five.


Jason, justifiably worried by this, aimed a [Consume Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) at Brian’s face, reversing the life totals to 9-5 in Jason’s favor. He also added a [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk) to the table, but that met a [Consume Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) from Brian. Brian also ran out another [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul), leaving him one card in hand. Jason checked on the card with a top-decked [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), but finding only land didn’t help him when he was unable to draw any more creatures. Over the next few turns Brian played a [Runeclaw Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeclaw+Bear) and a [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat), and Jason’s remaining life did not last long.


Brian 1 - 0 Jason


Brian requested an additional basic land between games, a request fulfilled by a nearby judge. Jason led with [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk), while Brian repeated his earlier trick of [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth). A turn three [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) was [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter)ed, though, and Jason swung again before adding a [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) to his team. Brian played his fourth basic land type - a Plains - and a [Runeclaw Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeclaw+Bear) to threaten a trade, but when Jason had an [Ice Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage) for it Brian fired a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) at the [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk) and [Celestial Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Purge)d the bloodsucker. Jason refilled with [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination).


Brian’s second [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) made it into play, while Jason set up his next turns with [Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl). Brian attacked on his next turn and summoned a [Mold Adder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mold+Adder), prompting a quiet “Uh-oh” from Jason. The Snake grew to 3/3 off [Phantom Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior) and the Spider being [Assassinate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate)d, and Brian attacked on his next turn, also playing [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul). Jason only attacked on his next turn, but [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) on the [Phantom Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior) drew a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) on the Adder. Brian continued to swing with his 3/2.


Jason’s [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) retrieved [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk), and he added both that and a Drudge Skeleton to his team on the next turn. Brian, meanwhile, had played [Centaur Courser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser), and then finally found a second Swamp for Lilianna Vess. The discard ability on Jason forced his last card - [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) - out of his hand, and the Centaur back into Brian’s. On Jason’s turn he swung with all of his creatures but the 0/1 Merfolk, losing [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) in exchange for [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul) and three life. An [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant) threatened additional beatings.


Brian replayed the 3/3 and searched with his planeswalker. Jason continued to ignore Lilianna and swing directly at Brian, this time losing [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk) but dealing five damage. Brian’s counterattack was chump-blocked by the selfless Merfolk, and the return strike drew the [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) that he’d tutored up. The life gain from hitting [Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) kept him alive at one life, but when [Centaur Courser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser) was [Assassinate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate)d he now needed to answer both the Servant and the [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons). [Consume Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) couldn’t even keep him alive for a turn.


Brian 1-1 Jason


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/qf-lynch.jpg)*Brian Lynch*Brian chose to draw for the third game, anticipating a game of attrition rather than tempo. Jason tried to prove him wrong with [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons), [Phantom Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior) and [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat). Brian’s first play of the game was a turn four Tendrils, making Jason sacrifice the [Phantom Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior) to deny him the life gain. His army reduced, Jason played land and swung in for three.


[Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) from Brian met with a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), and [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant) entered the battlefield. His stay was brief, though - Brian cast [Consume Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) on it for 0, and also conjured up a [Runeclaw Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeclaw+Bear). It seemed that Jason had been saving some creature kill, though, encasing it in an [Ice Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage) before attacking Brian down to seven. A second [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) from Brian jumped in front of the Aristocrats, but they munched on the [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) to win the fight. Jason showed off his duplicate as well with a new [Phantom Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior).


Brian passed the turn with no play, but when Jason swung in again he removed the [Ice Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage) by tapping a green mana and targetting his Bear with... [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)? A confused Jason directed Brian’s attention to the card he’d tossed out, prompting Brian to shake his head and replace it with the [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) that was legal to play. The Bears were now big enough to win a fight with the Aristocrats, but Brian was down to five and still had to deal with [Phantom Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior). Jason added to his troubles with [Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl). Brian could only attack for two, and Jason dug one card deeper with another [Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl). Finding only land, Brian packed it in.


Jason Lundberg defeats Brian Lynch 2-1.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/qf-marlon.jpg)*Marlon Egolf*Marlon lead with a [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) and Zach gained a life off of his [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden). Marlon discarded a [Craw Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craw+Wurm) to his looter and played two [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant)s over the next two turns. With only a [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) for defense and stuck on three lands Zach was on his back foot quickly to double imaginary 3 / 4 flyers.


Zach worked hard to figure out why Marlon had two each of island, mountain and forest. “I don’t put you on a planewalker, but what else is double red?” Zach asked. A [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll) showed what the double green was for.


An [Armored Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Ascension) was [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)d by Marlon keeping Zach off an effective defense. The troll and the two servants soon had Zach declaring he would play first in the second game.


1-0 Marlon


In Game 2 [Shivan Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon) did not need the [Levitation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Levitation) that Marlon had paired with it. “I knew it” Zach said as the source of the need for double red mana was finally revealed. But a Pacificism cleared the way for Zach’s army and they were on to the deciding game.


1-1


The game looked like it might end very fast. A fifth turn [Shivan Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon) threatened to seal the deal for Marlon. Zach begged his deck to deliver a Pacificism and slowly peeked at the top card of his library.


No play came from him and it looked like the old-school dragon could go all the way.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/qf-zach.jpg)*Zach Efland*The next turn a [Deadly Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse) offered a way for Zach to trade with the dragon. An [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) for six from Marlon could kill off the last hope Zach had. But [Safe Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Passage) ensured his creatures survived and when [Wind Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) attacked it died to the spider without trading. Marlon continued to verbally beat himself up for the misplay of throwing away the flyer for several turns.


Zach had been warned for missing a [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) trigger and was now very precise about each trigger. He even had SOUL WARDEN written on a piece of paper in front of him. But when a [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) came into play and Zach yielded to the land trigger some discussion occurred over whether a spectator had blatantly coughed to remind Zach to catch the other trigger. When the judges could not determine any foul play the match continued.


Marlon now tried to break the standstill by Looting every turn and found a [Howl of the Night Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howl+of+the+Night+Pack). With [Levitation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Levitation) in play the wolves threatened a considerable amount of damage to Zach. A [Sleep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) from Marlon only made matters worse.


“That is the first [Sleep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) that was cast against me all tournament!” Zach said. It would be the last spell cast against him in the Grand Prix as well as he extended his hand in congratulations to the semifinalist.


After the game Zach filled in Marlon on some of the dangerous removal that he had passed downstream. Overall the top eight players were friendly and relaxed with each other through the playoffs.


Marlon Egolf wins 2-1 and advances to the semifinals


**David Feinstein v. Jonathan Pearlman**


Jonathan and David start a full game behind at the next table after a bathroom break and discussion of sleeve quality and shuffling approaches. The players had already met twice this weekend and an amiable discussion of their past games and an intentional draw into the top eight ensued.


David gained life off his [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) twice before sending it into the red zone against a [Goblin Piker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piker). Jonathan thought for a while before declining to trade his 2/1 for the one drop cleric. The next turn the same trade was offered again along with a [Runeclaw Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeclaw+Bear). Jonathan opted to trade the two drops and followed with a [Dragon Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Whelp) on the next turn.


Jonathan seemed to be falling behind on the board and in life totals, but a [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) netted Jonathan four creatures for one spell including two [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage)s. Only a only a Pacified [Dragon Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Whelp) was left on the board. Without any creatures and now faced with [Berserkers of Blood Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserkers+of+Blood+Ridge) David’s 25 life did not seem to be a high barrier.


David implied that Jonathan had played him a little with talk of needed to find a good spell and indeed Jonathan revealed that the sweeper had been in his opening hand.


But David found gas in a Lightwelder Paladin. The Paladin killed off the Berserkers that attacked into it without a trick. (The [Harm’s Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%E2%80%99s+Way) that David had declined to draft over the Paladin would have saved both of his [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage)s from the earlier pyroclasm. Now it looked like either first pick might get him through this game.)


David’s creatures bantered with each other and seemed to be interfering with Jonathan’s thinking. Without a good answer to the ground fatties Jonathan’s life total soon ran out.


David has turned around a game that had looked completely out of reach after his board was cleared and came away with a win.


“Mom I got Game 1,” David yelled into the audience. “I am too tired to drive so my mom came to pick me up.” David explained, “My mom does not care how I do, she just wants to get home.”


David 1-0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/qf-feinstein.jpg)*David Feinstein*With the rest of the matches now finished they prepared for the second game.


David was interested in whom they would face in the semis, but judge Eric Levine kept mum. “He’s a judge he can’t give us information,” Jonathan said. “I can give you rules information,” Eric said. The players joked about asking he judge how flying worked. It turned out that judge Chuck Rodman had actually been asked how flying worked in the main event. No question is too basic for our highly trained judging staff.


David swung with his 2/3 Vetran Armorsmith into Jonathan’s fresh [Dragon Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Whelp). Jonathan’s block showed the attack was no bluff: a [Glorious Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Charge) beefed the Armormisth up enough to kill the Whelp.


The next play from Jonathan was an upgraded 4/4 flying [Air Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental). When the armorsmith attacked again Jonathan “erred on the side of caution” and did not block with his 4/4 flyer leading to groans from both players at the pun.


For a two turns the players traded blows. A Lightwelder Paladin and an [Air Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) taking chunks out of the two life totals.


Then both 4/4s stopped attacking, one changing teams and the other headed back to its owner’s library. An [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) sat the air elemental back on the bench for now.  

The [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) that David had feared took away his 4/4 first striker.


What had seemed like a race between flying and ground 4/4s now slowed down with Jonathan ahead on life 12 to 9. When he refused to replay the air elemental David made clear he suspected [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel). An excommunicate to put his own lightwelder on top of his library was met by the very three mana counter he had suspected.


Both players built their boards for several turns with the pair of Armormsmith and Swordswmith turning into hill giants for David.


A big attack from David came after he played a [Rhox Pikemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Pikemaster) to turn his 3/3s into first strikers. Jonathan’s available blockers were [Air Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental), Lightwelder, and [Viashino Spearhunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Spearhunter). With only a forest and plains open and two cards in hand David threatened a variety of tricks. Jonathan carefully weighed his possible blocks. Only the lightwelder choose to stand on defense and it was dispatched with a [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth).


The next turn everything came over for David hoping to take the last sixth life away from Jonathan before the [Air Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) finished him the enxt turn. All of Honathan’s creatures died on defense but the [Air Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) lived to swing away. When no fog effect came from David they were on to the third game.


Match tied 1-1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/qf-pearlman.jpg)*Jonathan Pearlman*With the rest of the matches long finished a restless crowd had gathered for the final game.


“This is kinda a classic matchup” Jonathan said, enjoying his first Grand Prix top eight.


David again mulligan once in the final game. His second turn Pegasus and third turn [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) faced down an empty board. Jonathan dug through his deck with [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) and carefully considered what to discard- a berserkers of blood ridge finally headed to the bin.


A fourth-turn [Snapping Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake) was [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate)d and David attacked Jonathan down to 12.

When he [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)d an [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) it was clear why he kept the two mana open.


Jonathan had a dangerous [Goblin Artillery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Artillery) in his hand but with no second red source he was forced to only drop hill giant.


A Lightwelder Paladin from David threatened to wreck havoc on the ground. With the [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) making blocking difficult David seemed to be taking over the final game.


When David called on his mom for moral support there was a discussion of putting David’s mom on the stack. The players and spectators discussed how juvenile a “your mom” joke would have to be to make the event coverage, but none seemed to pass the test.


[Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) from Jonathan showed him mountain, [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) and island. David asked aloud that no [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) be found there. But facing down a [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider), the paladin, and the Pegasus and Mage still on the board from the opening turns of the game it was not clear that the Control Magic would arrive in time for Jonathan.


David’s friend the Kird Ape, who had been a constant source of humor through the round seemed to buoy his spirits now.


“My deck is so bad... five hundred more dollars bad?” David wondered aloud. With encouragement and support from both Kird Ape and his mom it looked like David might just win the extra money.


The next turn David debated how to go for the kill and wondered aloud how not to screw it up.


He finally [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate)d one blocker and tapped another with [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage). When Jonathan blocked the paladin a [Glorious Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Charge) took him exactly to zero. The [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) on the top of his library would never see the light of day.


As Jonathan packed up his bag he commented, “That gentleman is the only person I lost to all weekend. I am 13-0 against the rest of the field. I am 0-2-1 against that guy.”


Thinking back on the match the one play that he felt could have changed the outcome was killing the soul warden on turn two of Game 1. “That guy gained him 11 life, I might have won if I had killed it early.”


David Feinstein wins 2-1 and advances to the semifinals.




Semifinals - Ben Stark vs. Jason Lundberg
-----------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

Jason Lundberg was truly just happy to have made Top 8 and over the moon about having gotten past the grizzled PTQ and Pro Tour veteran Brian Lynch -- even if he was not quite sure how he had done it. His opponent this round was two time Pro Tour Top 8 competitor Ben Stark. There was a brief period of time where Ben was one of the most consistent Magic players on the planet with his two Top 8s coming during the same season in close proximity to a handful of Top 16 finishes. He has recently dug in his heels to stay qualified for the Tour again and this event was doing double duty.


“This is awesome,” exclaimed Ben, who was one of five players to sweep through Day One. “This should qualify me for Worlds as well. My rating was like 1900 coming into this.”


### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/sf-lundberg.jpg)*Jason Lundberg*Jason mulliganed and Ben suddenly realized he had to pick up his cards and make the same decision due to the M10 rules change that mandates both players mulligan simultaneously. Fifteen years of habit is hard to break.


“I don’t know if you saw my note on Facebook,” said Ben as he declared he would be keeping his seven cards. “I thought that was a good rule change -- it just saves time.”


Ben was not as enthusiastic about some of the other changes: “I wish there were three different windows during combat for people to stack their stuff. I like a lot of decisions.”


Jason kept his next six cards and his turn two [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk) fell to [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice). “Must be nice,” sighed Jason who played a [Phantom Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior) on the next turn only to find it staring down the [Goblin Artillery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Artillery). Stark played [Stone Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Giant) and used the Artillery to kill the Warrior. Jason played [Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) into [Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) and three lands. Stark killed the Owl. Jason played the other Owl and conceded when it revealed four lands. He showed off the cards he was looking at to his opponent and conceded the game.


“I don’t know how I beat Brian Lynch,” laughed Jason. “[Sage Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) did at least 10 to him.”


### Game 2


This game started out exactly the same with [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk) dropping to one of Ben’s three Sparkmages. Jason played [Disentomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) and replayed the merfolk. Ben shrugged and passed the turn with no play, perfectly happy to put his Sparkmage in the path of the fish. Jason attacked into the Apprentice and grew back the merfolk again, this time with [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger). Ben played [Stone Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Giant) which fell to Jason’s [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). Now it was Ben’s turn to dig graves and he grew the giant back. He added [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath) to the board for his next turn while Jason complimented his [Coral Merfolk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk) with [Merfolk Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Sovereign). “There are exactly two Merfolk in this deck,” he laughed.


Ben played [Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) hitting [Assassinate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate) and [Consume Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) and replayed the [Stone Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Giant) and added [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat). He fell to 10 on Jason’s attack but was able to crack back for nine with his team before playing [Capricious Efreet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Capricious+Efreet). On Ben’s next upkeep Jason began searching for a randomization device. Ben told him not to worry about it as he targeted his own [Stone Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Giant) and Jason’s [Merfolk Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Sovereign) -- you only have to choose up to two targets of your opponent’s and target one of your own. By making his own target invalid it was a 100% shot that the Sovereign would get smoked.


“I am probably not going to need that die. I learned how this worked last round.”


Jason pulled the card toward him and gave it a read, “Let me learn.”


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/sf-stark.jpg)*Ben Stark*The table judge was also uncertain about the interaction and wanted to make sure it worked that way. Ben, who had already sacrificed his [Stone Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Giant), raised an eyebrow.


“If this doesn’t work this way me and the judge from last round are going to fight,” he joked nervously.


“Yeah,” said a hopeful Jason. “You should fight him.”


“I think this is the most complicated interaction in all of M10,” said the judge as went to make sure. When he came back and announced that Ben was correct in his understanding of how the cards and timing worked spectator Zvi Mowshowitz was happy.


“I learned something today,” declared Zvi.


Jason also learned that he would not be advancing to the finals as Ben rumbled over for the win.


Jason’s friends ‘consoled’ him: “Oh well...you won a match with that deck”


Final result: Ben Stark advances to the finals of a major event for the first time in five tries.


“I would love to have a trophy,” said Ben. “I had a pretty bad deal in my two Pro Tour Top 8s. I played Nassif in the Top 8 of my Constructed PT and Herzog in my Limited Top 8. That is pretty raw.”

Finals - Ben Stark vs. Marlon Egolf
-----------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/finals-egolf.jpg)*Marlon Egolf*Ben found himself on the draw for the third time in the Top 8.“I’m glad this isn’t Extended,” said Stark. “I have lost all three of my die rolls. It doesn’t really matter in this format but in Extended I would be like a 60/40 underdog.”We briefly discussed Jim Davis who chose to draw each of his Standard matches in Honolulu and posted the best record among the Constructed players.“He’s a genius,” said Ben, who played in that tournament as well. “In hindsight I wish I had drawn every game. Genius.”Ben is an avid shuffler and the two players spent a lot of time randomizing. I talked the new England native, Egolf about his preparations with local hero Mark LePine.“He has been really fired up,” said Egolf about Mark. “The whole week has been email quizzes like name all the combat tricks by color and rank them.”“Wow,” said Ben. “You were a lot more prepared than I was. I went to the Prerelease.”


With the match finally about to start, the trophy was placed on the table between the competitors prompting an objection from Stark supporter and recently crowned U.S. Champion Charles Gindy.


“Man,” exclaimed Gindy. “That is nicer than my trophy!”


“You just had to show us what we are playing for,” said Stark with a shake of his head. Despite this being his fifth Top 8 at GP and PT level competition, Ben’s mantle is bereft of trophies. “Make us all nervous.”


“I actually got more nervous when he put that down,” admitted Egolf, who has never been to the Pro Tour and was actually playing in his first ever individual Grand Prix.


Ben missed his third land drop for two turns and tried to be philosophical about it as his supporters groaned along the rail. He looked back at them. “I am not going to mulligan two Mountains on the draw.”


Meanwhile, Egolf was amassing the assets needed to play his bombs and fetched a land with [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger). Rather than discard, Ben played [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice). A turn later he thanked his deck as it turned up two Mountains that let him [Seismic Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Strike) a [Craw Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craw+Wurm) but he had no answer for the [Ant Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ant+Queen) that came down the next turn.


“Is this the best of three or best of five?” asked a visibly excited Egolf as he played his [Levitation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Levitation) and flew over.


“Best of three,” sighed Stark who conceded rather than struggle vainly for another turn. “I don’t want to show you more of my deck since I already know all of yours.”


Ben had time to watch Egolf’s last match and saw most of his deck. Egolf tried to think about what he had seen from Stark.


“Hmmm...” he said, ticking off the cards. “I actually just saw three cards.”


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/finals-stark.jpg)*Ben Stark*Stark sided out a Mountain and [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath) for a pair of [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark)s and hoped for the best.


“I have drawn so well all tournament,” said Ben with a rueful grin that suggested he knew his good fortune was coming to an end. He implored his deck to carry on for one more round. “My Sealed Deck was not that good but I drew what I needed. Not now. Not in the finals deck.”


He announced that Egolf could start: “I will draw...just like Honolulu.”


The game kicked off with [Mold Adder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mold+Adder) from Marlon and a turn two Visionary. When Ben laid a turn two Mountain Egolf said, “Do it.”


Ben obliged and took care of the [Mold Adder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mold+Adder) before things could get out of hand.


“Did it,” nodded Egolf as he sadly sent his Visionary into the Sparkmage. “Two for ones are good.


“You drew a card. It is not really two for one,” said Ben who played [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night) while scuffling for mana. He had to hope it was good enough to race against the [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll) that Egolf played with regeneration mana up. [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant) came down next for Egolf but it was taken out by another Sparkmage. Ben sent in his [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night) to do damage and gain two life. Egolf offered up [Oakenform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oakenform) and pointed it at his Troll. “Make him bigger?”


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/finals-egolf-2.jpg)*Marlon Egolf*“That’s pretty sweet,” admitted Ben as he chumped with his Sparkmage. Ben played [Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) and Egolf discarded [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) and -- sheepishly -- a [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) he could have just used to counter it instead. He swung in for two again but was still only on three lands while Egolf played [Shivan Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon) in addition ot his green regenerating monster.


Ben pointed [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) at the Troll and grinned and extended his hand in defeat when Egolf regenerated.


“I tried to sneak that in there,” admitted Ben with a grin. He showed off the [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) he was holding and mused about what could have been with four lands. “If I had drawn a land there I could have stolen it and I don’t think he would have put up a shield there. Then I could [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) it on my side.”


Marlon Egolf could scarcely believe he had won a Grand Prix -- much less the largest North American tournament in history and one of the largest of all time anywhere. He picked up his trophy with a dazed smile on his face and announced, “I need to take a walk.”


Congratulations to Marlon Egolf, the 2009 Grand Prix Boston Champion
