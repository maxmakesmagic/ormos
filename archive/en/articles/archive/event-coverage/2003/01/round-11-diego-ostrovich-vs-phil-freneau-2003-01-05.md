Round 11: Diego Ostrovich vs. Phil Freneau
==========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 5, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a950.jpg)Phil Freneau's been doing pretty well for himself since abandoning thoughts of retiring from the game. His victory and Grand Prix - Los Angeles last year was all the incentive he needed to stay, and he admits with a smile that leaving was "probably never possible". Now he's tearing up the swiss this weekend, with Red Deck Wins 2K2. He's facing a nightmare match in Argentina's Diego Ostrovich. The Magic Invitationalist and Worlds Top 4 competitor is playing his own version of Big Green, a deck that powers out the likes of [Deranged Hermit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Hermit) with Rofellos and [Gaea's Cradle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Cradle). It also features [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) and [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth), trouble for the red mage. 

### Game 1

Freneau led with a fetchland and [Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup), a fine start. Ostrovich had no turn one play, so Freneau was free to add a [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic). Ostrovich tried Rofellos, but the Fanatic was ready to take it down, clearing a path for the Pup. Freneau made a face-down creature and ended his turn.

"I wonder what that is?" - Diego Ostrovich

"It's a [Battering Craghorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battering+Craghorn)." - Phil Freneau

Ostrovich's next play was exactly what he needed: [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd). Freneau's facedown charged in, and was revealed to be the [Blistering Firecat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat) everyone knew it was. He had no other play. Ostrovich flashed back his [Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call) and followed that up with [Deranged Hermit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Hermit), while Freneau had only lands and [Seal of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire). Ostrovich dropped him to seven, then tapped out for [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth) and another Rofellos. Freneau scooped it up.

Ostrovich 1 - Freneau 0

### Game 2

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a949.jpg)Freneau boarded in the land destruction package for Game 2. He played first, but didn't have a kill spell for Ostrovich's Rofellos. [Stone Rain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain) made sure it couldn't enable more than [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth). The next turn he tried [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage), and this time Ostrovich had no additional land. Unfortunately, that meant that he was bashing for six. Freneau [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage)d again. Ostrovich hit again and played a forest.

Too late, Freneau drew his [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer). He [Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer)ed Rofellos and hoped to survive to hide behind his [Fledgling Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Dragon)s. Ostrovich bashed him to two, then played another Rofellos. Freneau drew, and scooped.

**Final Result:** Diego Ostrovich defeats Phil Freneau 2-0
