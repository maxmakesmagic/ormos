========================



 Posted in **Feature**

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







While the appearance of *Morningtide* hasn't completely transformed the Extended landscape (yet), there have nevertheless been many interesting changes in the metagame, capped by the performance of a new look at an old favorite.

I don't know what is more surprising, that we finally have a week without Dredge taking an invitation or that it's like (Standard) Regionals 2004 all over again in some parts of the country. Affinity versus Goblin Bidding in the finals? In Extended? Don't blink or a [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom) might spontaneously appear at the table to your right... Or maybe Death Cl... *Oh, bother*. At least [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) isn't legal this time.


Anyway, the Seattle PTQ finals saw Gavin Verhey's [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy) Affinity deck bested by Travis Woo wielding the breakout deck of the week, B/R Goblins. Before we get to Woo let's take a look at the deck he had to beat to win the invitation.

![Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Arcbound+Ravager)

In case you haven't seen this style of Affinity before, it is pretty similar to the basic, heavily linear Affinity builds from Mirrodin Block Constructed onwards. As always, Affinity is a fast aggressive deck with undercosted threats thanks to numerous artifacts. [Arcbound Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) is the ace, and [Cranial Plating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) turns even the lowly [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) into a front side like [Blistering Firecat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat). The differentiating elements of *this* build are four [Atog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog)s main deck as well as the surprising, and surprisingly lethal, [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy). Everyone knows that just one or two errant hits from a [Cranial Plating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) on *anything* can prove deadly when facing Affinity... [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy) just gives the deck more breakout power with the flyers and a very dangerous "B" game with the [Atog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog). Letting the [Atog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) hit you -- especially when you are a creature deck -- is basically suicide if the Affinity player has [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy) in hand. Two lands, a [Springleaf Drum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum), and an [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) can easily Voltron for lethal given today's mana bases.

The *actual* big winner, though, and the recipient for most Blue [Envelop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Envelop)es on the week, was Woo's B/R Goblins deck. *Lorwyn* and *Morningtide* seem to have settled the Goblins splash color on black for now with [Auntie's Hovel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auntie%27s+Hovel) and the prowl mechanic adding value to the team. [Auntie's Hovel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auntie%27s+Hovel) is just another dual land, but it's a fine one that can help shoulder the somewhat prohibitive BB of [Patriarch's Bidding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Bidding). [Patriarch's Bidding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Bidding) gives Goblins both a "critical mass" combo kill with [Skirk Prospector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector), [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander), and [Goblin Sharpshooter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter) + [Goblin Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief) (all laced together with [Goblin Matron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Matron) and [Goblin Ringleader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ringleader), of course), but maybe more simply, it just gives the deck a nice (and by "nice" I mean "devastating") topdeck out of [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God).

While it's not unusual by any means, don't miss Woo's [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic)s. Along with [Skirk Prospector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector), [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) is a worthwhile puzzle piece in this deck... That also helps to fight Dredge by passively attacking [Bridge from Below](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below); [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) can also knock a [Narcomoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba) out of the sky, keeping Dredge under the critical three creatures necessary to play [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return).


Reasonably fresh off his Top 8 at the end of the Time Spiral Block GP season, onetime Junior Super Series standout Brett Blackman took the Philadelphia area PTQ with what has been the standout deck of the Gray Matter / Northeast area this season, Doran, making for at least the third PTQ win by the deck in that region... and he won a mirror in the finals.

Is it possible? A PTQ winning green creature deck *that doesn't play [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)?* Scott Young's Beasts Rock deck had plenty of fat creatures, but he needed to choose [Ravenous Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth)s and [Spiritmonger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiritmonger)s to fit into his Beasts strategy with [Contested Cliffs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+Cliffs).


It might be borderline heretical to say this, but in my Rock-on-Rock testing, [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) has underperformed when at least one of the participants had [Pernicious Deed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed) in his deck. At some point, all your 6/7 [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s will die and my one, lonely, 4/4 Raveous Baloth will live to clean up the game. There are times -- not many I'll grant you -- where [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)'s bargain mana cost can be a bit of a drawback.

![Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Through+the+Breach)

One of the genuinely new decks to come out of Morningtide is the [Through the Breach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) / [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) combo deck. If you haven't seen this deck in action, it is pretty close to Legacy Hulk Flash in execution, if not speed and versatility at this stage.

Basically the goal is to get one of your [Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk)s into the graveyard; of course you are loaded with [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish)es and [Summoner's Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoner%27s+Pact)s to artificially increase the number [Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk)s past four (in fact, in order to get "four more" you only play three main). The simplest way to get your [Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) where you want it is to play [Through the Breach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) on the second or third turn via [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) and other accelerators. When the trigger on [Through the Breach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) occurs, your [Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) will go to the graveyard, and then the Hulk's trigger follows suit and the fun begins.


[Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) goes to the graveyard and finds six mana worth of creatures; the scripted play is [Body Double](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double) and [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder). [Body Double](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double) comes into play copying [Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk); therefore when you sacrifice [Body Double](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double) to [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder), it, too, gets a trigger for six mana... Conveniently [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) and [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) add up to exactly six.


At this point your opponent is basically dead, regardless of his life total (unless he can somehow break up the combo, of course). Throw [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) at the enemy; he takes one. Sacrifice [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) to [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder); you get to return a pair of small creatures to play thanks to that great white hope... [Body Double](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double) and [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) are excellent candidates. You can do this forever! Note that this combination is very difficult to break up with creature removal. Sittner played two or more copies of every relevant combo piece. That way, even if his opponent had creature removal, he could double up a [Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) and find the relevant parts again in the middle of a combo, as if he were starting it fresh.


Extended is a format full of unfair and sometimes un-fun combo decks. How does the [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) deck stack up? In terms of speed, the deck can theoretically win on the first turn ([Gemstone Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemstone+Mine), [Simian Spirit Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide), [Simian Spirit Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide), [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song), [Through the Breach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach), [Protean Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk))... and that hand is actually one of the more likely first turn kills in Extended. Don't count on it, of course. The major criticism against the Hulk strategy is that, despite its potential for a first turn kill, it is functionally slower than Dredge on average, yet has "all of Dredge's weaknesses" specifically its reliance on the graveyard and vulnerability to cards like [Leyline of the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void). On balance, this deck *also* has most if not all of the shortcomings of [Enduring Ideal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Ideal), boasting a mana base only a mother (or possibly Brian Kibler) could love; commonly played permanents like [Pithing Needle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) will also slow it down considerably.


That said, this deck is exciting, powerful, and brand spanking new!

![Spark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spark+Elemental)

Mono-Red Burn has been a staple of the "metagame" (if you can call it that) from the very beginning of Magic tournaments. This version is the latest incarnation, combining numerous cards that deal *three* points of damage or more. Think about it like this: The Philosophy of Fire says that cards are worth about two life, so you need ten cards to win; in this version of Red, where nearly everything does three, the deck needs only *seven* spells to cash in the "W*."*
One oldie but goodie that has made the transition into the Extended burn deck is [Spark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spark+Elemental). This card consistently gets in for his money, early... Card advantage? This deck doesn't concern itself overmuch. Because it is going to the graveyard anyway, no one wants to trade with [Spark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spark+Elemental)! From the other side, a new card that is costed to impress is [Shard Volley](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shard+Volley). The worst [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) ever? Okay, fine, it's "bad." Take three.


Like the week's breakout archetype, Goblins, the burn deck plays a full set of [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic)s. In addition to being one of the best beatdown creatures ever for one mana, [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) helps the deck race against Dredge; as you know, Dredge without [Leyline of the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) in play has to protect [Bridge from Below](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below) when playing against a deck like this one, or any deck with [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic).


I don't know if it is the right deck to play this weekend, but my pick for the coolest deck is Matt Laurents's 2d place deck from LA, CA.
