Lighting the Lantern
====================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 13, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











The whispers started slowly.


Like most of the new decks that pop up in tournaments from time to time, it can be hard to separate hype from reality. So when the rumors of an odd mill deck began circulating the room at Grand Prix Charlotte in June, they were easily dismissed.


But they persisted, and a quick cursory glance around the event confirmed it: [Codex Shredder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder) had been spotted at the top tables. A fun novelty, but hardly something to concern yourself over at a premier-level Modern tournament. A deck that stole wins but couldn’t beat the top decks consistently. An oddity that would crumble when faced with any level of scrutiny. Even when the deck finished in the Top 16 of the tournament, it was deemed by some a one-off deck, soon to be shipped into oblivion and never seen again.


Zac Elsik has heard it all. At Grand Prix Oklahoma City, he silenced all the critics.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpokc15/gpokc15_elsik1.jpg)  
*Zac Elsik*


“My goal for Oklahoma City is to Top 8 with this deck,” he said shortly after moving to 11-1 for the tournament. “I knew in Charlotte I could have won the thing if I played a little better; my two losses were to myself. I practiced a ton for this event, and it has definitely played off.”


So far, it has. Elsik ran the table on Saturday, finishing the day 9-0 with the deck he debuted to the world with his run in Charlotte. Titled after its namesake [Lantern of Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), Lantern Control is a unique beast, even in the diverse field that is Modern. The deck uses discard spells to tear apart opponents’ hands early, [Ensnaring Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) and [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) to control the battlefield, and [Lantern of Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), [Codex Shredder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder) and [Ghoulcaller's Bell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulcaller%27s+Bell) to deny a profitable draw step. All told, it provides an ever-growing lock on the game and finishes it off by removing every card in an opponent’s library.


“The deck is very consistent and resilient,” he said. “I came across it and started testing, and it just kept winning. I was a bit nervous and hesitant to play it in Charlotte but I decided I had to trust my testing, and I’m glad I did. It can beat any deck. Yesterday one of my opponents ultimated Ugin twice and then conceded because he still couldn’t win.”


After his success in Charlotte, Elsik’s *Magic* world changed. Suddenly pros like Luis Scott-Vargas and Sam Black were picking up the deck to try out, and people Elsik had never met began to approach him for advice.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpokc15/gpokc15_elsik2.jpg)  
*Zac Elsik introduced Lantern Control to the world, and today he’s trying to ride it all the way to the Top 8.*


Elsik takes it all in stride, happy to share his deck with others and contribute to his favorite format.


Of course, he’d be even happier if he rode [Lantern of Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) all the way to the Pro Tour.


“I think it’s pretty cool that other people are playing and enjoying the deck, and it’s awesome to see some of the pros pick it up,” he said. “I like Modern because I love brewing, and I think there’s a lot of room left unexplored, and this deck is a big part of that.


“The only thing left is to Top 8 this event.”
