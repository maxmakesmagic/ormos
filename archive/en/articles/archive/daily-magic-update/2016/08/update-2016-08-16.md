Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Cody Lingelbach's Green-Blue Crush Control](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppor16/9-32-decklists-2016-08-14)

Finishing juuuuuuust outside the Top 8 in 9th place at [Grand Prix Portland](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppor16/) was one of the more fun decks to be found in the tournament—Green-Blue Crush Control.


The deck is built around the surge super-spell [Crush of Tentacles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Tentacles) that resets the board and, when surged, leaves behind an eight-legged friend (who also happens to be an 8/8).


The deck is, at its core, a ramp deck that needs to dump a ton of lands on the battlefield to function, but it's got a few cool tricks up its sleeve.


First up is the use of [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) and [Oath of Nissa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Nissa), two permanents that help smooth out draws and stall out opponents—and also happen to get bounced for a positive effect with [Crush of Tentacles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Tentacles). [Nissa, Vastwood Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Vastwood+Seer) is a pretty good creature/planeswalker to bounce as well.


And the real kicker? You can bounce the board every turn once you have ten lands in play, thanks to [Den Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) flipping up, returning Crush, and then bouncing back to your hand again. You didn't need permanents anyway, right?


The deck uses cheap cantrips—like [Oath of Nissa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Nissa) and [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary), plus [Anticipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate)—to trigger [Crush of Tentacles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Tentacles), but for those instances where it needs to cast it on the same turn it hits five mana, there's [Sight Beyond Sight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sight+Beyond+Sight). The rebound spell gets played on four mana, and then gets played *for free* again the following turn, allowing you to turn on surge for [Crush of Tentacles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Tentacles).


[Part the Waterveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Part+the+Waterveil), [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger), [Den Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector), and [Lumbering Falls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumbering+Falls) represent the deck's non-Octopi win conditions—as if you'll need them.
