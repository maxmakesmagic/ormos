Spotlight Cube Series – Proliferate Cube Returns
================================================



 Posted in **Magic Online**
 on May 4, 2021 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/hero/2018_Daily_WOTC_icon_0.jpg)
By Emma Handy











The updated *Proliferate Cube* is available to play on *Magic Online* May 5–12.




---

Howdy, gamers!


My name's Emma Handy, and I'm a member of the Play Design team here at Wizards of the Coast. [Last year](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/magic-online/proliferate-cube-2020-10-13), I had the privilege of bringing my *Proliferate Cube* to *Magic Online*, and it went well enough that I have the privilege of updating it for another week of counters, combat, and combos!


The Basics
==========


For anyone new to Cube, synergy is the name of the game. Drafting cards that are worth more than the sum of their parts is going to lead to more success than simply taking the best "rate" card out of each pack.


The archetypes are largely the same as the last version, with a bit of an overhaul to blue, but more on that later. Here are basic breakdowns for each archetype:


Green Stompy
------------


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pelt+Collector)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pelt+Collector)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kalonian+Hydra)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Hydra)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Strangleroot+Geist)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist)
Previously defined by how it ramped, the most successful green decks in this cube are going to be interested in hitting fast and hitting hard. With a bunch of efficient two-drops that use +1/+1 counters to size into the mid- and late game, being proactive is key here.


Ramp is still certainly possible with cards like [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker), [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage), and [Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid)—there are just fewer ramp payoffs in this cube.


Red Aggro
---------


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rift+Bolt)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Soul-Scar+Mage)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul-Scar+Mage)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Runaway+Steam-Kin)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runaway+Steam-Kin)
Second verse, same as the first. It's astonishing how many of mono-red aggro's all-time greats involve counters. For anyone who's drafted much mono-red aggro in Cube, you'll find a bunch of the mainstays you've come to know and love.


Black-Based Sacrifice
---------------------


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Carrion+Feeder)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Skyclave+Shade)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyclave+Shade)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Geralf%27s+Messenger)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger)
Black-based sacrifice is the center of a chunk of synergy in the cube, with a bunch of synergies playing from the graveyard or paying players for their creatures dying. Red has a good number of cards that play well to this, and green has some serious combo potential with cards like [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast).


White Aggro
-----------


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Student+of+Warfare)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Student+of+Warfare)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Basri%27s+Lieutenant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basri%27s+Lieutenant)
White goes the widest of all the colors in the cube, with some token themes and a ton of small creatures to get out onto the battlefield quickly. From there, cards like [Venerated Loxodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venerated+Loxodon) and [Unbreakable Formation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unbreakable+Formation) build a smaller battalion into an unbeatable army.


Blue Blink
----------


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Man-o%27-War)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Man-o%27-War)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Watcher+for+Tomorrow)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watcher+for+Tomorrow)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ghostly+Flicker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker)
Here's the biggest deviation from the first version of *Proliferate Cube*: blue flicker. This one is straightforward and is certainly going to resonate with any Pauper fans who find themselves in a *Proliferate Cube* draft pod thanks to creatures that get a ton of their value as soon as they hit the battlefield and cards that let you recycle those enters-the-battlefield effects for max durdle. [Soulherder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulherder) is the premium uncommon for this deck, so if you see one early, **move in**.


There's still some of the same combo-y goodness from *Proliferate Cube*'s first appearance (you can find more [details in the first article](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/magic-online/proliferate-cube-2020-10-13)), just in case battlefields get too clogged or removal doesn't seem important enough.




---

As with the first time, the more you play, the more you'll learn. Try out some of the sideways decks that feel a bit more "hidden" ([Astral Cornucopia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Cornucopia) Ramp, anyone?) and unlock some games that wouldn't happen in any other *Magic* format. I can't wait to tear open some packs and metaphorically sink my teeth into some more counters-fueled shenanigans, so I'll see you in the queues.
