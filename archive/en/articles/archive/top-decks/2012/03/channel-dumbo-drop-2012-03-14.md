![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/td/td186_souls.jpg)[Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) | Art by [Bud Cook](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Bud+Cook%22%5D)

For those of you who only remember Caw-Blade as a machine-gun tournament winner, the scourge of the Star City Games Open Series about a year ago... Martell was actually part of the team that initially put Caw-Blade on the map. He played it to a Top 8 finish in Paris last year, losing only to eventual winner Ben Stark (also with Caw-Blade).

In this case, Martell associated the [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) deck very much with Caw-Blade and—with some brewing help from Sam Black—was all-in on the deck very quickly. It turned out to be a pretty good choice.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

So how does this deck work?

Well... it's basically Caw-Blade. [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) goes and gets a spicy piece of equipment, and then somebody carries it through the Red Zone to accumulate value and eventually win. Like Caw-Blade, Tom's Legacy brew can play an aggro-control game (using its counters and discard to protect a threat in play) or as a true control deck, eliminating threats and winning on card advantage, which is exciting...

... although Tom says what was most exciting was a High Tide opponent bricking after a successful [Time Spiral](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Spiral).

The deck is fairly straightforward. Most of it is good cards, but some things might stick out for you.

**Intuition**

Martell could channel the spirit of Tomi Walamies and his first-run [Call of the Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) by playing [Intuition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) for [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). How many 1/1 Spirits is that exactly?

**3 Snapcaster Mage and 3 Force of Will**

These three-ofs stuck out like sore thumbs to me. Aren't they some of the best cards in the format?

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td186_force.jpg)[Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) | Art by [Terese Nielsen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Terese+Nielsen%22%5D)

"Most StoneBlade decks need the body on [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to carry a Sword," Tom told Top Decks. "But I have all these [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) to go to battle for me. [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) is powerful, but is actually kind of slow."

[Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) is primarily in the deck for combo decks. Yes, you do need the fast answer against a deck that is going to beat you on the first or second turn, but Legacy is full of all kinds of decks that don't do that.

"I only kept [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) in against [Hive Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind), [High Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Tide), and Dan Jordan, because when he has [Choke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choke) in his deck, he is basically playing an unfair combo deck."

"My deck—with all its [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls)—can beat any deck playing fair. But [Choke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choke) is very un-fair. If the opponent goes first and has a [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), you can't even play [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) unless you have a [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). But if you [Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) the [Choke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choke), they are stuck playing a fair game and you can beat decks that are playing fair."



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Tom is quick to admit that [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) isn't very good against broken combo decks, but when it's good... it's really good. In the finals, his opponent was forced to [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) the front half of a [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) because his board was nothing but 1/1 creatures!

"He literally couldn't beat a [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls)! Typically, the card [Nimble Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) is a problem for straight control decks in Legacy, but even if they get to 3/3, my deck can just chump them all day while doing something else."

I asked Martell what the lesson of his 11th-hour Cinderella story was.

*Watch Facebook?*

"The real lesson is to keep an open mind, because even smart people can be dumb. All weekend I was being told how bad my deck was (because [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) isn't 'good enough' for Legacy), but the reality is, it was the best deck for the tournament."

If you want to hear Martell say more smart stuff like that reframing on [Choke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choke)... well...

High Tide is effectively [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)-proof... so decks based on [Crucible of Worlds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crucible+of+Worlds) recursion have less pointy teeth, in-matchup.

But like many combo decks, it benefits from opponents in Legacy being forced to respect creature decks... they have wasted draws that help give this fast deck even more time.

Here we have an archetype sharing many intersections with the High Tide description, above. RUG Delver is a deck that also appreciates [Mental Misstep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep)'s bowing out.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td186_del.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/sun_arttransformbutton_active.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/moon_arttransformbutton_static.png)[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Nils+Hamm%22%5D)

Twenty-eight of Caleb's main deck cards cost literally one mana! Including, of course, the archetype's signature starters, [Nimble Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) and [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets).

By now you have probably heard of this [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) card; relatively young, the [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration)-to-be has successfully found success in... essentially very available format. Hall of Famers swung for 3 in the air on turn two to score Top 8s at Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*; it has scored Modern Blue Envelopes; and, of course, it pairs nicely with Mongoose here, as an undercosted aggro-control threat.

Nimble Mongoose might not fly... but it also doesn't die. You can't target the 'goose, and when the 'goose is loose at 3/3... the game shifts. RUG Delver has fast counterspells like [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) and [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), and can use them to stop or punish combo players, but it is much happier protecting a board position while cracking for 3 to 6 a turn. Unlike many blue decks, this one has the efficiency on offense of green, cheap counters to protect the queen, and can close the game out with a flurry of red death via good old [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).

Caleb's specialized card quality was so high he felt no need for the fourth—or even third—[Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) (and, for that matter, would be willing to cut another)!

Not only can it play a beatdown or aggro-control game with burn backup, but with [Counterbalance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) + [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) it can move to a combo-control line as well.

Nobody likes it. Everybody hates it. Everybody had best also respect it: Dredge.

Dredge (as a concept) has been a consistent and consistently feared weapon of choice in essentially every format where it has been legal. The Legacy version with new *Dark Ascension* sorcery [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) puts the deck on a totally new level in terms of inevitable openers.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td186_dredge.jpg)[Dredge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge) | Art by [Donato Giancola](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Donato+Giancola%22%5D)

Or... do you [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) or [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) when your opponent attempts to play a first-turn [Breakthrough](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) for zero?

I guess "it depends," but Dredge is the kind of deck that laughs off traditional notions of cardboard card advantage. It can go down a card in [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) (or all its cards in [Breakthrough](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough)) and put an opponent in an essentially no-win situation. It can fill the graveyard via [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll) and [Stinkweed Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp), allowing it to flip [Ichorid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorid), [Narcomoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba), and other troublemakers. While it might seem down in cards, when you can use a free body to flash [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) (also free) and simultaneously come up with one or more additional bodies via [Bridge from Below](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below)... the realization of what Dredge can do with "no" cards might reframe some players' approach to answering that "[Breakthrough](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) for 0" question.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

In the past, Dredge—especially on its most muscular draws—would flip a ton of Bridges and [Narcomoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba)s, flash [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) using free bodies to get back [Flame-Kin Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame-Kin+Zealot), and then attack for a ton. Ferguson's deck subs in [Flayer of the Hatebound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+of+the+Hatebound) to shortcut the Red Zone, making every free Zombie a free [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) and Ichorid a free [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). And yes, via undying, [Flayer of the Hatebound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+of+the+Hatebound) can also play a pretty good body for [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) shenanigans.

I think it was Stuart Wright who said Dredge is the kind of deck—because of the [Ichorid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorid) and Cabal Therapy inclusion—where even if you can answer the "main" plan of Bridges and flashy turns, the Dredge deck can put you in a worse position than it is in, using entirely free resources.

And it's not like those [Ichorid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorid)s aren't just going to kill you anyway. Most conventional creature removal is unprofitable at best, and irrelevant even more often.

Dan Jordan's build is tutor-tacular! It is so search-y this green-white deck is almost blue.

Dan's deck has both [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) and [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) as search engines. [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) can be played on the first turn for [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) (where it functions much as a [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves)) or it can get specialized bullets like [Gaddock Teeg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg) to shut down expensive combo cards like [Time Spiral](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Spiral).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td186_green.jpg)[Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) | Art by [David Rapoza](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22David+Rapoza%22%5D)

Having four [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)s and four [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)s really helps Dan set up the fast [Choke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choke) that Martell feared, and [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) plays... [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) (there really is no other). Jordan can go and get any manner of Swords (depending on what kind of creatures the opponent plays), or [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), Legendary ruiner of creature fights.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td186_gcn.jpg)  
Even more, we have [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), which can itself get all kinds of powerful one-ofs: [Bojuka Bog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Bog) for Dredge; [Karakas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) for [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn); [Maze of Ith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith) as another specialized defender. Of course, [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) has the less bullet-like special abilities of "making a mana like a [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves)" (tap a land before sacrificing it) and "just being huge."

While superficially the same macro-archetype as Dan Jordan's deck, Pascal Maynard's build is quite a bit different. Although he played [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), he has no [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) searching. Instead, Pascal played [Crucible of Worlds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crucible+of+Worlds), allowing for a [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) lock down and a fairly unique angle with Olle Rade, [Sylvan Safekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Safekeeper). The mana denial sub-theme is even more compelling with [Aven Mindcensor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor), which can put the opponent on an evasive clock *and* make life difficult for *Onslaught* and *Zendikar* sac-duals.

Both Jordan and Maynard can get huge with [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) and [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze)... but did you notice [who isn't playing in some of these lists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)?

*That's* how varied and open Legacy can be!
