House of Commons: *Zendikar* Edition!
=====================================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 1, 2009 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hey all, and welcome to the new world of **Magic**! Hopefully you'll have played this game before, but if not, don't worry: *Zendikar*, the set that has been slowly previewed over a three-week span, is also a newbie to the tables of casual players. And as longtime From the Lab readers will know, there's a certain tradition that I like to follow with every new large set's release.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl58_zenLogo.jpg)  
I'm talking about the budget-conscious conundrum called House of Commons, first [kicked off](/en/articles/archive/house-commons-2007-02-07) two years ago by my friendly predecessor Chris Millar. In this series, every common of a new large set (in this case, *Zendikar*) is used in a multitude of decks. Easy, right? Here's the tricky bits: No other rarity of the set can be used (example: no [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane) + [Nissa's Chosen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen) shenanigans), and each common must be used exactly four times. This means I can't put 4 copies of [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) in each deck, and actually must use [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) 4 times. For more articulate explanations, go ahead and visit the links to the [*Lorwyn*](/en/articles/archive/house-commons-blowout-spectacular-2008-01-10), [*Shadowmoor*](/en/articles/archive/house-commons-blowout-spectacular-2-2008-07-10), and [*Shards of Alara*](/en/articles/archive/house-commons-iii-shards-edition-2008-10-29) editions of this series. Right now, I've got ten decks of liteness to lay on the table, so let's kick it *Zendikar* style!

### Monophonic

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl58_cross1.jpg)  
This deck is one of four mono-color decks that I wound up making, which was a major departure from the multicolor heavy *Shards* edition. This particular one is mono-blue, with some controlling elements such as counterspells ([Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce), [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel)), bounce ([Whiplash Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whiplash+Trap)), and creature lockdown ([Paralyzing Grasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paralyzing+Grasp).) The win conditions are beefable [Windrider Eel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windrider+Eel)s and [Shoal Serpent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shoal+Serpent)s. To draw into lands for these creatures, I turned to [Reckless Scholar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Scholar) and [Ior Ruin Expedition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ior+Ruin+Expedition).

[Khalni Heart Expedition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition) is your accelerator, and [Nissa's Chosen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen) can hold down the fort until your late-game creatures do the job.

Moving on to the inky black tribe of Vampires. ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl58_cross5.jpg)  
From the monocolored decks that I made to the dual-colored ones! Oddly, only one of the four of these decks turned out to be allied colors. I guess my puzzling subconscious is drawn to the enemy colors, which explains why the new enemy fetch lands excite me so!



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Zektar Shrine Expedition | Zendikar Farguide |

Let's go green and red for a bit here. This deck is all about landfall: A whopping twenty cards in the deck (over thirty percent!) bear the latest ability word, and in this case, they wield it perfectly. [Plated Geopede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) is frightening as a 3/3 first striker bashing in on turn 3, and [Zektar Shrine Expedition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zektar+Shrine+Expedition) can get painful quickly. [Grazing Gladehart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grazing+Gladehart) can keep you afloat, and [Territorial Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Territorial+Baloth) knows how to pound. Normal creatures, like [Zendikar Farguide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zendikar+Farguide) and [Shatterskull Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatterskull+Giant), can become terrifying as well thanks to the cheap and deadly [Vines of Vastwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood). This deck might have used some mana acceleration, but the mana curve isn't too astronomical: there are 12 two drop threats! As long as you keep hitting your land drops, you should push into a victory.

### The Weirder and Wackier

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl58_cross9.jpg)  
Now on to the wacky stuff. Of course, not too wacky, as we are locked within a restrictive deck formula today. But as always, some of these decks turned out a bit weird. These last two have some funky color combinations and synergy.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Grim Discovery | Magma Rift |

This last one is mostly a green and red deck, with a splash of black for good measure. In fact, the splash of black was really what inspired the deck, since I wanted both methods of [Grim Discovery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Discovery) to have a use. But how best to get lands into the graveyard? (Creatures were a given.) I went with a combination of powerful spells ([Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) and [Magma Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Rift)) and powerful bodies ([Scythe Tiger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scythe+Tiger) and [Ruinous Minotaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruinous+Minotaur).) The efficiency of these cards should reduce your opponent's life total a bit. [Molten Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Ravager) was an addition in the name of potentially more pain, and [Goblin Shortcutter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Shortcutter) conveniently provides an alternate route for your attackers to hit your opponent directly, bypassing all those blockers.

[Tanglesap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tanglesap) is a curious staller, and [Goblin War Paint](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+War+Paint) is a slightly pumping enchantment. [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) can find all three of your land types, as well.
