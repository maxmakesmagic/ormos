2016 World Magic Cup Qualifiers
===============================




The 2016 World *Magic* Cup Qualifiers (WMCQs) take place June 18-19, July 9-10, and September 17-18 in up to 73 countries. Win a WMCQ, and earn a spot on your country’s team in the 2016 World *Magic* Cup!


















OVERVIEW
--------


Your path to the global *Magic* stage—and your chance to play alongside your fellow countrymen for fame, fortune, and national pride—starts here with the 2016 World Magic Cup Qualifiers!


2016 World Magic Cup Qualifiers take place on the following weekends:


June 18-19, 2016 (the format will be [Standard Constructed](http://magic.wizards.com/en/gameinfo/gameplay/formats/standard))  

July 9-10, 2016 (the format will be [Modern Constructed](http://magic.wizards.com/en/gameinfo/gameplay/formats/modern))  

September 17-18, 2016 (the format will be [Modern Constructed](http://magic.wizards.com/en/gameinfo/gameplay/formats/modern))


Each of the up to 73 countries that encompass the 2016 World *Magic* Cup will run three World *Magic* Cup Qualifiers.


The winner of each World *Magic* Cup Qualifier will represent their country on the national team at the 2016 World *Magic* Cup, which takes place November 18-20, 2016 in Rotterdam, the Netherlands.








Residency & Eligibility
-----------------------


Each country's 2016 World *Magic* Cup Qualifiers are open only to those qualified players who are citizens or have been residents of that country since January 1, 2015.


If a player competes in a 2016 World *Magic* Cup Qualifier in one country, then they may *not* compete in a 2016 World *Magic* Cup Qualifier (or be a member of a national team) of another country in 2016. Complete information can be found in the [*Magic: The Gathering* Premier Event Invitation Policy](http://wpn.wizards.com/en/document/magic-premier-event-invitation-policy).


If you are planning to participate in an upcoming World *Magic* Cup Qualifier, your first step should be to verify that you are listed under the correct nationality. To do this, just sign into the [Planeswalker Points](http://www.wizards.com/magic/planeswalkerpoints) website with your DCI number and password. Your current nationality is listed immediately to the right of your name.


If your nationality needs to be corrected, please fill in an appeal immediately using the form here (You have to be logged into the Planeswalker Points site with your DCI number and password to access this form). Nationality-related inquiries should have “WMCQ Nationality Appeal” in the subject line. Nationality-related appeals will be given high priority during the WMCQ season.


**2016 World Magic Cup Qualifiers are invitation-only tournaments.** These Premier Events will be held in countries that have been selected to have national teams compete at the 2016 World *Magic* Cup. The list of selected countries can be viewed in Appendix C of the [Premier Event Invitation Policy](http://wpn.wizards.com/en/document/magic-premier-event-invitation-policy).


A player who has already qualified and received an invitation to the 2016 World *Magic* Cup may not compete in further 2016 World Magic Cup Qualifiers.








Invitation Criteria
-------------------


The following players are invited to compete in the 2016 World *Magic* Cup Qualifiers:


* Players in the Planeswalker Points 2016 Yearly Award Points Season that have accrued the minimum amount of points for that country. The number of points required for each country can be found in Appendix C of the [Premier Event Invitation Policy](http://wpn.wizards.com/en/document/magic-premier-event-invitation-policy).
* Players from that country with Pro Tour Players Club Silver, Gold, or Platinum level (as of the date of the World *Magic* Cup Qualifier).
* Players (from that country) that are members of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame.
* Winners of World *Magic* Cup Qualifier Last Chance Qualifier tournaments.






Last Chance Qualifiers
----------------------


Most World *Magic* Cup Qualifiers will be preceded by Last Chance Qualifiers! The winner of a Last Chance Qualifier receives an invitation to compete in the World *Magic* Cup Qualifier taking place the next day.


Last Chance Qualifiers will be held the day before each World *Magic* Cup Qualifier. Their format will match the World *Magic* Cup Qualifier that they are tied to.


Contact the organizer of the World *Magic* Cup Qualifier you plan to attend to get specific date, time, and cost of the Last Chance Qualifier.


Complete details on Last Chance Qualifiers can be found in the [*Magic* Premier Event Invitation Policy](http://wpn.wizards.com/en/document/magic-premier-event-invitation-policy).








Multi-Day Even
--------------


Some World *Magic* Cup Qualifiers may be two-day events for countries with a large historical record of attendance. For events that are split into two days, the Swiss rounds will take place on Saturday, and the Top 8 playoff will take place on Sunday.


Events that will be two-day tournaments will be announced in advance and reflected on the schedule. Events that are two-day tournaments, once announced as a multi-day event, will not reverse to a one-day event. For full information, including which events will be multi-day tournaments, check with your local tournament organizer.

* Swiss rounds will each be 50 minutes in length.
* All players may participate in all Swiss rounds.
* Single-elimination playoff matches will have no time limit.
* The top players after the final Swiss round will advance to the single-elimination playoff.
* The number of players that advanced to the playoff is based on the number of players in the event.
* Number of Swiss rounds based on attendance according to the following chart:



| Attendance | Swiss rounds | Playoff |
| 8 | 3 Rounds (Single-Elimination) | None |
| 9-16 | 5 Rounds | Top 4 |
| 17 - 32 | 5 Rounds | Top 8 |
| 33 - 64 | 6 Rounds | Top 8 |
| 65 - 128 | 7 Rounds | Top 8 |
| 129 - 226 | 8 Rounds | Top 8 |
| 227 - 409 | 9 Rounds | Top 8 |
| 410 or higher | 10 Rounds | Top 8 |



* Playoff matches will be best 2 of 3.
* Playoff matches will be single-elimination.
* While there are no time limits in the playoff rounds, players are expected to play at a reasonable pace and finish their matches within 60 minutes.
* Standings after the Swiss rounds will be used to determine final order in the standings for losing players in the quarterfinal and semifinal playoff rounds.
* For the first game of each match in the playoff, the player that finished higher in the Swiss rounds chooses either to play first or to play second. For subsequent games in each playoff match, the usual Play/Draw rule applies (loser of the previous game decides whether to play first in the next game).

Promos and Prizes
-----------------


Each participant in a 2016 World *Magic* Cup Qualifier will receive an alternate-art Abrupt Decay promotional card (while supplies last).


The Top 8 finishing players will each receive an exclusive World *Magic* Cup Qualifier Top 8 playmat.


The winner of each event will receive an invitation and a [travel award](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/travel-award-faq) to compete on their country's national team at the 2016 World *Magic* Cup.


Other prizes are determined by the organizer of each event. Contact the organizer for additional details.
