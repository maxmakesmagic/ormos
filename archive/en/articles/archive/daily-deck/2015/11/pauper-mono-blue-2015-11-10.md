Pauper Mono-Blue
================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on November 10, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Welcome to *Magic Online* Week! This week on Daily Decks, we'll be looking at some of the top decks on *Magic Online*. Today's deck is a Pauper deck. While Pauper is played here and there in paper, the format really took off on *Magic Online* for its accessibility and ease of play. In Pauper, every card in your deck must be a common. It could have been printed at a higher rarity at some point, but as long as it was printed as a common in a *Magic Online* set, it's legal in Pauper.


*Magic Online*-user Redler piloted this deck to a 3-0 finish in the most recent Pauper Daily Event. This is a disruptive mono-blue aggro deck, or a deck that looks to cast early creatures and then use counterspells or other disruption to stop the opponent from developing their game plan.


As you can see from the decklist, many of the creatures can be cast for little or no mana. [Cloud of Faeries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+of+Faeries) allows you to untap lands, giving you access to [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) or [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive) in the same turn. [Spire Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem) can just be placed on the battlefield if you have six Islands in play, and [Ninja of the Deep Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours) can be snuck onto the battlefield at the cost of returning an unblocked creature to your hand. If that creature happens to be a [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), you have another chance to counter your opponent's spell.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cloud+of+Faeries)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+of+Faeries)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours)
This deck also plays a variety of card-draw and library-manipulation spells. [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) and [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) are so efficient at finding what you need that they've been banned in the Modern format for being too strong. [Gush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush), once restricted in Vintage and banned in Legacy, is a card-draw spell that is essentially free. In a deck with only eighteen lands, returning two Islands to your hand is often not a downside, as you can replay one of them to get ahead on mana.


This Pauper deck is a blast to play, and congratulations to Redler for going undefeated in the Daily!
