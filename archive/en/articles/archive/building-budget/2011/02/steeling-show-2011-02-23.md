=================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on February 23, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published

I first heard about this deck from Daily MTG's very own Steve Sadin. I was stuck in the Detroit airport for a few hours and decided to give my old buddy a call. Steve was quick to explain that he was working on a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck for the Pro Tour, and I couldn't help but get excited about the deck. On paper, it's a turn-four aggro deck. A turn-four kill is incredibly fast for a format like Standard. I was convinced of the deck's power within minutes of hearing the list.

Fast forward to the present.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab131_steel.jpg)  
A lot went wrong for the team running this deck at Pro Tour Paris, but the biggest problem for the Standard [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck may have been the success of Kuldotha Red at the StarCityGames.com Standard Open in Indianapolis:

This deck is very different from the [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck played by Sam Black, but Kuldotha Red's success may have put the [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck in an awkward spot leading up to the Pro Tour. You see, cards like [Ratchet Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb), [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm), [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith), and [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) were not in most people's main decks before the rise of Kuldotha Red. After Indy, all of these answers became must-have tools for Pro Tour competitors who were looking to keep up with the blazingly fast red token deck.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Tempered Steel | Kuldotha Rebirth |

Things have changed since the Pro Tour. Nowadays, everyone is back on [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) plans. Smart players are tuning their decks to deal with control mirrors. This gives us a great opportunity to attack the metagame with the most aggressive list we can muster.

Sam chose to splash blue off [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) and [Seachrome Coast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seachrome+Coast). He padded his splash in the sideboard with a [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress), an extra [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal), and a few [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s. [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) is the most impressive counter available, but I still have a hard time believing that the blue splash is necessary. I could make the deck a lot more budget-friendly by cutting the second color and sticking to a mono-white plan.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Mox Opal | Seachrome Coast |

[Myr Sire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Sire) seems great to me in this type of deck. It gives the deck more resistance against board sweeping effects, and it's significantly easier to acquire than [Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker). I'm going to go ahead and play with [Myr Sire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Sire).

![Myr Sire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Myr+Sire)  
[Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol) may seem a bit awkward, but it puts your opponent in a very rough spot, especially if he or she is relying too heavily on sorcery-speed removal. [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol) attacks for a lot of damage when you have a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) on the battlefield. Having [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol) and [Myr Sire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Sire) gives you draws where [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) can be laughed off. They cast [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), you swing back for 7 the next turn. Seems fair.

![Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glint+Hawk+Idol)  
Most of the creatures here are necessary to satisfy the deck's mana curve. Cards like [Vector Asp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vector+Asp) may seem awkward, but having more one-mana artifact creatures is absolutely necessary to your plan.

[Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) can be difficult to acquire, so I'm going to go ahead and cut those from the deck. If you're lucky enough to have some [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal)s from booster packs or trading, then I would go ahead and cut two [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) and a [Myr Sire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Sire) for three copies. The [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal)s aren't completely necessary to the deck's functioning, but they do increase the power of your best draws.

Building a sideboard for a deck like this may seem like a lesson in loose wiring, but I feel like we have a lot of options. For example, there are a good number of decks that simply lose to an [Argentum Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argentum+Armor) that has been cheated into play. We're already playing a great number of the tools necessary to building some [Argentum Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argentum+Armor).
