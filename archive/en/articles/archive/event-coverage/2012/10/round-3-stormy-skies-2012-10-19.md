Round 3: Stormy Skies
=====================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 19, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Walkinshaw-Profile.jpg)
By Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw












[Ivan Floch (RUG) vs. Conley Woods (Jund)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptrtr12/fm2)


William Jensen (Storm) vs. Shahar Shenhar (White-Blue-Red Delver)
-----------------------------------------------------------------


[Christian Calcano (Doran) vs. Geoffrey Siron (Tron)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptrtr12/fm4)




---

Grand Prix Salt Lake City 2012 winner Shahar Shenhar was looking to establish a fast clock with his White-Blue-Red [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck, disrupting his opponent's plan while the clock ticks.


William Jensen, who missed making it into the Hall of Fame this year by the narrowest of margins, was on a different plan entirely. His Blue-Red Storm deck aims to achieve a critical mass of spells as fast as possible, generally ignoring any of that silly "interaction" nonsense.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptrtr12/r3_jensen_vs_shenhar.jpg)*William Jensen is hoping to blow past the disruption of Shahar Shenhar's Delver deck for a speedy victory.* 
### Game 1


Shenhar came screaming out of the gates with a [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) and a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), attacking William Jensen for 4 on his second turn, before passing back the turn and cutting to the chase with a "Going off?"


"Hopefully," Jensen replied, paying two life to cast [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), and then [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot)'ing Shenhar's team. Shenhar rebuilt his offense with a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), now at 14 from his own lands, while Jensen was at 12 from a combination of paying life instead of mana for his spells and lands, and one feisty cat (now deceased).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptrtr12/r3_jensen.jpg)*Buying time is the name of Jensen's game.* 

![Eiganjo Castle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Eiganjo+Castle)

Jensen took some damage of his own, fetching a [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) with a [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) and falling to 9. He then cast [Sleight of Hand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand) and a [Goblin Electromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer). Shenhar attacked with his Geist, an [Eiganjo Castle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eiganjo+Castle) ready to protect it. Jensen declined to block, falling to 3 life. During Jensen's upkeep, Shenhar [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile)'d the Goblin to keep him from getting any use out of his extra land.


Jensen cast a [Manamorphose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose), then a [Pyretic Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual), and a [Desperate Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual). Each time, he cast his spell waiting patiently to see if Shenhar wanted to do anything with the two mana he was holding open. Jensen used the mana to cast [Past in Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames), and again, Shenhar allowed it. Jensen fanned out his graveyard. Assuming Shenhar was bluffing a counter with his mana, Jensen would have no difficulty using his two remaining untapped lands to re-cast the rituals in his graveyard, and then the [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) beneath them. But first, he had to see what Shenhar was holding. He fell to 1 life to again pay the Phyrexian price on a [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe).


"Sure," Shenhar shrugged, revealing a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) nestled between two other cards.


"Yep," Jensen admitted, swiping up his cards. The Storm count was currently six. [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) wasn't winning Jensen the game without the rest of the Rituals resolving first, and the [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) would be enough to bring Jensen's engine grinding to a halt before it could finish.


William Jensen 0, Shahar Shenhar 1


### Game 2


Jensen began game 2 with some [Sleight of Hand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand), while Shenhar just played a [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) untapped. Jensen then [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)d Shenhar the slow way, but passed back his third turn without a third land. Shenhar's turn two [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) jumped into action, a [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) fetching a [Sacred Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry), dropping Shenhar to 13 life, as he attacked Jensen to 16.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptrtr12/r3_shenhar.jpg)*Backed with explosive starts featuring aggressive creatures, Shenhar wants to end things before Jensen assembles the perfect storm.* 
Jensen again failed to find a land, and Shenhar flashed a [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) into play. A few turns later, and Jensen was again packing up his cards.


William Jensen 0, Shahar Shenhar 2


"I think if I had had drawn a Red mana, I could have killed you turn two, but I didn't," Jensen shrugged.


"Is this matchup good for you?" Shenhar asked.


"No, Aggro Control is the worst matchup for our deck," Jensen admitted, signing the result slip.
