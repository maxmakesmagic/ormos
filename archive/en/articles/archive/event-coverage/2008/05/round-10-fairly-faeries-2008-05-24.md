Round 10: Fairly Faeries
========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 24, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_12.png)
By By Josh Bennett











The specter of the Faerie deck hung over this tournament in the days leading up to it, and though their turnout didn’t hit the doomsday predictions of some, they still accounted for a quarter of the tournament. Fast-forward to Day 2, and many of them have survived in the hands of skilled pilots, such as Brazil’s Paulo Vitor “PV” Damo da Rosa and reigning Dutch National Champion Robert van Medevoort. They knew they were in for a mirror as they shuffled up.

### Game 1

PV’s first hand was a bit of a clunker, so he sent it back. His reward? Six nonlands. He quickly went to five, shaking his head. His first two turns were playing a [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and then attacking with it. Meanwhile, van Medevoort was suspending [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) and dropping [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). PV took the opportunity to main-phase a [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/pthol08/r10_damo%20da%20rosa_van%20medevoort.jpg)*Robert van Medevoort and Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa are both packing Faeries* Van Medevoort untapped, then went for the gusto with a second [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). For good measure, he suspended a second Vision. PV started to send his [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), keeping the Faerie population somewhat in check. The second time, van Medevoort tried to interpose a Vendillion Clique, but [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) sent it to the bin. Van Medevoort was still waiting on his fourth land.

Finally, his first Vision went off and gave him the land he needed. PV’s team charged in, and this time it was [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) that came to play for van Medevoort. PV handed it a [Rune Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag). Van Medevoort traded two Faerie Rogues for the Spellstutter, and chumped the [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault). After untapping, his [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s took him to 11.

PV didn’t have any action, but van Medevoort was running out of time. The next turn he got to cash in his second Vision, earning himself a [Pendelhaven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) and another Clique to drop in PV’s upkeep. This time, PV had [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) but chose to play it before van Medevoort championed, allowing van Medevoort to remove one of his [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s from the game permanently.

PV was out of gas, and the tide began to turn in van Medevoort’s favor. Soon he was sending his growing army of tokens into the red zone. PV fell to 15, then 10. He tried to intercept with a [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) of his own, but lost it to [Rune Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag). His last ditch was [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) backed by Spellstutter, but van Medevoort had Snag and Spellstutter in hand, securing the game.

van Medevoort 1, Damo da Rosa 0

### Game 2

Van Medevoort went down to six, and they were off. PV cleared the way for his turn-two [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) with [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), stealing van Medevoort’s, leaving behind [Faerie Conclave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave), [Secluded Glen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Secluded+Glen), [River of Tears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears), [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) and [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). He untapped and suspended a Vision, then Spellstuttered van Medevoort’s attempt at a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) (championing the Spellstutter) stole van Medevoort’s next turn, and after an attack, he was down to 11.

He tried to stall the tide with [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), but PV had his own to counter it and send van Medevoort’s Conclave back to his hand. Van Medevoort fell to 4. A second Command succeeded, catching PV’s Vision and locking down his team, but that only bought him a turn. Soon they were shuffling up for Game 3.

Damo da Rosa 1, van Medevoort 1

Meanwhile, PV was shaking his head, chiding himself for a mistake. “Could’ve won last turn,” he said. [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) on his Clique would’ve freed [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) to counter the Command, with enough Faeries left over to deal the remaining 4.

### Game 3

The deciding game saw both players with turn-two [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s, but van Medevoort’s opening was flanked by Vision on turn one and [Pendelhaven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) and [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) on turn three, a brutal opening. He took PV’s [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) and left him with three land and a pair of [Rune Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag)s.

PV was in a bind. Van Medevoort added [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) to his team, and began the aerial assault, his [Pendelhaven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) giving him a free pass from PV’s tokens. PV took a turn to carefully assess his situation, eventually suspending a Vision of his own, while van Medevort’s was already paying off. He took a couple more hits, feigning a four-mana spell. On his upkeep, van Medevoort went for an actual [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique). PV spent both his [Rune Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag)s, and took the opportunity of a tapped Pendelaven to hit with his tokens and a [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), bringing van Medevoort to 9. 

That was as close as it would get. Van Medevoort’s counterattack took PV to 7, and with [Pendelhaven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) back online, PV couldn’t punch through for more damage.

van Medevoort 2, Damo da Rosa 1
