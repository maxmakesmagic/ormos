The King of Conspiracy
======================



 Posted in [From the Lab](/en/articles/columns/from-the-lab-archive)
 on May 21, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 






Welcome to From the Lab, folks! It's Conspiracy Week here on DailyMTG, but rather than previewing a new card, I'll be talking about one that's already been revealed. This card excites me more than any other in the set, and I couldn't wait to start building decks around it. It's definitely designed with Johnnies in mind, and there's no end to the craziness you can create with it.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/tcg/products/cns/asdfsc3wfas234/UKVMNPITB3_EN.jpg)

Brago, King Eternal is like a [Galepowder Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galepowder+Mage) cranked up to 11. Although you have to hit your opponent to get the effect, once you do, you can flicker any number of nonland permanents, returning them to the battlefield immediately. This could allow you to abuse any number of enters-the-battlefield abilities, but I have something even more devious in mind.


### The King of Combat


The first card that came to mind when looking at Brago was [Aurelia, the Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurelia%2C+the+Warleader). With these two on the battlefield, you can get an infinite number of combat phases. Simply attack with both creatures, and Aurelia will give you an extra combat phase. Then, when Brago hits your opponent, he can flicker Aurelia. Since this Aurelia is considered a new object, when you attack again her ability will trigger once more. So long as you keep hitting your opponent, you can keep attacking until he or she is dead.


Although [Aurelia, the Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurelia%2C+the+Warleader) is the easiest way to get infinite combat phases, it's not the only one. [Godo, Bandit Warlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godo%2C+Bandit+Warlord) also creates an extra combat phase when he attacks for the first time each turn. There is a problem, however.

You see, Godo doesn't have haste, so you won't be able to attack again after you flicker him, meaning he can't give us our infinite combat phases the same way Aurelia can. But he can give us the means to do so.


When he enters the battlefield, Godo searches your library for an Equipment and puts it onto the battlefield. If you search for [Swiftfoot Boots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swiftfoot+Boots), you can set up a hasty Brago for later. Additionally, Godo can also search up [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) to untap all your lands. With [Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggravated+Assault), you can pay five mana for an extra combat phase as many times as you want. So long as [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) keeps untapping your lands, you can keep doing it forever.


[Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) can dispose of a pesky blocker or any creature that threatens to mess with your plans. It can also buy you time to set things up in the face of attackers. [Order & Chaos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Order+%26amp%3B+Chaos) can remove attackers as well, but its primary purpose is making sure Brago will get through on the turn you combo off, preventing all of your opponent's creatures from blocking for the turn. I've also included some mana-producing artifacts to let you cast the expensive spells in the deck a turn or two ahead of time.

### The King of Artifacts


This next combo has a lot of pieces, but the key card that started it was [Strionic Resonator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strionic+Resonator). If you can keep making copies of Brago's ability while it's on the stack, you can keep flickering permanents forever. By using artifacts, you can take advantage of any abilities that require the artifacts to tap. Brago will return them all to the battlefield untapped, and artifacts don't have any summoning sickness to worry about.


So, Brago will untap [Strionic Resonator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strionic+Resonator) each time by flickering it. The next step is to get two mana each time. Fortunately that's easy, as any artifact that produces multiple mana will do the job. I chose [Thran Dynamo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Dynamo) so you get infinite mana out of the deal as well.

Now you can flicker everything forever, but you still need a way to win the game. I've included two options. You can use your infinite mana to kill your opponent with [Goblin Cannon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Cannon), activating it a few thousand times before the first ability resolves and makes you sacrifice it. You can also use Brago's ability to untap artifacts by flickering them to mill out your opponent with [Millstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millstone).


Since the combo uses so many pieces, I decided that rather than include four copies of each piece and hope to draw the right combination, I'd include just two copies, in case the first gets destroyed, and leave it to an assortment of tutors to find them. [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) and [Enlightened Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) can both do the job, and [Tezzeret the Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret+the+Seeker) can put artifacts directly onto the battlefield. Since you'll need to find Brago as well, I added in [Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse) and [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) to help dig through the deck.

This combo will often take some time to set up, so you'll need a way to protect yourself. [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) and [Render Silent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Render+Silent) allows you to stop a spell or two you don't like, and [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) can help if you miss something important.


With so many ways to grab artifacts in the deck, I included a couple cards that can be searched up if you need them. [Whispersilk Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak) can be used to protects Brago, King Eternal from removal spells and blockers alike, ensuring you can trigger his ability. Finally, [Brittle Effigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brittle+Effigy) can be tutored up if there's a creature on the battlefield that simply must go.

### The King of the Hill


Two kings, one throne. Only one Brago can take his place as the true king, and there's only one way to decide who gets the job: A good old-fashioned fight to the death. Fortunately, these kings aren't too high and mighty to get their hands dirty. Let's take this down to the arena and watch the battle unfold.


**Game 1**


Eternal Combat started off by using a [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) to get [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry). The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine played a [Temple of Enlightenment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Enlightenment), scrying a card to the bottom. Eternal Combat cast [Coldsteel Heart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coldsteel+Heart), and The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast [Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse). Eternal Combat followed up with Brago, King Eternal on turn three, but The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine exiled Brago with [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere).


Eternal Combat cast a second copy of Brago, and The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine used [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) to get [Strionic Resonator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strionic+Resonator). Eternal Combat attacked for 2 with Brago and cast [Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggravated+Assault) before passing the turn.


The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast [Whispersilk Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak) and ended the turn. Eternal Combat grabbed a Mountain with [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) and attacked with Brago. [Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggravated+Assault) created a second combat phase for another 2 damage.


The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast [Enlightened Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) at end of turn, getting [Brittle Effigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brittle+Effigy) and using it to exile Brago. Eternal Combat cast [Aurelia, the Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurelia%2C+the+Warleader), and attacked twice. The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast [Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse), then cast Brago, King Eternal and equipped [Whispersilk Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak).


[Aurelia, the Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurelia%2C+the+Warleader) attacked for 3, and Brago blocked. Aurelia attacked again during the second combat phase, and with 3 damage already Brago was forced to step aside. [Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggravated+Assault) created one more combat phase, and Aurelia got in for another 3 damage.


The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast [Goblin Cannon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Cannon) and attacked with Brago. [Whispersilk Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak) prevented Aurelia from blocking, and the combo went off, with [Thran Dynamo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Dynamo) producing a few million mana to power [Goblin Cannon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Cannon).



![Goblin Cannon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Goblin%20Cannon&options=)


**Game 2**


Eternal Combat started off with a [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry), and The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine played [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain). Eternal Combat cast [Boros Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Signet), and The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine simple played a land and passed.


Eternal Combat cast another [Boros Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Signet) and [Ring of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ring+of+Thune), and The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast Impulse at end of turn. The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine ended the turn after just playing a land again, and Eternal Combat cast Brago, King Eternal, which was countered by [Render Silent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Render+Silent).


The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine played a land and passed again. Eternal Combat cast [Aurelia, the Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurelia%2C+the+Warleader), which was met with a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine played a land and passed, and Eternal Combat cast [Godo, Bandit Warlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godo%2C+Bandit+Warlord). This one resolved, and searched up [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine).


The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) at end of turn, getting [Tezzeret the Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret+the+Seeker) and land. An [Enlightened Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) then searched up [Thran Dynamo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Dynamo). The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine cast [Thran Dynamo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Dynamo) followed by Brago, King Eternal, and ended the turn.


Eternal Combat equipped [Godo, Bandit Warlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godo%2C+Bandit+Warlord) with [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine), then attacked for 5. The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine took the damage, and [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) triggered. Godo attacked again for another 5 damage, and Eternal Combat cast a second copy of Godo to get the trigger, searching up [Swiftfoot Boots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swiftfoot+Boots) and equipping it to the first Godo.


The [Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) Machine Cast [Tezzeret the Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret+the+Seeker) and used him to put [Strionic Resonator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strionic+Resonator) on the battlefield. [Millstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millstone) was cast from hand, and with just enough mana left to activated the Resonator the first time, the combo was completed. Eternal Combat lost the game during the next draw step.
