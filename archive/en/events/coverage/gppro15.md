TOP 8 COVERAGE



Sky Mason usually attended Grand Prix as a judge. This time, with Red-Green Devotion in his hands, he attended as a winner. After defeating Pedro Carvalho in the semifinals, Mason took down his finals opponent Neal Sacks in three games and fulfilled a promise to his friends from the previous week: Win a Grand Prix.


As 1,053 players demonstrated, Standard isn’t quite settled. Though the finals showdown between Red-Green Devotion and Abzan Aggro was a familiar sight the decks beyond ran the gamut. From Abzan Megamorph in Josh McClain’s and Bant Megamorph in Neal Sack’s semifinalist finish to Pedro Carvalho’s Dragonlord Atarka twist in Sidisi Whip and even an appearance by Five-Color Dragons – built on a green-black shell – from the champion’s quarterfinal opponent Oliver Tiu to even top-ranked and World Championship competitor apparent Steve Rubin’s Red-Green Dragons, it was impossible to predict how things would shake out. Patrick Dickmann narrowly missed the Top 8 with a Dragonlord Ojutai-backed Jeskai take that surged throughout the weekend.


The waning days of *Dragons of Tarkir* Standard will be filled with even more exploration and decisions as Grand Prix Buenos Aires follows hereafter. While there are no surefire answers in Standard now, Providence found its one champion: Congratulations again to Sky Mason, winner of Grand Prix Providence!

DAY 1 COVERAGE

Strategies that could go over the top of everyone else’s have paid off all day, and it showed across the top of the standings.


At the top of the leaderboard of the 134 returning tomorrow are four undefeated players: Josh McClain, Noah Walker, Matt Tumavitch, and Brian Braun-Duin.
