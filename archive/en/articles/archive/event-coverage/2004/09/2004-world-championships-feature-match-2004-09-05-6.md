2004 World Championships Feature Match
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 5, 2004 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20171224024527im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_MikeTurian.jpg?itok=LXCQPehw)
By Mike Turian




Before joining Magic Online as its digital product manager, Mike worked as a producer for Wizards's technology department, a product manager for organized play, and as an R&D lead developer. He has played Magic Online since it came out in 2002 and Magic since The Dark. 







![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/tfin1_1.jpg)


A Zink made it to the final day of 2004 Worlds, but it wasn't reigning World Champion Daniel Zink. Instead, his twin brother Sebastian saw action Sunday as a member of the German national team. Sitting across from him was Dilson Ramos Da Fonseca of the Belgium team, which had been in the top half of the leaderboard for the entire weekend.


### Game 1


Sebastian Zink won the die roll but chose to draw first. The two players were in the A seat. Zink's deck featured [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball), while Dilson Ramos Da Fonseca had a [Greater Harvester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Harvester) as his big bomb.


A turn-one [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) saw Ramos Da Fonseca placing both cards on the bottom of his library. Zink played first-turn [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) followed by a [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats). Ramos Da Fonseca played a [Spire Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem) on his turn while Zink cast a [Nim Abomination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Abomination).


[Spire Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem) came across for 2 damage and Ramos Da Fonseca played a [Fleshgrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshgrafter). Zink didn't want to worry about the Nim's drawback so he sent it in. Ramos Da Fonseca didn't block with the [Fleshgrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshgrafter).


Ramos Da Fonseca played a [Neurok Spy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy) and the passed the turn back. Once again, Zink sent in [Nim Abomination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Abomination) but this time it was blocked by the [Fleshgrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshgrafter). [Fleshgrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshgrafter) grew once a Thermal Glider was pitched to it. A [Consume Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) hit [Neurok Spy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy). Ramos Da Fonseca played a [Arcbound Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Stinger).


Zink cast [Night's Whisper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night%27s+Whisper) and a [Myr Quadropod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Quadropod) to guard the table. [Synod Centurion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Synod+Centurion) came out for Ramos Da Fonseca not to be outdone Zink played a [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer). The Enforcer and Centurion traded with one another.


After much deliberation, Zink finally played a [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica). On his next turn, he cast a [Clockwork Condor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockwork+Condor). Ramos Da Fonseca brought out some removal with an [Essence Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain). It was good timing because the next turn Zink made Ramos Da Fonseca discard a land to [Fill with Fright](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fill+with+Fright).


Zink attacked with [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats) and [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica). Ramos Da Fonseca's [Arcbound Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Stinger) traded with [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica) that dealt two more thanks to the [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault).


An attack with [Spire Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem) put Zink at 5 life but Zink's [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats) came over unblocked and put Ramos Da Fonseca to 6. Zink was quick to make X=6 on his [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) at Ramos Da Fonseca's head.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/tfin1_2.jpg)Sebastian Zink 1, Dilson Ramos Da Fonseca 0


### Game 2


Ramos Da Fonseca kept his opening hand while Zink went back in for a six new cards. Ramos Da Fonseca cast a turn-two [Arcbound Slith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Slith) that was poised to cause big problems for Zink. He did find a blocker with [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats) and the two traded when Ramos Da Fonseca attacked.


Ramos Da Fonseca refilled his hand with a [Night's Whisper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night%27s+Whisper). Then on the following turn, he played out a [Clockwork Condor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockwork+Condor). Zink once again had no play. [Greater Harvester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Harvester) came out for Ramos Da Fonseca.


Zink would need to find an answer to the Harvester quickly or the match would be tied at 1-1. All he could manage was a [Myr Quadropod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Quadropod). Zink took the Harvester and Condor putting him down to 10 life. Ramos Da Fonseca cast an [Ebon Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ebon+Drake) to continue the assault. [Myr Quadropod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Quadropod) chump-blocked on the next attack. When the [Electrostatic Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Bolt) tried to finish off the Harvester, Ramos Da Fonseca had [Wail of the Nim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wail+of+the+Nim) waiting. Zink had no more answers to the [Greater Harvester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Harvester), so he conceded.


Sebastian Zink 1, Dilson Ramos Da Fonseca 1


### Game 3


Zink once again chose to draw for the third and deciding game. Ramos Da Fonseca had a turn-three [Talisman of Progress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talisman+of+Progress) while Zink played out a [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica) on the third turn. Ramos Da Fonseca drew two cards with [Night's Whisper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night%27s+Whisper) to fill his hand back up.


Zink sent in the [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica) and then played a [Fleshgrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshgrafter) but it was met with a [Condescend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend) for two. Ramos Da Fonseca played a [Synod Centurion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Synod+Centurion) that was only being protected by the [Talisman of Progress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talisman+of+Progress).


The [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica) once again hit Ramos Da Fonseca, who soon got to [Annul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annul) a [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) -- visibly paining Zink. Ramos Da Fonseca swung in with the Centurion and played an [Arcbound Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Stinger) and [Neurok Spy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy) afterwards. Down 16-12 on life, everything else looked to be in Ramos Da Fonseca's favor.


Zink played out a [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats) and passed the turn. Ramos Da Fonseca attacked with the [Arcbound Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Stinger) and [Neurok Spy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy). Zink was knocked to 13 life.


Zink attacked with both the [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats) and the [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica). Ramos Da Fonseca did not block and went to 8. Zink cast a [Nim Devourer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Devourer). This gave Ramos Da Fonseca the perfect chance to cast [Wail of the Nim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wail+of+the+Nim). He did -- with entwine -- killing both the Devourer and the [Nim Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Replica).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/tfin1_3.jpg)Ramos Da Fonseca attacked with everything, putting Zink to 4 life. Then Zink refilled his own life when he [Essence Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain)ed the [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats). Finally he cast an [Arcbound Slith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Slith). Zink needed a miracle card.


Zink played a [Nim Lasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Lasher) and a [Clockwork Condor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockwork+Condor) in a last effort to turn the tide of the game. But there was no help coming for Zink, and Ramos Da Fonseca notched a match win for Belgium.


**Final Score**  

Sebastian Zink 1, Dilson Ramos Da Fonseca 2


**Match Score**  

Germany 1, Belgium 1
