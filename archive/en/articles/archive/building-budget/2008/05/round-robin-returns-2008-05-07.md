The Bringer deck uses [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) plus [Deathrender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrender) to massively accelerate huge creatures into play. If you put [Deathrender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrender) on [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) and then sacrifice your guy, you can end up with two Bringers in play in one fell swoop! The deck also uses [Coretapper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coretapper) for a good sacrifice effect.


As mentioned above, the budget for this column is thirty tickets or less, and the initial version of the Bringer deck, as provided by MagicProVirus, is well over forty tickets. In addition, there are a few components to the deck that tear it in too many directionsâfor instance, [Coretapper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coretapper), while it is an easily sacrificed artifact creature, doesn't really have much synergy with the deck outside of [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) (which is hard to get past one counter, as there are only four non-blue-producing lands in the deck) and [Lightning Coils](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Coils).


Â 



![Composite Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Composite+Golem)

MagicProVirus really liked the [Lightning Coils](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Coils) aspect of the deck, so I decided to go more in that direction for the initial rebuild. [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) is a great sacrifice outlet for a Bringer deck, since it can translate directly into a Bringer without the need for additional colored mana. Moreover, [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) combos well with an underutilized card from *Time Spiral*: [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise). If you enchant [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) with [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise), you will be able to create a loop: [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) adds ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif) to your mana pool, [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise) returns [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) to play and [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise) to your hand, and you can use the mana from [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) to re-enchant the Golem and start anew. This is great for upping your storm count (in case you want to go that route), but in the spirit of the intent of this deck, it is also fantastic for getting an arbitrarily large number of charge counters on [Lightning Coils](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Coils) for a truly massive attack of 3/1 creatures.

Since I want to focus more on the combo aspect of this deck, we can safely take out three of the four [Sundering Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan)s. They are great creatures, but they also cost nearly four tickets each, and removing three of the four will take us closer to budget. I also took out the [Coretapper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coretapper)s and [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism)s in favor of [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise) and [Prismatic Lens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens). Since this deck already runs [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge), I thought that [Seat of the Synod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seat+of+the+Synod) would be a better fit than [Coastal Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coastal+Tower); this gives us a land drop that can also function as a good card to discard against the Thirst. Last, I took out one [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver), one [Legacy Weapon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legacy+Weapon), and one [Bringer of the White Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bringer+of+the+White+Dawn) to add in [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) (a great tutor in an artifact-based deck, especially when I now have several one-ofs) and a third [Lightning Coils](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Coils).

**Game 1: Zeb364 (Red-White-Blue Scepter)**


Zeb364 hits me with [Lava Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Spike) on turn one and [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) on turn two, then imprints [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) on [Isochron Scepter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) on turn three. I drop two Urza lands the first two turns and find the third one with [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) by turn four. Zeb364 plays [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) on my [Deathrender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrender), but I get down [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) and start attacking. My first [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) target ([Sundering Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan)) is also met by [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), but my second one ([Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver)) lands, allowing me to burn him out with his own spells left in hand.


Record: 1-0


Notes: This game, I got stuck with two Bringers of the White Dawn in my hand. I decide that I really do want the [Coastal Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coastal+Tower)s in the deckâI have more white Bringers than any other color Bringer, but I still want access to blue for [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) and [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate). I decide to yank out the [Cloudpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost)s. They aren't great without the ability to tutor them up, when I already have the Urza Lands for colorless mana acceleration. I also add in one [Academy Ruins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins). I have a lot of one-of artifacts, and I'd love a way to recur them if they get used or countered and I don't have a [Bringer of the White Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bringer+of+the+White+Dawn) handy.

Record: 2-1


While I did finally go "infinite" with the [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise) combo, the deck has been wayyyy too slow, and I don't have nearly enough sacrifice outlets for [Deathrender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrender). I need something I can drop earlierâso after searching [Gatherer](http://ww2.wizards.com/Gatherer/Index.aspx) for blue or artifact sacrifice outlets, I decide to go with [Hapless Researcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hapless+Researcher). I also want to be able to play the Bringers without the absolute need for [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem). To this effect, I go with a fairly radical solution: instead of adding in specific color fixing, I add in one of every non-blue-producing Signet from *Ravnica* block! The theory here is as follows: I want a two-drop that can produce two off-colors of mana, since I'll likely have access to blue, but not necessarily green, red, black, or white mana through my lands. If I get any one Signet, I can [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) for another one, and fix my mana to all five colors...or grab the one [Darksteel Ingot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Ingot). I go with the multi-Signet plan instead of [Prismatic Lens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens) and [Lightning Coils](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Coils), and get ready for version three of this deck!

**Game 4: Diak0gotik (Artifact Sac)**


Diak0gotik drops [Spawning Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit), [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), [Myr Retriever](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Retriever), and [Arcbound Worker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker) within the first three turns. I get a draw with three Signets, enabling me to play [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) on turn four and activate it on turn five, adding a [Hapless Researcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hapless+Researcher) to my board. This allows me to sacrifice Diak0gotik's entire board to [Spawning Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit), play the [Arcbound Reclaimer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Reclaimer) in his hand, and make him return both the [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and the Worker to the top of his deck. The next turn, I play [Deathrender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrender), equip it to my Researcher, and put [Bosh, Iron Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bosh%2C+Iron+Golem) into play for free after attacking for 3. Diak0gotik tries to get back into the game with [Lodestone Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Myr), but I am able to hardcast [Bringer of the White Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bringer+of+the+White+Dawn) (thanks to Signets of varying colors!), allowing me to get down the [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) lock.


Record: 3-1


**Game 5: misterpid ([Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) / [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon))**


Misterpid drops [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) on turn one and suspends [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) on turn two. This board is followed by [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) on turn four and [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) on turn five. For my part, I drop two Urza Lands and a [Coastal Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coastal+Tower), and proceed to draw four [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) and three [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate), fail to find my third Urza land (I had three Towers and four Power Plants by the end of the game, between my hand, board, and graveyard), and concede the turn before I'm going to die to double [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon).


Record: 3-2

Nyad99 plays [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), [Wanderer's Twig](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wanderer%27s+Twig), and [Civic Wayfinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder). Nyad99 kills my Bringer with [Death Rattle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Rattle), and I drop [Bringer of the Blue Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bringer+of+the+Blue+Dawn) straight into play! Nyad99 is unable to deal with both a 7/7 trampler and my ability to draw three cards a turn, and quickly succumbs to my forces.

This was a long, long game. Enright gets [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) and [Looter *il*-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor) and starts discarding [Squee, Goblin Nabob](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squee%2C+Goblin+Nabob) all over the place. He follows them up with [Words of War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+War) and [Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion), and kills off three straight Bringers. However, those Bringers are a diversion, as I am building up enough mana to get and use [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver). I take out his entire board after Mindslaving him, and take out his hand thanks to a [Goblin Lore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lore).

Enright then drops [Chamber of Manipulation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chamber+of+Manipulation) and another [Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion), and I have to [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) for both [Legacy Weapon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legacy+Weapon) and a Signet to use it! This allows me to start taking out his permanents one-by-one, slowly whittling away at both his card-drawing cards (to take Squee offline), and then his lands. [Sundering Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan) accelerates my kill, and soon I have won the game!


Record: 5-2


One direction I definitely could have taken the deck from the start was towards charge counters and +1/+1 counters, utilizing the [Coretapper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coretapper) plan. As it stands, the Bringer deck is slow, but powerful. Cards like [Sun Droplet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) would work wonders for stabilizing the early game, but I already have a [Sun Droplet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) / counter deck in mind for post-*Shadowmoor* (hint: If you remove a -1/-1 counter from a persist creature using [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit), you can immediately give that same creature a +1/+1 counter. Have [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) die as a 3/2 and come back as a 4/3!), so I didn't want to go that route just yet.


I did, however, really like the pacing on Enright's blue-red deck. It had a lot of good, clean effects, a lot of synergy, and best of all, a pretty tight budget: outside of Squee, all of the cards in the deck could be gotten for a pittance. Let's take a look at his deck:
