### Question 1 of 4

It's Round 1, Game 1. It's the beginning of your third turn, and your board consists of [Veinfire Borderpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veinfire+Borderpost), [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), and [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders)

Your hand consists of [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray), [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders), [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie), [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber), [Fatestitcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatestitcher), and [Resounding Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Thunder).

Your opponent's board consists of [Jungle Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Shrine) and [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (both tapped) and [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) (untapped).

What do you do?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li40_hand1.jpg)  
Well, the very first thing you should do is attack with your [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders). If your opponent blocks your 3/1, you can still [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) their [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) and trample over for 3.

While I personally would not [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) if my [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) were blocked (I'll get to why in a second), the fact is that you incur literally no risk by attacking and have the potential for noticeable rewards by attacking with your trampler before doing anything else. 

If my opponent blocks, I would let the two creatures trade and then play a replacement [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) post-combat. 

I make this play because I'd rather have a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) left in my hand than a [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders). When I draw another land I'll be able to play [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie) and [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) in the same turn. Or I might want to (or have to) wait on using my [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) until my opponent plays a nasty utility creature such as [Drumhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drumhunter).

![Drumhunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drumhunter)  
If my opponent doesn't block, then we've got a slightly more difficult question on our hands.

We could play a second [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders). Then if our opponent plays a creature next turn, we'll probably be able to [Resounding Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Thunder) it and get in for 6 damage, knocking them down to a precarious 11 life points. 

![Resounding Thunder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Resounding+Thunder)  
But that only happens if we draw a land.

If we don't draw a land we'll likely end up attacking with both of our [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders), trading one of them and then having to [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) post-combat to prevent things from getting out of control (or to keep it from just trading with [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) the following turn, which would be an option that your opponent would likely follow if he or she did not have impressive mana-hungry plays to make).

The other option is that we can [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) the Elf post-combat. While we waste a mana to make this play, it immediately takes away a mana source from our opponent, thus equalizing whatever apparent disadvantage we might normally incur when we make plays that do not maximize our mana.

![Druid of the Anima](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Druid+of+the+Anima)  
If my opponent does not block, I would [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) post-combat. I do this because it is both a more desirable play to make if things do not go according to plan over the next couple of turns and because our opponent might be in desperate need of that mana. 

There are some true nightmare situations that can occur when your opponent has four (plus) mana sources to your two. And there are plenty of situations where your opponent literally will be unable to cast spells for a turn (or more) if you eliminate a mana source.

However this is not a strictly dominant play\* as we do give up some of the best-case scenarios that we could get with two [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) in play and some good removal in our hand, but those situations are not easy to come by.

\*In Game Theory, dominance occurs when one strategy/play is better than another strategy/play for one player, no matter how or what that player's opponents may play.

### Question 2 of 4

It's Round 1, Game 1. It's the beginning of your third turn, and your board consists of [Veinfire Borderpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veinfire+Borderpost), [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), and [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders).

Your hand consists of [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray), [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders), [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber), [Dark Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Temper), [Crumbling Necropolis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis), and [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).

Your opponent's board consists of [Jungle Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Shrine) and [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (both tapped) and [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) (untapped).

What do you do?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li40_hand2.jpg)  
Like in the last question, the very first thing we should do is attack with our [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders), because we give up nothing to do so.

![Goblin Deathraiders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Deathraiders)  
If our opponent blocks with [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima), I would let the two creatures trade, then play a replacement [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) and a tapped [Crumbling Necropolis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis).

Like last time, I'd prefer to have the [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) in my hand than a [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders). If we make this play then next turn we will be able to play [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber) or [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) and still have mana to play [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray). (I'd make my decision of whether to play the [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber) or the [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) based on whether or not I drew a land. If I drew a land I'd cast the [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber)s first, as that would allow me to cast [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) and [Dark Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Temper) on the subsequent turn.) 

If our opponent does not block I would play a second [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) and a [Crumbling Necropolis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis) post-combat.

Unlike last time, we already have all the pieces we need for the best-case scenarios to take place. Not only are the best-case scenarios more likely to occur, they're better than they would have been in the previous question.

![Magma Spray](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Magma+Spray)  
If our opponent plays any creature, on our next turn we can [Dark Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Temper) it and [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) that pesky [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) all the while knocking our opponent down to a mere 11. 

In this case, we give our opponent an extra mana to use on his or her next turn, but in doing so we are able to utilize all of our mana this turn and on our next turn.

### Question 3 of 4

It's Round 1, Game 1. It's the beginning of your third turn and your board consists of [Veinfire Borderpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veinfire+Borderpost), [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), and [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie).

Your hand consists of [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray), [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders), [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber), [Dark Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Temper), [Crumbling Necropolis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis), and [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).

Your opponent's board consists of [Jungle Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Shrine) and [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (both tapped) and [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) (untapped).

What do you do?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li40_hand3.jpg)  
Well, the first thing to notice is that unlike in the previous two scenarios it is not completely free to attack with your creature before making any other plays. It is conceivable that you might want to [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) precombat in order to get through for two damage with your [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie).

![Dregscape Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dregscape+Zombie)  
Another thing you might think to yourself in a situation such as this one is "Well, there's no way that I'm going to let the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) live through this turn, so I'm going to [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) it before combat and attack with my 2/1."

While that might sound like a reasonable thought process, it actually is not. 

If you attack, your opponent could very easily decide to block (in fact, the entire reason to [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) precombat is based on the assumption that your opponent might block). And blocking also leads to a dead [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima).

So assuming that you know you want the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) to die this turn, your question of whether to attack or not should be based on how you value your [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie).

Now, I don't believe that the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) has to die here.

Getting back from these thought process questions, the first thing that I will do is to attack with my [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie). 

Then, regardless of whether my opponent blocks or does not block, I will follow my attack up with a post-combat [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) plus [Crumbling Necropolis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis).

![Crumbling Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Crumbling+Necropolis)  
The reasoning for this is exactly the same as in the last question.

While it does not actually affect my play decisions, it is important to pay attention to when you can do something completely for free. Sometimes the best way to improve your ability to spot strictly dominant plays is to contrast them with similarly appealing, but not completely dominant plays.

### Question 4 of 4

Your hand consists of [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray), [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders), [Goblin Outlander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Outlander), [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber), [Resounding Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Thunder), [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), and [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).

Your opponent's board consists of [Jungle Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Shrine) and [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (both tapped) and [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) (untapped).

What do you do?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li40_hand4.jpg)  
I [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) here. 

If we had a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) to replace one of the [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s it might be a different story as I could then cast a [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders) this turn, and on the following turn I could [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) the [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) and play [Goblin Outlander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Outlander).

But as it is, I'm just not prepared to leave my opponent with four mana against my two. 

Even if I did play [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders), then I'd probably have to cast [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) on the following turn to prevent my opponent from getting to a frightening five mana too quickly. 

![Kathari Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kathari+Bomber)  
As it is, by playing [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) now, next turn I'll be able to cast [Kathari Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Bomber) and by the turn after that I'll probably have drawn something impressive that I will want to cast, or I will have drawn a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) to allow me to cast both of the gold two-drops that are currently littering my hand.

By casting [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) here, the one mana that we are "wasting" gets paid back to us as our opponent will be down a mana source. In fact, this play saves us a lot of mana because we will either end up [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray)ing on our following turn (which could leave us down as much as two mana) or we will end up allowing our opponent's [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) to live even longer, producing more and more mana for our opponent and causing us a bigger and bigger net mana loss.

### The Common Thread

All these questions ask us to find the best way that we can use our mana. And that's what your early-game decisions are almost all going to be about. Always think about what your mana can do for you. Sometimes you have a series of very excellent, nearly scripted plays, as we do in questions 2 and 3. Other times you are in undesirable spots where you need to figure out the best way to avoid falling behind, as we are in questions 1 and 4.

At times you will have to make plays that does not allow you to maximize your mana, and that's OK. It's especially OK if giving up a bit of mana efficiency on one turn allows you to truly maximize your mana (and prevent greater inefficiency) on a subsequent turn.

Just because there is less on the board than there will be later in the game doesn't mean that your plays are any less important. Your first couple of turns can make or break your game, if you use your cards to the best of their ability, then you will have a very strong foundation from which to build the rest of your game from.
