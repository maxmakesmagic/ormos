Devotion to Mogis
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 16, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Even though there is no Mogis in this decklist, I think it would do Mogis proud. On one hand, we've got the pure aggression that red is famous for, although this red deck goes a little larger than most. On the other hand, we've got black's disruption in the form of [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), [Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games), and [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return), with some removal thrown in for spice. 

![Fanatic of Mogis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fanatic+of+Mogis) So why is this a winning recipe? Red devotion decks have seen success ever since [Pro Tour *Theros*](/en/events/coverage/dezani-proclaims-allez-les-bleus-pro-tour), even if it's only been the third- or fourth-best devotion deck. [Fanatic of Mogis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fanatic+of+Mogis) goes a long way in closing games, especially if you've got a [Hammer of Purphoros](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer+of+Purphoros) in play to give it haste and provide a steady stream of threats. 

 Clearly, [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) is an issue, which is why [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) is in the sideboard. It also gives you a fighting chance against burn-based red decks, which are often faster. The black splash also gives you [Ultimate Price](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price) for fighting [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon); [Polukranos, World Eater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater); and opposing [Stormbreath Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbreath+Dragon)s. While its slot used to be occupied by the slightly more efficient [Chained to the Rocks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chained+to+the+Rocks), [Ultimate Price](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price) has the upside of not being able to be [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay)ed. 

We've seen all flavors of devotion decks with nearly all flavors of splashes to boot, but this one looks like a winning combination. It fixes its problems with the splash, as any good splash should do, but still maintains its aggressive stance and consistency.
