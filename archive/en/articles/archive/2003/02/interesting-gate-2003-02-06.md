An Interesting Gate
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 6, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar












When Bennie Smith, of "[Single Card Strategy](http://archive.wizards.com/default.asp?x=mtgcom/columnarchive&column=singlecardstrategy)" fame, and I started discussing the *Legions* set, we inevitably compared our lists of favorite cards. One of the cards on my list was [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper). My list, not Bennie's. This isn't a humongous shock, as two deckbuilders are bound to be attracted to a different sample of cards when a new set comes out. In fact, I think the whole of our conversation about [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper)went something along these lines . . .



![Celestial Gatekeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Celestial+Gatekeeper)

**Jay:** I like [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper). Cards that bring creatures directly into play are spiffy.


**Bennie:** Eh. Too expensive. Too narrow.


**Jay:** Yeah. I still like him, though.


As you might expect, I have these sorts of conversations a lot.


I think anyone who looks at [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper)sees this:


* You are paying ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) for a 2/2 flier.
* You get to put two creatures into play when it dies, but not *any* two creatures. They need to be either Clerics or Birds. A quick mental checklist of **Magic** spoilers brings no killer Clerics or Birds to mind.
* [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) has to die first. It can't sacrifice itself, so you need to find another way to kill it.
* It removes itself from the game, so you can't reuse the ability.
* Wait a minute, doesn't [Glory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) cost ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)?

Conclusion: NEXT!


What *I* see, however, is something slightly different:


* Hey, neat, creature reanimation in white. I love creature reanimation in white.
* It's only good on Birds and Clerics? Wow, that's interesting. What kind of Birds and Clerics are out there that could make [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) really annoying?
* It sure is cool in a deck with [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God).

Conclusion: LET'S GET STARTED!


Now, I should emphasize that what I see does not trump what a more sane **Magic** player sees. [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) is awfully expensive for what it does. No spectacular Birds or Clerics really exist. If picking between [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) and [Glory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory), you should pick [Glory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) nine hundred and ninety-nine times out of a thousand. I am not by any means saying that [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) is a powerhouse card.


Instead, I'm saying that [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) is an *interesting* card, and interesting cards get my mind whirring.


Below is an outline of a number of possible ways that I see how a deck built around [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) might take shape. As is my usual tactic after a new set, I'll focus on Standard. Also, I'll spend most of my time talking about the options in white because, well, it's White Week.


### THE GREAT WHITE GATE


First, the basics. Here are some cards that I find showing up again and again in my own Gatekeeper deck creations:


* [Starlit Sanctum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starlit+Sanctum)

Laugh all you want, but white doesn't have very reliable ways to sacrifice its own creatures. [Starlit Sanctum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starlit+Sanctum) lets you ensure that [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) will bring creatures from your graveyard to play on your terms rather than your opponent's.


[Animal Boneyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animal+Boneyard) plays a similar role if you need extra insurance. If your deck is swimming in mana, I suppose [Doom Cannon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Cannon) can work too.

 * [Breath of Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breath+of+Life)

If you like your Birds and Clerics enough to bring them back into play, you might as well maximize your chances of doing so. [Breath of Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breath+of+Life) is always fun to use (somehow more than [Zombify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify), I think, simply because it's white) and duplicates the Gatekeeper's ability nicely.


* [Disenchant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)

All decks need to worry about opposing enchantments and/or artifacts. I'm putting [Disenchant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant) here as a friendly reminder.


* [Marble Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marble+Diamond)

To be honest, it doesn't bother me too much that [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) costs five mana to cast. The reason I'm okay with is that if it were too fast, I would need to find ways ([Tireless Tribe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe), [Patrol Hound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound), [Millikin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millikin), [Hypochondria](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypochondria)) to quickly get creatures into my graveyard. On the other hand, I can cast my Birds and Clerics early, wait for them to die then cast [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) to clean up the mess. In these cases I find waiting for the Gatekeeper on turn 5 or later to be perfectly acceptable.


If, however, you want to mess around with a rapidly filling graveyard and a quick Gatekeeper—or a deck with a lot of high-end creatures—then you need mana acceleration. In white, that means [Marble Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marble+Diamond) (and a hefty dose of prayer).


* [Pariah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pariah)

Another sneaky way to get your [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) killed. [Pariah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pariah) doesn't give you as much control as something like [Animal Boneyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animal+Boneyard), but it can be a lot more frustrating for your opponent. As an added bonus, [Pariah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pariah) acts as pretty decent white creature removal.


* [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God)

Speaking of creature removal . . . play [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) while you have [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) out and you are suddenly up two creatures to zero. Trust me, that's a nice situation in which to find yourself, measly Birds and Clerics or not. Of course you can always use things like [Kirtar's Wrath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Wrath) and [Akroma's Vengeance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Vengeance), too.


Once the basics are out of the way, it's time to really make an important decision: Clerics or Birds? Which kind of creature do you want the Gatekeeper to fetch out of your graveyard?


### THE HOLY GATE


I think probably the most obvious answer is Clerics. [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary) is a creature that is a real pain in the tuchus to kill. Once he's dead, very few opponents want to see him come back into play with a second Cleric sidekick (especially if the sidekick happens to be [Akroma's Devoted](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Devoted)). In addition, some Clerics, like [Nova Cleric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nova+Cleric) and [Benevolent Bodyguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benevolent+Bodyguard), sacrifice themselves for good effect, while others, like [Ancestor's Prophet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestor%27s+Prophet), [Nomad Mythmaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Mythmaker), [True Believer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer), and [Stern Judge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stern+Judge), often have big targets on their backs. So you're bound to have at least two worthy Clerics in your graveyard, ready for the [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) to set them free.


An interesting feature in white Clerics is their absolute love of gaining life. Since [Test of Endurance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Test+of+Endurance) remains in Standard, it's kind of fun to try a deck like the following:

### THE AVIARY


Birds are a bit trickier, but certainly no less interesting. The very presence of [Soulcatchers' Aerie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcatchers%27+Aerie) and [Battle Screech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Screech) mean that monowhite Bird decks can flex some feathery muscles. Although most of the Aven compete with [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) for the most expensive card in your deck, there are more than enough reasonably costed ones to make a deck.

### THE HOLY AVIARY OF THE IVORY NIMBUS


Of course you may be staring at [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) and wondering why you have to choose between a dedicated-Cleric deck and a dedicated-Bird deck. Other than a thematic appreciation of "tribal" decks and the use of nifty cards like [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary) and [Soulcatchers' Aerie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcatchers%27+Aerie), you don't. Feel free to mix and match beaks and robes and wings and incense . . . after all, the Gatekeeper itself can't decide between the two.


I've really only begun to scratch the surface of possibilities here. Fetch [Celestial Gatekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Gatekeeper) back from its own death with [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) while rescuing your [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). Make a black-white Birds deck with [Screeching Buzzard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard). Or cycle Clerics and Birds into your graveyard before reanimating them. Just to show you how crazy "crazy" can get . . .
