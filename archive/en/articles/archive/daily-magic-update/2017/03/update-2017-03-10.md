After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [Should Jace Be Modern-Legal?](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/34707_Should-Jace-Be-Modern-Legal.html) | StarCityGames.com | Shaheen Soorani

There have been a number of articles on the upcoming banned and restricted announcement (coming Monday), with opinions varying all over the place. Really, I thought about including a few, but there's one specific question most of these articles seem to touch on, even if they don't agree—should [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) be un-banned in Modern?


Shaheen Soorani has his take on it (I'll give you one guess where he comes down), but he also lays out the Modern landscape, the arguments against the move, and where and how Jace could fit in.


1. [Unlicensed Disintegration and Blue-Red Improvise](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/unlicensed-disintegration/) | Top Level Podcast | Mike Flores and Patrick Chapin

Patrick thinks we should ban—[Unlicensed Disintegration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration)? Huh. Guess you'll need to listen to find out why. Also check out the Blue-Red Improvise deck that Chapin was *this* close to playing at [Pro Tour *Aether Revolt*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptaer). You can't see how far I'm gesturing when I write *this*, but it's pretty darn close.


1. [Ancestral Recall #2—Who's the Beatdown?](http://manadeprived.com/ancestral-recall-2-whos-beatdown/) | Mana Deprived | Roman Fusco and Mike Flores

All Mike Flores all the time! The "Who's the Beatdown" author and Fusco discuss, um, "Who's the Beatdown." Seems apt.


1. [The Entire *Kaladesh*/*Aether Revolt* Story/Lore Summary for *Magic: The Gathering*: Part 2!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uoEfYuWI92U&t=3s) | The Mana Source | Wedge

As we round the final corners to *Amonkhet* (stories pick back up on March 29), now's a good time to catch up on the *Kaladesh*/*Aether Revolt* story. Wedge walks you through all the happenings, with this video picking up where *Kaladesh* left off.

Standard is in a constant churn right now, as decks ebb and flow to adapt to the latest flow or ebb. Friedman runs through some of those changes with an eye to figuring out what might be next.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Joe McMahan's Blue-Red Zombies](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=112131)

This deck has so many names. Blue-Red Emerge (thanks to [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend)), Blue-Red Visions (thanks to [Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions)) or "That deck that Raphaël Lévy keeps playing." Whatever you call it, the deck is terminally cool.


This is the only deck in Standard looking to actively discard all the things. [Advanced Stitchwing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advanced+Stitchwing), [Stitchwing Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitchwing+Skaab), [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe), [Cathartic Reunion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathartic+Reunion), and [Tormenting Voice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormenting+Voice) all require you to discard at some point. They key is, the deck has a lot of cards that don't mind being discarded—[Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return), [Prized Amalgam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prized+Amalgam), and [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) are first among them, and when they're in the graveyard, they give you really good control over what the board looks like at any given time.


The key, however, is [Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions), which is part win condition, part fuel for all this discard. It keeps your hand full, even with a graveyard full of Stitchwings, and it keeps your opponent under pressure.
