The Grace of Angels
===================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on July 22, 2013 










With ***Magic** 2014* having just hit shelves this weekend, it's prime deck-building season. Players across the globe are experimenting with the new cards, trying to discover the next big thing. Of course, here at the Lab, experiments are our specialty, and I've suspended a couple of the set's rares in a plasmatic oxidative solution for further testing. My experiments yielded some intriguing results, and I'm happy to share them with you today. 

![](http://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl257_elitearcanist.jpg%20)  
One-Trick Arcanist
[Elite Arcanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Arcanist) follows in the footsteps of [Soul Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Foundry) and [Prototype Portal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prototype+Portal), letting you exile a card from your hand and then cast it every turn by paying its converted mana cost. The Arcanist, however, works with instants. This brings to mind a different imprint card: [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter). The Scepter has historically been used with cards like [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant) to prevent the opponent from doing much of anything, and [Elite Arcanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Arcanist) can be used in the same way. Although I also considered using [Turnabout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turnabout) and an array of mana-producing creatures to produce infinite mana, in the end, the fact that [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) is also in M14 and the ability to use both the Scepter and the Arcanist pushed me in the other direction. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
[Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) and Orim's chant will prevent your opponent from casting any spell that's not an instant, and if you pay the kicker cost, [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant) will also prevent your opponent's creatures from attacking you. Since [Elite Arcanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Arcanist) and [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) cast a copy of the exiled card, you'll be able to pay the extra white mana each time you activate them. 

[Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) takes a different route from the other two cards, simply preventing you from losing the game. Your life total will stay at 1 while your opponent bashes away uselessly. Of course, all of these cards have one weakness: instants. If your opponent can cast spells on your turn, or with the Arcanist's ability on the stack, you might be in trouble. I'll throw in some [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) to protect the Arcanist from instant-speed removal, and [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb) will protect your face from [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 The win condition for this deck is going to be your opponent running out of cards, but there's no harm in helping to speed that along. [Jace, Memory Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept) can get rid of ten cards every turn to accelerate you toward victory. [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) can be used to find [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter), a pair of [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) for the Arcanist, or [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb), and [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) will help you deal with... well, pretty much anything. It can stop your opponent from progressing too far before you get the lock set up or protect you from instants.

Deathbringer of Thune
[Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) has been the center of much discussion with regards to its playability in Standard. This creature can certainly create a lot of potential value, but it might surprise you to find out it has combo potential as well. The key is finding a way to translate putting a +1/+1 counter on each of your creatures into gaining life. 

 The conduit I found for making this happen was [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion). If you give it lifelink, you can remove a counter to deal 1 damage to your opponent, which causes you to gain 1 life, which triggers [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune), putting a new counter on the [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion). [Deathbringer Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) serves as backup, with the small drawback of not entering the battlefield with a +1/+1 counter ready to go. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 To give the creature lifelink, I included a few different cards. [Basilisk Collar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) requires the most mana, but it grants deathtouch as well, allowing [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) to shoot down a couple creatures if you don't have [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) yet. When equipped to [Deathbringer Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar), it allows you to kill all of your opponent's creatures, since the Thoctar gets a new counter whenever something dies. 

[Nearheath Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nearheath+Pilgrim) is vulnerable to removal, but doesn't require any mana the turn you want to use it. By spending the two mana to cast it beforehand, you can combo off as soon as you get the other pieces on board. [Lifelink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifelink) is the straightforward option, giving you a no-frills way to complete the combo for just one mana. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 To tide you over until you get the combo together, I'll include [Mephidross Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire), which can team up with [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) or [Deathbringer Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) to gun down all your opponent's creatures. You can remove a counter from [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) to deal 1 damage to a creature, and [Mephidross Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire) will trigger, giving it a new counter with which to deal 1 more damage, and so on. 

[Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) will allow you to search for whichever piece of the combo you're missing. If you're missing more than one, remember that you have ten cards you could draw to fill the lifelink part, eight that deal the damage, and only four [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune). It's best to tutor up the one you're less likely to draw. 

[Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) can get rid of problem permanents, either creatures that are in danger of killing you, or things that can stop you from killing your opponent with your combo. Finally, [Rebuff the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuff+the+Wicked) can protect your creatures from removal, ensuring that your infinite damage chain won't be cut short by a timely [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade).

Creatures and Spells
We're gearing up for an epic battle here in the arena, with two diametrically opposed decks battling it out for the title. Will the instant-based deck win the day, or will the creatures prove victorious? Let's head down into the pits and watch the carnage unfold.

**Game 1**

 Silence opened up with a land, and Life amp; Death cast [Basilisk Collar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) to get things started. Silence cast [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves), and Life amp; Death played a land and passed the turn. Silence cast [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter), imprinting [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace). Life amp; Death once again played a land and passed the turn, and Silence passed back with no play. Life amp; Death cast [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), and Silence played a land and passed. Life amp; Death cast [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune), and Silence ended the turn with no play. 

 Life amp; Death cast [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion), but Silence used [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) to stop it. [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) attacked for 3, putting a counter on itself. Silence cast [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate), getting [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb), and passed the turn. Life amp; Death cast [Deathbringer Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) and enchanted it with [Lifelink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifelink). [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) attacked for 4, and Silence cast [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) before the Angel put a counter on the Thoctar. Life amp; Death dropped Silence to 1 life before ending the turn. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 Silence cast [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb), and [Deathbringer Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) removed a counter for 1 damage in response. Silence activated [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter), and without another counter to remove, Life amp; Death was forced to let it resolve. Silence passed the turn, and [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) attacked for the win. Silence was prepared with an [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) from hand to stop it, however. Life amp; Death cast [Mephidross Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire), and used the [Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) to ping itself until all three creatures were arbitrarily large thanks to [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune). 

 Silence cast [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate), searching up another copy of [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) and casting it, imprinting [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant). Life amp; Death cast [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), targeting [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb). Silence activated [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) in response, and after [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) resolved, the Orb was exiled. Life amp; Death passed the turn, then during Silence's upkeep, removed a counter from the [Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) to deal 1 damage. Silence activated [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) in response, but with the Scepter's ability on the stack, Life amp; Death used the [Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) again for the kill. 

**Game 2**

 Silence started off with a land, and Life amp; Death cast [Basilisk Collar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) on turn one again. Silence cast [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter), imprinting [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace), and passed the turn. Life amp; Death played a land and passed. Silence cast [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate), finding a [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb). Life amp; Death played a land and passed the turn, and Silence cast [Elite Arcanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Arcanist), imprinting the deck's [namesake card](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=silence). 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 Life amp; Death cast [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), exiling [Elite Arcanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Arcanist), and Silence cast [Jace, Memory Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept), milling ten cards. Life amp; Death cast [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) and passed the turn. Jace got rid of another ten cards, and Silence cast [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb). Life amp; Death cast [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) to exile [Jace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept) and passed the turn. Silence played a land and passed back. Life amp; Death cast [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune), and Silence cast [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves). 

[Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) hit the battlefield, and [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) attacked, putting an extra +1/+1 counter on it. However, Silence cast a second [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter), imprinting [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant). With its combo one card short of completion, and without the ability to cast spells or attack, Life amp; Death conceded. 

**Game 3**

 Both sides started off with lands until Silence cast lightning Greaves on turn two. Life amp; Death played a land and passed the turn, as did Silence. Life amp; Death cast [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), and Silence cast [Elite Arcanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Arcanist), exiling [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) and equipping the Arcanist with [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves). Life amp; Death's [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) could only hit the Greaves, and with nothing on board and no chance of casting spells, Life amp; Death conceded.
