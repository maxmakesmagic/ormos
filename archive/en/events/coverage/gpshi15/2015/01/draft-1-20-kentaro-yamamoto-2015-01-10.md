DRAFT 1: (20) KENTARO YAMAMOTO
==============================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX SHIZUOKA 2015](/en/events/coverage/gpshi15)
 on January 10, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett










In Japan, Kentaro Yamamoto has long been a player with a strong reputation. However, international recognition eluded him. All that changed with this year's World Championship. Yamamoto took on the game's elite and proved that he belonged among them with a Top 4 finish. Now that 2015 is in full swing he's looking to build on that success. His 8-1 performance yesterday gave him a good position to make a run at the Top 8 here at Shizuoka.



For his first draft he found himself at a table without other big names, already a good sign. His opening pack offered him the choice between powerhouse rare [Necropolis Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropolis+Fiend), strong uncommons in [Winterflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winterflame) and [Mardu Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mardu+Charm), and green's #1 common [Savage Punch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Punch). “It was an easy pick,” he said afterwards, “Necropolis Fiend is very powerful and not hard to enable.” Unfortunately there was no similarly easy follow-up. The only black card offered was [Disowned Ancestor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disowned+Ancestor). Yamamoto stuck to his guns and took it, despite the presence of [Master the Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+the+Way) in the pack. “Yes, [Master the Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+the+Way) is a very strong card, but I'd much rather stay black than spread out my picks early.” Not even the presence of [Warden of the Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warden+of+the+Eye) fourth pick could dissuade him.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpshi15/draft1_yamamoto2.jpg)  



As the pack wound down he collected a number of unexciting green and black creatures. “The card quality in this draft was very low, and not just for me. After pack 1 I was mostly interested in getting cards like [Scout the Borders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scout+the+Borders), [Bitter Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Revelation) and [Rakshasa's Secret](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakshasa%27s+Secret) to enable delve.” It paid off better than Pack 1, giving him strong cards like [Heir of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+the+Wilds), Pinewalker, [Sultai Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sultai+Scavenger) and [Woolly Loxodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woolly+Loxodon). It looked like he would miss out on enablers for another pack, but then found both [Scout the Borders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scout+the+Borders) and [Bitter Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Revelation) waiting for him eighth pick. He picked [Scout the Borders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scout+the+Borders), explaining “The difference between three and four mana is big, and [Scout the Borders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scout+the+Borders) gives more cards for delve.” His deck to this point was solidly black-green. He had a couple of blue spells but no fixing for them.



The final pack started out strong with [Incremental Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incremental+Growth), and led right in to [Sultai Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sultai+Scavenger) and [Tuskguard Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tuskguard+Captain). Yamamoto passed on a [Smoke Teller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smoke+Teller) for [Rakshasa's Secret](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakshasa%27s+Secret), then snagged an [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm) to go with the [Blossoming Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Sands) he'd gotten in pack 2. The strong removal spell was just what his deck was lacking. He also collected a pair of [Bitter Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Revelation)s and wound up playing both. I asked him if he was concerned to be playing so many delve spells in his deck. He shook his head. “Sultai Scavengers are fine to cast even at full cost. [Hooting Mandrills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hooting+Mandrills) too. So it's really just the [Shambling Attendants](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambling+Attendants) and the [Necropolis Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropolis+Fiend) that will need lots of cards in the graveyard.” Overall he wound up a couple playables short and was forced to maindeck a [Despise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Despise). This draft was an exercise in making the best of a bad situation. Yamamoto wound up with a functional deck with a solid plan. He was confident he could manage a 2-1 record and stay on-pace for a Top 8 finish.
