Brewing with Born of the Gods
=============================



 Posted in **Perilous Research**
 on January 30, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






It's that time again! The *Born of the Gods* Prerelease is just a few days away. We should be sure to reserve a spot at our local Prerelease if we haven't had the opportunity yet. These events have limited space and it's something we don't want to miss. The new set is full of hits and everyone seems to be bursting at the seams to play with cards like [Searing Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blood); [Xenagos, God of Revels](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+God+of+Revels); [Brimaz, King of Oreskos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos); [Bile Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight); [Courser of Kruphix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix); and [Drown in Sorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow). Today, we'll be taking a look at some of the most exciting *Born of the Gods* previews, discussing their Constructed applications, and building decks that fully utilize their power. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
[Brimaz, King of Oreskos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos) is one of the most powerful cards we've seen in quite a while. We're sure there's a streamlined strategy that uses Brimaz to win the game as fast as possible, but we'd like to stretch the card to its limit. [Bile Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight) and [Drown in Sorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow) are sure to make the Black Devotion strategy and more controlling, removal-based control decks into major players. Aggressive creature strategies will be forced to adapt. It's no longer reasonable to play an army of Humans that dies to [Drown in Sorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow) before it has an opportunity to apply any real pressure on the opponent. Creatures with 3 and 4 toughness become necessary, especially in post-boarded games. 

 What has 4 toughness and wins games by itself? [Archangel of Thune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune). [Archangel of Thune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) is a card that keeps us out of range from the Red decks while being resistant to [Drown in Sorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow) and [Bile Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight). We can play enough creatures to ensure that our Angel isn't dealt with via [Devour Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh). I'm thinking that green-white has a lot of potential here. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 How do we beat a [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat)? It's unfortunate, but we have no way to deal with an army of Rats. However, we can beat [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) in a straight-up fight a huge portion of the time. [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice) in conjunction with [Archangel of Thune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) lets our team grow at a faster rate than the Rats and all the life we'll be passively accruing can put us out of range from even the largest [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel) triggers. 

[Brimaz, King of Oreskos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos) is particularly filthy with the Trostani/Archangel combo. Whenever Brimaz attacks or blocks we'll be getting a guy, gaining life, growing our entire team, and presumably destroying our opponent. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
[Soldier of the Pantheon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soldier+of+the+Pantheon) also interacts favorably here. Opponents will be casting multicolor spells and, if we have an Archangel on the battlefield, we'll be growing our team as a result. 

 This deck will obviously be a monster against aggressive strategies. The combination of [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and a lot of 3 and 4 toughness will mean that Black Devotion strategies will have a lot of trouble keeping up and using their cards effectively.

This Selesnya deck has a lot to offer, especially if the format becomes a place where everyone wants to be attacking. That may not be the case, though. It's entirely possible that we'll be facing off against decks that are rife with removal spells.

[Ephara, God of the Polis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephara%2C+God+of+the+Polis) is another card that's worth exploring. This card generates card advantage (the overarching strategy that involves trading cards for more cards) passively and conveniently becomes a game-winning creature once all the card advantage has pulled us ahead enough. There are a few different ways we can go about building a deck with [Ephara, God of the Polis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephara%2C+God+of+the+Polis): We can make a reasonable aggressive deck that pounds away at the opponent's life total while never running out of gas thanks to the God. We could also explore the possibility of playing a godly top-end strategy that uses [Heliod, God of the Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%2C+God+of+the+Sun) to ensure that we can always draw cards and advance our board state with [Ephara, God of the Polis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephara%2C+God+of+the+Polis). 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

  
 Cards like [Precinct Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) and [Brimaz, King of Oreskos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos) let us put creatures into play without having to resolve a spell. This means we'll be able to draw extra cards with [Ephara, God of the Polis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephara%2C+God+of+the+Polis) and keep the gas flowing indefinitely. [Precinct Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) and [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) will likely be quintessential pieces of all decks that try to abuse [Ephara, God of the Polis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephara%2C+God+of+the+Polis). 

[Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) is one of the most powerful cards in pre-*Born of the Gods* Standard. The card deals with packs of [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat)s, Gods, Planeswalkers, and most of the creatures that are currently played. [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) gives us two devotion for Ephara and will likely be a four-of in any deck that plays the God.

 This deck has a lot going for it. We can play an aggressive game and close the deal with [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike) and [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm). We can also play a more controlling strategy against the most aggressive decks where all of our removal, in conjunction with [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), should be enough to make those matchups very good. [Ephara, God of the Polis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephara%2C+God+of+the+Polis) works very well in a deck with burn spells. We don't need to fire them off when we draw them; we can lull our opponent into a false sense of security by holding a few burn spells and firing off a huge blast between his or her end step and our turn when our opponent taps all of his or her mana. 

 This deck also gets to play with an infinite combo. We can use [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) and [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) to give one of our [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)s lifelink and indestructible when it's about to take damage. Then we can repeatedly target the [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) with its own trigger and gain millions, billions, or trillions of life.

There's definitely a wealth of homes for this indiscriminate removal spell, but the most obvious to me is the deck I played at [Pro Tour *Theros*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptths13/welcome).
