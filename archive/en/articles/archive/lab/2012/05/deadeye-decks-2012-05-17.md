Deadeye Decks
=============

 on May 17, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova












Hello and welcome back to the Lab. At the end of last week's article, I posed a single-card challenge of sorts, in the spine-tingling form of [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator), a new card that caught my eye as a Johnny card to watch. After sifting through a thudding amount of emails, I've tried to scrape the coolest ideas out of the bunch. Thanks to all who submitted! (Ideally, this prompt only whetted your appetite for emailing me about cool deck ideas.)


### Navigation Tips


The general consensus was that [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) had insane interactions with "enters the battlefield" creatures. So many of these creatures exist, and likewise, so many of these effects can be abused with the Navigator. Many of you smelled infinity, and it's true that [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) is proficient at tipping 8s over. We'll build up to those, though, and begin with some more fluid, synergistic (and Standard legal) builds.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl195_dea.jpg)[Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) | Art by [Tomasz Jedruszek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Tomasz+Jedruszek%22%5D)


Here's Andrew Anderson's flicker-heavy build. He wrote:



> 
> Almost every creature in here enjoys being flickered, or enjoys their friends being flickered. Add [Tamiyo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage) for some extra control, and end-of-turn [Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing), and you've got an engine to blink everything you have as much as you want. On a side note, I've discovered that you need to make sure to have enough mana to blink Deadeye at least once after you cast him. Otherwise, he's the opponent's target, so you have to protect him. And [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) just loves him!
> 
> 
>

Flicker-heavy decks were quite popular. Robbi Ramirez sent in a powerful-looking one. Robbi wrote:



> 
> The plan here is obvious: ramp into Deadeye and [Massacre Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm) until you can use the combo to take down your opponent's board, then your opponent.
> 
> 
> [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) provides both ramp and an ETB ability. The other Titans provide more of the latter in a pinch. [Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) supplements [Massacre Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm)'s ability and also triggers when your [Viridian Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Emissary)s pop. [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) is a good backup plan with the smaller creatures, especially Birds, and also untaps your land to supplement the expensive Plan A.
>

Will Cooper sent in a wonderfully zany decklist that showcases the infinite potential with [Morselhoarder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder). As he wrote:



> 
> The first thing I thought of was to pair [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) with [Morselhoarder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder)—remove the two counters from the [Morselhoarder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder) to pay the cost of the flicker ability, then when it comes back you can re-pair it with the Navigator and repeat ad nauseam. [Smokebraider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) can do the same thing, if you have a way to give it haste every time it comes back; I ended up choosing [Urabrask the Hidden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urabrask+the+Hidden), [Ashling's Prerogative](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashling%27s+Prerogative) (since most of my important creatures ended up having even-numbered converted mana cost), and [Yet Another Æther Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yet+Another+%C3%86ther+Vortex). Unfortunately, creatures that appear on top of your library with the Vortex don't trigger enters-the-battlefield abilities (see the [*Unhinged* FAQTIWDAWCC](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/article.aspx?x=magic/faq/unhinged)) but they can still pair with things that enter the battlefield in the normal way.
> 
> 
> 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl195_cha.jpg)[Champion of Lambholt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Lambholt) | Art by [Christopher Moeller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Christopher+Moeller%22%5D)



> 
> So what to do with all this flickering? [Ceaseless Searblades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ceaseless+Searblades) and [Champion of Lambholt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Lambholt) will get pumped up to ridiculous sizes (the latter becoming unblockable in the process), or you can just kill things with [Warstorm Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warstorm+Surge).
> 
> 
> 
>

> 
> How to find what we want?
> 
> 
> [Flamekin Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger) is good with all the Elementals in the deck.
> 
> 
> [Wild Pair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair) is surprisingly versatile here: [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) will find [Morselhoarder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder); while [Morselhoarder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder) can find Navigator, Searblades, or Urabrask (remove an appropriate number of -1/-1 counters in response to the [Wild Pair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair) trigger); and [Champion of Lambholt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Lambholt), [Flamekin Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger), and [Smokebraider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) can all find each other.
> 
> 
> ["Ach! Hans, Run!"](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%22Ach%21+Hans%2C+Run%21%22) is a fun way to get whatever we need—and [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) lets us keep it past the end of the turn.
> 
> 
> We have a whole lot of cards with converted mana cost 6, so [Ethereal Usher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Usher) seems good, and it can also help us connect with [Ceaseless Searblades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ceaseless+Searblades) if necessary.
> 
> 
> [Cream of the Crop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cream+of+the+Crop) is useful once we start getting our more expensive creatures, especially if we also have [Yet Another Æther Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yet+Another+%C3%86ther+Vortex) in play.
> 
> 
> I think the deck wants a little more mana acceleration; [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) is cheap, we can use it to find a few things via [Wild Pair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair), and it can produce various colors of mana if we pair it with the *Ravnica* dual lands.
>

Finally, Michael Jeffry ends our day with a lovely red and blue combo deck, featuring [Priest of Urabrask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Priest+of+Urabrask) as the key piece. He wrote:



> 
> The required card was [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) with it's delicious ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) blinking ability, so the key was to find a way to make ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) by blinking a creature. [Priest of Urabrask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Priest+of+Urabrask), a modern version of Priest of Gix, makes ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) upon entering the battlefield, but we need a way to make ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) to go infinite. [Gemstone Array](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemstone+Array) can easily fix the color issues for us while allowing us to save up for our pricey [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator).
> 
> 
>

> So how the combo works: We have [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) on the field then cast [Priest of Urabrask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Priest+of+Urabrask) to add ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) to the mana pool and pair it with the Navigator. Spend ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) to put one counter on [Gemstone Array](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemstone+Array), remove the counter to add one ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) and spend the ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) to blink the [Priest of Urabrask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Priest+of+Urabrask), and, viola! We're back with ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) to do it all over again.
> 
> 
> Then all we have to do is combine this engine with some enter-the-battlefield trigger and go for infinite damage. [Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) is an old classic but I think the new [Vigilante Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigilante+Justice) would be more fun, with [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) as a backup win condition. [Emrakul's Hatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%27s+Hatcher) can stand in for [Priest of Urabrask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Priest+of+Urabrask) if you have [Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) or [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker), as well generating a ton of blocking bodies to stall until you get the combo
> 
> 
> [Draining Whelk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk) is a good Plan B, allowing you to counter any spell your opponent plays for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif). [Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mist+Raven), Man-o'-War, and [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) can all bounce creatures for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) as well as serve as blockers, but I think [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) would go best with [Vigilante Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigilante+Justice) around. [Ghitu Slinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Slinger) is a flickerable burn creature and fits well with the Human theme we got going on. [Ghostly Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker) is a shoo-in with all the enter-the-battlefield triggers we have. [Elgaud Shieldmate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elgaud+Shieldmate) is a fun singleton that can help protect your [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) until [Priest of Urabrask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Priest+of+Urabrask) shows up or just throw a monkey wrench into the plans of a deck with lots of spot removal. [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) do a good job of greasing the wheels of any combo deck and allows us to cheat on our mana.
