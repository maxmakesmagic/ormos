The Decks of Gods
=================



 Posted in **Command Tower**
 on October 3, 2013 










I hope all the Greek food isn't giving your Commander decks indigestion.

  
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ct/ct267_oink.jpg)[Curse of the Swine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+the+Swine) | Art by [James Ryman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22James%20Ryman%22%5D)

 The release of new sets is one of the most exciting moments to be a **Magic** player. Heads are filled with dreams of [Xenagos, the Reveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+the+Reveler) alongside [Wort, Boggart Auntie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+Boggart+Auntie), or what [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx) can do in a monocolored deck. The new tools that will fill decks, like [Curse of the Swine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+the+Swine) as great blue removal, are easy to discuss simply because *they never existed before*. 

In fact, I could spend an entire article just pointing out the new cards I love most, and why:

* [Nighthowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nighthowler) is finally a way to get two [Lhurgoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lhurgoyf)s for the price of one, and I really like [Lhurgoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lhurgoyf).
* [Fleecemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleecemane+Lion) and [Pharika's Mender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%27s+Mender) are useful tools that fit right into the strategies of their respective colors.
* [Reaper of the Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+of+the+Wilds) seems custom-created to get me to play [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) [yet again](https://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/182).
* [The cycle of Temples](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;name=+%5Btemple%5D+%5Bof%5Damp;set=+%5B%22Theros%22%5D) upgrade one of my favorite monocolored lands ([New Benalia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=New+Benalia)) for a more multicolored world.
* As a lover of strange-but-surprisingly-powerful Equipment, [Prowler's Helm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prowler%27s+Helm) is my new [Trailblazer's Boots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trailblazer%27s+Boots).
* [Burnished Hart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burnished+Hart) is my preferred style of mana ramping for monocolored decks (putting the lands directly into play).
* There's a [killer cool whip](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/rc/264) and a [new spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=new+spear) for my white token decks.

 Listing sweet cards isn't as exciting as playing **Magic**, however, particularly in Commander. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ct/ct267_wits.jpg)[Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=83133) | Art by [Edward P. Beard, Jr.](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Edward%20P.%20Beard,%20Jr.%22%5D)

 Within the trove of all things new there are always new standouts among the legendary in the set. These new commanders are where many Commander players start their journey. While you may be a grizzled veteran of multiplayer and Commander like me, it's clear from the feedback *the rest of you* share that newer sets are precisely where Commander begins. 

 Those new legendary creatures are the shining points of Commander in *Theros*. We've already [looked at a few](https://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ct/265) but there were plenty more to show off. Fortunately, you were up for the challenge. 

 An Enchanted Evening 
 While my love for [Erebos, God of the Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erebos%2C+God+of+the+Dead) is an easy sell, it isn't alone in *Theros*'s pantheon of overlords. Each God represents different attributes of **Magic**, as gods are apt to do, and playing with the different themes is one of the ways new commanders are most exciting. 

 It is any surprise then that Andrew shared this [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) deck?

>  Rather than make the best use of Thassa to win the game, I opted to make the most flavorful sea god deck I could. I obviously included all the new *Theros* cards related to Thassa herself, such as [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) and [Thassa's Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Emissary). From there, I let [Shipbreaker Kraken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shipbreaker+Kraken) lead the way for the rest of the sea monster ensemble. A handful of Merfolk and a little tribal support for them came next, as they are the wardens of the seas after all. I included a diversity of sea critters like [Spiny Starfish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiny+Starfish) and [Giant Oyster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Oyster) to showcase **Magic**'s diversity of aquatic life. Note that many of the creatures have islandwalk, so I included a suite of cards to make my opponents' lands into Islands. Finally, the rest of the spells in the deck are representative to oceanic forces that Thassa would control.

When I considered forging my own deck around Purphoros, this is almost exactly what I had in mind:

* Lay down our indestructible God.
* Pop out as many tokens as we can, as quickly as we can.
* If the tokens get wiped out, use a few other ways to generate the final points of burn needed.

 Raymond's plan was a common thread across the dozen or so Purphoros decks, and I'm excited to hear so many of you will be making [monocolored Commander happen](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ct/260). (Generally speaking, I'm also a fan of [Commander decks that make life totals go boom](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/225). [Purphoros, God of the Forge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purphoros%2C+God+of+the+Forge) delivers here, too.) 

Of course, not every Commander deck that goes after life totals is based around aggressive red and black cards. You can also dig into blue and white to strike, as Geoff shared:


> [Daxos of Meletis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daxos+of+Meletis) is one of the most interesting legendary creatures from *Theros*. This deck focuses around Daxos's first ability of not being able to be blocked by creatures with power with 3 or greater. This allows Daxos to swing under the defenses of most Commander decks, as they tend to focus on large late-game creatures and commanders. 
> 
>  The second ability, while nice, isn't as important. The theme of this deck is to swing early, swing hard, and do lots of commander damage. As a result, this deck focuses on the exalted mechanic, Equipment, and Auras: exalted to make Daxos large enough to stomp anything small that gets in the way, and Auras and Equipment to pump Daxos, and apply abilities, particularly "can't be blocked." Who doesn't like unblockable commander damage, and with [Aqueous Form](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aqueous+Form) it only costs ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif). 
> 
>  For Daxos, I like the [Ordeal of Heliod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordeal+of+Heliod) and Thassa because they provide a small permanent boost with +1/+1 counters plus a nifty effect that rewards you for swinging away. Another great Aura is [Gift of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Immortality). While not extra damage, it does allow Daxos to survive longer by making removal half as effective.

When I think of white-blue Commander decks, I think about "flickering" effects that make the most of whatever valuable creatures are hanging around. But in Limited, it's all about "White-Blue Skies"-type aggressive decks that slip past defenses and out-tempo opponents.

 Geoff's deck is the Commander equivalent of the Limited version of white-blue, pushing an evasive creature across the battlefield to cut down any enemies. Suiting up [Daxos of Meletis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daxos+of+Meletis) makes all the sense in the world.
