General Information
-------------------


24 of the world’s best players and the two best teams of the 2016-17 season will compete at the Castle at Park Plaza in Boston, Massachusetts featuring three days, multiple formats, and a grand total of $350,000 being awarded.


The 24 competitors in the *Magic: The Gathering* World Championship main event will compete from October 6-8 across 14 rounds of Swiss and a Top 4 playoff for their share of a $300,000 prize pool, with a cool **$100,000** going to the winner.


The Top two teams from the 2016-17 Pro Tour Team Series will compete on Sunday for their share of a little over $50,000 and the title of 2016-17 Team Series Champion.

*The* [Magic: The Gathering Tournament Rules](http://wpn.wizards.com/en/document/magic-the-gathering-tournament-rules) *and* [*Infraction & Penalty Guide*](http://wpn.wizards.com/en/document/magic-infraction-procedure-guide) *are in effect for this event unless specifically overruled by information in this Format document.*


Day One will consist of 3 rounds of *Ixalan* Booster Draft, followed by 4 rounds of Standard Constructed.


Day Two will consist of 3 rounds of *Ixalan* Booster Draft, followed by 4 rounds of Standard Constructed.


Day Three will consist of two parts: The Team Series Championship, and the Top 4 Playoff.


The Team Series Championship will be *Ixalan* Team Sealed.


The Top 4 Playoff will be Standard Constructed.




For the World Championship:


* 14 Swiss Rounds total, 60 minutes per round.
* When time is called, the active player will finish the turn and five additional turns will be taken.
* Stalling and slow play rules will be strictly enforced during the tournament.


* All players may participate in all 14 Swiss rounds on Friday and Saturday’s competition.
* The Top 4 players after Round 14 will advance to Sunday’s single-elimination playoff rounds.

Team Series Championship
------------------------


The Team Series Championship will consist of *Ixalan* Team Sealed, in which both teams split into two groups of three.


Teams will construct decks on [Day Goes Here]?, with each group within their team receiving 12 booster packs from which to construct a total of three decks per group. The two groups may help either during deck construction. Deck building takes place before Sunday, and the tournament staff will hold onto card pools and decks until Sunday’s play once teams have finished deck construction.


Match play with the Team Sealed decks will take place on Sunday, October 8, to start the day. The groups in each team will be paired against one another randomly, and will two groups that face off will play one at a time.


All matches for the Team Series Championship are best two-out-of-three. When two team members in a single group have won two games, their group is declared the winner of the entire match.


If, at the end of two team matches, one team has won both, that team will be declared the Team Series Championship. If both teams are tied at the end of two matches, the winning group from each team will face off to determine a winner.




The Team Series Championship will consist of *Ixalan* Team Sealed, in which both teams split into two groups of three.


Teams will construct their decks on a day that will be announced as we get closer to the event, with each group within their team receiving 12 booster packs from which to construct a total of three decks per group. The two groups may help either during deck construction. Deck building takes place before Sunday, and the tournament staff will hold onto card pools and decks until Sunday’s play once teams have finished deck construction.


Match play with the Team Sealed decks will take place on Sunday, October 8, to start the day. The groups in each team will be paired against one another randomly.


All matches for the Team Series Championship are best two-out-of-three. When two team members in a single group have won two games, their group is declared the winner of the entire match.


If, at the end of two team matches, one team has won both, that team will be declared the Team Series Championship. If both teams are tied at the end of two matches, the winning group from each team will face off to determine a winner.




* Playoff matches will be best 3 out of 5.
* The first two games played in a 3 out of 5 playoff match on Sunday are pre-sideboard games. After Game 2 is completed, players may exchange cards from their deck for cards in their sideboard.
* Standings after the Swiss rounds will be used to determine final order in the standings for losing players in the semifinal playoff round.
* While there are no time limits in the playoff rounds, players are expected to play at a reasonable pace and finish their matches within 90 minutes.
* For the first game of each match in the playoff, the player that finished higher in the Swiss rounds chooses either to play first or to play second. For subsequent games in each playoff match, the usual Play/Draw rule applies (loser of the previous game decides whether to play first in the next game).


* Planeswalker Points Multiplier 12x (Lifetime Total only), REL: Professional.
* Players are required to use the same Standard Constructed decks turned in to tournament staff for all days of competition.
