Spotlight Cube Series – Live the Dream
======================================



 Posted in **Magic Online**
 on September 7, 2020 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/hero/2018_Daily_WOTC_icon_0.jpg)
By David McDarby











Hello! My name is David McDarby, and I'm an operations administrator and game designer here at Wizards of the Coast. You might know me from being the creator of Commander VS, or that guy who's been cosplaying as Jace Beleren or Ral Zarek for about a decade.



![alt text](https://media.wizards.com/2020/images/daily/david.jpg)*2011: The last U.S. Nationals at Gen Con*
I'm here to tell you about a cube that I've been working on, which is the next entry in the Spotlight Cube Series: *Live the Dream*!


Ask any of my friends or coworkers, and they'll tell you that I'm pretty into Commander. I've been on multiple design teams for it and have created tons of content for it, with this cube being my most recent endeavor. The main goal of this cube is to carry over some of the feelings of playing that format into a Limited environment where you get to feel like you're building a Commander deck on the fly! What this means is that this is a format with multiple layered synergies and archetypes in a slow enough environment to allow crazy multi-card machinations to unfold, but not quite as time-consuming as your average Commander game. Shenanigans abound in this cube, with so many wacky combos and decision trees that you might even experience something that inspires you to build a new Commander deck. I sure did!


The power band here is a bit lower than in most cubes, with cards generally chosen for their raw potential for greatness and their interactivity with other parts of the cube, rather than for their brutal efficiency. We'll leave that for 60-card formats. Here, cards cost more mana, have more versatility, and are more, dare I say, *mythic*. So instead of cards like [Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup) and [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), we're talking [Fiery Emancipation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Emancipation) and [Sublime Epiphany](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Epiphany).


But I had a lot of help devising this complex web of maniacal synergies. David White ([@thrilryder](https://twitter.com/thrilryder)) was there 11 years ago when I just started playing this wonderful game to tell me that [Niv-Mizzet, the Firemind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+the+Firemind) and [Curiosity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) was not a good way to make friends while navigating a format called "EDH." It is only fitting that over a decade later, he applied some rationality to my more ambitious ideas and reined in my fierce appetite to make every card have an average of three mana symbols and a converted mana cost of 5.


Working on every Limited environment we construct here at Wizards combined with my love of Commander deck building drew me to an archetype-heavy cube that aims to blend the line between synergy and combo. Now, when I say combo, I don't mean combining [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) and [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) on turn four to immediately end the game. I mean things like [Righteous Confluence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Confluence) and [Vito, Thorn of the Dusk Rose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vito%2C+Thorn+of+the+Dusk+Rose). [Mizzix's Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzix%27s+Mastery) and [Aminatou's Augury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aminatou%27s+Augury). Each color pair has an archetype with the potential to, as we say in the biz, *Live the Dream.*


Let's just take a look at each of the eleven archetypes.


**White-Blue Blink**
--------------------


* [![Brago, King Eternal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brago%2C+King+Eternal)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brago%2C+King+Eternal)
* [![Conjurer's Closet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Conjurer%27s+Closet)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conjurer%27s+Closet)
* [![Cavalier of Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cavalier+of+Dawn)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavalier+of+Dawn)
* [![Watcher for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Watcher+for+Tomorrow)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watcher+for+Tomorrow)

Enter the battlefield effects are aplenty in all the colors in this format, but white and blue have the best tools to abuse them. Attempt to wreak havoc in the skies and grind all who oppose you to dust one cantrip at a time! Now *Thassa* good time.


**Blue-Black Ninja Thievery**
-----------------------------


* [![Yuriko, the Tiger's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Yuriko%2C+the+Tiger%27s+Shadow)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yuriko%2C+the+Tiger%27s+Shadow)
* [![Thief of Sanity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thief+of+Sanity)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thief+of+Sanity)
* [![Knowledge Exploitation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Knowledge+Exploitation)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knowledge+Exploitation)
* [![Praetor's Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Praetor%27s+Grasp)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Praetor%27s+Grasp)

Ninja Pose! Sneak through your opponent's defenses with evasive creatures and turn their own spells against them! Maybe even pick up some of your own creatures for ninjutsu value as you pick up your opponent's goodies. Plus, you get to say cool things like "I [Despise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Despise) you" and "I attack with [Looter il-Kor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor)" as you turn your opponent's dreams into their own nightmares.


**Black-Red Self-Discard**
--------------------------


* [![Chainer, Nightmare Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chainer%2C+Nightmare+Adept)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%2C+Nightmare+Adept)
* [![Azra Oddsmaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Azra+Oddsmaker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azra+Oddsmaker)
* [![Bone Miser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bone+Miser)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Miser)
* [![Squee, Goblin Nabob](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Squee%2C+Goblin+Nabob)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squee%2C+Goblin+Nabob)

Generally, you want your opponents to discard cards, but here, you want to pitch those cards from your hand into your graveyard and turn it into a *rave*-yard. Madness? No, this is Cube. Whether you're cheating [Yidaro, Wandering Monster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yidaro%2C+Wandering+Monster) into play, churning through your deck with [Tomb Robber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tomb+Robber), or just casting big 'ol [Rakdos, the Showstopper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%2C+the+Showstopper), your music will be playing at double speed, as somebody's life total is sure to be low.


**Red-Green Bigguns**
---------------------


* [![Ruric Thar, the Unbowed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ruric+Thar%2C+the+Unbowed)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruric+Thar%2C+the+Unbowed)
* [![Sarkhan's Unsealing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sarkhan%27s+Unsealing)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan%27s+Unsealing)
* [![Warstorm Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Warstorm+Surge)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warstorm+Surge)
* [![Ghalta, Primal Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ghalta%2C+Primal+Hunger)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghalta%2C+Primal+Hunger)

If you prefer large creatures, and you cannot slander, have I got an archetype for you. Set up with some small creatures that make mana, then go to town and start deploying high-powered creatures to mess up said town. All creatures beeg and smol are able to help keep the cards and damage flowing at a remarkable rate, and I've yet to meet somebody who can outsmart a 12/12 on turn four.


**Green-White Copy Populate**
-----------------------------


* [![Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice)
* [![Spitting Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spitting+Image)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Image)
* [![Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Doubling+Season)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season)
* [![Helm of the Host](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Helm+of+the+Host)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helm+of+the+Host)

Like most everything else in this cube, this theme goes over the top. Going wide with a battalion of 1/1s is fine and all, but what about an army of [Honored Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honored+Hydra)s? Sculptures, Food, Cats, Wolves, Wurms, Treasure, Phyrexian Praetors; really anything in this cube can be doubled. Splash a [Mirror March](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+March), and you could even go infinite! [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season)'s interaction with planeswalkers in this cube is a feature, not a bug.


**White-Black Life Manipulation**
---------------------------------


* [![Karlov of the Ghost Council](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Karlov+of+the+Ghost+Council)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karlov+of+the+Ghost+Council)
* [![Witch of the Moors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Witch+of+the+Moors)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witch+of+the+Moors)
* [![Felidar Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Felidar+Sovereign)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Sovereign)
* [![Crested Sunmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Crested+Sunmare)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crested+Sunmare)

People may laugh at you for playing cards that gain life in your deck, but they won't be laughing when you drain them for 15. The game's only over once somebody runs out of life, and you've got the most of it. This is the archetype that will make you the most thankful when Play Design puts "gain two life" onto a removal sell.


**Blue-Red Chonky Spells**
--------------------------


* [![Jhoira of the Ghitu](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jhoira+of+the+Ghitu)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhoira+of+the+Ghitu)
* [![Omnispell Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Omnispell+Adept)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omnispell+Adept)
* [![Expropriate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Expropriate)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expropriate)
* [![Searing Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Searing+Wind)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Wind)

Cast spells with more chonk than is reasonable for the midgame, and if you're really feeling it, copy them a time or two. Planeswalkers and extra turns are a combo, and you don't need mana fixing for [Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) or [Inspired Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspired+Ultimatum) when you're casting them without paying their mana costs. For you math fans, I've included [Double Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Double+Vision), [Precursor Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precursor+Golem), and [Rite of Replication](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication). Good luck!


**Black-Green Graveyard Critters**
----------------------------------


* [![Izoni, Thousand-Eyed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Izoni%2C+Thousand-Eyed)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izoni%2C+Thousand-Eyed)
* [![Spider Spawning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spider+Spawning)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spider+Spawning)
* [![Syr Konrad, the Grim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Syr+Konrad%2C+the+Grim)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syr+Konrad%2C+the+Grim)
* [![Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Genesis)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis)

If you're reading this, you probably enjoy [Spider Spawning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spider+Spawning). Good news everyone: it's an entire archetype! Mill it with [Carrion Grub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Grub), find it with [Gravebreaker Lamia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravebreaker+Lamia), or rebuy it with [Greenwarden of Murasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenwarden+of+Murasa); the possibilities are endless. As long as these possibilities involve arachnids or insects.


**Red-White Damage Redirection**
--------------------------------


* [![Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Reckoner)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)
* [![Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blasphemous+Act)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act)
* [![Stuffy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stuffy+Doll)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll)
* [![Swans of Bryn Argoll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Swans+of+Bryn+Argoll)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swans+of+Bryn+Argoll)

Sometimes red spells deal more damage than is reasonable to creatures, and being unreasonable is what this cube is all about. While removal spells alone cannot traditionally win you the game, it sure can here. Red-white decks are not as combat oriented in this cube as in most environments but pile up the damage doublers, and even [Thraben Inspector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Inspector) will be a force to reckon with in the archetype that finally proves [Chain Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+Lightning) can be better than [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


**Green-Blue Flash**
--------------------


* [![Prophet of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Prophet+of+Kruphix)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophet+of+Kruphix)
* [![Glademuse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glademuse)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glademuse)
* [![Yeva, Nature's Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Yeva%2C+Nature%27s+Herald)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yeva%2C+Nature%27s+Herald)
* [![Voracious Greatshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Voracious+Greatshark)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voracious+Greatshark)

Everybody loves mana, but instead of doubling it to cast ten mana spells on turn five, here we double it wide to cast spells on your opponent's turn. Getting to "untap" every turn means you're playing twice as much! Plus, who wants to auto yield all the time when you've got cards to draw? This is the color pair most likely to draw two cards with [Fblthp, the Lost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fblthp%2C+the+Lost), and is there any greater dream than that?


**Five-Color Dream**
--------------------


* [![Coalition Victory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Coalition+Victory)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Victory)
* [![Chromatic Orrery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chromatic+Orrery)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Orrery)
* [![Niv-Mizzet Reborn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Niv-Mizzet+Reborn)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet+Reborn)
* [![Sisay, Weatherlight Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sisay%2C+Weatherlight+Captain)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sisay%2C+Weatherlight+Captain)

The dreamiest archetype of them all aims to cast all the sweetest cards in the multiverse. [Bloom Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) and [Faeburrow Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faeburrow+Elder) will be your BFFs, and ideally, your cards will just be better than your opponents'. Get yourself a good Companion at your side, some nice mana fixing, and if your cards don't say "win the game" de jure, they sure say it de facto.


And those are just the eleven main archetypes of the cube! If you're not feeling any of these, I've also included a dino-sized helping of single-card build-arounds. Like any Izzet mage worth their Mizzium, not even I can fully comprehend what this cube is capable of, but I hope y'all find ways to break it, and most importantly, have fun doing so.
