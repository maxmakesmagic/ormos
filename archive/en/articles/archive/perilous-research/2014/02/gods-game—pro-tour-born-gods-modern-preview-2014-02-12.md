Gods of the Game—Pro Tour Born of the Gods Modern Preview
=========================================================



 Posted in [Perilous Research](/en/articles/columns/perilous-research-archive)
 on February 12, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 





Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive [**Magic Online**](https://accounts.onlinegaming.wizards.com/) column. Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* is just nine days away. We'll all be tuning in to [the live coverage here](https://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/protour/bornofthegods14#viewer), but we can get a lot more out of our viewing experience if we have a strong understanding of the format. [Last week](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/pr/285), we discussed the changes to the Modern banned list and their implications. Today, we'll be taking a look at the Modern format in its final iteration before the new Pro Tour format. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/pr/2014/pr_wk06_286_cardart_cruelultimatum_c13.jpg)[Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) | Art by [Todd Lockwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=[%22Todd Lockwood%22])

 Modern is an exciting format that affords players the opportunity to stick with a deck they've come to know and love for many years. Since its inception, Modern has allowed players to get creative while remaining successful. The massive volume of cards in the Modern format makes for a lot of interesting game play and the variance of the fastest or most disruptive decks is balanced by the incredibly difficult degree of play skill needed to play well. As we'll see, Modern is a place where innovation and creativity are rewarded. Let's take a look at some decks we can expect to see at Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* next weekend:
