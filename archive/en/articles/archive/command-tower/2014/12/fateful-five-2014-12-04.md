The Fateful Five
================



 Posted in [Command Tower](/en/articles/columns/command-tower-archive)
 on December 4, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 





Building decks in Commander is tough. Assembling dozens of unique cards, fiddling with the right lands to cast spells of multiple colors, and massaging exactly how the deck is built is a process that never ends. There's always something to tweak.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/images/daily/cardart_haplessresearcher.jpg)



[Hapless Researcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hapless+Researcher) | Art by [Ron Spears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=["Ron Spears"])



While there's never a given endpoint for a deck there are, fortunately, multiple starting points. Decks that friends and previous opponents have built can inspire up to follow a narrow theme. Decks in articles (like, typically, this one!) online provide an explicit list to assemble. Certainly the most convenient of all starting point are preconstructed Commander decks: *Magic: The Gathering**—**Commander* (2014 Edition).



Getting into Commander or starting down the path of a new theme is easy when you pick up a whole deck without even looking up a card, and that's exactly what we're talking about today: Which of the five new Commander decks you might pick, and why someone else did.



Commander Fact
==============



Far and away the most popular of the Commander decks picked by readers was Built from Scratch, the red-based deck.

It's filled with artifacts and ways to get as much mileage out them as possible. For anyone who's only seen red as the way to cast small, aggressive creatures and burn spells it's a pleasant surprise. Depth—the presence of deeper synergies and interactions that provide options over time—is something easy to overlook in red. Built from Scratch delivered.



Michael shared the shiny feature that drew him in: [Daretti, Scrap Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daretti%2C+Scrap+Savant):



Of the five 2014 *Commander* decks available, the one I'm interested in the most is Built from Scratch, in large part due to the Planeswalker commander that comes with it.



Green and black I don't have much interest in at all. Blue looks like it has some fun stuff in it that I wouldn't mind having for parts, though Teferi lacks a real hook to latch onto as a commander. White, likewise, is a deck that I'd like for a lot of the new pieces and reprints, though amusingly I've joked that it's a deck I already have thanks to [my Equipment-heavy deck that uses Kemba as its commander](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/opening-moves-2014-05-29) (which, on a related note, made it fun to see Kemba get a reprint simply on the basis of seeing one of my go-to commanders be deemed worthy of getting an official Commander-deck reprint).



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Teferi%2C+Temporal+Archmage)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Temporal+Archmage) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kemba%2C+Kha+Regent)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kemba%2C+Kha+Regent)



Daretti's red artifact deck, on the other hand, provides me with a new deck option and commander—Planeswalker commander, no less—to build around, which gives me a new theme that I don't have yet, in a color that I don't yet have a Commander deck for. Which means that of the five new Commander decks, it gives me the greatest value option.



Already, I've begun to channel my own inner-Daretti by thinking about which pieces I'll want to toss away, once I've played it in its default state once or twice, to make room for cards that are more thematic and/or make the best use of Daretti's abilities. Including at least a few artifacts like [Elixir of Immortality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality) and [Feldon's Cane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feldon%27s+Cane) (Extra bonus points for extra thematic value!) to let me more freely toss cards away with less worry about not being able to get them back.

Max agreed that the tools in red were a blast:



My favorite of the five decks would be Built from Scratch. Mono-red is tricky to work with in a format where you may need to deal more than a hundred damage to win, and that deck allows the color to do some really fun and interesting things that I wasn't expecting. Picking one deck to be excited about is hard. I purchased all of them and all of my decks want something from one of the decks. Whether its replacing [Reverberate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverberate) with [Dualcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dualcaster+Mage) because [Xira Arien](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xira+Arien) likes creatures; testing [Comeuppance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comeuppance) in [Selvala, Explorer Returned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selvala%2C+Explorer+Returned); or finally getting a [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) for [Lyzolda, the Blood Witch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyzolda%2C+the+Blood+Witch), all of my decks have a card or two to find room for. My recently constructed [Ashling the Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashling+the+Pilgrim) deck just got fifteen cards changed out because of the Daretti precon. The blue preconstructed deck features Island art from *Time Spiral*, the most nefarious block of all time. Typhoon lightning strikes above jagged sea crags, the perfect backdrop for when [Stitcher Geralf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher+Geralf) cobles together his skaabs. This sounded like the terrifying opening scenes of a fantastic horror movie. (Since we were faced with this questions shortly after Halloween it might have played a role in my reading.) Less scary, however, was the mixing and matching Andrew pulled out of Peer Through Time for another fun deck:



You ask the hard questions. I love the direction red is going, and will be stripping that deck down for my [Kurkesh, Onakke Ancient](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kurkesh%2C+Onakke+Ancient) deck. The black deck has lots of fun toys for [The Mimeoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Mimeoplasm). I'm thinking about reviving my Equipment deck with Nahiri as the Commander. I have so many green decks that can use cards from this product. But my final answer is the blue deck. Because [Reef Worm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reef+Worm).



More generally, I love that the blue deck contains three awesome new sea monster cards: [Reef Worm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reef+Worm), [Breaching Leviathan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaching+Leviathan), and [Stormsurge Kraken](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormsurge+Kraken). My [Thassa, God of the Sea](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) deck is full of flavor, with sea monsters being the lynchpin of victory. Leviathans and Krakens are some of my favorite creature types, so these three cards are a welcome addition.

But wait, there's more! [Teferi, Temporal Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Temporal+Archmage) is a mono-blue deck's dream, using his -1 to ramp up mana like crazy. Don't even care about the other stuff. I just want to power out more big fat fatties. [Sapphire Medallion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Medallion) is another obvious mana-boosting, gold-star winner. Love that this cycle came back. Finally, I can't express how much I love [Unstable Obelisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unstable+Obelisk). I'm a huge fan of mana rocks that do other stuff, and when the other stuff is nuking a permanent it's an instant swoon.

Ian put it in blunt terms:



The easy answer is black. Why? Because I'm a black player.



I love to sacrifice life/creatures or other goodies to get the better toys. [Ob Nixilis of the Black Oath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ob+Nixilis+of+the+Black+Oath) is exactly that, stealing opponents' life force to get their demons and sacrifice their creatures to get back more life and cards.



But to top it off is [Ghoulcaller Gisa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulcaller+Gisa). My favorite card type is Zombie. I have a [Grimgrin, Corpse-Born](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimgrin%2C+Corpse-Born) Commander deck; [Korlash, Heir to Blackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korlash%2C+Heir+to+Blackblade) Commander deck; mono-black Zombie deck; a black/blue Zombie mill deck; and a black/green deck Zombie deck.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ghoulcaller+Gisa)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulcaller+Gisa)



And seeing Gisa just wants me to make a Commander deck with her.



And Kyle took to the Demon angle:



Out of all the Commander decks, the red one obviously looks more powerful than the rest. However, I like the black deck quite a bit. Getting 5/5 flying Demons just for a small amount of life, which you will be gaining from the first ability. I now also realize the potential of [Abyssal Persecutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor). Making your opponents unable to lose might cause them to not want to attack you, as they become indestructible basically.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Abyssal+Persecutor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor)



Matt even went as far as to have a bullet point list of reasons:



Sworn to Darkness is the best of the five.



* It has better card draw than Peer Through Time. It always has ways to gain life to pay for its card draw.
* It has the best removal considering that decks in this set have very few enchantments. [Pestilence Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestilence+Demon) can repeatedly wipe out all of Guided by Nature's Elves and Forged in Stone's tokens. Nahiri's Equipment isn't a threat when there are no tokens to wield them. [Æther Snap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Snap) removes not just token armies but the Planeswalkers commanding them.
* It has more explosive potential: It is the only one with more than one mana-doubling permanent ([Magus of the Coffers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Coffers) and [Crypt Ghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Ghast)) and it can steal a creature from a graveyard with [Necromantic Selection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromantic+Selection).
* It doesn't have directed hate toward its strategy. Black is the deck running [Bojuka Bog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Bog), not any of its opponents.
* Most importantly, black can most freely convert its resources. Life and creatures can be converted to drawing cards. Creatures can be converted into life. Creatures can even be converted into more creatures.


As Mark Rosewater explained: Black is the color that most rewards playing more black.



So it's arguably the best deck against the other four, but why do I like it the most? That is because the deck has the most cards I didn't already own!



In the immortal words of AC/DC: Yes I'm back in black! (Cue power guitar riff.)

**Delayne:** Hey quick question for you. What new Commander deck would you buy? Then why? I'm very new to Commander. Wanting to buy my first deck.



**Stybs:** Honestly, I've heard the white is surprisingly strong. With some of the built-in token strategy it's an easy deck to morph into white-green, Naya, or Abzan for a full on token deck. Tokens are usually resilient and can overpower in situations so I think that's a good place to start.



Adding the green deck to the mix is a pretty easy way to build a token deck, but I'd hedge white if you have to choose one.



**Delayne:** So buy the 2014 green deck to and put the white and green together? And who would be the commander?



**Stybs:** You'd have to find one you liked. [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice) and [Rhys the Redeemed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) are solid options.



**Delayne:** So never playing before I'll get the white deck …Then what is the next thing to get?



**Stybs:** It's up to you! As you play you'll discover cards you like or strategies you want to try, so those cards are where you'll look next. What you want to play is something only you can decide. Good luck!



Commander Unchained
===================



The biggest takeaway from "Which deck should I get?" is, always, "What deck do I want to explore?" Preconstructed decks are just a stepping stone. They can ease you into a format for the first time or provide a springboard to overhaul your favorite deck.



What you choose is, always, up to you!
