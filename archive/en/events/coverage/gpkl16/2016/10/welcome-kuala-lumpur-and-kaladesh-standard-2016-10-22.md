Welcome to Kuala Lumpur and Kaladesh Standard
=============================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX KUALA LUMPUR 2016](/en/events/coverage/gpkl16)
 on October 22, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











Pro Tour *Kaladesh* is in the books and we saw Shota Yasooka win his second Pro Tour. Aside from *Kaladesh* being an incredibly wondrous world, the Standard format also seems to be multitudinally diverse with combo, aggro, midrange, and control decks witnessed.


Let's take a quick look at what to expect this weekend in Kuala Lumpur (and Providence) based on the results from Pro Tour *Kaladesh* and how are our inventors adapting to the ever-changing climate?


The Combo Decks
===============


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aetherworks+Marvel)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel)
* Temur Aetherworks

It has been a while since we saw a full-fledged combo deck do its magic in Standard but the introduction of [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) has certainly made it a possibility. Temur Aetherworks was the most played deck at Honolulu from among the field of nearly 500, even though the common consensus is that the surprise element is gone or that it is vulnerable to a more readily-prepared populace.


For example, [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind), [Ceremonious Rejection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ceremonious+Rejection), and [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) are great options to prevent a turn-four [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) from hitting the board!


Are there other options out there for the combo player?

The Aggro Decks
===============


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Electrostatic+Pummeler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Pummeler)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Smuggler%27s+Copter)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter)
* Red-Green Energy
* White-Red Vehicles
* Mardu Vehicles

If an aggro deck with a combo kill is your cup of tea, Red-Green Energy might just be right up your alley. [Servant of the Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+the+Conduit) and [Voltaic Brawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Brawler) accumulate energy to abuse [Electrostatic Pummeler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Pummeler) and [Bristling Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bristling+Hydra) with, while the threat of being killed out of nowhere by Larger than Life, [Built to Smash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Built+to+Smash), [Blossoming Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Defense), and [Uncaged Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uncaged+Fury) continue to keep opponents on their toes.


[Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) leads the charge in pushing the format into a more aggressive direction. Crewing vehicles is made easier in decks that run upwards of sixteen or twenty creatures and the fact that vehicles evade sorcery-speed removal make them resilient threats that are coherent with the aggro players' game plan.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Depala%2C+Pilot+Exemplar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Depala%2C+Pilot+Exemplar)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Scrapheap+Scrounger)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger)
White and Red cards form the core of the Boros and Mardu decks, thanks to the existence of [Toolcraft Exemplar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toolcraft+Exemplar), [Veteran Motorist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Motorist), and [Depala, Pilot Exemplar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Depala%2C+Pilot+Exemplar), all of which have seen heavy play in the past weeks. In some cases, players have also taken the liberty of making a black splash for [Scrapheap Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger) and [Unlicensed Disintegration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration).

The Midrange Decks
==================


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spell+Queller)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grapple+with+the+Past)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past)
* White-Blue Flash
* Black-Green Delirium
* Temur Emerge

After Joey Manner's impressive run at Honolulu, White-Blue Flash has become a heavily-favored choice going into this weekend simply because it is a deck that offers a nice mix of threats and countermagic. [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller), [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn), and [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare) are not only difficult to play around, it can be devastating if handled inadequately.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grim+Flayer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tireless+Tracker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker)
On the other hand, Black-Green Delirium has much less finesse but have significantly more removal. They can attempt to control the board with their removal suite, tear apart an opponent's hand with discard spells or offer pressure via [Grim Flayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer), [Liliana, the Last Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope), and [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker). [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) can search for [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) or [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) when required. Alternatively, they can just grind you out with the good old [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker).


Last but not least, Temur Emerge seemed to have died down a little after Pro Tour *Eldritch Moon*, but it is capable of preying upon players who underestimate it. [Filigree Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filigree+Familiar) can enable premature [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend), along with complimentary board wipes in the form of [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) before closing the game with [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End).

The Control Decks
=================


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Torrential+Gearhulk)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk)
* Grixis Control
* Jeskai Control
* Blue-Red Spells

Control decks are back in full force thanks to the printing of [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) in *Kaladesh* and it was not surprising to see Shota Yasooka take down Pro Tour *Kaladesh* with a couple of these bad boys. The finalist Carlos Rom√£o was also sporting the powerful gearhulk.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Painful+Truths)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painful+Truths)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fumigate)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumigate)
Grixis Control lends strength from [Painful Truths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painful+Truths), [Unlicensed Disintegration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration), and [Essence Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Extraction), while Jeskai control prefers to utilize [Immolating Glare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Glare), [Blessed Alliance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Alliance), and [Fumigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumigate), among other tools. Either way, both archetypes are happy to include [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning) and [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames) to help them survive to the late game where the deck can shine.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dynavolt+Tower)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dynavolt+Tower)
[Dynavolt Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dynavolt+Tower) is another card which helped blue control decks make a resurgence and only time will tell if Pierre Dagen's "counter burn" strategy will become a mainstay. Whatever it is, we expect *Kaladesh* Standard to be as exciting as ever!
