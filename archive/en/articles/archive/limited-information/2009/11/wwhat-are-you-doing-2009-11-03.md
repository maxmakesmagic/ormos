This pool's red was pretty weak. Only the [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) (and maybe the [Tuktuk Grunts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tuktuk+Grunts) if I wound up with an ally heavy deck) stood out to me as a card that I really wanted to play. With that in mind, I was able to quickly eliminate red from contention except for a potential small splash if I wound up in green, thanks to the [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) plus [Khalni Heart Expedition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition), and/or black thanks to the [Akoum Refuge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Refuge). 


Once I pushed the red out of the way, I noticed that my white, green, and blue weren't much deeper.


Going by simple playable density I realized that I was going to have to play black as a main color. If I went white-blue I would have enough playables for a deck, but that deck looked pretty lackluster

This deck perfectly demonstrates the problems with white-black decks in *Zendikar*. There are just so many color-intensive cards in white and black that it becomes very difficult for them to work together. 


Let's say you have an opening hand with [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Heartstabber Mosquito](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartstabber+Mosquito), [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), and [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage). What do you do on turn one?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li63_handOfCards.jpg)  
For what it's worth, the right answer is probably to cast the [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), because there are a variety of draws, such as [Adventuring Gear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adventuring+Gear), another [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), or [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure), that would make your turn two relevant (whereas if you play the [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) first, the best that you can do is have a [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) on turn two).


This deck, while fairly fast and powerful if it functions properly, just has too many potential mana problems to justify playing. The upside presented by having the potential for a turn-one [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) is more that off-set by the cost of needing to run an eighth white source, drawing a [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) late when you have moved past your hyper aggressive stage of the game, and having draws where you can't play your ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) two-drop on turn two if you play a [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) on turn one.


So I cut the two [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx)es, a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) and the [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) added a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), Bala Ged Theif, [Bog Tatters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Tatters) and [Soul Stair Expedition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Stair+Expedition), making the deck look like this:

### Understanding What Needs to Go Right


When you are building a Sealed Deck you have the luxury of laying everything out and seeing what combination of cards will give you the most potent concoction to go into battle with. You can make conscious decisions for when you want more power, when you want more consistency, when you need more early action, and when you need more ways to break a game open. 


In a booster draft, you have the ability to control your own destiny. Through your draft picks, you will get to consciously decide what is valuable to you every time you see a new pack. Sometimes you will be choosing between two dramatically different types of cards—say, a [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) and a [Spire Barrage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Barrage). And other times you will find yourself choosing between two cards that appear quite different from one another, but are actually functionally quite similar.


So when you open up your first pack and you see an [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) next to a [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) with nothing else of note, you might think that you are choosing between two wildly different cards. In some ways you are, but in many ways those cards are actually quite similar. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Oran-Rief Survivalist | Kor Aeronaut |

If you end up with a lot of white in your deck, having a 2/2 flier that you will be able to regularly play on turn two will be great. But if you end up only playing a little bit of white, say nine white spells off of 8 [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), then that [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) will still be good, but it won't be anything special.


If you take the [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist), you will have a fantastic card if your deck ends up with another nine Allies. You will still have a pretty good card if you only grab four or five more Allies, and you will still have a reasonable card if you only wind up with one or two more Allies in your deck. 


The point is that both of these cards need something to go right for you, beyond just playing that color, for them to reach their full potential. In that way, both [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) and [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) actually share more in common with [Spire Barrage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Barrage) than they share with [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) during the first couple of picks of the draft.


### Potential Picks


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li63_2CardsOverlaped.jpg)If you take a [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) over an [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) first pick out of an otherwise empty pack, it should be because you have a preference for white, a dislike for green, a reason why you wouldn't want to try to draft Allies, or something else along those lines. Keep in mind that you are taking a reasonable risk by taking a ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) two-drop first pick. 


If you take a [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) over an [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist), you should do so because you have a preference for blue, a dislike for green, or a reason why you wouldn't want to try to draft Allies.


If you take a [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) over a [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) first pick out of an otherwise empty pack, it should be because you have a strong preference for white or a strong dislike of blue. The reason why you need to have a much stronger reason to make this pick than you would when you were taking the [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) over the [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) is because [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) has in game functions that are quite similar to [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut) while being significantly easier to cast.


When you are comparing two cards that have similar potential impact levels, where both have about the same level of impact when that potential is reached, then you should take the one that has an easier time reaching that potential.


When you are comparing two cards that have very different potential impact levels, such as [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) when compared to either [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) or [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut), then you have a lot more to consider. If Oran Rief Survivalist reaches its full potential, it will be better than [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) or [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut)s could ever hope to be, but if it doesn't it could end up being pretty mediocre.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li63_oranRiefSlice.jpg)  
There is no single "right pick" in these three examples. Personally, I would take the [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) over either [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) or [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut)s, and [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) over [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut)s because I like blue a lot right now and dislike white. But I could easily reverse those picks if my color preferences changed.


Just make sure that you aren't doing something like taking [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) over [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern), [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) over [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut)s, and [Kor Aeronaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut)s over [Oran-Rief Survivalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist). If you are, then you probably haven't thought through why you are making those picks.
