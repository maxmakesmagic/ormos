Twenty Zendikar Decks
=====================



 Posted in [Reconstructed](/en/articles/columns/reconstructed-archive)
 on September 29, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 





You've read [the story](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/battle-zendikar-story). You've seen [the cards](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/card-image-gallery/battle-zendikar-2015-09-18). You've played at the Prerelease.


Now it's time for the next step: Build some decks!


But perhaps you're not sure where to start, which Zendikar-fueled weapons to unleash. Well, that's where this article comes in!


Over the past week and a half, readers have sent in all sorts of decklists. Big and small, aggressive and defensive . . . you name it! Now it's time showcase a whopping 20 of them and see what exciting new decks have been brought to the table—plus offer some deck building advice along the way.


Ready to journey through Zendikar? Here we go!


Beatdown
========


Let's start with the fast and the brutal: beatdown decks!


[Last week](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/reconstructed/fore-2015-09-22) we looked at a quick black-red beatdown strategy. But what other new takes on aggression might we see now that *Theros* block is out of the picture and *Battle for Zendikar* is firmly in?


Well, here's one:

Landfall was a backbone of many beatdown decks in original *Zendikar*—and it's back and ready to smash face this time around as well!


[Scythe Leopard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scythe+Leopard), [Snapping Gnarlid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Gnarlid), [Makindi Sliderunner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makindi+Sliderunner), and [Undergrowth Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undergrowth+Champion) all make up the core of a red-green landfall deck this go around. The key that I would really focus on is having a low curve and landfall creatures—it's easy to be tempted into cards such as [Explosive Vegetation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Vegetation) and [Nissa's Renewal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Renewal), but those cards are only good when you already have a board presence.


Instead, I'd double down on being aggressive and add [Zurgo Bellstriker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zurgo+Bellstriker), twelve fetch lands, and—because it's one of this deck's biggest MVPs—more [Atarka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka%27s+Command)s. This deck can come out to some truly blisteringly fast starts in a way Standard decks normally don't, and [Atarka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka%27s+Command) fuels a lot of that.


Imagine this start:


Turn 1: [Scythe Leopard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scythe+Leopard)


Turn 2: [Makindi Sliderunner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makindi+Sliderunner)


Turn 3: Fetch land, cast [Atarka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka%27s+Command) dealing 3 and playing a fetch land, use both fetch lands. That's 14 damage on turn three!


Yeah, that's powerful.


So that's what the forces of Zendikar have in store—but what about the Eldrazi? Last time around I looked at a black-red deck, and ReConstructed-stalwart Qoarl expands on that further by taking it into Grixis!

This decklist is very much a streamlined ramp strategy. Its plan is simple: Curve a two-drop accelerator (Rattleclaw or Whisperer) into a four-drop accelerator (Archive or Vegetation) and start unloading threats such as [Dragonlord Atarka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Atarka) as early as turn four!


[Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) ends up being a key card for this deck, both ramping it forward and allowing you to turn it into two fresh cards if you end up flooded. [Nissa's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Revelation), while not a creature, really shines here with both [Desolation Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolation+Twin) and [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) as overwhelming cards to hit, and [Dragonlord Atarka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Atarka) an entirely reasonable backup.


This deck is the new goldfish metric for whatever deck you build. Can you stand up to something like this? If not, you may want to make sure you have tools to fight it.

This version of a ramp deck still features plenty on the top end and Ulamog to curve into, but it uses a [Conduit of Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conduit+of+Ruin) package to help get there. By expanding into blue, it also picks up [Herald of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herald+of+Kozilek), which is an excellent way to buy time and also makes your Eldrazi easier to cast. Four copies of [Titan's Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titan%27s+Presence) serve as a catch-all removal spell.


If I was looking to add to this deck, I would probably look into some copies of the new [Kiora, Master of the Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiora%2C+Master+of+the+Depths). She can both massively accelerate you and help dig you into additional creatures.


But maybe ramp isn't quite your thing and you just want to play something more in the middle. Well, brace yourself: Midrange is up next!

Save for a bit of light mana ramp (which it probably should have in the form of [Whisperer of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whisperer+of+the+Wilds) or [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic) anyway) this is an extraordinarily traditional midrange deck with some new twists from *Battle for Zendikar*. You even have [Smothering Abomination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smothering+Abomination) and [Evolutionary Leap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolutionary+Leap) as a continual engine to draw you more cards!


I would probably play some mana ramp (as previously noted) and something like [Catacomb Sifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Catacomb+Sifter) potentially as well. If you end up with enough of a critical mass of cheap creatures in the deck, [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) becomes an option, in which case this deck really could pick up board presence quickly.

Allies return in *Battle for Zendikar*—and this deck takes advantage of their synergies by creating a beatdown package fueled with some of the life gain themes in black and white as well.


[Kalastria Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalastria+Healer), [Zulaport Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zulaport+Cutthroat), and [Drana's Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drana%27s+Emissary) create lots of 1 life chunks for your [Serene Steward](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serene+Steward) to marvel at, and Drana herself grows your team even larger.


And then, of course, you have the endgame: [March from the Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+from+the+Tomb) with [Kalastria Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalastria+Healer). If you return two Healers plus any other Ally, you're draining your opponent for 6 on the spot—and it's not that hard to even work another Ally in there as well. It really gives you long-game inevitability.


That kind of combo aspect can be really fun. So what if you wanted to go in on that further? Well, why bother with [March from the Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+from+the+Tomb) when [Rally the Ancestors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors) is ready to get everything back!

Super Mana
==========


One of the craziest things *Battle for Zendikar* brings to the table is the new dual land cycle of [Sunken Hollow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunken+Hollow) and friends. Thanks to the basic land types on those dual lands, Standard finally features a fetch land and fetchable dual land mana base—and it's time for everything crazy to break loose.


Playing four or even five colors has become easier than ever. If you build your deck properly, any fetch land can find two of three colors of mana! Casting all the best cards is a legitimate strategy going forward. So let's look at several of the above archetypes through the lens of super mana.

Tyler feels when it comes to the best multicolored cards, why choose? Your three-drops here could be any of [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider), [Savage Knuckleblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Knuckleblade), or [Anafenza, the Foremost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+the+Foremost)! And don't worry—you'll follow that overstatted creature up with a [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) and really put the hurt on.


[Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) is especially brutal here. With so many powerful three-drops, you can easily add 8 power to the board!


And of course, this deck also features [Bring to Light](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bring+to+Light)! It can find you a Rhino to put right into play—or just be an extra [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) when you need it. Regardless of the case, this deck has a ton of flexibility.

Zendikari versus Eldrazi
========================


Wow! We've covered 20 new decks in a single article—what a bunch of cool new stuff there! This format is really shaping up to be exciting. And with the [Pro Tour](http://magic.wizards.com/en/protour/bfz) only a couple weeks away, it's going to be thrilling to see how the pros tackle these decks.


In the meantime though, I'm curious to see how you keep building up these great decks! In two weeks, we're going to have Zendikar versus Eldrazi week—and I'm going to be taking a look at decks on each side. So for your task this week, pick a side and send me your best representation!


**Format:** Standard


**Restrictions:** Your deck must either be a Zendikar-themed deck or an Eldrazi-themed deck


**Deadline:** Monday, October 5, at 6 p.m. Pacific Time.


**Submit all decklists by emailing me at** [**reconstructeddecks@gmail.com**](mailto:reconstructeddecks@gmail.com)**.**


Decklists should be submitted with YOURNAME's DECKNAME at the top. Underneath should be one card per line, with just a leading number. For example:


12 Mountain


4 [Makindi Sliderunner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makindi+Sliderunner)


3 [Valakut Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut+Predator)


4 [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash)


. . . and so on. Please don't use anything but a space to separate the card numbers and names—don't write "4x [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash)," for example. Well-formatted decklists have a much better chance of being read and making it into the column. Poorly formatted decklists are more likely to be ignored. If I can't read your decklist, I certainly can't talk about it!


What exactly does that mean? It's up to your interpretation! And, of course, you are welcome to send me multiple decks so you can accurately show off what you can do on both sides. I want to see your best efforts!


Speaking of best efforts, I want to let you all know about some important (and exciting!) ReConstructed news.


Ever since this column's humble beginnings in March 2012, it's been posted on Tuesdays. Now, surely you've trained yourself to be glued to your computer come Tuesday for your weekly dose of ReConstructed. But next week, that's changing; ReConstructed is moving to Mondays!


That's right: You can catch ReConstructed a day earlier, sitting right alongside Mark Rosewater. Of course, if you do still tune in on Tuesday (habits are hard to break!) you can just go back a day and find it.


So next week, join me at a different time, same channel as we kick off your week with a deck building bang on ReConstructed's first Monday slot! If you have any feedback or thoughts in the meantime, please send it my way either by writing me [a tweet](http://www.twitter.com/gavinverhey) or asking me a question on [my Tumblr](http://www.gavinverhey.tumblr.com).


Talk with you again on Monday!
