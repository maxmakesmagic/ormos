Whiteout
========



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on July 16, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




Mike Cannon signed on to write From the Lab at the end of 2012. An ardent casual player and lover of bizarre synergies, he'll be bringing you a selection of crazy combo decks every Monday. 





Hello laboratorians! This week I started digging a bit deeper into *Magic 2015* in an attempt to discover some combo cards that weren't quite as obvious at first glance. It was surprisingly easy to gather ideas, confirming my belief that *M15* is the best core set in several years. In fact, I didn't even make it through white before I had enough ideas for my article today. The other colors will have to wait for now, because it's time to put the W in WUBRG.



We Strike at Midnight



[Midnight Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Guard) isn't a new card, but it is one I haven't used before. It basically has its own little built-in [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm), and that's a card I've abused plenty. [Presence of Gond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Presence+of+Gond) was the first combo card that came to mind, as it was the [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm) combo I used the most. The next card I thought of was also from *Shadowmoor*: [Elemental Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Mastery). Strangely, it was only later on that [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) surfaced from the maelstrom of my consciousness, and with two solid red Auras to use, I decided to ditch green and go with the hasty tokens.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Midnight Guard)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight Guard)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elemental Mastery)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental Mastery)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Splinter Twin)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter Twin)



There's also another creature with [Midnight Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Guard)'s ability we can use, although most players aren't aware of it. [Sunstrike Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunstrike+Legionnaire) wasn't exactly the cream of the crop even in *Legions*. In fact, much of the time its role was better filled by [Whipcorder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipcorder), an uncommon from the previous set.



In this deck, however, its ability to tap creatures doesn't really matter. Neither does the fact that it doesn't untap normally. What matters is that if you stick [Elemental Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Mastery) on it, you get to have a party, and everyone's invited. Seriously, like an arbitrarily large number of people are coming.



[Torchling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torchling) doesn't go completely crazy like the other two, but it can make a very large number of tokens all at once. Putting 3 power on the board with haste for every red mana you spend certainly isn't a bad deal.




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sunstrike Legionnaire)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunstrike Legionnaire)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Torchling)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torchling)



*M15* also gives this deck a brand-new card to work with. [Heliod's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%27s+Pilgrim) can search for [Elemental Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Mastery) or [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) to help you combo off more consistently. I've also included [Faith's Fetters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) and [Chained to the Rocks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chained+to+the+Rocks) as removal spells that can be searched for in a pinch.



[Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) is a more traditional removal spell that often has no drawback for this deck due to its propensity for going infinite. I've also included [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) and [Rebuff the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuff+the+Wicked) to stop your opponent from killing your creature with removal when you try to enchant it.

Haunted Altar



[Spirit Bonds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Bonds) was another card that seemed to have some amount of combo potential. The first idea that came to mind was [Nether Traitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor). When [Nether Traitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) enters the battlefield, you can pay ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) to make a Spirit token. Then sacrifice [Nether Traitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor), followed by the token, to trigger the Traitor's ability, returning it to the battlefield and allowing you to make a new Spirit. With [Phyrexian Altar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar) to sacrifice the creatures for mana, you can perform the loop indefinitely.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spirit Bonds)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit Bonds)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nether Traitor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether Traitor)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian Altar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian Altar)



Now, this combo isn't exactly new. It can also be done with two copies of [Nether Traitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor), but [Spirit Bonds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Bonds) makes it so you don't have to rely on getting two out of your four copies. I'll also include [Genesis Chamber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Chamber) as an additional backup that doesn't require any mana to work.



[Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) and [Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble) provide an easy way to win the game once you get the loop going. The fact that they both kill your opponent and give you life means that, even if your opponent has something like [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) to survive, you won't be at risk of dying to a subsequent attack.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blood Artist)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood Artist)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Falkenrath Noble)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath Noble)



Since this combo requires four pieces to work, I'll include a number of ways to search your library for the pieces. [Enlightened Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) can get [Phyrexian Altar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar), [Genesis Chamber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Chamber), and [Spirit Bonds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Bonds). Meanwhile, [Rhystic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Tutor) and [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) can get anything, although [Rhystic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Tutor) requires you to wait until your opponent has tapped all his or her lands for a spell.



[Hero's Downfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall) and [Ulcerate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulcerate) give the deck some removal to stay alive until the pieces can be put together, killing off most creatures for very little mana.

Gotta Love the Hate



[Hushwing Gryff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hushwing+Gryff) was designed as a safety valve that players can use if decks abusing certain interactions become a problem. However, it can also be made into a problem of its own with the right team behind it. To add to the fun, we also have access to [Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) for redundancy as well as [Sundial of the Infinite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundial+of+the+Infinite), which can secretly perform a similar function by ending the turn with the triggered ability on the stack.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hushwing Gryff)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hushwing Gryff)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Torpor Orb)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor Orb)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sundial of the Infinite)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundial of the Infinite)



Now comes the choice of what creatures to abuse, now that their detrimental enters-the-battlefield abilities no longer trigger. [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought) is an obvious choice. A 12/12 for just one mana is a bit above the normal curve, after all. At two mana, [Hunted Horror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Horror) is a tempting target, giving you a 7/7 without giving your opponent anything at all. [Treacherous Pit-Dweller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treacherous+Pit-Dweller) also works, with [Hushwing Gryff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hushwing+Gryff) preventing the ability that donates the creature to your opponent and leaving you with a 5/4 after it dies.



[Eater of Days](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eater+of+Days) is absolutely massive, and flying and trample make it very hard for your opponent to stop. Its drawback is normally prohibitively costly, requiring you to give up your next two turns in exchange for your oversized creature. With [Hushwing Gryff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hushwing+Gryff), you suddenly have a 9/8 for four mana, with no other costs.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian Dreadnought)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian Dreadnought)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Eater of Days)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eater of Days)



[Dust Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Elemental) isn't as big, but flash can help you play around counterspells and catch your opponent off guard. Finally, [Demonlord of Ashmouth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonlord+of+Ashmouth) is the weakest of the bunch as far as power for cost goes, but it does have undying to allow it to dodge removal spells. Unlike most of the other creatures, it can also be cast normally in a pinch if you don't have a way to get rid of its drawback. I've also added [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to defend you from any particularly problematic creatures.
