The Evolution of Abzan
======================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 28, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






This Standard season has been one of the most exciting and diverse in memory. Throughout the season, we've seen new and old decks emerge and disappear as the metagame shifted. Abzan has presented one of the most interesting and intricate evolutions over the last few months.


Let's take a look at the Abzan strategies that defined the color combination over the course of this Standard season.


At Pro Tour Dragons of Tarkir, Abzan Aggro was one of the most successful decks. The strategy approached an unknown field effectively by backing up good disruption with a fast clock and good removal.

Lucas Siow found a midrange version of the deck that attacked the metagame from the perfect angle.
