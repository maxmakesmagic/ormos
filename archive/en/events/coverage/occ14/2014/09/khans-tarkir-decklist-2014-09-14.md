Wizards of the Coast




September 14, 2014
 










Here are all eight Clan-Pack Sealed Decks built by the victorious Community Team for their exclusive *Khans of Tarkir* Sunday Challenge.


Bjorn Andreasson
================


"I am the only Mardu player, and I think my deck is insane, honestly. With two Nomad's Outposts and two other mana-fixing lands, I can play anything in my deck fairly regularly. I opted to stay low on black cards, keeping my deck mostly white/red, so I get to have a great mana base. I have some big rares like Butcher of the Horde and High Sentinels of Arashin. The two copies of Ponyback Brigade and my copy of Hordeling Outburst seem like they are good. Morphs are especially good in Mardu, because you really want to always play guys and always want to be attacking to turn on your raid. Horde Ambusher seems especially good with all of these cheap creatures I have."

Erin Campbell
=============


"I wasn't sure what to do with my deck, honestly. I knew that Sulatai would be one of the slower clans, so I figured, "Let's get really slow!" To make that happen, I added a bunch of defensive creatures to durdle until I get where I need. Eventually, I will be able to cast Villainous Wealth, Sagu Mauler, and my other powerful cards out. I opened a Sorin, Solemn Visitor, and I had to play him. If I had to change anything, I might have decided to go Mardu with my deck. I have a number of great Mardu things in my sideboard, which might have been better with my Sorin."

Frank Lepore
============


"I am the lone Jeskai player of the team. I feel like I opened fairly well. I've got a number of bombs, including Jeskai Ascendancy and Zurgo Helmsmasherthe digital Big Z. I could have splashed either green or black into my Jeskai deck, and Big Z is one of the reasons I opted for the dark side. My deck is absolutely loaded with removal. I have eight pieces in my maindeck, so things should be clear for my Mantis Rider and other beaters. I also have Herald of Anafenza, which will really benefit from having the board clear, allowing me to churn out a number of creatures. I am almost certain that the Herald is going to be huge every time it hits play."

Mariah Pagliocco
================


"I'm not feeling too good about it. Sealed Deck at a Prerelease is one of those things where I just don't know enough to say whether or not I feel good about my deck. I know I'm in the right colors, since I opened an Abzan pack, but I don't know which of the creatures are better, or if I'm playing the wrong spells. It seems like Siege Rhino could be pretty good, and I also really like Wingmate Roc. But there are also cards like Kheru Bloodsucker. I don't know how good this card is going to be, because I only have a few cards that are big enough to trigger the ability. I justified it because I have some cards that add counters and a few prowess guys, so I'll probably get there. I definitely have a few cards that I might bring in from my sideboard, like two more Unyielding Krumars. I love Defiant Strike as a card, too, but I needed more creatures, so it had to stay in the board."

Paul Cheon
==========


"Maybe it's my love of blue that forced me into it, but I chose Temur. I like my deck, but I'm not exactly sure how strong it is. I'm not sure if you should delve into a fourth or fifth color. Every pool has like seven or eight lands that can produce more than one color of mana, but including them all slows you down quite a bit. Still, my deck had a ton of morphs, a couple combat tricks, and two copies of both Savage Punch and Force Away. I had dreams of Punching creatures away and then punching my opponents in the face afterwards. One thing I am wondering about is the number of lands a deck should play. Because there are so many morphs in this format, I think I should have been at 18 lands. I know you did that a lot back in Onslaught, and I think it might be right here, too. It doesn't feel like too much has changed in that regard."

Scott MacCallum
===============


"I am really in love with Temur. They seem like a clan that has a bunch of aggressive, above the curve, creatures, cards like Savage Knuckleblade. I opened up some pretty powerful five-color bombs. When I looked at the mana fixing I had, I felt it would be criminal not to play all of them. Besides, isn't the Sealed Deck rule to play all of your bombs? One of the biggest and best that I opened was Temur Ascendancy. It's incredibly powerful, and exactly what I want to be doing with my Temur(ish) deck. The other big card I looked at was Trail of Mystery. With all of the morphy creatures I have, I am going to be able to fetch out all of the lands I need, and I can have them attack for huge amounts. I was excited about this card when I saw Brian David-Marshall's preview article, and I'm stoked to get a chance to play it. In the end, I have an incredibly powerful deck touching all five colors with plenty of mana-fixing to make sure that it can all come into play. I have early game pressure with cards like Icefeather Aven and late cards like Zurgo Helmsmasher. I just hope I don't fall to someone playing the speedy Mardu clan."

Sean Plott
==========


Looking at what a lot of the other people seem to be doing, it looks like everyone seems to be loving the slowness. With all of the pretty colors and lands people have, theyre trying to play four or five colors, and their decks can be a little slow. My deck is straightforward and aggressive. Its pretty much black/white aggro, though I do have some of the late-game green cards to top things off. I have some of the outlast guys that grant really powerful abilities to a number of my creatures, too. If anyone stumbles with their slow, pretty decks, Ill crush their faces.

Tom Ross
========


"I opened Sultai, though I didn't really have a reason in particular for picking it. When I opened up this deck, I felt like it didn't do anything at all. This guild seemed really weird at first glance. The cards are all expensive, and there are a bunch of supporting cards that just worry about filling the graveyard, like Sultai Ascendancy. But they work surprisingly well together. You definitely need both pieces in healthy numbers to have a successful Sultiai deck, though I'm not sure what a good mix is just yet.


You need to pay attention to what you're doing in the early game with Sultai. Cards like Highland Game are pretty good for this clan, because they trade off with lots of the morph guys early on in the game, which gives you cards to use to pay dredge. You don't want to be wasting spells just to make your other cards playable. My best card was Necropolis Fiend, but it requires a bit of set up to be really good.


One important thing I think about this format is that the morphs are all good. Since there are so many good cards that you can play on turn three, the three-drops themselves have to be really good in order to actually make the deck. If they aren't, you're just better off playing the morphs and using your mana on later turns to unmorph them."
