2004 World Championships Feature Match
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 4, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New York–based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 







![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/fm20b_1.jpg)


Nassif's quest to make Magic history hit a slight snag last round when the French National Team fell to the squad from the Netherlands. This round they would be up against a rag-tag outfit from Germany bereft of any household names. The biggest name on the team name is actually only the surname. Sebastian Zink is the brother of last year's World Champion Daniel.


I asked Daniel if he gave his brother any advice prior to the draft and he waved that suggestion with a laugh. "No, no…but Kai did."


As Sebastian sat down with German National Champion Torben Twiefel, and Roland Bode the two teams inquired how the other did. Although the French had lost the Germans had won their match versus the South Koreans.


"Nice job!" teased Olivier Ruel with a cartoonish roll of his eyes. "We dropped one to the Netherlands."


Torben rolled with the punches, "I would rather beat the South Koreans than lose to the Dutch."


"It could be worse," grinned the French National Champion. "We could lose to the Germans."


And with that the draft got underway with Torben opening first.

*Mirrodin* got started with a [Viridian Longbow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow) for Torben and a [Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter) for Roland. The French took Slit Ascendant, [Goblin Replica](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Replica), and a [Skyhunter Patrol](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyhunter+Patrol) on their pass at the first pack. Zink showed Affinity with a [Chromatic Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere) and a [Great Furnace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Furnace). [Electrostatic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Bolt) for Bode was their only pick in the second pack before the French got their hands on it and Peset took a Patrol with a [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) and a Talisman going in order to Ruel and Nassif.


Peset opened a [Blinding Beam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Beam) to go along with the white Slith and Patrol already in his pile while Ruel took [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) and Nassif unearthed a [Granite Shard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard). Zink took a [Nim Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Shambler) when the pack swung around to their side and the next two guys got [Deconstruct](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deconstruct) and then [Leaden Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaden+Myr)/[Soul Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Foundry) on the wheel.


The big pick in the first round of packs came in the fifth pack when Nassif was opening. He had to take [Molder Slug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug), although he was not green to that point. With only one shot at the pack for his team he had to snag it but he passed them [Gold Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gold+Myr), [Arrest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest), and [Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter). Also big was Torben getting a [Blinding Beam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Beam) in the last pack when there was nothing else of note.



![Leonin Bola](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Leonin+Bola)

Zink opened his *Darksteel* pack and quickly settled on [Leonin Bola](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Bola) for his first pick. The French took [Unforge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unforge), [Grimclaw Bats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats) and a [Spire Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem) allowing a [Vedalken Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Engineer) to reach Roland to the amazement of the Germans. Nassif pack yielded a [Juggernaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juggernaut) and his mates took [Chittering Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chittering+Rats) and [Razor Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razor+Golem). Once again the Germans seemed confused by the French draft and did not seem to think the [Hoverguard Observer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoverguard+Observer) should have made its way around to Roland.


Peset took a second [Razor Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razor+Golem) after Ruel's [Echoing Ruin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Ruin). He also took a second [Spire Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem) the pack after that. He was solidly blue-white into Zink's deck of black and X which could be red, blue, or both. He had some middling blue cards and only a [Barbed Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbed+Lightning) in red. He committed to going blue when he was able to get a [Memnarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnarch) in the last pack of *Darksteel* that Torben opened and took a [Barbed Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbed+Lightning).


*Fifth Dawn* led off with an unspectacular but hearty [Tyrranax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tyrranax) while Roland's emerging sunburst deck picked up [Infused Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infused+Arrows). Peset grabbed a [Leonin Squire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Squire) and Ruel took a [Blind Creeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Creeper) while Nassif jumped on a [Wayfarer's Bauble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wayfarer%27s+Bauble). Roland looked to his teammates for help in choosing between [Grafted Wargear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafted+Wargear) and [Joiner Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joiner+Adept). The former is ridiculous in genera, while the latter is great in sunburst. Zink emphatically pointed to the equipment indicating that it was not something he wanted their team to face.


Nassif laughed at the third *Fifth Dawn* pack after Peset took and Anchorite and Ruel gathered up a [Vulshok Sorcerer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Sorcerer). He was playing [Beacon of Creation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Creation) in the block deck that carried him into the Top 8 of the event and he was about to take one for the team portion. The next pack drew laughs from the whole table as Ruel got another Sorcerer and Nassif took another Beacon.


He had a chance to take the [Blasting Station](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) that would recreate his Day Three deck but passed on it in favor of the much stronger [Summoning Station](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Station). Zink was happy to get a [Trinket Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) and even happier to open a [Mephidross Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire) in the last pack.

**Nassif vs. Bode: Game 1**


Gabriel kicked things off against the 25-year old Business Mathematics student by tossing back a zero-land hand. His next six contained two lands and a [Molder Slug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug) and he kept it. Bode kept his hand and made the first play with a [Leaden Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaden+Myr). He used it to accelerate into two drops on turn three -- [Vedalken Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Engineer) and [Thought Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier). "Please don't have [Granite Shard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard)."


Gab drew for his turn and there was the Shard on top. He laughed, I guess it is better than the land I wanted." He cast the artifact and passed the turn without a fourth land to activate it. He frowned at a [Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter) from the German side. "Land would have been better."


Nassif's Berserker sped in for 3 points on the next turn but was halted in its tracks the next turn by a 5/5 [Sawtooth Thresher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sawtooth+Thresher). Gab missed land number five and made three tokens with Beacon. Bode played a [Grafted Wargear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafted+Wargear) and swung with his equipped Myr. He also played a [Nim Abomination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Abomination).


Gab's fifth land allowed for a [Molder Slug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug) and Bode slumped at the sight of it. He sacrificed his Myr and picked up the equipment and handed it to the [Nim Abomination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Abomination). Gab took six from the Nim and chumped the Thresher. He followed up the attack step with an [Infused Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infused+Arrows) for four.


Gab thought quite a bit about how to play this next turn. He could [Unforge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unforge) the Wargear or Beacon for five but not both. He decided to bide his time and cast the Beacon. Bode shot the Berserker full of Arrows ad the end of the turn and took down one of the tokens before sacrificing the quiver to the Slug. He swung with both his big guys and Nassif put a pair of insects in their path. He followed up with a [Goblin Replica](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Replica).



![Goblin Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Replica)

Gab was left with only an uncastable [Rain of Rust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rain+of+Rust) after Unforging the Battlegear on the next turn and passed hoping to wait out the Thresher behind his insect tokens and [Molder Slug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug). Bode ditched the Replica and looted to an [Echoing Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) to bounce Nassif's tokens. He attacked with the Thresher and the German pulled all the counters off to drop Nassif to two when he had no blocks. After combat he made a [Soul Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Foundry) imprinting [Hoverguard Observer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoverguard+Observer).


Nassif knocked on his deck for a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) but all he found was [Krark-Clan Stoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krark-Clan+Stoker). He attacked with his [Molder Slug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug) and left Bode with an on-board kill. Bode activated his Foundry during his upkeep and sacrificed it to the Slug. He then was able to attack with his three 1/1's--Engineer, [Thought Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier), and Thresher--past the lone 2/2 for the kill.


Nassif 0, Bode 1
