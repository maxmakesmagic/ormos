### DAILY DECK


Chinese WMCQ Winning Decklist
=============================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)

Adam Prosak

From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer.


September 3, 2014
 











[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Xenagos, the Reveler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos, the Reveler)


 This past weekend, the second round of WMCQs took place around the world. Spots on all countries' World *Magic* Cup teams were won, and now there is only one spot for each country left up for grabs. If you would like to earn one of these spots, check out [the WMCQ schedule](http://magic.wizards.com/en/wmcq/schedule) and make plans to attend your country's final WMCQ for the year!




 Huachao Song will not need to make such plans, as he won his way onto the Chinese World *Magic* Cup team playing Jund Planeswalkers. This deck capitalizes on the mana acceleration and removal options available to the Jund portion of the color pie, while closing the game with one or more of the eleven Planeswalkers found in the deck. This deck is full of very versatile cards outside of the Planeswalkers as well, providing for many options while playing the deck. [Golgari Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Charm), [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), and [Bow of Nylea](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bow+of+Nylea) provide many different modes that allow this deck to react to any situation. Congratulations to Huachao Song!
