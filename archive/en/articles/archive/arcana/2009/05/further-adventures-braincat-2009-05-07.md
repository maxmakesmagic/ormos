The Further Adventures of Braincat
==================================



 Posted in **Arcana**
 on May 7, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











Remember [Braincat](/en/articles/archive/braincat-2008-03-04)? It was a playtest card that looked like this:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/arcana1000/braincat.jpg)  
That card ended up as [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) (it actually says "Brain*cut*", but everyone agrees that it looks more like a cat than a cut).


Now, we return to Braincat in a new feature called...


![Why R&D Isn't Responsible for the Art](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/arcana/184_01.jpg)  
R&D member Mons Johnsons recently needed four [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)s for a playtest deck, and kept the colloquial name "Braincat". And he drew charming pictures for the four cards, which tell a fascinating story:


![Braincat 1](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/arcana/184_02.jpg)  
![Braincat 2](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/arcana/184_03.jpg)  
![Braincat 3](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/arcana/184_04.jpg)  
![Braincat 4](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/arcana/184_05.jpg)
