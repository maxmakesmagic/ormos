Feature Match - Round 4: Desmond Ong vs Yuuya Watanabe
------------------------------------------------------


****by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw****

Singapore local Desmond Ong had to be happy with how his tournament was starting out at 3-0 with no byes. That is until his opponent; Player of the Year 2009, Grand Prix Melbourne Champion 2009 and Grand Prix Kobe Champion 2007 Yuuya Watanabe rolled a ten on two d6 and elected to play. Ong missed his third land drop, but got in early with a [Putrid Leech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) , while Watanabe accelerated his mana with double [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) . The Leech was last seen skulking near an [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) , but was replaced by a [Sprouting Thrinax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) . A [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) found another Thrinax, so Watanabe sent the first one on a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) after drawing a lowly two cards with [Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) .


Another [Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) filled up Watanabe's hand while Ong's team took the Player of the Year to 10 life. Ong dropped a [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) all over Watanabe's Chalices, but then binned his guys to a [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) . The Replacement Saprolings charged in and dropped Watanabe to 7, and then 6 when he cracked a [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) to [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) another Thrinax. Watanabe summoned [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) to his side, and [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) ed for an answer. The Saprolings dropped him to 3, but he had the [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) for the first [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) . Unfortunately, he only had a hand of six land and a smile for the second.


Ong 1 – Watanabe 0


 ***![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl10/01_R4_Ong.jpg)*Singapore's Desmond Ong takes Game 1.**** 
Watanabe had to discard on his second turn in Game 2, but not because he'd missed a land drop, he'd just been on an especially successful [Treasure Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Hunt) for five. Ong could only grin with his solitary [Savage Lands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands) in play, adding another on his second turn. Ong's turn three [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) sent a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and a [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) to the graveyard to hang with the Jace and two other land Watanabe had binned the turn before. Watanabe summoned a [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker) , and then lost a [Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) to a [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) , leaving a [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) in hand along with a land and a Baneslayer angel. Watanabe drew another Firewalker off the top and placed it next to the first, leaving his mana open to protect them from any potential [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) shenanigans.


When Ong ended his fifth turn without play, Watanabe took out a [Savage Lands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands) with a [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) , before dropping Ong to 10 with the Firewalkers and tapping out for a [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) . Ong killed it with a pair of [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) s. Watanabe tapped out for another Angel, and Ong killed that with a [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) . Watanabe drew a third Angel off the top and slammed it into play. Ong made a [Broodmate Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) and friend, but they could block exactly zero of Watanabe's attackers, and they were on to Game 3.


Ong 1 – Watanabe 1


 ***![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl10/01_R4_Watanabe.jpg)*Player of the Year Yuuya Watanabe plans ahead with Halimar Depths.**** 
Ong's turn three Thrinax fell to a snap [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) , but his second caused Watanabe to pause, shifting in his chair, before spending a second [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) on that one too. Missing his fourth land drop, Ong tried for a third Thrinax, this time landing it while Watanabe simply summoned a [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). Ong found his fourth land and aimed a [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) at the Angel. Watanabe replaced the Angel with another, this time with two mana open to help protect it. Ong pointed a decidedly non-red [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) at the Angel, scoring a second hit as the Thrinax dropped Watanabe to 14. A Leech joined the Thrinax, and a [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) pointed a [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) at an [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) , before succumbing itself to a [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) . The Leech and the Thrinax charged in, the Leech taking a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and Watanabe falling to 11.


Watanabe summoned a third [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), this time with [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) mana up. A [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) went digging for an answer, but only found a [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning). Watanabe Flashfroze it anyway, before climbing back up to 16 with the Angel and passing the turn back with nine mana open. Ong pondered his play, and went for another [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), again taking down the Angel. [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) destroyed a [Raging Ravine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) while a [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) blocked and killed the [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). Watanabe cast an [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) for 3 and cracked a [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn), dropping to twelve and receiving a warning for fetching a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) instead of an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), before activating his [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) and attacking Ong down to 8. With no cards left in hand, Watanabe could only watch as the Thrinax in turn, dropped him to 9. The Colonnade again attacked, Ong falling to 4. When Watanabe sent his land in for the last bite, Ong showed the [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) stuck in his hand, and offered the handshake with a grin.


Yuuya Watanabe defeats Desmond Ong 2 – 1.

Saturday, March 13: 5:12p.m. – Round 6: Surachaste Boonyasatian vs Yian Hsiang Chye
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------


****by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw****

While these names may seem unfamiliar to most of you out there, I recognized them as I'd typed out their desklists from the GP Trials Yesterday. Both Surachaste Boonyasatian from Thailand and Yian Hsiang Chye from Malaysia managed to win three byes yesterday playing Mythic Bant, and somehow they were both coming into this round on 13 points. Considering I would have thought draws were relatively rare for the Mythic deck, I asked them both how they ended up with their draws.


"I won Game 1 against Naya," Boonyasatian explained, "but then Game 2 we both gained heaps of life, and it went on for quite a while before I eventually lost. We didn't have much time for Game 3." Chye nodded in agreement, "In playtesting the match I was playing, I found I often lost if I overcommitted, so I tried to play conservatively." It turned out he played a little too conservatively.


Both players started Game 1 with fetch lands into turn one [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)s. Boonyasatian turned his into a second turn [Rhox War Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk), while Chye's draw was more explosive, summoning both a [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) and a [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) on his second turn.
