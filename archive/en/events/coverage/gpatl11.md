January 22-23, 2011












Grand Prix Atlanta 2011
-----------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/champ_620.jpg)Grand Prix Atlanta is over! The story headed into the weekend was the upcoming battle to determine the 2010 Player of the Year between Pro Points-tied Brad Nelson and Guillaume Matignon. The two found themselves in separate flights to start the tournament, on separate sides of the Phyrexian/Mirran war (Brad fighting for the Mirrans, Guillaume for Phyrexia), and on separate sides of the debate on who was favored in Paris. But they found themselves in the same boat Sunday, waking up late after flaming out of the main event (though Brad technically finished higher for the moral victory).


Instead, it was Owen Turtenwald and Ari Lax at the head of the pack for Day 2. Both had gone undefeated, and between the two that is a feat they have now accomplished an astonishing SEVEN TIMES! The deck they used to do it? The blue-black scourge: Faeries. In fact, the strategy was powerful enough to waltz them handily into the Top 8 matches alongside fellow Fae backer John Runyon.


But the Top 8 was a tale of innovation. Quietly dominating were the Valakut players, with three of them advancing to the semifinals as Christian Valenti, Jason Ford, and Ben Stark all won their matches. With the Faeries players knocked out, the base red/green strategy knocked out the final non-Valakut player, Jody Keith, and set up a finals between Ford and Stark. Jason felt he had the edge as his Turbo Land version of the deck gained added advantage from a blue splash. In fact, the team of Minnesotans that had created the deck for the event finished the first day of competition an astonishing 33-3-0!


The match was indeed in Jason's favor, and though the seasoned pro Ben forced a third game, Jason held on to take the title of Grand Prix Atlanta champion!

Quarterfinals – Christian Valenti (RG Valakut) vs. Ari Lax (Faeries)
--------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Bill Stark* 

Ari Lax has gone undefeated on the first day of a Grand Prix three times in his career. Atlanta marked the third such time, and his Faeries deck had proven very reliable throughout the weekend. He found himself using it to square off against Christian Valenti, a star of a number of independently run circuits outside the realms of the Grand Prix and Pro Tour.


### Game 1


Both players kicked off the match with mulligans, though Ari got to stay on six cards while Christian wound up down to five. They each kicked the game off with enchantments on their second turns, Valenti dropping [Khalni Heart Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition) while his opponent had [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). A [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) added to Lax's team, and he seemed solidly on his way to a quick victory.


A maindeck [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) allowed Christian to wipe his opponent's board, but Lax was ready with a [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), championing his [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). He followed that up with a [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique), but lost the 3/1 immediately to a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). His 4/4, however, was good, continuing to put his opponent under the gun while Lax sat back, his hand stocked with counters. A touch of mana screw didn't help Christian at all, stuck on three lands after his double mulligan with his Khalni Heart on two counters.


A second [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) from Ari to [Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) his opponent was enough to seal the deal; out of resources and unable to survive the 4/4 beatdown, Christian found himself down a game.


**Ari Lax 1, Christian Valenti 0**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/QF%20Christian.JPG)*Christian Valenti*
### Game 2


Christian opened the second game on [Khalni Heart Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition), just as he had in the first game. His opponent quickly slapped a [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) on Valenti's lonely [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), but Christian was ready with a [Terramorphic Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) he could use to find a second. When he attempted to cash in the poor man's fetch, Ari responded with [Consign to Dream](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consign+to+Dream). The instant put the Khalni Heart back on top of Christian's library, and when he resolved his Expanse he had to shuffle it back into his library.


Seizing on the fact his opponent was tapped out, Christian cast [Cultivate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate) on his turn, ramping up his mana. He was then able to resolve a [Spellbreaker Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbreaker+Behemoth), turning the table and putting Ari Lax in a tight spot. Lax couldn't counter the 5/5, and with it on the battlefield could no longer counter creatures with power 5 or greater. That allowed Christian to cast and resolve a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) he had topdecked, and Ari didn't seem pleased about the situation.


"This is real life," he said, laughing at the cruelty of the fates.


With 11 points of power on the table, Christian pressed his advantage, crashing in and fetching up lands with his Titan. "There's actually no way I can win this," Ari said, thinking about his possible outs. Proving the point, he scooped up his cards rather than try to fight on.


**Ari Lax 1, Christian Valenti 1**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/QF%20Ari%20Lax.JPG)*Ari Lax*
### Game 3


Opening on [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), Ari Lax saw a hand from his opponent that featured [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), [Spellbreaker Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbreaker+Behemoth), and double [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout). What Valenti didn't have, however, was a source of green mana. The 5/5 Behemoth went to his graveyard, and Christian spent his second turn whiffing on a green source and passing.


The game slowed down from there, with Valenti looking for green mana while Ari was happy to sit back and play lands. He cast [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) only to see his opponent had picked up two copies of [Guttural Response](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttural+Response). He forced one of the copies to the graveyard with the discard spell, but still didn't have any creatures. An [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) made its way to the top of Christian's deck, and he finally had access to green mana. But his opponent had a third discard spell, [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) again, to force the last [Guttural Response](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttural+Response) out of the Valakut player's hand.


Using the Wilds, Christian finally found himself with access to both of his colors. He attempted a [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), but had it countered by [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). Ari was up on the board state, but he didn't have any attackers and that was bad news for him. If he didn't put pressure on his opponent's life total, the fact that Christian had stumbled on mana early wouldn't matter.


A [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) was [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed, then a third [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) relieved Christian of a copy of [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) from his hand. A [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) hardcast was also countered by [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), but with the land counts rising to ridiculous heights, Ari Lax still did not have a threat. Finally a [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) came down for the Faeries player, but with his opponent holding double [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout), Ari's triple [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) start looked like it might come back to haunt him, his life low against a deck playing red.


His [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) slowly pinged Ari down, with Christian using the Fallouts to contain the table of attackers. Occasionally Lax would use a counterspell to take out his opponent's spells, but when Lax ran out of counter**Magic**, Valenti stuck a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan). That allowed him to fetch up [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) and with [Khalni Heart Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition) on the battlefield he was able to burn his opponent out.


**Christian Valenti 2, Ari Lax 1**

Gindy once again beat with his [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), dropping Jason to sixteen life, then played a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) off of a lucky third land. The turn was passed right back to Gindy after Jason played a Valakut and a [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen). The Omen wasn't long for this world, however, as Gindy quickly cast [Deglamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deglamer) on it promptly after untapping. It had appeared that no Omen shenanigans were to be had this day. Gindy then swung for five and it was back to Ford once again. Jason played his third [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore), but unfortunately neglected to play the second land off of it and passed back the turn. Gindy once again swung for five, bringing Jason to a mere eight life. Gindy tapped three lands and cast [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning), which Jason responded to with [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), countering the spell and drawing a card. The turn was Jason's and he cast a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) after mulling over his options. Gindy used [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman)'s ability, discarding a [Demigod of Revenge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge) only to find a second one.


"Got the land?" Ford asked.


"No…," Gindy joked back, "but I will."


Gindy revealed the card to the public as he drew it, showing a third Demigod! Not the land he needed. He then played a second [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), swung in, and passed the turn. Jason drew his card, and flung two cards onto the table casually, just as in game two. The card: a [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) and a [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift). That, boys and girls, would be game and Jason Ford would proceed to the semi-finals!


**Jason Ford 2, Charles Gindy 1**




Semifinals – Ben Stark (RG Valakut) vs. Christian Valenti (RG Valakut)
----------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Bill Stark* 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/SF%20Valenti%20v%20Stark.JPG)### Game 1


After dispatching Owen Turtenwald in the Quarterfinals, Pro Tour star Ben Stark (no relation to this author), found himself in a mirror match against fellow Red-Green Valakut player Christian Valenti. Christian had dispatched Ari Lax's Faeries build, meaning both were blue-black killers. "The all important roll?" Christian proposed to his opponent, indicating he felt the die roll was key to the mirror.


Winning it, Ben got an added bonus as his opponent started the match off on a mulligan. For the Valakut mirror, it was definitely a foreboding start for Mr. Valenti. Stark opened on [Khalni Heart Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition), with a [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) for good measure as a backup plan. Christian didn't have any acceleration with which to start, but did have a turn five [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) to gain 7 life and tutor up a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan).


With a [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) already on the battlefield for his opponent, Ben found his early start beginning to get overmatched by his opponent's draw. He used his Expedition to cast a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) of his own, fetching up a copy of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) of his own as well as a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). With a copy of Valakut already on the table for the Floridian, and a third as his land drop for the turn, he had turned the tables right back on his opponent.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/SF%20Valenti.JPG)*Christian Valenti*
Christian cast his [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), but Ben revealed a [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) in his hand. That meant his incoming attack from [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) would be lethal just from Valakut triggers alone, the [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) turning all of his lands into [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, and Valenti conceded.


**Ben Stark 1, Christian Valenti 0**


### Game 2


Christian Valenti had to take a mulligan again in the second game, but at least this time his opponent also had to send his opening seven cards back. They each stayed on six cards, and Christian got out to an early lead with an [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) to accelerate his draw. A [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) appeared for both players, and Stark matched his opponent's [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) with [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth). [Khalni Heart Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition) was the next accelerant for Christian, while his opponent had [Cultivate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/SF%20Stark.JPG)*Ben Stark*
Unfortunately for Christian, his opponent's accel was better. While Christian was able to build to five mana, he was short the second green he needed to cast [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command). When he passed the turn, ready to go nuts with Khalni Heart as soon as he untapped, Ben revealed his hand: [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen), and [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift). That was exactly what he needed to one-shot kill Valenti, and Christian accepted his defeat.


"That was just the absolute nut," Ben offered, apologetically. "Maybe in your next Top 8, which will be soon, you'll take it down!"


**Ben Stark 2, Christian Valenti 0**




Finals – Jason Ford (Turbo Land) vs. Ben Stark (Red-Green Valakut)
------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall* 

This was the sixth time Ben Stark had reached the Top 8 of a Grand Prix but the win has eluded him thus far in his career.


"I am good at getting here but not at winning them," sighed Stark as he and Ford sat down and went through the pregame small talk.


"This would be the second time you lost to a Boston native," said Ford, referring to Ben's loss in the finals at Grand Prix Boston a couple of years back.


"Are you better or worse than the last one I lost to?"


"I have no official comment," sidestepped Ford. "He has won a GP and I have not."


Before they began to play the competitors had a chance to review each other's deck lists. Stark pored over the innovative Turbo Land version of [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) that Ford had brought to the table courtesy of Gerry Thompson and Matthias Hunt. Ford gave Stark's list a cursory glance and began to shuffle.


"You should probably look at my deck," laughed Stark as he continued to study the deck.


"I already know what you are playing," shrugged Ford.


"I am playing a pretty standard version."


 


### Game 1


Ben sent his opening seven back and the obviously anxious Ford groaned a little.


"I hope you don't mulligan too much because you shuffle so much."


Stark kept his next six and led off the finals with [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) while Ford played [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). Ford had [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) on turn two and used it to play [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths) as his additional land. With Ford's mana tapped the coast was clear for a [Cultivate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate) from Stark netting [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/Finals_JasonFord.JPG)*Jason Ford*
[Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) for Ford was the first life change of the game and he played [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya). there was a Tectonic edge off the top and an [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) waiting on top for next turn. Stark played Valakut and cast [Harrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) but he did not have much longer to put his pieces together. Ford played [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) and played Valakut "Three you". He played another land and did another three and said "Go."


"Yeah I should have attacked you there," sighed Ford as he looked at his Oracle that was standing around doing nothing. Stark played [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and Ford used [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command).


He did six damage twice with two lands on his next turn and then remembered to attack for the killing blow.


Jason Ford 1, Ben Stark 0


 


### Game 2


Ford seemed reluctant during sideboarding and the two players discussed their previous matchup during the Swiss rounds.


"I have since talked to Gerry (Thompson) about sideboarding and he has very different ideas," said Ford when Stark was surprised that Ford seemed unsure.


"That hardly seems fair," said Stark.


"Everything he has told me about the deck has been wrong..." shrugged Ford.


"I think Gerry is the best Constructed player in the world but I should not be trying to convince you to listen to him," laughed Stark.


"I think it has more to do with how he would play the game and how I play the game," explained Ford.


Stark led off the second game with a Valakut while Ford plumbed the [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths). Both players went to three lands on turn two -- Stark used [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and Ford did it with [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore). [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) for Stark prompted a head shake from Ford. He winced as he took his turn and passed back to Stark after playing his fourth land.


During Stark's draw step he played [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique+)  and Stark revealed two [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift)s, [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), and [Verdant Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs).


"Keep em," Ford frowned.

He played a [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) on his turn and conceded to the two dozen points of damage he was going to take from Stark's fetch land next turn.


Jason Ford 1, Ben Stark 1


 


### Game 3


"I am good," said Ford of his opening seven. Stark was struggling with his decision and finally announced:


"I can't do this. I wanted to keep this hand."


"Was it the 'no green source?' " asked Ford.


"Yeah."


"Oh god!" said Ford in the tone of someone who had learned his lesson the hard way. "Don't do it."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpatl11/Finals_BenStark.JPG)*Ben Stark*
Ford led of with [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths) and sat back on his mana on turn two to [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) Stark's [Khalni Heart Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition). He untapped and played [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth). Stark played [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen), laid Valakut and passed the turn.

As it stood now he had to hope what he was looking for was in the top three cards of his library. .


Stark did some math, played [Khalni Heart Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition), and started bolting Ford with his lands.


"I think I am short..." said Stark and sure enough when the dust settled Ford was at three.


"I have the [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) in my hand and the Omens is on top of my deck," said Ford who did not want to slow roll his opponent.


Stark looked over the board and thought for a moment. "You need to have a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest). Show me a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and you win."


Ford cracked his Rainforest and was indeed able to get the green source he needed to cast the [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen). From there he had access to all the colors he needed.


Stark nodded and extended his hand to congratulate the Grand Prix Atlanta Champion Jason Ford.
