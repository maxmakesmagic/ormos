The response for this contest was overwhelming! There were over a hundred deck lists posted in the [forums](http://boards1.wizards.com/showthread.php?t=872308) of the Completely from Scratch article, and the creativity displayed was top notch. Out of a card pool of just 80 cards, there were no less than *twelve* distinct archetypes that people came up with for this challenge! Everyone did a great job of contributing, so give yourselves a round of applause (or the internet equivalent). Bravo!


 



![Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Forest)

On the original list, there were two problems. The first was that I accidentally listed [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s instead of [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s as one of the basic land types. This was corrected in the forums by our very own Kelly Digges, and so all entries that contained basic [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s were fine. The second was that [Golgari Rot Farm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm) was accidentally left off of the list of usable lands somewhere along the process between submission and publication of my article. To be fair to everyone, decks that used [Golgari Rot Farm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm) were disqualified from the contest—even though I intended for them to be used, they weren't on the list, there was no indication that they were allowed to be used, and it wouldn't be right to let some people use them, and others not.

There were other cards people were clamoring to use as well—cards such as [Elves of Deep Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elves+of+Deep+Shadow) and [Svogthos, the Restless Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Svogthos%2C+the+Restless+Tomb). The Completely From Scratch article was written to show the deck building process in its true, raw nature—and in this case, cards will be missed. I'm sure that every one of you can relate to this feeling: you've just built a deck, you've got a lot of great cards in it—and then you face an opponent who plays a card that would just be perfect, *perfect* in your deck, except you forgot about that card to begin with! That's Svogthos—it should have been on the list of available cards, but I just missed it. No deck or deck builder is 100% perfect—otherwise, everyone would be playing the same 60 cards, and nobody would be having any fun with **Magic**!


Enough preamble. Let's take a look at the twelve different archetypes that were submitted for this contest. Thank you again to everyone who submitted a deck – I read each and every entry, and even if your name didn't appear this time, there's always a fair chance that your deck will be featured in an article for my next deck building challenge!

Ttek says: *"Typically this deck will win by getting an early enabler down and then use it to discard some choice cards with dredge to get you going. Then move to some combination of recurring disruption, playing/reanimating big Trolls, or getting lots of Zombie tokens along with a Troll or Archon. Don't get too attached to the Bridges; they are powerful and can win you the game but it is often good simply because it makes your opponent start killing his own creatures. The rest of the deck can easily win off of Troll, [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void), and Archon."*


 



![Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bridge+from+Below)

Ben says: This deck does a lot of things right—it has enough early dredge enabling ([Llanowar Mentor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Mentor), [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker), [Fa'adiyah Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fa%27adiyah+Seer)) to make sure that you can be dredging by turn two. The last thing you want is to be stuck with a dredge card (or [Bridge from Below](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below)) in your hand with no way to get it to the bin.

There are also a few problems with this deck. The mana for this deck is way off – there is a single black-producing land in the entire deck that comes into play untapped, and only three black mana sources total. Even with [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker) (which can't grab Salvages), it's virtually impossible to hardcast [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) with this deck. Moreover, there are *eighteen* black cards in the deck (not counting [Bridge from Below](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below)), and you certainly want to be able to play things like [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast), [Stronghold Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Rats) and [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void) with reliability. Playing only twenty lands is also a recipe for trouble, especially with only one copy of Life From the Loam.


In addition, I think that this deck would also benefit from [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka)s. Simply put, there's no easy way for your deck to kill your own creatures (with a Bridge in the Graveyard) aside from two [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast)s. [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) would have a lot of synergy with the deck—getting rid of unwanted [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker)s/[Llanowar Mentor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Mentor)s, using [Llanowar Mentor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Mentor) tokens as -1/-1 fodder, and triggering [Bridge from Below](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below) at will. I'd also consider another good reanimator target or two as well—[Blazing Archon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Archon) and [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll) alone might not be enough when they are your only route to victory.

Bloosh123 says: *"The whole point of this deck is to quickly get dredgers in your graveyard, dredge to get reanimation targets in the yard, [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return), and the swing for game. [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker), [Llanowar Mentor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Mentor) and [Fa'adiyah Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fa%27adiyah+Seer) are supposed to get dredgers in the yard. These creatures are also used in the late game for [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) sacrifice purposes. Grave Trolls, Thugs and Imps, are for dredging, as well as [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Dakmor Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dakmor+Salvage)s. [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) is used to get more lands in your hand if you are mana-screwed. This deck likes to accumulate mana, and fast. If you have some fattie targets in your hand, use the mana and hardcast them. There are 4 different fatties for reanimation. The best one is Teneb, becuase with him out you can reanimate things in your opponent's grave also."*


Ben says: Another great effort! This deck uses [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) as a way to make sure it has enough mana in the mid-game, but I still think that 20 lands is too few to run in a deck that wants to make sure it hits four lands on turn four. Since reanimation targets are the way this deck intends on winning, I would have tried to fit in a couple more targets in this deck as well. The basic engine of this deck seems great, you just want to be sure that you don't get stuck with a ton of dredgers and dredge enablers, and nothing to win the game with!

Ben says: "I love the idea of combining [Avatar of Woe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Woe) and [Tombstalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tombstalker) as kill conditions—both are big, beefy, and evasive. I like having eight reanimation spells to go with eight major fatties, so the kill condition seems fit. However, there are a couple of problems with implementation in this deck. First, how do you get dredge cards into your graveyard? Unless your opponent has a first-turn creature you can [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast), the earliest that you'll be dredging is turn four (assuming you play a [Stinkweed Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp) on the third turn, and then it dies). Without a reliable way to get your dredge cards into your graveyard, there isn't going to be a lot of dredging going on. A card like [Stronghold Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Rats) would definitely help as a way to put your own cards from your hand to your graveyard.


Also, are there enough creatures in this deck to support [Avatar of Woe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Woe)? [Tombstalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tombstalker) should be fine because of dredging and [Edge of Autumn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn), but [Avatar of Woe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Woe) expressly needs creatures, and you only have sixteen creatures in this deck, total. You also aren't running a lot of creature removal, so you won't be able to directly put your opponent's creatures into their graveyard from play either. This deck has a solid concept, but I think that you probably would want to add green for a dredge engine, which would give you access to [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker) (as an early dredge enabler, and as another creature), [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll) (again, a creature and a dredger), and a way to play [Edge of Autumn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn) if you draw it early.

XmontyX says: *"I combined classic dredge reanimator with a [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) toolbox. First turn either [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker) or Mentor for a quick discard outlet and color fixer/thinner or accelerant. After this you can play the deck two ways. Either immediately start dredging by discarding upkeep and dredging during draw, hoping to dump reanimator targets, or play a little more control early on: fix and accelerate your mana, transmute for persecute to empty their hand. Or transmute for [Greater Good](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Good) as a draw engine with a fattened Grave-Troll once you start dredging. The kill is Grave-Troll, [Mystic Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Enforcer), Teneb, or [Blazing Archon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Archon)."*


 



![Persecute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Persecute)

Ben says: Wow, I really like everything that was put into this deck—with a full three point four tickets left to spare, XmontyX managed to cram [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute), [Mystic Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Enforcer), [Loxodon Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch), [Greater Good](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Good), [Teneb, the Harvester](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teneb%2C+the+Harvester), and [Blazing Archon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Archon) into one deck. In addition, he made it so that most of these cards could be tutored for with [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard)—which also goes to feed [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll).

That is not to say that this deck is without need of tuning. For one, there simply aren't enough lands in this deck to support a deck that needs a minimum of four lands to get going. Even with the mana acceleration, you're going to want another four lands in this deck to ensure smooth sailing. These would probably be [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s, since five [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s is not enough to reliably transmute [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard), even with [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker) to help out the mana base. I think I also would have liked to have seen one [Avatar of Woe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Woe) as a reanimation target, since this deck lacks any removal—it's just a good a kill card as any other creature in this deck, plus it gives you an out if you desperately need to kill an opposing creature on the board.

Chiefbandit says: *"I played a deck that was similar to this at Regionals. It had a good showing going 5-3. Between the [Edge of Autumn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn) and [Evolution Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Charm)s you shouldn't have land problems, and there should be enough removal to let this deck build into the real creature drops. With the ability to dredge [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void) you can handle control decks and combo decks."*


Ben says: This deck was one of the few to really feature a good amount of control cards—a suite of [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters), [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify), and [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) really works wonders for controlling the board. In addition, the mana-fixing is there with [Edge of Autumn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn) and [Evolution Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Charm). [Loxodon Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch), [Teneb, the Harvester](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teneb%2C+the+Harvester), and [Vulturous Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulturous+Zombie) are fine finishers, and [Debtors' Knell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Debtors%27+Knell) gives the deck a bit of a late game punch—especially if it is killing opposing creatures all day long.


Once again, though, there just aren't enough lands in this deck. This deck wants to get up to seven mana on turn seven, and twenty-one lands isn't going to cut that. In addition, this deck doesn't have a real way to get card advantage—all of its spells are one-for-one, so if the opponent starts recurring creatures (say, with their own Debtor's Knell) or outdrawing this deck (which gets one card a turn), this deck could run into trouble. I'd probably find a way to up the land count in this deck (maybe cut an [Edge of Autumn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn), an [Evolution Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Charm), and a [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void)) to make room for three more lands, and then consider cutting the [Bitter Ordeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Ordeal)s in favor of [Harmonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize). I think this deck would rather draw three cards than remove 1-2 threats from an opponent's deck. Otherwise, very solid—although this isn't a dredge deck, it shows off the power of the card pool available.

Suicidal Hamster says*: "I decided to avoid a Dredge strategy for two main reasons:*


*a) I thought it would help me stand out from the crowd.*


*b) I wasn't overly impressed with the selection of cards Ben chose for us to use (oh well, there goes my shot at winning this thing!). I struggled to construct a good dredge decklist without [Delirium Skeins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins) and especially Svogthos. The choice of reanimator targets was also unappealing, as the better targets are either in the wrong colour ([Bogardan Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite)) or too expensive (Akroma).*


*This deck aims to consistently play out efficiently costed creatures and beat down for the win. The Elves and [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) help me to accelerate into the likes of [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm), [Mystic Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Enforcer), and Big Dumb Elephants, and then also serve to remove blockers via [Glare of Subdual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Subdual). [Congregation at Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Congregation+at+Dawn) allows me to consistently draw the creatures I need and also serves as a tutor for utility creatures such as Saffi and the singleton Enforcer. Finally, the Guildmage can either pump out extra chump-blockers and tappers or pump my team for the last few points of damage, and the [Deadwood Treefolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadwood+Treefolk) give me some late-game staying power."*


Ben says: It's no dredge deck, but it is a pretty finely tuned machine. The deck starts out with acceleration and set-up effects ([Congregation at Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Congregation+at+Dawn), [Glare of Subdual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Subdual), [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots), [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves)) and then explodes mid-game with big fatties ([Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm), [Loxodon Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch)), recursion elements ([Deadwood Treefolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadwood+Treefolk), [Saffi Eriksdotter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saffi+Eriksdotter)) and removal ([Glare of Subdual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Subdual), [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters)). This deck is pretty much complete, and would only need very fine tuning—the most immediate change I'd make is trying to find a way to fit a third [Glare of Subdual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Subdual) in the list somehow.

DELangley says: *"My central goal for the deck was to find a reliable engine to exploit the mechanic... [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt) allows for mean, MEAN deck stacking (and [Reclaim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclaim) serves as a hella sick tutor) when a full quarter of your deck is dredgable. More chatter after the goods.*


*Ideally, the deck drops creatures steadily and is perfectly willing to trade them until its engine is intact, while serving the dredge/threshold cause in the meantime. [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) and [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify) serve to eliminate heavy threats. The [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt)s allow you to recycle both removal and tutors once the engine is running. Continue eliminating impediments until you can swing with baddies for the kill. With an appropriate sideboard (and maybe a little less budget restriction), I feel this deck can be a serious contender. Thanks, and holla with any comments."*


 



![Jotun Grunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jotun+Grunt)

Ben says: Ladies and gentlemen, we have our winner! This is the deck I'm going to start evolving next week—this deck just seems like it's got so much going on! Dredge works really well with [Mortivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortivore), [Mystic Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Enforcer) and [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) (since you get to throw them into the graveyard for free, and bring them back once something dies). It has mana fixing (bouncelands, [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker)), removal ([Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify), [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy)), card advantage ([Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) with Dredge), and best of all, an engine!

It's the engine which really drew me to this deck. If you can dredge your entire deck into the graveyard, and then play [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt), you get to put cards back on the bottom of your library in any order you want—effectively, turning [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt) into a way to set up your deck for the rest of the game. Not only is it a big 4/4 beater (that you feed with your own dredged cards), but it can ensure that you'll draw whatever you need, when you need it, late in the game.
