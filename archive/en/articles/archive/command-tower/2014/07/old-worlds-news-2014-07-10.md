Old Worlds News
===============



 Posted in **Command Tower**
 on July 10, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






I like lists. Organizing and sharing information with lists may be so frequent its banal, but I find myself drawn to them for everything:



* Rows of data in a spreadsheet list out my financial transactions, making it easy to see where things stand
* The cascade of tasks I set out to do, regularly adding more to the end than I lopped off the top before day's end
* Illustrating examples of lists using a list of bullet points in an article that begins by talking about lists

Lists are my go-to approach for making sense of the world, and encapsulating my understanding of it into digestible chunks. For example, when I wanted to see how much I was looking forward to *Magic 2015* I made a list of every card I wanted.


I was surprised to find 45 listed.

It's an eclectic mix of things for my all-commons Cube and box of Commander goodies, and it'd be far bigger if I was an avid player of Standard and Modern or didn't already have copies of returning cards like [Chord of Calling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) and [Raise the Alarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm).


While I could gush and explain my interest in all of them, there are five I'm going to summarize in a sentence each:



* [Boonweaver Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boonweaver+Giant) convinced me that [Bruna, Light of Alabaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bruna%2C+Light+of+Alabaster) is going to be my new Commander deck project for [Gen Con](http://www.gencon.com/).
* [Hot Soup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hot+Soup) will result in someone dying an unexpected and hilarious death, and I want to help make that happen.
* [Perilous Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Vault) will finally push [Nevinyrral's Disk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk) out of the average Commander deck; dealing with Planeswalkers can be important!
* [Soul of Ravnica](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+Ravnica) is the card-drawing engine I needed for my slow, colorful [Atogatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atogatog) deck.
* [Undergrowth Scavenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undergrowth+Scavenger) is a common that hits way above its rarity in multiplayer games (like, say Two-Headed Giant, which you might find at a [Prerelease](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/Prerelease-event-types-events) or [Friday Night *Magic*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/event-types/friday-night-magic) ).

Aren't lists awesome? I'd like to believe most of you agree with me, since all of last week's feedback came packed with decklists to show off the newest cards, like how John knows exactly where [Chief Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+Engineer) will go:



R&D hit M15 out of the park! I'm astonished by the amount of awesomeness! Here are my Top 5 picks for Commander:


**#5 [Resolute Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resolute+Archangel)** 


Resetting to 20 every turn is nice; resetting to 40 is even better! She's an auto-include in my [Vish Kal, Blood Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vish+Kal%2C+Blood+Arbiter) deck, where she'll make the life-for-cards theme almost painless.


**#4 [Ajani Steadfast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Steadfast)**


I've long wanted a Planeswalker that boosted other 'walkers. It just seemed like something Planeswalkers should do. I can't wait to activate Ajani's -2 ability!


**#3 [Soul of New Phyrexia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+New+Phyrexia)**


This card simultaneously frightens and excites me. One-sided board wipes are never fun for those on the receiving end, but players usually have to jump through hoops to enable them. [Soul of New Phyrexia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+New+Phyrexia) gives any and all comers easy, colorless access to one-sided table sweeping. I know I'll be running it in my [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) deck for that very reason.


**#2 [Chasm Skulker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chasm+Skulker)**


[Lorescale Coatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorescale+Coatl) is one of my favorite uncommons, and I can't help wondering if Mike Neumann was inspired by it. As a Mikey fan, I'm predisposed to like his card, but I'd be into this design no matter who created it. I haven't yet decided whether it will replace the Coatl or fight alongside it in my [Prime Speaker Zegana](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prime+Speaker+Zegana) Commander deck.


**#1 [Chief Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+Engineer)**


I'm an artifact junkie, and "Scotty" (can we please nickname him that?) is the latest addition to a long and venerable line of blue artifact enablers. [Arcum Dagsson](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcum+Dagsson); [Grand Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect); and [Muzzio, Visionary Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muzzio%2C+Visionary+Architect) would all enjoy talking shop with this fine fellow. There is a spot being prepared for him in my [Sydri, Galvanic Genius](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sydri%2C+Galvanic+Genius) deck:

I definitely agree that [Sliver Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Queen) is a little less terrifying outright than [Sliver Hivelord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Hivelord), but with Hivelord lurking behind the Queen an army of indestructible tokens is even scarier: I've used and been the victim of [Eldrazi Monument](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument) more times than I can remember. Hivelord can make that redundant.




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sliver%20Hivelord)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver%20Hivelord)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Eldrazi%20Monument)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi%20Monument)


Andrew cared more specifically about tokens and what *Magic 2015* brings to the table for them:



One of my favorite decks is [Ulasht, the Hate Seed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulasht%2C+the+Hate+Seed). The deck aims to pump out a ton of tokens to ensure Ulasht is as big as possible. There are six cards in *Magic 2015* (so far) that I'm looking to add. While [Restock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restock) and [Chord of Calling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) are reprints, I don't have any copies of them. M15 gives me an opportunity to get my hands on multiples, as both are amazing Commander staples.


[Genesis Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra) is looking to become another big staple, and I'll be sure to add it to almost all my green decks. [Soul of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+Zendikar) is mana intensive, but I think people underestimate how useful an army of 3/3s can be. [Feral Incarnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Incarnation) is in a similar vein; my tokens can convoke it early, or I can just ramp into it just fine.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Genesis%20Hydra)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis%20Hydra)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Soul%20of%20Zendikar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%20of%20Zendikar)



The biggest addition, however, is [Hornet Nest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Nest). The end-all-be-all of chump blockers, it should literally scare people out of attacking you. In a pinch, I can even send a giant burn spell its way or fire off a [Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) to get an army of angry tokens. [Gruul Ragebeast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Ragebeast), [Electropotence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electropotence), and [Warstorm Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warstorm+Surge) also help me send a ton of damage its way if my opponents aren't being very cooperative.
