![Elder Pine of Jukai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elder+Pine+of+Jukai)

In this week's article, I'm going to take the core of the deck, and rebuild around it based on reader suggestions. For instance, several players wrote in and suggested that I use [Elder Pine of Jukai](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Pine+of+Jukai) as the land-draw engine for the Sasaya deck. The thought is that a mix of arcane spells and spirits could buoy your hand to seven lands, allowing for Sasaya-flippin' good times. Fair enough â€“ but for something to go into a deck, something else has to come out.

If the land-draw engine is going to be retooled to work around [Elder Pine of Jukai](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Pine+of+Jukai) and spirits, we can safely remove the old land-draw engine. This includes [Journeyer's Kite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journeyer%27s+Kite), [Civic Wayfinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder), and [Seek the Horizon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seek+the+Horizon). In their place, we have [Elder Pine of Jukai](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Pine+of+Jukai) and [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) (both Arcane and land searching).


In order to get [Elder Pine of Jukai](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Pine+of+Jukai) working, we need to have a concentration of arcane spells and spirits. The deck already has [Carven Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carven+Caryatid), but just four Spirits aren't enough to make the engine work. I went over the list of available spirits in Standard, and decided that [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami) would be the best addition for the deck. [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami) allows you to recur your arcane spells (so far just [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) â€“ but just wait for more in a second), plus it costs only a Green to cast.


As far as Arcane spells go, the original version of Sasaya was having some difficulties with weenie rushes. [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) is a pretty efficient splice/burn spell â€“ it combos with [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami), [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach), and Elder Pine, so I can definitely find room for it in the deck. Plus, [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) can act as direct damage â€“ if you draw two, you get six points of damage (two from the first Ray, two from splicing the second Ray onto the first, and two from casting the second Ray). This is not an insignificant amount of damage, and [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami) can help insure that you end up with two [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray)s in your hand in the late game.


I also added three [Soulless Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+Revival) to the deck, along with a pair of Swamps. [Soulless Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+Revival) can go infinite with [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami), allows me to get back the extremely fragile, one-toughness [Elder Pine of Jukai](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Pine+of+Jukai), and brings Sasaya back from the dead after she takes one for the team. In addition, [Soulless Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+Revival) has a cheap splice cost, so it plays well with [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray).


Lastly, I felt bad about having [Time of Need](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+of+Need)s in the deck, but only one card (Sasaya) to tutor for. Patron of Orochi seemed like a good addition to the deck. It's a big fatty, it adds a lot of mana to your mana pool via its untap ability (letting it substitute for a flipped Sasaya in the late game), and it can Snake Offering off of Sasaya herself in a pinch.


Aside from the Kites, Wayfinders, and [Seek the Horizon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seek+the+Horizon)s, I mainly took out a lot of one-ofs of all the cards that had only one or four copies in the deck. A complete list of the changes are below, but the only card removed entirely from the deck was [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top). There were quite a few less shuffle effects left in the deck in the Spiritcraft build, so while the Top would still be useful, it wouldn't have maximum utility.

**Game 1:Mirror Lich ([Magnivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magnivore))**


Mirror Lich is on the play, and he [Stone Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain)s my second land. I have three more in hand, but hold back playing a Forest until I can get to a third mana for [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach). Luckily, he only [Demolish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demolish)es one other land, and I chain double [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) into Sasaya. He drops a [Magnivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magnivore) and swings, but I Snake Offering Sasaya to drop [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi) as a surprise blocker, killing the Vore. My Patron swings twice, and I end the game with a large [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze)


*Record: 1-0*


**Game 2: Anonymous ([Muse Vessel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muse+Vessel))**


The person I played here asked to be kept anonymous, but he was playing a version of my [Muse Vessel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muse+Vessel) deck. Unfortunately, he had a slightly sub-par draw, and gave up halfway through the match. Granted, I don't think he could have pulled it out given that he kept drawing lands and [Fellwar Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fellwar+Stone)s, but he also declined to use [Quicksand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksand)s to stop my [Elder Pine of Jukai](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Pine+of+Jukai) from dealing eight damage to him over the course of four turns.


*Record: 2-0*


**Game 3: MadDocPants (Rakdos)**

![Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jagged+Poppet)

It's only fitting that I face a Rakdos deck, given that I'll be playing one next week! MadDocPants puts on some really fast beatdown with [Jagged Poppet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) and [Gobhobbler Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gobhobbler+Rats), and I barely stabilize the board at seven life, thanks to [Savage Twister](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Twister) (which takes out both his board and his entire hand, thanks to the Poppet!). My last card in hand is [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi), and his only non-land permanent is [Seal of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire). I have seven lands on the board. He draws [Jagged Poppet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet), I draw [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray), he draws a second [Seal of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire), plays it, and kills me.

I could have cast [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) on his [Jagged Poppet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) during his draw phase, after he drew his card, since he would likely have been able to cast whatever he drew with the Poppet. At least that way I would lost only the Patron to his Poppet, instead of my entire hand. In fact, the right play there would have been to have him discard his drawn card to the Poppet via my Ray, and hoped to top-deck an answer the following turn.


*Record: 2-1*


**Game 4: Vivi2020 (B/G Tokens)**


Vivi2020 drops a ton of token creatures via multiple [Selesnya Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildmage)s, which I kill one after another with burn. I drop [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi) and [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami), and use [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami) plus [Soulless Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+Revival) to repeatedly recur [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach). [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) spliced with [Soulless Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+Revival) plus [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami) equals two Forests a turn. Before long, I have enough Forests in play to use the Patron to fuel a [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze) for twenty.


*Record: 4-1*


I realize that [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) has been pretty suboptimal, because I never have enough cards in my hand to discard lands. In addition, [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi) has been a huge help â€“ it's both a quick clock (7/7 creatures tend to be clocks) and an enabler for game-ending [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze)s.


In addition, I'm still having problems keeping small creatures down. I decide that I'd rather have [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) than [Savage Twister](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Twister). Although [Savage Twister](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Twister) offers more flexibility in the late game, it can't kill 2/2 creatures until turn 4. [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) sweeps the board of weenies a lot earlier than that. Also, since I'm running [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray), I can combine the two to kill larger creatures if need be.

The Patron gets a hit in, and then dies to Tenacioudon's [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze).

I [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze) him back for his life total, but he casts [Overrule](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrule) for two to stay alive at two life (I have the mana to pay for the [Overrule](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrule)). I drop [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami), draw [Soulless Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+Revival), and start swinging the 1/1 spirit into his newly-minted Saproling tokens, hoping to whittle away his last two life. After a couple of turns of this, I draw [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) and just burn him out for the win.


*Record: 4-1*

I [Quicksand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksand) the Swiftblade and [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) the flier. We build up for a while - I get Sasaya and [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach), he gets down [Boros Recruit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Recruit). I swing a couple of times, he swings a couple of times, and then he finally goes for it: Bluffhappy casts [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire), puts [Taste for Mayhem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taste+for+Mayhem) on it, and then tries enchant his [Boros Recruit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Recruit) with [Blessing of the Nephilim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessing+of+the+Nephilim).

He drops [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) on my Sasaya, but I Snake Offering it to the Patron, [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze) him for thirteen (Tip: You can tap Patron before the attack to untap your Forests, since he untaps himself!), and swing for his last seven life points. He casts [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) just to stay alive, and drops [Firemane Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Angel). He casts [Mourning Thrull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mourning+Thrull), and then casts [Boros Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Guildmage) and [Taste for Mayhem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taste+for+Mayhem) (on the flyer), uses the Guildmage's haste ability, and swings for seven. I use [Quicksand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksand) to kill his Guildmage, and cast [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) to kill his spirit-linked Thrull. A turn later, I draw [Soulless Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+Revival), get [Hana Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hana+Kami), and burn him out for his last life points.


*Record: 5-1*


At this point, I haven't even come close to flipping Sasaya with this new version of the deck. In fact, I've used Sasaya for two purposes in the Spiritcraft version of the Sasaya deck â€“ attacking for two, and getting out [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi) early. In light of these facts, I decide to just remove the entire Sasaya part of the deck, since the deck is working great as a [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi)/[Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze) deck. I have more threats to attack with, more direct damage, and a much more potent game against both weenie and control decks than the combo-version of the deck had.


I lose the Sasayas, Times of Need (since I no longer need to tutor for Sasaya), and the last [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam). I add in a third [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi), three [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder)s (snakes plus mana-fixing) and three [Sosuke, Son of Seshiro](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sosuke%2C+Son+of+Seshiro) (a solid beater, plus good for Snake-offerings).
