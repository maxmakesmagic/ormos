Live Coverage of 2005 Pro Tour Columbus
=======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 30, 2004 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By JUDAH ALT











What wonderful and interesting situations happened at Pro Tour-Columbus as the new sets met the old in a fresh extended environment? Surprisingly few. Overwhelmingly, the problems encountered by the judging staff involved language barriers or asking for the Oracle text of non-English cards (especially Cephalid [Illusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusion)ist).



![Kami of Ancient Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kami+of+Ancient+Law)

By now you've probably delved deep into the inner workings of the Cephalid Breakfast deck, but it had one interesting choice in [Kami of Ancient Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kami+of+Ancient+Law). The new 2/2 creature from *Champions of Kamigawa* does what [Seal of Cleansing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Cleansing) could never do: be [Exhume](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exhume)d to deal with [Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship). Also appearing in the sideboards of Affinity builds to take out [Energy Flux](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Energy+Flux), the [Kami of Ancient Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kami+of+Ancient+Law) proves its superiority over instants like [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant) with the ability to attack. You too might need to make the choice involving the 1W slot in an extended deck someday -- which is more important: the ability to attack, or the ability to destroy artifacts and be played as an instant?


[Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), perhaps the best card in *Fifth Dawn*, showed up everywhere from [Aluren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren) decks to Enchantress to [Awakening](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awakening) to the many variation on the black/green deck popularly known as "The Rock." Sean Salder even called his deck the "Witness Relocation Program" and used cards like [Vedalken Mastermind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Mastermind), [Aether Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial), and [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) to truly exploit the power of its coming into play effects.


One of the most interesting interactions I saw as floor judge involved a match where [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death) was cast with [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness)es lying in both graveyards. When [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death) resolves, all coming into play effects happen simultaneously and are added to the stack at the same time. The active player, the person who cast [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death), has his effects go on the stack first and must therefore pick targets for his Witnesses first. The non-active player's Witnesses have the added bonus of having their triggered abilities resolve first while knowing the locked in choices of the first player's targeting decisions. [Complicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Complicate)d plays like this are what truly define the pros.

Finally, Judge Paul Morris reported a complex situation during one of the matches he covered. [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) (imprinting [Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire)/[Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice)), [Sphere of Resistance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Resistance), and [Pyrostatic Pillar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrostatic+Pillar) were all on the table at the same time. [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) makes a copy of Fire/Ice which is played during the Scepter's resolution, additional costs, such as Sphere of Resistance must still be played. Pyrostatic Pillar will trigger since, as the converted mana cost of a spell is not increased by Sphere of Resistance. Unusual situations like these don't happen often, but our high-powered staff at the Pro Tour was more than equipped to handle it.
