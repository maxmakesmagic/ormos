Sunday, March 22: 9:48a.m. – Round 9: Masaya Kitayama vs Sam Black
------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

Sam Black and Masaya Kitayama finished day one undefeated with their Blue control variants. Black with Faeries, and Kitayama with Next Level Blue.


Black crushed the die roll, 11 to 5 and suspended a turn one [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) s, living up to his claim that he always borrows dice from his opponents. Kitayama had the [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) when Black tried to sneak out a [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) at end of turn, filtering out his mana with both [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) and [Flooded Strand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flooded+Strand) while Black's Visions counted down. The Visions resolved uncontested, and Black simply suspended another one and passed the turn back to Kitayama, who went in with a [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) , forcing it past Black's [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) with a [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) . The Clique sent a [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) packing, before suiting up with a [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) and swinging across to drop Black to 17. With Kitayama tapped out, Black capped the opposing Clique with one of his own, shipping a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) over a pair of [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) s, and [Venser, Shaper Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant) . Black rounded out his turn with an [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) for two. Neutralizing not only the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) in play, but the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) s in Kitayama's hand as well. Kitayama, unfazed, played a freshly ripped [Vedalken Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) instead.


Black was the next to make a move, filling up his hand with [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) , and then resolving his second [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) s. His hand now dwarfed that of Kitayama. Black then played a [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) of his own, killing off Kitayama's, and then rounded out his turn with a second [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte). Kitayama dropped the Venser on a [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) at the end of Black's turn, and the American was forced to discard down to seven after his copious card drawing escapades. With Black now the player in possession of a [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), along with his [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) for two, Kitayama played out his [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) s. The first fell to a [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) , the second making it into play next to Venser. Despite being way ahead on cards, Black was behind on the board, and pursed his lips while pondering his play. Kitayama animated his [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and sent it in alongside the Goyf and Venser. Black tried to animate a ‘Vault of his own, that that was stolen by the Shackles and Black dropped to six. On his turn, he played a [Sower of Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) , taking the now tapped [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) . Kitayama untapped his Shackles, returning Black his [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and passed the turn back, at least able to neutralize the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) if Black attacked back.


Black equipped the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) to his stolen [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) , and when he announced his attackers, the Shackles took control of the Sower, something Black didn't exactly want to attack in to. After combat, Black played a second Sower, this time taking Kitayama's Venser. Struggling to find a way to land the last six points of damage, Kitayama could only pass the turn back to Black. The game appeared to have stalled out, but Black had five cards in hand to Kitayama's two, and had a pair of Riptide Laboratories in play as well. Black played a [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) , but Kitayama countered it with a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) . A second Clique from Black revealed the last two cards in Kitayama's hand were a [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and a [Riptide Laboratory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Laboratory) .


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsin09/02_R9_Black.jpg)Black untapped, and moved his [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) to the Clique. The Clique swung in alongside Black's Sower, and was blocked by Kitayama's Sower. Before damage was assigned, Black bounced his Clique with one of his Riptide Labs, and replayed it, and filtered away his own [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) . He rounded out the turn by suspending yet another [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) s. Kitayama could only play a Laboratory of his own and end. Black repeated his previous turn, attacking in with the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte)>'d Clique and the Sower, and again bouncing his Clique to prevent anything from dying and giving Kitayama back the use of his Shackles. Black again improved his hand quality by replaying the Clique on himself.


Kitayama finally suspended a Visions of his own, but time was running out. Not only in the game but that match as well. Black kept reliving his Groundhog Day of a turn, the net result being Kitayama's life total slowly dropping and Black's hand improving. Kitayama tried for something different, moving to block the Sower instead. Before damage, Black returned his Sower to his hand, which gave Kitayama back his Venser. Also before damage, Kitayama returned his Venser to hand with his own Lab, and replayed it to bounce Black's Clique. Finally, after combat, Black replayed his Sower, taking back the Venser. I'm pretty sure the net result that turn was that nothing happened, but at least this time Kitayama didn't lose any life. Black again replayed his Clique, sending a [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) to the bottom of his deck before choosing to equip the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) to the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) .


Black bounced his Clique at the end of turn and replayed it on Kitayama, letting him keep a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) , ensuring the air was clear for his [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) s to resolve during his upkeep. At this point, there were 10 minutes left in the round, and neither player seemed especially close to winning Game 1. Finally, Black made a move that mattered. Main Phasing a Venser of his own, killing of Kitayama's and bouncing the [Vedalken Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) . Black wad finally free to attack, dropping Kitayama to 10. Black then bounced his Clique, and then sent the Shackles to the bottom of Kitayama's deck with it. Kitayama top decked and played an [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) for two, which resolved and promptly blew up the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) and the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) , letting Black go back to 10 life as well.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsin09/02_R9_Kitayama.jpg)Black animated two [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) s, and sent them in alongside his two Sowers and the Clique. Kitayama blocked one of the ‘Vaults with his own, saving it with his [Riptide Laboratory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Laboratory) but dropping to 1 life. Kitayama looked at his cards, but couldn't find a way out of this situation, and finally picked up his cards.


Black 1 – Kitayama 0


While sideboarding, Kitayama asked the judge how much time was left in the round, and looked aghast when he was informed there were only four minutes left. “Four??” he confirmed, holding up four fingers. “Four,” the judge deadpanned. “I think I've locked up at least one match point in this match so far,” Black joked, well aware that anything short of a miracle for Kitayama would end this match as a win for Black.


Sure enough both players kept their hands, Kitayama suspended an [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) s, but nothing much else was achieved. Lands arriving on both sides of the table but not much else. On turn four, Kitayama resolved a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) , but missed his fourth land drop. Black sniffed out Kitayama's hand with a Clique when the buzzer sounded, and Kitayama could only laugh as he packed up his cards.


Sam Black defeats Masaya Kitayama 1 – 0

Sunday, March 21: 11:21a.m. – Round 11: Shuuhei Nakamura vs Yuuta Takahashi
---------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

Yuuta Takahashi won two Grand Prix last year in Japan, both times playing Faeries. Standard in Shizuoka and Block in Kobe. Given the opportunity to play it again this weekend, he told me that he instead chose to play a deck that beats Faeries. Shuuhei Nakamura was Player of the Year last year, so we can agree that he too, is pretty good at this game.


Nakamura could only play a couple of [Wooded Foothills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills) and a [Windswept Heath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windswept+Heath) after his mulligan, while Takahashi had [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) to get his ball rolling. Not the kind of start Nakamura's Naya Burn deck was looking for. Takahashi hit a [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) on his first dredge, and without any kind of clock from Nakamura, was happy to get stuck into his hand, the [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) feeding his Retrace addiction. Nakamura's first play was an end of turn [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) , and then a [Seal of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire) . Still barely threatening Takahashi in any way. Takahashi finally played out a [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) , and used it to untap some land and keep his Loam engine running. Nakamura used [Incinerate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) and a [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) to kill the Planeswalker, but was still doing little of consequence.
