Analyzing the Spark Split: Week 1 MPL Metagame
==============================================



 Posted in **Competitive Gaming**
 on May 15, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











Last weekend marked the debut of divisional play within the *Magic* Pro League (MPL), [streamed on Twitch](https://www.twitch.tv/videos/423526411). From May 11 to June 8, MPL members play Traditional Standard, and top performers in each division will earn a guaranteed slot in Day Two of Mythic Championship III. Due to these stakes, MPL members were incentivized to bring their best decks.


Players may choose new decks every week. In this first week of league play, the recently released *War of the Spark* set had a huge impact on the metagame—but not in the way I had expected. With [Tamiyo, Collector of Tales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+Collector+of+Tales) and [Blast Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blast+Zone) being enormous upgrades to Simic Nexus, I had expected many MPL members to embrace that archetype. It was the most-popular deck at the StarCityGames.com Open in Richmond for a reason.


But no one wanted to play the deck that had a target on its back. Instead, they were all one step ahead, with decks or cards that matched up well against Simic Nexus. The most-played *War of the Spark* cards in the [submitted decklists](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/competitive-gaming/mpl-spark-split-week-1-decklists-2019-05-09) were [Teferi, Time Raveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler) and [Dovin's Veto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin%27s+Veto), which are both excellent at stopping [Wilderness Reclamation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilderness+Reclamation)–fueled [Nexus of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nexus+of+Fate)s or [Root Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Root+Snare)s.

The white-blue upgrades mainly found their way in Azorius Aggro, Esper Control, and Esper Midrange. The Esper Midrange deck, sometimes called Esper Hero because it's built around [Hero of Precinct One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Precinct+One), wasn't popular before *War of the Spark*. But its popularity swelled, partly because its namesake card can exploit the gold nature of Teferi and [Dovin's Veto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin%27s+Veto).


Popular Decks
=============


![Popular Decks](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/CG20190515_MPL_Week1_Popular.jpg)


White Aggro was most popular in Week 1 overall, with ten players bringing [Dauntless Bodyguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Bodyguard), [Legion's Landing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Landing), and [Benalish Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benalish+Marshal). These decks are capable of blazingly fast starts and could have run over Simic Nexus if any had shown up. Moreover, some of the deck's weaknesses were shored up by *War of the Spark* additions.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Law-Rune+Enforcer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Law-Rune+Enforcer) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=460940)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=460940)
[Law-Rune Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Law-Rune+Enforcer) found its way into all White Aggro decks, often as a four-of. It can tap down large blockers like [Wildgrowth Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildgrowth+Walker), improving the matchup against Sultai Midrange. Also, it has 2 toughness, which means that it can survive [Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler). [Law-Rune Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Law-Rune+Enforcer) generally took slots previously occupied by [Snubhorn Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snubhorn+Sentry), [Hunted Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Witness), and/or [Tithe Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe+Taker).


[Gideon Blackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Blackblade) is a sticky threat that dodges [Kaya's Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaya%27s+Wrath), which is huge against Esper Control and other decks that rely on board sweepers. As a result, [Gideon Blackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Blackblade) was often a two-of in the main deck or a two-of in the sideboard, usually in slots previously occupied by [Unbreakable Formation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unbreakable+Formation). William Jensen won both of his matches with two copies in his main deck.

Yukuhiro, who has made Mythic Championship Top 8s with such unusual cards as [Angel of Glory's Rise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise), [Bone Picker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Picker), and [Hollow One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+One), never disappoints when it comes to bringing something spicy. His take on Bant Aggro—it's certainly not your usual Bant Midrange deck—features eight one-drops in [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and [Pelt Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pelt+Collector) and then branches into a proliferate theme fueled by four copies of [Huatli's Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huatli%27s+Raptor).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vivien%2C+Champion+of+the+Wilds)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivien%2C+Champion+of+the+Wilds) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Huatli%27s+Raptor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huatli%27s+Raptor) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Venerated+Loxodon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venerated+Loxodon)
[Huatli's Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huatli%27s+Raptor) can produce a 3/3 [Pelt Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pelt+Collector) on turn two, boost the damage potential of [Chamber Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chamber+Sentry), or add loyalty counters to your planeswalkers. And with [Teferi, Time Raveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler), you can bounce [Huatli's Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huatli%27s+Raptor) to do it all over again.


But there's more. If you control [Vivien, Champion of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivien%2C+Champion+of+the+Wilds), then you can cast [Huatli's Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huatli%27s+Raptor), put its proliferate trigger on the stack, and convoke [Venerated Loxodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venerated+Loxodon) in response. (You need to hold the control button on *MTG Arena* to respond to your own abilities.) The end result is that all creatures you tapped, including [Huatli's Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huatli%27s+Raptor), would gain two +1/+1 counters.


[Venerated Loxodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venerated+Loxodon) fits the deck perfectly because if you convoke it via [Emmara, Soul of the Accord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emmara%2C+Soul+of+the+Accord) and [Growth-Chamber Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth-Chamber+Guardian), then you benefit from both of their triggers, allowing you to go both tall and wide. This battlefield presence, along with [Chamber Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chamber+Sentry) to ping down 2/1s, should yield a good matchup against White Aggro decks. Meanwhile, the matchup against Esper Control is greatly helped by the three-mana planeswalkers from *War of the Spark*. Yukuhiro's deck is a true masterpiece.

Grzegorz Kowalski also enjoys spicy decks, having won Grand Prix Lyon 2018 with his novel Red-Green Eldrazi deck. The Dimir Control deck he submitted this week, in which nearly half of the nonland cards stem from *War of the Spark*, shows that white is no longer a necessity for control players.


Indeed, [Enter the God-Eternals](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enter+the+God-Eternals) and [Tyrant's Scorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tyrant%27s+Scorn) help deal with early creature rushes (reducing the need for [Kaya's Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaya%27s+Wrath)), and [Liliana, Dreadhorde General](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+Dreadhorde+General) and [Ugin, the Ineffable](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ugin%2C+the+Ineffable) can lock up the game (reducing the need for [Teferi, Hero of Dominaria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Hero+of+Dominaria)). What's more, staying two colors unlocks [Blast Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blast+Zone) as a piece of interaction that doesn't take up any spell slots.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Augur+of+Bolas)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=God-Eternal+Kefnet)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Eternal+Kefnet) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils)
With 31 noncreature spells, Kowalski can exploit [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas), [God-Eternal Kefnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Eternal+Kefnet), and [Narset, Parter of Veils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils). [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) hits 78% of the time; Narset is 95% to hit per activation; and Kefnet can turn an [Opt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opt) on your opponent's turn into a huge value play.


Week 2 Predictions
==================


The first week of any new Standard format is usually dominated by aggro decks. With its abundance of white and red one-drops, this MPL debut week was no different. For Week 2, I expect the share of aggro decks to drop as players will gravitate toward adequate board sweepers. I'm also interested to see if Yukuhiro's and Kowalski's decks will catch on.


This was basically the first week to figure out the new cards, but the three-mana planeswalkers really impressed me. This was most evident in the match between Shahar Shenhar and Brian Braun-Duin. [Teferi, Time Raveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler) did it all: bounce your own [Oath of Kaya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Kaya), nullify your opponent's [Dovin's Veto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin%27s+Veto), or cast [Thought Erasure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Erasure) during your opponent's draw step. And [Narset, Parter of Veils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils) was essentially [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time) that preemptively stopped [Chemister's Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chemister%27s+Insight). For Week 2, I anticipate a surge in the number of [Narset, Parter of Veils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils) and a corresponding downtick in [Chemister's Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chemister%27s+Insight)s.


Finally, I'm excited to see if anything in Week 2 can top Glogowski's brilliant play.
