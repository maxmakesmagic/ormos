Has Pedro finally lost it? It's hard to say. [Ninja of the Deep Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours) is a dangerous creature and this deck has several ways to frustrate its opponents. It can [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) for several nasty targets and it even has the Troll-[Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship) lock. [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) is everyone's favorite double-off-color utility creature but rarely is [Thornscape Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornscape+Apprentice) also on hand for anyone who would think that this deck is normal.


I'm guessing the plan is to pose a whole bunch of different questions in the hopes that there's something there to stop everyone, then use the sideboard to replace the parts of the deck that aren't appropriate with new parts that are. While last year's Extended champion is forced to cheer from the sidelines, his friend Tiago Chan has taken the deck into the second day.

When you see the mana base this deck might look like Balancing Tings, but Bulger has something else in mind: Dragons. Lots of Legendary Dragons. He's going for the full nine mana and the instant kill off four copies of Kokusho - all you need is mana, and that's what most of this deck is. [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) brings him up to seven copies of his key spell and [Tainted Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact) can stop when it finds either of them. The deck might look weak, vulnerable and inconsistent on first glance but when almost all your cards provide more than one mana, getting to nine is not as hard as it sounds as Bulger rode the Dragons all the way to Day Two.


Then there's the other Dragon deck…

While [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) players pulled all sorts of drawn-out [Dredge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge) tricks, Avri Rahamim decided to take the direct approach: Take those lands and throw them at his opponents' head. He even used [Sprouting Vines](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Vines) and [Kodama's Reach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) to fetch even more lands to throw to [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) which he searched out with [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven). The deck is very rough around the edges. He looks like he could use far more 'creative' mana to do the same things as his Tog-wielding rivals, several more cards for [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) purposes and a way to punch [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) through resistance more effectively. The deck might even find a way to run Togs. It's certainly a different approach to using one of the most important Extended cards to come out of *Ravnica*.

Just don't let him talk you into taking the deck seriously. Objectively, the deck is terrible. You're spending lots of mana doing nothing in particular, your mana base is hideously slow and you have little chance of beating any of the top strategies. You never know what you're going to get with [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), and torturing your opponent with the shuffling and searching problems the deck has is part of the fun. The one thing you most certainly shouldn't expect is victory. Playing four colors gives the decks plenty of tools but Extended is a faster format than this. In Standard the deck is actually quite good, and it sent multiple people into the Top 8 of the Last Chance Qualifier.

Dark Confidant is a great addition to almost any black deck that has creatures and can hold down its casting costs, so Kreher figured why not add it to [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids)? The sideboard gets a lot of great versatile cards while the maindeck works together towards one goal. The sad truth however is that [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) was never on the same level as the top-tier strategies and while the deck has received help, it is not enough - you're far too dependant on a fragile plan, with [Engineered Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague) out of the sideboard the only hope against several aggressive decks and little way to accomplish much if your opponent can kill [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) or you fail to draw it. Even worse, [Life](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life) from the Loam threatens to invalidate the deck's core strategy even if you can attack it with four copies of [Withered Wretch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch).

"Let's see, I like [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter), and I like [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)… how about both!"


Why not, indeed? This list is essentially a Scepter deck with Tog thrown in - once again, all hail *Ravnica* dual lands. [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant) is an excellent way to make sure the coast is clear, but Scepter decks don't tend to have enough extra cards to make Tog the kind of thing worth splashing for. The new [Life](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life) from the Loam decks are finally teaching us what a real [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) can do.
