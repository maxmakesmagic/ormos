Glissa Control
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 10, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






The StarCityGames.com Open Series in Des Moines last weekend produced some great **Magic** action and decklists—including this one from Jeff Hoogland. [Glissa, the Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glissa%2C+the+Traitor) is a card players have been trying to break since its release, and Hoogland made Standard deck builders everywhere a little happier by cruising into the Top 8 with a Glissa-fueled control deck!

The core interaction with Glissa is alongside artifacts that destroy creatures. If [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) or [Perilous Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr) happen to destroy a creature—as they often tend to do—you can immediately return it to your hand. Of course, if you play plenty of strong artifacts, that *also* means you can bring those back as well. [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) and [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) lead the pack as powerful artifacts this deck can close out the game with.

The deck isn't just Glissa-or-bust, either. Five planeswalkers and an assortment of removal spells ensure this deck can just as easily lock down a game and then finish off with a [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine). It's this kind of versatility that propelled Hoogland to the top.

If you've been waiting for a dedicated Glissa deck that can go toe-to-toe with the top decks in Standard, you won't want to miss this one! Check out the decklist below.
