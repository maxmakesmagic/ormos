The April 17, 2017 Update
=========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on April 17, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on April 17, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by many congratulations. [Well, two](#8). But that's a lot for the Update.


What You Might Have Missed This Weekend
=======================================


1. [*Magic: The Gathering*—*Amonkhet* PrePrerelease](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fLCOGj3sm50&feature=youtu.be) | LoadingReadyRun

Nothing will prepare you more for your own Prerelease than 8 hours of hot, hot Pre-Prerelease action. I mean, it's not the 10 hours that my turn on the *Kaladesh* Pre-Prerelease clocked in at, but that's probably the best for your productivity this week.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/fLCOGj3sm50>


1. [The *Magic* Art of John Avon: Perspective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qblqF-CDzE0&feature=youtu.be) | TheMagicManSam

As always, if you love art—particularly if you love the art of John Avon—you should watch this 9-minute video from YouTube's pre-eminent *Magic* art video making guy.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/qblqF-CDzE0>


1. [Top 15 Cards from the Last Day of *Amonkhet* Previews!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VqLyndWWnJw) | Strictly Better MTG | Dev

Still digesting the last few previews from Friday? Dev too. So he takes a look at the final day of previews and ranks the 15 best of the rest.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/VqLyndWWnJw>


After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [Brewing *Amonkhet*](https://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/brewing-amonkhet?src=wizards) | MTGGoldfish | SaffronOlive

It wouldn't be a new set without a mountain of brews from SaffronOlive. From Zombies to cycling to aristocrats to [Fling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling), there's a bit of everything in this article. Except [Panharmonicon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panharmonicon). That might be a sin I cannot forgive him for.


1. [Draftaholics Adds *Amonkhet*](http://www.draftaholicsanonymous.com/p1p1-amonkhet/?v=7516fd43adaa)

I always enjoy this little game—you pick one of two cards, that helps rank all the cards in the set, and you can see how those cards rank in the set as a whole. It's fun, it's simple, it's a touch addictive, and it actually provides a ton of data on *Amonkhet* Limited impressions. Wins all around.


1. [*Amonkhet* Superlatives](http://www.hipstersofthecoast.com/2017/04/amonkhet-superlatives/) | Hipsters of the Coast | Rich Stein

In high school, I was voted "Most likely to cure cancer." While I guess there's technically still time, the odds are looking increasingly slim, unless Wizards of the Coast opens a cancer research division. What I'm saying is, high school superlatives are fun but not particularly binding. Similarly, these *Amonkhet* superlatives are pretty entertaining and worth your time, even if none of them cure cancer.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Empty Hand of the Gods](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/empty-hand-of-the-gods/) | ChannelFireball | Zvi Mowshowitz

During previews, one of the synergies we discussed was that of [Noose Constrictor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noose+Constrictor), Honored Hydra, and [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) mimicking the old power found in [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel), [Roar of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm), and, well, also [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper). Over the weekend, renowned deck builder Zvi Mowshowitz put pen to paper and concocted the following hard-hitting decklist.


For more information on the deck, you should really read [his description and primer](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/empty-hand-of-the-gods/). But if you just need some incentive to build along these lines, check out this ultra-aggressive decklist from a master.
