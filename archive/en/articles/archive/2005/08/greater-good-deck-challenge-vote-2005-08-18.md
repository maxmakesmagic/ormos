Greater Good Deck Challenge Vote-Off!
=====================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 18, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author_pic_bennie_smith.jpg)
By Bennie Smith




Bennie Smith began playing Magic in 1994 and started writing about it shortly after. A Virginia State Champion, he enjoys few things better than winning at tournaments with home brews. Bennie has a weekly column on StarCityGames.com. He also recently published The Complete Commander. Follow him on Twitter, on Facebook, and the occasional Commander games on Magic Online under the handle "blairwitchgreen." 







I was originally going to post five decks this week, and five decks next week for you to vote on for the [Greater Good Deck Challenge](/en/articles/archive/randyb-reveals-more-mirage-2005-08-04), but next week we've got something else going on, so we're showcasing all ten semi-finalist decks for you to vote on right here, right now. It was tough, with ultimately over 150 decklists to pour over. I managed to cull it down to about 30 decks and from there it was excruciatingly difficult to pare it down to 10. There are a lot of clever deckbuilders out there, and if yours wasn't selected, don't worry: you could very well have been close and we may do this again not too long down the road if the readers want it back.

**Why vote for Deck #1?** Because of the incredibly cool [Battlefield Scrounger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlefield+Scrounger)/[Greater Good](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Good)/[Verdant Succession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Succession) combo, drawing your deck and going nuts before dropping the Chieftain, Plating him up, and swinging for the stands. Sure, it's not going to happen every game, but when it does it's going to be spectacular!

**Why vote for Deck #2?** Because it's a Vanguard deck with a crunchy, old school Bloom Drain taste, sacrificing permanents and doing weird things that the individual cards weren't meant to do. How much mana can we squeeze out of [Inner Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inner+Fire)? How much damage is [Spiraling Embers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiraling+Embers) going to deal? You know you want to find out. The Birds of Paradise avatar is perfect here, allowing you to run the critical mountains while giving you green mana for your engine spells.

### Getting Inside Mark Gottlieb's Head for Prismatic


Dabbling in Prismatic was fun last week, and I thought it might be even more fun to dabble with the Prismatic decks Mark Gottlieb posted last year (see [last week's article](/en/articles/archive/prismatic-comedy-al-2005-08-11) for the links). Kill 'em All seemed a bit too brutal and nasty, so I decided to give his Prismatic Singleton deck a try.


The one problem with MaGo's decks is that they're pre-banning, which means they're chock full of no-no's. Thankfully, MTGO's deckbuilder is pretty good at pointing out what's wrong with your deck, and I quickly found the problem children. Spoiled brats, all of them, so I gave them the boot and replaced them with kids who know the meaning of the word fun:


* [Bribery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bribery) became [Overwhelming Intellect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overwhelming+Intellect)
* [Eladamri's Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eladamri%27s+Call) became [Genju of the Realm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genju+of+the+Realm)
* [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) became [One With Nothing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+With+Nothing)
* [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) became [Jushi Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jushi+Apprentice)
* [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) became [Miren the Moaning Well](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Miren+the+Moaning+Well)
* [Sundering Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan) became [That Which Was Taken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=That+Which+Was+Taken)
* [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) became [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) (okay, not exactly fun but quite handy)

Yes, I picked [One With Nothing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+With+Nothing) purely for silly style points, because as far as I know MaGo's deck isn't exactly built around discarding your hand. Then I realized that I would probably have [One With Nothing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+With+Nothing) in every single opening hand, or top deck it when I was desperate for an answer card, and then it would cease being cute and silly. So I actually replaced [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) with [Choice of Damnations](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choice+of+Damnations), which I think is perfectly in keeping with the spirit of MaGo and could actually do something useful.


Hopefully when or if Mark reads this, he'll approve of my (admittedly casual) changes!
