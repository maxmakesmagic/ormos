Huntin' Heads and Takin' Names
==============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 12, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar











In discussing the cards for this first week of Onslaught previews, Aaron conceded that I had the “weakest” card. Maybe so, but my card has the coolest name. Thragg? [Hystrodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hystrodon)? Puh-lease. Let’s talk about the [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter)!

![Headhunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Headhunter)I am immensely happy that a card finally has the name [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter). [Witch Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witch+Hunter) arrived very early in **Magic**’s lifespan, with [Bounty Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bounty+Hunter) and [Treasure Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Hunter) coming much later. I hadn’t even realized I missed [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) until it was sitting there in front of me, and now I wonder how I refrained from outrage all these years without it.

The question is: How do I *use* [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter)? I must use it; The name alone compels me to do so. The fact that it’s actually affordable (both in mana cost and as an uncommon) only seals [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter)’s pudgy, zombie-stealing body into future decks of mine.

Let me break down [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) for a moment and tell you what I see in terms of possibilities. I will be talking Type 2 here--the Type 2 where *Invasion* has left us in its multicolor wake--because when allowed a default, I default to Type 2. Keep in mind, too, that any decks I post are merely thought experiments. Any “real” Type 2 decks will have access to the rest of *Onslaught*.

To start things off... Why would I want to play [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) *without*  morph?

First of all, as I just said, it’s affordable. ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) is reasonable even for a 1/1. Just look at [Werebear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear), [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats), [Waterfront Bouncer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waterfront+Bouncer), [Mogg Maniac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Maniac), or [Beloved Chaplain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain) as examples of 1/1s for two mana that belong in plenty of decks. [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) may not be an offensive powerhouse, but it’s just fine for the price.

Second, if it actually makes it to your opponent, Headhunter can cause some hefty disruption. [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats) is great because it usually provides a 2-for-1 card advantage--it nabs a card right away via discard and then it requires another card to kill it. Given enough time and a way to remove blockers, [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) can turn out to be a much better deal than 2-for-1.

Ah, deck idea: Try [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) in combination with other creatures that do cool things when they deal combat damage, using either red burn or blue bounce to get rid of blockers. Intuitively, I like the idea of bounce best since [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) can then strip the bounced creatures away from an opponent’s hand. Besides, bounce is a more reliable way than burn to remove potential blockers.

Finally, [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) is a Cleric. This fact might seem irrelevant now, but trust me that when *Onslaught* is released you will care a lot about creature types. (as a sidenote, this also may seal *Onslaught* as my all-time favorite set ever.)

Deck idea: Use [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) in a Cleric deck. Unfortunately, [Caves of Koilos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caves+of+Koilos), [Vindicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vindicate), [Spectral Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Lynx), [Death Grasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Grasp), and [Gerrard's Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gerrard%27s+Verdict) will no longer be in Type 2 to spruce up white/black decks. But [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary) and [Tainted Field](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Field) are a fine starting point, and the combination of [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) and [Confessor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confessor) is kinda cute.

Okay, so I might find a way to use [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) even without morph. But this morph business is even more interesting.

Why would I want to play [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) as nameless, colorless, typeless critter?

First off, I’ve already noted that [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) is fairly anemic on offense. As a 1/1, he’s also very fragile. Sometimes--usually late in the game--I might want a 2/2, not a 1/1. Or perhaps I see an opportunity to end the game early by taking advantage of a manascrewed opponent. Or maybe my opponent has dropped a [Caltrops](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caltrops) which would otherwise nullify [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter). Whatever the case, sometimes it is nicer to have a beefier creature that can deal and take a bit more damage.

Deck idea: Use [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) in a fast, beatdown black deck. Headhunter will serve as disruption in its normal form and can add to the beatdown in its other form. Simple, straightforward, and maybe a little deadly.

Second, as the above deck at least partially demonstrates, Protection from black is an ability worth noting these days. In fact, [Phantom Centaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur) might be one of the premier creatures in Type 2. Oh, I know that Protection from black doesn’t help against black’s removal like [Innocent Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood), [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict), and [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate). But it does thwart mainstays like [Nantuko Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade) and [Faceless Butcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher). When an opponent is using pro-black creatures, it often downright sucks to have a [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) on the table. In these cases, a colorless critter sounds pretty good.

Finally, the Odyssey block has turned discarding into a strategy unto itself. Madness, flashback, and threshold have made creatures like Headhunter at best less effective and at worst a liability. If your opponent drops a first turn island followed by [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) and a [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) or two, you may just want to keep [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) a colorless, boring creature. It’s nice to have the option to do so.

Okay, now the tricky part: Why would I want to pay [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter)’s ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) morph cost?

The big reason, and it holds true for all morph creatures, is deception. By dropping your nameless 2/2, you make an opponent very nervous whenever that creature attacks, blocks, etc. This “bluffing” is most effective when you use more morph creatures than simply [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter). If you have several morph creatures of varying size and ability, every combat situation is a hand-wringing nightmare for your opponent. Using creature removal like [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) is risky for an opponent too, since you might well decide to Morph your 2/2 into a 6/6 Demon as easily as a 1/1 Cleric.

The good news here is that [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter)’s morph cost is so low that if it does manage to slip by blockers, you can easily morph it into a hand-stealing fat man when your [Snapping Thragg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Thragg) might otherwise be mana-tied to its colorless form.


Deck idea: Use lots of morph creatures of varying size to spook opponents.

Admittedly, Aaron may be correct that [Headhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headhunter) lacks the pizzazz of [Exalted Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exalted+Angel) or [Hystrodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hystrodon). It may not wreak as much combat havoc as [Snapping Thragg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Thragg). It also won’t be quite as surprising as Randy’s card tomorrow (oooo... foreshadowing). But it *will* hit the table before all of those losers and steal their heads before they can ever get played.
