Your Number's Up
================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 7, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







Greetings, laboratorians! [Several weeks ago](/en/articles/archive/lab/brought-you-letter-w-2013-08-19), I challenged you all to come up with decks built around cards with the same collector number. This was by far the toughest contest I've done, but Johnnies are nothing if not creative, and I still received a plethora of entries. Cutting it down to just a few decks to highlight was difficult, but in the end, it was these six that truly piqued my interest. 


### Truly Titanic


 Our first deck comes from Silas Waltzer, who chose the numbers 72 and 146 for his entry. He uses [Mad Prophet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Prophet) and [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) to discard large creatures like [Sundering Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan); [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan); and [Keiga, the Tide Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keiga%2C+the+Tide+Star), then reanimates them with [Recurring Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recurring+Nightmare) and [Stitch Together](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitch+Together). Additionally, each of these creatures has an ability that triggers either when it enters the battlefield or when it dies, making it even more profitable to repeatedly sacrifice and bring back creatures with [Recurring Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recurring+Nightmare). 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Mad Prophet | Recurring Nightmare |


[Necrotic Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Ooze) can function as a [Mad Prophet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Prophet) or [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) if one dies, and [Blast from the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blast+from+the+Past) and [Feast or Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feast+or+Famine) can be used either as removal or to create a token to sacrifice. [Wild Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Research) can be used to search up either one of the dual-purpose removal spells or [Recurring Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recurring+Nightmare). 


 Finally, [Reap the Seagraf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reap+the+Seagraf) can make a token for you to sacrifice even after being discarded, and [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites) can disrupt your opponent while allowing you to discard your reanimation targets.

### Gotta Draw Cards


 Jens Mueller decided to use just one number, building a mono-blue deck around the number 42. His list is built around activating [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension) thanks to a bevy of card-drawing opportunities. [Kami of the Crescent Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kami+of+the+Crescent+Moon) will guarantee a quest counter on each of your turns, and with [Inspiration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiration) you can get one on your opponent's turn as well. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Archmage Ascension | Kami of the Crescent Moon |


[Alchemist's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alchemist%27s+Apprentice) and [Runed Servitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runed+Servitor) can block to preserve your life total and draw you a card at the same time. Combine two copies in any combination to put a counter on the Ascension. 



[Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) and [Erratic Mutation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erratic+Mutation) can be used to get rid of problem cards, and [Knacksaw Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knacksaw+Clique) can attack, use its ability to give you another card to play, and then be available to block with on your opponent's turn. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Runed Servitor | Knacksaw Clique |

 The deck rounds off with a number of singleton win conditions which can be searched up once [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension) is online.

### Getting Aggressive


 Brian Froehlich sent in a straightforward but surprisingly solid white weenie deck using the numbers 23 and 26. This deck caught my eye due to the fact that's it's a list I wouldn't be surprised to see at a local [FNM](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/fnm) if the cards were all legal in Standard. 


 A number of solid two-drops, including [Knight of the Holy Nimbus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Holy+Nimbus), [Order of the White Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Order+of+the+White+Shield), and [Longbow Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Longbow+Archer), are backed up by token makers [Icatian Crier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Crier) and [Spectral Procession](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession). A few [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring)s round out the deck, giving it some versatile removal.

### Bouncing Jellies


 Cameron Stewart sent in a creative deck built around abusing enters-the-battlefield effects with [Sunken Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunken+Hope), using the numbers 37 and 41. When combined with [Man-o'-War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Man-o%27-War) or [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept), [Sunken Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunken+Hope) can present your opponent with a nearly unstoppable wall of bounce effects. Most decks can't manage playing more than two creatures in one turn, and even if your opponent can do that, you're not only tying up two of his or her creatures but a significant amount of mana as well.

[Inner-Flame Acolyte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inner-Flame+Acolyte) gives you a bit of extra aggression if you need it, giving itself or one of your other creatures +2/+0 and haste each time you cast it. [Archaeomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) plays much the opposite role, increasing your grip on the game through card advantage. 



[Inspired Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspired+Sprite) helps make sure you have the cards you need, and it can be untapped by your repeated casting of [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) or [Archaeomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer). [Minamo Sightbender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minamo+Sightbender) also triggers the untap ability and can be used to make a small creature unblockable before [Inner-Flame Acolyte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inner-Flame+Acolyte) boosts its power. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Inspired Sprite | Archaeomancer |


[Careful Consideration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration) and [Mind Spring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) make sure your hand is always full of cards, and [Convolute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Convolute) can stop your opponent from destroying [Sunken Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunken+Hope) or otherwise disrupting your plans. [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) and [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) can help you maintain control of the board, and each works quite well with [Archaeomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer), the latter effectively giving you another, albeit more expensive, [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept).

### Don't Tread on Me


 Our final deck, and my personal favorite, comes from Reggie Sauls, who used 31 and 117 to create a combo deck based on [Ink-Treader Nephilim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ink-Treader+Nephilim). Ink-Treader is one of my favorite cards, and has a crazy amount of combo potential. In Reggie's deck, one of the main angles of abuse is [Dominate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dominate). With it, you can easily gain control of every creature on the board at once. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Ink-Treader Nephilim | Dominate |

 The other avenue to victory is even more creative. With [Donate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate), you can give [Ink-Treader Nephilim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ink-Treader+Nephilim) to your opponent. Since [Donate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate) targets your opponent as well as the Nephilim, the creature's ability won't trigger and force you to give away all your stuff. Once your opponent has Ink-Treader under his or her control, you can target it with Char. The Nephilim will make a copy for every other creature and player, and your opponent will take 2 damage for each of those copies. So long as there are at least six other creatures on the board, your opponent will be taking 20 damage or more. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Donate | Char |


[Cerulean Wisps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerulean+Wisps) can be used to draw a huge number of cards for just one mana, and [Vanish into Memory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vanish+into+Memory) can potentially let you sculpt your ideal hand from almost your entire deck, depending on how much power is on the board. 



[Silkwing Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwing+Scout) can attack for damage, fix your mana, or a little bit of both, making it much easier to cast the four-color Nephilim. [Myr Turbine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Turbine) can put more creatures on the board for the Char win, [Arcane Denial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Denial) can stop your opponent from messing with your combo. Finally, [Comet Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm) can kill a few troublesome creatures or finish off your opponent if the Char combo doesn't quite get there. It can also be used as a board wipe in a pinch by targeting Ink-Treader.

| --- | --- |
| Vanish into Memory | Silkwing Scout |
