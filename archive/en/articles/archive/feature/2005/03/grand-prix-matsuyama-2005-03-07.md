The **Magic: The Gathering** Grand Prix tournament series is an international circuit of large-scale Qualifier tournaments featuring cash prizes and multiple invitations to Pro Tour events.


### Tournament Format And Schedule


The format for Grand Prix—Matsuyama is *Kamigawa* Block Sealed Deck + Booster Draft. All DCI Limited rules and **Magic** Floor Rules announced on October 1, 2004, along with all subsequent updates, will be enforced. .For the latest edition of the DCI and **Magic** Floor Rules, please refer to the [DCI Rules](http://www.thedci.com/docs) web site.


* **Friday, May 13**: Registration from 17:00-21:00. Players who cannot attend registration will be allowed to register on Saturday morning between 08:30 and 09:00 for an additional fee of ¥500.
* **Saturday, May 14**: Tournament begins at 09:00. The Top 64 players from Day 1 advance to Day 2. (In the event Grand Prix—Matsuyama has more than 800 players, the Top 128 players from Day 1 will advance to Day 2.)
* **Sunday, May 15**: Play begins at 9:00. The Top 8 players from Day 2 will play 3 rounds of single-elimination competition to determine the Grand Prix—Matsuyama champion.

### Tournament Details


* **Tournament Structure**  

 The structure for Grand Prix—Matsuyama is modified Swiss-style (50-minute rounds, with the number of rounds determined by the number of participating players), with the Top 64 players advancing to the Sunday finals for prizes and invitations. (In the event Grand Prix—Matsuyama has more than 800 participating players, the Top 128 players will advance to Day 2.) On Day 2, there will be a Booster Draft (consisting of 2 Japanese *Champions of Kamigawa* boosters and 1 Japanese *Betrayers of Kamigawa* boosters) and 3 Swiss rounds of competition, followed by another draft with 3 additional Swiss rounds, to determine the Top 8 players. After performing another draft, the Top 8 players will face off in single elimination play to determine the Grand Prix—Matsuyama champion
* **Tournament Byes** (*Byes are not cumulative through the season or year.*)  

 Byes for Grand Prix—Matsuyama are based on the 2005 Pro Tour Nagoya professional standings and DCI ratings as of February 9, 2005. Please refer to the Grand Prix—Matsuyama [bye list](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=grandprix/matsuyama05/byes,,en) for details. Byes may also be earned through a Grand Prix—Matsuyama Trial tournament.
* **Head Judge**  

 Masayuki Kunimitsu (Level 3/Japan)
* **Prizes**  

 The Top 8 previously uninvited finishing players at Grand Prix—Matsuyama will each receive an invitation to Pro Tour London (July 8-10, 2005). If a player who wins an invitation to Pro Tour Londonat Grand Prix—Matsuyama has already qualified for an invitation, the invitation shall be extended to the next-best finisher. Previous qualification may occur through a Qualifier tournament, through performance at a professional tournament, or by being an invited DCI player (valid on the date of the official rank announcement for the appropriate professional tournament).  

 Grand Prix—Matsuyama will also feature the standard main tournament prize schedule as well as a special Amateur prize schedule. The Amateur prizes will be paid to the highest-finishing amateur teams *in addition to any winnings those teams receive from the main tournament prize schedule* . All prizes are in U.S. Dollars.

* **Amateur Status**  

 A player is considered to have amateur status as long as he or she has 0 lifetime pro points. If a player has ever been awarded ANY pro points from a Grand Prix, Pro Tour, or World Championship event, that player no longer has amateur status, may not participate in the Amateur Championship or Junior Super Series tournaments, and is not eligible for amateur awards at Grand Prix or other tournaments.
