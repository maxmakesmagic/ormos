Nicol Bolas, Archenemy
======================



 Posted in **Savor The Flavor**
 on July 21, 2010 










Welcome to Multiplayer Week! Finally, a week that has more than one player in it.

  
Nicol Bolas knows all about being an [*Archenemy*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/TCG/ProductArticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/archenemy/productinfo). Even before we saw him reemerge last year in [planeswalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=planeswalker) card form, the draco-villain had millennia of devious schemes and world-spanning plots under his belt (although he dislikes his reptilian waist being referred to as a "belt"). 

Furthermore, he knows all about leading a [*Planechase*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/productarticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/planechase/productinfo), having set his unswerving sights on nothing less than control over the entire Multiverse. Wherever and whenever he planeswalks, countless surrender their wills, their lives, and sometimes their wills again.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/stf/stf100_bolas.jpg)  
So in honor of Multiplayer Week, I thought I would concentrate the miscreant flavor of Nicol Bolas into an *Archenemy*-ready deck, complete with a set of hand-selected schemes from among the four *Archenemy* game packs. 

I built the thing. I marveled at its cruelty. And then I built three more decks to run against it. This is a tale of Nicol Bolas and the three well-meaning gallants who tried to stand against him.

Building the Decks
To procure cards for this experiment, I ransacked both my collection at home and the contents of my somewhat overflowing desk at Wizards. When building, I considered no format other than multiplayer, no deck construction rules other than flavorful fiat, no banned list other than what I had available. As the decks came together I chose to make them singleton (no more than one of any card except for basic lands), but only because I had so many goodies that could fill up the decks. 

The "hero" decks are loosely based on the shards of Bant, Jund, and Nayaâ€”roughly the "good guy" shards that lent heroes to oppose [Nicol Bolas, Planeswalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nicol+Bolas%2C+Planeswalker) in the *Shards of Alara* block storyline. (Grixis was firmly under the sway of Nicol Bolas, and Esper was so obsessed with their dwindling supply of etherium that they ignored Bolas's influence there until it was too late.) I say they're based "loosely" on those shards because they definitely draw cards from beyond the plane of Alara; I used cards from *Alpha* through *Rise of the Eldrazi* and M11, but used them to embody the *themes* of those shards. 

The Elspeth / Bant Deck
Elspeth, the errant knight who claimed Bant as her adopted homeland, is the central figure of this deck. The green-white-blue deck is full of knights, clerics, the exalted mechanic, and a generous helping of angels and their righteous wrath. And ... [Novablast Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Novablast+Wurm). Because ... my desk had a [Novablast Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Novablast+Wurm) on it! I tried out a few M11 cards in here, such as the Akroma-reminiscent [Sword of Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Vengeance), the ally-friendly [Temple Bell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Bell), and the outright awesome [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan).

The Ajani / Naya Deck
Ajani's quest to discover his brother's killer led to a face-to-face conflict with Mr. Bolas himself, so I had to have an Ajani deck. As this hep cat hails from the shard of Naya, I made a mana-ramping red-green-white deck chock full of beasts, behemoths, and feline warriors. As I put it together it gained a "swarm and [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)" subtheme, featuring a few M11 debuts in the form of [Mitotic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mitotic+Slime), [Sylvan Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger), [Overwhelming Stampede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overwhelming+Stampede), [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), and, of course, [Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate).

The Kresh / Jund Deck
[Sarkhan Vol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol) was Jund's planeswalker-in-residence at the time of Bolas's plot, but ultimately, Sarkhan sided with the mighty dragon. That left Jund without a planeswalker hero, so Kresh, the warrior-chief, leads the black-red-green Jund-themed deck. Here you'll find a deck full of savage creatures, savage warriors, and, of course, the apex predator of Jund: dragons. I threw in [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), [Obstinate Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth), and the most brutally furious of the M11 Titans, [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan).

The Nicol Bolas / Grixis Deck
And now for the big daddy: the blue-black-red Nicol Bolas deck itself. Although I built it with flavor in the forefront of my mind, I'd be lying if I said I didn't know it would be powerful. Indeed, *power**felt flavorful* for the Nicol Bolas deck. It felt *right* for it to be full of old-school, multiplanar, ridiculously unfair cards. Besides, it's going to be three on one, right?

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/stf/stf100_3Cards4.jpg)  
Sarkhan makes his appearance here in the form of [Sarkhan the Mad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+the+Mad), the dragon-worshipper having succumbed to Bolas's rule. I threw in the new [Hoarding Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoarding+Dragon), [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), and [Time Reversal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Reversal) for some M11 love. This deck also features a copy of Evil Presents, Wizards' 2008 holiday [promo card](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/arcana/97). It's a card given out as a "thank you" to Wizards' employees and business partners, and feels like a delightfully evil trick for an archenemy Nicol Bolas to pull on an unsuspecting (or, heck, even a suspecting) hero.

Board-sweeping effects, reset buttons, card-drawing, creature stealing, and reanimation. All felt like fair game for Bolas. Which meant, of course, that it wouldn't be a fair game.

The Players
I needed three victimteers from the audience. My fellow Creative Team writers Jenna Helland and Adam Lee reported for multiplayer duty, and Kelly Digges chimed in for a fourth. We commandeered a meeting room (conference rooms at Wizards Corporate are often full of people playing **Magic**) and we divvied up decks. I hadn't actually played *Archenemy* without *being* the archenemy before (go figure), and Kelly was game, so he took the Nicol Bolas monstrosity and began shuffling schemes. Jenna took the angelic Bant deck, Adam took the swarming Naya, and I shuffled up the barbaric Jund. We shuffled up the *Planechase* planes (one big stack of all the planesâ€”that's how I like to roll) and we were ready to begin.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/stf/stf100_players.jpg)  
The Game
The planar chase begins at the [Academy at Tolaria West](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+at+Tolaria+West), a dull place for the early game, yet a scary place to roll on if you like your hand. Since the archenemy always starts, Kelly (Nicol Bolas) begins his turn, flipping over his first scheme. We heroes have dodged a bullet already; it's [Which of You Burns Brightest?](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Which+of+You+Burns+Brightest%3F) On turn 1, the answer is nobody. Kelly plays a land and passes it back to us. (See [this page](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/feature/91) for more on how you play **Magic** with *Archenemy*.)

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/stf/stf100_tolariaArt.jpg)  
It's the heroes' turn. Adam (Naya), Jenna (Bant), and I (Jund) all play lands. Jenna (Bant) casts [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), kicking off the Bant deck in style. 

It's at this point that we decide we need to figure out how our *Planechase* planeswalking will work with teammates. We're unaware of any ruling on this, but after a brief discussion, we decide that the costs for planeswalking go up for the team as a whole, but that any of the heroes can pay for a planeswalk on their shared turn. When we roll, we have to designate who's performing the planeswalk, in case it matters for the chaos roll or other effects, but any of us can pay the mana, if applicable. We decide that we can't, however, share mana to pay for a planeswalking roll. (See [this page](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/feature/51) for more on how you play **Magic** with *Planechase*.)

That settled, we decide to stay at the [Academy at Tolaria West](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+at+Tolaria+West). In retrospect my hand was slow enough that I probably could have done with a free new seven, but we're all a little gun-shy about hitting the chaos effect that could badly re-randomize our hands. Back to the archenemy.

Kelly flips over his scheme for the turn. It's [My Wish is Your Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=My+Wish+is+Your+Command): a disaster. Bolas gets to look through our hands and cast one spell for freeâ€”and after seeing [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant) but no white sources in Adam's hand, he chooses Adam's [Rampaging Baloths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Baloths) instead. Bolas has no green sources, and suddenly he's able to make a free 4/4 every turn. So, of course, Kelly plays and cracks a fetch land, going to 39, getting himself two 4/4 Beast buddies in addition to "his" 6/6 trampler.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/stf/stf100_turnTwo.jpg)  
14 power of green beasties. Nice. Turn. Two.

Our turn. Jenna comes to our partial rescue, plopping down an [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) off the [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), getting rid of the [Rampaging Baloths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Baloths)â€”but not its buddies. Adam and I play lands, suddenly regretting the slowness of our hands. I decide it's time to get out of this do-nothing plane, and start planeswalking. After a couple of rolls, we get to another Dominaria location, the [Isle of Vesuva](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isle+of+Vesuva). It doesn't have a helpful immediate impact, but we have a chance to faux-"legend rule" Kelly's Beast tokens if we hit the chaos. We're out of mana, so it's back to Kelly.

Kelly untaps and flips up his scheme: [Look Skyward and Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Look+Skyward+and+Despair). The tokens! The horrible, horrible tokens! Next time someone asks me to look skyward, I'm just going to skip itâ€”all I end up doing is despairing. At least Bolas isn't getting double dragon action, because [Isle of Vesuva](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isle+of+Vesuva) specifies "nontoken." Kicking us while we're down, Kelly casts [Bone Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Shredder) and shreds Jenna's [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). All of us, including Kelly, miss that he's supposed to get a token copy of [Bone Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Shredder), much to our detrimentâ€”he would have been forced to kill one of his tokens with it! Kelly-Bolas attacks Jenna mercilessly, taking her down to 12 with the illicitly-gained Beast tokens.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/stf/stf100_hippodrome.jpg)  
We get our turn and play lands. We spend the turn planeswalking our hearts out, trying to hit the chaos to off the Beasts. No dice. 

Kelly takes his turn, skipping the echo on his Shredder, then flipping up [Roots of All Evil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roots+of+All+Evil). Holy *crap* with the tokens, Bolas! Now your Grixis deck controls seven *green* creatures! Kelly sends the Beasts at Jenna again, taking her down to 3 after a [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) activation, and sends the Dragon token at me for 5. Kelly tries planeswalking now, attempting to get away from a place that could do some serious [Isle of Vesuva](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isle+of+Vesuva) damage to his name-sharing token army. Conclusions
The Multiverse is, in essence, multiplayer. It's a vast struggle of many, many planeswalkers and other beings, their conflict spanning times and worlds. The path of honor is a difficult one, and victory is by no measure assured. The momentum of thousands of years' worth of cruel plots and hoarded resources can overwhelm even the most resolute coalitions of right-minded heroes, even when those heroes are planeswalkers with powerful spell-weaponry at their fingertips. 

Would I play these decks again? With some tweaks, yes. I think the heroes can still win this uphill matchup with a bit of courage, some better-quality disruption, less do-nothing creatures, and maybe a freaking [Leyline of Singularity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Singularity). This day clearly goes to the villain, but we all succeeded in a wayâ€”we all spent an hour playing multiplayer in a conference room.
