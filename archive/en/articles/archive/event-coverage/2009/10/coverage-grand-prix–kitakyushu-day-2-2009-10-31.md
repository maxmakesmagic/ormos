Sunday, 10:50 a.m. – Drafting with Martin Juza
----------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*

 


Martin Juza is a man who needs no introduction.


Except that sentence.


Sharing a pod with, in fact sitting two seats downstream from, his POY race rival Yuuya Watanabe, Martin Juza was at the Japanese player’s whim. Juza opened his first pack and was immediately faced with a decision: [Hideous End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+End) or [Felidar Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Sovereign)?


"If this had been GP-Melbourne, before people knew that black/red was the thing, I might have taken the End. But now, the Sovereign is definitely the right pick. It might have even been the best pick back then."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit09/draftingWithJuza_01.jpg)*Martin Juza knows what the thing is.*
He took a [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) over a [Murasa Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murasa+Pyromancer) in the second pack.


"Geopede might just be the best common in the format."


He followed that up with some solid cards for his budding red/white deck, adding [Journey to Nowhere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Nowhere), [Torch Slinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torch+Slinger), and a [Goblin Shortcutter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Shortcutter) to his stack. It appeared, through the lack of black cards, that he had definitely made the correct decision about avoiding the [clear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=clear)ly overdrafted black. His solid bodies continued to come, including [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), [Kor Sanctifiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Sanctifiers), and a [Kor Aeronaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut)s. He even managed to snag a very late Windborn charge. As he took it, he looked up at me with a look in his eyes that effectively said, "Unreal."


After the first pack, Juza had ten cards that were going to make his deck, which is truly unreal. It appeared he was in the right seat for a red/white drafter and had made the correct decision to avoid the absent black cards.


His second pack started out a little less impressive than his previous. The only real playable card in his open was a [Zektar Shrine Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zektar+Shrine+Expedition), and he wasn’t happy about having to first pick it. There was a [Stonework Puma](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonework+Puma) in the pack as well, but, without and Blademasters and having passed on the Pyromancer earlier in the draft, it seemed he was avoiding the ally route. His next pack was fairly dry as well, providing him nothing more than a [Spire Barrage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Barrage) that would be pretty weak in his heavily white deck.


Then the gold hit. [Bladetusk Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bladetusk+Boar), [Kor Skyfisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Skyfisher), [Goblin Shortcutter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Shortcutter), and another [Spire Barrage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Barrage). He was going to get one of these cards back, more than likely, and they were all right at home in his very aggressive deck. Ultimately, he took the nigh unblockable Boar. He picked up another [Torch Slinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torch+Slinger) soon after, as well as a second [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx). By the end of the second pack, he effectively had a functional deck. The third pack would be about filling in holes and upgrading.


Unfortunately, the packs seemed to dry up quickly.


"Did the guy to my right move into red/white or something," Juza would comment after the draft?


The first three picks provided him with a [Kor Aeronaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Aeronaut), [Adventuring Gear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adventuring+Gear), and an [Inferno Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Trap) that he took over a second Geopede for his removal-light deck. After those picks, he passed two consecutive packs without a red or white card. He managed to snag a third [Kor Sanctifiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Sanctifiers) after the two dry packs, but they went right back to providing him with a whole lot of nothing. It was good for him he had a good deck after the first two packs, because the third certainly didn’t really help matters.


After the draft, I watched him build and we discussed a few of the cards he had chosen or passed up. The first thing he mentioned was his... love... for [Adventuring Gear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adventuring+Gear).


"This card is really terrible. I’ve never understood why people love it so much."


Well that was to the point.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit09/draftingWithJuza_02.jpg)*Can you spot the Adventuring Gear?*
"You want to be spending your early turns playing lots of creatures, not playing cards like this. People even play it in Sealed when they aren’t really playing creatures until turn three or so. What are you going to do? Play a creature on turn three and then equip it instead of playing a 4-drop on the following turn? That’s a good way to lose. I’ve never played it in a Grand Prix or Pro Tour. Until now, I guess."


With his massive amount of one- and two-drops, the Gear might actually be quite good at filling in the little one-mana holes his cheap creatures might leave for him in the mid game.

Nimana Sell-sword.


Unexpected.


When his second pack contained little of note except an [Inferno Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Trap), I didn’t really think he could surprise me with his pick.



![Lorthos, the Tidemaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lorthos%2C+the+Tidemaker)

Touché, salesman.


Apparently, Asahara *really* wanted to go blue/black. Honestly, I can’t blame him. The combination of [Surrakar Marauder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surrakar+Marauder)s , [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern)s, and the tempotastic removal spells the colors offer make it a terribly powerful combination. Not one to shy away from keeping his options open, Asahara snagged an [Oran-Rief Survivalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) to leave the green/black ally avenue open should he need to escape down it. At this point, the blue had more or less dried up. The black was close behind. This left him staring at a three-headed monster after the first pack. He had no removal, and could easily be either a black/blue, blue/green, or black/green deck still. It would be up to his second pack to make the decision for him.


His first two packs contained a couple of [River Boa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Boa)s, which are among the best creatures green hs to offer. His third pick presented him an interesting choice. Already showing his desire to go with an ally build, he could have taken an [Umara Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umara+Raptor) out of the pack and decided on blue/green. The Looter is a bit weaker than he is in other sets, mostly because players landfall is contrary to the best use for the Looter: turning unwanted lands into spells. With *Zendikar*, there’s no such thing as an unwanted land, so his hands are a little tied. All that said, he’s still a two-power creature for three, making a fine addition to a curve with a potential bonus of an ability.

He referred to it as “monster house,” which I thought was awesome.
