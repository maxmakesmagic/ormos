Drafting With Branco Neirynck
=============================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX LYON 2015](/en/events/coverage/gplyo15)
 on November 1, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











Branco Neirynck, who would lead the Belgian team at the 2015 World *Magic* Cup, had already made the Top 8 at two Grand Prix events this year (in Copenhagen and Utrecht). Now he was looking to add a third. At the start of the second draft of the day, he was sitting at an 11-1 record, which meant that he was likely to make it to the Top 8 if he drafted a deck that would be able to win at least two of his next three matches.

Neirynck took the best card out of the first two packs, and then solidified himself in Blue/Red Devoid with [Spell Shrivel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Shrivel) and [Nettle Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Drone).


Afterward, the red seemed to try up, and he hedged his bets with [Sheer Drop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sheer+Drop) (which works well with [Halimar Tidecaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Tidecaller)) and [Fortified Rampart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortified+Rampart). "I got the [Sheer Drop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sheer+Drop) pretty late, and [Fortified Rampart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortified+Rampart) is good for the white-blue archetype," Neirynck told me after the draft. "So after the first pack, I could go blue-white, but [Akoum Firebird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Firebird) is insane and I had passed a [Roil Spout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roil+Spout), so if I would have a chance to go for red then I would still do that."

Neirynck opened a relatively empty booster with [Kozilek's Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Sentinel), which could potentially push him back toward red.


His third pick was a tough one. "I was still not 100% sure I would go red," Neirynck said. "In blue-red, I think [Nettle Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Drone) and [Benthic Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benthic+Infiltrator) are close in power level. [Nettle Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Drone) is probably a little better, but I already had a [Murk Strider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murk+Strider) as a processor, and there was still a chance I would end up in blue-white, so I took [Benthic Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benthic+Infiltrator)."


A fourth-pick [Kozilek's Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Sentinel) allowed him to commit to red, and then he was presented with a loaded booster. "[Herald of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herald+of+Kozilek), [Nettle Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Drone), and [Benthic Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benthic+Infiltrator) would all be good for my deck. I took [Herald of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herald+of+Kozilek), which I like especially if it can ramp into cards like [Ruin Processor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruin+Processor), but in the end I think my deck would have been better with [Nettle Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Drone) instead. This was the only choice from my draft that I regret in hindsight."



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gplyo15/LYO_draft_neirynck2.jpg)


While that booster could have been an indication that blue-red was wide open, it was not to be—he only got a [Vestige of Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vestige+of+Emrakul) and a bunch of filler cards from the rest of the pack.


At the end of the second pack, Neirynck was solidly in Blue/Red Devoid. He had plenty of creatures but lacked noncreature spells which he would have to prioritize in the last pack. And with two [Kozilek's Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Sentinel), a [Nettle Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Drone), and a [Herald of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herald+of+Kozilek), he would have to favor colorless cards as well.

Was Neirynck tempted by the shiny expedition [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) that he opened? "No," he said. "[Vile Aggregate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vile+Aggregate) makes my deck so much better."


Fair enough. Unfortunately for Neirynck, his fifth pick and onward were lackluster, so he didn't look thrilled when he sat down at the deck construction table. This was what he built.

The last cards that Neirynck cut from his deck were [Salvage Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salvage+Drone), [Boiling Earth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boiling+Earth), [Ondu Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ondu+Champion), and [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery).


When asked how he liked his chances, he wasn't confident. "My deck is bad because I don't have a lot of evasion or tricks. Just a bunch of ground creatures. I didn't see a single [Outnumber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outnumber) or [Touch of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Touch+of+the+Void) in the entire draft! I will probably have to draw my [Akoum Firebird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Firebird) to win. Or [Clutch of Currents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+Currents) plus [Halimar Tidecaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Tidecaller). If I do that, then I may be able to get a 2-1 with my deck. Otherwise, it will be tough."


At the time of writing, Neirynck had just won his first match … without drawing the aforementioned cards. A turn-five 9/9 [Ulamog's Despoiler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%27s+Despoiler) did the trick. He now needed just one more win to reach yet another Grand Prix Top 8.
