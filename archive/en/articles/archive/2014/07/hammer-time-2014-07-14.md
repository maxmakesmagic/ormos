### DAILY DECK


Hammer Time
===========



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)

Adam Prosak

From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer.


July 14, 2014
 







This deck has a little bit of everything. Plan A is to accelerate out a [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) effect, preferably on the second turn via [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) or [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual). [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) has a huge effect on most games, stifling opposing mana bases often filled with dual lands and utility lands.


![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Koth of the Hammer)
Plan B is to play an efficient threat, many of which are capable of winning the game on their own. [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny), [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), [Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer), and [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) are each big threats that can close the game quickly when unanswered. Given the dedication to [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) and acceleration, each threat needs to be capable of winning the game on its own. These creatures have been selected to do so.



Finally, there is a suite of disruption. [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Skred](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skred), and [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) are all excellent removal spells, and [Skred](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skred) and [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) in particular have a great interaction with [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). As for noncreature disruption, [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) makes its way into the main deck so the sideboard can contain many different ways to interact with opposing strategies. [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity), [Shatterstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatterstorm), [Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb), and [Combust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Combust) provide some very hateful cards for popular strategies in Modern. Besides, you haven't lived until you've cast [Combust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Combust) on your own [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) for lethal damage!
