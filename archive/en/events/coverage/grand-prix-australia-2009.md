Mackintosh Makes It
===================




July 8-19, 2009












2009 Australia National Championship
------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/aunat09/The_Team.jpg)Playing in his first ever National Championships, Jamie Mackintosh stormed the Top 8 with his Green/White Elves Combo deck, and will lead the Australian National Team at the World Championships in Rome later this year. Joining him after a heart-breaking loss in the finals with his 5 Color Blood deck, will be fellow newcomer Ian Wood, and top 8 veteran Hugh Glanville, who played Faeries. Rounding out the team as the alternate is another frequent top 8’er, Jeremy Neeman, who also piloted Green/White Elves Combo. Over in the public events area as I type this, 2007 National Champ Steven Aplin is battling in the finals of Australia’s biggest ever PTQ with Pradeep Widawa-Pathirana. Naturally, we have the top 8 decklists from there as well!

Quarterfinals: Garry Wong v Jamie Mackintosh
--------------------------------------------


*by Pip Hunn*

Garry Wong has been a feature of Australian Magic for years, well-known through the country for his colorful character and tight play. He’s achieved further notoriety this weekend by getting stuck in an elevator at 1am after the event Hotel was evacuated due to a fire alarm.


Jamie Mackintosh is a newcomer to the high-level Magic scene, having not played in any events larger than the Regionals he used to qualify for Nationals. Jamie is piloting the Elfball combo-aggro


Mackintosh led off with a [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), followed quickly by a [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid). Wong had no early plays as Mackintosh accelerated into a [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), rapidly fetching out more men. Wong played out an [Anathemancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anathemancer) that traded with the [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), and followed up with a [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang). Mackintosh powered out a [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force), drawing 5 cards and restocking his hand. Wong cast [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) on the [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid)s, but Mackintosh just continued to play out men. When he got a second [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force) onto the board and Wong didn’t draw any answers, both players moved onto game 2.


Mackintosh – 1 Wong - 0


An early [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) from Mackintosh was [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse)d, only to be replaced by a Llanowar Elf. Rangers of Eos powered out a [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) and an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary). Wong used a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) to hold off attacks for a turn, but didn’t have any mass removal. Mackintosh implacably continued to play small men. A [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force) hit play, refilling Mackintosh’s hand. A second [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force) drew nine cards. He unleashed multiple Elvish Archdruids, overrunning Wong in short order with his plethora of elves.


Mackintosh – 2 Wong - 0


Mackintosh mulliganed his first hand. He was happier with the second, and Wong led play. Mackintosh had a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) on his turn, which was matched by a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech). Mackintosh dropped an Elvish visionary and passed. Wong swung and dropped a second Leech. His third turn play was a [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) into an Elvish visionary, followed by a [Windbrisk Heights](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights) hiding a [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender).


Wong dropped a [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) and swung in with his team, pumping both Leeches once no blockers were declared. Mackintosh fell to 5. Wong passed the turn and Mackintosh drew. Wong stopped Mackintosh, saying he had effects in upkeep. A judge quickly issued a warning to Mackintosh, making sure that both players give the opportunity to respond to plays. Mackintosh drew, and Wong [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile)’d the [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel), leaving a duo of Elvish Visionaries staring down two Leeches and a Ram-Gang.


Mackintosh cast a [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) and considered what he wanted to fetch.  

Wong chivvied his opponent along, and Mackintosh fetched two Forge-Tenders and passed the turn. Wong’s entire team attacked. The Ranger and a Visionary leaped in front of one of the Leeches, which Wong pumped to trade. The remaining Visionary stood in front of the last Leech, the Ram-Gang getting through to drop Mackintosh to 2.


Mackintosh played a Forge-Tender, a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel), and an Elvish Archdruid. Wong cast [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) on the Archdruid and attacked, with the Forge-tender blocking the Ram-Gang and the Sentinel chumping the Leech. Mackintosh cast a second Forge-Tender, a Mirror entity, and a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) before passing.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/aunat09/T8_wong_mackintosh.jpg)*Jamie Mackintosh swarms past Garry Wong.*Wong cast a second Ram-Gang and passed, unable to profitably attack. Mackintosh summoned an [Oversoul of Dusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversoul+of+Dusk) and passed, feeling more secure with the powerful creature on the board. Oversoul was invincible against all of Wong’s creatures and non-Path removal. Wong had no plays, holding his team back against the impending onslaught.


Controlling a Mirror entity and having 5 mana open, with three men that couldn’t be blocked by Wong’s creatures, Mackintosh went to declare attackers, swinging in with his entire team. Wong blocked the men he could, with a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) jumping in front of a Forge-Tender, leaving the Oversoul and the Forge-Tender unblocked. [Mirror Entity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) made everything a 4/4, leaving Gary with no creatures. A [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) came down post-combat for Mackintosh.


Wong had no action on his turn, but a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) on Mackintosh’s attack step to tap down his men. Mackintosh simply waited and tried again next turn. Wong played a [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout), but the Forge-tender saved the day.


Jamie Mackintosh defeats Garry Wong 3 - 0




Quarterfinals: Ian Wood v Aaron Nicoll
--------------------------------------


*by Pip Hunn*

Nicoll is playing a Merfolk deck of his own creation. He started nationals 2-3 and managed to make a 7-0 record from there in to the top 8. Wood was undefeated day 1, and was the first person practically guaranteed in the top 8. While shuffling, Nicoll accidentally flicked one of Wood’s cards onto the table. The table judge quickly warned Nicoll, and they continued.


Both players mulliganed their first hand. Wood led the play with a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech), answered by a Merfolk Sovereign. The [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) attacked unblocked, and was joined by another. Nicoll mirrored the play with a second Sovereign, but his men were unable to block Wood’s attack with two pumped Leeches. Nicoll played out with a [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher), which Wood countered with a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), bouncing one of the Sovereigns. Nicoll played a [Stonybrook Banneret](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonybrook+Banneret) and passed. Wood [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast)ed the remaining Sovereign, and Nicoll scooped his cards up.


Wood 1 – Nicoll 0


Nicoll started with a [Silvergill Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvergill+Adept), while Wood had no 2-drop to match. Nicoll swung in with his Adept, then laid another without playing a third land. Wood untapped and passed, taking 4 from the next swing. Wood still didn’t have a third land, losing both his men to wood’s end of turn [Jund Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm). Wood dropped a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), cascading into a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to Nicoll’s head. The Elf swung in for 3, dropping both players’ life totals to 14. Nicoll remained stuck on 2 land as Wood played a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and attacked with his Elf. Nicoll dropped a desperate [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher) as a blocker, but was overrun by the incoming men. Too far behind, Nicoll conceded and moved to game 3.


Wood 2 – Nicoll 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/aunat09/T8_wood_nicoll.jpg)*Ian Wood battles Aaron Nicoll.*Nicoll started out stronger in the third game, with a [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) followed by a [Merrow Reejerey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Reejerey) and a [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher), tapping down a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) and going on the aggressive. Wood attacked back with the Leech, choosing not to pump, and played out a Great Sable Stag. Nicoll didn’t slow down, playing a second Sovereign and a [Sower of Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) to take Wood’s Leech. A Lightning bolt on the Sower was [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher)’d, Wood dropping a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) to claw some life back. Another [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher) tapped down the Finks and Nicoll swung with his team, and the Great Sable Stag blocked the [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech).


In Nicoll’s upkeep, Wood cast [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), tapping all of Nicoll’s men and drawing a card. Nicoll didn’t draw a Merfolk to untap a beater and passed, gaining Wood some breathing room. Wood swung in with his two men for 6 damage, then passed. Wood tried the same play again in Nicoll’s upkeep with a second [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), but Nicoll had a Command of his own, leaving his men free to attack for lethal. Wood had a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), but Nicoll simply sacrificed his Forge-Tender and killed his opponent.


Wood 2 – Nicoll 1


No action from Wood for the first two turns let Nicoll play out a trifecta of [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher)s, who had had no effect on Wood’s Volcanic fallout. Nicoll laid down a Reejerey. Wood answered with a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) and cascaded into [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse), killing the lord before attacking.


Nicoll had a [Sower of Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) for the Elf. Wood was happy to [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) Sower, getting his guy back, albeit tapped. Nicoll cast a Banneret and followed with a Sovereign. Wood had a second Elf, cascading into another Pulse to knock out the Sovereign, attacking relentlessly. The Banneret looked lonely until joined by a [Sygg, River Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sygg%2C+River+Guide). Wood had a small window of opportunity with Nicoll forced to tap out of white mana to drop it. Wood had a third [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) in hand to kill the Legend and swung in again with his duo of Elves. Nicoll was unable to answer the 3/2’s and Wood took the Match.


Wood 3 – Nicoll 1




Quarterfinals: Aaron Nicastri vs Jeremy Neeman, Glanville vs Michael Xia
------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

Both players mulliganed, and simultaneously! (Thanks, Magic 2010!) Nicastri got the ball rolling with a [Mystic Gate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Gate), followed by a [Reflecting Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool), while Neeman rushed onto the battlefield with a [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) and a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel). Nicastri could only watch as Neeman fetched a [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and a second [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) with a [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), but at least untapped and stole the Sentinel already in play with a [Sower of Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation). Neeman attacked past the Sower with his Ranger, and dropped two [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel)s into play, holding back the Druid. Nicastri again passed the turn with no play, sitting on four mana, allowing Neeman to untap his Sentinels with the Druid, and then a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves). Nicastri attacked with his Sower, before clearing the board with an [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest), and summoning a [Stillmoon Cavalier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stillmoon+Cavalier). ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/aunat09/T2_wood.jpg)*Ian Wood tries to figure out how he’s going to stop Mackintosh’s Elvish army.*Wood untapped and cascaded up a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) with a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). Mackintosh played a pair of Elves and another [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force), drawing six cards. He then sent in his team. Wood now on 8 life, pondered his blocks, before deciding to eat the Druid with his Bloodbraid, and trade his Leech for the Ranger, and putting him at 1 life. “[Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) and [Jund Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) in the same turn?” Mackintosh asked, now that Wood had six land. “Pass the turn,” was all he could reply.


Mackintosh untapped and played a [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) on one of Wood’s filter lands, while Wood pondered his play, Mackintosh said, “just so you know, I’m going to find a [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher) and Evoke it.” That was enough to get Wood to scoop up his cards finally, ending an epic Game 1.


Mackintosh 1 – Wood 0


“Are you feeling the pressure?” Mackintosh asked. Wood simply shook his head with a smile. “You’re so calm!” Mackintosh exclaimed with a laugh, before admitting, “yeah, me too.”


It was Wood’s turn to mulligan. Before the match, he mentioned that he’d found the trick to this match was mulliganing into a sweeper, which was probably what he was trying to do now, keeping his six. Wood had a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) for Mackintosh’s turn one [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), and even though he missed his third land drop, had a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech). Mackintosh however, summoned a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) and an Elvish Archdruid. The Leech swung in unblocked, and while Wood had found his third land, not surprisingly, it came into the battlefield tapped. He still managed to [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) Mackintosh’s Archdruid during his upkeep.


Mackintosh cantripped a [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) and an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) into a [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender), and attacked with his Sentinel. Wood attacked back with his Leech, and then offered it up to a [Jund Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) by not pumping it, to tempt Mackintosh to allow it to resolve. Mackintosh wasn’t tempted however, and prevented the damage with his Forge-Tender, and replaced it during his turn, also summoning a [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid).


Wood finally untapped with four mana, and cascaded out a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) with a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). Mackintosh counted his four mana, and eyed up his two untapped Elves and one tapped Sentinel and shook his head. He then played another [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), and then a [Mirror Entity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity), before passing the turn back, on 8 life to Wood’s 11. Wood revealed a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), and asked if that was game? “Nope,” Mackintosh replied. Which helped clue Wood in to the fact that the Forge-Tender could fog the Bloodbraid, preventing Lethal damage. During Mackintosh’s upkeep, Wood tapped down his team and drew a card. Mackintosh summoned a [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), untapping the Sentinel and passing the turn.


Wood attacked with his Finks, Bloodbraid and Leech. Llanowar and Sentinel blocked the Leech, while the Druid blocked the Bloodbraid. Mackintosh tapped the three Elves for three mana, and activated the [Mirror Entity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) for three, and Wood [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)ed it in response. Wood then played another [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and passed the turn back.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/aunat09/T2_mackintosh.jpg)*Jamie Mackintosh shuffles after playing yet another Ranger of Eos*Mackintosh had two Druids, a Llanowar, a Visionary and a Forge-Tender left. He tapped three Elves and summoned a [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force), drawing five cards. He summoned two more [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and passed the turn back. “Go go gadget Bloodbraid,” Wood muttered as he drew his card, before passing the turn back. Mackintosh summoned an Elvish Archdruid, and attacked with his three [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and the [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force), one of the Finks blocking the 5/5. After combat, Mackintosh played a [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) on Wood’s [Vivid Crag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Crag), and gained 7 life, taking him far enough out of range that Wood scooped up his cards again.


Mackintosh 2 – Wood 0


While they shuffled up for Game 3, Mackintosh looked over the trophy. “Why [Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void)slime? Why not something like [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) that’s actually relevant?”


After another mulligan, Wood led Game 3 with a [Reflecting Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool). “No Vivids?” Mackintosh asked in surprise, summoning a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves). Wood then played a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), managing a [Flooded Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flooded+Grove) and a [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) on his third turn. Mackintosh, after another Llanowar Elf on turn two, sent back the Grove with a [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command), searching out a [Mirror Entity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) after much deliberation and a hurry on from the table judge. Wood attacked with his Leech and replayed his [Flooded Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flooded+Grove). “You have Blue, Black and Green available?” Mackintosh confirmed, summoning a [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender), an Elvish Archdruid and a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel), before getting in for 2 with an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary).


Wood found another [Reflecting Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool) and summoned a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), and braced himself for the Entity. Mackintosh attacked with his team, the Entity providing far more than enough damage, crowning Jamie Mackintosh the 2009 Australian National Champion in three straight games!


Jamie Mackintosh defeats Ian Wood 3 – 0
