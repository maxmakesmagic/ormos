Round 2 Feature Match: Goldnight or Glory?
==========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 11, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett












[Antonino De Rosa vs. Luis Scott-Vargas](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptavr12/fm1)


Gabriel Nassif (White-Blue-Red Control) vs. Elie Pichon (Reanimator)
--------------------------------------------------------------------


[Sam Black vs. Shuhei Nakamura](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptavr12/fm3)


It was a showdown between two faces of French **Magic** in the second round of Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored*. On one side of the table, Hall of Famer Gabriel Nassif, a living legend. On the other, Elie Pichon who made a deep run at last year's Rookie of the Year title (thanks in particular to a 3rd place finish at Pro Tour Amsterdam) before being surpassed by Matthias Hunt.


For this event, Nassif's longtime super-team (what else would you call a group that features Nassif, Finkel and Kai?) are flying under the banner of Star City Games as team SCG Black. They've put together a White-Blue-Red control deck that draws on the power of [Desperate Ravings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) and boasts a comprehensive removal suite. Pichon is playing a strange concoction that aims to exploit a number of utility humans, fill the graveyard, and then get them all back with [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptavr12/r2_nassif_vs_pichon.jpg)
*[Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Gisela%252C%2BBlade%2Bof%2BGoldnight), or [Angel of Glory's Rise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Angel%2Bof%2BGlory%2527s%2BRise)? Both Gabriel Nassif and Elie Pichon are packing game-altering Angels in their respective decks.*

### Game 1


Nassif mulliganed and kept his six. Pichon's deck was slow off the blocks thanks to [Clifftop Retreat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clifftop+Retreat) and [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds). His tardy [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) grew to a 2/2 thanks to [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) (fetching a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)) and attacked into Nassif's three untapped mana. Nassif cast [Desperate Ravings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings), losing a [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist), and fell to eighteen.


In the face of Pichon's creatures, Nassif played his fourth land and tapped out to [Amass the Components](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amass+the+Components). Pichon couldn't punish him, managing only an [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and a [Disciple of Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Griselbrand) that made Nassif lean in for a closer look. Nassif untapped and dropped [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage), locking down the Champion. The remainder of Pichon's creatures brought her down to just one loyalty. Pichon played a [Cathedral Sanctifier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Sanctifier) with a sheepish giggle and passed.


Nassif considered all his options before freezing [Clifftop Retreat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clifftop+Retreat) with Tamiyo and casting [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus). A clear board, a resolved planeswalker, a healthy life total and a grip full of cards: all signs pointed in Nassif's favor. It hardly mattered that Pichon's life total had climbed past 30 thanks to the Disciple.


Pichon tried to build a presence on the board, but it just wasn't happening. Whenever he tried to threaten Tamiyo, Nassif was ready with removal. Soon Nassif added [Lone Revenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lone+Revenant) to the board. Pichon [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch)ed Retoration Angel into his graveyard and played a [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) to chump for a turn. Nassif turned up the heat with [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight). Pichon played [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) on Humans and cast [Geist-Honored Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk), which chumped the [Revenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revenant), leaving two spirits behind. Nassif cast [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) and passed.


With a [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) on Angels, Pichon flipped the script by casting [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise). Suddenly his board was jammed with an 8/8 [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), an even bigger [Geist-Honored Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk), and two [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter)s for Nassif's creatures.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptavr12/r2_pichon.jpg)
*[Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) reversed the roles in Pichon's favor.*

Nassif drew, looted, then drew two off Tamiyo in desperation. Still, he could not find a [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus). He passed. Pichon worried over his creatures before turning all but the [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter)s sideways. Nassif played [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre), freeing Gisela from the [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter). She blocked the giant [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish). [Lone Revenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lone+Revenant) ate the [Disciple of Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Griselbrand), and thanks to Gisela the unblocked creatures left Nassif alive at 4.


He untapped, froze the [Geist-Honored Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk), and got back his [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) with Pillar of Fire on the other [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter). He was once again safe, albeit uneasily. For a few turns they played draw-go. Of course, that was tilted horribly in Nassif's favor thanks to a pair of [Desolate Lighthouse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolate+Lighthouse)s. An [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) from Pichon met a [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) off [Desperate Ravings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings). A [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) brought out another [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptavr12/r2_nassif.jpg)
*[Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus): a solid answer to a deck looking to resurrect all of its Humans.*

With time winding down, Pichon stood at 40 life. [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play) took 15 of that, and then Nassif found another Gisela. He spent the rest of his mana flashing back [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play), its damage doubled to 12. Pichon found another [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise), but the bulk of his creatures were on the bottom of his library, and Nassif had another [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play) in the graveyard, giving him the first game.


Gabriel Nassif 1, Elie Pichon 0


### Game 2


All that bedlam left just nine minutes on the clock. The players dawdled over their sideboards, Nassif in particular agonizing about a last-minute substitution.


Nassif drew up a sketchy seven and reluctantly sent it back. His six were no better. Nor were his five. With the clock dwindling he shuffled up a hand of four. Time ran out during Nassif's second turn. Pichon had only the extra turns to eke out a draw. He had [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) on Angel. Nassif played his third land and passed. Pichon played [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) at end of turn.


Nassif tapped three and went for [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate), saying that Pichon hadn't made explicit that he was using the [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) for colored mana.


Pichon looked at Nassif incredulously. Before the table judge could make a ruling, Nassif relented on his claim.


It didn't matter much. Pichon didn't have enough creatures to threaten lethal damage, and the second game ended in a draw.


Gabriel Nassif 1, Elie Pichon 0
