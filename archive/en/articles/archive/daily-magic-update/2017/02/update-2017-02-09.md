Deck of the Day
===============


1. Jun Ishihara's Sultai Delirium Control

One of the secret successes of Pro Tour *Aether Revolt* was the Sultai Delirium Control deck from Jun Ishihara, who finished 8-2 in Standard with the deck. If we listen to Reid Duke, now is a good time for Black-Green Delirium, and if we listen to blue mages everywhere, that deck can only be improved by adding blue cards.


In this case, Ishihara added [Glimmer of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmer+of+Genius), [Disallow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disallow), [Overwhelming Denial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overwhelming+Denial), and the ever-present [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk). If you've never cast [Grapple with the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past) to get back a [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) to get back a [Grapple with the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past), you haven't truly played the grindiest of grinds in Standard.


With [Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push); [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness); [To the Slaughter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=To+the+Slaughter); [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow); and [Liliana, the Last Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope), the deck is already stacked against Mardu Vehicles, and the addition of blue cards gives it some edge in the pseudo-mirror.


There's always one deck from the Pro Tour that goes under the radar but blooms in the new metagame afterward. Could this be the control deck we're all looking for?
