Deck Tech: Zombie Emerge Delirium with Eliott Boussaud
======================================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX WARSAW 2016](/en/events/coverage/gpwar16)
 on October 30, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/henke_author.jpg)
By Tobi Henke











We're always on the lookout for interesting decks, of course, and this certainly qualified. Silver Level pro and champion of Grand Prix Prague 2015 Eliott Boussaud had brought a four-colored monstrosity to this Grand Prix, a homemade brew borrowing elements of Temur Emerge, Zombies, and Delirium which kept his opponents guessing. Would they need to prepare for an onslaught of the undead? A chain of [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend)s? [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) and [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End)? Or maybe even [Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions)?


"All the other French guys told me that it's garbage," said Boussaud about his deck. But he was convinced otherwise. And he had the 10-2 record to prove it.


Boussaud explained that it all started with the deck he had played at the Pro Tour, what he called "Blue-Red Zombies." Despite its set of [Prized Amalgam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prized+Amalgam)s, this had been a straight two-colored affair, bearing almost no resemblance to the four-color weirdness that he now ended up with after further testing.

"The thing is, I realized that the mana in this deck wasn't even that great. If you wanted to return [[Stitchwing Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitchwing+Skaab)] and emerge [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) on the same turn, for example, you needed three sources of blue mana," explained Boussaud. "So it wasn't really much of a stretch to go into black for [Haunted Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Dead)."


From there, green was the next logical step. Boussaud stressed the importance of [Botanical Sanctum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Botanical+Sanctum) and [Blooming Marsh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blooming+Marsh). "The mana base is mostly green now, for all the delirium outlets. I'm basically splashing blue for [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend), red for [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) and [Cathartic Reunion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathartic+Reunion). [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) helps a lot too."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gpwar16/WAR_ZEDwithelliottboussaud.jpg)  
*Eliott Boussaud*


Boussaud needed even less black mana, just enough to activate [Haunted Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Dead) or the odd [Scrapheap Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger). But mana sometimes was an issue, Boussaud admitted. "I lost to Martin JÅ¯za's Black-Green Delirium, when I just didn't draw red mana."


Usually, though, Black-Green Delirium was a great matchup, said Boussaud. He had played the deck four times on Day 1, in addition to one match against Green-White Delirium.


"All midrange decks are good matchups, basically all the decks with [Kalitas, Traitor of Ghet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalitas%2C+Traitor+of+Ghet). If you emerge with [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) they get a Zombie first, then they lose everything to [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return)," Boussaud explained. "And they don't expect [Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions), which is a great sideboard card against them. They don't bring in [Natural State](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+State) or [Appetite for the Unnatural](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Appetite+for+the+Unnatural).


"White-Blue is tough but winnable. [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) is annoying because you can't just [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) their board. So all the decks with [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) are tough, but my assumption was that White-Blue was on the decline," said Boussaud. "Aggressive decks are definitely not good matchups. I mean, sometimes you go [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency) into [Haunted Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Dead) into [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) with [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) in tow, of course. And I bring in [Arborback Stomper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arborback+Stomper) and [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames). But I'd put the matchup at 45 percent at best.


"Control decks in general are great matchups. [Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions) is great against Grixis, and good against Jeskai unless the have [Nahiri, the Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nahiri%2C+the+Harbinger). The Zombies allow you to pressure them without committing much to the board. Then if they take care of them, you can maybe tap them out with [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend), and resolve your bigger threats. Because they don't kill you so fast, you can just harass them with your small creatures, and maybe later even resolve Emrakul.


"The deck is a lot of fun to play," said Boussaud. "I'll definitely keep tuning it. So if it's not today [that I make Top 8], it's going to be Grand Prix Madrid!"
