The January 13, 2017 Update
===========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on January 13, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on January 13, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by the [*Aether Revolt* Prerelease](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/prerelease-events-types-events)!


Today's Must
============


1. [Making Moves](https://company.wizards.com/article/press/making-moves) | Wizards of the Coast | Chris Cocks

If you haven't read this announcement yet (it was published yesterday after the Update was posted), I suggest you do. Though it doesn't have too many specifics, it does publicly kick off a renewed effort around our digital space.


A few outlets reported on the posting, including [this one from Philip Kollar at Polygon](http://www.polygon.com/2017/1/12/14259180/wizards-of-the-coast-magic-gathering-hearthstone-competitor-digital-online-dungeons-and-dragons) and [this one from Matt Miller at *Game Informer*](http://www.gameinformer.com/b/news/archive/2017/01/12/wizards-of-the-coast-signals-increased-video-game-investment.aspx).


1. [The *Aether Revolt* Prerelease Primer](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/the-aether-revolt-prerelease-primer/) | ChannelFireball | Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa

But if your mood is less "corporate communications" and more "OH YES! [THE *AETHER REVOLT* PRERELEASE](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/prerelease-events-types-events) IS FINALLY HERE!!" then check out Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa's Prerelease primer. He does some version of this for every set, and they're always really informative and helpful for anyone just this side of "I already know how to play, I want to win me some packs."


Oh, and if you're looking for more prep for the Prerelease, check out [The Mana Source's Prerelease Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NwmZvj5n05U) or [this report from Soh Weng Heng](http://www.mtgmintcard.com/articles/writers/soh-weng-heng/aether-revolt-prerelease-at-the-sss), who played *Aether Revolt* at the Super Sunday Series, *or* this [visual set review from Limited Resources](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0uBVhuMVbPU).

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Black-Red Control](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/34354_Aether-Revolt-Standard-Decks.html) | StarCityGames.com | Todd Stevens

Todd Stevens's article from a few days ago is the gift that keeps on giving. Today's particular gift is a Black-Red Control deck that might just represent a new direction for control in Standard. Who needs blue anyway? (Me. I do. It's almost pathological.)


The deck looks to maximize [Chandra, Torch of Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance) and [Liliana, the Last Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope). Now that [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) is no more and Emrakul can't promise any more ends, planeswalkers are back on the menu! Black and red have easily the best removal in Standard right now. [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock), [Unlicensed Disintegration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration), and [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness) can all break up the [Saheeli Rai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saheeli+Rai) and [Felidar Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Guardian) combo. [Ruinous Path](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruinous+Path) can cover you for a resurgence in planeswalkers, and [Collective Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Defiance) can be a Swiss army knife that can do the same, and more.


The real gift, however, is [Goblin Dark-Dwellers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Dark-Dwellers), a card that hasn't quite seen the play it should considering its power level. I'd like to see some amount of [Live Fast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Live+Fast) or [Succumb to Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Succumb+to+Temptation) in this list, simply because you sometimes want to be able to cast a proactive [Goblin Dark-Dwellers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Dark-Dwellers) when your opponent has nothing to remove.


From there, [Kalitas, Traitor of Ghet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalitas%2C+Traitor+of+Ghet) can still clean up, and [Bedlam Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bedlam+Reveler) can provide some extra fuel as well. It's a brutal deck, but it might be where control in the new Standard is headed.
