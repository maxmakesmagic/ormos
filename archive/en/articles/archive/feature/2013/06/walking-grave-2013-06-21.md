The Walking Grave
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 21, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I haven't written about Commander in a while, so I decided to build a deck. I started by looking for a relatively recently printed Commander and came to the obvious choice of [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped). While looking for cards to include in a [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped) deck, I came across [Skullbriar, the Walking Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullbriar%2C+the+Walking+Grave) and realized that card is awesome with [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped), but works much better as a commander, where it can really take advantage of its ability to keep counters. So I switched commanders. 

![Skullbriar, the Walking Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Skullbriar%2C+the+Walking+Grave) This deck is still very much built with [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped) in mind, and the plan is to find him as soon as possible to start scavenging onto Skullbriar, but Skullbriar just takes advantage of the Commander rules better. 

This deck is designed to be relatively casual, which I believe to be the appropriate power level for most Commander decks. It plays good cards and has a game plan, but the game plan is based around doing what the commander happens to want to do rather than doing one of the things that is most powerful in the format. This isn't ramp and card draw that incidentally wins in a particular way, this deck is, from the ground up, about putting counters on [Skullbriar, the Walking Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullbriar%2C+the+Walking+Grave), and then hitting opponents with him. It plans to win almost every game by dealing 21 points of general damage.

 There are a wide variety of really sweet cards to put +1/+1 counters on things if that's what you're looking to do, and building around them makes [Corpsejack Menace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpsejack+Menace) and [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) particularly exciting ([Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) always seems to be great in this format).
