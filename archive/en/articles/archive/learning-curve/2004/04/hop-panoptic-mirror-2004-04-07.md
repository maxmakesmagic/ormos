Hop on Panoptic Mirror
======================



 Posted in **Learning Curve**
 on April 7, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New York–based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 






Damn you Aaron Forsythe!

![Panoptic Mirror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Panoptic+Mirror)Everything was going so well until you wrote [that little article](/en/articles/archive/latest-developments/life-and-times-autospell-2004-02-13-0) about my favorite hidden gem, [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror), and the trixy way to stack its abilities at the beginning of your upkeep. Until then I was able to pick the card up pretty late in drafts. The card has begun to go higher and higher in people's pick orders. Things can only get worse now that our esteemed Limited specialist considered it as a possible first pick in [his most recent Limited Information column](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sw9).The worst was when I was in a draft and set up to win with my fliers over the next two turns with almost zero danger of losing on the return attack. My opponent played a [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror) and passed the turn. I attacked and dropped him down to one and had to avert my eyes when he properly stacked the Mirror and imprinted a back-breaking [Blinding Beam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Beam) on the Mirror and paid for the entwine cost. He tapped the creature I had just cast and my flying army was left locked down in the horizontal position. I would have needed three creatures—all of them with evasion—to get out from under his lock. I lost the game and match as a result and knew that the days of tenth pick [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror)s were behind me.

My friend Rich Fein had a draft that featured the Mirror and I watched him sail through his matches with an imprinted [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast). He would rip through his deck until he found and imprinted an [Essence Drain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain). Recently, there has been some interest in applying the powerful card to the Vintage format. There are any number of cards that make your mouth water when you think about imprinting them on a Mirror. [Ancestral Recall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall), [Intuition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition), and [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) all give you a ridiculous source of card drawing. [Balance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balance) and [Tinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker) are tantalizing items on the menu that is the restricted list and the possibility of a game ending [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) is too powerful a combo for even the most conservative deck builder to resist.

Scott McCord has built some of the more degenerate Vintage decks on the Neutral Ground tournament scene and shared two potential lists he has been tooling for competitive play. Before Vintage fans begin flaming me about [Worldgorger Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldgorger+Dragon)s and Long.dec--nobody is claiming that these are the Vintage decks to beat. They are just two lists that attempt to abuse the power of [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror) in Vintage and may have some potential.

While Scott has been sequestered in his Vintage laboratory I have been thinking about trying to play the card in Standard. My fantasy for Regionals has been a blue-green deck with [Vedalken Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Engineer)s and [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox)es. My ideal turn sequence would go a little something like this:

Turn one: Land, [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox), and [Vedalken Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Engineer)

Turn two: Land, make five mana, and play [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror)

Turn three: Stack Mirror and imprint [Plow Under](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plow+Under), Plow you Under

Turn four: Do a little dance

Unfortunately, if I was on the play I would need seven of my first nine cards to line up perfectly in order to pull that draw off and it doesn't get significantly easier at seven of ten on the draw. Still, I am drawn to shiny objects and what is shiner than the [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror)? We may not have [Ancestral Recall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall) and [Accumulated Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge) to work with but there is still abundant abuseable card drawing to work with. 

[Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) and Concentrate are both going into any deck I design with the Mirror. I am tempted to get greedy and include [Rush of Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rush+of+Knowledge). Who doesn't want to draw five cards a turn? I will content myself with topping out at three—that feels just greedy enough. A renewable [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) seems like a natural addition—especially in the face of [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp)s and Oversold Cemeteries. 

One of the things holding many artifacts back in the new Standard is the abundant hatred running around the format in anticipation of [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp)s and Ravager-Affinity decks. Now stepping up to the plate…[Leonin Abunas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Abunas). The deck I designed does not have the explosiveness of a deck with Standard's answer to [Mishra's Workshop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Workshop) but the Talismans allow you to play out a turn three hard-to-kill Abunas and follow it with a harder-to-kill [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror).

Now don't run out and build this deck for Regionals. It is not going to keep pace with Goblin Bidding or Ravager Affinity. Not as it stands right now. But I *am* convinced that [Panoptic Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror) is definitely one of the hidden treasures of the Mirrodin block and it will eventually find a home in a constructed deck—be it in Vintage, Standard, or Extended. In the meanwhile I will be playtesting the deck this week. I think that it if I find the right mix of instants and sorceries I might be able to make it work. As always I am open to suggestions about how to break the Mirror and welcome your comments.
