Legacy Death and Taxes
======================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on August 25, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Many Legacy players feel that if you're not playing [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)s and [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)s in your deck, you shouldn't even bother playing. While it's true that there have been many powerful blue cards printed over the years, blue is not the only viable color in Legacy. Michael Derczo proved that point at the most recent [StarCityGames.com Legacy Open](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/080815_washingtondc.html) by piloting this deck to a Top 8 finish.


Michael is playing four copies of both [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) and [Vryn Wingmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vryn+Wingmare). These guys can cause nightmares for combo decks and severely slow down any deck playing one-mana cantrips like [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). Before *Magic Origins* was released, it was not possible to have two of these creatures in play due to the legend rule. Now that [Vryn Wingmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vryn+Wingmare) exists, you can make noncreature spells cost an additional two mana. It's nearly impossible for most decks to win without first removing these creatures from the battlefield.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vryn+Wingmare)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vryn+Wingmare)
[Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) plays a very important role in this deck. It allows you to put creatures into play without paying mana for them, freeing up your lands for [Rishadan Port](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port) or [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) activations.


[Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) also changes the way your opponent plays the game. When you activate Vial, your opponent has to respond to it before they know what creature you are putting into play. For example, if you activate [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) on two and your opponent has only one mana untapped, they will have to cast [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) or [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) in response. Once Vial resolves and you put a Thalia on the battlefield, they won't be able to pay the additional one for that spell. This is a great way to force your opponent to do things before they are ready to. One interesting thing about this trick is you don't even need to put anything into play when the ability of [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) resolves.


White is a great color in Legacy. It does a lot of hateful things, and those things can often make your opponent unable to play *Magic*. Sometimes that's even more powerful than drawing cards and countering spells!
