Round 7 Feature Match - Eric Taylor vs. Mike Pustilnik
======================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 11, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_133.png)
By Eli Kaplan











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/952.jpg)Eric Taylor, widely respected for his playing skills and social prestige on irc channel #mtgwacky, is coming hot off of a top 64 finish at PT Nice. Mike Flores recently picked him as one of the seven most important **Magic** writers of all time. Facing him is no less than PT Los Angeles 2001 champion and Limited master Michael Pustilnik. The winner of this match would have very comfortable odds of making it to the Top 8.

Taylor was playing the breakthrough deck of the tournament, the red-white-blue control deck with [Goblin Trenches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches). He loved his [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel)s in his sideboard, attributing them to numerous wins. Pustilnik brought the ever-present [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) deck to the picnic.

Taylor won the roll, and chose to go first. The first play of the game was Pustilnik's [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), which was promptly set to burning with a Taylor Fire. Pustilnik then played the ravenous [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). Taylor [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)d it, then gained tempo by Memory Lapsing an end-of-turn [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Pustilnik then replayed his [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/951.jpg)The pace went from fast to frenetic as Taylor played a [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) at the [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). Pustilnik responded by playing a [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) of his own at the [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). But Taylor [Memory Lapse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Lapse)d Pustilnik's [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse), thus ensuring that the Doctor would not enjoy his next draw. Taylor then untapped and threw down a [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) in his main phase. Pustilnik wouldn't hear any of that and [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)ed the [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Taylor was looking at his lands and sighing; he had drawn mostly [Adarkar Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes) and [Shivan Reef](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Reef)s, making all of his mana painful to use.

Once again the [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) reared its ugly head. Taylor didn't counter it, but used a main phase [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Pustilnik divided the piles into [Goblin Trenches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches), [Fire/Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire%2FIce) or [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) with two Islands. Taylor took the [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) pile. He then dropped the Trenches which was in his hand. 

The hungry [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) looked forlorn, so Pustilnik decided to cheer the little guy up by playing a second [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). Sated with the companionship, the [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) came over for 1, bringing Taylor to 12 life. "Most of that was done by yourself," Pustilnik noted. At the end of Pustilnik's turn, Taylor played a [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), to which Pustilnik answered with a Fact of his own. Pustilnik acquired a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) and two Islands over [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) and [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic), and used the freshly minted [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) to counter Taylor's Fact. 

Taylor: "Why do I have so much pain, jeez?"

Taylor may have been taking pain, but the suffering was worth it as he tapped two [Adarkar Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes) to give him the two white mana to play [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). Pustilnik couldn't handle losing two of his best threats, and died after Taylor raised a battalion of Goblin Soldiers.

Eric Taylor - Michael Pustilnik 1-0

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/950.jpg)The second game looked bad for Taylor, as he drew two [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel)s and a [Goblin Trenches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches), along with an [Absorb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb), [Skycloud Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skycloud+Expanse), Island, and [Coastal Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coastal+Tower). He could do nothing against Pustilnik's turn two [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), and used [Memory Lapse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Lapse) on Pustilnik's second Familiar. Pustilnik tapped two mana, seeing his [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) in hand, but then realized he didn't have any cards in his graveyard. He mana burned for two. The jig was up; Taylor knew at least one Logic was up Pustilnik's sleeve. 

Taylor then played a [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) from off the top, naming [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). But he wasn't quite anticipating what happened next. 

Pustilnik tapped out and played a turn 4 [Lobotomy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobotomy), which saw the pair of [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel)s, a [Goblin Trenches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches), and an [Absorb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb). "Sonovagun!" Taylor was dismayed as Pustilnik took the two [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel)s out of his hand, and removed the 2 copies from Taylor's library. "Nice little [Hymn to Tourach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach)." But fortune smiled upon Taylor as he ripped [Shivan Reef](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Reef) off the top and laid down the [Goblin Trenches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches). Suddenly, he had a win condition on the table.

Pustilnik wasn't going to sit still, and brought the [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) ranks up from one to three. After several turns of dropping land, Pustilnik restarted the action with a (one-mana) [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Taylor [Gainsay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)ed, Pustilnik [Gainsay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)ed, Taylor [Absorb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb)ed [Gainsay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay), Pustilnik [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)ed [Absorb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb). Net result: Pustilnik got his [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) to resolve, grabbing a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) and [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short) over [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) and two Islands. 

With a comfortable supply of mana, Taylor decided to turn up the heat and start cranking out his goblin brigades. First 4, then 8 tokens amassed on his side. Pustilnik got nervous, realizing that if he didn't play a [Sickening Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickening+Dreams), he'd be up to his neck in big trouble. He [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short)ed Taylor on Taylor's next upkeep, which Taylor responded to by upping the count to 12 Goblin Soldier tokens. Pustilnik couldn't do the job, and conceded. 

Eric Taylor - Michael Pustilnik 2-0
