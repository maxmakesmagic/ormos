Abzan Aggro at GP Miami
=======================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on March 16, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 





I can't believe that *Dragons of Tarkir* is only a few days away and we are still seeing new decks and a new Standard metagame. The new Tarkir set provided an unusual, continuously changing format. This week, I will introduce some brand-new decks and some existing decks with brand-new changes from [Grand Prix Miami](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpmia15), held two weeks ago.



I will start off with Andrew Boswell's updated Abzan aggro. Although there are multiple changes made to the deck, the absence of [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) is the most noticeable. In its place are extra copies of Warden of the First Tree. These count as two-mana 3/3s and bring the number of two-mana creatures up to twelve.



Basically, the deck has changed its focus to board development instead of hand disruption. Decks in the current format do not depend on one specific card, so hand disruption doesn't always slow them down; they simply beat down with the rest of their hand.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Warden+of+the+First+Tree)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warden+of+the+First+Tree)

In short, the metagame has shifted to a more aggressive field. You can see this in the addition of [Brimaz, King of Oreskos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos), which is extremely strong in a match where both players are trying to take board control.



It has also really cut down on tapped lands—but not only does it want a turn-three double-white Brimaz, but also [Boon Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boon+Satyr) and [Hero's Downfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall). All three cards have double-colored mana symbols, on turn three, and the deck only plays seven lands that enter tapped. You can tell it's serious about early game mana requirements, though, with the full four [Mana Confluence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Confluence)s.
