Two-time Grand Prix Top 8 competitor Huang Hao-shan has been a fixture on the circuit for a couple of years now and his accomplishments are enviable. Currently at Gold Level, he hopes that he would be able to renew (or upgrade) his membership for the coming year.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/Huang%20001.JPG)

*Huang Hao-Shan shows off his wares* 
Despite taking a loss in the Featured Match to his fellow countryman (Kuo Tzu Ching) Huang Hao-shan has managed to enter day two in 15th seed, with a comfortable 8-1 record thus far. He would need to rattle off four wins to secure a berth in the Top 8. The *M13* draft format is still in its infancy (by the way, *M13* events have started on **Magic Online**), and I thought it would be a great time to learn from one of the top players in the region.


### Booster One


Huang started decently well, but good things never seem to last. He had the choice of [Silklash Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silklash+Spider) , [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and [Cower in Fear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cower+in+Fear) from a relatively weak pack and his next pack presented him with [Mwonvuli Beast Tracker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mwonvuli+Beast+Tracker) , perfect for tutoring up the bomb he had just picked. However, green started to dry up and there was no clear secondary color to dip into. At this juncture, Huang's draft seemed like a fiasco, and he was eager to rip open his next booster to try and pick up the pieces.

### Booster Two


Currently solidified in green but having no secondary color, Huang had to be content with a couple of powerful cards, but he was still lacking in the creature department. Presumptively, he was looking out for cards like [Centaur Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser) , [Canyon Minotaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Minotaur) or [Sentinel Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel+Spider) , but they were nowhere to be seen. He had to be happy with [Arms Dealer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arms+Dealer) , [Primordial Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra) and then [Akroma's Memorial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Memorial) . Despite picking up some good cards really late, it looked as if Huang's deck was awkward at best, an indication that he had some sort of red green control deck, capable of splashing a third color.

### Booster Three


The final booster was not entirely action-packed, but Huang managed to fill in the missing nooks of his deck, including double [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) (which would serve to smooth out his draws) and his second [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) and [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) to improve his already excellent manabase. Huang eventually decided to get a little greedy and splash [Faerie Invaders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) .



![Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elvish+Visionary)![Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Evolving+Wilds)

*Filling out the curve, smoothing the mana and fine-tuning the final product!*

His deck has the potential for the aggressive draw, but if that doesn't work out, he can play defensively and win with his [Akroma's Memorial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Memorial) or [Primordial Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra) . He expects to go at least 2-1 at this table, and feels that 3-0 is a little out of reach. We wish him the best of luck in his endeavors today, and thereafter!

Round 10 Feature Match - Hu Nan vs. Han Bing
--------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*
Both players had impressive sealed decks yesterday and have played marvellously attain the pristine 9-0 record. Hu Nan has travelled to Shanghai from Beijing, while Han Bing's hometown was He Bei. Both players chatted amiably about the state of the draft, while wondering what colors each other were in in this brief cat-and-mouse game. (On the topic of animals, if Hu had been playing with [Warclamp Mastiff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warclamp+Mastiff) , I would not hestitate to unleash upon you a "Hu let the dogs out" joke.)


They seemed to be relatively pleased with their respective arsenals, and proceeded with die-rolling and well-wishing.


### Game One


Han may have lost the die roll but he was the aggressor, leading with [Jace's Phantasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Phantasm) and [Void Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void+Stalker) . Wu did nothing until turn four and summoned [Primal Clay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Clay) with flying, stopping both his opponent's creatures temporarily.


Han was happy to play the waiting game, and simply resolved [Sands of Delirium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sands+of+Delirium) , opting to bide his time until [Jace's Phantasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Phantasm) could swell to dragon-like proportions. Using [Talrand's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%27s+Invocation) , [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) and [Faerie Invaders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) , he was able to defend against his opponent's [Shimian Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimian+Specter) and [Liliana's Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade) , until [Sands of Delirium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sands+of+Delirium) eventually milled Hu's library into oblivion.


Han Bing wins Game One.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/RD%2010%20Han%20Bing.JPG)

*Han Bing* 
Game Two


Hu began the game with [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats) , [Servant of Nefarox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+Nefarox) and [Liliana's Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Specter) . [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats) snuck in for two damage, then [Servant of Nefarox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+Nefarox) charged in for four, followed by an additional four from Lilliana's Specter. This series of attacks reduced Han to just ten life in a matter of three combat steps.


Unfortunately, his creatures were severely outclassed, could only supply "inferior" counterparts in the form of [Wind Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) and [Scroll Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) , incapable of putting up any form of defense other than being sacrificial speedbumps. Eventually, an unblocked [Liliana's Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade) grew to 10/10 (thanks to five [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) activations and [Titanic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titanic+Growth) ), spelling Han's demise.


Hu Nan wins Game Two, evening out the score.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/RD%2010%20Hu%20Nan.JPG)

*Hu Nan* 
### Game Three


Hu's offense within the first five turns comprised of only [Tormented Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Soul) but it was enhanced by [Mark of the Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+the+Vampire) , turning it into a racing machine.


Han took the time to assemble a synergistic army, including [Knight of Infamy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy) , Harbour Serpent and most importantly, Court Provocateur. Using this wizard, he was able to force any of Hu's creatures to block the potentially 4/4 [Harbor Serpent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Serpent) , including a freshly summoned [Liliana's Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade) .


Despite having a strong board presence, he still couldn't outrace the 3/3 unblockable lifelinking creature and was reduced to a mere 7 life. Hu was merely feigning weakness though, as he proceeded to gain sixteen life on the next turn, upsetting all the combat math which Han had meticulously been calculating.


Using [Titanic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titanic+Growth) on [Tormented Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Soul) , he reduced Han to three life (while gaining seven life), and then used [Prey Upon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) after combat (gaining another seven) and finally dropping [Bloodhunter Bat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhunter+Bat) to reduce Han to just one life. Unable to deal with both evasive creatures, Han extended the hand graciously.


Hu Nan wins Game Three, causing an upset and goes 10-0!




Sunday, 12:23 p.m. - Grand Prix Shanghai Draft 1: Shouta Yasooka
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
The familiar silhouette of [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) greeted long-time pro Shouta Yasooka as he scanned through his first pack of *M13* for the draft. With nothing else leaping out, his first pick was an easy one. A [Crimson Muckwader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader) pushed Yasooka down a synergistic path, but his next few picks were all solid Blue cards â€“ a [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) , [Faerie Invaders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) , and a pair of Scroll Thieves followed in rapid order. Mid-pack, Yasooka hoovered up a [Cower in Fear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cower+in+Fear) , a [Disentomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) , and some random filler, with nothing coming to push him out of a slow progression down a U/B Skies path.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/GPShanghai-Yasooka-Draft.jpg)

*Shouta Yasooka* 
Pack 2 was a little disappointing in terms of opening power, with Yasooka eventually settling on a [Giant Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Scorpion) . A second [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) and a [Bloodhunter Bat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhunter+Bat) filled out Yasooka's curve, but he still lacked any good instants or sorceries to take advantage of the powerful Wizard. Pack 2 continued to serve up solid mid-range creatures, with another Welkin Turn, a [Wind Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) , and another [Scroll Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) . Removal was light on the ground, with an [Encrust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Encrust) being the only option Yasooka was given.


The final booster yielded another [Bloodhunter Bat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhunter+Bat) and, finally, an [Essence Drain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain) . Yasooka switched gears and started to work on upgrading his creatures, picking a [Dark Favor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Favor) over other worthwhile cards. A late [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) helped round out the deck, and a [Kraken Hatchling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraken+Hatchling) would help Yasooka clog up the ground while his fliers went to town.


During construction, Yasooka was stoic, but not confident of victory. His deck was solid but lacked removal or any game-swinging bombs, and he had passed enough gas in other colours to know that were would be some quality opposition arrayed against him. He decided to not run the second [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) , instead opting for a [Disentomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) to lower his curve.

Round 12 Feature Match - Li Bo vs. Makihito Mihara
--------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim* 
It's not every day we get a match between two World Champions, so I have learnt that we should cherish such occasions. When the pairings went up, it seemed only natural that Li Bo and Makihito Mihara would have to converge in the Featured Match area.


Li Bo was part of the three-person team which won the team portion of the World Championships in 2009. Amiable and chatty, he is also an important figurehead within the Chinese gaming community. His opponent, Makihito Mihara, will never be forgotten as the 2006 World Champion, where he rode [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) to victory. He has also four Grand Prix Top 8s to his name, and was aiming to increase that particular statistic to five.


Both players had won their first two matches of the day, which meant that the victor here would be undefeated for their first pod, and remain in contention for the Top 8.


### Game One


This three-minute match made me feel like we were back to the *Coldsnap* days, where some strategies involved picking duplicates of cards voraciously. Cards like [Krovikan Mist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krovikan+Mist) and [Surging Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surging+Flame) become exponentially stronger as the number of copies in your deck increased.


Mihara was able to put up a defense with [Fog Bank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) and [Wall of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Fire) , but that was no match for Li's QUADRUPLE [Timberpack Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberpack+Wolf) by turn five.


Li had [Plummet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plummet) to press the advantage and Mihara soon succumbed to an army of monstrous howl-packs. Mihara was visibly affected, but still managed to let out a chuckle of mixed emotion, possibly consisting of bewilderment and slight amusement.


Li Bo wins Game One, brutally.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/RD%2011%20Mihara.JPG)

*Makihito Mihara wonders what he can do to a bunch of 5/5s which cost 1G!* 
### Game Two


Mihara's " [Grixis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grixis) " control deck seemed be working for him relatively smoothly this time round. He used [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) to kill an opposing [Centaur Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser) , which allowed [Scroll Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) to nab him a card.


[Furnace Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon) and [Wind Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) prompted Li to mutter "so many fliers" under his breath, but at least he had [Liliana's Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade) and [Prey Upon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) rid himself of the firebreathing dragon, while still holding [Plummet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plummet) .


[Encrust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Encrust) ensured that the shade would not be a problem in future, and Mihara tried to race with [Mark of the Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+the+Vampire) on [Scroll Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) . Sunday, 4:49 p.m. - Grand Prix Shanghai Draft 2: Yuuya Watanabe
---------------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
Watanabe opened a pack spectacularly devoid of content, with [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) being close to the only playable in it, and certainly the only one that warranted more than a few moment's attention. From there, he picked up a [Faerie Invaders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) , a [Switcheroo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Switcheroo) , and a pair of [Guardians of Akrasa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardians+of+Akrasa) from some otherwise lacklustre packs. A mid-pack [Fog Bank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) and an [Encrust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Encrust) pushed Watanabe firmly towards U/W, with the tail end of pack 1 leaving him low on aggressors but sitting nicely on a pile of defensive cards.


Unfortunately, it all started to unravel from there. Pack 2 started off with a [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) , a [Vampire Nocturnus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nocturnus) , and an [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) , Watanabe taking the powerful 2/3. A [Chronomaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton) and a [Krenko, Mob Boss](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%2C+Mob+Boss) joined Watanabe's picks when nothing was forthcoming in either Blue or White. Pack 2 was all about the red cards, with Watanabe picking up a [Krenko's Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command) and some [Rummaging Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) s. Watanabe was forced to shake his head and keep moving deeper into a third colour as the pack progressed, with a late flood of black cards taunting him.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/GPShanghaiDraftFeatureWatanabe.jpg)

*or once, Watanabe's stoic face truly indicates how badly things are going.* 
Pack 3 was even worse. A second [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) appeared as if to taunt him, but he was unable to take it and had to console himself with an [Essence Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Leak) . Unfortunately, the **Magic** gods were not smiling on Watanabe, as the [Essence Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Leak) proved to be pretty much the best card he would get from the pack, rounding out his mana base a little with a [Glacial Fortress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress) and some high-end U/W fliers.


"I cannot win with this deck", Watanabe said as he sat down to start construction. Sadly, I was forced to agree that this would be a challenge even for a player of Watanabe's calibre. The cards he had resembled more a freshly opened Sealed pool than a finely tuned draft deck, and it took the experienced player many minutes of grimacing and shaking his head before he was able to concoct something he could shuffle up.

Round 14 Feature Match - Ken Yukuhiro vs. Gu Fengwen
----------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*
Both players had fought hard to come where they were today, but none could afford to lose anymore. Gu Fengwen from Beijing would have to beat Platinum Level Pro Ken Yukuhiro (Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored* Top 8 Competitor) if he wanted to draw into the Top 8 the next round.


### Game One


Yukuhiro had a blazing start, recruiting [War Falcon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War+Falcon) , [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) and [Crusader of Odric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusader+of+Odric) , but that was stymied by Gu's [Deadly Recluse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse) . When [Crusader of Odric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusader+of+Odric) crashed into the red zone the next turn, Gu ambushed it with [Yeva, Nature's Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yeva%2C+Nature%27s+Herald) , which promised to further complicate matters for Yukuhiro.


The powerful green legendary creature commanded [Timberpack Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberpack+Wolf) and [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) into the war zone, knocking Yukuhiro down to 3 precarious life by the end of the next two combat steps. Gu unloaded [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Goblin Arsonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Arsonist) to maximize his power. [Prey Upon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) met with [Rewind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind) , but that was inconsequential. All Gu had to do was turn his five creatures sideways to prompt a concession.


Gu Fengwen wins Game One.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/RD%2014%20Ken%20Yukuhiro.JPG)

*Ken Yukuhiro* 
### Game Two


Yukuhiro had built up seeming impregnable fort consisting of [War Falcon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War+Falcon) and double [Fog Bank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) , fiercely guarded by three tokens generated by [Captain's Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain%27s+Call) . Nonetheless, he seemed to be having problems against Gu's [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) , which had grown to a 7/7 monstrosity. Conveniently, the [Ring of Kalonia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ring+of+Kalonia) granted trample, allowing Hu to go around [Fog Bank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) . Despite adding [Divine Favor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Favor) to one of them, Yukuhiro still had to lose some life each turn.


[Wind Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) and [Welkin Tern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) was no match against Hu's next play, the constructed powerhouse [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) . Yukuhiro tried to stabilize, as he bolstered his defenses with [Captain of the Watch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+of+the+Watch) , bringing up the number of Soldier tokens on the board to six, not that he could attack with any of them.


[Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) continued to grow, to the point that Yukuhiro had to use [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) to neutralize it. Gu bestowed [Ring of Kalonia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ring+of+Kalonia) upon [Deadly Recluse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse) , then used [Mwonvuli Beast Tracker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mwonvuli+Beast+Tracker) to tutor up [Duskdale Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskdale+Wurm) .


Hu decided it was time, so he turned three of his biggest creatures sideways. When Yukuhiro shoved a bunch of soldiers in their way, [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) sent [Captain of the Watch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+of+the+Watch) to its gallows, but thankfully Yukuhiro had [Divine Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Verdict) to keep his soldiers alive.


Hu's creatures were growing every turn, and Yukuhiro's life was slowly trickling away. Knowing that his fatties could never be blocked profitably, Gu attacked with his biggest creatures each turn. This put Yukuhiro in an uncomfortable position. He could either block with [Fog Bank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) s (and take some damage each turn), or gang block with a handful of his creatures (and risk losing them to a timely trick).


The game spiraled out of control when Gu drew not one, but two copies of [Prey Upon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) . This allowed Hu to remove [Fog Bank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) and [Kraken Hatchling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraken+Hatchling) , and Yukuhiro was forced to cast [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) and [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) to delay the inevitable. A trampling [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) eventually finished off Yukuhiro and dashed his chances of making the Top 8 today.


Gu Fengwen wins his match and would need just a draw for a Top 8 berth!

*Gu Fengwen*
