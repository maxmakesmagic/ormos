Best of 2013: Mastering Blue
============================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 6, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Hello everyone!


It's a pleasure to be bringing you the latest brews and technology from the various formats throughout **Magic**, but first I'd like to highlight the best decks from 2013.


The devotion mechanic from *Theros* has already begun to make a splash in Modern, but the first time we really got to see devotion in action was at [Pro Tour *Theros*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptths13/welcome), which was Standard. [The Top 8](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptths13/Top_8_Decks) featured four very different devotion decks, each focusing on a different color.


However, Mono-Blue Devotion had three copies in that Top 8, two in the finals, and won the tournament in one of the most dominant performances in Pro Tour history. In addition to that tournament, Mono-Blue Devotion has been tearing up Standard as of late, so not putting Mono-Blue Devotion on this list would be a huge oversight.


At first glance, the deck might not appear powerful. After all, cards like [Judge's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar), [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), and [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) haven't seen much Constructed play up until this point. Cards like [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) and [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) are only powerful in context—you might need to play some underwhelming cards in order to get Master and Thassa online, but once that happens, you'll be incredibly satisfied to have [Judge's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar) on your side.


On top of that, the deck makes pretty good use of the little creatures with [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) and the incremental damage they provide makes it much easier to close out games quickly (which gives your opponent little time to draw an answer to [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves)).


In general, it's a good idea to try someone's deck card for card before you start making changes. You might be surprised by the efficacy of an unassuming card like [Judge's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar).
