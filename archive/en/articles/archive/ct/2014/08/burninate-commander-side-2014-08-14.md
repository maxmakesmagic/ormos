Burninate the Commander Side
============================



 Posted in **Command Tower**
 on August 14, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






I've heard Commander described dozens of ways. Positive descriptions are the usual fare, but I have my own set of "elevator speech" breakdowns to entice the curious. But not all explanations are so endearing. One I've heard that stands out is: "Isn't that the format where everybody just plays Wraths?"


![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/images/cardart/cardart_smokestack_v14.jpg)
[Smokestack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokestack) | Art by [Daniel Ljunggren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Daniel%20Ljunggren%22%5D)


"Wrath," if you're unfamiliar with the term, derives from [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), the first and arguably most iconic way to clear the battlefield of creatures. A host of flavors, twists, and variants have been printed since then, notably both [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and [Planar Cleansing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing) in current Standard.


Why someone might derisively call Commander the format of Wraths is because, well, there's a little truth in that. Starting with extra life is designed to give players time to set up bigger plays. Casting splashy six-mana creatures and even bigger spells is part of the draw of the format: It's the atypical *Magic* experience compared to the mana curves of Limited and Standard.


Since Commander nudges us to longer, bigger games, it's easy to be pulled down the road of "answer all the things" approaches. There are spells in every color, color combination, and even without color at all that can reset the action and roll back other players' advances. When it's so easy for someone else stymie you, single cards that can take over a game become more important, too.


It's a vicious cycle: You choose stronger individual cards to help fight Wraths, which only leads to everyone carrying more Wraths to fight strong cards.


I won't argue that packing your appropriate version of [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) in a Commander deck is bad, but it's hard to say it's universally good too. There are always tradeoffs to using certain cards and, for Wrath effects one of them is how it smothers much more innocent and fun things creatures can do. Who *really* wants to see a [Phelddagrif](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phelddagrif) bite the dust?


Boomtown
========


Setting aside the downsides of blowing up everything, why do we use the things we do in Commander? It's a question I asked you a few weeks ago, and the answers I got covered a lot of bases, like how Chris pulls in a specific type of Wrath:


I know the focus in this article is on destruction, but there are a few commanders where this simply isn't good enough. My girlfriend plays a very powerful [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons) deck that can ramp into a consistent kill by turn five, usually ending a four-player game by turn eight. Totem armor also means I can't effectively deal with her with a board wipe. There are also decks like [Avacyn, Angel of Hope](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%2C+Angel+of+Hope) that make board wipes a little less appealing.


It is because of commanders like this, where they are indestructible, repeatable, or have totem armor, that I run [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) in any white deck I make. It is a perfect answer for Commander decks that build too specifically around their commanders.




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sigarda,%20Host%20of%20Herons)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda,%20Host%20of%20Herons)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Terminus)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus)





Nothing is as relieving as staring down death on your opponent's board and topdecking a [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) for its miracle cost. It has saved me multiple times, and I once tucked five commanders while keeping my own Kemba in the command zone.


My deck is Boots and Cats (Say it quickly and it sounds like you're beatboxing, so it has earned the official name of "Beatbox Commander") and runs [Kemba, Kha Regent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kemba%2C+Kha+Regent) as my commander. I've got a fair amount of board-wiping ability, and [Worldslayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldslayer) is powerful when equipped at the same time as [Darksteel Plate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Plate) or [Shield of Kaldra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shield+of+Kaldra).

Indeed, the safest places for a powerful commander is on the bottom of a library. But there are other ways unleash devastation, and Andrew knows:


One of my favorite commanders is [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea). Cheap commanders hit the board early, and Thassa helps filter all my draws in a high-variance format. Being an indestructible 5/5 is pretty neat, too. Being mono-blue, however, leaves the deck without many options in terms of removal. While [Rapid Hybridization](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rapid+Hybridization) and [Phyrexian Ingester](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Ingester) are neat, they're the exceptions to the rule.


Instead, this deck is packed with colorless removal cards. My favorites are [Spine of Ish Sah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spine+of+Ish+Sah) and [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated), which are in almost all of my Commander decks anyway. [Spine of Ish Sah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spine+of+Ish+Sah) works great with [Phyrexia's Core](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexia%27s+Core), allowing me to nuke any permanent for only eight mana. Even if Karn dies shortly after I cast him, seven mana to exile a permanent is perfectly reasonable.




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spine%20of%20Ish%20Sah)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spine%20of%20Ish%20Sah)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Karn%20Liberated)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn%20Liberated)





This deck also utilizes [Steel Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Hellkite), [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb), [Lux Cannon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lux+Cannon), and [Tower of Calamities](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+of+Calamities) for extra destructive power. While I don't have enough copies at the moment, I'm looking to bolster this suite with [Oblivion Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Stone), [Nevinyrral's Disk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk), and [Brittle Effigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brittle+Effigy). In general, I love colorless removal due to its power, flexibility, and lack of color identity (so it can go in any deck).


And [Dismiss into Dream](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismiss+into+Dream) gets hilarious with [Curse of the Swine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+the+Swine).
