Five-Color PauperTron
=====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on December 6, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







Today's list combines Pauper, Tron lands, and five colors of spells, which neatly explains why I chose to feature it. As much as I enjoyed playing [Cloudpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost), the format is better off without it, and assembling the set of Urza lands is hard enough that it won't be quite as oppressive to the format as a whole. 


![Urza's Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Urza%27s+Tower)
 That being said, I like casting ridiculous things, and this list certainly delivers on that front. Despite being a five-color deck, there are only seventeen lands here, and none of them tap for colored mana save the lone [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto). That can be explained by the full sixteen color-filtering artifacts, with four [Chromatic Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star)s, [Chromatic Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere)s, [Prismatic Lens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens)es, and [Prophetic Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism)s in attendance. By using all artifacts, the deck doesn't waste its time with more non-Tron lands than necessary, and many of its mana sources draw a card, smoothing out the games. 



[Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) and [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings) look for Tron pieces, and do so in a timely fashion, as we've seen in RG Tron in Modern. Once Tron is assembled, the fun starts, as [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)s and [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle)s let you tear through your deck. [Fangren Marauder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Marauder) is an awesome way to stay alive, and works well with the twelve artifacts that sacrifice themselves (plus, sometimes you get to play against Affinity). Tron also flashes back [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) easily, letting the deck play a one-drop that has utility even when the mana is flowing. 


 The actual winning comes from two copies of [Rolling Thunder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Thunder) and one [Ulamog's Crusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%27s+Crusher), although winning is a formality in many of the games. 


 The sideboard is a work of art as well, and I can barely imagine a more eclectic mix of cards. How often do [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), [Circle of Protection: Green](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circle+of+Protection%3A+Green), and [Earth Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earth+Rift) fight side by side, much less with [Pyroblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroblast) and [Rest for the Weary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+for+the+Weary)?
