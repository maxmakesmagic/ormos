Standard Hardened Scales
========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on August 11, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Many pro players chose to bring [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker) to Pro Tour *Magic Origins*, but it was mostly played alongside [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact)s or [Thopter Spy Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Spy+Network)s. Ken Yukihiro also brought [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker) to the Pro Tour, but he had a much different approach with his deck. Instead of playing a pile of artifacts, Yukihiro played a deck with a +1/+1 counter theme.



[Hardened Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales) is a *Khans of Tarkir* rare that many players just tossed aside as they were building Standard decks this year. While this card can be very strong in the right deck, there weren't really enough ways to exploit in in Standard when this card was released. *Magic Origins* provided us with a few new creatures that go very well in a [Hardened Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales) deck. The aforementioned [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker) is the most obvious new inclusion, but [Managorger Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Managorger+Hydra) also works incredibly well with [Hardened Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales). It starts out as a 1/1 for three mana, but with a Scales in play, every spell cast gives this guy a permanent +2/+2. If this is cast on turn three, it will very quickly grow out of burn or [Bile Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight) range.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hardened+Scales)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales)

[Honored Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honored+Hierarch) is another card from *Magic Origins* that has great synergy with [Hardened Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales). It's capable of becoming a 3/3 on turn two, and can attack *and* accelerate your mana due to vigilance.



There are a lot of fun and synergistic things going on in this deck. We have [Abzan Falconer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Falconer) to give your team flying, [Avatar of the Resolute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+the+Resolute) as a two-mana giant, and [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command)â€”which can do, well, anything. Every card in the deck either protects your team or gives +1/+1 counters.



One card that seems missing from the deck is [Inspiring Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiring+Call). It seems like a great way to protect your creatures while refilling your hand in the process. It looks like Yukuhiro chose to play [Ajani, Mentor of Heroes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Mentor+of+Heroes) as a card advantage engine, but the versatility of [Inspiring Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiring+Call) makes it worth considering.
