Scrubbed Mind
=============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 1, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova












*Sgwashh...sgwashh...sgwashh...bnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnn.* Hello.


Hello.


I've had an adjustment and now I is gone, just for our information.


It is good that it happened. We can listen to the whispers of the trees. They have a lot to say, these wooden colossi. Running the fingers of the hand over the gnarled cracks in the bark now feels like receiving a swift injection of insight. Craning the neck to observe the patterns of the branches now yields a dazzling harvest of detail. Hoisting the body up into the mesh of green, clutching the thick white beams above for support. Thin rays of sunlight dapple the leaves overhead... but no sky. The sky is green and white now. The sky... the sky...


Blu—


*STMMMMMM*!!!


Pressing the ear against the trunk before descending. Listening to the new voice. Slithery jelly words. Mat'Selesnya. The voice is in the tree and in the head.


Climbing down from the tree. One last caress of its oaken flesh. The hands come away scabbed. The hands drop. The hands stare at the tree. [Twitch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twitch)ing from a distance. A sterilized separation from the self. Clawing at the barrier with immobile limbs.


*Bnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnn*.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl215_growing_ranks.jpg)[Growing Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growing+Ranks) | Art by [Seb McKinnon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Seb%20McKinnon%22%5D)


### Voices


The [Voice of the Woods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods).


This innocuous Elf card, originally hailing from *Onslaught*, is a succinct generator of monstrous 7/7 trampling Elemental tokens. Naturally (haw), this breed of token is one of the most exciting prospects for mass population. Some of the other considered candidates include [Godsire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godsire), the [Serra Avatar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Avatar) token that results from [Ajani Goldmane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Goldmane)'s ultimate, and the mighty Marit Lage token hidden under [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths), which, with a bit of [Mirror Gallery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Gallery) hackery, can be populated (to create the most fearsome creature force ever).

In the end, [Voice of the Woods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods) was called back and eventually won the part. Now for the primary method of population. A total of eleven cards bear the new mechanic of Selesnya, some of which are also mentioned in today's column. With this deck, [Growing Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growing+Ranks) seemed like a mildly amusing way of accumulating a bunch of 7/7 trampling tokens, as it only requires a down payment of four green mana, and instantly starts humming. [Druid's Deliverance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid%27s+Deliverance) felt right for this deck as well, as a combat damage prevention effect is a worthy trick to consider in most green deck skeletons.

These effects of population should always be pointed at the 7/7s, but if [Voice of the Woods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods) hasn't shown up yet, there are some other token options. Since [Voice of the Woods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods) needs other Elves to tap, the deck was filled with many producers of 1/1 Elf tokens. [Gilt-Leaf Ambush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilt-Leaf+Ambush) is a handy spell that can often dole out a venomous surprise. [Hunting Triad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Triad) is a versatile spell that can either make three more Elves or slap three +1/+1 counters on a creature. [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) is a great power booster for Elves and can steadily churn out an army of followers. The singleton [Rhys the Redeemed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) holds a beloved spot in the **Magic**al minds of many, and for good reason (that being: It's a [Parallel Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Evolution)... on a stick!) Finally, [Spitting Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Image), which can [Clone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone)-token any creature on the board. Imagine casting a [Spitting Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Image) on an opponent's insane creature, and slowly populating it with [Growing Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growing+Ranks). Fuzzy.

Other Elves swarmed into the deck, providing typically Elvish services. [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) is the quintessential "mana Elf." [Llanowar Mentor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Mentor), meanwhile, is a nifty one-drop that produces token copies of [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) (that can be populated!!). Does that make [Llanowar Mentor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Mentor) the "mana Elf Elf?"


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl215_endless_elves.jpg)[Thornweald Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornweald+Archer) and [Elvish Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Harbinger) were the last recruits. The latter Elf can find any creature in the deck, including the singleton [Ezuri, Renegade Leader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ezuri%2C+Renegade+Leader), which can provide a solid secondary battle plan. Among the land choices, [Gargoyle Castle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gargoyle+Castle) seemed a fitting inclusion.

### The Voice of Selesnya


Trostani, the "dryad triad." Tacking an [Angelic Chorus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Chorus) effect onto a repeatable source of population is a recipe for insanity. The legend is open-ended enough to promote all sorts of wacky decks. [Phyrexian Processor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Processor), for instance, has a new bestie.


 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| 
Trostani, Selesnya's Voice
 | 
Angelic Chorus
 |

It seems like a worthy experiment to construct a Trostani deck out of the available Standard card pool. Within these confines, there were some juicy tokens that looked fun to populate. [Grove of the Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Guardian) can plonk out a vigilant 8/8. The rumbling [Armada Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armada+Wurm), for example, brings a 5/5 trampling playmate to the party, [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) style. [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) continues to astound, and its 3/3 Beast is always welcome under Trostani's watch.


 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|

 | 
Armada Wurm
 |

What's fascinating about those last two creatures is their complete synergy with the flickering sub-vibe of *Avacyn Restored*. Two of that set's soaring Angels play very nicely in this list. [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) is the current critical darling, but her kid sister [Emancipation Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emancipation+Angel) does excellent work as well. The rest of the deck's creatures certainly appreciate the flickering climate. [Gatecreeper Vine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) and Borderline Ranger are helpful mana-smoothing cards that trigger Trostani, and [Captain of the Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+of+the+Watch) provides another raw boost of power.


One recently printed card that seems gaga with Trostani is [Rhox Faithmender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender). Boasting a toughness of 5, the Faithmender's life-doubling is an obvious combination with Trostani. With the pair on the field, an incoming [Tree of Redemption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tree+of+Redemption) will jolt your life up by 26 points!

Four singletons give the deck some variance. [Collective Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing) is a thudding boon for any sort of token deck, including this one. [Ajani, Caller of the Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Caller+of+the+Pride) has grown on me since its release (it's probably the double strike.) And two miracles round things out. [Terminus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) can sweep the board, and [Entreat the Angels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entreat+the+Angels) can pour flying 4/4s into your ranks. Populate can turn even a minimal Entreat into a fully-fledged one, over time.

### The Voice of Avery Garon


This person emailed in a decklist with a curious idea: turn [Phantom General](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+General) into a token, so it can pump itself. Chuckles. Use [Rite of Replication](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication). Guffaws.


 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| 
Phantom General
 | 
Rite of Replication
 |

The email read: "I decided to break the mold and the card by making [Phantom General](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+General) a token. It pumps itself, it pumps other copies of itself. I figured ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) would be a good combination of colors, so I could include [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart) and [Rite of Replication](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication). I splashed green for [Parallel Lives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Lives) and the new Selesnya mechanic, populate. [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) pumps the [Phantom General](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+General)s as well as the [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), who makes his own tokens of the Angel variety. [Rhys the Redeemed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed), [Growing Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growing+Ranks), [Wayfaring Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wayfaring+Temple), and [Followed Footsteps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Followed+Footsteps) round out my token-producers. Finally, I included the great token pumper [Intangible Virtue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue) and [Idyllic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Idyllic+Tutor) to find any of the enchantments."
