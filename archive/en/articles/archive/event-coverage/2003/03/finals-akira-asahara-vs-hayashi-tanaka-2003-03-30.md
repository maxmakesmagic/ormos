Finals: Akira Asahara vs. Hayashi Tanaka
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on March 30, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpkyo03/a897.jpg)The long weekend culminates in this. Asahara's blue-white deck against Tanaka's red-green monstrosity, with cash and glory on the line. Both players are surprisingly relaxed considering the stakes. Fatigue definitely plays a part in that. Both have little energy left to play up to the crowd. They need their best focused on the game at hand.

### Game 1

Asahara had to send back his first hand, but his second was acceptable. [Imagecrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) showed up a turn late, but after that it was [Cloudreach Cavalry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudreach+Cavalry) and [Wingbeat Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingbeat+Warrior) to start the pain. Tanaka sat back on morph and Trimberwatch Elf. [Crested Craghorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crested+Craghorn) took care of the Cavalry, and Wingbeat traded with the morph, [Imagecrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) turning off the Timberwatch's ability.

Asahara came back with [Mistform Seaswift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Seaswift). Tanaka tried a [Treespring Lorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treespring+Lorian), but Asahara had [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and another morph. All Tanaka could muster was a [Berserk Murlodont](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserk+Murlodont). When Asahara flipped over [Quicksilver Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Dragon), Tanaka decided it was time to move on.

Asahara 1 – Tanaka 0

### Game 2

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpkyo03/a895.jpg)Tanaka got exactly the start he needed against Asahara's air force for Game 2: Back-to-back Timberwatch Elves followed by [Airdrop Condor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Airdrop+Condor) to snarl the air. Asahara had opened with [Stoic Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoic+Champion) and facedown [Ascending Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascending+Aven). He added [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) and [Mistform Seaswift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Seaswift) to the mix. Tanaka made a [Treespring Lorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treespring+Lorian), getting ready to attack.

Asahara mulled over the situation. He decided to [Sandskin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sandskin) the Condor and hit for two, before putting down [Imagecrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter). He took five from the incoming Lorian and then watched as Tanaka played a post-combat [Menacing Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Menacing+Ogre). Tanaka won the bid three to zero. The life totals were fifteen to thirteen in favor of Asahara.

It wasn't looking good for him, though. He hit for another two and dropped a [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber). Tanaka tapped seven. [Mythic Proportions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mythic+Proportions) for the Lorian. Asahara pretended like it wasn't the end, but it was.

Asahara 1 – Tanaka 1

### Game 3

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpkyo03/a896.jpg)Now it was Tanaka's turn to mulligan. With Asahara leading off, it wasn't looking good for him. Asahara started with a morph. Tanaka played a very unthreatening [Flamewave Invoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamewave+Invoker). Asahara showed him [Ascending Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascending+Aven). Tanaka hit for two and played [Symbiotic Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf), apparently drawing from the less-than-insane side of his deck. Asahara hit for three in the air and made another morph.

Tanaka untapped and frowned. He had no fifth land. He cycled [Barkhide Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barkhide+Mauler) into [Tranquil Thicket](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tranquil+Thicket) before sending in the Invoker. Asahara blocked with his morph, which turned into [Gravel Slinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravel+Slinger) to stay alive. On his turn he unmorphed [Mistform Seaswift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Seaswift) and dropped Tanaka to just eleven. [Keeper of the Nine Gales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keeper+of+the+Nine+Gales) provided slightly more pressure.

Tanaka was stymied. He had nothing to handle the aerial beats. He cycled [Macetail Hystrodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Macetail+Hystrodon) in desperation, but that was the death knell. Asahara hit for seven, and Tanaka extended the hand.

**Final Result:** Akira Asahara defeats Hayashi Tanaka 2-1
