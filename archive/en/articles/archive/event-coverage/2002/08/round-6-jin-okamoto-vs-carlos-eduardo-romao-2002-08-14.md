Round 6: Jin Okamoto vs. Carlos Eduardo Romao
=============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 14, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a901.jpg)All four of the Round 6 Feature Matches are between undefeated players to see which four of them will get to go to sleep tonight without a loss in the Standard format. 

The Americans are nowhere to be found; instead, players from France, Singapore, Hong Kong, the Netherlands, Malaysia, and Ireland are fighting it out at the top. This match features two competitors from two more countries, Jin Okamoto of Japan and Carlos Eduardo Romao from Brazil. (When it was pointed out that there are no Americans at 5-0, Carlos pointed out that he is a *South* American.) 

Jin is here on Pro points, and is the current APAC champion. Carlos finished in the Top 8 of Latin American Championships this year, and both players have won Grand Prix in their careers.

Both players are playing [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) decks—Carlos has the more traditional build, and Jin is splashing red in his. Jin played first in Game 1 and [Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice)d one of Carol's early lands, but he was unable to find a third land of his own. Carlos was more fortunate in that department, playing land for each of the first five turns.

Jin kept looking for land with cantrips, mainly two [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)s on the [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) Carlos kept playing, and another [Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice) on a land. Jin finally got his land situation straightened out, but not before Carlos got both the Familiar and a [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) in play.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a900.jpg)Jin got out his own Familiar to stem the bleeding, but his attempt at a [Flametongue Kavu](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu) was nullified by a [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). On Carlos's following turn, Jin tapped out to [Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice) one of the Brazilian's seven lands, and then was forced to chumpblock the [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) with his own Familiar for fear of dying to a [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)-fed monster. Had he simply tapped the 'Tog, he could have kept his Familiar.

Jin recovered somewhat with his own [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) on the following turn, and even managed to defend it from a surprise [Chainer's Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict) with a [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). He later put out a second 'Tog and the game ground to a halt.

Both players spent the next few turns trying to build ideal hands, playing in virtual silence because of the language barrier, depending on hand signals and overt gestures to indicate the end of phases and turns. A flurry of [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)s and [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)es left Carlos with the upper hand of a [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short), an [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), and tons of countermagic. After a permission war over the [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short) went Carlos's way, Jin extended the hand.

Carlos Eduardo Romao 1- Jin Okamoto 0

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a899.jpg)Romao sideboarded in a handful of [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)es for Game 2, and they came in handy. He cast one on each of the first two turns, nabbing a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) and a [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Jin put out a [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), but the Carlos flipped over five cards in a nightmarish [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction): two lands, two more Facts, and a [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis). Jin shook his head, sighed, and then split the piles. Carlos kept a Fact and the lands.

Carlos put out his own Familiar, which was Edicted, but then resolved another devastating [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), netting two more spells and another [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) in the yard. Jin, on the other hand, was not drawing any card-drawing spells. With only a single card in hand, he could only look on in horror as Carlos cast [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) again, then fed it to his 'Tog and Wished for it. With that kind of card advantage, one 'Tog hit was bound to be lethal, and that's precisely what happened.

**Final Result:** Carlos Eduardo Romao 2 – Jin Okamoto 0
