Lanthier Commands Victory in Quebec City!
=========================================




Dan Lanthier makes it two-for-two in Grand Prix Top 8s and wins with a birthday victory over Omar Beldon at Quebec City!

TOP 8 COVERAGE



Dan Lanthier had a beautiful birthday in *La Belle Province*, earning his second Grand Prix victory in as many Top 8 appearances at Quebec City. Facing down the indomitable nineteenth-ranked Reid Duke in the quarterfinals, Lanthier marched through his opponents Oliver Polak-Rottmann, winner of Grand Prix Utrecht 2014, and Day 1 undefeated player Omar Beldon in back-to-back Dark Jeskai Mirror matches.


While the rise – as the largest slice of Day 2 – and fall of Abzan to Dark Jeskai seemed inevitable by the time half of the Top 8 was decided with the deck, the march of Standard wasn’t quite over. Jake Mondello earned his second Top 8 birth on the back of Red-Green Eldrazi, built to ramp straight to Ugin, the Spirit Dragon and Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger. Pascal Maynard added Rally the Ancestors to the Blue-Black and Black-Green Aristocrats decks to make an amalgam of a puzzle that took every round to take him into Top 8 as well. Reid Duke put on a show with Esper Control, albeit hiding some Dragons away from Crackling Doom in the sideboard, and even Abzan cracking the final octet thanks to the power of Anafenza, the Foremost.


For now, the soaring power of Jace, Vryn’s Prodigy and Jeskai decks in general will be the talk of the town heading into Grand Prix Indianapolis and the next stage of Premier play with Standard. For Dan Lanthier, Standard is right where it’s supposed to be for a birthday gift of a second Grand Prix win in two Top 8s.


Congratulations to Dan Lanthier, champion of Grand Prix Quebec City!
