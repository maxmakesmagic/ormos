Throwback Standard Gauntlet 8: Innistrad Standard
=================================================



 Posted in **Magic Online**
 on October 25, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Headshot%209-2014_3.jpg)
By Randy Buehler











This gauntlet covers September 2011 through August 2013: the two years when *Innistrad* was legal in Standard. [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) was certainly one of the defining things going on, but there were several other things you could do: mid-range/big creature decks to satisfy your inner Timmy, plus sacrifice-based combo shenanigans to keep your inner Johnny happy.


Note: This event can be found in the **Limited Leagues**area of *Magic Online*play lobby.


**Innistrad Standard Throwback Standard Gauntlet League**


* Dates: November 1-8
* Location: Play Lobby -> Limited Tournaments -> Leagues
* Entry Options
	+ 10 Event Tickets
	+ 100 Play Points
* Product: One randomly-selected phantom deck from the below list. The cards in the deck will not be added to your collection.
* Structure: Deck review, followed by 3 Swiss rounds
* Prizes:
	+ 3 wins: 150 Play Points
	+ 2 wins: 100 Play Points
	+ 1 win: 40 Play Points
	+ 0 wins: 10 Play Points

**Pod**


While the power of [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) was only fully realized in the bigger card pool of Modern, where multiple infinite combos could be stitched together, it also saw a fair amount of play in Standard purely as a value card. [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) is an unsurprising superstar, with the rest of the deck consisting primarily of mana creatures and creatures that provide value when they either enter or leave the battlefield.

**Delver**


It may not have won a Pro Tour, but the most dominant deck of 2012 was without a doubt Delver. There were seven Standard-format Grand Prix held in the first half of the year, and Delver decks won fully six of them! **Junk Reanimator**


When Martin Juza won Grand Prix Bochum with a crazy [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) concoction, he credited Brad Nelson for the deck design. As the year continued and more cards got added to Standard, the [Craterhoofs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=425947) (and [Somberwald Sages](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=275711)) were left behind in favor of [Thragtusks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=430614) and [Angels of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=405126). The fundamental synergy between [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage), [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), and [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) formed the core of this deck, relying on support from mana Elves. Altogether, variations of this deck won two more Grand Prixs before the format rotated.

**UWR Flash**


Worlds 2013 was won by Shahar Shenhar, wielding what we would now call Jeskai Control as his Standard deck. It’s similar to the Esper control decks, in that it can trade resources and win with the card advantage from a big [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). But unlike those decks, this one has a lot more creatures, most of which can be played at instant speed, making it capable of shifting roles and winning the game surprisingly quickly.
