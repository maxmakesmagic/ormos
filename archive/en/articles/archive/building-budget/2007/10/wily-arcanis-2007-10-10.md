For this week's evolution, we were going to try things a little differently. Instead of adding and changing a ton of cards all at once, we were going to play a few games with the deck, and then examine one set's worth of cards to see which cards would make a good addition to the deck. By using this method, we will be able to see the strengths and weaknesses of the deck, and find cards that will plug those holes. I also made the decision to stick to sets that will be Standard-legal once *Ravnica* Block rotates, so *Ravnica*, *Guildpact*,and *Dissension* were not used for this article.

In order to do this, we need to get some games under our belt, don't we? Well, let's get a-playin'! Time waits for no man, and neither does the Standard casual playtest room on **Magic Online**.

**Game 1: Lord of Halitosis (Blue-White-Black Control)**

I drop [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave), follow it with [Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl), and begin beating for 3. He drops double [Court Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar). When he's at 10, I drop [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental). He plays Teferi, which I [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) on my turn, letting me beat for 5. He plays [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), which I attempt to [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel)—but he [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)s my [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). I untap, use [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) to steal his Shapeshifter (which is copying my [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental)), and swing for lethal!

Record: 1-0

Just as a side note, [Rod of Ruin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rod+of+Ruin) was in my hand this game... and was as useless as predicted last week. Seven mana (four to play, three to use) is a lot to spend to deal one point of damage, especially considering that blue has cards like [Prodigal Sorcerer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Sorcerer), [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor), and [Pirate Ship](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pirate+Ship) that can deal the damage without a mana cost past the initial investment of a permanent.

![Persuasion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Persuasion)**Game 2: hillbilly\_magic (Green-White Gauntlet of Might)**He gets down an early [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu) and then hits me with [Mwonvuli Acid-Moss](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mwonvuli+Acid-Moss) to bring me down to two lands. I build up with [Phantom Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior) (to block a [Civic Wayfinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder)). He follows with [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer). I play and throw [Aven Fisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fisher) in front of his 10/7 Kudzu to drop to one life, and then play [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) on his Kudzu.

He then plays [Yavimaya Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Dryad), gives me a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (to forestwalk,) and moves the Warhammer to his 2/1 guy. I [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) that as well, and swing for 8. He plays [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), and I play [Counsel of the Soratami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counsel+of+the+Soratami). He drops a second Warhammer, and I play [Denizen of the Deep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Denizen+of+the+Deep). He answers with [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), and I follow with [Tidings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidings) and a [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) on a [Panglacial Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panglacial+Wurm). By this point he has two [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power) out, both set to green.

I drop [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental), with [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon), [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon), [Remove Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) and [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) in my hand thanks to the [Tidings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidings). Five turns later, I've stopped any creature he dropped to the board by bouncing it to his hand, [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel)ed his third [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) of the game, and won in the air, coming back from a 30-1 life score.

Record: 2-0

And, yet again, that [Rod of Ruin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rod+of+Ruin). Never had a time where I could tap out and have it be useful, versus trying to stay alive, or dropping cards which have a more lasting impact on the game.

**Game 3: Noodles23 (Mono-Green Aggro)**

He drops a second-turn [Scryb Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger). No problems, think I, with a hand full of countermagic, [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion)s, and a rum-tum-tum "ROD OF RUIN! TM" Then he plays TRIPLE [Might of Old Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Old+Krosa) on his Ranger, hits me for thirteen on turn three, and smashes me the rest of the way with [Moldervine Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak).

Record: 2-1

After three games, I'm ready to make the first revision to the deck. So far, the card drawing, bounce, countermagic, and flying creatures have been working for the deck. What hasn't been working? Well, I'm a bit short on creature control (outside of [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion)), and the second game was a bit close for my tastes. Luckily, *Future Sight* has a really good control card in [Second Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Wind). 

[Second Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Wind) has three uses in this deck. More straightforward, it allows me to tap down an opposing creature each turn. When used like this, it is a slightly more expensive [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism). A second use is as a bad Vigiliance—if I put it on one of my own creatures, that creature can play both offense and defense. Last, it can allow any creature with a tap activation ability to use its ability twice in one turn. In this case, the only creature like that in the deck so far is Arcanis.

**Game 4: Babybull (Mono-White Soldiers)**

Thanks to judicious use of [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion), [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang), [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon), and Arcanis to draw three cards a turn, I make attacking a nightmare for him and his double [Celestial Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Crusader), triple [Field Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+Marshal) draw—anytime he attacks, I throw off the math horribly and am able to block favorably. Double [Mahamoti Djinn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mahamoti+Djinn) (one [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone), one with [Second Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Wind) on it) allow me all the offense I need to win.

**Game 5: lobo20 (Mono-White Flankers)**

[Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) wins the day here, as I grab both a [Pentarch Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentarch+Paladin) (set to blue) and a [Cavalry Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavalry+Master). [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) stops him from killing me too quickly, and [Cloud Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental) plus double [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave) come in through the air.

![Serra Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Serra+Sphinx)**Game 6: gravyj (Mono-White Soldiers)**Once again, [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) plus large creatures (Arcanis, [Scalpelexis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis)) save the day. [Scalpelexis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis) isn't great though—I'd have rather have had a large power than the 5 toughness and a special ability that isn't so special.

Record: 5-1

So far so good. Game 6 was the first game in which I drew [Scalpelexis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis), but it just wasn't that good—aside from basic lands, there is very little chance of [Scalpelexis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis) hitting two cards with the same name, and there are so many decks that play non-basic lands these days that that's not necessarily a strong deal. I'd rather have a creature that can beat for damage (or block and kill) than try fancy decking tricks.

Thankfully, *Planar Chaos* has just the card we're looking for! [Serra Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Sphinx) is the "timeshifted" [Serra Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel), which is the original big flying beater. [Serra Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Sphinx) is [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) with a free ability (vigilance). It is almost strictly better than [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) (aside from the fact that [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) is an Elemental, which matters for *Lorwyn*'s Elemental tribe). If I'm going to be running large flying creatures, why not run the one that is a strict upgrade?

To make room for a playset of Sphinxes, I simply took out four other flying creatures (two of which happened to be rare)—[Mahamoti Djinn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mahamoti+Djinn), [Scalpelexis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis), [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) and [Aven Fisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fisher). Functionally, [Serra Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Sphinx) and [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) / [Mahamoti Djinn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mahamoti+Djinn) serve the same role. [Aven Fisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fisher) is more of a road bump than a beater, and [Scalpelexis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis) is more suited for a deck that wins by decking—my deck has been winning by beating down!

**Game 7: skish27 (White-Black Comes-Into-Play Creatures)**

I get beaten because just as I set up, as he kills all my blockers with [Devouring Light](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Light) and [Sudden Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death). He has an army of creatures, including [Shrieking Grotesque](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrieking+Grotesque), [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats), and multiple [Blind Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Hunter)s.

Record: 5-2

**Game 8: cid86 (Mono-Black Discard)**

He triple-mulligans and still wins—[Mindlash Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindlash+Sliver), plus [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) and [Underworld Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams). He uses [Funeral Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Funeral+Charm) to take out an early attacking [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave), and I never recover enough mana to get into the game.

Record: 5-3

**Game 9: SaeKaos (Black-Red-White Slivers)**

I [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) his Slivers one by one and draw fourteen extra cards between Arcanis and [Thieving Magpie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Magpie). Even though he kills a bunch of my guys with [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify), he can't deal with me dropping a ridiculous air force to the board.

Record: 6-3

In two of the three games above, I lost for three reasons: 1) I didn't have any sort of early offense or defense to stop my opponent from getting in an early beat, 2) My hand was filled with high-cost cards which were dealt with as they hit the table one-by-one because I couldn't both playand protect them, and 3) since my hand was jammed with five-cost spells, I was easy pickings for a discard strategy.

I looked over the card list of *Time Spiral* for blue, artifact and land cards—the list was truly massive because of the timeshifted cards! *Time Spiral* has all sorts of options for a blue deck: bounce ([Snapback](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapback), [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)), large beaters ([Deep-Sea Kraken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep-Sea+Kraken)), card drawing ([Careful Consideration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration), [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice)), and countermagic ([Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy)). This is where I needed to decide exactly which direction I should take the deck.

There are two distinct paths for this deck—to play offense or defense. If I were to play offense, I'd want to move away from controlling cards (countermagic, stall) and pack the deck with early creatures to attack with. If I were to play defense, I'd want to move away from early offensive drops ([Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl), [Cloud Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental)) and move towards more stall (bounce, countermagic) and card drawing, to get to my big finishers.

In the end, I decided to go with the aggressive, offensively oriented build. So far, the games that I've won have been on a good curve of creatures, followed by a small amount of control / bounce just to seal the game. I don't want to wait for my opponent to come to me—I want to bring the game to him!

![Voidmage Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Voidmage+Prodigy)There is also a small Wizard theme going on in *Time Spiral* Block. [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy) is a walking [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), except it beats for 2 damage a turn! Why sit back with three mana untapped waiting for a spell to come when I could swing for 2 damage a turn with two mana untapped for the same effect? Sure, [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy) is a bit more fragile than a card in hand, but if my goal is to win, I want to swing.[Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr) is a straight swap for [Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl). I don't have any one-drops in my deck that are worth playing on turn one ([Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) doesn't count!), aside from [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave) (which I want to put into play as early as possible so I don't stunt my mana curve later on). While I do feel that in a void [Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) is a better card than [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr) (due to evasion), [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr) is a Wizard, and can be sacrificed to [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy)!

Speaking of mana curves, I want to be more active with my spells. That means that my reactive spells don't have as much of a place in the deck—so cards like [Remove Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) and [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) can take the wayside for [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), an early card-drawing spell that fills my early-mana slot alongside [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy). 

The last two additions to my deck are a single [Willbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender) and a single [Teferi, Mage of Zhalfir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Mage+of+Zhalfir). Teferi is a bit pricy (2.5 to 3 tickets), but he's a good complement to Arcanis. In addition, I skipped over [Vedalken Aethermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Aethermage) in my first go-around with *Future Sight*. With a few one-of Wizards (and the preponderance of Sliver decks in the casual room), [Vedalken Aethermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Aethermage) might be a good addition when I revisit *Future Sight*.

**Game 10: casperwes (Black-Green Saprolings)**

I get stuck at three mana, and he runs me over with Saprolings and [Fallen Ideal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Ideal). He doesn't kill me by sacrificing all of his guys at once due to the fear of bounce, but his token generation gives him inevitability after my slow start.

Record: 6-4

**Game 11: Nik83 (Blue-Red Suspend)**

I get double [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy) and a [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave), with [Thieving Magpie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Magpie) and [Cloud Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental) to assist. He gets [Rift Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Elemental) and suspends [Reality Strobe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Strobe), and then puts [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) on the board. He copies my Magpie, and when he tries to bring Strobe out of suspend during my combat using his [Rift Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Elemental), I shut it down with my Teferi. That proves to be the game-winner, as my assault is relentless.

Record: 7-4

![Cloud Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cloud+Elemental)**Game 12: Tolstoy (Black-Blue Eyes)**He gets an early [Fathom Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), and follows it with [Prodigal Sorcerer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Sorcerer) and [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor). My opening hand is [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave), [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), [Cloud Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental) and four [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s. My first draw is [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr), which completely changes the way I play this hand—I lead with the Sage and follow with [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) and then [Cloud Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental). I draw into [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion), grab his [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor), put another [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave) on the board, and use [Quicksand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksand) plus [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor) to hold down [Evil Eye of Urborg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Eye+of+Urborg).

Record: 8-4

I take a look at *Coldsnap*, and find that there are only two real contenders—[Martyr of Frost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Frost) and [Phyrexian Ironfoot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Ironfoot). Playing the Ironfoot would require me to turn all of my lands to [Snow-Covered Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Island)s, which would cost a good six to eight tickets—not exactly what I want in a budget deck! Plus, I've done that deck before with [Blue Snow Aggro](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb80) (in fact, literally a year ago as of this column!)

Just as an aside—it's amazing how versatile of a game **Magic** is! Look at the Blue Snow Aggro deck above, and then look at where we are with this Arcanis deck. Both are blue decks built around beating down, but they are completely different in how they play out, and what cards they use to win! Blue Snow Aggro went for the quick win with ground pounders, tapping creatures, and a twelve-shot of countermagic. The modified Arcanis's [Guile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guile) deck starts with early beats that double as stall, comes in through the air with mid-range creatures, and then uses Arcanis to refill the hand and [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) to turn its opponent's creatures against them. I love **Magic**, and this is the reason why—two blue aggro decks that are completely different, just a year apart!

### *Coldsnap*

None

Back to *Tenth Edition*! I want a little more beatdown in the air, and [Cloud Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental) (a.k.a. Puffy) has been great for that so far. I also want to be able to come back from behind, or punch through that last bits of damage. Good ol' Standby [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) is perfect for this; +3/+0, trample and lifelink go a long ways to victory. Thankfully, the Warhammer was an uncommon in *Mirrodin* and has been printed in three sets now (*Mirrodin*, *Ninth*, and *Tenth*) so it is not an uber-expensive rare.

I take out [Citanul Flute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Citanul+Flute) (which I've drawn all of twice, and it was dead in my hand), a [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy), the [Tidings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidings), and a [Serra Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Sphinx) to make room for these other four cards. I'm still jammed a little high on high-mana cards (quad Sphinx, quad [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion), triple Arcanis, Teferi), so dropping a Sphinx, a [Tidings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidings) and a Flute in favor of three-drop cards doesn't hurt. I debated between killing another Sphinx, the [Willbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender), or a Prodigy as the fourth card, but I decided in the end that with [Vedalken Aethermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Aethermage) coming up in the next revision, I wanted that one [Willbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender) in the deck still—and I liked having a trio of 4/4 vigilant flyers.

This leaves the three [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy) as my only countermagic in the deck—but with blue mana untapped, people are going to assume I have countermagic in my hand. This sort of bluff against people who are scared about [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s will be almost as effective as having countermagic itself.

**Game 16: ucinv (Black-Red Madness Control)**

We go back and forth for quite a while, trading card for card. He gets [Skullmead Cauldron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullmead+Cauldron) and Madnesses out a bunch of guys ([Grave Scrabbler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Scrabbler), [Nightshade Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Assassin)), and I get down [Serra Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Sphinx) with a [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer). The key play comes when we are back at parity, and he drops [Woebringer Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woebringer+Demon) when my only creature on the board is Arcanis. During my upkeep, I tap Arcanis to draw three cards, pay ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) to return him to my hand (which kills his [Woebringer Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woebringer+Demon)), and then drop a second [Serra Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Sphinx) onto the board with double [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave) backup. He can't recover from that card advantage (four for none), and I win.

Record: 11-5

He drops [Thallid Germinator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid+Germinator) and puts [Followed Footsteps](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Followed+Footsteps) on it. Record: 13-5

I definitely liked the approach of visiting one set at a time to tweak the deck. By playing the deck a few games at a time, and then only visiting a limited card pool, I was able to let the deck breathe its own life, and show me the way to shore up its strengths and weaknesses. If I hadn't been beating down with [Cloud Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental) and [Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) in the first few games, I might not have moved the deck to a more early beatdown focus with [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy) later on. Sampling quite a few different types of cards ([Scalpelexis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalpelexis), [Mahamoti Djinn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mahamoti+Djinn)) allowed me to see what would or wouldn't work in the deck, and then find appropriate replacements. This is definitely a playtest method I found very useful.
