Devoted to Rakdos
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 30, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







One of the cards I was most interested in playing after doing my set review was Gray Merchant of Asphodel. It just so happens that Josh Silvestri wrote about a sweet list that utilized him. In [his article](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/silvestri-says-standard-week-one/) Josh goes over a number of Standard decks, but the Black-Red Devotion deck is the one I'd like to focus on. 


![Gray Merchant of Asphodel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel)
 The deck slants more toward control than anything else, but has enough medium-sized threats that it can actually win games by attacking. The curve of [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) into [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon) does threaten to kill the opponent very quickly, and even when stalled Gray Merchants can come down to finish things off. Gray Merchant also combines well with the non-creature permanents the deck plays, namely [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) and [Whip of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos). Both those enchantments contribute BB toward devotion, and both synergize well with Gray Merchant. [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) lets you trade life for more cards, and [Whip of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) both gains you a bunch of life and brings back Merchants. 


 This may sound a bit slow, but luckily black and red have plenty of removal. Between [Anger of the Gods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger+of+the+Gods), [Dreadbore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore), Hero's Downfall, and [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), this deck is well equipped to deal with all manner of threats. It's especially good against Planeswalkers, with six direct kill spells and a pair of Rakdos's Returns to handle any stray Jaces or Domris. 


 I also like how much card draw is in the deck. The aforementioned [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) are supplemented by three [Chandra, Pyromaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Pyromaster), and two [Read the Bones](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Bones), which should give the deck access to a steady stream of removal and threats.
