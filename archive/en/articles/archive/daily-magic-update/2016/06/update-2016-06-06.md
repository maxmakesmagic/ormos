The June 6, 2016 update
=======================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on June 6, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on June 6, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by Seth Manfield, occasionally good *Magic* player (when the "occasion" is "whenever he plays *Magic*.")


What You Might Have Missed This Weekend
=======================================


1. [The World Champ Does It Again!](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpcos16)

Is Seth Manfield even real at this point? After his World Championship win last season, people expected him to either 1) continue to play at the highest level of the game or 2) fall back to earth a bit. Instead, Manfield has gone with option 3, "blow everyone out of the water." Currently leading the Player of the Year race, the race for Grand Prix Master (where he's tied), and pretty much anything else he's eligible to be in front of, Manfield is having a historic season. Check out the [Top 5 Moments](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpcos16/top-5-moments-2016-06-05), [Manfield's Finals win](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpcos16/seth-manfield-vs-brandon-fischer-2016-06-05), and both the [Top 8](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpcos16/top-8-decklists-2016-06-05) and [Top 32](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpcos16/9-32-decklists-2016-06-05) decklists to get a snapshot of the weekend.


1. [StarCityGames.com Atlanta Open Weekend](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/040616_atlanta.html)

Tom Ross took down another Open (Standard, this time, with Red-White Humans), Gerry Thompson started 16-0, and Merfolk won the Modern Classic. That's a lot of action to pack into one weekend. You can find decklists, deck techs, a metagame breakdown, and player profiles [on the Atlanta Open Weekend page](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/040616_atlanta.html), and you can check out the [Day Two replay here](https://www.twitch.tv/scglive/v/70748240).


1. [*Magic: The Gathering* Office Hours: Olivia Voldaren](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=otQWE3DXURs) | Tolarian Community College | The Professor

Christine Sprankle, or in this case Olivia Voldaren, stops by office hours to give The Professor notice. And maybe get some Five Guys.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/otQWE3DXURs>


1. [Top 8 Cards *Not* Seeing Play in Modern](http://www.channelfireball.com/videos/top-8-cards-not-seeing-play-in-modern/) | ChannelFireball | Mashi Scanlan

The best of the rest, this is a fun segment where Mashi Scanlan and Paul Cheon dive into a list that's probably better framed as the Top 8 underplayed cards, since they're all too powerful for people not to at least experiment with them. You know, like in college...when you experimented with [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) in White-Black Tokens. Ah, to be young again.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Grand Prix Costa Rica Champion](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpcos16/top-8-decklists-2016-06-05)

Mere weeks after winning [Grand Prix New York](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpny16) with White-Black Control, reigning World Champion Seth Manfield went the other direction and took down Grand Prix Costa Rica with Green-White Tokens, a deck that has solidified itself at the top of the Standard Metagame.


Manfield didn't do much that was fancy with his list. The one [Tragic Arrogance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Arrogance) is somewhat nonstandard, and the single [Declaration in Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Declaration+in+Stone) is a nod to other token decks, but otherwise the list is pretty stock, with a few numbers tweaked here and there. If you want to test with or against Green-White Tokens, start here.
