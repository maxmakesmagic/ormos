Round 12: Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa (Blue-Black Control) vs. Makihito Mihara (Black-Green Constellation)
=======================================================================================================



 Posted in [COVERAGE ARCHIVE](/en/events/coverage)
 on April 11, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/henke_author.jpg)
By Tobi Henke










This round, two members of the Hall of Fame faced off in the feature match area: Brazilian Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, and Japan's Makihito Mihara. With records of 8-3, both were still very much in contention for a Top 8 berth at this event.




Well, *another* Top 8, one should say. For Mihara, that would be his sixth, while Damo da Rosa, currently sitting at nine Pro Tour Top 8s, could reach double digits! Of course, all of that was at least four rounds away, a mere dream which shouldn't distract from the job at hand: winning this match.



To help him do so, Damo da Rosa had brought a Blue-Black Control deck, dabbling in white for [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai), one of the standards of the new Standard. Meanwhile, Mihara, known for his unusual deck choices, had brewed up an interesting version of Green-Black Constellation featuring [See the Unwritten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+the+Unwritten).



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/ptdtk/r12_pv_mihara.jpg)  



The Games
=========



Mihara opened with the infamous duo of [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) and [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix). The latter met Dissolve, but Mihara's next play of [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) resolved uncontested. As did [See the Unwritten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+the+Unwritten) a couple of turns later, after some of Mihara's smaller creatures died to Damo da Rosa's removal spells. [See the Unwritten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+the+Unwritten) put [Polukranos, World Eater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater) onto the battlefield and also put [Doomwake Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomwake+Giant) into Mihara's graveyard.



On his next turn, Mihara's Polukranos successfully attacked for 7. Damo da Rosa dug for answers via [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time). He found [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai) and passed the turn with three lands untapped. Mihara reanimated [Doomwake Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomwake+Giant), attacked with both of his creatures, and lost Polukranos to [Hero's Downfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall). He brought Damo da Rosa to 8, but also found himself without further attackers after that.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/ptdtk/r12_mihara.jpg)  




Damo da Rosa summoned another Dragon in [Silumgar, the Drifting Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%2C+the+Drifting+Death) which matched up nicely with the [Hornet Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen) Mihara cast next. On the following turn, SilumgarÂ joined [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai) on offense, wiping all Insects off the table. The race was on.



Between Ojutai and further [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time), Damo da Rosa's deck was operating on full speed now. Mihara's creatures all died to removal, and getting one attack out of each with [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) simply wasn't enough to keep up with the 8 flying points of damage coming in every turn.



Soon enough, Damo da Rosa attacked for lethal damage. Mihara nodded, picked up his cards, and said, "Very bad matchup."



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/ptdtk/r12_pv.jpg)  




The second game began how the first had ended. In other words, it went badly for Mihara. He took one mulligan, a second, then a third, and finally kept [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid), [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix), and [Llanowar Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes).



His turn one [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) took [Silumgar's Scorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%27s+Scorn) out of a hand which also included three lands, [Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate), [Drown in Sorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow), and [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time). When Mihara immediately hit more lands and was able to cast [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid), [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix), and even [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) all on curve, it appeared as if Mihara might overcome the disadvantage of his mulligans after all.



However, [Dragonlord Silumgar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Silumgar) removed any doubt as to what was really going on. A freshly summoned [Polukranos, World Eater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater) defected to the winning side. In tandem with [Dragonlord Silumgar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Silumgar), it finished Mihara in short order.



"Very, very bad matchup," Mihara stated again. And three mulligans didn't help either.



**Damo da Rosa 2- Mihara 0**
