A Time At A Time
================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on September 24, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova












Hello and welcome back to the Lab. By now, *Return to Ravnica* has been fully cemented in **Magic**'s timeline, thanks to last weekend's Prerelease. As I type, the event hasn't yet occurred, but I'm sure I had fun this weekend. I think I'm sure. Wait... *when* am I???


Ack, sorry. Nothing can discombobulate like a good time loop. Since moving to Mondays, I've been experiencing strange foggy patches that occasionally smear my thoughts. Sometimes I wonder if I've left a part of myself behind, stranded in the past. And if so, what's presently replaced it? And what's my relationship with my own future?


Bah. I suppose the real question is: Why am I babbling on about this twaddle? Well, to nibble at my word count, for one. And... that's it really. Okay, for serious: *Return to Ravnica* is, in my esteemed view, a mostly successful **Magic**al release that follows in *Scars of Mirrodin*'s footsteps as an example of the well-executed sequel set. It's interesting to note how the examined guilds have endured and evolved with time (cue the connective glance at my above blathering). I find enjoyment in nearly all of the set's mechanics (sorry, unleash, but you're boring) and am twittering with glee at the upcoming five new mechanics in *Gatecrash*, the next set. (As of yet, I don't tweet. Should I? Why?)


With a vast amount of new cards headed to tabletops (both wooden and electronic) across the Multiverse, pondering where to begin can conjure some queasiness. A helpful way to conquer non-theme weeks is to anticipate upcoming theme weeks and avoid cards that fall into those spectrums. So today's docket details some interesting Standard lists using cards from *Return to Ravnica*, as well as some unfinished business with my preview cards from the last fortnight.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl214_lpufodnhul.jpg)Nivmagus Elemental | Art by [Mike Bierek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Mike%20Bierek%22%5D)


### New Nivmagus Nuances


Nivmagus Elemental was a tricky card [to preview](/en/articles/archive/lab/nu-magnetism-level-2012-09-10), and as is the case with bizarre cards, some strategies concerning it managed to slip through the cracks of my mind. Happily, these deck ideas often resurface in my inbox, through the written messages of readers (like you!). The biggest pocket of synergy with Nivmagus Elemental that I sailed right by? The [Pacts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&name=+%5b%5d+%5bPact%5d&set=+%5b%22Future+Sight%22%5d), a cycle of free spells that provide a big boost, yet contain a hefty toll. Many readers pointed out that a deck full of Pacts could lead to an early and sizable Nivmagus.




![Nivmagus Elemental](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/rtr/t3xc7vq9ct_en.jpg)

![Pact of the Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pact+of+the+Titan)

Robert Richey sent in a fun-looking decklist that's chock full of interactions. Running seven Pacts allows Robert to throw in other cards that like Pacts, like [Djinn Illuminatus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+Illuminatus) (hmm... infinite 4/4 Giants... with a [Fervor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fervor) for the win), [Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) (often an instantly lethal companion to a Pact), and [Sundial of the Infinite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundial+of+the+Infinite) (a quirky method of dodging a Pact). He also gets crazy points for running singleton weirdos [Eye of the Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+the+Storm) and [Galvanoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanoth).

### Searching... Searching...


On the other hand, [Search the City](/en/articles/archive/lab/hey-architects-2012-09-17) didn't muster nearly as much reader interest, which is to be expected from a tightly sewn, finicky, and harder-to-use [Time Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp). Will Houser emailed me with his similarly tuned thoughts on the enchantment, yet doggedly pursued a deck that makes use of my preview cards, wrapping them up in an exciting red-blue list.




![Search the City](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/rtr/tpfbu0kw1w_en.jpg)

![Goblin Electromancer](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/rtr/y037fhuhnk_en.jpg)

He wrote: "[Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), and [Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) already provide lots of card draw. Search the City sends them into overdrive, even more so with Goblin Electromancer almost halving most of their costs. [Burning Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Vengeance) was the star of a popular rogue deck recently and returns here with support from Guttersnipe. Nivmagus Elemental is a cheap threat that the opponent will find tricky to block but must answer. Finally, Search the City risks blowing out the opponent if all five cards get played; not bad for what I'm pretending is five mana for drawing five cards. Oh, and this is accidentally a Standard deck."

### Humans, and Other Beasts


All righty. Now it's time for some on-purpose Standard decks. From the limited window of [previews](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/article.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/returntoravnica/cig) that's been opened so far, I settled on Wild Beastmaster as an intriguing build-around. I knew I needed a bunch of other creatures to attack alongside the Beastmaster, and I was also looking for methods of boosting the power of creatures.


In the end I went with a green and blue Human aggro deck. [Hamlet Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamlet+Captain) can trigger before a Beastmaster to buff it a bit. [Mayor of Avabruck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) pumps all your Humans statically, and, if flipped, [Howlpack Alpha](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+Alpha) can create more creatures to attack with. And [Elder of Laurels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+of+Laurels) can give a Beastmaster a giant boost, soon to be translated onto your swinging forces.




![Wild Beastmaster](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/rtr/1x5r5subti_en.jpg)

![Hamlet Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hamlet+Captain)

Other Humans made the list. [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) is threatening with all the pump effects, and [Champion of Lambholt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Lambholt) is just too crazy not to include. [Tandem Lookout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tandem+Lookout) is one of my favorite soulbonders, as it can keep the cards flowing to your hand. I never got around to using [Captain of the Mists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+of+the+Mists) this spring, so I threw in two copies of the tap master.


As for the noncreature cards, [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) was a must. Giving +2/+0 to a Beastmaster can lead to massive combat damage. And [Downpour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Downpour) can clear the way!

### I Love Lantern


Today's last deck begins with Chromatic Lantern. The artifact can light up your lands with [Joiner Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joiner+Adept)-style color-waves. It also enables a Standard deck that's mildly hilarious. In short: A mono-green [Door to Nothingness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+to+Nothingness) deck. With [Lost in the Woods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+the+Woods) protection.




![Chromatic Lantern](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/rtr/8dr1r2rnte_en.jpg)

![Door to Nothingness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Door+to+Nothingness)

[Dawntreader Elk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk), Borderline Ranger, and Gatecreeper Vine can all accelerate your land base while acting as occasional chump blockers. With twenty-four [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s, [Lost in the Woods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+the+Woods) can be effective and [Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrifying+Presence) supplements your defense. Inching to ten mana is the real challenge, but in case the Door plan fails, you can always beat opponents with Worldspine Wurm!
