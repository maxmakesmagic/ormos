Jack Kitchen's Painter SCG Premier IQ—Baltimore
===============================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on March 30, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 





A combo deck's biggest nightmare is [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will).



Being able to counter something for zero mana feels very unfair, especially after playing just that deck and bringing it to your first tournament. Your combo is disrupted somehow, even though you went off when they were tapped out. Just as annoying is when they have cheap counters, like [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) or [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare).



But, fine, if that's how they want to play let's go eye-for-an-eye and play main deck [Pyroblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroblast)s, laughing in the face of all those [Force of Wills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Wills).



If you're into mainboard anti-blue cards, why don't you try out this deck: Painter.



Painter's combo is playing [Painter's Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant), selecting a color, and activating [Grindstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone). This combo immediately destroys the opponent's library. [Painter's Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) effect covers the library so [Grindstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone) can mill the whole library into the graveyard.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Painter%27s+Servant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grindstone)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone)




Both cards are easy to cast; [Grindstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone) costs one mana and Servant two. Therefore, you have the flexibility of choosing from a one-drop, two-drop, three-turn kill, or building up your board slowly for a longer game. [Pyroblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroblast) comes handy in this slower plan.



The combo can be disrupted by removing [Painter's Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) with [Grindstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone)'s activation on the stack. To prevent this, Painter makes all spells blue. [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) are blue and therefore can be countered. Of course, those evil blue counterspells can be countered as well, at any time, for the low, low price of one red mana.
