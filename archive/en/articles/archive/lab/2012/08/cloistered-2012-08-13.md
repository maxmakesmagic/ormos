Welcome back. It's World **Magic** Cup week, which is a bit strange since I don't have many diverting thoughts about the event. I've got the usual package today: three decks around recently printed cards. No real thematic linkage this week. I considered a reprisal of my [article/experiment](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/98) from a couple summers ago, but then I stopped considering that, and started considering mentioning it in the intro.


### Blue Beats


As of late, blue has been dabbling in aggressively costed beaters. Sure, Merfolk have always been around (and newly bolstered by [Master of the Pearl Trident](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+the+Pearl+Trident)) but recent stuff like [Phantasmal Bear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Bear) and [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) have really pushed the pedal for blue beatdown. Now we have [Jace's Phantasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Phantasm), a card that packs a potential 5/5 heft for a miniscule ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif). The only restriction functions like a lengthier threshold, which in a deck with mill elements is ably achieved. Since I want to beat down with 5/5 flyers, though, I can't have an overly heavy mill focus. There's other ways of filling your opponents' graveyards anyway, like forcing them to draw and discard a bunch of cards. Well, if [Jace's Phantasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Phantasm) is floating around, his Archivist should be feverishly filing away nearby. [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist) can spin-cycle the board's libraries, dampening graveyards and washing hands alike. He's very hygienic.

The other Phantasm helper is [Hedron Crab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab), a more traditional mill card and a strong one at that. Some early Crab scuttling with [Flooded Strand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flooded+Strand)s can lead to quick boosted Phantasms. Other times, the deck's tone will feel more controlling, due to my blending of certain white cards into the deck. The major attraction was [Proclamation of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth), a card that could return three Phantasms at once from my graveyard to the battlefield. In a late-game situation, even two can tilt the game in your favor. [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) can wipe the board clear for a forthcoming Proclamation to return the probably-swelled Phantasms.

I added some other diverse one-drops to give my Proclamations some reach. [Goldmeadow Harrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldmeadow+Harrier) can stymie attackers, and [Weathered Wayfarer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weathered+Wayfarer) can help me out in a landless situation.


The last adds were a set of [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil)s and some [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer)s to return some of my key spells. And since his playthings anchor the deck, I gave a singleton slot to [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren).

### Black Magic Woman


It's been roughly five years since the introduction of Planeswalkers. I initially found them extremely off-putting and unwelcome in **Magic**'s state at that time. Since then, the game has evolved to accommodate their presence, and apart from some overpowered bumps in the road (the unfortunate [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)), the walkers have finally earned my acceptance. At this point, each of the five original Planeswalkers that popped up in *Lorwyn* has three distinctive incarnations. (Well, kind of four, in Garruk's case.) ***Magic** 2013* provided [Ajani, Caller of the Pride](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Caller+of+the+Pride) and [Liliana of the Dark Realms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Dark+Realms) to round this out. Both are intriguing, but Lily 3.0 is ultimately more exciting. As in, her ultimate is insane. And her other two tricks do excellent work.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl208_lily.jpg)[Liliana of the Dark Realms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Dark+Realms) | Art by [D. Alexander Gregory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22D.+Alexander+Gregory%22%5D)


While thinking on where to begin, I received a deck idea from Brian Lejeune, who wanted to add [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season), that goofster card. He wrote:


 



> 
> The focus of this deck is to get out [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) ASAP and then drop [Liliana of the Dark Realms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Dark+Realms). At this point she will enter with 6 loyalty counters and you can activate her ultimate and get that emblem immediately! Now all your swamps can feed your [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave)s and [Exsanguinate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exsanguinate)s FTW. [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) really helps if you have a lot of [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s after getting the emblem. Other fun things to do are [Sorin Markov](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin+Markov)'s ultimate immediately if [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) is on board, and he survives at 1 loyalty. Of course, the best is when this all happens at once off a huge [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave), putting in a [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) or two and ending up with multiple Planeswalkers all activating their ultimates.
> 
>

I liked the idea behind Brian's list enough to have a fresh swing at it. I started over with [Liliana of the Dark Realms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Dark+Realms) and [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season), aiming for the instant-ultimate soar to twenty-plus mana. Instead of [Exsanguinate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exsanguinate) (a fantastic card, no doubt), I went with [Profane Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profane+Command), a kill method with some extra gadgets. I decided to abuse regular +1/+1 counters with [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) rather than loyalty counters. [Fertilid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid) came to mind as a fitting mana accelerant, and [Spike Weaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Weaver) is incredible in [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) and merely great otherwise. [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog)ging the combat zone can allow Liliana to safely build up to 6 as well. In some situations, Lily might need to wreck an opposing creature, but to lighten her removal duties, I added a pack of [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse).

In fact, there's a bunch of mixed goodies in this deck, and I was happy to use [Diabolic Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Revelation) to find them all. Although the simpler [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) is often a better choice, this deck is capable of giant X values, and a monstrous [Diabolic Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Revelation) aptly mimics the notion of dark forces shoving twisted thoughts into my brain, and by that I mean it's fun. There's a lot to learn, too. [Mind Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Shatter) can destroy my opponent's hand, leaving him or her powerless to stop me. [Dark Impostor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Impostor) seemed really fun in the super [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) environment, since it can take out any problematic creature for the suddenly low price of six mana. And in the right Season, his counters will double! [Shrouded Lore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrouded+Lore) can be a foolproof method of recovering any card from your graveyard to your hand with enough black mana. [Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader) is a fine two-drop that can evasively attack for a lot of damage. Finally, [Consumptive Goo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consumptive+Goo). Bow before it in a post-emblem battlefield, for it'll prey on smaller creatures, and once it has counters it can start targeting itself in a pinch.

### Cold Eyes


During my sifting through the rares of ***Magic** 2013*, looking for a promising but forgotten card, my sensors went wild upon viewing [Xathrid Gorgon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xathrid+Gorgon). After delivering an appropriately flavorful message as ***Magic** 2013*'s preview card, the Gorgon's fame has receded to casual tables, where she shines. When re-reading her petrifying flavor text, I noticed that the Gorgon's victims are transformed into artifacts. So if a Gorgon starts stoning your opponent's creatures, something like [Viashino Heretic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Heretic) could start destroying them and dealing chunks of damage.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| 
Xathrid Gorgon
 | 
Viashino Heretic
 |

This thought process led me directly to [Liquimetal Coating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liquimetal+Coating). As a cheaper way of turning stuff into artifacts, it can combine with certain artifact destruction methods to deal damage to your opponent. [Smash to Smithereens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smash+to+Smithereens) is a primary tool alongside the Heretic. [Detonate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detonate) can bust something for damage.
