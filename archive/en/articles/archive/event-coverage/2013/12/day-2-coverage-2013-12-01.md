* ### Day 1 Undefeated Decklists


*by Frank Karsten*

Six players managed to escape yesterday's competition with pristine 9-0 records. Mono-blue was well-represented, with five out of six going for [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) and [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves). The deck of Andreas Ganz is more innovative: it's Mono Black Devotion at its core, but features a novel white splash.

* ### Round 10 Feature Match - Marcello Calvetto vs. (13) Jérémy Dezani


*by Tobi Henke*

Frenchman Jérémy Dezani crashed onto the scene last year with a victory at Grand Prix Lyon, followed by two more GP Top 8 appearances this year (and preceded by a Top 8 in 2009). A promising start and he certainly delivered on the promise at the big stage when he won Pro Tour *Theros* in Dublin. There, he was playing the then-new Mono-Blue Devotion deck, and he stuck with it for this event, earning a 9-0 record so far. Going into the tournament, he was ranked number 13 in the world, a position he would likely improve upon here.


9-0 was also the overnight score of the somewhat less known Italian player Marcello Calvetto, although he had made three Grand Prix Top 8s between 2008 and 2011. Calvetto, like Dezani, chose Mono-Blue Devotion, so the battle here was going to be a mirror match. Would proven mono-blue expert Dezani take the victory, or would Calvetto beat the champ at his own game?



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvie13/fm10_calvetto.jpg)
  
*Marcello Calvetto* 
 

### Game 1


In the early game it was all about building up an army. Calvetto assembled [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), Thassa, God of The Sea, and [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) plus three tokens, whereas Dezani had [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter), and [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought), whom he used to find his own [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves). Now the actual action started: Calvetto summoned a second [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) and attacked with his tokens, killing Jace.


Dezani fought back with another [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) and [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) creating six tokens, but Calvetto redoubled his efforts with [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa), attacking with tokens and the finally active [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea).


After blocks, the table was decidedly less crowded. For one thing, all Elemental tokens were gone and Dezani was left with just a 2/3 [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) and his [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter), whereas Calvetto still had his two copies of [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves), a 3/4 [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea), and the aforementioned [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa), which he now used to force Dezani to attack. Before blocks, though, Dezani returned the opposing [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) with [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift) and, in his second main phase, cast his own 5/5 Thassa. Suddenly, the board state didn't look quite as bad for Dezani anymore. However, the players' lifetotals had Dezani at a severe disadvantage. Two attacks with unblockable Thassa and two unblockable Masters sealed the deal.


### Game 2


Calvetto quickly boosted out three 2/3 fliers in the form of one [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) and two [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter)s, while Dezani held the fort with one 1/2 [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) and [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought)'s +1 ability. Dezani tried to fight back with [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves), but thanks to [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx), Calvetto was able to cast [Domestication](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domestication) on said Master (killing all of Dezani's Elementals) and [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea).


Dezani had his own [Domestication](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domestication) to steal one [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) and his own [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx). Next turn, Calvetto went for the blowout by overloading [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift). Dezani had the [Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) to stop it, then overloaded his own [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift)—a sudden turn of events. Calvetto picked up his nonland permanents and, on second thoughts, picked up his lands too. With just ten minutes left on the clock, the players were off to the deciding game, considerably speeding up their pace to finish the match in time.



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvie13/fm10_dezani.jpg)
  
*(13) Jérémy Dezani* 
 

### Game 3


Calvetto's [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) met Dezani's [Tidebinder Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidebinder+Mage). On turn three, Calvetto cast [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea), while Dezani passed without play, then countered [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) with [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay), before untapping and summoning [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves), generating three tokens. Calvetto, on turn five, still didn't have a fourth land but cast [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) plus another [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) and attacked with Thassa.


Dezani had another [Tidebinder Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidebinder+Mage) to tap down Calvetto's second Weird as well, then cast, with the help of [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx), [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and [Judge's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar), and attacked with tokens and [Tidebinder Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidebinder+Mage). Calvetto traded away his 1/2 [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) for a token and took 6, falling to 10. He untapped, scried again with Thassa, and still didn't find a fourth land. He glumly looked at his board of [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) and two tapped [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird)s and passed the turn.


When Dezani activated [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought)'s second ability revealing [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay), Calvetto offered his hand in concession.


### Marcello Calvetto 1-2 (13) Jérémy Dezani


After the game, Dezani revealed the four [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird)s he had safely tucked away in his sideboard and told Calvetto: "You really need to know how to sideboard." For a second it appeared as if Calvetto wanted to argue the point but in the end he thought better of it and simply accepted the advice.

* ### Round 13 Feature Match - Marcin Staciwa (Mono-Blue Devotion) vs. Robert Jurkovic (Mono-Blue Devotion)


*by Tobi Henke*

"You have no loss, right?" asked veteran Slovak pro Robert Jurkovic when he sat down at the feature match table. "Yes," said a smiling Marcin Staciwa, the last undefeated player in the tournament, who clearly hadn't expected such a winning streak. "It's crazy!" Jurkovic entered the round with a respectable 11-1 score, no match for Staciwa's straight 12-0, but intent on leaving both players with the same 12-1 afterwards.


The match-up here was one we've seen before, as both players brought Mono-Blue Devotion. There's just no way to avoid the mirror match this weekend, it appears, at least not at the top tables.



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvie13/fm13_staciwa.jpg)
  
*Marcin Staciwa* 
 

### Game 1


As always the first couple of turns were spent simply dropping creatures onto the battlefield. Staciwa had a pair of [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor)s, [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter), and [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves), while Jurkovic cast [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), [Judge's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar), [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter), and [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea).


The real action started now. Staciwa's [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) gave both his Raptors a third +1/+1 counter and then he attacked with all of his creatures save [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) and [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter). Staciwa lost two tokens and a [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), but Jurkovic lost [Judge's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar) and 11 life falling to 8.


To add insult to injury, Staciwa overloaded [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift) with the help of Nykthos Shrine to Nyx on his next turn. Thanks to [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and his own [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift) (not overloaded unfortunately) Jurkovic survived the ensuing attack on 3. He still lost to the recast [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) two turns later.


### Game 2


This was a quick affair. Jurkovic started with [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea), and Domesticate on the [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) that had been Staciwa's first play of the game. Staciwa's [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) with his one lonely Elemental token wasn't nearly enough to stem the tide. Jurkovic added his own [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) to the stolen one, attacked with everything, including Thassa and [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), and soon Staciwa shuffled up his cards for game three.



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvie13/fm13_jurkovic.jpg)
  
*Robert Jurkovic* 
 

### Game 3


It was clear Staciwa had a counter-heavy draw here as he passed turn after turn without play. After his turn-one [Cloudfin Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), Jurkovic succesfully baited a [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay) with [Tidebinder Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidebinder+Mage), then resolved a second [Tidebinder Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidebinder+Mage) while himself countering Staciwa's [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves). A second [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) resolved but was stolen via [Domestication](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domestication) and things looked dim for Staciwa, even though he had a third [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) and finally was allowed to keep it.


All of these trades had severely depleted the players' resources and soon both were down to one card in hand. However, Jurkovic had [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and used him to get [Rapid Hybridization](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rapid+Hybridization) and [Tidebinder Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidebinder+Mage). Staciwa's [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) was turned into a 3/3 Frog Lizard and that was tapped with [Tidebinder Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidebinder+Mage). Next, Jace came up with [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) and while the cards kept flowing for Jurkovic, the only thing in flux on Staciwa's side was his lifetotal. It kept falling and falling until he conceded.


### Marcin Staciwa 1-2 Robert Jurkovic


"So what score do you think makes Top 8?" Staciwa asked sheepishly.


"You probably need X-2."


"So one more win ..."


"Good luck!"

After [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) rotated out of Standard, I thought we had seen the last of Hexproof decks. Julien de Graat had other ideas. There are still a few hexproof creatures around, and beefing them up with [Deviant Glee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deviant+Glee) and [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) can still be a potent strategy against opponents who rely on [Ultimate Price](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price) and Hero's Downfall. The land count in this deck seems dangerously low, but the idea is interesting.

The little kid in me rejoiced upon seeing 3 [Worldspine Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldspine+Wurm) in a Day 2 deck. Of course, 11 mana is a lot. But [Garruk, Caller of Beasts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Caller+of+Beasts) can dump them in play early on, and Nykthos, Shrine to [Nix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nix) can potentially produce the mana to hardcast them. It may be a bit ambitious, but it's certainly exciting.
