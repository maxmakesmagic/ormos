The August 19, 2016 Update
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on August 19, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on August 19, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by a weekend of previews! The full [*Conspiracy: Take the Crown* Card Image Gallery](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/card-image-gallery/conspiracy-take-crown) will go up Monday, and we'll have some [special previews coming to you this weekend](#6).


Today's Previews—Selvala Day
============================


In addition to the previews you find on DailyMTG, there are a number of places around the web that will have preview cards themselves. I'll share the ones that have already been posted and point you toward what's coming down the pipeline over the rest of the day.


Today is Selvala Day, which means a ton of green and creature-centric cards are on today's docket.


1. [Regal Behemoth](https://www.facebook.com/MagicTheGathering.apac/posts/1098059920262868:0)

Of course Australia previews a giant Lizard. At least this one isn't trying to kill you. It just sets things up so everyone else will be.


1. [It's a Conspiracy!](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/snacktimewithmikaandant-08192016-snack-time-38-its-a-conspiracy/) | Gathering Magic | Mike Linnemann and Ant Tessitore

Some of the most gorgeous art to ever grace a *Magic* card. Oh, and the card is good, too!


1. [*Conspiracy: Take the Crown* Exclusive Preview—Birds of Paradise](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/birds-of-paradise/) | Top Level Podcast | Mike Flores and Patrick Chapin

The title gives away a bit of the surprise, but it's still pleasant to see [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) return. This iconic card is a staple in just about every format where it's legal, casual and otherwise. Chapin and Flores take the opportunity to tell some [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) stories from the way back machine.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Legacy Death and Taxes](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-legacy-constructed-league-2016-08-17)

With the printing of some choice new white cards in *Conspiracy: Take the Crown*, there's been a lot of talk about the white-based Death and Taxes deck in Legacy, so let's dig in a little bit.


The deck is, at its core, a disruptive white creature deck that uses cards like [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben), [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), and [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port) to stunt the opponent's mana while also beating down with efficient and powerful creatures like [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), [Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker), [Mother of Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes), and the always underrated [Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp).


[Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) ties the whole package together, letting you utilize [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s and [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port)s to your heart's content while still developing your board. And pushing out certain creatures at instant speed for free—[Spirit of the Labyrinth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Labyrinth), [Mangara of Corondor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mangara+of+Corondor), and [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader), to name a few—can be game-winning.


This particular list comes from *Magic Online* player SpaceGoblin, who went 5-0 in a Competitive Legacy League with the deck.
