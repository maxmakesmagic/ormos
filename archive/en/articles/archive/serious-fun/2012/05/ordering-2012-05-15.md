Ordering In
===========



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on May 15, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







Have you ever been overwhelmed by options? Say, for example, you sit down in a diner. It's a 24-hour joint, so you can order breakfast, lunch, dinner, or dessert any time of day. Let's also say you're traveling to a **Magic** event (perhaps for [Grand Prix Minneapolis](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/grandprix/minneapolis12) this weekend, or [Grand Prix Anaheim](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/grandprix/anaheim12) next weekend, as I am) so you won't be back again. You have one chance to sample something you might never see again.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf195_gis.jpg)[Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) | Art by [Jason Chan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Jason+Chan%22%5D)


How do you choose?


There are a lot of strategies, but the one I prefer is to simply try what the locals are having. What's the special everyone else is enjoying? What's the place known for? What's the most common order coming out of the kitchen? With a few well-placed questions and some patient observation, you dramatically increase the odds of what you're about to step into will be something worth remembering.


I hope that analogy helps you understand why I ask you all a question every week. Here's the one from last week.

What's really awesome about this particular poll is that after a day or so, long after many of you are clicking through to read the article, the poll was at a very close tie between [Gisela](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goodnight) and [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand). While the final result tipped the scale to Gisela, it didn't seem right to leave such a close race at that.


Today, we're playing both good and evil. I hope you're ready.


(And before we get too far, I just want to gently remind everyone that when I build Commander decks I build them from whatever I have in my [Commander Box](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/boxing-day-2011-11-08-0). Not every card and combination will be available, so if "something obvious" is missing please let me know—you could help me add the next neat thing to my collection!)


### Hell Hath No Fury Like an Angel Scorned


Let's look at [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight).



![Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight)

 

You know what this heavenly creature reminds me of?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf195_fur3.jpg)Yeah. I've seen Angel-themed Commander decks before, but Gisela isn't your usual type of Angel. She's a battle-hardened killing machine who empowers the Planeswalkers who summon her (that is, *us*) to create a cataclysm for our foes. Note her wording:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf195_cut1.jpg)This is the same type of wording as featured on a very different enemy-colored commander: [Edric, Spymaster of Trest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edric%2C+Spymaster+of+Trest).



![Edric, Spymaster of Trest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Edric%2C+Spymaster+of+Trest)

 

Everything dealing damage to our opponents gets in on the deal, which in Gisela's case means the damage is doubled. This firmly draws me to three ideas:



> 
> * Assemble an army of well-equipped Angels.
> * Discourage attacks against us.
> * Have enough mana to make both happen.
> 


Let's start with the easiest piece: Angels.

### The Dark Night Returns


[Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) isn't a silly mean Demon. He isn't an annoying taskmaster, requiring sacrifices or payments. He's actually quite generous, giving you free reign to convert 7 life into seven cards. If that sounds familiar, it's because it's an effect that's dominated the game over time.

As it turns out, this power is magnified the more life you have. We start with 40 life in Commander. We could potentially activate [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) five times to draw thirty-five cards, all in one shot. While there are a lot of game-breaking things drawing thirty-five cards can do, we're going to focus on one specific spell: [Exsanguinate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exsanguinate). It works like this:



> 
> * [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) and [Blanket of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blanket+of+Night) turn just about every land into a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).
> * [Cabal Coffers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Coffers) lets us add a ton of mana, counting up the number of [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s we have in play.
> * [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power), [Caged Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caged+Sun), and [Extraplanar Lens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extraplanar+Lens) build up how much an individual [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) taps for.
> * [Boseiju, Who Shelters All](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boseiju%2C+Who+Shelters+All) helps empower an almost unstoppable, game-ending [Exsanguinate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exsanguinate).
