Calca-Yes!
==========



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 24, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











After a couple of largely [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration)-free weeks in the Standard portion of the StarCityGames Open Series, [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) crashed back to the top of the Standard standings at Grand Prix Minneapolis last weekend... and did so in a brand-new, super-duper shell. Let's look at how this most recent Constructed GP shook out:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_chart2.jpg)  
Pretty interesting stack of decks, no?

Minneapolis came within one deck of *eight* different color/deck strategies (and truth be told, the two WU Delver decks have some substantial differences). That said, more than half of the decks played four copies of the mighty [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), and 7/8 played [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_calcano2.jpg)Christian Calcano broke out in Minneapolis with a deck that itself might prove a breakout archetype. Calcano's deck is quite a bit different from every Delver deck we have seen in Standard to date, but obviously proved successful for him. Will its combination of power and different-ness prove compelling for players in the coming weeks and months?

Let's examine how this deck works:

Calcano's deck has an eight-pack of [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) (constants across most every Delver deck), but the specific instants and sorceries he played to flip Delvers and gain value with [Tiago Chan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s swapped [Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence) and [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) for *Avacyn Restored* standouts [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) and [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_del.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/sun_arttransformbutton_active.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/moon_arttransformbutton_static.png)[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Nils+Hamm%22%5D)

Red spells have tremendous synergy with Calcano's side plan of [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) and [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681). Essentially, these instants and sorceries boost [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681) more or less exactly as well as the white ones... but can get blockers out of the way or double up on efforts ([Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) does 3 to the face itself, plus puts an instant into the graveyard for more Pike damage). The solo [Desolate Lighthouse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolate+Lighthouse) can help out the same way, or can clear a troublesome, non-miraculous [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) from hand.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_leyton.jpg)The former US National Champion and ChannelFireball stalwart Josh Utter-Leyton put together a dominating take on [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). In addition to white-blue constants [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), Utter-Leyton added [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) as a big four-drop.

Now, obviously [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) puts lots of white tokens onto the battlefield... as does the black-splashing [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). All together these cards make for good synergy with [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure). Previous token-heavy Delver decks used [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue), but what Utter-Leyton might lose in vigilance is made up for somewhat with his ability to make a 5-toughness [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) (much harder to block and kill in combat with a [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull), say) and even more value for [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). Josh's Geists don't die to [Whipflare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) once buffed by [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure), and his Angel tokens smack for 5 big damage in the sky.

New card alert!

Utter-Leyton played [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage) in his sideboard, a two-of five traditionally occupied on the curve by one [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) and one [Jace, Memory Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept). Tamiyo can lock down big threats and punish control; in addition, given Utter-Leyton's propensity to have a bunch of tapped attackers (generally tokens, en masse), Tamiyo's second ability can make for a good burst of card advantage.

Calling this deck "Esper" might be a little misleading (especially as we are no longer in *Shards of Alara* mode)... Utter-Leyton played only four [Darkslick Shores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkslick+Shores) and one [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) (off his two [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds))... his black splash being exclusively for the flashback on [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls); yet, a point of differentiation against the next two decks.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_laehn.jpg)Taylor Laehn played a straight White-Blue Delver list with quite a few instances of customization and innovation. While we have seen [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) before (a card originally played in the Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*-era Esper Spirits decks of Jon Finkel, Jelger Wiegersma, and company), Laehn's two-drop choice of [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) over [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) is not one we have seen for many months. Laehn still played [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681)... he just didn't pair that Equipment with its typical battlefield bearer.

Laehn's spell setup included quite a few one-ofs and a fair spread of Phyrexian mana... one [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), one [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), one [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot), and even one [Mutagenic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) all graced Taylor's main deck. While a stack of one-ofs might seem odd (forget about the fifteen one-ofs in Calcano's sideboard for a moment), in a deck like Delver, chock full of [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s and other manipulation spells (including the aforementioned one-of [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)), a one-of is not exactly the same thing as a one-of in a regular deck that has to rely on the top of the library and a nod from Fortuna for its spells. Taylor can even flip some of his instants and sorceries into the graveyard with [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) in order to set them up for [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)!

While he didn't play any Swords in his main deck, Taylor ran both [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) and the currently more popular [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) as one-of options in the side.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_schultz.jpg)So how does this deck work?

Well, it's mostly a [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) deck.

[Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) is a pretty good attacker (with its first striking buddy) and an elite defender; and if the [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) itself wants to start jumping in front of the enemy, it works quite well with [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan).

[Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) of course works with [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)... heck, half the time you just have *two*[Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)s!

[Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) is a huge three-of in this deck—taking precedence over much more commonly played cards like [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip)—again due to its synergy with [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan). You can kill something (putting the [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) into your graveyard), attack with the [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), and bring back [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) to kill something again. [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) equals more and more dead men.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_pha.jpg)[Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Nils+Hamm%22%5D)

[Forbidden Alchemy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) is the card that can help dig all these Splicers and Images and [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight)s into the graveyard to begin with.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

And in terms of non-[Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)-related graveyard awesomeness, this deck *also* packs four [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls)! [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) is of course a superb defensive card on turn three (perfect to hide behind while you set up), and great again when you flash it back the next turn.

While Schultz's deck can potentially get a crazy [Forbidden Alchemy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy)-into-[Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite)-and-fourth turn [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) (which can itself kill a fair number of small creatures), what this deck seems *mostly* great at is the murder of creatures. It has [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) on one, [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) on two, [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) on three, and the almighty [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) on four. [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) can lock down creatures (or eliminate them one at a time) on five, and then of course [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) can bring back more and more of these elimination cards turn after turn after turn.

Jerret played some substantial anti-control cards in his sideboard, including [Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) and [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated). That said, while he had two potentially backbreaking [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)s in his sideboard, Shultz played an Esper Control sans [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)... which, given the tenor of the format, might not be much of a liability.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_bishop.jpg)Rounding out this Top 8 was the lone non-blue deck: Stephen Bishop with a straight Red-Green Wolf Run Ramp deck.

This deck packs a whopping *eight* Titans! Both [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan)! To get there, Stephen played eight two-mana accelerators to bring him from two-to-four on turn three, whereupon he could play another accelerator or a [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) to set up turn-four Titan mana.

In previous formats, a turn four Titan might spell death in the mirror, but Bishop could actually answer one with [Beast Within](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within). It might suck to be down a ton of value due to a Titan's special abilities—and then give up another 3/3 body due to [Beast Within](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within)—but it's a heck of a lot better than losing on the spot.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td196_pri.jpg)[Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) | Art by [Aleksi Bricolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Aleksi+Bricolt%22%5D)

Many decks of this style in the past played (at least) one [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), along with [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) (turn-two [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) for [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) being essentially the same acceleration as a [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) or [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns)), where [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) could also go and get [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan); Bishop played a completely different X-spell in [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=247419) and three copies of [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) to try to get the jump on the obviously popular blue decks.

So what happens after a guy goes and gets a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) into play, anyway?

Since [the days of Brian Sondag](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/deck/817), [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) in Standard has set up [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) and [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus), of course, being a prime target for [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run)-buffing, capable of killing at twice the speed of a non-infectious creature—especially given the main phase-ness of so much of this format's removal.

Speaking of special lands, Bishop played three copies of [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) in his sideboard. [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) set to Giant can make for some essentially unstoppable Titans. Sorry [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). Sorry [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate).

I don't know about you, but the ebb and flow of this Standard since the release of *Avacyn Restored* has been an exciting roller coaster ride (so far) for me as a fan of the game. To think, I was worried for a moment that [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)—that multiple-format-bender and pre-AVR Best Card in Standard—might fall off the map! Delver came roaring back this past weekend, in both enhanced classical forms (White-Blue Delver with [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) or a black-splash with [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold)) and the radical new blue-red build (as well as Humans!) Who can say what secret lists will be Delved up in the coming week? Or will green burst back to the forefront with its [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)s or perhaps call up [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) from the Block Constructed D-League?

It might be Planechase Week for the rest of the site, but this is the **Magic**al movement I'm chasing. :)
