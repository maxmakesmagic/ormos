The Top Dogs of *Dark Ascension*
================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 10, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20180108123623im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_SteveSadin.jpg?itok=5-cQx5h-)
By Steve Sadin











 Whenever a new set is released, there are always a bunch of cards that generate excitement. Some of these cards will inevitably fail to live up to expectations (at least in the short term), but other cards will go above and beyond even their most vocal advocate's expectations. 


With the Pro Tour just underway, let's take a look at some of the cards that the world's top players have been clamoring about all week long.


### Sorin, Lord of Innistrad


![Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad)

 *The Lord of Innistrad is a planeswalker that demands respect.* 

 The *Dark Ascension* card that has been on everyone's mind since the day it was previewed is, of course, [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad). 


 While there is no doubt that Sorin is a good card, people have been wondering if Sorin will rule over Standard in the way that Jace once did. Or will his impact on the format be a bit more subtle? 


 Long time control player and rogue deck designer Shaheen Soorani made the Top 16 of last weekend's Star City Games Open with a planeswalker control deck full of powerful cards â€“ most notably three copies of [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad).

###  White: Lingering Souls


![Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lingering+Souls)

 *This sorcery has had a â€“ wait for it â€“"lingering" presence in the minds of players since it was revealed.* 


[Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) seems poised to have a huge impact on Standardâ€¦ if players can find a deck (or decks) that supports it. A [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), coupled with an [Intangible Virtue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue) or an [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure), is enough for a turn six kill.

###  Blue: Thought Scour


![Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thought+Scour)

 *Never underestimate the power of a one-mana blue cantrip. They are oftentimes capable of setting up some remarkable plays.* 

 While [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) might not seem all that exciting at first glance, it can do a lot of little things for you. 



[Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) helps fill your graveyard with flashback spells, as well as other instants and sorcery spells that you can get back with [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). It helps power up [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike), and it can get you past cards that you've put on top of your library with [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) that you've decided you don't want to draw. 


 Oh, and unlike [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), it's an instant, so you can leave up mana for counterspells or removal, and then cast it on your opponent's end step if they don't force you to use your mana on a reactive spell. 


 So if you're looking for a cheap way to help filter through your deck, you should definitely give [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) a second look. It can do more than you might think. 


###  Black: Gravecrawler


![Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gravecrawler)

 *Braaaaaaaaaains* 

 If you're looking for a black card that's sure to have an impact on Standard for as long as it's legal, then you need to look no further than [Tragic Slip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip). But if you're looking for a really exciting card that has the ability to spawn entirely new archetypes, then look no further than [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler). 


 While it isn't clear that players have had enough time to put together powerful Zombie decks for Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*, [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) is a card that many of the top professional teams tried to build decks around. 



[Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) might not have its time in the sun this weekend, but don't be surprised to see it crawling its way to the top at a tournament near you soon. 


###  Red: Faithless Looting


![Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Faithless+Looting)

*[Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) is back. And this time, it has flashback. Oh, and it's red.* 


[Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) gives players the ability to rip through their decks, and fill up their graveyards, at an amazingly quick rate. 


 Whether you're running it in your control decks to help you find whatever you need, your Reanimator decks to set up a quick and deadly [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), or [Havengul Lich](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Lich) combo decks to insure quick kills, [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) is *the* card that players want for setting plans up.

![Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Strangleroot+Geist)

 *With many big-name players backing the powerful green creature, [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) is expected to be a big hit this weekend.*

###  Artifact: Grafdigger's Cage


![Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage)

 *Looking to use and abuse things from your graveyard? Think again.* 


[Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) is one of those cards where its impact is determined by how powerful the other decks that surround it are. 


 If players are cleaning up with [Havengul Lich](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Lich)/[Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning)/[Perilous Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr) combo decks, [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) decks, and other flashback heavy decks that take advantage of some combination of [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy), and/or [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), then [Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) has the potential to become one of the most valuable sideboard cards in the format. 


 Are we already at a point where players will be able to generate significant in-game advantages by plopping down a [Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage)? Or will we have to wait a few more months for that to happen? 


###  Land: Evolving Wilds


![Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Evolving+Wilds)

 *It's back, and with the enemy color lands from *Innistrad*, [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) is better than ever.* 


[Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) has been around before, and it has rarely had much of an impact. So it's no wonder that many players simply overlooked [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) when they were taking their initial looks at *Dark Ascension*. 


But good mana fixing isn't something that you should ever simply write off.


 During the week leading up to Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*, many of the top pros in the world discovered that [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) was an essential tool that could allow them to splash cards like [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad), [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and a host of other cards at very little cost. 


 Simply put, [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) makes decks that would otherwise be prohibitively inconsistent into potential powerhouses. 


 Will players rise to the top with [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) fueled, [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad) decks this weekend? Or will old stalwarts such as White-Blue Delver of Secrets, and Red-Green ramp continue to rise to the top? 


Follow along all weekend long to find out!
