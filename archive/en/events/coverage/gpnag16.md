Hirami Conquers Nagoya
======================




Congratulations Tomonori Hirami! You've just won Grand Prix Nagoya 2016! Surviving the new perilous world against 2,564 other aspirants, Hirami emerged victorious thanks to the "Support" of his White-Green Aggro deck!

Top 8 Coverage



*Battle for Zendikar* Block Limited has changed forever, ever since the entrance of *Oath of the Gatewatch*. 2656 brave souls arrived in Nagoya to battle it out in the new, perilous world. Only 762 survived the relentless onslaught and earned the right to return on Day 2.


15 rounds later, only 8 heroes remain. Rising to the top are Sean Li, Kazuaki Fujimura, Ikuya Asai, Park Jun Young, Ryota Takeuchi, Tomonori Hirami, Shawn McNeace and Satoshi Haji. Park from MTG Mint Card was the most experienced, playing in his fifth lifetime Top 8 in the hopes of winning his second Grand Prix. Despite being the hot favorite, he quickly exited the tournament in the Quarterfinals.


To clinch his trophy, Hirami first had to defeat Sean Li, before dispatching Kazuaki in the semifinals. Down to the final match of the day, his White-Green Aggro deck with strong Support cards was able to triumph over Takeuchi's Black-Red Devoid in two swift games.


The eight have demonstrated that they know a thing or two about *Battle for Zendikar* Block Limited, and will be glad that they've all secured invites to Pro Tour *Shadows over Innistrad* in Madrid.


If you're still hungering for more *Oath of the Gatewatch* action, be sure to stay tuned as we swing over to the other side of the world. Grand Prix Mexico City and Grand Prix Vancouver are about to begin as we draw our curtains!


Congratulations once again to Tomonori Hirami for the well-deserved win and the spectacular weekend.

Day 2 Coverage



2656 arrived in Nagoya today, but only 762 return.


That makes today a rather special day, as we witness the LARGEST Grand Prix Day 2 Booster Draft portion in the history of Magic. With 96 Draft Pods taking place simultaneously, it is a record-breaking statistic that can only be dwarfed if you combine both Grand Prix Las Vegas #1 and #2!


While 762 players are drafting in Day 2 here at Nagoya, around 785 and 1929 and players are engaged in Sealed Deck in Mexico City and Vancouver respectively, which marks another momentous occasion where nearly 3500 players across three regions are competing in a Grand Prix simultaneously!


9 players achieved perfect records, including Pro Tour Prague 2006 Champion Takuya Osaka, who managed the clean sweep without a single bye. Grand Prix Minneapolis Champion and MTG Mint Card member Park Jun Young is also at 9-0. Makoto Horiuchi, Toru Inoue, Yo Tezuka, Yasutaka Nagao, Ryota Takeuchi, Shigeki Kimura and Reo Taniguchi round off the remaining seven. Keep a look out for Gold Pro Teruya Kakumae and former World Champion Junya Iyanaga as well! Also, never discount (14) Yuuya Watanabe, he knows how to make a comeback!


Enough chatter! It's time to draft!

Day 1 Coverage



After 9 rounds of competition, the field of 2656 is down to “just” 762. That makes tomorrow a rather special day, as we witness the largest Booster Draft tournament in *Magic*'s entire history, in terms of number of draft pods in Day 2!


9 players achieved perfect records, including Pro Tour Prague 2006 Champion Takuya Osaka, who managed the clean sweep without a single bye. Grand Prix Minneapolis Champion and MTG Mint Card member Park Jun Young is also at 9-0, hunting for his sixth Grand Prix Top 8 or 2nd Grand Prix title, either is fine. Hriuchi Makoto, Inoue Toru, Tezuka Yo, Nagao Yasutaka, Takeuchi Ryoa, Kumura Shigeki and Taniguchi Reo round off the remaining seven.


Two other players are also technically undefeated, with 8-0-1 records. Fujimura Kazuaki and Seki Hirotaka should be satisfied with this excellent record. Keep a look out for Gold Pro Teruya Kakumae and former World Champion Junya Iyanaga as well! Tomorrow's going to be an exciting day!


If you're still hungering for more *Oath of the Gatewatch* action, be sure to stay tuned as we swing over to the other side of the world. Grand Prix Mexico City and Grand Prix Vancouver are about to begin as we draw our curtains!
