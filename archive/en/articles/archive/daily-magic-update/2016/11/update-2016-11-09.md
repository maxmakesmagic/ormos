Deck of the Day
===============


1. [White-Black Aggro-ish](http://decks.tcgplayer.com/Magic/deck/search?format=Standard&location=2016-tcgplayer-standard-state-champs-ma)

It's listed as White-Black Tokens, but, beyond [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar), there's not much tokens-y about it. Instead, it's a low-curve, aggressive-ish white-black deck that uses removal to clear the way for some efficient creatures—and was good enough to snag fourth place at the Massachusetts State Championship last weekend, piloted by one Kenneth Olsen.


The deck utilizes a number of *Kaladesh* standouts, including [Scrapheap Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger) and [Kambal, Consul of Allocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kambal%2C+Consul+of+Allocation), alongside, um, [Aerial Responder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aerial+Responder), who might be better than we all realize. From there, the deck is a collection of powerful white and black creatures backed up by the format's best removal. Simple, effective, and potentially very strong.
