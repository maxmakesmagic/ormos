Respect the Mountain (But Not Too Much)
=======================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on January 7, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











The current Extended format is full of iconic opening sequences. You know, the ...

  
![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td72_cards1.jpg)  

> [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) + [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) (your answer); untap [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) (now a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)), [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) ... Marit Lage!

Or ...

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td72_cards2.jpg)  

> [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), [Rite of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame), [Desperate Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual), [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) ... [Deus of Calamity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deus+of+Calamity)!

Or the most powerful one of all (if, you know, *tournament results* are any measuring stick) ...



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |


> [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (of some stripe), [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch).

The iconic opening often—even "usually"—gives you an indication of the entire rest of the deck you are facing. [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) into [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)? Chances are the opponent is going to take my [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) over my [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). But [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) into [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)? We immediately start running the numbers. Is it going to be a [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) follow up? Can I answer the Knight *and then* a [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) a turn later? The extent of this particular discussion probably ends at "[Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) or [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm)?".

*Until now.*

Check out the deck that Christian Calcano used to [win a recent **Magic Online** Extended PTQ](/Magic/Digital/MagicOnlineTourn.aspx?x=mtg/digital/magiconline/tourn/828160):

If the name Christian Calcano rings some bells, it is because the onetime Top 8 Magic Mockvitational winner has been on a tear—both with innovative decks and online PTQs—in recent months. About three months ago, just after the release of *Zendikar*, Calcano gave the world its first look at Boros Bushwhacker, finishing second at the [October Philadelphia 5K](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/td/60).

Running on essentially no sleep, Calcano went 5-1 just to *qualify* for a **Magic Online*****Magic** 2010* Qualifier, went 5-1 in that tournament's Swiss ... and came up a disappointing second in his second big tournament in as many days. But last week's Extended PTQ turned out a lot happier for the talented New Yorker.

Is that Doran?

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20190711124430im_/https://www.wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td72_doranSlice.jpg)  
It must be early January, because *everything old is new again.*

So what is going on here?

Calcano apparently didn't like any of the existing decks in the format, and decided, on a back-to-back (Sealed Deck) PTQ road trip with Sean McKeown and others to go with a deck he had played about *two years ago*, updated with some new cards (Vindicates becoming [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse)s, [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)s, and [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) ... [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) is just "the nuts").

Looking at the Doran deck, you can see that it is actually quite similar in card choices to the Zoo decks that we typically see (and will become more so, see below) ... [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) to open, [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) being the best early threat, and [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) as the breaker.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td72_3cards1.jpg)  
According to Calcano, the reason to play this deck over Zoo (besides personal preference in his case) is its superiority against control and combo decks. Disruption in the form of [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)—combined with the still-quick offense—makes Doran a better option against [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) and Tezzeret than Zoo variants.

One of the things you may have noticed about this deck is its heavy anti-red sideboard. Calcano correctly predicted a heavy component of mono-red burn decks and All-In Red decks (which were exactly what showed up in the Top 8). However he cautioned against playing this sideboard in a paper **Magic** PTQ. Red decks are typically very cheap to assemble in **Magic Online**, so they will be slightly more represented online than in real life. 

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender)  
[Burrenton Forge-Tender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) is obviously a monster against red decks, capable of shrugging off every [Keldon Marauders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders) and [Goblin Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) an opponent cares to throw (and is quite a [Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jitte)-bearer). [Circle of Protection: Red](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circle+of+Protection%3A+Red) is superb both against conventional red decks and against All-In Red ... even better, in many cases, against All-In Red, given that the opponent will have gone, you know, *all-in* on a single threat. Calcano suggests de-emphasizing the anti-red sideboard cards, and maybe adding a third [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte).

[Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) was generally good—a card he never sided out—particularly against red decks and Zoo; the third would be a good card for the mirror (which might in fact become a factor given Christian's win), and a generally solid draw elsewhere, as well.

So what about more substantive changes?

The only main-deck change Calcano suggests is to swap [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) for [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm). [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) does everything [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) does, and a little more, and a little better, after all. I was surprised to hear that he would not change the mana base. "One [Watery Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave) is all you need" ... and anyway, every [Marsh Flats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats) and [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) in the mana base can find that dual. Don't forget, your [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)s can help a lot in the casting of this particular Charm!



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The champ's favorite cards in the deck were [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) and [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) ... and probably with good reason. Main-deck [Disenchant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant) capability can be randomly useful (Calcano beat an Affinity deck, for instance), but apparently exalted was the best. He recounted a humorous story where he played a (second) [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage), then cracked in with a [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) (set against an otherwise lethal [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift)). The opponent cast [Peer Through Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peer+Through+Depths), found a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), and proudly pointed it at ... *the now-4/4*[Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage)!

"That one drew the frowny-face."

That kind of profitable error will actually come up *more* in paper **Magic**, as the digital 4/4 Pridemages will actually say "4/4" in the bottom corner (at least temporarily).

And one last caveat: the "bad" matchup for this deck is Rubin Zoo! So either the Kibler [Austin](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptaus09/welcome)-winning [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) deck or the Saito-into-Coimbra [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) deck ... Both are significant challenges for Calcano's Doran. These Zoo decks match Doran good card for good card, but top up on [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) can also be a monster ... So between [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and those two, the Rubin Zoo decks can overtax Doran's available copies of [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). 

So watch out!

|  |  |

|  |  |

The deck plays all five basic land types in order to hit the big 5s with [Tribal Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames). That [Godless Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine) can help play [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) and [Yixlid Jailer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yixlid+Jailer), but [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) is just for style points.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td72_3cards2.jpg)  
The part of Bob Maher is currently being played by the ubiquitous [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary); the Knight, besides being a heck of a huge animal, can always find the [Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) in this deck's sideboard, in order to disrupt the Hexmage combination.

The main distinguishing characteristic of this style of deck versus the (now) generally more common Rubin Zoo is its faster offense: lots of attack-oriented one-drops rather than a progressive game plan of mana development and card advantage topping up on [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). With so many more [Kird Ape](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape)s and [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx)es, Domain Zoo is more of a "Zoo," and more vulnerable to cards like [Engineered Explosives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives). On balance—while it lacks the relentless [Cursed Scroll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll)-ness of a [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire)—it has tremendous reach with more [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)es as well as the [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast)-like [Tribal Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames). 

So as you're taking over a game, try to stabilize your life total at obnoxiously odd numbers, like 9, or better yet, 11.

[Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm)s mark the update to Rubin Zoo developed by Tomoharu Saito, on the path that put Andre Coimbra into Top 8 position at the 2009 World Championships. Boasting many of the incentives that made Rubin Zoo so good (if not the trademark [Grove of the Burnwillows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) engine), the [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) version is meant to have the edge in the mirror.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130626224816im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td72_3Cards3.jpg)  
First off, this version has four [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s (Kibler played only three in Austin); secondly, the [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm)s give the deck more copies of "[Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile)" ... meaning that it has not only more [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s, but more ways to deal with the opponent's [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). Of course, [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) is the most important card in a mirror match.

Most everything else is in place; [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) can find [Treetop Village](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) (though apparently WhisperingEye didn't run any [Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter)s). The Blue access for [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) carries over to the sideboarded [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)s and [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage)s. And with four [Ravenous Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Trap)s and three [Tormod's Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt)s, this deck really, really, doesn't want to drop a match to Dredge (or spend a mana defending itself).

[Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) remains a fine intersection of "oops, I won" mise-ability and strategic opportunity. The deck is full of elegant, very premium, cards. [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique), don't forget, were first played together in a [Counterbalance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) aggro-control variant!

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td72_voltron.jpg)  
The real plan with this deck, of course, is to set up a [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) + [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) combination, using [Beseech the Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beseech+the+Queen) and [Muddle the Mixture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture) to assemble the pieces. All together they Voltron into a 20/20 with a poor attitude and short attention span. The [Urborg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg) + [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) + Depths + Hexmage draw we talked about above is one of the strongest unassisted combination openers in present **Magic**.

So what does this Top 8 tell us?

Despite essentially no card pool changes between Austin and today, we have a very different looking Top 8 in one of the early PTQ events. In fact, we have a winner that was not on the radar at all! This is proof not just for more viability of more, different, decks, but a caution against the anti-deck. It is basically impossible to successfully anti-deck yourself through a format with literally *dozens* of viable winners. In Standard you might be tempted to play—and even win with—an anti-Jund deck, but that kind of strategy is going to be less successful in Extended. The closest thing might be Saito Zoo, which is meant to beat Rubin Zoo, both of which have an advantage against regular Zoo (and apparently Doran )... except Saito / Rubin schools are as close to the "Jund" of the format as you can get, so the analogy fails on logic (when expecting Paper, play ... um, Paper?).

So far, we see one digital Blue Envelope in favor of just playing what you like!
