The September 28, 2017 Update
=============================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on September 28, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on September 28, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by people playing *Magic* for the first time.

What People Are Talking About
=============================


1. [Is the Immortal Sun the Mirari?](http://tappedout.net/mtg-forum/spoilers-rumors-and-speculation/immortal-sun-speculation/)

There's a bit of speculation going around the Vorthos community that maybe, *maybe* the Immortal Sun Vraska and Jace and Nicol Bolas and basically everyone are looking for could actually be the [Mirari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) reshaped/remade/retconned/whatevered. I honestly have no idea if that's true, but the theory seems to be catching on. [Abzan Ascendancy on Tumblr](https://abzanascendancy.tumblr.com/post/165800221727/so-i-was-going-to-make-a-post-about-how-the-mirari) also has a succinct point/counterpoint on why it could or could not be the [Mirari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari).

While the deck is a holdover from previous Standard, it got some serious upgrades, plus a new/old card added to its repertoire. [Search for Azcanta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Azcanta) is the new hotness, setting the deck up in the early game, churning through cards in the late game, and getting to the second Approach even faster once flipped. This enchantment will very likely be a staple of the archetype. Similarly, Opt is a cheap, effective addition to the deck. [Settle the Wreckage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Settle+the+Wreckage), meanwhile, is a strong new addition to the deck's sweeper suite.


In the new/old upgrade realm, the deck has incorporated [Farm // Market](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farm+%2F%2F+Market) as a removal spell/draw spell hybrid. Additionally, [Gideon of the Trials](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials) joins the team as a way to control the board and provide a secondary win condition. It also helps that the card can be pretty difficult for some decks to beat Game 1.

The current approach to Dinosaurs looks like it ignores white in favor of a streamlined mana base. It's not like it loses much—there are fourteen Dinosaurs in the deck, plus two cards that make Dinosaurs cheaper. Throw in [Savage Stomp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Stomp) and [Commune with Dinosaurs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+Dinosaurs), and this is as close as you're going to get to a full-on Dinosaur theme deck in Standard. [Samut, the Tested](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Samut%2C+the+Tested) is an interesting inclusion, as, yes, double strike is awesome on a Dinosaur. But note that Samut's -2 ability can be used to enrage a [Ripjaw Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ripjaw+Raptor) as well.


Finally, we have a [Winding Constrictor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) deck from Chiralane that heavily leans on *Ixalan* cards.

At its core, this is a [Winding Constrictor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) deck with the normal trappings—[Rishkar, Peema Renegade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishkar%2C+Peema+Renegade); [Walking Ballista](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista); and [Verdurous Gearhulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdurous+Gearhulk). But then the *Ixalan* starts and makes things interesting.


The deck packs not just [Ripjaw Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ripjaw+Raptor), which is just a good rate, but also Ranging Raptor to grab a bunch of lands. And, of course, the best way to do that is [Walking Ballista](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista), which improves significantly when paired with [Winding Constrictor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) and [Ripjaw Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ripjaw+Raptor).


Oh, and did you notice [Savage Stomp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Stomp) adds two +1/+1 counters to whatever it targets if Constrictor is in play? Neat.
