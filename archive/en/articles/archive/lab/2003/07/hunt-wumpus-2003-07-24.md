Hunt the Wumpus
===============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on July 24, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_240.png)
By Mark L. Gottlieb












### Do you feel lucky?


Hello from Indianapolis! If you're reading this column on the day it comes out, I am, like 25,000 other people, at Gen Con. If you're not reading this column on the day it comes out, I am very, very disappointed in you. Slacker! But, of course, you'd have a good excuse for not reading it if you were at Gen Con too. Of course, many of you are reading it while at Gen Con and are no doubt overcome with the vapors at the thought of being in the same building as me. Don't swoon! OK, swoon. I can wait.


Done swooning? I won't be at the Wizards of the Coast area at Gen Con. Rather, I'll be manning the Gen Con Time Warp booth. The Time Warp is a convention-wide, 36-puzzle megapuzzle that I helped construct. Feel free to stop by and say hi, but don't expect a chat or a game. I'll be busy with the giant puzzle event. However, as interesting as that is, it has nothing to do with [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus).



![hunted wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hunted+Wumpus)

I love [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus). In the first sanctioned Constructed tournament I played in, I ran a [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Hunted%2BWumpus)[Armageddon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Armageddon) deck. People told me I was nuts: [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Hunted%2BWumpus)[Armageddon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Armageddon) had a fundamental design flaw. The point of playing a fattie and following it up with [Armageddon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon) was so your opponent couldn't get a reasonable answer into play. Wumpus counteracted the “I'm blowing up your lands so you can't play anything” strategy. I didn't care; I liked how the deck played. It also featured [Argothian Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argothian+Wurm), [Yavimaya Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Elder), [Groundskeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundskeeper), and [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest) and I piloted it to a decently mediocre finish. You see, [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus)'s drawback sometimes isn't a drawback at all—and that was the gamble I rode to (some) victory!


In an environment (and this can be a tourney environment or the environment around your coffee table) where no one is packing huge creatures, [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus)'s ability is negligible. Who cares if your opponent gets a free [Raging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Goblin)? You have a 6/6 beastie on (quite possibly) turn 3. Unfortunately, it's not always that simple. Your pal might be struggling mightily with a deck designed to hardcast [Bladewing the Risen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bladewing+the+Risen) . . . until the Wumpus makes the concept of mana development unnecessary. At a time when the pros are running [Akroma](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma)s and [Silvos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvos)es, counteracting the Wumpus's drawback by crossing your fingers isn't quite going to cut it.


### Now You See It . . .


So how can you complement our poor, hunted friend? Lots of ways! Perhaps the easiest is with bounce. Your opponent gets to transfer a creature from hand to table? You get to transfer a creature from table to hand. And let's not forget the major improvement that [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus) made on its way to *Eighth Edition:* All of a sudden, its creature type (Beast) is a huge benefit.

### Attempting Tempting


If you're going to play with [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus), your deck has to be prepared to deal with free, scary permanents plopping down on the other side of the table. And if your deck is prepared for that, you might—*might*—be able to handle [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus)'s younger cousin, [Tempting Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempting+Wurm). (They're related by marriage.) The Wurm is a lot riskier: Your opponent gets multiple permanents; lands, enchantments, and artifacts (not just creatures) could magically appear; and on turn 2, when you *really* want to play the Wurm, you are just not able to handle the consequences.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/global/images/mtgcom_daily_mg81_pic1_en.jpg)


Long, long ago, when I first started writing this column, (Huh? It's only been a few months? Yikes!) some of my reader mail either contained or requested ideas for how to make [Tempting Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempting+Wurm) playable. It's huge, it's cheap, it's neglected, and its drawback is intangible (in your mind, when you fantasize about playing the Wurm, your opponent just puts a tapped [Forgotten Cave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forgotten+Cave) into play and meekly passes back to you, the all-powerful Magicmeister, who has telekinetically willed all other permanents to cower at the bottom of the deck). In short, it's so, so tempting. And bad. It's so, so bad.


Dana Costa pretty much hit the nail on the head about how to play with the Wurm. He mailed me two different strategies, both of which are compelling. And he had this to say about [Tempting Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempting+Wurm) itself: “I think the card is hilarious and horribly unusable. Therefore I traded for four of them and are trying to put them to good use.” Now *that's* the spirit!


Idea #1 is to put the Wurm into a discard deck. Once you help your opponent out by relieving the burden of having to hold those heavy, heavy cards, neither the Wurm nor the Wumpus has a drawback anymore.

### Bait & Smash


Dana's other idea is to use [Tempting Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempting+Wurm) as bait. You play [Tempting Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempting+Wurm) on turn 2 or 3, then see what kind of goodies appear across the table. If your opponent doesn't have much to play out, you're in good shape with your 5/5 Wurm. On the other hand, if your opponent is too greedy and plays out a bunch of creatures for free, you wipe them all out (along with your lone Wurm) with Wrath of God. You *need* your opponent's creatures to come into play—if they didn't, how could you smash them?


![Hunted Wumpi](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/global/images/mtgcom_daily_mg81_pic2_en.jpg)


This next deck expands on that theme. Once again, it's a fine [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus) strategy. Follow up a Wumpus with a [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and you've easily neutralized your opponent's scariest threat. [Beloved Chaplain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain) can block the free creature all day unless it has evasion. And if you've got an [Intrepid Hero](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intrepid+Hero) in play when you play [Hunted Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Wumpus) . . . well, that's just delicious.


The rest of the deck centers around the massive amounts of mass destruction you've got. [Nantuko Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Monastery) and [Elephant Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) let you survive the apocalypses with some creatures intact. The Guide also makes a great combo with the Hero: Make the Elephant ride piggyback on any of your opponent's creatures and it becomes a big enough target for the Hero to knock off—then *you* get the Elephant token! And [Lhurgoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lhurgoyf) and [Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis) both interact well with the full graveyards you should provide.
