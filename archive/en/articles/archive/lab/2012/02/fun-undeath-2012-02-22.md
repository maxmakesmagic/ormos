Fun with Undeath
================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on February 23, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











*I'm back. I mean, welcome back, and all that. I'm just... okay, just, read. I died, okay? Last Saturday. But I came back, somehow. And I feel...stronger. More sturdy. A bit angrier, even. Okay, you caught me. Full blown evil. I don't even understand it. Mikaeus can't be behind this (unless I'm not human... there could be something there).*

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl183_1.jpg)  
*No, I've acquired undying from a different source. Undying's a neat ability to have. It meshes well with the "things get worse" vibe of* Dark Ascension*. Consider an oncoming [Vorapede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vorapede). Coming up with 4 damage suddenly isn't so much an answer as the birth of a new problem: a slightly larger [Vorapede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vorapede). Undying definitely gives me a sense of restlessness; a kind of unstoppable darkness to shudder in.*

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vorapede)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vorapede)  
*Anyone who was around for persist has a good grasp of how ridiculous this sort of effect can be. The two abilities have often been compared, and for obvious reasons; they're mirror images of each other. And just as persist was ripe for the shenaniganning, undying has a perennial capacity of crazy.*

*So let's get to today's enduring decks. Hold on*... there we go. Had to stab my voodoo doll self, something I do occasionally. (That is what's happening on [Gnarled Effigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnarled+Effigy), right?)

The Young and the Restless
Let's start the undying festivities with an awwww-worthy common from *Dark Ascension* (not the only one to be featured today). [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) is simply radiant, with its ferocious little stats and that vivid art. As what's known as a "French vanilla" creature (a creature with just one ability), [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) can let your opponent know of your necro-tinged intentions from the first turn. Follow it up with a turn-two [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist)—an exciting new spark plug for mono-green aggressive decks—and get attacking.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl183_2.jpg)[Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) | Art by [Ryan Pancoast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Ryan+Pancoast%22%5D)

While coming back with +1/+1 counters is an exciting prospect, I wanted to find ways to take advantage of undying that could result in hijinks of an infinite nature. One of my first finds was [Simic Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Guildmage), which can move a newly minted +1/+1 counter to another creature (possibly itself) and keep undying creatures coming back. But my gears began spinning rapidly upon reading an email from Owen Rodley, who suggested [Spike Rogue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Rogue). Whoa. And with an [Ashnod's Altar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashnod%27s+Altar) thrown into the mix, the results were zany.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

In Owen's words: "Sacrifice [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) to [Ashnod's Altar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashnod%27s+Altar), gaining two colorless mana. [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) returns to the battlefield with a +1/+1 counter on it. Pay two to [Spike Rogue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Rogue) to remove the +1/+1 counter from [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) and place a +1/+1 counter on [Spike Rogue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Rogue). Repeat for an infinitely large Rogue."



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Owen also pointed out the ridiculousness possible with [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores), and suggested the legendary Fungus for consistency. I wanted to keep things mono-green, though, so I instead used [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Root-Kin), which helps out all my creatures with +1/+1 counter and has the Rogue's ability as well. Having a huge attacker won't outright win the game without trample, so I added [Bramblewood Paragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon)s to give the deck lethality.

A mono-green deck usually has mana-ramping tendencies, and after some searching I went with [Devoted Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) and [Fertilid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid). With all the +1/+1 counters around, these cards can provide more mana than usual.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

I used [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) as an undying (of a sort) creature tutor. This led to some fitting singletons entering the deck. [Vorapede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vorapede) seemed pretty obvious. [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) can get huge in this deck. Finally, [Predator Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator+Ooze) is, for a three mana 1/1, a deceptive beatstick.

While I'm on the subject of mono-green decks with [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), I thought I'd take a tangent to discuss haste on green cards. This anomaly has actually reverberated throughout **Magic**'s history, and I wondered whether it was at this point possible to attempt a mono-green haste deck. (I've done the same with troll-shroud... I mean, hexproof.)



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

[Uktabi Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uktabi+Drake) kicks things off at my desired pace. A three-turn sequence of Drake, Geist, and [Boggart Ram-Gang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) seems formidable. The creature scale thickens with [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) being its insane self (and [Giant Solifuge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Solifuge) skitters in for 4 every now and then), topping out with the mighty [Thornling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornling). Just add mana!

For extra oomph, I added some of green's suspend creatures, which acquire haste upon settling into the present. [Groundbreaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundbreaker) seemed like a fun one-of as well. And after noticing a lack of a consistent two-drop punch, I went with [Skinshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinshifter). Sadly, it doesn't have haste, but it beats soundly and I've never used it in a Lab project before.

Among the noncreature spells lurks [Hunting Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Wilds), a fun land fetcher that also can make hasty green attackers in the late game. [Evolution Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Charm) is a multifaceted tool in any green mage's library. [Tower Above](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Above) is often a devastating spell, and [Hunter's Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunter%27s+Insight) gives the deck some card-drawing power.

The Figurehead is Undead
Finally, the rotting man himself. [Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed) is certainly one of the more exciting cards in *Dark Ascension*. As a representation of where Mikaeus (formerly the mighty Lunarch) finds himself these days, this card also harkens back to the legendary creatures in **Magic**'s past that also received two cards to emphasize change ([Kamahl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Pit+Fighter) is [more famous](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Fist+of+Krosa), but [Balthor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balthor+the+Stout) is a [more astute comparison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balthor+the+Defiled)).

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl183_3.jpg)[Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed) | Art by [Chris Rahn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Chris+Rahn%22%5D)

Let's start with the basics. Mikaeus weighs in at a 5/5 physique for six mana and carries three abilities. He intimidates blockers, which is neat, I guess. He's a quasi-[No Mercy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=No+Mercy) for Humans, which speaks to his newfound hatred for his former race. Finally, and most bonkers of all, Mikaeus gives all your non-Human creatures undying. Is this silly? Straight-up.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed)  
Through the help of many emails, I cobbled together a list that's packed with tons of Mikaeus combos. Matthew NoLastName suggested [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion). Remove the first two counters to ping your opponent, then use the last on the [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) itself. It'll be a 1/1 with 1 damage on it, so it'll die, and come back with an extra counter. So... infinite damage.

Jamison VanLoocke came up with a plan to ping-pong two [Fume Spitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fume+Spitter)s between the graveyard and the battlefield. In his words: "Pop one to saddle an opposing guy with a –1/–1 counter and have it come back with a +1/+1 counter. Pop the other to remove that counter, and repeat." MetaRundas came up with a similar combo and provided an appropriate victory condition: [Flayer of the Hatebound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+of+the+Hatebound). This guy is monstrous as a personal [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) for restless creatures only.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Too many people to name here suggested I combine [Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed) with a persist creature. I went with [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap). If you have a sacrifice outlet, you can sacrifice the Redcap over and over for infinite damage. [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) seemed cheap and useful.

I filled out the deck with some synergistic cards. [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) stands four deep in this deck, and it'll dish out serious damage. [Netherborn Phalanx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Netherborn+Phalanx) finds your important six-drops. With all the death and undeath, [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) is sure to be annoying, especially with Flayer or Mikaeus around. [Hex Parasite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hex+Parasite) was added as both a strong one-drop and a method of removing +1/+1 counters from your undying creatures, so they come back forever. And while I'm playing with this robotic Insect, I'll throw in a singleton [Phyrexian Etchings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Etchings). This combo has been in the back of my mind for a while. Use the Parasite to control the cumulative upkeep and draw scads of cards.

The deck resembles white weenie. Play efficient kitties and attack with said kitties. Equip certain cats (like [Sunspear Shikari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspear+Shikari) or [Skyhunter Skirmisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyhunter+Skirmisher)) to deal more damage. [Kemba, Kha Regent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kemba%2C+Kha+Regent) can make even more Cat tokens. [Taj-Nar Swordsmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taj-Nar+Swordsmith) can directly find equipment for Kemba or [Raksha Golden Cub](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raksha+Golden+Cub), the Cat tribal benefactor himself. [Seht's Tiger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seht%27s+Tiger), meanwhile, is a meme waiting to happen.

Hope you all enjoyed. Until next time!
