What's In a Name, Part I
========================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on October 13, 2009 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Kelly Digges











[![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130613201905im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_nameGoto2.jpg)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/62%20)Some months ago, I was sitting at my desk when a member of **Magic** Ramp;D came up to me looking for a specific article I'd written—one of the ones I wrote about the Star format, so that some of what I'd written could be used for a description of Shard Star in the *Alara Reborn* Player's Guide.

I was able to point to the correct article after a bit of searching, but not before this Ramp;D member shared an honest opinion: "You have perhaps the least informative article titles I have ever seen."

And you know, I have to concede the point. I put very little thought into my article titles—mostly, they're just snappy phrases vaguely related to my article topic that I think will get you, the reader, to click in and read the article. In fact, sometimes that leads me to make them less informative rather than more.

This is all well and good on a week-to-week basis, but as this Ramp;D member discovered, when you're going back and trying to find the "the one with the rules for Star" or "the one where Kelly dies to [Coalition Victory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Victory)," it's just about impossible. So, uh, sorry about that.

That's why, today, I decided to go back over the year-and-a-bit I've been writing the column and write a short blurb about each article. I've tagged them with categories as applicable—formats I wrote about, cards I previewed, deck lists I shared, etc. The hope is for this article to serve as a permanent reference to my run on Serious Fun thus far.

Because a full year of articles is an awful lot, and because I did want to write a paragraph or two about each one, I've split this article into two parts. The second will run two weeks from now.

Enjoy!

  
![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_primal.jpg)[**The Crux of the Matter**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf1)  
*Preview* ([Primalcrux](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux))

  
 My first article for Serious Fun came before I was actually the weekly author of Serious Fun. To fill in during the author search, I volunteered to write a pair of preview articles. In this first article, I made no effort to hide or restrain my enthusiasm about [Primalcrux](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux). The piecemeal way I chose to preview it might or might not appeal to you, but you've got to admit, it was big.

  
[**The Colors of Magic**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf2)  
*Preview* ([Murkfiend Liege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murkfiend+Liege))   
*Format* (Elder Dragon Highlander)   
*Game Report* (The one where I died to my own [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher) off of [Warp World](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World))   
*Deck* ([Momir Vig](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momir+Vig) EDH)

In this second preview article, I introduced several of my friends, as well as a number of ideas that would become staples of the column: the game report, the "Format Info" box, the "firestarter" at the end of the column, and my enduring love of green, especially in combination with blue.

[**Casual Casualties**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf5)  
*Social*  
*Format* (Two-Headed Giant)

This article, my first as regular author of the column, shared readers' opinions on keeping a less experienced player involved in a big, complicated game filled with old, unfamiliar cards. You all had a bunch of great suggestions for getting people started in multiplayer. This column also has the dubious distinction of rendering rather laughable my claim that the Format Info box covers a format "in brief," with a sidebar that runs alongside half the article.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_table.jpg)  
[**Star Power**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf6)  
*Format* (Star)   
*Game Report*

My introduction (and maybe yours) to Star **Magic**, and the subtle politics thereof. This time out, I used the simplest version of the rules. I also learned that if you're looking to play and write about multiplayer, it helps to bring a camera and some decks.

  
[**Call Me Mr. Suitcase**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf7)  
*Deck Building*  
*Deck* (Red-Green "Boartusk's Bite")

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130614035215im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_cardBoxes.jpg)  
With a huge collection and (at that time) no preexisting decks, I had a lot of work to do, the goal being to fill a "suitcase" with ready-to-play decks. Perhaps my situation was unusual, but nonetheless, here are some ideas for getting started. The title is an oblique reference to an old column in *The Duelist* magazine, ancestor of this site.

[**Elf Help**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf8)  
*Social*

While looking through **Magic**'s many Elves for Elf Week, it struck me that some of them reminded me of people I knew. I talked about [Elvish Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Champion), [Elvish Handservant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Handservant), [Hunting Triad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Triad), [Taunting Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taunting+Elf), and [Hunter of Eyeblights](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunter+of+Eyeblights). My only regret about this article is that I didn't include the obvious firestarter: Which Elf type are you and your friends, and what types did I miss? I chickened out and wrote some other, much less awesome firestarter. Lesson learned!

[**That New Card Smell**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/1)  
*Format* (Pack Wars, a.k.a. MiniMaster)   
*Game Report* (The one with the thinky face, plus the time I won with [Soul Reap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Reap))

I advocated playing Pack Wars as a way to make opening a booster pack more interesting, although I think I was a bit hard on just busting packs. Sometimes, you just want to bust packs. And that's OK.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_suitcase.jpg)  
[**Crowning Achievements**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/2)  
*Format* (Free-for-All, which barely counts)   
*Game Report* (The one with the [Palliation Accord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palliation+Accord))   
*Social*  
*Decks* (White-Blue "Tapped Out?", Green-White "Token Effort," Black-Red "Like Hell," Blue-Black "Stolen Secrets")

After a rousing game of good old Free-for-All, I pondered my role as kingmaker, and the difference between aggressive groups and merciful ones.

  
[**The Riddle of the Sphinx**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/3)  
*Preview* ([Sphinx Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+Sovereign))

In previewing a mythic rare for the first time, I told the tale of being in the room when the very first mythic rare was opened. I couldn't tell you at the time that it was [Empyrial Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empyrial+Archangel), nor could I tell you that [Elspeth, Knight-Errant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant) was also opened later in the draft. And I certainly couldn't show you the pictures we took of Elspeth, like all other real cards opened before their set is street-legal, being callously destroyed. It's probably better that way.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130614012011im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_titanicUlt.jpg)  
[**[Where Ancients Tread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Where+Ancients+Tread)**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/4)  
*Preview* ([Where Ancients Tread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Where+Ancients+Tread))

Like most of my preview articles, this is a basically straightforward meditation on the possible uses of the card, with some forays into Johnny country for wacky combos.

[**Building a Better [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/5)  
*Preview* ([Titanic Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titanic+Ultimatum))

My longtime love of [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) gets a three-color facelift. Note that the references to "double lifelink" are anachronistic; these days, you gain the same amount of life no matter how many times your creatures have lifelink. Sad in this particular case, but not so sad when you're at 3 life with a 3-power creature unblocked and a 10-power lifelink creature blocking something else. It's better this way.

[**Keeping Busy**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/6)  
*Game Report* (The one where John Carter called me a notorious cheater)

Stories and preliminary deck-building scribbles from the *Shards of Alara* Prerelease.

[**A League of Your Own**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/7)  
*Format* (Box League)   
*Game Report*

In a tenuously on-theme article for Bant Week, I discussed forming leagues (ritualized dueling, see?) and wrote about the Box League I was in at the time with the web team and some members of the creative team. While not everyone has the resources to open a whole box at once (and yes, **Magic Online** players, I do feel your pain), I stand by leagues as a fun way to get back to basics.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130613205828im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_kellyInHat.jpg)  
[**Deck-Building [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze)**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/8)  
*Decks* (Red-White, Exalted, Esper / *Mirrodin* artifacts)

 I got sick after a sci-fi convention and built some decks with the newly released *Shards of Alara*. I looked at several different ways of incorporating a big flood of new cards into decks and still had time to read an 800-page novel cover-to-cover.

  
[**Left and Right**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/9)  
*Format* (Attack Left / Attack Right)   
*Game Report* (The one where two planeswalker Ultimates go off in one turn)   
*Decks* (Changeling Lord, Ogredrive)

Myself and three friends tried Attack Right and Attack Left, in which you can only—well, you know. The Attack Right game was hilarious fun, with huge haymakers, gigantic [Amoeboid Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amoeboid+Changeling)s, many [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine)-type cards, and a [Rakdos the Defiler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+the+Defiler) and [Torrent of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrent+of+Souls)–fueled win for what would soon become one of my favorite decks, Ogredrive.

[**What's In the Box**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/10)  
*Game Report* (The one with two [Agony Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp)s and two [Sigil Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+Blessing)s)   
*Decks* (Esper, Exalted, Devour)

In a rare display of story continuity, I returned to the Box League from A League of Your Own and got some games in. This article features a funny story about [Agony Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp), a demonstration of the power of this fully armed and operational [Sharding Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharding+Sphinx), and some of the worst photography I've done since I was six. Sorry about that.

[**"Draft?"**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/11)  
*Format* (Plain ol' Booster Draft)   
*Game Report*

In this article, I indulged my love of a format that I play a lot more often than I write about: Booster Draft. The coverage drafts are casual affairs, and I certainly have fun doing them, so I figured it fit under the column's charter. Some readers opined, perhaps rightly, that ordinary Booster Draft is awfully Spikey for this column. A few suggested, as a more casual draft option, something that I happen to love: mixing and matching different sets to create weird draft formats.

![](http://wizards.com//mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_stickyFingers.jpg)  
[**Food for Thought**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/12)  
*Social*

With this article, I decided to see how far I could stretch the premise of a column about casual **Magic**. The article contains a reference to the *Aeneid*, an account of the history of the sandwich, and zero actual discussion of playing **Magic**. Readers' opinions were sharply divided; some were unhappy to read an article on **magicthegathering.com** that didn't seem to relate directly to **Magic** (and at least one facetiously tried to introduce me to "napkin" technology), but many others seemed to enjoy the diversion.

  
[**General Mayhem**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/13)  
*Format* (Elder Dragon Highlander)   
*Game Report* (The one where Brian puts a bounty on Dave)   
*Deck* ([Sharuum the Hegemon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharuum+the+Hegemon) EDH)

Although I'm a huge fan of Elder Dragon Highlander, I resisted writing about it in my column for a long time, because I know that not everyone is interested in a 100-card Singleton format with specialized rules. At the time I wrote this, despite several articles about it on our site and others, the popularity of EDH was mostly contained within the judge community. Since then, the format has really taken off in the wider world, and I've been more willing to write about it in my more recent columns.

Note that the Sharuum deck in this article is no longer EDH-compliant, as [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), [Tinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker), and [Metalworker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker) have since been added to the banned list. It has also gained a lot of cool stuff from recent sets, like [Magister Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magister+Sphinx) and [Sphinx of the Steel Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Steel+Wind). Click here to see the current list as of this writing.

[**The Dragons**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/15)  
*Game Report* (The one that didn't actually happen)   
*Decks* (Black-Green Good Stuff, Bant, Blue-Black Control, Red-White Giants, [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) / [Traumatize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traumatize))

For Jund Week, I transcribed an imaginary game between the dragons of Jund, essentially doing a more action-oriented version of what I did in Elf Help—mapping player types to specific cards. This remains probably my favorite column I've written. To answer a question I've gotten a few times: This game never actually happened, and the five dragons aren't pseudonyms for real people. I made the whole thing up. That made it a great deal of fun to write, but it was also really challenging to come up with the decks and invent a sequence of plays that made sense.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_dragoncross1.jpg)  
I'm quite proud of the decks themselves. I set a rule at the start of the process that I wouldn't use any of the dragons' namesake cards in the decks, because that would be weird. Then, when I saw that [Hellkite Overlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellkite+Overlord)'s deck had 3 [Dramatic Entrance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dramatic+Entrance) and a few slots to fill, I completely forgot about that rule and rounded out the deck with one weird, metatextual copy of [Hellkite Overlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellkite+Overlord). (In retrospect, I would put one fetchable [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) in this deck.)

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130613200921im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/sf/sf60_dragonCross2.jpg)  
The deck I'm most proud of is [Flameblast Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flameblast+Dragon)'s deck, which looks like—and is—a [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck, but has a bizarre self-targeted [Traumatize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traumatize) back-up plan. This idea was suggested to me by my friend Nik at the Thanksgiving gathering I wrote about in my next article, although I changed the win condition from his plan—Arcane spells + [Ire of Kaminari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ire+of+Kaminari)—to the [Worm Harvest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worm+Harvest) / [Flame-Kin Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame-Kin+Zealot) / [Dread Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) setup in the final article.

*Format* (Star)   
*Game Report* (The one where it's Matt and Rebecca vs. me and Elspeth)   
*Decks* (Three-color shard decks)

Four friends and I revisited the Star format, this time with three-color shard decks in ally-enemy order, rather than just whatever we had handy. We also tried out the option to pass the turn across the star instead of to the left, which has become my preferred variant. (Some have suggested passing left but putting each player next to his or her enemies and across from his or her allies. That makes sense, but for me it removes part of the aesthetic appeal of the format. Your mileage, as always, may vary.)

[**Thus Always With Tyrants**](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/23)  
*Preview* ([Blood Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Tyrant))

I specifically requested to preview [Blood Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Tyrant) because of its unique, multiplayer-specific player death trigger. The title of the article is a reference to "Sic temper tyrranis," the Latin phrase shouted by John Wilkes Booth right before he shot Abraham Lincoln. Purists in my comment thread pointed out that the more accurate English translation is "Thus always *to* tyrants." To them I say: Attack you with [Blood Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Tyrant).
