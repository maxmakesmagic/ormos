The Greatest Azorius 1.0
========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on November 1, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







The last time we had one of these Guild Theme Week things I focused on the Selesnya Guild's trademark deck, [Ghazi-Glare](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/history-lesson-best-selesnya-2012-10-04). Many readers appreciated the one-deck walk down memory lane so I would love to do the same thing for Azorius... but that is hard, seeing as Azorius never had any kind of Ghazi-Glare. 


 Which is not to say there haven't been good, impressive, important, all-important, and flat-out *groundbreakingly great* white-blue decks. In fact, the dawn of **Magic** strategy came thanks to Brian Weissman's essentially white-blue "The Deck" (which taught us most everything we know about card advantage and even creature-poor and creatureless strategies)...

### Æthermage's Touch



[Æthermage's Touch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86thermage%27s+Touch) took kind of a long time to catch on, but ultimately ended up a pretty good contributor to the white-blue cause (if a better one to white-blue-black and red-white-blue ones). 


![Aethermage's Touch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aethermage%27s+Touch)
  
 The cool thing on [Æthermage's Touch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86thermage%27s+Touch) was that it "only" cost four mana and was an instant. So you could get the value of a large (and ideally expensive) enters-the-battlefield creature for just four... and then reload the creature card. 


 Now, a fair number of things could go wrong with [Æthermage's Touch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86thermage%27s+Touch)... like you might miss entirely. Or you might get a terrible value (no one is impressed when you flip up a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) for four, using a card, that you have to re-cast if it lives); but we don't pick our tools based on their worst-case scenarios, do we? 


 A well-placed [Æthermage's Touch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86thermage%27s+Touch) could give you, say, an [Angel of Despair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair) during combat; which would take out the opponent's best permanent (often a creature) and present a 5/5 to squash a smaller attacker... before the pick-up, future re-play, incremental enters-the-battlefield, etc.

###  Azorius Guildmage


 Unlike its obviously powerful counterpart in Ghazi-Glare, the AzoriusGuildmage did most of its damage as a sideboard card. [Azorius Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage) looks a bit innocuous but it did a couple of things well. 


![97077](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=97077)
  
For one, it allowed slower white-blue decks to up-shift into aggro-control decks. They could play a two-drop like a beatdown deck and put the opponent on a clock. Now, with the beatdown, these white-blue decks could lean on their counterspells and play Counter-Sliver instead of Weissman.


 And while it isn't 100% sound to think you can lean on a 2/2 creature (quite fragile) paying three or more mana per turn to semi-contain an opponent's creature... the ability to "counter" an activated ability might just be worth the cost on [Azorius Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage). 


 While trying to stop [Glare of Subdual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Subdual) (or even a competing Guildmage) this way would be an exercise in futility, what about transmute? 


![Drift of Phantasms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drift+of+Phantasms)
  
 Transmute was an important ability circa *Ravnica* 1.0 for some decks to get key cards. For instance, [Drift of Phantasms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drift+of+Phantasms) could find both [Heartbeat of Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartbeat+of+Spring)—the center of the then-Standard format's most important combo deck—or [Invoke the Firemind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invoke+the+Firemind) (its kill card). 


![Muddle the Mixture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Muddle+the+Mixture)
  
 On the other hand, [Muddle the Mixture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture) could find [Weird Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weird+Harvest) (which, if it resolved, could fill a Heartbeat player's hand with sufficient copies of [Drift of Phantasms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drift+of+Phantasms) and [Maga, Traitor to Mortals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maga%2C+Traitor+to+Mortals) to win the game), or the simple [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) required to find the one [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) to cast Maga (or the one [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) to cast [Invoke the Firemind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invoke+the+Firemind)). 


 Sticking an [Azorius Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage) on turn two was *desirable* just to put Heartbeat on a clock. But tack on the ability to answer them three-for-three on transmute activations? Over and over? 



[Azorius Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage) may not have been the greatest Azorius... but it was a fine role player. 


### Court Hussar


 Probably the greatest catch-all creature in the Azorius retinue, [Court Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) was an important contributor to a number of three-color decks. 


![107265](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=107265)
  
 In Solar Flare/Solar Pox, [Court Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) was an important early-game blocker in the midrange control decks that we would, today, call Esper. Nothing super strategic here... other than it got them what they needed and kept them alive long enough to use that gas.

### Grand Arbiter Augustin IV


The greatest Azorius? Probably not... but still quite a productive Azorius.


 More than just a main deck threat in Thomas Drake's US Nationals deck, [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) was an important sideboard card against Standard [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) decks once *Time Spiral* came online. 


![Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV)
  

[Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) typically worked by playing lots and lots of red [Dark Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual)-esque cards ([Rite of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame), [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song)) that both gave [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) players a big handful of mana *and* ramped up storm for [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) itself. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Rite of Flame | 220561 |


[Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) is an expensive spell—unless you have a ton of mana, you can't cast it at all, let alone cast it in dramatic and devastating fashion. 


 Grand Arbiter made that annoying or hard to do. While it would not stop a red "[Dark Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual)" that produced net two mana or more ([Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) jumps from three to five, for instance), [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) did more than a little to spells netting only one mana. 


But no... great role player; fine sideboard option, sure... but not the greatest Azorius in our estimation.


But what about forecast?


Yes, forecast!


Forecast—like detain today—was the Azorius keyword mechanic the first time the white-blue color combination got its own guild play back in 2006. There are several cards with forecast written on them, but only two are of truly legendary status. I would posit that one is pretty fantastic but the other is the greatest Azorius.


### Proclamation of Rebirth



[Proclamation of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth) is a legitimate contender for the title. One half of a two-card combo that has spawned an entire deck archetype, [Proclamation of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth) is both powerful and capable of playing around blue counterspells... even when going big. 


![Proclamation of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth)
  
 When you use [Proclamation of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth) "right" you are forecasting it, not actually casting it. Your job is to go and get a [Martyr of Sands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) while collecting a ton of white spells in hand. Hilarity ensues. When you really have it going, you have seven mana in play and seven white cards in hand. So you can forecast while leaving up one mana... and gain 21 life per turn! 


 Leaving one mana up is important so you can sacrifice your [Martyr of Sands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) before next turn (you need it in the graveyard to be doing much mischief). 


 There have been a couple of different [Martyr of Sands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands)/[Proclamation of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth) decks over the years (including Conley Woods's [Sorin Markov](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin+Markov) deck), but for this writer's money, it is Gabriel Nassif's near-miss that makes for the most Azorius attempt:

 In this build, you play [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) with entwine to find [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar) and [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) has haste, so can immediately copy [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar); the [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar) copy untaps [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker), so you can run it back again over and over as many times as you like. 


![Tooth and Nail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tooth+and+Nail)
Then, you attack for some arbitrarily huge number.



[Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) + [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar) was great! For one thing, you could attack for as big a number as you wanted, regardless of the opponent's life gain. For another, it could often strike out of nowhere... Görtzen's deck had only one copy of each and could easily have won either a more straightforward ([Phantom Nishoba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nishoba)) or fair ([Loxodon Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch)) route. The idea it could combo-kill immediately upon hitting [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) was probably pretty jarring for at least some opponents. 


 Although Görtzen didn't win that PTQ he did go on to win [Pro Tour San Diego](/en/events/coverage/ptsad10) some three years later!

Similar endgame... quite a different path to get there.


 Joe Lossett scored second in a StarCityGames Legacy Open with a much faster route to getting an infinite number of [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar)s in play. 


 In Cephalid Breakfast, you can play a first-turn [Nomads *en*-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomads+en-Kor+) , then a second-turn [Cephalid Illusionist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Illusionist). Then your opponent is in a lot of trouble. You repeatedly target the [Cephalid Illusionist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Illusionist) with the [Nomads *en*-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomads+en-Kor), eventually exhausting your library. In the meantime you flip over all these [Narcomoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba)s and put several flashback spells in your graveyard ([Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) and [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy)). Your now-spent Nomads and Cephalids can team up with your [Narcomoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba)s to rip up the opponent's hand with [Cabal Therapies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapies), so long as you have three creatures left to [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) for free.

 Today, we have [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)s, [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s, and even [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) that can accomplish much the same thing as [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar) in various decks with and without [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). I myself won my last big tournament with [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) armored up with [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) about a year ago.

 But paving the way for those infinite combinations was [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar). And [Sky Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar) did it in lots of different places.... that weren't Azorius or even base-white-blue Control decks. You can certainly run tickertape parades for the various sideboard heroes of the Azorius—the [Azorius Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage) and especially [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV). Surely props go to [Proclamation of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth), which uses forecast in a much more "Azorius way," I think you will agree. 


But for the proudest color combination, maybe, in the history of the game (that, yet, lacks an iconic "Azorius" deck)... my vote goes to the five-drop flier that never played by the rules... despite being from the guild all about the rules!
