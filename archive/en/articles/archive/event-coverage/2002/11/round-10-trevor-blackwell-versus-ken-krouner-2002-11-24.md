Round 10: Trevor Blackwell versus Ken Krouner
=============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 24, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











“I’m going to go 0-3 with two [Slice and Dice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slice+and+Dice)”, bemoaned Ken Krouner right after the draft at his table. It might have been true-the table consisted of so many good cards that many players ended up with block constructed decks. His prediction came not true, as he and Trevor Blackwell sat down to game.

### Game One:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpla02/942.jpg)Ken started off the festivities by cycling [Choking Tethers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choking+Tethers), which led him to a not quite strong turn three play of [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder). Meanwhile, Trevor simply played swamp after swamp. On the fourth turn, Blackwell faced down [Ascending Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascending+Aven) and the goblin, but his nearly mono-black deck [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat)ted away the Sledder. 

Trevor’s deck contained a ton of removal, highlighted by [Cruel Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Revival), [Death Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Pulse), [Crown of Suspicion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion), [Feeding Frenzy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeding+Frenzy), double [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest), and quadruple [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat) (give or take the [Solar Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solar+Blast) in his red). He faced immediate problems with popular internet writer Krouner dropped [Charging Slateback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charging+Slateback), as his hand consisted of multiple [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat). He cycled one away, and used a third to kill a face-down [Skittish Valesk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skittish+Valesk).

Blackwell looked to stop the bleeding by playing [Aphetto Vulture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aphetto+Vulture) on the sixth turn. Ken turned his fellows straight into the flyer, exchanging the Slateback for the flyer. Trevor knew the rules well, and wisely chose to put the Vulture back on top of his library (since it could trigger its effect off of its own demise). Ken played [Graxiplon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graxiplon) on his next turn, after trading [Ascending Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascending+Aven) for the Vulture. The 3/4 unblockable creature went all the way as Trevor drew no answers to deal.

Ken Krouner 1 - Trevor Blackwell 0

“Schwats are no good here”, joked Krouner.  
 “[Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat)s and a bunch of land, you mean”, laughed Blackwell.  
 “I was looking at a hand full of morph creatures and I was like I’ll just play the goblin, thanks”, replied Ken.

### Game Two:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpla02/941.jpg) Trevor decided to play first, as both players kept their hands. Ken’s hand consisted of multiple Islands and a bunch of red cards, which Trevor came out strong with a hand full of fast zombie beats. Blackwell went straight to the offense with [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) and [Severed Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Severed+Legion), and it looked bad for the bleached blonde Krouner.

Fortunately for him, Ken finally drew a Mountain on his third turn, and was able to drop [Thoughtbound Primoc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtbound+Primoc) onto the board. Unfortunately for him, Trevor had one of his several [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat)s in hand to clear the flyer off of the table. Even more unfortunate, his fourth turn play consisted of [Ascending Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascending+Aven).

Trevor: “That guy can only block flyers, right?”  
 Ken: “Unfortunately, yes.”

Willing to race, Trevor cast [Crown of Suspicion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion) on his 2/2 fear creature to make it a 4/1 monster. His attack knocked KK down to a precarious five life. Ken, on his turn, cycled [Slice and Dice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slice+and+Dice) to kill the Legion, but lost his own Aven to [Feeding Frenzy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeding+Frenzy). This left the board at [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) to a newly cast [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder), with the life totals at 13-5 in Blackwell’s favor.

On the next turn, Ken chump blocked, and Trevor passed the turn. Ken cast [Flamestick Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamestick+Courier) to knock Trevor down another life (via the Anurid) to bring the totals to 12-5. He once again chumped, but finally got a sixth mana to cast [Graxiplon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graxiplon), 11-5.

Trevor thought for a mine, and then decided to cast [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) follow by [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat), killing the now 1/2 blue creature and allowing him to come through for one. Ken simply untapped and played [Mistform Mutant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Mutant), his second in a crusade of huge six casting cost blue critters. This finally stalled the game, with Trevor’s response only being a morph guy, while Ken summoned [Mistform Dreamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Dreamer) and [Clone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone)d his own Mutant, giving him two 3/4 creatures and a 2/1 flyer to Trevor’s 3/3 zombie and mystery morpher, with the life totals now a much more even 7-4. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpla02/938.jpg) Trevor decided he’d had enough, and swung with both of his guys. Ken sat long and hard deciding how to block, since he was aware of Trevor’s second [Swat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat). He couldn’t afford to let any damage through (since Trevor might have [Solar Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solar+Blast)), and he was afraid that Trevor’s last card in hand might be the second [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest). Wisely, he chose to block the 3/3 with one of his Mutants and the morph creature with his flyer. Trevor indeed had [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) number two, but it only killed one of the large and in charge blue guys. 

Ken, facing an empty board against an opponent with an empty hand, had no hard times winning with his remaining Mutant combined with a freshly drawn [Charging Slateback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charging+Slateback).

Ken Krouner 2 - Trevor Blackwell 0
