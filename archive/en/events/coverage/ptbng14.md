SUNDAY COVERAGE
---------------


Welcome to the Modern Magic world, Shaun McLaren. The 25-year-old Canadian best known as a Magic Online player defeated fellow countryman Jacob Wilson in the finals of Pro Tour Born of the Gods, earning the $40,000 first prize, a spot at the World Championship, and immediate promotion to Platinum status in the Pro Players Club. McLaren's WUR Control deck outmaneuvered Wilson's Melira Pod deck in a thrilling five-game match thanks to the timely use of Threads of Disloyalty. McLaren came back from a 2-1 deficit to win the last two games and secure the victory. Game 4 was particularly tense when it looked like Wilson might actually come back after losing all his lands to an ultimated Ajani Vengeant.


Spain continued to be a home away from home for Canadian Magic, as McLaren followed up on Alexander Hayne's win at Pro Tour Avacyn Restored last year in Barcelona. Expect to hear plenty from these three Canadians the rest of the season as all three jump into the hunt for Player of the Year.


The Pro Tour's return to Valencia opened with great anticipation on what the recent changes to the Modern Banned and Restricted List would do to the metagame. The absence of Deathrite Shaman opened up deck choices and made for a vibrant format that featured eight distinct decks in the Top 8. Couple that with the debut of Born of the Gods/Theros Booster Draft on the Pro Tour stage and the impressive performances by all the Top 8 players and you get a Pro Tour few will soon forget.

FRIDAY COVERAGE
---------------


Michael Hetrick stands alone atop the standings with the only 8-0 record at the end of Day One at Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* in Valencia, Spain. Close on Hetrick's heels is Lee Shi Tian at 7-0-1, and then a group of 12 players lurks behind them at 7-1. Day One opened with 393 players battling across three rounds of *Theros/Born of the Gods* Booster Draft, giving the pros a crack at drafting the new set for the first time on the Pro Tour stage. Then the pros turned their attention to Modern for the last five rounds before the cut. With the banning of Deathrite Shaman and the unbannings of Wild Nacatl and Bitterblossom, the metagame opened up to see only two decks—Zoo and Splinter Twin—being played by more than 10% of the field.
