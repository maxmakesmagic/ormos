Starry-Eyed Wonder
==================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on December 6, 2011 










I think I have a problem, you guys. I like to do something repeatedly. I don't mean riding the same rollercoaster twice, or planning to go back to the same vacation destination down the road. I mean doing the exact same thing, again, repeatedly. I'm talking about a format *du jour*, a way to play you just can't shake.

Sometimes I get a little too excited about something new. This is one of those times.

Star Power
For those of you who need a refresher on the format, you can click "Show me the rules!" and I'll, well, you know.

**Star Draft****In Brief:** Star Draft is the combination of two ways to play: [Star **Magic**](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf6) and [Draft](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/11).

**Rules Rundown:** With exactly five players, draft three booster packs. Then, each player builds a deck for a game of Star **Magic**. You can randomly assign seats for the draft or game portion (or both!), or keep everyone where they plopped down. In any case, each player's goal is to knock out of the two "enemy color" players across from them.

**Pros:** It's multiplayer! It's Limited! Most forms of Draft involve just duels or at least eight players. Star Draft requires just five, and everyone plays at once. With the restriction to end the game when the two opposing players are defeated, games move faster than Group Game Draft. And to throw a cherry on top, it's possible to win the game even after being defeated–as long as the two opposing players from you are gone before anyone else wins, you get it!

**Cons:** It's multiplayer! It's Limited! Players that don't enjoy one or the other will have a hard time piling both together. The usual multiplayer Limited caveats apply: deck building, card evaluation, and other ways we normally approach Limited (duels) doesn't necessarily apply. It can be an uncomfortable experience for new-to-multiplayer players.

As you can tell, there were some strange things happening with this draft. I was extremely lucky to open a premium foil [Hour of Reckoning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Reckoning), a card I happened to have been hunting for Commander. All of the token makers were getting picked elsewhere, so I quickly settled into a long term draft plan:


> * *Ravnica*: red-white
> * *Guildpact*: blue-red
> * *Dissension*: white-blue
> 

There were many things I considered along the way. Multiplayer life-loss machine [Poisonbelly Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Poisonbelly+Ogre) and the other black cards that were floating around late led me to consider switching my plan up. [Izzet Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Guildmage) reminded me that I was not picking up many noncreature spells. [Celestial Ancient](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Ancient) encouraged me to grab [Plumes of Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumes+of+Peace) (which turned out to be invaluable). But, above all, I just grabbed all the cards I wanted to play with. It's why I loved drafting *Ravnica* block so much!

While many of these faces aren't necessarily fresh—I have a really great group of friends—I was especially excited to sit down with Jamie again. When I first found myself back in **Magic**, Jamie and I spent quite a bit of time travelling back home from the store. Over car rides and card plays, we shared a lot about ourselves. When his work took him to Jerusalem, Israel a little over two years ago the store just didn't feel the same.

Back home, visiting for the holidays, it was excellent timing for him to stroll into the store: he filled out the fifth slot of our draft!

The Reckoning is at Hand
With the crew settled in and ready to roll, we fired things off the usual way by playing mana for the first few turns. There were a few early drops along the way—Brendan and [Boros Recruit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Recruit), Mo and [Elves of Deep Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elves+of+Deep+Shadow), and Jamie with [Aquastrand Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquastrand+Spider)—but it was Nick's [Necroplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroplasm) that got the table's attention. This, of course, came immediately after my [Gelectrode](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gelectrode).

| --- | --- |
|  |  |

While everyone spent another round of mana growth, Jamie used a [Pyrotechnics](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrotechnics) to kill the 'Plasm as Nick added an [Unliving Psychopath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unliving+Psychopath) to his team. Brendan decided to get frisky too by adding a [Fencer's Magemark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencer%27s+Magemark) to a fresh [Silhana Ledgewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silhana+Ledgewalker). Mo stole the show when he shared a [Hunted Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Horror) with Brendan.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/sf/sf172_pic08.jpg)

Facing down Brendan's tokens, Jamie made a [Streetbreaker Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Streetbreaker+Wurm) and grafted it. I sat on a [Festival of the Guildpact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festival+of+the+Guildpact) for a turn while Mo and Brendan started discussing joint victory. Nick dredged back his [Necroplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroplasm), which killed the tokens at end of turn. My [Gelectrode](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gelectrode) nailed Brendan's [Mourning Thrull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mourning+Thrull) in response to an [Infiltrator's Magemark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infiltrator%27s+Magemark).

Jamie added a [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire), looking to get in on the "Bash Nick" action, but Nick stymied the aggressive efforts with a replicated [Gigadrowse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gigadrowse). I used my Festival to draw a card at the end of Jamie's turn. I needed to dig.

Bingo! I found my [Hour of Reckoning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Reckoning). Unfortunately, I was stuck at five lands (though two of them were ["bounce" lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advancedamp;type=+%5B%22Land%22%5D+!%5B%22Basic%22%5Damp;block=+%5B%22Ravnica%20Block%22%5Damp;rarity=+%5BC%5D)). After looking over my cards I reread [Hour of Reckoning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Reckoning).

"Convoke?" I almost said aloud when I realized that I could cast my [Beacon Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+Hawk), keep it around, and use it to cash my answer. Triple white was a tough prospect for me otherwise. But getting there would cost me. Both Brendan and Mo took advantage while my shields were down, and dropped me to just 6 life. Before it finished wheeling to me, Jamie took the offensive against Nick, reducing him to 10 and casing [Gristleback](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gristleback) and [Bloodscale Prowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodscale+Prowler), both bloodthirty!

The final result was that Nick cast [Necroplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroplasm), but also chose to graft a +1/+1 counter over. My tokens were safe! Hurray logic!

Brendan stayed on the offense against me, but I just chump blocked with the [Belfry Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Belfry+Spirit) to haunt one of my Bat tokens.  I attacked Mo for 2 on my turn before I cast [Conclave Equenaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conclave+Equenaut).
