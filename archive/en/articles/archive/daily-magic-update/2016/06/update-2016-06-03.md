The June 3, 2016 update
=======================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on June 3, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on June 3, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter). It was only a matter of time.


Today's Must
============


1. [Twitch Plays Grixis: A GP Minneapolis Report](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/twitch-plays-grixis-a-gp-minneapolis-report/) | ChannelFireball | Luis Scott-Vargas

I've noticed something interesting in *Magic* content lately—a distinct dearth of detailed tournament reports. LSV doesn't go round by round, but he does go pretty deep, and it's one of the more satisfying tournament reports I've read in a while. Do recommend, would read again.


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [GW Tokens and Cheating in *Magic*](https://soundcloud.com/user-121566285/gam-e2) | GAM Podcast | Gerry Thompson, Andrew Brown, and Michael Majors

The second episode of the GAM Podcast hits its stride as the three discuss both Grands Prix (yeah, it's technically *Grands Prix*), the power of Green-White Tokens, and, of course, Fabrizio Anteri's cheating scandal.


1. [Ross VS. Majors: White-Black Control VS. Four-Color Rite](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33039_Ross-VS-Majors-.html) | StarCityGames.com | Ross Merriam and Michael Majors

Speaking of Michael Majors, his (free) work on StarCityGames.com is quite the boon to you, the reader. It's going to be rare that I won't share something like this from the Platinum pro, especially when he keeps churning out great content. Today's (second) entry is a versus video covering White-Black Control against Four-Color Rite, certainly one of the defining matchups of Standard that doesn't involve Green-White Tokens. Watch and learn.


1. [Izzet Time to Go Rogue?](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/izzet/) | Top Level Podcast | Patrick Chapin and Mike Flores

Featuring the deck of the day from, um, today ([see below](#dotd)), Chapin and Flores talk going rogue with two new blue-red brews that succeeded last weekend.


1. Triple *Lorwyn* Articles

We've got a few articles on this week's Modern Flashback format, Triple *Lorwyn*, possibly the best format ever created, because it has [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) at common. *Common!*


First up, Xger (probably not their real name) writes [Into the Past Wilds: Modern Flashback Week #17—Triple *Lorwyn*](http://puremtgo.com/articles/past-wilds-modern-flashback-week-17-triple-lorwyn) on PureMTGO. On that very same website, Joshua Claytor also covered [A Year of Modern Flashbacks: *Lorwyn*](http://puremtgo.com/articles/year-modern-flashbacks-lorwyn). Meanwhile, SaffronOlive (definitely not his real name) penned [Rough Drafts: Triple *Lorwyn*](http://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/rough-drafts-triple-lorwyn) on MTGGoldfish.


Meanwhile, you can find me awkwardly splashing [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) into every deck I draft. Every. Single. One.


1. [Magic the PROthering: Gerry Thompson](https://www.patreon.com/posts/5710167?alert=1) | Magic the Amateuring | Meghan Wolff and Maria Bartholdi

While each episode of Magic the PROthering is its own special little snowflake, this one is especially special, because the guest—one Mr. Gerald Thompson—happened to spend some time in R&D in addition to tearing through the pro circuit. He's one of the few players to see both sides of the wall, so it's a welcome insight to the inner workings of Wizards of the Coast—and of Gerry Thompson.

Deck of the Day
===============



1. [Blue-Red Ulamog Control](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpman16/top-8-decklists-2016-05-29)

There were variations on this deck as far back as Pro Tour *Shadows over Innistrad*, where a few intrepid players played Mono-Blue Ramp decks that utilized [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) and [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) on top of a plethora of counterspells. Now, with red added to the deck, suddenly we have a contender as Matthew Hunt made the [Top 8 of Grand Prix Manchester](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpman16/top-8-decklists-2016-05-29) with the list below.


The deck plays much like the old Blue-Red Tron decks that use burn and counterspells to control the board juuuust long enough to start slamming some Eldrazi. [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) helps out (and can immediately cast [Spatial Contortion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spatial+Contortion)) going big, and [Mage-Ring Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage-Ring+Network) and [Shrine of the Forsaken Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+the+Forsaken+Gods) make for some decent backdoor ramp.


The deck seems like it might be the real deal, as it shores up some of the weaknesses of the ramp decks while still getting to go bigger than just about anything.
