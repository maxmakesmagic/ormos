Rubin Transforms in Madrid

SUNDAY COVERAGE



Steve Rubin quietly amassed Pro Points with consistent, solid finishes at Pro Tours in the 2014-15 season alongside multiple Grand Prix Top 8s, earning enough to lock in a spot in last year's *Magic: The Gathering* World Championship.


This weekend was one of firsts for him, as he found himself under the Pro Tour feature match spotlights for the first time. Along with that came his first Pro Tour Top 8, a long time reward after many years of playing *Magic* whenever he could.


His Green-White Tokens deck allowed him to dispatch friends and former teammates No. 23 Brad Nelson and reigning World Champion Seth Manfield in the quarterfinals and semifinals, earning him a seat in the final match against 2015 World *Magic* Cup championship-winning team member Andrea Mengucci. There, the power of Archangel Avacyn, Westvale Abbey, Nissa, Voice of Zendikar, and the resiliency of his battlefield allowed him to attack through Mengucci's Bant Company defenses multiple games, as he defeated the Italian pro 3-1 to earn the Pro Tour champion title and the $40,000 first place prize that comes with it.


Rubin is no longer the American who will quietly amass great finishes on the sidelines. He has transformed into a Pro Tour champion, locking in his return to the World Championship, where we will see him many more times in the feature match area. Congratulations to Rubin on a spectacular victory here in Madrid, Spain this weekend.
