TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------


That's a wrap from the ol' San Antonio. Ryan Scullin used the power of the Mardu Horde to take down his competition in style. After two hard-fought days, Scullin faced his dream matchup in the finals against Orry Swift’s White-Blue Heroic and won the finals in convincing fashion.


The Top 8 was replete with the variety Standard has to offer. The only duplicate deck was two copies of Abzan Midrange, the clear front-runner during the Swiss rounds. Each clan was represented, with a Jeskai, Mardu, Temur, and Sultai deck joining the Abzaners. Rounding out the elimination rounds were White-Blue Heroic and a Mono-Red deck that splashed blue for the eternal-format all-star, Treasure Cruise.


Anything is possible in Standard right now. We all knew it, but the event here in San Antonio confirmed it. If you like a card, there’s a great chance it fits into one of the 20+ archetypes that made it into the second day.


A stellar way to end a Thanksgiving week, Grand Prix San Antonio is in the books, and it’s time to turn our heads to Worlds—less than a week away.


Ryan Scullin earned his trophy with panache, and I hope he celebrates with some leftover turkey, while remembering the Alamo.


Goodnight from Texas! See you all in France!

DAY 2 COVERAGE
--------------


The players are seated and there are still six more rounds to go before the Top 8 can be decided. Four players are sitting on the highest perches— Randall Gay, Jeremy Frye, and David Ochoa stayed strong finishing 9-0; while Angel Solache finished at 8-0-1.


Tons of decks are represented in the Day 2 field. Though, unsurprisingly, the top archetypes are Jeskai and Abzan Midrange, with Mardu Midrange close in tow, those three decks make up fewer than 50% of the field. The rest is split among around twenty different archetypes, and that’s without splitting hairs.


As people are getting ready for Worlds over in France, in San Antonio everyone’s already in full burn. Though there’s some more time before we’ll breach atmo, by the end of today we’ll have crowned a champion of Grand Prix San Antonio.

DAY 1 COVERAGE
--------------


The first nine rounds of the last American Standard event before Worlds is in the books. Up in the top tables are plenty of Abzan and Jeskai, joined by Mardu, Red-Green Aggro and Monsters, Mono-Red, Blue-Black & Esper Control and a Temur deck or two. There are some Four-Color Midranges percolating under the surface, but it remains to be seen if they have what it takes to last to the second day.


Only three players remained completely unscathed. Randall Gay, Jeremy Frye, and David Ochoa stayed strong through the final round to earn the coveted 9-0, with Angel Solache at 8-0-1.


Plenty of people fell by the wayside. Craig Wescoe and Paul Cheon were out early, while Brandon Nelson joined them in the Day 1–only bracket near the end of the day. In the bubble matches a few people skated in at 7-2. Jon Stern, Ben Yu, and Tom Ross converted their 6-2 record into a 7-2 record scraping into Day 2. While Brian Kibler and Texas boy Roberto Berni gained the final tick in the loss column and won’t be returning tomorrow.


There’s plenty more action bright and early on Sunday. All that turkey is finally digested, and people will be ready for another full day of Magic.


See you tomorrow!
