DeRosa Does it Again!
=====================











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gptoro06/fin.jpg)It has been quite a year since Antonino DeRosa had his silly likeness commemorated on a Player Card. Coming in to Toronto he had won the second Grand Prix of his career, became the 2005 U.S. National Champion, finished second at the team competition at Worlds, made his first Pro Tour Top 8 at Prague, way more than doubled his lifetime winnings, and capped it all off with another Grand Prix win this weekend.


DeRosa defeated his good friend, and occasional teammate Jon Sonne in two quick and dirty games in his ninth appearance in a Grand Prix Top 8.


There was little time for rejoicing though as everyone had to hurry home and get working on the 60-card Ravnica decks for PT Charleston that was looming in less than two weeks. See y'all then.

Sunday, June 4: 7:19 p.m. - Top 8 Draft: Drafting with Jelger Wiegersma
-----------------------------------------------------------------------


Ravnica


The first three picks were easy for Jelger: first pick offered him [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) over [Plague Boiler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Boiler), [Skyknight Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire) and [Conclave Equenaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conclave+Equenaut). Second pick gave him a bomb rare in [Cerulean Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerulean+Sphinx) and his third pick was [Snapping Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake) over some mediocre green cards. His fourth pick was a tough choice though: move into black with [Brainspoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) or [Dimir House Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) or stick to his colors with a safe [Benevolent Ancestor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benevolent+Ancestor), also fitting nicely into a deck with already two flying creatures. Fifth pick he once again decided to pick a safe [Screeching Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Griffin) rather than a [Dimir House Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) or [Moonlight Bargain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Bargain). The sixth pick surprisingly offered him a late [Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottled+Cloister), and he rounded the pack out with a couple of [Terraformer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terraformer)s and a [Courier Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courier+Hawk).


Guildpact


The first pack offered another great flying creature in [Harrier Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrier+Griffin) over [Pyromatics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromatics). Second pick brought the choice Guildpact often brings: [Steamcore Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird), [Ogre Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Savant), [Izzet Chronarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Chronarch) or [Blind Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Hunter). Jelger opted for the Weird but thought about taking Savant for a long time. His next pick showed that Jelger isn't *always* a greedy man when he took [Izzet Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Signet) over [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal), another Chronarch and [Ghost Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Warden). He still got a [Ghost Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Warden) in the next pack, shipping [Train of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Train+of+Thought), and in the next pack picked up Guardian's Magemark, ignoring Osiary Thrull and [Streetbreaker Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Streetbreaker+Wurm). The black cards kept on coming as the next pack contained [Exhumer Thrull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exhumer+Thrull) and Teysa, but Jelger realized that it was too late to pick up black cards and picked another [Ghost Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Warden). [Bloodscale Prowler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodscale+Prowler), [Thunderheads](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderheads), [Train of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Train+of+Thought) and [Withstand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withstand) rounded out a reasonable second booster.


### Dissension


The last booster came up, and Jelger's deck wasn't looking like it might win him the Grand Prix yet. He was in a good position for the final booster though: John Fiorillo on his right and Antonino De Rosa on John's right both didn't plan on picking any Azorius cards.


The first pick Jelger took [Plumes of Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumes+of+Peace) over [Ocular Halo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ocular+Halo) and Soulsworn Journey, and second pick he passed the same two cards and the white Eidolon for [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar). Another Plumes was a fine third pick, but the fourth and fifth picks were amazing for Jelger: two [Pride of the Clouds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pride+of+the+Clouds)! After that, he got passed an unexciting booster and then a very late [Azorius Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald), confirming once again that there weren't many Azorius drafters at the table. [Beacon Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+Hawk), Prahv, [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) and a very late white Eidolon rounded out a terrific pack for Jelger.

He traded Prides again and made [Cerulean Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerulean+Sphinx), but Mark replied with [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk). There wasn't really anything Jelger could do anymore to race the Knell; all he could do was use two [Plumes of Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumes+of+Peace) to tap two of Mark's flyings and drew [Steamcore Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird) to take care of the third while he swung his [Cerulean Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerulean+Sphinx) in for five. Mark thought long and hard if he had anything to get with his [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk), to block the Sphinx with, which he could sacrifice to his Rotwurm, but he didn't and took the hit down to two. "10.000 dollar Helix? Well, not quite, but pretty good", said Mark, but Jelger didn't have it and died in Mark's next attack step. Just for value, Jelger flipped over the top card of his deck and it was indeed the Helix. "Ohhhhhhhhhhh, that's almost Craig Jones style", Mark commented.


The third game was very long and tense, and both players got the chance to use their bomb cards but in the end Mark's [Savage Twister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Twister) combined with Debtor's Knell were too powerful for Jelger's two [Pride of the Clouds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pride+of+the+Clouds), [Cerulean Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerulean+Sphinx) and [Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottled+Cloister). Jelger commented that he might have unnecessarily overcommited the turn before Mark cleared the board with [Savage Twister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Twister) and that he should have kept back his [Azorius Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald), but then again, he never saw the Twister before this game so there was no real way he could have known.

"I broke my number of chairs back in the day, wanna guess how many?". Mark replied that he couldn't count that high, and after all the beats were laid, Mark shipped back his six. Infiltrator's Magemark made sure the Ragamuffin wasn't getting blocked, and Mark didn't have [Savage Twister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Twister) to take care of things so they went on to the second game.

"Some people...", Antonino commented, and Mark admitted that he was a scumbag for saying that. Antonino kept his six and we were on our way. Antonino shrugged and followed things up with a Rotwurm. Sunday, June 4: 10:33 p.m. - Semifinals: Jon Sonne vs. Kyle Sanchez
-------------------------------------------------------------------


This match pitted Pro Tour veteran Jon Sonne against amateur Kyle Sanchez, who is making his first Grand Prix top eight appearance. If Sanchez felt any pressure, he didn't show it. He'd already secured the prize as the top amateur when he defeated Jay Z. Jiang in the quarterfinals, so he was already guaranteed to leave Toronto with more money than winner of the tournament.


### **Game 1**


Jon mulliganed to six, but Kyle kept a very mana-heavy hand so being one card down might not matter. Jon led off the game with [Squealing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squealing+Devil). Kyle played a [Freewind Equenaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Freewind+Equenaut) and they started trading damage. Jon tilted the race in his favour with a [Greater Forgeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Forgeling), and Forgeling and the Devil drop Sanchez to 8. Jon added an [Entropic Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entropic+Eidolon) to the board. [Teysa, Orzhov Scion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teysa%2C+Orzhov+Scion) stymied [Squealing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squealing+Devil) for a turn


[Twinstrike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twinstrike) by Jon evened things up, but Sanchez had more creatures, playing out [Selesnya Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildmage) and [Minister of Impediments](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Impediments). [Squealing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squealing+Devil) hit for two, and Sonne used [Pyromatics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromatics) to nuke the Guildmage and Minister. Sanchez's Equenaut enabled him to play a bloodthirsty [Ghor-Clan Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Savage), which he traded for Sonne's Forgeling. They traded a few more creatures, but Sonne had no answer for the Equenaut and a [Gristleback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gristleback) for Sanchez put the life totals heavily in his favour. Kyle sacrificed an [Aurora Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurora+Eidolon) to his [Golgari Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Guildmage) and returned Teysa.


With Sonne at seven, Sanchez sent both the Guildmage and Equenaut. Sonne double blocked and [Golgari Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Guildmage) pumped itself once, killing both blockers. Sanchez replayed Teysa. Sonne Transmuted [Brainspoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) for [Izzet Chronarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Chronarch), which he played, returning both Eidolons and [Twinstrike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twinstrike). Kyle got in for four with Teysa and the Equenaut, before playing [Aurora Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurora+Eidolon) and [Orzhov Euthanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Euthanist).


On his turn, Sonne thought for a while but just played the [Entropic Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entropic+Eidolon), leaving up [Twinstrike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twinstrike) mana. It didn't matter though, since without Hellbent, Sanchez could save one of his creatures with his [Aurora Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurora+Eidolon) and alpha strike anyway.


Kyle Sanchez 1 - Jon Sonne 0


### **Game 2**


Jon opted to play first, and both players kept their opening seven. Jon laid a [Dimir Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildmage) and Kyle played a turn-two Guildmage of his own. Jon had the [Last Gasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp), and drew first blood. [Dimir House Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) joined Jon's squad, but a [Freewind Equenaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Freewind+Equenaut) and [Minister of Impediments](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Impediments) on Kyle's side meant that racing would be difficult. [Squealing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squealing+Devil) would've broken the stalemate but Kyle had a [Demon's Jester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demon%27s+Jester).


At this point, Kyle had the flyers to enable his big Gruul monsters, but he didn't have any Green mana. Jon's Izzet chronarch returned [Last Gasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp), and he aimed it at the [Demon's Jester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demon%27s+Jester), which enabled the Devil to race the Equenaut. Sonne sent everyone but his House Guard, which was still being Impeded by the Azorius Advisor. Kyle blocked and traded his [Freewind Equenaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Freewind+Equenaut) for Sonne's Chronarch. When Sonne played *another* Chronarch and returned the [Last Gasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp) a second time, Sanchez scooped up his cards.


1 Kyle Sanchez - Jon Sonne 1


### **Game 3**


Kyle chose to play first. After looking at his opening hand, he immediately went to six cards. Then he just as quickly went to five. He kept it: [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Gristleback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gristleback), and [Moldervine Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak). He led off the game with [Teysa, Orzhov Scion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teysa%2C+Orzhov+Scion), and Jon answered that with [Rakdos Ickspitter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Ickspitter). The Orzhov Guild leader cracked for two, and Kyle played a bloodthirsty [Gristleback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gristleback). One Ickspitter ping and house call by the [Orzhov Euthanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Euthanist) later, the beast was finished.


Teysa put on her [Moldervine Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak) and attacked for five, but Jon had the answer once again as his freshly played [Dimir House Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) let him Ickspit then Euthanize Teysa with the Euthanist's Haunt ability. At this point, Jon started to pull ahead. He played out creature after creature, while destroying the ones Sanchez could play. Kyle eventually got a [Moldervine Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak) on a [Beacon Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+Hawk), but Jon trumped it with [Djinn Illuminatus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+Illuminatus).


With just one creature back to block, Sonne played [Squealing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squealing+Devil) pumping [Restless Bones](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restless+Bones) and attacked with everyone, dropping Kyle down to three. He looked at his top card and extended the hand.


Jon Sonne 2 - 1
