Sworn Nemesis
=============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on June 17, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Bwahahah! By entering my column this week, you have doomed yourself to be subject to my evil plans! These include, among other nefarious plots, three decks of **Magic** that are based around adversaries, foes, and other antagonists. And of course, what kind of so called *Archenemy* Week wouldn't have some discussion on the latest multiplayer format? 

[*Archenemy*](/Magic/TCG/ProductArticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/archenemy/productinfo) will be released tomorrow, and I for one am excited. After the smash success of [*Planechase*](/magic/TCG/ProductArticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/planechase/productinfo), I was pumped when I learned another unique multiplayer format was being developed. Last time, [I covered an awesome game of *Planechase*](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/54) that I played with some members of Ramp;D. No such luck this time, but hopefully if I attend the [Release Event](/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/archenemy-facts), I'll have some fun stories for next week!

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_archart.jpg)  
But back to my other focus for today: The concept of archenemies, or villains, if you will. I decided that this week I'd run through some of the antagonists of the modern storylines of **Magic**, and discuss some deck building opportunities.

Mirrodin's Mad Monarch
*Mirrodin* premiered a while ago, but the plane of artifice contained one devious villain, [Memnarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnarch) himself.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_memnarch.jpg)  
I loved [Memnarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnarch) as a card when it was first revealed. In fact, as a creature with a repeatable activated ability, I freaked out upon reading it (as I did with [Filigree Sages](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filigree+Sages) and [Flamekin Spitfire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Spitfire).) [Memnarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnarch) has not one, but two activated abilities, both sans ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170424152623im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/tap.gif). In terms of deck building, [Memnarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnarch) is often best used as a finisher in artifact styled decks that can generate lots of mana. Pump blue mana into him to slowly gain control of every permanent on the board.

Kamigawa's Kontrolling Kami
As I've said before, the saga of Kamigawa is my favorite of all the **Magic** storylines. I love all the interesting characters, including the protagonists ([Toshiro Umezawa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toshiro+Umezawa), [Michiko Konda, Truth Seeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Michiko+Konda%2C+Truth+Seeker)) and the villains. Ultimately the mastermind behind the whole Kami War turns out to be the mischievous Mochi, the [Kami of the Crescent Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kami+of+the+Crescent+Moon). ([Konda, Lord of Eiganjo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Konda%2C+Lord+of+Eiganjo) plays an antagonistic role as well, bringing [That Which Was Taken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=That+Which+Was+Taken) into the physical world.)

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130609090546im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_kamiArt.jpg)  
As a card, [Kami of the Crescent Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kami+of+the+Crescent+Moon) is a fairly simple creature, basically existing as a blue [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) on legs. Nowadays, it seems like a solid card to include in a [Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap) deck. [Cerebral Vortex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerebral+Vortex) is a perfect contributor in these sorts of decks. [Lore Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lore+Broker) is a manaless way to make your opponent draw as well.

Ravnica's Ruinous Rulers
Ravnica, being a city made up of ten guilds, has various characters wheeling and/or dealing to be named Chief Villain. [Szadek, Lord of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Szadek%2C+Lord+of+Secrets) and [Momir Vig, Simic Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momir+Vig%2C+Simic+Visionary) had plans of their own to thwart the Guildpact and take control of Ravnica, but I'd have to mark [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) as the overseer of it all. From tricking Argus Kos into breaking the Guildpact to enslaving [Szadek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Szadek)'s ghost to being involved in the [Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter), the Azorius ringleader is the main adversary.

![](http://wizards.com//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_grandArt.jpg)  
As a card, Augustin works well with other white and blue spells that you play, while acting as a [Sphere of Resistance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Resistance) type of card to your opponents. I thought I'd take advantage of this by using the white and blue cards from *Shadowmoor* and the sets of Alara to cast spells on the cheap while slowly smothering your opponent. [Thistledown Duo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thistledown+Duo) costs only ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161223010200im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white-blue.gif) with the [Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbiter) on the board, and it should get in for some damage early on. [Swans of Bryn Argoll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swans+of+Bryn+Argoll) is an unkillable 4/3 flyer for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161223010200im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white-blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161223010200im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white-blue.gif). [Messenger Falcons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Messenger+Falcons) is a flying 2/2 that draws you a card for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20210429165526im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green-blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif). [Talon Trooper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talon+Trooper) and [Ordered Migration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordered+Migration) are other white and blue spells that are efficient and have to do with Birds. The secret last ingredient is a copy of [Kangee, Aerie Keeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kangee%2C+Aerie+Keeper) to pump up all your Birds for 2 less than normal!

Re-Enter Bolas
From here, [Nicol Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nicol+Bolas) pretty much takes over. He's basically been the archvillain of every block from *Time Spiral* onward (except the next section's.) He's the biggest and the baddest. Such a scoundrel is Bolas that he is represented with two different cards, the titular [Elder Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Dragon) and the 

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Conflux+era+planeswalker)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conflux+era+planeswalker). Both cards are ridiculous for various reasons, but I won't build with him today. Instead, check out [my article](/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/33) for last year's [Nicol Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nicol+Bolas) Week, in which the terrifying Dragon crushes my television and eats my cheesecake.Lorwyn / Shadowmoor's Shifty Lord
The dual worlds of Lorwyn and Shadowmoor fold into each other in a process called the Great Aurora. The main adversary of these worlds is [Oona, Queen of the Fae](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oona%2C+Queen+of+the+Fae). With her abilities of altering the day and night of the two worlds, she was a target for the heroes of the storyline, Rhys and Maralen.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20190621075712im_/https://www.wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_oonaArt.jpg)  
Oona is yet another eyeball-exploding card with a cool activated ability. Simultaneously milling and making Faerie tokens? Sounds great to me. Like [Memnarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnarch), deck building possibilities for Oona aren't exactly endless, but it's a great finisher in decks that could support her. [Painter's Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) can guarantee a ton of Faerie tokens, however, so there's a good place to start.

Zendikar's Hidden [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror)s
The Eldrazi probably qualify as the villains of the Zendikar story. They're big, powerful, and unstoppable. I'd thought all the Johnny-tastic combos with the Eldrazi were depleted, but I found some more inspiration starting with one of the archenemies of old, [Volrath the Fallen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath+the+Fallen). Hailing from *Nemesis* (itself a synonym for archenemy), [Volrath the Fallen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath+the+Fallen) has the cool ability to discard creature cards to pump itself up. Obviously, the larger the discarded creature card, the better.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130609082306im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_volrathArt.jpg)  
Imagine discarding [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) to Volrath. The Eldrazi will shuffle itself back into your library, while you have a 21/19 attacker at the ready. To make sure it has a safe route to your opponent, why not discard [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) as well? Spending ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) and discarding [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) and Koziliek, Butcher of Truth will leave you with a 20/18 flyer. Oddly enough, [Filth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filth) also works in this position, assuming you have [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) on the battlefield.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130609182802im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_3Cards1.jpg)  
The real reason I want to use blue is for a very forgotten rare, [Shifty Doppelganger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shifty+Doppelganger). I wanted a way to get [Volrath the Fallen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath+the+Fallen) or even one of the Eldrazi on the battlefield and attacking as soon as possible, and [Shifty Doppelganger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shifty+Doppelganger) can do just that. A neat combo is [Shifty Doppelganger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shifty+Doppelganger) with [Grozoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grozoth). Although you'll have to pay ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20160830042801im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless4.gif) more to attack with your 9/9, what this really accomplishes is loading your hand with 9 costing fatties to either pop onto the battlefield with the Doppelganger or discard to Volrath. Such fatties include [Kuro, Pitlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuro%2C+Pitlord) (perfect for the Doppelganger, as you avoid the upkeep payment and still get to swing and kill other creatures), [Inkwell Leviathan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkwell+Leviathan), and [Artisan of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek) (perfect in this deck, as you can cast it later on to return the discarded and/or sacrificed creatures to play.)

Archenemy: The Format
Okay, now that we're through talking of **Magic**'s various villains, let's get to the part where you become the archenemy of the table! In [my article during *Planechase* Week](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/54) I picked one of the pre-built planar decks and built a new 60-card deck around the 10 plane cards. I thought I was going to do the same thing here, with the pre-set scheme cards. However, I decided to shake it up a bit and build my own scheme deck first.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130610041214im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_2Cards1.jpg)  
I love the concept of schemes, but it's the ongoing schemes that really interest me. If you could ensure that your ongoing scheme would never be abandoned, you'd basically have an untouchable bonus of some kind. Take The Very Soil Shall Shake for example. Having all your creatures +2/+2 and trample is a great bonus, and unlike an enchantment, ongoing schemes can't be [Disenchant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)ed or [Demystified](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demystified) or touched in anyway. The only way it'll go away is if one of your creatures hits the graveyard. But if you have [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Samurai+of+the+Pale+Curtain) in play ... then it's a permanent scheme!

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_2cards2.jpg)  
Luckily, other ongoing schemes have the same abandonment trigger. I Know All, I See All won't be abandoned if you have the Samurai in play (unless you cast a lot of instants or sorceries) and you'll have a permanent [Seedborn Muse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seedborn+Muse) effect. My Undead Horde Awakens becomes a permanent [Debtors' Knell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Debtors%27+Knell) without fear of abandonment. Of course, [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Samurai+of+the+Pale+Curtain) prevents permanents from going to the graveyard ... but not cards directly from a hand or library. If you manage to mill some creatures for example, My Undead Horde Awakens can benefit.

![](http://wizards.com//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl95_2Cards3.jpg)  
To fill out the rest of my scheme deck (which needs to be a minimum of ten cards, up to two of any scheme) I went with one of each of the scheme cards that appears in every scheme deck. I also put in some scheme cards of my own choosing: [Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roots) of All Evil (free creatures,) Every Hope Shall Vanish (discard,) and My Genius Knows No Bounds and Every Last Vestige Shall Rot (fun cards in general.) 

So we're looking at a creature-heavy white-blue milling deck that likes to untap its stuff every upkeep. Hello, [Ambassador Laquatus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambassador+Laquatus)! [Drowner of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Secrets) ushers in the Merfolk theme. [Summon the School](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summon+the+School) won't cut it when graveyards aren't around, so I'll go with [Stonybrook Schoolmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonybrook+Schoolmaster) and [Judge of Currents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge+of+Currents).
