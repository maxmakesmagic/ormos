Round 4: Rob Dougherty vs. William Jensen
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 4, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a971.jpg)"Of course Rob's baby is cute, look at this guy!" - William Jensen

"I can't believe I'm being called a baby face opposite William Jensen!" - Rob Dougherty

The fourth round is when the stars come out, and this event is no different. William "Baby Huey" Jensen, Grand Prix and Masters Champion, squaring off against the heart and soul of Your Move Games, Rob Dougherty. Jensen is playing Angry Hermit Part II, the explosive reanimator combo-deck. Dougherty is with Benzo, the same deck that he piloted to the Top 8 at Houston. 

### Game 1

After arguing the appearance of everyone at the table they rolled to see who would go first. It was Dougherty. He opened with a swamp, giving the first move to Jensen. Jensen's play was a big one: [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), [Mox Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Diamond). [Hermit Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hermit+Druid), setting up the kill the following turn. Dougherty was prepared. Having slow-rolled the [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), he struck this turn. He saw a hand of [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate), [Exhume](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exhume) and land. He took reanimate and sat on his [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb). Jensen drew, tipped over his library with the Hermit, then sacrificed it to flash back [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), forcing out Dougherty's [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) for [Faceless Butcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a970.jpg)Now he held back. Dougherty had nothing to add to the table. Jensen brought back two [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate)s with [Krosan Reclamation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Reclamation). The first brought back [Petradon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Petradon), insurance against [Diabolic Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Edict), and the second brought forth the [Sutured Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sutured+Ghoul) for the kill.

Jensen 1 - Dougherty 0

### Game 2

The second game was a farce. The inconsistency of Angry Hermit Part II showed up and forced a pair of mulligans out of Jensen, and left him with a terrible five-card hand. He failed to draw action while Dougherty [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb)ed [Petradon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Petradon) and [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate)d it to start the beats and stunt his growth. The game went on a few turns, but it wasn't really in doubt.

Jensen 1 - Dougherty 1

### Game 3

Jensen opened the deciding game with [Llanowar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes). Dougherty's [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) on [Hermit Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hermit+Druid) hit, but Jensen just set himself up an [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) with [Vampiric Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampiric+Tutor), and spent his next turn putting [Verdant Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Force) into play. Dougherty put out [City of Traitors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors) and passed it back. Jensen brought him to twelve.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a972.jpg)But Dougherty had an [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) of his own, getting [Faceless Butcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) into his yard. He untapped and started thinking. He forgot to stop, though, and wound up with a warning for slow play. Eventually he found the line he was looking for. He Cabal Therapied Jensen's [Gilded Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake), then played a swamp and [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate)d it, losing his [City of Traitors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors) in the process. He was at a comfortable ten life. Jensen swung in with all but one Saproling and brought him to six, but Dougherty had the hammer in hand: [Engineered Plague](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague) on Saprolings to make passage for his [Verdant Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Force) and bring Jensen to one. Jensen held back on his Drake, and chumped. [Stronghold Taskmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Taskmaster) made sure he had no outs. He checked with [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb), and then scooped.

**Final Result:** Rob Dougherty defeats William Jensen 2-1
