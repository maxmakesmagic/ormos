Sunday, 10:00 a.m. – The Evolution of Delver of Secrets
=======================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 16, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/olle_rade_author.jpg)
By Olle Rade











After nine grueling rounds of swiss yesterday, the 1,587-player field is down to 186. Most of the fringe decks have been eliminated and the meta game has become a little more predictable. I took a look at what the players at the 
first 10 tables were playing to get a view of what decks did best yesterday and how field looks for the people very much in contention for the top 8.


The first thing that struck me was the rise in popularity of BUG Delver. Six months ago the most successful deck with [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) was Red, Blue and Green with [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and 
[Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) in addition to the *Innistrad* namesake. And the last Legacy Grand Prix in Washington in November saw the rise of the Blue, White and Red version with [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) 
and [True-Name Nemesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True-Name+Nemesis), which also ended up winning the event.

Although the Blue, White and Red [Delver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver) deck was a popular choice yesterday, the top ten tables this morning saw no pilots of it. Three [Delver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver) players however, opted for Blue, Green and Black version, and one player was with the good ol' Red, Blue and Green.


The reason for the rise in popularity in Black and Green over White and Red is a response to the increased amount of [True-Name Nemesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True-Name+Nemesis) currently in the format. Cards like [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Golgari Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Charm) out of the sideboard are good against it, and some players chose to play answers to [True-Name Nemesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True-Name+Nemesis) in additions to their own [True-Name Nemesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True-Name+Nemesis). Having access to [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) is also a good choice, both against other [Delver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver) decks, and against the Miracles deck with [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) that have been doing well this weekend. Spaniard Ruben Delgado won a trial Friday night with this list.
