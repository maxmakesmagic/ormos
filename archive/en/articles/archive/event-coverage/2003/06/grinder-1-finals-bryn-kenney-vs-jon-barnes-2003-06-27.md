Grinder 1 Finals: Bryn Kenney vs. Jon Barnes
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 27, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New York–based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 






![Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nantuko+Shade)Gut-wrenching.That is the only term to describe 256 people with a 1-in-64 chance of qualifying for US Nationals. The first grinder filled so quickly that the event staff had to shunt over 150 eager competitors into the Limited Grinder that was not scheduled to begin for several more hours.

Every round is grueling but never more so than in the round of eight. A long weekend would have only gotten longer had roommates-for-the-weekend Brian Stroh and Bryn Kenney ended up staring across the table from one another. Fortunately for them and the –no doubt—half dozen or so other players crammed in that room for the weekend, they ended up fighting side by side instead.

Bryn is a former JSS standout trying to make the difficult transition from the junior ranks to the big leagues. Rather than choose one of the myriad net decks available from various Nationals, Bryn chose to concoct a Blue-Black Zombie deck in his room last evening. 

Across the table from him, his opponent Jon Barnes is playing perhaps the most familiar and format defining deck of the past couple of years—Blue-Green Madness. Both players have traveled a great distance for this opportunity—Bryn is from the New York/ New Jersey area while Jon is from Radar O'Reilly's hometown of Ottumwa, Iowa.

Both players were relatively quiet as they shuffled their decks and triple checked that they had de-sideboarded. They are both aware that the odds of coming this far in one of the remaining Grinders are against them. And that one of them will be fighting those odds soon enough.

### Game 1

"That sucks!" Jon could not keep his opening hand and his frustration was bubbling over. His next six cards mollified him while Bryn chose to stick with his initial seven.

Bryn made the first play—a [Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade) off of a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and [Underground River](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River). Jon had opened on two [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s and he ripped the turn three [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) for a [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). Bryn was stuck on two lands and attempted to [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) it on his turn. The dog was unsummoned home and played again on the next turn. Bryn drew a third land—another River—and swung into the [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel). Jon blocked the dog and they took turns pumping their creatures. Jon ended up discarding a pair of cards—nothing with madness or flashback while Bryn got the better end of the deal only taking two damage before the two-drops traded.

Jon played another [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel), which was promptly [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother)ed on Bryn's turn. [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) hit play next and Bryn used [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) to bounce it—Jon wasn't hitting him but he had taken five from his painlands. Jon was confused as Bryn tapped all of his lands, "What costs four?" The answer was [Graveborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graveborn+Muse).

The 'Moeba made a reappearance but Bryn's extra card yielded a [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother). Bryn fell to 13 to play a [Shadowmage Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator). Jon played a [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) but the Muse inspired the fourth [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) to the top of the deck. When Bryn played a second Infiltrator, Jon was provoked to a defensive [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)—he played his sixth land to cast the [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) so Bryn was going to make the first land drop. "Let's see if we can start again with less [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother)s!"

Bryn was ahead on lands and was able to play a [Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade) while Jon was on a single [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). A [Withered Wretch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch) and a [Rotlung Reanimator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotlung+Reanimator) later they were sideboarding for Game 2.

Bryn - 1 Jon - 0

### Game 2

Jon sided in [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost)s and [Stupefying Touch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupefying+Touch)es. Bryn was excited that he would be sideboarding his [Stronghold Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin)s for the first time—he also brought in [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)s and two [Hibernation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hibernation)s.

![Stronghold Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stronghold+Assassin)One of the unfortunate side effects of doing well in the grinders is that everyone will know what deck you are playing with. In the case of a novel design such as Bryn's, players scouting for decks to play tomorrow will sit up and take notice. "They're flocking from everywhere. This is THE deck..." Bryn suddenly remembered he still had another game to win and quickly added, "So far."Neither player mulliganed and Jon opened with a turn two [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost). Bryn played a [Stronghold Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin) and Jon came back with a [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). When Bryn attempted a [Rotlung Reanimator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotlung+Reanimator), Jon discarded a pair of cards and a [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) to counter the endless supply of bodies for the Assassin.

Jon flashed back a [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis). When Bryn sent the turn back to him he made his [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) black and madenessed an [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm). Bryn cast [Hibernation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hibernation) and sent the Wurm and the [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost) back to his hand. With the [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost) out of the way, Bryn was free to [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) the [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) without giving his opponent a card.

Jon replayed the [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost) and used [Quiet Speculation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation) to put a [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) and a [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) in his bin. He flashed the Deep back and went to 12. The Assassin got to attack for a turn and Jon fell to 10. Bryn played a [Graveborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graveborn+Muse) and shipped the turn back to an obviously frustrated Jon.

Jon hard cast an [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), which fell to Bryn's Assassin—he played a [Withered Wretch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch) to fuel it, taking out a [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) along the way. Jon fell to seven on Bryn's attack step and Bryn [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ed his opponent, "Let's see that [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)!" Jon unsummoned the Assassin in response and Bryn got to see a couple of lands and another [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm).

Bryn groaned when Jon tapped all seven of his mana into his pool for [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)—to be fair, Jon had drawn two cards—one for the [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and another during his draw step. He had no post [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) pressure other than his replayed [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost). Bryn replayed a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and discarded a pair of lands and a Muse—giving Jon another card.

Jon thumped his deck in frustration looking for a two-drop but had nothing. He could not play anything on the third turn either as he had to sit on his [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) for his opponent's [Stronghold Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin).

Jon's next turn went pretty well. He played a [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) and discarded two Rootwallas into play. He also had the [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). "That'll teach me for overplaying," was all he could muster when Bryn had the [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate). He saved his [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) by throwing three cards at it making it a 5/5.

![Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mutilate)When Jon fiddled with an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) on his next turn, Bryn goaded him, "Someone has the [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study)...and an [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm)." Jon also had another [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) and his board was looking pretty solid only one turn after the [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate). "You are going to hate me..." teased Bryn as he showed the second [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate).Jon was drawing a card each time Bryn cast a spell and had ample creatures for reinforcements—[Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) and [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) came down this time and Jon braced for the third [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate). When Bryn tapped all six mana he shook his head, "That's not a [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)..." It was a [Visara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Visara).

"Got that [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)?"

"Sure..." Jon cracked for six and Bryn fell to eight. [Visara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Visara) made a reappearance and there was nothing Jon could do about it with [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) in hand. He passed the next turn with no attack. Bryn fortified with a [Shambling Swarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambling+Swarm) and a [Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade). Jon drew the [Stupefying Touch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupefying+Touch) for the [Visara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Visara) but it merely ate the [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) in response. [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) found a second [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost) but that could not deal with Bryn's threats on the board.

**Final Result:** Bryn - 2 Jon - 0

Jon did not have time to talk about himself or his deck after the match as his friends hustled him off to see if they could get him signed up for the next Grinder. 

As soon as he won, Bryn was besieged with requests for his decklist. A handful of players that had been taking notes needed Bryn to fill in some of the holes they were unclear on. Bryn was basking in the glow of qualifying with a deck of his own design—with a little help form some friends, "I made it last night. There were [Opposition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition)s in it yesterday but they were stains—Paul Jordan changed them to [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst)s!"
