The Reckoning
=============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 22, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I write all the decks for each week during the previous week. Right now, I'm on a plane on my way to [Pro Tour Gatecrash](/en/events/coverage/martell-calls-down-reckoning-pro-tour) in Montreal. 


 One card, [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), has been on everyone's mind in the week leading up to the [Pro Tour](/en/events/coverage/martell-calls-down-reckoning-pro-tour), and this is the other deck I considered playing that used it. Some versions are much more focused on setting up a variety of combos with it, but this is a more traditional RWU Flash shell that incidentally incorporates [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). 


![Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Reckoner)
 That said, the deck is certainly not without a Reckoner combo—it just isn't Plan A. This deck uses a single [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre), the least-talked-about card that can win the game with [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). Usually, it's just an awesome removal spell—like a [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) that can kill [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) and [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite), thanks to [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) and a large spell count to fill the graveyard—but sometimes, in very long games, you can set up a turn to dump your graveyard on your [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) and deal the same amount of damage to your opponent. There's only one [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) because it can be problematic in multiples early, but it's a high-impact single card. 


 The deck was tuned and played by Matt Costa, an expert in this kind of strategy. It combines the old favorites [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas), [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) in a control shell with [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) to buy time for [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) to lock up a game. 


 Matt Costa calls the deck "Raka-ner" based on the wedge's implicit name from the *Apocalypse* set. "Raka" was seen on cards [Rakavolver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakavolver) and [Raka Disciple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raka+Disciple).
