Ascension Pending
=================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 15, 2009 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Welcome back to From the Lab, **Magic** players of all shapes and sizes. I'm your ever-so-handsome host this Thursday (just like every Thursday), and today we're going to go on a quest or two together. No, not some sort of spiritually enlightening pilgrimage to a far off place, but a magically enhanced quest to some sort of ascending platform.


I speak, of course, of the cycle of Ascensions that can be found in **Magic**'s latest set, *Zendikar*. I'm slightly familiar with these enchantments, being one of the first casual players to lay eyes on [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) (my preview of which can be found [here](/en/articles/archive/lab/start-quest-tivities-2009-09-17).) My co-columnist and this site's editor Kelly Digges has [talked](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/55) at [length](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/57) about two other Ascensions, [Luminarch Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension) and [Beastmaster Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beastmaster+Ascension). Since I rarely feel comfortable riffing on a card that's already been well riffed, we'll pass on those three for now. Instead I really want to focus on the blue and black Ascensions, which haven't been as widely covered. These two Ascensions, [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) and [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension), seem at opposite ends of the spectrum: One requires a relatively easy three quest counters, the other a whopping six.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl60_5Cards1.jpg)  
I want to take a paragraph or two to express my personal Vorthos side in regards to this cycle. I love the idea of a bold adventurer of *Zendikar* hearing about this mystical quest that one can undertake to find one's true calling. Step by tiny step, this adventurer fulfills certain requirements of this calling, such as leaping into the fray of any battle ([Beastmaster Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beastmaster+Ascension)) or researching all sorts of information ([Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension)). Once the quest has been fully accomplished, this adventurer finds a great power within himself, such as summoning Angels out of thin air ([Luminarch Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension)) or doubling the capabilities of sorcery ([Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension).) This idea is such a large one, and to be accurately represented in a cycle of cards that are fun to play with makes me a happy Noel.


But I digress! There is some ascending that I must partake in! Let's get at the ideas and decklists, shall we?


**Overarching Themes**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl60_archmageSplash.jpg)  
Let's start with that big and blue member of this cycle, [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension). To start off with, this card intrigues me for many reasons. You're required to assemble six quest counters on this, meaning you'll have drawn at least twelve extra cards by the time you do so.


![Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Archmage+Ascension)
  
The problem with this is twofold. First, if you fill your deck with different spells that draw you two cards, by the time you fully charge up your Ascension there won't be much to search for. As cool as turning [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) into a double Demonic Tutor is, if you have nothing worth searching for other than more card draw, it's almost useless. 


Secondly, if you are drawing tons of cards in the first place, there's a good chance that you've already drawn into whatever you might want to search for. So if you're looking to use [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension) to cobble together singletons for a combo, you might as well drop it for a straight-up tutor.


How could I solve both of these problems (having tons of draw spells in the deck, and the probable drawing of your searchable cards)? We'll get there. First, let's go through some card choices that I considered and that would make a fine addition to any [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension) deck.




|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Magus of the Bazaar | Lat-Nam's Legacy |  |

[Magus of the Bazaar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Bazaar) is a decent creature that enables drawing two on your opponent's turn, as is [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer). Sawtooth Loon could be potentially bounced every turn to add a quest counter. Among the many instants, [Pulse of the Grid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Grid) stands out as a repeatable quest counter producer. [Lat-Nam's Legacy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lat-Nam%27s+Legacy) is a sneaky way to draw two on your opponent's turn while hiding a tutorable card in your library. Dismantling Blow and [Esper Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm) have flexibility, and Frantic Search is pretty busted. Finally, [Ior Ruin Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ior+Ruin+Expedition) is a free draw two that can be popped on your opponent's turn.




|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dismantling Blow | Ior Ruin Expedition |  |

While many of these cards are fine, I wanted to solve the above problems with one card, and I eventually found [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension)'s perfect companion: [Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl60_mindmoilSplash.jpg)  
As long as you have three or more cards in your hand, every time you cast a spell, you'll pull a mini [Teferi's Puzzle Box](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%27s+Puzzle+Box) and replace your hand *through drawing.* This allows me to play other instants besides card-drawing ones. For example, a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) will now let me create a quest counter for free, instead of having to cast a Catalog.


![Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mindmoil)
  
Also, [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension) offsets [Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil)'s inherent disadvantage of randomizing your hand. Instead of drawing randomly off of the top, if the Ascension is fully quested, you can find whatever cards you want. You can even find the exact hand you placed on the bottom of your library!




|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Brain Freeze | Scattershot |  |

What cards are best to tutor up with the Ascension, with a [Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil) on the battlefield? I've always thought that if [Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil) could be controlled, it would be great in a storm deck. Well, now that you tutor up lots of cards whenever you cast a spell, you could theoretically run lots of Scattershots and Brain Freezes. Here's the process: Cast a Brain Freeze. [Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil) triggers, and you slap your hand on the bottom of you library and tutor for more Brain Freezes. Cast another one, and rinse and repeat. This isn't an infinite combo, but it's a great way to get all your storm spells on the stack at the same time. You might not kill your opponent in one go, so I added some [Mnemonic Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Nexus)es to restock your library for next turn. This is kind of a weird deck, but what else is new?

There's a small toolbox in case the storm cards don't work out. [Hindering Light](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hindering+Light) can protect your vulnerable enchantments, and [Spellbound Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbound+Dragon) is an alternate means of victory (discard those extra [Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil)s!). [Research // Development](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Research%2B%252F%252F%2BDevelopment), meanwhile, becomes really good under a fully quested Ascension. Either you tutor for your other three [Research // Development](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Research%2B%252F%252F%2BDevelopment)s (or two and a [Mnemonic Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Nexus)), or you get three 3/1 Elemental creature tokens, or some combination thereof. The choice is suddenly a lot harder for your opponent when your every draw is a tutor.




|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Spiteful Visions | Truce |  |

Here's another [Archmage Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension) deck. This one loses the [Mindmoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindmoil)s and focuses on another synergistic enchantment: [Spiteful Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiteful+Visions). It automatically amps your card drawing to the point where you get a quest counter per turn, and once you assemble all six, you start breaking the Spiteful symmetry. Cards like [Skyscribing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyscribing) become absurd at this point, as they become a [Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze) and "![](https://web.archive.org/web/20160829100442im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/x.gif): Tutor for X cards" at the same time. [Etherwrought Page](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etherwrought+Page), among its many talents, can offset the life loss from [Spiteful Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiteful+Visions). [Truce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Truce), meanwhile, is just fun when cast under a fully quested Ascension: You'll tutor for two cards, and since you didn't draw the two, you'll gain 4 life as well!

**Bloody Good Ascension**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl60_bloodcheifSplash.jpg)  
[Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) is perhaps the most appealing of the five Ascensions on a purely visceral level. Think about it. It only costs one mana, it requires the second least amount of quest counters (three) to fully charge, and its final ability is a bit bananas.


![Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodchief+Ascension)
  
I suppose it's no secret or surprise, then, when I inform you all that I've gotten quite a bit of mail about [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension). Numerous combos with this *Zendikar* rare have been spotted—some of them even in Standard, no less!




|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Vampire Nighthawk | Archive Trap |  |

One email amongst these came from Brian Stroud. He shared many ways to put quest counters one the Ascension, such as [Blood Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Seeker) (preferably two of them), [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) (Brian cited its game-stalling ability and 2 power on a flying body as reasons enough), and [Hideous End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+End) (kill a nonblack threat and add a quest counter!). His favorite combo with a fully amped [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) was [Archive Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archive+Trap). As Brian said, "If your opponent is foolish enough to search their library, BAM, hit 'em with an [Archive Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archive+Trap) to instantly make them lose 26 life while you gain 26."


As crazy as this is, it's not my favorite [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) combo. The one that grabbed my top honors came from Leo Schwarz. Take it away, Leo:


"[Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) and [Memory Erosion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Erosion). When both are out and the Ascension is charged, your opponent now has a grand total of three spells they can cast for the rest of the game, and if one of them isn't [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize), they lose."


![Memory Erosion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Memory+Erosion)
  
Millicious! Your opponent gets milled for two every time they cast a spell, and if this spell is an instant or sorcery, it will hit the graveyard after being cast. All of this adds up to a painful drain of 6. If the spell is a creature, waste it with [Hideous End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+End). Eventually, your opponent will have to stop casting spells, which is, I'm pretty sure, not the best way to win a game of **Magic**.


Leo had his own suggestions for charging up [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) in the first place, and unlike Brian's, they were Extended legal. Leo's mind "goes automatically to [Inkfathom Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkfathom+Infiltrator) to add counters, or [Looter *il*-Kor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor) with a [Bonesplitter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonesplitter)."


Inspired, I looked around for some ways of my own to put quest counters on [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension). [Pooling Venom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pooling+Venom) is cheap and painful, and [Hissing Miasma](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hissing+Miasma) wards off attackers. [Parasitic Strix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parasitic+Strix) and its [Tidehollow Sculler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Tidehollow%2BSculler) cousin definitely seem worth it in a Standard build, as does [Countersquall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countersquall). [Spiteful Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiteful+Visions) make its second appearance in this article as another suggestion—it does basically [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) your opponent on each of his or her turns, after all. 


![Maralen of the Mornsong](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Maralen+of+the+Mornsong)
  
My favorite method that I found was [Maralen of the Mornsong](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maralen+of+the+Mornsong), which is no surprise given that it's a go-to card for me. Maralen stops all card draws and automatically causes a loss of 3 life on your opponent's turn. It also forces a library search, which is a good way to be able to cast your [Archive Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archive+Trap) for free. Of course, with a Maralen on the battlefield, a player won't lose to decking even if his or her whole deck is milled, so a sacrifice outlet will be key after a certain point. Use her to search up a [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) and cast it, choosing her as your sacrifice.


I made two different decks, one Standard legal and one Extended.
