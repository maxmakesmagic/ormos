Ral Zarek's Stasis
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 28, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







For a long time now, I've been looking to build a Legacy deck with [Winter Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winter+Orb) and [The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Tabernacle+at+Pendrell+Vale). I feel that each of them are among the strongest cards in Legacy that don't see much play, and they're amazing together. I've also been looking to build a deck that just casts [Meditate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meditate) for value, rather than as part of a combo, with a lot of free counterspells. 

![Ral Zarek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ral+Zarek) I'm late to the party on noticing the fabulous interaction between [Ral Zarek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek) and [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis), as [Ral Zarek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek) does everything you could ever want with a [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis), but when reader Justin Butler sent me his Vintage [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis) deck, I thought it might be the perfect shell for the other things I'd wanted to try in Legacy. [Meditate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meditate) is great with [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis), as your opponent can't really do anything with an extra turn anyway. 

 Since I was sure I was behind the times on the [Ral Zarek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek)/[Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis) combo, I did a quick search to see what other people had come up with, and found [this list by Adam Prosak](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=55727). I like his approach, and I think it might be better than mine, because it seems important to have a lot of counterspells, but all the pieces fit together so well that I'd really hope to be able to work in the extra card draw as well. 


[Mox Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Diamond) and [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) aren't great with [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis), but they're awesome with [Meditate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meditate) and [Winter Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winter+Orb). The problem is that you don't really want that many [Winter Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winter+Orb)s when you already have [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis) because they're redundant and bad against some of the same decks. Still, I think I want more than four of that effect. 

 Adam didn't use [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), which is strange to me, as it's the perfect card with [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis). When [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis) is in play, [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) is extremely difficult to play around and it functionally untaps a land for you. 

 The sideboard offers more counterspells or card draw as well as more Tabernacles, [Winter Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winter+Orb)s, and [Vedalken Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) against creatures. [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) is for opposing Planeswalkers and [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) is for opposing [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle)s. [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) is there as an alternate win condition, as I'm worried about the opponent doing something that stops you from winning with only [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and [Ral Zarek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek).
