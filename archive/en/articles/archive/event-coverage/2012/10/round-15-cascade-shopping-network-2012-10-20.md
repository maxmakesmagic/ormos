Round 15: The Cascade Shopping Network
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 20, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Walkinshaw-Profile.jpg)
By Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw












[Stanislav Cifka (Second Breakfast) vs. Kenji Tsumera (RUG)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptrtr12/fm14)


Matthew Costa (Jund) vs. Willy Edel (Jund)
------------------------------------------


[Pedro Carvalho (Robots) vs. David Shakarisaz (Splinter Twin)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptrtr12/fm16)




---

With around 30% of the field playing Jund, it's safe to say there's been a few Jund mirror matches played out this weekend. Anyone who was playing competitive **Magic** when Jund was a Standard deck knows what an intensive battle of resources it can be. It's quite likely that it might come down to which player is better at cascading.


Matthew Costa and Willy Edel have been playing near each other enough this weekend that they know what each other is playing, and they know what they're going to be in for. Both players are 11-3 with two rounds to go, so a loss for either of them would mean watching the Top 8 play without them tomorrow.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptrtr12/r15_costa_edel.jpg)*Matthew Costa and Willy Edel get settled in for a rousing game of cascade chronicles in this Jund mirror match.* 
### Game 1


After a mulligan to six, Costa was the first to attack. Removal spells flew left and right, but Edel was stuck on three land, and his life total was falling one bite at a time while Costa's was not. Things got serious for Edel when he was forced to take down a 6/7 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) with a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and a [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), paying the back-alley bargain price of 4 life and a single mana, putting him at a low, low 3 life.


Edel finally found his fourth land and used it to buy the hot, new [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) from *Alara Reborn* Inc! [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) is a 3/2 Elf with haste, but call now and you'll get a free...


[bad voiceover]: "[Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)! This wheeler and dealer comes with all of the advantages of a regular 2/1 creature, but will also tell you additional dreadful secrets every turn, at the low, low cost of sizeable chunks of your life!"


This looked dicey for Edel from his delicate position on 3 life, but Costa felt that letting the Confidant live would be even more dangerous, and attacked in, letting Edel trade his team for the last of Costa's.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptrtr12/r15_edel.jpg)*Edel's [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) comes with plenty of free goodies. Unfortunately, his life total was on a limited time offer.* 
With the board now clear, Edel was able to start attacking back with his [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine). He followed it up with a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) to bring him up to 5 life. Costa found a [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) to take out the Ravine, and a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) was waiting on top of his deck, bringing with it a...


[bad voiceover]: "[Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)! [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) is the ultimate in kitchen accessories with a lifetime replacement guarantee. If your Finks dies, we'll replace it, free of charge! (new product may appear smaller)"


Once again, creatures traded with creatures and removal spells flew from side to side, until the dust cleared and Costa was left with a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman). Edel was unable to find a removal spell as the Shaman got stuck into the instants and sorceries in his graveyard, and they were soon off to game 2.


Matthew Costa 1, Willy Edel 0


### Game 2


Edel began slowly again in game 2, playing a pair of [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village)s, while Costa's turn one [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) let him cast both a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) on his second turn, discarding Edel's [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull). Edel took out the 'Goyf with an [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay), and had to discard a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) when Costa summoned [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) a turn later. Not to matter, Edel's fourth turn was the new 3/2 hasty [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) from *Alara Reborn* Inc! Place your order now and you'll receive a free...


[bad voiceover]: "[Fulminator Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage), the hot, hybrid way to take care of pesky non-basic land-itis. [Sacrifice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacrifice) it at any time to rid yourself of one non-basic land-itis, forever!"


Edel's team started menacing Costa and his planeswalker, while he could only manage a second [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman). Costa's back was against the wall, until he found a 6/7 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). Edel shrugged and attacked into it with his [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). Costa wondered what could possibly go wrong, and Edel showed him, it slices! it dices! From the creators of [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), *Alara Reborn* Inc, it's [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast)! But wait, there's more! If you buy now, not only does your [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) deal 4 damage to target creature, but it comes with a bonus...


[bad voiceover]: "[Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)! The loveable Ouphe with the most charming smile, how can you ever stay mad at them?"


Costa summoned a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) of his own, today only, with a...


[bad voiceover]: "[Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)! He'll tell you everything and more (please read fine print to see if [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) is right for you, and consult your health professional if life loss persists)."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptrtr12/r15_costa.jpg) *Costa had the cards, but he didn't have the time or life total to enjoy any expensive spells that his [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) could cough up.* 
Unfortunately for Costa, the life loss did persist, and he was forced to reach for his sideboard, where his health professional was assumed to be hiding.


Matthew Costa 1, Willy Edel 1


### Game 3


After using [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) on Costa's opening [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), Edel tapped out for a [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll). Costa had missed his third land drop the turn before, but tore a fetch land off the top, allowing him to summon [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) to take care of the Troll, before he could post anything inflammatory on Costa's life total pad, which was already a little tender, thanks to Costa's typical Modern mana base.



![Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kitchen+Finks)

Edel bounced right back with a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), brought to you by *Alara Reborn* Inc! The purveyors of fine multi-colored cards. If you call within the next 10 minutes, you'll get a complimentary...


[bad voiceover]: "[Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)! The adorable gift that keeps on giving! Well, twice, anyway."


Ignoring Liliana, Edel attacked Costa down to 11. Still low on land, Costa was in trouble. Edel was on 21 life and attacking, while Costa was frantically throwing removal spells at Edel's team.


Edel's [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) dropped Costa to a dangerously low 4 life, but a last minute [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) brought him back up to 9. Edel summoned [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), and Costa a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf)! With a limited time offer available only to readers of Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* coverage, a handy dandy...


[bad voiceover]: "[Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek)! The premier answer to your opponent's low-cost in-hand spells, such as oh, I see, Edel has no cards in hand."


Edel's Confidant found a second one, dropping him to 18. Costa played another [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Inquisition%2Bof%2BKozilek), and found Edel had drawn and was holding back a third [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). He then [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay)'d one of the two in play. Edel's remaining Confidant flipped over an [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay), and then another [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf)! we're just giving these away this weekend to our loyal readers, and yes! there's more, a free...


[bad voiceover]: "[Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)! Yes, they really do keep coming back this often."


The Bloodbraid Elves traded, leaving Costa with [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) to Edel's Confidant and Finks. Costa traded his Village for the first half of the Finks, and summoned a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman). Edel again plucked his [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) off the top, and...


[bad voiceover]: "Really? We're still going to do this? Fine. It was a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)."


The [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) bit the dust, and the resulting 3/3 [Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast) Token could not withstand Edel's monstrous 'Goyf, and Edel finally took the grindiest of grind-out matches to almost assure himself a spot in the Top 8.


Matthew Costa 1, Willy Edel 2
