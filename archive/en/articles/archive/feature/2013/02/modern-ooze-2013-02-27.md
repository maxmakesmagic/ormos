Modern Ooze
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 27, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







With the banning of [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), a pressing question for Modern becomes figuring out exactly how Jund should look now. Some players on **Magic Online** have had success using a card I haven't seen outside of Block Constructed in their Jund decks: [Bloodhall Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhall+Ooze). 


![Bloodhall Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodhall+Ooze)
 jjflipped finished 8th in a PTQ with [Bloodhall Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhall+Ooze) before the banning went into effect on **Magic Online**, and a few players have retooled the deck with *Gatecrash*. The first player I can find playing the deck with *Gatecrash* (incorporating the aggressive [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager)) is Costa Rica's CPal90, who won a Daily event with this list. 



[Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and Putrid Leach are both perfect friends for the [Bloodhall Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhall+Ooze), letting it get two +1/+1 counters each turn, which should get completely out of hand in about one or two turns, considering the Ooze only costs one mana. The deck as a whole has a lower, more aggressive curve than we've seen out of Jund in the past, and it picks up additional burn from [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning). This is definitely an aggressive deck rather than occupying the traditional Rockish/midrange space people generally imagine when they think of Jund.
