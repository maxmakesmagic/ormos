Evolving Event Decks: Infect and Defile
=======================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on March 9, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






The *Mirrodin Besieged* Event Decks are an exciting new product. Players looking to play in sanctioned Standard events can buy a single product that's ready to compete in tournaments. This week, I'd like to discuss an evolution for Infect and Defile, a *Mirrodin Besieged* Event Deck.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab133_plagueMyr2.jpg)  
I didn't expect the Event Decks to be that impressive, but I was happily surprised after playing a few games with the original 75 cards from Infect and Defile. Event Decks go above and beyond what theme decks have been in the past. Here's the original list for Infect and Defile:

The deck performs very well straight out of the box. There are, however, a few quick changes that can be made to make the deck more competitive. The deck can actually evolve in a lot of different directions. We could add [Plague Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Stinger)s and [Phyrexian Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader)s and bash people with [Distortion Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distortion+Strike), or we can go the more controlling route and focus on the card advantage game.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Phyrexian Crusader | Distortion Strike |

If you like the way the original deck plays, you probably want this to be a more controlling evolution. The first thing I would like to do is add a few lands. We're just asking to miss land drops if we play fewer than 25 lands here. I'd go ahead and add two [Darkslick Shores](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkslick+Shores) in place of the two [Contagion Clasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp)s. If you can't find any of the *Mirrodin Besieged* rare land, then you could simply cut the two [Contagion Clasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp)s for extra [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s.

After playing a few games with the deck I was most worried about how it planned to handle planeswalkers. [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive) are both excellent here, but I felt like the deck needed more. I decided to cut two [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s and one [Jwar Isle Refuge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jwar+Isle+Refuge) to make room for some [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit)s. [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) may not deal your opponent poison counters, but it gives you a much better chance of beating resolved Planeswalkers.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab133_attacking.jpg)  
Since we're playing more lands and better fixing I figured it was safe to add a few copies of [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge), this gives me a great late-game answer to creature-lands or a midgame form of disruption. I would like to play a playset of [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus), but the *Mirrodin Besieged* rare is very difficult to acquire. If you're lucky enough to open any number of [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus)es, I would advise cutting up to all three [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)s and one more land to add the card.

![Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inkmoth+Nexus)  
Planeswalkers still seemed troublesome to me, especially [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor). Playing a few copies of [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) seemed like it could increase the overall power level of my deck and give me a better chance of beating [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor).

[Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) is an excellent tool for a deck like this. A lot of decks have access to a large enough swarm of creatures to overload your spot removal. Playing a few copies of [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) gives the deck a better answer to the most aggressive decks in the format.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab133_corrupted.jpg)  
Anyone who plays with the deck will realize that [Corrupted Conscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupted+Conscience) is the best card here. My personal favorite is when my opponent taps out for an [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) and I snatch it up with the *Mirrodin Besieged* Aura. It's an excellent answer, especially for Titans and [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s. I've had White-Blue Control opponents cast [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)s only to have them stolen. I even had a [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) in my graveyard to return when I attacked!

![Corrupted Conscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Corrupted+Conscience)  
[Phyrexian Vatmother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Vatmother) is surprisingly strong, and it's nice that it can profitably block a [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine). The Vatmother is obviously good enough for any deck that plans on winning with poison. It acts a lot like an 8/5 for four mana, that seems good enough for me. It's so good, in fact, that I've actually cut two other cards for additional Phyrexian Vat Mothers.

![Phyrexian Vatmother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian+Vatmother)  
Gerard Fabiano and Ben Lundquist chose to play [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) over [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) last weekend at the StarCityGames.com Standard Open event in Edison, New Jersey. It makes sense; these days it's more important to kill [Precursor Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precursor+Golem) and [Kuldotha Forgemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Forgemaster) than it is to kill [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan). Crazy world, I know. Even cards like [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) can be troubling when you can't aim your spot removal at them. I'm going to change the main-deck [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother)s to [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s. I expect a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) trend, at least in the immediate future. This trend will only make [Phyrexian Vatmother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Vatmother) stronger.

I also wanted to find room for the fourth [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain). The ***Magic** 2011* sorcery is the perfect addition to any control deck that seeks to gain an advantage by grinding opponents out in a long game. You have a lot of cards that are very powerful for specific situations. It's important to draw the correct pieces you need to establish control of the battlefield and finish your opponent off with poison counters.

I updated the sideboard to give you additional answers to planeswalkers. [Volition Reins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volition+Reins) is a great way to steal otherwise frightening planeswalkers and use them against your opponent!

![Volition Reins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Volition+Reins)  
[Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) is a cheap way to prevent opponents from accelerating into cards like [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) when they get to play first, even if you have two lands that enter the battlefield tapped!

![Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spell+Pierce)  
[Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) is a good way to clear out any counterspells so your [Corrupted Conscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupted+Conscience) can do the work it needs to. It's also an excellent way to nab swords out of your opponent's hand after they cast a [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic).

The deck needed better countermagic against [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) and Blue-Red-Green decks, so I decided to include a playset of [Flashfreeze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) in the sideboard. Having a hard counter is important when you're trying to fight decks that have as much mana as [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle).
