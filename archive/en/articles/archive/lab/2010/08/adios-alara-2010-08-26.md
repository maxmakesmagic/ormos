Adios, Alara
============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on August 26, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova












Hello and welcome back to the Lab! This week, as terribly non-theme as it is, I've decided to dedicate my thoughts and words to the soon to be rotated Alara block (made up of *Shards of Alara*, *Conflux*, and *Alara Reborn*.) It seems crazy that it's already time to say goodbye to the most triple colored of all the blocks in **Magic** history, but I guess every block eventually goes. I've made it an unofficial goal of mine to bid farewell to each block as it passes out of Standard, having paid tribute to *Lorwyn*, *Shadowmoor*, and *Coldsnap* similarly in the past. I even summed up *Time Spiral* block early on with a neat riff on [Riftmarked Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftmarked+Knight).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl105_rift.jpg)And now, I'm going to do the same with Alara Block. I realize I'm a tad early on this front then I have been in the past, but *Scars of Mirrodin* is really creeping on the horizon and such horizon-creeping makes me both excited and nervous that I may have missed something from Alara.


So here we go, starting with the first set of the block and proceeding to the last. There is a deck for each set, with some additional comments on cards that catch my fancy (although I don't recall throwing my fancy anywhere in the first place ... is that you, [Memory Erosion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Erosion)?)


### Saying Goodbye is Shards to Do


Shards of Alara was, I thought, a great idea for both a set and a block. Aligning the five sets of allied colors into different settings must have taken an immense amount of creative thinking and artistic direction, and I hereby applaud those responsible for such things. Oh yeah, and the cards were crazy. I remember scanning the spoiler, with my brain already reeling from such *Shadowmoor* cards as [Wilt-Leaf Cavaliers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Cavaliers) and [Boggart Ram-Gang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang), and falling out of my chair at a cycle of amazing three-color, three-drop (and four-drop) creatures. [Woolly Thoctar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woolly+Thoctar) and [Rhox War Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) pretty much blew my mind.

Death by Bog!

And now we get to our deck, based around an unfairly forgotten Grixis legend. [Sedris, the Traitor King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedris%2C+the+Traitor+King) was, I thought, destined to become the king of various graveyard-based casual creature decks. Somehow, it acquired a different, more boring destiny, that of being forgotten in the back of binders worldwide. I'm here to excavate, reveal, or (if I must) unearth it from that dusty dungeon. Yep.


A fun, casual Sedris deck doesn't have to be too complicated or combo frenzied. In my mind, all we need to do is land Sedris onto the battlefield unharmed, through creature-based resources. "Enters the battlefield" and "leave the battlefield" creatures should be staples.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl105_Sedris_the_Traitor_King.jpg)I looked up some unearth tricks that I had forgotten since prematurely dismissing the mechanic about a year ago. Any unearthed creature will be exiled if it were to leave play ... unless the spell that's removing it is also exiling it. Let's say I have both [Supreme Exemplar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Exemplar) and [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) in my graveyard. If I unearth [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), and then the Exemplar (with Sedris) I can champion the 'Drifter and swing with my flying 10/10. When the Exemplar's unearth trigger exiles it, my 'Drifter will come back as a normal [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)!


A cool trick that I enjoy with unearth involves one of my favorite corner case cards, [Pull from Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pull+from+Eternity). Unearth a creature normally, and on your next turn, cast the *Time Spiral* instant on that card. It'll wind up in your graveyard, ready to be unearthed again. It's a one off, so no true craziness, but it's still fun to find loopholes.

Finally, the new ***Magic** 2011* [Titans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&name=+%5BTitan%5D%7C%7Ctext%3d+%5BTitan%5D&set=%7C%5B%22Magic+2011%22%5D) are bonkers with Sedris. Imagine unearthing [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) and getting four Zombie tokens, or [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) for 6 divided damage.


With all this in mind, here's my attempt at a straightforward Sedris deck.

### Conflux Capacity


Alright, *Conflux*! Despite my enthusiasm for *Shards*' flavor, *Conflux* was actually my favorite set of the block. This is due in part to the return of domain, or at least the introduction of domain as an ability word. The "domain deck" picked up some mainstays from this set, from [Exploding Borders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploding+Borders) to [Spore Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spore+Burst) to [Drag Down](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drag+Down). Appropriately in a domain set, *Conflux* also carried a five-color theme, seen in the underrated [Dragonsoul Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonsoul+Knight) and the powerful [Child of Alara](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Alara), as well as the sunburst staple [Kaleidostone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaleidostone).

You must try it."

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl105_Charnelhoard_Wurm.jpg)Wow, that's pretty damn cool. With the Wurm on the battlefield, cast [Soul's Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Fire) targeting the Wurm and your opponent. The Wurm will deal 6 damage to your opponent, and since it did, its ability will trigger. By this time the [Soul's Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Fire) is already in your graveyard, so you can return it to your hand. Voila, a "![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif): 6 damage to anywhere" engine!


Of course, that engine would require ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220908064341im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless8.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) to kill your opponent in one turn. If [Charnelhoard Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charnelhoard+Wurm)'s power was, say, 10, you'd only need two [Soul's Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Fire)s (![](https://web.archive.org/web/20160830042801im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless4.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif).) One Emergence on the battlefield results in an 8/8 Wurm (three [Soul's Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Fire)s), and two gets us to the magical 10/10 level.

### Solid Gold, Say Goodbye


*Alara Reborn* holds the unique distinction of being the only set with 100% gold (multicolored) cards. It also introduced the wonderful cascade (led by torchbearer [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf)) and used a bit of hybrid here and there. [Lorescale Coatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorescale+Coatl) is a new Johnny classic, and [Wall of Denial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Denial) is possibly the best Wall ever for multiple-card combo decks.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl105_Lorescale_Coatl.jpg)My card for this section is the woefully underappreciated [Sages of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sages+of+the+Anima). Let's examine this Elf Wizard (these Elf Wizards?) for a second. Whenever you would draw a card, you'll reveal the top three cards and put any creatures into your hand, while flushing the rest to the bottom. This means you won't be drawing any non-creature cards for as long as the Sages are around. It also means [Lorescale Coatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorescale+Coatl) is *not* a super combo with the Sages, as you aren't technically 'drawing' anymore.


Naturally, to maximize this effect, the Sages work well in a creature heavy deck. Getting all three of the revealed cards is the goal. So I paired the Sages with their new best friend, [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler).


Think about it. Suspending the Chronicler for two after casting the Sages means you're getting another draw (which becomes another reveal three), and when it hits the board, it'll presumably be huge. [Overbeing of Myth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overbeing+of+Myth) is another perfect fit, drawing extra cards and swelling equally.

My win condition is swinging with these fatties, with the help of [Voyager Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyager+Drake). Ramping up to them should be my goal for the early game. Thus, I went with mana-creatures, such as [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder), [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), and others.


Creature abilities that draw multiple cards, such as [Sphinx of Lost Truths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Lost+Truths) or Riptide Survivor, can go nuts with the Sages on the board. [Fathom Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer) ([Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler)'s right hand man) can activate the turn you cast the Sages! [Reliquary Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reliquary+Tower) can keep your hand loaded.
