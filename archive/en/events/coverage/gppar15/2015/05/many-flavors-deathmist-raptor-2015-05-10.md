The Many Flavors of Deathmist Raptor
====================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX PARIS 2015](/en/events/coverage/gppar15)
 on May 10, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten










The raptor fences are down! Roughly 30% of all players in Day 2 of Grand Prix Paris were running a [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor) deck of sorts. Although there's always the lysine contingency if it really gets out of hand, it is fair to say that we're living in a Jurassic Standard right now.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deathmist+Raptor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor)

 The Raptor does it all: It attacks for a reasonable amount, trades with almost any opposing creature, and keeps on coming back from the dead. Yesterday, I described [various approaches to beating it](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppar15/seven-ways-beat-deathmist-raptor-and-den-protector-2015-05-09). Today, I want to highlight the diversity of [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor) decks.



I counted as many as nine (!) different megamorph archetypes on Sunday. Some, such as Abzan or Bant, are more established; others are more, well, out there. For all of them, I selected an exemplary list and briefly analyzed their key selling points. The archetypes are sorted in descending order of popularity.

**Number of Morph/Manifest Cards:** Fourteen (four each of [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector), [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor), and [Whisperwood Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whisperwood+Elemental) alongside two [Hidden Dragonslayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hidden+Dragonslayer)s).




**Tech for the Megamorph Mirror Match:** Exile their Raptors with [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm).




**Why Pick this Archetype:** [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) is still one of the best cards in Standard, and this deck has already proven itself. Ben Feingersh piloted it to a Top 8 at Grand Prix Toronto last weekend.
