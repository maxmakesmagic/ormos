On an Angel's Wings
===================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on July 26, 2011 










Of all the legendary creatures presented when ***Magic: The Gathering** Commander* was released a few weeks ago, it was an Angel that stole my heart. [Basandra, Battle Seraph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basandra%2C+Battle+Seraph) brought together a mix of colors and ideas in ways that delighted me, and we spent an entire article [showing off some of her potential](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/147).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf153_basandra.jpg)  
Today, I'd like to revisit where we left off, complete a deck list, share some experiences from playing it, and open the floor to making final improvements. It sounds simple, but nothing's easy when dealing with 100-card singleton and a world of "anything can happen" gaming.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Basandra%2C+Battle+Seraph)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basandra%2C+Battle+Seraph)  
I hope you're up for a wild ride.

The entire family of Sword of *Noun* and *Noun* is probably an overused group, but if we're going to play defense I'd like to have the most protection available. Similarly, [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) and [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) provide some heavy lifting, gaining life and otherwise keeping you going a little longer.

[Manriki-Gusari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manriki-Gusari) is the first really interesting Equipment here. I've found that there is already something lying around to be broken, and sometimes you really want to just bust up a Sword. Since this was my first draft, and full of awesome but untested ideas, I thought it would be good to run it through several games to see how things would play out. Instead of a focus on the upside of things, the follow modules look at handling something when it doesn't go quite to plan.

**Situation 1: Power Overwhelming**In many of the games I played, there was a moment where something exceptional entered the battlefield. Sometimes it was a [Darksteel Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus) or [Blightsteel Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightsteel+Colossus). Sometimes it was [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Infinite+Gyre) or [Carnifex Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnifex+Demon). In any case, a creature that overpowers our defenders leaves up very vulnerable to a punishing blow.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Should we change something just to handle these creatures?

  
Since our goal is to get these giant colossi of combat pointed at *other* players, I'm not sure that we would want to try something like [Hallowed Burial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Burial) or [Final Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Final+Judgment). After all, we like our own creatures and need them to stick around too!



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

A common card type that I didn't find exciting for our deck are those descended from [Swords to Plowshares](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares). [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Iona's Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iona%27s+Judgment), and [Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exile) are all ways to keep something ginormous in check without breaking up our defensive plan. If you find that there are just too many big creatures around, these cards can help you push back.

**Situation 2: Boom Goes the Dynamite**Playing big guys and splashy spells isn't everyone's plan when it comes to Commander. Occasionally, decks with [Boom // Bust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boom+%2F%2F+Bust), [Armageddon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon), and [Obliterate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obliterate) hit the big reset button on gaming. Without faster mana sources, our games may end up in a funk.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Should we change something just to handle when the world falls apart?

  
In general, our deck came out of the gates pretty slow. Sometimes this worked to our advantage as other players ignored us while we set out our front line. Quite frequently, thought, it felt like we aren't able to drop haymakers when the others started. We could still do most of what we wanted, but feeling like you're one mana short, and a day late, can be frustrating.

White has a few effects that can let us come tearing out of the gate faster, or at least keep us from falling too far behind everyone. [Land Tax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Tax) and [Weathered Wayfarer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weathered+Wayfarer) both provide some heavy lifting in the land department, and cards like [Tithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe) let us keep our land-get agenda hidden. [Boros Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Signet) and [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) can help us have mana even if all the lands fall away.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

If you feel like your mana needs a little more oomph, those are the kinds of cards to consider.

**Situation 3: As the World Turns** While I'm not the biggest fan of it personally, there are many players out there who like taking extra turns. [Beacon of Tomorrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Tomorrows) is the classic example of this type of card, and [Time Stretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Stretch) is the flagship way to do it. I rarely mind waiting for someone to take an additional turn, but there are several decks out there that will take *every* turn in the game as one, constantly looping [Beacon of Tomorrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Tomorrows) or [Time Stretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Stretch) to their own ends.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Should we change something just to handle those who take all the turns to themselves?

  
Compared to some other decks I've played, ours was able to break up combos reasonably well thanks to [Return to Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Return+to+Dust) and [Into the Core](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Core). The act of exiling something made it very difficult to bring back again, though one tricky green-blue deck had [Riftsweeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftsweeper) for just such a circumstance.

One of the new cards featured in ***Magic: The Gathering** Commander* is [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold), an enchantment built specifically to stop this type of activity. It can be the right type of card if you find too few of the turns taken are yours. Additionally, [Red Elemental Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Red+Elemental+Blast) and [Pyroblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroblast) can put an immediate halt to something blue that's creating the extra turns. I think we're okay in our case.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stranglehold)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold)  
**Situation 4: Night of the Living Dead** Buying back creatures from the graveyard is a common sight in Commander. I included [Breath of Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breath+of+Life) and [False Defeat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=False+Defeat) to join in on that approach. Sometimes, things went a little beyond what I expected. I would see a few spells cast, like [Frantic Search](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frantic+Search) and [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb), then [Living Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death) would hop out to play, flipping the living and dead sides of the battlefield. And that doesn't scratch the surface of graveyard-based hijinks: flashback, [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence), [Genesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis), and many more effects can make the graveyard a very rich place for opponents.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Living+Death)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death)  
Should we change something just to handle those who dig too deeply into the graveyard?

  
As much as it's disappointing to have the world flip ends, I've seen some pretty gnarly results from [Living Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death), and it usually means the game is ending sooner. I guess I have a soft spot for games that go quick.

Many players like to use catch-all anti-graveyard cards, like [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) and [Bojuka Bog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Bog), since a well-stocked graveyard can be a much richer source of strength than a library full of stuff. We didn't include any of these types of cards, but if the graveyard seems to be a place of treasure and terror more than where things go to die then switching in a graveyard reset or two can make sense.
