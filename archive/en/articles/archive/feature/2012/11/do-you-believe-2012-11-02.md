Do You Believe In...?
=====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 2, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods












All week here at DailyMTG.com, we have been paying homage to the white-blue guild formerly (and presently) known as Azorius. So far, Daily Decks has taken a look at white-blue in [Standard](/en/articles/archive/blumans-2012-11-01) as well as [Modern](/en/articles/archive/clique-clique-pull-2012-10-31), but that isn't the only place where white-blue is viable. Legacy has seen its fair share of white-blue decks including anything from [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) brews to more controlling [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) lists with only a handful of win conditions. *Avacyn Restored* introduced a pretty nifty keyword for older formats, though, and miracles is most certainly a solid white-blue option, as Justin Adams can attest to. 

![Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) Miracles is basically an extension of more traditional Counter-Top strategies. The idea behind Counter-Top is simply to establish the combo of [Counterbalance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) and [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top). At this point, you get to counter most things that cost four or less for the simple tapping of a mana. But with Top comes library manipulationâ€”something Legacy already knows a thing or two about. [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm); [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor); and fetchlands already allow for a very solid manipulation of your library, which just plays into miracles even more. Once you have ways of ensuring those six- and seven-mana spells don't clog up your hand, they are pretty powerful! 

 Most of the other technology in WU Miracles is just traditional adaptations from white-blue control over the past two decades. In fact, the spells in the deck are almost a who's who of control cards. [Swords to Plowshares](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) is the best removal spell ever printed. [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) and [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) are both powerful counterspells to help balance out your game against combo. [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) is on the short list for most powerful cards ever printed. And *Return to Ravnica* even makes an appearance with both [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) serving key roles!
