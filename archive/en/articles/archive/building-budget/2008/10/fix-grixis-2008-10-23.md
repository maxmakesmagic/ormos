Fix This With Grixis
====================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on October 23, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






![Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cruel+Ultimatum)Hello and welcome back to Grixis Week here on **magicthegathering.com**. It seems that everyone is talking about five-color "Quick 'n Toast" control decks nowadays. The Grixis-stamped [Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) is as unbelievable as everyone thinks and says. I went and played at my local shop this week, and everyone was playing some concoction with tons of Vivid lands and card advantage. But five-color control decks don't break the format to pieces. The situation is definitely remediable. I was there two hours early, so I decided to brew up a budget deck and see how I fared. Being that it's Grixis Week, I decided to make a Grixis deck focused on beating the five-color control decks that are dominating Standard right now.

The first card that I thought of when deciding the best way to beat the five-color toast variants was [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage). I wasn't 100%, but I figured this card had to be unbelievably powerful in the current Standard metagame. After a little thought I decided a [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) deck would probably be the best home for our flying anti-Toast friend.

I decided to get brewing and thought about how the curve for a deck like this might look. I fiddled around with a few builds and eventually decided to take a look at the [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) deck I [wrote about](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/kelpie-obi-wan-kenobi-2008-10-02) a few weeks ago.

There are a lot of cards I couldn't play with anymore here. I wanted to be playing more [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) to strip the hands of my Toast-wielding opponents. I think [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) needs to be in a deck like this. I need to accrue a lot of card advantage to beat the five-color decks. [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter) seems very strong, and the unearth mechanic works really well with [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie). I decided to draw up a new list. I figured I wouldn't be playing many creatures, so I decided to take a page from the Randy Buehler playbook and design my curve based on what my opponent's plays on any given turn might be.

### The Early Game

**[Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime):** [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) trades one-for-one early on and gives us a way to dump the extra lands we draw late in the game. Once I get my [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) into play I can turn all my lands into cantrip discard spells. If I have two [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) in play, it becomes absurd.

**[Remove Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul):** [Remove Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) is okay against the five-color decks, but really shines against [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark). [Remove Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) is quite good against most decks that people bring to battle in today's standard. It may or may not fit in the maindeck, but it's definitely worth looking at

**[Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate):** [Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) is really good. [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) counters [Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) for two mana. It also counters all the Charms and planeswalkers people have started to sling.

**[Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw):** An excellent removal spell and threat. You pretty much have to play [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) in decks with [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin); the synergy is huge.

### The Midgame

**[Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel):** [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) works very well in a deck like this. We can afford to leave three mana open once we empty our opponent's hands and counter anything interesting they draw off the top. It's also just a very elegant answer to anything our opponent's play that poses a problem. If there aren't many Faeries players in your area you could run [Faerie Trickery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Trickery); it removes your opponent's spell from the game, which can be relevant if they're packing [Demigod of Revenge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge) or [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) targets.

![Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sedraxis+Specter)**[Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter):** When this card first got previewed people were really hyped. Somewhere along the line people weren't as excited anymore. I'm not sure why, though. [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter) is an excellent threat for its cost before you even look at its unearth ability. Even if your opponent has a removal spell, you'll still probably get to dome them for 3 and pluck a card.

**[Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter):** Everyone already knows how good [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) is. The card advantage produced by this creature is really absurd. You can evoke it on your third turn and play [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) on your fourth turn and just put some opponent's in really tough spots. I knew I was playing four [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) in this deck before I knew what shirt I was wearing to the tournament.

**[Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage):** I think this card is really underplayed right now. Standard is dominated by five-color control decks right now, and this really seems to be the best answer available. Sticking a [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) with a blue mana open makes it almost impossible for a five-color player to win.

**[Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin):** I'm absolutely in love with [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) right now. It's one of the best ways to get back in a game that's being dominated by a control player. They have to tap out at some point, and you can use that opportunity to put a threat into play.

### The Endgame

**[River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie):** The card advantage you get off a single [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) is absolutely absurd. It's pretty tough for anybody to beat a single resolved [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie). It's also one of the best [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) targets in Standard. By far the most insane interaction in the deck is [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) with [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime).

I wanted a pretty diverse mana base. I can afford to play ten lands that come into play tapped when I'm running a deck like this. I like [Vivid Marsh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Marsh) more than the other Vivid lands because I can dump [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime)s into it without removing counters. I ended up settling on this mana base.

I wanted to focus my sideboard on beating creature based strategies. I've been looking at [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) a lot, it seems all the aggro decks are siding in [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) and I thought I could probably Jedi mind trick people into giving me a lot of card/tempo advantage on my [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest). I also like [Cruel Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Edict) a lot, I think cards like this are necessary in a format with [Shield of the Oversoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shield+of+the+Oversoul) and [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus). I want to side in [Remove Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) because it's good against the aggressive decks and very good against [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark). I decided to round out my sideboard with some [Agony Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp), the unbelievably powerful removal spell from *Shards of Alara*.

I borrowed the last few cards I needed, and the first round started almost immediately.

### Round 1 vs. Roy playing Five-Color Control

![Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage)**Game 1:** The first game started slow. I won the play and we both kept our opening hands and I played a [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) twice before anything else really got going. I evoked my [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) on turn three, and he did the same on his turn. On my fourth turn I just passed. My opponent passed back to me. I had two [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) in my hand, so I ran out a [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) on the next turn. Roy had the [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) and countered my creature. On his turn he just passed. The game kind of became a boring draw-go for about fifteen minutes after this. Eventually Roy made a [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher) on my end step. I played [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) to bring back my [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie). Roy attacked with his [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher) and put me at 11. Then he tried to play [Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum). I had the [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), and he had to pass the turn. On my turn I used the [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) in my graveyard to make him discard his last three cards and draw three in the process. I drew a [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) in the process and evoked it to kill his [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher). I attacked for three and passed with [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) mana open. The next turn I drew [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) and played it. I also made Roy discard the land he drew. The light at the end of the tunnel didn't really exist anymore for Roy and he scooped up his cards.

**Game 2:** Roy chose to play first, and he mulliganed to five. My hand was [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) and six lands. I kept. By my third turn Roy had no cards in hand, and I was ready to evoke my [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) the next turn. I kept Roy locked with [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) for the rest of the game and was able to swing for 2 at a time with a [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) I played on turn five until Roy died.

1-0

### Round 2 vs. Ashok playing Five-Color Control

**Game 1:** I knew Ashok was playing the same deck as Roy, so I kept [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter), [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime), and five lands on the play. After a first-turn [Crumbling Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis), on my second turn I played [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) twice. Ashok discarded a pair of [Firespout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout)s. On my third turn I played [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter). Ashok looked pretty flustered, as he had just discarded the only two ways he had to deal with it in hand. On his third turn he evoked a [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) and passed. I drew [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) and played a land. I attacked with my [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter), and he discarded a [Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum). I decided to pass the turn and wait to play my [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) until I had five mana so I could activate it the same turn. Ashok tried a [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) on his turn but I had the [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate). I drew another land and decided to make him discard his hand. The [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter) ended up going all the way, and Ashok picked up his sideboard looking for answers.

**Game 2:** Ashok kept his opening seven and I mulliganed this game. I kept an iffy hand and never played a third land... Awkward...

**Game 3:** This game went much better. I resolved an early [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter) again, and I got to hit him once before he [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Wrath%2Bof%2BGod)ed it away. I played [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) on it the same turn and got to beat for 3 damage and another card. On my turn I played [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) with an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) untapped. Ashok struggled for a bit and tapped the top of his deck, saying, "[Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher)!" He didn't draw it, though, so he was forced to just pass the turn back. I beat again and drew a [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime). I made him discard what remained of his hand and passed the turn back. A turn later he drew his [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher), but he was at 2 life and it wasn't much help anymore.

2-0

### Round 3 vs. A.J. playing [Shorecrasher Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shorecrasher+Mimic) / Bant

![Rafiq of the Many](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rafiq+of+the+Many)**Game 1:** I kept my opening seven on the draw. I knew A.J. was playing Bant, and I had two [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) in my opener along with a [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie), a [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), and three lands. The game started quickly. A.J. played a [Shorecrasher Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shorecrasher+Mimic) on his second turn. I drew a [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) and evoked [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw). He played a [Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) on his third turn I evoked [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) and drew two lands. He played a [Rafiq of the Many](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rafiq+of+the+Many) and attacked me for 6 lifelinked damage. I drew a [River Kelpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie), played a fourth land, and used [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) on my [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), killing [Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk). He attacked with [Rafiq of the Many](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rafiq+of+the+Many) and brought me to 8, then played another [Shorecrasher Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shorecrasher+Mimic) and passed. I attacked with my [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) and played my other [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) to kill his Rafiq. He drew his card and passed. I drew another [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) and attacked with both of my creatures before playing it. The game just became a bash and pass match until my opponent drew a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). I had the [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), though. He showed me a hand clogged up with land at the end of the game.

**Game 2:** I knew A.J. had [Shield of the Oversoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shield+of+the+Oversoul) in his deck, and I needed more removal anyway, so I sided in my [Agony Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp)s and [Cruel Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Edict)s. A.J. was on the play and kept his opener. I kept [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), [Sedraxis Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter), [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage), and four lands. A.J. played a [Seaside Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seaside+Citadel) on his first turn and passed. I played a land and passed.

A.J. drew his card and said, "Go."

"You forgot to play a land."

"I don't have one."

"Ugh, sorry buddy."

A.J. made his second land drop on his fourth turn. By then it was too late. Every threat he played I had an answer for, and he couldn't interact with the board because I had a [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) online. I don't think this was a very good match, but I'm happy it ended the way it did.

I made it to the finals! True, it was a ten-person tournament and eight of the ten players were playing Five-Color Control.

### Round 4 vs. Greg playing Five-Color Control

The previous two rounds had taken a long time with everyone playing Five-Color Control, and both Greg and I had to wake up early for work. We decided to draw and get some pizza.

After playing with the deck a bit more I think I'd rather have the [Remove Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) in the main deck instead of [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate). I'd also like to have a few [Guttural Responses](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttural+Responses) in my sideboard, because it's incredible against almost every deck in the format. If I had to run the deck again, it would look like this.

If I were going to make a non-budget version of this deck, I'd probably just get some [Reflecting Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool)s and [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command)s.

I had a blast playing this deck, and I think it's a great deck choice if a lot of people in your area are playing Five-Color Control. This deck accrues a lot of card advantage and keeps slow decks out of the game once things get rolling.

I'm leaving for [Pro Tour–Berlin](/en/node/640191) this weekend, and I'm really excited. Wish me luck and send me any comments or suggestions you might have.
