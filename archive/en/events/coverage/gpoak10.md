Grand Prix Oakland 2010
-----------------------


Nass Steamrolls Oakland in Dynamic Debut
========================================




February 13-14, 2010











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/finalBlurb.jpg)*Matthew Nass*
Grand Prix–Oakland has come to a close, and a player making his Pro Tour debut next week has come to the party ahead of time. Eighteen year old Matthew Nass, a California resident, simply stormed his way through two days of competition. He was the last player in the entire 769-strong field to taste defeat, going all the way to 13-0. Sometimes, that heralds a collapse, but not this time. Already with a Top 8 berth secured, he first navigated his way past Petr Brozek of the Czech Republic, piloting a fantastic Boros deck that has been the talk of the town here.


In the semis, Nass faced another fearsome challenge in Conley Woods, who again had deck tech to savor. Awaiting him in the final was Adam Yurchick, ready to battle Elves with his trusty [Sword of the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+the+Meek), [Thopter Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Foundry), and the mighty [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths). But it was Nass who made the most of his first visit to the Top 8. Now he heads out of town as the leader in the Player of the Year Race, the leader in the Rookie of the Year Race. He is, in fact, King of **Magic**  – at least for seven days.


Join us just five days from now for the first Pro Tour of the 2010 Season, as the California carnage continues. Roll on San Diego!

Quarterfinal: Depths of Despair? - Adam Yurchick vs. Tomoharu Saito
-------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon*

Earlier in the day we saw Tomoharu Saito in action against Gabe Walls. On that occasion, the Japanese [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) deck simply destroyed Walls and his [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) deck. Now Adam Yurchick must seek to overturn that result with his version of Thopter-Depths. The trouble, of course, is that [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) itself is so hideously one-sided. Still, there’s a reason they play the games rather than just work out who’s favorite, so let’s see if the American can sneak the victory.


Game 1 – [Gemstone Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemstone+Mine) began for Saito, and Yurchick used [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) to power out [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). He found waiting for him: [Demonic Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Dread), [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon), [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge), [Angel of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair), and [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide). It was the mana accelerator that hit the graveyard.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/qfSaito.jpg)[Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) was next for Yurchick, who ran out [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) before adding [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) to the board. Saito used [Demonic Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Dread) to Cascade into [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis), and promptly put [Angel of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair) into play. Yurchick passed, and Saito added [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) to the board. This time, Yurchick took advantage of the [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) to land [Thopter Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Foundry), but Saito also had [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) ready to bring in. With both players having passed, Saito now had to choose his targets for the Angel and The O Ring, which sounds like a daytime TV movie. The Angel targeted [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) targeted Saito’s own [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), and Yurchick’s [Thopter Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Foundry) and [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp). Yurchick got a pair of 3/3s out of the deal, and another pair when the [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) came back again, allowing Saito to completely wipe Yurchick’s board, apart from those four Elephants.


How is it that giving someone twelve power and toughness for free can mean they can’t possibly win? It’s a strange world, Extended. Saito piled in with a huge attack that left Yurchick with minimal hope, and that became no hope moments later.


Tomoharu Saito 1, Adam Yurchick 0.


Yurchick was quick to keep for Game 2, and turn one [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) found [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge), [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide), [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite), [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite), and three land in Saito’s hand. Once again, Yurchick took the mana producer away.


Out came [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) for Yurchick, and he added [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths), although still with no way to turn it on at rapid speed. Saito began the laborious task of charging his many lands, while Yurchick used the end of turn to cast [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge), pitching [Thopter Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Foundry).


In came [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) once again, before Yurchick imprinted [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) and cast [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge). This time, it was [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) that hit the graveyard. [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) took away [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) for the third time in the match, leaving Saito a hand packed with enormous men, namely [Angel of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair), double [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite), [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon), and [Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus). Now that’s a lot of power and toughness in one hand.


For all his digging, Yurchick didn’t seem to be finding what he needed. [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) number three saw him pitch [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths). He Transmuted Tolaria West for [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void), laid it at zero, and passed. Saito had nothing, and Yurchick went back to work with [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) in the red zone. [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) took [Ardent Plea](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ardent+Plea), and now Yurchick sent double [Vampire Hexmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) into play. Saito could see that Marit Lage was on the way, and he scooped to send a surprisingly tense match into a decider.


Tomoharu Saito 1, Adam Yurchick 1.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/qfYurchick.jpg)On the play, Saito kept his opening seven, increasing the pressure on Yurchick, who took a few moments before going in search of a better six. Yurchick had [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) on turn one, and faced [Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus), [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite), [Ardent Plea](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ardent+Plea), [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), and two lands. Unsurprisingly, it was [Ardent Plea](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ardent+Plea) that vanished. Another [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) revealed Saito’s hand had not improved. Away went [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), and the early game clearly belonged to the American.


A third land from the top allowed Yurchick to cast [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena), the Apocalypse card drawer par excellence. Still Saito sat doing nothing other than laying land. Yurchick Transmuted [Tolaria West](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolaria+West+)  for [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void), cast it for zero, and Saito simply charged his lands. At five mana, Saito finally had something to play, casting [Ingot Chewer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ingot+Chewer) to kill the [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void). Down came [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox), but without imprinting, since Yurchick was ready to [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) it. That in turn drew him deeper, and now he could cast [Vampire Hexmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage), with [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) an eager recipient of a future sacrifice.


It looked as if Saito needed a Cascade spell very badly, but he passed without an offering. Yurchick sacrificed the Hexmage, and now the 20/20 was on the battlefield. [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) caused Saito to respond with [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite), and there was no shortage of targets, since Saito had been gifting Yurchick tokens via [Forbidden Orchard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Orchard). When [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) resolved, [Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus) hit the bin, leaving just [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) in the Japanese hand.


Inevitably, the Hellkite blocked Marit Lage, but it was surely a matter of time before we were done. This was no Gabe Walls re-run, and Adam Yurchick advanced to the semifinals, still in the hunt to be the first leader in the Player of the Year Race.


Tomoharu Saito 1, Adam Yurchick 2.




Quarterfinal - Conley Woods vs. Joby Parrish
--------------------------------------------


*by Mat Marr*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/qfWoods.jpg)*Conley Woods*
Conley Woods continued to take his innovative decks far into pro play facing Top 8 newcomer Joby Parrish in the quarterfinals. Conley’s Extended Bant deck featuring the new Jace would face down Joby’s Zoo with [Bant Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm)s.


The players were friendly before the match with Conley wondering how long it took Joby to get here. A 14-hour drive from Idaho and 15 rounds of Magic including a perfect 9-0 start behind him Joby was much quieter than Conley.


Game 1 started with Joby mulliganing once to Colney’s full hand. Conley’s [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) on the play was matched by Joby’s first turn [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl).


Conley passed his second turn with mana open for either the [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and Path or the [Bant Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) in his hand.


Having reviewed each other’s decklist before the game Joby knew the possibilities when as he said "attempt to cast Pridemage" without ever setting the card onto the battlefield.


Mana Leak was indeed there and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) dealt with the Nacatl leaving the zoo player with an empty board and having done no damage after two turns.


Conley became the aggressor with two [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)s teaming up to attack for two. [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), which Conley’s teammate Brad Nelson is calling the best creature in multiple formats, tried to get the beats going again for Joby. [Bant Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) would however send it to the bottom of the library before it could find a land or attack for the Zoo player.


"17 that’s the highest I have ever been against Zoo at this point" Conley said, commenting on the lack of progress of the aggressive deck going into the fourth turn of play.


Tolaria West was transmuted and Conley debated between on turning it into a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) or an [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives). Conley settled on the Explosives but declined to play it, instead ruining Joby’s fetch land with an Aven Mindsensor at the end of his turn own turn.


The explosives would not turn out to be very potent as Nacatl, Pridemage and Knight impressively rebuilt the Zoo board.


Life totals stood at 8 for Joby 10 for Conley after an attack with Knight leaving a board of three creatures each, two Hierarchs for Conley to go with the Mindcensor. Mindcensor was attacking for four a turn thanks to exalted, meaning Zoo needed to find an out with his next draw step.


When Joby attacked again Conley took the safe route and blocked with Hierarch. This took a turn off his clock but meant he was not dead to a burn spell.


Conley attacked Joby to one and just needed to survive the turn and attack again to win. He lost his last heiarch to stay alive but his lethal attack back with the Mindcensor met a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


Joby attacked through Conley’s empty board for the win.


Parrish 1, Woods 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/qfJoby.jpg)*Joby Parrish*
"I made three of four mistakes, Top 8 jitters right?"Joby said. But the first game was still a win in his column.


A semi-local crowd of Northwest supporters celebrated the win in the background. The Idaho team deckbuilders have been a mainstay at West Coast Grand Prix events for years and this is a banner day for all of them.


Conley felt like he just needed more threats to win that game. Joby said "that’s why we did not put hierarch in this deck" it just does not attack well," I don’t understand why people play hierarch in Zoo."


Conley started Game 2 with a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) to Joby’s Nacatl.


When his pen flew into pieces Conley wanted his "joke pen" in the coverage but numerous members of the crowd were too quick to provide one for the moment to be worth mention here.


When Joby did not follow up on his first play Conley commented "So you have the one one-drop no two-drop hand, so you just have infinite removal" Joby replied only with "Maaaaybe."


That removal turned out to be there as first Jace then [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique+)  left play to Lighting Helix then [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile).


A second Vendilion revealed a strong hand for Joby of [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), Nacatl, [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf+)  and a [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile+)  that headed to the bottom.


A [Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) showed why Conley wanted the path gone. Stoneforge Mystic to grab [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) drew a sarcastic "your one copy" from Joby. Conley said "that’s why it's in here."


This game Joby was well ahead on life and the Ranger would put him ahead on creatures. However the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) threatened to change all that.


"So you have three [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl)s in your hand," Conley said. He continued, "If you put all three [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl)s into play at the same time you can beat anything but a royal flush." Joby wisely did not take the bait, "A royal flush named [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives)."


All three creatures jumped in front of the [Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) which took Conley back to 17. With a path and a jitte counter all three creatures were finished off by the one attacker.


Now the question was if Conley’s Mystic and Hierarch would be able to punch through Joby’s superiors creatures thanks to the equipment with only one counter on it. A goyf and two Nacatls seemed to indicate that jitte might not be enough.


Conley waited until after attacking to pop an [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) to get exalted. Joby declined to risk his Goyf in combat even though it would have killed Conley’s last creature.


A [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique+)  revealed [Loam Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Lion) and Nacatl. Neither provided a direct answer to the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) but Conley sent the bigger cat away regardless. Meanwhile Conley waited patiently for Nass to find a [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) that would let him finish off the game.


"If this was MTGO I would have won by now," joked Conley. The deck has so many triggers that are impossible to shortcut on **Magic Online**  that the deck is not heavily played in online events. Conley scooped when Nass finally found the Command with one card left in his library.


Game 2


"I built my deck for the Top 4 archtypes and not Elves," said Conley as he looked through his sideboard for something good in the matchup. "I made the last cards in my sideboard [Tormod’s Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%E2%80%99s+Crypt+)  instead of Chalice. I have not played the Crypts once. If I can win this and the [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End) deck wins maybe I will finally get a chance to use them."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/sfNass.jpg)"This is a keeper," announced Conley as he looked at his hand and he led with [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). "This is so much better on the play."


"Better than turn four on the draw?" laughed Nass as he sacrificed a fetch land.


"Are you taking three?"


"My deck never takes three."


"You have a [Temple Garden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden)."


"Did you see that?"


"Yeah, you played it after I countered the Witness which made me sad."


Conley played [Samurai of the Pale Curtain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Samurai+of+the+Pale+Curtain) on turn two and was able to attack for three a turn while picking off creatures as needed when Nass attempted to equip them with Jitte.


"I have gotten to do more this game than all of last game," grinned Conley as he aimed [Bant Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) at a [Cloudstone Curio](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio). He was able to keep Nass from ever getting the Jitte online and eventually finished him off with the Samurai and a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village).


Game 3


"I’ll keep," said Nass emphatically.


"Son of a..." grumbled Conley.


"This deck never mulligans," shrugged Nass sheepishly.


"Do you have a turn two win?" asked Conley as he contemplated his opening hand.


"No..."


"You are lying to me but I will run with it."


[Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) was an innocent enough of a start while Conley took three to make [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). He passed the turn warily.


"Okay here is the magical turn. If you win you get banned for lying."


Nass laughed and played [Essence Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden) and [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte).


"Jitte!?!" yelped Woods. "I don’t like where this one is going."


Conley transmuted [Tolaria West](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolaria+West) and Nass asked: "What are you getting?"


"I thought about [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village)..." teased Conley but he showed [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives).


Nass suited up one of his guys and killed the Hierarch.


The transmuted land was painful for Conley who did not have a third land and had now lost the [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch).


"Need a good card. Didn't get one," he sighed before brightening at the similar number of lands in play on both sides. "Wait did you miss a land drop too?"


"Everyone is missing land drops," said Nass.


Land continued to elude Conley and he had to pop the Explosives to kill [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and [Essence Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden). Nass restocked with [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel). Woods was still scuffling and played another Explosives -- this one set on 2. Nass attacked with the [Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel) and then played [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) to cycle [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) into [Verdant Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs). Woods popped the the Explosives to deal with the Jitte. Nass was unfazed and attacked with Nettle Sentinel before playing another Jitte.


Conley found a land and was paralyzed by his options. "Oh man. I don’t know what to do now."


Nass had enough mana to set Conley back the land he had just found by using [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) to [Fallow Earth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallow+Earth) a land and search. Conley played [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor) in response and crossed his fingers: "Was it there?"


"This was here," said Nass, showing him [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid). "Ship."


"No need for cussing."


Nass used another Glimpse on his next turn and played [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid). In response Conley used Path on the [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel). Nass picked up his deck to look for the land but he got a warning for looking at extra cards and had to shuffle and look at top four cards for his land due to the Mindcensor. He missed. Later in the turn he played [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) and whiffed again.


"Its like a big ol’ stop sign," laughed Conley who untapped to legend slay the Jitte with his own copy. Nass was content to play small ball with his Glimpses -- much like LSV in the finals of Berlin -- and put a few guys into play and found another Jitte which he played and equipped to the Ranger. It traded with the [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor). Nass was able to send in his small army of guys a couple of times and eventually finished Conley off by moving his Jitte around to two different creatures and pumping them both.


Final result: Matt Nass defeated Conley Woods two games to one to advance to the Finals against U.S. National team member Adam Yurchick.




The Final: Combo/Elves. But Which One? - Matthew Nass vs. Adam Yurchick
-----------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/finalsYurchick.jpg)*Adam Yurchick*
With 767 players vanquished, only two remained. For eighteen year old Matt Nass, this was a taste of the big time seven days earlier than he might have anticipated, since Pro Tour-San Diego next week will mark his debut at the big show. Playing Elves, he was the last man standing this weekend, getting to 13-0 before tasting defeat, at a point when he was already locked for the Top 8. Now Level 4 Pro Adam Yurchick stands in his way, with two Grand Prix Top 8s behind him, including a final appearance at Grand Prix-Philadelphia in 2008.


Nass had the ideal [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) turn one, while Yurchick, who mulliganed to six on the play, could only muster [Dimir Aqueduct](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Aqueduct) on his second turn. Turn two for Nass was the tasty [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid). [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) and a second [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) followed for Nass, who attacked for three. Yurchick ended the turn with [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge), pitching [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) and [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant).


[Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) showed him [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary), two [Boreal Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boreal+Druid), and a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel), none of which he could touch with the one-mana Sorcery, but at least he now had perfect information with which to sculpt his next plays. He elected to Transmute [Muddle the Mixture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture) into [Vampire Hexmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage), and, given that he'd pitched [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) earlier in the game, seemed likely to have the Coldsnap land in hand.


[Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) from the top of the deck signalled Nass' intention to go off, and it wasn't long before a veritable torrent of Elves was hitting play, with [Cloudstone Curio](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio) soon along for the ride. "3...2...5..2..3..1..5...4..3...6...5...7...2.." Well, alright, I'm making some of those numbers up, but it's a good reflection of the monologue spoken by Nass, as his mana ebbed and flowed, periodically dropping by five for [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command), rendering all possible resistance futile, as someone once famously said.


Matt Nass 1, Adam Yurchick 0.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/finalsNass.jpg)*Matthew Nass*
For a change, Yurchick felt able to keep his opening seven, while Nass had an easy decision to send his opener back to the shuffle. After a land from Yurchick, [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) opened for Nass. [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) was turn two for Yurchick, as it had been so often in this Top 8. [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) was up next for Nass, who seemed to be holding himself together, despite the pressure of the finish line looming.


Yurchick semi-reluctantly laid [Sword of the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+the+Meek) before passing, leaving Nass to cast [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid). Out came: [Essence Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden), [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary), and [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) (fetching [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel)), before Nass passed back to Yurchick, who was now looking in serious trouble. He had a full grip of seven cards to be sure, but when he cast [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge), he once again opened the door to Nass. Could he take full advantage?


Evefrything attacked, but perhaps the most crucial part of the turn was the [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) he laid post-combat, providing [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) defense. [Tolaria West](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolaria+West+)  Transmuted for [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives), and Nass looked concerned. [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) was Imprinted with [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal), allowing Yurchick to cast the Explosives for one.


Boom!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpoak10/finalsNassYurchick.jpg)Now just two [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) remained in play for Nass, but that would quickly change. [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) came out for Nass, and he had mana for [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) too, fetching a pair of [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel)s. Now it looked as if Yurchick's [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) had been just a temporary reprieve. He laid another Explosives, again set to one, but this time left it ticking. [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) untapped the [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel), and Nass sent everything into the red zone. Yurchick fell from 13 to just 4 at a stroke, and it seemed as if Nass was on the verge of a famous triumph.


Yurchick had no choice but to blow the Explosives at end of turn, which may have bought him some time, but it was clear that the [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) across the table was a serious roadblock to his ambitions of forcing Game 3. [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) from the top seemed unlikely to change anything, so he went to plan B of casting [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research). Was this the last turn of the final? He Transmuted [Muddle the Mixture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture), fetching out [Vampire Hexmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage), and used a newly Imprinted [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) to allow him to play it, also adding [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) to the board.


The two Elvish Visionaries attacked Yurchick down to just one precious life, thanks to [Pendelhaven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven). Now the proverbial fat lady was getting rather loud. Yurchick untapped, with only the top of his deck between him and the end of the road. He drew, but there was no help. By two games to none, the dominant player of the weekend refused to be derailed in the Top 8.


Matt Nass, the last man standing, is the 2010 Grand Prix-Oakland champion!
