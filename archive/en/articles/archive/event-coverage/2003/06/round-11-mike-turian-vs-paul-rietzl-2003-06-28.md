Round 11: Mike Turian vs. Paul Rietzl
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 28, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New York–based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a777.jpg)With a concession by Brian Kibler to Jon Finkel, this year's Top 8 is shaping up to be one of the best Top 8s in the history of Magic. Already locked in were Neil Reeves, Brian Kibler and—pending a draw—Finkel. If Mike Turian won this match he would join this historic group. 

No great Top 8 would be complete without the Cinderella story—and the hometown favorite but that's a fairy tale for another feature match. Paul Rietzl flew here from Boston hoping to grind in. He came within a match of accomplishing it in the first limited grinder but was defeated in the final round. Impossibly, he dashed across the hall to sign up for the final grinder and managed to go 6-0 with his blue-green madness deck. 

Before he even played a match at Nationals he was already 11-1 on the weekend. Coming into this match he was 19-3 on the weekend with his only Nationals losses coming at the hands of Brian Kibler. "My breakers are 6% higher than anyone else's!"

The winner here can likely draw in while the loser must endure a do or die match not unlike the two Paul has already played this weekend just to get to round one.

### Game 1

Turian opened up with a [Wooded Foothills](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills) into a [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer). Paul had the [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) and discarded [Roar of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) and an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). Turian pecked for a point and played another of the red wizards. [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) was the turn two play but [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) killed it. [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) could only stare idly as an [Elephant Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide)d wizard lumbered by. On the fourth turn Paul was able to do something that had eluded him all tournament—he flashed back a [Roar of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a779.jpg)Turian continued to send in the beats and Paul thought long and hard about whether or not to block and ultimately he opted not to and fell to nine. Mike fortified with more 1/1s—a Llanowar Elf and a Rootwalla. Mike only had three lands—two were [Mossfire Valley](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mossfire+Valley)s and he was tapped out to make his men. Paul took advantage and swung in his [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) and made an [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm). The Wurm 'traded' with the enchanted creature and Mike got a 3/3 token. When Mike also tried a [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) it was [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic)-ed through the [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba), giving Turian a window to kill it with a Lavamancer and he took advantage of it.

Paul cast [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) looking for answers but found only Looters that could not hold the ground against Turian's mounting army.

Turian – 1 Rietzl – 0

Paul replayed the game in his head while they sideboarded, "Maybe I should have blocked with my Wurm..."

Turian disagreed, "I thought I was in a bad position when you didn't block. If you do block I'm sure the game is mine. You just drew a lot of land and Looters."

Rietzl shrugged, "Blue-green..."

### Game 2

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a778.jpg)Paul opened with a careful Study but could only shake his head sadly and discard a [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) and a [Turbulent Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turbulent+Dreams)—hardly the textbook turn one for blue-green. Mike had the [Wooded Foothills](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills) into [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) again, which did little to improve Paul's darkening mood. [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) was the turn two play and Mike had an elf and [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) number two.

Paul had nothing going on and his next play was a fifth turn Rootwalla. Mike [Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer)ed the Aquadog and sent in the lizard. After a brief skirmish Paul unsummoned his fighter and replayed him next turn.

While Paul was doing nothing, Turian was sitting on [Threaten](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threaten). When Paul finally made two [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel)s that were dug up with a [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study)--"A little late to the party boys!"—Turian was able to finish him off with [Violent Eruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Eruption) and by threatening a [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel).

**Final Result:** Mike – 2 Paul – 0

The (arguably) best Top 8 ever got a lot better now that Mike could draw in. If Paul hoped to wear a glass slipper to the party he would need to win his final round.
