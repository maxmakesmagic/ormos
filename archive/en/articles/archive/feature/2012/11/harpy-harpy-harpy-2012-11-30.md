Harpy, Harpy, Harpy
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 30, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods












When I first heard about Legacy, I was introduced to the format by hearing about a lot of the cooler combo decks available. Goblins was mentioned to me, as was Merfolk, but the exciting decks were those using [Goblin Charbelcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher) or [High Tide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Tide) to create crazy, unbeatable turns. One of the combos mentioned to me was one using [Cavern Harpy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy). The idea was to play the [Cavern Harpy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) for free and then bounce it with its own ability a bunch of times, replaying it for free over and over and generating some type of crazy advantage. Whether you won with a [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) or a [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) really didn't matter, but the combo seemed awesome. 


![Cavern Harpy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cavern+Harpy)
 How can you possibly cast the same creature over and over again for free, though? That answer comes in the form of [Aluren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren). Traditionally, [Aluren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren) used [Cavern Harpy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) as its primary creature to play and replay, but as more cards have been printed, there are a lot of new choices these days. Martin Goldman-Kirs decided to use [Dream Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Stalker) instead of just [Cavern Harpy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy), which is nice because it provides a creature that does not die to [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). But, while you now have infinite bounce and replaying of creatures, you still need a way to win. 


 Enter [Imperial Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperial+Recruiter). While the Recruiter offers no essential function to the deck, he does allow the deck to reach an entirely different level of consistency. With four [Imperial Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperial+Recruiter)s, you can more easily find either of your bounce pieces. From here, you proceed to draw your deck using [Coiling Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle) plus [Cavern Harpy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy). At some point, you will draw another Recruiter or [Parasitic Strix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parasitic+Strix). From here, you continue to bounce your Strix over and over again with Harpy, draining your opponent for 2 with each iteration. Your fist full of countermagic helps ensure that victory is inevitable!
