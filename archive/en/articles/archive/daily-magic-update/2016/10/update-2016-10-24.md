What People Are Talking About
=============================


1. [A Rebuttal: The Lifestyle of the Professional *Magic: The Gathering* Player](https://www.reddit.com/r/magicTCG/comments/58slc5/a_rebuttal_the_lifestyle_of_the_professional/?st=IUL44XRL&sh=06948d30) | Gerry Thompson

Gerry Thompson wrote a moving, honest assessment of his life as a Professional *Magic* player. Touching, relatable for many, and worth a read for anyone.


1. > 
> Reach. [#gppvd](https://twitter.com/hashtag/gppvd?src=hash)
> 
> 
>  — eric froehlich (@efropoker) [October 23, 2016](https://twitter.com/efropoker/status/790332099458760704)

There were a number of tweets like this over the weekend, but this one hits two fronts, so we'll use it to talk about both.


First, there was a discussion about what the term "reach" means in *Magic* when discussing decks having reach (and not talking about the keyword ability that lets you block fliers). There wasn't a consensus, but the options came down to, essentially, "direct damage" and "a plan for the late game." Use as you see fit.


The particular reach Eric Froehlich is talking about here is the ending to [the finals of Grand Prix Providence](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppro/finals-seth-manfield-vs-yichen-wang-2016-10-23). There, Wang Yichen defeated No. 3-ranked Seth Manfield with the activated ability of [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) to deal the final 6 points of damage.


Oh, and Ishkanah just happens to have literal reach as well, for more confusion.
