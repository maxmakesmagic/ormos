Another Year Older, Part I
==========================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on July 16, 2009 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_m10Candle.jpg)Hey there, gang, and welcome back to the lab! We've got a great column for you this week, simply because it contains the first half of the long-awaited results of the Birthday Contest! Yes, we're going to ignore all the neat recent developments in **Magic** for the time being (***Magic** 2010*, the rules changes, and whatnot) and focus on a crazy Johnny contest that I [kicked off](/en/articles/archive/lab/birthday-presence-2009-06-04) about a month ago.

For newcomers to this contest or for those who like refreshments, the Birthday Contest was a typically restrictive but casual contest in which each contestant had to build a 60-card deck containing, at minimum, one copy of each **Magic** card that fell on their respective birthday in the [Card of the Day archive](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?page=0&tag=cardoftheday&description=Card%20of%20the%20Day).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_calendar.jpg)  
I suspected that whittling the submissions down would be tough, but I had no idea how much turmoil I was about to go through. Suffice it to say that I read every of the 238 emails, and sympathized with each lovely deck. My first pass through this file was a brutal one, leaving me with 64 decks (eliminating nearly 75%!). My second pass left me with 49, and my third 26. This last part was agonizing, as I was forced to let 10 more decks go to get a magic number of 16.

I guess now is as good a time as any to say, thanks to everyone for sending and participating. Although your deck may not have made the Sweet 16, I read your submission as fully as the others. The top 16 decks were, in my eyes, the best of the best, the ones that provide a diverse series of articles and creativity. Thank you all.

Okay, on to the show! Throughout this two-part series (eight decks per part), I'll be going through a delayed Top 10 countdown, with six other decks sprinkled throughout.

Brendon was lucky enough to have both [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare) and [Gilt-Leaf Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilt-Leaf+Archdruid). The plan is either to beat with [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare) or take all the lands with the Archdruid (which helps [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare).) [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) makes every land a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), and it allows you to readily cast [Ghost Council of Orzhova](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Council+of+Orzhova). With all the little Druids as fodder, [Ghost Council of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ghost%2BCouncil%2Bof%2BOrzhova) should hurt your opponent plenty. [Mirror Gallery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Gallery) wants to enable multiple [Azusa, Lost but Seeking](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azusa%2C+Lost+but+Seeking)s, to accelerate your land count. Finally, [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) is a problem-stopper, as is [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), which can trick your opponent into upping his land count for later Druid theft.

This next deck is also a good example of a contest deck, but whereas Brendon's deck was easy to grasp, Ryan Sextro's May 14 deck was wonderfully complicated.

**Cards:** [Martial Coup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup), [Dross Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Scorpion), [Arcanum Wings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcanum+Wings), [Sun Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Clasp), [Wirewood Hivemaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Hivemaster), [Opalescence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence)

Here are Ryan's card explanations:

![Opalescence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Opalescence)
> 
> **[Opalescence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence):** Core of the deck: he enables your other enchantments to win the game for you and will become a nifty, permanent [Armageddon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon) with [Enchanted Evening](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enchanted+Evening) in play.
> 
> 
> **[Sterling Grove](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sterling+Grove):** The enabler. This makes finding combo pieces easy.
> 
> 
> **[Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy):** Side combo with [Wirewood Hivemaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Hivemaster). Name Elf with this bad boy and watch the insects flow!
> 
> 
> **[Dispersing Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispersing+Orb):** The control. If you have spare insects, soldiers or [Academy Rector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector)s around, sac 'em and bounce your opponents' chances of winning.
> 
> 
> **[Enchanted Evening](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enchanted+Evening):** Hilarious with [Opalesence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalesence), [Yavimaya Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Enchantress), or [Ancestral Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask).
> 
> 
> **[Arcanum Wings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcanum+Wings):** The tricky side finisher with [Ancestral Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask). Picture this board: You control Opalesence, [Enchanted Evening](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enchanted+Evening), [Gilded Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus), [Dispersing Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispersing+Orb) and [Arcanum Wings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcanum+Wings) strapped to your [Dross Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Scorpion). The lands are obviously gone. You swing with the flying scorpion, your opponent doesn't block. You tap your [Black Lotus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Black%2BLotus) for blue, aura swap for [Ancestral Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask) and hit for 15!
> 
> 
> **[Sun Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Clasp):** Emergency help for dying important creatures. Can be aura swapped in a pinch and can save an otherwise dead creature such as the very important [Yavimaya Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Enchantress)
> 
> 
> **[Ancestral Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask):** Funny, just.... funny. Everything in this deck is centered on enchantments.
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_scorpion.jpg)
> 
> **[Dross Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Scorpion):** Seemingly out of place, but if this guy dies with [Gilded Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus) or [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm) in play, you get major mana or major recursion, you pick!
> 
> 
> **[Wirewood Hivemaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Hivemaster):** Very useful defense with [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy). Especially useful with [Dispersing Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispersing+Orb).
> 
> 
> **[Academy Rector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector):** See [Sterling Grove](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sterling+Grove). Another enabler that makes the combo work.
> 
> 
> **[Yavimaya Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Enchantress):** The beater. She hits hard in this deck. HARD!
> 
> 
> **[Gilded Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus):** Mana, straight up. Doesn't die to Opalesence + EE and is the perfect amount of mana for an aura swap.
> 
> 
> **[Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm):** Recursion. Very good for picking up lost [Sun Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Clasp)s, [Enchanted Evening](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enchanted+Evening)s, or [Dispersing Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispersing+Orb)s that have died to the odd [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) or the like.
> 
> 
> **[Martial Coup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup):** Your defense against creature beats. Can be used in a pinch for blockers or Orb bait.
>

> ![Ashnod's Transmogrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ashnod%27s+Transmogrant)**[Ashnod's Transmogrant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashnod%27s+Transmogrant):** This card is probably the best find I came up with, for this card pool. It changes both your own creatures and those of your opponent into artifacts so they can be destroyed by [Divine Offering](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering). It gives nonwhite 2-power creatures an opponent controls +1/+1 so that they will not untap due to [Crackdown](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackdown). And it changes our [Auriok Salvagers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Salvagers) and our Angel tokens into artifacts (and if need be our 6/1 Spirit [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) too) so that they can receive the Ravager's +1/+1 counters and can still untap if you have a [Crackdown](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackdown) in play (since the Ravager might not be able to do this due to its size). And as mentioned before it can simply be retrieved by the [Auriok Salvagers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Salvagers)' ability.

> THE SCENE
> 
> 
> Our protagonists are the humans, drawn to Antarctica by a satellite image of a heat signature, miles below a glacier. A fantastically rich businessman uses his wealth to convince a team of experts to accompany him to investigate the site, where they discover a huge ancient temple. Excited by their find, they venture into the temple and right into the center of an alien war that's been raging for millennia! Now surrounded by unimaginable horrors, their only hope of survival is to team up with the aliens and reduce the opponent's life total to zero! The fate of the earth depends on it!
> 
> 
> THE CAST
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_humansCross.jpg)  

> 
> THE HUMANS  
> **[Gwafa Hazid, Profiteer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gwafa+Hazid%2C+Profiteer) x1:** Charles Weyland, our industrial billionaire with the funds to get anything he wants.  
> **[Battletide Alchemist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battletide+Alchemist) x1:** Alexa Woods, the expedition guide, puts the safety of her team above all else.  
> **[Colos Yearling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colos+Yearling) x4:** Our assorted side cast of humans, none very important as they're only there to get killed for the sake of advancing the story, like a good little red-shirt (see *Star Trek*). But what's an Alien movie if the people aren't armed with flamethrowers!
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_PredatorCross.jpg)  

> 
> THE PREDATOR AND HIS AWESOME GEAR  
> **[Lord of the Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Pit) x1:** The B.A. Mofo himself. Nobody else in this movie is a match to him aside from the Queen. All he really cares about is the hunt. It makes no difference to him whether he's killing aliens, humans or his controller; as long as he's dealing 7 damage to something, he's happy.  
> **[Whispersilk Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak) x1:** He can turn himself invisible!! Sort of makes him hard to block or shoot at!  
> **[Leonin Bola](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Bola) x1:** Trapping the enemy is a standard part of his arsenal.  
> **[Viridian Longbow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow) x1:** What would our favorite stalker be without his shoulder cannon?  
> **[Lightning Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Blast) x4:** Sometimes you need a REALLY big blast from that shoulder cannon.  
> **[Nevinyrral's Disk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk) x1:** If all else fails and he's going to die, what does he do? That's right; activate the timer and BLOW EVERYTHING UP!
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_aliensCross.jpg)  

> 
> THE ALIENS  
> **[Thought Nibbler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Nibbler) x4:** The face huggers that come out of the infamous eggs. Small, quick, and hard to avoid. (More chosen for artwork than mechanics! This art always made me think of the Alien movies.)  
> **[Mistform Ultimus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) x4:** These aliens don't have their own specific form, they take attributes from whatever species they're birthed from.  
> **[Heart Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+Sliver) x4:** They're blindingly fast.  
> **[Talon Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talon+Sliver) x4:** They're almost always going to attack before you even know they're there.  
> **[Acidic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Sliver) x4:** And even if you kill them, their acid blood will make short work of you.  
> **[Sliver Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Queen) x1:** Add to it all their gigantic mother that spits out more like it's her job! And she's the only other creature in this fiasco that can face down the Predator.
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_sandsOfTime.jpg)  

> 
> THE PLOT DEVICE
> 
> 
> **[Sands of Time](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sands+of+Time) x1:** This temple is not static! It keeps fluctuating, walls move, stairways close off, doors open. Sometimes our characters get locked in until it is time for the temple to shift again. This keeps everyone on his or her toes and makes it all unpredictable.
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl47_settingCross.jpg)

> THE SETTING
> 
> 
> **[Archaeological Dig](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeological+Dig) x1:** This is the whole purpose for heading to the glacier to begin with.  
> **[Ancient Ziggurat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Ziggurat) x4:** The temple. Also helps to cast cards. This is a bizarre five-color mishmash. Fortunately, the deck is almost entirely made of creatures, so it can be used for almost everything.  
> **[Reflecting Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool) x4:** Again, five-color mishmash.
