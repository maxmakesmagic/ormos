Quarterfinals Stage 1: Joey Manner (White-Blue Flash) vs. Carlos Romao (Jeskai Control)
=======================================================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 16, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a member of Magic R&D. 






It's been nearly fifteen years since Carlos Romao made it to the Sunday Stage. With a World Championship title back in 2002, Romao was no stranger to high level play. Despite his long gap in Sunday play, he had been on a tear recently, dominating his last two Grand Prix: São Paulo back in July, and Atlanta just last weekend. He is arguably the hottest player in the game right now.


His opponent was 21-year old Joey Manner, owner of the The Only Game in Town (TOGIT) in Somerville, New Jersey. Pro Tour *Kaladesh* was the fifth Pro Tour for the young player, and his first Top 8.


The Decks
=========


In a world of aggression and Vehicles, both of these players brought different decks to fight them. Romao was piloting Jeskai Control, a typical control deck with plenty of removal and sweepers. The key card for Romao was [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk). The Gearhulk could attack from many angles, allowing Romao to recast the spell he needs, and the 5/6 body could end a game quickly.


Manner brought a different style of deck to Pro Tour Kaladesh. White-Blue Flash was not a deck that was expected this weekend. While tempo creatures like [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) and [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) dominated last season, it was not clear if they would make an impact in this hyper-aggressive world. [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) was the breakout card for Manner, giving the deck a way to end games quickly.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/ptkld/qf1_manner_romao.jpg)  
*Representing a new era of TOGIT, one of Magic's dominant east coast forces from the early 2000s, Joey Manner faced off against Carlos Romao, the 2002 World Champion, in a match that almost feels like a trip back in time.*


The Games
=========


Manner started things off with [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter), followed by [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) on Romao's end step. [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) got in the Copter and Manner looted, and all Romao could do was take the hit and cast [Glimmer of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmer+of+Genius).


Manner cast two more 2/1 aerial creatures at Romao's end step, and he was clearly in the driver's seat. The Copter and 2-power flyers entered the red zone, Romao cast [Immolating Glare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Glare) on the Copter, and Manner sacrificed his [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) to save it. A [Blessed Alliance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Alliance) forced Manner to sacrifice another flying creature, and Romao took a hit for 5.


[Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) entered the battlefield post combat and created a 2/2 Knight Ally token. Manner sent Gideon, Copter, and [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) into combat. Romao cast [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) before blockers and Immolating Glared the Copter. [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare) from Manner took care of the Gearhulk, and Romao fell to 5.


[Quarantine Field](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quarantine+Field) for two took out [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare) and Gideon, which returned the Gearhulk to the battlefield. Gearhulk cast a [Blessed Alliance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Alliance) for life, and Romao had begun to turn the corner.


The players traded creatures for removal, while Manner continued to beat down with his [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains), plucking away at Romao's life. Romao finally found a [Dovin Baan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin+Baan), which nullified the [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) and allowed him to stabilize.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/ptkld/qf1_romao.jpg)  
*Romao remains sharp with his understanding of control decks.*


Gideon came down on Manner's side and made a token, but a [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames) for three cleared the board. A second Gearhulk prompted a concession out of Manner.


Manner was on the play and kicked things off with a [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit). Carlos immediately targeted it with [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning), and Manner sacrificed it to deny Romao the energy. [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) was Manner's next play, an underwhelming card in this matchup. “Three-mana 2/3,” He said.


Manner played a second [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) with no target, and a [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames) for three from Romao took them both out. A tapped-out Romao prompted Manner to go for a main phase [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn), and Romao had to [Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate) to find a fourth land.


Manner chose to not add any more threats to the board. He was in the driver's seat with Avacyn in play, and he had [Westvale Abbey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Westvale+Abbey) to produce more threats without actually casting cards from his hand. Manner continued to swing in with the Angel and Cleric tokens, chipping away at Romao's life total.


Romao found a [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) which flashed back a [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning). [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) countered the removal spell, and [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) came down to protect Manner's creatures from potential sweepers. An attack from Manner dropped Romao to four and caused [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn) to trigger.


[Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn) transformed, and Manner sacrificed his [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) to save his creatures, but Romao had Harness Lightning to take care of Avacyn. The three damage from transformed Avacyn dropped Romao to 1 life.


Romao's next play was [Dovin Baan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin+Baan). “The name is Baan, [Dovin Baan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin+Baan),” said Manner lightheartedly.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/ptkld/qf1_manner.jpg)  

The 21-year-old store owner made a memorable weekend out of this trip to Hawaii with his first Pro Tour Top 8.


[Dovin Baan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin+Baan) began to gain Romao life and draw cards, and all Manner could do was activate [Westvale Abbey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Westvale+Abbey) to make Clerics. Romao was now in control of the game and cast a second [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk), casting a removal spell on [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller), Manner's last flying creature.


Manner found a Gideon, but it was too little too late as Romao's board full of Gearhulks continued to smash in. A desperation [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare) from Manner was met with [Summary Dismissal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summary+Dismissal), and [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn) from Romao sealed the deal.


Manner was on the play for Game 3 and started things off with [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller). Gideon came down next, and [Immolating Glare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Glare) and [Quarantine Field](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quarantine+Field) took out both permanents. [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn) and [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) gave Manner some threats, and he began beating down.


Romao tried for [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning) on the Avacyn, but Manner had [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) ready. In response, Romao cast a second Harnessed Lighting on the Avacyn. The Copter attacked, and Romao was down to 9.


Romao cast [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames) for three, which took out all of Manner's creatures, but Romao still had a [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) to deal with. Manner went for a main phase Avacyn to crew the Copter, and Romao responded with [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk), casting a [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning) on the Avacyn.


Manner had [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare) for the Gearhulk, and a few more swings with [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) gave Manner the win.


Manner began Game 3 with the first mulligan of the match. He found a six he was happy with and scryed a card to the bottom.


Manner kicked things off with Thraben inspector, but missed his second land drop. An attack prompted a [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) out of Romao and the Inspector hit the graveyard. Manner found a land and made a [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit), but with a [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) and four lands on the other side of the table, he was already really far behind.


Manner attacked with [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) and Romao responded with [Immolating Glare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Glare). Manner had the [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) to give it hexproof but a [Blessed Alliance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Alliance) from Romao caused the Spirit to get sacrificed anyway.


Romao played land six and cast [Linvala, the Preserver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+the+Preserver) and all Manner could do was summon another [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit). Romao untapped and activated his [Wandering Fumarole](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Fumarole) and attacked, and Manner fell to three.


Still with only two lands in play, all Manner could do was sacrifice his clue. Romao made a lethal attack and Manner extended his hand.


**Carlos Romao defeats Joey Manner 3-1 and advances to Stage 2 of the quarterfinals!**
