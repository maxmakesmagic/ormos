The Pillars
===========



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on September 2, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







This weekend's [Pro Tour](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx) is the first run of a new format, which will itself flow, and twist, and change again like quicksilver. While there are a good number of players that will play according to book, I doubt very much that this article will explore every piece of closely guarded hidden-tech secreted away by the most elite of Pro **Magic** teams.


But in terms of a framework, I think we can really tie some principles together ... Certainly we can sketch out the forces of the metagame, if not the specific whorls and angles and shadows cast.


*Let's begin.*


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td106_fire.jpg)The most popular non-land card to be played in this Pro Tour may well be [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire). Last year [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) was the secret sauce, defining the deck that Brian Kibler used to win in [Austin](/en/events/coverage/ptaust09). However this year, the tech is known ... and can go into a variety of decks just as seamlessly as it did in Brian's "Rubin Zoo" winner.

This is a good example of a modern [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck. Yes, it looks a lot like last year's (alternate) [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck! The main change is around the loss of [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), and the obviously different mana base.



![Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Scapeshift)

 

[Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) gave the 2009 red-green [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck a very obvious route to creature-war domination, even though it played relatively few creatures (just [Sakura-Tribe Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) in addition to the twelve you see here, plus the [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village)s). In exchange the 2010 version has [Harmonize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize) (which, to be fair, was played in some of the 2009 decks).


So what is going on here?


The red-green [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck has a couple of different plans it can pursue.


**1. It is a passable creature deck.** Despite the loss of [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), the presence of [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)—especially off of a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf)—can represent a damning amount of card advantage and battlefield-clogging creature mud. Opposing aggressive decks (especially ones with [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)-based offenses) will be frustrated.

**2. It can grind out attrition fights.** Regardless of match-up, the deck can lean on [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), [Harmonize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize), and to a lesser extent [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) to overwhelm opponents spoiling to trade body blows. The [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) + [Oran-Rief, the Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief%2C+the+Vastwood) (which can be found by [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), remember) is a complete and total creature-threat lockdown.


**3. [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) can go over the top.** Especially against a traditional (blue or blue-black Faeries-type deck), the red-green can go [Great Sable Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag) and [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) (and remember it has the [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) + [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) combo main deck), and pull the insect wings off the least popular tribe of all time, creature by creature, card after card.


**4. Oh yeah, it's also a combo deck!** The [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck has two copies of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle). As you well know, the combination of "[Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle)" plus "[Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s" can prove lethal. The [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck can use its [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) to set up a [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) kill. The main limiting factor to this deck is the disappearance of [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground), but having posted undefeated records in at least two different Extended daily events we looked at, red-green [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift)—with careful deployment of "basic [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)"—looks like it can overcome that land loss.

Despite having certain cards in common—[Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire), of course [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) (and in some cases [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf))—this version of [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift)*Zoo* is much different in outlook. Instead of a hybrid beatdown (or even mid-range aggressive) / combo deck, [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) Zoo is a purely offensive deck with an additional twist.


Did you notice the creature selection? [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede), and [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) all benefit greatly from playing the card [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift). [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) of course can get big (it's basically a Terravore) ... but creatures like [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) can get *really* big.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td106_3Cards1.jpg)For example you have a 1/1 [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) and three lands in play. You play [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) (+2/+2), and crack immediately for a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (+4/+4). You now play [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) and sacrifice all four lands for all three [Flagstones of Trokair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flagstones+of+Trokair) and the other [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest). All these lands come into play for an additional (+8/+8) ... for a total of +12/+12. The [Flagstones of Trokair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flagstones+of+Trokair) don't get along with one another and all die simultaneously to dig up the three [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains)—+18/+18. For good measure, you sacrifice the remaining [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest).


Don't forget [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) even *started* with a point of power!


That is more than lethal *with just one [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) in play*. There are all kinds of additional shenanigans possible when you mix up other creatures and types.


[Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) Zoo is still a combo deck, too.


While it might be thin on [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s relative to what seems to be required (seven [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s with no [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground)s in the format makes for hardly any damage at all ... in theory).

Easily the most exciting archetype from my perspective is the Extended upgrade to [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension).


It has got all the [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)/[Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) shenanigans of the Standard deck, but has wild opportunities on top of them. For example, [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose). Can you even imagine doubling up on a [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose)? It's not even like the card is *just* free. You get to draw *two* cards *and* get more than your mana's investment back. That is a heck of an update over [See Beyond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+Beyond)!


The upgrades don't stop there.


[Careful Consideration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration) over [Foresee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foresee).  
[Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) over [Kiln Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend).  
[Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) over not-[Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td106_cardsOverCards.jpg)In the realm of doubled up dramatic entrances, it's hard to beat a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). "Bounce that *and* that; draw *two* cards." Yowza!

[Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) is a combo deck that relies on the power of [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace). When you play [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace), all of a sudden flipping over expensive cards with [Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) starts looking a lot less lethal. You can actually lose below zero life with [Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) ... In theory you will die when you are done fooling around with this rapid fire-repeating spell ... unless you have covered yourself with a portable [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) of course!

[Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) will put a copy of, say, [Intervention Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intervention+Pact) out there for the opponent; he or she will have to pay for the [Intervention Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intervention+Pact) the following turn. Presumably your opponent will not be able to, and the game will end. Sometimes you will be Super Lucky Player and have more than one kind of Pact—then you can play them all. It is just not likely that the opponent will be able to pay for more than one, even if he or she is in the right colors. I don't like losing to combo decks, either, but yes, [Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) is a particularly embarrassing way to go.


"So what you're saying is that I have a 4/4, a damage prevention shield, and an adjustable rate mortgage coming up? Gotcha. Frowntown."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td106_2Cards1.jpg)[Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind)'s additional infinite combo kill comes by way of [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) + [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin). You put [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) on a [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) and say, "go." At the end of your opponent's turn you can tap the [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) to make a copy of itself; the copy hits the 'field and untaps the original; now untapped, you make another copy and the same thing happens ... Over and over until you have lots of imaginary [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite)s. They don't go away until the *next* end step ... so plenty of time to wield some 2/1 justice into the only attack you will need this game!


The cool thing about these combinations is that there is no upper limit to how much havoc you can produce. Is the opponent on one trillion and one life? Still dies to a Pact. For that matter your opponent still dies to however many [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite)s you want to make. Or either, or both.

This deck is essentially the Standard full-on Faeries from ages past. You know, the soul-crushing one that dominated all those tournaments.

First turn [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) or [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) into second turn [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), and the party just keeps coming from there. [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) becomes better and better countermagic as [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) tokens accumulate. [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) eradicates any notions of a fair fight, should it hit. The card pool may not be "perfect" for Faeries, but the presence of both [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) go a long way in paving the way to a win ... [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) and [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) (itself a Faerie of course!) are there to play cleanup.
