Infecting Legacy
================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 14, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Tom Ross is known for making unconventional choices during deck building and winning anyway. Last weekend was no exception, as he finished in the Top 4 of the [StarCityGames Legacy Open in Atlanta](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/010314_atlanta.html) with GU Infect. While Infect is a relatively known quantity in Legacy, it doesn't see a lot of play. There are just too many sweet decks to choose from, and Infect is often left sitting on the sidelines. However, Tom is attempting to change that. 


![Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glistener+Elf)
 Infect rarely, if ever, wins with actual damage, so getting to play a card like [Invigorate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invigorate) is a huge bonus. [Vines of Vastwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood) is a deceptively versatile card, capable of stopping a spot-removal spell or even stopping your opponent from equipping [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) for a turn! It's no surprise that Tom chose to play four of each, while playing only one copy of [Berserk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserk) and [Might of Old Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Old+Krosa), presumably just to test them. 


 In his Top 8 interview, Tom mentioned adding more [Berserk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserk)s and [Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle)s, both of which sound like reasonable choices. I can't wait to find out what his finished product will look like.
