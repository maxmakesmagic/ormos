House of Commons III: Shards Edition!
=====================================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 29, 2008 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello to all Johnnies! (Don’t worry, other psychological player profile types, you’re also welcome here!) I'm still riding some of the Grixis-y steam from last week, so I’ll start off today by wishing you all a happy Halloween! I hope you’ve all picked out a **Magic**-related costume, such as [Kamahl, Pit Fighter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Pit+Fighter), [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), or [Barbed Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbed+Lightning). And no changeling shenanigans are allowed! I felt bad about potentially scaring away all my candy customers with my spooked-up laboratory after last week, so I have taken down nearly all the scary decorations (my endless closet appears to be permanent) and filled my inviting lab windows with decks galore.

For those who aren’t aware, today marks another installment in the extremely fun House of Commons series kicked off by [Chris Millar](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Chris%20Millar). These articles usually pop up after the release of a large set such as *Shards of Alara*, *Shadowmoor*, or *Lorwyn*. Basically, House of Commons is a deckbuilding exercise that revolves around using four copies of every common from the new large set. This multitude of commons is spread out into many different decks. As Chris so expertly said in the last installment, "It's like a cross between doing a Sudoku puzzle and [Building on a Budget.](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?tag=Building%20on%20a%20Budget&description=Building%20on%20a%20Budget)"

![Banewasp Affliction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Banewasp+Affliction)Having never embarked on this deckbuilding exercise before, I ran into some snags almost immediately. *Shards of Alara*'s tri-color theme meant I’d have to ration out the limited mana fixers, the Panorama land cycle and the Obelisk artifact cycle. Once that was taken care of, I found that most of the commons jumped into decks of their own shard, with only a few stragglers (Hi, [Banewasp Affliction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banewasp+Affliction)!). This was my first time doing this, so keep the faith alive! More importantly, feel free to swap commons here and there or even start with a clean slate and mess around. The fruits of my efforts lie below, so let’s get started, shall we?

### Deck 1: White-blue Aggro

This deck uses just about all the common exalted creatures in combination with some hard-to-remove weenies ([Deft Duelist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deft+Duelist), [Sighted-Caste Sorcerer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sighted-Caste+Sorcerer)). In addition, a small pocket of life-gain is there to buffer your life total if the going gets tough. For a neat trick, exalt one of your creatures and if it survives combat, cast [Soul's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Grace) on it for a quick refreshment of life. [Yoked Plowbeast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yoked+Plowbeast) is basically your only late-game hope, but your abundance of early beaters should soften your opponent up until then. In the late stages of the whole process, I shoehorned the [Cathartic Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathartic+Adept) + [Excommunicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) interaction in, which results in permanent excommunication.

### Deck 2: Esper Aggro

Amidst the cutting, shaving, and trimming of other decks in this exercise, somehow the only Esper-colored deck survived totally unscathed. Artifact beats reign supreme with this straightforward deck. [Glaze Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glaze+Fiend) in particular can grow to absurd levels with [Etherium Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etherium+Sculptor) reducing the cost of your already cheap artifacts. [Courier's Capsule](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courier%27s+Capsule) can keep your hand full of cards while boosting [Windwright Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windwright+Mage). And here, casting [Call to Heel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+Heel) on your own creatures is Plan A for once, both for the card draw and for more [Glaze Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glaze+Fiend) growth.

### Deck 4: Naya Aggro

This deck is all about pedal to the metal aggro, starting at turn one with [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) and [Goblin Mountaineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Mountaineer), and turn two with the excellent [Rip-Clan Crasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rip-Clan+Crasher) and controversial ([Augury Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Adept)'s got nothing on this 2/2) [Cylian Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cylian+Elf). [Lush Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lush+Growth) is a triple-whammy here fixing your mana, helping out [Goblin Mountaineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Mountaineer), and pumping [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl). An array of [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) effects is around also. If your early weenies find themselves outmatched later, [Thorn-Thrash Viashino](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn-Thrash+Viashino) can clean up the board while creating a huge threat at the same time. [Soul's Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Fire) is great anytime, but here it's great cubed.

### Deck 7: Black/Red Synergy

This deck wants to take advantage of [Hissing Iguanar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hissing+Iguanar), which is a combo enabler if I’ve ever seen one. Quick Goblins can enter play, waiting to be sacrificed to a timely [Bone Splinters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) or just to die in combat. [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie) can come back for more, and [Dreg Reaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Reaver) provides a beefy body. [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) and [Banewasp Affliction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banewasp+Affliction) can provide even more pain, and all the while the plucky Iguanar can plink away at your opponent.
