Semifinals: John Rolf vs. Pascal Maynard
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 5, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











Pascal Maynard came to Pro Tour *Ixalan* going against the grain. While nearly half of the field turned out to play some sort of energy variant, Maynard came with a deck many considered poorly positioned: White-Blue [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift).


If Maynard's deck wasn't expected to be in the semifinals, John Rolf wasn't expected to be here at all. The Omaha, Nebraska, native had two Grand Prix Top 8 appearances to his name, but had never threatened to Top 8 a Pro Tour before.


That all changed in Albuquerque. Both players had been impressive in their quarterfinals wins; Maynard had done one better than his previous quarterfinals loss in the Top 8 of Pro Tour *Oath of the Gatewatch*, while Rolf hadn't showed any nerves in taking down Germany's Christian Hauck.


Rolf was on a defining archetype of the last year of Standard: Ramunap Red. Anchored by [Hazoret the Fervent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hazoret+the+Fervent), the deck filled with haste creatures and effects to prevent blocking—plus *Ixalan* addition [Rampaging Ferocidon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Ferocidon) to also prevent life gain—was the second-most popular deck choice of the weekend, though Rolf was the only player to convert it into a Top 8 showing.


### **The Games**


Both players opened the semifinals with a mulligan, but both had solid starts. Maynard curved [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries) into [Search for Azcanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Azcanta), while Rolf drew first blood with a turn-two [Earthshaker Khenra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthshaker+Khenra).


One of the innocuous but important cards in the matchup was [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat), and Maynard was able to embalm one on the third turn to provide an [Earthshaker Khenra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthshaker+Khenra) roadblock. Even worse for Rolf was that, thanks to the Minister's preaching, Maynard was able to flip Search and "ramp" out a fifth-turn [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention), which filled the board with blockers and also provided another lifelink creature on the battlefield.


The Angel fell to [Lightning Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike), but a second Angel soon followed. It too fell to a removal spell, but the Servos left behind were enough to leave a mark. However, Hazoret and then an eternalized [Earthshaker Khenra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthshaker+Khenra) forced chump blocks from the tokens, leaving Maynard on the back foot after an otherwise impressive start.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/PTIxalan-20171105-2939.jpg)  
*John Rolf (pictured) hoped to get off to a quick start against Pascal Maynard.*


But a [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) changed all that, and when [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) returned bigger and badder and still with lifelink, Rolf had seen enough. He picked up his cards and the pair was off to Game 2.


[Kari Zev, Skyship Raider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kari+Zev%2C+Skyship+Raider) began the beatdown for Rolf in the second game, and was soon joined by a [Harsh Mentor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harsh+Mentor). Meanwhile, Maynard used his first two turns to set up his game, including dumping a [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) into the graveyard before playing [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) as a blocker to buy time.


The big decision point came on the fourth turn, when Maynard opted to just embalm his [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) rather than go for the [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) in his hand on the Gift out of fear for the [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) Rolf was representing. The next attack knocked Maynard to 10 life, and when Rolf just passed back Maynard was forced to go for the [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish). Predictably, his [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) ran into [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade), and [Hazoret the Fervent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hazoret+the+Fervent) continued to put on the pressure. A chump block left Maynard at just 4 life, but a second [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) broke things open as [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) brought back [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention), and a second [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) followed.


Like that, Maynard again had life—both literally and figuratively. His next attack put him back up to 16 life, and a draw step later Rolf conceded the game.


The third game was when Rolf finally found a one-drop, in this case [Bomat Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier). He [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock)ed Maynard's [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries), and then had [Rampaging Ferocidon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Ferocidon) right on time to answer Maynard's [Authority of the Consuls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Authority+of+the+Consuls).


With no third land, a [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) that couldn't gain life, and cards piling up underneath the [Bomat Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier), Maynard found himself deep in the hole. A third land and [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) was his best attempt to dig out, but without a fourth land he was forced to concede as Rolf got on the board.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/PTIxalan-20171105-2916.jpg)  
*Pascal Maynard would look to seal the deal in Game 4.*


Maynard had the play for Game 4, but he also didn't have anything to cast on the first or second turn, while Rolf curved [Soul-Scar Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul-Scar+Mage) into [Kari Zev, Skyship Raider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kari+Zev%2C+Skyship+Raider). Champion of the Wits was his first action, though he wasn't able to discard either a creature or [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) into the graveyard.


[Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) removed [Rampaging Ferocidon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Ferocidon), and Maynard further developed his board with a [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries), staring down Kari Zev as well as a potentially scary [Scavenger Grounds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenger+Grounds). His next turn yielded [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention), though an [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) on [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) brought back [Rampaging Ferocidon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Ferocidon) to ping Maynard for the Servos the Angel brought with it.


Still, it was enough for Maynard to take [Rampaging Ferocidon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Ferocidon) off the battlefield, while following up with [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits). All Rolf had was a post-combat [Bomat Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier) to join Kari Zev in facing down Maynard's board of [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits), Servo token, and [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) token. Another [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits)—this time a 4/4 token—joined Maynard's side, and it began to look more and more difficult for Rolf to climb the hill he now faced.


[Chandra, Torch of Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance) was at least a way to do that. Chandra pinged out one of Maynard's creatures, and with several blockers at the ready, the red pyromancer represented a non-combat way to victory.


[Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) was the perfect response, though, as Maynard couldn't kill Chandra but was able to clear two of Rolf's creatures before following up with another [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) to continue sculpting his hand. Getting aggressive, the Canadian fired up [Hostile Desert](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostile+Desert) and served with most of his team, leaving just [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) and a token back to block. Rolf's last card in hand was [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) to remove [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention), but when his draw step failed to get him back in the game, he extended his hand as Pascal Maynard advanced to the final of Pro Tour *Ixalan*.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/PTIxalan-20171105-2962.jpg)  
*John Rolf (right) offered Pascal Maynard (left) the handshake to congratulate him on his win.*


As the players chatted after the match, while the cameras flashed and Maynard prepared to move on to the finals, he summed his day up succinctly.


"Normally, I can't smile—but today I can't stop."


**Pascal Maynard defeated John Rolf 3-1 and advanced to the finals!**
