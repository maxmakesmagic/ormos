Blinking With a Deadeye
=======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 25, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods












One of my favorite cards of all time is [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink). The card was extremely versatile in its uses, seeing play with just about every enters-the-battlefield creature in the formats it belonged to. One of the reasons [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) was so cool was that it offered multiple uses of the effect, which was all Blink decks wanted to do. With *Avacyn Restored*, we saw a lot of one-time blink effects printed, but not too many multiple-use blinks. One of the cards that did do this, though, was [Deadeye Navigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator). 


![Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Momentary+Blink)
 Now, traditionally, you probably wouldn't think of a six-mana 5/5 that needs other creatures to do anything special as a Constructed card, but Fergus Brown-O'Byrne decided it was exactly what he wanted to be battling with at the Newfoundland 2012 States! Obviously, when we are playing with a card like [Deadeye Navigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator), we want to be maximizing his blink effect, so every creature in this list has some synergy with the card. From [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) early to [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) late, you cannot have a creature in play that doesn't want to be blinked! 


 Of course, basing your entire deck around a single card can be tricky if you don't draw that certain card, but Fergus tried to make sure to avoid this trap with the inclusion of [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). Now, instead of only four creatures that synergize with your primary plan, you have eight! Of course, its not like any of the creatures in here are bad without a blink effect, making the deck play fine even if you don't draw Angel or Navigator. Throw in some solid removal and mana fixing and you have yourself a Top 8-quality list!
