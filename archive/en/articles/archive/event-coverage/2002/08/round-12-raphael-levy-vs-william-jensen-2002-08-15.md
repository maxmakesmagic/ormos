Round 12: Raphael Levy vs. William Jensen
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 15, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a787.jpg)Levy: "We're one-and-one, you and me."  
 Huey: "When did you beat me?"  
 Levy: "Teams."  
 Huey: "Whatever, that doesn't count."  
 Levy: "(shrug)"  
 Huey: "Do you think Gary Wise won a Pro Tour?"

Both of these players are 7-2 and would like nothing more to win here to give themselves a little extra breathing room in the wild block format tomorrow. Neither of them drafted a great deck—Levy's is about dealing quick damage and sealing the deal with "punisher" cards, whereas Huey deck is based on winning the damage race with a trio of [Skywing Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven)s. Both players are 1-1 with these decks so far.

Jensen's turn-2 [Mystic Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Familiar) was outclassed by Levy's [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). Jensen played out a [Skywing Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven), hoping to win the race in the air, but Levy bounced it with a [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) and then dropped two consecutive [Petravark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Petravark)s. Jensen played out some blockers, like [Aven Cloudchaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Cloudchaser) and a [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad), but Levy had both tempo and the tricks to back it up, including [Muscle Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muscle+Burst).

Eventually Levy put out a [Pardic Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pardic+Lancer), and between that and the Mongrel, Jensen has a really difficult time blocking. Jensen's last few draws were nothing but land, and Levy took Game 1.

Levy 1 – Jensen 0

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a788.jpg)Levy had Jensen on the defensive again on Game 2, with the same mix of quick creatures and combat tricks that worked so well in the first game, plus an [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger) in the graveyard. Eventually Huey stabilized at five life behind his [Luminous Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminous+Guardian) and was ready to start trying to win with flyers when Levy dropped a [Browbeat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat) on him. Huey had no choice but to let Levy draw three, and then down came [Dwarven Driller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dwarven+Driller) to start in on Huey's lands.

Huey sucked up the first two activations of the Driller, going to one, and then his [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s started disappearing into the graveyard. Huey was sneaking in hits where he could, and eventually got Levy to within range of a single all-out attack, and had a [Pay No Heed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pay+No+Heed) to stop the potentially fatal flashed back [Howling Gale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Gale).

But then Levy drew [Breaking Point](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+Point) and cleared the board with it, dropped [Balthor the Stout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balthor+the+Stout) and sent it in thanks to [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger). Huey had no way out.

**Final Result:** Levy 2 – Jensen 0

There were five cards Levy could have drawn on the last turn to kill Huey, but the fact that Huey came back from the brink of death to within one turn of winning that game was remarkable.
