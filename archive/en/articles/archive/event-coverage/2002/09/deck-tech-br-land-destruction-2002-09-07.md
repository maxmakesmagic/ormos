Deck Tech: B/R Land Destruction
===============================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 7, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/hero/wizardslogo_thumb.jpg)
By Nate Heiss











![Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mirari)At Grand Prix - Cleveland things looked very predictable - lots of Black, a large helping of Blue Green, and a spattering of others on the side. One man, however, refused to submit to the cycle of good cards that seemed to lock up the meta game - William "Huey" Jensen decided to run an unorthodox Black/Red deck based around [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish), [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari), and land destruction. He felt that the strong presence of Mono-Black let open a hole in the meta-game that made a mana denial deck to be plausible. After a week of hard testing, his work has clearly paid off. He's got a 7-1 record going into day 2.At first glance the deck seems like an ordinary mana-denial deck - until you realize that it is actually a card advantage deck - generating mass quantities of card advantage with [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) and [Recoup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup)s. The [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) base allows the deck to be flexible in tight situations...wishing for cards from of three different sources: 1) the Sideboard, 2) cards that were [Tainted Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact)ed away, and 3) cards that have already been used a few times with [Recoup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup). 

[Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood)s and Edicts fend off the critters in the early game while coming back later on with the help of [Recoup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup) and [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) to finish off any threats that get by the initial "cleansing". [Magnivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magnivore) is the most likely kill mechanism, but if it is killed or [Tainted Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact)ed away, the deck can go into an infinite [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish)/[Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) loop that will eventually destroy every land in the opponents deck, followed up with an infinite [Morbid Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morbid+Hunger) loop with the same trick (using [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) on the wishes in order to get a Wish and a card back).

![Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Burning+Wish)Perhaps the most interesting part of the deck is the plan against U/G - Sideboarding out much of the land kill in order to bring in [Overmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overmaster)s and a second [Magnivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magnivore) with some [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) backup. After this transformation, the deck plays much like mono-black but with the added bonus of having [Catalyst Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Catalyst+Stone)s and [Recoup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup)s to provide a seemingly never-ending supply of Edicts and Bloods. After most of the pressure has been removed, a huge [Magnivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magnivore) easily deals the final blows. This deck's hardest matchup by far is U/G Threshold, which is fast paced and drops many creatures that must be Edicted away - much of the matchup is determined by [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate), however it can be difficult to [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) for more than three in a two color deck. The deck is a great metagame choice when mono-black is so prevalent, and this clearly makes a statement for the power of the [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari)/Bruning Wish engine on a whole. I expect to see at least a few more decks based around this combo - perhaps not in this block, but certainly when Extended season rolls around.
