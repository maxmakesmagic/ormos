Friday, August 4: 12:15 p.m. - Round 1: Richard Hoaen vs. Liam Walker
---------------------------------------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/canat06/hoaen_rd1.jpg)*The grumpy, early morning Hoaen.*
A Calgary native, Walker is no stranger to Nationals, having qualified three times before, twice through Regionals and once by way of the Grinders. He was in tough in his first match of the day as he faced off with Pro Tour regular Richard Hoaen, who, I'm told, is very grumpy.


### Game 1


Liam won the flip and opted to play first. He got out of the gates quickly with a turn 1 [Isamaru, Hound of Konda](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isamaru%2C+Hound+of+Konda) and followed it up on turn 2 with a Bloodthirsty [Scab-Clan Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Mauler). Rich managed to [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) the second Mauler, but his [Sleight of Hand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand)s could find no answers to the little beaters. When Liam sent a [Char](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char) to the dome at end of turn, Rich scooped up his cards.


Liam Walker 1 - Richard Hoaen 0


### Game 2


Hoaen opted to play first in this game. Unfortunately, he started things off with a turn 1 "misclick," laying an [Izzet Boilerworks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Boilerworks) on the table instead of the [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) that was also in his hand. Walker pointed out the error and Hoaen dutifully returned the land and passed the turn. Walker led things off with [Brushland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brushland), Isamaru and followed up the hound with a pair of Watchwolves on consecutive turns.


Rich had a [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) for the hound, but no answer to either of the Wolves. A [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) later, the life totals were heavily in Walker's favour, although he had taken eight damage from his own mana-base. At this point, Hoaen started to take control of the game. He bounced one of the Watchwolves with [Eye of Nowhere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Nowhere) and [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed it when Liam tried to replay it. Meloku, the Clouded Mirror came down the following turn, but since Rich was at such a low life total, he was forced to chump block [Watchwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf). Liam finished off the Moonfolk with [Seal of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire), and added a [Savannah Lions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions) to his team. An enhanced [Steamcore Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird) killed the Lion and chumpblocked the [Watchwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf) on the following turn. Liam drew land after land, while Rich played [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) and [Magnivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magnivore) on back to back turns. [Watchwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf) bought Liam a turn to find a burn spell, but he couldn't find one and the pair of 7/7's finished him off.


Liam Walker 1 - Richard Hoaen 1



![Meloku the Clouded Mirror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Meloku+the+Clouded+Mirror)

### Game 3


Liam opted to play first and once again had a turn 1 Isamaru and turn 2 [Watchwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf). Rich just played [Sleight of Hand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand), but had an 18-16 life advantage thanks to the [Brushland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brushland) and [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) on Liam's side. A pair of [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)s and a [Steamcore Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird) bought Rich some time, but Walker just replayed his guys, two Watchwolves and a [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape). Rich got in for one with his Weird, and then cast back-to-back [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm)s. Liam saved one of his Wolves with a textless [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth). A turn later, [Paladin en-Vec](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paladin+en-Vec) squared off against Meloku and an Illusion token. Liam played a second Paladin, but Rich [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)ed it at the end of turn and made three more Illusions with Meloku.


Meloku and company started hitting for five per turn, and since Walker had taken so much damage from his lands, he didn't have much time to find an answer. Rich bounced the [Paladin en-Vec](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paladin+en-Vec) twice with [Eye of Nowhere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Nowhere) and kept the [Watchwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf) at bay with Illusion tokens. Once again, there were no burn spells in sight for Liam and he quickly fell to the Clouded Mirror of Victory.


Richard Hoaen defeats Liam Walker 2-1.


  

Friday, August 4: 12:35 p.m. - Do Not Taunt Immigration
-------------------------------------------------------


Japan, Mexico, Australia, China, England, the Czech Republic. These are all places I have traveled to cover events. Canada? Notsomuch. It's not that I have anything against Canadia, former colony of Eng-er-land, I just never really had a reason to come here before. However, considering it was more North than anywhere in the United States, and that it felt like the flesh was being seared from my body when I walked across the tarmac to my flight at Dulles/Abu-Dhabi airport, I was quite happy to touch down in this land of supposedly friendly people. I was not, however, prepared for the grilling I would get from Canadian Immigration.


Snarky Immigration Officer (SIO): Why are you here?  

Me: Business - A convention.  

SIO: What kind of convention?  

Me: A Magic the Gathering convention.  

SIO: What company do you work for?  

Me: Wizards of the Coast.  

SIO: Do you have a laptop?  

Me: Yes…  

SIO: Is it yours or your company's?  

Me: I own it.  

SIO: \*raises eyebrows\* I see… When was the last time you were in Canada?  

Me: Never.  

SIO: Why not?

When Andrew played a [Burning-Tree Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Shaman), Guillaume Sealed the Shambler's Doom, preventing any counters from being Grafted on to the Shaman.


After a few uneventful turns, Guillaume began to use his Orzhova, Church of Deals at the end of Andrew's turns, which, combined with [Pillory of the Sleepless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillory+of+the+Sleepless), would kill Andrew in a mere five turns if the board remained stale-mated. After Guillaume passed the turn back to him, Andrew sent all of his creatures into the Red Zone. He lost most of his team in the exchange, including the now 8/8 [Vulturous Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulturous+Zombie) to the [Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp). The only creature that made it through was the [Orzhov Euthanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Euthanist). The two damage knocked Guillaume to seven life and meant that Andrew's next play, [Skarrgan Skybreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skarrgan+Skybreaker), was a Bloodthirsty 6/6. The Skybreaker broke and the life-loss from [Moroii](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) on Guillaume's upkeep sealed the deal.


Andrew Noworaj 1 - Guillaume Daoust 0


### Game 2


Both players keep their opening hands. Guillaume elected to go first and curved out with [Mourning Thrull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mourning+Thrull), [Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp), Blind hunter, and [Agent of Masks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agent+of+Masks), while Andrew's only play was a turn 2 [Aquastrand Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquastrand+Spider).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/canat06/r4_daoust.jpg)*Guillaume Daoust*
Andrew had a full hand, and Guillaume thought for awhile before sending everybody. Andrew blocked the Agent with his Spider and pumped it up with [Might of the Nephilim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+the+Nephilim). The rest of the creatures got through, dropping Andrew to 11. Guillaume, meanwhile, was at a healthy 24 life. Andrew looked to get back into the game with his [Vulturous Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulturous+Zombie). Guillaume attacked with his [Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp), and Andrew was set to trade his [Aquastrand Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquastrand+Spider) for it. Guillaume had other plans, using [Carom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carom) to redirect the Imp's damage to the Zombie, killing it.


A freshly played [Silhana Starfletcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silhana+Starfletcher) helped to stop the bleeding for a turn, since Guillaume had no profitable attacks. Still, he sent his team, and Andrew blocked the [Blind Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Hunter) with the Spider. With damage on the stack, Guillaume tried to [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) his Bat, but Andrew had the Douse of Gloom to kill it first.


At this point, Andrew's fatties started showing up and he played [Cytospawn Shambler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytospawn+Shambler) and [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) on back to back turns. Guillaume was still plinking away with one-power flyers, but he wasn't putting a whole lot of pressure on Andrew until he played  
[Moroii](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii). [Thrive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrive) pumped up Andrew's entire team and he sent the Hulk which Trampled over [Mourning Thrull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mourning+Thrull), which had been Haunted by the [Blind Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Hunter). [Moroii](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) attacked back for four, knocking Andrew to 5. Guillaume's board featured two [Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp)s, a [Beacon Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+Hawk), and [Moroii](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii), while Andrew had two 7/7's and a 2/4 [Silhana Starfletcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silhana+Starfletcher)..


Hulk came in again, and this time Guillaume blocked with his [Beacon Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+Hawk), pumping it up to 1/4. All that mattered, though, was the trample damage as Andrew played a Bloodthirsty [Skarrgan Skybreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skarrgan+Skybreaker) again, this time piled high with four Canadian dollars on it (a toonie and two loonies). At a precarious five life, Andrew bought a turn by chumpblocking [Moroii](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) with his Starfletcher. Then, on his turn, he used [Douse in Gloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Douse+in+Gloom) to clear out one of the Imps, and served with all the fatties (two 7/7's and the 6/6 Skybreaker). The 7/7's trampled through for five damage each, and the Skybreaker once again sealed the deal.


Andrew Noworaj defeated Guillaume Daoust 2 - 0

Friday, August 4: 5:02 p.m. - Round 6: Phil Samms vs. Duncan McGregor
---------------------------------------------------------------------


In the final round today, battles for 3-0 took place and we're here to cover one of them. Duncan McGregor is perhaps better known for his status as a level three judge, but he's no slouch when it comes to playing, as he's rattled off an impressive number of 9th places at high-level tournaments, including Canadian Nationals last year. Phil Samms, on the other hand, is best known for his internet celebrity status and general buffoonery. He is often seen in the quotes sections in various articles, usually spouting excessive ribaldry and being generally entertaining. There's no better feature match in my mind for the end of the day, and these games will surely prove to be lively.


### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/canat06/r6_mcgregor.jpg)*Pensive Level 3 judge Duncan McGregor.*
Duncan keeps quickly, and Phil thinks for a while before doing the same. The first few turns are typically uneventful, as Duncan started with a [Mourning Thrull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mourning+Thrull) and a [Gnat Alley Creeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnat+Alley+Creeper) only to be foiled by a [Soulsworn Jury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulsworn+Jury). Duncan attacked anyway and [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) finished the job, but another wall came down for Phil in the form of a 2/5 [Molten Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Sentry)… that was Hit straight to the bin. Duncan had no further plays, though, and Phil started to stabilize with a Guardian of the *Guildpact* and a hand full of cards to Duncan's two.


An [Ogre Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Savant) came down on the following turn to return the bothersome Thrull, and all the judge had was a raw-dogged [Orzhov Euthanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Euthanist). One of Samms' *four* [Train of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Train+of+Thought)s made an appearance, and the tempo of the game shifted fully as card after card came while Duncan sat with no action. A [Mausoleum Turnkey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Turnkey) finally gave Macgregor some action, returning the Creeper. Phil still had a full grip at his disposal, but with his life at 8, he had to make that his defenses remained impenetrable. Duncan continued with his team of small red men as he dropped both [War-Torch Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War-Torch+Goblin)*and* [Kill-Suit Cultist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kill-Suit+Cultist), but Samms was determined to keep his board position as a [Steamcore Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird) tried to finish the Turnkey but [Peel from Reality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peel+from+Reality) would have none of that-and on the following turn, Duncan moved all-in.


After all was said and done, a number of one-toughness creatures were dead and Samms had taken one point. Turnkey came down once more to fetch back [War-Torch Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War-Torch+Goblin), and a [Silkwing Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwing+Scout) came down but was [Char](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char)red out of the skies. It was once again time for Phil to gas up, and gas up he did with his third [Train of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Train+of+Thought) for five. A Silkwing of his own came down to continue the defense, but Duncan's rejoinder was in the form of *[Living Inferno](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Inferno)*. Another Guardian tried to hold the fort, and Duncan just replayed the War-Torch and passed the turn with the giant fiery beast at the ready.


Samms just held back with his Guardians and the board had maintained its stalled state. Nothing Phil could play would live and nothing Duncan could attack with would make it through alive. There were more creatures left in Duncan's deck, though, and it seems like it would only be a matter of time before he overcame the Maginot line in front of him. [Lurking Informant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Informant) almost spelled doom by itself, but it saw the Error of its ways as it was countered. It didn't seem to matter, as creatures kept coming off the top of McGregor's deck, and with the Inferno keeping Samms on lockdown, it was only a matter of time before he packed in his cards.


McGregor - 1, Samms - 0


### Game 2


Phil keeps quickly, and this time it's Duncan's turn to think. He decides he's happy with his seven, and the first couple turns just have bouncelands, with McGregor discarding a Euthanist. [Freewind Equenaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Freewind+Equenaut) was matched by [Gnat Alley Creeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnat+Alley+Creeper), but [Enigma Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enigma+Eidolon) held the ground for Phil. [Demon's Jester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demon%27s+Jester) emerged from Duncan's side only to get met by a [Pillory of the Sleepless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillory+of+the+Sleepless) (and a mill for three). [Greater Forgeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Forgeling) came down and attacked into Samms' board with the Creeper, and Equenaut was returned to the hand thanks to Peel. Turnkey decided to return a [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice), and it seemed like Duncan once again had the advantage. He continued to deploy creatures with a [Silkwing Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwing+Scout) ready to swing in the air.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/canat06/r6_samms.jpg)*Phil Samms, bumblebee.*
He had fallen to eight in the process, though, with Pillory damage pinging away and [Boros Fury-Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield) killing the Turnkey and keeping the replayed Equenaut alive. The next attack had the Scout and the Creeper trade for Samms' Equenaut and Eidolon, and the Forgeling came through for six, dropping Samms to five. Chronarch brought back both Fury-Shield and [Enigma Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enigma+Eidolon), though he didn't have the mana to bring down Eidolon as well and was forced to chump block.


But the battle was not finished and the Pillory damage continued to mount-the Fury-Shield threatened to be lethal, so a solution would have to surface soon. He played out a [Lurking Informant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Informant) to try and filter his draw quality. Samms took a risk in casting [Train of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Train+of+Thought) for two to dig for [Plumes of Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumes+of+Peace) and cast [Enigma Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enigma+Eidolon) to hold up the defense. Unfortunately, the risk did not pay off for him, as a [Lurking Informant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Informant) activation revealed a [Steamcore Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird), taking down the Eidolon and attacking for the last six points of damage.


Duncan McGregor wins 2-0 over Phil Samms to finish the day with a 5-1 record. Phil drops to 3-1-2 and will need to work some magic tomorrow to make a run at the elimination rounds.
