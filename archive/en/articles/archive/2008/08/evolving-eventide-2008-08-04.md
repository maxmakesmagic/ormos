Evolving *Eventide*
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 4, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







It was a swelteringly hot day in the Summer when I was 9 years old. My older brother Matt was going to his friend Jeremy's house for the afternoon when my mom got a call and had to go to the hospital with my grandma. I didn't really understand what was going on, but I got dropped off with my brother at Jeremy's place. My brother told me that Jeremy played **Magic**, so I had brought my deck over. I didn't know much about the game back then, but I had been honing and adding to my red deck til' it was packed with [Chain Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+Lightning)s, [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s, [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball)s, and [Ball Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning)s. I had just learned how to play the game a few months earlier.


Jeremy was 13, so, naturally, I thought he was the coolest guy in the world. My brother and I brought our decks over and played **Magic** for hours. Looking back on it, this is probably some of the most fun I've ever had.


Fast forward 13 years: Kelly Digges asked me to write an article on the theme decks of *Eventide*.


I've always liked theme decks. Every time I've ever been absent from **Magic** for a few months and wanted to get back into it; I've picked up a few precons and played them against each other.


[![Return_to_Battle](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/PK/Return_to_Battle.jpg)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Return%2Bto%2BBattle)So I stared at my computer screen, not really sure what I would write about, and randomly logged onto Facebook to find this message:



> 
> *Hey Senor Van Lunen-*
> 
> 
> *A bunch of guys I work with here at Sony have started playing Magic again and I was contemplating joining them. But I haven't played since Junior High School. Any recommendations for the best way to jump back into things?*
> 
> 
> *Hope all's well.* 
> 
> 
> *Later on,*
> 
> 
> *-Jeremy*
> 
> 
> 


Awesome! Jeremy wants to start gaming again. I quickly responded:



> 
> *Yeah, the best way to get back into it initially would probably be to buy a couple theme decks and just play them against each other."*
> 
> 
> *-Jacob*
> 
> 
> 


The great part about theme decks is that you can easily add cards to them as you draft, play Sealed, or just crack packs for fun. It's a great way to have a deck with some oomph without having to put it together from scratch. Let's take a look at the theme decks of *Eventide* and see what they have to offer.

I really like this theme deck. If you're cracking packs of *Eventide* as a newer player you can do a lot to make this strategy quite effective. If I were trying to find some commons that would take this strategy up a notch, I'd look for a four-of play set of [Edge of the Divinity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+the+Divinity), some [Nightsky Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightsky+Mimic)s, and a couple more [Unmake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unmake)s. It's important not to play fewer than 20 creatures in a deck like this, though, so make sure you don't cut too many cards that can attack and block.



![Edge of the Divinity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Edge+of+the+Divinity)

![Divinity of Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Divinity+of+Pride)

If you can find them, more copies of [Divinity of Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divinity+of+Pride) would really make the deck awesome.

"Sidestep" is probably my favorite of the *Eventide* theme decks. I've always been blue-red mage at heart. Something about playing card draw and burn in the same deck really gets me going. I'd like to add some more efficient burn to this deck, though, so a few [Puncture Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puncture+Bolt)s and [Puncture Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puncture+Blast)s would be very welcome. If you've cracked any *Tenth Edition* you might have been lucky enough to open a [Tidings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidings) or two, and those really help the cause of a blue-red deck.




![Puncture Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Puncture+Blast)

![Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Greater+Gargadon)

If you're looking for rares to add, you might be able to find a few [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon)s that will go really well with your [Dominus of Fealty](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dominus+of+Fealty) and your [Unwilling Recruit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unwilling+Recruit)s. I'm going to stress that you need to have at least 20 creatures again, because it's probably more important in a deck like this where you're running 3 [Clout of the Dominus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clout+of+the+Dominus)—super-powerful Auras that are super-terrible if you don't have targets for them.

Superabundance is a lot of fun to play. It has so many cantrips (cards that draw a card in addition to having some effect). One of the most annoying things that can happen in a game of **Magic** is running out of cards. This deck avoids that by replacing many of the cards it plays. I definitely want to add another [Snakeform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) to the deck.



![Snakeform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Snakeform)

It's important to bluff early and often when you're playing "Superabundance." After a game or two, your opponent will be trying to play around your [Invert the Skies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invert+the+Skies), your [Snakeform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform)s, and your [Cerulean Wisps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerulean+Wisps). If you have a 2/2 in play and your opponent has an 8/8, a lot of the time just leaving three mana open will be enough to make your opponent not attack you.


### Ask and Ye Shall Receive


[![Trash_for_Treasure](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/MRD/Trash_for_Treasure.jpg)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Trash%2Bfor%2BTreasure)When you've just played [Friday Night Magic](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=events/magic/fnm) at your local store and everyone is packing up their fresh Sealed or Draft decks after four rounds of battle, it never hurts to ask the people you've spoken to throughout the night if they have any of the commons you're looking for to fill out your theme deck. A lot of players have been playing the game for years, and these commons will just get lost in a pile of laundry, food, or empty Red Bull cans. One man's trash is another man's treasure!
