Scatterbrain
============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on November 10, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











My mind's hopping all over the place lately. I can't seem to aim its frenetic beam into one place of focus. By "I," I mean it, of course. And by it, I mean the mind. See what I mean? Argh. NeverI.

This is what you get for tuning in every week, you know. I reserve the right to slobber forth my internal froth at times. The occasional moments where this whole façade slips, and the random takes over, fueled by stress and deadlines. As for those who might have stumbled into "my diabolical laboratory" from the blistering cold, well, apparently you have no idea what's going on. The skinny is that I usually build three-ish decks of potent combinations and gibber all the while. Usually I'm bound to the most recent release for material, and am occasionally shackled to a theme week.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl168_vedalkenshackles.jpg)[Vedalken Shackles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) | Art by [Mark Zug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Mark+Zug%22%5D)

With no such cuffs winking from my wrists this week, I am faced with a barren wasteland of a white Microsoft Word page, with only these words trekking onward so far. They're searching for an ancient sentence of great power, said by mystics of old to be hidden somewhere in this infinite blank. Legend foretells of its mighty article-ending ability. Using my words as delegates, I have embarked upon this quest.

What is this mythic sentence? Well, if I don't quit this insane narrative, you'll never find out, because you've clicked away in annoyance. I'll shut up now, and get on with my decks... the first of which comes at popular request, and stems from [last week's prompt](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/167). I wrote about a fun morph combo deck that I actually play in real life, but due to a self-imposed restriction (I do things like that) I couldn't reveal the true list. In addition, my list is linked thematically to a film. My hints were pretty bald, and many got it right: It's an *Inception* theme deck. (Although, a side of applesauce to the lone left-field guess of *Being John Malkovich*.)

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl168_inception.jpg)  
For reference, here's the combo again. You control [Ixidor, Reality Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixidor%2C+Reality+Sculptor); four morphs; and six lands, and your opponent has a creature. Have Ixidor flip up [Chromeshell Crab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromeshell+Crab), and exchange your face-down creature for his or her creature.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

When I first uncovered this combo, I was jolted by the barbed surprise it contains. You see, at this point, your opponent controls a creature while remaining clueless of its identity. What does it mean for her to look at it? Is she reminded of an idea long since buried, or is it a planted ruse? Either way, I use Ixidor to flip up the [Unstable Hulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unstable+Hulk), and your opponent therefore *breaks into her own mind.* (Oh, and I use two [Weaver of Lies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weaver+of+Lies) to gain infinite turns. Read [last week's article](/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/167) for clearer details.)



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The genesis of this combo gave off vivid waves, and I recalled pondering similar depths while watching the film *Inception*. It's a mental behemoth of a movie, equal parts daring and dazzling. I had only smiled at the concept of a theme deck before, but this combo gave me a great reason to attempt one.

Some choices are obvious, like [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep), [Dream Fracture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Fracture), and [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice). [Lonely Sandbar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lonely+Sandbar) is the beach from the opening scene. [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) is certainly fun to spin once I've achieved infinite turns (*Will it topple?)*.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl168_senseisdiviningtop.jpg)[Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) | Art by [Michael Sutfin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Michael+Sutfin%22%5D)

Others require more explanation. [Opportunity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opportunity) represents Saito, as he provides Cobb's one chance to get back home. Cobb and Ariadne are the [Hada Spy Patrol](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hada+Spy+Patrol), the victory condition (and look—three levels!). [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) provides the "kicks". [Echo Tracer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echo+Tracer) and [Weaver of Lies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weaver+of+Lies) are tougher to justify as part of the theme. I've made the patchwork call that they're Arthur and Eames, respectively. The legendary [Ixidor, Reality Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixidor%2C+Reality+Sculptor) acts as Christopher Nolan himself, pulling the strings of his masterpiece.

It's there to actively antagonize me; if she's out when I go off, I'll get decked! And her thought-provoking flavor text offers an interesting interpretation of the film.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dreamborn+Muse)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamborn+Muse)  
It's one of my prouder moments in deck building, if I may say so. Pulling it off fills me with joy.

The Most Tangled Knot
There we go. That gimmick was bursting through its restraints, so I had to finally release it. Anyway, [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot). It's awesome. Not news, right? [Algae Gharial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial) was a former go-to for taking advantage of infinite death, but [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) one-ups it. A fat Gharial could be chump-blocked all day long; a [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) can pick up a [Behemoth Sledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge) and thump your opponent directly.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lumberknot)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot)  
How should I assemble a combo machine of infinite death, preferably using an intriguing card from *Innistrad*? Hmm... [Mentor of the Meek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek) gives me that feeling of sliding into lunacy, so I'll start with that. If I set up a way to have creatures with power 2 or less enter the battlefield over and over, I could draw lots of cards. But... what if those draws provided tokens themselves, automatically? Ridiculous notion, right? Nah.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mentor+of+the+Meek)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek)  
My favorite cycle of all time is [the Words enchantments](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?name=+%5Bwords%5D+%5Bof%5Damp;set=%5B%22Onslaught%22%5D) from *Onslaught*. From ability to implied flavor, this cycle sunk into me and has remained ever since. There are so many possible interactions, of course, but the one I'll pick for this situation is [Words of Wilding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wilding). By paying ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif), I can skip a draw to make a 2/2 Bear. [Mentor of the Meek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek) triggers and demands ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif) for another draw. I smell the makings of an engine, but I'll need an altar of some sort. Ashnod steps in with [her famed contribution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=her+famed+contribution) to Johnnyness. Now I can sacrifice the Bear for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif) in response to the Mentor's trigger, to pay for the draw and the subsequent Bearifying of the draw. A couple eons of this and my [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) becomes beefy enough to swing for the win.

|  |  |

The deck's other cards provide synergy. [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) finds a land, is Meek, and pumps up [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot). [Llanowar Augur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Augur) provides early weenie defense and another trample outlet for its Knotty friend. The aforementioned [Behemoth Sledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge) is one of a small assortment of weapons in the deck. [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) is pretty darn solid and triggers [Mentor of the Meek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek), as does [Skinwing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinwing) (which provides additional evasion for [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot)). Finding these Equipment is none other than... [Steelshaper Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steelshaper+Apprentice)? I wanted to avoid using the stupendously busted [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), and the Apprentice actually fits into this deck better, since it and the Mentor make for an unwieldy card draw machine. A singleton [Congregation at Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Congregation+at+Dawn) can find combo pieces or other creatures I haven't yet mentioned.

Another Contest!?
I've decided to run another contest, for a multitude of reasons. You all seem to like them, judging by the amount of participation, and I know I like them for nebulous reasons of my own. For instance, this week I would have loved to plug in a barrage of reader decks and swivel over to my other, piling responsibilities.

Just like the last time I announced one, I will present a choice between two contest ideas, and if either of them strikes your fancy, you might vote in the poll below. The first contest contestant (...) is coming off two consecutive defeats: the Collector Number contest! As I have previously described it: "Pick two collector numbers. (The collector numbers can be found near the bottom of each **Magic** card.) Build a deck using only those two collector numbers. For special notice, use only one collector number."

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl101_number.jpg)  
The other idea riffs off my *Inception* deck in a general sort of way: Build a theme or tribute deck. You can choose any film, book, album, game, story, person, or whatever to build around. However, I must stress: These decks ideally work when there's an ironclad resemblance to the source material within the deck's game play. *Translate* what the deck does and work backwards from there.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl168_theme.jpg)  
I'm curious to see which will win your attention. However, with one deck left for me to build, I thought I'd anticipate a possible (if not probable) result and actually build a collector number deck. This way I will have provided two examples of each contest. I know by doing so I'd basically use up a number, but oh well. This number's card pool was diverse and strange enough that I wound up with two decks, which is sometimes the case with weird restrictions like this. If you've been paying rapt attention during my time here in the Lab, you should be able to guess my number in a snap.

With all the death going on, [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest) might actually make a Demon or two.
