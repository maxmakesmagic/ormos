*Choose* *two* decks to talk about? Absolutely!



How many new *Dragons of Tarkir* cards featured between the final versions of them? Why, *two* of course!



What does the card that both decks share do? *Choose two* of something!



I somehow managed to make my own complicated Venn Diagram of choosing, just for this article. Not to mention, I'm not even using any of the Commands as you might expect! No, the card I'm using is a little more of a surprising choose two than the Commands.



Ready to dive into exactly what's going on here? Well, perhaps you should *choose* *to* continue onward!

Collected Persistence
=====================



A card that has generated tons of excitement since it was first revealed is a little green instant from *Dragons of Tarkir*: [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company).



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Collected+Company)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company)




Now, in Standard there are a few cool things you can do with this card. However, you *really* have to build around it to make it work. And really, in Standard there just aren't quite enough efficient, low-mana-cost creatures to play in most of your green decks. And it's very important that [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) can hit two creatures when you cast it. While it might very well have a space, I haven't quite seen what I'm looking for in Standard deck with it.



Modern is an entirely different story.



Modern is a format built on a foundation of cheap, efficient creatures! If there was ever a place for a spell that cheats a bunch of cheap creatures onto the battlefield, Modern would certainly be it.



Like, for example, this powerful take on an old favorite:

Many of you might be familiar with the Melira-Persist combo, especially if you played with the [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks (may they rest in peace) of days past. But if you aren't quite sure how it works, the basic premise is this:




> 
> **Step 1:** Have [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast); a [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) or [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks); and a way to repeatedly sacrifice creatures on the battlefield.
> 
> 
> 
> **Step 2:** Sacrifice your persist creature to your sacrifice outlet.
> 
> 
> 
> **Step 3:** The persist creature comes back and triggers its "enters the battlefield" effect—and since Melira is in play, no -1/-1 counter is put on the creature. Since persist tracks itself by whether there's a counter or not, you can repeat steps 1-3 as many times as you want and gain millions of life or deal millions of damage to the opponent.
> 
> 
> 



All it takes is three creatures.



Three small creatures.



Three creatures that (Redcap aside) have a converted mana cost of 3 or less.



Sounds like an absolutely perfect fit for [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company)!



The odds you'll draw one or two of these creatures on your own is very high. Then, a single [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) can help you set up the rest of the combo—and all at instant speed. Even if you have nothing else on the battlefield, it's very easy to cast a [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) during your opponent's end step, untap, cast the third piece, and immediately win the game.



Now, that's a pretty strong addition in and of itself. However, it isn't the only big tool this deck picked up out of *Dragons of Tarkir*. Another tremendous card in the set for this deck is [Anafenza, Kin-Tree Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit):



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit)




Now, why is this good? Well, there's a slightly obscure rule in *Magic* that happens to be extraordinarily relevant here: if you put a +1/+1 counter on a creature that has a -1/-1 counter on it, the two counters equal out and remove each other.



Normally, that doesn't mean much except for that it's easier to see what's happening on the battlefield. However, in the case of persist where it's tracked with a -1/-1 counter, if you can remove that counter then you can persist again!



If you replace Melira with Anafenza in the three-card combo, when you sacrifice your persist creature and it returns Anafenza will trigger to put a counter on the creature with the lowest toughness—which, your infect opponent's [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth)'s aside, can always be your persist creature since both Finks and Redcap come back with 1 toughness. The +1/+1 counter obliterates the -1/-1 counter of the persist and the process begins anew, letting you "go infinite" and win the game.



Ah, music to my combo-loving ears.



What would I want to tweak out of a deck like this? Well, a lot of the pieces need to be there. However, there are still some modifications I would make.



[Congregation at Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Congregation+at+Dawn) always sounds great in theory…but is a lot less effectual in practice. You're spending a card and three mana to generate nothing on the board, so it's kind of slow. And then there's all kinds of random downsides, like not being able to use a fetch land until after you've drawn all the cards you want. While it is cute to set up a [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), I'd rather just have four [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling)s as an option that puts the creature onto the battlefield immediately.



Additionally, I would look at the one-of Chords targets and tweak them a bit. I don't think you really need [Orzhov Pontiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff) or [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), main deck. However, I would love to have a single [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) to find. Not only can you Chord for it to pick up a deceased part of your combo, but the Witness is also always a great card to see off of [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), since you can return the same [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) that found the Witness!



I'd also certainly play one [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) as another sacrifice outlet that also happens to be resilient to removal. With those small tweaks made, that brings this deck to:

Collected Beats
===============



In addition to being a card that finds exactly the pieces you're looking for, [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) also "cheats" creatures onto the battlefield.



Now, normally this just means some mana savings and a bit of a two-for-one. You ended up ahead on card and tempo advantage, or as we saw with the last deck, ahead on "game advantage" as you kill them with your freshly assembled combo.



But this next deck—well, it takes the idea of cheating creatures onto the battlefield really to heart.

Well, like I said—this deck likes to cheat things onto the battlefield.



Thanks to [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) (and its partner in crime, [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial)) you can entirely skirt the restrictions around all of these otherwise undercosted cards.



Need to use mana from creatures to pay for [Myr Superion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion)? Nah, I'm good—I'll just put it onto the battlefield for free with [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company).



[Epochrasite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Epochrasite) needs to die and then suspend to come back as a 4/4? Ain't nobody got time for that! How about I just tap my [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) with 2 counters instead?



While almost all of this deck's strong creatures require being cast in a very specific way, this deck eschews those restrictions entirely. If you've ever wanted to just drop a pair of [Skaab Ruinator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Ruinator)s on your opponent at the end of their turn, then this is the deck for you!



The one change I know I want to make is upping the number of mana creatures. Not only does this help you cast [Myr Superion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion) and [Talara's Battalion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talara%27s+Battalion) if you don't have the [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) for them, it also speeds you up since you really want to cast [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) on turn three if you can.



While [Illusory Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusory+Angel) isn't quite strong enough to make the cut, [Skaab Ruinator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Ruinator) is a really powerful card both cheated onto the battlefield early and also as added gas later on. While splashing a double-blue card is a little odd, you aren't actually paying the mana cost of this card very often anyway, and so I'd kind of like a third copy.



[Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate) is a little weak here—while you do really need Company or Vial to have this deck work, you also can't really afford to spend the time trying to find it. It's crucial you draw them naturally and mulligan aggressively for them.



With those changes made, that brings us to:

This deck is a completely wild ride to play with. While it's a bit inconsistent, the games where you lead with a turn one [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) can be absolutely brutal as soon your opponent is facing down an army of oversized creatures far earlier than they anticipated. I mean, who needs [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) when you have [Myr Superion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion)?



Have fun with this deck!
