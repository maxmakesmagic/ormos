The December 9, 2016 Update
===========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on December 9, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on December 9, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by the DailyMTG holiday schedule.


We're getting close, so I just want to make sure you're all aware. On December 19, we go into "Best of" mode, where we'll be re-running the best DailyMTG articles of the year. During that time, the Daily *Magic* Update will also be on hiatus, as I lounge around mostly figuring out how to jam [Cloudblazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer) into all of my Commander decks. Daily will resume its normal publishing schedule on January 2, and the Update will return on January 3.


Today's Musts
=============


1. [Best of 2016 Awards](http://collected.company/2016/12/08/best-of-2016-awards-111/) | Masters of Modern | Alex Kessler and Ben Bateman

2016 was a weird year for the world at large, but in *Magic* there were a ton of things worth celebrating. And discussing. And sometimes whistling while ignoring completely. Kessler and Bateman cover all of it, combing through the year month-by-month in the world of Modern to pick the best of the year. But it's Modern-centric, so I can't even really hope for a [Cloudblazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer) plug or even a whisper of [Panharmonicon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panharmonicon).


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [The Return of Mardu](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/34176_The-Return-Of-Mardu.html) | StarCityGames.com | Adrian Sullivan

Sullivan told you so, and he's here to tell you he told you so. And why, too.


1. [Heart of Kiran and Three New Planeswalkers](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/heart-of-kiran/) | Top Level Podcast | Mike Flores and Patrick Chapin

The Top Level guys explore what Heart of Kiran means for Standard while also covering the new planeswalkers who might be crewing it.


1. [Have No Fear](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/thegirlfriendbracket-12092016-the-girlfriend-bracket-82-have-no-fear/) | The Girlfriend Bracket | Erin Campbell, Hallie Santo, Katie Neal, and Kriz Schultz

Grand Prix, SCG Opens, and local game stores, oh my! The women of the Girlfriend Bracket talk all of that, plus a [No Fear challenge](https://backtablesmtg.wordpress.com/2016/12/08/krizs-no-fear-challenge/) from Kriz.


1. [Living Out of a Suitcase: My Real Superpower](http://www.mtgmintcard.com/articles/writers/chapman-sim/living-out-of-a-suitcase-my-real-superpower) | MTGMintCard | Chapman Sim

This one is a bit less about *Magic* and a bit more about the philosophy of travel that can often go with being on the road for *Magic* events. The article is personal, introspective, and chock full of advice for the potential world traveler. It's a bit different from the normal offering, but, hey, that's the best kind of article to highlight.


1. [Puzzling Improvements](http://www.hareruyamtg.com/article/en/category/detail/44) | Hareruya | Pierre Dagen

Dagen thinks the way to be a great *Magic* player is to solve puzzles—and he talks about how to do just that.


1. [Magical Christmas Land](http://manadeprived.com/men-modo-15-magical-christmas-land/) | Men from MODO | David Savill and Travis Sowers

Even though they start off with a groan-worthy joke ("Say hello, Travis." "Hello, Travis!"), 'tis the time of year to get all festive, so give this Christmas-themed episode a chance. I mean, they talk about [Panharmonicon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panharmonicon) right off the bat, so that's enough of a present for me.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. Red-Green...Land Destruction? That's a thing?

Apparently so! The deck below went just 2-2 at a Standard Showdown, but man those two who lost to it must have been frustrated. R&D has worked to get land destruction out of Standard, but where there's a will, there's a way.


This just shows you that you can play whatever you want. Sure this player went 2-2, but I guarantee they were cackling with every destroyed land. You do you, land destruction person. The Standard Showdown is a pretty good spot for that.
