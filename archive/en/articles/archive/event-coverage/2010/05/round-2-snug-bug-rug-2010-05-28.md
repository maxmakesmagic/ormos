Round 2: Snug as a BUG in a RUG
===============================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 28, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_DaveGuskin.jpg)
By Dave Guskin











### Ben Rubin (Green-Blue-Black Landfall) vs. Josh Utter-Leyton (Blue-Red-Green Combo)


With such a quick match between Ruel and Martell, there was plenty of time to reveal the secret second feature match between Channel[Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball).com superstar Josh Utter-Leyton and Pro Tour Hall of Famer Ben Rubin. Utter-Leyton was up a game with his Blue-Red-Green control deck versus Rubin's Green-Blue-Black landfall control-combo deck, which they described as "a good game, until the [Comet Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm) for 10." A quick turn three Jace followed by a turn four [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) gave Utter-Leyton a huge land advantage, so that he was ready on the last possible turn with a gigantic [Comet Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm) to take down [Avenger of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) and all of its Plant brethren.


Josh Utter-Leyton 1, Ben Rubin 0


### Game 2


Both competitors led with [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths), but Rubin was first into the nonland portion of the battlefield with [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra). Utter-Leyton took an opportunity to increase his card quality by fetching away the top cards into [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore), [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). The arms race for mana acceleration continued as Rubin went [Verdant Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) into [Harrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow), but had no follow—uponly the beats, bringing the life totals to 20-17 his favor.


![Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=LOTUS+COBRA)
Utter-Leyton had his own [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), and used the mini-boost in mana to make its twin. Rubin wasn't comfortable with that much potential on the other side of the table, so he end-stepped a [Vendetta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendetta) on the second Cobra. On his turn, he fetched again, and with five lands and two Cobra mana, he made an [Avenger of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) with five Plant buddies.


Utter-Leyton calmly untapped and cast [Vapor Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snare) to snare himself his very own Avenger. Rubin had nothing but the five Plants, so he passed, and Utter-Leyton began the beatdown with the Avenger. Rubin bashed back with an activated [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit), mostly evening the score at 13–14 to Utter-Leyton's advantage. 


Utter-Leyton cast a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and used the [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) ability to continue to press Rubin, but had no response to Rubin's [Rampaging Baloths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Baloths) and two accompanying Beasts off a fetch.


With the life totals in his favor with 12–15, Utter-Leyton decided to continue to improve his game with an [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) and a free [Kazandu Refuge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Refuge) (and life point) off the top. He left his stolen Avenger back to block any Beasts that might rumble over.


Unfortunately for Josh, Ben's turn was full of excitement: another swing from the [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) assassinated Jace, and then a Jace of his own bounced the Avenger back to his hand. Content to ride out his massive turn-by-turn advantage from the Baloths, Jace, and the soon-to-be-replayed Avenger, Rubin passed it back to Utter-Leyton.


Utter-Leyton seemed out of excitement of his own, merely casting another [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) and playing more lands. When Rubin attempted to recast his Avenger, though, Utter-Leyton was ready with [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive) resetting his [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths). Rubin was unfazed, however, activating Jace's [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) ability, making another Beast off of a land drop, and then crashing over for 14. Utter-Leyton, with only the meager Oracle for defense, conceded before taking the Beastly damage on the chin, evening up the duel at one apiece.


Josh Utter-Leyton 1, Ben Rubin 1


### Game 3


The players sideboarded in silence, with only a brief break for confirmation about how much time remained in the round—only ten minutes. Utter-Leyton, now on the play and looking to finish his opponent quickly, found his opener and replacements both inadequate and mulliganed down to five. He led with a [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths), attempting to regain some measure of control over his draw step. Rubin matched the Depths with his own.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Oracle of Mul Daya | Growth Spasm |

Utter-Leyton broke a fetch for a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and a follow-up [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), but Rubin had the appropriate counter in [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Vendetta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendetta). Utter-Leyton had only a [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine), while Rubin continued to ramp with a [Growth Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spasm) and resulting Eldrazi Spawn token.


Luckily, Utter-Leyton's sorcerous apprentice [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) arrived to dig him out of his mulligan woes, and he passed after an activation of Jace's [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) ability. Jace looked to be not long for this game with an [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) arriving and revealing another copy of Jace on Rubin's deck. Rubin used [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths) again to rearrange slightly, garnering another land for his trouble and resetting Jace on top.


Utter-Leyton, with his own Jace's time running out, again activated the planewalker's 0: ability and found [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) for Rubin's [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya). He finished his turn with an Oracle of his own.


Rubin cast [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) and used the additional mana and his Spawn token to bin the two Jaces. He then cast [Vendetta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendetta) on Utter-Leyton's Oracle and passed, with the life totals at Rubin 17-19 Utter-Leyton. The board was now mostly clear, and when Utter-Leyton added his own Cobra, it looked like a Snake race might be on. Rubin looked to step up the pressure with an [Ob Nixilis, the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ob+Nixilis%2C+the+Fallen) and a [Khalni Garden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden), draining Utter-Leyton to 14.


![Ob Nixilis, the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ob+Nixilis%2C+the+Fallen)  
Utter-Leyton had a nigh-perfect answer, however, with [Vapor Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snare). Rubin responded by breaking his remaining fetch land, and the life totals went to 14-11 after a Cobra attack by Utter-Leyton.


The one-upmanship continued with Rubin's [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) on the [Vapor Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snare). [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) and his own Cobra swung through for five, taking the life totals to 14-6 in Rubin's favor. Another two draw steps yielded no ancient, powerful magics for Utter-Leyton, and after chumping [Ob Nixilis, the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ob%2BNixilis%252C%2Bthe%2BFallen) and dropping to 1, Utter-Leyton extended the hand.


Utter-Leyton 1 – 2 Rubin
