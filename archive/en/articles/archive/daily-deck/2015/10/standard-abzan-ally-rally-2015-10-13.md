Standard Abzan Ally Rally
=========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on October 13, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Ever since I saw the cards [March from the Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+from+the+Tomb) and [Kalastria Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalastria+Healer) in a Future Future League Standard deck, I knew there was something special with Allies in *Battle for Zendikar* Standard. The synergy with Allies in the new format is just too strong to ignore. Josh Bartling also discovered that synergy and took a very similar deck to a Top 4 finish at an StarCityGames.com Invitational Qualifier event last weekend.


The power in this deck comes from [Kalastria Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalastria+Healer). The Healer will drain 1 life from your opponent whenever it or another Ally enters the battlefield under your control, and the life swings that this deck creates allows you to win games without ever attacking. That may seem difficult coming from a measly 1/2, but combined with other synergistic Allies and a way to return them all to the battlefield from the graveyard, it's pretty easy.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kalastria+Healer)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalastria+Healer)
The other card that makes this deck tick is [Zulaport Cutthroat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zulaport+Cutthroat). This guy, similar to the powerful [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) that came before it, will make your opponent think twice before killing any of your creatures. There will come a point where your opponent will be at a low enough life total that attacking into your army of Allies will be deadly.


You also need a way to bring all of your Allies back to the battlefield at once. Josh chose to use [Rally the Ancestors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors) to do this, but [March from the Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+from+the+Tomb) from *Battle for Zendikar* does this just as well. [Rally the Ancestors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors) is an instant, which makes it more difficult to play around, but [March from the Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+from+the+Tomb) will make your Allies stick around for much longer. Both cards accomplish the same goal: They return your [Kalastria Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalastria+Healer)s and all of your Allies to play. The more Healers you have, the more life your opponent will lose. A full four healers will drain your opponent for 16 life, and any other Ally that you may return will be even more.


This is a pretty cool combo deck that can win games from different angles. Congrats to Josh for his IQ Top 4.
