Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Pascal Maynard's Bant Eldrazi—Grand Prix Los Angeles Top 8](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpla16/top-8-decklists-2016-05-22)

Oh, I'm sorry. Did you think the Eldrazi would go away just because we took their Eye?


Pascal Maynard begs to differ, making the Top 8 of [Grand Prix Los Angeles](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpla16) on the back of the Eldrazi Menace That Once Was. Now considerably slower, but picking up [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to help with the loss of [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin), this is the Eldrazi deck R&D envisioned when it banned only half of the Eldrazi lands available in Modern. With [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) still available, Maynard was able to smash and disrupt his way to the Top 8. He even had the [Drowner of Hope](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) and [Eldrazi Displacer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Displacer) combo to drown out opponents who tried to play fair. For anyone playing unfair, [Grafdigger's Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage), [Stubborn Denial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stubborn+Denial), [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), and a few anti-artifact spells were ready and waiting in his sideboard.
