Day 1 Coverage
==============



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 20, 2010 










![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/blurb_picture.jpg)  
Day one of German Nationals is in the virtual books. Only one player stands undefeated after four rounds of Standard and three rounds of M11 draft. Sebastian Knörr, after finishing in 20th place in Grand Prix-Madrid this year, is the one 21-point player to start day two with an unblemished record. Close behind is Carsten Schäfer. Jörg Unfried, the last remaining member of Team Germany at the infamous 2006 World Championfails, also stands up there with six others on 18 points.


Even more interesting than the top pod tomorrow morning will be draft table number two. With Florian Pils, Tobias Gräfensteiner, Denis Sinner and Dennis Johannsen making up one half of that pod, we will keep a close eye on that pod come Saturday.


With UK and US Nationals also happening this weekend, the metagame comparisons are particularly interesting. Jund topped the German charts, making up 17 percent of the field, followed by U/W Control and Naya decks, the vast majority of them with the ubiqitous Fauna Shaman. The UK turned affairs around, putting Naya in the top spot and Jund in third. 


Also making waves in Germany are three fairly new decks. The first is a list deck that Zvi Mowshowitz shipped across the Atlantic to good effect for a handful of players here. The second deck is based around Pyromancer Ascension with a new twist: With a sideboard that lets the deck switch to [Polymorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph+)  combo, it adds [Spawning Breath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Breath+)  for extra oomph. And the third deck is a [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman)-enabled DredgeVine deck, creatures only and quite, quite fun.


Join us tomorrow when we continue our coverage from German Nationals 2010 in Aschaffenburg! Meanwhile, enjoy the coverage of US Nationals, providing a continuous stream of coverage due to the time zone difference.

Feature Match Round 1 – Simon Görtzen vs Klaus Jöns
---------------------------------------------------

*by Hanno Terbuyken*
When the pairings for round one were announced, a cry of surprise was heard among the players when they heard the feature match. Simon Görtzen is Germany's most recent Pro Tour Winner, Klaus Jöns a Nationals mainstay who has had his time on the Grand Prix circuit and is a well-known figure in Germany's ***Magic*** scene.


Both players knew exactly what their opponents played, as they had discussed their respective decks just the previous night. Görtzen had brought Valakut Ramp to the table and was able to recite Jöns' list card by card as the players shuffled up for game one. It was a Bant-colored deck centered on the duo of [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), a list that Klaus Jöns had received from Zvi Mowshowitz who is playing the same deck in US Nationals, happening this weekend, too.


### Game 1:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/rd1_klaus_joens.jpg)*Klaus Jöns*Jöns decided to keep a one-land hand on the play, knowing that Görtzen would not be able to disrupt him significantly. He lead off with [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), holding [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) at the ready to ward off Görtzen's first attempt at mana acceleration.


With three [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s in his deck, Jöns decided to counter Görtzen's [Explore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore), but assuming that Görtzen held [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) back. [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) joined Jön's side of the battlefield, while Görtzen had nothing but a fourth land in his fourth turn. Judging by the cards on the table, the momentum was on Jön's side, but the former motormouth fell quiet as he very well knew that Görtzen's Ramp deck could explode at any minute.


Jöns studied Görtzen's six cards in hand. "Double [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) would be very broken", Jöns quipped. He activated [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), discarding [Admonition Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Admonition+Angel), searching for [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and playing the mighty green monster. On 16 life, he considered that [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) from Görtzen could nearly kill him. The anticipated double [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) from Görtzen did indeed happen at end of turn as foretold.


But then Görtzen untapped... and all he did was Avengers of Zendikar. Jöns knew he had to finish Görtzen now or never. [Eldrazi Conscription](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Conscription) on [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) who gained protection from green through [Sejiri Steppe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sejiri+Steppe), and together with the rest of Jön's army that was plenty of damage to kill Simon Görtzen.


### Game 2:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/rd1_simon_goertzen.jpg)*Simon Görtzen*The two friends bantered as Görtzen threatened Jöns with [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm). "You can't afford that one-land hand with [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm)", said Görtzen – and kicked off game two with a mulligan. "That is mighty indeed", he commented as he went to five cards. Two times in a row he had not found green mana in his opener. And with all the mana acceleration in his deck relying on [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s, he would not risk a hand without trees.


However, his first land drop turned out to be [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle). Then [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), but [Explore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) without a land to go with it signalled a dearth of actual lands in Simon's hand. [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) helped matters along, but as Jöns was developing with [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), Görtzen needed to get his wiggle on. Missing his fourth land drop altogether didn't help the mana-cled PT champion at all. Jöns activated [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) to get [Sovereigns of Lost Alara](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sovereigns+of+Lost+Alara) in his hand and to play the Ghost. [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) attacked, received [Eldrazi Conscription](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Conscription) and Simon Görtzen went to three. 


However, before the match ended, Klaus decided to give up the match in Simon's favor. Why? Jöns explained: "It is always a pain to get paired against a testing partner and friend in the first round of Nationals. I do not need Pro Points this year, and Simon is trying for Pro Level 8 this year." Also, Klaus explained that he had not drafted a single set of M11 yet, and he did not see a chance for him to make Top 4 and win a trip to Worlds in Chiba.


Simon Görtzen 2 – 0 Klaus Jöns



  

Friday, 12:30 p.m. – Yesterday's Winners
----------------------------------------

*by Tobias Henke*
As always, Thursday saw the so-called „grinders", the [Last Chance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Chance) Qualifiers. In each, 32 players entered and played five rounds of single elimination, with the winner receiving the coveted prize of a last-minute Nationals invite.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/blog_grinder_winners_sutor_filler.jpg)*Emanuel Sutor & Michael Filler*Below are the eight deck lists, which won nine of those tournaments. Wait? Eight? Nine? There is actually a pretty sweet story behind the first list. In the final of the first grinder, Emanuel Sutor beat Michael Filler. Then Filler took Stutor's deck, joined the next grinder and *then actually won that tournament!*

Feature Match Round 2 – Daniel Gräfensteiner vs. Florian Koch
-------------------------------------------------------------

*by Tobias Henke*
Florian Koch won Grand Prix–Lyon this year, Daniel Gräfensteiner was last seen on the big stage in Rome last year, making Top 8 at Pro Tour–San Diego. They are certainly no strangers to the feature match area, though this may well be the last chance this weekend for one of them as both started with a disappointing first-round loss.


Not much happened for a couple of turns: Gräfensteiner had [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) to go along with his [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s and [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, Koch had [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) with [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s and [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s. On turn four Gräfensteiner cast [Foresee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foresee) and found [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), while Koch accelerated up to seven mana with [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate) and another [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth).


Gräfensteiner cast [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), his [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), and then [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), to put the first counter on the enchantment. Koch summoned [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar), made seven Plant tokens, played a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), and passed the turn. End of turn Gräfensteiner hit Koch for two with [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning). A second [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) on his turn rather took down a token, after Gräfensteiner figured he would not be able to cast [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) just yet.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/rd2_daniel_graefensteiner.jpg)*Daniel Gräfensteiner*Still, his Ascension was now fully leveled and his deck was positively on fire. [See Beyond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+Beyond) was copied, then [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to shoot down the Avenger. All Koch was left with were six 1/2 Plant tokens. Not to sneer at, those Plants took Gräfensteiner down to 14 life. Then Koch laid [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) and cast [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate). Gräfensteiner fell to 11.


But certainly, at least now Gräfensteiner would have the [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) to seal the deal, wouldn't he? Apparently not. He went looking with a copied [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), but again no [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp). Next up, [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), once again copied by the Ascension... "Uh-oh." Gräfensteiner had no Warp and also not the three [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s he needed to kill Koch right away. Furthermore, he was now out of blue mana, so he couldn't really interfere as Valakut worked its ***Magic***. Another attack and one [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow) later the score stood at...


Daniel Gräfensteiner 0 – 1 Florian Koch


The second game went much quicker and also in a different direction. Gräfensteiner had [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) for Koch's [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and then [Khalni Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden) on turn three.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/rd2_florian_koch.jpg)*Florian Koch*"Oh no, not the [Polymorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph) morph sideboard!", Koch wailed.


"Well, who knows...", responded Gräfensteiner, trying to conceal what was all too apparent.


And sure enough, on his next turn Gräfensteiner had [Polymorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph), which resolved uncontested and quickly found [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). Koch sighed and conceded.


Daniel Gräfensteiner 1 – 1 Florian Koch


The final game began with a mulligan for Gräfensteiner and in completely different fashion from the previous two games. Both players played lands for the first four turns. Just lands. No [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), no [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), nothing. But while Koch simply had a bad draw, Gräfensteiner was actually up to something. At the end of Koch's turn he cast [Spawning Breath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Breath), then on his turn cast [Polymorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph) with [Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) as back-up.


Daniel Gräfensteiner 2 – 1 Florian Koch



  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/rd4_adrian_rosada.jpg)*Adrian Rosada*Adrian Rosada has won the largest Limited tournament of all time, Grand Prix–Paris last year, while Reinhold Kohl had a similarly busy year 2009, getting into the Top 8 of two Grand Prix, in Rotterdam and Brighton. Rosada is playing Raphael Levy's red-green token-aggro concoction from French Nationals, Kohl is sporting the new crazy, four-colored "Neo-[Dredge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge)" with [Hedron Crab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab), [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), [Sedraxis Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter), and [Extractor Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extractor+Demon) to go along with the ubiquitous [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine). Enough with the introductions, off to some ***Magic***!


The game began with [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) for Kohl and [Khalni Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden) plus [Nest Invader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nest+Invader) for Rosada. On turn three, a second [Nest Invader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nest+Invader) invaded the battlefield on Rosada's side. Meanwhile, Kohl did nothing but discard [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), searching for another one. On turn four, Kohl was still stuck in prep work, summoning [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and playing [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse). Rosada, however, was ready for the big move: He cast [Hellspark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental) and followed it up with a kicked [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker). Seven creatures attacked for a total of 15 damage, three of which were valiantly fended off by [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman). Nevertheless, 12 damage got through, leaving Kohl at precarious 5 life. At least the Shaman had discarded the second [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine).


Now it was Kohl's turn to bring some fat: [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) cascaded into [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), which resurrected two [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s. One of the Vines attacked for five, due to [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)'s exalted.


The theme of big swings continued: Rosada sacrificed one Eldrazi Spawn to have six mana, unearthed his [Hellspark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental), and cast [Sarkhan Vol](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol). The subsequent attack brought Kohl to 2. Kohl killed the Planeswalker in combat and summoned [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) as well as Aether Adept, thanks to his [Ancient Ziggurat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Ziggurat).

Friday, 5:25 p.m. – Drafting with Jan Schmidt
---------------------------------------------

*by Hanno Terbuyken*
Jan Schmidt had his breakout performance at last year's Grand Prix in Prague, where he took home the trophy. At German Nationals, he kicked off to an undefeated 4-0 start in the Standard portion of the tournament. But watching him build his first draft deck, the Limited champion of GP Prague was shaking his head in disgust. The first draft of Nationals, with the brand new suite of cards that ***Magic*** 2011 brought, did not go his way at all.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/draft_jan_schmidt.jpg)Schmidt had opened the first booster to a promising start, quickly putting Doomblade into his pile, with [Chandra's Outrage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Outrage) and [Armored Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Ascension) as the only viable alternatives. Little did he know that the black would shrivel up pretty soon. After Schmidt quickly shuffled the green and red cards in his second pick to the back and looked briefly at [Jace's Ingenuity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity), he chose [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor). Pick three had nothing to follow up, and Schmidt moved into white with [Assault Griffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Griffin) and [Armored Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Ascension) (over [Black Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Knight)).


[Steel Overseer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) in pick five remained the highlight of the first booster, as Schmidt had nothing of real value to come, with [Mystifying Maze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystifying+Maze), [Nether Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Horror), [Vulshok Berserker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Berserker) and two [Act of Treason](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason). 


Schmidt knew that his draft had gone awry as he looked through his initial pile of 14 cards. The deck he wanted, or even any deck, didn't look as if it came together. For the second booster Schmidt knew what he wanted, as he explained after the draft: "You can always get red, nobody wants it. Red-white is always my fallback plan."


With [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion), [Condemn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn) (over [Blinding Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage)), [Wild Griffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Griffin) and double [Mighty Leap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mighty+Leap) (where he briefly considered [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) and [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) instead), some white made his way into Schmidt's pile. Picks six to 14, though, remained uneventful, giving Schmidt highlights like double [Thunder Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunder+Strike) and [Volcanic Strength](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength).


"I have far too much pump", Schmidt would say afterwards, looking back at his draft, with two [Mighty Leap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mighty+Leap), two [Thunder Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunder+Strike), a [Volcanic Strength](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength) and an [Armored Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Ascension) in his pool.


And the lackluster draft continued in pack three. [Brittle Effigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brittle+Effigy) gave him another rare, but the rest of the third round of choices consisted of [Vulshok Berserker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Berserker)s, [Arc Runner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Runner)s and the like.


"I would have liked black better, but that didn't work out," Schmidt said. The packs that went through his hands seemed empty to him, "but I really don't know if the boosters really were as weak as they seemed to me." Little could he know that a [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) in his vicinity had cut off all the black, and his side of the table had another aggressive white drafter.


"I have a few good combos, like [Steel Overseer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) and [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion). I just need to draw the cards that I need when I need them." On the other hand, Schmidt knew: "I have comparatively many high-variance cards." He estimated the deck to be capable of at least a 1-2, maybe a 2-1 performance: 


"At least one game I will win in some fashion or other."
