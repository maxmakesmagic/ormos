Martin Müller, leader of the Danish National team that won the trophy at the World *Magic* Cup, opted for Amulet/Bloom. The deck aims to play multiple bouncelands with [Summer Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summer+Bloom), untap them with [Amulet of Vigor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amulet+of+Vigor), and then get [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) into play as early as turn two. His list is essentially a copy of Alexander Hayne's list from Grand Prix Charlotte, except for the replacement of [Tendo Ice Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendo+Ice+Bridge) by another basic land to improve against [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) and for the addition of [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) to the sideboard.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpcop15/COP15_vt_muller.jpg)  
*Martin Müller*



"I think it's a powerful deck, and I don't expect too much hate for Amulet Bloom in the metagame now," Müller said. "It didn't do very well last weekend in Charlotte, and I expect players to replace their [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) and [Fulminator Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) by cards that beat the new decks like Elves, [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), and [Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam)."

Simon Nielsen, who was also member of the Danish WMC team, registered the Grishoalbrand or [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) deck that broke out at Grand Prix Charlotte. "I haven't really faced a bad matchup yet in testing. The deck doesn't interact much and can get turn-two kills, just like the Amulet deck, but people are not prepared for it. I think it is one of the best decks until people adjust."



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpcop15/COP15_vt_nielsen.jpg)  
*Simon Nielsen*



"[Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) doesn't need to attack to win with this deck. The way the combo works is that you pay life to draw cards, at which point you will have probably drawn [Nourishing Shoal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nourishing+Shoal) and [Worldspine Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldspine+Wurm). You gain 11 and continue to draw your entire deck. At some point you exile two [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide)s to splice [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual) on [Nourishing Shoal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nourishing+Shoal), and eventually you get up to enough mana to cast or splice [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). [Borborygmos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borborygmos) Enraged enters the battlefield, and you should have enough lands to kill your opponent at that point."

Steve Hatto, national champion of Luxemburg, opted for [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift). "I love to play a controllish deck with a one-card-kill combo," he said. "And I love the challenge to play against a metagame full of [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon). I can beat it by bouncing it, countering it, or destroying it ... and sometimes not at all," he laughed.

Eduardo Sajgalik likes [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy). He piloted it to the Top 8 of Grand Prix Milan 2014, the previous European Modern Grand Prix, and he's back for more. Although many people discarded the deck after [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) was banned, Sajgalik (along with his testing partner João Choça) didn't give up.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpcop15/COP15_vt_sajgalik1.jpg)  
*Eduardo Sajgalik*



"I looked for a replacement that could refill your hand purely card-wise. I tried a lot of cards, but [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) was the one I ended up with because it had dredge. That was key for me. It allows you to mill a lot of your deck so you can find [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Fatestitcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatestitcher). And it adequately fills your hand size for Ascendancy."


This deck deserves a slightly lengthier explanation than the preceding ones, so I sat down with Sajgalik to talk about his deck. He was happy to explain how it ticked. "A turn-three kill that is hard to disrupt would go as follows. Turn one tapland. Turn two [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid). Turn three [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy). From there, any 1-mana spell you play will be free and will allow you to loot through your deck. At some point, you'll hit [Fatestitcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatestitcher) and discard it. You unearth it, and from that point any 1-mana spell you cast nets you one additional mana. Eventually you will hit [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), which allows you to return some fetch lands or lands you discarded. With the draw and discard from Ascendancy, you can set up a dredge chain, drawing and discarding Loams. Eventually, you'll hit more [Fatestitcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatestitcher)s, which will get bigger thanks to Ascendancy, and you attack for 20. You don't have to go infinite."


But what if the opponent gained infinite life or if you need to go off in your second main phase? "Then [Wheel of Sun and Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Sun+and+Moon) provides an alternative kill condition. So you combo off, and at the end of the combo you have a [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions), [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), and [Glittering Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glittering+Wish) in hand, with one card in your library. You [Glittering Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glittering+Wish) for [Wheel of Sun and Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Sun+and+Moon) and cast it targeting yourself. [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) mill them for two, [Wheel of Sun and Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Sun+and+Moon) puts it back, and then you redraw it with [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions). You can repeat this enough times to mill them out."



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpcop15/COP15_vt_sajgalik2.jpg)


How about sideboarding? "[Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) is against Jund and Burn, and [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) is there against all the blue decks. But I never board much because of the [Glittering Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glittering+Wish) sideboard. As for what to take out: If they play [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), then [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) come out. Other than that, I mostly shave one [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose), one [Sleight of Hand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand), one [Glittering Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glittering+Wish), one [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence), and/or one [Slaughter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter) Pact, depending on the match-up."


What are good and bad match-ups of the deck? "Jund and Burn are bad match-ups. If I could pick a card to never see, it's [Eidolon of the Great Revel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+the+Great+Revel). But Tron and Affinity are amazing matchups. In general, any deck that tries to win on turn four and doesn't interact much with you is a really good matchup."


Did Sajgalik consider the deck a good choice for Modern right now? "I think the deck is definitely playable in Modern. You can never play it online, only in real life. There are so many triggers, it's extremely unforgiving, and you have to know exactly how to play every match-up. But it's inherently powerful, extremely potent, and it kills on turn four consistently. Many people also don't know how to play against this deck because they've never seen it. As long as no one expects it, it's fine."
