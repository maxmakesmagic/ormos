### DAILY DECK


Just a Little Short
===================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)

Adam Prosak

From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer.


September 9, 2014
 










[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tarmogoyf)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)
As any tournament veteran can tell you, 9th place is often the most heartbreaking finish. You are in contention for the entire tournament, just to fall one place short of playing in the elimination rounds. Often, the difference comes down to tiebreakers, which amplifies the "Oh so close" feeling.


This weekend, Kyle Abell had the misfortune of coming in 9th place...twice. At the [StarCityGames](http://www.starcitygames.com/) Open in St. Louis, Kyle finished 9th in both the Standard tournament on Saturday and the Legacy tournament on Sunday. Kyle played Jund Planeswalkers in Standard and then this Sultai Delver deck in Legacy.


This deck, like all Legacy decks based off [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), functions primarily as a tempo deck. Deploy a cheap threat or two and then stunt your opponent's development with [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) and [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland). What helps separate this deck from other [Delver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver) decks is its ability to play a bit of a longer game. A low land count combined with [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) help give this deck the push it needs even when its early threats are dealt with.


Congrats and condolences on a weekend well done, Kyle. (Note that the reported decklist has only 14 cards in the sideboard.)
