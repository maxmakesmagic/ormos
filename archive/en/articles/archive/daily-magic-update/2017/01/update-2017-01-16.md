Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Black-Green Cryptolith Combo](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/andrewjessup-01162017-new-decks-for-aether-revolt-standard/) | Gathering Magic | Andrew Jessup

Jessup has a number of decklists in his article today, but this one in particular caught my eye. Here, Jessup is combining [Cryptolith Rite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptolith+Rite)—an eminently breakable card—with [Paradox Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Engine) and [Lifecrafter's Bestiary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifecrafter%27s+Bestiary) to go off, much like the [Glimpse of Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) decks of old. The key is to get a few creatures, [Cryptolith Rite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptolith+Rite), [Paradox Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Engine), and [Lifecrafter's Bestiary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifecrafter%27s+Bestiary) all working in sync to let you draw through your deck with each new creature you draw. You can sometimes do without [Cryptolith Rite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptolith+Rite) with enough creatures that make mana naturally, meaning the deck can play out like a normal black-green creatures deck with some sacrifice synergies before turning the corner and becoming something else entirely.


It's almost certain someone will try to crack [Cryptolith Rite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptolith+Rite) and [Paradox Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Engine), and this kind of shell, especially adding in [Lifecrafter's Bestiary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifecrafter%27s+Bestiary), is a really strong start.
