Crewe Crowned Champion in Melbourne
===================================




March 31-April 1, 2012












GRAND PRIX MELBOURNE 2012
-------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel12/Winner.jpg)After years of consistently solid finishes at Australian premier events, Brisbane's Dave Crewe has finally made it to a Grand Prix Top 8. Not wanting to waste the opportunity, he didn't lose a game as he muscled his way through Thomas Rafferty, Simon Harnden, and Geoff Zhao to take the finals in a quintessentially Australian fashion, battling Savage Arachnids with his Undying, [Selfless Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Cathar).


The rest of the Top 8 were an impressive array of Australia's **Magic**al elite. Dan Unwin again proved he is more than just a constructed master, while Oliver Oks crushed Jeremy Neeman's dreams of three straight Australian Grand Prix wins. Rounding out the Top 8 was Hao Wu, who snuck in at 8th place by the narrowest of tiebreaker margins.

-------------------------------------

Wu applied early pressure, while Zhao mustered what defences he could. Geoff Zhao 1 - Hao Wu 1


Zhao mulliganed to 5, but then curved out perfectly in a game that turned out to be a straight race. This match could be related thusly:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel12/GPMelb-Top-8-Wu.jpg)*Wu wishes his creatures could stand up for themselves a little better.*


Zhao: Creature, Creature, Creature, Creature, Pump?  

vs.

Geoff Zhao 2 – Hao Wu 1




Quarterfinals - Dan Unwin vs. Simon Harnden
-------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

Unwin led play with a [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). Harnden had a [Dawntreader Elk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk), while Unwin summoned a [Laboratory Maniac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Laboratory+Maniac). Harnden had a [Cobbled Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobbled+Wings) but no creatures to equip it to, while Unwin summoned a [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake).


Harnden wasn't having any of it, though, a [Slayer of the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slayer+of+the+Wicked) sending the Drake to the graveyard in short order. Harnden followed up with a [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) and a [Village Survivors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Survivors), while Unwin had a [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab) – half Fortress, half Crab, all blocker.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel12/GPMelb-Top-8-Harnden.jpg)*Simon Harndens his heart and contemplates a Crab.*


The problems with [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab)s, though, is that they can only block one aggressor at once. Harnden had a few more of them, curving out through more mid-range attackers into a [Kindercatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kindercatch). Unwin went for a double [Dream Twist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Twist) on Harnden and an [Increasing Confusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Confusion), although this was mostly to look through Harnden's deck before reaching for his sideboard.


Simon Harnden 1 – Dan Unwin 0


During deck construction, Unwin had confided in me that he'd never draft a U/G Mill deck before, and, while the deck seemed powerful, he wasn't 100% confident. He didn't look in a comfortable position, mulliganing to 6 and having Harnden load out a pile of attackers in the early game. Unwin tried to stem the bleeding with a [Claustrophobia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claustrophobia) and a [Spider Spawning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spider+Spawning) for 2.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel12/GPMelb-Top-8-Unwin.jpg)*Is he boggling or goggling? Either way, Unwin's in trouble.*


Harnden was having none of that, though, and powered up with a [Kindercatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kindercatch). The [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) on the board and Unwin's life total being at 6 spelt out how this game was going to end.


Simon Harnden 2 – Dan Unwin 0




Quarterfinals - Jeremy Neeman vs. Oliver Oks
--------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw* 

Oliver Oks has had some reasonable Grand Prix finishes around the World, including a top 4 finish at Grand Prix Stockholm in 2007. Jeremy Neeman has had one or two good finishes, too, including winning Grand Prix Brisbane last year, and Grand Prix Sydney the year before.


Having finished at the top of the Swiss, Oks elected to go first, but threw away his opening hand.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel12/QF_Neeman.jpg)
*Jeremy Neeman* 

"How's yours?" he asked Neeman

Crewe had an [Avacynian Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacynian+Priest) and a [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) to try and answer the troublesome spider.


Zhao only had an [Ulvenwald Mystics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Mystics). Crewe spent a turn equipping his dudes and flashing back the [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), but wasn't about to run into anything like a Hasty Hobbit. Zhao had an [Elder of Laurels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+of+Laurels), while Crewe summoned a [Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble). With the Priest tapping down Zhao's Recluse, the flying [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) tokens attacked unimpeded.


Declaring an attack, Zhao swung in with his 3 untapped creatures, choosing not to pump anything out of respect for Crewe's threatening [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch). Crewe decided to force the issue at the end of Zhao's turn, pinging the [Niblis of the Mist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niblis+of+the+Mist). Zhao pondered long and hard before letting his creature die, taking a point of damage from the Falkenrath.


Crewe attacked with all of his creatures. Zhao aimed a [Spidery Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidery+Grasp) on his flipped [Ulvenwald Primordials](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Primordials).


"I hope this wins the game, because it's very, very cool", said Crewe.


"[Undying Evil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undying+Evil)?", he asked, pointing it at his [Selfless Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Cathar), exactly 4 mana open.


Very, *very* cool.

Top 5 Cards
-----------




![Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodline+Keeper)

I wanted to start this paragraph with 'The little rare that could', but [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) is anything but little. It's the Army Knife of the Swiss, cutting through opponents like butter. It makes dudes. It makes the dudes bigger. It slices and dices, and probably comes on an easy payment plan. Heck, at 2BB, it *is* an easy payment plan. What more could you ask for in a rare? The Top 8 drafters were lucky – there were two of them, one ably piloted by Dave Crewe in his winning deck.



![Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lingering+Souls)

Another card that's made of 70% win and 40% awesome, [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) defies expectations and basic mathematics at every turn. Loved and reviled (depending on who's casting it) in equal measure, [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) has been on everyone's radar since it was revealed early in the spoiler season. It's just *that* good. Fliers? And then more fliers? Players everywhere are signing up for this card's sweet, sweet goodness.



![Fires of Undeath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fires+of+Undeath)

Burn you once, shame on me. Burn you twice? Smiles from me, frowns from you. The power and flexibility this card offers is normally only found in the International Cyborg Ballet. Fine as a [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) variant, the power of flashback compels players to splash, self-mill and stretch mana bases to get as much abuse out of this card as possible. The last time something doubled up this perfectly, the Banach-Tarski paradox was born.



![Undying Evil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Undying+Evil)

The card that won the GP. The card most popular in our 'Most Underrated Card' question we were asking pros all weekend. A combat trick that works equally well on defence and offence. Normally the purview of White, the ability to let a creature die and bring it back stronger than before is a powerful one indeed, especially combined with comes-into-play effects or, like Dave Crewe in the final moments of the weekend's competition, sacrifice effects.



![Increasing Savagery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Increasing+Savagery)

Is this card a beating? There's only one way to find out, and that's to cast it for yourself. But don't take our word on it: cast it twice. Every time I saw this in someone's graveyard, they were usually winning. The one time I saw it removed from the game, the opponent was near tears. 4 mana for a permanent 5-power boost is impressive, especially in this Limited format, replete with evasion of every flavour. A true powerhouse, Green style: unsubtle as a brick to the head, and just as effective.
