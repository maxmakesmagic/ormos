Round 9: Brian Kibler vs. Marco Blume
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 18, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_626.png)
By Victor van den Broek











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a811.jpg)"We could wrestle to see who goes first. He's way out of my weight class but I'd wrestle Marco" – Brian

"I'd probably lose horribly though" – Marco

"Isn't wrestling all about technique?" – Mark Rosewater

In the end these two friendly gentlemen agreed on using another method of deciding who got to choose, and Brian won it. Playing first he dropped a second turn [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid), only to be answered by a [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) on Marco's side. The [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) didn't kill a risky [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage) by Brian as Marco played an [Undead Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Gladiator) on his third turn. The Gladiator traded with the [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) and Marco's [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) killed Brian's freshly played [Wirewood Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Herald). It fetched another [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage). Marco put on some pressure by making a [Screeching Buzzard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard). Brian's second [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage) hit the board on his fifth turn, and he hoped that that second goblin on Marco's side wasn't making its appearance that turn.

"Not a goblin, not a goblin!" – Brian

Marco just played a morphed creature and returned his Gladiator to his hand.

"You're not a goblin. Time to go off!" – Brian

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a812.jpg)The American played a [Treespring Lorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treespring+Lorian) on his turn and drew two cards off the Savages. Marco cycled his Gladiator and returned it to his hand again. He then played [Festering Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin), finally enabling him to finish off the first of the two Savages with the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). The poor [Festering Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin) got [Death Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Pulse)d by Brian, the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith)[Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother)ed and a [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) hit play to stop the bleeding by the Buzzard a bit. Marco cycled his Gladiator again, finding a [Pinpoint Avalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pinpoint+Avalanche) to destroy the [Treespring Lorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treespring+Lorian). Marco's morph fell next turn to Brian's Savage that got a [Wirewood Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Pride). Another Morph was added to Brian's forces, along with an [Anurid Murkdiver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Murkdiver) on his next turn. The Murkdiver got hit by an [Erratic Explosion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erratic+Explosion) for 6, revealing [Slice and Dice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slice+and+Dice) but one [Symbiotic Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf) and [Treespring Lorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treespring+Lorian) later the German had to scoop up his cards for Game 2.

Brian 1 – Marco 0

"You know Kibler, you should've drafted more dragons" – Mark Rosewater

"I tried, I have some big guys in my deck. Also I have this card ([Mythic Proportions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mythic+Proportions)) to make them even bigger!" – Brian

 "Yeah, we called that card 'Brian's card' in playtesting" – Mark Rosewater

"Make something GIGANTIC!" – Brian

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a813.jpg)It wouldn't make an appearance this match though. Marco opened with a [Festering Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin) (Uncle Fester as many people call him) and a [Shepherd of Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shepherd+of+Rot). Brian only had a turn 2 [Wirewood Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Elf) together with a turn 3 [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage). Marco added a [Flamestick Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamestick+Courier) to the mix to make combat a bit more difficult. Brian made a [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) and a [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) and blocked Marco's incoming [Festering Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin) with his [Wirewood Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Elf). The Courier pumped the goblin while the [Wirewood Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Elf) received a [Wirewood Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Pride), allowing the two to trade. Brian's Anurid served on his turn and was joined by a [Serpentine Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serpentine+Basilisk). In the meanwhile, Marco had been whittling away both lifetotals with the [Shepherd of Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shepherd+of+Rot), and he cycled a [Death Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Pulse) on the [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher). A [Severed Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Severed+Legion) hit his side of the board to put on some more pressure. Regardless, the American attacked with all his creatures, and his [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage) traded with the [Severed Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Severed+Legion). He then morphed a [Towering Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Baloth), which got [Pinpoint Avalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pinpoint+Avalanche)d before there was an opportunity to unmorph it. Marco chumpblocked the [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) and had to scoop up his cards on his next turn. 

**Final Result:** Brian 2 – Marco 0
