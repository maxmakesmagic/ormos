The Key to My Spellheart
========================



 Posted in **Reconstructed**
 on November 5, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 







This week's ReConstructed takes two very popular requests and puts them in one place: building decks on a budget and spell-heavy Izzet decks. If you like both budget decks and casting instant and sorcery spells, you've come to the right article!


Ever since *Theros* was released, I've received a torrent of [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera) decks each week—and this week it's finally time to take a peek at one.

Sent in by the enigmatic Robin (who I can only presume is out there somewhere running around in tights yelling, "Holy Chimeras, Batman!") this is a controlling take on using [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera). ### Budget Rules


Before I get into how the deck plays out, let's take a quick look at my rules of thumb for budget deck building.


* I will not add any new rares or mythic rares to the decklist. I'd rather make the deck extra budget-y and then let you season to taste with delicious rares than cook it so rare you won't eat it at all.
* The one exception to the above is mana fixing. Fortunately, for this article you won't have to worry about this one—this deck is one color!
* I try not to make substitutions. Budget doesn't need to mean making a worse version of a current deck—it just means building toward an archetype that has easier-to-obtain cards. Cards like [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) simply can't be replaced in decks that need them.
* Budget doesn't mean bad. I'm not setting out to make a deck we know will be suboptimal through this process. There have been plenty of highly successful low-rare decks throughout **Magic**'s history, and there are certainly ways to follow in their footsteps.

If you want more explanation on any of those points, check out the beginning of [my first budget article](/en/node/689546).


Now, with that out of the way, let's move onto exploring the deck's battle plan!


### The Battle Plan


There are many kinds of Izzet decks and many kinds of [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera) decks. There are versions that aim to kill quickly with cards like [Armed // Dangerous Dangerous](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armed+%2F%2F+Dangerous+Dangerous), and there are versions that use cards like [Guttersnipe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttersnipe) to put more pressure on your opponent with each and every spell. This version on the deck definitely falls on the more controlling end of the spectrum.

By dealing with each threat your opponent presents by casting efficient instant and sorcery spells, this deck eventually aims to close out the game with a massive [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera) or a swarm of [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) tokens. While it looks like there aren't a lot of win conditions, don't fret: one [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera) will often be enough to seal the deal on its own. This deck can very easily end up with ten+ instants and sorceries in its graveyard, making one or two hits with the Chimera all you need.


What are you doing until that point? Well, controlling the game, of course! A plethora of burn spells take out any creatures that get in the way, and a suite of countermagic can help answer any of the noncreature problems you might encounter. Draw some cards, deal with some problems, land a Chimera, and ride it to victory—all the while perhaps amassing tokens from a [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer).


A lot of this deck's core is solid, but there are some tweaks that can be made to optimize its goals further. Like what? Well, you're about to find out—it's time to move onto the deck breakdown!


### Deck Breakdown


Which cards fit into the mix and which ones just need to burn away? Let's take a look and find out!





![Spellheart Chimera](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spellheart+Chimera)


 
Ah, one of the cornerstones of the archetype. Isn't it cute? Don't be fooled, though—the prettier it looks, the more likely it is to kill you. [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera) is pretty irreplaceable in this deck, providing a ton of power for only three mana. While you won't actually cast it on turn three that often—rather waiting for a later turn once he's larger, perhaps with countermagic backup—the fact that he only costs three mana is a huge boon later in the game, meaning you don't need to tap out for your win condition.



There is an argument to be made for only playing three copies, since you don't want to draw a grip full of these right away, but the card is so crucial and effective once you've cast just a handful of spells that I'm happy with the full four.





![Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Young+Pyromancer)


 
While this deck will usually kill with [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera), don't discount the power of this little guy. In a deck like this one where most of your nonland cards trigger his ability, he's incredible. He can either create a large swarm of tokens over several turns to overrun your opponent with, or buy you a ton of time turning each spell you cast into an extra blocker. What will happen most often when you play him on turn two is use the tokens to block for the first few turns, and then take control of the board and start pushing through some damage with them.



[Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) is a card that only gets better in multiples. While we do have to be careful to not include too many non-instant/sorcery cards in this deck, Pyromancer is definitely worth carving out space for.





![Omenspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Omenspeaker)


 
In general, I like [Omenspeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omenspeaker) in control decks that look like this. Often, you need to buy a little bit of time and dig to the spells—or lands—you need, and [Omenspeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omenspeaker) does that well.



However, what [Omenspeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omenspeaker) is *not* is an instant or sorcery. With eight creatures prominently in the deck and eating away from instant/sorcery space, I'd rather have something here that accomplishes the goals of [Omenspeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omenspeaker) while also playing into the rest of my game plan. Unlike [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera) and [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer), [Omenspeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omenspeaker)*is* replaceable.


The card I'd like to put here instead is [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet). It similarly helps you scry to find what you're looking for and can slow down a creature onslaught, but it has the added bonus of being an instant. Between the damage and scrying, [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet) is quite good in this deck—let's play with the full four.



![Magma Jet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Magma+Jet)




![Lightning Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lightning+Strike)


 
Dealing 3 damage for two mana has become the industry standard for red mages these days, first with [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) and now with [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike). While it is good in this deck, something to keep in mind is that burn spells here are most important for killing creatures.



What I mean is that, unlike in an aggressive red deck where you want to maximize your damage output to your opponent's noggin, in this deck what matters is most is taking out creatures. And, in this format, 2 damage is often comparable to 3. There are a few cards it matters for—[Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner); [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter); combining with a 2-damage spell to off a [Polukranos, World Eater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater)—but on the whole, 2 damage will often get the job done.


Why do I bring this up? Well, the scry from [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet) is extremely valuable in a deck that is trying to find the right instants and sorceries and then a [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera). I'd rather have the fourth [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet) than the fourth [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike). I'm going to shave down a couple [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike)s here. It certainly doesn't mean it's a bad card, but this deck already has a heavy saturation of burn spells (including [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), which already kills huge creatures) and I'd rather make room to play some other ones with powerful abilities attached.





![Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mizzium+Mortars)


 
And speaking of [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), here we are! This flexible removal spell kills off a lot of the larger threats in the format and serves as a board sweeper in the late game. While it is weak against control decks, [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) can help you cycle some dead Mortars away. I'm happy to keep the full four copies of these.



If you don't own [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) and it's a little too rare for your tastes, it isn't crucial—you could easily play some [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike)s, other burn spells, or even [Anger of the Gods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger+of+the+Gods) (if you have those) instead. While I'd play it if you can, if you're really looking for the bare-bones budget version of this decklist, they are cards you can take out without destabilizing the core of the deck.





![Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Izzet+Charm)


 
[Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) is a great fit for this deck. All three modes are highly useful: it can fend off creatures early on, find you what you need, or give you answers to troublesome noncreatures. The "looting" feature to draw and discard is especially nice in this deck, since chaining instants and sorceries is great with [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer), and discarded instants and sorceries don't completely go to waste; they still power up your [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera). I'd like to keep all four of these.






![Steam Augury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Steam+Augury)


 
[Steam Augury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Augury) hasn't found a great home elsewhere because you leave the choice in your opponent's hands. Often, that's not what a control deck wants: it is looking for specific cards, which is what made [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) so powerful and [Steam Augury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Augury) not quite as splashy.




![Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fact+or+Fiction)

However, what if instead of a get-what-I-want spell, we looked at [Steam Augury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Augury) as a value draw spell?


[Steam Augury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Augury) shows you five cards—and even if you end up with the pile you don't want, that's still churning through a ton of instants and sorceries for your [Spellheart Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellheart+Chimera) and [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer). So many of your cards are interchangeable that [Steam Augury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Augury) actually works pretty well here. This is one of the archetypes where I'm happiest with the card—and I'm comfortable keeping all of them here.





![Turn // Burn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn)


 
[Turn // Burn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn) serves a vital role in this deck: killing large creatures. If your opponent lands something large like a Polukranos, it's hard to remove without spending two cards in the process. [Turn // Burn Burn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn+Burn) helps take out cards like that at instant speed.



This card is also extremely important for fighting off [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves). How so? Well, simply casting the Turn side on [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) kills off all the Elemental tokens (presuming that's their only Master) since the Master loses its Elemental-enlarging ability. And then, if you still want to get those two blue mana symbols and future Elemental helper off the board, you can always use another spell to kill it, since the protection from red is temporarily gone as well.


[Turn // Burn Burn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn+Burn) fills such an important gap in this deck that I'd like to play the full four copies. You can always just use it as a 2-damage burn spell, and the versatility is going to be useful often enough that I want to make sure I can find one.





![Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Essence+Scatter)




![Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Negate)


 

Countermagic is fairly well positioned in this deck. Not only is it the right card type to help out your creatures, but it gives you answers to threats you might have to two-for-one yourself to deal with otherwise. [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) is definitely important, dealing with threats like a turn-three Polukranos on the draw.


[Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) covers a lot of your early-game noncreature bases, so I'm not as concerned with keeping [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) around. I'd rather have something that is stronger as the game goes on and is better against more threats, meaning you have additional answers to something like Ætherling. The card I'd like for that spot is [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve), swapping straight over [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate).





![Opportunity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Opportunity)


 
The singleton [Opportunity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opportunity) looks a little odd, but I actually like it. It's a massive card drawer you can find late to close out the game, makes [Steam Augury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Augury) piles more difficult for your opponent, and is just generally the one extra source of card advantage I was looking for. I'm happy keeping this.

I actually think this deck is fairly competitive, and there isn't much I'd look at tweaking if it were unbudgetized. One card I would definitely consider if I wasn't operating on a budget is [Anger of the Gods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger+of+the+Gods) as another sweeper—but that card is such a nonbo with [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) that I might not even consider it.


If you're looking for an easy and effective way to get into Standard, give this a try! Practically all the rares can be swapped for something else if need be—[Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) for basic lands, [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) for [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike)s and more counterspells, [Steam Augury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Augury) for [Divination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination)—so it's fairly easy to build up. Have fun with it!
