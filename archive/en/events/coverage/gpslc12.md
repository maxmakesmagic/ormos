Grand Prix Salt Lake City 2012
------------------------------


Shenhar Snags Second Victory in Salt Lake!
==========================================




March 31-April 1, 2012












Grand Prix Salt Lake City 2012
------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/trophy_620.jpg)


It's been an incredible weekend for **Magic** here in Salt Lake City. 1136 players showed up to battle with their favorite standard decks. The field was whittled down to 146 players for the second day of competition. When the dust settled only eight players remained. Tom Martell, Marcel Angelo Zafra, Michael Peterson, David Gleicher, Bryan Olvera, James Cooper, Shahar Shenhar, and Michael Hetrick found themselves in the elimination rounds.


Shahar Shenhar managed to best fellow Californian, Michael Hetrick, in a difficult quarterfinal match. Another Red/Green deck, this time piloted by James Cooper, couldn't stop Shenhar in the semifinals match. Finally, Shenhar was able to defeat another player from his local area, Michael Peterson, in a mirror match to take home the trophy, $3,500, and the title of Grand Prix Salt Lake City Champion.


This is Shenhar's second Grand Prix victory in less than six months. Shenhar has crossed the 30 pro point threshold and will be a fixture of the Pro Tour for the remainder of 2012 and the entirety of 2013. Only 18 years old, there's still a world of potential in this young master. Expect great things.

Quarterfinal - Michael Hetrick vs. Shahar Shenhar
-------------------------------------------------


*by Jacob Van Lunen* 
Michael Hetrick is somewhat famous as ShipItHolla on MTGO. This is his first Top 8 in a major large event.


Shahar Shenhar won Grand Prix San Diego in November. This is his second Grand Prix Top 8 in less than 6 months. Pretty impressive for an 18-year-old.


Hetrick and Shenhar are both California players. They've clearly played before and their friendship was obvious. Both players are already qualified for Pro Tour Avacyn Restored, they'll be playing for Pro Points, money, and glory.


### Game 1


Hetrick led things off with a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and Shenhar used [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) to see the contents of Hetrick's hand: [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) , [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) , [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) , [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) , and [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) .


Shenhar also had [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) and [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) on his first turn.


Hetrick cast [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) to deal with Shenhar's [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) . Shenhar [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) ed, looking for another [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) , which he found.


Hetrick couldn't find a third land and was forced to pass without a play.


[Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) helped the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) flip, while Hetrick missed his third land drop.


Hetrick conceded after Shenhar cast [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) before he made his second land drop.


### Game 2


Hetrick led things off with [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) while Shenhar had [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) .


Hetrick cast [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) on his second turn and sent it into the red zone. [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) made Avacyn's Pilgrim and he passed the turn with three creatures in play.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/qf%20Shenhar%20vs%20Hetrick.jpg)

Shenhar pondered for an answer to Hetrick's aggressive start. He failed to find a second land.


Hetrick sent all his creatures at Shenhar, who traded his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) for Hetrick's [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) .


Shenhar found a second land and used it to cast [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) .


Hetrick was sitting on a few burn spells and things were looking grim for the Grand Prix San Diego champion. Hetrick sent in his team again and added [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) to the board.


Shenhar had a [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) , but Hetrick had an [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) he was waiting to use until Shenhar equipped. Another attack brought Shenhar down to three.


With no help from the top of Shenhar's deck they were ready to move on to game 3.


### Game 3


Shenhar kept his opening hand, but Hetrick's opener wasn't quite good enough. Six cards were good enough for Hetrick.


Shenhar had the first play of the game with [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) .


He followed it up with [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) , but Hetrick had [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) to legend rule it away. Another [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) came down for Shenhar, but this time Hetrick had [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) to take control of the board.


Shenhar had [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and left open mana instead of equipping his [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) .


Hetrick cast [Arc Trail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail) , killing the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and dealing two damage to Shenhar.


Shenhar made a token with his [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) .


Shenhar's spirit started poking in some damage, and Hetrick was left with a hand full of lands.


Shenhar got a chance to make another Spirit and the game started to get out of Hetrick's hands.


Another [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) pushed things further in favor of Shenhar.


Hetrick decided to get aggressive with his [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll). He attacked and dumped all his mana into [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) .


Shenhar had a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) for [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) and he flipped his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) the next turn. Another attack was enough to take the match.


Shahar Shenhar wins 2-1!


 
Quarterfinal - David Gleicher (Blue-White Delver of Secrets) vs. Michael Peterson (Blue-White Delver of Secrets)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin* 
David Gleicher came into this event with 14 Pro Points, and in need of a good finish here, and at Pro Tour Avacyn Restored in Barcelona, in order to reach Gold Level in the Pro Players Club. Well, a Top 8 berth here is certainly a good start – and if he manages to win the whole thing, Gleicher will find himself guaranteed to reach Gold just by showing up in Barcelona.


Michael Peterson, on the other hand, needs to win his quarterfinals matchup just to earn his invitation to Pro Tour Avacyn Restored in Barcelona.

Michael Peterson 1 – David Gleicher 1



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/qf2%20Gleicher.JPG)
*Michael Peterson*

[Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) bought Gleicher some time, but he wasn't able to do much with those extra turns. A few hits later, and Michael Peterson had advanced to the Top 4.


**Michael Peterson 2 - David Gleicher 1**


 
Quarterfinal: Bryan Olvera (White-Blue Humans) vs. James Cooper ([Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) Ramp
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin* 
At Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*, White-Blue Humans, and [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kessig%2BWolf%2BRun) Ramp were two of the defining decks of the format. But since that event, they've almost completely fallen of the map. While [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), Zombies, and Blue-Black control have taken the spotlight away from [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) , and [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) in recent months – Olvera, and Cooper have each advanced to the Top 8 piloting these "forgotten" archetypes.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/qf3%20Cooper%20Olvera%20A.JPG)
*Bryan Olvera (left) and James Cooper(right)*

### Game 1


Bryan Olvera got off to a strong start on the play with a [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) , and a [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) , but he quickly sputtered out after that. With Olvera out of threats so early, a [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) , and an unanswered [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) were enough for James Cooper to take the first game with ease.


James Cooper 1 – Bryan Olvera 0

[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) died to Cooper's [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) , but a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) , and a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) gave Shenhar a very quick clock. And when Cooper's next few draws failed to yield him anything of note, Shenhar was off to the finals.


Shahar Shenhar 2 – James Cooper 1


 
Final - Shahar Shenhar (Blue-White [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)) vs. Michael Peterson (Blue-White [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets))
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin* 
After two days of play, a field of 1,136 players has been cut down to just two players piloting the defining deck of the format. Blue-White [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets).


Grand Prix San Diego Champion Shahar Shenhar and his finals opponent Michael Peterson have known each other for a long time as they both play regularly at Adventures in Comics and Games in Sacramento. These two strong players are joined by Ricky Sidher and Michael Hetrick, all posting strong finishes here at Grand Prix Salt Lake City and representing a surging second base of power to combat the ChannelFireball crew in the west.


Piloting similar decks, it would be the slight differences in card selection, as well as play that would decide which of the two would be the one to hoist the trophy this weekend. Either way, it seemed sure that the other, while disappointed, would be proud.



 

### Game 1


Michael Peterson went first, and passed his turn without playing the deck's signature card: [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) .


Shahar Shenhar's [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) revealed that Peterson had kept an opening hand with [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) , [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) , [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) , [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) , [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) , but no second land.


"Is it greedy?" asked Peterson with a smile.


"A little bit" replied Shenhar.


[Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) was enough for Peterson to find the second land that he needed. This allowed Petereson to counter an [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) with a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) , before resolving a [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) that threatened to make any creature he played a force to be reckoned with.


Shenhar cast a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) , and while Peterson was able to play a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to block it with, he didn't have enough mana to equip it with [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) . Shenhar's replacement [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) got legend ruled away by Peterson's own Geist, but Shenhar had yet another Geist to keep the pressure on.


Peterson then played a [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) , equipped it with [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) , and saved it from Shenhar's [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) with a [Mutagenic Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) . Shenhar was unable to attack into the first striking [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), and had to pass his turn.


At this point, Peterson looked to go on the offensive with his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) , and a second [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) – attacking for nine, and putting Shenhar into a very unsound position.


But a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) , flashing back [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) (killing Peterson's 9/1 [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) ), and a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to counter [Dungeon Geists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) allowed Shenhar to pull himself within a game of winning his second Grand Prix.


Shahar Shenhar 1 – Michael Peterson 0



 

### Game 2


A series of [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) s, and [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) s kept the board empty through the early turns of the game – until a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) , and an [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) (protected by multiple [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) s) gave Peterson a substantial lead.


At this point Shenhar, who was stuck on four lands and thus unable to cast the [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) in his hand, could only watch as Peterson's [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) clobbered him to death.


Shahar Shenhar 1 – Michael Peterson 1



 

### Game 3


After the players spent their first turns digging for good threats – Peterson used a [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) to clear the [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) s out of Shenhar's deck, while Shenhar went on the offensive with an [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) .


Peterson continued setting himself up for the long game by using a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to flashback [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) , stripping Shenhar's deck of its [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) s.


After the two players' [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) s legend ruled each other away, Peterson used a pair of [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) s to counter [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) -- before Shenhar's second [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) was allowed to stick.


When Peterson (who had taken quite a bit of damage from his Phyrexian mana spells, and Shenhar's [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) ) passed his turn without killing the Geist --Shenhar quickly did some math, before attacking, and bouncing his [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) with [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) after Peterson blocked it.


Shenhar replayed his [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) , and when Peterson's next draw failed to yield him a way to kill the legendary [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) , or block the angel token that it makes, it only took Shahar Shenhar one more attack phase before he won his second Grand Prix!


Shahar Shenhar defeats Michael Peterson two games to one to become the Grand Prix Salt Lake City Champion!

*by Jacob Van Lunen* 
![Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Honor+of+the+Pure)### 1) [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure)


Humans had fallen off the radar in the past few weeks. No one expected it to have such a big impact this weekend. Bryan Olvera managed to Top 8 the event with the forgotten archetype. [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) was also added to [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) by Josh Utter-Leyton in an effort to go "over the top" of other versions of the deck.


![Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gideon+Jura)### 2) [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura)


[Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) is one of the most powerful spells in standard, but there aren't many decks that want to play with this Planeswalker. This weekend, Andrew Cuneo showed up with a traditional Blue/White control deck that looked like it was delivered in a DeLorean, fresh from 1998. The deck served Cuneo well and took him very deep in the competition.


![Runechanterâ€™s Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Runechanter%E2%80%99s+Pike)### 3) [Runechanter’s Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%E2%80%99s+Pike)


[Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) has overtaken [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) as the equipment of choice for [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) players. The latest versions of [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) play over twenty instants and sorceries and even include Thoughtscour over [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) to take full advantage of the rare equipment.


![Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells)### 4) [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells)


[Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) had a great showing this weekend. [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kessig%2BWolf%2BRun) Ramp decks saw a revival and RG aggro decks were battling at the top tables throughout the event. The new Naya-Pod decks, popularized by Brian Kibler, took full advantage of [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) . [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) gives players something to do without casting a spell, making it easy to flip Huntmaster into [Ravager of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravager+of+the+Fells) . [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) also lets players trade their [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) in for [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) , leaving behind a 2/2 wolf and some lifegain.


![Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Delver+of+Secrets)### 5) [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)


[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) decks continued to dominate in Salt Lake City. Both decks in the finals included four copies of the *Innistrad* common. A turn one [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) that quickly flips into [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) provides an exceptionally fast clock that can be defended with [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) , [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) , and other cheap disruptive spells.
