Braving Standard
================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 9, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






![Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brave+the+Elements)

One of the most powerful cards to build a deck around in Standard is [Brave the Elements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements). For a single white mana to provide protection to all of your white creatures is extremely effective and versatile. While the normal-use case is getting your creatures through any blockers, [Brave the Elements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) is also good at protecting your creatures from cards such as [Anger of the Gods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger+of+the+Gods) or the monstrous ability of [Polukranos, World Eater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater). 


 The biggest issue with [Brave the Elements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) is that you have to build your creature base to include nearly all white creatures. This deck takes the straightforward approach, and plays as many one- and two-mana cards as possible. In fact, only a pair of [Banisher Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banisher+Priest)s cost more than two mana in the entire main deck! This allows the deck to play a very low land count while still including [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault). A whopping 20 one-mana creatures allow the deck to get off to some fast starts, and [Brave the Elements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) is one of the best ways to sneak through the last points of damage.
