### DAILY DECK


Temur, Better Than All
======================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)

Adam Prosak

From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer.


September 29, 2014
 










[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace, the Mind Sculptor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace, the Mind Sculptor)
*Khans of Tarkir* is coming! Three-color wedges are about to make their way into Standard and every other Constructed format, but by no means will they be new to tournament *Magic*. Today and last week we looked at Pro Tour-winning decks that have been based in one of the wedge clans from *Khans of Tarkir*.



Today's Deck is from Hall of Famer Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, and is his winning deck from [Pro Tour San Juan](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptsj10/welcome). Playing *Zendikar* Block Constructed, Paulo's deck combined the best cards each color had to offer to become the first player to win a Pro Tour after losing his first two matches.



The backbone of the deck comes from its mana acceleration. [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore), and [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) make it easy to quickly put lots of lands into play. This makes [Avenger of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) an incredible threat, and it can easily provide a lethal attack in one turn cycle. Red provides some efficient removal to help buy you enough time to develop lands (and kill opposing Cobras and Oracles!). Blue? How about [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)? That seems like a great reason to play blue by itself.
