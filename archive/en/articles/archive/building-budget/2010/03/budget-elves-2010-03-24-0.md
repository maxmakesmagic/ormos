Budget Elves
============



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on March 24, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week we made [a budget-friendly version of Jund](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/know-your-enemy-2010-03-17) that specializes in mirror matches. This week I'd like to focus on my inner Timmy. Some of my favorite reoccuring themes in **Magic** are tribes. Slivers get the nod as my favorite tribe for obvious reasons, but Elves have always been a close second for me. Elf decks always seem to flow beautifully for me. Just playing a generic Elf deck would probably be pretty boring, though. I'd like to introduce you folks to an exciting new card interaction.

I have received a bunch of emails since the release of *Worldwake* that pointed out the interaction between [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller) and [Quest for the Gemblades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Gemblades). The potential of something like this is really absurd. An active [Quest for the Gemblades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Gemblades) becomes a very scary board presence. At first, I was skeptical of using [Quest for the Gemblades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Gemblades); I assumed that it would be situational most times and outright mediocre if I did not draw a [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab83_jorga.jpg)  
I was wrong, though. I found that an active [Quest for the Gemblades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Gemblades) made my opponent play a very different game. Especially when playing in the casual room, I found that the enchantment created a lot of interesting and fun game scenerios. The current Standard environment seems to have far too many copies of [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) and [Martial Coup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup) flying around for an Elf deck to make any waves, but **Magic** is not always about attacking a metagame. Sometimes you just need to embrace the building desire to attack with an army of 7/7 Elves!

Lets start by discussing the cards I chose to play with.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab83_theCardsHeader.jpg)  
**[Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves):** Classically elvish to the core, these are staples in this type of strategy. [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) allow us to dump our hand as quickly as possible in the early game. Their usefulness doubles up at the later stages of the game when they turn into gigantic monsters via [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller).

**[Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller):** An obvious inclusion.

**[Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid):** Another Elf lord. [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) combos pretty nicely with [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller), as you can use all the mana you generate to kick [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller) many times.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab83_theCardsCross1.jpg)  
**[Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary):** A body that replaces itself with another card is always a welcome addition.

**[Quest for the Gemblades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Gemblades):** An on-board trick that makes life very difficult for your opponents, it also combos very nicely with [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller).

**[Nissa's Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen):** A 2/3 body for two mana is a pretty good deal.

**[Nissa Revane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane):** Nissa is awesome in this deck. She also happens to be the least budget-friendly card, though. If you cannot find this mythic rare, she is not Nissasary. You can replace her with any of your favorite Elves.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab83_theCardsCross2.jpg)  
**[Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun):** Another classic Elf finisher. [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) seemed like an obvious inclusion to me.

**[Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood):** This is one of the best green tricks ever printed. It can save your creatures from virtually any removal spell and can even mess with our opponent's Equipment or Auras if it catches them trying to attach.

**[Leatherback Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leatherback+Baloth):** Every Elf needs a pet. Oh, I'm also a big fan of playing 4/5s on the second turn.

The deck is surprisingly easy to build on **Magic Online**. A lot of players have mentioned that recent online trade values seem low. This is just another reason to download the client if you have not tried **Magic Online** yet. You can build decks on a reasonable budget and find someone to game with at any time of the day!

As of this writing, here's what the rares were trading for on **Magic Online**:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab83_ticket.jpg)[Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller) .4 tickets x 4 = 1.6 tickets  
[Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) 1.4 tickets x 4 = 5.6 tickets  
[Nissa Revane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane) 5.5 tickets x 2 = 11 tickets  
[Oran-Rief, the Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief%2C+the+Vastwood) .4 tickets x 4 = 1.6 tickets

Total ticket cost of rares = 19.8 tickets

All the commons and uncommons in this deck can be easily gotten for a tenth of a ticket or less. So you should be able to trade for the entirety of the deck for under 25 tickets.

I put the deck together on **Magic Online** this past week and played some games with it. The first three games I played were in the tournament practice room.

I drew an [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), played my [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), cast [Leatherback Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leatherback+Baloth), and passed the turn. I blocked the Guide, and my opponent finished my [Leatherback Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leatherback+Baloth) off with a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). I drew the [Nissa's Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen), played [Oran-Rief, the Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief%2C+the+Vastwood), cast [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Nissa's Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen), and passed the turn. My opponent cast a [Hell's Thunder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hell%27s+Thunder) and attacked me down to 14.

I drew a [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller), cast [Nissa Revane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane), searched up another copy of [Nissa's Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen), activated Oran-Rief, attacked for 2, and passed the turn. My opponent cast an [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) for 3, destroying my Nissa and my whole team except the 3/4 [Nissa's Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen). I drew a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), played it, cast [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), activated [Oran-Rief, the Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief%2C+the+Vastwood), attacked for 4, and passed the turn. My opponent played a fifth land and unearthed [Hell's Thunder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hell%27s+Thunder) to put me at 10. I drew another [Joraga Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Warcaller), cast it kicked twice, activated Oran-Rief, and attacked with my 7/8 [Nissa's Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen). My opponent drew for the turn and conceded.

This deck is a lot of fun to play. Competitive gaming can be a lot of fun, but sometimes you just want to attack with an army of green monsters. If you wanted to make this deck more competitive, you could probably splash red for [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), use [Eldrazi Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument) over [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), and work on the curve a bit. The deck is easy to build and will certainly do well in the casual room or at your local Friday Night **Magic**.

Happy brewing!
