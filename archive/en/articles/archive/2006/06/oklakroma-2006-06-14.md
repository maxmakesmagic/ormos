Oklakroma!
==========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 14, 2006 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar












Welcome to Akroma week! I had big plans for this week. This was supposed to be my first all-talkin', all-singin', all-dancin' column - a live webcast from my living room in the style of the Hollywood musical. Called “Angellzapoppin'”, it would've featured me swing-dancing with a life-size inflatable Akroma doll (not yet on the market). Unfortunately, I ran into a few snags. On the day the performance was to take place, my piano man called in sick, the costumers could only find *nine*-gallon hats, and, as it turned out, I was completely making the whole thing up.


It's hard to dispute that [Akroma, Angel of Wrath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Wrath) is one of the wackiest cards ever made. Whether it's a madcap attack for six, or a screwball block, or the outright zaniness of being reanimated on turn 2, it's clear that lost among her many abilities is an overpowered funny-bone. As the kids say, she's all that. Some would also say that she's a bag of chips, but that seems a bit farfetched to me. When Akroma's not slicing and dicing, she's making very thin French fries for some reason. I guess she likes potatoes.


How do you build a deck around a tuber-loving vengeance-seeker? However she wants you to.


### Rock and Roll Lobster Girls


Compare Akroma to, say, [Trained Orgg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trained+Orgg). Each of them can flex some 6/6 muscle, but for one more mana, Akroma gets infinitely more abilities and is three times more photogenic. I'm not sure what happened to our Red friend after seeing that quack Orgg psychologist. Sure, he got over his crippling fear of Elves, his Llanophobia, but he lost his mean streak in the process. [Trained Orgg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trained+Orgg)'s Beastly brother in Blue, [Vizzerdrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizzerdrix), has the same stats, but without all the training, something I credit to home-schooling. Sadly, my math-major friends tell me, without hyperbole, that Akroma is a million times better than [Vizzerdrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizzerdrix) and [Trained Orgg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trained+Orgg) put together.


Now compare Akroma to *Fallen Empires* favourite, [Deep Spawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Spawn). One is arguably the best creature ever printed, while the other can be found on the menu at Big Daddy's Crab Shack (and Oyster Bar). Did you know that when [Deep Spawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Spawn) was a little craw-boy, he'd lie in his sea-bed and dream of becoming an Angel of Wrath?


Well, Deep Spawn, it's your lucky day! I'm here to make your dreams come true! All I need is a little help from our graveyard-dwelling friends, the *Judgment* Incarnations! With [Valor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valor), [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), and [Anger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger) in the ol' discard pile, [Deep Spawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Spawn) becomes a 6/6 Flier with First Strike, Trample, and Haste. Activate [Glory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) two times and you have a Homarid with Protection from Red and Protection from Black. All that's missing is the Vigilance, but I'll let [Serra's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra%27s+Blessing) take care of that. Add a little seasoning and Bam! The Lobster from the bad part of town becomes the superstar he always knew he could be!


To get those Incarnations where they need to be, I turned to old standbys, [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study), and [Buried Alive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive). With those pieces of the puzzle in place, it just made sense to pursue a Reanimation strategy. Sure, you could cut out the middle-man and just reanimate Akroma, but that'll get you zero points for style. We want chrome on those rims! Besides, doesn't it bring a tear to your eye watching has-beens like ol' Vizzy, [Trained Orgg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trained+Orgg), and [Deep Spawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Spawn) take off their thick black-rimmed glasses, squeeze into that phonebooth, spin around a couple times, and come out looking like angels? It's a little cramped in there, but no pain, no gain.

The deck is legal in Extended if you take out the [Deep Spawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Spawn)s. I've been playing a version on MTGO with three of both [Vizzerdrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizzerdrix) and [Trained Orgg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trained+Orgg), in place of the Homarid.


### Enchantment Under the “O”



![Opalescence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Opalescence)

It's one thing to turn a jumbo-sized crawdad into the villain (?) of the *Onslaught* Block story line, but it's another thing altogether to build fair Akroma out of a paper-clip, a stick of gum, and everyday household enchantments. You know, like [Opalescence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence). I'm going to be using a lot of enchantments in this deck, so [Replenish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Replenish) seemed like a good choice, allowing to put many of them into play at once. To get said enchantments into the graveyard, I turned to [Attunement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attunement). Luckily for me, this combo is in no way overpowered and did not, as a result, dominate the tournament scene during the time it was legal. Okay, maybe it did, but I'm much too young to remember *that*, Gramps.

“But Opalescence is just a vanilla 4/4,” I hear you say. Sure is, Jonesy, but what if we add a pair of [Glorious Anthem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Anthem)s to the mix? Opie would become a 6/6, that's what. That's pretty sweet, to be sure, but Akroma is still sweeter. After shopping around, I discovered that all of Akroma's keyworded abilities come in the form [Opalescence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence)-aiding Enchantments. [Serra's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra%27s+Blessing) grants Vigilance, of course. [Knighthood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knighthood), [Primal Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Rage), [Fires of Yavimaya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Yavimaya), and [Levitation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Levitation) have your First Strike, Trample, Haste, and Flying covered, while [Absolute Law](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absolute+Law) and [Absolute Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absolute+Grace) will make sure your makeshift Angels don't meet their demises at the hands of some Black and/or Red party-poopers.


[Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) is there to back-up [Attunement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attunement), while [Collective Restraint](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Restraint) should help to keep your enemies at bay while you set things up. If the whole enchantment angle doesn't work out, you can turn to the place of worship of those six-legged Monks of the Krosan forest, the [Nantuko Monastery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Monastery). With [Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) and [Attunement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attunement) filling up your graveyard, you should be at Threshold in no time. Since the Monastery has First Strike built right in, you won't even need Knighthood to start bashing your opponent, Akroma-style.

### A is for Akroma


One of the best things about Akroma is that her name starts with “A.” That's “A” for awesome, “A” for amazing, and “A” for apopleptic, which is what your opponents might be when you play Akroma for the umpteenth time. With the Angel leading things off, I decided to build a deck based around Legendary Creatures. Those with superior pattern-recognition skills will quickly realize that the deck is based around a system of ancient Roman symbols. Scholars call this obscure system The Alphabet.


![Hazduhr the Abbot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hazduhr+the+Abbot)*Hazduhr never gets any love.*
With “A” out of the way, I moved on to “B.” I decided on Bosh. I like his Artifact-flinging and his tenacity on the boards for the Toronto Raptors. [Captain Sisay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+Sisay) is a no-brainer for this deck (did I mention that it was full of Legendary Creatures?). [Dromar, the Banisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromar%2C+the+Banisher), [Ertai, Wizard Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ertai%2C+Wizard+Adept), and [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) were also easy choices. “F” presented a bit of a problem, with only [Frankie Peanuts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frankie+Peanuts) and [Fumiko the Lowblood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumiko+the+Lowblood) in that slot. Rather than strain the mana-base even further or fraternize with Tusk-any gangsters, I opted to play latent-Legend [Faithful Squire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithful+Squire), otherwise known as Kaiso, the Unflippable. [Hanna, Ship's Navigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hanna%2C+Ship%27s+Navigator) might be a controversial choice, since I know how beloved [Hazduhr the Abbot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hazduhr+the+Abbot) is, but I like her interaction with Bosh and the [Terrarion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrarion) I intend to use in the deck. Can you say “Card Drawing Engine”?


[Isamaru, Hound of Konda](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isamaru%2C+Hound+of+Konda) and [Jareth, Leonine Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jareth%2C+Leonine+Titan) were the next two to make the team. “K” was a difficult choice. In order to keep the mana-curve from looking like Cliff Hangers on The Price is Right, I chose [Kira, Great Glass-Spinner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kira%2C+Great+Glass-Spinner) over [Keiga, the Tide Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keiga%2C+the+Tide+Star), among others. For the same reason, I went with [Lieutenant Kirtar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lieutenant+Kirtar) over pricier choices like [Lord Magnus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+Magnus).


“M” had to go to [Mistform Ultimus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus), who, I'm told, wields a mean bo staff and is a wizard with a set of nunchucks. At the same time, he makes all of the Legendary tribal lords one-twenty-sixth better (I'm looking at you, [Nagao, Bound by Honor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nagao%2C+Bound+by+Honor) and [Zuberi, Golden Feather](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuberi%2C+Golden+Feather)). “O” went to [Opal-Eye, Konda's Yojimbo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opal-Eye%2C+Konda%27s+Yojimbo), furthering a Samurai sub-theme with Nagao and [Takeno, Samurai General](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Takeno%2C+Samurai+General).


[Phelddagrif](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phelddagrif) is awesomeness-in-a-can, so in it went. Unsurprisingly, the next letter was lacking in Legendary options. Flavour aficionados get on the horn and alert your local congressperson that we need a “Q” Legend, stat! In the meantime, here are my suggestions:


Quigley, Who is Down Under  

Quizzledorf, Devourer of Souls.  

Quartenov, the Milk Bottler.  

Queen Winnifred, Master of Ponies.


Hrm, maybe I'll leave the naming up to the Creative Team...



![Phelddagrif](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phelddagrif)

[Phelddagrif](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phelddagrif) is one of the best Hippos of all time, and it's becoming increasingly clear that its all-marble diet has led to some useful mutations, including non-Legendary status and some niftier abilities. That's why I opted to fill this slot with his Questing cousin. Did you know that [Questing Phelddagrif](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Phelddagrif) is an anagram for Pig Squandered Flight? Another R & D secret exposed.

[Reki, the History of Kamigawa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reki%2C+the+History+of+Kamigawa) is Card Drawing Engine number two (How could this deck *not* be good?). [Sakashima the Impostor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakashima+the+Impostor) does the letter “S” proud, and allows you to have two “Akromas” on the table at once! “U” and “V” aren't Legend-heavy letters, but [Uyo, Silent Prophet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uyo%2C+Silent+Prophet) and [Vhati il-Dal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vhati+il-Dal) went in next over the [Sewerdreg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewerdreg)-foiling [Ur-Drago](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ur-Drago) and something I probably couldn't cast. Like “Q”, “W” is utterly lacking in *Legends*. How about Winnifred, Queen of the Ponies? Or not. You don't have to decide right away. With no good Legendary Creatures, I went with the potential mana-fixer, [Weathered Wayfarer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weathered+Wayfarer). *Portal Three Kingdoms* has a couple of “X” *Legends*, but I chose [Xiahou Dun, the One-Eyed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xiahou+Dun%2C+the+One-Eyed) because this deck needs more Horsemanship and less eyeballs. Compared to “W”, “Y” has a relative abundance of creatures, but I'm sure as Shimatsu not picking Yukora (see [last week's article](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/cm22) for reasons why). Instead I went with Yosei. [Zuberi, Golden Feather](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuberi%2C+Golden+Feather) filled in the “Z” slot.


I rounded things out with some much-needed artifact mana and a handful of Legend aids. [Concerted Effort](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concerted+Effort) and Akroma combine to make all of your other creatures into little Angelings, while [Day of Destiny](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Destiny) and [Vassal's Duty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal%27s+Duty) make Akroma and company even harder to successfully dispatch.
