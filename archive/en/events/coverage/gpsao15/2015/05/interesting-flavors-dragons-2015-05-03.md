Interesting Flavors of Dragons
==============================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX SÃO PAULO 2015](/en/events/coverage/gpsao15)
 on May 3, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman_icon_0.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











Round 13 has just ended and my, oh, my! Dragons, dragons, everywhere!


Ever since the release of *Dragons of Tarkir*, everyone's learning how to train their dragons! With the introduction so many cards pushing the dragon theme, there's bound to be something for everyone whether you're an aggro, midrange or control player!


Here are some of the Dragon-themed decks from Day 2 of Grand Prix São Paulo, along with their respective pilot's current score after Round 13! Just two more rounds and we will find out which dragon is the mightiest of them all!


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Haven+of+the+Spirit+Dragon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haven+of+the+Spirit+Dragon)


Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa has gone the distance and decided to play with the full set of [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai) today, shaving a copy of [Silumgar, the Drifting Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%2C+the+Drifting+Death). He has also found a spot for [Dragonlord Silumgar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Silumgar) in the maindeck, kicking [Ugin, the Spirit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ugin%2C+the+Spirit+Dragon) to the curb.


That brings his "dragon count" up to six, making [Silumgar's Scorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%27s+Scorn) and [Foul-Tongue Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foul-Tongue+Invocation) considerably better! [Dragonlord's Prerogative](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord%27s+Prerogative), another Dragon-themed card, will prove crucial in the mirror match.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dragonlord+Silumgar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Silumgar)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Silumgar%27s+Scorn)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%27s+Scorn)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dragonlord+Ojutai)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai)

Caio Amaral has shown up with a midrange Red Black Dragons deck, splashing a pair of Silumgar, the Drifting Death with the aid of Haven of the Spirit Dragon and a pair of Shivan Reef. His main offense comprises of Thunderbreak Regent and Stormbreath Dragon, who also help with "switch on" Draconic Roar and the full set of [Foul-Tongue Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foul-Tongue+Invocation).

Despite being the same colors as the previous list, Guido Barlaro has a very different approach. [Draconic Roar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draconic+Roar), [Silumgar's Scorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%27s+Scorn) and [Foul-Tongue Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foul-Tongue+Invocation) in his Grixis Control deck all aid in his mission stabilize the board. Barlaro is possibly the player playing with the most "Dragon Matters" cards in the entire tournament. Twelve in total, even without counting [Crux of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crux+of+Fate) and [Dragonlord's Prerogative](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord%27s+Prerogative)!


He has [Anger of the Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger+of+the+Gods) and [Crux of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crux+of+Fate) to wipe the board, and also [Icefall Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icefall+Regent) and [Dragonlord Silumgar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Silumgar) for a touch of board control. Eventually, the goal is to win with [Silumgar, the Drifting Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%2C+the+Drifting+Death) or the super awesome [Dragonlord Kolaghan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Kolaghan)!

Moreira's sideboarded [Rending Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rending+Volley)s can only mean one thing. He is prepared for Ojutai today!
