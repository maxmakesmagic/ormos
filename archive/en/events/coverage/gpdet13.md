Grand Prix Detroit 2013
-----------------------


McClain's Pod Plays Cop in Detroit
==================================




September 13-15, 2013












Grand Prix Detroit 2013
-----------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpdet13/trophy.jpg)


With a deck built to be part machine, part creature, and all combo, Josh McClain brought a third victory for the [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) and [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck in as many Grand Prix. After falling in defeat to Reid Duke in the finals of Grand Prix Miami, McClain had his revenge and policed the Jund menace that put six decks into the Top 8. While Melira-Pod was a constant threat throughout the weekend, it was only at the end of the event it mattered most.


Duke's Jund was one of many flavors found over the weekend, and the dominating presence of [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) was felt throughout. Modern is a format alive with diversity. Day 2 alone featured thirty-seven different types of decks, with those among the top tables drifting from combo to control to midrange and back again. In the end it was Jund that took the most top slots the longest despite Melira-Pod's final performance.


As we move on from Detroit, we know it won't be long for amazing **Magic** to happen again. With the *Theros* Prerelease and release events coming soon, everyone can get in on the action. After that, we'll revisit Modern in Oklahoma City to find out if *Theros* can push new decks to the top of the format.


Farewell from Detroit. We'll see you next time!

Finals - Josh McClain (Melira-Pod) vs. Reid Duke (Jund): The Revenge
--------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Marc Calderaro*

Â 

So they meet again, and so soon. The last time Josh McClain and Reid Duke faced off was in Miami in the finals of the [Grand Prix earlier this summer](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpmia13/welcome#8).


McClain recalled the time saying "Yeah, last time we met, he beat meâand with [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) too.""Beat" was a nice way to put it. I believe some of the coverage terminology included McClain being "systematically torn to shreds." But this time Duke was not even running the maligned Legend, though often a staple in Modern Jund. Duke's metagamed Jund list he's been calling "Chandra Jund," has proven itself perfectly tuned and launched him to the finals with minimal difficulty. But McClain's Melira-Pod could make short work of Jund decks, like Ben Stark learned in the semi-finals. The avenues of attack from Melira-Pod are varied and can keep any Jund player on his toes.


Duke played the aggressor in all three games. His fast, solid openings consistently set the pace, but McClain's deck wouldn't give up without a fight.


When Reid Duke approached the finals table, he looked McClain in the eye and said, "Josh McClain, fancy meeting you here." Reid Duke gave a warm smile, extended his hand, and McClain shook it heartily. The two sat down for the rematch. McClain had already walked across the broken glass barefoot, and he was ready for Reid to die hard.


### Game 1


Duke fired off quick, aggressive disruption. Jund came of the gates with two [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), and an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) revealing two [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer), [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling). McClain had stuttered early, and Duke, smelling blood, pressed the advance. He plucked [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and passed the turn back.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpdet13/finals_duke.jpg)  
*Reid Duke* 
Duke's next play was the strike that gave him command of the game. With the two Seers now on the battlefield for McClain, Duke calmly laid a [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and emptied McClain's hand of the final [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling). McClain had only two [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer)s and a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to his name, accompanied by some unhelpful land.

Josh McClain was down 0-1. But in Miami, it was Duke who found himself behind after the first game but rallied the next two to victory. McClain began to flip the script.


### Game 2


Duke again was able to control the board early. [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) into both [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and Grafdigger's Cage set a major crimp in McClain's style.



![Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shriekmaw)

Since combo street was temporarily blocked, McClain tried the aggressive route. His early plays of [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) built up the board. And he kept the secret of a [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) and [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) in his hand, waiting for the right moment to strike and blow Duke out of the water. Duke's secret was that after his initial aggression he had only lands left in his hands. There were manlands ([Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) and [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine)), but that was not enough gas.


Duke continued maximizing every inch. Though behind, he pushed and prodded, and even took the life-total lead when he used [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) to take the only way McClain had to deal with Duke's large [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). But it was just too slow. By that point McClain had cleared the Grafdigger's Cage, had a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and chained up a [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) with the other combo pieces on the board.

### Game 3


The audience was tense. By that point, all the spectators knew how much a win would mean to Josh McClain. Not only to win his first Grand Prix title against an incredible player, but against the very player snatched the title away from him the first time. Duke had been pressuring hard, and that didn't stop in the third game.


Unlike McClain's semi-finals win again Ben Stark, Duke continually threw out the opening salvos and made the underdog follow his lead. [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) into [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) was becoming an increasingly common sight for McClain. However this game, McClain was able to throw up a big stop sign early with a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpdet13/finals_mcclain.jpg)  
*Josh McClain* 
Duke was undeterred and had drawn his trump cardâ[Sword of Light and Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow). The turn it hit the board we all heard a resounding "thud." But, like last game, Reid's support in-hand was just land. McClain saw a chink in the Duke's armor.



![Sword of Light and Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow)

McCain decided to keep the [Sword](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow) without a wielder until he could destroy the equipment itself. Reid, for the second time in the match, extended his hand and said "You got me. Congratulations, Josh." The crowd erupted in applause. Josh McClain had defeated his rival to claim his first Grand Prix victory.


As the applause died down, someone in the audience yelled out "Until the next one!" And he was exactly right. Though today was Josh's day, the lifetime series of Duke-McClain was still tied 1-1.
