The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on April 7, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by [*Innistrad* throwbacks](http://necrogen-egregore.tumblr.com/post/142373319140/random-shadows-over-innistrad-throwbacks-bonus).


Today's Must
============


1. [Why I cosplay from *Magic: The Gathering*](http://www.themeadery.org/b/cspranklerun/read/why-i-cosplay-from-magic-the-gathering/) | The Meadery | Christine Sprankle

This tale from *Magic*'s most well-known cosplayer is two parts inspiring, one part depressing. The type of harassment Sprankle describes in her piece is one that's all too prevalent in the gaming world, and one that, simply put, doesn't belong in the *Magic* community. Or any community.


*Magic* is a game and a community that should be welcoming and inspiring to everyone. Sprankle's work [ever since that first event](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/feature-christine-sprankle-knight-errant-2011-11-19-0) has helped open up doors for cosplayers and women, and we think she's a wonderful ambassador for both—and for the game as a whole.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Modern Eldrazi Tron](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/32715_The-Dawn-Of-A-New-Modern-Era.html)

Since today seems to be unofficial Modern brewing day, we might as well lean into it.


Yesterday Michael Majors posted a whole bunch of [Modern decklists](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/32715_The-Dawn-Of-A-New-Modern-Era.html), but the one that caught *my* eye was the combination of the two decks weeping openly for the loss of theirs—Eldrazi and Tron.


With [Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) gone, Tron decks lose their best card for holding inevitability in the long game. Eldrazi decks, meanwhile, lose half of their reason for being. Maybe two halves can make a whole? Though the Tron deck is more like 72/75ths or so, so that math doesn't really work out.


Either way, Tron decks love having a ton of colorless mana, and so do Eldrazi decks, so it's a natural pairing. Majors swapped out [Eldrazi Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Mimic) for [World Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=World+Breaker) to go higher on the curve, plus the deck gets [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) to play with.


Who knew that Urza would be teaming up with an interdimensional threat one day to try to take down the Multiverse? For shame, Urza. For shame.
