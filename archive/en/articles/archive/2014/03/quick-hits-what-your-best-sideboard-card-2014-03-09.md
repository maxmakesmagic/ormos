QUICK HITS – WHAT IS YOUR BEST SIDEBOARD CARD?
==============================================



 Posted in [NEWS](/en/articles?source=MX_Nav2020)
 on March 9, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/calderaro.jpg)
By Marc Calderaro













![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gprich14/qh_froehlich.jpg)***Eric Froehlich**: Matej "Big Z" Zatlkaj* 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gprich14/qh_lau.jpg)***Gaudenis Vidugiris**: [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens). Because people are really bad at playing around it. Last round someone set [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) on [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot), then I untapped and cast [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) for 12.* 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gprich14/qh_zvi.jpg)***Zvi Moshowitz**: [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite). So many decks, when they do their thing, need to target something. Boggles just folds ... and Twin can’t go off either if it’s in play.* 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gprich14/qh_leyton.jpg)***Josh Utter-Leyton**: [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). It’s universally good versus combo ... it forces uninteractive decks to interact with you.* 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 


 



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gprich14/qh_ravitz.jpg)***Josh Ravitz**: [Fulminator Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage). It’s used in the most matches; and even some places where you wouldn’t think. I side them in against both [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End) and Affinity.*

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gprich14/qh_parke.jpg)***Jamie Parke**: [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel+)  #4 and [Flame Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Slash) #4. It's boring, but the extra copies are important. [Flame Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Slash) takes out [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) even when [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) doesn't ... and the extra one-mana counterspell is key against all the control decks.*
