Vintage Fro-Bots
================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 16, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






One thing I love about *Magic Online* is that you can play any format at any time. Most *Magic* players can't play Vintage, because they just don't have the means to acquire the highly sought after "Power Nine[[1]](#_ftn1)." It's a whole different story on *Magic Online*, however. Extremely rare cards like the Power Nine, [Mana Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Crypt), [Mishra's Workshop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Workshop), and many others are readily available, making the Vintage format much easier to get into digitally than in face-to-face *Magic*.



Today's deck is a Vintage deck that is an innovative take on a classic Vintage archetype usually known as Stax. The name came about because it used to play [Smokestack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokestack). The deck archetype consists of artifact mana as well as a lot of "lock out" cards like [Chalice of the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void), [Lodestone Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Golem), and [Sphere of Resistance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Resistance). The idea behind the deck is to play a ton of mana acceleration and then spells that make your opponent's spells cost more. Combine that with [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) and [Strip Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strip+Mine) and your opponent will never have enough lands to play their spells, thus locking them out of the game.



[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chalice+of+the+Void)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lodestone+Golem)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Golem) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sphere+of+Resistance)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Resistance)

Montolio's take on the deck earned him a 4-0 finish in a *Magic Online* Vintage Daily. This version is a little different from traditional Stax decks. Instead of large artifact creatures like [Steel Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Hellkite) or [Duplicant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant), this deck plays smaller creatures like [Arcbound Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) and [Phyrexian Revoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker). Low mana creatures are a smart move, as you can usually cast one early and attack with it until the game ends. [Arcbound Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) is especially strong, because as the game goes on you will likely have lots of artifacts in play that are no longer useful—like [Tangle Wire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Wire) or the Moxen.



The Vintage metagame is full of creatures like [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), so [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice) is another nice addition to this deck. Even [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull)—a card that usually only makes an appearance alongside [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)—is good here because it's pretty easy for you to accelerate to five mana with this deck.

[[1]](#_ftnref1) The Power Nine are nine cards from *Alpha*, *Beta*, and *Unlimited* that are widely considered to be the most powerful cards ever printed. These cards are: [Ancestral Recall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall), [Black Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Lotus), [Mox Emerald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Emerald), [Mox Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Jet), [Mox Pearl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Pearl), [Mox Ruby](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Ruby), [Mox Sapphire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Sapphire), [Timetwister](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timetwister), and [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk).
