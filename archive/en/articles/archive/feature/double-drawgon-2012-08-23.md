Double Drawgon
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 23, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











M***agic** 2013* has stolen most of the show recently when it comes to new and innovative decklists, which has naturally meant a focus on Standard. Still, at Daily Decks, we like to mix it up from time to time. Commander, Legacy, Modern, and Block all produce some pretty awesome decklists, which is why we turn our focus to the big-deck format today: Commander. Specifically, Hanyou submitted the following hundred-card list over at TCGPlayer.com, and it has quite the case of [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) killing the cat, along with everything else.


You see, about seven years ago, one of the greatest **Magic** sets ever, *Ravnica*, hit shelves. In fact, we will be [returning](/en/node/662986) to [that very place](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/feature/209) in a month or two. In this land of multicolor cards, many favorites were born. One Dragon stood head and shoulders above the rest, though, and he is today's Commander: [Niv-Mizzet, the Firemind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+the+Firemind). [Niv-Mizzet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet) has obvious player appeal, being a blue dragon and all, but he also happened to have one of the cooler text boxes anyone had ever seen. Immediately, a combo involving [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) plus [Niv-Mizzet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet) was realized and casual fans (along with some rogue competitive ones) got to work.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Curiosity | Niv-Mizzet, the Firemind |

Naturally, the combo works by [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) drawing a card upon damage being dealt, while [Niv-Mizzet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet) deals damage for each card drawn, creating a near-infinite loop limited only by the number of cards in your deck. Hanyou decided to play a little more fair, though, limiting the number of [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) clones to things like [Tandem Lookout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tandem+Lookout). Instead of using an instant-win combo, which is frowned upon in some circles, Hanyou played with a bunch of cards that everyone loves, like [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) and [Font of Mythos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos). It's pretty easy to make it to the late game and get all of your interactions working properly when everyone wants to keep you alive as you draw them additional cards!


Remember that in Commander, you have easier access to your general than any other card in your deck, allowing decks that are built around a single card to still exist. In this case, [Niv-Mizzet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet) turns most of the cards in your deck into powerhouses, so having access to him whenever you have the mana to cast him is quite nice. Now, where did I put those [Spellbook](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbook)s...
