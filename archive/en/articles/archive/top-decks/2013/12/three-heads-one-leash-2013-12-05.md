Three Heads, One Leash
======================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on December 5, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Welcome back to Perilous Research! Today, we'll be preparing ourselves for any and all Standard action going down this weekend. We'll be looking at the current Standard metagame in an effort to design a new and powerful Standard strategy for this weekend's tournament, whether it's a premier-level event like [Grand Prix Dallas/Fort Worth](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/grandprix/dallasfortworth13) or your local [Friday Night **Magic**](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/fnm). 





![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/widgets/storelocator/fnm.png)Find an FNM Near You!



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/widgets/storelocator/EN_LargeStoreLocatorGOButton_Static.png)

 The weekend before last, Owen Turtenwald made history by winning his second Grand Prix in as many weeks in [Albuquerque](/en/events/coverage/grand-prix-albuquerque-2013). The Top 8 in Albuquerque was dominated by two distinct archetypes: mono-blue and mono-black decks have firmly established themselves as the decks to beat in the current Standard format.

 One of the more interesting strategies that's been popping up as of late is a version of the Esper Control deck that combines the most powerful elements of that strategy with the best cards from the Mono-Blue Devotion deck. This deck was first brought to my attention by Jonathon Job, a pilot of interesting decks that routinely do quite well. A lot of players are wary of playing with a lot of the less-than-stellar one-drops we see in Mono-Blue Devotion decks, and this version of the deck allows them to play more cards that are independently strong without having to sacrifice the closing power of [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) or [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves). This deck's inconsistent mana can mean that it might not be the best choice for a large tournament like Grand Prix Dallas/Fort Worth, especially if we don't have any byes. However, if you're planning on entering an event with single-digit Swiss rounds then this is probably the strongest deck in a vacuum, considering the current state of the format.

 These are established decks that we can expect to do well this weekend, but I'd like to brew up something new and exciting. The popularity of Mono-Black Devotion has been pushing me further and further toward the ever-fluffy and adorable [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus). [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus) is one of the strongest available cards against decks like Esper Control and Black Devotion; these decks aim to grind us out of the game with one-for-one removal spells and big card advantage swings via [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections); [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought); or [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). Scooping up a pile of creatures from our graveyard usually puts these opponents in a pretty awkward spot. It's a shame we don't have access to a [Flametongue Kavu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu) or something like it, but the creatures we do have available are very strong and should give us a worthwhile deck to get started with. [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus) should be easy to trade for even as a mythic rare. Grab some unplayed copies out of your friend's binder; everyone loves a good rescue dog. 

 If we're playing with [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus), then we should probably be packing a full play set of [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze). It's a good card as it is, but removing the contents of our opponent's graveyard will be very important if we plan on resolving our [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus). We'll probably be grateful for the large body and lifegain, too, especially in creature matchups that require a lot of combat interaction.

 We want our deck to be packing a lot of creatures and one of the best ways to achieve this goal is to play a lot of mana producers that give us explosive starts. [Elvish Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic) and [Sylvan Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) may be a liability for many of the decks in Standard when facing off against [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), but a deck that's playing [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus) will be able to recoup that loss easily by simply letting its best creature die. We'll be playing a lot of [Elvish Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic) and [Sylvan Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) in this deck. 


[Reaper of the Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+of+the+Wilds) is a very powerful card. It's not as strong as it was for [Pro Tour *Theros*](/en/events/coverage/dezani-proclaims-allez-les-bleus-pro-tour), when the Standard format's removal spells of choice were [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) and [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). Still, the card gives us a great body and plenty of relevant text for only four mana. I'm a big fan of this card and I'll be happy to play a good number of it if I'm going to try out this color combination.

[Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon) is still one of the best four-mana creatures in Standard. [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) lets us charge at our opponent as early as the third turn. This deck really wants to apply pressure quickly. Getting the opponent to play defensively gives us an opportunity to stick a relevant [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus).

 I'm going to have access to a lot of mana if I'm playing three colors and a lot of [Elvish Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic)s and [Sylvan Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid)s. I can already dump my mana into [Underworld Cerberus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Cerberus), but we'd like to have other big spells of relevance. [Sire of Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+Insanity) and [Polukranos, World Eater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater) both seem like great cards to fill this role. [Polukranos, World Eater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater) is good enough that we may want to be playing additional copies, perhaps over some number of [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon)s or [Reaper of the Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+of+the+Wilds).  We'll need some removal spells if we want to stand a chance against Mono-Blue with a deck like this. A healthy mix of [Dreadbore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore), [Ultimate Price](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price), and [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) should do the job.
