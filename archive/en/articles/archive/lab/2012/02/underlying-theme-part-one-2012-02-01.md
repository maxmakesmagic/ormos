The Underlying Theme, Part One
==============================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on February 2, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello and welcome back to the Lab. Although my attention has recently been devoured by *Dark Ascension* and all of its goodness, I have some thematic matters to attend to for this week and next. Before last year ran out, I announced another of my silly but endearing contests.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl180_theme.jpg)  
This contest was all about building a deck around an external theme of the submitter's choice, be it a film, game, character, or something like that. I was particularly looking for decks that somehow conveyed the elements of what they were based on through their gameplay or card choices. Many decks were really fun to read and analyze. I felt pained nearly each time I eliminated a deck, but I had to whittle the field down to a smallish number. I went with ten submissions in the end, sorted by media type and split up for this week and next.

Enough chatter? Okay, let's begin.

Films!
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl180_memento.jpg)Many submissions were based on films. Nick Sorensen sent in a great deck based around the film *Memento*. 

"If you have not seen *Memento*, I suggest you do, because it is a very good but bizarre movie. You are the amnesiac Leonard Shelby, and you must set up a sequence of events that leads to the end. [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist) is your means of giving yourself and your opponent anterograde amnesia while [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Whispers of the Muse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispers+of+the+Muse), and [Halimar Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths) let you set up the next sequence of events that leads to your opponent's demise. [Arcane Denial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Denial), [Defense Grid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defense+Grid), and [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt) set you up to continue following your path."

I won't spoil the film for anyone, but this looks like an accurate reflection to me. [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist) essentially wipes your short term memories away, replacing them with a new set. And just like Leonard, you can [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) to keep yourself on track.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The victory condition? "The lone [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) is what ties this deck together. As Leonard, you have to hunt down your foe by using your cunningness." Having only one [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) also reflects one of the deeper secrets in the film, as you could draw into it too late and lose your grasp on what actually happened and what your memories really reveal.

In game, [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) obviously should be the death knell when you have a couple of [Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archivist)s working away.

Music!
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl180_abbey.jpg)I was delighted to receive a couple music-themed submissions. My radar for quirkiness twisted happily after reading Paul Bileci's deck based entirely around the song "Octopus's Garden" by the Beatles. If you haven't heard it, it's a joyful tune off of *Abbey Road*, a fine record that somehow, though, is only my third-favorite Beatles album.

Paul says, "It's a mana-ramp deck that builds up to casting [Lorthos, the Tidemaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorthos%2C+the+Tidemaker) so that you also have enough mana to activate his triggered ability. Alternatively, you can use [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) to pump out plants and pump them. [Wall of Kelp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Kelp) provides more plants you can use to block with while you ramp up. Most of the lands in the deck are [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s, so you can maximize [High Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Tide).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

How it's relevant to the song:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl180_lorthos.jpg)
> * The Octopus—[Lorthos, the Tidemaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorthos%2C+the+Tidemaker).
> * The Garden—[Khalni Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden), [Wall of Kelp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Kelp), [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) all make plant tokens. [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement) and [Sprouting Vines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Vines) suggest plant growth.
> * "Ask my friends to come and see"—All the various plant tokens the deck creates.
> * "We would be warm below the storm"— [Flusterstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flusterstorm) and [Sprouting Vines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Vines) both have the storm mechanic. Often, a storm brings a [High Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Tide) and [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas).
> * "In our little hideaway beneath the waves"—Hideaway lands [Mosswort Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mosswort+Bridge) and [Shelldock Isle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelldock+Isle); [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) is a wave along with [High Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Tide) and [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas). Also, [Asceticism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Asceticism) is living like a hermit, thus "hiding away."
> * "In an octopus's garden near a cave"—[Shelldock Isle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelldock+Isle), [Rhystic Cave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Cave), [Shimmering Grotto](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto).
> * "We would sing and dance around"—[Seasinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasinger), [Flusterstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flusterstorm) (I see the card as a bunch of faeries dancing around in the sky).
> * "Because we know we can't be found"—Hideaway lands; [Asceticism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Asceticism) gives all of your creatures hexproof, meaning they "can't be found."
> * "The coral that lies beneath the waves"—[Coral Atoll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Atoll).
> * "Oh what joy for every girl and boy, knowing they're happy and they're safe"—I just imagine all the little plant creatures (boys and girls) just dancing about and feeling happy and safe under the sea, in the caves, under the water, and under the protective cover of [Asceticism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Asceticism)."
> 

Awww.

Characters!
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl180_sherlock.jpg)Well, one D.C. Morgan decided to build a deck around Sherlock Holmes, the fictional detective.

D.C. says, "Sherlock was a clever fellow. He collected oodles of data about a crime scene ([Peek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek), [Spy Network](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spy+Network)) and combined this with his uncanny knowledge of the human mind ([Crystal Ball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Ball), [Architects of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Architects+of+Will)). This one-of-a-kind [Riddlesmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riddlesmith) (both flavorful and productive) uses his trusty magnifying glass (played by [Glasses of Urza](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glasses+of+Urza)) to gain as much information as possible on the criminal. He might thwart the villain's next few moves ([Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), [Addle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Addle), [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)) before the police ([Abyssal Persecutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor)) can come take the bad guy to the chokey. Or he might just lay a [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) for his nemesis. He's a [Compulsive Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research)-er, and has gained tons of useful knowledge ([Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)). Though he can be a bit [Stoic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoic+Rebuttal), he's one of the greatest detectives of all times!

"In play, you have your opponents discard cards as you draw more and more. Throw down [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) armed with your knowledge of the opponents' decks to swipe half their life, or swing with [Abyssal Persecutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor) before [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)ing it or flashing back [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy). I only put one [Riddlesmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riddlesmith) in the deck to show how one-of-a-kind Holmes is in his detective work (notice: no other looters). The mana-base pretty much works mechanically; the names themselves have no significance, but they help the deck feel more Sherlock-ish. [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) because of Watson's first encounter with Sherlock in 'A Study In Scarlet,' where Sherlock pricks his finger to demonstrate his new test for hemoglobin."

I like it! A [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) deck seems really fitting for Sherlock. [Abyssal Persecutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor) could also be the manifestation of Holmes's internal demons, sometime barring his way to uncovering something—perhaps the key to the whole case.

Anime/Manga!
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl180_fma.jpg)Hold your horses. Only one deck came in under this category. Don't freak out. I don't possess an expert knowledge of anime or manga stories, but one I am familiar with is the saga of *Fullmetal Alchemist*. Robert Richey submitted this one.

He says, "My submission for the theme deck contest is a tribute to *Fullmetal Alchemist*. The general theme of this deck reflects the themes from *Fullmetal Alchemist* by utilizing cards that refer to alchemy specifically, convert resources via the law of 'equivalent exchange,' and use the transmute or transfigure mechanics to tutor out the appropriate card or combo for a win. Some darker flavor, especially via life payment and exchanges made with the graveyard, is meant to convey the ultimate taboo of 'Human Transmutation'—the attempt to use alchemy to create life or bring back the dead. The most coveted object in the world of alchemy is the Philosopher's Stone, and if **Magic** has ever printed such a card, I would like to point out that the card is [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte). I stray away from the concept of mana as the means by which the deck casts spells, because alchemy is about exchange, not about magic. For this reason, you might have to look a little deeper to understand the way that converting life into mana, counters into creatures, life into creatures, and damage into power reflect the theme."

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte)  
Robert didn't have an extensive list of similarities, but that's okay; some are easy enough to ascribe. [Demonmail Hauberk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonmail+Hauberk) or [Ritual of the Machine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ritual+of+the+Machine) could be Alphonse. The transmute and transfigure synergies accurately echo the series in its versatility.

A couple ambitious readers built two thematic decks pitted against each other in the vein of the Duel Decks series. This week it's Nafiz Erman with his Jedi vs. Sith decks!

  
[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mind+Control)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control)  
Here are explanations for the Jedi deck:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/flt180_rafiq.jpg)
> * [Seaside Citadel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seaside+Citadel)—It really looks like the Jedi Temple.
> * [Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden)—The Jedi temple has a beautiful garden, apparently.
> * [Okina, Temple to the Grandfathers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Okina%2C+Temple+to+the+Grandfathers)—The only legendary creature I have is one copy of Rafiq, but this card looked Jedi-esque to me.
> * [Prahv, Spires of Order](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prahv%2C+Spires+of+Order)—This is also in because the art looks like the Jedi Temple.
> * [Rafiq of the Many](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rafiq+of+the+Many)—The new alternative art of him looks like Mace Windu, you know.
> * [Force Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+Spike)—No explanations are needed I think.
> * [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)—Obvious choice. Very Jedi-ish ability.
> * [Deflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deflection)—Another very fitting card.
> * [Telekinesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telekinesis)—How do you think they move those rocks around?!
> * [Mind Control](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control)—"You will take me to Jabba now!"
> * [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision)—"Difficult to see. Always in motion is the future."
> * [Plumes of Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumes+of+Peace), Path of Peace—These are two very fitting removal spells for the Jedi.
> * [Sword of Body and Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Body+and+Mind)—Lightsabers!
> * [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace)—The most fitting one.
> * [Armadillo Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak)—The Jedi wear cloaks all the time!
