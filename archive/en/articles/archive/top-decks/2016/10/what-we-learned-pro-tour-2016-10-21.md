What We Learned at the Pro Tour
===============================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on October 21, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 






There are some big stories coming out of the Pro Tour, and two control decks meeting in the finals (both piloted by Pro Tour Champions, no less) is one of the biggest. Today we are going to focus on the most exciting decks to come out of the Pro Tour and what the strengths and weaknesses of each are.


Let's jump in!


The Last Control Decks Standing
===============================


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Torrential+Gearhulk)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk)

**Game plan**: I put these decks together because they are quite similar—they are both blue-based decks touching red plus another color, and both heavily rely on the [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) plus [Glimmer of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmer+of+Genius) engine.


The plan of these decks is simple—kill or counter everything the opponent plays and win the game with [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) (sometimes [Wandering Fumarole](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Fumarole), [Thing in the Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thing+in+the+Ice), or [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn)).


**Key Features**:


* Powerful late game—[Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) ensures these decks don't easily run out of gas and don't lack a kill condition. They can turn on a dime, and go from playing defensively to attacking with 5/6s very quickly.
* Good card filtering—[Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate) and [Glimmer of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmer+of+Genius) make the three-color mana bases work and help these decks find the answers they need.
* Wide range of removal—These blue control decks can answer anything as long as they have enough time to find the answer.

**Weaknesses**: The main drawback to control decks such as these is that if they draw their answers in the wrong order, they can often get run over. Drawing counters against Red-White Vehicles or removal spells against [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) is a disaster, and [Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate) can only go so far. These decks also can sometimes run into decks they aren't equipped to deal with at all, and the lack of a proactive game plan makes that more likely.


**You Should Play This If**: You like being completely reactive and want to have all the answers. Gearhulk Control is a control mage's control deck, and we haven't seen decks this deep on control elements in quite some time.

**Key Features**:


* Effectively harnesses the power of [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) thanks to the twelve one-drops
* Uses extremely efficient removal spells like [Outnumber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outnumber) and [Declaration in Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Declaration+in+Stone)

**Weaknesses**: The cards in this deck are less individually powerful than in other versions of red-white, making its draws more inconsistent and worse in the late game. It's also more vulnerable to sweepers, as it often just has to overextend into [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames).


**You Should Play This If**: You like beating down. This is the fastest aggro deck in the format. It's also the best against linear decks like Temur Aetherworks as it has the highest chance of outracing them.

* This is the fastest deck in the format when its plan works.

**You Should Play This If**: You don't want to interact with your opponent's deck (and you like slamming Emrakul on turn four). This deck is particularly good in a field of red-white and black-green midrange.

**Game plan**: Attack the opponent with efficient creatures backed by removal spells and pump spells. Use [Servant of the Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+the+Conduit) to support a higher curve than most aggro decks.


**Key Features**:


* [Grim Flayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer) is excellent in a deck with [Verdurous Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdurous+Gearhulk) and [Blossoming Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Defense).
* Kalitas becomes especially dangerous when the opponent is forced to start blocking.
* [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) gives the deck extra lands while turning into extra action once the deck hits delirium.

**Weaknesses**: Black-green is slightly slower than most of the red-white builds (which is also why it does well in those matchups—you want to be a little bigger in the aggro mirror). That means it's vulnerable to decks like [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) and may not always be able to pressure control before [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk)s start raining down.


**You Should Play This If**: You liked the black-green deck from last Standard. This deck preys on a field of smaller aggro decks, and can demolish unprepared opponents thanks to [Blossoming Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Defense).

**Game plan**: Use cheap threats to establish a board presence, then punish the opponent with flash creatures like [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller), [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains), and [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn).


**Key Features**:


* It's one of the few decks that utilizes [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage).
* [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) and sideboard counters make the deck very good against linear decks like [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel).
* The deck is very difficult to play against because it operates at instant speed.

**Weaknesses**: This deck suffers against slower control decks, as [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage)s and removal are largely dead and the amount of pressure blue-white can apply is often not sufficient.


**You Should Play This If**: You like passing the turn with all your mana up. This deck is also a great choice in a field full of [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel), and has a robust game against red-white.


What Should You Play This Week?
===============================


Whatever deck speaks to you most! As usual, I'm a firm advocate of playing to your strengths, and there's a ton to choose from when looking at these decks. If you want an aggressive deck, a control deck, a combo deck, or a midrange deck, there are decks for you.
