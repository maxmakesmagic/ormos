Boros
=====



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 29, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Today, I'll be continuing my *Gatecrash* guilds week with Boros. Unlike with [Orzhov](/en/articles/archive/orzhov-2013-01-28), it feels a little wrong to do anything other than mindlessly attack with Boros. There are a lot of ways to do that, but I'm going to try to do it by pushing battalion as much as possible. Most of the best creatures with battalion also happen to be Humans, which invites [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish)—a card I've had a lot of success with. 


![Frontline Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Frontline+Medic)

[Doomed Traveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) isn't the most aggressive creature by itself, but it offers a relatively unique kind of staying power that helps us keep our battalion active before [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) is ready to defend it. With eight 3-power two-drops on top of eight roughly 3-power one-drops, we should easily be aggressive enough to get the opponent in range of our [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider)s and [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm)s.
