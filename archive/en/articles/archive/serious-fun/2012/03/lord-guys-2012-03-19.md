Lord of the Guys
================



 Posted in [Serious Fun](/en/articles/columns/serious-fun-archive)
 on March 19, 2012 









Let's get medieval today: lords are *en vogue* this year, and you're invited to party with the creature elite. It's a formal affair, so you're going to need to brush up on style for the occasion. Lords are particular about who they admit, but the rewards are fantastic.

Being a creature among your elite comes with prestige well worth the effort.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf187_zom.jpg)[Zombie Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Master) | Art by [Jeff A. Menges](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+[%22Jeff+A.+Menges%22])

"Lord" in **Magic** is an anachronistic term inherited from the beginning of the game. [Goblin King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+King) was not a Goblin but a *Lord* who granted all Goblins +1/+1 and mountainwalk. [Zombie Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Master) was similarly a Lord that granted regeneration and swampwalk to all Zombies.

While the creature type Lord is long gone, the feeling of tribal royalty is still strong. Are you ready to play courtier on the battlefield?

Like a Marionetteâ€”The House of Skeletons
If you want to dance with bones, you need to know the long history of the House of Skeletons:


> * [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) first appeared in *Limited Edition* (Alpha), and remained a staple of core sets throughout the years.
> * Some functional reprints were created: [Restless Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restless+Dead) from *Mirage* and [Unworthy Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unworthy+Dead) from *Urza's Saga*.
> * Several adaptations were printed: [Kjeldoran Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Dead) from *Ice Age*, [Urborg Skeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Skeleton) from *Invasion*, [Dimir House Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) from Ravnica, [Skeletal Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Changeling) from Lorwyn, [Reassembling Skeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton) from ***Magic** 2012*, and [Manor Skeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manor+Skeleton) from *Dark Ascension*.
> * A lord for Skeletons, the fearsome [Death Baron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron), first appeared in *Shards of Alara*.
> 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf187_death.jpg)[Death Baron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+[%22Nils+Hamm%22])

As far as tribes go, the Skeleton tribe has many members but only the aforementioned [Death Baron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) as a lord. (Note: [Adaptive Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton), and to a similar extent [Brass Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Herald), is the "[Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus)" of lords, and is just generally assumed.) Fortunately, their long history grants them a variety of interesting options.
