Sunday, 11:12 a.m. – Australian Road Warriors
---------------------------------------------


*by Event Coverage Staff*
Australia is, as we like to put it, bloody huge. It's the same physical size as the USA, except instead of Texans we have the world's deadliest collection of snakes, spiders, sharks, crocodiles, bunyips, drop-bears and swimming knives this side of a Bond villain's lair.


Despite the fact that our entire country seems *specifically* designed by Nature to maim the unwary, Grand Prix: Sydney has attracted a record-breaking 692 players over the course of the weekend.


And how they have travelled – we have contingents from every corner of Australia – from Perth, the world's most geographically isolated capital city, through Hobart and Auckland (Although some players claim New Zealand is its own country).


### The Adelaide Assembly


I caught up with Lee Copus, owner of *Infinity Games*, one of Adelaide's best-loved **Magic** stores. Lee and the Adelaidian **Magic** community organised a truly impressive contingent this year, with more than 30 players making the 1,407km journey up to Sydney. Adelaide has a proud history in Australian **Magic**, producing a National Champion, a good handful of National Team members, and a community of judges and players that is widely recognisable at all Australian **Magic** events. The range of players is impressive: Lee had brought his 9-year-old son, Ben, along for the weekend, who has been having a fantastic time at the GP.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/02_R9_Adelaide.jpg)Lee talked about how useful having access to social media like Facebook has been. "We were able to organise ourselves far better than we had previously", Lee said. "The store made a 'GP Sydney' Facebook group, and members posted decklists, talked about the Sealed format, and used the page as a gathering place to organise flights and hotel rooms amongst the players."


Lee highlighted how useful it was to have somewhere where players could organise themselves easily and efficiently. Coordinating accommodation amongst groups of **Magic** players – no easy task – becomes much easier where there's a place to post information and coordinate times.


### The Tasmanian Contingent


Tasmania is a little place in a big country, and has been forgotten about by the mainland on more than one occasion. Nonetheless, Tasmanians are some of Australia's most avid **Magic** roadies, making regular pilgrimages to the 'mainland' for PTQ's, Nationals, and the more recent WMCQ's. Tasmania regularly punches above its weight, 'stealing' mainland PTQ's and giving Australia a Nationals team member, Maitland Cameron, in 2011.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/02_R9_Tasmania.jpg)This year a sizeable Tasmanian team flew their way up to Sydney for the weekend. I had a chat with Ricky Aherne, Level 1 judge and pillar of the Tasmanian community, about their travel plans. The team had, like the Adelaide crew, mostly organised themselves on Facebook, and are sharing accommodation near the venue. More uniquely, Ricky has traveled not only as a player but also a legal guardian – several Tassie players are under 18, their parents entrusting Mr Aherne as a chaperone for the weekend.


"I've tried not to debauch them too much", Aherne said, "But they insist on drafting Golgari. What can you do?"


### The Internationals


Australia, in addition to being very large, is quite far away from the rest of the world. Without the advantage of plane tickets, it can be an economic strain for international players to attend Australian or NZ GP's.


A healthy handful of players from the Asia-Pacific are in attendance. Japan is directly above Australia, so for many Japanese pros, Australian GP's are their only chance to fly somewhere without jetlag. Czech players Martin Juza and Stanislav Cifka have made their way out after GP: Singapore, staying with some Sydney players and engaging in some of our national pastimes – beers and barbeques. Cheers!



Sunday, 11:21 a.m. – Round 10 : There can be only one
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*
Many older players will be familiar with the casual format affectionately referred to as "Highlander" where players can play no more than 1 copy of any card in their deck. Here in Australia, were life can be pretty competitive, what with all of the deadly animals and the like, we have a competitive Singleton format known as "7 Point Highlander."


In 7 Point Highlander, certain cards usually found on Banned or Restricted lists have been given a point value between 1 and 4, and your deck cannot contain more than 7 points of these cards. For instance, [Ancestral Recall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall) , [Black Lotus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Lotus) , and [Tinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker) are worth 4 points. [Demonic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Tutor) , [Sol Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring) , Moxes and a few other tutors are worth 3 points. Things like [Balance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balance) , [Library of Alexandria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Library+of+Alexandria) , and [Tolarian Academy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolarian+Academy) are worth 2, and a bunch of powerful stuff is worth 1 point, like [Hermit Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hermit+Druid) , [Mana Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Drain) , [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) , and [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) . The full points list, which is updated every few months or so, can be found [here](http://mtgau.com/viewtopic.php?f=12&t=20&sid=5abf0f7020ca565c73bdb991c73e62fe).

Round 9 Feature Match - Stanislav Cifka vs. Shuuhei Nakamura
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
### Game 1


Nakamura led the action with a [Centaur's Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur%27s+Herald) and a [Brushstrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brushstrider) . Cifka summoned a [Doorkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doorkeeper) , while Nakamura had a [Gatecreeper Vine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) to sit through his mana. Cifka summoned a [Drudge Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Beetle) , while Nakamura had a second Herald. Cifka had a [Rubbleback Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rubbleback+Rhino) , Nakamura popping one of his Heralds and then summoning [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile) . Neither player was keen on attacking.


Nakamura grew his army with a [Golgari Longlegs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Longlegs) , which Cifka was quick to kill with an [Auger Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree) . Cifka summoned a [Viashino Racketeer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Racketeer) , and both armies stared each other down across the battlefield. A second [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile) for Nakamura meant he outnumbered Cifcka's army considerably. Both players played draw-go for a few turns, the [Doorkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doorkeeper) slowly going to work on Nakamura's library.


Nakamura summoned a [Phantom General](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+General) , while Cifka had an [Axebane Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian) to bolster his defense. Nakamura had seen enough,and swung with two Centaurs , his [Brushstrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brushstrider) and two [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile) s. After blocks, Cifka lost his [Drudge Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Beetle) and Racketeer, and Nakamura binned a token and a Crocodile. Post-combat, Nakamura summoned a [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) and passed.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/02_R9_Cifka.jpg)*An opportunity to pun? Cifka would Nivver Mizzet.*
 

Cifka tapped his Guardian to summon [Niv-Mizzet, Dracogenius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Dracogenius) . Nakamura frowned and hurled an [Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact) at it, but Cifka had a [Launch Party](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party) to ease the sting a little, killing Nakamura's [Phantom General](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+General) . Nakamura attacked, knocking Cifka to 8, but Cifka had a [Street Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm) to clear Nakamura's board. Nakamura rebuilt with a [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) , but Cifka had a [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) to net himself a [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps) .


After an impressive two-turn reversal, Cifka went from being on the ropes to in a comfortable position, his Chaos Imp taking huge chunks out of Nakamura's life total. Nakamura looked at the ground defenses arrayed against him and decided to move on to the next game.


Stanislav Cifka 1 - Shuuhei Nakamura 0


### Game 2


Nakamura began the action with a [Centaur's Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur%27s+Herald) , using a [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) to take down Cifka's [Doorkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doorkeeper) . Cifka had a [Mana Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom) to help him ramp ahead, and summoned a [Rubbleback Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rubbleback+Rhino) , which fell to Nakamura's Centaur after a [Savage Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Surge) .


Cifka had no action, letting Nakamura summon another Herald and a [Concordia Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concordia+Pegasus) . Cifka found a [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion) and an [Axebane Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian) to give himself some board presence. Nakamura traded his Centaur for the Scorpion, but the Pegasus got in for a little nibble.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/02_R9_Nakamura.jpg)*7.3s after this photo was taken, the oxygen at Nakamura's focal point spontaneously combusted.*
 

Cifka summoned an [Ogre Jailbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Jailbreaker) and scavenged his Scorpion onto it. Nakamura kept pressing with his Pegasus, leaving his Herald back to block the Jailbreaker for a turn. Cifka summoned his [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps) – more than a match for the Pegasus – before passing. Nakamura had a [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) but was unable to attack. Cifka had a [Thoughtflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtflare) to re-stock his hand, but that was just rubbing salt in the wound as the Jailbreaker and [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps) crashed in to take the match.


Stanislav Cifka 2 – Shuuhei Nakamura 0
