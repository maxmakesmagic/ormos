Top 25
======


1. The Grand Prix Bump

We had three Grand Prix this weekend, and the bump players got from strong finishes at those events was very real. Two of the winners—Brad Nelson and Lukas Blohon—had big jumps. Nelson (who won [Grand Prix Omaha](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpoma17)) rose to No. 6 while Blohon (who won [Grand Prix Amsterdam](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpams17)) entered the charts at No. 18. Meanwhile, Sam Pardee, who lost to Nelson in the finals of Omaha, also jumped into the rankings at No. 19. Likewise, Martin Jůza, Eric Froehlich, and Oliver Tiu all saw gains this week.


But this also means we saw two more players drop from the rankings. Yuuki Ichikawa and Alexander Hayne couldn't keep their grips on the Top 25 and slid off the rankings. Both will almost certainly be back, but, for now, they're on the outside looking in.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Lukas Blohon's Black-Green Delirium](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpams17/top-8-decklists-grand-prix-amsterdam-2017-06-04)

There was a time, not that long ago, when Black-Green Delirium—and its ancestor Black-Green Constrictor—was the best deck in the format. This is something of an amalgam between those two decks, sporting the [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) packages but also rocking [Grim Flayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer) and [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald).


The deck is clearly back on the radar again, and Blohon used it to win a Grand Prix the same weekend teammate Brad Nelson won with Marvel—so it's not like Blohon didn't have access to that deck. Instead he chose to sleeve up a few bears, a 2/3 for two, and as many [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista)s as he could legally play.
