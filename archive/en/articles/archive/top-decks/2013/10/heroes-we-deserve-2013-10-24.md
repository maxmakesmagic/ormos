Heroes We Deserve
=================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on October 24, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Last weekend, [Grand Prix Louisville](/en/events/coverage/braun-duin-leader-pack-louisville) showcased the latest and most powerful strategies in Standard. **Magic** needs heroes, and there was no shortage of heroism at Kentucky's Grand Prix. A few days prior to the Grand Prix, Todd Anderson had made a new black devotion deck that he and his friends deemed the deck to play for the weekend. Five players arrived on site on Saturday morning armed with the latest dark technology. Of those five, three (Brian Braun-Duin, Brad Nelson, and Todd Anderson) found themselves in the [Top 8](/en/events/coverage/braun-duin-leader-pack-louisville).


[![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr270_tormentedhero.jpg)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=373541)
Brad Nelson's Top 8 in Louisville last weekend marks his third Top 8 in a row at Standard North American Grand Prix events. Even more amazing is the fact that he's Top 8ed four out of the last five. Nelson even Top 16ed the only North American Standard Grand Prix event in the last five that he didn't Top 8. With a Top 32 finish at [Pro Tour *Theros*](/en/events/coverage/dezani-proclaims-allez-les-bleus-pro-tour), it has become crystal clear to all those paying attention that Brad Nelson is the best Standard mage in North America, if not the world.


Brian Braun-Duin has been quietly amassing his own resume over the last couple years. Braun-Duin was my teammate for Pro Tour *Theros*, and in the month leading up to that event I was able to witness his tuning prowess and work ethic firsthand. Last weekend, Braun-Duin heroically bested 1,046 others to be crowned the Grand Prix Louisville champion. This means that Braun-Duin will be qualified for the upcoming Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* in Valencia, Spain, and it puts him in a strong position to achieve Gold status on the Pro Tour. I am confident that Braun-Duin has what it takes to play at the highest level and I'm looking forward to his rise to power.


Today, we'll be taking a look at the Standard format in the wake of Grand Prix Louisville. We'll start by taking a look at the breakout deck from last weekend's Grand Prix and then move on to examine the most successful archetypes on **Magic Online**.


Let's look at some decklists!

Black Devotion has come out of obscurity and found itself atop the new Standard metagame. The deck uses tons of spot removal and disruption to survive the early turns. It eventually grinds out card advantage with [Erebos, God of the Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erebos%2C+God+of+the+Dead) and [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), until its opponent helplessly flounders and is overwhelmed by a pack of rats or [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel) triggers. [Temple of Deceit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Deceit) allows the deck to cast blue spells found with [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter)—these can be changed to [Temple of Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Silence) if the format starts leaning white instead of blue.

[Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) gives us three blue devotion even though we're spending black mana to cast it. This ensures that [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter)ed [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) makes a ton of Elemental tokens. The deck preys on midrange and control strategies, but struggles to keep up against the most aggressive decks. In the coming weeks, we can expect White Weenie variants, aggressive Red strategies, and green-white decks to experience a resurgence in the wake of Black Devotion's dominance. [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) in conjunction with tons of lifegain makes this deck extremely difficult to contend with in a long game. The best way to combat this deck is to run it over quickly.

We can expect the Blue Devotion decks to oscillate from the bottom to the top of the totem pole on **Magic Online** in step with [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)'s popularity. The deck's [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) comes completely out of nowhere and can often leave an opponent with no cards in hand. This won't be the case once players catch on to the deck, but it's still fringe enough that we should be able to catch our opponent off-guard. [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) seems pretty difficult to cast here, and I might cut a [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx) or two in favor of more islands. That being said, the deck plays a lot of Standard's most powerful cards and I wouldn't be surprised if this type of strategy became one of the major players in the new Standard.

It's strange seeing a Suicide Black deck at 4–0 in a Standard Daily event, but it makes sense when you think about it. [Tormented Hero](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Hero) and [Rakdos Cackler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) give the deck eight one-mana creatures with 2 power. [Lifebane Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie) represents a consistent source of damage against all the other decks that aren't Black Devotion while also seriously disrupting decks like White-Black Midrange, Gruul Devotion, and Red-Green Midrange. [Xathrid Necromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xathrid+Necromancer) makes the deck resistant to [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), [Hero's Downfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall), and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) all give the deck enough disruption to power through most opposing game plans. The deck does, however, seem very weak to Black Devotion, especially on the draw, and this may be a nail in the coffin until the Black Devotion deck wanes in popularity a bit. This is likely a good aggressive strategy to have in your back pocket if the format becomes dominated by [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) control decks and Green-White or Red-Green midrange strategies.

This deck has a lot of haste and its strong removal package makes it a nightmare for Blue Devotion strategies. The deck can struggle with Esper Control if it draws too much burn and not enough Planeswalkers and hasty monsters. This can be offset with [Chandra's Phoenix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Phoenix), but we're not guaranteed to draw our key 2/2 haste flier. Postboard, the deck can become an anti-control-black devotion monster. This deck might struggle to deal with a postboarded Gruul Devotion strategy and it seems somewhat draw dependent, but the overall plan here seems like a very good place to be in the current Standard format.
