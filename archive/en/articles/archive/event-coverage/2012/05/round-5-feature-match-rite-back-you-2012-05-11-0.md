Round 5 Feature Match: Rite Back At You!
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 11, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett












[Melissa DeTora vs. Florian Pils](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptavr12/fm4)


Pat Cox (Reanimator) vs. Jeremy Neeman (Blue-Red-Green Control)
---------------------------------------------------------------


[Brad Nelson vs. Kai Budde](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptavr12/fm6)


Australia's Jeremy Neeman is perhaps best known as Luis Scott-Vargas's professional nemesis. The two have met seven times at top-level play, and Neeman has won five of them. Despite winning a pair of Grand Prix events, Neeman is still looking for that one big finish to put an exclamation point on his resume. That's making this weekend doubly important. Even if he he doesn't Top 8 this weekend, a strong performance would lock up the APAC slot at the Players Championship for him this summer.


This round he face off against Pat Cox, who was a relative unknown until his Top 8 at Pro Tour Nagoya last year. For pros in the know, this came as no surprise. Cox has quietly built a reputation as a master in the making. This weekend he's playing a reanimator deck designed by Pro Tour Champion David Sharfman, ingeniously named "Raisin' 'Brand".



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptavr12/r5_cox_vs_neeman.jpg)
*While Pat Cox is piloting a deck with a sweet name, Jeremy Neeman is hoping to lock up his invitation to the Players Championship.*

### Game 1


Neeman didn't take long to send his first hand back. He kept his six, and they were off.


Cox led with [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), then set up a big turn with [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), getting rid of [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight). He played a second Pilgrim, and passed. Neeman dispatched one of them with [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame). Cox [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch)ed some lands to hand and hit for one. Neeman got rid of the other Pilgrim with [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play).


Cox flashed back his Looting and binned a few more useful spells. Neeman played [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) and passed back with [Sulfur Falls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Falls) and two [Desolate Lighthouse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolate+Lighthouse) open.


Cox tapped four. "Is [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) going to resolve?"


Neeman frowned. "It's going to resolve."



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptavr12/r5_cox.jpg)
*I heard turn four [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Gisela%252C%2BBlade%2Bof%2BGoldnight)s are nice.*

[Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) entered the battlefield. Neeman fetched up a forest, then untapped and made a Huntmaster. [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Gisela%252C%2BBlade%2Bof%2BGoldnight) paid him a quick visit to the tune of ten damage. Another draw and Neeman was packing up his cards.


Pat Cox 1, Jeremy Neeman 0


### Game 2


Neeman laughed and shook his head. "Yeah, that was a pretty good draw. Try not to do that again."


Cox took one mulligan, keeping his six. Neeman led with [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds), getting an island after Cox played [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim). [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play) took care of it, but Cox had a replacement on the following turn. After Neeman played [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist), Cox dropped [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells). Neeman had [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame), and a second Huntmaster put him even further behind.


What he really needed was [Rolling Temblor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor), but it was nowhere to be found. He hit for three in the air and added [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) to his team, locking down Cox's [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptavr12/r5_neeman.jpg)
*Neeman attempts to battle his way through multiple Huntmasters.*

Cox drew and considered things. He eventually settled on [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), freeing his Huntmaster, getting him another wolf, and putting a huge blocker in the air. Meanwhile, [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) was taxing Neeman's already-constrained mana. He tapped two for [Desperate Ravings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings), losing a land and playing another one, then passed the turn.


Cox sent his wolves and angel in. Neeman's fliers blocked wolves, and a post-combat [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) for two left Neeman at 10 and with no board. He untapped and played Tamiyo, freezing the Angel. The rest of Cox's squad killed Tamiyo and he passed the turn to flip his Huntmaster. Neeman was down to just 8.


He untapped and played [Desperate Ravings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) into [Hinterland Harbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hinterland+Harbor) and a Huntmaster of his own, clawing his way back to 10. Cox's Huntmaster flipped back, and he attacked with his Angel and a wolf token. The wolves traded, and then came a post-combat [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons). Neeman looked at one more card, then conceded.


Pat Cox 2, Jeremy Neeman 0
