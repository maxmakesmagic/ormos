Standard Jeskai Black
=====================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on October 9, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a member of the Wizards of the Coast coverage team. 






Most players have speculated that [Crackling Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Doom) would become more powerful once *Battle for Zendikar* rotated into the format, and sure enough, [Crackling Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Doom) showed up in a variety of decks last weekend. Interestingly, Mardu was not the color combination of choice for [Crackling Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Doom), and instead players splashed the card in their existing Abzan or Jeskai decks.


Today's deck is a Jeskai build that splashes black for [Crackling Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Doom). As you can see, Jeskai didn't change all that much after the rotation. We lost [Goblin Rabblemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Rabblemaster) and a few burn spells, but the core cards are still present: [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider), [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy), [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash), and [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time).


There are a few innovations in Jeskai, the most obvious one being [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) and [Dragonmaster Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonmaster+Outcast). [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) had already been seeing play due to its synergy with [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy), but [Dragonmaster Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonmaster+Outcast) is a new addition to the party. The Outcast doesn't do much in the early game besides soak up some removal spells or get discarded to [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy), but the strength of this card after turn six is too high to ignore. You can easily cast an [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) on your opponent's end step to return your Outcast to play, and if your opponent's removal is only sorcery speed, then there's nothing stopping you from getting a free Dragon.


The new *Battle for Zendikar* lands play a really important role in this deck alongside fetch lands. These lands make it very easy to play four colors, and as you can see from the decklist, [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery), previously the best land in the deck, only shows up as a two-of. Additionally, with the high number of basic lands in the deck, you have far fewer lands entering the battlefield tapped, allowing you to play a faster deck with a less awkward mana curve. The high fetch land count also makes your [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time)s better. You will be filling your graveyard more quickly, allowing you to cast them earlier.


Four-color midrange decks seem to be the norm now with the new options in *Battle for Zendikar*, and I would be surprised to see many midrange decks playing less than three colors going forward.
