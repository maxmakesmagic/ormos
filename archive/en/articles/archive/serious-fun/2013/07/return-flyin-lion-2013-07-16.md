Return of the Flyin' Lion
=========================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on July 16, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_brucerichard.jpg)
By Bruce Richard




 Bruce's games invariably involve several friends, crazy plays, and many laughs. Bruce believes that if anyone at your table isn't having fun, then you are doing it wrong. 







A[few articles ago](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/flyin-lion-2013-06-04), I built an alpha version of a deck featuring [Krond the Dawn-Clad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krond+the+Dawn-Clad). The deck was created because I like the card and wanted to try to feature him. I mean, a flying lion who wears sunshine?! I included a variety of cards, most of them somehow related to Auras, then mashed them together to see which cards were exciting and which just weren't going to make the cut.

At the end of the article, I asked for your thoughts on the deck. The plethora of emails and comments in the forum were great. Between your comments and my experiences with the deck after a few games, I have made some significant improvements to the deck.


### Problems


After just a few games, a couple of problems were very obvious. The first was that the deck was simply too slow. Most multiplayer games let you dance dangerously with your mana. With multiple opponents, a slow start is something that you can get away with in many games. This deck, though, was just too slow. The mana base offered very limited ramping, and I was rarely able to play Krond on turn six. His triple-white, triple-green cost was just too much, even for my deck loaded with nonbasics that could produce either color. When the feature card in your deck rarely gets on the battlefield before turn eight, you know you are going to have problems.


The second problem was closely related to the first. Since [Krond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krond) was often a non-factor, I was forced to use other cards in ways they weren't meant to be used in the deck. Wrapping [Faith Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith+Healer) in three Auras is cute and makes for some interesting in-game discussions, but it really wasn't what I was going for.


### Easy Outs


[Gatecreeper Vine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) and the [Selesnya Guildgate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildgate)s. The Guildgates are simply subpar lands if you don't have a way to take advantage of the Gate subtype. The [Gatecreeper Vine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) just didn't do enough to justify the Guildgates. Getting a Guildgate in my hand wasn't enough upside when compared to dealing with lands that enter the battlefield tapped.

[Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage). I really like this card, but it just isn't as good as [Armadillo Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak). The Cloak allows you to stack lifegain, making multiple copies even better. The Cloak also lets you gain life when you attach it to an opponent's creature, so it can be a neutralizer in a pinch. [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) does none of that. If you don't have [Armadillo Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak), [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) is a fine substitute. For this deck, I wanted other options, but when you are looking at [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) in your decks, go with [Armadillo Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak): there is no reason not to use them.

### Easy Ins


Almost everyone who wrote in recognized that mana was likely going to be an issue. A couple of players suggested [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern) as a solution. Others wanted more [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), [Harrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow), and other green ramp spells. While I like the idea of the [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern), since it would allow me to splash cards from any color, I decided that I didn't want to be that dependent on a card for some of my spells when I'm already leaning on [Krond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krond).


I opted for a solution many others recommended: [Selesnya Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Signet). While artifacts are more likely to be destroyed than lands in most games, I have found that these low cost artifacts don't tend to draw the hate the way a [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring) or [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) does. The Signets were easy to include.


Other popular suggestions were [Somberwald Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage) and [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). Many players saw the Sage as an easy way to fulfill at least half of [Krond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krond)'s color requirements early in the game, then as a chump blocker once [Krond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krond) was already in play. [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) have the two-fold purpose of pumping your mana base and providing an evasion creature that can carry Auras in a pinch.

### Other Suggestions


Brian wrote in with several suggestions, including [Ghostly Prison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison) and [Sigil of the Empty Throne](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne). Considering the problems I was having with the early game, [Ghostly Prison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison) seems like an excellent solution. The Prison is an effective deterrent in the early game and shuts down opponents relying on overwhelming you with 1/1 tokens.


[Sigil of the Empty Throne](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne) is a different bird entirely. The Sigil really demands a deck of its own. Cards like [Flickering Ward](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickering+Ward) and enchantments with flash are some of the cards that make the Sigil really shine. Those cards don't really work in this deck. I've seen a couple of powerful [Sigil of the Empty Throne](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne) decks, but this deck doesn't make the card pop.

### Final Cuts


If I was going to add in the recommended cards, I was going to need to remove many others. [Evershrike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evershrike), [Faith Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith+Healer), and [Kitsune Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Mystic) were the first creatures to be dropped. [Evershrike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evershrike) was five mana and demanded the Auras be put on it. That was proving difficult, considering [Krond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krond) wants at least one Aura as well, and as you'll see, the Aura count has come down. [Faith Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith+Healer) was in there as a way to gain life by repeatedly sacrificing [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor). With Felidar Umbra in the deck, [Faith Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith+Healer) became redundant. [Kitsune Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Mystic) was another card that just took too long to develop and wasn't as helpful with Felidar Umbra in there already moving to whichever creature needed it.


[Dryad Militant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Militant) and [Aura Gnarlid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Gnarlid) were more difficult to cut but were the next to go. The [Kor Spiritdancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Spiritdancer)s were already doing the job the [Aura Gnarlid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Gnarlid) was doing, and it was giving me free card drawing as well. The [Dryad Militant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Militant) had done good work in the games that I had played, but it was as a 2/1 for one; its effect wasn't doing much since it was invariably used as a chump blocker.
