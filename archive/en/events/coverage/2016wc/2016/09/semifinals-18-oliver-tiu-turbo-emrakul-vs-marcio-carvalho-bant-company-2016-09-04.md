Semifinals: (18) Oliver Tiu (Turbo Emrakul) vs. Márcio Carvalho (Bant Company)
==============================================================================



 Posted in [2016 WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP](/en/events/coverage/2016wc)
 on September 4, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











After fourteen rounds of intense competition, the field of 24 has boiled down to just four. After watching Brian Braun-Duin demolish Shota Yasooka in the first semifinals, all the attention has shifted to this match, a showdown between the Draft Master and the Constructed Master. The victor will advance to the finals and battle against Braun-Duin for the immortal title of 2016 *Magic* World Champion.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/2016wc/WC-20160904-396.jpg)  
*Márcio Carvalho (left) and Oliver Tiu had fought long and hard to advance to the Top 4 from a field of the best 24 players in the world.*


Now that the Draft rounds were over, it would seem like Tiu had the upper hand—being the Constructed Master and all. The matchup leaned in his favor, not only because of his staggering statistics but also because Turbo Emrakul could go over the top against Bant Company.


Tiu's machine is centered around casting [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) as quickly as possible, and its late game is incredibly powerful. A long-drawn game will often benefit Tiu, but since his intricate machine relies on several non-overlapping spinning parts, he will appreciate all the time he can get to gather them.


What spinning parts, you may ask?


Firstly, [Primal Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Druid)s are great roadblocks against Bant Company's early forces, and it will go a long way in preserving Tiu's life total. [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) serves a similar function, to clog up ground while also keeping opposing fliers at bay.


Next, in order for Emrakul and Ishkanah to be effective, Tiu will also need one of his many graveyard enablers, such as [Gather the Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+the+Pack), [Grapple with the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past), and [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency). With these cards, he will not only to be able to find a desired threat but also bin [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kozilek%27s+Return)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End)
Now, [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) is the one card any Bant Company player has to be extremely wary of. Since Tiu has eight Eldrazi in his main deck and the potential to search for them very consistently with [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald), having [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) in the graveyard simply means that Carvalho could lose his entire army in a blink of an eye.


To cast some of these expensive Eldrazi, Tiu will also need to find a small creature such as [Primal Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Druid) or [Pilgrim's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim%27s+Eye) as sacrificial fodder since [Wretched Gryff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Gryff) and [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) have emerge. Lastly, to get sufficient mana for all the endgame bombs and to keep the gears oiled, there is also [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage) for mana acceleration and [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) for additional card selection.


What does this entail for Carvalho?


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Collected+Company)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spell+Queller)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)
Carvalho's goal is to quickly summon some creatures to apply early pressure and hope to overload Tiu with [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company). He'll also need to use [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) aggressively on [Primal Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Druid) or [Pilgrim's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim%27s+Eye) to prevent oversized Eldrazi from emerging too early. He has two copies of [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) of his own to tap down Tiu's lands during the crucial "Emrakul turn" and also to entangle [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) and her babies, which can *also* be elegantly exiled with [Declaration in Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Declaration+in+Stone).


Most importantly, Carvalho has [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)—the best card against Tiu's deck. If he finds multiple copies to counter some of Tiu's key spells and protects his board with [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) or [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn), he will be in good shape. After sideboarding, Carvalho's trump against Emrakul and her accompanying trigger is the pair of Dismissal Summarys. Also, [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) is near impossible for Tiu to remove once it hits the board.


It's difficult to say who will *emerge* victorious at this point, so let us get on with the games!


The Games
=========


Carvalho was on the play, but Tiu had the first spell of this game in the form of [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency) He decided against cracking it open because Carvalho summoned [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter) on his second turn, and Tiu certainly didn't want the Recruiter to transform. He opted for [Primal Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Druid) instead, but it was promptly sent home by [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage). Unable to recast the Druid, Tiu opted for [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage) instead.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tamiyo%2C+Field+Researcher)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+Field+Researcher)
The lack of blockers was a golden window of opportunity for Carvalho to fully take advantage of [Tamiyo, Field Researcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+Field+Researcher). Not only did he successfully connect with two attackers, he drew two cards in process. Tiu [Gather the Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+the+Pack)ed and summoned [Nissa, Vastwood Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Vastwood+Seer), hoping that she would stick around. She didn't, because Carvalho was ready with [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command), killing Nissa while turning [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter) into a 3/3. Both creatures crashed in for 5 more damage and Carvalho helped himself to another two cards as the fruits of Tamiyo's research.


Things were certainly looking good for Carvalho, and it looked like things were spiraling out of control for Tiu. With Tiu down to 9 life and facing a 4/3 [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker), 3/3 [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter), and 2/3 [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage), all Carvalho had to do was tap down [Primal Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Druid) with Tamiyo's -2 ability and clinch the first pre-sideboarded game.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/2016wc/WC-20160904-449.jpg)  
*Carvalho held off Oliver's Tiu to claim victory in Game 1.*


Things did not work out very well for Tiu in Game 1, but the outlook seemed a little better now that he was on the play. Kicking off with [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) and [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage), he found himself under pressure from [Noose Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noose+Constrictor) and [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker). Carvalho did not have white mana, so it appeared he was behind, but he also didn't know that Tiu's hand contained only lands and two awkward Emrakuls he couldn't cast.


To sum it up, Tiu was pretty much trying to maintain his [Empty City Ruse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+City+Ruse) with a straight face.


Lowering Tiu down to 13 life after attacking, Carvalho summoned [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) and passed, missing his fourth land drop. When Carvalho turned all his creatures sideways next turn, Tiu blocked [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) with [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) and activated it before damage to jump the planeswalker out of harm's way. The severely land-flooded Tiu topdecked [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return), which he cast at the end of Carvalho's turn. Untapping and summoning [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) triggered [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) again to clean out Carvalho's board.


Thereafter, Tiu popped [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency) and [Grapple with the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past), receiving two copies of [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow). Suddenly, there were seven Spiders on the board! Then, Tiu used [Jace, Telepath Unbound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Telepath+Unbound) to recast [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) for Elder-Deep Fiend, safeguarding himself against any potential slip-ups.


Topdecking another [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) thereafter, Tiu searched up his final copy of [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) and whittled Carvalho's board down with a suicidal attack. Carvalho now had two creatures against Tiu's nine. After confirming that no card in his deck could save him, Carvalho scooped up his cards and reached for the sideboard.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/2016wc/WC-20160904-428.jpg)  
*Tiu equalized the match with a Game 2 win.*


Being land-light at the start of Game 3, Tiu popped [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) for an Island. He soon faced [Krallenhorde Howler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krallenhorde+Howler) because Carvalho passed his third turn, representing [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller). Tiu played around it, declining to cast [Pilgrim's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim%27s+Eye). To keep up the pressure, Carvalho flashed in [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) anyway and the attacks brought Tiu down to 13.


Carvalho's next creature was [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate), and you'll notice that all three creatures he controlled had 3 toughness. This was relevant against the [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) in Tiu's hand. In order to get it into the graveyard where he wanted it to be, he burnt through it by casting it despite not affecting the board at all. [Pilgrim's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim%27s+Eye) was the next play, and it was the perfect creature to sacrifice to any emerge creature. Carvalho wasn't going to let that happen and bounced the flier with [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage). After Carvalho attacked for 7 more damage, Tiu was down to 5 life and saw no way to get out of this mess.


On to Game 4!


Tiu got to work, filling out his graveyard with [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency) (oh, delirium!) and ramping ahead with [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage), temporarily ignoring [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate). With Carvalho only at three lands, Tiu knew that [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) would resolve for sure and things were in great shape when it did. The follow up was a 3/3 [Gnarlwood Dryad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnarlwood+Dryad), and Tiu looked like he had a great defense. All he needed to do was buy himself more time so that he could cast [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) with [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) backup. His next two plays of [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency) and [Gather the Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+the+Pack) didn't resolve, which impeded his progress. [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)s had chomped them up.


Then, Carvalho's next series of plays turned the tides entirely, using [Declaration in Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Declaration+in+Stone) to kill all Spider tokens and [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) to bounce Ishkanah. Tiu pondered for a moment before sacrificing Ishkanah to [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) in response, buying himself a turn of respite. However, now that the skies were totally clear, Carvalho was able to soar through with the pair of [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)s.


Three combat steps later, Carvalho put himself through to face Brian Braun-Duin for the title of 2016 *Magic* World Champion.


**Márcio Carvalho defeated Oliver Tiu 3–1 to advance to the finals!**


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/2016wc/WC-20160904-610.jpg)  
*Márcio Carvalho basks in euphoria, having survived past Oliver Tiu to reach the final match.*
