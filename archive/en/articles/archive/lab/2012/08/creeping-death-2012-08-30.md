Creeping Death
==============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on September 3, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Welcome back to the Lab. *Return to Ravnica* previews! All right! But first, some quick thoughts on death.

We are all in the presence of death, living up until the firm experience. Death blankets life, spreads over and through it, tunneling a path to a nowhere. We witness the rigidity of death from various distances without fully understanding. We only die when we live, or, as Metallica put it, "[To Live Is to Die](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/To_Live_Is_to_Die)."

I'm sure that reading my amateurish philosophic droppings (gleaned from many Metallica records) is entertaining and all, but I do have a preview to unveil. Recognize (and dare I say it, *embrace*) Death's Presence. 

![Death's Presence](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/tcg/products/rtr/qnaq9p9lfk_en.jpg)  


If the Golgari ever recorded a thrash metal album, I would be beside myself. As Death's Presence can aptly demonstrate, the black and green guild that lurks in Ravnica's dungy greenhouses and rotten pastures knows how to channel the atmospheric force that is death into various forms of aggression and power. In the past, the guild was adept at stuffing the graveyard, using the head-twisting mechanic known as dredge that went on to enable, oh, decks to win the game on the first turn without spending a drop of mana. This time around, the Golgari seem to be content with scuttling and scavenging, as revealed by the likable Dreg Mangler.

![Dreg Mangler](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/tcg/products/rtr/gpe5rp56g0_en.jpg)  
So it makes perfect sense that Death's Presence is tinged with a bit of Golgari flavor. But whether you are battling on Ravnica or not, this enchantment can definitely tip the scales in your favor. It bears many of the hallmarks of a classic Johnny card: it hinges upon a death trigger that happily recognizes the death of tokens as well, and it is costed at a slightly expensive level to prevent shenanigans from blasting off too early in the game. Of course, there are ways around everything. But Death's Presence is a welcome piece of my revolving combo card complex, and I'd like to show off some of the many deck skeletons that incorporate it.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl211_whr9skb41q.jpg)Death's Presence | Art by [Ryan Barger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Ryan%20Barger%22%5D)

Death's Presence is a straightforward card that obviously wants to see lots of your creatures die. Not of all them, of course, since you need a body to stack your many +1/+1 counters on. Preferably an evasive or sturdy body. The enchantment also increases in value when your dying creatures have somewhat high power. Again, this is pretty evident, but I find it useful to use sentences to slosh the card's potential around in my head.

The most obstructive barrier to using this card is, as mentioned previously, its cost. Six mana ain't cheap, but with a bit of ramp, you can easily drop it on turn five or even four. My first card pairing suggestion follows these lines of mana acceleration: [Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid)! At its worst, [Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid) will oust itself and leave two fresh basic lands in its midst, but in a late-game scenario, Death's Presence can stock up the [Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid) with lots of counters to either remove for more lands or embolden an attack.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fertilid)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid)  
[Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid) led me to consider other Elementals, specifically those with both high power and a low casting cost. [Nova Chaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nova+Chaser) was a good first inclusion. A 10/2 for four is snazzy, and with a Death's Presence out, it can attack without worry. But things get really wacky with [Force of Savagery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Savagery). One of the weirder cards out there (of course it was printed in *Future Sight*), the Force will die instantly upon hitting the battlefield and load up another creature with eight +1/+1 counters!

I used [Flamekin Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger) to find all my Elementals and [Smokebraider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) to accelerate them out. The evoking Elementals were perfect fitsâ€”such as [Spitebellows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitebellows), a scalding bit of removal with 6 power. The singletons [Walker of the Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walker+of+the+Grove) and [Glarewielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glarewielder) help in various ways, too, as the former has 7 power and can evoke for five for a 4/4 Elemental token, and the latter can push some potential blockers aside for your giant counter-laden creature to rumble in for massive damage.

| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Some other Elementals made the deck, such as [Ashling the Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashling+the+Pilgrim), which in deathly circumstances can be a fiery reset button. [Sunflare Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunflare+Shaman) is an early piece of removal that sends itself to the graveyard. [Sparkspitter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkspitter) is a fun singleton that can discard excess Death's Presences or other junk in hand for a [Spark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spark+Elemental) token. But my favorite Elemental I found a home for is [Thornling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornling). Maligned for its jarringly bulbous nose and non-symmetrical art (see: [Morphling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morphling) and [Torchling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torchling)), [Thornling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornling) nonetheless is a powerhouse here. Haste is kind of nice, but trample and especially indestructibility at the flick of a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) make [Thornling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornling) the ideal target for all those +1/+1 counters. And in a pinch, you can activate [Thornling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornling)'s fourth ability four times for a pseudo [Force of Savagery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Savagery), which makes me giggle.

Even though Death's Presence isn't an early-game drop, I wondered what its effect would be on a traditional aggro deck. If I kept casting a bunch of efficiently powered creatures and attacking, a late-game Presence could swing things my way.

I also wanted to create a situation in which I drop Death's Presence and then immediately sacrifice something to trigger it. After some searching, I rediscovered [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth), and the Beasts poured in. I've been having lots of fun with [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) this summer, and the fun continues here. [Garruk's Companion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%27s+Companion) and [Leatherback Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leatherback+Baloth) bring some oomph to the early game. [Valley Rannet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valley+Rannet) can find basic lands, and it's a 6/3 body.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

I [combed Gatherer for obscure Beasts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;subtype=+%5b%22Beast%22%5damp;color=+%40%28%7c%5bR%5d%7c%5bG%5d%29) and found one in [Shivan Wumpus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Wumpus). It's a trampling 6/6 for four, and its "drawback" sets your opponent back a land, which can be vital in the early game. I've been on a [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) kick lately, and for good reasons. The 9/7 Beast suspends for a mere ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif), and it can trigger Death's Presence at will by sacrificing my other creatures. Finally, the long-forgotten [Spearbreaker Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spearbreaker+Behemoth) rears its durable head to protect virtually all my creatures.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The last two additions were some [Starstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starstorm)s (a techy board wipe that cycles) and [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker). Garruk 1.0 can ramp right into a Death's Presence, and his Beast and [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) tendencies are welcome in this list.

Finally, I wanted to tap into the power of the infinite using Death's Presence. Inspired by [Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid) (which I am, daily) I searched for other creatures that could remove +1/+1 counters for a helpful effect. I found my second piece in [Mindless Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindless+Automaton). If [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) is the "Sad Robot," the Automaton sure is the Mad one. Removing two +1/+1 counters can allow me to draw a card.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mindless+Automaton)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindless+Automaton)  
However, what if that draw... was a Bear! Stay with me. If I pay one to activate [Words of Wilding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wilding), I'll get a 2/2 token instead. Mmm, Life, and with that inevitably comes Death. If I speed up the process (or bring it to its preordained end, whichever you prefer) with [Ashnod's Altar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashnod%27s+Altar), I'll make two mana and restore two +1/+1 counters to the Automaton. Infinite mana is the result, which funnels into [Helix Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helix+Pinnacle) for the win.

How to survive and find my combo pieces? [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) and [Drift of Phantasms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drift+of+Phantasms) can certainly dig. [Moment's Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace) can stall incoming attackers, and [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) is a whippy bounce spell. [Dawntreader Elk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk), meanwhile, finally gets the call over [Sakura-Tribe Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder).
