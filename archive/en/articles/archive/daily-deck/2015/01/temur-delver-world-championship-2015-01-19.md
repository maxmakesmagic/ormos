The World Championship Week wasn't only for Standard decks.



I would like to talk about the next Pro Tour's format, Modern, starting with the Temur deck the Japanese (Kentaro Yamamoto, Yuuya Watanabe, Yuuki Ichikawa) piloted.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tarmogoyf)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)

The question being, how to develop the blue-red [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) deck I introduced last week even further.



Their decision was to add green, or more specifically [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), the strongest two-mana creature in the format.



[Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) is two mana but doesn't die to [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). It easily grows into a 4/5 green monster due to the numerous sorceries in the deck.



That's probably enough to explain how good this card is.



[Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) do not work well together. However, even if you have no graveyard, your opponent has one. I guess they decided it won't matter because the graveyard will probably contain three or so cards the next turn anyway.
