Equip and Swing!
================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on February 17, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week, I discussed [an aggressive Esper deck](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/vault-awesome-2010-02-10) featuring one of my favorite cards from *Worldwake*, [Lodestone Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Golem). This week I would like to work on a deck that features a number of incredible cards from *Worldwake*.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab78_firewalkerSplash.jpg)  
[Kor Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker) seems like one of the better creatures in Standard. This card was the subject of a lot of hype when it was first spoiled; somehow, though, the excitement has ebbed over the past few weeks. This card is an incredible metagame tool. It crushes decks like Jund, Cruel Control, Boros Bushwacker, and especially Mono-Red. This card deserved its original hype, and it deserves a home.

| Kor Firewalker | Stoneforge Mystic |

[Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) is another card that seems criminally undervalued at this point in time. Playing a [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) never leaves you behind. A two-mana automatic two-for-one is a welcomed addition to any white deck's repertoire. In Extended, this lovely engineer can fetch up powerhouse cards like [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) or [Sword of Fire and Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice). In Standard, we can find efficient spells like [Trusty Machete](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusty+Machete), [Basilisk Collar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar), and [Behemoth Sledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge).

After examining [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) for a bit of time I noticed that both creatures were Kor. The cogs in my head started spinning, and I recalled cards like [Armament Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armament+Master), [Kor Duelist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Duelist), and [Kitesail Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitesail+Apprentice). A [further examination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?type=+%5Bkor%5D%7C%7Csubtype=+%5Bkor%5D%7C%7Ctext=+%5Bkor%5D&format=%5B%22Standard%22%5D) of Gatherer revealed that [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge) actually puts Kor Soldiers into play. I decided that an aggressive Kor deck deserved to be built. Here's the first list I put together.

I played about fifteen games with the deck and was both impressed and disappointed.

**The Good**

The one drops seemed very powerful. [Kor Duelist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Duelist) was especially strong. (Double strike ends games quickly.) [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) was very powerful; the ability to find Equipment and get a body to equip it to is blatantly absurd. [Kor Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker) seemed like it would be an automatic four-of in any white-based deck; the applications of this card are really unbelievable. [Sejiri Steppe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sejiri+Steppe) was also very strong; I had a number of games where it allowed me to attack an otherwise unstoppable planeswalker or just kill my opponent outright.

**The Bad**

The deck had a lot of trouble with opponents that drew more than one removal spell. I wanted to include cards like [Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) or [Harm's Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%27s+Way) to minimize the effect of spot removal like [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). [Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) seemed like a better choice, because it could also be used as a finisher that made my creatures unblockable. Unfortunately, [Armament Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armament+Master) was a liability. Every time I dumped mana into equipping it and swung with my team, I would be met with a removal spell that made me lose the game on the spot. Removing the [Armament Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armament+Master) causes a chain reaction in the deck's development. [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge) loses a lot of value when I take out the [Armament Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armament+Master), so that will likely be cut as well. [Marshal's Anthem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marshal%27s+Anthem) loses a lot of value when I cut the [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge). The Anthem was always an incredible card, but cutting the [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge) lets me lower the land count, and I can't justify playing [Marshal's Anthem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marshal%27s+Anthem) in a deck with twenty-two lands.

Something about the deck still felt lackluster to me. I went back to Gatherer and began my search again. The creature type of my creatures, was no longer relevant so a whole new slew of efficient creatures became available. The one card that I wanted more than anything else was a copy of [Behemoth Sledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge). I imagined the insanity that might ensue if I was ever given the opportunity to equip it onto a [Kor Duelist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Duelist). Splashing green seemed easy enough to me. I could play four [Sunpetal Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunpetal+Grove) (a card that has gotten much easier to trade for since the release of [Stirring Wildwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stirring+Wildwood)). I wouldn't have to play more than four lands that made green mana, because [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)'s ability will allow me to put the [Behemoth Sledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge) into play without using green mana.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab78_2Cards1.jpg)Having creatures with protection from red or from black seemed like a very strong metagame call. Creatures with protection from key colors are prime targets for our Equipment. Vampires will be hard-pressed to beat a [White Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=White+Knight) carrying a [Behemoth Sledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge), and Jund and Grixis will have fits dealing with a variety of creatures that dodge key removal spells. I decided to include a playset of [White Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=White+Knight) and at least a few copies of [Devout Lightcaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Lightcaster).

The last addition I made to the deck was a few copies of [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring). The four [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) were incredible, but I wanted a little more removal. [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) deals with problem artifacts or enchantments as well as creatures, and it really shines against [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) / [Font of Mythos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) decks.

The deck works beautifully. I played a few matches with the deck that I would like to share with you.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab78_match1Header.jpg)  
Jund is, without any doubt, the deck to beat. When constructing new decks, I find myself aiming to beat Jund more than anything else. My original list had trouble with draws that included an early [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) followed by a [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse). The new list should do better, with the inclusion of protection from black creatures and [Behemoth Sledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge).

I drew [Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements), played a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), equipped [Devout Lightcaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Lightcaster) with [Behemoth Sledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Behemoth+Sledge), and attacked for 4, putting my opponent at 15 and me at 20. It may seem odd that I tested the deck against two decks with an inherit weakness to protection from red and black, but most decks in the format fall into this category. Vampires and Jund are probably the most played decks right now. Standard Haterator has a very strong match-up here with its powerful hate cards. The equipment theme is strong and very fun. I'm happy to say that this deck is incredibly budget-friendly, very powerful, and exceptionally original. If your local [Friday Night **Magic**](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/fnm) is overrun with decks like Grixis, Jund, and Vampires—as many are right now—then this could be a very strong metagame choice.
