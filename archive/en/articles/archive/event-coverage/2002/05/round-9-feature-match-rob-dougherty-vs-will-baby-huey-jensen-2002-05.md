Round 9 Feature Match - Rob Dougherty vs. Will "Baby Huey" Jensen
=================================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 12, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_633.png)
By Zev Gurwitz












![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/936.jpg)For the first round of day two, two stellar competitors sat down, and one of them had 244 cards in his deck. It is true, Baby Huey is playing [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), and he is doing well, as he and Your Move Games owner Rob Dougherty both have six wins, a loss, and a draw. Huey apparently knew what Rob was playing, which turned out to be Squirrel/Opposition, proclaiming "my worst match up" as he readied for the fight.


Mr. Jensen chose to play first, a privilege granted when Rob pulled a black card out of Huey's playing card deck, which Huey took out to amuse himself while Rob actually pile shuffled Jensen's gigantic deck. With a first turn [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) from Rob and a responding [Addle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Addle) from Huey, the game had begun.


The [Addle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Addle) took a [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd), leaving Rob with a hand of [Beast Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Attack) and [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Rob pulled a [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) off the top, tapping out to play it on a forest. Huey started trying to control the game with consecutive creature kill turns, first Urza's Raging the Elf, and then giving a Call token a licking with a [Flametongue Kavu](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu). Rob made a new Squirrel at the end of Huey's turn and did absolutely nothing, failing to play a spell on his turn.


This gave Jensen the time he needed to cast a [Wild Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Research), probably the most powerful card in the [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck. Rob responded with a [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), but is tapped out and the red enchantment resolved. While splitting the [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), Huey consulted the list he made form the [Addle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Addle) several turns earlier, to make sure he knew exactly what was in Dougherty's hand. The FOF netted Dougherty two much-needed land, and sent a [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) and two [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd)s to the graveyard for latter use.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/938.jpg)Dougherty's turn was very strong, playing an [Opposition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition) to help control Huey's ability to play his many colored spells, and creating the potential of a hard lock with the [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) in play. On Huey's next upkeep, Rob tapped a [Cave of Coilos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cave+of+Coilos), Huey's only white source at the time, to prevent the Research from getting [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). Jensen floated colorless and went for his draw step, and after drawing, activated [Wild Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Research) and sent for [Recoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil). After a quick search for the blue/black instant, which was fortunately in the first pile Jensen chose to look through, the random discard ability of [Wild Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Research) was resolved. The card sent to the graveyard? RECOIL.


If Jensen was flustered at his poor luck, he showed none of it, calmly searching again for another instant, [Fire/Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire%2FIce), which stayed in the hand this time, sending [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) to the graveyard in its place, and killing off Dougherty's squirrels. Huey, taking the offense, swings with one of the four creatures in his deck, the FTK that took out an elf a couple of turns earlier.


Dougherty continued trying to overrun his opponent with tokens, playing a [Deserted Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deserted+Temple) to go with his [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest), and casting one of his [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd)s out the graveyard. Huey saw the Nest as a valid threat, and searched for [Recoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil), discarding [Absorb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb). The [Recoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil) took out the nest and Jensen got in another swing with the FTK.


Dougherty continued, casting main phase [Beast Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Attack), but Jensen's Research netted him a [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) to bounce the beast, which tapped the Kavu, letting Dougherty get a swing in with his elephant. Rob made another Beast token, and passed the turn. Huey searched again for [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse), but hits that very same [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) with the random discard. Undeterred, Jensen researched again, and gets [Fire/Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire%2FIce), pitching [Absorb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb), and killed off Rob's last Squirrel, then trades the FTK with the beast token in combat, leaving Dougherty with a lone Call token.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/937.jpg)Rob roared back with double flash back [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd)s, and swung to bring Huey down to 11. Huey continued trying to cantrip away Dougherty's tokens, searching for yet another [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)! For a guy with 244 cards in his deck he only seems to use two! But the card is not called [Wild Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Research) for nothing and the [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) ends up in the bin. Jensen had some mana from a land tapped by [Opposition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition), so he searched again, leaving two mana up to get a [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse), which managed to stay in the hand. The third land however, the one Jensen needed to actually cast the [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse), went to the graveyard and Jensen had nothing to do but say "GO."


Rob untapped his elephant army and went to the mattresses, swinging for nine and putting Jensen at two. Jensen had an end-of-turn effect, searching for [Tainted Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact). The Pact sticks and on his main phase, Jensen cast the [Tainted Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact), but cards #5 and #7 were both [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), and game one went to Rob Dougherty.


Rob sided in [Disrupt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)s and [Gainsay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s, while Huey has [Hibernation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hibernation) and [Slay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slay) available for game two, along with [Shadowmage Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator). Huey kept and went first, casting [Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) at his opponent, forcing Rob to say, "I feel violated." Rob's hand this game was stellar, ramping from a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), to two consecutive [Spellbane Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbane+Centaur)s and finally to a turn five [Oppositition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oppositition). Meanwhile, Huey did... nothing. He failed to draw another land. Jensen swayed his large head back and forth in disappointment and simply said, "I didn't want to mulligan, it would take too long."


Rob Dougherty wins, 2-0

#### GP Milwaukee Top 64: Battle of Wits
