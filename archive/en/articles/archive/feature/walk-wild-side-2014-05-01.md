A Walk on the Wild Side
=======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 1, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 







This deck is wild! Sometimes, it is best to build a deck that simply lowers its head and runs straight for its goal, largely oblivious to what potential obstacles might be in the way. This mono-green deck, played by **Magic Online** user Havik917, is a great example of a deck that just wants to attack with the largest creatures it can. 


![Wild Beastmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wild+Beastmaster)
 This deck is built to take advantage of the interaction between [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) and [Aspect of Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aspect+of+Hydra). There are lots of creatures that provide both bodies for [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) to pump and green devotion symbols for [Aspect of Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aspect+of+Hydra) to do its best [Might of Oaks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Oaks) impersonation. One cool trick with this deck involves attacking with the [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster), putting its trigger on the stack, then bloodrushing [Slaughterhorn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn) to give all of your attackers +4/+4 (or more, with additional pump spells!) 


 Havik917 apparently doesn't like losing to Mono-Blue Devotion and has dedicated seven sideboard slots to protection from blue creatures. If you don't expect many [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s, maybe this deck is a great choice for you!
