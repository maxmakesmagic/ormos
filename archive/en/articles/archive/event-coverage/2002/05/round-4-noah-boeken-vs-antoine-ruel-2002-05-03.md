Round 4: Noah Boeken vs. Antoine Ruel
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 3, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Craig-Jones-Author-Photo-150x150_0.jpg)
By Craig Jones











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a921.jpg)This round saw a clash of two top European players currently in leaner times. Antoine Ruel is a Frenchman with many solid Top 8 Grand Prix performances but since making the Semi's at World's last year as had a very quiet year by his standards. Noah Boeken is also a highly successful Dutch player, but only managed to break his drought with a recent win at Grand Prix Barcelona.

Noah Boeken has a solid blue-black deck while Antoine Ruel has a very poor looking green-black deck that he's somehow managed to pilot to a 2-0-1 record. 

### Game 1

Boeken went first and got to [Afflict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Afflict) Ruel's first play, an [Organ Grinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Organ+Grinder), and then follow it with a [Mindslicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer). Ruel's [Nantuko Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Elder) didn't look particularly menacing, but it did allow him to accelerate out a Stone Tongue Basilisk.

Boeken looked at it dismissively, waving the [Waste Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waste+Away) in his hand.

The Basilisk was duly wasted but the [Narcissism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcissism) Ruel followed it with finally discouraged the [Mindslicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) from attacking.

Instead Ruel could start attacking back with a bunch of dodgy mana fixers – the Elder and a [Krosan Restorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Restorer) – while an [Organ Grinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Organ+Grinder) held off the [Mindslicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer).

A [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) gave Boeken an attacker with evasion and a Looter that dug him to another unblockable creature in the form of a [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a919.jpg)Ruel had a lot of damage in his hand in the form of [Muscle Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muscle+Burst), [Strength of Lunacy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+of+Lunacy) and the [Narcissism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcissism). But now Boeken had the Shade he was happy to block the Grinder with the [Mindslicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) and then negate any tricks with the [Waste Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waste+Away) he'd returned with a [Scrivener](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrivener) earlier.

Flashing back one of the two [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) he'd already cast gave Boeken the 4th swamp he needed to finish Ruel off with the Shade and [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven).

Boeken 1-0 Ruel

Ruel brought in some mountains so he could splash for a [Savage Firecat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Firecat) and [Rites of Initiation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Initiation).

Ruel had to mulligan and went further behind on card advantage as he needed to use a [Waste Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waste+Away) to kill Boeken's Looter, thankfully having the [Nantuko Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Elder) to provide the acceleration.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a920.jpg)Boeken needed the Looter though as he missed a few land drops and didn't have a swamp. After his [Rotting Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Giant) was Liquefied Ruel couldn't really take advantage with his multiple land, [Muscle Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muscle+Burst) hand.

Boeken discarded a [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and flashed it back to quickly recover. Soon Antoine was being battered by a [Treetop Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Sentinel), [Cephalid Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Sage), [Frightcrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler) and [Hydromorph Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydromorph+Guardian). The Frenchman's Elder and [Krosan Restorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Restorer) weren't really up to the task.

"Play the Firecat," Boeken exhorted, as the matchup seemed completely one sided. He even showed the [Faceless Butcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) waiting in his hand should Ruel actually draw a decent spell.

Even Ruel's last ditch attempt at defiance was squashed as Boeken countered his [Rites of Initiation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Initiation).

The Frenchman just hadn't drawn anything this game.

**Final Result:** Noah Boeken beat Antoine Ruel 2-0
