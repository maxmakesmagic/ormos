2008 Worlds Round 1: Up in Smoke
================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on December 11, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











Last year’s World Champ, Israel’s Uri Peleg, hasn’t been keeping a high profile this past season. His only Pro Tour this year was Berlin, and he confesses that he’s been giving **Magic** a lower priority in his life. He’s shown up this weekend with an exciting Standard take on Extended Elves. His opponent is Grand Prix–Kitakyuushu Champion and 2005 Japan Nationals Semifinalist Junya Iyanaga. A PT regular, Iyanaga has put his trust in Standard’s dominant deck: Faeries.

**Game 1**

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds08/r1_iyanaga.jpg)*Junya Iyanaga shows off his sleeves.*Peleg opened with Brushland and Llanowar Elves. Iyanaga showed [Peppersmoke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peppersmoke) off [Secluded Glen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Secluded+Glen) and wasted no time dispatching Peleg’s Elf. It was a sucker-punch for Peleg’s one-land hand. With no help waiting on top of his deck, he played Commune with Nature and found himself an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary).

Iyanaga continued his by-the-numbers Faerie game with a turn-two [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). Peleg’s tardy second land showed up, enabling the Visionary, but he would need some cooperation from Iyanaga to get off the back foot. Iyanaga played his third land and waited for Peleg’s next move.

Peleg floated two green of [Wooded Bastion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Bastion) and offered [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid). Iyanaga went into the tank and decided to spend his [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite). Peleg put down a second Llanowar Elves and passed it back. Iyanaga had no fourth land, but Peleg’s progress was so hobbled that Iyanaga could safely play the aggressor. [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) buffed his airforce and he sent them in for four. Peleg still couldn’t find land and Communed himself more Llanowar Elves. Iyanaga untapped and supersized his team with a second Scion, sending Peleg to his sideboard.

Iyanaga 1, Peleg 0

Peleg sideboarded in a package of Gutteral Responses, [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher)s, and [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus). Iyanaga was no less prepared with Flashfreeze, Deathmark, and [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest).

**Game 2**

The second game saw Peleg’s deck find its legs. He led with [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) and [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), then kicked off the third turn with [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). He saw a hand of Flashfreeze, [Peppersmoke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peppersmoke), two [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique)s, [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and Underground River. He snagged the Flashfreeze but had no immediate followup.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds08/r1_peleg.jpg)*Uri Peleg doesn’t like what he sees.* Iyanaga peeled [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), turning his Cliques from blanks to live ammo. Peleg did his best to smile and answered with a [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) that really wished it could’ve arrived a turn earlier. Iyanaga surveyed his options. He was at 13 with four mana available, two of it blue. He passed it back to Peleg and dropped [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) on Peleg’s upkeep, removing his [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). Peleg floated mana and doubled his Colossus, crashing in for eight and leaving Iyanaga at 5.

Iyanaga’s deck coughed up another Underground Sea, putting [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) back into the equation. He swung in with a token and [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), putting Peleg to 8, then dropped another Clique on Peleg. Peleg thought hard but none of his options were appealing. He half-heartedly evoked [Cloudthresher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher) (forgetting to untap his Sentinel) and watched Iyanaga tuck [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) underneath it. [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) traded with Colossus and left Iyanaga with lethal damage at the ready. Even if the Sentinel had been untapped, the [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) in Iyanaga’s hand would negate it.

Iyanaga 2, Peleg 0

After the match, Peleg admitted that the one deck he didn’t want to face this weekend was Faeries, and especially not one with [Peppersmoke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peppersmoke). He des-ideboarded and shuffled up his deck to show me how it goldfishes.

It was really something. It can go: third turn, [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) your land and tutor up [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos). Fourth turn, Ranger for [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) and [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid). Fifth turn, go nuts. With the tutoring power of Commune with Nature, [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) and [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), it’s easy for the deck to draw the works.
