Day 2 Coverage



Thiago Saporito of Brazil has consistently performed over the years, both on Magic Online and—since Pro Tour Khans of Tarkir—offline as well, but what has eluded him up until now has been a title. Grand Prix Las Vegas changed that, as Saporito entered Day Two of the Limited main event at 8-0-1 and never stopped winning throughout Saturday.


After being the first player to clinch a Top 8 spot, Saporito drafted a powerful red-black deck that propelled him through the competition with precision, besting Bolun Zhang and Paul Herr before overcoming Pro Tour Shadows over Innistrad champion Steve Rubin in the finals.  
  

Congratulations to Thiago Saporito on his long-deserved victory, and for being the second of three players to walk home with a trophy at Grand Prix Las Vegas this weekend!

Day 1 Coverage



The second day of Grand Prix Vegas is in the books. While Day 2 of Legacy came to a close, 2,562 players arrived in the convention center ready to begin the next chapter of the tournament - Limited. They cracked boosters, sorted cards, and deliberated over builds, until they settled on their final forty cards.


At the end of nine rounds, only nine players' perfect records remained intact.Michael Simon,Andrew Cuneo, Zach Dunn, Paul Herr, Steven Deng, Chris Botelho, Charles Wong, Vincent Pascoe, Thiago Rosenmann lead tomorrow's field of players returning for two drafts and six rounds of play as they fight their way to the Top 8.


Join us here, and at [twitch.tv/magic](http://twitch.tv/magic), to see the continuing action at Grand Prix Vegas!
