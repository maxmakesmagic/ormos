Draft 2: Gary Wise and Mike Turian
==================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 27, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New York–based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 






Gary Wise and Mike Turian—teammates on the Pro Tour winning Potato Nation—both need to go 3-0 in order to make the Top 8. Only one player form their pod can advance and the former teammates found themselves in seat one and eight as the draft got underway.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a895.jpg)* Gary Wise
* Dan Rodemann
* Gerard Fabiano
* Matt Severa
* Pat Sullivan
* John Sonmez
* Chris Manning
* Mike Turian

Gary went immediately into white, choosing [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) over [Snarling Undorak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snarling+Undorak), [Swat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat), and [Chain of Plasma](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+of+Plasma). Mike took a [Mistform Dreamer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Dreamer) and [Crown of Suspicion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion)s on the wheel. In the second pack—which opened on a [Silklash Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silklash+Spider)—Turian took a [Haunted Cadaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Cadaver) and a [Misery Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misery+Charm) on the bounce while Gary took a [Wave of Indifference](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wave+of+Indifference) and a cycling card on the wheel.

The third pack opened with a first pick [Nantuko Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) and then [Contested Cliffs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+Cliffs). Turian seemed content to be the only blue mage at the table—is that the lot of the eighth seat? —and took a [Screaming Seahawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screaming+Seahawk). Gary took an [Akroma's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Blessing) and got a [Daru Cavalier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Cavalier) on the way back—this would become a habit for Gary.

Matt Severa's started to emerge as the early front-runner in the pod when he opened [Centaur Glade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Glade) for his G/R deck. Turian remained the only blue mage and took a [Fleeting Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleeting+Aven) while Gary nabbed a late [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) and a [Goblin Piledriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piledriver) on the flip side. Gary's nearest competitor for white cards? Pat Sullivan—four seats away!

Sullivan's B/W was coming together nicely and he opened a [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) that required little thought. Turian took a [Fallen Cleric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Cleric) and Wise was able to pick up a fairly late [Gustcloak Harrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Harrier) with a [Gustcloak Runner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Runner) on the flip side.

Sonmez opened a [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) that Gary looked at longingly but it was not to be as Sonmez nabbed it giving Manning [Voice of the Woods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods) and Turian a [Severed Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Severed+Legion). Gary consoled himself with a [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber). The seventh pack plopped a second pick [Nantuko Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) into Turian's lap and Gary picked up [Crude Rampart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crude+Rampart)s and [Renewed Faith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renewed+Faith). Turian first picked a [Voidmage Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy) to close out the first eight packs and Gary nabbed a [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) over Avarix, which found its way into Severa's beastly pile of cards.

The next set of packs reversed directions and Turian scooped up a [Swat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat). Manning took a Jareth with the second pick over a [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage). With two Undoraks and a [Voice of the Woods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods) already under his belt the Savage seemed like a sure bet for the YMGer. [Foothill Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foothill+Guide) and a random [Complicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Complicate) comprised Gary's wheel. The Savage ended up going seventh. Severa chose a Mauler over it for his deck.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a894.jpg)The ninth pack yielded a [Unified Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unified+Strike) for Gary and a [Mistform Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Stalker) and [Fallen Cleric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Cleric) for Turian on the wheel. Gary took a [Pearlspear Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pearlspear+Courier) in the next pack while Turian took [Read the Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Runes) with a bounced [Mistform Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Wall). Gary got another [Daru Cavalier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Cavalier) on the way back. Sullivan took a [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) out of his pack but there was still an [Improvised Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Improvised+Armor) for Gary. Turian took a [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin) and got a [Frightshroud Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightshroud+Courier) on the bounce. Gary happily took a [Daru Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Healer) the long way back.

Matt Severa's deck took a turn for the frightening with a first pick [Gratuitous Violence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gratuitous+Violence). Wise and Turian took a [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and a [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin) respectively. Wise found nothing better than a [Foothill Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foothill+Guide) in the next pack and Turian took a [Thrashing Mudspawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrashing+Mudspawn). Wise woke up X-mas morning and found a second pick [Slice and Dice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slice+and+Dice) under his tree. Turian nabbed a [Tribal Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Golem) to compliment his Mistform men. Wise closed out the second set of packs with a nigh unkillable [Tephraderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tephraderm) while Turian took a [Dirge of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dirge+of+Dread).

Christmas turned into an eight day Chanukah celebration for Wise when he opened up a Jareth to start the final round of packs. Turian wheeled a second Seahawk and a [Wretched Anurid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid). Somewhere in the last eight packs Dan Rodemann emerged as the other blue drafter a the table and picked up a pick one [Callous Oppressor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Callous+Oppressor) that would surely have made it to Turian otherwise. Turian took another [Dirge of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dirge+of+Dread) and a [Mistform Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Wall) while Gary wheeled his third [Daru Cavalier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Cavalier) and a [Secluded Steppe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Secluded+Steppe).

Fabiano first picked a [Lavamancer's Skill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavamancer%27s+Skill)s out of a very good pack. Severa finally got the [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage) his deck was pining for, Sully took a [Mobilization](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mobilization) and Sonmez took a Gourna. Turian was excited to grab a [Riptide Biologist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Biologist) and a [Barren Moor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barren+Moor) while Gary grinned at Sully as he picked up a [Dawning Purist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawning+Purist).

Severa picked up gratuitous [Gratuitous Violence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gratuitous+Violence)—his second! Turian snatched a [Screeching Buzzard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard) to go with his [Screaming Seahawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screaming+Seahawk)s and Wise picked up the Avarix he missed earlier. Nothing else exciting happened until the final three packs when Wise picked up a twelfth pick Cavalier—his fourth!—and [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith)s in the last two packs! His deck went form good to formidable in the final two packs.

I sat down with Turian as he spread out his cards.

Turian: "The only guy that could possibly open a [Callous Oppressor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Callous+Oppressor) and have it not end up my deck. I was so mad, I wanted that guy!"

He seemed pretty happy with his [Read the Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Runes) as he examined the possibilities of using the card with his multiple [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin)s. "This card is a bomb", he nodded seeing its potential more clearly with his cards out in front of him. Apparently he regretted one pick, which might come as a surprise to most readers. He tried to bounce an [Imagecrafter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) over a [Severed Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Severed+Legion) but the 1/1 wizard did not come back to him. Had he to do it all over again, Turian claims he would pick the wizard.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a893.jpg)I asked him bout his chances to 3-0 the table and he seemed confident that he had the best deck. He conceded that Wise's deck was also quite good, "We cooperated really well together."

When I asked Wise how he felt about his deck he could only laugh maniacally and show off his pack 23 and pack 24 [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith)s. "I think I got the most powerful deck at the table!" With the exception of his own he felt that Severa's deck had the best chance to run the table. "I really wanted a [Demystify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demystify) but it just wasn't there." He is running the [Dawning Purist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawning+Purist) in case he has to face the [Gratuitous Violence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gratuitous+Violence).dec. As I walked away he was still cackling, "I have a [Slice and Dice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slice+and+Dice) with FOUR [Daru Cavalier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Cavalier)s!"

I also had the chance to ask Manning about his choice of Jareth over [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage). He could only shake his head and show me an Elvish Pathfinder he had to play in his deck alongside TWO [Voice of the Woods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods)! "I think I overestimated Jareth and underestimated the Savage. I'm not even playing with him."

In the end I think that both Wise and Turian have a good shot at the Top 8 but if you are looking for a dark horse candidate keep an eye out for Matt Severa.
