Round 10 Feature Match #1 – Martin Zimmermann vs. Helmut Summersberger
----------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

"It just doesn't work without blue mana," Austrian Helmut Summersberger said after his 1-2 loss against Germany's Martin Zimmermann. "Or green," he added, shaking his head in resignation. "I have eight sources of each of my three colors and no double casting cost either. During the draft, I was even first-picking Guildgates!"


The two players, who both have multiple Grand Prix Top 8s to their names, had had an interesting first game, followed by what Zimmermann called "two non-games."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/fm10-1-summersberger.jpg)  
*Helmut Summersberger* 
In the first, Summersberger's green-white-blue deck was working properly and his [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) easily stopped Zimmermann's early offense, his [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) stopped [Thrashing Mossdog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrashing+Mossdog), and his [Simic Fluxmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Fluxmage) finally took full control. Zimmermann had tried to [Drown in Filth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Filth) the [Flux](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flux)mage but had missed on a second land, and he also lost a 3/3 Centaur token to [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder). Summersberger seemed to have the perfect answer at every turn and soon took the lead.


Zimmermann started quickly into the second game, however, with [Shadow Alley Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Alley+Denizen), [Drudge Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Beetle), and [Undercity Informer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Informer), followed by [Far & Away](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%2F%2F+Away) to kill Summersberger's [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer). Summersberger was missing blue mana, and only cast one more spell during the whole game, a [Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn). Neither the [Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn) nor the game lasted for very long.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/fm10-1-zimmermann.jpg)  
*Martin Zimmermann* 
Then, for the final game of the match, Summersberger's mana troubles increased even further. Even after sacrificing his [Azorius Cluestone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Cluestone), which had been his only spell so far, he still had no source of green mana. When he finally topdecked a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), Zimmermann already had four creatures on the battlefield, and added [Necropolis Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropolis+Regent). Summersberger extended his hand in concession.

Round 10 Feature Match #2 – Max Schultze vs. Andre Müller
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

In this all-German feature match, one duel was decided by color screw and another by mulligans. In the third game, Andre Müller's five-color deck malfunctioned, with a double mulligan leading to a chumpblocker by turn five as his only spell, while Max Schultze's green-white-red deck couldn't come up with a source of red mana in the second game, leaving [Ruination Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruination+Wurm) and [Foundry Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Champion) stranded in his hand and leaving Schultze helpless as Müller's [Ripscale Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ripscale+Predator) and [Tenement Crasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tenement+Crasher) finished the job in short order.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/fm10-2-mueller.jpg)  
*Andre Müller* 
So let's focus on the first game instead. There were two key situations here. Rather early in this long drawn-out affair, Schultze was ahead on the board and Müller tried to block [Ruination Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruination+Wurm) with [Wrecking Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrecking+Ogre), walking right into Schultze's [Swift Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swift+Justice). The [Ruination Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruination+Wurm) traded with [Ripscale Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ripscale+Predator) next turn, but the swift 8-point lifegain would prove crucial later on, as it prevented Müller from entering a damage race.


Forced to take the defensive role, Müller had to hold back with his [Dark Revenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Revenant), despite having the only flier on the battlefield, to prevent Schultze from attacking with [Boros Mastiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Mastiff). Lots of other creatures traded, but then Schultze had [Foundry Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Champion), "targeting your draw step," i.e. targeting [Dark Revenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Revenant).


Next, [Holy Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Holy+Mantle) on [Foundry Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Champion) forced Müller to cast [Cyclonic Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift) (without overload) on the Champion. "Yes, really happy about that." The Champion was summoned again and killed [Dark Revenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Revenant) again, costing Müller yet another draw step.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/fm10-2-schultze.jpg)  
*Max Schultze* 
By now, a great many lands had accumulated on both sides of the table, making it impossible for Müller to kill [Foundry Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Champion) in combat, even without [Holy Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Holy+Mantle). He produced an astonishing number of chumpblockers, but no removal, and finally succumbed to the champion.

Round 11 Feature Match – Samuele Estratti vs. Jonathan Bergström
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

"[Gruul War Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+War+Chant) was the best card in this match, as it always is," Jonathan Bergström summarized. "I only have one [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) as an answer, but I never drew it."


Showcasing the power of [Gruul War Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+War+Chant), Samuele Estratti (the Pro Tour Philadelphia champion) defeated Jonathan Bergström (a member of the Grand Prix Utrecht winning team) in three games. Although Bergström won a game in which Estratti flooded out on lands, the other two games were decided by the devastating enchantment from the Gruul clan. As a result, Estratti moves to 9-2, in a good position to aim for another Grand Prix Top 8. Bergström falls to 8-3.


The first game that Estratti won was a very quick affair. At first, Bergström was able to block [Towering Thunderfist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Thunderfist) with [Murmuring Phantasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murmuring+Phantasm), but when [Gruul War Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+War+Chant) came down, the equation changed and Bergström couldn't come up with good blocks anymore.


The second game that Estratti won was much longer, and more complicated. Especially after [Gruul War Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+War+Chant) came down, players were thinking long and hard. Two attack phases stood out as particularly interesting ones. The first one is pictured below.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/Attack-step.jpg)  
*A complicated attack step. Not pictured: Estratti's freshly cast and untapped [Crowned Ceratok](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crowned+Ceratok).* 
As you see, Estratti (at 6 life) attacked with a 3/3 Centaur token, a 6/5 [Ripscale Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ripscale+Predator), a 4/6 [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura), and a 5/1 [Armored Transport](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Transport), all getting a +1/+0 boost from [Gruul War Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+War+Chant). He also had a [Crowned Ceratok](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crowned+Ceratok) with summoning sickness on the battlefield.


Bergström (at 9 life) decided to block the [Ripscale Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ripscale+Predator) with his 7/7 [Lavinia of the Tenth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavinia+of+the+Tenth) (courtesy of a [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) and a +1/+1 counter) and his 1/2 [Simic Fluxmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Fluxmage). He also blocked the [Armored Transport](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Transport) with the 4/4 [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake) and the 2/2 Knight token.


Accordingly, [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura) and the Centaur token were left unblocked. [Downsize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Downsize) from Bergstöm ensured that he would survive for another turn, though he lost three of his creatures in the process. Crucially, he lost his [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake). "I shouldn't have blocked with my 4/4 flyer; I should have blocked with [Wind Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) instead," Bergström said after the match. "I felt it was the safest play to block with my biggest guy because [Lavinia of the Tenth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavinia+of+the+Tenth) would be lethal on the next turn anyway. But then it lost flying because I lost my [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake)." Indeed, Estratti was able to chumpblock Lavina of the Tenth with [Crowned Ceratok](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crowned+Ceratok) on the subsequent attack.


But Bergstöm shrugged off his mistake and fought back with the Azorius part of [Beck & Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck+%2F%2F+Call). As a result, he managed to stay into the match, but he still had no permanent answer to [Gruul War Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+War+Chant). A few turns later, we had the second interesting attack phase of the game.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/Estratti-and-Bergstromm.jpg)  
*Estratti (on the right) decides to go for the safe play in fear of removal from Bergström (on the left).* 
In this attack phase, Estratti (at 6 life) had to decide between going all-out or going for the safe route. He had three lethal creatures on the board, while Bergström (at 1 life) only had four blockers. So if Bergström had no removal spell in hand, he would be dead. But if he had, and Estratti would attack all-out, then [Lavinia of the Tenth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavinia+of+the+Tenth) would win the game on the backswing. "If you had the [Profit & Loss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profit+%2F%2F+Loss) that I saw in an earlier game, I would be dead," Estratti explained to his opponent afterwards. Eventually, Estratti decided to attack with his [Armored Transport](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Transport) only. He did the same on the next turn, slowly mauling through Bergstöm's creatures one-by-one.


In the end, Bergstöm didn't have the removal, and Estratti took the match several turns later.

Sunday, 12:35 p.m. – Meet the Swedish World Magic Cup Team
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

What tournament **Magic** player doesn't dream of representing his or her country at the World **Magic** Cup (WMC)? For each country, the WMC team consists of the winner of the three WMC Qualifiers and the top player based on Professional Points in the 2012-2013 Season.


For Sweden, this year's WMC team consists of Joel Larsson, Elias Watsfeldt, Olle Rade, and Joakim Aberg. Hastags #ggrestoftheworld and #theworldtrembles have already been used to describe this team on Twitter.


Joel Larsson is a Gold level pro who made the finals of Pro Tour *Gatecrash*. Elias Watsfeldt is a Silver level pro with multiple Grand Prix Top 8s to his name. Olle Rade has five Pro Tour Top 8s, a Hall of Fame ring, and a Pro Tour trophy going back to the earliest days of the Pro Tour. Finally, Joakim Aberg, a 33-year old teacher, is the least accomplished player from the team, but he is a strong player nevertheless: he holds a Nationals Top 8 and is a self-described Endboss at the local FNM. "But I have never been to Pro Tour, so this is great", Joakim said.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/Swedish-Team.jpg)  
*From left to right: Olle Rade, Elias Watsfeldt, Joel Larsson, and Joakim Aberg.* 
I sat down with the team and asked them how they qualified, how they liked their chances, and how they are planning to prepare.


**Q: For the WMCQ winners: What Standard deck did you use to qualify?**


**Joakim:** "I won with Jund."


**Olle:** "I played with Gruul aggro. Elias played the same deck."


**Elias:** "Same deck, same sleeves, same playmat. Except for one small change: I swapped out [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) for [Volcanic Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength)."


**Q: How do you like your chances as a team?**


**Elias:** "It's 50-50."


**Joel, laughing:** "He always says that."


**Olle:** "People say we have the best team."


**Joel:** "I like the team part. I think we're pretty good at teams," referring to him and Elias making the finals of the Team Grand Prix in Utrecht earlier this year.


**Joakim:** "Yeah, it should be fun!"


**Olle:** "I haven't played in Team worlds since the 90s, so it's kind of a new and old experience."


**Q: Do you think your team is well-balanced; does everyone bring in different qualities?**


**Olle:** "Well, I have all the cards in Standard; that's what I bring in."


**Elias:** "Everyone likes different decks. Joel like the white decks, Olle likes the aggro decks, I likes the blue decks, and Joakim likes Jund decks."


**Q: Have you already talked about how you will prepare for the World **Magic** Cup?** 


**Joel:** "We're just starting because the last qualifier was just last weekend."


**Joakim:** "I made a Facebook account; that's a start."


**Elias:** "I guess we'll just prepare online. Basically, we're living in 4 different cities, so we'll do online testing and speak on Facebook."


**Olle:** "We also need to practice opening boosters, to make sure we're prepared for the Sealed portion."


**Q: What countries stand out as the teams to beat?**


**Olle:** "Denmark, always Denmark! Whoever is on the Danish team, we always want to beat Denmark."


**Elias:** "The Scandinavian teams are all strong in general."


**Joel:** "We must watch out for Tzu-Ching Kuo when he's playing three matches at once."


**Q: Last year at the WMC, players started out with individual performances and then teams cut down to three-person teams after several rounds. This year, players will elect which three of the four players play in each format. Have you already started thinking about who gets to play what?**


**Elias:** "I think a die roll will decide that question."


**Joel:** "It should depend on how much experience everyone has with the different formats. But we haven't thought everything through yet."


The Swedish WMC team still has almost 2 months to get ready for the World **Magic** Cup, which will be held on August 2-4 in Amsterdam. For all other people watching at home or competing in the event, you can use that time to prepare for the Swedish menace. Get ready!

Sunday, 4:04 p.m. – Naming the 4-Color Decks
--------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

The number of different color combinations that is seeing play today is mind-blowing. It didn't use to be that way: In triple *Return to Ravnica* and triple *Gatecrash* draft, we only had five Guilds to choose from, and the fast nature of those formats often pushed players towards streamlined 2-color beatdown decks. However, now that *Dragon's Maze* has entered to format, Limited decks are looking differently. There's just so much depth, and many players have drafted a 4-color deck today.


While the multicolored nature of this block is a lot of fun, it also raises a problem: How to name the 4-color decks? We have apt names available for the 2-color decks and some of the 3-color decks: Golgari, Azorius, Jund, etcetera. But how about the 4-color decks?


One way to come up with names is via a cycle of cards from *Guildpact*: the Nephilims. There are five Nephilim, one for each combination of four colors:


* Dune-Brood Nephilim
* Glint-Eye Nephilim
* Ink-Treader Nephilim
* Witch-Maw Nephilim
* Yore-Tiller Nephilim

So, if you drafted all colors except green, then you drafted Yore-Tiller. If you drafted all colors except blue, then that would be a Glint-Eye deck. Not quite as catchy as Azorius or Jund, but it's certainly shorter than saying Green-Black-White-Red.


To be fair, I doubt the Nephilim naming convention will catch on because many people will have no clue what you're talking about when you say that you are in, e.g., the Ink-Treader colors. Still, it's an idea that I wanted to share while the Swiss rounds are drawing to a close.

Round 14 Feature Match – Fredrik Persson vs. Joel Larsson

*by Tobi Henke*

Here, the early game was all about Larsson's [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets) and Persson's [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark). ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/fm14_larsson.jpg)  
*Joel Larsson*

Larsson opened on [New Prahv Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=New+Prahv+Guildmage) and [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet) and got in several hits, while Persson had his first play on turn four in [Fathom Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Mage). His next play, [Armory Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armory+Guard), met [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), and Larsson was pulling further and further ahead.


[Maze Glider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+Glider) and [Lavinia of the Tenth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavinia+of+the+Tenth) for Persson stopped Larsson's offense; however, by then Persson was already down to 6 life. It took a while, but Larsson's [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet) finished the job via extort.


### Game 3


This time Larsson had no early offense, and neither his [Ascended Lawmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascended+Lawmage) nor [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet) provided good blockers for Persson's creatures. At one point, Larsson blocked [Armory Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armory+Guard) with [Ascended Lawmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascended+Lawmage), tried to [Smite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite), and was stopped via [Scatter Arc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scatter+Arc)! He managed to keep his Lawmage around with the white half of [Profit & Loss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profit+%2F%2F+Loss), but that clearly could have gone better.
