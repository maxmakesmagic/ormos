D'Aniello Madrid's Marvelous Master
===================================




Using the breakout deck of the tournament, Red-Green Marvel, Italy's Carmine D'Aniello bested all others, including Marco Cammilluzzi in a semifinals mirror and finally Nicolas Legendre playing Black-Red Aggro, to become champion of Grand Prix Madrid 2016!

TOP 8 COVERAGE



1,456 players had entered the tournament in total. 441 of them qualified for the second day of competition. Eight advanced to the playoffs, including Alexander Hottmann playing in his third Grand Prix Top 8 and Marco Cammilluzzi playing in his fifth. But when all was said and done, when the Promised End had come, it was 22-year-old Carmine D'Aniello from Italy who had bested them all.


This was D'Aniello's tournament, but it also was Aetherworks Marvel's tournament. For months now, Standard had seemingly been a battle of two decks, White-Blue Flash and Black-Green Delirium, vying for the top spot. This event saw Red-Green Aetherworks Marvel rejoin the fight, and it returned to the metagame in full force, with four of the Top 8 players running the deck. Meanwhile, Black-Green Delirium was surprisingly absent from the Top 8 when (15) Joel Larsson's run ended in ninth place, and various recent innovations like Mono-White Humans, Blue-Red Control, and Black-Green Zombies proved that Standard still was an inventor's paradise.


Congratulations to Carmine D'Aniello, master of Red-Green Marvel and champion of Grand Prix Madrid 2016!

Day 2 Coverage



Welcome back to Spain where 441 players out of the original field of 1,456 reached the requisite record of 6-3 or better and qualified for Day 2 of Grand Prix Madrid 2016. Most notably, Pro Tour *Fate Reforged* champion Antonio Del Moral León and four others began the day at 9-0, closely followed by the world's current No. 20 Petr Sochůrek and Gold-Level pro Grzegorz Kowalski at 8-0-1.


As for decks, the two big players, White-Blue Flash and especially Black-Green Delirium, were again out in full force this weekend. But a third, a challenger to the established order, had appeared with the resurrection of Red-Green Aetherworks Marvel, and had taken two of the 9-0 spots already. Other than that, we'd already seen Mono-White Humans do well and Black-Green Zombies too. It would appear that Standard still had a few tricks up its sleeve.


Join us and 441 players on our way through six more rounds of Standard, a journey toward the Top 8 and the epic conclusion of Grand Prix Madrid 2016!

Day 1 Coverage



After nine rounds of Standard play, Day 1 was done. Out of the original 1,456 players, 441 had earned the eighteen match points necessary to come back on Sunday and compete for the title of Grand Prix Madrid champion.


Over the past couple of months, Standard had seemingly solidified into a battle of Black-Green Delirium and White-Blue Flash vying for the top spot. Indeed, Black-Green Delirium was the most popular choice among the players with three byes. But a recent resurgence of Red-Green Aetherworks Marvel threatened to put the dominance of Black-Green Delirium into jeopardy, and new inventions such as Green-Black Zombies had appeared.


Leading the field overnight with perfect records of 9-0 were five players: Philip van Donselaar (R/G Marvel) from the Netherlands, Daan Pruijt (B/G Zombies) from the Netherlands, Antonio Del Moral León (W/U Flash) from Spain, Luis Gobern (R/G Marvel) from Portugal, and Julien Henry (B/G Delirium) from France. This was but a first step on the way to eternal glory, however, and these players would need to prove themselves on Sunday as Petr Sochůrek and Grzegorz Kowalski were circling hungrily behind at 8-0-1 records.


Join us here tomorrow for more text coverage as we see who can successfully navigate through six more rounds of Standard to make it to the Top 8!
