Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpslc05/fin.jpg)The Mono-Blue archetype in *Kamigawa* Block is generally considered an anti-[Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) deck. If you consider the three byes Antonino took to Salt Lake – not to mention the late finish results of the Grand Prix's Top 8 – aiming for Gifts as the Deck to Beat might not be a bad idea. Blue honestly doesn't hold up very well against most creature strategies: the majority of its citizens are inefficient 1/2 and 0/2 creatures for decent mana investments… But that's not where Mono-Blue is at its strength.


[Jushi Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jushi+Apprentice) on the second turn is a powerful threat against Gifts and many other slow decks. Starting on turn three, you've essentially got your own [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine). When the opponent's deck is designed to take big turns with expensive recursion sequences, even one permission spell can doom his prospects, so keeping a grip full of [Disrupting Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupting+Shoal)s, [Hisoka's Defiance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hisoka%27s+Defiance)s, and especially [Hinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hinder)s is a great strategy if you can keep your mana open... and Mono-Blue with [Jushi Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jushi+Apprentice) waiting to draw cards certainly can.


Sitting across from Antonino in the finals was amateur Karl Briem. Though we've seen a ton of Black Hand over the course of the season, Karl's deck is legitimately a little different. He is pretty heavy on the disruption, starting [Pithing Needle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) *on top* of playing [Psychic Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychic+Spear) and [Distress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distress) main. These cards are meant to attack the development of a slower deck before its cards in hand translate into legitimate threats and answers. Meanwhile, the speed of Karl's [Nezumi Cutthroat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Cutthroat)s is meant to end the game while Black Hand still has the initiative.


### New


On the subject of aggressive Black decks, have you *seen* [the Top 8](/en/events/coverage/jason-olynyk-leads-canadian-rat-pack-worlds) of [Canadian Nationals](/en/events/coverage/jason-olynyk-leads-canadian-rat-pack-worlds)?


Eight decks, two BlueTooth (UrzaTron Blue), *six* of only one additional archetype. The Canadian Nationals Top 8 featured 23 [Llanowar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes), 23 [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial)s, and 22 [Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter)s main deck.


Has the best deck changed once again? Here's the Canadian Champion's Weapon of Choice .

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/canat05/f_olynyk.jpg)Rats, already a contender in pre-*Ninth Edition* Standard, only got better with the addition of probably the most feared Black creature of all time. Now when I first started playing tournaments, we had to contend with turn *one* [Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter). With [Dark Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) a thing of the past, R&D has said that the classic [Necropotence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropotence) threat isn't all that broken… The problem is, [Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter) with [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial) is more-or-less a combination that represents the keys to the current kingdom.


Look at the top decks in Standard, pre-*Ninth Edition*. For all their mana accelerated power, neither BlueTooth nor [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) can easily deal with a 2/2 flyer once it's already in play. Sure, BlueTooth can aim a bounce spell at Lord Hypno, but that just sort of means gaining 2 life if nothing else can be done (you were going to drop a card anyway… why not “choose” to discard [Echoing Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) and pick up two life?). The Red Decks can deal with [Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter), but that's about all. Hypno should feel free to beat up on big, slow, decks as long as they refuse to prepare with [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet)s.


[Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter), which is already rumored to be going for 20 tickets on ***Magic** Online*, isn't even the most broken card in the modern Rats deck. Some months back, when [Arcbound Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager), [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault), and even [Ancient Den](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Den) bit it in Standard, the card that strung consistency alongside the power of Affinity – [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial) – was spared. [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial) doesn't quite boost out second turn [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and third turn [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) in the Rats deck, but it does give the deck a lot of play against the previously dominant BlueTooth decks. Here is Canadian National Team member Tyler Blum's post-*Ninth* version:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/canat05/3_blum.jpg)Notice that Blum's main weapon for stopping [Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter)… is to counter it. He can also slow the scary 2/2 down with [Echoing Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth), as we've mentioned, but that doesn't really “stop” it when [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial) has three counters. Even if it isn't exactly the fastest offensive play Rats can make, sitting behind a developing [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial) can be pretty strong when the recently-best deck is all about the [Condescend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend)s and [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s. Basically a Vial can crawl up to two or three counters and force through whatever its master wants; creatures can appear at the end of turn for a queer sort of haste and counters become irrelevant. Ruh roh Raggy.


This is also where the near-maximum number of Llanowar Wastes comes in. All these Rats players commanded three if not four Viridian Shamans to seal the deal. Say you completely dominate the best deck. The smug BlueTooth players, with their UrzaTrons and consistent draws, their Top tricks and counterspells, might have dominated before Ninth Edition, but you're taking the starch out of their counters, and you know they can't easily deal with your discard creatures. How do you make sure that you win? You might be able to strip their hands and run around their Condescends at the end of turn, but what happens when they just tap out for Memnarch?


You destroy it, of course.


Don't forget that Rats wants to hit three counters on the [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial) *anyway* for [Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter) and [Chittering Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chittering+Rats). [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) can come along for the ride. [Tendo Ice Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendo+Ice+Bridge) and [Llanowar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes) make the Shaman a reasonable cast for games where Vial isn't ready yet. So sorry, [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion)! You really should have waited until ten mana on that [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver)…

“… this was what some are calling the best Grand Prix Top 8 ever.”


That sure is a great Top 8. Antonino won coming off a Nationals win. With only two amateurs in the Top 8, every other player has Grand Prix if not Pro Tour Top 8 experience, and there are some fine theorists in the group. In fact, Gadiel just *won* the relevant Pro Tour (with another Top 8 and Grand Prix win under his belt just this season)… and Kenji is one of the hottest and best players on Tour right now.


But *best* Grand Prix Top 8 ever?


I can't agree with my friend Teddy Cardgame on this one. This title *still* has to go to the old men…


**Grand Prix Vienna 1998**  

Kai Budde  

Christian Gregorich  

Jon Finkel  

Erik Lauer  

Randy Buehler  

Jakub Slemr  

Dirk Hein  

Peer Kröeger


[This Top 8](http://archive.wizards.com/sideboard/article.asp?x=results/GPVIENNA99) had the best player ever to shuffle a deck *and* the second-best player. Budde *and* Finkel! The crazy part is that because this was 1998, before Kai was officially Kai, many pundits would have said that, coming off an Extended Pro Tour win the previous season, tearing up the Grand Prix circuit himself, and in the pre-Bob Maher era the most fearsome Extended player in all the realms of Dominia, the second best player on the Pro Tour after Finkel was *Randy Buehler*, who grabbed a last-minute flight with his teammate – and arguably the best deck designer of all time – Erik Lauer. In an unprecedented move, Buehler and Lauer came across the Atlantic with a [Memory Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Jar) deck, both made the Top 8… and then got the card emergency banned almost immediately! The feat has not been matched by any players or cards since; not even by [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp).


Unlike Salt Lake City, Vienna 1998 had zero players without top-level experience. Gregorich had a Grand Prix Top 16 at the time, and had played in the World Championships; Hein had played in two Worlds. The rest of the Top 8 included Lauer, again the best deck designer of his era, holding an in-season Top 8 at Rome; Slemr, a World Champion; and Kröeger, who held multiple Pro Tour Top 8s in the ancient days of **Magic**… and actually came back for *another* Top 8 just one World Championships removed: sixth place at Worlds 2003!


I suggest the “some” who are calling Salt Lake City the best Top 8 ever check out the [Tournament Center archives](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=sideboard/coverage).

Alongside being just plain good, this deck is extremely creative. Particularly cool is the conceptual leap that Gundersen and his friends made with [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song). The deck can play turn two [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism), turn three [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) into [Enduring Ideal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Ideal). With [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox), the same can be accomplished on turn *two*!


Once [Enduring Ideal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Ideal) comes online, the opponent is in big trouble. The deck can ace Mono-Red with [Ivory Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivory+Mask) + [Form of the Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon). [Form of the Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon), a card that usually keeps a man down at five life forever, provides an odd brand of life gain when combined with [Zur's Weirding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zur%27s+Weirding). Against key permanents, including UrzaTron parts, [Confiscate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confiscate) every turn is yet another powerful tool.


But don't let me tell you. Thomas Gundersen wrote a tournament report about making Top 8, and you can check it out [here](http://www.starcitygames.com/php/news/article/10299.html), thanks to StarCityGames.com. Check it out. I sure learned a lot.
