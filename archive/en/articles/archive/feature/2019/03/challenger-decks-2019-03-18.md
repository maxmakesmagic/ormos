Challenger Decks 2019
=====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 18, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Top-8-Donald-Smith.jpg)
By Donald Smith











Challenger Decks will be available April 12, 2019, and will have an MSRP of $29.99 (only applies to the United States). These decks will be available worldwide in English, with Japanese available in Japan.


Challenger Decks are your on-ramp to Standard. These four individual 75-card decks are geared toward Standard play for the Friday Night *Magic* player and forged out of some of the most powerful strategies in Standard.


Each Challenger Deck comes with a complete 60-card main deck and a 15-card sideboard, and is intended to be playable and competitive at a local level right out of the box. All cards will have been previously printed in Standard and are Standard-legal.


Hey y'all, I'm Donald, the lead designer for this year's Challenger Decks. You might know me from registering [Wily Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wily+Goblin) at the World Championships in 2017 or, perhaps, leading the last year of Challenger Deck design alongside Allen Wu. I'm here to give a little background and behind-the-scenes insight on the decks we've designed for this year. I want to give a shout out to Michael Majors and the whole Play Design team for giving feedback and building these decks with me.


One of the biggest reasons for the success of last year's Challenger Decks is that they were authentic. Those decklists were modeled after lists that placed highly in premiere tournaments at the time, and my goal was to recreate that authenticity with this round of Challenger Decks as well. However, there was a unique challenge presented by *Ravnica Allegiance*. These decks were designed around the time of Pro Tour *Guilds of Ravnica*, so we wouldn't know exactly *how* *Ravnica Allegiance* would shake up the format. Even without considering all the powerful cards *Ravnica Allegiance* has introduced into Standard, the "shock lands" ([Blood Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt), [Breeding Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pool), [Godless Shrine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine), [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain), and [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground)) would open up *countless* three-color combinations for Standard! Actually, there are only ten three-color combinations, but, regardless, we decided to stick to guilds and known decks from *Guilds of Ravnica*. These decks are meant to be competitive out of the box, so the risk of guessing on a guild or three-color combination that *Ravnica Allegiance* enabled was too high. Something as simple as misjudging the optimal curve and playing too many three-drops could cause a deck to not function. Now let's break down each decklist!

There were a lot of different options when we were building "United Assault," the mono-white deck. The Pro Tour *Guilds of Ravnica* featured the full spread of one-mana white creatures: [Snubhorn Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snubhorn+Sentry), [Leonin Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Vanguard), [Skymarcher Aspirant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skymarcher+Aspirant), [Hunted Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Witness), [Dauntless Bodyguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Bodyguard), [Healer's Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Healer%27s+Hawk), and [Rustwing Falcon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rustwing+Falcon) (aka Trusty Rusty). As the metagame evolved after the tournament, we also saw these numbers change. Notably, [Hunted Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Witness) and [Snubhorn Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snubhorn+Sentry) set a precedent over [Rustwing Falcon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rustwing+Falcon) as [Deafening Clarion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deafening+Clarion) became a staple of control decks and [Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler) became less played.


We decided to model "United Assault" off [Luis Scott-Vargas's 2nd-place deck](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptgrn/top-8-decklists-2018-11-10) from Pro Tour *Guilds of Ravnica* because it minimized the amount of guesswork we had to do in terms of the metagame at the decks' release. Luis Scott-Vargas's build was the most self-contained and cohesive build of White Weenie and the least sensitive to metagame pressures.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate)
If you're looking to modify the deck, [Tithe Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe+Taker) and [Unbreakable Formation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unbreakable+Formation) from *Ravnica Allegiance* have been doing great work to fight against control decks! [Ajani, Adversary of Tyrants](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Adversary+of+Tyrants) and [Venerated Loxodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venerated+Loxodon) have been popular options for White Weenie decks, and I encourage you to try different configurations of one-drops, such as more [Hunted Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Witness), [Snubhorn Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snubhorn+Sentry), or [Rustwing Falcon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rustwing+Falcon) if you're running into [Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler)s. Adding either blue or red as a secondary color is also a viable option. Blue gives access to [Deputy of Detention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deputy+of+Detention) and counter spells for more interaction, and red lets you play [Heroic Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heroic+Reinforcements) for explosive attacks.

"Lightning Aggro," the mono-red deck, was much more stable and predictable to build than "United Assault" if only looking at the *Guilds of Ravnica* metagame. However, we knew that the powerful spectacle cards and the new guilds would make an impact to the archetype. To avoid misjudging the optimal configuration of spells (which people are still debating) and potentially ruining the deck, we decided to stick to the tried-and-true [Wizard's Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wizard%27s+Lightning) and [Runaway Steam-Kin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runaway+Steam-Kin) plus [Experimental Frenzy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experimental+Frenzy) packages.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Runaway+Steam-Kin)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runaway+Steam-Kin)
If you're looking to try out different cards for this burn deck, I recommend Light up the Stage, [Skewer the Critics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skewer+the+Critics), and [Risk Factor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Risk+Factor) first and foremost. Then you can explore adding green or black to the deck. [Alex Majlaton's deck](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2019MC1/top-8-decklists-2019-02-23) from Mythic Championship I is a good place to start if you want to add green for cards like [Cindervines](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cindervines) and [Collision // Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collision+%2F%2F+Colossus). If you're more of a [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) like myself, I suggest trying [Rix Maadi Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rix+Maadi+Reveler), [Theater of Horrors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Theater+of+Horrors), [Carnival // Carnage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnival+%2F%2F+Carnage), or, my favorite mirror breaker, [Sovereign's Bite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sovereign%27s+Bite).

"Arcane Tempo," the blue-red Challenger Deck, is yet another deck that was going through lots of fluctuations as the metagame evolved. The number of [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer)s constantly changes, [Enigma Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enigma+Drake) and [Crackling Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Drake) are either played in tandem or one over the other, and cards like [Murmuring Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murmuring+Mystic), [Niv-Mizzet, Parun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Parun), and [The MirariÂ Conjecture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Mirari%C2%A0Conjecture) occasionally appear. We decided to go with a much more controlling build with main deck [Niv-Mizzet, Parun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Parun) and [Murmuring Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murmuring+Mystic) for archetype diversity between the Challenger Decks. Much of our inspiration came from [Pascal Vieren's Izzet Drakes deck](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptgrn/27-30-match-point-decklists-2018-11-11) with which he went 9-1 at Pro Tour *Guilds of Ravnica*.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Parun)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Parun)
If you're interested in being more aggressive and playing a tempo strategy, you can consider adding [Pteramander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pteramander), [Enigma Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enigma+Drake), and [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce). I also recommend [Andrew Jessup's 1st-place deck](http://www.starcitygames.com/decks/125569) from the StarCityGames.com Invitational in December and [Luis Scott-Vargas's deck](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2019MC1/top-8-decklists-2019-02-23) from the Top 8 of Mythic Championship I as inspirations to edit your decklist. This archetype is very open-ended!
