Grix and Treats
===============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 22, 2008 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=%5Bcard%5D)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%5Bcard%5D)Welcome Johnnies of all varieties, and prepare to enter my haunted laboratory! Bwahahaha! Seriously though, I've tricked out the place for the next couple weeks. Of course, it was a bit spooky in the first place, but now it's even spookier. You might want to watch your step. Instead of the normal spiders dangling from the rafters, there are freaking rocket launchers waiting for those below. I call it "missiletoe." 

I also took a hint from the Navidson Record and had my very own "Five-and-a-Half Minute Hallway" installed in my closet. (It also came with a free minotaur, but I saved that for today's first deck.) The endless closet is creepy all right, but after more than a few explorations, I still can't find my socks. But the most terrifying addition to my lab beat all those in terms of true fright factor. Now, when I trap people in my Laboratory Labyrinth, to add to their terror, I blast the music of the Jonas Brothers through the speakers. It doesn't get much worse then that, folks. Let's just say I don't get very many trick-or-treaters around this time of year. 

Sure, I know Halloween is next week, but Grixis Week seems thematically nightmarish in its own right. Think about it: on Grixis, it's probably Halloween every day, except the vampires hand out blood-flavored candies, zombies keep popping out of the ground, and costumes aren't necessary. Since I've got a bit of a treat for you next week, consider this week as a trick or two.

Speaking of tricks, my computer pulled quite the fast one on me last week. We're not talking about a simple [Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter) effect here; this baby got hit with a [Return to Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Return+to+Dust) and was RFG'd faster than a [Molten Firebird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Firebird). Lightheartedness aside, I want to formally apologize for missing a week. Being left high and dry without any notice isn't much fun, and having your computer crash is even less fun, so the whole affair was a mess and I'm sorry.

Usually this is the part where I transition from sadness to happiness, but Grixis isn't exactly a joy-inspiring place from the looks (and denizens) of it. So I'll throw in my first trick of the day with the first deck: DRAGONS! Hey, they make me happy.

Grixin' It Up
[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Death+Baron)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron)We'll get to the dragons in a bit. First, I'll introduce my first guest, the [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron). Seriously, check this guy out. I'm pretty much a full Johnny, but outstanding art or card names really appeal to my Vorthos side. This is the [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron). Don't mess with him, or you may find a bone shoved in your left nostril. It's possibly my favorite name in the set, next to a certain spell from Esper. This Tycoon of Thanatopsis (thanks, Thesaurus.com!) can restore the fire in the old bones of Skeletons and Zombies alike. Since most of the creatures with unearth in the set are Zombies, [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) helps them pack a punch. 

The basic roots of this deck were planted even before the full spoiler of *Shards of Alara* came out. Back when the Visual Spoiler was plodding along, I noticed something neat. A turn-three [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) followed by a turn-four [Crucible of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crucible+of+Fire) would make for a big [Bladewing the Risen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bladewing+the+Risen), and here I was thinking we'd just finished a tribal block. Then it hit me, just like it's probably hit many of you already: changelings. The infamous asterisks can come down on turn two thanks to [Fire-Belly Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Belly+Changeling) and [Skeletal Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Changeling) (ding goes the theme bell), and the aforementioned tribe pumpers expertly curve out. 

I added most of the red and black changelings, but [Cairn Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cairn+Wanderer) stuck out like a sore thumb. There were no crazy abilities on my creatures, and it was just an expensive changeling for a while. That is, until [Corpse Connoisseur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Connoisseur) was spoiled. 

The two are a neat combo. The Connoisseur can find any creature and pop it into the graveyard, which then spiffs up the Wanderer. A great creature to bury in your graveyard is [Hell's Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hell%27s+Thunder), not only for unearth shenanigans but to transform [Cairn Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cairn+Wanderer) into a sort of permanent copy of it (a flying, hasty 4/4.) Other quality selections include the fear-inducing [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), the hasty and trampling [Spark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spark+Elemental) (which kills itself pretty efficiently), and the pretty much awesome all-around [Hellkite Overlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellkite+Overlord) as a fun silver bullet. 

Lost in the [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron)'s text is the nifty deathtouch ability, which I decided to take advantage of with [Thornbite Staff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornbite+Staff), harkening back to the [Moonglove Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonglove+Changeling) / [Thornbite Staff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornbite+Staff) super [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) combo. And to ramp up the flavor train, plus to give the deck some legs against, well, actual creatures, I added some [Skeletonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletonize)s. A sort of reverse [Crib Swap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crib+Swap), [Skeletonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletonize) can "Bolt" something and give you a Skeleton token to be pumped by [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron). Plus, the card concept is extremely cool.

I've skipped out on some of my own suggestions, just to fit everything in. There are many ways you can take this deck, so customize away. Anyway, this is only using the Standard pool. In Extended you've got Connoisseur selections such as [Mirri the Cursed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirri+the+Cursed) and [both](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=both)[Akromas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akromas), while actual reanimation spells such as [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) could team up with the Connoisseur to switch up the strategy a bit.

[Taurean Mauler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taurean+Mauler) is the minotaur I mentioned in the introduction. And as a quick note, if a couple of those references bypassed your reference-detector, go out and read *House of Leaves* by Mark Z. Danielewski. Now.

A Sundae AM
[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ad+Nauseam)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam)The grotesque yet Grixisly cool art of [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) doesn't usually conjure thoughts of eating ice cream sundaes in the morning, but hey, anagrams never lie. 

A couple of readers out there offered their guesses on which *Shards of Alara* card I'd codenamed "evil Pac-Man" during the [Invincible Hymn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invincible+Hymn) discussion , and some were right on the money: [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) has drawn interest from all psychological profiles of **Magic**, and for good reason. According to [a recent Ask Wizards](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/askwizards/1008#1016), the build-around-me sorcery is from Grixis, and it gushes nightmarish flavor. If I had to guess, I had say those scattered pages are from the Necronomicon. Look how shriveled that guy looks. An obvious Deadite.

Getting back to the deck-building at hand, a successful [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) wants to be like a one-shot, instant-speed [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), drawing you lots of cards at the cost of (hopefully) not lots of life. I immediately realized that high converted mana costs are obviously out, and that thought turned me in the other direction. If my deck had a lot of cards with only one or even zero mana symbols, I'd get a lot of mileage out of a Nauseam. 

This first deck falls into the slightly straightforward brand of "Turboland" decks. These decks use far more lands than usually played, in order to take advantage of the effects of spells like [Manabond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabond), [Exploration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration), and [Summer Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summer+Bloom) to have tons of lands on the board very quickly. I figured [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) would be great alongside these landlubbers, and especially with [Manabond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabond). Dump a truckload of lands into your hand with a Nauseam, which then get dumped into play with the [Manabond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabond).

For win conditions, I turned to two recent spells that love having tons of lands to guzzle mana from. [Helix Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helix+Pinnacle) can be dropped on the very first turn, and while 100 tower counters seems daunting, with all the mana to be made with this deck, you'll get there sooner then you might think. Plus, if you reveal it during an [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam), its low converted mana cost won't hurt you too badly. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The other is [Sigil of Distinction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+Distinction). First off, it has a converted mana cost of 0, since X costs count as 0 when the spell isn't on the stack, such as during an [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam). Next, you can soup it up with 21 or so charge counters, equip it to one of your creatures at the cost of one counter, and swing for the win right then and there. The Sigil is basically a free [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze) to your opponent's dome! Of course, we need some creatures to equip to, which is why I benched [Exploration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration) for a creature counterpart, [Budoka Gardener](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Budoka+Gardener), who offers a third way to win. Since we're running over 30 lands here, I used some creature lands as well.

The [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) helped me a lot whenever I drew it. Early on, you can suck [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam)s out of your deck and into your hand, or just fetch land for the [Budoka Gardener](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Budoka+Gardener). In the late game, it's a great topdeck, because you can search up your win conditions.

Because [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) can fill your hand with a lot of lands, I tinkered with the "hand size matters" cards from *Saviors of Kamigawa* for a bit, ultimately leaving them out. [Sasaya, Orochi Ascendant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sasaya%2C+Orochi+Ascendant) could probably make an appearance somewhere, and [Descendant of Masumaro](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Descendant+of+Masumaro) could get huge fast. If you're averse to [Sigil of Distinction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+Distinction) for some reason, [Empyrial Plate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empyrial+Plate) is another equipment option. Hey, this might even be a home for [Sophic Centaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sophic+Centaur)!

And before I get tons of emails about it, yes, [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) is a great option is you want to go red. However, I'm more psyched about red because of [Bulwark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bulwark). Yes, [Bulwark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bulwark). Look that monster up.

This next [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) deck was sent to me by Dominique H., and it's got a doozy of a combo. Instead of trying to lose as little life as possible, as I did with the first deck, Dominique wants to blow your entire life total and put your whole library in your hand. Usually, that would come with the side effect of losing, but Dominique came prepared: "You need to have an [Elvish Piper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Piper) and a [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thousand-Year+Elixir) in play. With those two permanents in play, cast [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) during your upkeep. Use the Piper to put a [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) from your hand in play or cast [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace). Then dig through your deck until you draw a [Magus of the Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Mirror). Put it in play with the Piper and then proceed to change your negative life total with your opponent using your hasty Magus."

Cruelsville
Alright. No more theme-shirking. Today's final deck contains all the colors of Grixis, which resemble especially painful bruises when mixed. Perhaps the embodiment of the Grixis shard is its respective Ultimatum, [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum).

To be honest, when the Ultimatum cycle sunk in upon reading the spoiler, the first thing that jumped out at me wasn't the crazy effects on them. No, it was the whopping *seven* colored mana symbols in that mana cost. I thought we'd seen the reigning champion of colored symbols with [Primalcrux](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux) a couple months ago. Because [Primalcrux](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux) is basically just a big beatstick, I didn't use the card I was thinking of with it. However, with these crazy Ultimatums in existence, I can unveil my super secret tech: [Charmed Pendant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charmed+Pendant).

This underused (actually, probably never-used) rare from *Odyssey*, can, assuming there's a [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) on top of your library, tap for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif). Obviously, this amount of mana is just what you need to cast the epitome of cruelty, so with a small down payment of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) or ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) to play or flashback [Recoup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup), you'll be able to play [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) far earlier than normal. ([Dralnu, Lich Lord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dralnu%2C+Lich+Lord) could also work, but its high mana cost and painful replacement effect were big danger signs for me.)

This sort of mentality works perfectly with the various unearth creatures. Use the Pendant to suck the colored mana out of a [Fire-Field Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Field+Ogre), then unearth it. Not only do you basically unearth for free, you've actually made a small (although colorless) profit! [Avatar of Discord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Discord) sports three colored mana symbols while getting your unearth creatures into the graveyard. I was looking for some sweepers with colored symbols, and [Flamebreak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamebreak) got the start. [Manaforge Cinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manaforge+Cinder) can help fix your mana despite its slight restriction, and [Scourge Devil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+Devil)s can pump up your army of spooks.
