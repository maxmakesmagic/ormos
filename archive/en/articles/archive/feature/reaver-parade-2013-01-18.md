The Reaver Parade
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 18, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Last week, [I wrote about populating](/en/articles/archive/trostanis-angels-2013-01-10) 
[Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)'s Angels with [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice). Well, since then, PillPallPoll on **Magic Online** has turned populate up to eleven by throwing [Cackling Counterpart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart) into the mix. 

![Drogskol Reaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drogskol+Reaver) PillPallPoll's deck replaces [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and Planeswalkers with a robust, synergistic lifegain theme, pushing it much further than we usually see. This deck combines [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice); [Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender); [Chalice of Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+Life); and [Drogskol Reaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Reaver). Any two of those work together spectacularly, and each of them buys time so you can get them all together. With [Cackling Counterpart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart), you can make a token of [Drogskol Reaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Reaver), which you can then populate with Trostani, at which point I quickly lose track of how much life you gain and how many cards you draw. 

 If you're looking to go over the top with a deck that gives you plenty of time to rub it in, this is an outstanding choice for Friday Night **Magic** to really win spectacularly.
