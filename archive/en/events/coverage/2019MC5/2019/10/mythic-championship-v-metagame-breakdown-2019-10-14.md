Mythic Championship V Metagame Breakdown
========================================



 Posted in [Event Archive](/en/events/coverage)
 on October 11, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











Mythic Championship V, the second Mythic Championship to be held on *MTG Arena*, will start on Friday, October 18. The tournament features 32 *Magic* Pro League members and 36 Challengers who get to test their mettle in *Throne of Eldraine* Standard. There's a total prize pool of $750,000 on the line and a fresh, post-rotation Standard format to explore.


Once Upon a Time ...
====================


... is the most-played card among all 68 decklists.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Once+Upon+A+Time)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Once+Upon+a+Time)
No, really, with 169 total copies, [Once Upon a Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Once+Upon+a+Time) is the most-played card in the entire event, followed by [Hydroid Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydroid+Krasis) (156 copies) and [Growth Spiral](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spiral) (138 copies). The next most-played cards from *Throne of Eldraine* specifically are [Fabled Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabled+Passage) (128 copies), [Questing Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Beast) (98 copies), and [Oko, Thief of Crowns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oko%2C+Thief+of+Crowns) (94 copies), so these are relatively safe bets if you're wondering what to spend your wildcards on.


But the most-played cards are merely a sneak peek. I broke down the entire metagame, so follow me on an adventure through the new Standard with a representative list for each archetype from [all 68 decklists in the field](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2019MC5/all-mythic-championship-v-standard-decklists).


![](https://media.wizards.com/2019/events/2019MC5/MCV_Metagame_decks_Popular.jpg)


These are all archetypes that were registered by four or more players, and together they make up 79.5% of the metagame. We'll get to the remaining 20.5% later.


What stands out is the dominance of [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead). At 33.8%, Bant Golos is the most popular archetype by far. And if we add up Bant Golos, Golos Fires, and a single Four-Color Golos deck, then a staggering 42.7% of the players are planning to create a stack of Zombie tokens. Even though [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) rotated out, [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead) is still busted. So let's start by breaking down the number-one deck at Mythic Championship V.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Golos%2C+Tireless+Pilgrim)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golos%2C+Tireless+Pilgrim)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Field+of+the+Dead)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead)

Starting with [Arboreal Grazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arboreal+Grazer) and [Growth Spiral](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spiral), this deck aims to ramp into [Golos, Tireless Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golos%2C+Tireless+Pilgrim), [Realm-Cloaked Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Realm-Cloaked+Giant), or [Hydroid Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydroid+Krasis) as quickly as possible. Once you hit your seventh land (which is easy to do because the deck usually has 28 to 29 lands, most of which having different names) it's easy to keep triggering [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead), generating a horde of Zombies. Alternatively, you can activate Golos to drown your opponent in card advantage.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Growth+Spiral)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spiral)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Realm-Cloaked+Giant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Realm-Cloaked+Giant)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hydroid+Krasis)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydroid+Krasis)
[Once Upon a Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Once+Upon+a+Time) means you can execute this plan quite consistently, and the never-ending stream of Zombies provides inevitability in midrange matchups. It's hard to go over the top of Bant Golos or to beat them in a late-game attrition war.


The sample list shown above was registered by Seth Manfield, who won his MPL Core Split, which means he's already qualified for Day Two. He's not the only MPL Core Split winner who made this deck choice, as Carlos Romão is also in Day Two with Bant Golos. Their lists are a few cards apart. For example, compared to Manfield, Romão has one more [Realm-Cloaked Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Realm-Cloaked+Giant) and one fewer [Time Wipe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Wipe), and he has more [Beanstalk Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beanstalk+Giant)s and fewer [Arboreal Grazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arboreal+Grazer)s. There are also minor differences in the mana base and the sideboard. However, the broad strokes are similar, and both of them are ready for the mirror match with trump cards like [Kenrith, the Returned King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kenrith%2C+the+Returned+King), which can give Zombies haste, [Beanstalk Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beanstalk+Giant)s trample, and put any excess mana to good use.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kenrith%2C+the+Returned+King)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kenrith%2C+the+Returned+King)
Bant Golos, given its metagame dominance, is clearly the deck to beat in Standard right now. Fortunately, there are ways to beat it. With the right interaction or the right strategy, Bant Golos can be attacked. Let's go over the other archetypes.

This archetype may be described as Simic Midrange, but I labeled it as Simic Food because it exploits the best new Food cards from *Throne of Eldraine*, namely [Gilded Goose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Goose); [Oko, Thief of Crowns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oko%2C+Thief+of+Crowns); and [Wicked Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wicked+Wolf). Any game where you can curve into these three synergistic spells by turn three will be hard to lose. In other games, an enormous [Hydroid Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydroid+Krasis) fueled by [Nissa, Who Shakes the World](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Who+Shakes+the+World) can easily take it down.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gilded+Goose)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Goose)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Oko%2C+Thief+of+Crowns)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oko%2C+Thief+of+Crowns)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wicked+Wolf)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wicked+Wolf)
These is some variety between Simic Food decks, but I particularly like this build submitted by Kai Budde, Andrew Cuneo, Reid Duke, William Jensen, Gabriel Nassif, and Shahar Shenhar. The standouts in their build are the four main deck copies of [Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Disdainful+Stroke)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke)
[Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke) seems like a perfect metagame call, because countering a single Golos or [Time Wipe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Wipe) represents an enormous swing that can disrupt the opponent long enough for you to close out the game. It's an ideal answer card if you don't expect many players to show up with low-curve aggro decks. [Teferi, Time Raveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler) obviously prevents [Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke) from being cast, but Simic Food has enough haste creatures to mow Teferi down.

Piotr Głogowski recently won the Sapphire Division of the MPL Eldraine Split with Golgari Adventure (which qualified him for Day Two at Mythic Championship VII in December), so it makes sense that he stuck with the same deck. [Legion's End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+End) remains an excellent answer to Zombie tokens. He has cut [Assassin's Trophy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassin%27s+Trophy) to make room for [Vivien, Arkbow Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivien%2C+Arkbow+Ranger) since then, but by and large his list is similar to the one he found success with in his MPL Split.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Legion%27s+End)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+End)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vivien%2C+Arkbow+Ranger)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivien%2C+Arkbow+Ranger)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rankle%2C+Master+of+Pranks)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rankle%2C+Master+of+Pranks)
[Lucky Clover](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lucky+Clover) is not included because the Adventure theme is not that heavy (indeed, you could also label Głogowski's build as Golgari Midrange with an Adventure sub-theme) which means that there aren't enough Adventures worth copying. And freeing up slots for an evasive threat like [Rankle, Master of Pranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rankle%2C+Master+of+Pranks) that can fly over Zombie tokens is worthwhile too. However, Takeshi Senoo still had faith in [Lucky Clover](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lucky+Clover); he registered a Golgari Adventure list with the artifact and [Smitten Swordmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smitten+Swordmaster), which can set up a deadly drain combo.

Over the past year, some of the biggest Standard developments have come out of a house in Prague, the Czech Republic, that is home to both Stanislav Cifka and Ondřej Stráský. Decks like Four-Color Dreadhorde, Bant [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), and Kethis Combo were either innovated or popularized by the pair, with Cifka in particular having a great feeling for how to tune decks and change cards around so that things make sense. When both of them qualified for the Mythic Championship via the grueling MCQW—where they bested thousands of Mythic-ranked competitors—all eyes were on them for Standard innovation in the new format.


They teamed up with Martin Jůza, Grzegorz Kowalski, and Oliver Tiu to prepare for Mythic Championship V, and their solution to the format was this Bant Ramp deck that is built to maximize turn-three Nissa. Indeed, with four copies of [Gilded Goose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Goose) and [Once Upon a Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Once+Upon+a+Time), two copies of [Arboreal Grazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arboreal+Grazer) and several two-mana accelerants, this busted-dream start can happen with startling consistency. And when you can't grind out [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead) in the late game, going faster is a great plan. Their ramp plan is supported further by main deck [Agent of Treachery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agent+of+Treachery), which can steal [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead) as early as turn four.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deputy+of+Detention)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deputy+of+Detention)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Agent+of+Treachery)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agent+of+Treachery)
The white splash is mainly for [Deputy of Detention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deputy+of+Detention), which is one of the best answers in the format to an army of Zombie tokens. They can even bounce it with [Teferi, Time Raveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler) to exile all Zombie tokens once more.


Although their list features a minor Food theme in [Gilded Goose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Goose) and [Oko, Thief of Crowns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oko%2C+Thief+of+Crowns), they don't have main deck [Wicked Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wicked+Wolf), which sets their list apart from Bant Food decks. All in all, the Czechs told me that they are confident in the deck; they believe they have a decent matchup against Golos, and a good game overall against everything.

[Circuitous Route](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circuitous+Route); [Golos, Tireless Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golos%2C+Tireless+Pilgrim); and [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead) are familiar, but this archetype (registered by Mythic Champion Autumn Burchett, amongst others) plays out notably different from Bant Golos.


The red splash most importantly grants [Fires of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Invention). The enchantment is reminiscent of [Wilderness Reclamation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilderness+Reclamation) in terms of mana potential, and it even circumvents colored mana requirements, which can lead to some busted draws. For example, you could play [Fires of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Invention), immediately sweep the board with [Deafening Clarion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deafening+Clarion), and use [Fae of Wishes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fae+of+Wishes) to grab and cast [Nicol Bolas, Dragon-God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nicol+Bolas%2C+Dragon-God) on the next turn. Getting [Unmoored Ego](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unmoored+Ego) from your sideboard and naming [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead) can also be quite effective.

In the meantime, [Fires of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Invention) keeps all your lands untapped, allowing you to sink that mana into [Kenrith, the Returned King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kenrith%2C+the+Returned+King) to accrue further resource advantages.

You know what is good against an army of Zombie tokens? Evasion!


You know what's good against [Time Wipe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Wipe) or [Cast Off](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Realm-Cloaked+Giant)? Haste!


Gruul Aggro, the deck chosen by World Champion Javier Domínguez and three Challengers, combines both of these aspects. Evasion could take the form of flying in [Skarrgan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skarrgan+Hellkite), unblockability in [Questing Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Beast), or trample in [Gruul Spellbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Spellbreaker). But no matter which creature you draw, their evasive abilities tend to match up well against a horde of 2/2 Zombies.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Skarrgan+Hellkite)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skarrgan+Hellkite)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Questing+Beast)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Beast)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gruul+Spellbreaker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Spellbreaker)
The finishing power of this deck is also absurd, thanks to [Embercleave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Embercleave) and [Collision // Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collision+%2F%2F+Colossus). Suppose your opponent is at 17 life and blocks your [Questing Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Beast) with a 4/4. Seems pretty safe, right? Well, if both the Equipment and the pump spell come down, then [Questing Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Beast) turns into a 9-power double-striking deathtouch trampler, which (due to the interaction between trample and deathtouch) means that all of a sudden the attack is lethal. Don't underestimate the damage output potential of this deck.

One of the best ways to beat Bant Golos is by going under it, and this deck exceeds at that. This means that you need to go blazingly fast, which implies running a *lot* of one-drops. Thanks to [Tournament Grounds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tournament+Grounds), this list can support one-drop Knights in three colors and get out of the gates extremely quickly.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tournament+Grounds)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tournament+Grounds)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Weaselback+Redcap)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weaselback+Redcap)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inspiring+Veteran)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiring+Veteran)
Ben Stark, who settled on the above list together with Eric Froehlich, even has [Weaselback Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weaselback+Redcap) to up the one-drop count to sixteen! Since Stark won his MPL Core split, he's already through to Day Two. I'll be honest, [Weaselback Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weaselback+Redcap) is not a card I had expected to see in Day Two of a Constructed event, but since it has the right creature type for [Inspiring Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiring+Veteran) and [Acclaimed Contender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acclaimed+Contender), here we are.


In the sideboard, I want to point out [Tectonic Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Rift) as sweet tech. Kenji Egashira's list represents a small departure from the stock Bant Golos list, as he runs [Deafening Clarion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deafening+Clarion) along with three copies of [Oko, Thief of Crowns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oko%2C+Thief+of+Crowns).

His sideboard contains three copies of [Ashiok, Dream Render](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashiok%2C+Dream+Render), which means that he's well-prepared for other [Field of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+the+Dead) decks. A single Ashiok can prevent [Golos, Tireless Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golos%2C+Tireless+Pilgrim); [Circuitous Route](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circuitous+Route); and [Fertile Footsteps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beanstalk+Giant) from grabbing any lands, thereby shutting down a big part of the deck.

One benefit of the Jeskai build is that its mana base enables [Narset, Parter of Veils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils) and [Drawn from Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drawn+from+Dreams), which ensure you'll be able to keep chaining free spells and won't run out of gas.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Parter+of+Veils)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drawn+from+Dreams)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drawn+from+Dreams)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sarkhan+the+Masterless)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+the+Masterless)
Another card that fits into the Jeskai mana base is [Sarkhan the Masterless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+the+Masterless), which is a scary threat to face for Bant Golos players. In conjunction with other planeswalkers, Sarkhan threatens a fast clock, can't be blocked by Zombie tokens, and can't be touched by sorcery-speed sweepers.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Casualties+of+War)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Casualties+of+War)
One final note: In a Jeskai shell, it seems even more amazing to grab [Casualties of War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Casualties+of+War) from your sideboard and cast it without controlling a single green or black land. [Fires of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Invention) definitely enables some cool plays.

Combining Rotting Regisaur or Questing Beast with Embercleave seems to be a theme, and I can imagine why Caleb Durward went that route. As I've mentioned several times already, you can't go over the top of Bant Golos, but you can go under it. Building a huge double striker is an excellent plan that can end the game on the spot, especially since Bant Golos generally lacks instant-speed removal spells.


Durward's list is not as aggressive as Gruul Aggro because he lacks one-drops and his two-drops are more concerned with generating card advantage in the mid-game. But make no mistake—between haste, evasion, and pump spells, this deck packs a punch.

Lee Shi Tian is the only Mono-Red player in Mythic Championship V, which is a little surprising as I had expected more players to take Mono-Red to the event. However, because Lee won his MPL Core split, he's already through to Day Two.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Torbran%2C+Thane+of+Red+Fell)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torbran%2C+Thane+of+Red+Fell)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cavalcade+of+Calamity)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavalcade+of+Calamity)
The big new addition from *Throne of Eldraine* is [Torbran, Thane of Red Fell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torbran%2C+Thane+of+Red+Fell), which can triple the damage output of [Cavalcade of Calamity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavalcade+of+Calamity). This can set up quick kills, which is where you want to be in this metagame. Bant Golos wants to make the game go as long as possible so it can establish an enormous army of Zombies, but a hyper-aggressive red aggro plan can easily go underneath.


Notable in Lee's list is the lack of main deck burn spells. There's no [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock), [Slaying Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaying+Fire), or [Skewer the Critics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skewer+the+Critics). Instead, he focuses on repetitive damage sources only. This seems like a good deck-building choice in a field dominated by Bant Golos, which has no creatures you really want to burn anyway. While Mono-Red Cavalcade may not be well-positioned against [Lovestruck Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lovestruck+Beast)s or [Questing Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Beast)s, I believe it has an excellent matchup against Bant Golos, so I could see Lee making a deep run at Mythic Championship V.
