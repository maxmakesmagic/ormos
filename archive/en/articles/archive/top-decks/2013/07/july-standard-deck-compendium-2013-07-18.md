July Standard Deck Compendium
=============================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on July 18, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG's exclusive [**Magic Online**](/en/articles/archive/2011-10-17) column. ***Magic** 2014*  hits the shelves tomorrow night. There's a lot of speculation about the Standard metagame and how it will look once ***Magic** 2014*  becomes legal. Let's take a moment to discuss the implications 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr256_liliana.jpg)  
 I expect [Gladecover Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gladecover+Scout) and [Fiendslayer Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiendslayer+Paladin) to push a lot of players toward cards like [Unflinching Courage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) and [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor). As a result, cards like [Rolling Temblor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) will probably see a significant increase in play. We can also expect [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), [Far & Away](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%26amp%3B+Away), and [Devour Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh) to become more common. 



[Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) is my favorite card in ***Magic** 2014* . We have no way of knowing what kind of removal people will be playing, but I expect [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) to be somewhat popular, at least out of sideboards. There are a number of ways to force the Angel through your opponent's removal, though. Cards like [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) and [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) seem like the best options available. I have many fond memories of [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) and the new ***Magic** 2014*  mythic rare is eerily similar in the way that it dominates a game. 



[Kalonian Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Hydra) is one of the more impressive cards. It can't claw you back into a game like [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune), but it closes a game faster than just about anything. The card has amazing synergy with [Exava, Rakdos Blood Witch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exava%2C+Rakdos+Blood+Witch) and I wouldn't be surprised if we saw an aggressive/midrangey Jund strategy that took advantage of this excellent interaction. The card also combos nicely with most of the other Golgari and Rakdos creatures from *Return to Ravnica* and *Dragon's Maze*.

 Today, I'd like to take a final look at pre-***Magic** 2014*  Standard. I'm impressed with the current Standard's diversity from start to finish. Here we are, in the last week of the format, and there are still more than ten unique and competitive archetypes. Learning about these decks will help us lay a groundwork for our ***Magic** 2014*  creations. Let's jump right in and start checking out some decklists.

 This is a reasonably exciting new deck. The deck takes elements from the aggressive Jund strategies and couples them with [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite). This puts the deck in an incredible position against most decks in the field. I really love the fact that the deck can [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) into [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek). It's important to establish board presence quickly in the current Standard format and that seems like a great way to apply pressure, and put up defenses, all while ramping you into a juicy Bonfire or powerful four- or five-mana spell.

[Angel of Glory's Rise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) made a comeback in the last month as players started taking graveyard hate out of their decks. The newest versions of the deck don't bother using [Undercity Informer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Informer) to "go infinite" and win the game that turn. There aren't enough Esper decks in the field that can actually grind you out once you combo anymore. Having the loop with a [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) or even double [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) is almost always going to be good enough. Try leaving an Angel exiled with [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) to protect yourself from a big [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) in the appropriate matchups and comboing will almost always result in victory.
