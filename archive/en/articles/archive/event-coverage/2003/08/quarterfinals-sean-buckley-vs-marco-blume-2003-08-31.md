Quarterfinals: Sean Buckley vs. Marco Blume
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 31, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_509.png)
By Mary Van Tyne











Marco Blume is the other member of the infamous Phoenix Foundation, who are crashing in Atlanta after the Grand Prix. You've already been hearing about him all weekend. Sean is from Connecticut, and seems slightly awed to be playing against the impressive Marco.

### Game 1

Marco was forced to double-mulligan in this game, giving Sean an incredible early advantage. His first spell was a cycled [Renewed Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renewed+Faith). Sean kept playing mountains and goblins, mustering a strong offense after Marco's sluggish start. He played a [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) and a [Goblin Warchief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief), then busted in. He followed up with [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) directly at Marco.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpatl03/a893.jpg)The numerous attacks brought the stalwart German down to five life in short order. Facing a Piledriver, a Sledder, and the Warchief, Marco drew and played a [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), but it wasn't nearly enough to save him.

Marco Blume 0 – Sean Buckley 1

### Game 2

Sean kept trying not to smile as he sideboarded rapidly. Marco, for his part, looked deeply contemplative as he shuffled his cards together and broke up his [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s. He kept stealing looks at the game happening right next to his, where an animated Zvi Mowshowitz was duking it out with Keith McLaughlin.

This time it was Sean who reshuffled his cards, eventually happy with six. Marco led with a [Krosan Verge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Verge) and a forest, and Sean's first creature was a [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). He then calmly followed it up with a [Sulfuric Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfuric+Vortex). Marco was forced to allow the Vortex to resolve, and sacrificed his Verge to find a plains and a forest. He shook his head as he presented his deck for Sean to cut.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpatl03/a897.jpg)Marco frowned at his cards as he fanned them out. He played an [Anurid Brushhopper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Brushhopper) and passed the turn, going to seventeen. Sean played a mountain and then a [Clickslither](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clickslither), sending both his creatures into the red zone. Marco blocked the [Clickslither](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clickslither) with his Brushhopper, and Sean sacrificed the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) to make the [Clickslither](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clickslither) larger. Marco then opted to discard two Deep Analyses to remove the Brushhopper from the game, then took his damage from the unblocked Goblin and the Vortex.

On Marco's turn, he played another Brushhopper and attacked with the original one. He grinned as he passed the turn back, asking the table judge for a pen to keep track of his life total. Sean's next plays made Marco pretty sad, though. He played a [Goblin Warchief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief) followed by another [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), and sent the new Smith and the [Clickslither](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clickslither) into the red zone. Marco blocked with the Brushhopper again, allowed Sean to sacrifice the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), then played a [Moment's Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace).

Marco attacked with his unhindered Brushhoppers, bringing Sean down to seven. Sean played a [Barbarian Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Ring) and passed the turn to Marco, who drew and then attacked with a shrug. Sean blocked with the Warchief and the [Clickslither](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clickslither), then sacrificed the Warchief to prevent the [Clickslither](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clickslither)'s untimely demise. Marco discarded a [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) and a [Skycloud Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skycloud+Expanse) to remove the Brushhopper from the game.

On Sean's turn, he played a [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) and passed again, going to three from the Vortex. Marco untapped and attacked again, prompting more of the aforementioned combat tricks. Either way, Marco won the race, and both players quickly came to that conclusion.

Marco Blume 1 – Sean Buckley 1

### Game 3

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpatl03/a896.jpg)Sean opted to keep his original hand this game, leading with a mountain, but no first-turn play. His second-turn play was another mountain and a [Defense Grid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defense+Grid), followed by a third-turn [Sulfuric Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfuric+Vortex). Marco played a bunch of lands, followed by an [Anurid Brushhopper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Brushhopper) on his third turn. 

Sean's first creature was a [Goblin Piledriver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piledriver), which he decided not to throw in front of Marco's attacking Brushhopper. Marco played another land on his turn and calmly threw down another Brushhopper, drawing exclamations of approval from the crowd. Sean played a [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) at Marco's head, trying to get some more damage in before things went from bad to worse.

It was a grasp at a sinking ship, as Marco played a [Mirari's Wake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari%27s+Wake) on his next turn. Attacking with bigger Brushhoppers certainly isn't terrible. Sean drew his card on his turn after Marco's attack, then extended his hand.

"You're not really happy unless you're attacking, are you?" Matt Ranks asked.

"I feel I should do it. For the people," Marco answered. 

**Final Result:** Marco Blume 2 – Sean Buckley 1
