Going Big and Going Home
========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on April 25, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







Everybody loves [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped), or at least that's [what I gathered from you](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/do-you-know-mushroom-man-2013-04-11). His flourishing body fungus (and likely halitosis) didn't deter a landslide of feedback about cards that work with him, and the palpable excitement about *Dragon's Maze* in general was clear as well.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ct/ct244_varolz.jpg)[Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped+)  | Art by [Adam Paquette](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Adam%20Paquette%22%5D)


We'll get to *Dragon's Maze* proper later (you'll want to jump to the end if Varolz isn't your cup of Commander tea), but it's time to look over what Varolz means to you and which six cards you felt were indispensable for the legendary Troll Warrior.


### Theme Versus Value


When [we first looked at](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/do-you-know-mushroom-man-2013-04-11) [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped), we ended with a recap of the many angles of cards we could take with him:



* Creatures you want to scavenge
* Creatures that put themselves into the graveyard while giving us effects
* Creatures you want to sacrifice
* Cards that care about the graveyard and sacrifice
* Cards that care about power or +1/+1 counters
* Cards that care about having lots of creatures
* Cards that are just awesome in black-green

The fact we can easily list this many options for approaching him led to diverse feedback, except for a handful of cards that appears in most of your lists:



* [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought) and [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)
* [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) and [Swiftfoot Boots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swiftfoot+Boots)
* [Corpsejack Menace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpsejack+Menace) and [Riftsweeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftsweeper)

At least one of these six cards appeared in most of the lists you sent in, and they all serve different, valuable purposes.


[Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow), and its distant relative [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought), are the epitome of getting bang for your granting-scavenge buck. Generally, casting either card will let you move your creature to the graveyard immediately, where spending just one more mana will put a pile of +1/+1 counters on something. Maximizing your counters to mana spent is an easy idea to follow, but it comes at a price.

Consider [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) and [Swiftfoot Boots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swiftfoot+Boots), which grant haste and protection when equipped. This is certainly helpful for Varolz, but these Equipment are staples in Commander that enhance nearly any deck. Supporting your best creatures with Greaves or Boots is helpful at any point in the game. Cards like [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) are best *only* with Varolz doing his thing. This means that when Varolz is otherwise unavailable, that titanic [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought) is going to seem pretty weak.

It's the same drawback for [Corpsejack Menace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpsejack+Menace) and [Riftsweeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftsweeper): If you put too many cards into your deck that work *exclusively* with your commander, you can find yourself in situations where there's little for you to do. I don't mean to say *don't* put cards like [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) or [Corpsejack Menace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpsejack+Menace) into a Varolz deck (I suggested [Corpsejack Menace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpsejack+Menace) for Varolz, too!), but you want to consider how many picks won't shine without the commanding dance partner.

Many of you naturally picked up on this balance with your suggestions, like Angus:



* [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)—Insane to scavenge
* [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought)—Insane to scavenge
* [Thought Gorger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger)—Draw all the cards
* [Mindless Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindless+Automaton)—Draw all the cards
* [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion)—Scavenging you the best fireballs ever
* [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic)—Suit up with counters

[Thought Gorger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) and [Mindless Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindless+Automaton) are clever ways to make the most of +1/+1 counters. Drawing cards is always powerful, but without Varolz around you'd still be happy to use them. [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) and [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic) are offensive ways to apply counters but still do some work without any extras.

Joshua had similar thoughts:



* [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought)/[Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)—Both serve the same purpose: die upon coming into play and immediately provide 12 or 13 counters for one mana with scavenge.
* [Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn)/[any bloodrush creature]—Use bloodrush to pump something and then scavenge it back out of the graveyard.
* [Death's Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Presence)—Now you get double +1/+1 counters; Dreadnought becomes two mana for 24 counters.
* [Wild Beastmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster)—Why put counters on another solitary creature when she can pump them all?
* [Primordial Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra)—24 counters from the Dreadnought? Make that 48 next go—with trample.
* [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll)—Sick of spending all your mana scavenging huge creatures to see them removed? Here, have someone who can't be targeted or killed.

[Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn), and the green half of the bloodrush family, work wonderfully with Varolz just as described. The pump-all [Wild Beastmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) and legendary Ascetic himself, [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll), have obvious uses with +1/+1 counters, but are otherwise serviceable (particularly alongside bloodrush).

The self-doubling power of [Primordial Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra) was another running theme, such as in Alan's suggestions:



* [Hell's Caretaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hell%27s+Caretaker)—For getting back some of those "comes into play" creatures and sacrificing a creature you want to scavenge.
* [Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon)—So that chump blocking isn't an option for your opponent.
* [Death's Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Presence)—Get counters when you sacrifice as well as when you scavenge.
* [Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid)—Scavenge onto him to get more and more land fetch.
* [Primordial Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra)—Put him down cheap, grow him fast with scavenge and counter doubling.
* [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters)—Destruction and sacrifice.

Alan focused in on the idea of using creatures with "when this enters the battlefield" effects ([Viridian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman), [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal), and friends) as a way to build a bigger backbone. [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) plays off the fact we *want* to have extra creatures in the graveyard, and [Hell's Caretaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hell%27s+Caretaker) is a great way to play switcheroo for effects on creatures who have passed on. The *Return to Ravnica* counter-maker [Death's Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Presence) is a way to double up counters that won't startle opponents the way [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) would—at least until you start using it.


The card I like most in Alan's list is [Fertilid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid). Having enough mana to scavenge, or scavenge and go on to do other stuff during the turn, is certainly something to keep in mind. As a rule, I try to include plenty of mana sources—and ways to get them—in my Commander decks.

Going back to [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped), piling the counters up onto one creature is a risky proposition. Some of you, like Mark, looked wider for placing the +1/+1 counters:



* [Cauldron of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cauldron+of+Souls)—Persist is strictly better when +1/+1s are readily available
* [Puppeteer Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer+Clique)—+1/+1 x Persist + steal + targeted graveyard removal = awesomeness
* [Forgotten Ancient](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forgotten+Ancient)—+1/+1 counters for everyone
* [Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine)—Even more +1/+1 counters
* [Deity of Scars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deity+of+Scars)—Beasty and great scavenge value

Honorable Mention: Because I'm a Timmy, [Armor Thrull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armor+Thrull). Knowing the faces people would make for playing it, let alone sacking it for the +1/+2 to scavenge it. Priceless.


[Armor Thrull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armor+Thrull) is cute, and certainly won't scare anyone, but everything else in Mark's list is solid. Since [+1/+1 and -1/-1 counters cancel each other out](/en/articles/archive/ask-wizards-april-2008-2008-04-01), using [Cauldron of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cauldron+of+Souls) and [Puppeteer Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer+Clique) (among any other creatures with persist) can get silly quickly. [Forgotten Ancient](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forgotten+Ancient), the [first creation from You Make the Card](/en/articles/archive/you-make-card-wrapup-2002-12-06), lets you move +1/+1 counters around from it as you see fit when you start your turn, which is ideal when [Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine) is around to proliferate. [Deity of Scars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deity+of+Scars) is a fine fit if you'd prefer to see more [Spiritmonger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiritmonger) and [Ant Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ant+Queen)–type creatures that work well on their own and are dandy to scavenge later.

Tribal always pulls on me harder than any other in Commander, and though Varolz's tribes don't provide much, one is quite potent: Warriors. All six of the cards here have the subtype; the first three are specifically capable of helping said subtype as well. [Reassembling Skeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton) and [Drumhunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drumhunter) are more utility cards that should aid Varolz even without the tribe setting up. [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) mostly has the opportunity to make a ton of Warriors quite suddenly, and allows Varolz through easier. [Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon), though, is the star, playing in both the Tribal angle and the built-in +1/+1 counter theme Varolz brings with scavenge.


When you look at Varolz you can immediately see that he's a Troll. Rob's Warrior angle is a compelling way to edge our fearless leader into combat. [Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) is a free way to grant haste when we bring Varolz to the battlefield, and [Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon) ensures he hits hard on the attack. Everything else is gravy, and that's okay too.

* [Morality Shift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morality+Shift)

Ever since my opponents started tucking my commanders—most of them central pieces of their decks—I started to put alternate win conditions into my decks, or at least ways to get the key components back. My opponents always go for the commander with the most impacting rules text—Varolz basically has a huge target painted on him.


My choice of cards not only protects your Commander, but adds useful tools that under the right circumstances can win the game on their own.


Tucking—putting an opponent's card into his or her library—is a strong way to fight against powerful commanders. Philip's options let him play on despite the potent move by maximizing the idea of a graveyard full of creatures. [Morality Shift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morality+Shift) can dump them all there in one shot, while [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death) and [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End) can put them onto the battlefield instead. [Tombstone Stairwell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tombstone+Stairwell) is one way to leave creatures in the graveyard but get something for putting them there without Varolz around. [Time of Need](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+of+Need) is the foil to tuck effects, and [Eldrazi Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument) will stop all but the most powerful of ways to remove creatures from the battlefield.
