Round 13: Kazuya Mikawa vs. Akira Asahara
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 25, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gphir03/a944.jpg)Akira Asahara is respected both as a player and as one of the masters of Japanese constructed Magic. His decks are always the hottest things around. Today his choice is a little more pedestrian: [Tinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker). Asahara squares off against hometown hero Kazuya Mikawa, resplendent in his flaming red leather jumpsuit. Mikawa is with blue-green madness.

### Game 1

Asahara's draw was sluggish, with just a pair of islands and [Voltaic Key](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Key) to start things off. When he missed his third land drop, Mikawa's draw turned savage. He added a pair of [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel)s to his [Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwalla) and [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter). Before Asahara would play a third land, Mikawa would [Frantic Search](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frantic+Search) into [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm).

Asahara drew island number three. He ignored the [Tinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker) in his hand, going instead for the [Metalworker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker). This meant that Mikawa was free to [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) for [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), discard his hand to the [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel), and pump his [Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwalla) for exactly lethal damage.

Mikawa 1 – Asahara 0

### Game 2

Asahara mulliganed for the second game, then started with [Ancient Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb), [Grim Monolith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Monolith), and then a [Stroke of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stroke+of+Genius) for three on his second turn. Mikawa had just a [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) on the table, so the coast was clear for Asahara. He had [Tinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker) at the ready, but no convenient mana. He made another Monolith and passed it back, locking down Mikawa's green mana with is Port.

Mikawa played [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study), ditching [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) and [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), playing an island before turning it back over to Asahara. He added and island, and risked nothing further. Mikawa Looted up a [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) at end of turn, then untapped and dropped [Spellbane Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbane+Centaur). Asahara took the opportunity of his tapping out to play a [Stroke of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stroke+of+Genius) for four.

Now he was back in control. He powered out [Voltaic Key](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Key), [Thran Dynamo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Dynamo) and then [Tinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker)ed up [Masticore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masticore), shooting down the Looter and adding [Seal of Removal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Removal) to his side. Mikawa tried [Gilded Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake), and Asahara let it happen. He threw the Drake in front of the incoming [Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwalla), and Mikawa was kind enough to tap out and trade.

The door was open. Asahara tapped all his artifacts and lands and played [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). While he worried about maximizing the mana he got from replaying his artifacts, Mikawa was scooping.

Mikawa 1 – Asahara 1

### Game 3

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gphir03/a941.jpg)Mikawa started with [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study), pitching [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) and land, then added [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) to the table. Asahara's line of play was a little bit better. [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), [Voltaic Key](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Key), then [City of Traitors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors), [Grim Monolith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Monolith), [Thran Dynamo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Dynamo), setting up a third turn of [Masticore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masticore) and [Tangle Wire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Wire).

Mikawa took some beats, then escaped for one turn with [Frantic Search](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frantic+Search). All he could muster was an [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm). Asahara had enough mana to assassinate it. He hit again and added another Wire, 'Core-locking himself. Mikawa had a [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) to get rid of it on his next upkeep, but Asahara was free to hit again, bringing him to one. Without a [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) in his graveyard, [Gilded Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake) was no solution. [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) provided a speedbump. Then he passed a turn with seven mana open. Asahara swung, and Mikawa bounced the [Masticore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masticore) with [Rushing River](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rushing+River). He had [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) to stop it for good.

All Asahara had left was [Smokestack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokestack). They entered the extra turns. Asahara's fifteen life was too much for Mikawa to overcome.

**Final Result:** Kazuya Mikawa draws with Akira Asahara 1-1-1
