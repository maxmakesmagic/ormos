A Skitter Pill to Swallow
=========================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on June 17, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Hello, and welcome to another edition of Building on a Budget. I was battling at a local PTQ this weekend and saw a few of you bring [Dating Baiting](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/too-fast-too-furious-2009-06-10), [Transmuter](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/transmute-artifact-2009-03-05), or [Tender Bender](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/love-me-tender-2009-05-06) to play with. At least one of you was in the Top 8. I'm not sure how it worked out, but I want to give a shout out to everyone who went out this past weekend. 


This week I would like to explore the possibility of building a deck around [Necroskitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroskitter). Remember when you first read this card? I do. I remember thinking to myself, "This is either going to be a chase rare or a bulk rare; it could go either way very easily." Time has told its story, and [Necroskitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroskitter) is officially a bulk rare these days. 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab43_skitter.jpg)  
That may change, though. [Incremental Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incremental+Blight) has been seeing quite a bit of successful play these past few weeks. If [Incremental Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incremental+Blight) is good enough to make the cut these days, then [Necroskitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroskitter) has to cut the mustard. I still have nightmares about a Limited game at Nationals where my opponent cast [Incremental Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incremental+Blight) with a [Necroskitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroskitter) in play. 


Let's start with a play set of each of these: 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab43_playset.jpg)  
I went through the other colors to find cards that complement our eight-card deck list: 


**Green:** [Flourishing Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flourishing+Defenses), [Tower Above](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Above)


**Red:** [Puncture Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puncture+Blast), [Puncture Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puncture+Bolt)


**Blue:** [River Kelpie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie), [Leech Bonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leech+Bonder)


**White:** [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God)? 


**Colorless:** [Gnarled Effigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnarled+Effigy), [Grim Poppet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Poppet)


Blue and red both seem appealing, but I think there's a good chance the deck may be best if it only plays black. A version that splashes blue for [Leech Bonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leech+Bonder) and [River Kelpie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kelpie) seems strong. A green version that ramps its mana into a big spell like [Grim Poppet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Poppet) is another route we can take. 


![Snakeform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Snakeform)  
I think the deck needs a blue or green splash. [Chameleon Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) has gained a lot of popularity recently, and a mono-black deck seems like a bad answer to a creature with protection from black. We can, however, play [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform). [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) will result in a lot of stolen Colossuses. It also seems like a fine choice against most other decks in the format.


"[Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) your [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)!" 


"[Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) your [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation)!" 


"[Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) your [Wilt-Leaf Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege)!" 


We'll be more than happy to cast [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) against pretty much every deck in the format. Remember, if you cast [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) on a creature that already has a -1/-1 counter on it, the creature dies. This results in a [Control Magic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Control+Magic) effect if you have a [Necroskitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroskitter) in play. 


I'll throw together a few lists and see what we have to work with.

This is the more controlling version of the deck. Cards like [Dusk Urchins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Urchins) are really good at grinding out card advantage. This list has a lot of removal and would do well against most decks that are being played in today's Standard. The big problem for a deck like this is Faeries. A Faeries player can easily play around most of the cards that give you an edge. There are a few fun tricks that can be used against them. For example, I was playing this version on **Magic Online** this week and I had stolen a [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) using [Soul Snuffers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Snuffers) and a [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform). My opponent tried to [Remove Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) my [Midnight Banshee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Banshee), and I cast [Scar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scar) on a [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite). The [Necroskitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroskitter) triggered, the [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) came into play under my control, and I countered the [Remove Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul). Seems fair.




|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Remove Soul | Spellstutter Sprite |  |

If your local metagame is light on Faeries I strongly suggest sleeving up this version and giving it a try. It does very well against Green-White Tokens, Black-White Tokens, Elves, Red-White Tokens, Kithkin, and most [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) decks.

This version seems like it has the rawest power. A deck like this has the ability to beat just about everything. Unfortunately, we have to make some sacrifices when it comes to our mana base. You may notice that this list is lacking in the [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) department; we already have [Puncture Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puncture+Blast) to deal with [Chameleon Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) in this list, and there just isn't room for [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) pre-sideboard. 


![Slave of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Slave+of+Bolas)  
I played a bunch of tournaments on **Magic Online** with the blue version earlier this week. I'd suggest building a new sideboard depending on your metagame. My sideboard changed between almost every match I played. This is what I was working with: 


4 [Consign to Dream](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consign+to+Dream)  
 3 [Slave of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slave+of+Bolas)  
 4 [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark)  
 4 [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror)


[Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) decks have been gaining popularity, and [Slave of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slave+of+Bolas) is a really good tool if you want to beat those people. When you [Slave of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slave+of+Bolas) a [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)—"That's game, boys." 


[Consign to Dream](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consign+to+Dream) is a great tool against green decks. Most people will side in their [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer)s with plans to equip it onto a [Chameleon Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus). After board we have [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) and [Consign to Dream](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consign+to+Dream). We'll get a lot of tempo advantage here—seems great. 


[Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) is an obvious inclusion against White Weenie and Elves. 


Our deck has a lot of trouble with [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique). We need a way to kill [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), and [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) does that.
