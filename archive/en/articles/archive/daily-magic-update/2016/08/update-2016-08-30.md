The August 30, 2016 Update
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on August 30, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on August 26, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by Shuhei Nakamura and Eric Froehlich going toe-to-toe—or maybe keyboard-to-keyboard—in tonight's Vintage Super League finals.


Today's Must Watch
==================

Tonight's the night—the culmination of a long season of Vintage action. The final match pits Shuhei "[Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids)" Nakamura against Eric Froehlich to crown a season champion. Everything starts at 5 p.m. PT/8 p.m. ET on [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic).

Something old and something new, as one archetype of the past returns in Modern, and a rotating monster from Standard finds new life in Modern.


1. [Draft Leagues Are Great](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/draft-leagues-are-great/) | ChannelFireball | Matt Sperling

I mean, they are. Or will be. But the real key here is that Sperling discusses why cross-pod pairings aren't actually the worst and how the effect of this is largely overblown.


1. [Podcast 084: Modern Triple GPs, Standard Rotation](https://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/podcast-084-modern-triple-gps-standard-rotation) | MTGGoldfish | Chas V, Richard, and SaffronOlive

I'm highlighting this because no one else I've seen (or heard) has started talking about rotation much yet. Typically, in this period right before rotation but also before previews start, we get a number of articles about cards that are rotating out and what decks will be dying and what can survive. Granted, there's still plenty of time for that, but it's interesting that this is the first discussion of it I've really found. Maybe we're not yet used to the new rotation. Maybe *Conspiracy: Take the Crown* is crowding out some of that discussion. Maybe we're all just ready to see [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) go and nothing else matters. Either way, get a taste of what rotation will bring with the MTGGoldfish crew.


1. [How Would You Change the Color Pie? Q&A](https://rocketjump.com/listen/change-color-pie-qa-120) | The Command Zone | Jimmy Wong and Josh Lee Kwai

I would move burn from red to *absolutely nowhere*. [Flame Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Slash) can stay. What about you?


1. [Tales from the Fringe](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/alexullman-08302016-tales-from-the-fringe/) | Gathering Magic | Alex Ullman

An article in which Alex Ullman writes about Pauper. Here, he examines the fringes of the successful Pauper decks, and finds some hidden gems.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. Modern Azorius Control at Grand Prix Indianapolis

Still no [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)s, but there are several copies of the highly underrated [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) in this White-Blue Control list in Modern. In a format where Naya Burn can win on turn three and [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) Aggro can, once in a blue moon, win on turn two, you have to respect a deck playing [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) in the main deck.


In this case, Brian Cooper took his Azorius Control deck to 19th place and a 12-3 record in Indianapolis last weekend, but you can see the hoops he jumps through to attack the format right now. [Sunlance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunlance) is a very underrated removal spell at the moment for control decks (which are few and far between) that utilize white but not red (which virtually don't exist), and [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) is really strong in a format full of two-casting-cost spells that matter.


It's the planeswalker suite that really stands out, plus a few sideboard cards worth highlighting. [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) has been a popular choice for a planeswalker in Standard, but this is the first we're seeing it make a dent in Modern. If the ever-popular and powerful [Elspeth, Knight-Errant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant) isn't seeing play, then Gideon certainly wasn't on anyone's radar. But the deck does need a way to finish off opponents. [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion) can also fill that role, though there are only so many six drops you can play in Modern (not counting cards with delve).


Speaking of six drops, [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) is one that pops up in Modern from time-to-time. This time it's bringing back [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren)s, and the occasional [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter). If the deck is going to have a finisher, one that brings with it incremental advantage seems like a strong way to go when gaining control of a game and then keeping it can be tough.


But it's the sideboard that really shines. [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) has all kinds of applications—from burn to [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)—and [Rest for the Weary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+for+the+Weary) is worth its weight in life total against decks that pressure you from that vector. And costing only two mana means it can actually show up in time to help against the hyper-aggressive decks. [Stony Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence) and [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) are obvious inclusions, while [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) is a fine option when you just need to nuke a single card while keeping your own [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s doing their thing.


Oh, and [Bribery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bribery)? Anyone trying to get cheeky with [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) is going to have a bad time.
