Oathing up a Storm
==================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 18, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






When nearly the entire card pool of the history of *Magic: The Gathering* is at your disposal, truly incredible things are likely to happen. DailyMTG's own Luis Scott-Vargas is not only a Hall of Fame professional player, but he is [a Vintage enthusiast](/articles/archive/developing-your-griselbrand-2014-07-11) as well! Recently, he managed to 4–0 a Daily Event with a deck that is off the charts in terms of power level.



![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Oath%20of%20Druids)

Luis starts with the [Forbidden Orchard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Orchard) + [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) combo. Against creature decks, let's call it the [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) +two mana "combo." Turn one, any-color Mox + [Forbidden Orchard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Orchard) + [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) is an incredibly start, and fairly common at that. One resolved trigger of the Oath will bring out [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), as there are no other creatures in the deck. Because there are only two creatures in the deck, the graveyard is likely to be fairly full. This works well because [Yawgmoth's Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Will) allows Luis to bring back most of the graveyard, ending in a [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) combo finish.



But why is Yawgmoth's Will in the sideboard then if it's so good? One of the clever things about this deck is using [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) to approximate four copies of Yawgmoth's Will instead of one. By keeping the Yawgmoth's Will in the sideboard, this deck can use [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) not only to get access to Yawgmoth's Will more frequently, but you can also use [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) to hide your [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) in your sideboard. The beauty of this deck is that it doesn't need to Oath into [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) to win its games—there are draw-sevens such as [Windfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windfall), [Wheel of Fortune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fortune), and [Timetwister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timetwister) to refuel your hand even without [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand). These are more than capable of generating a storm kill by themselves.
