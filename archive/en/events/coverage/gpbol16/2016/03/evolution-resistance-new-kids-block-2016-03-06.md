*Leszek Luty*


It has become common knowledge that [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) is a pretty good foil to the lumbering Eldrazi. The artifact is commonly seen in Lantern Control or in the sideboard of Affinity, but Leszek Luty exploited it in another strategy: 8-Rack, the deck that overloads on discard spells and ultimately wins with [The Rack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) or [Shrieking Affliction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrieking+Affliction).


"I figured that with the Eldrazi supposedly everywhere, this deck with [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) and discard for their [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) could work," he told me. "I like it over Lantern Control because I have experience with it, which I think is one of the most important things going into a GP, and it has more angles of attack with creatures and planeswalkers."


When asked about his record against Eldrazi decks, he answered that he had played against two: one red-green and one white-blue. "The red-green deck didn't care much about my Bridge due to [World Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=World+Breaker), but against the white-blue deck, I played a turn-three [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge), and my opponent just scooped!"

*Nils Gutierrez Von Porat*


Enchantments are a weak point of the Eldrazi decks, as they typically have no way to remove them. Creatures are not that big of an issue thanks to [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return), and/or [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), but enchantments are a more problematic card type. Nils Gutierrez Von Porat capitalized on that with a combo deck featuring not only [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) but also [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy). Cards like [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) fuel the enchantments, and the deck eventually wins by attacking with a huge [Fatestitcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatestitcher).


"Before the Eldrazi, I was testing this deck together with Danan Morera, who is also in Day 2," he told me. "We liked the strategy of mixing [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) and [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), and it worked really well against Abzan Company and control decks. I personally first played more of a combo deck with 4 [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) instead of 4 [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), but I eventually made the switch to [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)."


"Then the colorless Eldrazi came out," he continued. "They played 4 [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void), so I switched to a different deck. But then, when we saw that Chalice was going to be a lot less played and the blue-white Eldrazi version was going to be more played, we went back to our deck. I think it's 50-50 against Eldrazi---we bring in the token strategy with [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) and [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) for game 2, which won't be 1/1s thanks to [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy)---but I think this deck was mainly a good choice for this tournament because there was quite a lot of Abzan Company, a good matchup, and not a lot of Burn, the bad matchup for our deck."







#### Jacob Sorensen – Living End Moon – Grand Prix Bologna 2016

*Jacob Sorensen*


[Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End) is a good way to sweep a board infested by a bunch of Eldrazi creatures, no matter how big they are. While Jacob Sorensen isn't the only player on [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End) today, he has the relatively unique addition of [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) to his main deck. Especially with [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide), he can shut down [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) and [Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) before they enable a discounted [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) or [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher).


"I tested a lot against blue-white Eldrazi, and the turn-2 [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) was lights out for them." Sorensen did have to figure out the challenge of how to not get locked under his own [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon), but the landcyclers offered the solution to that conundrum. "[Twisted Abomination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Abomination) and [Valley Rannet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valley+Rannet) are better than [Pale Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pale+Recluse) because you can actually hard-cast them, and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) is not played much these days so three-toughness bodies are fine."


I had one final question for him: why the snow-covered basic lands? "Because they're pretty!" he answered. "And it even prompted one of my opponents to make a wrong choice: he thought I was on [Skred](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skred) Red, so he put [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) on one rather than zero!"
