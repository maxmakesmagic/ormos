Deck Tech: Atarka Red with Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa
===================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 17, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 





Hall of Famer and eighteenth-ranked Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa has been regarded as one of the best control players in the game for the last decade. That's why it came as such a surprise to everyone that he chose to battle with a hyper aggressive strategy like Atarka Red this weekend. Damo da Rosa just clinched a seat in his tenth Top 8 here at Pro Tour *Battle for Zendikar*, becoming the third player ever to Top 8 ten separate times at the big show. I decided to sit down with Damo da Rosa in hopes of learning why he chose to be on the other side of the aggro/control coin for this event.


When I asked Damo da Rosa what could make a control master like himself sleeve up red cards, he just shrugged. "Well, I guess the deck is just very good," he admitted. "I tried testing it against all the other decks in the format and I felt like nothing could consistently beat it. This deck is different than other red decks because it's very difficult to play against. The [Become Immense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Become+Immense) combo with [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage) forces opponents to not attack and it often gives us two or three extra turns to draw the cards we need to finish them off. Sometimes it's a mistake to give us extra draw steps. Other times, they attack and I just kill them. It's more than a red deck because of all the angles of attack."


We've already seen a lot of Atarka Red in the new Standard, but I wanted to know what differentiated Damo da Rosa's deck from the other red decks we've been seeing recently. "The other red strategies are usually playing [Thunderbreak Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderbreak+Regent) and [Outpost Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege), but I didn't want to play with clunky four mana cards," he explained. "We don't want to tap out against the mirror because we usually just die to [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage). The controlling decks of the format seem to turn the corner fast enough that a card like [Outpost Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege) is rarely more than a [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination). Our version of the deck doesn't bother with the four-drops and I feel like that's been a big advantage."
