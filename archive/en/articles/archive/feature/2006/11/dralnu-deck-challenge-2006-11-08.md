The Dralnu Deck Challenge
=========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 8, 2006 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar











![Dralnu Lich Lord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dralnu+Lich+Lord)
Welcome to the Season Finale of the Dralnu Deck Challenge! For those of you who are just tuning in, allow me to explain what's going on. Two weeks ago, I announced that the WizOs were going to be hosting a deckbuilding challenge and they wanted me be the final judge. I ran a poll featuring a number of fine Johnny cards, and you, the readers, selected [Dralnu, Lich Lord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dralnu%2C+Lich+Lord). Many of you built decks around the *Time Spiral* legend, and the WizOs ran another poll to determine which of the decks were your favourites. You voted! After narrowing the field down to the top eleven (or each person that received seven or more votes), I made another round of cuts, down to six.
 Without further ado, let's see what the finalists came up with! Each entry will be followed by comments the deck's designer (in black), by me (in, say, blue), and by the ever-crusty Gleemax, of Brain in a Jar fame (in plum).

**mattginsberg:** "As I'm sure many people have discovered, it's tough to play around Dralnu's permanent-sacrificing drawback. So why not let your opponent have to deal with it? This deck works by giving your opponent the Lich Lord, via [Donate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate) or a [Spawnbroker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnbroker), and then goes one-sided [Apocalypse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apocalypse) on them with a big-ol' [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball). [Spawnbroker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnbroker)s are sure to find a target thanks to the [Forbidden Orchard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Orchard)s, but just in case you have to hold on to Dralnu for a couple extra turns, [Hatching Plans](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) makes for a juicy sacrifice target. The deck also managed to find space for a fun little [Reweave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reweave) engine, turning a Signet into a Colossus or a [Hatching Plans](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) into a [Form of the Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon) plus three cards! As well, [Drift of Phantasms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drift+of+Phantasms) buys us some time early and helps us finish with a toolbox of three-mana spells."


![Donate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Donate)
**Chris:** That's one way to neutralize Dralnu's drawback! He's your problem now, sucker! As they say, it's all fun and games until someone's replacement ability causes you to lose all of your permanents. I like how you can either donate the Lich Lord and blast him with burn (causing severe perm-loss), but if that doesn't work you can just use him yourself to send a seemingly endless amount of direct damage at your opponent's head and/or nugget and/or dome.
 The inclusion of [Tinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker) and [Darksteel Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus) loses mattginsberg some points on my heretofore unknown judging criterion, the "purity of concept." While having random "I win" combos in your deck may fly with the leather-jacket wearing, motorcycle-riding tournament crowd, I feel like it takes the focus away from the task at hand, namely showing off what [Dralnu, Lich Lord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dralnu%2C+Lich+Lord) can do. In a similar vein, while you'll find no bigger fan of [Reweave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reweave) than me, I don't know if it really has a place in this deck as it stands. With nothing to splice it on to, you will end up having to pay full price for your 'weaves, something that won't make you too happy when you pay six to turn your [Rakdos Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Signet) into another [Rakdos Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Signet). I might spend those few slots on cards that will help you survive in the early game, like [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp) or [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak).


All in all, though, I think mattginsberg did a very nice job.


**Sadistic Gleemax**: Giving Dralnu to your opponent? Matt's almost as mean as I am. I suspect that he spent a considerable part of his youth stuck in a jar.

**rureddy31:** "As you can see, I designed a B/G/u/w Rock-style deck to maximize the card advantage generated by [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) as well as Dralnu. The goal of this deck is to get a Dralnu in play against decks that won't burn him out (luckily, very few decks in Extended run much burn). Once he is in play, the card advantage will roll in. [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) is completely broken with Dralnu. Whatever you need, Gifts can find it. Gifts no longer reads 'Search for four, get two,' but 'Search for four, get all four.' Whatever you need to wreck your opponent, you get. [Zombify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify) is also good in some situations where you want to Gifts for creatures. [Genesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis) also fits into this mould. All in all, this is a strong, competitive deck in Extended. Dralnu truly is a Rock Star."


**Chris:** rureddy31 gets maximum points for submitting a deck that looks like it has a lot of game. [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) seems like the perfect card to pair with Dralnu. You're damned if you do, and damned if you don't. It reminds me of Vintage decks that allow you to put [Recoup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup) in your Gifts piles. Therein lies the problem. We've seen decks like this before, so I've got to deduct some creativity points (I don't know, how about five of them?). [Loxodon Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch) and [Pernicious Deed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed) are fine cards, to be sure, but I would like to see them replaced with instants or sorceries so that Dralnu is more relevant to winning the game (maybe [Crime // Punishment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crime+%2F%2F+Punishment) and [Ribbons of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ribbons+of+Night)?).


**Sadistic Gleemax:** At least it wasn't Dralnu-finity.

**Chris:** Similar to mattginsberg, cultist cooljeanius seeks to give Dralnu to the opponent before damaging him directly. Cooljeanius doesn't splash red for burn spells, instead using black's life-draining spells to accomplish the same goals. Limiting the deck to two colours makes it much easier to get the mana you need when you need it. Lim-Dul seems like a fine tag-team partner for Dralnu, whether you "donate" the Lich Lord or you just him to recur your removal spells. The one problem I see is that if you send Dralnu to your opponent and then ping him, your opponent will just sacrifice Dralnu himself. While it's not the worst result in the world (you get a Spawnbrokered creature, your opponent gets nothing), it's not very efficient. I'd rather see more copies of [Ribbons of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ribbons+of+Night) and [Psionic Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Blast) in the deck. Still, the deck seems fun and very playable.

The goal of the deck is to use Dralnu and his crusade in combination to make massive amounts of goblins, turn them into zombies, and beef them up with the [Undead Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Warchief).
