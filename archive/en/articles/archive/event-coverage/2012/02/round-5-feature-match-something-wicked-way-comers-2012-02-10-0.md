Round 5 Feature Match: Something Wicked This Way Comers
=======================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 10, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_SteveSadin.jpg)
By Steve Sadin












[Matthew Costa (White-Blue Delver) vs. Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa (Wolf Run Ramp)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_4)


Andre Mueller (Grand Summoning) vs. Adam Yurchick (White-Blue Delver)
---------------------------------------------------------------------


[Carrie Oliver vs. Ricky Sidher (Draft)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_6)


Two time Pro Tour Top 8 competitor and resurgent pro Andre Mueller sat across the table from Adam Yurchick, a strikingly consistent American Pro who has flown a bit under the radar since his win at Grand Prix Houston in 2010.


Going into the fifth round, both players found themselves at 3-1 piloting blue decks that have little in common with each other beyond the fact that they both contain basic [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s.


Yurchick is playing a fairly traditional White-Blue Delver deck, while Mueller is playing a deck that has affectionately come to be known as Something Wicked this Way Comers, in honor of the deck's designer, Hall of Famer Alan Comer.


Mueller's deck uses [Grand Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) and [Heartless Summoning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) to help power out quick copies of [Myr Superion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion), [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), and other assorted artifacts (like [Contagion Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine), [Steel Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Hellkite), and – wait for it – [Geistcatcher's Rig](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistcatcher%27s+Rig)) that he can tutor up with [Treasure Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r5_mueller_vs_yurchick.jpg)
*Both Andre Mueller and Adam Yurchick have a habit of flying under the radar to successful finishes.* 

### Game 1


Mueller won the roll, but had to mulligan while Yurchick kept his opening seven. Both players started off the game with [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s to help craft their hands, but it was Mueller who made the first proactive play of the game.


"I guess it's [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) time," said Mueller as he attempted a [Grand Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) into Yurchick's two untapped lands.


"No [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s here, unfortunately," said Yurchick.


"Aww sweet!" said Mueller, as he immediately used his [Grand Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) to power out a [Myr Superion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion).


Yurchick [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)ed in search of something that could get him back into the game, but all that he could find was a Delver of Secrets.


While Yurchick had to be content with casting a 1/1, Mueller further built up his board with a [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine).


Yurchick had a couple of [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s to get additional uses out of his [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s, but he couldn't find anything of use. He quickly found himself down a game to Mueller's explosive Blue-Black deck.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r5_mueller.jpg)
*Andre Mueller's unanswered [Grand Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) leads to a quick first game.* 

Andre Mueller 1, Adam Yurchick 0


Yurchick: "Your deck seems really cool."


Mueller: "Alan Comer played this deck at Worlds, and he went 2-3-1 with it. I don't know why he didn't do better, the deck seems so good!"


### Game 2


Yurchick got off to a better start in the second game with a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to counter Mueller's turn two Heartless Summoning, and a Geist of Saint Traft to put Mueller under some serious pressure.


Yurchick's lead wouldn't last for long as Mueller exploded on his third turn with a Heartless Summoning, a [Myr Superion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion), and a [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to legend rule away Yurchick's Geist of Saint Traft.


Yurchick tried to get back into the game with a [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) copying [Myr Superion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion), but Mueller had a [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) to copy his own [Myr Superion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion), and a [Grand Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) to deal with his opponent's [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image).



![Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grand+Architect)![Myr Superion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Myr+Superion)


![Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phantasmal+Image)

*This doesn't come up every day, but if you copy an artifact with Phantasmal Image, it won't live long if your opponent has a Grand Architect.*
Even though Mueller had gotten off to a fantastic start, Yurchick was able to turn around and demonstrate why Delver decks are just so prevalent in Standard right now.


A couple of [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s, a [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike), a well timed [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), and a [Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence) later, and suddenly Mueller found himself in need of a strong card to pull back into the game.


[Contagion Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine) seemed to be just that card, but Yurchick's third [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), flashing back [Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence), again put the American Pro in the driver's seat.


But the hits just kept coming for Mueller, as he was able to stick a [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) that not only forced Yurchick to chump block it every turn, but also gave him a huge cushion in the race against Yurchick's [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) tokens.


It looked like Mueller had the match all but locked up when Yurchick found a second [Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence) and a fourth [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) (flashing back the [Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence)) to again claw his way to a lead.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r5_yurchick.jpg)
*Adam Yurchick keeps his head in the game, as [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence) keep him on even footing against Mueller's monsters.* 

Mueller's deck continued to cough up good threats, as he found another [Myr Superion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion) that traded with Yurchick's entire army of spirit tokens.


After spending much of the game throwing haymakers back and forth at one another, the two players found themselves with next to nothing left in the tank, allowing Mueller to ultimately scratch his way to victory by attacking with a couple of [Grand Architect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect)s.


Andre Mueller 2, Adam Yurchick 0
