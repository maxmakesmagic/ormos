======================

TOP 8 Coverage



Ever since the entrance of *Shadows over Innistrad*, Standard has been more vibrant than ever. With numerous archetypes that are constantly evolving, all eyes were on Tokyo (and New York) in anticipation for a wave of new surprises.


3,335 combatants showed up in the first Grand Prix Tokyo in 19 years, making it the largest Standard Grand Prix in the history of Japan. 957 made it through to Day 2, and six rounds later, only 8 remain. The survivors are Carlos Ballester, Takaya Saito, Kensuke Kato, Eng Chu Heng, Takuma Morofuji, Kazuki Yada, Kazushige Suzuki and our champion Riku Kumagai.


Most of the players in the Top 8 are first-timers, and indication that anyone can shine on the big stage if they bring along a great deck, play well and run good. Perhaps the most amazing story of all is how Eng Chu Heng from Singapore steamrolled his way to a 13-0 finish to become the first player to secure his Top 8 berth.


In particular, Japanese National Champion 2005 and Grand Prix Kobe 2015 Champion, Takuma Morofuji, was the most experienced of the lot. Competing in his second Grand Prix Top 8 and going for a second title, he was most unfortunately waylaid by Kazushige Suzuki in the semifinals. Advancing to the finals with his trusty Grixis Control deck, Suzuki met his match when Kumagai overpowered him with a myriad of threats that even the almighty control deck couldn't answer.


As the ancient *Magic* adage goes, "there are no wrong threats, only wrong answers"!


That couldn't be more apt when Kumagai secured his victory with a Planeswalker (Gideon, Ally of Zendikar), a creature which transforms into a Planeswalker (Nissa, Vastwood Seer) and a land that becomes a creature (Needle Spires). True enough, Suzuki stumbled and Kumagai emerged victorious in three close games.


The others should not be too disappointed either, since everyone wins complimentary airfare and coveted invites to Pro Tour *Eldritch Moon*, which is scheduled to take place in Sydney this summer (or in Australia's case, winter!). The eight have demonstrated that they know a thing or two about the new Standard format, and should be ready to display their prowess on a larger arena.


Congratulations once again to Riku Kumagai! Your Naya midrange deck will likely shake up and shape up the Standard metagame for the upcoming weeks and months!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Yesterday, a total of 3335 players converged at Big Sight, one of the biggest tournament sites in Tokyo. After 9 rounds of intense competition, “only 957” have made it past Day 1. Nine players have the perfect 9-0 record, while five others are also technically undefeated at 8-0-1. 


Key players to watch include two-time Player of the Year (21) Yuuya Watanabe, three-time Pro Tour Top 8 competitor (24) Kentaro Yamamoto, Hall of Famers Shuhei Nakamura, Shota Yasooka and Kenji Tsumura, as well as World *Magic* Cup Champion Tzu Ching Kuo.


Several other Grand Prix mainstays are on their heels, such as Yuuki Ichikawa, Yuta Takahashi, Lee Shi Tian, Jason Chung, Huang Hao Shan, Raymond Tan, Park Jun Young among others.


Just six more Rounds to go before the Top 8 of Japan's largest Standard Grand Prix is forged, and when the dust settles, only one can call himself (or herself) the Grand Prix Tokyo Champion!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



Tokyo Big Sight is quite literally a big site! A total of 3335 players converged this morning to battle it out in the new world of Standard. This is a rather memorable weekend for us all, since we've not had a Grand Prix hosted in Tokyo for nearly two decades. To make things even merrier, this overwhelming turnout makes it the largest Standard tournament in Japan to date.


After 9 rounds of competition, the massive field has been reduced down significantly, but only nine players emerged unscathed. Shawn McNeace, who made the Top 8 at Grand Prix Nagoya earlier this year leads the standings, followed by Hiroaki Nagai, Hiroyuki Gotou and Takaya Saito.


Two-time Player of the Year and seven-time Grand Prix Champion, Yuuta Watanabe, was also one of the nine 9-0 players, along with Kazuo Washimi, Koji Maruyama, Eng Chu Heng and Tadayuki Kumazawa.


Five other players are also technically undefeated, with 8-0-1 records. Other key players to watch include Raymond Tan, Yuuki Ichikawa, Huang Hao Shan, Shuhei Nakamura and Kentaro Yamamoto, all at a respectable 8-1 record! And that's just a few names from among the hundreds whom are going to be returning tomorrow to battle for the champion trophy.


With Standard being more diverse than ever, a slew of new brews have arisen If you're still itching for more Standard action, stay tuned as we swing over to the other side of the planet. Grand Prix New York will be equally monumental and pairings for Round 1 just went up as we're winding down in Tokyo!
