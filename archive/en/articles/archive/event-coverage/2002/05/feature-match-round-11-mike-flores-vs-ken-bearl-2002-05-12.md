Feature Match Round 11 - Mike Flores vs. Ken Bearl
==================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 12, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_633.png)
By Zev Gurwitz












![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/913.jpg)Friendly banter abounded as Ken and Mike sat down across from one another to begin the home stretch towards the top 8. Bearl, who received his first lost last round was playing [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), while Flores had a Madness [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition) deck teaming with monsters. They rolled a die, Ken won, and they started the game at a sign from head judge James Lee.


Going first can be very important in the Red Blue Green on [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) match up, and this was amazingly obvious as Ken started off with a turn two [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) while Flores's board was clear. Flores wasted no time breaking the blue enchantment, summoning a savage bastard, the [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). Ken had no turn three play, and was forced to discard a land, passing the turn back to Flores.


Mike went nuts on his turn, attacking with the Mongrel and pitching an [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), and paid the madness cost. The wurm was countered with [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) from Ken, so Flores took the initiative by discarding two copies of [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), pumping the Hound up to 5/5 and taking a 1/4th of his opponent's life total on his first attack. Ken had nothing to do on his turn but to play another land, giving Flores priority.


Flores opened up his turn by casting [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) from the graveyard, Ken had a another [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) ready, but Flores pumped his Mongrel with a logic of his own, falling right into Bearl's trump card, [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt). Not wasting the rest of the turn, Flores attacked with his pumped Mongrel, bringing Ken down to 12.


Ken once again did a spellbinding nothing on his turn, giving the turn back to Mike. Flores once again led with [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), only to have it countered once again by [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). The Mongrel got in one more smack before being [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst)ed back to Flores's hand at the end of his turn.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/914.jpg)Ken actually cast a spell on his turn this time, summoning the [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar). Flores recast the Mongrel, Ken countered with, well, [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), which left Flores open to drop a [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter). The looter was [Repulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)d before it became active, and Ken tapped out to cast an end of turn [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Flores took that opportunity to Fire the Familiar, but couldn't stop FOF from resolving, and gave [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) and [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) to Bearl while a [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) and two land went to the discard pile.


Ken took advantage of Flores being tapped out by casting a main phase [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), and picked up a [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) and a [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), the played a land, passing the turn. On Flores's turn, an [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition) tried to come into play, but a [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) sent the enchantment to the bin. For some reason, the [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), Ken's, ended up underneath the [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition), in Flores's yard. This managed to work itself out without anyone ending up on the DCI watch list, and the game continued with Ken resolving a [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) on his next turn.


Flores tried once again to play an [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition), this time the spell resolved, despite Ken having cast [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) in response. The FOF put a Psychatog, [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) and land into Ken's hand, sending the [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) to the graveyard with a land to keep it company.


On Ken's turn, the Tog was tapped by the Looter, and another [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) came down from Ken's hand, sending everything back, as Flores scooped.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/915.jpg)Ken showed me his hand at the beginning of game two. Three [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)s and a [Ghastly Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise), admittedly not what he wanted to draw. Flores started off the game with a turn two Mongrel, turn three Mongrel, counter Ken's [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) with [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) after having discarded a land. Flores followed up with a turn four [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition), while all Ken could play was a [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar).


Flores came in with the Mongrels despite [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition), and cast [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay) when his opponent tried to play [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), Ken cast a [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay) for one mana thanks too the Familiar, but Flores had another [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) waiting. This left Mike with no cards in hand, and one of his [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel)s to a [Ghastly Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise).


Mike managed to get in another attack, by tapping the Familiar at the end of his opponents turn, and swinging, which took Ken down to 4. On his turn, Ken tapped out and cast [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), Flores answered with [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay). On his next turn, Ken tapped out for [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), Flores again answered with [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay). Flores played a looter, tapped it to take out the Familiar, and swung, bring Ken to 2.


"Bet you don't have the other [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)" taunted Flores, who could only frown and Ken tapped out again to cast [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). All Flores could say was "But I could be wrong."


Ken Bearl wins 2-0
