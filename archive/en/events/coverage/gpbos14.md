TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------


Once again, Modern Constructed spurred a monster Grand Prix turnout. Worcester's DCU Convention Center was filled almost to bursting with 2,394 mages from all across the globe. There was even a strong Pro presence as the Grand Prix made an ideal stopover for those en route to Portland for Pro Tour *Magic 2015*. Europe's best outnumbered their American counterparts, who were enjoying the luxury of another week in the lab. With Pro Points to be won and so many of the World *Magic* Cup captaincies being undecided there was ample incentive to take a weekend off. Modern is known as a format where anything goes - a wide open metagame means that you'll get your best edge on the opposition by knowing your deck inside and out, and having a versatile sideboard that can tangle with a diverse field.


That diversity showed itself in Day 2, with dozens of different archetypes being played in the field of 278, fully seven of which boasted over twenty pilots. The tentpoles of the format were out in force: Splinter Twin, Affinity, Birthing Pod, and the Black-Green-based decks. Where once Jund had reigned supreme, it was being edged out by a new flavor, dropping the red for the power of Lingering Souls. There were also big numbers of Blue-White-Red, last seen in the hands of World Champion Shahar Shenhar, as well as Scapeshift Combo and Burn.


However, where Pod had been the biggest player in Day 2, it had proven a victim of its own popularity. Players had come prepared to beat it, and not a single Pod deck made the Top 8. Instead we saw two of these new White Rock decks, two Affinity, one Jund, and then a Blue Moon and a pair of Infect Decks. That's two out of only four competing on Day 2! Pro Tour *Theros* Finalist Pierre Dagen Was the Top 8's biggest star, but Slovenia's Robin Dolar had quietly notched his third GP Top 8 in three attempts, and he had a lot of big names rooting for him from the sidelines.


The quarters saw the full three mirror-matches, with fan-favorite Phil Napoli winning for Infect and Dolar winning for White Rock. They met in the semifinals, where Dolar crushed him in straight games. Alex Bertoncini advanced from the Affinity mirror, where he met Andrew Boswell. Boswell triumphed. So it was a Black-Green finals, with the old fighting the new. It went the full three games, and in a nailbiting finish Robin Dolar emerged victorious.


Congratulations to Robin Dolar, Grand Prix Boston-Worcester 2014 Champion!

DAY 2 COVERAGE
--------------


As a gray dawn breaks over the city of Worcester, 278 mages have returned to the DCU Convention Center to finish what they started. The stakes are high, and with a field this big, nobody has room for error. It's a mix of big names making a stop en route to the Pro Tour in Portland next weekend, northeast Grand Prix fixtures, and amateurs hungry for that breakout performance. Keep eyes on the six of the Top 25 Ranked Players who managed to make it across the Day 2 cutoff: Jeremy Dezani (2), Stanislav Cifka (3), Alex Hayne (16), Josh McClain (18), Jacob Wilson (19), and Raphael Levy (24). Europe is making its presence known, as the top of the standings is littered with names like Pierre Dagen, Timothee Simonot, Andrea Mengucci, Guillaume Wafo-Tapa and Samuele Estratti.


Modern proves to be as diverse a format as ever. We'll be bringing you a full breakdown of the Day 2 decks, but even a cursory glance at the top tables reveals a wealth of options beyond the Big Four of Twin, Pod, Affinity and Rock. 


Just six rounds stand between these players and Top 8 glory. Who of the eight 9-0 players will see their run continue? Will a long-forgotten deck resurface and steal the trophy? Will the visiting pros crush the competition, or will a hometown hero win it for the Good Ol'? Stay tuned to our live coverage to see all the action!

DAY 1 COVERAGE
--------------


Nine rounds of competition have done their cruel work, and where once there were over twenty-four hundred players, now there stand just 278. Leading the charger are the eight who managed perfect 9-0 records: Wesley Hovanec, Charlie Rinehart, Nathan Holiday, Ross Merriam, King of the Hill Timothee Simonot, Denys Miasoutov, Sebastian Villeneuve, and Samuele Estratti. They won't have any time to rest on their laurels, however. With a field this large and just six rounds between tonight and the cut to Top 8, they'll need to bring all their experience and preparation to bear.


It's a tough field, too. Six of the Top 25 Ranked Players are still in the hunt: Jeremy Dezani (2), Stanislav Cifka (3), Alex Hayne (16), Josh McClain (18), Jacob Wilson (19), and Raphael Levy (24). Guillaume Wafo-Tapa is technically undefeated after picking up his signature draw for the day, and the 8-1's include names like Pierre Dagen and Andrea Mengucci.


Modern proves to be as diverse a format as ever. In the last round, a tour of the top tables revealed nineteen different archetypes across just fifty-four players. Even if you tossed out the nine singletons as lucky outliers and lumped all the black-green-based decks together, you were still looking at seven different decks. There were the Big 4 of Rock, Twin, Affinity and Pod, of course, but there were also surprising numbers of Scapeshift, Urzatron, and Red-White-Blue Control. And that was just a sampling. We'll have a clearer picture of what decks were winning once we've crunched the numbers on the Day 2 Metagame.


Our coverage begins at 9am tomorrow. Will anyone put up a 15-0 sweep of the swiss? Who will make gains in the player rankings? Will an established deck bulldoze the field or will something out of left field steal the show? Tune in to find out!
