Krzikalla Goes Fishing For The Title
====================================












 



2008 Germany National Championship
-----------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat08/day3_blurb2.jpg)*Olaf Krzikalla lured enough fish to the surface to drown everyone else in a scaly heap.* Merfolk! The little blue men carried Olaf Krzikalla to a German National Championship. Against all expectations, his own as well as those of the metagame experts, the deck performed exceptionally here. Strong draft performance marked the second leg of Olaf’s journey. He heads a National team that carries some prowess. Former PT top 8 finisher Fabio Reinhardt has risen magnificiently to the occasion of finally playing some Magic again, as has Harald Stein for whom the top 4 finish at Nationals marked the end of long months without magical cards.


As an event, Nationals was a big drawcard for players from all over the country. Almost 150 Legacy players, more than 70 teams in the Two-Headed Giant champs, and two PTQs with more than 100 players each framed the main event. For the German pro players, though, the pro points they stood to gain here did not materialize. Only Jan Ruess fought back from a 2-4-1 start on day one to go 7-0 on day two, finishing in 15th place to earn himself one pro point.


With Nationals over and done with, the next event players especially here in Germany are looking forward to is Pro Tour: Berlin. From October 31st to November 2nd, pros from all over the world will converge in Berlin to find an Extended master. Before that, everybody has the chance to show his magical prowess on the Grand Prix circuit, in Rimini and Paris. Join us here at the new magicthegathering.com to read, hear and watch all about how you too can play the game and see the world!

log: Sunday, August 31st, 9:45 a.m. -- A Series Of Tubes
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat08/20_1.jpg)*Tobi Henke and Sebastian Abresch hard at work.*The internet is a series of tubes, we all know that one. Unfortunately, these tubes are sometimes blocked, obstructed, or simply vanish. This was precisely what happened yesterday night during round 13. It is the nightmare of every coverage reporter, and yet it keeps happening at almost every event.


Coverage reporters need two things: electricity and internet. (We nourish from ions.) If either one breaks down, so does the reporter. Then the big hunt for internet started. In the hotel, we unplugged the free internet kiosk in the lobby to plug our laptops in. In our rooms, we tried to connect via the phone lines. In the end, a phone call pointed us to a bar across town that had free wireless, right next to the Invasion bar.


Tobias Henke and Sebastian Abresch from PlanetMTG and myself grabbed a cab and chased the internet. When we arrived, it also came with refreshments. Luckily, the problem is apparently fixed today, so expect some coverage from the top 8!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat08/20_2.jpg) *The Invasion bar.*




 



Feature Match: Quarterfinals -- Roland Bode vs Harald Stein
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

 


Before the match could start, judges had noticed that Roland was playing two of his Figures of Destiny as Foil versions. They bent noticeably, and the judges asked Roland to replace them. In the ensuing scramble for two non-foil Destinies, Harald remembered that he also had two of those, but couldn’t find a replacement anywhere, and his weren’t as bent anyway.


Traditionally, red aggro decks have had an edge against their traditional arch-enemy white weenie decks, if they can overcome the hate in the tradition of [Soltari Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soltari+Priest) and Silver Knight. This was also the general opinion before this match: Roland would probably take this one home after sideboarding.


**Game 1:**


Roland won the die roll. It didn’t help him much, as the Kithkin leader Harald had the first play with [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender), and followed it up with Spectral Possession. Roland struck back with [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang), of which he had four in his maindeck. An unusual choice for the field, but Roland had figured that blocking was more important than the extra point of power. In this match, that was certainly true.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat08/21_1.jpg)*Roland Bode hunched over his side of the board.*Harald entered a second Spectral Possession into the mix. Roland was clearly outnumbered despite his (non-foil) Figure of destiny. [Wilt-Leaf Liege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) from Harald got hit by Skred. Harald sacrificed his Essenmann to prevent that Skred, but Roland had a second one.


At the end of turn, Roland forgot to put a counter on his [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny). He realized it just after his untap, but of course couldn’t back up. Harald attacked with his flying Spirit army, bringing Roland to 5 life, and bolstering precisely nothing with his new [Wizened Cenn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wizened+Cenn).


Harald was on 12, and carefully figured out how to block, as he knew that Roland held Incinerate from the trigger on [Countryside Crusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher), that had recently come into play. All five Spirits entered the non-existent red zone, and to keep himself from death, Roland had to Incinerate one of them. But Harald had two [Mana Tithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Tithe) to stop the burn, and the Spirits killed Roland easily.


Roland Bode 0 – 1 Harald Stein


For sideboarding, Roland removed his single [Ashenmoor Gouger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashenmoor+Gouger), 2 [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) and 3 [Unwilling Recruit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unwilling+Recruit), puting in 2 [Sulfurous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast), 2 [Brute Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brute+Force) and 2 [Sulfur Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Elemental).


Harald took his 4 [Mana Tithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Tithe) out of his maindeck to replace them with 3 [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) and his sideboarded [Unmake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unmake).


**Game 2:**


Roland had the faster start on the play, as he opened with [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll) and [Blood Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight). Harald answered with [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) and [Knight of Meadowgrain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Meadowgrain), but the [Blood Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight) could be a dire problem. The race was on.


Three Spirits joined Harald by way of Spectral Possession. The bigger guys were on Roland’s side, though, as he added another [Blood Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight) and Boggar Ram-Gang to his already imposing force. The first Knight and the fresh Ram-Gang took Harald to 13. But the man from Cologne wasn’t about to hand the game to Roland. [Wilt-Leaf Liege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) meant that his flyers carried considerable more weight, and he hit back with eight damage, taking Roland from 19 to 11 life in one fell swoop.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat08/21_2.jpg)*Harald Stein, despite a fever shaking his insides, managed to hand on for dear life.*The two [Blood Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight)s remained Roland’s first line of offense, who added a new defensive lineman in the form of [Countryside Crusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher). Harald’s turn took a good while. “This is hard, and decisive. No pictures please!” said the tense player, who did not want to be disturbed in thinking through this turn.


His attack was two Spirits and [Knight of Meadowgrain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Meadowgrain). Roland blocked the Knight with everything he had – Ram-Gang, Crusher, and Magus. Harald killed the Ram-Gang with first strike, added [Unmake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unmake), and killed [Countryside Crusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher), while still dealing four damage to Roland.


The former Team World Champ went to 7. His Skred on [Knight of Meadowgrain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Meadowgrain) ran into [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender), and when Harald added a second [Wilt-Leaf Liege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) to the board, Roland conceded the game.


Roland Bode 0 – 2 Harald Stein


“Now the match-up doesn’t look as good anymore,” said Roland, who had done 25 post-sideboarded matches yesterday night, and won 20 of those. “That’s not a representative sample,” smiled Harald with his two-game lead.


**Game 3:**


Harald took enough time to decide about his mulligan to gain himself a slow-play warning. He appealed, as he felt that he hadn’t taken enough time yet to think this through. But the ruling stood.


The hand Harald finally kept contained just one land, as he failed to make his second and third land drops. Harald still had [Goldmeadow Stalwart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldmeadow+Stalwart) and [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny) to make plays, but at the end of his third turn, he had nothing to do but to pump his Figure.


Roland, meanwhile, also had a [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny), but his was 4/4 already when it took Harald to 16. [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll) and [Blood Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight) promised Roland a solid midgame. As he added [Demigod of Revenge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge), Harald shook his hand. He had not played a land in his fourth or fifth turn, either. “I’m not conceding out of principle, because people conceded games they could have won too often,” said Harald.


Needlessly, as it turned out, because there was no way for him to pull out this one.


Roland Bode 1 – 2 Harald Stein


Post-game, Harald complained about the slow-play warning, arguing that he should have more time to think about his opening hand in such a difficult beatdown match. Headjudge Michael Wiese upheld the warning on the grounds that game and tournament rules have to be followed, including pre-game procedure time limits, even in an untimed match like this one. The Kithkin player took a short break from the match to regain his composure and to get some fresh air, citing a fever he had. Indeed, Harald looked pale and sounded coarse. So far, we didn’t have to break out the Karsten bucket – yet.


**Game 4:**


On the draw, Roland could not keep his initial seven which contained [Sulfur Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Elemental), four Demigods and a single land. “Turn 20: Demigod, Demigod, Demigod!” Roland described how that game would have turned out.


[Goldmeadow Stalwart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldmeadow+Stalwart) and [Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) from Harald, [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll) from Roland were the openings. A Demigod of Destiny from Harald received an immediate upgrade and sidled over to Roland to take four life from him, taking Roland to 11.


By now, Roland’s board was [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll) and two [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang)s. Spectral Possession addded three fliers to Harald’s board, and now Roland was in trouble. He knocked the top of his deck – “Elemental!” -- hoping to find [Sulfur Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Elemental). Instead, he had [Demigod of Revenge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge). On 11 life to Harald’s 20, he had nothing to say but “go”.


[Burrenton Forge-Tender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender) and two unblocked Spirits brought Roland to 8. Three more Spiritis came from Harald’s hand, and now Roland *really* needed that Elemental, not the [Countryside Crusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher) he played. On the other hand, there was no point for him to hold back all of his ground forces. Since he wouldn’t be able to stop more than one flier anyway, engaging in a creature tangle to start some damage seemed good.


Not to Roland, though. In the end, that thought was moot, as Harald plunked down [Wilt-Leaf Liege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege), and Roland had no choice but to extend the hand.


Roland Bode 1 – 3 Harald Stein


Roland: “Crap.”


He commented that Harald won every game he did through Spectral Possession, typically pumped by Liege. His opponent disagreed, claiming that his other beatdown draw would also work fine against Roland’s red deck. Either way, Harald Stein would go on to face the winner of the quarterfinal between Olaf Krzikalla and Babak Mojtahedy.




 



log: Sunday, August 31st, 10:05 a.m. -- Quarterfinal round-up
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

 


While Harald Stein fought his fever and Roland Bode, so far only winning one of those match-ups, three other quarterfinals happened. This is their story.


**Olaf Krzikalla vs Babak Mojtahedy**


Olaf’s Merfolk did have more trouble than expected with Babak’s green men. Babak took the first match on the back of Olaf’s double mulligan, despite two [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command)s from Olaf to fight back. Game 2 went to Olaf, who had multiple [Sower of Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) from his sideboard to decimate Babak’s board presence. [Groundbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundbreaker) plus [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) didn’t save Babak from his loss.


In the third game, it was the [Groundbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundbreaker)s that did indeed pummel Olaf to death, with a little help from [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) and [Stonewood Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonewood+Invocation). Olaf proved that Merfolk can pretty much do the same: Two [Merrow Reejerey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Reejerey)s and a timley [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) on a 5/4 Battalion equaled the score in that match.


The decider saw an exciting match, with Olaf down to five life by turn four, but hanging on to it dearly. A flurry of trades between [Sower of Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) and [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) on the one side and [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) and [Stonewood Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonewood+Invocation) led to a gigantic ground battle. Olaf had gradually whittled down Babak’s life to his own level, fended off an 8/8 [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) boosted by [Stonewood Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonewood+Invocation) and finally killed Babak through the air.


Olaf Krzikalla 3 – 2 Babak Mojtahedy


**Thoralf Severin vs Fabio Reinhardt**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat08/22_1.jpg)*The match between Fabio Reinhardt and Thoralf Severin was the first to be broadcast to the adjoining room, where players could watch and hear a live commentary by André Müller and Christian Reinschmidt.*The first game took aaaages, until Fabio delivered a gigantic [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), clearing Thoralf’s board and saving himself on a mere three life. Thoralf tried to finish him with [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander), but Fabio had [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) to keep it at bay. Eventually, Fabio won the game with [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) beatdown.


Game 2 saw a mulligan to four cards from Fabio, so that Thoralf almost had the game in the bag. Fabio recovered with [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision), and almost had turned the game round. But Thoralf drew [Profane Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profane+Command), let his Gargadon come into play with mana floating and gave the Gargadon [Fear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fear) to kill Fabio.

Olaf smiled, and revealed [Venser, Shaper Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant), to become the German National Champion 2008!
