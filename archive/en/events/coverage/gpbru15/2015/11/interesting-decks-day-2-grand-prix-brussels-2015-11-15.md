Interesting Decks in Day 2 of Grand Prix Brussels 2015
======================================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX BRUSSELS 2015](/en/events/coverage/gpbru15)
 on November 15, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











As I went through the Day 2 decklists earlier today, there were several decks that looked particularly interesting to me. These decks might not be perfectly tuned yet and most of their pilots soon found themselves out of contention for the Top 8, but the decks were evidently good enough to make it to the second day of competition here in Brussels, and they are certainly something different. Most of them look like they would be a lot of fun to play and, at the very least, can serve as a source of inspiration. Let's get to the lists!

Between [Chief of the Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Edge), [Blood-Chin Rager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood-Chin+Rager), [Arashin Foremost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Foremost), this black-white deck has plenty of ways to take advantage of the Warrior tribe. I also like how [Brutal Hordechief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brutal+Hordechief) and [Sorin, Solemn Visitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Solemn+Visitor) can turn [Secure the Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Secure+the+Wastes) into a huge damage swing.

At Grand Prix Indianapolis, Bob Culp piloted his Abzan Tokens deck to a 12th-place finish. Here in Brussels, several players picked the archetype as well. Matthias Hittel's version featured a few new inclusions (such as [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) and [Planar Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Outburst) in the main deck, as well as [Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke) and [Self-Inflicted Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Self-Inflicted+Wound) in the sideboard) but he retained the core shell of [Catacomb Sifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Catacomb+Sifter) plus [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar).

This is mainly a Sultai deck that aims to get a [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor) into the graveyard with [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) or [Sidisi, Brood Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidisi%2C+Brood+Tyrant). The megamorph creature can then be returned via [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic) and [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector). Mike Hofmann's deck also included a small red splash for [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) because the mana base allowed for it.

[Bring to Light](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bring+to+Light) decks are always fun to check because they sometimes contain interesting cards in their toolbox. Looking at this list, there's the usual [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino), [Crux of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crux+of+Fate), and [Silumgar's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%27s+Command), but there was also a surprise inclusion: [Zurgo Helmsmasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zurgo+Helmsmasher)! I am not entirely sure when you would want to tutor for this card, but I can imagine that a power-7 haste creature can come in handy to take out unprotected planeswalkers or to provide a quick clock against G/R Eldrazi Ramp.

According to a story that was being told yesterday, Raoul Zimmermann played a match against another Eldrazi Ramp deck in which he chained a turn-three [Crumble to Dust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumble+to+Dust) on his opponent's [Shrine of the Forsaken Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+the+Forsaken+Gods) into a turn-five [Oblivion Sower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Sower). Because he had exiled all of his opponent's two-mana lands, that [Oblivion Sower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Sower) gave Zimmermann the mana needed to cast an [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) on the same turn!
