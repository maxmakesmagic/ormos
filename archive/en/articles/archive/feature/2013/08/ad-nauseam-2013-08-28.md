Ad Nauseam
==========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 28, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I find that [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) continues to interest me in Modern, and I found another sweet deck that takes advantage of the "can't lose" clause. I've actually played today's deck a fair amount, albeit not in the last three years. Before [Pro Tour Amsterdam in 2010](/en/events/coverage/rietzl-sweeps-victory-amsterdam), Team [ChannelFireball](http://store.channelfireball.com/landing) (before we were a proper team, even) tested the [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) plus [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) combo. 


![Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ad+Nauseam)
 How the deck works is a little simpler than [last week's](/en/articles/archive/plunge-shadows-2013-08-20) 
[Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) deck, if only because the win condition is always the same. If you cast [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) or have [Phyrexian Unlife](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Unlife) in play, [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) draws your entire deck. Once you have picked up all your cards, [Lightning Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Storm) conveniently gets cast off [Simian Spirit Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) and deals a hefty amount of damage to your opponent. 


 Setting up the combo isn't that hard, mainly thanks to [Mystical Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings). Teachings gets both halves of the combo, and because of flashback, one Teachings will do the trick if you have enough time. Speaking of time, it is nice that [Phyrexian Unlife](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Unlife) and [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) can provide it, although it does require that you burn the [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace). Unlife works a little better, and usually represents at least one extra turn of (un)life, with two turns being the most common. 



[Pact of Negation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) and [Slaughter Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact) are also cute in this deck, mainly because you can Pact your opponent on his or her turn, then [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) on upkeep to not die from the trigger, and then go off that turn. We had [Tolaria West](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolaria+West) in our deck to find Pacts, but that may be too slow these days. 


 The deck is a bit of a one-trick pony, but it's a really good trick, and I only regret that we didn't get it into proper shape for the Pro Tour (instead settling for casting [Treefolk Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treefolk+Harbinger) instead).
