The Gods have spoken and Nam Sung-Wook is your Grand Prix Melbourne 2014 Champion!Defeating local hero Patty Robertson in the finals, Nam also makes history by becoming the first South Korean player to win a Grand Prix. To make this moment even more special, his victory comes from ousting over 900 other players in Australia's largest ever tournament. 

 Semifinalists Wei Yifan and Ash Webster have also tried their best to defend the trophy and they should not be disappointed to return home with a hefty prize check, along with airfare and a much-coveted invitation to Pro Tour Journey to Nyx. 

 Rounding off the rest of the top 8 include four-time Grand Prix Top 8 competitor Huang Hao-Shan from Taiwan, Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* Top 16 competitor Zheng Jingwei as well as Luke McGlaughlin and Craig Chapman. Congratulations to all, and may the Gods be with you!

- ### Quarterfinals - Patty Robertson vs. Jingwei Zheng

*by Pip Foweraker*
  
 The Robertson-Zheng matchup promised to be that classic test of skill, the U/W control mirror. Both decks feature the same core set of cards and become even closer mirrors post-sideboard. A great deal of the matchup will come down to resource and tempo management. Traditionally, players focus on hitting their early land drops so that they can fight more effectively over the powerful late-game spells like Planeswalkers and [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). This careful, studious matchup often comes down to a single game in Swiss rounds. The finals are untimed, meaning the players will have to beat each other the 'fair' way. 

 The first clash of the game came over [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought). Zheng blinked first and had his Jace [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate)d, Robertson resolving his a turn later. The players dug through spells for a few more turns, focussing on hitting their land drops and moving into the later game as rapidly and fluently as possible. Zheng fired off a [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), but wasn't able to stop Robertson from resolving an Ætherling. Zheng settled down to glare at the Ætherling and see what he could get done. A [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) showed Robertson with a hand full of power, with another Jace, a [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), a pair of Revelations and some [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere)s. 

 Zheng went for an [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion), and generated some tokens with her after it resolved. Robertson dropped a [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) to stop Elspeth from getting too out of hand, but Zheng had another to keep the pressure on. Zheng started generating more tokens, but the unblockable Ætherling was enough for Robertson to take the first game. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel14/qf_zheng.jpg)  
 *Zheng: You want to kill which dude?*   
Patty Robertson 1 - Jingwei Zheng
 Both players started off with early [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)s, but then didn't do much but play lands for quite a few turns. Robertson tried another [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), but it got hit with a [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve). Zheng started sneaking in some points of damage with his [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) while both players accumulated cards and lands. 

 A few turns later, the [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) was still going. 

 The same thing kept happening. And then the unusual, if not unthinkable, happened: Zheng's [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) did a combined total of 18 damage (Plus the earlier [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)) to take the second game, drawing an appreciative wave of applause from the gathered crowd. All those other, massively powerful spells? Not so relevant in this game. 

Jingwei Zheng 1 - Patty Robertson 1
 A pair of early [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)s from Robertson cleared out Zheng's Jace's. Zheng tried for a freshly topdecked third, but Robertson had a [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) to keep it out of contention. Zheng chuckled when Robertson activated his [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and swung. "Ah, the bitter taste of my own medicine', Zheng grinned as he adjusted his life total. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel14/qf_robertson.jpg)  
 *Robertson: THIS ONE. I KILL THIS ONE.*   
 Zheng summoned an [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion) to slow down the [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), but Robertson had a [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) to keep it out of play. Robertson cast a Supreme *Judgment* to clear out the Elspeth tokens, holding a copy of the Planeswalker in hand but needing an extra mana to cast it and keep [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) as a backup. 

 Zheng tried a [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), which gave Robertson pause before he went to [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) it. Zheng had a [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve) to get the discard spell through, and nabbed the Elspeth, stymieing Robertson's schemes. 

 Robertson switched back to his [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) plan, steadily dropping Zheng's life total downwards. He tried to accelerate it with a [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa), but it got [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve)d as well. Zheng summoned his own, which Robertson cleared out with his Verdict. Both players threw a few more haymakers, but nothing stuck on either side before the inimitable [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) sealed the match for Robertson. 

Patty Robertson 2 - Jingwei Zheng 1

 Xenagos took form as a planeswalker this time round and Huang seemed to be back in the game, generating a couple of tokens and adding Ghor-Clan Rampager to the board. Unfortunately, the efforts were all undone with an "end of turn" [Fated Retribution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fated+Retribution) and Wei looked like he was firmly in control of the game, who proceeded to draw five cards off [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). 

 Soon after, each of Huang's threats were deftly maneuvered, one after another. [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) got rid of [Stormbreath Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbreath+Dragon), [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) dealt with [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) and [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) locked down [Polukranos, World Eater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater).

- ### Semifinals - Patty Robertson vs. Wei Yifan

*by Chapman Sim*
  
Both players have successfully piloted their Esper control decks to the semifinals, bagging themselves a healthy prize check, a handful of Pro Points as well as the much-coveted invitation to Pro Tour Journey to Nyx in the process. This also meant that at least one Esper control deck would make it to the finals and both players would need to outplay the other if they wished to advance further.

 Game One 
 After throwing back his initial seven, Wei found himself stuck on two Islands but had just enough mana to stop Robertson's [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) with [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate). After cycling [Quicken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicken), he managed to draw a crucial [Temple of Enlightenment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Enlightenment), allowing him to scry into two successive Plains. 

 When Robertson baited out [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve) using [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), that gave him free reign to slam [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) onto the battlefield, prompting an instant concession from Wei. Even in a deck stacked with solutions, it was not possible to come back from this dire situation. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel14/sf_wei.jpg)  
 *Down one game after stumbling on mana, Wei was now fighting an uphill battle.*   
Game Two
 Wei went on the offensive and ran out [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) over two successive turns. However, Robertson was at the ready with [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) and [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve). 

 Upon reaching six mana, Robertson presented his own copy of Jace and Wei allowed it to resolve, preferring to draw three cards using [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). He untapped to plop [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa) onto the table, leaving two mana up for [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay). [Blood Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron) would not be easy to deal with post-board, for a couple of [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s have surely been boarded out, and Wei pummeled at his opponent's life total four by four. 

 Robertson had [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) to access Wei's grip of cards, currently consisting [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere), [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling), [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay) and [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve). After a few moments of deliberation, Robertson decided to remove [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve). 

 Nothing else resolved for a few turns thereafter, with [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s and [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve)s stopping all the crucial spells. Now that both players were out of countermagic, Robertson restocked his hand with a [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for six new cards, and proceeded to drop Elspeth, protecting it against [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) with yet another [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay). 

 With Wei holding two utterly useless [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s, he watched as Robertson's horde of Soldier tokens and [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) flood the board. Despite gaining a bunch of life from the [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa), that did not stop him from extending his hand as Robertson eventually ravaged through his life total.

*by Pip Foweraker*
  
 Sung-Wook opened with a [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), revealing a hand replete with goodies. Double [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), double [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) and [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) stared back at him. He took the [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere), and Robertson simply laid lands and kept passing. 

 Sung-Wook followed up with a [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter), forcing Robertson to blow one of his Verdicts to keep the troublesome flier at bay. Sung-Wook snuck some damage in with a [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), having used a scry land to spot an upcoming [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). 

 Robertson played an untapped [Hallowed Fountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) to get to Jace mana, then immediately used his draw ability to nab a [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) and a land, edging him out of the danger zone. Sung-Wook finished off Jace with his [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and summoned another [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter). 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel14/f_robertson.jpg)  
 *Patty Robertson*   
 The [Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) attacked Robertson to 8, stealing an [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion) from his library. A [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) met with a loooooong pause before Robertson revealed his hand, still with double Revelation, an therling, a [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and a [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). Sung-Wook took the [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) and Robertson took the opportunity to cast a [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for 3, going back to 11 life and hunting for answers to the troublesome flier. With two of his best win conditions gone, Robertson was under pressure to come up with the goods. 

 Another attack from the [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) landed Sung-Wook a [Temple of Enlightenment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Enlightenment), meaning he was only a single white source off being able to cast his opponent's [Elspeth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion). Robertson fired off another [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) and finally used a [Last Breath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Breath) to take out Sung-Wook's flier. 

 Robertson summoned a [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought), whose second ability provided a split of [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) vs. [Elspeth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion). After some thought, Robertson took the [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). Sung-Wook cast an [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), and Robertson spent a [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve) to keep it off the board. A second Connections resolved, however. 

 Robertson cast his [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and saw a hand of double [Hero's Downfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall) and a [Bile Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight), taking one of the Downfalls. Sung-Wook's [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) started sneaking in for some damage, the Korean player ignoring Jace and aiming to pressure Robertson into acting. Robertson fired off his third Revelation for 5. His first plan of action was a [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) on Sung-Wook's [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections): if there was any card drawin' to be bein' done, the U/W player was to be the one doin' it. 

 Sung-Wook kicked it up a notch with a [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon). A [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) took care of the troublesome little spice weasel and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) cleared out Sung-Wook's second [Hero's Downfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall) before summoning an Elspeth. Sung-Wook cast a [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel), sapping Robertson back down to 10 life, and had another [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) to try and find some answers to the dangerous Planeswalker. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpmel14/f_nam.jpg)  
 *Nam Sung-Wook*   
 Robertson summoned another [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and went digging with it. Sung-Wook went to [Bile Blight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight) one of the Soldier tokens, but Robertson offed it with a [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) to keep his army ticking over. Sung-Wook had a [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), giving him an option of Robertson's final [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), a [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate), [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) or a [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve). Holding two Gray Merchants of Asphodel in hand and with his opponent on 10 life, he took the Revelation. 

 Sung-Wook attacked with his Gray Merchant, who got gang-blocked by a pile of Soldier tokens. Returning the attack, Robertson swung with all his Soldier tokens and a [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), keen to push his offence. Sung-Wook ate a Soldier with his [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and took the rest, falling to 10. Another attack, and Robertson took the epic first game and sent both players to their sideboards. 

Patty Robertson 1 - Nam Sung-Wook 0
 A [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) from Sung-Wook revealed Jace, Elspeth, and four lands, Sung-Wook taking the Jace. Robertson played (obviously) lands for a few turns, while Sung-Wook summoned a [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) and began attacking with it. 

 Sung-Wook followed up with an [Erebos, God of the Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erebos%2C+God+of+the+Dead). Robertson had a [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), while Sung-Wook used [Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erebos)'s [Greed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greed) ability to dig for more threats. While Sung-Wook was tapped out, Robertson fired off a [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for 2. 

 Robertson cast an [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion), which Sung-Wook countered with a [Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve) from his [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter). Sung-Wook then summoned a [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon) and attacked with his Specter and [Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erebos). Robertson had a [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), knocking himself to 5 life before casting a [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) to clear out the [Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) and Demon. 

 A [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) took Robertson's [Fated Retribution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fated+Retribution) and another [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter) made an appearance. With his hand stripped and his life total dropping, Robertson scooped up his cards and headed for the decider.
