July 17, 2014
 







Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive *Magic Online* column. Over the last two months, we've seen the Modern format evolve through the [Pro Tour Qualifier season](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/qualifiers-pro-tour-khans-tarkir-honolulu-2014-06-10). Decks like Jund, [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), and [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) variants have remained major players in the format. Recently, a number of less-known archetypes have begun putting up great finishes in *Magic Online* Daily and Premier Events. Today, we'll take a look at Modern's latest tech and discussing the recent changes that tier-2 decks have adopted to gain an edge on the top dogs of the format.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/pr/2014/cardart_bitterblossom.jpg)
[Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) | Art by [Rebecca Guay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=[%22Rebecca+Guay%22])


Combo strategies are eating themselves alive. [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) strategies become less combo-oriented and more interactive with each passing week. Decks like [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) overperformed in the early weeks of the PTQ format because a lot of players weren't familiar with the matchup and often tapped out on a dangerous turn. A few weeks ago, Infect rose up from the ashes as a super-fast way to punish the other combo strategies. Now, [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) is rising to prominence once more, and strategies like infect and affinity need to evolve to stay competitive. Let's take a look at what's been winning in Modern!

There had been a lot of buzz about Faeries leading up to [Pro Tour *Journey into Nyx*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptjou14). Unfortunately, the deck didn't get played by many people and it put up a less-than-stellar performance. Since then, the archetype remained fringe at best for more than a month. Then, we saw a big innovation. [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) solves Faeries's problems beautifully. The deck has issues with flooding, but [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) allows the pilot to pitch extra lands for Rats. When games become races, the Modern Faeries deck often found itself chumping with [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) tokens without a way to strike back for big damage, but again, [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) solves this problem. There were already four [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and at least seven one-mana discard spells in the deck, so it seems like a natural inclusion here. It's also worth noting that [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) is exceptionally strong when we have an active [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine), often winning the game the very next turn thanks to the extra use we get out of our mana.

The deck performs very well against a field of combo and control. The best aggressive strategies, Affinity and Infect, are well contained by an endless stream of 1/1 fliers, a lot of cheap spot removal, and [Ratchet Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb)s.

The new version of Zoo takes advantage of [Delay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delay) as a powerful piece of countermagic; the deck is aggressive enough that the [Delay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delay)ed card won't be coming back a pretty large portion of the time. Affinity has been a major player throughout this Modern season. The deck suffered a major blow when [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) started showing up in a lot of the decks. Opposing [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) often proves to be at least a two-for-one, and is often much worse for the Affinity player. It's hard to keep a good deck down, though. [Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium)'s anthem effect allows the deck to power through [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) in a way that it couldn't before. [Dispatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispatch) allows the deck to effectively deal with [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) or [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) so it can get through on the ground with its massive [Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium).

Against combo decks, this deck needs to attack the opponent's mana base or use [Beast Within](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within) from the sideboard, but the deck seems nearly unbeatable for the decks that are trying to play fair. The nice thing about this deck is that it does very powerful things even when it isn't comboing. For example, it won't be unusual to [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) our opponent's land on the third turn. This is an archetype with a ton of potential that will probably be refined over time. I wouldn't be surprised if there's a tier-1 strategy somewhere in here. Will one of us be the one to find it?

Merfolk is probably the best aggressive tribal strategy in Modern. This version of the deck seems particularly strong. The deck aims to jam as many lords onto the battlefield as quickly as possible to run over the opponent. [Master of Waves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) is incredible against the fair decks, it's especially fun to Vial in [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image), copying [Master of Waves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves), during combat on the opponent's turn.

[Sygg, River Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sygg%2C+River+Cutthroat) gives the deck a steady stream of gas that was missing in previous iterations of the deck.

Again, we see a Jund evolution. We've dropped [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and picked up [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) is the best card against [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), Infect decks, and versions of Affinity without [Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium). [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) also combos very nicely with [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker). We can cast [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) on turn three, cast Garruk, +1, and flashback [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) on turn four, and we'll be presenting lethal damage with Garruk's overrun in the vast majority of games. This is a deck with good removal, the best disruption, and the best threats. We can be sure it's a competitive strategy.
