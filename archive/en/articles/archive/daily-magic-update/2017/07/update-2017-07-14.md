Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Temur Delirium](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2017-07-13)

We're starting to get the first results on *Magic Online* from *Hour of Devastation* Standard, and we've started to see the newest set sneaking its way into more and more decklists as people get repetitions with the new cards under their belts.


One such belt belongs to Oosunq, who went 5-0 with a Temur Delirium deck that highlights a few *Hour of Devastation* cards.


First, [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) is an easy enough inclusion, standing alongside [Magma Spray](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) and [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning) as the red removal of choice. But then things get a little unexpected. [Strategic Planning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strategic+Planning) makes an appearance alongside [Grapple with the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past), as the cards are similar but different enough that they're worth playing together.


The real winner, though, is [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits), which helps fuel graveyard strategies like delirium, is perfect fodder for [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend), and even comes back as a card-drawing engine later in the game.


Combined with [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) and Temur mainstays like [Glorybringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer) and [Rogue Refiner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue+Refiner), Ossunq is certainly on to something that can be developed out from here.
