ROUND 4: (6) SHAUN MCLAREN (ABZAN CONTROL) VS. MAKIHITO MIHARA (GREEN DEVOTION)
===============================================================================



 Posted in [COVERAGE ARCHIVE](/en/events/coverage)
 on April 10, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Craig-Jones-Author-Photo-150x150_0.jpg)
By Craig Jones











 No.6 Shaun McLaren has been on a tear for the last year or so with a win at Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* being followed by a second place finish at Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir*. This time, he's left behind his usual Jeskai colors and is instead running an Abzan control deck.



His opponent is Hall of Famer Makihito Mihara. Mihara burst onto the scene thanks to a World Championship win in 2006 with a [Dragonstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) deck. Surprisingly, there were no dragons this time for Mihara however, as he's back to running green spells with [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx) to power out even more powerful green spells.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/ptdtk/r4_mclaren1_mihara.jpg)  



The Games
=========


Mihara won the dice roll and opted to go first. Both players kept their opening hands.


It was Mihara with the early action, as a [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) allowed him to power out [Polukranos, World Eater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater). McLaren's [Hero's Downfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall) sent that packing, and when Mihara had no follow-up action on the following turn, McLaren took the opportunity to build up his hand with [Read the Bones](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Bones).


That was the last glimmer of light for McLaren in this game, as Mihara's deck overpowered him with pure, brute force.


Mihara had no action at five mana, but at six mana was able to fire off [See the Unwritten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+the+Unwritten). A [Hornet Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen) was waiting on top of his library and brought in four angry friends. On the next turn he placed a second land, which meant he was able to cast the second [Hornet Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen) in his hand.


McLaren had a lonely [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) on his side of his table. In normal circumstances, it's one of the strongest creatures in Standard. Not in this game. McLaren sent it in to attack and had to use [Hero's Downfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall) to prevent a blocking hornet from killing it with deathtouch.


Mihara wasn't even done. He had [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx) and enough green devotion to ramp out first a [Doomwake Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomwake+Giant), and then a [See the Unwritten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+the+Unwritten) with ferocious turned on to put both a Polukranos and [Voyaging Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyaging+Satyr) into play.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/ptdtk/r4_mihara1.jpg)   



That was enough for McLaren, and it was time to go to the sideboards.



 That was a classic example of one player playing moderately "fair" *Magic* being trampled into the dirt by a deck doing "unfair" things.



So why play the "fair" decks when you can play the "unfair" decks spitting out armies of flying deathtouch horrors?


The answer to that is consistency and reliability. Decks that push the power envelope do have draws where they flatten their opponent into dust. However, they're also capable of those draws where they fall apart in comedic fashion. What the player with the "fair" deck should do is try and nudge the "unfair" deck into imploding as much as possible.


McLaren did this in the second game by going after Mihara's mana.


He cast [Drown in Sorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow) on turn three, even though it only removed a single [Elvish Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic). This seemed to slow Mihara's deck down enough for McLaren to be able to bulk up his hand with first an [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm) and then [Read the Bones](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Bones).


A [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) from McLaren revealed the reason for Mihara's lack of action. He was stuck on three lands with nothing in his hand costing less than four mana.


Mihara found a [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix), but that didn't help him right immediately as it revealed a slow "land" on top of his deck in the form of another [Elvish Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic). The next card was also not a land, but instead [Polukranos, World Eater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater). McLaren kept Mihara pinned to three mana by using [End Hostilities](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=End+Hostilities) to wipe the Courser and freshly-summoned [Elvish Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic).


Mihara could only laugh as a second Courser found another slow "land" on top of his library, this time a [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid). At this point he'd fallen too far behind as [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion) had appeared for McLaren and was growing an army of soldiers.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/ptdtk/r4_mclaren1.jpg)  



A second [End Hostilities](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=End+Hostilities) kept Mihara pegged back and then Elspeth's ultimate ability was enough to end the game.


Courser isn't quite as impressive when no lands appear on the top of the library.


In the third game, Mihara got back in the driver's seat. Mihara going first was a significant advantage.


There must have been a nervous moment when a turn two [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) from McLaren revealed no land in Mihara's hand and a bunch of four mana spells.


Fortunately for Mihara, this time the land did come off the top of his library and [Polukranos, World Eater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater) entered play on turn three, thanks to his second-turn [Voyaging Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyaging+Satyr). He followed that with [Eidolon of Blossoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Blossoms) to find more land.


Being on the draw left McLaren a turn behind, and he couldn't quite wrest the advantage back. [Drown in Sorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow) took care of the Satyr and Eidolon, but then he was facing [Nissa, Worldwaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Worldwaker), who began throwing 4/4 lands into the fray.


McLaren thought for a while in turn before running out [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix), perhaps trying to find a fifth land for [End Hostilities](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=End+Hostilities) on the next turn. Mihara just had him under too much pressure though. An attack with Polukranos and two 4/4 lands left him at 3 life, but a [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) added further complications. McLaren needed to wipe the board, but even if he did so, he'd still be defenseless against the Whip bringing the Hydra right back into the fray. This left him with no option but to extend the hand.



**McLaren 1 – Mihara 2**
