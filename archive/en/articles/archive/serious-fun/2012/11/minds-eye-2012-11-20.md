The Mind's Eye
==============



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on November 20, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







How do you build your decks?

 It's a broad question that has many answers. How you approach Draft or Sealed Deck is different from something like Commander. While I will gladly leave Limited deck construction to [the esteemed Steve Sadin](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Steve%20Sadin), approaching laidback **Magic** is what I speak to best. When [we dipped into the idea of building decks of different power levels](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/commander-laws-public-gatherings-2012-10-30) I was light on specifics, aside from what went wrong with the deck I had. 

This week, I want to cover the opposite direction and build up a deck that meets our new needs. I've shared why I want to engage Commander again. Now I can walk us through a plan to get there, and how it worked out.

### A Three-Step Process

 There are many ways to slice building decks for **Magic**. I've reduced my process to just three main decisions: 


1. Choose a theme.
2. Choose an experience.
3. Choose the cards.
This is simplified but easy to follow. The progression is natural because we're asking a logical sequence of questions:


1. What do I want to do?
2. How do I want this to go?
3. Which cards do I want to use to achieve both?
The devil is in the details, so let's put concrete answers to all of these by building my latest Commander deck.

### Choose a Theme


*Theme* isn't just a flavorful and creative way to consider decks. For competitive formats, the answer to "What do I want to do?" is most likely "Play the best deck" or "Attack the metagame I expect." Theme is simply what you want to play.

 Since I needed a new Commander deck I considered my pile of legendary creatures. I couldn't remember the last time I played a Grixis—blue, black, and red—deck with a hundred cards. I also have three different options for a commander: 


* [Garza Zol, Plague Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garza+Zol%2C+Plague+Queen)—an average commander
* [Thraximundar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraximundar)—a powerful, aggressive commander
* [Sol'kanar the Swamp King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol%27kanar+the+Swamp+King)—a classic commander


|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Garza Zol, Plague Queen | Sol'kanar the Swamp King |

 This fulfills my desire for having multiple options for commanders. My premium copy of [Garza Zol, Plague Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garza+Zol%2C+Plague+Queen) beckoned me to build a deck, but upping the ante into [Thraximundar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraximundar) or switching gears down to the humble [Sol'kanar the Swamp King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol%27kanar+the+Swamp+King) let me scale the threat level as needed. 

 All three also support the things I enjoy about Grixis: 


* Graveyard action—recurring things that are dead
* Killing Creatures—filling the graveyards with things to recur
* Demons, Dragons, and other fatties—awesome creatures I just love to play
* Tricky business—unique cards for Grixis decks
 That's a lot that I put into Grixis. This is a good thing for Commander. The next step will fill out even more things to add. 

### Choose an Experience

 Given that my last deck was built around [Maelstrom Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Wanderer) and being as powerful as possible, I knew my choice this time would be different. I wanted the "casual Commander" experience. If you've played with any of the [***Magic: The Gathering** Commander*](https://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/productarticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/commander/productinfo) preconstructed decks then you know what I wanted: 


* Think big—expensive spells and "going big"
* Multiplayer focus—what counts here will differ
* Classic Commander—what's typical for a Commander deck
![Maelstrom Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Maelstrom+Wanderer)  
Could I be looking for repeatable card draw, defensive enchantments, or board sweepers? Sure, and that may be what you want to do in Commander. I wanted something other than that usual suite of experiences, and these ideas will lead me away from overloading oppressive power.

With our governing theme and the experience outlined, it's time to get picky.

### Choose Your Cards

Having all of these ideas to fulfill with cards is awesome for Commander. Seven ideas is a solid number, but I went back to the preconstructed decks for inspiration. *What thought went into these decks? Why are some cards in here?*


My answers led me to add three more ideas:


* [Serious Fun](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?tag=Serious%20Fun&description=Serious%20Fun) previews—slam some awesome cards from Serious Fun's history
* Will it work?—unproven, new-to-me cards just to try
* Looked sweet—cards I've highlighted in action in others' decks
Do these ideas add randomness, variety, and uncertainty to the deck? Yes, and that's what I saw in the preconstructed decks. It's okay to have awesome things for the sake of awesome, different cards that may or may not work out, and classics to relive that proved their power. That's part of Commander's mystique and why I love the format so much.

### Filling in the Blanks

Choosing cards is fun. It's what I enjoy most about building decks, and Commander lets me pick almost everything I want up front. This is how I filled out these ten ideas.

Graveyard Action:


* Creatures: [Corpse Connoisseur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Connoisseur); [Geth, Lord of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%2C+Lord+of+the+Vault); [Sewer Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Nemesis)
* Fillers: [Memory Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Jar), [Moonlight Bargain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Bargain), [Read the Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Runes)
* Returners: [Beacon of Unrest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Unrest), [Diabolic Servitude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Servitude), [Rise from the Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+from+the+Grave)
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf222_combo1.jpg)
*[Sewer Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Nemesis), [Read the Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Runes), [Rise from the Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+from+the+Grave)*
  
 I associate the graveyard with Grixis so much I split the action into three subtopics. Whether it's creatures that take advantage or feed the graveyard, spells that quickly fill it up, or the usual suspects for recursion, everything I want gets piled in. Grixis owns the graveyard, and so should my deck. 

Killing Creatures:


* [Big Game Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Big+Game+Hunter) usually works out well.
* [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder) is a fine way to poke at the entire table.
* [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) is a handy one-of for an emergency.
![Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Damnation)  
I'm leaning away from sweeping the battlefield every turn, but it's still okay to include things that will. Ideas aren't absolutes and rules, but magnets that draw types of cards out for you.

Demons, Dragons, and Fatties:


* [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) feels like the Dragon I've always wanted.
* [Niv-Mizzet, Dracogenius](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Dracogenius) is the newest Dragon I can smash with. He also pulls a fair [Garza Zol impersonation.](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garza+Zol+impersonation.)
* [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) is a nod to my love of tokens and a way to gain a little life for things like [Moonlight Bargain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Bargain) and [Bloodgift Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodgift+Demon).
![Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inferno+Titan)  
 Before [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) met his ban in Commander I had loaded a deck up with every Demon I had. While a few were sprinkled into this deck, I wanted to see other cards I haven't played in some time. 

Tricky Business:


* [Crater Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crater+Hellion) throws damage around then puts itself into the graveyard.
* [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) draws cards, but having a random flying blocker opponents may want you to actually keep alive isn't bad either.
* [Forbidden Alchemy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) gets me to something I want and helps the graveyard stay full even as the game goes on.
![Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Forbidden+Alchemy)  
 Some cards just ooze the awesome of Grixis, and these do it for me. I can't think of any other decks I've made that want cards like this more. 

Going Big:


* [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) is a giant flying creature, instead of just the efficient [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction).
* [Rune-Scarred Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune-Scarred+Demon) is a giant flying creature, so gets the nod instead of just the efficient [Demonic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Tutor).
* [Gilded Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus) takes some mana to get to but is the face of getting to the big stuff for Commander.
 I gladly packed in cards like [Darksteel Ingot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Ingot) and [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern) because there were plenty of things to do at six and seven mana. Being unafraid to leap into big things helps flesh out lots of other ideas. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Darksteel Ingot | Chromatic Lantern |

Multiplayer:


* [Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) will get killed. If it miraculously doesn't... wow.
* [Grave Betrayal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Betrayal) was a *Return to Ravnica* preview I hadn't yet seen in action.
* [Blood Tyrant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Tyrant) is the best multiplayer card I can think of for Grixis. It will get the game moving no matter what, and keeps the game moving once it gets going.
![Blood Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blood+Tyrant)  
Serious Fun has a rich history of awesome things for multiplayer. Celebrating that makes sense in a deck designed for serious fun. (Ha!)

The Experience:


* [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) may be the most-played card in all of Commander. It does everything I want!
* [Demonic Collusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Collusion) is the only tutor with buyback and it plays well with graveyard decks. I see it everywhere recursion is king.
* [Spinal Embrace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spinal+Embrace) is the very first "Commander card" I ever went out of my way to find. Paying homage seems fitting.
![Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Solemn+Simulacrum)  
 Staples for Commander are easy to know. [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring) and dual lands are the most common to think of, but I reached further for ones that fit my themes. I'm also not the biggest fan of [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring), so I rarely include it anyway. 

Will it Work:


* [Mercurial Chemister](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mercurial+Chemister) was added to [Maelstrom Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Wanderer) right before I tore apart that deck. It's time to really use it.
* [Keep Watch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keep+Watch) I found in a pile of commons I had set aside for a reason I forget. With all the attacks that happen, and wording that doesn't specify attacking me, I thought it'd be fun to see how it works.
* [Geralf's Mindcrusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Mindcrusher) looks like it fits my bill of being a big guy and helping graveyard shenanigans. He doesn't come to mind when I think of graveyards in Commander, so I hoped he would fly under the radar to give me some action.
What's awesome or terrible is always relative. Discovering how I'd like some cards would make every draw step exciting.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Keep Watch | Geralf's Mindcrusher |


 I've Seen it Work Before: * [Havengul Lich](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Lich) is the little engine that could. While I won't plan any of the many busted things he can do, I'm sure I'll run into situations where he's very powerful.
* [Crypt Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Angel) demands to be in a Grixis deck. I'll be glad to get an Angel that brings something else back for another turn.
* [Anvil of Bogardan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anvil+of+Bogardan) was something I saw at work in [the very first game I played with](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/i-walk-line-2012-10-09) 
[Maelstrom Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Wanderer). For a graveyard deck, I couldn't think of another way to get everyone to join in.


|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Crypt Angel | Anvil of Bogardan |

I [steal like an artist](http://www.austinkleon.com/steal/), borrowing themes and ideas from others to use in new ways. While the mechanical work a card does won't change from deck to deck, the context I include it in is unique to me. Stolen, but made anew.

Pulling all of these ideas together resulted in a deck I was proud to take with me to [Grand Prix Philadelphia](/en/events/coverage/nakamura-storms-back-0-2-take-gp-philadelphia).

* Games lasted longer due in part to the slower and obvious ways I could kill.
* More players were able to do what they wanted to do rather than be forced into a decision by my deck.
* [Mercurial Chemister](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mercurial+Chemister) really shined by killing a big creature while placing one in my graveyard, which was immediately picked up by a recursion spell.
* [Keep Watch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keep+Watch) drew almost thirty cards at once. The token deck was attacking someone else, as desired.
![Mercurial Chemister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mercurial+Chemister)  
The Bad:


* It felt light on lands. Having a few more earlier in the game would prevent some of the bottleneck that happened when I couldn't go up to six mana right away.
* When someone else pulled way ahead in creatures I felt short on ways to catch up. This may be due to the higher power level of someone else's deck rather than a weakness in my own.
The Ugly:


* There was an accidental combo that took over a game.
The good and bad is just how decks works. There is no perfect deck for every game, but contributing to a better game for everyone with a few minor hiccups along the way is a solid first pass in my book. It's the ugly that took me by surprise. Here's the set up:


* [Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed); [Flayer of the Hatebound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+of+the+Hatebound); [Urabrask the Hidden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urabrask+the+Hidden); and [Havengul Lich](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Lich) are in play.
* I cast [Sewer Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Nemesis), naming myself, since I discarded plenty of cards after drawing almost thirty from [Keep Watch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keep+Watch). [Sewer Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Nemesis) is around a 30/30.
* I cast [Evil Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin) and choose to copy my own [Sewer Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Nemesis).
* Welcome to Combotown.
![Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Evil+Twin)  
 With [Mikaeus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus) out I could use [Evil Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin) to kill [Sewer Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Nemesis) but get it back immediately, triggering [Flayer of the Hatebound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+of+the+Hatebound). Dealing 30 or more damage in one shot kills players quickly in any format, even Commander. With [Havengul Lich](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Lich), I could recast these guys from the graveyard, too. I quickly knocked three players out before a [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) put a stop to things. 

![Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Relic+of+Progenitus)  
 You might be asking, "Why did you do it at all? Just choose not to!" I didn't do this at first, but another player had a [Meishin, the Mind Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meishin%2C+the+Mind+Cage) out with seven or so cards in hand at all times, and was using [Font of Mythos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) and other card-draw effects to stay full. Nobody's creatures could attack, so I nuked that player out first.

Once I started down that path of knocking out players I decided to keep going: Either I'd be stopped (which happened) or the game was over in another turn anyway. After an hour of playing I think it was reasonable to move to end it. These decisions are always a judgment call, particularly when you didn't intend to set it up from the start.
