![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/d1_closing.jpg)Day One of Grand Prix Prague 2014 is in the books. It's been a great day of exciting **Magic**, with 1,396 players fighting through nine rounds of Modern play. Only 185 of them escaped the carnage with records of 7-2 or better and will return tomorrow, for an additional six rounds before the Top 8 play-offs.


Overnight, six players sit atop the standings with unblemished records of 9-0: Valentin Mackl, Bernhard Wurmitzer, Vjeran Horvat, Marcel Kachapow, Andrej Rutar, and Lukasz Szplit. But several big names are close behind: (15) Martin Jůza, Vincent Lemoin, Florian Pils, Joel Calafell, Alessandro Portaro, Lukas Blohon, Florian Koch, Pierre Dagen, Jan van der Vegt, and Emanuele Giusti are all 8-1 while, among others, (9) Jérémy Dezani and (10) Stanislav Cifka managed to squeeze into Day Two on 7-2.


Meanwhile Modern continues to impress with its variety of viable deck types. Blue-White-Red, Merfolk, Urzatron, Splinter Twin, and Jund with and without white all managed 9-0 records. Tomorrow, we'll have more insight into the format, more action, and more stories straight from the battlefield. Join us then. Good night!

Saturday, 9:55 a.m. – Grand Prix Trial Winners
----------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

The weekend's main event may just be in its very early stages, with actual play having barely even begun, but already we have some winners to report. As usual the action started yesterday with a number of Grand Prix Trial tournaments, the last chance for players to earn two byes for this here Grand Prix, and the first chance for us to glean an idea of the frontrunners and the new developments in the format.


As it stands, Modern, more than anything else, continues to be a very diverse format. However, there are some news to come out of the Trials. For example, Junk, the green-black-white midrange deck, completely overshadowed Jund, and everything else for that matter, with one version taking at least three of the top spots here. That particular version is basically two-colored with just a hint of white for [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and notable sideboard options like [Stony Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence).


The second most succesful deck was Merfolk, which had constantly been climbing in popularity after Raphaël Lévy's memorable performance at Grand Prix Antwerp. [Master of Waves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves) brought the deck back to the forefront and apparently is a force to be reckoned with.


Suspiciously absent from this collection of winning decks is [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and Jund, but clearly they're bound to show up rather sooner than later.

Saturday, 9:30 a.m. – The A to Z of Modern
------------------------------------------


*by Olle Rade*

 

Modern is one of the largest formats currently played at Grand Prix and it is certainly a jungle to navigate through. A large card pool ensures a large number of playable decks and to get a grip on all of them is almost impossible. To give you a little help on the way, and maybe a few facts you didn't already know we have composed a summary of some of the key cards, expansions and archetypes in the format.


So, here is the only encyclopedia you will need this weekend. The A to Z of Modern.


**A** – Affinity, the old school name of the deck built around cheap artifact creatures, boosted by [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating), [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager), and [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer). Some people insist on calling it Robots, but regardless of the name it is certainly one of the most powerful decks of the format, sometimes killing as fast as turn two or three.


**B** – [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) – The ultimate Johnny card in Modern. It does everything and a little more. Allowing to search for infinite creature combos like [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast), [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer), and [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap), or [Spike Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) and [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune). Or it can grind out games with value by sacrificing [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), getting [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), gaining life, value, and a big frown on your opponent's face.


**C** – [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) – The best countermagic in Modern. Although the format doesn't have a lot of effective counterspells, this one certainly fits the bill. It was also the key card in Shahar Shenhar's Blue/White/Red deck that claimed the World Champion-title in Amsterdam last summer.


**D** – [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) – Is it a mana creature? Is it an anti-graveyard card? Or is it simply the best one drop ever printed? No matter what, it is a creature that has really made a breakthrough in Modern. Whether in midrange Jund or Junk decks, multi-colored control strategies or in the five-color Zoo deck, this little fellow gets the job done. Whatever it is.



![Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)

**E** – [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) – What better way to spend 15 mana? [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) sees play both in decks built around the Urza lands to accelerate him into play and decks that cheat him into play with [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) or Fist of the Suns. Extra turns and opponents sacrificing permanents. Good fun, and more often than not, Good Game.


**F** – [Fulminator Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) – In a format defined not only by a variety of dual lands, but also man lands ranging from [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) to [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade), the [Fulminator Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) has a lot of job to do. It's also well suited for blowing up Urza lands, and the deck that utilizes the [Elemental Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Shaman) the most is probably the deck built around [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End), recurring them along with a bunch of other creatures to destroy even more lands.


**G** – "Greatness at any cost", the flavour text on the oh so powerful [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). Originally designed by Bob Maher as his prize for winning an Invitational, the card was also reprinted last summer and will be out in numbers this weekend in Prague. The question is how many we will see in total? I'd put the under/over line at 1500.


**H** – Hexproof – The keyword that doesn't only appear on creatures like [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), it also spawned it's own archetype. The green white deck is based upon untargetable creatures like [Slippery Bogle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slippery+Bogle) and [Gladecover Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gladecover+Scout) and wins by enchanting them with auras like [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), [Hyena Umbra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hyena+Umbra) and ultimately Day Break Coronet. Although somewhat of a fringe deck, pro played Duke Reid found it good enough to pilot in the World Championships last year, taking him all the way to second place.


**I** – Infect – Another mechanic that for a while had a deck of it's own in Modern. For the moment it seems to have disappeared, but there was a time where one of the most powerful starts in the format included casting a [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) on turn one and on the second turn casting pump spells enough to grant it 10 power for the win.


**J** – Jund – Originally one of the five shards of Alara, the deck that was a force to be reckoned with in standard back then is now very much so also in Modern. The Red, Green, Black combination built around mana efficient discard, removal, planeswalkers and the best creatures mana can offer has for a long time been the deck everyone is gunning to beat. Will it remain on top? Or is the letter namesake Junk (Green/Black/White), the new deck to beat? This weekend might decide!



![Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Karn+Liberated)

**K** – [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) – A big moment in lore history was when [Venser, the Sojourner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Venser%252C%2Bthe%2BSojourner) used his last mana to give life to [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated), releasing him from Phyrexian tyranny, free to roam as he wanted. Little did he know that he would end up in a deck built around Urza's Powerplant, [Urza's Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower) and [Urza's Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine), making him castable already on turn three. Lore aside, the "Tron deck" is one of the staples in the Modern format, and noone should leave for a tournament without preparing for it. In Prague vendors have already sold out on one of the key cards to defeating it, [Sowing Salt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt), so time will tell if [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated) is sent back to his prison, or if his unholy alliance with his creator's lands keep him free to dominate the format.


**L** – [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) – Without contest the most played planeswalker in Modern. With [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) still on the ban list the black discard machine will probably remain her throne for quite some time.


**M** – *Modern Masters* – Last summers expansion released to give the Modern format a boost. Good old staples were reprinted, and green mythic dragons were upsetting limited players everywhere. If they only had all been [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s.


**N** – [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) – One of Brian Kiblers favorite cards of the format. Utilized in any green deck it both serves as mana acceleration and boosting attackers. The Hierarch pops up both in [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) based decks and more straight-forward green white creature decks, like Kiblers hate bear deck.


**O** – [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) – The legendary vampire is one of the more successful attempts at replacing the, now banned, [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) in the Jund archetype. Featured at least in the sideboard of many of it's reincarnations.


**P** – Philadelphia, the city where the Modern format had it's debut at a Pro Tour in 2011. Although several sets have been added since then, and a lot of changes have been made to the ban list, some people still have fond memories from Philadelphia. Good times were had with second turn wins either through Storm combo or [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) and [Blazing Shoal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Shoal).


**Q** – [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) – So far the only card beginning with the letter Q that sees play in Modern. Although it might be a doubtful accomplishment, the card still makes the list.


**R** – *Return to Ravnica* – The last set that had a huge impact on Modern. Not only did it feature a reprint of the [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) dual lands that originally appeared in the Ravnica block. It also added Deathrite Shaman, [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) which all have seen heavy play.


**S** – [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) – An enchantment that is based for another \*drumroll\* exciting combo deck. Whether enchanting a [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch), [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) or a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) with it, it allows for an infinite amount of token creatures, a scenario only really losable on **Magic Online** where you might run out of time clicking through the triggers.



![Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tarmogoyf)

**T** – [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) – whether a meager 2/3, a decent 3/4 or a huge 5/6, the two mana creature from *Future Sight* is one of the most iconic cards in Modern, and it's reprint in *Modern Masters* only added to it's popularity. Whether a green mage to start with, or just splashing for it, this [Lhurgoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lhurgoyf) is one of, if not the best creature in the format.


**U** – Urza, master artificer, Mishra's brother and one of the first characters in the storyline of **Magic**. 20 years later, and his Mine, Power Plant and Tower, originally from *Antiquities* still allows for some unfair turn three plays. Although this time neither [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) on [Tetravus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tetravus) makes the cut.


**V** – [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) – The most recent card unbanned in Modern. Originally [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) was banned, being considered too powerful in combination with [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) and [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) to allow for a one turn combo kill. [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) was kept on the banned list until the end of 2012. Since then the [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) combo deck has reappeared and claimed a few top 8 performances. Although not quite being over the top insanely unstoppable.


**W** – [Walk the Aeons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walk+the+Aeons) – A key card in a somewhat obscure combo deck based around card drawers like [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) and [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren). It goes off by chaining [Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) effects each turn, drawing an awful amount of cards and finally winning by casting [Laboratory Maniac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Laboratory+Maniac) with an empty library.


**X** – Xpedition Map – Ok, the spelling is a bit liberal, but there really aren't any cards that begin with X that are played in Modern. [Xenagos, the Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+the+Reveler) might make it one day, but until then, the [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) will have to represent the letter X.


**Y** –Yuuya Watanabe – Japanese pro player and multiple Player of the Year winner. He was also one of the sculptors of the Modern Jund deck, that he also won the 2012 Player's Championship with.


**Z** – *Zendikar*, the expansion that brought us the fetchlands that provide half of the mana bases in Modern. The demand for these has skyrocketed in the last year and a lot of people are keeping their fingers crossed for a reprint.


Did we miss something obvious? Or did you enjoy reading the article? What cards or phenomenon would you put in an encyclopedia of Modern. Let us know on Twitter with the #GPPrague hashtag!

Round 4 Feature Match - Jan van der Vegt vs. Roberto Taroni
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Olle Rade*

 

As the first three rounds have finished here in Prague the tension rises. The people with 3 byes have entered the tournament, and the ones with two byes and a win, like Jan van der Vegt are fighting for their fourth. Being the online personality he is, before the start of the match he asked his Bologna opponent if it was okay to film the match for a video project. "No problem," answered the Italian as he shuffled up for his first ever feature match.


### The Decks


Jan van der Vegt's deck has already sparked quite some interest, winning through spectacular fashion by casting [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) at the discount prize of 5 mana using [Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fist+of+Suns). Roberto's deck is green white, filled with "hate bears", focusing on shutting down his opponents resources and making their spells more expensive to cast by summoning [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben). The good news for Taroni is that his deck suits up well against Jan's, the bad is that van der Vegt beat a deck just like it in the previous round.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/fmr4_taroni.jpg)  
*Roberto Taroni* 
### The Games


The match started off in a friendly fashion, with the hat wearing Italian summoning a [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and a [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) in his first two turns. Jan van der Vegt answered by [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)ing the Hierarch and cast [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), revealing another [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), a second [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and two copies of Wilt Leaf Liege.


"Not gonna take those," joked Jan, getting a smile from his opponent as he discarded his [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), hoping to strand him on mana.


His own face wasn't as happy when Taroni promptly played two lands from the top of his deck the following two turns and summoned the second voice along with a Wilt Leaf Liege to quickly put Jan out of the game.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/fmr4_gameplay.jpg)
Game two however, was all van der Vegt's show. Casting [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) on his second turn, discarding the Cube all-star [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) and on turn three he put it into play with [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance). This allowed him to draw 14 cards and attack for seven before summoning a [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) and next turn following it up with an [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn), also animated by [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance).


For the third game Roberto Taroni reached for his sideboard, realizing he might have underestimated Jan's deck, and added [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze)s to try and fight the reanimation strategy.


"Is your deck all colors?," asked Roberto before the start of the final game. Claiming he was familiar with the archetype, but hadn't seen the [Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fist+of+Suns) one on **Magic Online**.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/fmr4van.jpg)  
*Jan van der Vegt* 
Jan was off to a quick start game three as well, when a [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) allowed a turn one [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid), somewhat nullifying Robertos [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben). A second Caryatid followed, before van der Vegt cast his decks namesake card [Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fist+of+Suns), crossing his fingers looking down at the [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) in his hand.


Unfortunately for him, Taroni summoned a [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) on his turn, threatening to destroy the [Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fist+of+Suns). However, as Taroni calmly passed the turn, van der Vegt, not as calmly, slammed down another land and cast the [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) from his hand. Taking his extra turn, he could attack with a [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) to deal lethal damage in addition to his opponent having to sacrifice six permanents.


"You should have probably blown up the Fist with that Pridemage," Jan hinted at his opponent after the game, as surprised as anyone watching.


### Conclusion


**Jan van der Vegt (NLD) beats Roberto Taroni (ITA) 2–1**

Deck tech: Jan van der Vegt, Tin Fists
--------------------------------------


*by Olle Rade*

 

Dutch streamer, **Magic Online** grinder and Cube expert Jan van der Vegt is playing one of the most exciting decks this weekend. Dubbed Tin Fists, as a spin on the Legacy deck Tin Fins, it is built around getting Griselbrand or [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn), the Aeons into play through either Fist of the Suns, [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) or [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). "[Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) is such a powerful card, so my goal was to break it. I don't think I got there completely, but the deck is pretty good," he says.


  

The deck very much has the feeling of it's Legacy counterpart, or other similar combo decks from the more heavy combo format of Legacy. Jan van der Vegt also compares in to the Sneak and Show deck. "Fist of the Sun is sort of like Show and tell and Through the [Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breach) acts as a one time [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack)," he explains.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/dt_van.jpg)  
*Jan van der Vegt* 
The dream opener for the deck involves casting Fist of the Sun as early as turn 2 or 3 and following it up by casting [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) for 5 different mana the next turn. Getting the [Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk)-effect, and being able to attack for 15, wiping out your opponent's board in the process.


"With Fist in play and 5 mana out you have all five colors roughly 90 per cent of the time, so you can actually cast [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn), rather than having to sacrifice him at the end of turn," says Jan with a smile.  
  

Will the deck be good enough to put him in day 2? Or is it just a fun deck that has too many weaknesses that can be exploited? The followers of Dzyl's (as van der Vegt calls himself online) stream probably already know, the rest of us will find out during the weekend.

Saturday, 3:55 p.m. – Making It Count
-------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

The 2013–2014 Pro Points season is nearing its halfway point. For most of the European players, this Grand Prix and the one in Barcelona next month are the last remaining events before Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* which concludes the first half of the season. I talked to two players who have already made some news on the Grand Prix scene and are now on the hunt for additional pro points to reach the Gold and possibly even the Platinum level.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/blog_count_mackl.jpg)  
*Valentin Mackl* 
Valentin Mackl of Austria currently sits at 24 points, with most of the points coming from his three Top 4 results at Grand Prix and his Top 75 finish at Pro Tour *Theros* (each of which is worth five points). "My goal is definitely Platinum, for which I need to get to 45 points," said Mackl. "However, Gold is the minimum of what I expect to finish the season with. If I don't get there with this start—that would be extremely frustrating."


The number of points needed for Gold level is 35, so with 24 under his belt with hardly half the season over, Mackl appears to be in astonishingly good shape. "But nowadays only the best five Grand Prix results count toward your season total, which is especially sad from my perspective. I think I already have like three Grand Prix finishes that are simply gone from the record, and I need a Top 32 to even get any additional points at all," Mackl complained.


Of course, this makes the Pro Tour all the more important. "A Top 16, I believe, would lock me in for Platinum, but my minimum goal for Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* is to place in the Top 75. That way I'd be on schedule for Gold," said Mackl. He then spotted fellow Silver-level pro Wenzel Krautmann and added: "Actually, I don't care too much, just as long as I finish the season with more points than Wenzel!"


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/blog_count_krautmann.jpg)  
*Wenzel Krautmann* 
Wenzel Krautmann's story is a similar one. The GP Warsaw champion began the weekend at 21 points, missing 14 for Gold, 24 for Platinum. Like Mackl, he too got most of his points through Grand Prix and was concerned for the nigh-impossibility of getting any more that way. "I love traveling to Grand Prix, but the current system really disincentivizes such behavior," said Krautmann. "I'll probably still go to a lot of Grand Prix, simply because they're such great fun, but I feel like there should be some reward point-wise for doing so too. Maybe even a ranking-based rather than point-based level system would be better," he mused.


As for his goals regarding the near future, he said, "I want to finish in the Top 25 in one of the next two Pro Tours to be safe for Gold. I have a good team and will be doing a lot of testing. Gold will then qualify me for the all-important fourth Pro Tour. Apart from that, I intend to have as much fun as I can possibly have. I'm pretty optimistic about that, at least."

Round 5 Feature Match - Patrick Dickmann vs. Mikaël Rabie
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Back in October, Patrick Dickmann won Grand Prix Antwerp, the most recent Modern GP, playing [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), and this weekend he again picked up his [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite)s and [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)s. So far he managed a 4-0 start into the tournament, but so did his opponent, Frenchman Mikaël Rabie, playing a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck of the [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) variety.


While nominally two combo strategies, both of these decks have an amazing ability to kill without their respective combination, especially when facing heavy disruption. The [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck is chock-full of creatures anyway, and Dickmann's [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Splinter%2BTwin) version in particular, with its [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique)s and [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer)s, can go the beatdown route as well.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/fm5_dickmann.jpg)  
*Patrick Dickmann* 
### Game 1


Here, the two decks delivered on their promise, as there was no fast combo kill but instead a long and slow grind. On one side, Dickmann's [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) was working overtime, killing, alongside [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s and [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze), [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) as well as [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and its token. On the other side, however, Rabie's defense mechanisms proved quite a problem for Dickmann's creature control. When [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) tried to kill [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast), for example, [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) intervened, and when Dickmann tried to off the Angel, [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) intervened by getting [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite).


Slowly, Rabie was eking out an advantage on the board, and cemented it when [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) searched up [Orzhov Pontiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff) to destroy Dickmann's [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) plus [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite). Meanwhile, Dickmann had lost a lot of life to his lands and to incidental attacks, and needed to make a move. In a last-ditch effort, he attempted [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) on his [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) with neither any preparation nor back-up. Rabie's [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) then ended the game.


### Game 2


The second game began in similar fashion. ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/fm5_rabie.jpg)  
*Mikaël Rabie* 
Both players' resources were very much depleted at this point, and when Dickmann tried to pull ahead with [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) on [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), Rabie had [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) at the ready. Round 6 Feature Match - Stanislav Cifka vs. Jonas Koestler
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Olle Rade*

 

With both players at undefeated 5-0 records, the winner of this match will almost for sure secure their spot for tomorrow. Not that every round doesn't count, but both players would surely like to draw the victorious sigh of relief in this match.


### The players


If the reigning champion of Modern format is the latest Pro Tour winner of the format, it is Stanislav Cifka. He won Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* in 2012 with a deck so powerful that one of it's key cards, [Second Sunrise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise), ended up being banned. His opponent, german Jonas Koestler, once famous for wearing a guy Fawkes-mask to tournaments, but admits that he stopped doing so when he started traveling to the US, where it has been adopted by the Occupy-movement.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/KoestlerCifka.jpg)  
*Stanislav Cifka vs. Jonas Koestler* 
### The Decks


Stanislav Cifka, is running the artifact deck notoriously known as Affinity. After pondering all the decks in the format, he and fellow teammate Ivan Floch decided that it was the best. "We both agreed that affinity is very powerful, and we wanted to try it," says Cifka, whose tech of the tournament is four main deck copies of [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar).


Jonas Koestler on the other hand is running a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) based deck that wins with Kiki-Jiki, the Mirror Breaker, copying either [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) or [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), and then blinking or untapping the Kiki-Jiki, to be able to do it as many times as he pleases, creating an infinite number of creatures, winning in a single attack.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/Board_state.jpg)  
*Cifka's increasing board state.*
### The Games


In a matchup between two of the most powerful decks in the format, the action sure wasn't disappointing. Key plays stared from the very first turns from both players. Koestler's [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and a second bird was met by both first and second turn [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer)s by Cifka. However, Cifka's lack of cards to interact other than through creatures proved a critical weakness as Koestler cast a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), followed by a [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) and a sequence he has been doing all day:


Sacrifice [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), searching up [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image), cloning [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), untapping [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), sacrificing the cloned [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), getting [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), blinking [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), untapping [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), sacrificing [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), getting Kiki-Jiki Mirror Breaker and creating an infinite amount of copies of [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel).


In a game between two silent players, it was clear that the important round had them focused on the outcome of the game rather than making small talk. Game two followed in fast manners as Koestler again had the opening of [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and a second [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). This time followed by a [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), that threatened becoming a [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) if Koestler held a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod).


"Thinking," Cifka exclaimed, realizing he might be facing the lethal combo again if Koestler held a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). He took a few minutes thinking before laying out his plan. The plan? Turned out to be an [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) and activating his [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) to pump his team crossing his fingers that Koestler didn't have the [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). He did however, but seemed to have sideboarded out one of his combo pieces, so Cifka got another turn to try and find something.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra14/Pod_chain.jpg)  
*The Pod combo*
When he didn't though, Koestler let out a sigh of relief, untapped and exclaimed, "That should be game", once again setting up his chain of infinite creatures, forcing Cifka to extend his hand in concession. "I sideboarded the [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) out, since it's rare to win against Affinity the the [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) chain as it costs both a lot of life and a lot of mana," Koestler explained after the game.


"Cifka would have won the first game if he had started,", Koestler added graciously, now at 6-0 and with big toe into making day 2, whereas Stanislav Cifka can only afford one more loss in the coming three rounds.

**Jonas Koestler (GER) beats Stanislav Cifka (CZE) 2–0**

### Max Sjöblom (Melira Pod) vs. Sylvain Martins (Blue/White/Red)



![Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Baneslayer+Angel)

Finnish Sjöblom, who made top 8 in the very first Modern Pro Tour in 2011, and recently won a qualifier for the next one in Valencia was hoping to get a weekend of practice and hopefully a few Pro Points with him on his way to Spain. His French opponent however wanted something else and took down the match 2-1, sealing the deal in the final game with Stoney [Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence), shutting down any [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) attempts by Sjöblom, and eventually winning in the air with Vendillion Clique and [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel).


### Tomas Holzinger (Kiki-Jiki Pod) vs. Mateusz Myslicki (Affinity)



![Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Birthing+Pod)

Austrian youngster Tomas Holzinger might have been playing a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck, but it was [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) that was the MVP in his last round 2-0 defeat of an unsuspecting Polish Affinity player. Not even [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) could win the race, and Holzinger makes day here at Grand Prix Prague.
