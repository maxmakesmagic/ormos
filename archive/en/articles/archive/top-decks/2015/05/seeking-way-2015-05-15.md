Seeking the Way
===============



 Posted in [Top Decks](/en/articles/columns/top-decks-archive)
 on May 15, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_luisscott-vargas_0.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 






It's time to take a tour of the [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery). It's a hard location to get to, and more people seem to prefer visiting the [Sandsteppe Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sandsteppe+Citadel) these days, but there are great rewards contained within. Let's see what it offers us.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/cardart_MysticMonastery.png)


[Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery) | Art by [Florian de Gesincourt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5b%22Florian%20de%20Gesincourt%22%5d)




At the base level, what [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery) does is tap for blue, red, or white mana. That's abundantly clear, but the implications of that are the interesting part. It enables a variety of decks, decks that definitely have their place in Standard. I've played those decks a lot, and it may be time for them to be awesome again. Standard has been great this year, with a ton of ebb and flow, and figuring out when decks like the different Jeskai decks are well-positioned is a big part of being successful. Getting a jump on the format is one way to get a big edge, and knowing about decks that have faded in popularity is a way to be ready when the format shifts enough for them to be good again.

I no longer like [Goblin Rabblemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Rabblemaster) as the amount of removal being played right now is too high, and I (unsurprisingly) did want to add the fifth delve card, [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time).





---

**What This Deck Does:**



The main game plan here is pretty simple. It revolves around casting [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy), and using that to fuel delve spells, which incidentally also kills the opponent by making even one token-making card lethal. [Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate) helps find Ascendancy, and the high number of cheap spells make it possible to cast [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) or Dig even without the help of Ascendancy.



The backup plan of casting [Seeker of the Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seeker+of+the+Way) into [Hordeling Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hordeling+Outburst) and attacking a bunch is viable too, as the deck can easily deal 4–8 damage via burn, but [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) is really what makes the deck tick.





---

**What This Deck is Great Against:**



[Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) is very good against one-for-one removal spells, essentially shrugging them off completely (especially now that there are no Rabblemasters in the deck). It's also powerful enough to go over the top against just about everything if Ascendancy lives. It may be the most powerful deck in Standard when it is doing its thing, though Devotion decks do come close. Still, casting free Stokes, one-mana [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise)s, drawing 20 cards, and then attacking for 100 is a pretty impressive turn. And this deck can do that.



This deck is also very good against Mono-Red Aggro and other token-based aggressive strategies. [Raise the Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm) and [Hordeling Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hordeling+Outburst) are very hard for a deck with [Firedrinker Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firedrinker+Satyr) and [Lightning Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Berserker) to beat, and having [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash), [Seeker of the Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seeker+of+the+Way), and [Lightning Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike) certainly doesn't hurt.





---

**What This Deck is Bad Against:**



[Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) is the most annoying card to play against, because it's the most main-deckable card that kills [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy). Part of the reason that Tokens has faded in popularity is that Abzan Aggro with [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) is a larger part of the metagame than it was at the Pro Tour. [Bile Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight) is another card that is effective against this deck, though it really just trades for one card in the end. Tokens would certainly rather not play against Bile Blight, but unless it's Blight + pressure, [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) will ultimately win.



Similarly, [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) can disrupt this deck more than other decks, because this is a synergy-based deck, and those fare more poorly when their engine pieces are taken. Once again, given enough time, this deck can shrug off [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), but some [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) decks tend to attack.



Creatures that don't die to Stoke can also be problematic, so [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino); [Polukranos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater); and [Silumgar, the Drifting Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%2C+the+Drifting+Death) are all cards this deck doesn't want to see. [Valorous Stance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valorous+Stance) can only go so far, even if you can add more after sideboard.





---

**You Should Play This Deck If:**



Abzan Aggro goes down in popularity. Abzan Aggro has the right combination of pressure plus good answers to give this deck problems, and can even play [Bile Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bile+Blight) if it really wants to. If I knew Abzan Aggro was going to be a small part of the metagame, I'd recommend Tokens, especially if Mono-Red or decks like Bant are a big part.

Jeskai Midrange
===============



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Soulfire+Grand+Master)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mantis+Rider)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dig+Through+Time)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time)

**What This Deck Does:**



Pressure the opponent with hard-hitting threats, all of which cost 2–4 mana, then back them up with: removal, burn spells, and [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time). It's similar to Jeskai Tokens in the delve/burn aspect, but the threats are way faster and do more by themselves. It doesn't play Ascendancy, instead opting for more individually powerful cards.



The way this deck wants games to go is for it to stick even one threat, and deal 10–12 damage with creatures, after which it can usually Dig into the requisite number of burn spells (with [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider) often counting as a burn spell). It also does a good job playing the attrition game, especially this build and its choices of [Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master), [Stratus Dancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stratus+Dancer), [Ashcloud Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashcloud+Phoenix), and [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command).





---

**What This Deck is Great Against:**



This particular build is very strong against control, with [Stratus Dancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stratus+Dancer) and Phoenix providing some very good threats, and [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) giving the deck a powerful instant to cast on the opponent's turn. [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider) is also generally good against removal decks, as it often gets a hit in before dying, which is sometimes all you really need.



Midrange decks often have trouble with flying pressure backed up by good removal spells and a robust card advantage engine, and this deck checks all those boxes. [Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master) also makes racing difficult, as Soulfire into [Stoke the Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoke+the+Flames) is a big swing.





---

**What This Deck is Bad Against:**



[Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) decks were also very hard for Jeskai to beat, but those too have been left on the sidelines recently. The massive life gain is hard for a deck that can't deal much more than 20 per game, and Jeskai isn't fast enough to beat Whip before it plays its eponymous card.



Likewise, White-Green Devotion decks that have good flying defense can get their engine going before Jeskai can seal the deal. The life gain from [Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mastery+of+the+Unseen) is a big problem, and once manifests start turning face up, the game is usually over.



The power level of this deck is a notch lower than many decks in the format, which isn't a very specific weakness, but is a real one. To play a lower-powered deck, you want to be sure it's well-positioned, as you are giving up ridiculous cards like [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) or engines like [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) in order to do so.





---

**You Should Play This Deck If:**



Esper Control decks are still heavily played. Dancer into Phoenix is a great way to pressure these decks, and the sideboard counterspells make things even better.



People are spending too much time getting cute with [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor) and [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector). Just kill them with [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider)s and [Ashcloud Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashcloud+Phoenix).



The more "standard" list of Jeskai Midrange is a little different.

**What This Deck Does:**



Stop the opponent from doing anything relevant with counterspells, removal, and sweepers, before finishing the game with Elspeth or Ojutai.



[Lightning Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike) and Anger give the deck much more game against fast green decks, and [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor) in particular. Additionally, getting to play [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery) means that going solid three colors is now possible. This deck gets the best of white, blue, and red…unlike Esper Dragons, which essentially just plays blue and black.





---

**What This Deck is Good Against:**



Green decks that are soft to Anger/Elspeth are much easier to beat with Jeskai than with Esper.



Aggressive red decks also get easier, now that the deck has access to [Arashin Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Cleric), [Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master), and better mana.





---

**You Should Play This Deck If:**



You only want to play one round of the [Standard Super League](http://standardsuperleague.com/).
