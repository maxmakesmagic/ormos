The main difference between this deck and the previous winning list was Jordan's moving three [Goblin Ruinblaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ruinblaster)s to the main—over [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar), [Deprive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive), and [Volition Reins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volition+Reins). [Goblin Ruinblaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ruinblaster) is a much-storied threat that can hit the 'field on turn three via [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) or [Explore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore)—rather than a card like [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar), which is only great when you have already gotten [Oracle of Mul Daya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) online.

This is a new take on Blue-Black Control, largely credited to superstar deck designer / Grand Prix winner Gerry Thompson.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td116_mimicTitan.jpg)  
The hot new tech is [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat). You have lots of [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s to kill their stuff (maybe a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) if you are super lucky), and [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) can spit it out turn after turn. The creatures in this deck are obviously quite strong; though you will see [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) more often than (just) [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan). [Abyssal Persecutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor) is mitigated by the "[Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)" ability on [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), of course.

See also Round 8, below.

Against decks where you have a huge advantage in quality, values of individual cards change as you try to figure out how the opponent can win. For example, [Oracle of Mul Daya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) is a tremendous threat against control, and can set up a tremendous combination via [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and [Halimar Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths). However against an aggressive Red-Green deck, it is mostly a 2/2 creature for four mana that you never want to draw. It can't block [Cystbearer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cystbearer) and will probably die to any and every thing they can present. I am full-on "medium" about [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) in these situations; I am fine with it. It is fine. It can counter. It can get cut ... With different sideboard options you can just cut two, or whatever. In this case I wanted to focus on casting better stuff on my own turn ([Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth), [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), most of my main deck cards), so I was less apt to leave mana open.

But when I did, [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) seemed like an objectively better choice; after all it can counter most of the spells in both colors. Bringing in [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) was another reason I cut [Oracle of Mul Daya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya). I am fine with trading [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) with [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend) if my opponent will let me ... but I don't really want 100 things hanging out with their 1-2 toughness on the battlefield when I am setting up [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm).

The [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ulamog%252C%2Bthe%2BInfinite%2BGyre) occurred because I had the choice to cash in my [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) on a hard cast [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) (which I did) or spend my [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze). ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td116_kuldotha.jpg)I was initially frightened of this match up, despite the fact that I knew Jordan beat it in the finals to win the SCG Open two weeks ago, just because it is so aggressive and fast.

The free land I got was a [Copperline Gorge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Copperline+Gorge), which prevented me from taking a second Guide strike to the jaw.

I went [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) + [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) all in one turn. My opponent killed the Jace, preventing me from blocking with a [Panic Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panic+Spellbomb). So I utilized my not-dead [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) to make a [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td116_vat.jpg)So for the last match of the evening I got to play against the other big, respectable, blue deck in the format, Blue-Black Control.

I figured either deck could win, but that Blue-Red-Green would have the advantage because [Oracle of Mul Daya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) is kind of like a second Jace. Both decks have [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan)s to finish but one deck has a much spicier route to the end game.

The first one kind of got away from me. I had multiple active [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan)s and an active [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) but managed to lose to opposing Titans. My opponent drew multiple [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s for my Titans, and had cast an early [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat). The Vat quickly had a [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) underneath it (my opponent's, actually ... I pointed two [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s in its direction), and I had to use my Titan(s) to keep the Vat down. Despite my Jace, my opponent out-drew me and took the first.

Both the second and third games went similar directions. I had [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) and/or [Explore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) and had much more explosive starts than my opponent did, or could. He or she had [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) and [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) both games, but I had enough of a lead to hit Jace and [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) ... eventually locking down with [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td116_match8Footer.jpg)  
So that was a great run!

6-2 isn't quite perfect (and in a Pro Tour Qualifier I'd probably be a match out of Top 8), but I felt both of the losses were winnable. The Boros was the 0-2 stomp, but like I said, there were lots of sac lands, and I probably could have done something differently (killed the first [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) on my turn, say); and the Blue-Red-Green mirror match? Well, for one thing, at least a Blue-Red-Green deck won that one (not really tarnishing this deck's win percentage). But I probably could have won the first one if I didn't keep that one-land hand.

All in all, this deck has some of the highest card quality in Standard, and a mana base that genuinely adores you. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td116_ruinblaster.jpg)  
The one thing I found interesting—and maybe this doesn't matter, it's not like eight matches is some kind of super high *n*—I sided out [Goblin Ruinblaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ruinblaster) a huge amount of the time. I know the defining feature of *this build* is the main deck addition of the Ruinblasters ... but it might be the case that being flexible on their inclusion (or maybe exclusion) is the way to go.
