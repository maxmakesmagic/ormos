Squee's Legacy
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 22, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Despite the amount of people working on building **Magic** decks, there is always something that shows up that no one has ever seen before. In this case, I'm talking about the deck that won the [StarCityGames](http://www.starcitygames.com/) Legacy Open in Indianapolis—Jund Depths. 

![Squee, Goblin Nabob](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Squee%2C+Goblin+Nabob) Kennen uses the new legend rule to take advantage of the interaction between [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) and [Thespian's Stage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage), but in the meantime his deck functions like a normal Lands deck. However, his version also has some Jund elements, with [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire), plus he uses [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox). 

 Maybe I'm in the minority here, but I absolutely love decks like this. Kennen wastes absolutely no slots on extraneous copies of [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) or [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime). Instead, he uses [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) as a toolbox to tutor for the things he needs. Maybe it's a [Nether Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Spirit) to block, a [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) to kill creatures, or a [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) to pressure combo decks. Sometimes you'll search for a [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) or a specific land to get back with Loam. 

 Of course, the true hero is [Squee, Goblin Nabob](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squee%2C+Goblin+Nabob). With [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), you might need something to continuously discard and Squee fits the bill nicely. Sometimes you'll deal the killing blow with the legendary Marit Lage token, but we know who the real legend is. 

 Some versions of Lands are able to cripple their opponents and stabilize, but take too long to kill their opponents. Kennen has additional disruptive elements and a way to kill quickly. The [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) toolbox gives him a ton of flexibility and [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) provides the win condition. What's not to love?
