Once More Through the Breach
============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 24, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I love a good Tron deck, and this deck reminds me of a similar list I tried a while ago. It still keeps the blue part from blue-white Tron, which means all sorts of sweet cards like [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge), [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), [Compulsive Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research), [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), and [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal), but the second color this time around is red. Red brings a few removal spells to the table, but the big addition is [Through the Breach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). 


![Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Through+the+Breach)
 By adding [Through the Breach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach), this deck gets to play a five-mana spell that annihilates the opponent's board, as well as dealing a bonus 10–15 damage. Of course, the deck is still quite capable of casting giant Eldrazis, but having a way to cheat them out makes things much easier and makes the deck much more dangerous. [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) also likes heading in the general direction of the breach, providing a 12-point life swing and the two tokens it is famous for. 

 Much like how the red-green Tron deck is just land-searching, [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated)s, and lands, this deck has all card-draw in the non-threat slots. [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) are the token disruption spells, and they are overshadowed (both numerically and conceptually) by the litany of card-drawing the deck has. There's nothing better than chaining [Compulsive Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research)es and [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge)s, with a stray [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) thrown in here and there. Gifts doesn't even have any specific combos—certainly nothing as streamlined as [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). Instead, it just gets you value, the way it should be. 

 Tron decks are getting more and more focused, a far cry from the blue-white Tron deck I used to play. That's fine, and a sign that Modern is becoming more advanced, so I'll content myself with the thought that [Compulsive Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) is a Modern-playable card.
