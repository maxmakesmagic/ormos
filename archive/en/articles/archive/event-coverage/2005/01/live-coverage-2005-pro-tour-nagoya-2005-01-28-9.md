Live Coverage of 2005 Pro Tour Nagoya
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 28, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_paulsottosanti.jpg)
By Paul Sottosanti











Masashiro Kuroda is a master of his realm. He won Pro Tour–Kobe last year and then won Grand Prix–Osaka as a member of PS2 earlier in January, and was now sitting at 7-1 here in Nagoya. Interestingly, he doesn't have a string of average performances in between these events. So far he has declined to leave the [island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=island) of Japan, so this has been his first Pro Tour since his win in Kobe. Conveniently, the World championships will also be in Japan this year, giving him a chance to cap what has started as a very strong season. ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnag05/jb7.jpg) *Kuroda defended his home soil against Jeroen Remie.*


Jeroen Remie won Pro Tour–Seattle 2004 as part of Von Dutch and has two other Top 8s to his name. He's been a top Pro Player for several years now and is looking to follow up on his success.


### Game 1


In the first game, Kuroda's white-blue deck gummed up the ground, but Remie had the edge with an early [Nezumi Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Cutthroat). Kuroda was in even more trouble when a [Kabuto Moth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kabuto+Moth) resolved on Remie's side. He raced as much as he could, but it was all over when Remie slapped [Devouring Greed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Greed) onto the table. Despite being at a reasonable life total, Kuroda didn't even bother to bluff the counterspell and they were quickly on to the second game.


**Jeroen Remie 1, Masashiro Kuroda 0**


Preparing for Game 2, Kuroda brought in the anti-Spirit plan.


Out: 2 [Ghostly Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison), 3 [Floating-Dream Zubera](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Floating-Dream+Zubera)  

In: 3 [Kitsune Diviner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Diviner), 1 [Thoughtbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtbind), 1 [Hisoka's Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hisoka%27s+Defiance)


### Game 2


The second game started slowly as both players put out double [Kitsune Diviner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Diviner), but Kuroda was the first to strike with a [Soratami Rainshaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soratami+Rainshaper). The rest of the game had, understandably, ground to a halt. Eventually Remie mustered his own threat in the form of [Villainous Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villainous+Ogre), but he was behind in the race. Kuroda was content to just keep attacking for two, but when he drew [Counsel of the Soratami](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counsel+of+the+Soratami), he cast it to search for a fifth land.



![Kitsune Diviner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kitsune+Diviner)

Not finding it, he passed on casting the *third* [Kitsune Diviner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Diviner) so that he could have [Blessed Breath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Breath) up, conscious of the fact that this was the first time in awhile he'd tapped below Rainshaper mana. Good thing too, as Remie immediately attempted a [Befoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Befoul).


The two creatures continued to race, but Kuroda still had the advantage, especially when he added a [Kitsune Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Blademaster) to the team. Still, Remie was willing to send his Ogre in, and Kuroda respected the possible tricks by tossing an extraneous Diviner in front of it. Remie played out [Wicked Akuba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wicked+Akuba) and then fell to two on the return attack.


He then sent [Kami of Ancient Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kami+of+Ancient+Law) and [Villainous Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villainous+Ogre), hoping to make something happen. Kuroda committed the Blademaster to the Ogre and let the other through. [Indomitable Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indomitable+Will) targeted the Ogre after first strike, but Kuroda let it all resolve despite having both [Consuming Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vortex) and Candle's Glow in hand, realizing that the death of his Blademaster wasn't all that relevant.


When he attacked back in the air, Remie shrugged and scooped.


**Jeroen Remie 1, Masashiro Kuroda 1**


Kuroda sideboarded again, bringing in the [Ghostly Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison)s and removing some Spirits.


In: 2 [Ghostly Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison)


Out: 1 [River Kaijin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+Kaijin), 1 [Hundred-Talon Kami](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hundred-Talon+Kami)


Remie also did some sideboarding, bringing his total configuration to this:


In: 1 [Lantern Kami](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Kami)


Out: 1 [Ashen-Skin Zubera](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashen-Skin+Zubera)


### Game 3


Remie started off the game with a turn-two [Nezumi Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Cutthroat), which slid past a [Kitsune Diviner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Diviner), but Kuroda had turn-three [Ghostly Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison) to slow things down. Remie was happy to pay the tax and send the Cutthroat again.


On his fourth turn Kuroda made a [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) and passed the turn, but activated it when Remie attempted [Kabuto Moth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kabuto+Moth). He saw [Hisoka's Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hisoka%27s+Defiance), [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), and [Teller of Tales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teller+of+Tales), with no lands in hand. Eventually he chose to turn the Top in for the [Hisoka's Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hisoka%27s+Defiance) and countered the creature.


When Remie attacked again, Kuroda cast [Sift through Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sift+through+Sands) spliced with Candle's Glow and gained two life while drawing some cards. He then untapped and made a [Kabuto Moth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kabuto+Moth) and the Top, leaving up a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) to possibly bluff [Blessed Breath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Breath). Remie called him on it and immediately [Befoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Befoul)ed the Moth, sending it to the graveyard. Kuroda just used the Top on end step.



![Reverse The Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Reverse+The+Sands)

Down came a sixth land and [Sire of the Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+the+Storm)s, but he fell to 10 from the Cutthroat. [Devouring Greed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Greed) had to be a concern at this point, with two Spirits in play on the opposing side. Kuroda's hand was now [Consuming Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vortex), [Teller of Tales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teller+of+Tales), [Candles' Glow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Candles%27+Glow), and [Reverse the Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverse+the+Sands). This posed an interesting dilemma, as he wanted to fall low on life to abuse the eight mana White card, but he needed to stay out of [Devouring Greed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Greed) range as he'd already used his Defiance. He summoned [Teller of Tales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teller+of+Tales) and was done with his turn.


The Cutthroat rumbled in, and after some thought he simply cast [Candles' Glow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Candles%27+Glow) to gain 2 life and draw a card. He could have spliced [Consuming Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vortex), but realized he wouldn't gain much from it, as Remie was flooded and had plenty of mana to replay creatures at this point. Kuroda then played a land and was done again, and the Cutthroat knocked him back down to 10. He played an eighth land, now having access to [Reverse the Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverse+the+Sands).


The rat knocked him to 8 but Remie still couldn't put any additional pressure on the Japanese player. Kuroda activated the Top and only looked at the top card, but Remie corrected him, showing three fingers. "Not a Deceiver," Kuroda grinned sheepishly. He checked the top three instead, then bounced a [Mothrider Samurai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mothrider+Samurai) with [Consuming Vortex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vortex), tapped a [Lantern Kami](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Kami), and drew a card. He checked the top three cards again, rearranged, then put four mana in his pool and burned down to 4.


[Reverse the Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverse+the+Sands) hit the table and he attacked in the air for the win.


**Masashiro Kuroda 2, Jeroen Remie 1**


Jeroen shook his head, ruing one of his earlier plays.


"If I don't [Befoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Befoul) your Moth then I kill your Sire and I think it looks bad for you…"







#### Jeroen Remie
