Lessons from the Magic Online Championship
==========================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on March 28, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Hello and welcome to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive **Magic Online** column. Last weekend, sixteen of **Magic Online**'s best and brightest gathered at PAX East for the [**Magic Online** Championship](/en/events/coverage/butakov-tramples-his-way-past-opposition). Today, I'm going to take a look at the top-performing Standard decks from that event and discuss these decks' positions in the current **Magic Online** metagame. 

 The **Magic Online** Championship is an opportunity for the best players on **Magic Online** to show off their talent for huge prizes and even larger bragging rights. [Brian David-Marshall](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Brian%20David-Marshall), [Rich Hagon](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Rich%20Hagon), [Marshall Sutcliffe](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Marshall%20Sutcliffe), [Rashad Miller](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Rashad%20Miller), and [Steve Sadin](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Steve%20Sadin) did an excellent job providing insightful coverage of the whole event. You should [check it out here](/en/events/coverage/butakov-tramples-his-way-past-opposition) if you didn't have a chance to follow along with the action. 

 Dmitriy Butakov may have won the **Magic Online** Championship, but I'm going to be looking at the top-performing decks from the Standard portion of the event. Let's start by taking a look at the decks that managed 3â€“1 records on the final day of competition.

 Junk [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) strategies similar to those played by Dzy and Malteko have been dominating **Magic Online** Standard Premier Events for the last week. The deck uses fast mana acceleration to ramp into things like [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) and [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). Sometimes the deck is able to [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) or [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) its way into VERY fast wins on the back of [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). The deck can play like a midrange creature deck, but it's top end is far superior to the rest of the format. This makes the deck extremely well positioned in a midrange format. I'd expect to see a lot of this in the coming weeks. The deck struggles with board sweepers, but it's ability to play from so many angles means that no matchup is unwinnable. The popularity of Junk Rites and its presumed continued success in the coming week could prompt players to start including some number of [Curse of Death's Hold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) in their sideboards or even main decks. [Curse of Death's Hold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) is probably the best card available against Junk Rites. Keeping your opponent's [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) in check is one of the harder angles to deal with and a single curse significantly decreases the decks' ability to alpha strike for lethal out of nowhere with [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth).

Aristocrats performs well against aggressive decks, tooâ€”cards like [Orzhov Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Charm), [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip), [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), and inexpensive creatures help hold off the most aggressive decks until the Aristocrats deck can get one of its strong interactions online. Naya Blitz can be too fast for the Aristocrats deck, but I think it's the only deck that goes low enough to be a problem. Decks like Junk Rites also offer a unique set of problems for Aristocrats. The matchup is difficult because the Aristocrats deck needs to play as aggressively as it can with removal spells, but it may often find itself dead to [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) or [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) regardless.

[Ground Seal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal) occasionally pops up in a main deck, and Reid chose a good time to sport the green enchantment. [Ground Seal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal) completely turns off [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), the most powerful card in the most popular archetype, at little cost. [Ground Seal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal) also shuts down [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), which isn't quite as relevant, but it gives the card additional splash damage and upgrades it from a cantrip in several non-[Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) matchups. Two copies of [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) are another nice addition that let the deck pull off some seriously impressive starts like turn-three [Garruk, Primal Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter). The deck doesn't go all in on any of its strategies, it's just a very good deck with good removal and well-positioned card-advantage engines. Jund midrange will likely continue to be one of the better decks in Standard.

 The Bant Midrange decks that have been popping up are extremely well positioned for the format. They can struggle with Junk Rites in Game 1, but the matchup gets significantly better after they bring in extra [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere)s, [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s, and [Rest in Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace)s. The deck demolishes Jund in a way that never really seemed possible. [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) with [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) makes a big board presence early enough to deal with the most aggressive decks like Naya Blitz. Unfortunately, the deck doesn't always get to ramp into a [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter), so the deck can often finds itself on the receiving end of a beating from aggro strategies in the first game. Nonetheless, the deck still ramps into [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s and bring [Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender)s into the fray for Games 2 and 3. I expect this deck to gain a reasonable amount of popularity among the most competitive crowd before it has to deal with something like Esper Control.
