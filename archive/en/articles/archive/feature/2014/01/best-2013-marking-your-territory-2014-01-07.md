Best of 2013: Marking Your Territory
====================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 7, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












It was no surprise to see Josh Utter-Leyton attacking with [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) at the [World Championship](/en/events/coverage/youth-served-flash), as he's played Jund at almost every Modern tournament he's played in. The strange part was seeing him playing straight BG, eschewing the red from the deck entirely. As a replacement for [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), Josh turned to [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) and... [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark)? 


![Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deathmark)
  
 In the biggest tournament of the year, Josh took a gamble that the vast majority of his opponents would have green or white creatures in their decks. Doing something like that can be a huge risk, but it paid off for him with [a Top 4 finish](/en/events/coverage/youth-served-flash). 


 By sticking with just two colors, Josh was able to incorporate [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) into his mana base, which gave his lands more utility and allowed him to fight Tron decks and help mitigate the effect of his opponent's [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)s. The recent release of [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) gave him another creature similar to [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), which was another reason not to splash something like [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). 


Since that tournament, people have more or less returned to the usual Jund or Junk (or both), but Josh provided everyone with a valuable lesson that more isn't always better. Sometimes it's correct to keep it simple and only play with the bare essentials.
