Round 4 Feature Match: Masahiko Morita vs. Kazumasa Shiki
=========================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 31, 2002 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Sideboard Staff











Like most of the other pro players, Morita has chosen to go with Psychatog. Shiki, a Regional Champion, is playing a Trenches variant, a matchup which is being repeated again and again today. 

![Kazumasa Shiki](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat02/969.jpg)*Kazumasa Shiki* Game one started slowly for Morita, who missed a land drop and ended up getting his [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) burned by Fire. With Morita stuck at three lands, Shiki tried to play a [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage), only to have it countered. He had backup, however, and brought out another, naming [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse). Finally drawing into lands, Morita built up his mana base for a few turns, then sparked a counter war by playing a [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). On the losing end of that battle, Shiki used a main turn [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) to draw a Flametongue, only to have Morita reset the game on the following turn with [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). Both players proceeded to play conservatively, wary of each other's counterspell ability. After several turns of draw-go, Morita played a [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) in Shiki's end phase, starting a counterspell war that drew off a few cards from both player's hands. The [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) ended up being countered, but it was all part of the plan, as Morita dropped [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) during his turn, while Shiki could only look at his tapped lands and nod.

The next turn, Shiki used the same tactic, forcing through a [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel) after drawing a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) from Morita's hand using [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage). He conceded the following turn, however, when after having his hand stripped with a kicked [Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe), Morita attacked with the [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). 

![Masahiko Morita](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat02/968.jpg)*Masahiko Morita* After sideboarding by both players, the second game began with draw-go for several turns. It was Morita who made the first move, [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ing Shiki and taking a Trenches. He left a Fact or Fiction there, countering it the next turn when Shiki tried to play it. Morita began riffling through his deck with [Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe), [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), and [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), looking for [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), while Shiki merely looked on and continued to play lands. After burning through over half his deck, Morita finally found a pair of [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)s. Shiki countered one, but a [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) from Morita revealed that Shiki was out of tricks. Confident that his opponent couldn't stop him, Morita played [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), followed by the second [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), leading to Shiki's concession. 

**Results: Morita wins 2-0**
