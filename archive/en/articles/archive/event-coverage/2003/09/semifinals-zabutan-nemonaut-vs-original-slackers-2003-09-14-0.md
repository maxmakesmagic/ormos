Semifinals: Zabutan Nemonaut vs. Original Slackers
==================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 14, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_311.png)
By Tomi Walamies











Just say no.

That's what Morgan Karlsson and Olle Rade were thinking when Rickard Osterberg asked them to join him on a trip to Oslo. The purpose was to play a team qualifier. The guys were qualified anyway, but figured the extra practice and potential money wouldn't hurt. Rickard ended up being persuaded by Lovre and Jake to give it a shot with them, and theyswon. Rickard felt comfortable with the new team, and decided to play with them instead, leaving his homeboys without an invite. The Original Slackers went 4-2 in the Team Sealed and then 5-0 in Draft, and here they are now in the semifinals.

Osterberg's opponent this round, Eugene Harvey, is widely respected as a Limited player. He also plays piano, but that is all I know--maybe there is an equally interesting story behind their team. He thinks that his white-black deck has an edge over Rickard's white-red due to superior speed, but is a bit worried about the late game. Rickard believes himself to be the favorite since he has better Clerics and a more stable deck. A problem for Rickard, though, is the fact that Eugene's [Daru Sanctifier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Sanctifier) and [Aven Liberator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Liberator) work well against triple [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism).

"English only, please," Gary Wise told his Swedish-speaking opponents before the playing started. Interestingly enough, those were the exact same words spoken to Gary by the English deportation agency some months ago.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptbos03/a792.jpg)Eugene played first, and Rickard took a mulligan. Eugene dropped Farseer and morph, while Rickard had a morph. Eugene played [Stoic Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoic+Champion), and Rickard followed with another morph. The next turn Eugene all-out attacked; morphs blocked the morph and Champ. After damage was on the stack, Eugene flipped the Liberator to save itself, and his opponent showed a [Zombie Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Cutthroat). The Swede was dangerously low on life facing a 3/3 and a 2/3 flier, but managed to dispose of the former with a [Crested Craghorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crested+Craghorn). Scales beefed up the Liberator and enabled it to do double-duty on offense and defense. Rickard dropped a [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) but was still in serious trouble.

But then something weird happened. Eugene stopped hitting with the 3/5 flier and merely put [Lingering Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Death) on the Bomber. [Wipe Clean](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wipe+Clean) took care of that, and the 3/4 kept attacking. Eugene was just standing there while Rickard dropped more guys in the form of [Daru Spiritualist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Spiritualist) and [Aven Redeemer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Redeemer). The American had way too many lands, and Rickard put out an [Aven Farseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Farseer). 

Harvey tried to [Misery Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misery+Charm) away Rickard's fresh [Aven Redeemer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Redeemer), but [Daru Mender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Mender) put an end to such nonsense. Eugene drew a [Starlight Invoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starlight+Invoker) and started using it, but Rickard was hitting for more and more every turn. As Cutthroat, morph, Farseer, and [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) brought it on, the Liberator finally joined the action by blocking. Osterberg morphed out a Lancer to do more damage. 

Eugene was going low on life and showed true desperation by playing a face-up Sanctifier. This was due to the opposing Farseer, but still not the play one wants to make against the original [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) deck. Rickard Pacified the Liberator and attacked lethally with four fliers.

Rickard 1 - 0 Eugene

After the match, Eugene said that he forgot the Liberator's total toughness of 5 and would have won the game by playing correctly. But now it is time for the second game.

Both players played Farseers, and then the [Birds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds) traded in the air. Eugene had a Husk, but Rickard merely cycled a [Forgotten Cave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forgotten+Cave) during his turn. Eugene had no more creatures, but he did take care of a Redeemer with a [Misery Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misery+Charm). Osterberg got the mana for a [Dawn Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawn+Elemental), and Eugene played [Gempalm Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gempalm+Avenger). Then both players dropped Redeemers.

Rickard got some damage in with the Elemental and hard-cast [Daru Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Lancer). At that point, Eugene once again ran out of steam and just sat there waiting. Redeemers made the combat steps seem a lot less intensive. Eugene dropped Scales on the Redeemer and played a morph, showing that he was holding back the Scales while waiting for an answer to a possible [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism). The Swede hit with the Elemental, which got blocked by the Redeemer. Damage went on the stack, and then Rickard targeted the Redeemer with [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber). Sanctifier took care of the Scales, but Eugene had [Defender of the Order](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defender+of+the+Order) to save his guys.

At this point, both Mike and Gary have lost their matches, so Eugene scooped it up.

**Final Result:** Osterberg defeats Harvey 2-0
