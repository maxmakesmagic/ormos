The November 10, 2016 Update

Today's ~~Must~~ *Commander (2016)* Musts
=========================================


1. [*Commander 2016* Review!](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/34012_Commander-2016-Review.html) | StarCityGames.com | Sheldon Menery

This one is on Yidris, for when you like [Maelstrom Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Wanderer), but "cascade, cascade" just isn't enough cascade.

Gathering Magic has two Commander pieces today you could check out, [one from Jason Alt](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/jasonalt-11102016-reach-out-and-hit-someone/) on combining [Silas Renn, Seeker Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silas+Renn%2C+Seeker+Adept) and [Sidar Kondo of Jamuraa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidar+Kondo+of+Jamuraa), and [another from Brandon Isleib](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/brandonisleib-11102016-lifelink-partners-tymna-and-bruse-tarl/) on the pairing of [Tymna the Weaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tymna+the+Weaver) and [Bruse Tarl, Boorish Herder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bruse+Tarl%2C+Boorish+Herder). Top 25
======


1. Round and round the rankings go

For yet another week, we have yet another change at the very top of the rankings. A new, old, new, old No. 1 has claimed the throne—Seth Manfield!


Thanks to his Top 50 finish at [Grand Prix Dallas/Fort Worth](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpdfw16), Manfield gained just enough of an edge on Yasooka to once again climb into the No. 1 slot. A single point separates them, so don't be surprised to see things change again soon.


Meanwhile, Samuel Pardee, Ryichi Tamada, and Matthew Nass all jumped way up in the rankings, and Brian Braun-Duin took his Modern Dredge deck to a Top 8 and subsequently to No. 22 on our charts. The World Champion is quickly becoming one of the most reliable Grand Prix performers in the game, so I wouldn't expect him to drop off either.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Aetherworks Delirium](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2016-11-09)

[Aetherworks Marvel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) was a card that was everywhere at [Pro Tour *Kaladesh*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptkld)—and then nowhere. Turns out people figured out how to beat the one-dimensional deck pretty quickly, and instead White-Blue Flash (a major foil to Marvel) and Black-Green Delirium (a major foil to Flash) rose to the top of the metagame.


So *Magic Online* user Yukynag decided to just combine Marvel with Black-Green Delirium and bypass that whole Flash thing.


The deck below, which went 5-0 in a Competitive Standard League, is part Delirium, part [Aetherworks Marvel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel), and all powerful. It has the potential for a fourth-turn [Emrakul, the Promised End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) or [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger), but it can also sit behind [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) or jazz up its graveyard enough to simply cast Emrakul.


Note the transformational sideboard that lets Yukynag go straight-up Delirium when the matchup calls for it. What kind of surprise would you get when you prepare for [Aetherworks Marvel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) and instead get served up a second-turn [Grim Flayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer)? Not the good kind.


Could this be the future that gets [Aetherworks Marvel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) back in the limelight? Who knows, but it would make for a heck of a Friday Night *Magic* surprise.
