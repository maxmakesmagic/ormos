Watanabe Steels the Show in Pittsburgh
======================================




August 27-28, 2011












Grand Prix Pittsburgh 2011
--------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/winner_620.JPG)It's three consecutive Grand Prix now. Coming into Pittsburgh, Yuuya Watanabe had finished second at Grand Prix Kansas City and then rectified his *poor* performance by just outright winning Grand Prix Shanghai last weekend! After his performance this weekend, he's made it 2nd, 1st, 1st. About the only thing he can do to improve that is win a Pro Tour, and he's got a chance next week, just across the state! Thanks to his performances over the last month, he has gone from an afterthought for Player of the Year to a severe threat to take the title for the second time, especially if he continues to play as well as he has been. This truly has been the summer of the Yuuya.


Coming into the event, it was clear to everyone that [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)[Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine) was the deck to beat. No matter whom we asked we didn't get any answer other than that. However, if we learned anything from the first day of play, it was that maybe players should have been paying more attention to [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) decks. They comprised a much larger portion of the winning decks than [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)[Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine) did, especially if you add up all of the varieties. With each event, the preferred method of assembling the combo seems to change. Early on, it was definitely the RUG Pod version of the deck that had the most traction, but for this event it appeared that the UR version had the most believers. Ultimately, only the UR variety made the Top 8, eventually losing to Yuuya Watanabe on his eventual path to victory. While it's true he is playing [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk), there is a big difference between losing to [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk) and losing to Yuuya Watanabe, especially right now.


Congratulations to Yuuya Watanabe, your Grand Prix Pittsburgh 2011 champion!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/qf%20roundup.jpg)

 


### Matt Nass vs. Yuuya Watanabe


This game started as all [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk) [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine) vs. [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) matches do. Watanabe started searching out his creatures with an early [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) while Nass searched and dg through his deck, attempting to find the cards he needed to assemble the combo. Watanabe never let up, recruiting more [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s and an [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) and her Birds over the next turns. Nass had nothing. He eventually had to use an [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) to return the [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel), but it didn't buy him enough time. He got to get a [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) down to stop an attack from the Angel on the following turn, but Watanabe just activate his [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) and attacked for the win.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/qf%20nass.jpg)
*The Nass-ty One himself.*

Matt Nass 0 – Yuuya Watanabe 1


The next game saw things start a little better for Nass, mostly because they didn't start as well for Watanabe. He only had a [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) for early aggression, and, while that grew to more [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s soon, it wasn't nearly as bad as the [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel). Nass was able to get a [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) into play for a method of killing [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s and things looked ok.


And then Watanabe drew [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel). It was just big enough that Nass didn't have a clear way to kill it at hand. He dealt with it for a few turns with [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil)s and [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)s, but Watanabe's air force started to swell, little by little. Nass had multiple [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Deceiver%2BExarch)s in play to equip, but he never found a clear window to land a [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), and Watanabe's Birds pecked him to death fairly rapidly.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/qf%20watanabe.jpg)
*Watanabe could never have done it with a little help from LSV...tokens.*

**Matt Nass 0 – Yuuya Watanabe 2**


 


### Joel Larsson vs. Lukasz Musial


Musial started with a blistering draw, getting a [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite), [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) into play all on the first turn thanks to a [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal). A [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) joined in time to get one counter, and his force started slamming into Larsson. Larsson stopped the bleeding some with an [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) on the Overseer and a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) for a [Porcelain Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porcelain+Legionnaire), but the damage had been done. He got one more turn of reprieve thanks to a [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura), but the turn after that, he succumbed to Musial's metallic army.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/qf%20Larsson.jpg)
*Larsson couldn't rust Musial's Steel Squad.*

Joel Larsson 0 – Lucasz Musial 1


Larsson got to slow things down a *ton* the second game when he used [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) to kill Musial's [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) in response to a [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk). However, Musial sped them right back up with a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel), [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge), and a [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol). A [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) traded for the [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge), but Musial just replaced it with a [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) and a second [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel). [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) once again made a token attempt to stop Musial's team, but it only took a turn for them to finish the planeswalker off. After that, Musial had already gotten Larsson low enough that a single swing finished him off.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/qf%20musial.jpg)

------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin* 

German pro Florian Pils is one of many international players who decided to take a pit-stop to compete in Grand Prix Pittsburgh on their way to Pro Tour Philadelphia. Across the table from Pils is Max Tietze – a long time New York area player with a number of Pro Tours under his belt, playing in his second Grand Prix Top 8.


Game one


Pils got off to a fast start with a turn one [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), while Tietze tried to set up some defense with a [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite). Tietze cast a [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk), which chump-blocked a Teetering Peaked [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), before Pils further developed his board with an [Ember Hauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Hauler)


Tietze's [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) traded in combat with a [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge), but Tietze replaced it with another [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) a turn later.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/QF%20Florian%20Pils.JPG)
*Florian Pils*

Pils attacked into the [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) with his [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), and his [Teetering Peaks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks) enhanced [Ember Hauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Hauler). Tietze thought for a bit, but ultimately took the six damage – falling to 9.


Pils cast a post-combat [Shrine of Burning Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Burning+Rage) before passing the turn to Tietze who attacked in with his [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold), generating two tokens and knocking Pils down to 11, before adding two copies of [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) to his suddenly fearsome board.


Pils again attacked with his [Ember Hauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Hauler), and his [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), prompting Tietze to carefully considered his options before ultimately deciding to double block the incoming [Ember Hauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Hauler) with his two hawks.


[Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) targeting Tietze resolved to knock the American down to 6 and tick up Pils's [Shrine of Burning Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Burning+Rage) to two counters.

The copy had already returned the [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) he'd killed with a Slime earlier, and the recursion might be too much to handle. Thankfully, he still had an [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) in play, which might discourage Watanabe from attacking with his Titan.


Eventually, Chapin chose to allow the [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) to die, killing it with his [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) and chumping the Titan. On his next attack, Chapin's [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) ate the Image and two [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer)s, leaving Yuuya with a Titan and a Golem. Things looked like they were starting to turn slightly in Chapin's favor when disaster struck. With only a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) and a random dork as blockers, Chapin found himself a creature short. Watanabe activated his Colonnade and sent his team. He used the [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) to return a [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) to play, giving him enough mana to tap his Colonnade and the rest of his lands to use [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) to kill Chapin's Image, leaving him with one blocker for Watanabe's three attackers. He had no answer and conceded, ending an incredibly tight final game.


Patrick Chapin 1 – Yuuya Watanabe 2




Final - Lukasz Musial vs. Yuuya Watanabe
----------------------------------------


*by Nate Price* 

With Heinz Field just down the street and black and gold flying all around, could you have imagined a better scenario than a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck making it into the finals of Grand Prix Pittsburgh? Watching Musial climb through the Top 8 without dropping a game made him seem more like a robot, mercilessly crushing all who stood in his way. On the other side of the field, Watanabe had to eke out an incredibly close and complex win to make it to this point. For him, though, that's all in a Grand Prix's work. This marked his third consecutive Grand Prix finals appearance. I'm not talking the last three he has played in (though that's true, too). I'm talking the last three Grand Prix held, which he attended and *dominated*. Could Musial steel himself against the blades of Watanabe?


### Game One


The first game started out incredibly aggressively. Musial struck first, making a pair of [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)s and a [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) on his first turn. Watanabe had a [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) to slow things down, but Musial had a [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) to make sure his team would be able to attack through on the next turn. Knowing this, Watanabe went searching for an answer finding a [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) with a [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), which he used to kill the inactive Overseer. When Musial found a [Contested War Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone) on the following turn, he began a dangerous game. He sent his team, attacking Watanabe down to 12, losing his [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) in the process.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/F%20Musial.jpg)
*Lukasz Musial, better known as the Man of Steel.*

Watanabe made a replacement for his bravely sacrificed [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk) and passed the turn. Musial untapped and began to think. Needing to keep Watanabe on the defensive, he sent his two [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)s. Watanabe traded his [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk) for a [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite), dropping Musial to a lone [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite). When Musial tried to make a [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk), Watanabe dropped to 6 to [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) the last [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite), killing the [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk) as well.


Things started to look bad for Musiel. He was in danger of losing his first game of the Top 8. He had no choice but to activate and attack with his pair of [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus)es, putting Watanabe to a meaningless two poison. Watanabe started attacking back, stealing the [Contested War Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone) from Musial. He then added a [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) to his side, giving him more artifacts in play than the [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck. Musial had no choice but to swing back again with his Nexuses, reclaiming his [Contested War Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Contested%2BWar%2BZone). Unfortunately, Musial couldn't deal with an equipped [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk). Watanabe attacked, untapping the re-stolen [Contested War Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Contested%2BWar%2BZone), and used it to cast [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura). With no way to beat through the defenses Watanabe had assembled, Musial conceded and went for another try.


Lukasz Musial 0 – Yuuya Watanabe 1


### Game Two


Taking a page from Tomoharu Saito, Watanabe gave himself a couple quick slaps after presenting his deck to Musial. Musial got a slap in the face of his own, but it was considerably less pleasant. His opening seven cards had no gas whatsoever, and he was forced to mulligan on the play while down a game in the finals of a Grand Prix. I can tell you from personal experience (editor's note: no he can't): that feels miserable. His next six were acceptable, and he started with a first-turn [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest). Watanabe had a [Mental Misstep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep) for it, though, and Musial looked understandably displeased. He did manage to make a [Shrine of Loyal Legions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Loyal+Legions) on the following turn, though, which was incredibly powerful against the slower control decks in the field.


Watanabe's deck wasn't as slow as the other control decks, though. He started to build a Squadron of [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s, which could easily overrun Musial before his Shrine became relevant. Musial added a [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) and a [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) to his board and passed the turn. Watanabe went ahead and added a second [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) to his side, leaving a sole land untapped. Into this calm, Musial struck. He cast a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel), making his little monsters slightly less little monsters. They swung in, dropping Watanabe to 12. The Steel also added a third counter to his Shrine, which looked more and more like it might decide the game, especially if Watanabe were forced to use an [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) on the [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) instead of it. He added a [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) to his board and passed the turn. On his turn, Watanabe added a third [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk) to his team and passed the turn back with two mana available.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/F%20watanabe.jpg)
*Which I guess makes Yuuya Watanabe kryptonite.*

Musial swung in with his team. Watanabe carefully lined each of his [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s up with an attacker and returned the [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) to his hand with [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil). All of the creatures died. Musial made a [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol) and passed the turn. On his turn, Watanabe dealt with the [Shrine of Loyal Legions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Loyal+Legions), too, hiding it under an [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring). When Musial activated his [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol) and attacked, Watanabe threw his last two [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s away, clearing the board. With everything else gone, Watanabe cemented his advantage with an [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel), making tokens and attacking the helpless Musial. One more turn and it was over.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppit11/F%20handshake.jpg)

**Yuuya Watanabe defeats Lukasz Musial 2-0 to become the Grand Prix Pittsburgh 2011 champion!**




Top 5 Cards
-----------




![Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phantasmal+Image)

**[Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image)**


There has been no card quite as versatile this weekend as [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image). Just like [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) before it (or alongside it in some cases), it allows players who can cast it to have a cheap answer to most situations they come across. We've seen it turn into Titans, [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime)s, Heros of both [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Hero%2Bof%2BBladehold) and [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Hero%2Bof%2BOxid%2BRidge), even [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) at one point. And all of that for a mere two mana. In Yuuya Watanabe's winning deck, it combined with [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) to do filthy things, especially against opponents whose creatures had enters the battlefield effects. It was a cornerstone of his deck, a large reason why he won the Grand Prix, and incredibly deserving of the title "Top Card at Grand Prix Pittsburgh."



![Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tempered+Steel)

**[Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel)**


Players had almost completely written off [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) in the weeks leading up to Grand Prix Pittsburgh. But as players started shaving their Nature's Claims from their sideboards, and their [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment)s from their maindecks suddenly the hyper aggressive artifact deck became a potent choice. Lukasz Musial took advantage of the fact that he was facing a field full of unprepared opponents, taking his metal army all the way to a second place finish.



![Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Splinter+Twin)

**[Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin)**


Throughout the weekend, [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)/[Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) decks were seen tearing up the top tables at Grand Prix Pittsburgh. At the end of Day One, five of the seven undefeated decks sported the killer combo, and while their ranks thinned a bit by the end of the tournament – putting only Matt Nass into the Top 8 – it's clear that [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) is going to remain one of the defining decks of Standard until the release of *Innistrad*.



![Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blade+Splicer)

**[Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer)**


[Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) played a key role in Yuuya Watanabe's Grand Prix winning [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)[Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine) deck – giving his deck a source of early pressure against control decks, multiple blockers against aggressive decks, and two bodies capable of holding swords against decks that are chock full of removal. Expect to see [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) rise dramatically in popularity in the coming weeks.



![Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge)

**[Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge)**


Who says that red decks can't beat [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements)? While the white sideboard card can be quite a pain for unprepared red decks -- it only takes a single [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) for a player to go from being unable to break through an army of soldier tokens, and [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s, to having 4, 10, or even 13 points of unblockable damage.
