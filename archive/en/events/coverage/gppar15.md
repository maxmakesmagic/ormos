TOP 8 COVERAGE



It's been an amazing weekend of Magic here in sunny Paris, France. 1,426 players had entered the tournament Saturday morning, full of hopes for fame and glory, but by nightfall all but 171 of them had already been eliminated. Sunday's six rounds further reduced that number to eight. These eight included Pro Tour *Fate Reforged* champion Antonio Del Moral León as well as Zan Syed, coming fresh off his first Top 8 at Grand Prix Miami two months ago. But in the end it was little-known Amand Dosimont of Belgium who walked away with the trophy after dispatching both Del Moral León and Syed in the semifinals and finals, respectively.


Green, spearheaded by the duo of Den Protector and Deathmist Raptor, had been the dominant color of the tournament. Overall, Standard showed a great variety of viable decks, not least because no two megamorph decks looked exactly alike. Collected Company made a splash as did Crackling Doom as did Thunderbreak Regent. As had happened in the past and surely would happen again in the future, however, the final points of damage were delivered by Siege Rhino.


Congratulations to Amand Dosimont, champion of Grand Prix Paris 2015!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Welcome back for Day 2 of Grand Prix Paris 2015! The original field of 1,426 players has been reduced to a mere 171, and these 171, everyone who managed to escape yesterday's rounds with a record of 7-2 or better, are back for more Standard.


So far, we have seen lots of Den Protectors and Deathmist Raptors, naturally quite a few copies of Dragonlord Ojutai, and of course many, many Goblin tokens charging across the battlefields of the event. We have also seen an uptick in the popularity of Mardu Dragons with main-deck Crackling Doom to combat Ojutai and sideboarded Anger of the Gods to hose megamorphs. And we have seen Collected Company, both in more traditional green-white megamorph strategies as well as in one particular four-colored deck. That deck, featuring not only Savage Knuckleblade but Mantis Rider too, managed to stay undefeated yesterday and was clearly one of the big stories of the weekend so far!


So yes, there's still a lot to find out about Standard. And we will. Join us!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



It all began with 1,426 players in the morning, but through nine grueling rounds of Standard 1,255 players were eliminated—lost in the deathmist, roasted by some dragon's stormbreath, et cetera. Now, at the end of Day 1, 171 players remain in the running and will return in the morning to fight for their shot at the fame, the glory, and the trophy.


Among those 171, four players stand above all the rest, with perfect records of 9-0: Manuel Faber, Jochen Frenster, Amand Dosimont, and Yohan Dudognon who played an innovative Collected Company deck featuring both Mantis Rider and Savage Knuckleblade. These five may have a head start on the second day, but big names like Robin Dolar, Joel Larsson, Jérémy Dezani, Lukas Blohon, Pierre Dagen, Javier Dominguez, Tomoharu Saito, and Martin Jůza aren't far behind.


Tune in again tomorrow, when the tournament enters its home stretch, when the Top 8 gets determined, and when finally a new champion emerges.
