2004 World Championships Feature Match
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 5, 2004 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20171224024527im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_MikeTurian.jpg?itok=LXCQPehw)
By Mike Turian




Before joining Magic Online as its digital product manager, Mike worked as a producer for Wizards's technology department, a product manager for organized play, and as an R&D lead developer. He has played Magic Online since it came out in 2002 and Magic since The Dark. 







![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/sf1_1.jpg)


It didn't take long for Manuel Bevand to knock the rust off his game, defeating top-seeded Kamiel Cornelissen 3-1 in the quarterfinals. Rookie Aeo Paquette also knocked off a favored player -- soon-to-be-crowned Player of the Year Gabriel Nassif.


Bevand went 6-0 during the Standard portion while Paquette went 5-1, proving there was some serious juice behind these darkhorses' pursuit of the individual title.


### Game 1


Bevand's deck revolved around the [Krark-Clan Ironworks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krark-Clan+Ironworks)/[Myr Incubator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Incubator) combo while Paquette was playing Affinity. He led off with a Vault of the Whispers. Paquette played a [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) and [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) before passing the turn.


Bevand had a much more explosive turn two. He dropped out a Talisman, a non-imprinted [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) before casting [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast). Paquette played down an [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker) and [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter).


Turn three and four were where Bevand's deck can quickly become lethal. He defeated Cornelissen on turn three multiple games in the quarterfinals. After laying a [Great Furnace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Furnace), Bevand began to think. Eventually he decided on a [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) and a [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge). Paquette's forces had not put Bevand on a short clock, with his next attack putting Bevand on 16. Paquette threw a preemptive strike at Bevand, hitting him with a [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) and reducing him to 10 life.


Bevand played out a [Krark-Clan Ironworks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krark-Clan+Ironworks) but didn't have the rest of the combo pieces to finish off Paquette. Bevand played [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) in the following turn. Once again Paquette got in a small attack that left Bevand at 6. Bevand tried a [Condescend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend) on Paquette's [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker), but this was just the opportunity Paquette was looking for finishing off Bevand with a second [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast).


Aeo Paquette 1, Manuel Bevand - 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/sf1_2.jpg)"I just wanted to let you know I countered your [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker)," said Bevand while sideboarding for game 2.


"What?" Paquette replied.


"See I wanted to let you know we interacted," Bevand replied with a smile. Ah, combo decks.


### Game 2


Bevand played a land while Paquette played [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox), land, Disciple of he Vault and an [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker). On his second turn Bevand played a [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) and a UB Talisman.


Paquette could only watch helplessly as Bevand uses a [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) to wipe out Paquette's board. Paquette starts to search with a [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) and again.


Bevand passed his turn with six mana untapped plus a full [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism). He used the mana to [Condescend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend) a [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) from Paquette. Paquette had planned on playing a [Furnace Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon). With the [Condescend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend), Bevand found a [Furnace Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon) of his own which wiped the board clear and won him the game.


Aeo Paquette 1, Manuel Bevand 1


### Game 3


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/sf1_3.jpg)As Paquette took a mulligan to start game 3, the two players talked about the potential finals match ups.


Bevand was rooting for the [Astral Slide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide) deck to win calling the match up a bye. He was a little unsteadier when it came to Goblins saying it could go either way in five games. Paquette said that he would rather face down Goblins than Slide but that neither are especially good match ups for his deck.


Paquette had a turn-one [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker) and a turn-three [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) for the beatdown while Bevand just played out land. A turn-four [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) was [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed by Bevand. The Ravager would have been a much faster clock than the three a turn from the Worker and [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite).


After an attack that put Bevand to 12 life, Paquette cast another [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker). Bevand let the Worker in but used some card drawing when he cast [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge)


Paquette attacked Bevand down to 8 life. Each turn Bevand would lay a land and say go without casting anything. Paquette thought and then decided to cast a [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) that resolved leaving Bevand at three life. He died when attacked by [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker).


Aeo Paquette 2, Manuel Bevand 1


### Game 4


Neither Paquette nor Bevand have any early plays in game 4. The only spell cast was a second-turn [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) from Paquette. The hoard of little artifact creatures did not come out, as they are accustomed to doing.


Bevand searched into his library looking for some business spells with a [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge). When he tried a [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) Aeo was quick to [Annul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annul) it. Paquette probably remembered how much the Prism helped Bevand cast [Furnace Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon) game 2.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/sf1_4.jpg)Paquette played a [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) followed by a [Furnace Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon). The Dragon was [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed by Bevand. Bevand does get a [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) into play on the following turn but has no other play.


Paquette tried another [Seething Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) followed once again by [Furnace Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon). This time the [Furnace Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon) resolved, wiping out the board. Bevand didn't play another land for the rest of the game and died to the [Furnace Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Dragon).


**Final Score**  

Aeo Paquette 3, Manuel Bevand 1
