Round 13 Feature Match: Lingering Threats
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 11, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Tim Willoughby












[Christian Calcano (White Weenie) vs. Robert Jurkovic (Blue-Black Control)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_12)


Jon Finkel (Delver Spirits) vs. Shouta Yasooka (Blue-Black Control)
-------------------------------------------------------------------


[Jason Schousboe (Wolf Run Aggro) vs. Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa (Wolf Run Ramp)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_14)


"Howdy, I'm Jon," smiled Jon Finkel as he approached the feature match area to play against Shouta Yasooka of Japan. Yasooka beamed back, and introduced himself with a handshake. It was a comparatively lo-fi meeting considering that Finkel is one of the consensus best players ever to have played **Magic**, and Yasooka is a former Player of the Year himself, known for playing innovative control decks at the very highest level, something he had been doing throughout this Pro Tour.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r13_finkel_vs_yasooka.jpg)
*Finkel and Yasooka exchange pleasant and humble formalities.*

### Game 1



![Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drogskol+Captain)

Finkel looked on as Yasooka rolled an 11 on two dice. He picked them up and rolled a 12 for himself, electing to play. He led off with [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) seeing two copies of [Ratchet Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb), [Darkslick Shores](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkslick+Shores), [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), and [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper). Yasooka's hand looked solid, but possibly less so now that Finkel had full information on it.


The next play of the game was the same [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), thanks to a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) that was offed by [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). A [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) came from Finkel, but it too fell, this time to [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil). Finkel remained unperturbed. He sniffed and cast another [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain), and seemed satisfied when all Yasooka had for his own turn was [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) and [Ratchet Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb). Tellingly, Yasooka had not used [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Liliana%2BVess) to force a discard. It would not help keep the planeswalker alive, and the Japanese Pro clearly valued an extra card in his hand more than an extra card for Finkel.


Finkel cast a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to get another [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain), and [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) to take out [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), before attacking [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Liliana%2BVess) to finish her off. Yasooka simply kept playing land, and went for it with [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper). There was a [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) for the big vampire, and [Revoke Existence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence) to ensure that [Ratchet Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) did not pose an undue threat.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r13_finkel.jpg)
*Finkel, calmly and casually ridding himself of all the things.*

Finkel was out of cards by the time that [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) came out a second time, and the Hall of Famer still had 11 damage to deal. He attacked Yasooka to 6, knowing that the former Player of the Year would not want to lose his [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) by blocking. While Yasooka had a [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) to potentially gain back all the life that two [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain)s could deal, a [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) spirit at end of turn meant that Finkel had just enough power to be able to race both of Yasooka's powerful rares before they could take control of the game.


Jon Finkel 1, Shouta Yasooka 0


### Game 2


Yasooka was in the play for the second game, and he used a [Despise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Despise) to take a [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) from Finkel's hand. He had a [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) ready for a second one, and a [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) to follow up. [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) from Finkel made a couple of [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) tokens, and Yasooka passed without a fourth land drop, which must have felt good for Finkel. Yasooka had just one [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and two [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s, meaning he could not do anything of substance as Finkel flashed back [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and built up a force, along with a [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) on [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r13_yasooka.jpg)
*Yasooka's control deck struggles to cough up the lands he needs.*

[Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) meant that Yasooka dropped to 10 on attacks. He knocked on his deck, looking for a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) to be able to cast [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith), but only found [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to copy the captain. When Finkel had another [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) the next turn, it was all over at lightning speed.


Jon Finkel 2, Shouta Yasooka 0
