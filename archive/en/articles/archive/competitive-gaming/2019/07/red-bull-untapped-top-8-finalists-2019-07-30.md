Red Bull Untapped Top 8 Finalists
=================================



 Posted in **Competitive Gaming**
 on July 30, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_authorpic_larger.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











![Red Bull Untapped](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/evojkpuqY9.png)


The competitors for the Red Bull Untapped Finals are set and ready to face off for their share of the $60,000 prize pool. Before you follow all the action live on [twitch.tv/redbull](https://www.twitch.tv/redbull), let's take a moment to meet the Top 8!


Anton De Smet
=============


![Anton De Smet](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Anton.jpg)


Anton De Smet won the offline qualifier event in Brussels, Belgium, his home country. He's been playing *Magic* for fifteen years, and this is his first win in a major tournament. His favorite card is [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman).



![Anton De Smet decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Anton%20De%20Smet%20decklist.jpg)*Anton De Smet's decklist from the offline qualifier event in Brussels*
Ricardo Beja
============


![Ricardo Beja](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Ricardo%20Beja.jpg)


2017 Portuguese National Champion Ricardo Beja was the runner-up in Brussels. He's been playing *Magic* for fifteen years, and his favorite card is [Teferi, Time Raveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Time+Raveler).



![Ricardo Beja decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Ricardo%20Beja%20decklist.jpg)*Ricardo Beja's decklist from the offline event in Brussels*
Javier Dominguez
================


![Javier Dominguez](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Javier%20Dominguez.jpg)


Javier Dominguez of Spain is the 2018 World Champion. He was runner-up at the 2017 World Championship and has had two Mythic Championship Top 8 finishes and ten Grand Prix Top 8s in his career. He qualified for the Red Bull Untapped Finals by winning the offline event in Florence, Italy.



![Javier Dominguez decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Javier%20Dominguez%20decklist.jpg)*Javier Dominguez's decklist from the offline event in Florence*
Luca Simonato
=============


![Luca Simonato](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Luca%20Simonato.jpg)


Luca Simonato was the runner-up in his home country of Italy. He has been playing *Magic* for five years and previously also qualified for Mythic Championship I in Cleveland. His favorite card is [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch).



![Luca Simonato decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Luca%20Simonato%20decklist.jpg)*Luca Simonato's decklist from the Florence qualifier*
Lino Burgold
============


![Lino Burgold](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Lino%20Burgold.jpg)


Lino Burgold earned his spot in the finals by winning Online Qualifier II. The 2009 Rookie of the Year has been playing *Magic* for 22 years, and he has three Grand Prix Top 8 finishes with one win.



![Lino Burgold decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Lino%20Burgold%20decklist.jpg)*Lino Burgold's decklist from Online Qualifier II*
Thomas Holzinger
================


![Thomas Holzinger](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Thomas%20Holzinger.jpg)


Thomas Holzinger is the runner-up from Online Qualifier II. He was back-to-back Austrian National Champion in 2012 and 2013, and he has one Pro Tour Top 8 finish, three Grand Prix Top 8s, and a Top 8 from the 2013 World *Magic* Cup with the Austrian National Team.



![Thomas Holzinger decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Thomas%20Holzinger%20decklist.png)*Thomas Holzinger's decklist from Online Qualifier II*
José Neves
==========


![José Neves](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Jose%20Neves.jpg)


Portuguese player José Neves locked in his Finals seat by winning Online Qualifier I. He's been playing *Magic* since *Eighth Edition*, and this is his first major competitive achievement.



![José Neves decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Jose%20Neves%20decklist.jpg)*José Neves's decklist from Online Qualifier I*
Alex Majlaton
=============


![Alex Majlaton](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Alex%20Majlaton.jpg)


American Alex Majlaton was the runner-up in Online Qualifier I. He's been playing *Magic* for fifteen years and has appeared in 35 Pro Tour/Mythic Championship events. He has one Mythic Championship Top 8 finish and eight Grand Prix Top 8s.



![Alex Majlaton decklist](https://media.wizards.com/2019/images/daily/Alex%20Majlaton%20decklist.png)*Alex Majlaton's decklist from**Online Qualifier I*
