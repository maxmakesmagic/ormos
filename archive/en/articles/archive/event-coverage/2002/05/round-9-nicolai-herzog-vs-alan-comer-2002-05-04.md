Round 9: Nicolai Herzog vs. Alan Comer
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 4, 2002 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Sideboard Staff











Alan Comer is the popular American with a fondness for unusual "tech" and a number of high level Pro Tour finishes. Nicolai Herzog is a former European Champion and regular member of the Norwegian national team.

Comer has a slowish red-black deck with bombs. Herzog has a very good white-blue deck splashing red for [Chainflinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger) and other removal. He's not short of bombs of his own with an [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) to reset the board to his advantage.

### Game 1

"Can you believe *Torment* was bad for me," Comer said after his red-black deck dropped no play until turn 4. Herzog started with [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) and gained some life off [Teroh's Faithful](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful). 

![Cabal Surgeon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cabal+Surgeon)"Good gracious, you're playing that!?" Herzog said when Comer summoned a [Cabal Surgeon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Surgeon)."It was bad for me," Comer answered, although his next creature was a more acceptable [Soul Scourge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge).

Herzog sent it to the top of Comer's library with [Repel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repel).

Comer didn't recast it as a [Cabal Patriarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Patriarch) was a much more inviting proposition.

Herzog didn't have a counterspell and could only bounce it with an [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst). Next turn it returned and the Aven returned itself to Herzog's hand rather than beat itself to death with a [Crackling Club](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Club).

"You know the best thing to do with a filthy Cur."

"I think I have an idea."

The [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) went back to Herzog's hand once more.

The Norwegian had his own bomb â€“ [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). Both players got to restart except Herzog now had threshold an a 2/3 pro-black [Mystic Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Familiar). He followed it with a [Mystic Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Visionary) and then the [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) and there was no way Comer could recover in time.

Herzog 1-0 Comer

Comer went first and his turn 3 [Unhinge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unhinge) got them both to draw a card as Herzog discarded an [Obsessive Search](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsessive+Search).

Then a [Frightcrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler) started to race a [Nomad Decoy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Decoy). A [Teroh's Vanguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Vanguard) rushing out at end of turn tipped that in Herzog's favour.

Comer went to tip it back only for Herzog to pluck his Vanguard out of a Butcher's grasp with an [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst). The Norwegian [Concentrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concentrate)d to gain some cards and dropped [Patrol Hound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound)s.

Comer recovered some card advantage by using a [Violent Eruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Eruption) to wipe Herzog's board and swung with the Butcher and [Frightcrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler).

Herzog gained the life back and a big wall in the form of [Teroh's Faithful](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful).

Comer dropped another of the big scary black creatures in his deck as Cursed Monstrocity flopped onto the table. [Repel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repel) from Herzog was annoying as Comer had to discard a card and still put the monstrosity on top of his library.

![Acceptable Losses](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Acceptable+Losses)Comer sent the Butcher and [Frightcrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler) in and Herzog summoned the [Teroh's Vanguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Vanguard) again for an instant speed blocker. Then the American unfortunately made an uncharacteristic mistake as he tried to remove the Vanguard with [Acceptable Losses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acceptable+Losses). The spell is a sorcery and as Comer had tapped his lands first he also had to take 4 mana burn as well. This prevented him from recasting the Monstrosity and the loss of life looked very relevant as a [Chainflinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger)Comer was still kicking though as he cleared the board with [Sickening Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickening+Dreams) and paved the way for the Monstrosity to return. Herzog had a [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) and a second [Nomad Decoy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Decoy) looked like it might cause the Monstrosity problems if Comer hadn't had a [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) to ping it off the board

The mana burn had been costly as it lost Comer both tempo and life and this allowed Herzog to finish the American off with a [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven) and [Mystic Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Familiar). Comer hoped he might block with the Monstrocity for a turn and then strike back with that, a [Filthy Cur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filthy+Cur) and a freshly summoned [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade). Unfortunately Herzog had a [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper).

Afterwards Herzog said he felt bad about the mana burn on the Accepable Losses but Comer was adamant that the rules had to be enforced equally on everyone to ensure a level playing field, even though in this case it may have cost him the game.

**Final Result:** Nicolai Herzog beat Alan Comer 2-0
