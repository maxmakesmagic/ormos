Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Off the Scrapheap](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2016-10-20)

This deck, which went 5-0 in a *Magic Online* Competitive Standard League, plays [Lupine Prototype](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lupine+Prototype).


There really shouldn't be much more required to get you intrigued.


This black-red list by *Magic Online* player Niederschatten is a low-to-the-ground, all-in aggressive deck with a bit of a burn component. [Bomat Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier) both refills your hand after playing everything out *and* drives [Smuggler's Copter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) pretty well for a mail-delivering robot. And [Scrapheap Scrounger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger), one of the most aggressive artifacts printed in a long time, provides a bit of resiliency that the deck might otherwise lack.


From there, you have a lot of the expected suspects. Some [Unlicensed Disintegration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration), a full helping a [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper), and even a [Key to the City](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Key+to+the+City) or two. It's aggressive, it's mean, and it [Bomat Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier)'s pretty hard. What more could you want?
