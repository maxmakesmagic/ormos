Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Jund Delirium](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/standard-mocs-2016-09-11)

Continuing with this week's theme of decks that survive rotation relatively intact. Today's deck is Jund Delirium from Bone55, who finished in 3rd place at last weekend's *Magic Online* Championship Series tournament. The deck is, at its core, a [Traverse the Ulvenwald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) Delirium deck that seeks to top things off with [Emrakul, the Promised End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) and [Distended Mindbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distended+Mindbender) while holding firm with a ton of removal.


The deck does lose a few important cards. [Nissa, Vastwood Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Vastwood+Seer) is pretty perfect for the deck's value engine. The deck can function without it—it just doesn't want to. [Languish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) is more or less irreplaceable, but with only two copies main and one in the side, the deck be able to make due with more spot removal, depending on how the metagame shapes up. Finally, [Fiery Impulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) can easily be replaced by [Galvanic Bombardment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Bombardment). The rest of the main deck stays untouched.


Plus, you'll get some new toys. Chandra, Torch of Defiance is on the top of every red mage's list, and will certainly get a look.
