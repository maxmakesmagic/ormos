Feature Match Round 12 - Mike Pustilnik vs. Chris Benafel
=========================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 12, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












The match started with two words of wisdom from Chris Benafel:


1) "I haven't played a deck with black all day, but I should beat [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)"  

And  

2) "I wish I could be a judge. There's be so much less stuff going on."


### Game One


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/909.jpg)At least before sideboarding, Chris was right on about the first observation. Mike won the roll and played first, but kept drawing cards that were useless against Benafel's creatureless deck. He stared at [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) and [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst), discarding them when his own [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)s brought him above eight cards. Benafel on the other hand kept a tight grip, and played land after land, building his hand with [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)s of his own, and keeping Mike's mana tight with the Ice half of [Fire/Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire%2FIce).


Mike attempted to slip through an early [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), but it met with a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). A later Tog was removed from the game with [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate). A third one was lost to a [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). The rest of the game was merely a formality, as Chris manhandled Mike with multiple [Prophetic Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt)s, and a [Goblin Trenches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches) which spewed forth a fair bevy of goblin soldiers.


Chris Benafel 1-Mike Pustilnik 0


### Game Two


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/910.jpg)Once again the good doctor chose to play first, and this game he came out blazing. A second turn [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) fueled the way for a third turn [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) backed by a [Gainsay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay) for Benafel's [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). This time the game went completely in the opposite direction, as Chris drew nothing but lands, and faced down an angry Tog. With his life total rapidly dwindling, Benafel played down a [Goblin Trenches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches). At the end of his turn, Dr. P used Mana Short to force Benafel's hand (Chris made 4 goblins in response), but they met with an [Engineered Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague).


Clearly shaken by this misfortune, Chris attempted to crack a Coliseum with only six cards in his graveyard, but was stopped by Mike before any harm was done. A last ditch [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) (naming [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)) simply served as a roadblock, as the smiling monster completely smashed through all defenses, starting with the hapless Pikula and continuing directly to the remainder of Benafel's life total.


Chris Benafel 1-Mike Pustilnik 1


### Game Three


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/911.jpg)Chris started out the first dozen turns the same way his previous game had began-by playing a land each turn.. The only problem was that lands were the only cards he seemed to be drawing. Mike had the exact opposite problem-he took a one-land mulligan and was stuck at three lands until the 10th turn.


Fortunately for the good doctor, an early [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) slipped through Chris's low-spell hand. It started nibbling away at Benafel's life, and soon the mana began flowing for the blue/black mage. At the same time they swapped [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)s (netting Pustilnik a [Lobotomy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobotomy) and [Exclude](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude), and Benafel a [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) and [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)), but Mike used a [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short) on the 13th turn in order to force through a [Lobotomy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobotomy) against a pair of [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage)s.


Unfortunately for each player, time was called shortly thereafter. At this point, Mike Pustilnik made a last play to try to take the game in extra turns. Due to the [Lobotomy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobotomy), Chris had only one unknown card. Mike cast [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), floating a black and a blue mana, on the turn time was called. Chris floated two blue. Mike resolved [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), played a land, and attempted to cast [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). Chris used [Gainsay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay) to counter the creature, signaling the effective end of the game.


Afterwards, it was debated if Mike should have cast a [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) instead of the Tog, he would have been able to cast the Tog on his second turn (with the Familiar in play) and won. Either way, it could be argued either way how this game could have been played, but either way it was played well.


Chris Benafel 1-Mike Pustilnik 1
