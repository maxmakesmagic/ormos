=================================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**

|  * Building on a Budget is dedicated to making decks that cost 30 tickets or less on **Magic** Online. Weekly deck testing is done using **Magic** Online. All prices quoted in this article are taken from the **Magic** Online buyers and sellers rooms!
* This week's deck is the third [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) evolution. The first, starting with *Dissension*, can be found [**here**](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/battle-wits-budget-dissension-stylin%E2%80%99-2006-07-03). The second, with *Coldsnap*, was in two parts. They can be found [**here**](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/battle-wits-minus-100-degrees-part-1-2-2006-10-16) and [**here**](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/battle-wits-deep-freeze-part-2-2-2006-10-23). You're reading the *Time Spiral* update!
* The format? Standard. This includes 9th Edition, *Ravnica*, *Guildpact*, *Dissension*, *Coldsnap*, and *Time Spiral*.
* 243 cards of pure fun!
 |

Welcome back to Building on a Budget! For those who are new to the column, welcome for the first time to the Building on a Budget [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) evolution! Each set release, I take a budget build of [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) and update it with the newest cards and technology available. There have been a lot of changes since the last iteration of the BoaB BoW Deck, especially in abbreviation tech, so let's get down to business.



![Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Battle+of+Wits)

First, if you're new to this series-within-a-series, I suggest checking out the first three parts from last year. I've linked to them in the sidebar of this article (up top!), so you have easy access. Second, there are the ground rules for the budget [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck, just as a reminder:


 


* [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) is the only rare allowed in the deck. This is a budget deck, and 240-250 cards already pushes the boundaries of many budgets.

 


* The cap on uncommons will be forty, and forty will be the cap on uncommons. Even if all of the cards in this deck were bulk commons on **Magic** Online, the base price of this deck would be six tickets (four for commons, two for the four copies of [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits)).

 


* Standard is the format! In this article, that means 9th Edition, *Ravnica*, *Guildpact*, *Dissension*, *Coldsnap* and *Time Spiral* are legal.

 


* The intent is to win with [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). It's fine if it comes down to alternate win conditions every now and then, but we're not just going to take [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm), Solar Flare, Boros Deck Wins and [Lightning Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel) control, throw them in a blender, and watch the puree congeal. This is a [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck, first and foremost.

The biggest change to the [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck this time around is the rotation of an entire block! Here's how the deck looked, when last we saw it:

Out:  

Betrayers: 3 [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours), 1 [Quash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quash), 1 [Befoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Befoul)  

Champions: 1 [Distress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distress), 1 [Hideous Laughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+Laughter), 1 [Nezumi Graverobber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Graverobber), 1 [Petals of Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Petals+of+Insight), 4 [Rend Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rend+Flesh), 1 [Soratami Cloudskater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soratami+Cloudskater),  

Saviors: 1 [Death Denied](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Denied), 1 [Exile into Darkness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exile+into+Darkness), 4 [Ideas Unbound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ideas+Unbound)


The biggest hit here is, believe it or not, the [Hideous Laughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+Laughter). There are no silver bullets currently in the common or uncommon slots of black or blue that can mass-remove creatures. The rest of the cards were all targets for transmute spells.


Also, it was pointed out in the forums of the last [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) article that the deck runs too many lands mathematically. Therefore:


Out: 7 [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), 7 [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)


All right, time to add some cards. The most significant addition to the deck are 4 [Mystical Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings). The strategy for this deck is to use transmute cards, tutors, and card drawing cards to get to [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). Frequently, games go turn 3 Transmute [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) for [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), turn 4 [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) for [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), turn 5 [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). Because there are so many cards in this deck, it's entirely possible to run several silver bullets (cards that are marginally useful against most decks, but extremely useful against individual strategies), since you have the room to stuff them in, and the ways to search them out. [Mystical Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings) is a double silver-bullet grabber, and an incentive to stuff more instants in the deck.


Speaking of one-of instants, I added one [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot), one [Tendrils of Corruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption), and one [Wipe Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wipe+Away). I also put in four [Sudden Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death) (straight replacements for [Rend Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rend+Flesh)), four [Chromatic Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star) (card drawing and color fixing), 4 [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) (in place of [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s/[Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s), 4 [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr) (early deck fixing), 4 [Looter il-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor) (good card drawing), 4 [Dimir Aqueduct](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Aqueduct) (which should have been in the deck previously, but somehow escaped my notice!), 4 [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) (solid card drawing), and 4 [Prismatic Lens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens).

On turn twenty, I get [Brainspoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil). Record: 10-1-1


For the third time in a row, this was a successful run with [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). We took out a lot of uncommons and added a lot of commons, so the deck is cheaper than ever at this point! If you're looking for a fun diversion that plays like highlander but isn't, I highly recommend throwing this deck together online. It costs less than 20 tickets to build, and a good majority of the cards are ones you'd want to have for other decks you build anyhow.

As a special feature over the next few weeks, I'm going to be going set by set and pointing out the cards that make great additions to any budget deckbuilder's portfolio. I'll be separating the cards into two categories: Essentials and Specialized. This basically means as follows - the Essentials are cards that, if you're playing that color, you'll probably use over and over again. Examples of Essential cards are [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize), [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer), and [Izzet Boilerworks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Boilerworks). Specialized cards are cards that aren't at universally played as Essential cards, but have their uses. Examples include [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World), [Mark of Eviction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+Eviction), and [Chastise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chastise).


This week's set? *Guildpact*! I'll list the cards by color and note whether they are Essential or Specialized. All cards on this list cost two tickets or less. For a primer on how to find the best prices using **Magic** Online, please refer to my "[Buying on a Budget](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/buying-budget-2007-01-08)" article Most of the commons and Uncommons on this list can get gotten at 16-for-a-ticket and 8-for-a-ticket, respectively. Most of the Rares are either 2-for-a-ticket, 1 ticket, or 2 tickets each. If a card costs more than 2 tickets, it is not included on this list (though these are few and far between for *Guildpact* - mostly the Dual Lands and [Giant Solifuge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Solifuge) aka Cap'n Tickles). Happy bargain hunting!
