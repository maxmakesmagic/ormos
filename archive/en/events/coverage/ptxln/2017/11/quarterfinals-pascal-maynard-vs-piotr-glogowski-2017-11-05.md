Quarterfinals: Pascal Maynard vs. Piotr Głogowski
=================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 5, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











"Oh, this is different," Piotr Głogowski said as he donned the headphones the players would wear for their match.


"Yeah, it really is," agreed Pascal Maynard.


It really is. That's because it's the quarterfinal of Pro Tour *Ixalan*.


It was Maynard's second trip the Pro Tour Top 8, after making it to the Sunday rounds at Pro Tour *Oath of the Gatewatch* last year. Głogowski, meanwhile, was playing in just his third Pro Tour and first this year. Still, both players had played exceptionally through the Swiss rounds to advance to the Top 8, and had their eyes set on much more as they squared off with a trip to the semifinals on the line.


Głogowski was playing the most popular archetype of the tournament in an energy deck. His build included a fourth color in order take advantage of [The Scarab God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Scarab+God), and he also had [Skysovereign, Consul Flagship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skysovereign%2C+Consul+Flagship) instead of [Glorybringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer) in the main deck. Maynard, on the other hand, went a little more rogue for the tournament, opting to bring a straight white-blue turbo [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) deck that aimed to dump the seven-cost artifact into the graveyard along with creatures and then bring it back with [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) to begin making giant creatures as early as the fourth turn. Pieces like [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries) and [Strategic Planning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strategic+Planning) helped make that a reality.


The Games
=========


Both players kept their opening hands, with Głogowski looking at a typical Temur Energy hand—a few lands, a [Servant of the Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+the+Conduit) for some acceleration, and a [Rogue Refiner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue+Refiner) to rebuild. Maynard's hand was a favorable opening, with [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries) to fill his graveyard and a [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) to bring back [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) should the Minister hit one.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/PTIxalan-20171105-2702.jpg)  
*Piotr Głogowski (left) and Pascal Maynard (right) both sought to continue their strong play in the quarterfinals.*


The action got started early, as [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) began the beatdown for Maynard. Głogowski developed his own board with the Servant, while a second [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries) arrived for Maynard, soliciting a "Wow" from Głogowski.


[Bristling Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bristling+Hydra) was a threat, but the [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries) landed a [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) into the graveyard, and [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) put it into play right on time. Like clockwork, it returned [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention), and the giant Angel crashed in for 7 damage.


"I choose not to block," Głogowski said drolly.


"Good decision," Maynard replied without missing a beat.


A few [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) attacks later and the pair were off to the second game.


Game 2 featured duplicates on both sides—a pair of [Longtusk Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Longtusk+Cub)s for Głogowski while Maynard had two [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries). Four activations later, Maynard found a [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) for his graveyard, and had [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) to put into play to reanimate [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) and refill his hand. A few seconds later, Głogowski said simply "You've got me," and conceded the game.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/PTIxalan-20171105-2706.jpg)  
*Pascal Maynard took an early lead in the quarterfinals, going up 2-0.*


With his back against the wall, Głogowski began Game 3 with a strong opening, filling his board early with [Longtusk Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Longtusk+Cub) and [Rogue Refiner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue+Refiner). Maynard managed to maneuver a [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) into his graveyard thanks to [Chart a Course](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chart+a+Course), but was still under the gun as Głogowski knocked him down to 15 life.


[Skysovereign, Consul Flagship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skysovereign%2C+Consul+Flagship) took control of the skies for Głogowski, but it was cleanly delayed by [Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions), which removed the Flagship for a time before falling prey to [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning). That allowed Głogowski to attack Maynard to 9 life. But the best thing about [Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions) is that it comes back, and embalming the creature again took the Vehicle out of action. That forced Głogowski to fill out his board further, and Maynard played right into that plan with [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention).


Głogowski was falling behind on board, but things threatened to get even worse as Maynard drew [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) for his turn. He opted to hold it in hand to play around [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), and attacked for just 2 with [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention). The play was punished, as Głogowski drew and slammed a land and cast [River's Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River%27s+Rebuke), bouncing all of Maynard's board and ending the game in one big swing. His back was against the wall, but Głogowski responded with a dramatic flurry to push the match into a fourth game.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/PTIxalan-20171105-2698.jpg)  
*Piotr Głogowski needed to win back-to-back games to continue his pursuit of the title.*


Maynard's opener in Game 4 didn't fill his graveyard quickly, but it did have the tools to set up a fourth-turn [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) thanks to [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) and [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish), while Głogowski had [Attune with Aether](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attune+with+Aether) and a [Servant of the Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+the+Conduit) to open.


Głogowski had [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) in hand, but opted for [Bristling Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bristling+Hydra) on the third turn, opening the window for Maynard's [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) to allow him [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) to reanimate [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits). [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) took down the [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift), but Maynard's hand was stacked after drawing four from the Champion. While [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) did take out the [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift), the damage was done as Maynard now had plenty of tools to work with.


[Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions) took out [Servant of the Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+the+Conduit), while the 4/4 [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) suicided into [Bristling Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bristling+Hydra), which became a 6/5 after Głogowski dumped all his energy into it. When he passed the turn back, Maynard punished the play by casting [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) to take the Hydra off the board. Clinging to just a [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) and staring down a board of a 4/4 [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits), [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden), and an [Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions), Głogowski found himself in desperate need of help.


[Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) brought that help, taking out [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) and allowing [Bristling Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bristling+Hydra) to eat the 4/4 token. Still, Głogowski fell to 1 life from the attack. When his draw step failed to yield any help, he extended his hand and congratulated Maynard for advancing to the semifinals of Pro Tour *Ixalan*.


**Pascal Maynard defeated Piotr Głogowski 3-1 to advance to the semifinals!**
