Most players, when asked about [Mind Unbound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Unbound), say it's rather weak or downright unplayable. After all, it certainly is slow and, at times, dangerous. But if you have means to ensure that your opponent's library depletes faster than your own, the card might be a veritable powerhouse. [Jace's Erasure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Erasure) is one such means, [Merfolk Mesmerist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Mesmerist) another. Müller liked [Mind Unbound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Unbound) so much, he picked up one copy of it as his first pick in the first pack.


And to be sure, he got yet another one for his second draft deck today, again featuring the milling strategy. But this time, his deck has even more tricks up its sleeves. [Jace's Archivist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist) and [Jace's Erasure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Erasure) are clearly insane in combination, but so is [Mesa Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mesa+Enchantress) along with seven enchantments providing extra draws that again translate into milled cards per [Jace's Erasure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Erasure).
