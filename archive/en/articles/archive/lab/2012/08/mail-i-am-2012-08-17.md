Mail, I Am
==========



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on August 20, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Welcome back to the Lab. Enter, please! Back...this way! Follow my voice! Okay, take a left at the plasma boiler, then a right by the hanging dodo skeletons...try to find a giant antechamber type place. There? 'kay. 

\*CHUNNK\*

That was the door locking behind you. You'll never escape. Unless you hit Back.

Content with internal imprisonment? I sure am. So let's get to this week's shenanigans, which have a bit of a retro vibe.These decks have been gradually filtering in over the summer, gathering in the cluttered train station that is my inbox. I felt these deck ideas could be strung together for a column, because they each revolve around some older combos and cards from **Magic**'s past. I usually discuss material from the most recent **Magic** releases (like last week, for example), but this week I felt like stepping back and offering a grab bag of slightly older goodies, cooked up by some inventive readers.

Looking forward to the upcoming weeks, it seems fitting to drift into the past for a bit, if only to rev my nostalgia gears in preparation for *Return to Ravnica*. My bookkeeping will likely extend into next week, when I'll give the rotating *Scars of Mirrodin* block a send-off of sorts.

Enough intro. There are some cool ideas to show off! Let's get to it.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl209_training_ground.jpg)[Training Grounds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Training+Grounds) | Art by [James Ryman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22James+Ryman%22%5D)

Training Day
Any effect that lowers the costs of mana payments is welcome in my book of spells. Many of these cards lend themselves to broken combos, and [Training Grounds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Training+Grounds) is certainly affiliated with the silly. Reader Alex Churchill emailed me with his delightful spin on the card. He wrote:


> Just thought you might like to see a recent comboriffic deck I assembled. With the three key cards on the table, you get the ability "![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif): All creatures you control get +8/+0, first strike, and trample." And the three cards in question cost one, two, and three mana. It can be pretty explosive. :)
> 
> The deck's built around [Training Grounds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Training+Grounds). When searching for abilities that could be activated multiple times with a cost of at least two generic mana, I turned up the Flamekin from *Lorwyn*. In particular, [Soulbright Flamekin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulbright+Flamekin) and [Inner-Flame Igniter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inner-Flame+Igniter) are key. With [Training Grounds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Training+Grounds) on the table, [Soulbright Flamekin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulbright+Flamekin) only costs you ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif) to give you its jackpot of eight red mana, while incidentally giving your team trample; and [Inner-Flame Igniter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inner-Flame+Igniter) will pump all your creatures at the low, low cost of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif), throwing in first strike somewhere along the line for free.
> 
>

> If they manage to stop that attack, other tasty [Training Grounds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Training+Grounds) targets include [Sphinx of Magosi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Magosi), [Merfolk Seastalkers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Seastalkers), and [Flamekin Spitfire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Spitfire). It's also got the [Lavaclaw Reaches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches)/[Ceaseless Searblades](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ceaseless+Searblades) combo in there for an alternative route to infinitely large Elementals. (Fortunately, [Training Grounds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Training+Grounds) still lets you activate [Lavaclaw Reaches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches) for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20210707095534im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless0.gif) if you want to!)

Rhox in Crocs
While I knock back a shot of poison for that sub-headline (laziness triumphs), here's Will Cooper's adrenaline-junkie style, life plummeting and soaring deck, featuring [Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender). ("*I thought today was solely old cards!!!*" Well, [Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender) is basically a walking [Boon Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boon+Reflection), for your info, Alleged Mystery Voice That I Actually Produced.)

Will wrote


> The point of this deck is to win by going into negative life totals. My favorite win condition is to get down to at most 2 life (possibly with the help of [Hex Parasite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hex+Parasite)), cast [Soul Echo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Echo), then [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate) an [Arbiter of Knollridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbiter+of+Knollridge) with [Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender) on the battlefield, which will put you to 50+ life for [Test of Endurance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Test+of+Endurance) if your opponent is still at 20. But there are other fun routes to victory here, too. Example: Put a singleton on the bottom of your library with [Mistveil Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistveil+Plains), use [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) and [Spoils of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spoils+of+the+Vault) to get rid of all of your life, then cast [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind). It's a little awkward that [Arbiter of Knollridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbiter+of+Knollridge) and [Sorin Markov](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin+Markov) are the only ways for you to gain life, but I like the concept anyway.
> 
>

Beastly Timing
Blocks of reminder text aside, suspend was awesome. Paying time instead of mana for a spell was a stuck-in-your-brain kind of concept, and I for one would be happy to raise a flag for its future return (perhaps suspend itself is in suspension...), if the mechanic weren't singlehandedly responsible for the upswing in complexity creep.

One of my favorite suspend cards is [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon). Capable of mass sacrifice for a larger purpose, this essentially 9/7 French vanilla beatstick has lumbered into many a Johnny strategy. Reader Steve Theobald messaged me with his take.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |


> I believe I have stumbled across a bit of a gem of a deck [that is] very unusual and leaves people posting things like "???" "what just happened?" and "huh?" after the game ends on **Magic Online** (my favorite type of reaction :) ). It has about a 60% win rate (but 100% fun rate!).
> 
> The deck centers around landing a [Psychogenic Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychogenic+Probe), then sacrificing all your other permanents to a suspended [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) and following up with a [Bitter Ordeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Ordeal). Ordeal forces the opponent to shuffle his or her library numerous times and the Probe activates each time to reduce the opponent to 0 in one fell swoop.
> 
> Flavor wise, it's pretty evil: first you show your opponent the looming threat of a [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) then everything starts exploding as the Gargadon comes closer, then you subject the opponent to a terrifying ordeal which causes them to forget half of their memory, and while frantically trying to reorganize their thoughts they are drilled in the head repeatedly until death (thats how I like to see it anyway! ;) ).
>

Steve explained his card choices:


> **[Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer)/[Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon)**—Mass sacrifice outlets for the big finish.   
> **[Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod)**—Two permanents to sacrifice + removal   
> **[Perilous Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr)**—Perfect for sacrificing to neutralize an early threat or helping get in some extra damage to supplement the probe   
> **[Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb)**—Can deal with problem permanents and helps the big finish   
> **[Ichor Wellspring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Wellspring)/[Dusk Urchins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Urchins)**—Gargadon fodder + card draw   
> **[Pawn Of Ulamog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pawn+Of+Ulamog)**—Helps you get to the critical number of sacrifices by turning dead creatures into self-sacrificing, mana-producing creatures   
> **[Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor)**—Sometimes you need to search up the last piece   
> **[Black Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith)**—Nice sweeper plus can draw cards off [Dusk Urchins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Urchins)  
> **[Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter)**—Perfect land here, on the big finish turn you can sacrifice it to put two permanents in the yard and make the opponent shuffle if they foolishly search out a land (they always do!); can also deal with problem lands
> 
> 

Well, until next time.
