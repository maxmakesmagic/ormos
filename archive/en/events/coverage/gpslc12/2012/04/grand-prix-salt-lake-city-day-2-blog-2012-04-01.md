Round 10 Feature Match - Iain Bartolomei vs. Bryan Eleyet
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Jake Van Lunen*

Iain Bartolomei grew up playing magic with LSV and Paul Cheon. Just a few months ago, in Seattle, Iain made it to the finals of a Grand Prix. Currently sitting in the X-1 bracket here in Salt Lake City, he's hoping to top that performance with a win.


Bryan Eleyet got some attention last year when he made it to the finals of Grand Prix Providence, a legacy event. Bryan is prepared to use his level 3 invite to go to Barcelona, but he'd much prefer to top 4 this event and keep the ball rolling.


### Game 1


Bartolomei, playing Naya Ramp, won the roll and chose to play first. Eleyet, playing Naya Pod, had the first play of the game with [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) . Bartolomei developed his mana with [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) .


Eleyet needed to get aggressive. He cast [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and sent it into the red zone.


Bartolomei had [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) to continue ramping his mana and put a speed bump in front of Eleyets creature onslaught.


Eleyet cast Hellrider and sent it into the red zone alone. Bartolomei took four, calmly untapped, and dropped a land and [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) on the table.


Eleyet leaned forward as Bartolomei searched his library for a pair of [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) . Bartolomei finished searching his library and passed the turn back to Eleyet.


Eleyet had [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) to make his [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) into a [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) . He used the *Innistrad* uncommon to exile Bartolomei's [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) . He sent his team into the red zone and Bartolomei was put on the back foot.



![Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hellrider)

Bartolomei had a big turn, though. He cast [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) for three guys and six life, then resolved a [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) , Gideon gained two loyalty counters and demanded that all Eleyet's creatures attack.


Eleyet used his [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) to exchange a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) for a [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) . He sent his team at Gideon and used Hellrider's trigger to deal three damage to Gideon before Bartolomei even had a chance to block.


Bartolomei chose to chump block everything and Gideon still had five loyalty counters. Another [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) from Bartolomei put him back at twenty life and gave him another three tokens. He used Gideon to kill Eleyet's [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) and his [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) returned to find some more lands.


Eleyet wasn't ready to give up yet. He used his [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) to search up an [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) to destroy the [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) that Bartolomei had searched for.


Bartolomei didn't seem to mind, he ticked up his gideon, sent a quartet of [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) into the red zone, and passed the turn.


Eleyet continued podding up toward [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) , he found a [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) and passed the turn.


Bartolomei put Eleyet at 8 poison, but he was forced to pass and let Eleyet search up [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) . Suddenly, things were looking pretty good for Eleyet.


Bartolomei shrugged and revealed [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) . With no Elesh Norn to shrink the [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) , Eleyet lost to Poison.


Bartolomei 1 - 0 Eleyet



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/rd%2010%20Iain%20Bartolomei%20.jpg)
*Iain Bartolomei looks strong at X-1.*

### Game 2


Eleyet got to be on the play, which can be pretty important against a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) deck.


Eleyet led things off with [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) . He had a [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) for the second turn and things were looking good.


[Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) joined the team and Eleyet attacked for six on his third turn.


Bartolomei put the breaks on with a TImely [Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reinforcements) , but there's only so much time you can buy when your opponent has such a strong start.


Another [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) for Eleyet helped keep the onslaught going, but Bartolomei had another [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) to further gum up the ground.


Eleyet had a [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) to make his team a lot more impressive. He sent everything into the red zone and activated his land after Bartolomei had declared blocks. Bartolomei took eight damage and things were looking grim even with his double [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) . All Bartolomei had was an Inkmoth attack.


Eleyet cast Hellrider and sent his team into the red zone. Bartolomei had an [Ancient Grudge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) for both Eleyet's golems, but still took a ton of damage from the attack.


Gideon bought Bartolomei a turn, but it wasn't enough and he was forced to move onto game three when the top of his deck didn't offer anything else for the next turn.


Bartolomei 1 - 1 Eleyet



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/rd%2010%20Bryan%20Eleyet%20.jpg)
*Bryan Eleyet is getting aggressive in Utah.*

### Game 3:


Bartolomei made the first play of the game with [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) .


Eleyet didn't have a play for his first or second turn and Bartolomei happily used this time to develop his mana with another [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) .


Eleyet made his first move on turn three when he cast [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) , but at this point Bartolomei already had access to a lot of mana. Eleyet made an attack with his [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) , cast [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) , and passed the turn with a formidable force on the table.


Bartolomei had a plan, though, he tapped out and cast [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) , wiping Eleyet's board of everything except a Golem token, which was now a 1/1.



![Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite)

Bryan Eleyet had [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) for [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) and he got in for another three damage. Meanwhile, Bartolomei was sneaking in a point per turn with his [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) .


Eleyet continued applying pressure with his Golem token, but couldn't find another threat to further his board position.


The random Inkmoth attacks were beginning to add up and Eleyet found himself at six poison. He attacked Bartolomei down to 6, but he needed a removal spell to survive if Bartolomei chose to move all in on his [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) .


Bartolomei had a [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) , but his lethal attack from Inkmoth (and [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) ) was met with [Act of Aggression](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Aggression) .


Eleyet tried to stabilize with [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) , but Bartolomei had [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) . Bartolomei continued applying pressure with his Inkmoth and Eleyet only had one turn left.


Eleyet tapped all his mana, cast Hellrider, cast [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) , and sent them into the red zone for exactly lethal damage.


Iain Bartolomei lamented playing around an instant on his last turn, he shook his head, "yep, you got it."

[Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) tried to make its way back onto the table, but Svien had a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) .

Svien tried [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) on [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) to bounce Hetrick's [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) , Hetrick tapped it to cast [Brimstone Volley](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) which targeted Svien's [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) .

*by Steve Sadin*

The Standard Pro Tour Qualifier season begins this Saturday, April 7th and while there's certainly room for players to win with entirely new strategies, most players are going to opt to play decks that they've grown comfortable with over the course of the previous few months.


Sometimes, they'll play these decks without making any substantial changes, but other times players will go out of their way to find that one special card that can put their deck over the top...


[Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) is a card that's seen a lot of play in recent months as a core component in White-Blue Humans, and in White-Black Token decks. So it should come as no surprise that some of the best players in the world chose to put [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) in their decks this weekend. What should come as a surprise, are the decks that people have been playing [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) in.


Three time Pro Tour Top 8 competitor, and current runner up in the race to become the United States team captain at the World **Magic** Cup, Josh Utter-Leyton fell just short of making Day Two with a Blue-White-Black Delver deck that sported three copies of [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) .


Josh put [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) in his deck because he wanted a non-fragile way to pump his [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) s, and the tokens that he makes with [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) , and [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) .


Is [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) a better choice than [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) , [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) , or additional Swords?


That remains to be seen  but it's clear that [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) is at least worth considering in your Delver decks.

Sunday, 2:31 p.m. - Deck Tech with Conley Woods
-----------------------------------------------


*by Jacob Van Lunen*

Conley Woods is well-known for his exciting and innovative decks. Time and time again, Woods has finished at the top of the standings with offbeat decks that no one expected. Woods brought his latest standard creation to Salt Lake City this weekend. I got a chance to sit down with Conley and ask him some questions about his latest brew.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/Deck%20Tech%20Conley%20Woods.jpg)
*Conley Woods* 

So tell me about the deck you decided to play this weekend?


"It's a Black/White/Green [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) Ramp deck. On the one end you have a traditional ramp deck, but the deck has entirely different side: [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) , [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad) , [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) , and [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) . Both sides of the deck help it either set up [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) or recovery from [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) . Sorin is really good pre-wrath, and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) makes opponents overextend into [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) . After [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) , a titan can come down and dominate the board. [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) is really good in this format, that and [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) are why I'm playing white. Originally I had four [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) but I moved one to the board because it's often a dead card against control decks."


What are your best matchups?


"Any creature decks. If my opponent plays [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) or [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) on turn one, it's very unlikely they're going to win. Wolf Run decks are also very good matchups. They don't have effective ways to kill my titans, while I have effective ways to deal with theirs. They're also playing [Viridian Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Emissary) now, which slows them down a lot when we're racing our ramp up to titans."


Your worst matchups?


"Equipment delver can be rough, while Drogskol Captain versions of the deck aren't that bad. Blue/Black Control is definitely the worst matchup; they have a lot of countermagic and it's hard to stick a big threat when they're drawing so many extra cards."

Sunday, 3:00 p.m.: Deck Tech - Blue-White Control with Andrew Cuneo
-------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin* 
Grand Prix Lincoln finalist Andrew Cuneo posted a Top 16 finish at Worlds with a Blue-White Control deck of his own design. Fourteen matches into Grand Prix Salt Lake City, Cuneo is 12-2 and a mere match win away from posting his second Grand Prix Top 8 of the season with a deck that's only a few cards off from what he played at Worlds.


"This is the first time that I've played the deck since Worlds. I don't like any of the other decks in the format, and I knew that I would have fun if I played it, so I figured why not."



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/Cuneo%20Deck%20Tech.png)
*Andrew Cuneo*

In addition to the fact that he felt comfortable with the deck, Cuneo gained a distinct advantage against opponents who either didn't know how to sideboard against him, or didn't have the right tools to bring in.


"I played against multiple people who kept [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) in against me after sideboarding this weekend -- and I even played against someone who had [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) in his deck after sideboarding because he just didn't have enough cards to board in."

Cuneo uses his many counterspells, and his removal to stall the game out to a point where he can win with his Planeswalkers.


"I generally win by attacking with [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) . If I'm playing against a deck with a lot of removal, then I want to wait until I get [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) to empty their hand before I start attacking. You can also restart the game with [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) , and win that way -- but that's not a very good strategy against decks with really low curves like Zombies. Against Green decks, however, you win a lot by restarting the game with [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated) "


Cuneo's deck has more than a few interesting cards in its maindeck, and in its sideboard. But one card that really stuck out to me was his [Elixir of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality)


"To beat Blue-Black Control decks with [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) , you absolutely need [Elixir of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality) . The [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) matchups almost always come down to decking. And even though I don't have any cards in my deck that actively mill my opponents -- I still usually win by decking thanks to [Elixir of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality) , [Buried Ruin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Ruin) , and Blue Sun's Zenith."


"If you really want to win the Blue-Black control matchup, you should have more than one [Elixir of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality)  but in a tournament like this, you won't be able to finish a lot of three game matches in fifty minutes. If I were playing online, where I have more control over the clock, I would probably have two."


When asked what his best matchups were, Cuneo said that he had little problem beating Zombies, or "Any control deck that doesn't have [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) ."


"I have real counterspells, and they don't generally have them. Also Esper Control decks tend to have some big creatures in them, so my [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) s are still relevant. [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) , and Blue Sun's Zenith are the best cards in control mirror matches  and I just have more of them than they do."


When asked what his toughest matchups are, Cuneo singled out two of the more popular decks in the format.


"Delver's not easy. I've actually beaten it three times so far this tournament when it was being played by good players  but I don't think their draws were very good. If you really wanted to improve the matchup -- you could add [Divine Offering](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) to your maindeck, but the four ratchet bombs, and the [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) are already there for Delver."


"The problem with the matchup is that you just can't beat the draws where they go turn one Delver with [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) backup, or the games where they have [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) plus [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)  but I think most decks lose to that anyway."


Cuneo's two losses so far this tournament have come at the hands of his other bad matchup, aggressive Red-Green decks.


"I don't think is a good matchup either. They have a lot of cards that [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) isn't good against. Like, they'll have boards where you [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) them, and you only increase their power. They can also play a turn two [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) , which this deck basically can't beat."


So if you're looking to play a dedicated control deck, and you don't mind having some tough matchups against Delver, and Aggressive Red-Green decks, then you should definitely give Cuneo's concoction a try. It's certainly been serving him well.
