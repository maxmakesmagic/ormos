Sunday, 10:31 a.m. – Undefeated
-------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 
Yesterday we took a look at the metagame of Grand Prix Atlanta (which happened just three weeks ago) to give us an idea what to expect this weekend. The top three decks back then were Stoneblade, Maverick, and RUG [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), with the latter clearly leading the pack. And now look at the four decks that were piloted to perfect 9-0 records on day one: one each of Maverick and Stoneblade with two RUG [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)s. Surprised?

Round 10 Feature Match - Simon Görtzen vs. Tom Valkeneers
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 
Everyone has their own Sunday rituals. When it comes to Simon Görtzen, winner of Pro Tour San Diego, if there is a Grand Prix going on, the smart money is that he will be up bright and early and playing. Görtzen had finished well enough on day one to start his Sunday the right way – playing to keep the top 8 plan alive. Against Tom Valkeneers of Belgium, he would be playing a new concoction debuted at this tournament; [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience).


### Game 1


Görtzen was down to six cards to lead the game off, and started on [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta). He saw a turn one [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) from Valkeneers off an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), which made things pretty clear – he would be up against Merfolk for his first match of the day.


A [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) came from Görtzen, who seemed happy to find Valkeneers without a play other than [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) for turn two. That an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) had come first suggested that there might be a [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) in his opponent's hand; something to bear in mind and play around.


A [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) was Görtzen's next play, and with one extra mana up, it resolved. Valkeneers looked a little confused, but nodded knowingly once he saw what Görtzen was fetching; a [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell). Valkeneers would now be aware to fight over the powerful sorcery, and had a [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) in the hope of buying more time before that fight came.


[Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) ticked up to two counters. Thus far the artifact had been inactive, but it seemed likely that it would be deploying threats each turn for the rest of the game, putting an inexorable clock on Görtzen. [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) came in, knocking Görtzen down to 16.


[Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) was met first with a [Silvergill Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvergill+Adept) to draw a card, and then by a [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). The [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) was stopped by [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze). What would each player get to play for free? Well, Görtzen had [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), who would prove problematic with 14 life remaining to use. Valkeneers had a [Master of the Pearl Trident](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+the+Pearl+Trident).


On Valkeneers' turn he swung with his squad. Most had islandwalk, but for the master, who Görtzen blocked. A [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) on [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) prompted him to draw seven cards, and cast [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze).


"I had to try it." Remarked a dejected Valkeneers.


Attacks from [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) put Görtzen to 15 life, and he cast a second copy of [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell). Valkeneers had an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) to put into play, while Görtzen had [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience). Close game. [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) allowed Görtzen to 'hard cast' [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn).


"Ok, that'll do it" smiled Valkeneers, scooping up his cards.


"Good [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze)s" remarked Görtzen with a small smile. "Once you played [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) I knew you didn't have [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), which made mine all the better."


Simon Görtzen 1 – 0 Tom Valkeneers

### Game 2


A turn one [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) on the play from Valkeneers was stopped by [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) from Görtzen, pitching a copy of [Personal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Personal+Tutor). He had a turn one [Ancient Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb), threatening a fast [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), but lost it to Wastland, and did not have a second land for a number of turns.


Fortunately for Görtzen, having stopped [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial), it took Valkeneers some time to apply more pressure than a [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault). At least in principle, Görtzen had time to draw out of his mana woes. When he finally found an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), he had a [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) at the ready, almost certainly engineering things such that his second land would not be as long coming.


A lone [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) soon had Görtzen at single digits, but he eventually found a [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) to bring his land count up to two. Valkeneers' lack of plays was suspicious – if Görtzen was to resolve a [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) it would likely be through quite a bit of resistance, and with such a strong land advantage, Valkeneers would be unafraid of [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze).


[Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) combined well with [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) to keep Görtzen's draws rosy. He just needed enough time to resolve that one big spell. A Defence Grid came from the German. A nice test spell, if this resolved, then getting other spells to work would be far far easier. It did.


Valkeneers seemed unimpressed by more lands, but did have a [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher) to potentially make life tricky. One extra mana would not be enough to stop Görtzen from getting the necessary done though. He had a [City of Traitors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors), to allow a [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), which plopped [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) into play. He didn't have enough life to use the demon immediately, but it would pose quite the road block. Valkeneers seemed almost relieved to see the demon, holding the perfect answer in hand. He untapped and cast [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image), using the legend rule to kill off [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand). Without any blockers, Görtzen succumbed to Merfolk attacks, and it was on to game three.

### Game 3


Görtzen had the play for the deciding game, and started on a [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). With tempo in his favour from the start, he could potentially ignore most of what Valkeeners was doing, and focus on resolving his one big spell. Görtzen had [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) in response, but no counter. Görtzen was clearly still digging a little for his combo, using running copies of [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) to get to the cards he needed. A [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) was stopped by [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) but Görtzen seemed unperturbed. [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault)s alone were not attacking him too fast, and he had a slew of card drawing to get to the point he needed. A [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) came from Görtzen, followed by a [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and shuffle. Finally Görtzen went for Show and tell, with 3 mana up.


It resolved, and while Görtzen had [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), Valkeneers had [Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker), naming [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand). The very next turn, Valkeneers used [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to off the legend, and attacked Görtzen to five. The German, out of gas, extended his hand to an attack the very next turn.

Sunday, 12:30 p.m. - How Maverick Rolls
---------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 
"I've spoken to a lot of people, who seem to think that Maverick is all about making small creatures and attacking with them. Let there be no mistake... Maverick is a control deck."


It was unlikely that English player was going to give up all the secrets of his Maverick build, which was a bit of a let down, but there is plenty of Maverick running around the top tables here at GP Ghent. Thanks to a little investigation, I've been able to pick up quite a few tips on how to get the most out of the green white deck is serving many of the combo decks in the format their just desserts.


The shell of Maverick is straightforward enough. Green/white, with acceleration in the form of [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) (and occasionally [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) for [Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor)) and a whole slew of disruptive creatures. Depending on the exact flavour of your metagame this mix of creatures could include any of the following;


[Mother of Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes)


[Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor)


[Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze)


[Gaddock Teeg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg)


[Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben)


[Kataki, War's Wage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kataki%2C+War%27s+Wage)


[Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker)


[Linvala, Keeper of Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence)


[Harmonic Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonic+Sliver)


[Ethersworn Canonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist)


All of these cards have the things in common – they make your opponent's life that little bit harder. On their own, they likely won't end the game, but they buy time to make your midrange deck able to profitably hit that 'mid-game' that is the sweet spot for a lot of its plays. From there, there are some value creatures being played quite a bit to strong effect;


[Scryb Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger)


[Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)


[Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman)


[Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary)


These four all do fairly nifty things for the deck. [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) is fairly straightforward, typically fetching the Swiss Army Knife that is [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte). [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) doesn't really work by the Swiss Army Knife principle, with it comes a whole toolbox of options fetching other creatures. [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) we'll get to in a moment, working as both enforcer and land searcher. The most interesting to me is [Scryb Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger), who lets the Maverick deck pull off all sorts of nifty tricks. With a great many creatures in the deck having useful tap abilities, being able to use them twice a turn can be a very big game. Likewise, being able to 'reset' a land, by bouncing and replaying it affords the Maverick deck with an effective way to manage its mana. My favourite tricks around [Scryb Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger) involve [Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) though. As a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) creature, it can be used to block and then bounced with [Scryb Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger) to manage things like opposing [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), lifelinkers or massive attackers.


[Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) seems a little unfair in Maverick, by virtue of the fact that the deck can leverage a lot of powerful lands. [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) has been many players go to answer (after a fashion) for [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn). It is so good in this role, that many Maverick decks have taken to running [Crop Rotation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crop+Rotation) to more quickly find it. There are plenty of other exciting lands to fetch though. [Maze of Ith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith) is a good answer to big attackers, and even lets you do tricks with the likes of [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) by activating it to untap the knight after combat damage in the rarely explored 'end of combat' step. [Gaea's Cradle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Cradle) provides Scavenging Ooze with enough mana to satisfy its hunger at lightning speed, or simply to power out threats at a rate that can compete with even the most aggressive of decks. [Horizon Canopy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Canopy) is an extra card when you really need to draw, and [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) works well with a deck replete with humans. Then there's good old [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), the answer of choice to opponents trying to get too clever with their lands.


One way or another Maverick is able to suppress the best draws of many decks in the format, thanks to a redundant amount of searchers, and powerful disruptive options. Some versions even go big out of the sideboard with [Natural Order](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order) for [Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus). It may not be big, it may not be clever, but you'd best be prepared, because Maverick definitely could roll you.

*by Tim Willoughby* 
There are quite a few [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) decks here at Grand Prix Ghent. Some are looking to put powerful enchantments like [Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind), [Dream Halls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Halls) or [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) into play. Still more are going for the big creature plan involving some mix of [Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus), [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn), often aided by [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack). My favourite of the day though might have to be this one, piloted by Pietro Cavalletti, simply because it chooses to go bigger and faster than the other [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) decks in the room, with explosive results, pairing the [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) plan up with [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis).

Throw in eight spirit guides to speed things up, and this deck can create some truly spectacular board positions from nowhere.


At the time of writing, Cavalletti is still well in the race to make top eight, and suffice to say, if he makes it, I will be the one racing to cover his matches. Given the option of going big or going home, Cavalletti is unabashed in having chosen to go big.

Round 13 Feature Match - Timo Schünemann vs. Felix Munch
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 
Felix Munch is the last undefeated player in the tournament, just two draws off a perfect record now. He's been toying with making coverage for some time now with his lands deck, finishing 7-0-2 in the last big European Legacy GP (just outside getting his list featured), then 8-0-1 yesterday. Finally, he makes it to the feature match area, and seemed pretty happy to get the spotlight with what is an unusual deck choice for the weekend.


Schünemann started off with a [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), seeing [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port), [Zuran Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Orb), [Crucible of Worlds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crucible+of+Worlds), [Mox Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Diamond), [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island), [Tranquil Thicket](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tranquil+Thicket) and [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition). The jig was very much up in terms of what Munch was playing, and he pointed out the synergy in his hand with a smile. Schünemann had a second [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), but no other play than a [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgnt12/FM%2013%20Felix%20Munch%20supported%20by%20some%20impressive%20facial%20topiary.jpg)

*Felix Munch supported by some impressive facial topiary.* 
Munch, with Mox, [Zuran Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Orb) and [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port), was in good shape to lock down his opponent's lands, but could only look on as an [Infernal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) found [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) for his opponent. Combo was likely not his best matchup, but if [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) was Schünemann's win condition, then at least [Zuran Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Orb) would force his opponent to build a higher storm count.


Schünemann wasn't about to wait and watch as Munch got in the game, casting a pair of copies of [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual), followed by fetching a [Volcanic Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island) with [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) to afford him enough cards in the graveyard to cast [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual) with threshold. He had three copies of [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual) in total, followed by [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames). That would get him to the storm he needed to get past [Zuran Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Orb), and Munch scooped up his cards.


Timo Schünemann 1 – 0 Felix Munch


"I'm only playing Lands because 'nobody' is playing your deck. If you decided to change decks in the next 5 minutes, I'd be totally cool with that" quipped Munch.


"I think I'm ok" smile back Schünemann after his lightning fast game one.


Munch had a mulligan on the play, but still had [Exploration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration) into [Mishra's Factory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Factory) on turn one. Schünemann's first turn was not particularly explosive, but did see an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), revealing [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void), [Exploration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration) and [Smokestack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokestack). The Chalice was quickly consigned to the grumper. Schünemann, a regular Vintage player, had a smile about the [Smokestack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokestack), and to have taken away the [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void).


This was Schünemann's first big tournament with storm, a reaction to having had a rough time lately playing Dredge. It seemed a timely switch. Munch's second turn only saw a [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit), while Schünemann had a [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) into a fairly ridiculous turn. Two copies of [Lion's Eye Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) were followed by [Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal), [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual), and [Infernal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) for [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish). The [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) found [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens), to make 16 goblin tokens.



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gpgnt12/fm%2013%20timo%20sch%C3%BCnemann.jpg)

*Timo Schünemann stops Munch's run at perfection* 
Munch had no play for his turn, and went to 4 on attacks. He did have an [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) at the end of turn, but could not find a combination of cards to fetch to keep him in the game, having sideboarded out Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale. With a smile, he extended his hand.


Timo Schünemann wins 2-0!

"Lots of people were intrigued by the sheer power of [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) and soon decks sprang up on various sites. So were we, but we build our own version which does a couple of things differently," said Simon.


Let's get to the basics first though. "The most important thing about this deck is its extreme redundancy. With [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish), and [Personal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Personal+Tutor) we effectively have eight copies of [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell). And four each of [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), and [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) make sure we always find one," Simon explained. "Then there's [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) and [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), also eight cards to put onto the battlefield with [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell). [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) obviously needs another spell, but between [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) and the four creatures we again have eight cards that do the trick."


"There are two options to actually kill opponents once you have [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience)," said Simon. "We can either [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) for [Petals of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Petals+of+Insight), cast that for free over and over again and sort our library in the process—if the number of cards in it is not divisble by three—then wish for [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot). Or we can simply cast [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). We also can [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) for [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) and get [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) out of the sideboard. It's important to have this option because [Petals of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Petals+of+Insight) really doesn't work against [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) or [Gaddock Teeg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg). If needs be, [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) for [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) for [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) even works without [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), simply putting the Eldrazi into play with [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell)."



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgnt12/GHENT_omniscience.jpg)

*Simon Görtzen on the left, Florian Koch on the right* 
"Mana is very important, especially permanent mana sources, because having extra mana available negates so many of the format's counterspells," Simon said about their mana base which has less [Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal)s than many other [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) decks. "That's also why we don't run any counterspells of our own which cost mana. Having stuff like Flusterstorm or [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) really doesn't help at all. [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) is much better."


"The other thing differentiating our version from most is the inclusion of [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor). That's a card all control decks absolutely have to counter," said Simon. At that point Florian Koch came back from his match and chimed in: "I just won a game with turn-two Jace off [Ancient Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb) and [Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal)."


"He's a threat on its own, he's another blue card for [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), and when you have [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) but no creature or Wish, he's another [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)," Simon added. "And sometimes [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) and one attack with [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) is not enough. Then you can use Jace to bounce and recast [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn). Against [Humility](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Humility) or [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge)[Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn)'s not a win either, but we can wish for [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) and at least take infinite extra turns, then win with Jace later."


The most unusual card in the sideboard probably is [Defense Grid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defense+Grid), about which Simon had the following to say: "[Defense Grid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defense+Grid) is better in theory than in practice, but it is a trump card in that it can completely blank a lot of cards if an opponent really overloads on counterspells."


Finishing things off with a sweet story: "Funniest win I got so far was against an opponent who knew I had [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) and [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) in hand. He had six lands including [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) and held [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). I topdecked [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) and cast Show. He smiled, looked at his [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas), and let it resolve. Let's just say, he was pretty upset when I dropped the [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience)."

We all know that [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) (well, its dark side, [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration)) is a fine choice of aggressive threat in Legacy. In many cases, it is played in a fairly controlling shell, on the understanding that a protected [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) does not take too terribly many turns to end the game. However, that doesn't mean to say that this is the only way to deploy the Brundlefly. One option being played by a few players here in Ghent is [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) backed up by card drawing in what amounts to a burn deck.


Burn as a strategy is one that is potentially quite powerful in Legacy, as it does present a consistent clock for opponents to race. However, it can run out of steam a little, and is in many matchups cold to opponents who can either deal with your plan, or win the race. By having access to things like [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) and [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), not only can the burn deck ensure that it draws the best of its burn spells (like [Fireblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireblast) and [Price of Progress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Price+of+Progress)), but it can set up interactions with [Thunderous Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderous+Wrath), to really put the hurt on fast. Being able to put up token resistance to other fast start with the likes of [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) is really just the icing on a rather burnt cake.


Below is a list from just one of these decks playing here in Ghent on Sunday.

Round 15 Feature Match
----------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 
Denmark's Thomas Enevoldsen has never made the Top 8 of a premier event so far, but he did have a couple of Top 64 finishes at the Pro Tour, and he's obviously looking to finally add a Top 8 to his résumé this weekend. With a score of 12-2 he was now facing possible elimination should he lose against Germany's Lukas Maurer. The match-up here was Enevoldsen's "Death and Taxes" against Maurer's Elf deck.


### Game 1


Enevoldsen started on [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) to his opponent's opening of [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), [Fyndhorn Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fyndhorn+Elves). The Danish player followed it up with [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) getting Umezwawa's Jitte while Maurer recruited [Ezuri, Renegade Leader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ezuri%2C+Renegade+Leader). On turn three, Enevoldsen just made another [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) and passed with two mana up, looking to use [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgnt12/GHENT_fm15_maurer.jpg)

*Lukas Maurer quickly takes game one with his elves* 
On his turn three, however, Maurer cast [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature), proceeded to summon about twenty Elves with a couple of [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote)s thrown in for good measure, and prompted a concession from Enevoldsen.


Thomas Enevoldsen 0 – 1 Lukas Maurer


### Game 2


Enevoldsen thought long and hard about his opening seven, but kept with a frown on his face. He had [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port) and [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) for turn one, while Maurer made [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Scattershot Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattershot+Archer). Neither player had a land on turn two, revealing the cause of Enevoldsen's frown.



![Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wirewood+Symbiote)

Maurer summoned [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), Enevoldsen vialed in a [Mother of Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes). Again no land on turn three for neither player. Maurer cast [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote), Enevoldsen got [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) via [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial).


Maurer finally found a second [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and cast [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), but [Mother of Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) meant the equipment wasn't really a problem for Enevoldsen. At end of turn [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) gave him a [Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker) which revoked [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote)'s ability. A turn later, with Enevoldsen still stuck on one land, Maurer cast [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature). Enevoldsen responded by smoothly activating his [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) to bring in [Ethersworn Canonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist). That was that.


He also found a second land and things started to look dim for Maurer. Even more so when [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) went to three counters and spat out [Mangara of Corondor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mangara+of+Corondor). Next turn, Enevoldsen attacked with [Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker) which was blocked by the now useless [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote). Enevoldsen used [Mother of Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) to protect his creature, but that meant he had to use Mangara to take care of Maurer's [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), something Enevoldsen had intended to do anyway. On his next turn he cast his own copy of the legendary equipment, and another turn later the Jitte began its dirty work. Reduced to casting one spell per turn thanks to [Ethersworn Canonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist), and staring down [Mother of Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) as well as Jitte, there was not much Maurer could do. One after the other his elves died and, when none where left, he did.


Thomas Enevoldsen 1 – 1 Lukas Maurer


### Game 3


Enevoldsen had to mulligan twice to achieve this, but for the third time in this match he started with [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) on turn one. He had another one on turn two but again no land. Meanwhile, Maurer had summoned a [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and a [Quirion Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Ranger), and on turn three he cast [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) followed by [Fyndhorn Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fyndhorn+Elves), [Birchlore Rangers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birchlore+Rangers), Elvish Visonary, and [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte). Sundar, 4:22 p.m. - Giant Monsters in Legacy
--------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 
Sometimes **Magic** is about painstakingly eking out lots of small advantages over the course of a long and hard-fought battle. Sometimes, however, it's simply about making big stuff happen very fast. Whether you prefer the first or the second kind of **Magic**, Legacy provides both, with matches between RUG [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), Maverick, or Stoneblade decks responsible for the former. To be honest, though, the latter clearly makes for better stories. Here are some that happened this weekend.


You may have already heard about this one, so I'll make it quick. A player dropped five Leylines onto the battlefield before turn one, then, on turn one, played [Serra's Sanctum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra%27s+Sanctum) and cast [Opalescence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence), turning all of his Leylines into 4/4 creatures. As they had been on the battlefield since the beginning of the first turn, they were able to immediately attack for 20. Done.



![Opalescence++Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Opalescence&options=)
![Opalescence++Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Omniscience&options=)

Meanwhile Pro Tour champion Simon Görtzen has been tearing up the field with his [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) deck, winning as early as turn one with [Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal), [Ancient Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb), [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) for [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), summoning [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), drawing a bunch of cards, casting [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) for [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) for [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn), and casting that as well. Sweet, eh?


[Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) creates some truly crazy board states. Ever seen [Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus) and [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) on the battlefield on turn two? With [Urabrask the Hidden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urabrask+the+Hidden) for good measure? Well, I did and it's every bit as awesome as it sounds. Admittedly, only for a very short amount of time ...

However it's not just combo decks that smash in with giant monsters. Admitted, Merfolk takes a little more time to get there but these fishes get huge too.
