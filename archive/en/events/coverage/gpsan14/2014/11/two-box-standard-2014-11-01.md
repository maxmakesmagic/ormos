TWO-BOX STANDARD
================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX SANTIAGO 2014 - COVERAGE - EVENTS](/en/events/coverage/gpsan14)
 on November 1, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_richhagon.jpg)
By Rich Hagon




Rich Hagon combines a deep knowledge of the players of the Pro Tour with a passionate love of the game. He's a regular commentator for Pro Tour and Grand Prix live video coverage, and is the official Pro Tour Statistician. He has been covering Magic events since 2006. 






 Last week, at Grand Prix Stockholm, Team Coverage heard a story. It was a good story, and we kept hearing it. There was this player, and he was playing in his first GP. He was playing in his first Standard tournament, period. He was at 10-2. He had no cards, so opened up two boxes of *Khans of Tarkir*, and built the best deck he could from that collection. And he was 10-2.




*Magic*players love a good story, so it's probably not a surprise to you that, after a spot of Woodward-esque investigative journalism, all wasn't *quite* as it first seemed. For one thing, although this was indeed his first Standard tournament, he was no stranger to tournament MTG, and was, in fact, widely regarded as one of Sweden's finest Legacy players. Second, he *did* have *some* cards for Standard, just not a lot of them, and he filled in the blanks with the best he could find from two *Khans* boxes.




 That's still a fine story, and he nearly made the Top 8. But with thirty three hours of travel between my house and Grand Prix Santiago, I started wondering, 'What if it had been true? All of it?'. Could you possibly build a competitive Standard deck from just 72 boosters of *Khans of Tarkir*? Don't get me wrong, I'm not talking about a 7-2 make Day Two deck, just something that you could sit down with and feel you weren't 100% to lose.




 That's when I spotted two unopened *Khans* displays in the corner of my office. Guess we'd better try the experiment then...



And so it came to pass that Hagon, worn down by the rigors of Manchester Airport, did spend seven hours on a flight to Chicago, cataloguing 72 boosters, and making customarily pithy notes on each:



 B1 - [Wooded Foothills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills) is great, but I don't imagine mana is going to be my problem. Then there's [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise), [Hordeling Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hordeling+Outburst), [Murderous Cut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Cut).




 B2 - Can't see [Trail of Mystery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trail+of+Mystery) being a thing. [Scout the Borders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scout+the+Borders) and [Sultai Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sultai+Scavenger) for Delve, [Jeskai Student](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Student) for some horrible aggro deck.




 B3 - An exciting mythic in [Pearl Lake Ancient](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pearl+Lake+Ancient). [Sultai Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sultai+Charm) is the only other serious card, then things like [Valley Dasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valley+Dasher) and a second [Jeskai Student](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Student).




 B4 - I like [Clever Impersonator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator), because it could be [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino). [Heir of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+the+Wilds) is real. I wonder if I can do something grindy with [Archers' Parapet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archers%27+Parapet)?




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Clever Impersonator)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever Impersonator)


 B5 - Thrilled as I am to see [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy), I believe I may be shy of the complete combo. Just a hunch. Maybe [Mardu Hateblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mardu+Hateblade) is the first card for my BW Warrior deck.




 B6 - The only card in Standard is Anafenza, but she's awesome. I can imagine [Kill Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kill+Shot) in some UW Control list, but I have a *very* active imagination.




 B7 - Another fetch, plus [Arc Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Lightning). Not sure how many burn spells I'm going to find for red decks.




 B8 - Half a dozen cards of interest - [Highspire Mantis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Highspire+Mantis) might be my [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider). [Burn Away](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burn+Away) to kill [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) *and* nail their graveyard? Boo-ya. Possibly.




 B9 - Just a couple of Sideboard-ish cards - [Erase](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erase), and [Stubborn Denial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stubborn+Denial), which could be main.




 B10 - [Utter End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utter+End) could make it, but not much else.




 B11 - It costs a ton, but [Duneblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duneblast) certainly does something. First [Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke), which I might need four of.




 B12 - [Chief of the Scale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Scale) for Warriors, and a [Jeskai Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Charm).  B14 - A second [Seeker of the Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seeker+of+the+Way) is about it here, unless there's some weird [Goblinslide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblinslide)/[Trumpet Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trumpet+Blast) deck waiting to happen.

[Ivorytusk Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivorytusk+Fortress) is awesome, but not for Standard.




 B18 - A nice [Bloodstained Mire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodstained+Mire) for the collection, but all I can say here is that [Raiders' Spoils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raiders%27+Spoils) probably won't be in the Warriors deck I won't be building.

 B24 - A really nice rare in [Rakshasa Deathdealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakshasa+Deathdealer). Then there's another [Raiders' Spoils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raiders%27+Spoils). OK, I probably *will* end up building it.

Also, I've never seen [Trap Essence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trap+Essence) before today. I'm so blessed.

Did I mention [Mindswipe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindswipe) is my favorite card in *Khans*?

[Abzan Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Ascendancy). Sultai. Warriors. Red Tokens/[Trumpet Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trumpet+Blast). Red-White Aggro.I would say, 'So many decks, so little time', but it's still almost 27 hours to Santiago. Let's see what booster box number two gets us:

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Duneblast)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duneblast)


 B55 - Pretty sure I'm filled up on [Murderous Cut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Cut) and [Hordeling Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hordeling+Outburst). Suspect the Cut may be the one making the, er, cut. No outbursts please.

 Huh. A decklist that looks like a decklist. 4-ofs and 3-ofs, and even the singletons look like things you might actually be choosing to do, rather than through *some completely insane and arbitrary imposition of unlikely deckbuilding restrictions*. It's easy to see that the deck could and should have a decent board presence by turn four. The trouble is, where's the payoff? There's 25+ Warriors in the deck, but outside the Creature Type-agnostic [Master of Pearls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Pearls), no way to pump them in a game-ending fashion. [Trumpet Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trumpet+Blast) would be good from Red, but I just can't imagine turning up to a Grand Prix with cards like [Raiders' Spoils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raiders%27+Spoils) and [Rush of Battle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rush+of+Battle) in my main deck. Maybe that's the way to go – be incredibly single-minded, and take out the five removal spells and the [Wingmate Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingmate+Roc), and replace them with Spoils and Battle. Even then, it's hard to imagine rushing into battle with this deck.




**Prediction: 2-7** 




 Sigh. It seems as if two boxes of *Khans of Tarkir*  are not enough alone to make a day two Grand Prix decklist. That said, I can certainly imagine taking the Jeskai deck into Friday Night Magic and having a ton of fun, and at that level there would certainly be wins available for all of these decks, except the Sultai list, which may turn out to be a crime against humanity. So, next time you're faced with multiple eight-hour flights, and just happen to have a couple of boxes in your hand luggage (this happens to me *all the time*), feel free to take the Two-Box Standard challenge. Meanwhile, if you were playing in the GP this weekend, and simply *had* to pick one of these monstrosities, what would you play?
