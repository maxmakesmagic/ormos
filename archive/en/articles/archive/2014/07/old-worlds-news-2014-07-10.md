Old Worlds News

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)

Adam Styborski

Adam "Stybs" Styborski joined DailyMTG.com in 2009 to take over Serious Fun, before switching over to begin Command Tower in 2013. With his passion for Commander and community inclusion, you'll find plenty of opportunity each week to share your thoughts about everyone's favorite casual format.


July 10, 2014
 







I like lists. Organizing and sharing information with lists may be so frequent its banal, but I find myself drawn to them for everything:



* Rows of data in a spreadsheet list out my financial transactions, making it easy to see where things stand
* The cascade of tasks I set out to do, regularly adding more to the end than I lopped off the top before day's end
* Illustrating examples of lists using a list of bullet points in an article that begins by talking about lists

Lists are my go-to approach for making sense of the world, and encapsulating my understanding of it into digestible chunks. For example, when I wanted to see how much I was looking forward to *Magic 2015* I made a list of every card I wanted.


I was surprised to find 45 listed.

It's an eclectic mix of things for my all-commons Cube and box of Commander goodies, and it'd be far bigger if I was an avid player of Standard and Modern or didn't already have copies of returning cards like [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) and [Raise the Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm).


While I could gush and explain my interest in all of them, there are five I'm going to summarize in a sentence each:



* [Boonweaver Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boonweaver+Giant) convinced me that [Bruna, Light of Alabaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bruna%2C+Light+of+Alabaster) is going to be my new Commander deck project for [Gen Con](http://www.gencon.com/).
* [Hot Soup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hot+Soup) will result in someone dying an unexpected and hilarious death, and I want to help make that happen.
* [Perilous Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Vault) will finally push [Nevinyrral's Disk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk) out of the average Commander deck; dealing with Planeswalkers can be important!
* [Soul of Ravnica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+Ravnica) is the card-drawing engine I needed for my slow, colorful [Atogatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atogatog) deck.
* [Undergrowth Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undergrowth+Scavenger) is a common that hits way above its rarity in multiplayer games (like, say Two-Headed Giant, which you might find at a [Prerelease](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/Prerelease-event-types-events) or [Friday Night *Magic*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/event-types/friday-night-magic) ).

Aren't lists awesome? I'd like to believe most of you agree with me, since all of last week's feedback came packed with decklists to show off the newest cards, like how John knows exactly where [Chief Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+Engineer) will go:



R&D hit M15 out of the park! I'm astonished by the amount of awesomeness! Here are my Top 5 picks for Commander:


**#5 [Resolute Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resolute+Archangel)** 


Resetting to 20 every turn is nice; resetting to 40 is even better! She's an auto-include in my [Vish Kal, Blood Arbiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vish+Kal%2C+Blood+Arbiter) deck, where she'll make the life-for-cards theme almost painless.


**#4 [Ajani Steadfast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Steadfast)**


I've long wanted a Planeswalker that boosted other 'walkers. It just seemed like something Planeswalkers should do. I can't wait to activate Ajani's -2 ability!


**#3 [Soul of New Phyrexia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+New+Phyrexia)**


This card simultaneously frightens and excites me. One-sided board wipes are never fun for those on the receiving end, but players usually have to jump through hoops to enable them. [Soul of New Phyrexia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+New+Phyrexia) gives any and all comers easy, colorless access to one-sided table sweeping. I know I'll be running it in my [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) deck for that very reason.


**#2 [Chasm Skulker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chasm+Skulker)**


[Lorescale Coatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorescale+Coatl) is one of my favorite uncommons, and I can't help wondering if Mike Neumann was inspired by it. As a Mikey fan, I'm predisposed to like his card, but I'd be into this design no matter who created it. I haven't yet decided whether it will replace the Coatl or fight alongside it in my [Prime Speaker Zegana](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prime+Speaker+Zegana) Commander deck.


**#1 [Chief Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+Engineer)**


I'm an artifact junkie, and "Scotty" (can we please nickname him that?) is the latest addition to a long and venerable line of blue artifact enablers. [Arcum Dagsson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcum+Dagsson); [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect); and [Muzzio, Visionary Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muzzio%2C+Visionary+Architect) would all enjoy talking shop with this fine fellow. There is a spot being prepared for him in my [Sydri, Galvanic Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sydri%2C+Galvanic+Genius) deck:


—John

Andrew cared more specifically about tokens and what *Magic 2015* brings to the table for them:



One of my favorite decks is [Ulasht, the Hate Seed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulasht%2C+the+Hate+Seed). The deck aims to pump out a ton of tokens to ensure Ulasht is as big as possible. There are six cards in *Magic 2015* (so far) that I'm looking to add. While [Restock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restock) and [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) are reprints, I don't have any copies of them. M15 gives me an opportunity to get my hands on multiples, as both are amazing Commander staples.


[Genesis Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra) is looking to become another big staple, and I'll be sure to add it to almost all my green decks. [Soul of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+Zendikar) is mana intensive, but I think people underestimate how useful an army of 3/3s can be. [Feral Incarnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Incarnation) is in a similar vein; my tokens can convoke it early, or I can just ramp into it just fine.





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Genesis Hydra)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis Hydra)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Soul of Zendikar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul of Zendikar)



The biggest addition, however, is [Hornet Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Nest). The end-all-be-all of chump blockers, it should literally scare people out of attacking you. In a pinch, I can even send a giant burn spell its way or fire off a [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) to get an army of angry tokens. [Gruul Ragebeast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Ragebeast), [Electropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electropotence), and [Warstorm Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warstorm+Surge) also help me send a ton of damage its way if my opponents aren't being very cooperative.

Ah, [Zedruu the Greathearted](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zedruu+the+Greathearted). It's a Commander that at once looks wacky, offbeat, and exactly the kind of wild fun that I love about the format. But behind the humble Monk lies the dark heart of a Minotaur: Zedruu consistently takes new permanents and turns them into unexpected weapons. [Aggressive Mining](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggressive+Mining) looks awesome, and I'm not just speaking as a fan of Minecraft. Giving it to an unwary opponent just seem cruel, particularly with Zedruu tipping the trade even worse in our favor.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aggressive Mining)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggressive Mining)
For some of you, that's exactly the type of deck you wanted to see. If so, I'm glad you enjoy it. I prefer to avoid such filthy, effective means.


Tapped Potential


[Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling), [Chief Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+Engineer), and [Obelisk of Urd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obelisk+of+Urd) are all tied together by convoke, the returning mechanic of *Magic 2015*. While it's something that I really like for a lot of reasons, I'll hold off on that list until you tell me your own: **Which convoke (or similar) cards do you use in Commander, and why?**



* Feedback via email
* 300-word limit to explain which *Magic 2015* cards, and why
* **Sample decklist is requested** (does not count against word limit)
* Decklists should be formatted with one card per line with just a leading number, such as "3 Mountain"—just a space (no "x" or "-") between the number and the card name, without subtotals by card type (Submissions that don't follow this rule will be ignored.)
* Name and email required (non-personal information to be used in column)

While the actual [list of cards with convoke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advanced&text=+[convoke]) isn't impressively long, cards that ask you to tap creatures ([Devout Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Invocation), [Mossbridge Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mossbridge+Troll)) or let you do even more with them ([Energy Arc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Energy+Arc), [Vitalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitalize)) definitely count toward convoke shenanigans.


I'm looking forward to the corner cases you've already found to use.
