Round 4 Feature Match: Goro Matsuo vs. Masayuki Higashino
=========================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**







![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_authorpic_larger.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast












Goro Matsuo, a 1997 National team member, has drafted a 5-color deck that features [Draco](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draco), [Questing Phelddagrif](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Phelddagrif), and a [Ghitu Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Fire) along with mana acceleration in the form of [Harrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harrow), 2 [Quirion Trailblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Trailblazer)s, and 2 [Fertile Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertile+Ground)s. His opponent, Masayuki Higashino, is playing a Treva-color deck with a splash of red. Both players are famous for their off-the-cuff remarks and humor. They bantered constantly throughout their match, often eliciting laughs from both the table judges and the crowd.


### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/JPNAT01/253F.jpg)*Goro Matsuo*Higashino chose to draw the first game, as his is a slow deck that needs time to develop. Although he was able to produce a first-turn [Thornscape Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornscape+Apprentice), he was unable to use it to best effect as he was unable to draw a Plains. In the meantime, Matsuo's deck performed flawlessly, allowing him to play a fourth-turn Phelddagrif. Higashino attempted to slow his opponent's offense by bouncing the purple hippo with [Barrin's Unmaking](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barrin%27s+Unmaking). Matsuo was unfazed, choosing to play a [Goham Djinn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goham+Djinn) instead of replaying the hippo. Higashino finally drew into the white mana he desperately needed in the form of an [Elfhame Palace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elfhame+Palace), meaning he had to chump block the Djinn until the Palace could come online. Although Higashino eventually found a more permanent solution to the Djinn when he drew [Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shackles), his life was low enough that Matsuo was able to finish him off with Ghitu Fire.


Result: 1-0 Matsuo


### Game 2


Higashino chose to draw again this game, and was pleased to watch Matsuo mulligan. Playing both his green and blue tappers backed up by a [Galina's Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galina%27s+Knight), Higashino took an early lead in the damage race, with Matsuo's first play being a Harrow, leading into a 5th turn [Nightscape Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Battlemage) played with red kicker to destroy Higashino's only source of white mama, an [Irrigation Ditch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Irrigation+Ditch). Both players emptied their hands in quick fashion afterward, with Higashino drawing into Plains and lining up a kicked [Benalish Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benalish+Lancer) and a [Disciple of Kangee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Kangee), and Matsuo fielding a hippo and a [Volcano Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcano+Imp). Matsuo was unable to topdeck a solution to Higashino's tappers, and soon scooped.


Result: 1-1


### Game 3


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/JPNAT01/254F.jpg)*Masayuki Higashino*Higashino was forced to mulligan, but that didn't solve his land troubles. He was stuck with only one Forest for three turns, forced to discard a [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) and an [Armored Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Guardian). On the other side of the table, Matsuo played a land every turn, as well as one creature after another. His life slowly being whittled away by a Battlemage, Imp, and Unicorn, Higashino finally found enough land to Harrow and play [Ancient Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Spider), only to succumb to Ghitu Fire.


Result: 2-1 Matsuo
