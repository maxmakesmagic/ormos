The October 28, 2016 Update
===========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on October 28, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen

The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on October 28, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you the full [*Commander (2016 Edition)* Card Image Gallery](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/card-image-gallery/commander-2016)!


What to Watch This Weekend
==========================


1. [Grand Prix Warsaw](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpwar16)

Standard takes center stage again at Grand Prix Warsaw this weekend, starting Saturday at 10 a.m. local time/1 a.m. PT/4 a.m. ET on [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic). My fiancée is out of town this weekend, so the chances of me running FNM into watching this stream into getting no sleep are not small.


1. [North American Eternal Weekend](http://www.cardtitan.com/eternal_weekend)

We just had European Eternal Weekend, so you might already be salivating over this. Card Titan hosts a weekend full of Eternal format competitions in Columbus, Ohio, and will be streaming the whole thing on [twitch.tv/cardtitan](https://www.twitch.tv/cardtitan). In fact, they're likely streaming as you're reading this, as they were already underway when I started writing this very item.


After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [Spark of Genius](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/aliaintrazi-10282026-spark-of-genius/) | Gathering Magic | Ali Aintrazi

Never stop brewing. As you might have seen yesterday when SaffronOlive [played my Panharmonicon deck on stream](https://www.twitch.tv/mtggoldfish/v/97485305) (like I wasn't going to mention that), there's still a ton of fun, unexplored space in Standard. Here Ali Aintrazi does just that.


1. [Three's Company](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/thegirlfriendbracket-10282016-the-girlfriend-bracket-77-threes-company/) | The Girlfriend Bracket | Erin Campbell, Hallie Santo, Katie Neal, and Kriz Schultz

SCG Milwaukee, missed triggers and concessions, and a diversity initiative at Grand Prix are all on the docket for The Girlfriend Bracket.


1. [How NOT to Deal with a Prized Amalgam](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/) | Top Level Podcast | Patrick Chapin and Mike Flores

[Prized Amalgam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prized+Amalgam) has proven itself a powerhouse in Standard *and* older formats, and man does it feel impossible to deal with. Chapin and Flores talk about maybe Geralf's greatest creation (sorry, [Geralf's Masterpiece](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Masterpiece)).


1. [*Kaladesh* Re-Review](http://lrcast.com/limited-resources-360-kaladesh-re-review/) | Limited Resources | Marshall Sutcliffe and Luis Scott-Vargas

With some time to reflect, Marshall and Luis revisit their early impressions of *Kaladesh*, plus pivot on a number of cards.


Thankfully, [Cloudblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer) is still great.


1. [Top 10 Cards to Play for Halloween](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/insidethedeck-10272016-top-10-cards-to-play-for-halloween/) | Gathering Magic | Inside the Deck

I couldn't share this yesterday when I was buried under so many previews, but I wanted to share it anyway with Halloween coming up. Why not play something thematic on the spookiest of holidays?

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [White-Blue Panharmonicombo](https://twitter.com/blakepr/status/791668559541784576)

I'm not going to claim this is a tier 1 deck, or guarantee that you'll crush a Friday Night *Magic* with it. But I will guarantee that it's fun.


This deck, unlike most decks I feature here, is my baby. The list posted here changes every few hours I test it (you can see the image above is different from my current list below), and I even have multiple versions of the deck saved on *Magic Online*, but the core of the deck is this:


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cloudblazer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Panharmonicon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panharmonicon)
Sure, the deck does other things, but do you care?


The deck is actually packed with enters-the-battlefield abilities. [Thraben Inspector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Inspector) makes multiple Clues, [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) bounces multiple creatures, [Filigree Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filigree+Familiar) gains 4 life, and [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) makes four Scions. The real kicker is that last one, as you can combine [Panharmonicon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panharmonicon), [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope), and [Eldrazi Displacer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Displacer) to make as much colorless mana, as many Scions, and blink as many things as you like. The deck ends up playing like a midrange deck that kicks into high gear with a [Panharmonicon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panharmonicon) *and* it has a combo finish. It even has [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) to help dig for the right pieces.


The deck isn't a world-beater, but it is an absolute blast to play. I'll be playing it at Friday Night *Magic* tonight. How about you?
