Grand Prix Minneapolis 2009

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmin09/endblurb.jpg)


It’s been a great weekend here at Grand Prix – Minneapolis. Almost 1,200 players showed up to play in the last Grand Prix of the year, making it one of the three most-attended American Grand Prixes in history.

Top 8 – Drafting with Brian Kibler
----------------------------------


*by Dane Young*

Brian Kibler entered this All-American Top 8 as the fourth seed. His interest in Black-Red decks has been known since Pro Tour – Austin, which he won, as you may remember. It would be interesting to see if his neighbors would cooperate with him more due to this, or if they would use the information against him and try to force Kibler out of his comfort zone.


The draft started off on the right foot as Brian cracked open [Kazuul Warlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazuul+Warlord). He quickly drafted the powerful rare, but passed [Shatterskull Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatterskull+Giant), [Nimana Sell-Sword](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimana+Sell-Sword) and [Murasa Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murasa+Pyromancer). It would be a nice gift if any of them made it back.


 



![Kazuul Warlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kazuul+Warlord)

[Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) was the best non-Green card in the next pack, so Kibler passed along Turntimber Basilistk, [Mold Shambler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mold+Shambler) and [Nissa’s Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%E2%80%99s+Chosen). [Bladetusk Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bladetusk+Boar) was a welcome third pick that Kibler surely translated as Red being open, but he had to pass [Mind Sludge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sludge), [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) and [Magma Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Rift) to take it.

[Crypt Ripper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Ripper) fourth made it look like Black was available too, so Kibler settled into his favorite archetype. [Blood Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Seeker) came right behind it, followed by the solid [Giant Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Scorpion) over [Goblin Ruinblaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ruinblaster).


If there were any questions about Black being open, [Mind Sludge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sludge) seventh pick answered them. He took the powerful spell over [Guul Draz Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guul+Draz+Vampire) and [Whiplash Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whiplash+Trap) and then picked up [Slaughter Cry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Cry) over [Bog Tatters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Tatters).


[Molten Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Ravager), [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension), [Vampire’s Bite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire%E2%80%99s+Bite), [Pillarfield Ox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillarfield+Ox), [Unstable Footing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unstable+Footing) and [Sunspring Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspring+Expedition) closed out the pack for the Dragonmaster.


[Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) was a great piece of removal that could also go to the dome, but he had to ship a [Disfigure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) for it. Kibler took [Bladetusk Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bladetusk+Boar) over [Quest for the Gravelord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+the+Gravelord) next, and followed it up with [Surrakar Marauder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surrakar+Marauder) to continue the intimidate theme.


 



![Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vampire+Nighthawk)

[Soul Stair Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Stair+Expedition) over [Needlebite Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Needlebite+Trap) and some White cards was a slight disappointment, but a fifth pick [Stonework Puma](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonework+Puma) filled out the mana curve better than [Hagra Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Crocodile). [Nimana Sell-Sword](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimana+Sell-Sword) was taken over [Shatterskull Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatterskull+Giant) to go with his [Kazuul Warlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazuul+Warlord). [Zektar Shrine Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zektar+Shrine+Expedition) was next up over [Blood Tribute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Tribute), followed by [Goblin Ruinblaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ruinblaster) over another [Hagra Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Crocodile).

[Makindi Shieldmate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makindi+Shieldmate), [Slaughter Cry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Cry), [Blade of the Bloodchief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+of+the+Bloodchief), [Shieldmate’s Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shieldmate%E2%80%99s+Blessing), [Demolish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demolish) and [Caller of Gales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caller+of+Gales) ended pack two.


Kibler opened a sweet pack with [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel), [Living Tsunami](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Tsunami) and [Hideous End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+End). The removal spell made its way into the pile, but two people would be happy about the great flying creatures coming their way.


[Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) was an amazing second pick and Kibler tried his hardest not to smile at as he found a [Halo Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halo+Hunter) waiting for him next. Kibler favored a second [Giant Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Scorpion) over [Vampire Lacerator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Lacerator), followed by [Slaughter Cry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Cry) and [Seismic Shudder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Shudder). [Teetering Peaks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks) was taken over [Hagra Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Crocodile) next. [Blood Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Seeker), [Molten Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Ravager), [Blood Tribute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Tribute), [Zektar Shrine Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zektar+Shrine+Expedition), [Hedron Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab), [Scythe Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scythe+Tiger) and [Turntimber Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Grove) rounded out the draft for Kibler.


Pack three was a beautiful payoff for Brian, with the best uncommon and one of the best rares in his color coming to him. It definitely seemed like the people passing to him – Matt Sperling on his right and Brad Nelson on his left – cooperated with him. Check back to see if the Dragonmaster can take down Grand Prix – Minneapolis.

His follow up was to make his land drop, then bash for 4 before casting [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) and [Cliff Threader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cliff+Threader). Matt Sperling cast [Joraga Bard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Bard), but he needed more than a 1/4 to stop his opponent’s army of flyers. An unkicked [Oran-Rief Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Recluse) joined the cause for Sperling, but he lost access to his 3/3 [Umara Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umara+Raptor) when Bhagat used his second [Paralyzing Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paralyzing+Grasp) to lock the creature down.


[Ior Ruin Expedition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ior+Ruin+Expedition) finally found itself with three counters, and Matt cashed it in for two fresh cards. He didn’t have any plays, however, and sent the turn back to his opponent. Zohar found a land and attacked with his 4/4 [Windrider Eel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windrider+Eel) to put his opponent to 14. He cast a post-combat [Merfolk Seastalkers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Seastalkers), but was dismayed to see his opponent use [Whiplash Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whiplash+Trap) to bounce two creatures of his own: [Umara Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umara+Raptor) and [Deadly Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse). The 1/3 Spider came back down to blow up [Windrider Eel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windrider+Eel), but Bhagat continued on the warpath using his Seastalkers to tap his opponent’s reach creature and bashing for 4 from [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) and the Merfolk (thanks to Matt’s [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s).


Sperling was doing his best to stay alive, re-casting [Umara Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umara+Raptor) to block some of his opponent’s flyers, but the road was beginning to look long. Bhagat had doubled the number of [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern)s he had on the board, but a [Noble Vestige](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Vestige) from his opponent made the 2/1s less attractive as attackers. Still, with Seastalkers getting in each turn and keeping his opponent from attacking profitably, Zohar was in good shape.


[Seascape Aerialist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seascape+Aerialist) came down for Matt Sperling which merited a flurry of responses from Bhagat who had to use his Seastalkers in response to tap portions of his opponent’s team. Matt attacked for 3, then passed and Zohar moved all in. He cast [Whiplash Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whiplash+Trap) for five mana to bounce his opponent’s [Noble Vestige](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Vestige) and [Umara Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umara+Raptor). Then he turned his entire team sideways with only [Oran-Rief Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Recluse) available to block for Sperling. When he opted to get in front of a [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern), Bhagat revealed [Bold Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bold+Defense). Matt checked the math and shook his head.


Zohar Bhagat was the Grand Prix-Minneapolis champion!
