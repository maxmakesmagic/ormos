A New Cannon for Modern
=======================



 Posted in **Perilous Research**
 on February 20, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






 We're just one day away from Pro Tour *Born of the Gods*. The best players in the world have gathered in Valencia, Spain, with dreams of hoisting a trophy above their heads on Sunday afternoon. The Constructed portion of Pro Tour *Born of the Gods* is Modern. Modern is a fast-paced format with a huge pool of available cards. We'll be watching combo strategies that deal millions of damage on the fourth turn, aggressive decks that are capable of fast victories while effectively disrupting any opponent, and control decks that have access to some of the most skill-intensive cards ever. Today, in preparation for Pro Tour *Born of the Gods*, we'll be taking a look at the new landscape of the Modern format in its current state. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/features/2014/feat_hagon_wk07_287_ptbng.jpg)  
 Modern can be a confusing format to watch, especially if we haven't played much with the popular decks. Things will seem strange even for those of us who have been playing Modern in the last year or so. The banning of [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and unbanning of [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) heralded some big changes to the Modern format. Jund will likely fall out of favor while decks like Faeries and Zoo have new life breathed into them. A solid understanding of the major archetypes in the new Modern format will enhance our viewing experience by giving us the knowledge to have input on specific plays within a match. We've got a lot of decklists to cover in preparation for the Pro Tour; let's get to it!

 We'll kick things off by taking a look at [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) Storm. When playing [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) strategies, we're aiming to get our namesake enchantment in play and use it to start doubling inexpensive spells while creating huge amounts of mana with copied [Manamorphose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) and Rituals. Once we've sifted through enough of our library we can fire off some very large [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot)s to finish the game. The banning of [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) has reintroduced this strategy as one of the better combo strategies in the Modern format.

[Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) is the best one-card combo in Modern. When we have seven lands in play, we can cast [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) and deal 18 points of damage, which will usually be enough to end the game in a format with so many [shocklands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;format=+%5B%22Return+to+Ravnica+Block%22%5Damp;type=+%5B%22Land%22%5Damp;text=+%5Bpay%5D+%5B2%5D) and [fetch lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;type=+%5B%22Land%22%5Damp;text=+%5Bsearch%5D+%5Byour%5D+%5Blibrary%5Damp;set=%7C%5B%22Zendikar%22%5D). Things start to get crazy when there's a [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) on the table, though. [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) with six lands and [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) and we can fire off 72 points of damage. This deck gets to play a reasonably interactive game and it has a lot of individually powerful cards. The resilience and lack of reliance on the graveyard make this a strong combo choice for Pro Tour *Born of the Gods*.

[Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks are essentially powerful midrange strategies that happen to have a number of infinite combos hidden within. [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) allows players to abuse the persist mechanic if they have access to a sacrifice outlet like [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer). We can use [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) to deal infinite damage, or [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) to gain infinite life. The deck's combo interactions don't stop there, though. [Archangel of Thune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) in conjunction with [Spike Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) can make our team infinitely large while also gaining us infinite life. It's important for players to remain on the offensive against a deck like this; playing a reactionary game will be a futile endeavor for most non-[Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) decks.

 Red-White-Blue Control is the most successful dedicated control strategy in the current Modern format. The deck plays a lot of interactive removal and countermagic and eventually takes over the game with a big spell. The deck will enjoy the amount of Zoo it will get to prey on after [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl)'s unbanning, but [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) packs a longevity that's hard for a deck like this to deal with. Time will tell if this deck continues to do well with the new banned list, but it's looking like a very strong contender for the deck to beat going into Valencia.

Always surprising, and never disappointing, Faeries won the premier event last Sunday. Faeries plays like a tempo strategy, getting onto the board early and then controlling the game with interactive removal and disruption. Most of the deck's threats can be cast at instant speed, meaning that the deck uses all of its mana very efficiently on most turns. We're not sure how good Faeries can be in a format with a lot of aggressive Zoo decks, but the deck has proven its resilience time and time again.

[Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) has been one of the more successful combo strategies in Modern for some time now. The deck aims to win by assembling [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) or [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker+)  with [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) or [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) to make INFINITY FAERIES and win the game. The latest trend with [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) is to play it in a tempo/midrange strategy that just happens to have the capability of going infinite. This makes the deck a lot more resilient to hate by giving it additional routes to victory. Also, the presence of strong, hard countermagic and cards like [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) make the deck very good at breaking serve in a combo mirror where we lost the roll and have to be on the draw.
