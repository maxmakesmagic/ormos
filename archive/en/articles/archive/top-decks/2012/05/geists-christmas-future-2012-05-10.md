The Geists of Christmas Future
==============================



 Posted in [Top Decks](/en/articles/columns/top-decks-archive)
 on May 9, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 





Last week [here on Top Decks](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/td/193), we looked at some of the existing archetypes of the *Innistrad*-*Dark Ascension* two-thirds iteration of *Innistrad* Block... the so-called "Geists of Christmas Past and Present."


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td194_shi.jpg)
[Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto) | Art by [Cliff Childs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+[%22Cliff+Childs%22])


But this week in Barcelona is [Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored*](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/protour/avacynrestored12), the first big *Innistrad* Block event with new set *Avacyn Restored*. What does the future hold for players participating in the Pro Tour?


Here are six decks you will probably see this weekend, a gauntlet of decks I think are going to be relevant in the new format.

How does this deck work?


The mid-rangiest of midrange decks, this black-red-green strategy is good at killing creatures (often more than one at a time) and plays high-quality creatures.


New cards:


**[Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger)** 


[Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) was almost tailor made for a midrange (and obviously green) deck like the Jund colors. It has a respectable body that can trade with half a [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) or half a [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), scare off [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), or slow down a [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish). At the same time, it is a two-for-one card-drawing machine that fixes mana.


You might already know this but I have always had a soft spot for ye olde [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger).

**[Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)** 


This card is already acquiring a reputation as one of the best cards in the set and we haven't even seen a Pro Tour where it is legal yet. [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)—especially going late—is a good way to contain Planeswalkers and possibly kill other players. Last week, in "Geists of Christmas Past and Present," we saw that a large proportion of the existing shells for *Innistrad* Block Constructed are fast aggro decks... the kinds of decks that commit many small creatures to the battlefield... right into [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned).


And if you hit it on miracle?


Bananas!


[Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) on miracle is every kind of awesome: a [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) to the face; a [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) to any and all animals on the opposing offense.


**[Ulvenwald Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Tracker)** 


This is just a one-of in the sideboard, but it still seems like an exciting addition from *Avacyn Restored*. Not only is it a fast drop that allows a deck to seize the initiative from turn one, but when you have powerful, durable, or even unkillable (or undying) creatures on your side? The curtain will quickly fall for the other fellow's animals.


**[Abundant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth)** 


It's like a green [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas)!


Well... kind of the opposite, as it is more the kind of card you would enchant your own land with to fix colors rather than one you would use to manascrew the opponent. [Abundant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth) is a cheap and unspectacular cantrip card. It costs next to nothing to play, and ultimately nothing at all as it draws you a card.


Now, there are a fair number of tournament-quality cards in the history of *Magic: The Gathering* that merely cost very little and drew you a card, but this one also fixes your mana, so it is on the bonus.


I think [Abundant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth) might quietly prove itself one of the best cards in *Avacyn Restored*.

**[Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame)** 


Here is a card that is *also* both cheap and unspectacular. And even more certainly than [Abundant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth), [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) will prove a very important tool this weekend.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td194_pil.jpg)
[Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) | Art by [Karl Kopinski](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+[%22Karl+Kopinski%22])


As we've said on multiple occasions, the default strategy for *Innistrad* Block seems to include fast creatures supplemented by red spells. Well, [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) *is* a red spell... and is great against fast creatures!


In particular, this is a card that can trade straight up with undying paragons like [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) without any loss of card advantage, and does quite a number on [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), and [Stromkirk Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble) as well.

The Rock is a deck that often rises up to meet weenie beatdown strategies. It lacks the potential for red miracles, but The Rock has greater mana consistency on account of being only two colors—although it can make use of many of the same tools we saw with Jund's inheritors.


This deck runs a stack of durable and high-quality creatures. They all trade for two cards, draw extra cards, make more creatures, make mana, or more than one of these. Like the Jund-type decks, The Rock can make fine use of...


**[Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger)** 


Still a two-for-one and capable blocker and can help with difficult mana costs on [Vorapede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vorapede) while hooking up the necessary double-black for threats like [Bloodgift Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodgift+Demon).


**[Dark Impostor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Impostor)** 


Some decks are not very good at dealing with creatures at all, or at least not good at dealing with black creatures; those decks—if they give up enough time—might find themselves ground to powder by [Dark Impostor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Impostor).



![Dark Impostor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Dark%20Impostor&options=)


Obviously not the kind of card you want against flexible, fast, removal—or against super-fast aggro decks—but if you can slow down the battlefield with your blockers, [Dark Impostor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Impostor) can be a one-card ticket to inevitability.


**[Human Frailty](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Human+Frailty)** 


When I first saw this card, I thought it might be a sideboard card a la [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark)... fast, flexible when considered against very specific opponents, but ultimately the kind of card that can consistently contribute just due to its cost. I actually think [Human Frailty](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Human+Frailty) is a good deal better than I originally did, just based on the scope of different Humans you might have to kill.



![Human Frailty](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Human%20Frailty&options=)


[Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish)...


[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)...


[Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells)...


Many of the best *Innistrad* Block creatures across every color are Humans!


Let's face it, even with a smaller palette of potential targets (which is irrelevant considering the set it was printed in), this card would be priced awfully right.

How do you differentiate yourself against the Black-Green or even Black-Red-Green midrange decks in the field? Add not just the red back but another whole color! And while you're at it... make that LL in the casting costs!


The Four-Color Green deck plays a combination of powerful threats and answers... and has yet another mana fixer/accelerator—one that might be essential if you are going to go after BBB, RR, and LL... all in your base-green deck.


**[Sigarda, Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons)** 


What is there to say about Sigarda? Sigarda is actually one of my favorite cards in the set... it seems very "[Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)" to me; no lifelink, sure, but makes up for that with a level of durability almost unheard of.



![Sigarda, Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Sigarda,%20Host%20of%20Herons&options=)


**[Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)** 


It will be interesting to see how viable a deck like this one will prove in the long term. Personally, I love decks that jam tons of great cards into a single shell, but given the speed of [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), and [Vexing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Devil), you have to wonder about such varied and demanding mana requirements.


That said...


**[Vessel of Endless Rest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Endless+Rest)** 


...seems like quite the fixer. It isn't obviously powerful the way a [Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&name=+[Signet]) or [Everflowing Chalice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) is, but even at its higher mana cost, [Vessel of Endless Rest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Endless+Rest) does good work. For one, it fixes the mana for a deck with—again—BBB, RR, and LL all in a base-green deck.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td194_ves.jpg)
[Vessel of Endless Rest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Endless+Rest) | Art by [John Avon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+[%22John+Avon%22])


Plus, [Vessel of Endless Rest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Endless+Rest) has a handy little side effect. While you aren't always going to be able to hit its enters-the-battlefield ability profitably, sometimes you will be able to deuce a [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), or [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and take some oomph out of the opponent's graveyard. You know, a little something extra.

**[Appetite for Brains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Appetite+for+Brains)** 


A lot of players have been likening this card to [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), its spiritual mirror image. The reality is, if [Appetite for Brains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Appetite+for+Brains) is going to be good, it is going to be a more strategic card than [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) ever was. [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) preys on good decks playing good cards. You have open black on the first turn? Cast it! You are very likely to hit; and if you don't, your opponent's hand probably isn't very good.


[Appetite for Brains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Appetite+for+Brains), on the other hand, is a card that is going to reward patience. You have to look your opponent in the eye; give the old soul-read. Is this the right time to play it? This is a card that is going to feast around turn four or five, and in matchups when a particular threat is projected to bedevil you.

**[Vexing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Devil)** 


[Vexing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Devil) is one of the more controversial cards to come out of the new set. It is like a "punisher" tool from another age. [Browbeat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat) 2012.


On one hand, we have an absurd creature for R... bigger and less dangerous to its master than [Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup). On the other hand, we have a card that deals more than [Lava Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Spike). Problem? The opponent gets to pick.


So here's the thing: [Vexing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Devil) might be a star in a bigger format like Modern or Extended, where it will have [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s and [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)s to play with. Well what does that say about its chances in a tiny format like Block? Less redundancy, sure, but its relative power level is quite a bit above the average, doncha think?

First, how does this deck work?


The Rites deck uses [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) and [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) to improve its hand *and* dump creatures into the graveyard. Well... creatures and other stuff. You can easily dump both a big guy and an [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) into the graveyard in a single swoop, allowing you to flash back on turn four.

[Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) has an amazing set of abilities that work together along with its massive size. Drawing a new hand at will is the kind of superpower that will win games, and lifelink lets you counterbalance against the steep cost in life points.


Oh, and it doesn't take very long to win with a black creature this size that, you know, flies.

**[Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts)** 


A really powerful game changer. Literally a game changer in the sense that it can turn the game on its ear. A 3/3 haste creature was viable (if at a fringe level only) at 2RR; and a [Threaten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threaten)/[Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason)/[Traitorous Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitorous+Blood) of some stripe has been continuously viable in almost every format where there was a Red Deck to seize up a [Silvos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvos). In sum, at a hefty five mana, [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) is actually a deal by maybe RR.


While this is undoubtedly an [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) deck, it can access many of its high-quality creatures the old fashioned way—not just [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts)—but [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) on four, and via some bending over backwards, can even hit the [Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=+240033).


So anyway, here are a few decks to consider going into a PT that has inspired not one, not two, but two-and-a-half super teams.
