Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Craig Wescoe's White-Black Aggro](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppro16/9-64-decklists-2016-10-23)

At [Grand Prix Providence](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gppro16), Craig Wescoe finished 28th with the Craig Wescoe-iest of decks: white-based aggro. It also had black cards, but just barely. It's essentially a mono-white main deck splashing for the activated ability on [Scrapheap Scrounger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger), plus the odd [Shambling Vent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambling+Vent) activation, but there are a number of cool things going on here.


First is [Stitcher's Graft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Graft), which loses much of its downside when [Always Watching](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Always+Watching) is in play or when [Aerial Responder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aerial+Responder) picks it up. Make your own [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) and all that. Meanwhile, although the deck is essentially a white attacking deck, it has several ways to gain card advantage, both virtual and real. [Smuggler's Copter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) is, of course, present, and [Thraben Inspector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Inspector) is its usual Clue-giving self, but there's also [Selfless Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit), which can blank a lot of removal.


But the best thing the deck had going for it was its pilot—Craig Wescoe, king of attacking with small white creatures.
