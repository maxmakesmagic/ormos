Spotlight Cube Series – Classic Cube
====================================



 Posted in **Magic Online**
 on August 18, 2021 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Ryan Saxe











My name is Ryan Saxe ([@rcsaxe](http://twitter.com/rcsaxe)), and I am extremely excited to share my *Classic Cube* with the world!


My vision for this cube is **"*Powered Cube*, but where the fast mana comes at a cost."** *Powered Cube* is an awesome experience, particularly because playing with Moxen and the like feels special. My goal is to let players power things out early while making those interactions more engaging. In *Classic Cube*, Moxen and other early fast-mana cards like [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring) and [Mana Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Crypt) are replaced by cards like [Lotus Petal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal), [Crystal Vein](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Vein), and [City of Traitors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors). You can still power out a crazy play on turn one or turn two, but you also need to calculate the risks associated with throwing away resources to do that. This leads to an unpowered cube that plays out much closer to *Vintage Cube* than *Legacy Cube*.


On top of my initial vision for this cube, I have three other purposeful design decisions:


**1. Let Players Discover**


*Classic Cube* is a "classic cube," so it is mostly full of historically good cards. But that doesn't mean you can't come up with novel strategies in the draft. I've seen players use [Fae of Wishes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fae+of+Wishes) to grab [Oath of Druids](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) from their sideboard to mill their whole deck and take infinite turns with [Nexus of Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nexus+of+Fate). This cube is seeded with so many mini combos that, if you pay attention during the draft, you can always assemble some secret sauce!


**2. Archetypal Diversity**


I believe it is important to support many flavors of any color combination, and I purposefully seed unconventional cards in order to change the texture of what different archetypes look like. For example, Azorius Control can look very different if it ends up with [Approach of the Second Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Approach+of+the+Second+Sun) as a finisher instead of planeswalkers like [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion). Every card in the cube is there for a reason, which allows the drafter to pursue different perturbations of any archetype and express creativity.


**3. Create Tension with Speed**


While there are many sweet things to do in this cube, you cannot ignore the speed of the combo decks and aggressive decks. The decks in this cube will trample all over you if you can't interact with them. Prioritize having a density of cards low on the curve regardless of archetype in order to keep up with the speed of other decks. This has the benefit of having games feel intense right from the get-go.


Archetypes
==========


Green Creature Combo
--------------------


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kitchen+Finks)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vizier+of+Remedies)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Birthing+Pod)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)
My favorite archetype in my cube is a green deck focused on assembling a combo with some creatures. I love this deck because it's all about playing a midrange strategy with good creatures while pairing that with tutors to assemble an "I win" button if the opponent can't interact. It is the perfect [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck!


There are way too many viable combos for me to put into this article, but the most common and supported variant is persist. Draft a creature with persist, a sacrifice outlet, and any of the following cards: [The Great Henge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Great+Henge), [Vizier of Remedies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies), [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast), [Good-Fortune Unicorn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Good-Fortune+Unicorn), [Grumgully, the Generous](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grumgully%2C+the+Generous), [Metallic Mimic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallic+Mimic), or [Anafenza, Kin-Tree Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit). I should note that there are quite a few other ways to combo off with these cards, too. For example, [Basri's Lieutenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basri%27s+Lieutenant) can work instead of a persist creature alongside most of these.


This strategy is most commonly Selesnya with a splash, but basically any green color pair will work. Furthermore, if you find yourself playing half of a combo and have some tutors, it could be a good idea to throw in the other half: Any green deck that plays [Devoted Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) and has some X spells can toss in [Vizier of Remedies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies). Any deck that's playing [Walking Ballista](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) can add [Heliod, Sun-Crowned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%2C+Sun-Crowned), and at that point maybe [Spike Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder). Don't be afraid to speculate on half a combo in this cube!


Blue Control Combo
------------------


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Splinter+Twin)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thassa%27s+Oracle)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Oracle)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hullbreacher)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hullbreacher)
Blue control is an all-time favorite Cube archetype. In a cube with as many combos as this one, it's pretty common for a combo to be the win condition in a control deck. Rather than trying to win the game with something like an [Aetherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=368961), blue decks in this cube will control the board until they can do something powerful with their counter magic up. And it really can be anything. Throw a [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) and a [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) in your Izzet deck. If you have enough non-basics, play [Thassa's Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Oracle) alongside [Tainted Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact). If you end up with [Timetwister](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timetwister), then try and pair it with [Hullbreacher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hullbreacher).


There are a lot of combos in the cube, and throwing basically any of them in a deck with [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) and [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) is rarely a bad idea.


Cheatty Combo
-------------


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Reanimate)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sneak+Attack)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Oath+of+Druids)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids)
Out of all the combos in the cube, some have more support than others. The examples I gave above with [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), [Thassa's Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Oracle), and [Hullbreacher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hullbreacher) are cool, but the combo pieces don't have enough redundancy to yield a hyper-linearized version of that strategy. Cheating things into play, however, has a pretty high density. There are a lot of large creatures to put into play, and lots of tutoring, card draw, and discard outlets in blue, red, and black. This deck should be viable and strong at every table.


Welder/Artifacts
----------------


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Welder)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Welder)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tinker)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sundering+Titan)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan)
Most commonly Izzet, but occasionally Rakdos, this deck is all about artifacts. Play as many mana rocks as you can and get a big artifact into play; or just cast [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). One of the other benefits of this deck is that the strategy doesn't fall on its face without access to the expensive artifacts. [Goblin Welder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Welder) can gain good value off cards like [Retrofitter Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retrofitter+Foundry) and [Chromatic Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star). And as far as value goes, there is redundancy in [Emry, Lurker of the Loch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emry%2C+Lurker+of+the+Loch), [Goblin Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Engineer), and [Daretti, Scrap Savant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daretti%2C+Scrap+Savant). My main word of advice is not to forget about discard outlets, as getting the artifacts into the graveyard is the hardest part.


Izzet Spells
------------


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Young+Pyromancer)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thing+in+the+Ice)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thing+in+the+Ice)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Awoken+Horror)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awoken+Horror)Transform[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Magmatic+Channeler)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magmatic+Channeler)
Thanks to the increased density of cheap red interaction and cheap blue cantrips, it is not uncommon to end up in an Izzet deck where most cards in the deck are instants and sorceries. This deck most commonly splashes white for [Monastery Mentor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery+Mentor) but can also splash black with good enough mana to pair cards like [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) or gain access to [Sedgemoor Witch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedgemoor+Witch).


The Rock
--------


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tarmogoyf)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grim+Flayer)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dark+Confidant)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)
This is a Golgari midrange deck that often has ways to leverage the graveyard. Whether that is through [Delirium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium), reanimation, or recursion is dependent on the cards you have access to. This deck very often splashes for gold cards like [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), [Oko, Thief of Crowns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oko%2C+Thief+of+Crowns), or [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). [Emrakul, the Promised End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End) is often the best top-end if the deck is properly constructed to use it, and black comes with the tools to tutor for it. Additionally, there is a variant of this deck that is more heavily red to play [Dragon's Rage Channeler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon%27s+Rage+Channeler) and [Ragavan, Nimble Pilferer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ragavan%2C+Nimble+Pilferer) on turn one.


Green Ramp
----------


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Natural+Order)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rofellos%2C+Llanowar+Emissary)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rofellos%2C+Llanowar+Emissary)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)
It wouldn't be a *Classic Cube* if it didn't support one of the most "classic" Cube archetypes: green ramp. Drafting all the mana elves and expensive creatures is definitely a viable strategy, although you don't have to go all in. I personally prefer to draft green decks with less top-end, less ramp, and more of the midrange three- to five-drops that I can play ahead of curve with mana dorks. This leaves me less vulnerable to situations where my opponent can remove my [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) all while letting me gain an advantage via ramp. That is why my cube lacks [Hornet Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen) and [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) and fills those slots with cards like [Voracious Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voracious+Hydra) and [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless).


Aggro
-----


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Usher+of+the+Fallen)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Usher+of+the+Fallen)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodsoaked+Champion)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodsoaked+Champion)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Guide)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)
This cube supports aggressive decks in three colors: white, black, and red. Given this, it is quite rare for nobody to draft an aggressive deck at any given table, and it's not uncommon for there to be two. Each color, including hybrid cards, has access to at least ten one-drops, so these strategies are reliable and fast. Each aggressive deck plays a little differently, too. Each deck also has a subtheme. This subtheme is rarely a key part to success with the archetype, but it's a cool thing to do when the pieces come together.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Benalish+Marshal)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benalish+Marshal)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Armageddon)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant)
The white aggressive deck has classic tax-style cards like [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) and [Esper Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Sentinel). To compete past the first few turns, it can scale those up with cards like [Benalish Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benalish+Marshal), cast planeswalkers like [Elspeth, Knight-Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant), or close the gap for the opponent to come back with [Armageddon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon) and [Ravages of War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravages+of+War).


**Humans subtheme**: The white deck in this cube has a Humans subtheme, making [Thalia's Lieutenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%27s+Lieutenant) and [Elspeth, Sun's Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Nemesis) better than normal. Additionally, it can create some fun deck-building challenges to maximize [Winota, Joiner of Forces](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winota%2C+Joiner+of+Forces).


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gravecrawler)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hymn+to+Tourach)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Contamination)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contamination)
Black aggro has been in my cube since its inception and has put up high-performing results forever. What black provides that red and white don't are hand disruption and recursion. Picking apart opponents' hands is one of the best forms of disruption in cubes of this power level. Pair that form of disruption with threats that are resilient to removal, and you have a recipe for success.


**Aristocrats subtheme**: Many cubes support a Rakdos Aristocrats deck, but it's a little different in this cube. [Judith, the Scourge Diva](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judith%2C+the+Scourge+Diva), [Goblin Bombardment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bombardment), and [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) are all fantastic splashes for a black aggro deck. Think of [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) as an extension of black aggro rather than its own separate thing.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hazoret+the+Fervent)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hazoret+the+Fervent)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sulfuric+Vortex)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfuric+Vortex)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Eidolon+of+the+Great+Revel)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+the+Great+Revel)
Red has the fastest variant of aggro: cast one-drops, cast burn spells, and finish the game with some resilient top-end like [Hazoret the Fervent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hazoret+the+Fervent) or [Sulfuric Vortex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfuric+Vortex). It's a classic Cube recipe, and it's a good one.


**Artifacts subtheme**: The introduction of [Urza's Saga](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Saga) and the red [Goblin Welder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Welder) archetype creates a fascinating perturbation of mono-red. There are many cheap artifacts to play, the best being Equipment, and this helps turn on [Dragon's Rage Channeler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon%27s+Rage+Channeler), too.
