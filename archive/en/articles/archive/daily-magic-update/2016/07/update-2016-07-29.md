The July 29, 2016 Update
========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on July 29, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on July 29, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by Australia, where there's a Grand Prix this weekend and a Pro Tour next. It's also where I, and a bunch of other people, will be traveling to next week. As such, the Daily *Magic* Update is going on hiatus for a week, since I can't be awake when the US sites update and all of the ringers who would otherwise step in for me will also be in Australia. We'll have an update on Monday to capture what happens over this weekend, but then the Daily *Magic* Update will not return until the following Tuesday.


I'll still try to highlight some cool content I find on Twitter, so if you're not already following me on there [@blakepr](https://twitter.com/blakepr), now would be an excellent time to do so.


What to Watch This Weekend
==========================


1. [Grand Prix Sydney](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsyd16)

This is a big weekend. It's the first look at high-level *Eldritch Moon* Limited ahead of the Pro Tour. It's the conclusion of [a thrilling Grand Prix Master chase](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/top-players/statistics/2015-16-grand-prix-master). And it all starts—tonight! Thanks to Australia living in the future, coverage begins at 11 a.m. local time on Saturday—which is 6 p.m. here on the West Coast and 9 p.m. on the East Coast. Might I suggest asking your [local game store](http://locator.wizards.com) to play coverage during FNM? Coverage will be on [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic).


1. [StarCityGames.com Baltimore Open](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/300716_baltimore_fact_sheet.html)

The Open Series features the second batch of Standard for the season, and the big question is, will anyone bring anything to defeat [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)? Or are people still holding back ahead of the Pro Tour? Defeating our new winged counterspell isn't easy, so watch to see what players come up with starting 10 a.m. ET/7 a.m. PT on [twitch.tv/scglive](https://www.twitch.tv/scglive).

Decks of the Day
================


1. [Colorless Eldrazi Ramp](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=105748)

This one comes out of a StarCityGames.com Invitational Qualifier, where it took first place in the hands of Travis Morton, and it is *not* on many people's radars.


The deck is the natural result of pushing colorless-matters, but, in this case, it seems to have completely worked. There are zero cards in the deck that require red, white, black, green, or blue mana, which means the deck is jam-packed with utility lands—and Eldrazi.


Unlike some of the midrange Eldrazi decks we've seen, this is a straight-up ramp deck, and it appears colorless has the tools to do just that. [Hedron Crawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crawler) has seen some play as essentially a [Mind Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone), [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) is an all-star even in green ramp decks, and [Oblivion Sower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Sower) is wildly underplayed for its size and ability to ramp well past six mana.


Plus, the deck gets to play a ton of utility lands. [Spawning Bed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Bed)s, [Westvale Abbey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Westvale+Abbey)s, [Mage-Ring Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage-Ring+Network)s, [Foundry of the Consuls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+of+the+Consuls)—while most decks have to dip their toes into those pools, this deck just gets to play four of each. That gives the deck a ton of play even when it floods. And when you're playing things like [Warden of Geometries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warden+of+Geometries) for even more ramp, flooding is certainly possible.


And the cool part? After a certain threshold, all of your business spells are [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)-proof.
