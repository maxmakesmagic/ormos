Round 2: Gary Wise vs. Katsuhiro Mori
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 3, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_157.png)
By Ben Ronaldson











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a944.jpg)Earlier on this year Gary had predicted that Katsuhiro would make his first Pro Tour top 8 this season and had played him twice since. Neither player was particularly happy to be facing each other due to mutual respect for each other's games.

Gary was running a Kamahl and a [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) along with a few strong removal cards in black, but it was a bit of a three colour monstrosity as far as mana went. If he had good land draws he felt he could do reasonably well with it for the next three rounds.

Katsuhiro was sporting a very aggressive r/g beat down deck.

### Game 1

A Second turn [Mad Dog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Dog) saw the offense begin for Katsuhiro, followed by a [Minotaur Explorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minotaur+Explorer) (discarding an [Ivy Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Elemental)). Gary could not manage much defense to start off with as all he could produce was a [Think Tank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Tank) to help his fourth turn draw out. Mori then made a [Krosan Beast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Beast), which was eaten by a [Slithery Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slithery+Stalker), but Wise was on low enough life by this stage that he had to chump the [Minotaur Explorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minotaur+Explorer) and the [Krosan Beast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Beast) came back into play to join a freshly recruited [Seton's Scout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seton%27s+Scout). The Squirrel had not become a giant at that stage, but the creatures on the table were easily enough to finish of the affable Canadian Englishman.

### Game 2

After quite some thought Gary decided to draw. "You're welcome," he said with a wry smile.

Once again the turn two [Mad Dog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Dog) made an appearance, but had no turn three play to back it up. Gary could do nothing till turn four, when he made a [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) and correctly guessed that an Elephant token would come into play at the end of his turn. The Broker was duly [Flame Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst)ed. Wise insinuated that it wasn't allowed with a smile, knowing how much trouble he was already in, then [Thermal Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thermal+Blast)ed the Elephant Token.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a945.jpg)When Gary managed to summon an [Aven Windreader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader) the [Mad Dog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Dog) had finally met it's match, but the 3/3 flyer was then facing down a [Krosan Archer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Archer) and a Krosan Restrictor. No problem – he '[Repel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repel)led' the Archer and cast [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict) on the Restrictor, leaving his [Aven Windreader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader) free to attack for a turn. By this stage Mori had managed to reach the five mana needed to Flash back his [Elephant Ambush](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Ambush). Wise traded a Soul Skirge with it – relieved that his opponent did not have a pump spell.

Soon afterwards Gary's [Aven Windreader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader) was [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper)ed and the board was cleared for a moment when Gary flashed back the Edict to kill the Archer that had been recast after the [Repel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repel).

Suddenly gaining the advantage Gary summoned a [Cabal Torturer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer) and a Barbarian outcast – [Afflict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Afflict)ing his own Barbarian after combat to push home the advantage with whatever he might draw – a [Fledgling Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Imp). This secured game 2.

The edict was flashed back and Gary made a [Cabal Torturer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer) and a [Barbarian Outcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Outcast), [Afflict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Afflict)ing it after combat to find a new threat – a [Fledgling Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Imp) that sealed the game.

Gary started considering [Immobilizing Ink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immobilizing+Ink)s, but elected not to put them in.

### Game 3

Katsuhiro elected to play first but managed neither a turn 2 nor turn 3 play.

"Go?" inquired Gary, happy to be able to make the first creature for once – a [Fledgling Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Imp). When Mori then seemingly failed to make a fourth turn play Gary was suspicious of the [Elephant Ambush](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Ambush) again (Mori had 2 in his deck), but attacked into the untapped four mana anyway. Sure enough the Elephant came, but Gary had an [Afflict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Afflict) and the two creatures traded.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a943.jpg)Once again the Japanese made no play in his own turn and Gary was then facing five untapped mana. Nevertheless he made a [Pardic Firecat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pardic+Firecat) and swung over with haste. This seemed to work that turn, but the next turn, Mori now having 6 mana open, the Firecat hit a second Elephant. Shrugging, Wise cast [Churning Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Churning+Eddy) and killed the token and bounced a land.

Once again there were no creatures on the board and Gary was feeling reasonably confident I imagine, as he was still on 20 life against the beat down deck. Mori then made a [Minotaur Explorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minotaur+Explorer) (discarding [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)), and cast [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) on Wise's [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker).

After Gary had made a [Barbarian Outcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Outcast) Katsuhiro started pulling some good cards from the top of his deck – his next few draws being: Constrictor, Archer, land, [Ivy Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Elemental), [Seton's Desire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seton%27s+Desire)! (Which won the game). On top of this he could also flash back his Elephant tokens from the bin. In the meantime all Gary could muster was a Soul Skirge, [Dirty Wererat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dirty+Wererat) and a [Coral Net](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Net).

When the [Seton's Desire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seton%27s+Desire) was put on the Archer there was nothing Wise could do as a huge [Ivy Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Elemental) sailed over for the victory.

**Final Result:** Katsuhiro Mori won 2-1.
