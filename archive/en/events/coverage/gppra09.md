Grand Prix Prague 2009
----------------------


Schmidt Did It!
===============




September 4-5, 2009











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/end_blurb.jpg)Drawing cards has always been really good in Magic, and Jan Schmidt proved that truth today. The German shot out from nowhere to take down Yuuya Watanabe in the finals of Grand Prix Prague. With [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter)s and three [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination)s in his draft deck, Schmidt ousted some stiff competition, getting his first ten pro points ever in the process. For Yuuya Watanabe, it was the third foiled attempt at taking a GP title – in three consecutive GP tournaments! That in itself is a feat worth noting.


The eight pro points that Watanabe gets give him a push to a fourth place in the Player of the Year race, tied with his fellow countryman Tomoharu Saito. A handful of other pros picked up a point here and there when all was said and done in Prague. Magic 2010, with its throwback to the fantasy in days of yore, attracted many of them and also old-timers like the 1997 World Champion Jakub Slemr, himself in contention for Top 8 until the very end.


Now the halls of Grand Prix Prague no longer lie under the spell of Magic 2010. For two days and 18 rounds of high-level Magic, M10 Limited held more than 1500 players in its sway. From Prague, Tobias Henke and Hanno Terbuyken on the text coverage and Richard Hagon on the audio side of things brought you the nitty-gritty of a Magic Grand Prix.


Join us here at dailymtg.com for the upcoming Grand Prix Melbourne, and of course Pro Tour Austin in October!

Top 8 - Drafting with Yuuya Watanabe
------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

The Top 8 has a total of 139 Pro Points, 500 less than the last two GPs, and less than ten percent of Grand Prix Brussels 2008, which featured Kamiel Cornelissen, Gabriel Nassif, Raphael Levy, and Antoine Ruel! Now here, Yuuya Watanabe carries approximately two-thirds of those points on his shoulders alone. He is the clear favorite among the eight, so naturally, we went to watch his draft.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/draft_yuuya_watanabe1.JPG)And it kicked off with a bang: [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess). Not only was Watanabe going for the commonly preferred color black, he also got one of the cards you cannot pass on! And [Cemetery Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper) as his second pick just sweetened the deal.


From there on, Watanabe was locked into black. He considered the occasional off-color card, like [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) as third pick and [Howl of the Night Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howl+of+the+Night+Pack) seventh (after already letting one go through in the sixth pick). Besides that, he got from the first booster (in order): [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat)s, [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness), [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat), [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath), [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) (over [Celestial Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Purge)), and assorted last picks.


Booster two had [Looming Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade) and [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) staring at Watanabe. He took the precious removal card, and then the black aria continued. [Black Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Knight) 2nd, [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night) 3rd, [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons), [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood), and then he had a sixth-pick decision between [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul), [Wall of Bone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Bone) and [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), having cut off black pretty well to his left. He chose [Wall of Bone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Bone), picked [Terramorphic Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) (over [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel)) 7th, got [Windstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windstorm) 8th out of an otherwise empty pack and finished off with a 9th pick [Looming Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade).


Oh wait, he didn’t! [Underworld Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams) had tabled around to him again as 13th pick, and he hesitated not a second to put it in his pile.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/draft_yuuya_watanabe2.JPG)Booster three saw Watanabe having a choice between [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness), [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism), [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage), [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul) and [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night). Giving away solid black was inevitable, so Watanabe decided he wanted [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) most out of that pack. More removal came in pick two there, when he had to decide between [Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin) and [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)! He took all the time he had, but when the judge called “Draft!”, Watanabe picked the obvious choice with [Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin), although he was sad to see the [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) go.


Grave Digger over [Dread Warlock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Warlock) was pick three, then [Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) (over [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night) and [Rhox Pikemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Pikemaster)). [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul), [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat)s (over [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath)) and a random [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) were his next picks. At pick 8, Watanabe once again had to make a decision: [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood), [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) #2 or another Drudge Skeleton? He chose the card drawer, which would go well with his [Underworld Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams) as a fancy little combo.


Pick nine was [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul) as obvious black pick, but Watanabe also saw [Inferno Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Elemental) and decided that one was to dangerous to be around. The rest of booster three gave him random late picks, but nothing relevant to his deck.


During deck construction, Watanabe hedged his bets. “The deck is so-so,” he said, not wanting to commit to any prediction. The deck has game-winners, but the supporting structure could have been a little better. He was especially sad that no further [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) had found their way to him. But Liliana? “How lucky!” And of course, a rock-solid mana base and playing skills should be enough to carry Watanabe further than the quarterfinals at least. In the end, Watanabe ended up splashing his few blue cards ([Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), [Alluring Siren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alluring+Siren)) into the mainly black deck.




Quarterfinals - Marcel Kondrk [SVK] vs. Yuuya Watanabe [JPN]
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

**Game 1:**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/QF_Yuuya_Watanabe.JPG)*Repeatable tutoring is good, we hear.*The first game was firmly a card advantage affair. Watanabe had his two [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood)s early on and followed them up with [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess) and Cemetary Reaper. That kept Kondrk’s hand small and his board under pressure. Even [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) fom the 15-year old Slovak could only do so much.


Looking through Kondrk’s graveyard after the first few turns also revealed that his card quality was pretty low overall, save for one [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) that had a brief stint as removal spell for two [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) – one of which regenerated.


When the army of darkness Yuuya’s [Cemetery Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper) amassed became too much for the empty-handed Slovak, Kondrk scooped.


Marcel Kondrk 0 – 1 Yuuya Watanabe


**Game 2:**


Watanabe led with [Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin), but Kondrk had [Seismic Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Strike) to prevent the Assassin from Assassinating – for a moment at least, as Watanabe used [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) to get the dagger-and-cloak guy back. However, [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) and [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) for Kondrk let the Slovak develop his board into [Fiery Hellhound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Hellhound) and [Berserkers of Blood Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserkers+of+Blood+Ridge), putting Watanabe at 13 with just the [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) in play.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/QF_Marcel_Kondrk.JPG)*Outclassed in cards and pro points, Kondrk offered little resistance.*[Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin) came back and [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) weakened the Berserkers, but Watanabe still took 6 from them and the Hellhounds. 7 life, and Kondrk sat at a comfortable 20! [Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin), though, was still there to do his dirty work on [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage).


Kondrk attacked, and Yuuya cleared the board by blocking with [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) and his new [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath). [Centaur Courser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser) and [Prized Unicorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prized+Unicorn) from Zunko were no match for Yuuya’s [Cemetery Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper), especially with [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) on the Unicorn.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/sf_yuuya_watanabe.JPG)*Yuuya Watanabe*Zunko put [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll) onto the battlefield, but green decks have shown this weekend that they are notoriously prone to dying to any flying army. Drawing cards with [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood), making a Zombie out of every creature Zunko’s graveyard offered: Yuuya knew he had to make every move count before [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) would decimate his team. [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) from Yuuya removed the Pyromancer from the battlefield before it could do any harm, and of course that game [Cemetery Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper) yet another Zombie. The combination of [Alluring Siren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alluring+Siren) forcing small creatures to attack, a 3/3 Zombie blocking them and [Cemetery Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper) then making more Zombies out of them was really getting into gear, but Zunko kept casting small critters. What else should he do? Either get them into play and eaten, or get them discarded by Liliana. Watanabe essentially had Zunko locked out, and the Slovenian was in eternal topdeck mode.


Realizing that, Zunko packed it in.


Yuuya Watanabe 1 – 0 Bojan Zunko

Watanabe replied with [Cemetery Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper), also a quite good card.


Zunko had [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll). When Watanabe played [Alluring Siren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alluring+Siren), the board position mirrored that of game 1 almost to a tee: Watanabe even had the [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons)! The [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat) that Watanabe found didn’t even matter, as the Japanese had enough creatures anyway to crack Zunko’s life total in two big swings.

Semifinals - Ognjen Cividini [HRV] vs. Jan Schmidt [DEU]
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon*

After a lengthy Quarter Final, Jan Schmidt of Germany faced the Croatian Ognjen Cividini, himself fresh from a routine victory in his opener in this Top 8. Both players featured White, with Cividini adding plenty of Black, and Schmidt majoring in Blue for tricks aplenty on both sides.


**Game 1:**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/sf_cividini.jpg)*Ognjen Cividini*Schmidt led with [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), and the now-familiar pattern of turn two [White Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=White+Knight) for Cividini followed. [White Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=White+Knight) number two and three came up next for Cividini, meaning only Gladys (Knight) was missing from the full set. [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night) was his only non-Knight as he smashed into a board featuring the [Wall of Frost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Frost) and a newly-laid [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon). [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) and [Divine Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Verdict) sprayed the battlefield, and the German was assuming control. [Holy Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Holy+Strength) functioned as a removal spell, finally finding a way to target the [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant), but [Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph) continued the 3/4 flying theme, dropping Cividini to five, then two. [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) became active for Schmidt, who piled through his deck with the Looter and [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination).


[Serra Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel) for Cividini looked to give him hope, but [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) was a deadly answer from Schmidt. Only one mana remained for Cividini, which was enough for [Harm’s Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%E2%80%99s+Way) to send two points of damage back onto Schmidt’s [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter). Even so, he was at just one life, and there was no second chance, as the [Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph) crashed over for the win.


Schmidt 1 – 0 Cividini.


**Game 2:**


Cividini invited Schmidt to open the second game, which he did with a turn two [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) off a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) and [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress). Three flying damage dropped him to fifteen, and Schmidt reloaded with [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) before passing the turn.


The Ghoul lived up to its name and went on the Warpath, Cividini making some nice defense with [Razorfoot Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin). Schmidt therefore went back to the ground, bringing out a hefty [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon). [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) joined the Croatian side, leading Schmidt to dig further into his deck with a second [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination). ![Armored Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Armored+Ascension)

The life totals were soon twelve apiece as [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul) and [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) returned the favor. Schmidt was happy to block the Aristocrat with [Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph), and [Harm’s Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%E2%80%99s+Way) from Cividini sent the [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) to the winged graveyard in the sky.

**Game 3:**


[Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) opened for Schmidt, while Cividini had a start of [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) and [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul), which quickly dropped Schmidt to fifteen, followed up by [Rod of Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rod+of+Ruin) – rather good against a German board position that now had a second [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/sf_schmidt.jpg)*Jan Schmidt*[Gargoyle Castle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gargoyle+Castle) helped to fuel [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) for Schmidt, who cast [Solemn Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Offering) at the [Rod of Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rod+of+Ruin), killing it handily, and speeding him back to nineteen, if only briefly. In came Cividini’s team, but [Razorfoot Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin) met [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), and the German was back to screaming his way through his deck with red hot Looter action. [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon) ended his turn, but Cividini had nothing to offer, so priority was soon back with Schmidt.


[Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph) looked to tilt things his way, but [Serra Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel) was bigger and badder, until [Ice Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage) encased it in frosty uselessness. The Seraph dealt three, and [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) arrived for Schmidt. Cividini had the [Holy Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Holy+Strength) trick ready, getting rid of the [Ice Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage) and piling in with the still-awesome [Serra Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel), now with added [Holy Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Holy+Strength) mitigating the [Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph) ability.


Thirteen played eleven, as [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) had at least temporary control of the game, with Cividini adding [White Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=White+Knight)s aplenty, neither of which were an answer to a 3/4 flyer backed up by a tapping [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage). And then there was the [Gargoyle Castle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gargoyle+Castle) to worry about...


Schmidt pushed the go button on the 3/4 at end of turn, and six damage left Cividini at just four life. One more activation of the [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage)... one more foray into the red zone by Gargoyle and Guardian...


...and we were done. Cividini had pulled off an epic win in the second game, but it wasn’t enough. And Schmidt, in that Game 2? He had the [Divine Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Verdict). And the [Safe Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Passage).


Schmidt 2 – 1 Cividini.

Finals - Yuuya Watanabe [JPN] vs. Jan Schmidt [DEU]
---------------------------------------------------


*by Tobias Henke*

This is it: the final battle of the tournament, the final match, for glory, for the trophy, for the title, and for eternal fame. Facing off against each other are Japan’s Yuuya Watanabe and Germany’s Jan Schmidt. The former is a seasoned veteran of the international pro scene and on a hot streak right now that saw him make top eight at three consecutive Grand Prix around the globe. The latter is here in his first top eight and, naturally, the underdog. If he is one of the sort that barks or one that bites, however, is yet to be determined.


Watanabe had the fist play of the game with [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) on turn two, but that would not do him any good, as Schmidt immediately took to the air with [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant). Both players skipped a turn without play, then Watanabe had [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat), while Schmidt went for [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/f_watanabe.jpg)*Yuuya Watanabe*On turn five, Watanabe went to the think tank for quite some time, before finally emerging with [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess), making Schmidt discard and then chump-blocking [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant). Schmidt refilled his hand through [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) but had no other play. Right now, his position in this game completely depended on [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant), possibly the most fragile creatures in the format. Yuuya tried for [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger), but Schmidt had [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter). Unable to sustain his Planeswalker any longer, Watanabe tutored for a card, before Schmidt took it down by attacking with his Servant and [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter).


[Looming Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade) and [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath) came down for Watanabe, [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage), [Wall of Frost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Frost), a second Looter, *and* [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) for Schmidt. Now his deck was operating on full power, cycling lots of cards every turn with the help of the amazing Merfolk. Watanabe had [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) to get rid of [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant) (too late, sadly) and then another to kill [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus), but with two active Looters, Schmidt was in no need to hurry. In fact, he was already beginning to discard [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination). He summoned [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon), then cast and used [Magebane Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magebane+Armor). A 5/9 Mastodon went to attack; it died in a trple-block but left Watanabe with a lonely [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) to Schmidt’s Blining Mage, [Wall of Frost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Frost) and two [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter)s.


It didn’t take long before Watanabe scooped up his cards and the players started shuffling for Game 2.


**Yuuya Watanabe 0 – 1 Jan Schmidt**


Playing first, Watanabe took on the role of aggressor right from the start with [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night), then [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat), and double [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) on turn four. To this point, all Schmidt had to offer was one [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), but the Merfolk dug up one of the finest blockers available in [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel). Watanabe just attacked with his [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons), regenerating one of them (blocked by [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel)) and getting in a total of one damage.


Next, Schmidt summoned [Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph) to shut off this option as well as providing an offense of his own. Watanabe was looking to [Looming Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade) to get himself out of this predicament, but Schmidt had the [Ice Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage) already waiting.


[Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph) and [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) were attacking for four damage a turn now, and soon Watanabe had to do something about them. But also, he had to worry about the steady stream of reinforcements, happily provided by [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter). [Weakness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weakness) killed [Illusionary Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant) but the original clock kept ticking. With Watanabe down to five, Schmidt went for the throat and tapped low for [Wind Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake). Actually, though, this was just what Watanabe had been hoping for. With the danger of [Safe Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Passage) out of the way, he directed [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) at [Guardian Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Seraph), geting back to eleven life.


Suddenly, Schmidt’s air force was reduced to just [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) ([Wind Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) stayed back), while Watanabe made a comeback in the form of [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath). The Looter kept working, though, and came up with [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) and [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage). Next turn, Pegasus, Drake, and Griffin went in. [Wind Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) was blocked, but saved by [Safe Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Passage), while Watanabe lost [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat), his only flier. Watanabe took three damage (down to seven), then ran his [Child of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night) into its certain death (back to nine).


[Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) dug out [Kelinore Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat) and this time around it traded with [Wind Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) ([Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) and [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) getting Watanabe down to six). Schmidt cast [Wall of Frost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Frost), Watanabe cast [Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin).


The Pegasus and [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) recklessly attacked despite the Assassin (three life left for Watanabe). Also, when Watanabe declared combat, [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) tapped down [Zombie Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath). Lots of viable targets for Watanabe’s Assassin, but really, he had to kill off Schmidt’s 2/1 flier to stay alive at all. [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) attacked (two life) , and another [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) entered the battlefield, threatening a potential match win for Schmidt on the very next turn.


And indeed, the vigilant fliers flew to victory.


**Yuuya Watanabe 0 – 2 Jan Schmidt**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppra09/f_schmidt.jpg)*Congratulations to Jan Schmidt, the Champion of Grand Prix - Prague 2009!*
