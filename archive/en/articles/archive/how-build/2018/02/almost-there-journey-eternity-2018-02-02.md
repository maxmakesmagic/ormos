 Paulo has been playing Magic since he was eight years old. At fifteen, he ventured outside of Brazil for his first international tournament, and he's been globetrotting as a professional player ever since. 






**Pro Tour *Rivals of Ixalan* is this weekend!**


Follow all the action live from Bilbao, Spain, on [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic)! Coverage of Pro Tour *Rivals of Ixalan* begins Friday, Saturday, and Sunday at 9 a.m. local time (CET)/12 a.m. PT/3 a.m. ET.


The *Rivals of Ixalan* gold flip cards have very strong effects if you manage to flip them. So far, we've already seen [Path of Mettle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+of+Mettle) being played in aggressive red-white decks, and now we have this [Journey to Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity) deck that was played by DR\_OOM to a 5-0 finish on *Magic Online*:

This deck is very interesting, but seems to be a little too all-over-the-place for me. It's hard to imagine that [Scrapheap Scrounger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger), [Rhonas the Indomitable](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhonas+the+Indomitable), and [Journey to Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity) belong in the same deck. What I would do is streamline it a bit more, and turn it into more of a value deck with fewer aggressive components.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Journey+to+Eternity)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Atzal%2C+Cave+of+Eternity)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atzal%2C+Cave+of+Eternity)Transform
If we want to play a [Journey to Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity) deck, we have to think of two things: how to flip it, and how to best make use of it once it's flipped. Luckily for us, it seems like you don't have to go very far out of your way to achieve either of those things in this Standard format.


Imagine you're playing against an aggressive red deck and you play land, go. They play [Bomat Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier), and on your turn you play [Dusk Legion Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Legion+Zealot). They play [Earthshaker Khenra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthshaker+Khenra) and attack for 3. Now, on your turn, you play [Journey to Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity) on the Zealot. What are they supposed to do? They can't burn it down with [Abrade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade), [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike), or [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock), and they can't really attack into it. If they sit back, it's good for you, and if they attack, it's also good for you—you kill one of their creatures, get your [Dusk Legion Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Legion+Zealot) back, and flip your Journey into [Atzal, Cave of Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atzal%2C+Cave+of+Eternity).


Against people who aren't interested in attacking, flipping Journey is going to be a little bit harder. The key there is either putting it on a creature they have to kill, flooding the board so they have to [Fumigate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumigate) everything, or killing the creature yourself. Against control decks, [Fatal Push](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push) is going to be bad anyway, so you can just use it to flip your Journey. And once you do, you should be set up very well for a long game.


I think that, right now, the best creature with a sacrifice ability is [Thrashing Brontodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrashing+Brontodon), because the body is solid and the ability is very good to recur. If they have an artifact or enchantment in play, you can enchant your Brontodon with [Journey to Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity), destroy their permanent, and immediately bring the Brontodon back with your flipped land. If they're playing a deck full of targets, such as Mardu Vehicles, [God-Pharaoh's Gift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift), or White-Blue Cycling with [Drake Haven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drake+Haven), then the ability to start recurring Brontodons every turn can win you the game on its own. The best part is that, due to the way the rules work, you can sacrifice Brontodon to kill your own [Journey to Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity)—you choose the target (the Journey), then you pay the costs (sacrificing the Brontodon). The Journey will trigger, returning both the Brontodon and [Atzal, Cave of Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atzal%2C+Cave+of+Eternity) to play, and by the time the original Brontodon ability resolves, the [Journey to Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Eternity) that was targeted will be long gone. In practice, this means you can always use Brontodon to flip Journey if you want to.


Once Journey flips, it becomes very hard to lose a long game. Your creatures become immune to most removal, and, with the right combination of [enters-the-battlefield effects](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/play-design/m-files-hour-devastation-part-1-2017-07-14#enters-the-battlefield-effects), they also can't be interacted with in combat very well. Any mill effects you have also become much better (such as [Liliana, Death's Majesty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+Death%27s+Majesty)). Against other creature decks, a stream of [Ravenous Chupacabra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Chupacabra)s and [Noxious Gearhulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Gearhulk)s will be very hard to push through, and against slower decks, you should be able to generate a lot of advantage from grindy value creatures such as [Gonti, Lord of Luxury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gonti%2C+Lord+of+Luxury), [Jadelight Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jadelight+Ranger), [Kitesail Freebooter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitesail+Freebooter), and [Dusk Legion Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Legion+Zealot).


Past that, I think we should probably just streamline the deck and play four of most cards that are good, including Journey itself. This is how I'd build it:
