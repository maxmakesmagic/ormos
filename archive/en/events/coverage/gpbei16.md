Sugaya Takes A Bite Out Of Beijing
==================================




In his 5th GP Top 8 Hironobu Sugaya and his army of Vampires took the blood out of Xu Su to become the champion of Grand Prix Beijing!

Top 8 Coverage



Armed with a deck full of Vampires, Hironobu Sugaya bested Xu Su in two action packed games. With Beijing, Sugaya not only adds another trophy to his mantle, but achieves his fifth Grand Prix Top 8. En route to the finals Sugaya bested Gao Zhen Xing and Toru Inoue.


Beijing was the first time Shadows over Innistrad cards got in the hands of pro players. After a 9-0 run in the first day of competition, Pro Tour Hall of Famer, Makihito Mihara, ended the swiss rounds in first place and earned his 10th Grand Prix Top 8. Unfortunately for Mihara, he met his fate to the hands of Kazuaki Fujimura and his 4 color deck in the quarterfinals.


Beijing wasn't the only Shadows over Innistrad tournament that happened this weekendâ€”there are simultaneous Grand Prix in Barcelona and Albuquerque. While Hironobu Sugaya is heading home with his trophy now, there will be two more Grand Prix Champions this weekend.


Congratulations once again to Hironobu Sugaya, your 2016 Grand Prix Beijing Champion!

Day 2 Coverage



The sun has come out here in Beijing and 300 of Asia's best are ready for their first crack at *Shadows over Innistrad* booster draft. Overnight four players emerged with a perfect 9-0 record: Makihito Mihara, Takuma Morofuji, Huang Hao-Shan, and Gao Zhen Xing.


Hot on their heels are many other well known players including GP Manila champion, Hironobu Sugaya, *Magic Online* Specialist, Shintaro Ishimura, Pro Tour Mainstay, Tomoharu Saito, and No. 23 Kentaro Yamamoto. A swath of players barely scraped into day 2 at 6-3 including Hall of Famer, Kenji Tsumura, Yoshihiko Ikawa, and Chapman Sim.


This is the first professional event where players will be able to test their skills at *Shadows over Innistrad* booster draft. What strategies will emerge? How will the draft format transform? Stay tuned for continuing coverage of GP Beijing 2016!
