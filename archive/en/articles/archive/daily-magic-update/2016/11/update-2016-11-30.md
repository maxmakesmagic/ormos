The November 30, 2016 Update
============================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on November 30, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on November 30, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by waiting just one more week—not even—to open your *Aether Revolt* presents.


Today's Must
============


1. [Jeskai Control in Standard](https://www.youtube.com/watch?list=PLxg8UAfS2YJbp8pmGa7TAl4LriOg3ANVp&v=v_peR1j3-5A) | TCGPlayer | Seth Manfield

Seth Manfield, No. 3–ranked player in the world, goes a different direction for testing out Standard today. Jeskai Control has picked up some steam as a viable alternative to Blue-White Flash or Black-Green Delirium, and Manfield puts it through its paces in these four videos today. Standard Showdown fodder, maybe?


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [A Guide to Black-Green Delirium](http://www.mtgmintcard.com/articles/writers/martin-dang/a-guide-to-black-green-delirium) | MTGMintCard | Martin Dang

Whether you're in beat 'em or join 'em mode, you should know all about Black-Green Delirium heading into the Standard Showdown this weekend. [Pro Tour *Dragons of Tarkir*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptdtk) winner Martin Dang has the low down on Spider town.


1. [A Father's Duty—Sidar Kondo of Jamuraa](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y2a5WmA3pEY) | SeibenLore

Seiben takes a look at one of the newest partner commanders with some of the juiciest lore in the set. He's [Gerrard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=209157)'s adoptive dad. And Vuel's (also known as [Volrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=21328)) birth father. Basically, he was father to the antagonist *and* the protagonist of the *Weatherlight* Saga. Maybe he needed to work on his parenting skills a bit before all the flanking nonsense.

Top 25
======


1. Yamamoto Returns!

After winning [Grand Prix Chiba](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpchi16), Kentaro Yamamoto was also the biggest winner on the Top 25, shooting all the way up to No. 13 after not being ranked for some time. Big wins will do that. Joining him on his climb up the charts is fellow countryman Yuuya Watanabe, who also made the Top 8 of that Legacy Grand Prix, jumping up six spots to just barely enter the Top 10.


At the very top of the charts, Shota Yasooka holds fast, while Owen Turtenwald and Seth Manfield trail by about 6 points each. They would essentially need a Grand Prix win to get within sniffing distance of the top spot at this point.


Meanwhile, I haven't pointed this out before, but how about a round of applause for Luis Scott-Vargas, who is still ranked in the Top 10 after moving to the commentary booth this year. It speaks mostly to how incredible his last season was that the decay and not playing in events hasn't even dropped him below No. 9.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. Green-White Energy Delirium Control

This might be my favorite innovative deck to come in through the Standard Showdown. It's unexpected, it's many of the things all together, and it's in a (currently) underplayed color combination, especially for control.


The deck actually dares to combine [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) and [Dynavolt Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dynavolt+Tower) in *the same deck*, so that you can use the abundant energy the deck is capable of producing to do, well, any number of things—including activating [Consulate Surveillance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consulate+Surveillance)!


The deck also plays eleven Wrath effects. Creatures are not sticking around against this deck.


But the real kicker here is [Verdant Crescendo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Crescendo). I had to look it up too. It's from the Nissa Planeswalker Deck, as is [Nissa, Nature's Artisan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Nature%27s+Artisan). I'm not sure if this player decided they were powerful or if they just wanted to build with them, but this has to be the first time I've seen them in a decklist anywhere. Either way—challenge accepted and completed!
