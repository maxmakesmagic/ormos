Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Donald Smith's Treasure Red](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2017wc/2017-world-championship-standard-deck-lists-2017-10-06)

It didn't work out so well for [the future #wotcstaff](https://twitter.com/donaldwsjr/status/916830018923323393), but Treasure Red has some interesting things going for it.


For one, it goes bigger than other Ramunap Red decks thanks to [Wily Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wily+Goblin) and [Captain Lannery Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+Lannery+Storm) speeding up the turn when he can play [Chandra, Torch of Defiance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance); [Hazoret the Fervent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hazoret+the+Fervent); and [Glorybringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer). It keeps the aggression of Ramunap Red while including the power of some of the beefier, scarier red cards in the format.


There's some room to work with the deck, certainly. CodeProvider, for example, [went 5-0 with an updated version of the deck on *Magic Online*.](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2017-10-09)


If you want to know more about the deck, [check out the deck tech from over the weekend](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2017WC/treasure-red-with-donald-smith-2017-10-06). If you'd never click on that link because this is a dirty, dirty mono-red deck, well, then you and I can be friends.
