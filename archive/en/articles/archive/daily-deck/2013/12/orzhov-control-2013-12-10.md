Orzhov Control
==============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on December 13, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







Mono-Black Control (dubbed Excalibur by Paul Rietzl) took down [Grand Prix Albuquerque](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpabq13/welcome) in the hands of Owen Turtenwald, with Paul Rietzl, Joseph Nix, and Todd Anderson also making [Top 8](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpabq13/welcome#0), but it failed to repeat its performance at [Grand Prix Vienna](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpvie13/welcome). Instead, Andreas Ganz made [Top 16](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpvie13/welcome#2) with an interesting take on Mono-Black, now with less mono and more white cards.


Andreas kept the core of [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), and [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat), which are the most important parts of the Mono-Black shell, which necessitates keeping the white splash to a minimum. In fact, [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon) also made it into his list, along with much of the same removal. One might even begin to suspect that this is a Mono-Black list in disguise...


Where the white splash comes in is the powerful [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa). I've been a fan of this card ever since we played it at [Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdgm13/welcome), and I've never even looked at [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) since Blood Baron has begun his rule. When Baron is good, he's *really*good, and now is one of those times. Besides Blood Baron, a lone copy of [Sin Collector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) and a pair of [Last Breath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Breath)s make up all the white in the main deck, with only a single Wear amp; Tear in the sideboard (and the deck can't even cast Wear).


Between [Temple of Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Silence) and [Godless Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine), most of the mana requirements are covered, and Andreas only played one Plains to supplement them. He even kept all the [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault)s, which are a big selling point of being monocolored to begin with, so if the deck is able to function with them, that's a huge point in its favor.


When a deck is extremely popular, even a slight modification can pay huge dividends. Andreas identified a powerful card (Baron) that only requires a small cost in consistency, and by keeping most of the important parts of the proven Mono-Black deck, he gets to play the card in a very strong shell.
