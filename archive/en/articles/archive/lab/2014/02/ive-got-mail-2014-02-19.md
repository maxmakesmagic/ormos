I've Got Mail
=============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on February 19, 2014 










It's been a good week for feedback here at the Lab, and I've received quite a few interesting deck ideas. Although I don't reply to every email, I do read them all, and I'm always eager to hear your thoughts on new cards, old decks, or the column itself. Today, I'll be highlighting two emails I received from readers, and using them to construct decks around underappreciated cards.


### Challenge Accepted


The first email comes from Mark of Austin, Texas. He issued a challenge to build a deck using a card he had never seen anyone use: [Immortal Coil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Coil).



![Immortal Coil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Immortal%20Coil&options=)


Reminiscent of [Nefarious Lich](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nefarious+Lich) from *Odyssey*, [Immortal Coil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Coil) uses your graveyard as your life total. On one hand, this can be a great thing if your graveyard is full of cards, allowing you to essentially gain a large amount of life. On the other hand, if your opponent can exile your graveyard, you lose the game instantly.


This sparked an idea. What if "you" isn't you? If you use [Donate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate) to give the Coil to your opponent, you can then exile his or her graveyard with [Tormod's Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt). Suddenly, this dangerous drawback becomes a lethal weapon.

[Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) can also be used to give away the Coil, without requiring you to use mana to do it. As a backup to [Tormod's Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt), [Nihil Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) does the same thing for one more mana. You can also draw a card when you sacrifice it, making it useful even when you draw more than one copy.


[Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) helps tutor up [Immortal Coil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Coil), ensuring that your [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) isn't left with nothing to trade. [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) also helps find the combo pieces, while putting cards in your graveyard to make sure you don't die to the Coil as soon as you cast it.

Although [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) allows you to give away the Coil immediately, [Donate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate) costs three mana, meaning you'll usually be stuck with the Coil for a turn. Since this deck isn't devoted to putting cards in the graveyard, this can be a dangerous moment. To forestall your demise, I've included [Darkness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkness), a one-mana spell that stops creatures from killing you for a turn.


Finally, [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) buy you time while you get things set up. You don't want your opponent killing you before you finish wrapping your present.

### Luck of the Draw


The second email was sent to me by Jan from Holland. I'll let him tell the story.



> 
> Dear Johnny... I mean Mike,
> 
> 
> My inspiration comes from frustration. When I started playing Magic a good friend gave me some goblins and I had fun destroying and sacrificing my own cards. But I never had the fun of winning and more important (since I didn't own enough cards), I never had the fun of deck building. I decided to start buying my own cards, but being a student I was a bit... poor. I went to the shop and bought a booster of *Champions of Kamigawa*. I opened it, hoping for an aggressive big bomb and my rare was... [Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask).
> 
> 
> 
> ![Uba Mask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Uba%20Mask&options=)
> 
>  
> 
> Frustration...
> 
> 
> The next week I bought four more packs for revenge... but apparently I disfavored Phenax. Two rares I cannot remember, but the two [Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask) staring at me, left an impression that woke me up in the middle of the night for a few weeks.
> 
> 
> 


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/2014/ftl_wk07_287_dreamingofuba.jpg)


> 
> More frustration...
> 
> 
> I decided that the only way to win this battle was to be happy with what I got. I started building my [Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask) deck. I am proud of my firstborn. I never saw the combo with looters mentioned anywhere else. I managed to meddle the frustration toward my opponent. Many games have ended with an opponent without permanents or library.
> 
> 
>

The idea is to use creatures to make your opponent draw and discard cards on your turn. [Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask) will exile the drawn cards, and since they can only be played this turn, cards other than instants are rendered useless. In addition, your opponent will still be forced to discard cards, emptying his or her hand in short order.


Creatures like [Temporal Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Adept) return permanents to your opponent's hand. [emmin's Aura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=emmin%27s+Aura) and [Freed from the Real](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Freed+from+the+Real) allow you to activate your looters multiple times to keep your opponent's hand empty. You can win the game by exiling your opponent's entire library, or by attacking with creatures after all blockers have been cleared away.

I liked this idea, but there were a few changes I wanted to make to the list. The first card I added was [Magus of the Bazaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Bazaar). It can't target your opponent, but once you've emptied your hand it taps to draw two cards with no downside.


I also included [Venser, Shaper Savant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant). Like an [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) cranked up to 11, he can return spells as well as any permanent. He only costs one more mana, and you still get a 2/2 out of the deal.

### War of the Underdogs


Both of these decks are built around cards most players wouldn't touch in a million years, but only one can be crowned champion. Let's take this fight to the arena.


**Game 1**


Über Mask won the roll, and both sides started off with lands. Über Mask cast [Lore Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lore+Broker) on turn two, and [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) came down on the opposing side. Über Mask played [Mikokoro, Center of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikokoro%2C+Center+of+the+Sea), and Bizarre Traitor simply played a land and passed the turn.


[Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask) hit the board, and [Lore Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lore+Broker) made each player discard a card. It was too late to stop Bizarre Traitor's combo, however, as [Immortal Coil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Coil) and two copies of [Tormod's Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt) sealed the deal.



![Immortal Coil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Immortal%20Coil&options=)


**Game 2**


Both sides played lands and passed the turn until Über Mask cast [Temporal Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Adept). Bizarre Traitor countered it with [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) then cast a [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) after the turn was passed. Über Mask ended the turn with no play, and Bizarre Traitor played a land and passed back.


Über Mask cast [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) and passed the turn. Bizarre Traitor cast [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) during the end step, managing to hit five lands in a row. Bizarre Traitor played a land and passed the turn.


[Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask) came into play, and [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) forced Bizarre Traitor to discard a pair of lands. Bizarre Traitor cast a [Nihil Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) and passed the turn. [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) forced Bizarre Traitor to discard two more cards, and Über Mask ended the turn.


Bizarre Traitor cast [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), but Über Mask returned it to hand with [Venser, Shaper Savant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant). Über Mask played [Mikokoro, Center of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikokoro%2C+Center+of+the+Sea), and passed the turn. Bizarre Traitor played a land and passed back.


Über Mask tapped [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) to make Bizarre Traitor discard the last two cards. Bizarre Traitor sacrificed [Nihil Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) in response, exiling Über Mask's graveyard and drawing a card. Über Mask cast [Magus of the Bazaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Bazaar) and passed the turn.


Bizarre Traitor cast Fact of Fiction at end of turn, getting [Immortal Coil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Coil). Bizarre Traitor cast it and tried to give it away with [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader), but Über Mask [Recoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil)ed the Coil, tossing it in the graveyard. Bizarre Traitor ended the turn. Über Mask used [Magus of the Bazaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Bazaar) to draw two cards, casting [Recoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil) on [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) and [Lore Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lore+Broker). [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) exiled two more cards from Bizarre Traitor's library, and Über Mask passed the turn.


Bizarre Traitor played a land and passed, and Über Mask used [Magus of the Bazaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Bazaar) to grab two extra cards. [Pemmin's Aura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pemmin%27s+Aura) enchanted [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker), and Über Mask passed the turn. Bizarre Traitor cast [Immortal Coil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Coil), then tapped it to draw a card. Bizarre Traitor cast [Donate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate) to give the coil away, but Über Mask still had cards in the graveyard.


Über Mask activated [Cephalid Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Broker) several times during the end step and [Lore Broker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lore+Broker) once, whittling Bizarre Traitor's library down to ten cards. After untapping, the Broker exiled the rest of Bizarre Traitor's library. With no cards left and faced with the inevitability of a [Venser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser), [Recoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil), or [Temporal Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Adept) getting [Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask) of the battlefield to win the game, Bizarre Traitor conceded.

**Game 3**
