Grand Prix Kobe 2017 Day 1 Highlights
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 27, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











2,802 players arrived at the Kobe International Exhibition Hall but after nine rounds of Modern, "only" 868 players will return tomorrow for Day 2. What were the most interesting stories of the day and what has *Amonkhet* brought to the tables?


Let's find out in a matter of minutes!


Amonkhet Additions
==================


As this was the first Modern Premier Event since *Amonkhet*'s release, there was a lot of room for innovation. What were the most notable cards from *Amonkhet*?


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vizier+of+Remedies)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Devoted+Druid)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid)
[Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies) was cited as the most impactful card and it was seen in various shells including Elves, Abzan Company, as well as Kiki-Chord. Together with [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), you were able to generate infinite mana as quickly as turn three. Once you were swimming in unlimited power, you could summon [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter) to "draw" your entire deck before creating an infinitely huge [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) to seal the deal.


In fact, newly-minted Platinum pro Kelvin Chew had modified one of his pet decks to house the combo, a deck that his fellow teammates and friends had decided to nickname "Chew Knightfall".


More on that tomorrow, we promise!


What other *Amonkhet* cards saw play today?


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gideon+of+the+Trials)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials)
[Gideon of the Trials](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials) was sighted in several midrange decks. As a three-mana planeswalker, he could not be ignored for very long. Gideon locks down a threat temporarily, while transforming into a 4/4 attacker or a pseudo-[Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship) at will.


It is great against combo decks such as Ad Nauseam and Blue-Red Storm. The latter became increasing popular ever since [Baral, Chief of Compliance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baral%2C+Chief+of+Compliance) entered the fray, which meant that [Gideon of the Trials](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials) had relevant applications.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Horror+of+the+Broken+Lands)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horror+of+the+Broken+Lands) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Desert+Cerodon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desert+Cerodon) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Living+End)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End)
When it came to cycling, no other deck abused it better than [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End).


Now that you had [Horror of the Broken Lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horror+of+the+Broken+Lands) and [Desert Cerodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desert+Cerodon), you were able to phase out clunky alternatives such as [Jungle Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Weaver) and [Pale Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pale+Recluse), while also upgrading [Deadshot Minotaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadshot+Minotaur) into more formidable threats which don't accidentally end up killing your own [Faerie Macabre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Macabre). These were significant improvements because you could possibly shorten the clock down from two turns into one, making end-of-turn [Violent Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Outburst)s scarier than ever before.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Irrigated+Farmland)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Irrigated+Farmland)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Canyon+Slough)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Slough)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sheltered+Thicket)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sheltered+Thicket)
On the topic of cycling, it was common consensus that two mana for cycling was an extremely huge cost in a mana-tight format as Modern. Additionally, they will never enter the battlefield untapped, which is why Modern players are adverse to playing more than one or two copies of it. For example, White-Blue Midrange or Jeskai Control decks were happy to swap out one land for [Irrigated Farmland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Irrigated+Farmland), but any more than that was potentially hazardous.


[Sheltered Thicket](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sheltered+Thicket) saw a little more action, simply because it was a "Mountain Forest", coincidentally the exactly the subtypes you want in Red-Green [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) and [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) decks. Having extra [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground)s and [Cinder Glade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cinder+Glade)s were great but when you can cycle your lands, it could mean the difference between life and death! You never know when your game-winning [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) is sitting on the top of your library!


Also, [Canyon Slough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Slough) in Dredge decks enabled you to dredge back important cards such as [Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp) and [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) at instant speed, a relevant ability considering that there are numerous graveyard hate cards in the format such as [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus), [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) and [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze). However, I'm still searching for the [Fetid Pools](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fetid+Pools) and [Scattered Groves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattered+Groves). Some players might be playing them but I haven't sighted any thus far.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity)
Last but not least, [Cartouche of Solidarity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity) was seen in the sideboards of White-Green Hexproof Aura decks, also known as Bogles! If you ask any Bogles pilots, they will likely tell you to always play around [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), because it is the best card to get rid of your untargetable creature.


In addition to keeping a fetch land to find [Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) in times of need, [Cartouche of Solidarity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity) can also create sacrificial fodder! Nifty!


Deck Tech: White-Black Eldrazi
==============================


"What were the top three decks you expected today?"


When the majority of the three-bye players were polled, their responses were a combination of "Collected Company decks", "Death's Shadow decks", "the usual aggro decks (such as Affinity and Boros Burn), as well as the immensely-popular and immensely-sized "Eldrazi Tron decks".


However, according to Modern Master Lee Shi Tian, he was quick to point out that *Magic* had five colors, so you were technically shortchanging yourself if you opted to play with none. Grand Prix Brisbane 2013 Champion Justin Robb and fellow Australian teammate Anthony Lee also felt that "the deck was so underpowered that it could not even beat Mardu Vehicles in Standard".


Joe Soh might not have been involved in that conversation, but he felt like White-Black Eldrazi was the way to go. As the Grand Prix Kobe 2015 finalist – he fell to Takuma Morifuji in the finals – Soh was back with yet another of his spicy brews.


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpkob17/u_gpKobe2017_Day1Highlights_JoeSoh1.jpg)**


"I had good results with Black-Red Eldrazi last season so I picked it up again this time. I found out that it had a poor matchup against Eldrazi Tron, which was quite popular. However, after adding white, it seemed to have solved my problem. In fact, going [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Tidehollow Sculler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler), and then [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) is good enough to beat most decks."


After intensive playtesting on *Magic* Online, Soh found that [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) had weakened. It was a poor answer to wildly popular cards such as [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow), [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), as well as opposing Eldrazi. 9 competitive *Magic* Leagues - 45 matches in total - scored him three 5-0 finishes and four 4-1 finishes, which were very impressive results.


How many matches did he lose to Death’s Shadow?


Just one!


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpkob17/u_gpKobe2017_Day1Highlights_JoeSoh2.jpg)**


Before *Aether Revolt*, Soh had already been working on his current list but he didn’t have enough one-costed removal spells now that he had cut [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s. You don’t really want to use [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) on turn one and yet you cannot play that many [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember)s. With [Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push), that made Soh feel more comfortable about switching from Black-Red to White-Black. In addition, he received a wide range of tools which he felt were all very well-positioned in the current metagame.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Path+to+Exile)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tidehollow+Sculler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lingering+Souls)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stony+Silence)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cast+Out)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cast+Out)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Disenchant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)
Before he left for his next round, he added that Death’s Shadow was still the best deck, while the Company decks have actually weakened with the addition of [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies).


What gives?


"I personally feel that devoting eight slots to the combo (4 [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) and 4 [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies)) is too much. They don’t do much on their own and the combo is also easy to disrupt, given that Modern is filled with efficient cards such as [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and more."


Thankfully, Soh felt that Death’s Shadow variants were his best matchups, which was also why he had selected this deck today. Maindeck [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) and [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) kept [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), [Gurmag Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gurmag+Angler) and Dredge under control. On the flip side, he did not envy "the land matchups" such as Tron and [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) strategies.


Finishing at 8-1, it seemed like his predictions had panned out! Good thinking!

Deck Tech: Blue-Red Gifts Storm
===============================


Nicholas Wong had played *Magic* from the very beginning and he was known for being Singapore's very first National Champion, in addition to racking up six lifetime Grand Prix Top 8s. Today, he showed up with Blue-Red Gifts Storm, an iteration of an existing archetype.


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpkob17/u_gpKobe2017_Day1Highlights_NicholasWong1.jpg)**  
*Nicholas Wong, showing off his favorite cards from his deck!*


When it came to metagame positioning, Wong felt like he "was on Level 3. Level 1 were the [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) decks and then Level 2 were the decks which beat [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow), such as Dredge and [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End). Level 3 are the ones good against Dredge and [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End), so I feel like that's where I am. The [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies) decks I categorize them somewhere between Level 1 and Level 2, because there hasn't been any track record of them succeeding on Premier Events just yet. Even the 5-0 decklists on *Magic* Online are quite different from one another so we're not quite sure which is the best build at the moment."



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gifts+Ungiven)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pyretic+Ritual)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Desperate+Ritual)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Manamorphose)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Past+in+Flames)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames)
Eschewing Pyromancer's Ascension, the relatively-new Blue-Red Gifts Storm deck relies on [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) to gain critical mass. Wong shared that the most standard package to go for was [Pyretic Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual), [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual), [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) and [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames).


"The worst case scenario is them giving me [Pyretic Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual) and [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose), while putting [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual) and [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames) in the graveyard. This combination gives me the least mana (but draws me a card), while making [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames) cost one more."


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Electromancer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Baral%2C+Chief+of+Compliance)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baral%2C+Chief+of+Compliance)
Wong's mastery with Blue-Red Gifts Storm is mostly due to the fact that it was the only Modern deck he had played for the past four months, ever since [Baral, Chief of Compliance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baral%2C+Chief+of+Compliance) saw print. That card allowed Storm players to run up to eight copies of the "mana reduction engine", making the deck way more consistent than before.


"Death's Shadow is going to be popular because it is considered to be the best deck, but this is Modern we're talking about. The metagame is always going to be pretty wide and even if [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) occupied 15% of the field, we're still only going to face it one every seven matches."


This property of the format is not without empirical evidence. Historically, no deck has ever occupied more than 20% of the Modern metagame with minor exceptions such as the Eldrazi Winter, because there were at least thirty to forty viable strategies across the board.


As the fastest "goldfishing" deck in the format, Wong was hoping that his gambit would pay off. So far, he seemed to be having a great day and finished at 7-2 to make Day 2.


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpkob17/u_gpKobe2017_Day1Highlights_NicholasWong2.jpg)**  
*"So... the Storm count is at 10?"*


"Against the [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies) and [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) combo decks, I have some disruption to break them up. Also, the deck is pretty consistent and I can reliably win on turn three or turn four. In very rare situations, the deck can even win on turn two!"

Deck Tech: Esper Polymorph
==========================


Joe Lam is a dedicated grinder from Hong Kong and he had been playing *Magic* for over fifteen years. He routinely attends regional Grand Prix and he is known by the local community to never be afraid to brew. Naturally, with *Amonkhet* in the mix, he wasted no time conjuring up yet another concoction. Here were just some of the juicy cards in his 75, a few of them from recent expansions!



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gideon+of+the+Trials)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Start+%2F%2F+Finish)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Start+%2F%2F+Finish)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hidden+Stockpile)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hidden+Stockpile)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Beckon+Apparition)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beckon+Apparition)
"I believe that [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies) and [Gideon of the Trials](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials) are the best cards in *Amonkhet* for Modern. In turn, this creates a creature-heavy metagame which relies on [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company). In order to keep that card effective and to have the potential of turn-three kills, this means that they need to play many creatures, which means they also have less removal."


Notably, Lam explained that [Gideon of the Trials](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials) was very effective in locking down [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow), and they have a hard time dealing with planeswalkers since they don't have that many creatures. Gideon was also great against unfair decks, which makes him very playable in Modern.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Polymorph)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)
Of course, he also transforms into a creature and is ready to take the [Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph) bullet.


"Since I have no other creatures in my deck, I am sure to hit [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) if I resolve [Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph). My creatures are in the form of tokens, such as [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Hidden Stockpile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hidden+Stockpile), [Beckon Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beckon+Apparition), [Start // Finish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Start+%2F%2F+Finish) and Gideon himself."


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpkob17/u_gpKobe2017_Day1Highlights_JoeLam.jpg)**  
*Joe Lam never was afraid to experiment with new brews.*


Those were a lot of unorthodox cards but Lam was quick to explain his choices.


"Hidden Stockpile might not be very good in Standard but there are fetch lands in Modern. I'm able to create tokens and scry with every revolt trigger. [Start // Finish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Start+%2F%2F+Finish) is also great against [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow), because it stays in the graveyard even after they've used [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) or [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) on me. I'm always guaranteed to have a removal spell since it sits in the graveyard."


Esper [Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph)'s ideology is similar to existing control decks such as Jeskai Control, Grixis Control, and similar variants. Using cheap disruption to hamper your opponent's game plan, it has great resilience against midrange decks while also being able to go over the top with an unfair win condition, not unlike cheating out Emrakul with [Nahiri, the Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nahiri%2C+the+Harbinger) or [Platinum Emperion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Emperion) with [Madcap Experiment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Madcap+Experiment).


Finishing Day 1 at 7-2, he proved that his product was viable and he was excited about making Day 2. No matter what happens tomorrow, Lam wasn't going to return to Hong Kong disappointed.


"I flew to Kobe on Friday night after I ended work and I was very much looking forward to a vacation. The weather in Kobe is great and everyone was so nice and friendly. After the Grand Prix, I'm looking forward to a Kobe beef meal as well as a visit to the hot springs!"


In the meantime, he's going to keep churning out Emrakuls for 4 mana and hope to reach the Top 8 in glorious fashion!

A Battle of Wits
================


Masakatsu Imai had a very interesting inquiry to the Head Judge as he stepped into the the tournament venue today.


"If I am unable to balance my deck in a single pile, can I be allowed to separate my deck into two piles?"


Naturally, that left the men in red confused (at first), but upon further investigation we discovered that Imai had brought a [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck to the main event of Grand Prix Kobe! For some reason, he felt that bringing 75 cards wasn't enough, so he chose to show up with 300 cards instead!


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Battle+of+Wits)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits)
**![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpkob17/u_gpKobe2017_Day1Highlights_MasakatsuImai1.jpg)**  
*Masakatsu Imai won numerous games with his deck's namesake card today.*


Regretfully, he finished Day 1 with a 5-3-1 record, which was perhaps one match or even just one game short of making Day 2! He wasn't playing slowly, but in order to randomize his deck sufficiently, he might have burned away a minute or two which ultimately resulted in a draw.


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpkob17/u_gpKobe2017_Day1Highlights_MasakatsuImai2.jpg)**  
*Not sure if [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) or Searching until Tomorrow.*


No matter, he's won our hearts for being the happiest *Magic* player in the entire room, who came to Kobe to have fun more than anything! Most importantly, he nearly made Day 2, which meant that his choice wasn't utterly terrible!


If you're keen to build a [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck, here's Masakatsu's decklist in its entirety!

***Magic* Accomplishments:**  Yaruo Cup Top 8

I feel sorry for my opponent but I did win one game because he submitted a decklist with an error. This taught me to always be careful.

You have to teach me.
