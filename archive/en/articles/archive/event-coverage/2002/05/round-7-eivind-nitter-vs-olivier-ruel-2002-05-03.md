Round 7: Eivind Nitter vs. Olivier Ruel
=======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 3, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TobyWachter.jpg)
By Toby Wachter











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a865.jpg)Two of Europe's finest Magic players squared off in the final round of day one. The winner would draft on Saturday, and the loser would go sightseeing. 

Well, not Ruel since he lives in this country, but you get the point.

### Game 1

Nitter slammed down an Egg, and Ruel answered back by forcefully slamming [Phantom Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Whelp) on the table. Not to be outdone, Nitter went one better by summoning [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound). [Phantom Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Whelp) attacked and Nitter decided to not take the risk of losing the Hound to [Shelter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter) or [Embolden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Embolden), and took two. [Phantatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantatog) also joined Ruel's side, while Nitter brought out [Hallowed Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Healer). Ruel added to the stalemate with [Teroh's Faithful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful), and Nitter had [Rotting Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Giant). A [Nomad Decoy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Decoy) came out on Ruel's side to give him some control over the board.

[Aven Fisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fisher) gave Ruel a way to get through for some damage, but Nitter had [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire). The Decoy tapped down the Healer and the Whelp attacked, but this time Nitter called the bluff and blocked it with the Hound. He chose to not discard a card for first strike, and the dogs traded straight up. Nitter used [Morgue Theft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morgue+Theft) to bring his Hound right back. [Phantatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantatog) attacked and was blocked by [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound). A land was discarded to pump the 'Tog, but Nitter had [Embolden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Embolden) to keep his creature alive. Caustin Tar would have been bad news for Ruel, but he had [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Four lands were discarded to [Phantatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantatog) to gain threshold, and the Decoy tapped down two of Nitter's creatures to clear the way for an attack.

The board continued to be locked up, as Ruel brought out a [Patchwork Gnomes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patchwork+Gnomes), and Nitter had a pair of [Militant Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Militant+Monk)s. [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) for three gave Ruel some extra cards, and Nitter had [Mystic Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot). Ruel continued to gain card advantage, bringing back [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) with [Scrivener](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrivener). [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge) was cast by Nitter, but it would take a lot for it to get through, especially after Ruel played a Zealot of his own. The board was at quite a stalemate, although Ruel had the upper hand thanks to his Decoy with threshold. Nitter used [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire) to nullify the Zealot, but Ruel used [Shelter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter) to remove it and go up yet another card. 

Nitter sent a huge [Mind Sludge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sludge) at Ruel, drawing out the [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Ruel used his own [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire) on Nitter's Zealot, which cleared the way for his own. With the assistance of his Decoy tapping down healers, Ruel seemed to be finally breaking the stalemate. However, Nitter had a [Shelter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter), and targeted his Zealot to take away the Desire. The board was now back to square one. Ruel summoned [Aven Flock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Flock) to build up the air a bit more. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a866.jpg)Nitten drew, and said "Oh my God!" a few times with a stunned look on his face, as Ruel clutched his Pooh bear and cowered in fear. [Crippling Fatigue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue) targeted the [Aven Flock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Flock) with Ruel tapped out, and [Gloomdrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gloomdrifter) hit play to annihilate the board. The end result was [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge), [Gloomdrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gloomdrifter), [Rotting Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Giant) and [Mystic Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot) on Nitter's side, and only a Zealot and [Teroh's Faithful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful) on Ruel's. Still, Ruel bounced back, summoning [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer) to bring back [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire), which was played on Nitter's Zealot. A second Desire was played on the [Gloomdrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gloomdrifter), and Ruel attacked with his whole team. [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge) blocked the Zealot, and [Rotting Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Giant) blocked the [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound) to kill it. This brought Nitter to one life point. Ruel continued on the offensive on the following turn by playing [Strength of Isolation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+of+Isolation) on his [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer). With Nitter at one, the last point of damage was squeezed out to give Ruel the first game.

Nitter- 0 Ruel- 1

### Game 2

Ruel kept a one land hand, and it obviously didn't pay off as Ruel missed his second land drop, but found another land on turn three. Still, he was stuck at two lands, and Nitter was going full speed ahead, bringing out [Rotting Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Giant) and [Militant Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Militant+Monk). Ruel slowed down the beats with [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire), but Nitter kept the offense coming by summoning [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge). Ruel scooped soon after.

Nitter- 1 Ruel- 1

### Game 3

While laying out his seven card hand, Nitter accidentally flipped over an [Aven Cloudchaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Cloudchaser), which is a big deal in a matchup involving so many [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire)s. I can't do Ruel's reaction justice. Nitter simply banged his head against the table.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a867.jpg)Mirror [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound)s started the game off, and Ruel sent his in for two points. The second time, Nitter had mana available so he blocked. Ruel gave his Hound first strike, discarding [Strength of Isolation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+of+Isolation), which he played on the dog. Nitter used [Shelter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter) to save his Hound, and brought out [Aven Cloudchaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Cloudchaser) to kill off the enchantment. Ruel cast [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire) to stop Nitter's [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound), and a [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge) was countered by Ruel thanks to [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound) madness casting of [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Both [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound)s traded to clear up the board a bit, and it was now simply Nitter's Cloudchaser against Ruel's [Patchwork Gnomes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patchwork+Gnomes). 

Nitter then played [Caustic Tar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caustic+Tar) to break the stalemate right in half. [Morgue Theft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morgue+Theft) brought back the [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge), but Ruel had yet another [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Ruel delayed the inevitable with [Teroh's Faithful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful), but the end was inevitable, as Nitter flashed back [Morgue Theft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morgue+Theft) to get back [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge). That was enough to advance Nitter to day two.

**Final Result:** Nitter- 2 Ruel- 1
