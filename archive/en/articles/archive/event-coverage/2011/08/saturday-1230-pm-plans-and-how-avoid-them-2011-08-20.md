Saturday, 12:30 p.m. - Plans, and How to Avoid Them
===================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 19, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_richhagon.jpg)
By Rich Hagon




Rich Hagon combines a deep knowledge of the players of the Pro Tour with a passionate love of the game. He's a regular commentator for Pro Tour and Grand Prix live video coverage, and is the official Pro Tour Statistician. He has been covering Magic events since 2006. 





Yesterday, Seb Parker went 3-0 in his first M12 draft here at Great Britain Nationals. A big fan of both red-blue and red-white, he got himself into red early and stayed there, while it took a little longer to put together his second color, since bits and pieces of blue and white kept coming. [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter)

All very satisfactory, and the deck propelled him from 3-1 to 6-1 overnight. Now, though, he had a new set of decisions to make, and his plans to head down a similar red-white or red-blue route were comprehensively destroyed when he opened [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter). That's really tough to pass, and even tougher to leave in your sideboard.


Chances were, Seb would be playing green.


Although he took [Stingerfling Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingerfling+Spider) and [Lurking Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Crocodile) soon after, most of his first pack was blue, featuring [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), [Phantasmal Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Bear), and a pair of [Aven Fleetwing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fleetwing). As seems typical in this format, there were a couple of useful sideboard cards ([Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), [Autumn's Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Autumn%27s+Veil)) late.


  




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mind+Control)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control)

As pack two began, Seb briefly considered dipping into black for [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), before he found the princely uncommon [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) towards the back of the pack. From there, the blue was all systems go. [Belltower Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Belltower+Sphinx), [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept), a late [Djinn of Wishes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+of+Wishes), [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) - only [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) represented an addition to a small green collection. There was one intriguing pick very late (pick 11) when he left [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) in the booster, taking [Sunpetal Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunpetal+Grove). At the time, this felt like a moment of rare-drafting weakness, especially as [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) is precisely the kind of card opponents sideboard in to deal with big threats like a [Djinn of Wishes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+of+Wishes) or a [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter)...


  





[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aether+Adept)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Adept)


There was more to that pick than met the eye, however. Pack three opened with [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept), before [Arachnus Web](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arachnus+Web) and [Stingerfling Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingerfling+Spider) cemented the green. With his fourth pick, Seb found two quality white cards - [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) - stacking up against not much for either green or blue. Knowing that [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) was an effective answer to his Planeswalker, Seb defensively drafted it, but now the taking of [Sunpetal Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunpetal+Grove) took on more significance. Should he feel the need, a white splash was now a more palatable option, and [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) exactly the kind of card that might need to be summoned to his rescue sometime during the next three rounds.


When [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) and [Spirit Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Mantle) joined his pile, the white splash was increasingly an option. Drafting green? Not the plan.


White splash? Not the plan. Knowing when to change a plan? Now that's a plan worth having.


  



Afterwards, we spoke with Seb about the draft:





|  |
| --- |
| 
*Seb Parker* |



*Did you ever contemplate not taking Garruk?*


It's the only card I can open that puts me in green. Maybe [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan). I've heard the Hydra is good, but I still don't think I'd take that. There are some uncommons I'd take over the Hydra . But Garruk is just super-stupid!


*You took [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) over [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves). Explain.*


I don't like [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) that much. I've avoided green a lot in draft, and picking cards like [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) is like putting extra lands into your deck, so you increase the risk of flooding out. But [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider) is always solid, and helps you hold things up until Garruk arrives. I'm also headed towards blue rather than green.


**How happy were you to see [Djinn of Wishes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+of+Wishes) pick six in pack two?**


I was in shock. It shouldn't go that far round the table, and it's a massive signal that blue is coming from the left, and makes me confident that I've chosen the right colors.


*You kept avoiding [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), but seemed pleased to get [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). One is a hard counter, one isn't. What's up with that?*


The format is aggro, and at that point I had so much late game that I needed the early game, so [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) is a potential early drop to complete my curve and compete with the early drops that other people have. If you [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) an early guy, then they have to cast their [Blood Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Ogre) without Bloodthirst, and then you drop [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider)....that's what you want. [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) often just does the same job for one more mana.


*What were you thinking when you took [Sunpetal Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunpetal+Grove)?*


Green-blue has [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon), [Arachnus Web](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arachnus+Web), and [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) at common.


None of them deal with a huge creature, so the Sunpetal opens up the possibility of a white splash if I open [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) or [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), and I can get a [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) to go find a Plains.


*[Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) over [Pentavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus). Don't you like [Pentavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus)?*


My deck is [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control), [Stingerfling Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingerfling+Spider), Garruk, [Belltower Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Belltower+Sphinx), [Djinn of Wishes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+of+Wishes)....I don't need the late game, I need to get there, and [Æther Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) helps me do that.


*Tell us about the [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) pick. Purely defensive, or were you thinking about the [Sunpetal Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunpetal+Grove) and a splash?*


The picks were connected, but I decided not to play the splash. I didn't pick up the [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), and my deck is really strong, and I didn't want to destabilize my mana.
