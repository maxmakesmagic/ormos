News Flash (and We Do Mean "Flash")
===================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on January 27, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











News flash—Faeries is awesome. The "flash"-y squad of [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique), and sometimes [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) continued their butterfly-winged travels through the land of Extended success this past weekend, leading all archetypes in the Top 8 of Grand Prix Atlanta.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_spellstutter.jpg)  
Consider:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_chart1.jpg)  
The little blue-and-black creatures came in a couple of different varieties; while all the decks played four copies of [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and four copies of [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), almost no other non-land card proved an un-trim-able sacred cow.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grasp+of+Darkness)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness)  
Owen Turtenwald performed his fourth X-0 sweep of a Grand Prix Day 1 with this Faeries list. Notably the deck plays twenty-six lands (the other two Faeries builds in the Top 8 play "only" twenty-five), and runs [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness) as the auxiliary creature elimination spell (after two copies of [Disfigure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure), which were present in all the Top 8 Faeries decks); while [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness) might not seem anti-mirror at first blush (especially when the others were running [Agony Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) and everyone had [Peppersmoke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peppersmoke)), remember that the Grasp can hit a [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) with Champion on the stack. Owen's deck played a whopping three Planeswalkers ... two copies of "baby" [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) and a solo "big" [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_3Cards1.jpg)  
Now the head-turning looker in this build comes out of the sideboard. We all know that anybody who's anybody runs [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) in Faeries (and sometimes main deck!) ... and Owen played two of those; what's more, he ran three copies of the wee Wurmcoil, [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk)! 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_3Cards2.jpg)  
[Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) gives the Faeries deck faster resistance to aggression than [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) by virtue of its much lower mana cost. When combined with fast removal like [Disfigure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) and [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) actually looks pretty effective against aggressive Red opponents. Worst-case scenario, the Nighthawk is absorbing a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), and if it ever hits, the 2-point life boost can buy additional turns, and is in fact, worth the better part of a (red) card.

John played a full set of [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona). Players tend to be hot and cold around the Scion. Originally in Standard, they were 4-of standard; later, lower creature number Faeries decks with lots of control cards (more like Owen's), relying on the All-Stars [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), and [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) for the majority of the offense increased in popularity.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Scion+of+Oona)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona)  
Generally the Scion decks are better at racing (their [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) tokens can double damage in a hurry), and with yet another flash creature in the mix, they gain some extra frustrating tricks (counter your removal card at instant speed).

Relative to Owen's deck, Runyon has one land less, and no Jace support.

Jason Ford won the whole thing with a Blue-Red-Green [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) strategy.

Though [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) ostensibly likes to be sitting next to a bunch of [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) allows Jason to pretend his other lands are all [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s for purposes of not just killing non-flying creatures with [Firespout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout), but killing opponents with [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The deck has two different plans. One of them is just playing a ton of lands and mana acceleration. Jason's deck plays twenty-seven lands, plus four [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore)s and four [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth)s. If he has [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) on the battlefield, he can just lay lands to murder the opponent in a grind-'em-out fashion, turn after turn by 3s and 6s. There are some cool tricks you can run with this strategy, including playing [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) with [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) on the battlefield (both [Valakut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut)s think the incoming [Valakut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) is a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)); another is running out a [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) or [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest): 

"Take 6."

Ford's deck is primarily green-blue, with the blue there for library manipulation and countermagic ... both blue's fortes!

[Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) help find turn to [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) or mana acceleration, and [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) is joined by [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) on defense. A miser's [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) is there, presumably, to help Jason mise.

The other strategy is to lean on [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), again with [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) on the battlefield.

From this side, you can just cast [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) and get a bunch of lands (including multiple copies of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle)); all the lands think they are [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, so doom will generally follow.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_3Cards3.jpg)  
Ford's deck can beat a main-deck [Runed Halo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runed+Halo) or [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) due to the presence of [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) main, but he supplements the strategy with a decent sideboard battlefield plan: [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), [Obstinate Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth), and [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) are champion beatdown-destroyers, and [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) is an annoying clock against both other combo and control (which may or may not have a way to deal with a 3/1 flyer sideboarded).

Christian's deck is another Standard-looking [Valakut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut)-ramp, but with significantly different details. Valenti's deck has much less emphasis on [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) (only one copy) or a fast [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) kill (three copies of [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift)). Instead, he uses creature offense, driven with help from [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_primal.jpg)  
[Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) costs five mana; presumably this twenty-seven-land deck can hit a sixth the following turn, so the most common mode will generally involve finding a creature. Both "[Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk)" (putting a land on top of the opponent's deck) and gaining 7 life are generally more popular than "[Feldon's Cane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feldon%27s+Cane)" (but remember you can Command-Cane an opponent's graveyard to, say, nuke a squadron of [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s or [Demigods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigods)) ... All four modes on this card can be useful; there may be no spell in Extended whose successful play more consistently correlates with winning than [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command).

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Primal+Command)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command)  
Another important card to consider is [Guttural Response](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttural+Response). Essentially this card gives Red and Green players the option of a one mana anti-countermagic countermagic. It is particularly satisfying (not to mention mana efficient and often backbreaking) to trade your [Guttural Response](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttural+Response) with the opponent's [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), and with that, probably his entire turn.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_gameState1.jpg)  
When smashing a [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) this way, I recommend proclaiming "Gutterball!" *Just sayin.*

Valenti said that [Spellbreaker Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbreaker+Behemoth) was his MVP. A cool side effect of playing with this card is that, provided no one [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s or [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark)s it, your subsequent [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) will inevitably resolve.

Charles Gindy, already all different kinds of champion, notched another fine Grand Prix finish with a pretty straightforward Jund deck.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_fauna.jpg)  
You will see essentially every Jund deck adding [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) as an incremental two-drop in Extended, though the numbers will vary from two to four; Gindy played three copies. [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) has a couple of different duties to perform in Jund; one of which is just offensive two-drop (i.e., get in for 2, as [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) hits the battlefield). A more important role is to rack up a particular kind of card. For example, against a red deck (in the unlikely event it lives for more than a couple of turns), [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) can turn any creature card into a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) or [Obstinate Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth). Finally, [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) helps rack up copies of [Demigod of Revenge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge). You can pitch up to three copies of [Demigod of Revenge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge) into the graveyard on the way to finding the fourth. Then, when you cast that fourth they all come out!

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fauna+Shaman)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman)  
Rock and roll!

A notable sideboard card is [Deglamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deglamer). To a casual observer, [Deglamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deglamer) seems about as good as [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) (or maybe worse, as it doesn't seem to "permanently" deal with the artifact or enchantment), or for that matter [Nature's Claim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Claim), which costs half the mana.

So what gives?

[Deglamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deglamer)—like its competitors—is a versatile solution to [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen), and other cards; the biggest difference is that [Deglamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deglamer) is much better at fighting a [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) than one of the solutions that would put [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) into the graveyard (and by extension, turn on its 3/3-producing trigger).

The Atlanta Top 8 was rounded out by Jody Keith's White-Blue Control deck.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_sun.jpg)  
With [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) as a finisher, I was surprised to see only one copy of [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), and for that matter, in the sideboard. Jody's main line planeswalker action was from big Jace, three starting.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sun+Titan)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)  
Keith's white-blue deck is set up to be able to do anything. Main deck he has "most of" a couple of different deck identities: anti-beatdown, eight pieces of countermagic, strong tap-out finishers. It can then be customized to emphasize each of the different plans via the sideboard. 

Against beatdown, the white-blue can present a harrowing curve of [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) to [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) to [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment). All biding time until [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) gets a hit off.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td127_4Cards1.jpg)  
Against combo and control, it can steal mana with [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) (which can stop an on-curve [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) even on the draw). A [Runed Halo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runed+Halo) naming "[Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique)" is actually pretty humorous—more important against [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle), though. [Ratchet Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) and [Wispmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wispmare) are both fast enough to fight the format's important two mana enchantments, and also play nicely with [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan).

At the end of this Extended's first big tournament, we see a pair of past Standard boogeymen rising to Top 8 prominence (with one of those ascents no surprise at all). That said, the format seems to be diverse, with decks focused on [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) next to those centered around [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle). And you know what's really cool? The biggest winner's deck ran both.
