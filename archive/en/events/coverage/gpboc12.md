Jůza Repeats in Bochum
======================




November 17-18












Grand Prix Bochum 2012
----------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/d2_closing.jpg)This weekend had it all: big names, rising stars, close matches, and exciting new decks. Czech super-pro Martin Jůza used one, a [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage)-powered [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) concoction developed by Brad Nelson, to take home the trophy.


But let's start this short recap at the beginning. On Saturday, a whopping 1,731 players had entered the tournament, played in nine grueling rounds of Standard; afterwards the field was cut down to just 198 who returned on Sunday for seven more rounds; at the end of those, only eight players remained. Among a lot of promising young talent, one name immediately stood out: Martin Jůza had now made the fourteenth Grand Prix Top 8 of his career, with wins so far in Hiroshima, Portland, and actually right here in Bochum two years ago. Would he be able to repeat his performance from 2010? Of course he would.


His Top 8 matches were close—all went to three games—but he won them all. He never got to cast the signature card of his deck, [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth), not in the quarterfinal, where he bested Sascha Stein's Jund deck, not in the semis, when his [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens outnumbered Kamil Napierski's Zombies, and not in the finals, where [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) and [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) raced Fabian Dickmann's UWR tempo deck. But he did make good use of an excellent, somewhat transformational sideboard which kept his opponent guessing and earned him a deserved fourth Grand Prix title.


Congratulations to Martin Jůza, repeat champion of Grand Prix Bochum!

### Pierre Dagen


**Age:** 25  
**Hometown:** Paris, France  
**Occupation:** Business consultant  

 
**Guild:**  

Orzhov


**Previous **Magic** accomplishments:**   
  

Played several Pro Tours (one Top 50 in Nagoya), three Grand Prox Top 16s.


**What deck are you playing and why did you choose it?**  
  

Bant Control because [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) seemed to be way ahead of this Standard's power level. Basically, it crushes any form of nonblue non-[Threaten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threaten) deck. The sideboard is fairly efficient too, and the mana base is sweet. You absolutely can't beat UW, though.


**Would you change anything about your deck and why would you change it?**  
  

Minus one [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate), plus one [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) maindeck. (Aggro decks found a way to beat [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk).) Possibly a second [Staff of Nin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staff+of+Nin) instead of the [Terminus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) (way more resilient against Jund, but weaker against Rites.)

Quarterfinal Round-Up
---------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

### Lukas Tajak (U/W Flash) vs. Tomas Vanek (B/R Zombies)


  
  

"Oh no! Four [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls), I hate you," Tajak despondently said when he saw Vanek's decklist. Still, Tajak pulled ahead in game 1 with a quick draw of [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). Vanek, in turn, made a close damage race out of it with [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) and [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts), but it was already too late given Tajak's blazingly fast start.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/qf_tajak_vanek.JPG)  
*Tajak (Left) and Vanek (Right)*

  

In game 2, Vanek had an amazing mana curve that gave him 9 power worth of creatures on turn 4. Tajak's first play was a turn 3 [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and looked very far behind. [Swift Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swift+Justice) to gain 5 life was nice, but it wasn't enough to race Vanek's Zombies.  

 
In game 3, Tajak's [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) was fending off [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), but [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) plus [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) started flying over Tajak's ground defenses. Tajak fought back with [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) and [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) while attacking with [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and holding a bunch of cards in hand with mana open. This gave Vanek pause. Would Tajak be laying a trap for him? Still, Vanek decided to keep pressing on with his creatures, and it was the right decision. Tajak was stuck was two more copies of the legendary [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) in hand and had no tricks left up his sleeve.


Lukas Tajak 1 - Tomas Vanek 2


### Sascha Stein (Jund) vs. Martin Juza (Hoof, there it is!)


"No, not Jund," Martin Juza said when he received Stein's decklist, apparently unhappy with the matchup. In game 1, Juza's [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens and 1-toughness mana guys were no match for Stein's [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), and Juza was off to a quick loss.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/qf_stein_juza.JPG)  
*Stein (Left) and Juza (Right)*

In game 2, Juza's transformational sideboard plan of [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter), [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), and [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) (boarding out some mana guys to avoid losing to [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren)) worked to perfection.

Could Stein overcome Juza's big reanimation plan? He certainly did his best in game 3, by putting on pressure with [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and by burning Juza's creatures, but Stein was stuck with two [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) in hand and only 4 lands in play. When Stein finally found his 5th land, it was already too late as [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) and [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) again came in for the kill.  
  

Sascha Stein 1 - Martin Juza 2  

 


### Kamil Napierski (B/R Zombies) vs. Enrich van Eijsden (Jund)


  

Enrich van Eijsden is only 16 years old, but is already playing in a Grand Prix Top 8---a great start of a **Magic** career. In game 1, van Eijsden's early [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) was stolen with [Mark of Mutiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+Mutiny) and subsequently sacrificed to [Bloodthrone Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire). A nice play, but Napierski hadn't drawn many creatures, and the few ones that he did draw fell to van Eijsden's removal. A short while later, a duo of [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s gave van Eijsden the first game.  

 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/qf_napierski_eijsden.jpg)  
*Napierski (Left) and Eijsden (Right)*

In the second game, Napierski had [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) to ensure that [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) would not exile his [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger). Van Eijsden's blockers ([Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren)) fell to [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) and [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear), respectively. A few swings with [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) later, Napierski had evened the match at one game apiece.  
  

Van Eijsden had to take mulligans down to 5 in search of a keepable hand, and it cost him: An onslaught of [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger)s was awaiting him. Van Eijsden had [Sever the Bloodline](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sever+the+Bloodline) for the first one, but couldn't deal with the [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) that followed. After adding a [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) to the board, Napierski attacked for lethal damage.  
  

Kamil Napierski 2 - Enrich van Eijsden 1  

 
### Pierre Dagen (Bant control) vs Fabian Dickmann (UWR Geist midrange)


  
  

Game 1 started out slow with both players ramping their mana and drawing cards. A bunch of counterspells and [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s later, the board still hadn't developed much. Dickmann's [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) gave him an advantage, though, and a group of 1/1 tokens got in for many, many turns. A flashed-in [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) later, Dagen was already down to 4 life. Double [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) then handed the first game to Dickmann.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/qf_dickmann_dagen.JPG)  
*Dickmann (Left) and Dagen (Right)*

The start of game 2 featured excellent [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) timing. The Top 5 Cards of Grand Prix Bochum 2012
-----------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

Here are five cards that sum up some of the biggest stories of the weekend.



![Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Silverblade+Paladin)

5. [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin)


Three out of four players that went undefeated on Day 1 had [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) in their deck, and it is a cornerstone of the most popular deck on Day 2: Green-White Aggro. Especially when enhanced with [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) or [Sublime Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel), [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) allows you to pump out mind-blowing amounts of damage every turn. It makes the 5 life provided by any opposing [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) insignificant in comparison.



![Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nightshade+Peddler)

4. [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler)


The centerpiece of the innovative brew by Max Pritsch and Jan-Moritz Merkel; [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) does an insane amount of work when bonded with [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) or [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren). The seemingly awkward 1/1 for 2 mana has been raising eyebrows the entire weekend, and it has been headlining the list of cards that people were certainly not expecting. Still, both Pritsch and Merkel finished in the Top 32, and their crazy deathtouch-ping concoction will be a Standard force to be reckoned with going forward.



![Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)

3. [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)


Named to be either the most powerful card in Standard or the most powerful card in their deck by pro players Stanislav Cifka, Olivier Ruel, and Martin Juza. The card is indeed one of the most backbreaking late-game plays in the format, and dealers couldn't seem to keep them in stock. Top 8 competitor Pierre Dagen, who played it in his Bant Control deck, felt that [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) is way ahead of this Standard's power level. Nevertheless, Dagen fell in the quarterfinals; the Top 4 was fought not with this powerful X spell, but was decided by some of the most powerful creatures in the format.



![Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Geralf%27s+Messenger)

2. [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger)


When *Return to Ravnica* was just released, many players predicted that Zombies would dominate the format. But that prediction did not come to be, in large part because [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) stops the brain-hungry creatures dead in their tracks and because [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) is simply so efficient at dealing with [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger). But metagames change every week; as players were preparing for Blue-White Flash and Green-White Aggro, they started to pay less heed to Zombies. So, cards like [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) and even [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) were on the decline this weekend. This gave [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) its moment to shine in the hands of Tomas Vanek and Kamil Napierski, who piloted Red-Black Zombies to the Top 4. As a cheap, aggressive creature that can sap away the opponent's last life points, [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) is the cornerstone card of a deck that everyone will have to pay due respect to again.



![Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)

1. [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)


And finally, the signature card of Martin Juza's winning deck, which aims to accelerate into a turn 4 Behemoth with either [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage) or [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). And when Hoof enters the battlefield, it almost always instantly kills the opponent as the deck if filled with cheap 1/1 creatures such as [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim). The deck was affectionately named "Hoof, there it is!" by Brad Nelson, who invented the deck and is currently tearing up Grand Prix Charleston on the other side of the ocean. But here in Bochum, it was Juza who piloted this amazing [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) deck to take the title once again. Hoof!
