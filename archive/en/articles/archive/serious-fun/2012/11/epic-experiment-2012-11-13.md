An Epic Experiment
==================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on November 13, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







A few weeks ago I showed  [a few guild-based decks for sharing **Magic**](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/govern-guildless-2012-10-23). At the end of the article the results for a poll were shown: You chose the Izzet, in a narrow victory over the Golgari, as the guild for an unspecified purpose. 

Welcome to Izzet Week, where no thing's purpose is unspecified.

 You see, *everything* has a purpose and use, at least if you're an Izzet. *Finding* the use you need may be an altogether different tale. For us, what guild you chose set me down the path to playing as that guild. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf221_05_izzet_2.jpg)  
 As I clarified in [last week's Sealed adventure from yesteryear](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/old-time-rock-and-roll-2012-11-06), I did not manage to discover time travel. That is a Guild Prerelease pack, and it was played after the *Return to Ravnica* Prerelease has passed. So what gives? 

 While I admit I'm not [the model family man like Mark Rosewater](/en/articles/archive/making-magic/kids-play-part-1-2012-11-05), I help out as best as I can. Due to the combination of a class my wife was taking and an unavoidable family function, I wasn't able to attend a Prerelease for *Return to Ravnica*. 

 Yes, I do plan to show Kate this article as proof that I love her more than **Magic**. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf221_16_kate.jpg)  
 Thanks to my friends in the area, I was put in touch with one of the stores that did not sell out of the Prerelease: [Beyond Comics](http://www.beyondcomics.com/) in Gaithersburg, MD. Getting my hands on the Prerelease packs was the easy part. What to do with them was harder. 

Like an Izzet, I puzzled and tinkered with many ideas.


* [Group Game Draft](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/compleat-group-game-2011-05-24)—but each pack is a full Sealed pool, not a draft!
* Multiplayer Sealed—but those tend to stagnate as players avoid any risk!
* Attack Left Multiplayer—but I [already had a plan for that](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/old-time-rock-and-roll-2012-11-06)!
 As  [Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*](/en/events/coverage/ptrtr12) creeped closer, I remembered the format I learned at [Worlds 2011](/en/events/coverage/mwc11): [Star Draft](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/starry-eyed-wonder-2011-12-06). With a thud of dropped mizzium it hit me that each of the guilds in *Return to Ravnica* share a color with only two other guilds. If you laid them into a pentagonal grid, you'd have a star shape. 

 Guild Star Sealed was born. Each player would have a Guild Pack from the Prerelease, but play a game of  [Star **Magic**](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf6) where the goal was to defeat the two guilds you didn't share a color with. 

It's was an overcharged contraption to be sure, but I couldn't imagine anything less than silliness with this much multicolor firepower floating around.

I recruited a few familiar faces around my local game store to fill in for the four other guilds.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf221_01_dorian.jpg)  
Dorian is a stern figure in most of our games, enjoying both the insanity that Commander and other formats bring as well as the strategy of plotting a victory through it all. He also has a killer beard.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf221_02_min.jpg)  
 Min is a [Level 2 DCI judge](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=judge/welcome) and connoisseur of casual contraptions. He always enjoys a Limited event, and he was very glad to hop in. He also has a killer goatee. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf221_03_russell.jpg)  
Russell's one of the few faces I see every week I can make it to the shop. Without fail, he wears a grin and carries a bag full of decks. I've never caught him without something in hand to play. He also has a killer beard.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf221_04_john.jpg)  
John isn't somebody I had the chance to play many games with, but he's always willing to give something a shot. Thanks to him being the fifth volunteer, we were able to fire my personal Prerelease. Thanks John! He also has a killer goatee.

 With our [Movember](http://us.movember.com/)-suitable table of contenders, we each set about building from our Guild Packs. These are where we ended up, in turn order.
