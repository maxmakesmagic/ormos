SUNDAY COVERAGE



This weekend proved to be monumental for Spain, and it is all thanks to Antonio Del Moral León. Coming into his fourth Pro Tour, the 2014 *Magic Online* Championship Series season winner battled through Top 25 players, Hall of Famers, and eventually one very talented Pro Tour first-timer before ultimately achieving victory at Pro Tour *Fate Reforged* in Washington, DC.


Piloting a traditional Splinter Twin deck, Del Moral León traversed through an intense quarterfinal match against sixth-ranked player Lee Shi Tian and his Burn deck. From there, he quickly trumped Hall of Famer Jelger Wiegersma in the semifinal mirror match pitting Splinter Twin against Splinter Twin. After that, he overcame Pro Tour first-timer Justin Cohen's devastating Amulet Bloom deck in the finals, securing the trophy, $40,000 USD, Platinum Pro status, and an invitation to the 2015 World Championship.


Perhaps worth more than all of that though, is Del Moral León's place in *Magic* history as Spain's first Pro Tour champion. 


Modern has been turned on its head, but sometimes an old favorite never goes out of style, and Del Moral León proved just that.

SATURDAY COVERAGE



At the end of sixteen rounds, only eight remain. Sitting at the top of the standings is last week's Grand Prix San Jose team champion, Platnum Pro, and eleventh-ranked Eric Froehlich, but he is not the only all-star in tomorrow's playoffs. Following him is Dutch Hall of Famer Jelger Wiegersma, along with three-time Grand Prix champion Seth Manfield.


Joining these three is Pro Tour Born of the Gods finalist and fifteenth-ranked Jacob Wilson, who has proven his mastery of the Modern format this weekend. 


Also in tomorrow's Top 8 and another Modern all-star is Lee Shi Tian of Hong Kong, arguably the best Magic player from Asia as he earns his incredibly fourth Pro Tour Top 8 in two and a half years. He will be joined by 2014 *Magic Online* Championship Series winner Antonio Del Moral León of Spain, newcomer Justin Cohen, and Grand Prix Nashville team champion Jesse Hampton.


Eight great players, three more rounds, and tons of Modern excitement await you tomorrow. Be sure to tune in, because you won't want to miss a second of the action.
