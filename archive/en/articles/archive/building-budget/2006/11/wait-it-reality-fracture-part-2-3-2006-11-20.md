Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget! I'm going to continue evolving the Reality Fracture deck from [last week's article](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/evolution-fractured-crusading-sliver-fungus-part-1-3-2006-11-13). But before we get to the blue-red mix, I have a breaking news story for you!


At the end of my article two weeks ago, I ran this little poll:




| **Which *Time Spiral* Theme Deck would you like to see Ben doctor first?** |
| --- |
| Fun With Fungus | 2715 | 37.6% |
| Hope's Crusaders | 963 | 13.3% |
| Reality Fracture | 1750 | 24.3% |
| Sliver Evolution | 1786 | 24.8% |
| **Total** | **7214** | **100.0%** |

Clearly, Fun with Fungus won that poll, and by a sizable margin. However, I chose to evolve Reality Fracture first.


Scandalous!


Let me offer my sincerest apologies to anyone who may have been offended by last week's article. To clear up any confusion: I usually get poll results on Wednesday around noonish, and my articles are due in around 10am on Thursday. This means that whatever polls I have in an article usually will apply to the article two weeks down the road.


Clearly what I should have done here was either word the poll differently (such as "Which Theme Deck would you like to see evolved the most?"), or I should have held off evolving any *Time Spiral* Theme decks for a week, and then gone with the Fun With Fungus deck.


For what it's worth, I've definitely learned my lesson from this, and I have two solemn promises. One: The next theme deck evolved in this column, after Reality Fracture, will be the Fun with Fungus deck. I have ideas, yes I do! The second is that never again will you see a poll in this column. I don't want to cause any hard feelings, so they are banished from Building on a Budget forever!


Anyhow, enough of that - let's get to the meat of this week's article.

When last we left off, I had just fixed the mana configuration of the deck and added in some [Jhoira's Timebug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhoira%27s+Timebug)s. I'm still unsure about how the deck plays out, since my last few games were marred by inconsistent mana draws. Before I make any huge cuts to the core of the deck, I want to get a better feel of how Storm and Suspend work, hand in hand.


**Game 1: Weeell (Slivers)**


Turn 1, I suspend [Keldon Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Halberdier). I then suspend [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) on turn four, and he drops link, stuck at three mana. I would have had an [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) for six on the next turn.


Record: 1-0


**Game 2: Mack\_Nasty (Mono-Black Control)**


Turn 1: Suspend [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier)


Turn 2: [Jhoira's Timebug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhoira%27s+Timebug)


Turn 3: Suspend [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron)


Turn 4: Suspend [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)


I time it so my [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier) and [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron) come into play on the same turn. He casts [Funeral Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Funeral+Charm) on my [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier), and I respond by using Timebug on the [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate), to try to get it into play. He in turn responds with [Sudden Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death), killing my [Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephemeron). I do get to save my [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier), but that good play saves him 8 points of damage on one turn.


Mack\_Nasty then drops two [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena)s and draws a billion creature kill spells. The game ends on back-to-back-to-back turns of [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) thrown straight at my nugget.


Record: 1-1


**Game 3: TheMeek666 (5-Color Highlander Toolbox)**


I get him to 10 with Suspend guys, but he kills them with [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify) and [Wrecking Ball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrecking+Ball). I set up a turn where I go [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt), Clockspin it, then [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) twice (once for three and once for four) for exactly the win.


Record: 2-1


**Game 4: LordSanada (U/R Izzet)**


He gets [Gelectrode](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gelectrode), burns me with some *Ninth Edition* burn spells, and then gets [Wee Dragonauts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wee+Dragonauts). He has one big turn where he gets me down to 7 thanks to double sorcery/triple ping/double Dragonauts. I unsuspend [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron)(he went up in the air on turn three), and it gets hit by [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel). That allows me to [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) for three


(killing his flyer) and then [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories) for four, hitting 13 points worth of damage in his hand. Unfortunately, one of those cards in his hand is [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze), and he [Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blaze)s me out of the game on the next turn.


Record: 2-2


**Game 5: Jhestyr (White Weenie)**


He drops a couple of smaller guys, including [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden), and I firm down the board with [Giant Oyster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Oyster)...that is, until he drops [Witch Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witch+Hunter)! I don't get burn, and he bounces my Oyster off the board. He then drops [Celestial Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Crusader) and starts swinging. I cast [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) to get his bouncer off the board, but he gets [Mouth of Ronom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mouth+of+Ronom) and kills my guy right back.


I then suspend both the [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier) and [Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephemeron) on one turn, but he drops [Opal Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opal+Gargoyle), and things look grim indeed. Both my guys hit the board, and I swing for 4 (the [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier) dies). I then burn [Whispers of the Muse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispers+of+the+Muse), cast [Coal Stoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coal+Stoker), and hit him with an [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories) for five. His hand: [Proper Burial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proper+Burial). This does 20 damage to him...but he's at 27 life thanks to the [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden), and I only did 24 damage in this one turn. I lose on the counterattack.

One-toughness creatures don't last long against this deck, and I use [Coal Stoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coal+Stoker) plus [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) to kill his team. On the next turn, both of my flyers come into play, I swing for 11, and then I [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories) into a handful of his four-to-six cost spells, bringing [Alabaster Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alabaster+Dragon) well into the negatives.


Record: 4-5


**Game 10: Pondash (W/R Mid-Range)**


He drops [Zhalfirin Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zhalfirin+Commander), and I suspend [Deep-Sea Kraken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep-Sea+Kraken) on turn three and [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron) plus [Keldon Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Halberdier)s on turn four. He attacks for three, and I suspend triple [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) on turn five. I have to use two of them to take out his Commander, but I aim the third at his head (taking him to 17) and then cast [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories) for four, hitting [Icy Manipulator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icy+Manipulator), [Icy Manipulator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icy+Manipulator), [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) and [Cloudchaser Kestrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudchaser+Kestrel) (15 damage). If I had sent either of the other [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt)s directly at Pondash, I would have killed him outright in one turn! As it stands, he's unable to deal with my other guys as they unsuspend, and I have no trouble dealing the last two damage to win.


Record: 5-5
