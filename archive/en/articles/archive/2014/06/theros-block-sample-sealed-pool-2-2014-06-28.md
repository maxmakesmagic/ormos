THEROS BLOCK SAMPLE SEALED POOL #2
==================================



 Posted in [ARTICLES](/en/articles)
 on June 28, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






Building a deck is among the first features of the game we learn about, but it's also one of the most elusive skills to master. While the dizzying array of choice in formats like Standard and Modern are difficult due to their size and consistency, Limited – particularly the booster pack-dependent Sealed – is an altogether different challenge.


Sealed Deck is six booster packs—eighty-four random cards, minus the occasional premium foil basic land or two—to find, build, and play a deck with. The sample Sealed pool featured here features some obvious hits that excite any player:


[Brimaz, King of Oreskos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos); [Godsend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godsend); [Dictate of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dictate+of+Kruphix); [Mistcutter Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistcutter+Hydra); [Daxos of Meletis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daxos+of+Meletis); and [Phenax, God of Deception](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phenax%2C+God+of+Deception).


All of these rares have homes in the right deck, the question left to you is to find any of them in the pool. Is white's array of power (backed up with removal like [Banishing Light](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banishing+Light) and tricks like Ajani's Presence) enough alone, or do we pair it with blue to add [Daxos of Meletis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daxos+of+Meletis) (and tricks like Voyage's End)? Is the beef in green, led by the ever-scaling [Mistcutter Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistcutter+Hydra), where we start instead? What about the win-from-defense angle presented by [Phenax, God of Deception](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phenax%2C+God+of+Deception) and creatures like [Mnemonic Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Wall) and [Grim Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Guardian)?


Take a look at the options and discover what you find yourself. We'll see what the pros had to say about it later.
