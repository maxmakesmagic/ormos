Round 15 Feature Match: Causing a Ratchet
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 11, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BillStark.jpg)
By Bill Stark












[Jason Schousboe (Wolf Run Aggro) vs. Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa (Wolf Run Ramp)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_14)


Mamoru Nagai (Three-Color Wolf Run) vs. Jelger Wiegersma (Delver Spirits)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------


[Samuele Estratti (White-Blue Delver) vs. Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa (Wolf Run Ramp)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_16)


Pro Tour *Dark Ascension* entered the next-to-last round of competition with three Pro Tour Hall of Fame members in contention for the Top 8: Jon Finkel, Brian Kibler, and Jelger Wiegersma. It was Wiegersma, however, who found himself at the feature match table. His opponent was Japan's Mamoru Nagai who was a lock for the Top 8, a fact wasted no time in trying to leverage.


"You're already in Top 8?" He asked his opponent. After a nod from Nagai he added, "You should concede!" The statement earned only a confused look from his opponent, its meaning lost in translation.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r15_nagai_vs_wiegersma.jpg)
*Hall of Famer Jelger Wiegersma was not getting out of this round the easy way. His opponent, Mamoru Nagai, came to game.*

### Game 1


A first-turn [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) from Wiegersma revealed his opponent to be playing some type of black and green deck. The Japanese pro's grip included [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime), and [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), and he led with a [Woodland Cemetery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Cemetery) for his opening land. Across the table his European opponent went probe crazy casting a second, then third, then using [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to flash one back from the graveyard all in a single turn! That left Wiegersma at just 12 life while his opponent accelerated his mana with a [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth).


A [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) for Wiegersma gave him a heartier ground force, but his opponent managed to stall things significantly with a [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum). But the 2/2 Robot was dealt with via [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) and Wiegersma was able to get his beat on, making the totals 14-12; they still weren't in his favor, but at least he was getting on the board.


The players traded back and forth, Nagai casting [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) while his opponent had [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain). The 2/2 Captain allowed Wiegersma to drop his opponent to just 6 and Nagai found himself in difficult to maneuver territory. He needed a solution to his opponent's flier, [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists), [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain), and the spirit tokens being pumped by said captain.


Did he have it? He used an [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) to find a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), revealing a likely red, green, and black version of the Wolf Run deck as his chosen pick for battle. He used [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) to take out his opponent's Captain, then cast a second [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum). The play left him tapped out, however, and Wiegersma revealed a [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) in his hand to deal exactly lethal with his next attack; the players headed to the second with Wiegersma leading.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r15_wiegersma.jpg)
*Wiegersma takes an early lead with his spirits.*

Mamoru Nagai 0, Jelger Wiegersma 1


With a language barrier impeding communication, the two players shuffled in total silence for the second game.


### Game 2


The infamous [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) led the way for Wiegersma in the second game, though he was on the draw. In no time it was an [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) before being joined by to spirits from a [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) took out the 3/2 flier, but Wiegersma continued dropping elbows as he plunked [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) to the battlefield. The 2/2 buffed his team of spirits and allowed him to put the totals at 20-12 in his favor.


[Curse of Death's Hold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) for Nagai helped him scramble to catch back up but thanks to the Captain it served mostly to put Wiegersma's team back to fairer size. [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) gave Nagai an extra land but what he was really looking for was a flying blocker. He wasn't finding one, and fell to 6 from Wiegersma's repeated attacks.


[Black Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) wiped Wiegersma's team from the battlefield as Nagai successfully stabilized. Without Captain on the battlefield Wiegersma's job of winning became a lot harder: the [Curse of Death's Hold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) would keep his 1/1 tokens well in hand. A second [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) joined Nagai's first and *he* began to beat his opponent down.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r15_nagai.jpg)
*Nagai's three-color variant on Wolf Run Ramp proved to be a valuable tool for him throughout the tournament.*

With things looking bad for him, the European Hall of Famer's Delver Spirits deck failed to assist. When Nagai resolved a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and searched up his deck's namesake [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) it was over.


Mamoru Nagai 1, Jelger Wiegersma 1


### Game 3


The early plays of the final game of the match moved slowly, and it wasn't until the third turn that Wiegersma cast a creature in the form of [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). The 1/1 became a 3/2 the following turn while Nagai worked on building up his mana via [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum). A second Delver soon joined Wiegersma's first and Nagai tried to catch up with a [Glissa, the Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glissa%2C+the+Traitor) and [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb). The combo allowed him to take out his opponent's [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) without losing the [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) and forced Wiegersma into the tank in order to come up with a new plan.


The Glissa/[Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) combo left Nagai in the driver's seat and Wiegersma tried to dig his way to a new plan with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). A [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) was what he came up with, but the creature-creating land was unlikely to be good enough; the spirits it could generate were immediately vulnerable to Nagai's Glissa combo!


A [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) allowed Wiegersma to finally break up his opponent's combo, stranding the [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) in Nagai's graveyard, but he was still facing a superior board. To make matters worse, after re-casting the 3/2 Nagai could potentially get the Bomb back anyway if at any point in time his opponent had a creature die. Glissa began attacking for Nagai, but a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) proved too greedy as it was countered via [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) from his opponent. A [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), however, resolved successfully a turn later and Nagai had the match in hand. The following turn Wiegersma extended a handshake and scooped up his cards.


Jelger Wiegersma 1, Mamoru Nagai 2
