Christensen Breaks Through in Gothenburg
========================================




June 7-9, 2013












Grand Prix Gothenburg 2013
--------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/trophy_shot.jpg)It was an amazing weekend full of **Magic** here in Gothenburg, Sweden. 1,006 players entered the tournament on Saturday, 128 came back to draft on Sunday, and at the very end it all came down to one final booster draft in the Top 8. Here, Danish newcomer Oscar Christensen defeated all and took the title of Grand Prix champion!


This weekend marked the debut of the *Return to Ravnica* Block Sealed Deck format at the Grand Prix level, and it was only the second event, after the Pro Tour three weeks ago, to feature *Dragon's Maze* Booster Draft. And if one lesson could be gleaned from this tournament, it was that no strategy dominates; the age-old battle to balance power and consistency was fought at every turn here in Gothenburg. Navigating the intricate new Limited format, some players went with four or even five colors, others chose but two. Both extremes could be, and have been, succesful.


Take, for example, the final match of the weekend, where Christensen's blue-black-red control met straight Rakdos, as drafted and piloted by Mats Törnros of Sweden. This time, the control deck prevailed, but the games were close and the result could have easily been the other way around. Or look at Jan van der Vegt's two-color draft strategy which has been wildly succesful over the course of the weekend but abandoned him in the quarterfinals. The point is, there's still a lot of room to explore in the maze of Ravnica.


Of course, further exploration will be done right next week at Grand Prix Houston as well as all over the world at various FNM locations and on **Magic Online**. Meanwhile, the coverage team says good bye from Sweden, and congratulations to Oscar Christensen, champion of Grand Prix Gothenburg 2013!

Sunday, 5:33 p.m. – Talking Draft Strategy with Jan van der Vegt
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Just after he had won his win-and-in match for this Grand Prix's Top 8, I had the chance to chat with Dutch up-and-coming pro Jan van der Vegt about his general approach to drafting the Return the Ravnica Block. At Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* one of the bigger stories were two-color decks, and Jan drafted one of those here this weekend to great success, finishing the Swiss rounds in first place.


"I usually try to draft a *Return to Ravnica* guild. By about pick five or six of the *Dragon's Maze* pack, one often gets very clear signals what guild is open," explained Jan. "Most people seem to prefer the *Gatecrash* guilds for the two-color approach, because then you can cut the guild you want to draft, sending signals rather than receiving them. But there are so many ways how that can go wrong: Maybe the person to your left picked up two good Gruul cards early and will stick to it no matter what, or will even switch into your guild at the beginning of the second booster, because that's still kind of early enough, or a three-color drafter will throw a wrench into things."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/blog_draftvegt.jpg)  
*Jan van der Vegt* 
What guilds in particular? "Selesnya and Rakdos are good, but the best one is Azorius," said Jan. "It seems somewhat underdrafted and also fliers are so amazing in this format. [Wind Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind+Drake) is actually a strong card."


And what if the two-color strategy doesn't work out? "Well, of course, there's always the five-color control deck as a fall-back," Jan admitted with a shrug. "I have drafted a lot of those too, I regularly second-pick Guildgates for example. Sometimes there just isn't a two-color in the packs. It still is a multicolor block after all."

Sunday, 8:30 p.m. – Top 8 Draft Graphical Overview
--------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

The picture below described the draft seating, the direction in which the *Dragon's Maze* and *Return to Ravnica* packs were passed, and the colors that all players drafted.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/Top-8-draft-colors.jpg)
Clearly, red was overdrafted: All players except for one dabbled in red. Blue and black, on the other hand, were drafted by only three and four players, respectively. Notably, eventual champion Oscar Christensen was in the Dimir colors. Apparently, reading the table and moving into the underdrafted colors is a great strategy for winning a Grand Prix!




Quarterfinals – Roundup
-----------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/qf_1.jpg)  
*Antti Varhimo 1-2 Robin Steinborn* 
The match between Antti Varhimo and Robin Steinborn, was the first to finish, and to no one's surprise, as this pairing pitted Varhimo's straight Boros deck against Steinborn's Boros/Rakdos hybrid. In the first game, Steinborn curved out with [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps), [Hellhole Flailer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellhole+Flailer) unleashed, [Court Street Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Street+Denizen), and [Viashino Firstblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Firstblade). Varhimo never had a chance. In the second, Steinborn mulliganed to three cards, evening the score. And in the third game, Steinborn accelerated into [Maze Rusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+Rusher) with the help of [Crypt Ghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Ghast) and had sufficient detaining capabilities, in [Azorius Arrester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Arrester) and [Lyev Decree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyev+Decree) to carry the advantage to victory.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/qf_2.jpg)  
*Jan van der Vegt 0-2 Oscar Christensen* 
Meanwhile, Oscar Christensen's blue-black-red deck had succesfully deployed Mirko Vosk, [Fluxcharger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fluxcharger), [Mindeye Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindeye+Drake), and [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) to even keep Jan van der Vegt's army of Bird tokens from attacking. Nothing moved on the board, except for Christensen's [Lobber Crew](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew), slowly whittling away at van der Vegt's life total. Christensen then ended the game by activating the first ability of his [Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage) and hitting van der Vegt with [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering). For the second game, van der Vegt had a sweet start of Sindic of [Tithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tithe)s, [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer), and Bronzeback Moa but lost the Syndic to [Devour Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh), then couldn't get past [Woodlot Crawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodlot+Crawler), and soon lost to Mirko Vosk.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/qf_3.jpg)  
*Mark Dictus 0-2 Thoralf Severin* 
In the third quarterfinal, Thoralf Severin and his blue-green-red deck beat Mark Dictus and his Golgari deck (splashing Boros) 2-0. In one game, Dictus didn't have an answer for Severin's [Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) until it was way too late; in the other he mulliganed down to five cards, while Severin opened strong on [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark), [Deathcult Rogue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathcult+Rogue), and [Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager). Dictus fought back valiantly with [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage) and [Lotleth Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll), but Severin's [Chorus of Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chorus+of+Might) decided the damage race in his favor.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/qf_4.jpg)  
*Michal Gajewski 1-2 Mats Törnros* 
This leaves the match between Michal Gajewski who had drafted blue-red-green and Mats Törnros on Rakdos. The latter rushed the former with [Rakdos Shred-Freak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Shred-Freak), [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast), [Riot Piker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riot+Piker), and [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker), even managing to disable [Runner's Bane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runner%27s+Bane) via [Civic Saber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Saber), and took the lead. After mulligans, however, Törnros lost the second game. For the third, it was Gajewski turn to mulligan. He kept a five-card hand with one [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), one [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), blue and red three- and four-drops, then proceeded to draw four green cards straight. When he found a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), Törnros's [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator) had made it almost impossible for Gajewski to come back into the game, and [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) made it totally impossible.




Semifinals – Mats Törnros vs. Thoralf Severin
---------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

Well, that didn't take long. Mats Tornros and his blazingly fast Rakdos deck defeated Thoralf Severin in 2 quick games.


In game 1, Törnros' had [Shadow Alley Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Alley+Denizen), [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker), [Gutter Skulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gutter+Skulk), and [Wight of Precinct Six](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wight+of+Precinct+Six) over the course of his first four turns: an impressive amount of pressure. Severin never had the opportunity to muster a defense. When Törnros found his fourth land and [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator) came down, it was over.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/sf_Severin-Tornros.jpg)  
*Thoralf Severin (left) vs Mats Tornros (right)* 
In game 2, it was Severin who was the early aggressor, with [Deathcult Rogue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathcult+Rogue) and [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) attacking. "But he never got green mana," Törnros summarized after the match. Törnros played [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler), [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast), and [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator) and simply started attacking. "I was a bit scared of [Ætherize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therize) since I passed it to him in the draft." But as it turned out, Severin had nothing except for a bunch of uncastable green cards. When he finally found a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), it was too late.




Semifinals – Robin Steinborn vs. Oscar Christensen
--------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

This one took a bit longer, but Oscar Christensen's high-impact spells defeated Robin Steinborn's White-Red-Black deck in two games.


In game 1, Christensen had the defender team of [Murmuring Phantasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murmuring+Phantasm) and [Lobber Crew](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew) to stave off Steinborn's early attacks. Christensen's plan of winning the game with [Mirko Vosk, Mind Drinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirko+Vosk%2C+Mind+Drinker) seemed to be thwarted by Steinborn's [Launch Party](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party), but it immediately entered the battlefield again due to [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering). Steinborn didn't have a removal spell for the second incarnation, and was quickly milled to death.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/sf_Christensen-Steinborn.jpg)  
*Oscar Christensen (left) vs Robin Steinborn (right)* 
In game 2, Christensen again had the same defenders holding the fort. Steinborn used [Shadow Slice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Slice) plus [Lyev Decree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyev+Decree) to push through a lot of damage, but didn't manage to deal 20. Steinborn then hit a bunch of lands. "I think I was pretty lucky, since my opponent drew too many lands," Christensen said after the match. At some point, having [Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact), [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch), and [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering) in hand but needing a sixth land to play them, Christensen chose to play [Dimir Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Charm) on himself. "I figured, why not", he mentioned. It worked out well: He found a land in his top three cards. A few turns later, his 6-mana haymakers had taken the game, and the match.




Finals – Oscar Christensen vs. Mats Törnros
-------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

"Break you ...?" asked Oscar Christensen in the final moments of this final duel of the weekend, revealing [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering). Mats Törnros held his opponent's gaze for a bit, then nodded, "Sure." And with that, Christensen won the game and match to become the champion of Grand Prix Gothenburg 2013, to the deafening cheers of his friends.


But it was no easy path that led to this triumph. At the beginning of the match it seemed as if Christensen's control-oriented blue-black-red deck might be at a disadvantage in the fight against Törnros's straight-up Rakdos.


"How many have you got?" Christensen asked in disbelief when faced with two [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator)s on the fifth turn of the first game. Törnros just smiled. "At least two," Törnros answered, with a third copy still somewhere in his deck. Meanwhile, Christensen had missed his fourth land drop, and when he made it, he was still missing blue mana. He never stood a chance.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/f_toernros.jpg)  
*Mats Törnros* 
So game one was utter carnage, literally, and Christensen now found himself down one game, with his back against the wall.


The second game, however, saw the traditional roles curiously reversed. Here it was Christensen's [Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage) who led the beatdown. Christensen followed it up with [Bane Alley Broker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bane+Alley+Broker) and [Bane Alley Blackguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bane+Alley+Blackguard), while Törnros only had two 1/1s in [Wight of Precinct Six](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wight+of+Precinct+Six) and [Gateway Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gateway+Shade).


But once again [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator) came down on turn four, and a second one joined its colleague on turn five. The Gladiator attacked, and Christensen didn't dare block with his freshly-summoned [Mindeye Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindeye+Drake)—a good choice as [Weapon Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weapon+Surge) was waiting in Törnros's hand. Instead, the 2/5 flier continued what [Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage) and [Bane Alley Blackguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bane+Alley+Blackguard) had started, getting Törnros down to 9.


Of course, two [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator)s on the opposing side didn't just sit there. Trying to win the race, Törnros attacked with all of his creatures, but, unfortunately, managed to deal exactly zero points of damage thanks to Christensen's overloaded [Downsize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Downsize). [Mindeye Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindeye+Drake) put Törnros at 7.


And again Törnros attcked with all of his creatures. Even though Christensen cumpblocked both Gladiators, their triggers along with [Gateway Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gateway+Shade) and [Wight of Precinct Six](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wight+of+Precinct+Six) dealt a whopping 8 damage, half of Christensen's life total at this point. He wouldn't survive one more attack. Luckily, he didn't have to. His [Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact) put Törnros at 2 and [Mindeye Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindeye+Drake) finished the job just in time.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot13/f_christensen.jpg)  
*Oscar Christensen* 
Now the score stood at one win each, and it all came down to the third. Here, Törnros stumbled on his mana. Stuck on two [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, he lost his [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker) to [Devour Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh), then didn't do anything for a couple of turns. He even had to discard.


Meanwhile, Christensen had no red mana, but he did have [Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage) and begun the slow process of beating down for 2 points a turn. He sped that up a little by activating the Guildmage's first ability, followed by [Dimir Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Charm)'s third mode. Two [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s made their way to Törnros's graveyard, though now he did topdeck a third [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), cast [Viashino Racketeer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Racketeer), and finally found a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).


But it was too little too late. Soon, Christensen activated [Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage)'s first ability one last time, then pointed [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering) at Törnros, making him lose all of his remaining life total and claiming the title of Grand Prix champion!




Top 5 Cards of Grand Prix Gothenburg
------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

 


![Feral Animist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Feral+Animist)


 

### 5. [Feral Animist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Animist)


This card can do a ton of damage for the low, low cost of three mana. Top 8 competitor Mark Dictus named it as the best card in both of his 3-0 Gruul draft decks. He combined it with [Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) to turn the 2/1 into an impressive 12-power trampler to win game after game. It just goes to show that the synergies in this Limited format run very deep. Players such as Mark who recognized the value in cards like [Feral Animist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Animist) were rewarded over the course of the weekend.


 
 


![Azorius Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Azorius+Guildgate)


 

### 4. [Azorius Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildgate)


Mana fixing is a top priority in the multicolored Ravnica Block Limited format. In the Sealed deck portion, most players started by checking how many Guildgates they got. In the draft portion, several players were going as far as taking Guildgates first-pick. One of the biggest advocates of mana consistency was Kenny Oberg, who made Day 2 with an ultra-consistent blue-white deck featuring 3 [Azorius Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildgate)s. While some players are weary of running come-in-play tapped lands, Kenny didn't have to take many mulligans and was always able to cast his spells. But Guildgates are not just for fixing: we saw plenty of off-color Guildgates being played over the weekend to turn on [Saruli Gatekeepers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saruli+Gatekeepers) and the like.


  

 
 


![Verdant Haven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Verdant+Haven)


 

### 3. [Verdant Haven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Haven)


[Verdant Haven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Haven) saw a lot of play over the course of this weekend as an enabler for the five-color mages. It fills three important roles simultaneously: fixing mana, accelerating into the expensive haymakers, and staying alive. The Green enchantment was one of the centerpiece's of Jan van der Vegt's 9-0 Sealed deck; it allowed the Dutchman to cram his best cards from all five colors into his deck. [Verdant Haven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Haven) also allows for some sweet combo potential: Jan's fastest wins came from draws that provided turn 1 Guildgate, turn 2 [Greenside Watcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenside+Watcher), turn 3 [Verdant Haven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Haven) on the Guildgate, into turn 4 [Trostani's Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Summoner). How is that fair?


  

 
 


![Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Carnage+Gladiator)


 

### 2. [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator)


This weekend, we saw Rakdos annihilating anyone at a moment's notice, and we saw [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator) showcased in the way it is meant to be: absolutely punishing opponents who stumble on early drops and mana. A 4/2 regenerator for 4 mana wouldn't be a bad deal by itself, but its ability to make blocking into a nightmare is just insane for any aggressive Rakdos deck. When you're on the receiving side of [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator), it doesn't matter what you do. Block or don't block; your life total will go down regardless. In the Top 8, Mats Törnros had as many as three copies of [Carnage Gladiator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Gladiator) in his blazingly fast Rakdos deck. They quickly carried him to the finals, but in the end it was still Oscar Christensen who took the trophy with our number one card.


  

 
 


![Breaking // Entering ](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering+)


 

### 1. [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering)


It doesn't matter whether it's Round 1 or Round 18; when you're playing **Magic**, you are continually learning about new interactions. While drafting in the Top 8, Oscar Christensen didn't notice the interaction between [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering) and [Dimir Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildmage) right away. It wasn't until a game in the quarterfinals when he worked it out. With his opponent at 8 life, he used the first ability of [Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage) followed by the Dimir part of [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering) to win the game out of nowhere. In the semifinals, he used the Rakdos part to get back his eventually game-winning [Mirko Vosk, Mind Drinker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirko+Vosk%2C+Mind+Drinker). And in the crucial game 3 of the finals, the exact same situation as in the quarterfinals happened again. With Mats Törnros at 8 life, Oscar Christenen again had [Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage) plus [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering), and took the title of Grand Prix Gothenburg champion.
