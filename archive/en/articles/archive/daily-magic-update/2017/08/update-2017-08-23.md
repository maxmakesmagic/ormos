The August 23, 2017 Update
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on August 23, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on August 23, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by getting started hunting down all that sweet, sweet [*Magic* geocaching](https://www.geocaching.com/promotions/promo/magic/).


Today's Must
============


1. [Hatred Outlives the Hateful](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0VK9D1ti-Yg&feature=youtu.be) | TheMagicManSam

The best line of flavor text in the game? Maybe. But Sam uses that bit of flavor text from [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) as a way to answer the question "What makes good flavor text?"


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/0VK9D1ti-Yg>


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [Tolarian Tutor: Mulligans – A *Magic: The Gathering* Study Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q6aUeTn-DYY) | Tolarian Community College | The Professor

Prof is kicking off a new series that teaches skills for *Magic* players—like how best to mulligan. The Prof not only brings his own insight, but consulted with top *Magic* players to assemble the advice needed to mulligan better.


And there isn't a person on the planet who couldn't get better at mulligans. Myself for example; my creed is "Never mulligan, but if you have to, maybe don't?" That doesn't always work out.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/Q6aUeTn-DYY>


1. [7 Keys to Leveling Up Your Sideboarding](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/7-keys-to-leveling-up-your-sideboarding/) | ChannelFireball | Frank Karsten

While we're learning things, why not learn how to sideboard better. Some of these tips are eye-openers that make a ton of sense but have rarely been presented so simply and clearly.


1. [Mairsil the Pretender](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o1oiMFo8j8s) | Commanders Brew | Sean Tabares and Andy Hull

Ignore the Canadian economics banter at the beginning and skip right to the part where they start brewing with the most intriguing commander from *Commander (2017 Edition)*—Mairsil the Pretender.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/o1oiMFo8j8s>


1. [A Fraction of the Colors](https://www.hipstersofthecoast.com/2017/08/fraction-colors-commander/) | Hipsters of the Coast | Levi Byrne

Byrne asks a simple but certainly interesting question—would you play fewer colors with one of the commanders with eminence, just to take advantage of that single ability without ever casting them? For example, would you ever play a mono-blue [Inalla, Archmage Ritualist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inalla%2C+Archmage+Ritualist) deck?


1. [How to Tweak Temur Energy for Your Metagame](http://www.mtgmintcard.com/articles/writers/arnaud-soumet/how-to-tweak-temur-energy-for-your-metagame) | MTG Mint Card | Arnaud Soumet

Temur Energy is the deck of the moment, but also quite customizable. Soumet shows you how to make tweaks to tackle the decks you expect to face.


1. [Commander VS: *Amonkhet* Gods, Round 2!](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/35691_Commander-VS-Amonkhet-Gods-Round-2.html) | StarCityGames.com | Justin Parnell and Stephen Green

More like *Amonkhet* versus *Hour of Devastation* Gods, as this battle pits [Bontu the Glorified](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bontu+the+Glorified) and [Kefnet the Mindful](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kefnet+the+Mindful) against [The Locust God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Locust+God) and [The Scarab God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Scarab+God).

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Scarab God Control](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2017-08-21)

The newest hotness is [The Scarab God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Scarab+God), a hard-to-kill, cascading threat that runs away with games as soon as you get to untap with it. As creature-based as Standard is, [The Scarab God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Scarab+God) is quite the trump card, especially when backed up by so much removal. If it's this powerful now, it's likely going to be one of the premier threats in Standard. For now, it'll have to settle for being, well, one of the premier threats in Standard.
