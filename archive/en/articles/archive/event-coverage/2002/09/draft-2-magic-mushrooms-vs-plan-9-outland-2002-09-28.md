Draft 2: Magic Mushrooms vs. Plan 9 from Outland
================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 28, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptbos02/a871.jpg)Norway and France are two of the top Magic-playing countries in Europe, and many of the teams from those countries are having success here in Boston. Two of those teams – Plan 9 from Outland and Magic Mushrooms – are facing off here in Round 8.

Plan 9 is Sigurd Eskeland, Oyvind Odegaard, and Endre Skjetne. Eskeland and Odegaard have played together in this event for three years, but this is the first time they've made Day 2.

Magic Mushrooms is Franck Canu, Raphael Levy, and Christophe Haim. Levy and Canu finished 12th at this event last year, but this time traded in Wilfried Ranque for Haim who has proven himself this year.

As the draft began, Levy palmed a credit card which he used to covertly "point" at cards on the table by pointing to corresponding positions on the credit card. This way, Outland had no way of knowing what the Mushrooms were going to take until they actually drafted.

Levy started into white in the B seat with a [Shelter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter), shipping a [Springing Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springing+Tiger) to Haim. Outland took three black cards – [Dirty Wererat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dirty+Wererat), [Afflict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Afflict), and [Ghastly Demise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise) – and Canu ended up with [Devoted Caretaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Caretaker).

Haim busted the bomb [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) with his pack, passing [Savage Firecat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Firecat), [Elephant Ambush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Ambush), and [Puppeteer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer) to Skjetne, Odegaard, and Eskeland respectively. Canu took a [Hallowed Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Healer) and an [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst), looking to go white/blue in A, and Levy wheeled [Afflict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Afflict) and [Skeletal Scrying](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Scrying).

Outland opened [Patriarch's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Desire), [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel), and [Mystic Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot); Skjetne stayed in black, Odegaard in green, and Eskeland in blue/white. Canu took [Angelic Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Wall), Levy a [Morgue Theft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morgue+Theft) and [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari), and Haim wheeled [Springing Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springing+Tiger) and [Muscle Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muscle+Burst). Canu ended up with a [Crashing Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crashing+Centaur) that no one else wanted.

Odegaard's second color was decided when he cracked [Shower of Coals](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shower+of+Coals). Eskeland got an [Aven Windreader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader) out of it, and the Mushrooms took [Patrol Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound), [Dirty Wererat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dirty+Wererat), and a [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger) that Haim ended up not playing.

The last few packs of *Odyssey* were nothing special, except for Canu getting a second [Crashing Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crashing+Centaur).

Going into *Torment*, Skjetne was B/r, Odegaard G/r, and Eskeland U/W. Canu was W/x (currently X was looking more green than blue), Levy was B/x, and Haim was G/u.

In *Torment*, Skjetne was hoping to abandon red for green because he didn't want to fight with Odegaard over the thin red. But when Eskeland opened [Possessed Barbarian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possessed+Barbarian), Odegaard insisted that Skjetne take it. Skjetne ended up taking a [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) in the next pack, passing [Pardic Collaborator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pardic+Collaborator) to Levy. Odegaard didn't think Levy was going to be red, so he thought nothing of it at the time, but it was a definitive pick for Levy's deck.

Canu cemented himself in white/green by taking an [Anurid Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Scavenger), a [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), and later a [Possessed Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possessed+Centaur), which made the fact that Skjetne opened a [Grotesque Hybrid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grotesque+Hybrid) sting all that much more. The Hybrid can single-handedly beat Canu.

Levy continued to take red cards like [Temporary Insanity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporary+Insanity), and he opened a [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) in the last *Torment* pack. Odegaard wasn't thrilled.

*Judgment* began with Levy opening a pack containing [Phantom Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur) and [Anurid Brushhopper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Brushhopper). Haim took the Centaur, and Outland wasn't sure what to do with the 'Hopper. Eventually Odegaard settled on it, and planned to play at least 3 colors.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptbos02/a872.jpg)Eskeland's deck came together well in *Judgment* with two [Wormfang Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Crab)s, two Vigilant Sentries, a [Chastise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chastise), and a [Battlewise Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Aven). He must have forgotten about the [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) in Haim's deck, however, as he never took an [Envelop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Envelop). He never opened a [Prismatic Strands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Strands), either, which would have helped greatly. Also, he took the [Battlewise Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Aven) over a [Shieldmage Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shieldmage+Advocate), which he later said was a mistake.

Skjetne didn't get much for his black/red deck, just an [Arcane Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Teachings), and [Ember Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Shot), and some 2/2's. Odegaard picked up a few more good creatures and two [Sudden Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Strength)s, plus a [Krosan Verge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Verge) and [Riftstone Portal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftstone+Portal) to help him cast the Brushhopper.

Haim's big pickups were a [Wormfang Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Drake) and a [Lost in Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+Thought), plus a gift [Keep Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keep+Watch) that combos well with his two [Acorn Harvest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acorn+Harvest)s. Levy got a [Masked Gorgon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masked+Gorgon) (not great against Odegaard), and [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger), and some other random guys. Canu made out well with an [Elephant Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide), an [Unquestioned Authority](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unquestioned+Authority), and a few fliers.

After the draft, Outland felt like they weren't the favorites. Eskeland felt it would come down to his match with Haim, which he figured he could win (his tone indicated that he probably had forgotten about [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)). Skjetne expected to lose to Canu because he didn't have enough creatures, and Odegaard expected to win.

On the other side, Canu really felt like he was going to lose (both A players were not confident, probably because Canu's switch into white/green meant they'd be playing a matchup neither understood). Levy was shaky and expected to lose, but Haim was sure he could beat Sigurd. (Both C players expected to win. Is white/blue better than blue/green? The debate rages on.) I put Outland on winning just because white/green is not a good deck, especially against a deck featuring [Grotesque Hybrid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grotesque+Hybrid). Haim will win, Levy will lose, and Canu will lose, giving the match to Plan 9 from Outland.
