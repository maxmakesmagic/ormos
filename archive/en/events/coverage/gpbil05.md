GRAND PRIX BILBAO 2005
----------------------


Ruel Rules in Bilbao!
=====================




November 18-20











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpbilb05/fin.jpg)Ladies and Gentleman, we have a new leader in the Pro Player of the Year race.


Yes he's done it again. Turn away Kenji Tsumura and Masashi Oiso, Olivier Ruel has just picked up his third Grand Prix title of the year. While the rest of field was still locked into the Affinity-Boros Deck Wins-[Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) triangle, Olivier Ruel blew them apart with an updated version of the CAL (Confinement Assault-Life) deck.


It was a big tournament. Grand Prix in the south of Europe are always well attended and this was no exception as just under a thousand players came to the Bilbao Exhibition Centre for a chance to qualify for Pro Tour Honolulu.


Day one claimed some big scalps as both Julien Nuijten and Antoine Ruel failed to advance. There was also a strong local challenge as three Spanish players made it into the top 8. Of those only Jacob Arias Garcia advanced to the semi's where he fell to Ruel despite having 4 [Suppression Field](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suppression+Field) and 3 [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant) in the sideboard of his Boros Deck Wins. Neither Rogier Maaten or Jonathan Rispal, leaders after day one, were able to advance further than the quarters and the final boiled down to Olivier Ruel and Marcio Carvalho's Boros Deck Wins.


The final was lightning fast and brutal. In the first game Carvalho had no answer to Ruel's early [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement)-[Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) lock and scooped. The second game was much closer. All the early aggression was from Carvalho but then Ruel turned it around in a couple of turns with two [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch) and then an [Overgrown Estate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Estate) that ended any hopes Carvalho had of racing.


With just Grand Prix Beijing and the World Championships to go, the Pro Player of the Year race is really coming to a boil.


Congratulations to Olivier Ruel, champion of Grand Prix Bilbao.

Sunday, November 20: 6:26 p.m. - Quarterfinals: Olivier Ruel vs. Sergi Herrero
------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Hmm, the Pro Player of the year race is getting very interesting. Apologies to Kenji Tsumura and Masashi Oiso, but the crazy Frenchman has made yet another top 8. A win for Olivier Ruel here would see him draw dead level with Kenji. Ruel has switched from the Affinity deck he played a few weeks back and picked up the crazy [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement) - [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) - [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) ('CAL') deck that debuted in the top 8 of GP Kitakyuushuu a few weeks back. He's made a few alterations, most specifically the addition of Bob Confidant.


Standing in his way is Sergi Herrero, one of three Spaniards to make the top 8 here. The Affinity dragon has not quite been slain and now Ruel finds himself on the receiving end of the deck he's played since the start of the season.


Ruel won the die roll (all four of them) and led off with a [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) that netted nothing more than a [Chromatic Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere). Herrero's hand was solid rather than explosive: 3 land, Enforcer, Scale and Worker. Ruel blocked the Worker with a Tribe Elder and fetched a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). Herrero saved the Worker with the Scale and then his draw did get explosive as a [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) enabled a two mana Enforcer.



![Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Life+from+the+Loam)

A second Elder 'fogged' an Enforcer and found a second [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). [Fear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fear)ing [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault), Herrero dropped a Needle on it. Ruel found [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and cycling cards and started drawing cards. He needed some answers as he was facing down 8 power worth of monsters with nothing more than some basic land. An attack dropped him to 8 and left him facing lethal damage.


The Frenchman made a Top and dug for an answer.


He deliberated for a long time before casting [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) to fetch [Shatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter)stom …. no, that doesn't appear to be in this format. Instead he had to be content with a [Hull Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hull+Breach). He used it to kill the Enforcer and sat back nervously.


A lot of cards would kill Ruel at this point. Ravager….[Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating)…..


An attack dropped Ruel to 4. He cast Witness and fetched back the [Hull Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hull+Breach) to remove the [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle).


He had to pass another turn and wait to see if Herrero could kill him. The tension wracked up as Herrero cast a [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast). Even [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb) would spell game at this point. Still nothing. Herrero's attack dropped Ruel to a slender two life. The Spaniard dropped a second [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and Enforcer.


Now Ruel could fight back. [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) hit the table and Herrero's board disintegrated under a hail of land. He still had outs if he could just draw one of his 3 [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb). He didn't and Ruel kept casting and dredging back [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) until he had enough land in hand to pitch them all at Herrero.


Ruel 1-0 Herrero


Herrero had to mulligan the first hand and ended up with a hand that contained very little pressure. His opened with [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) and could only cast a Plating on turn two. Ruel's [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) revealed there were two [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite)s and a Jitte waiting to arrive, although a Thoughcast was sent packing.


Herrero finally exploded on turn three as he turned both his Nexi into artifacts, laid a Vault and then busted out both [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite). Ruel simply burnt them away with a [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm). Herrero got in for three with a Plated up Nexus but on the next attack the Plating was Putrefied. Herrero's hand was looking dangerously slow this game as a [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) fetch [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and kick-started Ruel's cycling engine.


Herrero dropped the Jitte but would need to get very busy, very quickly. Ruel was down to eleven, but was drawing a lot of cards. A [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) suddenly gave Herrero hope. He hit two [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement) and then flashed it back for the [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness). An attack dropped Ruel to 6 life. Now the Frenchman needed to get busy.


Herrero threw a big spanner, or rather needle, into the workers as Two Needle shut down Ruel's green and red cycling lands. Nexus-Plating threatened a win on the following turn.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpbilb05/qf_ruel.jpg)*Olivier Ruel*
Ruel used [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) to fetch [Hull Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hull+Breach) to deal with the immediate threat of the Plating. An [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) fetched back the [Hull Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hull+Breach) and freed up the cycling engine.


Ruel took a couple of pecks from the 1/1 Nexus to drop to four. Herrero would need an [Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) to do any further damage as Ruel made [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement). Herrero found a third [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) and tried to slow down or stop the engine completely.


It got worse for Herrero as Ruel flipped the top and put [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) into play. With red and green cycling lands shut down, Ruel had to let the Solitary confinement go. He tried to find a black land but in the end he didn't need it as he was able to cast two [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and go up to the required 11 land needed to burn Herrero out.


And that ties Ruel in first place with Kenji Tsumura for the Pro Player of the year race.


Oliver Ruel beats Sergi Herrero 2-0.


  

Sunday, November 20: 6:41 p.m. - Quarterfinals: Rogier Maaten vs. Marcio Carvalho
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpbilb05/qf_maaten.jpg)*Rogier Maaten*
Rogier (from the Netherlands) is playing Affinity and Marcio (from Portugal) is playing Boros Deck Wins. This is Marcio's second GP top 8 this year and it is Rogier's third GP top 8 this year, so these guys are no newcomers to the playoffs.  

Most players think that this matchup heavily favours the Boros Deck Wins, but Rogier disagrees. He had already beaten three Boros Deck Wins this tournament and thinks that the matchup is around 50-50; "if Boros Deck Wins cannot keep Kataki on the table, my [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog)s, fat Ravagers and Platings should allow me to win the game." We shall see.


### Game 1


Marcio won the important die roll and got to play first. However, he did not get off to a fast start. He kept a hand with lands and burn spells and simply played some saclands on his first two turns.


Rogier played [Seat of the Synod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seat+of+the+Synod), Sulphurous Springs, [Terrarion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrarion), [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) and [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) over the course of his first two turns. A [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) was flung at [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) end of turn, but it ate [Terrarion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrarion) to stay alive, netting Rogier a card in the process.


Marcio topdecked [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight), which was a nice foil to the red [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog). Rogiers affinity machine powered out a bunch of spells on the third turn: [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker), [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager), [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) and 2 [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite)s. Rogier did not have the game in the bag yet, but he definitely had more permanents on the board.



![Savannah Lions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Savannah+Lions)

Marcio used [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage) to destroy the Ravager. Rogier put the modular counter on [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite), which attacked on the following turn. Marcio blocked it with the first striking [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) and he traded a [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) plus a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) for the 3/3 [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and an [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker) modular counter.


Marcio destroyed an attacking [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) with [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and drew into [Savannah Lions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions) and [Goblin Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Legionnaire), whose activated abilities were shut down by [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle).  

Marcio then tried to break the creature stall and attacked with [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight). Rogier took 2 and attacked with [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) on his turn. Marcio, probably realizing that this was not going to work, chumped it with [Savannah Lions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions) and proceeded to leave [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) on defence.  

The next couple turns featured a couple uninteresting one-for-one-trades: [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) was destroyed by [Char](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char) and a land was killed by [Molten Rain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Rain).

Olivier, at 16-ish life, would play [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) (getting back another Witness), sacrifice lands to [Overgrown Estate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Estate) and play [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam). Jacob would burn the Witness and attack with [Goblin Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Legionnaire).  

That sequence of plays repeated itself for a while, until Olivier dredged into [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) and got it back with [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness). He took [Hull Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hull+Breach) out of his sideboard and destroyed the [Suppression Field](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suppression+Field). Now that his lands only cost 1 mana to cycle and 0 mana to sacrifice to [Overgrown Estate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Estate), he could go berserk.


Olivier cycled and dredged, cycled some more and found [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish). He got [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction) out of his sideboard. He removed his opponent's [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)s and played [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement) for the lock. Thanks to the [Extract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extract)ion, Jacob did not have any outs left. Jacob could not win anymore and conceded.


Olivier 2 - Jacob 1


  

Sunday, November 20: 9:33 p.m. - Finals: Olivier Ruel vs. Marcio Carvalho
-------------------------------------------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpbilb05/f_ruel.jpg)*Hello... Kenji?*
There was a slight delay at the start of the final as Olivier Ruel attempted to ring Kenji Tsumura to tell him he'd just overtaken him in the player of the year race. Unfortunately (or rather fortunately for Kenji. I have no idea what time it currently is in Japan, but it probably isn't sociable) he couldn't remember the country code.


So this is it. One more game to go. In one corner we have Olivier Ruel, current leader of the Pro player of the year race (since about five minutes ago) and a one man Grand Prix wrecking ball. In this final he will be gunning for an incredible third Grand Prix title for this year alone. This kind of form hasn't been seen since Kai Budde hit his first peak in 1999.


Ruel's opponent, Marcio Carvalho of Portugal, is no slouch either. If he wins this will be his second Grand Prix title of the year after winning Lisbon back in April.


Carvalho is running close to a fairly standard Boros Deck Wins while Olivier Ruel has the deeply unconventional CAL ([Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement), [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault), [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam)) deck. This will be tough for Carvalho. Ruel managed to get past another Boris Deck Wins in the semi's that could board both [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant) and [Suppress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suppress)ion field and Carvalho doesn't have anywhere near that level of hate.


Carvalho won the die roll and kicked off.


Carvalho managed the standard "sac land to fetch Foundry and start on 17 life" Boros start but his first turn critter was a Lavamancer rather than a Hound or Lion. Carvalho followed up with [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) and then a [Molten Rain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Rain) on Ruel's [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpbilb05/f_carvalho.jpg)*Marcio Carvalho*
It was a good start but Ruel had been able to nullify both the attack and [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage) with a Sakura Tribe Elder. [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) fetched him as much land as he needed and when Ruel dropped [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement) on the table Carvalho responded with an immediate concession.


Ruel 1-0 Carvalho.


Ruel sideboarded out 3 [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), 2 [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), 2 Divining Top and [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement). He replaced them with 2 [Overgrown Estate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Estate), 3 [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch), 2 [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) and a [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress).


Carvalho decided he wanted to start on 17 life again and this time he did have the first turn Hound of Konda. Ruel only took 1 point off his sac land to fetch a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and put a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into play.


It died. The bird always dies. Although it did delay the arrival of a [Goblin Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Legionnaire) for a turn.


Ruel made a second bird. It also died. Ruel decided to play a mana source where dying is actually the desired result and Sakura Tribe Elder entered play. Ruel seemed to be a little screwed as a result of mulliganing and was actually laying his cycling lands rather than cycling them.


Carvalho added a [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) and Ruel got an opportunity to [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) the Legionnaire for no damage when Carvalho went hunting for his third land. The Portuguese player was looking fairly good at this point. A Lavamancer joined the assault and a [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage) took out Ruel's plains. Although I couldn't see Ruel's hand I suspected that really hurt as the possibility of casting Hierarch had been taken away.


Oliver drew and laughed before showing me a [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault). He'd been making cycling lands and had no red sources in play.


"Not what I needed at this point".


But then things picked up as he found a [Wooded Foothills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills). That fetched him [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry), which then allowed him to cast [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch). Carvalho attacked into it, throwing away a creature to force damage through.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpbilb05/f_ruel2.jpg)*Olivier Ruel is the 2005 Grand Prix Bilbao Champion!*
Ruel was down to a slender 4 life. A second Loxodon Heirarch took him up to a more comfortable 8. Carvalho's attack was stalling against the elephants but Ruel's life total was still within reaching distance.


In the next turn Ruel attacked with both Hierarch, which confused the hell out of me. Surely he was on way too low life to risk racing. But then it all made sense as he dropped [Overgrown Estate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Estate). With five lands in play that was an extra 15 life. Suddenly Carvalho was facing down two 4/4's with only a 2/2 and a [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) with no prospect of racing. As his life was just 3 the game had suddenly gone from advantageous to unwinnable in a blink of an eye.


The match was so fast I never even got a chance to take photo's (As you may have noticed).


Olivier Ruel beats Marcio Carvalho 2-0 and takes home an incredible third Grand Prix title for the year.
