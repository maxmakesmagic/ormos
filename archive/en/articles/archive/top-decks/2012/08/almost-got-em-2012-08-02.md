Almost Got 'Em
==============



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on August 2, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






Both the StarCityGames Standard Open in Buffalo, NY, and the TCGPlayer Open $5K in Providence were won by [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) decks.


*Oh well!*


Blah blah [Talrand, Sky Summoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%2C+Sky+Summoner).


Grumble grumble [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td206_yawn2.jpg)  
Yes, yes, these are very good cards, complementing nascent good cards [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag).


Instead, this week we are going to focus on a couple of new decks and synergies that *almost* got there. If you are down for a good synergy—or what looks like a super happy fun time—I suggest sleeving up one of these.

Joseph Snyder earned Top 8 in the TCGPlayer event with a new take on some fairly well-established and very good cards... but he added them together and spun them into a new deck taking advantage of some different cross-synergies.


### Duress, Liliana of the Veil, Ravenous Rats, Smallpox


Snyder's deck has a strong hand destruction theme. He can start on turn one with a [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), then make you discard again with his [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats). You are annoyed and you kill it; or maybe he gets a trade against your one-drop to boot. Did you play another creature? Bam! [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox)!




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Ravenous Rats | Smallpox |

Perhaps he re-plays his third land and goes for the lockdown with [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil).


Snyder's deck doesn't play a lot of cards we haven't seen before. The most "out there" one in his main is probably [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox). But anyone who remembers the last time [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) was legal in Standard—and the shenanigans that it gave rise to in that era's Solar Flare shell—know that [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) in Standard is more of a rediscovery than a newly blazed trail.


I really like *how* Snyder put these cards together. We don't see a lot of [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox)- and Liliana-breaking of this stripe... and you have to wonder why. Once you see these cards together, it is sort of obvious that they would be great together!


### Geralf's Messenger, Gravecrawler, Lingering Souls


[Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) is a fine one for either of two different pipers [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) has to pay. Do you want to protect your [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats)? Have another Zombie on the battlefield? You can sacrifice a [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) in play and just get it back as soon as you have a spare [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).


[Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) is potentially devastating with [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox). *Drain you for two, [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox)* (sacrificing [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) of course), *take 1, drain you again!*




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Geralf's Messenger | Gravecrawler  |

Not only does [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) significantly blunt the bite of [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) there, but you still have a 4/3 in play after all that pugilism.


[Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) is a fine card to discard to [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), too... or, for that matter, [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil). Cards like [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) and [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)—that force both players to undertake some distasteful activity in equal measure—are a pain and a drain on both players. In theory, they are "even." With [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), you can get a little ahead by playing it on the play (which just might put your opponent back to no lands); and if your opponent went second and ran a one-drop, you are doing an emotional lap around the room!


But still, while you both have to discard cards, you actually had to *spend* one, as well.


[Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), while leveling up, requires both players to discard a card. In either of these situations you can break the symmetry by discarding some kind of a renewable resource (like [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), which has an easy flashback, or the buyback-body [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)).




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Liliana of the Veil | Lingering Souls |

There are tons and tons of subtle synergies in Snyder's deck... Like what if the opponent has an awesome guy and a crappy one? And you make your opponent sacrifice a creature with one of your "[Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edict)" effects from [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) or [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil). Your opponent chuckles a bit and sacrifices the crappy creature.


*[Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip)... YOU!*


Unless the awesome guy is *really* awesome... not a huge chance it can withstand a morbid [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip).


Far be it for me to undersell the presence of token creatures and token generators. [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) is not *just* a flashback spell that can help you recoup the up-tick process on [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil). It is *also* a token-maker! So when you have to sacrifice a creature to [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox)? While the opponent is picking which mythic rare is going to go to the bin? You can send a 1/1 wooden nickel to the graveyard. [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls); [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad); and [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) out of the sideboard all excel at producing a small creature you would not mind sacrificing to a symmetry. And [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements)? You can even set yourself up with a little life loss to help ensure maximum Reinforcement!

Kurt Crane's GW Elves deck is simply a joy to look at, isn't it?


(It's getting to talk about decks like these that really put the joy into this gig, by the way.)


Kurt's deck is like any old Elves deck. It has a bunch of acceleration (twelve one-drop accelerators among [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), and [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves)) and can hit a lord on turn two to get its beat on. In addition, it has [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) for a bit of additional consistency.


Kurt plays value creatures like [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) (how about flickering that [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary)?)... cards and cards and consistency and synergy. Out of his sideboard he can bring in not one, not two, not three, but four full copies of [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) to slow down control decks and really put the hurt on [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). *Oh, you kept a one-land hand with your [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s and [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)s and [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s? You might very well get out of this, but I am going to make it expensive for you.*

With [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart)—by its lonesome, or as a target or beneficiary of one of his X-spells—Kurt can pair an elite combat creature with one of his smaller Elf threats, and present an attacker that doesn't roll over to [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) (for all its virtues, Kurt's deck is still small creatures).


None of that, of course, is the single reason we are talking about this deck.


Crane's deck, in addition to being an Elves creature deck, is probably the fastest offensive combo deck in Standard at present. It has all kinds of lines, but its true explosiveness centers around [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) and [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave).

The *other* [Village Bell-Ringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) hooks you up as well, giving you a total of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif). Luckily for you, you still had a [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) in hand, which you can now launch for five. Lands, more Elves (including ones that let you draw a card!), and one or more [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s (to flicker [Village Bell-Ringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer)) untap your [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid)... and because this is the most realistic example we could think of... you have yet another [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave). I have long since lost count of the specifics but certainly you can see that a fair amount of mass can be generated on even the third turn (at least with a little bit of luck).

You need far less luck with a fourth- or fifth-turn kill.


[Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) can get you your [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid). Getting to sufficient mana explosiveness for [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) is basically what this deck does. You pay a huge X. Maybe you draw some cards-cards with [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary). If you stick [Soul of the Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+the+Harvest)—wow! Draw all the cards you want now!


Putting a ton of permanents into play with [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave)*and* drawing cards thanks to [Soul of the Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+the+Harvest) and [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) is what can dig you to your next [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave). [Village Bell-Ringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer)s and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s keep your Archdruid fresh to tap and tap again. All of a sudden you have the better part of your deck in play, with tons of mana and tons of buffing.

You don't even need a huge number of attackers with the right buff. [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) needs a [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) of a certain size, but if you can hit X=8 you are almost certain to enjoy the fruits of many a body, which will result in big pumps. If you have even just one or two additional attackers that can swing in this turn, you might easily be able to put the opponent to -30 or thereabouts in a single swing.


[Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) is flashy... but also a bit expensive, even for an X-deck.


[Ezuri, Renegade Leader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ezuri%2C+Renegade+Leader) needs something different from [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth), but gives you something in return. You can just play the much cheaper Ezuri to defend your creatures from removal spells—even mass removal spells like [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) or [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment)—(as long as you have the mana).


Given sufficiently critical mass, Ezuri is no less lethal than [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) when you have enough mana.


You know how [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) is often game-over? What about if you can cast the [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) not just because you got lucky and topdecked it? If you have Ezuri on the battlefield, every five mana you make is like an over—and over—and over again 'run. Every five mana will make *all* your potential attackers +3/+3.

Now, assuming you have some potential Elves to attack with (and that you got here with one or more big and profitable [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave)s), you might have plenty of Archdruid mana and tons of fodder to help it along.


Big [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)? Rich Archdruid feeding the "other" three-drop Elf lord? You know what is *also* a cool way to turn lots of mana and creatures in play into a win? [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod)! If you have twenty guys out and access to forty mana (not hard with twenty guys out, one of which is an active [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), maybe with a little help from a Bell-Ringers) you can just [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) the opponent out. Your [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) even finds it for you!


Crane finished second with this deck at the Buffalo Open, but when I look at this deck I see much of the same wealth of possibilities that made Wolf Run Ramp a breakout when Brian Sondag won his Open those many months ago. Crane's deck does so many things, and is such a fresh combination of consistency (twelve one-drops, [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)), power, and flexibility that I am sure we are going to see it—or decks descended from it—in the remaining months leading up to *Return to Ravnica*.

"It's just a Planeswalker you can't attack."   
 —Brad Nelson


Brad's deck is tuned to fight a metagame largely defined by playing lots of guys. He has not just [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) but also [Whipflare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) to fight small creatures, and has resurrected cards like [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) to both set up [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura)'s [Royal Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin)-like ability... and protect Gideon from large attackers. Of course, [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) forces opponents to commit extra creatures to the battlefield... which then sets them up for his copious [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment)/[Whipflare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) package.

Like many decks we have seen in recent weeks, Brad played a [Phyrexia's Core](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexia%27s+Core) package with many [Ichor Wellspring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Wellspring)s and [Mycosynth Wellspring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Wellspring)s. [Phyrexia's Core](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexia%27s+Core) has synergy with lots of artifacts, from [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) to [Spine of Ish Sah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spine+of+Ish+Sah), and he can recoup any of them with [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post), going long.


The deck is built on synergy upon synergy, but the iconic one (if we can talk about a one-month-old card as being in some wise *iconic*) is probably [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post) + [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine). You have [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) and—lo and behold, [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) is an *artifact—*you sacrifice it to draw a card, leaving two 3/3 creatures in play. You can later sacrifice one of those creatures to re-buy your [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) from the graveyard, and start the cycle anew! Because [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) essentially has three bodies, even without the extra oomph from drawing a card, you can use one [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) to draw and loop multiple artifacts.

Consider:


* [Spine of Ish Sah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spine+of+Ish+Sah): Destroy that, sacrifice this, destroy that? Absolutely filthy!
* [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver): You need a little mana to pull of the loops in one turn (11+), but if the goal is to take all the opponent's turns, you probably have time.

[Nihil Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) is a cantrip that holds Brad's multicolored deck together, and a key point of defense against popular threats like [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist).


And of course, it is a card-drawing cantrip of an artifact, which can help you to find your [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post) and the lands to get this engine purring.


So... whether they made Top 8, a heartbreaking finals, or not the elimination rounds *yet*, ***Magic** 2013* has already helped to produce several new archetypes with contributors like [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf)... all the way to haymaker flagships like [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post). What new strategy are *you* going to try this weekend?
