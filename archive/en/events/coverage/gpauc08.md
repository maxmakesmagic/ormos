Lo Reaches New Heights in Auckland
==================================




December 6-7












Grand Prix Auckland 2008
------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc08/winner.jpg)


Dominic Lo of Sydney, Australia plowed through the field on day one, and did the same again on day two, finishing at the top of the field going into the playoffs. This morning I speculated that there would be a bloodbath today, with several well-known Pros sitting at X - 1 just waiting to take on the local players. However, when the smoke cleared, only Olivier Ruel remained, and even he was dispatched by Auckland's Nick Tung in the quarterfinals. Lo and Tung squared off in the finals, with Lo's Bant deck finally flying in to win game three and the title of Grand Prix: Auckland Champion 2008.

Sunday, December 07: 6:18p.m. - Top 8 Draft with Olivier Ruel
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

To say that Olivier Ruel is an experienced mage is quite frankly, putting it lightly. He's played in more Grand Prix's than the entire New Zealand population alone. Well, before this weekend, that is.


He tore a [Sarkhan Vol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol) out of his first pack, but passed a [Naya Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Charm), a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl), [Dragon Fodder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder) and an [Executioner's Capsule](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Executioner%27s+Capsule) downstream. He followed that up with a [Rip-Clan Crasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rip-Clan+Crasher), a [Soul's Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Fire) and a 6th pick [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl). By the end of pack one, Ruel seemed quite confident with his Green/Red deck.


Pack two gave him a [Branching Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Branching+Bolt) with a pump of the fist, over a [Stoic Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoic+Angel), a [Woolly Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woolly+Thoctar), a [Mosstodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mosstodon) and a [Jungle Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Weaver). I know some of those aren't anywhere near as good as the Bolt, I only mention it because the only card that made it back to him from that pack was the Weaver. Ruel was far from the only Green drafter at the table. Pick two was a Hissing Iguana, but the third pick had nothing, and Ruel was forced to hate-draft a [Fire-Field Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Field+Ogre). Pick four was also quite underwhelming, a [Gift of the Gargantuan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+the+Gargantuan), followed by a [Rockslide Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental), a [Jungle Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Weaver) and another Gift. Ruel's confidence in his draft was dropping quickly as he thumbed through the playables during the second review period.


The third pack brought Ruel a second [Branching Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Branching+Bolt), an [Algae Gharial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial) and a [Drumhunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drumhunter), but he was also forced to dip into Black for a [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) to go with his [Thorn-Thrash Viashino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn-Thrash+Viashino)s and a [Thunder-Thrash Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunder-Thrash+Elder) he'd picked up earlier.


"My deck is missing one card to be really good, a second [Dragon Fodder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder)," Ruel explained as he built his deck. "I have these devour guys to go with [Sarkhan Vol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol), but I really need another [Dragon Fodder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder) to tie them together. I don't think I can win the whole thing with this, but probably my quarterfinals match at least. The problem with my first pick ([Sarkhan Vol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol)) is that I passed too many good Green from that pack."

Sunday, December 07: 6:37p.m. - Top 8: Nick Tung vs Olivier Ruel
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

"High roll?" Ruel asked, frowning as he rolled a 7 on three die. Tung scored 11 and chose to play


Tung kept and led with a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) into [Necrogenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis), followed by a [Vithian Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vithian+Stinger). Ruel made a Druid of Anima, and fed it to a [Thorn-Thrash Viashino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn-Thrash+Viashino) before the Stinger could have its way with it. Tung made an [Executioner's Capsule](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Executioner%27s+Capsule) and a [Court Archers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Archers), taking 4 from the Thorn-Thrash before Ruel played his [Algae Gharial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial). The [Court Archers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Archers) swung in for 2, and Tung wondered how big that Gharial might get before [Necrogenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis) would run out of steam. The Capsule took out the Thorn-Thrash, and the Gharial came in for 2. Ruel then played his [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) and sent the turn back, his face becoming a regular target for the Stinger. Tung had four colors of mana in play now, and dropped a [Woolly Thoctar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woolly+Thoctar) onto the table.


"That's good, that's good," Ruel muttered under his breath, and ended without play. Tung cycled a [Ridge Rannet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ridge+Rannet) and continued to peck away at his opponent with the Stinger. The Thrinax chumped the Thoctar, and Tung made another Stinger while Ruel finished the Thoctar with a [Branching Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Branching+Bolt). The 4/4 Gharial now swung in unblocked, Tung saving his Saprolings to attack back, knowing that his Stingers would help him win the race. Ruel let it through, dropping to 11 against Tung's 10 life. Tung then played a [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon).


"Yes," was all Ruel could say as he untapped and played a bomb of his own, [Sarkhan Vol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol), which promptly stole the real Dragon of the two. The Dragon and the Gharial attacked Tung down to 6, but Ruel had to let the Dragon return, having nothing he could devour it with. Tung's Dragons took Game 1 on the return. Apparently during the space of that game, Dominic Lo had already dispatched Basam Tabet 2 - 0, and Justin Cheung and Jason Chung were both up a game each.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc08/T8_ruel_tung.jpg)


Ruel threw back his first hand in Game 2, and shook his head unbelievably before sending the next one back as well. He kept his five, and Tung kept his seven. Tung made the first play with a [Cylian Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cylian+Elf), while Ruel faked being short on mana, cycling a [Jungle Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Weaver) before playing his third land. Tung made a [Dregscape Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie) while Ruel summoned a [Court Archers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Archers) to his defense. Tung just made a [Necrogenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis), causing Ruel to sigh, again playing his [Algae Gharial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial). Thanks to the cycled Weaver, Tung got a head start on his Saproling army. The Archers blocked and killed the [Dregscape Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie), while the Elf and the Saproling token dropped Ruel to 15. Ruel made a [Scourge Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+Devil)s and sent in his Gharial for 4. Tung pumped his fist as he drew his next card.


"Cards in hand?"  

"Still two," Ruel replied, staring in disbelief as Tung again summoned his [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon).  

"Topdeck," Tung explained, as if the fist pump hadn't given it away.


Ruel again made his [Sarkhan Vol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol), but this time increased the loyalty count to send his [Scourge Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+Devil)s in for 5. A Saproling chumped, and then Tung Bone Splintered the Archers, his Dragons dropping Ruel from 15 to 7. Ruel untapped, and stole the Dragon Token with [Sarkhan Vol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarkhan+Vol), and attacked back with his 5/5 Gharial, the Devils and his misappropriated Dragon. Saprolings calmly chumped the ground pounders, and Ruel then devoured the Token with this [Thunder-Thrash Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunder-Thrash+Elder). Nick Tung defeats Olivier Ruel 2 - 0 and no more than a minute later, Justin Cheung finished off Chris Hay 2 - 0 and Jason Chung rounded out the Quarters, beating Joe Combs 2 - 0.

Sunday, December 07: 8:03p.m. - Finals: Nick Tung vs Dominic Lo
---------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc08/finals_lo.jpg)


It comes down to this. Dominic Lo kept the rest of the field at his feet all weekend. Undefeated day one and sitting at the top of the standings going into the top 8. Nick Tung was playing catch up, having to win the last round to make the playoffs, but catch up he has. Crushing Olivier Ruel in the quarterfinals and Justin Cheung in the semis. Lo currently hails from Sydney, Australia, but has lived in Wellington, New Zealand from time to time. Tung, despite claiming on his Top 8 profile that he was from Taipei, has lived in Auckland for years.


Lo won the roll, and chose to draw, making the first play of the finals, a turn two [Knight of the Skyward Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Skyward+Eye), to which Tung replied with a [Cylian Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cylian+Elf) on turn three. [Dawnray Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawnray+Archer)s allowed the Knight to swing past, but couldn't stop the Elf returning the favor before being joined by a [Vithian Stinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vithian+Stinger). Lo drew his card and sank into thought. He sent the Knight in for another 3 and played a [Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk), leaving the Archer to die to the Stinger. Tung then sent the Elf in, representing a trick, which Lo respected, letting it through. Tung then fed the Elf to a [Thorn-Thrash Viashino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn-Thrash+Viashino). Lo made a [Rhox Charger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Charger), and the War Monk dropped Tung to 10 and returned Lo to 20. Tung returned fire with his Thorn-Thrash, shot the Charger and then cleared it, the Stinger and the Knight out with a [Jund Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm). Only the Thorn-Thrash and the War Monk remained. Lo attacked Tung down to 7 and refilled his hand with a [Courier's Capsule](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courier%27s+Capsule), while Tung finally took out Lo's last attacker with an [Executioner's Capsule](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Executioner%27s+Capsule). Lo replaced it with a [Cloudheath Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudheath+Drake), and then dropped to 11 from the Thorn-Thrash. Lo teamed a Guardian's of Akrasa with the Drake to drop Tung to 3, then played a [Jhessian Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhessian+Infiltrator) and an [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) on the Viashino. It was definitely a good [Courier's Capsule](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courier%27s+Capsule), and Tung agreed, reaching for his sideboard.


Lo 1 - Tung 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc08/finals_tung.jpg)


Tung elected to play, while Lo took a mulligan, settling on his six. Tung opened hostilities with a turn three [Topan Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Topan+Ascetic) off of [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). Lo's third land came into play tapped, so he was still without play when Tung dropped a [Jund Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Battlemage). Lo rallied with a [Rhox Charger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Charger), but the Battlemage made a sacrificial Saproling that powered out a [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) to remove the blocker. Lo replaced it up with a [Dawnray Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawnray+Archer) and a [Steward of Valeron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steward+of+Valeron). Tung swung in again, dropping Lo to 11 before playing a [Goblin Deathraiders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders). Lo finally attacked back for 3 with the Steward, but had no play with his six mana and three cards in hand. Tung again sent in his [Topan Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Topan+Ascetic), the [Jund Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Battlemage) powering out critters to let the Human Monk land for 5. After combat, Tung made a [Thorn-Thrash Viashino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn-Thrash+Viashino), sacrificing a single Saproling only, in case Lo had something to bounce it back. When Lo's draw revealed no answers, he picked up his cards and said, "let's go to the next game."


Lo 1 - Tung 1


For the decider, Lo again chose to draw, with both players keeping their hands this time. Lo cantrip'd through his second turn with an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary), while Tung found a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) with his [Naya Panorama](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Panorama), playing his trusty [Topan Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Topan+Ascetic) on turn three. Lo sent his Visionary in at the Ascetic, but Tung let it through. Lo then played a [Naya Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Battlemage). Tung swung back, holding a Hissing Iguana in reserve. Lo played a [Guardians of Akrasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardians+of+Akrasa) and sent his Elf in for 2, with White mana up for the Battlemage to tap down one of Tung's men. Settling in for the long game, Tung made a [Jund Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Battlemage), and attacked his Iguana into Lo's wall, but a [Resounding Roar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar) ensured the combat step had only one survivor. Lo simply upgraded his position with a [Rhox Charger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Charger) and sent the Elf in for 2 again. The [Naya Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Battlemage) tapped down the Ascetic, but the Charger wasn't feeling up to blocking the Iguana, and Lo dropped to 14. Tung then played [Necrogenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis) and passed it back.


"Cards in hand one?" Lo confirmed while he pondered his options before sending in the Charger for 4, taking Tung down to 11 and playing a [Cloudheath Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudheath+Drake). Meanwhile, the [Necrogenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis) and the [Jund Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Battlemage) were getting a posse together. Tung swung in with his [Topan Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Topan+Ascetic) while Lo was tapped low, beefing it up with his Iguana and Battlemage. Lo fell from 14 to 10, and Tung tapped out himself for a [Ridge Rannet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ridge+Rannet), his army practically spanning the table. Lo lapsed into thought, before playing a [Dawnray Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawnray+Archer) and sending the Drake in for 5, dropping Tung to 6. When Tung went to attack back, the [Naya Battlemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Battlemage) tapped down the 6/4, and it was Tung's turn to go into the think tank. Trying to figure out how many guys to send in, how many to pump the Ascetic with and how many to try and defend with, eventually deciding to send in a pair of Saprolings alongside the 3/3 Ascetic. The Charger ate a Saproling while in turn, the Ascetic ate the [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary). Tung had one card in hand to Lo's four, Tung on 6 life and Lo on 7. Lo untapped and played an [Akrasan Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire), and entered his attack step.


"Can I attack?"  

"Yeah," Tung replied.


Lo turned his Drake sideways, three exalted triggers went on the stack and Tung simply offered his hand in congratulations with a smile.


Dominic Lo defeats Nick Tung 2 - 1
