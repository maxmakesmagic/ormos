States of Rogue
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 28, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar











Welcome to Rogue Week! It's time to take a look at the crook, the thief, his lowlife, and their robbers. And some proper Rogues as well. First, a fascinating piece of trivia: Did you know that the word "rogue" [does not appear in the flavour text](/en/articles/archive/fascinating-piece-trivia-2008-02-27) of a single **Magic** card? It also appears in the name of [Rogue Elephant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Rogue%2BElephant) as well as every article title this week. Sadly, the word is also missing from each of the English-to-Pun dictionaries that I use to write my column, so all of you pun enthusiasts will have to go without. Pun haters rejoice.

Enough of my long-winded preamble. If there's a place for Rogue decks, it's this place. Yep, the Internet. From what I [Gatherer](http://ww2.wizards.com/gatherer/index.aspx?), Rogues are a big part of *Morningtide*. I'm always up for some tribal shenanigans, so let's see what do with the latest batch of knaves, rapscallions, scamps, scoundrels, ne'er-do-wells, and other nouns stolen from thesaurus.com.

### Keeping Your Witsniper About You

If you read Kenneth Nagle's [excellent feature article](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/443) a few weeks ago, you are familiar with the merrowing tale of a certain Merfolk Rogue, [Merrow Witsniper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Witsniper). One-mana 1/1s with comes-into-play abilities are pretty rare (though usually common). As I've mentioned before in this column (and to anyone who'll listen, really), I'm a big fan of [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr), the [Fugitive Wizard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fugitive+Wizard) / [Index](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Index) mash-up that Aaron Forsythe dubbed "an endearing little man." I probably like these cards because they "combo" with some of my other favourite cards, like [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours) and [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy). Don't misunderestimate their power, either. One of the most legendary plays that occurred in my gaming group involved a lowly [Maggot Carrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maggot+Carrier) that killed not one, but two players at the same time! It would've been three, but the fourth player had already been the recipient of an unmerciful [Ember Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Shot) smackdown. I think. My memory is a little fuzzy or I've blocked out the entire incident.

![Merrow Witsniper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Merrow+Witsniper)What [Merrow Witsniper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Witsniper) does is mill somebody for one. The ability is not that exciting, really, at least if you just run him out there. You'd need to have four [Dual Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dual+Nature)s and two [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season)s in play before you'd surpass the milling power of [Glimpse the Unthinkable](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+the+Unthinkable), which costs only one mana more. The Witsniper gets much more exciting after someone plays one of the *Lorwyn* Harbingers, a [Personal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Personal+Tutor), a [Congregation at Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Congregation+at+Dawn), or some other spell that manipulates the top of the library. Bye-bye, powerful card of some description! Foiled by a glorified [Merfolk of the Pearl Trident](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+of+the+Pearl+Trident)! Besides this kind of purely reactive use, [Merrow Witsniper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Witsniper) can team up with a number of other cards to produce (slightly) more powerful effects. For instance, new evoke Elemental [Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker) lets you put a nonland permanent on top of its owner's library when it leaves play. Combine it with our endearing little merman and you have a mono-blue method to effectively destroy a creature, enchantment, artifact, or even planeswalker. You just have to make a quick stop at the library first. There are plenty of other cards that allow you to set up the top of your opponent's library. Two that sprang to mind, for whatever reason, are *Future Sight*'s [Lost Hours](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+Hours) and *Tenth Edition*'s [Agonizing Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agonizing+Memories). The former lets you take a card from your opponent's hand and put it three cards from the top of their library. The latter lets you take two cards from their hand and put them directly on top. A well-timed [Merrow Witsniper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Witsniper) or two will let you get rid of those cards forever!Now, I know what you're thinking: "A two-card combo that makes your opponent discard one card of his or her choice? Seems like a long way to go, especially when you've also got a card like [Noggin Whack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noggin+Whack) in the format." And you'd probably be right. However, I also wanted to use some of the other library-eating Rogues from *Morningtide*, like [Grimoire Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimoire+Thief) and the new [Jester's Cap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jester%27s+Cap)[Mass of Ghouls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mass%2Bof%2BGhouls), [Earwig Squad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earwig+Squad). By tapping, the Thief will remove from the game all the cards you put on top with either [Lost Hours](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+Hours), [Agonizing Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agonizing+Memories), and [Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker). Cards in the library are also preferable when you play [Earwig Squad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earwig+Squad), since it prevents the possibility of recursion. The [Earwig Squad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earwig+Squad)'s shuffle effect (post-[Agonizing Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agonizing+Memories), say), can also muck up your opponent's plans. The last but not least reason for these quasi-discard effects is Rogue sorcery [Knowledge Exploitation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knowledge+Exploitation), a kind of [Bribery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bribery) for spells.

[Vedalken Mastermind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Mastermind) lets you reuse your Witsnipers, [Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker)s, [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite)s, [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)s, and [Earwig Squad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earwig+Squad)s, while [Looter *il*-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor)'s near-unblockability helps to ensure that your prowl spells will always be at their cheapest. Here's where I ended up:

Of course, there are many, many places you can take such a deck. All you need are creatures with "comes into play" abilities and some way to reuse them. In this case, I avoided combining [Vedalken Mastermind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Mastermind) with too many evoke Elementals (Ben Bleiweiss already did a fine job [exploring this realm](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/jokes-still-you-2007-11-21)). [Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker) was a key to my deck, however, and [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) was too hard to resist. [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) is obviously excellent, as are [Aethersnipe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Aethersnipe) and, somewhat surprisingly, [Mournwhelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk).

Two Rogues that I really wanted to squeeze in the deck were [Marsh Flitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flitter) and [Highway Robber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Highway+Robber) (yup, it's a Rogue now). Both of them have comes-into-play abilities that can turn the tide of the game and, if left unfettered, bring you victory, slowly but surely. As always, there is much too little space. That's why Highlander / Singleton is such a great format.

I used [Vedalken Mastermind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Mastermind) as my bounce mechanism, but you could certainly try something else. Glen or [Glen Elendra Pranksters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Pranksters) could form the backbone of a fun deck, with [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), [Faerie Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Harbinger), and [Dewdrop Spy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dewdrop+Spy) filling in the ranks. The Spy's ability to play peek-a-boo with the top of your opponent's library makes it a natural pair with [Merrow Witsniper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Witsniper). Outside of Standard, you've got [Wizened Snitches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wizened+Snitches), an older Faerie Rogue that performs the same function (as well as ensuring that you will always be able to win your clashes, should you decide to include such cards).

The aforementioned [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) and [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours) are other ways to bounce your 'Snipers. [Crystal Shard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Shard) and [Man-o'-War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Man-o%27-War) are other personal favourites that also accomplish this goal. [Rootwater Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwater+Thief), meanwhile, is another Rogue that helps dismantle your opponent's library and could also find a place in a deck with [Grimoire Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimoire+Thief) and company.

### Two Throngs Don't Make a Right

![Notorious Throng](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Notorious+Throng)Before I discovered (by reading the card!) that [Notorious Throng](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notorious+Throng) makes black Faerie Rogue creature tokens and not blue ones, I cooked up this deck using one of my favourite ingredients: [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power). It does two of my favourite things. One, it provides you with a ridiculous amount of mana, which is always a good thing to have, especially if you're a Johnny. And two, it pumps up your creatures (usually), which is also nice, especially if you're a Timmy. Unfortunately, you can't use a Gauntlet to both double your blue mana and pump up black creatures at the same time. That would take two separate Gauntlets or a deck that just splashed for [Notorious Throng](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notorious+Throng). Of course, if you have a Gauntlet set to blue in play, and can hit your opponent with a [Mistform Skyreaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Skyreaver) (Hint: Turn it into a Rogue!), the subsequent [Notorious Throng](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notorious+Throng) will almost surely be game ending. As far as I know, [Mistform Skyreaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Skyreaver) is the largest creature with evasion that is a Rogue or can easily be turned into one. The other way to make huge Rogues is the mana-intensive [Riptide Replicator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Replicator), which fits right in with [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power).

### Getting a Handle on the Vandals

![Starke of Rath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Starke+of+Rath)As Doug Beyer mentioned yesterday, the Rogue creature type didn't even exist until *Mirrodin*, when [Neurok Spy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy) put them on the **Magic** map. Where on the **Magic** map? If I'm not mistaken, they're located in a little village on the outskirts of Beebleton. All I know is that until recently, Rogues had a thing against shiny baubles, and, to a lesser extent, beebles. I'm not sure when the Rogues' ancient grudge against artifacts first took hold, but it's undeniably there. Of course, most of the Rogues I'm referring to have only been given that creature type during the recent Grand Creature Type Update. Artifact-hating cards like [Goblin Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Vandal), [Joven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joven), [Chandler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandler), [Aladdin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aladdin), and [Starke of Rath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starke+of+Rath) have all been retrofitted with the mark of the mountebank. Apparently, one-time Townsfolk [Keldon Vandals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Vandals) moved to the big city to take on larger-scale graffiti projects, play more competitive games of mailbox baseball, and take part in other forms of scalawaggery.The nucleus of this deck comes from one of my favourite reader rogues, Christian M. of Denmark. I did my best to random it up. He writes:


> 
> The deck wants to use cards like [Thran Forge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Forge) and [Mycosynth Lattice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Lattice) to turn the opponents' permanents into artifacts while the 3x3 Rogues (3x [Aladdin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aladdin), 3x [Joven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joven), and 3x [Chandler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandler)) want to wreck said permanents. [Memnarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnarch) serves double duty here, both making artifacts but also taking control of them.
> 
> 
> 

I made some changes, mostly to fit in more Rogues. The 3x3 Rogue matrix became a 2x4 matrix, as those three were joined by [Starke of Rath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starke+of+Rath). I also made room for the Vandals from above, as well as [Looter *il*-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor) and [Neurok Spy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy). I added some [Crystal Shard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Shard)s for their synergy with [Keldon Vandals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Vandals) and [Starke of Rath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starke+of+Rath) (activate his ability and then bounce him for repeatable artifact and creature kill).

Of course, you can use other, non-Rogue artifact kill like Christian did. [Viashino Heretic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Heretic) is pretty amazing with [Mycosynth Lattice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Lattice), as are [Shattering Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Pulse) and [Shattering Spree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Spree). For a more in-depth look at Lattice-Spree, check out Ben Bleiweiss's Grand Spree article [here](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb61).
