Thailand RPTQ Location Change
=============================


1. Recently, Thailand's King Bhumibol Adulyadej passed away, leading to a nationwide mourning that is expected to last twelve months, restricting all entertainment activities. As a result, the RPTQ that was scheduled for March 2017 has been moved to Grey Ogre Games in Singapore. For the updated schedule, please refer to the [Regional Pro Tour Qualifiers for Pro Tour *Amonkhet*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/instoreplay/rptqakh) event information page.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Red-Green Energy](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2016-10-19)

There are a number of ways to build red-green-based energy decks. There are the ones that go for individual card quality over speed, usually eschewing too many pump spells. Then there are the ones that look to kill quickly and sometimes in a combo-esque fashion, usually combining [Electrostatic Pummeler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Pummeler) plus an array of pump spells. Both have plenty going on for them, but today we're going to look at the former.


This list, from a 5-0 Competitive League performance by billster47, still plays plenty of energy-centric cards—[Longtusk Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Longtusk+Cub), [Lathnu Hellion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lathnu+Hellion), [Bristling Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bristling+Hydra), [Voltaic Brawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Brawler), and [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning)—but skips too many pump spells and instead battles with cards like [Chandra, Torch of Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance), [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker), and a few Vehicles.


This approach will likely become more popular as Jeskai Control and Grixis Control—decks with plenty of removal—are everywhere right now. That makes going all in on something that can be removed by two- and three-mana removal spells a dubious proposition. Note this deck does keep one pump spell, [Blossoming Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Defense), which acts more like a counterspell for removal than a pump spell much of the time.
