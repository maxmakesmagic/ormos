What People Are Talking About
=============================


1. > 
> I am so extremely honored to announce that my piece Guardian Automaton has been nominated for a Chesley Award. [#mtg](https://twitter.com/hashtag/mtg?src=hash) [pic.twitter.com/Dz5GPbCwDH](https://t.co/Dz5GPbCwDH)
> 
> 
>  — Vincent Proce (@vproceart) [June 7, 2016](https://twitter.com/vproceart/status/740174767521685504)

The Chesley Award—yes, Chesley, that isn't a typo—is one of the most prestigious awards science fiction and fantasy artists can receive. Over the years, [a number of *Magic* artists and art pieces have won or been nominated](http://originalmagicart.com/artists/chesley-awards/) for the award bestowed by the [Association of Science Fiction & Fantasy Artists](http://asfa-art.org/). You can see [all of the nominees here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1hlJ_Gl1vm2SJrq5B_HzS66kTdzI7YLaZ-eVxRagx6Nw/edit), but specifically the *Magic* ones (all in the Best Gaming-Related Illustration category) include:


* Clint Cearley for [*Dragons of Tarkir* Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=394623)
* Vincent Proce for [Guardian Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Automaton)
* Anna Steinbauer for [Blessed Spirits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Spirits)
* Ryan Yee for [Fruit of the First Tree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fruit+of+the+First+Tree)
* Min Yum for [Sandblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sandblast)

Yes, *Magic* cards have a clean sweep of nominations for that category. Additionally, Wizard's own Jeremy Jarvis was nominated for Best Art Director.


So, yeah, it's kind of a big deal.

He's our kind of people, and we're excited to have him.


After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [From the Spice Cabinet—The Participation Trophy](http://www.hipstersofthecoast.com/2016/06/spice-cabinet-participation-trophy/) | Hipsters of the Coast | Jerry Mee

All right, Jerry Mee, you got me. I laughed. I read on. I laughed some more. In this article, Mee creates a Legacy deck whose only goal is to make sure everyone wins because no one loses—because the games all end in a draw. I don't know Mr. Mee, but I envision someone cackling with glee every time they activate [Helm of Obedience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helm+of+Obedience) targeting someone enchanted by [Wheel of Sun and Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Sun+and+Moon). And I dig it.


1. [Blue Moon Deck Guide](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/blue-moon-deck-guide/) | ChannelFireball | Paul Rietzl

A thoroughly thorough guide to Blue Moon in Modern for all of the fun-haters who just want to see people struggle to cast spells. You know who you are.


1. [*Eldritch Moon*: Set Symbol Mystery](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p7Xuoq6QRjY) | Seibenlore

Wait, we forgot to reveal the *Eldritch Moon* set symbol?! What?! Shoot, I've gotta go talk to some people.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Modern Allies](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=103773)

The [Top 8](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/deckshow.php?event_ID=36&t%5bT2%5d=28&start_date=2016-06-05&end_date=2016-06-05&state=GA&city=Atlanta&order_1=finish&limit=8&t_num=1&action=Show+Decks) of the StarCityGames.com Atlanta Modern Classic was basically a trip through Zendikar. Two [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) decks took us on trips to [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle), Bant Eldrazi took us through the Eldrazi portions of Zendikar, Abzan Aristocrats brought along a [Zulaport Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zulaport+Cutthroat), and Merfolk spread some seas—plus, there are Merfolk on Zendikar, right?


But no deck was more Zendikarian (no clue if that's the correct way to write that) than Joe Ambrosio's Modern Allies deck, which is about eight spells and ten lands away from being a *Zendikar*-plus-*Battle for Zendikar* theme deck. Featuring 30 creatures, all of which are Allies (including honorary Ally [Mirror Entity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity)), the deck is a blazingly fast creature deck where basically every creature is a lord.


Held together by [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) and [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company)—the two pillars of Modern aggressive creature decks—the deck is brutally fast and single-minded. The power of Ally triggers means it can do quite a bit with a single spell, and tides can turn in the blink of an [Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin).
