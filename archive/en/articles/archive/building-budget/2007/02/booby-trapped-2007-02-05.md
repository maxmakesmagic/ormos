Booby Trapped!
==============



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on February 5, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss

Around the time of the Constructed State Championships last year, [Bennie Smith](http://www.wizards.com/default.asp?x=mtgcom/authorarchive&author=BennieSmith) (who currently has a weekly column at [StarCityGames.com](http://www.starcitygames.com/pages/articlefinder.php?keyword=bennie+smith) on Mondays) IMed me with much excitement in his digital voice.


“I am very excited!” said Bennie Smith, in a sentence I don’t think Bennie’s actually ever IMed me. “I have this totally awesome new deck and I have completely broken this Time Spiral rare in half!” he emphasized, using none of those words.


“Ok, Bennie, I’ll bite. What’s the deck?”


 



![Mishra, Artificer Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mishra%2C+Artificer+Prodigy)

His answer? [Mishra, Artificer Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%2C+Artificer+Prodigy) combined with [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap).

I’ll admit I was intrigued and repulsed at the same time. This seemed like the king of rickety-jank combos on one hand, but on the other hand it was twenty to the dome, and Mishra himself is a hefty 4/4 beater for four – hardly useless without the other half of the combo. Bennie gave me the outline for the deck:


Step One: Play [Mishra, Artificer Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%2C+Artificer+Prodigy)  

Step Two: Play [Mishra’s Bauble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%E2%80%99s+Bauble), grabbing a second copy.  

Step Three. See the top card of their deck. If it’s a basic land, wait a turn, and use the second Bauble.  

Step Four: If it’s not a basic land on the top of their deck, double [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) their deck (naming that card), and inflict twenty points of crushing pain during their next draw.  

Step Five: Profit.  
  

Yes, Bennie’s idea was so mind-blowing that profiting was moved down two steps. Scandalous!


Imagine my surprise when Bennie shows up to States and is not running his fabled [Mishra, Artificer Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mishra%252C%2BArtificer%2BProdigy)/[Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) deck, but some horrible green/blue or green/white or green/green deck that went 0-20 and lost to several draft decks that were plucked from the side event area. I never heard another peep about this deck, until I decided that, in lieu of Bennie ever making the deck for reals, I would be able to take the idea and see how the deck would look on a budget.

The core of the deck remained intact from its original concept, which can best be explained by this mathematical equation:


[(1MAP) + (2MB)] \* (2BT) = 20


Now came the tough part of building the deck. There’s an intrinsic problem that must be solved when building a deck around [Mishra, Artificer Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%2C+Artificer+Prodigy) – you want to run a lot of artifacts to take advantage of his ability, but you also need to support three colors of mana by turn four to get him out in the first place!


My original build of Booby Trapped! focused almost exclusively on getting the [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) to go off. [Chromatic Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star) is there to smooth the mana and to act as a card-drawing spell, especially when combined with Mishra. [Coldsteel Heart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coldsteel+Heart) is also in the deck to fix the mana, as well as to accelerate to a potential third-turn Mishra. My lands were a pretty even spread across blue, red and black, with the *Ravnica* bounce-lands liberally sprinkled throughout to allow a better chance of getting all three colors at once, early.


 



![Booby Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Booby+Trap)

[Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows) was the only other artifact in my first build, as it’s the most efficient creature-removal artifact in Standard right now. It’s also a card that isn’t redundant with Mishra – against decks where you want [Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows), you would not mind drawing two at the same time. This is not so much the case with, say, [Aladdin’s Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aladdin%E2%80%99s+Ring).

The rest of the original deck focused on returning permanents to the top of my opponent’s deck. The three cards chosen for this were [Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy) (which can hit both lands and creatures), [Time Ebb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Ebb), and [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser). It’s important to note that [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) can trigger off of any nonbasic land. There are times when you’ll want to [Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy) a bounce land or a pain land, and then name that with your [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap).


The natural enemy of this deck? Dredgers. Luckily, I didn’t face one all week!

**Game 1**: Pure Grit (Mono-B Gravepact)  

He gets an early [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor). I [Time Ebb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Ebb) it, then play [Mishra, Artificer Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%2C+Artificer+Prodigy). Mishra gets double [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) (I blindly name [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor), since I’ve already seen one), and then swings in for eight over two turns. He plays [Grave Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Pact), and I answer with [Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows). He trumps with [Nantuko Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk), killing my Mishra by sacrificing his [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor). I kill the Husk with my double Arrows, and he plays [Sengir Autocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Autocrat).


I [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser) his Autocrat, removing the tokens from the game. (They do not go to the graveyard in this case, because [Sengir Autocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Autocrat) specifically says to remove the tokens from the game. Normally, if a token takes lethal damage or dies, it counts as hitting the graveyard for effects like Grave Pact.) He replays the [Autocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Autocrat), and I [Time Ebb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Ebb) it, swinging in for two. He plays it a third time, and I drop [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap), naming [Sengir Autocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Autocrat). He drops a second Autocrat (which was already in his hand). Just as the board locks up, he draws a [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) and takes twenty damage, ending the game.  

Record: 1-0



![Mishras Bauble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mishras+Bauble)

**Game 2**: Davnel (W/B Orzhov Control)  

He gets stuck on mana, and I get down Mishra, [Mishra’s Bauble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%E2%80%99s+Bauble+)  (drawing two cards), Chromatic Sphere (drawing more two cards), and then [Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows) (killing his Thoughtpicker Witch). He casts [Pillory of the Sleepless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillory+of+the+Sleepless) on Mishra, but I draw my third Bauble (the first ones turned up land – frown!), see [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify), and then drop double [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap), killing him.  

Record: 2-0
A Memorable Quote From This Game:  

(After I play the [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap)s)  

Davnel: “I wonder what my next card is?”  

Me: “I’ll give you two guesses, and your first one doesn’t count!”


**Game 3**: Blazing\_Archon ([Bond of Agony](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bond+of+Agony)/[Lifegain Deck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifegain+Deck))  

Well, I find one weakness of my deck: concentrated lifegain. Blazing\_Archon proceeds to slap me around with Faith’s Fetters, Lightning Helix, and other lifegaining spells, putting him wellllllllll out of [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) range. In fact, he’s out of quadruple [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) range by the time he plays back-to-back [Bond of Agony](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bond+of+Agony) to kill me.  

Record: 2-1


**Game 4**: Full Collapse (U/B Dimir Mill)  

He plays [Glimpse the Unthinkable](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+the+Unthinkable) four times, [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)s and bounces my [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap)s off the board several times, and uses Mystical Teachings to draw several control and Counterspells. I start attacking his mana with [Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy) and name Watery Grave with double [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap). He shuffles his library during his upkeep with [Mystical Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings), keeping himself away from death. I have three cards left in my library when I drop a third [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap), naming [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal). He draws [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) the following turn and dies (he was below 10 from Mishra).  

Record: 3-1


**Game 5**: KingofOBX (W/U/G Selesnya)  

He gets [Juniper Order Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juniper+Order+Ranger) and a bunch of green/white guys, then flips his [Thelonite Hermit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thelonite+Hermit), making his Ranger huge. I get a third-turn Mishra, [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) him for [Juniper Order Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juniper+Order+Ranger) on turn five, chump block his 10/12 monster, and [Time Ebb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Ebb) it for the win. Yays!  

Record: 4-1



![Juniper Order Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Juniper+Order+Ranger)

So far, I don’t have a lot of confidence in the deck. Sure, it’s 4-1, but half of the games have come down to last-minute heroics (or luck), and any sort of lifegain seems to rock my world. I want to add in some more ways to deal damage (so that my deck isn’t completely reliant on [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) to win), and I also have been drawing too many lands each game.

In short, I take out the Dismissers, as they seem to be overkill against many decks. If a deck is already running short on creatures, [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser) is a dead card in hand (since it’ll only return itself), and a 6-mana dead card at that. I also take out one [Time Ebb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Ebb) (haven’t needed it as much as Eddy, which can set up the nonbasic land/[Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) combo), an Island, a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), and a [Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows).


I still want mana acceleration in the deck, so I swap the [Coldsteel Hearts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coldsteel+Hearts) straight for [Phyrexian Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem). I only need to hit twice with a Totem to get an opponent into single [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) range, plus I can fetch out double Totem with Mishra in the later game. [Coldsteel Heart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coldsteel+Heart) past turn five is generally useless.


I also decide to go with a mini-discard theme. I’ve been itching to try out [Blizzard Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blizzard+Specter) from *Coldsnap* for a while now, and I figure that I can combine it with [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) (which Mishra can also grab two of) to have a third (and fourth) source of damage.

**Game 11**: Ivan912 (Mono-Red Burn)  

I get burnt and [Stuffy Dolled](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Dolled) to death while drawing a million lands. I am helpless against an onslaught of [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer), [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock), [Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast) (which deals six to me with [Stuffy Doll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll) out), [Browbeat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat), and other burn spells.  

Record: 7-4


Well, I drew [Blizzard Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blizzard+Specter) and triple [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) this game, and they were pretty useless. The Specter immediately died, and the [The Racks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Racks) (and yes, it’s the [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack). Just like that other set is the *The Dark*!), did diddly and squat to help my board position.


This was definitely a case of me losing sight of the goals of the deck. This isn’t a [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) deck with a discard subtheme. This is a [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) deck with a [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) theme.


Months ago, I wrote my [five rules of deckbuilding](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/deckbuilding-101-five-tips-better-deckbuilding-2006-12-18). People often misinterpret rule #3, and this is a perfect time to clarify it.


Rule #3: Focus Your Goals, but Don’t Be a Slave to your Theme.


In the first build of the deck, I was a slave to my theme. The theme was [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) plus [Mishra, Artificer Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%2C+Artificer+Prodigy). This is not a problem – these two cards make the deck work. However, I threw in literally every “put that card on top of your deck from play” card in blue, and I found them to be redundant, and useless against decks that were creature light. This was being a slave to my theme.


In the second revision, I didn’t focus my goals. Instead of focusing on getting Mishra plus [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) each game, I diversified into a discard theme, which had nothing to do with Mishra at all. These cards weakened the deck, as they functioned independently of the other 52 cards in my deck. Yes, there are decks that want a diversity of threats. Combo decks are not one of them – they want to get their combo, as often as possible.


To get back on track, I took out the [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack)s and the [Blizzard Specters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blizzard+Specters), and added in four [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze+)  and three Compulsive Research. The Researches are just good card-drawing spells, and the [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze)s are cantrip creature removal, which buys me time (and digs me through my deck) against early threats. They also combo really well with [Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows).

Definitely a fun deck to try out, but I wouldn’t recommend it for long-term use. It’s a pretty one-trick pony, and while that trick is initially very impressive (BOOM! TAKE TWENTY!), it could get repetitive after a while. Unlike my Exploding Wurms! deck (which could win through [Undying Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undying+Flames) or through reanimation), this deck either wins by [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap), or it doesn’t.


But still, you get to win with [Booby Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Booby+Trap) and [Tunnel Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tunnel+Vision) – what more could you want?
