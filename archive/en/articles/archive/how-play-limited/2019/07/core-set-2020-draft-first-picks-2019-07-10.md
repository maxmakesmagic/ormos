Core Set 2020 Draft First Picks
===============================



 Posted in **How to Play Limited**
 on July 10, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 






I've been playing a ton with *Core Set 2020* since its release on *MTG Arena*, and this set is a blast for both Sealed and Draft. It's got a fantastic mix of sweet themes, resonant cards, and good gameplay, so I'd definitely recommend going out and trying it for yourself. To that end, I have a Pack 1, Pick 1 article for you, and we are going to go over all the categories of cards you could open in your first pack. Let's dive in!

If you are lucky enough to see one of these cards, you should take it and do what you can to end up playing it.

These cards don't have the raw power level of the tippity-top cards or are a little more expensive to cast, but they will win you games and are definitely good early picks.

While all these cards are perfectly playable, they aren't where I'd like to start my draft. This is a mix of solid commons, sideboard cards, and more situational/synergistic options. A card like [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) can vastly overperform, but you need sacrifice fodder to make that happen, so I don't like taking it early. If you open and take one of these cards first pick, you are starting a little behind, but you can still certainly end up with a great deck.
