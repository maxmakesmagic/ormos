Goblin Burn
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 1, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






Wizards of the Coast R&D members were out in full force at stores around the United States to celebrate the *Avacyn Restored* Prerelease, and few people were more excited to have the chance to play with *Avacyn Restored* than the set's lead developer, [Dave Humpherys](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Dave%20Humpherys)! Dave took some time out of his busy schedule to stop by Seattle store [Card Kingdom](http://cardkingdom.com/) and play some games. One of the Standard decks he took with him? Goblin Burn!

This red deck features some elements you might expect out of a burn-heavy deck. [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), [Shrine of Burning Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Burning+Rage), and [Spikeshot Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Elder) are all proven staples. Perhaps even [Goblin Arsonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Arsonist) and [Goblin Fireslinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Fireslinger) to complement a full set of [Goblin Grenade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Grenade) isn't too out of place. But some new cards from *Avacyn Restored* put a unique, fun twist on the deck!

One mana for 5 damage is a burn deck's dream, and [Thunderous Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderous+Wrath) offers exactly that if you miracle it. Six mana can be quite unwieldy if one starts in your opening hand—but [Tibalt, the Fiend-Blooded](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tibalt%2C+the+Fiend-Blooded) and [Reforge the Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reforge+the+Soul) help out with that, giving you a chance of replacing an unwieldy miracle with some burn to finish off the opponent!

It looks like burn might have been given some fresh legs with *Avacyn Restored*! Check out David's deck below, and stay tuned all week for more R&D decklists featuring *Avacyn Restored*!
