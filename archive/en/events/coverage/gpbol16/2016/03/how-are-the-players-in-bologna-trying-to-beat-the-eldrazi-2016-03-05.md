How are the players in Bologna trying to beat the Eldrazi?
==========================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on March 5, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











Eldrazi is the consensus best and most-played deck in Modern right now, but everyone else also has this knowledge. As a result, there is an opportunity to exploit the field with a strategy tweaked against the Eldrazi menace. Of course, you could go for the tuned-for-the-mirror blue-white Eldrazi (whose [Eldrazi Displacer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Displacer) and [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) give an advantage against opposing [Endless One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+One) and [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher)) but that's not particularly innovative. Instead, I sought out several Gold pros and other accomplished players who registered different archetypes. 


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gpbol16/gpbol16_eldrazi_cammilluzzi.jpg)  
*Marco Cammilluzzi, captain of the Italian team that won the 2015 World* Magic Cup

Marco Cammilluzzi showed up with an innovative Bant Company deck capable of awesome plays against Eldrazi. How does [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) into [Intrepid Hero](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intrepid+Hero) and [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) sound? Copy their [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher), then shoot it down! Or a Company into [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) plus [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage)? Bounce their [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) to draw a card, and then name it so they can't replay it!


"Two weeks before the tournament, I started thinking about a deck that could defeat Eldrazi," Cammilluzzi told me. "Affinity seemed like a good idea, but I never played it and I think you need a lot of practice to play it well. So I looked further and found [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) as a huge creature that is definitely good against them, [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) as an incredible answer to [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) and [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher), and [Intrepid Hero](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intrepid+Hero) to shoot down all big Eldrazi. [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) ties it all together. I played lot of games, and think I have a good matchup against Abzan Company and Eldrazi."

Martin Jůza registered Bogles, an archetype that he praised in his article on ChannelFireball earlier this week. This deck tries to go over the top of Eldrazi: If you thought that an 8/8 [Endless One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+One) was huge, you've never seen a [Slippery Bogle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slippery+Bogle) with [Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor), [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), [Daybreak Coronet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daybreak+Coronet), [Unflinching Courage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage), and [Hyena Umbra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hyena+Umbra)!


"It just crushes the Eldrazi decks," Jůza said. "They have a bunch of targeted removal and you have hexproof creatures. And once you get a huge lifelinker with vigilance, they can't realistically attack or block anymore." Jůza also explained that hate cards like [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) or [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) are on the decline, which was another reason for him to choose the deck. "Of course, as it turned out, I lost to [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) in Round 4 and Liliana in Round 5, but overall there is not much hate against me."

Alessandro Portaro, a control player at heart, went with blue-white control. "Right now, you obviously shouldn't play a deck that can't beat Eldrazi," he said. "My blue-white deck is a little over 50% against Eldrazi, and I like to play control, so it was an easy choice for me."


This deck exploits the fact that the Eldrazi fall to removal spells just like any other creatures, so Portaro has four [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) in addition to several [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy). "[Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) is the most important card against Eldrazi," he said. "It also helps make the deck good against Affinity and Abzan Company."

Martin Müller opted for [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End), a deck that got better positioned now that that most Eldrazi players have removed [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) or [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) from their deck. "Initially, I was pretty set on playing Eldrazi myself," he said. "But after playing it on Magic Online and thinking about it, I anticipated a lot of mirror matches on Day 2, and that's a 50-50 matchup with not a lot of play to it. So I switched to [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End)."


"It's not hugely advantageous against Eldrazi, but I'm probably favored," he continued. "I'm not playing any [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), [Ingot Chewer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ingot+Chewer), or [Pale Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pale+Recluse)--the way to beat Eldrazi is to reliably and quickly sweep the board with [Living End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End), so I'm playing more [Jungle Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Weaver) and [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) instead. [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) from their sideboard can be annoying, but it still costs them a full turn to play it, and that gives me the time to find [Beast Within](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within)."

Simon Nielsen chose Lantern Control. "I think it's really good versus Eldrazi," he said. "It plays [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge), which is really hard for especially the blue-white version to beat. They have to use [Eldrazi Displacer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Displacer) with [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) to get cards in my hand, but I have [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) to stop that. Red-green versions have [World Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=World+Breaker), which is a problem, but you can [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) it, you can have [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) or multiple Bridges to protect yourself, or you can mill their lands."


"I think my deck is well-positioned: it's good against the decks that are good versus Eldrazi, such as Affinity and Abzan Company, and bad against the decks that are bad versus Eldrazi, such as Jund and Burn." But all next-level metagame considerations aside, his choice came down to one simple fact: the unwieldy Eldrazi cannot cross an [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge).
