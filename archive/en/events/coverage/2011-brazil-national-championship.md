Camargo No 1 Brazillian Pod Caster
==================================




September 17-18, 2011












2011 Brazil National Championship
---------------------------------



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/branat11/top%204%20group.jpg)

It took almost seven hours - and that was just his top 8 journey. In the end, it was unbelievable perseverance and skill under pressure that navigated Marcus Camargo to a National Title here in Brazil. In the quarter final, his [Bant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant)[Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck had needed the full five games. The semi final, five games. The final, five games. At every turn it seemed that he was ready to make the right move from many, many choices. He thought, and he fought, and he caught opponents at the right moment to stay in the games he needed to, and win the ones that really mattered - the game five with a place at Worlds on the line. The game five with a place on the team on the line. The game five with history on the line.

Quarter Final Round-Up
----------------------


*by Rich Hagon* 

**Reinaldo da Silva vs. Guilherme Medeiros Merjan  

Luiz Henrique Miguel vs. Marcus Camargo  

Allison Abe vs. Igor Sousa  

Marcus Pacheco vs. Jonathan Melamed**


So here's how this here 'one of me, four matches' thing is going to work. First of all, it's only three matches, since Reinaldo da Silva, having got to 9-0 and paced the field all weekend, can't go to Worlds, and has conceded to Guilherme Medeiros Merjan, making him a very happy man. That leaves three matches to look at. Our good friend Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa is keeping an eye on Miguel v Camargo and Abe v Sousa, leaving me to concentrate on the match between Jonathan Melamed and Marcus Pacheco. Oh, and don't forget that this is Nationals, so it's best three-out-of five.


Pacheco opened with [Drowned Catacomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowned+Catacomb)s, while Melamed was straight into action with [Halimar Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths). [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) found [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil), [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith), [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge), and [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) in Pacheco's hand. [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) dealt with [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) the following turn.


*Already the first game was over in Luiz Miguel v Marcus Camargo. A turn three [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) followed up by turn four [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) was enough for Miguel to take the opener.*


***Luiz Miguel 1 - 0 Marcus Camargo***


Melamed spent his fourth turn casting [Shrine of Piercing Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Piercing+Vision), and didn't take long to crack it in search of more goodness for his [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo. He added [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) to the board, the only non-land between the two players. At end of turn he aimed for [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch). [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) from Pacheco was met with [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel). Now Pacheco went for a second [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat), and that resolved, killing the [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Deceiver%2BExarch) threat.


Melamed cast [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle), now holding six cards in hand. Pacheco too had a grip full of cards. Next up was [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) from Melamed, which persuaded Pacheco to try [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) to wipe the board. [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) was a teaser. Pacheco could pay, but then leave only two mana open. He elected to let his board sweeper hit the graveyard instead. Melamed cast [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), not on an [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Deceiver%2BExarch) as normally happens, but on [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle). That was a hideous engine to have to overcome, and virtually forced Pacheco to do something about it. [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) was that something, which Melamed redirected to his [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite). [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) from Pacheco met [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce), and the [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) resolved. Advantage Melamed. [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) reappeared too.


Now Pacheco had [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), causing Melamed to get some value by casting [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) with Kicker on his own [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle). He was still six cards to three up.


*Elsewhere, Igor Sousa had taken his opener against Allison Abe. Although Abe had played a cagey game, having used [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) to see early on that Sousa had a lot of removal, in the end Sousa always had one more removal available than Abe could avoid, and Sousa led 1-0.*


***Igor Sousa 1 - 0 Allison Abe***



![Consume the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Consume+the+Meek)

Back at Melamed v Pacheco, and Pacheco had landed his first [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) of the match. Melamed cast [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) once more, a second [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), and a second [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer). He didn't have an answer to [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), however. Pacheco attacked Melamed down to five before casting [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum). For Melamed, at least [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) was shooting down Zombies, but Melamed would need something more. [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) attached itself to [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle), and it was back to the Titan Pacheco.


This time he had [Consume the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+the+Meek). Melamed responded by activating his [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) to see deeper into his deck, but there were no answers. The board was swept away, leaving [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) to waltz in unopposed to claim the opener.


Jonathan Melamed 0 - 1 Marcus Pacheco


*In Miguel v Camargo, Luiz Miguel had gone for his [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo on turn four, but Marcus Camargo was ready with [Nature's Claim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Claim). Camargo followed up with [Linvala, Keeper of Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence), which stops the combo, and Miguel couldn't find a way back. All square once more.*


***Luiz Miguel 1 - 1 Marcus Camargo***


Melamed opened game two with [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe). Pacheco didn't want him to see, so cast [Mental Misstep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep). Melamed really wanted to look, so cast [Mental Misstep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep) too! He found [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), and four land. When Pacheco cast the [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), he found [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) in the way, so couldn't get the same level of information that Melamed had. Pacheco cast [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), leaving the way clear for Melamed to begin the Planeswalker battle with [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), who promptly drew Melamed a card.


[Despise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Despise) finally got Pacheco a look at Melamed's hand: [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel), and [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest). Little changed over the next few turns, with [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) for Pacheco and [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) for Melamed, who was nevertheless advantaged by the status quo thanks to his [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), which he had restocked to 3 loyalty. Pacheco tried for a Jace of his own, but [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) resolved. At end of turn, Melamed went for [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch). Was the Jace a mana-sapping fatal mistake from Pacheco?


He was down to three mana, and let the [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Deceiver%2BExarch) resolve. Melamed untapped. He drew an extra card with [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren). Then he attacked with his [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Deceiver%2BExarch) and [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer). Pacheco fell to 16, and then Melamed passed the turn. [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) continued to draw him extra cards, pulling further ahead of Pacheco. Even so, the presence of [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) on the board was stopping Melamed from pulling the trigger on his [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin).


Pacheco cast [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), and again the [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) trap was set. If Pacheco paid, he would be back down to two mana. He declined to pay, laid a [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit), and passed back to Melamed. [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) resolved, as Melamed hit the ten mana mark. Jace drew him another card, and the same dance occurred again, [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) blocking [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch), and Pacheco taking one from [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer).


*On the back table, Igor Sousa took a 2-0 lead when he cast [Memoricide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memoricide) on Allison Abe, removing all the [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)s from the deck.* 


***Igor Sousa 2 - 0 Allison Abe***


*Meanwhile Luiz Miguel was able to find his [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo to take a 2-1 lead over Marcus Camargo.*


***Luiz Miguel 2 - 1 Marcus Camargo***


Pacheco once again wanted [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) to resolve, and this time it did, Pacheco taking [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) and finding all four copies of [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) in Melamed's hand. With [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite)[Shatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter)ed by Melamed, he was in great shape. He cast [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) on his [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle), rather than his [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Deceiver%2BExarch), ready to bait Pacheco into a counterspell war. Pacheco responded with [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), and the [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) was no more. Now Pacheco had three cards. You know what happens to aggro rush decks? They get burned by Wrath effects, like [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith). Still, Sousa could only cast it for two, so the board wasn't completely clear. In its place, something even more fearsome than the [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) it had been masquerading as - [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite). ### Game Five


Six and a half hours had passed since these two had started their top 8 campaigns. Now, however long it took, there could be only one more game. Marcus Pacheco would be going to Worlds. Guilherme Medeiros Merjan would be going to Worlds on the Team. Both these two would be on the team, but one as Champion.


One more push, one more bullying of frayed nerves, tired limbs, sluggish thinking. 10.30 at night, and still this most wondrous and brutal of games continued to ask the most mind-shredding questions of the only two still left standing.


Both players kept seven cards, which was a relief - nobody wants a Final game five decided by mulligans. [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) turn two from Camargo was the opening play. His [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) were denied by [Mental Misstep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep) from Sousa, after a little thought. [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) saw [Sylvok Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Replica), [Devout Lightcaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Lightcaster), [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph), and [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge). Bye bye [Devout Lightcaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Lightcaster).


[Sylvok Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Replica) arrived uneventfully, and began the beats. [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) took out [Darkslick Shores](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkslick+Shores), attempting to stunt Sousa for mana. He continued to lay land, however. The [Sylvok Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Replica) attacked him to 16. Five mana - please sir, may I have [Venser, the Sojourner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+the+Sojourner)? No, you may not, [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) denying Camargo a huge step towards victory. Next turn, please may I have [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)? No, you may not, [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive) getting in the way.


Surely a third must-counter threat would be Sousa's undoing. [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) from Camargo was not that threat. Six mana for Sousa? [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) for four, and the board was clear. He had survived [Venser, the Sojourner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Venser%252C%2Bthe%2BSojourner), and [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and now saw an empty board. How close was Sousa now to the finish line?


He activated [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) and attacked, Camargo now at 14. From the top? [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)! It resolved, getting back [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite). Unbelievable **Magic**. Sousa untapped now facing a Titanic threat. He activated two [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit)s, and hit with both. Camargo was down to 8 life. Sousa was at 15. What a climax. With Sousa tapped out, Camargo could do what he wanted. He used [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) to blow up a [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit). He cast [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) to create a second Sun titan. He attacked Sousa to 9.


Sousa needed a land, and he needed [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) for six. He passed the turn.


Camargo untapped. He announced his attack. In came two [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)s. [Sylvok Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Replica) came back, [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) took out [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit). [Mystifying Maze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystifying+Maze) kept Sousa alive, but barely. [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) returned [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) at end of turn, blowing up [Mystifying Maze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystifying+Maze). Surely Sousa was done?


For one last time, he passed the turn. For one last time, Camargo attacked. For one last time, someone fell to zero life. For one last time, someone emerged victorious, and that man was Marcus Camargo.


**Marcus Camargo 3 - 2 Igor Sousa**

*by Rich Hagon* 


![Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Preordain)

[Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) - If ever there was a card that kicked off more games than any other this weekend, it was this innocuous little number. It's exactly the kind of card that newer players ignore utterly. It isn't a creature, doesn't deal damage, doesn't kill anything, doesn't force discard, doesn't gain you life, doesn't even counter a spell. It's just really, really good.



![Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Splinter+Twin)

[Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) - It was interesting that so many players here in Brazil would have chosen to play [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) in Modern, if they'd been at Philadelphia. There's no doubt that infinite damage is quite a lot, and more than your average opponent is equipped to handle. Nonetheless, the quarter finals were a beating for the twin decks, with three losing, and the fourth losing by default when the pilot conceded. Even so, Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa would still play this if Nationals started again tomorrow, and that's a sign of how strong the card, and the deck, is.



![Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Torpor+Orb)

[Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) - It's a negative card to be sure, but boy oh boy does it ever do good things. Time and time again it has been the last line of defense, and time and time again it gets a player over the finish line. Another great card that doesn't look super-exciting at first glance. It's [Null Rod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Null+Rod) that has the priceless flavor text:  
*"But it doesn't **do** anything."*  
*"No, it **does** nothing."*


[Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) feels a bit like that. Flint-hearted spoilers rejoice, [Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) is your friend.



![Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace+Beleren)

[Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) - You know what's better than 'better than all'? Well, maybe not, but the disappearance of the Mind Sculptor has reminded us just what an utter kicking [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) is. There may be twenty Jace variants twenty years from now, but people will still talk about this guy. He draws you cards, and lots of them. He draws you a card for three mana, and draws opposing attackers like a moth to a flame. That's the *worst* that can happen, for three mana. A gentlemen's wager: when and if Planeswalkers reach three figures, [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) will still be in the top ten. Real men can admit it - Jace, we love you.



![Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Birthing+Pod)

[Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) - Any time you see Kenny Oberg of Sweden coming to game with a deck, you know that it's probably going to be what's euphemistically known in the trade as a 'skill-tester'. Coming into Pro Tour Nagoya, [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) was a card that was getting a lot of under-the-radar interest. If anyone could work out just what the optimum build might be, maybe it was the sort of card that could 'break' a Pro Tour environment. That didn't happen, but [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) is still an awesome card to have fun with, and as Marcus Camargo showed, go a very long way with, if you can play it right.
