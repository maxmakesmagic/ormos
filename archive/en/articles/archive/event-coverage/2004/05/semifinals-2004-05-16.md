Semifinals:
===========



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 16, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett












Nicolai Herzog vs. Mike Turian
------------------------------


![Michael Turian](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptsd04/sf1_1.jpg)Mike Turian is calling this his farewell Pro Tour. He's certainly going out with a bang. Following up on his Top 8 at Amsterdam, Turian is going one better this time around, and now stands in a position to win the whole thing. Standing in his way is someone who knows a thing or two about winning Limited Pro Tours: Nicolai Herzog. He took home the title at Amsterdam after Turian's quarterfinal exit. If the need to win wasn't pressure enough, both of these players are in the race for the Player of the Year title.


### Game 1


Herzog won the roll and played a second-turn [Iron Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr). Turian led with a mountain, which had Herzog calling for the [Slith Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Firewalker), but Turian just played a plains and passed the turn. Herzog busted out [Granite Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard) and shot Turian when he played out a [Skyhunter Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyhunter+Cub). Next up for Herzog was [Myr Matrix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Matrix).


"[Myr Matrix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Matrix). Powerful." - Mike Turian


"It is!" - Nicolai Herzog


Turian just nodded with a smile and dropped [Specter's Shroud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Specter%27s+Shroud) on his Cub, snagging a [Darksteel Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Gargoyle) from Herzog. Herzog untapped and considered his options. He could [Echoing Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Decay) the Cub and then ping it with the [Granite Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard), or he could [Echoing Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Ruin) the Shroud and ground it, then Decay it. He chose the latter. Turian replaced his monster with a [Skyhunter Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyhunter+Patrol). Herzog played the last card in his hand, [Grimclaw Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimclaw+Bats), and passed it back.


Turian looked at his four land and ended up doing nothing. Herzog played another land. Turian made a [Leonin Abunas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Abunas), and Herzog shot him and made a token. He played another land and did the same after Turian found his fifth and made a [Loxodon Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Mystic). Soon he had three tokens on his side. Turian swung with his Patrol for two and waited on seven mana.


Herzog got up to four tokens and swarmed in with his tokens and bats, leaving [Iron Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr) at home. Turian blocked two tokens with Abunas and Mystic. They resolved damage. Herzog tried to finish the Mystic off with his Shard, but Turian's [Razor Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razor+Barrier) made the save. Herzog followed up with [Electrostatic Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Bolt), and the tapper went to the graveyard.


Turian swung all out, and played [Slith Ascendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Ascendant). Herzog shrugged and pushed all his men into the middle. Turian entwined [Roar of the Kha](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Kha). In response, Herzog pinged the Slith and [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror)ed another creature, leaving Turian no escape.


Herzog 1 - Turian 0


"Stupid [Granite Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard), so good." - Mike Turian


"It is actually ridiculous." - Nicolai Herzog


"It wins games all by itself." - Mike Turian


While waiting for the cameras to roll, Herzog shiverd.


"It's really cold in here â€¦ and I'm from Norway!" - Nicolai Herzog


### Game 2


Turian answered an [Iron Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr) with [Skyhunter Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyhunter+Cub), and then played [Leonin Den-Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Den-Guard) when Herzog only made a Talisman. Turian attacked but had no further play. Herzog's five mana gave him a [Panoptic Mirror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panoptic+Mirror). The next turn, when he tapped five more and put [Essence Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain) on it, Turian gave an appreciative frown.


"I was hoping that when you tapped five it would be [Myr Matrix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Matrix) again." - Mike Turian


He drained the Cub and passed it back to Turian. He, in turn, dropped [Neurok Hoversail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Hoversail) on his Den-Guard.


"I should have hit the Guard. Wow, I *really* should have hit him." - Nicolai Herzog


He started sending the Mirror to the face.


"Can you win against this?" - Nicolai Herzog


Down came an [Ogre Leadfoot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Leadfoot) for Herzog.


"I can win against *that*." - Mike Turian


Turian was accumulating cards in hand. Herzog dropped [Moriok Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moriok+Scavenger) and [Vulshok Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Berserker). Again Turian did nothing. Herzog hit with Scavenger and the Ogre, but Turian had [Raise the Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm) to help his cat take down the 3/3. Better still, he saved his token with an [Awe Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awe+Strike). The other one exited after chumping the Scavenger. He untapped and dropped [Skyhunter Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyhunter+Patrol) and [Mindstorm Crown](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindstorm+Crown).


Herzog was more interested in doing unfair things. He Drained the Patrol, played [Granite Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard) and offed the token, then Bartered a path for his Berserker. Turian drew, and then scooped.


Herzog 2 - Turian 0


### Game 3


Turian kicked off with [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb), and then a tardy [Auriok Transfixer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Transfixer). Herzog made an [Iron Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr). Turian swung in heedless and Herzog made the trade. His being on three mana was good for Turian's [Slith Ascendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Ascendant), just in case he had [Granite Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard) in hand. Herzog played a [Leaden Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaden+Myr) and passed it back.



![Myr Matrix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Myr+Matrix)

[Vulshok Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Berserker) began the beats in earnest for Turian. Herzog got an [Ogre Leadfoot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Leadfoot) stand in the way. Turian's Spellbomb offed the Myr and he dropped [Whispersilk Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak), giving Herzog just one turn to deal with the Ascendant. He aimed a [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) at it. Unfortunately for him, [Slith Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Firewalker) was on deck ready to pick up the Cloak. Herzog was in a pickle.


He played his sixth land and [Mirror Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Golem) before accepting more beats. He swung back and played [Greater Harvester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Harvester), then immediately realized the opening he'd left.


"Do you have Grab in hand? Because that would be insane." - Nicolai Herzog


"Well, I just drew it." - Mike Turian


Herzog deflated in his chair.


"At least it's not going to kill you." - Mike Turian


"It's not going to make him stronger, either." - Gary Wise, from the gallery


Herzog sacrificed his Harvester and hoped to rip the Barter. He did not.


Herzog 2 - Turian 1


"So lucky!" - Nicolai Herzog


Turian gave a goofy giggle.


"Oh well. I'm sure I would have won anyway." - Mike Turian


They started shuffling for Game 4.


"I was surprised you traded the Myr for the Transfixer." - Mike Turian


"Well, I had plenty of land, and red. I was happy." - Nicolai Herzog


"I was happy." - Mike Turian


"We were both happy." - Nicolai Herzog


"â€¦ You were really happy?" - Mike Turian


### Game 4


Now it was Herzog's turn to play first. He led with [Iron Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr), then [Vulshok Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Berserker), which ran headlong into [Raise the Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm). Turian had no third land, and gave it back.


Herzog got the crowd to gasp when he dropped [Greater Harvester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Harvester).


It looked like it was all over for Turian. He played [*Mirrodin*'s Core](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrodin%27s+Core) and passed it back. [Awe Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awe+Strike) bought him a turn. Herzog added [Lodestone Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Myr) to his team. Turian's fouth land was waiting on top of his deck.


"Got the [Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purge)?" - Nicolai Herzog


All he committed to was putting [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) on the board. Herzog untapped and considered things, then put the [Lodestone Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Myr) in his yard. Turian was visibly shocked.


"Wow." - Mike Turian


He sent the Harvester and the [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) took it on the chin. [Razor Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razor+Barrier) saved it. Herzog added a [Leaden Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaden+Myr). Turian busted out with [Slith Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Firewalker) and [Slith Ascendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Ascendant). [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) dispatched the Ascendant at end of turn, then Herzog untapped, lost a land, played [Granite Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Granite+Shard) and pinged the Firewalker. The [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) got run over.


![Nicolai Herzog](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptsd04/sf1_2.jpg)Turian played a very sad Abunas. Herzog stung him during upkeep and lost his Myr, then brought the Harvester again, taking care of the Abunas. This time, though, Turian had no play on his turn. Herzog lost a land and went to swing, but Turian's unentwined [Grab the Reins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grab+the+Reins) worked as a fog.


All Turian could manage then was [Whispersilk Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak), desperately trying to run Herzog out of permanents to sacrifice. The Harvester hit and he lost the Cloak and a land. He untapped and played [Arcbound Slith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Slith) and [Sunbeam Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunbeam+Spellbomb). Slith took the hit while Herzog continued to draw land. [Sunbeam Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunbeam+Spellbomb) turned up an emergency [Auriok Glaivemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Glaivemaster) that was happy to hit the graveyard. But after that Turian's deck was running on empty. He fell to two lands and hoped to draw the [Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purge). When he didn't, he extended the hand.


Nicolai Herzog defeats Mike Turian 3-1
