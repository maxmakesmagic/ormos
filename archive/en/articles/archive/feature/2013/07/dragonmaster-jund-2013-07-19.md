Dragonmaster Jund
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 19, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







If you want an interesting Dragon-related Standard deck, there's no better source than Brian Kibler. [He recently built an aggressive Jund deck](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/26461_Taking-My-Dragons-To-South-Beach.html), which he not only played at [GP Miami](/en/events/coverage/duke-draws-blood-miami), but also [has been streaming](http://www.twitch.tv/bmkibler). 

![Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) The deck eschews playing all the removal that normal Jund plays, instead rocking the full set of [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite)s, [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat)s, [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar)s, and [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist)s, along with a couple [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped) for the late game. It still runs some removal, with the full complement of [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) packing the most punch. 

 I like that this deck not only can curve out well but has a bunch of relevant internal synergies. [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) combines well with [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) and Varolz, and Varolz lets you spend a ton of mana while not casting a spell, in order to flip [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells). I also like that it has sixteen haste creatures, which means sorcery-speed removal is much less effective than it would be normally. In fact, even something like [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) is almost irrelevant here, being unable to kill half the creatures and getting hit by the other half before being able to kill them. 

 The sideboard is also pretty nice, with a sick anti-control package of [Ruric Thar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruric+Thar) and [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade), both of which are incredibly effective against removal-heavy decks. I also like the [Rolling Temblor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor)s and [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren)s against the fast decks, since this deck can morph into midrange control fairly easily.
