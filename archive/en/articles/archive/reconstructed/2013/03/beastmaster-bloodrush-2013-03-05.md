Beastmaster Bloodrush
=====================



 Posted in **Reconstructed**
 on March 5, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 







Set down your blue magic, throw aside your law-abiding mentality, and hop on your war indrik—it's Gruul Week!


  
  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/rc/rc237_pitfight.jpg)[Pit Fight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pit+Fight) | Art by [Matt Stewart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Matt%20Stewart%22%5D)


This week, I'm going to take a look at a Gruul deck with some elements you might expect... and a few twists.


The things you expect? A rush of creatures—followed by a *bloodrush* of combat tricks. Attacking is the Gruul way, and I wasn't going to pass up the opportunity for Gruul Week.


But what you might not expect? Well, how about this guy:



![Wild Beastmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wild+Beastmaster)

 

You might remember that a few months ago I wrote about a [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) deck that proved to be pretty popular. (It was budget-friendly as well—[check it out](/en/articles/archive/reconstructed/unleash-beastmaster-2012-12-04) if that's what you're looking for.) With the addition of *Gatecrash*, [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) gets even better!


But I won't let myself steal the thunder from Future Gavin—he'll tell you about it in a moment. In the meantime, take a look at the deck we'll be starting with today: Kyle Gascoigne's Wild Game!

### The Battle Plan


This deck is plenty aggressive, planning to attack at each opportunity. You want to turn your creatures sideways most turns, using your plentitude of combat tricks to your advantage.


And speaking of combat tricks, now that Future Gavin is here, let's talk about [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) a bit.


Before, the key to the Beastmaster was casting a ton of pump spells on her, attacking to make all of your creatures colossal, and then defeating your opponent.


*Gatecrash* changes everything! Now the plan is to attack, then cast a bunch of pump spells on her!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/rc/rc237_wildbeastmaster.jpg)[Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) | Art by [Kev Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Kev%20Walker%22%5D)


...okay, so maybe there's a *bit* more to it than that.


*Gatecrash* adds several great new elements to this deck. The first up is core to Gruul: bloodrush.


Before, you wanted to play all kinds of instants and sorceries to pump up your creatures. However, a big problem was that you didn't want to risk drawing hands of all pump spells without enough creatures to grow big with [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster).


Bloodrush solves this problem perfectly! You can attack, put [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster)'s triggered ability on the stack, and then use bloodrush on it. When the ability resolves, it will take the post-bloodrush power into account. Bloodrush gives you a suite of creatures, which also double as aggressive pump spells. Since you only really want to pump your [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) on offense anyway, the limitation to attacking creatures isn't a big deal at all!


Second, the card Burning-Tree Emissary goes a long way. It puts an essentially free creature onto the table, meaning you can load multiple creatures onto the table right before playing a Beastmaster without any problem. Imagine a hand, for example, that goes turn-one [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf); turn-two Emissary, Emissary, Beastmaster. A single [Slaughterhorn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn) or [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) on [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) turn three makes for lethal damage!

Finally, you have [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade). Domri not only serves as a huge threat to control and as a removal spell, but he also allows you to keep up a steady stream of creatures—of which many are pump spells! Domri can't help you draw [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth)—but he sure can help you draw bloodrush creatures! The addition of Gruul cards give this deck a much different—and deadlier—texture than before.


### Deck Breakdown


So, where should we start to look at changes? Let's go through each card in the deck and see what belongs and what can go.





![Wild Beastmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wild+Beastmaster)


 
This is a core focal point of this deck. We're going to build around her and making her as strong as possible. This deck will definitely want all four copies.






![Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Arbor+Elf)


 
The key element that can make this deck more powerful than other beatdown decks in its class is the blistering speed it can present. We have to maximize its speed, and [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) does that masterfully.



One-mana Elves are great for this sort of strategy since not only do they accelerate you, but they double up as creatures for when it's time to go whole-hog (sometimes [quite literally](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=quite+literally)) with your [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster). To this end, I definitely want to play as many one-mana Elves as I can. In addition to four [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf)s, a full set of [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) would make a good fit as well. While this deck doesn't need the white mana, the acceleration is still good enough that I want to play Pilgrim as well.





![Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Burning-Tree+Emissary)


 
As noted before, [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) is awesome in decks that want to quickly run their opponents over with [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster). Playing Emissary into Beastmaster is quite powerful, and you definitely want to play the full four to maximize this situation.






![Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager)


 
Pow! Take 4!



+4/+4 and trample for two mana is an extraordinarily good price, and, as one of the flagship bloodrush creatures, it's a great fit in this deck. It can even be a perfectly reasonable creature on its own when it's time to beat down! I definitely want to keep the full four copies of these.





![Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Slaughterhorn)


 
Pow! Take 4!



While it might not be a [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager), [Slaughterhorn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn) is still an incredible trick for this deck. It's more or less a [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) that can also be used as a creature to beat down with. While a 3/2 for three isn't setting any world records (well, maybe on the world of [Segovia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Segovia)), it can still get in for plenty damage if you have a blistering assault.


It's the next bloodrush creature I want to play, and I want to play all four.





![Legion Loyalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Legion+Loyalist)


 
[Legion Loyalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist) is attractive for a few reasons. First, it can give all of your creatures trample. When you're attacking with [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster), [Legion Loyalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist) ensures your huge creatures can't just be chump blocked. Second, even in normal combat situations without a Beastmaster, first strike helps your creatures win in combat. It's also something else you can do on turn one if you don't have an Elf.



However, every card comes at a cost. To play a card, it means you're not playing something else. And while [Legion Loyalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist) can be strong, it's really only best when you're already winning. If you're attacking with a massive [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster), you're probably in good shape anyway! I would rather play something that is more consistently good in this deck. He may be loyal—but this take on the Gruul Clans is not the place for him.





![Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Strangleroot+Geist)


 
One of the major strengths of this deck is that, in addition to just killing your opponent with a huge [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster)–influenced army, you can just be a reasonable beatdown deck otherwise. Cards like [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) are a core part of that. It's a fantastic aggressive creature, is plenty resilient, and works well with [Domri](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri). I'll be keeping all four.



In addition to [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), I would like to up the beatdown element of this deck even more. The card I would look at doing this with fits in a similar vein: [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar). As a 3/3 for only two mana, with the potential to gain haste, [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) can create some really explosive and beatdown-heavy draws.





![Wrecking Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wrecking+Ogre)


 
While brutally powerful, at five mana it's a bit expensive to cast. Most of the time I would rather have something like [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart).



If we shave out our most expensive creatures, we can keep this deck on an incredibly low curve that won't need nearly as many lands. If this deck tops out at three or four and has eight one-drop mana accelerators, we can probably get away with twenty or twenty-one lands. (Which, in turn, makes our [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade)s better.) While he can be a wrecking, this Ogre isn't quite a fit.





![Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Domri+Rade)


 
[Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) is a card-drawing, creature-killing, ultimate-threatening machine. With eight turn-one accelerators, you can cast him on turn two fairly often. At that point, he will dig you closer to the crucial elements of your deck, filling your hand with bloodrush creatures—or even the [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) herself.



Control decks often gnash their teeth to fight off an early Domri, and against beatdown decks he can completely dominate a game if left unchecked. I'd like to move up to the full four copies of Domri. He's just yet another powerful turn-two threat you can present.





![Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Giant+Growth)


 
With the surplus of bloodrush creatures and the move to four [Domris](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domris), the noncreatures in this deck really need to count. I'm willing to play some noncreatures—but not many, and they have to be worth it.



I think the card I would rather have here is [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor). While there are some times when [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) is deemed better—such as [Grand Prix Quebec City](/en/events/coverage/turn-three-take-fourteen-christiansen-mauls-quebec-city) a couple weeks ago—the ability to use [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) over and over again, as well as being able to use it to beat down early, makes up for the fact that it pumps [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) for 1 less on the turn you cast it. You have plenty of other [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth)–like effects in your deck, and so having some variation is good. [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) it is!

Now, this deck does stray from a lot of things you might expect from other Gruul decks. For example, it doesn't play [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells). That way, you can keep the mana curve incredibly low (nothing costs more than three, save for a hard-cast [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager)), meaning you can get away with few lands in your deck.


However, there are certainly some other directions you can take this deck. You can go bigger and play cards like [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells). (Which does work pretty well with the [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster), since you get two creatures.) You could also try and go smaller and even faster, with cards like [Lightning Mauler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler). [Lightning Mauler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) can create some pretty insane starts with both [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) and [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary), and you could definitely use a setup like that to try and lower the curve further.
