Modern Green-White Elves
========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on June 17, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a member of the Wizards of the Coast coverage team. 






Today’s deck combines small creatures and fast mana to overwhelm your opponent. Elves is a strategy that has been around for years in all formats. Legacy, Standard, and even Extended (a now-retired format) have seen their fair share of Elf decks at all levels of play—from casual *Magic* up to the Pro Tour.



The main combo in the deck uses [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and a pair of [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel)s. Tap the Sentinels and another Elf to add three mana, cast an Elf, untap your Sentinels, rinse and repeat. This combo is capable of generating a large amount of mana that you can use for pretty much anything. Some Elf decks use the mana for [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). This deck uses mana to activate [Ezuri, Renegade Leader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ezuri%2C+Renegade+Leader) multiple times, turning your Elves into giant trampling threats. If that plan fails, you also have [Mirror Entity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) to make your creatures very large.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Heritage+Druid)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nettle+Sentinel)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel)

What if you don’t draw your main mana engine in [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid)? You will likely still have lots of Elves in play, which makes [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx) tap for a large amount of mana. It definitely can’t give you as much mana as a [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) can, but it’s still a reasonable backup plan.



What this deck has going for it is consistency. It’s great at finding the right creatures at the right times. [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) is your main way of searching up any creature in your deck at a very reasonable cost. If you fail to draw that, you also have the new *Dragons of Tarkir* card [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) to search the top six cards of your deck. No matter which method you use, it’s pretty easy to assemble the pieces you need to win the game.
