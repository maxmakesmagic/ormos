Team Round 2: Playing for Pride
===============================

 on November 17, 2011

By Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw

After a hard day of Standard battling, both Switzerland and Japan are hovering within arm's reach of the top of the teams standings.




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/japan.jpg)

*Team Japan*

Representing Team Japan:


Ryuichiro Ishida – Standard


Makihito Mihara – Modern


Fujimoto Tomoya – Legacy




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/switzerland.jpg)

*Team Switzerland*

And representing Team Switzerland:


Dario Vicedomini – Standard


Matthias Künzler – Modern


Matteo Rusconi – Legacy


Modern was the first to finish game one, with Japan summoning a [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), followed by a [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch). Switzerland, having earlier cast [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), knew there was a [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) waiting in the wings for Japan, and after their [Serum Visions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) determined they could not muster a lethal storm count, the Swiss scooped up their cards.


Meanwhile, Legacy was looking bad for Switzerland, after Japan's [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul), the Aeon's Torn staged [Sneak Attack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack), taking all of Switzerland's permanents with it. Switzerland valiantly attempted a come-back, but Japan quickly put [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) to work finding a replacement monster.


In Standard, Switzerland's Illusions deck stopped a [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) with [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), but couldn't stop the [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) that followed. A [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) later and Japan had a game one clean sweep across the board.


Standard: Ishida 1 – Vicedomini 0


Modern: Mihara 1 – Künzler 0


Legacy: Tomoya 1 – Rusconi 0


Total: Japan 3 – 0


Switzerland again began their Modern match with some [Serum Visions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions), while Japan took a peek with a [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe). Switzerland [Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe)d back, seeing an [Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exarch), a [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire), but no other action to speak of. But action Japan did find, engaging in a little [Sleight of Hand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand). Japan again successfully summoned a [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch), before untapping and wining the Modern match with a [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), and an unimaginable number of [Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exarch)[Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twin)s.




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/teams_r2_switzerland.jpg)

*Switzerland has their collective back against the wall!*

In Legacy, Switzerland got the fight started with [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and added insult to growing injury by Stifling a [Misty Rainforest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest). Japan finally managed to [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) up a [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) with one turn left on the Tarmoclock. Japan had [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), [Volcanic Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island), and [Ancient Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb) untapped, and cast [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell).


Switzerland, along with their [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), had two [Tropical Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island)s, and a [Volcanic Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island). In hand were a pair of [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)s, a [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), a [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) (with a [Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) and a [Red Elemental Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Red+Elemental+Blast) in the graveyard), and a [Krosan Grip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip). After much consultation with their Modern contingent, Switzerland finally threw away the [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) for the first [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), prompting a [Red Elemental Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Red+Elemental+Blast) from Japan. Switzerland considered getting funky with the [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) potentially enabling their first and now spent [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), but with only one tick of the clock remaining, just chucked it to the second [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). Japan had nothing, and so they were off to the decider.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Viridian%20Emissary)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian%20Emissary)
In Standard, Japan managed to hold back a [Phantasmal Bear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Bear) with a couple of copies of [Viridian Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Emissary), while Switzerland transformed their [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), and began flying in for 3. Without much more in the way of offense from Switzerland, Japan changed gear, summoning [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) and sending the Emissaries in for a little aggressive negotiation. The [Phantasmal Bear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Bear) traded with one, while the other took a [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot). Switzerland then added a [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) to their board, and it wasn't long before [Thrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun) had outraced Switzerland to the finish.


Standard: Ishida 2 – Vicedomini 0


Modern: Mihara 2 – Künzler 0


Legacy: Tomoya 1 – Rusconi 1


Total: Japan 5 – 1


With the match officially over as far as accounting was concerned, the Legacy front continued to battle for pride. Switzerland attacked early and often with a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), while repeatedly reducing Japan's resources to (and with) [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s. Japan eventually managed an [Ancient Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb) to go with their [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), and cast a [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), but Switzerland left them [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze)d with their response (hint: it was a [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze)). Japan was happy to try again on their following turn, and successfully revealed an [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). Switzerland hoped to draw a [Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) on their turn, but didn't, leaving Japan with not only the match, but the pride as well.


Japan defeats Switzerland 3 – 0
