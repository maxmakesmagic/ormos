Round 4: Brock Parker vs. Matt Linde
====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 26, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_602.png)
By Matt Urban











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a960.jpg)Matt Linde is a former US National Champion, and also has a Worlds top 8 to his credit. Brock Parker is a gravy train pro player. Linde and Parker are friends who live across the street from each other and are constant money draft partners. They are both playing with 2 byes and have won their third rounds. They sit down, kind of knowing what the outcome will be since they have played this matchup in practice. Linde summed it up nicely by saying, "I could have played this from the comfort of my own home." Matt had a tight w/r deck with a few off color cycling and morph creatures. Brock had a slower r/g deck, also with a few off color morph creatures.

### Game 1

Brock won the die roll and elected to play. He took the dreaded trip to Paris while Matt kept his hand. Despite the mulligan, Brock led with a turn 2 [Elvish Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior). Matt countered with a turn 2 [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). The [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) did little to hold off the Warrior, and Brock continued with a [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) and a [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder). Matt shot the [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) and summoned a Morphed creature. Brock untapped and cast [Solar Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solar+Blast) targeting the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), frustrated that he didn't have the second [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) to cycle it.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a961.jpg)Linde's Morphed creature was revealed to be a [Daru Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Healer), but it could not save the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) on its own. All the while, [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) continued gaining Brock life and [Elvish Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior) kept working, bringing Matt to 11 and Brock to 21. The shenanigans seemed to end when Matt summoned [Exalted Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exalted+Angel). 

The bomb Angel really didn't do a whole lot, though, because Matt didn't have anything beyond the [Daru Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Healer) and a few Morphed creatures backing it up and the [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) was able to offset half the damage done to Brock. Brock just summoned large green men in the form of [Krosan Groundshaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Groundshaker) and [Treespring Lorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treespring+Lorian) to keep the pressure on. The Lorian eventually fell to two off color Morphed creatures, but the Groundshaker kept working to keep Matt from gaining too much life.

The game was being played at a breakneck pace until Brock summoned a bomb of his own: [Kamahl, Fist of Krosa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Fist+of+Krosa). Matt untapped ant took some time to look over the board. The situation was:

Matt had [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), [Daru Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Healer), [Exalted Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exalted+Angel), and an off color Morph creature, with 17 life and 3 land cards in hand.  
 Brock had Kamahl, [Elvish Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior), [Krosan Groundshaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Groundshaker), a Morph creature, no cards in hand, and 7 life to go with 9 land in play. 

Brock had lethal damage on the table by animating lands and using Kamahl to emulate Overrun, but he had to hope Matt didn't have some source of burn. Linde attacked with the Angel, leaving the life totals 21-7, and passed the turn. Brock animated 2 lands, used Kamahl to overrun, and sent the team. Matt dropped his hand of land in disgust. He had drawn land towards the end of the game when one of several action spells would have won it.

Linde - 0, Parker - 1

### Game 2

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a962.jpg)Linde chose to play first, and no one took a mulligan. Both players had good starts: Brock had turn 2 [Voidmage Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy), but that was met by Linde's turn 3 [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), slipped in while Brock was tapped out. Kai met his end at the hands of the goblin, and Linde played a Morphed creature. It was revealed to be [Exalted Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exalted+Angel) turn 5.

Brock had the answer ready in the form of [Solar Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solar+Blast) and [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock). Linde summoned some more pressure with [True Believer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer) and a second [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), but it wasn't enough as Kamahl came down for another visit. After reviewing his options, Matt extended the hand to his friend and wished him luck with the rest of the event.

**Final Result:** Brock Parker beat Matt Linde 2-0
