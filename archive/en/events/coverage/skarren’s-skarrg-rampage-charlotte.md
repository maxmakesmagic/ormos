Skarren’s Skarrg Rampage in Charlotte
=====================================




February 22-24, 2013












Grand Prix Charlotte 2013
-------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha13/Trophy-Shot.jpg)What started as the largest Grand Prix, and **Magic** tournament, in history ended with a crash as Frank Skarren brought the Gruul to bear in Charlotte. While Boros and Orzhov proved powerful throughout the weekend, the final draft of the day demonstrated what a keen eye can do during draft. Skarren's opponent in the finals, Gerard Fabiano, wielded a potent Simic deck to muscle by the usual suspects in white.


It's hard to overstate what happened this weekend. 2,672 players showed up to play *Gatecrash* Sealed Deck on Saturday, opening nearly 250,000 cards from booster packs in the span of just a few minutes. It took 17 rounds of play, including six rounds of Booster Draft, just to get to the Top 8. And, finally, two guilds underrepresented the entire weekend helped their players to the Finals. With the dust settled it's clear that *Gatecrash* Limited isn't as settled as players think, and **Magic** events will never be the same.

Quarterfinals – Ben Stark (Boros/Gruul) vs. Gerard Fabiano (Simic)
------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Adam Styborski*

 

If you've been following **Magic** scene recently it's been hard to avoid Ben Stark. From his 2013 season-starting victory at Grand Prix Indianapolis through his Top 8 appearance in last week's Pro Tour *Gatecrash* and you'd believe that he's just a young face with a hot streak.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha13/gpcharlotte-Semis-StarkFabiano-Stark.jpg)  
*Ben Stark* 
His 2012 Grand Prix Top 8 appearances in Costa Rica and Atlanta, 2011 victory in Pro Tour Paris, and several more top finishes over the years beg to differ. Stark is in an elite class of player, considered by many to be among the best Limited experts in the world. For him to make the Top 8 of the largest **Magic** tournament in history seems natural.


Across from Stark sat Gerard Fabiano, wearing his characteristic grin. He's an enigma among players, though he's been around just as long. From his team's excellent finish at Pro Tour Boston in 2002 to a more recent third place at 2010's Grand Prix Nashville and victory in 2008's Grand Prix Philadelphia, he and Stark felt easy together.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha13/gpcharlotte-Semis-StarkFabiano-Fabiano.jpg)  
*Gerard Fabiano* 
Longtime players with a lifetime passion can make the biggest events seem cozy. But only one could move on to the semifinals.


### Game 1


Stark led with [Boros Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Guildgate), and followed it with [Gruul Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Guildgate) and [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), but didn't have a creature. Fabiano's end of turn [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) grew thanks to [Frontline Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic), and attacks begin. In turn 4 Stark had a [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura), but Fabiano served anyway. When Stark blocked with [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura) and pumped it with [Burst of Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+of+Strength), Fabiano used his [Simic Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Charm) to clear it away.


Each played an [Adaptive Snapjaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Snapjaw), and Stark's ate Fabiano's [Frontline Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) on the next attack. After Fabiano added [Ivy Lane Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen), Stark preempted any +1/+1 counter action with a [Pit Fight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pit+Fight) for his fresh [Skyknight Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire).


"I guess I'm going to two-for-one myself every turn," said Stark. Without any more creatures over the next two turns, Stark fell.


Stark 0 – Fabiano 1


### Game 2


[Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) into [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) let Stark explode, chaining [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) into [Disciple of the Old Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Old+Ways). Fabiano's [Prophetic Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism) let Stark strike for four, and a [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura) on turn three put Stark in command.


Fabiano played a [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) without another creature, leaving blue and green mana ready. After thinking, Stark added an [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast) to trigger [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura)'s evolve, then attacked. [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) blocked the [Disciple of the Old Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Old+Ways), getting pumped by [Simic Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Charm). [Scab-Clan Charger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Charger) on Fabiano's turn put a sturdy blocker up, but a [Pit Fight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pit+Fight) let Stark eat it after the next combat.


Stark's [Firefist Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firefist+Striker) forced the [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) to dodge blocking, letting Fabiano's [Adaptive Snapjaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Snapjaw) trade with the [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast). With just four life left, Fabiano fell with Stark's next attack.


Stark 1 – Fabiano 1


Fabiano played first this time, leading [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) into [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest). Stark's [Gruul Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Guildgate) and [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) let him repeat his [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) into [Disciple of the Old Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Old+Ways) play, but Fabiano's third turn [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) promised to change the tides if given time.


Stark had [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) for [Sunhome Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome+Guildmage), but Fabiano's [Frilled Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus) put a sturdy blocker in the way with the mana behind needed to pump. Without a play, Stark passed back where Fabiano resolved [Syndic of Tithes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndic+of+Tithes). Passing the turn without a play again, Stark took a moment to read [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) before Fabiano's [Simic Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Keyrune) entered the battlefield.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha13/gpcharlotte-Semis-StarkFabiano-Board.jpg)  
*AIt's an evolving board state.* 
At the end of the turn, Stark created a red-white Soldier, and passed back again. Fabiano cast Leyline Phantasm, then let Stark have the turn again, content to wait for the right time to use his Manipulator. Stark started counting damage and checking life totals, a healthy 18 each, before adding another [Disciple of the Old Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Old+Ways). Fabiano used six mana to cast [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake), and elected to steal Stark's [Sunhome Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome+Guildmage) when he created another token.


The game had slowed considerably, but [Pit Fight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pit+Fight) cleared Fabiano's [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) before Stark played [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura). [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake) dropped him to 11, but Stark cast [Skyknight Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire) and left it at home. It kept Fabiano's Drake at bay, and the passing of turns to and fro began again. Breaking the silence in style, Fabiano struck in with everyone and Stark began to play with blockers.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha13/gpcharlotte-Semis-StarkFabiano-Attack.jpg)  
*Alpha strikes are terrifying to face.* 
One Disciple blocked the Guildmage, Disciple, Emissary, and a token stopped the [Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oculus), another token stopped Leyline Phantasm, and [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura) blocked [Syndic of Tithes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndic+of+Tithes).


What changed Fabiano's position? [Biomass Mutation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Biomass+Mutation), where X equals 7, allowed his team to completely demolish Stark's battlefield. Without an answer for the army Fabiano still had, his 10 life vanished the next turn.


**Stark 1 – Fabiano 2**

Quarterfinals Roundup
---------------------


*by Steve Sadin*

 

### Ben Isgur (Orzhov/Boros) vs. Patrick Sullivan (Orzhov)


"Have you ever drafted a deck, sat down assuming you were going to be in some colors, then wound up not being in those colors?" asked Ben Isgur just moments before playing a [Slate Street Ruffian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slate+Street+Ruffian) out of his solidly three color Orzhov/Boros deck.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha13/QF-Isgur-v-Sullivan.jpg)  
*Ben Isgur (left) vs Patrick Sullivan (right)* 
While Isgur managed to find all three of his colors of mana, his draw didn't have any sort of staying power – allowing Sullivan to take the first game without breaking a sweat.


Patrick Sullivan 1 – Ben Isgur 0


### Game Two


Even though the players had spent most of the first game laughing about just how poorly Isgur's deck had (apparently) turned out – Isgur was nonetheless able to demonstrate that his deck had some good things going for it, as he won the second game in a matter of minutes.


Patrick Sullivan 1 – Ben Isgur 1


### Game Three


Sullivan, fully aware of just how fragile Isgur's mana was, chose to draw first in the deciding game.


Isgur, missing the red mana that he needed to cast several of his spells, still got off to a reasonable start with a Kingpin's Pet, a [Syndicate Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndicate+Enforcer), and a [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards).


However, his draw once again lacked any sort of real staying power – allowing Sullivan to hack away at his life total with a [Dinrova Horror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dinrova+Horror), a Nav Squad Commander, and a pair of [Zarichi Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zarichi+Tiger)s.


After making some tough blocks, and playing a [Deathpact Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathpact+Angel) – Isgur was able to put together an on board lead at a mere 3 life.


However, that wouldn't last for long as Sullivan drew, and immediately cast, a game winning [Obzedat, Ghost Council](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council).


**Patrick Sullivan 2 – Ben Isgur 1**


### Richard Nguyen (Simic/Gruul) vs. Frank Skarren (Gruul)


Nguyen went to the air early with a pair of [Metropolis Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metropolis+Sprite)s, and a [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis) – but a [Mugging](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mugging) bought Skarren all the time that he needed for his [Ivy Lane Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen), and his army of plus sized Gruul creatures (including Rubbleback Raiders) to smash their way to a one game lead.


Frank Skarren 1 – Richard Nguyen 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha13/QF-Nguyen-vs-Skarren.jpg)  
*Richard Nguyen (left) vs Frank Skarren (right)* 
### Game Two


After spending much of the game trading creatures with his opponent, Skarren eventually played a [Hellkite Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellkite+Tyrant) that Nguyen simply had no answer for.


A few hits from the dragon were ultimately all it took for Skarren to advance to the Semifinals.

Top 5 Cards
-----------


*by Adam Styborski and Steve Sadin*

 

 


![Boros Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Guildgate)


 

### 5. [Boros Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Guildgate)


Boros was considered among the best archetypes upon the release of *Gatecrash*. While how many players overdraft it is up for debate, what's clear is that it's a force to be reckoned with regardless. Patrick Sullivan parlayed his love for white-red and its difficult-to-cast two drops, such as [Wojek Halberdiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wojek+Halberdiers) and [Truefire Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Truefire+Paladin), into a 14 round win streak and seat at the Top 8 draft.



 


![Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Plains)


 

### 4. [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains)


Basic lands usually aren't exciting, but at the beginning of Day 1 sleeving several of these up may have made you very happy. Only two of the nine undefeated Day 1 decks didn't play [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), and 52 [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) were in a total of 154 lands - a third of the entire mana available, including the two decks without them. Casting white spells was a good deal players didn't pass up.



 


![Syndic of Tithes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Syndic+of+Tithes)


 

### 3. [Syndic of Tithes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndic+of+Tithes)


As the Booster Draft rounds of Pro Tour *Gatecrash* made clear, Orzhov is a powerhouse in this Limited format. Between aggressive Boros decks looking to fill their two-drops out, or slightly less aggressive Orzhov decks that could capitalize on either attacking or generating extort triggers, [Syndic of Tithes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndic+of+Tithes) was in demand all weekend long.



 


![Ivy Lane Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen)


 

### 2. [Ivy Lane Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen)


Gerard Fabiano, and Frank Skarren both made great use of [Ivy Lane Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen) on their roads to the finals. Skarren was able to overpower Richard Nguyen in the quarterfinals thanks to the +1/+1 counters that his [Ivy Lane Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen) gave his (already large) Gruul creatures - and Gerard Fabiano used [Ivy Lane Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen) along with a flashed in Brambleshark to win a particularly key combat in his semifnals match.



 


![Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager)


 

### 1. [Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager)


While Gruul stayed off the radar between Sealed Deck rounds, the power of the guild was brought to bear in the final Booster Draft. Frank Skarren, packing two copies of this powerful *Gatecrash* uncommon, smashed his way through the Finals, and put red-green at the top of the heap at the end of Grand Prix Charlotte.
