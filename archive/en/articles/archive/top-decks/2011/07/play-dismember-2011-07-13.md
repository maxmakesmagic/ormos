Herr's winner from Charlestown, Massachusetts wasn't really surprising in any way; Vampires was already on the rise before the format was relieved of [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) (plus, [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) is obvious in a black deck); with Roystan's, we see what kind of music white and blue might make together now that their signature Standard cards are no more.

Back to a more traditional White-Blue Control!

Roystan's [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) can do a couple of different things... It can get a [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) lock against the opponent's nonbasics, or it can re-buy [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) or especially [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) for lots of card advantage. This deck is actually fairly reminiscent of Blue-Black Control decks we have looked at in recent months, with a ramp up to [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated), and [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) on six.

A former Daily Deck, Durward's Equipster deck looks like it will end up one of the more influential builds of the coming months. If White-Blue Control is a more obvious route in terms of what you might do with your [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade)s nowadays, Equipster espouses much of the *spirit* of Caw-Blade, even if the cards are different.

Obviously this is a deck that is still very much Equipment-driven. In fact, Caleb played fifteen different pieces of Equipment in his main deck, and more in the sideboard! There are a lot of neat things going on with this list; one of them is that many of the Equipment cards are living weapons; therefore, even though Caleb only had twelve creatures, it could play as if it had many more because [Flayer Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+Husk) and [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) double as dudes.

Another is that [Trinket Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) kind of does [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) duty in this deck. While [Trinket Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) can't find a [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) (and the deck doesn't play [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) at all), it can grab [Accorder's Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accorder%27s+Shield), [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar), [Flayer Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+Husk), or [Sylvok Lifestaff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Lifestaff), plus non-Equipment [Mox Opal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal).

In a deck so brimming with artifacts, [Dispatch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispatch) is kind of a better [Swords to Plowshares](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) or [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) (most of the time)... and yet, the deck runs [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) in the sideboard.

Part of that means sacrificing some early-game card quality in favor of "synergy." You'll notice this deck has lots of [Thrummingbird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrummingbird)s and the occasional [Lighthouse Chronologist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lighthouse+Chronologist). Successful decks in 2011 largely showcase the best available cards, deploying them with the most efficiency; if Jace and [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) taught us anything, it is that you want to be playing the best cards (no surprise there). **Ponder**

If [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) had a one-line description, maybe in a magazine interview or on a job application, it would be "the [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) good enough to play in Legacy." With [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) banned in Standard, the best card left (pre-M12) is [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain). Well, here is [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)'s opposite number, back from the departed mists that were M10!

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=244313)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=244313)  
[Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)'s immediate impact is going to be seen on the combo front. This card is going to make [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) better than ever. Who knows what the long-term implications are for the format? [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) is a better [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) given the right conditions, but will be less exciting when [Misty Rainforest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) and [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) rotate in a few months.

Whether it proves out as the number-one card in M12 may be contingent on the availability of another shuffler. If we have something better than a [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) available, [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) will yield more consistent mana on turn one, and dig harder and deeper when you are under pressure; if not, it will probably link arms with [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and contribute... but not in the wide and sometimes controversial way [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) has for the past twelve months.

**Mana Leak**

Whatever I said about [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)? It *mostly* applies to [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), as well (both cards actually took up more Top 8 space than [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)). [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) is like Standard's great, big, stop sign. Got something cool you want to do? Best make sure it can get past a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)!

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=241831)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=241831)  
[Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) is actually pretty good against most of the other big spells in the set—[Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura), [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), and so on—as well as being one of the few reasonable counterspells you can play that is actually useful against the Exarch Twin combo (at least early on in a game). It will appear in Top 8 deck lists for as long as it is legal in Standard.

**Primeval Titan**

I think I was a little bit generous to this creature (I am not 100% convinced it is better than [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise)), but I am pretty sure this is around where most players have it. [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) is clearly the most powerful of the Titan cycle, and the enabler of Mono-Green Eldrazi and [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) both. At one point it was the strongest creature in Standard, but unless [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) reasserts itself the way some pundits originally predicted, I don't know that the jolly green Giant will be able to make that claim, at least until rotations and *Innistrad* shock the system in a few months.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=241832)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=241832)  
**Day of Judgment**

The disappearance of Caw-Blade has made [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) fantastic in Standard again. We have already seen Vampires rack up some Blue Envelope action, and [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) is one of the main cards that is great against such swarm strategies. [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) is a limiting factor for the format in general, strong against Elves and a cause for pause for anyone who wants to run out lots of little guys.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=220139)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=220139)  
Plus... It's not like you can't take out big guys or just one or two decent monsters; you know, for value.

**Grim Lavamancer**

[Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) is one of my favorite returning cards. I think that coming back right now—when the Lavamancer can run in Standard next to [Arid Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) and [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) even in a straight Red deck—is such a great opportunity for red mages. This is a card that you have to respect, and have to be aware of. It is a murderer of any straggling small creatures (that matter) and a source of several [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock)s. Kill it, or it is going to help kill you.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=234428)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=234428)  
**Birds of Paradise**

One of the best one-drops ever, from the first set of the game, is back again with M12. This card continues to perform in a variety of decks, yet manages to be overlooked at times (I think it is probably the lingering memory of [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)). The most likely early [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) contribution is going to be in [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), both progressive card advantage versions and Deceiver Twin hybrids (speaking of which, [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) makes the [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) / [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo a full turn faster, while fixing your second red mana); going long it will be a staple... *somewhere,* just as it has always been.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=221896)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=221896)  
**Gideon Jura**

The only planeswalker on this list, [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) is almost unbeatable for some strategies. Even the mighty Exarch Twin combo can be held off for a turn by Gideon's "attack me" ability, and Gideon has (again!) performed as both a defender and a potential attacker since the big ban. I don't know if [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) + [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) is the best thing you can have set up in Standard right now, but it has to be close.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=238329)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=238329)  
**Oblivion Ring**

I might have wanted [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) up a bit higher in this list, but if you are going to compare two cards (say [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) versus [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring)) you have to give the nod to the active card rather than the answer. It is going to be played heavily in the coming months, but is held in check a little bit in that it is a bit clunky against super-fast / hyper-efficient beatdown creatures like [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) or [Goblin Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide). That said, [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) is a super-important addition to the Standard format; now we have a legitimate and consistent answer to planeswalkers, or any problem permanent, with relatively little design cost.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=234567)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=234567)  
**Grave Titan and Frost Titan**

I was not sure which to have rated higher; my initial sense was [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) due to the nod it has been getting in [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks and [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) decks—spots you might assume would be set aside for [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) given their base colors—but [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) got the very tight better position due to its pedigree in Blue-Black Control.

[Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) was, once upon a time, the preferred kill card in Blue-Black Control, but was replaced by the World Championships and kind of never let go; [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) perseveres there, and has lately started to see play in Mono-Black Control, et al. Both Titans are going to continue to be popular, and played in a variety of decks, yet neither is a defining enabler like their green cousin.

**Solemn Simulacrum**

[Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) is probably my favorite card in the set, and the old-new card I am most excited to see returning. Quite simply, [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) has performed at a high level across multiple formats from the moment it was first printed, racking up tournament wins in every role from splashing an extra color to getting you one step closer to some huge number of mana for a defining sorcery to sacrificing itself to deal 5 damage to the opponent's nug. I just want to sit back and see the next [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) / Kuroda-style Red / White-Blue Control that that Jens Thoren's Invitational card helps enable or bring to the next level. Really, just one of the best!

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=236907)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=236907)  
You know what's cool?

Turn four [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) (turn three with an [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice)); Titan *the very next turn*! Great acceleration potential, especially for nongreen decks. Look for this card in the 2011 era to be played in blue control decks where they might have been tapping out for [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), and acting as a combination of life gain boost (by blocking) and mini-[Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring).

**Manabarbs**

Last and least (at least for this list)—but still ahead of so many others—is [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs). I think this card will prove an important re-addition to the format. It just does so many things, none of them friendly. Once upon a time it hassled white mages with [Circle of Protection: Red](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circle+of+Protection%3A+Red) in play; today, it doesn't even have to beat a Circle.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=235193)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=235193)  
[Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs) is the kind of card you can follow an opponent's [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) with, or use to hold off a massive mana machine, like the operation of [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade)s or intimidating down the 7-life investment of a [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated). One of the things I look forward to most is the stories... Blue mages love recounting how they beat a [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs), because it happens relatively seldom. With so many of the best cards in the format boasting five or six up in the right corner, you know [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs) is going to have an impact, forcing opponents to think twice or face the consequences. Early prediction: "the sixth Titan," [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)—the one with lifelink—gets better relative to in-color Titans... just because [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs) exists again.

That's my Top 12 M12 list... what do you think?
