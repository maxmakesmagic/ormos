It's Halloween week here at Building on a Budget, and you know what that means!


October 31st, 2005: JMS, “[Selesnya United: Tokens on my Table](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/jm124)”  

October 29th, 2004: Nate Heiss, “[The 10 Best Budget Cards of all Time](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/nh38)”  

October 27th, 2003: Nate Heiss, “[Budget Control](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=magic/magiconline/articlenh102703)”


That's right – absolutely nothing! For better or for worse, neither of my predecessors really liked to give out any sort of treats for Halloween. What a curmudgeonly pair! The thought of depriving the neighborhood youts of boxes of raisins and penny-jars fills me with the very essence of Juffo-Wup (10 points if you get this reference).


I've been itching to get my hands on *Time Spiral*, but it hasn't been made available online yet. This will likely be the same for next year as well, given the release dates of sets versus the release date of Halloween. To spice things up, we're going to start a new tradition:


### Trick or Treat: A BoaB Halloween


Each year for Halloween, I'm going to drop everything else going on in this column and build a deck that is completely out there. This Halloween's deck? Five-Color Highlander Standard.


Definitions time!


**Five-Color**: A deck which contains all five colors – black, blue, green, red and white.  
**Highlander**: Aside from basic lands, there can only be one of any given card in the deck.  
**Standard**: Online, *Kamigawa* Block, *Ravnica* Block, *9th Edition* and *Coldsnap* are currently Standard legal. For reasons that you'll see in a moment, I only used *Ravnica*, *Guildpact*, *Dissension* and *Coldsnap* for this deck. But you knew that already – you read the nifty new boilerplate at the top of this article!


As many of you know, I wrote a very different column on **magicthegathering.com** before I wrote Building on a Budget. That column was [Uncommon Knowledge](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/columnarchive&column=UncommonKnowledge), and it dealt with a study of **Magic's** history. One article in particular points towards my love of form: the “[Sets of Five](/en/articles/archive/sets-five-2002-12-25)” piece. In that article, I dissected all of the cycles in **Magic's** history (up until the point of *Onslaught* – remember, that article was written in 2002). I took that article as an inspiration to build this week's deck.

82 Cards 




![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Garrison)


















You'll notice there are 82 cards in this version of the deck. These are the cards I initially considered running in this Highlander deck. They include the following:


* One of each basic land, and one of each Snow-Covered basic land: Technically, you can run any number of a basic land in Highlander. I wanted to take this one step further, and so I made it one of any card, period. Thank you, Snow-Covered lands, for making this possible!
* The ten *Ravnica* bouncelands: Initially, I was worried about running this many bouncelands, since they are slow in these quantities. This fear was unfounded – they allowed me to run a sixty card deck with only twenty lands and barely face any mana screw issues.
* The ten *Ravnica* Guildmages: Each of these guys is 2/2 for two with two special abilities, and so they form the backbone of my offense.
* The twenty *Ravnica* cross-over mana spells: These are [Boros Fury-Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield), [Ribbons of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ribbons+of+Night), [Plaxmanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxmanta), [Squealing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squealing+Devil), [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar), [Azorius Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald), [Steamcore Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird), [Shrieking Grotesque](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrieking+Grotesque), [Ogre Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Savant), [Patagia Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patagia+Viper), [Tin Street Hooligan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tin+Street+Hooligan), [Rolling Spoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Spoil), [Seed Spark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seed+Spark), [Crypt Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Champion), [Flash Conscription](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash+Conscription), [Revenant Patriarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revenant+Patriarch), [Vigor Mortis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigor+Mortis), [Induce Paranoia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Induce+Paranoia), [Dryad's Caress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad%27s+Caress), and [Gruul Scrapper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Scrapper).
* The five uncommon *Dissension* split cards

I also included a bunch of creatures and spells that have a higher power level or a comes-into-play effect – cards like [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix), [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze), [Keening Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Banshee), [Civic Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder), and such.


I needed to cut out cards, and I decided to stick as closely as possible to the cycles theme. However, there were too many slots dedicated to cross-over mana spells – twenty is a lot of spots in any deck! In the end, I cut seven cards from that cycle ([Crypt Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Champion), [Flash Conscription](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash+Conscription), [Revenant Patriarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revenant+Patriarch), [Vigor Mortis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigor+Mortis), [Induce Paranoia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Induce+Paranoia), [Dryad's Caress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad%27s+Caress), and [Gruul Scrapper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Scrapper)). I also cut several comes-into-play creatures ([Carven Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carven+Caryatid), [Indrik Stomphowler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indrik+Stomphowler), [Keening Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Banshee), [Stalking Yeti](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Yeti), [Vedalken Dismisser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser)), several good creatures ([Blizzard Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blizzard+Specter), [Gelectrode](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gelectrode), [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator)), some removal ([Condemn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn), [Faith's Fetters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters), [Last Gasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp)), and a handful of utility spells ([Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research), [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), [Into the North](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+North), [Recollect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recollect)). This left me with the following sixty-card Highlander Standard deck, titled “There Can Only Be Fun!”

**Game 1: Odin51085 (R/G Aurochs)**


I get turn-three [Spectral Searchlight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Searchlight) and follow it with turn 4 [Selesnya Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildmage). He drops [Earthen Goo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthen+Goo) on turn three, and I [Ribbons of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ribbons+of+Night) it on turn five to get myself back up to nineteen life. He plays [Sheltering Ancient](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sheltering+Ancient), swings in for five the next turn, and then drops [Stalking Yeti](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Yeti) the turn after. I respond by playing [Plaxmanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxmanta), countering his effect. He drops [Aurochs Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurochs+Herd), lets his [Sheltering Ancient](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sheltering+Ancient) die before it can make all my guys larger than his, and then swings. I double block with my 3/3 Guildmage and 2/2 [Plaxmanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxmanta), and he casts [Resize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resize) on his Aurochs. I neuter that with [Boros Fury-Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield).


I clear the board the next turn with [Savage Twister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Twister) for four and then drop [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) and [Patagia Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patagia+Viper) on turn eight to take momentum. *Record: 1-0*

The Grotesque blocks a [Blind Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Hunter), and I drop both [Azorius Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage) and [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) to lock down his board. *Record: 2-0*

I don't get aggressive enough with my [Azorius Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald) and [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), and I lose the turn before I kill him. He had [Juniper Order Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juniper+Order+Ranger), double [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk), [Twilight Drover](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twilight+Drover), and [Carven Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carven+Caryatid). I should have kept killing his guys in response to their [Juniper Order Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juniper+Order+Ranger) trigger, but I kept back for too long.


*Record: 5-2*


**Game 8: Pure Grit (U/G Simic)**


I get [Azorius Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage), a ton of signets, and [Gruul Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Guildmage), and I hit eight mana before he can get his blue mana up. By the time he gets an Island, I'm swinging with two guys who can tap down his defense and then become 8/8.


*Record: 6-2*


 



![Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Court+Hussar)

**Game 9: HELIOS CREED (U/G Simic)**
I get [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) into [Hit // Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run), [Mark of Eviction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+Eviction) and [Azorius Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage). I keep the [Mark of Eviction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+Eviction), using it to bounce my [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar). He drops [Plaxcaster Frogling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling) and [Assault Zeppelid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Zeppelid). I try to block, but he casts double [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) to wipe out most of my team. I drop [Patagia Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patagia+Viper) to try to stabilize and then draw into twin removal spells ([Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) and [Mortify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify)). He accidentally runs his [Plaxcaster Frogling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling) into a goblin token, courtesy of [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage). (This happens more than once over the course of this article – people seem to forget the [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) has an ability other than giving a creature -2/-2!) After I clear out his board, he concedes.


*Record: 7-2*


**Game 10: tfiggink314 (Blue Snow Aggro)**


He gets turn-one [Martyr of Frost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Frost) and turn-two [Ninja of the Deep Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours). Thankfully, I'm on the play and have [Mortify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify) to kill his Ninja. He repeats this sequence on turn four, and this time he has [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) for my [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy). I drop [Azorius Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage) and keep his Ninja tapped down. He casts [Rimewind Taskmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rimewind+Taskmage), and I drop [Selesnya Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildmage). We sit there at an impasse for a while, until he drops [Phyrexian Ironfoot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Ironfoot). This leaves him with one mana open, and I [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) his Taskmage at end of turn and then [Tin Street Hooligan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tin+Street+Hooligan) his artifact creature. This gives me total and absolute control of the board, which the Blue Snow Aggro deck cannot deal with (I built the deck – I know what it loses against, i.e., anything that hits the board and stays there). Without an out, tfiggink314 concedes.


*Record: 8-2*


As a note, Blue Snow Aggro really needs a bounce effect of some sort, so it's not helpless against the board. I've come to realize this in retrospect, and I would definitely add in something like four [Wipe Away](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wipe+Away) post-Time Spiral to the deck.


**Game 11: heyhowyoudoin123 (R/G/B Damage-Dealing)**


He gets [Akki Lavarunner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akki+Lavarunner) and then [Coalhauler Swine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalhauler+Swine). I kill and bounce everything, get in there with Guildmages and [Azorius Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald), and don't face much other resistance.


*Record: 9-2*


I'm still having a little trouble with mana, but in this case it's getting the right colors at the right time. Green searchers can help this, so I add in a couple to smooth out the mana base. In addition, I add in [Stalking Yeti](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Yeti) – now that I can search out Snow-Covered lands easily, he's too good to turn down. I take out three of the cross-mana cards that I've used the least – [Squealing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squealing+Devil) (always stuck in hand), [Tin Street Hooligan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tin+Street+Hooligan) (the Blue Snow Aggro deck is the only deck I've faced with artifacts) and [Boros Fury-Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield) (sort of clunky).

**Game 12: Seraz (4-Color Snow Reanimator)**


He gets lots of fatties in his graveyard but can't draw reanimation. I get [Patagia Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patagia+Viper), [Civic Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder) and [Boros Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Guildmage), and he drops [Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp). I bide my time, with a hand of [Supply // Demand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supply+%2F%2F+Demand), [Trial // Error](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trial+%2F%2F+Error), [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy), and [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze). When he casts [Sisters of Stone Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sisters+of+Stone+Death), I kill it with [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy), [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) his [Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp), and swarm him. He tries to get a last minute [Rimefeather Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rimefeather+Owl) on the board (12/12 to boot!), but I'm at 20 life, he's at 2, and he can't deal with all my creatures.


*Record: 10-2*


**Game 13: Flipsix (U/R Izzet)**


He drops [Izzet Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Guildmage), and I immediately [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) it. He drops [Wee Dragonauts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wee+Dragonauts) two turns in a row, and I massacre them with [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) and [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix). I've found that, against this style of Izzet deck, it's best to kill creatures the second they hit the board. That way, the Izzet mage won't get double use out of his spells (as a creature pumper/duplication outlet/[Gelectrode](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gelectrode) untapper), and won't be able to mount an offense. This is exactly the case, and he begins to throw burn at my head. I show him [Selesnya Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildmage), and suddenly his Shocks and [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer)s are aimed at my creatures instead.


 



![Niv-Mizzet, the Firemind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+the+Firemind)

I get an offense going, finally, by casting [Seed Spark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seed+Spark) on my own Searchlight and then dropping [Patagia Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patagia+Viper). He plays Niv-Mizzet, and I bounce it with [Ogre Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Savant). In response, he tries to [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) most of my team away, but I counter it with [Trial // Error](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trial+%2F%2F+Error). He redrops Niv-Mizzet, and I kill it with [Ribbons of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ribbons+of+Night). That pretty much ends the game, as he can't deal with all of my token creatures.

*Record: 11-2*

**Game 15: 53N531 (U/B Ninjas)**


He gets early beats left and right, thanks to [Dimir Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Infiltrator), [Ronin Warclub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ronin+Warclub), and [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter). I wreck him with a well-timed [Seed Spark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seed+Spark) on his [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and manage to stabilize the board. He drops [Higure, the Still Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Higure%2C+the+Still+Wind), and I attack him down to 6. He uses Higure to get [Ninja of the Deep Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours) and taps out to play that Ninja. I end up attacking him down to 1 and killing him with [Spectral Searchlight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Searchlight). He (and I) both realize immediately that if he had gotten [Mistblade Shinobi](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistblade+Shinobi) instead, he would have been able to use Higure as a mana sink, and ended the game at 1 life – so this game could have been a loss, but I'll take it as a win. ;)


*Record: 13-2*


This deck outperformed expectations in every way imaginable. I didn't expect to go 13-2 with a highlander utility deck, especially one that was more themed than constructed at first, and especially not when *every game I played was against a regular Standard legal deck.* That's right – trick or treat! This deck held its own against non-highlander decks and was a blast to play. Every game played out differently, and I always had a ton of options during gameplay. There Can Only Be Fun gets the A+ recommendation seal of Building on a Budget approval.
