Crazy Unglue
============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 19, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_240.png)
By Mark L. Gottlieb











The only set specifically designed for casual play, *Unglued* has some intriguing mechanics that will surely alter the course of a typical game—yet, a bit surprisingly, it doesn't have that much to offer in the way of combo pieces. A careful examination of the set reveals a few hidden gems, though, including one infinite damage loop—


What?


*What?*


Oh, I see how it is. I'm supposed to be *funny*. “*Unglued* is the funny set. Mark is the funny columnist. He'll be funny today. Dance, little monkey, dance!” Forget it, bubsy. It doesn't work like that. I was funny [last week](/en/articles/archive/extra-pulp-2004-08-12). I was funny, uh, I don't know, a few months before that. I don't like being told when to turn on the humor hose and let the pure, burbling comedy splash out onto the bikini carwash that is this website. I write humor *when* I want, *where* I want, *who* I want. Period. The end. So there.


What was my point?


### Chick Flick


Let's start with an *Unglued*y deck. This deck is so *Unglued*, it's practically falling apart at the seams. About a year and a half ago, I was in a casual multiplayer game with a green-black hodgepodge of powerful cards and sacrifice effects. I had just gotten out [Avatar of Woe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Woe) and I was about to start ownzoring the table… until Peter, the player to my right, whipped out [Fowl Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fowl+Play) and turned the Avatar into a Chicken! Before: Fear-enabled 6/5 assassin-at-will. After: 1/1 Chicken. The whole table, including me, had a good laugh. (For the purposes of this story, full-on blown-gasket rants count as laughs.) I got my revenge later (or earlier, I can't remember) when Peter brought out a [Chaos Confetti](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Confetti) and tried to shred a proxy Plains instead of the *Unglued* card itself. That flies in the face of everything [Chaos Confetti](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Confetti) stands for, and I absolutely wouldn't allow it. You've gotta take the ung with the lued, you know what I mean?


What was my point?


Oh, right, Chickens. *Unglued* doesn't just have a chicken theme… It has a Chicken deck sitting in there. The ruler of the flock is [Chicken a la King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chicken+a+la+King), the molting monarch—the rooster among roasters—that permanently pumps up all your Chickens as long as you roll enough dice to hit 6's. We've got to roll dice, huh? That can be arranged. The green Chicken and the red Chicken-to-be in the set both incorporate automatic dice rolling, as do [Growth Spurt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spurt); [Urza's Science Fair Project](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Science+Fair+Project); [Strategy, Schmategy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strategy%2C+Schmategy); and [Goblin Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Tutor). The Tutor needs a range of card types to hunt for, so that's another check in the “yes” column for [Growth Spurt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spurt) (an instant) and the Science Fair Project (an artifact). [Strategy, Schmategy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strategy%2C+Schmategy) is there just because it's there. It's probably bad for you to play it as this deck wants to build up permanent count, so it really hurts the deck's strategy… and that seems perfect.


The whole point of this deck is to hatch some Chickens and then roll 6's. So it only needs two more things: More Chickens, and more 6's. Simple. Extra 6's can be accomplished by rerolling dice that aren't 6's. Like 3's. Let's face it—3's are half as good as 6's, so Clam-I-Am will help those 3's try to improve their lot in life. Goblin Bookie can take care of the rest. And more Chickens? I already mentioned [Clam-I-Am](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clam-I-Am) and [Goblin Bookie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bookie), right? [Unnatural Selection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unnatural+Selection) will Chickenify both of those puppies and the Science Fair Project too. [Mistform Dreamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Dreamer), [Mistform Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Wall), and [Proteus Machine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proteus+Machine) will Chickenify themselves. [Riptide Replicator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Replicator) clucks out even more Chickens, and [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) starts out ahead of the game as the game's only black-bordered Chicken.

### Making Contacts


I actually don't play with *Unglued* that much. I prefer to make [my own Magic humor](/en/articles/archive/breaking-darksteel-2004-01-29) by turning [Wirefly Hive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirefly+Hive) into a [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) machine or cracking open a [Summoner's Egg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoner%27s+Egg) I've given to my opponent and revealing the Phage yolk [inside](/en/articles/archive/cruel-and-unusual-2004-06-03). But I do enjoy the cards when they pop up at appropriate times. For example, [the wackiest format of all](/en/articles/archive/reject-rare-draft-2-2004-03-18), Reject Rare Draft can only be enhanced by the couple of *Unglued* cards like [Strategy, Schmategy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strategy%2C+Schmategy) or [Incoming!](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incoming%21) that always seem to sneak in. In the first RRD that I ran here at Wizards, Richard Garfield did obscene things with [Incoming!](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incoming%21), [Angelic Chorus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Chorus), and the fattiest fatties he could draft.


There are certainly combos to be had with this set—not all *Unglued* cards are based on who is or isn't wearing pants. [Incoming!](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incoming%21) can do simple things ([Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) & B.F.M.) or complex things (Summoning [Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Station), [Arcum's Weathervane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcum%27s+Weathervane),… and I'll leave the rest to your imagination). I've explored the infamous [Ashnod's Coupon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashnod%27s+Coupon) combo that Simon Sandlake (aka Pyftig Jork) sent in—you can find the setup [here](/en/articles/archive/attack-bombos-2004-04-15) and the solution [here](/en/articles/archive/triple-helix-2004-04-22). Although it's slowed to a trickle now, I still get emails from people excavating the archives who stumble on the bombo article and suggest the answer to this befuddling conundrum… despite the fact that the solution was posted in the very next article.


Back in October, Kenn Mikos mailed in the first combo to break [Urza's Contact Lenses](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Contact+Lenses): [Urza's Contact Lenses](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Contact+Lenses) + [Lodestone Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Myr)! As long as your arms don't fall off, the Contact Lenses can pump [Lodestone Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Myr) up infinitely. +8,000,000/+8,000,000 from a 0-mana artifact… seems pretty good. The free untaps turn [Clock of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clock+of+Omens)'s ability into “Tap an artifact: Untap target artifact,” which can be silly. But let's think about this. The “Clapper” ability on the Contact Lenses was utterly pointless at the time of *Unglued* (which Mark Rosewater was the lead designer of) and it stayed useless until [Lodestone Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Myr) in *Mirrodin* (which Mark Rosewater was the lead designer of) and [Clock of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clock+of+Omens) in *Fifth Dawn* (which Mark Rosewater was the lead designer of). Hmmm. Let me reiterate: *Hmmmmmm.* Did Mark just pull off what I think he did? Was he seeding combo pieces in his niche set six years ago? Oh, it gets better—the Contact Lenses are a cog, so they're fetchable with [Artificer's Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificer%27s+Intuition) or [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) (also *Fifth Dawn* cards). Amazing.

### Sensible Censorship


I did mention something at the top about an infinite damage loop, right? I wasn't fooling around about that. *Unglued* is not a fooling-around kind of set. What combos exist in here? [Gerrymandering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gerrymandering) + [Zuran Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Orb) (or [Overgrown Estate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Estate)) steals half your opponent's lands, [Free-For-All](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Free-For-All) + Phage is gamblin' man's way to end the game, and [The Cheese Stands Alone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Cheese+Stands+Alone) + [Decree of Annihilation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Annihilation) is an instant win. (You can also put [The Cheese Stands Alone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Cheese+Stands+Alone) on the stack and respond to it with [Kaervek's Spite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaervek%27s+Spite) for the same result.) But those are too tame, and none of them is an infinite damage loop. Check out this insanity:


  
*Censorship (set to “Ow”) + Ow + Opalescence = sudden death overtime*
Finally, [Opalescence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence) finds a good home in a combo deck. With this combo on the table, the next player to be damaged by a creature will (barring something silly like [Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship)) lose the game. It doesn't matter if that player has a million life. In fact, it's *better* if that player has a million life. Feel free to spot your opponent a million life if he lets you go first.


How does this work? The combo is out on the table. I damage you with a creature. [Ow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ow) triggers. If you say “ow,” [Censorship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Censorship) will trigger and deal 2 damage to you—and since [Censorship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Censorship) is a creature thanks to [Opalescence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence), [Ow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ow) will trigger again. Go back to the top of the loop! If, on the other hand, you *don't* say “ow” when you're damaged, [Ow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ow) itself will deal 1 damage to you—and since [Ow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ow) is a creature thanks to [Opalescence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opalescence), [Ow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ow) will trigger again. Go back to the top of the loop!


Finally, Opalescence finds a good home in a combo deck.Of course, the deck can easily backfire—it works against *you* as well. Don't assemble the combo if your opponent has an active [Goblin Sharpshooter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter)! Once the combo is active, you can't even inadvertently say “ow” or [Censorship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Censorship) will start the loop and you'll lose! If your opponent hits you with a shovel (check Aaron's column tomorrow), I strongly suggest you say “ouch” or simply weep in silent dignity.


But the nuttiness doesn't end there. See, in order to win, the deck needs a way to deal damage with a creature while also preventing you from being hurt. [Samite Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Samite+Archer) and [Stinging Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinging+Barrier) both fit the bill. One ping sets the loop in motion. But pinging with either of those creatures isn't nearly as fun as attacking with your animated, unblockable [Ow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ow)! (After hours of careful consideration, I chose [Infiltrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infiltrate) over both [Teleport](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teleport) and [Trailblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trailblazer) as the means to grant unblockability.) For a more “What the hell is going on?” experience, I recommend [Bureaucracy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bureaucracy) (also settable with a “Say 'ow'” command) and [Sorry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorry) (which can interact with [Censorship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Censorship) and [Bureaucracy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bureaucracy) if you don't have the “ow” thing going on). The rest of the deck is made of boring good cards that help you find and protect the combo. Yawn.
