Innistrad in Italy Sees Ricci Run Rampant
=========================================




October 8-9, 2011












Grand Prix Milan 2011
---------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/winner_618.jpg)
It was no horror show in Milan this weekend, as almost 1800 players delivered an epic two days of *Innistrad*  battles. [Pitchfork](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchfork) s and [Unruly Mob](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unruly+Mob) s abounded, hackles rose, and howls filled the air. And that was just the players.


The home team of Italy was well represented in the Top 8 after 16 rounds of Sealed Deck and Booster Draft action had seen 1,780 players fall by the wayside. While Davide Vergoni and Marco Ricci had dreams of making a name for themselves, both National Champion Marcello Calvetto and Pro Tour Philadelphia Champion Samuele Estratti had already proven themselves over and over against the best.


Opposing the four Italians were Michael Milis of Belgium, Germany's Jörg Unfried, and Alexandru Dimitriu from Romania, by our calculation becoming the first ever Top 8 representative from that country. Then there was the man on a mission, the French Hall of Famer Raphael Levy. Thirteen years since his first Grand Prix victory in Lyon, Levy was back in the Top 8 spotlight, after a barren spell of three years since his previous (and 16th) GP Top 8 in Manila back in 2008.


Estratti and Levy set up a mouthwatering semifinal clash, but the match was comfortable for the Frenchman. In the other half, Italy was guaranteed a finalist when Ricci and Vergoni went toe to toe, with Ricci emerging to tangle with the Hall of Famer in the final.


In the end, the Italians proved once again that they knew how to defend their home turf and **Magic**  honor. It was Ricci who emerged triumphant, clenching his fists in triumph at the moment of victory, while Levy added yet another chapter to his storied career.


*Innistrad*  proved a huge hit this weekend, with a powerful and deep Limited format eagerly explored. The new double-faced cards added an entertaining and strategic extra layer to draft calculations, while Sealed Deck saw classics such as curve, tempo, and bombs vying for the top slot. As for colors, green-white seemed very popular, with red splashes everywhere, fuelled by cards like [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto+)  and [Traveler's Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveler%27s+Amulet) . Flyers also came out to party in a big way, giving a healthy uplift to fans of cards with wings.


Next stop in the global cavalcade of card carnage is Grand Prix Brisbane on the other side of the world, with the downhill run to worlds coming thick and fast. Santiago, Amsterdam, Hiroshima, San Diego...then it's the big one in San Francisco. For now, though, Marco Ricci is on top of the **Magic**  world. Even in a world of horror, Good can triumph!


Congratulations to Marco Ricci, Grand Prix Milan 2011 Champion!

Top 8 - Draft Round-Up
----------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 

From a field of 1,788 players there were only 8 left for the final draft of the day. The format hadn't changed, and each player had already shown themselves to be adept in this burgeoning new format. Would players stick with archetypes that had performed for them in the Swiss portion of the event, or would they strike out into new territory in the final push for the title of champion?


Rather than follow any one player throughout the draft, I was watching the shape of the draft as a whole. What signals or picks pushed players into colours or archetypes, and immediately following the draft, were there any firm favourites, or hopeful underdogs?


The first point to note from the draft was that for all three packs, the flip cards were underwhelming. Nobody was windmill slamming a first pick flip card into their pile, meaning that any signalling going on with flip cards was inevitably more subtle. Of the flip cards that came out, there were also noticeably fewer green werewolves than I've seen in most drafts, meaning that one archetype was sparsely populated.


This isn't to say that there weren't some powerful opens though. [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) was a clear first pick for Alexandrou Dimitriu, who gradually moved into green black to support his planeswalker. For Marco Ricci, it was [Angelic Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Overseer) , which was soon followed by [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) that cemented him in green/white humans.



![Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)
![Angelic Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Angelic+Overseer)

A [Divine Reckoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Reckoning) for Michael Milis of Belgium seemed a nice one, but the black/white deck he found himself with at the end of the draft did not seem abundantly powerful. For Marcello Calvetto, a Blue/Green concoction sporting a [Mindshrieker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindshrieker) and some [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) seemed potentially powerful, though it was rather heavy on the three drop.



![Divine Reckoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Divine+Reckoning)
![Mindshrieker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mindshrieker)

Raphael Levy had a difficult decision around a rare in pack three. Already firmly in Red Blue, and quite aggressive, he had to choose between [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger) and [Balefire Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balefire+Dragon) . The rare is a lot slower and more expensive than the dagger, and with many good humans to give the dagger, it was a tough choice for him. He picked the rare, but this remained the pick he was least sure about in the whole draft.



![Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger)
![Balefire Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Balefire+Dragon)

Samuele Estratti's deck looked... a little complicated. An esper deck that for a long time in deck construction had three copies of [Curse of the Bloody Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+the+Bloody+Tome) in the mix, it was the one I was least able to get a firm grasp on while watching the draft pan out. I am, it must be said though, not a Pro Tour champion, so I could have been missing something.


Jorg Unfried was more to the point, and he certainly had some power. With [Angelic Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Overseer) and [Elder of Laurels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+of+Laurels) among others, his deck certainly had some power to work with.



![Angelic Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Angelic+Overseer)
![Elder of Laurels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elder+of+Laurels)

Finally we have David Vergoni. His deck was more notable for its ticks than its creatures. Triple [Spidery Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidery+Grasp) , plus some [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations) could lead to difficult board states to deal with, perhaps making his deck a dark horse for the final three rounds of competition.

Quarterfinal - Samuele Estratti vs. Michael Milis
-------------------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon* 

Philadelphia, USA. 2011. Pro Tour. Winner: Samuele Estratti.


Milan, Italy. 2011. Grand Prix. Winner: Samuele Estratti???


Standing in the path of a historic double is the Belgian player Michael Milis. That much is certain. It's also possible that standing in the way is Estratti's own deck. It's certainly among the more interesting concoctions we've seen this weekend, featuring no fewer than three copies of [Curse of the Bloody Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+the+Bloody+Tome) . Maybe Milis is going to die while he doesn't die at all...


### Game One


Milis began the match with a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) , while Estratti had the excellent turn one of [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) , the 1/1 Deathtouch. Quarterfinal: Unleash the Rage - Raphael Levy vs. Alexandru Dimitriu
--------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 

"It has really been 3 years since my last GP top 8?" Hall of Famer Raphael Levy seemed a little stunned as he waited to hear the official top 8 announcement. A quick internet search confirmed this to be the case. Miraculously, in spite of having made far more top 8's in limited events, Raph's three GP wins all come in constructed. This is something he hoped to change over the next three rounds.


While in the swiss portion of the event, Levy had drafted blue/black, an archetype championed by Martin Juza as the best plan for the format, in the final draft of the day he found himself red/blue and quite aggressive. His opponent, Alexandru Dimitriu of Romania, was a slightly slower green/black build, with plenty of power, topping out at [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) – the first pick that had sent him firmly into black as a colour.


### Game One


Dimitriu was on the play, but it was Raph who started things out, first with a [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) , and then with a [Village Ironsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Ironsmith) . The green/black control deck of Dimitriu was slower, but managed to put the breaks on Levy's start with a [Grave Bramble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Bramble) . Levy had a little chuckle. He didn't have a play for the turn, and when an [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul) came out, things looked even worse for the Frenchman.


Levy had to do something to keep his aggression up, as green black seemed likely to be able to power out sizeable threats given appropriate time. He used a [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) on Abbatoir Ghoul, and then combined an [Ironfang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ironfang) attack with [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) to take down [Grave Bramble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Bramble) .


Onto an empty board Dimitriu played [Hollowhenge Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollowhenge+Scavenger) . That would hold off the ground pounders, but do little against a freshly cast [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows) from Levy. A [Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) was the play from Dimitriu. If Levy was going to filter through his deck every time a creature died, Dimitriu was going to get a hefty monster.


Levy was not about to let his creatures die yet though. He attacked in the air unimpeded with his crows, and then cast a [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) , continuing his bird themed beatdown plan. Dimitriu cast an [Ulvenwald Mystics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Mystics) , which flipped at the first opportunity when Raph simply attacked with his flyers and passed. Dimitriu was on just 8 to Levy's 20, and the Frenchman did not seem worried. He had 7 points of damage in play and a [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) in the graveyard. The [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) took Dimitriu to seven, but a [Victim of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) on [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows) bought Dimitriu a little more time.


Levy could have drawn a spell on any turn after turn one and his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) would have flipped to give him the game. The spell did not come though, and Levy was forced to sweat it out, attacking with [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) to gradually whittle his opponent down. The whittling worked though, and Levy took game one.


Raphael Levy 1 – 0 Alexandru Dimitriu

### Game Two


Dimitriu chose to go first in game two, and this time he had a one drop in the form of [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) . He followed up with [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse) , very much the beatdown for game two. At the end of Dimitriu's second turn, Raph used [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) , setting up for a [Lantern Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Spirit) on turn three. Dimitriu was stuck on two lands, but had more to fight with, having an end of turn [Ambush Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Viper) that Levy seemed fine with trading with.


After a little thought on his turn, Levy cast a [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut) . It seemed a risky proposition in the face of [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) , that could easily trade with it. Dimitriu, meanwhile had found his third land, and then cast [Caravan Vigil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caravan+Vigil) to bring it up to four.


Levy cast a [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows) before attacking with [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut) . A [Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) from Dimitriu would need some time to become large, while [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows) was big enough to put a dent in Dimitriu's life total from the very beginning. It swung in, beginning to make up for the early beatdown Dimitriu had achieved. The Romanian was still able to attack Levy to eight, and when he tried to keep the pressure up with an [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul) , he was thwarted by a [Lost in the Mist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+the+Mist) which also bounced his 2/2 zombie.


Raph tried for the deathblow, in casting [Balefire Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balefire+Dragon) , but it was swiftly despatched by a Victim of the Night. He was on 6 to his opponent's 12, and had to content himself with getting stuck in for just four before casting an [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) . The life totals were 6 to 8. This would be close.


Dimitriu rumbled in for four, and Levy did not block with his [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) . A [Spidery Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidery+Grasp) was just enough to get the Hall of Famer, and forced a deciding third game. A small, but enthusiastic, Romanian crowd went wild.

### Game Three


Levy was on the play for the rubber game, and looked around to see that his was the last quarterfinal going. While his games had been fairly fast, it seemed that everyone else's had been even faster.

He didn't have a creature for the first couple of turns, but did have a [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) ready for the end of Dimitriu's second turn, which had only seen a [Cobbled Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobbled+Wings) . Dimitriu had a [Grave Bramble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Bramble) but no offence, while Raph seemed content to flash back his [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) and see what his opponent could bring.


An [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul) gave Levy pause. He killed it at the end of turn with [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) , and continued to pass on his own turn, potentially representing countermagic. A second [Grave Bramble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Bramble) was not about to be stopped by Levy, who simply had more copies of [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) to cast, and then flash back. Levy's first creature of the game was a [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) , and while he was tapped out Dimitriu cast both a [Darkthicket Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkthicket+Wolf) and a [Caravan Vigil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caravan+Vigil) .

Dimitriu took 4 from [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) s, and then took another 4 when his walls blocked and killed Sellhoff Occultist. Semifinal - Samuele Estratti vs. Raphael Levy

Now this is a clash to get excited about. In one corner, a Frenchman with a gargantuan history in the game. A Hall of Fame ring. Multiple Grand Prix titles spanning the globe. Three Pro Tour top 8s. In the other, the reigning Pro Tour Champion, on home soil, looking to keep the title right here.

[Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonds+of+Faith) prevented a stern kicking from the [Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juggernaut) , Levy 'replacing' the [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Galvanic%2BJuggernaut) with [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) , the 3/2 flyer. [Moan of the Unhallowed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moan+of+the+Unhallowed) created two 2/2 Zombies for Estratti, with Lev ending the turn with [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) .

[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) was next for the Frenchman, whose [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) inflicted the first damage of the match. An attack and a Rage Igniter came from Ricci. Levy had used a Rage Igniter of his own to good effect in the quarter-finals, and didn't seem too happy to see one on the other side of the board, even if it did mean that his opponent was out of cards in hand.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/Finals%20Marco%20Ricci.jpg)
*Marco Ricci*

Levy had to attack with [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut) , it was just a question if any of the rest of his team was coming in. He elected to also let the [Lantern Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Spirit) do some damage, making the life totals 11 each. With six lands, Gavony Township was now active, and Ricci thought a little about what to do for his turn. He elected to equip his [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) token, making it a 3/4 and swung. He then moved [Mask of Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+Avacyn) to [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) , before passing and watching Raph flash back [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) , looking for something to turn the game around. Raph attacked for two before bouncing and recasting his [Lantern Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Spirit) . An expensive form of vigilance, but in this case an effective one.


Ricci played and used a [Traveler's Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveler%27s+Amulet) finding a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) to play, meaning his [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) was still active, and his deck was a little thinner. An attack from [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) , [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) and the [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) Token looked pretty strong, and would force a block from Levy. Given the amount of damage [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) represented from creatures dying, and the creatures themselves represented if left unblocked, Raph was in a very rough spot, and looked for a while for the best block, while counting up in French.


Eventually he settled on blocking [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) with [Lantern Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Spirit) and [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) with [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) . The Sprite was bounced before damage, and Ricci used his Township. Raph ended the attack on just three life, and had to find something pretty amazing with a flashed back [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) . He didn't find what he needed, and had to extend his hand.


As Ricci took it, he won the game, and the match, to be Grand Prix Milan 2011 Champion!




Top 5 Cards
-----------


Grand Prix Milan 2011 is in the books, but the cards that defined the tournament will stay with us. Whenever there's *Innistrad* Sealed Deck or a Booster Draft going on, the lessons learned this weekend will be worthwile to keep in mind. Milan was the first premiere event to showcase *Innistrad*, many others will follow. These are some of the key cards that shaped the tournament:



![Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shimmering+Grotto)

### [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto)


The land appeared on a number of pros' lists of most important commons. And for good reason: between off-color flashback costs and removal spells with low color requirements, most people like to splash. More so in Sealed than in Draft, but the card made it into three of the Top 8 draft decks as well.



![Burning Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Burning+Vengeance)

### [Burning Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Vengeance)


Among a number of build-around-me cards, [Burning Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Vengeance) proved to be the one with the most devastating effect. Point in case: on his way to the Top 8, eventual champion Marco Ricci used it as the centerpiece of a draft deck with which he went 3-0. Even relatively harmless flashback spells, like [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) or [Nightbird's Clutches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightbird%27s+Clutches) , become very good cards, if only you have enough copies of [Burning Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Vengeance) . (The highest number we ever saw on the battlefield at any one time was three.)



![Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Travel+Preparations)

### [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations)


Two-drop, three-drop, and on turn four [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations) and [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations) flashback ... Many a game this weekend was quickly lost to this start. One of the fans of the aggressive green-white archetype is road warrior Shuhei Nakamura. He's done his travel preparations.



![Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Villagers+of+Estwald)
![Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Howlpack+of+Estwald)

### [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) // [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald)


The most basic of all the Werewolves appears on this list as a testament to the importance of double-faced cards in general. The flavorful representation of familiar horror tropes just works so much better on two sides than on one. Also, transforming Werewolves adds an interesting strategic element to games via spellcount.



![Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gavony+Township)

### [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township)


+1/+1 on all of your creatures? How about +2/+2 or +3/+3? Admittedly, it takes some time and mana, but if you have enough of both and at least some creatures, [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) will turn even the lowliest Human or [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) token into a powerful fighter on par with the largest monster. As witnessed in the Top 8: the card was instrumental in getting Marco Ricci to the finals and then helped him to clinch victory.
