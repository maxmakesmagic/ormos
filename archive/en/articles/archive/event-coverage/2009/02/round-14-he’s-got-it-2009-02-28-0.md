Round 14: He’s Got It
=====================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 28, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author_pic_nate_price.jpg)
By Nate Price




 A longtime member of the Pro Tour and Grand Prix coverage staff, Nate Price now works making beautiful words for all of you lovely people as the community manager for organized play. When not covering events, he lords over the @MagicProTour Twitter account, ruling with an iron fist. 






### Ari Lax (Faeries) vs. Brian Robinson (Dark Bant)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptkyo09/r14_lax.jpg)*Michaganian Ari Lax makes a run at the Top 8.*”High roll?” Lax asked Robinson as he shook the dice in his hand. When the dice finally hit the table, his head soon followed, but the resulting tremor failed to move the dice from the snake eyes he had rolled.

**Game 1**

Despite this match having a Top 8 berth on the line, the players remained fairly jovial, exchanging quips back and forth as they shuffled. After a bit of banter about their deck choices and what they may or may not be playing, Robinson opened the first game with a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) followed by a turn-two [Doran, the Siege Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower). Lax made his own trademark second-turn play: [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). As he pulled out a stack of what had to be like fourteen Spirit tokens, he joked, “As you can see, I really was going to play Red-White.”

Robinson attacked Lax down to 15 with his Doran before playing a [Rhox War Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk). Lax got his first of many blockers from the [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and passed the turn after drawing and playing a land. Robinson activated the [Treetop Village](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) he had played on the previous turn and sent his team in. Before blockers, Lax decided to play a [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique), targeting Robinson. Before letting the ability resolve, Robinson used a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to remove the Clique from the game. The hand he revealed contained nothing but a couple of land. After the dust settled, Lax was dropped all the way to 7.

On his turn, Lax went through the same motions as before: draw, land, go. When Robinson tried to attack for the win, Lax used a [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) to Ice his whole team. That bought him another token, and a little more breathing space. His [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) was taking its toll now, though, and his life dipped down to an insignificant 5.

Robinson went for the kill one more time, and this time Lax had another [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) to block all of Robinson’s creatures, as well as a [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) to kill the [Treetop Village](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village). That let him [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) the turn one more time and set himself up to get back in the game. After dropping to 4, he dropped a [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) into play and passed the turn with one creature and a couple mana up. When Robinson came across with his Doran and War Monk, Lax flashed in a [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) and used it and the Faerie token from his [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) to give him yet one more turn. After combat, Robinson added a [Rafiq of the Many](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rafiq+of+the+Many) to his team.

Lax passed his turn without doing anything, just choosing to sit back on his [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave) and [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) token for one more turn. During Robinson’s upkeep, he flashed in a [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) to tap him down. After a messy combat step, Robinson reduced Lax’s team to absolutely nothing. The game only lasted one more turn, with Lax muttering to himself that he’d made a mistake.

Brian Robinson 1, Ari Lax 0

**Game 2**

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptkyo09/r14_robinson.jpg)*Brian Robinson’s deck sports Rafiq, Doran, Rhox War Monk, and other goodies.*Lax came into the second game with the “all-star” [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), following it up with a [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer). Robinson failed to make a second-turn [Doran, the Siege Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower) this game, but he did manage to get a [Rhox War Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) in its stead. Following hot on the Monk’s heels was a [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) to steal a [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) before it could coerce any of his creatures to join the dark side. That left Lax with a [Plumeveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumeveil), [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), and [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) in hand. Joining the Sculler on the table was an innocently dangerous [Scattershot Archer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattershot+Archer)s.

Lax drew his card and announced a “main-phase” [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), removing a token from the game. 

“Do you got it?” Lax asked as he passed the turn to Robinson.

“I dunno. What’s ‘it?’“ 

“‘It’s’ it.”

I’m pretty sure that exchange was Lax’s attempt at a real-life [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), but Robinson was apparently ready with a [Guttural Response](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttural+Response). If the “it” to which Lax was referring was a [Rafiq of the Many](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rafiq+of+the+Many), Robinson did indeed have it. The kingmaker general gave the Monk the extra power needed to sail past the [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) to vastly swing the life totals. The [Scattershot Archer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattershot+Archer) made sure that the sky was free of tokens as well.

During his next turn, Lax merely equipped the Clique with his Warhammer and passed the turn. Robinson added another exalted creature to his side: the [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). A second [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) drove the last nail in the coffin, stealing a [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) out of Lax’s hand. At this point, Lax just dropped the remainder of his hand on the table and shook Robinson’s hand.

“I punted both games. I don’t know if I could have won there by just attacking. But that first game ....”

Brian Robinson 2, Ari Lax 0
