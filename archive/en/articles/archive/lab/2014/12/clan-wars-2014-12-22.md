What's up, labbies? You know, week after week here at From the Lab it's just me coming up with new ideas and spitting out new decks. Today, I'm taking a break from all that. After all, I'm not the only Johnny deck builder out there. In fact, there are literally thousands of them reading this. Chances are you're one yourself. Over the last month, I've tapped into that resource, by which I mean your brains, and extracted a number of interesting deck ideas built around the clans of Tarkir. Today, I'll be showing off a few of my favorites.



The Jeskai Way
==============


Jeskai received significantly more deck submissions than any other clan, which did not surprise me in the least. Blue and red have long been partners in crime, enabling some of the most crazy combo shenanigans in history. Although Temur contains this color combination as well, that clan is centered on green, which pulls the focus toward big creatures.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/images/daily/cardart_narsetenlightenedmaster.jpg)
[Narset, Enlightened Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Enlightened+Master) | Art by [Magali Villeneuve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=[%22Magali Villeneuve%22])


Since there were so many decks to choose from for this clan, I'll be showing off two of them. The first comes from Alex Cohen, who built a deck around [Narset, Enlightened Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narset%2C+Enlightened+Master). With the Jeskai's focus on instants and sorceries within *Khans of Tarkir*, it's easy to forget that the clan actually works with any noncreature spells. That means Narset can cast free artifacts, enchantments, and even Planeswalkers from the top of your library. Alex included a few of each in the deck to give Narset a versatile suite of cards to choose from.

Alex cleverly skirts around Narset's noncreature restriction by loading the deck with artifacts, enchantments, and Planeswalkers that can turn into creatures once on the battlefield. Spells like [Mana Severance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Severance) and [Endless Horizons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+Horizons) reduce the chances that you'll hit lands with Narset's ability. Finally, [Ethereal Usher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Usher) pulls double duty as a way to search your library for Narset and a way to make her unblockable if you already have one in hand.


The other Jeskai deck I wanted to share with you all is completely crazy in the best possible way. Steven Woolery put together a sweet infinite combo built around [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy). I'll let him give you a quick rundown,


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jeskai Ascendancy)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai Ascendancy)
The basic premise is to have [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy), [Memory Crystal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Crystal), [Iron Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr)s, and [Palladium Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palladium+Myr)s out on the battlefield. This allows you to keep casting and buying back [Searing Touch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Touch) repeatedly. If for whatever reason the opponent has protection, you can target [Palladium Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palladium+Myr) with [Searing Touch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Touch). Since it is 2/2 and [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) keeps making it grow, it will not die. You can then use [Rogue's Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue%27s+Passage) to allow your colorless creatures to swing in for the win.





Complex? Certainly. But it's also inventive, and incredibly satisfying to pull off. Besides, I've never been one to shy away from complexity in a deck.

The creatures I chose all have a power of 4 or more and cost three or less to play, except the single Nylea, who gives your army trample and is a great mana sink if you start to generate extra from your mana creatures and [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm). I especially like [Vexing Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Devil), as it draws you cards and untaps your creatures for a single red mana whether your opponent chooses to keep it around or not. Also, [Savage Knuckleblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Knuckleblade) has the ability to return to your hand if you need an untap or card draw.

And with [Temur Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Ascendancy) giving everything haste, your opponents won't know what hit 'em. Even if you don't get the full combo put together, the assortment of powerful creatures can get the job done the old-fashioned way.

This deck has a lot going on, so I'll let Rudy walk you through some of the main interactions.


If you can sacrifice the undying creature, remove its +1/+1 counter with [Retribution of the Ancients](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retribution+of+the+Ancients), and sacrifice it again, you'll get a loop to gun down every targetable creature on the board. Doesn't matter if they are as big as Emrakul, or as indestructible as the mighty [Blightsteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightsteel+Colossus). For the loop, we'll need a black mana source to activate [Retribution of the Ancients](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retribution+of+the+Ancients), so the classic tool to sacrifice a creature to gain mana is [Phyrexian Altar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar) (or [Ashnod's Altar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashnod%27s+Altar), for 2 colorless mana, but I don't like using a deck slot for [Initiates of the Ebon Hand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Initiates+of+the+Ebon+Hand)). Now the combo is complete, and you'll have a clean board to slowly win through attacking them with your undying creatures.


But wait, what if your opponent had hexproof or shroud creatures? How does an army of infinite Spirits sound for an alternative win condition? Enter [Abzan Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Ascendancy). Not only does it provide some fuel for [Retribution of the Ancients](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retribution+of+the+Ancients), it also provides us with endless Spirit tokens for every loop iteration from above.







[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Abzan Ascendancy)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan Ascendancy)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Retribution of the Ancients)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retribution of the Ancients)





If you're short on [Retribution of the Ancients](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retribution+of+the+Ancients) (or short on target creatures), [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) (and another Zombie on board) + [Phyrexian Altar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar) could get the same infinite loop that triggers [Abzan Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Ascendancy), [Grim Haruspex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Haruspex), or [Fecundity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fecundity) as well. Or you could just sacrifice an undying creature or [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) to [Diabolic Intent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Intent) and search for whatever combo piece you're missing at the time.


The Sultai Brood
================


Rudy submitted decks for several different clans, and in addition to the Abzan deck above, the one for Sultai really caught my eye. Like almost all of the Sultai decks I received, it's built around [Sidisi, Brood Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidisi%2C+Brood+Tyrant). However, Rudy's deck had that little extra oomph the others lacked thanks to the combo of [Tombstone Stairwell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tombstone+Stairwell) and [Extractor Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extractor+Demon).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sidisi, Brood Tyrant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidisi, Brood Tyrant)
By casting or unearthing [Extractor Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extractor+Demon) with the Stairwell on the battlefield, you can mill two cards for each creature in any graveyard when all the Zombies leave the battlefield. That should give you more than enough Sidisi triggers to take over the game, not to mention all the extra tokens you'll be getting from [Tombstone Stairwell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tombstone+Stairwell) next turn.

The Mardu Horde
===============


Although Mardu does have a lot going for it, it's not exactly the most Johnny of clans. In fact, out of the dozens of deck submissions I received, only two readers chose to represent the Horde in the battle of the clans. Although the decks were similar, Garry Hough added a bit of extra spice to go with his decklist.


Although I have been long on the campaign, I cannot forget the day [Zurgo Helmsmasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zurgo+Helmsmasher) came to my village.


One of his chieftains ordered those of fighting age to gather. Then Zurgo himself said loud and clear, "Join my horde for blood, glory, and plunder, but I only want warriors who have a lust for carnage. Those of you with no thirst for battle, have no fear, you can stay to herd and harvest, so your village can pay tribute next season."


Most of the villagers made no move, but my cousin Balig and I stepped forward and we shouted our allegiance. We had dreamed of a life beyond the village since we were little. Our chance had come and we were tired of tending goats. Balig and I were each given a spear. The others were not so lucky.


"Your young and old are enough to tend to the crops. The rest of you I cannot suffer to live. Cowards weaken the tribe, so their life's blood shall nourish the earth," [Zurgo Helmsmasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zurgo+Helmsmasher) proclaimed. Then he turned his gaze to me and I knew what I had to do.
