Daniel Royde Crowned King of Great Britain

2011 Great Britain National Championship
----------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat11/Final%20Blurb_600.jpg)


What makes a champion?


Passion, commitment, confidence, composure, luck, grit, determination and (it turns out) a whole ton of stamina.


The Great British Nationals 2011 were brought to an epic and hard-fought conclusion after a final that lasted a mind-draining THREE AND A HALF HOURS. To put that in context, that's long enough to watch the whole of Lord of the Rings: Return of the King and then make a start on the DVD extras. Any way you slice it that's a long time to have to sustain your best possible Magic, under pressure, at the end of three long days of tournament play. It was mammoth. Emerging unscathed from that epic struggle, we crown a new king of Great Britain – handing yet another title to the Grand Prix London champion, Daniel Royde!


Undefeated throughout the whole of the constructed rounds of the tournament, Daniel Royde's Eldrazi Green deck finally ended the dogged and wily resistance of the multi-national pro Eduardo Sajgalik. Finishing as runner-up, Sajgalik becomes a two-time National Team member (having previously been on the Canada National Team). Royde and Sajgalik had begun Sunday as the two big names in the Top 8 and the pairings drew them inexorably together into a final confrontation that Royde eventually won 3-2 – much to his disbelief.


Tim Pinder will join Royde and Sajgalik on Team GB, after recovering from his Semi-final defeat to battle along the long road back from 2-0 down in the 3rd/4th Playoff and have his Vampires defeat the Caw-Blade spells of Jamie Hannah, who was unlucky to see his early dominance turn into an eventual defeat.


It all marked the end of a fantastic Top 8, but also what has been a great Nationals Championship. From the last LCQ spots awarded late on Thursday, to the final arrival of Emrakul, the Aeon's Torn that ended the Final, Great British Nationals 2011 has been a memorable event.


A huge congratulations to everyone in Team GB, and you can be sure that we'll be rooting for them all in San Francisco!


Rule Britannia!

Quarterfinal - Dominic Penton vs. Eduardo Sajgalik
--------------------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon* 

He's only 16, but Dominic Penton has quietly been amassing an absurdly good Nationals record. In his third event, his Limited record is 6-0, 6-0, 5-1. For those that can't count, that's 17-1. The top 8, however, is Constructed, and it's mono-red that Dominic will need to fuel the fire of his Worlds ambitions. Across the table sits Eduardo Sajgalik, a man who has already played at Worlds, as a member of the Canadian National Team! Sajgalik also has a top 8 from Great Britain Nationals, making him arguably the favorite coming into the top 8 - although Grand Prix London Champion Daniel Royde might have something to say about that.


### Game One


Sajgalik got us under way with [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) and a [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk), a nice start against a mulligan to six and no turn one play from Penton. Or turn two, for that matter. A second [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) followed, and it was all Sajgalik in the early running. [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) was Penton's first play, but that isn't what you want on turn three. When Sajgalik cast his third [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk), we understood just how pernicious the card advantage is, Penton using [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to deal with just one [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk), at the price of an entire card. Penton added a second [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), and then [Chandra's Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Phoenix), which met [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) with kicker.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat11/QF%20Dominic%20Penton.jpg)Life totals - [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)-[Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine): 20. Mono-red: 13. Yeah, really.


Sajgalik cast [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel), and followed it with the perfect land, [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn), netting him a free 1/1 with the promise of another.


[Chandra's Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Phoenix) was replayed, but this was all one way traffic. When Penton attempted to combine his Phoenix with a [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) activation, Sajgalik was ready with [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) with kicker to save his [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel). [Shrine of Burning Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Burning+Rage) found [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) waiting, and the torture was soon over for Penton, who had been given as comprehensive a beating as it was possible to imagine in the opening game of the top 8.


Dominic Penton 0 - 1 Eduardo Sajgalik


### Game Two


There was a substantial delay before the start of game two, with multiple mulligans. Penton stuck at six, while Sajgalik went all the way to four. [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) opened for Penton, not the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) which might have brought Sajgalik back into the game. Still, he managed three lands and [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements), forcing Penton to [Staggershock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staggershock) himself in repsonse. [Arc Trail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail) dealt with the Reinforcement soldiers, and the [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) put Sajgalik to 17.


After [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), Sajgalik had [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), which met with four life and [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) from Penton.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat11/QF%20Eduardo%20Sajgalik.jpg)[Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) was next for the red mage, which equipped the [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Grim%2BLavamancer), which then attacked. Sajgalik, off a mulligan to four remember, laid a sixth land, slammed it down, cast [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), returned [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), and cast [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) next turn. Ridiculous. Fast forward two turns, Sajgalik added [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) to the board, forcing Penton's team to attack the Planeswalker. That team was now the [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), plus [Chandra's Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Phoenix). Eduardo was down to 7, with Dominic at 13. The Phoenix duly died at the hands/wings of [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx), Sajgalik activated [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Gideon%2BJura) as a 6/6, and he had completed a remarkable escape act, and now held a comfortable lead in the best of five match.


Dominic Penton 0 - 2 Eduardo Sajgalik


### Game Three



![Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gideon+Jura)

Although both players kept their opening hands, Penton was once again without an explosive start, beginning game three with [Chandra's Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Phoenix), playing straight into [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements). Penton cast a second Phoenix, and pushed Sajgalik back down to 20. It didn't. [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) dealt with that particular threat.


Out came [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura), who forced Penton's team to attack him. With a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and a third [Chandra's Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Phoenix), [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Gideon%2BJura) bit the dust. Sajgalik activated his [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) to drop Penton to 4, and repeated the dose one turn later to complete a thoroughly one-sided demolition.

Quarterfinal – William Blake vs. Daniel Royde
---------------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe* 

If it's decks that win Top 8s, then Kevin Blake is in the box seat at Great British Nationals 2011. He has the UB Control deck – the very deck that saw Ali Aintrazi through the Top 8 of US Nationals a fortnight ago. If it's players that win Top 8s then the edge surely sits with Daniel Royde – the Londoner has already won one major event this year, when he defended British pride at Grand Prix London to take home the big prize. The momentum of the weekend also seems to be with Royde – of all the players in the Top 8 his draft record was the worst (3-3) but that just means that it was his Standard performance that secured him a place in the Top 8, with an undefeated 7-0-1 record. His Eldrazi Green deck has crushed all-comers


### Game One


Winning the dice roll, Kevin Blake wasted no time at all in ripping into Daniel Royde's cards – an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) tore a [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) from Royde's grip. Blake followed that with [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) and began to draw cards, although a second [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) missed as Royde had only lands and big hitters in hand – [Artisan of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek) and [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) among a bunch of lands.


From there things turned into a waiting game – Kevin Blake was happy to suck up extra cards from [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), while Royde had little to do but play a few lands, an [Overgrown Battlement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement), and spawn a couple of plants from Khalni's Garden. Turns passed by as the two players waited for the right time to switch things up a gear.


It was Blake who made the move – sucking up a final card from his Planeswalker, and consigning Jace to the graveyard, he tapped out to play a [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx). That gave Royde the chance to drop his own hefty threat, and the GP London champ replied with a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), finding a pair of [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple)s in the undergrowth of his library to complete his set of four. That was a bit too much mana for Blake, and he sacrificed a [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) to cut down one of Royde's Temples.


The [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) was immediately sliced in half by a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), but that suited Royde all the more – playing his [Artisan of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek), Daniel Royde brought the [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) right back into play, searching out a pair of [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)s from his deck. The [Artisan of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek) was countered by a [Stoic Rebuttal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoic+Rebuttal), but most of the value had come in returning the [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) anyway. Not quite finished with his turn, Royde dropped a [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) to help him protect his [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) from future unpleasantness.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat11/QF%20Blake%20v%20Royde.jpg)*Daniel Royde tries to reach his Eldrazi*
The game appeared to be spiralling out of Kevin Blake's control. He played [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) and immediately upped him to 5 loyalty drawing three cards to Royde's one, then attacked with his [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx)... but there was nothing he could do about Royde's [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan). The Titan smashed Jace down on Royde's next turn, filtering out another two lands from the green deck. Worse yet, Royde had his [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)s in play, and he sacrificed two of them to cut Blake back to just 4 lands. It seemed like Blake was taking two steps backwards for every step forward.


Another attack from the [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) – a [Mystifying Maze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystifying+Maze) and [Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) were added to Royde's collection as he built towards a finishing blow. Blake's [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) was drawing extra cards, but he simply couldn't keep up with the flow of powerful cards coming from the Eldrazi deck – [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) arrived unopposed, and Royde was all set to bring search up [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) on his next turn and play the manifestation of pure evil.


"I don't think there's anything you can play that means you don't die now" he offered.


"Nope, I don't think so either", Blake agreed, "about the only thing I can do is [Praetor's Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Praetor%27s+Grasp) your [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn)"


Blake drew a card, looked around for a last glimmer of hope, and conceded the game.


Kevin Blake 0 – 1 Daniel Royde


### Game Two


"We were actually making international phone calls last night, trying to work out how to beat you" Daniel Royde said, smiling as the two players shuffled up.


"Well that's something, at least! It's actually a really interesting matchup. There's lots of things we can both do to change it" Blake agreed, turning to his sideboard notes.


"Yeah we found there's a lot of depth in it, more than we thought at first. We were just about to go to bed last night, and with the last thing my roommate said as we were going to sleep he had an epiphany. On went the lights and we were back to testing something new."

"Gah!" cried Blake, when [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated) was revealed, "I'm trying to think of a card I want you to draw less. I think that's about it.."


Blake had to hurry up against the threat of [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated) shredding his hand, and he dropped a [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), while Royde responded with a [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) at the end of Blake's turn, and he started to reveal the top seven cards of his deck...


[Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith), Land, Land, Land, Land, Land... [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker)

But that wasn't all Royde had in mind – he followed up the [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) with an activation of his [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), discarded a second [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker) to find... [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Infinite+Gyre). Blake started counting up the mana Royde had available, and didn't like the answer he got – the GP Champ was just one land short of playing the big Eldrazi. Would Royde draw the land he needed?


[Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple), [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ulamog%252C%2Bthe%2BInfinite%2BGyre)

Taking his own turn, Blake flipped the top card of his deck... and picked up his cards, crushed by yet another Eldrazi titan.


Kevin Blake 0 – 2 Daniel Royde


### Game Three


Size is relative – *Scars of Mirrodin* proved to us that a 2/2 can be king in a land of 1/1 creatures – but usually a 6/6 is big enough. Not when Daniel Royde's Eldrazi are around, though, and here in the Top 8 all of Blake's best creatures are being swatted around like flies by the cards Royde can summon. Two games down, it looked as though Blake desperately needed to find a way to change things, because he had seemed helpless to prevent his losses in the first two games.


[Disrupt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)ing Royde's deck from the outset was perhaps the key – an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) removed a [Beast Within](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within), [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) struck down an [Overgrown Battlement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement), and finally [Memoricide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memoricide) stripped [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) from Royde's hand and library. All that left the GP Champ with was [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) and a bunch of lands – no acceleration, no search – and the Eldrazi master would have to do things the hard way.


Blake didn't plan on giving Royde the chance to do that, though, and he dropped a [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) before sucking up two cards at the start of Royde's turn. Royde hit back with [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated), but instead of removing the Sphinx from play he began draining Blake's hand of cards, forcing him to [Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exile) an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). On his next turn Blake hit back with his own Planeswalker – [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) – and [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated) had seen enough, Exiling the [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) at the second attempt.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat11/QF%20Kevin%20Blake%20v%20Daniel%20Royde.jpg)*Kevin Blake looks for a way out*
How to kill [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated)? That was the problem facing Kevin Blake, but he had an elegant answer: play your own [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated)! Faced with a mirror image of himself, the two [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated)'s left play.


That seemed to spell real trouble for Daniel Royde. He had lands, but that was all – two cards in hand didn't stack up well against the five cards that Blake had, and Blake still had Jace by his side whispering advice and offering up additional spells. Royde had nothing to lose and played his hand onto the table – an [Artisan of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek) and a [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), although the [Artisan of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek) could return nothing more exciting than an [Overgrown Battlement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement) from his graveyard.


[Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) was Blake's reply, but that seemed to leave the door open to Royde, and the GP Champ seized the opportunity. Drawing a creature on his turn, he tapped his [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) to search up [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Infinite+Gyre). [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ulamog%252C%2Bthe%2BInfinite%2BGyre) was countered by a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), but he destroyed the [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) even so. That left the [Artisan of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek) as the only thing in play and Kevin Blake was cut in half – a turn from death and clinging to his pair of [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit)s to block the Artisan and buy time!


Royde wasn't done there. Drawing a second creature on his next turn, he just repeated his last turn – finding [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ulamog%252C%2Bthe%2BInfinite%2BGyre) and bringing the evil Eldrazi to the board again. It was too much for Blake, and he offered his hand in congratulations.


**Kevin Blake 0 – 3 Daniel Royde**


The Grand Prix London champ marched on into the Semi-Finals, extending his undefeated record in the Standard portion of Great British Nationals 2011. The rest of the Top 4 need to watch out, before Royde is bringing what seems to be unstoppable firepower – who would bet against him making it two titles in the year, and adding GB Champion to his GP title?

Semifinal - Eduardo Sajgalik vs. Tim Pinder
-------------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon* 

Both these players have two more matches here at Great Britain Nationals 2011. One of them will advance to the final, with a guarantee of a place in the World Championship team for San Francisco, with a chance at the National title, while the loser here faces a 3rd/4th play-off for the final slot on the team. Still, at least a place on the plane to California is already secure.


### Game One


The curse of the mulligan struck early, with Pinder holding at six, but Sajgalik starting at just five. [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) was countered by Sajgalik's [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce). [Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade) also got countered, this time by [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), meaning [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) was the first card into play.


"So, let's see" said Sajgalik. "I basically mulliganed to eight..." as he cast [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk), and reloaded.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat11/SF%20Tim%20Pinder.jpg)*Tim Pinder*
[Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) attacked for the first damage of the match, Sajgalik adding a second [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk). Pinder added [Vampire Lacerator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Lacerator), but Sajgalik had [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) ready with kicker during the next attack.


[Blackcleave Cliffs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blackcleave+Cliffs) represented red mana for Pinder who had a lot of good cards left in hand. Sajgalik, meanwhile, wiped the board with [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment).


The second phase of the game began with Sajgalik at 12, Pinder quickly making that 9 with [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), 7 with [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), and [Gatekeeper of Malakir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir) coming down. [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) and [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) came down for Sajgalik, who was back up to 13, just when it seemed Pinder was closing in on the opening game. Semifinal - Jamie Hannah vs. Daniel Royde
-----------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe* 

The Semi-Final brought together two unbeaten records – two immovable objects going head-to-head. Daniel Royde's standard record with his stomping Eldrazi Green deck is 8-0-1, while the young student Jamie Hannah has piloted his [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)-[Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine) deck to a 6-0-3 record, picking up three draws along the way. Both players had also sailed through their respective Quarter-Final matches 3-0, but something had to give. The GP London Champion looked calm and relaxed, handing around his oversized Elton John sunglasses at the start of the round, but if Jamie Hannah was phased at all by the occasion he didn't show it.


"I haven't seen any Eldrazi Green around for ages", remarked Hannah, as the players chatted while shuffling up, "I haven't really ever played against it. Have you been playtesting this matchup much?"


"[Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)-[Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine)? Yeah – it's the point of the deck!" smiled Royde, "although your list is a little weird."


### Game One



![Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Emeria+Angel)

With the pre-game show out of the way, the dice was rolled and we were underway, although things went immediately wrong for Jamie Hannah – losing the dice roll and having to mulligan. Daniel Royde began to ramp up his green mana with an [Overgrown Battlement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement), which Hannah immediately spent four life to [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember). Royde replaced the Battlement with a [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker), but his Elf was dealt with just as brutally by a second [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember).


[Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) was the next play from Royde, but the Planeswalker was denied by a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), with Hannah continuing to disrupt Royde's mana – destroying an [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) with [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge). Four turns played, and neither player had anything in play other than land, Hannah having managed to fend off Royde's advances so far.


It was time for Hannah to press the advantage, and he summoned an [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) to the battlefield, and pulling up a Bird token when he played an [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa), and a second Bird as the Mesa searched up a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). Across the table, things seemed to have stalled for the Eldrazi deck, with Royde missing a pair two land drops, although he managed to deploy an [Overgrown Battlement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gbnat11/SF%20-%20Hannah.jpg)Having stalled Royde's early game ramping, Hannah needed to end the game before the Eldrazi deck could get to it's big guns the hard way. Equipping one of his Birds with a [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) the young student went onto the offensive, cutting Royde down to 13 life as his Angels and Birds swooped into the red zone (presumably with one of the birds desperately clutching a sword on its beak. Or something. It's probably best not to try and think of the logistics of things like equipping birds with swords). A second [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) cut Royde back to four mana, and Hannah passed the turn.


[Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) was countered by [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), the [Overgrown Battlement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement) was thrown back to hand by [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil), and Daniel Royde was left sampling the unfamiliar taste of defeat.


Jamie Hannah 1 – 0 Daniel Royde


### Game Two



![Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Joraga+Treespeaker)

That had been an exemplar lesson in how to beat Royde's green deck – cut of it's mana, and then after you've done that... cut off it's mana some more. Royde never had five mana available at any point in the game, and Hannah had wasted little time in closing things out once the opening was there. Was that a fluke, or did it signal that Hannah had the right ingredients to end Royde's run to the title? The laughter and chat was gone as the two players sideboarded for the return leg, with Daniel Royde lost in concentration as he planned his comeback.


Royde's comeback began with a [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) to find a [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker), although as soon as he dared to level his Elf up to produce mana, Hannah returned it to hand with [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil), before following up with a [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader). Royde returned his [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker) to play and began to level it up, then added a [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) alongside the [Joraga Treespeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Joraga%2BTreespeaker). This was more dangerous than the first game – Royde's creatures were sticking around, and even with Protection from Green the [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) was nothing like the clock that the [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) had been in the first game.


Accelerating the clock a little, Hannah put down a [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) and a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) that copied the [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader). That was fine, but he hadn't been able to disrupt anything that Royde wanted to do in this second game, and was tapped out again... this was moving into the sort of territory where the Eldrazi deck could begin to seize control with it's sheer power. In truth, Royde seemed stunned by the sheer range of options available to him – with [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and seven mana available he could do a lot, but after taking a brief dip in the tank the GP Champ emerged and played a [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith), searching out a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and two [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple)s.


Things were heading rapidly downhill for Jamie Hannah, and by his body language it was clear that Hannah knew it – he sent his pair of unblockable [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader)s across the table, reducing Royde to 8 life, and smashed one of Royde's Temples with a [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge), but had to pass the turn and hope things didn't go too badly wrong too quickly - his pair of Double Striking [Crusade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade)rs would end the game next turn so long as Royde couldn't find a colorless blocker!


The [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) struck back, searching out the last two [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple)s. That was a start but it was really a sideshow to the main event. Needing to find a colorless blocker, Daniel Royde pitched a creature to his [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and summoned forth an [Artisan of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek)! The Artisan would serve as a blocker but was countered by [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak).


Royde was tapped out, with no colorless blockers. But he wasn't done. The Artisan being countered meant that the GP Champ could play his [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) for free. In fact, he could play BOTH of his [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap)s for free! It was a critical moment as Royde flipped through the top fourteen cards of his deck, looking for one of his immense Eldrazi.



![Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ulamog%2C+the+Infinite+Gyre)
