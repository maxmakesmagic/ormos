Clever Girl is Just a Catch Phrase
==================================



 Posted in **Command Tower**
 on September 18, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






*Khans of Tarkir* is almost upon us, with Prerelease events all this weekend for you to whet your appetite on clan warfare. It's been an exciting few weeks of previews, and the opportunity to share one of the set's biggest cards wasn't lost on me.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/images/daily/cardart_cleverimpersonator.jpg)
[Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator) | Art by [Slawomir Maniak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Slawomir%20Maniak%22%5D)


[Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator) is a card I know I'm going to be seeing often in upcoming games of Commander. As friend, DailyMTG.com editor, and "Value Khan of Commander" Blake put it in his email to me, "Yeah, this is pretty much going in every deck I ever make ever."


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Clever+Impersonator)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator)
I'm not sure I could sum it up more succinctly than that.


It's one of several cards in *Khans of Tarkir* I'm head over heels in love with and looking to track down, and I know there's plenty more to talk about next week too. This week, I wanted to open the floodgates and unleash the obvious torrent of disgusting, powerful, funny, and *clever* combos made available by [Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator).


There were a *few* that came through.


Coping with Copying
===================


A few words of warning: both predicting how unreleased cards will play out—it's another week until *Khans of Tarkir* is available at your local game store—and how useful copy effects will be is a dicey proposition. I like to gets the cards in hand, then onto the stack or battlefield, before coming to final decisions about how I feel.


While some cards have obvious uses and resonate well, the real gems of a set take time to suss out. I suspect the common cycle of lands—[Wind-Scarred Crag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind-Scarred+Crag) and friends—will fill in the holes of my Commander mana bases for years, and I'm already looking forward to sitting on [Cranial Archive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Archive) for several turns until it has its moment to shine.




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wind-Scarred+Crag)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wind-Scarred%20Crag)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cranial+Archive)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial%20Archive)





I think [Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator) is in the former category of cards, and it isn't close. And you agreed, as Aidan explained:


[Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator) can copy artifacts and enchantments in addition to creatures, so there's a few guys in my own deck I'd love to copy.


The deck is all about modular and counters, so naturally I've got proliferate. [Inexorable Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inexorable+Tide) works really well for ramping counters up on everything super fast, so why not make a second one? Every time I cast a spell, my Arcbounds grow twice, not to mention filling up my Planeswalkers and charge-counter artifacts.


Alternatively I could copy a [Lux Cannon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lux+Cannon); one [Lux Cannon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lux+Cannon) gets me some much-needed removal for tricky permanents like enchantments and Planeswalkers, but having two means I can start gunning down my enemies from afar before swinging in with massive Arcbounds.


Copying my [Grave Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Pact) can also be quite nice; the deck enjoys losing creatures due to combat or sac effects, but if each opponent lost two creatures every time I did so, the field would empty quite quickly, which would give me free rein to smash some faces.




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lux%20Cannon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lux%20Cannon)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grave%20Pact)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave%20Pact)





My group has quite a few nasty cards going around, though. One that comes up a lot is [True Conviction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Conviction). Double strike and lifelink would be two very nice blessings for Marchesa since the deck lacks any lifegain to keep up when the match becomes a war of attrition. I could even copy my friend's [Argentum Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argentum+Armor) or even his commander, [Drana, Kalastria Bloodchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drana%2C+Kalastria+Bloodchief). Drana's removal and flying makes her quite nasty, but if I have my own I can start eating his stuff (including using my Drana clone to kill his Drana!).


Overall I think that card would slot in beautifully. I thought about [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) in the past but didn't have one to put in, but [Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator) does what Metamorph could and so much more!

I've played against several [Marchesa, the Black Rose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marchesa%2C+the+Black+Rose) decks already and I've been continuously impressed with how solidly they work. It turns out that making a nigh unkillable army, in combination with effects that let you steal creatures, is a pretty solid way to march to victory.


Aidan's approach is similar to what I imagined at first, relying on Arcbound creatures and others that come equipped with +1/+1 counters. Sliding something like [Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator) into here is a way to play both sides of the fence: Double down on something awesome we have going, or "borrow" the best an opponent has to offer.


It's exactly how I plan to run my [Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator).


John had a different plan when he pulled together a decklist, and I fell for the trap:


The #1 card that I and everybody around here is excited about is [Clever Impersonator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator)! I'd love to jam it into every deck I own that taps for blue mana, but if it winds up being one of the chase cards of the set I may have to settle for just one or two for my favorites.


Another card I'm looking forward to rocking in my blue decks is [Kheru Spellsnatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kheru+Spellsnatcher). [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) and [Nekusar, the Mindrazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekusar%2C+the+Mindrazer) in particular want this card.


[Hooded Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hooded+Hydra) appeals to me. The counters and the token generation are both enticing for a deck like [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores), but the card would also be quite suitable for [Prossh, Skyraider of Kher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prossh%2C+Skyraider+of+Kher) or [Vorel of the Hull Clade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vorel+of+the+Hull+Clade).

[Jeering Instigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeering+Instigator) looks like a lot of fun to me. I will probably get a copy of this for one of my newest decks, built around [Kurkesh, Onakke Ancient](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kurkesh%2C+Onakke+Ancient) from *Magic 2015*. I run a number of sacrifice-creatures effects for card drawing, along with various [Act of Treason](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) effects to allow them to double as removal. The Instigator fits right into this plan.

Hey Adam,


It was a pleasure meeting you at the PAX Party this past weekend.


Below is the decklist we were chatting about that I play in commander. You'll see the deck is heavily centered around Venser and plays to the idea of making your opponents just wait a bit. They can still cast their spells, I'm not the bad guy, ya just gotta be patient. Cast it next turn instead.


There is a lot of play on the stack (my style of *Magic*) and a lot of politics that this deck engages in because you are full of redundant answers to problems but none of them are permanent. There is also the benefit of bouncing permanents to your opponent's advantage to gain favor; i.e., letting them rebuy their ETB trigger off a creature on the board.


I often become the player people don't want to kill but look to align with. Because of this I will usually last late into the game, which is great, as I don't enjoy being eliminated early and waiting for the next game.


The deck doesn't have many ways to win—mediocre beats, [Capsize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Capsize) lock, etc.


The way I often end a game if it needs to end is cast [Decree of Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Silence), let it counter things and then when the trigger goes on the stack that would cause me to sack Decree, I can bounce the Decree back to my hand and recast it to counter even more spells. I will usually only implement this when the game is running long and needs to end.


Anyway, it's a lot of fun and I hope you enjoy it.


Cheers,  

Phil
