Building a Champion
===================



 Posted in **Learning Curve**
 on October 22, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New Yorkâ€“based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 






S[tate and Provincial Championships](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=events/magic/champs) are going to cause me physical harm. I'm fairly certain I am going to walk into an open manhole cover while trying to figure out what I want to play. Even worse, I am going to finally figure out what to play and while lost in the epiphany I will not see an oncoming truck. There seem to be so many decks that are possible with all of the cards in Core Set and *Mirrodin*. Last week I looked at a smattering of decks that I expected to do well. 

The big three decks that I expect to be facing are Mono-Black Control, Goblins/Goblin Bidding and Astral Slide. None of those decks really strike my fancy. I want to play with new cards and test out new ideas at States. Yes, I want to win but I want to do it on my own terms with a deck slightly off the beaten path. I think the deck I want to build is an updated version of Aaron Forsythe's [Angry Hermit](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/111) deck from US Nationals in 2000. I have always had an unnatural affection for both [Vine Trellis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vine+Trellis) and [Plow Under](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plow+Under) and I don't know if I can pass up the opportunity to dust off my old foils and play them on Saturday.

The potential for a turn three [Plow Under](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plow+Under) ([Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Birds%2Bof%2BParadise) followed by [Vine Trellis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Vine%2BTrellis)) is way too appealing for me to not play this deck. I am confident in its ability to battle against Mono-Black and I think it can hold its own against Slide. The match-up that concerns me isâ€”of courseâ€”Goblins. I think that I can muster a fair game one with the Trellis and the [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ravenous%2BBaloth) buying me time to draw into [Starstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starstorm). [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) for the remaining two games should even things out somewhat. I will be testing and tuning this deck and I hope to have it ready for action by Saturday.

I was definitely leaning toward Mono-Black Control for a while. I like the all purpose utility of the [Oblivion Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Stone). I just don't like [Karma](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karma). Whether it is literal or metaphorical, [Karma](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karma) is going to get you. I have tried to work around Swamps as best I can but you have only so many options.

This deck leads to some very aggressive opening draws. You can attack for four damage on the second turn with a [Savannah Lions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions) and a [Bonesplitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonesplitter). If your [Leonin Den-Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Leonin%2BDen-Guard) picks up the [Bonesplitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonesplitter) you have an impressive 4/4 creature that does not tap to attack. I saw some people testing out similar versions of this deck with [Auriok Steelshaper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Steelshaper) where I have [Savannah Lions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions). I prefer the Lions although that is probably just because I have been looking for an excuse to play my four Alpha Lions since they were reprinted. 

In reality I prefer the Lions because the deck wanted to have a one-drop that could pick up the [Bonesplitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonesplitter) and charge into combat with it on turn two. The [Steelshaper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steelshaper) is an excellent man and will undoubtedly see abundant use during the *Mirrodin* Block Constructed season. With eight pieces of equipment I had a hard time making room for another two drop in the deck.

![Mask of Memory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mask+of+Memory)I know that [Empyrial Plate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empyrial+Plate) is a controversial card to leave out of this build but I prefer [Mask of Memory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+Memory). It costs less to equipâ€”allowing you to cast it and equip it on turn threeâ€”and it gives you both card advantage and card selection. The Mask may shove [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) into a dark corner form whence it is never heard from again. Not only do you not have to warp your mana base to play the Mask in this deck but you don't lose any card advantage if the intended wearer of the Mask is killed in response to you equipping it.One of the other decks I saw being played was a Blue-White control deck. Toward the end of the last PTQ season there were a number of regions that were dominated by Blue-White control. The deck should only get better in Standard where you have access to [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), [Rewind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind) and [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). Whenever I played Blue-White in the past I relied on [Nevinyrral's Disk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk) to get me out of tight fixes so I began to play around with adding [Oblivion Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Stone) into the deck. With the mana consistency generated by [Eternal Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Dragon) and [Temple of the False God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+the+False+God) it should not be unreasonable to reach eight mana in enough time to push your reset button.
