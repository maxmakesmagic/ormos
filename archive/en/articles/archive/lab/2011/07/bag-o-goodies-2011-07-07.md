Bag o' Goodies
==============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on July 7, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











So many Lab blueprints! They've flooded my mind, body, and traditional welcome sentence. Hmm... if those three were sinking, I probably wouldn't save the last one. It's so dull to fit into the formula each time. I might shake things up a bit here and there, you know. Like for instance today's column.

Due to the unprecedented ***Magic: The Gathering** Commander* squished in between the radical *New Phyrexia* and the latest core set, there's an abundance of cool new effects out there. Usually I'd pick one release and stick to that for one article, but this week I think I'll open it up to all three. A mixed bag of goodies, perhaps. We've got some decks left over from *New Phyrexia*, a deck based on an interesting ***Magic** 2012* card, and my first stab at a Commander deck of my own.

### Taking Commander

I might as well start off with that last one. A couple people seemed miffed that I didn't build actual Commander decks during Commander Week. I see how that would make sense, but still I opted to build 60-card decks as usual. Secretly though, in my Real Life chamber, I was playing around with the Political Puppets deck with fellow **Magic**ians. After some fun times with the deck, I decided to rebuild the deck from the ground up, with some of my stylistic touches.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl150_ruhan.jpg)  
For my Commander I went with [Ruhan of the Fomori](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruhan+of+the+Fomori), who ushered in a hefty Giant subtheme. Lots of Giant cards actually excel in multiplayer, examples being [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath) and [Taurean Mauler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taurean+Mauler) (lots of +1/+1 counters) and various board-clearing big guys (like [Hammerfist Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammerfist+Giant), [Bloodfire Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfire+Colossus), [Desolation Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolation+Giant), and [Thundercloud Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundercloud+Shaman)). Some other Giants provide the much required wacky factor in multiplayer, examples being [Arbiter of Knollridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbiter+of+Knollridge), [Favor of the Mighty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Favor+of+the+Mighty), and [Boldwyr Heavyweights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boldwyr+Heavyweights) (which is tasty when followed up with a board clearer!).

Here's my first stab at this format:

### Phyrexian Email

There's an idea that's been nibbling at me for a while. The Chancellor cycle from *New Phyrexia* intrigued me from the get go. Like the Leyline cycle of ***Magic** 2011 / Guildpact*, these cards have an effect on the board before the game has even begun, before the slightest land has filled the infinite void.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl150_chancellors.jpg)  
With nine Leylines and five Chancellors now in existence, it wasn't long before mad mailbox mainstay Darth Parallax came up with a wacky deck list for them. It's very avant-garde; more than half the deck is made up of Chancellors and Leylines. The goal, quite obviously, is to have your opening hand explode like fireworks. "Oh, before we start... mill seven, I get a 1/1 Goblin token, and you can't counter my creature spells." I'd be grinning from ear to ear, and that's not even the god hand.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl150_leylines.jpg)  
The god hand is to get lots of [Chancellors of the Tangle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chancellor+of+the+Tangle) in your opening hand, and spend that green mana on [Elvish Piper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Piper) or (easier) [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), which can find [Elvish Piper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Piper). [Hypergenesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) is another option. Once you start Piping creatures out, you can swarm with your Chancellors.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl150_gameState.jpg)  
Since I have lots of permanents of different colors in the deck, I decided to add two copies of [Bloom Tender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloom+Tender) to the deck. It's fetchable with [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and can really ramp things up manawise.

And now to twist one section into another without a headline: Another *New Phyrexia* card that I hadn't gotten around to using was [Xenograft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenograft). The color-shifted (and color-corrected) [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) has been doing well for itself, being used in many green Ally decks featuring [Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger) for infinite Wolf Ally tokens. I adore this combo and would have even made a deck around it for the column... except that I never got around to it.

![Xenograft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Xenograft)  
However, I have come up with a new way to use [Xenograft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenograft). A very new way. So new, it could have only come from ***Magic** 2012*. Specifically, the very first Spider lord, Arachnus Spinner.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl150_spinner.jpg)  
How could these two possibly be connected? Well, it's not a stretch to suggest naming Spiders with [Xenograft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenograft). That way, any creature you tap will fetch an Arachnus Web. What's vital here is that the Web doesn't have to be in the library; it can also be in the graveyard.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl150_2Cards1.jpg)  
So let's say we have a Web in the graveyard and the tag-team of [Xenograft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenograft) and Arachnus Spinner out. Tap a Spider to return Arachnus Web to the battlefield. What to enchant?

How about [Bramble Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramble+Elemental), a funny little card from the classic *Ravnica* set? Whenever it has an Aura attached to it for any reason, it produces two Saproling 1/1 tokens. Cute.

![Bramble Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bramble+Elemental)  
Except... they're not just Saprolings. They're also Spiders. And they can be tapped with the big bad Spinner. Hmm, if only we had a way to jettison the Web, so we could go infinite with Spider Saprolings (a new sensation, for me at least). [Gatherer of Graces](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatherer+of+Graces) can sacrifice Auras (and she's not too shabby in an Aura-focused deck), so there we have it.

To recap: Have [Xenograft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenograft) and Arachnus Spinner out, along with [Bramble Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramble+Elemental) and [Gatherer of Graces](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatherer+of+Graces). Tap a Spider to find Arachnus Web and attach it to [Bramble Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramble+Elemental). Make two 1/1 Spider Saprolings. Sacrifice the Web to the Gatherer. Repeat, using one of the new tokens each time. This works because of Arachnus Spinner's wording, which dodges the tokens' summoning sickness. Eventually you'll wind up with infinite 1/1 tokens. Do this all at the end of your opponent's turn to swing for the win next turn!
