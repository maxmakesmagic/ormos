Setting up the Bombs
====================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 23, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











At the end of Grand Prix Philadelphia, I went home with all the competitor’s deck lists. There was one question on my (and everyone else’s minds): Do broken rares really influence the *Onslaught* Sealed format to an unfair degree? I took all the decks, and tallied up the records of deck which *played* various bombs in the format. These bombs included the five pit fighter legends (Jareth, Arcanis, Visara, Rorix and Silvos), the best morph creatures ([Exalted Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exalted+Angel), [Quicksilver Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Dragon), [Grinning Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grinning+Demon) and [Silent Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter)), [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) effects ([Akroma’s Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%E2%80%99s+Vengeance) and [Starstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starstorm)), and three other heavy hitters ([Insurrection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insurrection), [Butcher Orgg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+Orgg), and Kamahl).

Other cards which might be considered bombs, but were not included in my tallies include [Aurification](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurification), [Glarecaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glarecaster), Oversold Cemetary, [Future Sight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight), [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth), [Hystrodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hystrodon), [Dragon Roost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Roost), [Tephraderm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tephraderm), [Mythic Proportions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mythic+Proportions), [Gustcloak Savior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Savior), and any uncommons ([Slice and Dice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slice+and+Dice), [Centaur Glade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Glade), and [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest)). 

![Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sparksmith)In addition, many players claim they’d rather open [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) (the agreed upon best common in the set) than any other card. Included in my numbers were the little red goblin who stood with the big, bad boys.All decks are broken down as follows:  
 Name of Card: # of decks (Match Record, % of total possible match points achieved)

Byes were *not* counted in these stats. These records reflect only the matches actually played during the swiss rounds. Also, these records do not include decks which decided not to play bombs. If a player decided not to run Rorix, Rorix wasn’t counted below.

And without further ado, let the numbers speak for themselves!
