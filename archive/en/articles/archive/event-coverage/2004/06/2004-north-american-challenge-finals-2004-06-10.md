2004 North American Challenge Finals
====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 10, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_alexshvartsman.jpg)
By Alex Shvartsman











### Game 1


Rietzl opened with an [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and an [Arcbound Worker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker), praying that his opponent wouldn't [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm). All Minicucci could do was try to accelerate his mana with [Birds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds) and [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves), so Rietzl was free to set up his board quickly – he got a pair of [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer)s into play and a [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). He used a [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) to remove one of the [Vine Trellis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vine+Trellis)es and then attacked with his whole team.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/nac04/aMinicucci2.jpg)*Domenic Minicucci*On the following turn, Minicucci cast a [Duplicant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant), removing one of the [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer)s and giving himself a large blocker. Rietzl cycled through some [Chromatic Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere)s and cast [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast). He then attacked and used a [Welding Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) to save his remaining [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer), taking out the [Duplicant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant). Minicucci played out a pair of [Birds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds) on his turn, now having access to eight mana, assuming he and his creatures could survive the turn.


Rietzl played a Ravager so Minicucci had to block [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer). Minicucci had nothing but a Colossus in hand so his outs were severely limited as Rietzl continued to attack every turn and finally Minicucci could not block both artifact creatures and had to give up the game.


### Sideboarding


**Minicucci**:  
 -4 [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom), -1 Kamahl, -1 [Sundering Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan)  
 +4 [Oxidize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize), +1 [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman), +1 [Vine Trellis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vine+Trellis)


**Rietzl**:  
 +3 [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), -3 [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast)


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/nac04/aRietzl3.jpg)*Paul Rietzl*Both players were off to a slow start. Minicucci took a mulligan and despite having kept his hand, Rietzl did not have much to do till the third turn. Minicucci used a [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) on his [Wirewood Symbiote](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote) to make up the card economy he lost in Paris, and Rietzl brought out a Ravager and a [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite). 


With second Symbiote in play, Minicucci was on the [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) plan, trying to kill Rietzl's artifacts faster than they were coming into play. It was no easy task as Rietzl played out a second Ravager and a [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer).


All Rietzl needed was an opening – an unblocked artifact creature that could get through. He was down to a [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer), a [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and a 5/5 Ravager against [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves), [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) and [Wirewood Symbiote](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote). If Minicucci blocked with a Symbiote he would be in a world of trouble next turn, but if he let one creature through, he stood to lose if Rietzl had [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast). Rietzl bluffed it perfectly, causing his opponent to block with just two creatures and dropping that Blast, lightning fast, onto the table.


**Final Result:** Rietzl 2 – Minicucci 0
