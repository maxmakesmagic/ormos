Feature Match Round Four - Yuri Markin versus Craig Jones
=========================================================



 Posted in [NEWS](/en/articles)











You will already have seen how Yuri and his W-U-R deck made it through. Craig is just entering the Grand Prix after having had three byes. He is also playing W-U-R but a more unusual one, with [Sunscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunscape+Familiar)s and [Lightning Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel)s as the creatures.


### Game One


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPWARSAW01/967.jpg)Craig had to mulligan an opening hand containing [Battlefield Forge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlefield+Forge) as it's only land. Yuri's decision was a little more difficult as he had two lands and was going second, but he chose to mulligan as well as he had no white mana for the two [Galina's Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galina%27s+Knight)s in his hand. His six cards didn't contain any land at all, so he probably regretted the choice. Eventually he kept a five card hand that was very similar to his opening draw, but with one less land.


Yuri missed a land drop on his second turn, but Craig only had [Repulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)s and Ices, so he couldn't really take advantage. He [Absorb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb)ed Yuri's attempt to play a [Galina's Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galina%27s+Knight), but a second one resolved.


The Knight attacked until Craig to [Repulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)d it, as he wanted to have an [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude) to counter it if Yuri replayed it. By Icing Markin's two blue sources - he was still stuck on four lands - Craig managed to get a [Lightning Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel) into play. Jones used his [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude) on Yuri's Angel, and finished the game with [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt) and Angel attacks, as Markin didn't have enough mana to get a spell through the Englishman's defences.


Craig Jones 1 - Yuri Markin 0


Of course Yuri sideboarded in his four [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s again and Craig took out his [Absorb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb)s, [Goblin Trenches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches), and [Evasive Action](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evasive+Action)s for [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s, and [Flametongue Kavu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu)s which are good against opposing Angels in this match. He also brought in two [Rakavolver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakavolver)s in the hope that Yuri had reduced his number of [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude)s.


### Game Two


Once again both players had to mulligan. Yuri's six-card hand was bad as well, with a [Coastal Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coastal+Tower) as his only land, and he had also drawn two [Aura Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Blast)s after Craig had taken out both of his [Goblin Trenches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trenches).


Nevertheless Yuri managed to draw land for a couple of turns and both players managed to get to three land before doing nothing for a while. Even with three land Yuri could force through a [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage), by Icing one of Craig's lands, and then [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)ing his [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay).


The Mage prevented Jones from summoning his [Lightning Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel), but he did get a [Sunscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunscape+Familiar) to slow down the assault. Markin brought out a [Goblin Legionnaire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Legionnaire) and continued to attack. Without enough land, Craig found his attempts to [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt) persistently [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)ed, and Craig died as much to damage from his own land as from attacks by the [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage).


Craig Jones 1 - Yuri Markin 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/GPWARSAW01/968.jpg)I was slightly surprised that Craig didn't bring some countermagic back in, particularly the [Evasive Action](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evasive+Action) as they seem quite good when you are going first.


For the third time this match, Craig's opening hand was rather dubious, but he kept this one, and it worked out for him as he drew the Mountain he needed to get all three colours of mana.


The early game was rather empty of spells, apart from a [Sunscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunscape+Familiar) on Jones's second turn, and he added a second Familiar a few turns later. The first real threat of the game was Yuri's [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) - but it was Excluded. By the time Craig played a third Wall there were only about eight minutes left in the round, and not one relevant card had really been cast. In mortal terror of [Exclude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude), and without counter-magic to back it up, Yuri decided to discard [Galina's Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galina%27s+Knight) rather than cast it. Only a [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt) from Craig elicited any sort of interest, as Yuri won a counter war by two [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s to one.


With a handful of ineffective [Aura Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Blast)s, and [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)s, Yuri let a [Rakavolver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakavolver) resolve. Perhaps he should have tried an Ice on one of Craig's lands, as the top card of his deck was an [Absorb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb). Craig cast another [Prophetic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Bolt), and Yuri [Absorb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb)ed it, and then [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)ed it. A rather bizarre order to cast the spells as it meant he didn't gain any life after Craig chose not to pay the mana for the [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt).


Nevertheless Markin didn't have an immediate way of killing the [Rakavolver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakavolver), he could only play a [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) naming [Absorb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb) to protect an answer if he drew it. Time was called but the [Rakavolver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakavolver) was large enough to end the game in Craig's final turn.


Craig Jones defeats Yuri Markin 2 - 1
