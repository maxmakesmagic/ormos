Temur Ascendancy at StarCityGames Open
======================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on February 13, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






This week's last deck is an infinite Ascendancy combo deck. But it isn't [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy). Today's deck is [Temur Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Ascendancy) combo. The characteristic of this combo deck is that just looking at the decklist, it doesn't look like a combo deck at all.



Other than [Temur Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Ascendancy) being a little unordinary, the deck is a regular green deck with mana creatures and big monsters. Although I guess there are a couple of odd ones on the side. You could probably trick someone into believing it's a constellation deck with twist; or maybe green devotion. I bet there are a bunch of games the deck wins just by attacking with a manifested [Nylea, God of the Hunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%2C+God+of+the+Hunt); [Polukranos, World Eater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polukranos%2C+World+Eater); and [Arbor Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Colossus).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Temur+Ascendancy)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Ascendancy)

However, there is indeed an infinite combo installed in this deck. The key creature is the new face from *Fate Reforged*: [Temur Sabertooth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Sabertooth). This creature has a very unique ability for an uncommon. Originally, it was designed to protect other creatures from removal or to replay creatures with trigger abilities, while giving itself indestructibility. However, under a certain condition, you can make a loop.



Here is the process:



* Have [Temur Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Ascendancy) in play with seven or more green devotion, including [Temur Sabertooth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Sabertooth) and [Voyaging Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyaging+Satyr), plus [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx).
* Two mana to activate [Temur Sabertooth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Sabertooth), two mana to summon [Voyaging Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyaging+Satyr), and two mana to activate Nykthos after untapping it from the Satyr. If you can produce more than six mana from Nykthos you have infinite mana.


The next question is what to do with this mana. Having [Temur Sabertooth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Sabertooth), you should have a couple of choices how to kill your opponent. You have unlimited resources with [Eidolon of Blossoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Blossoms) and [Primordial Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra); infinite pump plus trample with [Nylea, God of the Hunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%2C+God+of+the+Hunt); infinite Hornet tokens with [Hornet Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen); and/or the ability to wipe your opponent's board with Polukranos's monstrosity. Your choice.
