Double Your Pleasure
====================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on August 12, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Hello, and welcome to another edition of Building on a Budget. Many of you probably already know that [Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) is one of my favorite cards in Standard. I haven't had the chance to sleeve up my Axes recently, but that's probably just because a certain key piece of information had eluded me for the past few months.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab51_axe.jpg)  
[Marisi's Twinclaws](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marisi%27s+Twinclaws) is a card I've wanted to play with since it was printed. It was printed alongside [Colossal Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colossal+Might), and its power level definitely warrants Constructed play. The key piece of information I had been missing was the Twinclaws' creature type. [Marisi's Twinclaws](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marisi%27s+Twinclaws) is a bona fide Warrior. This means that after your opponent plays [Hallowed Burial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Burial) you can simply cast [Marisi's Twinclaws](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marisi%27s+Twinclaws) and bash in for 8 damage. Excited yet?

Here's another fun fact about an underplayed *Shards* block creature: [Viashino Slaughtermaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Slaughtermaster) is also a card-carrying Warrior. This means my new [Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) deck can play a full eight creatures with double strike. Imagine having two copies of [Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) in play—your opponent can virtually never tap out.

Enough hype—let's make a deck list. I'll start by taking a look at what cards might make the cut beyond [Marisi's Twinclaws](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marisi%27s+Twinclaws) and [Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe). From there, we can trim the deck and make it as beautiful as it deserves to be.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab51_theCardsHeader.jpg)  
**[Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon):** A key creature in the Warrior archetype. I think we can confidently assume that we'll be playing four of this fine card.

**[Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel):** A very efficient one-drop. One-mana 2/2s are the gold standard for aggressive, creature-based decks. [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) is a very likely candidate for the main deck.

**[Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves):** Not a Warrior, but these Elves enable us to accelerate into our Battle-Axes. We will probably end up playing at least a few copies of "Old Faithful."

**[Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher):** Deals don't really get better than this. A 3/3 for two mana that can fearlessly charge into [Plumeveil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumeveil) seems like a solid roleplayer. As long as our Elf count is high enough we can count on this being a four-of.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab51_theCardsCross1.jpg)  
**[Viashino Slaughtermaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Slaughtermaster) and [Marisi's Twinclaws](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marisi%27s+Twinclaws):** I've already explained how good double strike is in this type of deck. I want to give the list a test drive with a full count of eight double strikers.

**[Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting):** I've always been a huge fan of [Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting) in aggressive Warrior decks. If we have room for the [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) package then [Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting) may be a very good choice for this deck. If not, I think we'll be better off playing some spot removal.

**[Colossal Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colossal+Might):** Perhaps the best pump spell printed since [Berserk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserk). When playing the deck, you should be careful to not get blown out by [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) or other instant-speed spot removal. It's very important to watch your opponent and be aware of how confident they seem. If they were just given a great opportunity to blow you out with spot removal and didn't, then perhaps you should just play your [Colossal Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colossal+Might)s with reckless abandon and hope for the best. Cards like this are great skill-testers. You can and will steal a lot of games with [Colossal Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colossal+Might). You just need to find a happy medium between patience and aggression.

**[Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe):** This is the reason we're building this deck to begin with. This card was Tier 1 material when it was first printed in Standard. I think we can prove that it still has what it takes to cut the mustard these days.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab51_theCardsCross2.jpg)  
**[Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect):** Another classic friend of the Axe. When [Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) was first printed, [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) was its partner in crime. Cast my [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect), attach my Axe, make a token, attach my Axe, and swing was a regular day in the neighborhood. The printing of [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) definitely changes things, but [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) is still under consideration.

**[Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang):** Probably the best Warrior in standard. [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) is fast, efficient, and reasonably costed. Almost definitely a four-of in our final list.

**[Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt):** One of the best spot removal spells in Standard (alongside [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile)). I really want to squeeze a playset of these into the list.

**[Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf):** One of the most powerful spells you can cast in Standard. It's an Elf, and it "tutors" up our Battle-Axe. I can virtually guarantee that four of these will make the cut.

The final list misses out on a lot of the cards I'd like to be playing. I would really like to be playing [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) over the [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect)s, but I couldn't bring myself to a point where my deck didn't have a high enough Elf count to play [Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher). After I play a few matches with the deck I'll go back and take a look at what I might be able to change about the list.

I made a fairly generic sideboard to use for now:

After playing a few games I think the deck needs [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and probably can't support [Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher) once we cut the [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect)s.
