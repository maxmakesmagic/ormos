Round 11: Jeff Cunningham v. Phil Freneau
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 24, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpla02/936.jpg)Canada's own Jeff Cunningham is looking to repeat the performance that landed him the title at Grand Prix - Philadelphia a month ago. He's off to a great start, already 2-0 on the day. Former ABU member Phil Freneau is right there with him, and as always looks slightly on-edge.

The game did not start auspiciously for Cunningham, with a mulligan. Then another. Then another. He stopped at three lands and a morph creature. Freneau went to six, out of a sense of fairness. He did not go further.

Cunningham's morph was answered with another by Freneau. [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) was a little late to the party, and Freneau swung in. Cunningham let it by. Frenau made a [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber), and he was happy to trade it for Cunningham's morph on the following turn.

Cunningham's cycled [Krosan Tusker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Tusker) helped make up some of the lost ground, and it even turned up [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth) for the following turn. Freneau meanwhile had [Crown of Suspicion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion) for the [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) and [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk), then [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) for the Baloth. Cunningham tried [Symbiotic Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf), and traded it for Freneau's morph (a [Foothill Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foothill+Guide)). [Severed Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Severed+Legion) followed.

His last gasp was a [Threaten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threaten) on Freneau's [Severed Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Severed+Legion), which he strangely allowed. Cunningham hit for four and sacrificed it. It hardly mattered. [Glarecaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glarecaster) was just a 3/3 body to speed Cunningham's inevitable demise.

Freneau 1 - Cunningham 0

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpla02/935.jpg)The board froze early in game two, with Cunningham's [Wirewood Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Herald) and morph opposite Freneau's curve of [Festering Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin), [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) and [Screeching Buzzard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard). Freneau was more than content to hit for two while frustrating Cunningham's board. 

[Crown of Suspicion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion) increased his clock. Cunningham, on six lands still hadn't made another play. His seventh hit and he tapped four for [Symbiotic Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf). He swung in with the Herald, and Freneau blocked to kill it. That let Cunningham search out his [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage), which he put into play.

Freneau had no shortage of answers. [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) dispatched the Savage, and [Cabal Archon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Archon) hit the table to [Threaten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threaten) a really short game. Cunningham responded with a too-slow [Symbiotic Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Wurm). [Battlefield Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlefield+Medic) for Freneau was icing on the cake. Cunningham swung all out, but Freneau was not duped into losing, somehow. Cunningham extended the hand.

Phil Freneau defeats Jeff Cunningham 2-0
