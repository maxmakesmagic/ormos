Saturday, March 21: 9:17a.m. – Trials decklists.
------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

Three byes are always something you want coming in to a Grand Prix, and Grand Prix Singapore is no exception. GP Trial events have been running throughout the region for the past few months, with first prize being the much-coveted trio of wins. For those who had yet to earn their byes, the traditional Grand Prix trials were running hot last night, their spots filling up quickly as players queued for the opportunity at a leg up in the main event. The winning decklists of the five trials you ask? Well, I have them right here!

Saturday, March 21: 10:27a.m. – Round 2: An Extended Primer
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

While you'd expect everyone reading this to know at least a little bit about Extended, it's a fair assumption that not everyone is going to be on the same page. I know for a fact that my friends Jen and Martin are reading along at home, and they know practically nothing about the game! So with that in mind, I've summed up the key decks in the format at the moment. Although being able to understand what I'm blathering on about here may still end up being a bit of a stretch for Jen and Martin, I trust they'll give it a go anyway, and with any luck, this will be of some help to some of you as well.



![Venser, Shaper Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant)

Faeries: Unlike the Standard version of this control strategy, Extended Faeries seldom run any Black cards, which means [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) is off sulking in a corner somewhere. Being mostly mono-blue, with access to a few Dual Lands via their Sac Land friends to help power up [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) , this deck often has more Wizards than Faeries. This is because [Venser, Shaper Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant) and [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) tend to skew the numbers slightly by being Human Wizards, rather than flighty wee Faeries. The real back-bone of this deck comes from [Riptide Laboratory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Laboratory) , which give [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) a great deal more range than usual, thanks to the generally low mana curve found in Extended.


Next Level Blue: This is basically Faeries, with less of them and a great deal more [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) s. Four more [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) s, to be precise. With fewer Faeries, Next Level Blue also tends to run more Spell-based [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) s. This helps in one of the tougher match ups for Blue decks, Affinity. Their unusually high mana costs tend to render things like [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) quite ineffective, and the Fat Green Man really carries his weight here.


Naya Burn: While [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) sees next to no play in Standard, it shines here with a single [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) satisfying both criteria for the Nacatl to be upgraded from a Medium to a Large. Together with [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) and other assorted cheap beaters, the Nacatl takes people into burn range as quickly as possible, before the burn spells come online, seeking to finish off the happy meal the animals started.



![Might of Alara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Might+of+Alara)

Gaea's Might get there: Although I guess it should be called [Might of Alara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Alara) get there now? I dunno. Another deck that functions solely because of the incredible mana bases available in Extended. This deck is basically a greedier version of Naya Burn, and instead of a wealth of burn spells, manages to back up the [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) s and [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) s with the likes of Tidehollow Skuller and the decks name-sake, an Instant Speed +5/+5 for a single Green mana. The degrees of greediness can vary, often up as far as the double-strikers [Viashino Slaughtermaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Slaughtermaster) and [Boros Swiftblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Swiftblade) to really get value out of the Mights.


Bant Aggro: Was touted as The New Deck at Pro Tour Berlin for all of two seconds before The Worst Kept Secret, Elves stomped all over it. Now that the Elves are being kept in check, Bant Aggro is having some time in the sun, combining the new and beefy [Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) with the old and tasty [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) , with a smattering of countermagic thrown in to throw people off.


TEPS: The Extended Perfect Storm is, as the name suggests, a Storm count combo deck. The fundamental turn for this deck can be as early as two or three, but is usually around turn four, where it will explode with a flurry of mana accelerants before dropping a Minds Desire on the table for a bunch. The kill card of choice is usually a [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) , but can easily be an [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) . I've even seen people on Magic Online packing [Dragonstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) as an answer to [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) and [Trickbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trickbind) . A single [Dragonstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) can still drop a 5/5 on you that will eat a fair amount of the board before eating your face.


Affinity: Many would have thought that Affinity would be a deck that received no help from sets these days, after it had caused the banning of the Artifact lands in Standard all those years ago. However, those tricksie Esper Mages have managed to sneak a few new weapons across the boarder to Affinity, who is even now, still riding strong with Master or Etherium and battling Combo decks with [Ethersworn Canonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist) .



![Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Engineered+Explosives)

Elves: The fastest deck in the format, but also the most fragile. The Elves combo deck still manages to exist in the same way that Affinity does. If everyone is packing hate for it, it will stay down, but as the results for either deck wane, so will the sideboard cards dedicated to hating them out. Elves can suffer from splash damage somewhat, with cards that are good against them also being good enough to maindeck anyway, such as [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) . With Elves taking out the top spot last weekend in Hanover, it seems unlikely that people will be caught out by the Mini Green Menace this weekend.


AggroLoam: And this is the corner [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) is sulking in. This deck first appeared in straight Green/Black flavors in the hands of Mike Jacob who piloted it into the top 8 of GP: LA at the start of this year. Since then, it has been seen packing some White cards as well, for the impressive [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) and Path of [Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exile) . This is probably the deck with the most late game inevitability thanks to Life of the Loam, powering up [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) and Eventually [Worm Harvest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worm+Harvest) .


And now, on to the tournament itself. Who knows? Maybe we'll even added a new archetype or two to that list before we're done this weekend.

Saturday, March 21: 12:23p.m. – Round 4: Ruel vs. Vidugiris
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

What a powerhouse of a feature match to start off the weekend. With the byes wearing off this round, the Big Guns are finally entering the fray. On my left we have Olivier Ruel, Hall of Fame inductee in 2008, and on my right, Gaudenis Vidugiris, Lithuanian national team member last year, and finalist in last weekends Grand Prix Hanover.


Ruel lost the roll, dropping a [Breeding Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pool) into play tapped, that Vidugiris matched by searching one out with his [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) . Ruel went for an [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) for two, but Vidugiris [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) ed it before suspending an [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) s and passing the turn back. Ruel's [Vedalken Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) met a [Condescend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend) , so he followed that up with a [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) for Zero.
