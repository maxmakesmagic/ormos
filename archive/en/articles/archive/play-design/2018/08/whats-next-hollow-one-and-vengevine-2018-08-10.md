What's Next for Hollow One and Vengevine?
=========================================



 Posted in **Play Design**
 on August 10, 2018 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20181218035009im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_Tom-Ross.jpg?itok=C-Hg0IJ3)
By Tom Ross











Graveyard decks have always been a mainstay in Modern. Various incarnations of [Dredge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge) have come and gone. [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) has been spotted here and there. [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) pops up in assorted decks when people are playing removal-heavy decks.


Before I get into the results of [Pro Tour 25th Anniversary](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/pt25a) and what Modern graveyard decks will do moving forward, let's look back to March at [Pro Tour *Rivals of Ixalan*](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptrix).

* [![Goblin Lore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Lore)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lore)

Hollow One (the deck) has several good openings that can play multiple [Hollow One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+One)s (the card) as early as turn one. [Burning Inquiry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Inquiry) can do it in one fell swoop. A combination of [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith) does it too. There are redundant elements that are all slightly different, which makes proper sequencing so important.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodghast)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flamewake+Phoenix)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamewake+Phoenix)
[Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and [Flamewake Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamewake+Phoenix) provide resilience against spot removal and sweepers . . . or anyone intending on blocking you. It also has big creatures in [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang) and [Gurmag Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gurmag+Angler) and a suite of burn/interaction like [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and [Collective Brutality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Brutality) that can finish the job.


The [Hollow One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+One) decks were dismissed at first because of their random discard elements, which are generally unappealing for those aiming to maximize every edge. Turns out, the deck is super strong, and the cloud of variance was merely a distraction from its high power level.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Surgical+Extraction)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Relic+of+Progenitus)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus)
For a while, Black-Red [Hollow One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+One) was consider a top deck, and perhaps even the deck to beat. Opponents had to learn that a single [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) or [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) wasn't enough to tame [Hollow One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+One).


A few months ago, I saw a sweet [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) deck that I just had to take for a spin.

This version packed two new angles to attack from:


* [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) into [Reckless Bushwhacker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Bushwhacker).
* Discard [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), then play two creatures.

All while still preserving a lot of the potency of multiple turn-one [Hollow One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+One)s. The piece seemed to fit well, which had me excited. Also, I like simply getting my beatdown on with a smattering of random small creatures.


The variety of explosive opening hands is what had me hooked to this build. Every hand was a surprising combination of synergy.


While the green version was beloved to me, most players kept to the more established black-red Hollow One deck. That is until *Core Set 2019* brought [Stitcher's Supplier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier) to Modern.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier)
Then Pro Tour 25th Anniversary happened. From that tournament emerged a powerful [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) deck that's prominently red and black.


The deck has been going 5-0 in *Magic Online* Leagues for weeks now. Of course, it takes a while for *Magic Online* results to affect the real world. Let's take a look at a list from the Pro Tour.

* [![Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vengevine)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)
* [![Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bridge+from+Below)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below)
* [![Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hangarback+Walker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker)
* [![Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Walking+Ballista)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista)
* [![Insolent Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Insolent+Neonate)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insolent+Neonate)
* [![Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Faithless+Looting)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting)

The deck has several explosive starts. The namesake [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s can enter the battlefield as early as turn one if you play a [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), discard a couple, and cast two zero-mana creatures in [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker) or [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista). [Insolent Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insolent+Neonate) is particularly powerful since it's a one-cost creature that can discard and help set up [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) on its own, all while digging you deeper into your deck.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Greater+Gargadon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Viscera+Seer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer)
[Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below) also benefits from you casting 0/0s that immediately die. A common opening is to bin a [Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below) and make two or three Zombies to get the pressure going. [Insolent Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insolent+Neonate), [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer), and [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) are ways to have agency over your flow of tokens. The recursion of [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) give you plenty of food.


[Stitcher's Supplier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier) is a double-duty enabler that puts cards from your library into your graveyard . . . twice. The fact that it's a Zombie to go along with [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) is what really makes it shine.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gravecrawler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)
Since you actually *cast* [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) from the graveyard (as opposed to simply entering the battlefield like [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)), that means [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) will see it; [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) will count toward the two creatures required to bring [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) back.


While Black-Red [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) might've been the breakout deck a week or two ago, it still has room to innovate. I've seen people dabbling in various additional discard outlets.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Collective+Brutality)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Brutality) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lightning+Axe)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cathartic+Reunion)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathartic+Reunion)
They're all well and good on their own. They get the cards that you want in your graveyard there pretty efficiently. Then I saw a list playing a card that serves multiple roles.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodrage+Brawler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodrage+Brawler)
A well-sized creature that triggers [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) as well as a discard outlet. You even get a discounted 4/3 if it's your last card in hand.


Perhaps [Bloodrage Brawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodrage+Brawler) is the next move?

I always like presenting a new axis to pressure my opponents when the price is right; including one [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) is a price I'm willing to pay.


I like [Collective Brutality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Brutality) as the deck's discard outlet. It provides the most amount of interaction compared to the others. The choice also takes a page from the [Hollow One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+One) book, where I noticed the lists gravitate toward [Collective Brutality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Brutality) instead of the narrower discard outlets.


Of course, the flexible discard outlets are so close in power that it's a matter of preference. Perhaps after a few months of the world grinding out games a "best version" will emerge.
