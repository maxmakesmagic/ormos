The Many Flavors of Delver of Secrets
=====================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 2, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 







Since it's printing in *Innistrad*, [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) has been a Legacy staple, fueling many different blue tempo decks. [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), and [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) are Legacy staples that are commonly seen with everyone's favorite [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration). RUG. RWU. UR. BUG. Many different color combinations have been successful in combination with [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), but Legacy aficionado Eric Rill has taken it up a notch. He built a four-color deck featuring [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman); [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer); and a singleton copy of [Edric, Spymaster of Trest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edric%2C+Spymaster+of+Trest). I can only imagine how incredible [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) + [Edric, Spymaster of Trest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edric%2C+Spymaster+of+Trest) are in combination. 


![Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Young+Pyromancer)
 One of my favorite interactions in this deck has to do with the [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) and [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) from the sideboard. If you cast [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer), you can cast [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), generating a token from [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer), which is perfect fodder for sacrificing to [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy). Not only that, but playing the flashback is essentially free, since the Elemental token you sacrifice to pay the cost on the flashback of [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) is replaced by the [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer)! [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) is also powerful with [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), allowing you to know what to name. 


 Eric Rill took his new take on [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) to a 1st-place finish at [SCG Milwaukee](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/050414_milwaukee.html), and just to prove it was no fluke, Michael Ferrante finished 7th two weeks later, at [SCG Detroit](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/190414_detroit.html). There might be a new [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck in the Legacy metagame!
