Counterintelligence
===================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on July 2, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget, the column dedicated to build decks using **Magic Online** that cost 30 tickets or less! We're in the middle of *Eventide* previews this week on **magicthegathering.com**, and poor ol' me never gets a preview card of my very own. So I gussied up my courage, and said to mean ol' Kelly Digges, "Hey Kelly? Can I get me one o' them preview cards?" and he said "Heck no, Ben! I'm-a busy previewing [one of my own](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/sf1)! But I kept asking, and I kept asking, and finally Kelly agreed to meet me halfway: he let me put the focus of this week's deck under a hide/reveal link.


Click here to see what Counterintelligence is all about!




![Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Power+Conduit)

 


Ever since the persist mechanic debuted, I'd wanted to get my hands on a playset of [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit), and take them down to Funkytown. Nate Heiss once tackled [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit)[in this very column](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/nh56), but counters have come a long way since *Darksteel*. And by a long way, I mean from the graveyard as -1/-1 counters. Let me break it down:


### Peanut Butter Theory


Ok boys and girls, it's time to share with you Ben's Peanut Butter Theory:


First you make the peanuts.


*Then* you make the peanut butter!


What's this have to do with **Magic** and deck building? Well, you ever gone to one of those restaurants that brings you a big ol' tub of peanuts in a metal bucket, and you're supposed to shell them and eat them and then throw them on the ground? And then they make the busboy or wait staff sweep them up every hour or so? This is nothing like that. Weren't you listening? This is about PEANUT BUTTER! If you don't have peanuts, you can't make peanut butter—or in this case, if you don't have the base materials to work with, you can't have a refined end product.


To boot: [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) is great for moving around counters. However, *Lorwyn* and *Shadowmoor* blocks are focused around (among other mechanics) two themes: +1/+1 counters and -1/-1 counters. And when a creature has a +1/+1 counter and a -1/-1 counter at the same time, the two counters negate one another and vanish. So:


 



![Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kitchen+Finks)

Peanut: [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit)
Peanut: [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)


[Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks): 3/2, gain 2 life, persist, yadda yadda yadda. [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) dies: 2/1, -1/-1 counter, gain 2 life. [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) dies again: Dead Finks. Hello graveyard!


But let's rewind here! [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) of the just-dead: a 3/2 with a -1/-1 counter—no good for persist! Why, let's tap [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit). That removes that nasty -1/-1 counter, and turns it into a nifty +1/+1 counter! Holy schlabolies, 4/3 Finks! Ready to persist again!


### PEANUT BUTTER!


Or, how about we grok this one out for size:

![Etched Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Etched+Oracle)

What likes +1/+1 and charge counters? Most of the cards in the deck, that's what! This is a base green deck with many ways to produce other colors of mana, without sacrificing stability in the early game. [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots), [Devoted Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), and the Talismans give an early boost of mana, leading up to [Orochi Hatchery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orochi+Hatchery) or [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) as kill conditions. [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), [Safehold Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safehold+Elite), [Sun Droplet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) and [Clearwater Goblet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clearwater+Goblet) are in the deck to buy time, while [Etched Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Oracle) works as a reusable card-drawing mechanism—mainly because with [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit), you can get it past four counters, and then use it every few turns to [Ancestral Recall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall) yourself.

So, you might ask yourself, why am I using Talismans in this deck instead of green mana acceleration, *Ravnica* block signets, or other non-pain-inducing ways of accelerating mana? Simple—if I have two [Sun Droplet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet)s in play, I want to be able to have a way to damage myself, so that I can give both of the Droplets counters and *gain* life if my opponent is not actively damaging me!


I also have ten lands that can fuel [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) in case that my other cards in play cannot—but still, I like the idea of producing a green mana with [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots), making it a 0/4, and then using [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) to bump-a-dump my Wall to a 1/6 blocker. And hey, what's wrong with dropping [Orochi Hatchery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orochi+Hatchery) on turn four as a two-Snake-producing contraption, and then using [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) to grow it by a Snake a turn? Heck, if I've already got the Conduit in play, I can have four Snakes by turn five: move a counter the turn I play Hatchery, and move a counter the turn I'm going to activate it!


This deck has so much love, and so little jelly. Let's see how it does in play in the Extended casual room!


**Game 1: fstatc (Mono-Artifact Equipment)**


Fstatc starts the game with double [Cloudpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost), [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), [Myr Servitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Servitor), [Paradise Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradise+Mantle), and [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer). I get [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and Saltcrusted Step, along with [Talisman of Progress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talisman+of+Progress) and [Vivid Grove](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Grove). This allows me to play a [Etched Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Oracle) with four counters on turn four, and [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) (wiping away fstatc's creatures) on turn five. Fstatc then plays [Blinkmoth Urn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Urn), but I have lots of artifacts to play out of my hand with the extra mana. I drop [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) and [Mirrodin's Core](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrodin%27s+Core), and start moving the charge counters of the Core onto my [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion), killing fstatc quickly thereafter.


Record: 1-0


**Game 2: Golden [Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void) (Green-Blue "Comes into Play" Creatures)**

Golden [Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void) plays [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) for [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman), killing my Conduit. He then gets another [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) for [Duplicant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant), removes [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) from the game, and wrecks me out of the game.

**Game 12: killerkow316 (Red-Green Land Thin / [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher))**
This is a very long and intense game. Killerkow316 begins with game with a lot of mana acceleration thanks to Sakura Tribe-Elder and [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach), and stops me from abusing [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) / [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) with [Duplicant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant). He then drops a [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher), and I answer with double [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks). I keep my Finks alive and running with the [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit), and he starts getting down [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum)s (which I kill with [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap)). Eventually he starts recurring his graveyard with [Gaea's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Blessing), and I start turning on the offense with two Finks and a Redcap against his 0/5 [Duplicant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant).


In no short time, killerkow drops a second [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher), and I get [Sun Droplet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) to stop any real offense. He nearly wipes the board clean with [Starstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starstorm) plus Charbelchers on my Finks, but I get [Ion Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ion+Storm), and start shooting off his guys. He uses [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) to fetch out [Panglacial Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Panglacial+Wurm), and we trade creatures multiple times. In the end, I stick a [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) to the board against his two Charbelchers and get enough charge counters on my [Sun Droplet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) to deal the last 8 damage with my [Ion Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ion+Storm).


Record: 6-6


I've hit the .500 mark, and that's a great place to be, considering I started at .200. The red added a ton to the deck, including ways to directly kill the opponent without needing to get a [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) or a 4/3 Finks. Other cards I considered using for this deck include [Ageless Entity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ageless+Entity), [Spike Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder), Thallid, [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths), [Chainbreaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainbreaker), [Sculpting Steel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sculpting+Steel), [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism), [Well of Lost Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Well+of+Lost+Dreams), so if you're going to take this deck for a spin, feel free to innovate however you'd like!
