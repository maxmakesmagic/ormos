 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**

By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on March 29, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by a serious moment about April 1.


This year, April 1 is a Saturday. We don't publish new content on Saturdays (typically) that isn't event coverage and, as such, we are not doing anything for April Fools' Day. I'm telling you this now, as far away from April 1 as possible, so that there's at least the slightest chance you'll believe me. This isn't me throwing you off the scent, this isn't part of a long con, and it isn't misdirection. As a company, we are not doing anything for April Fools'.


Seriously. It's a Saturday. We'll all be home enjoying the holiday with our families, eating Spam like tradition dictates.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Stephen Menendian's Doomsday](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/magic-online/vintage-super-league-season-6-decklists)

Today I wanted to show off a deck with [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual)—which was revealed as an *Amonkhet* *Invocation* yesterday on, fittingly, Vintage Super League—and it just so happened that there was exactly one deck with exactly one copy of [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) in Vintage Super League—Doomsday by Stephen Menendian. The deck also happens to have [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)—also an *Amonkhet Invocation*.


If you haven't seen this deck in action on Vintage Super League, it's very complicated. Even if you have, you might not get what's going on. The basic gist is to resolve [Doomsday](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomsday) and set up a five-card library such that you win, usually, that very turn, typically by decking yourself with [Laboratory Maniac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Laboratory+Maniac) and some combination of [Gush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush), [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), or [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), depending on the situation.


It's all very complex and weird (aka, Vintage), but it's a wild ride that can utilize [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) as part of its weird chain of events.
