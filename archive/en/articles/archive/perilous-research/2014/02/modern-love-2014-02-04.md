Modern Love
===========



 Posted in **Perilous Research**
 on February 6, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive [**Magic Online**](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Digital/MagicOnline.aspx?x=mtg/digital/magiconline/newsandupdate) column. Since we last met, Modern's banned list [saw some changes](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/feature/285). [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) was banned, while [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) and [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) were unbanned. Sure, this is a small change in terms of size, but the implications are huge. With a Modern Pro Tour just around the corner, the effects of the new changes are far-reaching. *Born of the Gods* Prerelease events start in less than two weeks on **Magic Online**. Until then, we'll take a break from **Magic Online** metagame speculation in favor of more relevant subject-matter. Today, we'll be exploring some of the new possibilities in Modern with the recent changes to the banned list. 

Bitterblossom
[Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) has been unbanned! [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)'s legality has always been a hotly contested issue. The card, while powerful, isn't oppressive in a format as large as Modern. There are a few strategies, and a few cards in particular, that are sure to be very excited about the prospect of playing with [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). 

  
 The first and most obvious deck for [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) to unlock is Faeries. Faeries is one of the greatest Standard decks of all time. The deck is tremendously good at staying ahead once it's in the lead and its ability to completely dominate control and combo with a draw-go plan makes it a strong choice for the Pro Tour. Many of you are already familiar with Faeries, but a quick refresher course never hurt anyone. The best piece ever written on Faeries is by Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa, hall of famer and greatest Faeries player of all time. I recommend reading his [Faeries primer](http://www.channelfireball.com/home/pvs-playhouse-playing-fae-from-a-to-z/) if you plan on playing Modern in a world of [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). 

 Making a new Faeries list will be difficult. We know the appropriate ingredients, but the numbers and card choices will depend greatly on the landscape of the new Modern format. The biggest question deck builders face when attempting to build a Faeries deck is whether or not they should be including [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona). [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) is sure to improve many White-Black Token strategies, and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) will remain one of the strongest cards in the format. With all the opposing 1-toughness fliers that people are already battling [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) with, it stands to reason that [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) would be very strong in the current Modern environment.

 Faeries is the obvious home for [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), but White-Black Tokens is sure to be very happy about unlocking the enchantment. White-Black Tokens matches up very well in a format where the removal spells of choice are [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix), [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay), and [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). People are happy to kill a [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) or [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) with these spells, but things get awkward when they're forced to use a whole spell to kill one of our tokens. The White-Black Tokens deck gets to play with [Zealous Persecution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Persecution). [Zealous Persecution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Persecution) is exceptional against Faeries and also gets a lot stronger now that players will be forced to play [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) over [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman). 

[Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) is another big winner with [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) being reintroduced to the format. It's hard to beat an active [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) and [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) provides us with an endless stream of evasive creatures ready to be equipped.

Wild Nacatl
 Sure, [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) is a great card, but [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) is arguably the best one-mana creature in the format now that it's been unbanned. (Much less arguably now that it doesn't have to compete with [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman).) [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl)'s return to the format means that aggressive decks will once again have footing in the exceptionally powerful Modern format. 

  
 Since [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) was banned, we've seen some success from aggressive artifact strategies with [Cranial Plating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating), but the format became a haven for control and combo strategies to duke it out. Reintroducing [Wild Nacatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) means that "Zoo" decks of all varieties will have an opportunity to prove their worth in the Modern format. 

 Zoo can take on a lot of shapes and sizes. One of the best examples of this diversity can be seen in the [Top 8 decklists from Pro Tour Austin](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptaus09/top8decks). Brian Kibler, the eventual winner, played a slower and slightly bigger Zoo deck that was well suited for aggressive creature mirrors. Meanwhile, the Czech master, Martin Juza, Top 8ed the very same tournament with an extremely aggressive version of the deck.
