Akito Shinoda showed that greed is good in draft with his Five-Color Goodstuff deck, defeating China's Bo Sun and his Mardu Control in the finals of Grand Prix Shizuoka.

TOP 8 COVERAGE



In the twilight of *Khans of Tarkir* Limited, some 2205 players descended on Shizuoka, Japan to vie for greatness. The field was a cross-section of the *Magic*-playing populace. Amateurs shuffling up for their first major event sat beside veterans from the earliest days of Japaese Pro *Magic*. There were visiting heavyweights like #2 Ivan Floch and #9 Lee Shi Tian. And of course there were Japan’s current all-stars, #7 Yuuya Watanabe and #20 Kentaro Yamamoto, fresh off their Top 4 performance at World Championships. Two days and thousands of matches of *Magic* later only one player remained standing: Akito Shinoda.


Day 1 was Sealed Deck, and the story was mana. Players were stretching their manabases to head into a fourth and sometimes a fifth color in pursuit of more power. It wasn’t just lands, either. The Japanese pros had embraced the clan Banners as one more way to make the most of their pools. While most of the pros struggled through with 7-2 records, a whopping nine players put up perfect records: Daisuke Ueda, Hiroya Wada, Sho Ishikawa, Akio Chiba, Fumiya Matsumoto, Hiroshi Ishida and Akihiro Kashima.


Of course that was all preamble to Day 2’s draft tables, and one by one the 9-0’s fell by the wayside. Round 15 was heartbreak for most, including Lee and Yamamoto. The only ranked player to crack the Top 8 was #23 Yuuki Ichikawa, who cast a fearful shadow over the others, players still looking for their first big finish. However, he was upset in the semifinals by Shinoda, who had drafted a sweet five-color deck. On the other side of the bracket it was Bo Sun of China and his Mardu Control deck. Bo had managed a quarterfinal appearance at GP Kobe last year, and now was just one match away from hoisting the trophy.


But Shinoda would not be denied. In their first game he weathered a storm of renewal and then landed Sage of the Inward Eye, taking over completely. The second game began with early trades, but turn six again brought the mighty Sage, and Shinoda followed up with Ashcloud Phoenix and Abzan Ascendancy, steamrolling to victory. Congratulations to Akito Shinoda, Grand Prix Shizuoka 2015 Champion!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Welcome to Day 2 of Grand Prix Shizuoka 2015!



Yesterday, the original field of 2,205 players had been whittled down to just 243 after nine grueling rounds of *Khans of Tarkir* Sealed deck. It was a day filled with intensive competition, and at the end there were seven players left standing with pristine 9-0 records, all from Japan: Daisuke Ueda, Hiroya Wada, Sho Ishikawa, Akio Chiba, Fumiya Matsumoto, Hiroshi Ishida, and Akihiro Kashima. Lurking right behind them are No. 20 Kentaro Yamamoto, No. 23 Yuuki Ichikawa, No. 7 Yuuya Watanabe, and plenty of other great players at a single loss.



Today, we'll follow some of the game's best and brightest as they vie for the title of Grand Prix Shizuoka 2015 champion. The echoes of “Draft!” will ring twice before the Top 8 is determined and the final draft decides it all.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



Earlier this morning, 2205 players arrived to battle in *Khans of Tarkir* Sealed deck, looking to achieve the 7-2 or better record required to unlock Day 2. Mana bases were made, combat phases were navigated, and morphs were flipped over. After nine grueling rounds of Swiss competition, 243 players made the cut for Day 2 and get to pick their way to destiny tomorrow.


Leading the field overnight with perfect records of 9-0 are seven players, all from Japan: Daisuke Ueda, Hiroya Wada, Sho Ishikawa, Akio Chiba, Fumiya Matsumoto, Hiroshi Ishida, and Akihiro Kashima. But despite their great starts, these players will need to prove themselves in the draft portion tomorrow as we have No. 20 Kentaro Yamamoto, No. 23 Yuuki Ichikawa, No. 7 Yuuya Watanabe, and plenty of other seasoned sharks circling hungrily behind them with a single loss.


Join us here tomorrow for more text coverage as we follow some of the game's best and brightest compete for the title of Grand Prix Shizuoka 2015 champion! Drafters, on your marks...
