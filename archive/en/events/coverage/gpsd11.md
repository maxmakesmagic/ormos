The Shahar Thing! Grand Prix San Diego
======================================




November 12-13, 2011













Grand Prix San Diego 2011
-------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsd11/winner_620.JPG)

Lurking back in the pack of the Rookie of the Year crowd with just 8 points the California native Shahar Shenhar catapulted himself into the thick of the race with a thrilling victory over 1044 other competitors here at Grand Prix San Diego. Not only did he get himself to 18 points but he gets another crack at padding that total since he can now play at Worlds next weekend. Shahar lurked quietly throughout the first Swiss rounds of the tournament after getting off to a 7-2 start. He went 9-0 in drafts on Day Two -- including the Top 8 -- pairing white with green, blue and black through his three drafts. He beat Aaron Cheng in the quarters, his good friend Ricky Sidher in the semis, and three-time Top 8 competitor Richard Bland in the finals in a back and forth game three set that saw a complicated board and plenty of combat tricks.


Coming into the Top 8 the story was Owen Turtenwald who made his record seventh Top 8 this season and pulled out further ahead in the Player of the Year race but when the dust settled and Owen. Elias Watsfeldt, Lokiman Chen, and Alex West had fallen by the wayside it was the rookie Shahar Shenhar who was hoisting the trophy.

Quarterfinal - Owen Turtenwald vs. Ricky Sidher
-----------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall* 

Ricky Sidher -- on the brink of playing In excess of 20 rounds of **Magic** next weekend -- had better hope he has not used up all his wins before he gets to San Francisco on Monday. Other than an unintentional draw with Aaron Cheng in round four and an intentional one in with the same player in Round 15 Sidher has done nothing but win this weekend. Sidher was playing a a blue-black zombie deck had recursion, milling, and [Endless Ranks of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+Ranks+of+the+Dead) as ways to win -- oh yeah he also opened [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper).


Both he and the keeper of bloodlines had their work cut out for them in the form of Owen Turtnewald who would further pad his Player of the Year lead with every win in this Top 8. Unfortunately for Owen, he did not like the deck he had drafted and was bemoaning it during deck construction. While Owen was making his record setting seventh Grand Prix Top 8 appearance this season he has yet to win at this level andthat seemed to wearing thin for him.


### Game One


Both players kept and Owen was on the draw. He led off with [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth) while Sidher played an unfulfilled [Ghoulraiser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulraiser). Owen crashed in for three and passed the turn only to have Sidher play Undead Alchmist and attack for two with a [Ghoulraiser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulraiser) which suddenly found its meaning in unlife. Owen rolled one creature into his graveyard and Sidher netted a zombie token.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsd11/QF%20Turtenwald.jpg)
*Owen Turtenwald*

A turn later the Undead Alcemist took the air courtesy of [Spectral Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) and attacked for six. He hit two creatures and added [Vampire Interloper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Interloper). Owen tapped out for [Manor Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manor+Gargoyle). Sidher attacked with everyone. Owen blocked the three tokens -- he had also played [Ashmouth Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashmouth+Hound) two turns earlier -- and took 8. That was three more zombies for Sidher.


Geist Catcher's Rig took out the Alchemist and, despite not taking any damage from the previous zombie attacks, Owen was very much back on his heels. Sidher flew over for two with Interloper and deployed the [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper). Owen flashed back [Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) and only 8 cards left in his library -- he played [Reckless Waif](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Waif) and [Avacynian Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacynian+Priest).


Sidher attacked with one zombie and Owen reluctantly ate it with [Manor Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manor+Gargoyle). [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) from Sidher was enough to go to game two


### Game Two


[Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) from Owen at the end of Ricky's second turn was the first play of the game. He uuntapped to play [Mentor of the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek). [Tribute to Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribute+to+Hunger) when Owen played [Rakish Heir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakish+Heir) took out the Mentor and Sidher hoped to hide behind a One-Eued [Scarecrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scarecrow).


Owen pushed ahead and removed three cards from his yard to cast [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) to kill the creature and get in for two. When Sidher played Stiched Drake it looked like he was ready to concede in the face of Owen's Geist Catcher Rig, which allowed the Heir to get even bigger.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsd11/QF%20Sidher.jpg)
*Ricky Sidher*

Sidher decided to play on and he cast [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul) and [Vampire Interloper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Interloper) and began chumping the Ghoul. He played [Civilized Scholar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civilized+Scholar) and [Ghoulcaller's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulcaller%27s+Chant)ed back two zombies. Aa turn later he played out his hand and passed.


Owen shrugged and attacked into [Civilized Scholar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civilized+Scholar), [Rotting Fensnake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Fensnake), and [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) with his two 5-toughness creatures, Sidher activated his Scholar and drew and pitched a [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) -- he untapped his 5/1 and was able to take down both of Owen's creatures in combat. Sidher started going to work with his two fliers and hoped that he could finish Owen off before the PoY leader drew a burn spell.


**Final result:** Owen laid down a hand full of [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s and congratulated Sidher on advancing to the semifinals. Ricky Sidher had still not lost any matches on the weekend.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsd11/QF%20Sidher.jpg)
*Ricky Sidher*



Quarterfinals Round-Up
----------------------


*by Marc Calderaro* 

It's pretty safe to say this draft was trying for a few players. Aaron Cheng abandoned his [Burning Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Vengeance) plan because he thought everyone was playing Blue so he went Red. However, everyone was actually playing Red. Alex West, Owen Turtenwald and Aaron Cheng, all next to each other, all really wanted Red. This helped the other end of the table quite a bit. Lokman Chen was able to concoct a svelte Green-White deck sporting [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township), and Shahar Shenhar got a powerful Black-White deck with two [Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble)s.


Shahar Shenhar (B/W) vs. Aaron Cheng (U/B)


 


### Game One


Shenhar was able to overtake Cheng's [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy)/[Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) shenanigans, but not with out a fight. Bland went first and used that half-turn to his advantage. A [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth) that quickly became an [Unholy Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unholy+Fiend) was joined by a [Darkthicket Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkthicket+Wolf) and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim). Shenhar also cast three creatures in three turns with a [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler), [Silverchase Fox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverchase+Fox) and a [Spectral Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Rider). But since Bland went first, he had the lead at 16-12 when he dropped a gigantic, I mean, [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut). To add insult to injury, Bland also had a [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) and the mana to use it. As opposed to Shenhar, who was having some mana troubles. He was on three land and they were all [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains).


On the next attack, there were some complicated on-board tricks that muddled things up. First, the [Darkthicket Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkthicket+Wolf) was pumped in the face of the Fox, and the Wolf Run was used to give [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut) the ability to travel all over the Traveler whose fate was sealed long ago.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsd11/Finals%20Shenhar.jpg)
*Shahar Shenhar*

"Where's that [Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuke) going to go?" Bland asked. He wisely split up the damage between two creatures. The removal spell ended up on the wolf. Shenhar took seven and went all the way down to 5 life. He had at least found his fourth land, but there was still no sign of [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s.


More attack steps brought more shenanigans. The [Unholy Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unholy+Fiend) was trample-ified with the Wolf Run, and a gang block plus a [Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment+of+Heroism) took down the big ol' artifact. After [Lifelink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifelink) and damage, it was 14-3.


On the next attack step, Shenhar tried to double-block the Fiend to stay alive, but it wasn't enough when Bland gave the possession girl +5/+0 and trample with the constructed-worthy land.


Bland had evened the score.


Shahar Shenhar 1 - 1 Richard Bland


### Game Three


This was my favorite White opener. [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler)s on both sides, both bounding off on another into the graveyard so the players can get the clearly superior 1/1 flyers. So then two 1/1 [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit)s were in a showdown.


Shenhar struck with his [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) and a new [Screeching Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Bat). Bland tried to [Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment+of+Heroism) his own [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) to kill the bat, but for the first time in this entire Top 8, his heroic moment was trumped by another removal spell. Shenhar just cast [Victim of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) on the [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) and when about his way.


Shenhar's [Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble) needed to use his [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) to stick through Bland's [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) (targeting his [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut)). But once it stuck, it threaten to drastically change the tenor of the game. It was 14-15, then 11-10, then 6-10; Bland was in single digits. He had to cast an unkicked [Festerhide Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festerhide+Boar) to attempt to stabilize things, but you know what messes with board stability? Another [Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble). Gross.


The scores became 2-8. There are very few combinations of things in this format that can overcome being at two life and facing down two [Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble)s. And though Bland had seen many Moments of [Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heroism) throughout this Top 8, his moment had passed.


And now it was Shahar Shenhar's moment. Bland extended his hand and scooped up his cards.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsd11/Finals%20Shenhar%20wins.JPG)

An entire bevy of fans immediately assaulted the young Shahar Shenhar. His first time in a Top 8 and he just took the whole thing down. Amidst all the hugs and shouts you could barely make out Shenhar saying to Bland, "I'm sorry; they're being really rude. Good game." I hope this new-found fame won't change such gallant behavior.


**Shahar Shenhar 2 - 1 Richard Bland**


Congratulations to Shahar Shenhar and good luck at Worlds!




Top 5 Cards
-----------



![Burning Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Burning+Vengeance)


Aaron Cheng used this card to plow through Day Two of Grand Prix San Diego. It provided him with either board control or an "alternate" win condition to damage his opponent when decking was not going to get there. He abandoned the Red-Blue archetype in the Top 8 when he saw double-faced cards in Red and Blue get picked early on either side of him. It was soul crushing for him to watch a pair of the enchantments wander aimlessly about the Final Table in pack three. C'est la vie.


![Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Falkenrath+Noble)


Grand Prix winner Shahar Shenhar had two copies of the card in his winning decklist and it proved to be an unending source of frustration for his opponents. Even when they did kill it, he could play another one, or even bring it back twice with [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) in his winning Black-White deck.


![Charmbreaker Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Charmbreaker+Devils)


Another build-around rare on this list, [Charmbreaker Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charmbreaker+Devils) kept cropping up in the oddest of places. Whether it was simply returning a [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) every turn, or ostentatiously returning [Divine Reckoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Reckoning) (an LSV special), the Devils were a great source of card advantage and a great source of beating power. +8/+0 is nothing to sneeze at, especially when you use [Silent Departure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Departure), bouncing a blocker or two to get it. Not to mention, casting a [Traitorous Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitorous+Blood) on your own guy makes an 8/4 Trampler without much effort at all.


![Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Moment+of+Heroism)


This little guy is yet to get all the glory he deserves. But he's getting there. +2/+2 has been done so many times before, and even in the same color, but just that small twist of Lifelink change completely change the pace of a game. Not only does it kill a creature you didn't expect, but now you're not even ahead on damage. Richard Bland used this card throughout the Top 8 to stay one creature and one life ahead of his opponents. Is it fate that the one game someone had the answer for his [Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment+of+Heroism) was the game that lost him the finals? [Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment+of+Heroism) indeed.


![Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Plains)


As we close out the Grand Prix 2011 season, it'd be unfair to exclude an oft-forgotten basic land that has proven to power the elite color in Innistrad limited. Whether it was being tapped to cast [Mausoleum Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Guard), [Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment+of+Heroism), [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler), [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter), [Slayer of the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slayer+of+the+Wicked), [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer), [Chapel Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chapel+Geist) – I could keep going for a while – it was doing something powerful and exciting. It might not have been a coincidence that both decks in the finals sported copious amounts of this sunny land.
