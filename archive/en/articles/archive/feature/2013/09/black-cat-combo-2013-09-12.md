Black Cat Combo
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 12, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I'll admit, this deck isn't actually based around [Black Cat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Cat), but it really does play one, and I assume it's a critical part of the plan. It's pretty funny that [Black Cat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Cat) would be a better **Magic** card if it cost one mana, but that would make it way worse for the purposes of this deck. As it turns out, a casting cost of two is exactly what this deck is looking for, and the reason for that is the powerful and underplayed [Immortal Servitude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Servitude). 

![Black Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Black+Cat) All this deck is trying to do (besides enable [Black Cat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Cat) recursion) is fill its graveyard full of two-mana creatures, most of which cycle, and cast a chain of [Immortal Servitude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Servitude)s. Each Servitude draws a couple cards, and add a [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) or two into the mix and opponents will quickly burn through all nine of their lives. 


[Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) and [Forbidden Alchemy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) help stock up the graveyard, and each also digs for critical pieces of the combo. [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) is what ties everything together, as he acts as copies of [Immortal Servitude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immortal+Servitude) 5â€“8, and is something you can select off [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage). Given ten mana, you can even go off with Servitude, return Snapcaster, Servitude, although I wouldn't expect that to be a common occurrence. 

 Rounding out the deck are a few removal spells to keep you alive, and some [Bloodthrone Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire)s to make sure all your creatures head to the graveyard when appropriate. The sideboard provides more disruption, with every card being a reactive card of some sort.
