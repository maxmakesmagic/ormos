DAY 2 COVERAGE



So much happened yesterday. Just a grab-bag of examples: the famous family Team Kiefer took a brief hiatus from pairing with each other; a T-Rex stalked the halls trawling for votes; and two teams sit at a truly perfect 9-0.


Grand Prix San Jose 2012 winners and 2015 finalists, Matt Sperling, Dave Williams, and fourth-ranked Paul Rietzl want their *third* team Top 4. And Devon O'Donnell, Marc O'Connor, Dustin Taylor are hoping for their first. Both undefeated, their chances are better than most.


But plenty of teams without perfect records are still gunning for the top prize. Defending North American Team champions, Matt Nass, Sam Pardee, and 18th-ranked Jacob Wilson are still in the hunt after six rounds as Kings of the Hill, and are looking for a repeat of Grand Prix Detroit 2015. This morning a member tweeted that they wanted to “Feast of the Unicorn.” An old-old card that grants +4/+0. They just need 4 wins and 0 losses.


But they’ll certainly have stiff competition, as will everyone who makes a Day 2 like this. We’ve got five more rounds with a new Sealed build. Then we cut to Top 4 and do two successive Team Drafts.


There’s a lot more *Magic* to be played. Strap in as the largest Team event weekend soldiers on.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



This has been a monumental day of Team Sealed at Grand Prix Washington, D.C. A record 3,366 players showed up, increasing the previous Team Sealed record by over 50%!


The hall was abuzz all day long as *Oath of the Gatewatch*and *Battle for Zendikar* cards flew across the battlefield, and were points of many teammate discussions and disagreements.


After nine rounds of clashing, only two teams will start tomorrow completely unblemished. Grand Prix San Jose 2012 winners and 2015 finalists, Matt Sperling, Dave Williams, and fourth-ranked Paul Rietzl are looking to cement their legacy as a team for the ages. While Devon O'Donnell, Marc O'Connor, Dustin Taylor are looking to create theirs.


They'll be plenty more text and video features tomorrow starting bright and early (an hour earlier, in fact—dastardly Daylight Saving Time). Can Sperling, Williams, and Rietzl get there again? We'll find out soon enough.
