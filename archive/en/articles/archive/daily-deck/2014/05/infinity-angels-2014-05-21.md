Infinity Angels
===============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on May 21, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






You sit down to play at a [Grand Prix Minneapolis](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpmin14/welcome), and your opponent is none other than then-reigning [Pro Tour Champion Shaun McLaren](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptbng14/welcome). You have him pegged as playing the same deck he won the Pro Tour with, a Red-White-Blue Control deck featuring lots of removal and card drawing. An [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) here, a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) there, a [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) everywhere, and you've got Shaun figured out. You've tested this matchup, and you know how you're going to defeat a Pro Tour Champion.



![Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Kiki-Jiki,%20Mirror%20Breaker&options=)"At the end of your turn, [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)."

Interesting. Shaun didn't play [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) at Pro Tour *Born of the Gods*! This is a pretty reasonable card to play, and you'll have to be on your toes for the rest of the match.


"Untap, draw, play Kiki-Jiki, copy [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), blink Kiki-Jiki."


Oh no, you've been had.


Don't worry, you're not alone. Shaun defeated many players in this way on his way to a semifinals appearance at Grand Prix Minneapolis. Adding a combo element into his control deck gave his potent Pro Tour–winning deck a new dimension and kept his opponents on their toes all tournament long.
