The Phyrexian Crusade
=====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 23, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I first saw today's deck (or at least the archetype) while preparing for [Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*](/en/events/coverage/cifka-savors-pro-tour-breakfast-champions). There was at least one player who was playing it in Daily Events on **Magic Online** and doing well with it consistently but, unfortunately, I don't remember the username. Regardless, the deck is notable both in that its strategy is substantially different from most other decks and that it has had consistent, moderate success on **Magic Online** without ever really breaking through to major physical tournament success that I'm aware of. 


![Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Runechanter%27s+Pike)
 This is a mono-black deck, which is unusual enough in itself, that pushes discard as far as possibleâ€”so far that it's playing the rarely used [Howltooth Hollow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howltooth+Hollow) without anything particularly flashy to cast with it. The goal of the deck is to empty the opponent's hand and kill all his or her threats, and then win with a single infect creature, which, either due to being a land or having protection from red and white, should be difficult for the opponent to kill. 



[Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) serves to dramatically increase the rate at which these infect creatures end the game, but that itself is unusual in a non-blue deck that has no way to draw extra cards and nothing like a [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) to power it up. This deck just plays so many instants and sorceries that make the game go longer by dealing with the opponent's cards that [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) is expected to get big enough the hard way.
