Sunday, 10:10 a.m. – 9-0 Decks
------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Just four players stood the test of nine rounds of competitive Legacy play yesterday and finished the day with perfect records. One might assume their decks were some of the staples of the format, RUG maybe or Stoneblade or even Jund. But no. Which decks went 9-0? Storm, Elves, Death & Taxes, and one very cool-looking [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) / [Dream Halls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Halls) / [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) / [Enter the Infinite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enter+the+Infinite) concoction. Check it out!

Sunday, 11:16 a.m. – Metagame Breakdown
---------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby*

 

I've mentioned it in passing a few times this weekend, but wow, Legacy really is the format where diversity rules supreme. Going through the decklists categorising them on day two was both a voyage of discovery, and something of a stone around my neck – there's just so much going on!


As you might have spotted, there are a few decks that stand out as being the 'most popular' choices, but even that is a little bit of a misnomer, as quite simply, there are a tonne of different decks in the room. For today's metagame breakdown, I've split out BUG and Shardless BUG, and Jund/ [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) Jund, but otherwise kept things fairly general. Let's take a look.

Round 10 Feature Match – Michael Bonde vs. Andreas Bendix Nielsen
-----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

"I read what the other 9-0 decks were yesterday, and they're all bad matchups," complained Bonde. "I'm playing the only fair deck, the rest is all combo." But he surely must have played against some combo decks on the way to his 9-0, no? "Only one. I beat Andre Müller playing Elves."


Michael Bonde got this far with "Death & Taxes," the mono-white creature deck. His likewise undefeated opponent in this all-Danish feature match, Andreas Bendix Nielsen, was playing Storm.


**Game 1**


Bendix Nielsen started on [Underground Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+Sea) and [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) , Bonde on [Rishadan Port](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port) and [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) . Next, Bendix Nielsen [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) ed and cracked a [Polluted Delta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) , then cast [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) . Bonde revealed [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) , [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) , and lands—one of them a [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) that took out one of Bendix Nielsen's [Underground Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+Sea) s. But another [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) provided [Polluted Delta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) , so there really was no danger of mana screw here.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpstr13/fm10_bendixnielsen.jpg)  
*Andreas Bendix Nielsen* 
On turn three, Bendix Nielsen cast [Dark Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) , [Cabal Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual) , and [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) which was cracked in response to the [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) that followed. He flipped two more rituals, [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) , [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) , another [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) , [Lotus Petal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal) , [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) , and even [Tendrils of Agony](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) , then stopped. That was well enough for Bonde to concede on the spot.


Michael Bonde 0-1 Andreas Bendix Nielsen


**Game 2**


The game began with [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) , [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) on one side, [Polluted Delta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) for [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) followed by [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) on the other. Bendix Nielsen saw [Rest in Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) , [Phyrexian Revoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker) , [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) , [Mirran Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) , and an [Ethersworn Canonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist) which he made Bonde discard. On turn two, Bonde cast [Rest in Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) , then lost his [Phyrexian Revoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker) to [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) before vialing out [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) .



![Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lotus+Petal)

On turn three, Bendix Nielsen went for it. He cast [Lotus Petal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal) , [Cabal Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual) , and [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) , but in response Bonde vialed out the [Phyrexian Revoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker) that he had just topdecked. "Nice draw, eh?" he laughed.


With [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) switched off by the Revoker, Bendix Nielsen could only [Burning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) for [Massacre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre) , wipe the board, and pass the turn. After that he was left with just three cards in hand, far from another attempt to go off. Bonde put pressure on him with [Mirran Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) , Bendix Nielsen bought some time with [Chain of Vapor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+of+Vapor) .


Bendix Nielsen cast [Burning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) and in response sacrificed [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) to get and then cast [Diminishing Returns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diminishing+Returns) . With [Rest in Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) on the battlefield nothing was shuffled back into his library and the Returns also exiled his only copy of [Tendrils of Agony](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) . But his new seven looked awesome: [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) , [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) , Lotus, Petal, [Lotus Petal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal) , [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) , [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) , [Dark Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) . He cast all of the artifacts, cracked a Petal for [Dark Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) , cast [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) , cracked the Diamond in response, searched for [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) , taking note of the remaining contents of his increasingly small library, and cast it.


I didn't get a good look at his library before, but it seemed as if he needed to get more than a little lucky with [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) . He didn't.


Michael Bonde 1-1 Andreas Bendix Nielsen


**Game 3**


Bendix Nielsen opened with [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) , seeing [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) , [Rishadan Port](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port) , [Mirran Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) , [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) , [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) , [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) , and [Ethersworn Canonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist) . He played a land and passed the turn to Bonde who cast [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) and passed right back.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpstr13/fm10_bonde.jpg)  
*Michael Bonde* 
Bendix Nielsen had [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) to get rid of [Ethersworn Canonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist) (seeing a new [Aven Mindcensor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor) in Bonde's hand) on turn two and then on turn three (apparently the usual combo turn here) he pondered his options. After a while he decided to wait a little longer. Bonde summoned [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) , which left Bendix Nielsen with no choice but to wipe the board with [Burning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) , [Massacre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre) . With the [Burning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) gone, all that remained in his hand was mana (both rituals and artifacts) and a [Chain of Vapor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+of+Vapor) . No Tutor, no [Burning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) , no [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) , no nothing.


That didn't change for the next couple of turns (only more of the same accumulated) and it really only took a couple of turns for Bonde's [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) , [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) , and [Mirran Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) to take the game.


**Michael Bonde 2-1 Andreas Bendix Nielsen**


So how bad is this matchup for Death & Taxes really, Mr. Bonde?


"Really?" Bonde asked. "No seriously, it's not good. Even after sideboarding, with [Ethersworn Canonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist) in addition to Thalia, you basically can't do anything against a turn-two kill if they're playing first."


Luckily, Andreas Bendix Nielsen never had the turn-two kill in any of these games and Michael Bonde advanced to 10-0.

When Baier went to the discard step of his turn, Levy used [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to play [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) again. Now was his time to be aggressive. [Counterbalance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) combined nicely with [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) to make it very hard for Baier's deck to get into action, and [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) was a fine attacker backed up by [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) . Attacks took Baier to six life, and he was still hurting for lands.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpstr13/Raphael-Levy.jpg)  
*Raphael Levy* 
By the time that Baier had a second land, to potentially stop [Counterbalance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) with [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) , he didn't have the time, given the attack force Levy had marshalled.


"I have a one and a two cost spell on top, and countermagic in hand, just in case" declared Levy matter-of-factly.


While Baier thought through his plays, Levy smiled. "I don't think you're winning this one." After surveying things a little longer, Baier agreed and extended his hand.


**Raphael Levy wins 2-0!**

Sunday, 4:27 p.m. – Decktech: Putting the BURG in Strasbourg
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

BUG? RUG? Why not combine the two and make it BURG? A group of German players including Legacy mastermind Carsten Linden, day-two regular Mike Hoffmann, and the Grand Prix champions Timo Schünemann and Florian Koch had this very same idea and actually made it happen. Three of them made it into day two with the deck, including Koch who told me how that works.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpstr13/blog_BURG_koch.jpg)  
*Florian Koch* 
"It's basically RUG with black for two [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) s and three [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) s, though it's not clear whether that should be four. Of course, it puts additional strain on the mana, but [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) kinda also helps out with that. In any case, we found we could still run just 18 lands including four [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) s," said Koch. "Originally, we wanted to leave it at blue-producing dual lands only, but when you open on [Underground Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+Sea) you really want that [Taiga](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taiga) .


"Anyway, [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) is totally awesome, great against everything and extremely powerful. The most important targets for [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) are probably [Counterbalance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) specifically and more generally [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) . Just killing stuff without having to worry about any countermagic is a big factor. The rest of the main deck is pretty standard, I guess."

[Fire Covenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Covenant) ? What does that card do?


"Well, for one thing it killed [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) , [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) , and a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) when I played against BUG," said Koch. "And someone killed nine Elves with it. It's a great sideboard card against creature decks and most of the fair decks. Not that great against RUG though, because they have [Nimble Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) and more counters."


What's up with the rest of the sideboard? [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate) ? [Koth of the Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) ?


" [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate) is good against some of the same decks too. Just getting a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) or another [Nimble Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) can put you at a huge advantage already. You simply have more of the best creatures. Of course, what it's really insane against is Reanimator. It actually makes sense for a Reanimator player to cast [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) during his main phase, instead of waiting till the end of your turn, to get around [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) and [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) and stuff," Koch explained.


"And then [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate) comes just at the right time. I personally reanimated [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) today, which is highly recommended by the way. [Koth of the Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) is mostly for Blue-White Miracles. We were looking for a resilient threat a.k.a. a planeswalker and Garruk just didn't cut it because he wasn't aggressive enough. The rest of the sideboard is pretty boring, I guess. I believe [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) for the [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) lock is old news by now."
