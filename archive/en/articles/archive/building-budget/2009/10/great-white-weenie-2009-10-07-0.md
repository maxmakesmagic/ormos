The Great White Weenie
======================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on October 7, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week I examined some of the overlooked cards from *Zendikar*. The two cards that really stuck out for me were [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge) and [Luminarch Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension). This week I'd like to build a deck that utilizes the power of [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge). After a small amount of testing the card has proven itself to be very powerful. With the rotation of the *Lorwyn* Kithkin, I was able to trade for a play set of [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) quite easily last weekend. I had already drafted enough *Zendikar* to have a play set of [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge). I brought home the cards and went through the commons and uncommons I had at home with the intention of building a budget White Weenie deck. I'll start by going over the cards I considered.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_pledgeSplash.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_theCardsHeader.jpg)  
**[Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith):** A 2/3 for two mana is fine by any standard. [Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith)'s ability to pump my entire team's toughness is a great bonus. I figured this would probably be a four-of.


**[Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith):** This seemed like another obvious four-of to me. It turns even an un-kicked [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge) into 12 points of power.


**[Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard):** I've always been a big fan of [Savannah Lions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions), and this guy really ups the ante by being a Soldier.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_theCardsCross1.jpg)  
**[Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile):** Some of you may not own [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), or may not have four. If you're having trouble finding them, you can always replace them with another removal spell like Journey to nowhere. [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) is an uncommon but in high demand, and I've gotten my playset by playing a good deal of Limited tournaments. This is the type of card you want to make sure you have four in your collection. I figured this was an obvious inclusion in any deck that was base white.


**[Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos):** I just can't help myself sometimes. [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) is one of my favorite cards ever printed. I find myself picking up the Rangers every time I'm going to the drawing board with the intention of creating a new deck. The card is traded at a very low value, it has a power level that challenges tournament staples like [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), and Invitational winner Antoine Ruel seems to have a St. Francis thing going on in the art. Most importantly, [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) is one of the few cards that you play against [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) and actually end up ahead. I always felt guilty including [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) in budget decks with [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny) in Standard. I didn't want to lie and say you were getting full value from the card when the best target for it would break the budget. I was ready to include this card in the deck before I even realized it was a soldier. The creature type pushed this card way over the top in my mind.


**[Akrasan Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire):** I want this deck to have enough targets for the Rangers. [Akrasan Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire) is an efficient beater on its own. The fact that it's a Soldier and can be tutored for with Ranger makes it a perfect fit.


**[Kazandu Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Blademaster):** Even with no other Allies in your deck, [Kazandu Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Blademaster) is an excellent beater by itself. A 2/2 first striker with vigilance that gets a bonus from its creature type is quite good. If you draw these in multiples they become absurd.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_theCardsCross2.jpg)  
**[Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge):** This is the reason I'm building the deck in the first place. This card is an improvement on [Cloudgoat Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger). Putting 6 points of power on the table for 5 mana that can't be dealt with via spot removal is already incredible. Throw in a [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith) or [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) and this card becomes truly absurd. Imagine having two [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) in play and casting this card. That's 18 points of power!


**[Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure):** [Crusade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade) was always a staple in White Weenie decks of the past. This card is markedly stronger because it doesn't haphazardly help your opponent by pumping their creatures. It combos very well with [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge) and happens to be quite good with all our other creatures too.

It's a pretty straightforward deck list. Nine four-ofs and twenty-four basic lands. I brought the deck to a local shop and played some games against the people there.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_match1Header.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_geopede.jpg)I lose the die roll and keep [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard), [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure), [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith), and four [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). My opponent plays [Savage Lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands) and passes the turn. I draw a [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge), cast my [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard), and pass the turn. My opponent casts a [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) and passes. I draw [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), cast [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure), and attack for 3 with [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard). My opponent plays another [Savage Lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands), attacks for 3, and casts [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech). I draw [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), cast my [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith), and pass. On his turn he plays a fetch land and pops it to give the Geopede +4/+4, then casts a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) and flips into another [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede). He attacks with everything. I block the [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) with my [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard), take 8, and go to 9.


On my turn I draw another land, cast [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), and search for an [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard) and an [Akrasan Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire). (Don't want to walk into [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse).) My opponent casts [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse), destroying my [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith), and attacks with his [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). I trade with my [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos). I draw an [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure), cast it, and cast both my one-drops. My opponent plays another fetch land, pops it, and attacks with two [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede)s for 5 damage each. I chump-block both. He casts [Ob Nixilis, the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ob+Nixilis%2C+the+Fallen), but I cast [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) on the legendary creature during his end step. On my turn I draw another [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith), cast my [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge) and pass the turn. He casts a [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) and flips into a [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout), but I have two [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) on the battlefield, and he puts the instant on the bottom to avoid taking two. On my turn, I draw another [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), cast [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith), and attack for lethal damage.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_match2Header.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab59_master.jpg)I win the die roll and keep [Akrasan Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire), two [Kazandu Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Blademaster), [Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith), [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), and two [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). I cast my [Akrasan Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire) and pass. He plays a [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress) and passes. I draw a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), attack for 2, and cast a [Kazandu Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Blademaster). My opponent casts an [Esper Stormblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Stormblade) and passes. I draw [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), cast another [Kazandu Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Blademaster), and attack for 4 with the first one. He plays a third land and passes the turn. I know he has [Ethersworn Shieldmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Shieldmage) in his deck, so after I draw a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), I just cast my [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), search for two [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard)s, and pass the turn. He casts the [Ethersworn Shieldmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Shieldmage) on my endstep, then casts a [Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium) and attacks for 4 in the air with his Stormblade. I really like this kind of deck in general for the new Standard. It seems like White Weenie is very consistent, while the other decks in the format will probably struggle a bit with their mana. I fear the cascade decks have a lot going for them in the way of degenerate draws, and I want to prevent any deck from running us over in a way that we didn't foresee.


I remember including [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) in a [Sanguine Bond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanguine+Bond) deck that abused [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos). [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) seems like it may be a better fit than [Akrasan Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire) here. So I'd like to make that change and see how things work out. The [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden)s are not Soldiers, but their ability to stall in the aggro mirror matches will work in our favor when we need to be swinging the game in our favor with cards like [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge).


I also want to start formulating a sideboard. I feel pretty confident that Cascade decks will be a huge portion of the upcoming Standard format. I feel strange putting [Ethersworn Canonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist) in the maindeck, but it may be the correct thing to do. For now I'll keep it in the sideboard, but I want to keep myself open to new ideas and I think Canonist may be a good call for decks that are trying to neuter the Cascade decks. Here's the list I'll probably be playing at FNM next week:

The list hasn't changed much on paper, but the [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden)s make the games last a bit longer. Generally speaking, life gain has never been a strong ability because it simply delays the inevitable. Fortunately, however, the inevitable in this deck is having 12 or 18 points of power from a single card.


I think this deck is a strong and inexpensive choice for those looking to compete in the new Standard.
