Glittering Kavu
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 8, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







As more gold sets come out, the card [Glittering Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glittering+Wish) gets better and better. I was optimistic about this card's future before [Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*](/en/events/coverage/cifka-savors-pro-tour-breakfast-champions), but it never really took off. *Gatecrash* and *Dragon's Maze* have offered a wide variety of additional options for the card, so now is a great time to try to take advantage, possibly at [Grand Prix Portland](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/grandprix/portland13) this weekend? 


![Glittering Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glittering+Wish) Today's deck was played by Segal on May 1 to a 4–0 finish on **Magic Online** without *Dragon's Maze*. *Dragon's Maze* offers too wide a variety of additional options for me to consider all of them, but off the top of my head, [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa) and [Wear & Tear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wear+%26amp%3B+Tear) might be good fits. 


 I suspect the most common card Segal intends to Wish for is [Fiery Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Justice), which made a name for itself in *Time Spiral* Block Constructed by killing the opponent's creatures while giving +5/+5 to [Kavu Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kavu+Predator), and it's looking to do exactly the same thing in this deck. 


 The rest of the deck is similar to the Naya/Jund/Junk hybrid that has been popular in Modern, with regular staples like [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) joined by what may be the new heir to [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf): [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant). It's the use of [Kavu Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kavu+Predator) as a potential trump to its more popular sibling from the same block, [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and the exceedingly versatile [Glittering Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glittering+Wish), that really set this deck apart. 


 As a final note, there really needs to be a better way to refer to the increasingly popular Modern color combination "everything but blue"—the Nephilim from *Guildpact* are occasionally suggested as a way to refer to decks of their colors, but [Dune-Brood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dune-Brood) isn't really the kind of name that rolls off the tongue. Here, I've decided to refer to that collection of colors simply as the "brood."
