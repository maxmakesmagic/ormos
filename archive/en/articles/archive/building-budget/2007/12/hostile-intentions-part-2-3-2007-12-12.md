Wow, Classic and Singleton finished neck and neck! Because there was so little variance here, I'm going to have a revote with just three options:


 **Which format would you like to see Ben explore on a budget?** Classic, where most cards are legal. Highlander, where there can only be one! Classic Highlander â€“ I like them both!The winning choice from this poll will be featured in a future column!


Back to the [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) deck challenge. When I did the [Dredge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge) deck challenge some months ago, I intentionally restricted the card pool to a certain degree. This was to encourage creativity, not to stifle it! Mark Rosewater has oft-stated that having to work within a set of rules makes him have to work harder (and often better) in order to find solutions within a finite set. The same goes for this challenge â€“ I've cut out two colors entirely (no [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), no white!), only given budget options (Garruk!) and tried to make the card pool fit within an Elemental/direct damage theme.


This is not to say that all cards available are Elementals or burn. Within the cards available for the [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) deck were potent card-drawing engines ([Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing), [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine), [Wheel of Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fate)), the "Pickles" engine ([Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), [Brine Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brine+Elemental)), Scryb and Force ([Spectral Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force) and [Scryb Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger)), tons of color-fixing, mana-acceleration, and elements for a control, aggro or combo deck!


There were tons of great decklists. It was difficult to choose any given one to represent an archetype (especially since some decks were similar), but in the end here are the featured deck lists that came out of the [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) deckbuilding challenge. Before I get in depth with each individual deck, let me address a couple of deckbuilding problems I found showing up across the board.


### Problem #1: Too much [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider)


Â 



![Smokebraider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Smokebraider)

[Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) is an invaluable part of any heavily-Elemental build. It is a double [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), at common, for that tribe. I would definitely consider [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) (and push for him, in fact) if you are playing an elementalful deck â€“ especially if you are multiple colors. However, many people had decks that went as follows:

Burn spells  
[Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s  

4 [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider)  

4 [Flamekin Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger)  

4 [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility)


If you don't have a bevy of Elemental spells to use with [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider), he's too often a 1/1 doof for two mana. You can easily cast [Flamekin Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger) if you can cast [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) (a single red mana), leaving just four cards in your deck that can take advantage of [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) â€“ Hostily itself. Having four cards in your deck to accelerate another four cards is not great deckbuilding. Chances are not good of getting both a [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) and a [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) in the same game (without help from the Harbinger, which probably will just get [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) in this case, and not [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider)). In fact, [Rite of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) or [Grinning Ignus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grinning+Ignus) would probably be better for this kind of build, because they can at least power out your 28ish non-Elemental burn spells.

Guardian Phoenix Says:  *"The ideal play for this deck is [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone) to accelerate into a turn 4 [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power). Add a fifth [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) turn 5, you've got 11 mana, which enough for [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) and any one of the last 3 burn spells listed. The important aspect with those is that they'll hit for at least 4, which turns into 4 4/2's (Gauntlet, remember), plus the 7/7 [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), and you win from nowhere with 23 points of damage. (Just remember to not [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) with the Command, that will kill your newly created guys.)* 


*Use the [Lash Out](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lash+Out), [Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast), and [Incendiary Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incendiary+Command) to stay alive early, possibly asking the Command to give you a new hand to go looking for your pieces. Also don't be afraid to sac the [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone) to keep digging.* 


*If you're having problems finding [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power) will power [Disintegrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disintegrate) to the dome quite nicely as well."* 


Ben Says: I intentionally did not include mass-removal spells that only hit creatures ([Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm)), but this deck includes the ones that hit either/both creatures and players ([Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast), [Incendiary Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incendiary+Command)). One of the problems with playing a deck with [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) as the main/only win condition is that you often have to use your burn on opposing creatures to stay alive â€“ and if they play something too large to easily burn ([Spectral Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force)?) you're just out of luck. In this build, the [Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast)s and Commands can take out weenies and the acceleration into [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power) lets you race against other decks â€“ plus, the Gauntlet makes your hasted Elemental tokens that much larger. I'd probably have found a way to include [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster) in this build (especially with the ticket wiggle room), but Guardian Phoenix's deck is a solid effort for the archetype as-is.

Rallius says: " *This deck attemps to gain an advantage by having an arsenal of instant speed spells at the player's disposal that can be utilized at a minimal tempo cost via 4 braids of fire. What, you say that braid of fire has just as good of a chance to kill its owner as it does enable that individual to do something effective? Well, there are several mana sink outlets to avoid burn. Ghitu encampment can be repeatedly activated to become a creature many times over. You can grab 0 mana from fungal reaches as many times as your heart desires. Lastly, it can be used to pump ashling, as he can quickly get out of control gaining 2 +1/+1 counters both on your turn and your opponent's. Ashling can also be a solid defender and strong finisher after you've expended all of your direct damage. [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) will shine in combination with any of your 22 spells that can potentially deal dome damage. If using all of that potential mana still has anyone concerned, then you can rely on drawing beacons with some amount of consistency as they reshuffle after use and you're drawing two cards a turn with rites of flourishing. [Squall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall) line can be an effective finisher with your upkeep mana, and several split second spells lend the deck some versatility against control. Rites of flourishing, along with braid of fire, are perhaps the most important cards for this deck's success. I originally began deck design using howling mines, but decided to omit them as a result of not being able to resolve a personal conflict of spending a third of my available fiscal resources on a playset of howling mines (2.5 each). It doesn't hurt that it enables useful, split-second molten disasters and plays involving hostility that much quicker as a virtue of dropping lands at an increased pace. I found lash out to be a solid contributer as approx. 38% you're also dealing dome damage that will trigger hostility."* 


Ben says: This deck had some of the best uses for [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire), which is a very potent spell in the right deck. Many decks that get [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) out might not be able to follow him up with much, but this deck can drop [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), potentially pump out a few dozen tokens, and then swing for lethal.


Also, one of the problems with burn decks is that, again, you run out of burn before your opponent runs out of creatures. However, burn spells tend, as a whole, to be more efficient (in mana) than the creatures they are killing. [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) lets you keep drawing more and more burn to outpace the opponent. I'd love to find a way to get [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) into this deck, maybe by losing the [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster)s. As Jareth pointed out later in the thread, [Upwelling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upwelling) also works wonders with [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire), since you can carry the mana over past your main phase.


In the end, Rallius's deck is the one I decided to go with for the evolution. It has a lot of neat combos, room to grow with other cards in the list, and looks like it can be a real winner given the power inherent in the mana it can pump out. I'll definitely be looking for inspiration from other decks in the thread as well to evolve this one, and borrowing perhaps from Blacker Lotus's deck as well.
