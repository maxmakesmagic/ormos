Round 8 Feature Match: The Ancient Kavus vs. Raw Dogs
=====================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 29, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











The team of Brian Hacker, Gab Tsang and Jeff Cunningham faced off against Mike Pustilnik, Matt Vienneau and Gary Krakower respectively. After a long and grueling day of Magic, this match truly was a test of each team's endurance, as the winners might sneak into day two and would have to be up in a mere six hours for the first Rochester Draft of day two.

### Game One: Brian vs. Mike

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/912.jpg)Brian, playing black/red, faced off against Mike playing blue/green. Brian killed a couple of Mike's early and smaller creatures with a [Crippling Fatigue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue), but the good doctor brought out the fatty patrol with an [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) and [Rabid Elephant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rabid+Elephant). Brian drew no answers to the huge creatures, and fell quickly to the onslaught.

Mike 1-Brian 0

### Game One: Matt vs. Gab

Gab's white/blue deck seemed like a great foil against Matt's red/black deck, as an early [Mystic Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Crusader) shut down Matt's entire offense. Matt soon faced even more insurmountable odds, staring down an active [Hallowed Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Healer), [Mirror Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Wall), and [Angelic Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Wall). Matt attempted to get back into the game via [Laquatus's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Laquatus%27s+Champion) and life loss, followed up by a [Cabal Torturer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer) to take care of the Healer. [Morbid Hunger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morbid+Hunger) aced a newly summoned [Hydromorph Gull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydromorph+Gull). Things didn't seem so hopeless for Matt anymore, as the wall traded for some blockers, leaving the board nearly cleared. Unfortunately, the Crusader had been taking its toll, and right before Matt could come back fully, Gab reached threshold and played a [Mystic Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot), ending the game. 

Gab 1-Matt 0

### Game Two: Matt vs. Gab

Once again Gab drew the Crusader, dropping it on turn three. Shortly thereafter, the game was over, as the "Protection from Matt's deck" creature just couldn't be stopped at all. Gab 2-Matt 0 (Raw Dogs 1-The Ancient Kavus 0)

### Game Two: Brian vs. Mike

Brian came out a bit faster this game, leading with a [Minotaur Explorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minotaur+Explorer) (discarding [Patchwork Gnomes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patchwork+Gnomes)). It traded with a [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), and the next play in the game was Brian's turn five [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade), with four Swamps on board to pump the undead creature. This was followed shortly thereafter by [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict) and [Caustic Tar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caustic+Tar), making this game as lopsided as the last, except this time the Californian showed the Brooklyn native what the score was.

Mike 1-Brian 1

### Game One: Jeff vs. Gary

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/911.jpg)Gary took an early mulligan, but gained a slight tempo pick-up with a [Millikin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millikin), powering turn three [Mystic Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot) in his white/blue deck. Jeff played his forests, and seemed to have a good offense built with [Anurid Barkripper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Barkripper), [Ironshell Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ironshell+Beetle) and [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), bringing Gary to eight while Gary attempted to stabilize. This plan seemed to work, as Gary played [Shelter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter) and [Prismatic Strands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Strands), the latter making life extremely difficult for Jeff's mono-green board. A couple of more turns of fending left Gary without any tricks in hand and at four, but a newly summoned [Cephalid Retainer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Retainer) looked to stabilize the board entirely. With a board full of islands, and a [Mystic Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot) on offense, Gary nearly completed the comeback over the course of the next few turns, aided by a pair of [Teroh's Faithful](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful) to put him at a more comfortable double-digit life total. The turn before death, Jeff finally drew his first island of the game, allowing him to [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) the Zealot off the board. Gary promptly replayed it, as Jeff contemplated his next move. It was a [Dematerialize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dematerialize), sending the Zealot back to Krakower's hand yet again. Gary drew an [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) of his own, shipping a couple of creatures back to Jeff's hand. This allowed him a turn to tap out to play [Aven Flock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Flock) and [Mystic Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot). With Jeff at two life, the game finally looked to be over. As Gary tapped a [Swarmsnapper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swarmsnapper) and [Kirtar's Desire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire)d the [Fogbringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fogbringer) to swing for the win, Jeff revealed a second [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) of his own (third of the game) to bounce both of Gary's flyers and [Cephalid Retainer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Retainer). 

On his own turn, Jeff brought a Looter into play, followed with a [Cephalid Scout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Scout). The Looter came online the following turn as Gary played out two flyers, and then down to the Retainer versus card drawing. Jeff drew four cards between the two Cephalids and his draw phase, but couldn't find a solution and was forced to scoop.

Gary 1-Jeff 0

### Game Three: Brian vs. Mike

Brian killed all of Mike's early creatures with [Lava Dart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) and [Ghastly Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise), and started swinging with a [Reckless Charge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Charge)d [Filthy Cur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filthy+Cur). Mike brought a turn five [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), but it died to a combination of [Patriarch's Desire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Desire) and [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt). [Caustic Tar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caustic+Tar) hit the board on turn seven, and the game looked to be over before it began. Mikey P topdecked [Call of the Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) at a precarious seven life, flashing it back. The board stood at Brian's [Caustic Tar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caustic+Tar) and [Filthy Cur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filthy+Cur) versus Mike's Elephants. However, a quick combination of Tar, untap, Tar, flashback [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) sealed the game despite Mike's best efforts.

Brian 2-Mike 1 (Raw Dogs 2-The Ancient Kavus 0) 

Match Win to the Raw Dogs

### Game Two: Jeff vs. Gary

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/910.jpg)Game one took thirty minutes, leaving only twenty minutes left to play game two. The Gab/Matt thrashing took less time than Gary and Jeff's first game, and the third match was well into the third game while these two were shuffling, so it all came down to whether this game would finish or not.

Jeff started out with the absolutely insane draw of [Werebear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear), [Cephalid Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Looter) and [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) to Gary's nothing. Gary was forced to allow Jeff to draw three cards, but at this point Brian won the match for his team. Playing for tiebreakers at this point, Jeff continued to pound with [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) and [Possessed Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possessed+Aven) (which would have been quite handy the previous game). He then [Dematerialize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dematerialize)d and [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst)ed all of Gary's blockers and swung with a huge team. Krakower tried to staunch the flow of blood with [Sphere of Duty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Duty). It didn't work, as Jeff simply destroyed Gary's nearly empty board.

Gary 1-Jeff 1.

### Game Three: Jeff vs. Gary

The two raced to finish their match, as Gary wanted to win for pride. Jeff came out fast with [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) and [Phantom Nantuko](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nantuko), but they were matched by [Cagemail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cagemail) and [Lost in Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+Thought). These were trumped by [Simplify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simplify) and [Dematerialize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dematerialize) (bouncing the enchanted Nantuko) and Jeff looked to take control by replaying his team plus [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) against Gary's sole [Battlewise Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Aven). 

Gary changed his fortunes via a [Gallantry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gallantry), drawing a card against Jeff's three, but killing the Rootwalla in the process, keeping equal card count (Jeff's three card drawn versus the Rootwalla, [Gallantry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gallantry) cantrip card, and actual playing of the [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill)). He further complicated matters with a [Sphere of Duty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Duty) and [Teroh's Faithful](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful), although the Nantuko continued to make himself larger and larger with each passing turn.

Jeff then thought for a minute, cast [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), and swung with a team of rather large Elephants, Squirrels, and Nantukos, ending the game.

Jeff 2-Gary 1. (Raw Dogs 3-The Ancient Kavus 0)
