Domo Arigato, Mr. Mutato
========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 31, 2006 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar











Mutants. Freaks of nature. Unnatural lifeforms augmented through rampant experimentation. No, I'm not talking about the contestants on Canadian Idol. I'm talking about the creations of those creepy bio-mancers of the Simic Combine. Led by **Magic's** first Elf Chimp Wizard, Momir Vig, the Simic hold a lot of appeal for those of us who spent part of our youths pulling the wings off of flies (and putting them back on) (their heads). Of all the jobs a kid could want to have when he grows up, bio-mancer was third on my list, after professional baseball player and computer programmer. (Since I check the Toronto Blue Jays' box scores online, I figure I'm halfway there.) I'm pretty sure that any job sounds better if you tack “mancer” on to the end of it. For a while, I was what is commonly called a “gas jockey,” but from now on my resume will say that I was a “petro-mancer.” My plans to work my way up to CEO of Shell Corp. fell through, so instead I “grew up” to become a logo-mancer. A word magician. Watch me make good grammar *disappear*! 

Instead of actually writing an article from scratch, I decided that this week I would pay homage to the Simic and “graft” together a bunch of different words I selected at random from the dictionary. I hope it turns out well!

U/G is my favourite colour combination. Green gives Blue the mana-acceleration, graveyard recursion, and artifact/enchantment destruction that it lacks, and in return Green gets powerful creatures like Keiga and Meloku (and counter-magic and card-drawing). [Wood Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Sage) is one of my all-time favourite cards, so when the *Dissension* spoiler was released, I was pleased to see the [Wood Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Sage)-esque [Vigean Intuition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigean+Intuition). Back in the days of *Odyssey*, I used [Wood Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Sage) in my Threshold/Flashback decks instead of [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter). My only gripe with [Wood Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Sage), unsurprisingly, is that he's not an Elf. It's the same reason I don't really like [Keiga, the Tide Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keiga%2C+the+Tide+Star). Couldn't you have just put some pointy ears on him, painted a few trees in the background, and made him an Elf Dragon Spirit? For me?

There aren't a whole lot of creatures with the mana cost UG, but they are all pretty cool: [Pygmy Hippo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pygmy+Hippo), [Gaea's Skyfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Skyfolk), and [Wood Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Sage). [Coiling Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle) is the latest, and possibly the greatest. The best thing about [Coiling Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle) is that he's an Elf (Snake Druid)! Instead of building another Elf deck, though, I'd like to kick things off with a couple of Simic-enhanced non-Elf tribal decks.

### Beasts and Snakes and Overdone Phrases … Oh My!

In the days leading up to Regionals, I was browsing some **Magic** sites and saw a couple of nifty Snake decks, including one by my esteemed editor, Mr. Ted Knutson. Since my deck uses the Extended cardpool (and the wackiness of [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio)), I decided to write about it anyway. The inspiration for the deck came from a reader named Gabe who's a big fan of [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio) tricks and noticed that [Patagia Viper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patagia+Viper) can generate two tokens for each ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif) you pay. Just play the Viper with the requisite mana, and when the tokens it produces come into play, use [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio) to return the Viper to your hand. Neither [Coiling Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle) nor [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) will mind too much if you keep returning them to hand. [Sosuke's Summons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sosuke%27s+Summons) enjoys long walks on the beach, dinner by candlelight, and Snakes coming into play, so I included a full set, as well as a set of [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder)s so I'd have the mana to cast everything. [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) also likes [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio). With two Witnesses, you can play one and bounce the other, each time returning something nasty from your graveyard. Something like [Temporal Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Spring), perhaps, which will ensure that your opponent draws the same card for the rest of the game.

With all those Snake tokens around, I threw in some [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition)s as a “win condition.” Unfortunately, that deck got lower scores on the Fun Meter than watching the Blue Jays bullpen in action, so I decided to replace them the incredibly fun, incredibly fair, incredibly Moon-based [Meloku the Clouded Mirror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meloku+the+Clouded+Mirror). Meloku's token-making ability is awesome with [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio), allowing you to use it to save your creatures from removal (just make an Illusion and bounce the targeted creature). Those cards also form a soft-lock with [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake), since you can now counter a spell for each five mana you have in play (just make an Illusion, bounce [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake), and use it counter the spell).

Sure it's “win more.” But that's better than winning less, in my opinion.

| *Dissension brings a new load of beasts to the party.* |

The next deck is an update of a fun (and very straightforward) Beast deck I played circa. Nineteen-Ninety-Onslaught. *Dissension* helps out with some of that deck's weaknesses. [Molder Slug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug) and [Woodripper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodripper) kept opposing Artifacts at bay, but if I wanted to nuke Enchantments, I had to use the lackluster [Root Greevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Root+Greevil) (who, apparently, is responsible for much of the world's greevil problem). In the end, I used [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize)s, but they were an imperfect solution. They often got in the way of Amplify and they certainly didn't attack very much. Luckily, [Trygon Predator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator) and [Indrik Stomphowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indrik+Stomphowler) arrived to save the day. The other problem with the deck was that my [Blastoderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blastoderm)s, [Molder Slug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug)s, and [Woodripper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodripper)s often had a hard-time getting through my opponent's defenses. [Fangren Pathcutter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Pathcutter) helped a bit, but I really wanted something a little more reliable. Luckily, [Trygon Predator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator), [Assault Zeppelid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Zeppelid), and [Helium Squirter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helium+Squirter) hit the scene and gave the Beast deck that much-needed evasion. The Squirter, in particular, works well with such counter-laden Beasts as [Karstoderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karstoderm), [Clockwork Vorrac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockwork+Vorrac), and the good Amplify Beasts from *Legions*, [Canopy Crawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canopy+Crawler) and [Feral Throwback](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Throwback) (sorry, Glowering “Joe” Rogon). [Fangren Firstborn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Firstborn) turns all of your attacking creatures into valid targets for some squirting helium. [Plaxmanta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxmanta) is the other *Dissension* Beast I used, since it gives the creatures-only deck some quasi-spells and lets you get “clever.”

### It's Evolution, Baby

![Evolution Vat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Evolution+Vat)As you all know, each of the ten *Ravnica* Block guilds has an affiliated artifact. The Boros have [Sunforger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunforger), the Izzet have [Mizzium Transreliquat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Transreliquat), and the Azorius have an anthropomorphic filing-cabinet ([Walking Archive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Archive)). The Simic's artifact is [Evolution Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Vat), a combination of [Icy Manipulator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icy+Manipulator), [Dragon Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Blood), and some TLC. Vats and evolution go together like macaroni and cheese. If want a heightened mental and physical abilities, a bucket of fluorescent goo is the only way to go. Just think: How did petty criminal Jack Napier become the super-criminal known as The Joker? He fell into a vat. I wouldn't be surprised if a vat was responsible for the Wuzzles, or the mutant hybrid known as “Brangelina.” Charles Darwin came up with his theories of evolution while sitting in a vat. (I might have my facts wrong there.)When it came time to picking creatures to dunk in the Vat, I picked ones that don't really mind if they're tapped. [Spike Weaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Weaver) leaves your opponent's creatures fumbling in a Fog, whether it's turned sideways or not. [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) can still mow down opposing weenies while he's hanging out in the blue goo. [Novijen Sages](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Novijen+Sages) evolves into a reusable [Counsel of the Soratami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counsel+of+the+Soratami), and [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus) can make a limitless number of Pentavites. Perhaps the best card to stick in the Vat is *Exodus's* [Workhorse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Workhorse). The first “evolution” doubles its counters to eight, the second gives you eight more, the third gives you sixteen, and so on. With some way to turn the colourless mana into ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif), you will eventually have infinite mana. Instead of doing that, however, I decided to use that finite mana for a big [Hurricane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hurricane). [Spike Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) and [Spike Weaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Weaver) ought to be able to give you a decent life-cushion, and [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus) won't mind that you destroyed all of its babies if you win the game at the same time.

### Mo' mir Vig, Mo' Problems

![Bioplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bioplasm)As I said, Momir Vig is the leader of the Simic. Steward of the Ravnican wilderness, Momir is like Yogi Bear's arch-nemesis, Ranger Smith, except creepier and more chimp-like. Mr. Vig has arguably the most powerful ability of all the Guild leaders. Being able to get a free [Eladamri's Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eladamri%27s+Call) whenever you play a Blue and Green creature can lead to ridiculous plays like chaining [Simic Sky Swallower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Sky+Swallower)s in the late game (or even chaining [Coiling Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle)s in the mid-game). Unfortunately, as a measly 2/2, my man Momir is a little fragile for a five-mana creature. Sheesh, Momir: take some time out for yourself! Evolve yourself that bigger, [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock)-proof butt you've always wanted! To protect Momir, I've included four [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling)s and the [Simic Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Guildmage) + [Shielding Plax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shielding+Plax) combo [outlined by the diabolical Mark Gottlieb on Monday](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/336).There are many cards that work well with Momir ([Call of the Wild](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Wild) and [Killer Instinct](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Killer+Instinct) come to mind), but I'm going to use *Guildpact's* Ooze-with-an-attitude, [Bioplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioplasm). It seems kinda Simic-y. Probably came from a vat. Fits comfortably in one, at least.

Since it's Simic Week, I'm going to skip some of the Selesnya cards that work so well with [Bioplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioplasm) (your Congregations at Dawn and your [Autochthon Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Autochthon+Wurm)s) and rely solely on Momir Vig. To set up a big [Bioplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioplasm) attack, you will have to have the Simic Guild leader on the board when you play a mono-Green creature in your pre-combat main phase. Luckily, I've included a few such creatures, the most relevant of which are [Verdant Eidolon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Eidolon) and [Cytospawn Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytospawn+Shambler). With all of the U/G creatures in the deck, [Verdant Eidolon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Eidolon) should return to your hand on a regular basis, allowing you to set up [Bioplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioplasm) turn after turn. (It also allows for turn 4 [Cytospawn Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytospawn+Shambler)s and [Simic Sky Swallower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Sky+Swallower)s). The Shamblers, meanwhile, can give your [Bioplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioplasm)s Trample if you've managed to get a counter on them (with either [Simic Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Guildmage) or [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling)) and their six power and toughness make them decent [Bioplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bioplasm)-enhancers if you have Momir Vig working his Mo-jo. [Ed. Note: Chris would like to acknowledge that yes, he missed that that last part doesn't actually work since the Shambler's base power/toughness is actually 0/0. Whether or not that leaves the trample ability as "decent" we leave up to you, gentle reader.]

This might be the first (and last) deck in **Magic** history to play more [Cytospawn Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytospawn+Shambler)s than [Simic Sky Swallower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Sky+Swallower)s, but I'm “proud” to be the one to do it.

In keeping with the Make-Your-Own-Creature theme of the Simic, you can easily mix and match these decks. For example, Momir Vig and [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) seem like they'd make a great couple, if only you could get them both to the same party. Sparks will fly, I guarantee you.

Until next time, claptrap squash blindfold homogenize symbiosis!
