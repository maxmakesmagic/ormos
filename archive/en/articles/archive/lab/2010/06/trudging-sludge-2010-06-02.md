Trudging in Sludge
==================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on June 3, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hey all, and welcome back to the lab! Watch your steps this week: I've tracked a bunch of sludgy tar through the usually spotless lab atrium. Good thing I have more than one atrium, or atriums. (Atriui?) Anyway, the reason this sludge is ruining a perfect slate of cleanliness is attributed to a tricky trio of black rares that I've been studying lately. One of which, [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger), I've been juggling for a while now. Earlier this year I asked all you faithful readers to put your heads together and think up some cool ideas on [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) and [Renegade Doppelganger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Doppelganger). Now, while the Doppelganger has gotten two decks since, the Gorger simply hasn't gotten one.

Well, today's the day I even out the scales, by providing the two best reader produced [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) decks. In addition, I'll also cover two other black rares from *Rise of the Eldrazi* that haven't gotten their due in some form. These cards, [Baneful Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneful+Omen) and [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind), are big, bulky, expensive, and thoroughly *black.* Let's swampwalk over to today's five examining tables. (Just imagine each paragraph is an examining table, with the deck as the main experiment. Hey, I am supposed to be a mad scientist, remember?)

Payfront, Payback
Like the other black rares on today's docket, I'm going to quickly examine [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind)'s massively black flavor. Not to step too hard on newly printed Vorthos Adam Lee's toes, but I like to take some time to discuss subjects that interest me, and flavor often qualifies as such a subject.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl93_repay.jpg)  
[Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind), first of all, has a great name. It is simultaneously mouthwatering and ominous, a rare juxtaposition of traits in card names. Then, add the grim image of [Sorin Markov](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin+Markov) staring out from the art box, holding a long blade and savoring a cup of wine (at least, I hope its wine.) Finally, the card text and the flavor text hit like twin stakes in your opponent's coffin. I can imagine someone in a giant multiplayer game taking a bunch of early pain, only to be left for dead, after which he unleashes his 'perfect vengeance'. Sounds sweet to me.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Repay+in+Kind)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind)  
But as this column is neither [Savor the Flavor](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?tag=SavortheFlavoramp;description=Taste%20the%20Magic) nor [Serious Fun](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?tag=Serious%20Funamp;description=Serious%20Fun), let's get to the combos! A couple of weeks ago, a reader by the name of Daniel Bennett let me know about his secret [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind) combo. It involves the three-card combination of [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind), [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam), and ... [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel). In his words, "With [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) on the board, cast [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) and keep revealing until you find a [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind). You may be at a negative life total at this point and if you get to cast [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind) ... all your opponents lose!"



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

It is devilishly simple. All the pieces are in Standard, so I went ahead and kept the rest of the deck that way. The goal in the early and middle game is to kill your opponent's threats and safely drop [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel). Once that is accomplished, wait until the end of your opponent's next turn, and then cast [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam). Reveal everything you can until you have both a negative life total and [Repay in Kind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repay+in+Kind) in hand. Cast it on your turn to stylishly cause your opponent to actually lose of negative life!

Baneful, Just Baneful
[Baneful Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneful+Omen) is a remarkably black card. Triple skulls in the converted mana cost, artwork with barely a shade of light, and another lovely 'each opponent' punishment trigger. If black is going down, it's sure as hell taking everyone down with him, and [Baneful Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneful+Omen) is a testament to that. The unlucky merfolk poring over that dark lore knows what I'm talking about.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl93_omen.jpg)  
I love playing with cards that play easily in the early game, and yet contain enormous converted mana costs. As an example, check out my [Hedron Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Matrix) deck I built [a couple weeks ago](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/89). Suspend, evoke, morph, and more are all some of my favorite mechanics for this reason.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Baneful+Omen)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneful+Omen)  
We'll worry about sticking [Baneful Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneful+Omen) on the battlefield later. For now, I'm concentrating on maximizing the levels of life loss. Large converted mana costs are a must of course, but I'd really like a way to repeatedly manipulate the top of my library. [Words of Waste](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Waste) can keep a high costing card on top of my library while bugging my opponent. [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) (one of the more ludicrous cards to be recently printed) can provide free [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)s turn after turn as a permanent. Yeah. Scry cards such as [Cryptic Annelid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Annelid) and [Condescend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend) can help stack your deck. [Liliana Vess](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess) and [Bringer of the Black Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bringer+of+the+Black+Dawn) are expensive ways to do the same.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

So, how to actually get [Baneful Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneful+Omen) onto the battlefield at a desirable turn? Classic methods from *Time Spiral* block come to the rescue. Storage lands like [Dreadship Reef](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadship+Reef) and the incorrigible [Coalition Relic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic) can amp you up to the correct levels. The rest is elementary: Just sit back and let your Omen go to work.

Feast of Thoughts
Here are the three best [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) decks that I received via email over the last couple of weeks. [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) is such a cool card for many reasons, and I'm glad that creative deck-builders saw fit to reward with an innovative deck or two.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl93_gorger.jpg)  
The first deck comes from Fluffysalt (yep) and focuses on comboing the Gorger with [Gibbering Descent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gibbering+Descent), that forgotten king of madness. Discard a Descent to an early [Patrol Hound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patrol+Hound) or [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders) to get it on the battlefield early. "Then," says Fluffysalt, "play [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger), discarding your hand and triggering hellbent. Now your opponent is losing life and cards, while you don't have an upkeep."

Taking advantage of your one-sided [Eon Hub](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub) are some cheap beaters that normally are affected by upkeeps, such as [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm) and [Phyrexian Soulgorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Soulgorger). I loved that idea of [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) + madness, so I added some [Brain Gorgers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Gorgers) (theme bell!)

The next deck is similar to the previous one in that it focuses on pairing [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) with a synergistic mechanic. This time, it's primarily hellbent. As deckbuilder Kyle Brecht said, "The best card to pair with big, evasive beaters that leave you handless is quite obviously [Anthem of Rakdos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anthem+of+Rakdos)." Kyle was strongly reminded of [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) when reading [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) for the first time, and added the discard happy Demon Dragon. Other related cards made Kyle's deck, including [Dread Slag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Slag) (a useful beater when played at the right time), [Avatar of Discord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Discord) (a staple of early hellbent lists), and [Cragganwick Cremator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cragganwick+Cremator) (a fun looking card that I never used during *Shadowmoor* block.) I added hellbent titans [Jagged Poppet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) and [Rakdos Pit Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon), and the deck was successfully modified.

This way, your [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) Is twice as big each timeâ€”or nearly four times as big if you used [Vanish into Memory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vanish+into+Memory). Another way is to use [Lorescale Coatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorescale+Coatl), which will become huge as you repeatedly draw and discard cards (and also likes to have [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) around.) Finally, you can discard lands and repeatedly dredge [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) to make your [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) bigger.
