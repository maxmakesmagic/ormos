The June 14, 2016 update
========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on June 14, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on June 14, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by [convincing your opponent to make the wrong play](#item1). The ultimate mind trick.


Today's Must
============


1. [How to Convince Your Opponent to Hand You the Game](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/how-to-convince-your-opponent-to-hand-you-the-game/) | ChannelFireball | Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa

One of the skills that truly separates the best from the rest is the ability to get your opponent to do what you want them to do simply by playing in a certain way. In this article, PVDDR talks about not just bluffing, but creating scenarios where you convince and incentivize your opponent to act the way you would like them to act.


So Is Green-White Tokens Good or Bad?
=====================================


1. Your Deck Sucks/Is Historically Good | StarCityGames.com

Green-White Tokens is probably the best deck in Standard, and [Ross Merriam thinks it's historically good](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33097_GW-Tokens-Is-Historically-Good.html). But then if you scroll down on StarCityGames.com just a tad, you'll see that [Mark Nestico has an article all about how much it sucks](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33092_Your-Deck-Sucks-2016-Edition-GW-Tokens.html).


So which is it, StarCityGames.com?! Quit playing both sides!

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Fish in All Formats](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/legacy-constructed-league-2016-06-13)

Merfolk has started to get its due in Modern, and it even (somehow) pops up in Vintage now and then. But Legacy? It hasn't really made much of an impact there.


But *Magic Online* player Haneman took the challenge and the fishy ones to a 5-0 record in Legacy League. The deck is about twelve cards off a Modern deck, and the core of the list is still Modern-legal, but Legacy-only items like [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), [Chalice of the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void), and [True-Name Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True-Name+Nemesis) give the deck another dimension.


The sideboard is packed with goodies too, as [Back to Basics](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Back+to+Basics), [Ensnare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnare), [Cursed Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Totem), and [Submerge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Submerge) pile on against other archetypes that the mono-blue deck can aim for.
