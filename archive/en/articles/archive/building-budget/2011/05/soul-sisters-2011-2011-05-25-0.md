Soul Sisters 2011
=================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on May 25, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






I had the pleasure of spending a good deal of this past weekend with the infamous rogue deckbuilder Conley Woods. Conley was happy to show off his latest brew for Standard. I quickly noticed that the list could easily be made into a budget deck if I swapped a few cards in and out. The deck looked unique, fun, and competitive. It seemed like a perfect deck to showcase for the column.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab144_cross1.jpg)  
Last summer a few players took U.S. Nationals by storm when they played an aggro life-gain deck known as Soul Sisters. The deck used [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) and [Soul's Attendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Attendant) alongside cards like [Serra Ascendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Ascendant) and [Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate). It seems strange, but an aggressive life-gain deck was probably the premier aggro deck of the tournament.

| --- | --- |
| Soul Warden | Soul's Attendant |

We haven't seen much of Soul Sisters since the rotation of the 2010 **Magic** Core Set. However, *New Phyrexia* brings a number of new tools to the table. [Suture Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest) is actually a huge upgrade over [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden). Sure, [Soul Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) is a single mana cheaper, but [Suture Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest) is usually good for at least 4 or 5 life points if it sticks around.

![Suture Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Suture+Priest)  
The most exciting addition adds a whole new element to the deck. [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) is one of my favorite cards from *New Phyrexia*. The ability to cast a [Clone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) or [Copy Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Copy+Artifact) for three mana, regardless of color, is relatively unfair. The ability to gain "infinite" life, however, is absolutely unfair in every sense of the word.

![Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian+Metamorph)  
How do we gain an arbitrarily large amount of life? First we need a [Suture Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest) or [Soul's Attendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Attendant) alongside a [Leonin Relic-Warder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder) on the battlefield. Once we've assembled those two pieces we can cast [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) and copy the [Leonin Relic-Warder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder). The Metamorph-Warder's trigger will target itself. (Remember that [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) is still an artifact.) As soon as the Metamorph-Warder leaves the battlefield, its other ability will trigger, thus putting [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) back onto the battlefield. Simply have it target itself a few billion times, gaining life each time, and it should become very difficult for your opponent to put even the smallest of dents in your life total.

![Leonin Relic-Warder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Leonin+Relic-Warder)  
This combo has been discussed at length by amazing deckbuilders like Patrick Chapin. Most people have attempted to put the combo into [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks. However, our deck, like Conley's, is attempting to be as aggressive as we can possibly be. The combo is more of a Plan C. Conveniently, every piece of the combo is an excellent spell entirely on its own.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab144_exarch.jpg)  
[Inquisitor Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisitor+Exarch) serves double duty here. The life-gain ability can actually be used aggressively and the other ability gives white decks reachâ€”in this context, the ability to close out a game even if the opponent stabilizes, something they've never really had access to. Reach usually refers to burn spells in an aggressive strategy. Once your opponent has successfully stabilized after your early creature onslaught they're usually at a very low life total. A card like [Inquisitor Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisitor+Exarch) allows us to go "over the top" and attack our opponent's life total without getting in the red zone.

![Inquisitor Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inquisitor+Exarch)  
[Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate) is far and away the best two-drop available here. There are a lot of games where you're swinging with a 5/5 or 6/6 [Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate) as early as the third turn. Pridemate has excellent synergy with the entirety of this deck.

![Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate)  
[Serra Ascendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Ascendant) is also quite ridiculous here. Things get really awkward for your opponent when you have access to one-mana 6/6 lifelink flyers. It may sound unreasonable, but the majority of your hands will be able to achieve this goal as early as the fourth turn.

![Serra Ascendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Serra+Ascendant)  
[Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) isn't very good in this deck, but I worry about being weak against [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). Our deck has the ability to gain an arbitrarily large amount of life, but we can be at ten billion life and still lose to poison counters. [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) lets us fight in the air, and it helps prevent us from taking hits from [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Squadron Hawk | Sword of Feast and Famine |

[Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) is another card that helps us when playing against [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). It's also an excellent answer to cards like [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull), [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine), and [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace). Unlike other decks, we can actually put the life gain from [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) to good use.

![Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Divine+Offering)  
[Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) is quite good as a combo piece, but it's also an incredible card in its own right. Most Sword-based decks will be quick to land a [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) or [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) to help defend themselves from your super aggressive starts. [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) can copy either of these pieces of Equipment and give you an edge in the Equipment war. [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) was the promotional giveaway card at Launch Party events around the world, so it shouldn't be difficult to acquire these if you're willing to trade a little bit.

We want our sideboard to be well prepared for [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus), [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm), [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), [Phyrexian Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader), and [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura). These and [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) seem to be the worst cards for us. Luckily, most Caw-Blade decks have stopped running [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) altogether. This gives Soul Sisters a window to stomp the existing metagame.

When fighting against decks with [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) it's important that we have some extra copies of [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering). I have no problem going up to full four copies of [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) after sideboard against these kinds of decks.

[Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) is one of the best anti-[Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) cards ever printed for White Weenie decks. It's no different here. I want to side in a full playset of [Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) against decks with [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) and/or [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm).

![Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brave+the+Elements)  
[Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) may seem to have terrible synergy with the deck, but it really only hinders our [Serra Ascendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Ascendant). In any case, [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) is the most effective answer available when playing against decks with [Phyrexian Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader). [Phyrexian Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader) is probably the best card against us in all of Standard, and I have no problem packing a little bit of heat for it after sideboarding.

![Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dismember)  
[Shrine of Loyal Legions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Loyal+Legions) gives us an excellent way to flood the board with threats after a [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment). The Shrine gets especially powerful when you have an [Ajani Goldmane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Goldmane) on the battlefield.

![Shrine of Loyal Legions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shrine+of+Loyal+Legions)

I played a match with the deck to see how it fared against one of the most popular decks in the current standard format. *New Phyrexia* isn't quite on **Magic Online** yet, so getting in a lot of testing games is somewhat difficult.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab144_match1Header.jpg)  
Before I begin, I'd like to explain why this is such a good match-up. [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) only has 1 power. This means your opponent cannot actually combo you when you have a [Soul's Attendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Attendant) or [Suture Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest) on the battlefield. Each [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) token that's made will net you a life. Your opponent can make thousands of 1/4s, but you're going to break even on life regardless. If you happen to have a [Suture Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest), then your opponents will actually kill themselves if they attempt to combo.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/gameOne.jpg)

This deck is certainly capable of great things. It has an incredibly strong match-up against Darkblade and Grixis Twin. It's at least even against White-Blue Caw-Blade. The deck may struggle with decks like Blue-Red-Green and [Valakut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut), but those decks have become significantly less popular with the rise of [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) decks that absolutely crush [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra)â€“based strategies. Soul Sisters also has an incredible match-up against other aggressive decks. Red and Vampire decks, for example, will have fits trying to deal with all of your lifegain.

This is a pretty good place to be right now. Most Standard tournaments are heavily populated by Caw-Blade, Darkblade, Mono-Red, Vampires, and [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo. The deck is powerful and very interesting. It's quite difficult to play, though, so I recommend playtesting a lot. It's important to learn all the interactions that [Soul's Attendant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Attendant) and [Suture Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest) have with cards like [Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate).
