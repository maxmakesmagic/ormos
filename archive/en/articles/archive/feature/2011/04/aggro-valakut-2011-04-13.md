Grand Prix Dallas-Fort Worth gave the Standard format plenty of interesting new decks. While Caw-Blade might have won the title, Texan Justin Corbett's team of six players took an Aggro [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) list into battle and did very well. Using a beatdown plan powered by [Bestial Menace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bestial+Menace) and [Hero of Oxid Ridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge), the deck can kill as early as the fourth turn. And if that plan doesn't work, the players switch gears and use [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) as a turbo win condition! Of the six who played the deck, five made Day 2. Read more about Grand Prix Dallas-Fort Worth in the [official event coverage](/en/events/coverage/shiels-stands-alone-dallas).
