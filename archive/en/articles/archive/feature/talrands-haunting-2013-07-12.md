Talrand's Haunting
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 12, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







Apparently I'm on a tokens kick this week, but I really like the look of this list. It was played to 3–1 in a Daily Event by HelloHouseplant (hello!), and plays a lot of cards I like playing with.


![Intangible Virtue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Intangible+Virtue)
 Despite playing the same base as many other blue decks ([Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas)), this deck uses them to power out a variety of token-making cards, finishing the game with [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue) and a [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike). 



[Talrand's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%27s+Invocation) in particular is a card I've not seen much of, so getting to play with it is awesome. Between that, [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Midnight Haunting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Haunting), and Sorin, you should rarely be short tokens, especially once you consider how well Augur digs and how well Snapcaster flashes back. 


 The removal suite is also atypical, relying on [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip) and [Warped Physique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warped+Physique), both of which are incredibly efficient when used correctly. In this case, chumping with a token lets you [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip) anything that isn't [Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus), and [Warped Physique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warped+Physique) should often be enough given the hand-filling power of Augur and flashback cards. 


I've played a fair amount with tokens in Standard, but I have yet to try the blue version, and after seeing this deck I think I shall rectify that.
