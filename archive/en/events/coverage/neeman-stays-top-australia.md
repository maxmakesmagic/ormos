Neeman Stays on Top in Australia
================================




October 14-16, 2011













Grand Prix Brisbane 2011
------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri11/winner618.jpg)

Congratulations to Jeremy Neeman, winner of Grand Prix Brisbane! After a battling through a talented and international Top 8, Neeman takes home his second Australian Grand Prix trophy in a row, following on from his victory in Sydney last year.


Jeremy Neeman, piloting Blue/Black control, narrowly beat out Tim Fondum, piloting G/W Tokens, in three tense games. Rounding out the Top 8 were Shouta Yasooka, Andreas Pranoto, Jacky Zhang, Luke Mulcahy, Daniel Unwin, and Hao-Shan Huang. Congratulations to all the Top 8 finalists, and once again to 'The' Jeremy Neeman, the Grand Prix Brisbane champion!

Quarterfinals - Hao-Shan Huang vs Tim Fondum
--------------------------------------------


*By Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

Huang managed a feisty 9 on 2 D6, but Fondum crushed it with a 12. Having used up all his luck on the die roll, Fondum promptly followed that up with a mulligan.


Huang powered out a turn 3 [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) with a [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), while Fondum summoned a turn two [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Lunarch), a turn 3 [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), and a turn 4 [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold). The Golem token attacked, and Huang chump blocked it with the [Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simulacrum).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri11/T8_Huang_Match.jpg)
*Huang*
Huang's fourth turn was an impressive one, a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) for a [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) and an [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). But unfortunately, impressive because it looked like it wouldn't be anywhere near enough, as Fondum spread +1/+1 counters across his team, untapped and sent in the 4/5 Hero (plus two 1/1 Soldiers) , the 4/4 Golem Token, and the 2/2 [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), with Mikaeus sitting at home, ready to lend a hand from a distance. Huang pondered his options, but basically just died.


Fondum 1 – Huang 0


Fondum's start was less explosive in game two, with his double [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim)'s being swept aside by a [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm). He followed up with a [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), and a [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold). Huang spent two [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast)s on the Hero, but Fondum had a second. [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) further developed Huang's mana, but if he didn't have a way to kill that Hero, he was going to take a lot of damage, and quickly.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri11/T8_Fondum_Match.jpg)
*Tim Fondum*
Fondum attacked with the Hero and chums, and the [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) and Golem. Huang animated an [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus), and holding that and the [Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simulacrum) in each hand, leaned over the table to consider his blocks. He finally placed the Nexus in front of the Hero, and the [Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simulacrum) in front of the [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer). Fondum sank all of his mana into his [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township), and that was all he (as in me) wrote.


Tim Fondum defeats Hao-Shan Huang 2 – 0

Fondum powered out an aggressive start with a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into a [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), followed by a [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold). Still on two lands, Mulcahy shook his head and played a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), still off [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) mana... If he even had it. The next attack step was a big one, with Fondum's army swinging for 14. Smiling, Mulcahy showed the [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) stranded in his hand.


Tim Fondum 2 – Luke Mulcahy 1




Semifinals - Jeremy Neeman vs Andreas Pranoto
---------------------------------------------


*By Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

"Six Garruk's!" Neeman exclaimed as they perused each others decklists. "Seven," Pranoto corrected him with a smile, as he had a third [Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) in his sideboard.


Neeman tried so hard to best Pranoto's 5 on one D6, that his first two throws rolled right off the table. His third rolled a 1. Pranoto got things started with two [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), and a third turn [Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless). Neeman tried to suppress Pranoto's mana with a [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) for one, but he kept laying lands, while Garruk kept laying Wolves.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri11/T4_Pranoto.jpg)
*Andreas Pranoto stares death in the face. Well, Neeman in the face, anyway.*
Pranoto had Neeman down to 11 before he managed to dig up his [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) again with a [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy). He leveraged his breathing room into a pair of [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx)es, the first evidently had a [Beast Within](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within), the second stuck around long enough to kill Garruk, draw Neeman a fist full of filthy lucre, and basically take game one alongside a [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan).


Neeman 1 – Pranoto 0


Where game one was all about Neeman clawing back from the brink of an almost certain Wolf-related fatality, he was all over game two. Pranoto's first non-mana play was a [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter). That was hit by a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). He tried a [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) for 6, but [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) milked more mileage out of the [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). Neeman summoned a [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx), and when Pranoto didn't have a [Beast Within](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within) in hand, Neeman was able to ride the steady stream of cards to victory.

*Jeremy Neeman slowly and surely taking apart all-comers.*

Sunday 16th October 9:20pm – The Top 5 Cards of Grand Prix Brisbane
-------------------------------------------------------------------


*By Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*


![Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Snapcaster+Mage)

[Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) – Tiago Chan waited a long, long time to see his reward for winning the 2007 **Magic** Invitational become a reality, and it was no doubt well worth the wait. [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) played an important role in Jeremy Neeman's winning Blue/Black Control deck this weekend, and he wasn't the only one embracing the power of the widely sought after 2-drop. Blue/White decks were flashing back [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s, Blue/Black decks were flashing back [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s, and Solar Flare decks were flashing back anything they could get their hands on. Tiago Chan, we love your work.



![Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)

[Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) – while the number of [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Liliana%2BVess)s getting played in both Blue/Black decks, and Solar Flare decks varied from player to player, there's no question that this new streamlined version leaves the original in the dust. She may not seem as flashy as some other Planeswalkers, but as the turns pass, her sheer inexorable power becomes clear. [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Liliana%2BVess) will see plenty of play in the years to come.



![Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Garruk+Relentless)

[Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) – Speaking of flashy Planeswalkers, Garruk breaks (and flips) the mould. Both Tim Fondum's and Andreas Pranoto's top 4 decks used Garruk to pump out a steady stream of Wolves, putting pressure on the various control decks they faced this weekend.



![Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kessig+Wolf+Run)

[Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) – Everyone assumed that last season's [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) decks were a thing of the past, but [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) has helped ensure those [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)s will be crashing into the red zone with lethal certainty for some time to come.



![Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Geistflame)

[Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) – This one's a little subtle, but the Mono Red decks have been loving themselves a little [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) action this weekend. [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) it sure aren't, but you don't have to look far in the format to see a wealth of tasty, soft, one-toughness targets. And then there's enabling Bloodthirst, [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) does it all. (As long as your definition of "all" is as loose as mine.)




Finals: Tim Fondum vs Jeremy Neeman
-----------------------------------


*By Pip Hunn*

Fondum mulliganed to five, but still managed an impressive start of [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) into [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer). Neeman shrugged and took the five from Fondum's attack, and then let a [Viridian Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Emissary) resolve. His end of turn [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) killed Fondum's Golem, but left him with men on the board, incuding a freshly cast [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise).


Neeman nonchalantly fell to 11 and passed, this time with four mana open. The [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) he represented failed to appear, with all the creatures getting through unmolested. Neeman fell to seven, still with nothing on his turn. Fondum attacked, and Neeman [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)d the Emissary, taking two and falling to a precarious five life.


[Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) made Fondum's task look a lot harder... Until [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) landed on the table. On to game two!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri11/Finals_Fondum.jpg)
Tim Fondum 1 – Jeremy Neeman 0


Fondum led off with an [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), which was quickly [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)d. Neeman, keen to take less of a beating this game, wasn't going to let any creatures stick around longer than he had to.


Unfortunately for Neeman, the next creature Fondum cast was, "Your friend and mine!", [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll). Neeman had the answer in a [Geth's Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict), and once again the board was clear. Neeman milled himself with a [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) and picked particularly well, milling himself into a pair of [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice).


Fondum went for a second [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll), but Neeman simply Thought, Thought Twice, then flashed in a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and re-cast his [Geth's Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict). Then, for good measure, he untapped and summoned a [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx). Fondum didn't cast anything for the next two turns. The rest, as Holmes would say, was elementary\*.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri11/Finals_Neeman.jpg)
Jeremy Neeman 1 – Tim Fondum 1


*Yes, I'm well aware Holmes didn't actually say that. The saying still stands.*


Both players mulliganed to five, and joked about going to zero to increase the tension. They kept their hands and began the last game of Grand Prix: Brisbane. Fondum began the game with a turn three [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll). Neeman only had a [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), and no action on his turn.


Fondum followed up with a [Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk), but Neeman had a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to stop it. Fondum powered up his [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll) with a [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township). Neeman found a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to deal with [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll), but the Birds were relentless. Fondum tried an [Elspeth Tirel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth+Tirel), which was [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)d, and then Neeman summoned a [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx).


The first Sphinx was short-lived, as Fondum had an [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), but Neeman had drawn another, and the 4/6 flier started to draw him the gas he needed to pull ahead. Fondum summoned a [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine).


When the [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) rumbled into the combat zone, Neeman had a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) for it. Fondum tried a [Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless), but Neeman was again able to [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) into a [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) to stop any pressure. Meanwhile, his [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) had started getting aggressive in the air. A [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) from Neeman killed both Fondum's Wurmcoil tokens, but Fondum fond another [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll) on the top of his deck.


Neeman once again dug through his deck, and this time he found a [Geth's Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict). That, and a [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), prompted Fondum to extend his hand.


Congratulations to Jeremy Neeman, winner of his second Australian Grand Prix in a row, and champion of Grand Prix: Brisbane!


Jeremy Neeman 2 – Tim Fondum 1
