IntoTheAether Invokes the Ultimus Principle
===========================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 12, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar













|  |
| --- |
| *Mistform Ultimus, recently sighted in a field of forget-me-nots* |

I issued a (small, admittedly) challenge, and you rose from your computer chairs to meet that challenge. Thanks to you, I played a thoroughly enjoyable week of Tribal Wars games versus a menagerie of kooky decks. I saw monoblack-Beasts, Spiders, Cephalids, Illusions, Kavu, Dwarves, and Battlemages, just to name a few. Well done, folks! 

[Last week](/en/articles/archive/men-vedalken-and-rachelr-2004-10-05) I tackled two tribes spawned by *Mirrodin* block, Humans and Vedalken. Several readers pointed out to me that although only five different Vedalken exist, in Tribal Wars there is *always* [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus). The Ultimus is a great addition to otherwise anemic tribes of any kind, and it's something that often helps you against other tribes (imagine facing Slivers, for example, or [Elvish Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Champion)). You'd think I would remember [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus), since I said these exact same things in [my preview of him](/en/articles/archive/ultimate-octopus-2003-01-16) many moons ago. Anyway, in my Vedalken deck I think dropping [Synod Artificer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Synod+Artificer) for [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) is a terrific idea. Let's all agree that from now on, any deck that needs an extra boost to its tribe can invoke the Ultimus Principle.


> 
> *The Ultimus Principle: When in doubt, give [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) a Tribal Wars shout.*
> 
> 
> 

This week I want to do some preparation for *Champions of Kamigawa* on the horizon. *Champions* is going to introduce or enhance a whole slew of Tribal Wars tribes, so I thought it would be fun to look at a few of these tribes to see how they fare pre-*Kamigawa*.

### Before Spirits Joined A War

Since it's Spirit Week, let's start there. The Spirit decks I've seen online are almost exclusively green/white and revolve around *Odyssey's* Phantom creatures--like [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad) and [Phantom Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Tiger)--in combination with a permanent toughness enhancer like [Elephant Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide), [Mirari's Wake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari%27s+Wake), or [Shared Triumph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shared+Triumph). This idea is a good one, but I've got this “create my own original deck” curse that has me wanting to try something different. For example, a black Spirits deck is possible with [Vampiric Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampiric+Spirit), [Revenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revenant), [Emissary of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emissary+of+Despair), [Bellowing Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bellowing+Fiend), and [Graveborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graveborn+Muse) (or hey... you could invoke the Ultimus Principle!). 

Another idea is to focus on the Phantom creatures from a different angle. I decided to try my hand at a monowhite Spirit deck that relies on adding +1/+1 counters via [Test of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Test+of+Faith) (one of my favorite utility cards in *Darksteel*) and [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit). Note that you can focus on a mostly green deck using the artifacts above along with [Seedborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seedborn+Muse), [Phantom Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Tiger), [Phantom Nantuko](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nantuko), [Phantom Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur), and [Phantom Nishoba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nishoba). **Spirits vs. Wizards (blue/white)**

![Pure Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pure+Reflection)Props to graveltoungue for using as many tricks as Wizards can manage all in the same deck. Early on I was worried because he put [Pure Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pure+Reflection) into play and then used [Artificial Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificial+Evolution) to have “Reflection” say “Spirit.” Ouch. He also had [Riptide Biologist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Biologist), [Stormscape Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormscape+Apprentice) and [Patron Wizard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+Wizard), the last two of which were really frustrating. I played a [Sun Droplet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) to keep me in the game and two [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit)s just because I could. Eventually I drew into two [Eternal Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Dragon)s to sift through all of my land and make sure the “Spirit” token was always on my side. He drew [Wirefly Hive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirefly+Hive), but lost two of three coin flips to slow down my Dragons. Whew. I wish I could have drawn [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant) to make the game less stressful.**Spirits vs. Beasts (black)**

That's right, it's a monoblack Beasts deck. He used [Prowling Pangolin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prowling+Pangolin), [Spined Basher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spined+Basher), [Putrid Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Raptor), and [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) with things like [Cruel Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Revival), [Spawning Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit), and [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) as support cards. It's a neat idea, but he thought my deck was spiffy too since I'm able to get a [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad) to go along with [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit), two [Sun Droplet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet)s, and [Mirrodin's Core](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrodin%27s+Core). His creature removal eventually got the best of me despite some amazing topdecking on my part, and he ran me over sitting on one life (which he would lose at his next upkeep thanks to the Arena). Good stuff and a fun game.

**Spirits vs. Soldiers (white)**

The Spirits faced off against a fairly typical *Onslaught* deck this time around, though it was a Soldier deck with [Honorable Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honorable+Scout) and [Reborn Hero](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reborn+Hero) to go along [Leonin Shikari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Shikari), [Daru Warchief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Warchief), and equipment like [Bonesplitter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonesplitter) and [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer). A second turn [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad), coupled with a grip of three Tests of Faith meant that the military folks really had no chance. My Nomad mowed through the opposition while the combination of [Windborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windborn+Muse) and [Sun Droplet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) kept me at twenty life. A [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) in the end simply sped up the Soldier's death.

**Spirits vs. Spiders (green/blue/red)**

![Silklash Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Silklash+Spider)When I played my first Spirit, my opponent said “Ah, just the kind of deck I like.” I was nervously wondering what he meant, since all I had seen to that point was a [Shivan Oasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Oasis), [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), Forest, and Island. Turns out he had a Spider deck, including Spiders of the Pincer, Canopy, Giant, and Spitting Variety. It was when he dropped [Silklash Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silklash+Spider), though, that my heart sank. Flying Spirits versus Spiders? Urgh! A [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) made the situation look even more bleak. So the Spirits lost, right? No way! Not with Faith and Hope on my side! Two timely Tests of Faith kept an [Emissary of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emissary+of+Hope) out of Silklash reach, and then a [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) combined with [Mirrodin's Core](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrodin%27s+Core) kept it growing. My 13/12 Spirit finally overwhelmed the arachnids and that warhammer they had stolen from the Elephants.**Spirits vs. Angels**

Ah, a nice peaceful conversation between two friendly white tribes. [Sustainer of the Realm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sustainer+of+the+Realm), [Serra Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Advocate), and [Angel of Mercy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Mercy) faced off against a [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad), [Windborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windborn+Muse), [Sun Droplet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet), and [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit). Once again a timely [Test of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Test+of+Faith) turned the tide of battle, robbing my opponent of an Angel while allowing my Nomad to become enormous. I had a grip full of Spirits while my opponent had stalled on five land, then he conceded because of needing to leave. I would say I had divine luck on my side, but I was playing against Angels after all.

**Spirits vs. Shamans (green/red)**

The trouble with Shamans before *Champions of Kamigawa* is that the best Shamans either have double-green or double-red in their mana cost. My opponent's deck was pretty much how I would have built it, with [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), [Spikeshot Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Goblin), [Vulshok Sorcerer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Sorcerer), [Viridian Acolyte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Acolyte), and although I didn't see them I'm betting [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic) was in there too. He capped the deck off with some affinity tricks like artifact land, [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating), [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter). Unfortunately, he was stuck on green mana for too long, which let my pair of [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad)s be aggressive. [Windborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windborn+Muse) and [Sun Droplet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Droplet) kept his offense in check. By the time [Spikeshot Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Goblin) and [Vulshok Sorcerer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Sorcerer) came into play, I already had three Spirits with multiple counters on the offensive.

**Spirits vs. Cephalids (blue)**

My opponent started the game with [Manipulate Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manipulate+Fate), which was fun to see. Cephalids of varying shapes and sizes appeared, but not fast enough as my [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad), [Windborn Muse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windborn+Muse), and [Emissary of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emissary+of+Hope) ate into his life total. When he tapped out to play [Parallel Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Thoughts) (commenting on how blazingly fast the Spirits were), I looked sorrowfully at the three [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)s in my hand. Poor Cephalids and their well-laid plans. I'm sorry to say I forgot what was hiding under the Thoughts since I waited an hour to write the game log. A flurry of [Mesmeric Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mesmeric+Fiend)s, [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher)s, [Chainer's Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict)s, and [Corrupt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt) kept my [Ravenous Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats) and [Relentless Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Rats) in check. I [Echoing Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Decay)ed twice to balance the board, and eventually it came down to a topdecking war between two 3/3 Rats and a Butcher. Unfortunately, I drew Swamps while my opponent drew, fittingly enough, [Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare). **Snakes vs. Birds (blue/white)**

You know what? Snakes don't fly. I could only [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse), [Exclude](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude), and [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) so many times. My opponent was using [Frozen Solid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frozen+Solid) as creature control, which managed to shut down a [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) and an Islandwalking [Anaconda](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anaconda). Aven of various kinds and a [Suntail Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suntail+Hawk) chirped their way over my Snakes' hissing heads. We played another game after that and the Snakes triumphed easily, largely because 1/1 flying bodies couldn't stand up to 3/3 non-flying bodies. The Snakes and Birds will have to wait for the rubber game, I suppose.

**Snakes vs. Elves (green)**

This was just a weird game. My opponent played a quick [Wirewood Hivemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Hivemaster) and two [Everglove Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everglove+Courier)s. I [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake)d a [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher), then [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)d it back to my hand to nab a [Heedless One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heedless+One). The Elvish beats kept on coming, though, and the only thing keeping my opponent in check was a [Tangle Asp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Asp) and a few 3/3 bodies. An [Anaconda](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anaconda) of mine evolved to have Forestwalk, so I ticked away at his life but eventually had to hold back to block. Then... the Elves... stopped. No more attacking. No more casting. I guess my Snakes looked intimidating, but I'm sure if my opponent kept up the pressure I would have died. Instead, I gathered [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake)s and [Exclude](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude)s in hand to counter the Elves he drew, freeing up my [Anaconda](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anaconda) to attack. I cast [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) six times in the game thanks to [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) and at one point had ten Snakes on the table.

Oh, there are so many other tribes left untouched. My approach to Tribal Wars has been to make some unusual tribal decks and jump into the fray so you can see what games are like. As I said, when *Champions of Kamigawa* rolls around, expect me to revisit the format to look at the new and improved tribes. Tribal Wars harkens back to my earliest experiences with theme deckbuilding, so it's no surprise that it turns out to be one of my favorite formats to play. Expect some Demon, Ogre, Fox, and Moonfolk decks in a month or two.

The basic idea is to use solid comes-into-play creatures multiple times, winning through sheer stubbornness. In testing, as I said, the deck performed beautifully. I'm not sure I ever lost a game in which I hit three mana. 

Then I showed up for [PDC 15](http://boards1.wizards.com/showthread.php?t=314465).

The good news is that almost fifty players showed up to match all-commons wits, doubling the usual attendance. Throughout the evening players would send me messages to let me know that they were playing in the event because of my article and were either winning, having fun, or--usually--both. All in all, I think PDC 15 was a great success (I'm assuming [PDC 16](http://archive.wizards.com/link=http://boards1.wizards.com/showthread.php?t=318727) was fun too, though I didn't make it to that one).

The bad news is that I got creamed. It all started off well enough, beating EJO's monoblack Zombie deck 2-0. Then I fell to Guler's Madness deck 1-2, Repressed's monoblack deck 1-2, and, finally, got steamrolled by toda1905's Affinity deck. I ended the evening at 1-3 in matches, 4-6 in games. Ah well. After the PDC Worlds, a new season of Pauper **Magic** will roll around, so maybe you haven't heard the last of my all-commons ineptitude.

Next week it's time to roll out a whole new format and take a peek into *Champions of Kamigawa* Beta Land. In the meantime, let's hope we get a chance to match wits online.
