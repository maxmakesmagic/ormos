Draft 2: Anton Jonsson and John Larkin
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 15, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Headshot%209-2014_3.jpg)
By Randy Buehler











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a828.jpg)Anton Jonsson and John Larkin had to be thinking about the national team competition when they sat down for the second draft on Thursday. Their 7-2 records earned them a seat on table 2, but even more important they are each national champions and their countries sit first and second in the team competition. Larkin's Irish squad is a slim one point against of Jonsson's Swedes. Jonsson has been on quite a tear this season, with two Top 8's and counting for the rising star of Team Punisher.

Jonsson's draft wasn't terribly interesting. He first picked a [Shadowmage Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator) and his neighbors seemed perfectly happy to have him in blue-black. His second pack was a [Cephalid Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Looter) (with a common missing he had to think that Cole Swannack had taken a [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel), which was indeed true). Then his third pick was a [Ghastly Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise). The only really interesting decision Jonsson had to make was I his very first pack of *Torment*: [Faceless Butcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) or [Nantuko Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade)? It was a nice decision to have to make ... he took the Butcher.

Larkin took [Ghastly Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise) first and it looked like the two of them might wind up fighting, but Larkin's second pick was a [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) and there turned out to be plenty of black for he and Jonsson to share. Both players wound up with great decks going into *Judgment*, but all they got passed in *Judgment* was lots and lots of green cards. Apparently, after first picking that Mongrel, Swannack got passed an [Iridescent Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iridescent+Angel) and took the hint, switching into white and winding up with an amazing white deck. All the decks I saw had flaws and the outcome of the next thee rounds looks like it will be determined by raw play-skill. Whoever wins this table is in great shape to make Top 8 and even a 2-1 mark could leave any of these guys in good shape to make a run.
