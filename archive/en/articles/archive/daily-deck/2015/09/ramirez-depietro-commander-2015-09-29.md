Ramirez DePietro Commander
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on September 29, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Today's deck is a Commander deck built by *Magic* R&D member Robert Schuster. Robert was on the Wizards team at this year's [Magic Community Cup](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/2015-magic-community-cup), and he built our nearly perfect-score Commander deck featuring the Pirate legend, [Ramirez DePietro](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ramirez+DePietro).


[Ramirez DePietro](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ramirez+DePietro) is a Pirate who pillaged the seas of Jumuraa, one of the major supercontinents on Dominaria. Robert chose to build the most pirate-y Commander deck that ever pirated, and as you can see, the deck is full of Ships, Pirates, Rogues, and treasure. Even the lands have a lot of pirate themes, both in the names and in art. Ramirez is a flamboyant Pirate who loved to wear flashy and colorful clothes and jewelry, which is also represented in the deck with cards such as [Sisay's Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sisay%27s+Ring), [Traveler's Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveler%27s+Cloak), [Sapphire Medallion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Medallion), and [Jet Medallion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jet+Medallion).


One of the interesting things about Ramirez is he lies about his age. Robert used cards like [Elixir of Immortality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality), [Chalice of Life](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+Life), and [Fountain of Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fountain+of+Youth) to show that Ramirez was actually quite old but covered it up well. Finally, another card that was included in the deck was [Clever Impersonator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator), and it turns out that years after Ramirez ruled the seas, a Shapeshifter named [Halfdane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halfdane) assumed his form. You may call him a [Clever Impersonator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clever+Impersonator).


Although Robert's deck is packed with flavor, it didn't really have a good way of winningâ€”and he lost our match against Community team member Joel Larrson. The Pirate deck was able to put up a decent fight, with spells such as [Tidal Wave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidal+Wave) to take out a blocker and [Island of Wak-Wak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island+of+Wak-Wak) to take care of flyers, but eventually [Medomai the Ageless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Medomai+the+Ageless) and the endless flow of [Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk)s got the best of him. Regardless, it was an epic battle and the most interesting game of Commander I've ever seen.
