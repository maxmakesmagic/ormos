U/B Pickles, Boros Deck Wins, U/G Scryb&Force, and U/W Urzatron remain the best decks to gun for, just like the previous weeks. All of those decks are good, solid, and proven choices. I “solved” the controversial naming issue of Katsuhiro Mori's brainchild. TriscuitTron? PickleTron? DemonTron (that's Yasher\_Huyasher's deck with a red splash)? How do you tag a deck with so many possible win conditions? Well, I just labeled all these extremely similar decks with one general name: U/W Urzatron. It doesn't sound cool, but at least everyone knows what we're talking about.

![Sedge Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sedge+Sliver)The big surprises this week were the rise of Mono Green Aggro and the decline of Mono Black Rack discard. Mono Green Aggro leans heavily on [Blanchwood Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blanchwood+Armor) and green creature enhancers to deal a lot of damage with critters like [Scryb Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger) and [Silhana Ledgewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silhana+Ledgewalker) quickly. If the online results are an indication, it is a viable archetype that is also conveniently cheap to construct, ticket-wise. The Black Rack discard deck appears to have been a one-week fashion. It has disappeared before I could even start thinking about how good the deck exactly is, but those fast drifts happen all the time in the hectic ***Magic** Online* world. We also saw two interesting new deck archetypes in the Premier Event Top 8s, which I will investigate if they continue to do well in online events:

* JockeD's B/G/R Aggro, playing all the good stuff in those colors, like [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape), [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), [Sedge Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedge+Sliver), and [Demonfire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonfire). It looked solid.
* noehcnoen's Searing Martyr, which included the standard [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) and [Proclamation of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth) combo, but the innovation was to add red for [Searing Meditation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Meditation), a cool combo with [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands).

### The State of Extended

In last week's online Extended events, [Blue/White Urzatron](http://boards1.wizards.com/showpost.php?p=10859004&postcount=44), [TEPS Desire](http://boards1.wizards.com/showpost.php?p=10858887&postcount=43), and [Aggro Loam](http://boards1.wizards.com/showpost.php?p=10859678&postcount=51) are the big three decks. Next up are [Trinket Angel](http://boards1.wizards.com/showpost.php?p=10859271&postcount=47), [Affinity](http://boards1.wizards.com/showpost.php?p=10859965&postcount=56), [CAL](http://boards1.wizards.com/showpost.php?p=10876539&postcount=65), and [Boros Deck Wins](http://boards1.wizards.com/showpost.php?p=10858670&postcount=42). Those are the other major decks to look out for.

Compared to last week, TEPS, Blue/White Urzatron and Aggro Loam taken huge leaps in popularity. Boros Deck Wins and Gifts Rock have declined. I will cover the state of the online Extended metagame in detail again soon, as I understand many eyes are on that format in [this PTQ season](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/events/ptq). For now I'll leave you with the notion that having an answer to [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) might be the key in surviving Extended this weekend.

### The Online Tribal Wars (Standard) Metagame

![Coat_of_Arms](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/9ED/Coat_of_Arms.jpg)Tribal Wars is a casual ***Magic** Online* format that emphasizes creature combat: One-third of every deck must be of a single creature type. There are no sideboards. Tribal Wars – Standard uses the normal Standard card pool. A Tribal 4x Premier Event took place last weekend, so I figured this week would be the perfect time to cover the format. 

So what does the metagame look like? I won't put up detailed tables, simply because there haven't been enough major Tribal events to properly statisticize. Since the *Time Spiral* release, we've just had a couple small Premier events, and two big 4x Open tournaments (one was last weekend, the other was last month). Looking at these results, we can see that over half of the Standard Tribal Wars decks use Humans as its tribe. Humans, of all creature types! Powerful mythical creature types such as Dragons and Elementals don't impress in this creature-heavy format. Nope, blank boring Humans. Disappointing, but with the amount of playable humans in the card pool, that is not a surprise. The tribal rules hardly seem like a constraint when you are adding the likes of [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Knight of the Holy Nimbus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Holy+Nimbus), [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands), and [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll) to your deck. While other creature types are scrambling for playables, the humans have all the choice in the world. Here are representative versions of some of the major archetypes:

Just like Nassif's deck, this deck is designed to exploit [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) plus [Proclamation of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proclamation+of+Rebirth). Nassif's version already included some Humans in [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) and [Weathered Wayfarer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weathered+Wayfarer), so the leap to Tribal was a natural one. In order to get up to the required 20 Human creatures, the deck had to cut staples like [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research), replacing them with [Magus of the Disk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Disk) as [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) #5-7, as well as [Sky Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar), which is mostly just a good card drawer but can also function as a win condition if needed. [Icatian Javelineers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Javelineers) and [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) aren't that exiting, but hey, you need something to round out your creature base (and to pay the forecast of [Sky Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky+Hussar)). In a format that is dictated by creatures, this archetype should be strong. Once you are gaining 15+ life per Martyr / Proclamation loop, the opponent has to commit a lot of resources to deal any damage. Then you spring the [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) and win the game. A vulnerability of this deck is to disruption like [Persecute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute).

This is essentially a white control deck with [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), which remains a commandingly influential card in a format where every deck has at least 20 creatures. The creatures in this deck cost more mana than [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), which is the way it is supposed to be, since it allows you to first clear the board without any losses on your side and then dominate it with powerful flyers like [Adarkar Valkyrie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Valkyrie). Easy. [Blinding Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Angel) adds a special element to the deck, delivering a “lock” that not every deck has outs against. The deck dips into red for [Firemane Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Angel) and black for [Angel of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair) to increase the Angel count up to 20. When I saw the black cards, I immediately thought “Why not blue for [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel)?”, but black might still be better because it adds [Persecute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) to the mix. Without that discard spell, this deck would not have a chance against the Martyr Proclamation engine.

This mid-range good stuff deck is cleverly built, because it can execute any game plan it wants to and switch between them at will. It can run out a fast curve with [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll), [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice), [Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaya+Ballard%2C+Task+Mage), and [Avalanche Riders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders), or it can go for the control route with [Martyr of Ashes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Ashes) and [Stuffy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll). The good thing is that most cards in the deck have a dual aggro/control orientation. In a format where you have no sideboards, such flexibility is invaluable. [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) can either attack for some points or clear the board. [Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaya+Ballard%2C+Task+Mage) can nug the last couple points to the dome or peck off opposing creatures. A vital part of the deck is [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon), which can destroy certain decks. Urza lands or dual lands there? Not anymore now, let's turn your entire mana base into one giant color screw. Topping off with a snow land engine and [Skred](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skred)s, this great deck might just also finish you with a [Skred](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skred) on its own [Stuffy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll).

If I would participate in a Tribal tournament, I would pick the B/W/R Angel control deck. It has [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), which just has to be the best card in the format, as well as creatures that are larger than the opponent's. The deck also has [Persecute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) against Martyr Proclamation and can beat everything in the long game with [Firemane Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Angel). Playing control seems like the correct choice in this format, and B/W/R Angel seems like the best option to me.
