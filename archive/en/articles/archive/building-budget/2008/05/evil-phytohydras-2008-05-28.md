Evil Phytohydras!
=================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on May 28, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_253.png)
By Evil Ben Bleiweiss












Greetings, my poppets! Have I gotten here through some gateway or parallel dimension? Am I a rampaging Id to Ben's normally in-check Ego? And what is this "evil" anyhow? Am I evil just because I've drugged Ben, lathed him up in the finest of honey, and thrown him in a closet with crazed, starving weasels? No! I call that a public service for the greater good of **Magic**, it is!

Let me explain the ins and outs of this-here 25-0 masterpiece, because I'm sure that you all can't fully appreciate the genius that went into this deck.


 



![Sprouting Phytohydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sprouting+Phytohydra)

First off, I got [Sprouting Phytohydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Phytohydra) in this deck because it's a [Sprouting Phytohydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Phytohydra) deck. Duh. Then, I got [Ashes of the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashes+of+the+Fallen) and [Haakon, Stromgald Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haakon%2C+Stromgald+Scourge) because so few of you care that it's a Plant Hydra. We'll make it a Knight, cause that'll make 98.1% of you unhappy that I'm focusing on creature type, and 1.9% upset that I'm making sure it doesn't matter that our Plant Hydra is a Plant Hydra!

8.8% of you want proactive hand removal, so [One with Nothing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+with+Nothing) it is. You think I want [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) or [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) or [Mind Shatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Shatter)? Your gotta prove yourself to use cards like that, you punks. Instead, papa Evil is gonna lay a whoppin' on his own hand, and then beat you down anyways despite the 'look ma, no hands!' approach to this deck! I don't want a win—I want a humiliation.


[Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) is there because people like playing with creatures, and I don't want them to have fun playing against me. If I kill all their creatures, maybe they'll disconnect and I'll just count the game as a win for this column. Or something. I'm just going to say I won every game anyhow, and edit out all the losses. 25-0 record, here I come!


[Fecundity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fecundity) lets me draw my entire deck and get my hand stacked for [Spiraling Embers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiraling+Embers) / [Rite of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) / [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) at the end, so I can do my hand's worth of damage to an opponent. Someone emailed Good Ben or posted in the forums about the Embers, but I'm taking credit for the suggestion, because this here column is my column this week, and not theirs.


I bet you wanna see how this did. I'll oblige, but only because I'm the Evil Genius, and you are not.


**Game 1: Korn1399 (Elves)**


This guy drops [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) on turn two, and then puts double [Blanchwood Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blanchwood+Armor) on her. The nerve! What am I supposed to do other than drop [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) to chum- block, and then [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) his board out from under him? Then I get [Fecundity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fecundity), which bites a chunky [Tranquility](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tranquility). [Tranquility](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tranquility)! That could wreck this whole deck! Well, not if I have a second [Fecundity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fecundity), [Æther Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Flash), and [Sprouting Phytohydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Phytohydra). At 6 life, I draw the majority of my deck and give him the big ol' how-do-you-do known as a 26-point [Spiraling Embers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiraling+Embers).


korn1399: i think that was the cheapest lamest way to win


I yam what I yam!


Record: 1-0


**Game 2: Sargis (Red-Green Land Destruction / Burn)**


 



![One with Nothing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=One+With+Nothing)

He wins because I get stuck with three [One with Nothing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+with+Nothing) in my hand. I have him locked down with double [Æther Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Flash), and [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation), but I get burned to death over [One with Nothing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+with+Nothing). [One with Nothing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+with+Nothing)! What kind of no-name jerk plays with that card? Not me, anymore! I had enough lands, spells and everything to survive four land destruction spells.

Record: 2-0


**Out:** 4 [One with Nothing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=One+with+Nothing)  
**In:** 4 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)


What I really need is [Abandon Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abandon+Hope)—but I'll settle for just taking the best card out of my opponent's hand. That's one less card they can play against me! But wait, you say, doesn't that leave me with no way to get [Haakon, Stromgald Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haakon%2C+Stromgald+Scourge) out of my hand? How perceptive!


**Out:** 4 [Haakon, Stromgald Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haakon%2C+Stromgald+Scourge), 4 [Ashes of the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashes+of+the+Fallen)

Now is the hour of my discontent, and my discontent comes from dreams of millions of plants, carried away on roots of one bitter ordeal after the next.


Is this a fate worse than honey-weasels? Until next we meet, I will bid you adieu, and take with me the profits of your time, your budget, and your sanity, in that order. Farewell!
