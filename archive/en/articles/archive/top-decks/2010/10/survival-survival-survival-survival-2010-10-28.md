Survival Survival Survival Survival
===================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on October 28, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






A few months ago I got to [preview](http://www.wizards.com/magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/td/97) a little two-drop by the name of [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), a bang-up creature quite reminiscent of a little two-mana enchantment by the name of [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest); I called the preview article "Survival" for obvious reasons. Well this article is *four times* that article's, you know ... title.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td114_survival.jpg)  
So what inspired this unusual naming?

It was the results of the Star City Games Legacy Open in Nashville, TN a couple of weeks back; I was actually really enamored of the Legacy results this time and wanted to write about the decks last week ... But "[How to Think About **Magic**](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/how-think-about-magic-2010-10-21)" happened instead.

![Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Survival+of+the+Fittest)  
I rarely pay close attention to the Star City Legacy results because Legacy only has Grand Prix or other Premier Event support a couple of times per year, but this time I just couldn't help myself. In this Top 8 there were four [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) decks. All different ... and some of them colossally cool!

Let's see what evolution is bringing us:

Jessie Butler's tournament winner is probably the most straightforward of the four. We have certainly seen straightforward [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) aggro decks in Legacy in the past, but Jessie's is *even more* simple and easy to follow! This is literally a stack of good green and white creatures, a little removal, and three pieces of Equipment—all laced together with [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest).

![Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stoneforge+Mystic)  
**1 [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic):** In this deck, you can use [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) to find [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), kind of like how you use [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) to find [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) in a Standard deck. Jessie has his pick of [Sword of Fire and Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice) or one of two copies of [Umezawa's JItte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+JItte) (one in the board!) in this deck. The right Equipment can, of course, make even the most humble one-drop into a one-creature army ... and the classics are some good Equipment.

| Basking Rootwalla | Vengevine |

**2 [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), 4 [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine):** One of the baseline strategies for this deck is to dump a ton of [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s into the graveyard, then use the [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla)s to get them back for free. A handful of green mana will transform an otherwise empty battlefield into a slaughterhouse between [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) and these six creatures; no, you don't have to go all-out if you don't want to.

![Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wonder)  
**1 [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder):** Because that [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island) is really going to ruin somebody's day.

![Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aven+Mindcensor)  
**1 [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor):** Because this particular singleton, in a deck based around searching through and shuffling your library, is comical. And might just prove effective against decks that need to search through and shuffle their libraries for basic operation. 

![Faerie Macabre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Faerie+Macabre)

Zach Strait's green-blue deck is probably more recognizable to most readers as the Legacy beatdown [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) deck. This strategy has its roots in the *Odyssey* Block, when [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) was discarding [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) and [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder+) ... But co-mingled with [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) in a way only Legacy can accomplish.

![Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Memnite)  
As with Jessie Butler's deck, we have a [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) + [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) engine. But unlike Jessie's deck, we have the full compliment of [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla)s, and even [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite), as another free creature to help set up [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) (not unlike [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) + [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) in Standard). The full four [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla)s makes more sense in this deck, as with [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) and [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel), they get more action than they would in the Green-White [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) variation.

Speaking of which ...

**3 [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba), 3 [Wild Mongrel:](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel%3A)**That isn't right, is it?

When was the last time you saw that?

Four [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) is the default before we, at least historically, have seen respect for [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) or other madness outlet creatures. 

So what gives? Why promote [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) to three copies when [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) is only at three copies?

![Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Force+of+Will)  
The simple answer? Aquamoeba is blue. You need blue cards to be able to discard [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) in the first couple of turns. [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), and to a lesser extend the other countermagic in this deck, allow for meaningful interaction with combo decks.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Noble Hierarch | Trygon Predator |

**4 [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), 3 [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator):** [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) is just the best mana acceleration creature in this kind of a deck. It's a one-drop that produces both of the colors that the deck needs. But unlike [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) can actually contribute some damage (and in multiples lots of damage), but the flying is less relevant because we have [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder).

First turn [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) is of course the first step to second turn [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator). [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator) is an awesome card in a format where [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) and [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) are some of the key cards, along with [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial), countless affinity artifacts (allegedly) ... and of course, the topic of today's article, *enchantment* [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest). [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator) is kind of like the angry, raging warmonger version of [Ophidian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ophidian).

You'll notice that both of the [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) decks we have looked at so far play [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte); in fact, despite the strength of many of the cards in these decks, they rely heavily on [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) for punch and creature elimination. [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator) can tie up an [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) very well. With two counters on it, a [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Umezawa%2527s%2BJitte) is not "big" enough to take out a Predator, but both counters per turn (assuming the opponent can keep putting counters on it) are required to keep the Predator from eating the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Umezawa%2527s%2BJitte) to death.

![Llawan, Cephalid Empress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Llawan%2C+Cephalid+Empress)  
**1 [Llawan, Cephalid Empress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llawan%2C+Cephalid+Empress):** This *Odyssey* Legend is just a great one-of bullet in a strategy that can find any creature in the deck at will. Not quite a complete lockdown against a deck like Merfolk (they still have [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) to get around [Llawan, Cephalid Empress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Llawan%252C%2BCephalid%2BEmpress)), it is still a tremendous threat that can really help control the tempo of a game.

### Question Marks

One thing you probably noticed moving from the Green-White to the Green-Blue [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) decks is the *lack* of a lot of staple-type stuff. For example, the green-white version played [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) whereas Strait's green-blue deck has precious little on-battlefield creature control.

At an even more basic level, Butler played [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)—basically the Cadillac of offensive two-drops; the green-blue deck? Not so much.

| --- | --- |
| Tarmogoyf | Brainstorm |

How about this one: The Green-Blue [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) deck *doesn't play [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)*. If it were up to me I would try to find a spot to play [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), because not only does it improve your opening hands (greater chance of drawing [Survival of the Fiittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fiittest), plus the ability to hide and shuffle away excess copies), but it serves every much as the go-to for [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) companion in an otherwise blue-thin deck as [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba).

Cai's deck has a little something extra.

While it is fundamentally quite similar to champion Jessie Butler's deck, Adam Cai (along with a number of other small changes) plays two cards that really set his build of Survival off:

> * [Iona, Shield of Emeria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iona%2C+Shield+of+Emeria)
> 

It's pretty simple, really. You can use [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) (or in this deck, [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman)) to find and then dump [Iona, Shield of Emeria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iona%2C+Shield+of+Emeria) into the graveyard. Then (and possibly part of the same sequence) you can find [Loyal Retainers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Retainers). [Loyal Retainers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Retainers) is like a three mana [Zombify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify) that you can find with [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest).

[Loyal Retainers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Retainers) does his thing (dying) and you get a relatively inexpensive 7/7 mythic rare Angel that can lock down entire decks just by *being*. I guess that's why she costs nine! 

... Except in this deck!

Just another (again, *in this deck*) three to four mana super threat that is next to impossible to target and kill, and represents a gigantic victory in the face of R&D's trying to correctly cost cards.

Now most of what we have seen so far would have been possible without *Scars of Mirrodin*. But that's not necessarily surprising—Legacy presents an opportunity to sleeve up the old cards, for Green-Blue Madness fans to hook up with [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest). For players to remind themselves just how much they liked cards like [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) or [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte).

But the cool thing about Marmulstein's deck is that it would be, at least in part, impossible without a new card from *Scars of Mirrodin*.

![Necrotic Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Necrotic+Ooze)  
[Necrotic Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Ooze) has the superpower to copy the abilities of any kind of creature that is in your graveyard. What kinds of creatures of ill repute hang out in graveyards, anyway?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td114_ooze.jpg)  
Well of course there is [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), the card that makes it impossible for the opponent to block. And there is [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), waiting to be broken by [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla). That is a card that really gets along well with the graveyard. [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) sends creatures to the graveyard as this deck's disruptive element, and the presence of [Necrotic Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Ooze) makes black mana a must; so it isn't surprising that [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) (as we may have seen in the green-blue version) is replaced here by [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise).

> 

[Phyrexian Devourer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Devourer) ... Now there is a card that you probably had to look up (I did). 

Why does this matter?

There is the matter of that other artifact creature, of course!

[Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) allows you to remove a +1/+1 counter to shoot target creature or player for one point of damage. That makes this a full-on combo deck! If you get [Necrotic Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Ooze) in play, you can set up the combination of both [Phyrexian Devourer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Devourer) and [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) in the graveyard to accumulate +1/+1 counters (over the course of a few uses, twenty, for no mana) ... and then do with those what you will—using [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion)'s ability to convert +1/+1 counters into damage.

The presence of this combination makes Rion Marmulstein's version of Survival the most interesting of the four, to my mind at least. But the coolness doesn't stop in the main deck!

When you really, really have to race; race so fast that a [Necrotic Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Ooze) auto-win is too slow for you, you can try [Natural Order](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order) for [Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus).



|  |  |

| Natural Order | Progenitus |

Ta da! 

With [Natural Order](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order), this deck has basically the same incentive as the top end of resolved [Hypergenesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypergenesis) in last year's Extended Pro Tour Qualifiers.

Pretty cool, huh?

There you have it: From the most recent Star City Games Legacy Open tournament, a variety of very different decks, all in the Top 8, all based around one classic enchantment.

The *Mirrodin Besieged* prizes will go to players who have factionalized their decks. The Top 2 players with Mirran-affiliated decks will receive a Peace Strider, and the Top 2 Phyrexian-affiliated players a Pierce Strider.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab114_mbsCards.jpg)  
*Factions? Huh?*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab114_symbols.jpg)  
A deck is considered factionalized if it has at least TEN cards with one watermark (Mirran or Phyrexian) and ZERO cards with the opposing watermark. Factionalizing is actually pretty easy, so it might take only minor tweaking to properly affiliate the deck you want to play with. You can search watermarks [here](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Advanced.aspx). 

... But I'll make it easy:


> * 184 [Mirran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&mark=+%5Bmirran%5D) cards
> * 41 [Phyrexian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&mark=+%5B%22Phyrexian%22%5D) cards
> 

Just off the top of my head, if you want to play an infect deck, getting ten Phyrexian-affiliated cards into your deck is going to be almost automatic. You just want to make sure you don't accidentally add one of those pesky Mirran watermarks. Note that cards that were printed previously and have now been reprinted bearing watermarks count toward your total of that watermark even if the actual card you're using doesn't physically have one. So [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) will help you get to your total number of Mirran watermarks, but no putting [Mindslaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) in your Phyrexian deck.

Going the other way isn't hard either. Heck, a White-Blue deck with four [Seachrome Coast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seachrome+Coast) and four [Ratchet Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) is a measly *two* cards off of a Mirran affiliation from the get-go!
