Breakfast of Champions
======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 3, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Yesterday, I wrote about Yuuya Watanabe and Jund in the 2012 [**Magic** Players Championship](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/playerschamp/2012/main). I indicated that wasn't the last we'd see of either the player or the deck, and, in fact, at the very next [Pro Tour](/en/events/coverage/cifka-savors-pro-tour-breakfast-champions), we almost witnessed a repeat performance by the same player and deck. Ultimately, though, he was defeated in the finals by Stanislav Cifka playing a [Second Sunrise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise)–based combo deck with a hotly contested name. 

![Faith's Reward](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Faith%27s+Reward) The deck is an evolution of a much older deck called Eggs that uses a cycle of artifacts called "eggs" (since they have names like [Skycloud Egg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skycloud+Egg)). The eggs are artifacts that cost one mana to play and can be sacrificed to turn two colorless mana into two colored mana of a specific combination and then draw a card. This has led to any cheap artifact that can be "broken" to draw a card occasionally being called an "egg." 

 The Egg Combo deck (also called [Second Sunrise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) Combo or Second Breakfast) builds up a board full of "eggs" like [Chromatic Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere), [Chromatic Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star), and [Conjurer's Bauble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conjurer%27s+Bauble) before beginning a big turn in which the player sacrifices all of them, and anything else in play, to draw more cards and generate whatever effects happen to be available before playing a [Second Sunrise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise)... which returns them all to play, letting the player sacrifice them all again. [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) can be sacrificed for three mana, which is enough to cast [Second Sunrise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise), and the eggs draw you to more copies of [Second Sunrise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) or its sister, [Faith's Reward](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Reward). After reiterating this loop several times, the opponent will usually fall asleep, at which point the [Second Sunrise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) player can declare him- or herself the victor. (Failing that, it's possible to actually deal lethal damage by reusing [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb).)
