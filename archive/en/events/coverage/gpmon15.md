TOP 8 COVERAGE



Mike Sigrist delivered on the admiration and respect of his fellow players by adding another Grand Prix title to his resume in Montreal. Defeating David Goldfarb, a 2014 World Magic Cup competitor for Canada, in the finals Sigrist stood last in a Top 8 where he was joined by Pro Tour Hall of Fame player Jelger Wiegersma and top-ranked Reid Duke.


*Dragons of Tarkir* has run its Premier Play course as Montreal served to sunset the high-flying format but that wasn’t the only action of the weekend. Among the 1,220 players that arrived, the Grand Prix Pro Points race for a World Championship slot started Saturday in a tie between Alexander Hayne and Pascal Maynard. It quickly shifted as Maynard fell in Day 1 and Hayne rattled off an impressive series of wins to close out Sunday with a record of 12-3, good enough to put a three point lead behind him.


All eyes look forward to *Magic Origins* and Grand Prix Dallas where their race, and plenty of new *Magic* cards, will set the stage for the last Pro Tour for Worlds – and the world will be watching.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



The City of Saints started with 1,220 players attending Grand Prix Montreal, highlighted by the latest stage of the Pascal Maynard and No. 24-ranked Alexander Hayne race for a World Championship slot.


At the end of the first day the landscape changed. 143 players will return, Maynard not among them, with four undefeated leading the way: Jason Ellis, No 20-ranked Andrew Cuneo, Stephen Whelan, and Martin-Eric Gauthier.


*Dragons of Tarkir* Sealed Deck sunset with just another day of Draft left in its Premier Play journey. With the aforementioned Hayne, No. 7-ranked Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir winner* Ari Lax, Pro Tour Hall of Fame player and Pro Tour *Fate Reforged* Top 8 contender Jelger Wiegersma, No. 19-ranked Reid Duke and others still in the running Montreal will continue to be a skillful showdown of a mature format.
