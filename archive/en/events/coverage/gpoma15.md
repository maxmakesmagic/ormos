Peters' Pod Sowed Victory In Omaha!
===================================




Through a Top 8 field of slippery Merfolk, flying Aberrations, and turn-one Titans, Erik Peters and his Birthing Pod chained wins together at Grand Prix Omaha to take home the trophy!

TOP 8 COVERAGE



Erik Peters used his trusty Birthing Pod and company to claim victory at Grand Prix Omaha! Beating a different archetype in each round of the Top 8, he built his wins from the ground up, culminating in an unexpected victory over Stephen Speck's Amulet Bloom combo in the finals.


Coming into the weekend the story was how many copies of Treasure Cruise would make it to the end, but the total count here was "zero." There were only four in the whole semifinals. How this will shape the metagame for Pro Tour Fate Reforged in Washington, D.C.—only time will tell.


Birthing Pod was the deck to beat all weekend, with many famous pilots throughout, but it's a fitting end for a hometown boy like Peters to pilot the Pod to the end of the tournament.


The Pro Tour is coming soon, but we have so much more Magic to play before then. Both Grand Prix San Jose and Grand Prix Mexico City are coming up the weekend after the Fate Reforged Prerelease, just a few short days away. Farewell from Omaha!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



After a full weekend of spell-slinging, this huge Modern field has been whittled down from 1,139 to 8. Your Top 8 is Pascal Maynard, Erik Peters, Stephen Speck, Jonathan Paton, Zac Roorda, Scott Lipp, Andrew Elenbogen, and Stephen Berrios. Outside this arena, everyone is looking at these decks and players to see what the metagame looks like going into Pro Tour Fate Reforged in Washington, DC. Is Treasure Cruise omnipresent? What about Birthing Pod? At this point, maybe we should start asking about Amulet of Vigor?!


But inside this Top 8, there's only one thing on everyone's minds: Only one person can be crowned the winner. And with three more rounds of player, we'll figure out who that is.


Let the games begin.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



After nine grueling rounds, we have completed the first day of Grand Prix Omaha! There are four players left undefeated navigating these treacherous Modern waters. Modern Grand Prix winner Josh McClain, Four-time Grand Prix Top 8 finisher Pascal Maynard, Grand Prix San Antonio Top 8 finisher Randall Gay, and Missouri store owner Scott Lipp are all sitting at the top of the heap at 9-0. Each undefeated player is on a different deck—Blue-Red Delver, Birthing Pod, Abzan Midrange, and Red-Green BreachScape.


And below those top finishers, there are some big names below them. No. 4 Owen Turtenwald, No. 8 Shahar Shenhar, Matt Sperling, Valentin Mackl, Patrick Dickmann, Brian Braun-Duin, Eugene Hwang, and Amulet Bloom pilot Matthias Hunt must all brave the Nebraska cold to come back tomorrow.


This Modern format has shaped up to be quite crazy indeed. It’s changed a lot since the release of *Khans of Tarkir*, but it’s just as vibrant as ever. Tune in tomorrow to see which decks can pull through and enshrine themselves in history’s halls, before Pro Tour *Fate Reforged* comes in and mixes everything up.


Have a great night everyone! See you tomorrow.
