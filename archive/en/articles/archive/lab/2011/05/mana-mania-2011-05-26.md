Mana Mania
==========



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on May 26, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello, and welcome back to the Lab. This week I'm tackling the fuel behind the entire game of **Magic**, mana itself. There's a wide selection of our favorite intangible energy to choose from; the most commonly used are the five different colors and the pale "colorless" mana. Of course, since **Magic**'s an infinite chasm of mental activity, there are bound to be some oddballs in the mana family. My previous favorite of these was snow mana, introduced in *Coldsnap*. Appearing as a snowflake, this mana came only from snow permanents.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/products/coldsnap/snow.jpg)  
Snow mana intrigued me, but I was always sort of bothered that the snowflake symbol never made it into actual mana costs. As I've said many a time, I love weird-looking **Magic** cards, and such a card would definitely qualify. Five years later, *New Phyrexia* has come out with a new set of bizarre mana symbols, and this time around they went where snow mana had never been before. As the saying goes, one small step for mana, one giant leap for manakind. (So...in the physics of a **Magic** card, the converted mana cost is the moon to the text box's Earth? And the Phyrexians are Neil Armstrong? And snow mana represents the Russians? Actually, that fits quite nicely.)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/li/li144_pMana.jpg)  
Apparently I love weird tangents as well. Anything weird, really. Let's continue onward to this week's weirdness, based around the Phyrexian mana symbols and the cards that bear them. (Although that was a cool tangent. Now every time I see a crazy and unfathomable galactic presence in the cosmos, I'll think of [Reaper King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+King).)

### Mmmm... Souls

Ahh, paying 2 life. Phyrexian mana has brought it back into the mainstream, but like any quality trend, it has been in the spotlight before, in some iteration. The most popular instance of paying 2 life showed up on the ten dual lands of *Ravnica* block ([Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden), [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents), etc.) Historically the phrase has thrived in black card draw, as seen in cards like [Greed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greed) and personal favorite [Street Wraith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith).

Some Phyrexian-mana cards only bear the symbol in their mana costs, meaning you have the option to pay 2 life only when casting them. Others, though, have the symbol nestled in their text boxes. I'm talking specifically about the deceptively good Souleater cycle. At common, these guys showed up many a time during *New Phyrexia* Prereleases (at least, they did at mine.) [Blinding Souleater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Souleater) aside, the others can repeatedly suck 2 life out of you if necessary. Of course, making [Trespassing Souleater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trespassing+Souleater) unblockable over and over at the cost of lots of life seems incredibly redundant and silly...

...unless you're a reader named Joe D., who decided that doing so would be wonderful if you also had a [Soul Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Conduit) primed in the background. Pay ghastly amounts of life to the [Trespassing Souleater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trespassing+Souleater) (putting yourself ideally at 2 or 1), then activate the Conduit. Suddenly it's your opponent who has the low life total! And the Souleater is unblockable, so swing in for the surprise win.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Trespassing Souleater | Soul Conduit |

A lot of readers mentioned that I "forgot" about [Soul Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Conduit) in regards to my Melira, Sylvok Keeper + [Phyrexian Unlife](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Unlife) deck [a couple weeks ago](/en/articles/archive/lab/compleat-combos-2011-05-05). Perhaps I did. Or I was strategically saving the Conduit for this week. Regardless, another toy that Joe mentioned was the wonky [Wall of Shards](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Shards) to get your opponent's life total sky high. The 1/8 flying Wall for two can keep attackers at bay while you cast your [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate)s and other tutor spells to find [Soul Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Conduit). Even without [Trespassing Souleater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trespassing+Souleater), you can use this combo to acquire a ton of life.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Wall of Shards | Norn's Annex |

Playing [Soul Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Conduit) in the first place allows me to stuff the deck with fun Phyrexian-mana cards. [Norn's Annex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Norn%27s+Annex) and [Cathedral Membrane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Membrane) add extra defense while you set up the combo, and [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) is a perfectly acceptable [Peek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) for this deck. Spy on your opponent's hand and make sure he or she has nothing to stop you. Sounds like Phyrexia to me.

While we're on the subject of Phyrexian-mana cards, let's address the new crazy. [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) is unreal. It was nice casting you, [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) and [Sculpting Steel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sculpting+Steel), but this weird silvery ooze of tentacles has taken both of your places at the same time. The blue Phyrexian symbol only makes the Metamorph more versatile, being both a three-drop and a four-drop.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl144_metamorph.jpg)[Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) | Illustration by [Jana Schirmer & Johannes Voss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Jana+Schirmer%22%5D)

Reader Daniel Haas emailed me about an interesting three-card combination involving the Metamorph that many people (including me) seemed to miss:


> You need a [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph), [Leonin Relic-Warder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder), and a [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden)-type card (the new [Suture Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest) works fine).
> 
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Phyrexian Metamorph | Leonin Relic-Warder |


> 
> Here's how it works:
> 
> 
> Make sure you have the [Suture Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suture+Priest) and the Relic-Warder on the battlefield. Play the Metamorph copying the Warder. As it enters the battlefield, it is now an artifact creature Relic-Warder, so it will trigger its ability to exile an artifact as well as the Priest's lifegain trigger. Gain 1 life and make the Metamorph target himself. Then, when you let his trigger resolve, he will (just barely) cause his second ability to trigger to bring himself back to the battlefield. Rinse and repeat to gain N life, where N is any number of your choice.
> 
> 

I love subtle stack trickiness like this, so props to Dan for this neat combo! As he also mentioned, "The great part about this combo is that it doesn't even require cards that are terrible alone. All three cards are fine utility." Which is why I'm shoehorning this combo into the above deck skeleton. Lots of combos in one deck? Sounds fun to me! And I'll throw in a singleton of [Felidar Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Sovereign), too.

### It's All the Rage

Next, I'll take on the only card that truly begs to be built around with other Phyrexian-mana cards, [Rage Extractor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Extractor). Speaking as a huge fan of direct damage (when the mood is right), [Rage Extractor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Extractor) got me salivating. It didn't take me long to pair another eye-catching red Phyrexian card with the angry artifact: [Moltensteel Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moltensteel+Dragon). Certainly capable of winning the game by itself, the Dragon will prompt a blast of 6 from [Rage Extractor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Extractor).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Rage Extractor | Moltensteel Dragon |

What are other expensive cards that work here? [Slash Panther](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slash+Panther) effectively becomes a hasty 9/2 for four mana and 2 life. [Thundering Tanadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundering+Tanadon) is a beefy beatstick that comes with an additional 6 points of Rage damage. And [Act of Aggression](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Aggression) becomes even more aggressive, dealing 5 on its casting.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl144_extractor.jpg)[Rage Extractor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Extractor) | Illustration by [Raymond Swanland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Raymond+Swanland%22%5D)

Of course, with all this life payment, I should be careful and keep my life total at a reasonable number. There's lots of ways to accomplish this goalâ€”giving lifelink to one of my fatties, for instance. [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) is cheap and versatile. But a wackier route (and thus, a route I shall embark on) would be the underused [Tainted Sigil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Sigil) from *Alara Reborn*. Since you'll be paying lots of life and dealing lots of damage in the same turn, the Sigil could swing you a profit. And it's not necessarily a one-shot effect if I also have cards like [Remember the Fallen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remember+the+Fallen) bringing back my artifacts and creatures (like fallen [Rage Extractor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Extractor)s or [Moltensteel Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moltensteel+Dragon)s.) And you know what? I'm adding [Children of Korlis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Children+of+Korlis) too. C'mon kids, let's go play with the Phyrexians!

### More Birthing

I'm proud to own one of the first fancies that [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) tickled (through my [pseudo-preview of the wacky card](/en/articles/archive/lab/pod-power-2011-04-28)) but since *New Phyrexia*'s release I've gotten all sorts of fun emails from readers with their own Birthing ideas. As I mentioned previously on this subject, the Pod is ridiculously open-ended and slides into tons of strategies.

![Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Birthing+Pod)  
Nate Hannon happened to send me his take on [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and it's mostly genius. He used [Dross Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Scorpion) (one of the better combo enablers in all of **Magic**, and definitely the best Scorpion ever) as the Pod untapper of choice. From there, I hand the microphone over to Nate. Pay attention, though...


> Here's another way to go infinite with [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). It's a very complex combo, but all you need to start it is a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), a [Dross Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Scorpion), and a [Cathodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathodion) - and the [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) can find the other two pieces.
> 
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Dross Scorpion | Cathodion |


> 
> Use the [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) to sacrifice the [Cathodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathodion) and find a [Su-Chi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Su-Chi). Use the trigger from the [Dross Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Scorpion) to untap the Pod. Repeat this process to turn the [Su-Chi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Su-Chi) into any of the five-mana creatures in the deck, then into [Sharuum the Hegemon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharuum+the+Hegemon) (getting back [Cathodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathodion)), a [Myr Battlesphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Battlesphere), a [Filigree Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filigree+Angel), an [Inkwell Leviathan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkwell+Leviathan), and finally [Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek). Assuming you pay life for all of the Phyrexian mana costs, this costs a total of 7 mana and 14 life, but it gives you 7 mana and at least 21 life (thanks to [Filigree Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filigree+Angel) and the Myr tokens).
> 
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Sharuum the Hegemon | Myr Battlesphere |


> 
> Now, turn the [Cathodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathodion), which you retrieved with Sharuum, into a [Su-Chi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Su-Chi), then into a five-mana creature, Sharuum (getting back [Cathodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathodion) once again), and [Quicksilver Gargantuan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Gargantuan), copying [Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek). This costs 4 mana and 8 life, and yields 7 mana. The legend rule sends both [Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek)s to the graveyard, which then gets shuffled into your library. You have gained 3 mana and some Myr tokens. If one of the five-mana creatures was [Anodet Lurker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anodet+Lurker) or if you had another artifact on the battlefield (including a Myr token from a previous iteration), you have also gained life. Repeat for an arbitrarily large amount of mana and Myr tokens.
> 
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Kozilek, Butcher of Truth | Quicksilver Gargantuan |


> 
> To finish the combo, stop the first chain at [Myr Battlesphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Battlesphere), then stop the second at [Golem Artisan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golem+Artisan). Give the Battlesphere haste and attack for as many points of damage as you have Myr tokens. The [Academy Ruins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins) and [Patchwork Gnomes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patchwork+Gnomes) can help if you draw too many copies of one of the creatures.
