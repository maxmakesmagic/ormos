Top Stories of Grand Prix Lyon 2018
===================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 18, 2018 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











Earlier on Saturday morning, 2,058 players arrived in Lyon to play in the main event—the last major Modern event before [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) would become legal. In the end, it was Grzegorz Kowalski who was triumphant with his innovative Red-Green Eldrazi deck, defeating No. 20 Márcio Carvalho and his Abzan deck in the finals.


Over the course of the weekend, we saw plenty of sweet decks, but in the end the Top 8 was dominated by the color green. David Juric on Jeskai Control was the only non-green player in the Top 8; all others ran either [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) or [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings).


The following are my picks for things to remember from this Grand Prix.


FOUR TRON DECKS IN THE TOP 8
============================


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Urza%27s+Mine)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Urza%27s+Tower)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower)
The name "Tron" refers to assembling all three Urza lands, originally dubbed as the "Urzatron". When [Urza's Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower), Urza’s Power Plant, and Urza’s Mine are all on the battlefield together, they tap for extra mana, allowing [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) to be cast as early as turn three.


And Karn came down quite frequently this tournament, as half of the players in the Top 8 reached the elimination rounds with Mono-Green Tron, relying on [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings) and [Sylvan Scrying](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Scrying) for consistency. Although a black splash for [Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push) and [Collective Brutality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Brutality) was popular at Pro Tour *Rivals of Ixalan* two weeks ago, this weekend the competitors were more afraid of [Field of Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Field+of+Ruin) and put their faith in basic Forests.


Tron's performance makes sense because of its solid matchups against Five-Color Humans and Lantern Control—the two breakout decks from Pro Tour *Rivals of Ixalan*. Given that these decks were on the rise, Tron seemed to be well-positioned this weekend, and the deck choice paid off for Davide Cappiello, Joshua Bausch, Julien Stihle, and Matic Penko.


Yet despite the high number of Tron decks in the Top 8, the archetype did not dominate the event as a whole. There were only two Mono-Green Tron decks among the 9-32 place finishers. What's more, there was no Tron deck in the finals.


FUN FOR EVERYONE
================

The crowds that artist Magali Villeneuve drew this weekend were out of this world, reminiscent of Kaladesh's [Inventors' Fair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inventors%27+Fair). The Grand Prix Lyon playmat featured her beautiful art for the *Rivals of Ixalan* [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), and fans were queuing up all weekend for her autograph, alters, and sketches. In fact, after the finals completed and the entire feature match had been packed up, she was *still* signing!


Fortunately, she was not alone. From Friday to Sunday, she received some help at the artist booth from [Chandra, Torch of Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance), one of the many *Magic* cards that Villeneuve so beautifully illustrated and cosplayed this weekend by Manju. We're hoping to see both of them at many future GPs to come!


SCHULTE'S SOULFLAYER SURPRISE
=============================


Modern had always been a brewer's paradise, and the turnout of wacky designs didn't disappoint at this event either. The one which likely drew the most attention and raised the most eyebrows was Andreas Schulte's [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer) Surprise, which remained undefeated up until Round 7.


* [![Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Soulflayer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer)
* [![Zetalpa, Primal Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Zetalpa%2C+Primal+Dawn)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zetalpa%2C+Primal+Dawn)
* [![Spark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spark+Elemental)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spark+Elemental)
* [![Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sylvan+Caryatid)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid)

[Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer) had made a new friend with the recent release of *Rivals of Ixalan*. When asked what he thought upon seeing [Zetalpa, Primal Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zetalpa%2C+Primal+Dawn) for the first time, Schulte said, "I knew I would put it in my deck. It's such a good card; I had to put it in as a four-of."


Here's what it looks like to win a game with your usual everyday hasty, flying, vigilant, double striking, trampling, hexproof [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer):


<https://clips.twitch.tv/embed?clip=CarelessProtectiveSnoodBrainSlug&autoplay=false&tt_medium=clips_embed>


One of the neatest things about Schulte's deck was that [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer) was hardly ever impacted by things like [Nihil Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) or [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction). After resolving [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) or [Commune with the Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+the+Gods), Schulte would have priority first and could pay delve costs before the opponent had a chance to intervene.


"Multiple times on Saturday, players were like, 'Before you do that, I want to remove your graveyard,' and I had to tell them that that's not how it works," Schulte recounted. "There were a bunch of judge calls, and at least one opponent basically rage-quit when he received the ruling. A lot of fun really."


[Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) or [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) could still throw a wrench into the deck's main plan, but no need to worry. [Drogskol Reaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Reaver) was supposed to be put into the graveyard to grant [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer) flying, double strike, and lifelink, and [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) was supposed to add hexproof to that. However, in a pinch, these creatures could also be summoned, and [Drogskol Reaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Reaver) could close a game just as well ...


<https://clips.twitch.tv/embed?clip=FaintSpoopyRaccoonDancingBaby&autoplay=false&tt_medium=clips_embed>


We wondered how often Schulte won by hardcasting [Drogskol Reaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Reaver)? "I never did it before," he said after his Round 9 feature match. Well, he saved the best for the stream.


Schulte also admitted that his list wasn't anywhere close to optimal yet: "For instance, [Spark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spark+Elemental) was fine as a 1-drop with haste, but only dying at end of turn was a problem several times. [Fanatical Firebrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fanatical+Firebrand) would have been much better in its place. There's still a lot of room for improvement here."

"I don't think it's well positioned, but I love it," Niklas Kronberger said. The goal of his deck is to get [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) and a mana creature onto the battlefield as quickly as possible, and then repeatedly untap it while churning through his deck with cheap spells. Eventually, a 20-power [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) or [Fatestitcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatestitcher) can attack for the win.


Kronberger finished the tournament with a respectable 11-4 record, and it was nice to see him come prepared for the tournament with custom-made "tokens" for the various [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) triggers. The tokens made it easy to visualize the stack, which was nice for his opponents, for himself, and for the people watching his Round 10 feature match.

Although we didn't get to see Thomas Riedo on camera, his list is a spicy one. If you play [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) and search for only two cards, which is a legal play, then your opponent has to put both into the graveyard. This means that fetching [Ashen Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashen+Rider) and [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) will work out nicely for you.


[Bring to Light](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bring+to+Light) can act as [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) number five through eight. And with a large amount of mana acceleration, his deck was capable of turn-three Gifts with startling consistency. Riedo finished in 31st place with a 12-3 record.

KOWALSKI TAKES THE CROWN WITH RED-GREEN ELDRAZI
===============================================


Polish Gold level pro Grzegorz Kowalski, in his fifth Grand Prix Top 8, took the trophy this weekend, and with a spicy deck to boot!


While most of the players running [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer)s and [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher)s had them in colorless Eldrazi Tron shells, Kowalski used them in a red-green build without the Tron set. Instead, he relied on [Karplusan Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karplusan+Forest) and [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) to cast his colorless Eldrazi, and he supported them with [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


You can learn more about his deck and the power of [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) and [Eldrazi Obligator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Obligator) in his [Deck Tech](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptrix/deck-tech-rg-eldrazi-grzegorz-kowalski-2018-02-03) from Pro Tour *Rivals of Ixalan*, where he unveiled the deck to the world and promptly piloted it to a 7-3 record in Modern. That was already an impressive result, but this weekend he truly showed the power of the deck.

Compared to his Pro Tour build, he cut an [Ulvenwald Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Tracker) for the second [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) in the sideboard. Yet he kept the other 74 cards intact, most notably the singleton [Forked Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forked+Bolt) that he said was his MVP all weekend.


In the Top 8, Kowalski put on a clinic in how to play the deck. He started by defeating Davide Cappiello's Mono-Green Tron in the quarterfinals. Given that his deck featured colorless threats that can't be exiled by [Ugin, the Spirit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ugin%2C+the+Spirit+Dragon), along with [Eldrazi Obligator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Obligator) to steal their massive creatures, the matchup looked to be in his favor.


He then smashed another Tron deck in his semifinals against Julien Stihle, reminding everyone that you can indeed select [Crumble to Dust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumble+to+Dust) with [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings). Yup, it's colorless! He chose the card with [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings) and proceeded to exile his opponent's hopes of ever assembling the Tron.


And in his finals against No. 20 Márcio Carvalho, he beautifully navigated tight damage races that were made even more complicated by the potential of [Eldrazi Obligator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Obligator) and the presence of [Kessig Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf) run. The finals went to three games, but in the third his [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher)s showed up in droves. Over the course of three consecutive turns, he chained three [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher)s, made wise attacks in the face of [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), and won the tournament with a deck that almost no one saw coming.
