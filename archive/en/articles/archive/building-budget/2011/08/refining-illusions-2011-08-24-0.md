Refining Illusions
==================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on August 24, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






[L](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=L)ast week's Eggs primer was a huge success. Modern is shaping up to be one of the most exciting formats we've seen in quite some time. This week, I'm going to take a break from Modern brewing to offer up an updated version of my Standard Illusions deck from a few weeks ago. A lot of you are planning to attend [Grand Prix Pittsburgh](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtg/event/grandprix/pittsburgh11) this coming weekend, and you've asked me to write about Standard this week.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab157_lordOfUnreal.jpg)  
I knew the Illusions deck I had posted was strong, but I had no idea it would become such a cult phenomenon in such a short period of time. In fact, it took me entering three separate two-player Standard tournaments on **Magic Online** to finally find an opponent who wasn't also battling with Illusions. The deck has acquired some new tools in the past few weeks, and I'd like to start by sharing some of the latest incarnations that have done well at recent tournaments.

Thomas Carter went 7-1-1 with Illusions in a large Standard tournament recently. Let's take a look at his list:

The biggest innovation here is the inclusion of [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) and [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). My version could only cast [Phantasmal Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Bear) on the first turn. This meant that I wasn't doing anything on my first turn a pretty large portion of the time. Playing with [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) also encourages you to play with more two-ofs. The current Standard format tosses a lot of problems at you, and it sometimes seems impossible to answer all of these problems with just sixty cards. By playing a broad swath of two-ofs, you give your deck a lot of versatility. Cards like [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) will help you draw the right mix of cards to defeat any opponent.

I'd like to talk about [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) while we're on the subject of cantrips. Thomas Carter's list plays three copies of the "free" spell. [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) would give Thomas perfect information on a key turn. For example, there would be no reason for Thomas to keep up [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) for his opponent's fourth turn if he were absolutely certain that his opponent didn't have a [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) in hand.

![Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gitaxian+Probe)  
[Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) is another huge innovation for this deck. It's so good that just about every list plays four copies. [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) is a lord for all blue creatures. It's a lot better than the [Adaptive Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton)s in my original list were. Thanks to [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect), you can make a lot of really powerful plays. Turn-three [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)s do very well against red decks. My personal favorite is the turn four [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) into [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph). You cast your [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect), tap it for two mana, tap a land, cast [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph), copy [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect), and suddenly your [Phantasmal Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Bear) and [Lord of the Unreal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Unreal) become a two-turn clock.

![Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grand+Architect)  
[Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) has been good for me, but it seems like everyone is casting [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph)s and [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image)s these days. It's difficult to gain an advantage in the Wurmcoil war. When a [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) gets copied it will normally result in both players gaining a bunch of life. This will prolong the game, which isn't the goal of the Illusions deck. I'm not saying Wurmcoil is bad in this deck—in fact, I think the ability to cast a Wurmcoil and repeatedly copy it is about as good a plan as you can have against some decks. I'll probably keep one of these in the main and move the other one to the sideboard. These are mythic rares, but they're fairly easy to come by. If you have trouble finding the normal version you should see if any of your friends still have their promotional copy from the Prerelease last September.

![Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wurmcoil+Engine)  
[Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) is an obvious inclusion once you add the [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect). The deck is basically a pile of lords. All the creatures make your other creatures bigger and more dangerous. One of the nice things about this deck is that you're rarely in a spot where you're terrified by your opponent's creature. An opponent might cast a Titan, but you can simply copy it and offer a race. This deck is very good at racing.

![Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian+Metamorph)  
Thomas's list also has two copies of [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas). These may seem a little random, but after playing a few matches with the deck I've actually grown to like them a lot. [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) is especially strong with [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe). It can cut players off of the correct configuration of mana they need to wipe your board. Once you cast the first [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas), your opponent will likely be playing catch-up on colors. If you wait until the third turn you can usually get a [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) off on one of their lands before they get to double white or black mana.

![Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spreading+Seas)

Griffin's list is much more straight-forward than Thomas's. Griffin chose to include a full four copies of [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), making it somewhat clear that the correct version of the deck plays at least four [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) and some number of [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab157_preordain.jpg)  
Griffin chose to include (Get ready to have your mind completely blown!) [Renegade Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Doppelganger) in his deck. [Renegade Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Doppelganger) kind of acts as a suspend [Ball Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning) in this deck. Usually, it gets in for 2, sits around for a bit, and eventually gets in for another 5 when you cast a Dragon. I don't hate the [Renegade Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Doppelganger), but I don't love it either.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab157_phantasmalDragon.jpg)  
My favorite part about Griffin's list is that he's still fighting with Dragons. I'm a big fan of [Phantasmal Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Dragon) and I was upset to see it cut from Thomas's list. The Dragon completely blanks every [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) in your opponent's deck, even when they're carrying [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) or [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace).

![Phantasmal Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phantasmal+Dragon)  
I'd like to update the Illusions archetype and make it more prepared for the expected metagame at Grand Prix Pittsburgh. Neither of these decks are playing [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), but [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo wasn't a very large factor when they did well. With [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) decks having such success at more recent tournaments I figured it's a good time to bring back the [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember)s to the sideboard.
