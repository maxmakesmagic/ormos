GP Sacramento 2018 Day One Wrap-Up
==================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on July 22, 2018 






![](https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Corey-Burkhart_50x50.jpg?itok=a5E5B4q0)
By Corey Burkhart











1,253 players made their way to Sacramento California to test their skills with the first weekend of *Core Set 2019* Sealed deck and draft. After eight rounds we cut to all players with eighteen points or more, which cut us down to 239 players. Being so late in the season many of the pros made their way to the west coast in their attempt to earn those last few pro points before the qualifications to the 2018 World Championship cutoff. I met up with some of them throughout the day.


Building a *Core Set 2019* Sealed Deck
======================================


Building a sealed deck from can be a difficult task. I walked through the process with Sam Black and (14) Martin Jůza this weekend to talk about how they approach this sealed deck format. Getting into the mind with two of the best limited players in the world and having them breakdown their thoughts and processes of how they would build a sealed pool. If you haven't checked those out, you can find Sam Black's [here](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsac18/sealed-deckbuilding-sam-black-2018-07-21) and Martin Jůza [here](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsac18/sealed-deckbuilding-martin-juza-2018-07-21).


The Undefeated
==============


Winning a match at the Grand Prix level is an accomplishment in its own rite. Starting off the first day with an undefeated record is another. These five players (Richard Liu, and John Walski at 8-0-1) have ended the day of *Core Set 2019* undefeated! I caught up with each of these players to discuss some of the keys to their success and what you can do to improve your *Core Set 2019* sealed deck game.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpsac18/2018_GPSAC-ThiagoSaporito_EOD.jpg)


*Thiago Saporito*


Best Card in deck: [Vampire Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Sovereign)


Most Important Thing for Sealed Deck of *Core Set 2019*: Focus on a good curve if you don't have the removal.

California Dreamer
==================


Before the day ended I caught up with some of the pros in attendance and asked them the important question on everyone's mind as you go to sleep tonight. If you can open any card in *Core Set 2019*, what card do you want to open. The answers may surprise you.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpsac18/2018_GPSAC-SteveRubin.jpg)


Steve Rubin – [Ajani, Adversary of Tyrants](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Adversary+of+Tyrants)  

"I haven't drafted (*Core Set 2019*) yet, but Ajani was really good for me in sealed deck."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpsac18/2018_GPSAC-MikeSigrist.jpg)


Mike Sigrist – Drayad [Greenseeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker)  

"It's my favorite card in the set, otherwise I'd choose [Patient Rebuilding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patient+Rebuilding)"


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpsac18/2018_GPSAC-AlexanderHayne.jpg)


Alexander Hayne – [Vivien Reid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivien+Reid)  

"I think Ajani and Vivien are really good, but I'd take [Vivien Reid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivien+Reid) over Ajani."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpsac18/2018_GPSAC-NealOliver.jpg)


Neal Oliver – [Banefire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire)  

"It's splashable, and it's just incredibly powerful."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpsac18/2018_GPSAC-JonStern.jpg)


Jon Stern – [Ajani, Adversary of Tyrants](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Adversary+of+Tyrants)  

"I could choose Nicol Bolas or Tezzeret because they're in my sealed deck, but I really want to open Ajani."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpsac18/2018_GPSAC-BenStark.jpg)


Ben Stark – [Banefire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire)  

"[Banefire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire) is flexible, it costs a single red, so it's splashable, it's not the most powerful card like one of the mythic rare dragons, but it's a card I'm confident when I first pick it, it will be in my deck."
