2009 World Championships Public Event Information
=================================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 3, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast












### Special Events


**Wednesday Events!**


The World Championship starts on Thursday, but public events will start Wednesday! Join us on Wednesday, November 18 for the start of public events. We will sign-up and run 8-player, single-elimination events from 14:00 to 20:00. All drafts will be **€ 5** and all constructed events will be **free!!** (while supplies last)


**Play the Game, See the World!**


3 Events in the Thursday through Saturday Public Events schedule are designated **"PLAY THE GAME, SEE THE WORLD"** events. The top 2 players from each of those events (top 2 teams from the 2HG event) are invited to play in the **PLAY THE GAME, SEE THE WORLD** Championship on Sunday at 9:00 AM. The winner of the **PLAY THE GAME, SEE THE WORLD** Championship receives paid airfare and hotel to the 3 Pro Tours and World Championship in 2010. For complete details, visit [http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/worlds09-ptgrules](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/worlds09-ptgrules).


**A Planeswalker's Journey through Worlds**  

"Do you rule?"


Join us for this fun activity at the World Championships! In order to participate, pick up your badge at the Wizards Information Booth or the Public Events area. The badge has xx achievements for you to try and accomplish during your weekend at Worlds.


When you fulfill one of the items, your badge will be stamped to show your achievement. Get as many stamps as you can and lord it over your friends!


### Weekend-Long Public Events


Weekend-long Public Events have no specific start time. Registration for these events is continuously open and each event will start once the appropriate number of players has registered (Subject to space and judge availability). They are available during the following hours:


Registration Opens: 9:00  

Registration Closes: 20:00 (18:00 on Sunday)


### Open Dueling


Players bring their own Standard-format deck. Players are then matched up against others for game play. After 5 games, players receive a foil, **Magic** promo card. After 10 games, players receive a second foil, **Magic** promo card. Come to the Open Dueling area and play whenever you have time! Play the whole weekend! Cost: FREE!


### Single-Elimination Events


Booster Draft, Sealed Deck, and 2HG Booster Drafts will be run using either *Zendikar* or *Magic 2010* boosters.


We will also offer "wacky" drafts throughout the weekend. "Wacky" drafts will consist of random boosters from the following blocks: *Zendikar, Shards of Alara, Shadowmoor, Lorwyn, and Time Spiral*.  

(Example: *Shadowmoor, Conflux, Future Sight* booster Draft)

### Elder Dragon Highlander (EDH)


Cost: € 5


Games will begin as soon as 4 players register. Players will play a single EDH game. Current EDH rules can be found at <http://csclub.uwaterloo.ca/~geduggan/EDH_rules.html>.


Each player will be given an "EDH Ticket". When a player eliminates another player, he takes the other player's ticket. Each ticket can be redeemed at the Public Events registration desk for 3 boosters of either *Zendikar* or *Magic 2010* (while supplies last, during registration hours.)


### *Planechase*


Cost: € 20


Games will begin as soon as 4 players register. Each player will receive a random *Planechase* Game Pack. Each player will be given a "*Planechase* Ticket". When a player eliminates another player, he takes the other player's ticket. Each ticket can be redeemed at the Public Events registration desk for 3 boosters of either *Zendikar* or *Magic 2010* (while supplies last, during registration hours.)


### Scheduled Public Events


The following applies to all scheduled Public Events:


* Unless otherwise indicated, all events are run Swiss with a Top 4, single elimination finals. Prizes are based on standings.
* Registration opens 1 hour before the scheduled start time and closes 15 minutes before the scheduled start time. Players should be seated at least 5 minutes before the scheduled start time.
* Booster pack prizes will be awarded based on attendance. Unless otherwise posted, top finishing players will receive current set boosters. Special events may offer special prizes as posted (i.e. iPod, Power 9, Digital Camera, etc), in such events the winner of the special prize will receive only that prize.

### Pro Tour Qualifiers


Qualifiers for Pro Tour tournaments award an invitation and a travel award to a specific Pro Tour tournament for the top-finishing competitor. For more information, visit [www.wizards.com/protour](/en/articles/archive/pro-tour%E2%80%93amsterdam-artists-2010-08-26)


### Grand Prix Trials


Trials for individual-format Grand Prix tournaments award a three-round bye to a Grand Prix tournament for one or more of the top-finishing competitors. The number of three-round byes awarded will be determined by the number of players in the Trial as shown in the table below. Grand Prix Trial byes do not pass down - if a player already possesses a three-round bye, finishes high enough to win another bye from a Grand Prix Trial, that bye will not be passed down to the next eligible player.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Number of Players | Number of 3 Round Byes |
| 8 – 32 | 1 |
| 33 – 64 | 2 |
| 64 – 128 | 4 |
| 129+ | 8 |

**\* Travel:**


The players eligible to receive a travel award will receive instructions from Wizards of the Coast on whom they must contact and the deadline by which they must arrange their travel awards. Wizards of the Coast will not refund travel made outside this service. Wizards of the Coast reserves the right to award a cash equivalent award in lieu of any travel award in its sole discretion.


All travel itineraries must be from the international airport nearest the player's home and to the host city of the applicable Pro Tour or World Championship or event, and must include all the dates of that event. No award will be substituted if member is unable to participate. All other expenses are the responsibility of the member and may include, without limitation, accommodations, ground transportation, taxes, itinerary change fees, passport and visa fees.


This award must be redeemed through member's arrangement of travel itinerary no later than (30) days before departure. Failure to complete travel arrangements as directed by Wizards of the Coast prior to this deadline will result in forfeiture of the award. Every member receiving a travel award must provide to Wizards of the Coast an executed liability release, the appropriate US taxation form (W9 or W8) (or have one on file), current contact information (<http://webapp.wizards.com/dcimember/>), proof of ID and passport (when required by international destination). In all cases, all such documentation must be provided to Wizards of the Coast, or previously on file, at least thirty (30) days before departure. Failure to timely provide such documentation will result in forfeiture of the award.


**All awards:**


Players must attend, compete (though not necessarily on the same team), and be listed as finishing in the applicable tournament for which he or she received the award. Failure to comply with these requirements will result in forfeiture of the award.


All taxes are the responsibility of the members, and members acknowledge Wizards' recommendation that they consult a tax advisor in their jurisdictions for questions regarding their tax liabilities.


Awards are subject to change without notice.
