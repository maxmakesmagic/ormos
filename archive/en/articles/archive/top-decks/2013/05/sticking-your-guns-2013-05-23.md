Sticking to Your Guns
=====================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 23, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







This past week in San Diego, California, popular [TCGPlayer.com](http://tcgplayer.com/) writer Craig Wescoe proved triumphant at [Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*](/en/events/coverage/ptdrm13). Just [last week](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/detour-2013-05-16), Top Decks was commenting on Wescoe's love of the White Weenie archetype... and at Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* he was able to knock out dozens of fellow pro players with small white (and green!) creatures laid out in front of him.


And for everyone else?


Here is how the Top 8 of Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* looked:

Craig joked during the Pro Tour interviews that the one card he contributed to this seventy-five was [Civic Saber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Saber)... but the rest was the kind of deck a longtime **Magic** reader might imagine Craig Wescoe playing.



![Civic Saber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Civic+Saber)

 

Wescoe's GW deck can start out quickly with [Dryad Militant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Militant)—a 2/1 that can drop on the first turn given any land but [Selesnya Guildgate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildgate)—but is steadier than it is speedy. This is a deck that not only has heavy hitters like [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) but a tenacity and relentlessness. [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) is hard to handle. Not only is it the quintessential **Magic** card (a 2/2 for two mana) but is also exceptionally resilient against removal and synergistic with a swarm.

[Call of the Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Conclave) plays [Watchwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf) while [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm) does, seemingly, "it all" (removing [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) from the table; going over the top of an opposing blocker; or giving Craig a Knight to start swinging). Both of these cards produce token creatures, which is, of course, highly synergistic with the populate mechanic. In this deck, that means extra capitalization on [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses). You can block to gain value, trade, or keep damage off—hopefully slaying many an opposing threat while keeping your side of the table alive and well. [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses) is also a kind of [Dismiss](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismiss) for [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). You might not be able to strictly counterspell [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), but [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses) certainly makes it feel like you can! You know, with extra value in the form of an additional token creature.


...and sometimes, that token creature is a huge 5/5 from [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm)! Not 100% "weenie," as is Wescoe's usual way of doing things, but awfully effective in any case. How great is it, as the weenie beatdown deck, to be completely comfortable with the opponent nabbing you with a two-for-one or three-for-one with a [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)? You kind of smile and wink and counter it with your [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses), or just shrug, let it hit, and make a 5/5 at the end of the opponent's turn? Either one is just fine. Swing in with your [Civic Saber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Saber)!

Craig's deck starts off steady and flexible, is clearly powerful, but can go completely over the top if you give it the chance. The creatures are already high quality, but if you want to get into a "fair fight" with them, you are going to feel the world is a bit unfair, I think, once the game goes to boards. [Druid's Deliverance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid%27s+Deliverance) is a [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses)—a populate catalyst—that punishes opposing aggression for over-committing. Five—not four, but five—copies of [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) (when you consider [Gift of Orzhova](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Orzhova) as the redundancy) make racing conventionally on the ground difficult at best. And then [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice)? My recommendation is to get it off the table as quickly as possible if you are the opposing beatdown deck.


Only you can't.


The games where Trostani is in, you might not have relevant removal *at all*. You have to close out quickly, because if you don't, you won't. Trostani is huge on the backside and keeps blockers coming forever; you know, while gaining unending amounts of life. Trostani is the top of the power curve for a sideboard strategy of lifegain and perpetual blocking/defense that can only be categorized as hell.



![Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice)

(As long as your opponent is not nothing-but-blockers-and-lifegain) This strategy seems like one of the most fundamentally sound and offensively effective available. Boros here comes out super fast. [Foundry Street Denizen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Street+Denizen), [Legion Loyalist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist), and [Rakdos Cackler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) all help this deck to start out with the initiative, and it just gets worse.


[Gore-House Chainwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker) is a kind of [Call of the Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Conclave)—a 3/2 on the second turn—but [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) is the two-drop you really want; it can, of course, often give you both its own 2/2 body as well as a [Gore-House Chainwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker). Once you have three creatures in play, battalion starts getting interesting. [Firefist Striker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firefist+Striker) can help keep blocking under control (or at least key blocking) and once you have [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) on the battlefield, you can just start to race. Get in with three or more creatures and they can all walk away from the fight to attack another day. The Boros guys are smaller and less resilient than the Selesnya ones, but when [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) is part of the equation, it is hard to imagine a battalion more brave.


The Boros Aggro deck is perhaps the most straightforward in this Top 8; but I would guess that many players have question marks around the presence (or not-presence) of two cards in particular:


* [Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden)
* [Boros Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite)

[Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden)... what the heck?!? There aren't even any green cards! Why take 2 damage? The secret is in the [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary). As we have said on multiple occasions, [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) makes for some of the most explosive draws. It does, however, cost *de facto* ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) in an RW deck. [Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden) here is a "Plains" that can help cast [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary). Tricky and non-intuitive (and sometimes seemingly unnecessarily painful); subtle, though, especially given the nature of the deck.


[Boros Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite)... is not here. What gives!?! This is a Boros deck, right? A battalion deck, even! Where is the mighty [Boros Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite)? This is actually another "[Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden)"-style issue. Utter-Leyton chose to weight his deck in the direction of red. He only played the three Plains in addition to his obvious Sacred Foundries and so not-obvious [Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden)s. Is [Boros Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite) a more, ahem, *elite* one-drop than some of the other choices? Probably. But not if Josh can't cast it consistently on the first turn! And starting on a Plains is poison for the potential explosiveness of [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) in any case.


That is how you have a Boros deck that doesn't have the Cadillac Boros one-drop, but does often start on one with the half-name "Rakdos."

Wow!


So... what is going on here?


This is a deck that has quite a few different themes going on, across every nonblue color.


This is a value deck. It is full of two-for-ones in [Gatecreeper Vine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) or [Sin Collector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector). While not two-for-one enters-the-battlefield in terms of *cards*, [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) continues this theme while also bridging to the next.

 |

[Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) is a great defensive blocker... and it is not alone. [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) has much the same vibe. And what about [Alms Beast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alms+Beast)? It's huge! And like Honey Badger, [Alms Beast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alms+Beast) just don't care. [Alms Beast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alms+Beast) is a huge monster that will just tangle with whomever. It is so big. Gain life? Sure, go ahead and gain life. Castellon's deck is built to keep going over the long haul.



![Alms Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Alms+Beast)

 

Expensive creatures—expensive windmill slams—like [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa), up to the seven-drop [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity), continue this theme. Castellon wants to play a long game where he has better cards over a high end.


Perhaps his most powerful high-end card is [Deadbridge Chant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadbridge+Chant).



![Deadbridge Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deadbridge+Chant)

 

[Deadbridge Chant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadbridge+Chant) puts the top ten cards into its controller's graveyard... which makes for a sort of Golgari [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). This not only becomes a route to multiple cards over time, some of which "merely" go to hand, some of which [Tinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker) into play (you know, the often-powerful and high-end creatures), not only make for a great synergy with [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) (either as a [Deadbridge Chant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadbridge+Chant) beneficiary or as a six-to-seven up the curve), but takes us to yet another theme: the graveyard!


[Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) is joined by [Deadbridge Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadbridge+Goliath), giving the deck a bit of a scavenge theme as well.

Everything to everyone, almost.

I wished to high heaven that Castellon *also* had a [Maze's End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze%27s+End) way to win, but in this deck it is merely a testament to his desire to play a long game, especially against other long-game decks. [Maze's End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze%27s+End) here is a sign a midrange shopkeeper puts into the window saying, "I am not going to miss any land drops... what about you?"

Andrejs Prost played the most straightforward of the true control decks: two colors only, four copies of [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), and four copies of [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict).


Everything about this deck is pretty straightforward; it is mostly four-ofs with little ambiguity. The only potential head-scratcher is [Renounce the Guilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds), which is just a [Diabolic Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Edict) most of the time (unless Prost has his own [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)s in, and he can play around this). [Renounce the Guilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) is obviously a foil to opposing Reckoners and quite a few other cards; it even gives Prost an instant-speed answer to a resolved [Sire of Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+Insanity) from Castellon's deck (you know, one of the worst possible scenarios for a WU deck).

Prost finishes games with [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling). [Morphling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morphling) all grown up, [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) is poised to be the next big finisher in Constructed **Magic**. You can already see [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) pop up in not only many of these Block Constructed control decks, but in Standard builds various. It is great at defense (huge blocker), next to impossible to kill once it resolves, relentless as an [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) on offense, and plays offense/defense like a [Serra Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel).


And before you cry "it costs six!" remember that [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) beats the tar out of a [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) given time, and is a heck of a lot faster than a [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard).

[Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) is a fast and flexible answer, and [Turn & Burn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%26+Burn) is a potential big play. But [Mugging](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mugging) out of the sideboard gives the deck unprecedented defensive deck speed for a WU build.


Shrout played only three [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)s but his red splash gave him access to [Thoughtflare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtflare) as a fast redundancy to five (and at five); but the really surprising (and I assume ultimately very effective) splash was [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return)!

Thanks one [Godless Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine)! Bigger thanks to [Prophetic Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism).


**Esper Control**


All of the PT *Dragon's Maze* Top 8 Esper decks had similar outlooks and many tools in common. All three played four copies of [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and (again) three or four copies of both [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) and [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), as well as three or four copies of [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%26+Away).

[Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%26+Away) can play [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), dealing with multiple creatures at once. One half can handle hexproof men, making it a dangerous answer even in Standard. The [Far](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far) side is synergistic with a player's own [Sin Collector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) or [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity), but even more effective against an opposing [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm) token or other token creature. To that end, [Dramatic Rescue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dramatic+Rescue) (played in the sideboard of several Top 8 decks) is a good answer to token creatures, and one that trades at value.


The three colors and wide potential card palette of *Return to Ravnica* Block allowed for the most variation in certain specific answers ([Devour Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh), say, versus [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) versus counterspells). Mihara, for instance, started [Psychic Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychic+Strike) but played only one [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate), while PT finalist Dustin Ochoa went with the full four copies of [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate).

Ahlberg didn't make Top 8, but his [Maze's End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze%27s+End) deck did produce a very solid Constructed record. For our purposes here during Gate Week, it is a pretty perfect way to finish an article!



![Maze's End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Maze%27s+End)

 

This deck obviously plays a ton of Gates—twenty, in fact—giving it some redundancy against land destruction. Ahlberg really went in on the Gates theme. His only main-deck creature was [Saruli Gatekeepers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saruli+Gatekeepers), a respectable 2/4 chosen to keep him alive while he searched for more and more Gates.



![Saruli Gatekeepers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Saruli+Gatekeepers)

 

The style of lands Ahlberg played allowed him to run every single color, so he could touch on best-of-breed answers from everywhere. He could sweep with [Merciless Eviction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merciless+Eviction) or [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), go one-for-one with [Mugging](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mugging), [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy), or [Warleader's Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix). He could draw cards not just with [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)... but [Urban Evolution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urban+Evolution)! And in this deck, [Urban Evolution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urban+Evolution) might be even saucier; for one, it is generally faster, but the concept is that you want to get a bunch of lands in play and [Urban Evolution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urban+Evolution) helps you do just that.



![Urban Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Urban+Evolution)

 

Most of the decks from the Top 8 have existing Standard analogues; even Castellon's four-color multi-themed beast has traits of Jund and Junk... but Ahlberg's Gates is something quite a bit different. If you want to brew up something really different for  [Friday Night **Magic**](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/fnm) ... I can't think of a better inspiration.


At least not during Gate Week.
