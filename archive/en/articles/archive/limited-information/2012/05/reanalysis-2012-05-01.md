Reanalysis
==========



![](http://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_SteveSadin.jpg?itok=irxMrP8k)

Steve Sadin




May 1, 2012
 








The *Avacyn Restored* Prerelease was this past weekend, and I learned that a lot of the assumptions I made during my first few passes over the [*Avacyn Restored* Card Image Gallery](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/article.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/avacynrestored/cig#) were completely off base.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/li/li193_kru.jpg)[Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) | Art by [Christopher Moeller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+[%22Christopher+Moeller%22])

My biggest miscalculation (that I've discovered so far) came when I was guessing the speed of the format. I expected the *Avacyn Restored* Sealed format to be relatively slow and bomb dependent—but over the course of the Prerelease (and in the days since) I discovered that decks tend to be a lot faster than I had anticipated they would be.

Green, red, and white all have quite a few good two-drops; blue is loaded with evasion creatures and cards that allow you to capitalize on an early advantage (like [Into the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Void)). But more importantly, there aren't that many spells that cost three to five that really punish players for filling up their decks with cheap, aggressive creatures.

So if you fall behind early in *Avacyn Restored* Sealed, it's very likely you will be dead before you are able to take over the game with your relatively powerful but expensive spells.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kruin+Striker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker)*Make sure you have a plan for the early game... or else!*

If you're playing in an *Avacyn Restored* release event this weekend, don't expect to be able to kick back and wait until your bombs can take over the game for you. There are going to be a lot of decks out there capable of killing you quickly, so make sure you can hold off that initial onslaught—or kill them first!

My Sealed!
I'll have more to share about the speed of the format once I've gotten a few dozen more games under my belt, but until then, let's take a look at the Sealed Pool I opened this past weekend.
