Round 10 Feature Match - Alexander Hayne (Melira Pod) vs. Ari Lax (Kiki-Pod)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*
Alexander Hayne and Ari Lax sat down, shuffled up, and then... spoke casually. Both players were open to discussion on their preparation for GP Toronto's Modern matches, both content and calm about their choices for the weekend. Given their records, it was clear why they were so relaxed. Both players finished 8-1 yesterday, with Hayne piloting Melira Pod and Lax piloting his own variation on Kiki-Pod.


With [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) serving both so well, and with both players having a strong familiarity with their respective decks, this upcoming Pod on Pod matchup should be an interesting one!


### Game 1


Lax won the die roll, took a look at his hand, and said, "This is a fine seven". Hayne nodded that he'd keep his hand as well. Lax led with [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into second-turn [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) . Hayne was content to go a little slower, playing a second-turn [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) .


Lax played a [Hallowed Fountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) untapped on the third turn, cast [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) , and he used it twice thanks to upgrading [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) into [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) (going to 7 all through his own cards). [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) became [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) , shutting down Hayne's [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) , who rebuilt with [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) .



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptor12/r10_lax.JPG)  
*You know someone is familiar with their deck and the matchup when they are willing to go to 7... all through self-inflicted life loss!*

Lax thought for a moment, breaking the pattern of fast play in the match, before finally deciding to use [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) for [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) (sacrificing [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) ). The Pridemage disposed of Hayne's [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) , and [Murder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) ous Recap attacked, putting Hayne to 13 (and marking the first damage dealt to another player in the match!).


Hayne played a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) of his own, going to 11 and then 9, in order to upgrade his [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) , which broke Lax's [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) . Lax, however, had another one in wait, going to 3 in order to play it and use it, sacrificing [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) for [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) . [Murder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) ous Recap persisted back into play, knocking Hayne down to 8, as Lax passed with the legendary Goblin untapped.


Hayne went to 5 in order to turn his newly cast Merlia, Sylvok Outcast into [Orzhov Pontiff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff) . Lax responded by making a copy of [Murder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) ous Recap, bringing Hayne to 4. When Hayne's only follow-up was [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) , Lax revealed [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) , copying [Orzhov Pontiff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff) and letting Lax Pod the newly copied Pontiff into [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) to go with his [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker). Hayne nodded that Lax had assembled the kill, and proceeded to the second game.

Sunday, 10:50 a.m. - Day 2 Metagame Breakdown
---------------------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen*
Jund is, far and away, the most played deck on Day 2. Let's get that out of the way.


It's no secret that Jund would be out in numbers at Grand Prix Toronto. Over the past few months the deck has clearly positioned itself as a reliable, always relevant player in the metagame, no matter what else is being played. Not only are old standbys like [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) always powerful, but new additions and innovations such as [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) have only added to the deck's capabilities.


So it comes as no surprise that Jund would make up nearly 25 percent of the Day 2 field in Toronto. So unsurprising we won't spend much time on it. Jund is good. Be prepared.

But after that, things get interesting.


Grand Prix Lyon, held last month, was [similarly dominated by Jund](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/coverage-grand-prix-lyon-day-2-2012-11-04), with 21 percent of the Day 2 decks piloting the reliable midrange concoction. But immediately on Jund's heels at that tournament were Robots and Infect, at 17 percent and 12 percent respectively.


This weekend, however, those decks have seen marked drop offs. Robots is hanging around with just 8 copies, or about 6 percent of the field. Meanwhile, just three Poison decks made Day 2, barely 2 percent of the decks here on Sunday.


So what took their place?




![Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Karn+Liberated)![Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle)


RG Tron and [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) clocked in at 12 percent and 7 percent, respectively, as the next two decks, up significantly from their levels at Lyon.


But why?


RG Tron is the interesting one, as it's a clear-cut second place, putting 16 players onto Day 2. Part of the reason is that, with Jund moving away from [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) , [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated) is more difficult to answer for Jund lists than previous incarnations. The second is that fast combo decksthe kind of lists that prey on Tronappear to be at an all-time low. Just four copies of Storm, three of poison and two of Eggs made Day 2, with a significant-but-still-low 9 Twin decks making the cut. All told, non- [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) , non-Pod combo decks comprise just 13.4 percent of the field on Day 2.

Unlike many here this weekend, he ignored [Chord of Calling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) due to its cost to play, and isn't playing [Commune with Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+Nature) either. The reason being that, in their place, he can simply fine-tune the creature line-up and find more foils for the right matchups.


"I just included small changes with this deck. I found the best deck that could combo with [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) , and then found that best deck that could kill without the combo. In the end, I chose the deck that has a harder time comboing off, but that had better one-ofs to find and one that could kill without [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) more often." In Lax's case, [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) gives him access to the best creatures for the right time.


But what if you don't have [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) ? What if you're just playing a lot of creatures that produce mana, or a lot of one-of cards that may not serve their purpose at the right time.


Well, that's where the [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) s come in.




![Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gavony+Township)

[Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) has proven itself to be one of the defining cards of this weekend's [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks.



"You can just have a bunch of [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) , some 2/2s, an [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) , and then you can just kill them with [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) , pumping them up," he explained. "Against one of my Jund opponents, I needed a way to kill them, and only had two [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) . But after two turns, I used [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) , and I was just attacking for 4 a few times.


In his previous feature match with Alexander Hayne, both players could not say enough good things about [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) in [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) . It turns [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into an evasive attacker. It gives you a sink for your mana when you don't have an active [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) . It's a land that lets you cast things while also giving you extra pseudo-spells. Heck, it even resets your persisted creatures, which is far from irrelevant in a deck such as this one.


With a solid understanding of his deck and the format, Ari Lax looks like he's in a great position to finish in the Top 8 by the end of today. Will his success continue, and will we see a lot more [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) action in the Top 8? It's certainly possible, and would not be surprising if it happens.

Sunday, 12:08 p.m. - Deck Tech: Hellkite Jund with Willy Edel
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen*
I know what you're thinking.


"Why are you doing a deck tech on Jund? Isn't that pretty much the best known deck in every format ever since the beginning of time? What's the point?"


If that is indeed what you're thinking, clearly you overlooked the "Hellkite" moniker in the title. This is not our daddy's Jund (you know, if your dad played Jund in the last month or so).


Willy Edel, the Brazilian Pro and current South American Pro Points leader, knows his way around Jund. He made the Top 8 of Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* with a version of Jund, and has been piloting it deftly ever since. But that doesn't mean he's stuck by the stock list. Not by a long shot.


First, the similarities. Edel is packing the full four of [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) , [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) , [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) . He has a pretty standard package of discard split between [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) . I guess his mana base is pretty similar too.


That's mostly where the similarities end.


Edel opted to trim his [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) s to just two in order to make room for another two drop that hasn't gotten much love in Modern: [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) . I'll let Edel explain.




![Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dark+Confidant)![Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=LOTUS+COBRA)

Two two-drops can be too much for many decks to handle.



"My bad matchups are all the combo decks. They can usually finish by like turn 2 or 4, so against those decks, Confidant is good, but not that great. I only want to draw a few cards. Instead, I wanted more speed to play [Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) turn 3 without just relying on [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) ," Edel said, noting that, in the mirror, [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) pretty much always dies, while [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) can enable some pretty explosive openings.


Edel also shied away from [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) , choosing instead to go bigger. Much bigger. Dragon bigger.


" [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) is very good right now," Edel said, as everyone who has played Standard in the last month can attest. "The way to beat the mirror is to be aggressive. It's not [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Olivia%2BVoldaren), it's not [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) ."


Edel also cited the [rise in](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/grand-prix-toronto-day-1-blog-2012-12-09) [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) as a huge plus in Hellkite's corner. With so many 1/1 Spirits flying around, especially in the ever present Jund mirror, Hellkite gives Edel a huge leg up in those matchups.


Speaking of [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) , Edel isn't as up on the card as other players are. [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) , he said, gives an opening for decks like Zoo to make a comeback (or, I suppose, like the Haunted Zoo making the rounds today), and is really only at its best against Poison and Affinity.


So instead of going all in on Sprits, Edel split his main deck and sideboard up with copies of [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) , two in the main with the rest in the board. [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) , he said, has also given him a leg up in the mirror, where they're especially good.




![Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lingering+Souls)![Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kitchen+Finks)

These threes are threats to a variety of decks.



As for Edel's sideboard, he's chosen flexibility over specific hate cards.


"I don't think you should play any narrow cards in this format. The format is too diverse," Edel said.


So every card in his sideboard is chosen to serve multiple purposes. For example, [Rakdos Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Charm) can be artifact hate against Affinity or [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) , or it can be a combo surprise against Twin or, well, Pod.


Edel says his only bad matchup is against RG Tron, which was also his only loss on Day 1. This is due, in part, because he declined to play the matchup specific [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) s or [Sowing Salt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt) s that are needed to make that matchup feasible.


"I pretty much concede against Tron," Edel said, adding, "I really like my deck, I think if I don't play against Tron, I'll be fine."



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptor12/Edel%20Jund.JPG)  
*Willy Edel likes his version of Jund, and [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) and [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) are two very big reasons why.*

He even considers himself favored against other Jund decks, calling the match "60/40" in his favor. He said his unique sideboard strategy (no, I'm not going to tell you what it is while he's in contention) is part of the reason it works.


The best advice Edel said he could give for other aspiring Jund mages out there was to stop thinking of the deck as a 50/50 deck, and to start innovating.


"People say Jund is 50/50, but it's not," Edel said. "It depends on how you build your deck."

Round 12 Feature Match - Alex Majlaton (Robots) vs. Sam Pardee (Melira Pod)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenbergo*
Alex Majlaton's Robots may have some explosive starts, but he's going up against the man from Berkeley who has been on a tear the whole weekend. Sam Pardee has piloted his Melira Pod to a perfect record for the past eleven rounds, putting him in a near lock for Top 8.


Will the Robots run over Pardee, or will the Melira Pod player pilot his way to continued perfection?


### Game 1


Pardee led with [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) , but Majlaton had a much speedier start with [Vault Skirge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) , two [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) s, and [Memnite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) . [Vault Skirge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) sent for one in the air, and [Steel Overseer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) came down next. Pardee, meanwhile, had [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) and [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) on his next two turns.


Majlaton continued his fast start with [Cranial Plating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) and a [Steel Overseer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) activation, putting Pardee to 7... which quickly became irrelevant, as Pardee had [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) with [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) and [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) . [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) continued sacrificing [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) , which persisted back into play without any -1/-1 counters thanks to Melira, gaining him approximately 900,000,000 life.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptor12/r12_pardee.JPG)  
*Sam Pardee demonstrates just how his Melira Pod deck wins games out of nowhere to his opponent.*

Majlaton drew and attempted to gain some life with [Vault Skirge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) , but when Pardee played [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) on the next turn, revealing [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) out of his deck, Majlaton picked up his Robots and moved to the second game.


Majlaton 0  Pardee 1


### Game 2


Majlaton came out of the gates explosively with a first-turn [Vault Skirge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) and [Mox Opal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) followed by a second-turn [Cranial Plating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) , equip, and attack to put Pardee to 15. Pardee, meanwhile had a first-turn [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) , and then pointed an [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) at the [Cranial Plating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) to stymy Majlaton's start. Pardee had [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) to recover some lost life on the third turn, going to 16. *by Blake Rasmussen*
Every tournament there's a deck that catches the eyes of the coverage crew and the audience at home. Sometimes those decks flame out in the late rounds, sometimes they cement themselves as part of the format's permanent metagame. Now deep in Day 2, Collin Morton's unique UW control deck is vying for that title in Toronto.


His opponent, Dean Bilz, has done some blitzing of his own. His nearly mono-Red burn deck has torn through unprepared opponents, running roughshod over opponent after opponent on the way to a 10-2 record. Though he was, admittedly, a little nervous about being in the feature match area.


"I don't want my ridiculous play mistakes documented," Bilz said, mostly joking. I think.


### Game 1


Bilz led with a suspended [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) , promising a potentially quick game. [Ash Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) sped things up as Morton was down to 15 before playing his second land. And when he fetched up an untapped [Hallowed Fountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) , that quickly became just 12.


[Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) held back [Ash Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) for a turn before falling to [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet) . [Hellspark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental) made that 6 life after Morton sought out another [Hallowed Fountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) . Tapped this time.


"You want to take two? No? Ok," Bilz deadpanned.


Detentions Sphere took out the [Ash Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) , but [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and the unearthed [Hellspark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental) were exactly enough to take the first game.


Bilz 1  Morton 0

Both players cheekily showed me their hands prior to starting, and Bilz's was pretty nice. control with Collin Morton
--------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen*
What if I told you that I had a deck for you? A deck that not just beat Jund, but pretty much crushed it. And what if I told you that deck could handle a good swath of the rest of the Modern metagame as well? And what if that deck was a true control deck that played a four mana Jace?


Is that something you might be interested in?


I thought so.


Enter Collin Morton of Sarnia, Ontario. After 13 rounds, Morton stands at 11-2, leaving trail of Jund players in his wake, including former player of the year Owen Turtenwald. And it all started with [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) .


"I asked myself 'how did I beat Jund back in the day?" He said. "I played [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) and [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) ."




![Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wall+of+Omens)![Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spreading+Seas)


Indeed, those were two key components to handling Jund's occasionally shaky mana base back when [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) was terrorizing Standard. With fetchlands and Ravnica duals, the mana is far better now than it was then, but that doesn't change the fact that it is a weakness that can be attackedespecially when you combine [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) with [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) , as Morton has.


Morton said his first major success at any tournament of note was when he played the old Super Friends deck at a Regionals tournament several years ago, and he wanted to recreate that deck for the modern Modern format. But, he noted, not for a second did he ever consider [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) in the sideboard.


"I just wanted to be the paper in the room for rock basically," Morton said of being basically pre-boarded against Jund.


The Seas and Edges have advantages in other matchups as well. They let him skip on direct hate for Tron decks, and he said he's even caught Mono Red decks with two lands in play but unable to cast any spells with their mono- [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) board.


But it's the combination of [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) and [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) that has really put the plan over the top, he said.


"It's been incredible. They play their land, I [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) , then they have to play more lands, then I get to tech edge," he said.


That very thing was how he beat Turtenwald as well. Turtenwald had been holding back lands to prevent [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) from getting active and cutting him off from a color, but when Morton played [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) , Turtenwald was left with no choice but to play right into the [Strip Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strip+Mine) Variant.


Those two centerpieces of the deck informed a number of other choices throughout the list. Morton skimped on [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) as much as he felt he could in order to keep from giving his opponents free lands. Instead, he turned to [Oust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oust) which, as it happens, can sometimes make mana screw more pronounced.


The two odd mana stones were a split because [Azorius Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet) is better against [Blood Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) and the [Talisman of Progress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talisman+of+Progress) is better at casting [Oust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oust) or [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) . He split the difference because he wasn't sure which was better.


His trio of Planeswalkers is also something to behold. Elspeth, [Knight Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Errant) is a long-forgotten gem of the format and, as far as I can tell, he's the only one playing a card that was formerly considered among the best in the format. [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) is also an original and new to the format. It has the bonus of shutting down [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) combos, but still loses to [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) and other [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) targets.




![Elspeth, Knight-Errant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant)

Remember me?



To that point, Morton does admit his combo matchups are pretty rough (his two losses were to [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) and Eggs). With no main deck counterspells or ways to force early pressure, decks like Storm and Twin can mostly ignore anything Morton does game one. However, those decks have become increasingly small portions of the metagame as the weekend has gone on.


He's happy, on the other hand, to play against Jund or anything creature based, including Robots, Poison and Mono Red. Considering the metagame has been, as Morton said, "all over the place," that seems like a good place to be.

Round 14 Feature Match - Orrin Beasley ([Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift)) vs. Ben Moir (Jund)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*
Orrin Beasley and Ben Moir enjoyed a little small talk as they shuffled up for their match. "How are you doing?" Moir asked his opponent. "Well, I'm still playing for the Top 8, so I can't complain much," Beasley replied.


After the two exchanged pleasantries and rolled to see who'd go first, both players quickly got underway.


### Game 1


Both players kept, as Moir led with [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) . Beasley revealed a hand of [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) , [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) , [Misty Rainforest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) , [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) , [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) , [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) , and [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) , and Moir was quick to jot down Beasley's hand before discarding [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) .


Basley led with a tapped [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) , and a second [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) left Beasley short of an [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) , revealing that he drew a [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) . Moir quickly cast [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) , and continued to work away at Beasley's hand, his strategy of getting to a lethal land count quickly being laid to rest.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptor12/r14_moir.JPG)  
*Ben Moir's Jund deck is well-equipped to attack a combo player's hand.*

[Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) didn't cascade into anything useful, but it gave Moir a plan of attack. Beasley, who needed an answer to [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) , found one when Moir attempted to go to 6 loyalty with her +1 effect, and no cards in hand. [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) bounced the planeswalker, forcing Moir to discard it to its own effect.


Despite that, the race was still on, and while Beasley reached seven lands, he had already discarded [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) and had no way to combo off. [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) gave Moir a faster clock, and left Beasley needing to buy time with [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) , tapping Moir's field and bouncing a [Raging Ravine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) .


Beasley drew, but when another [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) did not make its way to his hand, he picked up his cards to Moir's lethal [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) activation.


Beasley 0  Moir 1


### Game 2


Beasley looked at his first hand and quickly chose to mulligan, while Moir was content with his hand full of disruption. His six-card hand was good enough, but it quickly sank when Moir had a first-turn [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) , revealing [Firespout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout) , [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) , and three lands. The [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) quickly hit the graveyard.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptor12/r14_beasley.JPG)  
*Beasley fights hard through discard in order to reach the lands needed for a lethal [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) .*

Beasley added [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) to his board, as Moir cast [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) . Beasley was forced to [Firespout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout) the powerful card-drawing creature, giving way for Moir's third-turn [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) to do what she does best.


Beasley fought back in an attempt to hit his land drops, and [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) let him keep even at four lands despite a fourth-turn [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) from Moir. However, back-to-back [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) s after that left Beasley stranded, and Liliana at seven counters.


Beasley kept making land drops, but her ultimate left Beasley stranded on two lands and hurting for colored mana.


Despite finding some [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) s to keep Liliana from getting too big, Moir found a [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) that allowed him to remain way ahead on cards. Beasley kept pushing ahead, trying to stay in the game, but Liliana and [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) kept Moir in the driver's seat, and with his deck short on basic lands, the [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) s he was drawing weren't going to let him draw for a lethal [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) . A few turns later, and Beasley offered the handshake.


Beasley 0  Moir 2


 
Round 15 Round-Up - Ben Moir vs. James Vance and Collin Morton vs. Charles Wiper
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen*
With 37 points the target for Top 8, several matchups drew immediately. Jon Stern and Alon Chitizwho started the tournament with no byesdrew at the top of the standings. With 40 points, Chitiz will be the top seed in the Top 8.


Sam Pardee also drew with Willy Edel, giving them 38 and 37 points, respectively, while Dan Jordan and Alex Majlaton drew to go to 37 points apiece.


That left four matches with players able to reach the Top 8 with a win, potentially leaving two players with 37 points out in the cold if results didn't fall their way.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptor12/rd15_feature.JPG)  
*The feature match area was brimming with matches that mattered for the Top 8.*

### 


Ben Moir vs. James Vance


Vance overran Moir's defenses and outpaced his [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) s in game one, but Moir was able to hold out long enough in the second game to resolve a lethal [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) .


In the third and final game, [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) countered an early [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) , leaving Vance with mostly air, but a missed land drop and a [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) off the top put him back in the driver's seat. Liliana tore through Moir's hand and disrupted his game plan early on. However, a [Threads of Disloyalty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threads+of+Disloyalty) on Vance's [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) eventually gave him the match.


At 39 points, Vance is a lock for the Top 8.


### 


Collin Morton v. Charles Wiper


Morton dropped Wiper low with Elspeth and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and confounded his mana with [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) and [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) , but couldn't close the deal even as Wiper dodged multiple potentially lethal [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) triggers. On the last turn, at one life, Wiper turned up...another land. Winning by the skin of his teeth.


"Your [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) are incredible," Wiper said.


In the second game, Wiper missed some early land drops and was forced to pass without much action for multiple turns. Pretty soon a pair of [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) effectively cut Wiper off from anything but Green mana as [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and Elspeth, [Knight Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Errant) teamed up to push Morton ahead and take the second game.


The third game was back and forth as time ticked town on the round. Planeswalkers fell to [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) s and attacks, lands fell under [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) and [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) s, and [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) s were met by [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) s.


Eventually, the entire match, and the Top 8 fates of both players, came down to extra turns. Eventually it came down to the top of Wiper's deck. If it's a Lighting Bolt, Wiper wins. If not, Morton takes it.


The top card?


[Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) .


Morton makes the top at 39 points.

###

Stenborg and Smith split the first two games with Smith comboing off with [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) to force a decider.
