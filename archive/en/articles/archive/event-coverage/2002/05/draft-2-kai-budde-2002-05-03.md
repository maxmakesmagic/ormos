Draft 2: Kai Budde
==================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 3, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











Kai is 4-0. Big deal.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a914.jpg)Even were he to be 7-0, I'm sure no one would be surprised. But 7-0 is the best he can accomplish today, so that's what he's out to do.

He's seated at table 2 between American Gary Talim and Dutchman Kamiel Cornellisen. Directly across the table sits his friend and next-round opponent, Patrick Mello.

Kai's first pack contained [Ashen Firebeast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashen+Firebeast), [Muscle Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muscle+Burst), [Aven Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Archer), [Chatter of the Squirrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chatter+of+the+Squirrel), [Whispering Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade), and [Thermal Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thermal+Blast). He looked long and hard at the Burst, but chose the [Aven Flock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Flock). "It was the best card in the pack," he said later, "and good white is very powerful." So he had no problem picking a card of the "worst color." Somewhere, Zvi smiled.

Talim, to his right, first-picked an [Aven Smokeweaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Smokeweaver), passing Kai an [Aven Fisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fisher). Kai took the Fisher over [Dusk Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Imp) and [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger), showing a strong preference for blue/white. Talim switched into green soon after though, and passed Kai an [Aven Windreader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader).

After taking a [Hallowed Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Healer) and a [Beloved Chaplain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain), he was faced with the tough decision of [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) or [Dreamwinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamwinder). He opted for the 4/3, however, citing that he had just picked two white cards, making the 'Tog that much harder to cast. "If I had picked [Scrivener](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrivener) and [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) instead of the white cards, I would have definitely taken [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)," he commented.

The decision to play white paid off when he got a sixth-pick [Master Apothecary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary), and then took two Pilgrims of Justice to help it out.

Talim had switched almost completely into red/green, abandoning blue, and Kamiel took the [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) and decided to play black in his blue/green deck.

In pack two, Kai picked a [Mystic Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot) over the more versatile [Puppeteer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer). "If you're playing blue/white and have no removal," he explained, "you need really powerful flyers. And toughness-2 creatures always die to random [stuff], like [Crippling Fatigue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue)."

After grabbing a [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts), his third pick was an interesting one: [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger) over [Aven Cloudchaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Cloudchaser). He said the 'Flinger was not a counterdraft, but just another option for him to play. White/blue needs removal, remember, and the 'Flinger can be easily splashed.

Late in the second pack, he was passed [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast) and [Phantatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantatog). Although he was already considering splashing red, and the Beast is a very good card, he took the Atog, claiming he wanted to have something to do with his extra lands, and discarding them to the 'Tog was good enough. Now *that* is covering all the bases. He did admit later that allowing Talim to have a ninth-pick [Ember Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast) was a little absurd, even if it was correct.

Anticipating long games, Kai took [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) and [Angel of Retribution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Retribution) with his first two *Torment* picks. He claimed to be upset having to take the big Angel over [Skywing Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven), since the Aven is just a better card. "But sometimes you stall the game and need to race [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger), so the Angel should work out."

The only other interesting pick was [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) over [Teroh's Faithful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful). The Faithful is generally considered the best white common in *Torment*, but Kai already had lots of 4-mana plays. "I worry a lot about mana curve," he said. "I needed more 2-drops."

His final deck ended up being white/blue with two [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s and a [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger). "If I have to face red/green, I'll win for sure." He wasn't quite as confident about the other matchups, but I think he'll pull off a 2-1 and end up in great shape going into day 2.
