Grand Prix - Gothenburg 2010
----------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/trophy.jpg)The dust has settled and we have our Grand Prix Gothenburg champion - Kenny Oberg from... Gothenburg! Making the most of his home turf advantage the Swedish pro made no mistakes in making short work of Nikolai Herzog and Marijn Lybaert to set up an all-Swedish showdown against Anton Jonsson in the Final. But in the end his aggressive Green/Red deck, including two Acidic Slimes that proved crucial against Jonsson, was simply too good for the rest of a star-studded Top8.


It marked a triumphant end to what has been a fantastic Grand Prix. Over a thousand players fought their way through the wiles of M11 Sealed, and while the Pros, including Oberg, move on to Pro Tour Amsterdam, first the champion has a shorter trip - just ten minutes to his home.


Congratulations to Kenny Oberg, Champion of Grand Prix Gothenburg 2010!

Top 8 Draft - How They Line Up
------------------------------



*by David Sutcliffe*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/t8DGrp.jpg)With a Top 8 draft pod that comprised so many top players it was hard to know where to look - the champion could so easily come from any quarter; from seasoned pros like Sam Black to Swedish hope Kenny Oberg, to a wildcard like Markku Rikkola who is by his own admission playing the best **Magic** of his life. With such an unpredictable Top8 we thought that rather than follow a single drafter we would to take a step back and track all eight of your contenders as they entered the starting gates.


Quarter Final One: Marijn Lybaert vs. Sam Black


**Marijn Lybaert** had drafted a blue black that was, by his own admission, not good enough. When I asked him if he was happy he could only manage a half-hearted "Not Really". His deck was light on standout cards, and for a blue/black deck had very little solid removal either.


In Three Words: "Seven playables short"


**Sam Black** was much happier. Initially he wasn't sure he liked his deck, but after completing his build he felt things had gone ok. The question Sam had to face was which of his really good cards he could play, as he'd taken powerful cards in four colors. He had settled on a GR deck, but could splash or black for better cards if he felt he needed to.


In Three Words: "One Word. Rares"


Quarter Final Two: Nikolai Herzog vs. Kenny Oberg


**Nikolai Herzog** was solidly in a green/red deck and was pretty happy with his cards. His main problem had been trying to prioritise cheaper cards over the more exciting 4-drops that he kept seeing, and he wasn't sure that he had the balance right. "Given the cards I kept seeing it was a really strong pool".


In Three Words: "Really really aggressive"


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/t8dOberg.jpg)*With a strong Red/Green deck under him was Kenny Oberg destined to pick up the trophy?*
**Kenny Oberg** was initially unsure about his deck, but once he'd finished the build he decided that it was very similar to the decks he'd made earlier in the day so he was pretty happy. Also in red/green his quarterfinal against Herzog would be decided by pure muscle power.


In Three Words: "Strong Cards. Removal."


Quarter Final Three: Anton Jonsson vs. Markku Rikkola


**Anton Jonsson** would only commit that his deck was 'fine, but not awesome' even though it seemed to contain most of the hallmark blue/white aggressive cards. His three word summary of his deck was similarly non-committal...


In Three Words: "Blue White Beatdown". Fair enough.


**Markku Rikkola** was not happy with his deck at all - perhaps not the first player to find their virgin entry to a Top8 draft a daunting experiencer. After completing the build he said that his black/white deck was quite aggressive but that it would depend on what decks he was playing against. To round out his picks he had to take some color-specific removal. Play the right deck and he was good, but he feared the two [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) that he had passed along.


In Three Words: "Needs good draws"


Quarter Final Four: Allison Abe vs. Sammi Haggkvist


**Allison Abe** responded to my question 'are you happy?' with an emphatic "NO!" - the verdict on his blue/green deck being that it was terrible after he had decided to switch colors halfway through the second pack. Ever upbeat, his three word summary said it all...


In Three Words: "Not good enough"


**Sammi Haggkvist** wasn't too cheerful either. Much like Rikkola, Haggkvist had filled his deck out with cards that were dependent on the colors his opponent played, like [Combust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Combust) and [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark). Play against a white deck and he was happy, but face a green deck and he could be in trouble. It was a situation he described as 'awkward'.


In Three Words: "Come on White!"

Quarterfinal: Kenny Oberg (SWE) vs. Nikolai Herzog (NOR)
--------------------------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe*

Two of the veterans of the game, and two of the most successful Scandinavian players of all time, this Quarter Final threw together two players who would be popular champions - Oberg in particular being welcomed to the feature match are by the sort of female screams usually saved for rock stars. There was little doubt that the Swede was the hot ticket for the title as far this feature match crowd was concerned.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/qf1Oberg.jpg)*Blink and you miss it - Kenny Oberg raced to a 1-0 lead*
Once inside the ropes, the talk between these two seasoned pros turned inevitably towards the coming Pro Tour.


"Are you going to Amsterdam?" asked Oberg, as he shuffled his deck.


"I don't know if I'm playing," Herzog replied, "I don't have a deck. But I'm definitely going down to see friends. Maybe I'll get given a deck that I like?"


"It's still six rounds of limited", Kenny Oberg reminded his opponent


"True", Herzog nodded, "so maybe. Maybe."


To an audible groan from the crowd Oberg had to begin the match with a mulligan, while Herzog went to the tank for a good long while before deciding to keep his initial seven cards. The Norwegian's claim that his would be 'really really aggressive' seemed to misfire, though. His opening gambit of a [Goblin Piker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piker) was answered by a [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer), then after he had used [Chandra's Outrage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Outrage) to destroy the Pyromancer the Norwegian found himself eating a faceful of tree. Oberg had played an [Awakener Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awakener+Druid) then imbued a [Sacred Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Wolf) with [Volcanic Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength). With nothing larger than a [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer) of his own at hand to defend himself from Oberg's sudden and unexpected offense, Nikolai Herzog was a game down inside six turns!


"Sigh", said the Norwegian, turning to his sideboard for help.


**Kenny Oberg (SWE) 1 - 0 Nikolai Herzog (NOR)** 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/qf1Herzog.jpg)*After a strong showing in the swiss, Nikolai Herzog found his swift defeat hard to swallow.*
Before the second game Oberg called for some extra basic land, indicating that he was making some changes to his deck in sideboarding. Sure enough he began the second game with a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), although a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) suggested he hadn't entirely changed tack. But things had gone from bad to worse for Nikalai Herzog. He was stuck with just three [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s while Oberg played out a pair of [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll)s and a Spectral Wolf.


Herzog finally found a mountain, although it seemed like it could be too late to fight off Oberg's forces. The Norwegian could only muster a [Runeclaw Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeclaw+Bear) and Goblin Pike to his defense, and when Oberg again boosted the Spectral Wolf with a [Volcanic Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength) the unhappy Herzog had to concede a shockingly swift defeat.


**Kenny Oberg (SWE) 2 - 0 Nikolai Herzog (NOR)** 


"Was your deck good?" asked Oberg, after the match? Certainly they had seen almost nothing from Herzog's deck in the two games.


"I thought so. Pretty good, yeah"


But with a blistering and irresistible offense it was the Swede, Kenny Oberg, who would carry Sweden's hopes into the Semi-Finals. Would Anton Jonnson be able to join him in a dream All-Swedish final?




Quarterfinal - Anton Jonsson vs. Markku Rikola
----------------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon*

An all-Scandinavian quarter final sees Finland's Markku Rikola take on the potential Hall of Famer from Sweden, Anton Jonsson. While Rikola is in his first top 8, his opponent has an illustrious history. This is Jonsson's ninth Grand Prix top 8, and he has five Pro Tour top tables to go with it, highlighted by finishing second at Pro Tour Nagoya in 2005.


Rikola opened proceedings with a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), while Jonsson opened on [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard), which traded the following turn for Rikola's Ajani Pridemate. [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) and [Infantry Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infantry+Veteran) joined the Swedish team, and Jonsson's [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) allowed him to [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) a [Condemn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn). [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon) for Rikola was next, and [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) sent it packing.


'[Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk)!' yelled Jonsson, sotto voce, before dropping his Finnish opponent to nine. [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl) hopefully sent some land to Jonsson's aid, although he was still attacking profitably. At six life, Rikola dropped [Wild Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Griffin) to stem the bleeding somewhat, but Jonsson had indeed sent a mana to the top of the deck. That meant four mana. That meant [Ajani Goldmane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Goldmane). Suddenly everything got bigger, and Rikola was in a world of Planeswalker pain.


**Anton Jonsson 1 – 0 Markku Rikola**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/qf2Rikola.jpg)*Markku Rikola*
[Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate) opened game two for Rikola, with Jonsson taking to the air (and the top of his deck) with [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl), joined by [Cloud Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental). Rikola was aggressive, with [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) for the [Cloud Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental), [Black Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Knight) hitting the battlefield, and both [Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate) and a [Palace Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palace+Guard) coming in, with Jonsson at fifteen.


[Aether Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Adept) bounced the [Black Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Knight), before trading with [Ajani's Pridemate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Pridemate), Rikola adding [Nether Horror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Horror) to his board, making efficient use of all his mana. Jonsson continued to nibble away with the [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl), supported by [Infantry Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infantry+Veteran). [Howling Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Banshee) shortened the clock, though, as Jonsson now stood seven to fourteen behind. Intriguingly, he used [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) to deal with the [Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banshee), then cast [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus). [Clear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clear)ly he was unafraid of another three point drain on both players.


Nine to three was Rikola's lead as Jonsson untapped once again. A second [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl) sent everything to the bottom of the library, and Jonsson used [Sleep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) to gain the tempo advantage. That advantage didn't last long, however, with Rikola spending five mana on [Serra Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel). [Aether Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Adept) bounced the Serra back to hand, and now it was five to three in favor of the Finn. Down came [Serra Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel) once more. [Phantom Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Beast) was the play from Jonsson, leaving Rikola to spend six mana. On [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion). With Anton at three life.


'I'm specializing in really expensive [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s today' said the Finn, having done the same thing to Bram Snepvangers on three life with [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan). Sometimes, you just have to pay the price to get the job done.


**Anton Jonsson 1 – 1 Markku Rikola**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/qf2Jonsson.jpg)*Anton Jonsson*
[Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard) was the perfect opener for Jonsson. [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) was a pretty good turn two, although [Black Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Knight) on the other side could be problematic. The Pegasus flew in for two, while Rikola took to the skies with [Liliana's Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Specter), forcing Jonsson to discard. His choice? [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate). For the second game running, [Garruk's Companion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%27s+Companion) faced [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl) by the end of each turn two. [Sylvan Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger) fetched Öberg a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) after the Companion had dealt the first damage of the match. That was enough to attract [Assassinate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate) from Lybaert, who nonetheless was under pressure, at sixteen, and facing [Canyon Minotaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Minotaur) and [Sylvan Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger). [Phantom Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Beast) changed matters, but only briefly, [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer) arriving for Öberg.


[Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) allowed Lybaert to dig for answers, but, just as black does, those possible answers would come at a price. Knowing the [Phantom Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Beast) was destined for the graveyard, Lybaert attacked, choosing [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) to bounce the [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer), protecting his Beast for at least another turn.


Öberg chose not to replay it, opting instead for [Greater Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Basilisk). [Liliana's Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Specter) forced Öberg to discard, which was only a humble [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest). [Barony Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barony+Vampire) joined the Belgian board position, which was now confronted by [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer), [Sacred Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Wolf), [Canyon Minotaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Minotaur), [Sylvan Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger), and [Greater Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Basilisk). The decider was turning into a decent game, with both players on eleven life, and down to just two cards apiece.


At last, the [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer) was able to dispose of the [Phantom Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Beast), Lybaert trading [Barony Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barony+Vampire) for [Canyon Minotaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Minotaur). The Sorceror's Strongbox had been great for Öberg in game two, but not in game three, as Lybaert used [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to counter it. The [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer), though, was going to be a big, big issue....which is why [Quag Sickness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quag+Sickness) put it in the graveyard at top speed.


[Greater Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Basilisk) and [Sylvan Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger) resumed hostilities, dropping Lybaert to four, staring across at Öberg on nine. Next time around, the [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl) got in the way of [Greater Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Basilisk). Lybaert at three. [Plummet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plummet) arrived for Öberg, and suddenly it was all over. The all-Swedish final had become a reality, a tremendous achievement.


Marijn Lybaert 1 – 2 Kenny Öberg




Final - Kenny Öberg vs. Anton Jonsson
-------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/final1.jpg)1,001 players began their Grand Prix Gothenburg journey. Most of them weren't Swedish, but everyone else needn't have bothered. Home turf has been thoroughly defended, and how, with both finalists representing the home country. Both have a fine pedigree, with Öberg coming to prominence as 'The Tezzerator', building the devastating deck that took him to the Pro Tour Berlin top 8 in 2008.


Jonsson's [Infantry Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infantry+Veteran) was quickly trumped by Öberg's [Garruk's Companion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%27s+Companion). [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) was thrown – literally – by Jonsson at the 3/2 Trampler, leaving the path clear for the Veteran to attack for the first damage of the match. Öberg found a use for the Pacified Companion, casting [Fling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling) to aim it at Jonsson's freshly-cast [Cloud Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+Elemental). [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl) went into Scrying action for Jonsson, who was playing at a brisk pace in front of a huge home crowd.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/finalOberg.jpg)*Kenny Oberg radiated icy coolness in the final*
Five mana in the semi final had generally meant [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) from Öberg, and that was the case here, although the land destruction was at least partly negated by Jonsson having [Terramorphic Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) at the ready. [Infantry Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infantry+Veteran) pumped the [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl), with Jonsson suspiciously passing the turn with four mana open. [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer) resolved, however, the counter**Magic** failing to materialize.


Big Moment – any time a Planeswalker hits the battlefield it's a big deal. [Ajani Goldmane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Goldmane) granted permanent bonuses to both [Infantry Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infantry+Veteran) and [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl), with the Veteran pumping once it was busily in the red zone. Suddenly Öberg's [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer) wasn't looking so powerful against a board that was no longer made up of 1/1s.


Big Moment number two - [Volcanic Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength) on [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) made it 4/4. Same turn, Big Moment number three – [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) from Öberg. He added [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll), while Jonsson couldn't get past four mana. [Augury Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augury+Owl) would be a little help, as would [Aether Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Adept) for the [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll), but Öberg seemingly had all the answers, with [Combust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Combust) and [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) allowing him to take the lead.


It turned out that the really Big Moment of the game had been that [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime).


**Kenny Öberg 1 – 0 Anton Jonsson**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpgot10/finalJonsson.jpg)*The trophy was within reach for Anton Jonsson*
With a mulligan to six, Jonsson needed a bit of help. At least [Infantry Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infantry+Veteran) was a good start, but without anything to follow. Öberg was next up, with turn three [Sacred Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Wolf). [Foresee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foresee) brought Jonsson plenty of choices, quick to send three cards to the bottom and collect one good plus one mystery card. [Sacred Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Wolf) and [Infantry Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infantry+Veteran) traded, leaving Öberg to run out [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll). Against blue-white, Öberg didn't really need to fear removal. Instead, Jonsson had [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief), which looked an unlikely source of card advantage.


In came the [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll), Öberg repeating his five mana trick with [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime). Jonsson this time was content to [Excommunicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) the Slime, apparently unafraid of more land destruction. [Terramorphic Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) again allowed him to keep access to blue mana, but there was little evidence that he was gaining the upper hand in this must-win game two.


WOW! Öberg had another [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) in hand, killing the last [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) on Jonsson's side of the battlefield. When was the last time Land Destruction won a Premier Event? Well, it won this one, as Jonsson put the [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) in the graveyard and reached across the feature match table to grasp his countryman's hand in gracious defeat.


**Kenny Öberg 2 – 0 Anton Jonsson**


His final foe vanquished, Kenny Öberg is the Champion of Grand Prix Gothenburg 2010
