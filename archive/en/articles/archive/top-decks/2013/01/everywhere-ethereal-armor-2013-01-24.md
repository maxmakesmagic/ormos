(Relatively) recent developments in both Standard and Modern (the current PTQ format) have revealed some very different successful strategies than the last time we touched on them in this column.


###  Ethereal Armor in Standard—Hexproof Mirror Match at Grand Prix Atlantic City


 The top *two* decks in the finals of the recent [Standard Grand Prix](/en/events/coverage/stern%E2%80%99s-bant-auras-take-atlantic-city) were both aggressive decks based on hexproof creatures loaded with enhancements. Reminiscent of the Team SCGBlack "combo" deck that put two players into the Top 8 of [Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored*](/en/events/coverage/ptars12), these hexproof decks are designed for minimum interaction and maximum impact (with the opponent's face).

The Grand Prix–winning deck is a good example of the Standard Bant Auras.


 As you can see, it has eight solid hexproof creatures—[Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)—and a ton of ways to make them big. Bant Auras can start the game by fixing its mana with [Abundant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth), and that [Abundant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abundant+Growth) can boost an [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) down the line. [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) is a top-flight enhancer "good enough for *green* decks" and obviously quite effective on [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker), which can neither be blocked nor targeted. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Invisible Stalker | Abundant Growth |

 The most obvious "combo" in the deck is [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) + [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight). [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) is one of the most powerful and frustrating offensive creatures across a variety of formats. If you drop a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) turn two off an [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) it isn't even vulnerable to [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)! Pretty much the only thing an opponent can do about the Geist is to put a creature in front of it (generally taking the 4/4 on the jaw). But if you apply [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) to [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), it not only gets big and puts the opponent on a three-turn clock, but it takes away the principle option for interaction. Flying creatures, quite simply, are tough to block. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Geist of Saint Traft | Spectral Flight |


[Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) is quite the deadly assassin in this strategy, but maybe not in the most obvious ways. Sure, if you have a huge creature wearing a hundred [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor)s and [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor)s, perhaps the manyfold bounties of an [Increasing Savagery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Savagery), doubling damage will be attractive... but what about not pairing [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) when you have a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) already on board? You can instead pair the [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) with the 4/4 Angel token, allowing you to hit for 8 in the sky... 2 + 8 is 10, or half the other player's life total—might or might not be better than a more obvious double strike route. Just know that—especially with the Angel's evasion—this is an important option for the deck.

 The much-decorated Joshua Utter-Leyton put some interesting twists on his Bant Auras; in the main deck we see [Fencing Ace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace)—which starts off with double strike—as well as [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity). *Just* putting [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) on a [Fencing Ace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace) can give you quite the little card-drawing engine, and [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) on a [Fencing Ace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace) can strike down a [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), live, and hit the opponent. [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) in general not only works synergistically with [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor), but—[Fencing Ace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace) or no—it can keep the deck flowing, especially when working with evasion creatures. Although the deck is fast (and many of the creatures are quite clearly hard to deal with, or at least target) it still has to commit tons of permanents to the battlefield. Having a backup—a hand full of Plan B—reduces the deck style's reliance on a single dimension.

 Utter-Leyton's sideboard was built to beat creature removal—and sweepers more specifically. [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) might be targetable, but its point is to be good against cards like [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)... the kind of cards where [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker), and especially [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), are by nature exposed. [Wolfir Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger) not only lives through quite a few kinds of commonly played creature removal, but Josh could play it at the end of the opponent's turn (ideally a turn where the opponent just tapped down or out for a big defensive card).

 It is probably fair to say that Jund is still THE defining strategy of the Modern format, but if you plan to win a [Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* qualifier](/en/node/640136) tournament, likely you will have to be prepared to beat the Bogle deck. 


 Actually predating the Standard version by a short time, this deck is based on similar principles: Play a hexproof creature that the opponent can't target. Load it up with [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) and other synergistic creature enhancements. Bang, bash, kill quickly. 


 Now, the Modern format gives you access to quite a few more cards than Standard, so the card choices will be different other than [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) (a card that exists at the perfect intersection of card power and being one mana). Two-thirds of the creatures in this deck are one mana, which can set you up for some immediate beatdown with [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) or [totem armor Umbras](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&name=+%5BUmbra%5D&type=+%5B%22Enchantment%22%5D). 


 Because a favorite play of the dominant Jund decks in Modern is to flip [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) off their Bloodbraid Elfs, the Bogle deck—hexproof that it may be—has to find a way around the [Cruel Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Edict) ability on that powerful Planeswalker.

 That is where [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) comes in. 


![Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dryad+Arbor)
  
 A Forest, [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) is accessible off of [Misty Rainforest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) or [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs). If a Jund player is careless (or doesn't know that [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) is coming), the player might think he or she is about to get an X-for-none, taking down a threat covered in creature enchantments... but the Bogle deck can just go and find [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) in response and sacrifice *that*. In a pinch, the 1/1 body on [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) can itself play offense and get just as a big as an actual offensive creature. 


 Surprisingly mighty **Magic** from this sector. 


 Now, despite the viability of the Bogle deck, the Top 8 of the [most recent Modern Grand Prix](/en/events/coverage/manders-modern-man-moment) featured five successful, different archetypes... none of which involved an [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor). While Jund remains a popular powerhouse, [Grand Prix Bilbao](/en/events/coverage/manders-modern-man-moment) showed us [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite)s, [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman)s, [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)s, and even [Clutch of the Undercity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+the+Undercity) as defining spells. Red-White-Blue Midrange has quickly become a darling of Modern, closing multiple PTQs as well as clinching the Bilbao title.


 Red-White-Blue Midrange does one thing well, which is potentially everything. If it draws the right cards, it can present an advantageous position in any matchup. [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) can tear up [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens. [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) can make [Bump in the Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night) look silly. [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)—especially with [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) clearing the path—is just as lethal in Modern as it is in seemingly every other format. [Aven Mindcensor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor) is a direct—and potentially clock-putting-on—answer to land-search strategies from [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) to a theoretically lethal [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift). 


And on top of all of those, Red-White-Blue Midrange has room for a nice spread of permission cards.



* [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull), [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix), and [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) against damage...
* [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) against [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) (or creature enchantments)...
* Even [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) and [Engineered Explosives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) against small creatures (including Goblin tokens)...

Red-White-Blue can potentially have the upper hand, regardless of who is sitting across the table.

While [Bituminous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) was never the most mana-efficient way to take out a creature, the nigh-certainty of card advantage (as well as the possibility of potentially "winning the cascade lottery" by hitting [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) and *another* card) make [Bituminous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) one of the most exciting catalysts in the Jund arsenal.

### Scapeshift


 The general concept of the [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck is to use green acceleration—[Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow), [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder), and so on—to generate a critical mass of lands in play; then use [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) to get one or more copies of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) + Mountains. Ka-boom.

| Scapeshift | Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle |


[Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) decks have two major incentives: (1) they play lots of cards that are just very good (like [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) or good creatures out of the sideboard) and (2) [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) itself is a one-card combo; if you have enough lands in play, you win immediately.

 The GP Bilbao coverage staff considered Deltour's innovation of [Clutch of the Undercity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+the+Undercity) its [#1 card of the tournament](/en/events/coverage/manders-modern-man-moment). [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), as we've said, is a "one-card combo," and playing [Clutch of the Undercity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+the+Undercity) just makes it much simpler to draw that one card. With seven or more lands in play, you can seamlessly transmute for [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) and play it immediately. Like we said, *ka-boom*.

 Modern [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks can chain up with value from none-to-five; they can block well, accelerate mana, and find solutions to any number of the format's problems. But these decks tend to kill with infinite [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) combinations. 


![Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker)
  
 Interestingly, [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) can combine with creatures at the three, four, or five. 




![Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deceiver+Exarch)

* [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)—[Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) copies [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch), which untaps [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker), setting up another [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Deceiver%2BExarch) copy.




![Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Restoration+Angel)

* [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)—[Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) copies [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel); its token "blinks" [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker), which of course leaves us with an untapped Mirror Breaker (ready to do another copy, blink, and re-copy).




![Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Zealous+Conscripts)

* [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts)—Like [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch), [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) can untap [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker), as can each copy.



 While most successfully navigated Pod games end up with [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) setting up an attack for essentially infinite damage, how you get there is most of the fun. If your opponent has a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and a persist creature... watch out! 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Birthing Pod | Glen Elendra Archmage |

 At the four, Pod decks play both [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) and [Glen Elendra Archmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage). If you have a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) in play, you can sacrifice the four, find [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts), untap the [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and sacrifice the persisted second four (which can find [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker)). 



[Chord of Calling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) can serve in similar setup capacity.
