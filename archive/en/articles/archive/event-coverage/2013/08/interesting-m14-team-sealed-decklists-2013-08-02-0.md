Interesting M14 Team Sealed Decklists
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 2, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten












The first format for the World **Magic** Cup competitors is **Magic** 2014 Team Sealed. With twelve boosters in total to build three decks out of, it is much more likely to get multiples of a certain card than in a six-booster individual Sealed event. Moreover, there tend to be more interesting synergies and combos to choose from. As I walked around the room during deck construction, I saw a bunch of sweet decks being built. Here are my highlights.

 The Spanish team opened as many as four [Predatory Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predatory+Sliver) and two [Blur Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blur+Sliver). And let me tell you: attacking on turn three with a hasty 3/3 is not just a good start to a game---it's a great start. Besides the two-drop and three-drop slivers, the Spanish team had access to Slivers of the [Megantic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megantic+Sliver), [Battle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Sliver), and [Steelform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steelform+Sliver) variety. If they would manage to get every single Sliver in Zuñiga's deck on the table at the same time, then [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) would turn into an 11/10 monster. Not bad at all. 


 But that [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) could get even bigger. Did you see the [Door of Destinies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+of+Destinies) lingering in Mario's pool? Now that typically is a hard card to pull off, but it fit perfectly with the multitude of Slivers in Zuñiga's deck.

[Gladecover Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gladecover+Scout) is a card that regularly goes as a last pick in draft. After all, it's just a fragile 1/1 creature for one mana, and hexproof rarely matters on a 1/1. Unless, of course, you combine it with two [Lightning Talons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Talons) and two [Trollhide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trollhide). Team Hong Kong saw the interaction and made sure to put all those cards in the same deck.

 Many, many decks chose to submit a Black-Red sacrifice theme deck. When you have [Blood Bairn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Bairn) in play, [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) turns into a hard removal spell with a bonus. It's one of the stronger synergies available in M14 Limited, and one that many teams made sure not to miss. France was one of the teams who opened as many as four [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason), but surprisingly chose to leave two of them in the sideboard. 



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/wmc13/levytreason.jpg)  
 *Raphaël Lévy was one of many players sporting the synergy between black's sacrifice outlets and red's [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) today."* 

 I sought out Pro Tour Hall of Famer Raphaël Lévy to ask him why. "We didn't have that many sacrifice outlets," he told me. "Two of them are [Gnawing Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnawing+Zombie), so you need 5 lands for the [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) combo, which can be difficult. Also, if you draw 3 [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) in your opening hand without a sacrifice outlet, what are you going to do?" His explanation makes sense, and teaches us an important lesson in restraint. 


That said, I had trouble restraining myself from firing up an M14 draft to assemble some of these sweet synergies myself.
