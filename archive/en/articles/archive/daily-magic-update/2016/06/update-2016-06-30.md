What to Watch Tonight
=====================


1. [Community Super League Finale](https://communitysuperleague.com/)

This is it, everyone. The big finale show for the Community Super League. Paul Cheon and Gaby Spartz start the action, playing best two out of three **formats**—in this case Pauper, Canadian Highlander, and No Banned-List Modern, as voted on by fans. The winner then has to do the same dance again with top-seed Kenji Egashira.


But wait, there's more! I've been promised some "plot twists" during the show as voted on by the audience. I honestly don't even know myself, so I'm curious to find out.


It all starts tonight at 5 p.m. PT/8 p.m. ET on [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic).

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Blue-Green Tentacles Crush Ramp](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13362&writer=Seth+Manfield&articledate=6-30-2016)

This deck has, virtually out of nowhere, become the darling of anyone looking to do something different in Standard over the past few days. Which is kind of funny, because there's a player in the [First Friday video we produced back when the format first started with *Shadows over Innistrad*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k7TVh3Fdris) who was playing this exact deck. It's the first player interviewed in the video.


The deck looks to abuse the very powerful and very underplayed [Crush of Tentacles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Tentacles), even going so far as to loop it with [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector). Is the deck good? Who knows. Is it a blast to play? Clearly.


There have been a few things written about it lately, but the [videos from Seth Manfield posted today](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13362&writer=Seth+Manfield&articledate=6-30-2016) on TCGPlayer are especially worth watching.
