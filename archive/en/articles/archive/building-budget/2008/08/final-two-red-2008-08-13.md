Immediately I can see that there are a few dead cards here ([Wild Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Research) without mana to activate it? [Mudhole](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mudhole) at all?), but random is random, and what color embraces randomness more than red? Still, I'm pretty sure that there are a few candidates here that are not long for the world, so with that in mind, I shuffled up (electronically) for Game 1!

**Game 1: astateofdissipation (Poison [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone))**


I end up trading a [Reckless Embermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Embermage) for a [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), at which point astateofdissipation drops [Swamp Mosquito](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp+Mosquito), clones it twice, and backs them with double [Swarmyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swarmyard). Although I have a lot of creatures on the board, I am unable to outrace his ability to regenerate his Mosquitoes and then poison me to death.


Record: 0-1


**Out:** [Impatience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impatience), [Mudhole](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mudhole), [Wild Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Research), [Tide of War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tide+of+War)  
**In:** 4x [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone)


I like the idea of having something to use with a lot of [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, and [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone) seems like a ton of fun for a mono-red deck—kind of like [Volcanic Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Wind)s, but able to go straight to the head. I also have visions of being about to conspire it with Wort, for a good 12-14 damage to the head.

**Game 2: resqueda (Black-Base Shrines Land Destruction)**


Resqueda starts the game with an [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth), then a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), and then drops two [Spreading Algae](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Algae) on each of my first two lands. He then blows up one of my lands with [Rancid Earth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancid+Earth) and proceeds to drop [Honden of Cleansing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honden+of+Cleansing+Fire). I build up to six mana and start dropping creatures, the first of which ([Reckless Embermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Embermage)) dies to [Porphyry Nodes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porphyry+Nodes). I drop [Karplusan Yeti](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karplusan+Yeti) and [Tephraderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tephraderm), and resqueda answers with [Honden of Life's Web](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honden+of+Life%27s+Web).


I break through by ramping up to eight, allowing me to drop [Wort, the Raidmother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+the+Raidmother) without having to lose my [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) / [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s to his Algae. When he drops another [Porphyry Nodes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porphyry+Nodes), I play and conspire [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone), hitting each of his tokens for one, and doming him for 12 damage, plus 10 from my attacking creatures. He gets two more blockers on his turn, but I have a second [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone), and I am able to send 18 damage directly to his head, outracing his life gain and winning the game.


Record: 1-1


**Game 3: electricmessiah (Black-White Lifegain)**


 



![Shivan Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=shivan+dragon)

Electricmessiah starts out with [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden), and by the time I get my first play ([Reckless Embermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Embermage)), he's accelerated into back-to-back [Beacon of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Immortality), courtesy of [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone) and [Orzhov Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Signet). He then drops triple [Hissing Miasma](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hissing+Miasma) and double [Righteous Cause](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Cause), and tops it off with a third [Beacon of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Immortality) for good measure.

I start to get back into the game with a [Shivan Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Hellkite), but it is shut down quickly with [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters). I then drop [Shivan Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon), and start swinging for an upwards of 17 damage a turn (taking 3 damage each time I swing). I hit him five times with [Shivan Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon) (for a total of around 100 damage), and then drop [Wort, the Raidmother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+the+Raidmother). This enables me to get back-to-back [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone) for 24 each, bringing electricmessiah back down to a manageable life range.


As I get stuck on 2 life (without any more chance to attack), I start pinging electricmessiah once a turn with my Embermage (once on his turn, and once on my turn), adding [Lavaborn Muse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaborn+Muse) for an extra 3 damage a turn against his empty hand. He gets a last-ditch Beacon to stave off death for a turn, but I am able to finish him off with [Lavaborn Muse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaborn+Muse) to seal a victory that was, at one point, 160ish life points away.


Record: 2-1

**Game 6: eightc (Enchantress)**


Eightc starts with [Verduran Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verduran+Enchantress) and [Unstable Mutation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unstable+Mutation). I get down [Cragganwick Cremator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cragganwick+Cremator) and then drop [Shard Phoenix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shard+Phoenix). Eightc gets [Living Terrain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Terrain), drops [Simic Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Guildmage), and uses [Persuasion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion) on my Cremator. I block the Treefolk land on the attack (while eightc is down to one mana, and so can't move around the Mutation), and sacrifice the Phoenix to take care of the now-shrunk Enchantress and the Guildmage. I then drop [Wort, the Raidmother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+the+Raidmother), take a 10-hit from two attackers, and wipe out eightc's board with a conspired [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone).

I've had a few games that ended so far with a hugely conspired [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone). So I decide to make the focus of the deck "Bang! You're dead!" I want cards that will destroy my opponent from out of nowhere. [Blazing Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Shoal) is a fun choice, because I already have a bunch of high-cost cards in my deck (4 Wort, 4 [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone)), so it will often pitch for +6 damage. I add in [Akroma, Angel of Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury), because I can both play it early game (turn three), morph it (turn six), drop it steady (turn eight), or pitch it to Shoal (+8 damage). [Oxidda Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidda+Golem) is another "[Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s matter" card to add to this deck, adding a bit of speed via haste.

**Game 11: DarkCyrus (White-Blue-Black Blink)**


DarkCyrus starts with [Phyrexian Rager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Rager), and follows with [Aven Riftwatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Riftwatcher). I get face-down Akroma ([Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror)ed) and [Wort, the Raidmother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+the+Raidmother). I then play [Jaws of Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone), and I misclick, sending 7 damage to a creature, instead of 14 to his head. The next turn, I have [Oxidda Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidda+Golem) with [Blazing Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Shoal) for 9 (removing another Wort from the game), but he has [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) on [Aven Riftwatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Riftwatcher) to stay alive. I stare helplessly at the [Cragganwick Cremator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cragganwick+Cremator)s in my hand, as I send him down to 1 life—and he drops double Riftwatcher the next turn with [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) backup to basically blow me back out of the game. It's not often I end up losing to a misclick, but in this case, 100% accurate.


Record: 6-5


Were I to keep evolving the deck, I would continue to focus on cards that can wipe out my opponent in a single turn—perhaps more [Relentless Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Assault) (which work well with Wort and with [Blazing Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Shoal)) so that my creatures are more often lethal in a single (set of) swings. I also might want some global sweepers, such as [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), [Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast), or the such, because X/1, X/2, and X/3 flying creatures seemed to give me trouble.
