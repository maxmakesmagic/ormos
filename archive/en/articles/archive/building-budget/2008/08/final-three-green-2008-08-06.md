The Final Three: Green!
=======================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on August 6, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget, the article dedicated to building decks that cost 30 tickets or less using **Magic Online** prices! If you tuned in last week, you'll know that this is one of my last three columns on this web site. In the meanwhile, the current series I'm working on involves taking 35 random budget rares (each of which costs .25 to .5 tickets) in a single color, adding in 25 lands, and seeing where the deck would take me from there. This week's color? Green!


Before we get started, I will say that this article is a little truncated due to two major factors, the first being that I just spent the entire last week at the U.S. National Championships for **Magic: The Gathering**, working the StarCityGames.com booth with Ken Adams and Wes Moss. The second? My wife Kate and I just celebrated our one-year anniversary yesterday (I love you Kate!), so I didn't have as much time as usual to work on this article. Do not fear! Next week's article will finish up the green evolution, and do the entire red evolution at one timeâ€”it'll be a supersized BoaB extravaganza!


All right, so onto the deck! I took my handy-dandy spreadsheet of budget rares, and rolled my random numbers on random.org to randomly select from the following list:

**Game 1: tefus ([Tunnel Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tunnel+Vision) / [Living End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End))**

He plays [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), and then plays [Tunnel Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tunnel+Vision) on himself, naming [Living End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End). Unfortunately for him, the [Living End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End) in his deck is the 4th from the bottom card, and he mills away both of his [Clockspinning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning)s. I drop [Rhox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox) and [Spike Tiller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Tiller), make two of my lands 3/3 creatures, and kill him within two turns.

**Game 2: Chadskee (Black-Green Elves)**


We go back and forth on creatures for a while, with Chadskee dropping some smaller elves (plus a [Moonglove Winnower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonglove+Winnower)), and me using [Immaculate Magistrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immaculate+Magistrate) to pump my midrange guys. I get down [Nature's Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Will) plus [Upwelling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upwelling), allowing me to store up a truly massive amount of mana for [Nourishing Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nourishing+Shoal), keeping me from ever having a chance of dying. I finally drop [Sekki, Seasons' Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sekki%2C+Seasons%27+Guide), which gets around his deathtouch creatures (since it prevents damage to itself), and combos great with [Immaculate Magistrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immaculate+Magistrate) (which can make sure it keeps coming back). He eventually dies to a horde of 1/1 Spirits plus [Sekki, Seasons' Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sekki%252C%2BSeasons%2527%2BGuide) itself.


Record: 2-0


Keys to the game: Deathtouch only triggers when damage is dealt, so [Sekki, Seasons' Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sekki%252C%2BSeasons%2527%2BGuide) was the way I was finally able to get through his wall of [Winnow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winnow)ers!

**Game 4: yiorgosv (Selesnya)**


I get stalled on three mana for a little bit, and have to trade [Isao, Enlightened Bushi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isao%2C+Enlightened+Bushi) with [Selesnya Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildmage) when offered the trade, without enough mana up to regenerate. He drops [Safehold Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safehold+Elite) and [Watchwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf), backed with [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault). I put down [Immaculate Magistrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immaculate+Magistrate) and then Emperor Croc, hoping to stave off his attack. He gets down [Wilt-Leaf Cavaliers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Cavaliers), and then enchants them with [Shield of the Oversoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shield+of+the+Oversoul). Even though I get [Rhox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox) and [Nature's Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Will) in the next couple of turns (ensuring I can double my mana each turn), I can't outrace his 6/7 flyer.


Record: 3-1


Keys to the game: I had no way to deal with an enchantment, which is very much "not green" (green should easily deal with both enchantments and artifacts). The question is: which enchantment destruction spell would I want to add to this deck? Signs point towards something like [Indrik Stomphowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indrik+Stomphowler), to keep up the theme of midrange to huge fatties that has been doing well so far.


**Game 5: sennin\_pl (Zombies)**

![Nourishing Shoal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nourishing+Shoal)

Sennin\_pl starts the game early with [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) and [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin). My first play is on turn five, when I get down a 1/1 [Wurmcalling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcalling) token (with buyback). He sacrifices his [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin), swings for two, and cycles [Gempalm Polluter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gempalm+Polluter). I drop a second [Wurmcalling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcalling) token the following turn (2/2), and he answers with [Lord of the Undead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Undead). I continue dropping creatures, the first two of which are met with [Dark Banishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing), but I finally get to stick [Spectral Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force) (great against mono-black!) and [Kamahl, Fist of Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Fist+of+Krosa), against [Undead Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Warchief) and [Soulless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulless+One). Finally, sennin\_pl goes all in for the attack, as I am tapped out, and he has lethal damage on the board even through my blockers. As I put damage on the stack... I pitch [Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi) from my hand to gain 8 life from [Nourishing Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nourishing+Shoal), setting sennin\_pl back quite a few turns of development!

I then get down [Spike Tiller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Tiller) and [Stone-Tongue Basilisk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone-Tongue+Basilisk), and sennin\_pl starts rebuilding with another [Lord of the Undead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Undead) (bringing back the first), a [Gravedigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger), and [Call to the Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Grave). I drop [Plated Slagwurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Slagwurm), and then use [Loaming Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loaming+Shaman) to dump all of his zombie cards back into his library. I then start activating Kamahl on each of our turns, targeting his lands on his upkeep (so he has to sacrifice them to [Call to the Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Grave)) and targeting mine on my turn (so I can lose my least important resource). After a couple of turns of this, I finally hit seven cards in my graveyard for threshold, allowing me to swing with my entire team. That means [Stone-Tongue Basilisk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone-Tongue+Basilisk) can kill all five opposing creatures in one fell swoop, while the rest of my creatures get +3/+3 thanks to Kamahl, finishing the game in a single turn!


Record: 4-1


Strangely enough, this deck has worked the absolute best (out of the gate) so far out of the three rare-centric decks! The assortment of large fatties (many of which have been good to have in different situations) has worked well, and I have the ability to just turn the game around by dropping huge creature after huge creature mid-to-late game. Do I want to focus on [Nature's Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Will) and creatures like [Rhox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox) and [Thorn Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn+Elemental)? Would it be better to have utility fat ([Patron of the Orochi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Orochi)) or huge beaters ([Plated Slagwurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Slagwurm))? Do I need more artifact and enchantment kill? Tune in next week to see the big finish to the evolution of the Final Three: Green deck, and the full evolution of the Final Two: Red deck!
