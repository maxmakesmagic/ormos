Deck Tech: Oath of Beasts
=========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 5, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











Oath of Beasts, or Black/Green Oath comes as a brainchild of the Kibler/Rubin think tank. Initially, Ben Rubin designed the deck—he sent Brian Kibler a rough copy of the deck about a month before Grand Prix New Orleans. This was similar to their development of Super Gro deck in last year's extended environment. Kibler got a rough copy of the deck based on Rubin's skeleton. Up to the very last minute, the duo were tweaking the deck little by little. 

![Krosan Tusker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Krosan+Tusker)The biggest addition to the deck was [Krosan Tusker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Tusker). This allowed the deck to effectively have an uncounterable [Yavimaya Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Elder), one which can be recurred easily via [Volrath's Stronghold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath%27s+Stronghold). player to cycle to get lands through counterspells (as [Yavimaya Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Elder) can be countered). It also gives the deck additional monsters to Oath up.The deck is designed to beat a field U/G, The Rock, and red (both Red Deck Wins and Sligh). Pretty much any deck with creatures fares poorly against this collection of cards, with Kibler boasting a 90% win ratio against the Madness deck before sideboarding.

Ben and Brian decided that counterspells aren't that effective in this environment, opting instead to run several cheap black cards such as [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother). These were decided upon over the evolution of the deck, with [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) and [Avatar of Woe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Woe) barely missing the cut. 

[Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) ended up in the deck as an interesting answer to the metagame. Red decks might be able to get a quick start, and then finish the game off with [Blistering Firecat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat)—and neither [Diabolic Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Edict) nor [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) can usually deal with this threat. The [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) originally was [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) as a way to deal with [Verdant Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Force) when Reanimator ruled, but [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) was deemed equally able to handle that particular threat. 

![Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Haunting+Echoes)Another one-of card of interest is the [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes). It's mainly in the deck to combat The Rock. It gives a significant edge in the [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death) wars as multiple [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth)s on the other side of the board can negate a [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death) entirely otherwise. The [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes) tips this match far more in the favor of the B/G Oath deck . Against [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), a resolved [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes) mid-game can often leave an opponent with too few cards left in their deck to power the tog to 20.[Kamahl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl) came as a last minute addition on Friday night before the Grand Prix. The backup plan for the deck was to use [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) with the most versatile possible sideboard. With the addition of [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) main deck, not only could Kibler and Rubin tutor up important lands such as [Dust Bowl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Bowl) and [Volrath's Stronghold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath%27s+Stronghold), but they could have a near instant win by breaking open a creature stalemate through [Kamahl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl). Even more nastily, Oath of Beasts can throw a one sided [Armageddon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon) their opponent's way via [Kamahl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl) and [Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed).
