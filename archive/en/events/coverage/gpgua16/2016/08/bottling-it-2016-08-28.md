What properties of [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) can we appreciate from Yaso's masterpiece which he piloted to a second place finish at the 2012 Player's Championship? In a nutshell, here are the fundamental benefits.


* [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) provides mana advantage, allowing you to summon creatures for free while keeping your mana available for other business.
* [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) provides tempo advantage, allowing you to recruit multiple creatures ahead of schedule to overwhelm your opponents with.
* [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) offers mana fixing, allowing to put creatures onto the battle even if you don't have the correct amount of mana or the correct colored mana to cast it.
* [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) breaks the rules that creatures can only be cast at sorcery speed and effectively grants all of your creatures flash.
* [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) allows you to evade opponent's countermagic and sorcery speed removal. Since you're putting a creature into play directly, your opponents cannot use cards such as [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) or [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to counter it and neither will they be able to kill your creature with [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) or [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) if you "vial out" your creature at end of your opponent's turn.
* [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) allows you to disrupt your opponent's hand after the draw step. [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) can pluck away a card which your opponent freshly-drew, and if you use (spoiler alert!) [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) he won't even get to replace it immediately.

Now that we've taken a brief trip down memory lane, let us check out three decks which made it to the top tables here at Grand Prix Guangzhou. Coincidentally, they're all tribal decks as well!

The White-Blue Spirits deck has a very similar goal as Merfolk, to quickly whittle away at your opponent's life total while countering key spells and keeping your team alive. The idea is to use [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) to "gain" mana such that you're able to play two or more creatures on a single turn. For example, once you get two counters on [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial), you'll be able to summon both [Leonin Arbiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Arbiter) and [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) on turn three. Such a line of play forces your opponent to deal with multiple threats, but they may be unable to because they might be bottlenecked on mana during the early turns.


Free [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)s? Sign me up!


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flickerwisp)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Restoration+Angel)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)
"Vialing out" [Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp) is one of the most annoying plays you can imagine. Conveniently, it is also a Spirit which pumps [Mausoleum Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Wanderer). Not only can it remove an attacker or blocker temporarily (a lifesaver against [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)), it can also save your creatures from spot removal, preserving your army in the process. Han throws in [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) for more blinking goodness plus additional attackers in the sky. In conjunction with [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage), [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) and [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), Han has a lot of tools in his arsenal against various decks out there.

The caveat of playing a 4-Color deck is usually a painful manabase, since you'll usually take a lot of damage from Fetch lands and [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) lands. However, [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) mitigates a huge part of that because it happens to be an excellent mana fixer itself! [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) and [Sliver Hive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Hive) are also perfect since they are essentially [Mana Confluence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Confluence) without a drawback.

Last but not least, Cai's W/B Eldrazi has the same concept Legacy's infamous Death and Taxes archetype. However, he added black mana for a generous portion of hand disruption and removal. Functioning like a traditional Hatebear deck, it aims to disrupt with an assortment of annoying weenies. [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) is very upsetting to a combo or control player, as are [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) and [Tidehollow Sculler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thought-Knot+Seer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tidehollow+Sculler)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler)
The synergy between [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Wasteland Strangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland+Strangler) isn't new tech, but for it to be housed alongside [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) makes it a rather fresh prospect. To make things even crazier, [Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp) and [Eldrazi Displacer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Displacer) help you re-trigger [Wasteland Strangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland+Strangler) as well as [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer). In conjunction with the capabilities of your mighty bottle, you'll be able to present a ton of mischievous pranks at instant speed.
