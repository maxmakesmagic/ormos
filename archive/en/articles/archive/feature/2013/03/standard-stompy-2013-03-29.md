Standard Stompy
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 29, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 






Continuing the theme of exploring decks that work in multiple formats, I'm going to look at what passes for "mono-green" aggro in Standard these days. [I said it's rare](/en/articles/archive/stompy-2013-03-28) that mono-green can be effective in most Constructed formats, but ehhh managed to put up a decent result with this green-splash-red aggro deck.

![Predator Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Predator+Ooze) The primary reason to build a heavily green creature deck in Standard these days is [Predator Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator+Ooze), a card that can represent a serious problem for decks that don't bother to have a good answer to indestructible creatures. This deck backs that with mana creatures and huge green men in the form of [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) and the rarely played [Primordial Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra). 

 This deck makes up for green's lack of removal by using [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) and [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) to fight opposing creatures, as well as [Revenge of the Hunted](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revenge+of+the+Hunted) and [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) to push through them. If all else fails, [Predator Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator+Ooze) with a [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) should eventually get through most anything, as can [Primordial Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primordial+Hydra). 


[Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) is an interesting card to see in this deck, because the only two-mana spell the deck can cast with it other than another one is [Scorned Villager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorned+Villager). This means the mana will often go to waste. Or it would, except that this deck will sometimes have three mana on turn two, thanks to [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), in which case Burning Tree Emissary can actually function as a mana fixer, allowing [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) to be cast on turn two with only basic [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s. If there isn't an [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) involved, the same play can be made on turn three after doing something else on the second turn, like playing a [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist).
