Round 10 Feature Match - Justin Bing vs. Ken Bearl
==================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 12, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe












![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/922.jpg)At the top table in Round 10 sat the last two 9-0 competitors: Justin Bing, 22, and Ken Bearl, 23. Both are from Minnesota and have played each other before on the PTQ circuit, with Justin usually winning. Justin is an amateur that came into the tournament with 3 byes; he won a trial in Madison. Ken has a few Pro Points to his name; he played in PT London, PT New Orleans, and made the Top 16 at GP Minneapolis. At Minneapolis, Bearl was the last undefeated player at the beginning of Day 2, and he's hoping to duplicate that feat here.


Both players are running [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) decks, but Bing's eschews [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), opting instead for maindeck [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict)s and [Recoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil)s. Bearl's version is fairly typical aside from the two [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)s.


Bearl won the roll and played first. He was forced to mulligan a one-land double-[Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) draw. His new grip - five land and an [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) - was not significantly better, but in this slow match up it would suffice.


On turn 4, Bearl attempted a [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), but Bing had the counter. He tried another on turn 6, and Bing let it resolve. Bearl then upped the ante with a [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill), which would leave him with two cards in hand. Bing wasn't up for that, so he [Undermine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undermine)d it. Bing then [Recoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil)ed the Familiar, forcing Bearl to discard an Island. Bing then dropped his own creature, the [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), and used it's discard ability to facilitate a [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) on Bearl's [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). On Bing's next turn, he [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict)ed away the Familiar and attacked for one.


Bearl, with his back to the wall, had to cast his last spell, [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). Bing had the counter, and things started going downhill rapidly for Bearl. Bing put out a [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) which Bearl was forced to break with a [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), which Bing Logicked, and after a quick 1.5x + .5y + 1 computation, Bing swung in for the kill.


Bing 1 - Bearl 0


Bearl sided in Edicts, [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s, and [Mana Short](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short)s for most of his bounce and [Disrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disrupt)s. Bearl sided in [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)es.


Bing complained that he didn't like the attention of Feature Matches, so I passed on the advice of "Stop winning." We'll see if he listens or not.


Bing had to mulligan this time. He started the action with a turn-1 Duress, taking one of Bearl's two [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s, leaving him with two [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic)s and a Familiar. The Familiar came into play, but was quickly dispatched with [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict).


Bearl had another Familiar on his next turn, and then a [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) with [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay) backup; Bing knew about the [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay), but had to throw a [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) at the 'Tog anyway, just to try to deplete Bearl's hand.


Bing retaliated with a main-phase [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), which Bearl spilt into "Tog or No?" piles of [Cabal Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Pit), [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic), and [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) vs. two [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)s. Bing said no-thank-you to the 'Togs, and put out a Familiar to stop the beats.


He then played his own [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) (the third one - how lucky!) and a counter war ensued - Logic, [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay), Logic, Counter. The 'Tog resolved.


Bearl's last card was [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), however, so he calmly reset the board. His Familiar fell to [Cabal Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Pit), but his 'Tog stuck, and he was sitting on a pair of Edicts for Bing's Familiar and 'Tog. Bearl drew very well after the [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), and Bing could never stop him.


Ah, the [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) card.


Bing 1 - Bearl 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/923.jpg)Bing kept a hand with double Coliseum as his only blue sources, but drew into an Island early. He put out a quick Familiar which started attacking for 1. Across the table, Bearl was having mana issues - only three Islands, holding stuff like [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) and [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict). He had to discard once, then Bing decided to test the waters with a [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). He won the permission was easily, mostly because his [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s only cost 1 thanks to "Sexual Chocolate," the [Nightscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar).


The Familiar wasn't exactly pummeling Bearl, giving him time to draw black mana. Another counter war ensued over a Bing atog, but Bearl untapped and drew his second Edict, killing the Familiar and the [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog).


With no creatures in play, Bing managed to resolve a [Haunting Echoes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes), stripping Bearl's deck of all its Edicts, [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic)s, [Gainsay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gainsay)s, [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s, and [Mana Short](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short)s. The third and fourth [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)s were not in Bearl's library, however, meaning they were in his hand. The game was not over.


Bearl got one of the FoF's through, and Bing offered splits of Familiar + [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) vs. land + 2 [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). Bearl kept the former pile. He then FoF'ed AGAIN, and Bing responded with his own FoF. After much splitting and choosing, Bing got a [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) to stick. Bearl tried to [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), but with all the permission stripped from his deck, he could do nothing but watch as Bing countered it, then swung in for the kill with the atog.


Bing 2 - Bearl 1


Justin Bing is now the only 10-0 in the tournament.
