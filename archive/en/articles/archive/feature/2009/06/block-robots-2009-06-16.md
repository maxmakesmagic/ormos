Fears Naya and Jund would run Pro Tour–Honolulu rampant proved to be unfounded. Brian Kibler and Paul Rietzl both managed to make the Top 8 with an innovative Esper deck that focused on abusing [Thopter Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Foundry) to get around the prime removal available in the *Shards of Alara* Block Constructed format. For more about Pro Tour–Honolulu, including all the top decklists, [read the official event coverage](/en/events/coverage/mitamura-finds-gold-paradise).
