Demon's Rise
============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 27, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I haven't seen a [Homicidal Seclusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homicidal+Seclusion) in play since *Avacyn Restored* Draft, so imagine my surprise when I found a deck with multiple copies of it in Standard. The sweetness doesn't end there, as this deck also sports a couple other cards that have recently seen their popularity rise: [Haunted Plate Mail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Plate+Mail) and [Demonic Rising](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Rising). 


![Demonic Rising](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Demonic+Rising)
 This deck has plenty of the mono-black aspects you would expect, with [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate), [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood), and removal spells all providing a good base. Where it diverges from the norm is the "loner" package, with [Demonic Rising](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Rising), [Homicidal Seclusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homicidal+Seclusion), and [Haunted Plate Mail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Plate+Mail) all taking advantage of the "control one creature" mechanic. 


 There are a number of fantastic combos here, many of which rely on [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and [Haunted Plate Mail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Plate+Mail) being able to turn themselves on or off as required. Playing [Demonic Rising](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Rising), activating a [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) or [Haunted Plate Mail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Plate+Mail) and getting a Demon is awesome, but it gets even better when you decline to activate the next turn and get a second Demon. [Homicidal Seclusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homicidal+Seclusion) ensures that you will have plenty of life (and therefore time) to have your plans come into fruition, and gains you near-infinite life if you happen to equip a Demon with a [Haunted Plate Mail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Plate+Mail). 



[Lifebane Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie) is the only actual creature in the deck, mainly because it provides an incredible amount of value against some threats this deck would otherwise have problems with ([Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) is the main one). It's also the only creature that dies to [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate), with [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and [Haunted Plate Mail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Plate+Mail) conveniently dodging your Wrath with ease.
