Round 16: You're Fired!
=======================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 17, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Tim Willoughby












[![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/arrowL.png)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/worlds11/fm15)
  

Richard Bland  
(Punishing Zoo)  

vs.   
Ian Wood   
(Zoo)

[![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/arrowr.png)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/worlds11/fm17)
  
[David Caplan  

(CounterCat)  

vs.  

Janus Kofoed  

(Bant-Zoo)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/worlds11/fm17) 

Richard Bland has had quite the trip to the USA. The Englishman finished second at Grand Prix San Diego, and has followed up that finish with a run at Worlds that leaves him needing two wins out of the next three rounds in order to make top eight. Considering that he started Day One at 1-3, this is no easy feat.


His opponent Ian Wood of Australia is in similar position for the tournament. While each has room for a loss, neither would be keen to have to win out in the dying stages in the tournament.




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/r16_bland_vs_wood.jpg)

*Both decks are Zoo, but one of them has a surprise for the mirror match!*

  

 


### Game 1


Both players had Zoo as their weapon of choice for the Modern format, certainly a popular decision today. It was Wood who was on the play, leading with [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) into [Temple Garden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden), for a first turn [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). Bland had just an [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) for his first turn, and looked on as Wood followed up his Hierarch with a [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage).


Bland's [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) found a [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) tapped, keeping the Englishman ahead on life, at least initially. He had a  [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Misty+Rainforest+)  for a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), and cast a [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) to take out [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). Bland had expressly tuned his version of Zoo to be good for the mirror, a match he expected a great deal of. Having dispatched Bing Luke in a Zoo mirror earlier, he looked good to keep doing well in the matchup, dropping a [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) on the table almost nonchalantly.


When Bland tried to off [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) with [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire), Wood responded by taking it out himself with a [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), eager to get more lands into play. He found a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), and on his turn cast a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), one of the few creatures that would not be impacted by the [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) plan in a big way.


Bland's plan for [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) was simple. He cast his own, along with a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl). This simple plan was met with a simple [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). Bland would probably have preferred to have a monster on the board, but he didn't have that option. Wood cleared a path for [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) using a [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) on [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl), before playing one of his own. When Bland returned [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire), it put the life totals to 22-11 in the Australian's favor.




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/r16_bland.jpg)

*Richard Bland's [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire)s have been putting in work against his Zoo opponents coming in to this round. Would Wood be able to combat the fiery synergy with the Zendikar red spell and [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows)?* 


Bland cast the first [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) of the match (a 5/5) and then a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) (a 3/4). The Englishman wasn't about to lie down to the Australian assault. Wood had a third [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and used it on [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary). Bland now had enough mana available to start casting multiple copies of [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) a turn, and having returned the Fire already, it seemed likely that he would do so while [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) wasn't too big.


Attacks took Bland to 7, and a fetchland made it 6. Bland had a second [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows), but it was unclear if he would have time to best employ it. After a little thought, Bland cast a [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) and passed.


Wood had Elspeth, [Knight Errant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Errant), which forced Bland to use his [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) on [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) twice to keep it from flying in. Bland then double blocked [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) to let the two creatures trade. Suddenly the board was looking a lot clearer, and Bland compounded this by attacking and burning out Elspeth, and killing off [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch).


The tide looked to have turned, but Bland was still on just 6, while his opponent was on 20. A [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) put Rich on just 3. Wood had a  [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Knight+of+the+Reliquary+) , which ate some [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire), and Bland set to attacking that big number before Wood could find a burn spell. [Stirring Wildwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stirring+Wildwood) and Elspeth helped that clock greatly. Soon Wood was on 11. The Australian drew for his turn and...didn't have anything. It was on to game two.


Bland 1, Wood 0


  

 


### Game 2


Going into the second game, Wood had to take a mulligan, and lamented not having [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) in his deck. Given the way that the field at the top tables looks, it would have been a great choice. Wood led with an [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa), while Bland had a [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) for [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl). Wood was quick to kill off the cat with a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) before it got a chance to start swinging. Bland was a little slower in dealing with [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), taking one hit before he found a [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to stop all of that nonsense.


A 4/4 [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) from Bland outmatched the 3/3 one that Wood could muster, though the Australian did have a [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) at hand if he decided he needed to pump it. That need came along quickly, as Bland pointed a [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) at it. He followed up with a Knight activation, to fetch and use [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) for a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). Suddenly Bland's [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) was a 6/6, and to cap it off, he had the [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) to finish off Wood's Knight.


With each passing play it looked less likely that there would be a Wood and a Woods in the Worlds Top 8. A [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) let Bland start getting back his [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire), right on time to fight off an Elspeth, [Knight Errant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Errant). Wood had a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl), but Bland had a second [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary). Rich's knights got to work on filtering through the lands in his deck, making them each into 7/7 monsters.




![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds11/r16_wood.jpg)

*The dream of a Woods versus Wood match-up in the Top 8 is slowly disappearing.*


Knowing he was out of cards in hand, and facing insurmountable pressure, Woods extended his hand. "You've got it man."


Bland 2, Wood 0
