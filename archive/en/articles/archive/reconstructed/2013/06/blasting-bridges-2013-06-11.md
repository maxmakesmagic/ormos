Blasting Bridges
================



 Posted in **Reconstructed**
 on June 11, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






*Dredge.*



The utterance of that single word may conjure up some memories—either good or bad, depending on if you were the Dredge player or its opponent. One of the strangest decks of all time, Dredge plays more out of its graveyard than anywhere else, using its graveyard almost like a primary hand. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/rc/rc251_lifefromtheloam.jpg)
[Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) | Art by [Terese Nielsen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Terese%20Nielsen%22%5D)


 There are a lot of archetypes seeded in *Modern Masters*, and dredge is one of them. Dredge hasn't done a lot in Modern so far, showing up a few times as a fringe result. Once the stuff of nightmares, it is now reduced to an exhibit in the history books. But it is certainly one of those decks that is just waiting for one thing to break—one more crucial card to be printed—to head over the top and into Constructed playability... 

 While most Dredge lists lean toward the traditional, today's deck takes Dredge in a direction unlike any I've ever seen before. It immediately caught my eye as something original and unique—and with plenty of potential for power—unlike *any* Dredge deck I've seen in the eight years since the mechanic was released. I was instantly enticed and built this deck up on **Magic Online** to play games and see how it worked. 

Let's take a look:

### The Battle Plan

What is Dredge?

 Well, the dredge mechanic lets you replace your draws by "drawing" a card out of your graveyard. The "cost" to doing this is to put a bunch of cards from your library into your graveyard—but in a deck built around it, that's hardly a cost at all! Featuring plenty of cards that want to be put into your graveyard, like the oddballs [Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below) and [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba), this deck can quickly speed through its library. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Bridge from Below | Narcomoeba |

 Usually, with Dredge, the endgame is reanimating something via [Dread Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) (and in Modern, where that's banned, [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites)) or attacking with a bunch of Zombies created by [Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below). (Or with a [Flame-Kin Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame-Kin+Zealot), or sometimes both!) This deck does things a little differently. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Unburial Rites | Flame-Kin Zealot |

 You see, it's all about the new fuse card [Beck & Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck+%26+Call). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/rc/rc251_beckncall.jpg)  
 Unlike [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) before it, [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck) draws you a card every time a creature enters the battlefield. A creature like, say... a [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba), [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), or a Zombie token! And, since we're playing Dredge, every draw translates into a dredge, flipping more cards into the graveyard. When this deck goes off, it goes off *big*, flipping most of its library into the graveyard and creating ten to twenty (or more!) Zombies in a single turn. 

 And that's where [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) comes in. 


[Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) has a couple roles. First, it gives you a free sacrifice outlet for all of your [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s and [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba)s. It's a bit odd to say that you just want a free way to get rid of one of your creatures, but Dredge is an odd deck. You want your [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s to go away so you can bring them back yet again, netting you tokens from [Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below) every time. 


[Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) also just lets you kill your opponent in one turn. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Blasting Station | Bloodghast |

 As I mentioned before, when this deck goes off, it goes off big. If you just chuck every Zombie you make at your opponent's noggin as you're going off, it will often be enough to kill him or her. This won't always be the case—you have to evaluate about how many Zombies you're going to make when you start—but I've had plenty of turns where I've made enough Zombies, [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s, and [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba)s to outright ping my opponent to death with the station. (Keep in mind that it untaps every single time a creature enters the battlefield.) 

 In addition to your big combo finish, you can also always just beat your opponent by beating down with [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba)s and [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s, making a bunch of 2/2 Zombies over time, and "grinding out" a game. It just depends on your draw. The operations of this deck take some practice, but soon you'll be Blasting your opponents out in no time. 

### Deck Breakdown

There's a lot going on in this deck, and all of the pieces work together to create a larger goal. Let's go over them.



![Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodghast)




[Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) is one of the integral pieces of this deck. As a free creature, it comes back out of your graveyard, drawing a card off of [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck), and can be sacrificed to create Zombies. With fetch lands like [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) and [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest), you can even double up on [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) uses, bringing it back, drawing a card, sacrificing it, making Zombies, then searching for another land to do it all again! I wouldn't play fewer than four copies. 




![Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Narcomoeba)



 Ever since *Future Sight* was released and the Dredge deck was truly born, [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba) has been at its center. A 1/1 flier may not look like too much, but a free 1/1 flier goes a long way in this deck. Like [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), it draws you a card off of [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck) and can help make Zombies with [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station). You definitely want all four. 




![Stinkweed Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stinkweed+Imp)


![88994](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=88994)



 These cards bodies aren't that impressive and, while you may cast them on occasion, that's not the reason why they're there. (Though it is worth noting that [Golgari Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Thug) can put [Narcomoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcomoeba) back on top of your library, which can be pretty useful.) Mostly, it's because these creatures sport the dredge mechanic and you can use them to flip several cards from your deck into your graveyard. 

 I definitely want to play these eight cards. Additionally, I'd like to play a couple more dredgers. [Dakmor Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dakmor+Salvage) is good in this deck because you need to hit land drops to return your [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), and I'd like to add a second. A singleton [Darkblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) can help you while going off, either killing your own creatures in lieu of a [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) or, if you can find it early, keeping opposing creature rushes under control. 



![Hedron Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hedron+Crab)



 Mister Crabs is a strange card in that it just serves to target yourself and dump a ton of cards into your graveyard. A turn-one [Hedron Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab) into a fetch land means six cards will tumble into your graveyard. It's not hard for a single card to flip twelve or even sixteen cards in this deck. 


 While normally I'd be very happy with that result, I found [Hedron Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab) to be underperforming in this style of Dredge deck. This deck was already flipping most of its library when going off, and several turns I found myself actively choosing to not dredge because my graveyard was plenty stocked and I wanted to try and find a specific card. It surprised me that I wanted to cut this card, but I did. 

 The card to replace it is a card I knew I wanted to add: [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting). [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) was always a Dredge staple, and [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) is that with the potential of a flashback. Whether letting you discard cards early or dredging twice, Looting goes a long way toward this deck's consistency. (Especially since it really wants to naturally draw a [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck) to play optimally.) 



![Drowned Rusalka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drowned+Rusalka)



 Despite the weak look, [Drowned Rusalka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowned+Rusalka) was one of the cards I was most overwhelmed by when playing the deck. I had originally expected that [Hedron Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab) would be awesome and Rusalka would be the card I would cut based on previous Dredge experience—but the opposite was true. When you're returning [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s every turn, [Drowned Rusalka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowned+Rusalka) can generate so many extra cards. 


 Additionally, it's a sacrifice outlet that isn't [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station). In games where you have to go into "grind mode" and win by attacking with creatures and Zombies, it's an important asset to have around. 



![Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bridge+from+Below)



 This very odd **Magic** card is one of the keys to this deck, generating tons of extra cards via [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck) and an army that you can either choose to attack with or fling away via [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station). I definitely want to play all four. 


My largest advice with this card is just to familiarize yourself with how it works. It's an incredibly odd card with lots of corner case interactions, so make sure you know how it works and how to stack the triggers if you're going to take this deck to a tournament.



![Beck // Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Beck+%2F%2F+Call)



 Another one of the deck's centerpieces, this newcomer from *Dragon's Maze* works overtime here. You can't cast the [Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call) side (and I never found a situation where I wanted to,) but [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck) is pretty crazy with all of the creatures you produce. Once again, keep in mind that you can dredge off of all of these draws too! I always want to draw exactly one and there are plenty of ways to pitch extras, so I'm keeping all four. 




![Ideas Unbound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ideas+Unbound)



 Cards like [Ideas Unbound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ideas+Unbound) are great in this deck, providing consistency while also putting more cards into your graveyard. Between [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Ideas Unbound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ideas+Unbound), I feel confident that I can find most of the pieces I need. You could consider going down to three, but I value consistency highly enough that I want to keep all four. 




![Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blasting+Station)




[Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) serves two primary roles in this deck. 


 The first is to sacrifice as many of your creatures as you want. Whenever a new creature enters the battlefield [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) untaps, meaning that you can continually chuck [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s and the ilk at your opponent's head to get them off the table and generate more Zombies. 

 However, that's not the only reason to play it. If I just wanted a sacrifice outlet, I would play [Spawning Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit) (which works great with [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck)) or [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer). 

 The second role, then, is that [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) allows you to kill your opponent in one turn by throwing all your Zombies (and other creatures) at your opponent to deal lethal damage in one fell swoop! Taking one fewer turn to defeat your opponent is something I prize highly. With some draws, [Spawning Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit) might speed up the deck by a turn anyway, but overall I like cutting my opponent off from a turn enough that I'd rather have the Station. (Plus, in a pinch, you can always remove an opponent's creature that's causing problems!)

 I made a couple tweaks to the mana base. You'll notice that I cut the [Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor). While it seems cute with [Beck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck), [Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below), and [Drowned Rusalka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowned+Rusalka), I never actually found a situation where I wanted to search for it and so I would rather give that slot to something else. 

 If you wanted to play a slower version of this deck, one route you could go down is to utilize a [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) engine with all of the landfall triggers. Then I would consider playing a [Buried Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Ruin) to return back your [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station) when necessary.

 Another card to consider is [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). I would definitely sideboard it, and if you can find the room I would love to main-deck it. This deck is vulnerable to other combo decks since I found it to generally kill on turn five, and many Modern combo decks are turn-four kills. [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) would help out some in that regard.
