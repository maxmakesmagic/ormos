Superbeast
==========



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on September 6, 2011 










I'd like to share the "Word of the Day" today: *serendipitous*. Here is the definition, according to [reference.com](http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/serendipitous):

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf159_def.jpg)  
1. come upon or found by accident; fortuitous: *serendipitous scientific discoveries*.
2. of, pertaining to, or suggesting [serendipity](http://dictionary.reference.com/browse/serendipity).
3. good; beneficial; favorable: *a serendipitous occurrence for our preview*.

That last example isn't an exact quote from the site, but it is an accurate statement for today. Hence our word for the moment.

You see, I'm a tokens kind of guy. Managing multiples on mostly corporeal critters is something I enjoy regularly. [Rhys the Redeemed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) is my favorite legendary pick for Commander. [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) is one of my favorite cards, and it inspired me to write up a little [soft set of puzzles](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/157) (and corresponding [solutions](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/158)). Anything that makes token strategies stronger (like [Caged Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caged+Sun)), creates tokens of things on the battlefield (like [Spitting Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Image)), or just loves tokens altogether (like [Twilight Drover](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twilight+Drover)) makes me happy.

Today's trip to *Innistrad* shares something that feeds all those things *and more*. One feature of tokens that's often overlooked is the *sameness* of them. All those white Soldier tokens are just 1/1 dudes. All those green Wolf tokens are 2/2s. What if the same thing that helps tokens get better also homogenized creatures too?

It's essential for you to click to see this:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/tcg/products/isd/toc4ggtokl_en.jpg)  
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/gresdfguyujknaybvytgcdfgjvdmayhsd.jpg)Essence of the Wild | Art by [Terese Nielsen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Terese+Nielsen%22%5D)

Essence of the Wild isn't your average creature. While it hints at [Mirror Entity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) and [Humility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Humility) in some ways, it's an entirely different card—a weird, wonderfully awesome card. Welcome to another day in *Innistrad*.

The Same Difference
Let's be honest for a moment: this newfangled Avatar is a little goofy. It has a few interactions that should be cleared out upfront.


> * Every creature that enters the battlefield under your control does so as a copy of Essence of the Wild. (Hooray tokens!)
> * Because every new creature is a copy of Essence of the Wild, your creatures with abilities of all shapes and sizes (from [Sylvan Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger) to [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) to [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton)) won't have them.
> * Each creature that enters the battlefield is a redundant back up to the original; killing the original Essence of the Wild doesn't stop more creatures from becoming it so long as at least a single copy remains.
> 

This is a bit of a mixed bag, then. All your subsequent creatures are 6/6s that make all of your subsequent creatures 6/6s, but they don't have any other abilities working for you. A restriction like that can be interesting, though...

Let me sing to you that this deck has got it going on. It's a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck, which means we're planning to chain up through creatures for fun (and likely profit). We start with some cheaper mana-getters, like [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger), then chain up through [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) and [Urabrask the Hidden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urabrask+the+Hidden). What happens after that can be something curious:



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |


> * If we're loaded down with a hand of more small critters, we can pop out Essence of the Wild and start making one- to three-mana 6/6 creatures. (Including using [Beast Within](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within) on one of your own lands!)
> * If we're pressing the advantage, we can chain up through Essence to grab [Thorn Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn+Elemental). *Boom, headshot*!
> * If we're not in a position to attack, we can grab [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) and [Bronze Bombshell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bronze+Bombshell). Sprinkle [Noxious Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Revival) as needed.
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

What makes me really smile about shoving the [Bazaar Trader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+Trader) / [Bronze Bombshell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bronze+Bombshell) combo into a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck are the multiplayer possibilities that open up to it. Need to help a teammate with mana? Here, have a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). Your friend with the weenie deck wants to cast creatures with oomph? Try this Essence of the Wild!

Of course, if you *only* play multiplayer you'll want to change this deck up a bit. [Yavimaya Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Elder) can replace [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) (but can be given to allies as well), and [Hornet Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen) can replace Thorn Elemental. For some convoluted, [Noel deCordova](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Noel%20deCordova)–style interactions, try adding [Ensnaring Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) to your deck, then giving Essence of the Wild to *opponents*: there will be good odds that they won't be able to cast new creatures that can attack you. The possibilities are wild!

It's time to flip out and loop an undead army as Avatars. [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) is our tutor, turning usual graveyard suspects [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and [Reassembling Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton) into Essence of the Wild. [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) and [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) keep the mana flowing to let us cast our Avatar. Then, the fun starts. Once Essence of the Wild is down, rebuying a Skeleton or [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) nets us a 6/6 instead—that can come back again as it reverts to its return-from-the-graveyard base case upon death.

Now this is a way to lay the sixes down! It's a "token" deck, obviously, and it's filled with requisite representatives of such decks. [Rhys the Redeemed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed), [Cenn's Enlistment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cenn%27s+Enlistment), [Imperious Perfect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect), [Selesnya Evangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Evangel), and more make an appearance. The "slick" interaction of [Cream of the Crop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cream+of+the+Crop) turns token making into scry time, and Elspeth and Garruk headline the main event.

Or so your opponent thinks.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf159_cream.jpg)  
[Chord of Calling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) is usually scary in token decks, so [Fierce Empath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fierce+Empath) plays back up to let us grab Essence of the Wild. Once that's out all sorts of silly things happen. Every token enters the battlefield as a 6/6, making [Cream of the Crop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cream+of+the+Crop) even more absurd. [Mercy Killing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mercy+Killing) lets you convert one 6/6 into *six* 6/6s. [Fists of Ironwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fists+of+Ironwood) gives one 6/6 trample with a bonus two more 6/6s stapled on. Even [Vitu-Ghazi, the City-Tree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitu-Ghazi%2C+the+City-Tree) is fearsome with you're paying four mana for a 6/6 instead.

I find Prerelease events, like those [coming up for *Innistrad*](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtgcom/events/prerelease-facts), to be a great time to introduce new **Magic** friends to one of the oldest **Magic** traditions: playing in events. Not everyone can attend every event, but making the time to meet and greet more than just a few local friends is only asking for a good time!

And having a good time is an excellent segue to a call I'm placing to players like you: *give me your stories*. I'd like to hear how your local groups meets, what you play, how that started, and what works (or doesn't) about it. If you're attending an *Innistrad* Prerelease or Launch Party, what did you think? How did that go?

It's not as much stories about individual cards I'm looking for (although those are just fine too!), but the players you meet and experiences you create. Many of the requests I get through feedback are related to building play groups and keeping the fun alive.
