Sealed Deck Building With Martin Jůza
=====================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX LYON 2015](/en/events/coverage/gplyo15)
 on October 31, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











This Gold-level pro already had a staggering 23 Grand Prix Top 8s to his name, but he was here in Lyon in the hope of adding the 24th to his resúmé. As one of the eleven players in the room with three byes, I sat down to observe his process for building a *Battle for Zendikar* Sealed Deck.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gplyo15/LYO_sdb_juza1.jpg)  
*Martin Jůza*


To start, here is the complete pool that Jůza received. It's formatted as a single deck to allow you to load up the list on *Magic Online* and try to build it yourself; a more easily digestible color-by-color overview will be provided afterward.

Given that Jůza was no fan of 2/2s for 2, green didn't offer much to him except for the pair of [Tajuru Beastmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tajuru+Beastmaster)s, which could go well with his [Resolute Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resolute+Blademaster)s.


The first deck that Jůza laid out was Red/White Allies with a low mana curve and a small green splash. All Eldrazis were moved to the side, and it looked like a decent deck. But with more than enough time left on the clock, Jůza wanted to explore his other options, and he laid out a focused White/Blue Fliers deck with a small red splash.


Unsure what the best build was, he sleeved up both decks, drew opening hands, and played a few sample games. Ultimately, Jůza decided to go for Red/White Allies.

"I think I had exactly enough Allies to make it work," Jůza told me after he finished registering his deck. "Although I'm not a huge fan of cards like [Expedition Envoy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Envoy), the fact that it all worked together with my uncommon Allies was good enough for me. White/Blue Fliers looked nice at first, but lacked ways to win and would face problems closing out a game."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gplyo15/LYO_sdb_juza2.jpg)


"I think I will have a hard time winning games without them," Jůza said but that, if he drew a [Resolute Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resolute+Blademaster), his deck would be very powerful. During the byes, I watched Jůza play several practice games where he managed to curve out perfectly with early creatures into [Ondu Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ondu+Champion) and [Resolute Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resolute+Blademaster), and he crushed his opponents with them.


"I considered playing red-white without the green," Jůza explained. But he decided against it because the green cards offered him additional ways to break through a board stall and to trigger his [Resolute Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resolute+Blademaster)s. His list had two Forests, which along with the two [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) gave him four sources of green mana. "That's enough for a 4-drop and two 6-drops." Given that he had a double-white card in [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare) and a double-red card in [Rolling Thunder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Thunder), he didn't want to risk drawing multiple Forests. His heavy color requirements were also the reason why [Blighted Gorge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Gorge) and [Exert Influence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exert+Influence) didn't make his main deck.


"Akoum Stonewalker and [Tandem Tactics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tandem+Tactics) were among the last cards that I cut from my deck," Jůza said. "There is a limit on how many early creatures and aggressive cards you can play, and my other early drops were better."


After seven rounds, Jůza had managed a 6-1 record, which meant that he would only would have to win one out of his next two matches to make it to the second day of competition. "My deck is pretty good and in the first three rounds my opponents didn't put up much resistance. But I just lost to an opponent who used [Oblivion Sower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Sower) to ramp into a [Quarantine Field](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quarantine+Field) that exiled my whole board," Jůza said. "The only thing I might change about my deck, in hindsight, is to possibly play [Akoum Stonewaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Stonewaker) over [Courier Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courier+Griffin). But overall my deck is fine."
