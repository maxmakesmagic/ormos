So These Are the Good Guys?
===========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











The world is strange.

  
Not in the "It's a strange world / let's keep it that way" sense of a British Lord of the Jungle's superpowered German daughter on Monster Island. Strange more like black and blue being the good guys.

*I know!*

You ever think about who we consider the good guys (at least versus "the bad guys")? No one ever wags their fingers at Naya. Naya, with their little green monsters and... not a lot else. Naya is the good guys! But swap white for blackâ€”all of a sudden we are talking about the most dangerous forces to ever pull young people towards underage drinking and smoking.

VanMeter "only" finished ninth at last week's Star City Games Open (so just out of the Top 8), but he did so with a deck that is pretty different from most of what we have been looking at in Standard... at least since the rotation of *Shards of Alara* block.

**Old Stuff**

VanMeter's deck is a [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) deck. [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) is there as a consistent two-drop, but one that can both set up [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s and set up bullets. Yes, the [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) package is weakened by the rotation of [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), but it is still a super powerful threat... and one that is once again well positioned in the format. A few months ago, there were nothing but Titans in every deck. Some decks even had *eight* Titans! Big 6/6 Titans were like walking stop signs for a speedy 4/3, and while there are still Titans in the format, there are fewer, potentially opening up a window for [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

While Naya can no longer lean on big sister [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), it has still got [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) as a potential source of [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) re-buy. [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)'s synergy is pretty beautiful here. One [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) activation can dump a [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) and find one [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk), and as we all know, one [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) can be the same as all four [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s; and on four mana, two Hawks are about the same as a [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), mana wise and re-buy wise, anywise... er... any*way*.

**New Stuff**

VanMeter's Naya has all four copies of [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic). Yes, [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) has been a stalwart of Naya decks since Tom Ross and Luis Scott-Vargas's amazing Swiss run in [San Diego](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptsd10/welcome), but Naya decks, especially [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) versions, have rarely if ever chosen to run all four. Now, however, we see Naya borrowing from the success of white-blue and red-white archetypes with four [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s and four [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)s. Though he was eventually taken out by StarCityGames.com Open Series hero Gerry Thompson with DarkBlade (Caw-Blade splashing Black), Kibler and his Blue-Black Infect deck battled to third place.

This is a remarkably inventive and surprisingly effective weapon of choice.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td136_phyCrusader.jpg)  
A huge incentive to playing this strategy is [Phyrexian Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader). The little black poisoner is remarkably well positioned in a format where seemingly everyone is playing four copies of [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) and four copies of [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic). They can't block, and you kill very quickly. Similarly, Boros-type decks with their [Plated Geopede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede)s and [Goblin Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)s can't get through, and can't... you know... block.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

As awesome as [Phyrexian Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader) and [Skithiryx, the Blight Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skithiryx%2C+the+Blight+Dragon) are offensively, Kibler's deck is a bit light on offense. It's kind of like a Black-Blue Control deck that has opted to play [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) rather than [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor).

Even if you get past the superb positioning of [Phyrexian Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader), infect's unique positioning here really comes to the forefront when you consider the presence of [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

In this deck, a single scratch from an [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) can eventually fester into a lethal case of poisoning, given sufficient time. (Like I said before... *this* is the modern-day good guy?) But [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp) does a ton of other awesome stuff, too.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td136_tumble.jpg)  
The power of [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) in this format almost can't be exaggerated. [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) shuts down mighty Titans and, more importantly given the supreme power of [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), locks down an opponent's [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) (plus another card, at least in the short term). The downside of [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) is that it can run out of counters... but not with [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp)! [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp) not only increases the poison counters killing an opponent to death, but it can keep [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) going indefinitely. There are other counter-driven permanents in this deckâ€”e.g., [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren)â€”so [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp) always has something to do (you know, after it gets done killing [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) to death).

Blue-Red-Green
Blue-Red-Green remains one of the most popular decks in the Standard metagame. Though it is one of the (seemingly) few decks that lacks second turn [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) and [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s, Blue-Red-Green still has the mighty [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), that explosive [Dark Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual) with legs (well, figuratively).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td136_cobra.jpg)  
Blue-Red-Green can command from a diverse base of threats, the best in Titans (and continually chooses [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan)), the best in planeswalkers, the lightning-quick [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), the ability to counter target spell, and... well... almost anything it wants.
