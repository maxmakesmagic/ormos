Round 12 Feature Match: Tsuyoshi Ikeda vs. Katsuhiro Mori
=========================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 1, 2002 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Sideboard Staff











This is it: the final match of the day. At 24 points each, there's a chance that neither player can make it to the Top 8 evenif they win, but that doesn't mean they're not going to try. After a solemn handshake, Mori and Ikeda began shuffling their decks.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat02/924.jpg)Mori kept a starting hand with only 2 Swamps, but lucked out with his first draw, netting a Forest that allowed him to play [Harvester Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvester+Druid). Ikeda summoned a [Cephalid Scout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Scout), thinking it could hold off the Druid, but Mori powered it up with [Elephant Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) and began knocking down Ikeda's life total. Trying to play out his hand, Ikeda made a [Krosan Restorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Restorer). Mori didn't let up, removing it immediately with [Crippling Fatigue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue) while keeping up the Druid beatdown. Desparate to get rid of the superhuman Druid, Ikeda made a [Dreamwinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamwinder) and traded it for the Druid. However, that didn't change the board situation much--instead of a 4/4 Druid, he still had to deal with a 3/3 Elephant. Lucking out and drawing another [Dreamwinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamwinder), he was able to stabilize the board, but was already running precariously low on life and cards. Unrelenting, Mori produced in quick succession a [Soul Scourge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge) and [Nantuko Cultivator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Cultivator). Ikeda was able to hold the line on the ground with a [Nullmage Advocate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nullmage+Advocate) and [Cartographer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartographer), but couldn't do anything about Mori's flier and scooped.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat02/923.jpg)Mori kept his tempo from the first game with a first turn [Diligent Farmhand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diligent+Farmhand) and a second turn [Putrid Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Imp), but Ikeda was able to nullify his offensive with a third turn [Nullmage Advocate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nullmage+Advocate). The standoff continued, as Mori produced a [Krosan Restorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Restorer) and an [Ironshell Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ironshell+Beetle) (powering up the Imp), only to have Ikeda counter with a [Hydromorph Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydromorph+Guardian). In the end, the deciding factor of the match was luck: Ikeda ran out of lands and began discarding, while Mori's deck offered up a stream of game-swinging cards: [Rabid Elephant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rabid+Elephant), [Morbid Hunger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morbid+Hunger), [Arcane Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Teachings), [Seton's Desire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seton%27s+Desire), [Crippling Fatigue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue)... Drawing only one land from the start of the game, Ikeda could only shake his head in disbelief at his poor luck, begrudglingly offering his hand to Mori.

**Results: Mori wins, 2-0**
