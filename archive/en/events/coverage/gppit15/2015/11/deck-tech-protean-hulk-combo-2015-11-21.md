Deck Tech: Protean Hulk Combo
=============================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 21, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











Zac Elsik is no stranger to playing new decks in Modern. The Grand Prix Oklahoma City champion was among the first to bring Lantern Control to the forefront of Modern, and after a deep run in Charlotte and then a title in Oklahoma City, he put it squarely on the map.


So it may be a little surprising to hear he's not playing [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) this weekend.


“Yeah, we did that already,” he said with a small laugh at Grand Prix Pittsburgh. “It was time for something else, and I think this deck is really good.”


Don't worry, Elsik isn't conforming to the norm; the deck he's referring to is [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) combo.


**[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=PROTEAN+HULK)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=PROTEAN+HULK)**


Elsik is one of a few dozen players who showed up in Pennsylvania sporting copies of the *Dissension* beast, aiming to combo off with its ability. The basic loop is this: When [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) dies, search out [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) and [Body Double](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double), which copies the [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) in the graveyard. Sacrifice the [Body Double](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double) to search out [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) and [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic), which you sacrifice to deal damage to the opponent. From there, you can loop [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark), [Body Double](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double) and [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) to deal arbitrary amounts of damage to the opponent.


The most common way to get Hulk into play in the first place is [Footsteps of the Goryo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Footsteps+of+the+Goryo). Discard outlets [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) and even [Taigam's Scheming](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taigam%27s+Scheming) get Hulk into the graveyard, and Footsteps or [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) brings it back.


The combo itself isn't new, but the streamlining of the deck is. Justin Maguire put the deck on the map with a Top 16 finish at a StarCityGames Modern Open three weeks ago, and it's caught on in the weeks since. While older versions used [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) as an alternate way to get Hulk into play, [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) has been popularized recently thanks to its resilience to [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). The move also makes the mana slightly better and allows the deck to get away from [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism), which had become a liability in a world full of [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) and [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse).


What's even more impressive about the deck is its consistency. Once the combo begins, opponents can't interact. With proper management of its creatures and triggers, the Hulk player's combo is immune from spot removal like [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) or [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


“Mannequin isn't necessarily better than Breach, but it's important that it makes the mana better and lets you avoid artifact hate,” Elsik explained. “It's really good because of how consistent it is, plus the games where you just win on the third turn.”


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gppit15/gppit15_elsik-01.jpg)**  
*Elsik's deck makes great use the seldom-used Taigam's Scheming, filling its graveyard to combo. Hulk Combo is extremely difficult for opponents to stop, and can happen as early as the third turn.*


As powerful as the deck is, it has a few weaknesses, some of which can be addressed on some camp. [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity), for instance, prevents [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) from finishing the job. That's why [Thopter Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Engineer) makes an appearance in the sideboard, providing the ability to make as many hasty thopters as necessary to end the game in an attack step.


The bigger problem is graveyard hate. While Hulk manages to dodge most traditional forms of removal and has access to counterspells in [Swan Song](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swan+Song) and [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) to protect its combo, cards like [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) and [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) present major problems. There's no easy sideboard answer to this problem, but Elsik said the deck's resiliency more than makes up for it.


“It's better than the other graveyard decks because you have more filtering,” he said. “You get to play blue, so you can find the spells you need, whether that's a combo piece or a sideboard card like [Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) to set up your combo. Also, unlike [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), this deck doesn't have a failure rate once you start your combo.”


It may have begun the weekend under the radar, but one thing is for sure: Hulk Combo is quickly sinking itself deep into the Modern metagame, and the footsteps of the Goryo are growing ever louder.
