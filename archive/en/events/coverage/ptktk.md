SUNDAY COVERAGE



For Gold-level pro Ari Lax, his first Pro Tour Top 8 was a long time coming.


While the Boston resident has had many close calls in breaking through to the Pro Tour Sunday stage, including a 9th place finish at Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*, it wasn't until this weekend that he finally got his big opportunity.


Lax's Abzan Midrange deck was one of the key archetypes to emerge out of the Pro Tour. With big cards like Siege Rhino and Wingmate Roc, Lax was able to power though tough competition in all three rounds of the quarterfinals. After dispatching 11th-ranked Yuuya Watanabe in the Quarterfinals, he successfully got by *Magic Online* regular Thiago Saporito in the Semifinals thanks to a timely Elspeth, Sun's Champion. Then, in one of the rare occurrences during this Top 8, Lax was able to win a game on the draw against finalist 8th-ranked Shaun McLaren, breaking serve and paving the way to his 3-1 victory.


At Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir* here in Honolulu, it was Arix Lax, his drafting prowess, and his Abzan deck that outlasted a field full of incredibly talented players. Congratulations to Ari Lax, newly minted Platinum Pro of the Pro Tour Players Club, and winner of Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir*!

FRIDAY COVERAGE



Day One of Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir* has concluded, and 227 players will be returning tomorrow to keep their hope for a Top 8 alive. Among those players however are two with very solid buffers to put up a strong overall finish: 11th-ranked Yuuya Watanabe of Japan, and Mike Sigrist of the United States. Both players will be heading into Day Two with undefeated records, and Watanabe will start out in our King of the Hill seat for tomorrow. 


The verdict is in, and the *Khans of Tarkir* Limited format has had players exploring a variety of two, three, and even five color options, with the four and five-color decks being particularly powerful. As we look at tomorrow's drafting results, we will have a better picture of what has trended as the best Pro Tour draft strategies.


The new Standard format has proven to be an environment full of options, with the Jeskai Way and its plethora of powerful white, blue, and red spells leading the pack through a variety of play styles. However, there are plenty of other interesting decks out there, and tomorrow, we'll see what emerges at the top.


Who will power through tomorrow and finish in the Top 8? And who among those players will hoist the trophy and the $40,000 check at the end of the weekend? Tune in tomorrow starting at 9 a.m. local time to find out!
