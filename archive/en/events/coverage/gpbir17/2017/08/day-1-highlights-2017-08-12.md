Grand Prix Birmingham 2017 Day 1 Highlights
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 12, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/henke_author.jpg)
By Tobi Henke












 A whopping 1,743 players had traveled to Birmingham this weekend to test their mettle in Modern. And it was a new Modern too, the most modern Modern yet! For the recent release of *Hour of Devastation* had brought at least a couple of interesting options for existing archetypes, not to mention the possibility of combining [Solemnity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemnity) with [Phyrexian Unlife](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Unlife) for a brand new one. [Claim // Fame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame) was thought to give another boost to all kinds of [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) builds, particularly those involving [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage); [Hour of Promise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Promise) had been busy already, searching up [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) here and there and looking quite promising indeed; and [Driven // Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Driven+%2F%2F+Despair) might just drive a few [Dredge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge) opponents to desperation.




![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpbir17/BIR_d1highlights1.jpg)



 It remained to be seen whether these influences would do anything to challenge or to cement the status of [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) as the number one archetype, which had cast a long shadow on the format for so long. In any case, this tournament, together with the weekend's other event in São Paulo, marked the first time players could use *Hour of Devastation* at a Modern Grand Prix. Sure enough, we trained an eye on the battlefield specifically to watch out for any of the new cards.



THE NEWS
========


"I don't know how much of this can be attributed to [Hour of Promise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Promise)," said Jasper Grimmer, "but Titan Shift seems to be everywhere these days. It's crazy!"


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hour+of+Promise)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Promise)
The three-time Grand Prix Top 8 competitor mused, "It may just be that the metagame is in a good spot for the deck right now, or that it had been underrepresented for a while. Not everyone is even running [Hour of Promise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Promise) after all, so it's not like the card suddenly pushed the archetype over the top or anything. But it certainly didn't hurt to get this extra option."


Another card many people had high hopes for was [Claim // Fame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame), but this one never lived up to the initial hype. Despite earlier claims to the contrary, its fifteen minutes of fame seemed to be up already.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame)
"Frankly, the card's terrible," Gold-level pro Marc Tobiasch who was running Grixis [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) himself said, with conviction. "Sure, you can come up with scenarios where it's going to be amazing. But I consider it effectively unplayable."


He elaborated, "First of all, it's in your hand and does nothing. I mean, you only have eight targets. And if you don't randomly hit one of them with [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), [Claim // Fame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame) is going to sit there for a while. So you'll first have to play a [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) or [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), which you do ... when? Turn three at the earliest? Turn four or five in some games?



![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpbir17/BIR_d1highlights2.jpg)  
*Marc Tobiasch claimed [Claim // Fame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame) was effectively unplayable*



"Then the creature still has to die. Plus, haste isn't even that great often and getting back a reactive card like [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) on your own turn is rather unimpressive too. Finally, you'll almost always have to board it out because of all the graveyard hate floating about. You might even encounter [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) or [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) in the first game. So sometimes you'll have a situation where [Claim // Fame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame) could shine for once and then everything will be spoiled by the opponent after all."


Tobiasch's conclusion: "It's nice to imagine what happens when you hit your [Claim // Fame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame) targets with [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour). On average, however, the card is simply much worse than [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command)."


However, some brand new aftermath did happen. It just wasn't [Claim // Fame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claim+%2F%2F+Fame) but rather [Driven // Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Driven+%2F%2F+Despair). This card was picked up by a number of [Dredge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge) players, most notable among them Hall of Famer Raphaël Lévy.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpbir17/BIR_d1highlights3.jpg)  
*Raphaël Lévy, aftermath genius*



Lévy put the Despair side to great use in his Round 6 feature match against fellow Gold pro Simon Nielsen where it made Nielsen discard something like seven cards across two games. But the most important card in the matchup against Nielsen's [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) deck, Lévy pointed out, had been another somewhat new aftermath card, namely [Failure // Comply](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Failure+%2F%2F+Comply). He explained, "You're basically always one turn behind, but every Comply buys you a turn. If you can actually play the card from your hand and counter, it buys two turns!"


In fact, Lévy went to a lot of trouble to punish his opponents' failure to comply. His latest iteration of [Dredge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge) not only had three copies of the card in its sideboard but also one [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) to go along with them.

THE TOP ONE PERCENT
===================


What decks did the experts choose? Seventeen players with three byes attended Grand Prix Birmingham to make for an almost literal one percent of the field: Hall of Famers Guillaume Wafo-[Tapa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tapa) and Raphaël Lévy; Gold pros Pierre Dagen, Ivan Floch, Steve Hatto, Thomas Hendriks, Grzegorz Kowalski, Joel Larsson, Niels Molle, Matteo Moure, Simon Nielsen, Mattia Rizzi, Petr Sochůrek, Ondřej Stráský, and Marc Tobiasch; plus two Platinum pros in (17) Javier Dominguez and (9) Martin Jůza.


Not to give away too much information, we don't say what every single one of them decided to play. But the aggregate looked as follows:


Six Eldrazi (four Tron, one Bant, one Hatebears hybrid)


Three [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) (one each based in Grixis, Jund, and Esper)


Two Titan Shift


Two Affinity


Two White-Blue Control (one splashing black)


One Dredge


One Black-Green Rock


So where did all of these Eldrazi come from all of a sudden? And why?


THE ELDRAZI INFLUX
==================


"I was surprised the deck was played so little until now," Ondřej Stráský told me when I asked him about the recent rise of the extraplanar menace in Modern. The two-time Pro Tour Top 8er was part of a small band of four Gold pros who all came to Birmingham with the completely colorless Eldrazi Tron.


"We basically ended up with the deck because Sam Pardee told me to play it. He's got a pretty high opinion of the deck," said Stráský. "And it does some very powerful stuff, which is exactly what you want to do in Modern."



![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpbir17/BIR_d1highlights4.jpg)  
*Ondřej Stráský – pro level: Gold; deck: colorless*



He continued, "Apart from the obvious broken things, you have [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) which is good, especially against [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow), and you have like the best mana base. Seriously, you never take pain, you never miss a color."


About Eldrazi Tron's position in the metagame Stráský said, "It's at an odd point right now. So many people have picked up on it, it might just be the top deck in the format. Either this or [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow). Then again, it's Modern; there are so many decks that people can't just invest a lot to target a single deck."


Talking about matchups, Stráský mentioned that he considered his chances against [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) to be about even. "It's close. It depends on how they build their deck exactly. At worst, I'd say, it's fifty-fifty. [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) is actually one of the bad matchups, but you can still get them with [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer). Overall, I really like that you have game against everything."


The list Stráský and friends were running only featured a few select updates. "We wanted to maximize our [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple)s. That's why we have three [Endbringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endbringer). We cut some [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) which is great with Tron but really hard to cast otherwise. Finally, we have one quirky main deck inclusion in [Basilisk Collar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar). May look weird, but gives you a huge edge in the mirror!"
