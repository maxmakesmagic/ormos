TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------


Eduardo dos Santos Vieira's friends wanted to make absolutely sure he did not miss this event. Two of them went so far as to register him for the event themselves. Their confidence in him was proven to be warranted as the MTGO maven stacked up wins all weekend, soon finding himself in the Top 8. Alongside him his fellow Brazillians helped make up fully half of those still in the hunt for the trophy: Fernando Barros, Pedro Carvalho, and the godfather of Latin American *Magic*, Willy Edel. Chile was not without heroes, however. Both Rodrigo Humberto Soto Sánchez and Daniel Antonio Gaete Quezada made sure the hometown fans had someone to cheer for. Adding further international flavor were Argentina's own Nicolas de Nicola, and Bolivia's David Sologuren. When the Top 8 was announced there were loud cheers from every corner of the room.


Six hundred and forty-three hopefuls were whittled down to just seventy-one Day 2 competitors, and the diversity of Standard was on parade. The good mana and powerful three-color spells of *Khans of Tarkir* have made their mark, and the format is feeling a push towards the middle of the aggro/control spectrum. The Top 8 made things even clearer - fully three Abzan decks (one Aggro, one Midrange, and Vieira's fresh take on things) and three Temur Monsters deck (straight from the Brian Kibler playbook) dominated the field. The other two decks were one Boss Red and Willy Edel playing Sidisi-Whip.


The Top 8 began with a couple big upsets as both Pedro Carvalho and Willy Edel fell to their opponents. In the semifinals, only Vieira remained to fight for Brazil. However, Soto kept hope alive for the Chilean fans, advancing to face Vieira in the finals with his Abzan Aggro deck. Vieira took the first game with his deck's trademark flourish - the deadly interaction between Soul of Theros and the air force generated by Hornet Queen. Soto battled back in the second with Anafenza, the Foremost helping to shut down Vieira's graveyard shenanigans. The final game turned on a pair of monstrous Fleecemane Lions on Vieira's side. The indestructible threats hit again and again, and Soto was unable to stop them. The assembled Brazillian fans went wild.


Congratulations to Eduardo dos Santos Vieira, Grand Prix Santiago 2014 Champion!

DAY 2 COVERAGE
--------------


It's time for day two here in Santiago, Chile, and the remaining seventy-one players are set to do it all over again. Six rounds of Swiss separate these seventy-one from a Top 8 berth, and a crack at the Standard elimination rounds. Both the field, and the setting, have narrowed. With hundreds expected to participate in a slew of side events (fun fact: in Spanish, that's los eventos parallelos), the main event has moved downstairs to a room altogether quieter, more intimate, more intense. 


And that's great, because ultimately the destiny of this South American crown will hinge not on countries nor cities nor buildings nor even rooms. It will hinge, as it always does, on two players facing across a single table, asking and receiving no quarter in the championship match. Who will be there, and what decks will they be playing?


Javier Luna of Argentina, and David Sologuren of Bolivia pace the field with perfect 9-0 records. While Luna has gone with the well-travelled Green-Black Enchantress plan showcased by the likes of Ivan Floch and Stanislav Cifka in recent weeks, Sologuren has chosen a more Kibleresque route, allying himself with the Temur clan. Chile has the third undefeated record, with Richard Moreira having picked up a draw yesterday to leave him on 25 points. With such a small day two field, that 'extra' point may turn out to be more of a help than a hindrance. We will see.


Amongst the 8-1s (and there are 13 of them overnight), the headline act is undoubtedly Brazil's Willy Edel. Twice a Pro Tour finalist, a Grand Prix Champion, repeat member of Team Brazil, Edel is poised to crack his Sidisi-Whip deck and reach another Top 8. Also watch out for the travelling Pavel Matousek. He's a new name around the top of the standings, but his results are starting to look like no accident – it could be a big day for him today.


So, the stage is set. 33 Chileans against 38 from around the world. It's time to find a champion.

DAY 1 COVERAGE
--------------


Six hundred and forty-three of Latin America's finest mages flooded the Gran Palace Convention Center to do battle in Santiago's fourth Grand Prix. Last year Luis Navas gave Chile its first hometown champion, and all the local heroes turned out trying to make it two in a row. In a field this big it's hard to say anyone is a favorite, but you couldn't go wrong choosing names like Brazil's Willy Edel, Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir* Semifinalist Thiago Saporito, former Rookie of the Year Felipe Tapia Becerra, and Navas, the reigning champ.


Nine rounds of swiss did their cruel work. Only Argentina's Javier Luna and Bolivia's David Soloquren remained unscathed at 9-0. Chile's Richard Moreira sat just outside at 8-0-1. The 8-1's included Edel, Argentina's Nicolas de Nicola and GP Rio de Janiero 2013 Champion Jose Francisco Silva. Those at 7-2 will be battling with their backs agasinst the wall, so Pedro Carvalho, Felipe Tapia Becerra and Gonzalo Ferrari Spampinato will have their work cut out for them.


The assembled field served up all the big decks of current standard: Abzan Midrange, Boss Red, Jeskai, Black-Green Devotion, Whip of Erebos decks, Blue-Black Control, and Mardu Midrange. Tomorrow we'll bring you a complete breakdown of the decks that made Day 2 as we head down the stretch towards the Top 8. Will a big name steal the spotlight? Will Chile put in a repeat performance? Will David Soloquren continue his strong run? All this and more on Sunday!
