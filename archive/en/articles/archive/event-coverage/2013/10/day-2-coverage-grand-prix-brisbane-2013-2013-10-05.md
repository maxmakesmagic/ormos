Day 2 Coverage of Grand Prix Brisbane 2013
==========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 5, 2013 










![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/opening2.jpg)Brisbane: 466 players fronted up to Grand Prix Brisbane yesterday, but we did the cruellest thing we could think of and made them fight it out amongst themselves, and only 80 players survived\* to play Modern constructed today.


(\*figuratively, we've had no actual fatalities so far this weekend, fingers crossed.)


Dillon Kikkawa ramped his way up to the top of the field at the end of day one with his Urza's Lands, while Daniel Ball employed [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) and [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) to trick his monsters into play. Joining them at 8-0 was Wilfy Horig, who didn't Junderstand the appeal of getting ahead of himself, playing a Green/Black/White Midrange deck\*\* instead.


(\*\*you know, there was a time we used to call that deck "Junk," grumble, grumble, getoffmahlawn.)


Chasing behind them with their eye also on the Top 8 this afternoon are Jake Hart, Garry Wong, Justin "Juzza" Cheung, Zen Takahashi, Daniel Bretheron, and Dan Unwin, with plenty more way back at 6-2. We'll dig into the metagame today, as see if we can find you any interesting hidden decklist gems as we battle towards the playoffs.

Round 9 Feature Match - Jake Hart vs. Dillon Kikkawa
----------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

Hart opened the game with a first-turn [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), revealing an excellent Tron hand from Kikkawa. Hart took an [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) to slow his opponent down, then followed up next turn with a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). Kikkawa cast an [Azorius Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet) and passed, eager to ramp into his more powerful spells.


Hart put himself to 12 to cast another [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Inquisition%2Bof%2BKozilek), knocking Kikkawa out of non-land cards in his hand before playing a [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and passing. Kikkawa, on the ropes, couldn't find an answer before Hart's [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and Spirits crushed his life total.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/r9_hart.jpg)  
*Kikkiwa finds himself suffering a total eclipse of the Hart.* 
The second game started off in a similar fashion, with Hart's [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Inquisition%2Bof%2BKozilek) stripping a [Talisman of Dominance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talisman+of+Dominance) from Kikkawa's somewhat greedy hand, filled with high-cost bombs and a [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) but a little light on mana sources. Kikkawa's greed paid off as he cast and then used an [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map), hunting down his missing Urzatron pieces while Hart summoned a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).


A [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) from Hart revealed Kikkawa had drawn another [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), his hand dense with goodies. Hart summoned [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze), confident that Kikkawa wouldn't play his [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). Another [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) from Hart stripped Kikkawa of his second [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), and Kikkawa drew a colour-fixing [Azorius Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet) a turn too late to stop the incoming beats.


The match highlighted the power of the popular G/B shell, giving deckbuilders plenty of opportunity to throw off their opponents' game plans while leaving a third colour very splashable. Kikkawa's deck, previously undefeated, packed more powerful punches, but simply failed to land any in these two games - a classic example of well-executed disruption edging out over a powerful control/combo strategy.


### Jake Hart 2 - Dillon Kikkiwa 0

Round 10 Feature Match - Daniel Ball vs Justin Cheung
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

Cheung's opening [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) revealed Ball's game plan - to cheat massive creatures into play at obscene speeds. His hand filled with mana, draw and enablers but lacking a fatty, Cheung nabbed a [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) to slow down the impending pain. Ball cast a [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) while Cheung summoned a pair of [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman)s.


Ball used a [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) to filter through his deck, dropping a second Looting and a [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) into his graveyard. Cheung's [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman)s looked to disrupt Ball's graveyard, but Ball had a [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) to power up his grip further.


Cheung summoned a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) revealed Ball had 2 [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) and a [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance), but no gigantic monsters were to be found. After the top of his library yielded a pair of lands, Ball moved to his sideboard.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/r10_cheung.jpg)  
*Cheung, wrecking Ball.* 
Ball opened the second game with a [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), discarding a [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) and threatening some dangerous Legendary action as early as Turn 2. Cheung had an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) to stop the shenanigans, showing Ball's grip of [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) and a [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). Cheung took the Looting and passed, while Ball drew a blue mana source and passed.

After the match, Ball and Cheung discussed the challenges of pushing reanimator decks through a barrage of hate. "It was much easier in Day 1", Ball said, "Because people didn't know what I was playing. It makes a huge difference in whether they choose to lead with some discard or a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), or develop their own game plan."

Round 13 Feature Match - Sam Sedgman vs. Ben Tudman
---------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

Both players settled in for their first-ever feature match, looking surprised at the crowd that gathered around them within a few moments of their sitting down. Newcomers to the GP scene, both had exceeded their expectations by making it this far - the penultimate round, both in a double-win-and-in scenario. They shook hands and leaned in, eager to get the match underway.



![Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Searing+Spear)

Sedgman opened with a Mountain and a [Lava Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Spike). "I know what you're playing!", laughed Tudman, summoning a [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). Sedgman had a [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) for it, knocking his opponent to 14 in short order. Tudman tried again with [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), while Sedgman had a [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders).


Tudman went to stabilise with a [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image), giving him some blockers against any ground assault. Sedgman attacked with his [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders), then killed the resulting [Voice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) token with another [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze). He followed up with a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to Tudman's dome, dropping him to 7 - never a good sign for the control player.


Tudman summoned a [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and another [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence). Sedgman passed the turn with mana open. Tudman passed, and Sedgman tried a [Flame of the Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+of+the+Blood) Hand, but Tudman had a [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) in response, fetching out a [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) and countering the Flames.


Sedgman summoned a [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), which attacked unblocked, Tudman falling to 4 in order to keep his [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) unmolested. Sedgman tried a [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) during Sedgman's upkeep, but Tudman had another [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) for a [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts), giving him enough creatures to attack for the win after the dust had settled.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/r13_tudman.jpg)  
*Tudman keeps careful track of his plummeting life total.* 
Sedgman opened the second game with the eponymous [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) To The Face, following with a [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders). Tudman had a [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), which jumped in front of Sedgman's [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) the following turn. Tudman shored up his defences with a [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and a [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage), while Sedgman simply played a [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) and passed.


Tudman, down to 10 life but with a little breathing room, tapped out to play a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). Sedgman used a [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) to off the [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) before swinging with his [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide). Tudman upgraded his [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) to a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), then copied it with a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image), gaining some precious life back. Sedgman threw what little burn he could find at Tudman's creatures, but a pair of [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s and a Kiki-Jiki clinched the match in short order.


Both matches highlighted the glass-cannon, all-in nature of mono-Red burn decks - the final points of damage being frustratingly elusive for Sedgman, while providing some heart-stopping moments for Tudman on defence.


### Bed Tudman 2 - Sam Sedgman 0

Sunday, 5:00 p.m. – Modern with Dan Unwin
-----------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw* 

 

I can't remember the first time I sat down to talk to the Premier Australian deckbuilder Dan Unwin, but I know this won't be the last time. A long time fan of the format, having played at the first ever Modern Pro Tour in Philadelphia in 2011, Unwin had a lot to say about it.


"I agree when everyone says that it's a healthy format, I just think it could be even better if they unbanned some cards," he explained. "The first round of cards they banned after the first Pro Tour made sense, but after removing cards like [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), now it's got to the point where Modern plays a lot like Standard."


I asked Unwin about his preparation for this event. "I didn't get to prepare nearly as much as I would have in the past," his new slimmed down physique hinting at what he's been doing with his spare time, "but when there's an event on with a great prize, I'm gonna be there.


"I audibled away from the deck we had been working on for the event because I had a bad day playing it in the Grand Prix Trials on Friday, and I just wasn't confident I could play it properly in the main event. Patty Robertson is playing for the Top 8 with it, though, and Sam Loy made day two with it as well."

Sunday, 5:15 p.m. – Ad Nauseam Combo with Wedges
------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

In a sea of decks both familiar and novel, it behooves coverage staff to keep an ear out for scuttlebutt around the top tables for unusual plays. After a few rounds of catching mutterings like "*Drew his entire deck*" and "... *can't believe he played that*", I traced the source of so much contention to Matthew 'Wedges' Hare, a mainstay Sydney player who's been tearing up the tables with his [Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) deck. I pulled him aside to have a quick chat.


### So, why pick this deck to run at the GP?


Well, I'd had some experience running it and other variants on the [Ad Nauseaum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseaum) engine in the past, so I know how to pilot it against control, aggro and other combo decks. It's off most people's radars and is pretty non-interactive, so it was easy to throw in a ton of goldfishing practise to get myself up to speed.


Looking around the expected field, none of the new or emergent decks were a particular concern, and many of them were more than half blank against an entirely creatureless combo deck, so it seemed like a good choice.



![Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ad+Nauseam)

### Where did you get the list?


Erikbobo on **Magic Online** has been posting some great results with it in recent weeks. I pulled the list and made a few minor changes for what I thought the metagame in Brisbane would present - adding a [Spoils of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spoils+of+the+Vault) and taking out one of the [Phyrexian Unlife](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Unlife)'s sped the deck up a fraction.


### What are your matchups like?


Anything that doesn't have a decent disruption package or is non-interactive and slower than me is pretty much dead in the water. Jund has been surprisingly favourable, especially with [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) helping slow or shut down their disruption suite.


The deck's also surprisingly resilient - I won my last match despite being attacked with an [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) on turn 2 in or 3 in all the games, something which most other decks wouldn't be able to battle through.
