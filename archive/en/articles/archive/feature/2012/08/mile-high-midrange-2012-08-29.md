Mile-High Midrange
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 29, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











Standard seemed to be solved. There were Delver decks, decks that used to play Delver, Birthing Pod decks, Ramp decks, and the occasional undead deck would crawl out of the grave for a Top 8. Sure, some fringe decks filled things out, but the metagame seemed pretty stable. But Michael Lee showed a different side of Standard this past weekend at the StarCityGames Open in Denver.


![Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Strangleroot+Geist)Mike set aside the Delvers and Titans to play with the unappreciated [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and his buddies in a green-white shell. Mike looked to capture the power of some of the best creatures in Standard at every point of his curve. [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), followed by [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) into [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), is a powerful sequence for any deck, but Lee's list has plenty of ways to enhance even his biggest creatures.


Swords of Anything and Everything have been popular since seeing print, but [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor)â€¦ now that's a new one. [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) is an extremely powerful card in Limited, and has seen Constructed play in the past, but many questioned whether it would stand the test of time with removal being significantly better now than before. While the iconic enchantment still needs to demonstrate staying power, Lee's performance is a pretty good sign for stompy lovers everywhere!
