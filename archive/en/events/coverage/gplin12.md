Bronson Magnan Grand Prix Lincoln Champion!
===========================================




February 18-19, 2012













Grand Prix Lincoln 2012
-----------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplin12/champion620.JPG)

Fighting his way through one of the most diverse formats ever, Magnan found an angle that no one else saw, reaching into the way back machine to resurrect CAL - Confidant Aggro Loam - a deck that used [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) to superpower [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) , [Flame Jab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) , and [Raven's Crime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) . His epic game three against Andrew Cuneo will certainly go down as one for the ages as both players traded blows in a match that was not for the faint of heart.


The weekend will also be remembered as the first Modern Grand Prix, the first Grand Prix held in Lincoln, Nebraska, and anything but the first Grand Prix Top 8 for Luis Scott-Vargas. Team ChannelFireball didn't quite dominate this event like they had recently, but Scott-Vargas held the torch all the way into the Top 4.


But it was Magnan's [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) 's that are heralding a shift in the format in the midst of a PTQ season, showing just how unexplored and wide open Modern can be.

Quarterfinal - Andrew Cuneo (Pod) vs. Derrick Rutledge (Jund)
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen* 

### Game 1


An early [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) showed Cuneo was on the mono creatures plan to start off, as he resolved [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and [Linvala, Keeper of Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence) early on, but both of them were already smaller than Rutledge's 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) .


Despite some early disruption from Rutledge, Cuneo assembled his combo of [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) , [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Melira%252C%2BSylvok%2BOutcast) and [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) , but simply sat on them for fear of removal in response. By waiting while ahead on board, he could combo off at instant speed any time he wanted.


With no removal in sight and getting beat down by [Linvala, Keeper of Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Linvala%252C%2BKeeper%2Bof%2BSilence), Rutledge opted to move on to game 2.


Cuneo 1 – Rutledge 0


### Game 2


Rutledge bolted the Birds right off the bat, as all good boys and girls are trained to do, and a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) followed up to put Cuneo under immediate pressure. Cuneo tried to stem the bleeding with a [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) on the [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) , but [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) quickly moved the roadblock out of the way.


The [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) traded with an [Entomber Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomber+Exarch) , but that cleared the way for a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) to begin beating down on Cuneo.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplin12/qf1%20Rutledge.JPG)
*Derrick Rutledge*

When [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) attacked, Cuneo flashed in a [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) hoping to block and scry, but a [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) kept it from stepping in the way. It was, however, able to help find a [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) to take care of one [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) .


A second [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and a [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) , however, put too much pressure on Cuneo, who was already far behind, and succumbed quickly.


Cuneo 1 – Rutledge 1


### Game 3


Rutledge was in trouble immediately as he missed his second land drop while Cuneo played [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) , [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) , [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) .


Rutledge's first play? [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)


Cuneo answered with a [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) for [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) , and his overwhelming board position was quickly putting Rutledge in an awkward position. A second [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) stuck, revealing a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) on the next upkeep. The land was welcome, but the fact that it came in tapped was problematic.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplin12/qf1%20Cuneo.JPG)
*Andrew Cuneo*

A [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) found [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) , ratcheting up the pressure even further. A [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) on [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) saved some damage, but at 9 life, Rutledge was facing 11 damage.


He played a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) to block while leaving up Bolt mana to try and scrape his way back into the game. A Bolt and a block let Rutledge stay at 2 life, but he was a green mana short of casting [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) . Seeing the writing on the wall, he scooped up his cards.


Cuneo 2 – Rutledge 1




Quarterfinal - Mary Jacobson vs. Samuel Friedman
------------------------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen* 

Mary Jacobson and Samuel Friedman had crashed their way to the Top 8 blazing similar paths.


### Game 1


Friedman had the play, and thus the initiative, leading off with [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) , [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) and a [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) .


Jacobson traded her [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) for Friedman's, playing a [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) and [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) .


"Let's race!" Friedman said, banking on his [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) s to get him there.


He upped the ante with a [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) on the same turn he killed Jacobson's. She could tell she was in trouble.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplin12/qf2%20Jacobson.JPG)
*Mary Jacobson*

"I think I just scoop now. I gotta at least try," she said, after playing and equipping a [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) that connected for six on a [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) .


A second [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) put things over the top for Friedman, and like that he was up a game in the mirror.


Friedman 1 – Jacobson 0


### Game 2


[Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) , [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) , [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) and [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) led to an explosive start for Jacobson. A Galvanic blast on [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) and [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) slowed things down slightly, but Friedman was still way behind right off the bat.


Friedman started using removal spells to slow the beats, first an [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) , then trading away an [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) . But with [City of Brass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Brass) as his only colored mana source, he was getting pinged as legions of small robots kept crashing in.


Bit by bit he whittled Jacob's attackers down till he sat at 4 life facing [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) and [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) with just an [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) . His plethora of removal gave him enough time to find a second [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) , [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) and a [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) . Still, at a precarious four life, Jacobson had several turns to find virtually any burn spell.

"And, victory condition," Magnan said as he played both an [Obstinate Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth) and [Lavaclaw Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches) . "Two victory conditions."


Friedman began working on some victory conditions of his own, hitting for two poison and killing the Baloth while Magnan was tapped out.


A second Baloth ballooned Magnan's life total, but Friedman was clearly leaning on a poison kill. In response to a [Darkblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) , Friedman used his [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) to pump the one that was targeted and put Magnan to five poison.


But on the next attack, Magnan dealt with both Inkmoths with a [Nature's Claim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Claim) and a [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) , though he oddly threw away a [Darkblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) when both removal spells killed the poisonous lands without any help.


It was about that time that Magnan drew a [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) , supercharging his position thanks to the presence of [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) and [Flame Jab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) in his graveyard.


With [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) and [Darkblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) , Magnan was set up to deal with any threat the Affinity player could offer. What he didn't have was a way to actually win, as [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) killed his [Lavaclaw Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches) , or at least as much as a land can die when [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) is in play.


Magnan then began jabbing at Friedman's still high life total, retracing [Flame Jab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) one land at a time.



![Flame Jab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flame+Jab)

However, Friedman found his trump with [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) and backed it up with two [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel) s. Magnan had no answer for the 2/2, but was well ahead on life totals 25-9 after an attack with [Lavaclaw Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches) .


Friedman kept scratching and clawing his way back, killing [Lavaclaw Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaclaw+Reaches) with [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) and casting a second untouchable [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) . But as far behind as he still was, he started daydreaming about what card he could draw to find a win.


"It's a new card, it's called *Darksteel* [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) . It's [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) , but indestructible," Friedman said.


But when he didn't have any answer to another turn of [Flame Jab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) s, he extended his hand to Bronson.


"Do I have time to go throw up now?" Magnan asked before his trip to the finals of Grand Prix Lincoln.


Bronson Magnan defeats Samuel Friedman 2-1 to move on to the finals of Grand Prix Lincoln.




Final - Andrew Cuneo vs. Bronson Magnan

*by Blake Rasmussen* 

Previously unknown Bronson Magnan certainly won't be so anymore. His CAL deck has reminded the world what a potent combination [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) can be, shaking up the Modern landscape and probably every PTQ in the coming weeks.


Standing in his way was longtime pro Andrew Cuneo piloting one of the better known decks coming into this event. He had proved his mastery over the Melira Pod archetype over the course of 15 rounds and two Top 8 matches.


### Game 1


Magnan's [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Inquisition%2Bof%2BKozilek) revealed [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) , [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and two [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) , giving him an array of options to disrupt Cuneo.


"You kept that hand?" Magnan mused to himself, puzzled at the lack of action before binning [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) .


Cuneo attacked for a point at a time for several turns with a [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) before both players played their signature cards in [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) . Magnan fired off another powerful three drop with [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) , discarding [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) in order to get his engine started if he ever drew green mana.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplin12/final%20Magnan.JPG)
*Bronson Mangan had plenty of reasons to raise an eyebrow in the finals.*

But the Pod player had a powerful engine of his own, and a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) allowed him to Pod up [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) for [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) and Noble Heirarch.

[Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Liliana%2Bof%2Bthe%2BVeil) and [Flame Jab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) killed [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) and [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) , but Cuneo had a powerful follow up. Top 5 Cards
-----------



![Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dark+Confidant)

### 5 - [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)


Bob Maher's invitational card [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) has been gold in every format since its printing in Ravnica, providing a steady stream of card advantage to players who are after greatness, at any cost. With Modern re-opening the vault for older cards, [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) was an easy go-to despite a fragile body and no evasion, demanding an answer lest the opponent get run over by an avalanche of card advantage. The dangerous little guy was all over the top tables throughout the tournament, with twelve copies breaking through to the Top 8.



![Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Life+from+the+Loam)

### 4 - [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam)


This innocent-looking sorcery has been the centerpiece for many powerful decks over multiple formats, first fueling [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) before moving onto [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) and [Solitary Confinement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement). It got fully exploited when combined with *Onslaught*'s cycling lands, providing multiple dredges every turn to churn through the deck. Bronson Magnan proved that [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) was good enough even without cycling lands, using the powerful sorcery to keep his retrace spells going before eventually killing opponents with [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) on his way to the Grand Prix Lincoln championship.



![Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cranial+Plating)

### 3 - [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating)


With "Affinity" decks morphing into a super-explosive pseudo-combo decks, [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) has taken the place of [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) as the robotic "Fairy Godmother" that Pierre Canali won Pro Tour Columbus with in 2004. The equipment has become so crucial to the archetype's effectiveness that players have stretched their mana to incorporate [Steelshaper's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steelshaper%27s+Gift) —a card that struggled to find a spot in any constructed format until relatively recently—in order to find it.



![Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)

### 2 - [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)


Affectionately known as the "Flying Spaghetti Monster" for its impressive collection of tentacles, [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) has seen play in formats everywhere, from Standard Eldrazi Green, to Legacy [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), to Modern Tron decks. The alien's prohibitive casting cost almost exclusively requires him to be cheated into play, and Tron players have been doing that with [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach), borrowing the line from Pro Tour Philadelphia decks, but with the backup ability to realistically hardcast the Eldrazi like Luis Scott-Vargas did all the way to a semifinals appearance.

### 1 - [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)


While [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) has certainly been a player in Standard, its spot on this list is more about [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast), [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer), [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap). Having Melira and [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) in play allows the Melira Pod player to sacrifice [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) repeatedly for infinite life thanks to [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast)'s power keeping the persist creature from getting -1/-1 counters. The same applies to [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) providing infinite damage. The combination, tied together by [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling), make up one of the most popular and powerful decks in the Modern format, as evidenced by Andrew Cuneo's finals appearance.
