Let's Do That Again: *Besieged* Edition
=======================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on February 2, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






I hope everyone had a great time at the *Mirrodin Besieged* Prerelease! The new set is sure to shake things up in Standard and Extended. Those of you that read me regularly are already aware of how much I love infinite turn combos. *Mirrodin Besieged* introduces a new way to take infinite turns to the Extended format. Sure, combo decks like this can be weak to decks like Faeries, but we're hoping [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) deters enough players from playing those decks. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab128_time.jpg)  
[Time Sieve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Sieve) has always had potential. The deck did very well in Standard back in the Summer of 2009. As a deck builder, it's generally good practice to keep your eyes on any card that has a colon followed by the words, "take an extra turn." 

I have visions of casting a [Thopter Assembly](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) on turn four, and sacrificing five other artifacts to my [Time Sieve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Sieve). Taking my next turn, bouncing the [Thopter Assembly](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly), making five Thopters, sacrifice the five Thopters to take another turn, replay my [Thopter Assembly](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) ... 

Enough with the pipe dreams, lets get down to brewing: 

![Ichor Wellspring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ichor+Wellspring)  
[Ichor Wellspring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Wellspring) is another excellent addition to this type of deck. I already planned on sleeving up [Elsewhere Flask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elsewhere+Flask), [Kaleidostone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaleidostone), and [Prophetic Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism). [Ichor Wellspring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Wellspring) draws the extra card when it enters the battlefield, and does double duty by letting us draw again when we sacrifice it to the [Time Sieve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Sieve). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab128_3cards1.jpg)  
Now we're already playing sixteen two-mana artifacts that draw us a card when they enter the battlefield. The consistency created by such a high density of cantrips reminds me of the [Fluctuator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fluctuator) decks that would have dominated Constructed if it weren't for the banning of [Fluctuator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fluctuator). I did a quick [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) search looking for cost reduction. I was pleasantly surprised to find that there's a pseudo-[Fluctuator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fluctuator) for this type of strategy. 

![Semblance Anvil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Semblance+Anvil)  
[Semblance Anvil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Semblance+Anvil) hasn't seen a lot of play off the kitchen table, but it's a card that I've been trying to work out the kinks with since the release of *Scars of Mirrodin*. When you've got sixteen two-mana artifacts that draw a card when they enter the battlefield, though ... Well, then [Semblance Anvil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Semblance+Anvil) lets you saw through the top of your deck and find the [Open the Vaults](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Open+the+Vaults) or [Thopter Assembly](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) that will seal the deal. 

I still want to play a few copies of [Open the Vaults](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Open+the+Vaults) to make the deck more consistent. Sometimes you're going to need to "go off" and win through a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). 

[Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) is one of the best combo cards ever printed and I'll be happy to have a playset in this type of deck.

If the opponent fails to interact with you, then this deck can take infinite turns by turn five with a startling level of consistency. Unfortunately, it isn't realistic to assume that anybody will be caught without ways to interact with you or your combo, especially after sideboarding. 

![Mindlock Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mindlock+Orb)  
This deck gets a lot of options when you start thinking about the sideboard. [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) decks will have a rough time trying to deal with [Mindlock Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindlock+Orb). These types of decks achieve wins by searching their library. [Mindlock Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindlock+Orb) isn't a freebie win because most [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) players have cards like [Nature's Claim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Claim) and [Primal Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command), but it certainly doesn't hurt. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Howling Mine | Temple Bell |

[Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) alongside some disruption will make the non-Faeries control match-ups really good. In fact, this type of effect is so powerful in these match-ups that I'd like to go ahead and add a couple copies of [Temple Bell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Bell) to the mix in an effort to increase the frequency with which we draw a Mine effect. 

Actually winning the game before we deck ourselves can become a liability once we've gone the way of the Mine. I'd like to include a single copy of [Tezzeret the Seeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret+the+Seeker) in the sideboard to make sure this type of thing isn't too much of a problem.

The deck may have trouble with the speed of other combo decks or the draw-go nature of Faeries, but it's an excellent choice if you're looking for a fun Extended deck that lets you do something extraordinarily powerful. This is a Johnny deck if I've ever seen one. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab128_master.jpg)  
My [Master Transmuter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter) decks are a favorite amongst readers. I've been looking for more reasons to play with this type of strategy, and *Mirrodin Besieged* offers up reasons aplenty. First off, [Go for the Throat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) is going to become the removal spell of choice for extended Faeries players. Sure, there might be some [Peppersmoke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peppersmoke)s in the mix, but the days when we needed to fear cards like [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) and [Grasp of Darkness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness) are long gone if all of the creatures we play are artifacts. [Thopter Assembly](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Assembly) also gives this deck a new tool that could be great when fighting the Fae. I'm going to revisit this archetype next week and talk about *Mirrodin Besieged*'s effect on the archetype. I'd love to see your versions of Extended Transmuter via email or in the forums.
