Allied Command(er)
==================



 Posted in **Command Tower**
 on December 31, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






War never changes, but *Magic* does. There's always something new around the corner, like the wonderful wave of awesome incoming with *Oath of the Gatewatch*. As a Commander aficionado, I'm constantly looking for a new way to engage in multiplayer mayhem.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/ogw_239nCi30ks3/x0VSRSMy85.png)


Joraga Auxiliary | Art by [David Gaillet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22David+Gaillet%22%5D)


Generally, it's a new commander—or "general" for those who have played long enough to remember—that gets me fired up. Today I'm pleased to share what may be the most highly awaited legendary creature in a long time. Meet General Tazri:


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/ogw_239nCi30ks3/en_PChmYP81XD.png)


This is the definitive Ally commander of choice. While [Karona, False God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karona%2C+False+God) has been around forever, and [Munda, Ambush Leader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Munda%2C+Ambush+Leader) was a three-colors-too-few alternative, General Tazri is the real deal.


* Although a 3/4 for five mana won't light any fires, it's enough toughness to resist small fries and most burn spells.
* The enters-the-battlefield trigger is exactly what you want for an Ally leader, rallying another Ally to follow along behind.
* The full monty of mana required for the final ability isn't just show: pumping all your Ally creatures is one way to secure victory, but more importantly, it defines General Tazri's color identity as all five colors.

You can play every Ally you want with Tazri—and you should! The ability to tutor out whichever Ally you need next is a powerful tool to use over and over. The best way to take advantage of it is to include plenty of Ally creatures that come with some utility attached:


* [Agadeem Occultist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agadeem+Occultist) can steal creatures back from opponents' graveyards, as long as you control enough Allies.
* [Chasm Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chasm+Guide) ensures a new Ally gets to attack right away.
* [Kazuul Warlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazuul+Warlord) is the epitome of pumping up Ally creatures (though [Drana, Liberator of Malakir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drana%2C+Liberator+of+Malakir) and the new Munda's Vanguard are also serviceable).

![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/ogw_239nCi30ks3/en_vodci76jGB.png)


* [Lantern Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Scout) and [Talus Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talus+Paladin) provide lifelink to stay alive through combat.
* [Veteran Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Warleader), [Turntimber Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger), and Grovetender Druid can pile up extra tokens and take advantage of them to spread out the attacks.
* [Hero of Goma Fada](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Goma+Fada) and [Kabira Evangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kabira+Evangel) can make a weak position into an impressive attack all in one move.

That isn't an exhaustive list of sweet Ally creatures to use, and if you go back into the *Lorwyn*/*Morningtide* era you'll find plenty of changeling creatures that can cooperate too:


* [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) is simply a powerhouse.
* [Shapesharer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shapesharer) can turn into whatever you need it to.
* [Cairn Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cairn+Wanderer) takes advantage of every graveyard's goodies.
* [Changeling Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Changeling+Berserker), [Changeling Hero](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Changeling+Hero), and [Changeling Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Changeling+Titan) are excellent, since they can champion General Tazri herself and provide another enters-the-battlefield trigger when the Changeling goes away.

Going all-in on Ally creatures means a whole host of supports exist for General Tazri. Everything from [Patriarch's Bidding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Bidding) and [March from the Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+from+the+Tomb) (to get back those Ally creatures) to [Eldrazi Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument) and [Steely Resolve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steely+Resolve) (to keep our Ally options safe) works together to make a mass of Ally creatures move to victory.

This deck is rough—and it'll be weeks until we can put it through its paces—but it highlights a good start to making the most of General Tazri:


* [Turntimber Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger) and [Conspiracy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) are a two-card combo, though [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) or [Grovetender Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grovetender+Druids) can do a good impersonation when the moment's right.
* [Victimize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victimize), [Aura Shards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Shards), and [Grave Sifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Sifter) all play off a creature-heavy focus, providing support as the game goes on.
* [Boros Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) and [Ghostway](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostway) are situational, but when a [Planar Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Outburst) rolls in, having one in hand means you can gently "counter" the effect. Keeping our army in play is going to matter if we plan to attack to victory.
* [Sword of the Animist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+the+Animist) and [Blade of Selves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+of+Selves) make life easier in a five-color deck full of utility creatures that don't mind getting attacks in.
* [Command Beacon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Command+Beacon) means even when General Tazri costs a little too much, you can fire her off for a quick one-two of finding the next creature to play.

It's worth mentioning that the mana for a five-color deck has gotten easier over the *Magic* years. Aside from a few splashy choice rares to add in, Standard can provide plenty of good lands to make things happen. Using enough basic lands and fetch lands means the new duals from *Battle for Zendikar* ([Prairie Stream](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prairie+Stream) and friends) work great. If you wanted to downshift, [the enters-the-battlefield-tapped-but-gain-1-life lands from *Khans of Tarkir*/*Fate Reforged*](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&set=%7c%5b%22Khans+of+Tarkir%22%5d%7c%5b%22Fate+Reforged%22%5d&type=+%5b%22Land%22%5d+!%5b%22Basic%22%5d&rarity=+%5bC%5d+!%5bb%5d) can supply multicolored options just as well. If you're worried about mana, start with the commons!


Where General Tazri goes from here is up to you. Do you want her to wield powerful Equipment and Auras? Would you like her to become a combo piece based on [Xenograft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenograft) and [Conspiracy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) in a token-focused deck? What other Ally creatures do you want to add to her arsenal of options?


I can't wait to watch someone add all five colors to their mana pool and pump an army into an astronomical, lethal attack. I hope you'll be just as excited to see it too!
