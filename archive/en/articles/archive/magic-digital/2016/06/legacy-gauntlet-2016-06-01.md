The Legacy Gauntlet
===================



 Posted in **Magic Digital**
 on June 1, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






In celebration of *Eternal Masters*, *Magic Online* is running the Legacy Gauntlet using thirteen top Legacy decks.


What Is the Legacy Gauntlet?
============================


Much like the Pro Tour Gauntlet that runs after each Pro Tour, the Legacy Gauntlet is a way to test-drive decks that have been successful in tournaments. When you sign up for the Legacy Gauntlet, you will get a randomly selected phantom deck from the decks listed below. You'll be able to play the deck during the queue, but you won't get to keep the cards at the conclusion of the event.


Why Legacy?
===========


Typically, the Pro Tour Gauntlet follows each Pro Tour and uses top-finishing decks in those tournaments. However, the Pro Tour is never Legacy, and we wanted to give you the opportunity to test-drive a sampling of Legacy decks. The upcoming release of *Eternal Masters* contains many cards that you will see in the Legacy Gauntlet (as well as other Legacy decks).


What Decks Are in the Legacy Gauntlet?
======================================


When selecting the decks, we did not have the luxury of a Pro Tour to pick and choose decklists from. Instead, we chose decks we feel represent a cross section of decks that make the Legacy format feel unique. We scoured decklists from both *Magic Online* and paper *Magic*, took those, and adjusted them to be representative of what an average decklist for the archetype might look like.


Let's take a look at the decklists!

**Miracles**: The key card in this deck is [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top). It allows you to manipulate the top of your library, which is important for countering spells with [Counterbalance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) and for drawing miracles at the right time. [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) can manipulate the top of your library in a pinch as well, but [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) allows this to be the premier control deck in Legacy.

**Shardless Sultai**: This deck has the greatest amount of card advantage of any deck in Legacy. It starts with [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) (often cast via [Shardless Agent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shardless+Agent)), but [Baleful Strix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baleful+Strix) and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) provide card advantage, which is relatively difficult to do in a format as fast as Legacy. Past that, this deck is a hyper-efficient set of creatures and spells—[Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), and [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) basically read like a list of the best cards in the entire Legacy format, and they are all here in one deck.

**Storm**: This is one of the premier combo decks of the format. The goal of this deck is to cast nine or more spells, then finish with the single copy of [Tendrils of Agony](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony). The easiest ways to get extra spells are [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) and [Past in Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames). One important synergy is using [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) or [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) to know what's in your opponent's hand prior to [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy). However, the most important interaction is between [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) and [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond). If you cast [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor), then respond by sacrificing [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) (on *Magic Online*, hold the control key as you cast [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor)), you will have hellbent for the [Infernal Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor), and you will have the mana from [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) to cast the spell.

**Lands**: There are tons of powerful lands in Legacy, as well as a few cards that are very powerful when your deck contains 37 lands. [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) (with an assist from [Rishadan Port](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port)) can often lock opponents out of mana, while [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) and [Grove of the Burnwillows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) can take out creatures repeatedly, and eventually the opponent. If you make [Thespian's Stage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage) a copy of [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) (and keep the copy with the "legend rule"), you'll end up with a [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) with no counters on it, which means a 20/20 Marit Lage token. [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) is the perfect card with [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), as you rarely care if the card you searched for ends up the graveyard.

**Death and Taxes**: This is a mono-white deck full of "hatebears"—creatures that disrupt your opponent while providing a small battlefield presence. [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) is a key card that allows you to play creatures to the battlefield in addition to using the abilities of your lands for disruption. [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) is also very important in this deck, as it can protect your other creatures from removal. As a rule, it is not worth attacking with [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) unless you are sure that your opponent can't remove one of your other creatures.

**Elves**: Elves is one of the most unique decks in Legacy. While there aren't many cards in Elves that appear in other decks in Legacy, don't mistake that for a lack of power. [Gaea's Cradle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Cradle) is one of the most powerful lands in Legacy, worth playing the full four copies despite its legendary status and the fact that it can't produce mana on the first turn. This deck has a multitude of synergies layered within the Elf tribe. [Heritage Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) plus [Nettle Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) allows you to produce tons of mana, while [Glimpse of Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) allows you to draw more Elves as you cast more Elves. [Wirewood Symbiote](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote) protects your creatures from opposing removal while letting you return [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) as a secondary draw engine. [Natural Order](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order) has lots of functions in this deck, providing a finisher in [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) or a nearly unstoppable creature in [Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus).

**Eldrazi**: Once the scourge of Modern, Eldrazi are powered out by the presence of many lands that produce more than one mana. [Ancient Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb), [City of Traitors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors), [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin), and [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) provide a significant base to be able to cast cards such as [Thought-Knot Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer) on turn two with regularity. The Eldrazi creatures are supported by disruptive spells such as [Warping Wail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warping+Wail) and [Chalice of the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void). A turn-one [Chalice of the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) for one can be disastrous for many other decks.
