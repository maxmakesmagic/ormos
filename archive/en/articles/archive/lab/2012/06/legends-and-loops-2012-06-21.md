Legends and Loops
=================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on June 21, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova












Hello and welcome to the Lab. This week continues the smorgasbord of coverage, with the focus hovering over **Duels of the Planeswalkers** 2013 for now. Some exciting ***Magic** 2013* previews escaped their tethers last week, and it seems their cohorts will be similarly released to the public eye next week. I know I'm pumped to continue learning about the latest core set.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl200_odric.jpg)*Odric, Master Tactician | Art by [Michael Komarck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Michael+Komarck%22%5D)*
For now, though, this week is geared around the aforementioned video game, which represents a new introductory gateway to **Magic**'s splendorous realms of fun. I'm sure it's quite enjoyable, but in terms of this particular column, I wasn't sure how to angle things. After some research, I discovered that a focal point of the game's pre-built decks are the five legendary creatures of ***Magic** 2013*. With that shred of info to cling to, I trained my deck building reticles on those five cards for this week.


The return of legendary creatures to the core set certainly makes sense for a video game tie-in, and on a wider scale, they're sure to resonate with the curious folk who open them in booster packs. Let's see what I could muster with these legends. Some decks are straightforward and others have, well, that infinite glint.


### Tactical Mastery


Odric, Master Tactician starts off the festivities this week. His eye-popping combat ability allows you to totally dictate your opponent's blocking plans, provided you attacked with three other creatures as well. I was a fan of [Master Warcraft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Warcraft) back in the day, and the effect resonates more effectively on a 3/4 first striker.


When building, I knew the deck wanted to be aggressive and stuffed with bodies, so Odric could activate. [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession) was a good start. Then I wondered what strategy benefited from attacking with multiple creatures. Battle cry was the decisive answer. In came [Accorder Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accorder+Paladin) and the fluttery [Signal Pest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest), as well as flagship battle crier [Hero of Bladehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold).




![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/m13/1l1lk1mylk_en.jpg)

![Accorder Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Accorder+Paladin)

I also wanted Odric to set up certain blocking situations, in which I could swoop in and wreck my opponent. [Gaze of the Gorgon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaze+of+the+Gorgon) is such a card. Odric can force a gang block on one of your creatures, and a Gaze will wipe out the blockers! [Midnight Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Charm) is another tricky spell in this vein, and [Profane Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profane+Command) completes the multifaceted trio of black spells.


[Typhoid Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) is a snippy little card and [Blind Creeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Creeper) beats well for a two-drop. And for some silly singletons, I went with [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest) (Odric can orchestrate a morbid combat) and the weird [Jangling Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jangling+Automaton) (so they have untapped blockers to mess with!)




![Skirsdag High Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Skirsdag+High+Priest)

![Jangling Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jangling+Automaton)

### Talrandom


Onto Talrand, Sky Summoner. Talrand is a pretty straightforward kernel of a card. Simply play a bunch of instants or sorceries, make a bunch of Drakes, swing for a bunch of damage, repeat a bunch of times.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl200_talrand.jpg)*Talrand, Sky Summoner | Art by [Svetlin Velinov](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Svetlin+Velinov%22%5D)*
I don't want to step on Adam Styborski's toes if I can help it (he previewed this 2/2 flying token machine [last week](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/199).) Adam built a fun blue-red deck, so I'll avoid that color duo. Instead, let's go green. [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and [Deep Reconnaissance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Reconnaissance) can increase my land base so I can cast more spells! [Primal Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) is ultra versatile and able to fetch Talrand. [Wrap in Vigor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrap+in+Vigor) is a neat trick that can protect Talrand and his Drakes. Finally, the singleton [Parallel Evolution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Evolution) can double your Drake army!




![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/m13/7580i54y9o_en.jpg)

![Parallel Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Parallel+Evolution)

As for the blue spells, they start out cold and lean (the flashbacking [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), the kickable [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil)) and veer into cold and *mean* territory ([Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and [Condescend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend) stop your opponent's spells in their tracks). Even [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) is here. Hey, despite its grossly pushed numbers, it's a cool creature, and I've been looking for a casual Lab deck for it on the sly.


To wash out that harsh blue taste, rinse with some mellow blue spells. [Oona's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oona%27s+Grace) is a fun retrace spell, meaning it can be played again and again. And a Talrand deck just wouldn't feel right without his Invocation.

### Nefaroxic


Let's move onto Nefarox, Overlord of Grixis. That's a surprising shout-out to a former shard of Alara, but I guess it shouldn't be, since that set kindly donated exalted to ***Magic** 2013*. My question is, how did Nefarox acquire such a Bantian ability? Regardless of the answer, this Demon will eat you in a hurry.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl200_nefarox.jpg)*Nefarox, Overlord of Grixis | Art by [Aleksi Bricolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Aleksi+Bricolt%22%5D)*
When thinking about Nefarox, I couldn't help but think about the "loner" sect of *Avacyn Restored*. Cards like [Homicidal Seclusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homicidal+Seclusion) and [Demonic Rising](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Rising) facilitate a solo creature strategy, and Nefarox seems to gel with that. A Homicidal Nefarox will swoop in as a 9/7 lifelinking, [Cruel Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Edict)-ing monster!




![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/m13/dpzk2n2za4_en.jpg)

![Homicidal Seclusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Homicidal+Seclusion)

Besides Nefarox, the other creatures function well on their own. [Demonic Taskmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Taskmaster) beats hard for a three-drop. [Korlash, Heir to Blackblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korlash%2C+Heir+to+Blackblade) is a gem, able to accelerate my lands via shedding extra copies of himself. Sounds like a loner to me! (I know my own kind.)


The rest of the deck is control. [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) kills stuff and, if outnumbered, [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) can wipe the board. (Although I'm playing four Cathedral of War as an extra buffer, [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) can [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)-ify them. While I'm at it, two copies of [Spawning Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pool) can't hurt.) [Brainspoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) is further removal and when transmuted it becomes either of the five-drop enchantments (or [Beacon of Unrest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Unrest), a nice recurring weapon). Finally, [Lightning Greaves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) and [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) speed up different aspects of my game plan (finding my big vigilantes and beating down with them.)

### The Gobfather


Ah, Krenko, Mob Boss. Something about this guy is instantly appealing. It's either his name or his ability. Or both. Krenko needs only a few other Goblins around him to cultivate a fully fledged army of them, with him in the cigar-clouded center.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl200_krenko.jpg)*Krenko, Mob Boss | Art by [Karl Kopinski](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Karl+Kopinski%22%5D)*
Goblins are traditionally quick off the ground, but Krenko enables some particularly blistering draws. How about a turn-two Krenko's Command into a turn-three [Goblin Chieftain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chieftain) (attack for 6) into a turn-four hasty Krenko, who spits out four more Goblins (attack for 14?). Factor in the rest of the deck's speedy squad (highlights: [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal) for more tokens, [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker) for more oomph, [Goblin Matron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Matron) to find Krenko or the Chieftain) and the damage should pour.




![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/m13/pk7y4hy8k8_en.jpg)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/m13/x1n8qu8x5k_en.jpg)

And yes, Krenko is broken with [Intruder Alarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm), because that card is completely busted, like my printer. I'm a bit sick of it, to be honest. Between [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) and [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), these blue-red "win now" decks have definitely circulated the **Magic**al trenches by now. Instead, I used [Crab Umbra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crab+Umbra). This totem armor card can protect Krenko *and* untap him!

### Flashy by Nature


Finally, Yeva, Nature's Herald. I'm a big fan of the flash mechanic, [as previously](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/97) [expressed](/en/articles/archive/lab/opening-business-2010-08-19) [in this](/en/articles/archive/lab/adios-alara-2010-08-26) [column](/en/articles/archive/lab/sequel-sequence-2010-09-02) [through](/en/articles/archive/lab/rootin-home-team-2011-02-24) [my love](/en/articles/archive/lab/no-contest-2011-12-22) [for](/en/articles/archive/lab/alabaster-mastery-2012-04-12) [Leyline of Anticipation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Anticipation) and [Shimmer Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmer+Myr). Yeva is set to join these beloved ranks.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl200_yeva.jpg)*Yeva, Nature's Herald | Art by [Eric Deschamps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Eric+Deschamps%22%5D)*
I wondered if I could devise an infinite combo around green creatures. That way, I could wait until my pieces were set up, then go off at the end of my opponent's turn by first flashing out Yeva, then the combo. The green creature I landed on was the innocent-looking [Quirion Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Sentinel). Odd, but with the proper pieces, full of infinite potential.




![Quirion Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Quirion+Sentinel)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/tcg/products/m13/fyhu6r759l_en.jpg)

The first piece is [Emerald Medallion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emerald+Medallion). This way, the Sentinel will cost ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif). Hmm... that means it'll generate enough mana to cast it again. The second piece is [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio). Ahhhh. Now I can cast one Sentinel and bounce another to my hand, and then cast it via the first Sentinel's mana ability, and bounce the other... et cetera. [Essence Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden) can give you infinite life, [Wirewood Hivemaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Hivemaster) can make infinite 1/1 tokens to swing with, and the super-awesome [Champion of Lambholt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Lambholt) enables a swing for the win. [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) can help you find your artifacts and [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) seems nuts with the Curio and Medallion around.
