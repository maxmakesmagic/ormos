Ben Stark is the Grand Prix Indianapolis 2012 Champion!
=======================================================




December 22-23, 2012












Grand Prix Indianapolis 2012
----------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/gpinday_winner.jpg)


After battling his way through fifteen rounds of *Return to Ravnica* Limited action, the 2012 Pro Tour Paris champion and team ChannelFireball member found himself in an incredible star-studded Top 8 that featured six Grand Prix champions and two teammates. Ben Stark stuck to his strategy of choice for *Return to Ravnica* Limited, drafting a powerful Golgari deck that featured the highlight cards of the green-black guild, including [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage), [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage), and plenty of creatures with scavenge.


After battling his way through teammate Tom Martell in the quarter finals and Brian Demars in the semi finals, Ben Stark clashed against Chris Fennell, who played through the entire weekend without a single loss (including the Grand Prix Trial that he won on Friday). There, Ben Stark finally ended Fennell's winning streak, earning himself his first Grand Prix title.


The calendar year is coming to a close, and the next Grand Prix stop will be Denver in January, where players will bring their best Legacy decks to battle in the first Grand Prix of the 2013 calendar year. But for this weekend, it's all about the pro who played Golgari to perfection for the first Grand Prix of the 2012-2013 season. Congratulations to Ben Stark on his Grand Prix victory this weekend!

Top 8 Draft – Luis Scott-Vargas
-------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg* 

 

The Top 8 players for Grand Prix Indianapolis consist of a whopping six former Grand Prix champions. The two who haven't won a Grand Prix? Ben Stark (Pro Tour Paris 2011 champion), and Matthias Hunt (2011 Rookie of the Year). That's quite the resume for this Grand Prix Top 8!


With such an impressive number of players in this Top 8, it was hard to focus on one player in particular. But given the long-standing history that Luis Scott-Vargas has in the game, featuring five Pro Tour Top 8s (including a win at Berlin in 2008), ten Grand Prix Top 8s, and a National Championship title, the choice quickly became easy. To his left was Matthias Hunt, and to his right was teammate Ben Stark.


Given player and guild preference, where would Luis Scott-Vargas end up in this Grand Prix Top 8 Booster Draft?


Scott-Vargas got things started with a pretty exciting first pick in [Isperia, Supreme Judge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%2C+Supreme+Judge), followed by [Lyev Skyknight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyev+Skyknight), cementing him pretty heavily into Azorius.


However, as his picks were simple, his left neighbor, Matthias Hunt, agonized between two cards: [Lotleth Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll), and [Skymark Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skymark+Roc). He ultimately took the latter, which could be problematic to Scott-Vargas if his leftmost neighbor chose to force an Azorius deck of some kind. While Hunt has a preference for aggressive blue-white, there existed some overlap in cards that both Hunt and Scott-Vargas could want, which may prove to be a problem in the second pack for the Team ChannelFireball player.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/top8draft_scottvargas.jpg)*Luis Scott-Vargas aligns with Azorius in an effort to earn his fifth Grand Prix victory.*
Scott-Vargas followed with a third-pick [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition), and in the fourth pick, he found a a few enticing options, including [Seller of Songbirds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seller+of+Songbirds) and [Eyes in the Skies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyes+in+the+Skies). Ultimately, he went with the populate instant, and followed with [Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul). Faerie Imposter followed, and then [Azorius Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Keyrune), as Scott-Vargas's Azorius strategy was shaping up nicely.


Hunt, meanwhile, had a collection of cheap white creatures, but was still sticking to his plan with multiple [Azorius Arrester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Arrester)s, [Swift Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swift+Justice). With rightmost neighbor Ben Stark safely on Golgari, his guild of preference, Scott-Vargas would have little to worry about in the third pack, and as long as he could scoop up some more defensive cards to go with his Azorius guild leader, Scott-Vargas was in great shape.


In the second pack, Scott-Vargas led with a first pick [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition), shipping a fairly juiced pack with [Rogue's Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue%27s+Passage), [Rix Maadi Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rix+Maadi+Guildmage), and a couple of other goodies. In the second pick, Scott-Vargas found [Dramatic Rescue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dramatic+Rescue), shipping [Rakdos Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune) as the other major notable. The third pick, however, sent off alarm bells, as the Azorius well quickly dried up. Scott-Vargas took [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake), passing a late [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) along with an [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire). Hunt, who had not moved from Azorius, denied Scott-Vargas a [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird), leaving him to pick only an [Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) and passing [Thrill-Kill Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrill-Kill+Assassin), Augur Spree, and [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth).



![Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vassal+Soul)

[Hover Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hover+Barrier) came after, thankfully, as Scott-Vargas quickly picked up the defender. [Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul) followed, but the pick after that contained no options for Scott-Vargas, who took a [Sewer Shambler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Shambler) in hopes of denying a Golgari player that pick. [Phantom General](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+General) followed, but after that, the picks quickly dried up.


The third pack first pick was a fairly large whiff for Scott-Vargas, who found Vitu-Ghazhi Guildmage, [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return), [Call of the Conclave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Conclave), and a bunch of other great cards that he couldn't play. He hate-picked the guildmage, knowing he would have problems beating that card, and followed with the second pick [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor), but passing Azor's Elecutors and [Hussar Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hussar+Patrol). A saving grace came in the third pick for Scott-Vargas, as [Martial Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Law) made its way into his pile of cards, which was followed by [Seller of Songbirds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seller+of+Songbirds) and [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment).


[Selesnya Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Keyrune) came next, and could open up the option to splash for the [Vitu-Ghazi Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitu-Ghazi+Guildmage) he tooked earlier, and a [Hussar Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hussar+Patrol) followed that, along with another copy of the flash creature. However, things quickly dried up past that. A late [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) came, but in the same pack as a [Selesnya Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildgate), and ultimately Scott-Vargas went with the blue counter.


Ultimately, Scott-Vargas ended up with a fairly solid Azorius deck featuring the guild leader herself, alongside a powerful curve of creatures, despite a partial guild feud between him and Hunt during the draft. Now, the only thing standing between Scott-Vargas and a fifth Grand Prix win were three more rounds. Can Azorius guide the accomplished **Magic** pro to victory?

Quarterfinals – Ben Stark vs. Tom Martell
-----------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall* 

 

There were way too many good match-ups in this Top 8 for two reporters to possibly handle. Chris Fennell and Adam Yurchick were squaring off in a battle of GP Champs who have yet to crack the Sunday stage on the Pro Tour. Rookie of the Year Matthias Hunt was locked in battle with fellow teacher and GP Champion Brian Demars. You also had living legend Luis Scott-Vargas, winner of GP and PT alike, taking on GP San Antonio Champion Tyler Lytle.


And there was still the match-up between Tom Martell -- who won a Legacy GP in this very hall earlier this year -- and Pro Tour Paris Champion Ben Stark.



![Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tectonic+Edge)

"We have a great storyline..." Stark said to Martell. They had been debating whether or not they were going to be the featured quarterfinal match.


"I have never beaten you and I am trying to defend my GP Indy title?" said Martell who has lost to Stark in more matches than their titanic five game set in the quarterfinals of Pro Tour Paris. Both players could still recall vivid details and subtle card byplays of the match and began exploring other realities of what could have been if only Martell had chosen not to play for a [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) (which led to him getting his [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)[Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)d).


"I think we are the only match between two PT Top 8 competitors," said Stark, one of only two players in the Top 8 without a GP win -- although almost every one of them would happily cash their GP hardware in for Stark's PT trophy.


### Game One


While the players were randomly seated at the draft table the player and not playing in seeded pairs the player with the higher finish still got to choose whether to play or draw. Stark chose to play.


"You sure?"


"Not by much," shrugged Martell who played [Centaur's Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur%27s+Herald) on the draw. [Drudge Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Beetle)s came down on both sides of the table and when Stark played turn tree [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) and attacked with both Martell traded Beetles. He passed the turn with mana up to make a Centaur after blocking the Mangler with his Herald but when the turn passed back to him his Centaur was bleeding from a [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound). .com.com


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpind13/qf_martell.jpg) *Tom Martell* 
Martell's Aazorius [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest)er locked down the Mangler and the Centaur beat in for one. Martell was missing his fourth land and played a second Herald without the ability to cash it in immediately. Stark could not attack and was content to let the Wound fester while adding an attack-ready [Ogre Jailbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Jailbreaker).


Martell was still scuffling for that fourth land while Stark was almost drowning in his. Fortunately for him he Golgari has plenty of uses for extra mana and he scavenged the Beetle onto the [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) and attacked with both.


"I wish I had land," groaned Tom. He was able to get rid of his [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound)ed creature and hold the Jailbreaker at bay while making another token. He drew and played his fourth land.


"You got your wish," said Stark who prepared to take five after [Azorius Justiciar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Justiciar) told his team to take a timeout. He did have another [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) for the new token.


Martell played [Growing Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growing+Ranks) but was going to have to put his token in the path of the 5/5 Mangler. He was hoping to trade his pair of two powered creatures for the [Ogre Jailbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Jailbreaker). Stark made the trade but just rebought the 4/4 with [Treasured Find](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasured+Find).


Martell was behind and had to take damage if he wanted his token generators to sync up with [Growing Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growing+Ranks). [Seller of Songbirds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seller+of+Songbirds) joined the team and immediately regretted the decision since it was going to mulched by the oncoming Golgari horde. While Martell was vocally griping about his mana difficulties Stark was at a low simmer about his inability to put the game away as his deck yielded plenty of land when he wanted action cards.


He played Gisly [Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salvage) into Sewer Shanbler and sneered at the 2/1: "Garbage"


"Guys are guys here," sighed Martell who needed to find some respite to get his deck's populate going. He kept his bird token around while his Seller jumped in the path of the Mangler. He took four from the Jailbreaker to set up something to populate. He did and added [Sunspire Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspire+Griffin).


Stark just kept on in [Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juggernaut) mode. The Griffin ate the Shambler and a token stepped in the way of the 5/5. Martell fell to three from the unblocked Jailbreaker.


Martell played [Golgari Decoy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Decoy) and though maybe he was going to be able to scratch his way back into the game until he heard Stark exclaim during his draw step.


"That's a good one..." said Stark as he played Korizda Guildmage and made it impossible for Martell to block the intimidating Jailbreaker.


Ben Stark - 1 Tom Martell - 0

He also played [Treasured Find](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasured+Find) -- a card that Ben Stark made look better than I have ever seen it before throughout the Top 8 -- to get back the Guildmage and replay it.


A turn later Stark was able to sacrifice his Indrik for four saprolings and swarm Fennell nearly to death. He had enough mana that when Fennell put a recently cast [Towering Indrik](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Indrik) in the way of a token, Stark had three activations to kill the 2/4 in battle. He pawed one more card off his deck and then scooped up his sideboard.


Ben Stark - 1 Chris Fennell - 0


### Game Two


Fennell was already concerned about [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound)s without having to worry about unblockable [Sewer Shambler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Shambler)s. He went to his sideboard to bring in Arial Predation, [Horncaller's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horncaller%27s+Chant), and [Sundering Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Growth) along with some additional [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s in place of Vraska, [Lotleth Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll), and Towetring Indrik. Fennell kept his opening hand while Stark agonized over his decision.


"It is not a clear mulligan. It is so close," said Stark with his head in his hands. He finally decided to send back five lands, [Axebane Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian) and Daggerdrone Imp. He kept the next six and had the game's first play with [Drudge Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Beetle). Fennell played Seller of Singbirds which looked pretty evenly matched until Stark came roaring into the red zone with five points of power thanks to a hasty [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler).


Fennell had picked up his pen but when Stark picked up his pen as well Fennell cautioned him: "Hold on I may actually block."


"I am tapped out do whatever you want."


He took five.


Twice. His hand was top heavy but really wanted him to have creatures. He had both [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment) and [Collective Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing) and could not just be throwing any creatures away.


Stark added a Daggerdrone Imp to his team while Fennell played [Azor's Elocutors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azor%27s+Elocutors). [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) gave Stark a fifth land and put [Sewer Shambler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Shambler) into his yard where he could scavenge it onto his Imp. He attacked for three and the score was already 20 to 7 in Stark's favor.


Fennell played [Eyes in the Skies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyes+in+the+Skies) to throw one guy in the way of the Imp -- stuck now on five mana with multiple plays if he could draw that sixth land. His time was running out as Stark was gaining life from the Imp and enchanted the Elocutors with a [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound).


[Call of the Conclave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Conclave) added to Fennell's board but what he really wanted to add was that elusive sixth land. Stark was in cruise control. He was getting two in a turn guaranteed and his Imp had a steady supply of birds to prey upon. He even had the luxury of playing [Treasured Find](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasured+Find) to grow back Arial Predation. He also played a [Sewer Shambler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Shambler).


Fennel drew his sixth mana and tried to figure out some exit strategy with [Collective Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing) and [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment). Ultimately he just passed and when Stark attacked with the Imp he went into the tank.


"I have to play to one of two cards and if I choose the wrong one I am going to tilt so bad," groaned Fennell. He blocked the Imp and played [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment) on his own Stabwounded Elocutors. Stark let the populate resolve and then used Arial Predation on the fresh token.


Fennell drew one final card but he did not have enough creatures still standing to make the [Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessing) matter. He laid his cards on the table and congratulated the Pro Tour winner on becoming the seventh person in this weekend's Top 8 to win a Grand Prix trophy.


### Final Result: Congratulations to Ben Stark on winning Grand Prix Indianapolis and handing Chris Fennell his first loss since he started playing **Magic** on Friday in the Trials.

Top 5 Cards

*by Event Coverage Staff*

 

 


![Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ethereal+Armor)


 

### 5. [Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor)


Here's a question for you. When your Sealed pool features big hits such as [Desecration Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon), [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps), [Dreadbore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore), along with three copies of [Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) and two [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor), do you play the heavy rare Rakdos deck, or the aggressive white deck full of auras? If your name is Tyler Lytle, you go with the latter. The three [Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor)s served him out in out-pacing opponents all throughout Day 1. The Grand Prix San Antonio champion blasted through the Sealed portion of this weekend's Grand Prix with an 8-1 record, picking up more than a fair share of wins off of speedy starts featuring the one mana aura, which was capable of out-pacing decks that had a wider range of powerful cards but a clunkier deck as a result.


  

 
 


![Civic Saber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Civic+Saber)


 

### 4. [Civic Saber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Saber)


2011 Rookie of the Year Matthias Hunt blasted through both of Sunday's Booster Drafts with aggressive Azorius decks, and [Civic Saber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Saber) was one of the key cards for his draft strategy. A first-turn [Judge's Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar) can attack for 3 on the second turn with a [Civic Saber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Saber), [Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul) attacks as a 4/2, causing opponents to back-pedal quickly against creatures that are far from easy to block. The damage that [Civic Saber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Saber) adds to the multi-color flying creatures that Azorius offers makes this card an unexpected find, and it also makes the Azorius aggro strategy that Matthias Hunt favors an archetype to look for more of on **Magic Online**.


  

 
 


![Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stab+Wound)


 

### 3. [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound)


Of all the forms of removal in *Return to Ravnica* Limited, [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) is the only one that many players will take over some of the format's more powerful rares. Why? It's a form of removal that can also double as a win condition. Throwing [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) on a defender is not an uncommon way to finish someone off after your early creatures have dealt the first points of damage, and sometimes, it forces opponents to throw their own tricks at their creatures just to survive. Ben Stark proved how potent this can be in his semi final match against Brian Demars, where Demars had to fight just to make sure his own creatures actually succumbed to their [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound)s!


  

 
 


![Korozda Guildmage ](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Korozda+Guildmage+)


 

### 2. [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage)


Many by now are familiar with the power of the guildmages, most notably the [Vitu-Ghazi Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitu-Ghazi+Guildmage), one of the crowning uncommons of *Return to Ravnica*. [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage), however, is not to be underestimated, and has proven itself to be on a similar power level here this weekend. Its ability to send creatures through a slew of blockers makes it difficult to race, and its second ability complicates removal while also acting as a way to make sure your creatures with defender can turn into an army of attackers at the drop of a hat. Ben Stark proved the power of both abilities well in his semi final match, where [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage) made use of [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest)ed creatures by transforming them into saproling, and it allowed Stark's smaller attackers to get through Brian Demars's big blockers without any issue.


  

 
 


![Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grisly+Salvage)


 

### 1. [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage)


While [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage) is certainly one of the most powerful Golgari cards, it is the guild's most defining card in Limited. That title belongs to [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage), perhaps one of the most important cards to the Golgari deck. [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) helps you hit your land drops each turn, it can find creatures that you need to cast, and at the same time it stocks your graveyard full of goodies that you can scavenge back, giving this two mana instant a ton of versatility. Sometimes, even the instants it sends to your graveyard can be made useful too, as Ben Stark demonstrated with [Treasured Find](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasured+Find) to return an [Aerial Predation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aerial+Predation) that hit the bin, a play which dramatically altered the pace of play in his final match against Chris Fennell.
