Standard Evolution: The Origins, Ascent, and Survival of the Fittest
====================================================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX LONDON 2015](/en/events/coverage/gplon15)
 on August 15, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Craig-Jones-Author-Photo-150x150_0.jpg)
By Craig Jones










One of the interesting things about the Standard format over the past year is how the metagame has evolved on a week-to-week basis. It's no longer a case of finding the "best" deck and plugging away with it until the results follow. There might not even be a "best" deck, and even if there is it's only on a temporary measure depending on the expected opposition. There are predators and prey in a complicated cycle and the challenge for most players is to find the right combination of 60 cards plus sideboard to put them in the "predator" role for the day's tournament.


Take a look back to the release of Dragons of Tarkir earlier in the year. At the Pro Tour it was Martin Dang's aggressive red deck splashing green for [Atarka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka%27s+Command) that ran away with the trophy. Yet a week later, with the control decks having a better idea of what to aim at, Esper Dragons was the talk of the town. And after that it was green with [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and the durability of megamorphs like [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) and [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor).


Now, we're post-Magic Origins. This has added a new cycle of planeswalkers, some very tasty red spells in the form of [Abbot of Keral Keep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbot+of+Keral+Keep) and [Exquisite Firecraft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exquisite+Firecraft), and the incredibly versatile [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker).


And once again the evolution is happening.


At Pro Tour Magic Origins the buzz was about a newer, burn-oriented version of the red deck and a brutally fast blue-red artifacts deck with [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) as the centerpiece. Here is Player of the Year Mike Sigrist's version of the [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) deck that took him to second place at Pro Tour Magic Origins.

[Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) has been around for a while, but previously lacked the depth to make it consistent enough. Magic Origins offered up the tools it needed. An aggressive-but-fragile Plan A of turn one artifact, turn two Ensoul it and bash face for five until opponent falls over gained a sturdier Plan B of swarming an opponent with Thopters pumped up by [Chief of the Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Foundry). Not only does Magic Origins draft all-star [Whirler Rogue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirler+Rogue) generate Thopters, it also provides a way to slip any [Ghostfire Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostfire+Blade)-equipped, Ensouled monsters through annoying screens of Goblin tokens, Elves, and other nuisances to deal the killing blow. With [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) threatening to rip out the last quarter of an opponent's life total at any moment the deck is undisputedly very powerful and competitors this weekend at Grand Prix London will need to be aware of it.

This is back to the heady burn-heavy days of Red Deck Wins. It's built more on classic lines—little guys to nip in for 8–10 damage, then a full suite of twenty burn spells to finish the job. Other decks might have picked up flashier additions from Magic Origins, but Constellation didn't do so badly either with [Herald of the Pantheon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herald+of+the+Pantheon) and the late-game inevitability of [Starfield of Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starfield+of+Nyx). Will this show up in greater numbers this weekend in London?


These are not the only options either. The [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino)s of both control- and aggro-flavored Abzan haven't gone away. Neither have the [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai)s and Silumgars of Esper Dragons. Nor have the [Dragonlord Atarka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Atarka)s and [Genesis Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra)s of Green-Red Devotion.


This all makes for a fascinating, diverse Standard field. Where will the metagame dial turn this weekend? Which decks will be predator, and which prey? Follow this space here as we track how Grand Prix London unfurls.
