Above and Beyond the Call of Deck-Building
==========================================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on March 18, 2009 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl30_callOfDuty.jpg)This week, as per the title, we'll be going above and beyond the call of deckbuilding—or, as it is commonly abbreviated, A and B the C of D, wot! The D usually stands for "Duty," but as it goes in my friendly laboratory (well, my friendly antechamber), crazy deck-building is my solemn duty. I'm coming at you all today with a special four-pack of *Conflux* decks based around cards that have slipped out of the mainstream for one reason or another.


Two of these decks will be based around certain cards with gold symbols that haven't gotten much attention or playtime. In short, reject rares. They're funny things, reject rares. They vary from the promising ([Vicious Shadows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vicious+Shadows)) to the mediocre ([Twinning Glass](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twinning+Glass)) to the downright awful (the [Mudhole](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mudhole)s, [Pale Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pale+Moon)s, and [Moonlace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlace)s of the world.) Yet somehow, all three of these sub-categories carry within them a strong appeal to rogue Johnny deckbuilders, who would like nothing more than to see an unused card get some love. Reject rares have even spawned their own draft format (the straightforwardly titled Reject Rare Draft) that is a surprising blast to take part in.


The other two decks are based around a *Conflux* mythic rare that seems to be a bit of an outsider among the [Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitus)es and Nicol Bolas, Planewalkers of the world. Hopefully I'll be able to pull [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) to these lofty heights, starting with the next paragraph!


### What Do You Get When You Cross a Demon and a Dragon?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl30_malfegorSliver.jpg)A Demon Dragon you shouldn't cross under any circumstances.


pause


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl30_twins.jpg)Boy, that was a quiet rimshot! In any case, it's true of [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor), the black and red lord of Grixis, second in command to only Nicol Bolas himself. Don't mess with this guy, or he'll hold you down with one hand and beat you down with the other three. According to his flavor text, [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) can neither be trusted nor ruled, and here I am attempting to do both of those things. Hey, if I can get along with [Rakdos the Defiler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+the+Defiler) (who seems like [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor)'s twin brother who was separated at birth), I'm sure I can handle the latest ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) monster.



![Malfegor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Malfegor)

So, what's mechanically interesting about [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor)? Well, for starters, he's a 6/6 flyer for six mana in red and black, colors that have historically been subpar in flying. That's certainly efficient. What's more exciting is the mass sacrifice he creates for your opponents at the cost of your hand. Therefore, I felt the best use of [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) would be in a hellbent-ish deck, using [Rakdos Pit Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon) to beat down. [Cackling Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Flames) and [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) seem like appropriate red removal in this sort of deck. And [Dread Slag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Slag) is downright perfect: early on, it's a useless card that's fine to discard to [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor), and later it's a brilliant topdeck.


While pondering [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor), I couldn't help but think that at times, you'd be discarding your whole hand to kill maybe two creatures. Although the times where you wipe out an army of Saproling tokens with [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) are obviously the most desirable, those potentially underwhelming moments bothered me. Therefore, I went with [Burning Sands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Sands) as a doubly wrecking permanent alongside the Demon Dragon. Now, only killing two creatures comes with two free [Stone Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain)s! And during mass sacrificing, [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) could act as the latter half of Boom.


However, removing both creatures and lands still isn't enough! Speaking of Boom, there's a certain card printed in *Future Sight* that was immediately paired with the red [Armageddon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon): [Bitter Ordeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Ordeal). With [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) and [Burning Sands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Sands) kicking around, as well as your removal, [Bitter Ordeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitter+Ordeal) should always be at least half a [Cranial Extraction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction).

I was thinking about using [Ignorant Bliss](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignorant+Bliss) as a way to dodge discarding a ton of cards, but then I realized that [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) wouldn't do anything! I settled on some good old card drawing—namely, [Night's Whisper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night%27s+Whisper) and [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor). Since this deck wants to hoard cards in the early game and find [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor), those two should fit the bill.

This deck is slightly tricky to play. Try and land a [Rakdos Pit Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon) as soon as you can, and once you're at six mana, start holding onto your lands for [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor). You'll usually want to Tutor for one of your dragons as well. Between your burn and [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor), you should keep the board clear enough to stabilize. [Howltooth Hollow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howltooth+Hollow) is mostly for fun, but a different version of this deck could be based on discard and flashback, using such cards as [Skull Fracture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+Fracture), [Pain Magnification](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pain+Magnification), or [Syphon Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syphon+Mind) (which, along with [Malfegor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malfegor) himself, thrives in multiplayer). In fact, this more focused deck sounds like fun.

### Professional Profiteering


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl30_gwafaSplash.jpg)[Gwafa Hazid, Profiteer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gwafa+Hazid%2C+Profiteer) is one of the more annoying creatures to be printed in recent times. The slimy Bantian rogue plays both sides for fools and could probably sell a hen her own eggs back ... for a profit, of course. Between the counter producing and the [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) effect, we've basically got [Aurification](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurification) in creature form.


What's cool about Gwafa Hazid is that since he'll demand removal from somewhere, the bribery counters that he has placed on your opponent's creatures will remain where they are. Then, when you play another Gwafa, he won't have to re-bribe these creatures, as they'll once again be locked out of combat. To make Gwafa as recurring and annoying as possible, I went with [Sigil of the New Dawn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+New+Dawn), which can repeatedly bring back the briber. [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) (possibly more annoying than Gwafa) is pretty ridiculous with the Sigil in play. [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) and the other evokers from *Lorwyn* and *Morningtide* also work well with the white *Onslaught* enchantment. [Slithermuse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slithermuse) in particular becomes well worth the investment, as Gwafa Hazid can keep your opponent's hand larger than yours most of the time.

The idea is that Gwafa's card bribes will psychically help you out. Personally, I'm not so much of a fan of this combo, as if Gwafa is killed, your card-drawing engine dies with it.

A similar (and also annoying, which has somehow become the theme of this deck) card to run here is Jace Beleran, who can help fill your opponent's hand for a giant [Slithermuse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slithermuse). If you ever activate his ultimate ability, it'll help you activate [Shelldock Isle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelldock+Isle), which could, say, pop your fun singleton of [Forced Fruition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forced+Fruition) into play.

[Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) can save your Gwafa if necessary, and it can target a [Slithermuse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slithermuse) or [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) for great synergy. [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) can leave your hand on the cheap, and restock you later in the game. Finally, [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) can both clog up your opponent's hand by acting as a [Sphere of Resistance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Resistance) and help you play your stuff faster.

This deck can reasonably hold its own in a one-on-one game, sure, but it can also be decent on the multiplayer front as well. Not to step on Kelly Digges's toes in the multiplayer pool, but during my analysis of [Gwafa Hazid, Profiteer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gwafa+Hazid%2C+Profiteer), I came up with a decent Two-Headed Giant idea. Gwafa specifically targets "a creature you don't control," not "a creature your opponent controls." Thus, you could target your teammate's creatures to give him or her extra draws. Sure, this will Pacify those creatures, but that's why your teammate came with a [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) deck! With the *Mirrodin* artifact in play (of which I'm a Giant Fan) Gwafa becomes, "![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif), ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170424152623im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/tap.gif): [Battlegrowth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlegrowth) your teammate's creature. Your teammate draws a card."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl30_handShake.jpg)If you wanted to try this idea, the above Gwafa deck is, as I said, fine in Two-Headed Giant. Both Jace and [Noggle Ransacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noggle+Ransacker) have global effects, and [Mind's Eye](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind%27s+Eye) is bonkers when four players play at the same time. I'm no multiplayer expert, but this seems fun!


### Jet Lassos: Yum!


Okay, so it's a stretch to make that anagram seem relevant. Appropriately, drawing enough cards off of a [Soul's Majesty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Majesty) to make it worth its mana cost is also a long shot. For sharing a converted mana cost with [Tidings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidings), [Soul's Majesty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Majesty) better draw me at least three cards (I'll knock off a card for it being green.)

Another *Conflux* card that cares about the power of your creatures is [Wall of Reverence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Reverence), a rare that once straddled the line between reject rare and decent rare before rising to fame within Gabriel Nassif's [Pro Tour–winning](/en/events/coverage/nassif-greater-power) deck. Alongside fellow defender [Plumeveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumeveil), [Wall of Reverence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Reverence) gave Nassif upwards of 4 life a turn while holding down the fort.


In casual, [Wall of Reverence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Reverence) can be paired with [Cradle of Vitality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cradle+of+Vitality) for tons of +1/+1 counters on the Wall, which will gain you more life. And with all the +1/+1 counters floating around, [Soul's Majesty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Majesty) is bound to draw you some cards. I added [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and [Plumeveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumeveil) to the budding decklist, on the basis that the former is amazing with [Cradle of Vitality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cradle+of+Vitality), and the latter for the above synergy.



![Cradle of Vitality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cradle+of+Vitality)

 

Want more synergy? I always thought that [Cenn's Tactician](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cenn%27s+Tactician) stood out from its fellow class-boosters in *Morningtide* in a rather sad way. The [High Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Ground) ability is just not as good as the Rogue's [Larceny](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Larceny) or the Warrior's [Primal Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Rage). In this deck, however, where the goal is to get +1/+1 counters on your Walls, [High Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Ground) becomes quite useful. In addition, [Cenn's Tactician](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cenn%27s+Tactician) can slowly grow a Soldier to powerful levels, ripe for [Wall of Reverence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Reverence) or [Soul's Majesty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Majesty) to take advantage of. [Burrenton Bombardier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Bombardier) and [Chameleon Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) are perfect Soldiers for the deck, as the former creates more +1/+1 counters, and the latter can increase its power for obvious synergy.


I wanted to add some [Runed Stalactite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runed+Stalactite)s to the deck, for the possibility of making [Wall of Reverence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Reverence) a Soldier, so I went with [Stonehewer Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonehewer+Giant) as an Equipment fetcher. This allowed me to run a small toobox of Equipment for the right situation.

You could go any way with this mold: more Soldiers, more life gain, more controllish cards, etc. Just remember to cast [Soul's Majesty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Majesty) at the right time. Although the deck evolved in a different way, it was the card I intended to build around. And hey, running it lets me name the deck after a Doors song. Which, really, is one of the best ways to name a deck.
