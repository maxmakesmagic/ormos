Extending Urza
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 1, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar











Here's a secret: I *hated* the Urza sets while they were legal in Standard. In fact, I can barely stand them now. Oh sure, *Urza's Saga, Urza's Legacy,* and *Urza's Destiny* introduced cards I use often, such as [Exploration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration), [Iron Maiden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Maiden), [Smokestack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokestack), [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes), the "[Ghitu Encampment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ghitu%2BEncampment)," [Argothian Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argothian+Enchantress), [Plow Under](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plow+Under), [Chimeric Staff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chimeric+Staff), and even [Imaginary Pet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imaginary+Pet). The list is quite long, actually. The three sets also brought casual-player staples like [Lifeline](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifeline); [Multani, Maro-Sorcerer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Multani%2C+Maro-Sorcerer); [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell); [Radiant, Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant%2C+Archangel); [Pattern of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth); [Phyrexian Processor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Processor); and [Serra Avatar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Avatar). In addition, I routinely play almost every reprint (and there are many) from Urza block now in *Seventh Edition.*

But I still hate them.

See, I was the same kind of player then as I am now. I liked turning weird cards into weird decks. Yet I routinely found my quirky ideas overwhelmed by the Ã¼ber-powerful Urza cards. I sighed every time a [Morphling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morphling), [Masticore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masticore), [Tolarian Academy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolarian+Academy), [Memory Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Jar), [Academy Rector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector), or [Yawgmoth's Bargain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Bargain) hit the table to steal victory from my otherwise superior board position. I cringed when my opponent played [Tinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tinker), [Yawgmoth's Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Will), [Stroke of Genius](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stroke+of+Genius), or [Replenish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Replenish). I later learned to fear [Donate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate). Losing to these cards was quick, painful, and frustrating.

You may be someone who yearns for the glory days of Urza block. I can say only that in the Urza days, I almost quit **Magic** because the game had become too fast for me to feel creative. Moreover, it always seemed that only my opponent *or* me was having fun at any given time. I never laughed when losing to a [Morphling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morphling). Losses to [Soulcatcher's Aerie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcatcher%27s+Aerie), on the other hand, make me giggle.

Anyway, I've become a less-whiny person since then. Now I can look back on the Urza block and see a number of cards with great potential. Today I continue my "New Extended" [series](/en/articles/archive/extending-rath-2002-06-20) with a look at my most hated of blocks and that blasted artificer, Urza.

Below are several odd cards from the Urza days that make for fun new decks, especially when combined with spells from more current sets. The decks themselves will be legal in Extended in [November](http://archive.wizards.com/default.asp?x=sideboard/news/20020517a). But because we don't know what the *Onslaught* set will bring, consider these decks more as interesting--and a little bizarre--thought experiments. Over the next few months, you can expect similarly wacky looks at the Masques, *Invasion,* and *Odyssey* blocks. For now, though, let us do a little [Taunting Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taunting+Elf) tango into the past . . . .

### Titania's BoonTitania's Boon

Just so you can be sure I'm talking crazy here, I start with [Titania's Boon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titania%27s+Boon). Who starts a look at cards from Urza block with [Titania's Boon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titania%27s+Boon)? Oh, sorry . . . I did that last time. (Booby!)

[Titania's Boon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titania%27s+Boon) has shown up in a few of my decks as a way to pump up untargetable creatures like [Blastoderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blastoderm), [Citanul Centaurs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Citanul+Centaurs), and [Blurred Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blurred+Mongoose). In fact, an "untargetables" deck is almost always fun to try out, usually with either red or blue to remove potential blockers. [Titania's Boon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titania%27s+Boon) isn't necessary in a deck like this, but it's a neat trick.

More recently, after seeing "[Mr. Babycakes](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/features/MrBabyCakes5.jpg)" take shape, I have a new appreciation for +1/+1 counters. The pieces began forming in *Judgment,* which brought with it [Forcemage Advocate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forcemage+Advocate) (see Bennie Smith's great [Forcemage contemplation](http://archive.wizards.com/default.asp?x=mtgcom/feature/66)) and the Phantom creatures, which added spice to the Spikes from *Tempest* block. Suddenly, anything that can add +1/+1 counters en masse looks like it might be the basis for a tasty deck. In fact, you can push the idea without using a single rare.

However you build your deck, just be sure to bring lots of pennies, six-sided dice, glass beads, corn chips, or whatever else you use for counters.

### GambleGamble

Poor [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble). Red's silly little tutor just never seemed as good as [Vampiric Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampiric+Tutor), [Enlightened Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor), or even [Eladamri's Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eladamri%27s+Call). The fact that you might lose what you searched for always seemed like too much of a . . . a . . . gamble to take seriously. The only decks that could previously find a use for [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) were combo decks like [Sneak Attack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack), which could afford the risk if it meant instant victory.

Luckily, *Odyssey* block has made discarding fun for the whole family. Now [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) can retrieve cards playable from the graveyard via flashback ([Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) and [Volley of Boulders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volley+of+Boulders)) or even from a discard via madness ([Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) and [Violent Eruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Eruption)). A particularly funny trick is to [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) for a [Recoup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup) in a sorcery-heavy deck, allowing you to smile away the random discard--or even to flashback [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) itself.

One tenet of using [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble) has always been to try to keep your hand as full as possible to minimize your chances of tossing away important spells. In the aforementioned sorcery-heavy deck, both [Overmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overmaster) and [Browbeat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat) seem ideal.

In addition, *Odyssey* block provides both the perfect creature for a sorcery deck and the perfect red threshold creature if you happen to be filling your graveyard.

### Iridescent DrakeIridescent Drake

Lost amidst the ridiculous power level of the Urza block is an interesting thematic cycle of cards that includes [Academy Researchers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Researchers); [Iridescent Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iridescent+Drake); [Metathran Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metathran+Elite); [Rayne, Academy Chancellor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rayne%2C+Academy+Chancellor); and [Thran Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thran+Golem). The idea behind these creatures was to make creature enchantments more playable and less ripe for card disadvantage. Yet even the power of [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) (also in Urza block, sigh) could not persuade most people to use them.

[Armadillo Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak) aside, [Elephant Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) is probably the most playable creature enchantment to come along since [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor). As an added bonus, [Elephant Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) just loves being used twice (or three times, or four . . .) to create a herd of Elephant tokens. In Standard, [Nomad Mythmaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Mythmaker) allows for recursive [Elephant Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) tricks, but in Extended, I might as well dip into blue. [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) still being legal in Extended makes for at least eight legitimate creature enchantments for such a deck--something that might look something of like this . . . .

### Flicker

Finally, who doesn't love [Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker)? Take a card out of play and then immediately plop it right back into play. My only consternation is that it isn't an instant, which would allow you to give opposing creatures "summoning sickness" at opportune times. Besides, doesn't "flicker" just *sound* like an instant? Ah well, it's still loads of fun with any "comes into play" or "leaves play" permanents.

Besides, now we have [Liberate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liberate), which is faster, although it misses the immediate payoff of [Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker) and can target only creatures. Just like [Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker), [Liberate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liberate) suggests hordes of activations and re-activations on any creature you play. But which to put in a deck? I say use them both and go crazy.
