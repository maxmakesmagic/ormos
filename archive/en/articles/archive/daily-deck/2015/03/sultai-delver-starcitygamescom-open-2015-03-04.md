Sultai Delver at StarCityGames.com Open
=======================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on March 4, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






I know not everyone will agree Sultai Delver is the strongest deck in Legacy, but I'm pretty sure not many will argue with it as "one of" the strongest decks.



[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is the most aggressive one-mana creature card printed. Think of it as a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) that somehow got flying simply by giving up a few points of toughness. Of course, the deck needs a certain number of instants or sorceries, but this is not a problem in Legacy, where blue decks have no lack of cheap cantrips,


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Delver+of+Secrets)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Insectile+Aberration)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration)Transform

[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)'s requirements are so easy to fulfill, which results in a variety of color combinations: The blue-red deck, loaded with burn spells. The Temur deck, adding powerful creatures. The almighty Jeskai deck. Finally, the Sultai Delver deck, which I will talk about today.



The deck's main color is blue. Green is the color of [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). Black brings the refueling [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), the convenient removal spell [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay), and [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman).



The black discard spells are in the sideboard. Main-board, the deck plays as a regular Delver deck and adds disruption in the combo matchups after sideboard. I like the plan of playing more disruption rather than adding counterspells. This way, you can see what your opponent's up to while removing disruption or a key card, with a counterspell in hand to seal up the game.



It's a simple but effective game plan—my favorite.







#### Jim Davis's Sultai Delver—1st Place, StarCityGames.com Legacy Open
