Karn's Legacy
=============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 17, 2013 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Luis Scott-Vargas











I've always liked the idea of colorless Commander decks, and while browsing Commander lists on TCGplayer, I found a [Karn, Silver Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn%2C+Silver+Golem)[list made by a user named terry\_suave](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/deck.asp?deck_id=1139269). In order to harness the power of [Karn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn) as your Commander, you do have to give up colored spells of any kind, which is a big cost. Luckily, that cost is more than made up for by sweetness, as [Karn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn) opens the door for all sorts of cool interactions. 

There are clearly too many combinations to list, but here are a few of my favorite ones:

[Rings of Brighthearth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rings+of+Brighthearth) + almost anything = value. There are a ton of ways to go infinite, but my favorite combos are the ones that are explicitly not infinite. It's so much more fun to just make a bunch of [Lux Cannon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lux+Cannon) counters or [Phyrexian Processor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Processor) tokens than to go off and win the game. 

[Temporal Aperture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Aperture) + Eldrazi ([Ulamog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog), [Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=193632), etc.). If you are lucky enough to flip an Eldrazi, you get the full benefit, which is awesome. There are also plenty of other very expensive spells, so even a "miss" might not be. 

[Darksteel Forge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Forge) + [Nevinyrral's Disk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk). This may not be the most amusing for your opponents, but getting to blow up the world every turn is powerful indeed. 

[Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) + seven mana. You may not know this, but [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) is one of my favorite cards, and just casting it is enough to make me happy. 

 Past the combos, this deck has enough huge monsters and powerful cards that I know I'd have a blast playing it. Accelerating into [Myr Battlesphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Battlesphere) or [Steel Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Hellkite) sounds awesome, and as long as you stay away from hitting the infinite combo space too hard, your opponents shouldn't feel it too necessary to kill you first.
