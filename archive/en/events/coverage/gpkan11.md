LSV Vapor Snags a Trophy in Kansas City
=======================================




June 18-19, 2011












Grand Prix Kansas City 2011
---------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpkan11/lsv_620.jpg) Luis Scott-Vargas capped an amazing week by defeating Yuuya Watanabe in the finals of Grand Prix Kansas City. His victory comes on the heels of a Top 8 at Pro Tour Nagoya last weekend and a win in the **Magic Online**  Community Cup during the week at Wizards of the Coast headquarters in Renton, Washington. It is the fourth Grand Prix trophy for Scott-Vargas' crowded mantle and -- combined with his Top 8 in Nagoya -- thrusts him right into the Player of the Year race at the halfway mark of the season.


Yuuya Watanabe, the Player of the Year in 2009, had expected to be playing in side drafts on Sunday when he came out of the bye rounds with two quick losses -- he didn't even win a game. Then, with his back against the wall, he started to win and did not stop until he ran afoul of LSV in the finals. All told he won 12 straight matches before his finals exit. Watanabe crossed into 200 lifetime Pro Point territory this weekend in a very short time on the Pro Tour.


Veterans Tim Aten and Willy Edel showed everyone they still had their chops despite no longer playing at every single event with their Top 8 performances while four players -- Zach Jesse, Gregory Jolin, Samuel Friedman, and Matt Costa -- made their debuts on the Sunday stage.


It has been a grueling several weeks for many pros as they have traversed the globe from Prague to Providence to Singapore to Nagoya and back to Kansas City -- with a brief stop in Seattle for a certain Grand Prix champion -- and everyone will enjoy a well-deserved break as we segue into Nationals season and prepare for the release of ***Magic**  2012*.

Top 8 Draft: Drafting with Yuuya Watanabe
-----------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin*

 

Yuuya Watanabe started off his Top 8 draft with a [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), a [Porcelain Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porcelain+Legionnaire), and a [Pristine Talisman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman). Watanabe made his first non-artifact, non-Phyrexian mana pick of the Draft when he took a [Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent) fourth.


Watanabe had just 3-0ed his previous draft pod with Blue-Black Infect to lock up his Top 8 berth, so he was certainly open to drafting Blue Infect should the opportunity present itself to him.


From there he took a [Sickleslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickleslicer), and a sixth pick [Reaper of Sheoldred](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+of+Sheoldred) – suggesting that Infect could very well be open.

Despite getting passed multiple good infect creatures late, there were no more on-color infectors in store for Watanabe, and he instead rounded out his pack with [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge), [Kiln Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Walker), [Lost Leonin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+Leonin), [Maul Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maul+Splicer) and a [Dementia Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dementia+Bat)s.


Watanabe ended his *New Phyrexia* pack with six colorless (or Phyrexian mana) cards, so he would be able to go in any direction during his *Mirrodin Besieged* pack. Watanabe knew that if he opened or got passed a bomb and/or some solid removal that he would be able to take it and build his deck around it.


Watanabe started off his *Mirrodin Besieged* pack with a [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) that he took over [Burn the Impure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burn+the+Impure) and [Copper Carapace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Copper+Carapace). Next up he took a [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) out of a pack that also contained [Spread the Sickness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spread+the+Sickness).


[Blisterstick Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blisterstick+Shaman), and [Virulent Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Virulent+Wound) insured that Watanabe would be able to deal with opposing [Porcelain Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porcelain+Legionnaire)s and [Embersmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Embersmith)s. A fifth pick [Phyrexian Rager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Rager) made a nice addition to Watanabe's Black-Red deck.


Watanabe was visibly happy when he got an eight pick [Lumengrid Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumengrid+Gargoyle), as that would give him a very real way to win, and seemed almost as pleased when he got a tenth pick [Ogre Resister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Resister).


At this point Watanabe had a very reasonable Black-Red deck, but it was clear that his deck needed some more power (or a lot more removal) for it to be where he wanted it.


A [Carnifex Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnifex+Demon) followed by a second pick [Skinrender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinrender) certainly made Watanabe's deck a lot stronger, but the rest of his pack really didn't have anything that good for Watanabe's Red-Black deck.

Top 8 Draft: Drafting with Willy Edel
-------------------------------------


*by Ben Swartz*

 

Willy Edel started off his Top 8 draft with an [Artillerize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artillerize) out of a relatively weak pack. He followed that up with a second pick [Blinding Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Souleater) over a [Grim Affliction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Affliction) showing that he had a preference of pairing red with white in this draft format. He added another [Artillerize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artillerize) and an [Immolating Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Souleater) to his deck before having a tough pick four: [Forced Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forced+Worship) or [Invader Parasite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invader+Parasite). He decided to go with the [Forced Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forced+Worship) passing the 3/2 to his left. Edel then rounded out his *New Phyrexia*  pack with a pair of [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s, looking for more aggressive red and white cards out of his *Mirrodin Besieged* pack.

Edel opened his *Mirrodin Besieged* pack and searched for anything that would go into his deck. The only playable red or white card was a [Banishment Decree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banishment+Decree). Assuming it would come back to him 9th pick, Edel decided to take the off color rare, [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm), in the off chance that he might switch into blue. Edel got passed another weak pack with [Flayer Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+Husk) being the only card that would be good in his deck. He was relieved to get passed third and sixth pick [Leonin Skyhunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Skyhunter)s and a fourth pick [Skinwing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinwing), passing up on a [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) along the way. Edel managed to pick up a [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) before ending *Mirrodin Besieged* on a [Crush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush) and a [Banishment Decree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banishment+Decree). Looking into the final pack of the draft, Edel had a solid Red White deck hoping to profit from the many white fliers in Scars of Mirrodin.


Edel was greeted by just that in his *Scars of Mirrodin* pack: a [Kemba's Skyguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kemba%27s+Skyguard). However, he opted to take Cerebral Erruption over it. Edel got passed another [Kemba's Skyguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kemba%27s+Skyguard) pick two before taking a [Clone Shell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone+Shell) over nothing and an [Origin Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Origin+Spellbomb). Edel decided to counter draft a [Volition Reins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volition+Reins) over a [Saberclaw Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saberclaw+Golem) fifth pick. From there Edel finished out his *Scars of Mirrodin* pack with a [Darksteel Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Sentinel) and a [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol) giving him an aggressive Red-White deck that he hoped would be fast enough to defeat his first round opponent, Yuuya Watanabe.

Quarterfinals: Yuuya Watanabe vs. Willy Edel
--------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

 

This was the most evenly matched of all the quarterfinals in terms of experience on the so-called Sunday Stage. Edel has played on Sunday in two Pro Tours -- one more than Yuuya -- reaching the finals both times. Watanabe has the edge on Player of the Year titles and with his Top 8 here this weekend is assured of crossing into the 200 lifetime Pro Points club. Edel, has a way to go to catch up in that department and a win in this event would close the gap just a hair. It is also safe to say that you could hardly find two players in the room who had traveled from further away on each side of the match-up; Yuuya from Japan and Willy Edel from Brazil.


### Game 1


Edel led things off on the play with a turn two [Leonin Skyhunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Skyhunter), he had been drafting aggressive white decks all day long and this one was no exception. Watanabe wasted no time Going for the Throat of the flier on his second turn. Edel frowned at this turn of events and played his third land and prepared to cycle an Orgin Spellbomb at the end of Watanabe's turn.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpkan11/qf_edel.jpg)*Willy Edel*


Watanabe had a little deck velocity of his own and played [Phyrexian Rager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Rager) and they both drew a card in his third turn. [Skinwing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinwing) joined the fray for Edel but he did not block the Rager as Watanabe drew blood and added [Bloodshot Trainee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodshot+Trainee) to his team. Edel's deck was not looking exactly "aggresive" as he played [Loxodon Partisan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Partisan) which Watanabe whittled down to a managable size with [Skinrender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinrender).


Edel attacked in the air and played [Forced Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forced+Worship) on the 'render. Yuuya just dialed up the heat and attacked with both his eligible creatures. Edel chumped with the Myr token and the score was 15 to 12 in Watanabe's favor. He played [Moriok Reaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moriok+Reaver) and [Porcelain Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porcelain+Legionnaire) -- going to 13 -- but Edel had [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) for the Legionnaire.


Edel could not reasonably attack and it was a good thing that he did not since [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) sprung into the red zone -- ultimately trading with a germ token -- but Edel took 10 from unblocked creatures. He was at 2 and scooped on the next turn.


### Game 2


Flayed Husk for Edel attacked for one and was followed up with Skyhunter but Watanabe had the [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) again. Edel followed up with [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol) while Watanabe played [Phyrexian Rager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Rager) -- dropping him to 17. Edel played [Brass Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Squire) and swung in for 2 more with his Idol.


[Sickleslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickleslicer) for Watanabe could only look skyward as the Idol continued to chip away. Watanabe's [Kuldotha Ringleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Ringleader) submitted to [Forced Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forced+Worship) and he fell to 11but a [Lumengrid Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumengrid+Gargoyle) stemmed the bleeding. Edel could not attack and when Watanabe played [Trigon of Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trigon+of+Rage) he attacked with everyone. Edel shrugged and took 8 and Yuuya reinforced with Ogre Register. Edel's [Artillerize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artillerize) did 5 at end of the turn and a second volley finished the job after the Idol flew over 2.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpkan11/qf_yuuya.jpg)*Yuuya Watanabe*


### Game 3


Legionnaire led off for Watanabe while Edel had one of his Skyhunters. Watanabe played a turn three [Kiln Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Walker) that was promptly killed with [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) but he smashed into the red zone for a brutally fast 8 points on turn 4 with [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge). Edel had to sit back even though his [Brass Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Squire) he just played was not allowed to block thanks to the Hero. Edel traded the Skyhunter for the Hero but he was down to 8 as Watanabe played Ogre Register.


[Skinwing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinwing) came down for Edel which could have made for some interesting interactions with [Brass Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Squire) if Watanabe did not have the [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), paying four life, to kill it. He had enough mana left over to play [Trigon of Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trigon+of+Rage) and attack Edel down to 2. He conceded a turn later.


Final result: Yuuya Watanabe advanced to the semifinals over Willy Edel 2 games to 1.




Quarterfinals Roundup
---------------------


*by Steve Sadin*

 

**Luis Scott-Vargas vs. Zach Jesse**


Luis Scott-Vargas defeated Zach Jesse in two quick games. In Game 1 Jesse played three pieces of equipment, but no creatures – and in the second game Jesse's only creature was a [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf). Once Luis Scott-Vargas was able to deal with the [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) (which was backed by a bunch of giant growth effects) he was able to ride his [Scrapdiver Serpent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapdiver+Serpent) to victory.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpkan11/qf_jesse-lsv.jpg)*Zach Jesse and Luis Scott-Vargas*


**Tim Aten vs. Matt Costa**


Tim Aten defeated Matt Costa in a three game set. After falling in the first game, Aten put the pressure on hard to start Game 2. Costa managed to "stabilize" at 2 life, but was unable to stop Aten's [Elspeth Tirel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth+Tirel).


In the third game Costa went to the air with a [Moltensteel Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moltensteel+Dragon), paying the full four life to be able to cast it. A [Banishment Decree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banishment+Decree) forced Costa to pay another four life to recast his dragon, which allowed Aten to burn him out with a [Kuldotha Flamefiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Flamefiend) and a [Concussive Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concussive+Bolt).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpkan11/qf_aten-costa.jpg)*Tim Aten and Matt Costa*


**Greg Jolin vs. Sam Friedman**


It looked like Greg Jolin was mere moments away from the Semifinals when his board consisted of a [Hexplate Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hexplate+Golem), a [Flameborn Viron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flameborn+Viron) and a [Tangle Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Angler) equipped with a [Grafted Exoskeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafted+Exoskeleton) that he had stolen with a [Corrupted Conscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupted+Conscience) to Sam Friedman's six lands. However, this was not to be.


Friedman lived through Jolin's attack by flashing in a [Darksteel Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Sentinel), he then killed off Jolin's postcombat [Silver Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Myr) with a [Blisterstick Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blisterstick+Shaman) and moved his [Grafted Exoskeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafted+Exoskeleton) onto his [Darksteel Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Sentinel). The stolen [Tangle Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Angler) died as a result of [Grafted Exoskeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafted+Exoskeleton) becoming unattached from it, and Friedman was able to swing in for lethal poison damage with his [Darksteel Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Sentinel) – moving onto the semifinals in dramatic fashion.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpkan11/qf_friedman-jolin.jpg)*Sam Friedman and Greg Jolin*




Semifinals: Luis Scott-Vargas vs. Sam Friedman
----------------------------------------------


*by Ben Swartz*

 

Fresh off of his Top 8 finish at Pro Tour Nagoya last weekend, Luis Scott-Vargas clawed his way to the semifinals of Grand Prix Kansas City.


His opponent, Sam Friedman, a breakout player from this event, drafted a Red Green Dinosaur deck, which faced off against LSV's Blue-Black deck.


### Game 1


Both players kept their opening hands with Scott-Vargas making the first play of the game: [Flayer Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+Husk). Scott-Vargas followed that up with a [Perilous Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr), while Friedman fetched up a third land with his [Mycosynth Wellspring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Wellspring). Friedman played a [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) announcing that, "this card is unimpressive without creatures."


Friedman's creature, [Rot Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rot+Wolf), got countered by Scott-Vargas's [Stoic Rebuttal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoic+Rebuttal), which allowed Scott-Vargas to continue bringing the beats before using [Despise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Despise) to counter Friedman's [Pith Driller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pith+Driller).

Friedman drew and played [Carapace Forger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carapace+Forger) stopping Scott-Vargas's beats momentarily, while both players were stuck on three lands.


Friedman attempted to equip and attack with his [Carapace Forger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carapace+Forger), but LSV quickly [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag)ged it. Scott-Vargas's two 1/1s had dealt ten damage by this point, but Scott-Vargas had a bigger game in mind: [Sangromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sangromancer). Still stuck on three lands, Friedman had no play and LSV continued to add 3/3 fliers to the board, this time in [Sky-Eel School](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sky-Eel+School). With no answers to the fliers Friedman quickly conceded.


**Luis Scott-Vargas 1 – Sam Friedman 0** 


Friedman bemoaned about the tempo advantage that was afforded to LSV by the [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag)—without it he felt that he could have won the game.


### Game 2


Friedman chose to draw first for the second game and both players kept their opening hands. Many people would be impressed with someone simply being awake at this point if they were in Luis Scott-Vargas' very well-traveled shoes. Yuuya Watanabe knows a thing or two about traveling and winning himself. During his Player of the Year tear in 2009 he rattled off a string of finishes that would be make up a career worthy of envy for most Magic players in something like 2 months. If he could win this match he would tie LSV with three Grand Prix wins for his career. Prior to the match, LSV predicted that Watanabe would get there easily.

Five Cards That Mattered in KC
------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall and Steve Sadin*


![Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vapor+Snag)

### Vapor Snag


If you blinked for a minute you would have missed Luis Scott-Vargas' blazingly fast win in game one of the finals. You need only look at the two copies of [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag+)  in his graveyard to understand what transpired as the Pro Tour Berlin champion was able to keep the gas pedal pressed all the way down while clearing the path and sneaking in some extra life loss along the way. In the second game [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag+)  was used defensively to protect the throat of his [Sangromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sangromancer+)  from an inconvenient removal spell. In game three it provided the clear path for the killing blow with his opponent at five, four points of attackers, and one nervous looking chump blocker that was bounced to Yuuya Watanabe's hand.



![Pith Driller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pith+Driller)

### Pith Driller


When *New Phyrexia*  hit stores a couple of weeks ago, [Pith Driller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pith+Driller+)  was just another card. Sure you would play it in your draft deck, but it wasn’t the kind of thing that you would go out of your way to take. Now it’s regarded as [one of the top commons in the set](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/grand-prix-kansas-city-day-1-coverage-2011-06-18).



![Soul Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Soul+Conduit)

### Soul Conduit


Don't go drafting this card highly or anything but it was a lesson in card evaluation during the first draft when Owen Turtenwald went 2-1 with a blue-black deck that he was not happy to have drafted. He did have [Life's Finale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life%27s+Finale+)  but the rest of the deck could be described as awkward at best. Realizing that he was going to be taking a beating from his opponents early he decided to play the [Treasure Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage)-able [Soul Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Conduit+)  as a way to regain lost ground. The card won him multiple games -- some by virtue of it being a card players had no experience against -- and he got people to reevaluating a card that most pros had relegated to the stone-cold unplayable pile in their draft experience. Do not be surprised if you see someone trying to get the [Trespassing Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trespassing+Souleater)/[Soul Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Conduit) combo on in an 8-4 draft sometime in the near future.



![Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blighted+Agent)

### Blighted Agent


While many players have intentionally chosen to shy away from blue, white, and red infect creatures, other players have chosen to embrace them. And with great success, I might add. Yuuya Watanabe needed to 3-0 his second draft pod in order to make it to the Top 8. He was able to pull off this feat while piloting a Blue-Black Infect deck full of proliferate, and equipment. In some games, Watanabe would get in a couple of early hits with his [Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent), and then grind his opponent out with Throne of Geth activations – in other games, Watanabe simply made his [Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent+)  into a unblockable three power infector which he would use to make short work of his hapless opponent.


![Twisted Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Twisted+Image)

### Twisted Image


[Twisted Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Image+)  is the kind of card that players typically use to marginal effect in Limited. When it does end up in a player’s deck, it usually gets cycled in search of an extra land or used to punch through an extra couple of damage in an already aggressive deck. But in the hands of a skilled player it can do so much more.


In the second game of the finals, Yuuya Watanabe was able to dominate play thanks to his combination of [Bloodshot Trainee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodshot+Trainee+)  and [Trigon of Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trigon+of+Rage). Before he began the final game of the tournament, Luis Scott-Vargas looked through his sideboard for a way to mitigate the effectiveness of his opponent’s power-enhancing artifacts, and he found [Twisted Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Image).


While Scott-Vargas didn’t end up getting the favorable interaction against [Trigon of Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trigon+of+Rage) that he was looking for when he boarded in his Twisted Image, he was still able to use it to great effect – sneaking in an extra three points of damage with his [Neurok Replica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Replica+)  – a play that caused the normally stoic Watanabe to let out an audible squeak.
