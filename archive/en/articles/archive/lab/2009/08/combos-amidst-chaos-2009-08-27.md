Combos amidst Chaos
===================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on August 27, 2009 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hey there, and welcome to this laboratory of fun! Today I am taking a special note on the current state of **Magic** craziness, as there is an abnormal amount of it in comparison to regular Septembers.


What do I mean exactly? Now that ***Magic** 2010* is officially old news, attention would normally shift to *Zendikar*, the upcoming large set. However, there are some certain ... distractions swirling around this fall. Not only is *Zendikar* releasing to great attention around the Internets, but *Planechase* is as well! The vastly fun multiplayer format releases extremely close to *Zendikar*. I would say more about it, but we'll save that for next week, I think.


To round out the bubbling trio, *Masters Edition III* is breaking the surface for **Magic Online** devotees. That is hitting the electronic shelves this September as well, making for a very cramped back-to-school month. I guess schoolwork will have to fall under the "optional" list.


So what to talk about today, in a week in the eye of the storm? [**Magic** shoes](/en/articles/archive/future-shifted-2009-07-15)? [**Magic** soda](/en/articles/archive/magical-jones-soda-2009-07-14)? **Magic** swimming trunks? (Coming soon ...?) How about a set I haven't mentioned yet today: *Alara Reborn*! If ***Magic** 2010* can be momentarily forgotten in this chaos, *Alara Reborn* must already be fading from your minds. Not to worry, though! There are still some fun things to do with cards from "codename Scissors," which I will outline ... below!


### Triple Double


When I read a spoiler for a new set for the first time, I mark the cards that produced a visceral reaction. Some of these cards wind up becoming my favorite cards in the set. Of course, I also take flavor text and art and fun-based playability into account. For an immediate gasp of awe, though, not much beats [Sen Triplets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sen+Triplets).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl53_sen1.jpg)  
Although the ability of [Sen Triplets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sen+Triplets) is condensed to one giant text line, there's a lot of things going on with it. Let's go through its mini-abilities one at a time. (I'll use its Oracle text, which isn't much different from the original.)


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl53_sen2.jpg)  
**At the beginning of your upkeep, choose target opponent.** – This one is necessary, but not exactly combo material. Moving on.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl53_sen3.jpg)  
**This turn, that player can't cast spells** – I'm breaking this sentence into thirds. Since this ability triggers on your turn, your opponent is therefore prevented from playing instants or things with flash on your turn. Therefore, the spells you play on your turn can't be countered.


**or activate abilities** – This is another method of completely nullifying your opponent on your turn, ensuring that he or she can't even cycle cards or tap permanents.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl53_sen4.jpg)  
**and plays with his or her hand revealed. –** So, [Sen Triplets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sen+Triplets) is also a pseudo-[Telepathy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telepathy). Sure, it only works on your turn, but that should still give you a good idea of what to expect when you pass the turn.


**You may play cards from that player's hand until end of turn. –** This is the bomby effect that I love. However, if you're not careful, it could lose its fruitfulness. If you go crazy and play everything from your opponent's hand at once, you deplete it that quickly, effectively reducing [Sen Triplets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sen+Triplets) to a one-shot effect. There are certainly merits to this HyperSenesis, but I'm going to try playing things a little slower. Besides, you won't always have the appropriate mana to cast your opponent's spells, even though you can play lands from his or her hand as well. If he or she is piloting a [Boartusk Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boartusk+Liege) deck, complete with [Boggart Ram-Gang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang)s, you could be in trouble.


I'm going to try to keep this Standard-legal, just for fun. Now what's a good way to fix mana? [Elsewhere Flask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elsewhere+Flask) makes all your lands a new type, [Terraformer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terraformer)-style. [Exotic Orchard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exotic+Orchard) is perfect, since your opponent's lands already have the right colors.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Exotic Orchard | Fabricate |

[Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate), newly in Standard, seems a good way to fetch [Sen Triplets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sen+Triplets), while [Wall of Denial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Denial) is a good wall to hide behind pre-Triplets. Bounce effects can stall early on and place threats back in the opposing hand for the Triplets to steal. [Call to Heel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+Heel) seems mediocre at first, but adding cards to a hand that's also yours sounds good to me. [Thoughtcutter Agent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcutter+Agent) (another artifact for [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate)) can play [Telepathy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telepathy) for the early game, as can [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) is another early annoying creature, but after a [Sen Triplets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sen+Triplets), it gains another layer of usefulness by filtering your opponent's hand, which is really your hand. Altogether, this is a pretty straightforward look at using and abusing [Sen Triplets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sen+Triplets) with some synergistic components alongside it.

### A Convicts Glen


![Cloven Casting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cloven+Casting)While fantastic [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver)-esque effects excite me, big and expensive enchantments with interesting effects intrigue me. Unfortunately, in all the Magical chaos of the summer I've completely forgotten about [Cloven Casting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloven+Casting), which might have been a flagship card for *Alara Reborn* in a parallel universe. I mean, it promotes playing multicolored cards! Okay, so [Defiler of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defiler+of+Souls) does that too, but does that guy let you play double [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum)?


Didn't think so.


So doubling up on [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) is a definite beating. That's not the only gold spell to clove, however. Imagine following that up with a double [Dream Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Salvage)! Although this trick requires ten mana (eight of it colored), it's pretty devastating, and totally worth it. Besides that slice of insanity, this deck offers simpler, yet just as fine, tricks, such as double [Grixis Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grixis+Charm)s and twin [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate)s. Again, this is simply another straightforward deck using a quirky *Alara Reborn* rare with the Standard card pool.

### Postmodern


There are lots of different directions that I could head with [Mage Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage+Slayer). I mean, it's a cool, red and green Equipment that gives its bearer a weird version of double strike: deal damage *as* it attacks, [Rage Forger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Forger) style.


I could go with another relatively straightforward deck based around high-powered creatures, or I could build an Equipment-subthemed deck. But this time I am going to get a little wackier. Not full blown crazy, but a little off the beaten path.


![Mage Slayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mage+Slayer)  
I'm talking about certain creatures from the olden days. Specific creatures that, during combat, gave you a choice between dealing damage or a different effect. For reference, some of the more prominent ones here are [Bone Dancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Dancer) and [Dwarven Vigilantes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dwarven+Vigilantes). Keeper of Tresserhorn goes a step further, and doesn't even give you the option of dealing damage normally.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Bone Dancer | Keeper of Tresserhorn |

Often, it was hard to choose between the damage or the effect. Do I [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) a creature or hit for 2? Do I [Zombify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify) something or hit for 2? And do I really want a 6/6 that casts [Misery Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misery+Charm) (the third choice) instead of hitting for 6?


[Mage Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage+Slayer) evens the odds a bit. Now Dwarven Vigilantes becomes a fully fledged [Lunge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lunge). [Bone Dancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Dancer) beats for 2 and rises something from the grave. Keeper of Tresserhorn becomes marginally better with a [Mage Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage+Slayer) in it's hand.


What are some other creatures and effects like this? Say hello to the [Laccolith cards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?name=+%5Blaccolith%5D) of *Nemesis*! They're very similar, differing only in that they must be blocked in order to activate. So a mere [Laccolith Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Laccolith+Grunt) with a [Mage Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage+Slayer) becomes a threat.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Laccolith Grunt | Carrion Wurm |

The two [Carrion creatures from *Torment*](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advanced&name=+%5Bcarrion%5D&set=%7C%5B%22torment%22%5D) become virtually drawbackless with a [Mage Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage+Slayer) in their capable grip. Your opponent will really have to think about exiling those cards in the graveyard, since [Mage Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage+Slayer) is already dealing 6 damage from the [Carrion Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Wurm). To go with the Carrion creatures, the aforementioned [Bone Dancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Dancer) and [Rysorian Badger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rysorian+Badger) step in, a mismatched pair that offer more choices for you.


We wind up with a fun Jund-colored deck that uses both old cards and new ones together in weird combat synergy.
