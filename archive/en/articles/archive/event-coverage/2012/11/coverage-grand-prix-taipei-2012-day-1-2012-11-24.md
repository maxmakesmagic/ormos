Saturday, 12:05 p.m. – Grinders Winning Decklists
-------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

 

Quite surprisingly, none of the winning decklists contained any [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) s, indicating that there is still hope for players who are not so fortunate to receive a copy of the pesky rodents in their card pool. Of the players who went 5-0 yesterday, 6 of them were affiliated with Selesnya (mostly with a touch of Golgari for some removal) and 4 of them with Rakdos. Perhaps this is an indication of greater things to come!




![Vitu-Ghazi Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vitu-Ghazi+Guildmage)

*The next best thing after [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) s perhaps?*

Saturday, 1:42 p.m. – Rubbing Shoulders with the Local Community
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

 

Needing just about 70 more players to more than triple the previous turnout in 2008, today is a good day for Taiwanese **Magic** history. 726 players converged to play **Magic**, enjoy themselves and meet new (and old) friends. Home to the World **Magic** Cup Champion Team and Platinum Pro Kuo Tzu Ching, she is no longer an unknown city in a world of professional **Magic**.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/JaceTraditional.jpg) *[Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) in Traditional Chinese, exclusive to Taiwan and Hong Kong!* 
A group of passionate players has fueled the growth of the game rapidly, in part due to the local community website, CardWalker. Jim Jen, who has been playing **Magic** for over 15 years, says it was tough for local players to keep up with the game on a global scale, in part due to the language barrier. Players have had to scour English and Japanese sites for information, latest deck tech and tournament results. Thus, he decided to create a Chinese website to aid the local community by providing invaluable resources.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_CardWalker.jpg) *Jim Jen, Grand Prix Taipei 2000 Top 8 competitor and founder of CardWalker* 
Jen is a marketing executive by day and manages the local website by night, also often utilizing many precious weekends that could be otherwise devoted to shopping at local malls and night markets for instance. With over 15 years of experience in competitive play, he is often seen as a figurehead in the local community. Aside from Taiwan, CardWalker has made quite a name for itself in mainland China, seeing how their audience is also predominantly Mandarin-speaking.


In 2012, CardWalker was determined to do more for the Taiwan community. It saw many new players enter the realm, but some left as they had no place to gain a deeper understanding of the game. Thus, CardWalker decided to partner up with a local card shop to hold "**Magic** School" events, where newer players were able to learn how to pick up the game and be introduced to the various formats (understanding what Vintage, Legacy, Modern, Standard, Booster Draft, Sealed Deck, Commander and Two-Headed Giant is a daunting task for an amateur). Though these workshops, new players to become fully immersed in the mystical land of **Magic**.


Just two weeks ago, CardWalker held a tournament to commemorate it's second year since inception which attracted 124 participants, the largest non-Grand Prix event to be held in Taiwan within the last few years, a testament to the Taiwanese's dedication to the game.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_CardMaster.jpg) *Professional Model and Cosplayer Ashley, and Card Master Games store-owner Wu Junxian (more affectionately known as Gua Gua!)* 
One of the most popular stores in Taipei, Card Master Games, has also went one step further to delight the local players to great effect!!! Aside from being one of the designated retailers of the event, they have also engaged in a professional model and cosplayer to dress up as Liliana!


Taiwan isn't a huge place, but it's enthusiasm for **Magic** definitely doesn't lose to any other country!

*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

 

Fresh off of his Top 8 in Grand Prix Shanghai earlier this year, Nick Wong is looking to build on his solid history in the Asia-Pacific region. Fresh off of an 18 month suspension that kept him out of the **Magic** Hall of Fame, Tomoharu Saito is looking to build a more respectable reputation to match his once fearsome one.


Entering round 5 with a loss each, both Saito and Wong had allied themselves with the Izzet Guild. Saito unleashed an early [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker) , which quickly bounced off Wong's [Doorkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doorkeeper) . Wong [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) 'd Saito's [Cobblebrute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobblebrute) , but he followed that up with a [Runewing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runewing) , which hung back to defend against Wong's [Izzet Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Keyrune) .


Saito topped up his hand with a [Thoughtflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtflare) , and when Wong went to summon a [Skyline Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyline+Predator) , Saito broke its back with an [Essence Backlash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Backlash) , marking the first lifetotal change of the match. Wong summoned a Chainwalker of his own, and a [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) bounced the [Runewing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runewing) for a turn. Saito replayed his [Runewing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runewing) , and a pair of [Rakdos Shred-Freak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Shred-Freak) s, emptying his hand.


Wong attacked with his Keyrune, and Saito accepted the trade with one of his Freaks. Wong then summoned an [Azor's Elocutors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azor%27s+Elocutors) . Saito's [Runewing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runewing) disrupted their filibusting for a turn, until Wong sent them in [Pursuit of Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pursuit+of+Flight) .


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R6_Wong.jpg) *Nick Wong tries to filibust his way to victory.* 
Saito unleashed a [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) , and a turn later, pronounced an [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction) on the Elocutors, also detaining the [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) with an [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) , leaving Wong with only the [Doorkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doorkeeper) on defence. Saito attacked with the 5/4 Giant, 2/2 [Runewing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runewing) , 2/1 Shred-Freak, a 3/3 [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug) , and the 3/2 Chainwalker, dropping Wong to just 3 life.


Wong's [Rakdos Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildgate) allowed him to [Dreadbore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore) the Skywatch, but he couldn't soak up enough damage from Saito's next attack, and was left reaching for his sideboard.


Saito 1 – Wong 0


Wong mulliganed twice in game two, and was under attack from a Shred-Freak almost immediately. Wong threw away a [Skyline Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyline+Predator) to dig for gas with his [Viashino Racketeer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Racketeer) , while Saito pushed through 2 more damage with an [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction) . Wong played a [Rakdos Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune) , and managed to trade his Racketeer for the Shred-Freak when it came in the following turn. Saito again unleashed a [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) .


Wong bought a turn with a [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) , but the Giant was simply replaced by a [Batterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterhorn) , destroying the Keyrune. Wong summoned a [Splatter Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug) , but kept it on the leash, while Saito again unleashed his Giant, before playing a second [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction) on the Thug to push 4 damage past the 'Wielder.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R6_Saito.jpg) *Tomoharu Saito detains everything in his way.* 
Facing a pair of sizeable monsters, Wong could only pass back his turn with 6 mana untapped and the 'Weilder and Thug standing between himself and a battering. Saito happily summoned his [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) , again detaining the Thug, and attacked Wong down to 3 life for the second time this match. Wong drew his card for the turn, and used it to scoop up his permanents.


Saito 2 – Wong 0




Round 6 Feature Match – Sam Lau vs. Albertus Law
------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

 

Albertus Law and Sam Lau have been playing **Magic** for a really long time, for almost two decades now. Long enough that the newer players may not even know who they are. At the peak of their careers, Albertus broke into the Top 8 of two Grand Prix (winning Grand Prix Singapore 2001) and a Pro Tour all in the same season, while Sam is the proud owner of three champion trophies (claiming Grand Prix Singapore 2000 and two Singapore Nationals titles).


Both have taken a step back from **Magic** owing to their devotion to their careers but remain well-respected (and a little feared), especially within the Singaporean and Malaysian community.


### Game One


Albertus was forced to keep a one-lander after mulliganing down to six. Sam opened with [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) and [Sunspire Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspire+Griffin) , and proceeded flood the board with three 1/1 Soldier tokens. Albertus' deck failed to produce a second and third land in time and was quickly overrun by the time he managed to cast [Hover Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hover+Barrier) and [Lobber Crew](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew) .


"That wasn't a very interesting game actually," commented Sam. I couldn't have agreed more.


Sam Lau 1 – Albertus Law 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R6_Law.jpg) *Albertus Law* 
### Game Two


Albertus was ready with [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) to stop [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) , while both players matched [Sunspire Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspire+Griffin) s. [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) was able to push two damage at the expense of tapping two [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) , but Albertus would rather leave his mana untapped to represent a trick. Sam gummed up the board further with [Hover Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hover+Barrier) , causing a boring stalemale.


Albertus tried to break that monotony by playing a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and casting [Stab Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) , a sideboarded card which came in for this mirror match but Sam quickly undid that with [Dramatic Rescue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dramatic+Rescue) . Albertus shrugged and showed Sam a second copy and once again Lau undid that with [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) on his own [Hover Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hover+Barrier) , bringing back the game to a slow crawl.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R6_Lau.jpg) *Sam Lau* 
That was until Albertus resolved [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) and [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) in succession, creating an overpowering air force. Fortunately for Sam, he was able to reset the board with [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) , and add a threat of his own, in the form of [Skymark Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skymark+Roc) .


After that, he proceeded to summon two [Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul) s, which Albertus deftly answerered with [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) , but all Sam had to do was add another [Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul) and [Sunspire Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspire+Griffin) to seal up the game.


Sam Lau 2 – Albertus Law 0


\*owing to similar last names, both players' first names have been used in this Featured Match for easier reading.




Round 7 Feature Match – Huang Hao-shan vs. Moriya Toshihiko
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

### Game One


Both decks were pretty aggressive but if I had to choose, I'd give the edge to Huang, who started with triple [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler) s, all unleashed and usually very scary.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/Dead_Rev.jpg)*Not sure if really intimidating...*



However, Moriya was able to survive with a counteractive opening (which comprised [Vitu-Ghazi Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitu-Ghazi+Guildmage) , Eyes in the Sky, [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) and [Golgari Longlegs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Longlegs) ). Once the Japanese player had managed to stick two four-toughness creatures on the board, it rendered the trio of [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler) s useless because Huang didn't have a combat trick. Since they couldn't block, Moriya simply marched in for the win over two turns, while leaving some tokens behind as sacrificial lambs.


  

Huang 0 – Moriya 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R7_Moriya.jpg)
### Game Two


Huang was forced to mulligan down to six and sought to steal a win with [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) s. Moriya wasn't having any of that, and quickly pointed [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) at the rodents before they could procreate.


[Centaur's Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur%27s+Herald) eventually evolved into a 3/3 Centaur token, but Huang was able to stop it with [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion) . Moriya reloaded with [Towering Indrik](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Indrik) and [Golgari Longlegs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Longlegs) , and Huang seemed to be in trouble. Severely flooded, he stared at six lands on the battlefield, and two more in his hand, and decided to make a 5/5 with his final card ( [Slime Molding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slime+Molding) ), emptying his sleeve of tricks.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R7_Huang.jpg)
Moriya seemed to think the token was irrelevant and swung with both his creatures anyway. Sensing a trick, Huang declined to block and hoped to draw some gas. If he had blocked right there, he would be left with nothing on board and practically left with no other survival options.


His decisions seem to be founded by sheer willpower and his prayers were answered. When Moriya tried [Ultimate Price](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price) , Huang was able to punish him by recreating the token and exiling [Golgari Longlegs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Longlegs) with a freshly peeled [Trostani's Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment) .


Huang proceeded to rip [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) off the top, and it would seem like the tides had turned. A third successive topdeck in the form of [Daggerdrome Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp) (enhanced by re-utilizing the fallen [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion) ) ensured that Huang would be winning this race for sure.


Huang 1 – Moriya Toshihiko 1


### Game Three


Huang was stuck on four forests, but was able to make two 3/3 Centaurs, thanks to a pair of [Centaur's Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur%27s+Herald) s. Moriya, on the other hand, was stuck on two lands, a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) , and was forced to discard an awkward [Sunspire Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspire+Griffin) but he did receive [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage) within the next two turns.


Hao-shan was quick to capitalize on the situation and quickly recruited [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler) and [Sluiceway Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sluiceway+Scorpion) . By the time Moriya could draw his fourth land to call forth [Towering Indrik](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Indrik) , he was down to just six lowly life. Huang further pressed the attack with a second copy of [Dead Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler) , unleashing this one as well.


Moriya was not without a plan and he was blessed to draw a fifth land to execute it. Using [Common Bond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Common+Bond) and [Ultimate Price](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price) , he was able to reduce Huang's board to a single Centaur token, while maintaining a 4/6 "Giant Spider" on the board, stabilizing the situation.


The back-breaker and game-ender arrived two turns later. [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) made its appearance and wiped away Huang's board. Long story short, it's usually kind of hard to come back after facing this powerful mythic rare.


Moriya defeats Huang and is now at 6-1, while he sends Huang into the elimination bracket, and possibly the gallows.




![Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Angel+of+Serenity)

*Nasty or nice? It really depends which side you are on!* 
Huang 1 – Moriya Toshihiko 2




Saturday, 7:15 a.m. – Deck Construction with Lee Shi Tian
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

 

Grand Prix Birmingham 2008 Champ and more recently Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* Semifinalist is here at Taipei, ready to follow-up his recent finish with (hopefully) another trophy.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_STLee_deckbuilding.jpg)
"I'm so dead," giggled Lee, as he sat down and fanned through his card pool. It seems that his best "bomb" was [Grave Betrayal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Betrayal) , which caused me to cringe on the inside. "I don't think I have any other choice other than Golgari splashing some red, its not like I have other real options."


His white cards included a duo of [Sunspire Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunspire+Griffin) s, [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest) and [Avenging Arrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenging+Arrow) , but that was about it. Other noteworthy cards included [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) (which Antonino De Rosa believes to be the most underrated card in the format) and [Seller of Songbirds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seller+of+Songbirds) , but neither are good reasons to delve into white. His blue cards were similarly lackluster and were insufficient to provide for either an aggressive strategy, nor a controllish one.


Lee (member of newly-formed Team MTG Mint Card) explained to me that he usually starts by pulling out all the removal he had. That included two copies of [Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact) , [Auger Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree) and [Ultimate Price](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price) . If he dipped into green, he would receive [Aerial Predation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aerial+Predation) , to go alongside his [Corpsejack Menace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpsejack+Menace) . He seemed pretty satisfied with his suite of removal, but cringed when he proceeded to examine his mana curve.


Unfortunately for Lee, his one and two drops were terrible. He couldn't reliably cast [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) , and [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) didn't help him if he wanted to block. He was unable to splash for his double [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) s. In a word, disastrous. His strategy was to attempt to stay alive, in order to take over in the late game. Currently sitting at 3-2, he would need Lady Luck to shine upon him if he wished to advance into Day Two.

Round 8 Feature Match – Antonino De Rosa vs. Yong Han Choo
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

 

Antonino De Rosa is certainly a familiar name to **Magic** players of old, with 1 Pro Tour Top 8, and 11 Grand Prix Top 8's. De Rosa often finds himself out and about in the Asia-Pacific region for work, and has been seen battling in the local Grand Prix events a few times over the last couple of years.


De Rosa's opponent Yong Han Choo is no slouch himself. While he has only 1 Grand Prix Top 8, he finished in the Top 4 of Pro Tour Hollywood in 2008.


"Is your deck good?" De Rosa asked as they shuffled, "mine is kinda kooky, all of my wins have been 2-1, with 2 or 3 life left."


"It's just normal," was all Choo could reply, with a smile.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R8_Choo.jpg)
Yong Han Choo is not just a token feature match recipient.


Both players traded cards in the early game, with De Rosa [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest) ing Choo's [Korozda Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage) , and declaring [Martial Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Law) as the dust cleared. But Choo wasn't done, summoning an [Axebane Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian) that allowed him to summon a [Vitu-Ghazi Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitu-Ghazi+Guildmage) .


De Rosa's [Martial Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Law) was stuck tying up a [Golgari Decoy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Decoy) , while a [Voidwielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) nullified a 7/7 Ooze Token spat out by a [Slime Molding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slime+Molding) . However, De Rosa had no way to stop the Guildmage, which happily made Centaur after Centaur until Choo dropped a [Collective Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing) on the table.


"Oh, your deck is normal! De Rosa cried out, as he scooped up his cards, "such a good liar."


"No, it's..." Choo tried to reply


"It's ok man, I still like you," De Rosa assured, "even though you lied to me."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R8_DeRosa.jpg)*Antonino De Rosa is unimpressed with Choo's idea of "normal".*

Round 9 Feature Match – Shuhei Nakamura vs. Kuo Tzu Ching
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

 

Recently winning Grand Prix Philadelphia and Costa Rica 2012, Shuhei Nakamura is looking to capture just one more Top 8 appearance to finally catch up with Alex Shvartsman, who has been sitting on his twenty one Grand Prix Top 8s for almost a decade now. Kuo had also received an accolade recently, namely the World **Magic** Cup champion. Both players have impressive resumes but due to some unfortunate mishap, they have ended up in the elimination bracket.


### Game One


Kuo mulliganed to six but promptly opened with [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) , then passing his third turn without a third land. Nakamura was blessed with all three of his colors by then, and was able to summon [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) .


Drawing [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) off the top, Kuo detained the opposing drake with [Lyev Skyknight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyev+Skyknight) but Nakamura was ready with [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) . He followed up with [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) (successfully enchanting [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) ), prompting Kuo to kill the aura with his lone creature.


Reduced to an empty board, Kuo could only watch helplessly as he continued to take damage from the [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) and Knight token. When he did find a fourth land, [Armory Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armory+Guard) made an appearance, but a second [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) on the vigilant Knight token (in conjunction with [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) ) rendered it ineffective as either a blocker or an attacker.


Jace, Architect of Thought was Kuo's next move, but an ensuing attack and a blast of [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire) finished off the powerful Planeswalker. Nakamura forged ahead with [Skymark Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skymark+Roc) . Kuo could only delay his inevitable death with the "mini-combo" of Azorius Justicar and [Faerie Impostor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Impostor) .


Nakamura 1 – Kuo 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R9_Nakamura.jpg)
### Game Two


Kuo chose to play but before the game could get started, both players decided to switch their initial seven. Kuo's first few plays of New Pravh Guildmage, [Azorius Arrester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Arrester) and [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) were effectively nullified by a singular [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) . Kuo could only push minimal damage with an activated [Azorius Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Keyrune) .


Shuhei suited up an unleashed [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker) with [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) , but Kuo was ready with [Swift Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swift+Justice) , granting him five points of first-striking damage to off the 5/4 Human Warrior. Kuo then summoned [Sphinx of the Chimes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Chimes) , but Nakamura replied with Chaos Imp, declining to unleash, clearly preferring the option to trade as opposed to the currently irrelevant trample ability. He could only pray that Kuo's bag of tricks were exhausted, as he attacked with [Chaos Imps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Imps) , before summoning [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) to represent 11 points of flying damage should Kuo dare to counterattack.


Clearly at the end of his line, Kuo finally accepted Nakamura's trade, then attacking with all his "walking" guys, hoping to bring down some of the fliers which he couldn't block. It worked decently, at least forcing Nakamura to push [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) in front of his [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) .


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai12/01_R9_Kuo.jpg)
The flurry of assaults brought Nakamura to just six life, Kuo ten. Drawing [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) , Kuo was able to shut down the opposing blocker, before swinging with [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets) and Azorius Justicar, knocking down Nakamura to just two life.


Unfortunately, Nakamura's final card was [Eyes in the Skies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyes+in+the+Skies) , which enabled him to block all of Kuo's creatures the next turn, while finishing off all of them. [Skyline Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyline+Predator) ate up [Isperia's Skywatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) , two Bird tokens traded with Azorius Justicar, which [Frostburn Weird](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) munched up the [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets) .


Kuo Tzu Ching's tournament is over, while Shuhei Nakamura lives to fight another day.


Nakamura 2 – Kuo 0
