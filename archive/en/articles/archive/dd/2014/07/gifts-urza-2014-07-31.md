The Gifts of Urza
=================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 31, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






The [Urza's lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&name=+%5BUrza%5D&type=+%5B%22Land%22%5D&set=+%5B%22Ninth+Edition%22%5D)—Mine, Tower, and Power Plant—are explosive mana-producing lands that are often referred to as the UrzaTron. Typically, these are seen in red-green decks that are focused on assembling the three lands as quickly as possible. Today's deck is a little different, although this deck still makes great use of the Urza's lands.


![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gifts%20Ungiven)
This deck wants to prolong the game, allowing the deck to gradually build toward assembling the three Urza's lands. While [Expedition Map](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) is still present to help search up the correct land, this deck is lacking the explosiveness of its red-green cousin. In exchange, this deck gets some resiliency and is not as reliant on assembling the UrzaTron. This deck is perfectly capable of functioning as a normal control deck.


The other major reason to play white-blue is the [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) package that is capable of producing a powerful threat quickly. One common play with this deck is to cast [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) and only get two cards—[Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and a large creature, such as [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite). Because you only found two cards, they will both go to your graveyard, which conveniently lets you reanimate that creature on your next turn. [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) has many possibilities in this deck, but using [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) is one of the most straightforward and effective.


Modern Pro Tour Qualifier Season is underway, so look for [a PTQ near you](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/pro-tour-qualifiers-events-types-events)!
