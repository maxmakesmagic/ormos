Enchantments Everywhere
=======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 28, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 







For most players at the Pro Tour, a very important goal is reaching the Top 25. Finishing in the Top 25 gives you an invitation to the next Pro Tour and a good shot at reaching the Silver, Gold, or Platinum levels in the Pro Players Club. At [Pro Tour *Journey into Nyx*](/en/events/coverage/chapins-journey-takes-him-pro-tour-title), Franck Dubus finished in 17th place, giving him an invitation to Pro Tour **Magic** 2015 later this year. 


![Doomwake Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Doomwake+Giant)
 Franck's deck of choice takes advantage of one of the new mechanics in *Journey into Nyx*: constellation. Constellation is the type of mechanic that rewards a deck builder for playing more enchantments. Fortunately, there are many enchantments in *Theros* Block! 


 The core of this deck is [Eidolon of Blossoms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Blossoms), which provides fuel for the constellation engine. [Doomwake Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomwake+Giant) is also a great constellation card, swinging combat in your favor and wiping out hordes of Soldier tokens. The rest of the deck is filled out with many enchantments with some subtle synergies. For example, [Nyx Weaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nyx+Weaver) helps fill your graveyard so you can fuel [Pharika, God of Affliction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%2C+God+of+Affliction). Because the tokens are also enchantments, this deck is capable of some truly impressive things once it gets going.
