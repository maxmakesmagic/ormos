This is a control deck with heavy creature removal and a small [Trapmaker's Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trapmaker%27s+Snare) package. A surprising number of difficult problems can be solved via the exile clauses on [Mindbreak Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindbreak+Trap) ([Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), [Banefire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire)) and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) ([Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), [Magma Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Phoenix)). This deck particularly showcases the debilitating [Lavaball Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaball+Trap) as a tutorable instant-speed wrecking ball that leaves [Sphinx of Lost Truths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Lost+Truths), [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), and [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) unscathed. Players who lean on their [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) or [Emeria, the Sky Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria%2C+the+Sky+Ruin) for a late-game trump against slow control decks like this are in for a rude awakening via [Lavaball Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavaball+Trap).
