Round 2 Feature Match: Itaru Ishida vs. Tsuyoshi Fujita
=======================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 30, 2002 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Sideboard Staff











With the announcement of this feature match, exclamations of surprise could be heard throughout the hall: Japan's two best-known Pro players had been matched up. Those in the know were giving the nod to Fujita, assuming his Psychatog deck would be able to push past Ishida's counter-light combo deck.

![Itaru Ishida](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat02/976.jpg)*Itaru Ishida*The first game, Ishida got the pieces of his combo quickly, throwing down a [Deserted Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deserted+Temple), followed by a [Squirrel Nest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) played after Fujita let his guard down to hunt for a [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) with a kicked [Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe). Fujita was able to finally [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) into a [Psychatog,](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog%2C) but the number of squirrels on Ishida's board was rapidly growing. Using another [Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe), Fujita was finally able to find an [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). With 2 Familiars helping, he played [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) on the 7th turn, leaving enough for [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). Ishida attempted to stop it with a [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic), only to scoop after Fujita played one of his own. 

Game 2 saw Fujita get off to a slow start, with [Ancient Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Spring)s and [Cephalid Coliseum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Coliseum) as his only lands. Ishida, meanwhile, was able to play a second-turn [Squirrel Nest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) with the help of a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). After missing two land drops, Fujita conceded when Ishida played a [Deserted Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deserted+Temple).

![Tsuyoshi Fujita](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat02/977.jpg)*Tsuyoshi Fujita*Fujita's luck didn't get much better in the third game, stalling at two Islands and forced to discard two turns in a row. Fujita could only watch helplessly as Ishida played [Mana Short](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short) in his end phase, then go on to play [Squirrel Nest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest). He was finally able to draw more lands, but only after Ishida had a veritable army of squirrels marching across the table. He knocked his deck looking for something that would give him black to play the [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) in his hand, and got...[Salt Marsh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salt+Marsh). Cursing his misfortune, he started picking up his cards. All in all, the match lasted less than 20 minutes. 

**Result: Ishida wins 2-1**
