Arcane Melee in Standard
========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 6, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TrickJarrett.jpg)
By Trick Jarrett




Patrick "Trick" Jarrett oversees web content and social media for Magic, and acts as the publisher of DailyMTG.com and Magic content on the web. He's an ardent Commander player and lover of the game he's played since Ice Age. 






Grand Prix Anaheim was our second chance to see the *Innistrad* block on display, and although miracles won the Pro Tour, the Grand Prix metagame was still quite broad. [On TCGPlayer.com](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=10507), Steve Guillerm explores his deck for the event and then discusses the process for converting from Block to Standard. Here is an excerpt of his article, and the new Standard deck.


> 
> Whenever you're looking to port a deck, it's important to take a look at what the deck's actually trying to do, and what parts are actually necessary. Let's take a look at the deck's game plan:
> 
> 
> Step 1: Don't die. By way of spot removal and sweepers, maintain a positive life total. Note that not every deck actually has this as part of the game plan. Control decks often operate with a motto of, "If I'm not losing, I'm winning." Aggro decks often prevent death by virtue of another motto: "If they're dead, they can't kill me!"
> 
> 
> Step 2: Use draw spells and card selection to find combo pieces. [Forbidden Alchemy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) and [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) do the heavy lifting, digging deep through the deck to find what we need for later.
> 
> 
> Step 2a: Fill the graveyard for later. This is more incidental than part of the plan.
> 
> 
> Step 3: Resolve [Arcane Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Melee). While we can certainly win without [Arcane Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Melee), it is really, really hard. The core of this deck is the interaction between [Mystic Retrieval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Retrieval) and [Runic Repetition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runic+Repetition). Assuming [Runic Repetition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runic+Repetition) hand, [Mystic Retrieval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Retrieval) in the graveyard, and a resolved [Arcane Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Melee), we can execute the following loop:
> 
> 
> R: [Mystic Retrieval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Retrieval) returning (something)  
>  U: [Runic Repetition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runic+Repetition) returning [Mystic Retrieval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Retrieval)  
>  1U: [Mystic Retrieval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Retrieval) returning [Runic Repetition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runic+Repetition)
> 
> 
> That's a Regrowth (well, [Call to Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+Mind)) with buyback for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif). Establishing this game state quickly becomes a lock when you've got a few removal spells in hand. Without [Arcane Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Melee)? The loop costs ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170424152843im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless7.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif), and so you're spending all your mana each turn for a single Regrowth.
> 
> 
>
