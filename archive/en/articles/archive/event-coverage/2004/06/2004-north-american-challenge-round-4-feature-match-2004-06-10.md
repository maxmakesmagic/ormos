2004 North American Challenge Round 4 Feature Match
===================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 10, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_alexshvartsman.jpg)
By Alex Shvartsman











Fabiano had a perfect 3-0 record coming into round 4. Clavette did not have as great a start – he was 2-1, but got paired up against the undefeated Fabiano. It was a rare match at the NAC where neither deck featured [Arcbound Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager)s, but this was one such matchup. Fabiano played a monored Goblins deck, while his opponent brought a [March of the Machines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines) Blue-White build to the table.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/nac04/aFabiano.jpg)*Gerard Fabiano*The matchup is pretty bad for [March of the Machines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines) in the first game, but improves significantly after sideboarding.


### Game 1


Once Fabiano saw [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) and [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s, he was playing a very controlling game for a Goblin deck. He played a [Skirk Prospector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector) and a [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp). On the third turn he played out [Goblin Burrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Burrows) and used it to pump up his Goblin instead of summoning more guys.


In the meantime, it was buying Clavette the time he needed. Clavette played a turn three [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) and cast [March of the Machines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines) on turn four. This shut down the [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp)'s card-drawing power, but allowed it to contribute more directly by attacking for a point of damage each turn. 


On the following turn Clavette summoned a [Darksteel Ingot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Ingot) – an indestructible 3/3 creature at this point – and [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed a [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander). With [Pulse of the Fields](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Fields) as his backup, he was now able to reclaim most of the life points he had lost to this point.. [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) cleared Fabiano's side of the board and a second and then third Ingot entered play. Clavette played extremely conservatively, slowly gaining control of the board rather than trying to win the game a turn or two sooner. He did not want to take any chances. 


Clavette played out a [Future Sight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight) and then another [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), clearing his opponent's board yet again and attacked with his Ingots. Up to over 15 life points and fully in control of the board, Clavette easily won the first game.


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/nac04/aClavette.jpg)*Raymond Clavette*Clavette experienced some mana problems early, failing to play a third land for several turns. Instead he would play out a [Silver Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) on turn two, Talisman on turn three and an Ingot on turn four. Fabiano's draw was far more explosive.


Turn one [Skirk Prospector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector) allowed him to cast a second turn [Sulfuric Vortex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfuric+Vortex). He then summoned a pair of [Goblin Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief)s but his offense was stalled by the lone [Silver Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight). 


Down below ten life points, Clavette triumphantly slammed a [Worship](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship) into play. Fabiano's mono-red deck had no way of dealing with it. [March of the Machines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines) entered play next, giving Clavette yet another creature Fabiano couldn't deal with – his Ingot. Fabiano, who had been taking plenty of damage from his own Vortex, fell a few turns later.


**Final Result:** Clavette 2 – Fabiano 0
