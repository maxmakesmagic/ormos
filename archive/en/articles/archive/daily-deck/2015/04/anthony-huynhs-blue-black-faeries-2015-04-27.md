Anthony Huynh's Blue-Black Faeries
==================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on April 27, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Faeries was a dominating deck during its time in Standard. There was no better play than turn one [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) into turn two [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) back then. While Faeries did see play in the now-retired Extended format, it never really caught on in Modern. Of course, [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) was banned during Modern's first few years of existence, but given that the card has been legal for a year now, I would have expected to see a lot more of it.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bitterblossom)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)




I loved Faeries back in *Lorwyn*-era Standard. I played it in practically every tournament and I even enjoyed playing the mirror match. I always tried finding the best sideboard tech in order to gain an edge against my opponent's [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s.



Seeing this archetype in the Top 8 of the [StarCityGames Premier IQ in Providence](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/deckshow.php?event_ID=45&t%5BT3%5D=28&start_date=2015-04-18&end_date=2015-04-19&order_1=finish&limit=8&action=Show+Decks&city=Providence) last weekend definitely put a smile on my face. Anthony Huynh's list is your typical Faeries deck and all of the main components are there. Three [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)s and three Inquisition of Kozileks are your most important cards. They clear the way for your [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) to come down on turn two. They can stop early aggression by stripping your opponent's hand of burn spells or early creatures.



Some would argue that [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) is a scary card to play. Most life loss from this deck is caused by your own sources. The self-inflicted damage from [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta), [Watery Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave), and [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) really adds up. Anthony does a nice job of counteracting that with [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull), [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), and [Sword of Light and Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow) out of the sideboard. Regardless, playing [Bitterblossom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) against a Mono-Red Burn opponent is pretty scary.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Batterskull)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wurmcoil+Engine)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow)




[Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) is a great choice for Modern right now. The most played cards in the format are arguably [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt); and the Sprite counters them without requiring another Faerie. It wields a Sword pretty well too.



It's always refreshing to see something different in Modern, so props to Anthony for his fantastic deck choice.
