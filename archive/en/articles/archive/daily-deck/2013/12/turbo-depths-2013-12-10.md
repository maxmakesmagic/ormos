Turbo-Depths
============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on December 12, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







This is a banner week for [Into the North](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+North), as this is the second decklist to feature it, and this time it's even an integral part of the deck. This deck goes pretty deep into the depths, with the only plan being to turbo out an Avatar of Marit Lage and fly over for 20 (or go straight to the face with [Fling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling)). The card that made this a more legitimate strategy is [Thespian's Stage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage), which, when combined with a [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) and the new legend rule, gets you a [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) token for the low price of two mana. Play Stage, copy Depths, keep the Stage, and sacrifice it immediately because it doesn't have any ice counters. What a performance!


It may seem a little odd that the deck only has three [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths), but once you look at all the searching cards, it makes sense. [Crop Rotation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crop+Rotation) is by far the fastest, although most costly, with [Into the North](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+North), [Sylvan Scrying](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Scrying), and [Expedition Map](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) coming in at two and three mana, respectively. Because of all the land searching (another common theme this week), this deck even gets to play a couple sweet tutor targets. [Sejiri Steppe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sejiri+Steppe) combines with [Crop Rotation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crop+Rotation) for a build-your-own [Blessed Breath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Breath), and the sideboard [Bojuka Bog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Bog) and [Karakas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) are both powerful hate cards.


[Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) is public enemy number one, which is why the deck has four [Pithing Needle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle)s main deck. Playing a Needle on [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) isn't an uncommon turn-one play, even in the dark, just because of how commonly played the card is and how devastating it is to the combo. [Crucible of Worlds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crucible+of+Worlds) offers another way to get around [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), and lets the deck reassemble the combo if the Avatar is dealt with somehow.


Because [Swords to Plowshares](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) exists, there are matchups where attacking with an Avatar token isn't going to be enough. For that, the deck has [Fling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling), which also conveniently can speed up the clock by a turn sometimes. Between [Fling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling), the full eight Spirit Guides, [Lotus Petal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal), and [Ancient Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb), it isn't unreasonable for this deck to kill as early as turn three (or two with an absolutely absurd draw).
