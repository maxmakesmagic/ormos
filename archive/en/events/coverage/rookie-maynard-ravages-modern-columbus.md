Rookie Maynard Ravages Modern in Columbus
=========================================




July 21 - 22, 2012












Grand Prix Columbus 2012
------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/winner_shot.jpg)Jacob Maynard is the Grand Prix Columbus champion!


In a field of [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s, [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn)s, and [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)s, it was Jacob Maynard and the machines that were left standing here in Columbus. Armed with the latest version of Affinity, a deck capable of exploding onto the table with an army of artifacts as early as turn one, Maynard blasted through his competition in Day One to earn his first Day Two appearance at a Grand Prix.


And what a Day Two appearance it was! Maynard's [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion)s, [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast)s, and other hard-hitting artifacts earned him a spot in the Top 8, where he took down opponent after opponent with a series of explosive plays. It all built up to the last game against finalist Lucas Siow, when a powered up [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) crashed through a nasty [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) to deal the last points of damage, securing the rookie the trophy.


The success of Jacob Maynard and Affinity shows that the Modern format is still wide open, and that no matter how good one deck is, there will always be something else out there that can take down the tournament.


Congratulations to Jacob Maynard and all of the GP Columbus Top 8 players!

Quarterfinal - Aaron Estrin vs. Shahar Shenhar
----------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*
Grand Prix San Diego winner Shahar Shenhar is facing down Aaron Estrin, one of the undefeated players from Day 1, and one of the pilots in the Top 8 of the powerful [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) combo deck. Would Shenhar's W/U/R [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck conquer Estrin with the help of his sideboard conversion, or would Estrin's [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) continue to get freaky?"


### Game 1


Shenhar led off with [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), while Estrin began with [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). The [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) transformed as Shenhar revealed [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), allowing him to put his opponent on a dangerous and early clock. [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) hit [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots), and the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) continued to crack in. [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) gave Shenhar some serious muscle, and Estrin would need to set up his combo fast if he wanted to survive.



![Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lightning+Helix)

[Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) followed by [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) would be a start, but Shenhar was lying in wait with two [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)es on top of his deck (which he left there via [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions)). [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) disposed of [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and suddenly Estrin was in threat of dying in a matter of one more turn as Shenhar's team was sent in.


A second [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) spelled extra trouble, as Estrin sat at 9 life, and it was going to transform on the next turn.


Estrin tried to mount a defense with two [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s, but the revealed [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and the [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) waiting in his hand posed problems. In fact, Estrin would have been dead at that point, if Shenhar had the mana in play to cast both.


Estrin passed on his next turn, and [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) for [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) forced Estrin's hand. [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) for 5 called on [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). The legendary Goblin got to work in making a billion [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s, and the [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) resolved.

In a battle between the machines of the past and the new blue kids on the block, who would win?


### Game 1


Siow led off with [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions). Maynard had a couple more plays than that on his first turn.


[Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel), [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum), [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum)[Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge).


I think the word for that kind of start is "fast".


Siow dug some more with [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions), and then passed back to Maynard. [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) shut down [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating), and the [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) cracked in for 1. Siow tapped out for [Vedalken Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) on turn three, looking to secure control of the board quickly.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/maynard_finals.jpg)

*Maynard's off to a quick lead in the finals* 
Maynard played another [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) after attacking for 1. When Siow attempted to take one of the flying lifelink artifact creatures, Maynard responded with [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast). After an attack, Siow sat at 8 life. Another [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) in response to the [Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shackles) and its second activation left Siow at 3, and [Steelshaper's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steelshaper%27s+Gift) got him Cranium Plating.


Siow had no further lands and passed back. Maynard drew into [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus), and played it before casting [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating). [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) bought Siow a turn, but the [Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shackles) wasn't going to get him away from a lethal [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) attack on the next turn. When [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) tapped his [Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shackles) target on Maynard's next turn, Siow reached for his sideboard.


Maynard 1 – Siow 0


### Game 2


Siow kept his seven, while Maynard was quick to ship his mana-starved hand back for six cards. Those six cards were about as impressive as the previous hand, and Maynard went down to five. Those five warranted a "Good enough," from Maynard, who retaliated to Siow's turn one [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) with [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest), [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal), [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel), and [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge). Not exactly in that order.


Siow flipped his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) off of [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) on the second turn. The [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) went in for 3, and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) shut down the [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) when Maynard went to combat. A second Signet Pest posed problems for Siow, who went on the offensive with another [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). The transformed ally went into the red zone, bringing Maynard to 12.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/siow_finals.jpg)

*Siow evens up the score* 
Siow debated using [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) on [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) when it woke up on Maynard's turn for combat, but he instead chose not to, taking 3 poison and 4 damage. The Bolt instead went at Maynard's face, and when the second [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) transformed, another Bolt finished things off.


Maynard 1 – Siow 1


### Game 3


Maynard led with [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) off of [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel). [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) ate [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce), but a second [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) and [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) gave Maynard the mana for a second-turn [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion). It and an animated [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) went in for 3, and a post-combat [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) into [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) (thanks to [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum)) added more power to his army of mechanical creatures.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/siow_finalist.jpg)

*Siow shows off his finalist plaque* 
Siow drew and played an untapped [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground), necessary for the [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) that he played in response to a [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) activation. The Fallout ceared away Maynard's creatures...well, except for the well protected and now 3/3 [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion). [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) in response to the Fallout and the attacks left Siow at 6 life, and the fourth-turn [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) wasn't going to be an acceptable answer to protection from all colors, as the [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) was ready and waiting.


Another attack put Siow to 3. When [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) didn't find an answer to the [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion), Siow offered the handshake to Maynard.


Jacob Maynard is the 2012 Grand Prix Columbus champion!



Top 5 Cards

*by Marc Calderaro*
 


![Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Steppe+Lynx)


 

### 5. [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx)


This unassuming 0/1 is a pillar of the format. [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) represents just how aggressive Modern can be. With the right draws, often seen out of the WUR [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck, [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) can be a 4/5 attacker every turn until an opponent's death. Combined with fetch lands, the [Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx) has also proven to be a significant force on defense as well. The three toughness is crucially relevant in a format full of two-powered hate bears, Merfolk, Geists of Saint Trafts and even opposing [Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx)es. Just the threat of searching out a land at instant speed has been thwarting attacks all weekend. If you are planning to play a deck, make sure you have a good plan to stop a turn-one [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx). Otherwise, you will just lose. Hard.


 
 


![Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Karn+Liberated)


 

### 4. [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated)


A pillar at apposition with [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), not just for its mana cost, but also for its role in the field, is [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated). Alongside [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn), this Planeswalker is the kill condition of choice in every Tron variant around. And this weekend was a big emergence of the Tron decks offline. [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) is the centerpiece of that deck; it makes the deck tick; and it's the card everyone fears. Perfectly cast at the price of UrzaTron, when you tell your friend about the bad beats you sustained from a Tron deck, the first question is always, "Did he get the turn-three [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated)?" If you don't remove that beast immediately, you have just about no hope, because by the time you've expended your resources getting rid of it, Tron's ready to cast a [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), a giant Eldrazi, or hell, just another copy of [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) of which they obviously play four.


Though only Caleb Estrada made it to the Top 8 piloting the list, [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated) showed up to droves and caused all the dealers to sell out of [Molten Rain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Rain), [Sowing Salt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt) and [Fulminator Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage)s. With that in mind, getting even four copies to the Top 8 shows the sheer power of the deck and the card. Beware the turn-three [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Karn%2BLiberated)!


 
 


![Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lightning+Helix)


 

### 3. [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)


What could possibly encapsulate all the different variants of Aggro-Control and Tempo decks that abounded this weekend? They are trying to win just the moment before their opponents do. I know that's a silly thing to say, but in those kind of decks, you want to win as safely as you can. That means not taking any unnecessary risks and not putting yourself in the position of being blown out, while also working towards your overall goal of winning. You know what does that perfectly? [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix). Though there are many cards you want to keep your [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) for, I can't think of one that would be on the mind more while you're trying to race. A well-timed burnitating of an [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) while putting your life total back into the safe zone – and all for two mana – has been winning games all weekend. The RUW [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck is certainly that uses this card to great effect. Generally, [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) flashing back a [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) really seals a game up quickly.


Both Chris Piland's and Max Tietze's deck played a full complement of the Ravnica instant, and my guess if you allowed them to play twelve, they would have.


 
 


![Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Restoration+Angel)


 

### 2. [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)


Yes, yes, this card was already the number one card from Grand Prix Yokohama. Why is it in the Top 5 cards again here at Grand Prix Columbus? Because it's that good. This card does all sorts of silly things and what you have to assume when your opponent has out four mana, only one of which is require to produce white. Or if there's a three-drop and an active [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). Or a two-drop and an active Pod if they are playing [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch).


Mid-combat this thing is bonkers. It can save a blocker, from either removal or from combat damage; and it creates a new blocker with both the Angel herself and/or untapping a big dude that had already swung in.


Let's not even get into what happens when you play an entire deck of enter-the-battlefield abilities like Pod. Nevermind, lets. [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap), [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer), [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots), [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage), [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter), [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts), [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique), and [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) are just cards that I thought of off the top of my head right now. We will be seeing [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) making a mark in Modern for a long time to come.


The last interaction I'd like to talk about is [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) and [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) and [Vedalken Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles). Like [Ghostly Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker) and [Cloudshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudshift), the Angel states that you return the card to the battlefield under your control. So if you've temporarily taken a creature with [Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shackles) or Conscripts, blink it with the Angel and it'll be your for keeps. This card's good.

### 1. [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion)


[Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) is what secured Jason Maynard the Grand Prix win. The little 2/2 picks up a [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) and sails past defenders with ease. Even without the artifact lands to power up the Affinity deck, things like [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) and [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) all but guarantee the [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) will have protection from all colors whenever you need it to.


In the last game of the finals, this little guy got a +1/+1 counter from [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) to survive [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) and keep pressure on Lucas Siow when it was most needed. And the card was helping Maynard before the finals. He used it to win his semi-final match against Aaron Estrin's Naya Pod deck. He had two attacking on the last turn and dealt the final points in an unstoppable fashion.


Though when [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion)'s paired with the busted equipment, it adds to the explosive nature of Affinity, even when it's flying solo it adds inevitability. Though the grey ogre is not much on its own, with Affinity he rarely is without its best buddies.


Affinity is the top-tier deck that for years everyone is convinced its not top tier. It put Mary Jacobson in the Top 8 in Lincoln; it putHiroya Miyamoto in the Top 8 in Yokohama; it gave Jason Maynard the win in Columbus.
