What to Expect at Pro Tour Amonkhet
===================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 11, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 






Tune in to Pro Tour *Amonkhet* on [twitch.tv/magic](http://www.twitch.tv/magic) this Friday (May 12) starting at 9 a.m. local time (CT)/7 a.m. PT/2 p.m. UTC to see how the new set shakes up the format!


[Pro Tour *Amonkhet*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptakh) features not only a format with the addition of a new set, but one that got shaken up by the removal of one of the pillars of the format. With [Saheeli Rai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saheeli+Rai) losing her partner in crime, [Felidar Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Guardian), the field has opened up significantly. As such, I expect to see representatives from each major archetype, which we will take a look at today.


Aggro
=====


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar)
Don't worry, Gideon isn't going anywhere, and Vehicles aren't either. Mardu Vehicles is still the premier aggro deck of the format and in fact is just the best deck in the format too. Most decks will be built with Mardu Vehicles in mind, and this will be one of if not the most-played deck in the field.

**Game Plan**: As before, Mardu Vehicles attacks early and often, getting started with [Toolcraft Exemplar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toolcraft+Exemplar) on turn one and curving out from there. It is resilient thanks to cards like Gideon and [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran) and has removal to clear out potential blockers.


It's worth noting that Mardu Vehicles is down to just [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran) as a Vehicle, but the deck still plays out mostly the same. This particular list has a cool transformational sideboard that many players have adopted, where [Oath of Liliana](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Liliana) and additional planeswalkers turn the deck into a Mardu Planeswalkers midrange deck.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lord+of+the+Accursed)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Accursed)
The Zombie deck has risen from the dead and is viable once more. Not since the days of [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) can I remember such a thing, but thanks to *Amonkhet*, they are back. There are multiple versions (mono-black and white-black being the two I've seen have the most success), and it really comes down to how much you want access to [Wayward Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wayward+Servant) versus how bad of a mana base you are willing to put up with.

**Game Plan**: Attack the opponent with a horde of Zombies, taking advantage of multiple tribal payoffs. [Lord of the Accursed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Accursed), [Diregraf Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Colossus), [Cryptbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptbreaker), [Metallic Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallic+Mimic), [Relentless Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Dead), [Liliana's Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Mastery), and [Dark Salvation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Salvation) all give you significant bonuses for having a critical mass of Zombies, and added together that's almost every spell in the deck. [Dark Salvation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Salvation) and [Cryptbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptbreaker) were just waiting for assistance, and now that there are enough pieces to accompany them, the deck has become a real threat.


I like how resilient this deck isâ€”[Relentless Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Dead) and [Dread Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Wanderer) are hard to kill, and [Dark Salvation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Salvation) plus [Liliana's Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Mastery) make many tokens at once. That gives the deck a lot of gas, and normal removal spells often won't do enough to keep the horde at bay. Decks like this are why I like playing four [Magma Spray](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray)s in any red control deck, as we will see later.

**Game Plan**: Control the board with removal spells and solid creatures like [Grim Flayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer). Acquire delirium thanks to Flayer and [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency), and use [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) to search up the best answer for any situation. As with any midrange deck, the cards this deck plays tend to be good on offense or defense, and the deck is capable of playing either side fairly well.


It can lock up the game with Ishkanah and planeswalkers, tutoring up [Noxious Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Gearhulk) as removal, or it can curve [Grim Flayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer) into [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) and cast multiple removal spells. This deck is a combination of good cards that work together, and is a solid choice as a result.

**Game Plan**: Counter or kill everything the opponent plays, getting card advantage via [Glimmer of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmer+of+Genius) and [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk). Eventually finish the game by attacking with Gearhulks or Fumaroles.


While the game plan of this deck is simple, actually playing it is not. You have a ton of removal, but you need to use it judiciously to keep the board clear. Using [Censor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Censor) when possible is an easier decision, but knowing what to use [Disallow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disallow), Commit, or [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning) on is a little trickier. As always, playing control decks requires a lot of format knowledge, so keep that in mind when deciding what to build.


Figuring out the metagame is also rewarding for control decks, as this deck could play slightly different removal and counterspell configurations depending on what you expect. This is what I've liked right now, but I could see changing numbers on [Sweltering Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sweltering+Suns), [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), and [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), if the texture of the threats change.

**Game Plan**: This is the same as blue-red, with the addition of [Gideon of the Trials](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+of+the+Trials) giving the deck some more aggressive openers.


I will say that I like this version less than blue-red, since [Magma Spray](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) really is that good, but I include it for completeness' sake. If you expect more midrange, the removal in this deck is better, and [Cast Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cast+Out) is a powerful enough card that it can't be that bad to have it in your deck. I'd still go with blue-red, but I do expect to see some blue-white.

**Game Plan**: Here's how the combo works:


* Cast [New Perspectives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=New+Perspectives), and get up to seven cards in hand.
* Cycle all your cards for free. Each [Vizier of Tumbling Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Tumbling+Sands) adds a manaâ€”two if you have a land with [Weirding Wood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weirding+Wood). Each [Shefet Monitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shefet+Monitor) does as well.
* Cast [Shadow of the Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+of+the+Grave) to draw a ton of cards and gain more mana.
* Cast [Approach of the Second Sun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Approach+of+the+Second+Sun), then cycle and cast another one.
* [Sphinx of the Final Word](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Final+Word) makes Approach uncounterable, and Traverse finds Sphinx or more cyclers.

This deck is really neat. It can be vulnerable to enchantment removal and counterspells and straight-up doesn't work without [New Perspectives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=New+Perspectives), but it can buy a lot of time with the cyclers like [Haze of Pollen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haze+of+Pollen) and [Cast Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cast+Out). It also dodges most interaction, as creature removal does nothing against it.


This deck is a little newer, but it looks legit. I wouldn't be surprised if it showed up at the Pro Tour, though it's not quite as proven as the rest on the list. There's always an appealing thing about attacking from a new angle, and this certainly does that.
