What Now? What Next?
====================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 23, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











The [Banned amp; Restricted List announcement](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/feature/148a) earlier this week has put the nail in the coffin of Caw-Blade.

This upcoming weekend will be something different; maybe something special. For many players—the players who have been plugging at it the past several tournaments—it will represent a last chance to tap four mana for [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) in Standard. Some players—especially those who have never summoned a [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) or bounced a mana-producing creature with the most vilified of planeswalkers—may see a first chance and a last chance at once.

So what now?

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td148_jaceStone.jpg)  
Now that the DCI has made it official, if you want to win this last weekend, my recommendation is to have [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) in your deck. And though the latest results have the first-place winners for the Philadelphia PTQ season (which includes a fair amount of pre-*New Phyrexia*) looking like this...

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td148_chart.jpg)  
... I would actually not recommend straight White-Blue Caw-Blade, but Twin Blade.

This is the deck that Ben Wienburg used to win the 6/11 Louisville, Kentucky PTQ.

*Given that map, why Twin Blade over Caw-Blade?*

I've said it before... The [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) combination is the most powerful thing you can do in the present Standard format. Combining that with the best turn-two play you can make ([Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) on the play) allows Twin Blade to operate at a completely different level than other decks in the metagame.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

In my experience, you actually win about 75% of games with the [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) / [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combination, but of those, most of those wins come after the opponent is forced to spend cards and mana answering the momentum generated by your [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)-into-[Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) Plan A.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Batterskull)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull)  
[Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)-into-[Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) is, in case you haven't been paying attention since the appearance of *New Phyrexia*, a pretty good Plan A. Many beatdown decks have elected to run main-deck [Manic Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal) or even [Crush](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush) (which can't be shifted with a [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite)) to deal.

Well, with the opponent worried about basically a free [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) attacking on turn four, spending cards and mana to not lose to that, you have a great big opening to set up and win with the Exarch combination ([Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) + [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) / infinite attackers, et cetera).

My guess is that if the format had been allowed to progress without the June 20 bannings, Twin Blade (*also* a [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) / [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) list, to be fair) would have supplanted straight Caw-Blade as the premiere deck in the format by combining most of force of the two best strategies in Standard. Check out Ben's sideboard... He could *fall back* on [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) (Exarch Twin's Plan B-slash-RUG's Plan A) as yet another way to win! Only a deck that can produce ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) has that kind of flexibility and power.

We'll come back to the theme of doing more than one thing well in a bit.

Looking at the above chart, through June 4 Caw-Blade had won more PTQs for Philadelphia *than every other strategy combined.* How the heck did we get to this place?

[Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), like [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), had been available for play since *Worldwake*. But it wasn't until [Pro Tour Paris](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptpar11/welcome) and the rise of Caw-Blade that Mystic became a "problem." No one is saying [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) isn't a super-duper card—because the fact that it can Top 8 [a Legacy Grand Prix](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gppro11/welcome) is quite telling—but what became the best two-drop in Standard lagged a good deal behind [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) (and other stuff) until [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine).

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine)  
The *Mirrodin Besieged* Sword did two things that ultimately changed the face of Standard:


> 1. [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) invalidated big green decks. The protection from green is pretty obvious, right? Going into Pro Tour Paris, [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) was in large part considered the Deck to Beat and in fact was the most popular deck on both Day One and Day Two. The ability to get in early with the Sword, which leeched the opponent's limited resources while running by potential blockers and dealing a serious packet of damage, essentially extincted the once-dominating [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) decks. So what next?
> 2. [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) gave Caw-Blade—and essentially only Caw-Blade and its variants—access to the most important quantity in Magic: mana. A great many big turns started with the Caw-Blade deck putting all of its mana into an attack, getting in, untapping, and then making another big play like [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) or [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor); alternately, Caw-Blade could attack with a big threat like [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) (typically tapping you out) and could leave back counterspell mana. You can see as Caw-Blade evolved to fight itself how players like Edgar Flores read the importance of these turns and fell away from playing expensive spells like [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) in favor of super-fast defensive cards like [Condemn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn), [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil), and later [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember). So what next?
> 

I actually think that it is possible that Ramp;D could have just banned [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) in lieu of [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) and Caw-Blade would have still been a fine deck... but it wouldn't have been the Cadillac of Cadillacs that it has been these past months. The problem, of course, is that [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) came with *New Phyrexia* and suddenly [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) had an *even better* Equipment to get!

But however you cut it, the future of [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) is going to be... different.

Likely we will see fewer copies of [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) in tournaments, which will be good for Titan **Magic**. Before Pro Tour Paris, there used to be a joke that you can play whatever Titan you want in Standard...


> * [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) had Mono-Green Eldrazi, [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut), and [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave).
> * [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) had RUG.
> * [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) had Blue-Black Control.
> * [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) had various Control roles—Blue-Black, White-Blue, [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave)—and is still seeing play even in the Caw-Blade era.
> * [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) had Mono-White Control (where it was a superstar), White-Blue Control, and a nigh-unbeatable role as a singlegton in Caw-Blade.
> 

All the Titans continued to see play during the Caw-Blade era, but not in their previous numbers. I think the most immediate effect of the [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) banning—and the resulting reduction in [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) play—will be a jump in Titans popularity.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td148_titans.jpg)  
An interesting possibility is that we see *different* Equipment play. [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) didn't *make* Equipment. This isn't even the height of popularity that Equipment has seen in competitive formats! There was a time when [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) was played as a four-of in greater than 50% of decks, and that dances between [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) and [Manriki-Gusari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manriki-Gusari) or even [Celestial Kirin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Kirin) were common.

In Standard, [Troll Ascetic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic) was a premier Equipment-carrier. It lugged around not just a [Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jitte) but [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice), [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer)... all kinds of stuff.

Remember when people thought [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) was going to be a "problem" in Standard? While I think that it is possible that people will still summon a [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk), [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) is no longer going to be used primarily to attack or defend [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor). [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) may see its *first* heavy Standard play.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td148_thrun.jpg)  
Let's talk a moment about the effect of banning [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)...

Had the DCI chosen to ban *just*[Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), that would have cemented Caw-Blade as the best strategy until the next rotation. The reasons would have been two big things: 1) Exarch Twin leans heavily on [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) to defend against [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), and 2) Caw-Blade uses Jace but doesn't *need* Jace to execute on its Plan A.

Consequently, just as these bannings have taken the legs out from underneath Caw-Blade, I think we have seen the end of Exarch Twin as we know it in Standard.

Previously there was a cadre of players who thought [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) was good against Exarch Twin. Those players were not correct. [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) is many things, but a determiner of games against a prepared Twin player was not one of them. The combination of [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) allowed Twin to set up its turns to win, regardless of the opponent's little 0/4.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spellskite)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite)  
[Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) was therefore not just "good," but central to the Exarch Twin strategy. Twin used Jace to draw the cards it needed to assemble the combo, deny the opponent the tools to resist its best-in-breed Plan A, and ultimately to remove the great colorless hope that was supposed to shut off the combo.

Now? None of them.

I don't think that we've seen the end of the [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin)*combo*... How can we? It's the best thing you can do in Standard, and a heck of plan when the average opponent plans to attack with a six-drop. However, with its adamantium Swiss Army knife gone, Twin is going to have to change and evolve.

[Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) is going to be better than ever.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td148_spellskite.jpg)  
All of a sudden [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) is going to be effective against Twin! In addition, it is going to put up wins against all kinds of kids trying to take the place of Caw-Blade in the metagame. [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) is fantastic against [Teetering Peaks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks), laughs off [Staggershock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staggershock), and *blocks*. And especially when we see decks with [Mutagenic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) capable of taking PTQ wins in the era of Caw-Blade, the question of *what's next* has big implications for the little 0/4.

But how about today? What *now*?

Ironic that Bjorn won a PTQ in *Philadelphia* for Pro Tour Philly?

Domi's deck does multiple things well. It has [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)*and*[Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman). Unlike builds like Naya, it can [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)*into*[Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman). The rest of the metagame is losing a premier two-drop, but Domi's hybrid plays both of the next two best twos.

He can pour [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s into the graveyard and use them to set up the best thing you *can* do in Standard: find [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)! In addition, the [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) engine gives an inexpensive route to running [Manic Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal) as a main-deck solution to [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), and future versions can probably run a bunch of [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite)s to defend against other people's Exarchs.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Domi already ran a sideboarded [Trinket Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage); this subtheme can be expanded to a [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) for a future [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage) or [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) plan a la Naya. Domi already ran one [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod); this can potentially be expanded to more copies to replace the loss of [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) (which was only a three-of to begin with).

In a time of uncertainty and impending exploration, Blue-Red-Green with [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and Exarch Twin seems to have the tools to take over.

But you know what's cool?

That's the cool thing *about* uncertainty!

We *don't* know what is going to be the best yet. Titans will rise again. There is still a powerful combo to exploit. Brewers will see wide open fields of wheat and hops and card advantage. Mana ramp will return, and like Matt Saracen in the first season of *Friday Night Lights*, the second-best Jace is going to have a chance to prove just how good a blue hood can be with "just" three abilities.

It's a strange world; about to get stranger. Let's keep it that way.
