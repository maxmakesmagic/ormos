Round 5: Angels and Witches
===========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 28, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_DaveGuskin.jpg)
By Dave Guskin











### Gerry Thompson (Vampires) vs. Daniel Gräfensteiner (Red-White Landfall)


American Gerry Thompson, 7-time GP Top 8 competitor (winner of GP–Denver) and hero of the people, faced down German Daniel Gräfensteiner, a new addition to the Pro Tour scene, in the arena. Gerry's Vampire deck was an interesting choice, well-positioned to take down the slower decks in the format with his heavy discard component. Daniel's deck was a hyperaggressive red-white landfall deck – bound to have some reasonable late game as well thanks to his [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel)s.


### Game 1


Gräfensteiner kicked it off with an [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds), and grabbed a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) to Thompson's [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp). A second turn [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), never a happy occasion for its controller, came down for Gräfensteiner, followed by another [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds). Thompson was unconcerned, making a [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) of his own off of a [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs).


Gräfensteiner tanked for a while and then laid a second [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), attacked, and sacrificed the second Wilds (fetching [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)) for 4 total damage, bringing it to 20-15 his lead. He finished the turn with a [Kor Skyfisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Skyfisher), reloading his first [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) for another go.


Gerry laid down a second copy of the Hexmage, presenting quite the wall of first striking defense. Four points of power seemed like it wouldn't be enough to stop Daniel's fetch-land-powered [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) as the German smashed in with both creatures, his Skyfisher now suited up with a [Trusty Machete](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusty+Machete) ... but the Lynx became horribly [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure)d and Gerry only took the 4 in the air, making it 20-11.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptsj10/r5_graefensteiner.jpg)*Germany's Daniel Gräfensteiner in action.*  
Thompson smashed in with both Hexmages and then cast [Consuming Vapors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vapors), jumping the life totals to Gräfensteiner 15, Thompson 13. Gräfensteiner had a little surprise with a little poke from a hasty [Tuktuk the Explorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tuktuk+the+Explorer) next turn. Thompson declined to rebound the Vapors and give Gräfensteiner a free Tuktuk the Returned token, instead just attacking with the Hexmages and laying a third. The life totals stood at 11-12.


Gräfensteiner suited up Tuktuk with [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar), ready to block, but Thompson didn't mind as he poured it on with [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and [Malakir Bloodwitch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malakir+Bloodwitch). The huge 5-point drain left Gräfensteiner at 1 with few outs, and after a peek at the next card, he scooped.


Gerry Thompson 1, Daniel Gräfensteiner 0


### Game 2


The first nonland permanent on the board was a turn two [Plated Geopede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) for Gräfensteiner, answered quickly by a [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) by Thompson. [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) was next to the slaughter, murdered by Gräfensteiner's lightning-fast [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage). Not to be outdone, Thompson kicked a Malakir Gatekeeper to kill the Sparkmage, then kicked another to edict the Gräfensteiner's follow-up [Kor Skyfisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Skyfisher). When the dust settled, Gräfensteiner was forced to pass and start taking beats from the Gatekeeper twins.


Daniel was down to 14 before he found [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) to keep the Vampires at bay, but the freshly cast [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) from Gerry begged to differ. Daniel had an answer for that too, though, in [Flame Slash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Slash).


Thompson had no fear. His bloodsuckers were on the prowl. He swung in again, and Gräfensteiner declined to block with his air force of [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) and two birds. bringing the life totals to 16-9 his favor when—all of Thompson's loss of life was fetch land inflicted to this point. The two birds swung in for "first blood," taking Thompson to 14, and Gräfensteiner reinforced his position slightly with a [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx).


Gerry shook his head with a slight smile and plopped down Gatekeepers #3 and #4, leaving the board 4 Gatekeeper to 1 [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) and 1 Bird. Despite no further land to power the Angel, Daniel did have a second copy of the powerful landfall-bird-maker. Gerry watched the Angels and bird fly over and when the (kicked Burst) Lightnings followed, he was dead.


Gerry Thompson 1, Daniel Gräfensteiner 1


### Game 3


Gerry started off with two mulligans. He seemed unperturbed, however, and laid down a turn-two [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) off a fetch. Daniel matched him with a [Plated Geopede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede), but took the damage on the chin, making it 19-18 in Gerry's favor.


The crack back was brutal, however: [Teetering Peaks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks) meant 5 damage from Gräfensteiner's 'Pede. Thompson had little follow-up, stuck on four lands and hurting from the mulligans. Gräfensteiner continued to pound on his American opponent with an [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel), then a second, with plenty of fetches to generate bird advantage. Even Thompson's [Consuming Vapors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vapors) couldn't stop the aerial beats, with only the Geopede and a Bird token taken out.


When Thompson conceded a few turns later, Gräfensteiner showed him "all these" [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel)s, adding a third from his hand to the battlefield. Thompson, not to be outdone, showed a fist full of [Malakir Bloodwitch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malakir+Bloodwitch)es that would have quickly turned the tide ... if only he had a fifth land drop.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptsj10/r5_thompson.jpg)*Gerry Thompson gives us the puppy dog eyes.*  
Daniel Gräfensteiner 2, Gerry Thompson 1
