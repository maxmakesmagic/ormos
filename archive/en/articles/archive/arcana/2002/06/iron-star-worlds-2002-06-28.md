Iron Star at Worlds
===================



 Posted in **Arcana**
 on June 28, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











The five life-gaining "Lucky Charms" are oft-maligned, but every once in a while, they show up in tournament decks. Two years ago, one of the Top 8 decks from a Regionals in Australia featured [Throne of Bone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Throne+of+Bone), but the pinnacle of the Lucky Charm's existance may have come at Worlds in 1995, where the format was *Fourth Edition/Fallen Empires/Ice Age*.

Italy's Andrea Redi was playing a deck that was sort of precursor to Sligh -- mono-red burn with a bunch of efficient creatures. His answer to the mirror match was to side in two copies of [Iron Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Star), turning each spell cast into a chance for a little extra life.
