Zvi Mowshowitz’s Chromantiflayer
================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on April 22, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






I played a lot of [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer)-[Chromanticore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromanticore) in *Fate Reforged* Standard and thought the deck was a blast to play, but I didn't think it had what it takes to compete in a Pro Tour. The deck has quite a few inconsistencies. Sometimes you draw too many [Chromanticore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromanticore)s with no way to get them into your graveyard. Sometimes you draw too many [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer)s with nothing to delve away. Then there are times where you don't draw either, and just have a pile of creatures that don't really synergize with each other.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Soulflayer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chromanticore)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromanticore)


While I love the deck, I didn't expect to see it at Pro Tour *Dragons of Tarkir* last weekend. To my surprise, quite a few accomplished pro players chose to play the deck last weekend, including Hall of Famer Zvi Mowshowitz.



The basic idea of the deck is to use your self-mill spells like [Satyr Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Satyr+Wayfinder) and [Commune with the Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+the+Gods) to get a variety of creatures in your graveyard, including [Chromanticore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromanticore). Then you cast your [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer) for only two black mana, delving away your creatures with powerful abilities, thus making a [Soulflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulflayer) with pretty much any keyword ability you can think of. Having played the deck, I can tell you that once you have a creature with flying, first strike, lifelink, trample, indestructible, and hexproof on the battlefield, it's pretty hard to lose.



This deck plays the [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor)-[Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) card advantage engine, which makes it much less vulnerable to control strategies. Before *Dragons of Tarkir* was released, controls decks were its biggest weakness. I'm happy that *Dragons of Tarkir* was able to give this deck a little boost and I'm looking forward to giving this new version a try.
