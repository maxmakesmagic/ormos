Sunday, 11:38 a.m. - Metagame Analysis and Transformational Sideboards
----------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

As the Day 2 metagame overview showed, the current Standard format is very diverse â€“ the list of raw data contained as many as 33 separate archetypes! While this is exciting, the fact that there are so many viable decks to choose from can be quite daunting. With this in mind, I set out to break the metagame down into more manageable pieces.



![Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Champion+of+the+Parish)

If we lump several archetypes together (e.g., consider UB Zombies, Mono-Black Zombies, UBR Zombies, and RB Zombies together as a grand "Zombie" archetype), we get the following picture: In the lead are white Human decks (including both versions that splash blue for [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt), as well as versions with a token theme that are splashing black): roughly 19% of the Day 2 participants here in Lille are running [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish).


Next up, we have a tie between Delver decks (both Blue-White versions and the [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) variants), slow control decks (Blue-Black Control, Esper Control, etc.), and [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) Aggro (spearheaded by Red-Green Aggro, but also including Mono-Green and Green-White versions): each of these three grand archetypes account for 16% of the Day 2 metagame.


Further down the list, we see that roughly 13% of the Day 2 players are piloting Ramp decks (Green-Red, Green-Black, etc.), 11% are playing Zombies, and 6% are playing Frites. That rounds up the top archetypes here in Lille.


The remaining archetypes are all fringe, including the Mono-Red deck that I advocated as a good choice for the current Standard metagame. Hardly anyone played it on Day 1, and only one brave player â€“ Asboern Anthonsen â€“ made it to Day 2 with Mono-Red. He's currently not in a likely position to make Top 8; thus, my prediction that at least one Mono-Red deck would make it to the Top 8 is unlikely to come true. Instead, the metagame analysis suggests that we'll probably see a bunch of Human decks in the Top 8, although it's still too early to tell definitively.



![Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll)

As I went through the decklists, the sideboard of various Frites decks stood out to me. Frites is a popular choice among the French players here today, and some of them included a surprise in their sideboards: the possibility to "transform". The idea is that you take out various reanimator cards and bring in [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll), [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) and/or [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace). This allows you to approach game 2 from a completely different angle, putting a lot of pressure on your opponent with cheap, hard-to-kill threats, while your opponent is hopefully stuck with now-useless [Surgical Extraction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction)s and Grafdigger's Cage in hand. It's a great way to sidestep the hate.


Among the players running this funky sideboard are Raphael Levy and Elie Pichon. I briefly sat down with Hall of Famer Raphael Levy to ask how the sideboard has been for him this weekend. "It has been very good for me", Raph said. "[Surgical Extraction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) just wrecks you otherwise, but now I don't really care about it. Also, [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) are extremely difficult to deal with for many decks."

### Game 1


Parker got to play first, and started strongly with [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul) and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger). Kowalski, on the other side of the table, was deploying [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) and [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) to get ahead on mana.


Parker used his fourth turn productively: he played [Geth's Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict) to clear the way and added [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) copying [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) to the board. This blazingly fast start already took down Kowalski to 6 life, and it looked like the Polish player was in trouble.


The best come-back he could muster was [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), but it was not enough. Another [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) and [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) from Parker was enough to convince Kowalski to move on to the next game.


Richard Parker 1 â€“ Grzegorz Kowalski 0


While shuffling Parker's deck, Kowalski noticed that some of Parker's cards were facing the wrong direction, and called a judge to verify that there was no pattern. Parker explained that he must have shuffled his cards from last game back into his deck upside-down by mistake. The judge at the table checked the deck, ruled that there was indeed no pattern, and instructed Parker to make sure that all of his sleeve openings are on the same side of his deck. After that, we were underway again.


### Game 2


Kowalski mulliganed down to five in search of a keepable hand, with Parker going down to six cards as well. A [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) accelerated the Pole into a third-turn [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), while the Brit started with [Fume Spitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fume+Spitter) and [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul).


When Parker attacked with both of his creatures on turn 4, Kowalski thought for a while before eventually trying to get rid of the 1/1 [Fume Spitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fume+Spitter) by blocking it with his 2/2 Wolf token. Parker, however, had plans for his [Fume Spitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fume+Spitter): he sacrificed it targeting [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), and a post-combat [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) dealt the second point of damage to kill the werewolf.


On the next turn, [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul) and the Wolf token traded; as a result, the board was completely swept clean again, save for a [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) that remained on Parker's side of the table. A [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) from Parker next dealt a ton of damage to Kowalski, before dying (twice) in a flurry of burn spells.



![Geth's Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Geth%27s+Verdict)![Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phantasmal+Image)

A couple turns later, an interesting situation arose: Kowalski board consisted of a freshly summoned [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) and an untapped [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). Parker had two [Geth's Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict) and a [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) in hand, with only enough mana to cast two of them. Parker could use [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to copy his opponent's Titan and subsequently try to kill the Titan with [Geth's Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict), but the end result of that play would be that Kowalski would lose his [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) instead and that Parker would lose his [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to Kowalski's [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) attack trigger. Thus, Parker just cleared his opponent's board with double [Geth's Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict).


Parker drew another [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) on his next turn, now stuck with two of them in his hand and no creatures to copy in sight. Kowalski eventually drew a creature in [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), searching up [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run). This gave Parker the opportunity to put his [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image)s to good use. However, Kowalski's Wolf Run could shoot down one of Parker's 6/6 Images by just targeting it, and Kowalski did just that before passing the turn again.


Parker drew [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) on his next turn, and given that his Polish opponent was already down a single-digit life total, the Brit saw the following sequence of plays to win the game that very turn: he started by casting [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) and equipping it with [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod). He subsequently attacked with his Phantasmal Titan, which was promptly blocked by Kowalski's real [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan). Mid-combat, Parker sacrificed his Messenger to deal one damage to his opponent, and followed it up with a [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip) on Kowalski's blocker. This allowed the Phantasmal Titan to trample for lethal damage. Excellent timing and a Morbid way of winning the match!

Sunday, 1:21 p.m. - Divulger of Secrets
---------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

Matthew Costa is not only a Pro Tour Top 8 competitor and Grand Prix champion, but it appears he's also one very nice guy. After his win at Grand Prix Baltimore a week ago, he was contacted by Germany's Sascha Schwarz through **Magic** Online who asked him about his deck, its match-ups, and advice on how to play it. Schwarz planned to play Costa's deck this weekend, and he was happily surprised to find Costa willing to share his insight.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplil12/blog_divulger_schwarz.jpg)*Scwarz started out this weekend at 9-0*
"He even wrote me an email and explained his sideboard choices," Schwarz said. "That was so cool. Also, what he told me about R/G Aggro completely transformed my view of the match-up. For example, he said that [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), if used correctly, was way better than you might think it would be against an aggressive deck, and that [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) was the MVP."


"In a lot of match-ups, it's apparently correct to board out [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker). So I thought: How about replacing them altogether with [Midnight Haunting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Haunting)? But Costa told me that the preboard games are very different and that the main reason for cutting the Stalker postboard was the opponent's artifact removal. I don't know whether I would've made the connection between artifact removal and [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) myself," admitted Schwarz, "and I'm happy Costa pointed it out."


"Another example: I originally wanted to have [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) in my sideboard," Schwarz continued, "however Costa warned me about the match-up against U/B Control. It's about evenâ€”on a theoretical levelâ€”though much harder to play correctly for the Delver player. That's why Costa had [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) in his sideboard and why I have them too. It's still very good against R/G Ramp, but simply not as narrow as [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze)."


Schwarz made good use of the advice he got, and started the tournament with a clean 9-0 sweep. Final words from Schwarz: "Thank you!"

Named "Heathy at Sam this morning" on the deck registration sheet, which also lists Ben Heath and Ross Silcock as the deck designers, this Blue-Black concoction throws together the best of Delver and Zombies. A card that stands out in particular is [Altar's Reap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar%27s+Reap), which seems like a perfect fit for this deck; it's an instant to reveal to [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), it can be flashbacked with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and it can eat [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and Geralf's Messenger for profit.

This interesting Ramp variant tries to accelerate into an early [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite), which is completely backbreaking against many decks in the format. Instead of [Slagstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) that the Red-Green ramp versions typically run, Dimitriu's deck has access to [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), and [Elspeth Tirel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth+Tirel), which also excel at stopping aggro decks. Although the Green-Black-White color combination misses access to [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run), Dimitriu makes up for that with [Vault of the Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+of+the+Archangel) and [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) as tutor targets for his [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan).

Nassif's list (designed by Sylvain Lauriol) is very similar to Mark Dictus' Zombie deck, which was featured yesterday. There are a few small differences, however. Firstly, Nassif has [Vault Skirge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) where Dictus has [Fume Spitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fume+Spitter). Secondly, Nassif has extra lands along with additional copies of the fearsome [Lashwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe) and [Phyrexian Obliterator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Obliterator), where Dictus has reactive cards in [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb). And finally, Nassif has extra [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) instead of Dictus' [Cemetery Reaper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper)s. The prospect of suiting up a [Vault Skirge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) with [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) is pretty exciting. With Nassif having overtaken Dictus in the standings, it is very well possible that the above card choices will become the standard for Mono Black zombies in the future.
