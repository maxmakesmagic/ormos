Adam Prosak’s Blue-Green Threshold
==================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on June 26, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Today's deck comes from Adam Prosak, a developer in *Magic* R&D and also a former writer of the Daily Decks column. Adam was the lead designer of *Tempest Remastered* and was also on the development teams for *Vintage Masters*, *Khans of Tarkir*, and *Modern Masters 2015 Edition*, to name just a few. Adam is also one of our best deck builders in R&D and is currently leading our FFL Team (Future Future League, our internal Standard league).



When I asked Adam for his favorite deck, he didn't hesitate to tell me about Blue-Green Threshold. Blue-Green decks were the strongest decks back during *Invasion-Odyssey* Standard but they usually consisted of [madness cards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advanced&output=spoiler&method=visual&text=+%5Bmadness%5D). The deck played creatures that allowed you to discard cards like [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) and [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) along with cards that you wanted to discard. [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm), and [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) were just a few of the cards these decks played that gained you value if they were discarded.



Adam's deck did something a little different. He was still discarding cards for value, but instead of casting madness spells, he was trying to fill up his graveyard to reach a threshold of seven cards. [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) and [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear) were the main rewards for doing this, and are the primary win conditions in the deck. The problem was that sometimes 3/3 and 4/4 creatures were not good enough to win. That's where [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) came in.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nimble+Mongoose)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Werebear)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Upheaval)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)


[Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) is a powerful *Magic* card. It allows you to play a control game by drawing cards, playing lands, and countering everything that is a threat to you. Then, when the time is right, tap all of your lands and cast [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), preferably with some mana floating to recast those [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose)s and [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear)s. When you have large creatures and your opponent has no permanents, winning is a whole lot easier!



Adam piloted Blue-Green Threshold back in 2002 and finished in the Top 8 of Ohio Valley Regionals with it. Soon after that, *Magic Online* launched for the first time and Adam jumped on and immediately built this deck. Blue-Green Threshold made him fall in love with the card [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) and his success with it marked the beginning of his *Magic* career.



Thanks for sharing your story, Adam!
