Zombie Aristocrats
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 3, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I've been getting some requests lately for more [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped) decks, which is fair, since I did say it was the card I was most excited to build with from *Dragon's Maze*, and today I've decided to share a deck of my own. 


![Varolz, the Scar-Striped](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped)
 I've been playing a lot with Junk (BGW) "aristocrats," which breaks up the aristocratic pair of [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) and [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) and replaces [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) with [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped). 


 Well, I thought, if the idea is that I need seven or eight creatures that let me sacrifice others, what happens if I keep [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) and cut [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat), while adding [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped)? After all, most of the creatures I want to sacrifice are cheap, so the curve could work better if my expensive cards are the ones I'm sacrificing my cheap cards to. 


 I lose [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) if I cut white but, lately, I haven't been that attached to it anyway. Losing [Doomed Traveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) hurts, but it's still worth considering. 


 I started with [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf), and [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) as my core cheap creatures, but I wanted another cheap creature I'd like to sacrifice, which brought me to [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), which brought me to wanting more Zombies, which brought me to [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger), and without [Twilight Mire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twilight+Mire), I really couldn't play [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) comfortably. 


 The final product ended up with a conservative splash of a single [Thrull Parasite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrull+Parasite), a card that could easily overperform, since its remove-counter ability works with both [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger). Originally, I skimped on [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist), but with sacrifice outlets and [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), I just had to max out on it. 


I expect this alternative to BGW to fare far better against control and far worse against aggro, so it's really a metagame call, but there could easily be a time and a place for this direction, and the sideboard offers a lot of cheap removal for opposing aggressive strategies.
