2004 World Championships Feature Match
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 5, 2004 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20171224024527im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_MikeTurian.jpg?itok=LXCQPehw)
By Mike Turian




Before joining Magic Online as its digital product manager, Mike worked as a producer for Wizards's technology department, a product manager for organized play, and as an R&D lead developer. He has played Magic Online since it came out in 2002 and Magic since The Dark. 






### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/qf4_1.jpg)Murray Evans said that to win this match he would need to get early [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) with one or two creatures to keep the pressure on Nuijten. Then he could use the card-drawing power of [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) and [Night's Whisper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night%27s+Whisper) to keep the threats coming versus Julien Nuijten's removal.


Evans came out of the gate quickly just as Affinity was designed to do. Evans played down two [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker)s, a [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating). Nuijten cleared the board with a third-turn Wrath of God while sitting at 14 life.


[Somber Hoverguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somber+Hoverguard) came out for Evans while Nuijten played the namesake of his deck, Astral Slide. Evans sent in the Hoverguard for six and then Mana Leaked what would have been a devastating [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness). The [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness)-Astral Slide combo was what Nuijten's deck was designed to do but Evans didn't allow the combo to happen.


Nuijten was forced to continually find cycling cards to keep [Somber Hoverguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somber+Hoverguard) from hitting him. He played a [Viridian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) and destroyed a [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating). When Evans cast a second Plating, Nuijten simply cycled out the Shaman.


After Evans played a second Hoverguard, Nuijten played a second [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) to fetch back Wrath of God. After casting the Wrath, Nuijten was at a precarious 1 life.


Evans tried one last push for the final damage but Nuijten had taken total control of the game with a Decree of Justice.


Julien Nuijten 1, Murray Evans 0


### Game 2


Evans started out game 2 with a mulligan. After the mulligan he played a first-turn [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). Nuijten tried to get an easy win by destroying Evans first land with an [Oxidize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize) but Evans had plenty of land even after the mulligan.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/qf4_2.jpg)Evans kept sending in [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) for one damage a turn while countering two of Nuijten's Astral Slides with Mana Leaks. Nuijten was determined to get out an Astral Slide and played an [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) to get one back. Evans used a [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) and a [Night's Whisper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night%27s+Whisper) to refill his hand. Then he played out [Slobad, Goblin Tinkerer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slobad%2C+Goblin+Tinkerer), [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager), and an [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker) but Nuijten had a Wrath of God and then a Akroma's Vengeance to first deal with Slobad and then kill the previously indestructible artifacts.


The Vengeance wiped out too much of Evans' board and conceded after Nuijten played a couple of creatures.


Julien Nuijten, 2 Murray Evans 0 


### Game 3


Evans found himself in a must-win situation going into game 3. He needed to win three straight games in order for him to advance to the semifinals.


Once again Evans, started off the game with a [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) but this time he also had an [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) to join in the assault. While Nuijten set up his mana base with two Rampant Growths, Evans had sacrificed an [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker) to pump his [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) up to a 3/3. Nuijten's [Viridian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) hit the [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) but Evans had played a second [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker) to modular the counters onto.


A [Slobad, Goblin Tinkerer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slobad%2C+Goblin+Tinkerer) came out just in time to save a small piece of Evans' board from Akroma's Vengeance. A [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) knocked Nuijten to one life for the second time in the match. Nuijten played out a Eternal Dragon to hold of the Nexus but if Evans drew a land his second Nexus would deal lethal damage.


He knocked on his deck and drew . . . [Night's Whisper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night%27s+Whisper). The Whisper found Evans a [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) that killed Nuijten the following turn.


Julien Nuijten 2, Murray Evans 1


### Game 4


Nuijten mulliganed to start game 4. Evans started off the game with a [Chromatic Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere). Once again, Nuijten went for the mana screw plan, using his [Oxidize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize) on Evans' first land. This time the strategy seemed to be successful, as Evans didn't have a second land.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds04/qf4_3.jpg)On his third turn, Evans played out a [Seat of the Synod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seat+of+the+Synod) and used his [Chromatic Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere) to make the mana for a [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). Nuijten used an [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) to bring back the [Oxidize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize) to leave Evans landless once again.


Evans discarded on his next turn and then for the rest of the game whenever he found a land Nuijten would have another [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) to retrieve the same [Oxidize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize) over and over. After Nuijten had three Witnesses in play Evans had finally had enough and conceded with a lone City of Brass in play.


The 15-year-old Julien Nuijten was moving on to the Worlds semifinals.


**Final Score**  
 Julien Nuijten 3, Murray Evans 1
