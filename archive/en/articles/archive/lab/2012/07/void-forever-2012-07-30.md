Okay, right, welcome back! Today's laboratory jaunts stem partly from my reflection on last week's article. I featured some reader decks based around [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), that new heady powerhouse. It was also Exalted Week, and I made use of [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection), which stands in similar Johnny-jewel territory. I thought that in fairness to this also-illustrious blue enchantment, I'd share some more ideas concerning it this week. So it's a slight return to *Avacyn Restored*, a neat set which has been slightly dimmed in the wake of ***Magic** 2013*.


### Infinite Rhinos


In returning to [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection), it's worth it to take a quick scan over the card and remember what shenanigans to actualize with it. While turning all of your creatures into a copy of another is quite exciting, it's also beneficial to recognize that [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) is a blue permanent. Why? Because of the exciting Reflective potential of everyone's favorite Rhino Soldier, [Mirror-Sigil Sergeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror-Sigil+Sergeant).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl206_infiniterhinos.jpg)  
Timothy Mellors emailed me about the idea, and included his take on it in sixty-card form. His deck was a green-white-blue concoction that used creature mana ramp to land its six-drops quickly, with [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) perhaps injecting some crazy.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Doubling Season | Mirror-Sigil Sergeant |

I liked the idea and decided to try my own version of the deck, taking some hints from my first Lab stab at the Sergeant ([three years ago](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/26)). This time, I left [Paradox Haze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Haze) on the bench (with some faint sadness) and suited up with [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) and [Followed Footsteps](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Followed+Footsteps) as my blue Auras of choice. To fetch them, I enlisted [Totem-Guide Hartebeest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Totem-Guide+Hartebeest). (Is there another type of Hartebeest, or is this the only type of Hartebeest? Hartebeest.) The Aura tutor effect opened a tiny window for Aura-shaped removal to enter the deck. The curt clampdown offered by [Crystallization](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystallization) and [Claustrophobia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claustrophobia) can keep some creatures kicked to the curb... and they're blue permanents for the Sergeant.


Instead of mana guys, I went with defense guys. A parade of walls and little skilled creatures hold down the fort and act as [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) fodder later. [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) and [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) Back are great barriers that serve useful early-game purposes. [Palladium Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palladium+Myr) can launch you into six mana on turn four, which is welcome. [Reckless Scholar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Scholar) provides an insular method of fiddling with your spells in hand, helping you find the right card. Finally, [Ethereal Usher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Usher) can transmute into any of your six-drops, be they Rhinos or Reflections (or the emergency [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command)).

> Obviously this deck wants to start out by summoning a bunch of Elves (and maybe one or two [Somberwald Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage)s). Once you have your Elves, there are a few things you can do with them:
> 
> 
> * The dream is to get enough mana to cast [Wormfang Manta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Manta) followed by [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) in the same turn. At this point, your opponent has at most two turns to kill you (one turn if you left a creature untapped) or else he or she will be forced to give you extra turns just to stave off your 6-power Nightmare Fish Beasts. Or if your opponent is being uncooperative and holding off your guys without killing them, then [Denizen of the Deep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Denizen+of+the+Deep) will let you cash in all your extra turns at once.
> * Alternatively, you can put [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) on [Soul of the Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+the+Harvest) and draw increasingly many cards for each new creature you summon. [Soul of the Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+the+Harvest) can also help you draw into [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) or whatever else you're looking for in the late game.
> * If you happen to draw [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) with enough mana to cast it, then you are in excellent shape—every other card in your deck is a permanent, so you can dump most of your deck onto the battlefield. If that includes a [Wormfang Manta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Manta) and an [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection), then even mass removal might not be enough to save your opponent.
> * And of course, if all else fails, you can pump up your guys with [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) (possibly with [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) on it) and swing.

> [Mark of Eviction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+Eviction) serves several functions in this deck. First, it serves as temporary but repeatable removal; [Alchemist's Refuge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alchemist%27s+Refuge) is in here mainly to let you do this on your opponent's turn (otherwise he or she gets a turn to use the creature before the Mark can bounce it). Second, it lets you draw more cards every turn if you use it on [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) or if you control a [Soul of the Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+the+Harvest). Finally, it lets you reset [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection); this is particularly important with [Wormfang Manta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Manta), since you don't want every creature you summon to make you lose a turn.

> This is just sick. Thanks to [Essence Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Harvest), [Dimir Machinations](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Machinations) can now find both parts of the combo, allowing this deck to win on about turn four quite often. The rest of the deck is there for serious redundancy—Rites work as Harvest five and six; Negator, Drinker, Avatar have great power-to-CMC ratios so they can deal quick damage and then be sacrificed away. This works especially well because Disciple only cares about power (unlike [Momentous Fall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentous+Fall)). [Soul Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Spike) plays defense, deals extra damage if you need it (although with everybody playing fetchlands, shocklands, and Phyrexian mana you might not even need it), or can win the game in multiples after Disciple has drawn a number of cards. And if nothing else, I hear "Ritual, Negator, go" has also won a fair share of games...
