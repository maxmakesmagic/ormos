Bloodghasts for Borborygmos!
============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 1, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Continuing from the theme of [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) as a shell, I want to discuss a Legacy deck I built last week that I'm particularly excited about. 


![Borborygmos Enraged](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Borborygmos+Enraged)
 This deck uses [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) to enable an extremely wide variety of graveyard shenanigans with [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) fueling a variety of sacrifice costs. Specifically, [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), [Goblin Bombardment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bombardment), and—most importantly—[Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return). 


 Using [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) with dredge to fuel [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) and [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) is nothing new—Dredge has been doing it for years, but because the dredge engine is [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) can actually be cast (fairly easily) for four mana. Additionally, the lands allow the deck to cast other spells, allowing it to play a regular, fair game of **Magic**—something I always like to be able to do in case my opponent can stop my first plan. 



[Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) helps play that fair game, offering access to a variety of creatures that are happy to be around my full graveyard—[Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary)—as well as giving me access to [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) to deal with any enchantments or artifacts my opponents might play to try to remove my graveyard. 



[Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) allows me to find [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) or a particular land to get back if I already have [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), and when I'm ready, it can find a big legend or a [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) to let me do a Reanimator impression. 


 The single [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) looks particularly out of place in a deck with no other Zombies, but [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) is a reliable way to return [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and can be found by [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) or [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary). [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) is important because it enables [Goblin Bombardment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bombardment) in a way other creatures don't. 


 The sideboard offers a full complement of [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s to assist the [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay)s in controlling the opponent's [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) population, which is one of the best weapons against this kind of strategy. [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) can replace the reanimation package against opponents who will be good at disrupting that plan, but it's risky to include both in the deck at the same time, as [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) could hit you for 8 at any time if they're both in the deck.
