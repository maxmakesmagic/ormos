Don't Call it a Comeback
========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 13, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







R*eturn to Ravnica* block in Standard has proven a fluid and flexible set of sets. At the start of the season, RWU was all about porting miracles from *Innistrad* block and locking down the board with the tag team of [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage)... [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) as a red splash was largely there as a foil to [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) and [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler). RWU has reinvented itself into a variety of styles... from an aggro deck with [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) to a flash deck based around two-drop Wizards and permission spells to a variety of [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) strategies (including an infinite-life combo deck). 



[Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) itself has proven to be an amazing tool in Standard; promoting not just an infinite life sequence but the "brain you for 13" combination with [Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act). In The Aristocrats, those cards linked arms to give [Tom Martell a PT victory](/en/events/coverage/ptgtc13), and that WBR deck has continued to evolve via the good work of [Jake Van Lunen](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Jacob%20Van%20Lunen), Brad Nelson, and a host of inheritors. 


 Naya has had some of the wildest swings in the format, manifesting as Midrange, Ramp, and mono-aggro Blitz decks, all. When [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) was previewed, it seemed like an aggro player's dream—a 4/4 creature for only three mana—but [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) has done more good work as a blocker of beatdown creatures than an active attacker; many sorts of Naya decks remain active and successful. 


Let's look at some of these moves from the most recent Standard Open...

Act 2 is the present evolution of The Aristocrats.


 This iteration of the deck has eschewed the explosive potential of the original with [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), instead focusing on powerful synergies keying on [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) and [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). The Aristocrats has always been a deck based on "sacrificing its own creatures" and the addition of [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) gives the deck a good way to exploit the sacrificial abilities on [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) and [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat).

| --- | --- |
| Blood Artist | Cartel Aristocrat |

 While Act 2 has many powerful cards, one of the centerpieces is [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) is a lockdown defender against Blitz decks (either it guarantees a two-for-one or discourages attacks at all)... and in this deck it is half of Act 2's endgame. Watch out for this deck's aggressive opens: it doesn't need to deal 20 damage... it only needs to get you to 13 life. 



[Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) + [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) gives the deck a great way to win without being forced to attack for all 20 points. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Blasphemous Act | Boros Reckoner |

But if you *do* "have" to attack?


 There are worse attacking decks than The Aristocrats. [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) is an awesome four-drop. It can gobble up whichever creatures to defend itself, but is in particular synergistic with the deck's many Human creatures. 


 The five-drops are really devastating... [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) and [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion) out of the sideboard can rip up [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) or produce armies many times what a [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) will typically produce. [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) is an awesome threat, a 5/5 attacker for only five mana, and quite difficult to pin down with removal.

### Naya Blitz


Naya Blitz is a RGW deck with a low mana count (often just twenty lands) and super-low mana curve. Naya Blitz, generally speaking, wants to do one thing: attack!


Naya Blitz is, offensively, one of the fastest decks in the current Standard, but it has the odd card it's afraid of:


![Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Reckoner)
  
 As [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) is big enough and fast enough (in creature combat) to put a serious slowdown on Blitz's blitzes, clever Naya Blitz players have modified their decks to help get creatures through in combat.

 Naya Blitz in general is a deck that starts quickly and hits hard. [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), [Experiment One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One), and [Boros Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite) are all one-drops that can get big quickly to punch well above their collective one-drop weight class. [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) gives the deck an unquestionable explosiveness, and [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) makes more or less all blocks bad.

 Millner added a couple of different cards to his deck that allow it to function a bit differently from other Blitz decks. While still quick and aggressive, the non-Blitz Naya/Naya Zoo decks favor card quality over speed.


 Look at the deck at various casting costs... [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) on two; both [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) and [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) on three. All these cards are great at defending in the red zone. 


![Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Voice+of+Resurgence)
  
 On balance, the deck has [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) as one of the finest finishers in the format. 


 Edgar switched up a couple of elements from the stock Naya deck. Many similar Naya decks play [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) on four (a great combo with [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), of course) but Edgar went with [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) in that slot. Along with [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm), [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) helps all those great creatures win fights on offense. 


 Now, speaking of fights, [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) is quite a serious piece of action in this deck. Again, [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) fights well above its casting cost, making [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade)'s -2 great right out of the gate. [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) as a 5/5 can beat up most everything. But the real killer? [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)! Again! 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Domri Rade | Boros Reckoner |


[Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) can beat up smaller creatures and gets the bonus trigger allowing it to beat up another creature or just damage the opponent. 


 Naya Zoo plays a wide variety of awesome and flexible cards in the sideboard. It can go big with [Garruk, Primal Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) and fight a variety of strategies with specialty cards like [Ray of Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ray+of+Revelation) or [Ground Seal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal). [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) lets the deck go over the top in combat, and [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) gives it both offensive capabilities (double strike and burn to win outside the red zone) and resistance to [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Garruk, Primal Hunter | Ray of Revelation |

But...


Did you see it?


Did you see the infinite combo?


 Oh yeah, *[Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) again.*



 You can give [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) lifelink (say, with [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage)); get in a fight, creature combat, get your guy burned, block, whatever... and then use [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm). [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) can make [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) indestructible—sorry, *indestructible*—such that when its damage trigger comes around, it can damage itself over and over and over again. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Unflinching Courage | Boros Charm |

 You can have your [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) damage itself an arbitrary number of times, gaining life each time thanks to lifelink, playing a lucky charms game. 


Does it come up often?


Not so much.


But when it does? *Infinite*.

Jund remains Standard's premier midrange deck. It is a classic combination of high-quality threats and answers, capable of competing with opponents on many levels.


 Mispagel's creature suite of [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s makes for quite a bit of life gain, and [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) can take over a game all by herself.

Jund has Planeswalkers to generate value, cards, card advantage, threats, and whatnot with no incremental cost in mana.


 Jund has disruption like [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) and [Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) to rip up the opponent's hand (and sometimes kill opposing Planeswalkers) and can keep Reanimator or [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) strategies honest with [Ground Seal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal). 


 On top of all manner of removal—[Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned), [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay), [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy), [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame), and more—even Jund's acceleration is shooting at an angle.  Blackard played a relatively aggressive version of RWU with few four-ofs. His only main-deck four-ofs were super-high-quality creatures [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), plus staple draw-smoother [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm). 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Snapcaster Mage | Azorius Charm |

Playing a wide variety of different cards allows Blackard's version to play many different games.


 This deck can put pressure on the opponent with [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and keep blockers out of the way with tons of different kinds of point-removal spells. Of particular note are *Dragon's Maze* burn spells [Warleader's Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix) and [Turn & Burn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%26+Burn). Both of these cards can kill a [Sire of Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+Insanity)... a dangerous threat for any permission deck. 



[Ral Zarek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek), too, can tap potential blockers out of the way, as well as playing double [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s to help finish off a game. 



[Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) is a great finisher here, especially with all the burn.

This Top Decks concludes with the rare Esper deck.


Standard's best-performing decks tend to be aggressive ones, although the various midrange decks also have their adherents. Esper Control is a deck that is focused on creature removal and card advantage; essentially true control.


 All the cards in this deck play defense. [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) and [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) can fight... but are superb on defense both. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Aetherling | Augur of Bolas |


[Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad) can both potentially win the game... but spend many turns jamming up the red zone before getting to the break point. 


Every other card in the main either generates card advantage or somehow contains threats (or both).


 Esper has many attractive aspects. It doesn't care if you gain infinite life. Although Sipka's fastest way to win is [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling), it can run you out of cards with [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard), no matter how much life you have. Esper dodges removal. Full of removal itself, the deck is remarkably resilient to creature removal in general... [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) both trade with removal cards with value, and [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling)—once it has stuck—is quite difficult to get rid of. Esper can answer a wide variety of opponents. Although the deck is full of creature removal, [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) and [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) don't really discriminate. It can even side up to four [Devour Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh)es + two [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%26+Away)s + four [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s to fight all the [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)s and their [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker)s, too! 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Oblivion Ring | Dissipate |

If you are in the market for an answer deck (rather than the more typical proactive ones), Esper can make for quite a convincing argument.


 The present Standard gives players many options for viable decks. Beatdown, midrange, somewhere between beatdown and midrange, a little combo action out of The Aristocrats... and even the odd control deck!
