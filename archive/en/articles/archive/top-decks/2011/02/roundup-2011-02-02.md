![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores

As with the season as a whole, all different kinds of decks won PTQs, lots more made Top 8 ... and Faeries remained the most popular and successful archetype overall.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td128_chart.jpg)  
Let's look at the big winners, and then some of the new and different decks we haven't discussed yet, at least this season.

Believe it or not, "Faeries" isn't just any old Faeries. The deck, as we have it in Extended, is highly customizable, despite a slavish adherence to core cards like [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). For example, Hanson's seems geared more towards combating control and combo. He has twenty-six lands and one in the sideboard. Of these, he has added two main and one side [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)s. The Faeries creature mix can go a lot of different directions ... Andrew went with three copies of [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique), which perform strongly against combo and control decks as a combination of clock and disruption.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Beyond the customization, Hanson has all the toys ... [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) for super threats; [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) for top-end blue spells. Andrew supplemented the four staple copies of [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) with [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), and [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) with the [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique)-answering [Grasp of Darkness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness).

He was a bit more aggressive with his non-Faeries sideboard creature package ... Two copies of [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)*and* three copies of [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk)... Pretty good setup against the beatdown.

This is a Green-Blue [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) / [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) deck very similar to the one that Jason Ford used to win [Grand Prix Atlanta](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpatl11/welcome).

The deck works by playing out [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) to "turn on" [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle), despite playing only one [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). 

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle)  
Unlike some of the other [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) decks in the format, Green-Blue absolutely needs to have [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) in play in order to win with [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) damage. As such, players of all different strategies have started adopting cards that can deal with a [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) as part of their main decks and sideboards. To wit:


> * [Deglamer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deglamer) – A [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) analogue that shuffles away a [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen), most excitingly while [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) is on the stack. [Deglamer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deglamer) is slightly worse than [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) when dealing with [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) specifically (especially against [Wargate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wargate) variants), but far superior against [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) decks. Most commonly found in Jund sideboards.
> * [Consign to Dream](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consign+to+Dream) – A [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang) effect that can target ... whatever. A reasonable tempo card, this one rubs up against [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) versus red and green permanents. When you play [Consign to Dream](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consign+to+Dream) with [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) on the stack, you force the opponent to shuffle [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) away, rather than "just" putting it on top of the opponent's deck. Because a deck with multiple copies of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) in play—as well as [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen)—can deal a ton of damage in a hurry, forcing the opponent to shuffle the card away rather than just forcing him to waste a card can be very desirable. Most commonly found in Faeries sideboards.
> * [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) – A fine bringer of beatdown. A good threat, a good buddy to solo attackers a la [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel); very low cost to play main deck. Almost always played in Naya [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) decks, and is also seen main deck in builds like Green-White Caw-whatever.
> 

News Flash: Green-Blue [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) Omens is going to be among the Decks to Beat, at least until *Mirrodin Beseiged* shakes things up.

> * Eight fetch lands – Some players are puzzled at the presence of [Arid Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) and [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) in straight red decks. This one takes advantage of the fetches to break [Plated Geopede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) on offense, and flips [Searing Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) live even on the opponent's turn.
> * Four [Teetering Peaks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks) – Useful in particular with the many haste creatures.
> * [Stigma Lasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stigma+Lasher)! "You cannot gain life."

The mana in Extended is pretty great. As you can see, Alfano was able to add black for [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) main-deck without compromising his mana base. [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) is a stronger card than almost every card in the default red deck. 

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thought+Hemorrhage)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Hemorrhage)Additionally, he has all kinds of gas in the sideboard, made available by the introduction of black:


> * [Anathemancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anathemancer) – [Anathemancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anathemancer) provides brutal reach against many kinds of decks in Extended, in particular decks like Four-Color Control, or even Jund variants with very few basics. You can run it out there on turn three, or wait until much later for more impact (and, as the case may be) enough space to pay for a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). [Anathemancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anathemancer) is ferocious and to a degree inevitable. You can block a bigger creature and re-buy later. Control decks can't even counter the [Anathemancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anathemancer)'s unearth.
> * [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) and [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) – One of the traditional enemies of red decks is the White Weenie deck. [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker) and its cohorts slough off [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s and rush past red blockers. Similarly, big green decks (like the presently popular big Naya decks) can pose similar problems; they are offensively fast and pose threats like [Woolly Thoctar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woolly+Thoctar) and [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) that are too big for a [Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bolt). Adding [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) and [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) gives a red deck more—and mana efficient—options for fighting these enemies of the red.
> * [Thought Hemorrhage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Hemorrhage) – Red decks are often soft to combo. They are fast compared to fair decks, but not necessarily fast compared to decks that can win on turn three or four. Resolving a [Thought Hemorrhage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Hemorrhage) will often rattle a combo deck's ability to win with a perfect, often uncontested, fast goldfish kill. Just another bonus Bogarted by bringing on that second color.

Duncan's deck is another classic example of an upgrade to an existing archetype by adding an additional color; in this case, white.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

What does white give Kyle?


> * [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) – One of the highest-quality creatures in the format, [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) gives Duncan the ability to dig up more copies of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle), as well as the ability to field a powerful board presence several turns before [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) comes online.
> * [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) – In addition to a good and fast defensive spell, [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) is a kind of thirteenth acceleration spell. In a pinch you can Path your own creature to set up a lethal [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift).
> * Sideboard cards ([Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) and [Hallowed Burial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Burial)) – Duncan can slow play against a creature deck by taking the control role. He could methodically get ahead and stay ahead with removal, buying time before going lethal.
> 

While this deck plays four copies of [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen), given Kyle's nine [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, his deck doesn't strictly need [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) out to win (but especially with [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), it can be nice).

A lot of the "double Standard" Extended decks take their inspiration from past (and in the case of Red-Green [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) Ramp, *present*) Standard decks. Boutin's Green-White Tokens deck is reminiscent of the dominant strategy from about two years ago, but jazzed up with some recent bombs.

Basic game plan: The Green-White Tokens deck seeks to break [Windbrisk Heights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights) (a lot like Green-White Hideaway), but is more focused on a solid and consistent beatdown than going over the top with a freebie [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul) or Iona. I mean a free [Ajani Goldmane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Goldmane) or even [Wilt-Leaf Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) is still quite advantageous in terms of mana spent (you devote two lands) and the fact that it is a bonus card.

You can go [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) into [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession), then smash for 6 with a boost. You can get lethal quickly with [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), compete on power via your planeswalkers, or take out a [Prismatic Omen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) or [awesome Blossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=awesome+Blossom) with your [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage).

The Green-White Tokens deck plays a fair number of spoilers—[Dauntless Escort](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Escort) prevents an opposing control deck from sweeping your team, and [Linvala, Keeper of Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence) can *silence* an entire Elves or Mythic Conscription deck. Lots of stuff against Faeries ([Great Sable Stag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag), [Cloudthresher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher), [Guttural Response](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guttural+Response)!) all out of the board, too.

(Don't forget to say "Gutterball" when you spike a [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command)).

The White-Green Tokens poses a surprisingly quick and versatile attack deck with lots of ways to interact with the other top decks in the format without playing the usual suspects.
