=============================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 14, 2013

Everyone back? Great, let's get started.


One of the primary issues facing the Commander format is the disparity in the power level of decks built by different players. As a casual format, the goal is not to build the most powerful deck you can, and every player constructs a deck with a different idea in mind. Some players find things like infinite combos and land destruction unacceptable, while others enjoy these aspects of the game.


The goal of a game of Commander is not necessarily winning, but creating a game environment in which every player has fun. This becomes even more difficult when you consider how nebulous the idea of fun really is. However, one of the things you can do to foster such an environment is to build decks of differing power levels. If you play with people who hate infinite combos, locks, and land destruction, having a more casual deck allows you to play a real game with them rather than crushing them all in the grip of your super-combo. On the other hand, if you run across a group of more cutthroat Commander players, having a deck that can handle that kind of pressure can keep you from feeling left out of the game.


Today, I'll be taking a look at the first of two very different Commander decks. Both use interesting Johnny strategies to win, but they operate at different levels on the casual spectrum. We'll start out by taking a look at the more cutthroat of the two: A [Riku of Two Reflections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riku+of+Two+Reflections) deck designed around a card that is not [Riku of Two Reflections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riku+of+Two+Reflections).

 This deck is built to do one thing: Cast [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World). As many times as possible. Ok, maybe not as many times as possible, because then you'd be sitting at the table until you died of starvation, but at least enough times to allow you to kill everyone. 


 There are three parts to keeping the world warping continuously. The first is returning [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) from your graveyard to your hand. Fortunately, there are quite a few creatures that will do this when they enter the battlefield. 


![Warp World](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Warp+World)
  
### Anarchists



> 
> 
> [Anarchist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anarchist)  
> [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer)  
> [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness)  
> [Izzet Chronarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Chronarch)  
> [Mnemonic Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Wall)  
> [Nucklavee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nucklavee)
> 
> 
> 


 Put any of these onto the battlefield with [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World), and you can return it to your hand from the graveyard, allowing you to cast it again if you have the mana. Now, you might still have mana floating from before the world warped but, if not, you'll need to be able to produce some now. That's the second part. Almost every land in this deck enters the battlefield untapped, making it much easier to muster up the eight mana to cast [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) again. I've also included a number of pieces of mana acceleration, most of which also enter the battlefield untapped. This also helps you get your first [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) set up much more quickly.

 The last part to enabling you to cast [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) as many times as you want is making sure the number of permanents you're shuffling doesn't get smaller each time. Since there's always a chance of hitting an instant or sorcery when flipping cards off the top of your library (even though this deck only has seven of them), you'll need some permanents that put tokens onto the battlefield, making your number go up instead of down. This also helps make that first [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) big enough to ensure you hit an [Anarchist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anarchist).

 All these tokens will also come in handy for winning the game. Even without any other shenanigans, if you manage to [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) until your entire deck is on the battlefield, [Chancellor of the Forge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chancellor+of+the+Forge) will make 160 hasty goblins all on its own. Of course, 160 damage isn't that much. I'm sure we can do better. 


 To be able to attack with all those creatures you just got, you'll need a way to give them all haste. Fortunately, there are a number of permanents that can do that and more. In the end, I chose to include [Fires of Yavimaya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Yavimaya), [Akroma's Memorial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Memorial), and [Ogre Battledriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Battledriver). The Battledriver also makes all your creatures a bit larger, and [Akroma's Memorial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Memorial) grants a whole slew of handy abilities.

 If you can't put your entire deck onto the battlefield for some reason, you'll need a way to pump up the creatures you do have. [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) is the best one, making for a truly absurd amount of damage. With a deck-sized [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) you can easily exceed 100,000 damage, and with the help of [Riku of Two Reflections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riku+of+Two+Reflections) you can approach one million. [Beastmaster Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beastmaster+Ascension) is less impressive at only a couple thousand damage, but it still gets the job done. I've already mentioned [Ogre Battledriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Battledriver), and although [Eldrazi Monument](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument) only gives +1/+1, it also makes all your creatures indestructible, protecting you from cards like [Rout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rout) and [Starstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starstorm). 


 Although attacking for enormous amounts of damage is my personal cup of tea, I wanted to include an alternative win condition in case that wasn't feasible for some reason. To this end, I added [Hedron Crab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab) to the deck. If you do manage to go infinite with [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World), you can use the landfall triggers from [Hedron Crab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab) to mill out all your opponents.
