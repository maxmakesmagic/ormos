Primal Surge Ramp
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 4, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






You might have seen plenty of [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)-based ramp decks before, but never like this! R&D member Steve Warner loves playing with wacky cards, and one of the craziest cards in all of *Avacyn Restored* is [Primal Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge). Steve brought this deck to Seattle-area **Magic** store Game On! for spellslinging at last weekend's Prerelease. Several opponents found themselves in a rough place as [Primal Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) threatened to flip half—or more—of his deck onto the table.


The tricky part of [Primal Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) is that you don't really want any other non-permanents in your deck, pushing normal ramp options like [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) right out. [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) is still there, but what else can you use?


Turns out, there's quite a bit available in Standard! The trifecta of [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212), and [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) all made the cut, and [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) just added to the punch. Steve dug a little deeper and came up with two fairly underused cards: [Rites of Flourishing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) and [Shrine of Boundless Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Boundless+Growth). These can quickly help push you into [Primal Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) range.


Primal Surging into [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)s should usually be more than enough defeat your opponent, but you can also just cast your creatures in this deck! [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth); [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan); [Soul of the Harvest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+the+Harvest); and [Sigarda, Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons) all pose gigantic threats to your opponent's life total.


Check out Steve's decklist below. If you've been looking to tweak your ramp decks to make use of this new ten-mana sorcery, Steve's deck is right up your alley!
