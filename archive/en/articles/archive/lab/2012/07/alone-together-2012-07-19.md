Alone, Together
===============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on July 23, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello and welcome back to the Lab. This week I've got a mixed bag of sorts for you all. The most obvious party favor in the bag is exalted, the given theme to this week's website coverage. However, I have a lot more [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) decks in my inbox that I think deserve a mention, so the second half of today's column will be dedicated to those.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl205_thunder.jpg)[Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) | Art by [Svetlin Velinov](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Svetlin%20Velinov%22%5D)

Exalted isn't exactly bursting at the seams with interactions in the first place, so I think I can safely splice my column so. I'm not knocking the mechanic, fresh with attention due to its inclusion in ***Magic** 2013*. Attacking alone has its own savory rhythm, one that I feel a certain kinship with. I liked the mechanic back in *Shards of Alara* and this summer it has bled into a fourth color. Throw [Lunk Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lunk+Errant) into the mix, and exalted seems to cover the whole color pie!

I have two decks to present based around exalted today. One comes from a reader named Bryan, and the other is my take on [Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel). Lets sally forth!

Dark Exaltation
I knew I wanted to touch on black exalted today, and Bryan's idea was relatively straight-forward. In his words: "Essentially the deck started as a way to try to capitalize on the ability of [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) to swing in and hit unimpeded. It then grew to incorporate flying haste creatures, with a backup of black exalted to punch through."

Sublimity
Alright, onto [Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel). Leave it to a mythic rare to vastly expand the potential of exalted. I don't think I've ever read the phrase "each triggers separately" with such reverence. (Sorry, [Cavalry Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavalry+Master).)

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl205_sublime.jpg)[Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel) | Art by [Cynthia Sheppard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Cynthia+Sheppard%22%5D)

[Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel) makes sense in a deck with a ton of creatures. That way, she'll give them all exalted and thus pump up a future solitary attacker. And if all those creatures themselves had exalted? Double the bonus!

And if all those creatures were also [Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel)s? Um, that's a lot of exalted damage. And that situation isn't actually that unlikely, once you introduce a little bit of infinite. [Infinite Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection), that is.

With merely four creatures on the board, one of which being a [Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel), a Reflection targeting it will leave you with four Archangels. They all have exalted (four triggers) and all cause the others to have more of it. When the libertarian dust settles, a single incoming [Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel) will be exactly a 20/19.

Of course, with all the Bant-flavored aggro in the deck, you should be able to whittle your opponent down somewhat before an Infinite strike. [Akrasan Squire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akrasan+Squire), [Aven Squire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Squire), and [Sigiled Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigiled+Paladin) get the party started. The cascading [Ardent Plea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ardent+Plea) will hit one of those creatures while providing another exalted boost.

[Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) is a solid problem solver, and [Silent Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Arbiter) is a wonky way to turn your exalted strategy into a solid defense plan. You don't mind swinging with just one creature, but your opponent might.

More Omni
Alright, onto the other [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) decks. Again, these are just fun decks that I thought were worth a mention, even if they crowd exalted's spotlight a bit this week.

Michael Jeffery sent in a list with an admirable plan: dome your opponent with multiple [Searing Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Wind)s. With Eldrazi backup. He wrote: 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl205_omni.jpg)[Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) | Art by [Jason Chan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Jason+Chan%22%5D)


> My deck's game plan is pretty simple: ramp to ten mana with artifacts, chain a bunch of draw spells together, and kill my opponent with a few [Searing Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Wind)s.
> 
> The artifacts I chose are ones that have a use besides making mana, after all once [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) hits the battlefield we don't really need mana anymore. [Dreamstone Hedron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamstone+Hedron) and [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone) both can be sacrificed for more cards. [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem) is here because once he is on the field we are pretty much guaranteed to get ten mana on the next turn. I also included [Crystal Vein](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Vein), [Dwarven Ruins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dwarven+Ruins), and [Svyelunite Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Svyelunite+Temple) in the deck. Each can sacrifice for two mana, so they're great in this scenario where all we need to do is hit ten mana and then we never need lands again. They allow for some pretty explosive draws, allowing you to get [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) out as early as turn four.
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |


> In a similar vein I have included a few copies of [Devastation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devastation). Blowing up all lands doesn't bother us after we have [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) out and the deck has so few creatures that the spell has virtually no effect on us. On the other hand, it will completely reset our opponent's board, giving us breathing room to advance our game plan.
> 
> And what a wonderful plan it is. Once [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) is out and our opponent is helpless we can play draw spell after draw spell after draw spell after draw spell until we have the tools to we need to win. Two [Searing Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Wind)s to the head will kill our opponent dead, while a [Kozilek, Butcher of Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%2C+Butcher+of+Truth) and or [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) running into the red zone will do the same thing in short order but with more style. Seriously, in a deck designed to cast big bad spells, how could anyone resist including [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul)?
> 
>

For some reason, I really like these lists with just a single [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) in them. It just... feels right to me.

Finally, Aaron Golas continues the Standard bent with his blue-black idea. He wrote: 


> First, we want a bit of mana ramp to kick things off. [Palladium Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palladium+Myr) and [Gilded Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus) fill this role with support from [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph), letting us cast our biggest spells (including [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience)) as early as turn five.
> 
> [Rune-Scarred Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune-Scarred+Demon) is a powerhouse, and even better when he's free. The most obvious thing to fetch with the Demon is another Demon (or a cheap Phyrexian knockoff), putting a ton of power in the sky at once. He can also grab his big brother [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) for even more card-drawing power.
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |


> But if we want to get tricky (and we do), we add [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) and [Ghostly Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker) to the mix. [Ghostly Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker) can reset Metamorph to something more useful and gets extra tutoring out of a Demon. Meanwhile, flickering [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) lets us return [Ghostly Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker) to our hand, turning him into a variation on [Deadeye Navigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator).
> 
> Thus we come to the combo. A flight of Demons is pretty saucy, but what if we don't want to wait for our attack step before we close out the game? [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) + [Rune-Scarred Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune-Scarred+Demon) + [Ghostly Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker) + [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) = tutor for our entire library, cast [Laboratory Maniac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Laboratory+Maniac) and [Amass the Components](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amass+the+Components) for the win. Now that's true [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience)!"
> 
>
