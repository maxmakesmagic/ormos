Assembling Contraptions
=======================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on June 17, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







Welcome to the Lab! It's Modern Week here on DailyMTG, so I decided to take some inspiration from the first block of the Modern format: *Mirrodin*. *Mirrodin* was a gold mine of interesting rares, brimming with cool artifacts fit for any number of awesome combos. Today I'll be taking a number of cards from throughout the block and combining them into some great combo decks.


### Brain Damage


The first card I wanted to take advantage of was [Psychogenic Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychogenic+Probe). I've always had a soft spot for odd, narrow cards like this, and I was sure I could find some way of making your opponent shuffle his or her library an infinite number of times. As it turns out, the first step in making that happen is [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight). It costs only a single mana to cast and can then sacrifice itself to make your opponent shuffle. There are a number of cards in *Mirrodin* block that return artifacts with converted mana cost 1 or less from your graveyard. With *Modern Masters* fresh in my mind, [Auriok Salvagers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Salvagers) was the first thing I thought of. However, after playing around with that for a while, I found an easier way.

[Salvaging Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salvaging+Station) can tap to return [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) to the battlefield and untaps itself whenever a creature dies. The key to making a creature die on every loop was simple: [March of the Machines](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines). When you sacrifice your 1/1 [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), it will untap [Salvaging Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salvaging+Station). Since [March of the Machines](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines) only affects what's on the battlefield, [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) is a noncreature artifact in the graveyard and can be returned by the untapped [Salvaging Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salvaging+Station). [March of the Machines](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines) did create one problem, however: summoning sickness. Since [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) is a creature, it can't tap to activate its ability right away. Fortunately, yet another artifact can solve this problem. [Thousand-Year Elixir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thousand-Year+Elixir) allows you to activate your creatures' abilities as though they had haste, meaning you can tap and sacrifice [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) right away, even though it just entered the battlefield.

| --- | --- |

 |

There are a lot of artifacts that need to be searched for here, so I've included both [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) and [Tezzeret the Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret+the+Seeker) to help you assemble this massive contraption. Note that you should usually use [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) to search for [Salvaging Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salvaging+Station) if you don't already have it, since that's the only one Tezzeret can't get right away. I've also included [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) to help you filter through your library and [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) to put a roadblock in front of your opponent.

### What Doesn't Kill You Makes You Stronger


Another one of my favorite cards from *Mirrodin* block is [Rite of Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Passage). It turns all your creatures into [Fungusaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungusaur)s, giving them a +1/+1 counter whenever they take damage. *Fifth Dawn* also has [Mephidross Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire), which gives your creatures a +1/+1 counter whenever they deal damage to a creature. With both of these out, if your creature can deal damage to itself, it will get two +1/+1 counters. [Power of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire) seems like the easiest way to make this happen, allowing your creature to tap to deal 1 damage to itself. [Viridian Longbow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow) and [Arcane Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Teachings) serve as backups. Although they cost a bit more mana, each has its own advantages. [Arcane Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Teachings) makes your creature a bit harder to kill, and [Viridian Longbow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow) will stick around if your opponent does manage to kill your creature.

| --- | --- |

So, we have the ability to make a creature tap to give itself one or more +1/+1 counters. Now to find a way to abuse that. The first creature that popped into my head is [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid). It allows you to untap it if you put a -1/-1 counter on it. Since the -1/-1 counter and the +1/+1 counter cancel each other out, you'll be right back where you started. If [Rite of Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Passage) is giving the counters, you can't accomplish anything more than giving it one counter per turn, however. If you're using [Mephidross Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire), on the other hand, you can at least kill off all of your opponent's creatures, and if you have two copies in any combination of the two, you can put an infinite number of +1/+1 counters on [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), allowing you to untap it infinite times and deal infinite damage with [Power of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire). However, note that the Druid will have a bunch of damage marked on it from hitting itself with [Power of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire), so you'll need to wait until your opponent's upkeep to start putting -1/-1 counters on it.

Since we're already playing black, I've also included [Cinderhaze Wretch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cinderhaze+Wretch). Although it costs more mana than [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), it does combo off the same way. [Kodama's Reach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) and [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) help you get the mana you need faster, and [Flame Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Slash) is an easy way to kill most creatures without using a lot of mana.

### A Painful Death


Our challengers have entered the arena, and they're itching to start dealing some damage. The time has come to see who is top dog, so grab a seat and let's watch the carnage unfold.


### Game 1


Rite of Fire started things off with a land, and Shufflin' Time cast a turn-one [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions). Rite of Fire cast [Viridian Longbow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow) and Shufflin' Time simply played a land and passed the turn. Rite of Fire passed back with no play, and Shufflin' Time used [Fabricate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) to search up a [Salvaging Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Salvaging+Station). Rite of Fire played a land and cast [Rite of Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Passage), and Shufflin' Time merely passed the turn.

Shufflin' Time drew and played a land, and [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) revealed the final combo piece on the top of the library. Rite of Fire had only a [Kodama's Reach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) on top, and conceded.


### Game 2


Rite of Fire started thing off with [Savage Lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands), and Shufflin' Time cast [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight). Rite of Fire played a land and passed, and Shufflin' Time played a second Lantern. Rite of Fire cast [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid), and Shufflin' Time cast a [Thousand-Year Elixir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thousand-Year+Elixir). [Rite of Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Passage) hit the board, and Shufflin' Time cast [Psychogenic Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychogenic+Probe), sacrificing a Lantern to deal 2 damage and shuffle away [Mephidross Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire).

### Game 3


Shufflin' Time started off with [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions), and Rite of Fire suspended [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow). Shufflin' Time cast [Psychogenic Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychogenic+Probe), and Rite of Fire cast [Viridian Longbow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow). A [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) revealed the top cards, and [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) searched up a Forest. A [Mephidross Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire) was on top after the shuffle, and Shufflin' Time sacrificed [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) to shuffle it away and deal 2 damage.
