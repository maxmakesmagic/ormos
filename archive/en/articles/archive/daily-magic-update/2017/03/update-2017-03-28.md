Fair warning from the Egyptologist herself, because—wait, I'm getting ahead of myself. *Magic* has a wide and diverse fan base, so *of course* we have an Egyptologist among our fans. [@Rudjedet](https://twitter.com/Rudjedet) (Sonja Greer) has been sharing some initial thoughts on *Amonkhet* (and there will likely be more), but that isn't what her account is all about. But, hey, if you really like Egypt, then you're in for a treat following her! Still, some of her insights on *Amonkhet* have been fun to read so far. *Amonkhet* isn't "Egypt, but on *Magic* cards," but more along the lines of a *Magic* twist on Egyptian themes. So, for example, there are no Dragons in Egypt (obviously), but Greer sees [making the Dragons *Magic* has in the set appear crocodilian as a big plus](https://twitter.com/Rudjedet/status/846406760806187008).


Anyway, it's interesting stuff if you want to see the connections between ancient Egypt and *Amonkhet*.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Blue-Red Colossus](http://www.masterofmagics.co.uk/decks/standard-decks/pptq-patriot-games-sheffield-top-8/)

It takes a brave person to play an artifact-based deck in a world where [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran) puts a hard target on all artifacts, but Richard Mellors did just that at a six-round PPTQ where he made the Top 8 with Blue-Red Colossus, a deck that has had spurts of popularity, but never really broken through.


The deck utilizes a ton of artifacts and cost-reducers to quickly power out a [Metalwork Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalwork+Colossus) and, well, that's the main game plan. Giant 11/11s are pretty much a plan on their own.


But the deck utilizes several backup plans. [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) takes advantage of all the extra mana and random [Glint-Nest Crane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Nest+Crane)s sitting around. [Skysovereign, Consul Flagship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skysovereign%2C+Consul+Flagship) serves as removal as well as supplying nearly half the cost of a [Metalwork Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalwork+Colossus)—and often being crewed by a newly cast one. Heck, even [Cultivator's Caravan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivator%27s+Caravan) gets in on some attacking action.


Note the red removal in the main deck, which keeps Vehicles in check long enough to bust out and can disrupt Copy Cat long enough to attack with giant machines.
