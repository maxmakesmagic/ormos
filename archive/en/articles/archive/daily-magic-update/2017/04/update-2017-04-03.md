Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Gregory Orange's White-Blue Control](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsa17/top-4-decklists-2017-04-02)

With nods to the Affinity and Jund decks played by his teammates on the winning Grand Prix San Antonio team, Gregory Orange's White-Blue Control was the "breakout" deck of the weekend. Versions of it were played on a number of top-performing teams, and it saw a good deal of camera time this weekend.


I use the quotes because it's hard to be a breakout deck in Team Unified formats, as the constraints of the format often push decks in directions they may not otherwise go. For example, note that the winning configuration covered all five colors separately (white-blue and black-red-green) plus artifacts (Affinity), so there was no chance of overlap.


But there was some interesting technology that definitely will see some play moving forward. [Condemn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn) is interesting against [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) decks, where it can be used to remove another creature while incidentally shrinking, or even killing, a [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow). In fact, as was pointed out on stream, a single [Condemn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn) gets rid of two attacking [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s, no matter what.
