TOP 8 COVERAGE



Just two weeks ago, Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa carried his Esper Dragons deck to a Top 8 finish at Grand Prix Krakow. The trophy eluded him there, but it made him hungry for the win. This weekend, he once again registered his trusty control deck and, after 15 rounds of Swiss, found himself in the Top 8 once again. But this time, there was no stopping him. In the quarters, he bested Ricardo Nunes Martins in an Esper Dragons mirror match. Then, he dismantled Sergio Barrientos’ Green Devotion deck in the semifinals. And finally, he triumphed over Maximiliano Ramiro Sanchez’s Red/Green Dragons deck and laid claim to the second Grand Prix title of his triumphant career.


Hall of Famer Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa was playing for more just his second Grand Prix title though. With Thiago Saporito and Willy Edel both picking up three Pro Points this weekend, they were bumped up to 42 and 40 respectively. The 8 Pro Points from his victory puts Damo da Rosa at 43, catapulting him to become the new point leader of Brazil. More importantly, he is reveling in the fact that he had just locked up Platinum this season and the next.


But let's go back to the beginning. Earlier on Saturday morning, a huge crowd of 1,297 players arrived in Sao Paulo with their Standard decks, making this the largest Latin American Grand Prix to date. We've had a great weekend all around, with a large variety of archetypes running around the top tables. Abzan Aggro, Red Aggro, and Esper Dragons were the three most popular decks, although Deathmist Raptor decks were explored by a lot of players as well.


In the end, though, it seemed that Esper Dragons still had what it takes, at least with PV holding the cards. Congratulations again to Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa, champion of Grand Prix Sao Paulo 2015!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Welcome to Day 2 of Grand Prix São Paulo 2015!


Yesterday, 1,297 players entered to show off their skills in Standard, making this the largest Latin American Grand Prix all-time. We saw all kinds of cards competing at the top tables, from Deathmist Raptor / Den Protector to Foundry Street Denizen, Dragonlord Ojutai, Dromoka’s Command, Stubborn Denial, Ensoul Artifact, and so on.


At the start of the Sunday competition, 133 players had unlocked the 21 points required to advance, and four players are at the top of the standings with undefeated records: Julio Cesar Maciel (9-0, Bant Heroic), Ricardo Felipe Camargo (9-0, Temur Aggro), Renato B Wohlers (8-0-1, Esper Dragons), and Fabio Barroso (8-0-1, Abzan Control). These four have a head start today, but the competition isn't far behind and includes such notable names as Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa, who piloted his trusty Esper Dragons deck to an 8-1 record.


Today, all Day 2 players get to compete for the title of Grand Prix São Paulo Champion. Join us here for more text coverage as we see who can successfully navigate through six more rounds of Standard to make it to the Top 8!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



After nine rounds of Standard play, Day One is done. Earlier this morning, 1,297 players arrived to battle with their best Standard decks, making this the largest Latin American Grand Prix ever to be held. After nine rounds of Swiss, 135 players have unlocked the 21 points required for Day 2.


We’ve seen all kinds of interesting cards and decks competing today. We've seen the Deathmist Raptor / Den Protector engine grind out Esper Dragon players all day long, we've seen the continued popularity of Red Aggro, and we've seen the rise of Temur Aggro and Bant Heroic to the top of the standings.


At the end of the first day, only four players remained undefeated: Julio Cesar Maciel (9-0), Ricardo Felipe Camargo (9-0), Renato B Wohlers (8-0-1), and Fabio Barroso (8-0-1). These four have a headstart over their competition today, but the competition isn't far behind and includes such notable names as Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa, who piloted his trusty Esper Dragons deck to an 8-1 record.
