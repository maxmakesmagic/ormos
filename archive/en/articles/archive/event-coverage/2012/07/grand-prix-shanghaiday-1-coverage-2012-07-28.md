Round 4 Feature Match - Ken Yukihiro vs. Bin Jia
------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
Ken Yukuhiro arrives in Shanghai fresh off a Top 4 finish at Pro Tour X, and has two GP Top 8's to his name. Bin Jia is a well-known player from Beijing who has made the Top 8 of Chinese Nationals 3 times.


### Game 1


Yukuhiro summoned a turn 1 [Chronomaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton) , while Jia had a [Dragon Hatchling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Hatchling) to start the game off. A [Servant of Nefarox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+Nefarox) from Jia let the Hatchling sneak in for a nibble. Yukuhiro, unfazed, kept growing his [Chronomaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton) . The Servant attacked and Yukuhiro declined to block, Jia following with [Canyon Minotaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Minotaur) . Yukuhiro determinedly kept growing his [Chronomaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton) but otherwise had no plays.


Another Dragon hit the board for Jia, this time a Whelp. Jia attacked with both his fliers, and Yukuhiro [Murder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) ed the Whelp. On his turn, he summoned a [Duty-Bound Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duty-Bound+Dead) and attacked with his [Chronomaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton) , knocking Jia to 13 in one blow.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/GPShanghaiR4-Feature-Jia.jpg)

*From little things, big things grow, much to Jia's discomfort.* 
Jia piled on the pressure with a [Harbor Bandit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Bandit) s and a [Giant Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Scorpion) to hold the growing threat at bay. Yukuhiro mustered a [Zombie Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath) and kept regenerating his [Duty-Bound Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duty-Bound+Dead) . A [Cower in Fear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cower+in+Fear) knocked Jia's army back down to size, but he calmly rebuilt with a [Fire Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental) and a [Disentomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) for his Scorpion. Yukuhiro attacked with his Goliath to take out the Scorpion, and had a post-combat [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) to get back a [Murder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) .


[Mark of the Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+the+Vampire) on the [Chronomaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton) meant that Yukuhiro was able to gain 11 points of life in one swing, tilting the board firmly in his favour. A double [Murder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) from Yukuhiro cleared the way for the final swing, and the Little Robot That Could trundled in to take the first game.


Ken Yukuhiro 1 – Bin Jia 0


### Game 2


A [Harbor Bandit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Bandit) s from Yukuhiro was met by [Canyon Minotaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Minotaur) from Jia. Jia followed with a [Mindclaw Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindclaw+Shaman) , who revealed a hand of [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) , [Disentomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) , [Redirect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Redirect) , and two [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) s. Jia opted to play the [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) , which Yukuhiro [Redirect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Redirect) ed to himself.


An [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) got back the [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) , and [Duty-Bound Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duty-Bound+Dead) hit the battlefield to clog up the ground. Jia had a [Flames of the Firebrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flames+of+the+Firebrand) to deal with the [Harbor Bandit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Bandit) s, and then both his creatures rumbled in. Yukuhiro, on the defensive, elected to block with his [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) .



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha12/GPShanghai-R4-Feature-Yukuhiro.jpg)

*Yukuhiro Archaeomancipates his graveyard* 
Jia summoned a [Harbor Bandit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Bandit) s of his own. A [Divination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) and a [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) later, Yukuhiro's hand was full but his board was looking a little empty, with only the [Duty-Bound Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duty-Bound+Dead) staring down Jia's team. *Sui Xin* 
"My deck is all trash!"


Taking a quick glance at his card pool, it was not hard to see why Sui Xin had such an exclamation. Firstly, I saw him flip the top card of his deck away (a foil [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) ), which meant that he had one less card to work with.


Then he showed me his assortment of rares that aren't particularly impactful. Spearheading the list of lackluster rares was [Door to Nothingness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+to+Nothingness) , [Sunpetal Grove](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunpetal+Grove) , [Cathedral of War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+of+War) , [Sands of Delirium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sands+of+Delirium) , and [Slumbering Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slumbering+Dragon) .


Sui started to sift through his cards and was quick to set aside his ten white cards (awfully disproportionate to the standard deck size of 84) and green was also next to go.


It seemed like he had a bunch of solid black cards, including Xanthrid Gorgon and a pair of [Rise from the Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+from+the+Grave) to ensure "Medusa's" eternal existence. He had two of each of Duty-bound Skeleton and [Servant of Nefarox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+Nefarox) (as well as [Duskmantle Prowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Prowler) ), which meant that he had no problem attacking if he needed to. While it was obvious that he had solidified himself in black, he seemed to face some difficulty while trying to find a secondary (or tertiary) support color.



![Xathrid Gorgon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Xathrid+Gorgon)

Trying to utilize his myriad of exalted creatures, Sui turned to blue to try to maximize a duo of [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Scroll%2BThief), and filling out the rest of the slots with [Tricks of the Trade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tricks+of+the+Trade) , [Talrand's Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%27s+Invocation) , [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) and [Watercourser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watercourser) .


With only approximately twenty black, blue and artifact cards, Sui had to find a third color to fill out the rest of his deck. He had the [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) everyone looks out for and was able to comfortably splash double [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) and [Flames of the Firebrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flames+of+the+Firebrand) . [Cathedral of War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+of+War) would have to sit in the sideboard, to minimize mana complications.


All in all, he says that he has a great deck, and has potential to go undefeated, although a score of 8-1 would not disappoint. He commented that the sideboard plan is quite important in this format and has an assortment of cards which can get him out out any situation.


His plan against a slow control deck involves [Tormented Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Soul) and [Dark Favor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Favor) and possibly [Harbor Serpent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Serpent) . Against [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) s, he has [Volcanic Strength](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength) as an improved [Tricks of the Trade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tricks+of+the+Trade) . Against weenies and tokens, he has the option of bringing in [Chandra's Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Fury) and [Krenko's Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command) to stave off the swarm. All in all, rather well-thought! For your viewing pleasure, here is Sui's entire card pool and his decklist. Would you have done it differently? =)
