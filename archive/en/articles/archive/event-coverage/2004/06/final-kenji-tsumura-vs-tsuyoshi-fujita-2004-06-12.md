Final: Kenji Tsumura vs. Tsuyoshi Fujita
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 12, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TobyWachter.jpg)
By Toby Wachter











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat04/fin1_1.jpg)Despite appearances by a handful of surprising decks in the Top 8, the tournament has appropriately come down to Goblins vs. Affinity. However, Fujita's Goblin deck is no mere one-dimensional aggressive creation. By splashing green, Fujita has been able to combine the aggressive nature of Goblins with the disruption of efficient artifact removal. Fujita has had a great deal of success with Goblins in major events, including a win at Grand Prix Bangkok where he popularized Goblin Bidding. With the Japanese National Championship on the line, there's probably no other deck he'd rather be playing. ### Game 1


Both players mulliganed their opening hands. Fujita started with a Prospector, and Tsumura played [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and a Ravager, which was quickly killed with [Electrostatic Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Bolt). Fujita missed his second land drop, and Tsumura began to pull ahead with [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast), then put [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) on his [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite). It got through for three damage, and Fujita dealt one with the Prospector, still lacking a second land. Tsumura played a Worker, Enforcer and Disciple, and Fujita Bolted the Enforcer. He finally found a second land, but it was too late- a second Disciple came out, and the game was over.


Tsumura- 1 Fujita- 0


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat04/fin1_2.jpg)Fujita had a second turn Sparksmith, and Tsumura's start merely had two lands and a [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar). [Viridian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) removed the Jar, and Tsumura cast [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) to clear the board before playing [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker). On the following turn he made a [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer), which died to [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize). Fujita untapped, and destroyed the Worker with a Shaman. A [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) went on the Shaman, and it attacked for three. Tsumura's [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) kept the Shaman back, until Fujita played and attacked with a Clickslither. Tsumura played a [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar), and Fujita killed the [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) with a Bolt in response. Fujita played a second [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp), and when he tried to give it to the Clickslither, Tsumura responded with [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast). The Shaman attacked for three, and the other [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) went on it to put Fujita far ahead. Goblin Sledder on its own merited a [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), and Fujita came back with Goblin Goon. A turn later, Tsumura conceded.


Tsumura- 1 Fujita- 1


### Game 3


Tsumura mulliganed down to four, as none of his hands were good enough to keep. He managed to do okay for a four land hand, playing a Disciple followed by a Worker. Fujita Bolted the Worker and played a Sledder, then a Sparksmith on the following turn. It blocked an attacking Disciple, and the Sledder was sacrificed to help it survive combat. [Viridian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) got rid of Tsumura's [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar), and Fujita added a Sledder to his side. Tsumura played a [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) three turns in a row, and each time they were removed easily. Once Fujita played a [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp), Tsumura conceded.


Tsumura- 1 Fujita- 2


### Game 4


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat04/fin1_3.jpg)Tsumura cycled through a [Chromatic Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere) to play a Worker, and missed his second land drop. Fujita had Goblin Sledder and a Sparksmith, but Tsumura wasn't out of the game despite his bad mana draw. He played [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) and a [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp), followed by a free [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite). [Oxidize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize) destroyed the [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp), and Fujita played one of his own. [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) was sacrificed to save the [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) from a Sparksmith shot, and Tsumura played a second Worker. With only one land though, Tsumura was in no position to put up a real fight. The Sparksmith took down creatures as the [Skullclamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) provided more cards. Thanks to [Viridian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) destroying Tsumura's only land, Tsumura was left with no permanents. When Fujita played a few more threats, Tsumura conceded to make Tsuyoshi Fujita the Japanese National Champion.


Tsumura- 1 Fujita- 3
