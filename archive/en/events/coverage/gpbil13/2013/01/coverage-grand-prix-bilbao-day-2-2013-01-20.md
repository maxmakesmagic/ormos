Sunday, 9:35 a.m. – Day 2 Metagame Breakdown
--------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby*

 

With the field shortening from a fairly hefty number close to a thousand to a much more manageable 131, I have had a fun round or so of reading every decklist left in the tournament. Let's see how they shake out.





| Jund | 15% |
| Spirit Jund | 11% |
| Kiki-Pod | 8% |
| Splinter Twin | 8% |
| UWR Flash | 5% |
| Boom Bust Zoo | 5% |
| Burn (with Bump in the Night) | 5% |
| Past in Flames | 5% |
| Scapeshift | 5% |
| Zoo | 5% |
| Infect | 4% |
| Affinity | 3% |
| Auras | 3% |
| Gifts Ungiven | 3% |
| Red/Green Tron | 2% |
| Boom Bust Jund | 2% |
| Doran/Junk | 2% |
| Eggs | 2% |
| GW Aggro | 2% |
| Living End | 2% |
| UW Midrange | 2% |
| BW Tokens | 1% |
| Esper Control | 1% |
| Mystical Teachings | 1% |
| Red deck wins | 1% |
| UR Tron | 1% |
| UW Tron | 1% |


 

So Jund is a fairly hefty part of the metagame, but we can see it is far from the only player. Once you factor together the different versions of Zoo, they come second, and it's worth potentially looking at Boom/Bust decks as being an archetype just as we do Jund or Zoo perhaps. For control and combo players there are plenty of options, and it will be instructive to see how these 131 decks turn into eight as we fast approach the knockout stages of the tournament here in Bilbao.

Round 10 Feature Match – Elias Watsfeldt vs. Bernd Brendemuhl
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby*

 

Coming in to day two of Grand Prix Bilbao the field is now quite a bit smaller, with just a little over 10% of players still in contention for top eight. For the first round of Sunday's play, we have two players who just snuck in with a 7-2 record yesterday, but who have the calibre of results in the past to suggest that they can still make a run at the top eight. Elias Watsfeldt of Sweden has just come off a second place at Grand Prix Lisbon, and is now here in Spain playing red/blue Storm, the Modern combo deck favoured by the likes of Jon Finkel, which can sometimes simply ignore an opponent's plan and race. His opponent, Bernd Brendemuhl of Germany, led his country at the World **Magic** cup and is also a regular on the top tables of the European Grand Prix circuit.


Bernd won the roll, and was quick to show what he was up to, with a turn one [Glistener Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf). Here was a combo deck that might be able to race the storm combo of the young Swede. Elias, before even playing a land fired off a [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) for the cost of two life. He saw two copies of [Groundswell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundswell), [Ichorclaw Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr), [Overgrown Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb) and [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay). This game would not be a good one for him...


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/Round-10-Elias-Watsfeldt.jpg)*Elias Watsfeldt*
Two more copies of [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) followed, and a [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour). Bernd, somewhat predictably, used his two copies of [Groundswell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundswell) the following turn to make [Glistener Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) a 9/9, putting Watsfeldt just a single poison counter away from death.


Watsfeldt had little to fear from taking pain from using fetchlands for the likes of an untapped [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents), but everything to fear from Brendemuhl's creatures. He cast a [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) to kill off the [Glistener Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf), buying at least a turn.


Brendemuhl reloaded with an [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) and [Ichorclaw Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr). Either one would be lethal, giving Watsfeldt pause for thought. He cast a [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song), followed by [Manamorphose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose). [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) dug him a little deeper as his storm combo deck tried to go off on turn three. [Pyretic Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual) further built Watsfeldt's mana, following which he cast [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), [Pyretic Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual) and [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour). He had three mana and an active [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), but was unable to get any further with his combo. Facing the final point of poison the following turn, Watsfeldt picked up his cards. It was on to game two.


Bernd Brendemuhl 1 – 0 Elias Watsfeldt


A turn one [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) from Watsfeldt showed him that he might have a tiny bit more time for the second game than the first. With [Phyrexian Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader), [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) as Brendemuhl's main action, the German's poison plan was a little more controlling now that it was on the draw.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/Round-10-Bernd-Brendemuhl.jpg)*Bernd Brendemuhl*
Brendemuhl's [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) saw [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension), [Manamorphose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose), [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents), [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and two copies of [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song). He took the [Manamorphose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose), as the one card drawing spell in Watsfeldt's hand. [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) came down on turn two for Watsfeldt, while Brendemuhl had an [Ichorclaw Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr) who soon fell to a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


[Phyrexian Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader) was not about to fall to lightning. If Watsfeldt was to get this infect creature off the board, he'd need a blue spell. Watsfeldt was content to bide his time, and lost one of three copies of [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) in hand to [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), with no way to combo out. Brendemuhl, meanwhile, had his combo all ready. Knowing that he was safe to do so, Bernd cast first [Might of Old Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Old+Krosa), and then [Vines of Vastwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood) with kicker on his 2/2, attacking in for 10 poison in one noxious hit.


**Bernd Brendemuhl wins 2-0, advancing to 8-2 in the tournament.**

Round 11 Feature Match – Valentin Mackl vs. Thomas Holzinger
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Valentin Mackl is an up-and-coming player from Austria with a number of Grand Prix day two finishes to his name. A little more than a year ago, the same could have been said for fellow Austrian Thomas Holzinger. Of course, he did go on to finish in the Top 8 of Pro Tour Avacyn's Restored and is now one of the elite few in the highest level of the Pro Players Club. Before the game the two friends lamented the unfairness of having to play each other. In all likelihood, one would now kick the other out of Top 8 contention.


### Game 1


Mackl won the die-roll and started on a tapped [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground), while Holzinger had [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Glistener Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf). The Elf died to [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), but Holzinger had [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) to keep up the threat of sudden death by poison.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/fm11_mackl.jpg)*Valentin Mackl*
Mackl took a [Groundswell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundswell) out of Holzinger's hand via [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), leaving him with [Might of Old Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Old+Krosa) as his only spell. Holzinger did keep a second [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus), though, and that came down just in time to replace the first which died to a second [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).

Selesnya, right?" Well wrong, at least that last bit.

"We're all looking forward to playing with Boros Charm," said Royde to general nods of agreement. "That card is amazing."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/blog_royde-oberg-watsfeldt.jpg)*Left to right: Daniel Royde, Kenny Öberg, Elias Watsfeldt (with Matej Zatlkaj on photobombing duties)*

Round 12 Feature Match – Tiago Chan vs. Jonas Kostler
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby*

 

Invitational winner Tiago Chan of Portugal has had a surprisingly long Christmas break. He had intended to come to Portugal for Christmas, from China where he now lives. That trip got extended a little for Grand Prix Lisbon before Christmas, and once Tiago found out that there was a large contingent of Portuguese players hiring a bus to come to Bilbao, his trip ended up being a little longer still.


Playing blue/white/red flash, this is the first time that Chan has been able to play with the card that he designed for winning the **Magic** Invitational. That card, [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), has become something of a powerhouse in more or less every format, and he's been able to leverage it to some success thus far here in Bilbao. Chan's opponent, Jonas Kostler was not a big player on the tournament scene when Chan was at the height of his success, but has since become one of the regular faces seen at the top tables here in Europe on the Grand Prix circuit.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/Round-12-Tiago-Chan.jpg)*Tiago Chan*
Kostler won the roll, and led off with a [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), which was soon followed by [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite). Kostler's deck was [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), with a vast variety of creatures for every situation which could be searched up as necessary. The deck has received a shot in the arm from being able to include the [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) / [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) combo, which it can search up with surprising consistency, while also having the option of more directly attacking.


While Kostler had a [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens), Chan's deck was slow to start playing spells, simply developing its mana base, and keeping that mana up to stop any big threats from the German player. Kostler seemed happy to wait. He had another [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) and another [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), but wasn't about to start throwing down combo pieces. At end of turn, Tiago used [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze), split between [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) and [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), such that Kostler couldn't redirect damage away from his mana creature. Chan then passed the turn, holding up mana ready for a [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) on [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod).



![Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Celestial+Colonnade)

Chan used [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) to dig a little for action, but was content to keep up mana in Kostler's turn to stop whatever was coming next. Kostler cast [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) a second time, and lost it to [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). This did leave a window for Kostler to resolve a [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), but life totals even after the angel got its first attack in were even at 13 apiece, thanks to damage Kostler had taken from fetchlands and phyrexian mana.


Chan played an end of turn [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) of his own. He used [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) targeting Kostler's angel, and the German sank into thought, working out if now was the time for his [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) to jump under a bus or not. In the end, he decided it was, letting the 0/4 be exiled.


On Chan's turn, he animated a [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade), and attacked in the air with it and the [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). A [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) jumped in the way of the Colonnade, and Kostler dropped to 10 life. Half way there.


Now though, there was a window of opportunity for Kostler. He cast a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and used it to turn [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) into a [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks). The Finks, with persist, would be an ideal creature to sacrifice to [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) in future turns, as well as a nice source of some extra life. Chan needed to be very careful. Removal would break up the [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) combo, but with an active [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), there was plenty more than simply that which Kostler could do.


Chan animated his [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade), only to see it tapped down by [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch). Could this be it? Chan elected not to attack at all, and could only look on as Kostler turned [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) into Glen-Elendra Archmage. He then simply cast [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker), and was able to protect it from countermagic with the Archmage.


Jonas Kostler 1 – 0 Tiago Chan



![Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Birds+of+Paradise)

The players shuffled and sideboarded carefully for the second game. While Chan was on the play, it was unsurprising that Kostler had the first play of the game, in [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). Chan's deck is more reactive than Kostler's, and he was quite happy to begin taking the passive role. A turn two [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) was joined by a second [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), putting Kostler well ahead in the mana development race in spite of being on the draw.


Those mana creatures would, it seemed, be pivotal to Kostler's game, as he missed a third land drop. Chan wasn't able to punish with an [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) though, casting just a [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) for his turn, and looking on as Kostler resolved an end of turn [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). The best Chan could manage was a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) on [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), but he was still taking his hits in the air from the Angel.


An [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) came from Kostler, who at this point would likely have preferred a more straightforward land as a mana source. Chan cast a [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) for his turn. He lost his [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) to [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and Jonas suddenly had an opportunity to go off. Chan was entirely tapped out, and Kostler resolved a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), which he used to find a [Harmonic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonic+Sliver) to remove [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull).


That [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) now being on the board, Chan was in a position where he had to hope his aggressive plan with [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) would be good enough. He attached Kostler to nine life, and passed hoping not to face anything too damning. He took an attack which put him to seven, and saw [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) upgraded into [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons) by Kostler. The 5/5 hexproof flyer would be great insurance for Kostler against whatever Chan had in mind, and was one of the few cards in his 75 to be able to profitably withstand a [Celestial Colonnade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) offensive.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/Round-12-Jonas-Kostler.jpg)*Jonas Kostler*
Kostler went straight to his combat phase for the turn, and rumbled in with Sigarda, [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and [Harmonic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonic+Sliver). On just six life, Chan had to do something, and animating Celstial Colonnade was his first choice. [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) forced the Portuguese player to look for other options to stay alive. He had a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) for [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), but when he tried another on [Harmonic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonic+Sliver), a [Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) from Kostler was enough to keep exactly six damage coming in.


Jonas Kostler wins 2-0

Both players entered the fray this round with scores of 10-2, still in contention for Top 8 but probably unable to afford any more losses.

Dictus opened on [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) followed by [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), taking [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) out of Riffert's hand. Riffert had a replacement 'Goyf, but that was sent on a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). His [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) stuck, however, and Dictus's [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) didn't but was sacrificed to [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/fm13_dictus.jpg)*Mark Dictus*
When Dictus's [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) took out the [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), the game entered a phase without much action. The board was completely empty except for lands and Riffert's [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), a painfully slowly ticking time bomb. Dictus hit land after land, while Riffert was still on three lands and discarded spell after spell to add counters to Liliana.


In the end, Liliana didn't quite get there though. At one point Dictus found [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession) and Riffert found a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), now 6/7. The tokens didn't stand much of a chance, and soon neither did Dictus.


Mark Dictus 0-1 Till Riffert


### Game 2


Dictus started the game at a severe disadvantage, with two mulligans. "Usually Jund is a good match-up for me. I'm 6-1 against Jund so far," said Dictus, "but not this time, it seems."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbil13/fm13_riffert.jpg)*Till Riffert*
But at first it appeared to be Riffert's deck which malfunctioned. Stranded on two lands, Riffert soon found himself on the wrong end of a serious beating by various tokens from [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and [Raise the Alarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm), despite two [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse)s and a [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) in his hand. At least a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) provided a veritable roadblock for the pedestrian forces.


The Charm and one Pulse were discarded to Dictus's [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) before Riffert got his third land, but the second Malestrom Pulse got rid of all flying tokens. The fortune had reversed itself. Again, Dictus was left with too many lands, too little action, not to mention still suffering from the double mulligan, whereas the Jund deck was firing on all cylinders. [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) ... Riffert had it all, with [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s to boot, and it didn't take him long to turn the turnaround into a round win.


Mark Dictus 0-2 Till Riffert
