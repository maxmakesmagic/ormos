Feature: The Rogues of Day 2
============================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 29, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_113.png)
By Noah Weil











Much has been said about how wide open the format is. Completely true, yet despite how many viable options there are, the Day Two decks seemed to fall into a number of camps. [Dredge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dredge)atog, Burn/Jank, No Stick, and so on. They're all viable, all rather common.


Some players, however, decided to walk the road less traveled and come up with concoctions of their own. A *very* retooled Affinity deck, [Stormscape Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormscape+Apprentice)s, and the wildest Bidding deck ever marks the most unusual decks that played Day Two.

This version of Affinity eschews Dark Confidant for some reason. More oddly, it comes *sans* [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) as well. What it gives up in speed, it makes up for raw power and gigantic card-advantage engines. [Future Sight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight) loves to flip up [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite)s and [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer)s, ending in a huge [Temporal Fissure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Fissure).


*Fun Fact*: The average mana cost of the cards in this deck is 4.7. Who says Extended is fast?

I don't know who Pedro is, but his deck is *nuts*. Four colors can go aggro, combo, and control. Playing against Goblins? Ascetic + [Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship). No Stick? Grab a [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) with [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage), than pick it up with your Ninjas, and do it again! Has the ground gotten gummed up? Go to the nugget with [Stormscape Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormscape+Apprentice)'s black ability. It works in Invasion sealed, and it works in LA.


*Fun Fact:* This is the only deck at the Pro Tour that casts [Kataki, War's Wage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kataki%2C+War%27s+Wage) off of [Ancient Den](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Den).

Greg Weiss piloted this surprisingly resilient deck to Day Two. While Bidding + fatties has been around since *Onslaught*, *Ravnica*'s Flame-Kin Zealot was the card to allow both Elementals as the creature type of choice and a much, much faster win. [Twilight's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twilight%27s+Call) was the MVP that allowed Greg to win under [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) with [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant). Apparently, nobody plays the kicker, which allows Greg to use Boseiju to play the game-winning eight-mana instant version of [Twilight's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twilight%27s+Call). While this was a very solid choice going into the tournament, its growing publicity among competitors made it harder and harder for Mr. Weiss to sneak out wins. Still a very powerful deck.


*Fun Fact*: This was the only deck at the Pro Tour that could deal 20 damage on turn 2.
