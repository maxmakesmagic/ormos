Back in Mono-Black
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 6, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











For literally years, the idea of Mono-Black Control has been one of the most popular archetypes, both in casual and competitive circles. Some trace the archetype back to [Cabal Coffers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Coffers) and [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate), but in reality, ever since the dawn of **Magic** and the printing of [Drain Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drain+Life), the idea of playing nothing but [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s has been beloved. Generally, the archetype falls short in the tournament-success department, but there is hope!


Last weekend, Phillip Johnson took Mono-Black Control all the way to a Top 8 finish at the StarCityGames Open in Seattle. The deck is packed to the brim with many of the same things that have always been associated with Mono-Black: mana acceleration, removal to the brim, and beefy finishers—ranging all the way up to good old [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand)—cast the hard way. Phillip put many underplayed cards to use, such as [Increasing Ambition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Ambition), which was often thought of to be too expensive. Then again, with three copies of [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) in his deck, a [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), and four additional six-drops, Phillip clearly thinks five is more than acceptable!


The fact that this list was able to put up a performance in the current metagame is especially inspiring due to the information we have from [***Magic** 2013*](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/article.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/magic2013/cig#). [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) is being reprinted and the four-casting-cost sweeper has always been crucial to the archetype, whether that be [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) or the original [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate). And since you are playing with all of those [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s anyway, why not try out the newest of Lilianas: Liliana of the Dark Realms! This will sure to be a smash at Friday Night **Magic** around the world!
