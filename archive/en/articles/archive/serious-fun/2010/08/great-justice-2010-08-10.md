For Great Justice!
==================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on August 10, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






Last week was Feedback Week where, in case you missed it, Mark Rosewater [detailed](/en/articles/archive/feedback-basics-2010-08-02) just about every way to get in touch with those who make the game awesome and explore amazing parts of the community of **Magic** at large. I seriously recommend checking it out.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf103_stormtideLetter.jpg)  
However, this week is the week after Feedback Week—and feedback from you I have! [Setting it up two weeks ago](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/construct-deconstruct-2010-07-27), I asked you to help steer the direction of a [Standard](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=judge/resources/sfrstandard) deck and a general [multiplayer](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/two-opponents-one-deck-2010-07-20) deck for my upcoming trip to [U.S. Nationals](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/nationals/unitedstates). And before I forget, thank you to everyone who responded and voted.

The end result is a bit mixed so here, first, is the good news.

I'll admit: I've been spoiled by the **Magic** of yesteryear. When I think of a one-blue-mana card draw and filtering spell I think of [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm). When I think of one that costs four mana, [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) comes to mind.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf103_better.jpg)  
We're getting ahead of ourselves though because first and foremost we have an important decision to make: will this deck be a [Quest for Ula's Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+Ula%27s+Temple) deck or not?

### A Temple on Top

I like [Quest for Ula's Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+Ula%27s+Temple). It has a few neat features that landed it in another reader-submitted deck (for Noel deCordova's [From the Lab](/en/articles/archive/lab/adventureland-2010-03-11)):


> 
> * You get to see what's on top of your library during your upkeep, whether you decide to reveal it or not (if it's a creature)
> * Once active you get to put a creature into play for free at the end of *every* turn, not just yours
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf103_temple.jpg)  
If you've read some of what [The Ferrett](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=The%20Ferrett) and [Anthony Alongi](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Anthony%20Alongi) provided back through the years you'll agree that both of those features are pretty attractive for multiplayer games. Knowing what's coming up is great if you have ways to change that up, with cards like [Crystal Ball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Ball) or [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn)—or take advantage of it, like with the namesake quest itself. Moreover, free creatures are amazing in more ways than one since:


> * You can play other spells without worrying about how much our sea life costs
> * Leave mana open for instants like the aforementioned [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) and [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)
> * Pay other costs that can be crimping your style (like [Propaganda](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Propaganda))
> 

Getting that at the end of every turn is just gravy.

Of course, it isn't all glory and gigantic undersea creatures for nothing. Using the quest comes with some drawbacks:


> * Enchantments have a way of being a little [Disenchant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)-ing
> * It takes time, likely three more turns after playing it, to get up and running
> * Everyone is already wise to your crafty plan
> * You really want to see it as early as possible (i.e. it's not always reliable to lean on)
> 

![Quest for Ula's Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Quest+for+Ula%27s+Temple)  
With the mental gymnastics out of the way there is only one clear way to go: questing for the temple all the way, baby! Every deck I received about the [Leviathan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leviathan)s had [Quest for Ula's Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+Ula%27s+Temple) in it—and I'm confident to run full tilt with it.

While many of the submissions came with a full stock of four quests, I'm more a fan of finding it when I want it. [Enlightened Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) and [Idyllic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Idyllic+Tutor) are both ideal and can play into a subtheme of enchantments for multiplayer.

But first, the Krakens (and Serpents, [Leviathan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leviathan)s, and Octopuses)! A quick [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) search for everything eligible provides our awesome options:


> 
> * [Deep-Sea Kraken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep-Sea+Kraken) (great for games where players cast spells!)
> * [Inkwell Leviathan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkwell+Leviathan) (protects itself; Dennis included this too!)
> * [Simic Sky Swallower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Sky+Swallower) (also self-protective; flying and trample to boot!)
> * And those I already mentioned above
>

To turn the quest on we'll have to play more creatures than our titanic heavy-hitters. [Simic Sky Swallower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Sky+Swallower) would lean up towards some green, while [Wrexial, the Risen Deep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrexial%2C+the+Risen+Deep) would tilt up towards black. Since we're being greedy let's go with both:


> 
> * [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) (countering spells is handy)

The various tutors allow us to grab enchantments at will. While [Quest for Ula's Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quest+for+Ula%27s+Temple) will probably be the first thing we pick up, [Oversold Cemetery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversold+Cemetery) works well with our "spell creatures" like [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) and [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder). [Rhystic Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Study) and [Defense of the Heart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defense+of+the+Heart) serve as both game-accelerating options as well as flak that should draw fire away from our quest. Finally, just to shoehorn it in, [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) can grab back almost any of our enchantments as well as expired [Coiling Oracles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracles) or Elders.

Of course, this is just where I ended up—a far cry from the mono-blue deck we started with. Give me your thoughts and tell me what you think!

I love decks with variation, which is probably why I love singleton formats (like EDH) and often go with the "toolbox" route for decks. Of course, since I'm not looking to break into the competitive side of things I have the luxury of taking some liberties with what Robert had put together.

Clearly the plan, [according to Mike Flores](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/td/100), is to "start dumping [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s with the ability to re-buy them at any point for free cards" by grabbing something like [Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) which then gives you two small creatures to play. Pretty slick.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Vengevine | Ranger of Eos |

The toolbox part comes in with the variety of options to go grab: [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), [Thornling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornling), and [Sphinx of Jwar Isle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Jwar+Isle) are all featured. Everything else is a pretty standard fare of powerful planeswalkers and effects.

While Robert's plan was certainly a successful one, there are two other creatures that can be bought back on the cheap from the graveyard: [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and [Reassembling Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Bloodghast | Reassembling Skeleton |

Both of those cards don't mind if you play [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder), [Bloodthrone Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire), and [Mortician Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortician+Beetle). Throw in some copies of [Fling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling) and it's starting to feel like a complete deck.

Of course, [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) allows us to ditch a Skeleton or [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) to go grab specific creatures. [Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth), [Malakir Bloodwitch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malakir+Bloodwitch), [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) (featured in Robert's Sideboard), [Conquering Manticore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conquering+Manticore), and many different Titans are all options.

![Jinxed Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jinxed+Idol)  
One interesting deck idea that came in was a [Jinxed Idol](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jinxed+Idol) + [Leyline of Sanctity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) deck (Thanks Dustin!) With your Leyline out you can "give" the idol to your opponent who then can't give it back. Devious! However, since we're firmly without white (or blue for that matter) at this point the question becomes "Is the risk worth it?"

While it's 61 cards and uses [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), this is a decidedly different riff on a toolbox and graveyard get-back deck than Robert's. [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and [Reassembling Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton) both get dumped into the graveyard for a variety of uses:


> * [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) activations
> * [Bloodthrone Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire) pumping
> * [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder) trigger
> * [Bone Splinters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) additional cost
> 

For the toolbox, [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) is stocked with both [Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth) (great against any discard and nets some life gain when cast normally), and [Malakir Bloodwitch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malakir+Bloodwitch), who can block [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), and swing past her, all day.

[Sylvan Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger) and the [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) cousin ensure you're always getting a land for our [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s while [Mortician Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortician+Beetle) rides along for the fun sacrifice effects. The real kicker to all this is [Fling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling) which, thanks to the overload of creatures, will allow a [Bloodthrone Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire) to get big in a hurry—enough to hopefully finish the job.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf103_fling.jpg)  
The sideboard is a collection of useful changes:


> * [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) against decks that rely on their lands a lot
> * More Shaman targets with [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) being a great utility player
> * [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s and [Brindle Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brindle+Boar)s to help provide traction against decks that are a step faster
> * [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) is great not only against the [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s that I'm sure to see but also takes care of creatures with unearth
> 

I feel like I'm overlooking something but I'm confident this is a solid place to start.
