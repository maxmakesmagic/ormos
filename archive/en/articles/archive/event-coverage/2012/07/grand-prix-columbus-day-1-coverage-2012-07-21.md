**Saturday, 11:54 a.m. - Modern: One Month Later**
--------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*
Last month, *Avacyn Restored* made its impact on the Modern format loud and clear, as [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) put [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks on a whole new level of awesome. However, despite the fact that *four* players found their way into the Top 8 with variations of the different [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks, none of them were able to battle their way through their competition for win.


What ended up claiming the title at Grand Prix Yokohama was Jyun'ichi Miyajima, and his deck of choice for the event was White-Black Tokens. And his opponent in the finals, Kei Umahara, wasn't playing Pod either, but instead a mono-blue Faeries deck!


What do the results from last month's tournament mean for Grand Prix Columbus? Well, that's simple: the format may have some defined decks, but this tournament is still open for anyone! And with *M13* now part of the format, it's likely that things have changed up even more!


While there may be some wild new strategies being played here this weekend, it always helps to take a look at what was big in the previous events before diving into a weekend full of all-new Modern action. Let's start off by taking a look at the Pod variants.

The first deck is entirely based on the synergy between [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) and [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). If you haven't played the combo, it goes like this: you play a [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) at the end of the opponent's turn, then untap and cast Kiki-Jiki. When you activate Kiki-Jiki, targeting the [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), you get a token copy of the Angel, which then flickers Kiki-Jiki out. He comes back in, untapped, and you can use him to get another [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) token, which then flickers out Kiki-Jiki, etc etc. You get the idea.



![Restoration Angel++Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Restoration%20Angel&options=)
![Restoration Angel++Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Kiki-Jiki,%20Mirror%20Breaker&options=)

[Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) is the new [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), and provides a lot more utility that gives this Pod deck a number of ways to interact with the opponent. Heck, you can even use extra [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s *with* [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) to jump up to Kiki-Jiki!


The second deck, piloted by a number of players at the last Modern Grand Prix, combines the [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)/Kiki-Jiki combo with the other popular Pod combo: [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) and [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) combined with [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer). This combo kills opponents by sacrificing [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) a billion times which, thanks to Melira, doesn't come into play with -1/-1 counters, so its persist will continue to...uh, persist.



![Melira, Sylvok Outcast++Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Melira,%20Sylvok%20Outcast&options=)
![Melira, Sylvok Outcast++Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Murderous%20Redcap&options=)
![Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Viscera%20Seer&options=)

The strategy that meshes the two combos together, known as 5-Color Pod, looks to attack the opponent from two angles. One strategy uses two cards, but relies on four and five mana creatures, while the other strategy requires three cards but can be a lot easier to assemble out of nowhere. What results from this is multiple ways to threaten 0 to a million in the span of a turn, and it means opponents need to be on their toes when playing against this version of the Pod deck.


That said, the winning deck from GP Yokohama is sure to make waves. Are tokens going to take down Columbus?

This deck takes me back to the days of *Lorwyn*/*Shadowmoor* and *Shard of Alara* block Standard. Ahh, memories...



![Windbrisk Heights++Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Windbrisk%20Heights&options=)
![Windbrisk Heights++Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Lingering%20Souls&options=)

With tokens, the name of the game is beat downs. [Windbrisk Heights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights) can set the deck up for some free cards easily thanks to its large number of token generators. [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) even goes so far as to rival [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession) as the top-dog of token generators for white, and a package of disruption with [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) give the deck ways to combat some of the format's more frightening concoctions.


Combo decks and aggressive decks are prevalent in Modern, but what about control? Players who fancy themselves some counterspells will be happy to know that the strategy is still viable. Faeries might be down a [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), but that's not stopping them from showing their stuff.

Faeries have gone mono-blue, as Kei Umehara piloted the Faerie army to a second place finish at the last Grand Prix Modern tournament. The deck packs plenty of classic cards such as [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) and [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), and this version also runs a double-digit number of counterspells to keep the opponent off of their game-plan. While card draw such as [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision)s may no longer be available to the the deck, it gets by with access to things like [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command).



![Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vendilion+Clique)

Also, since your flying Faeries are capable of crunching in for large amounts of damage, who cares about over-the-top card draw when the opponent is dead? Faeries might be controlling, but it's still about playing threats, and keeping the opponents from doing anything to stop their assault. What better way of keeping long-game winners like Pod in check than putting a clock on how long they have to assemble a combo?


I could go on about what decks are viable for Modern, but I'd be spending the entire day doing that. The format truly is wide open, with a massive card pool and new sets now added into the mix. I'll leave you with one final decklist.



![Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Eye+of+Ugin)

This one came from one of the **Magic Online** daily events, and holds a place close to my heart as a man who loves casting big creatures with big mana.

**Saturday, 2:25 p.m. - Dealer's Metagame**
-------------------------------------------


*by Marc Calderaro* 
Knowing a metagame for any given weekend is a crapshoot. You can follow all the **Magic Online** dailies you want; you can read all the coverage possible; you can take a huge poll of all the best players; but in the end, what 1,050 people choose to play is impossible to discern. I've found the best way to take a sampling of the field is to talk to the people who are the players' right hand – the dealers. Whether it be a last-minute deck audible, a sideboard tweak, or a full-fledged hail mary, dealers accommodate the players in their time of need. Seeing what cards they have and what cards they don't can be great intel into the mind of the field, without conducting a thousand-person poll.


The story of this tournament, according to the dealers, is Kamigawa. The two hottest selling cards are both *Champions of Kamigawa* Legendary rares – [Eiganjo Castle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eiganjo+Castle) and [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). The 2/2 red haster is a centerpiece in three distinct archetypes –Naya Pod, [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) and Storm decks (some of them transform into [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) out of the sideboard). As Naya Pod seems poised to garner the title of "current best deck"– if such a title exists at all in the broad format – if you don't play [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker), you'd better have an answer for it.


From the dealers, that answer seems to be [Windborn Muse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windborn+Muse). A fantastic way to stop the infinite creatures that [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker) and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) make is to make a two-mana gate to attack. And though the [Ghostly Prison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison)-on-legs is more expensive than its enchantment counterpart, it's searchable via [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). This allows Pod decks of all stripes to answer the constant threat of endless hasted dudes. [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) has always been a splashy, swingy card, and in this format, it requires a solid response.


On the other end of the "splashy, swingy" scale is the humble land [Eiganjo Castle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eiganjo+Castle). The Legendary land may seem out of place and a bit underwhelming, but it's becoming a necessary staple in White-Blue [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets). With more and more [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) effects rearing their heads (think of the *Ice Age* [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) art), the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) variant needs a way to protect the [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) which is so important to the deck's success. [Eiganjo Castle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eiganjo+Castle) allows an elegant solution to the pyro-problem without sacrificing card slots.


Outside of those two Kamigawa rares, another CHK-block card that has been selling like gangbusters is [Sowing Salt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt) – the land-hater reprinted in *Betrayers of Kamigawa*. Alongside cards like [Molten Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Rain), [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) and Spreading Seaa which have also been selling well, the red uncommon does worlds for stopping the Urza Lands from becoming a big problem (Urza's Mine, Urza's Tower, Urza's Power Plant, for the players who are a touch younger than I am). The Mono-Green deck, UginTron, has made a big splash online, but has yet to break out in Grand Prixes yet. However, if the sale of all these land-killing spells is any indication, players are predicting UginTron will be a force with which to be reckoned this weekend. But only time will tell.


One of my favorite parts about getting a dealer snapshot is just how misleading it can be. As often as dealer sales predict [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) or [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) success, they also can be a red herring. Sometimes it doesn't show what the metagame actually is, but what players think the metagame is. Will [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) or [Eiganjo Castle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eiganjo+Castle) actually propel a player to the top eight? Though it's likely, how you get paired, and what other players brought with them, without the help of the dealers, can sometimes be all the difference.

A [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) from Cho locked away Reda's chance at stalling the game any longer via [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), as the two [Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx)es gave Reda some good licks.

**Round 4 Feature Match - Brandon Nelson vs. Mary Jacobson**

*by Marc Calderaro* 
There's a bit of pressure on Mary Jacobson this weekend. Her Top 8 finish and Grand Prix Lincoln earlier this year was one of the first Modern Grand Prixes. Could she follow it up? She took out the [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast)s for [Master of Etherium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium), and added a [Springleaf Drum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) to help get to three mana.

Jacobson offered Nelson a Red Bull.

The legendary Goblin made a [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) copy, which untapped [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker), which let him make another [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch), and so on, and so on, and so on.


From 0 to a million in only one turn. Blyden wasn't quite ready for that one, as he dropped game one to a million attacking [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)s.


Blyden 0 – Ochoa 1


### Game 2


Blyden led with a [Marsh Flats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats) while Ochoa kicked off his first-turn with [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). A second-turn [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) from Blyden would shut down any immediate [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) shenanigans from Ochoa, who had a second-turn [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben).


A third-turn [Windbrisk Heights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights) was all Blyden had, as he dropped to 16 from an exalted attack via Thalia. Ochoa passed after that without any new plays or lands, while Blyden fired off [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession). Ochoa had [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to copy his [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), which let Thalia attack for 4. A follow-up [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) on the next turn elicited "that's gross" from Ochoa, as [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) started to do his thing for Blyden. [Grafdigger's Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) received a nod of acknowledgment from Ochoa, who rebuilt with a third land and [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/fm%207%20Blyden.JPG)

*Jeff Blyden wipes away Ochoa's initial creatures and reloads with [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant).*

**Round 8 Feature Match - Brian DeMars vs. Max Tietze**
-------------------------------------------------------


*by Marc Calderaro*
This matchup is a fun one. Brian DeMars is a well-known Legacy staple, writing many columns for Star City Games and finishing Top 16 at Pro Tour Philadelphia in 2011. And Max Tietze, though he's finished in the Top 8 of three Grand Prixes, his first ever was back in Columbus in 2007. You know, right here. The format there was also Legacy. Modern, the new eternal format, offers a great opportunity to players who cut their teeth on Legacy to extend that deck-building and playing expertise into newer sets.


DeMars is playing with a [Bant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant) swath of [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s and [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique)s and [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s while dipping into Red for some choice removal, while Tietze is on the WBR [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) list with the featured hit [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and friends.


### Game One


Brian DeMars started with a [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) which was quickly dispatched by a [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame). A [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) soon followed and was already a 3/4. Max Tietze's [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) was unable to grow so quickly, and it sat as a lonely 1/1. However, he grew in spirit when he was joined by a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/D1R8%20DeMars.JPG)

*Brian Demars playing a Bant deck* 
A [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) took out Tietze's [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) and then DeMars's [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). Tietze sunk to 11 after [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) ripped out another sizeable chunk of his flesh. It was 16-11. He retorted by casting a [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and flashing back another [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) removing the [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). The road was clear for the [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) to get in for full damage each turn.


Though DeMars attempted to get back into the game after that, the Geist was too much to overcome.


It was a quick game one and we were shuffling up for game two.


Max Tietze 1 – 0 Brian DeMars


### Game Two


The players both kept their opening seven cards and DeMars started with another [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). Tietze showed his sideboarding skill with a [Twisted Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Image), turning the creature into a 1/0 and relegating it to the dust bin. Again DeMars followed up with a 3/4 [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and hit Tietze down to 17 to even the scores (DeMars had some fetching of lands to do).


A [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) brought a surprise to DeMars's face. When Tietze flashed his hand, it was five lands and a [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). Looking at Tietze's graveyard, DeMars said, "You can keep those," and they proceeded to knock Tietze down to 10.


Tietze's early [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) turned into a 3/2 and threatened to at least do some damage to DeMars, but it was unlikely he had too significant of a follow-up, based on, you know, the infinite land in his hand. Though DeMars knew thanks to the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) flip that Tietze also had a brand new [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to play with, which gave DeMars a pause.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/D1R8%20Tietze.JPG)

*Max Tietze* 
But that's all it was – a pause. After some thoughts, DeMars sent both his Clique and his Goyf in there. Tietze used both cards in his hand to try and save himself from damage. But his [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) hit a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and then his [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) did as well. The score became 9-3 in DeMars's favor, and he knew that his opponent only had lands in his grip.


Tietze found a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), but when DeMars flashed the [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) for the [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration), Tietze packed his cards in for the third game. A 2/2, with or without Hexproof, was not going to save the day.


Max Tietze 1 – 1 Brian DeMars


### Game Three


DeMars had to go down to five cards, and kept three lands, a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and a [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm). Tietze, who was on the play, started with a full seven cards and a [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) + [Arid Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa). Yes, it was nice. What was also nice was DeMars's top-decked [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). As he had started with it the last two games, he figured he should let it become eaten by a [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) again. It was.



![Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Noble+Hierarch)

When Tietze flipped his second-turn [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) on turn three, then dropped another fetch land, this time a [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn), DeMars threw his hands up in the air. This was a sad ending to a good match. DeMars listlessly flipped around cards in his hand as he sunk from 20 to 16 to 9. There wasn't much he could do.


The next-turn fetch land from Tietze just earned a chuckle. DeMars sacrificed his own land to find white mana for his [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm), going down to 6 in the process. Before blockers, he tried to Charm the [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration). If anything was going to happen for him, it needed to happen now.


Tietze asked, "You're at six?" When DeMars confirmed his life total, Tietze responded to the Charm by casting [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) on his own Aberration. This net a land and made [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) grow. He then used [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) #2 to take out the [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). With one more fetch land to use and no blockers for his meek [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), it was all the damage Tietze needed.


Brian DeMars lost a tough third game in a good match.


Max Tietze 2 – 1 Brian DeMars

**Round 9 Feature Match - Orrin Beasley vs. Aaron Estrin**
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*
I'm not going to lie. The siren's call for me when it came to picking this feature match was Orrin Beasley. The reason?


[Rakdos Augermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Augermage). Terry Soh, it's been a long time since I've seen you at a feature match table. The tech-happy three mana creature has been a key component of Beasley's Jund deck. Would Aaron Estrin be able to effectively handle the *Dissension* rare?


### Game 1


Estrin led off with [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), while Beasley used Verdant Catacomb for a [Blood Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt) (dropping to 18, thanks to Estrin's [Grove of the Burnwillows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows)) in order to [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) the one mana creature. Estrin fired back with a tapped [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) and [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), while Beasley got his card draw on with [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant).


Estrin untapped and played [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and then passed action back to Beasley, who revealed [Blood Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt) with [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). Beasley's creature swung in for 2, and then played [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil). The black Planeswalker lost two loyalty counters to take out Estrin's sole creature. Estrin untapped and played [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), and the pod upgraded the artifact creature into [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage). The Sparkmage immediately shot down [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant).


Beasley untapped. An [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) revealed two more [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)s in Estrin's hand. Liliana of the Vial forced one out of Estrin's hand, and [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) cleared away the [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage). Estrin untapped, shrugged, and played the [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). Liliana went up another point in loyalty, and forced Estrin to discard. Beasley got on the offensive with [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and Treetrop Village. A turn later, and Estrin went to 9 due to Beasley's attacks.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/FM%209%20aaron_estrin.jpg)

*Aaron Estrin attempts to Pod his way out of a hole.* 
Estrin drew into a [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). The Pods upgraded the [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) into [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) into [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), and suddenly, Estrin was one turn away from winning and had Liliana protection with a fresh new [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), courtesy of [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). Beasley drew a land, and used Liliana. An attack from [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) earned no blocks from Estrain, who had a life cushion thanks to the Pod and Finks tricks.


Playing it safe, as Beasley didn't play his land, Estrin sent the [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) into Liliana, and then Pods with the [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) that he drew got another [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks). A turn later, and Estrin was willing to pull the trigger on [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) by upgrading the fresh Kitching Finks (which became [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), which became the red legend).


When Estrin activated [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kiki-Jiki%252C%2BMirror%2BBreaker), Beasley conceded.


Beasley 0 – Estrin 1


### Game 2


While Beasley was satisfied with his open seven, Estrin didn't take a liking to his first seven or his next six. Going down to five against Jund can be pretty rough. Estrin drew his five, shook his head and said, "Okay...".


Beasley saw what Estrin was reacting to with a first-turn [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), which robbed Estrin of a wall of [Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roots) and revealed two lands, [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage). Beasley fetched out a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) with [Marsh Flats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats) on turn two and...passed, with [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), [Overgrown Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb), and [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) in hand. Estrin played a new land and passed, as Beasley untapped and dropped Liliana of the Vial into play. Liliana went into discard duty, as Beasley ditched [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) and Estrin discarded [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol12/FM%209%20orrin_beasley.jpg)

*Orrin Beasley comes out swinging with his Jund deck. Liliana, by the way, loves to put opponents in a no-win situation.* 
Estrin untapped and played [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), but Liliana's discarding and [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) left Estrin with almost no gas, and [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) have Beasley a win condition. Estrin untapped, played his land, and rand out [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). Then he promptly lost it to Beasley's [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse), as [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) swung in.


Estrin top-decked [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), which gave him a little leverage, but [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) from Beasley buried Estrin, as he was unable to fight against a ticking Liliana and the two powerful creatures.


Beasley 1 – Estrin 1


### Game 3


Estrin led off with [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), while Beasley kicked things off by fetching up a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and casting [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek). Staring back at him was a hand full of creatures: [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage), two [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), and [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness). "Wow..." Beasley said, as he tried to figure out what to rob Estrin of. He opted to get rid of Estrin's bird. Estrin drew, and reluctantly passed after thinking for a moment.


Beasley used another Verdant Catacomb, this time finding a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), and [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) gave Beasley some muscle. Estrin found wall of [Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roots) waiting on top of his deck, giving him much needed mana. Beasley played a [Treetop Village](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) and [Grafdigger's Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) after Estrin chose not to block the attacking Goyf.


Estrin drew into a Razorforge Thicket, and he cast [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and the [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) that he brought back. Beasley woke up his [Treetop Village](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) and got aggressive, sending his two creatures in. Estrin untapped and cast [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons), before attacking with [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) for 2.


Beasley drew and played [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), revealing a monstrous hand of three [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage). He discard the [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage), and then swung with his Goyf. When Estrin didn't block, Beasley used [Ancient Grudge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) on his own [Grafdigger's Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) to pump up the [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).


However, it was not going to help in the face of [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons). The legendary angel put Beasley on a clock that was difficult to race, and the two copies of [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) that Estrin played next turn, made racing an impossible task. When Beasley drew another land, he offered the handshake.


And sadly, no [Rakdos Augermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Augermage) during this match. Not even in his hand. Drat...


Aaron Estrin defeats Orrin Beasley 2-1!
