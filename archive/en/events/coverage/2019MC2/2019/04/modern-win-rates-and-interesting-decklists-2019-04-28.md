Despite being the most-played deck, despite putting two players into the Top 8, and despite benefiting largely from the London mulligan rule, Tron had a subpar performance overall. Players trying to assemble [Urza's Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine), [Urza's Power Plant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant), and [Urza's Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine) won only 47.7% of their matches in the Modern rounds.


A deck that did perform well was Humans. With a 54.3% win rate overall, it seemed to have been well-positioned, and it was not surprising to see three Humans players in the Top 8. One reason was that [Deputy of Detention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deputy+of+Detention) gave the deck a big boost. Even though it's not a Human, it acts as versatile removal that can be played via [Ã†ther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial). All Human players in the Top 8 had 3 [Deputy of Detention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deputy+of+Detention) in their 75, and it was the most-played *Ravnica Allegiance* card overall.


Two other decks that did particularly well were Hardened Scales and Ad Nauseam, at a 56.0% and 61.7% win rate, respectively. If their real match win rate would be 50% and every match would be independent, then seeing a result at least as good as this would be unlikely to happen (<5%) just by chance. You can find top-performing decklists of these archetypes among the [24-27 Modern match point decklists](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2019MC2/24-27-modern-match-point-decklists-2019-04-28).


In the "Eldrazi variants" and "Other" categories, there are several decks worth highlighting.

His Red Eldrazi variant featured [Eldrazi Obligator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Obligator), [Chandra, Torch of Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance), and [Ramunap Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ramunap+Ruins) in the maindeck, and an assortment of powerful red cards in the sideboard. They gave him more reach and a more powerful sideboard than the colorless Eldrazi versions, and they brought Jacobsen to a 10th-place finish.

Cheerios, named after a breakfast cereal that resembles the casting cost of all the equipment in the deck, tries to mulligan towards [Puresteel Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puresteel+Paladin) or [Sram, Senior Edificer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sram%2C+Senior+Edificer). You then pray the opponent doesn't have [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) or [Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push), draw your deck, and combine [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal), Retract, and [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) into a kill. Luke Mulcahy went 7-3 in Modern, and the other Cheerios player, Ken Yukuhio, went 6-4. This suggests that the archetype can thrive in the current Modern format, at least under the London mulligan rule.

This archetype was made possible by the *Ravnica Allegiance* card [Prime Speaker Vannifar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prime+Speaker+Vannifar). A throwback to [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), [Prime Speaker Vannifar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prime+Speaker+Vannifar) can turn a single [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) into an immediate kill by chaining Scry Ranger into [Renegade Rallier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Rallier) into [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) into [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) into [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). And with Eldritch Evoluton, you also have an entire toolbox of creatures at your disposal.


All in all, the Mythic Championship showed that Modern is diverse as always. There's a large variety of archetypes that can all achieve success, and things will get even more exciting with *Modern Horizons* on the, well, horizon.
