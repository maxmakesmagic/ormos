Five Questions on Commanding Hope
=================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 16, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






Much of the rest of the site is seemingly caught in the grips of the ***Magic: The Gathering** Commander* release this Friday (some of those cards are pretty cool!), but we here at Top Decks are concerned, per usual, with tournament **Magic** results.

As with many of you, I am anxiously awaiting the possible news that will come out next week RE: potential Standard bannings; these seem imminent, especially considering the not-ambiguous social communications of our beloved **Magic** boss:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td147_twitter.jpg)  
The sentiment among many players is that Caw-Blade has reached "Affinity" levels. Most of you know that I actually *like* this format and don't see a huge problem around Caw-Blade or its primary weapons, [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), but it is hard to argue the success that the deck has had since its inaugural Pro Tour win in the hands of Ben Stark back in Paris.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td147_jace.jpg)  
That said, I claimed a few weeks ago that the "Age of Caw-Blade" is done, and despite some continued success (e.g., PV's win in the recent Grand Prix), the deck's stranglehold seems to have loosened up. Consider the Top 8 of the most recent Star City Games Standard Open in Denver, Colorado:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td147_chart1.jpg)  
Not only did Caw-Blade fail to take 50% of the Top 8, but Brandon Montoya (Open Champion, with Vampires) and Jason Bulkowski (Red Deck) kept Caw-Blade out of the finals! Unprecedented! (Well, not really, but it's still pretty refreshing considering the last couple of months.)

### 1) So What's Going on with Vampires?

Vampires continues to succeed (with back-to-back Star City Standard Open wins) at least in part due to Caw-Blade's being optimized to beat Caw-Blade. The original Caw-Blade decks ran cards like [Sylvok Lifestaff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Lifestaff) to keep pace with beatdown, as well as the full four copies of [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) to take on all comers, including little beaters. Of the three Caw-Blade variants to make the Denver Top 8, none of them played more than two copies of [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), and only one of the three decks played [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) main deck.

While [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) is creeping back into deck lists, the reality is that so long as Caw-Blade is optimized to beat Caw-Blade, decks like Vampires can take advantage not only of the *lack* of particular defenses, but the giant glowing neon targets that the strategy presents.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td147_vandal.jpg)  
Case in point: Montoya's three main-deck [Manic Vandal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal)s, i.e. "free value."

### 2) How Does the Red Deck Interact with the Caw-Blade Metagame?

For all of Caw-Blade's virtues, the strategy has a fairly basic weakness: It can largely be boiled down to a deck with about eight small guys trying to pick up one piece of equipment and quite literally "run with it."

Ignoring newcomer [Shrine of Burning Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Burning+Rage), the standard Standard red deck plays [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning), [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze), and [Staggershock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staggershock)... not to mention the ability on [Ember Hauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Hauler). So, basically a 2-1 edge over Caw-Blade in terms of "efficient removal" versus "little guy" counts.

Not only can red decks get the beats on with first turn [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)s getting in there, but when Caw-Blade has to devote two to four mana at sorcery speed to get a piece of Equipment into position, the deck is basically leaving itself wide open to a removal spell and a massive tempo swing.

But what about [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull)?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td147_batter.jpg)  
For sure [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) and its lifelink can be an issue for a deck full of burn spells, but don't forget that if you kill the [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), all of a sudden Caw-Blade has to play fairly and run out [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) at the full five mana. [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) for five mana deals with a 4/4 living weapon nicely, thank you; Red Deck can get in there with little guys and maybe a [Teetering Peaks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks), just so.

### 3) What's the Deal With Sea Gate Oracle, Anyway?

While the biggest eyebrow-raiser in this deck is [Pilgrim's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim%27s+Eye), running a close second is brother three-drop [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Splinter Twin | Sea Gate Oracle |

I actually borrowed the card from an extended [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) deck with some input from Patrick Chapin; [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) is a fairly intuitive addition in Standard if you consider two things: 1) sometimes you have a [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), or a spare [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), but for some reason can't go for the win (for example, you have no [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)); in these cases, [Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) can play "bad Jace," and better yet, a Jace that can attack (someone else's Jace), defend, and of course just dig. 2) When you have the most powerful strategy (that is, infinite damage potentially on turn four) in a format where such fast kills are absent from most other decks, maximizing the consistency and redundancy of your deck is pretty attractive.

[Sea Gate Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) is particularly effective in a metagame with the rising Vampires. A 1/3 body in a world of [Pulse Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+Tracker)s and [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s (I mean, which deck has a good answer to [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)?) is very attractive.

![Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fauna+Shaman)

Both of them offer explosive possibilities, from a raging stream of 4/3 Elementals to the threat of a third-turn [Frost Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan).

[![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td147_frostPromo.jpg)](/en/articles/archive/duels-planeswalkers-2012-promo-cards-2011-03-30)

Pichon and LSV played [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) decks that were actually quite a bit different on the details. While both players ran [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol), only one ran [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk); Scott-Vargas ran [Origin Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Origin+Spellbomb) and more lands, as well as [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) over more [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge)s.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Glint Hawk | Blade Splicer |

Despite some differences on the details, the theme of the [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) strategy is pretty consistent: Cast lots of little artifact guys, pump them large with [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel), smash.

[Dispatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispatch) is a superb tool here (and in [Puresteel Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puresteel+Paladin) White Weenie, for that matter) as a kind of no-downside [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares). You basically always have metalcraft; you just do it. For one.

Grand Prix winner and general awesome dude standout Gaudenis Vidurgiris went with a straightforward and effective linear strategy.

There are lots of awesome things going on with this deck in the metagame, starting with [Virulent Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Virulent+Wound) versus [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite) (or whatever). But the best has to be the positioning of [Phyrexian Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader) in a room full of white and red decks.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td147_pCrusader.jpg)  
[Phyrexian Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader), untap, [Piston Sledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Piston+Sledge)? No one can block it. No one can remove it. [Mutagenic Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) you? Wow. Just wow.
