Welcome back to Building on a Budget! My name is Ben Bleiweiss, and we're rounding the corner towards the tail end of 10 Decks in 10 Weeks! In last week's article, I finished up with a white-blue deck centered around [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink), creatures with a comes-into-play ability, and the rescue creatures from *Planar Chaos*. In this edition of 10 Decks in 10 Weeks, I take out the blue and move to the [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/features/bb_10decks_logo.jpg)[Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) is one of those cards that everyone gives consideration to, but that rarely works out in practice. The reason why [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) often fails to achieve the desired results is because it is a very potent double-edged sword. Your opponent can use [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) just as well you can, and often will do so first since you have spent the majority of your mana to cast [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) itself.


Traditionally, there have been two successful types of decks that have employed [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium):


 


1. Straight Combo: [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), when it was first released, worked quite well with the Nemesis enchantment Saproling Burst. If you play a [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) and then a Burst, you can deal 21 damage immediately by making a 6/6 creature, letting [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) resolve, making a 5/5 creature, letting [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) resolve, and repeating this until you get down to having six 1/1 creatures in play at once.
2. Aggro-Combo: This is the typically the red-green deck that has [Primal Forcemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Forcemage), [Norin the Wary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Norin+the+Wary) and haste creatures. Strike hard, strike fast, and get double immediate damage out of your haste creatures. This way, they can definitely hit for damage before they die to [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) from creatures on the other side of the board.

Each of these deck types has had mixed success, for the reason that [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) cuts both ways. For every game that you get the [Norin the Wary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Norin+the+Wary) / [Primal Forcemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Forcemage) / [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) draw (and keep the Forcemage alive), you have a game where you drop [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), and your opponent responds by dropping ten power worth of creatures to the board and either burns you or all of your creatures out of the game.


With that in mind, let's take a look at how the deck looks this week:


Out: 4 [Azorius Chancery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Chancery), 4 [Azorius Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet), 3 [Azorius First-Wing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+First-Wing), 3 [Court Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar), 3 [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk), 6 [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), 4 [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink), 3 [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)


The typical removal of all the cards from the departed color of the week. In this case, I also yanked out the [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink)s – without the Blue flashback ability, they would be a lot less efficient in the deck.

In: 4 [Boros Garrison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Garrison), 4 [Boros Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Signet), 6 [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)


Next, I put in four copies of [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium). The deck is going to be built around the card, so I mightaswell get 'er in there.


In: 4 [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium)


Next comes the creature base. Here's the tricky part! As mentioned above, the main uses for [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) are in Combo or Aggro-Combo. This deck is not a combo deck in any shape, way or form. As you've seen from Rescue Me and A Blink in Time, this deck is just pure aggro – I drop creatures, swing, save my creatures from dying in combat with other aggressive creatures, and just keep going to the fences until those fences are smashed down. There are plenty of fancy tricks, but those tricks are based around coming into play and leaving play, and not around built-in [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth)s or broken enchantments that make huge creatures.


 



![Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pandemonium)

As I mentioned before, one of the drawbacks of [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) is that your opponent often gets to drop the first set of creatures after [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) hits the board, since the enchantment itself costs four mana to play. This deck already has several good interactions with [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium):

 


* [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm) can't be targeted by [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) (when an opponent plays a creature).
* The white rescue creatures can save your other creatures from being hit by [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium).
* The white rescue creatures can bounce themselves and recur [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) damage. Six mana with either a [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker) or [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion) is 6 damage a turn, at instant speed.

When approaching the puzzle that is [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), I felt that working around the "it costs four mana to play [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium)" drawback was essential. To this effect, I already have [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt)s in the deck – guys that can easily be played the same turn as [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), and who deal 4 damage for two mana under those circumstances. This left me wanting to stack the deck with more high-power, low-cost creatures.


[Keldon Marauders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders) immediately came to mind. I faced a red-white-blue Rescue / Blink deck featuring these guys a few weeks back, and it struck me how efficient the Marauders were in that deck. This build can use the Marauders as a recurring [Lava Axe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Axe) (one damage when it comes into play, plus three for an attack, plus one when it leaves play – preferably with a Rescue creature).


I also wanted to try out [Volcano Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcano+Hellion). Six power for four mana is huge, and [Volcano Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcano+Hellion) can kill virtually any creature that my opponent can put into play. Sure, I won't often be able to pay the echo on this monster, but I can always bounce it back to my hand during my upkeep with [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker) or [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion), and then reuse it to kill another creature later. Plus, it's great with [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) – it can do six to my opponent's dome, plus kill an opposing creature, plus survive virtually anything my opponent can drop back with [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) the following turn!


To round out the deck, I included 3 copies of [Sparkmage Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) (which I had alluded to last week when I took out [Icatian Javelineers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Javelineers)) and 2 copies of [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger) (to replace the departed [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)).


In: 4 [Keldon Marauders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders), 3 [Volcano Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcano+Hellion), 3 [Sparkmage Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice), 2 [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger)

On his turn, he uses [Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena) and kills my [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger), and then drops a two-power [Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid) to kill my [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker). Record: 1-0


**Rules Aside:** In this game, Cloyd used [Sporesower Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sporesower+Thallid) to kill one of my creatures during his main phase. After the game, I pointed out that [Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena) can be used after attackers are declared. As written:


"![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif), ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170424152623im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/tap.gif): Tap target creature you control and target creature of an opponent's choice he or she controls. Each of those creatures deals damage equal to its power to the other."


 



![Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Arena)

Note that costs for using a card are always before the colon—these are what must be paid for a card to work. "Tap target creature you control" is written after the colon, so it is an effect of the card. That means that once you pay the cost of "![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif), ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170424152623im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/tap.gif)" part of the effect is "Tap target creature you control" just as much as "Each of those creatures deals damage equal to its power to the other." This also means that you do not need to have an untapped creature to use this on! [Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena) does not say "Tap target untapped creature you control," so it is fine to declare an attacker, have your creature tap to attack, and then throw it into the [Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena)!

Game 12: Bkton (Slivers)  

He is playing the [Dormant Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dormant+Sliver) / [Firewake Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firewake+Sliver) build, but I kill his [Firewake Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firewake+Sliver) with [Sparkmage Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice), use [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger) and rescue creatures to bounce his key Slivers, and then drop [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) with him at 9 life. Record: 9-3


After this game, in which I drew [Volcano Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcano+Hellion), I realized that the big guy just wasn't working out all that great in this deck. I really could never pay the echo on him, and since he could never attack, he was a wasted slot in a deck that wanted to be all aggro. To remedy this, I put back in [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt)—I missed the Grunts the past few games, and realized that I had taken the wrong creature out of the deck for the Helixes.

Game 13: Drahcirone (G/R Aggro)  

I get a quick start with [Keldon Marauders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders) and rescue creatures. When he's at 10 life, I drop [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), allow him to drop his smaller creatures, and then untap and drop [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt)s and [Keldon Marauders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders) to deal 8 to his head. He can't deal with the bruisers, and dies after a turn of chump blocking.  

Record: 10-3


Game 14: Ktafin (R/U Suspend)  

He gets multiple [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)s, and I get multiple [Aven Riftwatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Riftwatcher)s. He suspends [Deep-Sea Kraken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep-Sea+Kraken), and I engineer the game to a point where it has one counter left. I then cast [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger). This puts the [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger) on the stack (not in play yet!), which triggers removing a counter from his Kraken. His Kraken unsuspends, and comes into play. Then, my [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger) comes into play and bounces his Kraken! This ends his hopes of coming back, and I end the game at 40 life to nothing.  

Record: 11-3


Game 15: 1cube1wheel (U/B Control)  

He gets double [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp), double [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast), and [Dark Banishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing). Targeted removal is a huge problem (for its user) against a deck packing multiple rescue creatures—every time he tries to kill a guy, I use [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion) or [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker) to save my creature. On the sixth turn, he uses his last card in hand ([Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)) to counter [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), and that leaves me open to play [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt)s with my last two mana. The Grunts get rid of his [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast)s, and my creature force comes into to win the day.  

Record: 12-3


Sheer [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) was a lot of fun to play—probably the most fun of the three rescue-creature based decks from the past three weeks! All three decks were definitely aggro-oriented, but this one was just all-out burn-your-opponent dead aggro. Because I have several two-drops with three and four power, it was not unusual to drop [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), untap, and then burn out half my opponent's life total with creatures!
