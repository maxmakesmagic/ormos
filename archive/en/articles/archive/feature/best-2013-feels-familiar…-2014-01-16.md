By Gerry Thompson











![Sunscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sunscape+Familiar)
First, [Frantic Search](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frantic+Search) got the axe in Pauper. Early in 2013, [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) and [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) also got banned. Regardless, Storm persevered, but eventually [Temporal Fissure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Fissure) joined its brethren. While I enjoyed playing the [Temporal Fissure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Fissure) deck during my Pauper stint, it was certainly no fun for my opponents to watch me take a ten-minute turn where the end result was them having no permanents. 

 There was the illusion of being able to interact with the [Temporal Fissure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Fissure) Storm deck by killing the Familiars or destroying the karoos ([Azorius Chancery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Chancery) and [Dimir Aqueduct](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Aqueduct)), but unless you have a fast clock, Storm would simply rebuild. Storm addressed that issue by having upwards of fourteen sideboard cards against aggro decks! 

 To the relief of Pauper players looking for a fair game, the banning of [Temporal Fissure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Fissure) will likely kill the archetype, if not significantly wound it. However, I have a decklist for next week that continues to showcase the power of [Ghostly Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker), [Snap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snap), and [Cloud of Faeries](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+of+Faeries), so stay tuned!
