The Skull Returns
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 10, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_240.png)
By Mark L. Gottlieb











![Skull of Orm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Skull+of+Orm)No one expected this. No one. ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif), printed ages ago, powerful enough to have been restricted in Type 1, and it's back. Against all odds, the *Skull* is being reprinted in *Eighth Edition*!
 I'm referring, of course, to the completely separate cards of [Underworld Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams) and [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm). Did you think I meant something else?


[Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm) is the "old favorite" I picked to write about this week. That description is half right, I guess; it certainly is old. I have to say that I feel a little foolish. When I was scanning the *Eighth Edition* cards looking for a topic for this column, I didn't pay much attention to the abilities. I was just looking at the art. I picked the coolest possible card: a gnarly artifact skull with a glowing eye, mini-tusk things, pointy chin spikes, a gold half-helm, and what look like tribal engravings. That had to be the most badass card in the set, right? Right? After learning the awful truth, I frantically read through Paul Barclay's *Eighth Edition* rules update. I was desperately hoping there was a slight tweak to the rules that said, "If an artifact's activated ability costs 5, T, you may pay 1, T instead." No luck.


### Vapor Ops


![Lethal Vapors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lethal+Vapors)Nah, none of that's true. (I specify this because I've received some scary email recently. Some people foolishly have their sarcasm detectors set to "low" when reading this column.) I wanted to write about [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm) for two reasons: 1) Some people are decrying it as the worst card in *Eighth Edition*, and 2) It makes a pretty cool combo with [Lethal Vapors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lethal+Vapors).
 That combo's not foolproof, of course. At some point, if your opponent ever wants to play creatures, he or she will give up a future turn to drop as many creatures onto the table at once as is possible. Then you have two turns to deal with them. If they're small, Ormable enchantments like [Crown of Suspicion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion) and [Infectious Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infectious+Rage) can sweep them away. If they're a little bigger, enchantments such as [Patriarch's Desire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Desire) and [Clutch of Undeath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+Undeath) do the trick. And perhaps my favorite bit of recursion is to bring back [Lingering Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Death) over and over. I know what you're thinking. The two-card combo of [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm) plus [Lingering Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Death) does in two turns what the single card [Predator, Flagship](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator%2C+Flagship) does in one. That's true. But Predator doesn't have cool mini-tusk things, now does it?


As long as we're recurring enchantments, [Planar Chaos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Chaos) and [Uncontrolled Infestation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uncontrolled+Infestation) seem like fine goofy additions to the deck. All this malarkey pretty much adds up to an oddball way to replace [Ensnaring Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) in a [Hammer of Bogardan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer+of+Bogardan) deck - which is really strange, because [Ensnaring Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) reappears in *Eighth Edition*. Hey, if my top priority was to make sense, would I be making *Skull of Orm* decks?

### Head Games


How else can you have fun kicking around the Skull? Easy! Let's take a look at blue, where options for enchantment recursion range from [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) to [Confiscate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confiscate) to [Decree of Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Silence)... you're playing a **Magic** variant where mana costs are free, right? You're not? Let's move right along to green and white then!


Green and white both like playing around with enchantments. Green has Enchantresses, an enchantment that can attack, and the always-fun-to-recur [Lure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lure). White has crazy enchantments that combo with [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm) to create a lock under which you take no damage. (Though [Pariah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pariah) bypasses the Skull in the *Core Set* like two ships passing in the card pool, check out what happens when you put them together.) And together, green and white provide the enchantment-munching [Thaumatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thaumatog), which is a great way to put enchantments into your graveyard so you can put them into your hand with [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm) so you can put them back into play.


The optimal strategy you want to use when playing this next deck (or whatever better version you come up with) is to have fun. It's built around a slow, expensive, slo-o-ow card, so it's not intended to be a world-beater. In fact, the scattershot card quantities are intentional because I'm laz-er, because it seems to fit the deck better. The more randomness built into the deck, the more each game will have a unique feel to it because you'll be drawing different cards. It's a terrible design strategy if you want to win, but it's a good way to build a deck if all you want to do is goof around with your freaky Skull.

### Permanent Permission


![Decree of Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Decree+of+Silence)Y'know, maybe I dismissed the [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm)-[Decree of Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Silence) combo too quickly. Using the Decree's cycling ability, the combo gets you a cantrip counterspell every single turn for the low, low price of only ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20171126205314im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless9.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)! [Forbid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbid)'s buyback cost was to discard two cards. Convert that into an equivalent cost in mana, add it to [Forbid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbid)'s ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) cost, and tack on whatever you'd have to pay for cantrippiness, and you're probably pretty close to eleven mana. Finally, a situation where [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm)'s activation cost is reasonably costed! And you thought permission decks were dead in Standard because [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) is gone. Hah!
 Until next week, have fun with [Skull of Orm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+of+Orm).
