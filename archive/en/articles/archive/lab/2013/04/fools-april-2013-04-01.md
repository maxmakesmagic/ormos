The Fools of April
==================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on April 1, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







Welcome to the Lab, minions! Today is April 1, the day we celebrate pulling pranks and making people look like fools. This is one of my favorite holidays, and it's a prime opportunity for pulling a few old tricks out of the bag. I've dyed the ice in the ice machine blood red, served up a bowl of breakfast cereal that was actually frozen solid, and made a friend think he dialed my number wrong and called a Chinese restaurant by mistake. Of course, there are some fun pranks you can pull off within a game of **Magic** as well.


### Oh, Never Mind


[Boldwyr Heavyweights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boldwyr+Heavyweights) has always been a pet card of mine. As an 8/8 for four mana, it is double the normal size for a creature of its cost, but it has the drawback of letting your opponent get any creature from his or her deck and put it onto the battlefield. Fortunately, [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold) turns that drawback into an April Fools' joke. "Hey, you can get any creature you want! Oh wait, I have [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold). April Fools!"

Even more cruel and hilarious is [Maralen of the Mornsong](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maralen+of+the+Mornsong). "Well, we can't draw cards, but it's okay, because we can search for a card instead. Well, at least, I can. You can't really do anything, can you?" Best of all, your opponent will still lose the 3 life to Maralen's ability, despite being unable to search for anything. You can tutor for [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) to offset the life loss from Maralen, and you can also search for an [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) to get rid of any problem permanent, or a [Rebuff the Wicked](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuff+the+Wicked) to protect her from removal.

[Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) is an efficient piece of removal that also loses its drawback when you have [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold). Your opponent won't even get a basic land to make up for his or her loss. [Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) functions similarly, turning into [Strip Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strip+Mine) with [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold) on the board.

[Aven Mindcensor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor) and [Leonin Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Arbiter) can play backup for [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold) if necessary, although to varying degrees of effectiveness. The Mindcensor will limit your opponent's search to four cards. Now, instead of getting the best creature in his or her library with [Boldwyr Heavyweights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boldwyr+Heavyweights), your opponent will get the creature at the top of his or her library. Maralen turns into [Impulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse) for your opponent, while you still get [Demonic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Tutor). [Leonin Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Arbiter) is a bit more fair, affecting both players equally, but it can still give you a nice window to cast [Boldwyr Heavyweights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boldwyr+Heavyweights) if your opponent doesn't have two mana available.

[Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) helps you find [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold) or search for Maralen to tutor up whatever else you need later. It can also grab one of your silver bullets if you need one immediately.


The last piece of the deck is my favorite. [Timesifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timesifter) essentially randomizes turn order. At the beginning of each upkeep, you clash with your opponent to determine who gets to take the next turn. With a [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold) out, however, it becomes very one-sided. If your opponent reveals the card with the highest converted mana cost, he or she gets... nothing. If you reveal the card with the highest converted mana cost, you get to take an extra turn. To demonstrate how awesome this is, let's say you win every other clash. You cast [Timesifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timesifter), and on your opponent's upkeep you win. After his or her turn, you take your extra one, and lose that clash. When you're done, your opponent skips the extra turn, and you take your normal turn. On your upkeep, you win the converted mana cost battle again, and you get a third turn in a row. After that you lose, and your opponent finally gets a turn, at which point the process starts over. So, if you're winning just fifty percent of the time with this card, you get to take three turns for every one turn your opponent takes. That is fairly absurd, and if you don't win the game shortly afterward, something has gone very wrong.

### Dying All Over Again


After you've made your opponent look like a fool, you can kill him or her like one with this next deck. [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise) is a theme-fitting card that's ripe for abuse with the right tools. The first card that came to mind was [Composite Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Composite+Golem). You can sacrifice the Golem for five mana, return it to the battlefield with [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise), then use that mana to cast the Aura again. This won't win the game by itself, but it does let you trigger [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) and [Falkenrath Noble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble) an infinite number of times. I originally used [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) for a more nostalgic kill card, but it won't work with our second route to victory.

[Academy Researchers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Researchers) will also combo with [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise). When it dies, put the creature trigger from [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise) onto the stack, then the Aura's own trigger. [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise) will return to your hand, then when [Academy Researchers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Researchers) returns to the battlefield, you can put the Aura onto the battlefield attached to it, ready to start the process over again. Of course, you'll need some way to sacrifice the Researchers to get this going. [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) gives you a way to sacrifice [Academy Researchers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Researchers) as much as you want, and can also be transmuted for [Falkenrath Noble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble). A [Fallen Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Angel) and a [Phyrexian Plaguelord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Plaguelord) give you other options for sacrifice outlets, and [Brainspoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) can transmute for either of them, as well as for [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise). It's also a fine, if not particularly efficient, piece of removal. Finally, [Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) should help you stop your opponent from ripping apart your combo before it's complete.

### Fight of the Fools


Now it's time for our foolish friends to battle it out the arena. Who will be the heavyweight champion, and which fool will meet his demise? Let's go find out, shall we?


### Game 1


No Search for You won the roll and started off with an [Isolated Chapel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isolated+Chapel). Foolish Researchers played a Swamp and passed back. No Search played a Mountain, and the Researchers summoned a [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). No Search put a [Blood Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt) onto the battlefield tapped, and the Researchers played a land ad passed the turn. No Search played [Clifftop Retreat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clifftop+Retreat) and cast [Maralen of the Mornsong](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maralen+of+the+Mornsong). Foolish Researchers took 3, then cast [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) and passed the turn. No Search lost 3 life, then played a land and cast [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold). The Researchers lost 3 life, cast [Fallen Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Angel), and passed the turn. No Search lost 3, played a land, and passed back. The Researchers lost 3, then cast [Falkenrath Noble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble) and attacked with [Fallen Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Angel).

No Search dropped to 2, then cast [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer), equipped it to Maralen, and attacked for 5. The Researchers took the damage, and the turn went to them and back with no play. Maralen attacked again, and both creatures blocked, leaving the Researchers at 6 and No Search at 5. Still unable to draw any cards, Foolish Researchers conceded.

The Researchers passed with no play, and No Search got rid of the [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) with [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring). The Researchers paid 2 life for [Coalition Relic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic) and put a charge counter on it before passing the turn. No Search paid 2 for [Godless Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine) and cast [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold).

### Game 3


No Search for You played [Isolated Chapel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isolated+Chapel), and Foolish Researchers started off with [Watery Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave). No Search followed up with [Clifftop Retreat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clifftop+Retreat), and the Researchers played a [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). No Search played another land and passed the turn, and the Researchers transmuted [Brainspoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) for [Fool's Demise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fool%27s+Demise). No Search paid 2 life for [Sacred Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) and cast [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold). [Academy Researchers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Researchers) hit the battlefield, and No Search cast [Boldwyr Heavyweights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boldwyr+Heavyweights).
