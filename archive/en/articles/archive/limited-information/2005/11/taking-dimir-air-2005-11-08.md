Taking Dimir to the Air
=======================



 Posted in **Limited Information**
 on November 8, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/hero/wizardslogo_thumb.jpg)
By Scott Wills











Now that *Ravnica* has finally arrived online we can at last get down to some real drafting. The triple *Ravnica* format will be the format of choice for the next four months online and with everyone scrambling to find *Ravnica* cards for their new standard format decks the format is going to very popular.


I've already expressed my appreciation of the format in previous columns, but I am still enjoying *Ravnica* drafts immensely and continue to believe this is one of the better Limited formats we've had in the last few years.


Enough preamble, there's no need to explain further as I'm sure you all know the format by now. It's a triple *Ravnica* booster draft so let's get on and open the first pack!


### Pack One


The opening pack of the draft presents us with the following goodies:


![Pack 1, Pick 1](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/limited/91_Pack1.jpg)


**Pack 1, Pick 1:** 
[Golgari Rot Farm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm), [Dogpile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dogpile), [Civic Wayfinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder), [Sparkmage Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice), [Nightguard Patrol](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightguard+Patrol), [Boros Recruit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Recruit), [Quickchange](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quickchange), [Infectious Host](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infectious+Host), [Selesnya Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Signet), [Snapping Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake), [Dimir Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Signet), [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii), [Golgari Thug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Thug), [Drooling Groodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drooling+Groodion), [Helldozer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helldozer).


**My Pick:**



**[Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii)**


There are a couple of great cards to choose from here and while I'm sure many players would jump for the rare in this pack that isn't the best pick. [Helldozer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helldozer) is nice but it basically is just a vanilla 6/5 monster with a tricky casting cost. The ability may well destroy a land or two but it likely won't do so until turn seven which may be too late to affect the game. If it weren't for the uncommons in the pack I might take it, but I think I might also be inclined to go with [Snapping Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake) over it.


Both [Drooling Groodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drooling+Groodion) and [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) are superior to all the other cards though and the choice comes down to them. Groodion is very slow, but also very powerful. If you untap with him in play it makes almost any combat unwinnable for your opponent as well as providing removal for cards like [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire) and [Snapping Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake) that might avoid tangling in combat. 


[Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) is another very powerful card and the extra point of toughness it has over [Drooling Groodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drooling+Groodion) is very relevant in this format of [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp)s and [Galvanic Arc](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Arc)s. The point of damage you take each turn is largely irrelevant as you're easily able to outrace an opponent when dealing four in the air. It's also a very nice card to drop on turn three with the help of a Signet.


My choice here is [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii). I think it is harder to deal with for one, but I also think that a lot of players shy away from blue when drafting and that it might be easier to get that colour than green during the first days of a new drafting format.

**My Pick:**


**[Watchwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf)**


![Watchwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Watchwolf) Only really [Stinkweed Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp) from this pack would allow me to stay on colour and I'm not ready to take that guy as a second pick. It's too early to read any signals into this pack and it's therefore wise just to go with the best card. It's a close tie between [Conclave Equenaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conclave+Equenaut) and [Watchwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf) for me but [Watchwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf) is just amazing on turn two in this format. Equenaut is great too, but I think there's a better chance of filling its role with Equenauts and [Screeching Griffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Griffin)s later on if we do end up green-white. Great two-drops on the other hand are very hard to come by and I think it's a better pick at this point in time for that reason.
 It sucks to take four colours from your first two picks but I may have to abandon [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) completely and that is just something I'll have to accept if it happens.

**[Brainspoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil)**


I don't think there is a lot of choice in this pack as [Brainspoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) is the best remaining card. It's expensive and slow but it does to a very good job of taking out its target. It also can be used to Transmute for other powerful five mana bombs if that situation arises.

[Boros Garrison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Garrison), [Tattered Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattered+Drake), [Mortipede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortipede), [Ordruun Commando](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordruun+Commando), [Golgari Rotwurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rotwurm), [Perplex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perplex), [Gate Hound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gate+Hound), [Zephyr Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zephyr+Spirit), [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel), [Vindictive Mob](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vindictive+Mob), [Blockbuster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blockbuster), [Reroute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reroute).


**My Pick:**


**[Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel)**


![Disembowel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Disembowel)Another very obvious pick here, with a solid removal spell coming 'round as a fourth pick. It's a little surprising that [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel) is still here, especially with a Rotwurm present too but that's a very good signal for me. It suggests that only one of the previous three drafters is black and that there might be two Boros drafters to the right of me, as I think many Selesnya players would definitely have taken the [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel) as it's easily splashable via the Golgari guild cards.
 The fourth pick [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel) makes me feel very confident I'm in the right colour combination and it's an easy decision to take it.

**My Pick:**


**[Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard)**


There's no reason to deviate from the black plan at the moment. I'm still planning on drafting Dimir at this point in time but I'm prepared to go Golgari with a blue splash if that option gets forced upon me. 


With that in mind [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) is clearly the best pick here. The fact that it Transmutes into a 4/4 flyer already isn't something to be overlooked either.


**Pack 1, Pick 6:** 
[Stasis Cell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Cell), [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser), [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard), [Seismic Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Spike), [Elvish Skysweeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Skysweeper), [Boros Recruit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Recruit), [Transluminant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transluminant), [Snapping Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake), [Perilous Forays](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Forays), [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand).


**My Pick:**


**[Snapping Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake)**


This is another pack that boosts my confidence in my colour choice. There are five cards here that could easily make the main deck of a Dimir deck and having that many options for your sixth pick isn't too common. At this point in time I'm fairly certain I won't be planning to mill anyone to death and I therefore want to continue the theme of aggressive, evasive creatures. [Snapping Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Drake) is the best card in the pack to fit in with that theme and I'm happy to add another solid flyer to the card pool.


**Pack 1, Pick 7:** 
[Terrarion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrarion), [Woodwraith Strangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodwraith+Strangler), [Incite Hysteria](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incite+Hysteria), [Scatter the Seeds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scatter+the+Seeds), [Shred Memory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shred+Memory), [Surge of Zeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surge+of+Zeal), [Golgari Brownscale](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Brownscale), [Netherborn Phalanx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Netherborn+Phalanx), [Glass Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glass+Golem).


**My Pick:**


**[Netherborn Phalanx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Netherborn+Phalanx)**


A late [Scatter the Seeds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scatter+the+Seeds) is going to be a nice gift for someone but it isn't a card I'd think about taking at this point. [Netherborn Phalanx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Netherborn+Phalanx) is expensive but I will regularly play it in the main deck. Against Selesnya, and even opposing Golgari decks it can often deal them a lot of a damage when they're sitting behind a horde of Saprolings and I've had several games decided on the back of this card. Sometimes it will sit in the sideboard if the main deck is good enough but it's a main deck card in most situations.


I also really like having another card that can Transmute into something useful. This can always go and fetch a [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser) if the situations warrants it but we might get lucky and open something like a [Cerulean Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerulean+Sphinx) that this will go fetch too.

**[Roofstalker Wight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roofstalker+Wight)**


Another fairly simple pick here. I need two-drops and having a two-drop that can take to the air in the late game is just what the doctor ordered. [Roofstalker Wight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roofstalker+Wight) is an easy choice over [Stasis Cell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Cell).


**Pack 1, Pick 9:** 
[Golgari Rot Farm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm), [Dogpile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dogpile), [Quickchange](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quickchange), [Infectious Host](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infectious+Host), [Selesnya Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Signet), [Dimir Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Signet), [Golgari Thug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Thug).


**My Pick:**


**[Dimir Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Signet)**


I don't like my two-drops to be 1/1s with mediocre abilities, especially when they can cost me a draw step when an opponent happens to have a [Sparkmage Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) in hand. If I get a [Golgari Thug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Thug) very late I might play it but at this point I'd much rather have the Signet to give my four-drops the chance to come out a turn earlier.


**Pack 1, Pick 10:** 
[Golgari Rot Farm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm), [Muddle the Mixture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture), [Grayscaled Gharial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grayscaled+Gharial), [Necromantic Thirst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromantic+Thirst), [Perplex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perplex), [Golgari Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Signet).


**My Pick:**


**[Golgari Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Signet)**


I always like picking up guild lands and Signets as late picks as these help you immensely during deck construction when you're trying to squeeze a third colour into your deck. While I don't have anything that might warrant the inclusion of the [Golgari Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Signet) at this point in time it does open up more possibilities for the rest of the draft and is my choice because of that.


**Late picks:** 
[Undercity Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Shade), [Perplex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perplex), [Surveilling Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surveilling+Sprite), [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser).


There are some very nice late picks here with several of the above cards potentially making the deck. You tend to know for sure that you're in the right colours when the filler cards like [Surveilling Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surveilling+Sprite) and [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser) make it all the way around the table back to you.

### Round 1


![Halcyon Glaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Halcyon+Glaze)I win the flip this game and start with a [Roofstalker Wight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roofstalker+Wight) on turn two and one of the [Halcyon Glaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halcyon+Glaze)s on turn three. My opponent passes his third turn with two Plains and a Mountain open, which just screams [Devouring Light](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Light) to me. As a result I elect to hold back the Glaze and just develop my board with a Signet while attacking for two.
 Another turn of draw, attack, go takes place and then I finally elect to cast an [Undercity Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Shade) on turn five as I figure even if my opponent has the Light, I still then have a Shade and a flyer. Sure enough, he has the [Devouring Light](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Light) and it's bye-bye [Halcyon Glaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halcyon+Glaze). He's on 12 already though, and still facing two evasion men while I still have plenty of gas in hand. He has a [Conclave Equenaut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conclave+Equenaut) on his next turn but the Dismisser undoes that while allowing me to drop him to 12. The next turn the Equenaut takes it's [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp) and that's basically all she wrote.


In game two I again have the [Halcyon Glaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halcyon+Glaze) in hand and elect not to make my Infiltrator on turn two when my opponent inexplicably elects to draw first. The Glaze comes down on turn three and gets powered up by the Infiltrator and the [Undercity Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Shade) on subsequent turns. One of the attacks sees the damage thrown back at me via a [Boros Fury-Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield) but I'm quite happy for my opponent to spend a turn and a card doing that so early in the game. 


[Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) takes care of the Shade but the Glaze continues to hit thanks to a [Surveilling Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surveilling+Sprite). I still have my [Netherborn Phalanx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Netherborn+Phalanx) in hand along with a [Clinging Darkness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clinging+Darkness) that soon takes out an opposing [Ordruun Commando](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordruun+Commando). I then get a little lucky and peel my [Peel from Reality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Peel%2Bfrom%2BReality) (from Reality), which returns my Shade to my hand along with an opposing blocker giving me another guy to switch on the Glaze next turn. My opponent is behind on tempo and lacking answers to either the Shade, the Glaze or even the Infiltrator, and the game ends the following turn with the Phalanx.


### Round 2


Another game, another coin flip won. My opponent is playing Boros again and opens with a [Veteran Armorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorer) and [Viashino Slasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Slasher). This time a second turn [Telling Time](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telling+Time) for me sets up the third turn [Halcyon Glaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halcyon+Glaze). [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) threatens to really make things unfair but I elect to block the [Veteran Armorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorer) with it to not get too behind on the damage race as [Boros Fury-Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield) and [Rally the Righteous](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Righteous) can easily swing close damage races in favour of the Boros deck. My opponent shows me a [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) to finish off [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) but I'm happy with that two-for-one trade.


The [Netherborn Phalanx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Netherborn+Phalanx) shows up to allow the Glaze to deal another four while stalling the ground and dealing three itself. 


![Plague Boiler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Plague+Boiler)My opponent stalled for one turn with a [Festival of the Guildpact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festival+of+the+Guildpact) and at that point I ran out of creatures with him sitting on a precarious two life. I top-decked [Plague Boiler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Boiler) at this point and along with the [Golgari Rot Farm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm) I was able to pop it up to two counters right away and then remove one every turn to stop it blowing up the world. My opponent had to wait to draw some men to get past the Phalanax while I drew lands for a couple of turns and sat behind the [Plague Boiler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Boiler).
 I finally draw a creature â€“ a [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) â€“ but my opponent had a [Boros Fury-Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield) for the subsequent [Halcyon Glaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halcyon+Glaze) attack. He made a mistake playing it though, as he waited until damage was on the stack and perhaps wasn't aware of the interaction with my freshly cast [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard). Because he waited until damage was on the stack I was able to simply sacrifice the [Halcyon Glaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halcyon+Glaze) to the House Guard and fizzle the [Boros Fury-Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield). Damage then resolved normally to take game one. If he'd just cast the Shield before combat damage I couldn't really sacrifice the Glaze (as I needed it to win) and he would've prevented its damage and dealt me four, definitely giving him at least another turn.


On to game two, where my opponent led with [Veteran Armorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorer) while I had my Infiltrator to block. Two [Viashino Slasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Slasher)s combined nicely with the Armorer and became even more threatening when a [Sunhome, Fortress of the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome%2C+Fortress+of+the+Legion) showed up for my opponent. My hand was full of tricks though with [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel), [Peel from Reality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peel+from+Reality) and [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp) all present. The next activation of the Sunhome on a creature met with the [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel) and a wasted turn from my opponent. A Glaze then showed up to provide some offence. 


Just as I was planning to activate the Glaze the second came off the top! I obviously waited a turn before casting my guys. A [Surveilling Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surveilling+Sprite) let the Glazes hit for eight, then a [Roofstalker Wight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roofstalker+Wight) let them hit for eight more. During my opponent's next turn I blocked both his men with the Sprite and [Roofstalker Wight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roofstalker+Wight) and used the Peel to bounce one of his unblocked guys and my Wight. The Wight let the Hazes hit for eight more and make sure one [Boros Fury-Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield) would not be enough to stop a second 2-0 win.


### Round 3


![Hex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hex)The first game was all about setting up [Hex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hex). I drew it on turn two while casting my [Surveilling Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surveilling+Sprite). I had another Boros opponent this round but his [Boros Recruit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Recruit) and [Veteran Armorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorer) stayed at home when a third turn Infiltrator came down for me. A [Skyknight Legionnaire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire) started hitting and then a second showed up to join it. I took the damage for one turn while making a [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) of my own. My opponent was then kind enough to make a [Viashino Fangtail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Fangtail) that allowed me to [Hex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hex) away all five of his men along with my [Surveilling Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surveilling+Sprite). Hopefully no further explanation is required as to the outcome of that game!
 The second game demonstrated the value of the two Signets that were included. A second turn Signet allowed me to get [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) in play against an opposing [Nightguard Patrol](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightguard+Patrol). My opponent didn't even try and bluff a trick with an attack and the Patrol stayed at home. I had an [Undercity Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Shade) on turn four and then the [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser) on turn five. There's not much an opponent can do about that draw and with no removal coming from his side of the board the game ended on turn seven. 


### Wrapping Up


Overall the deck felt very powerful. The two [Halcyon Glaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Halcyon%2BGlaze) showed up a lot but with two in the deck and some card drawing too that isn't all that surprising. They were both excellent and dealt tons of damage. Getting them both quite late suggests maybe people are undervaluing them slightly but I doubt that'll continue for long.


The other cards in the deck all fulfilled their role. It's interesting that I often had the [Netherborn Phalanx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Netherborn%2BPhalanx) in hand but never once needed to Transmute it into the [Hex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hex). Perhaps if I had been up against a Selesnya deck things would've been different. With the Phalanx and the card drawing in the deck I could've potentially cast [Hex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hex) in every match with this deck and feel confident that the deck could've taken on anything.


The last card I want to note is [Vedalken Dismisser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Dismisser). This guy is a fine inclusion in any aggressive deck and yet I somehow managed to pull one 15th pick. Some people perhaps ignore him as a six mana 2/2 but the â€˜[Time Ebb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Ebb)' effect is powerful and getting a 2/2 body attached isn't something to complain about. Overall the card is quite fairly costed; think how good it would be compared to [Kiri-Onna](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiri-Onna) from *Saviours of Kamigawa* at only five mana.


The sheer number of evasion creatures in this deck was very good, with 11 of the 14 men being able to attack through the defence of most decks. I think that too is a key factor when it comes to drafting the damage-based Dimir decks. [Roofstalker Wight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roofstalker+Wight) and [Dimir Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Infiltrator) are both solid little two drops that are useful in the early game while still able to deal damage when drawn later on too. 


Overall, a very nice deck and a 6-0 game performance to back it up. Dimir was definitely underdrafted at that table and it was clearly the right colours to be in. Next time you open up a [Moroii](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moroii) this is exactly the sort of deck you might want to think about aiming for.
