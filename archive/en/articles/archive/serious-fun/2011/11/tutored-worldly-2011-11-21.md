Tutored Worldly
===============



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on November 22, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






During last weekend there was some hyped-up event called [Worlds](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/2011-world-championships). Junya Iyanaga won the whole shebang, and Team Japan stands atop the heap of nations in attendance.


It's just a bunch of no-funs who take things too seriously. Who's cares, right?


[![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_worlds.jpg)](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/2011-world-championships)


I care. Let me explain.


I'm extremely fortunate to live in an area with great **Magic** stores, a buffet of events competitive and casual, and a tight-knit community of players. Many of these players are stateside and international travelers who have followed the longstanding decree of **Magic**'s Organized Play: play the game, see the world. Many of these players have a passion for **Magic** without rival.


Many of them are my friends.


I rarely join the main show at [Pro Tour Qualifiers](http://magic.wizards.com/en/protour/preliminaryqualifiers) and [Grand Prix events](http://magic.wizards.com/content/eventsschedulegrand-prix), but I do enjoy watching, discussing, and playing **Magic** with friends all the same. Competition isn't for me, but that doesn't mean there isn't something for me at competitions.


To start, many of my friends would be there already.


Through Twitter, online message boards, Facebook, and plain old email, I've forged friendships from around the globe. They, too, often attend these places of deck worship. And they, too, aren't always interested in intellectual jousting.


Many of my friends were already there to just have fun.


The world of **Magic** is wonderful.


For Those of You about to Rock
==============================


To showcase that events are about people, not just prizes, I'm going to share a few friends that met up, using an assortment of decks to demo.


It wasn't long ago when everything got a little hazy (and surreal) after our visit to Innistrad. Two representative decks were chosen to clash, one a gaggle of Zombies led by [Grimgrin, Corpse-Born](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimgrin%2C+Corpse-Born), the other a motley crew of Werewolves and, oddly, [Ixidron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixidron). The theme here is quite obvious: copy, borrow, and occasionally steal others' stuff. I've seen every type of against-my-grain blue Commander deck:


* The [Control Magic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Control+Magic) family reunion
* [Erayo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erayo), and everything else, says "No!"
* Breaking all the rules, taking all the turns

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Control+Magic)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Control+Magic) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Erayo%2C+Soratami+Ascendant)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erayo%2C+Soratami+Ascendant)
Blue has the worst reputation of any color for Commander (at least in my book). Complaining or aggravating other players doesn't build bridges either. I chose to lead by example with a blue deck that brings out the fun. We're still doing powerful things:



> 
> * Making larger-than-normal amounts of mana
> * Getting an expensive creature at a discount through copying
> * Attacking with well-equipped, hard-to-kill creatures
>

We Salute You
=============


Equipped with a deck for every face I could meet, it was time to find friends I've made in other places. The first was @[inkwell\_looter](http://twitter.com/inkwell_looter), the man behind [Unfinished Business](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/week-was/unfinished-business-2010-12-16).


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic01.jpg)


At random, Mr. Looter selected to command Zombies in battle, leading off with [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul)s and an [Endless Ranks of the Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+Ranks+of+the+Dead).


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic02.jpg)


While the Zombie engine started turning over, the Werewolves got *mean*. A [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) transformed into [Howlpack Alpha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+Alpha) for me, and a twosome of [Cackling Counterpart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart) let me have three [Howlpack Alpha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+Alpha)s—two of which were tokens and thus couldn't ever transform back—by the end of a turn.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic03.jpg)


Things were bleak for him already, but I looted the victory when [Ixidron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixidron) sealed the deal.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic04.jpg)


But the real victory was for the inkwell. He brought a stack of fantastic business cards he created for me.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic15.jpg)


He certainly appreciates the kind words I'll be shared about him for a while!


**Score:** Werewolves 1, Zombies 0


The next meet-and-greet was a purely Internet companion thus far: Ken (@[kenfortson](http://twitter.com/#!/kenfortson)). While we weren't firing off the *Ravnica / Guildpact / Dissension* draft [he was so interested in](http://twitter.com/#!/kenfortson/status/136823473120088064), he was still willing to lend a hand in determining if hairy or horrid was the king of *Innistrad*'s darkness.


Similar to Inkwell Looter's game above, Ken lead with a Zombie and [Endless Ranks of the Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+Ranks+of+the+Dead). My howlers, however, had other plans: [Gatstaf Shepherd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatstaf+Shepherd) and double [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck).


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic05.jpg)


Ken valiantly continued, soon landing his haymaker in [Grimgrin, Corpse-Born](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimgrin%2C+Corpse-Born). But the bundle of oversized Wolf tokens being produces out-swarmed the shamblers.


Thanks for the help, Ken.


**Score:** Werewolves 2, Zombies 0


Another formerly digital-only dude, Dave (@[dastels\_mtg](http://twitter.com/#!/dastels_mtg)), drummed up a hello and deal. We swapped some cards we had both been hunting and got down to gaming in short order.


A small aside: travelling with my wife had some unusual advantages and disadvantages. While she patiently waited in line to have an artist sign a few cards, she also tipped Dave off that "the blue-box deck" had already lost two games against "the brown one." Dave chose accordingly.


In what became a clear pattern, Dave led off with a [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck), transformed it, and went on to create a [Cackling Counterpart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart).


"Not so fast," was a welcome interjection from the Zombies, as I had a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to slow things down. With three [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton)s playing the part of zombie robots (awesome!), things looked up... until Dave had a duo of [Gatstaf Shepherd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatstaf+Shepherd)s, one transformed into [Gatstaf Howler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatstaf+Howler), and a pack of Wolf tokens from the unanswered [Howlpack Alpha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+Alpha).



Faced with casting either a [Grimgrin, Corpse-Born](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimgrin%2C+Corpse-Born) or [Endless Ranks of the Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+Ranks+of+the+Dead), I just couldn't muster enough bodies to block.


And, yes, my wife found this pleasing.


**Score:** Werewolves 3, Zombies 0


I finally ran into someone I'd physically met before when Uriah (@[CMDRDecks](http://twitter.com/#!/CMDRDecks)) popped in.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic08.jpg)


He's the man behind the on-going YouTube series [CMDRDecks](http://www.youtube.com/user/CMDRDecks) and someone I always enjoy bouncing Commander ideas off of. It helps that he's a really nice guy too.


In the interest of fairness, I went ahead and tipped him off to the running Zombie-Werewolf record. Uriah, too, ran with the hairy odds. Off with a sprint, Uriah had a [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) and [Villagers of Estwald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald), and gladly took a turn off to transform them into [Howlpack Alpha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+Alpha) and [Howlpack of Estwald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald) when the Zombies failed to even show up to shuffle about.


I thought I earned a reprieve to get back into things when I cast a [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) into [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul), putting the lycanthropes back to sunny-side up. Uriah decided to just moon me right back.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic09.jpg)


That was a lethal level of mauling.


**Score:** Werewolves 4, Zombies 0


The flow of videographers continued with Rich Castle (@[richardfcastle](http://twitter.com/#!/richardfcastle)), the man inside [Inside the Deck](http://www.insidethedeck.com/). I wanted Rich to have a good time as he usually spends entire events simply filming, so I selected the Zombie deck for myself.


He was able to go first, but led with back-to-back [Hinterland Harbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hinterland+Harbor)s. That slow mana was exactly what the Zombie deck wanted as two [Cemetery Reaper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Reaper)s, [Moan of the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moan+of+the+Unhallowed), and Endless Ranks of the Undead all appeared for me.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic14.jpg)


Rich was able to follow up with a double dose of [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck), but an [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton) and [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul) locked up the game for the flesh-craving swarm.


Sorry, Rich!


**Score:**  Werewolves 4, Zombies 1


With the clear victory going to the Werewolf family between the *Innistrad* titans, it was a relief to run into Nate, better known on Twitter as @[5wordfi5h](http://twitter.com/#!/5wordfi5h) and one of the creators of [a very entertaining video](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zHJgjz5_DIs).


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic10.jpg)


After creeping along at a steady stumble, my blue Commander deck leapt from my hands: [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring), [Swiftfoot Boots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swiftfoot+Boots), and [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power) set to blue made up my rapid-fire development.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic11.jpg)


Nate's [Rubinia Soulsinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rubinia+Soulsinger) deck wouldn't mind getting a boost to blue, but it struggled to give Nate any [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s. With a [Charging Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charging+Troll) out, he went to up the ante against my morph with [Armadillo Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak). My morph happened to be [Willbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender) and, as you'd suspect, I soon had the Cloak on my 'bender. A [Copy Artifact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Copy+Artifact) for the [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power), with Nate still missing [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s, made things a little unfair.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic12.jpg)


But tables can turn. While my [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) was face up (and therefore unable to take advantage of acting like another [Willbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender)), Nate placed a [Bear Umbra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bear+Umbra) onto his [Charging Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charging+Troll). It brought my attacks to a stalemate as he was able to pound in and untap lands.


His [Jhessian Balmgiver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhessian+Balmgiver) drew out one of my few steal effects, [Dominate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dominate), but it left the way clear for his [Rafiq of the Many](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rafiq+of+the+Many) off the [Gate to the Æther](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gate+to+the+%C3%86ther) I cast.


Jackpot!


[Sakashima the Imposter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakashima+the+Imposter) came down as a reflection of Rafiq, and with a little [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice) juggling, my [Willbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender) with the Cloak became an unblockable fatty with double strike.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf170_pic13.jpg)


"At least my cards helped someone win!"


Nate is ever the classy guy. Thanks again!


The Pick of Destiny
===================


Worlds was a fantastic time, and teased out even more of my desire to attend the biggest events in **Magic**. You don't have to plan on battle, put on your war paint, or trade fiercely for the last few cards you need. Just grab your decks, team up with friends, and take a trip to experience **Magic** where the kitchen tables stretch further than you can imagine.


I hope to see a few more friends on the next adventure!
