Return to Standard
==================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on October 25, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Welcome back to Perlious Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive **Magic Online** column. Standard seems to have been shrouded in mystery since the release of *Return to Ravnica*. Now, with the data acquired from **Magic Online** events, we're finally able to take an objective look at the new metagame. 

 The new Standard is a tough cookie to crack. [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) decks of all varieties seemed to dominate the format in the first two weeks. Hyper-aggressive black decks were being played by so many people that most lists looked pre-sideboarded for the Zombie matchup last weekend. 

 Things change quickly, though. The brewers of the world quickly caught on to the staleness of the format and suddenly there were herds of [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s stampeding all over the Zombie decks. That's where we are now. [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) has become the format-defining spell and players are being forced to build their decks accordingly. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr218_thragtusk.jpg)
[Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Nils%20Hamm%22%5D)


Here's a breakdown of the 4–0 decklists from October 19–21 by archetype:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr218_standard_chart.jpg) Jund Midrange decks are the big winner this week. The new big dog on the block is similar to the dominant strategy from *Innistrad* Block Constructed. The deck aims to grind out opponents with efficient removal and transition into a deck that piles on bomby monsters in the mid- to late game. All of the lists are very different, but there are a few commonalities that should make the deck recognizable. Four [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s is a must in a format where mono-red and aggressive [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) decks are enjoying some degree of success.

[Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) is also being seen in some number across the board. Now that [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) is gone from Standard, 3 toughness is the new 4 toughness, and [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) is the type of card that can completely dominate a game when an opponent gets caught without an appropriate removal spell. [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) proves itself to be Constructed worthy, finding its way into most lists.  i\_b\_TRUE's list seems to be exactly what I'd want to play in a relatively unknown format. The deck has answers to just about everything and it forces its opponents to play a long game, which will inevitably be taken over by [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) or [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections). The deck struggles with some of the newer ramp strategies that people are playing, but the incredible matchups everywhere else and the deck's natural resistance to any kind of hate make it an excellent choice for the upcoming Standard season.

 Jund Midrange was a reasonably obvious deck for the new format, given its success in the previous Block Constructed season. The next most successful archetype was made possible by *Return to Ravnica*'s deceptively powerful [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage). [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) has been used and abused since it first entered the fray of Constructed last year, but the latest version of the deck seems to be more consistent and less combo-driven than its predecessors. The newest [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) deck plays enough ramp to reliably cast [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) by turn four. The deck takes advantage of Standard's newest default top-end, [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity). [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) had been popping up in a lot of different lists and this deck seems to abuse it more than any other.  HyenaCJY's version of the deck is incredibly powerful. [Garruk, Primal Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) provides a steady stream of board presence and unparalleled card advantage when the deck encounters a board stall. [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) will often allow the deck to alpha strike for lethal damage, but the card can often be used to grind out wins against Jund Midrange opponents. When playing against decks with tons of removal it's usually correct to remove a bunch of your own creatures from your graveyard to get some free card advantage if your opponent happens to have the necessary [Dreadbore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore) to deal with your Angel. This deck has no obvious bad matchups in the current format and the list is surprisingly resilient to hate. Cards like [Rakdos Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Charm) might help stop the [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), but the deck's normal game plan can often be enough to close a game all by itself.

 Some players have opted to play red in their [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) decks. This gives the pilot access to [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars); [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells); [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting); and [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight). Sai199orz managed to go undefeated in multiple daily events this past weekend using this version of the deck. The deck might look like it's sacrificing consistency for power, but the [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting)s actually improve the deck's ability to reliably do broken things very early.  Aggressive red strategies have been lacking the necessary number of one-drops needed to be competitive, but *Return to Ravnica*'s [Rakdos Cackler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) provides the deck with another powerful one-mana creature to more reliably start some quick aggression. [Pyreheart Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) makes an appearance in Mono-Red as a very reasonable plan against the [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) metagame. I wouldn't be surprised if we started seeing more copies of [Pyreheart Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) in the main deck of red decks in the coming weeks. The most aggressive archetypes of the new format have their backs against the wall in a world of [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), but it looks like they're adapting. I wouldn't be comfortable trying to race from 20 to 0 when everyone is gaining 5, but these decks are well-positioned to make a comeback if the herds of [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) start moving on to greener pastures.

 If you're worried that Standard is about to be about board stalls and a race to the first unanswered [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) or [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), then this deck is your savior. The deck gains an early lead with cards like [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and aims to keep the opposing board empty with efficient counterspells and cheap removal. [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) lets the deck bash through [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), a card that is otherwise extremely difficult for the deck to beat. [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) is extremely versatile and, in combination with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), makes the game a nightmare for your opponent. It's just too hard to play around. I wouldn't be surprised if this deck made some waves in the coming week thanks to its better-than-even matchup against Jund Midrange and [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) decks.
