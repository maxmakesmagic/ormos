The Green Team
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 1, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











A little while back, here on Daily Decks, we talked about [a new deck](/en/articles/archive/elf-surge-2012-07-19) that looked to abuse [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge). Now, [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) is quite the unique card and building around it can be tough, making for decks that are likely just below the competitive threshold, but still offer a lot of fun and potential. Within the shell that contained [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge), though, secretly a competitive deck might have been hiding out the whole time. Once you can move past the ten-mana sorcery, the bunch of Elves that remained actually looked pretty formidable. In order to see their true potential, [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) would have to stop being the focal point of the deck...

![Primal Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Primal+Surge)Robert Castro did just that at a recent TCGPlayer.com Open in Providence, Rhode Island. Robert decided that while [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) was cute, what he really wanted to do was make pointy-eared friends and beat down. Robert recognized that while Elves are a strong tribal deck with plenty of lords to choose from and plenty of support to fill out a decklist, by going strictly tribal you miss out on some of the best reasons to go green. So rather than cut ramp entirely, this list kept just enough to offer that mid- to late-game pressure, without being so all-in that it was trying to hit ten each game. [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth), [Soul of the Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+the+Harvest), [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), and [Silklash Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silklash+Spider) are all strong cards to naturally build toward or fetch out with your reliable [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)s. The deck can kick everything into a whole different gear with [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave), which offers a robust late-game option.

As for the Elves themselves, they are far from shy here. The duo of [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) provide the deck with a lot of firepower right out of the gate, allowing the deck to accelerate straight into three mana. From there, the lords take over, with [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) being the scariest of the bunch. Beyond just the Elf stat boost, Archdruid allows for the deck's most explosive draws, even allowing a potential turn-four kill thanks to a [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton) and [Ezuri, Renegade Leader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ezuri%2C+Renegade+Leader) also fill in the parent role, allowing all of your small Elves to get that much bigger. This double-pronged approach allows the deck to transition between beatdown and combo pretty easily, making the Elf deck a blast to pilot at just about any level of play!
