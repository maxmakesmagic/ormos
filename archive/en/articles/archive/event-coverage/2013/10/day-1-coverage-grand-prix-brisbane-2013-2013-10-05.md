Day 1 Coverage of Grand Prix Brisbane 2013
==========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 5, 2013 










![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/closing.jpg)Brisbane: 466 players stepped up this morning to be considered for the title of Grand Prix Brisbane Champion, but now only 80 remain, the rest will have to console themselves with the awesome public events available here tomorrow instead. That's a record-breaking attendance for a constructed Grand Prix in Australia, too, breaking the previous constructed Grand Prix attendance record we set last time we were in Brisbane.


Undefeated at 8-0 are Dillon Kikkawa, Daniel Ball, and Wilfy Horig, and while I'd love to show you their decklists now – there's a [Jin-Gitaxias, Core Augur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jin-Gitaxias%2C+Core+Augur) in there – it wouldn't be fair to give that info to the players right behind them in the standings, including Jake Hart, Garry Wong, Justin Cheung, Zen Takahashi, Jack Ding, Daniel Bretherton, Henry Moore, and Dan Unwin. Also playing on day two of note are Simon Harnden, Robert Liu, Luke Mulcahy, Thomas Rafferty, Dylan Brown, Matthew Anderson, Ross Schafer, Robert Jackway, and Andrew Vance.


Join us tomorrow as we dig into the Grand Prix Brisbane day two Modern metagame, and see who has what it takes to fight through to the Top 8. We'll even bring you the top 16 decklists!

Modern is a curious beast, as formats go. It's broad enough that the introduction of newer cards is less likely to shake it up as much as other formats, but RnD's willingness to ban cards that dare to step out of line even a little bit does manage to keep things interesting.


Defined first and foremost by the availability of great mana, Modern is a format where players are free to play 3 or 4 colors easily, thanks to lands like [Verdant Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) and [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground). After that, the Combo decks establish the speed of the format, looking to do a variety of weird and wonderful things in the pursuit of ending the game by turn 4 or 5, like:


* Playing a [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) on a [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) or a [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), or pointing [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) at one of them, allowing them to put as many copies of that creature into play as they like, and attacking for lethal right away.
* Getting 6 to 7 land into play quickly, so they can sacrifice them all to a [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), fetching forth a [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) or two, and enough Mountains to roast their opponent on the spot.
* Facilitating a dangerous storm count with [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) and [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) and taking them from 20 to 0 with [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot).
* Playing and sacrificing a series of small artifacts like [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) and [Chromatic Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere), only to repeatedly bring them back with [Faith's Reward](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Reward), before finishing them off with a solitary [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb) over and over and over again.
* Dumping a [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) into their graveyard, and bringing it back with a [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance), or just plan dropping it into play with a [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach), to let them draw enough cards they can find a [Fury of the Horde](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fury+of+the+Horde) to attack again. Or doing the same with an [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn). One attack with [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) is often enough.
* And blurring the lines between this category and the next, a base-green deck that plays like a midrange deck, but can suddenly kill you out of nowhere with [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast), a [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap), and some way to repeatedly sacrifice it.

Everything else needs to abide by the clock set by the combo decks in whatever way they see fit. Midrange decks, usually based in Green for [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), often pair with Black for targeted discard like [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) to deny the combo decks their key pieces.

Blue/White decks and Blue/Red can also fit in this category, disrupting their opponents with [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique)s and [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s. Traditionally, any deck based in Blue would have been a control deck, but between Blue having access to good aggressive creatures, and the fact that giving combo decks as long as they like to try and win is just a very bad idea, these decks are more often considered Aggro Control decks instead.


Aggressive decks try to outrace the combo decks by amassing a huge amount of power on the table in one way or another. Artifact-based Aggro decks attack with small creatures with large [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating)s, while Infect decks aim to deliver 10 poison counters as quickly as possible with the help of [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and [Groundswell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundswell). Even the Green/Black Midrange decks can get aggressive if need be, with giant [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s, [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze)s, and [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman)'s digging up the killing blows from the graveyard.


With such a wide range of decks available, it seems that players are largely free to choose a play style that best suits them. Last month in Detroit, 6 of the Top 8 were Green/Black Midrange decks that tasted vaguely of Jund, while the other 2 were a MeliraPod deck and an Artifact Aggro deck. Close behind them in the Top 16 were [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), Blue/Red [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), Blue/Red/White [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), Naya Midrange, and a few more Jund decks. How closely will our Top 16 resemble that? Follow along with us this weekend while we find out!

Round 3 Feature Match - Isaac Egan vs. Gene Brumby
--------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

Gene Brumby and Isaac Egan were both off to good starts to the weekend, Egan piloting a U/W/R deck packed with high-powered instants, Brumby running a Jund list stuffed to the gills with removal and utility creatures.


Brumby led the game off with a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), and then took a peek at Egan's arsenal with a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). Seeing a hand jam-packed with medium-range creatures and a little utility, Brumby nabbed a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). Brumby's Confidant fed him a [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), which partnered with a [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) to clear out Egan's creatures as fast as he could summon them.


Egan stumbled on land, and Brumby mounted more pressure with a [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) before sealing the first game with a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), a classic example of what happens when a Jund deck's plan comes together.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/r3_brumby.jpg)  
*Brumby contemplates Egan's creatures' fiery demise.* 
Brumby opened the second game with an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), following up with a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) to nab Egan's freshly-drawn [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). When he tried a [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), Egan had drawn another Command, keeping the Planeswalker at bay and digging for more answers.


Egan's [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) finished off a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), but Brumby recovered with a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), pushing for aggressive card advantage against Egan's tempo-tastic brew. Egan, keeping in the race, baited out some removal with a [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) before laying another.


With his life perilously low, Egan had to point a [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) at one of Brumby's men, calculating whether to risk Brumby drawing out with the Confidant or gaining an edge off a Shaman. The point proved moot, as Brumby was able to unleash a flurry of burn to seal the final game.


"The games went about as well as I could hope", said Brumby after the match. He'd sideboarded out his higher-end spells for more aggressive cards, Egan unable to keep pace with the diverse threats the Jund deck presented.


### Gene Brumby 2 - Isaac Egan 0

Round 5 Feature Match- John-Paul Kelly vs. Daniel Unwin
-------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

Kelly and Unwin sat down to a 73-card mirror, having tested against each other on **Magic Online** for the event. "We'd checked out *Theros* carefully for cards that might disrupt the format", Unwin said, "And we didn't see anything that scared us too badly." Both were playing Junk decks, running a powerful G/B frame and splashing White for some utility and pinpoint removal, aiming to grind opposing decks out through relentless card advantage and powerful, undercosted creatures. This is exactly what happened in both games, victory being clinched by accumulative margins as opposed to the blow-outs so feared in Modern matchups.


Kelly started the match with a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), stripping a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) out of Unwin's hand, but letting him return fire with an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek). Kelly fanned his hand, grinned, and welcomed Unwin to "Tank Town".


Unwin surveyed a hand stacked with goodies, peering over [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay), [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), 2x [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and a [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena). Kelly had kept a risky one-lander packed with goodness, but it seemed like it might pay off as Kelly drew a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman). Unwin had one of his own, Kelly one turn ahead in summoning a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). Unwin had a [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) to lay some flying beats, while Kelly drew into another land and summoned a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).


Unwin pulled ahead by flashing back his [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). Both players took out the others' [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s, but Unwin's army crashing through the air put him firmly at an advantage. Stymied for land, Kelly's hand was clogged with powerful 2-drops - none of whom were able to deal with Unwin's array of fliers.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/r5_unwin.jpg)  
*Unwin plots how to edge ahead of Kelly in his mirror-match.* 
Unwin powered out of the gates with a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and a [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), while Kelly had an early [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena). Unwin unloaded a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). Kelly killed the Confidant with an [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and summoned a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) of his own. Unwin [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember)ed Kelly's [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and attacked with his team, eager to apply pressure.


Kelly cast and flashed back [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) in a single turn, building an army against Unwin's 3/4 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). When Kelly quad-blocked the next attack, Unwin flashed another [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) to take out one of the Spirits, then summoned a [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll). Kelly had another round of [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), but Unwin was having none of it, piling on more attacks until Kelly scooped up his cards.


After the game, both players discussed their sideboarding strategies, comparing the relative benefits of leaving [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) in against decks with [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). Kelly had sided his out for more creatures, unwilling to potentially feed Unwin's graveyard with useful cards. Unwin preferred the raw power of the Planeswalker, and she had stood him in good stead in the final game.


### Daniel Unwin 2 - John-Paul Kelly 0

Saturday, 5:15 p.m. – Five Minutes with Raymond Swanland
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Event Coverage Staff*

 

Raymond Swanland is an artist whom many fans are increasingly paying attention to, admiring his powerful, active style and unerring ability to land amazing cards to paint. Notable amongst them are [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) and [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa), as well as perennial casual and Commander favourites like [Thraximundar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraximundar) and [Banefire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire).


When we caught up with him he was busy sketching a Tony Tiger - of Frosty Flakes fame - onto a playmat for a grinning fan. He was kind enough to spare us a few words.


### Let's start with a toughie - what's your favourite **Magic** illustration?


It'd have to be [Wolfir Silverheart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) - it's a great piece of artwork. It came out a little less aggressive, less bestial, while still showing a tremendous amount of strength and power. It's a nice composition, with a good balance between detail and busyness.



![Wolfir Silverheart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wolfir+Silverheart)

### Do you play **Magic** yourself?


I used to when I was younger, but not so much these days - I've got so many projects on the go!


### Your professional career started by you working on the Oddworld series. How did you transition from there to working on **Magic** cards?


I started doing some work for Wizards with their Forgotten Realms line, doing illustrations and book covers for those. After I'd worked with them a while, Art Director Jeremy Jarvis approached me about doing some illustrations for **Magic**, and things kind of went from there.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/artist.jpg)  
*Raymond Swanland* 
### What do you like about doing artwork for **Magic**?


I really love the creative freedom. Wizards' art department, and Jeremy in particular, are great at managing artists - giving us exactly the right amount of direction, as well as a hefty amount of freedom. It's inspiring to work for a company where the quality of artwork is so high - there's a lot of friendly competition between artists at Wizards to lift our games and produce work that really shines.


### Thanks for your time!


No worries.

Round 8 Feature Match - Robert Jackway vs. Dave Crewe
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*

 

Crewe led off with a [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), while Jackway passed after searching up a [Blood Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt). A [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) finished off Crewe's Hierarch, while an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) took care of a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), leaving Crewe with a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) and a [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to work with.


Crewe summoned a pair of Deathrite Shamen, who were both [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse)d to oblivion. Jackway was comfortably ahead on cards as he summoned a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and zapped Crewe's fresh [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) with a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). A [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) from Jackway put him even further ahead on cards. Crewe sent the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) on a [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and Jackway summoned a [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), forcing Crewe to discard his [Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel).


Jackway tried another [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), only to see Crewe with a [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) and a [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) stranded in hand, hampered by an un-cooperative manabase. Jackway mounted pressure with a [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine), and Crewe, unable to muster a defence, moved to his sideboard.


### Game 2


Crewe led off with a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), while Jackway had a [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman). Crewe powered up with a [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). Jackway tried an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) but whiffed, Crewe proudly showing a pile of non-targets. Jackway followed up with a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) to get rid of a [Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless), letting Crewe enter his third turn with a Pod and Birds on the board.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri13/r8_jackway.jpg)  
*Robert Jackway* 
Crewe activated his [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), trading his [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) up for a [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence). Jackway got his own funky 2-drop in the form of a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), while Crewe traded up his [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) for a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), and then a [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll), Jackway keeping pace with a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and a [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze), stopping Crewe's graveyard shenanigans from getting out of hand.


Crewe decided to head through the air instead, summoning a [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) and swinging, knocking Jackway down to a perilous life total. Crewe then upgraded his [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll) to an [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) before summoning a [Spike Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder). Jackway [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate)d the [Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel), then passed with a couple of mana open. Crewe cast and flashed back a [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), making his [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) token impressively large. A swing for lethal loomed, and both players shuffled up for the decider.


### Game 3


Crewe's exploratory [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) was quickly zapped by a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). Jackway summoned a pair of Deathrite Shamen to get the party started, following up with a [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze). Crewe cast a [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), which Jackway removed before he could flash back.



![Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll)

Crewe summoned [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll). "Darn it," Jackway exclaimed, "I was thinking *anything but [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll)!*". Crewe laughed and passed, Jackway furrowing his brow at the troublesome legend. Jackway summoned a second [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze), but otherwise couldn't get past [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll), passing with no action. Crewe passed back. At the end of Crewe's turn, Jackway [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)ed him to 5 life, threatening trouble.


After another turn of nothing from Jackway, Crewe cast a [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) for 4, searching up a [Linvala, Keeper of Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence). Jackway used a [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) to off the troublesome Angel, and, with the Deathrite Shamen threatening to clear out the graveyard for lethal, Crewe scooped, wishing Jackway good luck in Day 2.


A few minutes after packing up post-match, Crewe pondered whether he had conceded too hastily. Re-checking the math, it turns out Crewe may have been able to use the lifegain from a [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze)'s ability to squeeze in another turn or two - but, as he wryly pointed out, the line of play wasn't obvious at the time, highlighting the complexities of this wide-open, highly interactive format.
