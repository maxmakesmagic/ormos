![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











A couple of weekends back saw *Zendikar* Game Day, where players around the world polished off their [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx)es and [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest)s in *Zendikar* Standard action. We have [deck lists](/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/zendikargameday/welcome) from at least four continents at this point; we are going to highlight some of the more interesting decks.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130606011739im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td64_neo3Cards.jpg)  
Now if you believe "the Internet" you might think of Standard as a kind of stripey Neopolitan format; but instead of the familiar chocolate, vanilla, and strawberry in the metagame's ice cream box (with the chocolate generally missing), you might expect Jund, nothing but Jund (okay, with a little Boros Bushwhacker and the odd Vampires deck playing cherry or whipped cream). But Game Day shows us that Standard has more interesting options than just [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax)es and [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk)s; following are a half a dozen of them.

We're going to open up with some pretty straightforward ideas. Will Watkins took some of the new cards from *Zendikar*—and mixed them up with existing ***Magic** 2010* and *Shards* block tools—and ran with them. And we mean *ran.*

Will's deck is a study in haste. He can open up on [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) (remind me to come back to that), follow up with [Hellspark Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental), then [Ball Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning), then mana screw you with [Goblin Ruinblaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ruinblaster) and look like an all-star doing it!



|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |

All of Will's creatures have haste (and even some of his non-creature action, like [Elemental Appeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Appeal), follows the theme), so he didn't go with [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede). That might have been a nice inclusion with those [Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa)s and [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn)s ... but then again, [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) (as [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel)-y as it can be) could prove a liability against his own [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake)s and so on. Anyway, there's nothing wrong with a 9/1 [Ball Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning) bouncing into the red (and we mean *red*) zone off a [Teetering Peaks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks).

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130606004443im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td64_3Cards1.jpg)  
In case you hadn't thought about it recently .... How great a card is [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning)? Absolutely famous, right? I have been playing with it happily for as long as the new cards have been legal, but it wasn't until I started writing this paragraph that I realized that [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) is *strictly better than [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock).* Wow! This is a card that can take out a [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) or [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) on the first turn, or turn around and act like the better part of a [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) (but one that can go to the face) later in the game. Just a great card.

So ... back to [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide). 

How about an early firestarter: How do you guys feel about this card? Certainly it fits with Will's aggressive theme, and helps take decent-sized chunks out of the opponent to pull him into the range of those [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s and [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning)s ... But personally, I actually *like* playing against [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide). Then again, I am the kind of person who will play 28 lands in a Standard deck (more on that later).

Twenty-two lands?

Even with [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra)... Seems like a stretch. Those Angels *define* pricey.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130606011300im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td64_3Cards2.jpg)  
But look over to the right hand columns and you will see that Paul has little interest in actually casting his gigantic Angels. This deck is all about [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap)!

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Summoning+Trap)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap)  
Here's the thing: There aren't a lot of true control decks in the Standard format right now; not the kind that actually go about countering a lot of creature spells, that is. So [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) is usually going to involve the retail counter rather than the free trial model, if you know what I mean. 

But that's okay!

Six is cheaper than nine, and with [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) can come online turn three with a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). No problem.

I direct you to new card [Trapmaker's Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trapmaker%27s+Snare).

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trapmaker%27s+Snare)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trapmaker%27s+Snare)  
*What a pounding!*

Holy instant speed, Batman!

This is the kind of card that can really put an opponent on tilt, ruin his day, you know: the good stuff. Say your opponent *is* one of these cats who has an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) in his opening hand. You can run out a creature, get it countered, then just cast [Trapmaker's Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trapmaker%27s+Snare) to go get the [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap). Instant speed and only two mana? A very economical option.

One of the things that I have trying to uncover in the current Standard is an inheritor to [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) Beatdown. [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) was one of the best decks from the pre-*Zendikar* Standard, a great performer over multiple National championships and a fine PTQ deck both. However the deck has lost its popularity with the rotation of [Demigod of Revenge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge) and [Figure of Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny), and of course the invigorated status of Jund.

Many players probably haven't considered a black-red [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) Beatdown deck because there is a black-red-*green* one to be had. [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) is my pick for best overall card in the current environment, and Jund smashes a [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) in the opponent's grill handily.

However there is a lot of incentive to playing a streamlined, attack-oriented, two-color [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) deck instead of a three-color one with more card advantage. One of those is the ability to get absolutely unbeatable draws against control.

How do you feel about [Warren Instigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warren+Instigator) pummeling out a *pair* of [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander)s on the third turn? Just filthy.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130606014719im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/td/td64_3Cards3.jpg)  
The attack in this deck is super focused on Goblins, making the Instigator and Chieftain stand out. The deck can obviously get aggressive early hands, but it also has some medium-speed action: [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) (and sideboard [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)) for the inevitable [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s, [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) to recover after dropping its hand ... and the potential for the double [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) draw that is so hard to beat.

I actually stopped working on this article when I saw this deck and booted up **Magic Online**. I had no choice! The strategy didn't disappoint.

Here we have a minor [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) subtheme grafted onto a red-white control shell reminiscent of Mark Hendrickson's successful Spanish Inquisition from US Nationals 2009.



|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |

In this deck Maverick largely exchanged [Obelisk of Alara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obelisk+of+Alara) for [Chandra Nalaar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra+Nalaar) as the big winner. The Valakut sub-theme forced him to play a ton of [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s and faux [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s ([Arid Mesa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) and [Naya Panorama](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Panorama)), so a card like [Obelisk of Alara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obelisk+of+Alara) loses a lot of its options opportunity. 

The deck is very direct damage-heavy. In addition to Valakut—which is fed not only by the [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)-heavy mana base but those familiar [Armillary Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armillary+Sphere)s—there is [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning), and even [Banefire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire) to kill.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td64_3cards4.jpg)  
I found this deck super rewarding to play. The general lack of counterspells in the format makes it a comfortable fit, especially with all those expensive cards. The format is predominantly creatures, and I found them to be pretty easy to suppress with the fast action of both [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and the many flexible burn spells. After a few attrition wars you can settle into planeswalker mode, where you actually activate multiple murderers per turn.

This deck from FUNtainment Berlin has so much going on!

It's a Sphinx-themed deck, a cycling deck, all about board control, and Empty the Vaults, too!

*Sphinxes:* A lot of these cards—even before you get to the insurmountable-if-it-gets-there [Sphinx of the Steel Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Steel+Wind)—are very good against the popular Jund deck. [Sphinx of Lost Truths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Lost+Truths) has a wide rear end that can absorb even a [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) assault. Its ability and the many cyclers can help dig for lands until the biggest Sphinx becomes a reality.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td64_3cards5.jpg)  
From the non-Sphinx (but still artifact creature) wing, we have [Vedalken Outlander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Outlander). Some decks will just never get through that.

*[Architects of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Architects+of+Will) + Glassdusk Hulk:* I am actually just surprised that Artie didn't play a second copy of [Sharuum the Hegemon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharuum+the+Hegemon). With two Sharuums killing and resurrecting each other indefinitely, a healthy [Glassdust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glassdust) Hulk can get lethal in one attack. 



|  |  |  |

*Board Control:* I am always impressed with a [Scepter of Dominance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scepter+of+Dominance) (or thereabouts) linking up with [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment). The Scepter can keep one threat occupied, forcing the opponent to commit multiples in order to deal *any* damage. Then, post-[Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), he or she is still up a creek behind that Scepter.

So there you have 'em ... Several different cool, reasonably effective, and non-Jund ideas you can take to FNM tomorrow, or your more significant tournament of choice.

As you can probably guess, I am absolutely gushing about the double Top 8 appearance of the five-color Cascade deck. For those of you who don't recall, I posted a pre-*Zendikar* version of the deck about six weeks ago. All 33 spells in the main survived the rotation of *Shards of Alara* block, so Lamm and Mong were able to devote their efforts to upgrading the mana base.

The mana bases are actually pretty close to one another here, with just slight differences in double ***Magic** 2010* and solo *Zendikar* dual lands; I felt that Cascade was the strongest strategy pre-*Zendikar*, but was worried about its ability to recover from the loss of Vivid lands and [Reflecting Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool). These two made it look easy themselves, scoring a Top 8 and second place in the Nashville tournament.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td64_cascade1.jpg)  
For those of you unfamiliar with the deck, it's simple: Every card is like a cross between [Mind's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind%27s+Desire) and an Ultimatum. You play a spell, you go up a card, or many. Any spell. [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) on turn four *always* flips over [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) or [Esper Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm) to hit the opponent's hand for two. That is the genius of the deck.

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/td/td64_cascade2.jpg)  
Because every single spell (barring a flip of [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) by [Enlisted Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlisted+Wurm) or [Enigma Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enigma+Sphinx), which is differently horrendous for especially an opponent with no cards in hand) results in some kind of crazy chain that finishes up with a "hit your hand for two" the deck is basically the most predictable deck possible, giving you the best room for planning of almost any deck in recent memory. Your cards always do the same thing, often at surprising speeds; it is just a matter of how badly you are going to demolish your opponent when you cast one.

For instance, you can wait until your opponent's draw to cast [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) on [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch). It is just a question of your receiving a bonus 3/2 or 4 life before you empty your opponent's hand; at that point it is a question of [Esper Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm) or a *real* wrecking from an instant-speed [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning). But the gross result is the same ... so you can always plan for this to go this way.

The thing I like about the deck more than any other Cascade deck is that you never have to hold back. Sometimes you play your fourth-turn [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) against an empty board and flip a [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) or some other useless removal spell. This deck never does that.

Despite Chase and Derek rebuilding the mana base, that is still the potential weakness of the strategy. The inherent power and predictability of the deck also makes it one of the slowest competitive decks in the history of **Magic**. There are no cards in the main deck under three mana *whatsoever.* You can go the first four turns without doing *anything.* In fact, playing against a beatdown deck, you might be posed with the question of playing a [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) on turn three and playing a [Savage Lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands), saving your [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) or whatever to play untapped on turn four; you will generally opt for the latter. Then again, doing nothing for four turns is not necessarily a great plan against some of the fast beatdown decks in the format.

![](http://wizards.com//mtg/images/daily/td/td64_cardsWithDiscard.jpg)  
On the other hand, this kind of deck can basically never lose to a great many decks that people play. Any control deck topping up to a [Sphinx of Jwar Isle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Jwar+Isle) or [Cruel Ultimatum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) is basically going to lose every time you manage to hit five lands. These controlling decks simply can't deal with being the butt of a [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) every turn. Ditto on Pyromancer's Ascension decks; I have shipped to Paris three times on the draw in games where my opponent played [Mind Spring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) twice and *still* won with him on no cards in hand. Why? Because every card is a [Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot), and even if there were counterspells, there wouldn't be enough.
