Before I get started on my playtesting results, let me answer some questions that came in on the forums of last week's article. There were several excellent questions and comments posted by you guys, and I'd like to address them now!


Jwmints asked: "hmmm... he said the deck was going to be mainly black with a splash of red, but he used equal portions of black producing lands and red producing lands."


Well jwmints, let me refer you to Jeff Cunningham's [Magic Academy column](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/columnarchive&column=MagicAcademy). Specifically, let's take a look at his column "[An Introduction to Mana Base](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/academy/25)".



> 
> "How many cards of each color are you playing? (26 Green, 10 Red) Knowing a deck's color ratio will help govern the color ratio of mana used.
> 
> 
> However, this ratio should not be followed exactly. It is difficult to win if you can't play all of the cards in your hand, even if you can play most of them. Again, we aim to be able to play all of our cards most of the time. Also, it is usually the case that any additional land of a color beyond the first or a second is redundant. Even if we were to have twice as many green spells as red spells, it is more important that we have (at least) one [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and one [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) than it is we have two [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s and one [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). That said, we still want to pad our green spells since, one, if we have to draw only one or the other, we would much rather it be a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and, two, there will be turns when we play more than one card. In these cases, since we have more green cards than red cards, we will need some extra [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s.
> 
> 
> So, it is generally better to bias heavily used colors down and lightly used colors up in the mana ratio."
> 
> 
> 


As a side note - I can't recommend reading Jeff's take on Magic Academy enough! It's one of the very best weekly **Magic** articles being written right now. No matter your level of skill in **Magic**, Jeff's clear, concise writing style and knowledge of the game provides tips that can improve your game. Several topics that my readers have asked me to cover (How do I beat a combo deck? How do I build a mana base?) have been covered by Jeff over at the Magic Academy!


Shalie said: "[Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) sees a lot more play in other formats, and your deck could use another aggressive 2-drop, so you might wanna switch those."


When building this deck, I had a choice about whether to put [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) or [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) in the card-drawing slot. Unfortunately, [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) doesn't play well with madness cards or morph creatures. With [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena), I pay one life to draw an extra card, regardless of the card drawn. With [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) and [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders) both turn into five-point hits, a pretty huge chunk of my life total.


In addition, [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) runs around four tickets on **Magic Online**. [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) runs five. This deck already hits 30 tickets with the [Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena)s, so I would have to make even more cuts to fit in Confidants.


Enough preamble. On to the games!



![Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Reckless+Wurm)

### Match 1: Silee (Black/White Weenie)


Game 1: I Madness out an early [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) and clear out his creatures with burn. This game is pretty one-sided in my favor.


(Sideboard In: 4 [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), 3 [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage))  

(Sideboard Out: 1 [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), 3 [Call to the Netherworld](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld), 3 [Phyrexian Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem))  

(Sideboarding notes: He is playing with small creatures, so [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) is useful as a [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God)-type effect. [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage) deals with small guys like [Savannah Lions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions) and [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). [Phyrexian Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem) isn't likely to swing through his board, and he isn't going to kill many of my guys, invalidating the need for [Call to the Netherworld](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld).)


Game 2: I get out an early [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage) and follow it up with [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage). He neutralizes some of my board with [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters), and we go back and forth damaging each other. Eventually, I get out [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) and he gets out Orzhova. He kills me the turn before I would kill him.


Game 3: We are mutually low in life, with my [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) and [Deepcavern Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deepcavern+Imp) facing down his [Knight of the Holy Nimbus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Holy+Nimbus) and [Ghost Council of Orzhova](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Council+of+Orzhova). I have double [Keldon Megaliths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Megaliths), so I am able to pick off his auxiliary creatures during this match (including [Soltari Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soltari+Priest)). On his attack I go down to two life, but am able to shoot him with my Megaliths, make a Goblin token at end of turn, untap, make a Goblin, attack him, and shoot him with a Megaliths to win.


Record: 1-0


### Match 2: TeetopND (Mono-Red Burn)



![Gathan Raiders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gathan+Raiders)

Game 1: TeetopND is playing a ton of weenies and burn spells. I stabilize early against his creatures by trading at every opportunity ([Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) for [Karplusan Wolverine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karplusan+Wolverine), [Mindlash Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindlash+Sliver) for [Scorched Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorched+Rusalka)). I have more creatures than him in my deck, but he has a ton more removal. He burns me down to two, and kills most of my creatures, but a Hellbent [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders) proves too much for him to handle. I draw 8+ cards off of [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) this game, giving me enough threats to divert his burn spells away from my head.


(Sideboard In: 4 Yixilid Jailer, 3 [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage))  

(Sideboard Out: 3 [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), 3 [Phyrexian Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem), 1 [Call to the Netherworld](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld))  

(Sideboard Notes: I wanted as many creatures as possible in for Game 2, because the match will come down to "who can keep a creature on the board." [Phyrexian Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem) is horrible against a deck packed with burn, so out it goes. I didn't see enough creatures to warrant [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), as the deck seemed around 75/25 burn/creatures for the non-land cards.)


Game 2: This game is very similar to the first, as I end up at two life again, but get a board filled with creatures. I could have died to any top-decked burn spell for one turn, but that one turn also saw me with a Hellbent [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders) and a [Deepcavern Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deepcavern+Imp) on the attack.


Record: 2-0


### Match 3: Jcmmcl (Mono-Black Control)



![Korlash, Heir to Blackblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Korlash%2C+Heir+to+Blackblade)

Game 1: I smash him down, and have a board with [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders), [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) and [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm). He casts [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation), and then casts [Tendrils of Corruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) on [Deepcavern Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deepcavern+Imp) on my next turn. This boost of life puts him out of killing range, and his 8/8 Korlash finishes the games shortly thereafter.


Game 2: He draws double Tendrils and double [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) within the first 10 cards, and I can't recover from that. I end up doing 20+ damage over the course of the game, but I have no creatures on the board and no cards in hand when he starts swinging in with an ever-growing Korlash.


Record: 2-1


So far, not too bad. I felt like I had little-to-no chance against the Mono-Black deck, as I was pretty much at the mercy of him drawing (or not drawing) Tendrils and [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation). To fix this, I decided to make a couple of changes to the deck.


First, I wanted to take out the [Phyrexian Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem)s. I almost never wanted to use mana to activate them in the games I drew them, and I sided them out two of the three matches I'd played already. In a land filled with burn and weenie decks, the Totem is not king.


Conversely, [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage) is really great - it kills lots of early-drop creatures, gives me another one-drop to play with, and can poke damage through large blockers. Those need to be moved from sideboard to main deck.


I've had a couple of games where I drew multiple [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena)s, but only wanted to have one in play. Because of this, I decide to take out a [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) (-4 tickets) to add in a fourth [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage) to the main deck, and add three [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) (+3 tickets - 1 each) to the sideboard. [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) gives me a double-threat against control decks. First, I can use it to sacrifice creatures that are being targeted by [Tendrils of Corruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption), [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) or [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) in order to counter those spells (which will avoid any lifegain on my opponent's side of the board). Second, it's great against many control decks. I can suspend it early against a Mono-Black or White/Blue deck, and have it drop to the board (with haste) on my turn, circumventing many sorcery-speed kill spells, such as [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation), or enchantments like [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters).

### Match 8: Geo0 (U/R Storm)



![Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Empty+the+Warrens)

Game 1: I get comboed out with [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) - he makes sixteen Goblins on turn four, and I don't have enough blockers or creature kill to stem the tide of his attack.


(Sideboard Out: 3 [Call to the Netherworld](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld), 1 [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm))  

(Sideboard In: 4 [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm))  

(Sideboard Notes: I'm hoping that this is a version of Storm that has been popular at States this year - one that runs [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) and [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories) as the kill, and eschews [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) and dragons. If so, I stand a chance by winning the game with [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) and keeping my hand empty. If it's [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm), I'm hosed.)


Game 2: He goes nuts on one turn, using [Perilous Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research) on [Hatching Plans](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) to draw five cards, and then casting nearly a dozen spells. His fifth turn ends with fifty Goblins on the board.


I untap, cast [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), and swing in with double [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders).


He has no hand left. I win.


Game 3: I get a quick start with creatures, and he has a draw that contains triple [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom), but little card drawing and no [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens). The turn before I kill him, he casts [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories) for five. My hand is [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) and [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), and I get knocked down from 20 to 6 life. Thankfully, that's all he has, and I proceed to drop my hand the next turn and kill him with [Deepcavern Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deepcavern+Imp) and double-Hellbent [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders).


Record: 6-2



![Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian+Arena)

Strangely enough, I haven't really needed to draw or play [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) to win games. Several games against aggressive decks or combo decks have been too much of a race to allow time to cast the [Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena) - it's either been stabilize, or die. In other games, I've curved out with creatures and spells every turn, never needed extra cards, and won the game on turn 6-7 making full use of my mana the whole game.


This is a good indication that I can cut the pricy [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) out of my deck. The three remaining copies cost twelve tickets total, so this frees up a lot of budget cap in the deck.


My losses so far have been to Mono-Black control and Red/Green/White [Stormbind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind), with a close call against Mono-Red burn and Red/Blue Storm. In all of these matches, one commonly seen card would have immensely helped my chances. That card is [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute).


[Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) costs three tickets in most places on **Magic Online**, but it's tickets well-spent. A single [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) can take 5+ cards out of an opponent's hand - especially against a reactive, defensive opponent. Moreover, Red and Black don't have any efficient counterspells (I'm not counting you, [Dash Hopes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dash+Hopes)!). [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) is a very proactive discard spell - against [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) or Storm Combo, I'd rather take out two [Rite of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame), a [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song), [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories) and [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) on turn four, than play a one-card discard spell (or two card, in the cast of [Stupor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupor) or [Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise) // [rise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=rise)) and leave them with multiple combo pieces.


[Call to the Netherworld](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld) has similarly been disappointing, since the only creature I've really wanted to get back is [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage). Instead of Call, I decide to try out two [Undertaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undertaker)s, though I'm not sure how good they can be. Yes, they are Madness enablers, and yes, they can get back [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) and [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders), but how often have you seen me need to start swapping creatures back-and-forth in the above games?

### Match 9: Airproductsandchemicals (B/R/W Control)



![Persecute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Persecute)

Game 1: I beat him down with little guys, and [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) out most of his hand on turn four, getting [Demonfire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonfire) and [Firemane Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Angel). He drops down [Peace of Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peace+of+Mind) and casts [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify) on a [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders), but I have four+ creatures on the board (double [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage), double [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage)) and beat him down over the course of a few turns.


(Sideboard In: 3 [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon), 4 [Blood Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon))  

(Sideboard Out: 3 [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm), 4 [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper))  

(Sideboard Notes: During that match, I saw him discard several huge guys like [Angel of Despair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair). Also, I saw eight lands, and I didn't see a single basic land. [Blood Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) for the win!)


Game 2: I suspend a first-turn Gargadon, and then play [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), [Undertaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undertaker) and [Shadow Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Guildmage). He tries to [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) my Guildmage, and I sacrifice it to my Gargadon. I then play [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders), swing for a turn, and he answers with a hard-cast [Firemane Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Angel).


On my next turn, I play a second (face-down) [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders), cast [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) (he loses his only creature - I lose my [Undertaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undertaker)), discard the last card in my hand to flip up my Raiders, and swing in for five. He doesn't have anything he can do about my board, as I'm left with two 5/5 creatures and a suspended Gargadon (four counters) and he's below nine life.


Record: 7-2


### Match 10: wyerleader (Mono-Green Aggro)



![Timbermare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Timbermare)

Game 1: I get stuck on 2 lands and get beat down by a horde of Elves, [Moldervine Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak)s, and [Timbermare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timbermare).


Game 2: In this game, I kill most of his small creatures with [Rakdos Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), including stopping his [Groundbreaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundbreaker). Unfortuantely for me, he drops down [Spectral Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force) once he hits five mana, and then puts [Moldervine Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak) on it the following turn. I'm not equipped to deal with an 11/11 trampler, and I die.


Record: 7-3


After my 10th Match, I decided to hang up the MadBent deck. Why is that? Because I was about to make the next change to the deck, and it was a major one: getting rid of the Madness cards entirely! I found that my Black/Red deck had a pretty good curve, but the cards I was often stuck with in hand were [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) and [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm). Both of them were unwieldy - [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders) gave me a 5/5 for three mana (without the need to discard it), but [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) required a lot of maneuvering to get going. I'd rather play with something like [Plague Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Sliver) (5/5 for four, nothing special to cast him other than mana) then have to tailor my deck around [Reckless Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm).


If I were to retool the deck, I would start pushing the Hellbent theme more (add another [Keldon Megaliths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Megaliths)) and move the deck in a more burn-heavy direction such as this deck by Kai Davis, which took second place at Portland Regionals last week
