Eight Decks for Fate Reforged Standard
======================================



 Posted in **Reconstructed**
 on January 13, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he wanted a job making Magic cards. Ten years later, his dream was realized as his combined success as a professional player, deck builder, and writer brought him into Wizards R&D during 2011. He's been writing Magic articles since 2005 and has no plans to stop. 





Ahhhh. Take a whiff of the air. It smells like…the past. The sulfur is fresher and there's a hint of dragons.



We're officially here. It's time for *Fate Reforged*.



The Prerelease is this weekend, giving you your first chance at getting your hands (or dragon claws; I don't judge) on of those fresh new cards.



But what should you keep your eye out for? What's new and exciting for the decks you want to build?



Well, that's where I come in!



The diligent and wonderful readers of ReConstructed have been putting together decks and sending them my way ever since the first *Fate Reforged* card broke its way onto the scene. Now, finally, it's time to put all of those decks to good use!



Today, we're going to take a quick look at a whopping *eight* reader-submitted decks! We're counting off two-by-two today: among them are two beatdown decks, two midrange decks, two control decks, and even two new combo decks! These should provide plenty of ideas for you as you plunge into *Fate Reforged* Standard.



All right! Let's hop right into it!

[Warriors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advanced&output=spoiler&method=visual&subtype=+%5b%22Warrior%22%5d&format=+%5b%22Standard%22%5d) showed up in *Khans of Tarkir* as a subtheme you could talk about building around, primarily using [Chief of the Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Edge) and [Chief of the Scale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+of+the+Scale). Well, in Tarkir's past—our future—it turns out that there are plenty more strong Warriors that enter the fray!



Mardu Shadowspear gives the deck another solid one-drop that hits for 2 (and can even dash in a pinch) and Mardu Strike Leader makes for a great three-drop that can also singlehandedly take over the game against control decks.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_dpz3w9x7bx.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_da0jw6iu3p.png)



One other great addition I don't see here is Brutal Hordechief. This four-drop hits hard, letting your entire team hit extra hard and killing from a distance. Once you hit five mana, you can even just keep pseudo-[Falter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=382932)ing them, throwing every one of their creatures in front of your smallest one.



If I were looking to tweak this deck, I would remove some of the spells: it doesn't really need all of the [Gods Willing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gods+Willing)s and [Feat of Resistance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feat+of+Resistance)s. You just want to curve out with your creatures and smash face! When you're adding creatures, you could also consider some token makers since those plays well with the Hordechief plus cards like Sorin and [Dictate of Heliod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dictate+of+Heliod).

For pre-*Fate Reforged* Standard, you have probably seen some of the white-blue heroic decks running around. Well, now that *Fate Reforged* is here, these decks get a whole new tool: Monastery Mentor.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_lvwdxadj5j.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_v081iddx0t.png)


Heroic decks already like to cast a lot of spells. They're all for targeting their creatures with cheap tricks like [Defiant Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defiant+Strike). Monastery Mentor gives these decks a whole new level of play, serving as a self-contained win condition in addition to its normal heroic tricks. You can always just fire off a bevy of cheap spells after you cast it and have an army of tokens—then do the same on your next turn and attack for a huge chunk of your opponent's life total.



Cards like [Gods Willing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gods+Willing) and Valorous Stance even helps to protect your Mentor! Play it with four mana up and a [Gods Willing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gods+Willing) in your hand, and your opponent is going to find the removal spell he or she was counting on woefully unhelpful.



Something in this space could be one of the major decks with *Fate Reforged* out. Give your spin on it a try, and have fun!

There are a couple new powerhouse green cards in *Fate Reforged*—namely, Whisperwood Elemental and Shamanic Revelation.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_bjph4xy49v.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_42zl82wr70.png)



The Revelation is something you don't see that often in green: a way to draw tons of cards *and* gain life. It certainly shares more than *just* the word Revelation with [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). It feels sort of like playing the [Freyalise Commander deck](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/arcana/commander-2014-edition-decklists-2014-10-31): you can just drop a ton of creatures onto the battlefield, Revelation, and still have plenty of gas left!



The Elemental is a complete powerhouse. It generates creatures every turn, getting out of hand very quickly…and even serves as anti-wrath protection to boot!



Itou's build is a little more manifest-focused than I would make mine, but I think there's the kernel for a really great mono-green deck here that explodes with creatures and then draws back up. If you cut the [Trail of Mystery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trail+of+Mystery)s, [Reclamation Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclamation+Sage)s, and Frontier Sieges for a mix of [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix), Polukranos, Whisperer of the Wilds, [Genesis Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra), and [Arbor Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Colossus), you have the beginnings of something very threatening.

Manifest is a curious mechanic. How often does it need to hit a creature to be worthwhile? How should you build your deck around it? Do you even need to build your deck around it?



Well, Marvin has an interesting take on the situation.



In his Abzan deck, there are plenty of cards he is happy to hit on their own—Brimaz, for example—that he can then flip up and bring the beats with. But he is also playing a couple of manifest "cheats." [Hooded Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hooded+Hydra) flips up for a paltry GG when manifested—and gets the five counters from doing so. When [Master of Pearls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Pearls) turns up for only 1W, its morph trigger still goes off. These are some perfectly reasonable creatures in their own right, and with manifest they get even better.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_1edpxrokk6.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_egvfmyuq3k.png)



Another interesting thing he does is have [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess) to help control his draws. If you want to, say, ensure you are going to manifest a [Hooded Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hooded+Hydra) under your Lightform, Liliana can help make that possible.



While I wouldn't exactly expect manifest Abzan to instantly supplant the [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino)-wielding version that has been so successful, a mixture of old Abzan and something like this could be something to look at.

You could even consider playing more in this deck, tapping out for Sieges and then controlling the game. Who needs creatures when you can just counter spells, wipe the board, and slowly drain the opponent to death?

> 
> Step 2: Cast Sandsteppe Mastodon, putting five +1/+1 counters on your [Sage of Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Hours).
> 
> 
> Step 3: Take an extra turn!
> 
> 
> Step 4: During your extra turn, return Sandsteppe Mastodon to your hand with Primadox. Repeat steps 1–4. Ta-da! Infinite turns!
> 
> 
> 




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sage+of+Hours)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Hours) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Roaring+Primadox)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roaring+Primadox) ![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/ujehvsbfrf153/en_4ms5v8e276.png)



Now, that may seem a little hard to pull off—and it certainly isn't simple. But [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) and [Commune with the Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+the+Gods) both offer redundancy, and I think you can also just build this deck to be a good green midrange deck as well. For example, you could probably play Shamanic Revelation and/or Whisperwood Elemental in here as well.



This deck looks like a blast to play when it works. See what you can do with it!

Ryota has uncovered a couple of pretty fun infinite token combos—and what better way to show them off than cram them all into one deck?



The new one is with Monastery Mentor.





> 
> Step 1: Have Monastery Mentor, [Midnight Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Guard), and [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) on the battlefield with a [Retraction Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retraction+Helix) in hand.
> 
> 
> Step 2: Play [Retraction Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retraction+Helix) on the guard. That makes a token.
> 
> 
> Step 3: Tap the token and the Drum for a mana. Tap the Guard to bounce the Drum.
> 
> 
> Step 4: Recast the Drum. Get another token. Guard untaps. Repeat steps 1–4 and end up with a million tokens—and also a million/million Monastery Mentor!
> 
> 
> 




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Midnight+Guard)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Guard) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Springleaf+Drum)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Retraction+Helix)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retraction+Helix)



That's pretty fresh and fun! Of course, there's also a [Setessan Tactics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Setessan+Tactics) combo:




> 
> Step 1: Have a [Hornet Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Nest) and a [Midnight Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Guard).
> 
> 
> Step 2: Make the [Hornet Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Nest) indestructible with [Ephemeral Shields](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ephemeral+Shields)/[Spirit Bonds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Bonds). (Or perhaps a to-be-added Valorous Stance.)
> 
> 
> Step 3: Cast [Setessan Tactics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Setessan+Tactics) on [Midnight Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Guard).
> 
> 
> Step 4: Tap the Guard to fight the Nest. Nest makes 4 tokens, but stays alive because it's indestructible. Guard untaps and can fight again. Repeat over and over until you have Hornets buzzing to your heart's content. (Or, perhaps, Mark Rosewater's discontent.)
> 
> 
> 
> 



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hornet+Nest)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Nest) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Midnight+Guard)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Guard) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Setessan+Tactics)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Setessan+Tactics)


Is putting all of these combos in one deck right?



Well, probably not. But you know what the big problem is in telling combo fantasy and combo reality apart? They're both ridiculous.



With some refinement, there could be something real here. Focusing on one combo in more of a midrange shell (perhaps like some of the others mentioned here today!) could be the secret to success. Give it a try and see where you get—or go further down the rabbit hole and see if perhaps something with [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) could work here!



Your Move

This week, I featured eight decks in eight different archetypes. And now, it's your turn!



These eight decks are all interesting—but they're not entirely formed either. They could each use some tuning, and I've offered some basic suggestions. Now we're going to try something a bit fresh to kick off the year: I want you to take one of these eight choices I've given you and try modifying it yourself!



Pick one of these archetypes, try and make the best build you can, and let's see what happens. Here are the full details for this mission:



**Format:** *Fate Reforged* Standard


**Restrictions:** Choose an archetype featured in today's column. Refine it, and send in your take on it!


**Deadline:** Monday, January 19 at 6 p.m. Pacific Time.



Submit all decklists by emailing me at [reconstructeddecks@gmail.com](mailto:reconstructeddecks@gmail.com).



Decklists should be submitted with YOURNAME's DECKNAME at the top. Underneath should be one card per line, with just a leading number. For example:



12 Mountain


4 [Firedrinker Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firedrinker+Satyr)


3 [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot)


4 [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)



…and so on. Well-formatted decklists have a much better chance of being read and making it into the column. Poorly formatted decklists are more likely to be ignored. **Please send your decks to** [reconstructeddecks@gmail.com](mailto:reconstructeddecks@gmail.com)**.**
