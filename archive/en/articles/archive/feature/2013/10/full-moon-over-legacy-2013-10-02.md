A Full Moon Over Legacy
=======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 2, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_luisscott-vargas_0.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I've seen some crazy things happen in Legacy, but a deck with eight Werewolves might just take the cake (and devour it messily). Optimis344 played this deck in a **Magic Online** event and, I assume, flipped some Werewolves on the way to a 3–1 finish. 



</sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_legacy_migration/flipcard/flipcard.html?ca=hanweirwatchkeep&cb=baneofhanweir&lang=en>



 The deck has many elements of the classic Dragon Stompy deck, which uses a combination of [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon), [Magus of the Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Moon), [Trinisphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinisphere), [Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker), and [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) to disrupt opponents. [Ancient Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb), [City of Traitors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors), [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide), and [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) all lead to casting a Chalice for one or a three-drop on turn one or two, and ideally both. 


 Once you move past the disruption things get hairy. Four [Hanweir Watchkeep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hanweir+Watchkeep) and four [Instigator Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Instigator+Gang) represent a bulk of this deck's threats, and both are in the deck in order to take advantage of an opponent who can't cast spells. Flipping a Werewolf should be trivial, and both wolves are incredibly large when flipped. Even if the opponent is lucky enough to be able to play cards, this deck can just take a turn off in order to upgrade its forces. [Rakka Mar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakka+Mar) also makes a good backup, summoning wave after wave of Elementals to crush the enemy. 


 The deck has only the lightest touch of actual removal, with two [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) and one Umezawa's Jitte able to interact with cards in play, along with the [Phyrexian Revoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker)s mentioned above. The main plan is to stop the opponent from casting anything, and that's something this deck is very capable of doing. 


I've never seen a Werewolf flipped in Legacy, but this deck may soon change that.
