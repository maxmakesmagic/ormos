VINTAGE MASTERS ROUNDUP
=======================



 Posted in [2014 MAGIC ONLINE COMMUNITY CUP](/en/events/coverage/occ14)
 on September 12, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author_pic_nate_price.jpg)
By Nate Price




 A longtime member of the Pro Tour and Grand Prix coverage staff, Nate Price now works making beautiful words for all of you lovely people as the community manager for organized play. When not covering events, he lords over the @MagicProTour Twitter account, ruling with an iron fist. 






*Vintage Masters* Booster Draft has proven to be one of the most popular formats in the history of *Magic Online*. Representing the first opportunity for players to own powerful cards like [Black Lotus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Lotus) and [Ancestral Recall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall), *Vintage Masters* managed to provide players with not only one of the most interesting Limited formats of all time, but it finally gave players the resources to play the Vintage Constructed format on *Magic Online*!




 For this event, while the Wizards team certainly had the edge in Pro Tour appearances, there are few players in the room with the nearly 100 *Vintage Masters* drafts that Community Team member Frank Lepore has under his belt. Anchoring one corner of the Community team, Lepore relied on World Team Champion Paul Cheon to anchor the other side.

 In the end, The Wizards team came out with slightly better records than the Community team. The big favorites from each team going into the first rounds of play were Mariah Paglioco's insane Goblins deck and Dave Humpherys's [Astral Slide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide)/[Lightning Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Rift) deck. After three rounds of play, Mariah was only able to pick up one win, while Humpherys managed two match wins with his deck. Each team had one undefeated deck after three rounds of play. Mike Turian and his excellent white/green Auras deck and Scott MacCallum's white/black aggro deck proved impossible to defeat. @MrScottyMac attributed his success to the all-star lineup of mentors that he had leading up to the event, including Pro Tour Champion Tom Martell, Twitch legend Kenji "NumotTheNummy" Egashira, our very own Jacob Van Lunen, top Pro Melissa DeTora, and his teammate Frank Lepore. Turian, on the other hand, preferred to attribute his performance to pure, unadulterated skill, as this Twitter exchange illustrates.
