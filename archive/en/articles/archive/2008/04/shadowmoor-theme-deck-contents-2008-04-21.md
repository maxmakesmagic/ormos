Did you enjoy the *Shadowmoor* Prereleases? Good! Now that that's out of the way, how would you like to see the contents of the *Shadowmoor* Theme Decks?


### Aura Mastery


When playing the "Aura Mastery" deck, you control the Auras, so you'll control the game! [Prison Term](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prison+Term) and [Curse of Chains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Chains) are Auras that lock down your opponent's creatures. [Armored Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Ascension) and [Steel of the Godhead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+of+the+Godhead) are Auras that enhance your own creatures, supercharging them into mighty combatants. [Mine Excavation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mine+Excavation) can return an expended Aura from your graveyard to your hand. And [Glamer Spinners](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glamer+Spinners), which can be played at any time, moves Auras from one permanent to another—including itself—when it comes into play!


But the fun doesn't end there! When you consider the cards that create surprise blockers and the cards that let you tap your opponent's creatures at will, it will be clear that Auras aren't the only things you've mastered.

### Overkill


If you believe that anything worth doing is worth overdoing, then the "Overkill" deck is right up your alley. It's filled with brutal creatures like [Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) and spells like [Flame Javelin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Javelin) that'll burn your opponent's creatures to a crisp. But when you're tired of fighting fair, the deck pulls out its secret weapons!


[Jaws of Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaws+of+Stone) deals damage equal to the number of [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s you control. [Howl of the Night Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howl+of+the+Night+Pack) gives you a 2/2 Wolf for each [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) you control. Both are good in their own right. However, thanks to [Elsewhere Flask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elsewhere+Flask) and [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen)—which turn all your lands into [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s or [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s—these mighty spells can be mind-bogglingly, jaw-droppingly explosive.

### Mortal Coil


Like to exploit weakness? Then grab the "Mortal Coil" deck! This deck focuses on tricks you can pull with -1/-1 counters. Of course, the most basic "trick" is simply to put them on your opponent's creatures, which [Grim Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Poppet), [Torture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torture), and [Incremental Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incremental+Blight) do with ease. But you're cleverer than that!


The deck has lots of creatures with persist, like [Gravelgill Axeshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravelgill+Axeshark). When one dies, if it didn't have a -1/-1 counter on it, it'll return to play with a -1/-1 counter. Then use [Leech Bonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leech+Bonder) or [Fate Transfer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Transfer) to move that -1/-1 counter to one of your opponent's creatures! Your opponent gets weaker, you get stronger, and persist is set to go again. Winning the game has never been so hilariously cruel.

### Army of Entropy


If you're ready to win without mercy, then the "Army of Entropy" deck is for you. This horde of marauding creatures is supplemented by spells and abilities that load up your opponent's creatures with -1/-1 counters. If [Scar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scar), [Puncture Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puncture+Bolt), or a creature with wither kills an enemy creature, you're rewarded with another -1/-1 counter for a different creature, courtesy of [Blowfly Infestation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blowfly+Infestation)!


What happens if the -1/-1 counters don't inflict death on their recipients? No problem—[Kulrath Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kulrath+Knight) will neutralize those creatures anyway. Barring that, your ravenous forces can just run over the depleted defenders. By the time you play [Din of the Fireherd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Din+of+the+Fireherd) to both add to your army and demolish your opponent's board position, the game is all but over.

### Turnabout


Normally, tapping your creatures leaves you defenseless. With the "Turnabout" deck, having a bunch of tapped creatures leaves you primed to strike! [Safehold Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safehold+Sentry), [Pale Wayfarer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pale+Wayfarer), and any creature equipped by [Umbral Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle) have ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20210430014038im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/untap.gif) abilities that let them untap to provide an awesome bonus! [Devoted Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Druid) and [Barrenton Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barrenton+Medic) can also untap at a moment's notice.


Take advantage of all your extra untaps not just by being ready to block, but also by playing spells with conspire, by using [Revelsong Horn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revelsong+Horn) to pump up a creature, and by enchanting a creature with [Presence of Gond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Presence+of+Gond) to quickly create an army of Elves. Use your tapping and untapping tricks to support a swarming, opportunistic fighting force, and you'll run circles around your opponent!
