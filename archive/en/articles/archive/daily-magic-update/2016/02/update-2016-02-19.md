The February 19, 2016 Update
============================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on February 19, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on February 19, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by the weekend. Do I need a reason? Weekends are awesome.


Today's Must
============


1. [MOCS to the Max!](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/mocs-to-the-max/) | Top 8 Podcast | Patrick Chapin and Mike Flores

The [*Magic Online* Championship Series Playoff Event might be the toughest tournament](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/magic-digital/recapping-standard-mocs%E2%80%94battle-simons-2016-02-18) around these days, which makes its results of particular interest to format detectives Patrick Chapin and Mike Flores. Here they explore their favorite decks, powerful strategies, and what it means to "splash" [Nissa, Voice of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Voice+of+Zendikar) in a nearly mono-red deck.


What to Watch This Weekend
==========================


1. [StarCityGames.com Louisville Open Weekend](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/200216_louisville_fact_sheet.html)

A Modern Open headlines the weekend in Louisville, flanked by a Standard Classic and a Legacy Classic. After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [A Very, Very Condensed History of the Multiverse](https://firesidemagic.wordpress.com/2014/11/04/multiverse-history/) | Fireside Magic | Seth Weinstein

Fireside Magic is a cool little blog we watch for now and then that isn't updated very often, but there is some cool stuff contained within. This condensed history of the Multiverse (and it earned both of those "verys") is one such cool thing. It has recently been updated to include the events of *Oath of the Gatewatch*, so check it out if you have a passing interest in flavor and want the tl;dr version at your fingertips.


1. [*Eternal Masters* Spoilers: Everything you Need to Know about this *Magic: The Gathering* Set—MTG!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NxYw6iUlldo) | The Mana Source | Wedge
2. [MTG—Top 10 Best Rares to Reprint in *Eternal Masters*—Part 1!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uFeZB1nbvUs) | The Mana Source | Still Wedge

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Shape Anew](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/modern-constructed-league-2016-02-18)

There are actually a number of cool lists that hit 5-0 recently in the [*Magic Online* Modern Constructed League](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/modern-constructed-league-2016-02-18) (check out the Turbo [Scapeshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) list and the Goblins decks, for instance), but the mostest cool of them all is a sneaky [Shape Anew](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shape+Anew) deck played by LessThanHubi to a pristine record.


At first, it looks like a fairly typical Jeskai deck with some atypical, but not unreasonable choices. [Pia and Kiran Nalaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pia+and+Kiran+Nalaar) is (are?) a Modern staple at this point, and [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) has been played here and there. [Thopter Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Engineer) is a bit weird, but it's two bodies, I guess. One [Blinkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) is seemingly out of place, but defensible if you squint.


Then you see this:


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blightsteel+Colossus)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightsteel+Colossus) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shape+Anew)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shape+Anew)
And you go "Ooooohhhhh!" And maybe "Ahhhhh!" The fact that the deck can play out like a normal Jeskai control deck is a powerful mark in its corner, and throw in an "I win" card like [Shape Anew](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shape+Anew) and you've got a potent combination. Because before the Eldrazi were around, the Phyrexians had that evil, format-warping thing down. Maybe it's time for a clash of the titans.
