The January 18, 2017 Update
===========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on January 18, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on January 18, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by [Quinton Hoover's fantastic art](#3).


Today's Must
============


1. [Top 20 Standard Cards from *Aether Revolt*](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/34402_Top-20-Standard-Cards-From-Aether-Revolt.html) | StarCityGames.com | Todd Stevens

Most lists, they go to ten. Maybe three or five if they're not numbered. If they're saucy, they go to eleven. The most expansive do a Top 3 in every color. But not Todd Stevens. Todd Stevens doesn't hold back. He's got a Top 20 list of *Aether Revolt* cards specifically for Standard. There's a lot of brewing to do in a refreshed format, and Stevens lays out which cards he thinks you should be paying attention to in Standard.


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [*Aether Revolt* Set Review](http://collected.company/2017/01/17/aether-revolt-set-review-142/) | The Command Zone | Josh Lee Kwai and Jimmy Wong

The Command Zone crew sinks their teeth into *Aether Revolt*, and it's delicious.


1. [Preview of Hoover Art Sales](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/mikelinnemann-01182017-preview-of-hoover-art-sales/) | Gathering Magic | Mike Linnemann

When Quinton Hoover passed away in 2013, he left behind an artistic legacy that runs deep in *Magic*, and now some of that art could be yours. Linnemann interviews Hoover's family about the collection, some stories about their father, and what art the collection has to offer.


1. [*Aether Revolt* Set Review: Rare and Mythic Rare](http://lrcast.com/limited-resources-372-aether-revolt-set-review-rare-and-mythic-rare/) | Limited Resources | Marshall Sutcliffe and Luis Scott-Vargas

Wondering what rares and mythic rares you should be excited to crack in a draft? Or which ones you should pass for better commons or uncommons? In a scant 1 hour 52 minutes, Marshall and LSV discuss every single rare and mythic rare from *Aether Revolt* with an eye toward Limited.


If you're looking for more Limited-focused *Aether Revolt* podcasts (What a world we live in where you have multiple options for that specific category!), check out [Constructed Criticism today](http://www.constructedcriticism.com/podcasts/limited-time-only-51-aether-revolt-commons-set-review/), as they look at the Top 3 commons in each color.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Black-Green Delirium](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/no-promised-end-to-delirium/) | ChannelFireball | Brian DeMars

You can't keep a good deck down, but you can imprison its best card in the moon. Black-Green Delirium, at points the boogeyman of Standard, lost its main reason to be—[Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End). But it didn't lose its 74 other cards (including sideboard), so Brian DeMars thinks there still might be something there. Ishkanah, the Grafwidow is still, after all, quite the *Magic* card.


It's impossible to replace Emrakul, but the deck does still get to go a bit big when it casts [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) thanks to a pair of Gearhulks—[Verdurous Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdurous+Gearhulk) and [Noxious Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Gearhulk)—that still let it go over the top of other creatures. Just not "Emrakul over the top."


And that's okay. What's more, it might still be very good.
