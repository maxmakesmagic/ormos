Quarterfinals: Jeff Cunningham vs. Zvi Mowshowitz
=================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 5, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_alexshvartsman.jpg)
By Alex Shvartsman











Cunningham is definitely on the hot streak. He already has a GP win this season, and is looking to follow it up with another. Cunningham was one of the few players to utilize the U/G madness strategy at Pro Tour: Houston, playing it to a 26th place finish. He did not fix what was not broken, choosing to bring the same deck to this tournament. 

Mowshowitz is playing the deck he is best-known for – Turbo-Land. Over the years he played it in a variety of tournaments, without tremendous amount of success. His best finish with the deck was making top 16 at GP Houston. Still, he never gave up on his favorite archetype and it finally paid off, putting him into the top 8 of this tournament. 

### Game 1

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a932.jpg)Cunningham was forced to mulligan. Playing with six cards, he opened with a pair of [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla)s. Mowshowitz played [Exploration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration) and dropped a second land into play. On his second turn, Zvi played out another pair of lands and cast an [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) with two blue mana untapped. Cunningham played his third land and used two mana to pump one of the [Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwalla)s, bringing Mowshowitz down to fifteen life points.

During his upkeep, Zvi milled about 60% of his deck to Oath up a [Battlefield Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlefield+Scrounger). His combo was now half way home. After one more Oath activation, he would end up with just three cards left in his library, ready to go infinite. He countered Cunningham's [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). 

Zvi displayed the fact that he could go infinite on the next turn, but his explanation was a bit confusing and he had to display the combo for Cunningham, leaving [Gush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush) and a pair of [Time Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp)s as the only cards in his library, able to take infinite turns and just win by attacking Cunningham was three points of damage every time. Once the combo was proven to Cunningham's satisfaction, he agreed to give up and proceed to the next game.

Sideboard

Cunningham: -2 [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm), -1 [Waterfront Bouncer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waterfront+Bouncer), -1 [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), +2 [Gilded Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake), +2 [Ray of Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ray+of+Revelation)

Mowshowitz: -1 [Horn of Greed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horn+of+Greed), +1 [Powder Keg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg)

### Game 2

A point of damage from [Yavimaya Coast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Coast) set off a [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) for Cunningham, but no [Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwalla)s entered play, nor [Roar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar)s entered the graveyard. He discarded a land and [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm). Zvi started his game in exact same fashion as game 1, with turn one [Exploration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration) and two lands. Cunningham cast another Study on turn 2, finding a [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) this time and pitching a land. Mowshowitz played two more lands and – once again a perfect draw so far – cast [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) with two untapped [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a933.jpg)Resolving [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) took quite some time as it was the fourth card from the bottom of Zvi's deck. With only three cards remaining, he asked the judge permission to sort his graveyard. Permission was granted, so both players would be able to figure out what the remaining three cards were, and it would be easier for Zvi to pick out cards to put on the bottom of his library with Scrounger. Technically, Zvi did not need the judge's permission. Few players use or even realize this, but you are allowed to arrange the cards milled with [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) in any order before placing them in the graveyard.

Mowshowitz activated Scrounger to send [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), [Time Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) and [Scroll Rack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Rack) on the bottom of his library. He then used [Accumulated Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge) to draw three cards, played two lands and cast [Scroll Rack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Rack) leaving two mana untapped once again. He would be able to go infinite on the following turn, unless Cunningham could do something to stop him.

Cunningham passed the turn. At the end of his turn, Zvi used the Scrounger's ability to "beef up" his graveyard to the size of four cards and cast [Accumulated Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge). Cunningham countered with [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Despite that, Mowshowitz was easily able to display his infinite combo again, prompting Cunningham to concede and propelling Zvi and his beloved deck into the semifinals.

**Final Result:** Mowshowitz 2 – Cunningham 0
