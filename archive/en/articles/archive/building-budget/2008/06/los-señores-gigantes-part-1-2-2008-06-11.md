Los Señores Gigantes! (Part 1 of 2)
===================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on June 11, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget, the **magicthegathering.com** column dedicated to building decks that cost 30 tickets or less! A few weeks ago, I wrote an article highlighting a [good cross-section of underutilized budget cards](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/boab-unplugged-budget-cards-think-about-part-1-2008-04-16). One recurring theme was that Giants, as a tribe, were just being flat-out ignored.


Giants are primarily a red-white tribe, so any Giant deck we build is going to be red, white or red-white. Usually, rare dual lands are out of the price range of a budget deck. Not so for Giants! [Ancient Amphitheater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Amphitheater) usually trades for half a ticket online, so you can get a full playset for the steal of just 2 tickets. To put this in perspective: a single [Gilt-Leaf Palace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilt-Leaf+Palace) or [Wanderwine Hub](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wanderwine+Hub) will run you *more* than an entire playset of the Giant land!


This makes for an excellent opportunity to start out a Giant deck with a great mana-fixer. Giants, by design, are a tribe of slow, large dudes with sometimes splashy effects. For the faster tribes, it is absolutely necessary to have your first land come into play untapped (Kithkin, I'm looking at you!). For Giants, it's not going to be as bad to play a tapped Amphitheater—unless you're splashing another tribe, you aren't going to have a one-drop creature.

Why is it important which Giants are Warriors? As I mentioned earlier, Giants are the slow tribe. Not counting changelings, Giants start at three mana ([Countryside Crusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher) / [Blind-Spot Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind-Spot+Giant)) and regularly hit the six to eight mana range ([Arbiter of Knollridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbiter+of+Knollridge) / [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth) / [Chronosavant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronosavant) / etc). There are two cards on-theme which can grant haste to Giants, speeding up their attack by a full turn: [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) and [Lovisa Coldeyes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lovisa+Coldeyes). Both give a 2-point boost to power in addition to haste.


Giants are mainly designed for attacking, but there are a surprising number of control-oriented cards in the tribe. [Desolation Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desolation+Giant) is a budget [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) variant (and one that leaves you with a 3/3 creature). [Wandering Graybeard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Graybeard), [Feudkiller's Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feudkiller%27s+Verdict), [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm) and [Arbiter of Knollridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbiter+of+Knollridge) don't just gain you life—they gain you a GIANT amount of life! (Which, honestly, was probably the original intention of the designers. Let little tribes gain little life. Let big tribes gain big life!) [Thundercloud Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundercloud+Shaman) is another board-sweeping choice, and three Giants ([Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger), [Hearthcage Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hearthcage+Giant), and [Jotun Owl Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Jotun%2BOwl%2BKeeper)) bring other creatures to the board with them.

One great aspect of Giants is that the majority of them have a very high toughness. This allows them to survive damage-based board sweeping effects that would easily kill other tribes—[Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast) and [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) become tools to allow for an alpha strike against a clear board, rather than just mere weenie removal. [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command)'s less-than-four clearing effect leaves you with your higher-cost Giants, if need be. [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy) goes from "deal double" to living up to its name—likely fatal damage on a huge Giant.


Given the list of Giants above, there are quite a number of possible builds. I'm going to present five of them below, and run a vote at the end of the column. The winning choice is the deck we'll start as the evolution for the Giant deck (in two weeks)! In the meanwhile, all of these decks are great ideas for where you could start with a budget Giants deck, and given how cheap they are right now, why the heck not?

In this first build, you are looking to have Giants be the top end of your curve, supplemented with earlier guys who either are Giants ([Fire-Belly Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Belly+Changeling)) or get benefits from Giants ([Kithkin Greatheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kithkin+Greatheart)). [Jotun Owl Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Jotun%2BOwl%2BKeeper) and [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) provide ways to put multiple creatures on the board at once, which in turn fuels both [Windbrisk Heights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights) (free spells!) or Ajani's second ability (putting +1/+1 counters on multiple creatures). This deck has a number of creatures with good power-to-cost ratios, many of whom are conditionally efficient:

[Kithkin Greatheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kithkin+Greatheart): 3 power for two mana  
[Fire-Belly Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Belly+Changeling): 3 power for two mana  
[Blind-Spot Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind-Spot+Giant): 4 power for three mana  
[Jotun Owl Keeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jotun+Owl+Keeper): 3 power for three mana  
[Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm): 4 power for four mana  
[Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger): 6 power for five mana


[Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) and [Lash Out](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lash+Out) provide ways to both remove early blocking creatures, and potentially finish off an opponent after an early rush. [Avian Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avian+Changeling) gives a little bit of air support (along with any birds produced by the Owl Keeper). Now that *Shadowmoor* is available online, I expect to fit [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession) in here somehow! [Mirror Entity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) is another consideration for this deck, but keep in mind that most of the creatures here already are pretty large on their own!

Big Guys, Big Discounts uses [Brighthearth Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brighthearth+Banneret) and [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkdrinker+Daredevil), along with [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone), to power out some truly massive monsters in the early turns of the game. If you get a two-drop accelerant and a Daredevil on turn three, you are capable of dropping your seven-drop guys on turn four. [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath), anyone? And even if you don't get your [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkdrinker+Daredevil), the deck is set up to deal with early-game threats so you don't get run over immediately—[Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate), [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) and [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command) take care of small guys just *like that*. Since this deck has a slower build-up, we can run multiple Vivid lands, to help offset double-white and double-red mana requirements on spells. [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy) is present, because a [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth), when Frenzied, will likely be 16-24 points of trample damage coming upside a opponent's soon-to-be-dead head!

This version of the Giant deck centers around [Stonehewer Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonehewer+Giant) and its ability to tutor equipment straight into play. Since [Stonehewer Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonehewer+Giant) is essential, I've started the deck with both [Giant Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Harbinger) to go get him, and [Resurrection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resurrection) to bring him back if he dies. This deck sports a foursome of [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) and [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer), as they are the best pieces of equipment for a deck spouting Warriors in Standard. In addition, I've added a smattering of [Cloak and Dagger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloak+and+Dagger) and [Whispersilk Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak) to protect [Stonehewer Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonehewer+Giant), and [Thornbite Staff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornbite+Staff) and [Diviner's Wand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diviner%27s+Wand) for spot removal and evasion, respectively.

There are three aggro decks in this column (which makes sense, because Giants are primarily an aggressive tribe). This one is the mid-range deck—it gets going around turns three and four but comes out strong and fast due to haste and lords. [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) is both a granter of haste and a lord—any Warrior it equips will get +2/+1 in addition to haste, making them frightful indeed! [Lovisa Coldeyes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lovisa+Coldeyes) also pumps up most of the creatures in this deck with an additional +2/+2 and haste. This deck is half Giant Warriors and half non-Giant Warriors, and is virtually a mono-red deck (the exception is the splash for [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm), who, as a 4/4 lifelink for four, finds his way into a lot of Giant decks!).


 



![Lovisa Coldeyes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lovisa+Coldeyes)
