Draft Coverage - Illuminati vs. Your Move Games
===============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 30, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_JonBecker.jpg)
By Jon Becker











With over 40 years of competitive M:TG experience and nearly a dozen Top 8 appearances between them, Illuminati [Justin Gary (A), Zvi Mowshowitz (B) and Alex Shvartsman (C)] faced off against Your Move Games [Darwin Kastle (A), Dave Humpherys (B) and Rob Dougherty(C)] in the ninth round of GP New Jersey.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/887.jpg)YMG won the flip and elected to open first, and Humpherys cracked a mid-level pack containing [Painbringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painbringer), [Werebear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear), [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade) and [Shelter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter). Hump declared a black preference immediately, selecting the powerful, but fragile, [Painbringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painbringer). Dougherty picked up [Werebear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear) and Gary elected to move initially into green with a [Chatter of the Squirrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chatter+of+the+Squirrel), leaving the [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade) to Zvi, who would seemingly be facing off against Humpherys' black deck. The pack was basically killed with Shvartsman's selection of [Shelter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter), leaving Kastle to snap up a [Leaf Dancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaf+Dancer) in response to Gary's Chatter. On the swing, Kastle snagged a [Peek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) and Gary picked up a [Last Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites).

Things began to get ugly rapidly though, when Dougherty busted the Pope ([Cabal Patriarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Patriarch)) in Pack two, shipping a [Dreamwinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamwinder), [Crypt Keeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Keeper), [Barbarian Lunatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Lunatic), [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) and a second [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade) to Illuminati. Gary took the Creeper and Zvi picked up his second [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade) - a strong signal to Humpherys to either abandon black (which can rarely support three players, let alone four), or to draft a house full of ways to answer the Shades. Shvartsman picked up [Dreamwinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamwinder) (over [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate)) and Kastle moved into white with an [Auramancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer). Humpherys was left with a solid wheel of [Barbarian Lunatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Lunatic) and [Fledgling Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Imp), and seemed content to play an unusual Seat B black-red mirror match, despite Zvi's two [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade)s. 

In the third pack, Gary opened a bomb of his own, [Thought Devourer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Devourer), and shipped an [Aven Windreader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader), [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate), [Crypt Creeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Creeper) and a [Krosan Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Avenger). Zvi picked up the Creeper, Alex drafted the [Windreader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windreader) and Kastle was left with the [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate). Humpherys passed the Avenger on to Dougherty to help out his top heavy curve, instead picking up a [Morgue Theft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morgue+Theft).

Over the next three packs, Gary settled into blue by adding a [Cephalid Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Looter), [Thought Eater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Eater), [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) and a [Scrivener](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrivener), leaving his second color flexible. Zvi kept mostly to red and joined the bomb club when he cracked and picked a [Shower of Coals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shower+of+Coals). Shvartsman picked up quality green men, selecting [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) over [Treetop Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Sentinel), which was a solid, but not spectacular creature against Gary's non-green deck. [Elephant Ambush](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Ambush) followed, plus a couple of cantrips.

On YMG's side, Humpherys drafted a [Dirty Wererat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dirty+Wererat) and two more [Fledging Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledging+Imp)s, but could manage only a [Flame Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst) as removal. Dougherty added another home run in [Roar of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm), and a few mid-level green creatures, and filled out his picks with a [Rites of Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Spring) to help find the heavy black needed for the Patriarch. Kastle, like Gary, did not seem to have settled on a second color, concentrating on blue by picking [Aven Fisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fisher), [Aether Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) and [Psionic Gift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Gift). However, he had indicated a willingness to draft what had been, to this point, almost non-existent white by picking up an additional late [Auramancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer).

The *Torment* packs were unusual, containing little burn (a single [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) and two [Sonic Seizure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sonic+Seizure)s), a lone Butcher (which ended up in Gary's deck) and only one strong black uncommon ([Sickening Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickening+Dreams)). In the first pack, Kastle picked a [Skywing Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywing+Aven), and shipped a dangerous [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) to Shvartsman as well as an Edict, which Zvi shipped to Gary in favor or a [Cabal Torturer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer). Shvartsman's deck really fell into place as he cracked a [Narcissism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcissism) and was shipped [Centaur Chieftain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Chieftain) and an [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba), which would facilitate a third turn [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) and allow him to take full advantage of a [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) and a pair of late [Obsessive Search](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsessive+Search)es.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/886.jpg)A good example of experienced drafting arose in the middle of the *Torment* packs, where Gary, who was quietly assembling a solid blue/black deck, had the opportunity to pick an underrated [Unhinge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unhinge) on the comeback, which would have fit well into his curve, but instead took a [Rancid Earth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancid+Earth). This allowed Zvi to shore up the three slot, in which he had only a [Cabal Torturer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer) and a [Fiery Temper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper). The [Unhinge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unhinge) would not only provide a little disruption for Humpherys but, Illuminati hoped, would help Zvi to hit his fourth (and later) land drops - the level at which the bulk of Zvi's few significant threats began. Four people drafting black, and a low concentration of red cards, led to Zvi packing a deck with an exceedingly low creature count (for red/black) - with only nine animals by the end of Torment, and ultimately, only twelve men in total (including [Earsplitting Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earsplitting+Rats), [Pardic Lancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pardic+Lancer) and two [Dwarven Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dwarven+Grunt)s). 

Unfortunately for YMG, *Torment* was not kind to Dougherty either, who was stuck drafting black in what is widely believed to be the worst black seat at the table, but was trying to get by on the third best black behind Zvi and Gary. Dougherty ended up adding primarily mid-level green spells ([Krosan Restorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Restorer), [Acorn Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acorn+Harvest), Calmer) though he did "steal" a [Soul Scourge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge) (that Humpherys had indicated he intended to take) and got a gift fourth pick [Sickening Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickening+Dreams). One bright spot for Rob was the [Centaur Chieftain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Chieftain) he cracked in his *Torment* Pack, which also contained a [Possessed Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possessed+Aven). It was picked up by Kastle, and looked to figure heavily in the blue on blue matchup with Justin Gary.

Kastle did pick up what looked to be another potential game breaker against Gary's blue/black deck in a fifth pick [Stern Judge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stern+Judge); at that time, Gary had only a [Chainer's Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict) to deal with the Judge. However, Gary added a Butcher and a [Crippling Fatigue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue) in the next two packs to buy himself a margin of comfort.

One particularly insightful selection was made by Shvartsman in the 5th *Torment* pack on the comeback, when he snapped up a [Churning Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Churning+Eddy). While his opponent's deck was full of powerful cards, it was also exceedingly top heavy (and, as it turned out, would grow only heavier in *Judgment*), and with Alex's rapid, low casting cost, madness-fueled deck, the Eddy would provide a much greater tempo advantage than it typically does. Additionally, this would give Shvartsman an additional way to handle a Roar Token - one of the few things that could meaningfully slow Alex's beats.

In *Judgment*, the teams both looked to fill holes - Zvi needed a bunch of decent animals, Dougherty needed a handful of two and three casting cost green creatures, and Darwin looked for the payoff in white and white/blue. Unfortunately, red and black offer little in the way of creature help in *Judgment* - [Treacherous Werewolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treacherous+Werewolf) and [Barbarian Bully](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Bully) being the exceptions. In fact, only a single Werewolf appeared, and it was snapped up by YMG, leaving Zvi with what would prove to be, potentially, his best chance at victory over Humpherys' black/red deck - [Worldgorger Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldgorger+Dragon). Humpherys had managed to stock his deck with plenty of blue-collar three drops (e.g., three [Fledgling Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Imp)s), which, while not inherently exciting, presented a problem for Zvi's grunts and 1/1 four drops. However, Hump had no way to remove the Dragon on a one for one basis - in fact, Humpherys would have to use a minimum of three, and perhaps four cards to kill the Dragon, if it hit play. However, Hump picked up a [Swelter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swelter).

Dougherty's card power level continued to soar in *Judgment*, when he added not one, but TWO [Treacherous Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treacherous+Vampire)s and a [Giant Warthog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Warthog), but he was unable to add any two or three drops beyond a single [Phantom Tiger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Tiger).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/893.jpg)Kastle's deck also came into its own in *Judgment*, where Darwin added [Mirror Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Wall), [Phantom Nomad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad), [Valor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valor) and the powerful [Wormfang Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Drake) and [Shieldmage Advocate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shieldmage+Advocate). Darwin also drafted a terrific potential splash in [Arcane Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Teachings). His opponent, Justin Gary, also picked up a [Mirror Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Wall) (though it was a purely defensive card in his deck) as well as two copies of [Lost In Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+In+Thought), in hopes of buying himself time to reach his [Thought Devourer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Devourer) and Butcher, and minimize the effectiveness of Kastle's [Possessed Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possessed+Aven) and [Wormfang Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Drake). However, Kastle had wisely picked up a late [Floating Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Floating+Shield) in *Torment*, which with two [Auramancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer)s, could spell real trouble for Gary in the air war.

In the final *Judgment* pack, Illuminati had the opportunity to pick up a card which is usually considered chaff, but which had strong application in the Shvartsman/Dougherty matchup; on the comeback to Alex, Justin and Zvi both enthusiastically gestured at the lonely [Envelop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Envelop), which Alex quickly drafted, giving him, weirdly enough, a potentially powerful answer to [Sickening Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickening+Dreams) and an additional measure of Roar defense. Alex ended up main decking his final pick of the draft!

In general, Alex's deck seems to be much tighter and have a far lower curve that Dougherty's. Dougherty's cards are ridiculously powerful, but also very slow, and it is not unreasonable to believe that barring mana issues for Alex (which are not likely to be a factor even though Alex decided to splash for [Glory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory), [Unquestioned Authority](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unquestioned+Authority) and [Shelter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelter) rather than running a straight blue/green given his two [Obsessive Search](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsessive+Search)es and [Diligent Farmhand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diligent+Farmhand)), or overextension by Alex followed by a busty [Sickening Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sickening+Dreams) by Rob, that Alex will out speed Dougherty before he can come fully online.

On the other hand, Humpherys looks to have a relatively easy time with Zvi's deck, unless the big Dragon makes an appearance, or Humpherys allows Zvi to maximize his removal until he can safely cast and protect his pair of [Whispering Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade)s. It appears, however, that the spreading of black over four decks and the lack of a significant number of red creatures in *Odyssey* left Zvi with a terrible creature base to work with. This match should go to Hump.

The deciding match looks to be between Kastle and Gary - both decks are well built and have a quality curve. Both have a blue rare bomb, and significant air forces and both have [Mirror Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Wall). While it appears that Gary may have a slight edge (in that he has actual removal in the form of Butcher, Edict and [Crippling Fatigue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue)) in a shorter game, if Darwin gets a [Floating Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Floating+Shield) on a beater or draws the game out long enough to activate [Shieldmage Advocate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shieldmage+Advocate) or [Possessed Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possessed+Aven)\*\*, or can swarm Gary with 3 casting cost flyers, Darwin should be able to squeak out a victory. 

Overall, a very interesting draft, with some insightful late picks and some important lessons regarding mana curve, color depth, and reconsideration of facially strong or weak picks in light of specific matchups.

\*\* Ultimately, Kastle opted not to play any swamps in his deck, thus rendering the potential assassination ability of the Possessed Aven all but useless.
