*Innistrad* Lexicon
===================



 Posted in **Arcana**
 on December 14, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Monty Ashley











Welcome to the *Innistrad* edition of the Lexicon, in which we explore the meanings of some of the more unusual words we use in the names of those cards. While many offbeat names in **Magic** (like "[moldgraf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=moldgraf)") are simply made up, we often dig around dictionaries for real little-used and archaic words for inspiration.

![Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Abattoir+Ghoul)**ab•at•toir**
  
*noun*

a slaughterhouse.

![Ulvenwald Primordials](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ulvenwald+Primordials)**pri•mor•di•al**
  
*noun*

1. constituting a beginning; giving origin to something derived or developed; original; elementary: *primordial forms of life*.
2. *Embryology.* first formed.
3. pertaining to or existing at or from the very beginning: *primordial matter.*

![Bloodcrazed Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodcrazed+Neonate)**ne•o•nate**
  
*noun*

a newborn child, or one in its first 28 days.

![Paraselene](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Paraselene)**par•a•se•le•ne** 
  
*noun*

a bright moonlike spot on a lunar halo; a mock moon. 

![Trepanation Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trepanation+Blade)**tre•pan**
  
*noun*

1. a tool for cutting shallow holes by removing a core.
2. *Surgery.* an obsolete form of the trephine resembling a carpenter's bit and brace.

![Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Morkrut+Banshee)**ban•shee**
  
*noun*

(in Irish folklore) a spirit in the form of a wailing woman who appears to or is heard by members of a family as a sign that one of them is about to die. 

*Source: dictionary.com*
