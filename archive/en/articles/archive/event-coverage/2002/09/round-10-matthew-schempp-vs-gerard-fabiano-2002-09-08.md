Round 10: Matthew Schempp vs. Gerard Fabiano
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 8, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_474.png)
By Eric Taylor











### Game 1

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpcle02/a931.jpg)Matthew Schempp played green/white against Gerard's blue/black upheaval deck, traditional enemies in Magic: the Gathering's story line. Green/white has creatures, "magic bullets" (like the [Phantom Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur) with protection from black), while blue/black has blue's power cards (like [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)) and black's creature elimination.

Gerard was incredibly friendly and relaxed. "Where are you from? Good luck." Matt was tense and on looking at his first hand he gave a tiny shake of the head. Mulliganned. Mulliganned again. Down to five cards, and game 1 seemed to be over before it even started.

Matt led with [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) then another [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla). There was no way he could win this game, it seemed. But Gerard just kept saying, "Go."

Gerard [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic)-ed Matt's [Anurid Brushhopper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Brushhopper) for zero. Matt picked up the Brushhopper for a second. It was worth a try. Gerard then Skeletal Scried it away for a cantrip effect. With the game 9 points for Gerard and 20 for Matt, it seemed like Matt had a chance, despite the mulligan to five.

Matt shook his head and started playing quicker. 2 points to 20 with a Brushhopper, and a couple of other little creatures in play, Gerard had done nothing the entire game but take his damage. On turn 6 Gerard played a land and [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)ed. Matt remembered to blink his Brushhopper and that was the game. At the end of the game all Gerard had was envelop and a bunch of lands. Even with a five card hand white/green can kill a goldfish in seven turns.

1-0 advantage Matthew Schempp

### Game 2

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpcle02/a927.jpg)The two players bantered. Fabiano pushed his thumb and finger together "It was this close, that circular logic." Schempp replied, "No. I was just waiting to see if it was a Circular logic or a [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate)."

Gerard led with land, and Matt started out with [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe) , and on Gerard's next turn, dropped a madness [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) in response to Gerard's [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood) to start the game. Gerard played [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) and like a truly magical David Copperfield, guessed right, taking down [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). Matt was meanwhile all the time keeping three mana up. Matt had a [Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) in his hand and [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe) on the board, so any time that Gerard wanted to [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) one of the creatures, Matt would be able to respond by pitching [Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) to the graveyard, and then activating the ability. However when Gerard actually did [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) the [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe), Matt didn't pitch [Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) to the graveyard in response. Instead he merely replayed the [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe), which was met with [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) by Gerard. The next turn Matt played an [Elephant Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) on his [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), which was met with a [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)ed [Ghastly Demise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise) by Gerard. Matt shook his head, and banged his fist on the graveyard.

With the board clean, Gerard played [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). 11 life points to 20 in favor of Matt, but really the game was controlled now by Gerard. The general rule of thumb in this situation is that white/green has to break the standstill because if you sit around, blue/black will draw their [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)/[Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) eventually and then the extra three cards won't help. In general, it's better to break it immediately while blue/black has less lands on the table. Matt broke the [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) and his [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) was met with a [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)ed [Aura Graft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Graft), putting this card on one of Gerard's lands. Gerard Cabal Therapied two [Phantom Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur)'s from Matt's hand and by this point, Matt was just pawing the table with the cards in his hand impatient for the coup de grace. A couple turns later [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) stomped in to finish him.

1-1

Gerard ever the gentleman, wished Matt, "Good luck," to start game 3. Matt had a slow start dropping lands on turns one and two and finally dropping his first creature, a [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) on turn three. Matt then started dropping a bit more meat on the table over the next few turns with [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest), [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest), and a turn six [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). Again Gerard pawed the table, this time excitedly impatient and hopeful for the game and match win. With the life score 20 to 6 in Matt's favor Gerard had a long think. Then he dropped [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). Matt, "No floating?" Gerard said, "No," and then dropped land before passing the turn.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpcle02/a928.jpg)Matt dropped a [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe) and a few turns later Gerard dropped [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). Gerard immediately broke the standstill, which is the general rule, but in this case it might have been a bit better to just keep attacking with the dinky 1/1 guy. Gerard had [Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) in the graveyard so his man would be unblockable and it was a five-turn clock, so it seems that Gerard would have had to break the [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) before he was ready to [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) again, in which case, Matt would draw the three cards. Probably the hardest judgement calls to make in Magic are the ones where you go against the general rule in order to play for the exception to the rule. 

As it was, Gerard picked up three cards from the [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) and wipe the board with [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood) and [Chainer's Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict). A few turns later, the score stood at 20 to 4 with both sides having a [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) in play (one of them Gerard stole from Matt with a [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)ed [Aura Graft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Graft)). With [Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) in Matt's graveyard and a [Sylvan Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Might) in Matt's hand (Gerard knew this from a previous [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy)), Gerard was forced to [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) just as time was called. Matt was able to put Gerard down to 1 life point on the fifth turn, but it wasn't enough for the win.

**Final Result:** 1-1; Draw







#### Matthew Schempp
