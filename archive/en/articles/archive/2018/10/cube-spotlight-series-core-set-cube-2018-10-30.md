Cube Spotlight Series – Core Set Cube
=====================================



 Posted in **Magic Online**
 on October 30, 2018 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_authorpic_larger.jpg)
By Kyle O'Neill











Hello, I'm Kyle O'Neill ([@imkyleoneill](http://www.twitter.com/imkyleoneill)).


I have designed many different cubes and have organized a weekly Cube night for the six years I've worked at Wizards of the Coast. I am the senior retail development representative for the Wizards Play Network, working with game stores to build communities and bring our games to life through organized play. My other credits include working on the Ravnica Guild Kits, as well as playtesting a myriad of other *Magic*, *Dungeons & Dragons*, and Avalon Hill games.


Today, I'm here to talk to you about the *Core Set Cube*, which you can play November 7 on *Magic Online*.


The *Core Set Cube*
===================


Core sets are wonderful. They set the tone of that period in *Magic*'s history. They provide a consistent reference point, a place to "check in" and see what *Magic* is up to in the broader sense. Looking through the card list for *Seventh Edition* tells a very different story than the *Core Set 2019* card list, for example.


Core sets offer straightforward and flavorful play experiences, designed to cater to all types of players. They also happen to include some of the most popular cards ever made.


When designing the *Core Set Cube*, I wanted to be true to both the nature of core sets and the traditional Cube experience. I've taken the best of the best in terms of power level, flavor, and nostalgia. In doing so, I discovered a unique Cube environment that is a little more powerful than you might expect.


The basic design rules of this cube are simple:


1. It only includes core set cards from *Seventh Edition* to *Core Set 2019*.
2. It's 540 cards, singleton.
3. It has its own personality.

I'm a big fan of limiting my selection pool for cubes and seeing what new experiences can come from it. We get to play in an environment with unique interactions and play with cards that are plenty good on their own but don't quite make the cut for a more traditional cube.


Of course, there wasn't room for everything. I've excluded a fair amount of the famous color [hosers](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/play-design/m-files-hour-devastation-part-1-2017-07-14#hate-card) and some of the more degenerate bombs. However, you might find yourself on the receiving end of a mountainwalk attack from a bunch of buffed Goblins or find it hard to kill a gigantic enchanted creature (unless you came packing enchantment removal).


Core Themes
===========


The *Core Set Cube* leans a little more on ten color-pair themes (and five monocolor themes!) that you can construct a strategy around. Many of the usual Draft archetypes are here, like white-blue control and red-white tokens. That said, these are not the only strategies available. Experiment with the card pool and discover new exciting strategies to try!


**White-Blue Control**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=129808)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=129808)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=11214)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=11214)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=83007)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=83007)
You have access to the strongest [board wipes](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/top-decks/what-expect-pro-tour-hour-devastation-2017-07-25#sweeper), paired with counterspells to keep the opponent at bay. How could you lose? But how do you win, you ask? Fair players might opt to swing in with their [Frost Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) a few times, but the real champions will resolve [Bribery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bribery) and win with whatever the opponent has instead.


**Blue-Black Reanimator**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=383405)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=383405)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=398434)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=398434)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace%2C+Telepath+Unbound)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Telepath+Unbound)Transform[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=135270)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=135270)
Draw cards and ditch big creatures to your graveyard, then reanimate them. How fast can you get a [Demon of Death's Gate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demon+of+Death%27s+Gate) into play? This deck also works well with countermagic and some of the most powerful black removal options. Maybe you could take one of your opponent's cards.


**Black-Red Sacrifice**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=129583)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=129583)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=83236)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=83236)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=11268)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=11268)
There's no feeling quite like casting [Threaten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threaten) on your opponent's giant threat and either attacking with it or feeding it to one of your other creatures**.** Red and black both have small and recursive creatures that can stand to die a few times.


**Red-Green Beatdown**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=235192)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=235192)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=13148)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=13148)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=83346)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=83346)
Do you like to just cast huge creatures? We've got that, too. Apply early pressure with cheap beaters, then [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) your opponent. If you want to go even bigger, try midrange instead! With plenty of acceleration, you can resolve a [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite) in no time.


**Green-White Auras**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=447360)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=447360)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=383394)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=383394)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=370716)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=370716)
Put Auras on things! [Verduran Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verduran+Enchantress) will dig you deeper into the deck while you make endless Angel tokens with [Sigil of the Empty Throne](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne). Put [Angelic Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Destiny) on a hexproof creature, like [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic), and watch your opponent crumble under pressure.


**White-Black Big Lifegain** 


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=130553)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=130553)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=370627)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=370627)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=370671)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=370671)
With aggressive creatures running amok, this color combination has the tools to get itself out of reach. Double your life a few times, and put [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) on your frontlines with a bevy of other life-gaining creatures. Dip into black for the game-ending [Sorin's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%27s+Vengeance). Take 10?


**Blue-Red Spellslinging**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=370751)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=370751)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=205227)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=205227)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=253701)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=253701)
Blue and red feature some of the more flexible cards that slot into a number of archetypes. Do you want to take a bunch of turns with [Nexus of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nexus+of+Fate)? Would you rather sling powerful X spells like [Mind Spring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring) and Fireball? All-star cantrips like [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), and [Sleight of Hand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand) help you keep the party going.


**Black-Green Graveyard**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=398575)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=398575)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=83035)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=83035)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=383387)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=383387)
Ach, Hans, run! It's the [Lhurgoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lhurgoyf)! Dump creatures into your graveyard and reap the rewards with huge graveyard scavengers and recursive payoffs like [Bone Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Dragon). The value creatures in green mixed with efficient removal options in black adds up to a midrange deck that just keeps coming.


**Red-White Tokens**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=193751)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=193751)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=383418)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=383418)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=370662)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=370662)
[Trumpet Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trumpet+Blast) is one of my favorite cards, so I had to include an archetype to enable it. Flood the board with tokens to go wide and destroy your opponent before they get a chance to stop you. Pump your team and get in for damage with classics like [Glorious Anthem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Anthem).


**Blue-Green Value Bounce**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=25568)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=25568)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=383268)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=383268)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=370622)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=370622)
Don't sleep on this one. Control the board and eke out value with the best [enters-the-battlefield effects](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/play-design/m-files-hour-devastation-part-1-2017-07-14#enters-the-battlefield-effects) core sets have to offer. [Sunken Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunken+Hope) will keep your opponent off balance, and beefy beaters like [Stampeding Wildebeests](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stampeding+Wildebeests) crash in for damage while buying back your value creatures. How many times can you cast or copy [Pelakka Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pelakka+Wurm)?


Monocolor
=========


Due to the restrictive card pool, you'll notice that the *Core Set Cube* only has a small number of multicolored cards and mana-fixing lands. A side effect of this is that it has a lot of support for monocolor strategies. Each color has its own unique strengths, so don't worry if you end up with seventeen of the same basic land in your deck.


**Mono-White Army**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=398428)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=398428)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gideon%2C+Battle-Forged)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Battle-Forged)Transform[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=259725)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=259725)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=191058)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=191058)
White wants to flood the board with cheap creatures and tokens, then pump the team up and plow through the opponent's forces. Some creatures—like [Imposing Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imposing+Sovereign)—also serve as ways to falter your opponent's efforts to slow you down.


**Mono-Blue Artifacts**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=447205)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=447205)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=205036)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=205036)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=106555)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=106555)
Core sets have some iconic artifacts, and they tend to work well with blue. Mana rocks ramp you to some of the bigger threats, or you could take to the skies with a brigade of Thopter tokens.


**Mono-Black Swamps**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=207106)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=207106)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=370658)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=370658)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=25637)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=25637)
Black likes Swamps. A lot of Swamps. Put Swamps in your deck and include other cards that care about Swamps! Straightforward. Super effective. Swampy.


**Mono-Red Goblins**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=15810)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=15810)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=383258)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=383258)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=220435)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=220435)
Goblins are the poster boys of red, and they're a lean, mean, face-smashing machine. Arguably the best Goblins we've ever made are in core sets, so get a piece of this action-packed tribe!


**Mono-Green Ramp**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=129626)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=129626)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=241832)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=241832)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=253670)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=253670)
Green ramps into beefy creatures. That's just what it does! Tower over your opponent faster than you can say "Forest, [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), go." You could also try building an Elf [tribal](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/making-magic/cats-2017-08-07#tribal) deck if you fancy that instead.


20 Things to Try
================


1. Put [Angelic Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Destiny) on a [Gladecover Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gladecover+Scout).
2. Build an Elf or Goblin tribal deck with [Elvish Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Champion) or [Goblin King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+King).
3. Combo off with [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm) and [Krenko, Mob Boss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%2C+Mob+Boss).
4. Cheat a [Darksteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus) into play with [Elvish Piper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Piper).
5. [Twincast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast) and/or [Reverberate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverberate) your [Inferno](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno).
6. Ramp into [Wildfire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildfire) or [Destructive Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force) with mana rocks.
7. Sacrifice a [Reassembling Skeleton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton) with [Grave Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Pact) in play. Then do it again.
8. Put [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) on [Elite Arcanist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Arcanist). Or [Memory Lapse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Lapse). Or [Time Stop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Stop).
9. Win the game with [Liliana's Contract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Contract). ([Adaptive Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton) is a Demon!)
10. Build an all-permanent ramp deck with [Warp World](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World).
11. Take the [Door to Nothingness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+to+Nothingness) challenge.
12. Cast [Touch of Moonglove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Touch+of+Moonglove) or [Spirit Link](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Link) on your [Crypt Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Rats), and go to town.
13. Loop [Archaeomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) with [Evacuation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evacuation).
14. Do something degenerate with [Strands of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strands+of+Night).
15. Gain 100 life off [Beacon of Immortality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Immortality) and [Alhammarret's Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alhammarret%27s+Archive).
16. Build a burn deck! [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock), [Pyrotechnics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrotechnics)!
17. Sacrifice every piece of a [Mitotic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mitotic+Slime) to a [Fallen Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Angel) for a solid +14/+7.
18. Combine [First Response](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=First+Response) with lands like [Adarkar Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes) or [City of Brass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Brass).
19. Flood the board with artifacts, then cast [March of the Machines](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=March+of+the+Machines).
20. [Force Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+Spike) an [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake). That'll show 'em.

That's all for now. I hope you enjoy this cube as much as I have and gain a fonder appreciation for the power of core sets. See you in the queues!
