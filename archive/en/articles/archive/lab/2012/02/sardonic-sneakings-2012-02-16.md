Sardonic Sneakings
==================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on February 16, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova












Welcome back to the Lab, from wherever you came from today, be it a former cyber setting or the crisp outside world. If the latter, I have some slight inquiries: What's it like there, and how often do you hallucinate? It's all in my head of course, but lately, when I walk around places I can see a faint blue mist, slithering around obscure corners or just hanging midair, blurring in and out. Could just be the climate lately, I suppose. Cloudy with a chance of lunacy.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl182_lost.jpg)Anyway, that takes care of the introductory randomness. Today I'm excited to finally talk about some exciting cards and decks that arise to my attention from *Dark Ascension*. Over the course of the coming weeks I'll do my utmost to wring all the combo juices out of the set (with occasional help via emails from readers... like you, potentially!).


With so much awesomeness to work with, I might not even get to everything. And due to the resounding blankness of the non-theme week, I'm not sure where to really start! After painstakingly sorting out the cards that deserve certain weeks for study, I've arranged all my non-theme weeks. Well, the first three, at least. One would think I'm just as organized in real world matters... sigh. Really, what is this "real world" anyway?


Oh, what's with today's title? I couldn't think of one, so I went with "Sardonic sneak," which is an anagram for "*Dark Ascension*." I preferred it with the "-ings" ending, though, so I guess I'm actually stealth-saying: "Sing, *Dark Ascension*."


### Misfortunate Musings


Lots of cards in *Dark Ascension* strike me as interesting to build around. Some of these cards smile knowingly at me from the screen. Others burst through the e-particles and shake me by the shoulders. Still others inspire me to take a sudden action, like pump a fist or giggle.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl182_curse.jpg)[Curse of Misfortunes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Misfortunes) | Art by [Terese Nielson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Terese+Nielson%22%5D)


As a fan of the Curse subtype in *Innistrad*, [Curse of Misfortunes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Misfortunes) inspired the latter reaction. (Fist pump; not giggle fit.) I wanted to make a Curse deck earlier, but there just weren't enough of them to work with. Now with *Dark Ascension* providing a glut of new Curses, I think it's time to have a go at building this deck. [Curse of Thirst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Thirst) becomes the perfect win condition, and with [Curse of Misfortunes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Misfortunes) finding a new Curse every upkeep, your opponent will soon bend to his or her many afflictions.

I figured a full set of [Curse of Death's Hold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) would be necessary as a measure of defense. [Bitterheart Witch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterheart+Witch) is a pretty excellent blocker and Curse finder, so in she goes.

I decided to run a handful of singleton Curses to support Misfortunes, including two heavyweights from *Dark Ascension*, [Curse of Echoes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Echoes) and [Curse of Bloodletting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Bloodletting). [Curse of Echoes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Echoes) is a nutty variant on [Hive Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind), while [Curse of Bloodletting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Bloodletting) can rapidly speed up the Thirsting process.


[Curse of Exhaustion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Exhaustion) is another fascinating new one. It makes perfect sense as an opponent-only [Rule of Law](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rule+of+Law) (slowing them down considerably), and it injects white into the deck. Not a bad thing, considering [Mesa Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mesa+Enchantress). With all the enchantments in the deck, an early Enchantress can really speed things up. White also gives me access to [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) (good stabilizing card), [Idyllic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Idyllic+Tutor) (ooo...), and [Seal of Cleansing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Cleansing) (solely there to destroy [Witchbane Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb).)

### Mmm...Toughness


Alright, the next card I want to examine is [Feed the Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feed+the+Pack), a fun Wolf-making enchantment. I've had a couple of emails concerning this card, and I definitely want to address the ridiculous potential quantity of Wolf tokens that can be made with it.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl182_pack.jpg)[Feed the Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feed+the+Pack) | Art by [Steve Prescott](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Steve+Prescott%22%5D)


Gavin NoLastName suggested [Tree of Redemption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tree+of+Redemption). Ooo...that's an idea kernel I can get behind. Use the Tree's trippy trans-toughness trick to make it a 0/20, and sacrifice it for 20 2/2 Wolf tokens! Lester Hawkes upped the ante with his suggestion: "[Feed the Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feed+the+Pack) plus [Tree of Redemption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tree+of+Redemption) plus [Parallel Lives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Lives). Some ramping and utility, and you could be attacking with 80 power by turn six. Standard legal even." Yep, that's crazy.

Although Lester does hint at a Standard version of this deck, I dipped into some older sets to smooth out some kinks. [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement) in particular attracted my attention. Imagine: A turn two Battlement into a turn three Tree into a turn four [Feed the Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feed+the+Pack), hug the Tree, then feed it to those twenty 2/2 Wolves that show up. Slip a [Parallel Lives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Lives) in beforehand for extra mayhem. Ahh... [Parallel Lives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Lives). Like the Tree itself, it's a green *Innistrad* rare I didn't get around to showing off that much. I'm making up for it with this deck.

### Super Silver Storage


Proceeding directly away from [Tree of Redemption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tree+of+Redemption), the wandering planeswalker stumbles onto the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault), that mysterious lynchpin of Innistrad's storyline. Seriously, [the art confirms their close proximity](/en/articles/archive/glimpsing-helvault-2012-01-31). So I suppose I should cap off today's article with a look at this legendary exiling machine.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl182_helvault.jpg)[Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) | Art by [Jaime Jones](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Jaime+Jones%22%5D)


With enough mana, a single [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) can take over a game, storing creatures from all loyalties in its silver depths. [Silver Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Myr) and [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) give the deck a mana boost to reach the magic number seven, which turns the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) onto full power. Even at middling power, the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) can still wreak havoc. If I take control of my opponent's creatures, I have the option of storing them in the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) for only one mana. To repeatedly steal, I turned to [Vedalken Shackles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) and [Beguiler of Wills](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beguiler+of+Wills) (who makes her Lab debut in style).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl182_3card.jpg)The deck basically plays as a mono-blue controlling deck, ideally winning through creature manipulation. [Epochrasite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Epochrasite) is just the robotic combat clogger the deck needed, endlessly stalling while you can land a [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) or the Shackles.


However, there is one wacky-ish trick to be pulled here. Let's say you have a bunch of creatures locked in the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault). When the time is right, start the festivities by sacrificing the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault) to [Throne of Geth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Throne+of+Geth). Then, in response to the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault)'s destruction trigger, cast... [Gather Specimens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+Specimens). And get everything. (The Throne works with many counter-related cards in the deck, like [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice), [Serum Tank](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Tank), and [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus)).
