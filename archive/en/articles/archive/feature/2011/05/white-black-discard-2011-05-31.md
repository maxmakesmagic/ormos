Since the deck's debut at Grand Prix Philadelphia in the hands of Chris Pikula, white-black discard/aggro decks have fallen in and out of favor in the world of Legacy. Count them as back in after Caleb Durward used this take on the deck at the StarCityGames.com Legacy Open in Louisville. Using powerful discard spells like [Hymn to Tourach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach) and [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), the deck rips apart an opponent's hand. Then it cleans up with a powerful creature like [Phyrexian Obliterator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Obliterator) or [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) while [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) keeps its hand full. Read more from the [coverage](http://www.starcitygames.com/php/news/expandnews.php?Article=21896) at StarCityGames.com.
