Commander BOGO
==============



 Posted in **Command Tower**
 on June 5, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






Everybody loves a good deal.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ct/2014/ct_wk22_302_cardart_moonlightbargin.jpg)
[Moonlight Bargain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Bargain) | Art by [Nick Percival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Nick+Percival%22%5D)


What makes a good deal in *Magic* is getting something extra for the same cost as something else:


* [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) gives a 1/1 Snake Shaman in addition to [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth).
* [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), at just 6 mana, gives *another* 6 power and 6 toughness of creature when it dies.
* [Indrik Stomphowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indrik+Stomphowler) gives [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) in addition to a reasonable body at uncommon.

If we were talking about these creatures on Twitter (I'm [@the\_stybs](http://twitter.com/the_stybs)!) I'd be using the hashtag #Value. All three of those creatures are what I'd call "spell creatures"—each gives you something extra despite ample existing capability. It's this buy-one-and-get-one-free bargain that makes these kinds of creatures so valuable in Commander.


Getting every last drop possible out of cards feels necessary when you have double or triple the usual number of opponents.


That's part of what you find in *Conspiracy*, the multiplayer set for drafting you'll see at your local game store this Friday. As an aficionado of Cube (drafting) and Commander (multiplayer), finding a booster pack-sealed set filled with #Value has been exciting for me.


* [Rousing of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rousing+of+Souls) both fits into a token-focused deck (such as my revived [Rhys the Redeemed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) deck), provides helpful information about what's in who's hands, *and* draws a card to replace itself. (Spell creatures can be spells that give you creatures!) [Selvala's Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selvala%27s+Enforcer) similarly draws into a card and information with a bonus attached.
* [Crookclaw Transmuter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crookclaw+Transmuter) is a small body for Commander, but is a natural for messing with combat and even killing creatures when [Meishin, the Mind Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meishin%2C+the+Mind+Cage) is around. [Jetting Glasskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jetting+Glasskite) serving as a quasi-shroud shield enchantment on a beefy flying body isn't to be ignored either.
* [Morkrut Banshee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) is removal—assuming morbid—on a stick. [Wakedancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wakedancer) is the reverse, giving you an extra creature for a low, low price.
* [Pitchburn Devils](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils) give out [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) when they bite the dust. Paired with [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) and [Dictate of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dictate+of+Erebos), cards like this help explain why black-red is such a scary color pair for Commander.
* [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) makes its triumphant return to booster packs, but [Terastodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) and [Wolfbriar Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfbriar+Elemental) are what you'd typically expect as green spell creatures for Commander.
* [Runed Servitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runed+Servitor) may look like a bad deal, but alongside [Nekusar, the Mindrazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekusar%2C+the+Mindrazer); [Notion Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Thief); and [Uba Mask](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uba+Mask), we all know how painful "giving out" extra draws can be for opponents.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rousing+of+Souls)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rousing+of+Souls)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nekusar%2C+the+Mindrazer)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekusar%2C+the+Mindrazer)

Creatures that give more are inevitably what fill my piles when building new Commander decks, and it's a clear trend that won't change anytime soon, given the piles that you sent in.



> 
> "Spell creatures" surely is a fuzzy definition. I don't want to just staple [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) to a 2/2 deathtouch body. That's just efficient. I want ridiculous. I want my creatures to be ANY spell I want. That's the foundation of how my [Melek, Izzet Paragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melek%2C+Izzet+Paragon) deck is built.

> 
> Basically, the goal of the deck is to cast as many instants and sorceries as possible. The problem with instants and sorceries is that they go away after you use them, unlike permanents. So my permanents help me get the most out of my instants and sorceries.
> 
> 
> The creatures come in many groups. The returners are classic "spell creatures" that recur cards from my graveyard, like [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) and [Charmbreaker Devils](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charmbreaker+Devils). Then there are the duplicators, such as [Echo Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echo+Mage) and [Djinn Illuminatus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+Illuminatus), that copy my spells to get more use out of them. Finally, Melek and [Galvanoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanoth) turn my deck itself into an instant-and-sorcery-slinging-machine.
> 
> 
> —Andrew
> 
>

My personal favorite is [Coiling Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle), just because of the sheer versatility. Getting either a land or a draw for two mana is all well and good, but with [Deadeye Navigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator); [Venser, the Sojourner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+the+Sojourner); or good old "Roon Burgundy" ([Roon of the Hidden Realm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roon+of+the+Hidden+Realm)) out, I can do it at least once a turn, giving me reliable ramping and/or card advantage for a minimal investment.

Creatures that carry spells that let you use the spells over and over? I mean, [don't twist my arm here](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/192).


We can also just go as big as we need to blow up *everyone*, as James does:



> 
> My favorite commander deck is my No Pain, No Gain deck, featuring [Toshiro Umezawa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toshiro+Umezawa). My favorite "spell creature" in the deck is [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger). Having grown tired of playing "safe" strategies, I designed the deck for maximum swinginess, no matter the cost. [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) is a beatstick that discourages killing it, and the downside is somewhat negated by Toshiro's fantastic ability. [Disciple of Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Griselbrand) is my second-favorite "spell creature" in the deck. This deck plays very riskily, paying lots of life for card draw (I have paid up to 20 life for [Pain's Reward](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pain%27s+Reward) when I really REALLY needed a kill spell) and Disciple really helps offset the damage. Plus, getting rid of an unwanted [Abyssal Persecutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor) or lethal [Baleful Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baleful+Force) is very helpful. Not to mention that saccing [Thought Gorger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Gorger) to the Disciple is akin to an early Christmas.

> While not as exciting or flashing, my most utilitarian "spell creature" would have to be [Rune-Scarred Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune-Scarred+Demon). Several of my win conditions require multiple parts to play successfully, and he is always there to lend a helping hand. Thanks to him, I have had many memorable victories with a combination of [Sadistic Sacrament](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sadistic+Sacrament) and [Mirror of Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+of+Fate), or [Caged Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caged+Sun) and [Nirkana Revenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nirkana+Revenant), or [Phthisis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phthisis) and an opponent's [Serra Avatar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Avatar). Although it isn't the most consistent deck I've made, and I kill myself as often as my opponents do, it's the most fun and creates the most memorable plays of any deck I have made to date.

You can also be a little more selective in what "blowing everything up" means, like Stephen:



> 
> My favorite spell creature is a bit of a difficult question to answer. But I feel I can answer it nicely with a mono-green deck I'm working on.
> 
> 
> It's a spiritcraft deck built around [Kodama of the Center Tree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama+of+the+Center+Tree). Plenty of the cards could be described as "spell creatures," but it plays a few that follow your definition. If my board isn't getting repeatedly crushed, I can often turn every Spirit cast into a potent spell creature with the spiritcraft triggers.
> 
> 
> [Bane of Progress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bane+of+Progress) is an all-star of the format, and the only mono-green card to destroy all artifacts and enchantments. With no spell to replace it, I guess it's my favorite spell creature. [Carven Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carven+Caryatid), [Eidolon of Blossoms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Blossoms), and [Haru-Onna](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haru-Onna) have the three most powerful words in **Magic** written on them and stay to the theme. If [Bane of Progress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bane+of+Progress) is green's nuke, [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) is the sniper. And what a beautifully crafted rifle it is.

Spiritcraft may not be at the top of your list of "**Magic** Mechanics That Create Impressive Value," but with the right creatures (there have been, and will be plenty more, Spirits made in the game) carrying the right spells, anything is possible.


Really, I mean *anything*. Zack even made the humble [Spore Frog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spore+Frog) a force of multiplayer mayhem:



> 
> The first deck I think of when "spell creatures" are brought up is my Karador deck. Basically everything in the list has an ETB or dies trigger (or both!), making something like [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) a Swiss Army knife for all sorts of threats. But my \*favorite\* creatures?
> 
> 
> Being a long-game-minded graveyard deck, I have to say that [Spore Frog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spore+Frog) is the guy I want to see every game. That little [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) Frog does a great job of "ramping" into my commander, helps me play politics with protecting life totals later on ([Gift of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Immortality) makes him an absolute nuisance), and is a bit of insurance against infinite-damage attacks (Prossh, Kiki-Jiki/[Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), that sort of thing).
> 
> 
> 


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spore+Frog)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spore+Frog)[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gift+of+Immortality)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Immortality)


> 
> [Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed) is always a blast to resolve. Crafting the deck to have few non-Humans to get maximum value out of his undying ability feels very good, and he's a better [Cauldron of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cauldron+of+Souls) with [Vish Kal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vish+Kal) hanging around, as well (that's an infinite combo that I like having in my back pocket). Add in how great he makes the evoke creatures ([Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), I'm looking at you) and he just has the whole package.
> 
> 
> One creature in my deck actually came in, replacing a spell with the same effect, because recurring the enters-the-battlefield trigger is more straightforward than recurring [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) or getting lucky off of [Deadbridge Chant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadbridge+Chant). I used to run [Pull from Eternity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pull+from+Eternity) to help protect against [Bojuka Bog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Bog)-style hate, but then found out about [Riftsweeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftsweeper) in *Modern Masters*. When your opponents Path or Swords something like [Ashen Rider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashen+Rider), it is incredibly satisfying to drop it back in the graveyard to be cast again. This [Grizzly Bears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears) has earned its place in the deck many, many times over.
> 
> 
> —Zack
