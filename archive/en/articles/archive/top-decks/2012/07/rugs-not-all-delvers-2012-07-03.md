RUGs (But Not All Delvers)
==========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on July 5, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











In Atlanta, Georgia, this past weekend, the Grand Prix circuit made one of its periodic stops in Legacy-land. Early intelligence going into [the Grand Prix](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpatl12/welcome) had [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) as a potential Public Enemy Number One—a kind of [Yawgmoth's Bargain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Bargain) that is also a huge creature *and* easier to get onto the battlefield (via [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb), [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study), and any one several [Reanimate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate) variants)—plus, of course, the recent un-banning of [Land Tax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Tax) had players talking. [Land Tax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Tax) was the backbone of any number of card-advantageous decks in years past, a sort of recurring [Ancestral Recall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall) (for basic lands)...

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td202_grisel.jpg)[Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) | Art by [Igor Kieryluk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Igor%20Kieryluk%22%5D)

And yet?

The Atlanta Top 8, while bringing us a couple of unexpected archetypes including one brand new one, had nary a [Land Tax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Tax) nor (even more surprising) [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) in sight. Here's how Atlanta looked:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td202_chart1.jpg)  
RUG Delver
RUG Delver is one of the leading decks in the current Legacy format. It is the definition of "good stuff" playing the best creatures ([Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) as well as Standard's best, [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)), efficient [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s like [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) and [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce), and the one-of-a-kind [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) (plus other fast burn spells). RUG Delver is kind of non-specialist best-of-the-best that can take on all comers via a combination of card quality and speed.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td202_vidugiris.jpg)Grand Prix winner Gaudenis Vidugiris played a RUG Delver deck with a fair number of new customizations. [Nimble Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) is at the forefront of this deck, a 3/3-for-one that is hard to deal with, a kind of slippery (if-non-flying) [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). While it doesn't play [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), the deck's ability to leverage threshold gives it an angle on [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour).

Gaudenis's deck is a counterspell monster. In addition to the format-defining and combo-stomping [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), Vidugiris ran the full four copies of [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce)*and* a couple of [Spell Snare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare)s to interact with various [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)s, [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte)s, [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s, or [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) Reanimator's [Animate Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animate+Dead) or [Exhume](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exhume).

The "general" take-on-all-comers plan for RUG Delver is just to play a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf); Gaudenis cut the fourth [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) in order to run a singleton Scavenging Ooze (trouble for any kind of Reanimator combo strategy).

In addition to both main deck and sideboard Scavenging Oozes, Vidugiris's RUG Delver could disrupt a proposed [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand)-from-the-graveyard via [Surgical Extraction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) or steal it after it hit the table with [Gilded Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td202_ayers.jpg)Daryl Ayers also played a RUG Delver deck, but one with very different capabilities. Like Gau's deck, Ayers's build played tight threats, high-quality support cards, and lots of potential permission. Seven free counterspells, the same full four copies of [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) that the champ ran, but also four copies of [Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle).

In addition to playing a kind of permission spell ([Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) is actually better at stopping [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) or [Tendrils of Agony](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) than a "real" counterspell), [Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) can also team up with [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) to lock down the opponent's mana. What could be worse than biting a [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) followed by a [Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) preventing you from your [Flooded Strand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flooded+Strand)? Ayers reinforced this land destruction sub-theme with a single [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam). This could potentially allow him to play [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) over and over again while building up the graveyard for [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Nimble Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td202_edelkamp.jpg)Edelkamp called his deck "VaporBlade" and joked that he wanted to play both Delver and StoneBlade at the same time. So he did!

VaporBlade is a straight white-blue deck (so needs a few more bodies to carry around those Swords and [Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jitte)s). He could get the super-fast jump with first-turn [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)*or* one-card-combo on turn two just by playing [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic). [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) is no less powerful a card here than we have seen in Standard. In fact, armored up with a [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)-driven [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) or [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), the Geist is potentially hairier than ever.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td202_stark.jpg)[Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) has a job: Never attack. Never ever attack (unless you are coming in for the last point or something). [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) is like Maverick's pre-emptive and recurring [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). This little one-drop keeps all the nasty [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s and [Swords to Plowshares](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) from killing Maverick's excellent creatures.

[Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)—The backbone of this deck's tutor engines. For a single mana, [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) can play [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and find [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor). At higher amounts of mana, it can do basically anything... find Scavenging Ooze to battle the opponent's graveyard or find [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) or [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) for even more tutoring!

[Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary)—Possibly the card that makes Maverick as a whole tick. [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) can get huge to play [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), or go for any of several different unique lands to take control of the game. [Maze of Ith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith) can keep gigantic monsters off your back and [Karakas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) can keep even the mighty [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) off the table. [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) after [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) can help lock down an opponent (especially once you have a sizable clock in play), and [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) after [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) (potentially backed up by that [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) in the graveyard) can make [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) bigger and bigger.

[Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben)—Maverick has moved away from tons of different "Hate Bears" to configurations like Ben's. This deck might have no copies of [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), but it does have four copies of this new two-drop. [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) puts a severe tax on fair decks that are reliant on fast one- and two-mana cantrips. For example, say you kept a one-land hand on the draw on the basis of having a ton of [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s, [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)s, and [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s? If Ben dropped Thalia on the second turn, you might have big problems keeping pace in the game *at all.* Thalia is even worse for unfair decks. [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher) or other storm combination? *Really* annoying for those kinds of decks. In case you didn't notice, Maverick is largely creatures, and the support spells are all super cheap, topping out on the often-cheap [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) and the oh-so-profligate [Sylvan Library](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Library) at two.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben)  
Maverick is a deck that doesn't do anything outlandishly powerful. It doesn't make a turn-two 15/15, let alone kill you on that second turn. What it does is slow down "broken" decks with Thalia or tutor up [Gaddock Teeg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg)... but can grind out other fair decks with its constant stream of small and incremental advantages.

And it's a nonbasic? How are you supposed to not get blown out by [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)?

*You're not.*

Belcher is the epitome of the all-in deck. If your opponent doesn't have [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)? Probably just dead.

Instead of playing lands, you just play [Land Grant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Grant), which allows you to grab a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) ([Taiga](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taiga) is a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) that can tap for red mana). Belcher—based on the conditions of [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher) itself—doesn't want to play too many lands. You want to find your one to get it out of the deck... And when you go for the [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) side of the deck, [Land Grant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Grant) builds storm as well.

So how does this deck work?

Almost every card in the Belcher deck makes mana or draws a card; so the vast majority of hands with either Charbelcher or [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) can "get there" immediately...

You don't have any [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s or other lands in your deck at this point, but that doesn't matter. Finding a land is only the stop condition on [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher). You can go ahead and reveal a gazillion cards in your deck to kill the opponent.

I am sure you can imagine any number of hands that can, if you replace [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher) with [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens), result in ten goblins on the first turn. While that is theoretically answer-able, the opponent is now on a two-turn clock and had better find an answer in a hurry.

I know what you're thinking:

*The Charbelcher deck really has a lot of "make mana now cards" so it wants a [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher) or [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) in its opening hand... why only three Warrens?*

I'm glad you asked!

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Burning+Wish)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish)  
[Burning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) allows you to "store" an extra [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) in your sideboard; so it's really like you have seven copies of [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) main deck. Plus you can get some bullets like [Shattering Spree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Spree) or [Reverent Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverent+Silence) even in Game 1. [Goblin War Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+War+Strike)? With a [Burning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish), ten Goblins off of a fast [Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) can be even more lethal!

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td202_lucy.jpg)Goblins is of course one of the most celebrated archetypes in the history of Legacy Grand Prix... New Jersey State Champion Jon Sonne took out Chris Pikula's White-Black Deadguy Ale in the finals of a long-ago [Grand Prix Philadelphia](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/events.aspx?x=mtgevent/gpphi05/welcomea), and the reigning Player of the Year put himself on the map with a Goblins finals appearance in the [Flash-Hulk Columbus](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/events.aspx?x=mtgevent/gpcol07/welcome).

J. Sawyer Lucy took Goblins to new and different places while keeping the incentives that have made Goblins, you know, *Goblins* for so long.

Goblins opens on one of two super-powerful one-mana cards: [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) and [Goblin Lackey](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Either of these cards can play the first domino in a line to eventual domination.

[Goblin Lackey](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey) ? [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander)—Yes, this is a thing.

Æther [Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vial) ? All kinds of stuff. Æther [Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vial) simply activates the kinds of draws that would have been awesome anyway, but turns all your creatures into uncounterable instants.

Some Highlights...

[Goblin Matron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Matron)—Finds any Goblin (usually [Goblin Ringleader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Ringleader), but often bullets like [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger)). Matron-into-Warchief (especially riding Æther [Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vial)) can build tremendous momentum.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

[Goblin Piledriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piledriver)—[Goblin Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief) into multiple Piledrivers can create a lethal attack out of nowhere. By the way, you can actually just tap four lands for [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander), which gives each of your Piledrivers an offensive boost.

[Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger)—A one-of (quite find-able with [Goblin Matron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Matron))... for [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)

[Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben)—How did that get in here? Goblins is powerful—and fast for a creature deck—but not unfair in the same way as an insta-[Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) or turn-one Charbelcher kill. Thalia provides resistance to fast kills and combinations. You can access it via the one [Plateau](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plateau) or any Æther [Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vial) draw.

[Gilded Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake)—The "Thalia" of the Goblins sideboard. Accessible via the one [Volcanic Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island), or again, Æther [Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vial). [Gilded Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake)'s job is to steal a big threat after it has already hit (whereas [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) is meant to keep the opposing big win off the table to begin with).

I found Lucy's build of Goblins to be pretty exciting, breathing quite a bit of life into a well established archetype.

Sam's deck is a snowball of synergistic interactions. There are tons of creatures that can come back and come back again ([Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)), which, like the many token producers ([Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls)), combine with sacrifice effects like [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) and [Goblin Bombardment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bombardment) to produce a scary amount of damage (all right, life loss) with [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist).

[Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) is both combo resistance and another sacrifice outlet; [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) is itself more resistance that has some cool synergies with the green-white or any of the sacrifice outlets. When [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) hits the battlefield, you can put its ability on the stack and respond by sacrificing it; it will leave play (triggering the "leaves play" part and presumably returning the opponent's pilfered card, which hasn't been stolen yet); *now* you can get around to forcing the opponent to give up a card in hand... meaning your opponent is never going to get it back if [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) dies!

Sam's deck, of course, also has a regular fast offense... [Carrion Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) on one as well as a 2-power one-drop in [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)... so it is doing all this cool stuff in a short window. By the time the opponent figures out what this "Zombies" deck is doing, it can already be too late.

Pretty great, very all over the place (yet [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand)- and [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul)-free) Top 8... A great showing for Legacy!

Some notes from the champ:


>  "I'd change the sideboard, assuming the metagame doesn't change at all (unlikely) to include two [Surgical Extraction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction)s and zero [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts). You need to answer [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) out of the 'Esper decks' and I don't think there is enough ramp being played to warrant two dead sideboard slots for a likely good matchup.
> 
> "I played against Delver variants three times, defeating all three relatively easily, though I did drop one game when my opponent [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)ted + [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember)ed my [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) while I was holding only lands, and drew only lands afterwards (it happens).
> 
> "I played the deck because I was tired of grinding out a 1% (or less) edge vs the field with Delver. Regardless of the amount of mistakes my opponents would make before or during the match, the games are always close and there's definitely better things to be doing. Delver isn't Caw-Blade: It is beatable.
> 
> "While ordinarily I wouldn't think of or recommend playing a 'rogue deck' I do think there is a lot to be gained through 'deck advantage' (i.e. playing Spirit Delver when everyone else is on regular Delver and not playing [Corrosive Gale](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrosive+Gale), or playing a powerful deck that is actually good and unexpected—Alex West's Faerie Blade in Extended, my [Fiery Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Justice) deck). Sometimes you just need to take an alternate path. Credit to John Kassari for doing well enough with the deck that I saw the decklist, and to Eugene Khutorsky for losing in the Top 8 of the PTQ last weekend, so the deck was still unexpected this week."
> 
> 

Kudos to Josh for taking one down with a very unexpected—yet still quite powerful—strategy. [Temporal Mastery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Mastery) in Ramp? Taking extra turns is pretty good with Titans and Karn both! Plus, having all that extra mana lets you cast your [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) if you need to.

Great finish, and a fine stack of decks to discuss this week, in both Legacy and Standard!
