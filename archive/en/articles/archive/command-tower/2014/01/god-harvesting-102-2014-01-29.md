God Harvesting 102
==================



 Posted in **Command Tower**
 on January 30, 2014 










We're just over a week away from the *Born of the Gods* release (with [Prereleases happening worldwide this weekend](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtgcom/events/Prerelease-facts)!) and I couldn't be more tickled. Sweet new Gods, inspired new cards, new tributes to one of my favorite multiplayer mechanics, and even some new ways to bestow action for my [pauper cube](http://thepaupercube.wordpress.com/) are all there. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ct/2014/ct_wk04_284_cardart_spirefulreturned.jpg)[Spiteful Returned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiteful+Returned) | Art by [Raymond Swanland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Raymond%20Swanland%22%5D)

 Of course, there are more divine matters to deal with. Two weeks ago, I challenged you to show me what you'd do with [Karametra, God of Harvests](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karametra%2C+God+of+Harvests) as [your commander in Commander](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ct/282). While I am an unabashed fan of her triggered ability and shared why, and how, I'd keep her on the battlefield as long as possible, there was no way to avoid her obvious attributes as a fatty. Being the leader of a deck, able to appear early in every game of Commander, is something you proved was far too appealing to pass up. 

I mean, who doesn't like a few decklists to get started on building with the latest set?

The Heavens Plowed
Let's start with what I'd call the "classical" look at using a God as a commander: Powerful creatures and cards, backed by the consistency and durability of a God that can be played early. This version was sent in by Alex:


> I wanted to give my input on how I would use the new God, Karametra, in Commander. I will be using her in my [Karador, Ghost Chieftain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karador%2C+Ghost+Chieftain) deck for sure, as I am always looking for powerful ramp engines on a creature for that deck. I composed a decklist that I feel works well with Karametra's abilities.
>

> In all honestly, Karametra's ability is powerful enough and doesn't require any abusing. Simply playing powerful creatures and triggering her ability even only once a turn is still a powerful application for her.
> 
> The deck has many targeted removal spells to protect itself, but also runs a decent selection of mass-creature and mass-artifact/enchantment wipe. It is a great way to clear the board of many threats and we won't have worry about our commander getting caught in them. She will live to swing in commander damage.
> 
>  Main win conditions are very large, powerful creatures that trigger Karametra's ability, allowing us to play more creatures. [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) and [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) are a great match together, and it is very convenient that Avenger [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)s into Craterhoof. 
> 
> Thank you for viewing my list, I hope it was interesting.
> 
> —Alex

While playing plenty of staples is something some of you don't enjoy, I find it's among the easiest ways to see how a particular commander functions. When the rest of the deck hums along like clockwork, I find it's easier to see if the commander is making a difference or not. Even in decks where I've packed in every powerful card I own, I still want the commander to be a feature and function in the deck.

 For me, it's why I find [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice) so different from [Rhys the Redeemed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) in a dedicated token deck: Rhys punches way above his 1/1 stature. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ct/2014/ct_wk04_284_rhyspunchingtrostani.jpg)  
But let's assume we know Karametra is going to put in the good work we want. What else is possible when she's at the helm of our deck?


>  First of all, Karametra is only the second legendary creature I have seen and immediately gone and built a deck for. (The first one was [Prossh, Skyraider of Kher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prossh%2C+Skyraider+of+Kher).) I think her ability could be absolutely bonkers in the right build. To that end see my attempt attached. Highlights of the deck include: 
> 
> **[Constant Mists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Constant+Mists):**  I think having access to a [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) is fantastic, and as we are going to have an abundance of lands, one we can buy back for a measly land seems pretty good value to me. 
> 
> **[Baru, Fist of Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baru%2C+Fist+of+Krosa):**  There is a minor token subtheme in the deck and while he won't buff the white tokens, playing two creatures out with Baru and [Deranged Hermit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Hermit) or [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) around seems pretty good. 
> 
> **[Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio):**  While it has the added bonus of being able to *Nyx* any pesky [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters)–like effects, it also has the value of letting us re-cast our creatures, a high proportion of which have ETB effects, and allows us to reuse Karametra. 
> 
> **[Budoka Gardener](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Budoka+Gardener):**  Flipping this into Dokai, Weaver of Life shouldn't be too hard in this deck and it should start pumping out some pretty large Elemental tokens. 
> 
> **[Rampaging Baloths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Baloths)/[Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel)/[Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar):**  The landfall mechanic seems good with Karametra.

There's plenty of staples to be had in Archie's deck, but the angle here is closer to where'd I'd probably end up myself. The opportunity to attack with Karametra will probably present itself, but leaning on tokens to put in most of the aggressive work is a tried-and-true approach in Commander.

Landfall makes its case loud and clear here, too, as chaining creatures into lands into bonus effects is what can be best described as a "value train" for us, although "bountiful harvest" would be an acceptable on-flavor alternative.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ct/2014/ctwk04_284_cardart_bountifulharvest.jpg)[Bountiful Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bountiful+Harvest) | Art by [Jason Chan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Jason+Chan%22%5D)

>  Once I had my list, I had to figure out what the game plan was. The first thing that leaped out to me is that [Crucible of Worlds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crucible+of+Worlds) allows me to recur [Grove of the Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Guardian) for a legion of 8/8 tokens. Second, [Seedcradle Witch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seedcradle+Witch) functions as a mana-sink to pump up the oodles of little tokens I am capable of creating, as I will have PLENTY of mana available. Finally, [Plow Through Reito](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plow+Through+Reito) lets me pump up a single unblocked creature while letting me replay my lands for extra benefits (such as [Seed the Land](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seed+the+Land) Snake tokens). 
> 
>

 If you grow it, you can play it. Part of what I love about Commander is that you can build almost any kind of deck you'd see in "normal" **Magic**, but the variety is kicked up to an entirely different level. The idea of a trip to Garden Grove with a deck like this seems like a Vorthos adventure just waiting to happen. 

 There's even a few gems in here I use as staples in my non-flavorful decks: [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel), [Diligent Farmhand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diligent+Farmhand), and [Seedguide Ash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seedguide+Ash) find their way into far more of my decks than you'd think. Almost every deck that's off the beaten path has given me a few new tools to try in more play-focused decks.  If flavor is one side, the opposing path should be littered with rules and thoughtful interactions. In Karametra's case, there's one card Richard found to be perfect to pair with her: [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge). 


>  Karametra is the perfect commander to exploit [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge), a card that is incredibly strong and can win a game the turn it is cast by essentially dumping your entire library into the battlefield and giving your creatures haste with [Akroma's Memorial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Memorial) (and [Elixir of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality), in case you go too far and need to reset). 
> 
> 

  

>  Due to the high mana cost, the deck requires both ramp and card draw to ensure you see [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) in a game and that's where spells like [Sylvan Library](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Library), [Mind's Eye](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind%27s+Eye), and [Scroll Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Rack), alongside card-draw favorite, Garruk, help see a lot of your library while Karametra helps you ramp with your forty creatures in the deck! 
> 
>  Creatures range in purpose, from enchantment creatures to enchant-your-beaters, like [Krond the Dawn-Clad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krond+the+Dawn-Clad) and [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons) (who greatly benefits from any enchantment), to creatures like [Indrik Stomphowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indrik+Stomphowler) and [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime), who are your artifact- and enchantment-removal in a deck that sees no spells or sorceries. 
> 
>  Perhaps the biggest strength of the deck, however, is the [persist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advancedamp;text=+%5Bpersist%5Damp;type=+%5B%22Creature%22%5D) combo made possible by [Cauldron of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cauldron+of+Souls) to compliment your [undying](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advancedamp;text=+%5Bundying%5Damp;type=+%5B%22Creature%22%5D) creatures (although Melira is added in for good measure). This, in a sense, makes your creatures Wrath-proof, thus eliminating your need to run any Wrath spells yourself. Even without this benefit, your creatures are likely to out-power your opponents, especially with monstrosity, an ability that lets you cast creatures cheaply, but rewards you later. 
> 
>

 Instead of a flavorfully tended garden, Richard's deck is built to maximize the number of triggers Karametra creates, and includes a card that effectively puts the entire library into play for fun and profit. [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) is no joke, as [I can attest from personal experience](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/219), and the dream of chaining out fatties to stomp the battlefield flat is far more achievable with all the extra land Karametra can provide. Relying on Auras to help dish out the damage otherwise is an easy way to distract your opponents from the secret Surge lying in wait: heroic creatures tend to speak for themselves when seen. 

 More Decks from *Born of the Gods*
 While I'm still not sure if I'll be building an entire deck around [Karametra, God of Harvests](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karametra%2C+God+of+Harvests), I'm 100% sure she'll be a popular sight across Commander tables soon. I hope you've found a few new tricks to try when you pick up Karametra yourself.
