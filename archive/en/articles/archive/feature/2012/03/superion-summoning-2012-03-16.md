Superion Summoning
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 16, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cardsâ€”and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






The combination of [Myr Superion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion) and [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) has been a topic of discussion since the black enchantment released, and **Magic Online** player Alienware\_ recently played a deck featuring this dynamic duo. Using the power of cheap acceleration alongside several colorless goodies, this deck took down a **Magic Online** Standard Premier Event.


The deck's core engine uses [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning), [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect), and artifact creatures. [Myr Superion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion) is the most explosive one, coming down for free on the same turn you cast either [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) or [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning), but [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph), [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), and [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) are no slouches either. Once this deck gets its mana engines rolling, a threatening army of artifacts follows close behind.


The deck features plenty of ways to help its consistency, with [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), [Treasure Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage), and [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage). Both of the Mages cost only ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) with [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) on the battlefield and can tutor up different tools for the deck to give it some flexibility.


[Treasure Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage) especially helps provide the deck with its finishing move: [Spine of Ish Sah](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spine+of+Ish+Sah). By using the explosive acceleration of [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect), the deck should have plenty of mana to use and reuse the Spine thanks to [Phyrexia's Core](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexia%27s+Core).


Many have tried to put together a deck with [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) and [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning), and this one looks like one of the best out there! Powerful, fun, and versatile, this deck has plenty to offer. Give it a try at Friday Night **Magic** tonight!
