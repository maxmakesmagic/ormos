Round 14: Trey Van Cleave vs. Peter Szigeti
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 5, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a939.jpg)After a long debate whether or not to draw, PTR and TVC sat down to game. These two have a long and sordid history with each other. As recently as GP Los Angeles (Trey's return to Magic after a DCI-enforced sabbatical), Szigeti went out of his way to antagonize and harass. If these two drew, they would both most likely make the top 16, but if they played, one had an outside chance to make the top 8. This seems like the perfect opportunity for Van Cleave to take revenge on his outspoken nemesis.

### Game 1

Trey won the die roll and decided to play. He opened with a first turn [Bloodstained Mire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodstained+Mire) (fetching a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)), using his mana to cast [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll). Peter trumped this draw with a first turn [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study), putting [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) into the graveyard. He followed this with a second turn [Waterfront Bouncer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waterfront+Bouncer), which promptly unsummoned a third-turn face down [Blistering Firecat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat). 

[Seal of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire) gigged the spellshaper, but it was quickly replaced by a 6/6 token creature. Trey activated his [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll) at the end of his opponent's turn, and the two players debated the randomization method of figuring out which card to pull for [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll). On his own turn, the [Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire)cat came back down in hidden mode, and traded for the Wurm on the block.

With the board at a stalemate, PTR hard-cast [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm). Trey fried the trampler with [Flametongue Kavu](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu). PTR tried, tried again with a second Wurm, which traded on the block, netting Van Cleave a two for one.

Trey began casting [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic)s and activating his [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll). PTR insisted on hand-picking the [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll) card, while Van Cleave insisted on rolling a die to randomly determine.

PTR: "Why should it matter if I pick one?"  
 Trey: "I'd just prefer to roll a die."  
 PTR: (Getting belligerent): "What's it matter?"  
 Trey: "Because a die is completely random."  
 Judge (stepping in): "Roll the die."

Trey's slow and methodical play, especially given that he was playing an aggressive red deck, seemed to grow on PTR's patience. Szigeti scowled, even as flashing back [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and getting [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) + [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) into play. Even as Trey tapped mana to activate his artifact again, Szigeti angrily snatched the die off the table. In response, Trey repeatedly asked to see Szigeti's graveyard. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a941.jpg)The game ground to even more of a halt as Van Cleave attempted to cast a second [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll) with Peter at eight life. Peter responded with [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) (which brought [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) to his hand and sent two [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder)s to the bin). This allowed him to cast [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) with enough cards in graveyard to stop the bleeding. It also made his [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) a 3/1, allowing Trey to clear the board with a Scroll activation plus a [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic). 

PTR rushed through his next couple of turns, hard-casting [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm), and then flashing it back with [Waterfront Bouncer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waterfront+Bouncer) support. Trey meanwhile whittled away at the life count, but ended up on the wrong end of an 11-5 life total—even though he was technically ahead, the two flying Wurms would end the game faster than the remaining [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) plus the one activation of [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll) left. Trey drew—[Blistering Firecat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat). The hasting trampler came down full fury, forcing a block and getting through a point of trample damage. This gave Trey the exact edge he needed to win the game.

Trey Van Cleave 1 – Peter Szigeti 0

### Game 2

Both players were doing their best to keep their heads in the match, but the game just kept getting more and more personal. Trey did his best not to play any sort of mind games, but PTR would continually stare down his opponent. The contrast and tension between the two was openly palpable. 

PTR began with [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), and Van Cleave answered with [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic). The pumper stayed back for defense, and blocked the goblin. In a game of mental chicken, the two competitors stared each other down, PTR hoping that Trey would attempt to sacrifice the Mogg so he could save his [Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwalla). Even as both players moved their cards towards the graveyard, you could feel Szigeti waiting for Trey to make a mistake.

A second [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) took one for the team to kill [Waterfront Bouncer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waterfront+Bouncer). Trey looked to run away with the game, playing out [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll) and [Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup) to PTR's second Bouncer of the game—but PTR played [Powder Keg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg), which wiped the board clear of threats. Trey did manage, however, to kill the Bouncer with a fifty-fifty chance using the [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll).

[Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) once again came down for five mana, except this time it swung across and empty board. Trey began building up his side again with a second Scroll and a [Powder Keg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg) of his own. After a couple of swings, the burn deck found a [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) to combine with the one casting cost artifact to kill the big creature. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpno03/a940.jpg)The whole while, Trey decided to hold back putting counters on his own [Powder Keg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg). This gave Szigeti the opportunity to draw a [Powder Keg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg) of his own, which summarily dismissed [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll) from the game. If Van Cleave had put even a single counter on his Keg, he would have been able to save his more important artifact. It took an [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) (the first play of the game after several turns of land-laying) to finally prompt Van Cleave to build up his boom-device.

The game stopped with twelve minutes left in the round as Trey cast [Flametongue Kavu](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu). PTR assumed that the targeting from the killer creature had already been decided, so he responded by discarding [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) to the [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba). Trey thought that the madness occurred in response to the [FTK](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=FTK), so he wanted to kill the 4/4 trampler. Colin Jackson came in to adjudicate the situation, ruling in favor of PTR. 

As play resumed, Trey also accumulated an official warning for slow play. He quickly hurried up his game, killing the [Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootwalla) with [Powder Keg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg), [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt)ing a freshly occurring [Waterfront Bouncer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waterfront+Bouncer), and swinging his Kavu back and forth past the [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), which kept turning sideways. It was obvious that neither player seemed concerned with blocking. Finally, Trey began drawing burn, and managed to win the game with a flashed [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) the turn before he would have died to trample damage from the Wurm.

**Final Result:** Trey Van Cleave 2 – Peter Szigeti 0
