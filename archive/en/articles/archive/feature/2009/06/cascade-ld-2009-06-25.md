One of the surprise hits to come from Grand Prix-Sao Paulo was Diego Cruciusâ€™s Cascade list. Foregoing the usual control elements or the [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault)/[Swans of Bryn Argoll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swans+of+Bryn+Argoll+)  combo, Diego ramped all the way up to [Enlisted Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlisted+Wurm+)  and was all too happy to cascade into either [Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Primal%2BCommand) or [Incendiary Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incendiary+Command). After board, he could even morph into a Land Destruction deck thanks to [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage+)  and [Rain of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rain+of+Tears). On top of that, due to a miscommunication with the friend who gave him the deck, Diego played an [Adarkar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes+)  instead of a [Llanowar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes+)  with no blue cards in his deck! You can read more about Grand Prix-Sao Paulo in the [official event coverage](/en/events/coverage/daniel-dominates-defending-brazil).
