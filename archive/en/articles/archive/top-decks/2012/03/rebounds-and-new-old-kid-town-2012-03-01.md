Rebounds and the New Old Kid in Town
====================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on March 1, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






Never one to bury the lead:



> 
> * Old favorite Blue-Black Control, after some time on the Standard bench, rebounded back in the hands of PT and GP Champions alike to play It Girl of the recent Grand Prix in Baltimore. Blue-Black Control posted three spots in the Top 8!
> * Matt Costa, already being called the Pro Tour's veritable Geist of **Magic** Future, made a return trip to the Top 8 tables with his White-Blue [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) deck... this time taking the title.
> * And... *wait for it*... after a Red-Green Wolf Run mirror in the Top 8 of Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*, the red-green in the GP Top 8 was of a very different breed. Difficult as it might be to fathom, there were no Wolf Run *ramp* decks in the Baltimore Top 8!
>

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/td/td184_costa.jpg)*Matt Costa*Costa's was the only Delver in the Top 8, as well as the eventual winner. And despite the standout performances of the [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) and Spirits version at Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*, Costa's performance came with the old faithful version: straight white-blue with Equipment.


Delver is, more or less, as Delver was. The deck is a super-tight white-blue aggro-control with only twenty-one lands. Costa's list (all Delver lists, really) rely on the [Comer](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/2005-11-22-2) [Xerox](/en/articles/archive/ub-trippin%E2%80%99-2005-04-21) model in terms of mana. Not only are the cards in the deck very inexpensive, but with four [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), four [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), and two [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), the deck plays like it has about twenty-six lands (though early-turn mana is often spoken for).


The Delver deck can get overwhelmingly powerful draws. Turn one [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), turn two double Delver (it's happened!) can put an opponent away with the speed of a combo or Sligh deck. Less ridiculous outliers (say, "just" turn one [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)) can put an opponent on a serious clock while managing the other player's board with backbreaking instants like [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) or [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag).

In addition to [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), Costa has always been happy to lean on Phyrexian mana; [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) and [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) give the deck functionality outside the typical blue and white; i.e., "killin' stuff."


Differences relative to Costa's Pro Tour Top 8 version:



> 
> * One more [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) (bringing the deck up to fifteen creatures). This gives the deck one more body for [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) and another swordsman for his Equipment.
> * Speaking of which, one more [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike). Costa played four total Equipment in both decks, moving away from [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) by one.
> * No [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s, one more [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember).
> * ...and a substantially different sideboard.
> 


Costa's PT sideboard had way more (or at least different) control elements ([Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx), [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment)) but moved to [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) as a supplemental threat and source of lifegain (in place of the third [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements)). A big addition adopted by many decks was [Corrosive Gale](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrosive+Gale). [Corrosive Gale](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrosive+Gale) is a card that any deck willing to pay 2 life for Phyrexian green can play... to smash Spirits.

Surprisingly, the straight Delver deck doesn't have *that* many flying creatures. ### Blue-Black Control


Blue-Black Control was one of the big stories of GP Baltimore. Had the last match gone the other way, in favor of Dave Shiels (Shiels was the winner, last year, of the 32 [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)/32 [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) GP at the height of CawBlade, you might remember), it might have given an even more dramatic narrative.


Even given Costa's impressive finish with White-Blue Delver, the list of players with Blue-Black Control in tow was anything but unimpressive, with "Geist of **Magic** Present" Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa one of three players with the strategy playing in the Top 8.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/td/td184_scr.jpg)*Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, Max Tietze, and Dave Shiels.*  
All three decks had a surprising amount of elements in common:



> 
> * Twenty-seven lands, although they differed on the number of [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard)s they ran main (PV played two, Shiels played three, and Tietze played all four). But all three players had a total of four copies between main deck and sideboard.
> * Relatively few creatures. Tietze played four total, with two [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s; PV and Shiels each played five, with three [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s.
> * The spell rundowns were all also very consistent: three [Black Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith), one [Blue Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blue+Sun%27s+Zenith), two [Curse of Death's Hold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold), two [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate)s, etc., etc. Everyone liked [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip), but not everyone played [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) (none for PV); Tietze traded in his last creature-finisher for [Sorin Markov](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin+Markov)!
> * Consistently few or no copies of [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated). Karn has been a steady two-of in most blue-black decks since its coming-out party in the hands of Reid Duke and Gerard Fabiano in late spring 2011 (at the height of CawBlade). In Baltimore, only Tietze played Karn—one copy—and it was in his sideboard.
> 


So what about those finishers?


Shiels commented that [Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) (a two-of in his main) won him the most games in the GP; PV played none of those (opting for double [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan)s instead). Max played one of each (again, along with a Sorin starting and the only Karn of the Top 8 on the bench).


![Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Consecrated+Sphinx)  
One thing to note: Shiels played [Go for the Throat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) over the more commonly chosen [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)... Which just happens to be a mana effective (if somewhat incomplete) solution to an opposing [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/td/td184_scott.jpg)*Matt Scott*Matt Scott's Blue-Black Zombies is one of the first looks we have at what should prove to be a very exciting archetype. With [Diregraf Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Captain) providing the boost, this Zombies deck is like the offensive twin to [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain), [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists), and their Spirit squad.


Zombies is quick. [Diregraf Ghoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul) and [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)—with or without Captain backup—are turn one [Jackal Pup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup)-type creatures. They have 2 power and only cost one mana. Worse yet, one of them (at least) is awfully persistent.


[Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest) is secretly one of the most powerful two-drops in Standard. Turn one 2/X Zombie, turn two [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest), turn three [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod)? You can quickly put yourself in the 5/5 Demon token business if this Cleric is left unchecked to an untap.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Skirsdag High Priest | Mortarpod |

The Zombies deck has lots of potential morbid to help set up [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip), as well as the High Priest. It can toss creatures around and get them back, like with [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) (or with the undying on [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger)).


Meanwhile, it can smash the opponent for an extra point with [Geth's Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geth%27s+Verdict) or try to take control via the generally underplayed [Phyrexian Obliterator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Obliterator).


...Do we finally have a card that can make Obliterator look good?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/td/td184_meng.jpg)*Eric Meng*French Frites, the nuts draw:



> 
> **Turn 1:** [Copperline Gorge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Copperline+Gorge), [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise).  
> **Turn 2:** [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), discarding [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) and [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). Find and play a [Rootbound Crag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootbound+Crag).  
> **Turn 3:** Casually tap the [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) for white, add another land, and flashback the [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) for a ho-hum turn-three [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite).
> 
> 
> 


There are lots of variations and generally good draws ([Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) can fill your graveyard, or you can just play another accelerator on turn two to hard-cast [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites)), but the clear thing is that in terms of the intersection of power and speed... it is hard to argue with Frites. The deck has an eight pack of creature-hating finishing fatties, and the ~~eight~~ *nine*-pack of Birds, Elves, and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim)s give the deck the capability to hit that third-turn reanimation.


Along with Zombies, Frites is a new-ish archetype you should watch in the coming months. It has a lot of the power we see in the Wolf Run or blue-black decks, but gets there faster. Moreover, quick [Grand Cenobites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobites) and [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan)s are just spectacular against any kind of fast creature deck. No [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration)s or random Humans are likely to live.


![Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inferno+Titan)  
With Frites now establishing itself in the mix, we have quite an interesting Standard cocktail that includes a kind of combo deck. Blue-black's rebounding gives us a solid control. We have never hurt for aggro representatives (Humans probably being the highest profile, but this would also include [Tempered Steel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel), Red Decks, and so on). And we have been spoiled by CounterSliver (that is, Delver) and Tinker (Wolf Run Ramp), giving us quite the metagame polygon.


The question is... What's next?


Formats with lots of viable options tend to reward the most explosive utilizations of mana and raw power. Prior to this week's results, I would have guessed Wolf Run would best fit the bill, but I dunno... Frites looks like it might be a tad faster and just as *Titanic.*


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/td/td184_mana.jpg)[Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) | Art by [Howard Lyon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Howard+Lyon%22%5D)


But when decks like those are good, you know what can be even better? *Playing all four copies of [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)* (like Matt Costa and the blue-black players all did).


The Standard battles look to be increasingly unstable and epic!


Good luck, whichever direction you choose.
