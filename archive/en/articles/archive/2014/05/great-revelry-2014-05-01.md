Adam "Stybs" Styborski joined DailyMTG.com in 2009 to take over Serious Fun, before switching over to begin Command Tower in 2013. With his passion for Commander and community inclusion, you'll find plenty of opportunity each week to share your thoughts about everyone's favorite casual format.


May 1, 2014
 










It's all the rage these days to kill a God.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/features/2014/wlnglaanse_art_1d.jpg)
[Deicide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deicide) | Art by [Jason Chan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/pages/search/default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=[%22jason+chan%22])


 


Who needs an indestructible enchantment that can turn into an opponent-slaying personification of a mortal attribute?"


I do.


From the first taste I saw of [the pantheon of Theros](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ct/263)—[Erebos, God of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erebos%2C+God+of+the+Dead)—to the [mana machinations](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ct/282) of [Karametra, God of Harvests](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karametra%2C+God+of+Harvests) [and](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ct/294) [Kruphix, God of Horizons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruphix%2C+God+of+Horizons), I've been in love with the power of the gods of Theros in Commander.

### God is in the TV


One of my favorite new Gods is [Pharika, God of Affliction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%2C+God+of+Affliction), and her handy ability to transform creatures in graveyards into annoying little Snakes with deathtouch.


Andrew felt the same way:



> 
> Players can't seem to decide if [Pharika, God of Affliction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%2C+God+of+Affliction) is good or not. I'm not answering that question at all, because this is a flavor-packed Pharika deck that features Gorgons and Snakes. I looked for all the cards representative of Pharika, Vraska, and the venomous side of the Golgari.
> 
> 
>

I like that this deck can sort of play a Group Hug role; Pharika can give other players instant-speed deathtouchy blockers, and there are plenty of other, "Hey, you, have some deathtouch!" cards. Otherwise, it's a pretty standard "kill everything until you win" style of deck.

> 
> I've been playing off-and-on since *Antiquities*. My first well-tuned deck featured [Verduran Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verduran+Enchantress), [Fastbond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fastbond), and [Contract from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contract+from+Below). I could churn through over half of my (forty-card) deck in a single turn! I've loved green-black ever since.
> 
> 
> My favorite commander is [Sliver Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Queen), not because I build a Sliver deck, but because I use her super-cheap creature generation as an engine for all kinds of combos.
> 
> 
> 
> ![Sliver Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Sliver%20Queen&options=)
> 
>  
> 
> That means that [Pharika, God of Affliction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%2C+God+of+Affliction) is an absolute must for me. She's easier to cast, indestructible, and her children have deathtouch! Her drawbacks compared to [Sliver Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Queen) are two-fold: fewer colors means fewer/weaker combos for which to act as an engine (i.e., no Purphoros, [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower), [Stalking Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Vengeance)); and she's much less likely to score a win with commander damage.
> 
> 
> Fewer combos doesn't mean none, though. I believe that Pharika and Savra will be good friends, for example. Probably the most complicated combo in the submitted deck is Pharika + [Life Chisel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+Chisel) + [Dark Prophecy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Prophecy) + [Cadaverous Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cadaverous+Bloom) + [Grim Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Guardian) + one colored mana = win the game.
> 
> 
>

> The other notable thing about Pharika is her hug factor. She'll happily share the deathtouch pain with anyone being attacked by ground-pounders, thereby giving you more time to pull together your combos.
> 
> 
> My first build will probably feature a lot of *Journey into Nyx* cards, just to try them out, but there's a lot of potential!

Another God I favor for my own deck building is [Iroas, God of Victory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iroas%2C+God+of+Victory). [Last week](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ct/296), I started a decidedly creature-focused look at building a Commander deck. Archie, quite the contrarian, looked in the opposite direction:



> 
> The God I'm most excited to build a deck around is [Iroas, God of Victory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iroas%2C+God+of+Victory) but not for the reasons you might expect. The twist—no creature spells, not a single one.
> 
> 
> I started looking at red and white enchantments, looking at what would be good for devotion, and came across a few beauties I hadn't seen before, namely [Burning Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Sands), [Æther Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Flash), and [Powerstone Minefield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powerstone+Minefield). So the idea for the deck was born. There are a couple of creature producers in the deck: [Sigil of the Empty Throne](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne) is too good to pass up and plays well with the 2-damage enchantments; [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion) is good devotion and the -3 ability is very useful; and, technically, [Ajani, Caller of the Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Caller+of+the+Pride) is also a token producer.
> 
> 
> 
> 
> |  |  |
> | --- | --- |
> | 
> Elspeth, Sun's Champion
>  | 
> Ajani, Caller of the Pride
>  |
> 
> 
>  
> 
> The coolest thing about creating this deck was finding a lot of cards I just didn't know existed. [Serra's Liturgy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra%27s+Liturgy) is a cool card with good devotion and a potential to be very powerful, [Repercussion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repercussion) is an old standby and plays very well with [Pyrohemia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrohemia).
> 
> 
> I'm very much looking forward to playing this deck and seeing if it's even possible to win with it, but I know it will be a lot of fun to play, regardless.
>

> —Archie

Trying things off the wall is something Commander leaves plenty of room to do, but sticking to tried, true, potent strategies is just as common. Matt's look at [Kruphix, God of Horizons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruphix%2C+God+of+Horizons) is exactly what I'd expect:



> 
> One that I'm tempted to build a deck around is [Kruphix, God of Horizons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruphix%2C+God+of+Horizons). My most recent commander is Momir Vig, and my love for Simic is probably what made me want to build this. Kruphix's ability to retain mana is most likely what the core of the deck will be built around, and he'll run somewhat similar to an [Omnath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omnath), but with more things to use and a lower inclination to keep the mana there.

If you had any doubts about the right way to handle this after your event, Andrew also had the perfect solution:



> 
> Don't be fooled by the commander, this deck is as God-centric as they come. Although the commander is [Child of Alara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Alara), the deck was built because I wanted to put ALL THE GODS into one deck. The indisputable core of the deck are the fifteen Gods and the five weapons. Then I stuff in as many multicolor-weight cards as I can to turn on devotion. Plus some necessary mana assistance and card drawing. I've been receiving lots of help on this deck in the Wizards forums (shout out to niheloim!) and it's changed a lot from what I first picked as the card selection. I even strayed away from Child for a bit before niheloim informed me that Child's boom trigger doesn't go off if I send it to the command zone, so I don't have to wipe the field if I don't want to. There is a one-card combo in the deck that was suggested to me by SeveredAngel: [Defense of the Heart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defense+of+the+Heart). ### Born Again


We've spent the last few weeks looking at *additive* ways to grow your collection of Commander decks: Shiny new cards, sweet new Gods, and oodles of flavor make it easy to find things to try. Next week, I want to get back to another angle of Commander deck building: **Which card or cards have you cut from your favorite deck, and why?**


* Feedback via email
* 300-word limit to explain your thoughts around recently removing some cards for others
* **Sample decklist is requested** (does not count against word limit)
* Decklists should be formatted with one card per line with just a leading number, such as "3 Mountain"—just a space (no "x" or "-") between the number and the card name, without subtotals by card type (Submissions that don't follow this rule will be ignored.)
* Name and email required (non-personal information to be used in column)

In order to add new features to a deck it's obvious that something else will have to go. With so many favorites to work with, and awesome cards in our decks we've come to love, finding "the right one" to take out is, in editor-speak, *killing our darlings*.


I want to know what you've recently cleared out for something new, and why you settled on what you did. I'm looking forward to tales of murder and pain... all for the greater Commander good, of course.


See you next week!
