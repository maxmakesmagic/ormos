2004 North American Challenge Semifinals Feature Match
======================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 10, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_alexshvartsman.jpg)
By Alex Shvartsman











Prior to the start of this match, the Top 4 agreed to split the money evenly with each player getting $1250. However, they were going to play out the top four for rating points, glory, and an automatic invite to next year's NAC!


### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/nac04/aRietzl2.jpg)*Paul Rietzl*Rietzl had to mulligan to six and his draw was hardly impressive. He played out two [Darksteel Citadel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel)s, two [Welding Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar)s, an [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and a [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite). With just one card in hand, his in-play pressure equaled a single [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite)!


Guevin played [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves), then cast [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom) and a [Vine Trellis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vine+Trellis). Rietzl top-decked a [Glimmervoid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmervoid) and played his last card – [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). "Wow, now I can draw [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast)*or*
[Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) to win!"


While Guevin now had access to all the mana he could want, he had nothing to do with it. Rietzl, on the other hand, top-decked a [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) true to his word, and cast an [Arcbound Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) he drew off of it. He then attacked with [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite), both of which Guevin was able to block. Rietzl sacrificed a total of five artifacts to Ravager and then sacked it to *itself*, moving six counters onto the [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter). The Birds died and Guevin was down to eleven life points – Rietzl would need to successfully attack twice more before Guevin drew [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) or any of his huge creatures to win.


Guevin cast a second [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom) and then a [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman), attempting to destroy the [Ornithopter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter). Rietzl used his last artifact – a [Welding Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) – to regenerate it. On his own turn, Rietzl attacked his opponent down to five and played a [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite). 


### Sideboarding


**Rietzl**:  
 +3 [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), -3 [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast)


**Guevin**:  
 +4 [Oxidize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize), +2 [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize), +1 [Leonin Abunas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Abunas), +1 [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel), +1 [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion), +1 [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman)  
 -4 [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), -2 [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom), -1 Kamahl, -1 [Duplicant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant), -1 Akroma, -1 [Darksteel Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus)


### Game 2


Rietzl opened with a bunch of inexpensive artifacts as usual, while Guevin's post-sideboard plan was the standard "kill the really bothersome artifacts". He [Oxidize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize)d a [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) and then used [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) to get rid of [Welding Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar). A second Shaman took out a Ravager, with counters moving onto an [Arcbound Worker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker).


The two Shamans then traded with a [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer), leaving nothing but a 2/2 Worker on Rietzl's side of the table. Guevin's only remaining creature was a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and he sacrificed it to the [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) gods. [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves) were more Clamp fodder, making this game look very favorable for Guevin.


Rietzl got out another [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer). Guevin's answer was to play his seventh land and cast [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom). The [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp)-equipped Myr attacked, but then Guevin got to untap with access to fourteen green mana in play.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/nac04/aGuevinTom.jpg)*Tom Guevin*[Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) with entwine produced some surprisingly small creatures – a [Wirewood Symbiote](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote) and a [Wirewood Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Herald). Despite not getting much larger creatures, this was exactly what Tom needed to gain total control of the game – he would be able to kill two artifacts per turn and there were no outs in Rietzl's deck that he could draw soon enough to matter. 


### Sideboarding


**Guevin**:  
 +2 [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), -2 [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom)


**Rietzl**:  
 No Changes


### Game 3


Rietzl kept a slowish hand, playing nothing but a land on turn one.


"If it's [Oxidize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize), I am in a world of trouble," he commented as Guevin was meddling with a card in his hand. It was in fact [Oxidize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize), taking out his first land. Rietzl top-decked a [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) and played it out, but Guevin was ready with a second [Oxidize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize), and he chose to kill the land again.


Rietzl just kept drawing more lands and pretty soon he had a pair of Ravagers, a [Myr Enforcer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) and a pair of [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp)s in play. Guevin kept cursing himself over a supposed mistake of killing a land over the first [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp), though most spectators thought he made the right play based on the information he had to work with at the time.


Guevin tried to generate mana as quickly as possible, playing out some [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves) and [Vine Trellis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vine+Trellis)es – but he was also forced to start chump-blocking all of Rietzl's artifact creatures as Rietzl used double [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) to draw four cards off of a [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and played several more artifacts along with a [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). 


Guevin survived two attacks, but he could not draw any answers and finally was unable to block a [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite). Rietzl sacrificed his artifacts to give it +10/+10, ending the game and Guevin's chances of winning the NAC title for the second time.


**Final Results:** Rietzl 2 – Guevin 1
