Nightmares of Devotion
======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 8, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












After falling off for a bit, "Mono-Black" Devotion is back! As it turns out, splashing red and splashing blue are both options that help Mono-Black reclaim its top spot in today's metagame. Last week, [I covered Eric Froehlich's BR Devotion](/en/articles/archive/new-flavor-devotion-2014-04-01-0), and today I have [Tamás Glied](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/feature/292#2)'s UB Devotion. 

![Ashiok, Nightmare Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ashiok%2C+Nightmare+Weaver) Under the screen name Mazuku94, Glied ran the tables with his version of devotion at the [**Magic Online** Championship](/en/events/coverage/dam-crowned-magic-online-champion). On top of the unusual choice to splash [Notion Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Thief), Glied also had the full four [Ashiok, Nightmare Weaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashiok%2C+Nightmare+Weaver)s in his seventy-five! It's clear Glied was ready for control decks, but even though there were only four in the field, he still won all of his Swiss matches. 

 Yuuya Watanabe won [Grand Prix Beijing](/en/events/coverage/watanabe-does-wonders) with Mono-Black Devotion featuring [Staff of the Death Magus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staff+of+the+Death+Magus), which will likely work its way into the sideboard of any black devotion deck. A couple other options I've seen floating around in the UB version are main-deck [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%26+Away) or sideboard [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate). 

Week after week, the innovators are getting paid off, even if it's only the addition of a new card or two. Those cards can make all the difference!
