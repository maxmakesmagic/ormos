Drafting with Ng Soon Lye
=========================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX HONG KONG 2015](/en/events/coverage/gphk15)
 on August 8, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman_icon_0.jpg)
By Chapman Sim










The Top 8 of Grand Prix Hong Kong 2013 was a star-studded one. Shuhei Nakamura won it all, defeating "BFF" Martin Juza in the finals, while Ken Yukuhiro and Yuuta Takahashi had their journey cut short in the semifinals.


A lesser-known Singapore also made the Top 8 that event, and happens to be lurking at the top of the standings today. Introducing Ng Soon Lye, one of the Top 8 competitors of Grand Prix Hong Kong 2013.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gphk15/gpHK_D2_Ng_Soon_Lye.jpg)  



Ng's *Magic Origins* dates back over a decade and is considered a veteran of Singapore's *Magic* community. In fact, I've had the honor of playing against Ng at the Top 8 of Singapore Nationals 2009.


After navigating through the Sealed Deck portion to finish 8-1, it was a good opportunity to check in with Ng to see if he can do a "back-to-back" stunt!


Pack 1
======


Ng's first booster presented him with weak commons and uncommons, but he wasted no time picking the rare. "Life is such a fragile thing," also happens to be my favorite flavor text in *Magic Origins*.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Languish)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish)
This Constructed-worthy board sweeper set the tone for Ng to draft a midrange or control deck and he soon decided to pair Black with Blue, picking up a pair of [Ringwarden Owl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ringwarden+Owl)s 3rd and 6th.


He was gifted with a 7th pick [Reave Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reave+Soul), which was not only one of the top black commons but more importantly, it was a strong signal from the players to his right that black was open. Sure enough, a late [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) and [Necromantic Summons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromantic+Summons) confirmed Ng's suspicions.

Pack 2
======


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Unholy+Hunger)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unholy+Hunger)
Ng's second booster presented him with [Managorger Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Managorger+Hydra) and [Sentinel of the Eternal Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel+of+the+Eternal+Watch), two extremely powerful cards that he could not play. Thankfully, there was [Unholy Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unholy+Hunger) in that booster which he was happy to pick up. He deliberated between [Tower Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Geist) and [Separatist Voidmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Separatist+Voidmage), and eventually decided on the latter. [Claustrophobia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claustrophobia) over [Fetid Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fetid+Imp) was Ng's third pick, and the makes of a powerful Blue Black "Midrange Control" deck was starting to shape up.


However, the power level of the cards for Blue and Black started to decline after Ng's first three picks but Ng took advantage of the weak selections to pick up [Runed Servitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runed+Servitor), [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) and [Screeching Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Skaab) to fill out his curve. A seventh pick [Relic Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+Seeker) was a shocker, but Ng declined to hate draft, preferring to pick up [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) for his sideboard.

### Pack 3 Picks


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Whirler+Rogue)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirler+Rogue)
[Whirler Rogue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirler+Rogue) was the no-brainer pick, even if Ng did have to pass a [Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) and [Blazing Hellhound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Hellhound) to his neighbors. [Eyeblight Massacre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyeblight+Massacre) was the second mass removal spell to join Ng's deck, a card that would complement his pair of [Shambling Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambling+Ghoul)s and [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk)s perfectly.


[Tower Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Geist) and a second [Reave Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reave+Soul) and a second [Unholy Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unholy+Hunger) added more raw power to Ng's deck. A crucial [Fetid Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fetid+Imp) tabled, adding his another coveted two-drop to the deck.
