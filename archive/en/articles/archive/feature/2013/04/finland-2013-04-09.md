Finland
=======



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 9, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 






[![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/wmcq_small.jpg)](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup/qualifiers)
The Finnish WMCQ is significant not only because it was won by a relatively new and interesting deck, but because Sami Häggkvist won both the WMCQ and the PTQ with essentially the same deck! That deck was a WBR deck that somehow closely resembles both an *Innistrad* Block Constructed deck (a control deck that used [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) to discard [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) played by Tom Martell in [Grand Prix Anaheim](/en/events/coverage/x-marcs-spot)) and the WBR Midrange deck that's popular on **Magic Online** in *Return to Ravnica* Block Constructed. 

 Sami's deck uses the same core flashback cards, but instead of trying to set up the ambitious play of reanimating [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand), this deck settles for returning a creature that costs a mere four or five mana. But it's still usually good enough to win the game, anyway. 

 Sami has a wide variety of two-mana removal spells ([Dreadbore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore), [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), and [Ultimate Price](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price)), followed by three-mana plays that blunt an offense ([Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), and [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)), into four- and five-mana creatures that can take over a game ([Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren); [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council); [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite)). This curve lets him hold off and then trump creatures, and the combination of [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil); [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council); and [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) make life very difficult for control decks. 

 This is the kind of deck that historically has problems of "drawing the wrong half" of its cards in a given matchup—[Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) against control and [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return)s against aggro—but Sami has [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) to pull it all together and help him construct a hand that has the right tools for the job. 

 With this deck and [Brad Nelson's Act Two](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/coverage/deck_tech_the_aristocrats_act_.html) debuting in the same weekend, fans of red, black, and white decks, myself included, have a lot to consider moving f orward. 

 I look forward to seeing how Sami Häggkvist performs at both the World **Magic** Cup in Amsterdam and Pro Tour *Theros* in Dublin after his impressive weekend in Finland. 

 You can join him in Amsterdam, if you've earned enough Planeswalker Points, by winning a World **Magic** Cup Qualifier in your country on [April 20–21](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup/qualifierlist).
