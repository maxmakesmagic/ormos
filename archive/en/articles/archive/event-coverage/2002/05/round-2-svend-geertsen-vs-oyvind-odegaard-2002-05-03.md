Round 2: Svend Geertsen vs. Oyvind Odegaard
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 3, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a956.jpg)Svend Geertsen of Denmark used to be one of the very best, but his studies at university have limited the time he can devote to Magic, so now he only attends limited PT's. His opponent, Norway's Oyvind Odegaard, was a member of last years Worlds Team runner-up, and has been enjoying a decent level of success since.

The two were seated across from each other during the draft, so they were not aware of what each other was playing. Svend lucked out and got a good blue/green deck, and Odegaard ended up with black/white.

Oyvind drew a creatureless opening hand of five land, [Ghastly Demise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise), and [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts), but he managed to find and cast a [Mystic Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Zealot) before Svend could get any damage through with his [Twigwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twigwalker) and [Springing Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springing+Tiger). When Oyvind said "go" at five mana, Svend coughed and said, "I smell [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts)." Oyvind played along.

"That's right. Never attack."

Oyvind added a [Soul Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Scourge) and [Kirtar's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Desire)d Svend's Tiger. Svend made the tough decision to [Ghostly Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Wings) his enchanted Tiger, turning it into a 4/4 flying wall. Oyvind raised his eyebrows.

The Norwegian had been drawing a steady stream of creatures, though, and added a [Cabal Torturer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer) and a [Frightcrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler), which could break the stalemate.

Svend had his own ideas, sending in a [Krosan Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Archer) and a [Cephalid Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Aristocrat) with [Twigwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twigwalker) backup. Oyvind blocked the Archer with his Zealot, trading it for the [Twigwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twigwalker).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a955.jpg)Svend attacked again with the Aristocrat; Oyvind tried to [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts) it, but Svend had [Rites of Refusal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Refusal), so Oyvind was reduced to using his Torturer to prevent one point of damage. The "milling" effect of the Aristocrat, however, had given Svend threshold, so he discarded a card to [Ghostly Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Wings), returning his Tiger and recasting it as a 5/5.

The next turn was a big train wreck of creatures dying – Svend attacked with most of his forces, and Oyvind used good blocking, the [Cabal Torturer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer), and a [Ghastly Demise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghastly+Demise) on the Tiger to wipe out most of the Dane's relevant monsters. He slowly took control of the board with a [Whispering Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Shade) and an [Aven Cloudchaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Cloudchaser) as the [Frightcrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler) attacked for one per turn... 15... 14... 13...12...

Oyvind eventually reached threshold himself by sacrificing a Pilgrim at the en of Svend's turn, making his Torturer's –2/-2 ability active and pumping the [Frightcrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler) to 3/3. The Torturer whacked Svend's [Leaf Dancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaf+Dancer), then the [Frightcrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler) brought him to 9.   
 But by this time, Svend had regrouped with a [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) and an [Aven Windreader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader). A [Churning Eddy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Churning+Eddy) on Oyvind's Shade and his own [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) (for Mongrel food) allowed him to attack Oyvind to one.

But one is not zero, and Svend's peeks with the Windreader showed nothing but hits on the top of Oyvind's library – [Morgue Theft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morgue+Theft) and [Teroh's Faithful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful). The extra cards and extra life proved vital, and after the [Frightcrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler) traded with the Mongrel, the Shade went onto the offensive and Svend couldn't stop it.

Odegaard 1 – Geertsen 0

After the game, Mark Rosewater let the players know that they only had 18 minutes left, so the two shuffled quickly.

Oyvind played a turn two [Frightcrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightcrawler), and Svend dropped a [Leaf Dancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaf+Dancer). When Oyvind chose not to attack, Svend correctly predicted that he had [Gallantry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gallantry).

"I have it all," replied the Norwegian.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptnice02/a952.jpg)Svend made the smart play of casting [Churning Eddy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Churning+Eddy) before attacking, setting Oyvind's [Gallantry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gallantry) plan back two turns. On his next turn he played an Aristocrat, and then [Centaur Chieftain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Chieftain) the turn after. [Sylvan Might](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Might) saw to it that the Aristocrat busted through Oyvind's [Blessed Orator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Orator). A continuous supply of large creatures from Svend prevented Oyvind from ever regaining his footing.

Odegaard 1 – Geertsen 1

Game 3 began with 4:30 left on the clock.

Oyvind stopped at two land, whereas Svend ramped up to four right away via [Diligent Farmhand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diligent+Farmhand) and cast a [Springing Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springing+Tiger), then a [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear), then a [Scrivener](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrivener). Odegaard never even got started, and was run over in a few quick turns. He did make a point to play at a reasonable pace, even when his death was imminent, and Svend won just as time ran out.

**Final Result:** Odegaard 1 – Geertsen 2
