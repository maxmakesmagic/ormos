Matteo Makes It in Malmo
========================




May 19-20, 2012












Grand Prix Malmo 2012
---------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmal12/champion_620.jpg)It's been a great weekend for **Magic** here in Malmo, Sweden. 921 players showed up to take part in the first Grand Prix of the 2012–2013 Professional Season and the first one to feature the brand-new *Avacyn Restored* Limited format. Nine rounds of Sealed Deck whittled the field down to 128, two drafts with three rounds each followed on Day 2, and at the end only eight players were left to battle it out for the title and the trophy in one final draft.


Going by previous accomplishments, Italy's Platinum pro Samuele Estratti was the clear favorite in this Top 8, but it was fellow Italian Matteo Versari who walked away a Grand Prix champion. He and his blue-green tempo deck with a splash of red first dispatched Estratti, then made short work of Spain's Pere Llimós Muntal, and finally, in the final, proved too much for Sweden's Oscar Almgren.


Over the course of the weekend we saw *Avacyn Restored* unfold in Sealed and Draft, we became privy to succesful strategies by some of the best players in the world, and witnessed close encounters between them. Thanks to GGs Live we even did all of that live on video, a first for a European Grand Prix.


It's been an amazing weekend, but this tournament has been decided. Congratulations to Matteo Versari winner of Grand Prix Malmo 2012!

*by Frank Karsten*

* Pick 5: [Lightning Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) (over [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) and [Scalding Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Devil) )
* Pick 6: [Thatcher Revolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thatcher+Revolt) (over [Barter in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barter+in+Blood) and [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) )
* Pick 7: [Soulcage Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcage+Fiend) (over [Geist Snatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+Snatch) )
* Pick 8: [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) (over [Scalding Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Devil) )
* Pick 9: [Necrobite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrobite) (over [Dangerous Wager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dangerous+Wager) )
* Pick 10: [Bladed Bracers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bladed+Bracers) (over Somberwald Vigilate)
* Pick 11: [Raging Poltergeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Poltergeist)
* Pick 12: [Grave Exchange](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Exchange)
* Pick 13: [Dual Casting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dual+Casting)

Almgren seemed unperturbed. His offence was rolling, and he had a [Hound of Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hound+of+Griselbrand) as his next creature. Versari's next was [Scrapskin Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake) , which would be able to fight the hound profitably at some point, or indeed fight the second Heirs of Falkenrath which Almgren played.


Versari's answer to the hound was to use a [Spectral Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Prison) , leaving him free to attack for two in the air before letting his drake deal with the vampire. The life totals were still well in Almgren's favour, with [Soulcage Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcage+Fiend) getting stuck in quite a bit, but he was still alive.


[Bloodflow Connoisseur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodflow+Connoisseur) posed a little bit of a problem from Almgren. The vampire would be able to kill off the [Hound of Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hound+of+Griselbrand) under [Spectral Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Prison) , such that undying could bring it back, and would also let him use [Soulcage Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcage+Fiend) as a 3 point burn spell at some point. With his life total low, this was a rough spot to be in.


After a little thought, Versari cast [Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mist+Raven) targeting the [Soulcage Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcage+Fiend) , forcing Almgren to pull the trigger if he was going to use the creature as a burn spell. Oscar was quick to do so. Versari was now on just 3 to Almgren's 14 life.


Versari used his last mana to cast [Guise of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guise+of+Fire) on the Connoisseur, but it wasn't enough. There was an [Undead Executioner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Executioner) to get eaten by the vampire, which was enough to in turn kill off [Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mist+Raven) , clearing a path for a lethal attack. The Grand Prix would come down to one final game.


Matteo Versari 1 – 1 Oscar Almgren



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmal12/f_versari.jpg)
*Matteo Versari*

### Game 3


For the deciding game of the match, Matteo Versari would be on the play – potentially a very valuable advantage, as Almgren's deck had shown it could be very aggressive when required. This advantage was compounded by a mulligan from the Swede. The six card hand had just a single [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) . Almgren looked at the rest. There was a [Lightning Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) in there which could be just what he needed. He kept.


Versari, on the play, had a [Wandering Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Wolf) , and Versari, on the draw, found the second land he needed just in time. [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) came down on turn two from Almgren, but didn't hang around for long, with a [Guise of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guise+of+Fire) to kill it.


Suddenly the keep was looking a little dicier. Versari ran in with his [Wandering Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Wolf) and an [Alchemist's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alchemist%27s+Apprentice) . Almgren had played his [Lightning Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) , but didn't dare block, as he would need the haste it could grant to race. What he needed more pressingly was land though, and that just wasn't coming. The best Almgren could do was a [Scalding Devil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Devil) . A crowd as replete with Italians as it was Swedes held their breath.


When Versari attacked in again with his wolf, Almgren felt compelled to block with his Mauler. He was punished with a [Joint Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joint+Assault) on the wolf. A third land finally came, and with it a [Bloodflow Connoisseur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodflow+Connoisseur) . [Timberland Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberland+Guide) was there to pump [Wandering Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Wolf) though, meaning that Versari could continue his attacks, putting the Swede down to 12.


Almgren passed without plays the following turn. He went to 9 from attacks, and saw the [Elgaud Shieldmate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elgaud+Shieldmate) that had spelled his doom in game one. It did not pair up, signalling perhaps a strong creature from Versari the following turn as well.


[Searchlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searchlight+Geist) helped Almgren's position only a little. He would gladly have traded it for another land to cast something more powerful. Attacks came from Versari, who already had Almgren on 9. [Alchemist's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alchemist%27s+Apprentice) and a 1/1 [Timberland Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberland+Guide) didn't get through for much damage, but that was fine – Verari was well ahead, and had a [Pathbreaker Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pathbreaker+Wurm) to pair with [Elgaud Shieldmate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elgaud+Shieldmate) threatening a deathblow.


Almgren had nothing for his turn, while Versari had yet another threat in [Mad Prophet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Prophet) . He swung in with his team and that was it. Almgren extended his hand. It was all over.


Matteo Versari wins 2-1 to claim the title of Grand Prix Malmo 2012 Champion!



The Top 5 Cards of Grand Prix Malmo 2012
----------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*
 


### [Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mist+Raven)



![Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mist+Raven)

Hailed by many as *Avacyn Restored*'s mightiest common, this Bird combines an astonishing number of features for such a simple-looking card. First of all, bounce works best in tempo decks. Evasion also works well in tempo decks. And finally, not having to divide one's time between casting bounce and creatures—but getting both in one neat package—is what really puts [Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mist+Raven) over the top. [Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mist+Raven) was the cornerstone of many tempo-oriented strategies this weekend, whether blue-white fliers or blue-green soulbonders. But there's another side to this card as well: as if its power in aggressive strategies wasn't already enough, it can play combo, too! Combine it with [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) or [Nephalia Smuggler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Smuggler) to reuse its ability over and over, or even with something as simple as [Peel from Reality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peel+from+Reality) , [Cloudshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudshift) , or [Ghostly Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker) for at least some additional gain.


 


### [Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger)



![Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wolfir+Avenger)

I can't count the number of games I have seen this weekend which were all but decided by a well-placed [Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger) , sometimes as early as turn three. This flashy surprise blocker doesn't only have a decent body which allows it to kill a lot of the small creatures in the format and live, but also regenerates to survive anything larger. The usual MO seems to be: opponent casts some small creatures, is happy to have the early advantage, attacks maybe a little too recklessly, loses one creature to the Avenger, then can't get past anymore, and subsequently loses to the bigger (and/or soulbound) creatures in green. As such, [Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger) played an important part in many of the more midgame-oriented green decks, especially red-green.


 


### [Thatcher Revolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thatcher+Revolt)



![Thatcher Revolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thatcher+Revolt)

Three creatures at once? With haste? Humans, too? Granted, they die at end of turn, but there are so many cards in *Avacyn Restored* which interact with a sudden influx of humanity. Even just staying in red, there's [Vigilante Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigilante+Justice) , [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) , [Riot Ringleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riot+Ringleader) , and [Havengul Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Vampire) , white adds [Goldnight Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldnight+Commander) and possibly [Cathars' Crusade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathars%27+Crusade) , green has [Champion of Lambholt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Lambholt) , and black will happily abuse the puny Humans with [Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) , [Bloodflow Connoisseur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodflow+Connoisseur) , and [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters) . Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa pointed out he often sees [Thatcher Revolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thatcher+Revolt) in decks where it really doesn't belong. But in the right deck it's not only good, but a beating.


 


### [Infinite Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection)



![Infinite Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Infinite+Reflection)

Despite the less than enthusiastic reception it received when Limited specialists saw the six mana enchantment for the first time, [Infinite Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) actually turned out to be the go-to card for wacky combos in **Avacyn Restored** Sealed and Draft. One of the biggest stories making the rounds on Saturday was of a player who controlled three soulbound creatures paired with three others, then turned all of those into copies of [Wolfir Silverheart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) and thus into veritable *12/12 monsters*. And the card continued to show up, during the draft rounds, and even all the way into the Top 8: Matteo Versari won his quarterfinal against Pro Tour champion Samuele Estratti when he cast [Infinite Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infinite+Reflection) on [Gang of Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gang+of+Devils) , he won his semifinal by casting it on his own [Latch Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Latch+Seeker) .


 


### [Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrifying+Presence)



![Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Terrifying+Presence)

It may look just like any other combat trick, but [Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrifying+Presence) is so much more. With a name like this, it should have been clear the card would make its presence felt, and terrifyingly so. Still it took players a while to figure out the huge tempo swing brought about by a selective [Fog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) effect. Some players at least. Thomas Holzinger picked it as the most underrated card in all of *Avacyn Restored*, and in Matteo Versari's Top 8 winning deck the instant was working overtime. Due to the lack of removal in those colors, succesful blue-green decks in particular need to play a tempo game, and [Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrifying+Presence) fits right in. None of your creatures deal damage *and* I get to kill one of yours—that's terrifying, isn't it?
