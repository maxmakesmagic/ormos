Top 4 Coverage



We started with 779 teams Saturday Morning; at the end of Sunday one team stood alone. Congratulations to your GP Kyoto 2016 Champions: Yuki Matsumoto, Yuuki Ichikawa, and Kazuyuki Takimura!


Considered one of the hottest teams coming into the event, the three Japanese pros crushed through the competition, ending the swiss in first place and quickly dispatching (25) Petr Sochurek, Tom Ristovsky, and Pavel Matousek in the semifinals. From there they took on the other hot team of the event, Ken Yukuhiro, Kentaro Yamamoto, and Hiroaki Kitahara.


Yukuhiro, Yamamoto, and Kitahara ran the tables day 1 with a perfect 9-0 record. In the semifinals they defeated Kenta Harane, Tomonori Hirami, and Fumiya Matsumoto. In the finals, the two teams of Japanese pros faced off. It all came down to one game between Ichikawa and Hiroaki. Ichikawa clinched the match and the tournament thanks to a timely Otherworldly Outburst.


Congratulations once again to GP Kyoto 2016 Champions Yuki Matsumoto, Yuuki Ichikawa, and Kazuyuki Takimura!

Day 2 Coverage



The sun has come out in Kyoto and it’s time for day 2! Overnight 779 teams have been reduced to 169. Two teams sit atop the standings with perfect 9-0 records: Ken Yukuhiro, Hiroaki Kitahara, and Kentaro Yamamoto; and Shunsuke Yao, Masahiro Kuroda, and Kojiro Omonishi.


Team sealed is a format where anything can happen and top players have teamed up to show their worth. Among the 8-1 teams include star-studded lineups such as Lee Shi Tian, Katsuhiro Mori, and Tomoharu Saito; and Yuki Matsumoto, Yuuki Ichikawa, and Kazuyuki Takimura. Finally, Hall of Famer elect Yuuya Watanabe’s team featuring Yoshihiko Ikawa and Hajime Nakamura finished day 1 with an 7-2 record.


We have 5 more rounds of Team Sealed here before we cut to a Top 4 and crown a champion. Stay tuned for continuing coverage of Grand Prix Kyoto 2016!
