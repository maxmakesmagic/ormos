How do you build this?

The first thing that I do with any Sealed pool is separate out the cards that are essentially unplayable in Limited, such as [Mirror of Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+of+Fate), and the sideboard only cards such as [Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li51_w.jpg)### White

First off, I remove 1 [Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silence) and 1 [Lifelink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifelink), leaving us with 1 [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden), 1 [Stormfront Pegasus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus), 1 [Silvercoat Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvercoat+Lion), 1 [Undead Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Slayer), 1 [Wall of Faith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Faith), 1 [Razorfoot Griffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin), 1 [Glorious Charge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Charge), 2 [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism), 1 [Excommunicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate), and 1 [Armored Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Ascension).

This pool's white is fairly good, but it only has the [Stormfront Pegasus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) and [Razorfoot Griffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin) for notable creatures. The two [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism)s could make a great splash, but there really isn't enough going on for white to be a main color.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li51_u.jpg)### Blue

First off I remove 1 [Telepathy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telepathy) and 1 [Jump](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jump), leaving me with 1 [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), 1 [Coral Merfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk), 1 [Horned Turtle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horned+Turtle), 1 [Illusionary Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusionary+Servant), 1 [Phantom Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Warrior), 1 [Serpent of the Endless Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serpent+of+the+Endless+Sea), 1 [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), 1 [Ice Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage), 2 [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), 1 [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), and 1 [Levitation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Levitation).

I like having [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) a lot in ***Magic** 2010* Sealed (and in Draft for that matter). There are a lot of very high-impact spells such as [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) and [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) that you absolutely have to be able to deal with or you're probably going to lose.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li51_negate.jpg)  
You can deal with them by killing your opponent before they get a chance to beat you with them, or you can stop them outright with cards like [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), or [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). It's also a very good way to stop cards such as [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) or [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) that might otherwise deal with some of your best cards.

In fast formats cards like [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) aren't very impressive because a lot of games will go by when you never get a chance to use them. When you do get a chance to use a card like this, you will end up burying yourself because of all of the time that you lost both leaving mana up for it and casting it instead of an immediately relevant card. But in M10 Limited, games tend to drag on long enough that you will have time to use a [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) to counter something great, or at least something very good. 

I should also mention that I'm not a very big fan of [Ice Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cage) in Sealed. I think it's a fairly good card in Draft, but in Sealed the games tend to go on a bit longer. This gives your opponent more time to draw their [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) or stick their [Blinding Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) to get rid of your would-be [Arrest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li51_lawyer.jpg)*Mages tend to make good lawyers.*  
There are also a lot more pieces of Equipment running around in Sealed than in Draft. Not only are there physically twice as many opportunities to get Equipment because you open up six packs per player instead of three, but people are also much more likely to play the Equipment that they do have. Sure you might start a [Whispersilk Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak) if you have one in Draft, but a lot of the time you are going to leave it in your sideboard for matchups where you specifically need a way to break through.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li51_3Cards1.jpg)  
This pool's blue is so close to being good. The [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), the [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) and the [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) stand out as the color's best cards, but the supporting cast doesn't have all to much going for it unless it's supporting a deck with very good creatures or some sort of very good controlling plan.

### Shallow Pool

![Kalonian Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kalonian+Behemoth)The biggest problem with each and every one of the pool's colors is the lack of good creatures. 

Right off the bat I removed blue from consideration. Then I tried looking at various green-white-red concoctions, but none of them were very impressive. So I knew that I was going to have to play black. But what color(s) would support it?

I'm a huge fan of the [Kalonian Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Behemoth) in Sealed. This might sound a bit extreme, but in this pool I think that I actually prefer it to [Shivan Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon). This pool has very few creatures and the ones that it does have tend to be pretty weak. That means that our opponents are going to be able to save their removal spells for when we play our firebreathing flier. But it doesn't matter if your opponent saves up their removal spells if your game-breaking creature is [Kalonian Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Behemoth). It doesn't matter at all.

For the same reasons why I would prefer to have the [Kalonian Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Behemoth) to the [Shivan Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon), the [Oakenform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oakenform)s are pretty weak in the context of this pool.

I decided not to play [Gorgon Flail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorgon+Flail) because the deck has very few creatures that you actually benefit from equipping it to. Putting a [Gorgon Flail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorgon+Flail) on a [Looming Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade), a [Zombie Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath), or a [Shivan Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon) doesn't do all that much, meaning that there are really only six creatures in the deck that get a noticeable benefit from the equipment. 

This build was wrong for a number of reasons. Due to my lack of experience playing M10 Sealed in tournaments, I thought the format was much slower than it actually is. The Sealed pools that I had opened while I was preparing for the event tended to offer me very controlling decks, and I made a pretty poor leap of logic. I built a deck that needed too much to go right for me to win when there were multiple builds that were actually quite reasonable given the nature of the format.

As it was I only had two spells that affected the board before turn three, and those were [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) and [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)—both spells that I would really, really want to save until late in the game. So if my opponent simply played a two-drop and a three-drop, I would be in terrible shape. The deck's creatures and spells weren't particularly powerful either, so I would have a lot of trouble when the game went late too.

And my deck probably needed 18 lands so I could ensure that it could cast its spells, but I didn't have very much action so I decided that it was more important to fit a second [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) into my deck. This was so that I would have a better chance to win if the game went late, because I would have an extra answers to top spells such as [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Tendrils of Corruption | Looming Shade |

This build was without a doubt one of the biggest blunders that I've made in a Sealed Deck tournament in a long time. I visualized myself winning games by casting big [Tendrils of Corruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption)s and attacking with big [Looming Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade)s. But if my opponent got a fast draw, a bomb, or something like a [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) to break the game open, I was probably going to lose.

Sure, the [Looming Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade)s and the [Tendrils of Corruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption)s are weaker in this build than they are in the black-red build that I played in the tournament, but the fact that we pick up two [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism)s and three creatures that cost two or less more than makes up for the loss in power that some of our cards suffer.

I don't like playing 3 [Looming Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade)s in this version as it only has 9 [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s. I opted to run a [Wall of Faith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Faith) instead so I could have an additional relevant card against five-mana 4/4s and the like. 

I'm still not excited about this deck, but it does have a lot of removal, a decent curve, and a legitimate shot at winning. The deck that I piloted had none of these things going for it.
