Plenty of Temples
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 31, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson











 Last week, I showed off [Ali Aintrazi's Five-Color](/en/articles/archive/bane-standard-2014-01-24-0)
[Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian). Today, I have a similar deck, although King Feeble's version is a more modest four-color deck featuring cheaper spells instead of [Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian)s and bigger spells. 

![Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sylvan+Caryatid) This deck takes a lot of what makes Mono-Black Devotion so powerful, like [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), and [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel), and adds [Sylvan Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) for acceleration, early blocking, and mana fixing. King Feeble also has [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) to deal with nonland permanents, which is a big step from Mono-Black, which can't remove an [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), [Thassa, God of the Sea](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thassa%252C%2BGod%2Bof%2Bthe%2BSea), or [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) once they are in play. 


[Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) is the finisher of choice, instead of the sometimes unreliable [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon). The inclusion of Obzedat makes [Whip of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) a fantastic choice 

 My first thought on the mana base was, "Wow! Sixteen [Temples](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&name=+%5BTemple%5D&type=+%5B%22Land%22%5D&format=+%5B%22Standard%22%5D)? That's going to go a long way toward fixing its draw steps." At closer inspection, I realized that [Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden) doesn't actually scry. Still, twelve Temples is pretty impressive, and I imagine it does wonders for the consistency.
