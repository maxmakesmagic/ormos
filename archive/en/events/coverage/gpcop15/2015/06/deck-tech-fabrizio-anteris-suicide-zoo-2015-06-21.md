*Fabrizio Anteri*


**What is the game plan?**


"The deck aims to resolve [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) on turn two, which you can do with a couple of fetch lands, [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), and [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith)," Anteri explained. "Afterwards, you can make it even bigger and use the combo of [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage) and [Become Immense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Become+Immense) to win the game. The deck has twelve cycling spells, so it's not as unlikely to get these pieces together."


**Why did you choose this deck?**


"After trying various Modern decks, I saw a [video of Frank Karsten on ChannelFireball](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/brew-of-the-week-modern-suicide-zoo/) with this Suicide Zoo. The deck looked like a pile at first, but I gave it a go and it provided surprisingly consistent turn-three and turn-four kills. It's hard to interact with it, and you surprise people with it."


**Did you change any cards in the maindeck?**


"The original list I saw contained [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), but I didn't like them as they made for really awkward draws. Drawing multiples was horrible; they had to be played on turn one to be efficient; and they also put a hefty requirement on the mana base. I replaced them with [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape). I also added [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) and replaced [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) with [Godless Shrine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine). Ideally, you fetch for [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) and [Godless Shrine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine). You can cast all of your spells with that configuration.


"Another swap that I made was to take out [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) for [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). I found myself bringing in [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) for most of the matchups, so I just added it to the main. [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) protects the combo and pumps [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow). Finally, I added two [Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood) to protect my creatures. Often, opponents wait with their removal spell with the plan of responding to [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage) or [Become Immense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Become+Immense). With [Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood), I can give my creature hexproof to save it at that point. I like it better than [Apostle's Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apostle%27s+Blessing) because the +4/+4 boost is relevant with [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage)."


**Did you change any cards in the sideboard?**


"The original list had [Hooting Mandrills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hooting+Mandrills) and [Nourishing Shoal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nourishing+Shoal), but I didn't like that strategy. I have [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) against Burn instead. I also added [Stony Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence) to try to beat Affinity because it's a bad matchup otherwise. And I added [Nihil Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) because I wasn't sure how popular the [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand)/[Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) deck would be and I wanted to be ready for it. Also, [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember). Because [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) is a hard card to face, and [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) is a good answer that also grows my [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)."


**What are the good and bad matchups for the deck?**


"The worst matchups are Burn and Affinity. You need to deal 8â€“10 damage to yourself to operate, and these decks are the best at exploiting that. All fair decks like Jund or Abzan are good because you are much faster than them; if their first play is a turn-two [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), then they are just dead."


**Any final words?**


"Phyrexian mana is your friend."
