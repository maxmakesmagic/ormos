[Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting) seems more powerful now than ever to me. A Warrior deck with [Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting) is very capable of winning games on the fourth turn more often than not. 


I'll start by explaining each of the cards and their purpose in the deck. 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab42_theCardsHeader.jpg)  
**[Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves):** The Elves are a perfect fit for this deck. They accelerate us into our more powerful cards like Obsidian Battle Axe and [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect). 


**[Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel):** The Extended combo piece is perfect for this deck. It even gets a +1/+1 counter from our [Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon). The most exciting thing here is the fact that we can untap it after using it to conspire our [Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting). 


**[Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon):** Any Warrior-themed deck would be hard-pressed to not play four of this powerful creature. Its +1/+1 counter clause will also allow us to steal games with the reinforce ability on our [Hunting Triad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Triad). 


**[Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect):** Most of our creatures are Elves. [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) also happens to be a Warrior, so it gets bonuses from our Paragon and can be suited up for free with an [Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab42_theCardsCross1.jpg)  
**[Obsidian Battle-Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe):** The Axe is incredible here. It gives the deck a lot of resistance to [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Wrath%2Bof%2BGod) effects. 


**[Hunting Triad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Triad):** Three Warriors for one card is a nice deal, and the reinforce will steal a lot of games. 


**[Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher):** This is probably the most powerful two-cost creature in standard. It has a lot of limitations as to what decks you can put it into, but the Vanquisher is a really important piece of this deck.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab42_theCardsCross2.jpg)  
**[Boggart Ram-Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang):** I love this card so much. It trumps [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) a lot of the time. It makes [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) just sit there gathering dust. Most importantly, though, it's a 3-power haste creature that only costs three mana to play. 


**[Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting):** The namesake of the deck. [Giantbaiting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giantbaiting) will steal a lot of games here. It's very important that we play four.


**[Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun):** One of the big issues this deck can have is a board stall. The deck is obviously a dog when your opponent has card like [Cloudgoat Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) and [Spectral Procession](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession) in these scenarios. Luckily for you, you can just topdeck an [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) and end the game. 


**[Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village):** Spells? We don't need no stinkin' spells. 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab42_theCardsFooter.jpg)

The deck is almost identical to its original incarnation. The difference between now and then is pretty significant, though. With the absence of *Time Spiral* block we lose most of our roughest matchups. We also find ourselves in a format where very few decks can keep up with this kind of speed. 


The sideboard for a mono-green deck practically builds itself.


I want **[Scattershot Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattershot+Archer)s** for the Faerie matchups. I used to want to bring it in against decks with [Spectral Procession](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession) too, but nowadays those decks all seem to have [Glorious Anthem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Anthem) effects, or they side out their Processions expecting the Archer. 


**[Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize)** seems good against Swans, so I'll definitely pack some of those. 


**[Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)** is great for the creature matchups, so it's good to have extra.


**[Snakeform](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform)** is awesome against decks with difficult creatures like [Reveillark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) or [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus). 


**[Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command)** is a great answer to burn spells. 


Once all is said, and done the sideboard looks like this:
