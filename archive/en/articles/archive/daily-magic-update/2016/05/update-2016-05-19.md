Deck of the Day
===============


1. [The Modern Deck to Beat](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=102880)

We've actually pointed out this deck before as "new and interesting," but it's now gone past that and might just be the defining deck of this weekend.


But, you rightfully point out, isn't Jeskai Control a longstanding mainstay of the format? What's new about it?


Glad you asked, hypothetical voice in my head! You see, everything changed when [Nahiri, the Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nahiri%2C+the+Harbinger) was printed. These days, Jeskai Control decks are still playing all of the hits—[Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions)—and sprinkling in four copies of [Nahiri, the Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nahiri%2C+the+Harbinger) and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). Those five slots give the deck—which is otherwise prone to not being able to finish a game quickly—a very easy way to win: play Nahiri, protect her for two turns, bring Emrakul into play. Profit.


The fact that Nahiri is just fine until she ultimates, looting through dead cards, makes this a pretty painless way to finish the game. And this weekend, it's going to have a big target on its back—or it's going to run rampant.


Also of note, in the list below, StarCityGames.com Indianapolis Modern Open Champion Peter Ingram played just two copies of newly unbanned [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) in his sideboard. The card is clearly powerful, but people seem to have come to the conclusion that it's too slow for many matchups in Modern, relegating it to the sideboard only.
