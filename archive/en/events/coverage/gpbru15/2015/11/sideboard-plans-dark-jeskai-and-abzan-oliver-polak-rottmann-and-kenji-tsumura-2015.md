Sideboard Plans for Dark Jeskai and Abzan with Oliver Polak-Rottmann and Kenji Tsumura
======================================================================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX BRUSSELS 2015](/en/events/coverage/gpbru15)
 on November 15, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











Sideboarding is an important skill in Standard, and the decision on which cards to bring in and which ones to take out in a certain matchup can be pivotal. Surprisingly, however, not much has been written on sideboarding strategies with the two most popular decks in Standard: Dark Jeskai and Abzan.


Fortunately, there are plenty of great players in attendance here in Brussels who can provide some advice. I sought out two sheer masters with these decks, each of whom wrote an article on one of these decks for their local shop's website recently: Oliver Polak-Rottmann on Dark Jeskai, and Kenji Tsumura on Abzan.

Oliver Polak-Rottmann made the Top 8 of Grand Prix Quebec City a few weeks ago with Dark Jeskai, so he certainly had a good grasp on the deck. "I have played the deck a lot," he told me. " Mana sequencing and playing fast is important, but sideboarding is important as well. I think how I do it is the right way."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpbru15/BRU_sideboard_polak-rottmann.jpg)  
*Oliver Polak-Rottmann*


Sideboarding in the Mirror Match
================================


"Boarding in the mirror is really tough," Polak-Rotmann said. "First you have to guess what your opponent is trying to do. Some opponents board out all the creatures and try to kill you with [Mastery of the Unseen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mastery+of+the+Unseen), but most keep many of their creatures. So I have a stock sideboard plan against the creature plan for game two, but I will adjust for game three depending on what I see."


* +2 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) +1 [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) +1 [Painful Truths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painful+Truths) +1 [Dragonmaster Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonmaster+Outcast) +1 [Mastery of the Unseen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mastery+of+the+Unseen) +2 [Exert Influence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exert+Influence)
* −2 [Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master) −2 [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) −1 [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash) −3 [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) (on the play)
* −2 [Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master) −2 [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) −1 [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash) −1 [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) −1 [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider) −1 [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery) (on the draw)

It's always interesting to see which cards players choose to board out, so I asked Polak-Rottmann why he would cut [Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master), [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command), and [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) in the mirror. "[Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master) dies to every removal, and you don't want cards that get out-valued by [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command). The same applies to [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy), but I still keep Jace because the impact it has on the game is much higher than [Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master) if it lives. I dislike [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) because everyone has [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) and everyone plays around it. You never want the full set of [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) because they don't deal with Tasigur. Sure, they efficiently answer Jace and maybe [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider) later on, but that's not good enough, especially when some players board out [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider)."


I asked Polak-Rottmann about his choice to keep most fliers in his deck. "[Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider) doesn't provide card advantage, but it's still good on turn three. And [Dragonlord Silumgar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Silumgar) sometimes dies to [Crackling Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Doom), but often you can steal their [Jace, Telepath Unbound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Telepath+Unbound), immediately use it, and still get value."


Polak-Rottmann had a different plan depending on whether he was on the play or on the draw. "You can board out all the [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse)s on the play because you have the three-mana removal spells to answer Jace in time when you're playing first."


So what would he change if he saw hardly any creatures and [Mastery of the Unseen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mastery+of+the+Unseen)? "If I see the super control plan, then I would add [Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke) and [Felidar Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Cub) and shave [Exert Influence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exert+Influence)."


Sideboarding Against Abzan
==========================


Against a stock list of Abzan, Polak-Rottmann had the same plans on the play and on the draw.


* +2 [Exert Influence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exert+Influence) +2 [Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke) +1 [Dragonmaster Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonmaster+Outcast) +1 [Painful Truths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painful+Truths)
* −1 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) −1 [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) −1 [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash) −3 [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse)

"[Exert Influence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exert+Influence) out of the board is super great, and [Dragonmaster Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonmaster+Outcast) is a good threat. The creature you fear the most is [Wingmate Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingmate+Roc), so I want to add [Disdainful Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disdainful+Stroke). [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) doesn't kill a lot against them, so you should cut it."


Seeing which cards go in and out is always interesting, but how about seemingly weak cards that remain in the deck? "[Soulfire Grand Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulfire+Grand+Master) may seem bad, but they don't have a lot of answers to them, they give you life even if you cut [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse)s, and once you activate it it provides a huge benefit."


Sideboarding Against Red Aggro

* +2 [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames) +2 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) +1 [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) +1 card depending on the version
* −1 [Dragonmaster Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonmaster+Outcast) −1 [Dragonlord Silumgar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Silumgar) −2 [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time) −1 [Utter End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utter+End) −1 [Flooded Strand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flooded+Strand)

"The final card I bring in depends on their version. Against Atarka Red with [Dragon Fodder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder), I add [Virulent Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Virulent+Plague). "I shaved so many red hate cards from my initial sideboard that the matchup is not super great anymore, but I have the feeling that it's still good. I was actually cutting [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) to make room for [Arashin Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Cleric) and [Surge of Righteousness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surge+of+Righteousness), but now I don't even have those cards in my sideboard anymore."

It was quite a surprise to see Hall of Famer Kenji Tsumura show up for this European Grand Prix, but he had good reasons. "I went 38-3 with Abzan on *Magic Online*, so I booked a flight to see if I could also win that much at an in-person tournament. It's also good practice for me for Grand Prix Kobe next weekend."


Tsumura's list was similar to the Pro Tour winning one from Kazuyiku Takimura except for the inclusion of [Heir of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+the+Wilds) over [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker), a choice that was also made by Andrea Mengucci and several other players here in Brussels. The deathtouch creature is great in the mirror when you're on the draw because it trades for [Anafenza, the Foremost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+the+Foremost) or [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) at an efficient mana point. Moreover, neither [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) nor [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm) can take it out in a one-for-one trade. "You need [Heir of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+the+Wilds) to win the mirror when you lose the die roll," Tsumura told me.

Sideboarding in the Mirror Match
================================


"Sideboarding is difficult with Abzan, and the plan can differ greatly depending on whether you are on the play or on the draw," Tsumura said. This could easily be seen in his plans for the mirror match.


* On the play: +1 [Self-Inflicted Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Self-Inflicted+Wound) −1 [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector)
* On the draw: +3 [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind) +1 [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang) +1 [Self-Inflicted Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Self-Inflicted+Wound) −2 [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) −2 [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) −1 [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command)

"On the play, it is best to play aggressive. [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) and [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) are important for that. On the draw, you are often behind. So Gideon isn't as good, and the only good ways to deal with an opponent's [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) or [Wingmate Roc](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingmate+Roc) are discard spells."


I never got around to asking Tsumura about his sideboard plans against G/W Megamorph, but because Abzan and G/W Megamorph share many cards, I would imagine that the plan against the [Deathmist Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor) deck is similar.


Sideboarding Against Jeskai
===========================


The plan against Jeskai differed depending on whether one was on the play and on the draw. Of course, it also depended on the opponent's specific version, but the plans below were written with a stock version of Dark Jeskai in mind.


* On the play: +4 [Silkwrap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwrap) +3 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) +1 [Ob Nixilis Reignited](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ob+Nixilis+Reignited) +1 [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang) −4 [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) −3 [Heir of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+the+Wilds) −1 [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) −1 [Murderous Cut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Cut)
* On the draw: +4 [Silkwrap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwrap) +3 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) +1 [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang) +2 [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind) −4 [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) −3 [Heir of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+the+Wilds) −1 [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) −1 [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) −1 [Murderous Cut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Cut)

"[Heir of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+the+Wilds) is bad against [Wild Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Slash); it's certainly worse than [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker) in this matchup," Tsumura said. "[Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) is not great, but it is not so bad on the play, and I would consider maybe leaving two or three. But what can you cut instead? It's very hard to sideboard with this deck."


Sideboarding Against Red Aggro
==============================


* +4 [Silkwrap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwrap) +3 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) +1 [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang) +2 [Ultimate Price](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price)
* −4 [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) −4 [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm) −2 Wingate Roc

"In this matchup, it is always the objective to dampen their offensive, but you need to have an instant-speed removal so that you can answer their combo. So maybe I would keep one or two [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm)s after all; perhaps cut a [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) on the draw or a [Silkwrap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwrap) on the play."

Sideboarding Against Esper Tokens
=================================


* +3 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) +3 [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind) +1 [Ob Nixilis Reignited](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ob+Nixilis+Reignited) +1 [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang)
