Semifinals: Nicolai Herzog vs. Dustin Stern
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 19, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_626.png)
By Victor van den Broek











Although somewhat in the shadow of the Jon vs. Kai match on the other table, this was still a Semifinals match of a Pro Tour. Both players were content with their opening hands and it was Herzog to play first. 

### Game 1

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a660.jpg)Stern was the first to summon a creature though—[Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin). Herzog made a [Leery Fogbeast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leery+Fogbeast) and must've been happy to see Stern's third turn go by without him playing a morph. Herzog made a [Symbiotic Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf) and a [Barkhide Mauler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barkhide+Mauler) on his next turns. In the meanwhile Stern took it to the air with a [Screeching Buzzard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard) and he still had a [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) and [Aphetto Vulture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aphetto+Vulture) in his hand. His attempt to stop Herzog's beatings a bit, a [Daunting Defender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daunting+Defender), looked rather puny compared to the [Kamahl, Fist of Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Fist+of+Krosa) Herzog summoned on his turn. The Buzzard came in once again and Stern made a [True Believer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer) and [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber). Herzog charged with his [Symbiotic Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf) and Mauler. [True Believer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer) threw itself in front of the [Symbiotic Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf). One Overrun later and the [True Believer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer) was dead—and Stern was on 9 life. 

Stern's Buzzard came in once more, bringing the Norwegian down to 13. The [Barkhide Mauler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barkhide+Mauler) recieved a [Sandskin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sandskin) in an attempt by Stern to stop Kamahl's madness. That still didn't keep Herzog from attacking, just with the [Symbiotic Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Elf) this time. The Goblin's –1/-1 was aimed at Herzog's Fogbeast instead of the Elf to keep the Norwegian from having too many creatures available for his Kamahl. Herzog had an [Elven Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elven+Riders) to put on more pressure though. Stern finally found his second [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) so he could cast his [Aphetto Vulture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aphetto+Vulture). Herzog activated his Kamahl on his turn, and along with a [Wirewood Pride](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Pride) it was certain that Stern would receive lethal damage.

Herzog 1 – Stern 0

### Game 2

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a659.jpg)Again the first play of the game was a [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin) by Stern, but it didn't survive its first attack due to a [Vitality Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitality+Charm). Stern started making his air force again with his [Gustcloak Harrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Harrier) and [Screeching Buzzard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard). Herzog had to play a cycle land on his third turn, and then cycled a [Krosan Tusker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Tusker) on his fourth turn, leaving him. Herzog's fifth turn [Spitting Gourna](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Gourna) was targeted by [Oblation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblation) and Stern served for 6 – the two fliers and a morph he had summoned last turn. The Norwegian cycles a Dirge of the Dead and plays out a [Screeching Buzzard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard) of his own. Stern's forces hit the Red Zone again and the two Buzzards traded. [Aven Soulgazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Soulgazer) made its first appearance and that supplied Stern with enough flying power to seal this game.

Herzog 1 – Stern 1

### Game 3

And for the third time in a row, Uncle Fester hit the board on Stern's first turn. [Wirewood Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Herald) was Herzog's answer – and a good one at it. A [Wellwisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) joined it along with an Everglade Courier. Stern showed his mana problems - missing his fourth land drop and he only had [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s in play so far. [Barkhide Mauler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barkhide+Mauler) was up next for the Norwegian while Stern could only muster a morph. [Elvish Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior) joined Herzog's army and the Herald and Mauler went unblocked. The Courier pumped the Herald, bringing Stern down to 12. Finally finding his plains, Stern summoned a [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber). Still Herzog's beaters charged. Stern's morph blocked the Herald and Uncle Fester blocked the Mauler. The [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin) tried to take the Everglade Courier with it to the grave but the elf pumped itself [Wirewood Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Herald) did die though and it fetched another [Elvish Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior). A [Gustcloak Skirmisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Skirmisher) on Stern's side didn't quite stop the beats. Stern just blocked the Mauler and the Courier along with a [Wirewood Pride](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Pride) on one of the two [Elvish Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior)s finished off Stern.

Herzog 2 – Stern 1

### Game 4

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a657.jpg)Stern's hand for this game was mediocre at best, containing 5 [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and a [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber), but Herzog had to trade in his hand. His next hand was good enough apparently, as he played out his [Elvish Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior) on his second turn. Stern summons a [Gustcloak Harrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Harrier), the [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) and morphs his [Whipcorder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipcorder) on his 3rd through 5th turn. [Elven Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elven+Riders) made its first appearance on Herzog's fifth turn, after he added a [Disciple of Malice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+Malice) to his side of the board. Stern morphed his [Haunted Cadaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Cadaver) and tapped down Herzog's [Elven Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elven+Riders). While the beatings in the air still went unstopped, Herzog put on the pressure by summoning [Kamahl, Fist of Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Fist+of+Krosa). Stern slumped in his chair a bit and summoned a [Festering Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin). With life totals at 10 and 12 respectively it was time for some math – in the end Stern's two fliers hit the Red Zone to bring the Norwegian down to 6. Herzog did have a seventh land, screwing up Stern's math ad Herzog's Kamahl was tapped by the [Whipcorder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipcorder) so he turned one of his lands into a 1/1 creature and [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)ned (Overran?), taking the game and the slot in the finals, where he will have to face one of the two best players Magic has ever seen.

**Final Result:** Herzog 3 – Stern 1
