Point-Buying Deck Construction
==============================



 Posted in **Arcana**
 on March 22, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Monty Ashley












When **Magic** started, the minimum deck size was forty cards, not sixty. And there was no four-of rule. If you wanted to make a Constructed deck, you were free to take twenty [Black Lotus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Lotus)es, nineteen [Timetwister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timetwister)s, and a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) if you wanted to. That example would probably involve a lot of shuffling, but you can go ahead and think of other degenerate decks on your own.


So Wizards of the Coast invented the Duelists' Convocation, which invented the sixty-card limit and the four-of rule. But everyone wasn't sold on it and a few other ideas were thrown around. So here, from *Duelist #9*, are the proposed point-based deck-building rules.


Note that these are **not** the current rules. They were printed in February 1996 and might never have been used. But if you want to construct your own deck that conforms to this hypothetical system, remember that *Homelands* has just come out.


Okay, that's enough setup. Enjoy!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/arcana/672_tourneysystem.jpg)Want to try a new **Magic** tournament format? The **Magic** R&D team (the people who develop all **Magic** cards) has created an experimental, point-based, deck-construction system that allows a great deal of freedom while still ensuring game balance.


The idea of setting up a system of restrictions is neither new nor unfamiliar to most players. When **Magic: The Gathering** was created, players could have as many of a particular card in their decks as they wanted, with [Plague Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Rats) being a perfect example of a card intended to increase in potency as it grew in number. Unfortunately, this idea didn't work in practice. Certain cards proved too degenerate and the Duelists' Convocation instituted deck-construction rules (no banned cards, one of each restricted card, and only four of any other card). These rules were quickly adopted by players, ending the chance of ever playing a mean, tournament-level [Plague Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Plague%2BRats) deck.


Realizing that most cards were not a problem in large numbers, the **Magic** R&D team created a new tournament type, one that allows players to use multiples of most cards but that limited the number of degenerate cards in any one deck. The end result is as follows; enjoy, and may your [Plague Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Rats) flourish.


### Deck-Construction Guidelines


Â 


* Your deck must contain at least sixty cards.
* You may have no more than ten of any one card in your deck and sideboard.
* Any card not appearing on the Card Point Values list has a value of zero.
* You may have no more than twenty points in your deck and sideboard. You have three sideboard options under this system:
 
> Option 1: No Sideboard for zero points.  
> 
>  Option 2: A ten-card sideboard for one point.  
> 
>  Option 3: A twenty-card sideboard for two points.
* The following cards are banned in this tournament structure:
	+ Ante cards
	+ [Chaos Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaos+Orb)
	+ [Shahrazad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shahrazad)
	+ [Divine Intervention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Intervention)
	+ [Falling Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falling+Star)
