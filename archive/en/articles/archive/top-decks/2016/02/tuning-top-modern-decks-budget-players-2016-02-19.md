Tuning Top Modern Decks for Budget Players
==========================================



 Posted in [Top Decks](/en/articles/columns/top-decks-archive)
 on February 19, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






[Pro Tour *Oath of the Gatewatch*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptogw) is in the books, and the mighty Eldrazi have taken over the Modern format. I'm sure many of you would love to help defeat these monstrous creatures, but may not have the means to do so. Modern can be a challenging format to get into, especially for newer players or players on a budget. Today I'm going to talk about a few Modern Pro Tour decklists and how to tweak them for players who may have a more limited card pool.


Modern can be overwhelming. If you look at a typical Modern decklist, you may see a lot of rares and mythic rares, especially in the mana base. Fetches and shock lands are great at fixing mana, especially if you want to play more than two colors. However, by sticking to two colors, we still have plenty of options in creating a great mana base without using these high-end lands.


First, an aside on what we mean by "budget." When we say budget, we're primarily talking about cards that are hard to obtain—older cards, rares, and mythic rares. If you have some of these cards already—great! But if not, we're going to look for ways to build the deck with newer cards, uncommons, and even some commons, while still staying true to the spirit of each deck.


For example, when looking to build mana bases, the pain lands from *Magic Origins* are relatively simple to acquire. They're in Standard and are easy to trade for. You might even already have a few from some drafts.


We also have the "buddy lands" from past core sets and from *Innistrad*. The friendly buddy lands have seen multiple printings and are therefore plentiful. The enemy buddy lands from *Innistrad* are a bit more difficult to obtain, but are still not as sought after as fetches and shock lands. With these lands we have the backbone of a strong two-color mana base. There's also [Mana Confluence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Confluence), and while it does hurt to tap it, sometimes it's less painful than fetches and shocks.


Budget Deck #1: Blue-Green Infect
=================================


The first deck we're going to be talking about is Infect. Infect is a combo deck that looks to land a creature with infect as soon as possible and protect it with spells such as [Apostle's Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apostle%27s+Blessing) and [Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood), and then make the creature hard to block with spells like [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and [Distortion Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distortion+Strike). Once the coast is clear, the next step is to attack with that infect creature and then cast a flurry of pump spells to give the infect creature 10 or more power.

We're losing a big component of the deck here: [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) gave us a way to attack through blockers on the ground. Without Inkmoth, we only have [Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent) as an evasion creature. However, we do have ways to break through in [Distortion Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distortion+Strike) and [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor). These cards are really important for us, because they allow us to ignore blockers.


[Ichorclaw Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr) is our replacement for [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). It's great at getting into combat with opposing creatures and it wears a [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) really well. Even if it's blocked, the bonus it receives will ensure that trample damage will get through.


With this version of Infect, we have to cut down on copies of [Become Immense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Become+Immense). Without fetch lands, we won't have enough cards in our graveyard to fuel delve. As long as we are aggressively casting spells, we should be able to cast a one-mana [Become Immense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Become+Immense) during the game, but it's pretty unlikely that we can cast a second.


**Tips on Playing the Deck:**


* The best way to play this deck is to always protect our creatures. This means we may wait to cast our [Glistener Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glistener+Elf) until turn two or our [Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent) or [Ichorclaw Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr) until turn three so that we can keep mana open for our reactive spells, such as Vines of the Vastwood, [Apostle's Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apostle%27s+Blessing), and [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce). You can ignore this rule if you have backup creatures in your hand.
* Use [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)s aggressively, but don't use them all at once. [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) is an important card in our deck. It gives us valuable information about the opponent's hand so that we can play around whatever removal spells they may have. If you are holding more than one copy of [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), it's best to use one on turn one to learn your opponent's game plan, and then the other the turn you are going for the kill.
* Always hold at least one land in your hand in order to get the best use out of [Groundswell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundswell).
* Be conservative against opponents with open mana. If your opponent is letting your infect creature through, sometimes it's better to just have them take that 1 damage instead of risking a pump spell and getting two-for-one'd. If you are holding [Vines of Vastwood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood) or [Apostle's Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apostle%27s+Blessing), then go for it!

**How to Fight Eldrazi** 


* The Eldrazi deck can clog up the board with large creatures. [Apostle's Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apostle%27s+Blessing) doesn't do very much due to all of the creatures having devoid. One card that certainly helps in this matchup is [Distortion Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distortion+Strike). This card is your best friend against Eldrazi, as it can get your creature past all of those blockers.
* [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) is our best card out of the sideboard. It's good at killing [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer), [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher), and [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope). While we'll have to discard a card to kill a [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher), that extra discard can help fuel our [Become Immense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Become+Immense).

Budget Deck #2: Blue-Red Delver
===============================


The next deck we're looking at today is Delver. Delver is a pretty cool deck. It's disruptive aggro, one of my favorite deck styles to play. The game plan for Delver is to play an early creature, such as [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) or [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and then do everything you can to protect it. You'll often see Delver players with [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and even [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) in their decks.

As you can see, this three-color Delver deck is much harder to build than Infect. There are [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s, [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon)s, and tons of rare lands, all of which might be difficult to obtain. Kowalski is taking a controlling approach in his list, with his inclusions of spot removal spells, big delve creatures like [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang), and two-for-ones such as [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) and [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). We are going to take a more aggressive approach in our list.

This blue-red deck is much more aggressive than other Delver decks you may see. It gets the most use out of prowess and looks to cast many spells in one turn. [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag), [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), and [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) are great at accomplishing this. There's also plenty of countermagic that's useful for disrupting our opponent or protecting our creatures.


And if you have a few lying around, [Abbot of Keral Keep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbot+of+Keral+Keep) from *Magic Origins* can be a great addition to this deck as well. Since it's a rare from a recent set, it's a bit easier to find.


**Tips on Playing the Deck:**


* It's usually a mistake to keep a hand without at least one creature. Regardless of how good the hand looks, it's crucial to drop a creature early. While it may look like you're doing well in Remanding and Mana Leaking everything, this deck does not want to prolong the game. It wants to win as quickly as possible.
* [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) is our most useful spell. It's great that it triggers our prowess, but it's also really good for knowing how to play against your opponent. If you know their hand, you'll know if you should use your [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) now or if you should save it for something more threatening later.
* Save [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) until after you've played [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), or until there is something specific you need. [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) is a fun card to play, and it's really tempting to cast on turn one. Who doesn't love scrying? However, in this deck you may need specific answers at specific times. If you cast [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) on turn one on the play and see a [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) and a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), how will you know if you should keep them? They are awful cards against decks like Tron, Storm, or any creature-light deck, but are amazing against Affinity or Zoo. Waiting to cast [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) until you know what your opponent is playing is almost always a better play, and it's good at helping you transform your Delver, too.

**How to Fight Eldrazi:**


* [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) really shines against Eldrazi. Bouncing their large monsters can buy you enough time to get some damage through. The loss of 1 life really helps, too.
* [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) is great against [Eldrazi Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Mimic), [Eldrazi Skyspawner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Skyspawner), and Eldrazi Scion tokens.
* The creatures in this deck are great at getting past Eldrazi. [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) and [Stormchaser Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormchaser+Mage) are hard to block, and [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) can create enough tokens to overwhelm them.
* Lastly, the super-secret sideboard tech for beating Eldrazi is [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas). [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) on [Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) or [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) really slows down the Eldrazi deck, and you get to draw a card as well!
