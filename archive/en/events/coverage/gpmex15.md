TOP 8 COVERAGE



Yesterday, 626 players came to Expo Reforma in Mexico City with dreams of hoisting a trophy in the air on Saturday night. After nine rounds of Khans of Tarkir/Fate Reforged Sealed Deck, only 64 remained. Those players returned today for six more rounds of Khans of Tarkir/Fate Reforged Draft.


When the dust settled, only eight remained: Eduardo Vieira, Mario Flores, Martin Jůza, Andreas Canavati, Juan Carlos Botis, Chapman Sim, Axel Martinez, and Pascal Maynard had risen to the top of the field.


Pascal Maynard Duneblasted his way past Axel Martinez in the quarterfinals before making short work of Chapman Sim in the Semifinals. Only Mario Flores stood between him and the trophy. One of the best matches of the weekend ensued and a crowd gathered around the table to watch as each player’s board grew larger and larger. Eventually, Temur Sabertooth proved to be too much for Mario Flores to handle and Pascal Maynard had achieved victory in his third consecutive Grand Prix Top 8!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



Day 1 of Grand Prix Mexico City is in the books! This morning, 626 planeswalkers descended upon the Expo Reforma in Mexico City. After nine rounds of *Fate Reforged/Khans of Tarkir* Sealed Deck, only 64 players remain. A lot of big names made their way to The City of Palaces this weekend. Pascal Maynard is still in the hunt for his third Grand Prix Top 8 in as many events and Martin Jůza managed to go undefeated after starting with two losses to squeak his way into the second day of competition. The reigning Mexican National Champion, Marcelino Freeman, started strong, but ended up losing in the final round to enter Day 2 with a pair of losses.


Tomorrow, the 64 remaining players will return to try their hand at *Fate Reforged/Khans of Tarkir* Draft. Will a Mexican player emerge victorious and keep the trophy on home soil, or will part of foreign contingent snatch it away? Stay tuned to continuing coverage of Grand Prix Mexico City to find out!
