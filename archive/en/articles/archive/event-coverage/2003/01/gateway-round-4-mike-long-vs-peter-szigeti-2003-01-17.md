Gateway Round 4: Mike Long vs. Peter Szigeti
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 17, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











In a clash between two consummate bad guys, one would expect the sparks to start flying right away. But as veteran Mike Long and upstart Peter "PTR" Szigeti sat across from each other, there was nothing but tense silence. Long, the former Invitational Champion and always under a cloud of controversy, sporting a rugged mountain-man beard. Szigeti, who's game is only just now being recognized as much as his mouth, clean shaven and steely-eyed. At Houston, Szigeti ground into the Masters, and is on-pace for a repeat. They piled and riffled, then presented decks. 

"Good luck." - Mike Long  
 "Do you want me to win?" - Peter Szigeti  
 "I'd rather I win." - Mike Long  
 "Then why wish me good luck?" - Peter Szigeti  
 "I just ... Good luck." - Mike Long

That exchange set the tone for the rest of the match. Szigeti was all stares and irritated tone, while Long played the amicable opponent.

### Game 1

![Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Careful+Study)Long had the early advantaged when his [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) discarded [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), leaving [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) in his hand. Szigeti fired back with a Study of his own, but could only discard [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder). Long continued to press his advantage, flashing back his [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and then attacking into Szigeti's [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel), trading his turn for two cards out of Szigeti's hand. Szigeti beat in and made an [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba). Long answered with [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder). His pair of [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm)s would have to wait a turn. Szigeti's next turn made up some of that lost ground. He attacked, and when his [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel) went unblocked, he pitched [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) to it and flashed it back. He followed that up with a [Quiet Speculation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation) for two [Roar of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) and another Analysis. Long was no longer way ahead on cards. Long untapped and put out [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) with three mana at the ready. Szigeti swung into him, trading a card in hand for the [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), then tried a [Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar). Long had the [Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic).

Long dropped his sixth land and swung in with his [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba), madnessing an [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) for damage. Szigeti flashed back another [Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar) and held back. Long waited for him. The Wurm token came in, and Long madnessed the other [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) to take it down. He still had to hold back though. Szigeti calmly Speculated again, putting another Analysis and [Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar) in the yard. He flashed back the [Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar) and liked the strength of his position.

Long untapped and pulled up a good one: [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study), powering up his hand and turning on his two [Centaur Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Garden)s. He turned his [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) sideways, forcing Szigeti into a gang-block. It only meant that he traded his Wurm and two Gardens for Szigeti's token and [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel). Both players had just [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) on the board.

Szigeti gassed up with one of his Analyses, falling to six. He swung in and they traded their [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba)'s and irrelevant pitches. Szigeti tapped out for [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder). Long went one better, with a [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) from hand and a [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla). Szigeti Analyzed again, going to three. [Wonder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) brought Long to seven, and he played out [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel) and [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba).

Long tried a [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel). Szigeti paused, saying "Thinking". He let it by, and then when Long swung in with Rootwalla, he traded his [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) for it, madnessing a 'Walla of his own. Long was fast running out of steam. With just two lands in hand he resolved a Looter and hoped it was enough.

Szigeti pulled a key [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study). It madnessed an [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) into play, and traded a useless island for some more action. His Rootwalla traded for the two cards in Long's hand. Long drew Rootwalla and thought he had the answer. Szigeti swung in and when Long tried the Rootwalla via the Looter, Szigeti got it out of the way with [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Now Long had no way to stop the Wurm. His [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel) dove in front of Szigeti's but the damage had been done. At three life, he drew, Looted and then got bashed.

Szigeti 1 - Long 0

### Game 2

Game 2 was much more of a farce. Long double-mulliganed, then stared at a hand of [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), Rootwalla, [Moment's Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace), [Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar) and [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). In the end, he went to four. It had a little more game. [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel), [Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic), forest and island.

![Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aquamoeba)Szigeti got to play with seven cards. At first, it looked like Long would have the better of him. Szigeti opened with Rootwalla, and had no turn-two play so bashed for damage. Long dropped a [Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel), and then another, while Szigeti did nothing but sit back on his defender. Long swung, and pitched island and [Aquamoeba](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) to be rid of it.Szigeti played [Careful Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study), and when he madnessed [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), Long was ready with [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). He swung and brought Szigeti to twelve. Unfortunately, his good fortune ran out there. Szigeti had another Study for [Arrogant Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm). Long Looted in response, and Szigeti rolled his eyes, defying the chance of [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Long drew another [Moment's Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace), and sent one to the yard. Szigeti continued to punish with [Quiet Speculation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation) for [Krosan Reclamation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Reclamation), [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and [Roar of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm), and then [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel). Long didn't have the resources to keep up.

Szigeti soon declared a lethal attack, and after Long activated his Looter, dropped a pair of [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic)s onto the table, raising his eyebrows suggestively. Long extended the hand in defeat.
