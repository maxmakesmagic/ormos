A Ruthless Pro Tour deck
========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on September 25, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Oath%20of%20Druids)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath%20of%20Druids)
*Khans of Tarkir* is coming! Three-color wedges are about to make their way into Standard and every other Constructed format, but by no means will they be new to tournament *Magic*. This week, we will look at Pro Tour–winning decks that have been based in one of the wedge clans from *Khans of Tarkir*.



Today's deck is from Justin Gary, who won [Pro Tour Houston in 2002](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=sideboard/events/pthou02). If you recognize Justin's name, it is probably because he is on the Pro Tour Hall of Fame ballot or because you noticed the designer credit on [Spirit Bonds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Bonds) from *Magic 2015*.



Justin's deck is based around one of the most powerful cards ever, [Oath of Druids](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids). If your opponent so much as decided to play a creature, Justin's battlefield might shortly contain an [Oath of Druids](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) and a [Cognivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore), backed by a huge graveyard. If the opponent was not playing creatures, Justin's deck played like a classic blue deck full of counterspells and card drawing. In fact, the only removal spell in Justin's main deck was [Pernicious Deed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed), because [Oath of Druids](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) made it such a bad idea to play creatures.
