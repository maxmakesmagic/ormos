Can't Touch This
================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 6, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 







Hexproof is a powerful weapon against decks loaded with spot removal. As it turns out, one of the strongest decks, Black Devotion, is full of targeted removal spells looking to trade cards. A deck full of hexproof creatures can take away the primary game plan of Black Devotion decks by not giving them any targets for their [Hero's Downfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall)s. 


![Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ethereal+Armor)

[Gladecover Scout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gladecover+Scout), [Witchstalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchstalker), and [Bassara Tower Archer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bassara+Tower+Archer) are not particularly impressive creatures; they are played because they are hard to remove from the battlefield. To compensate for weak stats, Auras and other pump spells are vital to this deck's success. [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) is the strongest of these, and rewards you for playing lots of Auras and other enchantments. The combination of [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) and [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) works wonders here as an all-in move that is tough to answer. 


 One of the more subtle things about this decklist is the generous use of Temples: 10 of the 22 lands in this deck are [Temples](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&format=+%5B%22Theros+Block%22%5D&type=+%5B%22Land%22%5D&name=+%5BTemple%5D) that come into play tapped, giving the deck some added consistency in exchange for speed. This deck is definitely overwhelming once it can enchant a hexproof creature a few times, so this tradeoff makes sense for this deck.
