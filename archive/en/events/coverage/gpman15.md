Sclauzero Butchers the Opposition in Manila
===========================================




Joseph Sclauzero’s first Grand Prix exceeded all his expectations! The Australian amateur triumphed over 11-time GP Top 8er Tzu Ching Kuo in a nail-biting final to emerge victorious in Manila!

TOP 8 COVERAGE



1401 players converged at Manila to usher in the New Year and play their very favourite game at the biggest Premier Event to be ever held in all of Asia Pacific. Fifteen rounds later, the Top 8 consisted of 8 talented players from 8 different countries; a rare, momentous occasion.


Despite a stacked Top 8 comprising 10 Pro Tour Top 8 and over 30 Grand Prix Top 8 appearances, it was relative amateur Joseph Sclauzero who managed to bring it all home.


Breaking into his first Grand Prix Top 8 after ten previous attempts, Sclauzero's hard work had paid off handsomely. His route to the finals was far from easy. He first had to dispatch Won Dae Hoon and oust Hall of Famer Makihito Mihara in order to meet eleven-time Grand Prix Top 8 veteran Tzu Ching Kuo in the finals. Despite losing the first game, he valiantly turned the tables and secured victory for himself, Australia and the Mardu Horde.


The rest of the Top 8 included big names such as Martin Jůza, Lee Shi Tian and Christian Calcano, alongside local hero Taghoy Arnulfo. Congratulations to Joseph Sclauzero once again!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Day 2 of Asia Pacific's largest Grand Prix is underway! The field of 1401 players have been whittled down to just 123. Players are seated and ready to play at least six more rounds of *Khans of Tarkir* Standard before the introduction of Fate Reforged changes the face of Standard forever.


World Champion Makihito Mihara, Pro Tour Champion Ivan Floch and Gold Pro Christian Calcano sit at the perfect 9-0 record and will have a much easier route to the Top 8 compared to the rest of the pack. 


However, a throng of talent will be on their heels including Lee Shi Tian, Tzu Ching Kuo, Raymond Tan, Martin Jůza, Jelger Wiegersma and Nam Sung Wook. In addition, Ozaki Zax, Andrew Cantillana, Richmond Tan, Rey Villlar, Ortega Dominic and many more local heroes will be giving them a run for the money!


Stay tuned for the latest updates from the largest Grand Prix Manila to date!
