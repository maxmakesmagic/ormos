Top 8 Coverage



Bill Tsang blazed his way to victory by applying merciless pressure on his opponents with some of the most aggressive draft strategies we’ve ever seen in the *Khans of Tarkir/Fate Reforged* Draft format.


The final weekend of *Khans of Tarkir/Fate Reforged* Limited proved to be the most exciting yet. Bill Tsang had no previous success at the premier event level, but he proved his mastery of drafting aggressive decks in the Limited format by stringing together an astounding nine wins in a row here on Sunday.


Powerful rares seemed to dominate the conversation coming into this weekend, but the eventual champion preferred red commons the whole weekend. In the third game of the finals, Tsang decimated his opponent, Jake Mondello, with Act of Treason targeting Avalanche Tusker, forcing Mondello to block and trade with his only remaining creature, for a haymaker of a play that was good enough to earn him the title of Grand Prix Cleveland Champion.


*Khans of Tarkir/Fate Reforged* Limited has provided us with one of the most exciting and high powered draft formats of all time. We’ll forever remember Tsang’s Valley Dashers and Act of Treason when harking back to the format.

Day 1 Coverage



1844 players rocked and rolled their way to the Cleveland Convention Center this weekend with dreams of being crowned champion of Grand Prix Cleveland. After nine rounds of Khans of Tarkir/Fate Reforged Sealed deck, only 220 remain. (13) Seth Manfield, Gerard Fabiano, Eric Severson, Mark Herberholz, John Alexander, Eric Blanchet, and Jon Johnson are the only players to make it to the second day of competition with undefeated records. However, big names like Brian Braun-Duin, Valentin Mackl, (17) Willy Edel, Christian Calcano, (1) Owen Turtenwald, (10) Sam Black, (24) Josh Utter-Leyton, Paul Rietzl, (3) Eric Froelich, and Steve Rubin are nipping at their heels with only a single loss.


Tomorrow, players will return to play six rounds of Khans of Tarkir/Fate Reforged Draft before entering the Top 8 elimination rounds. Will masters like Seth Manfield, Mark Herberholz, and Gerard Fabiano continue to dominate? Or will new faces rise up from obscurity and prove their mastery of this Limited format with their last possible opportunity? Stay tuned for continuing coverage of Grand Prix Cleveland!
