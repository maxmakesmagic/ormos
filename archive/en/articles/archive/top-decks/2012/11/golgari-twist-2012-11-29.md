Golgari Twist
=============



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on November 29, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







Top Decks took a break last week on account of the Thanksgiving holiday in the US, so this week we are actually going to try to catch up on the last three Standard Grand Prix. Two weeks ago, we had Standard Grand Prix in both [Bochum](/en/events/coverage/j%C5%AFza-repeats-bochum), Germany, and [Charleston](/en/events/coverage/bolding-thunders-victory-charleston), South Carolina. This past weekend saw a Standard Grand Prix in [San Antonio](/en/events/coverage/lytles-zombies-big-texas) , Texas. So... lots of exciting results! 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td223_bochum.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td223_charleston.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td223_sanantonio.jpg)  
 BR Aggro may be the It Girl sunning herself under the current Standard spotlight. Taking two of the three recent Grand Prix titles (with two, if not three, Top 8 finishes in each of the three), black-red is a deck you *must* be ready for if you want to compete in the current format.

 Bolding's winner from Charleston takes a number of eye-catching turns. On the one hand, it is a [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls)–driven deck with quite a few Zombies. In a sense, this can be taken as a preemptive counterstrike against a room full of [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate)s and [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter)s. [Diregraf Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul) and [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) are both X/2 creatures that only cost a single black mana. Black and red ancestors like [Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup), [Sarcomancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sarcomancy), and [Carnophage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnophage) probably look down on the low downsides and substantial resilience of these creatures with fangs glittering ear-to-ear. [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) rounds out the Zombie theme as one of the most efficient threats available in the format... a combination of damage and resilience and damage again that is all the more attractive when it can't be countered.

 That said, Bolding was no slave to the Zombies-linear. He did not litter his deck with random two-drops and making his spell selections purely on creature type. Instead, he stretched out his horizons at the two to include [Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy) (a creatures with substantial advantages of its own)... but really changed the game by allowing for a higher curve with tons of haste. 

 At the four, Bolding ran both [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) and [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider). [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) packs a punch equal to its mana cost, and does so in a hurry. Moreover, this Vampire works well with creatures like [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger), borrowing their ability to play in the graveyard to enhance its durability. [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) works well with armies in general. Together, these four-drops put tremendous pressure on decks with primarily sorcery-speed removal. Even if the opponent is able to remove them, he or she is going to take a ton of damage first.

[Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) is more of the same... but packs the punch you'd expect from a five-drop. [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) erases the idea of sitting behind [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens and even does little things... like pulling a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) down into [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) range. 

As we said: breakout.

On the way to taking down Grand Prix San Antonio, Lytle seems to have borrowed quite a bit from Bolding's winning list: his land and creature configurations were identical.

 The big shift in this deck is in the spell slots. Likely in anticipation of mirror matches, Lytle moved [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) to the main and upped the [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) count to the Maximum Number. [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) is, of course, one of the best possible answers to a [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)—meeting it at similar speed and taking care of it permanently—and that goes double for [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger). You can't avoid the Messenger's painful enters-the-battlefield ability, but not having to deal with it in combat (twice!) is certainly a plus. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Pillar of Flame | Gravecrawler |

 In addition, Lytle added [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) to his sideboard. While [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) can be destroyed via [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) or [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear), it dodges [Victim of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) and is too big for a [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame). If [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) doesn't go anywhere, it makes for a hell of a blocker, trading with almost anything and racing brilliantly either on offense or on defense.

Martin Juza won Bochum with a combo Reanimator innovated by former Player of the Year Brad Nelson (Brad finished Top 16 with a similar list in Charleston the same weekend).

 While some Reanimator decks have played with [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth), most of the decks of such a stripe we have looked at this season have essentially been green-white-X-[Y] [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) + [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) decks that happened to be able to hit some extra value via [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). Most of those have topped up on [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity)... but, sure, we've seen a [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) or three. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Unburial Rites | Angel of Serenity |

 This deck has shifted the focus of the deck *primarily* to [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). It is all about playing out mana creatures (including [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage), so you can actually cast your [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)). All these little guys are out there, maybe they link arms with some [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) Spirit tokens, and... Boom! Everyone is [Overrun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)ning. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Somberwald Sage | Lingering Souls |

 The main deck features [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), [Tracker's Instincts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker%27s+Instincts), and [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) to fill your hand and fill your graveyard for [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). 

 Now, while the main deck is designed to go big and hasty with fists flying, as with [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat), [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider), and [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite), [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) murders players who are armed only with sorcery-speed removal. If you let this deck get to the Craterhoof, it may already be too late. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Thundermaw Hellkite | Craterhoof Behemoth |

 That said, like many Reanimator decks, this one can find itself exposed against cards like [Cremate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cremate), [Ground Seal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal), or [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace). It is also quite reliant on its many mana creatures. If there are no Arbor Elves or [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim)s on the battlefield, [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) might just be a hella expensive [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) (that doesn't fly) (and without most of the upside). 

 After sideboards, Juza can flip back to a more traditional creature deck, Reanimator or not. [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) plus [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)? It's in there! [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) can put you into a position of dominance against a control deck. And [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity)? Casting it has never been easier, thanks to [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage). Small note that we will see again in a moment: [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) can come down turn two if you've got the right one-drop.

### Contrasting GW Aggro Decks

 Despite being a strategy that is fairly restrictive in its spell selection—besides Ben Rasmussen's on [Faith's Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Shield), neither of the Top 8 green-white decks played a main-deck noncreature spell outside of [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and [Selesnya Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm)—GW Aggro has proven to be one of the more diverse playgrounds for customization in this format. The creature selection is diverse... everywhere from a hyper-concentration on low-cost (if high-impact) one-drops to white-blue-like flash subthemes.  Rasmussen also played quite a few Humans (starting on [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) as well), but played the full four [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin)s as well as dipping into the more conventional midrange powerhouses of the format... two [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s main and two [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s in the side.

 The hot Hot HOT combo in this deck is [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) + [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor). As [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) starts out with first strike, it is pretty tough to contain this little combo outside of removing the creature (you need a blocker with 5+ toughness to avoid a mini-blowout)... and the trample from [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) makes for an aces combination. Any time the [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) connects with an opposing player... hilarity (and [Saproling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling)s) ensue.

 Both green-white builds ran [Mayor of Avabruck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) as a kind of mini tribal [Crusade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade) that doubles as a severe penalty for control players, especially those without instant-speed interaction. 

 Although he did not make Top 8 in any of the Standard Grand Prix we are discussing today, I would argue from watching and playing **Magic Online** that the most influential green-white build of the recent weeks is actually Brian Kibler's Top 16 deck from Charleston. Kibler, the two-time Pro Tour Champion/Hall of Famer/Kickstarter superstar/heartthrob played an extraordinarily unconventional green-white deck that eschewed many of the accepted cards and strategies in favor of ones "no one" had been playing. 

 Like Rasmussen—in advance of Rasmussen, really—Kibler played [Faith's Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Shield)... but unlike either other green-white deck (or almost any green-white Standard deck you may have seen before) his low spell count  *did not include a [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor)!*  Wow.

### Reid Duke's Golgari Twist

From this writer's perspective, the most impressive deck of the recent Grand Prix belonged to back-to-back Top 8 competitor Reid Duke. Making any Top 8 is obviously impressive, although Reid's doing so on consecutive weekends is not the reason I like the deck so much. It may not have won consecutive Grand Prix, but... it's special. Its tuning—which Reid quickly credited to Draw-Go creator Andrew Cuneo and, especially, Sam Black—is a rare gem. If Caw-Blade hadn't happened so recently, I would have said this was the most impressively assembled deck in three years!

So what's so special about it?

 Week over week, Reid more-or-less added [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) to the main. Like Lytle and his shift on [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame), [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) makes for a fine blocker of [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)s. But the deck's special something remained and resounded. 

 The secret to this deck's tuning comes from [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek). [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) can find the one [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb) (you know, the Golgari Twist). The one [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb)—whether searched-for, drawn, or reassembled on a second go-round—pairs up with the one [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) to annihilate other white-blue or Bant control decks. The combination is inevitable. The deck assumes it will play many, many turns, so assembling it is more-or-less inevitable. Oh, and they have another shot!
