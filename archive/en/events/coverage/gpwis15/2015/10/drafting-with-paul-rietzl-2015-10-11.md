Drafting With… Paul Rietzl
==========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 11, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











*“This is my first time drafting this. Can I have extra time to read the cards?”*


Sorry, fourth-ranked player in the world and hall of famer Paul Rietzl, that's not quite how it works here on Day 2 of Grand Prix Madison.


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpwis15/gpwis15_rietzl-01.jpg)**  
*Hall of famer Paul Rietzl's first Battle for Zendikar draft would come in the opening round of Day 2 here at Grand Prix Madison.*


Grand Prix Madison has been our first foray into the *Battle for Zendikar,* and it's safe to say there's been a few surprises along the way. From wild four or five-color decks to the fastest Ingest decks, we've seen a ton of diversity across the format already, even if Rietzl hasn't had a chance to experience much of it yet.


*“Don't worry, I was reading a few articles about this last night, so I think I'm good to go.”*


Rietzl is one of the most outspokenly honest players on the circuit, and heading into the first draft of *Battle for Zendikar's* premier event, there was no better player to follow along for the draft.


The first pack began with a [Canopy Vista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canopy+Vista) staring at Rietzl from the rare slot. While a great utility card, it's not a clear first-pick, and with no easy choice to begin his first *Battle for Zendikar* draft, Rietzl delved deeper into the pack to find a direction. He found it in [Kozilek's Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Sentinel), a flexible two-drop that only looked better when he followed it up with a [Complete Disregard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Complete+Disregard). A stream of black cards followed, with [Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters), [Demon's Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demon%27s+Grasp), [Grave Birthing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Birthing) and [Silent Skimmer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Skimmer) going next as Rietzl did his best to cut off the color from flowing to his left as he settled into a black-red deck.


It was a decision that paid off in the second pack, as Rietzl opted for [Barrage Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barrage+Tyrant) over [Grip of Desolation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grip+of+Desolation) — a pick he agonized over both during and after the draft (16th-ranked Shahar Shenhar later confirmed he would have done the same) — followed by [Stonefury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefury), [Vile Aggregate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vile+Aggregate) and a fourth-pick [Nettle Drone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Drone). Just like that, Rietzl was firmly established in two colors, if not an archetype.


That changed when he was passed [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery) midway through the second pack. Picking up the unique enchantment, Rietzl had a plan, one that he stuck to devoutly in the third pack. A pair of [Forerunner of Slaughter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forerunner+of+Slaughter) came early in Pack 3, and a second [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery) rewarded Rietzl for his dedication to his colorless overlords. With such a focused game plan, he was able to take advantage of late-pick [Processor Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Processor+Assault)s and [Grave Birthing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Birthing)s to etch out a deck that was all-in on the colorless plan.

“Is this good?” Rietzl asked as he registered a deck that played a full 21 colorless cards out of 23. “This is the first time I haven't played 18 lands in this set, and I have no idea if this deck is any good. I guess we'll find out.”


Only time and games will answer Rietzl's question and tell us if the [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery) deck proves to be one of the stronger archetypes in the format, but Rietzl's Round 10 match against the world's No. 1 player in Eric Froehlich was any indication, it will remain a viable strategy.


With one Nursery in play, Rietzl was facing down a flying army from Froehlich with just a lonely [Barrage Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barrage+Tyrant) on his side. As he untapped for his turn at 2 life and only five mana to work with, things looked dire for Rietzl. That is, until he began the turn with [Complete Disregard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Complete+Disregard), taking out one creature and pinging another with [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery). With one of Froehlich's cards now in exile, Rietzl unlocked the [Processor Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Processor+Assault) in his hand to take down the [Windrider Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windrider+Patrol) and use the [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery) trigger to finish off Froehlich's last creature. In the blink of an eye and the flash of the Nursery, Rietzl turned a losing game into one he easily finished off a turn later.


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpwis15/gpwis15_rietzl-02.jpg)**  
*Battling with a deck containing two [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery) and 19 other colorless cards, Rietzl's draft deck was a study in focus.*


With just one draft down and the Pro Tour on tap next weekend, [Molten Nursery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Nursery) won't be the only card to see its stock rise since the set's release. The only question is, what will we learn next about *Battle for Zendikar?*
