### GRAND PRIX SYDNEY 2014


DRAFTING WITH TOMOHARU SAITO
============================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Neale.jpg)

Neale Talbot




August 23, 2014
 










Who is Tomoharu Saito? The short answer: He's one of Japan's most dominant players. Saito has achieved five Pro Tour Top 8s, including a team win at Pro Tour Charleston in 2006, and 19 Grand Prix Top 8s, including four wins. The last time Saito came to Sydney for *Magic* was for last year's Grand Prix Sydney *Return to Ravnica* Limited, when he came 3rd place. Saito looks like a strong competitor for a Top 8 slot, sitting at 8-1 at the end of Day One, so we wanted to get a peek at how he would draft today.


Saito's first pack offered one of the strongest cards in the format, the enemy eviscerating [Soul of Shandalar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+Shandalar). Saito quickly followed it up with a mix of red and white cards, including two [Heliod's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%27s+Pilgrim), with a mind to drafting some of the strong auras the format has to offer.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2014/events/gpsyd14/02_R10_SaitoDraft.jpg)
*Tomoharu Saito drafting upstream of Korea's Park Jun-Young.*

Pack 2 provided a first-pick [Yisan, the Wanderer Bard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yisan%2C+the+Wanderer+Bard), shortly followed by an [Elvish Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic) and [Siege Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Wurm). With no enchantments for the [Heliod's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%27s+Pilgrim)s to fetch and green flowing freely, Saito switched away from R/W to R/G. This was further reinforced by Pack 3, and by the end Saito was rocking a creature-heavy, 18 land R/G deck.



Saito's Round 10 match was against Mailtland Cameron's U/B control deck. Saito's aggressive deck raced out of the gates both games, with the 18 lands ensuring Saito would have the mana required to cast his big creatures, and in both games Cameron struggled to find answers to Saito's monsters. Saito bashed his way to a quick 2-0.


"I'm happy with the deck," Saito said, "Though I am light on removal. I just want to hit five mana and go creature, creature, creature. If I can do that, I think I will win."


With the first round of draft under his belt, Saito certainly seems likely to live up to his word.
