Ireland
=======



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 8, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 






[![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/wmcq_small.jpg)](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup/qualifiers)
The first [WMCQs](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup/qualifiers) were this weekend, and for the first time, they happened on the same weekend in every country. That means there were a lot of happy winners this weekend, and a lot of decks for tournament players to study to find out what did well this weekend.

David Tuite was this weekend's Irish champion. He played a unique take on Esper Control that made some very different pivotal choices. He's chosen to play [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) over [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) and the rest of his deck reflects this change. He no longer needs to maximize his spell density, which allows him to play [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) over other removal spells without hurting his [Augur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur)s. And instead of [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), which is good to find with [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) but redundant with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), he plays [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), which fuels his [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). The [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) also helps him find [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), which play well with his [Sorin, Lord of Innistrad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Lord+of+Innistrad).

He's set up well against other control decks because he has more counterspells, and the [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s let him play as if he has even more, but he also has a lot of early removal.

There is a danger that he could be too slow and lose too much life against aggressive decks like Naya Blitz, but he has the rarely played [Human Frailty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Human+Frailty), which is excellent in that matchup, and four [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus)es, so he can always win if he gets a little lucky.

Overall, this is a great choice in a control-heavy field, but it also has excellent sideboard cards against creature and graveyard decks. He accomplished this in part by skipping [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm), a card that's often considered an automatic inclusion, reminding us that we should always reconsider each card. If you're looking for some new ideas for Esper, this list is worth taking a close look at.
