New Build-Arounds with Eldritch Moon
====================================



 Posted in **How to Build**
 on July 22, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gabyspartz_0.jpg)
By Gaby Spartz




 By night, Gaby streams Magic on Twitch. By day, she's co-founder and VP of Content at Dose, the Chicago-based media company behind Dose.com and OMGFacts.com. 






*Eldritch Moon* has been released, and that makes this the best time for deck building. Today we're taking a look at some build-around-me cards and sweet decks we can brew around them. Our first build-around card is [Whispers of Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispers+of+Emrakul).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Whispers+of+Emrakul)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispers+of+Emrakul)
Having your opponent discard one card at random isn't strong on its own. But once we get delirium going, this card becomes [Hymn to Tourach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach), a card powerful enough to see play in older formats. Now we're talking! The longer the game goes on, the worse [Whispers of Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispers+of+Emrakul) gets. For this reason, we want to put [Whispers of Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispers+of+Emrakul) in an aggressive shell that can achieve delirium quickly.

This deck is trying to empty its hand as quickly as possible. Once our hand is empty, we start reaping the rewards.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lupine+Prototype)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lupine+Prototype)
[Lupine Prototype](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lupine+Prototype) is great in this deck. A two-mana 5/5 is an incredible rate, but not being able to attack or block is a downside that makes the card unplayable. Because this deck plays its hand out quickly, [Lupine Prototype](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lupine+Prototype) gets a chance to shine.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Asylum+Visitor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Asylum+Visitor)
[Asylum Visitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Asylum+Visitor) triggers on both players' upkeeps, which creates tension for our opponent. On one hand, they want to cast their spells, but if they play out their hand they let our [Asylum Visitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Asylum+Visitor) draw us cards. If they try to hold cards to stop us from getting card advantage, we can cast [Whispers of Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispers+of+Emrakul) and have them discard what they had left.


Our madness package means we get to play our hand quickly, without losing value. [Heir of Falkenrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heir+of+Falkenrath), [Tormenting Voice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormenting+Voice), [Sinister Concoction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sinister+Concoction), and [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe) enable us to discard [Stromkirk Occultist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Occultist) and [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper).


If you want bonus points, you can cast [Tormented Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Thoughts) sacrificing [Lupine Prototype](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lupine+Prototype) to have your opponent discard five cards on turn three. All aboard the discard train...choo choo!

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grim+Flayer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer)

This deck wants to do two things: be aggressive (B-E-aggressive!) and enable delirium quickly.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vessel+of+Nascency)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grapple+with+the+Past)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mindwrack+Demon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindwrack+Demon)
These are our delirium enablers. Vessel being an enchantment helps in the pursuit of delirium. [Grapple with the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past) smooths out our draws and helps us buy back key cards from our graveyard. [Mindwrack Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindwrack+Demon) doesn't come out until later in the game, but we lean heavily on him turning delirium on.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grim+Flayer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Flayer)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gnarlwood+Dryad)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnarlwood+Dryad)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mournwillow)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwillow)
Once delirium is online, our cheap threats from early in the game get buffed and become difficult to block. This deck doesn't have a strong late game, so it's important to get on the board quickly and leverage our cheap removal to reduce our opponent's life low enough that a [Mindwrack Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindwrack+Demon) can finish them off.

Emerge is a fun mechanic and this deck tries to abuse it. When we emerge a big Eldrazi, we are technically losing a card, so we have to mitigate that downside. Cue the introduction for the creatures who are willing to sacrifice themselves for the greater cause.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Palace+Familiar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palace+Familiar)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Matter+Reshaper)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Matter+Reshaper)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Foul+Emissary)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foul+Emissary)
These little critters let us be proactive in the early game and become fodder for our emerge creatures in the late game. Our opponents are disincentivized from killing them, because they draw us extra cards. If they do end up killing the creatures, they'll slow us down, but they aren't able to stop us from eventually casting our emerge cards.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elder+Deep-Fiend)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wretched+Gryff)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Gryff)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lashweed+Lurker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashweed+Lurker)
These are our Eldrazi overlords and primary win conditions. [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) has flash, which we can use on both offense and defense. If we cast [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) at the beginning of the opponent's combat, we can stop a large attack from them. If we cast it at the end of their turn, it'll let us tap their team and attack freely.


Fun fact about [Hedron Crawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crawler): even though it's only a two-drop, it can tap for mana and then be sacrificed to pay for the emerge cost. This little guy contributes three mana in total on its way out.

The last card I have a soft spot in my heart for is [Mirrorwing Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorwing+Dragon).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mirrorwing+Dragon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorwing+Dragon)
The coolest thing about [Mirrorwing Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorwing+Dragon) is that while its ability works for both players, the effect that it will have on the board is drastically different based on how each deck is built. We are rewarded for having beneficial spells that target [Mirrorwing Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorwing+Dragon), such as [Expedite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedite) or [Confront the Unknown](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confront+the+Unknown). Our opponents are punished if they have removal spells that target Mirrorwing, because it'll create copies and kill all of their creatures in the process.

There are three cards key to this deck:


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mirrorwing+Dragon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorwing+Dragon)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Zada%2C+Hedron+Grinder)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zada%2C+Hedron+Grinder)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Silverfur+Partisan)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverfur+Partisan)
All of these cards benefit from spells that target them. Mirrorwing and Zada create copies of the spells, and [Silverfur Partisan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverfur+Partisan) gives us Wolf tokens for our troubles. If we have three creatures in play and we target [Mirrorwing Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorwing+Dragon) with [Expedite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedite), we've built our own [Ancestral Recall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall). [Confront the Unknown](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confront+the+Unknown) is also cute because each additional copy will pump your creature +1/+1 more than the previous copy. This can help your smallest creatures win in combat or deal lethal damage in an attack.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/images/daily/HB20160722_Buffs.png)


These are the spells we want to target our key creatures with. They all either draw us cards or pump the creatures.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spirit+of+the+Hunt)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Hunt)
Lastly, we have [Spirit of the Hunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Hunt), a surprise blocker that has good synergy with [Lambholt Pacifist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lambholt+Pacifist), [Silverfur Partisan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverfur+Partisan), and Wolf tokens. While [Spirit of the Hunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Hunt)'s ability doesn't have any synergy with our key creatures, the extra toughness it provides can help our creatures survive through combat or a Wrath effect such as [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish), [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames), or [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return).
