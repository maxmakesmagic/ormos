Bronson Magnan won Lincoln with Aggro Loam, an exciting development for any players who are interested in:


> * Interactive **Magic**
> * Powerful Cards
> * Drawing [Powerful] Cards
> * Making Land Drops
> * Attacking with Gigantic Creatures
> 

...so, basically, everything anyone likes to do in **Magic** (at least other than tapping [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s, to be fair).

Aggro Loam really does quite a few things well. Modern is a format with some very fast and powerful combo decks, so for a mid-range deck like this one to be competitive, it needs to be able to preempt the opponent's win with relevant disruption. Magnan played seven discard spells main (three copies of [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) and four copies of [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime)), as well as [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) on top of those.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The 3–4 split on [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) versus [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) might seem odd, but Magnan's deck really wants to find a [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) (if even by the dredge on a [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam)) because [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) is such a powerful engine, turning lands—often free or bonus lands—into cards (namely the opponent's cards). It is kind of a backwards runaround, but [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) makes a massive amount of pure card advantage. But those cards are "just" lands; the lands fuel an ostensibly "eh" [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime), but in bulk, you have a situation where [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) is essentially always trading up, and going long enough with both [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) and [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), the opponent can be completely locked down.

[Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) has tremendous synergy with [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), as discarding [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) is basically long-run irrelevant. It can trade one-for-three every turn in essentially inexorable fashion; there is no amount of "fair" card trading that can ever stop a [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), and Liliana putting it in the graveyard is actually one of the best places it can be (for instance, there are decks in this Top 8 with the card [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique)). In addition, discarding [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) is great because then you can dredge it to set up some lands-in-graveyard card advantage.

A card that is very much like [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) (but weirder looking) is [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab). I wouldn't have thought of [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) as a particularly playable card in Modern, but I would have certainly been down with [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) (another card that Magnan played, albeit in the sideboard), and [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) can hit opponents in the face.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flame+Jab)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab)  
So what is the value of these reusable but superficially one-mana-for-one-ding/ping/point-of-toughness removal spells? Essentially, they are reusable! There is not a single non-[Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) Faerie that can realistically live through a [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) or [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab). [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite): Dead. [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique): Dead. [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona): Dead... dead Dead DEAD. Even [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) out of the sideboard can fall victim to "upkeep, [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) that, dredge back [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast), [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) that."

And again: [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) can go to the face.

Magnan played all kinds of powerful cards that have varying levels of synergy with [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), but [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) is the kind of card combo decks have been built around in the past. Basically, while Bronson is dredging up some of those twenty-eight lands, three-for-one every turn, rather than discarding he can just discard a land to deal 2 points. That 2 can be to the face (and can be a huge end-game flurry) or can be to a creature. The important thing: most of those cards being discarded are basically free, and [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) asks for no operating mana (big wow). You might have to bend your base a little to hit ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif), but it really isn't a huge amount to ask given the options and the returns in a Loam deck.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Seismic+Assault)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault)  
On top of this, Aggro Loam is one of the most card-advantageous decks in the format. Not only does it have [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam)—which is essentially an [Ancestral Recall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall) every turn, provided you can do something with lands—it has [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). Bronson played twenty-eight lands, ten one-drops, twelve two-drops, and nothing over three mana (save [Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth) in the sideboard, which *gains* life)... so Maher has little downside here.

In terms of creatures, that is where the "Aggro" part comes in. [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) is a known quantity. In a Dredge deck, filling your graveyard isn't really hard to fulfill. What is more interesting is [Countryside Crusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher).

If you've never played [Countryside Crusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher), here's how it works: You never draw land. Not only do you never draw land, the creature gets bigger every turn you were *supposed* to draw land. Here's how the card is really spectacular with [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam): [Countryside Crusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher) gets +1/+1 when lands hit the graveyard... which is subtly different from just counting how many lands are in the graveyard. What does this mean for a deck that is good at digging lands back out of the graveyard as well as milling them and dredging them in? Your [Countryside Crusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher)*stays* huge even when you use [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) to take lands out. More than that, if you pitch such lands to [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault), *you get even more +1/+1 counters...* for the same lands!

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td183_life.jpg)[Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) | Art by [Terese Nielsen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Terese+Nielsen%22%5D)

Magnan's deck certainly seems exciting, but it is important to note that the Lincoln Top 8 had all of two copies of [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm)—total—in sideboards (one was in this deck's), and Luis Scott-Vargas's [Grafdigger's Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage)s text that doesn't stop you from dredging [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) to recoup against [Countryside Crusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Countryside+Crusher), fuel [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault), or just make land drops. Hate is the big limiting factor on this deck. Because, unchecked? Like I said before, it provides the opportunity to do everything almost every player likes to do in **Magic**.

... well, other than tapping [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s.

Samuel Karls took care of that for this Top 8, though.

Pretty straightforward Mono-Blue Faeries here. There are pretty robust control capabilities to this deck (permission for their spells and [Vedalken Shackles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) as the big trump) but long-term counterspell control is kind of an illusion in Modern, with the powerful draw engines and the presence of the storm mechanic. Faeries doesn't just counter a target spell, but uses flash creatures (one of which is pretty big) to close out the game before the opponent can draw out of the [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s and [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)s.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vedalken+Shackles)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles)  
[Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) is there to deflect some defense, but Faeries's blue-based "take on all comers" philosophy might have some long-run problems following this Grand Prix. [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) was already a fringe card played in a variety of black decks, from Jund to Bump, to close out Wizards decks that could only win with Delvers, Snapcasters, and Confidants. And Faeries isn't in a much better spot... especially with Magnan's innovation of [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab).

LSV, the *de facto* consensus first-pick of Pro Tour Drafts, has bounced back from an off #PTDKA with a Top 8 in Lincoln. His White-Blue UrzaTron deck is packed with power... and, in particular, powerful creatures.

Most of the White-Blue UrzaTron decks we have seen to date in Modern have finished the game with a combination of [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) and [Academy Ruins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins). Instead, the ChannelFireball superstar chose to bend his copious mana to winning on the spot (because that's pretty much what happens when you tap [15](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=15) for [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)).

The repositioning on victory conditions allows for some heretofore unexplored lines of play; for example, LSV played [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites)... a card that, front-side, he might have some problems playing. But toss in a well-placed [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge)? Fatty plus flashback can very well be up in the opponent's grill (non-Eldrazi fatty, natch).

Uncontested, the Urza's mana engine is one of the most powerful things you can exploit in the Modern format, and LSV's individual card choices are reflective of that. His permission front-line of [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and [Condescend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condescend) don't just counter target spell... they dig. [Expedition Map](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) is there to not just finish an Urza's assembly... it can get [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin), too (by the way, [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) is quite friendly with [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)).

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Expedition+Map)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map)  
Luis Scott-Vargas was one of two huge names in the Top 8 of Lincoln; the other is the reemerged favorite Andrew Cuneo. Cuneo is not just one of the most seminal deck designers in the history of **Magic** (having invented the Draw-Go archetype back in 1997), but was considered one of the good men of the Pro Tour (Andrew once gave his opponent a take-back in an elimination round of a Masters grinder because he didn't want to win if there was any confusion about the number of token creatures on the battlefield that were tapped... he won the event anyway). One of the most consistently successful **Magic Online** performers, Andrew got back on the Pro Tour at the World Championships and has parlayed his Top 16 finish to a reinvigorated set of appearances... including a finals in Lincoln.

> * Meanwhile, the scry 1 on [Viscera Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) actually lets you filter through your deck—your entire deck if you like—while gaining "infinite" life...
> * So you can find [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap)! Ditto on the repeated sacrifices, except you hit the opponent ten times instead.

It is a testament to the diversity of the Modern format that we can have a BRG deck like Magnan's [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) in the same Top 8 as two other Jund-colored decks... and that those decks are distinct not just from the Loam model, but from each other.

Sure, both Mercier and Rutledge have similar cores to their builds... [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) as their four-ofs, with a shake of [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), but Rutledge played not just the full four Finks but even added Standard standout [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren). Mercier used those slots on additional functionality, like more land—and more man-lands—and [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm). [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) in the main deck can deal damage, save creatures from removal, annihilate the graveyard, or sweep a ton of tiny guys right under the rug. Plus, it is a medium-expensive three you can play for free off a [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). I don't know how often this will come up because of the timing on sacrifice effects (and the relatively low frequency of undying effects in Modern at present), but you can probably find a time when [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) can turn off undying (and it is actually worth a card).

Diversity, it seems, is the state of the game. Players reported playing as many as twelve different archetypes! Super diverse formats with no clear leader tend to favor the most powerful decks with relentless card advantage or mana engines; in your determination of which angle you will take, you certainly have a lot of options! Good luck.
