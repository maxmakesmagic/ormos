Taking Down the Establishment (Standard After Pro Tour Origins)
===============================================================



 Posted in [Perilous Research](/en/articles/columns/perilous-research-archive)
 on August 6, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 





Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG's exclusive *Magic Online* column. Last weekend, we watched the best players in the world take on a Standard environment that included *Magic Origins*. When the dust settled, aggressive red strategies and Blue-Red [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) decks stood atop a diverse field of powerful options. Today, we'll be taking a look at what's been winning on *Magic Online* in the wake of the Pro Tour.



Let's start by discussing what we've learned from last weekend.



Aggressive red strategies are a very real threat that will, at least for now, require us to devote a significant portion of our sideboards to beating the deck. Even our main decks will be warped in the coming weeks to handle the rise of the red boogieman. The most popular sideboard options at this point in time seem to be [Arashin Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Cleric) and [Feed the Clan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feed+the+Clan).



The Blue-Red [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) decks are capable of tremendously fast starts. These strategies encourage us to play more inexpensive spot removal and make cards like [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) much more appealing as main deck inclusions.



Identifying powerful cards that perform particularly well against both these strategies is also quite important. [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) has the ability to counter a burn spell while also killing a creature or pumping one of our own. This two-mana instant can also be used to deal with an early [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) in a more efficient fashion than other main deckable cards currently in Standard. We can expect decks that would want to be playing with [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) anyway, like Abzan Aggro and Heroic, to perform especially well in the coming weeks. [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) decks were deemed too weak against [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) for consideration at the Pro Tour, but [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) strategies underperformed and their share of the current metagame will shrink in the coming weekends.



Let's take a look at what's been winning on *Magic Online* since the Pro Tour!

Abzan Control didn't have a great Pro Tour, but Matt Sperling still managed to make the Top 8 piloting the deck. If red strategies continue to do well going forward, we can expect lists to steadily increase the number of [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang)s they play in the main deck. The sideboard of Abzan Control will be fully outfitted with [Arashin Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Cleric) and [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) to further hedge the red matchups. Abzan Control is already decently positioned against Blue-Red [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) decks thanks to the high density of removal available to the deck. This is the type of strategy that can beat anything if it's trying to, and now that the format has been more established we can expect big things from Abzan strategies once more.

[Demonic Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Pact) proved itself to be the real deal at Pro Tour *Magic Origins*. Various strategies employing the new enchantment performed well, but the Sultai version of the deck seems to be the most successful on *Magic Online*. This particular list seems very weak against red decks, but many versions are playing enough copies of [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang) and [Feed the Clan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feed+the+Clan) to pad their life total and win a long game against the format's most aggressive entity. [Demonic Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Pact) offers a very powerful effect for its cost, but it's worth noting that disenchant effects and cards like [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) will become more popular, and the deck may suffer enough splash damage to be pushed out of competition in the coming weeks.

Abzan Rally aims to fill its graveyard with powerful creatures before using [Rally the Ancestors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors) to bring back a swath of creatures, including [Mogis's Marauder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogis%27s+Marauder) and [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk), to win the game seemingly out of nowhere. Even without its namesake, the deck can win games simply by using [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) for value while trading aggressively with the opponent. [Arashin Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arashin+Cleric) and [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) are both on-color and should give this deck enough game against the new expected format.

[Harbinger of the Tides](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbinger+of+the+Tides) is one of the format's best answers to an early copy of [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact), and [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command) performs beautifully alongside it and against the wave of red strategies we've been seeing. Jeskai decks are traditionally strong against control strategies, and [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) upgrades the available two-drops significantly for a deck that's trying to card [Dig Through Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dig+Through+Time). This is definitely a deck to watch in the coming weeks.
