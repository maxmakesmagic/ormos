Oliver Tomajko's Abzan Reanimator
=================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on April 29, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Graveyard strategies are some of the most successful decks in today's Standard environment. The format offers us a great selection of self-mill cards in [Satyr Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Satyr+Wayfinder) and [Commune with the Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+the+Gods). [Satyr Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Satyr+Wayfinder) is one of the best utility spells in Standard right now. It helps find a land you need, which is important if you're playing a three-color deck, it fills up your graveyard for spells like [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos), and can be exploited with [Sidisi, Undead Vizier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidisi%2C+Undead+Vizier).



My favorite new addition to Abzan Reanimator is [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) from *Dragons of Tarkir*. Most self-mill spells only instruct you to put a specific card type in your hand, like creature or land. Sometimes you really need to draw a removal spell and it feels really bad when you mill them away. [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) lets you get back any card that you may have lost in previous turns. Getting back any card is pretty flexible, and while [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) is a bit mana intensive (three to cast face-down and two to activate megamorph), being able to get back a removal spell, a [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos), an important creature that died, or even a land makes it totally worth it.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Den+Protector)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector)


Oliver chose to play a few one-ofs in his list, and I think his choices are great. These are cards that you're happy to draw against some opponents but don't really want against others, like [Reclamation Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclamation+Sage). Playing self-mill spells allow you to burn through your deck quickly and let you see more cards than most other decks. Even if your one [Reclamation Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclamation+Sage) or [Sidisi, Undead Vizier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidisi%2C+Undead+Vizier) ends up in your graveyard early in the game, you have plenty of ways to get them back if you need to.
