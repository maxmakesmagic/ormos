Conspirallies
=============



 Posted in **Reconstructed**
 on May 20, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






Welcome to *Conspiracy* Week here on DailyMTG!


I must admit, when my editor came to me and let me know that we were oddly devoting an entire week in the middle of May to the *Mercadian Masques* card [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy), I was a little surprised. But when life gives you unusual punches, you have to try and make a knuckle sandwich (...or something) and soon after that initial surprise came excitement!



![19702](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&MultiverseId=19702&options=)


[Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) is one of those odd cards that people tend to bring up whenever a new tribal card comes around. "Oh man, I wish X card had creature type Y instead!" some player remarks. And inevitably, somewhere nearby you hear the remark, "There's always [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy)!" (It's scientifically proven that this is also most often the guy who reminds you about Changelings when listing off all the interactions a tribal card can have.)


After being reprinted in *Time Spiral*, [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) is legal for Modern play—so, what absurdity has [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) brought to the table today?


Let's take a look at this curious deck, sent in by Tony Youssef:

### The Battle Plan


This deck not only plays the card [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy)—it also plays out like it has a [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy)!


It's so innocent looking. Kind of like that budding serial killer you dated in high school (didn't we all?), this deck looks so cute across the table: "Oh, Modern Allies. How quaint. Isn't that adorable? Why don't you go run off along with your friends soulshift-with-*Innistrad*-Spirits and Assassin tribal and try not to get into too much trouble?"


But, like her, this deck has a dark side. A bit of a—if this were a movie, the camera would zoom in now—*conspiracy*.


It can certainly beat you down like an Allies deck. But it can also land a one-two [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) punch that ends the game out of nowhere.


The first is [Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger).



![Turntimber Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Turntimber%20Ranger&options=)


Let's say you play [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) and set it to Ally, then you cast a Ranger. You'll make a 2/2 Wolf and put a +1/+1 counter on the Ranger... but that Wolf is *also* an Ally, meaning it triggers the Ranger again! That creates another 2/2 Wolf and puts another counter on the Ranger and, well... you might see where this is going. Choose a number and get that many 2/2 tokens *plus*that large of a [Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger).


The other combo in the deck is [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). Now, on first glance, [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) is just a good fit for this deck. She's the 3/4 flash flier that we all know and love, and she also causes all of your Allies to get another trigger at instant speed.


But there's something else. An odd interaction I've actually never seen mentioned before.


[Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) doesn't *just* give everything of yours the chosen creature type. It's not just [Xenograft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenograft); it also takes away prior types. Now, normally in a [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) deck, this doesn't matter. But [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)'s ability has an odd turn of phrase.


To avoid flickering itself a million times and creating some kind of combo, [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) couldn't target itself. Normally, it would simply say "exile another target creature"—but because that would still create an infinite loop with another copy of itself, we looked for another solution. Until somebody found it: non-Angel.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/rc/2014/rc_wk20_300_restoangel.jpg)

But, if you were able to somehow alter [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)'s creature type... well, then you could just have [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) flicker itself a million times.


And if it were *also* an Ally, that would mean... well, a million triggers for all of your Allies.


Boom. Combo.


So, how can this deck be tuned? Well, let's take a look!


### Deck Breakdown


What cards in this deck are true Allies and which ones can be left on the bench? Let's take a look at the deck card-by-card and see what can stay and what can go.





![Hada Freeblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Hada%20Freeblade&options=)




As the only one-drop Ally creature (okay, fine, Changeling guy: the only one besides [Mothdust Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mothdust+Changeling)), you are going to want this card in any kind of aggressive Ally shell. The hands with him are some of your best, and the hands without him make you mutter "I wish I had [Hada Freeblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hada+Freeblade)" under your breath. I definitely wouldn't cut any copies.


In fact, I really want to add more one-drops into this deck. One of the most potent cards for a beatdown deck with a curve shaped like this one is [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial).



![Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=%C3%86ther%20Vial&options=)


Vial fits great in this deck and even lets you do tricks with deploying Allies at instant speed for surprising triggers. I would like to play four of them as well.


The other card I want to add is a creature that oddly isn't an Ally at all! Fortunately, he happens to work pretty well in this deck: it's [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish).



![Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Champion%20of%20the%20Parish&options=)


While it is a little awkward that he doesn't trigger your other Allies if you draw him later on, since all of your Allies are Human (unless [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) is on the battlefield naming Allies, in which case you're probably already doing your thing), on the first turn he's basically as good as a [Hada Freeblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hada+Freeblade)—and that's one of this deck's strongest cards. I'm willing to take the slight hit of synergy to play another really strong one-drop—and Champion is the additional one I want.





![Harabaz Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Harabaz%20Druid&options=)




This card is part of what makes the combo part of this deck tick. Not because it's actually part of the combo at all, but because it can accelerate you so quickly! A turn-one Freeblade into a turn-two [Harabaz Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harabaz+Druid) sets you up nicely to cast [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) on turn three, and then combo kill your opponent on the fourth turn! And while that draw won't happen too often (although you can substitute Freeblade with [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) and another two-drop), it's still something nice to maximize. Four Druids it is!





![Oran-Rief Survivalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Oran-Rief%20Survivalist&options=)




Ah, good old Survivalist. He's only a 2/2 on his own, but he grows incredibly fast. It's pretty easy to attack for 3, if not 4, the turn after you play him. And in an aggressive Ally deck, he's just the card you want.


Another card I'd like to play in this vein is [Kazandu Blademaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Blademaster). While it's a little harder on the mana than [Bojuka Brigand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Brigand), it's worth it: the vigilance and first strike are both very relevant, keeping opponent's creatures at bay while letting you send with your own. I'd like to play the full four.





![Ondu Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Ondu%20Cleric&options=)




[Ondu Cleric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ondu+Cleric) can be strong, especially in multiples. However, in an aggressive deck, I don't need the lifegain as much and would rather have a better-sized body to attack with. While gaining infinite life with the [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) combo is cute, it's not really that much better than the infinite Wolves you're going to get that turn or the million damage you're going to deal with your unblocked Ally.


Cleric can be a fine card out of the sideboard for beatdown matchups, but I'd rather not play them main deck here.





![Kabira Evangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Kabira%20Evangel&options=)




The Evangel was always good for pushing creatures through, and that role holds up well in this iteration as well.


However, something new to the Modern version is [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) putting out Allies at instant speed! It's a nice trick to have access to. Opponent attacks you? Vial in an Ally, give it protection, and block. Opponent casts a removal spell? Vial in an Ally, give it protection, and that removal spell isn't doing much of anything. Especially on your combo turn, this can be nice insurance in the games where you manage to set it up.


I don't want all four because you don't always want these and you don't always want to draw a ton every game, but I definitely like having three around.





![Hagra Diabolist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Hagra%20Diabolist&options=)




This card can double as another combo piece, which makes it pretty attractive. After all, if you combo out with [Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger) or [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) with a [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist) on the battlefield, your opponent instantly dies!


However, I don't think you really need it. This deck only has room for so many five-drops, and [Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger) and [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) already suck up more than enough spaces. And, once again, usually the combo will be enough on its own; you don't need to have a long-range kill machine like this up and running as well. I'd rather cut this to make room for some less-expensive cards.





![Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Restoration%20Angel&options=)




Good on its own in this deck with flickering Allies, and also a combo enabler, this is a card I'm plenty happy with. Keep in mind that it has flash as well, so when you have [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) on the battlefield and it's time to finish off your opponent you can attack and then cast it after blocks—hopefully killing your opponent. I definitely want all four of these.





![Turntimber Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Turntimber%20Ranger&options=)




Strong Ally? Check. Combo piece? Check. At five mana it's a little expensive, but with [Harabaz Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harabaz+Druid) around I'm comfortable enough playing some five-mana cards—and Ranger is strong enough to be worth it. I'm keeping these.





![Conspiracy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Conspiracy&options=)




And here it is! This oddly shaped Ally deck's *raison de vivre*.


It can be powered out fast with a [Harabaz Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harabaz+Druid), or if the game just stalls out you can always slam it on turn five and hope for the turn-six kill. (Or turn-five kill, if you have an [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) on the appropriate number.) This is definitely a fun piece to have around.


Unusually for me in combo decks, this is actually something I want to go down to three copies of. Every other card in the deck is pretty functional on its own, but drawing an opening hand with two (or more!) copies of [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) can really slow this beatdown deck down. I'd rather have a consistent beatdown deck with an occasional combo kill than something that stumbled too often because it gets loaded with a hand full of these. Plus, with the lower curve I want to trim a few lands, meaning I won't always be able to cast a five-drop. Three copies should be plenty.


With all of those changes made, and some mana base tweaks to account for it all, that brings the deck to:

If you're looking for a goofy, unique Modern deck to play, you've come to the right place! While I wouldn't expect this deck to take down a huge tournament anytime soon, it's still a blast.


If the idea of Allies is scintillating but you don't care for [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) as much, you can definitely lower the curve of this and turn it into a contender. The only black card is [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy), so you could easily move into red for [Akoum Battlesinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Battlesinger)—or just go all five colors and include [Bojuka Brigand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Brigand), which [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) and [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) let you do pretty easily.


You may also want to consider some other noncreature cards. [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) are both good options as staples of the format. But something outside the ordinary to consider might be [Lead the Stampede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lead+the+Stampede) (and, to a lesser extent, [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade)) which will have a pretty high hit rate in a deck like this.


Have fun!
