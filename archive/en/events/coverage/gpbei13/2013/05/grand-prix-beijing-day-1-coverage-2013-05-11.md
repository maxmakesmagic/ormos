Trostani, Selesnya's Voice 
 |
| *Trostani would be so proud!*  |

It seems that Selesnya is the top color pair for Sealed, with majority of the winnings playing with [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s and [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains)! A whooping 11 Grinders took place yesterday (10 Sealed, 1 Standard), rewarding their respective winners with three precious byes for the much-coveted headstart today! Check out the funky "American Auras" Standard decklist as well. Seems mildly terrifying!

Saturday, 11:15 a.m. – and Your Mana Base
-----------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

 

I'm sure you've had your fair share of fun and excitement during the Prerelease but competitors in Beijing this morning are utilizing an entirely different format. Rather than using two Guild Boosters and four Dragon Maze boosters, everyone is playing with two *Return to Ravnica*, two *Gatecrash* and two Dragon Maze boosters today.


  

This assortment promises to be mind-boggling and flummoxing, uncharted waters that await our exploration. With all ten guilds being equally represented in *Dragon's Maze* and only five guilds each in *Return to Ravnica* and *Gatecrash*, it would be difficult to construct the optimum deck with maximum power. The high density of gold cards in *Dragon's Maze* further complicates matters, as they force (or tempt) you to play with three or more colors.


Mana is obviously a huge issue in multi-color format and it is not to be overlooked. Here are some tips on pointers on how you can improve upon your deck construction skills by paying more attention to your mana base!



![Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Steam+Vents)

### Shocklands


While it is often better to receive a bomb in the appropriate color (like the somewhat ridiculous [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars)), players should not be unhappy to receive Shocklands like [Overgrown Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb) or [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground). The [Steam Vents](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents) you receive could be tremendous help in aiding you splashing [Explosive Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact), [Mugging](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mugging) and [Punish the Enemy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punish+the+Enemy) in your otherwise removal-light Azorius deck that also gives you a bit of reach to finish off an opponent when their life totals are low. The ability to include cards to support your intended strategy is something that is not to be underestimated. Shocklands allow you to do that without impeding your mana development. Sometimes, 2 life is not a lot to pay if it can help you cast an important spell on time and on target.



![Golgari Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Golgari+Guildgate)

### Guildgates


Guildgates also serve a similar function as Shocklands and also have the additional benefit of enabling cards like Ogre Jailbreaker, [Greenside Watcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenside+Watcher), [Way of the Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Way+of+the+Thief), [Gatecreeper Vine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) and the entire cycle of Gatekeepers (like [Ubul Sar Gatekeepers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ubul+Sar+Gatekeepers)). Heck, even [Maze's End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze%27s+End) is a decent card in a deck with multiple Gates, since it provides the possibility of "free cards" in the mid to late game.

### Keyrunes and Cluestones


Keyrunes and Cluestones prove to be an important part of deck construction, as they enable players to cast their huge threats and powerful spells with greater ease and speed. Cluestones double up as anti-manaflood measures, as they can be cycled in the late game when excess mana is no longer required. Some players have argued that Cluestones are worth "half a land" and you can safely run sixteen lands instead of the usual seventeen if you are running two or three mana producing artifacts.

### Other Notable Cards


Players will also need to look out for [Transguild Promenade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transguild+Promenade), [Prophetic Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism) and [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern), as they are most likely to make the cut in a deck featuring three or more colors. Green players have access to [Verdant Haven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Haven), [Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian) and to a lesser extent [Mana Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Bloom), which both also allow production of mana of all five colors while accelerating your game. Special mention goes to [Thespian's Stage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage). If you're color-screwed, at least you can rely on what your opponent has!


Final tip! If your deck is primarily blue and green, and you have access to a [Dimir Guildgate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildgate) and [Golgari Cluestone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Cluestone), it could be fine to splash three to four cards like [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy), [Dimir Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Charm), [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and [Mirko Vosk, Mind Drinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirko+Vosk%2C+Mind+Drinker) and get by with one or two [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s. Split cards like [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%2F%2F+Away) are also worth a try, because even if you didn't draw the black mana, you can still cast the blue half so it's not so bad. However, it would be greedy to try to include [Necropolis Regent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropolis+Regent) or [Grisly Spectacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Spectacle) with only four black sources in your deck! Practice makes perfect, do attend your next Sealed Deck tournament at your local store or rip open some boosters and do some practice sealed decks on your own!

Saturday, 12:35 p.m. – Beijing Then and Now
-------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim*

 


Switching alternately between blistering summers and frostbitten winters, Beijing is emblematic of a mighty nation undergoing colossal transformation. A city of neatly-ordered design, highlighted with marvels of ancient history, Beijing still bewilders first-time tourists, who are overwhelmed by its modernity and immensity, as well as its rich heritage and commerical vibrancy.


With a total area of 16800 square kilometers, Beijing is roughly the size of Belgium. It appears unforgivingly huge but Beijing is a city of orderly design. Think of the city as one giant grid, with the [Forbid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbid)den City at its centre. Aside from being home to the imperial palace, segments of the [Great Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Wall) and the majestic Temple of Heaven, it was also the host of the first ever Grand Prix that had ever been held within Mainland China!

Dong could be described as the "beacon of hope" for aspiring Chinese players and paved the way for future successes of the local community.

Building a Sealed Deck is never an easy task, especially in this world of chaos now that Dragon Maze is in the mix. Once deck construction began, Mr. X. quickly removed the unplayable cards and threw it into a virtual bin, the far end of his allocated table.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/DeckA.jpg) *Optimizing your card pool is harder than you think!* 
The blue cards had virtually no creatures, and proved so weak that they soon joined that pile of untouchables and Mr. X. quickly worked on assembling a Jund deck, featuring all his best black, red and green cards. A pair of all-star [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) and double [Launch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch) party provided him the necessary removal to deal with any bombs he might encounter but his assortment of creatures seemed lackluster.


Not wanting to play to too many sub-par creatures, he tried to assemble a Green Black White deck in an attempt to improve his creature quality, which gave also him new options of [Arrest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest) and [Ready & Willing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ready+%2F%2F+Willing), a potential stalemate breaker. [Lobber Crew](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew) was also cited as an important card, which would aid him in pushing the last few damage.

Round 4 Feature Match – Makihito Mihara (White Black Red) vs. Hao Yu, Gong (White Blue)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim and Jim Jen*

 


2006 World Champion Mikihito Mihara needs little introduction. Mihara has been known for his limited prowess, seeing how he most recently lifted the trophy (defeating Yuuya Watanabe, Motoki Abe and Akimasa Yamamoto) at Grand Prix Taipei 2012 just half a year ago. Currently sitting at 26 points, Mihara should have no difficulty acquiring a couple more to lock up Gold Level for the next season. In his way was local player Hao Yu Gong, armed with a seemingly quick Azorius beatdown deck.


### Game One


Mihara won the die roll and was given the chance to play first. Gong had to mulligan down to six and quickly faced assault from the World Champion's [Syndic of Tithes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndic+of+Tithes) and [Basilica Screecher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Screecher).

Gong 1 - Mihara 0

Round 6 Feature Match – Xu Bin (Black Red Green) vs. Huang Hao-shan (White Green)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim and Jim Jen*

 


Both players are prominent figureheads on the Asian Grand Prix Circuit. Gold Level Pro Huang Hao-Shan most recently made Top 8 at Grand Prix Singapore 2012 where he displayed prowess of the *Return to Ravnica* format. Xu Bin is also rather accomplishing, most remembered as the finalist of China Nationals 2011 and making the Top 8 at Grand Prix Shanghai that very same year.


### Game One


Huang won the die roll, opening with [Battering Krasis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battering+Krasis) and then evolving it with [Saruli Gatekeepers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saruli+Gatekeepers). Xu promptly pushed [Warmind Infantry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warmind+Infantry) in the way of the 3/2 trampler before it could grow any bigger.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/FMR6-XuBin.jpg) *Xu Bin* 
Xu replaced his fallen 2/3 with [Ivy Lane Denizen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Lane+Denizen) and [Thrashing Mossdog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrashing+Mossdog) (putting the +1/+1 counter on it). Huang had an even fatter threat, his trusty Armored Wolf Rider, but it fell to [Fatal Fumes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Fumes) when it stood in the way of the 4/4 [Mossdog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mossdog).


Obviously mana flooded, all Huang could manage was [Wild Beastmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Beastmaster) and a last-ditch effort [Launch Party](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party) on [Vassal Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul). Counting his 9 lands in play and holding 2 more, he could only watch Xu dismantle his board mercilessly with [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) and [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay).


Xu 1 - Huang 0


### Game Two


Huang opted to play first and had discretely swapped his White Green deck into his "Plan B", Esper Fliers. He led with [Daggerdrome Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp), [Seller of Songbirds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seller+of+Songbirds) and [Concordia Pegasus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concordia+Pegasus) and started to chip away at Xu's life total.


He could only manage [Golgari Cluestone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Cluestone) on turn three and then summon [Scab-Clan Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Giant) on turn 5. It managed to gun down [Concordia Pegasus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concordia+Pegasus) and Huang used [Soul Tithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Tithe) on the 4/5 Giant Warrior. Naturally, Xu declined to pay up and used his mana to fuse [Toil & Trouble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toil+%2F%2F+Trouble), giving himself two cards while dealing five damage to Huang.


Despite still being stuck on three land, Huang's air forces and [Syndic of Tithes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndic+of+Tithes) eventually lowered Xu to down to 9 life. [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) made an appearance alongside [Frilled Oculus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus) the next turn, fighting and shooting [Daggerdrome Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp) down from the skies.


Xu followed up that play with [Beetleform Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage), and used [Flesh & Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flesh+%2F%2F+Blood) to remove [Syndic of Tithes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syndic+of+Tithes). Xu refilled his hand once again with [Thoughtflare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtflare), then used [Stolen Identity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stolen+Identity) to duplicate [Seller of Songbirds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seller+of+Songbirds), ciphering it upon [Beetleform Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage) (attacking) and then making yet a second copy. Huang had [Fatal Fumes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Fumes) to stop the reproduction but it seemed like the damage was done and Xu's board was overwhelming.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/FMR6-Huang-Hao-Shan.jpg) *Huang Hao-shan* 
Fusing [Down & Dirty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Down+%2F%2F+Dirty), Xu picked up [Flesh & Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flesh+%2F%2F+Blood) and proceeded to use [Street Spasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm) and Punish the enemy to clear away Huang's threats. To be honest, anyone would think that Huang was done for, but he was hatching a devious plot.


Coughing up [Assault Griffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Griffin) as well as [Isperia's Skywatch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isperia%27s+Skywatch) to buy him some time, he bode his time and laid low, waiting for Xu to cast [Flesh & Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flesh+%2F%2F+Blood). Eventually, Xu "scavenged" [Scab-Clan Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Giant) onto [Seller of Songbirds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seller+of+Songbirds), creating a 5/7 monstrosity which Huang was happy to point [Grisly Spectacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Spectacle) at, milling five precious cards from his whittled library. This reduced Xu to just two cards in his library, and it was a matter of chump-blocking and staying alive until Xu's library emptied itself.


Xu 1 - Huang 1


With just two minutes on the clock, both players decided to take the draw and catch a breather before the next round.


**Xu Bin draws with Huang Hao-Shan, putting both players at 4-1-1 and (effectively) on the bubble.**

Round 8 Feature Match – Kuo Tzu Ching (White Black) vs. Zhao Tian Wu (Black Red Green)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim and Jim Jen*

 


Zhao Tian Yu (currently schooling in Japan) has crossed the ocean to participate in this Grand Prix, only to find himself seated across Asian juggernaut Kuo Tzu Ching. Kuo has had quite a spectacular season thus far and none could deny it.


Starting the season with a World **Magic** Cup win and a Player Championship appearance, he proceeded on to chalk up 39 Pro Points over the past few months, needing just 6 more to renew his Platinum membership once again. His most recent success (also his 9th Grand Prix Top 8) was at Grand Prix Quebec 2013, where he was one game short from clinching the champion trophy. Both players had to win if they did not want to be eliminated from Day Two. Unfortunately, only one would survive the harsh reality of the bubble.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/FMR8-Kuo.jpg) *Kuo Tzu Ching* 
### Game One


Kuo sought to end the game quickly with a swift opening ([Boros Mastiff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Mastiff) and Gutter Stalk), only to be momentarily held back by Zhao's [Riot Piker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riot+Piker). Kuo's [Sin Collector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) failed to pluck off anything, but he was informed of his opponent's upcoming [Gruul Cluestone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Cluestone), [Scab-Clan Charger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Charger) and [Scab-Clan Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Giant).


As expected, the mana artifact entered play next turn, but Kuo seemed to be low on totally out of gas and could do nothing but to beautify his side of the table with an irrelevant [Azorius Cluestone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Cluestone).


The turn before [Scab-Clan Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Giant) entered the battlefield (it munched up [Boros Mastiff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Mastiff)), Zhao unleashed [Gore-House Chainwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker), setting up a lethal attack which Kuo didn't expect.


When Kuo tapped out for [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council), Zhao revealed [Molten Primordial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Primordial) from his hand to wrench it, killing his opponent in one brutal blow.


Kuo 0 – Zhao 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/FMR8-Zhao.jpg) *Zhao Tian Wu* 
### Game Two


The second game ended even faster than the first.


Kuo summoned Gutter Stalk, [Sin Collector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) (nabbing nothing once again) and [Perilous Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Shadow) within his first four turns. Unfortunately, Zhao was able to gum up the grand with a 2/3 [Dead Reveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reveler) and [Warmind Infantry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warmind+Infantry) and started blitzing through the air with an unleashed [Rakdos Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Drake). A freshly-drawn, unanswered [Madcap Skills](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Madcap+Skills) made the flier 5/3 and ended the game two turns later, eliminating Kuo from Day Two. He would need to get all of his six points from Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*.


**Kuo 0 – Zhao 2**
