Hit the Ground Running
======================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 27, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive [**Magic Online**](/en/articles/archive/2011-10-17) column. [Last week](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/shifting-standard-2013-06-20), we looked at the Standard format and discussed some of the changes we were beginning to see in major archetypes. Many of the points we discussed last week are becoming truer than even I might have imagined. 

 The Standard format is more about a board presence than it has been since last year. [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) decks, especially those without [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), are finding themselves outpaced by the smaller decks. In fact, aggressive red-green decks were outperforming every other archetype in Daily Events until this past weekend. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Sphinx's Revelation | Farseek |

 Why are [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) decks suffering? Here's the problem with the big spell right now: There's a lot to interact with in the early turns of the game. You need the right mix of reactive cards for the early turns if you're going to survive to the point where your [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) is big enough to matter. A lot of the time, you get stuck on four or five lands and your reactive game plan starts getting outshined by your opponent's five-drops. 

 The best threats being played today all have pretty specific answers. You need exactly [Turn & Burn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn) to deal with [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk); exactly [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) to deal with [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger), or [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist); exactly [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) for [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) draws; exactly two burn spells to deal with [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite); and exactly [Renounce the Guilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) to deal with [Obzedat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council), [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), or [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion). This is a major problem for the control decks. If you find yourself without the specific card necessary for your situation you'll likely end up getting run over. It's hard to draw the seven lands you need for your [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) to be truly gamebreaking and still have drawn all the correct answers. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Renounce the Guilds | Supreme Verdict |

 As a result, red-white-blue players have begun playing a lot more creatures. It's a lot more reasonable to control the first four turns (rather than the first seven) with [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas), [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame), [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm), [Warleader's Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix), [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). Once the game transitions into big spells, you need to put your opponent on a clock immediately. This is where [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) comes in. The Dragon gives red-white-blue players the ability to pick a fight the moment the game looks like it's getting even. These decks then use [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm)'s lifelink mode aggressively with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to ensure they will be victorious in the racing situation. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Azorius Charm | Snapcaster Mage |

 These newer creature-heavy versions of control decks have been cutting [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). This makes a lot of [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) strategies jump out of tier two range and hop right up into tier one territory. Aggressive Jund lists have been the big winner this week in terms of viability jumps. 

 Bant Hexproof was on the cusp of tier one for a while. Now, with no [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s or [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) in sight, the deck is more powerful than ever. Sure, consistency can be an issue at times, but the deck's power level puts it a step beyond the rest of the format in its current state. I wouldn't be surprised if we started seeing a lot of [Ray of Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ray+of+Revelation)/[Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) popping up in sideboards this coming weekend. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Ray of Revelation | Liliana of the Veil |

Let's take a look at the new decks of Standard.


[Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) decks have gotten a lot better now that [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) is being played less, so let's see what's cooking in the aggro department!

 Aggressive Gruul strategies have been doing well since the start of the format. The deck is becoming more streamlined now and the lists are finally starting to look similar. [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) is almost always a four-of in the main deck now. Eight burn spells have become much more commonplace now that most decks are playing guys at two, three, and four mana. Some lists still have [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade), but most players are opting for the most aggressive build possible. [Volcanic Strength](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Strength) has become the mirror-breaker card of choice in the deck. [Electrickery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrickery) is good at sweeping away big swaths of tokens or mana creatures and it doubles up as a good way to interact with [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) in response to [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage). I expect this deck to remain the top dog in the aggro department for the foreseeable future.

 I played a few Daily Events last week in preparation for a Standard Event I played last weekend. This is still a [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) deck, but the fourteen creatures in the main deck let you interact on the battlefield while you wait for the big spell to become relevant. Since this event, I've cut another [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for a third [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) in the main. I've been really impressed with the Dragon and I expect most red-white-blue lists to look more like this in the coming weeks.

 Here's an interesting take on the red-white-blue deck that will probably remind you of a lot of the decks from [*Pro Tour Gatecrash*](/en/events/coverage/martell-calls-down-reckoning-pro-tour). This deck is interesting and powerful for the current format. The deck underperforms on **Magic Online** because it's pretty much impossible to "go infinite" with [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). In live events, the deck can use the lifelink mode of [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm), the indestructible mode of [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm), and a [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) to gain infinite life. Here's how it works: the Reckoner is going to take damage, so you use both charms, you repeatedly target the Reckoner with its own ability and gain life equal to the damage dealt each time. On **Magic Online**, you need to manually click through each trigger, you can still gain a ton of life and put most aggressive decks out of range, but there's definitely a difference between being at 200 and being at 200,000,000 life.

Draw a few opening hands with this deck and you'll realize that it's not as straightforward as everyone thinks. The deck gives you a ton of room to outplay opponents and a lot of the draws are simply auto-wins. I think this deck is the next big thing.  This is a deck I can get excited about. I'd like to find room for at least a single copy of [Rakdos Keyrune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune), but the general concept of playing the best creature every turn seems pretty good to me. There are a lot of nightmare cards for control decks here and the aggro matchups seem good. The deck even has a psuedo-reset button/combo kill in the form of [Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act).
