The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on January 11, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by World Champion [Brian Braun-Duin's big move up the Top 25](#7).

You read that last part correctly. White. Blue. Ramp.

Top 25
======


1. Grand Prix Louisville Leaves Its Mark

The big news out of [Grand Prix Louisville](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gplou17) this weekend was the Top 8 match between Brian Braun-Duin and Reid Duke. Duke came out on top—and on top of the Grand Prix—but both players moved up the rankings in strong ways. The reigning World Champion (that would be Braun-Duin) leapt up six spots to No. 18. Duke doesn't have that far to rise, but the one-spot bump to No. 4 has him 5 points above No. 5 Steve Rubin.


Remember last year when Braun-Duin made the World Championship on the back of his stellar Grand Prix performances? This year's starting off much the same, and you probably shouldn't expect any drop off from the champ.


Also making bigger moves this week are Alexander Hayne, up four spots to No. 19, and rookie of the Year Oliver Tiu, knocking out the irreplaceable Paul Rietzl to get back into the Top 25.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Mimic Engine Combo](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/standard-brews-with-aether-revolt/) | ChannelFireball | Frank Karsten

There's a lot going on here, as you might expect from Certified Math Genius Frank Karsten. Your best bet is to [read his article](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/standard-brews-with-aether-revolt/) about everything going on in this beauty, but I'll try to break it down by complexity levels.


* Level 1: The deck utilizes Elves (for [Metallic Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallic+Mimic)) and a +1/+1 counters theme to give it a reasonable beatdown angle. It's very possible to simply run over your opponent with ever-growing creatures.
* Level 2: [Metallic Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallic+Mimic) can name Servo in concert with [Animation Module](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animation+Module) to create as many 2/2s as you have mana for each turn. All you need to do is trigger [Animation Module](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animation+Module) once—it will create a Servo, which will get a +1/+1 counter, triggering [Animation Module](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animation+Module) again, and so on, and so on, until you have no more mana left.
* Level 3: [Paradox Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradox+Engine) infinite combo! This one involves a lot of moving pieces, so I recommend reading Karsten's explanation. Suffice to say, it works, and when it does, it's quite beautiful. Johnnies and Jennies everywhere will approve.
