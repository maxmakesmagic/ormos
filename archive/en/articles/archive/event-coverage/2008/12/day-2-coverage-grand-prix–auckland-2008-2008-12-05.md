Sunday, December 07: 9:43a.m. – Drafting with Shuuhei Nakamura and Luis “LSV” Scott-Vargas
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*
Well-known Pro Players Shuuhei Nakamura and Luis Scott-Vargas sat down next to each other for the first draft of day two, each of them flanked by the two undefeated players from New Zealand, Max Botkov and Tony Wootton. 


LSV’s first pick was a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) over [Naya Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Battlemage), [Bloodpyre Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodpyre+Elemental) and [Manaplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manaplasm). Nakamura’s first pick was [Esper Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Battlemage) over [Feral Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Hydra) and [Bloodpyre Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodpyre+Elemental). Both players have previously shown a preference for Esper draft decks through actually drafting them, so Nakamura’s pick was a reasonably risky one, especially considering he the fact that he keeps stating he prefers Jund or Naya. 


Pick two, LSV took an [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) over [Bull Cerodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bull+Cerodon), [Soul’s Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%E2%80%99s+Fire) and [Infest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest), while Nakamura picked up a [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder). It seems that both players had silently and mutually agreed which of them were going to draft Esper. Until pick three, when LSV took an [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp). To be fair, he didn’t have much choice, the remaining options were [Necrogenesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis) and [Executioner’s Capsule](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Executioner%E2%80%99s+Capsule), which Nakamura naturally snatched up to go with the [Infest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) he picked before it. 


By the end of pack one, Nakamura was clearly in Blue/Black. He had a couple of potential White cards, but all things considered, he had a tight and focused 14 card grip to review. LSV was in five colors. He’d picked up a [Savage Lands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands), but things looked messy, having rounded out the pack with [Kiss of the Amesha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiss+of+the+Amesha), [Cavern Thoctar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Thoctar), [Waveskimmer Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waveskimmer+Aven) and a tasty 11th pick [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm). 


Pack two LSV again opened and drafted a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray), and managed to follow it with a [Seaside Citadel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seaside+Citadel), a [Jungle Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Shrine) and an [Arcane Sanctum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Sanctum). He’d landed in five colors and hit the ground running. Nakamura continued to lower the curve of his Blue/Black deck with some unearth goodies, Screeching Kathari’s and [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie)s. 


In the last pack, both players slapped their rares down onto their piles. To be fair, anything playable LSV opened he could take, and take a [Battlegrace Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlegrace+Angel) he did, Nakamura took a [Sharding Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharding+Sphinx). The rest of the pack was reasonably uneventful in as much as Nakamura continued to receive Blue and Black stuff from LSV, who snapped up playables across the spectrum. 


“Really nice!” was how Shuuhei Nakamura described his draft, and it was finally good to see that grin spread across his face after his less than stellar sealed deck last week in Taipei. I asked him about the first pick [Esper Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Battlemage), and Nakamura said that he took it and prayed, knowing LSV’s predilection towards that shard and hoping that he would ship him the goods stuff anyway. 3 – 0? To which Nakamura replied, “I hope!” 


“I don’t like drafting 5 color,” LSV explained, “but pick three ([Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp)) was going to put me in Black along with Red and White, and that’s not a shard. Tony (Wootton) took his rare, so I had no signal to begin with.” He thought the deck worked out well enough, but had no idea how it would go. “It’s hard to tell, two out of my three possible opponents this round are (Guillaume) Wafo-Tapa or Shuuhei (Nakamura) so who knows? I could 3-0, but I could 2-1 or 1-2 as well.”

Sunday, December 07: 4:14p.m. - Round 13: Oliver Oks vs Nick Tung
-----------------------------------------------------------------

*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*
Coming into the last round, six players can intentionally draw into the top 8, and four players are left fighting over the last two spots. That's remarkably tidy as far as final rounds of a Grand Prix go, all things considered. This round we picked out New Zealand's Nick Tung, who came 5th at the New Zealand National Champs earlier this year, and Oliver Oks, an Australian ex-pat who's recently returned from living in Japan for a few years.


Oks started out with a [Savage Lands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands), and diversifying his options with an [Obelisk of Grixis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obelisk+of+Grixis) before taking a [Hell's Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hell%27s+Thunder) to the face. Oks then made a turn four [Dreg Reaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Reaver), which squared off against an [Incurable Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incurable+Ogre). The Ogre swung in and traded with a [Cylian Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cylian+Elf), inflating Tung's brand new [Rockslide Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental). When the Elemental tried to block and take out the Reaver, Oks trumped the first strike with a [Resounding Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar). Tung then made a [Corpse Connoisseur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Connoisseur), burying an [Undead Leotau](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Leotau). Oks could only play an eighth land and pass the turn back, not wanting to trade his Reaver for the Connoisseur. Tung tried to kill the 4/3 off with a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) and a [Blister Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blister+Beetle), but Oks had held another [Resounding Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar) in reserve, but unfortunately no White mana to cycle it. When Tung made a [Blood Cultist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Cultist) and finally sent in his Connoisseur, Oks took the trade, and replaced the Reaver on his turn with a [Drumhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drumhunter).


Tung attacked with the Beetle and the unearthed Thunder, and played a [Goblin Deathraiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Deathraiders). Oks could only make another Obelisk and pass the turn back. Tung unearthed the Connoisseur, burying a [Kathari Screecher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Screecher) and attacked. The [Drumhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drumhunter) trading with the Dreathraiders. Sitting on 6 life with a wealth of mana and nothing to do with it, Oks reached for his sideboard.


Tung 1 - Oks 0


Tung mulliganed Game 2, and it was his turn to be on the receiving end of a hasty beater, a turn two [Rip-Clan Crasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rip-Clan+Crasher) from Oks, who followed that up with a [Vithian Stinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vithian+Stinger). Tung played a [Kathari Screecher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Screecher), and offered the trade when Oks sent in his Crasher. This time, Oks didn't have a [Resounding Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar), instead summoning some [Dragon Fodder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder). Tung lapsed into thought, before passing the turn with four mana and no play. Oks' Fodder got in for 2, and he played a [Quietus Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quietus+Spike). At the end Oks' turn, a [Resounding Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Thunder) took out the Stinger. Tung untapped and made a [Dreg Reaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Reaver). One of the Fodder suited up in the Spike and swung in. Tung considered it and let the token through, losing half of his life and ending up on 5. The Reaver swung back, and Tung backed it up with a [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie) and a [Rockslide Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental). Oks could not only draw his card for the turn and pass.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc08/02_R13.jpg)The Reaver and the Zombie attacked, the 4/3 trading with the equipped Goblin. Tung played an [Undead Leotau](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Leotau), but Oks made a [Vein Drinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vein+Drinker). Low on life, Tung didn't have long to deal with it, and sent his team in. Oks chumped the Leotau with his Goblin and the Vampire ate the Dregscape. Oks unearthed the Stinger, equipped it and shot the [Rockslide Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental), killing it, and then passed back the turn. Having missed the Vampire's trigger when it ate the Dregscape, Oks couldn't swing back for the win and passed the turn. Tung swung in again with his Leotau, this time it was unblocked and dropped Oks to 7. Oks equipped his Vampire with the Spike and passed the turn back again without an attack, either worried about what was in Tung's hand (one card) or unaware that attacking would kill his opponent. Tung unearthed the Screecher and the [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie) and sent in his undead army. The [Vein Drinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vein+Drinker) blocked the Screecher, and Tung pumped the Leotau. In response, Oks [Resounding Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar)'d his Vampire, and then used it to take down the Leotau. Tung finished the Vampire off with a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray), Oks dropping to 5 from the unblocked Dregscape. Now with the board cleared, Oks could only pass the turn back without play. Tung unearthed his Leotau and attacked for the win, Oks revealing a hand with [Violent Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Ultimatum), [Prince of Thralls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prince+of+Thralls) and a [Grixis Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grixis+Charm), having been unable to play any of them. Cheering erupted from Tung's supporters, and once that had died down, Oks' friends could be heard explaining animatedly just how many turns he had missed where he could have attacked for the win.


Nick Tung defeats Oliver Tung 2 - 0
