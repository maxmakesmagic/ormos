Saturday, 1:36 p.m. – Grinding through M10 Sealed
-------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/grinding.jpg)With an expectation of record breaking attendance (and that expectation was met this morning with over 1500 attendees shattering the previous high water mark for a North American Grand Prix), Grand Prix Trials for three byes were in high demand last night. The fact that they were Sealed Deck, utilizing the incredibly scarce M10, certainly motivated players as well. It would also provide a first look at what color combinations and cards had what it took to make it through a 32-person elimination bracket.


Coming here on the train yesterday, I was chatting with Hall of Famer Zvi Mowshowitz about the format and what would be the dominant trait of winning decklists. There were 18 separate single-elimination tournaments last night and we thought we would take a look at some of the winning lists and see if the format was indeed dominated by rares like [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) and Planeswalkers, uncommons such as [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), [Mind Control](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control), and [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball), or if multiple copies of ther high end commons like [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) was what it took to push a deck over the top. Some of the decklists from later events have been misplaced overnight but the first nine paint a pretty interesting picture of what a winning decklist looks like.


Looking through the following lists you would certainly think that a [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), Planeswalker, [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), or [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) is required for entry into Day Two but Pro Tour Honolulu Top 8 competitor Brian Kibler provided textbook case of how to win in this format with an underpowered card pool. He swept through his Trial with a tight two-color deck that was bereft of any “Get Into Day Two Free” passes. His deck focused on a pair of [Mind Rot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) to trump the gamebreaking cards in his opponent’s hands, removal, and a motley crew of in-color critters to earn him 3 byes for the main event.

Saturday, 12:20 p.m. – Grand Prix Boston Photo Essay: Rounds 1 - 3
------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

The first three rounds of Grand Prix Boston are in the books and the Pros are limbering up to get their Goliath on in this 1500-person field of hopeful Davids. Feature Matches will commence shortly. In the meanwhile we wanted to give you a look around this massive convention hall.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/001-HJ-Wareman.jpg)


Head Judge Frank Wareman double takes before making the pre-tournament announcements to the largest North American Grand Prix field of all time. It is also the first time a Grand Prix has been broken up into two separate flights that will converge tomorrow for some red-hot M10 draft action.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/004-Dumpster-Diving.jpg)


1500 Sealed Deck players generate a lot of trash. More trash than these two judges wanted to go dumpster diving through in pursuit of an errant decklist that had gotten tossed with the wrappers.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/005-Security-Guard.jpg)


Magic 2010 is in high demand. This may be the only stockpile of it anywhere on the continent meriting some special attention at all times.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/007-Ryan-Pancoast-or-Convincing-Mirage.jpg)


Guest artist Ryan Pancoast only has two Magic cards in print so far; [Convincing Mirage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Convincing+Mirage) and a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains)...


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/008-Why-artists-dont-RnD.jpg)


...so he has resorted to making his own.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/009-Cyril-Van-der-Haegen.jpg)


In honor of Ryan’s War Rabbit, guest artist Cyril Van Der Haegen highlighted his own bunny.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/010-Lars-Grant-West.jpg)


Guest artist Lars Grant-West will get many requests for signed [Gorgon Flail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorgon+Flail)s this weekend.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/011-Sadin-and-LSV.jpg)


Limited Information author Steve Sadin and Pro Tour Berlin winner Luis Scott-Vargas put their decks through their paces while waiting for the bye rounds to expire.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/012-PV-Joins-the-Catch-Phrase-craze.jpg)


Recently crowned Brazilian National Champion Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa jumps in on the Catch Phrase phenomenon.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/013-Catch-Phrase-Superstar-Gerard-Fabiano.jpg)


Catch Phrase superstar Gerard Fabiano waited for a seat to open before he had to start playing actual matches.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/014-Bush.jpg)


It is actually a small world after all. Former GP Top 8 competitor -- and one of Boston’s more famous Magic players -- Michelle Bush was in the same building for a dermatologist’s convention. She came by to say hello to old friends after multiple people stopped her in the food court asking her about the Magic tournament. Rumor has it she may show up later and take a stab at drafting.


 

Round 4 Feature Match: Steve Sadin vs Josh Ravitz
-------------------------------------------------


*by Josh Bennett*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/rd4-ravitz.jpg)*Josh Ravitz described by Hall of Fame Chris Pikula as “a disgruntled employee, except he doesn’t work.”*“Limited Information” Columnist Steve Sadin came to the feature match smiling and rested, having enjoyed the fruits of three byes. He was joined there by his friend and now opponent Josh Ravitz, whose gruff exterior hides a heart of gruff. Ravitz had to earn his nine points the old-fashioned way this morning, after a heartbreaking 2nd place finish in one of last night’s grinders.


Sadin won the die roll and led with a swamp. He was soon on the back foot as Ravitz dropped [Elite Vanguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard) and [Deadly Recluse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse). His way worsened when he failed to find a third land on his third turn. Ravitz did not bother to feign sympathy.


Ravitz hit for three and added [Griffin Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel). Sadin’s third land finally showed up, enabling a [Looming Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade) that tackled the Vanguard. Ravitz replaced it with a [Cudgel Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll). Sadin played a [Bog Wraith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith) and shrugged as he pushed it in front of the Recluse. He was at 8 and had nothing to show for it. Ravitz sealed the deal with a [Serra Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel).


“Standard creatures versus no lands. Good game. Well played. Lots of interesting decisions.” - Josh Ravitz


Ravitz 1 - Sadin 0


 


Sadin’s draw for Game 2 had both lands AND spells, and he got on the board quickly with [Gorgon Flail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorgon+Flail) and [Relentless Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Rats). He met Ravitz’s [Centaur Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser) with a [Looming Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade), and breathed easy when Ravitz’s follow-up was only [Griffin Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel). He played his fourth swamp, suited up his Rats with the flail and turned his men sideways. Ravitz let both through, taking four.


Ravitz returned fire, but his Courser fell under [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). He could do no better than [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) and passed the turn back to Sadin. Sadin swung in again and knocked Ravitz to 8. Ravitz untapped and played [Serra Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel). Sadin played mountain and charged in again. Ravitz chose to put his Visionary in front of the Rats and let the Shade through. Sadin pumped it to 5/5 and showed him the [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


Ravitz 1 - Sadin 1


“Mulligan.” - Steve Sadin


“Awesome. Hope you take another one.” - Josh Ravitz


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/rd4-sadin.jpg)*Steve Sadin*Sadin’s six were good enough, but not quite as good as Ravitz’s seven. In short order he was on the wrong side of [Deadly Recluse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse) and [Centaur Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser). He made a feeble [Looming Shade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade) and took four. Ravitz made another Courser.


Sadin hit for one with his Shade and traded [Bog Wraith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith) for one of the Coursers, only to see it replaced by a [Giant Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider). He caught Ravitz’s last two cards, a [Kalonian Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalonian+Behemoth) and a [Craw Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craw+Wurm), but couldn’t deal with the monsters already on the battlefield. He traded his Shade for the Recluse and fell to six.


On his next turn he could do nothing but stare at his four lands and uncastable spells.


“Give up?”


“Yep.”


“Go get some ice cream?”


“Yep.”


Josh Ravitz defeats Steve Sadin 2-1


 

Round 5 Feature Match: Charles Gindy vs. Doug McCay
---------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/rd5-mccay.jpg)*Doug McCay*There were a pair of National Champions in the Feature Match area for round five. Unfortunately for Doug McCay and Justin Onusic they were not paired against each other. Doug “David” McCay had drawn the U.S. National Goliath while Justin was pairing off with Japan’s.


“Can you say ‘Hi’ to my mom for me?” said Gindy as he began his pregame shuffles. “That’s serious. I wanna say ‘Hi’ to my Mom.”


### Game 1


Gindy won the roll and elected to start second. That would be an unusual decision if the format were Standard or Draft but in Sealed Deck formats, where decks tend to more unwieldly than in Draft, the Pros will often opt for the extra card and the opportunity to have smoother draws by drawing first.


Despite starting second, Gindy summoned a [Stormfront Pegasus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) for the first play of the game and played [Kelinore Bat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kelinore+Bat) a turn later. McCay forged [Whispersilk Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispersilk+Cloak) but had nothing to put it on. He aimed an [Assassinate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate) at the Pegasus and shipped the turn back to a [Howling Banshee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Banshee) from Gindy. The Banshee was promptly enchanted with [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) McCay but he fell to 11 the next turn from the Bat. Gindy dumped his hand on the table playing [Black Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Knight) and [Blinding Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage). McCay wasted no time killing the tapper with [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) but he had no creatures for his lonely cloak.


[Serra Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel) from Gindy on the next turn was enough for McCay to reach for his sideboard.


### Game 2


Gindy brought in [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) for the second game while McCay, who chose to start the game on the play. sided out an [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) -- his deck was three colors with just over a dozen creatures -- for [Deathmark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark). Gindy led off with the sideboarded [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) on the draw and saw [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood), [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism), two more lands, [Royal Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin), and [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary). *by Josh Bennett*

californian journeyman pro Matt Sperling was all business as he shuffled up across from former World Champ and current Brazillian National Team Member Carlos Romao. They shuffled up in silence, Sperling with quick motions and Romao slow and methodical.


Sperling won the die roll and chose to draw. His advantage increased when Romao had to ship back his opener. Romao got on the board first with [Vampire Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat). They traded their 2/2’s, but now Sperling was ready with the big guns. [Shivan Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Dragon) hit the battlefield. Romao gamely played out his Specter and watched it eat a second Bolt. Sperling’s dragon brought the pain and was joined by a [Stone Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Giant). Romao peeked at his next card, and scooped.


Sperling 1 - Romao 0


Sperling made a color swap for the second game, pulling his green and white cards in favor of black ones. He led out with a pair of swamps and [Black Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Knight) while Romao sat back on two swamps and a mountain. The Knight fell to a Bolt. Sperling played [Sign in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) and passed it back.

Five mana gave Romao a [Zombie Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Goliath), and Sperling the [Berserkers of Blood Ridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserkers+of+Blood+Ridge). Matt Sperling defeats Carlos Romao 2-0

“Revenge,” said Nelson, then laughed, “though I guess I didn’t win nearly as much.”


 

Round 9 Feature Match: AJ Sacher vs. David Irvine
-------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/rd9-irvine.jpg)*Dave “Dirv” Irvine*Dave “Dirv” Irvine sat down with AJ Sacher to play for one of a handful of perfect records on the weekend. Both players had the benefit of three byes to start the event but had won their last five rounds against actual opponents. AJ was playing a Green-Blue deck that was splashing a little red -- highlights of the deck included [Djinn of Wishes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+of+Wishes) and two copies of [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep). In a previous match AJ was able to lock his opponent down with the first copy of [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep), put the second copy on top of his deck with [Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) and play it at instant speed off of the Djinn. Dave’s deck was a black-red abattoir of removal and slightly subpar creatures. There are players in this tournament who would have gladly swapped their decks for Dirv’s sideboard which inlcuded [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), Ajani, [Planar Cleansing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing), [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep), and plenty of other hits that were neither red nor black.


### Game 1


AJ mulliganed and kept a hand he described as, “pretty loose” and he stalled out on one land and then one color while Dave came out fast with [Fiery Hellhound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Hellhound), [Prodigal Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer), and [Bog Wraith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith). AJ tried to make a game of it with [Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) and [Stampeding Rhino](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stampeding+Rhino) but David was too far ahead and he scooped pretty quickly.


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbos09/rd9-aj.jpg)*AJ Sacher*Dave whiffed on a [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) -- more of a sub-Peek really -- and saw [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger), [Emerald Oryx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emerald+Oryx), and [Stampeding Rhino](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stampeding+Rhino). Dave played a [Fiery Hellhound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Hellhound) and AJ -- who had chosen to draw -- played [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger). [Bog Wraith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bog+Wraith) from Dirv was met with the Oryx from AJ. He [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)ed the Commander and dropped a [Rod of Ruin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rod+of+Ruin) to mop up its leavings. “[Rise from the Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+from+the+Grave)?” asked AJ as Dave thought about his play.

AJ knocked on his deck hoping for an answer and was rewarded with [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) but Dave had the [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) to make it elementary.
