Round 9: Derek Bruneau vs. Eugene Harvey
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 18, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a804.jpg)Read about Eugene's draft [here](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/draft-3-eugene-harvey-2003-01-18).

Going in to Round 9, only two players were 8-0: US Champion Eugene Harvey, and first-time PT'er Derek Bruneau. Harvey needs no introduction. Bruneua, 30, lives in Boston but rarely gets a chance to play with any of the city's other PT regulars. Instead he does a ton of drafting on Magic Online, and the skills he's honed in the digital world have translated into instant success professionally.

### Game 1

Derek drafted the "greedy" color combination of red/black, hoarding all the removal. His decision to do so created a ripple effect at the table that forced Eugene into the most maligned of all color combinations, white-green. Eugene is splashing for a [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) and a [Silent Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter), but he will certainly have his work cut out for him against the rookie.

Bruneau mullinganed twice to start the match but added that he couldn't complain about his luck so far in this tournament.

Bruneau's five-card hand gave him three lands, but nothing to play on the first few turns. Harvey, ever lucky, played forest-swamp-plains on the first three turns and dropped a [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage). Bruneau went to the air with a pair of [Screeching Buzzard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard)s, while Harvey put out a [Birchlore Rangers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birchlore+Rangers) and a facedown creature.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a810.jpg)The key moment in the game came when Harvey sent the face-down 2/2 in, and then used the Rangers' ability to morph it into its true form: [Silent Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter). A savaging ensued, especially when [Tribal Unity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Unity) allowed the Specter to survive being double blocked by two Buzzards.

Harvey 1 – Bruneau 0

### Game 2

Bruneau regrouped for Game 2 and came out with the killer opening of [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder), [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), and [Spitfire Handler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitfire+Handler). Eugene, playing mostly white/green, could do literally nothing about it. He put out a [Dive Bomber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) to see if Bruneau was willing to actually use the Smith. To no one's surprise, the Bostonian did so, and Eugene scooped up his cards.

Harvey 1 – Bruneau 1

### Game 3

Harvey breathed a sigh of relief when it was evident Bruneau had no turn-2 play. With no [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) in sight, he played out a [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage). Bruneau [Swat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swat)ted it, but Eugene had a replacement one, plus [Improvised Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Improvised+Armor) to suit it up.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a805.jpg)Bruneau deployed his standard air force of two [Screeching Buzzard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard)s (he had 3 in his deck), and the race was on. But Harvey had his own little racer, [Mobilization](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mobilization) with six land in play.

The Buzzards kept swinging in the air, and Bruneau started playing creatures—including [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith)—for the sole reason of chump-blocking the 4/7 [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage). Eugene kept making [Soldier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soldier) tokens, and eventually he won the game with a [Tribal Unity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Unity), turning all the [Soldier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soldier)s into 4/4's.

**Final Result:** Harvey 2 – Bruneau 1

Eugene is now the sole undefeated player.

After the match, Bruneau lamented mulliganing his second hand in game 1. He said it contained a swamp, a [Barren Moor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barren+Moor), [Rorix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rorix), and a [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). Had he drawn a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) early, the [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) would have given Eugene trouble. As it was, the five-card hand was too much of a handicap for his deck to overcome.
