Fishless Fish
=============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 11, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











If you were to ask casual Legacy players what defines the format, I am sure you would get a diverse set of answers, but most of them would involve the epic feel of the format. They would chant about the turn-one-win decks and tell stories of the deck that never had to play a land but won anyway. They would tell of the forty-three-land deck and the deck that puts a 15/15 into play on turn two. There are a lot of crazy things in the format, and you would hear about them. But what you would likely never hear about is a deck that just wins with 1/1s...

![Jace's Phantasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace%27s+Phantasm)While it might not be as exciting as a turn-one win, the Mono-Blue Tempo deck piloted by Brandon Semerau at a recent StarCityGames Open in Minneapolis had what it needed to win. The deck looks to eke out small advantages through one-for-ones that net it a mana advantage or in the form of two-for-ones with the slightest of edges, like [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite).

As the deck counters your plays and deploys aggression, its mana denial aspect turns into full swing. Cards like [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) force you to play around them while cards like [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) and [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) eat the available mana your opponent has available each turn. This allows you to generate card advantage through [Ninja of the Deep Hours](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours) and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). One cool addition to the deck is [Jace's Phantasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Phantasm), which, in a field full of fetchlands and [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s, gets pretty big pretty fast!
