Round 1: Feature Match – Andre Müller vs. Denis Sinner
------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 

Both these players are no strangers to the limelight. Andre Müller made it to the Top 8 of two Pro Tours, once in Philadelphia in 2005 and in Valencia in 2007, while Denis Sinner got his sole PT Top 8 in Berlin in 2008. Both took some time away from the PT, but are currently working on their comeback and have qualified for the upcoming PT Philly.


For this event, Müller chose blue-black control, whereas Sinner brought [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)-[Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sword%2Bof%2BFeast%2Band%2BFamine).


 


### Game One


Müller took a mulligan. "Huh. I had the exact same cards," Müller said. "Only one more. Guess, I'll take another mulligan."


As he shuffled, he mused: "You're pretty far ahead then already."


Sinner offered a shrug and muttered something along the lines of "Kind of."


"Nah, I heard, in the end, the best player always wins. There really is no luck in **Magic**," said Müller and stuck out his tongue. He kept his five cards.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat11/fm1_Sinner.jpg)*Denis Sinner*
Sinner started fast, with [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) and another [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) followed by [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine). Müller bought some time with [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), but quickly succumbed to the black-protected masses.


"Yeah, I heard that's how you win this match-up: double-mulligan for your opponent. double [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) for you, plus [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine)." Quite the beating indeed.


**Andre Müller 0 – 1 Denis Sinner**


 


### Game Two


Müller had [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) for Sinner's [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), leaving him with [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) and [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) in hand. Sinner made his [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) and reached for his library to resolve the spell. "That's right. When I have [Mental Misstep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep), you will instantly know it, because I'll sit on the edge of my chair waiting to stop you from resolving your spell," Müller joked.


Müller's [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) was stopped by another [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), Sinner's [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) met the same fate. Next, Sinner resolved [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura), but Müller didn't even have to resolve his two [Creeping Tar Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit)s. The lands attacked twice and took [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Gideon%2BJura) down. Since this had worked so well, Müller shrugged and simply continued in this line of play. His Tar Pits smashed in for 6, 6, 6 ... and the rest.


**Andre Müller 1 – 1 Denis Sinner**


 


### Game Three


The action started on turn four, when Sinner tried for [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) which, much to Müller's chagrin, resolved and searched up two more.


The [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk) attacked and was joined by [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) after the players had traded [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s to stop and save the [Crusade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade)r, respectively.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat11/fm1_Mueller.jpg)*Andre Müller*
Müller cast [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), seeing three more [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s as well as two copies of [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura). "But no more land, huh?" Müller said as he used his Tectomnic Edge to keep Sinner off five mana.


Still, [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) and two [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s put Müller on the clock. On 8 life, Müller used a kicked [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) on [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) during Sinner's end step. Three [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s brought Müller down to 5. During this turn's end step, Müller cast [Consume the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+the+Meek), but Sinner had the [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak).


Müller summoned [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), passed the turn, and fell to 3 life, while Sinner re-cast his [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader). "Best top deck ever, now please?" Müller knocked on the top of his deck. [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) or [Consume the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+the+Meek) were just about the only cards which could help him out of this particular pickle. He did't draw either and extended his hand in concession


**Andre Müller 1 – 2 Denis Sinner**





Round 2: Feature Match – Merle Barkowski vs. Thomas Steeger
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken* 

It is a rare sight to see a female presence on the games floor at German Nationals. Typically, the judges staff includes more women than the players list. However, there have been women who hold their own during Nationals, most notably Claudia Loroff, who put the average record of female Nationals players at 29th with her two appearances – which is notably better than the average finish for male competitors (obviously).


While Thomas Steeger had qualified for Nationals via the Nationals Qualifier tournament in Hamburg, Merle had to wait until yesterday to punch her ticket to Nationals, drafting her way to victory with *M12* in one of the Last Chance Qualifiers yesterday. Thomas played the popular U/B Control deck, Merle brought Vampires.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat11/fm2_mylittleponies.JPG) *Thomas Steeger came prepared: These were his 2/2 Zombie tokens.*
 


### Game One


Merle won the die roll and led off with a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), soon to be followed by three more and [Gatekeeper of Malakir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir). While she had decided to go aggressive with Vampires, Thomas had little to offer in terms of resistance – until he used [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to counter [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe).


[Despise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Despise) from Merle saw [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) and [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) plus four lands in Thomas' hand, and she knew that the way was clear for a second [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe). The accompanying token fell victim to [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil), but with Thomas on 12, he wouldn't have long to live.


Merle equipped [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe) to [Gatekeeper of Malakir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir) and drew out Thomas' [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) with it. She played [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) in a move to seal game 1, but Thomas had [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) of his own! [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe)'s +9/+9 bonus, equipped to one of the Zombie tokens, gave Merle the advantage in the war of the Titans. And for a moment, it looked as if that would be enough: Thomas had to block the attacking Titan and the huge Zombie with his own Zombies, leaving his own [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) open to [Gatekeeper of Malakir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir) with kicker.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat11/fm2_Thomas_Steeger.jpg)*Close your eyes and hope? For Thomas Steeger, planeswalkers came to the rescue.*
But on an empty board on his side, Thomas clawed back with [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) and [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated). The planeswalker exiled [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe), and a second [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith) killed the [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) for good. Thomas followed that up with a second planeswalker: [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess). Though at merely 5 life, Thomas had turned this game around on the back of two very powerful 'walkers. [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Liliana%2BVess) quickly gave Thomas a [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) and Merle's life total dropped steeply to below zero.


**Merle Barkowski 0 – 1 Thomas Steeger**


 


### Game Two


Thomas had the early action with [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), seeing [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat), [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), two [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk), [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) and two [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp). He chose the [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Inquisition%2Bof%2BKozilek) for Merle to discard.


Merle's pair of [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk)s whittled Thomas down to 12 before he dispatched them with [Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith). [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe) came from Merle the turn after, Thomas answered with [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), but Merle chose to attack Thomas with her 7/7 token. Thomas staved that off with [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat), but Merle had a second [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe)!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat11/fm2_Merle_Barkowski.jpg)*Merle Barkowski had won her way into Nationals via M12 draft. Standard did not favor her.*
But once again, the token from that fell victim to [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) and Merle was left with two [Lashwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lashwrithe)s and nothing to attach them to. [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) from Thomas showed him [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) and [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) – both nothing to fear for his two Planeswalkers.


Jace kept drawing cards to his death, giving Thomas [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) and Merle yet again nothing to work with. Thomas also held [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to stop any imminent threat. Reloading with [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) gave Merle nothing. Thomas' attack took her to 2 life, and her next card held nothing to save her.


**Merle Barkowski 0 – 2 Thomas Steeger**





Round 3: Feature Match – Klaus Jöns vs. Jörg Unfried
----------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken* 

Klaus Jöns and former Nationals champion Jörg Unfried are well known in Germany, have been testing together and would be expected to post a good result. So far, neither of the two did, though. "I keep drawing worse than in testing", Unfried complained as the players bantered before the match. "We've played this match a hundred times!" said Jöns. "Today is not my day. If I lose against Jörg, I'll have to drop."


Jöns had brought [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) to the table. Unfried had decided on Mono-Red [Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin)s. Both players came to the feature match area with an 0-2 record, already on the brink of elimination from Top 8 contention.


 


### Game One


Jöns kicked off with two mulligans, neither wanting to keep a hand with double Titans nor a hand without lands. His five cards he kept. Unfried had the first play, though, with [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) giving Jöns a land – and another one, and another one. Unfried kept killing Jöns' mana walls with direct damage, [Arc Trail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail) and [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze), and plinked away at his life total to take him to 10.


After Jöns played [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) to kill the [Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin), [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) gave Unfried 4 hasty damage and took Jöns to 6. [Stormblood Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormblood+Berserker) and [Immolating Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Souleater) completed Unfried's side of the board. Jöns had [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) to search out [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle), but even three damage wouldn't save him.


After the game, Jöns mulled over if he should have used his [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) earlier to kill the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide). He concluded that after a mulligan to five, he needed the extra chance for lands. Overall, his deck just had not delivered fast enough – thinking back, Jöns saw no out he could have found to win this game.


Klaus Jöns 0 – 1 Jörg Unfried


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat11/fm3_Joerg_Unfried.jpg)*Jörg Unfried*
 


### Game Two


Jöns decided to play first again, but once more, Unfried had [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) from the start. "Can you start just one game without [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)?", Jöns complained. Unfried smiled. "No", he said, "especially since you are so annoyed about this."


This time, though, Jöns had no reason to hope for land draws off the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) and kept the 2/2 at bay with [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet). [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) helped him find more land, and [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) killed the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) and an [Immolating Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Souleater) Unfried had just played.


[Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) from Unfried continued to beat down, taking Jöns to 13. Manic Vandal from Unfried took away Jöns [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) and sent him raging: "I am playing just two [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet)s! You only boarded those in because you knew I'd play those [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Tumble%2BMagnet)! I'll never test with you again." But things looked up für Jöns, as he drew [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), bringing his [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) count to four and his [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) count to three.


One attack from the Titan would allow him to search for the last [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) and outright kill Unfried – who had [Act of Treason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Treason) to draw out his loss, but that wasn't enough to turn the game around at the last minute.


Klaus Jöns 1 – 1 Jörg Unfried

### Game Three


"So, Jörg, what do you say to this: No [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) for you, no [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) for me?" Unfried did not agree to that, while Jöns took his third and fourth mulligan of the match: "Double [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle), double Titan, there's no way you can keep that."


And once more, Unfried opened with [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), following up with Sparkshot Elder, only to have both fall to [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) from Jöns. [Immolating Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Souleater) came down, as did Tumble Magnet on Jöns' side of the board. Unfried had gone to 18 from his fetchlands, so Jöns had to comment: "The magical 18 has been reached! You are already dead!"


Of course, Unfried wasn't actually dead yet. [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) dealt four damage to Jöns who decided to use his [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) on [Immolating Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Souleater) instead of the Hero. Looking at his cards in hand, Jöns figured out a plan. [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and [Terramorphic Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) enabled him to get exactly to Titan mana. He played [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) with a second one in his hand. But he was on a mere 8 life, and Unfried had [Act of Aggression](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Act+of+Aggression) out of the sideboard to steal Jöns' Titan. The single [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) could not hold off Jöns' own Titan, [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) and two [Immolating Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Souleater)s, and Unfried took the third game for his first win of the day.


Again, Jöns mulled over his play choices. He debated the wisdom of his early [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), but saw clearly that "I had no other play". Starting on five cards had severely hampered the speed of his deck. Jöns wasn't happy with that, but had to accept it nonetheless and took his third loss of the day. He didn't drop from the tournament, though – testimony of his competitive spirit. And **Magic** is fun,


**Klaus Jöns 1 – 2 Jörg Unfried**





Round 4: Feature Match – Kai Budde vs. Helge Nelson
---------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 

Kai Budde really needs no introduction. He basically won every type of tournament in existence, most of them a couple of times. And he came well prepared and in full juggernaut mode into this tournament. He was still undefeated, coming into this round.


His opponent, Helge Nelson, on the other hand didn't even know he would play this tournament yesterday. He won a Last-Chance Qualifier and proceeded to win his first three matches as well.


 


### Game One


Nelson won the die-roll, but had no one-drop, while Budde led with [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) off a [Copperline Gorge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Copperline+Gorge). Nelson had [Kargan Dragonlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kargan+Dragonlord) on turn two, Budde had [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), then played [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest), [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), and passed the turn.

Friday, 5:14 p.m. – Drafting with Florian Koch
----------------------------------------------


*by Thorben Thies and Hanno Terbuyken* 

Florian Koch was one of the twelve players who sat undefeated on top of the standings after four rounds of Standard.



![Flameblast Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flameblast+Dragon)
 



The winner of last year's GP Lyon stands poised to do well here, but champions are not made on constructed decks alone. In an era of Pro Tours split between Limited and Constructed, owning the draft format is an important skill. How did Florian fare in draft, then?


Opening his first pack, a pleasant surprise stared Florian in the face: [Flameblast Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flameblast+Dragon). [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and [Rampant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) couldn't come close to this solid first pick. The second pack held no red cards for him, though, so Florian agonized over [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll) and [Griffin Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel). He likes red-white more than red-green in *M12* draft, but this early in the draft, Florian decided to just take the better card – [Cudgel Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cudgel+Troll). Next he took [Blood Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Ogre) over [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), but Florian saw a fourth booster with [Stormfront Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Pegasus) and [Goblin Arsonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Arsonist) and decided that white would be open enough to consider taking his preferred color combination. He picked the Pegasus.



![Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Auramancer)

The next picks cemented his decision when Florian took [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard) instead of [Goblin Piker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piker) or [Fiery Hellhound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Hellhound), then [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer), [Benalish Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benalish+Veteran) and [Alabaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alabaster+Mage). Other than a [Bonebreaker Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonebreaker+Giant) as eleventh pick no more red came through for Florian.


But of course, that [Flameblast Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flameblast+Dragon) wanted to be played. So when Florian saw [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock), [Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin) and [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) in his second booster, he picked the [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock). Red looked okay in this round of pickings, but White did not flow. Small wonder, since he let [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) through and someone surely scooped that up. Florian's picks went accordingly: [Gorehorn Minotaurs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorehorn+Minotaurs) second, then [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer) over [Goblin Bangchuckers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Goblin%2BBangchuckers), as Florian felt that the 2/2 body of the [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer) would be playable even without targets.


[Divine Favor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Favor) was the only playable card available as fourth pick, and pick five gave him late [Gorehorn Minotaurs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorehorn+Minotaurs). [Manic Vandal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal), [Elite Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Vanguard) and [Kite Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kite+Shield) complemented his deck as picks six to eight. The last six picks gave Florian nothing of notable value.



![Stormblood Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stormblood+Berserker)

In the third booster, Florian opened a [Frost Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan), but passed it on in favor of [Armored Warhorse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Warhorse). In this Swiss-style draft, he wouldn't necessarily have to face the [Frost Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Frost%2BTitan), Florian explained, so hate-drafting it would have essentially wasted a pick. His second pick also proved contentious, as Florian took [Stormblood Berserker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormblood+Berserker) over [Incinerate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) and [Chandra's Outrage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Outrage). The GP champion had good reason, he said, as good Bloodthirst creatures are more important than Removal in a draft format this fast. [Gideon's Lawkeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%27s+Lawkeeper) joined his pile as third pick over [Blood Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Ogre).


The rest of the booster went by in a blur, giving Florian (in that order) [Crimson Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Mage), [Slaughter Cry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Cry), [Goblin War Paint](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+War+Paint), [Tectonic Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Rift), [Demystify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demystify) instead of a second [Goblin War Paint](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+War+Paint) and [Goblin Tunneler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Tunneler).


With the draft done, Florian was content with the result. [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer), [Divine Favor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Favor) and [Goblin War Paint](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+War+Paint) all made his final deck, not out of desperation, but as playable picks.

Well, not quite that helpless, as Gräfensteiner fiddled with [Mighty Leap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mighty+Leap) and [Fling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling) shenanigans to deal five damage to Riffert and dispatching the Dragon via [Fling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling). He even managed to stop another attack via blocking and [Chandra's Outrage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Outrage). But with a mere one life left, Gräfensteiner had not enough wiggle room to claw his way out of the hole, and extended the hand.


**Till Riffert 2 – 1 Daniel Gräfensteiner**
