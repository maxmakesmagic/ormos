Rescuing Overlords
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 28, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 







On April 18, **Magic Online** hosted an R&D Challenge where everyone was invited to play against members of Wizards R&D... and win their packs! **Magic Online** user Better\_then\_u took home the lion's share of the prizes, as the lone player to go 5–0 outside of **Magic** R&D. 


![Rescue from the Underworld](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rescue+from+the+Underworld)
 Better\_then\_u's deck of choice was one of the strongest decks in *Theros* Block Constructed, Junk Reanimator. While black, green, and white decks with reanimation elements were popular during last year's Standard season, due to [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) and [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), the *Theros* Block deck uses [Commune with the Gods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+the+Gods) and [Satyr Wayfinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Satyr+Wayfinder) to fill the graveyard and [Whip of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) and [Rescue from the Underworld](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rescue+from+the+Underworld) to bring back large creatures. The large creatures of choice for this *Theros* Block deck are [Ashen Rider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashen+Rider) and [Abhorrent Overlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abhorrent+Overlord), both of which have enters-the-battlefield triggers to take advantage of [Whip of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos). 


 Much like its Standard predecessor, this deck can play a normal midrange game if it can't get the reanimation pieces together. [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion) remains one of the most powerful cards in the format, while [Courser of Kruphix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix) helps you hit enough land drops to be able to cast your large, expensive creatures. Once you put it all together, you have a deck that is both powerful and consistent. 


 Find all of the [decklists from the event, here](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Digital/MagicOnlineTourn.aspx?x=mtg/digital/magiconline/tourn/7008791).
