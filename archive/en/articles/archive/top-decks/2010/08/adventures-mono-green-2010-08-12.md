Adventures with Mono-Green
==========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on August 12, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






I have been practicing ***Magic** 2011* Standard like crazy on **Magic Online**.

What did I ever do before **Magic Online**?

One match I got demolished by a Mono-Green Ramp deck. The first game was very competitive, but I made an error with my [Realm Razer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Realm+Razer) during a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) standoff (I was playing Naya [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) if you hadn't figured it out already) and my opponent was able to, you know, search up *and cast* [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) all in the same turn. The second game was less exciting ... for me, that is. He or she hit two pieces of acceleration—a first turn [Joraga Treespeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker) into a second turn [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement)—and that set up, you know, a third [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) via [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap).

Needless to say, I was fuming.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_trap.jpg)  
*Of course*, I had had the same idea too. I love [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple)s like almost no other mage, but it still sucked to be on the receiving end of the A+ number-one-super-combo. 

Apparently Pol Urós had had the same idea, too. He finished sixth at last weekend's Spain National Championship!

We will talk about the other seven decks of course, but this week I decided to take the Urós deck for a spin. I played eight two-man queues with Pol's deck ... it's not the same experience as a "real" Pro Tour Qualifier or Premiere Event, but I still love playing the two-man queues. You get tournament experience, your opponents generally have "real" decks, and everyone is trying their hardest to win.

I originally set aside time for seven matches, but ended up running eight (more on that below). Let's see how the Urós deck ran!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match1Header.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_emrakul.jpg)I got super lucky to win the first game. Super lucky insofar as my opponent didn't kill me (I have played a lot of Ascension, and I like playing against [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s with that strategy for the most part). 

He or she did play a [Khalni Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden) in the first game, so I assumed it was a [Polymorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph) transformational sideboard. Regardless, I sided in my enchantment-kill package on the theory that I didn't have a lot of ways to interact with my opponent if he or she were running [Polymorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph), but if it didn't transform, I would be able to slam-bam quite efficiently.

Game 2 was super depressing. Though he or she got a quick [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn), I felt like I should be able to compete; my opponent had only 5 life and I had a Baloth on the battlefield. Almost anything off a [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) would be good enough to pull it out ... I got a, well, *nothing.* I whiffed.

Game 3 my opponent "randomly" cast [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) on one of my two [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement)s. I was pretty sure I knew why. Yep. Naturally, he or she drew [Khalni Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden) (turn one) and [Polymorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph) on turn four for [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn). I had a [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) that would have gotten me an [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) of my own to deuce, but the [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) did its work.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_0_1.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match2Header.jpg)  
Basically I just got blown out.

My opponent had the super-quick Allies draws; I had the chance to [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) one of the games, but a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) kept me from, you know, thinking too much.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_0_2.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match3Header.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_baneslayer.jpg)This deck had some Mythic elements, but seemed more like the [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) decks of the past. He had [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) as well.

Game 1 was not competitive, but Game 2 I felt like I had a decent draw ... two lands, an [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust), two [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate)s, two [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap)s. I discarded twice before casting [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate), and then died. This was really a shame, as he or she was completely committed to the board and I had the mana to cast [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) the next turn.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_0_3.jpg)  
Zero-three? What the!?!

This was not going the way I wanted at all! I had never had so poor a showing to start in the one-on-one queues. I had to win the next four in a row in order to finish better than 50% ... and I am actually usually pretty happy when I can go four in a row with my favorite decks.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match4Header.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_pyromancer.jpg)  
Game 1 my opponent played a [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) and passed. The next turn he or she cast [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) in order to avoid discarding. This game my opponent cast all four [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s before scooping to my Baloth.

The second game was even less competitive than the one where my opponent never played a second land, if you can believe it. I correctly put him or her on [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) (justifying my anti-enchantment sideboarding), and luckily my opponent went first, playing a [Halimar Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths).

That's right: I said it was lucky he or she went first!

I cast a turn two [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement). My opponent thought a moment and ran out [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). I countered with [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) for [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Infinite+Gyre).

Finally got one! I don't care *how* I got one, I got one (don't pretend like you don't know what I am talking about).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_1_3.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match5Header.jpg)  
The first game I milked his or her board development with Walls and Baloths. He or she naturally drew the [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage) and had the [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) to set up the Stoneforge-Mystic-into-Basilisk-Collar combination. I just wanted to hit land drops and play out my creatures to buy myself some time. Then ...

All. Is. Dust!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_sparkmage.jpg)My opponent came back with a top-decked [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage), which took out something awesome (I don't remember what it was, but it cost six or more mana).

I had to cast [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) for the lonely pinger before cleaning up with one of the insane big creatures that this deck plays.

In the second game I had an interesting draw. I figured I would win by using [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) after an over-commitment. I drew [Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth) and decided to just try to race my opponent's [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine). He or she had two mana-creatures and cast [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs).

I often wonder what percentage of the time a Naya deck wins on [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs). I had two [Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth)s on the battlefield (i.e. 8 free life) and quickly put my opponent into a position where he or she could no longer attack. Even if I had to kill [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs), I had [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust), [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin), [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple), and [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement) on the 'field; meaning I wasn't going to take a lot of damage if I did have to kill it.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_2_3.jpg)  
Coming back! Two to go!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match6Header.jpg)  
In the first game I "stalled" on [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin). It was a weird stall for most decks ... I had a bunch of lands in play but I couldn't do anything because my hand was so expensive. If it had been another land, particularly an [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple), I feel like I would have won. I would have had the jump in the [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) fight. Instead my opponent had a Titan, ramped to multiple [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle)s, and did me in. 

The second game I got fourth-turn [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn).

The third game was the insane one. This was my opponent's graveyard at the end of Game 3:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_graveyard.jpg)  
I thought I had the third one free and clear with my acceleration, trumping the opposing [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander), but my opponent flipped [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) into [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) and immediately played [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds), generating a ton of 2/3 creatures. 

I had to do very pressure-filled math with a pair of [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement)s and a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) on the 'field. I was probably dead if he or she just played a land. I thought long and hard about if I should attack or not. 

I had access to a ton of mana ... Should I tap my Battlements to cast a [Terastodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) defensively? Though I had a lot of mana, to cast [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) I would have to tap a Battlement. Should I cast it, wait for combat, or what?

It turns out my opponent didn't have a pre-combat land. I had to block very precisely so as to stay out of [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander) range. Certainly my Titan rumbled with his or her Avenger.

I activated [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) at the end of his or her turn; [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander) shot down my wounded Titan, but I got [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn). I think he or she might have had it if a Wall had been shot instead. There was no way to know my only land in hand (which I would have needed if he or she took down three mana with a Battlement) was [Khalni Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden).

The amazing thing is that while I had a hard-cast [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn), I still hadn't won! [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk), annihilator 6—putting the opponent *next to* dead wasn't the same as "dead." I still cast my [Terastodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) defensively, essentially producing four blockers.

*whew.*

Even.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match7Header.jpg)  
I had what I felt like was a powerful opening hand with tons of acceleration but only one action spell. As it was, I had a fourth turn [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap). I did not whiff ...

I got an [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement).

Second game I got a quick annihilator from [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap).

In the third I was just down one piece of acceleration. The terrible thing about a deck like this is that often you will have a hand that looks explosive, has some stuff to do, but ultimately generates no action. I think I had three [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) at the end of this game, a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), and an [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) on the battlefield ... couldn't cast any of it and I got gobbled up by my opponents one action spell (a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_valakutAndMountains.jpg)  
Having only played the match-up twice I am not sure who is favored between Red-Green [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) Ramp and straight Green Eldrazi Ramp, but I can tell you the Red-Green deck has the more lethal Titan. Red-Green's Titan will just kill the other deck's Titan with one attack (either one or two [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle)s in place, searching up two [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s for as much as 12 damage), if not the opposing player.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_3_4.jpg)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_match8Header.jpg)  
The first game I held out with [Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth) in my hand, but died to sheer inconvenience. I had [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) for my opponents three [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) + [Liliana's Caress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Caress) board; he or she cast [Burning Inquiry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Inquiry) and managed to take out my [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) but leave me with the Baloth in hand; I took, like, 100.

The next two games were much simpler. I had a super cheap hand in the second and just hard cast two Baloths; my opponent couldn't really make me discard.

In the last game I had some enchantment removal and great acceleration. I hit [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) into [Pelakka Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pelakka+Wurm) into the Butcher of Truth, always leaving mana open to drill an enchantment if need be.

Winner!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_4_4.jpg)  
At the end of three or four hours of game play, I was up two ratings points, and had a fun run to share with you; just sorry it wasn't a more triumphant story.

This deck has lower performance than I would typically like in tournament queue testing. It has the problems most ramp decks have—you can draw all your acceleration and miss a [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap), or you can "only" draw one piece of acceleration and fail to cast a significant spell. The deck really needs two pieces of acceleration to function properly, and therefore is liable to "draw its cards in the wrong order" more than, say, Red Deck Wins. That said, you really can't argue with second-turn [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn), and there aren't a lot of other decks with that kind of blistering potential.

How about the rest of the Top 8?

Spain's winner played a toolbox Naya deck. All told, three noncreature spells—two copies of the archetype's favorite Planeswalker, and then just a [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) to go with [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) and [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage), all laced together with [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman)s.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td103_3Cards1.jpg)  
Bullets included:


> * [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel): Just a great card, but one of the best against other Naya decks, as well as other [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s
> * [Obstinate Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth): Jund, just a good card against beatdown
> * [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage): Anything with enchantments and artifacts, generally strong against combo
> * [Realm Razer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Realm+Razer): It's like being able to [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor) for [Armageddon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon)!
> * [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan): Battling cleanup, the Titan can generate all different sorts of value; what about getting back [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) multiple times?
>

| Dauntless Escort | Sun Titan |

Carbonero did not play [Dauntless Escort](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Escort) main, but he did have it in his sideboard. This card, in combination with [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), allows the deck to essentially ignore [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) effects (even more than "just" having [Dauntless Escort](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Escort)), *plus* attack into whatever it likes every turn (you can sacrifice and continually re-buy the Escort with [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)).

*don't* play [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), or necessarily [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). Another Top 8, and another finish by White-Blue Control. This one was built around [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), with lots of individual decisions, like [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), the many [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge)s, or even the solo [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) all essentially for that [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)'s buy-back potential.

Just one thing Brian David-Marshall pointed out to me, in general (for when you are playing against the updated White-Blue decks): They tend not to have a full supply of the cards creature-decks hate. Two [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) and two [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) are increasingly the stock includes. No different here.

Seven-and-a-half different decks across Spain's Top 8? M11 looks to be continuing one of the most diverse Standard formats in recent memory. Good luck, whether your field of battle is the next National Championship, or "just" a **Magic Online** one-on-one!
