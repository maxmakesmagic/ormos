Deck Tech: Izzet Ulamog with Matthew Hunt
=========================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX MANCHESTER 2016](/en/events/coverage/gpman16)
 on May 29, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











I had seen decks with [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) and [Confirm Suspicions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confirm+Suspicions) before in recent weeks, but when I saw them, I wasn't yet convinced that such a deck would be competitive enough. After seeing Matthew Hunt crush his opponents in Manchester with these cards, I have changed my mind.


The Player
==========


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gpman16/GPMan16_MatthewHunt.jpg)  
*Matthew Hunt*


Matthew Hunt is a 27-year old engineer from the UK. He currently lives just outside Manchester, so this Grand Prix could be considered to be on his home turf.


His previous Magic accomplishments include a qualification for Pro Tour *Dragons of Tarkir* in Brussels, at the very last PTQ before the introduction of the PPTQ system, and he is hoping to qualify for another Pro Tour this weekend, this time for *Eldritch Moon* in Sydney. He was 12-2 at the time of writing, so one more match win would get him there.


"This is actually the first time I've made Day 2 at a Grand Prix!" he excitedly told me. "And I think I have a good deck for the tournament too."

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mage-Ring+Network)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage-Ring+Network)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hedron+Archive)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive)
According to Hunt, the game plan of the deck is to use [Mage-Ring Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage-Ring+Network) and [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) to ramp you out a little bit into [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) and eventually [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger). The rest of the cards are counterspells, instant-speed removal spells, and instant-speed draw spells.


"[Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) is the staple of the deck", Hunt said. "You ramp a bit early and sacrifice it later on to draw some cards."


But the unsung hero of the deck is [Mage-Ring Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage-Ring+Network). "The thing you want to be doing is turn-one tapland, turn-two [Mage-Ring Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage-Ring+Network), and then you are set for the rest of the game," Hunt told me. "You pass and can play [Clash of Wills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clash+of+Wills) or [Spatial Contortion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spatial+Contortion) if your opponent does something worth answering. If not, you just put a counter on [Mage-Ring Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mage-Ring+Network). I've had games before where there were 6 counters on it and I was ramping every turn."


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drowner+of+Hope)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger)
The win conditions in the deck are colorless Eldrazi. "[Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) is a big 5/5, blocks everything, and doesn't die to [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) and [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness). Ideally, you go turn-four [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive), turn-5 [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) with [Clash of Wills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clash+of+Wills) mana open, and then turn 6 Ulamog to end the game," Hunt said.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Clash+of+Wills)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clash+of+Wills)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Void+Shatter)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void+Shatter)
The deck might look like a Red-Green Ramp deck--it even has [Chandra, Flamecaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Flamecaller) in there as a sweeper--but the big difference is the blue cards, in particular the counterspells. "[Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s are very good at the moment; people are playing expensive planeswalkers or even nine-mana sorceries on their turn," Hunt said. All in all, the deck plays more like a control deck than a ramp deck, except with Eldrazi instead of [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai) to win the game.


Hunt didn't create the deck himself: "Sam Rolph told me about the blue Ulamog deck. Adrian Sullivan played and wrote about it, and I saw similar blue-red decks online. I kept the same core, but I made a few small changes."


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brutal+Expulsion)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brutal+Expulsion)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Epiphany+at+the+Drownyard)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Epiphany+at+the+Drownyard)
"I like the singleton [Brutal Expulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brutal+Expulsion). It's a fantastic card at times, especially when you need a tempo spell to buy a turn to get to Ulamog. You can bounce a creature and kill another, for example. Typically, it acts as another counterspell or removal spell, but in rare cases, you could bounce your own Ulamog or Drowner. It's quite a versatile card."


"Another random card I added was [Epiphany at the Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Epiphany+at+the+Drownyard). I totally forgot about it until I watched Grand Prix Los Angeles the previous weekend, where I saw a Mono Blue Tron deck cast Epiphany for a lot. I thought it would be good in my deck too. When you have two [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) in play, you could get 10 cards at instant speed." It's certainly a sweet spell for the deck—a bit worse than [Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate) early on, but it scales well later in the game.


Positioning, Matchups, and Sideboarding
=======================================


"I like the deck against many of the midrange decks which are not doing much on turn two," Hunt explained. "A lot of them may run [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate), but they don't always have it. And if they don't put much pressure on you, then you can keep up counterspells until you win with Drowner and Ulamog." Against creature-light midrange deck, Hunt could improve further after sideboard by cutting [Spatial Contortion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spatial+Contortion) and [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) for more impactful cards.


"Bant Humans are a bad matchup," he admitted. "Their creatures get out of the range of your removal spells early on due to [Thalia's Lieutenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%27s+Lieutenant). [Lambholt Pacifist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lambholt+Pacifist) is already out of [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) range, and once it becomes a 4/4, you can't use [Spatial Contortion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spatial+Contortion) either. You never get much time against them, and they often play [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) in their own turn when you don't have counter mana up." Fortunately, Hunt found a nice sideboard innovation in [Ruin Processor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruin+Processor). "Against Human decks, it gives me another cheap way to trigger [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return)."


Tips and Tricks
===============


One piece of advice that Hunt shared was you should always keep [Spawning Bed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Bed) in mind when planning out your turns. "It helps ramp into an Ulamog that they might not see coming," he gave as an example.


"And I also played a game here at the Grand Prix where I my opponent was at 21 life with two blockers. I had two [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope), one Scion token, [Chandra, Flamecaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Flamecaller), [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive), [Wandering Fumarole](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Fumarole), [Spawning Bed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Bed), and seven other lands. I had lethal with that!" It's a bit of a puzzle, but [Spawning Bed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Bed) offered both the Scions to sacrifice for [Drowner of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Hope) and the mana to activate [Wandering Fumarole](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Fumarole). Sweet!
