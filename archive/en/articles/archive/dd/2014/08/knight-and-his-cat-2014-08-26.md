A Knight and His Cat
====================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on August 26, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






In 2009, Brian Kibler won [Pro Tour Austin](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptaus09/welcome) with a midrange Naya deck fueled by some of the most efficient creatures ever backed by some of the most efficient removal spells ever. Fast forward five years later, change the format from Extended to Modern, and those same creatures have found success once again.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Knight%20of%20the%20Reliquary)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight%20of%20the%20Reliquary)
Adrian Throop won a Pro Tour Qualifier at [Giga Bytes in Marietta, Georgia](http://www.giga-bitescafe.com/index.php), with a deck that draws much of its inspiration from Brian's Pro Tour deck. Many of the core spells are the sameâ€”[Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl), [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage), [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) are present in both decks. That is quite a bit of overlap, especially for an underrepresented archetype!


There are definitely some important updates to the deck to react the current Modern metagame. [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) is great against [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) is a potent land to get with [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), and the sideboard contains many potent responses to popular Modern decks. [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) shuts down Storm decks while [Stony Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence) puts a hurt on Affinity decks. [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor) and [Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) are great against [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) strategies, while [Kor Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker) makes life very difficult for a burn deck. A pile of efficient creatures and removal spells backed by a few hate cards for nearly every archetype in Modern provided Adrian with an invitation to Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir*. Congrats Adrian!
