Evolving Reanimator
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 20, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Diverse formats like Modern and the current Standard format tend to lead to a very slow development of new decks, with constant shifts in existing decks. This is because most of the best strategies are found early, and without a clear target, it's hard to build a level-two deck (that is to say, a weaker deck that's designed to prey on a known metagame). Instead, we just see gradual evolution among a changing shift in power and prevalence of the dominant decks.

![Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Restoration+Angel) Today, I'm going to look at an interesting way to develop the Junk Reanimator deck to prepare for a metagame of aggressive red-green decks and Bant Hexproof. L1X0 recently finished 4â€“0 in a Standard Daily event with this take on Junk Rites that minimizes the conventional creature count, instead relying on [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) to accelerate, which allows [Gaze of Granite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaze+of+Granite) to answer a wide variety of potential problems, from creature rushes to opposing dedicated hate cards like [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) or the more common main-deck card, [Ground Seal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal). 

 Aside from that, the creatures are chosen to support a heavy [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) theme. L1X0's choice to main-deck [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) instead of [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) or [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) fits the metagame prediction to be ready for aggressive decks with [Gaze of Granite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaze+of+Granite). And, of course, L1X0 still has [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) and [Acidic Slime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) in the sideboard.
