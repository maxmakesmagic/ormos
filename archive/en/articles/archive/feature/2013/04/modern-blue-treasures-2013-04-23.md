Modern Blue Treasures
=====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 23, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I haven't looked at Modern much lately, but with [Grand Prix Portland](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/grandprix/portland13) coming up, it's becoming a format for me to pay attention to. For the most part, the format is pretty stable, and recent **Magic Online** results feature a lot of the decks we've become accustomed to in Modern—Infect, Birthing Pod, Affinity, Jund, and the like. 


![Trading Post](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trading+Post)
 While using [Expedition Map](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) to assemble the [Urza's Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine), [Urza's Power Plant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant), and [Urza's Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower) combination known as "Tron" may not be especially new, ContraEgo takes the engine in a slightly different direction. 


 ContraEgo uses [Treasure Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage) to find any of a wide variety of high-impact artifacts: [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel), [Spine of Ish Sah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spine+of+Ish+Sah), [Sundering Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan), [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver), and—from the sideboard—[Steel Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Hellkite) and [Contagion Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine). These are backed by a heavy dose of counterspells, acceleration, and some removal, mostly in the form of [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal). 



[Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) offers this deck its card draw, as with most blue Tron decks, but what we don't see is a secondary card advantage instant like [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven). Instead, ContraEgo takes card drawing more incrementally with cards like [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) smoothing the heavy lifting done by [Thirst for Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge). 


 The card I'm most excited to see is the exceptionally fun [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post), which is at its best with cards like [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), but which also allows [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) to get to work stealing multiple turns before the [Academy Ruins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins) engine is fully online to allow ContraEgo to control every action taken in the rest of the game.
