Running the Maze
================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 16, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive **Magic Online** column. [Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/protour/dragonsmaze13) starts tomorrow. Be sure to follow along with the live coverage here on [DailyMTG.com](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx?x=mtgevent/webcast/home) or [Twitch.tv](http://www.twitch.tv/magicprotour). Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* will pit the best players in the world against each other in matches of *Return to Ravnica* Block Constructed and DGR drafts. [Last week](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/pummeling-prerelease-2013-05-09), I examined the new Limited format and talked about some of the archetypes I've had success with thus far. This week, I'm going to take a look at the new Block Constructed format in an effort to better understand what's about to unfold tomorrow on the biggest stage in competitive **Magic**.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr247_dgmprotour.jpg)The first results of post-*Dragon's Maze* *Return to Ravnica* Block Constructed are in! We finally have some groundwork for the upcoming PT that should help us set the stage for all the action. The best players in the world will be using these decklists to establish a metagame. Let's get right into it with some decklists:

Golgari decks have performed well in *Return to Ravnica* Block Constructed thanks to the excellent curve and efficient removal offered by the two-color combination. *Dragon's Maze* gives the deck access to [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) and [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped), two powerful cards that fit perfectly into the archetype. The deck plays like most aggressive decks you're used to, but the scavenge cards make you much better post-[Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) than most decks of this variety. You're going to want to be extremely aggressive with this deck in every matchup that isn't Mono-Red. [Vraska the Unseen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vraska+the+Unseen) comes in against controlling decks and punishes them for tapping out for [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon)s are excellent in the mirror and against Black-Green-White decks. [Golgari Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Charm) counters [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and functions as an erase/counterspell/combat trick/board sweeper against a lot of the decks in the format.

Black-Green-White decks get access to [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm), [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa), and [Obzedat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council), so playing straight Golgari over black-green-white is certainly going to weaken the raw power level of your deck. However, the consistency and aggression that straight black-green offers seems to be well positioned given the current format. The one-drops are, however, less than stellar against [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), and the prevalence of that card might deter people from playing this archetype in large numbers. Then again, maybe people will just play more [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon)s and run over the Voices in post-boarded games as long as the opponent doesn't find [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm)s.

Mono-Red is showing up in huge numbers in the latest Daily Event results, but we probably shouldn't be too quick to assume that it's going to be a major player at the Pro Tour. The deck picked up [Weapon Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weapon+Surge) and [Rubblebelt Maaka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rubblebelt+Maaka) with *Dragon's Maze*, which certainly improve its combat suite a great deal. The deck used to have trouble breaking through 3/3s or 4/4s, but these two cards go a long way in ensuring you'll be able to attack with your team every turn. [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) is very strong against the Red deck and, in conjunction with [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) and [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm), decks playing green and white may pose some big problems for the Red deck. The deck wants to overrun its opponents before they can establish any kind of board presence. We'll have to wait and see how many [Druid's Deliverance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid%27s+Deliverance)s and [Riot Control](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riot+Control)s people are playing tomorrow before we get a handle on how relevant the Red deck will be in the later rounds of Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*.

Esper decks are still a huge force to be reckoned with. [Notion Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Thief) didn't seem to pick up much play in the first post-*Dragon's Maze* *Return to Ravnica* Block Constructed event. [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) decks will have to worry about [Sire of Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+Insanity), but that card is still fringe in the Block Constructed format and the deck still has plenty of ways to deal with it at instant speed, especially because there's no [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) in the format. The deck now gets access to [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%2F%2F+Away) and [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherling). [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%2F%2F+Away) is one of the stronger removal/tempo cards in the format and [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherling) may be the best finisher. I expect this deck to show up in big numbers at Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* and I would be surprised if at least one copy didn't make the Top 8 of the event. I'm not sure how much [Sire of Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+Insanity) there will be, but I'm confident that this deck has what it takes to flourish, even in a hostile environment.

The Selesnya deck got an absurd boost of power with *Dragon's Maze*. [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm) are probably the best two cards in this deck and [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses) is going to cause nightmares for players who lean too hard on [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). This deck should completely dominate its aggro matchups, especially post-board, when it gets access to [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice). [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) also comes in from the board against aggro; punishing opposing decks for tapping out with an [Armadillo Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak) effect is usually going to be game against decks like Mono-Red. [Druid's Deliverance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid%27s+Deliverance) and [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) further push those matchups to the point where they're almost impossible to lose barring mana/mulligan issues. I'd like to see another copy of [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses) in the sideboard here for [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) strategies. I expect this deck to show up in reasonable numbers at the Pro Tour and I expect the archetype to perform reasonably well. [Wayfaring Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wayfaring+Temple) seems nice with [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm), but I'm not sure if three is too many. We'll find out tomorrow, though.

I thought a lot about which Black-Green-White deck I should discuss today. In the end, I decided to present the list that seemed to offer the most power. This deck has all the makings of a boogieman going into the PT. Look no further if you're looking for a collection of the format's most powerful cards. This list is a bit rough, seeing as it came from the first *Return to Ravnica* Block event on **Magic Online**. I expect a polished version of this deck to be the breakout deck at Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*. [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council); [Sin Collector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector); [Sire of Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+Insanity); and [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) do lots of work against the control decks, especially the Fog archetype.

The deck is a beast against more aggressive strategies: [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) continues to shine in these matchups. [Varolz, the Scar-Striped](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varolz%2C+the+Scar-Striped); [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm); [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay); [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter); [Lotleth Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll); and [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) make the deck a nightmare in the early game for creature-based decks. The instant-speed turn-four [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm) into [Scion of Vitu-Ghazi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Vitu-Ghazi) on turn five is a nail-in-the-coffin play against other black-green-based decks. I expect decks like this to drastically shape the format in the days leading up to the Pro Tour. Expect big numbers from this archetype and expect multiple copies of decks like this in the elimination rounds at Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*.

Imagine my surprise when I opened up the document with the decklist information from the first post-*Dragon's Maze* *Return to Ravnica* Block Constructed event on **Magic Online** and saw this as the first decklist. Are you as confused as I was? ehhh's deck uses lots of card draw to ensure that he can [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) his opponent into oblivion and reset the board from time to time with a [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). Eventually, he can deliver a killing blow with [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherling). The sideboard may seem strange, but the [Pithing Needle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle), [Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel), and [Psychic Spiral](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychic+Spiral)s ensure the deck has a strong game plan against the control decks while the [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm) and [Renounce the Guilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) give more outs to [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council). Well, it's looking like players are going to need to get pretty creative to beat this one.

Turbo-Fog strategies are traditionally nightmares for creature-based decks. Fortunately, the *Return to Ravnica* Block Constructed format offers some nice cards to help defend against this monstrosity. [Obzedat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council), [Notion Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Thief), and [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) are the first cards that come to mind; we can expect a lot of Voices tomorrow and that card in conjunction with [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses), [Golgari Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Charm), or [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) should be enough to punish players for attempting the Turbo-Fog plan. However, the Turbo-Fog strategy doesn't roll over to these plays, they just make the game more difficult for it. Those of you who have read my columns for a while will know that I absolutely despise Turbo-Fog decks. There's very little that's more frustrating than watching an opponent draw a bunch of cards while just casting [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog)s over and over as you try to attack for the win. What's worse is that you need to over-commit to the board against these Turbo-Fog decks to prevent them from using [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) as a super [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) that refills their hand with more [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s and [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog)s. I hope cards like [Obzedat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council), [Notion Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Thief), and [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) are enough to keep this deck in line. I dread even the thought of playing this mirror match, especially Game 1.
