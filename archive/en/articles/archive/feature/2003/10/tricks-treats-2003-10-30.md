Tricks & Treats
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 30, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_240.png)
By Mark L. Gottlieb











### Boo.


Happy Halloween, everyone! I love this holiday. It's such a refreshing change from all the other sickly sweet sapfests dotting the calendar. Valentine's Day is about *looove*. Mother's Day is about *looove*. The Christmas spirit of rampant consumerism has been diluted by *looove*. Ick. Halloween is a pure holiday celebrating the most wonderful aspects of the human soul: Taking candy from strangers. Dressing in grotesque costumes. Scaring the ooflotak out of your friends. Scavenging putrid souls for eerie rituals. Wholesome family fun!


I have to deal with a bit of competition on the site this week. Ben already wrote about the creepiest cards. Anthony already wrote about making Halloween theme decks *and* used made-up words. Today, I'll be... making Halloween theme decks using the creepiest cards. Luckily, I'm a lot more snorgwicious than those two jokers, so hang on-and don't get too freaked out.


### The Obligatory Deck


How could I let Halloween go by without making an [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) deck? Seriously, how? Help me out here! No, no, I love the card. As the first mass recursion card, it was revolutionary when it debuted. Visions of reanimated [Craw Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craw+Wurm)s and [Lord of the Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Pit)s (dumped into my graveyard via [Bazaar of Baghdad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+of+Baghdad)) danced in my head. It never happened, though, because it turns out that [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) is not on the high end of the quality spectrum. You play it. Your opponent snickers and takes a turn. You remove a time counter and spend your turn setting up for a big payoff. Your opponent guffaws, takes another turn, and [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)s your card at the last second. How can Halloween be [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)ed???


Since today is [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) - Eve, out comes the card again. Why use it over the vastly superior [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death)? Because it's so much cooler! It's from *Legends*! It's misprinted as a **"Sorcery"**! It's got a pumpkin on it, for crying out loud! [Living Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Death) is clearly the better choice, but [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) is earns you oodles of style points.


I don't want to just use it, though; I want to take advantage of it. And that's why I'm glad I got a [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) while out trick-or-treating this year. Pull a time counter off your sorc enchantment, and it starts to look a lot better. As usual, once I start using [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit), I don't want to stop at just one card. That's where the satisfyingly Halloweeny [Altar of Shadows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar+of+Shadows) and [Harbinger of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbinger+of+Night) come in. By pulling off the -1/-1 counters [Harbinger of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbinger+of+Night) puts on itself and transforming them into +1/+1 counters for another of your creatures, you've stabilized two creepy critters with a single Conduit-meaning you've allowed the Harbinger to eternally drain your opponent's creatures.



Letting an [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) go off doesn't do much without a freshly stuffed graveyard. [Buried Alive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive) is a creepily reliable way to throw some worm-infested soil onto spooky staples like [Spirit of the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Night), [Visara the Dreadful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Visara+the+Dreadful), [Necrosavant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrosavant), and [Carrionette](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrionette). [Twisted Abomination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Abomination) and [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness) will bury themselves from your hand thanks to cycling. [Mind Maggots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Maggots)-with its cringe-inducing art-lets you ditch any super-expensive creatures stuck in your hand *and* powers up the Conduit.


There's one last element to a super [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve): draining your opponent's graveyard. Who said a symmetrical effect should be symmetrical? Consider it the trick in this otherwise treat-filled deck. [Withered Wretch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch) is a no-brainer... so I left it out. It's a little too on-the-nose, and it's just not Halloweenical enough. [Entrails Feaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entrails+Feaster), on the other hand, is absolutely perfect for the holiday. It's a black cat-turned-rotting zombie! Possibly the Halloweeniest creature ever. [Eater of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eater+of+the+Dead), unsurprisingly, also fits into the deck, and [Gravestorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravestorm) gives you wicked art and gives your opponent a wicked tough choice. Now that you've filled your graveyard and emptied your opponent's, [Tombstone Stairwell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tombstone+Stairwell) is a horrifying way to reap the rewards. And with all those Zombies running around, I could not resist slipping in one of my all-time favorite gooseflesh-inducing cards: [Hecatomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hecatomb). A deck that tries to make [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) and [Hecatomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hecatomb) work? That is scary.

### Ghast of Characters


All the cards in that deck (with the exception of [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit)) are appropriately Halloween-themed. But that was more of a deck built around a specific card than it was a theme deck. For a brilliant high-concept theme deck, see [Anthony's most recent column](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/gently-rapping-your-door-2003-10-28). For a much more straightforward theme deck built around Halloween's familiar denizens, see what's lurking around the next paragraph.


No high-falutin' nineteenth-century poetry for me. When it's time for a Halloween theme deck, give me a werewolf, a ghost, a vampire, a bat, a skeleton, a mummy, a witch, a zombie, a poltergeist, an imp, a ghoul, a specter, a phantom, and a monster. Check that - I meant a [Phantom Monster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Monster). Some literary references sneak in; it's just so hard to leave [Headless Horseman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Headless+Horseman) and [Frankenstein's Monster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frankenstein%27s+Monster) out of a Halloween deck. It's even harder to put [Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banshee)*into* the deck, but it would not be denied. Luckily, a ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) 0/1 creature has two great uses here: It's the perfect card to discard to your Spellshapers, Vampire, or Looter, and it's fantastic for removing from your graveyard to feed [Barrow Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barrow+Ghoul), [Rotting Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Giant), or Frank.


Looter, you ask? Of course! The [Trickster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trickster+Mage) is the tricker, and the [Merfolk Looters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looters) are the treaters. They have an [Ancient Craving](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Craving) for candy, but when they encounter the [Scare Tactics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scare+Tactics) of a [Dread Reaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Reaper) under a [Bad Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon), you'll be able to hear their [Endless Scream](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+Scream) forever...


The deck is a weenie deck with some graveyard tricks. It leans on the [Mischievous Poltergeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mischievous+Poltergeist)s, which are difficult to exorcise, as well as the swarm of other little black flying nasties. Pump them up with [Bad Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon)s and the +X/+0 effects in the deck. The deck has lots of ways to feed your graveyard since being able to support an early [Barrow Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barrow+Ghoul) can be a big advantage. If you can kick the [Oversold Cemetery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversold+Cemetery) into gear, so much the better.

### Frightfest


Ever since Alpha, one of the elements that set **Magic** apart from other games is its phenomenal artwork. New settings, fantastical creatures, heroic characters - each card is a flavor capsule whisked in from a realm beyond imagination. And that realm has some freaky stuff lurking in its corners. Not to mention the sick minds that created it. This next deck is a nigh-Singleton deck (you'll see why) built solely out of the creepiest, eepiest, utterly gheepiest images ever committed to cardboard. The deck hangs together decently (not surprisingly, it's got some removal, some discard, some graveyard tricks, and some mean ol' creatures), but the point of playing it is just to show off the art. Really look at each piece when it comes up, then try not to have nightmares later that night.


**[Ashen Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashen+Ghoul)** - Is it ickier if that's its own blood or someone else's?


![Bloodcurdler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodcurdler)**[Bloodcurdler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodcurdler)** - Freaks me out no end. Can you believe this came in for a *blue* card (Pulsating Illusion)?
 **[Boneshard Slasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boneshard+Slasher)** - Don't let it sit in your beanbag chair.


**[Cackling Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Fiend)** - There's a fine line between insanity and raving insanity.


**[Carnophage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnophage)** - Dude, where's my skin?


**[Carrionette](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrionette)** - Gerrard hadn't dusted for cobwebs in ages.


**[Childhood Horror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Childhood+Horror)** - Adulthood too.


**[Chime of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chime+of+Night)** - What're *you* smiling at?


**[Coffin Puppets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coffin+Puppets)** - Not my kind of puppet show.


**[Contagion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion)** - The thing that always got me about this piece is that the cat-the haunted, death-possessed cat-seems scared too.


![Cosmic Horror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cosmic+Horror)**[Corpse Dance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Dance)** - Nice that it keeps an active social life.
 **[Cosmic Horror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cosmic+Horror)** - What the Flargg is this thing?!?!


**[Dance of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dance+of+the+Dead)** - One of my favorite art pieces. Go Grim Reaper go!


**[Dread Wight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Wight)** - And this is a *good* hair day.


**[Dungeon Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Shade)** - Its face is in mid-melt.


**[Grim Feast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Feast)** - It's not that he's eating a severed arm. It's how casually he's doing it.


**[Juzam Djinn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juzam+Djinn)** - My all-time favorite art piece, this is the stuff nightmares are made of.


**[Lake of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lake+of+the+Dead)** - Everyone out of the pool.


![Juzam Djinn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Juzam+Djinn)**[Lhurgoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Lhurgoyf)** - I still remember the visceral reaction I had when I figured out the monster's tube-like maw was slurping down a whole frozen corpse.
 **[Lingering Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Death)** - Oozing, smoking orifices. Bet it's even worse *inside* his head.


**[Living Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wall)** - [Wall of Bone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Bone), [Wall of Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Blood), [Wall of Corpses](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Corpses), and [Wall of Putrid Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Putrid+Flesh) all have grosser names. Just about all of them used [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) for the "A," but they diverged after that. I liked the idea that [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes) would clear out your opponent's graveyard to make [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) one-sided. [Eater of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eater+of+the+Dead), besides being a sizable creature, accomplishes the same thing. The macabre [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites) is the best way to fill your graveyard with fat creatures while disrupting your opponent. The evil [Evil Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Presence), while normally subpar, guarantees that your swampwalking [Odylic Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Odylic+Wraith) and [Witch Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witch+Engine) get through. And as long as you've got 24 Swamps, why not take advantage of them with the looming [Looming Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Shade) and the incredibly appropriate [Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare). Good job, everyone; pumpkins all around!
