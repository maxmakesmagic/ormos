The July 11, 2016 Update
========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on July 11, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on July 11, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by San Diego Comic-Con news!


San Diego Comic-Con News
========================


1. *Magic: The Gathering* is going to be at [San Diego Comic-Con](http://www.comic-con.org/)! (Held July 21-24.) How, you ask? Well, that's what I'm here for!
* You can find us at the Hasbro booth, No. 3213
* James Wyatt, writer of *The Art of Magic: The Gathering* books, will be hanging out at the VIZ Media booth, No. 2813 (VIZ Media publish the art books). He'll also be doing signings for *The Art of Magic: The Gathering—Innistrad* at both the VIZ Media booth and on the Hasbro stage. Check out the [SDCC schedule](http://www.comic-con.org/) as we get closer.
* Mark Rosewater will be doing [Blogatog](http://markrosewater.tumblr.com/) live (where he answers your questions) on Saturday evening from 6:30–7:30 p.m. in room 7AB.
* The Wizards of the Coast creative team [will be in attendance at SDCC for portfolio reviews](https://twitter.com/MarkWintersArt/status/750850577928564736). They will be held in the portfolio review area from 10 a.m. to 7 p.m. Friday and Saturday, plus 10 a.m. till 1:30 p.m. on Sunday. Advanced sign-up on site is required, but those details will be forthcoming. For anyone interested, watch [@Wizards\_Magic](https://twitter.com/wizards_magic) and [@MarkWintersArt](https://twitter.com/MarkWintersArt) for details.
* We *will* have exclusive promo cards at SDCC, just like we have in the past. Watch the internet for this news. I can’t say more now, but, once they are revealed, I’ll make sure to include them in the Daily *Magic* Update.
* Finally, Jessica Burton will be on hand cosplaying as Liliana with her Zombie minion in the booth for photo ops.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Goblin Prison—4th-Place at the StarCityGames.com Worcester Open](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=105147)

Like you, I did a bit of a triple take when I saw the deck name "Goblin Prison" next to Rob Blocher's name. It isn't exactly a widespread archetype, even by Legacy standards, and so it took a bit of searching to uncover what was going on. But when I did, it made a bit more sense.


The deck is, in a way, an ancestor to All-In Red, a deck that used rituals and the sol lands ([Ancient Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb) and [City of Traitors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors)) to quickly play a moon effect ([Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) or [Magus of the Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Moon)) or a threat big enough to kill quickly.


This version has the same initial plan—play a lock piece (in this case, the moons are also backed up by [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) and [Trinisphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinisphere))—and then follow-up with a threat that will end the game before the opponent can recover. The difference is that the threats are Goblins.


Now, you might have noticed that [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) and [Trinisphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinisphere) don't play well with your typical Goblins—your one- and two-drops. This deck plays none of those. No explosive [Goblin Lackey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey) starts here. Instead, the deck plays a suite of sweet Goblins that cost three or more, plus [Moggcatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moggcatcher), which can be used to put Goblins straight into play from your library.


From there, [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander), [Goblin Rabblemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Rabblemaster), and [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) clean up.
