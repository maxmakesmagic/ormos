Day 2 Coverage



Oh, what a marvelous weekend! The narrative of Grand Prix Montreal was driven by the powerful artifact Aetherworks Marvel, but the winning path was windy indeed.


Temur Marvel—the looming monster coming into the tournament—was predicted by many to dominate the archetype numbers in the Top 8. But only three made it through (just like in Grand Prix Santiago), and people thought they had overestimated the deck...


But, like one completing a trial on Amonkhet, Marvel's tenacity was rewarded. two of the three Temur titans faced off in the finals. Kevin Jones and Paul Dean recreated of the War of 1812—America vs. Canada. But in this version, the Americans actually performed well, and it was New Yorker Jones who burned the convention center to the ground.

Day 1 Coverage



It's a marvelous world, and we're just living in it. The story of the first day of Grand Prix Montreal was a cavalcade of cards trying to take down Aetherworks Marvel. We saw the usual suspects of Mardu Vehicles and Zombies of all sorts, but decks like White-Blue Vehicles, Mardu Planeswalkers, and various Control archetypes all had their goes in the Feature Match spotlight.


Though some of the rascally rogues are still doing well, it's not so easy to quell the Marvel spell. Three of the four players without a loss are on the Temur terror.Maxime Auger, Shaun McLaren, Paul Dean, are all 9-0, with Bradley Robinson a skosh behind at 8-0-1. Only the Canadian lone wolf, Shaun McLaren is against the tide, playing Green-Black Constrictor adeptly, fending off the Ulamogs at every turn.


Aetherworks Marvel has the target on its back going into tomorrow. Only time will tell if if the intrepid combatants at Grand Prix Montreal can take it to task.
