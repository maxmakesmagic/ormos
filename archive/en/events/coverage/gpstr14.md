TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------


Team Hungary couldn't be off to a better start for next weeks *Magic* festivities. Tamás Nagy winning the Grand Prix marks his first individual title, and now he hopes that the Hungarian team can repeat their top 8 performance from last year.


A wonderful weekend of *Magic* in Strasbourg is over, where an Orwellian 1984 players came to battle against both Big Brother and each other. 224 made it to the second day of competition, and when the smoke cleared for top 8 it was Martin Juza who came in as favorite. However, Tamás Nagy and his Sultai Deck took him out in the semifinals and then ran over Pierre Dagen in the final.


The *Khans of Tarkir* have once again tested their clans strengths, and this time Sultai came out on top. But more importantly, the playing skills and endurance of Tamás Nagy lasted all the way to victory. Now we are as excited as he is to see how far the Hungarians can go next.


Make sure to join all the action next week at both the World Championships and the World *Magic* Cup, where you will want to keep an extra eye on Hungary.

DAY 2 COVERAGE
--------------


Welcome back to Day 2 of Grand Prix Strasbourg! What had started with 1,984 players Saturday morning has, after nine rounds of Sealed Deck play, been reduced to 224. These 224 are now returning for two *Khans of Tarkir* booster drafts, each followed by three rounds. At the end of these six rounds, but eight players will remain and gather for one final draft to determine the champion of this Grand Prix. 


With the World Championship and the World *Magic* Cup just a few days (and a few kilometers) away, the turnout of big-name players had been impressive. However, many of the top pros are watching from the sidelines today (or rather resume their testing for next week's tournaments) like Player of the Year (2) Jérémy Dezani, reigning world champion (14) Shahar Shenhar, and Hall of Famer Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa. Still, more than enough pros remain in the running, including Pierre Dagen and Valentin Mackl who both ended the first day with records of 9-0, (5) Ivan Floch, Martin Jůza, Andrejs Prost, Hannes Kerem, Patrick Dickmann, Vincent Lemoine, Shuhei Nakamura, Kentarou Yamamoto, David Reitbauer, Timothée Simonot, Justin Cheung, Marijn Lybaert, (17) Tom Martell, and Daniel Gräfensteiner to name but a few.


So the stage is set, the players are ready to battle, the lights and the cameras are back on, and it's promising to be great show once again!

Day 1 of Grand Prix Strasbourg is in the books. Through nine grueling rounds of *Khans of Tarkir* Sealed Deck play, the original field of 1,984 has been whittled down to just 224. Those 224 players will return tomorrow for the Booster Draft portion of the event and to make a run at the Top 8.


Overnight, five players remain atop the standings with perfect records of 9-0: Pierre Dagen, Valentin Mackl, Nicolas Cuenca, Beat Tscharner, Oleg Plisov. They may have a headstart on the competition tomorrow, but the likes of Christian Seibold, Tamás Nagy, Martin Jůza, (5) Ivan Floch, Andrejs Prost, Bing Han, Daniele Canavesi, Chapman Sim, Shuhei Nakamura, Patrick Dickmann, Dmitriy Butakov, (17) Tom Martell, Timothée Simonot, Marcio Carvalho, Eliott Boussaud, Christophe Gregoir, and Marijn Lybaert aren't far behind and will certainly try to catch up and surpass the current leaders. 


It's been an interesting first day here in Strasbourg and it's surely going to be an even more interesting day tomorrow. Join us then!
