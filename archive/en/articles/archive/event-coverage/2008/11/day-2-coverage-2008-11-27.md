Sunday, November 30: 9:37a.m. – Drafting with Shu Komuro
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*

After eight rounds of play yesterday, we were left with three undefeated players, all of whom were (of course) drafting on table one at the start of day two. Shu Komuro was feeding fellow undefeated Way Chan, and was two people downstream of Osamu Fujita.


Komuro’s first pack offered him a few options, but it mostly came down to [Branching Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Branching+Bolt) or [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) . Komuro took the Bolt, while Shi Tian Lee upstream of him, drafted a [Jungle Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jungle+Shrine) over a [Cavern Thoctar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Thoctar) and a [Tower Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Gargoyle) . After a little deliberation, Komuro then chose the Thoctar over the Gargoyle, wanting to stay with his first pick rather than move into Esper. He followed that with a [Rip-Clan Crasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rip-Clan+Crasher) and a [Rockslide Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental) , before finally opening up to Jund when he noticed the number of [Blister Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blister+Beetle) s floating past. Meanwhile, ahead of Komuro, Lee was drafting almost exclusively Green and White cards, not quite treading on our protagonist’s toes. By the end of pack one, Komuro had a reasonable Jund deck. Upstream of him, Lee was still Green/White, while Osamu Fujita ahead of Lee was firmly in Naya.


Pack two had a reasonable [Rhox Charger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Charger) for Komuro, before being passed an impressive [Violent Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Ultimatum) by Chan. He was then passed a [Scavenger Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenger+Drake) , and picked a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) over an [Algae Gharial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial) fourth. Komuro rounded out the pack with A [Soul’s Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%E2%80%99s+Fire) , a [Carrion Thrash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Thrash) and a [Jund Panorama](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Panorama) to smooth out his mana. Now downstream of him, Lee was struggling with his third color, shifting between Red and Blue half-heartedly.


The last pack rewarded Komuro with what many people consider to be second best common in Shards of Alara draft, a [Vithian Stinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vithian+Stinger) . He then picked up another [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) and a [Savage Lands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Lands) . However, the rest of the pack was somewhat lackluster, only giving him a few more small creatures to fill out the numbers. Ideally, he would have wanted another [Carrion Thrash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Thrash) or two, and maybe some [Viscera Dragger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Dragger) s.


Afterwards, he admitted that the draft could have gone a little better for him than it had, although I can’t possibly think of any deck better than one that has three [Blister Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blister+Beetle) s! He doesn’t normally like Esper, which was why he ended up choosing the [Cavern Thoctar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Thoctar) over the [Tower Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Gargoyle) pick two in pack one. But in the end said that there were far too many Green drafters on the table, and moving into Esper may have yielded a better deck for him. He also thought that it was a mistake to pick the [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) over the [Algae Gharial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial) , as that would have taken his deck from merely “okay” to “solid.” Komuro thought his deck was 1-2 or 2-1 at best. I’m firmly of the opinion that 3 [Blister Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blister+Beetle) means 3-0, for sure.


  


Sunday, November 30: 10:13a.m. – Round 9: Way Chan vs Shu Komuro
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

Shu Komuro and Way Chan were two of the three undefeated players at the end of day one, and while Komuro has had quite the successful Magic career to date, apparently this is Way Chan’s first premier event. I don’t think anyone can claim that an 8-0 start is anything short of fantastic.


Chan won the roll and chose to play first, leading with an Unglued [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) , clearly a man who comes prepared to draft. Chan had the first play with a [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) into a [Sanctum Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanctum+Gargoyle) , while Komuro’s first play was a [Grixis Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grixis+Battlemage) . Without a fourth land, Chan played a [Drumhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drumhunter) , mostly likely hoping there was some earth on top of his deck that could potentially power up the [Drumhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drumhunter) with some big fellas. Komura made a Jund Obelisk and pointed a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) at it before things could get out of hand. Chan replaced it with a [Naya Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Battlemage) , still stuck on four mana. [Rhox Charger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Charger) came down for Komuro, allowing the [Grixis Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grixis+Battlemage) to swing in for 3. Chan pondered his options, before deciding to [Branching Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Branching+Bolt) the Charger, figuring there probably weren’t that many fliers in Komuro’s Jund deck worth waiting for. He then added a [Metallurgeon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallurgeon) to his team. Both players verified how many cards the other were holding, while Komuro gummed up his side of the table with some [Dragon Fodder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder) and a [Rockslide Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental) . Chan’s [Sanctum Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanctum+Gargoyle) continued to assault Komuro unhindered, while he in turn dug for an answer with a [Gift of the Gargantuan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+the+Gargantuan) , giving him a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and a [Carrion Thrash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Thrash) . The under-appreciated [Metallurgeon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallurgeon) was going to make it difficult for Komuro to stop the Gargoyle, while the [Naya Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Battlemage) was doing all it could to speed up the process. A [Resounding Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar) from Chan sent them to Game 2.


Chan 1 – Komuro 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai08/R9_chan.jpg)"You play first," Komuro advised Chan, who was more than happy with that plan, again leading with one of his many Unglued lands. Komuro’s [Vithian Stinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vithian+Stinger) traded point for point with Chan’s [Metallurgeon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallurgeon) , until a [Court Archers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Archers) came down, increasing the Artifact Creatures attack potential, two-fold. A [Blister Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blister+Beetle) finished what the Stinger started, leaving the Archer to attack on its own instead. Neither player had more to play after that, and were content to attack each other for two a turn, until Komuro played a [Carrion Thrash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Thrash) , that was promptly [Excommunicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Excommunicate) d by Chan. When it came back down, it was taken aside by an [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) instead. Komuro replaced it with a [Rhox Charger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Charger) , for which Chan had a [Resounding Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Thunder) , both players still managing to reduce each others life totals by 2 a turn only. Komuro tried to pull ahead with a [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) , only to see Chan negate it with a [Kiss of the Amesha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiss+of+the+Amesha) . Flustered, Komuro threw away a [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) on the attacking [Court Archers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Archers) , before realizing the Stinger could only do one more damage and couldn’t finish it off. "On Magic Online, it would have shown that as a 2/4," Komuro sighed with a laugh.


Berating himself, Komuro could only shake his head as Chan’s next attack reduced him to 4 life, while a [Sanctum Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanctum+Gargoyle) returned the [Metallurgeon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallurgeon) to play. [Blister Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blister+Beetle) and the Stinger took that out, and just when it looked like curtains for Komuro, he found the third Red source he need to wreck Chan’s board with a [Violent Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Ultimatum) . A [Bloodpyre Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodpyre+Elemental) killed the recently freed [Carrion Thrash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Thrash) , while a [Naya Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naya+Battlemage) looked set to hold back Komuro’s army of 1/1’s, now with added [Dragon Fodder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder) , if not for the [Vithian Stinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vithian+Stinger) . A [Scavenger Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenger+Drake) came down for Komuro, giving the Battlemage a potentially very legitimate target. A Goblin Token went in and was blocked by the Battlemage, who was in turn saved from the Stinger by a [Resounding Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar) . Komuro continued to drop small guys into play, and eventually ran his remaining Goblin Token into a blocker, to allow the [Scavenger Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenger+Drake) to finish Chan off via [Soul’s Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%E2%80%99s+Fire) .


Chan 1 – Komuro 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gptai08/R9_komuro.jpg)Komuro was again prepared for Dragons in Game 3, with plenty of Fodder should one present itself, while Chan summoned a [Sighted-Caste Sorcerer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sighted-Caste+Sorcerer) to his side. Sunday, November 30: 11:21 a.m. – Round 10: Marcio Carvalho vs Martin Juza
--------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

Marcio Carvalho won the roll, and forced Martin Juza to go first. "Nooo! Okay, this is the one match I would want to draw," Juza clarified, having earlier claimed he could always play. Both players appeared to being playing Esper, with Carvalho dropping a [Couriers Capsule](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Couriers+Capsule) on turn two, causing yet more cries of anguish from Juza. By turn three, both players had a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) , an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) in play, must be nice.


Carvalho added a [Windwright Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windwright+Mage) . "It’s not going to be hard to give it flying," Carvalho pointed out as Juza read the card. Juza’s fourth turn gave him a fourth color, as he dropped a [Fire-Field Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Field+Ogre) into play. Carvalho cracked his Capsule, and kept his life-total afloat with the Mage. On Juza’s fifth turn, he dropped a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) into play, completing Domain and proclaiming "mad skills!" with a grin. When Carvalho went to [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) the Ogre, Juza protected it with a [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm) . Carvalho played his fifth land and thought for a second, "Gargoyle?" Juza asked. "Seems like a good idea," Carvalho replied, retrieving his [Courier’s Capsule](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courier%E2%80%99s+Capsule) with a [Sanctum Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sanctum+Gargoyle) . An [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) from Juza killed the [Windwright Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windwright+Mage) and neutered the Gargoyle for a turn, while he added a [Rhox War Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) to his team. Anything’s possible with all five colors.
