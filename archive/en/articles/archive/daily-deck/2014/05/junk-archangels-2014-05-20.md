Junk Archangels
===============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on May 20, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






Jeff Hoogland is a player who has had quite a bit of success with under-the-radar decks. If you played against Black-Green Midrange last year or Blue-Red Faeries in Modern this year, you likely played against a Jeff Hoogland creation. Jeff is at it again, building a new deck with *Journey into Nyx* to help him reach the semifinals of a SCG Standard Open.



![Ajani, Mentor of Heroes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Ajani,%20Mentor%20of%20Heroes&options=)Jeff's deck is a black-green-white deck that takes advantage of a variety of card interactions and borrows strategies from other decks. In some games, you can use [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) to draw a steady stream of removal spells. In other games, [Archangel of Thune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) is very easy to trigger multiple times with [Courser of Kruphix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix) or [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze). I'm sure Jeff caught more than one opponent by surprise with [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm), especially as opponents did not know what to expect. Building new decks can unleash the power of uncertainty on your opponents!
