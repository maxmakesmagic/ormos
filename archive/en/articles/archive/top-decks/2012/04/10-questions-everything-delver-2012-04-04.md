10 Questions: Everything Delver
===============================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on April 5, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







* 1. What do you think is in the Helvault?

No idea. Let's talk about [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)!


* 2. What do "good players" think of Delver of Secrets?

A week or so ago I was playing in the StarCityGames.com Invitational and got paired against Grand Prix champion Dave Shiels. We were 2–0 at the time, and there were a fair number of well-known players sitting near us.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td189_del.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/sun_arttransformbutton_active.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/moon_arttransformbutton_static.png)[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Nils+Hamm%22%5D)



Look to the left, look to the right, there was Joshua Ravitz (who would go on to make Top 8 of the Invitational), well-known writer Larry Swasey, and even more well-known writer Gerry Thompson. We chuckled, as every single player we recognized had a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) in play (including each of us).


</magic/flipcards/flipcard.html?ca=jhfghionlmfhtytghdf777amp;cb=jcdbfuyugtehvursertrereamp;lang=en>
In a relatively short time, [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) has grown into one of the most influential creatures—influential ***Magic: The Gathering** cards—*in recent memory (maybe history). Tongue-in-cheek as its presentation might be, this article is about all the different ways you can run into a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets); how to know *which*[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) you are up against; and what the differences, advantages, and incentives are to the different builds.


I don't even know if this is a controversial statement at this point, but I think [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is not just good, but both the best creature and the best card in Standard.


* 3. What DID good players think of Delver of Secrets?

This is something that I was interested in and a question I asked myself when I first realized how influential [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) was becoming.


... did writers (in their various "*Innistrad* review"-type articles on the websites various) realize at the outset how good Delver might become?


**Quiet Speculation:**


"This is extremely marginal but if it's possible to set this up as an early 3/2 flier consistently, it may see some play."


**ChannelFireball.com:**


Luis Scott-Vargas rated [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) a "2" ("niche card"), focusing on the card's awesome flavor (and I think we can all agree there is some nice Jeff Goldblum going on on the card), although he did speculate that it might be "sweet" and possibly where.


**TCGPlayer.com:**


Conley Woods was the most optimistic about the [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration)-to-be, rating the one-drop a"3" (his "backbone of Standard" level, comparable to [Makeshift Mannequin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) or [Mind Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Spring))... but acknowledged that some might see this as a stretch. While [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) didn't make Conley's Top 10 list for all of *Innistrad*, it did make his "Top 3" mini-list for blue, saying it was one of the cards he was most interested in working on.


My goal isn't to call anyone out on this front, but more to illustrate that even the experts can miss (or at least underrate) some of the most powerful cards.


I think that in this case, that came from not realizing what a *very* little amount of tuning (playing lots of spells and a four-pack of [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s) could do for the Delver.


So what makes the Delver so awesome?


Essentially, it redefines a lot of what it means to be a blue creature. [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is aggressive. It is a one-drop... you can play it before the stage in the game where you "need" to keep your lands open for [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) or whatever. You can put other decks on notice (while threatening to take over with the aforementioned [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)). And what was really underrated initially: *[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) hits hard!*


Have you ever seen one of those draws where one player goes turn-one [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), turn-two *two* Delvers? I mean 9 damage coming in on turn three, over the top of potential blockers, is impressive almost unconditionally... but from a *blue* deck? Ka-pow!


Factor in all that stuff about holding back the old [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s, and you have something pretty special.


Did you ever come to the realization, looking at an Extended blue deck splash *just* for [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), that the age of [Keiga, the Tide Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keiga%2C+the+Tide+Star) in tapout strategies might be over? [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is that, over and over again ten times.


* 4. Okay, it's good... but which is better, Delver of Secrets or Snapcaster Mage?

Opinions differ.


Part of the distinction is clouded by the fact that the cards are so often played next to one another. Sure, [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) can be played in more decks (for example, control decks will often play some), but it is rare to see [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) without [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage).


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td189_sna.jpg)[Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) | Art by [Volkan Baga](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Volkan+Baga%22%5D)


Many players probably see [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) as the more flashy card. It certainly does some exciting stuff, especially when combined with cheap removal ([Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) + [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) is pretty dam-bursting), whereas "all" [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) offers is damage and evasion.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Snapcaster+Mage)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)
... although I guess put another way, an unchecked [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) is like a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) every turn.


Especially when the two cards first came out (and [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) was still largely underrated), [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) was considered the best card in *Innistrad*.


Me? Both cards are sweet, but I prefer the one-drop!


* 5. Surely you exaggerate... it's not like Delver of Secrets is played in bigger formats or anything, is it?

We're going to spend most of the "top decks" part of this Top Decks on Standard, but (since you asked) [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) has just racked up another impressive finish!

Former US National Champion Antonino De Rosa cleaned up Grand Prix Turin with a different take on the RUG Delver archetype.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td189_ant.jpg)*Antonino De Rosa, Grand Prix Champion, Turin 2012*


Here is a deck that—like the Standard (and predominantly white-blue versions)—is medium-light on creatures but super strong on cheap instants... [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce), [Spell Snare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare), [Serum Visions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) (to make up for Modern's paucity of prohibited [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s), and so many [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s Antonino even dipped into [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning)!

Between [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), the RUG Delver deck can get the quick drop on most opponents, and the medium counterspells and removal can keep opponents on their heels, hold a lead, push blockers out of the way, and generally disrupt.


But yeah, everywhere from Standard to Legacy... and with [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) banned in Block? Who knows?


* 6. So... more Delver of Secrets + Snapcaster Mage (like peanut butter and chocolate). Any other cards we usually see played together?

I would say, "certainly!"


The typical build around [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) in Standard includes not just [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), but eight to ten zero-mana or one-mana manipulation cards, always the Maximum Number of [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s, then usually four [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)s and one or two [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td189_inv.jpg)[Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) | Art by [Bud Cook](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Bud+Cook%22%5D)


[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is good, but it can't usually win a tournament all by itself. So it has buddies beyond even the very good [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) (which is also an awesome card, but fragile at 2/1). The Delver deck runs [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) as the very active B-Squad; they have hexproof so they make for great combinations with enhancers like [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) and [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike).


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Invisible+Stalker)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker)
Check [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) in particular... [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) thrives in a deck full of instants and sorceries; [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) loves having those instants and sorceries move from the deck (either via the hand or some other mechanism, be it [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) or your own [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)) to the graveyard. [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) is perfect synergy! It *also* loves being played with lots of instants and sorceries, and moreover also thrives when those are in the bin.


Hexproof creatures are perfect not just because they can be so powerful ([Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) with [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) is simply a race... and who wants to be on the wrong side of an unchecked Geist?), but because getting your guy killed in response to an equip is just a blowout frowny-face.

This version of the UW Delver is actually a week older than Shenhar's. Yuuya Watanabe used it to take down a Grand Prix the previous Sunday.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td189_yuu.jpg)*Yuuya Watanabe, Grand Prix Champion, Kuala Lampur 2012*


The structure of the deck is in large part very similar to other UW Delver decks, but there are two substantial differences:



> 
> * The inclusion of [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists)
> * The non-inclusion of [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)
> 


We are going to touch on the [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) issue in just a bullet or two, so I will focus on the other, which is actually the larger departure for the Standard Delver deck (while it also bears mentioning that Watanabe's winner played triple Pikes and no lifegain Equipment in the main).


[Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)—up until Watanabe's weekend—was an institution in Delver decks. You just had four. That was it.


There are different schools of thought on this; Josh Ravitz has an interesting idea, which is that as you expect more, better, players to be on UW Delver decks, [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) is less important. The sideboarded games typically see players with [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) in the sideboard, so you don't even need to know if they have the counter (they don't). So why not just play [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), which can actually set up your [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s and [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt)s?


* 8. I see that deck has a lot of Gut Shots. Why? (And how does he even cast that with his blue and white lands?)

[Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) is amazing in the Delver deck!


You can [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) a small creature, tap out for [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) again.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td189_gut.jpg)[Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) | Art by [Greg Staples](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Greg+Staples%22%5D)


[Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) is one of the best cards against the viable non-[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) decks. Red-green variants from beatdown to Naya Ramp rely on cards like [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to get the jump on other decks. [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)—being "zero" mana (especially in a deck that can't cast it legitimately)—lets a white-blue deck get a similar mana advantage, interactively, to a red-green deck advancing actively with an actual accelerating one-drop.


While we have seen some mixing and matching around Phyrexian removal (one [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) in Shenhar's main, two [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember)s and no [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s in some earlier builds), I would hazard that [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) will emerge as the Phyrexian signature card of choice.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gut+Shot)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)
Why?


You can get the jump on another Delver deck before its [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) shows up!


* 9. How about them Dungeon Geists?

So I said that Watanabe's deck had two big, different, things going on; one of which was the inclusion of [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists).


[Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) has had an on-again, off-again awesome metagame position with the Standard format.


![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td189_dun.jpg)[Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Nils+Hamm%22%5D)


I would guess that, "objectively," [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) is a strong creature for blue: 3/3 and flying for four mana? A hair smaller than [Troublesome Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troublesome+Spirit) maybe (and [Troublesome Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troublesome+Spirit) made [the greatest Pro Tour Top 8 of all time](http://archive.wizards.com/sideboard/article.asp?x=ptchi00%5C840t8decks))... but the current incarnation of Standard has no [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to punish any missing 4th toughness.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dungeon+Geists)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists)
Beyond the body (and evasion), [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) has a pretty awesome ability, especially in the context of a format full of Titans (at least some slice of the format looks like that). Subtly, [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) is just hard for another Delver deck to deal with effectively. As we said before, most of the [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember)s are being traded down for [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s; and a 3/3 is just bigger than a 1/1, 2/1, or 2/2 (as are the typical sizes).


We would be remiss if we failed to mention a resurgent Spirits deck; here is the Grand Prix Top 4 version piloted by [recent Top Decks subject Tom Martell](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/td/186):

* 10. Where to finish out? How about enhancement in general?

If there is one thing you find across at least the Standard UW Delver decks, it is that the creatures rarely come in alone.

Caleb removed the [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker)s... and with them went most of the incentive of trying to race with [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike).


But where an innovator closes one door, another one often opens; Caleb kept [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) (another very Equipment-eligible threat), one of the best solitary threats in the format... but somewhat vulnerable to... "blockers."


What about [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight)?


Kind of a weird solution, no?




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Well, you slap a [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) onto a Geist and you don't see any Wolf tokens jumping in front to take down the big man at the cost of 4 life. You don't see any ground-token chump-blocking against a first-striking Pike. The Geist just goes straight "to the air" and you have 8 in, and a two-or-three-turn clock.


Beyond these, you will sometimes see [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue) or even [Honor of the Pure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure) as UW Delver decks hybridize with Humans or Tokens. Those decks—obviously set up a bit differently from the mode—will often try to double up early with [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) on top of [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) as an aggressive one-drop, or substitute [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) (excellent on defense as well as offense) over [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)... particularly potent in [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue) builds.


Whew.


Although we still don't know what will be in the [Helvault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helvault), I hope you find yourself a bit better prepared to face [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). This is a creature—a powerful, even wonderful creature—that you can be sure you will face in Standard, or anywhere, really.


As the sensei used to say, "Study and grow strong." And if you can't beat 'em?*...* (*you know*)...
