The March 10, 2016 Update
=========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on March 10, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on March 10, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by Gerard Butler, for [delivering the quote](#quote) you'll be hearing for the next eighteen months or so.

Today's Must
============


1. [A Bright Future—Looking at *Shadows over Innistrad*](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13161&writer=Adam+Yurchick&articledate=3-10-2016) | TCGPlayer | Adam Yurchick

Yesterday our must-read was an [article from Frank Karsten on the cards leaving Standard](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/top-10-card-clusters-that-will-rotate-out-of-standard/) with the [upcoming rotation](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/metamorphosis-redux-2015-08-24). Today, Adam Yurchick takes a glance in the direction not many people always consider—forgotten gems that might have a chance to shine in the new world. We already have a lot of toys to play with—now we just have to see if we can break any of them in the new Standard.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Knuck's Last Ride](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/standard-constructed-league-2016-03-08)

Of all of the wedge-aligned creatures from *Khans of Tarkir*, it sure seemed like [Savage Knuckleblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Knuckleblade) [coulda been a contender](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z9mMBj-yFuE). It has so many abilities! It's so big! But, alas, it ended up playing, like, fourth fiddle to [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino), [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider), and [Sidisi, Brood Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidisi%2C+Brood+Tyrant). At least it was better than [Rakshasa Vizier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakshasa+Vizier).


At any rate, as befits [Savage Knuckleblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Knuckleblade)'s time in Standard, in this four-color [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) deck it's kind of just coming along for the ride, hoping to high-five [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider) as they crash in together, crossing its hairy fingers that the caster of [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) doesn't really want another [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage).


Interesting in HerosOfKern's deck is also the pair of [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel)s to go along with 26 creatures and two spells aimed at interacting with creatures. It speaks to the ubiquity of [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and [Rally the Ancestors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors) (plus some things like [Murderous Cut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Cut) or [Crackling Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Doom)) that those get a seemingly random-looking spot, especially in a deck that very much could play [Stubborn Denial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stubborn+Denial).


The sideboard is interesting as well, as three [Exert Influence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exert+Influence)s is probably the most I've seen in a decklist all season—a fact a few people have bemoaned lately. The deck probably gets into a lot of near-mirrors and midrange-ish matchups, and stealing [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) certainly clears the way for the deck's smaller creatures.


And, quite possibly, makes [Savage Knuckleblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Knuckleblade) feel just a little bit better about itself.
