Introducing the Spellslinger Starter Kit
========================================



 Posted in **News**
 on August 13, 2018 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20180809173306im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg?itok=-rI3VOu8)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






On October 5, we'll be releasing a new learn-to-play product aimed at helping new players, well, learn to play. Quickly.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/images/daily/coMWUMtMwr.png)


The Spellslinger Starter Kit will contain two 60-card decks, two quick-start learn to play guides, a booklet featuring the world and rules of *Magic*, two cards to help players get started with each deck, and two spindown dice. And in those 60-card decks? Well, more on that below.


The Spellslinger Starter Kit will be available in [local game stores](http://locator.wizards.com/) and mass market stores. It will have an MSRP of $14.99 (applies to the United States and Canada only).


The decks will be ready to play out of the boxâ€”meaning they're meant not to be shuffled. The card included on the top of each deck will walk players through starting the game with predetermined steps for the order the decks come in. Of course, once players have given that a spin, they'll be able to shuffle up and see why every game of *Magic* is different.





![Do Not Shuffle](https://media.wizards.com/2018/images/daily/deZa6t3dRj.png "Do Not Shuffle")
![Turn Order Cards](https://media.wizards.com/2018/images/daily/HpKxffFZEM.png "Turn Order Cards")

Turn Over


And since it comes out after *Guilds of Ravnica*, the kit will contain a number of cards from that set. We're still a few weeks away from previews for *that* set, so in the decklist below, they'll be listed simply as "GRN Card." We'll update the decklists once previews have taken place.


So, if you've got a friend or two who have been looking for a good place to start learning the game, check out Spellslinger Starter Kits on October 5 in stores everywhere.
