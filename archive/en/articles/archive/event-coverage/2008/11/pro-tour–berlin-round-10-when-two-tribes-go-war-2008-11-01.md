Pro Tour–Berlin Round 10: When Two Tribes Go to War
===================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 1, 2008 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Tim Willoughby












Rich Hoaen vs. Johan Sadeghpour
-------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptber08/r10_hoaen.jpg)*Rich Hoaen faces a real head-scratcher....*
Both Johan Sadeghpour and Rich Hoaen are best known for their skills with 40-card decks, but for Pro Tour–Berlin they have each found themselves a 60-card deck that they like and run with it. Johan Sadeghpour is the lone Goblins player here on Saturday, reveling in the fact that Elves! is doing as well as it is. Meanwhile Rich has a similar Faeries deck to Gadiel Szleifer (a Gerry Thompson creation that has served both of them very well thus far), and is loving playing turn-one [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) for fun and profit.


### Game 1


Johan won the roll and had a mulligan before playing [Bloodstained Mire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodstained+Mire) into [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground), a card and 3 life down before playing a spell. His first-turn monster was a [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder), who was able to get stuck in unmolested as Rich didn't have any turn-one play beyond a fetch land. Johan was a little mana-light, playing a [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) but no land on turn two. He had a [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal), and passed.


Rich played out a [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) to give him a steady stream of blockers, which was enough to prompt an echo payment from Johan for [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal). A [Goblin Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief) the following turn was countered by [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), but Johan still had play, effectively trading [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal) for two of Rich's team when Hoaen double-blocked [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) on attacks.


Another War Marshal soon followed, and in turn a [Skirk Prospector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector). When Johan ran in for further attacks, Rich had a [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) to improve his own hand and trade with [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder).


"Where's the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Umezawa%2527s%2BJitte)?" asked Sadeghpour with a quizzical smile.


"Not here yet," smirked Hoaen, who would be in great shape should the legendary equipment make an appearance.


On 10 life, Rich played [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). The Wizard was always going to be a chump blocker, and Rich stemmed the tide by trading with most of Johan's team, and following up with [Engineered Explosives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) to finish the job.


Now it was Hoaen's time to fight back. His [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) got active, and he had a [Spell Snare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) for [Goblin Piledriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piledriver). While a [Skirk Prospector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector) made it down, it didn't seem likely to attack too soon. Rich was on 5 from [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), but he had gained control. There was just one piece of the puzzle left to find.


"There it is," remarked Hoaen as he played [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte).


This was more than enough to entice Sadeghpour to scoop up his cards and move on to Game 2.


Hoaen 1, Sadeghpour 0


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptber08/r10_sadeghpour2.jpg)*...But it's Johan Sadeghpour who can't find any answers.*
Johan had a pretty solid start to the game, with a fetch land, [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox), [Rite of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame), [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic), and [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal). Rich, meanwhile, had to content himself with a turn-two [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), the workhorse of his deck .


By the time that Rich had three lands for an end-of-turn Thirst For Knowledge, Hoaen was on just 10. He dropped to 8 on attacks, but had a [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) to champion his [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and effectively end Johan's turn.


[Goblin Sharpshooter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter) from Johan resolved, but [Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) was stopped by [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite). That [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) was problematic, though. It came in for 4 points in the air three turns in a row, and to keep things safe, Rich had [Riptide Laboratory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Laboratory) to return and replay his [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), keeping Sadeghpour tapped down. [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) had done the early work, and the Clique was more than enough to finish it off.


Hoaen 2, Sadeghpour 0
