Feature: Day Two 3-0 Decks
==========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 16, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_paulsottosanti.jpg)
By Paul Sottosanti












Throughout Day Two so far there are twenty-nine decks that have managed to post a perfect 3-0 record. Three players-Nicolai Herzog, Aniol Alcaraz, and Mark Herberholz-were able to dominate both drafts and post perfect records throughout the entire day so far.


Herzog went with a Black/Red deck with a small splash of Blue in the first draft and a solid Green/Red build featuring [Grab the Reins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grab+the+Reins) and [Molder Slug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molder+Slug) in the second. His first deck featured [Arcbound Crusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Crusher), [Somber Hoverguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somber+Hoverguard), [Nim Shrieker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nim+Shrieker), and [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) as creatures that benefited from his four artifact lands and three Talismans. There were no bombs but just a solid assortment of creatures, removal, and utility artifacts like [Talon of Pain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talon+of+Pain) and [Thunderstaff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderstaff).

Aniol Alcaraz, a relatively unknown Spanish player, took Red and Black cards at his first table and then White and Red at his second. He ended up only playing one of his *two* [Skeleton Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeleton+Shard)s in his first deck, but double [Chittering Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chittering+Rats), [Spikeshot Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Goblin), [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) and other goodies were enough to take the draft regardless. His second deck featured [Empyrial Plate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empyrial+Plate), [Vulshok Gauntlets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulshok+Gauntlets), and two [Leonin Bola](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Bola)s as equipment along with a nice creature base. Two [Pteron Ghost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pteron+Ghost)s protected the equipment and a [Spikeshot Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Goblin) was ready to smash face. [Awe Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awe+Strike), [Shatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter) and [Unforge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unforge) provided decent options for combat tricks.

Mark Herberholz had two completely different strategies in his perfect drafts: Green/Red in the first one and Blue/Black in the second. The latter included [Crystal Shard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Shard) and [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) as bombs, along with [Chittering Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chittering+Rats) to provide a possible lock. The former was also quite a sick one. [Glissa Sunseeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glissa+Sunseeker) and [Arc-Slogger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc-Slogger) along with [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) were the main highlights. Triple [Oxidda Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidda+Golem) (and an [Echoing Courage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Courage)) made sure the beats would never stop coming. Add in [Deconstruct](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deconstruct), [Echoing Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Ruin), and [Barbed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbed+Lightning) and you've got yourself quite a spicy deck.

The real news, though, is the prevalence of Red in the 3-0 decks. Here are the numbers of decks (out of the 29) that included each color:




|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Color** | **Decks** | **Percentage** |
| White | 12 | 41.4% |
| Blue | 8 | 27.6% |
| Black | 9 | 31.0% |
| Red | 22 | 75.9% |
| Green | 10 | 34.5% |

It seems like Mike Turian, who has shown a consistent preference for White/Red in this format, is on the right track. My best theory for explaining this is that Red is the only color that has an easy time of answering both creatures and artifacts, so it's more likely to produce a flexible deck that can handle all sorts of opposing threats. Blue/Black decks are often shut down by [Viridian Longbow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow) or [Icy Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icy+Manipulator). Green can sometimes get run over by faster, sleeker decks. White can have problems if you take away its toys (i.e. equipment). Red, however, can roll with the punches.


Here are the numbers for all the individual archetypes:




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Color** | **Decks** |
| W/R | 7 |
| R/G | 4 |
| B/R/U | 4 |
| U/R | 3 |
| W/G | 3 |
| B/R/U | 2 |
| U/B | 2 |
| G/W/R | 2 |
| G/B | 1 |
| U | 1 |

White/Green wasn't the powerhouse some expected it to be, but it held its own. Little mention has been given recently to White/Red, which may have been a mistake, because the archetype performed admirably here today despite none of the above three players running it. The lone mono-color deck was filled with [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s and was piloted by Gabriel Nassif. Given Blue's relationship with artifacts (not to mention the presence of [Spire Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem)), that makes a lot of sense, and obviously worked out here.
