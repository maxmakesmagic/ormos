Round 14: Brian Hegstad vs. Alex Shvartsman
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 15, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











The day has just begun, but it is already do or die time for these two players. Brian Hegstad, a member of last year's championship Team USA, is playing the mono-black [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) deck tuned by Brian Kibler for the Mini-Colony. Alex Shvarstman, now of Your Move Games, is playing a blue/green threshold [Quiet Speculation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation) deck that tries to win by generating large quantities of creatures.

Both players had two-drops to start the first game—Hegstad's a [Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade) and Shvartsman's the less-impressive [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear). Hegstad swung in for three, and then Shvartsman used the bear to accelerate out the ultra-nasty [Phantom Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur). Hegstad cast [Tainted Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact), keeping [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari), and then untapped and [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)d the board clean.

Shvartsman had a second Centaur in hand but no second green source with which to cast it. He put out a [Millikin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millikin), and then Hegstad forced him to discard the rest of his hand, including the Centaur, with a [Mind Sludge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sludge).

Hegstad continued setting up for the long game, putting out the [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) and then copying a [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) to get another Tutor and a [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes). Shvartsman peeled a [Grizzly Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Fate) and put four bear tokens into play, which got one hit in on Hegstad. Hegstad then cast [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) and [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes) in the same turn, but Shvartsman had a [Krosan Reclamation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Reclamation) in his graveyard, which meant the [Grizzly Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Fate)s and his [Quiet Speculation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation)s would not be removed from the game.

It didn't really matter, as a string of copied Tutors and [Tainted Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact)s netted Hegstad a ton of extra cards, and eventually a [Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade) and a [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) did Shvartsman in.

Hegstad 1 – Shvartsman 0

Shvartsman sideboarded in the "anti-Edict package" and started out with an [Acorn Harvest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acorn+Harvest), a [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest), and a [Grizzly Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Fate). Hegstad was backpedaling, trying to stay alive with a bunch of one-for-one spells like [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood).

He eventually got his [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) down and trapped a [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear) under a [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher), but Shvartsman had [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) and reset the board. Hegstad couldn't regroup before a [Phantom Centaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur) came down with other, less relevant creatures to suck up the Edicts. 

Hegstad 1 – Shvartsman 1

Shvartsman had no green mana to start the third game, and could only set up by casting two [Quiet Speculation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation)s and a couple [Mental Note](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Note)s, emptying his library of [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) and [Grizzly Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Fate). Hegstad had all the time in the world to draw the right spells, and he did so, Sledging away Shvartsman's hand, leaving him with a packed graveyard, no hand, and four [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s in play.

Shvartsman drew a card and said go. Hegstad went to end the game with a [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes), but the one card Shvartsman drew was [Envelop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Envelop).

"That was the best draw ever."

Hegstad put out a [Grotesque Hybrid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grotesque+Hybrid) and hoped to kill Shvartsman before he could recover, but Alex drew a forest on the next turn and started making Wurm tokens.

Hegstad put out the [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) and began the sickening stream of Tutoring, but Shvartsman drew a second forest and started adding bears to his army of wurms. Shvartsman took the offensive, running his array of tokens into the Hybrid and getting Hegstad's life low.

Hegstad had one opportunity to recover: he needed either a Coffers, an Echoes, or a [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) in his hand and had to Tutor/[Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) for the other two. He [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari)'d the Tutor, and got a Coffers and the Echoes, and then took a big hit from Shvartsman's team down to 2 life.

Shvartsman, with one card in hand, chose not to play out any more creatures. Hegstad untapped, [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)d the board away, and then cast [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes) with [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) to play around [Envelop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Envelop). Shvartsman's deck was reduced to a single [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear), two [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest)s, two [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)s, and some land.

He played the [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear) and Hegstad trapped it under a Butcher. If Shvartsman could draw [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), he would with the game with the [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear), as Hegastad was out of [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood)s. But a [Nantuko Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shade) came down and killed Alex in two turns, ending the dream.

**Final Result:** Hegstad 2 – Shvarstman 1
