Deck Tech: Farmville with Kasper Eusen
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 17, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/olle_rade_author.jpg)
By Olle Rade











One of the most exciting decks this weekend has been Dutchman Kasper Eusen's brew built around the interaction between [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) and Thespian's
 Stage. The deck runs a control shell in the form of[Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). But it wins by putting a 20/20 token 
into play with [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths). The beauty of the combo is that [Thespian's Stage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage) an become a copy of Dark 
Depths, but without the counters, allowing it to instantly make an indestructible 20/20 flyer to win the game. Throw in some [Crop Rotation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crop+Rotation)s and it can be 
done at the end of your opponents turn, and [Exploration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration) allows it to happen as early as turn two. "The dream opening hand 
is [Exploration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration), [Crop Rotation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crop+Rotation) and four lands, one of them being either a Thespian's Stage or a Dark 
Depths,", says Kasper Eusen.


He actually assembled just that hand against an unaware opponent who tapped out for [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) on turn two, only to find himself staring down on 
a 20/20 flier at the end of his turn. Eusen managed to go undefeated with a 9-0 record on the first day with the deck he named "Farmville", but his luck seemed to have run out on day 
two, as he picked up three losses quite early, destroying his chances to make the top 8. "I am really happy with how I did the first day, but of course I am disappointed about how I've 
done on day two. But overall it's been a great weekend," he says.

The deck also has a lot of utilities with 4 [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) and 4 [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish). [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) allows him 
to fetch [Dark Depths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths), [Thespian's Stage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage) and [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), acting as a tutor for the entire combo, 
and [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) can fetch both lands and creatures from the sideboard. Ranging from various hate bears against combo decks to underrated invitational cards 
like the [Sylvan Safekeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Safekeeper) to protect his combo.


When asked about the decks worst and best matchups, the 26-year-old didn't have to think long.


"Anything with [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) and [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) can be though if they draw 
them all. And Miracles is a bad matchup since they have both [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) and [Terminus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) to kill your token, 
or [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) to bounce it. Everything else, the deck can handle pretty easily."


Most of his opponents this weekend would probably agree.
