U.S. Regional Championships 2009
================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 12, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_authorpic_larger.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











*All Universal Tournament Rules and Magic: the Gathering Floor Rules are in effect for this event unless specifically overruled by information in this Format Document*

### Date

* May 16, 2009

### Format

  
* Standard-constructed format
* Deck lists are required

### Invitations

  
* Alaska and Hawaii U.S. Regional Championships all award 2 invitations to U.S. Nationals
* All other U.S. Regional Championships award 4 invitations to U.S. Nationals. (Exception: If the total attendance of an individual Regional is 410 players or higher, then the Regional Championship will award 8 invitations to U.S. Nationals)

### Tournament Length

  
* 50 minutes per round
* Number of Swiss rounds based on attendance according to the following chart:   
  


|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Attendance | Number ofSwiss rounds |
| 9 - 16 | 4 |
| 17 - 32 | 5 |
| 33 - 64 | 6 |
| 65 - 128 | 7 |
| 129 - 226 | 8 |
| 227 - 409 | 9 |
| 410 or higher | 10 |

### Advancement

  
* All players may participate in all Swiss rounds
* If a Regional Championship offers 2 or 4 invitations to U.S. Nationals, the top 8 players after the final Swiss round will advance to the top 8 single-elimination finals.
* If a Regional Championship offers 8 invitations to U.S. Nationals, no top 8 single-elimination will be run. Prizes and invitations will be awarded on Swiss standings only.

### Top 8

  
* Top 8 matches will be best 2 of 3
* Top 8 matches will have no time limit. Players are expected to play at a reasonable pace and finish their matches within 50 minutes.
* Regional Championships that offer 4 invitations to U.S. Nationals will have 1 single-elimination round to determine invitation and prizewinners. Regional Championships that offer 2 invitations to U.S. Nationals will have 2 single-elimination rounds to determine invitation and prize winners.
* Where necessary, the standings after the Swiss rounds will still be used to determine final order in the standings

### Notes

  
* 32K, REL Competitive
* Players are responsible for knowing all DCI rules
* Players must bring pen, paper, card sleeves and appropriate counters
