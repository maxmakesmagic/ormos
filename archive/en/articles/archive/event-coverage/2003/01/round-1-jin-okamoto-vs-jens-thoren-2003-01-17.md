Round 1: Jin Okamoto vs. Jens Thoren
====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 17, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











Regarded as one of Japan's best players, perennial day two competitor Jin Okamoto came to beat down, sporting dyed-red hair to match his blisteringly fast Sligh deck. His opponent: none other than stone-faced PT Houston Masters champion Jens Thoren, who packed Mono Black control. 

While both players brought their game face, neither brought a die. After a brief examination of the audience, a ten-sided die was produced. Jin won the crucial roll and chose to go first.

### Game 1

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a296.jpg)Both players counted out seven cards, but Jens left his sitting on the table while Jin decided whether or not to mulligan (he did not). Thoren then examined his own hand, and decided to cash in his seven for a hand of triple [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Tainted Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact), [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother), and [Skeletal Scrying](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Scrying). 

The game swimmingly well for Okamoto, who led with two straight sac lands (thinning his deck significantly), [Raging Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Goblin), [Goblin Taskmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Taskmaster), and [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) all by the end of the second turn. Although Jens smothered a pump Rager on the third turn, he could draw nothing but land after land. Even his [Tainted Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact) betrayed him—it gave him [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), which swung and whiffed into Jin's hand of double [Barbarian Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Ring) and [Blistering Firecat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat).

Speaking of said Firecat, it smashed across the board the following turn, crushing Jens life total to a mere seven on turn four. Thoren top decked [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) to clear out the remaining goblin, but Okamoto reestablished his board quickly with [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) and [Goblin Taskmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Taskmaster). When the European attempted to [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt) the Taskmaster on the following turn, the Japanese player was ready with [Lava Dart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) to kill his own creature, fizzling the [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt), without enough life to survive the next turn between [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt), [Lava Dart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart), and now multiple [Barbarian Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Ring)s, Jens conceded the game.

Jin Okamoto 1 – Jens Thoren 0

Many players would say that stoicism and emotionless expressions are the hallmark traits of Jens Thoren. With this being said, that he was chewing gum during this match would be a cause for reporting here on the sideboard.

Game 2  
 |  
 Continuing his mana woes of the first game, Jens riskily stuck with a hand containing only a single [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), plus [Cabal Coffers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Coffers), Diabolic Edict, [Innocent Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood), [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate), and two [Undead Gladiator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Gladiator)s. On his second turn, he drew [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt), but top-decked land number three on turn three.

On the other side of the world, Jin kept a land-light hand as well, and ended up stuck at only two [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s. This stifled his offensive potential, as his first goblin (a Sledder) died to the Blood, and his next three plays were [Raging Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Goblin), [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) and [Goblin Taskmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Taskmaster)—on consecutive turns. 

Jens couldn't draw a fourth land to save his life, so he cycled his Gladiators to move through his deck. After drawing twin [Engineered Plague](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague)s, he finally got that third [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) that allowed him to clear the board of goblins. He followed this up with his second enchantment, naming Cats this time. 

Not to be deterred, Jin started throwing some hurting straight to the noggin. Twin [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer)s and a [Lava Dart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) brought Jens to a precarious seven life total, and a pair of [Barbarian Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Ring)s loomed on the board. Jens untapped, and drew [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate), buying himself five more life points.

Jin sighed at this turn of events, drew, and passed his turn. Jens spent his turn recurring and cycling Gladiators, his hand full of Edicts, [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother)s and [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)s. At the end of turn, Jin sent [Flaming Gambit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flaming+Gambit) for four across the board, and looked set to win the game with a graveyard of Gambit and a board of double [Barbarian Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Ring). Just when it looked to be over, Thoren ripped [Haunting Echoes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes) off the top of his deck! Jin sighed again, grinned, and paused to consider his plays. In the end, he flashed back [Flaming Gambit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flaming+Gambit) for three, flashed back his Dart (bringing Jens down to four), and flashed back his deck for Jens to peruse. 

Thoren skimmed through the deck, removing all copies of [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer). He left the remaining goblins in his opponent's deck (since they were useless under [Engineered Plague](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague)), and passed the turn.

Fate smiled on Japan's favored son, as Jin untapped, drew a fifth land, and cast [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) at Jens. With five lands and a [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) available the following turn, he looked a shoo-in to win. Jens brought back one of his Gladiators and drew [Tainted Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Pact). He cast the Impulse-a-like, revealing:

[Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)  
[Mesmeric Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mesmeric+Fiend)  
[Cabal Coffers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Coffers)  
[Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)  
 And finally...  
 Another swamp.

With no way to stop the incoming damage, Jens extended his hand to the victorious Jin Okamoto.

**Final Result:** Jin Okamoto 2 – Jens Thoren 0.
