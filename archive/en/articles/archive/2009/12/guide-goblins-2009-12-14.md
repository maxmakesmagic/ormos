A Guide to Goblins
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on December 14, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/hero/wizardslogo_thumb.jpg)
By Dan Paskins












On Earth, few creatures are as insignificant yet annoying as the lowly mosquito. But over in the Multiverse of **Magic**, it is not the mosquitoes that you have to watch out for, it's the Goblins. Generations of blue mages and similar pond life have scorned the puny-looking Goblins, only to find themselves on the receiving end of a beatdown.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/features/feature69_ramGang.jpg)I have fifteen years' experience summoning Goblins for fun and for profit, and there are few things as enjoyable as facing off against someone who has all the rare cards and a superiority complex, and introducing your Goblins to their face.


But although they might all look the same at first glance, it is important for any aspiring mage to understand how to choose the right kind of Goblin to meet their requirements. So I am pleased to introduce some characters from the Goblin Hall of Fame, and to explain when you might have need of the services that they have to offer.


### When You Want to Announce that Something Horrible is about to Happen to Your Opponent


We're going to start by rewinding to 1996. It was the first year of the Pro Tour, when Standard used to be called Type 2, and it was the era of the first great **Magic** deck: the [Necropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropotence) deck. Using a whole host of cards that would later be banned as too powerful, black mages would destroy their opponents' hands with [Hymn to Tourach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach) and [Hypnotic Specter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter), attack them with the [Order of the Ebon Hand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Order+of+the+Ebon+Hand), and then suddenly draw seven or more cards in one turn with the [Necropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropotence).


Alternatives such as white-blue control or green-white "[Erhnam Djinn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Erhnam%2BDjinn)[Armageddon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Armageddon)" simply couldn't compete.


But the [Goblins of the Flarg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblins+of+the+Flarg) could.



![Goblins of the Flarg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblins+of+the+Flarg)

 Â 

 Players who had jumped on the [Necropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropotence) bandwagon and were sure that they had the best deck met their match when a couple of brave inventors came up with the "Sligh" deck.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/features/feature69_flargSlice.jpg)The [Necropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Necropotence) mages would soon come to know that something horrible was about to happen to them when their opponents summoned a [Goblin of the Flarg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+of+the+Flarg), and then possibly an [Ironclaw Orcs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ironclaw+Orcs) or two. The [Necropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Necropotence) deck's creatures would be [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)ed or [Incinerate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate)d, and the Goblins, Orcs, and even Dwarves would continue their attacks (while the Sligh player tried to remember not to summon a Dwarf when the [Goblins of the Flarg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblins+of+the+Flarg) were around).


The "janky" (that's the way they used to describe decks full of seemingly bad cards back in those days) Sligh deck consistently beat decks which in theory had much more powerful cards in them.

**When You Want a Helpful Goblin** 


Fast forwarding to the present, *Zendikar* brought us the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide). The [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) is a very helpful Goblinâhe comes out on turn one and immediately starts attacking for 2. And you get to find out what their next card is!



![Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Guide)

 Â 

 The [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) also a very helpful Goblin for your opponentâhe lets them have extra lands in their hand if they are lucky.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/features/feature69_guideSlice.jpg)Mike Flores wrote [a great article](http://fivewithflores.com/2009/11/five-with-goblin-guide/) recently about the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) on his own site, FiveWithFlores.com.


The key thing to remember about the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), and cards like it, is that you might think that whether or not you win is down to your own skill and that of your opponent, but there are some creatures who take it on themselves to decide for themselves who is going to win the game. If your Guide feels like it, you'll get to attack for 2 every time and see the next card your opponent draws. Other times, your opponent will be desperately needing land to cast their spells, and the Guide will give it to them. He's in charge, not you.


### When You Want to Kill a Lot of Elves


Is there anything more fun than making your opponent put an Elf in their graveyard? Of course there is: making them put lots of Elves in their graveyard! And when you absolutely, positively, just have to wipe out a whole tribe of Elves, you need the [Goblin Sharpshooter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter).



![Goblin Sharpshooter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Sharpshooter)

 An honourable mention should go to such sadomasochists as the [Goblin Artillery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Artillery) and the Sparksmith, each of which offer you the pleasure of killing an Elf at the expensive of some of your life, but they can only kill one Elf per turn.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/features/feature69_sharpshooterSlice.jpg)Nate Heiss used to write Building on a Budget, and it's worth quoting [what he said](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Article.aspx?x=magic/magiconline/articlenh052703) about the Sharpshooter:

> Other cool interactions involve [Goblin Sharpshooter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter) and ***anything***. Sharpshooter is cool and useful no matter what the game situation. And while [Goblin Sharpshooter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter) works well with many of the cards in the deck, note that it has an interesting interaction with both [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) and [Goblin Turncoat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Turncoat). I think many games can be won simply on the merit of the Sharpshooter, assuming you use it correctly.


You'll probably have heard blue mages gloating that card advantage is the key to winning games, and that their colour is the best at drawing extra cards. It's worth reminding them that a Goblin who can kill a number of Elves or other creatures can generate card advantage just as quick as any blue card-drawing spell.

### When You Want a Banned Goblin


To date, there have been two Goblins who the Powers that Be decided were too powerful and which players in one or more Constructed formats were not allowed to play with. Do know which they were, and which one is still banned?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/features/feature69_lackeySlice.jpg)The first was the [Goblin Lackey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey). When it was banned, Wizards of the Coast [explained that](http://archive.wizards.com/Default.asp?x=dci/announce/dci20030901a) *"The Extended Constructed format has gotten too fast. One of the biggest culprits is the Goblin deck and [Goblin Lackey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey) is the most egregious offender. The introduction of the* Onslaught *block (and especially the* Scourge *set) has given Goblin decks some extremely high quality Goblins and the Lackey's ability to put them into play for free is simply too good for a first turn play."*



![Goblin Lackey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Lackey)

 Â 

 The first-turn [Goblin Lackey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey) led to all kinds of pain for opponents, such as second-turn [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander), [Goblin Warchief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief), or [Goblin Piledriver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piledriver), on top of actually casting any Goblins for two mana.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/features/feature69_warrenSlice.jpg)We will wait to see whether the [Warren Instigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warren+Instigator), which trades an extra ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) to cast for the ability to double strike, will end up having anything like a similar effect.



![Warren Instigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Warren+Instigator)

### When You Want a Goblin That Can Do Anything


There are other, more powerful Goblins, as we have seen. But if you want the Goblin who has got it all, you need the [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic), a.k.a. Mogg *Fantastic*.



![Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mogg+Fanatic)

 Â 

 The Fanatic symbolises the Goblin tribe. A simple 1/1 for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) with a seemingly straightforward ability, and yet with hidden depths and subtleties, all caused when it decides to blow itself up.


Since it was first unveiled in *Tempest*, the Fanatic has more Top 8 Pro Tour finishes than a Level 6 pro, has killed more creatures than a [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), and has dealt the finishing blow to more players than a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/features/feature69_fanaticSlice.jpg)You want to know how good [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) is? It's so good they changed how combat damage worked because Mark Rosewater was fed up with [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) blocking and killing one of his creatures, and then sacrificing itself to kill another one. Oh, sure, that may not be the "official" reason ... but then, the absence of [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) from the Japanese National Championships one year actually altered the space-time continuum itself, as you can read on StarCityGames.com [here](http://www.starcitygames.com/magic/misc/12319_Selecting_10th_Edition_Mogg_Fanatic.html).


So whether you are looking for a big or a fast Goblin, whether your priority is the noble task of slaughtering Elves or informing your opponent that something horrible is about to happen, then provided you can put up with the smell, you'll be able to find what you need.


I hope you've found this round-up of Goblins old and new useful, and that you are able to find the Goblin that's right for you, whatever your requirements.


Until next time, may your [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)s never reveal land.
