England WMCQ Winning Decklist
=============================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on September 4, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 







[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Master%20of%20Waves)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master%20of%20Waves)


 This past weekend, the second round of WMCQs took place around the world. Spots on all countries' World *Magic* Cup teams were won, and now there is only one spot for each country left up for grabs. If you would like to earn one of these spots, check out [the WMCQ schedule](http://magic.wizards.com/en/wmcq/schedule) and make plans to attend your country's final WMCQ for the year!




 In England, Francesco Giorgio won his way onto the English World *Magic* Cup team. His deck of choice was Mono-Blue Devotion, which has been one of the strongest decks in Standard since its breakout performance back at [Pro Tour *Theros*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptths13/welcome). As this Standard season winds down, Mono-Blue Devotion remains a threat to win any tournament with the power of [Thassa, God of the Sea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) and [Master of Waves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Waves). Looking ahead, will *Khans of Tarkir* have enough devotion enablers to replace cards such as [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) and [Nightveil Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightveil+Specter)? Or will Blue Devotion fail to maintain the success it has had this year?
