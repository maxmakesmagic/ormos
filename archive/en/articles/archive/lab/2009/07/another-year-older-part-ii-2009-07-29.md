Once again, for those just dropping in on this fun Johnny activity, the skinny on the contest itself can be found in [this link](/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/41). ![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl49_calendar.jpg)  
All right, now that everyone's caught up on the basics, let's dive into the final eight decks. If you recall, six of these are part of an internal Top Ten List, while the other two are just quirky decks that I liked enough for them to make the article.

In fact, let's start with one of these quirkier decks, and then head into the Top 10 list. Sound good?

The Decks
While grading the 238 submissions, I laughed at the many odd combinations of cards that some were stuck with. Next to these crazy six-card grab bags, though, were some birthday dates that, due to weekends or holidays, only yielded three or four cards. These particular decks were, obviously, easier to build, as the restriction level was lesser. However, I couldn't just discount these submissions based on this level; that wouldn't be fair.

Out of the couple of these decks, I particularly liked Adam Simpson's deck for December 24.

**Cards:**[Jackal Pup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup), Repentant Blacksmith, [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast), [Decree of Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Justice)

Here's Adam's deck description:

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl49_2cards1.jpg)  

> Great stuff for a RW weenie deck, except that darn [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast). It will sure be fun to play on [Decree of Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Justice) – but it will be hard to cast both! My solution? [Windbrisk Heights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights)! Once [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast) is hidden away, you can use it to double [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) or [Roar of the Kha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Kha) for some extra damage. You can make 4 tokens off of a retraced [Cenn's Enlistment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cenn%27s+Enlistment), make 6 tokens with [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession), or however many tokens you can off the Decree! If you get stuck with those [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast)s in your hand, feed them to [Gathan Raiders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gathan+Raiders). You can even feed [Cenn's Enlistment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cenn%27s+Enlistment) to the morpher without losing much steam. For mana I provided [Reflecting Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool)s, because I need other ways to play [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast). To round it out I put in [Balefire Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balefire+Liege) and [Glory of Warfare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory+of+Warfare) for some extra oomph.
> 
>

The Top Ten Birthday Decks: Number 6
![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl49_6.jpg)  
A quick aside on the Top 6: Each of these six decks is in my eyes exceptional enough to actually win this contest. In the end, only one could win. If your creation made this list, clap yourself on the back for me, because your submission contained enough synergy and charisma to win me over.

Here we go. At number 6 is Kenyon Colloran's deck for June 11, which uses the required cards in great interactive ways.

Kenyon had this to say:


> The first thing that jumped out at me was the synergy between [Phyrexian Scuta](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Scuta) and Bargain. Life gain and life payment naturally go together well, so I thought a bunch of cards that hit those themes would work well. And since I had [Skyship Weatherlight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyship+Weatherlight) I could put a lot of one of artifacts in the deck to facilitate that. Then I looked for ways to utilize [Wormfang Newt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Newt) and found a great combo, [Shelldock Isle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelldock+Isle). With [Skyship Weatherlight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyship+Weatherlight) I can turn on my isles as soon as I play the Weatherlight. And after that I can play newts that remove the Shelldocks [after using them] so if the Newts die I get another free card. The artifact lands are added to increase the number of cards I can grab with Weatherlight so that I can leave some business spells in the deck for my Isles.
>

Woo hoo! A creature that does stuff to artifacts? Which also gives me a way to control [Timesifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timesifter)? YAH!
> 
> **Sub-theme 1 – [Timesifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timesifter):** Take an extra turn FTW, but what if I don't have the right card? Well I'll blow it with the Sage, but what is a lot better is to know when I'm going to win this coin toss, so for that I bring in [Lantern of Insight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) that can also be sacced to the Sage. So that's a start.

I'm going to start with [Sporesower Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sporesower+Thallid) to increase the efficiency of my Saproling engine, putting an extra token makes my engine that must faster. Brett's combo checklist:


> 1. Using [Auramancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer) enchanted with [Flickerform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerform). You can use the Seals to destroy permanents or discard enchantments to [Knollspine Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knollspine+Invocation) to deal damage to creatures/players, then recurse the enchantments by [Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flicker)ing [Auramancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer).
> 
> 

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130626184952im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl49_2Cards3.jpg)  

> 2. [Baron Sengir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baron+Sengir) enchanted with [Farrel's Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farrel%27s+Mantle) gives you a tough choice of destroying an opponent's creature and growing the vampire, or dealing damage to the player. [Myr Quadropod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Quadropod) also works well with the Mantle.
> 
> 3. [Dizzying Gaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dizzying+Gaze), in conjunction with [Updraft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Updraft), means that you can kill your opponents' blockers before swinging in (again growing the Baron), or you can just use the [Updraft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Updraft) to make your [Myr Quadropod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Quadropod) (with [Farrel's Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farrel%27s+Mantle), preferably) unblockable.
> 
> 4. If all else fails, you try and build up to a massive late game [Rise of the Hobgoblins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+of+the+Hobgoblins) followed by a [Path of Anger's Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+of+Anger%27s+Flame) with [Anger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger) in the graveyard for a mass of 3/1 haste guys.
> 
>

> ![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl49_confusion.jpg)[Bushi Tenderfoot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bushi+Tenderfoot) likes to deal damage to creatures. [Stalking Yeti](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Yeti) makes creatures deal damage to it. A natural (and sufficiently Johnnyish) starting point for this deck is to flip [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bushi+Tenderfoot) by letting it kill [Stalking Yeti](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Yeti). Unfortunately, there are many obstacles to doing this, not the least of which is the Yeti's "an opponent controls" clause. To get around this, I added [Confusion in the Ranks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confusion+in+the+Ranks) (which is thematically appropriate given many of my birthday parties). If you use [Confusion in the Ranks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confusion+in+the+Ranks) to give your opponent [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bushi+Tenderfoot), then play [Stalking Yeti](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Yeti), you can stack the triggers so that you swap the Yeti for one of your opponent's creatures, then have it fight the Tenderfoot. If the Tenderfoot wins, you can then play another creature and take back the newly flipped [Kenzo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kenzo). (You can't swap the Yeti and Tenderfoot directly, since the Tenderfoot will be an illegal target for the Yeti trigger if you swap it first, and the Yeti won't be in play to exchange if you kill it off first.) Of course, the Tenderfoot needs a little help in order to win the fight, so I added several pump spells that can either help it kill the Yeti or pump its power after it's flipped.
> 
> Teferi is a double-edged sword here: he prevents your opponent from disrupting the combo, but he's not a creature you want to have your opponent steal with [Confusion in the Ranks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confusion+in+the+Ranks). Elspeth comes to the rescue. As a planeswalker, she's immune to [Confusion in the Ranks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confusion+in+the+Ranks), and is equally happy to make a token (stealing back [Kenzo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kenzo), Teferi, or whatever other creature your opponent took) or provide you with another pump spell. Plus, her ultimate is crazy with [Ali from Cairo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ali+from+Cairo).
> 
> Even if you can't pull off the full combo, there are synergies built in. For example, you may be able to flip [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bushi+Tenderfoot) just by killing an attacking or blocking creature with the aid of a pump spell. With [Confusion in the Ranks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confusion+in+the+Ranks) in play (even with no [Bushi Tenderfoot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bushi+Tenderfoot) in sight), [Stalking Yeti](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Yeti) can sometimes be a three-for-one. [Wildfire Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildfire+Emissary) isn't particularly synergistic with the combo, but can help to facilitate the Plan B of straight creature beatdown.
>

> Here is how it works: During the early game, play some slivers. The Gemhides fix the mana for the deck's four colors, and the Metallics are just really easy to play. The [Screeching Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Sliver)s allow you to start milling yourself, which gives you card advantage through the tons of flashback/retrace in the deck. ([Index](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Index) helps this by seeing and selecting what you will mill.) Many of the flashback cards load you up with token creatures, and you can use all of these to charge up [City of Shadows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Shadows) to tons of mana over time. This mana is used to get out a gigantic [Shifting Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shifting+Wall), and then smash face with [Rolling Stones](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Stones). (If either of these last two cards accidentally get milled, you can [Regrowth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regrowth) them back.)
