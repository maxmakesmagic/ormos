Evolving Event Decks: Into the Breach
=====================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on March 16, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Into the Breach, a *Mirrodin Besieged* Event Deck, is competitive directly out of the box. The deck bears some resemblance to Kuldotha Red, the most aggressive strategy available in the current Standard. Today, I'm going to walk you through an evolution of this powerful preconstructed deck.

It's easy to see how powerful this strategy can be. Your opponent will be dead on turn three or four with a surprising level of consistency. The deck can be more powerful than ever if you make a few small changes.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab134_bush.jpg)  
The first thing I'd like to do here is add the third and fourth [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker). Bushwhacker is perhaps the most powerful spell in this archetype. It's a common from *Zendikar*, so it shouldn't be difficult to acquire an extra two copies. I'd cut the [Spikeshot Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Elder) and a [Galvanic Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) for the extra copies of [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker). [Spikeshot Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spikeshot+Elder) is excellent in many red decks, but I'd like to make this deck as aggressive as possible.

![Goblin Bushwhacker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Bushwhacker)  
[Darksteel Axe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Axe) presents an interesting dilemma. I'd like to maximize the number of creatures we play in this deck, but I don't want to risk not playing enough artifacts. [Flayer Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+Husk) seems like a good replacement in this spot. The new *Mirrodin Besieged* Equipment fulfills all of our conditions of satisfaction. The 1/1 for one mana is going to do a lot more work than its power and toughness may lead you to believe. Cards like [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker), [Goblin Wardriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Wardriver), [Contested War Zone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone), and [Signal Pest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) will make everybody into an extraordinarily powerful threat.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab134_cardOverCard1.jpg)  
Speaking of [Contested War Zone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone), this land is the realest of deals. I must admit that I was skeptical the first time a friend spoke about the power level of this card. It didn't take more than a game or two to evangelize me, though. I found that the "drawback" on [Contested War Zone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone) was irrelevant. The level of aggression presented by this deck makes attacking a huge liability for your opponent. It's hard to pull the trigger and get in the red zone when your opponent is threatening a lethal swingback as early as the third turn. I'd like to add an additional three copies of this card. There was a lot of demand for [Contested War Zone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone) the first week after *Mirrodin Besieged* was released, but that seems to have died down a bit. It shouldn't be too difficult to get your paws on an additional three copies.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab134_3Warzones.jpg)  
As we move the deck in a faster direction, the [Iron Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr) and last copy of [Galvanic Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) seem a little out of place to me. I really want to get value out of my battle cry creatures, [Contested War Zone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+War+Zone), and [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker), so I don't really want to draw more than one non creature per game. [Chimeric Mass](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chimeric+Mass) seems like it could be an excellent replacement for these cards. [Chimeric Mass](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chimeric+Mass) is also another artifact that lets us cast our [Kuldotha Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Rebirth) on the first turn; I can't stress the importance of this enough.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab134_cardOverCard2.jpg)  
I'm very happy with the list we've arrived at now. Let's take a look at the new main deck:

This sideboard has a lot of game against a completely random metagame, but that's not what we're looking for anymore. We want ways to combat cards like [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb), [Slagstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm), and [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab134_myr.jpg)  
[Perilous Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr) is an excellent way to snipe away [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker)s. We can even sacrifice it to our Kuldotha rebirth to deal with the Firewalker and make three 1/1s in the process. [Unstable Footing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unstable+Footing) is a fine answer to [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker), but the high kicker cost makes it difficult to justify playing it over [Perilous Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr). It's also nice that [Perilous Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr) is an extra creature to increase the power level of your anthem effects. Two copies of [Brittle Effigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brittle+Effigy) will round out my ways to deal with a [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker).

![Perilous Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Perilous+Myr)  
[Tuktuk the Explorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tuktuk+the+Explorer) is an excellent answer to the board sweepers, the mirror, or decks like Boros that have trouble battling through a 5/5 body. There hasn't been a lot of demand for this guy, so he can usually be acquired with relative ease.

![Tuktuk the Explorer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tuktuk+the+Explorer)  
[Myr Sire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Sire) continues in the footsteps of [Tuktuk the Explorer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tuktuk+the+Explorer). For one less mana, you get an artifact that can be sacrificed to [Kuldotha Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Rebirth). [Myr Sire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Sire) is a great way to increase your chances against the board sweepers. I've also won a few games with the mini-combo of [Myr Sire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Sire), [Goblin Bushwhacker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bushwhacker), and [Kuldotha Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Rebirth). Attacking for 10 damage out of nowhere is a scary thing for any opponent.

I'd like to round out the sideboard with a playset of [Arc Trail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail). [Arc Trail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail) is a great way to clear the way for your horde against decks that intend on gumming up the table with cards like [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) and [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic).
