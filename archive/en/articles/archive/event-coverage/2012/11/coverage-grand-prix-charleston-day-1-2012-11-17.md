Saturday, 10:47 a.m. – The Evolution of Standard
------------------------------------------------


*by Jacob Van Lunen*

Hello and welcome to Grand Prix Charleston. Today, we're going to watch 661 players test their mental agility and endurance in an effort to make it to the second day of competition in this Standard Grand Prix. *Return to Ravnica* Standard has been in a constant state of evolution. Let's take a look at how this format developed over the last month and a half.


*Return to Ravnica* Standard was blazingly fast in the beginning. Red decks and aggressive [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) decks were the top dogs. Neither of these decks were particularly good at blocking, so the decks quickly became geared toward reducing the opponent's life total from twenty to zero in the shortest time frame possible, throwing caution to the wind.

Most players recognized the excessively aggressive nature of the format and decided to take a step back and slow things down. Players began ramping into [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and filling their Graveyards with [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage)/[Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) in an effort to [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) targeting [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) or [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight). The presence of [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) and [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) in tandem was enough to push the most aggressive decks out of the spotlight. By late October the format was all about [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites).

It didn't take long before [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) and [Purify the Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purify+the+Grave) began to show up in everyone's sideboards. The [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) strategies were still strong, albeit slow, and players needed to go one step slower to give themselves a better matchup against the tier one deck of the time. Green/White/Blue Control strategies, often splashing a fourth color, became the flavor of the week. The deck aimed to beat the [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) decks by controlling the board with Planeswalkers like [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage) while burying the opponent in card advantage with [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation).

Planeswalker control decks remained dominant for the better part of two weeks, but players in the know quickly recognized the strength of countermagic and Blue/White Control decks began their rise to power. The Blue/White Control deck can play a stifling control game, passing the turn and countering all the important spells played by the opponent. When given an opening, the deck begins to apply pressure with cards like [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) lets the deck close games very quickly, often in two attacks. Against more controlling decks the inevitability of [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) in conjunction with [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) is usually enough to grind out the game.

Saturday, 4:10 p.m. - Freighthopping with Christian Calcano
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Jacob van Lunen*
Christian Calcano has been hanging onto the train Indiana Jones style since 2010. In grave danger of falling onto the tracks after a disappointing performance at Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored*, Calcano has managed to reestablish himself as the real deal with a win at Grand Prix Minneapolis and top 25 finish at Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*. I had the opportunity to sit down with the most recent North American Standard Grand Prix Champion and pick his brain.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha12/GPChar%20Christian%20Calcano.JPG)*Christian Calcano*




- You had a pretty good run at Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*. Did that finish encourage you to continue chasing the train or were you going to be traveling to all the events this year regardless?


"I don't know about all of them. That finish definitely encouraged me to go to more events. I was definitely going to come here, but I probably wouldn't have gone to Chicage or San Antonio. Now I'm going to everything."


- You won the most recent Standard Grand Prix in America. How do you make a deck choice for a tournament this large?


"Well, I looked to see what decks were doing well. I wanted to play a deck that could do well against the field and a deck that I would enjoy."


- What do you think is the most underrated card in Standard right now?


"Definitely [Slaughter Games](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games). A lot of decks, at least those that have been doing well, they have one card that swings the game in their favor. Being able to get rid of their best card gives you a better chance of winning."


- You've had a reasonable amount of success on the Pro level. You worked for a long time to get where you are today. Do you have advice for up and coming PTQ grinders that are trying to get on the train?


"My best advice is to play in as many high level tournaments as you can."


Christian Calcano is on the rise and hunting for a spot in the 2013 Players' Championship. Round 4 Feature Match - David Ochoa (Blue White) vs. Korey McDuffie (Blue White Red)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin*
Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* semifinalist David Ochoa came into Grand Prix Charleston sporting what many top pros consider to be the deck to beat at this event – Blue White Control.


His opponent for this round, Grand Prix Dallas/Fort Worth Top 8 alum Korey McDuffie is also playing a base Blue White deck. But, rather than stick to straight Azorius, McDuffie chose to splash red for additional removal spells and [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite)s that he thought would improve his matchup against aggressive decks.


### Game 1


Ochoa won the roll and opened with a Runechanter's Pike on turn two, but neglected to follow it up with any plays for the following few turns. McDuffie meanwhile, got stuck on three lands, and when he went to discard Ochoa took the opportunity to cycle a [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), and an [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm). At this point, rather than discard, McDuffie decided to cast a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage).


Ochoa let the [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) resolve, and continued making his land drops while the mana starved McDuffie could only attack for 2 a turn. By the time McDuffie finally found his 4th land, Ochoa was in a very good spot with 6 lands of his own and a hand full of spells.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha12/R4%20FM%20Korey%20McDuffie.JPG)*Korey McDuffi*




Ochoa, now flush with lands and consequently unconcerned with the thought of tapping a little bit of mana cast an end of turn [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), flashing back [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour). Still on his own end step, McDuffie used a [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) to kill the [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) while Ochoa cast two more [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s to help him fill up his graveyard with instants and sorceries for his Runechanter's Pike.


Ochoa then untapped, and pushed an [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) past an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) with [Rewind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind) before passing his turn. When McDuffie tried for a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), Ochoa was ready with an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter).


At this point, Ochoa could have equipped his Runechanter's Pike to his [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) and attempted to kill McDuffie as quickly as possible. But he didn't


The ever patient Ochoa, wanting to ensure that he had the mana to play around any answer that McDuffie could have, waited until he had 8 lands to equip Runechanter's Pike to his [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas). McDuffie, not wanting to take a huge hit, tried for a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) that would have allowed him to flash back his [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear). But Ochoa had a [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) – allowing him to set up an attack for 8 that knocked McDuffie down to 9.


A turn later, McDuffie tried for another [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). Ochoa allowed this one to resolve, but in response to the [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) he cast a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) that saved his [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas).


At a mere 9 life, and staring down a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) and a Runechanter's Pike, the best that McDuffie could hope to do was to try to buy himself a little bit of time by playing a [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) that he would have to immediately chump block with. But an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) prevented him from doing even that, giving Ochoa a one game lead.


David Ochoa 1 – Korey McDuffie 0


### Game 2


Ochoa's Runechanter's Pike was once again the first non-[Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)/[Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) card that either player played in the second game. However, Ochoa didn't play it until he had 6 lands.


Knowing how deadly the Runechanter's Pike can be in this matchup, McDuffie tried to exile it with a [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere). And while Ochoa had a Faith's Shield to attempt to protect it, McDuffie had the [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) that he needed to win the counter fight.


Ochoa tried for an end of turn [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) a turn later – but McDuffie had an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter). Another [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) on the following turn met an identical fate.


Ochoa's [Talrand, Sky Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%2C+Sky+Summoner) was allowed to resolve, and he was even able to successfully protect it (while simultaneously generating a token) from a [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) with a [Rewind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha12/R4%20FM%20David%20Ochoa.JPG)*David Ochoa*




McDuffie, now staring down 4 power worth of attackers, used an end of turn [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) to draw a card, before casting an [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) which Ochoa Responded to with a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel).


McDuffie wiped the board with a [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), and then deployed his first threat of the game -- a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). After taking a couple of hits from the [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), Ochoa drew a [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) that he had more than ample fuel for in his graveyard.


Once Ochoa was up to 3 tokens (and down to 5 life), McDuffie cast an overloaded [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) that Ochoa countered with [Rewind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind) (a play which allowed Ochoa to generate yet another token with his [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt)). Ochoa untapped, attacked with his fliers, and promptly passed the turn back.


McDuffie then tried for another [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) on the following turn – and Ochoa responded with a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). McDuffie attempted to counter the wizard with an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) – but Ochoa was able to force it through with a [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel). Ochoa then flashed back a [Rewind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind), giving himself the mana that he needed to [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) McDuffie's follow up play of [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere).


Ochoa then played an [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) which found him a [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) that he immediately cast for 6. It seemed like Ochoa had McDuffie, who was now at 3, right where he wanted him...


....at least until McDuffie drew a [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) of his own that he used to draw himself 5 new cards – including a [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) which cleared away Ochoa's 4 spirit tokens.


After sticking the [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere), McDuffie attacked with his [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), and passed the turn with only a [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress) untapped...


....giving Ochoa just the opening that he needed for his Runechanter's Pike to take the match.


David Ochoa 2 – Korey McDuffie 0

Sunday, 4:35 p.m. - Drafting with Shouta Yasooka
------------------------------------------------


*by Marc Calderaro*
Beating [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) with Control


by Steve Sadin


Blue control decks have been making their presence felt in *Return to Ravnica* Standard. But as decks with [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter)s, [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate)s, [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate)s, and even [Rewind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind)s have risen to prominence, players have inevitably begun to look for answers. Enter: [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls).





![Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cavern+of+Souls)



But despite the fact that [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) has been steadily growing in popularity, many of the top pros in attendance still opted to play Blue control decks chock full of counterspells this weekend.


Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* Semifinalist, David Ochoa, was one of those players – choosing to shuffle up a Blue White control deck that (at least on the surface) looks like it would be particularly vulnerable to uncounterable threats. However, at 6-0-1 going into the 8th round, it's clear that [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) hasn't gotten him down yet.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcha12/David%20Ochoa.JPG)*David Ochoa*





![Supreme Verdict++Talrand, Sky Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Supreme%20Verdict&options=)
![Supreme Verdict++Talrand, Sky Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Talrand,%20Sky%20Summoner&options=)

"[Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) is definitely an issue, but this deck is cool. Didn't you see all the cool stuff that I got to do (during my Round 4 Feature match)? I drew so many extra cards!"


"If they have [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls), you obviously can't counter the creatures – so you need to rely on other answers. Against the [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) control decks, I hope to have a Runechanter's Pike, and a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) to invalidate their plan. Against a card like [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), you generally need a removal spell. And against the aggressive decks you can board in a bunch of removal, and use your counterspells on their non-creature spells."


"[Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) is probably the most important card for me against the aggressive decks – but [Talrand, Sky Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%2C+Sky+Summoner) is also very good. [Talrand, Sky Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%2C+Sky+Summoner) is the key card that the Blue White deck was not playing before this week, that I think makes it a lot better."


Not even the dominating power of [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) was enough to sway Ochoa away from his beloved counterspells this weekend. However he did bias his sideboard considerably out of respect for the card – giving himself the option to board out most of his counterspells when he needed to.


"It depends on how many non-creatures spells they're playing. The fewer spell-spells they have, the more counters I'm going to board out in favor of proactive threats and [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s."


Not playing Blue White? Don't despair! According to Ochoa every color combination has ways to mitigate the impact of [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls).


"If you don't have access to white mana for [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), you can also play a sweeper like [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars). Or Planeswalkers like [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage) and Jace Architect of Thought. Or you can play your own creatures that match up with theirs, [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s tend to be pretty good at that. Or you can hope that they don't draw their [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls)..."


"There's also [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter), but I hate [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter). You end up falling behind on lands, and that's just never been good for me in the past. I mean, maybe it's good now, but I'm skeptical."


So even though [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) makes life relatively difficult for Blue control players, that doesn't mean that you can't find ways to beat decks with it. You just need to keep an open mind, stock up on sweepers (and other removals spells) and bring enough proactive threats to make sure that you can kill your opponent before they inevitably take over with their uncounterable threats.
