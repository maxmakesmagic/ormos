Bloodcrank
==========



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on June 15, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






N*ew Phyrexia* has brought combo back into the Standard spotlight. It seems impossible to pilot your way through an FNM these days without running into at least one copy of that pesky [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) deck—and [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) isn't the only new combo deck out there.

I've mentioned using [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) with [Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank) on a few separate occasions here. To me, this seems like it has the potential to be very strong if our opponents don't come well prepared. Looking at recent Standard lists, it doesn't look like many people are packing cards like [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil) or [Kor Sanctifiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Sanctifiers). This puts our [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) deck into a pretty good spot.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab147_mindcrank.jpg)  
I'm not particularly thrilled with casting red cards in the new Standard if I'm not piloting a full-blown Pat Sullivan–style mono-red aggro deck; [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) is a huge problem and, thanks to [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), many opponents have access to it in almost every game. Fortunately, this deck has an effective answer to opponents that attain very high life totals. That answer is [Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank).

[Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank) may look like a bad card—and in most cases it is—but I've certainly played worse in the name of achieving a combo. How does the combo work? If you have an active [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) in play alongside [Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank), your opponent will lose his or her entire library the moment he or she takes any damage or has any cards go to the graveyard. Even something as mundane as casting a [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) will take your opponents to meet their doom. Each time the [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) trigger resolves, it will put more of the [Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank) trigger on the stack. Each [Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank) trigger will give you some Bloodchief triggers. It takes a lot of clicking if you're playing on **Magic Online**, but the RTS player inside of me can't help but think that's actually a good thing.

So we have a combo. Now we need to build a deck that's designed to successfully achieve our goal of activating an ascension. In the past we've seen [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) decks that are packed with nothing but burn spells. Burn spells are the easiest way to activate our Ascension at a very early stage of the game.

I want to make sure we have some consistent sources of damage for the games where we have to use our burn spells to deal with our opponent's creatures. [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) seems like the best fit here. [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) provides a constant stream of aggression, and its landfall ability makes it very powerful against decks like Blue-Red-Green (RUG) and [Valakut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut).

![Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bloodghast)  
[Manic Vandal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal) is another 2-power creature that I'd like to play. This deck doesn't love seeing a [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) on the other side of the table, and [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) can be obnoxious if we're not going for a quick combo. I've resisted for a while now, but I think it's time to put this gentleman in your starting 60 cards if you're planning on fighting with a red deck.

![Manic Vandal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Manic+Vandal)  
I want to play a healthy dose of card draw too. I often see decks like this that try to get away with playing a pile of burn spells and a combo. But in addition to helping us find our combo, card draw is already very synergistic with burn spells. If your opponent uses all their interactive spells to prevent you from comboing out, then you can simply kill them with [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s and a hand full of burn.

[Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit) is exceptionally strong in this type of strategy. Not only do we get to draw two cards, but we get to charge our [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) midturn. This gives the deck a new angle of attack that opponents may not expect. Stronger players may wait until you're about to go to three counters on your end step so they can bounce the Ascension with an [Into the Roil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil). Having access to [Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit) forces your opponent to play a straightforward game.

![Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit)  
[Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) is another exceptional draw spell for this type of strategy. Most of the time you're just going to be drawing two cards, but [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) can be pretty sweet when you need that final burn spell to activate your ascension and mill your opponent out with a [Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank).

![Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sign+in+Blood)  
Let's talk about our burn!

[Staggershock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staggershock) is staggeringly good in this type of strategy. For the low cost of three mana, you can kill two creatures, kill a single creature and put a counter on your Ascension, or just put a pair of counters on your Ascension. When you're not comboing an opponent, you can simply burn their face for 4 with the [Staggershock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staggershock).

![Staggershock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Staggershock)  
[Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) is an obvious inclusion. I can also include a few copies of [Burst Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) if I want more than four one-mana burn spells.

[Hideous End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+End) does very well as a removal spell that also charges up our [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension). It's a shame it can't kill the Germ that [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) is usually attached to, but it's a very good card at taking down [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s that have been equipped with [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace). A lot of opponents may think you're cold to a Sworded creature with protection from red and white. This gives the deck huge potential for blowouts.

![Hideous End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hideous+End)  
[Volt Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volt+Charge) continues the theme of burn spells that do a lot of work. [Volt Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volt+Charge) is especially nice because it's an instant with proliferate. This makes your [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension) very unpredictable. (I'm starting to see a theme here.)

![Volt Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Volt+Charge)  
I played around with some lists and decided that I wanted a couple copies of [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) for cards like Gideon jura.

After some number crunching I arrived at the following list:

I decided to play some games with the deck to see how it fared against some of the more popular decks in the field.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab147_match1Header.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab147_lacerator.jpg)I lost the roll and kept [Akoum Refuge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Refuge), [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds), [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), [Bloodchief Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension), [Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit), [Staggershock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staggershock), and [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast). This deck fares very well against creature decks. The card draw spells really make this deck perform a lot better than many builds I've seen floating around on the internet. If you can get the dual lands then you should probably have a copy or two of [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) and some fetch lands to help get landfall triggers for your [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s and [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze)s. I wouldn't be against going down to two copies of [Mindcrank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindcrank), because they're pretty bad in your opening hand and you never really want to draw multiples. The deck is very capable of winning without them.

If you're looking for a budget combo deck that has the ability to compete in the new Standard then this may be the right list for you. Put the deck together—I'm sure you won't be disappointed.
