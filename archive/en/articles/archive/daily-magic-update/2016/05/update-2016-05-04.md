Top 25
======


1. Mike Sigrist Leapfrogs Three

Mike Sigrist, last season's Player of the Year, was the lone mover this week, jumping three spots after his 15th-place finish at [Grand Prix Toronto](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gptor16). It doesn't look like much, but when the point totals are this tight (Sigrist is ahead of No. 18 Lukas Blohon by a mere .04 points), a 15th-place finish looks pretty good.


The rest of the Top 25 was quiet, as the only others who saw any movement were the three Sigrist jumped. But with [another Grand Prix this weekend](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/premierplay/grandprix/fact-sheet-gpny16), don't expect them to stay quiet for very long.

The venue for Grand Prix Madrid, taking place December 2-4, is being changed in order to accommodate a potentially larger turnout. The Grand Prix will *now* take place at the Madrid Arena. The address can be found below:

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Cryptolith/Displacer Combo](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2016-05-03)

What is "it" exactly? Why, it's the three-card infinite-life-loss combo in Standard people have talked about since [Eldrazi Displacer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Displacer) was previewed. And all it took was [Cryptolith Rite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptolith+Rite)s to make it happen.


So the basic combo of the deck is [Zulaport Cutthroat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zulaport+Cutthroat), [Brood Monitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brood+Monitor), and [Eldrazi Displacer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Displacer). With those three in play, you can sacrifice three Scions to generate three colorless mana (and get three Cutthroat triggers), use that mana to blink the Monitor with Displacer, and get three more Scions for your trouble. You then use those Scions to start the loop all over again. Repeat until your opponent dies.


[Duskwatch Recruiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter) can even help find combo pieces, and [Catacomb Sifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Catacomb+Sifter), if it's in play instead of [Zulaport Cutthroat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zulaport+Cutthroat), can let you scry through your entire library to find the piece you need next.


*Magic Online* player Romanmy actually managed to string all of that together into a deck with [Cryptolith Rite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptolith+Rite) and [Collected Company](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and go 5-0 in a Standard Competitive League. Now, I'm not sure if that's Seth Manfield's name online or not, but he wrote [today on TCGPlayer about taking the deck](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13252&writer=Seth+Manfield&articledate=5-4-2016)—pretty close to the exact list—to Grand Prix Toronto and doing well with it.


The deck borrows a few bits from the successful Black-Green Aristocrats deck from Pro Tour *Shadows over Innistrad*, and works in the combo and some colorless mana as well.
