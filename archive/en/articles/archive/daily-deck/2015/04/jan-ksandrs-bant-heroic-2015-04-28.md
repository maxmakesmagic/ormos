Jan Ksandr's Bant Heroic
========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on April 28, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 





Grand Prix Krakow was last weekend and the tournament was overrun by Dragon Control decks. While looking through the decks that didn't make Top 8, I stumbled across this innovative take on White-Blue Heroic. While Jan Ksandr failed to overtake the Dragons in Krakow, his Bant Heroic deck managed to earn him a very respectable ninth-place finish.



Bant Heroic isn't so much Bant as it is White-Blue Heroic with four [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command)s. Splashing for only four cards may not seem worth it, but [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) is one of the best heroic enablers that we've seen in a while. [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) is a modal spell, which means that you can choose what you want it to do. Unlike most modal spells, [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) lets you make two choices instead of the usual one. In a Heroic deck, that's pretty huge. This spell is capable of having multiple targets and can potentially trigger your heroic creatures twice. [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) can also be a removal spell if you choose the fight mode and can even take care of annoying enchantments like [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) and [Outpost Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ordeal+of+Heliod)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordeal+of+Heliod) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ordeal+of+Thassa)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordeal+of+Thassa)

One of the most interesting interactions I've seen with this deck is between [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) and the Ordeals. Most of the time when you attack with your heroic creature with an Ordeal attached to it, you will sacrifice it when your creature has three +1/+1 counters. However, the Ordeals read "When you sacrifice this." Your Ordeals will trigger whenever you sacrifice them, no matter how that happens. If you are in a tricky spot where you absolutely need to gain 10 life or draw two cards, you have the option of casting [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) on yourself, forcing you to sacrifice an enchantment and trigger your Ordeal.
