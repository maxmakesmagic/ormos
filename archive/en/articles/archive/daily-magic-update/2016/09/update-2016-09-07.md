The September 7, 2016 Update
============================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on September 7, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TrickJarrett.jpg)
By Trick Jarrett




Patrick "Trick" Jarrett oversees web content and social media for Magic, and acts as the publisher of DailyMTG.com and Magic content on the web. He's an ardent Commander player and lover of the game he's played since Ice Age. 






Blake's taking a well-deserved vacation this week, so you're getting a handful of special guests at the helm of the Daily *Magic* Update. Yesterday was Chris Peeler, today it's me—your old friend and former DailyMTG editor!

Today's Previews
================


1. [Exclusive *Kaladesh* Preview: Bristling Hydra](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13496) | TCGPlayer | Adam Yurchick (and Ryan, Jon, and Liana)

TCGPlayer leads off with a video featuring Ryan, Jon, and Liana introducing Bristling Hydra, then follows with an article by Adam Yurchick to look over a number of cards that have been previewed for *Kaladesh*.


1. [Paradoxical Outcome: Exclusive *Kaladesh* Spoiler](https://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/paradoxical-outcome-exclusive-kaladesh-spoiler) | MTGGoldfish | SaffronOlive

SaffronOlive dives into this unique blue instant, at first thinking it was something for his "Against the Odds" series only to realize that it is perhaps too powerful for it. He presents a handful of Modern and Standard decks for the new *Kaladesh* card Paradoxical Outcome.


1. [*Kaladesh* Preview: Noxious Gearhulk](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/kaladesh-preview-noxious-gearhulk) | ChannelFireball | Luis Scott-Vargas

LSV writes a short article about the black-aligned Gearhulk and explains a bit about why this card is powerful.

What People Are Talking About
=============================


1. [Reddit's discussion about Nissa, Vital Force](https://www.reddit.com/r/magicTCG/comments/51flje/kld_nissa_vital_force_gatheringmagic_preview)

The Reddit discussion after the newest green planeswalker card was revealed was super positive. It even peaked among the top posts overall on Reddit for a while, as did the response to the new Chandra in *Kaladesh*.


1. [Bulletin from the Kaladesh Consulate](https://www.facebook.com/KaladeshConsulate/photos/a.1759911120922588.1073741828.1725068314406869/1763770483869985/?type=3&theater)

The Consulate of Kaladesh put out a public bulletin about the renegade leader known as Pia Nalaar, causing quite the stir on Tumblr.


1. [*Magic: The Gathering* World Championship Trophy](http://nickfairdesign.com/project28.html) | Nick Fair

Nick Fair, the man who worked with Wizards to design this year's *Magic* World Championship trophy, wrote a post on his site diving into the designs and sketches and the overall process that went into creating the newest bit of bling for *Magic* players.


Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Ross Merriam's Blue-Red Reveler](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33612_Daily-Digest-So-I-Like-The-Card-Okay.html)

Ross Merriam discusses a blue-red deck built around [Bedlam Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bedlam+Reveler) that Chris Kelley played to 6th place at a StarCityGames.com Invitational Qualifier. The interesting thing about the deck (well, one of the interesting things) is that it foregoes what many might consider a required card: [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). Check out the list and maybe this could be your next Modern deck.
