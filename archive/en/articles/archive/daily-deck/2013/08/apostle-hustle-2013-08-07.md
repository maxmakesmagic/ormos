Apostle Hustle
==============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on August 8, 2013 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Luis Scott-Vargas











This week's Commander deck (courtesy of Scottingley, who posted it to [TCGplayer.com](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/deck.asp?deck_id=1144876)) takes advantage of a new engine card from **Magic** 2014, a card which even gets around the one-of rule. That card is [Shadowborn Apostle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowborn+Apostle), and as you can see by the twenty-eight copies below, we are well beyond even the normal four-of rule. Once you assemble six copies of [Shadowborn Apostle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowborn+Apostle), you have a long list of Demons to choose from, depending on what the situation demands. [Kuro, Pitlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuro%2C+Pitlord); [Pestilence Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestilence+Demon); and [Reaper from the Abyss](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+from+the+Abyss) all answer creatures in different ways; [Bloodgift Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodgift+Demon) and [Seizan, Perverter of Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seizan%2C+Perverter+of+Truth) both let you exchange life for cards; and [Rune-Scarred Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune-Scarred+Demon) lets you find anything you please. I also like the [Spirit of the Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Night), who has been updated to be both a Demon and a Spirit, although the [Shadowborn Apostle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowborn+Apostle) engine has sadly made [Urborg Panther](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Panther), [Breathstealer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breathstealer), and [Feral Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Shadow) unnecessary (although they are very cool still). 

  
 You can tell the focus of this deck is different than most Commander decks because I haven't even mentioned the Commander yet! Here we have [Shirei, Shizo's Caretaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shirei%2C+Shizo%27s+Caretaker), which combines very well with the Apostle plan. If have you Shirei on the battlefield and are able to assemble the full six Apostles, you just get to go off. You sacrifice all the Apostles, find some sick Demon, and Shirei brings them all back at the end of the turn, giving you a free Demon during each player's end step. You do have to sacrifice them main phase, so don't wait until your opponent's end of turn to do so or the combo gets broken. 

 Besides that combo, the deck features many ways to find Apostles, reanimate Apostles, and get value from losing Apostles (both [Rotlung Reanimator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotlung+Reanimator) and [Xathrid Necromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xathrid+Necromancer) look at Apostle and give you a Zombie in exchange). The deck also does just fine even when it can't cobble together six Apostles, as cards like [Crypt Ghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Ghast) and [Cabal Coffers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Coffers) let you cast Demons without much trouble. Lastly, Shirei saves a number of other powerful creatures, mostly creatures that start out big and end up shrinking themselves, like [Mindless Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindless+Automaton) and [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion). 

 If you've ever wanted to play twenty-eight copies of a card in Commander and don't like the idea of a relentless army of [Relentless Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Rats), I know some powerful Demons that have quite the bargain for you.
