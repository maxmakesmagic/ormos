"But Ben," I hear many of you screaming from the future forums, "this deck is a jumbled mess! It makes no sense, it has 240 cards, and the lands are all wrong? Plus, four theme decks is wayyyyyyyyyyyy over budget! Is this the end of all civilization as we know it?"


Well heck, I say to you - maybe it's my birthday! Maybe my grandma scrimped and saved for months so she could mail me a bucketful of shiny nickels, COD, so I could open up the bucketful of shiny nickels to pay the mail delivery guy for the bucketful of shiny nickels? Did you ever think about that?


I didn't think so.


In all seriousness though, I had an idea of taking all four theme decks, throwing them together, and then cutting cards 20 or so at a time until we ended up with a sixty card deck. After five games with this monstrosity of a deck, I scrapped that idea. Let's just say that this deck breaks virtually every rule of deckbuilding - and plus which, the nice folks at Wizards gave us some slightly more functional decks to toy around with. Decks like this:

I chose to start with Reality Fracture because it focuses the most on the new mechanics of *Time Spiral* - in this case, suspend. Suspend is a really interesting ability, because it has multiple game play implications.


 1) You can trade time for mana. [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron) is a 4/4 flyer - much like [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental). [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental) would cost five mana, but [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron) costs seven. Doesn't seem like a deal so far, right? Well, here's the catch - you can suspend [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron) on turn two and have it come into play as a hasted 4/4 flyer on turn six. You know what else comes into play as a hasted 4/4 flayer on turn six? [Volcanic Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Dragon). The difference is, [Volcanic Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Dragon) makes you tap out turn six. [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron) allows you to have all your mana open for other spells.


2) Suspend allows you to store up cards for future consideration. Take, for example, [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt). Let's say my opponent casts [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves). I suspend [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt), and it changes my opponent's choices. Maybe they were going to drop [Ohran Viper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ohran+Viper). If they do, then it will surely get fried by the bolt. If they don't, that's one less Elf they'll have.


3) Suspend works really, really well with storm. When you remove the last suspend counter from a card, that's when it counts as "being played." This allows the Reality Fracture deck to set up some really big turns, in which multiple cards become unsuspended at once, and then a storm spell ([Empty the Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens), [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot), [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories)) copies itself five or six times. Good stuff.


Let's take a look at the contents of the Reality Fracture deck, so we can understand what makes it tick.

The other four creatures are dubious at best.

Am I playing to a dead house in here?

**Game 1:** Darktama (Modified Reality Fracture)


Ha ha, funny - my first practice game with the non-modified Reality Fracture deck is against a modified Reality Fracture deck! I mulligan a one-land hand. On my first turn, I suspend a first turn [Viscerid Deepwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscerid+Deepwalker), and on the second turn, [Keldon Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Halberdier). He suspends [Wheel of Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fate) and then drops a lot of mana artifacts ([Prismatic Lens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens), [Izzet Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Signet)) and [Confiscate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confiscate)s an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). I respond by [Clockspinning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning) my [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier) and throwing [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) into the aether. The [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier)s and [Viscerid Deepwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscerid+Deepwalker) come into play and hit him, and I suspend [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt).


[Wheel of Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fate) goes off, and he [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)s the [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate) (but why? He has no creatures!) and then suspends Deep-Sea Kraken. He steals two more lands, leaving me with one. I play [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr), get a second land, and suspend [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)s on consecutive turns. He [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze)s the [Halberdier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halberdier) and [Confiscate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confiscate)s the Deepwalker. I drop [Claws of Gix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix) (which would have been good against all his Confiscating/Annexing action), land my first [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate) (returning the Deepwalker back to my hand), and then eat [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm).


 On my next turn the second [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate) comes onto the stack, which removes the last counter from his Kraken. The Kraken comes into play, then my [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate) comes into play, bouncing his Kraken. Ka-ching! I make a note that being able to engineer the time that my [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate)s come into play (through Timebugs or [Clockspinning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning)) will be really powerful. He [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang)s my [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate), and I cast [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) for 4 to finish him off.

Although I ended up with a winning record, I had to exercise all my self control to keep from heaving my keyboard across the room. Although there is some luck involved in **Magic**, I completed five straight games where I had mana problems in each and every game. The above games involved three mulligans, one land flood, and four games where I didn't draw a second color of mana until past turn four. Mana issues take any fun out of the game, since you literally sit there not being able to play spells while your opponent wails on you.


The first change I made to Reality Fracture was to add in four [Izzet Boilerworks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Boilerworks). These would smooth out the mana a lot and would keep me from having as many lack-of-mana issues in future games. To make room for them, I took out the [Dreadship Reef](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadship+Reef), two [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s, and a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain).


I also wanted to play around more with the cards which allow my suspended guys to resolve earlier, so I added in two [Jhoira's Timebug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhoira%27s+Timebug) and took out a [Clockwork Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockwork+Hydra) and a [Ground Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Rift). I didn't want to fiddle too much with the deck, since my mana issues had prevented me from really seeing the deck in full action. What I did see showed a lot of potential - huge turns with multiple suspend cards resolving at once, resulting in 5-6 [Grapeshot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot)s, 12-20 damage from [Ignite Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ignite+Memories), or 8-12 Goblins coming into play at once.
