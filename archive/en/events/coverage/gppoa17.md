TOP 8 COVERAGE



Just when we thought the format had been solved, the diversity showcased at Grand Prix Porto Alegre will likely come as a surprise to the world. Making the Top 8 are were five different archetypes and variants, including the tournament-winning Temur Tower.


Navigating his treasured pet deck deck past 569 other players, Victor Fernando Silva clinched the trophy on the back of Dynavolt Tower and Longtusk Cub. Having made Top 8 at Grand Prix Santiago 2016, his first place finish here was by no means a fluke, and the fact that he had to defeat Brazilian veteran Vagner William Casatti in the finals made it an even bigger triumph. On the other hand, this was a bittersweet moment for Casatti, who not only came in 2nd place at Grand Prix Porto Alegre 2015, but also this weekend.”


The rest of the Top 8 include semifinalists Rafael Bertolli Parra and Francisco Sifuentes, as well as quarterfinalists Sebastian Pozzo, Carlos Alexandre Dos Santos Esteves a.k.a \_batutinha\_ on Magic Online, Patricio Roman and crowd favorite Luis Salvatto.


With this victory, Fernando Silva qualifies for Pro Tour Hour of Devastation in Kyoto and transforms into a Silver pro once he gets there. Congratulations to Victor Fernando “almost Silver” Silva, your Grand Prix Porto Alegre Champion!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



570 players arrived from all over the region for their chance to be crowned the Champion of Grand Prix Porto Alegre. After nine rounds of Standard, only 175 players will return tomorrow. At the top of the standings are Sebastian Pozzo and Alexandre Cunha De Oliveira, who remain the only two 9-0's left in the tournament.


However, several big names are on their tails, including Grand Prix Porto Alegre 2015 finalist Vagner William Casatti and Pro Tour Shadows over Innistrad Top 8 competitor Luis Salvatto who are at 8-1 records. Standing at 7-2, there are also Pro Tour Aether Revolt Champion Lucas Esper Berthoud, Grand Prix Porto Alegre 2015 Champion Marcos Paulo De Jesus Freitas, and Grand Prix Sao Paulo Champion Guilherme Merjam!


As the curtains close here in Brazil, the show is about to begin in Japan! Swing over to Grand Prix Shizuoka in the meantime and we'll be back with all the live action tomorrow morning!
