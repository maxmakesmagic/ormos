Emrakul's Vengeance
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 21, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Prolific streamer Jan van der Vegt has typically done pretty well in European Grand Prix scene. While he typically plays something safe like Jund, he showed up with a masterpiece to [Grand Prix Prague](/en/events/coverage/hrvatskas-horvat-crowned-master-modern-prague). On the first turn I saw him play, he exiled a [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) to cast a turn-one [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid). What on earth was he playing? 

![Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fist+of+Suns) Thankfully, the coverage team figured that people might want to know that, so they did a quick write-up on his deck [here](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/day-1-coverage-grand-prix-prague-2014-01-11). As it turns out, he was attempting to put [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) or [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) into play as quickly as possible with [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance), [Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fist+of+Suns) (!), and [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). They called it "Tin Fists," after the Legacy deck Tin Fins, which also tries to accomplish the same goals, albeit in a slightly different way. 

Jan started 8â€“0 and eventually finished in 44th place. That's not too shabby considering he built an entirely new archetype for Modern.
