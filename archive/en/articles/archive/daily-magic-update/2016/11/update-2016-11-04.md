What to Watch This Weekend
==========================


1. [Grand Prix Dallas/Ft. Worth](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpdfw16)

Some nobody's from ChannelFireball will be hosting coverage of Grand Prix Dallas/Fort Worth all weekend, but maybe you've heard of them? Gaby Spartz, Luis Scott-Vargas, Marshall Sutcliffe, and William Jensen? Oh, you have? Well, then, coverage starts at 9:15 a.m. PT/12:15 p.m. ET/4:15 p.m. UTC on [twitch.tv/channelfireball](https://www.twitch.tv/channelfireball).


1. [StarCityGames.com Baltimore Open](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/051116_baltimore_fact_sheet.html)

While Dallas/Ft. Worth goes Modern, Baltimore is going Legacy. [Twitch.tv/scglive](https://www.twitch.tv/scglive) will be bringing you two days of Legacy action starting at 7 a.m. PT/10 a.m. ET/2 p.m. UTC Saturday and Sunday, hosted by Craig Krempels and Cedric Phillips.


After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [Gearhulk or Grafwidow?](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/verdurous-gearhulk/) | Top Level Podcast | Patrick Chapin and Mike Flores

Chapin and Flores talk flavors of black-green decks in Standard for this week's edition, but the question of the best five-drop for the deck is on the table—[Verdurous Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdurous+Gearhulk) or [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow)?


1. [Field Research in Standard](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33980_Field-Research-In-Standard.html) | StarCityGames.com | Adrian Sullivan

[Tamiyo, Field Researcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+Field+Researcher) doesn't get her due in Standard. That's me saying that, not Sullivan. Though Sullivan says it too, and that's why I wholeheartedly recommend this article. In it, Sullivan talks about his trials and tribulations with Tamiyo, the successful lists he's found, and what his testing has revealed for the powerful planeswalker.


1. [The Most Popular Casual Ways to Play *Magic: The Gathering*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eqedMcyqwxE&feature=youtu.be) | Magic the Amateuring | Meghan Wolff and Maria Bartholdi

Want to relax with some casual *Magic* at home with friends? Meghan and Maria talk about some of the most popular ways to spice up your home game. Plus, a bunch of other topics that are all entertaining and worth listening to come up as well, from Grand Prix Dallas to Flavor Text Theater.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/eqedMcyqwxE>


1. [Building a Mana Base and *Commander 2016*](http://collected.company/2016/11/03/building-a-mana-base-and-commander-2016/) | Masters of Modern | Ben Bateman and Alex Kessler

Even the Modern-iest of Modern players are getting in on some *Commander (2016 Edition)* talk. But they do circle back to Modern, and with Grand Prix Dallas/Fort Worth this weekend, it's good to get some Modern talk in your ear hole.

1. [The Pilot](http://manadeprived.com/men-modo-13-pilot/) | Men from Modo | David Savill and Travis Sowers

A mostly new podcast with an old name you might recognize, Men from Modo is an attempt at resurrecting a former podcast that was by players who you would consider *Magic Online* players, like Kenji Egashira. Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Esper Control](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=109075)

I'll admit, I got shivers when I saw this list played by Alex Clark at a StarCityGames.com Invitational Qualifier. It hit all of my control player bones. Planeswalkers like [Dovin Baan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovin+Baan) and [Liliana, the Last Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope)? Check. [Glimmer of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmer+of+Genius) plus [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk)? Check. [Noxious Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Gearhulk)? Check. [Fumigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumigate)? Check. It even plays [Unsubstantiate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsubstantiate) as a tempo play to get to the late game; it's a card that I very much want to be good, but never quite seems to make the big leagues.


The biggest issue I see with this deck is the mana. [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness), [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare), and [Void Shatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void+Shatter) all ask for double color as early as turn three, and while there are a ton of dual lands in this build, I can see it tripping over its mana now and then.


Still, if true control lists give you chills, this might just be the deck for you.
