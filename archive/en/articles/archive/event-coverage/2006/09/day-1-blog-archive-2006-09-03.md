I found out 9 Japanese players and a host of Europeans would be attending Phoenix a couple of weeks ago, and it got me wondering - if the best of the Japanese and many of the best Euros were in attendance, how many non-Americans (this includes Canadians like Rich Hoaen) could you expect to make the Top 8? I originally figured a solid estimate to be 3, meaning you could expect to see 3 non-Americans in the Top 8, and for fun, you could ask players to pick over or under the number. Eventually after talking to some more people, the estimate moved to 3.5 this week, and could have been as high as 4 this morning during a walk around the room. Setting it at a fair 3.5 however, and looking at the following list of non-American names, the question is simple: would you pick the over or the under?

Saturday, Sept 2: 2:44 p.m. - Deckbuilding with Jelger Wiegersma
----------------------------------------------------------------


Jelger Wiegersma is one of the world's top pros, a metronome of consistency and excellence. Though he hasn't had a Pro Tour Top 8 in two years, he's consistently in the Top 32 almost every time he plays. He's also one of the opponents most players least want to face, whether that's in digital form on Magic Online or live and in color. I sat down with him today to see what gifts *Ravnica* Sealed would bring him after he flew across the Atlantic to battle here in Phoenix.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphx06/jelger_deckbuilding.jpg)*Wiegersma attempts to make lemonade.*
Few builds in this sealed format can be considered easy. While many decks feature strong cards, they are often in disparate colors, and building your deck with mana consistency and raw power in mind has never been more difficult. It's true that players will occasionally get an easy, powerful 3 color build, it doesn't happen nearly as often as it did in pure *Ravnica* or *Ravnica*-*Guildpact*. Occasionally, however, even the best players get a card pool that completely stumps them.


Jelger may have opened one of the weakest RGD sealed decks that I've seen. He got one of those pools that are long on mana fixing but short on actual card quality, and what quality exists was all over the map.


His first build was blue-red-white with splashes for [Stinkweed Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp), [Agent of Masks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agent+of+Masks), and [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel), and [Rakdos Ickspitter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Ickspitter). The white was really just there to hopefully take advantage of [Azorius Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildmage), the one real bomb in his entire pile. Eventually white and blue were both jettisoned as Jelger picked up his black and green cards to see if he could find a playable combination that did not look terrible. This gave him access to [Plague Boiler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Boiler) and [Golgari Rotwurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rotwurm), but pushed the Guildmage to the side.


Next the bulk of the black left again, with blue coming back and an ironic chuckle from Wiegersma as he tossed [Agent of Masks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agent+of+Masks) back into the mix. "My mana is going to be nice…"


Laying his lands out, he ended up with 15 lands, 3 bouncelands, and 4 Signets plus a pile of scruffy cards that definitely separates the boys from the pros. Jelger's skill still makes him a favorite to make a Day 2 appearance, but with the deck below (which will be revealed at the end of Day 1), he'll have an extremely tough row to hoe if he's to participate in some *Coldsnap* drafting tomorrow.

Saturday, Sept 2: 5:21 p.m. - Round 4 - Ben Rubin vs. Rich Hoaen
----------------------------------------------------------------


The feature match area currently hosts Masashi Oiso, Gadiel Szleifer, Ben Rubin, and Rich Hoaen. I asked the guy with the most Pro Tour Top 8s in the area (Oiso, who has one more than Rubin's 4) which match I should cover, and he pointed to the Rubin v. Hoaen match, so that's what you get.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphx06/round4_rubin.jpg)*Future Hall of Fame member Ben Rubin.*
Both Hoaen and Rubin came into this match with a full set of byes, and also came to the event with in the same caravan as part of the Southern California contingent of pros. How ironic that they should square off at the start of the day as well.


Rubin cast a second turn [Dimir Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Infiltrator), which Hoaen answered with a [Simic Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Guildmage). [Steamcore Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steamcore+Weird) killed the Guildmage, but Hoaen reloaded with [Dimir Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildmage) and [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), fetching an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) from his deck.


"Hmm, didn't see that guy in playtesting," observed Rubin, as Hoaen threw a [Drooling Groodion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drooling+Groodion) onto the board. [Peel from Reality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peel+from+Reality) at the end of Hoaen's turn put the Groodion and Rubin's Weird back into their hands, triggering Circu, Dimir Lobomist twice, the second time coming from Steamcore's repeat appearance. In spite of all Rubin's action, Hoaen was doing just fine courtesy of [Seal of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire) on Rubin's [Dimir Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildmage) and having both Groodion and now [Cytospawn Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytospawn+Shambler) in play.


"Gugh," or something like it was the sound Hoaen made when Rubin cast [Izzet Chronarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Chronarch) to return his [Peel from Reality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peel+from+Reality). With a host of creatures on the board and a very problematic recurring advantage in the Chronarch-Peel combo, Rubin was able to put away game 1 without too much of a problem.


Rubin 1 - Hoaen 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphx06/round4_hoaen.jpg)*The intense Rich Hoaen.*
Hoaen had the first action of game 2, and quickly ran Rubin over with [Roofstalker Wight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roofstalker+Wight), [Giant Solifuge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Solifuge), and [Seal of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire) to remove any blockers and a [Char](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char) to finish things off. Rubin's slow draw was as much a cause of that loss as Hoaen's fast one.


Rubin 1 - Hoaen 1


Rubin mulliganned for game 3, and Hoaen again had the first business, this time in the form of [Compulsive Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research), discarding two creatures. [Indentured Oaf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indentured+Oaf) and [Seal of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire) followed, though the Oaf died immediately to [Cackling Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Flames). [Scatter the Seeds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scatter+the+Seeds) restocked Hoaen's Army, with [Simic Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Guildmage) close behind, while Rubin tried to stabilize with Pyrotechnics and [Galvanic Arc](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Arc), though he was clearly having mana issues. [Wrecking Ball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrecking+Ball) on Hoaen's [Barbarian Riftcutter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Riftcutter) and another concluding [Char](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char) gave Hoaen the match.

Saturday, Sept 2: 8:08 p.m. - News and Notes
--------------------------------------------


For some players, Magic is all about camaraderie. Players will fly thousands of miles to hang out with their friends and enjoy the times, while others will pick up strange hobbies or mannerisms that mimic those of their friends. Take Kenji Tsumura for example. Not only is he one of Japan's road warriors, but like so many members of the Rat Pack before him, the tiny master has taken up smoking so that he can hang out with friends like Tomoharu Saito and Rich Hoaen. Smoking is actually quite commonplace in Japan, but more than a few Americans were shocked to see Kenji light up outside the venue.


U.S. National Champion Paul Cheon is here this weekend, but he's not playing due to the fact that his impressive run in Limited a month ago gave him enough ratings points to likely qualify him for Kobe. He did not want to jeopardize attending the last two Pro Tours of the season by taking his chances here, but the Grand Prix was close enough that he drove here with friends to railbird for the weekend.


In round 6, Chris "Star Wars Kid" McDaniel was sitting at 7 life to his opponent's 20, and his opponent had 7 unblockable damage on the board next turn, while all Chris had were 3 saprolings, [Verdant Eidolon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Eidolon), and a [Selesnya Evangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Evangel). McDaniel thought about it for a minute before casting [Rally the Righteous](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Righteous) on his Evangel, then [Recollect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recollect) on the Rally, and then Rally again, reducing his shocked opponent from 20 to 0 just like that. He went on to win that match and stretch his record to 6-0 on the day.


Rumors of Masashi Oiso's retirement have been greatly exaggerated. His schoolwork has forced him to focus less on Magic over the last year, but I had a chance to chat with him today and he assured me that he plans to continue playing as long as he is able.

"Is your deck good?" asked Chris McDaniel, as Fiorillo entered the feature match area. "This is Limited - not sure I can beat you in this format. I beat your brother though," McDaniel preened. "Yeah, I know," said Fiorillo. The former New Jersey native was clearly out for revenge. Okay, perhaps not really, but Fiorillo is such a nice guy, you sometimes need to generate fictitious conflict for him just to give John an edge. Being composed entirely of jelly and goofiness, Star Wars Kid has no edges whatsoever.

Fiorillo 1 - McDaniel 0
