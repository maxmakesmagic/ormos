An Excess In Progress
=====================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on October 7, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello and welcome back to the Lab! My **Magic**al center of gravity has been jumping around lately, but I've officially re-aligned myself on the plane of Mirrodin. Today marks the first day that I get to fully dive into the new set, under this week's chosen theme of Proliferate. *Scars of Mirrodin* excites me for a bunch of reasons, and proliferate commands a fair bit of those reasons.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl111_engine.jpg)  
For starters, proliferate is pretty awesome, although you could insert any positive adjective of your choice in place of "awesome." Possible contenders include "crazy," "broken," "sexy" (available exclusively to R&D), and "endlessly exploitable." For a mechanic represented by only six cards ([Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp), [Contagion Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine), [Inexorable Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inexorable+Tide), [Steady Progress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steady+Progress), [Throne of Geth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Throne+of+Geth), and [Thrummingbird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrummingbird)) proliferate sure is more than the sum of its parts. This is quite fitting, given that the flavor of proliferate is all about Phyrexian oil slowly taking over the denizens of Mirrodin a la *Invasion of the Body Snatchers*, or Marvel's *Secret Invasion*.

I also am enraptured by proliferate because, well, it's a self-explanatory verb. Check out the elegant [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp) to see what I mean. I love that this kind of diction is becoming common in modern **Magic**. Devour from *Shards of Alara* is another example (who didn't smile at [Hellkite Hatchling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellkite+Hatchling)? It's so cute! ... I mean, its text box is so cute!), as is *Zendikar*'s update of kicker.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl111_puppy.jpg)  
Now, let's initiate the deck-building mode of this column. As today's title might suggest, I've got more than the usual three decks to share, to put extra emphasis on this great mechanic. Ever since my pen spilled ink on me at the Prerelease (in which I cobbled together a loopy poison/proliferate deck) I just can't stop talking about proliferate. Hmm, that was strange ink ... it's probably nothing.

**Step 1: ???? Step 2: Proliferate!**

That heading is pretty much the blueprint for all of today's decks. Whatever shenanigans I set up in Step 1 will ultimately lead to Step 2. Of course, after that I just segue into the obligatory "Step 3: ???? Step 4: Profit!" And yes, I did just layer the same joke on itself. Successfully.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl111_tide.jpg)  
It shouldn't be a surprise that I'm going infinite with most of today's decks. Proliferate is just that good, providing the necessary spark for some older combos to go off in new ways.

![Inexorable Tide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inexorable+Tide)  
For example, here's an [Inexorable Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inexorable+Tide) deck. Whenever I'm in blue, and want to cast a spell over and over for some kind of effect, I turn to mainstay [Shrieking Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrieking+Drake). Thus, the delicious engine: "![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif): Proliferate." Let that sink in for a minute. Then, when you're ready, add [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) to the mix to go infinite. As long as you keep at least one charge counter on the Prism in the early game, you'll be set to endlessly generate blue mana for the Drake.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Shrieking Drake | Pentad Prism |

In this way you can generate infinite mana (through [Calciform Pools](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciform+Pools) or a second [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism)), infinite damage (through [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion)), and infinite creatures ([Hoofprints of the Stag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoofprints+of+the+Stag), via the infinite mana combo.) Jace Beleran and [Grindclock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindclock) offer potentially infinite milling as well, while still acting as suitable control cards before the combo.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Mystic Speculation | Enclave Cryptologist |

[Mystic Speculation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Speculation) acts as a Drake-like spell that can repeat itself to a lesser extent while providing some important scry. [Enclave Cryptologist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enclave+Cryptologist) is an important card-drawer in the early game that will level up pretty quickly. Finally, I thought this deck would be a perfect home for [Archmage Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage+Ascension). You'll be able to get an initial quest counter on it via Jace, [Lonely Sandbar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lonely+Sandbar), [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), or the Cryptologist. From there, you'll quickly proliferate it to the Ascension of Super-Tutoring, which can come in handy if you want to find a win condition.

Another fallback in the combo world is that amazingly broken Construct, [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus). Whenever a quirky Johnny artifact comes out, [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus) is usually one of the first cards to pop into my mind. I'm going to pair it with [Throne of Geth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Throne+of+Geth).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Pentavus | Throne of Geth |

This one's easy. [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) returns to be our mana engine. Pay an initial colorless mana to remove a +1/+1 counter from [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus) to make a cute little Pentavite. Immediately sacrifice it to the Throne to proliferate. The Prism and [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus) each get a counter back, [Dross Scorpion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dross+Scorpion) untaps the [Throne of Geth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Throne+of+Geth), and you're instantly infinite. Since this time all the pieces are artifacts, I'll go with [Golem Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golem+Foundry) as the win condition. With infinite proliferation, the Foundry will produce infinite Golem tokens. Do this at the end of your opponent's turn for an instant Golem army!

Super cool. For anyone who's confused, the combo works as follows. Have the Scepter, the Engine, Tezzeret (or [Voltaic Key](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Key), but preferably Tezzeret), and a lot of mana. Pay ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20160830042801im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless4.gif) to give the Scepter a counter. Pay four more to activate the Engine and proliferate twice. This will give the Scepter two more counters (and two more loyalty counters to Tezzeret as well.) Use The Seeker to untap your two artifacts, and then tap the Scepter to take a turn. Repeat until infinite! You win the game with infinite Tezzeret ultimates, which might mark the first time that's ever happened.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl111_3Cards1.jpg)  
Abbott's other creation was a retro-style Prison deck, souped up with the proliferators and a certain game breaking level up creature. Funnily enough, his brainstorming began with a more aggressive fading deck, featuring [Blastoderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blastoderm) and [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm). I'll let Abbott take over from here:

"This deck, while perfectly competent, simply wasn't 'getting there'—it was neither incredibly fun to play nor notably consistent, and I abandoned it after a few revisions. But the fading idea stuck. A spin through [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) informed me that [fading](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&text=+%5Bfading%5D) had been printed on a lot of ostensibly vicious cards that had all been hampered by their turn limit. Proliferate, especially in large quantities, does away with this foolish limit—and after isolating from the bunch [Parallax Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave), [Parallax Tide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Tide), and [Tangle Wire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Wire), I knew I had a powerful prison deck in the works.

Of course, the deck couldn't 'just' be a prison deck—that would get old fast. I wanted to include a few eccentricities that would further my game plan while intuitively appearing clever; thus, [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) and [Calciform Pools](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciform+Pools) amass mana even amidst a [Tangle Wire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Wire) lockdown, [Lighthouse Chronologist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lighthouse+Chronologist) collects extra turns, and [Vexing Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Sphinx) draws an (occasionally overwhelming) pile of cards!"

As you can see, the goal is to lock down your opponent with a [Tangle Wire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Wire) or one of the Parallax enchantments, using quick [Thrummingbird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrummingbird) beats to keep the fade counters coming. The Chronologist is key here, as your opponent will probably be too disrupted to take him out before you hit the magic level count. Venser (in both forms) is helpful, particularly his planeswalker self. He can blink your fading permanents if they are getting low, and can make your [Thrummingbird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrummingbird) unblockable. With enough proliferation, you'll get to activate his ultimate, which I call the Exciting Exiling Emblem. I suspect it'll be fun to use [Venser, the Sojourner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Venser%252C%2Bthe%2BSojourner) to blink his [Venser, Shaper Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Venser%252C%2BShaper%2BSavant) self as well.

Kay, one final deck for today. While the quartet of decks above is pretty focused in terms of getting their respective combo off, this one is pretty relaxed in comparison. The concept for this one was simple. I thought, "You know what subset of cards would love proliferate? *[Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid)s.*"

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl111_saps.jpg)  
It's as simple (and fun) as that. I've always found [Thallids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&name=+%5Bthallid%5D) a ton of fun, and proliferate is generally quite fun, so meshing them together was a no brainer. No fancy explanations here. Just play a ton of [Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid)s, proliferate a bunch, make a swath of Saprolings, and run over your opponent. Hmm, a card that cares about counters and tons of tokens: [Beastmaster Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beastmaster+Ascension), you're up! Meanwhile, proliferating a freaking *[Mycoloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycoloth)* is my favorite new hobby. That's a lot of Saprolings.
