10 Decks in 10 Weeks: Battle Royale!
====================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on June 4, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












[![Battle_Frenzy](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/IA/Battle_Frenzy.jpg)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Battle%2BFrenzy)Hello everyone, and welcome to the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks Battle Royale! Over the course of a month and a half, I built 10 different decks, each a two-color combination. At the beginning of each week, I would take the deck from the week before, yank out one of the two colors, and use that skeleton of a deck as the starting point for a new two-color build! By the end of the series, I had built a full ten decks.


I intended to leave off with 10 Decks in 10 Weeks, but an overwhelming majority of readers said they wanted to see a Battle Royale! amongst these builds—and who was I to say no? In order to best test the mettle of all of these decks, I decided the format for the Battle Royale! would be a grueling gauntlet of massive **Magic**: each deck would be played, in a row, against the other nine decks. This process would be repeated for each deck, for a total of ninety games played! This would accomplish two goals:


 


1. This would allow each deck to play each other deck twice, so that unlucky draws (such as mulligans or land floods) would be mitigated by sheer numbers.
2. I would be able to play both sides of each matchup. I'm intimately familiar with how each deck works due to hours and hours and dozens of matches of playtesting, and my results with each deck might be different than other people's.

I sent out a general call for help in my last column, asking for people to volunteer their time to play one of the ten decks. Dozens of people responded, and I tried to incorporate as many players as possible into the gauntlet. Unfortunately, real life and timing (and interest in certain decks!) prevented me from being able to include everybody, but a special thank you goes out to everyone who volunteered!


For the record: the most volunteers were for Rescue Me, Aeon in Flux, and A Blink in Time (each had five people specifically want to play those decks). Literally nobody volunteered to play both A Wild Pair and Profusion Confusion—the only people willing to initially play those decks were people who "wanted to play anything."

If anyone wants to read 8,000-plus words of game summaries, I'm afraid that you'll have to wait until... never!

Ben's Thoughts: This was the second-most popular deck from the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks experiment, due to its quirky nature. I wanted to build a deck around having eight Yavimaya / [Mesa Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mesa+Enchantress)es, because having just four in a deck usually did not equal enough consistency to have a card-drawing engine. [Inspiration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiration) for this deck was drawn from decks circa *Tempest* / *Urza's Saga* Standard, which used [Argothian Enchantress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argothian+Enchantress), [Cloud of Faeries](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloud+of+Faeries), [Auratog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auratog) and [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) for the win. The stand-out card in this deck ended up being [Temporal Isolation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Isolation) in testing—it could allow me to attack with an unblockable [Auratog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auratog) for the win (declare attackers, swing through non-shadow creatures, and then sacrifice [Temporal Isolation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Isolation) between declare blockers and damage) or neuter an opponent's creature. Quite flexiable!


Game 1: G/R – A Wild Pair: He triple mulligans and I run him over. (0-1)


Game 2: U/R – Aeon in Flux: Countermagic and Jaya Ballard and [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) pretty much seal this game. (0-2)


Game 3: U/B – Grim Outlook: I get the Urzatron online, but all I have is a hand full of [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack), and he gets a quick double Enchantress with [Auratog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auratog) going to kill me. (1-2)


 



![Mesa Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mesa+Enchantress)

Game 4: B/W – Rescue Me: I hose his sacrificed [Aspect of Mongoose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aspect+of+Mongoose) with [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker), and then [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) does the rest to his team. The game ends in a showdown between his [Stormbind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) and my creatures, but I have too much rescue for him to deal with. (1-3)

Game 5: W/U – A Blink in Time: After a long game of back-and-forth of me bouncing his [Auratog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auratog)s with [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) over and over, he finally lands two [Auratog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auratog)s, [Retether](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retether)s [Temporal Isolation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Isolation) onto both, and comes in for double lethal. (2-3)


Game 6: W/R – Sheer Pandemonium: I drop [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), and then play [Keldon Marauders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders). In response, he [Temporal Isolation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Isolation)s it, stopping all the damage (including the [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) damage). However, he doesn't have any creatures in play, and eventually I get enough dudes to take him down. (2-4)


Game 7: B/R – Madness: We go back and forth, and I eventually drop a face down [Akroma, Angel of Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) with [Null Profusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Null+Profusion) on the board. He [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) it, but since morph isn't an activated ability, I am able to turn up Akroma, swing, and end the game in two turns. (2-5)


Game 8: B/G – Profusion Confusion: I combo off on turn five and win. (2-6)


Game 9: G/U – [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth): I get a slow start, but double [Harmonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize) gets me to the gas soon enough, and I have an army that is crushing him on turn seven. (2-7)


Week 1: G/W – The Two Ladies (Scouseboy – Craig Stevenson): 2 Wins, 7 Losses.

Ben's Thoughts: When I initially came up with the idea for 10 Decks in 10 Weeks, I had no clue exactly how well the "carrying-over cards from one week to the next" would work out in practice, versus theory. I had envisioned a deck where I kept a 0/2 Enchantress, had a bunch of 1/1 creatures, and used [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair) and other enchantments from the week before to fuel a [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair)-based Enchantress deck. This didn't work out at all, and instead I ended up with a wildly unpopular deck based around 2/2 creatures. I think this was somewhat of an unfair assessment, because anyone who's played against this deck knows that once [Cloudstone Curio](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio) and [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair) get onto the board, the game can turn around in an instant. Searching out triple [Avalanche Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders), multiple [Shard Phoenix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shard+Phoenix), and [Stingscourger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger) with a single [Ashcoat Bear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashcoat+Bear) in one turn can turn losses into wins in a heartbeat.


 



![Wild Pair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wild+Pair)

Game 1: G/W – The Two Ladies: I don't get an Enchantress, and he gets [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair). (1-0)

Game 2: U/R – Aeon in Flux: I counter three guys, but he lands [Ashcoat Bear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashcoat+Bear) with [Moldervine Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak). (2-0)


Game 3: U/B – Grim Outlook: I get a lock down with [Blizzard Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blizzard+Specter) and [Muse Vessel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muse+Vessel). (2-1)


Game 4: B/W – Rescue Me: It comes down to the last turn, but he gets Jaya to block my guy, shoots a flyer, and attacks me back with [Blood Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight) with double [Moldervine Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak). (3-1)


Game 5: W/U – A Blink In Time: He gets a couple of early [Avalanche Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders), but I get [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm), and get Akroma [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink)ed. (3-2)


Game 6: W/R – Sheer Pandemonium: Triple [Avalanche Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders) keeps me from getting enough mana to do much of anything. (4-2)


Game 7: B/R – Madness: He triple mulligans and then [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) takes out his world. (4-3)


Game 8: B/G – Profusion Confusion: I get out three [Null Profusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Null+Profusion), and misclick on a copied [Early Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Early+Harvest) with [Mirari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) out, which leaves me 10 Black mana under where I should be. I end up decking myself, while being able to copy a [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) for 18 with one card left in my library with [Mirari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari). (5-3)


Game 9: G/U – [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth): I get turn-two Vinelasher, turn-three Vinelasher, turn-four [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Root-Kin) and win. (5-4)

Ben's Thoughts: Of all the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks, this was the closest to a homogenized tournament deck. For those who have been paying attention to Standard, this deck comes very close to red-blue Izzet Control. However, at the time I wrote the original article, nobody, and I mean *nobody* was playing [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler). I found that the suspend guy was ridiculously good and pushed him heavily through word-of-mouth in my column on StarCityGames.com:



> 
> *[Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) is my pick for the sleeper card of the set right now. [Maro](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maro) has always been on the fringe of playability, but it's in the color that (before now) was short on card drawing. [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler), if you took away suspend, is a blue [Maro](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maro) that costs one more mana. In and of itself, that's not terribly exciting. Yes, blue's a better color for filling your hand than green, but it's not like people would flock to a white [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) that cost ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) instead of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif).* 
> 
> 
> *What makes [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) exciting, at least in my mind, is the suspend trigger. Let's say you suspend [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) for one on turn five. In the control / control mirror match, you can suspend the Chronicler for a lot longer, and have it act as [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena), except without the loss of life.* 
> 
> 
> *If there's one rare that is currently being valued at bulk that I would recommend people pick up now rather than later, it's [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler).*

Ben's Thoughts: One of the problems with writing Building on a Budget, in general, is incorporating reader suggestions and ideas. Due to the deadline / print time on articles, it's virtually impossible to use reader suggestions for the article I'm working on that week, unless it's a two-to-three part article. 10 Decks in 10 Weeks solved this problem, by giving me advance time in the form of a long-term deckbuilding plan. Readers could vote which two-color deck *they* wanted to see two to five weeks down the road, and I could take the most popular votes and run with them! The rescue mechanic is extremely popular, and the three decks that were built around rescue creatures (this one, and the next two) best embodied the ideas of 10 Decks in 10 Weeks—there are several similar white cards between the three decks, but each deck plays very, very differently!

Ben's Thoughts: This was the first match that I playtested for the Battle Royale! and it gave me an idea of what to expect (in terms of commitment, time, and length) for each of the rest of the matches. In general, it took about 90 to 120 minutes to play nine solid games of **Magic** in a row. Some decks took shorter (such as U/G [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth)) and some took longer (B/G Profusion Confusion would take a long time to combo out!). Of the three rescue decks, I believe this one is the strongest—Sheer Pandemonium is the most explosive, and Rescue Me is the quickest, but A Blink In Time is extremely tricksy, and is ridiculously resilient to most other decks. It's tooled to handle both fast and slow decks.


Game 1: G/W – The Two Ladies: He wins with [Azorius First-Wing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+First-Wing) and [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm). (1-0)


Game 2: G/R – A [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair): He gets Blink with Riftwing a few times, but I get [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair) and [Avalanche Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders) on the turn before he kills me, for the win. (1-1)


 



![Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Momentary+Blink)

Game 3: U/R – Aeon in Flux: [Conflagrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conflagrate) for 16 for the win! (1-2)

Game 4: U/B – Grim Outlook: I get a little mana shy, and he uses 4x [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) to keep me down. (2-2)


Game 5: B/W – Rescue Me: He suspends 2x [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), and I name it with [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle). That's pretty much that. (2-3)


Game 6: W/R – Sheer Pandemonium: He gets too many Blinks, though I [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker) two. (3-3)

Ben's Thoughts: The readers voted for this deck, and at first I wasn't too keen on the idea of [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth), the card. I'd seen creatures that were dependent on other creatures to survive flare out before ([Emperor Crocodile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emperor+Crocodile), [Ancient Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Ooze)) and I wasn't looking forward to playing around with a similar deck concept. Well, this deck proved the old saying "Never dismiss a card / deck idea without at least testing it first," because I was quickly coming across with 9/9 and 10/10 hasted [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth)s on turn four, leading an army of 3/3 and 4/4 creatures. This quickly became one of my personal favorite decks of the entire experiment, and it was definitely the most offensively oriented of any of the 10 decks!


Game 1: G/W – The Two Ladies: I get triple [Temporal Isolation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Isolation) and triple [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters), which buys me enough time to set up a one-turn [Auratog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auratog) kill. ### Some Final Thoughts


The 10 Decks in 10 Weeks experiment was a success, and I definitely will try something like it again in the future. It probably won't be the exact format as this time around (as many readers pointed out in the forums, too many cards that were carried over were obviously going to be cut at the first opportunity, making for bad deckbuilding), but it will use the basic framework to present a deck a week in a structured format! The [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth) deck has a lot of potential for FNM, or possibly Regionals, play—it pumps out creatures bigger and faster than almost any other weenie deck out there, and it can smash down control decks extremely quickly. With a more expensive mana base ([Breeding Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pool)s, [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise)) and some more control elements (some [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s or [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)s), it has the potential to be a real force.
