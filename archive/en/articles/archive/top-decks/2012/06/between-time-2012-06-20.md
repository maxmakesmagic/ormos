The In-Between Time
===================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 21, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











Last week—Cathedral of War.

  
Next week—... *now that would be telling!*

So this is an in-between week (don't worry, the preview for ***Magic** 2013* is going to be very exciting), and Top Decks is getting back to what we do best: the *top decks.*

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_delver.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/sun_arttransformbutton_active.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/moon_arttransformbutton_static.png)[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Nils+Hamm%22%5D)

There were a couple of big and important Standard tournaments last week, including an awesome-making [Grand Prix](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpmla12/welcome) and the StarCityGames Invitational. Both tournaments showcased a variety of interesting decks... as well as more new and different takes on Standard favorite [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets).

Let's see how these events shook out!

Grand Prix Manila:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_chart1.jpg)  
... and the StarCityGames Invitational:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_chart2.jpg)  
So, two high-profile tournaments and straight White-Blue Delver took half the slots in both Top 8s, including wins for Lauren Nolen at StarCityGames and an epic sixth GP title for Yuuya Watanabe.

Most Delver lists since *Avacyn Restored* have adopted [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) (and generally as a four-of), although other details have come to vary. [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) makes for a nice combination with (previous) minority inclusion [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer). You can produce a lot of first striking 3/3 Golems, and while [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) is net 2 power south of [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) (1 + 3 rather than 2 + 4) on offense, it makes for an elite defender, especially when combined with the aforementioned [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), and it really shines in the heads-up battle between [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft).

Another card that has started to come back up through the Delver ranks is [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember). [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) was the off-color Phyrexian removal spell of choice in Delver decks for months (superb against the card [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) itself and an occasionally sick combination with [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681)—really throwing off the opponent's math), but [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) has returned to popularity, again, in response to [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) in other Delver decks.

Both of these sometimes-changes—and one more fairly brave one—make Yuuya Watanabe's latest winning list all the more impressive:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_watanabe.jpg)The first thing that probably jumps out at you is the land count. *Nineteen* lands? The six-time Grand Prix champion has *some* kind of bravery!

Of the seven other White-Blue Delver players in these two big events, six played twenty-two lands and noted format breaker Gerry Thompson (appearing in his umpteenth StarCityGames Top 8) played twenty-*three*!

There is something about crossing that twenty land line that seems like crossing the Rubicon to most players. You can play twenty and draw some strange looks. Twenty-one lands was reasonably acceptable in Delver with its tempo and super low curve; [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) (as a four-of four-mana card) drove up the curve, and again, Gerry went one higher... but *fewer* than twenty lands? *Nineteen?*

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_glacial.jpg)[Glacial Fortress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress) | Art by [Franz Vohwinkel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Franz+Vohwinkel%22%5D)

Heck, I wouldn't be surprised if a nineteen-count seems like a violation of the sacred to some of you. Before you learned you were supposed to play twenty-four lands, maybe your first teacher (like mine did) told you you were supposed to play twenty creatures, twenty spells, and twenty lands ("that's just how you make a deck").

I know some of you are shaking your heads... or maybe even shaking your fists! But rest assured this wasn't Yuuya's first dance, but his sixth GP *win*. The man had his reasons, and they didn't fail him in this trial. Forget about whether Watanabe "should have" done this... let's look at the deck mechanics that allowed him to:

1. Three [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s—it might seem like a small thing, but almost every other Delver player in Dominaria is now packing all four of these gals (generally the most expensive card in the deck). By cutting one, Yuuya didn't just remove an expensive card, he opened up a slot to add a much cheaper (nonland) one. Like...
2. [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) and [Mutagenic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth)... Watanabe played a *ton* of [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s! Three in his main deck and the fourth in the sideboard. [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) is an unambiguous *zero* mana in this deck. Yuuya has no red or green mana, meaning that this always, Always, ALWAYS costs you "nothing" (but 2 life). Ditto on [Mutagenic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth)! Remember, many Delver decks have cut down on [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)s... Watanabe not only played that Phyrexian zero as a four-of, he literally doubled the (free) main-deck Phyrexian mana count.
3. Speaking of which... Yuuya could drop his curve even lower after boards if he wanted to... the fourth [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot), a second [Mutagenic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth), and his [Mental Misstep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep)s allowed Watanabe to add four *more* "zero"-cost cards to his deck.
4. All the cantrips! While other decks are skimping on [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s (no deck in the StarCityGames Top 8 played more than two) or [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)s (the other three White-Blue Delver decks in the GP Manila Top 8 played two, three, and three) Watanabe used the massive overload in zero-to-one-mana card drawing to tighten his mana base, playing essentially as a twenty-five-land deck. So... even though Yuuya only played three [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s and three [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s, his many cantrips + [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s helped him run through more of his deck than the vast majority of Delver players.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_boyd.jpg)Mono-Blue Delver!

Boyd pulled the white lands and white spells from his Delver list, meaning no [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) (or [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer)), no [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), and no [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) out of the side.

But what does he get in return?

  
[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inkmoth+Nexus)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus)  
This deck is super focused around [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681)—even more so than its cousin, the twelve-cantrip deck played by Watanabe. Boyd *also* played four off-color Phyrexian instants, but it is telling that he went with two [Mutagenic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth)s instead of Watanabe's three/one split in favor of [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot).

With his focus on [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681), he also went with the full four [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker)s.

The twelve-cantrip setup *both* helps him find his focused four-ofs, but *also* boosts the efficacy of [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681). Boom boom! You can easily kill the opponent with two strokes from the unblockable, hexproof [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker)... especially when you do stuff like give it +2/+2 (which is really +3/+2 with Pike).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

But this focus also lets you crush with [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus)!

Surely you've played against an eight-instant graveyard? One scratch from an [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus)? Just getting the Pike up to +9? Try killing the opponent with *one* hit from [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus)!

Boyd didn't quite go to Watanabe's nineteen, but twenty lands is still pretty tight to the line. Whether it's Dan Paskins grafting an artifact sub-theme onto Goblins for [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) or Tsuyoshi Fujita cutting—*gasp*—[Goblin Piledriver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piledriver) for [Goblin Goon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Goon), I have come to love it when brewers kill their darlings when templating archetype decks in order to add some compelling new functionality or way to win; as such, Adam's deck is probably my favorite new take I have seen in some time. Seriously? Who cuts *the entire color white* to poison a chap? It just seems so focused, so sure of what it wants to do... and so hard to play around given everything else going on. Cheers, bravo, etc.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_hetrick.jpg)We've looked at Naya Pod lists before (and will look at more today), but we haven't discussed a lot of RUG Pod since [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) was a hot new thing.

Most Pod decks have access to [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph), but this one also plays [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image)!

Two more spicy blue cards: [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) and [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch).

[Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) can do everything from allowing you to re-buy your copious enter-the-battlefield creatures to short-term dealing with the opponent's bomb creature. There was a time when [Man-o'-War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Man-o%27-War) was a staple of tempo creature decks (and later considered "too good" for Constructed), and [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) here really brings a tear to the eye of some of us longtime codgers.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

[Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) no longer has [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) as a buddy in Standard, but it can untap a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) to allow the deck to search up twice in a single turn; say ramping from two-to-four (through the Exarch). It can also give you some obvious dirty tricks, like screwing up a combat or even tapping down an opposing solo blue in order to resolve something next turn (when played traditional-style).

Out of the sideboard, Hetrick had the ability to get [Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) on six, adding a (hopefully uncounterable) way to dominate the opponent by drawing lots and lots of extra cards.

Now, as we said, RUG Pod is hardly the only Pod deck available. If you aren't up for [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), one of these Pod builds might be your answer:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_lee.jpg)So we have two Red-Green-White [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks here. Both decks have twenty-four lands, but neither one has the full four [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)s. Lee, for instance, ran the same number of [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)s as he did [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless).

In these colors, you can go all [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township)s or mix it up with a [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run).

Both these decks ran [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan), although [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) also looks like a reasonable possibility at six.

Lee went with almost all guys; main deck, his only noncreature spells were [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) (both of which search for creatures). In addition to four copies of [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), Lee had the tiny—but efficacious—[Ulvenwald Tracker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Tracker)! (Your boys are probably bigger than his.) He even had another Tracker in the sideboard!

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_tan.jpg)Tan could go all the way up to seven with [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite). His deck included a lot more twos and threes. While he had less consistent action around stuff like turn-two [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), Tan had a wide variety of potentially backbreaking (and hard to prepare for) effects. For instance, main-deck [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned).

... which brings us to the last deck I am going to highlight today, Martin Juza's [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) deck:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td200_juza.jpg)Guys, guys everywhere! All different types from Angels to Birds to Wolves (although Humans aplenty)—Juza's deck pushes [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) awfully hard!

Again, this deck is almost all creatures—only four [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) as noncreature spells in the main sixty.

Ultimately, Juza's Top 8 list is very reminiscent of an *Innistrad* Block deck... as far as the spells and creatures go, the only non-*Innistrad* Block card is [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise).

Martin's deck is more streamlined than most [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) decks. He has four [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), four [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212)s, and four [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer)s (pretty great on turn two). He has lots of [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s to help him pull dirty tricks with [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger), or [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), of which he also has four. While he lacks the step-up incremental capabilities of a Pod, he can potentially make up with seizing the initiative.

[Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) puts the kibosh on the kinds of cantrip-centered play we talked about earlier with the Watanabe and Boyd decks. [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) get a lot less interesting to talk about when they cost two.

In sum, many players were waiting with sweaty palms on this week's banned and restricted updates to change the format of Standard for them.

It turns out, you don't *need* a banned and restricted announcement to do that! Players are slicing out as many as *four* lands from competent Delver lists and others are taking out a whole color (so they can play a poison sub-game). Others are turning Pod colors (back) on their ears, working from different (forgotten) colors, or taking Pods out of their lists entirely. And we still have all these other archetypes to explore... Blue-Black Zombies or Black-Red? What about reanimating (literally) some French fries?

There is a lot of innovation still happening...

... even when it feels like just one buddy flick is getting all the half the choice opportunities.
