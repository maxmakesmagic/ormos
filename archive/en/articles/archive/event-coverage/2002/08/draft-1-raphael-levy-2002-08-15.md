Draft 1: Raphael Levy
=====================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 15, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TobyWachter.jpg)
By Toby Wachter











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a887.jpg)Levy may be relatively young, but don't let that fool you- he's one of the Pro Tours most accomplished veterans. The Frenchman made Top 8 at Worlds back in 1998, and had a Top 8 at Chicago 99 with a deck that sported, among other things, Fire Whip/Quirion Ranger technology. Yesterday he ran his somewhat unconventional green/blue threshold deck to a 5-1 record, and sat down at the draft table today in prime position to make a run for another Worlds Top 8 four years after his last one. 

The first pack contained [Flame Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst), [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger), [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) and [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts) as viable picks. Despite the fact that [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) and [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts) provide card advantage, Levy decided to force red and took the [Flame Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst). Since red is generally considered to be the weakest color in *Odyssey* Block draft, this was a calculated risk. Levy was hoping everyone else would avoid the color, and it certainly paid off. [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger) came second, [Patriarch's Desire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Desire) third, another [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger) fourth and a second [Flame Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst) fifth. [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites), [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood) and [Fledgling Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Imp) rounded out Ruel's black as he headed into *Torment*. 

With *Torment* came a first pick [Grotesque Hybrid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grotesque+Hybrid), and a second pick [Chainer's Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%27s+Edict), which has great synergy with all of Levy's burn. The rest of *Torment* didn't provide any [Crippling Fatigue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue)s or [Faceless Butcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher)s, but it did give Levy two [Pitchstone Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchstone+Wall)s, [Cabal Torturer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Torturer), two [Barbarian Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Outcast)s, and a sixth pick [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper). Obviously, Levy's red gambit had worked. *Judgment* wasn't particularly exciting, but then again you can't expect much in the third pack if you're black/red. [Toxic Stench](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toxic+Stench) was taken first pick over [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger) and [Treacherous Werewolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treacherous+Werewolf), and [Filth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filth) was picked second over [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart). The rest of the picks gave Levy some 2/2 creatures for three in [Treacherous Werewolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treacherous+Werewolf) and [Barbarian Bully](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbarian+Bully), in addition to [Sutured Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sutured+Ghoul). It is interesting to note that Levy never really considered taking late [Goretusk Firebeast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goretusk+Firebeast)s, and went so far as to counterdraft [Web of Inertia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Web+of+Inertia) over one. The Firebeast is not exactly a "platinum hit" but it often works well in an aggressive black/red deck.

**Sideboard:** How do you think it went?  
**Levy:** I think it's fine. I like this kind of deck; red/black. I drafted it at Euros (European Championships) and went 7-0.

**Sideboard:** It seemed like you were trying to force that from the start. You took [Flame Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst) over [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) and [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts). Do you like red specifically? Most players seem to avoid it.  
**Levy:** Mostly, players avoid red or force black, but in that pack there was [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger), [Flame Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst) and [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts). So, I thought the guy to my left wouldn't take [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger); he'd take [Second Thoughts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Thoughts) or something else. Otherwise, he would have taken [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) and forced black, and then I would have been in trouble. 

**Sideboard:** So you were looking for red/black from the start?  
**Levy:** Yeah.

**Sideboard:** It worked out well, you got some really good red stuff late.  
**Levy:** Yeah, I got that [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) sixth pick. That was a nice surprise.

**Sideboard:** Are you going to play both of the [Pitchstone Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchstone+Wall)s?   
**Levy:** Probably. I'll see how many cards I have.

**Sideboard:***Judgment* wasn't quite as good, but then again you're playing black/red, so you can't expect much.  
**Levy:** I had twenty playable cards after *Torment*, so I was just expecting Bullies and Werewolves- the 2/2 guys for three mana. I was just waiting for them.

**Sideboard:** You took [Toxic Stench](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toxic+Stench) first pick, but you were thinking about [Treacherous Werewolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treacherous+Werewolf). Was that because you were afraid you weren't going to get enough creatures?  
**Levy:** I had a lot of creatures, but I still didn't know exactly what to take. I took [Filth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filth) over [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) because I have two [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger)s, (Cabal) Torturer and a lot of burn, but [Filth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filth) just destroys my deck. I would need to use [Haunting Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Echoes) to remove it...it's good against black.

**Sideboard:** So it's more of a counterdraft, but it also helps you out against other black decks.  
**Levy:** Yeah. I could have taken the [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart), but...

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/worlds02/a788.jpg)**Sideboard:** I think most people would have taken the [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart). I'm not saying that what you did was wrong, but I think most people would take the Dart. You also counterdrafted a [Wormfang Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wormfang+Drake) fourth pick. There was just nothing in that pack for you?  
**Levy:** There was absolutely nothing else.

**Sideboard:** You took [Stitch Together](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitch+Together) over [Goretusk Firebeast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goretusk+Firebeast) and [Earsplitting Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earsplitting+Rats). Some players like the Firebeast, but I saw you give it no respect whatsoever. You took [Web of Inertia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Web+of+Inertia) over it. You just don't like the card?  
**Levy:** I like it in aggressive decks, but...

**Sideboard:** This is more defensive even though it's black/red.  
**Levy:** Yeah.

**Sideboard:** So the plan is to blow up most of their guys, hold back attacks with the walls and swing with some random guy?  
**Levy:** Yeah, that's the plan. The Beast is too expensive.

**Sideboard:** If that's your plan I guess [Sutured Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sutured+Ghoul) can be your kill card.  
**Levy:** Probably, or just 2/2 dudes. Plus I have two [Chainflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainflinger)s.

**Sideboard:** Any predictions?  
**Levy:** I don't know. We'll see.
