Saturday, 11:39 p.m. - Standard Overview
----------------------------------------


*by Jake Van Lunen*

Hello and welcome to Salt Lake City, Utah! Today 1137 players will be battling each other with their favorite Standard decks. The current Standard metagame is quite diverse. Let's take a look at some of the decks players are expecting to see this weekend.


Blue/White Delver has become the format default. The deck backs up absurdly cheap aggression ( [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) ) with cheap countermagic and tempo effects like [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) . This is, without any doubt, the deck to beat coming into this event after Yuuya Watanabe's win last weekend at Grand Prix: Kuala Lumpur.

Humans has evolved into a new version of Delver. This deck, an evolution of Sam Black's deck from Grand Prix: Orlando, gives its pilot access to the two most powerful one-drops in the format, [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) . The deck gets to play a similar suite of spells as the other Delver decks, but [Gather the Townsfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+the+Townsfolk) makes optimal [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) draws very impressive.

Red/Green Aggro is a powerful aggro deck that matches up well against Delver strategies. Cards like [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) make the deck a nightmare in racing scenarios. The deck has a fast clock, inexpensive removal, and a resilient creature base which makes it very good against snapcaster decks. However, Red/Green Aggro does not do very well against Red/Green ramp or Zombies.


Mono-Green Aggro has maintained a following since the first week of the format when it had a great deal of success. [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) and [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) are still some of the most powerful cards in standard. Mono-Green Aggro is better at casting a Sword on turn two than any other deck, and that's a really nice thing to be best at. The Swords eventually find themselves attached to Hexproof cards like [Dungrove Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungrove+Elder) and [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) making the game very difficult for any opponent.

Blue/Black Control was a reaction to Red/Green Ramp's success at the Pro Tour. Many players quickly realized that a dedicated Blue/Black control deck would be very well positioned against the ramp decks. The deck provides a rare opportunity to play a control deck in standard, but the current standard metagame makes the deck jump a number of hurdles. Spot removal spells no longer remain at parity with the creatures they destroy. ( [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) makes tokens, [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) leaves behind a wolf, [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) come back bigger.) Blue/Black Control needs to trust that it can draw a correct mix of removal spells, card draw, and disruption to achieve victory.

Round 4: Feature Match - David Sharfman vs. Andrew Cuneo
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Jake Van Lunen* 

David Sharfman is no stranger to the feature match area. The Florida native has a Pro Tour win, a Grand Prix Win, and multiple Nationals Top 8s under his belt.


Andrew Cuneo is an old-school Pro Tour player that's making quite the comeback. Cuneo popularized the Melira Pod deck in Modern with his impressive finish at Worlds 2011 and continued to show his prowess with the deck by making it all the way to the finals at Grand Prix: Lincoln.


Both players sat down and immediately started passing friendly jabs back and forth. "I hope you mulligan to five," joked Cuneo.


"All I need is a Delver and it doesn't matter."


### Game 1


David won the roll and led things off with a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) . He continued attacking for one, but [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) failed to flip for three turns. Meanwhile, Cuneo only had a [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) which he cast and Flashbacked on successive turns.


Andrew Cuneo made his first move with [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) , but Sharfman had a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) . Sharfman pressed his advantage with a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) .


Andrew was able to stabilize with a [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) , taking control of the game.


David answered with [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) . Not all that threatening now, but a Runechanter's Pike or [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) could turn it into a real problem.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/fm4%20Andrew%20Cuneo.jpg)
*Andrew Cuneo*

Cuneo started churning out card advantage with a Blue Sun's Zenith for two. A [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel) continued his theme of card advantage and he passed back to David, who only had [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) on the table.


David cast a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and passed the turn. A [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel) activation revealed ponder. Cuneo, now at 16, cast [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) , kept three on top, and passed back.


Sharfman flipped his [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and attacked for four. Cuneo found a pair of lands with [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel) , thanks to [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) , but was quickly falling behind again since Sharfman had been able to flip his [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) into [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) .


Sharfman tried to sway things further in his advantage with [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) , but Cuneo had [Psychic Barrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychic+Barrier) . A Blue Sun's Zenith for three was cast on Sharman's endstep.


Cuneo shifted the game in his favor by resolving [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) , which exiled [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) the moment it hit the table. Sharfman attacked and used [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) to get rid of the Planeswalker, but Cuneo had another [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) in hand, which he resolved the next turn. Sharfman Conceded.


### Game 2


Sharfman went down to five cards and Cuneo kept his opening seven.


Sharfman had [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) on his first turn and it flipped right away. Cuneo tried to answer with a [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) , but Sharfman had the [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) .


Another [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) came down for Sharfman, but Cuneo had another [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) . Sharfman chose not to flip the second [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and Cuneo used the [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) to kill off the [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) during Sharfman's attack step.


Cuneo took another hit and stabilized the board with [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) .


Sharfman started making tokens with his [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) and began poking Cuneo for a few points of damage at a time. Cuneo was quickly falling behind as he continued taking damage from the [Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) tokens. Cuneo tried to find answers with [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) and Blue Sun Zenith, but the Gideon he found was countered when Sharfman had [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to flashback his [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) .


Cuneo conceded. David managed to win despite his mulligan to five.

### Game 3


Both players kept their opening seven. Sharfman cast [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) on his first turn, but Cuneo used a [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) to kill the human before it became [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) .


Sharfman knew he couldn't play draw-go against Cuneo's control deck, so he decided to get aggressive with an end of turn [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and a [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) that was aimed at Cuneo twice. Cuneo took the wind out of Sharfman's sails when he had a [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) of his own, which he used to trade with Sharfman's.


Sharfman cast an [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) with a Runechanter's Pike in hand, hoping that he could sneak in lethal damage if Cuneo ever tapped out.


Cuneo took a few hits from [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) , but a [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) came down and began to tick up. A few [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) s and a Blue Sun's Zenith for three kept Cuneo's hand full, but he still couldn't find an answer to [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) .


David managed to to resolve a Runechanter's Pike on the turn it would be lethal, but Cuneo revealed that he had a [Divine Offering](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) and used the Pike to buy himself a few more turns of [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) hits.


[Pristine Talisman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman) came down for Cuneo, keeping his life total safe from [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) . Sharfman managed to resolve a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) , but Cuneo had a [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to legend rule it away.


Another [Pristine Talisman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman) came down for Cuneo, now Sharfman needed to win through Cuneo gaining two life a turn.


Sharfman tried to get threats onto the table, but Cuneo seemed to have an answer for everything.


Eventually, Cuneo managed to resolve [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) and Sharfman conceded a few turns later.


Andrew Cuneo wins 2-1!

Saturday, 4:30 p.m.: The Race to the World Cup
----------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin* 

On August 17-19, 2012 at Gen Con® in Indianapolis, USA, four player teams from over 50 different countries will compete in the World  ****Magic****  Cup for over $120,000 in prizes, extremely valuable Pro Points, and the right to bring the title of World **Magic** Cup Champion home with them.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/wmc.jpg)

Each of the selected countries will send a team comprised of a team captain – the player in their country who ended the 2011-2012 season with the most Pro Points – and the winners of three World **Magic** Cup Qualifiers.


Wondering how **you** can qualify for the World **Magic** Cup?  [Click here to learn more about 2012 World **Magic** Cup Qualifiers near you](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup/qualifiers#countries) .


Want to learn more about the frontrunners in the race to become the United States World **Magic** Cup team captain? Then read on...


### 1st: Brian Kibler, 56 Pro Points



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/Kibler%20Race.jpg)
*Brian Kibler*

When Hall of Famer Brian Kibler won Pro Tour *Dark Ascension* with his Red-Green Ramp Deck featuring the then underappreciated [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) – he earned himself a $40,000 check, locked himself up for Platinum Level in the Pro Players Club for 2012-2013, and qualified himself for the exclusive 16 person Player's Championship. Oh, and he also catapulted himself into the lead for most Pro Points in the United States (and the world).


If Kibler is able to maintain his lead, he'll represent his home country as the captain of the US team at the World **Magic** Cup this summer. But despite his lead (and his talent at the game) Kibler is far from a lock, as there are a number of extremely good players who are eager to pass him.


### 2nd: Josh Utter-Leyton, 53 Pro Points



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/utterleyton%20race.jpg)
*Josh Utter-Leyton*

While Josh "Wrapter" Utter-Leyton hasn't won a Pro Tour (at least not yet), he's had an incredibly impressive run over the past few months highlighted by a second place finish at Pro Tour Philadelphia, a Top 8 finish at Worlds in 2011, as well as Top 8s at Grand Prix Orlando, and Grand Prix Nashville.


Going into this weekend, Josh is only three Pro Points behind Kibler for the lead in the United States. If Kibler fails to Top 64 here in Salt Lake City, Josh will only need to Top 16 to tie himself for the top spot in the USA. And If Josh Top 8s, he can even start building a lead for himself...


### 3rd: Luis Scott-Vargas, 52 Pro Points



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/LSV%20race.jpg)
*Luis Scott-Vargas*

While most of the top players in the United States made their way to Grand Prix Salt Lake City in an attempt to cut into Brian Kibler's lead -- Pro Tour Berlin Champion, and arguably the best player in the world, Luis Scott-Vargas decided to make a slightly longer trip this weekend. Instead of making a relatively quick hop from California, to Utah – Luis decided to fly half way across the globe to compete in Grand Prix Melbourne.


The last time that LSV went to Australia, it worked out well for him as he finished in second place at Grand Prix Sydney. But will he be able to replicate that success this weekend? You'll need to [follow the Grand Prix Melbourne coverage](/en/events/coverage/crewe-crowned-champion-melbourne) to find out.


### 4th: Matthew Costa, United States, 47 Pro Points



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/Costa%20race.jpg)
*Matthew Costa*

While most of the other frontrunners in the United States have been forces to be reckoned with on the Pro Tour for a while now, the 19 year old Matt Costa began making his presence felt in competitive **Magic** less than a year ago.


When Matt Costa made the Top 8 of Grand Prix Kansas City last year, few people outside of the Northeast knew who he was. When Costa Top 8ed Grand Prix Toronto, the very next limited Grand Prix that he played in, players across the globe started to take notice of the young limited standout who had been terrorizing local PTQs for years.


But even after posting back to back Grand Prix Top 8s, Costa knew he could do better. Costa began putting countless hours into his constructed game, and was rewarded for his diligence with a Top 8 finish at Pro Tour *Dark Ascension* in Honolulu (where he was eliminated by none other than Jon Finkel), and a win at Grand Prix Baltimore.


While Costa has a good amount of ground to cover in order to become the United States team captain – you shouldn't count him out yet. A Top 8, or even a Top 16 finish at Pro Tour Avacyn Restored in Barcelona could be enough for Costa to lead his country's flag at the World **Magic** Cup this summer.


### 5th: David Ochoa, United States, 46 Pro Points



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpslc12/Ochoa%20race.jpg)
*David Ochoa*

Unlike the other players atop the standings, David Ochoa doesn't have a Pro Tour Top 8 to catapult his Pro Point total upward. Instead, Ochoa has gotten to 46 Pro Points through strikingly consistent finishes at the Pro Tour, and on the Grand Prix circuit. Heck, even if you take away the Pro Points that he received from his Top 8 finishes at Grand Prix Austin, and Grand Prix Orlando – David Ochoa would still be a lock for Platinum Level in the Pro Player's Club for 2012-2013 just by showing up at Pro Tour Avacyn Restored in Barcelona.


Over the course of the last year, Ochoa has demonstrated that he has the ability to play extremely high level **Magic** at every event he goes to.
