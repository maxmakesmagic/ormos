Tortured Existence
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 8, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adriansullivan.jpg)
By Adrian Sullivan











Whenever you strike out and attempt something new, it can be a bumpy road. Sometimes the new thing will take the world by storm – take the iPod as a great example. Other times it will flop. Sometimes even these flops are pretty grand, though; Lewis and Clark never did find their water-only route to the Pacific, but they gave back plenty of value from their journey.

One of the reasons that I initially introduced the idea of a Reader's Challenge to the Single-Card Strategies column was a question of my audience. You readers are a pretty diverse lot. There are people out there who want to see quirky applications of cards, people looking for practical applications of cards, people looking for a laugh, people looking for a combo, people looking for an edge in their home game, or just people looking for food for thought. Most of us will not strictly be a Johnny, Timmy, or Spike. 

I've been writing gaming articles for nearly ten years, initially for the game formerly known as Jyhad, and later for **Magic**. Getting decklists from readers has been one of the constants from the very beginning. By the time I wrote my second Single-Card Strategies article, I had received a *ton* of decklists from readers, and many of them had incredibly cool or innovative ideas. I just had to put them *somewhere*, and so the Reader Challenge (usually once every two months or so) was born. Some people really seem to have a knack for sending in the cool decklists – I've featured Denmark's Christian Moeller-Holst's decks several times now. 

This time around, I've mixed up the Challenge so that it is the readers and their decklists that will make up the bulk of what goes into the article. The idea is to see what the Readers can come up with on their own, without anyone telling them beforehand what to look for. I'll let you decide which style of Challenge you prefer at the end of this article.

### The longest journey begins with a single step

A few months ago, I was looking for a card topic for an upcoming article. One of the hard things about this column is that it focuses on a single card every week. This means you have to have enough to say about that card to make it worth an article. It also means that you generally can't write about a card that has been heavily covered already. Sometimes it is easy to get too close to the question of “What should I write about?” and not explore a card that really deserves close scrutiny. Abe Sargent's enthusiastic suggestion to use [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) was a lot like someone suggesting a favorite meal you hadn't had in a while. Thanks, Abe.

*The Survival Connection – the lovers, the dreamers, and Squee*

![Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Survival+of+the+Fittest)Tall.kid.with.glasses was the first to point out (mere moments after the first article went live) the connection between [Survival of the Fittest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) and [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence). In a lot of ways, these two cards are the exact same card from different ends of the spectrum – the grave and the library. Obviously [Survival of the Fittest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) is the more powerful card, since you start with a full library and a graveyard takes work to fill up, but the mere fact that they are basically working for the same effect is a big deal. Survival is one of the most powerful cards we've had in the game, and even if [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) is a “smaller” version, it's still got kick.Survival is a great way to fill up your graveyard with creatures. If you get one creature in your hand, you can fill up your graveyard with whichever creatures are most suited to the situation. With [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence), anything that you've already found to be useful you can pull back to mess with your opponent once again. Tey Junyuan's Peasant **Magic** deck takes this selection angle to the utmost extreme, replacing Survival with [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor) and [Commune with Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+Nature). Either way, the idea is the same: if I have a creature and it *was* a problem, well, it will probably be a problem for the opponent the second time around too.

With or without Survival, a deck that is interested in discarding creatures often finds itself going to Squee. Discarding Squee usually feels like cheating. It's not like discarding it at all. Tall.kid.with.glasses's deck takes the Tortured/Survival/Squee angle and decides that it can also use a [Solitary Confinement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solitary+Confinement). With a Squee to fuel it, this is clearly an easy decision, and you should pretty much be able to get by reusing any of your creature cards.

*More on Squee*

![Squee, Goblin Nabob](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Squee%2C+Goblin+Nabob)Squee popped up in decklist after decklist. This isn't a surprise. With a Challenge that already requires that you discard a creature, Squee is a natural choice. Squee changes the nature of almost any deck he is in. Plenty of cards suddenly become far more powerful if a Squee can be around manning the pumps for them. Aside from discarding him, other general graveyard filling gets a huge boost as well. Take one example, offered up by Minnesota's Zac Wendler: 
> 
> Basically, this deck uses Squee and [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) to recur any creature it feels like recurring, usually put into the graveyard by [Sneak Attack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) or [Gamble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gamble). Believe it or not, this is a highly efficient way to burn through enemies, even in a multiplayer game. 
> 
> 
> [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) + [Sneak Attack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) + [Squee, Goblin Nabob](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squee%2C+Goblin+Nabob) = Mucho fun.
> 
> 
> Here's a decklist of mine; it's Extended legal, and is a lot of fun to play, though it's not really fast enough to keep up with the likes of [Aluren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren) and Desire. For those of us that love Reanimator and Sneak decks, it's a little slice of heaven.
> 
> 
>

*A dash of salt – Astral Slide and Spirit Cairn*

[Astral Slide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide) and [Spirit Cairn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Cairn) were both popular subjects among readers as well. [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) could guarantee any of the cycling creatures you might draw could be brought back to your hand, feeding an [Astral Slide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide). [Spirit Cairn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Cairn)'s trigger (on any discard) makes each and every activation of [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) a potential 1/1 flier for only one White mana. Kenneth took this angle to make a Black/White control deck with some interesting potential.

I agree with Kenneth that this deck isn't incredibly powerful, but it does show great integration of the white cards. [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) already can trigger the [Spirit Cairn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Cairn), but triggering an [Astral Slide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide) with Cycling has the added bonus of triggering [Spirit Cairn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Cairn) as well. In a mid- to late-game situation Astral Rats can completely lock a player out of the game. If you tossed in a few [Krovikan Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krovikan+Horror)s you stop being an Extended deck, but you do get the ability to have your own pseudo-Squee.

*Soulshift*

A completely different approach to the problem of creatures in hand is Soulshift. While a Squee or [Krovikan Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krovikan+Horror) can keep your hand full of creatures, Jake Anderson's solution is going for a Soulshift chain.


>  Whenever you reach the bottom of the Soulshift chain, pitch the little guy to [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) for the biggest Soulshift guy and start the whole thing over again. [Thief of Hope](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thief+of+Hope) is a little champ in this deck.

*Tortured Existence and Kirin* 

Russel Lunt's use of [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) to power-up his [Celestial Kirin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Kirin) to the appropriate casting cost is particularly ingenious. One of the big problems with the Kirin (which are an otherwise impressively powerful cycle of creatures) is that they trigger at a very specific number. As a game draws out, you might no longer be able to have effective ways to trigger the appropriate number with your Kirin. [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) all but guarantees you the ability to do so.

Andy's use of [Confessor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confessor) is a great example of yet another card that you can take advantage of simply because of your ability to expect to be able to discard. In a pinch, just having a [Confessor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confessor) and Existence on the table at the same time can afford you a great deal of breathing room. 

[Buried Alive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive)'s mention as a tutor engine is also very relevant. By having access to potent creatures in your graveyard, a [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) can become increasingly similar to a [Survival of the Fittest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest). Andy's deck includes a [Valor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valor) and a [Glory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory) as potential tutor targets for the [Buried Alive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive), but any of the Incarnations are fair game, especially [Genesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis).

*More Traumatizing than being Buried Alive*

[Buried Alive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive) is a precise way of filling your graveyard. [Traumatize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traumatize) is a bit more like a sledgehammer. The big benefit of a card like [Traumatize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traumatize) in filling the graveyard is that it is pretty darn good at it without risking decking death. Aaron Weiner's deck could use a bit of trimming down and a bunch more land, but it does showcase the idea pretty well.

*Madness – the best Myr Servitor ever*

Madness is a natural pairing with [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence). Discarding a [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) to [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) essentially gives you a free creature back in your hand and a [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) on the table. Antonio Sanchez Puente took this idea as his initial inspiration and ran with it.

A long, long time ago, Paul Barclay made waves with his incredibly complex combo-deck “[Full English Breakfast](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/bb31)”. Tuomas Vanhanen revisits the idea.

Essentially, [Volrath's Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath%27s+Shapeshifter) makes use of one of the best features of [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence): the ability to reorder the graveyard. First let's look at his Extended Legal reworking.

Since the discard of the [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) is a cost and [Volrath's Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath%27s+Shapeshifter) always has the statistics of the top card of your graveyard, you can accomplish some pretty crazy feats with the two cards in play. Tuomas's version was but one of many, but I thought his was especially fun. Israel Sioson's version includes two other angles on [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) that are pretty important.

First of all, Israel includes [Intuition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition), similar in many ways to [Buried Alive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive) in giving you options for [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence), but better in a few ways. First of all, the card can actually *find* an Existence. In addition, not only is the card an instant, but it gives you the same access to any three creatures you might want that a [Buried Alive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive) does, but it leaves one of them in your hand.

Reanimation is the other important thing to think about. Much like Eric and Zach's use of [Sneak Attack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) or the use of [Elvish Piper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Piper) sent to me by the Sky View High School Gaming Club, reanimation in any form works quite well with [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence). [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) is not only an incredibly efficient means of getting a card into your graveyard, but it has the added bonus of having a potentially useful effect to take advantage of. There were lots of submissions that included reanimation in some form (most usually with the excellent [Nezumi Graverobber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Graverobber)), and it is important to keep this element of the card in mind when looking at it. 

### The Challenge Champion

Well, I have to say the readers did a great job really hitting the elements that make [Tortured Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tortured+Existence) such a noteworthy card. Everyone that participated really deserves a bit of applause. There certainly were a lot of great decks of course (with “Astral Existence” by krizc coming in a close second), but this one actually made me laugh out loud.
