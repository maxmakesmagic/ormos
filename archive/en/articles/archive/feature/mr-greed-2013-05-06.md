Mr. Greed
=========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 6, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







This deck fit this user name too perfectly for me to gloss over it. mrgreed on **Magic Online** has taken a shell that looks a little like RWU Flash at first, but added a substantial helping of black to the deck for awesome trump creatures like [Obzedat, Ghost Council](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) and [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), as well as, more importantly, [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) and "most powerful card in the format" [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). 


![Nicol Bolas, Planeswalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nicol+Bolas%2C+Planeswalker)
By combining the best of Jund, RWU Flash, Reanimator, and Esper, mrgreed has somehow managed to play most of the successful decks in Standard at the same time to a successful result himself.


 I'm extremely impressed by how well this deck manages to do everything it's trying to doâ€”every land taps for red or white for [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), and [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) are perfect for setting up [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) for [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). He has plenty of early defense from [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear), [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), and [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), with midrange trumps like [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) and [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), and control trumps with [Obzedat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat), [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), and [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return). 


 Not to be outdone, his greed goes so far as to include [Nicol Bolas, Planeswalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nicol+Bolas%2C+Planeswalker) in his sideboard. Greed of this level in deck construction is truly an art form, and I actually love this deck. 


 I honestly have no idea what to call this deck that just attacks from so many angles, so I'm sticking with Mr. Greed as the deck name. I sincerely hope he was the designer, but I suspect I may have missed this deck's real origin. 


 I'm not sure what the best additions from *Dragon's Maze* will prove to be, but this is the kind of deck that makes me want to load it up on **Magic Online** and play right away.
