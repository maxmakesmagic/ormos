Sweet Science of Stomping Ground
================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on February 7, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







T his past weekend showed us the first glimpses of *Gatecrash* in Standard play. And *Gatecrash* has not disappointed thus far! Many notable decks at the most recent StarCityGames Standard Open in Atlanta, GA, featured new cards, both strategically and "just" as mana-base components. Yes—and this probably has nothing to do with beloved Boros Week—there were some Sacred Foundries (and company) kicking butt and burning down opposing defenses. For those who love a 5/5 for five mana, [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) made its first—presumably of very many—Top 8s. But in Atlanta and abroad, the biggest impact, at least thus far, seems to be from [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) ... [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) in a variety of not just creature decks, but combo ones too!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td233_stomping_ground.jpg)[Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) | Art by [David Palumbo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22David%20Palumbo%22%5D)

### New Cards:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td233_newboroscards.jpg)*[Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) , [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) , [Sacred Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) , [Skullcrack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullcrack)* 
Andrew went with the typical pre-*Gatecrash* red beatdown shell and took a "Boros" angle to it rather than the pre-existing straight red or Rakdos angles. [Rakdos Cackler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) doesn't need-need black mana at the one... red mana is just fine. Andrew ran up the curve from one-drops to [Ash Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) , [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) , and huge [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) at the five.



![Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Reckoner)

The big switch here is [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) at three instead of [Pyreheart Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) or [Hellhole Flailer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellhole+Flailer) ... from this writer's perspective, [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) is a huge upgrade relative to the previous options. In a deck—of however many colors—that can hit ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) , it is just fantastic. A 3/3 creature for three mana is almost "green" in playability... and this one has multiple crazy abilities!


Taking down [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) in combat *and* living to tell the tale? First strike is quite the big game.


[Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) certainly taxes the opponent's resources—up to and primarily life total—if and when it is answered. This is a creature that will change how Standard will be played. No longer is blocking and trading with value an easy answer to red aggro.


[Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) rewards the deck higher up on the curve. The "really" red decks—rather than the black-based Rakdos beatdown decks—are much more vulnerable to mass removal than the ones with [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) . In addition to its damage-based abilities, [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) is a counter to [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and the like.



![Boros Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Charm)

...but what about those damage abilities?


Ka-pow!


[Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) is like a Char to the face for one less mana... and no collateral damage.


And combined with [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) ?


*10.*


...and it might not even be the best two-mana burn card available in this deck.



![Skullcrack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Skullcrack)

Two mana, 3 damage, ho-hum... *except* when the opponent is banking on [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) or [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) . Yes, the opponent will still be getting that big body or drawing a bunch of cards... but a big reason those cards are so popular—and so powerful—is the life swing they offer. No more.


With its new *Gatecrash* tools, Boros might be the deck that most redefines the rules of what is viable and how to play Standard. Attack decks were already great in Standard, but [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) and [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) really change the rules and shift the math of the format.

### New Cards:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td233_newespercards.jpg)*[Blind Obedience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Obedience) ; [Devour Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh) ; [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) ; [Godless Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine)* 
Eddie Walker put a totally new spin on the Esper Spirits archetype. Last year, Hall of Famers Jon Finkel and Jelger Wiegersma crashed into the Top 8 of  [Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*](/en/events/coverage/ptdas12)  with a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) build featuring [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) , [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) , and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) . While [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is still in Standard, the disappearance of [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) has severely restricted that cross-format All-Star's efficacy...


But if you can't go little, how about big?



![Obzedat, Ghost Council](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council)

[Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) is one of the most exciting new threats to hit the scene via *Gatecrash*. Exceedingly mana efficient on its face, Obzedat *also* has a stack of abilities that allows it to crush all different opponents from all different angles. Control? Try stopping this Spirit with sorcery-speed removal. Beatdown? Point after point, life totals going both ways (neither of which is good for the other guy). Obzedat is hard enough to deal with normally... but add [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) ? *Not exactly a fun time for the opponent.*



![Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drogskol+Captain)

[Blind Obedience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Obedience) helps to cut off the opponent's angles. Like Obzedat, [Blind Obedience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Obedience) grinds life points over and over—giving you a solid buffer against beatdown decks, as well as a way to kill an opponent putting you in lockdown without having to use the red zone. But, in addition, [Blind Obedience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Obedience) can turn off [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) shenanigans (no mid-combat surprise blocks for you!) and takes the oomph out of [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) , [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) , and [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) .



![Blind Obedience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blind+Obedience)

And with [Blind Obedience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Obedience) and Obzedat giving you such a life-point buffer?


What are a couple of points between friends?


[Devour Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh) isn't *quite*  [Diabolic Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Edict) ... but it's the [Diabolic Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Edict) we have. Once in a blue moon you will target yourself, and [Devour Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh) —a card designed to end creature's lives—might just keep *you* alive!

### New Cards:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td233_newcombocard.jpg)*[Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) , [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Cartel%2BAristocrat), [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Cartel%2BAristocrat)!; (and [Godless Shrine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine) ); [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) , of course!* 
 

One of the exciting new decks enabled by *Gatecrash* is Humans Combo Reanimator.


[Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) was always a big game with cards like [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) , but now you have a legitimate look at an infinite combo deck in Standard.


The early setup is similar to other Reanimator decks... [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) , [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) , and so on. The goal is to dump cards into the graveyard in various combinations. Lots of Humans in the graveyard is fine... you can either [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) or hard-cast [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) and they will all come back.

The unique setup of this deck is to assemble [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) , [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) , [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) , and some way to take advantage of an infinite loop— [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) or [Cathedral Sanctifier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Sanctifier) .


How you get the catalyst cards online is up to you. [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and/or [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) can be in play or they can be in the graveyard. You might have most of the tools already on the battlefield when you draw [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) . The most common setup is probably just to dump a ton of cards down and target [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) with [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) in the graveyard.


<http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_legacy_migration/flipcard/flipcard.html?ca=huntmasterofthefells&cb=ravagerofthefells&lang=en>
![Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Fiend%20Hunter&options=)This is what will happen:



* [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) will enter the battlefield.
* In our scenario, it will quickly be joined by [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) , [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) , and [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) (all of which are Human).
* [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) will target [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) .
* [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) will give you 2 life and a 2/2 Wolf.
* [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) will gobble up [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) (putting it back into the graveyard), and then [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) .
* [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) will return to the battlefield, returning [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) (and its 2 life and 2/2) as well as [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) (which will re-target [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) ).

You will do this over and over as many times as you want, assembling nigh-infinite Wolves and nigh-infinite life points. Even if your opponent has the sweeper... you know, *1,000,000 life and all that!*


Note [Appetite for Brains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Appetite+for+Brains) in the sideboard. This deck can go truly infinite given setup time, but it really, *really* has to pre-empt [Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) in order to avoid having its strategy slaughtered.

### New Cards:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td233_newnayahumans.jpg)*[Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) , [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) , [Sacred Foundry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry) , [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground)* 
 

Naya Humans has been more or less the darling deck of the Star City Open Series in recent months. Originally a bit counterintuitive— *where are the [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) s?* —Naya Humans has put up great results on the back of the [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) opening, solid follow-up, and redundant offense.


[Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) might be at its best in this deck. I have seen players run out main-phase [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) s to prevent their own Mayors from flipping into Wolf-machines. *Then my Humans would all get smaller!*


</sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_legacy_migration/flipcard/flipcard.html?ca=mayorofavabruck&amp;cb=howlpackalpha&amp;lang=en>
![Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Champion%20of%20the%20Parish&options=)[Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) lacks the Achievement Unlock with [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) in this build, but can accomplish a similar anti-beatdown enters-the-battlefield pairing with [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) .


Speaking of pairing, the offensive ace in Naya Humans is [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) . That card is a one-turn elbow drop and can paint an opponent into a corner even if he or she manages to survive a big attack... [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) obviously gets on well with [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) in longer game situations.

Naya Humans has quite a bit of card crossover with Combo Reanimator— [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) here, while older decks have sideboarded [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) as anti-sweep and anti-Zombies—but it is clearly a swarming offensive deck, rather than an infinite combo deck. As we've said, Naya Humans has also been a good performer for some time, happily putting up Top 8s without [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) . So why add [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) and company?



![Frontline Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Frontline%20Medic&options=)

[Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) is a good curve card in this deck; it can be played on the second turn via [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and does two things. First, [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) allows Naya Humans to get even more aggressive against other aggressive decks. An inherent risk to attacking is that the opponent gets to choose how he or she blocks... with [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) leading the charge, *there are no good blocks*. You might be able to stop some damage, but you shouldn't be able to block and kill the Naya deck's creatures via conventional creature combat.


And of course, [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) helps shut off important X-spells. These would include the format-defining [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) as well as cards like [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) that are great against Naya Humans itself. Lots of contextual value, all attached to an efficient body.


[Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) is surprising in one way: It's in the sideboard!


It seems like [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) offers everything that the Naya Humans deck already wants. Its double strike clause gives you "more [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) s" and can make for tricky turns given the quality and speed of these creatures. Like [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) , [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) can keep your guys alive. When getting into a huge brawl with another creature deck, [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) might be even better because the opponent doesn't necessarily see it coming. You might not *want* to block into a [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) waving the "I have battalion" banner, but you might have to. [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) doesn't give you the courtesy of a calling card on the other end. You might just lose all your guys. As far as combat tricks translating to Constructed go? This one is a blowout.


And, of course, "deal 4 to you" is a solid ability at two mana... Patrick Sullivan has had repeated success in Legacy—a huge format—with [Flame Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Rift) ... which does four to both players for two mana! In a deck like Naya Humans, whose victories are so predicated on creature attack, adding a little bit of reach—especially for this amount of damage at a reasonable cost—is a nice extra option to help put the deck over the top and end games that might have been closed down otherwise due to [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) , [Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) , or just big blockers.

### New Cards:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td233_newmonoredgreen.jpg)*[Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) , [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) , [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) , [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground)* 
This decklist—with exactly twenty lands—is obviously quite tight and, from my perspective, looks very exciting. It abuses the curve, and although it is still creature-based (like most Standard Red Decks), it is put over the top by its use of [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) .



![Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Rancor&options=)

[Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) —typically the tool of "green" decks—can make for some big problems here. [Ash Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) was not meant to be given a [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) hat. First strike and 4 power? Saito has the [Ash Zealot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) s that can not only kill a [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) , but walk away and deal a damage through as well. [Rakdos Cackler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) —a sometimes-dropped one-drop—is suddenly trading with everybody.


Of course, he has the general setup of decks like these... [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) near the top end, [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) we have talked about already (all twenty of Saito's lands tap for red)... but what about other new cards?


[Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) is extremely exciting here! Saito can chain two-drops almost like a Suicide Black deck of old. In addition to just providing bulk mana, Burning-Tree Emissary can filter out green... so with two or three Mountains in play, Saito could cast a [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) (3/3) or get the necessary color for his [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) .

Of all of these, [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) might be the most exciting new card of all. It's not exactly new ( [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) is from  ***Magic** 2013* , not *Gatecrash*), but it has just received the keys to the kingdom via—you guessed it— [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) . A deck like this—with sixteen Mountains of twenty lands as its mana base—is the perfect home for [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) . Although it is technically green, [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) doesn't care how you got the green, whether it was from a basic Forest, a [Rootbound Crag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootbound+Crag) , or filtered off a Burning Tree Shaman; but it really *really* cares about having a Mountain of some kind in play. [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) pulls this one from the "never gets played" pile to challenge for "best two-drop in Standard." It is essentially a [Watchwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf) / [Boggart Ram-Gang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) split card... huge on turn two, savagely challenging on turn three, deadly anytime thereafter.

Rounding out this week's discussion of successful [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) s is Tyler Lindsey's Experiment Jund, a Jund beatdown (rather than midrange) deck featuring numerous *Gatecrash* specials, starting on [Experiment One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) .


[Experiment One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) is [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) 's opposite number. In a green beatdown deck, it functions not just like [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) (a quickly growing offensive threat that takes great advantage of early game mana) but like a [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) or [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) ... [Experiment One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) is resilient against removal and can survive a good tussle.

It is not nearly the most interesting *Gatecrash* card in this deck.



![Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Ghor-Clan%20Rampager&options=)

[Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) has already been called the card with no downside, and it is easy to see why. A 4/4 for four mana is reasonable... but this card is also a mini- [Hatred](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatred) that provides trample and protects its target's toughness!... for relatively little mana.


This is especially compelling in Experiment Jund, where the name of the game is haste. This deck puts [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) next to not only [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) but [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) for a whirlwind of entering the battlefield tapped (and attacking). [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) makes an appearance here as well, although it is a mite less sexy than in Saito's deck, as Lindsey's won't always be 3/3. Still... a great two- (or three-) drop and a nice bit of bonus haste to put on pressure or give you Rampager targets.


I hope you liked this showcase of [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) and other new cards from this exciting new set. It will be interesting to see how the Standard format continues to shape up as we approach the upcoming Pro Tour *Gatecrash*!
