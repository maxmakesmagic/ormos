Grand Prix - São Paulo
======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 23, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_authorpic_larger.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











The **Magic: The Gathering** Grand Prix tournament series is an international circuit of large-scale Qualifier tournaments featuring cash prizes and multiple invitations to Pro Tour events.

**TOURNAMENT LOCATION**  
 Club Homs  
 Av. Paulista 735  
 Cerqueira César  
 Sao Paulo, Brazil 01311-100

**TOURNAMENT FORMAT AND SCHEDULE**  
 The format for Grand Prix-São Paulo is Standard Constructed. All DCI Constructed rules and **Magic** announced on September 1, 2001, along with all subsequent updates, will be enforced.

NOTE: *Judgment* will be Standard legal for Grand Prix - Sao Paulo.

* **Friday, July 12th :** Registration from 18:00 to 21:00.
* **Saturday, July 13th:** Late Registration from 7:30-8:30. Tournament begins at 9:00. The tournament will be Modified Swiss style, with 50-minute rounds. The number of rounds will be determined by the number of participants in the tournament. The Top 64 players advance to the second day on Sunday.
* **Sunday, July 14th:** Top 64 begin at 9:00, with six 50-minute Modified Swiss style rounds. The top 8 will immediately follow, with 60-minute time limits for the quarterfinal rounds and 90-minute limits for the semifinals. The Finals will be untimed.


> Players must use the same Standard deck and sideboard in all portions of the tournament.

**ENTRY FEE**  
 R$15

**HEAD JUDGE**  
 Juan Del Compare (level 3 certified judge from Argentina)

**TOURNAMENT DIVISION AND PRIZES**

The main tournament at Grand Prix-Sao Paulo is open to **Magic** players of all ages, and will award invitations to Pro Tour - Houston to the top 8 players in the tournament.

*In the event that a player who wins an invitation to a Pro Tour stop at a Grand Prix has already qualified for an invitation, the invitation shall be extended to the next-best finisher in that Grand Prix. Previous qualification may be through a Qualifier tournament, through performance at a professional tournament, or by being an invited DCI player (valid upon the date of the official rank announcement for the appropriate professional tournament).*

Grand Prix-Sao Paulo will feature the standard "main tournament" prize schedule as well as a special "Amateur" prize schedule. The Amateur prizes will be paid to the highest-finishing amateur players *in addition to any winnings those players receive from the main tournament prize schedule* . All prizes are in U.S. Dollars.

**Amateur Status**  
 A player is considered to have amateur status if he or she has 0 lifetime pro points. If a player has ever been awarded pro points from a Grand Prix, Pro Tour, or World Championship event, that player no longer has amateur status and is not eligible for amateur awards at Grand Prix or other tournaments.

**Tournament Byes**
*(Byes are not cumulative throughout the season or year)*  
 Byes will be given to players in the Grand Prix based on the following criteria: 


> 
> ***Third-Round Byes***
> 
> 
> * Players with 28 or more professional points in the 2002 Osaka Pro Standings.
> * DCI players with a Constructed rating of 1950 or higher. Byes do not pass down under any circumstances. Byes are based on DCI ratings published on March 20, 2002.
> * Winners of Grand Prix - Sao Paulo Trial tournaments
> 
> ***Second-Round Byes***
> 
> 
> * Players with 14 or more professional points in the 2002 Osaka Pro Standings.
> * DCI players with a Constructed rating of 1850 or higher. Byes do not pass down under any circumstances. Byes are based on DCI constructed ratings published on March 20, 2002.
> 
> ***First-Round Byes***
> 
> 
> * Players with 7 or more professional points in the 2002 Osaka Pro Standings.
> * DCI players with a Constructed rating of 1750 or higher. Byes do not pass down under any circumstances. Byes are based on DCI ratings published on March 20, 2002.
>
