Unstable
========



 Posted in [From the Lab](/en/articles/columns/from-the-lab-archive)
 on July 26, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello all and welcome back to the Lab. This week carries no theme for me to dive into, so I've got to do some quick riffing off the top of my head. It's not like I'm short of material lately. The unprecendented three-month stretch of new **Magic** cards that just wrapped up has provided a comfy pile of cards to fall back on for a while. My brain was addicted to this constant exposure to new cards for a while there; guess I've got to go cold turkey until [*Innistrad*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/productarticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/innistrad/productinfo) shows up this September. (And what's this about [*Dark Ascension*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/arcana/761) early next year? Looks like this upcoming year of **Magic** will be, let's say, tainted. In a good way, I hope.)

So what shall be today's subject matter, then? I'll tell you. Something completely unexpected. Uncharted waters. Something... *Un*.

It was all inspired by an email I received in response to last week's column. Not a deck list or combo idea, but a suggestion by Nathan No Last Name to the Evil Empire deck from [last week](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/152). That deck sought to combine the trio of Empirical artifacts and potentially go infinite. Nathan made an awesome suggestion:


> You should totally have included [\_\_\_\_\_](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) (from Unhinged). You can change its name to whatever piece you're missing, and you're already running [Mycosynth Lattice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Lattice) to make it an artifact. 
> 
> 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=74252)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=74252)  
I gaped at my computer screen. Looking back, it makes perfect sense for me. Besides the obvious interaction, [\_\_\_\_\_](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) is surely one of my favorite cards ever. (I really should lock myself in a penthouse apartment for three months and emerge with a steady Top 50 list one of these days.) I'm extremely fascinated with names, on a conceptual basis, and a card that has both no name and every name highly intrigues me.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_noName.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_noNameLeft01.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_noNameLeft02.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_noNameLeft03.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_noNameLeft04.jpg)[Mister No Name](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) has been covered in this column before, by the skilled Mark Gottlieb about seven years previous. A [whole article](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/mg153) was dedicated to [The Little Shapeshifter That Could](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252), and you should read every column Gottlieb put out during his run, including that one. Just a friendly reading recommendation.

I thought I'd see what hijinks [Underscore Man](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) could get up to in recent times. I landed on [Mitotic Manipulation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mitotic+Manipulation) and my heart soared. From there I decided to fully frame today's article in a silver border, building around various *Unhinged* cards. I've always wanted to do an *Un*-column, but I didn't want to reach back to *Unhinged* until I could invent a suitable excuse to do so. (Mission accomplished!)

I still live in hope that one day Mark Rosewater will decide that *Un*-sets can totally exist in today's **Magic**al landscape.

Thus, I'm Unstable today. Actually, make that every day. Mentally. I just hide it well.

  
Mitotic Me
So, back to that [Mitotic Manipulation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mitotic+Manipulation) deck I teased above. Of course the card I choose to link with [Awkward Pause](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) comes from a Gottlieb-designed set. [Mitotic Manipulation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mitotic+Manipulation) is a card I'd considered for a while, and at first I thought [Horizontal Line](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) was perfect. After all, you can activate its ability "anytime." Surely that means you can activate it in the midst of spells resolving, right? This is *Un*-Land, after all.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_mitotic.jpg)  
Well, you can't. According to the [*Unhinged* FAQ(TIWDAWCC)](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/article.aspx?x=magic/faq/unhinged), that's just too silly, even though you can activate [Identity Master](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) "anywhere," including from outside your actual deck. I was all pumped up to change [It](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252)'s name mid-Manipulation. Drat.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mitotic+Manipulation)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mitotic+Manipulation)  
Still, [A Magic Card](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) can still help us make use of [Mitotic Manipulation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mitotic+Manipulation). You just have to change the name before you cast the Manipulation, and cross your fingers. Hopefully you changed the name to one of the many colossal beatstick cards in the deck.

Another recent card that deals with names is [Doubling Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Chant). Since that ushers in green, I added a bunch of Elves, including [Elvish Archdruid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid). That way, you can actually ramp into your fatties if the [Doopliss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) antics don't work out. Doubling your Elves isn't too bad either.

Lingering After-Effects
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_stoneCold.jpg)  
While browsing [*Unhinged*](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advancedamp;set=%5B%22Unhinged%22%5D) in [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) in search of cards to break, I stumbled upon [Staying Power](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staying+Power) and knew instantly that I had found a deck. Containing what else, I wasn't sure... until the very next card in the set, alphabetically speaking: [Stone-Cold Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone-Cold+Basilisk). That's an ouch moment. Once your opponent reads [Stone-Cold Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone-Cold+Basilisk) with [Staying Power](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staying+Power) on the battlefield, that's it. He or she is stoned forever. You don't even need a win condition, since your opponent is helpless for the rest of the game.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

It's such a brutal combo that I have no idea how to build the rest of the deck. I started by protecting and tutoring for [Staying Power](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staying+Power) with [Sterling Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sterling+Grove). Then I added other fetchable enchantments that [Staying Power](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staying+Power) likes. [Wellspring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellspring) is basically a cheaper [Annex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annex), and [Gelid Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gelid+Shackles) can cause permanent defender.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

I couldn't not play [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) in the deck (check [Staying Power](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staying+Power)'s artwork), and threw in [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) as a soon-to-be-terrifyingly-large one-drop. [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) acts as a large finisher as well.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wild+Mongrel)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel)  
Finally, there were some diabolical white cards with power that I wanted to Stay. [Droning Bureaucrats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Droning+Bureaucrats) becomes that much more frustrating, and [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) should invoke despair, and/or tears. "You...can't...lose?"

There we go. Now onto my perhaps taboo idea, which involves [Fraction Jackson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fraction+Jackson) returning lots of creatures with a power of 1 and a toughness of 2. You know, ½ creatures.

Does that make sense? Cards are interpreted via their Oracle text, and in said text the line dividing the 1 and 2 is diagonally tilted, just like the line between the power and toughness of creatures. That was the idea-sparking moment.

So what ½ creatures would love to be returned to my hand over and over? Probably ones that could get back to the graveyard in due time. Probably ones that could produce lots of mana. [Llanowar Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Druid), you just won the lottery. Use the Druid to untap your [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s every turn for mucho green mana. But why stop there? Why not use [Umbral Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle) to untap [Fraction Jackson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fraction+Jackson), so he can return the Druid repeatedly (for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif).) Then cast the Druid, give it Lightning Greaves, and sacrifice it immediately. If you have six [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s, you can make [Fraction Jackson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fraction+Jackson) infinitely powered up (like a real superhero.) If you have seven or more, tack on infinite mana. And howww might I be finding these Equipment? [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic). Who's a ½. Yeah.

Burn, Baby, Burn
Now to my favorite deck of the column. I've said on previous occasions that my favorite decks don't set out to deplete my opponents' life total or deck size, but their sanity levels. When my opponent is screaming for the madness to end, that's when I grin like a maniac. The best part about this deck is that it makes that sentence a bit too literal.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl153_hottie.jpg)  
Check out [Red-Hot Hottie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Red-Hot+Hottie). Silly, no doubt, and a slight inconvenience, but ultimately harmless, right?

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Red-Hot+Hottie)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Red-Hot+Hottie)  
Wrong. One word: PROLIFERATE. I am going to proliferate as many third-degree-burn counters as I can. I want my opponent screaming until they choke up a lung or two.

If I give [Red-Hot Hottie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Red-Hot+Hottie) the [Power of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire), she can deliver third-degree-burn counters at will. The key here is that the damage doesn't have to be combat. The goal of the deck is simple: Ping as many different creatures as you can. Then proliferate them. At the end of every turn, your opponent is going to have to scream to keep those creatures alive. Sure, he or she could opt not to, but then you basically sweep the opposing side of the board.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

[Smokebraider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider), [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), and the rest of the Elemental gang join in here. I wanted more synergy with proliferate and [Power of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire), so I turned to some wither Elementals. [Kulrath Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kulrath+Knight) makes the third-degree-burn counters even zestier, denying their bearers the ability to enter combat at all. [Stigma Lasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stigma+Lasher) ensures that it's all downhill for your opponent, and [Tideforce Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tideforce+Elemental) is versatile and can untap more than usual.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

As a big finisher, I used [Hateflayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hateflayer), but only when the screaming plan isn't panning out. Seriously. When your opponent finally croaks a concession, the hoarse tone should make you tingle a bit.
