Modern Heart
============



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on December 7, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week, I asked if my readers would like to see my take on [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) in Modern. I received an overwhelmingly positive response. I decided to start working on a Modern [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) deck last Thursday and began playing the deck obsessively, tweaking it as I played matches. At this point, I have about thirty matches under my belt with various builds of [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) in Modern. Today, I'd like to share the finalized version of the deck.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab172_cross.jpg)  
[Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter) has been one of my favorite cards for a very long time, and [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) seemed like the engine that could elevate [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter) from the realm of pet card to the competitive world. I've dug as deep as [Semblance Anvil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Semblance+Anvil) in the past when looking for the same effect. [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) works beautifully with [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter), [Sphinx Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+Summoner), [Treasure Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage), and [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine).




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Heartless Summoning | Master Transmuter |

My first [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) deck in Modern was blue and black, splashing white for [Sharuum the Hegemon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharuum+the+Hegemon), [Sphinx of the Steel Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Steel+Wind), and [Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) in the sideboard. ([Grand Arbiter Augustin IV](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Arbiter+Augustin+IV) and [Lodestone Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Golem) make things very hard for combo opponents, especially when you have four of each.) However, the mana was rough for the three-color deck, I even used fetch lands and *Ravnica* dual lands to test the deck's maximum power level, and I was disappointed with how painful the mana base became.


I decided to switch the deck to a more consistent model. I cut the white entirely, and the deck became a [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) deck. I played three to four copies of [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) and four copies of [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation). [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning), [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter), and other accelerators allowed me to quickly stick the [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) and use [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) to protect it. (You don't have to pay for Pacts when you have [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) on the battlefield.) This version of the deck had one [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), and I found myself searching for it A LOT. I was searching up the [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) so much, that I ended up cutting the [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation)s and a bunch of the [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel)s in order to fill the deck with more copies of [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) and to max out on [Treasure Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage)s.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Platinum Angel | Treasure Mage |

I wanted to keep at least one copy of [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel), though. There are a lot of games where you're able to suit a [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) up with [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves), just about an auto-win in many Game 1s.


I would like to thank **Magic Online** user Danger (who says his real name is Jace) for playing a bunch of matches with me in the tournament practice room on MTGO while I refined my list.


After many games, I settled on the following list:

[Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) works really hard here. All of the creatures have a ton of value and making them all cost ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif) less is a huge deal. Don't cast a second [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) unless it's absolutely crucial to stick a [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) when you already have [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) on the table. One [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) is enough of an advantage mana wise, and losing [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter), [Etherium Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etherium+Sculptor), and [Treasure Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage) is enough of a disadvantage that we don't want a second copy on the battlefield.


[Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) are ridiculous here. Currently, I have three in the list, but I wouldn't be opposed to cutting a [Lodestone Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Golem) for the fourth one. [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) works especially well with [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter), often leading to turn-four [Sundering Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan) or [Platinum Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) with [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) equipped. It's also nice to haste up your [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)s and make them untargetable. In fact, it's nice to equip [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) to just about anything. It makes your clock deceptively fast and, in conjunction with the Wurmcoils, it makes it really hard for your opponent to set up a plan to burn you out.


![Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lightning+Greaves)  
[Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter) is up to her old tricks. [Sphinx Summoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+Summoner) is her best friend. It's worth noting that [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter)'s ability returns the artifact to your hand as part of the cost. This means that your opponent can't respond to the ability by killing the artifact you're bouncing. [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter) does a lot of work throughout the game. Once you've established a livable board situation you should find your [Sundering Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan) and use [Master Transmuter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter) to systematically destroy all of your opponent's lands. Once they have no lands you can further establish board presence and ensure victory with some [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)s.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab172_wurm.jpg)  
[Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) is the most important fatty for the archetype. These can be difficult to acquire, but the rest of the cards should all be very easy to pick up. [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) was the Prerelease promo for *Scars of Mirrodin*, so if you're having trouble finding the last copy or two you can probably borrow them from your friends who attended that Prerelease.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab172_wurmcoils.jpg)  
The interactions that occur in this deck can become very complex. I've lost at least two matches online because of the clock. Make sure you take your time and play a bunch of practice games with the deck before you start entering Constructed queues.

My opponent untapped and used [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge)'s flashback to destroy the unequipped Wurm token and attacked with the team. My opponent conceded.

The Zoo matchup seems very die roll dependent. I didn't see many [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge)s in testing, and I think people aren't playing more than one or two copies. A Zoo deck that draws more than one [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) will be nearly impossible to beat.


This deck is competitive. I won over 50% of the Constructed two-player queues I entered. The deck handily crushes the [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) decks. Be sure to shoot me an email or hit the forums with your latest deck ideas. I hope this deck was enough to get you excited for Modern season.
