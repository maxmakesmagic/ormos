The Blade of Goldnight
======================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on June 5, 2012 










It's sitting on my kitchen table."

  
Sometimes you realize things when there's time to fix them. You lost track of a favorite shirt only to have it dawn on you right before doing laundry. You couldn't remember the name of that place where you ordered that thing that one time, until it all rushed back as you see it coming up ahead on the road.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf198_gisela.jpg)[Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) | Art by [Jason Chan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Jason+Chan%22%5D)

Other times it's halfway through a cross-country flight when it strikes you that a 200-count box labeled "Gisela" is still sitting on your desk.

That's the situation I found myself in two weeks ago when traveling to [Grand Prix Anaheim](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpana12/welcome). Despite my best intentions, and the boatload of awesome fun I still has beside it all, I failed to bring the one deck I assured everyone would be there. While setting up [some Cube drafting](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpana12/day1amp;print=true#9) was something everyone seemed to enjoy, testing out [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) was something I had actually been looking forward to.

Thankfully, there's **Magic Online**.

With a recent update here on **DailyMTG.com**, every decklist now has a handy download button to get the deck text in a format ready to be read by **Magic Online**. So when I post [a vote-winning Commander deck](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/195)...

A Tale of Two Decks (in a Four-Player Game)
I played a few games using the deck because, as a righteous mathematician, I would never suggest that one game is a sufficient measure for performance. However, sharing just one game is more than sufficient for telling a story.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf198_transcendent.jpg)[Transcendent Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transcendent+Master) | Art by [Steven Belledin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Steven+Belledin%22%5D)

Contradictory forces are marvelous like that.

The game I chose is one that captured many different issues with the deck and how it interacts. While it's convenient for us, it also means I had to suffer through both the weaknesses of my deck *and* the overwhelming force of others it couldn't fight.

It was a confluence of conundrums, so to say.

I played with three others online: ChristChan, Gamepro\_man2004, and Helldendragon. We'll call them C, G, and H going forward. The commanders looked like this:

Some games start slow but this one had an amazing dash to begin. Thanks to a turn-one [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring), I had a turn-two [Transcendent Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transcendent+Master) level up to a 6/6 with lifelink and attack C on turn three. I attacked C because of the [Rhystic Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Study) then in play, but a [Venser, Shaper Savant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant) reset my aggression on the following turn.

With the haymakers dropping I decided to play my own by adding [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) to the mix. I was hoping for some multiplayer madness to happen, with damage to get thrown around elsewhere, but H cast [Thraximundar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraximundar) to attack *me*, stealing [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine).

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight)  
When C's turn rolled up, [Illusions of Grandeur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusions+of+Grandeur) appeared before being passed to my control. At this point I had the least potential mana and fewest cards in hand. To say I was confused about getting kicked while I was down would be an understatement.

Instead of delaying the inevitable, I let Illusions drop me to 13 life and cast [Sunblast Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunblast+Angel) to take out [Thraximundar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraximundar), Thada, and Zedruu (which had attacked me for good measure too). H wasted no time with [Bloodgift Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodgift+Demon) followed by [Plague Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Wind). The poor guy had barely made any new friends before they all left.

G's plan for the game was revealed when casting [Recollect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recollect) for [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), which brought back [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), which brought back the [Recollect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recollect), after all of which there was enough mana left over for [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura). C merely mustered Zedruu the second time before passing the turn, where I made [Razia, Boros Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razia%2C+Boros+Archangel) to attack and keep Ascension off four counters.

But H made a much bigger play by casting [Into the Core](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Core) for C's [Library of Leng](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Library+of+Leng) (which had kept C's grip overflowing) and G's [Lightning Greaves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves). This isn't interesting itself, but what it allowed was for H to follow through with a kicked [Rite of Replication](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication) for the now vulnerable [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan). H stole the [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) with [Volition Reins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volition+Reins).

G still had more resets in hand, this time a [Martial Coup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup) for five and the ever-present [Recollect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recollect) for the [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) still again. Better Than Bad, It's Good!
So what happened? Based on the other games I played, and some helpful deck dueling to confirm how several different hands would play out, both my deck and the players I ran into created some bad mojo:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf198_elspeth.jpg)[Elspeth, Knight-Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant) | Art by [Volkan Baga](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Volkan+Baga%22%5D)


> * The low count of lands, and reliance on [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring)-type sources of mana, left Gisela to naturally fall behind. Sometimes it was just bad draws and other times it was incidental destruction of artifacts. Hedging the probability more favorably seems on point.
> * There were some early plays, but having a few more would feel better. In the reverse direction, big Angels are awesome to play but don't necessarily hit as hard as things like [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) or [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) for recursion. Ways to join in or push back would be helpful.
> * While the group of friends I usually run Commander with all share somewhat similar views of decks and politics, the wide world of **Magic** ensures I'll always run into those who have a different perspective. Accounting for different experiences through just one constructed deck is very challenging; ideally I want to be able to keep pace within any game of Commander.
> 

The first two points can be adjusted easily.

| [Angelic Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Arbiter), [Oblation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblation) | [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) | Cheap ways to stymie an opponent. |

  
Streamlining the mana and adding some ways to break into opponents' actions will give Gisela a little more battle prowess at tables similar to what I saw online. The main thrust is unerringly true: go big and make Angels. After seeing so many situations where your deck's power is pulled apart makes the temptation to completely upend everything in order to fight future opponents is strong. But holding firm to how you want to enjoy your deck keeps things much more fun.

I know I've never found joy in feeling forced to do something simply because someone else insists on it. The line between cool and cruel can be very blurry depending upon how you look at **Magic**. I try to stay firmly on the cool side for everyone.

Which leads me to the final trick: giving Gisela another try. This time I'll take her to my local game store. I'll suit her up with some *Planechase*. I'll find some familiar faces who will have decks similarly situated. Gisela will fly again.

She still might not win, but playing with friends will certainly help gloss over any loss.
