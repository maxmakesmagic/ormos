Seek Ye the Darkness
====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 23, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar














|  |
| --- |
| *Spirit of the Night, from the* Mirage *set, can be put into play directly from your library by sacrificing Breathstealer, Urborg Panther, and Feral Shadow.* |

I was one of the many **Magic** players who, during the *Mirage* days, desperately tried to make a deck in which [Urborg Panther](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Panther), [Breathstealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breathstealer), and [Feral Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Shadow) united in sacrificial glory to bring [Spirit of the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Night) directly into play.


Most of the graveyard recursion during those times was of the "Enchant Dead Creature" variety, like [Animate Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animate+Dead) and [Dance of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dance+of+the+Dead)—enchantments that would "conveniently" fall off of a reanimated protection from black [Spirit of the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Night). Getting the Spirit into play was not easy; the thrill back then was partly in finding the correct pieces of the puzzle and partly in the thematic idea of Nightstalkers heralding a nigh-unstoppable avatar of death onto the table. The Nightstalkers and [Spirit of the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Night) have a huge coolness factor.


Coolness is back. And this time it’s a heck of a lot easier to bring your omnipotent-manifestation-of-all-that-is-evil into play.


We begin with [Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Supplicant). A 1/1 for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) is nothing to crow about—in fact, it’s worthy of disdain and pity. But the Supplicant can sacrifice itself and any two other Clerics (or three Clerics other than itself... sneaky little creep) to go find its dark master.


 



![Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dark+Supplicant)

Even without knowing what this big nasty Scion fellow does, we have an intriguing proposition...


Turn 1, play swamp, play [Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Supplicant) #1.  

Turn 2, play swamp, play [Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Supplicant) #2, play [Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Supplicant) #3, use [Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Supplicant) #1’s ability...


The Heir of Freakin’ Evil has arrived. Turn 2.


Improbable? Surely. But also not so far-fetched.


Luckily for everyone who adores black, [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness) lives up to its ominous name. In fact it happens to be one of the coolest fatties to come along since, well, [Spirit of the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Night).


 



![Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Scion+of+Darkness)

It is pretty clear to me that you will rarely, if ever, cast [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness). ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170413132456im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless5.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) is a lot to ask of any deck. I suppose on rare occasions you will find yourself on Turn 30, more than halfway into your deck, with access to eight mana and [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness) in your hand. It’s nice to know it *can* be cast in a pinch. But unlike a [Crush of Wurms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Wurms) or [Time Stretch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Stretch), it is unnecessary to pack your deck full of mana acceleration just to cast [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness) the good old fashioned way.


Instead, [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness) will enter play for one of two reasons: 1) You have activated a [Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Supplicant), or 2) You have somehow found another means of graveyard reanimation.


Which you use depends on a number of different factors. If your deck is built around graveyard manipulation—full of [Entomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb), [Cephalid Vandal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Vandal), and [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) along with [Zombify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify), [Exhume](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exhume), and [Reya Dawnbringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reya+Dawnbringer)—then all you care about is that [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness) is huge, it has trample, and it steals opposing creatures from their graveyards to join your own army. [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness) is bad-ass enough to warrant serious consideration for any deck that cheats its creatures into play.

If you choose the [Dark Supplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Supplicant) route, your deck is based around Clerics. You are probably using [Rotlung Reanimator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotlung+Reanimator), [Cabal Archon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Archon), and [Doomed Necromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Necromancer). Your deck is also probably either monoblack, black/white (taking advantage of white’s excellent Clerics) or black/blue (taking advantage of blue’s card-drawing and [Artificial Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificial+Evolution)-type abilities). [Scion of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Darkness), in this deck, is likely the only non-Cleric creature, unless of course you also choose to include [Gangrenous Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gangrenous+Goliath) or some other silliness.
