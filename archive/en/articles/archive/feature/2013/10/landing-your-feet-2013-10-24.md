Landing on Your Feet
====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 24, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







for years, the Lands deck has been floating around Legacy. The first list I can remember had forty-three lands and seemed more like an absurd joke than an actual deck, although it was neither. As new toys (and lands) show up in each new set, the land count has dropped to its current thirty-six, but the core of the deck remains the same. [Manabond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabond) is long gone, with [Exploration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration), [Mox Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Diamond), and [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) providing the main incentives to play millions of lands in your Legacy deck. 

![Thespian's Stage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thespian%27s+Stage) Besides adding mana, what do the lands in this deck do? It's clear that playing a ton of them is what powers [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Exploration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploration), but the lands actually have to have use for the whole engine to work. Luckily, Legacy is vast enough that there are plenty of lands with sweet abilities, and this deck plays a whole lot of them. [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) is the most obvious example, but the fun certainly doesn't end there. [Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter), [Bojuka Bog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bojuka+Bog), [Karakas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas), [The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Tabernacle+at+Pendrell+Vale), [Rishadan Port](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port), [Tranquil Thicket](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tranquil+Thicket), [Creeping Tar Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit), [Maze of Ith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith), and [Glacial Chasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Chasm) all bring cool powers to the table, and the power of [Tolaria West](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolaria+West) and [Intuition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) helps find whichever one you are missing. 

 That isn't the end of the list, either. [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) and [Thespian's Stage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage) deserve their own mention, because they combine to make a 20/20 for very little effort. Copy the [Dark Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Depths) with [Thespian's Stage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thespian%27s+Stage), keep the Stage, which conveniently has no counters on it, and profit. In other combo news, [Grove of the Burnwillows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) pairs with [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) to reward you for dredging [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), and gets rid of annoying 2-toughness creatures at very little cost. 

 The deck is rounded out by a couple other spells. [Zuran Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Orb), [Engineered Explosives](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives), [Ensnaring Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge), and [Oblivion Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Stone) all provide some solid utility, and all are findable with [Academy Ruins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins). [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) is a little hard to cast, but incredibly powerful, and gives the deck some much-needed disruption. 

 Playing the deck is a little strange. Its goal is to just get [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) going, and almost any hand with Loam and the mana to cast it is keepable. [Intuition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) also works to find Loam, but hands with neither can be quite dangerous. If you can't land a Loam in the first couple turns, the deck does have a fair amount of disconnected pieces, so be aware of that.
