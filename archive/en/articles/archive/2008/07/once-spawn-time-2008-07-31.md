Once a Spawn a Time
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 31, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar












A few weeks ago, in the Before Time when I wasn't alternately ruining and saving **Magic**, I asked the ever-diligent, always creative Johnnies of the world (who also read this column) to choose one of five interesting, but perhaps underused, *Shadowmoor* cards. I would then build some decks around the chosen card and take these decks with me to U.S. Nationals in Chicago to use at the gunslingling tables. You guys voted. The card is [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe). The decks are here (kind of). U.S. Nationals is this weekend. Chicago is down there somewhere. (Near the lake. You can't miss it.)


### A Brief Guide to Spawnwrithing


 



![Spawnwrithe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spawnwrithe)

When you're building decks around a particular card (in this case, [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe)), one way to start brainstorming is to break the card down into its component parts. I rarely do this as formally as I'm about to, but the following list consists of things that I always keep in the back of my mind when I'm trying to find a new and/or fun way to use a card.

1. [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) costs ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif). Being a three-mana green creature usual means one thing: mana Elves! Why wait all the way until turn three when you can play a turn one Elf or [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and play [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) on turn two? Get that guy out there lickety-split, before your opponent has a chance to get set up. The other advantage to the ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif) mana cost is that the single green means [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) is playable in two-, three-, four-, five-, and possible even six-colour decks!


Also note that the converted mana cost is three, which means you can return it to play with [Order of Whiteclay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Order+of+Whiteclay) or [Unearth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unearth), play it for free with [Aluren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren), and if you want to get fancy, with a [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) in play you can search out [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) with a number of Rebels and Mercenaries or return it from your graveyard to your hand with a variety of Kamigawan soulshifters.


2. [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) is an Elemental. What does this mean? Well, for starters, you can tutor for it with [Flamekin Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger). You can also play it with [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) mana or from your graveyard with [Horde of Notions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horde+of+Notions). You can play it cheaper with [Brighthearth Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brighthearth+Banneret), champion it with [Supreme Exemplar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Exemplar), sacrifice it to [Hearthcage Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hearthcage+Giant) or [Seething Pathblazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Pathblazer), put it directly into play with [Incandescent Soulstoke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incandescent+Soulstoke), and you can reveal it to play an untapped [Primal Beyond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Beyond) or a discounted [Flamekin Bladewhirl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Bladewhirl). You know, the usual. Putting [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) into an Elemental deck is just what a reader named Isai did. It's also what David Williams did at Pro Tour – Hollywood when he showed up with this (probably insane) twenty-land, thirty-seven-creature deck:

3. [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) is a 2/2. It's a Gray Ogre (or a souped-up Grizzly Bear), and, as a result, it's about as fearsome in combat. Anything from a large-beaked old crone to a bipedal burro can take it down in a fight. Obviously, there are ways to remedy this, but for now, I'm going to look at how this bug can actually be a feature. For example, the two power makes [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) a viable target for the abilities of Dwarven Warriors, Dwarven Nomad, [Crafty Pathmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crafty+Pathmage), and [Soratami Mirror-Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soratami+Mirror-Guard). If you'd prefer not to rely on a fragile one-toughness creature to sneak your guy through, you could do as Goran B. suggested and use [Writ of Passage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Writ+of+Passage). Tawnos's Wand is another permanent, power-checking way to make your [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe)s unblockable, while Hearth Charm or the obscure-for-a-reason Runed Arch provide a one-shot version of the same ability. Similarly, its two power allows you to fetch [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) with Imperial Recruiter and return it from your graveyard to play with [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark), while its two toughness makes it work with Goblin Kites, Phantasmal Mount, and other improbable modes of transportation.

Just watch out for Aysen Bureaucrats!


4. [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) makes \*gasp\* [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) tokens. I'll start with the more obvious stuff first. Any token producer could benefit from [Doubling Season](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season), [Rhys the Redeemed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed), or [Parallel Evolution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Evolution) (as Christian M., Drake, Christoffer and others pointed out by email). Copying [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) itself with [Dual Nature](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dual+Nature), [Followed Footsteps](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Followed+Footsteps), or [Spitting Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Image) seems like it could get out of hand rather quickly. Most tokens, for all intents and purposes, have a converted mana cost of zero. Since [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) makes tokens that are copies of itself, those tokens will also have a mana cost of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif). This means you would have to pay three mana to kill them with [Pernicious Deed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed), three fuse counters to destroy them with [Powder Keg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg), or pay ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) to kill a single one with [Disembowel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disembowel). It also means that the green-mana-symbol-loving [Primalcrux](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux) will get +1/+1 for each [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) token you control. And because the tokens also bear the name "[Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe)," they can all be pumped with [Echoing Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Courage), killed with [Echoing Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Decay), or bounced back to your hand with [Echoing Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth).


Just watch out for [Winnow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winnow)!


5. To make these tokens, [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) has to deal combat damage to a player. As far as I can tell, we can give Spawny some assistance in a couple ways. First of all, we can give it some kind of non-trample evasion. I've already talked a bit about making it unblockable with certain cards, but there are many, many other ways to accomplish the same thing. Besides straight-up unblockability with something like [Protective Bubble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protective+Bubble) or [Deepchannel Mentor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deepchannel+Mentor), as suggested by Kevin G., you can grant [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) a less reliable, but still effective, form of evasion like flying, fear, shadow, protection from [relevant quality], or landwalk.


![Double Cleave, Rage Reflection, and Fireshrieker](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/house/cm134_cardfan.jpg)The other thing we can do to help out [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) is to allow it to hit more than once per turn. There are a few ways of doing this. You can give it double strike with something like [Double Cleave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Double+Cleave), [Battle Mastery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Mastery), [Rage Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Reflection), [Sunhome, Fortress of the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome%2C+Fortress+of+the+Legion), or [Fireshrieker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireshrieker). Or you can give yourself another attack step with a card like [Fury of the Horde](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fury+of+the+Horde), [Waves of Aggression](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waves+of+Aggression), or [Godo, Bandit Warlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godo%2C+Bandit+Warlord). (Note that unless your creatures have haste somehow, any [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe)s created during the first attack step won't be able participate in round two.) You can also just take another turn with [Savor the Moment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savor+the+Moment) or another two turns with [Time Stretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Stretch) if you want to make a bunch of [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe)s all at once.


6. [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) has trample. Admittedly, trample is a form of evasion, but it gets less effective the smaller the creature. [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe), with its two power, can only ever trample over one-toughness creatures and there's a good chance that it won't survive any encounter with another critter. As always, there are ways around this, most notably pump effects. [Moldervine Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak), [Griffin Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Guide), or [Gaea's Anthem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Anthem) will do it on a permanent basis, while [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) and [Barkshell Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barkshell+Blessing) will help you get through once. Conversely, cards like [Snakeform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snakeform) and [Ovinize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinize) can shrink opposing creatures to the requisite one toughness. The reason I made this a separate category is because there are cards (okay, just one—[Stonebrow, Krosan Hero](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonebrow%2C+Krosan+Hero)) that care about trampling creatures.

This is another deck where you there are some nice budget alternatives. If you wanted to focus more on Stonebrow, you could try [Hungry Spriggan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hungry+Spriggan) and [Scuzzback Marauders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scuzzback+Marauders) since they both have an odd mana cost, have trample, and fit the colour scheme.

A number of people wrote in suggesting various ways to enhance [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe), many of which we've already looked at. Eli D. suggested using [Stonewood Invocation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonewood+Invocation) and [Evolution Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Charm) to make sure your [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe)s can connect. [Cold-Eyed Selkie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cold-Eyed+Selkie) fits right in to this sort of strategy, both wanting to connect and wanting to connect for a lot. In a similar vein, another reader, Skander, suggested playing [Cephalid Constable](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Constable) alongside [Spawnwrithe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawnwrithe) and using [Wings of Velis Vel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wings+of+Velis+Vel) to pump them. This got my attention, so I cooked up a deck. Here's the version I've been tooling with online. It could use some work, but my heart nearly burst out of my chest with excitement when I actually connected with a [Cephalid Constable](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Constable) enhance by both [Wings of Velis Vel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wings+of+Velis+Vel) and [Briarhorn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Briarhorn). Of course, I was so excited that I clicked right through the Constable's triggered ability and only got seven damage, rather than a one-sided [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), out of the deal. There are many, many directions you can take this deck, but maybe I will get into those at a later date.
