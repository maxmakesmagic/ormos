Black-Green Midrange with (11) Reid Duke
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 28, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a member of the Wizards of the Coast coverage team. 






Eleventh-ranked player Reid Duke needs no introduction. He's a member of one of the top Pro teams, Team Pantheon, is a Platinum player in the Pro Player's Club and has a Pro Tour Top 8 and a few Grand Prix titles under his belt. This weekend he's piloting a Black-Green Midrange deck, very similar to (12) [Sam Black's Great Aurora deck](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpny16/black-green-aurora-control-with-sam-black-2016-05-07) that he piloted at Grand Prix New York three weeks ago.


“I watched Sam's progress with his Great Aurora deck from Grand Prix New York. I liked a lot of things about it and I tweaked it to include [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) and [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker), my favorite cards.” Reid went on to explain that he really liked the ramp elements of Sam's deck, but he didn't feel the need to include [The Great Aurora](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Great+Aurora) or multiple copies of [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger). He did however feel that the ramp spells like [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage) and [Nissa's Renewal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Renewal) were necessary in the deck. The deck is extremely mana-hungry and in addition to casting expensive spells, you also need mana to sacrifice clue tokens, awaken [Ruinous Path](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruinous+Path), and activate creature lands.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gpmin16/gpmin16_bg-midrange_reid-1.jpg)  
*Reid Duke*


“My deck is a traditional midrange deck with some small elements of ramp,” explains Reid.


“I love [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) and [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate). They are my favorite cards in Standard. [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) is the best card advantage creature to play and [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) is the most efficient creature in Standard. I knew that I wanted to play these cards today so I built the best deck I could with them.”


Reid also played Black/Green Midrange at Grand Prix New York but his list was very different from what he's playing today. In New York, Reid opted for a more controlling deck with more copies of [Seasons Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasons+Past) and [Dark Petition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Petition). “I wasn't happy with the controlling [Seasons Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasons+Past) deck. I wanted to play a deck that had more angles of attack, and the version with creatures keeps you from falling behind early.” In addition to [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) and [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate), Reid is certainly playing some interesting creatures in his build including [The Gitrog Monster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Gitrog+Monster), [Woodland Bellower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Bellower), and [Kalitas, Traitor of Ghet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalitas%2C+Traitor+of+Ghet).


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gpmin16/gpmin16_bg-midrange_reid-2.jpg)


“[Woodland Bellower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Bellower) is my pet card. It searches for all my favorite creatures. It kinda feels like [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) because it gives you a [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) which is very likely to be a 4/5 by the time you cast him. It matches up well against spot removal and [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher) and it also survives [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish). I'm either searching for [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) or [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker), and these creatures are good in all stages of the game.”


Speaking of [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish), despite Reid's deck being very creature-heavy, he opted to play [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) in his main deck. “Languish is tricky to play because I want to play creatures early, but it's really important to have [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) main deck because you need it against [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and Humans. It's the best card to play in Standard right now. I'm only playing two copies main deck, but [Dark Petition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Petition) helps me find it when I need it.” Although [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) does sometimes kill your own creatures, [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) is likely to survive it and [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) has already given you plenty of value by the time you cast it. [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) also has nice synergy with [Kalitas, Traitor of Ghet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kalitas%2C+Traitor+of+Ghet). Although it will often die to your own [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish), any nontoken creature that your opponent has on the battlefield when [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) resolves will give you a 2/2 zombie token.


“Sam didn't play Kalitas in his version, but I love it,” explains Reid. “It's great against [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) decks and [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), and lifegain in a slow deck can be valuable.”


Despite Reid's deck being less controlling and containing more creatures, He still opted to play a decent amount of ramp including two [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage), two [Explosive Vegetation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Vegetation) and one [Nissa's Renewal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Renewal). The ramp helps [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) get big quickly so that it survives [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish). The ramp is also great with [Seasons Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasons+Past) because it gives you more mana to cast the spells you return. The last thing you want is to have a handful of cards but not enough time to cast them all. Although Reid is not playing many copies of the [Dark Petition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Petition)/[Seasons Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasons+Past) combo, he has been happy with the one copy of [Seasons Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seasons+Past) to search for when he needs to refill his hand.
