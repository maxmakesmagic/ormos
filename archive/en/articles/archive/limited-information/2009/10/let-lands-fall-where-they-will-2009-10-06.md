The problem was that it was too slow.


Sure, if my opponents gave me enough time to get into the game I would probably whomp them. But if I stumbled even a little, or if they came out particularly quickly I would have no shot.


With that in mind, I knew I had to look at other builds. 


### Making Things Faster


Often you will have to make concessions with your Sealed Deck, but before you do you should always be sure to explore your other options. Sure, you might think that the first deck that you gravitate towards in a Sealed pool is the way to go, and you might decide that you want to spend a few minutes with it to make sure that you know exactly how you would build it before you start looking at other configurations, but it is very difficult to hurt yourself by doing a bit of extra experimenting. 


Once I took a look at each of my colors organized by converted mana cost, I decided to look at white as my main color. 


I tried to put together a green-white-red deck, but the mana was kind of awkward for what I wanted to do. Yes the [Kazandu Refuge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Refuge) and the [Graypelt Refuge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graypelt+Refuge) helped a lot, but it still wasn't enough to make things run smoothly. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Kazandu Refuge | Graypelt Refuge |

There simply wasn't a way to make a coherent white deck that had green and red in it. The support cards just weren't there.


While I could have played green as my second color, my only real reason to do so was for mana fixing / landfall enablers, and there isn't much of a point in that if I wasn't going to go for a very color-intensive deck. I could have played red as my second color, but that wouldn't have offered me much outside of easy access to my [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning)s and a couple of cheap creatures. To top that off, once green is relegated to a splash, it actually offers very little save for a [Territorial Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Territorial+Baloth) and the tough-to-cast [Rampaging Baloths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Baloths).


I peeked at a black splash, but that was similarly underwhelming.


Then I looked at pairing by blue with my white.

### Exciting Ways to Do Unexciting Things


[Magosi, the Waterveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magosi%2C+the+Waterveil) deserves some extra mention. At first I was irrationally excited by this card. I saw all of the good that it could do. If you and your opponent are in a board stall, where it isn't really safe for either of you to make any attacks for fear of a big counterattack, [Magosi, the Waterveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magosi%2C+the+Waterveil) will completely turn things around for you.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li59_magosiSplash.jpg)  
![Magosi, the Waterveil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Magosi%2C+the+Waterveil)At the end of one of your opponent's unexciting turns, you activate [Magosi, the Waterveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magosi%2C+the+Waterveil) to put a counter on it, giving your opponent another likely to be unexciting turn. 


If you have something good to do, you could cash in your extra turn immediately, maybe sneaking in a few points of damage with your evasion creatures and then leaving everything back on defense on your second turn. Or you might just bank your turn, preventing your opponent from ever making too big of a move until you can find a time to put your extra turn to good use.


Now, my Prerelease pool wasn't one that could take advantage of those types of situations, but it was able to take advantage of the fact the [Magosi, the Waterveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magosi%2C+the+Waterveil) gave me an extra landfall trigger. I won several races by getting another landfall trigger for my [Windrider Eel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windrider+Eel) and my [Adventuring Gear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adventuring+Gear). 


I lost a couple of damage in a couple of games because I was unable to play my preferred spell because [Magosi, the Waterveil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magosi%2C+the+Waterveil) comes into play tapped, but all in all it wound up being better for me than any of the lands in the [Teetering Peaks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teetering+Peaks) cycle would have been.


### Come Equipped


I found [Grappling Hook](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grappling+Hook) to be quite underwhelming. I figured that with my relatively low curve I would want something powerful and mana intensive to help me close out the game. 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/li/li59_equipCross.jpg)  
Don't get me wrong—[Grappling Hook](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grappling+Hook) did win some games for me, but the majority of the time it sat in my hand unused, or on the board unequipped because my opponent had a removal spell ready the time that I spent a turn equipping it to one of my more reasonable creatures.


![Grappling Hook](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grappling+Hook)
  
Now you might not lose an extra card when a creature that has a piece of Equipment attached to it gets killed, but you do lose a ton of time. If your opponent [Hideous End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+End)s the [Windrider Eel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windrider+Eel) that you equipped your [Grappling Hook](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grappling+Hook) to, you will have spent a total of twelve mana over three turns on something that your opponent spent a mere three mana to deal with.


For the kind of mana investment that [Grappling Hook](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grappling+Hook) requires, it doesn't offer enough oomph for me to want to spend a draft pick on it, or even play it in the majority of my Sealed pools.


![Explorer's Scope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Explorer%27s+Scope)
  
The [Explorer's Scope](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explorer%27s+Scope)s in my pool didn't strike me as particularly exciting for my white-blue deck because of my relatively low curve, and they seemed weak in my green-blue-X builds because their curves were too high .... But they did pop out at me as having a lot of potential in decks that curve out more normally. In addition to being able to ramp you up and smooth out your draws, [Explorer's Scope](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explorer%27s+Scope) triggers your landfall, which can be extremely valuable.


![Adventuring Gear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Adventuring+Gear)
  
[Adventuring Gear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adventuring+Gear) was awesome for me, and while it's no [Trusty Machete](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusty+Machete), it's certainly better than a rusty machete. (I'm sorry, I couldn't help myself with that one. You actually have no idea how many puns I hold back on a week-to-week basis.). I think that [Adventuring Gear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adventuring+Gear) will show itself to be a fairly high draft pick, and an almost automatic inclusion in Sealed pools.


![Blade of the Bloodchief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blade+of+the+Bloodchief)
  
[Blade of the Bloodchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+of+the+Bloodchief) has a lot of potential. At almost any point in the game it will make combat extremely awkward for your opponent. The fact that it costs only ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif) to cast and ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif) to equip means that you will usually be able to reequip it without cannibalizing your turn, a trait which is of upmost importance for a piece of Equipment to have.


Be ready to move [Blade of the Bloodchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+of+the+Bloodchief) around frequently. While it can be okay to put all of your eggs in one basket, if you have the freedom to share [Blade of the Bloodchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+of+the+Bloodchief)'s +1+1 counters amongst several of your creatures, you should be able to ensure yourself an advantage even if your opponent has a well-timed removal spell.


![Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blazing+Torch)
  
[Blazing Torch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) is a very straightforward card that is pretty much exactly as good as it looks. You wouldn't want to spend one of your first few picks in the draft on this, but once you get to about picks three through five, this will start to stand out as a very attractive option.


![Spidersilk Net](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spidersilk+Net)
  
[Spidersilk Net](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidersilk+Net) seems like it's going to be an important role player in slower decks and in decks that need a way to deal with evasion creatures. While you shouldn't waste an early pick on this card, you should feel free to pick it up late in a pack if none of the other options are very appealing.


![Trailblazer's Boots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trailblazer%27s+Boots)
  
[Trailblazer's Boots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trailblazer%27s+Boots) has applications as a potential sideboard card, if you know that your opponent has several nonbasic lands, and you know that you're going to need a way to break through late in the game, and you're confident that you can get to the late game, and .... A ton of things have to go right for [Trailblazer's Boots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trailblazer%27s+Boots) to even be decent. Yes, it's better than a basic land or a [Trapfinder's Trick](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trapfinder%27s+Trick), but you shouldn't go at all out of your way to pick up [Trailblazer's Boots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trailblazer%27s+Boots).


![Trusty Machete](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trusty+Machete)
  
[Trusty Machete](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusty+Machete) is almost certainly the best piece of Equipment in *Zendikar*. [Blade of the Bloodchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+of+the+Bloodchief) might seem flashier, but [Trusty Machete](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusty+Machete) has the ability to take over a game very early or give you that extra oomph you need to win a race late. 


There are no doubt times when [Blade of the Bloodchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+of+the+Bloodchief) will be better in the middle of a drawn-out game, but the majority of the time it just won't be able to hold a candle to this uncommon piece of Equipment.


I would feel quite content to first-pick a [Trusty Machete](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusty+Machete), and I would be very happy if I got passed one.
