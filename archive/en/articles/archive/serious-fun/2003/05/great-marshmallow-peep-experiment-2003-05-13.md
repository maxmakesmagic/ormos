The Great Marshmallow Peep Experiment
=====================================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on May 13, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_anthonyalongi.jpg)
By Anthony Alongi











A science experiment for us all: 

In our best laboratory, with our finest material, what can we make with an Ancient Ooze? 

![Ancient Ooze](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/serious/ancient00ze.jpg)

No, you're not going to win a tournament with this. That's not what I mean by this science experiment. 

What I mean is, what are the universal limits of a deck that uses four of these? Are we doomed to the fate of Dr. Frankenstein wannabes, creating cute but deformed Marshmallow Peep decks? Or can we create something with a bit better stitching? 

There's only one way to find out. 

Fair warning: Unlike the past couple of weeks, I don't offer alternatives for the rares I suggest in these decks. Of course, there are always substitutions that work here and there, but with this card, you're either playing with eye-popping cards or trick cards—both of which tend to be rare, so no apologies. 

### THE RELENTLESS MARCH OF THE MARSHMALLOW PEEPS

I regret the second Marshmallow Peeps reference right there, but you have to realize I'm writing this a while after Easter, and the Marshmallow Peeps are still in my house. For more information on Marshmallow Peeps—I have no idea if these exist beyond America's borders, or if the rest of the world is unblemished—see **[peepresearch.org](http://www.peepresearch.org)**. There is a point where a Marshmallow Peep undergoes, and I quote, "a metamorphosis . . . the subject entered the stage which scientists call 'ball of charred goo'." Goo = ooze, and peeps are often ancient, so we're on topic here. 

The first deck is a standard "wow, look at those creatures!" deck.

Play creatures at instant speed from your hand or your graveyard. At sorcery speed (and triggered ability speed, as someone kills your [Elvish Piper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Piper) with a [Pattern of Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth)), play them from your library. Naturally, you could add [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) and go get them from outside of the game; but that seemed a bit much to get us started. The point is, you can make the Ancient Ooze much bigger at a reasonably fast clip, and use 1x your favorite green rares. (Or Dragons. But we did Dragons two weeks ago, and I never see [Carnassid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnassid) in a deck.) 

Props to Jamie Wakefield, Dojo writer of old, for the [Natural Order](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order) - [Sliver Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sliver+Queen) trick. I still love this one. Terrific when your opponents are playing Slivers. (Sliver Queen is a green creature.)

Please, in the name of all that is good and green on this earth, do *not* go out and buy a bunch of copies of [Karn's Touch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn%27s+Touch) and [Titania's Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titania%27s+Song) because you believe this deck will make you a star. 

In an earlier draft, I had a thing in there with [Rakalite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakalite) - [Tawnos's Coffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tawnos%27s+Coffin) - [Karn's Touch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn%27s+Touch) - [Risky Move](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Risky+Move), but that was too, er, powerful. 

How about that [Dominate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dominate) - Ancient Ooze synergy, eh? You can't get good tech like this from just anywhere, you know. 

You can only get it from the mouths of Marshmallow Peeps.
