Battle Pod
==========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 16, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











Let it be known that, if you put up a successful result with a [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) list, and I manage to stumble upon it in the vast world of the Internet, chances are you will be appearing in this very column soon thereafter. I have already told [my story of Battle of Wits](/en/articles/archive/battle-wits-2012-07-30) before, but I am far from the only lover of the card. [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) seems to stir up the brewer inside of us all, filling our heads with dreams of giant decks, untapping and announcing the trigger from our five-mana enchantment. One of the beautiful things about [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), though, and perhaps the reason everyone loves the card, is that outside of requiring an abnormally large deck, restrictions to your deck are nonexistent. You can build a [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck that fits your play style or contains your favorite colors pretty easily.


Rich Lisitza decided that he wanted to put his own spin on the deck for a recent TCGPlayer.com Qualifier tournament in Illinois. Rich took another popular card among players, [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), and combined it with [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) for an awesome brew. [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) is at its best when it is increasing consistency in your deck by providing a reusable tutor effect, and if you have ever played [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), one thing you certainly know is that the deck can always use more consistency. [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) had some influence on the creatures that Rich ended up running, such as [Balefire Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balefire+Dragon), but outside of some minor concessions to curve, [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) fits into most [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) shells.

This particular list gets to play some of my favorite cards that have never had a proper home. My favorite among these is [Etched Monstrosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Monstrosity). [Etched Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Oracle) has been a favorite of mine since it was printed, and although the Monstrosity is a little harder to pull off, it has a huge upside. [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) gets to play the card as a vanilla 5/5 in dire situations, but will usually be able to cash in for three cards as well, making the card pretty appealing. [Mwonvuli Beast Tracker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mwonvuli+Beast+Tracker) is another cool addition to [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), as it gives you even more control over what you draw each turn and makes for an excellent pair with [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). 


[Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) is hardly a solved deck, or even close to one, allowing new and unique versions to constantly be evolved. While I doubt it will be in the tier one range, well, ever, it is a lot of fun and, as Rich showed us, it can even do some winning from time to time!
