The Pauper Gauntlet
===================



 Posted in **Magic Online**
 on August 1, 2018 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20180720152131im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_Dan-Musser.jpg?itok=oLrbZZ3O)
By Dan Musser




 A former tournament grinder from Ohio, Dan is currently part of the Play Design team within Magic R&D. 






It is time for another *Magic Online* Gauntlet! This time we delve into *Magic*'s most "common" format: Pauper!


**What is the Pauper Gauntlet?**


This is an event for you to quickly dip your toes into the delightful Pauper format without doing all the work and spending a ton of time analyzing lists and lists of cards. Here, we have chosen eleven top decks that represent the Pauper metagame. When you sign up for the Pauper Gauntlet, one deck will be randomly selected, and you will be able to play that deck during the entire League, though you won't be able to keep those cards at the end of that event.


**Pauper? Why Pauper?**


Pauper has a robust history of support from the community. In the early days of *Magic Online*, before there was League support, fans and players took to forums to organize independent Pauper challenges and events. These days, community support for the format is larger than ever, with paper Pauper tournaments popping up everywhere from Grand Prix and other premier tournaments to even many local stores. There are forums dedicated to analyzing the format. But the largest and most vibrant Pauper community remains on *Magic Online*, where thousands of players compete each week.


**What decks are in the Pauper Gauntlet?**


Want to rebuy your [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) using the ninjitsu ability of your [Ninja of the Deep Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours), drawing a card and setting up another Spellstutter counter? How about draining your opponent's life with [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel) while you have [Cuombajj Witches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cuombajj+Witches) and [Oubliette](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oubliette) on the battlefield? Maybe sacrificing all your artifacts to the great [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog) to create one giant monster is more your style?


Pauper is full of awesome decks, and we have chosen eleven of the top strategies for you to enjoy.


The Decks
=========


**Izzet Delver**


Pauper's premier tempo deck. The main strategy here is to apply pressure with an early [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), backed up with removal in the form of [Skred](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skred) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) tossed in with a little disruption from the classic [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) pulls double duty, being a pesky attacker while also providing some light interaction. [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) and [Ninja of the Deep Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours) allow you to get back some desired value after tussling one-for-one with your opponent in the early game.

**Boros Midrange**


Value is the name of the game with this artifact-based Boros midrange deck. Begin accruing card advantage by deploying an early [Thraben Inspector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Inspector) or [Prophetic Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism). Follow that up with evasive threats [Kor Skyfisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Skyfisher) and [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk), which also let you rebuy your initial value permanents. After pecking away at your opponent with your flying threats, finish them off with your powerful burn spells, [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).

**Inside OutÂ Combo**


The most feared Pauper combo deck features a seemingly innocuous defensive creature, [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe). The primary goal here is to discard a whole bunch of cards, giving [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe) a huge amount of toughness, and then cast [Inside Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inside+Out) to attack for massive damage. Facilitating this delicate interaction are a few protection spells in [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) and [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). [Gush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush) can put four cards in your hand all by itself, giving you plenty of fuel to discard to the Tribe. To top it all off, make sure you can break through blockers by casting [Shadow Rift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Rift). (Note: Even if you discard cards to [Tireless Tribe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe) after casting [Inside Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inside+Out), you will pump its power!)

**Elves**


Throughout *Magic*'s rich history, plenty of powerful Elves have been printed. In the initial turns, your goal is to develop a few mana-producing creatures such as [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and [Priest of Titania](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Priest+of+Titania). What you do next will depend on what your opponent is doing. Need to gain a bunch of life? Use [Wellwisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellwisher) in combination with [Quirion Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Ranger). Does your opponent have spot removal? [Lys Alana Huntmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lys+Alana+Huntmaster) has your back. Ready to defeat the enemy? [Timberwatch Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberwatch+Elf) to the rescue! Finally, if you want to draw more cards than anyone else, [Lead the Stampede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lead+the+Stampede) and [Distant Melody](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distant+Melody) are just what you need.

**Mono-Black Control**


One of Pauper's longest-standing control decks. Using a myriad of the most efficient common removal spells in *Magic*, Mono-Black Control seeks to keep the battlefield clear of opposing threats. **Burn**


No Eternal format would be complete without the classic option to burn faces! This is one of the fastest decks in the format, so don't be afraid to start tossing [Lava Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Spike)s as early as turn one. Pauper Burn uses *Dominaria*'s own [Ghitu Lavarunner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Lavarunner) alongside [Thermo-Alchemist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thermo-Alchemist) to complement its potent package of 3-damage burn spells. Try to avoid using your burn spells on all but the most troublesome of creatures; [Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) is the perfect card for that task. A couple of [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s and a [Fireblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireblast) should be able to finish the job in fiery fashion.

**Stompy**


If you're looking for a good ol' attacking strategy, Pauper Stompy is your deck. [Quirion Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Ranger) lets you keep some land-light hands with its ability to tap a Forest, return it to your hand, then play it again for more mana.

**Five-Color Tron**


Don't want to use Elves to get your ramp fix? Don't worry, Urza's "Tron" lands are here to let you cast the biggest, flashiest common spells the Multiverse has to offer. In Pauper, Tron is mostly a blue-based control deck seeking to use [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) and [Prophetic Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism) to take advantage of the huge amount of colorless mana produced by [Urza's Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower) and friends. Once you have achieved Tron status, try and combo [Mnemonic Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Wall), [Ghostly Flicker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker), and any of your other sweet value permanents. You can also use your multitude of mana to simply [Rolling Thunder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Thunder) your opponent into oblivion, maybe even taking their board with them for funsies.

**Dimir Control**


Unlike Mono-Black Control, Dimir Control is a bit more traditional. This Pauper strategy uses removal spells like [Disfigure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) and [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) to destroy threats that make it onto the battlefield and counterspells like, well, [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), to defend you against anything too wild. [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) combined with [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) should keep the cards flowing. After the dust is settled and you've drowned your opponent in card advantage, you're ready to finish them off with a [Gurmag Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gurmag+Angler) or two.

**Affinity**


Unlike today's Modern Affinity decks, here in the Pauper format, we have actual affinity cards! With a mana base full of artifact lands like [Great Furnace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Furnace) and [Seat of the Synod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seat+of+the+Synod), you can get onto the battlefield quickly with [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) and [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer). Keep the artifacts coming with [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) and [Ichor Wellspring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Wellspring) to refill your hand as fast as you cast your discounted spells. And if your early beaters can't get the job done, go ahead and sacrifice a bunch of artifacts to the mighty [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog), and break through with either [Fling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling) or [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage).

**Bogles**


[Slippery Bogle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slippery+Bogle) and pals aren't justÂ Modern menaces. *Magic*'s history has plenty of great common Auras to enhance everyone's favorite neighborhood beast. Here, the name of the game is to start with a difficult-to-target creature and then load it with power-buffing Auras. Once you have successfully suited up your little buddy, look for a [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) or [Armadillo Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak) to trample over your opponent's pithy little creatures. Watch out for spells like [Diabolic Edict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Edict) that cause you to sacrifice a creature. To get around this, wait to create a giant monster until you have some extra expendable creatures from [Khalni Garden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Garden) or [Cartouche of Solidarity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity).
