TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------



 Just one week before, at Pro Tour *Magic 2015*, Blue Devotion had failed to leave its mark on the competitive scene. The Standard Grand Prix in Utrecht, however, marked the triumphant return of Thassa's followers to the top of the ladder with Oliver Polak-Rottmann proving to the world that Blue Devotion is still a force to be reckoned with!



Interestingly enough, all of the repeat Top 8ers at this Grand Prix, champion Oliver Polak-Rottmann, finalist Eliott Boussaud as well as Christoffer Larsen, piloted Blue Devotion decks. If that lead you to the conclusion that Standard is very clearly defined, however, you couldn't be more wrong; the other five players relied on five different archetypes and the event's metagame breakdown also suggested that the format is wide open.




 We saw armies of Goblins overwhelming opponents in lightning fashion, Blue-White Control players refilling their hands time and time again with Sphinx's Revelation, Pack Rats taking control of games as early as turn two and players getting blown out of their seats by Warleader's Helix and Stoke the Flames when they were least expecting it. Grand Prix Utrecht has once again proven that *Magic* is continuously reinventing itself and that just one mere week is enough to shake up a supposedly defined metagame.



Until next time and the next reinvention, we say goodbye and: Congratulations to Oliver Polak-Rottmann, champion of Grand Prix Utrecht 2014!

DAY 2 COVERAGE
--------------


Welcome to Day 2 of Grand Prix Utrecht 2014! Yesterday, the original field of 1,509 players had been whittled down to just 172 and all of those came back today for an additional six rounds of Standard before the Top 8.


The previous week's Pro Tour had certainly left its marks on the Standard metagame with White-Blue Control being one of the more popular decks here in Utrecht. We've also seen a surprisingly large amount of Mono-Blue Devotion at the top tables so far, which may have had something to do with the newly invented Rabble Red as well as the red burn deck; both have a rather hard time dealing with Master of Waves. Other popular picks were aggressive green-white decks as well as decks that made the most of Thoughtseize and Pack Rat.


Leading the field at the start of the day with perfect records of 9-0 were Grand Prix champions Florian Koch and Arjan van Leeuwen as well as Thoralf Severin, Martin Dang, and Erik Skinstad. However, Oliver Polak-Rottmann, (6) Stanislav Cifka, Alexandre Darras, Christian Seibold, Lukas Blohon, Fabrizio Anteri, Daniel Antoniou, Sebastian Knörr, Jan Van der Vegt, and Vincent Lemoine all made Day 2 on scores of 8-1, so there's a lot of strong competition.


Join us, both on text and live video, when we follow some of the game's best and brightest on their way to the Top 8 and when a new Grand Prix champion is crowned!

DAY 1 COVERAGE
--------------


It's been a very short first day here in Utrecht at the Grand Prix where judges and tournament organizers have truly outdone themselves! Despite an attendance of 1,509 players the tournament ran smoothly and today's nine rounds of Standard finished just past eight p.m. Throughout those nine rounds, the field has been whittled down to just 172 competitors, poised to come back tomorrow and make a run at the title.


The previous week's Pro Tour has certainly left its marks on the Standard metagame with White-Blue Control being one of the more popular decks here in Utrecht. Meanwhile, we also saw a surprisingly large amount of Mono-Blue Devotion at the top tables, which may have had something to do with the newly invented Rabble Red as well as the red burn deck; both have a rather hard time dealing with Master of Waves. Other popular picks were Jund builds as well as decks that made the most of Thoughtseize and Pack Rat.


Leading the field overnight with perfect records of 9-0 are Grand Prix champions Florian Koch and Arjan van Leeuwen as well as Thoralf Severin, Martin Dang, and Erik Skinstad. However, they'll have to bring their A game once again tomorrow, because the likes of Oliver Polak-Rottmann, (6) Stanislav Cifka, Alexandre Darras, Christian Seibold, Lukas Blohon, Fabrizio Anteri, Daniel Antoniou, Sebastian Knörr, Jan Van der Vegt, and Vincent Lemoine are in hot pursuit, all with scores of 8-1. Every single one of these players is eager to start the season with a strong finish, so tomorrow's six rounds will be brutal.


Join us then, as we follow some of the game's best and brightest on their way to the Top 8 and find out who will be the champion of Grand Prix Utrecht 2014!
