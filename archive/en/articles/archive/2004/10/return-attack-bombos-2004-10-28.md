The Return of the Attack of the Bombos
======================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 28, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_240.png)
By Mark L. Gottlieb












Aaron Forsythe based his [last column](/en/articles/archive/latest-developments/gottlieb%E2%80%99s-fan-mail-2004-10-22) on a particularly harsh email that I got. Let me assure you, that message was unusual. Nearly all of the mail I get is positive. Some are *very* positive:



> 
> Dear Mark Gottlieb,
> 
> 
> I understand you’ve been getting some unpleasant fan mail lately. I can assure you there is at least one (1) fan who would totally have tried to ask you out by now if she weren’t already married. I suspect the people who write in and dis you can’t say the same.
> 
> 
> [![Read the Anthony article in question](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/global/images/mtgcom_daily_aa147_pic1_en.jpg)](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/top-ten-top-ten-2004-10-26)So there.
> 
> 
> —A Perhaps-Too-Adoring Fan
> 
> 
> 


Wow. I warned Anthony not to spend too much time with Rebecca Romijn. Unfortunately, all of the messages I get like that are from married women or 13-year-olds. But interspersed with all the love, the crazy combos, and the occasional rudeness are the insidious seeds of ruination…


As a supervillain, I am often pestered by Captain Obvious. But even worse than him is his twin brother: Captain Oblivious! He sends me emails under many different names in order to confuse me, but I know it’s him. Superheroes are on the side of “good.” Supervillains are on the side of “right”—er, I mean “evil.” Captain Oblivious is a superneutral. He just gets in the way for no good reason. His weapon of choice? Why, it’s so obvious even Captain Obvious would say it’s obvious. Bombos!


### The Top 10 Bombos That I Felt Like Including in a Top 10 Bombos List



![Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cranial+Extraction)

**10. Captain Oblivious tries to improve my deck from [last week](/en/articles/archive/anti-gravity-2004-10-21)!**

> 
> After reading the deck “Rat Extraction,” I came up with this deck idea. Get a lot of [Relentless Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Rats) and combine them with [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction) and [Patriarch’s Bidding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%E2%80%99s+Bidding).
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!**  [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction) removes the Rats from the game. It doesn’t put them in the graveyard.


**9. Captain Oblivious responds to my correction of his baffling bombo with… a baffling bombo!**



> 
> I have found a famed Infinite Damage Loop. While playing my “Binding Memnarch” deck, I found that [Spellbinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbinder) + [Dream’s Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream%E2%80%99s+Grip) + any unblockable creature = infinite damage + serious annoyance.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** After you attack with your [Spellbinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbinder)-equipped creature, you can play a copy of [Dream’s Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream%E2%80%99s+Grip) and untap your creature. But you can’t attack with it again; you don’t have another combat phase.



> 
> I feel like an idiot. My combo *was* a bombo. So to make up for it I have been thinking of a new one. I hope this works: [Triskelion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) + [Dream’s Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream%E2%80%99s+Grip) + [Spellbinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbinder) = You see where that is going. :)
> 
> 
> 


Um… nope. No, I don’t.



![Sheep Token](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sheep+Token)

**8.5. Captain Oblivious has a farm!**

> 
> On the table you have [Aluren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren), [Concordant Crossroads](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concordant+Crossroads), and any creature. You drop an [Ovinomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinomancer), and put the “destroy it” ability on the stack. In response, you tap it to turn your creature into a Sheep. [Ovinomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinomancer) is now in your hand, and you respond by playing it and targeting your creature again. In theory, when these abilities all resolve, the game tries to destroy your [Ovinomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinomancer) for each comes-into-play ability, and then the game destroys your other creature, puts a Sheep into play, and attempts to destroy it again. Basically the combo was meant to include an [Echoing Courage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Courage) for the win. How cool is it to swing with an infinite number of sheep?
> 
> 
> 


Really cool. I mean **Wrong!** To be fair, this time Captain Oblivious asked whether this combo works, rather than stating it as a fact. It’s less fun when he’s not overbearingly pompous. I started counting how many Sheep this would create, and \*yawn\* it’s \*yawn\* only going to \*yawn\* make one. Since [Ovinomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinomancer)’s Sheepifying ability has a target, it will be countered if the targeted creature is gone when it tries to resolve. Stacking up a million redundant Sheep Rays will just make one Sheep.




|  |

| We interrupt this Top 10ish list to bring you a real combo
It’s been one of those kinds of weeks: I got sent two [Ovinomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinomancer) decks in two days. Weird. Doc Twisted paired [Ovinomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinomancer) with [Azusa, Lost but Seeking](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azusa%2C+Lost+but+Seeking), which happily offsets our Sheepish friend’s steep costs. Doc’s deck was filled with bounce effects and Tims, but being the supervillain that I am, I found it more interesting to genetically crossbreed it with Marcel’s Wizardly [Azami, Lady of Scrolls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azami%2C+Lady+of+Scrolls) deck. (Marcel’s deck sought to overstuff your own hand, then deck your opponent with a couple of monstrous [Tomoya the Revealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tomoya+the+Revealer) activations.) [Ovinomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinomancer) is a Wizard. [Prodigal Sorcerer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Sorcerer) is a Wizard. And Azami and Azusa should get along quite well as you draw scads of cards. |

**8. Captain Oblivious delves into his *Homelands* collection!**



> 
> The way this deck works is to slap down an [An-Zerrin Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=An-Zerrin+Ruins), naming something like Slivers and Imagecrafting your opponent’s creatures into Slivers on their turn so all their creatures are tapped for the turn.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!**  [Imagecrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) + [An-Zerrin Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=An-Zerrin+Ruins) is *not* a combo. No player can play spells or abilities before the untap step. Therefore, you can’t change your opponent’s creatures into Slivers (or anything else) with [Imagecrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) before he gets a chance to untap them as normal.


**7. Captain Oblivious tries to break the new set!**



> 
> Try this for a nice trick: [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) + one of the legendary Dragon Spirits in *Champions*. Take the black Dragon for example: Tap Kiki-Jiki targeting the black Dragon (preferably after having attacked successfully). Ideally, you should be able to make an opponent lose 15 life. Not bad for only two creatures. Also, consider using Kiki-Jiki with a single [Brothers Yamazaki](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brothers+Yamazaki). Try making this combo infinite just to bug the opponent.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!**  [Kiki-Jiki](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki) can’t copy [Kokusho](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kokusho) or the [Brothers Yamazaki](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brothers+Yamazaki). It can’t copy any legendary creatures, because… it can’t copy legendary creatures. Huh. That was kinda cyclical.


![Phage the Untouchable](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phage+the+Untouchable)*Always a bombo favorite!*
**6. Captain Oblivious goes on a date with [Phage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phage)!**



> 
> I was reading your [“Kill ’Em All” Online Prismatic deck](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/mg121), and if you put a [Phage the Untouchable](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phage+the+Untouchable) in there as the only creature, when your opponent used [Bribery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bribery) they would have to take [Phage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phage) and automatically lose the game.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** I got this one a lot, but to no avail. Your opponent wouldn’t be forced to take Phage. He can choose to “find” no creature at all. The Comp Rules sez: “If you’re required to search a zone not revealed to all players for cards matching some criteria, you aren’t required to find those cards even if they’re present.” The combo did halfway work, though, if you could rely on your opponent not knowing this rule. He might think he had to take [Phage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phage), and therefore would. This is all moot now, though: [Bribery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bribery) has been banned in the format.


**6A. Captain Oblivious tries again with Phage!**



> 
> Just play [Custody Battle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Custody+Battle) on [Phage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phage) and don’t sac your land.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** Changing controllers is *not* the same as coming into play. When you carry out your plan, [Phage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phage) is already in play and stays in play. Your opponent doesn’t lose the game when he takes control of [Phage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phage). Heck, he probably kills you with it.


**6B. Captain Oblivious tries again with [Custody Battle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Custody+Battle)!** 



> 
> I’ve been toying with the idea of a [Leveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leveler) deck. Basically the plan is to cast [Custody Battle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Custody+Battle) on the [Leveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leveler) and then phase it out (we even use banding cards) after I let it change teams. At the beginning of my opponent’s turn it would phase in, remove his library and then he would have no card to draw for the draw step. It’s not a killer deck, but thinking about removing my opponent’s library in this fashion just makes me grin when I think about it.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** Phasing in doesn’t trigger comes-into-play effects.


**6Z. Captain Oblivious tries again with [Leveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leveler)!**



> 
> Ever since *Mirrodin* came out there have been two cards that I’ve scratched my head over for a long time. These cards were [Fatespinner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatespinner) and [Leveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leveler). [Fatespinner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatespinner) gave up a crucial component of your (and your opponent’s) turn, and, if not in a combo with something, [Leveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leveler) might as well have read: “You lose next turn”. I thought [Fate Spinner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Spinner) would be perfect with [Leveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leveler) because it allows you to skip your draw step after the Leveler hits play. Is this a killer combo or a disastrous bombo? I’ll let you decide.
> 
> 
> 


I decide it’s a disastrous bombo! This is one of those cases where the fact that your card (in this case, [Fatespinner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatespinner)) is better than you think means that your combo is *worse* than you think. Unfortunately(?), [Fatespinner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatespinner) only hoses your opponent.

[Endless Whispers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endless+Whispers) brings the Dragons back at the end of the turn under your opponent’s control so they can repeat that debacle on you. At the end of the next turn, they’ll come back under your control again. In fact, every turn for the rest of the game features a visit from the twin [Yosei](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yosei)s.)
For a victory condition, Mickey included a [Darksteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus) whose intended purpose is to get shuffled back into your deck over and over when you discard it so your opponent will get decked and you won’t. But this doesn’t work if your opponent also has a [Darksteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus). What other victory conditions could there be? Artifacts and enchantments without mana costs can help ([Skullcage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullcage), [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault)). **5. Captain Oblivious goes infinite!**



> 
> I have found a very reliable combo that I think is interesting and that you will find interesting as well. The cards that make my combo up are [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak), [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons), and [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) or [Disintegrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disintegrate). The combo goes something like this: First you play [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak), then play Drudge Skeletons, and then play ether [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) or [Disintegrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disintegrate). This is how you pay for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20160829100442im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/x.gif): You sacrifice [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) for [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak)’s effect, but you use one of the black mana you get to regenerate it and you will have ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) left over. And you can keep doing this over and over doing any amount of damage.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** First of all, [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak) is an instant, not an enchantment. You only get to sacrifice one creature when you play it, not infinite creatures. Secondly, that’s not how regeneration works. When you sacrifice a creature, you can’t regenerate it.


**4. Captain Oblivious decides to rewrite the rulebook!**



> 
> The combo is [Mind Over Matter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Over+Matter) + [Future Sight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight). The top card of your library, for all intents and purposes, is in your hand. Tap your lands for mana, then discard cards to untap them. Repeat until you have found either [Ambassador Laquatus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambassador+Laquatus) or [Stroke of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stroke+of+Genius) and have enough mana to deck your opponent. If need be, discard everything except a [Reminisce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reminisce), play it, and repeat. You can completely control the game and slowly kill your opponent, or you could kill him in one turn.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** “For all intents and purposes”? Yikes! That has no meaning here! [Future Sight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight) lets you *play* the card on top of your library as though it were in your hand—but it doesn’t actually put it into your hand or let you use it for any other purpose. The cost of [Mind Over Matter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Over+Matter)’s ability is “discard a card from your hand.” Since the card on top of your library isn’t in your hand, you can’t discard it to pay [Mind Over Matter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Over+Matter)’s cost.


**π. Captain Oblivious thinks artifact is a color!**



> 
> I’ve got a cool combo I just came up with. This can be hard to pull off, but the look on your opponent’s face is worth using it. You need to have out a [Mycosynth Lattice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Lattice), a [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) with some sort of evasion, and a mix of 18 permanents (other than the Mongrel) and cards in hand. Attack with the Mongrel, then play [Retract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retract) before damage is on the stack. In response to [Retract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retract), turn the Mongrel a color. It doesn’t matter what color. Once [Retract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retract) resolves, you bounce all of your permanents... *except* for the Mongrel. Now discard everything to the Mongrel to deal 20+ damage.
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** Turning the Mongrel a different color doesn’t change it from being an artifact. It’ll just be a blue artifact, or a red artifact, or whatever. It’ll still return to your hand due to [Retract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retract).


**3. Captain Oblivious tries to teach me a thing or two!**



> 
>  
> 
> 
> 
> ![Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fist+of+Suns)
> 
> I love [Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fist+of+Suns) too. But, unless I made some mistake, [you and Devin missed](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/mg128) its most obvious use.
> 
> Look, here’s how you get a spell onto the stack:
> 
> 
> 1) You announce your spell.  
> 
> 2) You make any choices about its mode and targets.  
> 
> 3) You pay its mana cost.
> 
> 
> What [Fist of Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fist+of+Suns) does is let you replace step 3 with “You pay ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif).”
> 
> 
> So picture this. **1. Captain Oblivious gets the confusing terminology confused!**



> 
> 
> ![Tooth and Nail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tooth+and+Nail)
> 
> I found a nice little combo that... is quite evil!! Use [Tooth and Nail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) of course, but go for [Myojin of Infinite Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myojin+of+Infinite+Rage) and [Myojin of Cleansing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myojin+of+Cleansing+Fire). Why? Well, they both come in with a divinity counter on them. Use the white one to destroy all other creatures. The red Myojin lives because it’s indestructible. Then use it to destroy all lands. So now, all creatures and lands are dead, and you have a 7/4 and a 4/6 to beat down with. That’s a 2-turn clock and your opponent has no mana and no creatures (I’m guessing they have no indestructible creatures). How fun, a [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) and [Armageddon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon) all at once!! Enjoy.


**Wrong**! This is #1 because it’s the most common bombo I get. There is a difference between “playing a creature” and “putting a creature into play.” “Playing a creature” means paying its mana cost and putting it on the stack. **Magic** Rules Manager John Carter has a great shorthand to remember the difference: Just look at the verb. Did you *play* the Myojin, or did you *put* the Myojin? The Myojin don’t get divinity counters unless you play them legitimately—no freebies.


**0. Captain Oblivious goes bonkers!**



> 
> Okay, I am calm. I am sane. That reminds me... I’ve been takin’ a look at [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), that broken bounce card that dominated in *Odyssey*. Well, I found another broken card in *Odyssey* called [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer). Here’s how the insane combo works. First, have a [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak) and a [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) in play. Play [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). As the [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) resolves, sac the [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) to [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak). Doing that should force your opponent to discard all those permanents he just returned to his hand. Best of all, you have 4 black mana floating. Why not cast some nice cards? Toss down a zero-mana card and summon a [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) with the other mana. Keep slapping down permanents every turn and, well, your opponent is gonna lose everything he or she plays! Isn’t that fun! It is... well, I hope the rules lawyers allow it...
> 
> 
> 


**Wrong!** Oh, silly Captain Oblivious, blaming the rules lawyers when his bombos don’t work. This is a doozy of confused timing. [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak) can’t be in play because it’s still an instant, not an enchantment. More importantly, you can’t sac the [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) to [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak) “as [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) resolves.” You can’t do anything as a spell or ability is resolving. The best you can do is sac [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) while [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) is on the stack, which will make each player discard their hands *before*  [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) resolves and bounces all permanents. Captain Obvious also seems to have neglected the fact that [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) is symmetric, which means it zotzes his hand too. Where does that [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) come from? Oh, but the Captain wasn’t done yet.



> 
> Sorry for the second e-mail, but I found how to make the “bombo” turn into a true bomb. Keep [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak) and [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) in play, but this time have [Pattern of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth) attached to [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer). Play [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) and sac the [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) before [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) resolves, like you said. Then the [Pattern of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth) ability kicks in after the [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) resolves. Really wanna make your opponent scream? Grab [Hypnox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnox). Or (another fun thing) grab [Darksteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus). Or (even better) grab a [Kamahl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl) and smash your opponent to little bits as soon as it hits play. This bomb works. Trust me.

**Wrong!** This doesn’t come any closer to working. [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak) is still an instant. And the timing is still way off. You have [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) with [Pattern of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth) in play. You play [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) goes on the stack. In response, you play [Culling the Weak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+the+Weak) and sacrifice [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer). [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer) goes to the graveyard. [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer)’s ability and [Pattern of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth)’s ability both trigger. You put them on the stack in whichever order you choose. *But the important thing is that they both go on the stack on top of [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval)!* It’s a last-in-first-out stack. Both [Mindslicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslicer)’s ability and [Pattern of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth)’s ability will resolve *before*  [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) resolves. Whatever you put into play with [Pattern of Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pattern+of+Rebirth) will just wind up in your hand.


Trust me.
