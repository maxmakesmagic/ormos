Standard White-Blue Artificer Control
=====================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 27, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Last weekend was the [SCG Open in Chicago](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/180715_chicago_fact_sheet.html), the first premier Standard event where *Magic* *Origins* was legal for sanctioned play. I saw a lot of interesting decks while I was watching the coverage, but one of the coolest decks I saw was a White-Blue Control deck piloted by Jeff Hoogland. This deck looked exciting, and it was really fun watching Jeff play the deck to a seventh-place finish.



The most innovative card in this deck is [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker), and Jeff is playing a full set of four. This little guy is extremely versatile. When evaluating this card, it's important to not think of him as a six-mana 3/3 or an eight-mana 4/4. He is actually a two-mana 1/1 that just gets bigger the longer the game goes. In Jeff's deck, he can drop this guy on turn two and just pump him every turn. When the time is right he can cast [End Hostilities](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=End+Hostilities) to kill everything on the battlefield and cash in his [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker) for a bunch of Thopters. It also has great synergy with [Thopter Spy Network](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Spy+Network), another amazing card in this deck.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thopter+Spy+Network)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thopter+Spy+Network) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hangarback+Walker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker)

Jeff also chose to play a lot of great utility spells from *Magic Origins.* [Swift Reckoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swift+Reckoning) is practically a two-mana [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). Spell mastery will usually be active in this deck, allowing you to cast this spell with flash quite often. I also really like [Artificer's Epiphany](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificer%27s+Epiphany). [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) has not been in Standard in a while, and this spell is a [Divination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) at instant speed most of the time.



Out of the sideboard, Jeff chose to play [Displacement Wave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Displacement+Wave) as an additional sweeper. This card is a little slow, but it's a great reset button against decks like Red-Green Devotion or any deck playing [Dragon Fodder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Fodder) or [Hordeling Outburst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hordeling+Outburst). Keep in mind that you can bounce a [Genesis Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra) when X=2 for [Displacement Wave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Displacement+Wave), because [Genesis Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Hydra) has a converted mana cost of 2 regardless of what was paid for it.
