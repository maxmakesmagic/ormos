Third Eye Blind
===============



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on November 16, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






 
If the thick haze of a smoldering, pock-mark littered battlefield didn't tip you off already, it's Burn Week and you should be warned: it gets hotter than the kitchen in here.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf117_waiters.jpg)


 


[Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). [Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake). [Galvanic Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast). [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning). Slinging "burn" spells is something that's expected. Ordinary. Everyday average. Tragically common, yet fashionably hip. A decade ago it was [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate). [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock). [Fireblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireblast). It doesn't matter the era of **Magic**: there were always a bevy of spells to unleash that blew the face off of opponents and their creatures.


 


There's a lot to be said about decks that do just that as quickly and consistently as possible. I myself have had dreams of building a deck during the halcyon days of **Magic** beginnings when you could use any number of a card (as opposed to just four).

### Blue Öyster Cult


I have such a nostalgia about, and love of just slinging those burn spells I've realized that I rarely step back and take a second look at what I'm actually doing: consistent repetition. As an adamant lover of [EDH](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/13), and singleton decks in general, I can tell you that I adore the idea of ripping a new card off the top of my deck and it being quite different from anything else in my deck.


Yet, I still like *both*.


What I mean is that while there's a part of me that loves the absolute randomness and hilariously chaotic singleton piles of 99-card libraries there is another part of me that loves the super consistency of burn, burn, burn. So the question now becomes "Can these two ideas come together?" Is there a way to have the cake and eat it too?

[*Premium Deck Series: Fire & Lightning*](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/ProductArticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/pdsfirelightning/productinfo) comes out this Friday and it is something I've already got queued up to receive. I could give you a list of reasons why I, personally, am really excited about this, but I'll stick to the one most relevant here: there's a lot of variety in there.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf117_check1.jpg)


 


 


While the obligatory "4 [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)" still appears, it's the miscellaneous gems from **Magic**'s past and present that make me smile. [Hammer of Bogardan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer+of+Bogardan) and [Fireblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireblast)? Check. [Ball Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning) and [Chain Lighting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+Lighting)? Check. [Hellspark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental) and [Fire Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Servant)? Check.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf117_check2.jpg)


 


 


However those "normal" spells aren't what interest me as much as the *other* things. [Sudden Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Impact)? [Reverberate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverberate)? [Browbeat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat)? [Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaya+Ballard%2C+Task+Mage)? Check. Check. Check. Check!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf117_check3.jpg)


 


 


While I haven't been able to get my hands on it (yet!) something tells me that this is going to feel quite a bit more random than your everyday burn deck. Yes, the array of little red creatures will still strike hard and the fistfuls of spells that directly damage things are still present *en masse*.


 


But the feel of getting exactly the same thing every time is gone. Facing a deck that gains life? There's only one [Flames of the Blood Hand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flames+of+the+Blood+Hand). Need to deal with an annoying land or artifact? I hope this next card is [Pillage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage). Did they just draw a ton of cards? "Knock, knock—library, I need that [Sudden Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Impact)."


Maybe what I'm really after is a burn deck that does stuff other than just burn, perhaps something like this?

[Burning-Tree Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Shaman) and [Rumbling Slum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rumbling+Slum) are probably guaranteed sources of damage for most opponents, with [Stuffy Doll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll), [Rackling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rackling), and [Viseling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viseling) playing along in their own ways.

> * [Backfire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Backfire)? On their best attacker? Wait, what does [Backfire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Backfire) even do? Oh.
> * [Cerebral Vortex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cerebral+Vortex) gives your card-drawing opponent a little more of what they wanted, and a lot of what they didn't!
> * [Exploding Borders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exploding+Borders) is where mana fixing and ramping meets the world of burn. Sweet.
> * [Invoke the Firemind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invoke+the+Firemind) is a choice. Maybe. Either way it's awesome!
> * [Vengeful Rebirth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengeful+Rebirth) is where [Regrowth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regrowth) meets [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). Notice a theme here?
> * [Aeolipile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeolipile) and [Perilous Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr) are easy-to-cast, off-beat ways to colorlessly sling some damage.
> * [Overabundance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overabundance) gives you the best of [Manabarbs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manabarbs) and [Heartbeat of Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartbeat+of+Spring) rolled into one.
> 


The lands follow the singleton theme as well, giving you lots of ways to get the colors you need as well as the opportunity to cycle away some if you draw into a color you don't need anymore. While not quite on-theme, [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate) and [Sylvan Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Ranger) will help smooth out any mana issues when they pop up. And decidedly on-theme [Magma Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Burst) gives you something to do with extra lands that do get into play.


There is also a different route that many burn decks suggest: attacking with creatures. While shuffling up sets of very aggressive creatures is one way to do it, another is to simply have creatures that hit *hard*. I've been considering building a new EDH deck that actually uses some of the Eldrazi as well as plays right into the "go big and hit hard" theme that dovetails so well with burn decks.

There's even more going on here than in the last singleton deck! I won't drag on too long but here are a few of the highlights of a deck like this:

> * [Urza's Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Rage). [Comet Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm). [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster). I'll take "Things That go 'BOOM!' with Lots of Mana," for 2000, Alex.
> * [Reiterate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reiterate). [Wild Ricochet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Ricochet). [Fork](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fork) and [Reverberate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverberate). Yes, I'd like to do that too! Even better, I can copy one of my own big spells for an extra treat!
> * [Akroma, Angel of Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) is a creature I've seen used as a General before. She's even more surprising when no one can expect her!
> * [Hellkite Charger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellkite+Charger) is a dragon that exemplifies the idea of damaging an opponent. Add a liberal sprinkling of other creatures that like to swing big and you get instant awesomeness.
> * [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan): I'm always looking for excuses to break out the big guns and these guns happen to *really* like combat as well.
> 


Is this a true burn deck? In some senses, yes. It's got burning bodies like [Flametongue Kavu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu) and [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) with some big spells like [Banefire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire) and [Disaster Radius](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disaster+Radius). Chances are one of these types of things is going to hit somebody, or the things they own, pretty hard. Burn decks like attacking with creatures and love when there aren't any creatures in their way.


Just the kind of thing the little burn decks like *Boltslinger* do just on a far grander scale.


So where does that leave us? About as far from decks like *Boltslinger* as it gets while sharing some of the same stuff. And for me, that's a *really* good thing. I'm a big fan of cake and getting to enjoy the fruits of my fiery, hot labor is pretty awesome.
