### Deck Strategy


The goal of this deck is to lock up all of your opponent's permanents in their hand, making them entirely incapable of doing anything particularly useful. It achieves this goal through [Words of Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wind), [Vedalken Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Archmage), and an artifact that it can play for free. First, activate [Words of Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wind), then play the artifact. This will trigger the Archmage's ability that will then be replaced by the bounce effect of [Words of Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wind). Simply pay 1 mana to bounce and return the free artifact that you just played back into your hand. This translates into returning as many of your opponent's permanents as you have free mana. After a turn of doing this, your opponent can kiss their chances goodbye.


I wanted to add a little more depth to the deck so that it would work well even if you didn't draw an Archmage. [Chittering Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chittering+Rats) and [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) come to the rescue. If you have an advantageous position via creatures, using [Words of Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wind) on your [Chittering Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chittering+Rats) every turn (via draw step) can ensure that your upper hand doesn't change for a while. Using the Wind on [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) will ensure that nothing too nasty threatens you (except perhaps a large [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager)... can't solve everything! If you are afraid of this, just use Green with [Viridian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) instead). [Pulse of the Grid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Grid) can give you a much slower way to bounce your opponent's board, but it's still quite effective. You are pretty much guaranteed to get the pulse back since your net hand size will be -1 before the pulse checks hand sizes. You replace the drawing two, but you still have to discard the one, then you get the pulse back. This is particularly effective with 0 cards in hand.


### Tips on Playing the Deck




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| * [Pulse of the Grid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Grid) is an instant - this allows you to be tricky with [Words of Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wind), returning things at times your opponent might not expect, or even during their upkeep if need be.
* Don't underestimate the beating power of a rat tag team of 2 power guys. They might be unimpressive, but they still get the job done!
* Don't play out all of your artifacts if you do not need to - it is better to save them for when you draw an Archmage if possible!
* Generally, it is a better idea to play your Myrs before your Talismans if you only have 2 available mana, unless you can [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast) with the Talisman after you play it.

### Adding Money to the Deck


Taking a page out of Rubba Ducky's book, [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) and [Glimmervoid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmervoid)s would help this deck. Also, if you are making a paper version of this deck there are a few *Fifth Dawn* cards that fit nicely, one being [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) (quickly becoming one of my favorite *Fifth Dawn* cards) and another being [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions). I would almost recommend taking out some land for those.


There are lots of cool cards you can add if you are just looking to have fun with the deck - just look out for any interesting 'comes into play' effects! If you want to play with older cards, any card that comes into play and draws a card will let you do tricks without the Archmage, like [Wall of Blossoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Blossoms) (but you will need lots of mana to do it multiple times).


Until next time, may your Tradewinds be strong enough to bounce a mountain.
