Ta-da! Cue the heavenly choir, for I have built the budget [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck. It has only four rares, and the rest of the cards are basic lands. Anyone can afford this deck. I'll even throw in a strategy guide to play this deck.


1. Hope to draw [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) in your opening hand.
2. If you don't draw [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) in your opening hand, mulligan. Keep mulliganing until you get a [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), or until you are at zero cards, whichever comes first. Don't worry! Even if you go to zero cards, every game will play the same – play five lands, play [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), win! That is, unless you fail to draw [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits)…
3. Profit

Welcome *back* to [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) week here at Building on a Budget! Apparently one-hundred and twenty-three word articles just don't “cut it” anymore. When I was a youngin', them old players could get away with putting a smiley for a tournament report. Nowadays, you kids want bells, whistles, and your own pet atogs.


You can't have a pet atog. Only I can have a pet atog. Course, I gotta keep him away from the computer…


Even though I wasn't entirely serious about foisting a 216 Island, four [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck on the readership, the idea has merit – how many other cards in **Magic** can singlehandedly win games with so little effort? To win with [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), all you need to do is A) cast the card, and B) survive until your next turn. It's that easy!


There's just one little drawback – you need to have over 200 cards left in your library. Given your opening hand, and the first few draws of the game, that makes your minimum deck size around 212 cards. Since you're going to want a cushion due to potential card-drawing and tutoring effects, let's put the [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) card count at around 240-250 cards.


 



![Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Battle+of+Wits)

I came into this exercise with quite a few rules, and they are essential when building a huge deck such as this.

1. No rares are allowed in the deck except for [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). This is a budget build, and a deck with 240-250 cards is already going to be pushing some budgets.
2. No more than 40 Uncommons are allowed in the deck. This is for the same reason as above. You can usually pick up 64-128 Commons on ***Magic** Online* for a ticket, and 4-20 Uncommons for a ticket as well, depending on desirability at the moment. Remember, the budget for any given deck is thirty tickets *maximum* for this column. The first build of this deck, just by virtue of having over two-hundred cards, is around four tickets.
3. The deck must be Standard legal. For this version, it means that 9th Edition, *Champions of Kamigawa*, *Betrayers of Kamigawa*, *Saviors of Kamigawa*, *Ravnica*, *Guildpact*, and *Dissension* are legal.
4. The main focus of the deck must be winning with [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). We're not just going to stick together four 60-card decks, throw in four [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), and call it a day.
5. No revisions without playing at least five games with the deck as-is. This is the Jay rule, and in a 240 card deck, it's one I want to stick with, for better or for worse.

The joke/first build does have a sound premise – the point of the deck is to win with [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), so why distract from that goal? I decided that B/U would be the way to go for this deck. Blue gives me access to [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) and card drawing, while Black gives me access to tutoring, transmuting, and more card draw.


I divided my attention three ways:


1. *Cards that tutor for [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits)*: [Brainspoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) and [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) can both directly fetch [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). [Dimir House Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) and [Clutch of the Undercity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+the+Undercity) tutor for [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor). I envisioned games where I transmuted for D-Tutor on the third turn, cast it on the fourth turn, and dropped [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) on turn 5.
2. *Card drawing cards*: There are too many to list here, but suffice it to say that these cards run the gamut from [Reach Through Mists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reach+Through+Mists) to [Vision Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vision+Skeins) to [Counsel of the Soratami](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counsel+of+the+Soratami) to [Sift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sift). The purpose of these cards, moreso than in other decks, is to look for a way to get [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) – not necessarily to gain pure card advantage.
3. *Mana Acceleration*: What's better than dropping [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) on turn 5 and winning on turn 6? Dropping [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) on turn 4 and winning on turn 5! I threw in every artifact mana accelerant I could find, including four copies of all ten *Ravnica* block Signets, and [Honor-Worn Shaku](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor-Worn+Shaku).

240 Cards 




![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Island)


















One of the disadvantages of running a 240 card deck is that you never know exactly what you'll draw each game. The odds are against seeing any one particular card, so the key to winning is through redundancy – have a lot of cards that are similar and can fill a similar role. Another disadvantage is that your opponent will likely know your main win condition ([Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits)) before you even draw your opening hand, due to your deck size.


The advantages to [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) are two-fold as well. First, your opponent will never know exactly what he'll be seeing from your deck. You have a huge pile of 240 cards – *anything*¬ could be in there! In addition, you have room to pack those cards in there – with 236 non-BoW slots to fill, the possibilities of what you *can* put in the deck are limited only by your imagination.


**Game #1 Prufrock (U/G Aggro)**


 



![Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trygon+Predator)

I drop a couple of early Signets, and get ready to drop a fourth turn [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). That plan gets shot all to heck when Prufrock drops a third turn [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator). Dagnabbit! I start losing Signets to his flying monster, but still have hope - there is bounce in my deck. A few turns into the game, I draw [Clutch of the Undercity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+the+Undercity). Unfortunately, it is met with [Plaxmanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxmanta), and I get run over by his creatures.

*0-1*


**Game #2: B0dy\_C0unt (U/B Decking deck)**


Well, if there's one deck type I didn't want to face, it was one that can mill my deck past two-hundred cards. That's just what B0dy\_C0unt does – a couple of [Glimpse the Unthinkable](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+the+Unthinkable)s and a Szadek later, and I'm looking at an impossible board position. On the other hand, this game featured the biggest Szadek you've ever seen.


Ha ha ha frown.


*0-2* 


One thing became clear to me – I needed some way to affect my opponent's board position. More to the point, I needed creature removal. I made a note to add creature kill to the deck for the first revision.


**Game #3: greenhornet (G/W/U)**


I cast seven card-drawing spells, seven Signets (how's that for parity), and end up losing to [Giant Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Spider), [Anaconda](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anaconda), and [Aven Fisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fisher). Where are my tutoring effects? Where is my [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits)?


*0-3*


That's not a great start for this deck. I don't think I've had any of my budget decks play so miserably over the first sets of games. Hopefully this will turn around – after all, I could have won the third game, had I drawn anything that would even remotely draw me into the Battle.


**Game #4: jmcclenahan (B/R Discard)**


I get hit by [Rise // Fall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+%2F%2F+Fall) (discarding a card drawing spell, and revealing a land), then [Cry of Contrition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cry+of+Contrition), then [Rise // Fall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+%2F%2F+Fall) (discarding two card drawing spells), then [Ravenous Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats), then another [Cry of Contrition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cry+of+Contrition). On my end of the world, I cast [Sift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sift), then [Sift Through Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sift+Through+Sands), then [Train of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Train+of+Thought) for three, and then cast [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) for [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). Even though I've discarded six cards by this point, I've drawn eight extra and win instantly thanks to the Battle.


*1-3*


**Game #5: ferrethater (B/W/G Aggro)**


Don't worry – Ferrethater doesn't hate Ferrets – it's his wife who has an intense dislike for weasely rodents. Ferrethater gets off to a fast start thanks to [Sakura-Tribe Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder). The Rampant-Growth on scales accelerates him into turn 3, 4 *and* 5 [Blind Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Hunter)s.


Unluckily for him, my draw is nearly perfect for this deck. Here's how the game looks on my side of the board.


**Turn 1**: Island.  
**Turn 2**: Swamp, Signet.  
**Turn 3**: Island, Signet, [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research).  
**Turn 4**: Island, Signet, [Fellwar Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fellwar+Stone), [Spectral Searchlight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Searchlight).  
**Turn 5**: Transmute [Brainspoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) into [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). Play [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits).  
**Turn 6**: Win.


*2-3*


It's time to make some changes. I don't need nearly this much acceleration or mana, so I cut out the off-color signets, along with a few basic lands, and the [Honor-Worn Shaku](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor-Worn+Shaku). I also don't like the [Reach Through Mists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reach+Through+Mists) – [Sleight of Hand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand) would be better, since it lets me view two cards instead of one, and I don't have any arcane cards to splice onto Reach.


Creatures and creature kill are the order of the day for the additions side. [Dark Banishing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing), [Last Gasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp) and [Rend Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rend+Flesh) go in, as well as [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) (which can double as creature kill and a win condition). I also want to have more creatures which set up my win, so I add in [Crystal Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Seer)s, [Descendant of Soramaro](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Descendant+of+Soramaro)s, [Lurking Informant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Informant)s, [Ninja of the Deep Hours](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ninja+of+the+Deep+Hours), [Sage Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Aven)s, and [Thieving Magpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Magpie)s. These creatures can both dig through my deck for [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), or play defense to keep me from being run over by creatures.


Lastly, I add a single [Mnemonic Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Nexus) to the deck – I can transmute for it, tutor for it, and it will allow me to win the game with [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) even if I should fall below 200 cards in my library.

**Game #8: Weynu (R/W Weenie)**


 



![Crystal Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Crystal+Seer)

This was quite a long game. Weynu gets down some early beats, topped off by a [Sunforger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunforger). I kill every single one of his creatures, but he's at a comfortable 30+ life thanks to multiple [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and [Sunhome Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome+Enforcer) hits. I cast [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), but it gets torched by [Leave No Trace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leave+No+Trace).

Eventually, Weynu gets more creatures than I have removal, and I'm at a precarious six life. Luckily for me, his deck is out of burn spells – he's used them all pushing through damage earlier in the game, and therefore I am safe from being burned out of the game. This allows me to get [Crystal Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Seer) going – I have enough mana to cast it, bounce it, and recast it each turn, and this helps me set up multiple draws a turn thanks to [Thieving Magpie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Magpie) and [Thought Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier).


I get knocked down to two life due to a hard-cast [Boros Fury-Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Fury-Shield), but lock him out of any significant spells thanks to [Lurking Informant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Informant). I am in control of his draws each turn, and I stuff his hand full of insignificant lands and enchant creatures. I eventually draw both [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) and a four-drop transmute spell, transmute for [Mnemonic Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Nexus), cast [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), and Nexus at the end of his turn (he no longer has creatures on the board by this point) for the win.


*5-3*

**Game #10: Amerloque (CIP 4-Color Deck)**


Ever have a deck you just can't seem to beat? I lose, week-in and week-out, to the comes-into-play deck. This time around, I face triple [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch), triple [Carven Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carven+Caryatid), double [Civic Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder), double [Coiling Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle), double Kiki-Jiki, and [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice), and finally succumb after a dozen turns. For my part, I killed virtually all of his guys, drew over thirty-five extra cards via card drawing spells, and still failed to see a single transmute card, [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), or [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) which would have enabled me to win this game at virtually any point.


*6-4*


[Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) is immensely fun to play. You have a grandiose number of cards to play with, and that leads to games that can carry out in generally unpredictable ways. If you like playing with big decks, especially ones that can play differently every game, I highly suggest putting together this [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck. It clocks in at under ten tickets to build, so it's definitely well within the budget constraints of this column!

Do you remember my article “[Sets of Five](/en/articles/archive/sets-five-2002-12-25)?” There were mega-cycles of certain cards that took several blocks/sets to complete. This included the Atog cycle ([Auratog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auratog), [Atog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atog), [Chronatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronatog), [Necratog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necratog), and [Foratog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foratog)), and the Voice Cycle ([Voice of Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Truth), [Voice of Reason](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Reason), [Voice of Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Grace), [Voice of Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Law), and [Voice of Duty](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Duty)). I plan on making this [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck a regular fixture in this column – but only once per set release!
