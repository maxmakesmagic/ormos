Early Winners
=============



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on July 29, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











This week's update is going to take two different looks at one shiny new format. On the one hand about 1,800 players battled with M11 cards in North American Pro Tour Qualifiers this past weekend. Regarding those, we have some decidedly diverse results.

Plus, a look at the decks deck that is tearing up M11 Standard at the National Championships level. Get ready to dust off those Titans! (Maybe even [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan)s!)

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td101_chart1a.jpg)  
Jund
The more things change ...

Well, it looks like we *don't* exactly have a new best deck or anything; Jund occupied 25% of total Top 8 appearances and burgled twice the Blue Envelopes of the next-best deck (which, to be fair, was only two compared to the "ones" brought home by various other decks). Though 25% Top 8 representation might actually be an improvement against numbers from earlier this year.

Lee's deck features a trio of [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan)s where we might expect to see [Broodmate Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) or [Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander). [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) makes more power than [Broodmate Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon), and across more bodies. Attacking once gives you *14* power over five bodies. Don't forget that this is a *black* creature; you know, more-or-less unkillable.

You get their Titan for 6+ damage, whatever bonus the Titan generates (two lands, two creatures, whatever), *and* you get to kill what is probably the most dangerous creature on the table ... *to death.*

I'm just surprised there weren't more than two.

Blue-Red-Green Destructive Force Decks
[Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force) were made for each other like chocolate and peanut butter. Basically you can ramp out [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and then immediately go and get two additional lands. These can be functional lands like [Mystifying Maze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystifying+Maze) or creature lands for bashing or even garbage lands you intend to throw away the next turn.

Now you are already up to eight mana! [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force) only costs seven. You should already have an edge when the Force goes off, but with just one more [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) attack, you should be three lands or so ahead of the game. Kendrick's main is quite similar to the Blue-Red-Green [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force) deck we looked at last week, complete with the post-Force (or Titan-trump) lockdown, [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan). [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) lacks the natural synergy with [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force), but because it can lock down a land post-Force, the red-headed stepchild of the Titan cycle can keep the opponent from recovering; subtly, it is also the best Titan *against* other Titans.

White of course gives you the second offensive option of picking up [Stirring Wildwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stirring+Wildwood) in addition to [Raging Ravine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) for more and more creature-land beatdown. However the *real* strategic improvement comes from the mighty [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant). Great against pure beatdown, even better against control, and quite at home locking down a land in a mana denial strategy, [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant) gives a deck with [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and [Explore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) another mythic planeswalker to lay out there on the third turn (and in this deck, quite possibly, a more effective option). Ajani has always been the most dangerous planeswalker for White-Blue decks to face early, and in this deck, the mana denial can help force through the more dramatic mana denial of [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force). Plus, post-Force, it's basically a [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) (that doesn't, you know, hit for 6).

This deck is a fine integration of a format-defining new card into an existing, already excellent, shell.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td101_fauna.jpg)  
[Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) may be M11's single greatest threat presented to the already dominating White-Blue decks. Coming down on the second turn, it is not only an aggressive Bear (not that anyone would Grizzly or Runeclaw their way across the Red Zone on the third turn under most circumstances), but a card advantage engine. Unchecked, [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) is going to fetch (and pitch) [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s. Straight to the graveyard. You can get more and more [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s for more and more card advantage out of the graveyard (and more and more immediatehastydamage). Plus, [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) can come out in style! You can go and get [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) ... which in this deck flips all but a few cards for immediate [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) re-buy-ness.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Now this isn't *just* a [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) deck. In addition, [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) can play the [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor) role and grab singletons. For instancethe second you have an advantage, or the opponent foolishly taps outyou go and get [Realm Razer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Realm+Razer). The Realm? Consider it Razed. This can be a good play in response to a [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment)-type effect, or even the opponent tapping for an expensive planeswalker. Let's see how clever this blue mage is without any lands!



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The deck also has a pretty easy go of setting up the [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage) + [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) combination. [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) can act as copies 5-8 of [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage); plus the Shaman can go and get the one copy of [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), which in turn searches up the [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar).

[Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) makes quite a bit of sense in this deck. While [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) is the faster Jace in *any* Jace fight, it makes for a particularly attractive threat in a deck with [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan). To wit:

Turn three: [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), loyalty 3; draw a cardloyalty 2  
 Turn four: *(Doubtless the opponent is grumbling and refuses to trade Jaces)*; draw a cardloyalty 1  
 Turn five: Draw a cardloyalty 0.

Now, often you will be loathe to spend the last counter on your Jace, and actually deign to help the opponent draw a card ... but when you have [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), turn six goes like this:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td101_sunAndJace.jpg)  
Turn six: [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan); re-buy [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) at loyalty 3; rinse, repeat.

It's that easy!

I was surprised to see [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) completely blank [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) in Parez's deck, but similar shells were wielded by Pro Tour Championand onetime "best deck designer in the world"Guillaume Wafo-Tapa and fellow Top 8 competitor Gregory Pierron, so [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)-over-[Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) may end up a full-blown trend.

Besides a (possible) switchover in the finishers department, the most important feature change in the already quite serviceable White-Blue archetype is the popularity of permission. 

Wescoe played just the four [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s, actually up from Lindroos's two [Deprive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive)s. Either two or four is, of course, up from the status quo of no permission spells at all.

Parez glued the two [Deprive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive)s to the four [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s, moving up to six. Elarar went all the way to eight, adding three [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter)s and a [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) as his split.

Every mage added counterspells of some stripe to his sideboard.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td101_3Cards1.jpg)  
Here's the thing: Once you start adding [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to the mix, counterspells start getting cool. You don't necessarily want to play them without a flexible counter ([Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) isn't very good against Vampires, [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) is just one of the cards you are going to end up pitching to [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning), whereas [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) isn't going to save you from any of the format's awesome creatures), but once you have one, it might make sense to start building redundancy into your deck list. Heck, Wafo-Tapa had *nine*, including a much-maligned [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel)!

With all of those counterspells, we might just see a return to instant speed removal (under the theory that it's worth leaving your mana open); both Wafo-Tapa and Pierron played [Jace's Ingenuity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity), and while both players ran three copies of the [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)-friendly [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), Pierron did so over even a *third* copy of [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor).
