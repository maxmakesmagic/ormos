Day 1 Highlights of Grand Prix Memphis 2018
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 25, 2018 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











Welcome to Memphis, the largest city in Tennessee! Between Elvis lore, barbeque cookouts, and picturesque world-class attractions, Memphis also hosted the [2008 World Championships](https://magic.wizards.com/node/520256) almost a decade ago.


Today, 1082 explorers have brought their new Standard decks to the Memphis Cook Convention Center, ready to contest for a Grand Prix title. Grand Prix Memphis 2018 was also the first Premier Event to utilize *Rivals of Ixalan* Standard, so let's take a quick look at the most important snippets of the day.


Standard Made Great!
====================


[Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) were ousted from Standard in June 2011, a move to rejuvenate the stagnant and unhealthy metagame. Since then, no cards received the banhammer until 2017. Over the last twelve months or so, a total seven cards [were banned in Standard](https://magic.wizards.com/en/game-info/gameplay/rules-and-formats/banned-restricted).


These decisions were not taken lightly. All we wanted was to create a better world, a more balanced world. With the weakening of overly-dominant archetypes which were previously suppressing all others, we witnessed a surge in diversity today.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpmem18/gpmem18_d1highlights_01.jpg)


There were 15 distinct archetypes from among the 25 decks which won [Grand Prix Trials](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpmem18/trial-winning-decklists-grand-prix-memphis-2018-2018-02-24) yesterday, all of which were competitive enough to beat four opponents in a row. You still had to be sure that you could take down the big boys such as Mono-Red Aggro and Blue-Black Control, but the beauty was that you could now compete on the same level with at least ten other deck choices!


While we do not know what decks will make the Top 8 tomorrow, we are seeing a lot of exciting decks doing a bunch of beautiful things. Don't just take our word for it! Former World Champion and 15th-ranked Brian Braun-Duin played a different deck each round, and countless other players from all around the world would agree that Standard was now greater than before.

We can't spill the beans right now, and you'll have to wait until tomorrow to see some of these fantastic decklists in their entirety. Blue-Black decks were among the most popular choices among the pro players, while there were also plenty of aggro decks attempting to run them over before [The Scarab God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Scarab+God) could dominate.


Mono-Red remained a strong contender even with the lack of [Rampaging Ferocidon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Ferocidon) and [Ramunap Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ramunap+Ruins) (imagine if they still had those cards, shudders), while the relatively-new Monsters and White-Black City's Blessing decks add fascinating facets to our diverse Standard metagame.


Many of these pros will return tomorrow, and we will be able to shed more light on their deck choices.

Showcasing the Iconic Lands of Ixalan
=====================================


The double-face cards in *Ixalan* and *Rivals of Ixalan* had been a centric theme in Standard as soon as they entered the format. These unique and flavorful cards encouraged explorers to accomplish a quest before transforming into an inordinately-powerful land! In fact, many had abilities that were so powerful that players built entire decks around them.


* [![Search for Azcanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Search+for+Azcanta)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Azcanta)
* [![Legion's Landing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Legion%27s+Landing)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Landing)
* [![Arguel's Blood Fast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Arguel%27s+Blood+Fast)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arguel%27s+Blood+Fast)
* [![Treasure Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Treasure+Map)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Map)
* [![Thaumatic Compass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thaumatic+Compass)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thaumatic+Compass)

[Search for Azcanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Azcanta) quickly found a home in Blue-based control decks, while [Legion's Landing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Landing) saw heavy play in Vampires decks as well as token decks with [Anointed Procession](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anointed+Procession). [Arguel's Blood Fast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arguel%27s+Blood+Fast) and [Treasure Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Map)s add value to any deck which desires it. Yesterday, we even saw a Bant Approach deck take down a Grand Prix Trial with [Thaumatic Compass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thaumatic+Compass)!


Thaumatic Compass in Bant Approach
==================================


Larson's deck had no problem reaching seven lands thanks to ramp spells such as [Spring // Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spring+%2F%2F+Mind) and [Hour of Promise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Promise). [Thaumatic Compass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thaumatic+Compass) can get you a couple of basic lands if you can spare the time, but it becomes truly terrific when transformed. [Spires of Orazca](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spires+of+Orazca) is a nonlegendary [Maze of Ith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith), meaning you can control multiples and negate the most significant threats on your opponent's board.

Hadana's Climb in Blue-Green Pummeler
=====================================


[Electrostatic Pummeler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Pummeler) combo decks had existed from time to time, but Dixon gave it an additional nudge with [Hadana's Climb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hadana%27s+Climb).


You don't necessarily need to wait for three turns to give a single creature three +1/+1 counters because many of the creatures in the deck already can gain +1/+1 counters on their own. For example, if you have a 4/4 [Longtusk Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Longtusk+Cub), you can put a third +1/+1 counter on it, transforming [Hadana's Climb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hadana%27s+Climb) into Winged Temple of Orzaca. Flying [Bristling Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bristling+Hydra)s and gigantic [Electrostatic Pummeler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Pummeler)s are no joke!

Path of Mettle in White-Red Aggro
=================================


Last week, Ryuumei splashed White in a Mono-Red Aggro deck to give it a “value” boost. Adding three copies of [Path of Mettle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+of+Mettle) in the main deck and four copies of [Ixalan's Binding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixalan%27s+Binding) in the sideboard, he was able to go 5-0 at a Magic Online Competitive Standard Constructed League.


Since the creature base already contained so many creatures with the required abilities to transform [Path of Mettle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+of+Mettle), it felt like a justifiable splash. The enter-the-battlefield trigger was also excellent against opposing tokens, [Toolcraft Exemplar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toolcraft+Exemplar)s, and [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner)s, and tiny [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista)s. [Metzali, Tower of Triumph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metzali%2C+Tower+of+Triumph) provided the aggro player inevitability in the long game.
