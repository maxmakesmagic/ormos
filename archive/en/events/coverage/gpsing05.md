Grand Prix Singapore 2005
-------------------------


Japanese Powerhouse Invades Singapore!
======================================




March 19-20, 2015











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsing05/fin.jpg)**Itaru Ishida Is the Grand Prix Singapore Champion!**


Itaru Ishida is one the most long-tenured Pros in the Japanese **Magic**  community, and with his Top 8 appearance he pulled into a tie with none other than Kai Budde for the second-most Top 8 appearances in Grand Prix competition. The taciturn Ishida dubbed his deck the Ink-Eyes Special and he piloted it through a pair of players with Temporary Solution, with one Rock player squeezed in between, for the Grand Prix-Singapore title.


This was the largest Southeast Asian Grand Prix in history and much of that was due to an unusually large Japanese presence, with 35 players making the seven-hour journey. There were more Japanese players than Singaporeans on Day 2, and four of them (three if you don't count Australian ex-pat Oliver Oks) reached the elimination rounds.


Congratulations to everyone who did well this weekend and condolences to Sukhum Kiwanont of Thailand who could not quite get over a wall of three straight Japanese opponents and just missed the cut in ninth place.

Sunday, March 20, 7:08 p.m. - Quarterfinal Round-up
---------------------------------------------------


**Itaru Ishida vs. Oliver Oks**


Ishida took some early beats from Oks' [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) before he slowed things down with his [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). Oks played a [Gilded Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake) to steal the Tog and got in seven more points on his next attack but Ishida had access to [Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) and returned the Tog to its rightful owner. He was able to get it to lethal range in pretty short order.



![Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Echoing+Truth)

In the second game, Oks drew land where Ishida was drawing [Accumulated Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge)s. Ishida advanced to the semifinals taking the match two games to none.


**Sun Kit Yeung vs. Dennis Yuliadinata**


Sun's maindeck Swords and sideboarded [Engineered Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague)s made this a completely lopsided affair with Sun taking the match in two blisteringly fast games. He would go on to the semis to face Ishida after the two-oh sweep.


**Gabriel Kang vs. Shih Chien Chan**


This match went about how you expect when RDW faces off with Temporary Solution. In Game 2 Gabriel had ample opportunity to draw [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb)s to deal with a pair of [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight)s -- he could do little else with his mana being attacked by Tides and [Chill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chill)s. Shih advanced to face the winner of Komoro and Shimura also taking his match in two games.


**Shu Komoro vs. Ichirou Shimura**


Ichirou knocked down the champ in a three game set. In the two games that he won he managed to pull off an early [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction) on [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) and beat down more or less unmolested. Shu tried to go off in the third game but without [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) to get the key pieces he needed he fizzled and Ichirou advanced to face off against Shih in the only match to go three games.



Sunday, March 20, 7:22 p.m. - Semifinals: Itaru Ishida vs. Sun Kit Yeung
------------------------------------------------------------------------


Semifinals: Itaru Ishida vs. Sun Kit Yeung


Sun came out of the gates fast in Game 1 with [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and a [Sword of Fire and Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice). He equipped and elf and began beating down for five -- and a card -- per turn. Ishida bided his time with card drawing spells and working around [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) until he found Tog. Tog could not block the elf but Ishida cast a flurry of [Gush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush)es leaving himself only one [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) in play. He added up all the damage he could manage with his flying Tog and it was two points shy of being lethal.


"Good game," conceded Ishida.


Ishida mulliganed to open Game 2 and was fine with the next six. Sun tried to slow down his foe's mana with a pair of Ports while beating with an elf. On turn five, Ishida floated one mana and drew a card. He burned for one and passed the turn after playing a land. Sun prompted Ishida to break a Delta for a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) by Porting it EOT.


Ishida cast FoF at the end of Sun's turn and took a three card pile with land, [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), and [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) over [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) and [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish). Sun kept Ishida from black mana to cast his Tog. He tutored up a [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) and cast it naming [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). He saw Meloku, the desired counter, [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) x 2, [Gush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush), and Tog. He played a land and cast [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic). Ishida [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed it twice and Sun flashed back Therapy naming Tog.



![Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Psychatog)

Ishida untapped and played his Meloku. Sun played [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) and the legend battle was on. Ishida made two spirit tokens EOT and untapped to swing for four. He killed one of Sun's permanents by putting engineering a plague on elves. Sun continued to port Ishida's dwindling mana and the Japanese player kept making additional tokens in response to being ported.


Sun drew a Sword and equipped [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) and attacked. He used the ability from the Sword to shoot down a token. Ishida was down to one land and five guys and would kill Sun on the next turn. Sun sighed as he drew his card -- a second Sword. He equipped his [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) and attacked only to come up two points short.


If he had done the two to Ishida last turn he would have won.


Sun mulliganed a perfectly reasonable hand if you didn't account for the fact that it had no [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s. He liked his next six and led with an elf. Turn two was [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) on [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). He hit and saw two [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish), three land and [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) as well.


Sun took the Wishes. His [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) was [Counter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counter)ed on the next turn. Ishida tapped out to [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and Sun had a window to cast [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids). [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth) was countered a turn later. Ishida had a pair of [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) keeping his at four lands while Sun was pressing up against his own [Braids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braids) lock.


The Rock player tapped out to cast [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic) and then sacrificed his elf -- he had two lands left -- to name Tog. He whiffed. Ishida was locked at four lands and could not get out from under to cast Meloku.


Ishida [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)ed in response to [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) he tucked two Togs on top for safekeeping. Sun named [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) but it did not matter. Ishida played Togs over the two turns and Sun could not get in an attack. The damage wasn't quite lethal until Ishida showed him the [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) he was holding.


The Japanese fans burst into cheers while Sun slumped, no doubt replaying the turn from Game 2 where he could have taken the match. He looked up and smiled, "Pretty exciting match, huh?"



Sunday, March 20, 7:51 p.m. - Semifinals: The Topdeck Triumph!
--------------------------------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsing05/shimura.jpg)*Ichirou Shimura could taste victory!*
Shih Chien Chan took Game 1 of his match with Ichirou Shimura while the other semifinal was underway. Things were looking grim for the Malaysian in Game 2. His [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight)s were weakened by a [Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague) as were the spirit tokens from Meloku. His [Sword of Fire and Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice) was [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize)d in combat and there was a herd of elephants knocking on his front door.


He peeled a card slowly from the top of his deck and tucked it in the back of his hand without looking at it, "What can I draw here?"


"Nothing!" was the curt reply from Shimura.


Chan thought about it and disagreed -- still not looking at the card he had just drawn -- "[Parallax Wave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave) is the only thing that can save me."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsing05/chien.jpg)*But fate was on Shih Chien Chan's side!*
Sure enough, hidden behind a [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) was the top deck he had been waiting his whole life for. He 'wrathed' Shimura's team of tokens away -- save one that he stole with [Gilded Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake) -- and was able to get in the final five points of damage by the skin of his teeth. He would advance to face off against Itaru Ishida's Ink-Eyes Special in the finals.
