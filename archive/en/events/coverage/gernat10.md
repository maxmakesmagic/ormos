Johannsen Ramps to Glory in Germany
===================================




August 21-22, 2010












2010 National Germany Championship
----------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/winner_dennis_johannsen.jpg)On a sunny Sunday morning in Aschaffenburg, Dennis Johannsen's smile outshone the midday sun as he received the winner's trophy for the German Nationals 2010. Three years after a second place finish his wish had finally come true: Leading the German National team at the World Championships. It was also Johannsen's twelfth Nationals attendance, which made him the player with the most German Nationals ever under his belt.


Come Chiba, Johannsen will be joined by a strong team. Jörg Unfried, 2006 team member, is looking forward to his return to the Worlds stage. And finalist Tobias Gräfensteiner is ready to seize this opportunity for parity with his brother Daniel, who set the bar with his Top 8 appearance at Pro Tour: San Diego. The two brothers squared off in the quarterfinals, where Tobias defeated his older brother in five games.


Of course, being brothers, they were card-perfect on each other's decklist – small wonder, as they played the exact same 75 cards. The deck they played ascended through the standings until all five pilots finished inside the Top 32, including the 2009 National Champion Sebastian Thaler. It is a regular [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) deck, but includes a transformational sideboard that brings in [Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polymorph) and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). The plan worked, as the two Gräfensteiner brothers could well attest to on Sunday morning.


German Nationals 2010 is in the books, Grand Prix: Göteborg and Pro Tour: Amsterdam are next up on the schedule. Join us then and there here on dailymtg.com – and next year for German Nationals 2011!

Quarterfinal: Florian Pils vs. Jörg Unfried
-------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

Florian Pils is already qualified for Worlds in Chiba, but that didn't stop him from aiming for the Nationals title. Jörg Unfried was also highly motivated to take a slot on the team for Japan, if only to remedy himself for Worlds 2006. Four years ago, Unfried was left hanging as the rest of the German National team got themselves disqualified.


### Game 1:


Unfried came blazing out of the gates on curve in the first turns: [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech), [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax), [Obstinate Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth) – Unfried had gas enough. Unfortunately for him, gas prices went through the roof as Pils had [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), [Condemn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn) and [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive) in turn to stop Unfried in his tracks – despite taking a mulligan to six cards. [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) promised to rebuild Pils' ressources, but Jace met [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) after after brainstorming once, and the battlefield was barren again.


Unfried saw an opening and presented [Obstinate Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth), which stuck, gaining four life. While Pils was drawing cards with [Jace's Ingenuity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity), Unfried was putting the pressure on. Attacking with Lavaklauen-Gebiet and the Baloth, Pils used his [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) to remove the land, but went to 15 from the Baloth's attack.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/qf_pils_and_unfried.jpg)*Florian Pils vs. Jörg Unfried*
Unfried knew that victory for him lay on the battlefield, not in drawing cards. [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) joined his ranks, but had to expend its energy to kill Pils' second [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor). Unfried's next play was [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander). Apparently, Pils' U/W deck had severe hiccups, as it failed to cough up a way to deal with the Siege-Gang.


Florian Pils 0 – 1 Jörg Unfried


Pils sideboarded IN 2 [Celestial Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Purge), 1 [Jace's Ingenuity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity) and OUT 2 [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), 1 [Jace's Ingenuity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity).


Unfried sideboarded IN 3 [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), 3 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), 1 [Slave of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slave+of+Bolas), and OUT 3 [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), 2 [Obstinate Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obstinate+Baloth), 1 [Bituminous Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast), 1 [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate).


### Game 2:


Unfried equalled out the mulligan count and proceeded sedately with [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) on turn three. Pils shrugged, let the critter resolve and dispatched it with [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring). Tapped out, Pils had no chance to stop Unfried's [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf). Cascade revealed [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), which saw [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive), [Jace's Ingenuity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity), [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). Unfried decided to deny Pils potential options and to keep him from gaining more unknown information, so he took the card drawer. Pils went to 17 from the attack.


Unfried tried [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) to get [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) off the Thrinax. Pils countered that with [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive). Another [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) from Unfried revealed another [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse), and now the Thrinax did come back. Pils tried to block with Celestial Column, which fell to Doomblade. Pils fell to 6 life. He held [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) still in hand, but also [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), and had to decide which one to play. The white sorcery would kill both Bloodbraid Elves and the [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax), but also leave three Saproling tokens in play that Pils still would have to deal with.


On the other hand, Pils also had [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) in play, which would be enough to keep him alive through a full-on attack, so Pils decided on [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). Even after Unfried added [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) to his side, Pils would stay alive. But Unfried had [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning), and Pils discarded the two lands he held together with [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment). He had staved off that attack and turned his [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) sideways to gain some breathing room. He could afford to leave himself open that way because his next play was – [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)! Even through the floor, the crowd in the video room could be heard.


"Sorry," Unfried said, and the crowd went ballistic as he pointed his own topdeck, [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse), at the [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s.


Florian Pils 0 – 2 Jörg Unfried


### Game 3:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/qf_joerg_unfried.jpg)*Jörg Unfried*
Jörg Unfried expressed severe discontent at his opening hand and shuffled it back into his library. With six cards on the draw, Unfried opened with [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). Pils' decision to play first paid back, as he had the mana to [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)[Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) was next from Pils and the game finally seemed to go his way.


Catching a glimpse of Pils' hand, he was well set up with [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), [Flashfreeze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) and even [Celestial Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Purge) to remove [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech). Unfried tried [Master of the Wild Hunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+the+Wild+Hunt). Pils picked the card up and read it thorougly, then pointed [Flashfreeze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze) at it.


Turn five [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) from Pils looked solid, but Unfried's [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) took away [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), a land and three life and Doomblade slayed the bane of [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). Both players were now playing from the top.


[Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) and [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) from Unfried marched to the red zone, blocked by [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) and [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens). Pils waited until Unfried had pumped the Leech and played [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to save his Wall.


Strong on removal, Pils used [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) to kill the Ravine a turn later, and the [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) successfully prevented Unfried's new hasty [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) from attacking, though Cascade gave him [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning). Pils fell to 15. Another turn later, a second [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) revealed [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax), which Pils had a freshly drawn [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) for.


Still, the two Bloodbraid Elves were one too many for [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens), and they pecked away at Pils' life. But [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) let Pils check the top card of his library and build up loyalty counters, essentially creating a virtual blocker. Unfried wanted to kill that Jace, so both Elves attacked Jace. [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) blocked one, though [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) finished the Planeswalker.


15, 12, 9 – Florian Pils' life totals were dominated by the three-power Bloodbraid Elves. Like any good control deck, though, Pils had card drawing. Jaces's Ingenuity gave him a second [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) and protection from [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), as well as [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) and [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) off the Wall.


Now all Pils had to do was win somehow. [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) took a chunk of four out of Unfried's total, down to 11, then to 7. Unfried had [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) against the Colonnade, which Pils let resolve, because [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) came his way and returned the land! Unfried's removal was not exhausted yet, though, with Doomblade killing the Titan. With two cards left in his hand, Unfried fell to 3 from the Colonnade before used Doomblade on it. That one also went through, and Pils simply reloaded with [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), saving his [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak).


But he didn't need it: Unfried had nothing and died.


Florian Pils 1 – 2 Jörg Unfried


### Game 4:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat10/qf_florian_pils.jpg)*Florian Pils*
This time, Pils went to Paris – twice, because in his six cards, he found three [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s and decided that was far too clunky. Unfried's start was [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) turn two. Pils had [Deprive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive) for it, with the rest of his hand being [Jace's Ingenuity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity), [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress). A tough situation when Unfried player [Blightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning). Pils discarded [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), dropping to 17 life.


Unfried continued the slaying with [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) into [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), revealing that he had drawn another [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). Even though Pils had just three land in play, Unfried decided to get rid of the five mana card drawer, and the remaining [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) took out [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf).


Unfried had enough mana to smash in with [Raging Ravine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine), drew [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) of the top and took the life totals to 16-13 in his favor. [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax) joined the battlefield, while Pils was unable to draw a fourth land for four consecutive turns. 14-6 stood the life totals in Unfried's favor before Pils had [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) for [Sprouting Thrinax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax). Still, [Putrid Leech](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) took him to 2.


After missing six land drops in a row, Florian Pils had no chance to survive the onslaught that was the unsatisfying game four, and Jörg Unfried advanced to the semifinals!


Florian Pils 1 – 3 Jörg Unfried
