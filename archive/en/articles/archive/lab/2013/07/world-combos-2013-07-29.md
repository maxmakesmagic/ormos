The World of Combos
===================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on July 29, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







Hello, and welcome to the Lab. The **Magic** World Championship is this week, and in a few days all eyes will be turned to the big stage as the best players in the world battle it out for the title. In honor of this tournament, I'll be putting together some fun combo decks based on cards from past World Championship decks.


In 2000, the world champion was none other than the great Jon Finkel. He fought his way to victory with the help of [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker), which produced plenty of mana to power up cards like [Masticore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masticore) and [Phyrexian Processor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Processor), while tapping down his opponents' lands with [Tangle Wire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tangle+Wire) and [Mishra's Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Helix).

Cards that can repeatedly produce large amounts of mana generally aren't too difficult to go infinite with, and [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker) is no exception. [Sword of the Paruns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+the+Paruns) and [Umbral Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle) will both allow you to produce infinite mana, so long as you have at least two artifacts in your hand. [Umbral Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle) will also make [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker) enormous, but that won't win you the game so long as your opponent has just one creature to block with.

[Sands of Delirium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sands+of+Delirium) is a great win condition, especially when producing an unlimited amount of colorless mana. If you feel like winning with some *Future Sight* style, [Whetwheel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whetwheel) will do the same thing here. To provide a diversity of win conditions, I've also included [Goblin Cannon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Cannon). Although most of the time either a trillion damage or milling a trillion cards will work, there may be occasions where you need one rather than the other.


I wanted a second mana-producing creature to provide backup for [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker), and [Rofellos, Llanowar Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rofellos%2C+Llanowar+Emissary) seemed to fit the bill. If you have four Forests on the battlefield, he'll produce infinite mana just like [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker). Since the rest of the deck is colorless, that should be easy enough to accommodate.

The rest of the deck needs to be filled with artifacts to make sure you always have two in hand for [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker), so I searched for some that would provide support for the combo. [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thousand-Year+Elixir) seemed like an excellent choice, allowing you to activate [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker) and Rofellos immediately rather than having to wait for summoning sickness to wear off. You can also use it to untap the creature for just one mana, giving you more mana to work with when getting the combo started. If you have a [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker), [Sword of the Paruns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+the+Paruns), and two other artifacts in hand, [Thousand-Year Elixir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thousand-Year+Elixir) allows you to cast [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker), tap it for six mana, untap it and tap it again for another six, then cast [Sword of the Paruns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+the+Paruns), equip it, and untap [Metalworker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metalworker) to get the combo rolling.


[Conjurer's Bauble](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conjurer%27s+Bauble) and [Ãther Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Spellbomb) can both sacrifice themselves to draw a card, allowing you to dig through your deck a bit to find the combo pieces. [Ãther Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Spellbomb) can also be sacrificed to temporarily get rid of a problem creature. [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) is one of the most powerful card-selection tools ever printed. You can look at and rearrange the top three cards of your library at any time for just one mana, and if there's a card you need immediately, you can trade in the Top to draw it. I'll also include several fetch lands in the mana base to allow you to shuffle your library if you don't like what you see with the top. The fetches can also grab a [Breeding Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pool) to allow you to activate [Ãther Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Spellbomb) if necessary.

Flickering [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) once per turn is fine, but I think we might be able to do a bit better than that. The first card that came to mind was [Cloudshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudshift). If you cast it targeting [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), the Witness will flicker out and back in, and when its enters-the-battlefield ability goes on the stack, you can target [Cloudshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudshift) and return it to your hand. All you need to do this an infinite number of times is a way to produce one white mana each time.



![Cloudshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cloudshift)

Â 

I considered using something like [Carnival of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnival+of+Souls) or [Mana Echoes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Echoes), but that would also require a [Skyshroud Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyshroud+Elf) to filter the mana into white. In the end, I decided to use the old combo staple, [Earthcraft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthcraft). Each time [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) leaves the battlefield, it will return untapped, allowing you to tap it to [Earthcraft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthcraft), untapping a Plains to give you the mana to cast [Cloudshift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudshift) again.


All we have so far, however, is an [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) flickering in and out, with no real effect on the game. That's easily fixed, however. Having a [Soul's Attendant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Attendant) on the board will allow you to gain infinite life, making it almost impossible for most decks to kill you. There's still the matter of winning the game, however. If you enchant a basic Forest with [Utopia Sprawl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Sprawl), or a Plains with [Wild Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Growth), you'll net one green mana each time you flicker [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness). You can then cast [Helix Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helix+Pinnacle) and put 100 counters on it, winning the game on your next upkeep. Since the Pinnacle has shroud, it's unlikely your opponent will be able to remove it before that happens.

With lands potentially producing more than one mana, [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) becomes another option for flickering [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) infinitely. You can also still produce infinite mana by putting two copies in any combination of [Utopia Sprawl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Sprawl) and [Wild Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Growth) on a land, or just one [Dawn's Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawn%27s+Reflection).


[Eladamri's Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eladamri%27s+Call) and [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor) can search up [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and [Soul's Attendant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul%27s+Attendant), and [Enlightened Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) and [Idyllic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Idyllic+Tutor) can find [Helix Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helix+Pinnacle), [Earthcraft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthcraft), or one of the land enchantments. Combined, they make this fairly complicated combo much easier to put together.

These days, we have access to a threat far better than [Hunted Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Dragon) for this deck: [Karrthus, Tyrant of Jund](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karrthus%2C+Tyrant+of+Jund). Not only is it larger than [Hunted Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Dragon), it gives all your other Dragons haste as well, allowing you to win immediately with only three Dragons rather than four.  ***Magic** 2014*  also gives the deck another potential tool in [Scourge of Valkas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+of+Valkas). With three Dragons, the *Scourge*s only deal 1 less damage total than [Bogardan Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite)âstill more than enough to win the game. Once you reach four, however, The many triggers will nail your opponent for 30 damage overall, or allow you to deal 26 before attacking for 19. Perhaps that's overkill, but if you're facing down a deck that gains life, like the [Martyr of Sands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands)/Urzatron deck Mihara overcame in the semifinals, you might need it.

With [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) now requiring you to cast only two spells before it to win the game, getting to nine mana becomes the important part. [Grim Monolith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Monolith) works wonders here. It can give you one extra mana immediately, or you can invest two mana early on to get an extra three later on. [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) serves a similar function, producing one less mana, but with the ability to make mana of any color. [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom), [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song), and [Rite of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) were the key pieces of acceleration in Mihara's deck, and I see no reason to change that. All of them help ensure that explosive turn-four win.


[Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) is also still here, as an amazing way to protect your combo. If your opponent tries to counter one of your spells, you can counter the counter with [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) if you can still combo off after paying the extra mana. If you can't, you can counter your own spell, returning it to your hand and drawing a card to help you go off next turn.
