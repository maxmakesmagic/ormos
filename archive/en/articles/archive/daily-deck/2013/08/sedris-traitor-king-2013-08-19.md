Sedris, the Traitor King
========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on August 21, 2013 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Luis Scott-Vargas











It doesn't take much to sell me on a deck with [Arcanis the Omnipotent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcanis+the+Omnipotent), and that was the first card I saw [in this list](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/deck.asp?deck_id=1149985). While Arcanis isn't the commander, he certainly combines well with [Sedris, the Traitor King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedris%2C+the+Traitor+King), which coincidentally is another card I've enjoyed playing with. Granted, that is all in Limited, but the idea is the same. You get some creatures into the graveyard, whether the "fair" way by having them killed or the combo way by putting them there yourself, and Sedris does the rest. [Unearth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unearth)ing creatures with "enter the battlefield" abilities or sweet tap abilities is especially fun, and if you take a look at the list, that's exactly what's going on here. 

 The deck actually doesn't have a ton of creatures, but the ones it does have are awesome. Besides Arcanis, digging up [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer), various [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) creatures, or [Mindclaw Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindclaw+Shaman) all sounds excellent. This particular list is heavily focused on going infinite, which is something I have mixed feelings about, but I like to represent the range of the format. Some people do enjoy comboing off, and here we have a few different ways to do that. 

 Sedris makes assembling the Kiki-Jiki plus [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite)/[Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) combo much easier, and also assists in half of the [Deadeye Navigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) plus [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) plus [Gilded Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus) combo (if you blink the [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) over and over again, you get infinite mana by untapping the Lotus). [Rings of Brighthearth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rings+of+Brighthearth) also certainly factor into some of the shenanigans, and [Palinchron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palinchron) isn't much of a slouch in the combo department as well. 

 Besides all of the infinite combos, there are a lot of ways to get value in the deck, with a ton of card draw and tutoring offering answers to every problem. There are even some nice Planeswalkers here, something I also approve of. Taking extra turns with [Ral Zarek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek) is just about the sweetest thing you can possibly do in a game of **Magic**. 

 If you wanted to dial back the combo aspect some, putting in more value creatures (in the same vein as [the Kiki-Jiki deck](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/deck/1299) I made last week) could help, as well as widening the range of creatures overall. If you vary the cards that combine well with Sedris, you will have a much broader range of experiences with the deck, and I'd start by looking at "enters the battlefield" effects, with an emphasis on the [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)-types of the world.
