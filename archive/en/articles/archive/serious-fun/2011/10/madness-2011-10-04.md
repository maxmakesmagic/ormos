Welcome, fellow Internet denizens! This is the first, and only, Serious Fun dedicated to spinning the hairiest works and darkest decks *Innistrad* has to offer us. You won't find any Avacynian fallacies here. We're going to rock the blood red *all night, every night!*


That's right, you're reading Howlin' Mad Stybs's "Mad Minutes" feedback, a time when I take the crazy things *you* sent in and put them up for the entire Internet to see. Over the last two weeks I've challenged you to challenge me with decks for our [Werewolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advanced&subtype=%7C%5B%22Werewolf%22%5D) and [Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advanced&subtype=%7C%5B%22Zombie%22%5D) friends. Let me just say that some of you are real animals, ones even my hardened heart wouldn't want to meet during the day in Thraben.


This is the week we'll look at some of the best you minions have offered up. It's time to wake a sleeping dog to go dig up some dead.


Are. You. *Ready? Awoooooooo!*


Alright partner, I know you're ready to saddle up and ride the moonlight. Our first rodeo is from Mr. Langley, and he took our fuzzy buddies straight into multiplayer mayhem.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf163_moonmist.jpg)[Moonmist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist) | Art by [Ryan Yee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Ryan+Yee%22%5D)


 



> 
> In multiplayer, players cast spells: lots and lots of spells. There are plenty of nice white cards to limit spell casting (and werewolves are awfully meek when they first appear). There are two big problems. The first is practical. In my game group, telling everyone they can't play spells means I either die before I pull off my grand strategy or everyone sits around yawning while I win and I retire the deck for a long, long time. The second is about flavor. I want my werewolves to wolf out and howl at the moon, not carefully control the board like fiendish masterminds.
> 
> 
> 
> ![Moonmist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Moonmist)
> 
>  
> 
> So, my answer to the puzzle turns out to be the simple one: [Moonmist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist). Eight copies of [Moonmist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist) if needed. In addition to letting my werewolves do their thing, in multiplayer owning a one-sided [Fog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) makes everyone else seem like much better targets. By the way, with any werewolf deck, make friends with whoever takes their turn before yours. When the time is right promise to attack their enemies if they would just refrain from casting spells for a turn.
> 
> 
>

> I mostly concentrate on werewolves helping werewolves, with a couple of fun supporting humans. [Quicksilver Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Amulet) lets you put a creature into play without actually playing any spells. [Bramblecrush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblecrush) and [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) add some flexibility to the deck in multiplayer but can easily be replaced with your favorite answers. [Noxious Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Revival) keeps [Moonmist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist) around for later use, but can bring back other cards you might need.

Mr. Langley, I like you. I like you so much I'd let you meet my sister. What you have here is a wonderfully ferocious deck built under a daring design: just the most recent sets. While diving through treasure troves of sets in storage is an adventure we should take regularly, you pulled this right off the top. I applaud you.


And you did the dirty work of explaining it, too. You have all the elements of a multiplayer experience:


* A clear objective (Attack!)
* A bargaining chip (A [Fog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog)-type card, in this case [Moonmist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist))
* A little variety ([Elder of Laurels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+of+Laurels) and [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) for attacks, [Quicksilver Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quicksilver+Amulet) for spells)
* A way to recur ([Noxious Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Revival))


![Noxious Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Noxious+Revival)

 

Unfortunately, I can't give Mr. Langley full credit for the most entertaining list. *That* dubious honor, among things already quite dubious, belongs to a Mr. Eichenberger and a color I'm loathe to love: blue.


You see, Mr. Eichenberger is something of a smarty-pants. Instead of taking to moon-walking a Werewolf the usual way, he took a very different tack.


 



> 
> After I first read over the rules page for the new double-faced cards, I noticed all kinds of interesting rules interactions with copy effects (my favorite kind of effects) and with face-down cards (specifically [Ixidron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixidron)). According to the [rules page](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/article.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/innistrad/dfcrules):
> 
> 
> * Double-faced cards are always face up; they are never face down. If an effect (say, Ixidron) tries to turn a double-faced card face down, nothing happens.
> * If any card or token other than a double-faced card is instructed to transform, nothing happens. It won't be turned face down.
> * The only objects that can transform are cards that physically have two faces. If a token or a card with a regular **Magic** back is instructed to transform, instead nothing will happen. Even if a token or non-double-faced card is a copy of one face of a double-faced card, it can't transform.
> 
> My blue-green Werewolf-themed deck submission takes advantage of those interactions. Play a Werewolf (preferably a [Gatstaf Shepherd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatstaf+Shepherd) or [Mayor of Avabruck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck)), get it transformed (with [Moonmist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist), if you have to), then start making token copies of it with [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart) or [Rite of Replication](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication). Because they're copies, you won't have to worry about them ever un-transforming.
>

> I really like this setup because it's difficult to wipe the board with damage, and even if you do you're just setting me up for a [Skaab Ruinator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Ruinator) or [Unbreathing Horde](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unbreathing+Horde). It's a constant stream of pressure; instead of taking time to build up, it's almost always swinging hard on turn 4. Even if the game goes on and on, it can keep going. I think people will be hard pressed to keep with the token generation, so even if they can set up a strong early defense, a [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) or some buff elves won't save you from 20 5/5 Zombies.

Who needs Glissa when we're packing ruination? There's been a lot of chatter on those *other* columns about [Skaab Ruinator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Ruinator), but I really like what it's doing here. It's not about little tricks and subtle cues; this brain-hungry legion is out to get you. Swords, fire, and even divine judgment won't be enough to close off the tide of the dead.
