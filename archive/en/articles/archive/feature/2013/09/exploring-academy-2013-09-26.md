Exploring the Academy
=====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 26, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I know that Caleb Durward ("He Who Got Survival Banned" is his official title) has been a champion of a black-green Legacy deck with [Veteran Explorer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Explorer) for a long time (inexplicably called Nic Fit), so when I saw this list I immediately thought of him. This particular deck has added a whole new color, but all that does is up the sweetness factor, and who doesn't like sweetness? 


![Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Academy+Rector)
 There are two main combos going on here, and both involve sacrifices made for gain. First, you play [Veteran Explorer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Explorer) and sacrifice him to [Phyrexian Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Tower) or [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy). Second, you play an [Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector) with all that mana, and again make an offering. Rector fetches up [Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed), [Recycle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recycle), or [Recurring Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recurring+Nightmare), and the value begins. Deed is kind of the whammy, since getting it means you are under pressure, but if you have to, you have to. Getting [Recycle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recycle) or [Recurring Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recurring+Nightmare) is the best plan, as both provide you with a steady stream of card advantage. 


[Recycle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recycle) is pretty simple and is only bounded by the amount of mana you have available. Most times it comes out you end up dumping a bunch of stuff on the board and casting multiple [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy)s, making it hard for your opponent to come back. 


[Recurring Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recurring+Nightmare) is even a little more broken, as the deck has a laundry list of creatures that provide neat effects. [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), and [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) are the big three, and are all fully capable of ending games (albeit with slightly different approaches). The deck also has a toolbox of utility creatures, with [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze), [Gaddock Teeg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg), [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), and Sigarda all answering specific decks or scenarios. 

 Another piece of the puzzle is [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith). It starts by fetching [Veteran Explorer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Explorer), but it can quickly move up the chain to get all the cool creatures I talked about earlier (minus the non-green ones, of course). I'm surprised there isn't a [Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) in the deck, and I would definitely test one out if you decide to run the deck. 

 Lastly, I must mention the trio of [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top)s. It's nice to see the card outside [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) decks, and with all the shuffling, this deck is a nice fit for it.
