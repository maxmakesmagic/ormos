In celebration of Rerun Week, Daily Decks brings you Turbo Stasis! Played by present Wizards R&D member Matt Place to a U.S. Nationals Top 8 berth in 1996, Turbo Stasis was a critical development in the world of competitive **Magic**. The deck sought to out-metagame the field, performing very well against the Necrodecks that were so popular in the day. [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) netted Matt plenty of extra cards, and when his opponent was least expecting it, he'd drop [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis) to lock up the game. After paying for the enchantment for turn after turn, Matt would bounce the [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis) back to his hand, reset his mana while his opponent stayed tapped out, and rerun the same [Stasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis) right back onto the field. The deck also marked one of the first cross-continental efforts by professional players with Matt getting the idea for the deck from friends in Europe.
