Pro Tour–Berlin Round 4: Great Shades of Elvish
===============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 31, 2008 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Tim Willoughby












Makoto Nagashima vs. Shuhei Nakamura
------------------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptber08/r4_nagashima.jpg)*Makoto Nagashima faces his friend—and the mirror match—in Round 4.*
On Wednesday night, there was a little conversation going on as to the potential Top 8 players at this Pro Tour. Rich Hagon was quick to throw Shuhei Nakamura's hat into the ring.


"Shuhei only loses when it's Sealed Deck and he's opened a really bad pool!" he exclaimed.


It's a fair comment. Coming into Pro Tour–Berlin, Nakamura has had a formidable season, which puts him in a runaway lead for the Player of the Year race.


When we started talking about what deck Shuhei might be playing, there was only one choice. Elves got him to the Top 8 in Hollywood, and given that there is an Elf deck playable in Extended, it seemed likely that he'd be going the same way.


In many respects the Extended Elves! deck is not really the same deck at all. That exclamation point is just the beginning of the differences. With [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature), [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary), and [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force), it has a lot of card drawing, and [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) and [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) produce a huge amount of mana. The win conditions have been varied to say the least. [Predator Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator+Dragon), [Sundering Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundering+Titan), [Mirror Entity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity), and [Ulasht, the Hate Seed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulasht%2C+the+Hate+Seed) have all been talked about, and are easy to fetch with [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling). Other win conditions that have been seen include [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station), [Roar of the Crowd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Crowd), [Brain Freeze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Freeze), [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) and [Cloudstone Curio](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudstone+Curio). One way or another this is a deck that can go from zero to hero at blistering speed, and has proven a popular choice for the weekend, played by many—including both the players at the feature match table.


### Game 1


Nagashima won the roll and led with a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel), while Nakamura had a turn-one [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote). A second [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) and [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote) from Nagashima proved quite the beatdown squad, while Shuhei had only an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) for turn two.


It seemed early on that Nagashima was well ahead, soon putting down a [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) and a second [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote). These would be enough to get an engine going, but it seemed that Nagashima was short one crucial piece: the card drawing.


The [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) count was much higher for Nakamura, who had a second, but was uncharacteristically short on mana on his side of the board. Nakamura carefully pieced together what his turn *would* be, playing out cards face down and keeping a mental count of what was going on. After some thought, he played a [Summoner's Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoner%27s+Pact) to find [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid). This enabled a [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature), followed by a pair of [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves).


[Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) got returned by [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote) to untap [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), and then more [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) shenanigans allowed a second Druid and [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) to be played again. At this point, with a little wince, Shuhei passed.


Now it was Nagashima's time to do some clever math and make his play. His team convoked out a big [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) for [Predator Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator+Dragon), and the collective devour was enough to make the dragon lethal in a single swing.


Nagashima 1, Nakamura 0


The two Japanese friends had identical sideboards, and seemed likely to have sideboarded the same way, including [Orzhov Pontiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff) and [Goblin Sharpshooter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter) in their arsenal. Having [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) and [Birchlore Rangers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birchlore+Rangers), playing these off-colour monsters would not be a problem.


### Game 2


Shuhei had not a single one-drop, but he remained calm, playing a [Wirewood Hivemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Hivemaster) on turn two, which quickly proved a good choice as he gained Insect tokens from the Elves of his opponent. Nagashima was stuck on a single land, but looked likely to work his way out of trouble as he played back-to-back [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid)s.


When a second [Wirewood Hivemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Hivemaster) came from Nakamura, Nagashima's unflustered exterior showed its first signs of wavering. He played [Summoner's Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoner%27s+Pact) for [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) and played it. From there came a [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature). When Nagashima played just one more Elf though, it enabled Nakamura to convoke out a large enough [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) to fetch [Orzhov Pontiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff).


The Pontiff killed of Nagashima's team, leaving him woefully unable to pay for his [Summoner's Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoner%27s+Pact), ensuring his loss.


Nagashima 1, Nakamura 1


### Game 3


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptber08/r4_nakamura.jpg)*Shuhei Nakamura does not like what he sees.*
Both players kept their opening seven, and while Nagashima had a turn-one [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), Shuhei led with [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves). An [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) came from Nagashima, while Shuhei had a small [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) turn, drawing a few cards and ending up with a [Wirewood Symbiote](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote) and a pair of [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves).


As it turned out, though, Nagashima had a bigger turn ahead. With a pair of copies of [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature) and a [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) to go with his [Heritage Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), he was soon going completely nuts. After playing six or seven Elves, and drawing quite a few cards, he got up to his third [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature). With a pair of [Nettle Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel)s, he had a net gain on mana for playing most creatures, along with drawing three cards apiece.


The final piece of the puzzle was to actually win the game. [Birchlore Rangers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birchlore+Rangers) did that, allowing him to play [Predator Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator+Dragon) from his hand. He sacrificed eight creatures to make his dragon a 20/20 and swung. That would do it.


Nagashima 2, Nakamura 1
