Return of the Hawk
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 10, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







It's been long enough since we've seen [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s carrying Swords that I was legitimately surprised to see such a deck among Daily Event lists on **Magic Online**. This isn't exactly the UW list that dominated Standard for so long, as it's got an additional color, but the [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)-Sword combo is still alive and well. 


![Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Squadron+Hawk)
 The main thrust of this deck's strategy is to disrupt opponents with [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas), all while pressuring them with [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Squadron%2BHawk)s, Swords, [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), and Elspeths. The combination of [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) and [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) can sometimes just win games by itself, especially given the mana bases people tend to play in Modern. 



[Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) is awesome in this deck, too, even more than it normally is. It does its usual job of blocking small creatures, making removal spells less good, and totally hosing [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) and Hexproof, but there's *sword* of an important reason this deck gets to play it main. Equipping a [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) justifies its inclusion, mainly because it was so close to playable to begin with, and a 2/6 with protection from various colors really is difficult to stop. 


 Elspeth and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) provide more bodies for Sword purposes, as well as just playing offense and defense with their usual efficiency. I kind of like the one [Clutch of the Undercity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+the+Undercity) too, even if it does look a little random. It's basically an Elspeth/[Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) split card, with the additional bonus of aggressively bouncing a permanent if need be. 


 Speaking of sweet one-ofs, check out the sideboard: one [Blessed Reversal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Reversal) (which I'm sure you had to check to see what it does), one [Fracturing Gust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fracturing+Gust), one [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and one [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify), among others. I've always been a big fan of lots of one-of sideboard cards, just to make sure as many bases are covered as possible. I have to admit, I don't exactly know what [Blessed Reversal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Reversal) is for, but much like this deck, it seems sweet.
