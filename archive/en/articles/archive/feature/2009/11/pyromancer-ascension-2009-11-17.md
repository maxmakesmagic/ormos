![Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pyromancer+Ascension)In Tucson, Arizona Karl Goronowski came to play for the [2009 *Zendikar* Game Day](/en/events/coverage/zendikar-game-day). The deck he called upon for the task? A blue-black-red control deck with a powerful endgame of [Pyromancer Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension). By controlling the field early with spells like [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Double Negative](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Double+Negative), and [Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake), Karl was able to build counters on the Ascension. That meant late-game spells like [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) netted *two* additional turns, while [Worldly Counsel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Counsel) and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) drew double the cards.
