Draft 3 Report: Antoine Ruel
============================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 15, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_paulsottosanti.jpg)
By Paul Sottosanti











Antoine Ruel has been a fixture on the Pro Tour for some time now. He and his brother Olivier both have a number of strong finishes to their name. He's currently sitting pretty at the top table at 6-0 along with seven other undefeated players. Here's the seating order:


1 Salvin, Jean Charles [FRA]  

2 Haim, Christopher [FRA]  

3 Oiso, Masashi [JPN]  

4 Seck, Ben [AUS]  

5 Fabiano, Gerard [USA]  

6 Jonsson, Anton [SWE]  

7 Ruel, Antoine [FRA]  

8 Spaulding, Ian [USA]


Antoine's first pack presented an easy decision--[Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) was clearly better than [Raise the Alarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm), [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit), [Fireshrieker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireshrieker), [Fatespinner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatespinner) and [Tel-Jilad Chosen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tel-Jilad+Chosen). His next pack offered him a choice of golems; [Mirror Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Golem) and [Pewter Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pewter+Golem) were the only real options. He selected the [Mirror Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Golem) as it's a better card and doesn't represent a commitment. Ian Spaulding, sitting to Antoine's left, pretty much had to take the [Pewter Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pewter+Golem) (he had taken [Fatespinner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatespinner) from the previous pack) so this helped set up some good picks in pack two.



![Barbed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Barbed+Lightning)

In the next pack he had the choice between [Iron Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr), [Pyrite Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb), [Somber Hoverguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somber+Hoverguard), [Chromatic Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere), [Irradiate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Irradiate), and [Creeping Mold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Mold). The Spellbomb was slightly tempting but he chose to slide into an Affinity deck by selecting the Hoverguard. He was then rewarded with a second [Somber Hoverguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somber+Hoverguard), a [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast), a [Cobalt Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobalt+Golem) and a [Seat of the Synod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seat+of+the+Synod), all solid cards for his deck. It helped that the three drafters to his right all ended up in Green, with both of them to his immediate right in Green/Red. Talk about heaven for a Blue mage.


The rest of the first round was uneventful. He picked up a couple White cards as a possible splash and also a [Tree of Tales](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tree+of+Tales) (over [Chromatic Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere)), but he was still solidly intending to be Blue/Red. Meanwhile, Ian to his right was solidly in Blue/Black, as there were four drafters in a row (including Antoine) that were avoiding the Black cards.


Antoine's second pack presented an interesting option: stay true by taking [Neurok Spy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy) or splash a little bit with [Betrayal of Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Betrayal+of+Flesh). He chose to stick with the original plan and drafted the Phantom Warrior. He then picked up an [Electrostatic Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Bolt), a [Silver Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Myr) (over [Goblin Replica](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Replica)), and a second [Neurok Spy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Spy) from a pack that gave Ian a [Looming Hoverguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looming+Hoverguard). Ian was shaking his head as he passed the next pack to Antoine. It included both [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) and [Deconstruct](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deconstruct) (Ian had taken a [Somber Hoverguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somber+Hoverguard)). Antoine was tempted by the Shaman but ended up taking a badly needed [Iron Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Myr) instead. Anton and Gerard happily snapped up the Green cards.


In the very next pack Antoine picked up a [Copper Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Copper+Myr), making him regret the decision to pass on the Shaman, and then got a [Rust Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rust+Elemental), [Great Furnace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Furnace) and [Stalking Stones](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stalking+Stones) to round out the second round of packs.


Antoine's Darksteel pack gave him a [Barbed Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbed+Lightning) (over [Purge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purge)) and he then received an [Echoing Ruin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Ruin) after Anton took Vulshok War Boar. Then the Blue faucet opened, with double [Spire Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem), [Vedalken Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Engineer), and [Neurok Prodigy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Prodigy) all flowing into his stack. [Darksteel Ingot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Ingot) and Pendant rounded out the deck along with a late-pick [Echoing Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) that had earlier made it through the Blue mages to his left.


His deck ended up quite solid, with a strong Blue creature base and great removal in Red. Antoine hoped to 2-1 with the deck, although he felt 1-2 was also a possibility if his draws weren't strong. Basically the plan was to just "take it and see what happens."
