Pauper Green Post
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 14, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Aggressive mono-green decks and [Cloudpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost) decks have both been very popular in Pauper, but BUEDEVIL has combined the two into an aggressive mono-green ramp/land destruction [Cloudpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost) deck. 

![Cloudpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cloudpost) This deck is a clever metagame choice in a world where so many players are playing [Cloudpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost) because it's so good at attacking their plan while furthering its own plan. [Scattershot Archer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattershot+Archer)s are excellent against the popular blue aggro decks and the sideboard has a lot of removal for Affinity and Infect, the other common aggressive decks. 


[Aurochs Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurochs+Herd) is an interesting choice, but it offers a lot of card advantage that should make it difficult for control decks to deal with. [Ulamog's Crusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%27s+Crusher) is the best finisher against people who don't have removal, and [Fangren Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Marauder) is a good bullet against all the decks that are putting artifacts in the graveyard. [Fierce Empath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fierce+Empath) lets you choose the best direction to go with these. 

 I'm surprised by some of the cards this deck doesn't play. [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) are awesome, because they allow a turn-two [Thermokarst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thermokarst) to get the land destruction going, but they're not supported by [Fyndhorn Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fyndhorn+Elves) here. [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) is used instead, which still accelerates to most of the expensive spells, but it's interesting that blocking is apparently more important than getting the third mana on the second turn. [Quirion Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Ranger) is another card that would seem to have a place given the low land count, but it would require [Fyndhorn Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fyndhorn+Elves) to have enough creatures to let it consistently generate mana, since untapping [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) doesn't do anything.
