Round 10: Svend Geertsen vs. Kai Budde
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 4, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Headshot%209-2014_3.jpg)
By Randy Buehler











In the final round of Day 1, Kai Budde granted Svend Geertsen a take-back that cost Budde the game and helped Geertsen end the day with a perfect record. Three rounds later they met again and again a first place tie was at stake. Each had an eventful draft, with Budde starting with [Kamahl, Pit Fighter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Pit+Fighter) and Geertsen starting out with [Wayward Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wayward+Angel). Things worked out better for Budde from there with a second Kamahl finding it's way into his deck deep into pack 2. Geertsen's luck wasn't that good. He did get passed a [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) by Brian Davis, but Davis then drafted black-blue anyway and Geertsen wound up spread across three colors.

![Temporary Insanity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Temporary+Insanity)Budde drafted a pair of [Diligent Farmhand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diligent+Farmhand)s in *Odyssey* and that allowed him to splash blue for two copies of [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and a [Repel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repel). He seemed very happy with the card advantage he's been getting from the Analyses and he got to play it and flash it back early in Game 1 against Geertsen. That dug deep enough into his library to find a Kamahl, but Geertsen made things complicated by playing [Hypochondria](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypochondria) and using it to defend a pair of fliers. Budde would have been able to run Gertsen out of cards if only he could play the other Kamahl that was in his hand, but alas, Kamahl is a legend and that second copy wasn't very useful. Budde was forced to race an opponent who had out [Hypochondria](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypochondria) and three plains and wasn't casting any spells. Of course, he is Kai Budde. He attacked with Kamahl #1 and Geertsen blocked it, killing it. Budde then used [Temporary Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporary+Insanity) to steal a flier and preserve his last point of life. That bought him the turn he needed to play Kamahl #2 and attack for lethal damage.Game 2 was all about the beatdown. Budde smashed with a pair of [Krosan Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Avenger)s while [Nantuko Disciple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Disciple) threatened to pump them up. Geertsen was forced to line a bunch of creatures up in front of them in order to kill them off. He still took so much damage that even [Wayward Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wayward+Angel) wasn't good enough to stabilize the board. [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) caused Budde to pause briefly, but he still just sent his men into the red zone with [Muscle Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muscle+Burst) and the Disciple backing them up. Geertsen had no really good blocking options and actually rolled a die to help him decide what to do. He wound up with no hand, no graveyard, and a lone [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) in play. Budde promptly top-decked a [Temporary Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporary+Insanity) and didn't even give Geertsen a chance to chump block.
