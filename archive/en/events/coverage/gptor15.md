===============================

TOP 8 COVERAGE



The largest Grand Prix ever in Toronto is now in the books, and home-town boy Lucas Siow is your champion. After many Grand Prix near misses, Siow and his Abzan friends finally got there.


This tournament was full of interesting evolutions. Though there were many Esper Dragons decks and multiple Red decks that performed well, none converted to the Top 8. There were *seven* green decks with green in the Top 8, and all of them had Den Protector—sometimes with Deathmist Raptor, but sometimes not.


Everyone knew Den Protector was the real deal going into this weekend, but no one could stop it. Siow’s innovative Abzan build, using Den Protector without the Raptor, piloted both he and his friend Dan Fournier into the Top 8. Thanks to his shrewd piloting and his graveyard shenanigans, now Siow is Toronto’s Den Protector.

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Good morning from Canada! The players are seated and they are bubbling with anticipation. There are 185 players qualified for the second day, which is quite a large number. Though it’s about one-tenth of the original field of 1,613, there’s still a lot of *Magic* to be played.


To the surprise of few, the three Abzan variants—Midrange, Megamorph, and Aggro—make up the bulk of the field. Siege Rhino is still good, guys. However, both Esper Dragons and the Red Aggressive decks are doing quite well for themselves.


Six players stand a 9-0. Brad Nelson, Gordo Tonner, Eric Severson, Ben Feingersh, Robert Vaughan, and James Wray all left the first day unscathed.


But players like Christian Calcano, (19) Craig Wescoe, (13) Paul Rietzl, Mark Herberholz, Gaudenis Vidugiris, Jon Stern, Lucas Siow, Tom Martell, and Brian Braun-Duin are running closely behind them.


*Dragons of Tarkir* Standard continues to evolve. Though we’ve seen the first incarnations of the top archetypes, whose card choices are better? And more importantly, whose play can keep the choices from hurting them?


By the end of today, we will crown a Grand Prix Toronto champion. Tune into to twitch.tv/magic all day to find out who it will be.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



It’s been a hard-fought day in Canada, and the first day of Grand Prix Toronto is in the books! The field of names was staggering—heck, it still is—but the undefeated crowd is littered with some new faces, joined by one big dog.


At this point, calling Brad Nelson a “Standard Master” is pretty passé. Regardless, Standard Master is 9-0, along with Gordo Tonner, Eric Severson, Ben Feingersh, Robert Vaughan, and James Wray.


Right behind them are Christian Calcano, (19) Craig Wescoe, (13) Paul Rietzl, Mark Herberholz, Gaudenis Vidugiris, Jon Stern, Tom Martell, and Brian Braun-Duin—each with only one loss.


The *Dragons of Tarkir* Standard format is full of jockeying. The control decks are skewing more so to beat the mirrors, but that’s creating a hole for the aggressive decks to slip right in.  What player and what deck will make it to the top?


Tune in tomorrow at 9am Eastern to catch all the action at twitch.tv/magic and dailymtg.com.


Good night from Toronto!
