Duskmantle Delver
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 1, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I found this deck looking through **Magic Online** Daily results, and it struck me because no one earned six or more wins in Standard at [Pro Tour *Gatecrash*](/en/events/coverage/martell-calls-down-reckoning-pro-tour) with [Duskmantle Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Seer), a card this deck uses a full play set of. 


![Duskmantle Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Duskmantle+Seer)
 This is an aggressive Grixis deck that partners last year's dominant [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) with its old friends [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) alongside the powerful new flier from *Gatecrash*. 


 Aside from [Duskmantle Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Seer), the only card in this deck that costs more than two mana is a single [Counterflux](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterflux), so [Duskmantle Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Seer) shouldn't do too much damage to you. Most opponents, though, can be expected to have a least a handful of spells that cost five and, sometimes, several others that cost significantly more than that. 


 The deck only plays twenty cards that [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) can find or that flip [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), which is a little low, but [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is such an important part of the plan for this deck that it's willing to take a risk and maybe attack with a 1/1 a few times. 


 One of this deck's biggest advantages is the sideboard, where four [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)es allow it to stick a threat (ideally [Jace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace)) against control decks, and [Rolling Temblor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) and [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) handle any weakness it might have from small creatures.
