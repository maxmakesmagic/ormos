8 Decks with Amonkhet Goodness
==============================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 28, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











Since this was the first Premier Event since *Amonkhet*'s release, I took the liberty of poring through the pages, frantically searching for decks which utilized the newest cards. Well, they were digital pages, so it's not all that bad. After hours and hours of scouring, here were some of the cards that we've seen in the most popular decks so far.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vizier+of+Remedies)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rhonas+the+Indomitable)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhonas+the+Indomitable)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Desert+Cerodon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desert+Cerodon)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Horror+of+the+Broken+Lands)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horror+of+the+Broken+Lands)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Archfiend+of+Ifnir)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archfiend+of+Ifnir)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Start+%2F%2F+Finish)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Start+%2F%2F+Finish)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Insult+%2F%2F+Injury)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insult+%2F%2F+Injury)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=By+Force)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=By+Force)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sweltering+Suns)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sweltering+Suns)
* [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies), [Rhonas the Indomitable](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhonas+the+Indomitable) (Collected Company decks)
* [Desert Cerodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desert+Cerodon), [Horror of the Broken Lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horror+of+the+Broken+Lands), [Archfiend of Ifnir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archfiend+of+Ifnir) (Living End)
* [Cartouche of Solidarity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartouche+of+Solidarity) (Sideboard card for White-Green Hexproof Auras)
* [Start // Finish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Start+%2F%2F+Finish) (Esper Polymorph)
* [Insult // Injury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insult+%2F%2F+Injury) (Singleton in Blue-Red Gifts Storm)
* [By Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=By+Force) (used in numerous sideboards)
* [Sweltering Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sweltering+Suns) (Red-Green Titan Shift and Grixis Control)
* [Irrigated Farmland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Irrigated+Farmland), [Fetid Pools](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fetid+Pools), [Canyon Slough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Slough), [Sheltered Thicket](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sheltered+Thicket), [Scattered Groves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattered+Groves)

Then, there were also some rather unique brews which house some *Amonkhet* treasures. These decks might not have occupied a large percentage of the metagame but a brew is a brew and we absolutely wish to share these decklists with you!


These players may not have made the Top 8 of Grand Prix Kobe, but they certainly were among the eight most interesting brews of the weekend!


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kefnet+the+Mindful)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kefnet+the+Mindful)
Akira Kinoshita showed up with a "Turbo Fog" strategy except that it was Mono Blue. Using cards like [Gigadrowse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gigadrowse) and [Exhaustion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exhaustion) to buy himself a lot of time, the goal was to take numerous turns with [Time Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp), [Part the Waterveil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Part+the+Waterveil) and [Walk the Aeons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walk+the+Aeons) (oh, [Temporal Trespass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Trespass) too!) to eventually win with [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) or [Azor's Elocutors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azor%27s+Elocutors)!


[Kefnet the Mindful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kefnet+the+Mindful) in the sideboard does a great job at staying alive against removal-heavy decks, in addition to being a huge beat stick and a card-drawing engine!

[Harsh Mentor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harsh+Mentor) was a perfect foil against [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) because your opponent would need to deal with it before they wanted to gain infinite life with [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and [Vizier of Remedies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizier+of+Remedies). Issei Kahata would try to overload the board with threats in the meantime, and a total of six Bushwhackers and eight "free" creatures ([Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) and [Hidden Herbalists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hidden+Herbalists)) could quickly overwhelm any opponent.


He also played wrangle in the sideboard, which is pretty good against monsters such as [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)!

White-Black Tokens has been a solid archetypes for some time, and Mizuno Akimitsu took the liberty of also adding [Dusk // Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+%2F%2F+Dawn) to the sideboard, since it acted as a one-sided [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). The condition of [Bontu the Glorified](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bontu+the+Glorified) was easy to fulfill, since he will always have a bunch tokens to sacrifice!
