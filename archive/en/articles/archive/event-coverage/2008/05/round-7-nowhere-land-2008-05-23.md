Round 7: Nowhere Land
=====================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 23, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_187.png)
By By Dane Young











Round 7 found these two undefeated players from Denmark (which is “not close to Germany”) and Australia (ditto) fighting for pole position in Day 2.


### Game 1


Jeremy rolled an eight on two six-siders, but Enevoldsen’s roll was merely average at seven.


Neeman kept his seven, while Enevoldsen sent his back to Paris, which might also not be close to Germany. The next six were playable and they were off.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/pthol08/r7_enevoldsen.jpg)*Thomas Enevoldsen and his opponent both start the round undefeated.*A [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) for Neeman was matched by an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) for Enevoldsen. An [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) allowed Neeman to suspend [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate). Not to be outdone, Enevoldsen had a [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears), allowing him to land a [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom).


Neeman’s Cloudskate ticked down as he played a [Calciform Pools](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciform+Pools) and a [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone). [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) put a Faerie Rogue into play at the cost of 1 life before Enevoldsen played [Pendelhaven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven). Neeman used the [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone) to put a charge counter on his pools during Enevoldsen’s end step.


The [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) lost another counter, and Neeman laid an [Adarkar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes) to play [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark). Enevoldsen played a [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) on end step, untapping and putting another token into play, moving to 18 life. His draw step revealed no fourth land, so he passed the turn.


On turn five, Neeman’s [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) was ready for battle, coming in to bounce [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). A second [Adarkar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes) gave Neeman pause. After some thought, he sent his Cloudskate into the red zone. It was Enevoldsen’s turn to think before intercepting the incoming illusion with [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite). [Pendelhaven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) took the Cloudskate down with no losses on Enevoldsen’s side, but a post-combat [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) looked menacing.


Enevoldsen’ turn was again underwhelming, as he didn’t have a land to replay [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), giving Neeman free reign to crack his [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone), play [Mystic Gate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Gate), and send his [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)s rumbling in. [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) and two tokens blocked one of the big fliers, and [Pendelhaven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) again saved the Sprite. [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)’s death was avenged by the return of the [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), sending [Pendelhaven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) back to Enevoldsen’ hand to assure he would essentially miss another land drop.


On Neeman’s end step, Enevoldsen ran out another [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite). The [Pendelhaven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pendelhaven) came back into play, letting [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears) tap for black. [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) landed, and Neeman put a second counter on his [Calciform Pools](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciform+Pools).


Neeman’s turn was big, as he cashed in both of his charge counters and tapped out for a pair of [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation)s, stealing both [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite)s. [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) and Cloudskate took advantage of the opponent’s exposed noggin, smashing in for 6 damage.


A token came into play for Enevoldsen, putting him to 7. [Secluded Glen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Secluded+Glen), while late to the party, gave Enevoldsen some hope for kicking out of the situation he was in as he used a [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) on [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark).


[Venser, Shaper Savant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant) decided to show his face, bouncing the token and again clearing the path to Enevoldsen’s dome. During combat, before attackers were declared, a second [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) on Sower gave Enevoldsen back a sprite, but the rest of the team was unimpressed. Cloudskate, [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) and Sower all wanted a piece, and Enevoldsen decided not to block, going to 2 life.


[Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) put Enevoldsen on 1 during his upkeep. The draw step revealed an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), which came into play before the turn was passed.


[Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) tapped all of Neeman’s creatures and bounced his Sprite, but Enevoldsen grinned sheepishly as he remembered the lethal upkeep on [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom).


Neeman 1, Enevoldsen 0 


Enevoldsen sided in one [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), while Neeman fine tuned his deck, substituting out a [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) and pairs of [Body Double](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double), [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), and [Aven Riftwatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Riftwatcher) for some protection via [Pact of Negation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) and answers to [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) in the forms of [Crovax, Ascendant Hero](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crovax%2C+Ascendant+Hero) and [Wispmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wispmare). Teferi also came in to hopefully handicap the opposition.


### Game 2


Enevoldsen painfully sent his hand back again but kept his second hand of [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears), [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave), [Underground River](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River), [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize).


Enevoldsen led off with [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), while Neeman had and [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears) enabled [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) to get in for some crucial early damage. Neeman could only manage a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) for his turn.


A second [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) entered play for Enevoldsen, allowing him to play his [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) off of [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears). It revealed this little number: [Rune Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag), [Rune Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag), [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink), Venser, [Reflecting Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool), [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark), and [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation). Enevoldsen took out Venser in hopes of stealing the game with his man-lands. [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) put Neeman on 16, while Enevoldsen was ahead on 18.


Neeman just had the already-known [Reflecting Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool) and passed the turn back with a shake of his head. [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears) and a fresh [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) activated both [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault)s, which bashed Neeman down to 12.


Neeman’s turn revealed no land and no solutions to the angry shapeshifting lands on the other side of the table. 4 more damage put Neeman to 8 before [Faerie Conclave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave) joined Enevoldsen’s mana-pool party.


Neeman drew another blank and discarded [Rune Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag), taking 4 more before he packed it in, never drawing a fourth land. Enevoldsen was visibly giddy about stealing the game with essentially no spells.


Enevoldsen 1, Neeman 1


### Game 3


Both players kept their openers for the deciding game. 


Neeman started with [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), and Enevoldsen kept pace with an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). Neeman played his own [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) for his turn, while [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears) snuck a [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) into play, drawing a smirk from Enevoldsen.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/pthol08/r7_neeman.jpg)*Jeremy Neeman is a man with no lands.*Neeman wasn’t about to have another game stolen, so he played a second [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and a [Wispmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wispmare), destroying the [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). Enevoldsen played [Underground River](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River) and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) via [River of Tears](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=River+of+Tears), finding [Pact of Negation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation), [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark), [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink), [Careful Consideration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration), and [Rune Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag), initiating a short exchange.


“You sure don’t like those lands.”


“Nah, I don’t need them.”


[Careful Consideration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration) was seized and it was [Wispmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wispmare)’s turn to attack. Neeman had no fourth land, so he said go. An [Underground River](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River) enabled another [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), which resolved with two lands up.


Neeman drew his card for the turn, covered his face with his hands, and attacked his opponent down to 16. Still no land was forthcoming, and the action was back to Enevoldsen.


[Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) produced a token before a third [Underground River](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River) entered play. A second [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) resolved, concluding Enevoldsen’s turn. Neeman went back to the top of his deck, attacked with [Wispmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wispmare) to take his opponent to 14. With a grimace, Neeman tried to [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) his Elemental. It was allowed, but [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) protected the tribal enchantment.


Two tokens took Enevoldsen to 12 life. A fourth [Underground River](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River) and a [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) cleared the [Wispmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wispmare) out, allowing Enevoldsen to attack with a token and [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona).


Neeman missed yet another land drop, putting him too far behind to catch up to the mounting Faerie army.


Enevoldsen 2, Neeman 1
