Scott-Vargas, Froehlich, and Cheon Chew Up the Competition!
===========================================================




Team Luis Scott-Vargas, (12) Eric Froehlich, and Paul Cheon (team “Pork Bun Oath”) defeated previous Grand Prix San Jose winners (21) Paul Rietzl, Matt Sperling, and Dave Williams to stop the run-back, and claim the title for themselves!

TOP 4 COVERAGE



They were a favorite to win coming into this *Fate Reforged-Khans of Tarkir* Team Sealed weekend, and they didn’t disappoint.  Paul Cheon, (12) Eric Froehlich, and Pro Tour Hall of Fame member Luis Scott-Vargas went undefeated until deep into Day 2, then went undefeated in the Top 4 to claim the Grand Prix San Jose title.


In one of the most drama-filled Finals in recent memory, the team took down the previous Grand Prix San Jose title-holders—Paul Rietzl, Matt Sperling, Dave Williams—with panache, showcasing the diversity and difficulty of team Limited formats.


*Fate Reforged* is off to a bang.  The cards, from mythic rare down to common, have drastically altered limited, as the format evolves towards the Pro Tour next weekend in Washington, D.C.  What does *Fate Reforged* have in store for us at the Pro Tour?  Tune in on Friday, February 6th, at 9 a.m. EST to catch the action all weekend long!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



From 1,965 players, making up 665 teams, just 47 earned the right to return for Sunday here at Grand Prix San Jose. Fate Reforged/Khans of Tarkir Team Limited is the show for today, beginning with six more rounds of Team Sealed.


Starting the day off on the right foot are two undefeated teams: The lossless 8-0-1 local trio of Will Sampson, Andy Lee, and Perry Steever, and the perfect 9-0 super stars of No. 12-ranked Eric Froehlich, Pro Tour Hall of Fame member Luis Scott-Vargas, and former US National champion Paul Cheon. While they’ll start with new Sealed pools today, just like everyone else, both teams are well on their way for a potential Top 4 appearance.


Behind them feature an array of talented players, including teams such as Grand Prix San Jose 2012 winners No. 21-ranked Pro Tour Hall of Fame player Paul Rietzl, Matt Sperling, and Dave Williams; No. 10-ranked Pro Tour Khans of Tarkiri champion Ari Lax, Chris Fennell, No. 24-ranked Pro Tour Dragon’s Maze champion Craig Wescoe; and No. 19-ranked Pro Tour Hall of Fame player Ben Stark, Neil Reeves, and another Pro Tour Hall of Fame player Shuhei Nakamura, just to name a few.


Follow along the path to Top 4 and Team Draft with Brian David-Marshall, Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa, and Marshall Sutcliffe all day at [Twitch.tv/channelfireball](http://www.twitch.tv/channelfireball)!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



The first day of Grand Prix San Jose is in the books! Though 1,965 players showed up, making up 665 teams, only a single team has won each and every round they’ve played—(12) Eric Froehlich, Luis Scott-Vargas, and Paul Cheon. Perhaps you’ve heard of them. This team of all-stars are a hometown favorite, and left the convention center today with their heads held high. Coming up closely behind them are more San Joseans, Will Sampson, Andy Lee, and Perry Steever, the only team at 8-0-1.


*Fate Reforged-Khans of Tarkir* Team Sealed has proven itself to be an extremely skill-testing and rewarding format. Out here the wilds are being whispered, we’re being ambushed by ethereal beings, and being whisked away. And we can’t get enough. A total of 47 teams will be coming back tomorrow with names like (21) Paul Rietzl, Matt Sperling, and Dave Williams; (10) Ari Lax, Chris Fennell, (24) Craig Wescoe; and (19) Ben Stark, Neil Reeves, and Shuhei Nakamura, just to name a few.


Tomorrow morning holds five more rounds of Team Sealed for these select few teams, before the Top 4 team drafts commence! Tune in tomorrow for all the *Fate Reforged* action, here, and at twitch.tv/channelfireball.
