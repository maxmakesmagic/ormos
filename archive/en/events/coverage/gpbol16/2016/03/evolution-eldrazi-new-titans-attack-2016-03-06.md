Evolution of the Eldrazi: New Titans on the Attack
==================================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX BOLOGNA 2016](/en/events/coverage/gpbol16)
 on March 6, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/henke_author.jpg)
By Tobi Henke











Consider this an addendum to [yesterday's piece about the evolution of Eldrazi decks](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpbol16/evolution-eldrazi-eldrazi-world-2016-03-05). Once the dust settled at the end of the day, after the Eldrazi had trampled all over Bologna, it became clear that there was indeed more going on inside Eldrazi Headquarters than just players figuring out sideboard tricks to beat [Worship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship) ([Essence Depleter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Depleter)) or to succeed in the mirror match ([Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) plus [Tomb of the Spirit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tomb+of+the+Spirit+Dragon)).


In fact, several players made it to Day 2 with unusual color combinations or an unusual strategy within an established color combination. Most notably, World *Magic* Cup champion Andrea Mengucci was piloting a version of Red-Green Eldrazi without [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return), instead going for the full line-up of aggro Eldrazi from [Eldrazi Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Mimic) to [Eldrazi Obligator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Obligator).


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gpbol16/BOL_newtitans.jpg)  
*Andrea Mengucci*


"We knew from the beginning we wanted to play Eldrazi," said Mengucci who had built the deck together with Player of the Year Mike Sigrist. "We quickly decided on red-green, the main appeal being [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings) and [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows). Having eight triple lands, together with [Karplusan Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karplusan+Forest), as opposed to just the regular four is huge. Also the deck mulligans way less because of [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings).


"We decided not to go with the popular big version of the deck. The format is fast, after all. Sure, you have good cards against beatdown, but what about combo? The only shame is we have to play [Matter Reshaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Matter+Reshaper) which is not a good card in the deck."


Mengucci said that a total of five people were playing the same list in Detroit. "I think all of them went 8-1."

Not playing the same but a similar red-green list was Federico Ronchi, whom I watched in the feature match area as he demolished an unfortunate pilot of the big version of Red-Green Eldrazi. In a world full of [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher)s, [Eldrazi Obligator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Obligator) looked downright unfair, and [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) way too slow.


With a record of 10-1 at the time of writing, Wouter Noordzij meanwhile was the most succesful of the Eldrazi innovators so far, running green and white. The reasoning behind his choice of colors: "[Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings), and for white, the sideboard. White simply has the most unfair cards."


He described his matchup against other Eldrazi decks as "pretty even," and said, "I have [Oblivion Sower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Sower)s and [World Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=World+Breaker)s, so [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) doesn't really do all that much." He was less optimistic about his chances against White-Blue Eldrazi though and recommended. "Hope they don't draw too many Drowners."
