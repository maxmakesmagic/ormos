The Chosen One
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 12, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






![Ajani's Chosen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ajani%27s+Chosen)
In [this Latest Developments article](/en/articles/archive/latest-developments/core-developments-standard-2014-01-03), Sam Stoddard talked about how core sets contain cards that hint at what is coming in future blocks. One such "plant" card in Magic 2014 is [Ajani's Chosen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Chosen), who can be quite powerful once the full *Theros* block is unleashed upon it. 

 The core synergy with [Ajani's Chosen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Chosen) comes from the removal suite, [Banishing Light](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banishing+Light) and [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere). These two enchantments are some of Standard's most powerful removal spells, and getting a 2/2 on top of that is powerful. Outside of this core synergy, there are many aggressive, disruptive creatures like [Keening Apparition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) and [Lyev Skyknight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyev+Skyknight) to help keep an opponent off balance.
