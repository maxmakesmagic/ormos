Making the Jump to Modern
=========================



 Posted in **How to Build**
 on March 14, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






The sheer number of available tools at our disposal in a format like Modern can seem daunting. Unlike rotating formats, Modern continues to grow with each new set release. One might assume that a format this large would be relatively stagnant; there are so many cards that a few hundred more from a new set may just seem like a drop in the bucket. However, Modern continues to evolve as sets are released and continues to prove the endless depth of the greatest game ever conceived. Today, we'll be taking a look at recently printed cards that have made the jump from Standard to Modern and discussing their applications therein.


*Modern Masters 2017 Edition* looks like it may be the most powerful set ever released. There's no doubt that we'll all be spending a lot of time cracking packs and drafting in the coming weeks. Some of us may not have a Modern deck yet and *Modern Masters*, while providing a lot of powerful cards for our decks, will only get us so far in crafting our own Modern masterpiece. Luckily, a lot of cards that we have from Standard (and some that have recently rotated) are currently making waves in Modern.


Build Arounds
=============


Let's start by looking at more recent cards that we can build our deck around.


**Sram, Senior Edificer**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sram%2C+Senior+Edificer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sram%2C+Senior+Edificer)
[Sram, Senior Edificer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sram%2C+Senior+Edificer) and [Puresteel Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puresteel+Paladin) have joined forces to make a combo deck that draws through scary numbers of cards by playing free Equipment, then bounces all those cards back to their hand with Retract, then plays and draws even more cards before eventually finishing the game with a lethal storm spell.


People tried to play [Puresteel Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puresteel+Paladin) combo decks in the past, but the redundancy we get from [Sram, Senior Edificer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sram%2C+Senior+Edificer) makes the deck a lot more consistent than ever before.


The deck is fragile, and being unable to protect or find either [Puresteel Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puresteel+Paladin) or [Sram, Senior Edificer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sram%2C+Senior+Edificer) can pose some real problems. Of course, these are things we have to live with if we want to be winning on the second turn of the game with scary amounts of consistency.


Below is a version of the deck that Caleb Durward went undefeated with in a *Magic Online* League.

**Baral, Chief of Compliance**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Baral%2C+Chief+of+Compliance)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baral%2C+Chief+of+Compliance)
[Baral, Chief of Compliance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baral%2C+Chief+of+Compliance) combines with [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) to make traditional Modern Storm into a super-powerful version of its previous self. Like Sram, Baral creates redundancy and consistency that the storm deck never had before.


Having access to seven or eight cards that reduce the cost of spells allows Storm players to reliably play [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), which often feels like four [Demonic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Tutor)s when you're playing this deck. With [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), the deck no longer needs to lean on [Pyromancer Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer+Ascension) when it's trying to set up a combo finish.


Reducing the cost of spells transforms unthreatening cards like [Pyretic Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual) or [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) into absurdly powerful cards that don't feel like they belong in the format.

**Expertise Cards**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kari+Zev%27s+Expertise)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kari+Zev%27s+Expertise)Â [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sram%27s+Expertise)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sram%27s+Expertise)
Both [Kari Zev's Expertise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kari+Zev%27s+Expertise) and [Sram's Expertise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sram%27s+Expertise) are being used alongside [Beck & Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck+%2F%2F+Call) and [Breaking & Entering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breaking+%2F%2F+Entering) to do very unfair things in Modern. The deck usually aims to reanimate [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) or [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) as early as the second turn as its primary means of victory. Unfortunately, no versions of the deck have had wide tournament success just yet. However, the ability to cast very big spells for very small amounts of mana is something that can't be overlooked when constructing the Modern deck for you.


**Eldrazi and Walking Ballista**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Walking+Ballista)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Endbringer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endbringer)
One of the biggest developments in Modern recently has been the new versions of Eldrazi Tron. Todd Stevens brought the deck to a tournament and went an astounding 17-1 with his list.


The deck uses [Urza's Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower), [Urza's Power Plant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant), and [Urza's Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine) alongside [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) and completely eschews the ability to make colored mana at all. More recently released cards like [Endbringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endbringer), [Matter Reshaper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Matter+Reshaper), [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer), and [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) all make an appearance here. [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) and [Endbringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endbringer) get very exciting when combined with [Basilisk Collar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar), making any sort of creature matchup into a cakewalk by giving them deathtouch so they can demolish the other side of the table.

Cards that give us access to toolboxes
======================================


Modern is a format where we can find ourselves in a lot of different situations. Some new cards allow us to build our Modern deck so we can play few copies of situational cards that can be found when we need them.


**Traverse the Ulvenwald**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald)
[Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) has been a major player in Standard since it was printed. Now, the card has become the cornerstone of strategy in Modern's current top dog, [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow). The deck demolished [Grand Prix Vancouver](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpvan17), putting three players into the Top 8 and eventually securing Josh Utter-Leyton another trophy.


It's easy to quickly turn on delirium in Modern and, in doing so, we end up with some pretty gigantic [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s. With [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald), we can find a bunch of [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s when it's huge, just grab some more [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s when we're trading cards with the opponent, cantrip and make our [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) bigger with [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith), and set up a finishing blow, often comboing alongside [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage) with [Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager).


If you're looking to play the "best" deck in Modern, then this is probably at the top of the list right now. It's hard to argue with results, and this deck's are absurd.

**Collected Company**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Collected+Company)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company)
[Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) has proven itself as the heir to [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)'s throne in terms of creature combo. [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) allows Modern players to find all the pieces to creature combos while also finding specific cards that have the ability to dominate certain matchups.


Oftentimes, [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) is used to set up [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) by finding two-thirds or half of a creature combo before tapping the found creatures to find the finishing touch.


The ability to find sideboard cards or main deck inclusions like [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze), [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), or [Renegade Rallier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Rallier) when they're necessary for a specific game situation has really pushed [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) to the point of being a Modern staple.


We can expect various [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) decks to always be competitive in Modern. Here's a current version of the deck that made the Top 8 of Grand Prix Vancouver in the hands of Eric Severson. It includes the [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune)/[Spike Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) combo (Gain infinite life and put infinite +1/+1 counters on our creatures by repeatedly activating the [Spike Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder)'s ability and letting it and the [Archangel of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+of+Thune) trigger resolve) alongside the more traditional persist combo. (Use [Anafenza, Kin-Tree Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit) or [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) with [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) to gain infinite life and set up our draw step by repeatedly sacrificing the [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks).)

Powerful Upgrades
=================


Some of the cards we're seeing a lot of from recent sets are simply powerful and synergistic enough to find their way into some of Modern's most effective strategies.


**Jace, Vryn's Prodigy**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace%2C+Telepath+Unbound)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Telepath+Unbound)Transform
Two-mana planeswalkers are very scary, and [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy), while not currently the toast of the town, is sure to be a part of many powerful strategies going forward. It's hard to argue with the sheer power-to-mana ratio here, and there's enough to do with our graveyards in Modern to make this into a long-term staple of the format.


**Renegade Rallier**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Renegade+Rallier)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Rallier)
This card from *Aether Revolt* is already doing a lot of exciting things in both aggressive and midrange Modern decks. The card is most commonly being used in [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) decks, but we're also seeing it in other midrange decks and in some aggressive strategies.


**Reckless Bushwhacker**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Reckless+Bushwhacker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Bushwhacker)
Aggressive creature decks that aim to flood the board with inexpensive creatures have welcomed the addition of Reckless Bushwacker. The card deals absurd amounts of damage for a relatively inexpensive cost. The card is at its best when played alongside [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary). In fact, I found myself dead on turn three the other night when my opponent cast [Renegade Rallier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renegade+Rallier) resurrecting [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary), thus making mana to surge Reckless Bushwacker and attack me for some ungodly amount of damage.


I'm a sucker for a weird aggro deck, and this Human tribal deck from Zaelphur that went undefeated in a *Magic Online* League looks like the perfect place for [Reckless Bushwhacker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Bushwhacker) to serve as the finisher.

**Kolaghan's Command**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kolaghan%27s+Command)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command)
[Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) saw a bit of Standard play, but the card really shines in Modern for a number of reasons. It's much more likely that we'll be able to kill an artifact. Modern tends to be a format where people empty their hands quickly at times; in these situations, [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) being used to make someone discard during their draw step can often be the difference between victory and defeat. [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) also offers up card advantage without sacrificing tempo, and that's exactly what we want to be doing if we're trying to win the card war in Modern. The best deck that currently utilizes [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command) is likely the [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) deck that won Grand Prix Vancouver.


**Thing in the Ice**


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thing+in+the+Ice)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thing+in+the+Ice)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Awoken+Horror)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awoken+Horror)Transform
[Thing in the Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thing+in+the+Ice) has allowed a fringe strategy like prowess to enter the competitive ranks of Modern play. The card performs so well here that I can imagine it finding its way into a lot of other Modern decks in the future. The Horror is very easy to flip in Modern, and that's a whole lot of card for just two mana.
