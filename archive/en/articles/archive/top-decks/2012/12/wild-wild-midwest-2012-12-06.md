Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic."

 The winning deck from the event does multiple things well. It features one of the most hard-to-handle threats in the format ([Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)) with one of its most no-nonsense big bruisers ([Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite)). Hipp's deck shows us a mix of different kinds of interactive cards, from bomb-topdecks like [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) and a very different kind of X-spell in [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) to four different kinds of main-deck permission spells across [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate), [Essence Scatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm), and [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td224_interactive.jpg)  
 Beyond that, it has tons and tons of ways—different ways—to interact with creature cards; [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) and [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) are both very good against the red-hot [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger). [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) is a great anticipator of [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider)s. [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) and [Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) can take the hasty oomph out of even a [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) while you set up to do something else (like, in this deck, race with your own [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite)). 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Detention Sphere | Azorius Charm |

 The champ's deck can obviously put pressure on the opponent offensively, challenge and outclass defenses with its [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) big game, then finish games from a distance with its burn suite. Unlike single-minded offensive decks, Hipp's can refill or rebuild with [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), deal with mobs of creatures with [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), or even put control opponents into a real puzzler via [Curse of Echoes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Echoes). 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Thundermaw Hellkite | Curse of Echoes |

 Imagine you are a control player and you have just had the misfortune of being Cursed in this way... What exactly are you supposed to do? Say your deck is meant to run on a sequence of [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)s, or can't really get through the game without a flurry of [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm)s and both sides of a couple of [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice)s. Your whole goal in life is running through your deck to find the right cards or to achieve victory, eventually, by having a massive ton of resource advantage... more cards in hand leading to more lands in play leading to more mischief, various. 

What exactly are you supposed to do once Cursed?


*Really hope you have some way to get out from under the Curse,* I'd wager.

Bauer played a similar-strategy deck, but with some key innovations that you might not have seen before (at least not in the Red-White-Blue Midrange archetype):



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Spectral Flight | Burning Oil |


* [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight)—It's hard to think of something scarier than a *regular*
[Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)... but how about a 4/4 Geist you can't block? [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) is a livable card only because—as awesome as it is—you can in theory put half a [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) or the 2/2 molting on a [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger)'s land-search in front of it and take only 4 damage. But make it a 4/4 flier? Not only is the Geist now much harder to block, but blocking it with 2/2 creatures won't even kill it anymore! It gets pulled out from under possible death vial [Rolling Temblor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) two different ways! And subtly, unlike with almost any other creature-to-creature enchantment pairing... it is very difficult to kill a Geist in response, so you aren't even exposed to a potential two-for-one (at least most of the time).
* [Burning Oil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Oil)—I've thought about playing this card more than once; certainly if [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) can be upgraded to playability (primarily to fight [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider)s) then a "card-advantage [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear)" can be considered? The major issue for me is that hitting the first haste 3/3 is going to be all well and good (except that unlike with a regular [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) you will actually have to take however many [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) triggers on the jaw), but it will be trivial for the opponent to play around the flashback. On the other hand, this card has an undeniable synergy with Bauer's four-of [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s... you can dump [Burning Oil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Oil) as a surprise and kill a creature purely on the card advantage if you have five mana.

Gerry Thompson has quietly put together a superb run in recent weeks. One of the original best grinders of the StarCity Open Series, Gerry's previous two weeks saw him posting superb finishes in [consecutive](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/grand-prix-charleston-round-15-standings-2012-11-18) Standard Grand Prix [events](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/grand-prix-san-antonio-round-15-standings-2012-11-25), and he has continued the Standard streak at the TCGPlayer Invitational.

 Although also red-white-blue, and also (at least somewhat arguably) "midrange," Gerry's deck from this event is really more of a White-Blue Flash deck touching for [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) than it is similar to the previous two performers. 

 His creature base is all value enters-the-battlefields. [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) is going to "hit" the majority of the time given twenty-two available playmates main, and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) is close to being at its best in a deck with so many [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s, [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame)s, and other such super-cheap cantrips and answers. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Augur of Bolas | Snapcaster Mage |

 This deck can put up an iron wall of counterspell defense against an opponent that will give it time to make its land drops or go on a machine-gun murdering spree of completely dead [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)s as it dances between Pillars, Augurs-for-Pillars, and Tiago-flashed-back-Pillars. Like a traditional White-Blue Control deck, Gerry's red-splasher can reset the board with [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), then bury an opponent in (further) card advantage via [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). Trying to play a "fair" or progressive card economy game with it is fairly pointless. I would differentiate this from the potential flexibility of the above Red-White-Blue Midrange decks as not so much flexibility, but the ability to actually specialize in different lines of focused redundancy, actually molding distinct superior game plans depending on how the opponent is trying to win. 

![Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Supreme+Verdict)  
Gerry's ability to perform at a super high level three weeks in a row draws heavily from picking a strategy that allowed him both familiarity and the ability to contend against different kinds of opponents in a shifting field.


[Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) is a big game that doubles up his other enters-the-battlefields, and Runechanter's Pile can be played as a one-shot. It is like the [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) he has to find... eventually. Thompson could pick a spot and kill the opponent from double digits (there can be quite a few instants and sorceries in the graveyard, remember). Unlike most decks of this color combination, Gerry played [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) not at all, preferring to go over the top with [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) rather than trying to grind down the opponent's resources and poke for 1 every so often.

 Medanic played a substantially different Bant deck from the one we recently saw Reid Duke pilot to [back](/en/events/coverage/bolding-thunders-victory-charleston)-to-[back](/en/events/coverage/lytles-zombies-big-texas) Grand Prix Top 8s. This deck is built on a more traditional deployment of permanents, although it does include the one [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) and the one [Overgrown Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb) as an inevitability combo. 


[Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) does work with [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) here and can get the odd grimace of exasperation out of an [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) combo, as well. [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) does what it can do in reanimator strategies... it just has to be cast fair-and-square (seven is not "too much" for a lady of her quality, she would have you know).

[Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender) is a card several top players have been discussing, and it can either buy a lot of time against, or undo the card usage of, many sorts of aggressive decks if left on the battlefield. [Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender) by itself can play the Little Engine That Could, kind of erasing a [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) every tussle... but combined with [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)? You need to get in with [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) *twice* just to get back to even!

 Werner performed with a slightly less straightforward build of Junk Reanimator. He still ran one [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) main and one [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) in the sideboard, but this deck has less "fair fight" than we typically see in the color combination. 

 Werner can still start on [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), but he doesn't actually have a [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) or a [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) for turn two. With only one [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), his ability to unlock the [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) achievement is less frequent (if still there). I do like the one [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons) for the "fight fair" portion of the deck.

 On the other side, those little 1/1 accelerators work very nicely with Werner's abbreviated [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) theme. He can still [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) and [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) into [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), and hopefully the [5/5 Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=5%2F5+Overrun)... at which point the Elves and Pilgrims and 1/1 Spirits all get pretty exciting. 

 I would put this still on very much an "[Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites)" deck with an eight-pack of graveyard-filling sorceries and the full complement of Rites, although it is no "Craterhoof Combo"—despite having the potential combo-kill capability. More of a tweener.

 The Four-Color Reanimator deck generally differs from the Junk cousin in its ability to play [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting). [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) can help set up many of the [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) big game strategies starting on the *first* turn, and the flashback cost is even set up to come in right before you hit four mana for a cheap-side [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). 

 Fulop's deck takes advantage of the slightly greater amount of into-the-graveyard oomph with four [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity), although he plays "only" two [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)s as well.

 That said, red gives Chris some nice options, including [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), for his fair-side curve (what Zombies player is going to want to face [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) into [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) into [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) into [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)?)... as well as [Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) out of the side. [Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) is gaining respect with its ability to exploit the information revealed by [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and take the considerable gas out of [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)'s somewhat ponderous tank. It can also be quite useful against an opposing Reanimator deck; both when you think you'll have the time to spring it before you get walloped and in games where you have no intention of winning a progressive card-advantage fair fight. Why fight that fight at all?

[Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) is great, and in particular great against ultimate Planeswalkers, especially when powered out by [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls). But as with a certain black-red build, Chris can pair [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) with [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) for additional shenanigans. 


[Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) certainly has a lot to shoot at in Standard. In this article alone we have catalogued viable decks playing [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat), [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), and more! [Izzet Staticaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) is like a flashy, hasty [Royal Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin) in the right room. 

 But with [Nightshade Peddler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler)? 

 The two-card combination is probably obvious. The Izzet side can pop out at any moment, giving you a free hit or two in case the opponent exposes a creature he or she might not otherwise. [Evil Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin) is devilishly good sometimes, but it is tough to find a more malicious murderer of [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s than this combo. 

 Standard has become overrun with tough haste creatures. Black mages have re-thought their [Dreadbore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore)s for [Victim of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) and red mages have reincorporated [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) for [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider)s. The Peddler-pinger twosome doesn't care if you're a Devil or a Dragon. When it comes to murder, it's always appropriate.
