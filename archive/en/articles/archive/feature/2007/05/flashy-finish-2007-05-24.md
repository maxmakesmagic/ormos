Flashy Finish
=============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on May 24, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






I must say that I was surprised by the lack of Hulk Flash decks in the Top 8 of [Grand Prix–Columbus 2007](/en/events/coverage/hulk-flash-smashes-columbus). I know that there were three copies, more than any other archetype (there were five singletons), but I thought that if the metagame made any sense that there would be eight [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) decks in the Top 8—It's *that* much better than every other deck in the format. Alas, not enough players played [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) decks, and some spiteful young men played decks that could beat [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash), and the archetype that may never see the light of another DCI sanctioned tournament will have to settle for the best Swiss record and the Grand Prix win.

Perhaps I am getting a little ahead of myself.

*What is Hulk Flash?*

![Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flash)In the summer of 1999, there was a new set coming out called *Urza's Destiny*, the third and arguably most powerful member of Urza Block. Urza Block featured [Time Spiral](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Spiral) and [Windfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windfall) in *Urza's Saga*, [Memory Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Jar) and [Frantic Search](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frantic+Search) in *Urza's Legacy*, and was gearing up for [Replenish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Replenish), [Yawgmoth's Bargain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Bargain), and [Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector) in the final installment. [Yawgmoth's Bargain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Bargain) was really strong—essentially a game win when it resolved—and [Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector) was two mana less expensive (and cards that cost four are supposed to win the game anyway), but at least it was four. [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) was two.You could [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) out [Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector) for two mana, deploy [Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector), find [Yawgmoth's Bargain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Bargain), and that was that. Kill however you would—[Skirge Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirge+Familiar)s, [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault)s, even [Scent of Jasmine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scent+of+Jasmine) engines—if you can draw 19 or so more cards than your opponent and not win, you might as well hang up the wand.

The powers that be reached for their giant red Sharpie, and [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) went from an [Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector)'s best friend (that never was) to *merely* an at-end-of-turn [Morphling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morphling) enabler, a dormant minority option unheard of from U.S. Nationals 1999 until 2007... Three Bargain decks and a [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) deck all managed to make the Nationals Top 8 despite the errata.

The recent *reversals* of power level errata returned [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) to the vigor that never was. Conveniently, a Legacy Grand Prix gave it the stage it never had at the end of the last century.

With no [Yawgmoth's Bargain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%27s+Bargain) in sight or in format, [Academy Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector) was no longer the most broken card for [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) to facilitate; the choice became [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk). Counting the Ravager itself, that's just about a kill.

The kill engine could be refined. [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) was a liability. [Engineered Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague)s that people would already have for Goblin decks could be set to target [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). Drawing too many Disciples would make it impossible to combo-kill.

[Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) (all of them) could be combined with [Shifting Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shifting+Wall) and [Phyrexian Marauder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Marauder). A stack of 0/0 creatures could go to the graveyard simultaneously off the [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) (none of them taxing the Hulk's six), for a more robust finish.

Eventually, the preferred kill switched away from [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) to a more compact—but also more vulnerable—germ. While there remain any number of Hulk creature kills, all three decks in the Top 8 chose the same style.

[Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) (1)  
[Karmic Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karmic+Guide) (6)

![Carrion Feeder and Karmic Guide](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/swimming/mf147_cardfan.jpg)[Karmic Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karmic+Guide) comes into play and returns [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk). [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) sacrifices the Hulk, and it's time to go fishing again. How about [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) (5) (and maybe some one mana friend like [Sylvan Safekeeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Safekeeper) or [Benevolent Bodyguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benevolent+Bodyguard) (6))? Kiki-Jiki copies [Karmic Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karmic+Guide). In response, [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) eats Kiki-Jiki. [Karmic Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karmic+Guide) brings back Kiki-Jiki, who commences to re-copy [Karmic Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karmic+Guide). A million loops later, and infinite hasted [Karmic Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karmic+Guide)s attack for the win. 

Hulk [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) is exceedingly fast... The only card it has to play is the two-mana [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash). Versions with [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) can win on the first turn, and versions with [Gemstone Caverns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemstone+Caverns) and [Elvish Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Elvish%2BSpirit%2BGuide) or [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide)s can win on the opponent's upkeep, before even taking a turn! My guess is that the card [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) will be banned in Legacy, not just because of its performance in Columbus, but because of the *Future Sight* additions that were not yet legal. [Summoner's Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoner%27s+Pact) makes the deck twice as consistent, making sure that [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) is in hand next to [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) on the big turn, and [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) supplements [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), and [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)... and is probably even better than any of them in the fastest games.

Steve is one of my best friends and was my teammate at Pro Tour–Charleston last year. I couldn't be happier for him this time. He played a version of Hulk Flash primarily designed by Billy Moreno that hybridized [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) with [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) / Top. 

Steve's version of the deck really showcases the difference between a good [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) deck and a poor one. His deck has a massive edge in the mirror with [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), and [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), and unlike most [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) decks that are dogs to Fish, his build manhandles aggro-control decks with its wide variety of superior spells.

I am not usually the biggest fan of [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) (I think it is very good but generally overplayed), but Brian David-Marshall has forced me to concede that [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Dark%2BConfidant) can really seem unstoppable when paired with [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top). In this deck, Confidant can headline a legitimate creature offense should the deck be shut down by [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) or [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle).

Besides "just" being an absurdly fast and consistent two card combo deck that kills at two paltry mana, [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) Hulk has the pedigree of playing the best cards in the format: [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)... [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)... potentially [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)... [Mystical Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Tutor)... In Steve's version, [Mystical Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Tutor) can either find [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) for the quick kill, get a permission card to protect a kill he already has, put a correctly costed spell on top to foil the opponent with [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), or go Napster-style for the [Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth), [Reverent Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reverent+Silence), or [Massacre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre). There are a couple of cards that can shut down the [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) kill, including [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) (naming [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) or [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder)), [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) (which can void graveyard strategies), or [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) (which prevents the card [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) itself from being played). [Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) can answer any of these, and [Massacre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre) can kill Chris Pikula for free (along with most other Fish creatures that happen to also be in play). 

Like I said, Steve is a good friend, and I got the chance to speak with him about his deck and his win while preparing this article.

### Steve SadinThe Top 8 Questions for the Top8Magic.com Intern:

1. You told me before the finals that Goblins was a bad matchup, but you still won... What gives?

*Goblins is probably one of my worst matchups (among decks that actually showed up), but it's still favorable. When I said that I think I was a little shaken because both my record blemishes were to Goblins. I actually lost to Owen* [Turtenwald] *at 4-0 because he made me combo off. I had beaten three Fish decks and just showed them the pieces, but I didn't know how to combo off and I screwed up by sacrificing the wrong creature. Against another Goblins deck my opponent used [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) on my [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) and I didn't know how to win. I probably should have been able to win somehow with [Body Snatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Snatcher) but I was really tired and decided to just get another [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk) and try to kill him that way. I got a draw because he had to spend the whole round blocking my [Protean Hulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Protean+Hulk)!*

*Otherwise, I was just so advantaged. All my other matches (except Gadiel* [Szleifer] *conceding to me) were blowouts. I had the* two *best combos in the format. [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) is faster than the other fast decks, and I could slow down the slow decks with [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) / Top. I actually think that I would have had trouble with Landstill, but that wasn't a real deck in this tournament.*

2. One of the decks that came gunning for Hulk Flash was Fish...

*My deck absolutely could not lose to Fish. I think that that is one of the reasons it is so good. Their clock is embarrassingly slow. I can assemble [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) / Top and have plenty of time to win in the first game. After sideboarding, they have to bring in Leylines and [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage)s to keep me honest, and I bring in [Massacre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre)s to kill their [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage)s. Eventually I get there with a 2/2 or 3/3 [Quirion Dryad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad).*

3. You had some real nail biters in the elimination rounds. Is it true you won on a mulligan to four?

![Lim-Dul's Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lim-Dul%27s+Vault)*Yeah. I mulled to a Leyline, two lands and some spell. I pre-played the Leyline but he [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ed my spell (I don't remember what it was) and I was pretty sure my two lands weren't going to do anything. I ripped two Bobs, but they didn't really do anything either but find one [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze). He set up his whole game with [Lim-Dûl's Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lim-D%C3%BBl%27s+Vault) and [Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth)ed my [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) at the end of the turn. I [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze)d and he had to crack his [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) to pay... which ruined his [Lim-Dûl's Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lim-D%C3%BBl%27s+Vault). If he had just sacrificed his [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) before the Vault,* he *probably would have been playing in the Finals.*4. So Goblins wasn't as bad as you thought it would be... How did your Finals match turn out?

*The first game was a little tricky because I kept a no-lander on six, having drawn the [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder). I had a Mox and a Top, and was able to get him before he had access to his sideboard cards... But it was tricky because I had to go off over two turns. I was a little nervous because Owen was my only loss, but like I said, I punted that first one.*

*I just combo'd him on the second turn with double Force of Will backup in the third. This deck is just unbeatable.*

5. So is it your conclusion that [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) will have to be banned?

*Oh, definitely. Even without* Future Sight *it is too good. People who thought anything else before this tournament had just not seen tuned [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) decks. If you re-ran Columbus 20 times, Gadiel and I would have 12 Top 8s each, Billy would have 10, and Julian would have 2-4.* 

6. You think Gadiel's deck is as good as yours?

*For the tournament, my deck was better, but Gadiel's deck is better with the knowledge that people have today. We were playing and I had no chance against his 4 [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), 4 [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), 4 [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), and 4 [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). It's not like a bad Fish deck that has disruption but is slow... Gadiel has almost all of the Fish package and can still just kill me with two mana. He also transforms better than I do with [Phyrexian Negator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Negator) in addition to [Quirion Dryad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad). I was really relieved when Max beat him.*

7. What happened with Billy and Julian? (Billy Moreno was the primary designer of the deck, which was also played by Steve's fellow Top8Magic.com intern Julian Levin.)

*Billy actually made Day Two. Julian was 6-1 and had some bad luck the last two rounds. He played against Landstill, which I said was probably a bad matchup, and got killed on turn two by another [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) deck. His other loss was to Suicide Black, which is actually a deck. I didn't think it was that hard to beat when I played Bill Stark in the semis, but I had to play really tight. It was actually all [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) tricks to keep counters off the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte). Actually almost all my wins in the Top 8 were based on playing well with [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder).*

8. Anything else you want to share?

*Between my win and Matt's* [fellow Top8Magic.com personality Matt Wang] *in Boston, I have come to the conclusion that all you need to do to win a GP is donk it up and make one or two good attacks. :-)*

What he *did* have were four [Tormod's Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt)s (which can disrupt a graveyard combination, albeit not as effectively as [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void), depending on the opponent's version) and four Pyrokinesis (which could kill [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder) or Kiki-Jiki even if he was tapped out).

Bill's deck combines a relatively quick beatdown and a massive amount of disruption: [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), [Hymn to Tourach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach), [Unmask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unmask), and [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). After sideboarding, Bill could add [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void). Against Goblins, he could run [Engineered Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague) on the first turn with [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual), but I can't imagine that is the matchup the Londes.com editor was looking for.

Players were calling decks like Max's "Fish," but it is actually more reminiscent of Ben Rubin's Dump Truck than the combo killer Nicholas Lebarre took to the Pro Tour–Rome Finals (where he nevertheless lost to the title to 800 pound gorilla Academy). Max's deck combines some of the format's best cards in a package that was billed to beat Hulk Flash; [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage), [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares), [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle), and [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), combined with a reasonable clock, are all strong disruptive elements. Like Bill, Max could sideboard in [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) for a quick and highly effective measure against the core Hulk Flash combination.

Michael's deck was somewhat reminiscent of Chris Pikula's Deadguy Ale from last year's Legacy Grand Prix... Black creatures with disruption and removal, augmented by the color's mortal opposite. There are two things worth noting about this build: 1) [Plague Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Sliver), and 2) [Contagion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion). Mike Pustilnik actually tested out [Juzám Djinn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juz%C3%A1m+Djinn) last year, and [Plague Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Sliver) is better. Why? Some people go Slivers, and [Plague Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Sliver) can piggyback their abilities while punishing their masters with his. [Contagion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion), like [Pyrokinesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrokinesis), is a free spell that could disrupt the most common [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash) combo, even when Michael was tapped out.

I am not sure that the variety of decks in the Columbus elimination rounds will be enough to stave off [Flash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flash)'s predicted banning come the next format review. Hulk Flash put three times as many decks in the quarters as any other archetype, and two of them were forced to play one another in the first round of single elimination. Hulk Flash led the Swiss and ultimately won the tournament... And, provided the engine remained untouched, *Future Sight* would make the deck even better. 

On balance, the Eternal formats are meant to be more degenerate and fundamentally unfair than Block, Standard, or other more commonly played formats, and whether or not the card is banned *immediately* will have little effect on high-level competition (Legacy is played only once or twice per year). It will be interesting to see what the **Magic** team will decide on this one.
