Watch That Restoration Angel!
=============================



 Posted in [Top Decks](/en/articles/columns/top-decks-archive)
 on August 15, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 





*A not-exhaustive look at two formats.*


Here at Top Decks, we spend a lot of time talking about the Standard format. Heck, last week we talked about circa *sixteen* Standard formats (or at least some of their bad guys and bogeymen)!


In light of this weekend's upcoming [World *Magic* Cup](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup)—which in addition to Standard embraces the formats of Modern and *Innistrad*-*Avacyn Restored* Block Constructed—I thought we'd touch on some of those other formats for a little World *Magic* Cup sneak preview action.

Modern was—and is—a big idea and a reasonably big format; broader than Extended, but pruned ahead of time to defend the fun factor. No [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor); no [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic). Well, Estratti's Exarch Twin, too, has been similarly de-toothed since the Philadelphia win. Now gone are his one-drop consistency-makers, [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)... setting up [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) + [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) or [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) + [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) (either combo would give Estratti an engine for essentially limitless power by untapping either a [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin)-wearing blue guy or the machine gun legendary Goblin) has become a little less effortless.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pestermite)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker)

The Modern hybrid [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) strategy is unambiguously potent; playing against it is reminiscent of fighting LSV-era full-on Elves Combo with [Glimpse of Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+of+Nature). The deck is a creature deck. It can go [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) into second-turn [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) and beat you up (certainly, other builds actually have the full four [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Noble Hierarch)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble Hierarch) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kitchen Finks)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen Finks)
Or it can go [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and then chain up... [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) (draw a card), [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) (leave a body), [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) (reset, etc.).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Birds of Paradise)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds of Paradise) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Birthing Pod)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing Pod)
[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wall of Omens)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall of Omens) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Restoration Angel)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration Angel)
...but like Estratti's deck, this one can win (typically wins?) on an unlimited combo. Four copies of [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker); four copies of [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel); a [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) for good measure. You can aim [Kiki-Jiki](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki) at [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), blink the Legendary Goblin, do it again... over and over... literally *ad infinitum*. Send as many 3/4 creatures as you like.


To say that Modern is a "diverse" format might be a bit of an understatement. It has the twin assets of being huge and not-particularly-developed (no StarCityGames Open Series grinding it to crystal distillation, weekly and on camera). In addition to two-card infinite combo decks and the [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)/creature-based take on the same, it is a format full of Blue-Red Urzatron decks, White-Blue Urzatron decks, Red-Green Urzatron decks... and regular-old "good stuff" decks.

This All-American beatdown deck in Modern is basically a Standard Delver deck... but with awesome burn spells like [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)! This deck is the—*ahem*—"modern" realization of the "critical mass of great beatdown creatures" and "critical mass of great burn spells" we have seen in select formats passed. [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is an all-star in Legacy as well as Standard, so acquits itself swimmingly in Modern. Joined at the one by high-reward fast attacker [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx), this deck can hammer an opponent with [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). It can also circumvent the red zone with its high-value red spells [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)... and do it all over again with [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lightning Helix)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning Helix) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Steppe Lynx)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe Lynx)
Now, speaking of [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)—and speaking of Legacy—the topic probably inevitably drifts to Legacy format powerhouse RUG Delver. The color combination's signature mix of [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) is alive, well, quite viable, etc., in Modern, as evidenced by this Grand Prix finalist deck, care of Lucas Siow:

The above is a finals deck from this past May, and might be of particular interest as Siow is one of the World *Magic* Cup participants (for Canada). This deck in particular is a different take on the red-white-blue concept, with [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) as the undercosted heavy-hitter, and a bit of a more mana-intensive mid- and late-game.


Lucas played with recurring threat-answer [Vedalken Shackles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) (not particularly mana intensive, but requiring a fair number of [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s in play to do much going long) and [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) (which costs four mana).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Vedalken Shackles)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken Shackles) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cryptic Command)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic Command)
Both Lucas's Delver deck and the All-American beatdown play cards like [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) where Standard aficionados might expect a [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)... not just to help dig for lands early (and regulate draws overall)... but to help flip up [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) where a moment ago we had the regular old [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). These decks explode in three different directions on a Modern battlefield: They can play the aggressor, close like burn decks (or push supposed blockers out of the way), or slow down/disrupt/out-race combo decks with their light suite of counterspells.

You might have noticed that there isn't a whole lot of "affinity" in this Affinity deck—no [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast); no [Frogmite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) even. ### Arcbound Ravager and Cranial Plating


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Arcbound Ravager)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound Ravager) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cranial Plating)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial Plating)
[Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) can lend its artifact-impact to any artifact attacker, dodging hate, removal spells, and even blocks.


[Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) does the same (especially when combined with an evasive creature)... but does not require the Ravager's explosive commitment to finality. [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) + [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) is actually just game in two swings.


### Galvanic Blast and Shrapnel Blast


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Galvanic Blast)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic Blast) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shrapnel Blast)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel Blast)
*Boom.*


Remember the big [Galvanic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) finish, when [Brian Kibler surprised Jon Finkel with multiple shots for four](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/sf_2)? Part of the surprise was that a "regular" deck with just [Sphere of the Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) and [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) (conditional) don't really make for predictable metalcraft burn... but Modern Affinity basically always has metalcraft. The "Blast Brothers" always hit for 4–5, meaning you don't need to deal that much damage before you pull the opponent into range.


As I said in the subhead, this article makes no attempt at being an exhaustive rundown of every Modern (or *Innistrad*-*Avacyn Restored*) decklist, rather a peek at some of the decks that have been successful recently or in defining fashion (especially when played by a World *Magic* Cup participant). Modern is home to a lot of different decks! We could spend all day talking about turn-three [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) or the many flavors of Blue-Red Combo... but we probably couldn't hit every viable archetype even if we tried. I figure now is as good a time as any to move to the other format...


### *Innistrad-Avacyn Restored* Block Constructed


*Innistrad-Avacyn Restored* Block Constructed is kind of the opposite of Modern. The anti-Modern. Rather than a huge format of many different archetypes, it is confined to just three sets—*Innistrad*, *Dark Ascension*, and *Avacyn Restored*—and is therefore necessarily constrained to a smaller number of available cards and strategies. He's got [Rolling Temblor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) and [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) non-miracle answers to defend himself early on (before his miracles took over) and [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) as an additional miracle bomb.


Both [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) and [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) offer opportunities to draw a card on the opponent's turn, setting up more miracles opportunities. At least in Hayne's build, [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) can be used in combination with [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to set up and re-buy key spells. Remember: You don't *have* to [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) yourself! You can [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) the opponent if you think your top cards are so valuable... or if the opponent gives you reason to do other (say he or she reveals a game-breaking [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) with a [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) on upkeep), you can go ahead and ruin that plan.

Zac Hill [recently referenced](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ld/207) Sam Black's StarCityGames Black [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) deck as R&D's concept of a combo deck. This deck has some hard-to-hit guys in [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and the aforementioned [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker)... and kind of jams stuff on top of them. An [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) with [Increasing Savagery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Savagery) is a fast clock, and a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) wearing [Spectral Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) shirks that offensive wondercard's sole weakness: actually getting blocked.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Geist of Saint Traft)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist of Saint Traft) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spectral Flight)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral Flight)
The [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) deck is less a legitimate creature deck than some other Block options (and lacks the topdeck possibility of [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)), constricting its options to just moving forward; but there is no question that the opponent is put on a clock.


### Boros/Gruul/Naya/Jund/*et cetera*


The intuitive two- and three-color beatdown decks are all present and viable attack options in *Innistrad-Avacyn Restored* Block. They tend to have similar strategies (efficient creatures opening up the attack, finishing on red cards).

This is a deck of heavy fists. [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) does work, but not as much damage as a hasty [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) or [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts). Conscripts, by the by, doesn't just hit for 3, doesn't just steal the opponent's best creature... it can also steal an ultimate (to be) Planeswalker a turn before it is about to go off.


Kibler's deck has all manner of bombs. In addition to his heavy-fisted creatures, Brian played [Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) as a small-creature dominator as well as [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned). Because, hey... when you one-way [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) the opponent, you also want to clear off that pesky life total.
