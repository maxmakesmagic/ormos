Round 1: John Larkin vs. Jeff Cunningham
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 17, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











Jeff Cunningham, father of [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) in the Extended format, is a beatdown mage if there ever was one. His deck of choice for this event, red/green beatdown, is similar to the one he played at Canadian Nationals: [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel)s, madness, and tons of burn.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a294.jpg)Opposite him in this, the opening round of the Standard Masters, is Ireland's top player, three-time PT Top 8'er John Larkin. Larkin is playing the [Mirari's Wake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari%27s+Wake) combo deck that came up at the end of the *Odyssey* Block constructed format. The deck generates tons of mana and card advantage, eventually winning via decking with [Krosan Reclamation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Reclamation) or damage with a recurred [Elephant Ambush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Ambush).

### Game 1

Cunningham came out blazing with [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel), [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd), and [Elephant Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) on the token in the first four turns. Larkin was holding multiple copies of [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) but couldn't find another source of white mana to go with his [Brushland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brushland). He had to tap out on turn 4 to cast [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), and Cunningham took advantage of the situation by attacking him down to six (pitching some spare lands), and then hitting him with a [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) down to 3.

Larkin didn't find white mana, but he did get [Moment's Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace). Unfortunately, Cunningham had a [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer) that Larkin could do nothing about. Two activations later, the Canadian was up a game.

Cunningham 1 – Larkin 0

### Game 2

Cunningham had no early creatures in Game 2, just burn. He [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt)ed and [Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer)ed Larkin. He finally drew green mana and played a [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), which was countered. His second Rootwalla stuck, however, but Larkin was setting up. He had out a [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) and Wished for a [Chastise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chastise) to deal with the Rootwalla. Cunningham couldn't get anything else on the table.

Larkin eventually dropped [Mirari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari) and Wished for [Opportunity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opportunity). When Cunningham realized that he probably wasn't going to have another spell resolve, he scooped up his cards.

Cunningham 1 – Larkin 1

Cunningham had to mulligan to start the deciding game, but had a nice start of [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), and [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd). Larkin took the beats and eventually [Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath)ed at 9 life.

Unfortunately, the Irishman was stuck on 4 mana and had to discard a [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) on his next turn. Cunningham had flash backed his [Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call) and was content to have the 3/3 token do all the work.

Larkin was looking to stay alive with [Moment's Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace), but Cunningham had [Flaring Pain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flaring+Pain) to stop one of them. Larkin stayed alive—barely—by cycling a [Renewed Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renewed+Faith) and Wishing for Peace (something we should all do), but Cunningham just kept on attacking.

Eventually Larkin ran out of fog effects, and was dismayed to see Cunningham put [Elephant Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elephant+Guide) on the token, effectively neutralizing the [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) Larkin had planned to cast.

On the final turn, Larkin cycled a Remote Isle looking for a [Chastise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chastise), but to no avail.

**Final Result:** Cunningham 2 – Larkin 0

After the match, Cunningham said that he was torn between the red/green deck and the blue/green one that he lent Peter Szigeti for the Gateway. He was happy to have stuck with his first option, especially in a field full of [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) and Wake.
