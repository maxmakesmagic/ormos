Grand Prix Strasbourg 2006
--------------------------


Saitou Seizes Strasbourg!
=========================




May 19-20, 2006











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/champ.jpg)Japanese level 6 pro Tomoharu Saitou is the Grand Prix Strasbourg champion 2007! On the strength of [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon), the far-traveled Pro takes home 3000 US$. Much more important, Saitou grabs eight Pro points for his win, which puts him to 39 points and five points ahead of Raphael Levy in the Player of the Year race.


The second place goes to German amateur Raul Porojan, who gets additional amateur prize money and earns a bigger purse than even the actual winner. For him, this top 8 is the gateway to Pro Tour Valencia, where he aims to lose his amateur-ship. The home country of France managed to put no players in the Top 8. Instead, German could be heard all over the top tables: Three Germans, one Austrian, one Swiss player made up more than half of the Top 8, the rest was filled out by one Czech and two Japanese.


Deckwise, red- and green-based decks proved to be most popular and most resilient, with Saito's Mono Red taking the top spot. More than one fourth of all day-2-decks fell into the R/G Big Mana and Mono Red category. The "ramp" decks were most popular, but the Pro choice still was U/B, typically supported by a white splash. Shouta Yasooka piloted such a creation in the top 8. A surprisingly high number of players chose the rock-stable manabase of Mono Blue, among them Olivier Ruel, assuming that no White Weenie or fast aggro decks would impede their way. With just 15 White Weenie decks in day two, their assumption held true.


Now it's up to Future Sight and updates to the decks that have held their worth from Yokohama to Strasbourg. The PTQ season is coming up just around the corner, as well as the National Qualifiers. But the next stop on the circuit is Grand Prix Montreal on June 23-24. *Time Spiral* Block Constructed with Future Sight is the format, so keep the Strasbourg results in mind and meet the **magicthegathering.com** coverage reporters in Montreal!

Sunday, May 20: 7:18 p.m. - Quarterfinals: Florian Pils vs. Christoph Huber
---------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

 

The top 8 started pretty early in the day, with the six rounds of Swiss on Sunday going very smooth. Florian Pils from Germany has fought his way to the Top 8 with a green-red-blue big mana deck, similar to the one Sebastian Thaler Top 8'ed in Yokohama. On the other side of the table, Christoph Huber has brought the same colors, only in different emphasis. He is playing U/G Shifter, only with added [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) goodness.


### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/Pils.jpg)*I sense the force is strong in you... but I am stronger still, young knight!*
Both players kept their initial hand and had mana-rich openings. Florian brought double [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow)s, Christoph had [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). Morph from Christoph took a counter off the Wall, leaving one [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) untapped for the Austrian. Almost like a mirror, Florian played a morph of his own, only he used up all his lands. Christoph attacked with the morph and used the window of opportunity to Mwonvuli- Acid-Moss away Florian's only [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest). [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow)s went to get an replacement, but the German was still behind. Christoph had a second morph.


The first mystery was revealed when Florian declared his Morph to be a [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), copying the [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) on Christoph's side because he need the mana for [Harmonize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize). Christoph unmophed end-of-turn [Thelonite Hermit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thelonite+Hermit), gaining a sizeable attack force. If his second morph would also be Hermit, the Saprolings would be nigh unstoppable. And it was a [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), copying the Hermit, so Florian went down to 7. Christoph then had [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind), and against three cards in hand Florian conceded.


Florian Pils 0 - 1 Christoph Huber


Florian brought in 2 [Dead & Gone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+%2F%2F+Gone), 1 [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), 1 [Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst) and took out 2 [Avalanche Riders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders), 1 [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler), 1 [Weatherseed Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weatherseed+Totem).


Christoph brought in 3 [Utopia Vow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Vow), taking out his 3 [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind).


### Game 2



![Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spell+Burst)

Christoph on the draw was faced with a mulligan, but his next six looked good. Openings identical to the last game, with Search and [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) respectively, promised no mana shortage in this game. Where Florian had [Harmonize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize), Christoph had [Mwonvuli Acid-Moss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mwonvuli+Acid-Moss). Florian threatened first, suspending [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler), but when that came into play, Christoph had a timely [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) that Florian stopped with his single [Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst) off his only [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). He came in for the full 7 off the Chronicler, taking Christoph to 13.


The Austrian fought back. Unmorphing [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer) gave him extra cards, he suspended a Chronicler of his own for a turn and most importantly had [Utopia Vow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Vow) to incapacitate Florian's own Chronicler. Florian answered with brute force, hardcasting [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite) (thanks [Utopia Vow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Vow) for the eighth mana!) and doming Christoph for 5. Down to 8, Christoph cracked back with his [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler), taking Florian to 14, and had yet another [Utopia Vow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Vow) for the Hellkite! Bereft of Threats, Florian gunned down Christoph's only [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) with [Mwonvuli Acid-Moss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mwonvuli+Acid-Moss) and still had enough mana left to plunk down [Spectral Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force).


Christoph bounced it back to Florian's hand with [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), but again Florian pulled out a big gun: [Disintegrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disintegrate), aimed at Christoph's head, ended the game in the German's favor.


Florian Pils 1 - 1 Christoph Huber


### Game 3


Christoph was fast to announce "keep" on the play, but Florian had to go pack one step and reshuffle, as he preferred random six to his opening seven. Christoph opened with [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) again, and so did Florian, but where Christoph had just [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow)s, Florian went for [Harmonize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/Huber.jpg)*Huh... what now? I might just lose.*
[Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) from Christoph set Florian back a turn, bouncing an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), but it didn't get to do any damage as it ate the Dead half of [Dead & Gone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+%2F%2F+Gone). Florian suspended [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) for a turn and an extra card,Christoph just kept making mana. Chronicler took him to 12, and he needed a solution of some kind, especially as Florian put [Intet, the Dreamer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intet%2C+the+Dreamer) next to the Chronicler.


Christoph made a morph, [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), and drew cards just to find something. He went to 5 on the next attack, countered [Spectral Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force) with [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake), but it was just not enough. When he saw his next draw, Christoph gave up a futile fight.


Florian Pils 2 - 1 Christoph Huber


  


Sunday, May 20: 7:33 p.m. - Quarterfinals: Tomoharu Saito vs. Petr Martinek
---------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Sebastian Abresch*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/qfsaito.jpg)*Tomoharu Saito*
Finally, after 15 rounds of Swiss, the spirit - along with the temperature - got tense on site. We were off to the quarterfinal between Level 6 pro player Tomoharu Saito facing Czech Petr Martinek. Saito brought a greenless version of his Yokohama Top 8 'Pizza Stompy' deck, [Timbermare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timbermare) and [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) made room for [Disintegrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disintegrate), [Browbeat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat) and even more lands (25!) - quite the 'in your face' build. Martinek played a classic R/G Mana Ramp, combining clunky monsters like [Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite), [Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) and [Spectral Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force) (along with his girlfriend, [Scryb Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger)) with [Radha, Heir to Keld](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radha%2C+Heir+to+Keld), [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) and [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) as additional acceleration.


As both players shuffled up for Game 1, Saito did some gym and traditionally japanese face-slapping. That made him awake enough to win the die roll and choosing to play, keeping his opening seven. Petr Martinek, by contrast, went down to six before being happy with his opener.


The Japanese started by suspending [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon), perhaps the key spell of his deck - Petr just had [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). Saito brought up some pressure by suspending [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) on turn two, which got to eat Martinek's freshly played Radha on turn three. Tomoharu tried to go for some damage with [Browbeat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat) - or did he want the cards? Either way seemed to be okay, Petr decided to give the pro player the possibility to draw more damage.



![Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Greater+Gargadon)

Sunday, May 20: 7:51 p.m. - Quarterfinals: Klaus Jöns vs. Raul Porojan
----------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobias Henke*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/qfjoens.jpg)*Klaus Jöns*
Level 3 pro Klaus Jöns from Germany made his second GP-Top8 in as many weeks, his third lifetime. Though he might have been the favorite against relative unknown German player Raul Pojoran, his red aggro deck with the usual green splash for [Timbermare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timbermare) and [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) was about to experience a hard time against Raul's U/G-Shifter, splashing [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) as well.


### Game 1


Klaus won the die-roll, chose to play first, and kept his opening seven, as was his opponent. He came out fast and furious, and by turn three Klaus' side of the table was getting crowded with Goblin tokens due to two [Mogg War Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal)s and one suspended [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon). By now Raul commented bitterly on his opponent's "perfect draw".


But luck was about to swing back, as Klaus had no play, neither for turn 4 nor turn 5. Meanwhile, Raul acclerated with [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) into [Harmonize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize), then put his massive hand size to good use by playing [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind). While Klaus still had no drop, Raul played a Morph. The initial offense came to an immediate stop, as Klaus had no way to kill it. Instead he played still more land, visibly frustrated.



![snapback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=snapback)

At one point [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) entered the game, but Raul had the [Snapback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapback) ready... and his [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) as well. So instead of Klaus, now he controlled the fearsome 9/7 beatstick, which did exactly that on Raul's next turn. Klaus went down to eleven. Starting to chumpblock at this point could only result in a slow defeat, whereas the risky play might have given him the option to turn the table with a little help from [Timbermare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timbermare).


As it was, taking nine damage from "the [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) formerly known as Morph", turned out to be just a shortcut towards the inevitable, as Klaus' next draw steps provided no answer. Raul's [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) took care of the last points of damage.


Raul Porojan 1 - 0 Klaus Jöns


Raul's sideboarding: -1 [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake), -2 [Spectral Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force), -4 [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)

Klaus' sideboarding: -1 [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind), -1 [Dead & Gone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+%2F%2F+Gone), -2 [Sulfur Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Elemental)  

+3 [Avalanche Riders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders), +1 [Word of Seizing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Word+of+Seizing)


### Game 2


Klaus lamented that he had hoped to at least win before sideboarding, but his mood soon changed, when his opponent took two mulligans.


Raul kept a five-card hand with one land. He found his second land in turn three, when his first turn [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) left the suspend zone, and his draw step gave him another one.


At this point Klaus already had [Thick-Skinned Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thick-Skinned+Goblin) and [Mogg War Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal) in play, and [Avalanche Riders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders) followed. The synergy between the first Goblin and the two Echo- creatures might be noteworthy. Raul Porojan 1 - 1 Klaus Jöns

Klaus started with [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon), [Blood Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight) and [Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaya+Ballard%2C+Task+Mage) in the first three turns. Next up was a suspended [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) and a [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) from Klaus. He later told me, he always puts the elephants in when going second.) But no, [Snapback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapback) was already waiting...


With the combo-ish triangle of [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler), [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), and [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) firmly in place, Klaus conceded.


Raul Porojan 2 - 1 Klaus Jöns

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/fporojan.jpg)*Raul Porojan was behind from every turn one.*
And he'd need it against the [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) about to come into play in two turns or less. Raul was tapped out, not able to reveal a Shapeshifter to block an incoming Gargadon. If Saito sacrificed two permanents, he could come in for 7 and 3. The first morph, also a Shapeshifter, fell to Dead of [Dead & Gone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+%2F%2F+Gone) fame, the second morph blocked the Gargadon. It was [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), setting Raul back on lands but fueling his Chronicler and the copied twin. Raul took four from piglet beats, going to 7, and Saito had two [Blood Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Knight)s left to block any possible attacks.


Raul saw his chance in Chronicler beatdown and suspended yet another one for a turn. Saito pressed in with everything, Raul blocked to stay alive, but Saito had [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) and nailed Raul to the flames.


Tomoharu Saito 1 - 0 Raul Porojan


Sideboarding:  

Raul took out 4 Riftwing, 2 [Spectral Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force), 1 [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake), and brought in 4 [Spike Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder), 2 [Serrated Arrows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows), 1 [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler).


Sideboarding proceeded, and again, the Saito gymnastics and the reverential look to the sky, or the hall's ceiling, as it were, came into play. Saito took out 4 [Browbeat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Browbeat) and brought in 1 [Dead & Gone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+%2F%2F+Gone) and 3 [Word of Seizing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Word+of+Seizing).


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/Pigs.jpg)*Look, a herd of tapped piglets!*
All that praying apparently helped, because Saito opened with [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) into [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) for the sixth time in a row in this Top 8. And the goodness continued with another Gargadon and triple [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll). Raul had [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and [Spike Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) to stave off the first wave of attacks, soon joined by a morph, but was clearly not amused. "Slap" made Saito's hands on his cheeks, and slap made [Mogg War Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal), mother sow to a little silver 1/1 plastic pig for Saito. Raul kept his arms akimbo and his face impassive. Saito would start Scrolling him soon, but first added a third pig from a second War Marshal to his army. His two Gargadons would soon be feasting on roast piglet, then, as Raul had nothing but [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) to combat them. Any pig he'd fry would end up as Gargadon fodder.


Saito had maneuvered Raul into a very difficult situation. Even under the assumption that the morph he had was a [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), two Gargadons backed by one left-over [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll) would make mincemeat of him. Raul was on 24 life, owing to the trusty [Spike Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder), but Saito suspended two [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt)s. A collective sigh went through the audience. Raul was the underdog, crowd favorite, and even his second morph wouldn't save him - especially since Saito aimed a [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) at it ([Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer)).


The second [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) tried to fry the second morph, but that one was a Shapeshifter and took on the form of [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). Still, Saito had a chance to kill it: [Magus of the Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Scroll), naming [Sulfur Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Elemental). The Scrolling hit, taking away the Shapeshifter, and now [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) #1 came into play. Raul went to 12. He had to kill the Magus, and discarded [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer) randomly to his [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind) to do so. But his position nobody would envy.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpstra07/fin2.jpg)Saito wanted to clear a way for his second Gargadon, still suspended, and [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt)ed [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). Raul's board was empty save for [Stormbind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind). Discarding [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake), he killed a 1/1 piglet before Saito's combat phase. The Japanese player sacrificed two [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s to his suspended Gargadon, shoved everything into the red zone and seconds later, he could call himself GP Strasbourg winner and current leader in the Player of the Year race!


Tomoharu Saito 2 - 0 Raul Porojan


After the match, both players agreed that the match-up was in favor of Raul, but Saito's extraordinary luck carried him through to the title. Tomoharu Saito is the GP Strasbourg champion and the current leader in the Player of the Year race!
