Ganz wins ad nauseam in Charlotte
=================================




Andreas Ganz traveled all the way from Zurich, Switzerland to play in Grand Prix Charlotte. The past pro was confident that his Ad Nauseam deck was very strong and it wasn’t folly. Ganz overcame a massive field of 2400 players to combo his way to victory as he steamrolled the Top 8. Ganz needs to find room in his luggage for a trophy and get his wits about him as he prepares to play on the Pro Tour once more.

TOP 8 COVERAGE



Andreas Ganz comboed his way to victory at Grand Prix Charlotte!


2400 players came to Grand Prix Charlotte to try their hand at the exciting new Modern format. The format proved to be a crazy environment with a field of wildly diverse strategies. Known decks like Jeskai Harbinger and Jund had a huge presence, but exciting new strategies like Dredge made their presence felt.


After fifteen rounds of swiss, only eight players remained. Jacky Wang, Adonnys Medrano, (10) Samuel Black, Jon Bolding, Andreas Ganz, (16) Mike Sigrist, Robert Graves, and Eli Kassis.


Ganz’s Ad Nauseam deck overtook Jacky Wang’s Bring to Light Scapeshift deck in the quarterfinals, demolished Robert Grave’s Kiki-Chord deck in the semifinals, and easily won the finals against Jon Bolding’s Naya Collected Company.

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Welcome back to Grand Prix Charlotte! Yesterday, 2399 Magic players from around the world battled their way through nine rounds of Modern. When the dust settled, only Michael Ingram, Jack Wang, Logan Martin, Matthew Dilks, Brian Braun-Duin, Cody Hatfield, Zhengdong Shan, Michael Shea, Alex Bianchi, Eric Kurkian, Chad Kastel, and Justin Bransfield escaped undefeated. Today, the players with the best records from yesterday will return to sling spells for another six rounds of Modern before we enter the elimination rounds of the Top 8.


The new Modern format continues to evolve as timeless classics, innovative strategies, and newly established tier one decks shred their way to the top of the standings. Will this weekend’s rogue decks become the new Modern decks to beat? Or will Modern’s old standbys defend their throne? Stay tuned to continuing Coverage of Grand Prix Charlotte.

This morning, 2399 players came to The Charlotte Convention Center with hopes of being crowned Grand Prix Charlotte Champion! With nine rounds of Modern in the books, only the players with the best records will return to continue battle tomorrow.
