Marauding Tron
==============



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on May 5, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






Pauper decks generally need to solve puzzles with unique tools, given the limits of only commons despite a vast card pool. [Sage's Row Denizen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage%27s+Row+Denizen), [Nylea's Presence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%27s+Presence), and [Prismatic Strands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Strands) are all staples of the Pauper format, despite those cards being off the radar in nearly every other Constructed format. [Fangren Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Marauder) is another one of these cards, and a successful Pauper deck has been built to maximize its unique ability.



![Fangren Marauder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Fangren%20Marauder&options=)With [Cloudpost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost) making its way onto the banned list, Pauper players have turned to the Urza lands to produce extra mana. This has resulted in a deck that has many similarities to the Modern UrzaTron decks, featuring [Chromatic Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Sphere), [Chromatic Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star), and [Prophetic Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prophetic+Prism) to help cast colored spells with colorless-producing lands.

That's where [Fangren Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Marauder) comes in. By both producing lots of mana with the UrzaTron lands and having artifacts that can replace themselves from play, [Fangren Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Marauder) often triggers many times in a game, often putting games out of reach.
