Faerie Fire
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 16, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







It's been quite some time since I've cast a [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), and to be honest, I miss it. When I saw this beauty of a deck in the [Top 16 decklists](/en/events/coverage/manfield-rite-man-kansas-city) from [Grand Prix Kansas City](/en/events/coverage/manfield-rite-man-kansas-city), it immediately piqued my interest. Not only does it utilize the power of [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), it also has [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), more cards that are incredibly sweet. 


![Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spellstutter+Sprite)
 Besides Sprites, [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)s, [Snapcaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster)s, and [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique), the deck is all spells, which should come as no surprise. The vast majority of those spells are burn spells, which combine well with the aggressive bent of the deck. I particularly like how good [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) and [Electrolyze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) are these days, fighting against [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence)s, [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)s, and [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) alike. Plus, counterburn-style decks like this are already very good against control and combo, due to the massive amount of cheap/instant threats and counterspells, so any help this can get against the midrange creature decks is incredibly valuable. 


 The game plan here is very simple: counter or burn anything your opponent plays, all while beating down with one of your cheap utility creatures. There are plenty of tricky things going on within that, from using [Telling Time](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telling+Time) to set up [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) to power up Spellstutter, and Snapcaster to do everything. 


 The mix of burn spells and large quantity of instants will always keep your opponents guessing, and the flexibility offered by [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) certainly helps in that regard. Playing around [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning), and [Spell Snare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) all in the same turn is not an easy feat, and once you throw the possibility of an end-of-turn [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) in there, it gets even more difficult. 


 You can customize the sideboard in many ways, as long as you keep the number of spells roughly the same. For that reason, cards like [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce), [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), and [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) are very valuable, as they insure that you will be able to flip your [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)s and have targets for your Snapcasters. The [Threads of Disloyalty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threads+of+Disloyalty) shore up the [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) matchups, and [Blood Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) plus [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) is the anti-Urza lands package (although I could see using [Sowing Salt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt) instead).
