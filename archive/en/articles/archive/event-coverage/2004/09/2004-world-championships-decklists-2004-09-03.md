2004 World Championships Decklists
==================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 3, 2004 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20171224024527im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_MikeTurian.jpg?itok=LXCQPehw)
By Mike Turian




Before joining Magic Online as its digital product manager, Mike worked as a producer for Wizards's technology department, a product manager for organized play, and as an R&D lead developer. He has played Magic Online since it came out in 2002 and Magic since The Dark. 






Eugene Harvey wins Day 3! Harvey took his Affinity deck to a perfect 6-0 record on the day. He needed every point to squeak into the Top 16 of Worlds, finishing in exactly 16th place. Murray Evans did one better. He may not have won the day but he will be trying to win the best day -- Day 5. His Affinity deck's perfect record helped Evans make his first Worlds Top 8.


Of the six players who went 6-0, four were playing Affinity. Joining Harvey and Evans on that list was Wim Gins of Belgium and Alexander Sacal of Mexico. The last two decks were both green-red aggressive artifact removal decks. Kkyoung Geun Nam of the Korean team and Grgur Petric Maretic of Croatia were the last two players to go undefeated in *Mirrodin* Block Constructed.
