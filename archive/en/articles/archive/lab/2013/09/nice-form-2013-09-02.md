Nice Form
=========



 Posted in [From the Lab](/en/articles/columns/from-the-lab-archive)
 on September 2, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







Helcome to the Lab, friends! Today I have the pleasure of helping kick off *Theros* previews, and I've got a sweet card to share with you all. I know most of you are just going to scroll right down to it if you haven't already, so without further ado, I present to you Artisan of Forms.

 The first thing that occurs to me when I look at this card is that it's the exact opposite of [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image). Both have the ability to copy things, but [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) will die if it's targeted, whereas Artisan of Forms needs to be targeted to use its ability. Although the Artisan doesn't come pre-packaged as a copy of another creature, it does allow you to change your mind about what creature you want to copy later on. Since it only costs two mana, the ability to change it again and again as larger and more threatening creatures enter the battlefield is quite valuable. 

 There have been other [Clone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone)s that can switch from one creature to another in a number of different ways. [Vesuvan Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Doppelganger) and [Cryptoplasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptoplasm) both do it at the beginning of your upkeep, and [Sakashima the Impostor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakashima+the+Impostor) returns itself to your hand. Artisan of Forms, however, can copy another creature at instant speed, opening up some interesting possibilities. Unlike most [Clone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone)s, it doesn't copy another creature right away, so you don't get any "enters the battlefield" abilities that creature might have. Normally, this is a drawback, but with Artisan of Forms, it's not difficult to turn it into an advantage.

###  Artisan of Dreadnoughts

 Throughout **Magic**'s history, there have been a number of very large, inexpensive creatures with some sort of drawback. The most famous of these is [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought). A 12/12 with trample for only one mana is enormous, but the drawback is equally large. If you don't sacrifice 12 power of creatures when it enters the battlefield, you'll have to sacrifice the Dreadnought instead. With Artisan of Forms, however, you can deftly skirt around this ability. When [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought) enters the battlefield, its ability will trigger and go on the stack, at which point you can target Artisan of Forms and have it become a copy of the Dreadnought. When the Dreadnought's ability resolves, you can sacrifice it and still be left with your 12/12 Artisan. 


[Waning Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Waning+Wurm) is another large creature with a heavy drawback. Thanks to vanishing, it will usually only be able to attack once before dying. However, if Artisan of Forms becomes a copy of it, you can keep it forever. It will still have vanishing, but there won't be any counters to remove, so the ability that forces you to sacrifice it when you remove the last counter will never trigger. 

 The last big creature I'll be using is [Shah of Naar Isle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shah+of+Naar+Isle). A 6/6 with trample for four mana is very powerful, but the echo cost of allowing your opponent to draw three cards often isn't worth it. If Artisan of Forms copies it, however, the echo cost will never trigger, so long as the Artisan didn't enter the battlefield that turn. You'll be free to sacrifice or keep the original Shah as you see fit; meanwhile, Artisan of Forms will be busy attacking for 6.

| --- | --- |
| Waning Wurm | Shah of Naar Isle |

 Of course, none of this will happen without a way to target Artisan of Forms at instant speed. After digging through a few options, I settled on [Defy Gravity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defy+Gravity) and [Artful Dodge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artful+Dodge). Both cost only one mana, and both give the Artisan evasion for a turn to go with its new beefy proportions. They also have flashback, allowing you to easily copy another creature later or simply make sure the Artisan gets in for those last few points of damage. Imagine you cast Artisan of Forms on turn two, then on turn three cast a [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought) and cast [Artful Dodge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artful+Dodge) on the Artisan to copy it. You can attack for 12 unblockable damage that turn and flashback [Artful Dodge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artful+Dodge) the following turn to finish off your opponent. 

 Since you can't really rely on drawing Artisan of Forms every game, I threw in another creature that can fill a similar role. [Dimir Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Doppelganger) can copy creatures in graveyards. Since all of your giant things sacrifice themselves, getting them to the graveyard is easy. 


[Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse) helps you find the cards you need, allowing you do dig through the top four cards of your library and select whichever one you want most. [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) does the same for one more mana, but it has the additional bonus of putting the rest in your graveyard, giving you a creature to copy with [Dimir Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Doppelganger) or a spell to flash back on Artisan of Forms.

### Artisannihilator

 Artisan of Forms is quite good at taking advantage of creatures that aren't around for long. Another way to use this ability is with cards like [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). It can put any creature onto the battlefield, but forces you to sacrifice it at the end of your turn. With Artisan of Forms, you can get another copy to keep after the original dies. Of course, with the ability to put any creature onto the battlefield, there's no reason not to use one of the biggest creatures of them all: [Worldspine Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldspine+Wurm). By putting [Worldspine Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldspine+Wurm) onto the battlefield with [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) and copying it with Artisan of Forms, you can attack for 30 immediately. The Wurm's indestructible cousins, [Darksteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus) and [Blightsteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightsteel+Colossus), can serve as backups in case you don't draw it, while [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) can fill the same role as [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Through the Breach | Darksteel Colossus |

 Once again, however, we need some good ways to target Artisan of Forms. Since [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) costs five mana, and you need to be able to use the Artisan immediately, I went with some instants that can be cast without mana. [Mutagenic Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) and [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) can each be cast for the low cost of 2 life, and since the Artisan's ability will resolve before the spell that triggered it, casting a [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) on it won't be lethal. I'll also include [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart). It can be cast for only one mana, and if you have a Mountain, you can cast it earlier in the game, then flash it back on the critical turn without paying any mana. 

 To help you reach the five mana necessary for [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) and [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack), I'll throw in a couple ways to accelerate your mana production. [Worn Powerstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worn+Powerstone) guarantees you five mana on turn four, even without another land drop. This allows you to curve out quite nicely, with Artisan of Forms on turn two, [Worn Powerstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worn+Powerstone) on turn three, and [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) on turn four. If you don't have Artisan of Forms, [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) allows you to cast [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) as soon as turn three. Although it's a one-shot deal, the ability to float two mana to your next turn can be quite valuable.

### Arti-Standoff

Now it's time for our two shifters of shape (although technically they're Human Wizards) to battle it out in the arena. Only one can be victorious, so let's go see who's in top form and who will be left at the bottom.


**Game 1**


 Phyrexian Forms won the roll and started off with a [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents). Darksteel Artisan played a Mountain and passed the turn. The Phyrexians cast [Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse), getting [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought), and Darksteel Artisan cast Artisan of Forms before passing the turn. Phyrexian Forms summoned its own Artisan and passed the turn, and Darksteel Artisan killed the opposing Artisan with a [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart). 

 Phyrexian Forms cast [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy), getting a [Dimir Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Doppelganger) and putting [Defy Gravity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defy+Gravity) in the graveyard. Darksteel Artisan played a land and passed the turn. Phyrexian Forms cast [Dimir Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Doppelganger) and ended the turn, and Darksteel Artisan passed back with no play. [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought) entered the battlefield briefly before being sacrificed, and [Dimir Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Doppelganger) copied it and attacked for 12. However, Darksteel Artisan had a counterstrike, casting [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) to put [Worldspine Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldspine+Wurm) onto the battlefield. A flashed back [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) made Artisan of Forms a copy of the giant Wurm, and the pair attacked for 30 damage. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Lava Dart | Worldspine Wurm |


**Game 2**


 Each side started off with a land, and Phyrexian Forms cast Artisan of Forms on turn two. Darksteel Artisan cast [Pentad Prism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) with two counters, and Phyrexian Forms cast the [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought), using [Defy Gravity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defy+Gravity) to make the Artisan a copy of it. The Artisan attacked for 12 before ending the turn. Darksteel Artisan cast [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) and put in [Worldspine Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldspine+Wurm), attacking for 15. The Wurm was sacrificed at the end of the turn but left behind three 5/5 Wurm tokens. Unfortunately, a flashed back [Defy Gravity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defy+Gravity) allowed the [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought) to soar over them for lethal damage. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Defy Gravity | Phyrexian Dreadnought |


**Game 3**


 Both sides played lands and passed until Phyrexian Forms cast [Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impulse) on turn two, finding Artisan of Forms. Darksteel Artisan followed with a [Worn Powerstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worn+Powerstone), and Darksteel Artisan cast the Artisan before passing the turn. The Phyrexians cast another [Worn Powerstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worn+Powerstone) and killed Artisan of Forms with [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot). Darksteel Artisan cast [Dimir Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Doppelganger) and ended the turn. 

 Darksteel Artisan cast [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) but didn't have a second red mana to activate it and was forced to pass the turn. The Phyrexians cast a [Phyrexian Dreadnought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Dreadnought), sacrificed it, and made [Dimir Doppelganger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Doppelganger) a copy of it. The Doppelganger attacked for 12, but Darksteel Artisan responded by putting a [Blightsteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightsteel+Colossus) in with [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) and attacking for a lethal amount of infect damage.
