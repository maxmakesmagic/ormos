Karn's Legacy
=============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 12, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods












Legacy is a pretty wide-open format, but you do tend to see the same cards show up again and again because they are just that good. [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) is a great example of this. Despite the fact that decks featuring the card can be just about anything, from combo to control, the card remains a central link between them. New combinations of cards pop up all of the time, but for a new card to breakinto the format, a lot has to go right. Caleb Durward is known for trying to get just that to happen in Legacy, and I think his latest brew catches even the most dedicated Legacy connoisseur off guard. 

![Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Karn+Liberated) Not only did Caleb win the recent StarCityGames Open in Cincinnati with a blue deck that did not contain [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), but he did so with a deck that features [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated)! Despite the card being criticized for being too slow in formats like Standard, Caleb figured he would give it a go in Legacy, a much faster and less forgiving format for the seven-mana Planeswalker. Caleb didn't stop there, though, as there are seven more Planeswalkers in his deck, all costing four mana! 

 Caleb actually managed to play multiple cards that I have never seen grace a Legacy list before (although they very well might have). [Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), [Dimir Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Signet), and [Talisman of Dominance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talisman+of+Dominance) are all pretty novel, which is an exciting thing to say for the Legacy community. Caleb can just treat his deck like a ramp deck, similar to Urzatron strategies, but [Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas) also gives him an explosive clock when need be. This is a refreshing take on Legacy and I would expect a lot of similar strategies to be tried over the next few weeks.
