Allied Strategies
=================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on November 5, 2009 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello and welcome back to this wonderful Johnny themed column in which I present fantastic concoctions made from my secret diabolical laboratory! Today's topic? Nothing less then *Zendikar*'s most linear of themes, the tribe of Allies! Described as something of a 'Super-Sliver', an Ally works well alongside its compatriots, and the more you have, the stronger your alliance will be.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130614125842im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl63_goTeam.jpg)  
According to [this week's Making Magic](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/mm/63), which describes the design and nurturing of Allies as a set theme, this theme was almost keyworded thusly: teamwork. Personally, I love the idea of my creatures fully united in teamwork. I imagine them all putting their hands together, shouting some cheer, and throwing them into the air, which would be immediately followed by the Ally-in-question's effect. So a [Murasa Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murasa+Pyromancer) cheer would be punctuated by a torrent of fire, an [Ondu Cleric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ondu+Cleric) one could produce refreshing herbs and balms, and throwing hands into the air for a [Seascape Aerialist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seascape+Aerialist) could result in having the rest of your body follow suit.

Teamwork was eventually snuffed out as a keyword, but the Ally tribe remains, to our combination-seeking delight. But wait, I thought Allies were so linear you couldn't *find* any combos with them? Silly voices in my head, negativity is for skids! Thanks to the latest surge in what has been a tremendous (and extremely appreciated) recent wave of reader email, I've discovered two or three excellent combos with the members of the Ally tribe. Let's head on to these ideas, with the power of teamwork! (Or scrolling.)

Shrek It Ain't


|  |
| --- |
|  |
| [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist) is probably one of the most frightening Ogres in **Magic** this side of [Heartless Hidetsugu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Hidetsugu). Sure, some others may simply look frightening, like [Jagged Poppet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) or [Villainous Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villainous+Ogre), but the Diabolist has what the other Ogres don't: a supporting cast. Most Ogres prefer to work alone (there's no Ogre lord) but [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist) really encourages teamwork. Plus, that magenta aura around its hand freaks me out a bit.So what's more exciting about [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist) than the other Allies? Two different readers, Travis Kuster and Alexander Nolastname, filled me in on one particular play. As Travis put it, "[Rite of Replication](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication) + [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist) = 180 life lost. Multiplayer win." When five more [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist)s hit the battlefield, each one will trigger and the life loss will quickly stack up. Or as Laurie in the forums of last week's article put it, "6 Diabolists trigger 5 times each, for 6 times 6 times 5 life = 180 life. And that's assuming you have no other Allies..." |



|  |
| --- |
|  |
| Speaking quickly on [Rite of Replication](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication), which I talked about last week, I received some emails saying I missed a way to use the fun sorcery. To this I say "Come on, how could I not?" The Rite is so hilariously open-ended, it's no wonder it's one of my favorite cards from *Zendikar*. Seriously, pick any non-legendary creature besides [Kokusho, the Evening Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kokusho%2C+the+Evening+Star), and cast a kicked Rite on it. Tada! FUN!Back to Laurie's comment. Assuming I have no other Allies, the life loss count would be 180. I wonder though...how much life could I make my opponent lose? To up the count, I'll need some more Allies. [Umara Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umara+Raptor) is a cheap beater. [Bala Ged Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bala+Ged+Thief) is one of the more annoying Allies. [Amoeboid Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amoeboid+Changeling) not only is an Ally, but can turn your non-Ally creatures into them. [Shapesharer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shapesharer) can transform into a copy of [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist) in a pinch. |



|  |
| --- |
|  |
| Like last week, though, I need to think of a way to get to nine mana to kick the Rite. Last time I used Goats from a regular old [Springjack Shepherd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springjack+Shepherd) to fuel a [Springjack Pasture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springjack+Pasture). This time, I think I'll just play the blue and black control deck and keep the game under wraps until I hit nine mana. My plan is to load the board with Allies of all kinds and to stock counters on [Dreadship Reef](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadship+Reef). Eventually a kicked Rite will land on one Ally or another. [Umara Raptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umara+Raptor) has evasion and can become huge. [Brainspoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil) can find [Hagra Diabolist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hagra+Diabolist), [Seascape Aerialist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seascape+Aerialist), [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), or [Sea Gate Loremaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Loremaster). |

Nissa, We Miss Ya
Yeah, that was terrible. However, it does let me transition into my section on [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane), which I'm sure seems completely reasonable right in the thick of Ally week. Wait, it doesn't? This is completely random? Just wait; it'll all tie together at the end.

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130614122717im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl63_nissaSlice.jpg)  
So, [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane). She's the second monogreen planeswalker in existence and both of them cost 2GG. When I scanned Nissa for the first time, I hadn't yet seen what [Nissa's Chosen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Chosen) was, so I was slightly skeptical. Now that I know that the Chosen is an invincible [Elvish Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Warrior), Nissa looks a lot better. Her free Wellwisher ability is absurdly crazy, and is a good way to stall your opponent (notice that this ability adds, not subtracts, loyalty from Nissa.)



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Her third ability, though...I am sure that former From the Lab columnist Chris Millar was quivering with excitement upon reading this ultimate. It seems impossible that he, or any lover of the Elf deck, could pass up popping every single Elf from your deck onto the battlefield at once. Long story short: This is insane.

I was sure that there was a way to take an offbeat direction with a [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane) deck, and in a fine email, Havoc Jack proved it. Here's his ideal Nissa activation:

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl63_combo.jpg)  

> 1. Resolve [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane)'s ultimate ability, searching up a [Godtracker of Jund](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godtracker+of+Jund), an [Essence Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden), and no less than three [Wren's Run Packmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Packmaster)s.
> 
> 2. Play with triggers. With all three champion triggers on, exile the [Essence Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden), then the first Packmaster, then the second Packmaster. The first Packmaster comes back, exiling the third one, which brings the second one back, and oh, what a mess. If you muddle through it enough, the leaving and returning Packmasters make a steady cycle.
> 
> 3. Profit! The Godspeaker has an arbitrarily large number of +1/+1 counters on it, the [Essence Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden) has gained you infinite life, and the [Where Ancients Tread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Where+Ancients+Tread) you might have started with on the battlefield burns any number of opponents out.
> 
> 

Yes, [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane) seems the ideal way to pull the 'champion' trick, which revolves around countless leaving and entering triggers and is a rules headache. But Havoc Jack wasn't finished:


> Once you've gone that far, you can do even more silly things. For each additional champion (any of the changeling champions will do for this as well), you can add one more 'enters the battlefield' effect on that original creature. So if you kept resetting [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves), rather than just any old Elf to start it off, you'd end up with a whole stack of land. Or use Deranged Hermit for roughly a million Squirrels.
> 
> 

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130614113505im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl63_woodHermitAndSquirels.jpg)  
Or [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) for drawing your whole deck. Or [Wurmskin Forger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmskin+Forger) for beefing up your Elvish army. Well, I'm sure Jack can't ratchet up the complexity further. Let's ta...


> Think I can't ratchet up the complexity any further? Think again. If you search up no less than six champions, you can set up two independent cycles. The advantage of this is that if you also search up a [Heritage Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), two cycles net you infinite mana as well.
> 
> 

Okay, Noel, [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane) may be crizazy in an Elf deck, but this is Ally Week! What's with the Elves? Unless you're telling me there are Elf Allies...oh boy. Jack?


> One more, slightly unrelated. There are three currently printed Elf Allies. If you summoned all twelve with [Nissa Revane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa+Revane), you'd get twelve redundant copies of vigilance! More interestingly, all four [Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger)s would trigger twelve times each.
> 
> 

![](https://web.archive.org/web/20130614094803im_/http://wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl63_3Cards1.jpg)  
How many Wolves is that? By my count, I'd say at least 117. Seriously, though, slamming all twelve Elf Allies onto the battlefield is pretty awesome.

![](http://wizards.com//mtg/images/daily/ftl/ftl63_wolfTokensCountingOff.jpg)  
This leads us very neatly into a subsection about [Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger). Some readers have pointed out that with a slight manipulation of creature types, this is busted in half. Peter and Nathaniel Miles prefer to use [Conspiracy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy) (naming Allies) to create infinite 2/2 Wolf Ally tokens and have an infinitely powered Ranger. Others, like Karl Latremoullie and Mark R. go for Artificial Evolution, to change the Ranger's instance of "Ally" to "Wolf" or vice versa. In Mark's humorous terms, "[Turntimber Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turntimber+Ranger) + Artificial Evolution = Buckets of Lols."

Overall, just enjoy the mechanic!
