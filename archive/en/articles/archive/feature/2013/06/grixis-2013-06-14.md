Grixis
======



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 14, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







It's unusual to see a blue control deck in Standard without [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), but Purpler managed to 4–0 a Standard Daily Event with an honest Grixis deck. 

![Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) Instead of the powerful instant, Purpler relies primarily on [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to get ahead but often only needs to buy enough time to play a game-winning Demon, Dragon, or Vampire. 

 With fourteen cheap creature-removal spells, including [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%26+Away), which can both make the opponent sacrifice a creature and bounce [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to do it again, Purpler isn't planning to fall behind. This deck has no mass removal or big card-draw spells, it's just trying to kill everything as it comes down and then use a four-mana spell to pull ahead over the next few turns. 

 The removal mix is excellent, with [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) and [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) for small creatures; [Far & Away](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%26+Away) for hexproof; and [Victim of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) for troublesome big creatures like [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council). [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) preserves a healthy life total while blocking for Planeswalkers. The counterspell suite is fairly sparse, but the [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s can help it last a little longer, and the sideboard offers discard as a supplement against opponents who are playing things you can't just kill once they're in play. 

 The notable exclusions to me are [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling). Without [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), the deck doesn't need a ton of mana, so it wouldn't be that bad to occasionally discard a land to Liliana, and I think it's high-impact enough to play once the deck is already built to defend Planeswalkers. Ætherling is just such a thorough threat that it's hard for me to see passing up on it, but I do like the other threats Purpler's using. I'd probably play Ætherling over [Niv-Mizzet, Dracogenius](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Dracogenius), though.
