**Magic Online** Championship Series News
=========================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 21, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











The 2010 **Magic Online** Championship Series comes to a close when Season X ends with the downtime on October 13. If you haven't yet qualified for the 2010 **Magic Online** Championship in Chiba, Japan, and a shot at your share of $100,000, you only have three chances left: win the [MOCS End-of-Season X Tournament](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/other/121509a) on October 24, win the [MOCS Player of the Year Championship](#potychamps) on October 30 (if you qualify), or win the [MOCS Last Chance Qualifier](#mocslcq) on October 21. 


Do you have what it takes to be the 2010 **Magic Online** champion? 


### MOCS Player of the Year Championship


Players have been battling all year long in the **Magic Online** Championship Series and earning Qualifier points across 9 different categories: [Standard](/en/articles/archive/magic-online/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2009-04-09-0), [Block Constructed](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2009-04-09), [Extended](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2009-04-09-0), [Legacy](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2010-04-08), [Classic](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/2010-magic-online-player-year-standings-2009-04-09), [Specialty](/en/articles/archive/magic-online/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2010-01-14), [Sealed Deck](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2009-04-09-1), [Draft](/en/articles/archive/magic-online/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2009-04-09), and [Overall](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/2011-magic-online-player-year-standings-2009-04-09). Now all the practice pays off. The top 50 players in the Overall Player of the Year Category, along with the top 10 players in each other category, will be invited to the **Magic Online** Championship Series Player of the Year Tournament on Saturday, October 30, 2010 at 11:00 AM PDT (18:00 UTC). 


The winner of the MOCS Player of the Year tournament will receive the 11th invitation to compete at the **Magic: The Gathering** Worlds Championships in Chiba, Japan and an invitation the 2010 **Magic Online** Championship, also in Chiba. For complete **Magic Online** Championship Series rules, click [here](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/other/121509a).

### 2010 **Magic Online** Championship


Twelve of the best **Magic Online** players will meet in Chiba, Japan at the 2010 **Magic: The Gathering** World Championships and battle for their share of $100,000 dollars! Check out the complete **Magic Online** Championship Series rules [here](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/other/121509a).


**2010 Magic Online Championship**  
*START TIME:* The 2010 **Magic Online** Championship begins Thursday, December 9 and takes place over 4 days at the 2010 **Magic: The Gathering** World Championships. Events will be schedules so registered players can compete in both events.  
*LOCATION:* Live at the **Magic** World Championships in Chiba, Japan  
*ENTRY FEE:* None – Invitation Only.  
*FORMAT:* The 2010 **Magic Online** Championship features several formats over four days:  


* Thursday, December 9: Extended Constructed, 4 rounds, Swiss pairings
* Friday, December 10: *Masters Edition IV* Booster Draft, 3 rounds, Swiss pairings
* Saturday, December 11: Standard Constructed, 4 rounds, Swiss pairings
* Sunday, December 12: Standard Constructed Championship Finals, 1 round, single elimination

*SIZE:* 12 Players   
*SCORING:* Each of the first three events will award championship points based on the number of match wins as follows:   


|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| # of Match Wins | # of Championship Points |
| 1 | 4 |
| 2 | 10 |
| 3 | 17 |
| 4 | 25 |

  
Each player's earned championship points will be totaled after the first 3 events. The top 2 players will advance to the Championship Finals on Sunday, while the remaining players' standings will be based on the number of championship points earned. DCI tie-breakers will be aggregated across all three tournaments and used if needed.   
*PRIZES:* Prizes are based on final standings at the end of the tournament:  





|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Place | Prize |
| 1st | $25,000 USD, 1 complete premium foil digital set (players choice), and 1 complete standard digital set (players choice) |
| 2nd | $17,000 USD , 1 complete premium foil digital set (players choice), and 1 complete standard digital set (players choice) |
| 3rd - 4th | $9,000 USD and 1 complete standard digital set (players choice) |
| 5th - 8th | $6,000 USD and 1 complete standard digital set (players choice) |
| 9th - 12th | $4,000 USD and 1 complete standard digital set (players choice) |

  
Participants must play on the **Magic Online** account that they used to qualify for the 2010 **Magic Online** and supply their own decks for the constructed portions of the event. Product will be provided for the *Masters Edition IV* draft. 


Good luck to all the **Magic Online** Championship qualifiers and see you in Japan!
