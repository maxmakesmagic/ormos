TOP 8 COVERAGE



First-ranked Seth Manfield is unstoppable. Defeating Brandon Fischer in the Finals, who was playing a deck constructed to be good in the mirror, Manfield made it all look easy—living *pura vida* the whole time in Costa Rica.


Though Manfield will be what people remember about this Grand Prix, plenty of other story lines found solid closure this weekend. Oliver Tiu locked up Platinum for himself. Panamanian Saul Alvarado made sure to keep at Top 8 spot in Central America this time. Brian Braun-Duin has vaulted himself higher in the quest for Grand Prix Master. And Kai Budde broke his no-GP streak to show up in San José.


*Shadows over Innistrad*Standard has developed into seemingly its most mature form yet. The decks are streamlined and defined, and the metagame is as comprehensible as you'd want it to be. But it still provides for innovation and invention, revealing that there's still more to come.


From everyone here in Costa Rica, have a great night. And may you dream of rocking as hard as Seth Manfield.

DAY 2 COVERAGE



We’re back in Costa Rica! This time, with a much smaller battlefield. From a combatant count of 468, now there are only 151. Everyone in Day 2 has the ability have a strong finish, with Top 8 potential much larger than usual.


No single 9-0 player only exacerbates that fact! The four top finishers—Fifteenth-Ranked Yuuya Watanabe, eighteenth-Ranked Brad Nelson, Pro Tour Hall of Fame Member Ben Rubin, and two-time Grand Prix winner Brian Braun-Duin—all took a draw and sit at 8-0-1.


Bant decks, White-Black, and Green-White are the top-performing decks so far. Will these decks continue dominance into the Top 8? (don’t worry, there’s a complete metagame breakdown coming.) Will these storied pros at the top translate to an elimination-round finish?


It’s a star-studded second day here in Costa Rica, so anything’s possible. Heck, there are still *six* Pro Tour Hall of Famers in this field of 151. Almost 4% of the field are in the Hall of Fame! The Costa Ricans say *pura vida*, but here in the hall, it’s more like *pura vida loca*.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



The first nine rounds of Grand Prix Costa Rica are completed. The top of the field is a bit intimidating. Brian Braun-Duin, Yuuya Watanabe, Ben Rubin, and eighteenth-ranked Brad Nelson are all 8-0-1. This looks like a Pro Tour Day 1 undefeated list rather than a Grand Prix.


Also right in the thick of it are Oliver Tiu, (16) Alex Hayne, (14) Reid Duke, Yam Wing Chun, Shuhei Nakamura, Jon Stern, Chris Fennell, Javier Dominguez, and Saul Alvarado among many, many others.


Though the incredible scenery, pool-side seating, volcanoes, toucans, waterfalls, and hammocks are certainly attractions, the lure of Magic beckons about 150 people back tomorrow for more.


Sunday morning will bring the time to wake up to the sounds of the rainforest as an alarm clock to get to the venue on time.


See you when the monkey howls 9 a.m.!
