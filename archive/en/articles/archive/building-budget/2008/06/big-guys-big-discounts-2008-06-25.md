The concept behind this deck is to drop guys who double as mana accelerants ([Brighthearth Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brighthearth+Banneret), [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkdrinker+Daredevil)) in order to make the huge guys ([Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath), [Sunrise Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunrise+Sovereign), [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth)) cost a lot less than six to seven mana. For instance, if you get [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkdrinker+Daredevil) on turn three, you can drop [Sunrise Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunrise+Sovereign) on turn four, both [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm) and [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) on turn five, and finish it off with [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth) on turn six as a 12/12 trampling monstrosity!


However, you aren’t going to be able to get the curve every game, especially if your opponent is savvy and wants to kill your guys who give discounts. Therefore, I put in a bunch of early spot-removal spells ([Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate), [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring)) to stall the beginning of the game. If the game goes long, chances are that your Giants will dwarf (literally) any creatures your opponent can put into play; but you need to survive to that point, so you can’t just roll over and die to Elves / Kithkin / Merfolk without a fight.


 



![Brion Stoutarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brion+Stoutarm)

One of the key creatures in this deck is ironically the smallest Giant in the deck, [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm). Brion is hyper-efficient as a 4/4 lifelinked creature for four mana, but his second ability (throwing creatures at your opponent) is where it’s at in a Giant deck. Imagine: you play [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm), and follow it with both [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath) (6/6) and a [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth) (12/12, making your [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath) 18/18). Without ever actually needing to attack, you’ve got 30 points of direct damage on the board, with Brion champing at the bit!

Let’s see what happens with the deck when I take it into the casual rooms of **Magic Online**!


**Game 1: ravarshi (Withering Elfball)**


Ravarshi starts out with [Heritage Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), which I kill with extreme prejudice using [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate). I follow up by removing an [Imperious Perfect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) from the game with [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), and following that with [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkdrinker+Daredevil) and [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm). I then drop [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath), facing down against a second [Imperious Perfect](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect). My [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath) gets to 15/15 as Ravarshi continues to drop Elves, so I use Brion’s [Fling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling) ability to dome Ravarshi for 15 damage at the end of his turn, and then drop and immediately fling another Hamletback for 6 to win the game on my turn.


Record: 1-0


**Game 2: [Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flight) (Blue-White-Red Control)**


[Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flight) leads with [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision). I drop [Brighthearth Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brighthearth+Banneret), and have my [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm) countered by [Rune Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag). [Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flight) then drops [Ajani Goldmane](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Goldmane), and I get [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger), which is promptly killed by [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). Two [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision)s, a bunch of lands, a couple of [Lightning Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel)s, and an [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) later, and I am dead.


Record: 1-1


**Game 3: Shane061183 (Black-Green Elves)**


Shane061183 drops [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and [Wren’s Run Vanquisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%E2%80%99s+Run+Vanquisher), which I [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate). I get [Brighthearth Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brighthearth+Banneret), and get [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) on turn four. I then get [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkdrinker+Daredevil) and Borderguard Behemoth, though my [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) dies to a [Nameless Inversion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion). Still, the large number of 1/1 creatures lets me stay his Elves until I get [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm). I then have an 8/8 trampler, and a guy who will throw an 8/8 trampler at my opponent’s head. This is enough for my big guys to get a big win!


Record: 2-1


**Game 4: [name withheld] (Blue-Black Snow Control)**


My opponent gets [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision), [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), [Scrying Sheets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrying+Sheets), and [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation). After a spirited discussion about how appropriate that deck is for the casual room, I concede the game.


Record: 2-2


**Game 5: hillbilly\_magic (Red-White Control)**


 



![Borderland Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Borderland+Behemoth)

Hillbilly\_magic drops quadruple [Forbid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbid)den Tower and double [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) and [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Wrath%2Bof%2BGod)s my guys off the board three times! I eventually get double [Sunrise Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunrise+Sovereign), [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm) and [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth), and start swinging in for thirty-plus life a turn. He’s got a cushion thanks to his two Warhammers, but I eventually hit him with a 20/20 [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth), and then throw that Behemoth at him with Brion!

Record: 3-2


Just a quick note about Game 4: I was entirely willing to play that game out, but my opponent was concerned about how I would portray our match in this column, and so I conceded rather than push that point. Long story short is this: folks, if you’re going to play a deck that’s won a Pro Tour, or Top 8ed a Grand Prix, swapping out 4 to 8 cards for 4 to 8 other high-level tournament cards does not make your deck casual. As for me, I don’t mind a challenge, but the casual play room just doesn’t seem the place for a deck that is comprised of 100% high-tournament playable cards.


Commence discussion in the forums!


As far as the Big Guys, Big Discounts deck goes, [Brighthearth Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brighthearth+Banneret) hasn’t been great in this deck. On its own, it’s a 1/1 creature, which isn’t high licks or super kicks. Its reinforce ability is less than optimal in a Giants deck, because A) Giants are already huge on their own, and B) I can’t curve the reinforce ability well with five to seven cost critters. I decide to focus more on monsters, so I take out the Bannerets and the [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy)s (which have been stuck dead in my hand every game) in favor of three more high-end Giants ([Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath), which has been my favorite card to drop so far!), a [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth), a fourth [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command), and a fourth [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring).


Out: 3 [Brighthearth Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brighthearth+Banneret), 2 [Fatal Frenzy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Frenzy)  

In: 2 [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath), 1 [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command), 1 [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth), 1 [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring)

**Game 6: zinquox (Blue-Black Aggro)**


Zinquox gets Infathom Infiltrator with [Helm of the Ghastlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helm+of+the+Ghastlord), and it hits me once before I kill it with a topdecked [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate). I then lose a [Stinkdrinker Daredevil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkdrinker+Daredevil) and a [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) to [Nameless Inversion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion), but land a second Daredevil. This allows me to chain into [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath), [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth), and [Sunrise Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunrise+Sovereign), allowing me to swing with some truly massive tramplers for the win.


Record: 4-2


**Game 7: theotherplayer (White-Green Aggro)**


 



![Sunrise Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sunrise+Sovereign)

Theotherplayer curves with [Elvish Hexhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Hexhunter), [Safehold Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safehold+Elite), [Wilt-Leaf Cavaliers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Cavaliers), and [Wilt-Leaf Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege). He then enchants both of his high-end creatures with [Shield of the Oversoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shield+of+the+Oversoul), and I stop at five mana with an [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command) in my hand. [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm) cannot keep me alive long enough for me to find a sixthth mana, and I get run over.

Record: 4-3


**Game 8: Robert\_T (Mono-Red Burn)**


I get stuck at four mana, and Robert\_T gets off double [Wheel of Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fate), burning me to a crisp with burn spell after burn spell after burn spell until I’m finally dead.


Record: 4-4


**Game 9: Rage4Change (Red-Green Aggro)**


Rage4Change drops multiple mana-producing Elves, and then [Valleymaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valleymaker) and large guys. I take them out with [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), or by trading with my “smaller” Giants like [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) or [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm). I start dropping [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath)s and [Sunrise Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunrise+Sovereign)s, ending the game in a heartbeat.


Record: 5-4


I definitely have the big guys, but now I need more big discounts. I reach deep into my pockets to pull out [Coalition Relic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic)s, which usually run between 2.5 and 3 tickets each. Relics are the closest thing to running more Stinkdrink Daredevils in that both cost three, and both effectively “produce” two mana, moving me from four mana (the turn after they are played) to six mana (which is Giant territory, baby!) I also have had slight problems getting to double white for [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command) and [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger), so I take out a single [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) for a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains).


Out: [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command), [Borderland Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Behemoth), [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)  

In: 3 [Coalition Relic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic), [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains)

My initial investments of [Brion Stoutarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brion+Stoutarm) and [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) die to [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). I then decide to play the “one at a time” strategy, and drop [Sunrise Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunrise+Sovereign), burning [Doomed Necromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Necromancer) out of the way with [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) in case there were reanimation shenanigans down the road. I stack the +1/+1 counters ability from the Goliath on the stack, and then the Ranger’s “put three creatures into play” ability. The three Kithkin come into play, I tap all three of them to make my Ranger +2/+0, and this allows my Ranger to give an extra two +1/+1 counters to the [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath)! I swing in with a 19/19, which is blocked for one turn by a duo of [Mirror Entity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) and [True Believer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer) (pumped to 5/5 each) but is lethal the following turn.

Record: 6-4

He then drops [Preeminent Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preeminent+Captain), which I put in the [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring). He plays a fourth ring, basically leaving the board back with his Captain. I drop [Hamletback Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamletback+Goliath), and follow it with [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger). He lets it through for one turn, and a [Sunrise Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunrise+Sovereign) (to make my Hamletback a 20/20 trampler) finishes the game for me the next turn.


Record: 9-6


Big Guys, Big Discounts was a blast to play, and dominated most creature-based decks I faced. Non-control decks are not equipped to deal with 18/18 tramplers, and multiple times that’s how I ended the game: with a humungous force of Giants that could turn the game from 20 to 0 in just one to two combat phases. The deck is a little weak against weenie decks, though the spot removal helps; I might add in a couple of [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm)s if I were going to focus on hosing those decks in particular. Overall though, this was a good deck, and it smashed face... and isn’t that what you want from a Giant deck?
