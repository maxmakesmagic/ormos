Heroic Red
==========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 11, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I've lost to [Dragon Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Mantle) before, but not usually in Standard. When I played against Tom "The Boss" Ross on **Magic Online**, [Dragon Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Mantle) and [Titan's Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titan%27s+Strength) were cast, and I did in fact lose to them. That intrigued me enough to get his list, which he even ended up taking to 1st place at a [StarCityGames](http://www.starcitygames.com) Invitational Qualifier. 


![Akroan Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Akroan+Crusader)
  
 The base of the deck isn't that different from "normal" mono-red, which is to say that it has one-drops, [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary)s, and burn spells. I would have a hard time imagining not playing [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler), Emissary, or [Lightning Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike), and it would appear that Tom Ross agrees. 


 Where it gets interesting are the other spell slots, and what lands Tom chose to play. Seventeen Mountains doesn't seem too interesting, until you realize that there are zero [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault)s and only seventeen lands total, which is a big departure from the norm. Part of the power of this deck is that it plays not a single card that costs more than two, and the majority of the cards only cost one. That prevents mana flood, sets up more explosive draws, and makes the deck very consistent. The thing it does might not be as powerful as casting a [Fanatic of Mogis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fanatic+of+Mogis) for ten, but it does roughly the same thing every time, which is attack for a lot in a very short period of time. 



[Akroan Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroan+Crusader) and [Dragon Mantle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Mantle) are a pretty nice combo, and [Madcap Skills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Madcap+Skills) and [Titan's Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titan%27s+Strength) further push the heroic theme. There's even a singleton [Arena Athlete](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena+Athlete) to benefit from these spells, although it's not nearly as important as the Crusader. Even if you don't draw Crusader, all of the Auras and pump spells give you a great mana-to-damage ratio, and at very low cost.
