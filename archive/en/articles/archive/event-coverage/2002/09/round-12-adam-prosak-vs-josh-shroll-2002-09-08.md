Round 12: Adam Prosak vs. Josh Shroll
=====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 8, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_474.png)
By Eric Taylor











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpcle02/a913.jpg)Two relatively unknown players were at the top of the standings, both playing blue/green. The winner would certainly have a good chance at the Top 8 today. Adam Prosak's best tournament finish so far has been top 8 at the Ohio Regional's and 22nd at Nationals, and while Josh Shroll learned the game a while ago, he has only been playing competitively for six months. 

Josh started the game with a double mulligan and led with double Cephalid Colesium and an Aquameoba which was met with Adam's Catalyst stone. Josh was able to pitch [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) in the grave as he attacked. The next time Josh played an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) all his men would gain flying. Josh was able to cast [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). With [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) in the graveyard, Adam would probably have to break the [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill).

In the blue/green vs. blue/green matchup, if one player gets [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) and the other doesn't it's a huge advantage, perhaps even enough to make up for a horrible double mulligan and double [Cephalid Coliseum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Coliseum)/painland start by Josh. Adam was forced to break the [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) with [Mental Note](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Note), and he made a wry comment about breaking the [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) and giving Josh three cards with such a weak card. Adam followed it up with [Quiet Speculation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation), and filled his graveyard with [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) and then cast one of them.

Josh played his first basic land, a forest, [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) the Wurm, and attacked. 11 to 15 life points in Josh's favor. Josh also made a [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla). On Adam's turn was forced to hard cast [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), having no way to pitch it into the grave, and Josh had yet another [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst). It looked like Josh would be able to win this race now. Josh attacked and on Adam's turn he hard cast [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) yet again, and then made another Wurm token from the graveyard.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpcle02/a916.jpg)The next turn would be the critical turn. Josh attacked with [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) and [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla). Adam blocked the [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) with [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder). Josh flipped the power and toughness of the [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) by pitching a [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm) with madness. After this attack the life score would be 13-4 and the only way for Adam to possibly win would be to somehow get his [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) to die. Josh pumped his [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) before damage to save it. If only he had assigned 1 damage to [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), then pumped the [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla) after damage leaving [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) alive and not in the graveyard, he could have kept nearly all of Adam's men on the ground.

On Adam's next turn he attacked and put the life score 3-4, and then cast [Grizzly Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Fate), tokens which now having flying were able to foil Josh's next all out attack, giving Adam the win.

1-0 advantage Adam Prosak.

Game 2 was brutal. Let me tell you Josh's game: Turn 2 Aquameoba. Turn 3 madness [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), and attack with Aquameoba. Turn 4 make another guy, attack. Turn 5, pitch [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) kill you. Adam cast three or four spells that game, pretending to fight back as he got stomped into dust.

1-1

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpcle02/a917.jpg)"I like [Cephalid Coliseum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Coliseum), [Cephalid Coliseum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Coliseum) better," Adam said.

Game 3 Tim Aten walked up next to Josh to observe the match. Adam commented how bad luck Tim was. Sure enough, a double mulligan on Josh's side and a few turns later the game looked like: Josh with 1 creature in play, 4 lands, and a [Moment's Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace) and a couple other cards in the graveyard, while Adam had two [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel)s, a [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear) with Threshold, five lands in play, [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) in the graveyard and some [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm)s in the graveyard. In a couple of turns, Adam was able to maul his opponent, taking advantage of a double mulligan and being able to go first to attack for the win.

**Final Result:** 2-1 Adam Prosak

In the end, it all turned on that one single error Josh made in the first game. In this block format, especially blue/green on blue/green it's so often whoever wins the die roll to go first or whoever gets [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder) that wins, so it becomes even more critical to try to win the one game in the match where you can outplay your opponent.
