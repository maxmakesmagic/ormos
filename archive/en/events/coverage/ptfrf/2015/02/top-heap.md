Top of the Heap
===============



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 8, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 





We're establishing a much clearer understanding of the new Modern metagame as we wrap up the final day at Pro Tour *Fate Reforged*. Only six archetypes managed to post undefeated records in the Modern portion of the event on Day One: Scapeshift, Affinity, and Living End each had one undefeated pilot. Burn and Infect strategies secured undefeated Modern records for two players each, and various versions of Abzan allowed a whopping six players to start off with five wins through the first day of Modern rounds.



Let's discuss the decks that went undefeated through the Modern rounds on Day One and check in to see how their records have held up through the second day of Modern. 



At this point, Abzan strategies have been discussed a ton. The deck combines the format's best disruption (Thoughtseize and Inquisition of Kozilek) with powerful creatures and removal. The deck has clearly established itself as Modern's new tier one. The most successful Abzan players have tuned their decks to be well-positioned for the mirror match without sacrificing too much against the most aggressive decks. Shady Badran, Carlo Cordero, Jesse Hampton, Anthony Lee, and Vidianto Wijaya all managed undefeated records with the deck through the first five rounds of Modern on Day One.After another five rounds of Modern, these players won an average of 2.5 matches, with Jesse Hampton and Vidianto Wijaya being the big winners with 8-2 overall records through the Modern portion of the event.

Affinity was a strong choice for this weekend. The fastest of all the aggressive decks, Affinity aims to dump a handful of inexpensive or free artifact creatures on the table to end the game as quickly as possible. Even when the opponent has the requisite removal to deal with the initial onslaught, Affinity has eight lands that can attack alongside Cranial Plating to make every threat, however small, into a game ending finisher. Frank Karsten went undefeated with the deck in the first day of competition. The second day wasn't quite as kind to the Dutch Hall of Famer and he ended up with a very respectable 7-3 record in Modern.

Living End is a combo strategy that makes up for its lack of speed with a nearly impossible match-up for non-blue, non-combo decks to beat. The deck aims to fill its graveyard with cycling creatures and Fulminator Mage before it casts a three mana spell with cascade which will always hit the namesake Living End. This will allow the Living End player to wipe the board clean and return an army of awkwardly large bodies to the battlefield. The deck seems like a perfect choice for this weekend after seeing the field and it's no surprise that Living End pilot Stephen Murray achieved an undefeated record through the first five rounds. The decks weakness to other combo strategies and blue decks became a factor in Day Two and Murray ended with a reasonable 7-3 record in Modern.
