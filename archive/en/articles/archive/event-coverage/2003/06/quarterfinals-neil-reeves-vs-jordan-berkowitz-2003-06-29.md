Quarterfinals: Neil Reeves vs. Jordan Berkowitz
===============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 29, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a717.jpg)This Toothpick-mirror featured upstart Jordan Berkowitz versus the originator, Neil Reeves. Fast friends on the Tour, they adopted a master-and-apprentice relationship. Berkowitz inherited Reeves's love of the toothpick, and now they sit across from each other in a battle to make the semifinals. Berkowitz you might remember from his Top 4 at Pro Tour – Venice, while the always-outspoken Reeves recently added the Event Horizons Invitational Championship to his list of accomplishments. The matchup was apparently heavily weighted in Berkowitz's favor.

"This is like Darth Vader versus Luke Skywalker" – Dave Williams

"It's sick, I'm rooting for Neil, but Jordan's rooting for me." – Jon Finkel

"I'm not rooting for anyone." – Jordan Berkowitz

### Game 1

Reeves led with island and cycled [Lonely Sandbar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lonely+Sandbar) after Berkowitz got in with [Basking Rootwalla](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla).

"Hells yeah, Neil! Beat him like he was Tom Guevin!" – Jon Finkel

Reeves played a second land and was done. Berkowitz hit for one and added a [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer). It resolved. [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) took down the Rootwalla at end of turn. Reeves untapped and dropped [Innocent Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood) and [Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion). Berkowitz, unfazed, played [Call of the Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd). Reeves Edicted it away. Berkowitz kept a close eye on how many cards Reeves had in hand. He flashed back his [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call).

Reeves dug once at end of turn and once more on his before playing a fifth land. Berkowitz hit and tried a second [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call). Reeves was ready with [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). Another Lavamancer joined the fray. Reeves's [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) gave him an [Innocent Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood), but Berkowitz would get another hit with his Elephant. He went one better, serving up [Blistering Firecat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat) and bringing Reeves down to just four life.

"Of course you draw the one Cat." – Neil Reeves

Reeves [Concentrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concentrate)d, leaving three mana up. He played [Zombie Infestation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Infestation) and was done. Berkowitz dropped two [Violent Eruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Eruption)s on the table. Reeves showed no concern. Berkowitz picked them up and tried one of them. [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) gave him a zombie to chump the incoming token. Now things started to click for Reeves. He Blooded away the token, and [Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion)ed a [Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic) on the second Eruption. [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) left Berkowitz without cards. Reeves [Cunning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)ed for [Opportunity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opportunity), untapped, and drew four. Berkowitz played another Elf.

He did rip a [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call) the following turn, but Reeves had [Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic) for the first half, and then Blood and flashback Edict to clear the board. Berkowitz was out of plays, and Reeves was flush with cards. They played draw-go for a few turns, and by the time Reeves found his [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) he didn't want to risk Berkowitz having the [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). He worked his [Compulsion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) and card drawing, looking for his [Cunning Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish). Three cards from the bottom he found it. He searched out [Circular Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic), and then made with the [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). He floated two extra mana and burned for it, but was still at two with eight zombies on the table, and the insurance [Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic) in hand.

"You have no outs." – Neil Reeves

Reeves 1 – Berkowitz 0

### Game 2

Both players sideboarded quickly. Their late-night playtesting meant they knew their strategies well in advance. Berkowitz led with a Lavamancer that was Blooded away, and then resolved a [Compost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost). His [Call of the Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) met [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), but Berkowitz's [Anger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger) resolved. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a718.jpg)Reeves made no move. Berkowitz kept hitting. Lavamancer and [Call of the Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) hit the table. Reeves [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother)ed both, giving Berkowitz more cards. He sent a [Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer) to the face and made a Rootwalla. Reeves could only [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) again.

Berkowitz's [Compost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost) was keeping him in threats, so Reeves needed a big one. He got [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), and played it, turning up two blanks. He shrugged and flashed it back, falling to five and tapping out. Berkowitz untapped, swung, and sent Eruption to the face.

Reeves 1 – Berkowitz 0

"They can't hear us because we're not on camera, right? I don't care. I don't feel like talking." – Jordan Berkowitz

"You don't care? You've asked four times if we're on camera or not. Savage, savage lies." – Neil Reeves

### Game 3

Berkowitz was first on the board with a Rootwalla. He swung in and pumped, but Reeves had the [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother). Reeves untapped and [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ed. He didn't like what he saw: [Phantom Centaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Centaur), two [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call)s, [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer), [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt), and a third land. He shrugged and took a [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call). Berkowitz untapped and played the other. Reeves had a steady stream of creature removal at the ready.

Berkowitz's library was up to the task. First it gave him the fourth land for his [Call of the Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd). Then came another [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call). Reeves fought through, but Berkowitz had drawn more burn. And then there was Rootwalla. Reeves kept pace. He had [Logic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic) for the [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call) on the way back, and suddenly Berkowitz was back down to just the Centaur in hand. Reeves played [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and passed it back. Berkowitz drew and played [Anger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger), swinging for two.

"You're sooooo good at this game." – Neil Reeves

"Is that guy in?" – Jordan Berkowitz

"I'm thin'in'..." – Neil Reeves

He allowed it, then Edicted it away and flashed back the Analysis, falling to eight. Berkowitz untapped and flashed back the [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call). Reeves [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)ed. Berkowitz sent [Volcanic Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) to the face, causing Reeves to roll his eyes in frustration.

"Will you ever draw a land?" – Neil Reeves

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a719.jpg)He [Concentrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concentrate)d, pulling up three duds. He cycled [Lonely Sandbar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lonely+Sandbar), earning a momentary reprieve from [Hibernation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hibernation). Berkowitz played a [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) after combat. Reeves untapped and tried to bluff like he had something. Berkowitz cast his Centaur and smashed in.

"Seven spells in a row is pretty good. Especially [Anger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger). He had no business winning that game. No business." – Neil Reeves

Berkowitz 2 – Reeves 1

### Game 4

Again Berkowitz led with Rootwalla and pumped for damage while Reeves played a pair of islands. He played a swamp and [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ed, seeing [Compost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost), Rootwalla, [Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer) and [Violent Eruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Violent+Eruption).

"I'll take the, uh, whoa." – Neil Reeves

He snagged the compost, but watched in disgust as Berkowitz pulled a [Call of the Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd). He didn't have the tools he needed to win, and after drawing [Future Sight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight), sat there as his deck refused to give him the fifth land he needed. It came down a turn too late.

"This is actually the stone-cold nuts against you. If I'd drawn the fifth land last turn, you couldn't possibly win. 'Course now, you're gonna beat me." – Neil Reeves

This last statement was true. Reeves extended the hand the following turn.

**Final Result:** Jordan Berkowitz defeats Neil Reeves 3-1
