Saturday, 10:38 a.m. – What Would Frank Karsten Play?
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

Two weeks ago, I competed in Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* as a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame. Today, I will bring you all the latest updates on the evolving Modern metagame as a member of the event coverage team. To start off, let me tell you which deck(s) I would consider playing if I would be participating in the Grand Prix, and why.


If I would just want to have fun, then I would simply play my [funky Mono Red Highlander deck](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptrtr12/WhoWantstoLiveForever) again, with the dream of becoming the first player to make Top 8 at a Grand Prix with a full Singleton deck. Now, I won't be doing the Singleton challenge this weekend, but I know at least one player who will be. Exciting!


If I would want to maximize my chances of winning the Grand Prix instead, then I would let go of the self-imposed Singleton restriction, but I would still go for the burn spells. Why? Well, I went 5-5 in Seattle despite running some iffy cards, while the only other burn pilot in Seattle (Egor Alashev) posted a 7-3 record. To me, that indicates that Mono Red Burn has solid matchups across the field. Turn 4 kills are common with the deck, and even turn 3 kills are possible when the opponent is dealing a lot of damage to himself with his lands. And while I was playing my matches in Seattle, I felt like Mono Red had an advantage against Jund, which will likely be the most popular deck in Lyon as well. Jund has so many bad cards against Mono Red: [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) in particular will not help them when their face is being burned. In addition, most Jund decks are shying away from [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) these days. For reference, this was Egor Alashev's list from Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*:

Okay, so technically this is not a Mono Red deck since it splashes [Bump in the Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night) , but that is one of the reddest black cards of all-time, so Mono Red still feels like an accurate nomenclature.


I would take Egor's list as my starting point, although I might make a few tweaks. First, I might want [Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) maindeck (probably over [Flames of the Blood Hand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flames+of+the+Blood+Hand) ) to deal with Infect creatures, [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) . After all, [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) removing your [Hellspark Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellspark+Elemental) is quite devastating. Second, I would prefer [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix) over [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) in my sideboard. Both cards deal with [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) or [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo, but [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix) doubles as an answer to the Second Breakfast deck that has just won Pro Tour Ravnica. Thirdly, I would like to add a few [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) s to the sideboard. Bob provides a repetitive source of damage and cards; this gives you game against [Leyline of Sanctity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) and can help grind back against life-gain decks such as Soul Sisters.


There are various other decks available that, on the surface, appear to have a good matchup against Jund -- Green-Red Tron comes to mind -- but the classic turn 1 " [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) , [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) you" opening is just too much maniacal fun to pass up.


Okay, one more list. If I would have had sufficient time to tweak a novel deck, I would have been working on this brew in the days leading up to the Grand Prix:

[Form of the Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon) is the key attraction, as it seems good versus Jund. A hardcasted [Form of the Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon) will typically win the game by itself, as most Jund decks don't run [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) anymore (partly because it's weak in the mirror match). If you resolve an [Enduring Ideal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Ideal) , the plan would be to go for the "lock" of [Form of the Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon) with [Phyrexian Unlife](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Unlife) .


Second Breakfast and other combo decks are dead to stuff like [Dovescape](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape) , [Leyline of Sanctity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) , [Runed Halo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runed+Halo) , and [Stony Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence) . It is also worth pointing out that [Echoing Truth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) from combo players' sideboards won't help them a lot against this onslaught of different 1-ofs. You can beat Affinity and Infect with a turn 3 Night of Soul's [Betrayal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Betrayal) .


Will the turn 1 [Utopia Sprawl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Sprawl) into turn 2 [Wargate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wargate) for [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) plan work? Will [Idyllic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Idyllic+Tutor) for [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) of [Porphyry Nodes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porphyry+Nodes) buy enough time? How often will [Shadow of Doubt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+of+Doubt) steal a game against an unsuspecting opponent? Is the deck fast enough for the Modern format? I frankly don't know as this list is completely untested, but I do believe that a deck like this might have potential. Here's to hoping that someone else will be winning with [Form of the Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon) today!




Saturday, 10:44 a.m. – Trial Winning Decks
------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

In theory Grand Prix start on Saturday, and the main event does indeed. In practice, however, the hall was already packed with players on Friday, slinging their Modern decks of choice in one of the many Grand Prix Trials.


The winning decks, listed below, traditionally give a first impression of what to expect over the course of the weekend. In this case, expect a lot of Jund, followed by Affinity and Infect, and most of all: be prepared for an exuberant variety of decks! Will they continue to perform as well at the actual Grand Prix? We'll see.
