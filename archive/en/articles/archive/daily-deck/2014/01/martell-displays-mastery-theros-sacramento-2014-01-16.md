Martell Displays Mastery of Theros in Sacramento
================================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 19, 2014 










![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpsac14/martell_winner.jpg)  
No. 13 Ranked Player Tom Martell has done it again. 

 The Pro Tour *Gatecrash* Champion put up impressive finishes in his games all weekend here at Grand Prix Sacramento, but it was his Top 8 performance that truly showed off his mastery at *Theros* Limited. Martell's black-white deck featured an impressive range of creatures and plays. While his deck could take an early game lead thanks to his solid heroic creatures, his cards were also capable of locking up a win should the game go late. 

 And it didn't matter whether his games went early or late in the Top 8. Martell battled his way through three rounds in the Top 8 playoff, winning games that looked far from salvageable for the Northern California resident. Martell's victory in Sacramento marks his second Grand Prix victory, making this his third major **Magic** win alongside his victories at Grand Prix Indianapolis 2012 and at Pro Tour *Gatecrash*. 

 Sacramento has continued to show that *Theros* evolves with each major event. Card evaluations have changed, and new or undervalued draft strategies have taken center stage this weekend. Martell's Top 8 performance with black-white has given new life to the color combination as one of the format's most solid Limited archetypes. 

Congratulations once again to Tom Martell on his victory, and to all those who helped Grand Prix Sacramento become California's largest ever Grand Prix.

*by Jacob Van Lunen*
  
 Tom Martell is currently ranked 13th in the world on the Top 25 Pro Rankings. This is Martell's fifth Grand Prix Top 8. Coming into the Top 8 Draft here in Sacramento, Martell strongly favored Blue/White Heroic as an archetype. Let's see how things went for the Pro Tour *Gatecrash* Champion. 

 The first pack had [Triad of Fates](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triad+of+Fates), but spending a first pick on a two-color card is a risky proposition. [Staunch-Hearted Warrior](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staunch-Hearted+Warrior) and [Observant Alseid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Observant+Alseid) made their way to the front of the pack and Martell eventually settled on [Observant Alseid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Observant+Alseid).

- ### Quarterfinal – Aaron Lewis vs. Andy Voellmer

*by Mike Rosenberg*
  
Aaron Lewis, who earned his first Grand Prix Top 8 at Grand Prix Charlotte last year, hails from Madison, Wisconsin. His opponent, Andy Voellmer, made a short drive from Berkeley, California down this weekend to compete. The result for his decision is a qualification for Pro Tour Journey Into Nyx and his first Grand Prix Top 8. 

**The Decks**

 Aaron Lewis has the often underrated blue-red archetype, along with many of the strategy's key cards. [Flamespeaker Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamespeaker+Adept) and [Aqueous Form](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aqueous+Form) work very well together, and both are found in Lewis's deck. However, it isn't just these synergies that Lewis can fall back on, as [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) gives him access to a very powerful rare that can keep his hand stocked with goodies, including quick aggressive creatures as well as removal. 

 Andy Voellmer's deck is a little slower, but it can pack a punch. His green-black archetype has plenty of solid green creatures, along with some solid black removal and heavy-hitting creatures like [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel). 

**The Games**

 Lewis wasted no time in getting an early lead with [Spearpoint Oread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spearpoint+Oread) into [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) getting no major resistence from Voellmer, whose first play of the game was an [Insatiable Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insatiable+Harpy). Lewis dwarfed the play even further when he dumped more creatures onto the table with Wavecrash Titan and [Akroan Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroan+Crusader). 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpsac14/qf_lewis.jpg)*Aaron Lewis*  
 Voellmer turned the life tides a bit with [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel), but he was staring down a dangerous threat in the form of [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa). Lewis continued his card drawing with [Aqueous Form](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aqueous+Form) on the [Wavecrash Triton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wavecrash+Triton), locking down the Harpy, and then a second Form on [Akroan Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroan+Crusader), giving him creatures that couldn't be blocked. As a result, Voellmer had little to stop Lewis from re-stocking his hand. 

[Dissolve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissolve) on [Anthousa, Setessan Hero](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anthousa%2C+Setessan+Hero) a few turns later left Voellmer without any real pressure, and he ultimately succumbed to the avalanche of card draw that the [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) provided Lewis all game. 

 Voellmer led with [Flamecast Wheel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamecast+Wheel) into [Nessian Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nessian+Courser) in the second game, while Lewis's first play was [Wavecrash Triton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wavecrash+Triton). [Flamespeaker Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamespeaker+Adept) and [Aqueous Form](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aqueous+Form) on the Triton followed, locking down a freshly played [Voyaging Satyr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyaging+Satyr). The enchanted Triton and Adept made quite a combo, with the scry from [Aqueous Form](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aqueous+Form) triggering the Adept on each attack. [Minotaur Skullcleaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minotaur+Skullcleaver) came down with those creatures on the next turn, crashing Voellmer to 11. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpsac14/qf_voellmer.jpg)*Andy Voellmer*  
[Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) followed after that, and Voellmer, who was stuck on green mana only, struggled to keep up. He found a Swamp, his fourth land, on the next turn, which allowed him to pop his [Flamecast Wheel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamecast+Wheel) targeting the Adept, but Lewis had [Voyage's End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyage%27s+End) to save his creature. The Adept came down on the next turn, and a sequence of a second [Aqueous Form](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aqueous+Form), a [Rage of Purphoros](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+of+Purphoros), and two unblockable attacks earned Lewis the game and the match. 

"I guess I'll see you at the Pro Tour!" Voellmer said, offering the handshake. 

**Lewis 2 – Voellmer 0**

- ### Quarterfinal – Adam Mancuso vs. Eric Pei

*by Mike Rosenberg*
  
This Quarterfinals match featured two Californians facing off in their first Grand Prix Top 8. Adam Mancuso hailed from Santa Monica, while Eric Pei was just a short drive away from San Francisco. 

**The Decks**

 Eric Pei's blue-white heroic deck was capable of some very aggressive starts, thanks in part to some solid two-mana creatures like [Battlewise Hoplite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Hoplite) and [Phalanx Leader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phalanx+Leader). However, it also had access to some large blockers with multiple copies of [Coastline Chimera](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coastline+Chimera), a solid flying blocker that could hold back creatures while his heroic creatures stayed on offense. 

 Mancuso's deck, on the other hand, was much more about big ground creatures. His green-red concoction was capable of some brutal starts thanks to Two-Headed Chimera and his bestow creatures, but the deck also had access to a nasty mythic in the form of [Xenagos, the Reveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+the+Reveler). 

**The Games**

 Pei was first onto the table with [Battlewise Hoplite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Hoplite), which promptly ate a [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike) from Mancuso, who followed up that turn with [Agent of Horizons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agent+of+Horizons). However, Pei's next creatures were [Phalanx Leader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phalanx+Leader) and [Heliod's Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%27s+Emissary), which backed Mancuso against a wall early on. [Traveling Philosopher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveling+Philosopher) and [Chosen by Heliod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chosen+by+Heliod) soon came down, growing Pei's army. 

![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy//mtg/images/daily/events/gpsac14/qf_mancuso.jpg)*Adam Mancuso*  
 Unable to stop the board, Mancuso did what he could, trading when available and deploying a very large [Vulpine Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulpine+Goliath) onto the table. However, the [Heliod's Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%27s+Emissary) made blocking effectively moot, and the [Phalanx Leader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phalanx+Leader) duo powered through for a first game win. 

 Mancuso led off the second game with [Leafcrown Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leafcrown+Dryad) into [Nessian Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nessian+Courser), the Dryad held back by Pei's second-turn [Bronze Sable](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bronze+Sable). The artifact creature was joined by [Wingsteed Rider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingsteed+Rider) on the next turn. Mancuso sent in the [Nessian Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nessian+Courser) for 3, then aimed [Lightning Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Strike) at [Wingsteed Rider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingsteed+Rider). It was replaced with [Battlewise Hoplite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Hoplite) after an attack with the Sable on the next turn. Mancuso cast [Nylea's Disciple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%27s+Disciple) pre-combat, then sent in his creatures, attacking for 5. Pei had [Fate Foretold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Foretold) for his [Battlewise Hoplite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewise+Hoplite), growing it and drawing a card. [Traveling Philosopher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveling+Philosopher) was the only follow-up after that. 

 The Hoplite traded with the [Nylea's Disciple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%27s+Disciple) on the next attack from Mancuso, and it was replaced with heliod's Emissary on the next turn.  Tom Martell is currently ranked 13th in the Top 25 Pro Rankings. This weekend is the Pro Tour *Gatecrash* winner's fifth Grand Prix Top 8. His opponent, Ryan Miller, is a California Native in his first Grand Prix Top 8.

Miller, at a lowly 2 life, couldn't find an answer on the top of his deck and was forced to concede after chump blocking for a turn.
