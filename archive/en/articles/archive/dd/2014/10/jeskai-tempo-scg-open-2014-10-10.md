Jeskai Tempo from SCG Open
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on October 10, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 





The last deck for this week is  [one from the StarCityGames.com Open in New Jersey](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=73324), a Jeskai deck run by Jadine Klomparens.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Seeker of the Way)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seeker of the Way)
It's important to note from the outset that it's not a burn deck—it's a tempo deck. It doesn't try to win by throwing fire at the opponent's face; it invests in creatures as the main damage source. There's [Mantis Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mantis+Rider), [Goblin Rabblemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Rabblemaster), and...[Seeker of the Way](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seeker+of+the+Way)? That may seem a puzzling choice on the surface, but in this deck, it is a masterpiece.


Don't dismiss it as a mere 2/2 for two mana; if your trigger prowess on your turn it essentially increases the damage of each of your spells by 1, and the lifegain is a non-negligible contribution for a tempo deck, giving it a benefit from the inevitable creature fights. It is better to think of this as a two-mana 3/3 with lifelink.


This creature acts in many roles, both aggressive and defensive—it can stall the board, which buys time to draw additional firepower to throw at the opponent, and if your opponent tries to break through by using elimination on it, well, that's one less card the opponent has to use on the bigger and better creatures.


Not bad for a "mere" two-mana creature, eh?
