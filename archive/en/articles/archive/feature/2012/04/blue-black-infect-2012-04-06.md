Blue-Black Infect
=================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 6, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






Poison is back in Standard! Although poison has been fairly absent in Standard recently ([Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) aside), **Magic Online** player Sopet has found quite a bit of success in recent Daily Events with a unique blue-black poison decklist.


This deck aims to get a quick poison start, leading off with [Blighted Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent), [Plague Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plague+Myr), [Phyrexian Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Crusader), and the ubiquitous [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). Once the deck has established an early board presence, [Mutagenic Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) and [Livewire Lash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Livewire+Lash) help surge the size of its creatures to maximize its poison output.


[Livewire Lash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Livewire+Lash) is a card that is particularly strong in Sopet's deck. The creature that the Lash is attached to deals the damage—doling two poison counters out every time it's targeted by a spell. If you just so happen to have a [Mutagenic Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutagenic+Growth) or two, [Livewire Lash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Livewire+Lash) might just become lethal!


What's really unique about Sopet's version, though, is that, on top of the poison engine, the deck also features control elements! [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) can help you play a tempo or control game, and [Curse of Death's Hold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) helps get token blockers out of the way. Also at the top end is the relatively unplayed [Corrupted Conscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupted+Conscience)—an excellent way to deal with an opposing Titan or Praetor!


Poison has a lot of fans who have been looking to return it to Standard popularity. If you've been looking for a fun, powerful, and relatively budget-friendly deck to check out for Friday Night **Magic** tonight, take a look at Sopet's decklist!
