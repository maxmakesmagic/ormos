DRAFTING WITH HALL OF FAMER MAKAHITO MIHARA
===========================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX SHIZUOKA 2015](/en/events/coverage/gpshi15)
 on January 10, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten










Freshly minted Hall of Famer Makahito Mihara, who has five Pro Tour Top 8s to his name, is one of Japan's greats. At the start of the second draft of the day, he is sitting at a 10-2 record, which means that he is still live for Top 8. Let's see his picks!

Mihara's early picks were relatively straightforward, taking the best card from the pack early on and then slowly but steadily moving into green-black. The third [Rite of the Serpent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+the+Serpent) was perhaps one six-drop too many, but as he hadn't drafted many non-defender creatures yet, [Awaken the Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awaken+the+Bear) didn't look so hit either.



At the end of the first booster, I peeked at Mihara's neighbors. The player to his left was drafting Temur, and the player to his right was drafting Mardu. So, although Mihara didn't know this with certainty yet, both green/black clans would be open to him. Yet, he would have to keep in mind that he didn't have any mana fixers yet.

Early on in the second pack, Mihara stuck to his guns and couldn't be swayed into a third color. He kept on taking high-toughness creatures that would work well with his [Kin-Tree Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kin-Tree+Invocation) and [Death Frenzy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Frenzy). When the black/green cards dried up, he took some speculative, non-committal picks from Abzan and Sultai, keeping his options open for a splash color. But why choose one splash color when you can have both?

[Abzan Falconer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Falconer) was good enough to sway Mihara into a third color. His subsequent picks were made with his deck in mind: While [Ruthless Ripper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruthless+Ripper), [Dragonscale Boon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonscale+Boon), and [Savage Punch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Punch) are high picks for most decks, they didn't fit his deck very well. After all, he would kill his own [Ruthless Ripper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruthless+Ripper) with [Death Frenzy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Frenzy); he already had a [Dragonscale Boon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonscale+Boon) and wouldn't enter combat all that often; and he didn't have many big creatures for [Savage Punch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Punch). Instead, Mihara added [Mer-Ek Nightblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mer-Ek+Nightblade), [Tranquil Cove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tranquil+Cove), and [Abzan Battle Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Battle+Priest) to his deck, offering a stronger late-game presence and a mana-fixer for a late-pick [Abomination of Gudul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abomination+of+Gudul).
