### Round 8 Feature Match: Chang-Sern Kim vs. Robert Liu


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*
We ended day one with four undefeated players, one of whom was the quite diminutive 12 year-old Chang-Sern Kim, who'd be facing fellow undefeated player, Robert Liu to start day two.


"I thought you picked this match because we're both minors," Liu laughed, "I'm 15!"


I didn't have the heart to tell either of them that there's rather a substantial difference between the ages 12 and 15.


"I've played this match-up on **Magic Online** a lot, it's not good for me," Liu told Kim as they shuffled.


"You know what I'm playing?!"


"We sat next to each other all day yesterday!"


"Such a stalker," Kim chided.


Kim's aggressive Selesnya deck would be looking to come screaming out of the gates to catch Liu's Blue-White-Red midrange deck unprepared. If Liu could hold him off long enough, he'd be able to overwhelm Kim with his [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s and [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s.


Kim won the die roll, and was attacking on turn two with a [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor)'d [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim). Liu used [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) and [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) to blunt Kim's assault, before finally landing a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) at the end of Kim's turn.


Liu had an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) for Kim's [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), and it seemed like Kim was out of gas. Liu wasted no time flashing back a [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) with [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and then again with a second one to finish game one before Kim could get back in the driver's seat.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc12/02_R8_Kim.JPG)  
*Chang-Sern Kim can tell that "[Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), flashing back [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear), targeting you" means he's not winning game one.*

Liu 1 – Kim 0


### Game 2


Kim came out even faster in game two, with a turn two [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter). Liu again had an [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) to send it back. Kim summoned an [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) to go with his [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), but missed his third land drop.


The [Smite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite)r came back, and Liu locked it down with [Dungeon Geists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists). Kim found a third land, and between that and his two elves, summoned a [Sigarda, Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons). Liu heaved a sigh at the hexproof'd Angel, summoning a second [Dungeon Geists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) to tap down the [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf).


Sigarda attacked, and Liu frowned in thought, before finally deciding to double block. Kim didn't have the [Selesnya Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm), so Sigarda traded with the Geist holding down the [Smite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite)r, and Kim passed back the turn. When Liu attacked with his remaining Geist, Kim tried to summon a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), but Kim had an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) to let his Geist continue applying ghostly fist to face.


The [Smite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite)r attacked, but Kim only managed another Pilgrim after combat, so Liu [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)'d the [Smite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite)r and again attacked with his Geist. Kim could only summon a third Pilgrim, and sigh.


"Do you have another [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)?" Liu asked, before attacking.


"Yep, sure," Kim replied, but Liu had a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to rebuy his [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), keeping the Angel off the table.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc12/02_R8_Liu.JPG)  
*With the early game behind him, Robert Liu is feeling much more confident about his chances in this match.*

[Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) brought Kim back up to 14 life, but the Geists knocked him right back to 11. Kim tried to [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) his 'Tusk, but Liu [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear)'d it in response, ensuring the [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) would not come back. Kim followed up with another [Smite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite)r.


Liu thundered into his attack step with a [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite), dropping Kim to just 3 life. Kim attacked back, but didn't have enough to finish off Liu, who was happy to throw his [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) under whichever bus looked the biggest.


"Yeah, I'm screwed," the 12 year-old laughed, as he picked up his cards.


Robert Liu defeats Chang-Sern Liu 2 – 0

### Sunday, 2:15 p.m. - Deck Tech: NuiSéance with Dylan Brown


*by Pip Foweraker*
One interesting deck making waves this weekend is a W/G/B/R Reanimator deck featuring Séance, a pile of utility creatures and a top end that includes such hits as [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) and [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth).


Originally built by Jacob Dunn and Dylan Brown, the pair wanted to find a combo-based solution to the emerging format. They worked extensively on the deck, developing it throughout the spoiler season. While the basic premise of the deck remained unchanged, it went through several colour iterations, including one version with blue, piloted by their mutual friend Lachlan Ward-Smith to a breakout 4-0 on a recent MTGO Daily.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc12/R10_Decktech_Brown.JPG)  
*Jacob Dunn and Dylan Brown love brewing with Séance*

Brown has had a good run with it so far, and decided on the deck after only minimal testing, when they realised it was 'just beating everything'. The deck posts good results against Jund, whose non-interactive starts allow the deck to go about its business mostly unhindered. In any match that boils down to the '[Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) mirror', Nuiséance is playing 'more' [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)s. And nearly every deck has issues with [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth), particularly control, whose answers are often too slow. The deck occasionally has issues with Zombies, but finds them less of a challenge after sideboarding.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc12/R10_Decktech_Tokens.JPG)  
*The most adorable Séance tokens you're ever likely to see.  

From left to right:  

Top row: Avacyn's Pilgrim, Acidic Slime, Trostani, Selesnya's Voice, Tragtusk.  

Middle row: Angel of Glory's Rise, Centaur Healer, Craterhoof Behemoth  

Bottom row: 3/3 Beast tokens, Angel of Serenity, Zealous Conscripts*

Changes? Brown would make some minor changes to his sideboard, taking out the Temblors for some [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls), which are more effective against control matchups. The maindeck he is 'pretty happy' with, although any good deck is always a work in progress.

### Sunday, 2:45 p.m. - Deck Tech: Mono Red with Ivan Magoč


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*
Since relocating from Wellington to Sydney, Ivan Magoč hasn't had a great deal of time to play **Magic**, so he leapt at the chance to come over to Auckland to hang out with his friends from Wellington, Blair Gray and Mark Tung, who had just the deck for him: Mono Red.


"It's easy to pick up and play, which I needed," Magoč explained. "It's really good against control. You have plenty of 1-drops, Haste guys, and burn."


But what about [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), the Elephant Monstrosity Beast in the room.


"Yeah, [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) is really good against this deck, but of the 10 [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) decks I've played this weekend, I've beaten 8 of them," he boasted with a smile. "[Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) is the best card in the deck, it's criminally under-played right now."



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc12/R11_Decktech.JPG)  
*Ivan Magoč is [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) Tough.*

But that's not all Magoč has up his sleeve against the 5/3 lifegain menace. Standing out like a sore thumb on his deck list is 3x [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf).


"We had one in the sideboard during the Grand Prix Trials on Friday, and it was amazing. Playing creatures on turn 1 and 2, and following up with the Wolf is great. We can have trouble with the many 3 toughness blockers running around, but there's usually only one of them on defence, so the Wolf means it's practically game over. I took out 2 [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) and a land to fit them in the maindeck."


Magoč admitted that if he were to change anything about the deck, it would be that there wasn't much reason to be mono-colored. "The mono Red removal isn't great, and playing Black for something like [Sever the Bloodline](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sever+the+Bloodline) would really help break some of the ground-stalls that can happen. The sideboard is also really loose, I would have run a third [Thunderbolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderbolt) if I could've found one."



### Round 11 Feature Match - Dan Unwin vs. Gene Brumby


*by Pip Foweraker*
The only problem I have with covering these players is that their banter is too fast and witty for me to accurately catch while they play. So, for the next few paragraphs, assume that they're firing quips while playing with a sophistication that would put Tarantino to shame. Brumby and Unwin are two players at the top of their game. Both well known locally and internationally,


### Game 1


A pair of mulligans left the banter continue while shuffle times were had. Unwin missed his third land drop, while Brumby kept up his pace with a [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) and a [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells). Unwin found a green source to let him [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), while Brumby simply bashed. He followed up with a [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), and Unwin scooped his cards up when the top of his deck yielded a blank.


Gene Brumby 1 – Dan Unwin 0

Brumby summoned a [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer), while Unwin had a [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) to power up the board. Garruk drew Unwin some cards, and Brumby pondered his options. After a [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), Brumby passed, and Unwin began his aggression with his [Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast) token.

Brumby had nothing next turn, allowing Unwin to attack for lethal and move the game to a decider.


Dan Unwin 1 – Gene Brumby 1

Brumby attacked and Unwin declined to block. Brumby had a [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) to take care of the Sigarda, while Unwin took out Tamiyo with a [Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play). Brumby passed with no action and Unwin summoned a [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter), who made a [Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast)ly friend to keep him company. Dan Unwin 2 – Gene Brumby 1

At the end of Mines' turn, Denz used an Isolated Lighthouse to draw, hitting miraculous [Entreat the Angels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entreat+the+Angels) for 2, and then another on his draw step for 8. Mines' long-held final card turned out to be a [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned), which he could tap out for and play for exactly 4. He swung with his Keyrune, but Denz had an [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) to kill the attacker.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc12/02_R13_Denz.JPG)  
*When 9 Angels just aren't enough...*

Both players hunted for threats, Denz on 2, Mines on 17. An [Armada Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armada+Wurm) demanded an answer. [Terminus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) arrived. Denz followed with a [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage), tapping down Mines' land. Denz, needing to finish the game, went digging through his deck and happened across his third [Entreat the Angels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entreat+the+Angels), netting himself 3 hard-cast tokens. Mines picked one off, but couldn't answer the others fast enough as they swooped in to steal the first game.


John Denz 1 – David Mines 0


### Game 2


Mines started the second game with a [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), then followed it with a [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk). Denz had an [Entreat the Angels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entreat+the+Angels) for a single token, which left him with only a single mana open. Mines had a [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) for 4, and chose to leave his [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) home for the time being.


After a pass from Denz, Mines tried a second [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) for 2. Mines flashed back his [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), leaving him with a single card in hand. Mines cast a [Sever the Bloodline](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sever+the+Bloodline) to kill off the Angel token, then attacked with his [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk). Denz had a [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) to kill it off, and then an [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) to deal with the token. Denz's deck yielded him a [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought), while Denz looked for more pressure from his library.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpauc12/02_R13_Mines.JPG)  
*Despite digging for several turns, Mines fails to unearth an answer.*

A [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) was, perhaps, not what he was seeking. Denz assembled some powerful blue Planeswalkers, while Denz fell further behind in card advantage. He drew and played a [Dreadbore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore) on a Tamiyo, but there was still a [Jace, Memory Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept) to deal with. With cards in his library falling perilously low – two, in fact – Mines cast and resolved a [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) for 7, wiping his opponent's hand and killing the Jace. Mines flashed in a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) One card left in library. Hold breath. Draw. Shake head. Extend hand. Denz makes it through to the Top 8, and Mines is cast out\* to the wolves that have been circling the venue for the last few days, howling hungrily....


John Denz 2 – David Mines 0
