Rietzl Bumps His Way to the Top
===============================




March 24-25, 2012













GRAND PRIX MEXICO CITY 2012
---------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmex12/champion.jpg)

As all the tables and chairs get packed away and the ardent local crowd finally dissipates into the city night, we're finally able to catch our breath from the long, hectic second day of Grand Prix Mexico City. It looked like Josh Utter-Leyton, who was undefeated until Round 11 was going to clean house. But as the final rounds came to close, it was another American, Paul Rietzl, who was able to take home the trophy. In the finals, Rietzl met up with the only person who beat him in the Draft portion of the event, Venezuela's Humberto Patarca and took him down.


Rietzl's MVP for the final match was a card he had listed as the worst card in his Sealed deck – the deceptive yet blunt, [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night). Locals David Hernandez, Mario Flores, and Gustavo Nuñez rounded out a Top 8 with Craig Wescoe, Gottlieb Yeh and Pascal Maynard who all grabbed a couple more Pro Points going into the ever-approaching World **Magic** Cup. However, it's Rietzl who to be the happiest. His performance today got him up to 32 point, inching him closer towards the coveted Pro Player Club's ultimate level – Platinum.


It was a great weekend and from all of us here in Mexico City, have a fabulous night!

Top 8 Drafting with Paul Rietzl
-------------------------------


*by Ben Stark* 


![Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pyreheart+Wolf)

Paul Rietzl's draft started off with a tough choice between [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) and [Farbog Boneflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farbog+Boneflinger). I consider [Farbog Boneflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farbog+Boneflinger) to be the better card, but [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) has grown on me in recent weeks. During one of our first practice drafts for Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*, Conley Woods second-picked the Wolf. At the time, it seemed way too early, but now I think it was genius. [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) is a rock star in Green-Red Werewolves (one of the premier archetypes in **Dark Ascension*/*Innistrad** draft), and is consequently well-worth an early pick. Paul, however, went with the [Farbog Boneflinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Farbog%2BBoneflinger) because it "seemed safer," though he wasn't sure about it.


Next, Paul took a [Fires of Undeath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Undeath) over [Scorned Villager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorned+Villager). This pick was much clearer, as I consider Fires the best common in this draft format – and it is way better than the Villager. Obviously following up a Boneflinger made the pick that much easier.



![Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gravecrawler)

Third Pick brought [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), which is a little early, but about right. The Zombie is definitely not a first pick. In this case, the 2/1 fit perfectly with Fires and Boneflinger and he only came at the cost of some mediocre green commons.


Sadly, That's is pretty much the story of green in D-I-I draft. In *Dark Ascension*, the green commons are fairly unexciting. So if you fall into green, it's usually because the color is open late in the pack, not because the cards pull you in early.


From here, Paul just kept taking black and red cards for the rest of the pack. It was very obvious Craig Wescoe (seated to his right) wasn't heavy in either color and there was a good chance Humberto Patarca (seated to his left) was going to play white, as he took [Loyal Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Cathar) second out of Paul's opening pack.



![Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brimstone+Volley)

Going into Pack 2, Paul wanted to keep drafting more of the same, but prioritize removal and fleshing out his creature curve. He first-picked [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley), a card I consider to be the best overall common in *Innistrad*. Second, he took [Markov Patrician](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Markov+Patrician) over a [Village Ironsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Ironsmith) and [Scourge of Geier Reach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+of+Geier+Reach). The pack had a lot of playables – specifically a lot of red playables. By taking the only black card over of one of the many red ones, Paul had a better chance to wheel a good red card. He third-picked [Altar's Reap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar%27s+Reap) for its synergy with [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and potentially with some [Traitorous Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitorous+Blood)s to come in the future.


Rietzl almost went to time on his next pick. He had to decide between [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) and [Heretic's Punishment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heretic%27s+Punishment). Paul and I both agree that neither card is fantastic and Paul thought that since his deck was a little slow already, the [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Morkrut%2BBanshee) was correct. Unlike [Heretic's Punishment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heretic%27s+Punishment), the 4/4 impacts the board the turn you play it. Normally, the Punishment is right because Black-Red has a pretty low curve and needs finishers. But in this case, Paul already had Boneflinger and Fires, so his deck needed some type of speed. I think this pick was absolutely correct.



![Traitorous Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Traitorous+Blood)

Paul rounded out the rest of the pack by taking two [Traitorous Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitorous+Blood)s and a second [Altar's Reap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar%27s+Reap). These picks set him up nicely to abuse the sacrifice synergy he had going on.


Pack 3 started with [Screeching Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Bat) over [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch). This was a pretty clear pick. Paul's deck already had plenty of removal and could definitely use a good creature. Next came a second [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) over [Victim of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night). Victim is good, but Volley is simply better. Third-pick [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) over [Rolling Temblor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) and Typhoid Rat was also a fairly clear. [Into the Maw of Hell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Maw+of+Hell) came fourth. Paul said he's a huge fan of the card, and he thought it was better than the [Kessig Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf) in the pack. I agree completely. This pick also shows why it's important to balance your drafts.


By taking cards to fill out his curve and cheaper, less powerful cards earlier, Paul was able to take the more powerful card here. If he had taken [Heretic's Punishment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heretic%27s+Punishment) over the [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Morkrut%2BBanshee), and *Scourge* over Patrician, he would have had to have taken the Wolf. And his deck would have been worse for it.



![Night Revelers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Night+Revelers)

[Night Revelers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night+Revelers) over [Tormented Pariah](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Pariah), and [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) over [Village Cannibals](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Cannibals) came next. These were the only picks in the draft where we disagreed. I think the picks are close, but I prefer the [Pariah](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pariah) over the Revelers. Rietzl's deck has a lot of removal, so he has the opportunity to flip the werewolf on his turn, then kill a creature on his opponent's.


Also, the Cannibals seems better than Rats. I think Paul could have afforded to have taken the three-drop, especially since its stock has risen so much with *Dark Ascension*. As the format has gotten faster, more and more small creatures die, giving more chances for the Cannibals to grow. Also, with the larger number of aggressive decks you now face, a 3/4 or 4/4 for three mana means a lot. However, Paul wanted to make sure his deck wasn't too slow; this draft format is extremely fast, so Paul was definitely in the right frame of mind when he took the [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats), but would've drafted these two picks differently.


All in all, I feel like Paul drafted the best deck possible in his seat. But his seat was average and as a result, so was his deck. It's not bad by any stretch of the imagination though. The curve and removal is balanced, and the two [Altar's Reap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar%27s+Reap) + three [Traitorous Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitorous+Blood) synergy should work out well for him. If this were a three-round draft I would predict he would go 2-1. However, being one of the best players in the Top 8, he definitely has the skills to take the whole thing down.

By the time that Patarca was able to keep a creature on the board for more than a turn, it was too late – as Rietzl had a [Traitorous Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitorous+Blood), and a [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night) to become the Grand Prix Mexico City Champion!


Paul Rietzl 2 – Humberto Patarca 1




Top 5 Cards of Grand Prix Mexico 2012

![Lambholt Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lambholt+Elder)

### 5) [Lambholt Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lambholt+Elder)


Despite the fact that he doesn't even like the card that much, Paul Rietzl took [Lambholt Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lambholt+Elder) early in his first and his second drafts just so he could signal to his neighbors that he was drafting green!


While many high-profile players have disagreed with Paul's strategy of aggressively signalling with underpowered Double-Faced Cards, it's clearly worked out well for him as he was able to follow up his Top 8 finish at Grand Prix Seattle-Tacoma by winning it all here at Grand Prix Mexico City. And don't let her looks fool you, an unanswered [Lambholt Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lambholt+Elder) will end a game mighty quickly.





![Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Drogskol+Captain)

### 4) [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain)


White-Blue fliers is one of the best decks in the format, and if you're fortunate enough to get a [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) (or two) -- then you could very well end up with an amazing deck on your hands. Timothy Thomason cruised to an 8-0 record on Day One with two copies of the blue-white lord in his Sealed Deck, and Craig Wescoe easily earned his way into the Top 8 with a draft deck that sported two [Drogskol Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain)s.


With a single Captain in play, you'll make your [Chapel Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chapel+Geist)s and your [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron)s even more fearsome than usual -- and if you ever happen to have two copies in play, then there are few cards short of [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) that will allow your opponent to recover.




### 3) Tie Between [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) and [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto)

It's easy to overlook these cards, but it just can't be done any longer. [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto) was a begrudging necessity before *Dark Ascension*, but with the new set and the addition of [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds), splashing is a viable, oft-used strategy. In either Limited format these lands can get the job done. Craig Wescoe and Paul Rietzl used these to great effect throughout the tournament. Paul splashed a singular [Undying Evil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undying+Evil) in a Red-Green Werewolf deck, and Craig sideboarded into a whole new color thanks in part to these lands. The Grotto even has the extra bonus of helping other colorless-producing lands ([Haunted Fengraf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Fengraf), [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt), etc.) act like they produce colored mana.





![Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Feeling+of+Dread)

### 2) [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread)


Good lord it's hard not to become more and more impressed with this card as the format wears on. Whether it's in Sealed or Draft, having a copy or two of this Instant can be the difference between a mediocre deck, and one that can take you to the top. It's the Michael Jordan of cards -- it can play great offense and defense. Having two copies of [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) was the reason Humberto Patarca was able to get to the finals. And though it didn't get him the win, perhaps it was because he didn't have card #1 in his deck...





![Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bump+in+the+Night)

### 1) [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night)


By his own admission, Paul Rietzl's Sealed Deck was not good. Paul had a low curve, and a couple of removal spells -- but if you compared the 40 cards that Paul maindecked side by side with almost anyone else's Sealed Deck, then it would have been clear that Paul was starting with a distinct disadvantage.


But despite the low overall quality of the cards in his Sealed Deck, Paul was able to scratch his way to a 7-1 record on Day One thanks to his two copies of [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night).


While Paul's creatures alone weren't able to reliably deal his opponents 20 damage, they were usually fast enough to get them down to six. At which point he could use a [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night) to finish the job.


Once he was in the Top 8, Paul drafted an aggressive Red-Black deck that was significantly stronger than the deck that he played on Day One -- but it still had some trouble dealing those final points of damage with its creatures.


At times that seemed like it might be a problem for Paul, but three matches, and some well timed [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night)s later, and Paul Rietzl was the Grand Prix Mexico City Champion!
