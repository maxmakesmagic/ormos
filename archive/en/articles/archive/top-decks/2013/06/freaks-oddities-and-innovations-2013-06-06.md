Freaks, Oddities, and Innovations
=================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 6, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Hello and welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive **Magic Online** column. The last eight or nine months have been [a hectic time for Standard](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/features.aspx?tag=standard%20deck%20compendium). The format seems to change every few weeks. It can be difficult at times to pinpoint the moment of a format shift. Luckily, I've been watching **Magic Online** event results like a hawk and I'm here to give you the scoop. 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr250_steepleroc.jpg)  
 The past week's **Magic Online** events were, once again, dominated by Jund, Red-Green Aggro, and Junk Rites. The wisest deck builders will often use the three or four most dominant decks as hurdles for their newest creations. Today, I'm going to take a look at the newest innovations to come out of **Magic Online**. Let's jump right into some decklists!

 The first deck we'll be looking at today is the latest take on [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). I'm really excited about this deck and I've found that it's difficult to lose when I'm piloting this. I'd like to talk about this deck's approach to beating the big decks of the format. 


 This deck takes advantage of [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act)'s absurd power level against Junk Rites. Any of you who have played a reasonable amount with [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) strategies will be well aware of Junk's weakness to the card. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) | [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) |

 Previous incarnations of [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) strategies often found themselves being overrun by the Gruul deck, especially when losing the die roll. This version uses [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) to hold down the fort and essentially make any sort of combat into a complete nightmare for your opponent. Additionally, [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) has the potential to outright kill an opponent when comboed with [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) or [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre). [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) will have plenty of food when you're casting [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), and [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage); this seemingly innocuous removal spell has a TON of killing potential in a deck like this. Winning completely out of nowhere is always a good angle. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) | [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) |

 Against Jund, this deck wants to maximize the value it gets out of [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) and ride that to victory. The deck puts pressure on opposing Planeswalkers and players somewhat effectively with [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). In theory, the Jund player shouldn't be able to freely cast his or her Planeswalkers when facing off against this type of strategy. Eventually, you can [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) your [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) and make sure you've always got the board presence you need. [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) has, historically, been a strong card against the Junk Rites decks that have little to no removal; she's one of Jund's best ways to interact with a Junk Rites deck once it gets going. This deck, while not thrilled to see the Vampire legend, has access to [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) for the reset if necessary, its own Olivia, or a [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) to shoot her out of the sky. 




|  |  |
| --- | --- |

 It may not be entirely new, but the Red-White-Blue Control decks are still alive and well. [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) is seeing less play than last week and things may be slowing down to the point where we can start taking over games with [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) again. [Niv-Mizzet, Dracogenius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Dracogenius) is beautifully positioned in the current format. Think of it as a worse Olivia that happens to be awesome against control and Planeswalkers. This deck suffers in the Esper control matchup, but Esper is becoming more of an oddity. I remember playing [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) in the original White-Blue Flash deck last fall. No one was playing [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) and I was super impressed with the card. Now, we're back to a place where [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) is less popular; Naya Human decks have fallen to the wayside in favor of decks with [Advent of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm) or Gruul. Creatures these days do a lot of nasty things when they hit the battlefield; removal spells are weaker as a result. [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) is just another card in the graveyard with [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter)â€”no 3/3, no life. We can expect this deck to be the control deck of choice going forward.

[Two weeks ago](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/dust-settles-2013-05-30), I touched on the Borzhov Midrange deck, but I didn't get a good opportunity to showcase the strategy. This version uses a lot of unique cards that allow it to grind out a race against most decks in the format. [Nearheath Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nearheath+Pilgrim) may look strange, but it makes the deck incredibly difficult to race for other aggressive strategies, while the big spells are all awesome against the control decks. [Stensia Bloodhall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stensia+Bloodhall) makes an appearance here. The deck can get into a grind very easily with cards like Obzedat and [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa). You're gaining tons of life and you have burn spells, it's pretty nice to have [Stensia Bloodhall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stensia+Bloodhall) to grind your opponent into range of a [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) or a pair of [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame)s. [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) gets to interact with [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) here, too. This is an interaction we haven't seen much of, despite its incredible power. I think decks like this will continue to do well in the coming weeks.

 We already talked about Red-White-Blue, but I'm pretty excited about the latest incarnation of [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) that popped up earlier this week on **Magic Online**. People still don't understand what this deck is about and it's very easy to completely dismantle someone when they try to settle into a control game. The deck outright kills people with burn spells more often than not. Games are likely going to unfold in one of a few ways: If you don't have an early [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) or [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) then you have to decide early if you want to just aim midgame burn at your opponent's face or if you want to interact and play the control game. Against a creature deck that doesn't have a lot of pressure because of a [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) or double-[Augur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur) draw I would likely just start doming my opponent. If my opponent is playing a deck with [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) or has a strong board presence, I'm more likely to play the control game and maximize interaction.
