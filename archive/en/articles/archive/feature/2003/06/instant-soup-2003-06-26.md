![Wing Shards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wing+Shards)### Attack of the Everything

[Wing Shards](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wing+Shards) was the first *Scourge* instant I decided I had to build a deck around. It might seem like a poor choice at first. It’s a purely reactive card (it doesn’t do anything unless your opponent does something first), so how can a deck be centered on it? Well, the deck doesn’t quite revolve around [Wing Shards](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wing+Shards). It revolves around the delightful [Wing Shards](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wing+Shards)-[Grand Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Melee) combo. When all of your opponent’s creatures have to attack each turn, it becomes much easier to pick them off by (apparently) throwing really pointy feathers at them.

Another fantastic way to bend [Grand Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Melee) to your advantage is with [Ageless Sentinels](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ageless+Sentinels). It would be an undercosted 4/4 flying creature if it didn’t come with a new twist on the [Elder Land Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Land+Wurm) drawback. But since Big Bird (sorry, *Giant* Bird) is in no rush to join the battle, it gives some control to your opponent: he or she can normally choose to sit back and not wake up the Sentinels until it can be dealt with. That’s really not a very helpful attitude, so [Grand Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Melee) takes away the option of not attacking. [Grand Melee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Melee) has other ramifications, of course. Your creatures are forced to attack each turn as well, which is why many of the creatures in this deck don’t tap to attack. Why leave yourself defenseless? We’ve got the original ([Serra Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel)), the new and mighty ([Noble Templar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Templar), who cures mana screw in his spare time), and the ridiculous ([Akroma, Angel of Wrath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Wrath)). [Lieutentant Kirtar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lieutentant+Kirtar) is also a two-way star; it picks off attacking creatures as well.

But enough about creatures. This is Instant Week! This deck has a full complement of [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock)s and [Lava Dart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart)s, which serve a couple of purposes. See, your opponent can avoid attacking with utility creatures by tapping them for their effect before combat. If they’re tapped, they can’t run headfirst into [Ageless Sentinels](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ageless+Sentinels). [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) and [Lava Dart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) take care of a wide range of these pesky creatures, such as [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), and [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). The other thing these cheap red instants do is power up your [Wing Shards](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wing+Shards). [Lava Dart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) is especially great, because for the cost of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) and a mountain, you can potentially pick off *five* creatures: two 1/1 guys and three attackers. If any creatures are left after that, your defense can easily handle it.

### Hunting PackInto the Woods

Next up is [Hunting Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Pack). Finally, an uncommon green instant that creates multiple surprise 4/4 Beasts! We haven’t seen anything like that since—oh, I seem to be sitting on a [Beast Attack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Attack). Which is 2 mana cheaper. Huh. 

The cost difference between the two cards is hard to judge. On its own, [Hunting Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Pack) only gives you one Beast for 7 mana. But if you wait until you opponent plays a spell (which seems pretty likely in a **Magic** game), then it’s a pair of Beasts for 7 mana. But you can’t count on it. Maybe the person you’re playing is in solid control of the game by the time you get 7 mana and decides to stop playing cards unless you provide a clear opening. No, you’ve got to take the initiative to unpack the most Beasts from your Pack. That means playing other spells on the same turn as a 7-mana card, so that means access to lots (and I mean LOTS) of mana.

There is another way to go: free spells. Play [Land Grant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Grant) by revealing your hand, play [Invigorate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invigorate) by giving your opponent 3 life, play [Vine Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vine+Dryad) by pitching a card, then spend 7 mana to get 4 Beasts. There’s a neat deck in there, but I’m not going to build it all the way. If you have older spells with alternate play costs (and there haven’t been any since *Mercadian Masques*), dig them out and see what you can do with what’s in your collection. I’d be interested in hearing the results.

But back to mana generation. [Sprouting Vines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Vines) is another green *Scourge* instant with storm, so that fits right in. [Elvish Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Aberration) provides a forest early in the game or ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif) later on. You can’t go wrong with [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) (they’ll rotate out of Standard soon, but I bet they’ll never rotate out of your living room). And the [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves)-[Wirewood Symbiote](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Symbiote) combo lets you repeatedly return [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves) to your hand so you can fetch a fresh forest from your deck each turn. [Vernal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vernal+Bloom) doubles the forest mana available to you, but [Explosive Vegetation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Vegetation) is a fine substitute if you don’t have any.

Now that you have eight million green mana, what do you do with it? This is where [Hunting Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Pack) comes in, of course, and it goes well with any of the sixteen 1-mana and 2-mana spells in the deck (ten of which are instants). [Uktabi Wildcats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uktabi+Wildcats) threatens to be colossal, and [Ambush Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Commander) turns your forests into an army. (Just watch out for deforestation techniques like [Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) or [Nausea](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nausea).) [Blanchwood Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blanchwood+Armor) is a great way to suit up [Wood Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Elves), and [Vitality Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitality+Charm) (when not pumping [Hunting Pack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Pack)) can provide a breakthrough trample effect.

### Long-Term PlansFuturama

Blue gets some wacky instants in *Scourge*. [Long-Term Plans](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Long-Term+Plans) (called Lazy Tutor in playtesting) is wacky. Three cards from the top? That’s never been on a **Magic** card before. [Metamorphose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metamorphose) is wacky. One card goes from Zone A to Zone B, then another goes from Zone C to Zone A. [Brain Freeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Freeze) is wacky, and oh-so-tempting. The opportunity to mill 15 cards or so in one shot is spine-tinglingly delicious. It also lets you permanently dispose of whatever you may have [Metamorphose](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metamorphose)d to the top of your opponent’s deck. 

The natural engine for busting out blue instants is [Future Sight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight). You can keep playing 1-mana and 2-mana instants off the top of your deck until you hit a land or one of the few spells that isn’t a 1-mana or 2-mana instant. Even then, if you have a [Peek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) or a [Mental Note](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Note) in hand, you can clear out that obstacle at the top of your deck and keep going. Is this enough to make [Brain Freeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Freeze) a victory condition all by itself? Possibly, but it’ll be difficult. To keep an emphasis on cheap instants, I didn’t include any other milling effects like [Millstone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millstone) or [Wheel and Deal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+and+Deal). Maybe in a future deck, but not today. The main route to victory here is [Cognivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore), who’s had Instant Week circled on its calendar for ages. Take that, [Ichneumon Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichneumon+Druid)! When the milling effects don’t defeat your opponent, they’ll likely make Coggy nice and fat. And you can expect to have an instant-rich graveyard yourself. [Mental Note](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Note) both feeds [Cognivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore) and turns your [Long-Term Plans](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Long-Term+Plans) into Right-Now Plans by putting the card you searched for right into your hand.

The rest of the deck is full of bounce. These spells are instants, protect your [Cognivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore)s, and keep the board clear of threats. [Chain of Vapor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+of+Vapor) is included because it only costs ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) and your entire deck has only seven non-land permanents. It’s especially good, of course, before you have any of them in play.
