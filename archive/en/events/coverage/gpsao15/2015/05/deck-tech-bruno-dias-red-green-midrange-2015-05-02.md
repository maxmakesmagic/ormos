Deck Tech: Bruno Dias' Red Green Midrange
=========================================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX SÃO PAULO 2015](/en/events/coverage/gpsao15)
 on May 2, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman_icon_0.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











Bruno Martinez Pereira Dias, better known as Bruno\_Overlord on *Magic Online*, is a well-known Brazilian *Magic* player. A restauranteur from Rio de Janeiro, Dias' claim to fame was a MOCS win in 2013, qualifying him for the *Magic Online* Championship.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpsao15/Bruno-Dias.jpg)  
*Bruno Dias showing off his Red Green Midrange deck!*



He is well prepared for what may come his way, armed with a product that has been carefully sanded after hours and hours of practice. "I must have made around 35 QP with this deck in the past two weeks. I think I have a good deck for this tournament."


At first glance, it looks like a traditional Red Green Dragons deck. The usual suspects are in the mix, including [Xenagos, the Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+the+Reveler), *Thunderbreak Regent*, [Stormbreath Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbreath+Dragon) and the innocuous (but superbly powerful) [Elvish Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic).



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thunderbreak+Regent)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderbreak+Regent)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stormbreath+Dragon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbreath+Dragon)
  
*Dragons, Dragons, Everywhere!*



But, there's more...


"Esper Dragons did so well at Grand Prix Kraków, and I also expect Mono Red and Abzan to be popular this weekend." Because of that, he has decided to add *Deathmist Raptor*s and [Whisperwood Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whisperwood+Elemental) to his concoction.


The lack of two drops is the obvious gaping hole as can be seen from his mana curve, but Dias explains that it is not a problem. "Since my deck has a powerful late game, I am happy to use my burn spells to stabilize the board on turns one through three."



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deathmist+Raptor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmist+Raptor)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Whisperwood+Elemental)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whisperwood+Elemental)
  
*Dias jumps onto the Raptor Bandwagon.*



True enough, his deck has quite a lot of staying power. The engine of Deathmist Raptors and [Whisperwood Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whisperwood+Elemental) does give him a decent advantage in the attrition war, and the addition of [Outpost Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege) also adds a different dimension to his game plan.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Outpost+Siege)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege)


"[Outpost Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege) is such an outstanding card. Sometimes, when the board is gummed up and I need the final push, I set it on 'Dragons' and it helps me deal the last few damage. Against the slower decks like Esper Dragons and Abzan Control, [Outpost Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege) allows me to play the long game with them. I am very happy to have two copies in the maindeck. They took the place of [Xenagos, the Reveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+the+Reveler) because I expected a lot of [Searing Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blood) this weekend."


The addition of [Stoke the Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoke+the+Flames) is equally interesting. Dias explains that nobody plays around [Stoke the Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoke+the+Flames). "Sometimes, if I am tapped out with a few creatures in play, my opponent may attack with [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai) or think they have a lethal attack and I get trump to throw off the combat math. Additional burn also combine with [Crater's Claws](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crater%27s+Claws) for unexpected kills."


He also has secret tech in the sideboard and I was especially intrigued by the pair of [Display of Dominance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Display+of+Dominance). "I sideboard this in against mainly the black decks that have a lot of removal. My creatures are considerably big and if I do manage to "counter" a kill spell, that is all I need to win the game. It also removes [Ashiok, Nightmare Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashiok%2C+Nightmare+Weaver), [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) and [Encase in Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Encase+in+Ice), all great cards against him.


I shudder to think about it.


Can you imagine pointing [Hero's Downfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall) at [Thunderbreak Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderbreak+Regent) (taking 3 damage from its trigger), getting it countered, then getting attacked for an additional 4 damage?



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Display+of+Dominance)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Display+of+Dominance)
  
*Dias has a trick up his sleeve this weekend.*



In addition, he is also happy to swap out [Roast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roast)s in exchange of [Plummet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plummet)s in view of the high number of Dragons he expects to face. In addition, he also took the liberty of changing a second [Destructive Revelry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Revelry) to [Ainok Survivalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ainok+Survivalist). The additional Morph would go a long way in helping Dias resurrect his raptors!
