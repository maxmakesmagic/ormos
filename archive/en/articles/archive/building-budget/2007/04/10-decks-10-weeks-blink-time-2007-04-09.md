One of the concepts of the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks experiment is that you'd be able to save money, if you're on a budget, by reusing a portion of cards from one week to the next. Sometimes this has happened (Week 3 to Week 4), and other times it has not (Week 2 to Week 3). I'm happy to say that this week's build will keep a good number of cards from Rescue Me. Why can I say that with such certainty? It's because [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) interacts extremely well with the rescue mechanic.

Look at it this way – I've already got a deck full of creatures that trigger when they come into play or leave play. [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) "forces" a creature to leave and come into play in one fell swoop, which triggers most of the comes-into-play and leaves-play effects in this deck. [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) and the Rescue creatures each have advantages and disadvantages in relation to one another.

![Momentary Blink and Whitemane Lion](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/building/bb107_cardfan.jpg)Rescue saves your creatures from global removal, such as [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) does not.

[Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) puts your creature right back into play. If you do this at your opponent's end-of-turn, your creature will not have summoning sickness on your turn. Rescue puts the creature back in your hand, which means you need to recast it.

[Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) can be cast twice, thanks to flashback. Rescue creatures can either be cast once, or can be chained on top of one another (such as a [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion) bouncing a [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion)) indefinitely.

Rescue can return [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm). [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) can flip up a morph creature. 

Rescue puts the rescue creature into play untapped, if you care to block an attacker. [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) untaps a creature you already have on the board, allowing it to block.

As you can see, although [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) and rescue are very similar, they have very subtle and important gameplay differences that make them each better or worse than the other, depending on deck construction and board situation. I wouldn't want a [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) if my [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm) was down to one counter, but I'd definitely want it over a [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion) after I swing with my Jötun Grunt and my opponent attacks back – I get a 4/4 blocker plus I reset the cumulative upkeep on the Grunt!

This is the standard mana fixing for the move to a new deck. In essence, 50 of the 60 cards from Rescue Me are coming back for the first build of A Blink in Time – only [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal), [Orzhov Pontiff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff) and [Shrieking Grotesque](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrieking+Grotesque) are fully gone!

In: 4 [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink)

Since this is a [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) deck, the namesake card for this deck takes four slots.

In: 3 [Court Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar), 3 [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk)

[Court Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) is the best blue-white comes-into-play effect creature available that was not already in the Rescue Me deck. [Azorius Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald) is a consideration here, but I've been really happy with [Aven Riftwatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Riftwatcher) – the three toughness makes a huge difference over one toughness against removal and for blocking opposing creatures. Also, [Court Hussar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) and [Azorius Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald) are great with rescue but not so much with [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) – once I blink them, they die (I still get the effect of them coming into play), since I did not spend blue-white to play them (their trigger doesn't check the mana I spent on [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink), only if I spent that mana to play the creature itself. Since I did not play them after a Blink, they die.)

I've been on the receiving end of [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk) / [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) in the past in this very column, and it wasn't pretty. Now it's my turn to be ugly – not only do I get a late-game nail-in-the-coffin (no late-game heroics for you!), I also get a win condition, and a [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) that can be recurred on the board with [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink)! ([Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk) is another creature that works a lot better with Blink than with Rescue, though it has synergy with both. Still, two and four mana to recur at instant-speed is a lot better than eight or nine mana ([Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion) or [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker) plus a recast of the Whelk).

 He gets [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer), and I get [Icatian Javelineers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Javelineers), [Aven Riftwatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Riftwatcher), and [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm). He drops [Nantuko Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) plus Elves of the Deep Shadow, and I swing, get past, and shoot his Elves with Javelineers. He plays [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer), and I answer by hitting [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle) to the board, naming that equipment. He then deploys Savra and [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka). I keep coming in with the air force, and [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) my Riftwatcher to keep them around. He drops [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), and I swing with my team. He blocks the [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm) with his Husk, sacs his team to make his Husk 6/6, and makes me lose a creature to Savra in the process with his Rusalka. I gladly throw [Icatian Javelineers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Javelineers) to the bin, and rescue my Caldierm with [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion). In a last-ditch effort to stay alive, he tries to [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) my [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle), but I use the flashback on [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) to keep my creature on the board, naming [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) again (note: If I had to use a rescue creature on the Gargoyle, he would have been able to equip the Warhammer that turn!).

So far, so good, but it's time for a couple of changes. [Dust Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Elemental) has only mattered one or twice over the course of over twenty games between this week and last, and it's really awful with [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) – the last thing I want to have to do is Blink a [Dust Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Elemental) that I got to stick on the board. It's out.

![Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)In addition, [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle) has been good, but not great. I definitely recommend it for further use, especially since they can be gotten at two-for-a-ticket easily all over ***Magic** Online*. [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle) is better, though, in best two-out-of-three matches. If you get to see what's in your opponent's deck Game 1, you have a better chance of naming key cards in advance with [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle). Against completely random decks in best one-of-one matches, [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle) is a five-drop [Pithing Needle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) (albeit, one that beats for three in the air) that is a crapshoot at best.In place of [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle), I add in [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), as seen during weeks 3 and 4 of this series. [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) gives me an early use for my mana, has a great comes-into-play effect, and works well with both Rescue and [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink). In fact, if I Blink the [Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudskate), I can double it *as* a rescue creature!

I also add in a single copy of [Akroma, Angel of Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury). I wanted the biggest, baddest, largest morph creature available for the replacement slot for [Dust Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Elemental) – after all, what's the point of playing with [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) if you can't cheat the mana costs of some huge monster? For those who are unfamiliar, if you cast [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) on a face-down morph creature, it returns to play face-up. While I don't have the mana to pump Akroma's firebreathing, I'm sure I can make do with a 6/6 flying, trampling, protection from white and blue beater. At worst, Akroma is a 2/2 creature for three mana, so it's not like drawing the one Akroma in my deck is an entirely dead draw.

(Note: Most vendors I've found who were selling Akroma online had her at three tickets. This puts the total cost of the deck at around 13-15 tickets, most of which carried over from last week!)

Out: 3 [Voidstone Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidstone+Gargoyle), 1 [Dust Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Elemental)  
 In: 1 [Akroma, Angel of Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury), 3 [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)

Since I've got a couple of double-blue cost guys now, I swap out a couple of [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) for a couple of [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s. Last thing I want is to be stuck with [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk) in my hand at an inopportune time!

 Record: 10-2Last week, one question asked is "Are [Icatian Javelineers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Javelineers) really necessary to this deck?" The answer is yes and no – I want to have an out against utility creatures (and not have the deck auto-lose against [Fortune Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortune+Thief)!), but they have not been great. They cost a ticket each, and they are pretty small and useless a majority of the time. The problem is that they have summoning sickness, so every time I Blink / rescue them, I have to wait a turn to reuse them. If I could play and replay them the same turn to deal two damage, I'd have a lot more use for them in the deck.... (foreshadowing here folks!)... but in this deck, they are suboptimal.

In place of the Javelineers, I add in three copies of [Azorius First-Wing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+First-Wing). The deck has been winning with an air force, and I might as well add in one of the best early flyers in Standard right now. The choice came down to [Azorius First-Wing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+First-Wing) or [Leonin Skyhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Skyhunter). In the end, it was the mana cost of [Azorius First-Wing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+First-Wing) that won out! Between all the Signets and lands in the deck, it's a lot easier to get a blue and a white mana early, then it is to get two white. 

Speaking of mana bases, I finally decide to try out [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) (to help feed Jötun Grunt) instead of [Azorius Chancery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Chancery). This decision is really to see if they make any difference to the Grunt, and to allow for a more smooth transition to a turn-two First-Wing (turn one Expanse, getting the opposite color of white-blue depending on what is in my hand).
