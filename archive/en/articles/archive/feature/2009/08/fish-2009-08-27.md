In 1998, Pro Tour-Rome was defined by two cards: [Time Spiral](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Spiral) and [Tolarian Academy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolarian+Academy). One player who decided to make a go of things without either was Frenchman Nicolas Labarre. His Fish build was good enough to get second, powered by [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), [Curiosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity), and cheap blue creatures. He could plop a few threats onto the battlefield, then counter his opponent's most relevant spells long enough to attack for the win!
