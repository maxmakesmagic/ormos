Sultai in Legacy
================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on October 15, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 





[Prowess](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/dd/prowess-legacy-2014-10-14) isn't the only ability from *Khans of Tarkir* that's making a splash in Legacy. With graveyards stuffed full of spells and [fetch lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&type=+[%22Land%22]&text=+[search]+[your]+[library]&set=|[%22Khans%20of%20Tarkir%22]), [delve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&text=+[delve]&set=+[%22Khans%20of%20Tarkir%22]) is also changing the landscape of the format.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Treasure Cruise)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure Cruise)
In a deck that features fetch lands, cheap draw spells, hand destruction, and removal, it's entirely feasible to zero out the colorless mana portion of pretty much any spell. And the card that helps perfectly enable that ideal scenario for Sultai is [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise).


Not only is the color commitment for [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) low, needing only one blue mana, it's one of the few cards that can rightfully say it compares favorably with the original legendary draw spell, [Ancestral Recall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall). And, unlike [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision), it's always castable.


Of course, one of the distinguishing features of Legacy is that each card is incredibly strong, but it's still a nice bonus when [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) or [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) comes with an extra two cards.
