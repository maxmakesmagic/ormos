Grand Prix Amsterdam 2011
-------------------------


Sommen Rides Knights to Victory
===============================




October 22-23












Grand Prix Amsterdam 2011
-------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpams11/winner_620.jpg)Grand Prix Amsterdam has closed, with 1,878 players whittled down to just one – Pierre Sommen of France!


The Legacy format has delivered another knockout Grand Prix, with a huge variety of decks on display, from creatureless combo decks like Ad Nauseum that abused all the most powerful mana producing cards in **Magic** history to Dredge decks that couldn't generate a single point of mana at all! In between there was every flavour of deck you could imagine, and many of **Magic**'s greatest hits on display such as [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind), [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition), [Hymn to Tourach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach) and of course the ever-present [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will).


As well as the cards of **Magic** past there has been plenty of room for cards of **Magic** present here in Amsterdam, with [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) leading the charge of *Innistrad* cards into the Legacy format, ably supported by [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames), [Purify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purify) The Grave, and even the odd appearance of a [Sundial of the Infinite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sundial+of+the+Infinite).


In a Top 8 packed by Legacy specialists, Pierre Sommen scrapped his way through three tense matches to emerge as a deserving (if exhausted) champion. The Frenchman's "[Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull)[Bant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant)" aggro deck simply proved too much for Ciro Bonaventura's Canadian Threshold deck in the final, and before that Sommen had come from the very brink of defeat to edge past Christof Kovacs' [Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) combo in his semi-final, and Fabian Gorzgen in a mammoth quarter final. This Frenchman earned his trophy in a gruelling Top 8.


Grand Prix Amsterdam marks the end of the 2011 Grand Prix calendar in Europe, and the focus shifts to Japan and Grand Prix Hiroshima and the latest standard environment, but even before then you should skip over to catch the Top 8 of what is sure to be a thrilling Grand Prix in Santiago.

Quarterfinal - Elie Pichon vs. Kim Grymer
-----------------------------------------


*by Rich Hagon*

Of the top 8 here at GP Amsterdam, only Elie Pichon has top 8 experience at the highest level. He finished 3rd at Pro Tour Nagoya earlier this year behind eventual champion and Player of the Year hopeful David Sharfman. The Frenchman Pichon has piloted ANT - [Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) Tendrils - to numerous wins, but now any malfunction could result in the end of the road.


On the other side of the table sits Denmark's Kim Grymer. With zero byes, Grymer advanced to 10-0 on the back of numerous [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) effects and the deliciously nasty combo of [Grindstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone) and [Painter's Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant). Together, these spell doom for the opposition, milling the deck away at a single stroke.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpams11/QF%20Elie%20Pichon.jpg)
*Elie Pichon*
Grymer won the roll and elected to play. With the possibility of turn one [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon), that was no surprise. Pichon mulliganed to six, and then saw Grymer open up with [City of Traitors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors), [Chrome Mox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox), and [Magus of the Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Moon)! [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) from Pichon - that'll be a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) then.


[Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) - that'll be a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) too. [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) - that'll be a Mou..oh wait, that'll be a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp). [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) backup from Grymer, and a clock-shortening [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide), with another [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) the following turn, meant Pichon had yet to cast a spell.


Finally he did so, naming [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) with [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), and seeing another [Blood Moon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon) alongside a pair of lands. Not that it mattered. Probably.


Pichon began what had to be his last turn with [Lion's Eye Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond). He used his [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) for [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual), and then [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual). Then he cast a second [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual). One red and one black netted him [Infernal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor), which he responded to by sacrificing his [Lion's Eye Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond). The Tutor found him [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames). That got [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual), [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual), [Infernal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor), all newly with Flashback - thanks, *Innistrad*!


The [Infernal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor) provided the coup de grace, and [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony) (a card any dentist would love) took it on home to mama.


Pichon 1 - 0 Grymer


This time it was Grymer to begin with six. Would there be another exotic turn one? Not until he dropped to five there wouldn't, and then four. At that point, an exotic turn one would be a land and pass the turn...

Fire/Ice dealt one damage to each of two [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch)s, and [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) took out [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpams11/Finals.jpg)

That left Sommen with just one [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) in play, but he had [Tundra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tundra) ready to allow him to try for [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic). [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) from Bonaventura ensured that wouldn't happen. This time, Bonaventura remembered his Delver trigger, and it was [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) that crashed in for three damage, leaving Sommen at thirteen. A 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) joined the Italian board position, which was looking very strong.


Sommen cast the first [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) of the final, followed by more digging with [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). That led to [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), but since that was Sommen's last mana, Bonaventura's [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) would be an excellent counter. The Italian sent his team into the red zone, dropping Sommen to six, then adding [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose). [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) found nothing good for the Frenchman, and we were off to game two.


Sommen 0 - 1 Bonaventura


### Game Two


With both players starting at seven cards, Sommen laid [Windswept Heath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windswept+Heath) with no apparent need to crack it immediately. [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) went away for the Italian, who ended with [Volcanic Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island) in play. That led to [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), just as he had done in game one. Sommen continued to leave his [Windswept Heath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windswept+Heath) alone, adding [Tundra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tundra) before aiming [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) at the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). There was no argument from Bonaventura, who went back to twenty life.


The Italian had another [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) ready, and used [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) to take out [Savannah](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah). Sommen did at least get some use out of it, casting [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) on the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). A [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) of his own left both players with just one land in play. Bonaventura used his [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island) to cast [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). Sommen simply laid [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas), and passed the turn. Another Italian [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) took out Sommen's [Tundra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tundra), and this time Sommen couldn't add to his land in play. Bonaventura could, turning [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) into [Volcanic Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island).


Sommen slumped in his chair as another turn passed without land. Bonaventura searched for more pressure with [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), finding [Wooded Foothills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills) to play. [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) from Sommen saw the Italian shrug with something close to studied contempt - it was about as unexciting a land as Sommen could have drawn. [Wooded Foothills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills) became a second [Volcanic Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island), and now a third [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) of the game appeared for Bonaventura. Like the first two, this was destined to go nowhere fast, [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) seeing it exit stage left.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpams11/Finals%20Sommen.jpg)
*Pierre Sommen*

With [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island) arriving, Sommen was able to cast [Noble Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), potentially solving all his color and mana issues in one turn. The Italian allowed the Hierarch, but spent the end of turn killing it with [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) met an Italian [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare), Bonaventura still holding three cards. [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary)? [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) pitching [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) looked to say no. This time Sommen was ready with his own [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), pitching [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze). The [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) arrived on the battlefield with four land in Sommen's graveyard.


A third [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) from Bonaventura once again reduced Sommen to just white mana, at the cost of making [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) a rather fearsome 7/7. In it came, dropping Bonaventura to eleven, facing a two turn clock. He cast [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) with Sunburst three, which conveniently is the converted mana cost of [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary)...


[Windswept Heath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windswept+Heath) from Sommen became [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island), once again (at least temporarily) solving his color problems. The Knight attacked for eight more, leaving Bonaventura at just three. Whatever his plans, they definitely involved blowing his [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives). At end of turn Sommen cast [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), with Bonaventura using Fire/Ice to tap Sommen's [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island), and draw a card. Since this was upkeep, it seemed there was little for Sommen to usefully float mana for. The Frenchman attacked with the Knight, and to the surprise of precisely nobody, the [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) smeared Knight sauce all over the battlefield.


Sommen started again with [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), fetching the mighty [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull). The final [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) from the Italian sent Sommen back to being mono-white yet again. Sommen didn't care - his plans only needed white mana at this point. [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) put [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) into play, and that was the cue for Bonaventura to sweep up his permanents.


Sommen 1 - 1 Bonaventura


### Game Three


Advantage Italy, as Sommen took a mulligan to open the decider. The Italian opened with [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), although he accidentally put a [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) instead into his graveyard - quickly recovering, but not before Sommen noticed. [Misty Rainforest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) became [Tundra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tundra) for the Frenchman, who passed. Bonaventura landed [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) on turn two, while Sommen attempted [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), which got [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze)d. Sommen had [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) at the ready too, but Bonaventura had a second [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) to win the counterspell war.


[Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) attacked as a 4/5 - Land, Instant, Sorcery, Creature in the graveyards - and Bonaventura replayed his first land. In the Italian's next upkeep, Sommen used [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to remove the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) permanently from the equation. Bonaventura replied with [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose), while [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) from Sommen took out [Volcanic Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island). He cast [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), and quickly sought out [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull).


Without Threshold, the [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) couldn't attack into [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), but Bonaventura didn't plan on the Mystic sticking around for long, using [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to stem the threat. [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) resolved for Sommen. His [Windswept Heath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windswept+Heath) left play, but that was the cue for Bonaventura to at least play some mind games with the threat of [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle). In any case, the trigger went un-[Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle)d, and [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island) appeared on the scene. Sommen continued to quest through his deck, this time with [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder).

*Ciro Bonaventura*

The [Tropical Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island) got hit by an Italian [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), leaving Bonaventura to cast a [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) of his own, the tension noticeably increasing as the jockeying for position continued. Now the [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) had Threshold, and Sommen was down to ten. His reply? [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), with four land in the graveyard. Bonaventura cast [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), and it seemed as if neither player had any countermagic worthy of the name. Either that, or there was some very impressive body language performance going on.


[Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) again, this time for France. [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) sent [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) away, only to be replaced by another. The [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) dominated the board, however, stopping even a Threshold [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) in its tracks. The Frenchman still had a [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) to aim at the heart of Bonaventura, who only had two mana to work with. Now Sommen was up to four mana, then five, and that meant [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull).


This was a huge moment....and Bonaventura was ready for it, with [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) preventing the living weapon from entering the fray.


Now his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) became [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration), and the game was on once again. He faced an 8/8 [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), but had a 3/2 flyer, which he sent in. Sommen used his Knight to find [Maze of Ith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith), creating a peaceful no-fly zone around his life total. Finally Sommen went to [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) Bonaventura's [Volcanic Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island), but this time the Italian both had and used a [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) to keep his land safe.


As the second hour of the final began, Sommen cast Scavenging Ooze. His Knight was now into double figures as a 10/10, and it virtually sawed the Italian life total in half at a single stroke, from twenty one down to eleven. [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) sent Sommen to six life. Could Bonaventura burn the Frenchman out to claim the title?


The Italian had [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) for Scavenging Ooze, paying four life to fall to seven. That didn't stop Sommen from targeting another creature, which allowed Sommen to gain another life back. Now the Frenchman led eight life to seven. Over one hundred spectators looked on as thousands of matches across two days came down to these last few turns. Sommen used a new [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) to finally destroy [Volcanic Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Island). Bonaventura was down to one mana, and Sommen pressed his advantage, casting [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) and fetching [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), a card that had been ending games for years and years.


Now it ended this one.


Pierre Sommen 2 - 1 Ciro Bonaventura

Top 5 Cards
-----------


It has been quite a weekend here at Grand Prix Amsterdam, and with 1,210 cards legal in the Legacy format, it seems a little hopeful to try to sum up everything that has gone on here with just five of them. Nevertheless though, we will try.


![Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Delver+of+Secrets)


 


### [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)


It wasn't really any great surprise when [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) was all over the room here in Amsterdam, but this little wizard is one who hadn't received quite the same amount of buzz (do you see what I did there?) before the event. [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) turns out to be exactly the kind of mana efficient threat that Canadian Threshold decks need to be able to both attack and disrupt an opponent's game plan at the same time. As one player said to me this weekend "[Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) is the best card in Legacy, and [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is a natural combo with it, it was always going to see play." – perhaps it was no surprise at all that it made it to the finals.


![Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aven+Mindcensor)


 


### [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor)


Maverick is not one of the most overtly powerful decks in the Legacy format, but it is one that performed admirably nonetheless, combining a good level of disruption with a fast clock. One part of this package was [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor), whose ability to make search effects worse plays very well against virtually the entire format, whether decks be using [Natural Order](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order), [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition), or simply fetchlands to make their manabase do its thing. All the while the Mindcensor can attack and hold a sword as well as much of the format, something that the Maverick deck frequently asks of it.


![Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)


 


### [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)


While we're talking about searching, it would be highly remiss to avoid talking about the Zenith of *Mirrodin*'s newest sun. Fetching out everything from [Dryad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) to [Gaddock Teeg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg) to [Regal Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force) and more, the versatile sorcery means that green decks can now have an answer to most things available at a moment's notice.


![Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Force+of+Will)


 


### [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)


There have been plenty of new cards and exciting things that have gone on throughout this Grand Prix, but given that this is a Legacy event, it would be wrong to forget the format's roots, which are firmly planted by [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). Keeping decks honest since the mid 1990's, the powerful instant is just as good now as it ever has been, and remains one of the allures of playing blue in general. Don't forget, as one of Marijn Lybaert's opponents did this weekend, that sometimes it can even be cast for 3UU if absolutely necessary. He'd thought that not having blue cards in hand would leave him safe, but sometimes, FoW is simply a really expensive [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell).


![Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Surgical+Extraction)


 


### [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction)


Now that everything is fair game for flashback thanks to [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), the value of [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction), already a fine sideboard answer to many of the graveyard based decks in the format like Reanimator, has increased quite a bit. We've seen it used as a version of [Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek), to help decks know what is coming. We've seen it used as a [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) of sorts. We've seen it stop [Reanimate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reanimate) spells as they try to get back a fatty, and as a nice way of stopping flashback. If switcheroo sideboard plans are in effect, they will not be a surprise with [Surgical Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surgical+Extraction) around while the spell itself can be a surprise, as thanks to Phyrexian mana, any deck can cast it.
