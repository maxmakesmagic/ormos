Block Boros Beatdown
====================



 Posted in [Reconstructed](/en/articles/columns/reconstructed-archive)
 on May 13, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cardsâ€”and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






Ah, the Block Constructed Pro Tour.  [Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/protour/dragonsmaze13) pulls all of the recently printed cards onto the center stage, giving us a glimpse of what the Standard format to come will look like. 

 If [Mark Rosewater](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Mark%20Rosewater)'s adage "restrictions breed creativity" wore the face of a format, this is the one it would choose. Cards not quite good enough for current Standard can shine, and with the limited card pool, players are really forced to figure out how to attack problems in innovative ways. Compared to other formats, Block is fairly unexplored. 

 But that isn't to say there's a complete lack of information on the format. If you've been keeping your ear to the ground, you'll have seen some decklists. Block Constructed is in full swing on **Magic Online**, and there's quite a few decklists you can check out by poring through the tournament results [featured on MTGonline.com](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/digital/magiconline.aspx?x=mtg/digital/magiconline/whatshappening). 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/rc/rc247_viashinofirstblade_large.jpg)[Viashino Firstblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Firstblade) | Art by [Matt Stewart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Matt%20Stewart%22%5D)

 There are actually quite a few different decks that have been doing well, and with *Dragon's Maze* entering the format it has appeared to expand the metagame even further. The control decks have been given more tools like [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa) and [Far amp; Away](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%2F%2F+Away), and the midrange Selesnya decks have been strengthened by cards like [Advent of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm) and [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence). 

 However, one deck that is fairly ubiquitous on **Magic Online** is the Mono-Red deck. Sporting between eighteen and twenty lands, twenty-four or more one- and two-drops, and a couple versatile filler spells like [Dynacharge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dynacharge) and [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), this deck is as quick as Block Constructed decks come. 

But why Mono-Red? In a block about guilds, is it possible that there's another aggressive Red route to go down? That's what we're going to investigate today. Let's take a look at Paul Wilhelm's version of Boros:

The Battle Plan
The battle plan here is fairly clear: attack!

The big question when considering two colors versus one color is this: Does what you gain by adding the second color outweigh the hit to consistency and slightly slower draws?

To make the mana work in a two-color beatdown deck, some Gates are certainly going to be necessary. (And will be added into the final version of this deck.) Those will slow you down when you draw them. Since the advantage of Mono-Red is that you can overtake slower decks, what could be worth taking a slight hit to speed?

Well, how about some cards that speed you up?

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Viashino+Firstblade%2B%2BLegion%27s+Initiative)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Firstblade%2B%2BLegion%27s+Initiative) In an aggressive deck, you want to have as high an early damage output as you can, and 2 power for one mana is precisely what a deck like this is looking for. If the mana wasn't going to be so rough, I'd consider playing [Dryad Militant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Militant) and potentially [Boros Elite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite) for the same reasons, but the mana of the deck keeps me away from those two cards since this deck wants to be base-red. I'm definitely playing all four Cacklers. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Foundry+Street+Denizen)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Street+Denizen) When you need makeshift 2-power creatures for one mana, this Denizen is a good Goblin for the job. When you're trying to create the highest damage output you can, a consistent 2-power creature or occasional 3-power creature is exactly what you're looking for. Also, keep in mind that if you draw multiple Denizens, you can use [Legion's Initiative](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Initiative) to slightly increase your damage output in a pinch. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Legion+Loyalist)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist) Most of the Mono-Red decks are running the full four copies of this creature. In the conversion to Boros some cuts have to be made, and I think Paul has correctly identified [Legion Loyalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist) as the weaker of the three different one-drops and has dropped all the way down to one copy. 

One-drops are a little less exciting in the two-color version of this deck, since I'll want to play a Guildgate on the first turn sometimes, and all of the various abilities that Loyalist provides, while nice, don't overcome the better damage output of the other one-drops. I'm fine going all the way and dropping down to zero copies here.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wojek+Halberdiers)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wojek+Halberdiers) Two mana for 3 power that can be tricky to block? Sign me up! This is definitely one Boros soldier I'm interested in keeping all the copies of. [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker) was already a pretty good creature to play on turn two in the Mono-Red version, and this is similarly a 3/2 for two mana. (It's worth noting that I'd like to fit the Chainwalker into the final build of this deck as well.) 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sunhome+Guildmage)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome+Guildmage) I love creatures you can play in beatdown decks that have solid activated abilities you can use in the long game. Although it was a long time ago, this inclusion reminds me a lot of the four [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage)s in Tomoharu Saito's winning decklist from the first [Ravnica-centric Pro Tour](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx?x=mtgevent/ptcha06/welcome). It's just a solid creature you can play on turn two that provides great reach as the game goes onward. 

 However, in this deck, I don't think [Sunhome Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome+Guildmage) quite makes the cut. It's not a game of inches if the game goes long: what every other deck is doing in the long game is going to significantly outpower you. [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) and its control friends will pack the game away, and an extra token or two isn't going to help out often enough to be worth it. In this format, I'd rather play a creature that is more aggressive without requiring any other mana investment so I can continue to deploy threats and try to kill my opponent as fast as possible. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Truefire+Paladin)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Truefire+Paladin) Some of what I said for [Sunhome Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunhome+Guildmage) also applies here. I'd much rather have a card like [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) (which I would like to play in the final build), or the aforementioned [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker), that increases my damage output without additional mana investment. Make no mistakeâ€”[Truefire Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Truefire+Paladin) is certainly a strong card. It just doesn't seem to be what this deck is looking for. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Reckoner)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) This Standard staple is unsurprisingly phenomenal in Block Constructed as well, making combat difficult for your opponent and making your opponent's [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) very painful. This is one of the best beatdown cards you can play, and I definitely would not play fewer than four copies. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Viashino+Firstblade)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Firstblade) While the Mono-Red decks usually top out at three mana with Reckoner, and I don't want to add too much to the top end of my mana curve, [Viashino Firstblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Firstblade) is certainly worth it. 

 Firstblade is one of the cards you pick up in *Dragon's Maze* that makes Boros appealing and hits for a ton of damage. Turn-one Cackler/Denizen into turn-two Halberdiers/Chainwalker into turn-three Firstblade means you're hitting for a whopping 9 damage on turn three! Even if your opponent has a [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), that quickly puts him or her on the back foot. I definitely want to play all four of these. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Firemane+Avenger%2B%2BTajic%2C+Blade+of+the+Legion+)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Avenger%2B%2BTajic%2C+Blade+of+the+Legion+) Four-mana creatures are a little more expensive on my curve than I'd like to go. If [Firemane Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firemane+Avenger) is activeâ€”if you're attacking with three or more creatures on turn fiveâ€”then you're probably in shape to win anyway. Tajic, while a nice anti-control option, isn't that much of a threat on his own, and there are even plenty of answers to him, like [Far amp; Away](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Far+%2F%2F+Away), [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere), or even just a larger creature. I'd like to cut these to make room for more aggressive cards at the bottom end of this deck's curve. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Legion%27s+Initiative)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Initiative) I've talked up the merits of this card a reasonable amount already. Not only does it pump all of your creatures' power for two mana (and some of their toughnesses), but it can do numerous tricks. You can block and save all of your creatures for another day, avoid [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), or even just use it to untap your creatures after an attack so they're ready to block again. Against control decks, [Glorious Anthem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Anthem)â€“style effects are a good way to push through extra damage without having to commit extra creatures to the board, and it's even better when your anthems serve as double duty to protect your creatures as well! 

I don't think you want to play the full four copies, because you need to have enough pressure in your hand in the first place to make these good, but I'm certainly happy to play three.

Adding to the Legion
In addition to some of the cards mentioned above, how else would I like to round out the deck? Well, let's go over three cards I didn't mention but definitely want to add.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Burning-Tree+Emissary)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary)[Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) contributes to some of the most explosive draws beatdown decks have been able to produce in years. Any opening hand with two copies of this card puts a ton of power onto the board very quicklyâ€”especially if you open on a [Foundry Street Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Street+Denizen)! While the green mana it gives you doesn't let you cast your [Wojek Halberdiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wojek+Halberdiers), there are still plenty of one- and two-drops the Emissary can assist you in casting. This is a non-negotiable four-of. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Charm)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) This flexible spell is one of the defining reasons to play Boros. In conjunction with [Legion's Initiative](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Initiative), it really gives players who are leaning on [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) a tough time. It also gives the deck some reach, which the mono-red version didn't have a great source of. (Most played a copy or two of [Annihilating Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire) and used [Dynacharge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dynacharge) as a way to sneak in extra damage.) I'd definitely like four [Boros Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm)s. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Warleader%27s+Helix)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix) While I want to stay away from four-mana creatures, I'm okay with a couple copies of a four-mana spell, since you can cast it and have it make a difference as soon as you draw your fourth land. This is a fairly high-impact one, either turning the tides in the beatdown mirror or serving as another form of reach against the control decks. While is isn't as cheap to cast as [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) and can't kill off [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa), the life you gain back against beatdown decks (especially the mirror) is a big deal. What's the sideboard for? Well, to give you a quick rundown of the cards:

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Skullcrack)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullcrack) A lot of decks will, unsurprisingly, bring in lifegain to help fend off your assault. Not just [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), but cards like [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer), [Blood Baron of Vizkopa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Baron+of+Vizkopa), and [Warleader's Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix) are just a few of the cards you'll need to be worried about. One player who 4â€“0ed a recent **Magic Online** Daily Event was even running [Tablet of the Guilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tablet+of+the+Guilds)! (Which, admittedly, [Skullcrack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullcrack) isn't very good against.) 

 This triple-blast brain splitter of a card helps fight off this stream of lifegainâ€”all you have to do is leave mana up to cast it on the crucial turns. Be careful when you tap out after turn four or so in case you need to [Skullcrack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullcrack). (Or just represent [Skullcrack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullcrack) mana, which can often be scary enough!) 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mizzium+Mortars%2B%2BWarleader%27s+Helix)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars%2B%2BWarleader%27s+Helix)Against similarly placed red decks and some green decks, you can turn into a slightly more controlling deck that aims to take down their creatures. If you just trade off your creatures early and then have this suite of removal spells for the long game, you should be in pretty good shape.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Electrickery)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrickery)[Electrickery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrickery) is similar to what was mentioned above, but it is specifically for the Mono-Red mirror. Whereas Mortars is good against cards like [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) and [Call of the Conclave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Conclave), [Electrickery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrickery) is really only good against the Red deck's best draws. There's just one to supplement your other removal in that other matchup, and to help you not die to potential crazy draws. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Assemble+the+Legion)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion)[Assemble the Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion) is a long-game must-answer threat against control. Granted, control has [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) so it certainly can answer Assemble, but if control spends its Spheres fighting off your early plays that leaves Assemble to mop up. 

 Also, in a mostly unexplored format like Block I like being flexible with sideboard cards since you never know what you're going to face, and [Assemble the Legion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion) allows you to construct some on-the-fly sideboarding when necessary. Against some beatdown decks, for example, you can transform into a more controlling deck with [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) and [Warleader's Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix), then plan on winning the long game with Assemble. 

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Renounce+the+Guilds)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds)[Renounce the Guilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) is a pretty important Block Constructed card, removing everything from [Obzedat, Ghost Council](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) to [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) and everything in between. While you do have a lot of multicolored permanents that can get caught in this card's crosshairs, the ones you're removing are usually going to be far more problematic for you.
