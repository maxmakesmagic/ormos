### Piety

Matt Higgs, who made Part 1 with his Blitz deck, built an equally awesome Piety deck:


> 
> Honor, servitude and temperance. These are qualities of all clergy, both grand and meek. Whatever higher light they serve, clerics and monks adhere to a series of penitent axioms that dictate their every action. Discipline, fervor, and purity surround them like a clear mist; not a moment goes by without a liturgical thought or a holy deed.
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl164_iona.jpg)  

> 
> Their piety sets them apart from the laypeople that pass by, observing an old order to which they have eternally pledged themselves. Race, class, and station do not matter; only one's solemn vows and zealous devotion.
> 
> 
> PIETY is a deck based around those combatants who are pure of heart and mind; faith and selflessness are their armor, conviction and intolerance are their weapons. The deck itself utilizes stalwart clerics, monks and knights who yearn to defend the planeswalker who has called them. Meditate on the decklist and sling with purpose!
> 
> 
>

### Bacon?

Courtesy of one Brown Biggers, there is now a Bacon theme deck in **Magic**. But Mr. Biggers went a restrictive step further, which launched his submission into this article:


> 
> I chose BACON, because few simple words incite such a reaction amongst the nerd community. It's salty, savory, and despite being bad for you will flip you out of bed faster than anything - which leads me to the mechanic.
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl164_bacon.jpg)  

> 
> This deck is designed to flip enormous spells off the top of your deck as quickly as possible. [Bituminous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast) sizzles you into combo pieces. Ok, that's cascade, but sizzle makes more sense for theme and is more fun to say. [Noxious Revival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noxious+Revival) is the stinky, overcooked but still quite delicious leftovers from breakfast that make it to your lunchtime BLT. [Nemesis of Reason](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nemesis+of+Reason)? Can you think of a food that is worse for you, yet you can't stop eating? Then the [Omen Machine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omen+Machine) and [Crystal Ball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Ball) allow you to line up the perfect slices and get them onto the plate like the Omen of your impending 235 LDLs. [Butcher Orgg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+Orgg) delivers the choicest cuts. Chandra and the [Ancient Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Hellkite) are there to make sure that everything is cooked well, Bolas and [Blatant Thievery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blatant+Thievery) are there in case there's more on the plate across from you. [Bamboozle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bamboozle) and [Ornate Kanzashi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornate+Kanzashi) for when the roomie brings the applewood, and you're stuck with the store brand.

### Smoke

Steven Black went with Smoke as his chosen word:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl164_soot.jpg)  

> 
> Smoke is a deck that starts off by using enchantment cards like [Onslaught](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Onslaught) and [Smoke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smoke) to limit an opponent's blocking options. The deck is packed with ways of getting around you being only able to untap one creature per round yourself, such as the manlands created by [Koth of the Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) as well as the coup de grace in [Seize the Day](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seize+the+Day), which untaps a creature and allows an additional combat phase. Removal is [Scent of Cinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scent+of+Cinder) and [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot). [Scent of Cinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scent+of+Cinder) and [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot) are good against most of an opponent's early creatures, allowing you to get in your early damage and the smoke deck should kill things by choking them. If things stall, [Kulrath Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kulrath+Knight) and [Everlasting Torment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everlasting+Torment) can lockdown or minimize an opponent's creatures with -1/-1 counters. One of the most dangerous creatures in the deck is [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend) which should be untapped with [Smoke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smoke)'s effect, as the deck's sorceries pump its damage. Finally we have the source of the smoke, [Obsidian Fireheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Fireheart), which has set the land itself on fire.

> 
> Flavor-wise all the deck's creatures are Elementals, and mostly cinders from *Shadowmoor*, as they fit the smoke theme best. Another place that features a lot of smoke imagery is the forge, such as those of the Vulshoks, where [Koth of the Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) originates on Mirrodin. Kiln is the place within a forge that contains the flame and has a smokestack leading the smoke outside. The card [Smokestack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokestack) is a good way to get rid of [Smoke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smoke) if it becomes a problem for us such as against a vigilance-heavy deck.

### Party!

Griffin Guernsey typed up this comedic entry based around the word Party, complete with prime party animal [Animar, Soul of Elements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animar%2C+Soul+of+Elements):

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl164_animar.jpg)  

> 
> For one, Animar's power level has always amazed me. A steadily growing juggernaut that enables even more beefcakes to be played more rapidly AND has protection from two of the more destructive colors? Sounds great. He also fit the theme of a party (as more people attend, the more popular the party gets) and RUG just seems like party colors anyway.
> 
> 
> 

![Animar, Soul of Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Animar%2C+Soul+of+Elements)  

> 
> Animar is a party animal. [Tattermunge Maniac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattermunge+Maniac) is a maniac on the dance floor. [Rip-Clan Crasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rip-Clan+Crasher) crashes down the door to get to the punch bowl. There are your typical raging ragers. [Avarax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avarax) brought a few friends along (crazy things go on between him and Animar by the way). [Tattermunge Witch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattermunge+Witch) is the cranky chaperone. [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) is the annoying party prankster (read the flavor text). [Yavimaya Kavu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Kavu) is the shared spirit of everyone at the party! [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) is the bouncer (get it?). There's [Reflecting Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool), because it's a pool party, duh.
> 
> 
> The flavor/game plan of the deck as a whole is that everyone starts to party as soon as they get in the door (thus the haste).
> 
>

There were some jokesters. Ryan Printz was among them with his Inconceivable deck. Young kiddos are hereby advised to watch *The Princess Bride* as soon as humanly possible. Everyone else, read on:


> 
> The battle of wits has begun! It ends when you decide, and find out if you are right... or are wrong. It's so simple. All you have to do is divine from what you know of me: Am I the sort of man who would choose a card that I do not have or one I do? Now, a clever man would choose one he does not have, because he would know that only a great fool would agree with what he was told. You are not a great fool, so you can clearly not declare that I have the card. But did I know that you were not a great fool? Did I count on it? ### Syzygy

This is a word, folks. And Andrew Lawson chose it for this contest. Daring, but did he pull it off? Obviously, yes:


> 
> The word I chose was SYZYGY. In case you or your readers haven't heard of the word, the Wiktionary definition of syzygy is: A kind of unity, namely an alignment of three celestial bodies (for example, the Sun, Earth, and Moon) such that one body is directly between the other two, such as occurs at an eclipse.
> 
> 
> 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl164_greenSun.jpg)  

> 
> I could think of three main ways in which this idea could be applied to **Magic**:
> 
> 
> 
> 
> 
> 1. Cards with a celestial theme in their name or art.
> 2. Aligning several bodies in a particular conformation sounds a lot like assembling pieces of a combo.
> 3. Actually altering the alignment of the cards themselves tapping and untapping.
> 
> Here is the decklist I came up with based on these themes. I'll explain the individual card choices in more detail below.

> 
> [Yosei, the Morning Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yosei%2C+the+Morning+Star) - Fits all three categories. It not only has Star in its name, it can also be used as a combo piece to make your opponent skip all future untap steps.
> 
> 
> [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) - Fits categories 1 and 2 as it has Sun in its name and is involved in several combos in the deck.
> 
> 
> [Saffi Eriksdotter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saffi+Eriksdotter) - Fits category 2 only, but is one of the most important combo pieces. Can be fetched by [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) and recurred with [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) or [Yomiji, Who Bars the Way](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yomiji%2C+Who+Bars+the+Way).
> 
> 
> [Yomiji, Who Bars the Way](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yomiji%2C+Who+Bars+the+Way) - Fits category 2 only, combos with [Yosei, the Morning Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yosei%2C+the+Morning+Star) and [Saffi Eriksdotter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saffi+Eriksdotter).
> 
> 
> [Sunblast Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunblast+Angel) - Primarily fits in categories 1 and 3. However, does play a role in some of the combos in the deck.
> 
> 
> [Sunstrike Legionnaire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunstrike+Legionnaire) - Same as [Sunblast Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunblast+Angel) as it definitely fits categories 1 and 3 but can also be used in some of the less effective combos.
> 
> 
> [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden) - Primarily used as a combo piece (category 2).
> 
> 
> Zodiac animals - Primarily fit in category 1. Rather than choose all of the green zodiac creatures I chose the one with the highest power for each type of landwalk. The appropriate one(s) can be fetched with [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith).
> 
> 
> [Glissa Sunseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glissa+Sunseeker) - Primarily fits in category 1. Magic bullet that can be found with [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith).
> 
> 
> [Starved Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starved+Rusalka) - Primarily fits in category 2. An alternative sacrifice outlet to [Greater Good](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Good) which can be fetched with [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith).
> 
> 
> [Greater Good](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Good) - Primarily fits in category 2 as it is a key component in many of the combos.
> 
> 
> [Glare of Subdual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Subdual) - Primarily fits in category 3, although it is useful in some of the combos and the objects which emit most photons in our universe are suns / stars.
> 
> 
> [Guardian Zendikon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+Zendikon) - Probably unnecessary but to my mind the spherical zendikons look a lot like miniature planets, so category 1 but for art rather than name. Also useful as a speed bump to help ensure you get your combos online.
> 
> 
> [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) - Categories 1 and 2. The targets have been discussed above.
> 
> 
> [Sunlance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunlance) and [Sunscour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunscour) - Primarily fit in category 1. Much needed removal used to deal with early threats to ensure one or more of the combos can be assembled.
> 
> 
> [Second Sunrise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) - Primarily fits in category 1 (and to a limited extent in category 2).
> 
> 
> [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) - Primarily fits in category 1. Mana acceleration as most of the combos require a 6 or more CMC component.
> 
> 
> [Sunpetal Grove](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunpetal+Grove) and [Sungrass Prairie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sungrass+Prairie) - Both conveniently fit in category 1.
