Round 16: Jumping the Shark
===========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on December 11, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Walkinshaw-Profile.jpg)
By Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw











### Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa (Five-Color Control) vs. Love Janse (Elves)


Wearing the Swedish Shark Hat of Victory, Love Janse defeated Eric Froelich's Ooze combo deck in Round 15 to move to the top of the standings, closely pursued by Brazil's Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa.


**Game 1**


After losing the die roll by one, and watching his opponent lead with [Gilt-Leaf Palace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilt-Leaf+Palace) and [Heritage Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid), Damo da Rosa immediately lapsed into thought. An extended Elf deck can dump rather a lot onto the table in a short number of turns, so he knew he had to plan out his first few turns very carefully, finally deciding on a [Vivid Marsh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Marsh). Janse followed up with a [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman), which earned a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), and then an [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid).


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds10/r16_damodarosa_janse.jpg)  
*Love Janse and Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa match wits in the solemn competitive atmosphere of the arena. Also, shark hat.*


The Arch and [Heritage Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid)s represent the most threatening aspects of the Elf decks – the explosive mana. Letting Janse untap with those could easily allow his next turn to feature two more Elves and a [Regal Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force), ending the game not long after that. Damo da Rosa wasted no time killing them both with a [Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout), dropping to a reasonably healthy 15 life in the process.


Janse attempted to rebuild with a pair of [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and a [Joraga Treespeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker), but an offensive [Esper Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm) during Janse's upkeep left him with nothing in hand. A [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) for Damo da Rosa gave him room for a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), but Janse had a [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) for that. Another [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and an end of turn step [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) to bounce the Treespeaker fed nicely into a [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum), taking Damo da Rosa back up to 15 life. If that wasn't enough to convince Janse, a further [Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) was, and they were reaching for their sideboards.


Damo da Rosa 1, Janse 0


**Game 2**


Janse mulliganed away a three land, two [Regal Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Force), [Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), and [Mark of Asylum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+Asylum) hand. Damo da Rosa mulliganed as well, keeping three lands, [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens), [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize).


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds10/r16_janse.jpg)  
*It's a shark! It's a hat! It's a shark hat!​*


[Nettle Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) got in for 2 while Damo da Rosa again played a [Vivid Marsh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Marsh). Janse missed his second land drop, but at least summoned a [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), while Damo da Rosa took a [Heritage Druid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heritage+Druid) with a [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), leaving [Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) and [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap). Janse drew a second land, and summoned the Shaman, attacking Damo da Rosa down to 13. The Brazilian summoned his Wall, and passed the turn back with the Marsh open.


With two land, the Llanowar Elf, the [Nettle Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel) and the Shaman, Janse slipped into the tank. Finally, he discarded a second [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) to find and summon a [Burrenton Forge-Tender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender). Damo da Rosa just played another land and passed it back. Janse attacked him down to 9 and then 5, summoning another [Nettle Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Sentinel). At the end of Janse's turn, Damo da Rosa sent his [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) on a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), allowing him to untap and drop a [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) on the Swede, bringing him back up to 10 life.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/worlds10/r16_damodarosa.jpg)  
*\*thinkthinkthink\* \*thinkthinkthink\* \*winwinwin\**


Janse ditched the rest of his hand to the Ultimatum, sacrificed one of the Sentinels, and then attacked back for 4. Damo da Rosa cast [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), played a land and passed it back, using a [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) to tap down the Elves for a turn. He then summoned Jace to his side, [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)ed and again passed. Janse summoned an [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid), and a second [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) kept the Elves at bay. Damo da Rosa untapped, and Janse groaned as Jace bounced the Forge-Tender. Sure enough, a [Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) cleaned out Janse's team. Damo da Rosa followed up with a [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), but Janse responded with a smile and a handshake.


Damo da Rosa 2, Love Janse 0
