So I sat here, typing up deck lists, staring at my computer screen, while everyone else around me was building decks, casting spells and generally smiling. I couldn't take it anymore. I looked at the glistening white packs of *Avacyn Restored* beside me. I looked back at my computer. I did that looking order again. It was no use. If we had the power to resist such urges, we might not be here today.


I opened six packs, and instinctively sorted them by color and converted mana cost.


It looked like the makings of a pretty good Sealed deck. But just to verify, I took the cards to some of my friends to see what they thought about it, and how they might build it differently than I would. I found both perennial champion Brian Kibler and Mr. Hot-Streak Matt Costa (In this last month alone, Matt Costa has finished in the Top 16 of two Grand Prixes, and seemingly couldn't lose at the Star City Games Invitational. This is, of course, not to mention his Pro Tour *Dark Ascension* Top 8 finish which wasn't too long ago, now was it.). They sat down with me, one at a time, and talked about the pool.


Here's the pool I gave them. What would they think? What has changed in their card opinions since the beginnings of *Avacyn Restored*? How would ya'll build this?

Everything is just mashing up against everything else.


"There's a real tension between the Soulbond and the 'Loner' mechanics," and Kibler continued that kind of puts black at odds with everything else (perhaps a loner, if you will). And without specific sets of cards, it's just not going to work.


Even [Barter in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barter+in+Blood) , which seems like a knockout, is a card Costa said is just not worth it here. " [Barter in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barter+in+Blood) is weird. Your decks needs to be able to set up into it to really benefit from it. This deck can't do that, and all the cards you want to play are four-drops, which makes casting it all the more awkward. And with a [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) or two, both Costa and Kibler said they would've brought that to the tables.


Both players gawked at the card pool after submitting their final builds to me and told me they can only hope to have the building issues they've been discussing with me with their own Sealed pool for the Grand Prix. I wished them both luck and went back to my computer screen. Sigh.

Roundtable: Live **Magic** Streams, with Sam Black, Brian Kibler and Tom Martell
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Josh Bennett*
Live **Magic** Video is the biggest thing to hit our game in a long time, and judging by the viewership our appetite for it is voracious. Meeting this demand is the rise of live **Magic** Online streams, where pros and amateurs alike show off their skills and chat with the audience. Simply load up the page, and a video window gives you a player's-eye-view of their **Magic** Online client. Alongside this is a chat window where you can type in your questions and comments for the streamer.


Three of the most-watched **Magic** streamers are in attendance this weekend: Sam Black, Brian Kibler and Tom Martell. After building their sleep-in special Sealed Decks, I grabbed them to pick their brains about streaming.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvan12/Roundtable.jpg)
To start things off I lobbed the obvious softball: Why stream?


All three gave me a puzzled look and said in near unison "Because it's fun," but after a brief laugh at my expense, they moved into a serious discussion.


Black: It's a lot of fun being able to interact with your audience. But even beyond that it gives you a good feel for what people are interested in. As a writer that's an invaluable restource.


Martell: One thing I really like is the instructional element. I used to teach chess, so I definitely enjoy explaining strategies and lines of play. It's also helpful to me because it forces me to articulate the reasoning behind some of the plays I make. In **Magic** you make a lot of mental shortcuts, you have to, and sometimes you can unknowingly get stuck with the wrong ones. Your audience can help challenge your assumptions.


Kibler: I love having that interactivity. Another big thing for me is getting that instant feedback from the audience. You get to see a lot of different points of view.


Black: In a way that really helps give you a "Big Picture" look at a format. When I stream a draft, my viewers will be telling me their thoughts for each pick. That gives me a better understanding of what the popular view of a partiuclar card is, even if it's not the correct one.


Kibler: And of course I love the celebrity of it. You know, it's awesome to have another way to connect with my fans.


Here I interjected. Isn't it difficult to maintain that rapport, the constant interactivity with your audience, while playing **Magic**?


Kibler: Oh you play worse.


Martell: You play substantially worse.


Black: I'll tell my audience, yes, I'm terrible. I'm making a ton of mistakes here. But it's worth it. It's more important to have fun and be entertaining.


Kibler: I've had some catastrophic misplays just because I was so caught up talking with the audience.


So then it's not about maintaining the highest level of play?


Kibler: Well yes and no. I mean you still play as best you can, but I wouldn't stream a big tournament like the MOCS, that I really wanted to win.


Martell: Especially since your hand is basically on display for anyone who wants to see it. For the most part though the audience is actually really great that way. I once had some troll messaging my opponent what was in my hand, and my opponent told me what was happening and apologized.


Kibler: Yeah I've had people in my stream channel say "Oh you're in the same draft as me, so I'm shutting you off now."


Black: It really is more personal that way. You get that kind of a connection with your audience.


Martell: And it's an opportunity to show a bit more personality than might come through during match coverage, when you're completely focused on the game at hand. You can really tailor your stream to give your audience something specific.


Kibler: Yeah my rule of thumb is that I wouldn't stream something I wouldn't write an article about.


Black: I also really like it for trying out new constructed decks. I get to learn along with my audience.


Kibler: Although there's a danger there of brewing up something awesome. Like before GP Anaheim I was streaming an *Innistrad* Block Jund deck, and then at the GP it was all over the top tables.


Any closing thoughts?


Kibler: I think **Magic** streaming is still growing. More people are learning about it, and more people are streaming regularly. It's starting to be a bit like TV. During the week, you know some people are going to be on, and so you tune in.


Martell: I think as a tool for improving your game it's one of the best. Reading an article can only teach you so much, but this, being able to talk to pros about the specifics of what they're doing, there's no substitute for that.

[Brian Kibler: (twitch.tv/bmkibler)](http://twitch.tv/bmkibler)

Round 6 Feature Match - Terry Lau vs. Jackie Lee
------------------------------------------------


*by Marc Calderaro*
Unless you're a dinosaur like some of us, you probably haven't heard of Vancouver inhabitant Terry Lau. His last big finish in the Top 8 of Canadian Nationals in 2000, and as we talked he mused that he remembered when [Channel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Channel) - [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) was a deck, not a team. After he sat down in the feature match area, he wallowed in the spaciousness of the two chairs at a table all by itself  a relief from the cramped quarters inside the endless rows of tables. He adjusted a little bit in his seat and said, "This is nice. You know, for the next event, they should offer luxury seats. For just a little extra." I immediately called my supervisor; this guy's got the entrepreneurial spirit, I tell you. These are the sort of suggestions I expect from an engineer such as he.


Contrasting to Lau and my dinosaurosity is Jackie Lee. This past year has certainly been her breakout in the tournament scene, and she's been putting up some seriously consistent finishes and her articles for Star City Games are some of the best in the business.


The two shuffled up and readied for the round. Lau had a quick and powerful red and black deck, sporting the classic removal, burn and just-slightly-better-than-average creatures. Lee was playing a green and white deck that could certainly overpower Lau's, if the deck could stop the initial assault.


Both players kept their hands and we were off.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvan12/fm6%20Lee%20R6.JPG)*Jackie Lee*
### Game 1


Terry Lau started the party with a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) , [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and a [Butcher Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+Ghoul) , while Jackie Lee's deck offered her a turn two [Thraben Valiant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Valiant) .


Lau cast a [Searchlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searchlight+Geist) followed by a [Havengul Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Vampire) , attacking Lee for the first points of damage. Lee fired back with a flashed [Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger) and a [Moonlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Geist) . The two flying Geists traded and that meek [Havengul Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Vampire) started to get a little scary. Lee thought for a bit before eventually laying some [Angelic Armaments](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Armaments) .


After taking some damage from a second [Searchlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searchlight+Geist) , and getting to watch a second [Havengul Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Vampire) join the first, Lee equipped her Valiant and tied up the scores at 16-16. After another round of attacks, Lee bound the Avenger to a [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) and passed the turn back. The Vampires were getting bigger, but the threat of Deathtouch held them at bay.


The two continued to trade blows, but Lau was holding back some goodies, he just needed the right moment. Lee had a trusted Forcemage, [Righteous Blow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Blow) and a [Wildwood Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildwood+Geist) s sitting in her hands. She cast the Forcemage, bonding it with the Valiant and swung in. Lau gave his [Searchlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searchlight+Geist) Deathtouch and blocked. Lee considered, then decided to cast her [Righteous Blow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Blow) on the Geist. After Lau put the Geist in the graveyard, he had to ask for another die as his [Havengul Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Vampire) s continued to grow  one was a 3/3, the other a 5/5. It was 12-14.


This next turn was the big one for Lau. [Death Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Wind) on Lee's [Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger) might just have been the critical play of the game. With the regenerator gone, and the Peddler no longer with Deathtouch (not to mention the Vampires ever-growing), Lau felt safe to send in his whole team. The [Butcher Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+Ghoul) , [Riot Ringleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riot+Ringleader) and two Vampires crashed headlong into the [Trusted Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusted+Forcemage) paired with the [Thraben Valiant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Valiant) (with its Armaments) and the lonely [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) . After tons of variations put forth, Lee blocked the Ghoul with the Valiant, and the Ringleader with the Forcemage  she took ten. And she wasn't out of the woods yet. She then watched an [Essence Harvest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Harvest) drain her for the rest of her life.


If you've ever doubted the grinding power of a [Havengul Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havengul+Vampire) , try facing two of them.


Terry Lau 1  0 Jackie Lee


There was a bit of a time delay as the GGsLive feature match finished up early, so these two were moved onto the camera match. After a bit of waiting around (that's television, folks), the players saddled up for the second game.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvan12/fm6%20Lau%20calmly%20crushing%20Lee.jpg)*Terry Lau*
### Game 2


[Butcher Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+Ghoul) again started out the match for Lau, and it also came in for the initial point. Lee cracked back with her [Moonlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Geist) , and again with the [Angelic Armaments](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Armaments) . Lau's [Bloodflow Connoisseur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodflow+Connoisseur) was given some [Lightning Prowess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Prowess) as Lau even the scores at 18-18.


After Lee cast a [Wildwood Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildwood+Geist) and her flyer took Lau to 16, he shot the 2/1 flyer down with his pinging Connoisseur. When Lau got the turn back, he cast a second [Butcher Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+Ghoul) and a [Lightning Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) , pairing them together and hit with the Connoisseur. This match was looking like classic Red-Black vs. Green. A bunch of scrappy, annoying guys against one big, giant thing. When the [Wildwood Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildwood+Geist) suited up with the [Angelic Armaments](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Armaments) , it got even bigger and giant-ier.


The [Wildwood Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildwood+Geist) made a huge, flying swing and took Lau to 9, but the backlash was just as fierce. The Vampire, the Zombies and the [Berserk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserk) er all came crashing in. Though Lee had the [Righteous Blow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Blow) for the Mauler, she still sunk to 7, and a post-combat [Thunderous Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderous+Wrath) took out her only creature.


Lee replaced it quickly with a [Gryff Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gryff+Vanguard) , but Lau had none of that, dude. And for the second time in the match, a [Death Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Wind) decided the game. Lau calmly took out the flying bird, and attacked in for lethal damage.


Terry Lau 2  0 Jackie Lee


With one more win, Lau will secure a Day-Two appearance as he rises to 6-0. Jackie Lee will have to win a couple more as she drops to 5-1.

Round 7 Feature Match - Charlie Charrois vs. Owen Turtenwald
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Josh Bennett* 
Charlie Charrois is a relatively new player from White Rock, BC, who picked up the game during *Shards of Alara* block. Sitting at 5-1, he's actually excited to find himself paired up against last year's Player of the Year Owen Turtenwald. After all, to be the best, you must beat the best.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvan12/r7CharroisTurtenwald.jpg)

*Long time Pro Owen meets newcomer Charlie* 
### Game 1


Turtenwald was first on the board with a third-turn [Moonlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Geist) . Charrois passed on two forests and an island, and Turtenwald added [Corpse Traders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Traders) to his team. A [Nettle Swine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettle+Swine) from Charrois forced Turtenwald to hold the Traders back. He hit for two in the air and added [Marrow Bats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marrow+Bats) .


Charrois played [Trusted Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusted+Forcemage) , bonded to his Swine. He attacked with the big pig, and after Turtenwald made the expected block and regenerate with his Bats, Charrois played [Fleeting Distraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleeting+Distraction) for the save. Turtenwald untapped and dropped [Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) , immediately sacrificing it to his [Corpse Traders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Traders) . Charrois turned over his hand: [Revenge of the Hunted](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revenge+of+the+Hunted) , [Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrifying+Presence) and three lands. After binning the Revenge, Turtenwald was in great shape. He hit for six in the air.


Charrois hit back for eight and played the [Nephalia Smuggler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Smuggler) he'd just drawn. Turtenwald attacked with his fliers, drawing out the [Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrifying+Presence) . He played [Voice of the Provinces](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Provinces) and passed the turn. Charrois drew and conceded.


Turtenwald 1 - Charrois 0


### Game 2


Charrois chose to play, but after a pair of lands and a [Wingcrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingcrafter) he stopped putting permanents on the board, despite cycling a [Fleeting Distraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleeting+Distraction) . Turtenwald played out four lands and a [Corpse Traders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Traders) . Charrois hit a land and dropped [Trusted Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusted+Forcemage) , bonding it and attacking for two. Turtenwald swung back with his [Corpse Traders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Traders) and Charrois immediately traded away his Forcemage. Turtenwald replaced the Traders with [Seraph of Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seraph+of+Dawn) .


Charrois played a second Forcemage and chose not to bond it. Turtenwald attacked for two, but had no play on his six mana. Meanwhile Charrois had [Fettergeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) to soulbond to his [Trusted Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusted+Forcemage) , dominating the air. Turtenwald spent another blank turn and passed. Charrois hit for four and passed.


Turtenwald took some of the sting out of the [Fettergeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) with [Ghoulflesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulflesh) , relying on his [Seraph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seraph) to hold the fort. Charrois flashed in [Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger) at end of turn, pulling further ahead. Charrois swung out with his [Fettergeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) , [Trusted Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trusted+Forcemage) and [Wolfir Avenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Avenger) . [Seraph of Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seraph+of+Dawn) blocked, and Turtenwald was down to just six.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvan12/r7Charrois.jpg)

*Charrois attempts to even the score*

Charrois got another card off his [Gryff Vanguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gryff+Vanguard) and played [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage) . Charrois showed him [Terrifying Presence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terrifying+Presence) and Turtenwald chose to pay four life to regenerate, leaving him at just four. Charrois cluttered the board with [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) and [Geist Trappers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+Trappers) .


Turtenwald drew and considered things for a moment, then tapped a ton of mana to [Death Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Wind) away the [Geist Trappers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+Trappers) , going up to five from his [Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) . He swung in for seven with his fliers, threatening lethal the following turn with [Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) holding the ground.


Charrois had other plans. He untapped and played [Wingcrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingcrafter) , sending [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) to the air and dealing exactly five.

With pen and paper Conley worked out just how bad a Thatcher's Revolt could be. The answer was "Pretty bad."


He hit for five, leaving Romero at eight, then played out [Maalfeld Twins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maalfeld+Twins) and [Wingcrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingcrafter) , bonding them. According to his calculations then ten toughness would be just enough. It turned out he didn't need to worry, and they were soon on to game two.


Woods 1 - Romero 0


### Game 2


Romero got off to a faster start, opening with [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) and [Moonlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Geist) . Woods failed to play anything on his first three land, and Romero had Thatcher's Revolt, so Woods began his fourth turn with just seven life. He drew and played [Fettergeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) . Romero played his fifth land and passed.


Woods solidified his defences with [Elgaud Shieldmate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elgaud+Shieldmate) . Again Romero had nothing but a land. Woods gassed up with [Amass the Components](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amass+the+Components) . Still nothing from Romero. Woods took that as the green light to go on the offensive. He killed the [Moonlight Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonlight+Geist) with [Ghoulflesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulflesh) , then sent the Striker home with [Mist Raven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mist+Raven) . He hit for five and passed.


Romero nearly beamed as he untapped and then tapped six for [Terminus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) .



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvan12/r9Romero.JPG)

*Romero takes game 2 with faster start* 
Woods wasn't done yet. He summoned [Maalfeld Twins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maalfeld+Twins) . Romero spilled out a paired [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) and [Hanweir Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hanweir+Lancer) . Woods untapped and then played [Vessel of Endless Rest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Endless+Rest) and [Demonic Rising](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Rising) , getting a 5/5.


Romero wasted no time on his turn. He tapped a quick five for [Lightning Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) and then [Kessig Malcontents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Malcontents) , bonding them together and attacking for what was suddenly lethal damage.


Woods 1 - Romero 1


### Game 3


Romero had to send back his opener, but stayed on six. He had a turn-two [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) . Woods cycled [Fleeting Distraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleeting+Distraction) and played [Demonic Taskmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Taskmaster) . Romero added [Hanweir Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hanweir+Lancer) , paired it up to his Striker, and hit for three. Woods Amassed the Components, holding his Taskmaster back to block.


[Kessig Malcontents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Malcontents) from Romero put Woods to fourteen, and then he hit with his Striker for three more. All that was undone when Woods dropped [Homicidal Seclusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homicidal+Seclusion) and drained him for seven.


Despite that, Romero was still in the game. He played [Spectral Gateguards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Gateguards) , soulbonding it to his Malcontents, then hit for eight. Woods was down to ten, soon to be seventeen. Woods hit for seven more, then played [Wingcrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wingcrafter) and Bone Spliters to get rid of the [Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) . He added [Haunted Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Guardian) to his board and passed.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpvan12/r9Woods.JPG)

*Woods finishes off his opponent in 3.* 
Romero took a moment to think, then hit for two with his Gateguards. Woods took the damage. Romero summoned both [Lightning Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) and [Devout Chaplain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Chaplain) , pairing them so that he could destroy the lethal [Homicidal Seclusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homicidal+Seclusion) . Woods sacrificed his Guardian, hit for four, then played [Driver of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Driver+of+the+Dead) .


"Are you dead?" he asked.


"Am I dead? What a question!"


"Well, it seems like we're at that point of the game. Either you're dead or I am."


"It does seem that way," agreed Romero


Romero attacked all-out. Woods carefully considered his blocks, before deciding to block [Devout Chaplain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Chaplain) with [Driver of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Driver+of+the+Dead) . Nine damage left him at just six life. What trick did Romero have up his sleeve?


As it turns out, nothing. When Woods turned his Taskmaster sideways, Romero extended the hand.
