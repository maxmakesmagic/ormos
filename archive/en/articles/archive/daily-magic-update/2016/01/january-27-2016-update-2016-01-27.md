The January 27, 2016 Update
===========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on January 27, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on January 27, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by [weird, brutal blasts](#weird), [the weekly Top 25](#top), and [Tooth and Nail for a God and a Multiversal horror](#modern).


Today's Must
============


1. [Limited Resources 321—*Oath of the Gatewatch* Initial Impressions](http://lrcast.com/limited-resources-321-oath-of-the-gatewatch-initial-impressions/) | Limited Resources | Marshall Sutcliffe and Luis Scott-Vargas

Now that the set is released and they have a few drafts under their belt, Marshall and Luis share their initial impressions of the format in a taught 86-minute podcast. I'm not sure how they got it down that short, given that the discussion of [Loam Larva](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Larva) alone probably takes 85 minutes, but that's why they're the podcasters and I write pithy blurbs about podcasters.


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [MtA Episode 157: PPTQ Blues](http://www.magictheamateuring.com/mta-episode-157-pptq-blues/) | Magic the Amateuring | Meghan Wolff and Maria Bartholdi

There are so many topics going on in this podcast, I have no idea how to write the summary. Mostly it's PPTQ stuff, but then there's some kind of licking game and something about [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). Just go listen to it.


1. [Top Ten *Oath of the Gatewatch* cards for Modern with Brian David-Marshall](http://www.rocketjump.com/listen/top-ten-oath-of-the-gatewatch-cards-for-modern-with-brian-david-marshall) | Masters of Modern | Alex Kessler and Ben Bateman

Brian David-Marshall, he of the Pro Tour and this here website thingy, joins the Masters of Modern crew to pick apart the best cards for Modern from *Oath of the Gatewatch*. Spoiler alert: [Loam Larva](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Larva) does not make anyone's list.


1. [Hot Decks from New Standard](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13089&writer=Seth+Manfield&articledate=1-27-2016) | TCGPlayer | Seth Manfield

Standard is just getting started with *Oath of the Gatewatch*, but your reigning World Champion, Seth Manfield, is already digging in deep with the best Standard decks to emerge so far. He has one clear frontrunner—you get one guess, [Sam Black](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2016SSSC)—and a number of other contenders to consider. Somehow my [Loam Larva](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Larva) recursion strategy isn't among them.


1. [Standard Power Rankings](http://www.channelfireball.com/power-rankings/) | ChannelFireball

Speaking of early returns on Standard, did you know ChannelFireball puts out power rankings from time to time listing cards that are making waves in Standard? This week, five new entrants jump on the list—appropriate, given the new set—and you'll never believe which uncommons top the list!

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Modern Overgrowth](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/modern-constructed-league-2016-01-26)

Modern Green Devotion has been around for a while, usually featuring [Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx) and cards with a lot of green mana symbols, such as [Leatherback Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leatherback+Baloth) and [Wistful Selkie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wistful+Selkie). But *Magic Online* player Uegjo went a slightly different direction and emphasized the land untapping that green has gotten so good at. With [Voyaging Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voyaging+Satyr), [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), or [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker), you can load up a Forest with [Utopia Sprawl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Sprawl) and [Overgrowth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrowth) to create a ton of mana. And since your targets are usually basic Forests, you can dodge [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) and [Fulminator Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage). Just watch out for [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter). At any rate, it's cool to see someone go 5-0 with [Tooth and Nail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) once again, and Toothing up [Xenagos, God of Revels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xenagos%2C+God+of+Revels) and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) seems like a massively good time.
