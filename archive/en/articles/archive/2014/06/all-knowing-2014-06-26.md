### DAILY DECK


All Knowing
===========



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)

Adam Prosak

From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer.


June 26, 2014
 







If you can cast anything without paying it's mana cost, what would you want to cast?


Logan Mize would recommend [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). And if that's not good enough, another copy of [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Omniscience)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience)
There is a simplistic beauty to this deck. No fancy frills, no backup plans. Just a hyper-efficient plan to cast the biggest, baddest monster in *Magic: The Gathering*.


The basic idea is to cast a [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), putting [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) into play. After that, the goal is to find an [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn), and cast that for free. That will give you an extra turn, and an annihilator trigger that will likely decimate the opponent's board. With [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) in play, this deck is very capable of chaining search spells, and [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) (or [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) for [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition)) can find the needed Emrakul.


One of biggest strengths of this deck involves the sixteen blue cantrips for a single mana. [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), and [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) are all excellent cards that help piece together combos very quickly. These cards give this deck the speed and consistency needed to succeed in Legacy.
