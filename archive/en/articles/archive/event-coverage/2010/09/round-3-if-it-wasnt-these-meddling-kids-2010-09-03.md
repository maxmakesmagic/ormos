Round 3: If It Wasn't For These Meddling Kids
=============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 3, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_43.png)
By Tobias Henke












Marco Orsini-Jones (Ad Nauseam) vs. Conley Woods (Bant)
-------------------------------------------------------


"Is this were they'll show the Top 8?" asked famed deck designer Conley Woods, looking at the big auditorium the stage of which builds this weekend's feature match arena. He got the answer that this is indeed where the Top 8 games will take place, to which he replied: "OK, so I will be here."


He certainly was well on his way with a 2-0 start into the tournament, but so was his opponent, Marco Orsini-Jones. Orsini-Jones was running [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) / [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace), while Woods came with a Bant deck of his very own.


### Game 1


Both players spent the first two turns on mana development: Woods had [Glacial Fortress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress) and turned [Misty Rainforest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest) into a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) without the rain and mist, while Orsini-Jones was a little more busy with [Vivid Creek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Creek) and two [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom)s, then [Underground River](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River) and another [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom). The real action started, however, on Woods's side with a turn-three [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), whereas Orsini-Jones was still preparing for his big turn with [Coalition Relic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptams10/r3_woods.jpg)*Conley Woods loves his home brews; rumor has it he built his deck just last night.*
Woods passed his next turn without an attack and without any play except for another land, signalling some form of counter***Magic*** in his hand. The first two Lotuses bloomed, without Woods interfering. When Orsini-Jones failed to cast anything interesting, Woods just returned Orsini-Jones's [Coalition Relic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic) to his hand via [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command).


The Knight attacked for 4, and it was again back to Orsini-Jones, who cast his third [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) and recast [Coalition Relic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic).


"I don't think I have any problem with you having lots of mana," Woods said, then thought a bit and added, "Well, actually I do, I just don't have anything I can do about that."


Instead he chose to take the fight to a different level and cast [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage). Orsini-Jones responded with [Pact of Negation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation), but Woods responded with [Bant Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant+Charm). The Mage resolved and Woods named [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam).


Meanwhile [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) had Orsini-Jones down to 11. With his opponent completely tapped out, Orsini-Jones went for [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) into [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) into ... well, [Simian Spirit Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide).


"Casting it?" Woods asked, and raised an eyebrow.


"Casting it," Orsini-Jones sighed.


[Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) removed the blocker, before [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) and [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) charged in to put Orsini-Jones at 5 life. In the meantime, Woods's [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) met [Pact of Negation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation). Orsini-Jones payed for the Pact, played a land, and passed the turn right back to Woods.


His attack put Orsini-Jones at 1, thanks to his [Angel's Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace). Woods cast another [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and this time it resolved. He checked the top card of Orsini-Jones library, left it in place, and Orsini-Jones quickly shuffled it up, when he saw what that was.


Woods 1, Marco Orsini-Jones 0


### Game 2


Orsini-Jones was unhappy with his opening seven. He took quite some time to decide before finally keeping. Woods was quicker with his hand and almost instantly mulliganed his seven. His response to his six cards was equally quickâ€”and emphatic: "Keep."


Once again, Orsini-Jones started with [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom), though this time only one. Woods summoned [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), then a second [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) and a [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) naming [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptams10/r3-orsini-jones.jpg)*Orsini-Jones implores his Ad Nauseam deck to cough up the goods.*
Orsini-Jones had no third land, but he did have a second [Lotus Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) which he suspended as well. [Clear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clear)ly, he meant to deal with the first Mage soon. Woods, however, summoned a second [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) and named [Punishing Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) to protect his army from undue tampering. Then, he cast a third [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage), naming [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam). Orsini-Jones made a sad face.


Woods: "Yeah, my opening hand was double [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch), [Horizon Canopy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Canopy), triple [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage). A pretty sick six-card hand against [Ad Nauseam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam)."


Orsini-Jones could only agree, as the 2/2s took him down.


Woods 2, Orsini-Jones 0
