Tales from the Opening Bell
===========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on October 7, 2010 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











This past weekend gave us the first appearance of *Scars of Mirrodin* in heavy-fisted Constructed competition. The fur flew. Mana was Leaked. New planeswalkers appeared, and Top Decks-featured preview cards once again kicked butt.

But most of all, [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)s rumbled through the Red Zone, turning it green with all their combat-[Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate)d lands.

Let's explore:

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_chart.jpg)  
Red-Green Valakut Ramp
This format—at least one week in—seems to be ruled by [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)s.

There are all different kinds of [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) decks. We have seen decks that fuel [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force), various three-color decks (some employing additional Titans), and Turboland. There are even pure beatdown decks, just touching for [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) and flipping up [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)s for free! And of course we have the two distinct tribes of Titan decks (probably the most popular pre-*Scars of Mirrodin*) featured in this Top 8.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_pTitan.jpg)  
Of the Titan sub-metagame, it seems that Red-Green [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) Ramp is the most powerful Titan deck (I would humbly submit, as a former Green-White [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) player, that the beatdown style is by far the weakest player in intra-Titan conflicts, as it can only get ahead, not catch up).

TCGPlayer Champion Jack Vargas played essentially a classic Red-Green [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) Ramp deck, enhanced by new planeswalker, [Koth of the Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer).

How does this deck work?

In the early game, the Red-Green [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) Ramp deck uses its various acceleration cards to set up the big spells. [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar) is a legitimate finisher by itself—especially with all the ways to play multiple lands in a single turn—but the name comes from this deck's [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut)-centered butt-kicking capabilities.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_koth.jpg)  
The Red-Green [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) Ramp deck, as a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) deck, is bound to a certain amount of green mana, which is required to set up not only the ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif)[Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), but a large number of [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s. [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)'s job is usually to get a couple of copies of [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) onto the battlefield. With sufficient [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, each [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) becomes a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) gattling gun, such that with every attack, a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) finding more and more [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s can hit for an additional 3, 6, or 12 damage—maybe even more!

Eldrazi Ramp
This mono-green deck was largely popularized in the United States by Conrad Kolos at U.S. Nationals 2010. Conrad conceded that the mono-Green Eldrazi Ramp deck might have been a dog to the Red-Green [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) Ramp ([Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) can take out the straight green deck's [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)), but the green deck has such a powerful and different route paved by *its* specialty lands.

[Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) is a powerhouse that lets the Eldrazi Ramp player find a variety of threats, the most powerful of which is [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)! [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple)s help the deck play any of the expensive-but-effective colorless cards at a discount.

Tapal played largely a conventional Eldrazi Ramp deck. This deck is almost an Eldrazi-linear strategy, with basically all the tournament playable Eldrazi cards (save maybe [Eldrazi Conscription](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Conscription)) in the main deck. 

In this deck, [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) is used to find [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) and [Eldrazi Temple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) to set up the even bigger threat creatures, or [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) as a safeguard against Red-Green [Valakut](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut) Ramp or White-Blue Control.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_bomb.jpg)  
Tapal's primary addition from *Scars of Mirrodin* is [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb). Presumably he did not play [Joraga Treespeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker) or [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement), as they can both go boom when the [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) gets angry at the beatdown.

Landale and Li tore up both the TCGPlayer Trials and the Swiss in the $5K main event with their subtly awesome update to the classic.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

You will notice a couple of distinct differences between the Landale/Li list and Tapal's. First of all, they play both [Joraga Treespeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker) and [Overgrown Battlement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Battlement). As such, they are much faster in the mirror; in addition, they played all four copies of [Growth Spasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spasm). Landale shared that [Growth Spasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spasm) helps the deck play the fastest possible [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) ... even faster than [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate)!

And not only did they not play new card [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb), they didn't play the otherwise ubiquitous [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) either. Instead, the [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) slot is occupied by the sixth Titan, [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine).

Read [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) for a second.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=All+Is+Dust)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust)  
That's right! While this [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) can search up Eldrazi, it doesn't "search up Eldrazi." It searches up *any* colorless creature ... including [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)! [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) is like an artifact [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) in this deck; [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin)-available, easy to cast, and faster to play than the Eldrazi Giants—but no less resistant to [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust). 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_dust.jpg)  
[Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) is both resistant to White-Blue removal and absolute hell for the beatdown. 

One of the things that impressed me about this deck is how much attention the duo spent on the mirror. Case in point: They played all four copies of [Terastodon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) specifically for other [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) Ramp decks.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wurmcoil+Engine)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)

Elves is a deck designed to look at its hand, dump all the cards onto the battlefield, and use the massive amount of mana those cards produce to take massive action.

This can be somewhat vulnerability exposing (especially given the presence of not only historical sweepers like [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), but awesome new cards like [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb)). But Elves counterbalances that with the mighty [Eldrazi Monument](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_ezuri.jpg)  
In a deck with mostly Elves, this three-drop is essentially a walking [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun). Elves can easily cast a first turn [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) or [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), get the ball rolling, and then fire big muscles-style. [Ezuri, Renegade Leader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ezuri%2C+Renegade+Leader) is also a fine shield against removal or even combat interaction.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_wave.jpg)  
What to do with a ton of excess green mana production? How about dumping it all into a [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave)? This deck can hit anything but [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) itself with a [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave); sometimes—when playing eight or more—it can even run out [Eldrazi Monument](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument)!

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_arbiter.jpg)  
[Eldrazi Monument](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument) will defend Elves from [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), but not [All Is Dust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust). That's where [Tajuru Preserver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tajuru+Preserver) comes in! This little two-drop is not only also an Elf for [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) and [Ezuri, Renegade Leader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ezuri%2C+Renegade+Leader), he stops the stompings by way of [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Infinite+Gyre) or any other gigantic Eldrazi creatures.

... Not that it stops you from taking 10-15 on the jaw, or anything, but you can race.

How does this deck work?

Ford's deck has four basic classes of cards:


> * Mana/ This includes lands (obviously), as well as the four copies of [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice). Jason played a whopping *half* mana to enable his über-expensive high-end spells.
> * Cheap one-for-ones. This includes [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), which trades itself one-for-one for something else, and all the [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning)s and [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s that answer opposing threats.
> * Medium card advantage: [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm); [Jace's Ingenuity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Ingenuity); [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor).
> * Big stuff.
> 

Jason's big stuff was all either [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force) or things that lace up with [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force), for example [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) and [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan). The Titans have 6 toughness, and therefore live through [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force). 

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_tickle.jpg)  
Essentially Ford would take over the game with his one-for-ones, then put his stamp on it with one of his Titans. [Destructive Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Destructive+Force) was not necessary, strictly, for the win ... but it certainly made games difficult for the other guy.

White-Blue Control might seem like a straight port from pre-*Scars of Mirrodin* Standard, but if you look at the specific choices that Pedro Quintero made, you will see a deck that is about as rich in the new set as possible.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td111_arbiter.jpg)  
I am not going to go through the basics of how to play a White-Blue Control deck as it is the oldest and most well known serious strategy, ever. Instead I'll talk about all the new cards that Pedro played, and how they fit in.


> **[Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb):** This card seems awesome in White-Blue Control. As hinted, I played a Green-White [Summoning Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) deck with lots of cheap accelerators ([Joraga Treespeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker), [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), and so on). While the deck was a slight dog to other [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) decks, if there was one deck that I consistently smashed, it was White-Blue Control ... but not Pedro's build! The mere presence of [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) forced me to re-think how to approach the match-up ... and was absolutely punishing when I was wrong.
> 
> **[Elspeth Tirel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth+Tirel):** Pedro never actually used the [Nevinyrral's Disk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk) ability on the weekend! He used it primarily for tokens production, would consider running a third.
> 
> **[Leonin Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Arbiter):**[Leonin Arbiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Arbiter) is a powerful way to slow down both decks that rely on cards like [Arid Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) and green [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) decks (whose entire early games are typically a conclave of [Cultivate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultivate)s).
> 
> **[Revoke Existence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revoke+Existence):** Catchall enchantment/artifact removal.
> 
> **[Volition Reins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volition+Reins):** Pedro shared a great story about stealing—believe it or not—a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) on the way to the Top 8.
> 
> **[Seachrome Coast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seachrome+Coast):** Apparently these enter the battlefield untapped even if they are your third land (write that down); Pedro played them to help his consistency hitting [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) on the first turn.
>
