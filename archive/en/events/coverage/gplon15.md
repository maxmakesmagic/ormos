London's Own Anteri Takes Third Trophy
======================================




Playing on home turf, Fabrizio Anteri defeated Matteo Moure in an all-Abzan finals to become champion of Grand Prix London 2015, claiming his third title in his sixth Top 8.

TOP 8 COVERAGE



Standard is still evolving.  After Grand Prix San Diego last weekend players were left with a tricky puzzle at Grand Prix London to try and figure out where the Standard metagame would move next.  Some thought it would be Stormbreath Dragon, but ultimately it was back to re-tooled Abzan Aggro.  The main changes this time—the addition of Hangarback Walker to team up with Anafenza, the Foremost.


Both eventual winner, Fabrizio Anteri, and beaten finalist, Matteo Moure, utilized this strategy.  Standard powerhouse Siege Rhino hasn't gone away either, and a herd of them left Matteo trampled into the dust in a lop-sided and brutal finals.  England continues to be very good for Anteri.  This is his second Grand Prix title on English soil and third Grand Prix title overall.  The tournament also saw Czech pro Martin Jůza net his 23rd Grand Prix Top 8.


Overall, 2,152 players traveled to London for the Grand Prix and 238 survived the cut to day two.  In the end the only player left standing was once again Fabrizio Anteri.  That's all from Grand Prix London.  See you next time!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Welcome back to Grand Prix London 2015! On the first part of our journey, the original field of 2,152 players had been whittled down to a mere 238. Now it was time for another six rounds of Standard, to eliminate another 230 and to decide the Top 8 ...


The 238 players still in contention at the beginning of the day included such luminaries as (7) Brad Nelson, Marco Cammilluzzi, and Fabrizio Anteri on perfect records of 9-0 as well as Martin Dang, Javier Dominguez, Martin Jůza, (15) Ondřej Stráský, and Andrea Mengucci on 8-1, with a host of others at 7-2. With so many deserving and, indeed, proven champions of the game in the running the second day of competition was certainly going to be interesting.


Meanwhile, Standard also added some exciting storylines to the Grand Prix. Decks to note so far included Hangarback Abzan, Green-White Megamorph, Red-Green Mega-Dragons, Green Devotion, and Abzan Aggro, some new, all with minor or major updates. And these were just the 9-0 players' archetypes ...


So follow us as we follow some of the game's best and brightest throughout the day and stay tuned for the epic conclusion of Grand Prix London 2015!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



When the sun came up through the eternal British fog in the morning, a whopping 2,152 players had entered the tournament, but through nine grueling rounds of Standard that number had now been reduced to a mere 238.


Of these 238, six players managed to beat all comers and remained undefeated overnight: (7) Brad Nelson, Bartłomie Lewandowski, Sam Orchard, Julien Henry, Marco Cammilluzzi (pictured above), and Fabrizio Anteri (not pictured). They would have a head start over the competition on Sunday, but players like (15) Ondřej Stráský, Maciej Janik, Andrea Mengucci, Marcio Carvalho, Martin Dang, Javier Dominguez, Patrick Dickmann, Martin Jůza, Martin Müller, and Eduardo Sajgalik, who all made the cut as well, would certainly give them a run for their money and pro points.


So far Standard continued to impress with its large variety of viable decks and ever-changing nature. Hangarback Walker found a new home in Abzan Aggro, charging into battle alongside Anafenza, the Foremost. Meanwhile, Stormbreath Dragon gained in popularity, as did Tragic Arrogance and Ajani, Mentor of Heroes.


Tune back in tomorrow for more Standard, lots of decklists, and the exciting conclusion of Grand Prix London 2015.
