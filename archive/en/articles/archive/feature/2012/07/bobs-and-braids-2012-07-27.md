Bobs and Braids
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 27, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











It is generally the case that most Standard formats have some reigning king that holds control of an era. Right now, we are in the [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) era in Standard. You can follow the string back in time, though, through Caw-Blade to Faeries to Affinity. Each of these decks defined Standard for a significant portion of time. If you have ever been the victim of a [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning), though, you can quickly name a deck missing from that list.

![185053](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=185053)Jund was the big bad wolf for over a year as it evolved and adapted, all the while riding the back of [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) to victory. Eventually, Jund became a well-formed deck and we all knew what to expect, game after game. [Putrid Leech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech), [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning), [Bituminous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bituminous+Blast), [Sprouting Thrinax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax)... the deck did what it did, and did it well. Well, Orin Beasley decided to resurrect an old friend for last weekend's Modern [Grand Prix in Columbus](/en/events/coverage/rookie-maynard-ravages-modern-columbus), only with a bit of a facelift.

The list gets powered up with a deeper card pool, including additions like [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) (popularly known as Bob after invitational winner Bob Maher, who designed it), [Grim Lavamancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), and [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil). Orin even went deeper into the tank, playing Terry Soh's invitational design, [Rakdos Augermage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Augermage). Some of the same old Jund tricks stuck around, like [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate), but really, the show belongs to [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), still one of the most powerful and swingy effects in the format! Check out other lists from Grand Prix Columbus [here](/en/events/coverage/rookie-maynard-ravages-modern-columbus)!
