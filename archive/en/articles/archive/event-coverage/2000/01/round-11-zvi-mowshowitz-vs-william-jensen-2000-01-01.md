Round 11: Zvi Mowshowitz vs. William Jensen
===========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**







![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Headshot%209-2014_3.jpg)
By Randy Buehler











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptsd02/a629.jpg)*Zvi Mowshowitz*Zvi had 21 points, Jensen had 19. They both need to win out to make Top 8 and Huey would also need some serious help from his tiebreakers.


Game 1 was pretty straightforward: Zvi didn't draw any white mana until he was way too far behind. Huey's blue-white deck isn't exactly fast, but it didn't need to be to win this one. All he had to do was play [Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) and Zvi conceded as a response. Huey said "All right ... Peek for the win." Zvi then corrected him to "[Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) for the slightly faster win."


Huey got out a [Psionic Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Gift)ed creature and a [Puppeteer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer) in game 2 of the blue-white mirror match. He saw (via [Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek)) that Zvi had [Kirtar's Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kirtar%27s+Wrath) in his hand so he was careful about committing anything else to the board. Zvi wanted to wait on the Wrath even though Huey knew about it so [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst)ed the Gifted creature and tried to control the board with![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptsd02/a623.jpg)*William Jensen*[Nomad Decoy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Decoy). Huey also had [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) so that set Zvi back. Each player retrieved his 
Aether Burst with Scrivener and Zvi Wrathed for a small advantage the next time Huey was tapped out. Huey promptly played Amugaba and then Persuaded Zvi's [Nomad Decoy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Decoy). Zvi did have a Cloudchaser for the [Persuasion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persuasion), but he soon found himself extended his hand to congratulate his opponent. "I just never got the cards I needed to fight this kind of matchup," explained Zvi. "If I ever use my [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst) again I just lose, so I scooped." Turns out Huey had 2 [Aether Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Burst)s of his own and the matchup really was hideously lopsided.


**Final Result:** Huey 2 - Zvi 0
