Reanalysis
==========



 Posted in **Limited Information**
 on May 2, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_SteveSadin.jpg)
By Steve Sadin












The *Avacyn Restored* Prerelease was this past weekend, and I learned that a lot of the assumptions I made during my first few passes over the [*Avacyn Restored* Card Image Gallery](/en/node/642891) were completely off base.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/li/li193_kru.jpg)[Kruin Striker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker) | Art by [Christopher Moeller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Christopher+Moeller%22%5D)


My biggest miscalculation (that I've discovered so far) came when I was guessing the speed of the format. I expected the *Avacyn Restored* Sealed format to be relatively slow and bomb dependent—but over the course of the Prerelease (and in the days since) I discovered that decks tend to be a lot faster than I had anticipated they would be.


Green, red, and white all have quite a few good two-drops; blue is loaded with evasion creatures and cards that allow you to capitalize on an early advantage (like [Into the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Void)). But more importantly, there aren't that many spells that cost three to five that really punish players for filling up their decks with cheap, aggressive creatures.


So if you fall behind early in *Avacyn Restored* Sealed, it's very likely you will be dead before you are able to take over the game with your relatively powerful but expensive spells.



![Kruin Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kruin+Striker)

*Make sure you have a plan for the early game... or else!*


If you're playing in an *Avacyn Restored* release event this weekend, don't expect to be able to kick back and wait until your bombs can take over the game for you. There are going to be a lot of decks out there capable of killing you quickly, so make sure you can hold off that initial onslaught—or kill them first!


### My Sealed!


I'll have more to share about the speed of the format once I've gotten a few dozen more games under my belt, but until then, let's take a look at the Sealed Pool I opened this past weekend.

When I began going through my pool, my blue—highlighted by three copies of [Scrapskin Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake), an [Into the Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Void), a [Peel from Reality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peel+from+Reality), and an [Amass the Components](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amass+the+Components)—immediately stuck out at me. But I still needed to find a second color to go with it.



![Into the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Into+the+Void)

 

I really wanted to play with my two copies of [Lightning Prowess](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Prowess), but I just didn't have enough good cards to build a blue-red deck.


While [Barter in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barter+in+Blood) plus three copies of [Undead Executioner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Executioner) were quite appealing, the rest of my black was pretty underwhelming—so I was able to set it aside as well.

That left me to choose between my white and my green.


Both of these colors had a lot of high-quality cards, but if I played the white I would have had a huge hole in my early game. Because while I knew my three [Scrapskin Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake)s were going to be very strong offensively, if I were playing against an aggressive deck (that didn't have many cheap fliers), they would end up being irrelevant on defense.


And even when I was working under the assumption that this was going to be a fairly slow Sealed format, I wasn't willing to play a deck that would often be incapable of playing a blocker before turn four.

While my deck was definitely powerful, it was a lot less interactive than I would have liked it to be. If I developed a lead, it was generally easy for me to seal the deal. But in the games where I fell behind early, I just had no way to catch up.


### The Scrapskin Drake dilemma


It's important to distinguish between cards that are abstractly good, and cards that are good in specific situations.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/li/li193_scr.jpg)[Scrapskin Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake) | Art by [Kev Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Kev+Walker%22%5D)


[Scrapskin Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake) is a resilient, efficient, offensive creature. But if you're on the draw against an aggressive red deck that just opened on a [Kruin Striker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kruin+Striker), you're going to wish you had a creature that could actually block.



![Scrapskin Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Scrapskin+Drake)

 

My three [Scrapskin Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake)s made up a huge part of my early game and, consequently, I lost a couple of games where my opponents got off to aggressive (but fragile) starts. If I had the ability to sideboard out some of my [Scrapskin Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake)s on the draw, I definitely would have done that in at least two of my matches—but without any other relevant cheap creatures in my pool, I had to shuffle them up every game.


So if you have any [Scrapskin Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapskin+Drake)s in your next deck and you're worried about getting overran early, don't be afraid to sideboard them out for a blocker.


### [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist)?


I'm still not sure how I feel about [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist). There were games where I drew [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) and it was absolutely amazing. But every time the board got cluttered, I wished that it were any other card in my deck.



![Fettergeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fettergeist)

 

If you're off to an aggressive start, you will generally want to wait until you've played out the bulk of your spells before casting [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) (lest they rot away in your hand as you pay a bunch of mana to keep [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) alive every turn). If you've fallen behind early, then [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) can do a lot to give you an immediate presence—but the upkeep cost can make it difficult for you to continue your recovery (particularly if your opponent is content to let the board get a bit cluttered).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/li/li193_fet.jpg)[Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) | Art by [Izzy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Izzy%22%5D)


But any time you and your opponent have been slogging away at each other in a long-winded battle of attrition, [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) is going to be a steal of a deal.


At least for the time being, I'm going to put [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) into all of my sealed decks, and I'm going to look for any opportunity that I can to draft it.


While [Fettergeist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fettergeist) might have some very real limitations in Sealed, if I can draft a creature-light deck around it (or a hyper-aggressive deck), then it will consistently be an all-star.


### Outperforming expectations


As I was building my deck, I was pretty confident [Druid's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid%27s+Familiar) was going to be one of my best cards. But it managed to outshine even my lofty expectations, as I don't think I even came close to losing a game where I cast it on turn four.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/li/li193_dru.jpg)[Druid's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid%27s+Familiar) | Art by [Adam Paquette](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Adam+Paquette%22%5D)


It simply isn't every day that you get to spend four mana to add 6 power to your side of the board.



![Druid's Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Druid%27s+Familiar)

 

I think all of the soulbond creatures in the set are going to end up being noticeably better than I expected them to be. The fact there are relatively few removal or bounce spells in this set significantly lowers the odds you will get blown out by your opponent killing one of your soulbond creatures during the middle of combat.


Heck, even [Flowering Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flowering+Lumberknot), which I had thought was unplayable last week, could turn out to be a key card in soulbond decks everywhere over the next few months.



![Flowering Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flowering+Lumberknot)

 

Even though there isn't that much removal in *Avacyn Restored*, you still shouldn't expect every creature you play to live forever. Everyone you play against should have the ability to deal with a *finite* number of your creatures.


So while you might not need to worry about your opponents slicing through your army like butter, you still shouldn't expect any single one of your creatures to stick around indefinitely.
