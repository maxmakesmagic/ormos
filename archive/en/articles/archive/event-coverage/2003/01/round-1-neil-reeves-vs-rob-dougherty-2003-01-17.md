Round 1: Neil Reeves vs. Rob Dougherty
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 17, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TobyWachter.jpg)
By Toby Wachter











Two of the best players in America, let alone the world faced off in the first round of the Masters: Neil Reeves and Rob Dougherty. Reeves is from Arkansas, and is easy to spot at an event with his backwards hat, toothpick in his mouth, and unique Southern sarcastic humor. Dougherty is the founder of Your Move Games, and is looking to help his team continue their dominance in Constructed events. Both players came to Chicago with [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) decks, which can create quite a mentally draining mirror match for both competitors due to the untimed rounds.

### Game 1

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a290.jpg)Reeves mulliganed his opening hand, noting, "It's a good start to the Masters! Mulliganing is not as much fun as you'd think. I mean I enjoy shuffling, but not in this context." Both players played islands and black sources of mana for the first few turns, signaling that they were both playing [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). Neither player wanted to make the first move, and play was slow until Reeves tapped out for [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), which was [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)ed by Dougherty. Dougherty untapped, and played his own [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis). Reeves flashed his back to keep up in the card drawing race, and played [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion). Reeves used it to cycle through his spot removal cards, and countered Dougherty's [Concentrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Concentrate). Reeves rode his [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) to card quality advantage, and also used it to power his [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic)s. This continued for a long time, with Reeves using [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) to get to relevant cards, and both players going back and forth with [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) to draw cards. 

Eventually, Reeves used [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) to fetch [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short), but was still waiting for the right moment to go for the kill. Dougherty had his own [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short) though, and used it on end step. A huge counterwar resulted, and the [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short) did not resolve. Reeves played his on Dougherty's upkeep, and resolved. This cleared the way for [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), followed by [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). 

Reeves- 1 Dougherty- 0

### Game 2

Dougherty started with a first turn [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), seeing Reeves' hand of three lands, [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood), [Force Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+Spike), [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) and [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) headed to the graveyard, and Dougherty tried to play [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) two turns later to get around [Force Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+Spike), but Reeves had drawn a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) got [Read the Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Runes) for Reeves, and it was cast, improving card quality as he discarded spot removal spells. With Reeves tapped out, Dougherty was able to resolve [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion). [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) stripped Dougherty of a [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), and Reeves drew some cards with [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis). With [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion) in play it would seem that Dougherty would have the advantage, but he was stuck at three lands, which allowed Reeves to resolve his own [Compulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsion). Even [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) couldn't draw Dougherty into more lands, and he was quickly falling behind.

[Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) stripped [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) out of Dougherty's hand, and with eight lands in play to Dougherty's four, Reeves had a tremendous advantage in this permission mirror match. Reeves played [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish) to get [Opportunity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opportunity), and played [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), taking away [Mana Short](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Short). A main phase [Opportunity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opportunity) followed, and the phrase "from bad to worse" was never more appropriate. A last ditch effort [Persecute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) was attempted by Dougherty, but Reeves was prepared with [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell). Reeves easily resolved a [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), and shrugged off [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) with [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). Dougherty's own [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) was countered as well. This left Dougherty tapped out, and Reeves attacked for the win.

**Final Result:** Reeves- 2 Dougherty- 0
