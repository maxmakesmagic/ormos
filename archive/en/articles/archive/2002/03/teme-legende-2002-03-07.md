*Teme, Legende*
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 7, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar












In a week dedicated to nostalgia, I turn to theme decks.


Admittedly, I hadn't started playing **Magic** when *Legends* first hit the shelves. When, exactly, I *did* start playing is a bit of a mystery even to me. I think that I bought my first pack during *Ice Age*, entering constructed tournaments much later. In those early years, my wife, friends and I created constructed decks only during our vaunted “theme parties.”


The rules for theme parties Ye Olde Moldenhauer-Salazar Household are fairly straightforward. I first mentioned them during [Nightmare Week](http://archive.wizards.com/default.asp?x=mtgcom/daily/jm2), but here's a refresher:


1. Normal constructed guidelines apply (maximum 4 of each card, 60 card minimum, etc.).
2. You can use any card printed for **Magic**, including *Unglued*, *Starter*, and *Portal* cards, although certain cards can be banned or restricted for a particular theme.
3. The deck must be playable (it needs to have at least an outside chance of winning games).
4. Every card except [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Plains), [Scrubland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Scrubland), and [Caves of Koilos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Caves%2Bof%2BKoilos) must fit the theme.

As I have previously mentioned, theme decks are more about aesthetics than competition. Or, rather, they are about a competition for aesthetics. Think of them as deck beauty pageants.


Because it's *Legends* Week, I felt the need to throw a *Legends* (the set, not the creature type) theme party at our house. My hope was to bedazzle you with the weird and wonderful decks to make an appearance during the festivities -- all decks built around at least one card from the *Legends* set.


Unfortunately, my three month old baby caught his first flu. Then he generously gave it to my wife. Ye Olde M-S Household became Ye Olde M-S Convalescent Home for nearly two weeks, killing all opportunity for parties of any kind. Thus “*Legends* Theme Night” is on indefinite hold.


Pre-influenza, I did manage to pore over the *Legends* spoiler and make a few decks. Here are ten feature cards I considered, along with the top three decks I might have played. When looking over the list, keep in mind that we banned the most broken cards from Brokenville (e.g. [The Abyss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Abyss), [Moat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moat), and [Mana Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Drain)), and I personally avoided the challenge of the most loser cards from Losertown (e.g. [Great Wall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Wall) and [Seafarer's Quay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seafarer%27s+Quay)).


### #10: EVIL EYE OF ORMS-BY-GORE



![Evil Eye of Orms-By-Gore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Evil+Eye+of+Orms-By-Gore)

Since the Eye appeared in *6th Edition*, copies are easy to find. The black/blue deck is chalk full of cards like [Eye of Ramos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ramos), [Wandering Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Eye), [Seer's Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seer%27s+Vision), [Foresight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foresight), and [Sorcerous Sight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorcerous+Sight), with [Cuombajj Witches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cuombajj+Witches) thrown in for that “evil eye” feel. I have no idea what “Orms-by-Gore” means well enough to incorporate it into the theme.


### #9: THUNDER SPIRIT



![Thunder Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thunder+Spirit)

It is almost too easy to make a Storm Deck in **Magic**. Focusing on thunder and lightning focuses the choices a bit, including cards like [Thundermare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermare), [Lightning Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Elemental), and [Thunderbolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderbolt). The trick, really, is to fit enough white into the deck to justify [Thunder Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunder+Spirit), which is where more recent cards like [Orim's Thunder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Thunder) and [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel) fit in.


### #8: KILLER BEES



![Killer Bees](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Killer+Bees)

I'm still a little bitter that my Bees Deck didn't make it into the Auction of the People in last year's [**Magic** Invitational](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/2001-magic-invitational-2015-09-02). I probably played it more than any of the creature theme decks I've made. Besides being able to use both [Bee Sting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bee+Sting) and [Unyaro Bee Sting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unyaro+Bee+Sting), cards like [The Hive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Hive) and [Drop of Honey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drop+of+Honey) make it -- I think -- superbly fun.


### #7: ALL HALLOW'S EVE



![All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve)

I already used [All Hallow's Eve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Hallow%27s+Eve) in a black/green Halloween theme deck during an interesting night in which everyone built decks around a different holiday, and the deck translates perfectly here. Think [Cackling Witch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Witch) and [Penumbra Bobcat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Penumbra+Bobcat) (the only way I found to get a black cat into the deck).


### #6: MASTER OF THE HUNT



![Master of the Hunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Master+of+the+Hunt)

[Howling Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Wolf), [Lure of Prey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lure+of+Prey), [Hidden Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hidden+Stag), [Pit Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pit+Trap), [Serrated Arrows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows), and [Elven Warhounds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elven+Warhounds) make a terrific monogreen deck focused on the Master. This deck also revealed to me what a dearth of “rabbit” cards exist in **Magic**. (subtle “You Make The Card” hint inserted here).


### #5: AISLING LEPRECHAUN



![Aisling Leprechaun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Aisling+Leprechaun)

What an odd little fellow... not much of a combat champion, but he sure can turn creatures green! I'm not exactly sure why, but the idea of a Leprechaun deck makes me extremely happy. Something with [Rainbow Crow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rainbow+Crow), [Rainbow Efreet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rainbow+Efreet), [Golden Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golden+Bear), and [Venarian Gold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venarian+Gold), along with a clever way to play with color-changing creatures.


### #4: STANGG



![Stangg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stangg)

A [Stangg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stangg) theme deck? Think instead of a Twins Deck, complete with [Llanowar Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Sentinel), [Two-Headed Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Two-Headed+Dragon), [Bifurcate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bifurcate), and [Double Deal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Double+Deal), to name a few. I wish I knew the backstory to several of the Legends from *Legends*, and [Stangg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stangg) is near the top in terms of piquing my curiosity.


And now, on to the decks of which I am most enamored...


### #3: KOBOLDS



![Crookshank Kobolds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Crookshank+Kobolds)

I know "kobolds" is not a single card. I know, alright? I get it. [Crimson Kobolds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Kobolds), [Crookshank Kobolds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crookshank+Kobolds), and [Kobolds of Kher Keep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kobolds+of+Kher+Keep), however, are essentially identical except in name so it seems silly to play favorites. With all three, I can have TWELVE 0/1 red critters for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20210707095534im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless0.gif) in the same deck. Yeehaw!


My only prior experience with kobolds came on a theme night several years ago. John Henry, the guy who taught me how to play Magic, showed up one night with a deck full of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20210707095534im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless0.gif)-cost creatures and bunches of creature-enhancers like [Giant Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Strength) and [Unholy Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unholy+Strength). He called it his "Steroids" deck, which to this day cracks me up.


*Legends* was the first set to introduce a self-contained creature theme in it. As a result, a kobolds theme deck practically builds itself. What's left is to find a way to deal with those situations when I have three kobolds, a [Kobold Drill Sergeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kobold+Drill+Sergeant), and a [Kobold Overlord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kobold+Overlord) on the table with no [Kobold Taskmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kobold+Taskmaster) in sight. These situations, not surprisingly, come up frequently.


I think cards like [Mob Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mob+Justice) and [Reckless Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Charge) are probably in flavor with a kobolds theme deck. Here is a stab at it:

### #2: SOL'KANAR THE SWAMP KING



![Sol'kanar the Swamp King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sol%27Kanar+the+Swamp+King)

He is the Swamp King, he can do *ANYTHING*. From the silly to the stalwart, [Sol'kanar the Swamp King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol%27kanar+the+Swamp+King) is one serious dude. Blue/black/red clearly received the most efficient Legends in the set with Sol'kanar, [Gwendlyn di Corci](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gwendlyn+di+Corci), and [Tetsuo Umezawa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tetsuo+Umezawa), not to mention the most famous elder dragon in [Nicol Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nicol+Bolas). But it is both the art and title that draws me to Sol'Kanar.


If you include near-synonyms for “swamp” like bog, marsh, mire,and moor, there are far too many choices to figure out an appropriate theme deck. Since Sol'kanar is the Swamp King, I decided he probably wouldn't want to sacrifice Swamps à la [Marsh Lurker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Lurker). In fact, I decided to only use cards that really celebrate the presence of Swamps in the world. The result is a fun -- if not mucky and oozy -- deck:

### #1: EUREKA



![Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Eureka)

I love [Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eureka). I have always appreciated the art of the Foglios, which is one point in the card's favor. My great desire is that [Hypnox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnox) worked with [Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eureka) in an obscene way, but alas -- “played it from your hand” really means “played it fair and square by tapping your lands.” Oh well.


Still, the opportunity to actually create a theme deck around [Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eureka) makes it probably my leading choice for the whenever-it-happens *Legends* theme party. But what kind of theme?


After looking into an “exclamatory” theme deck to no avail, I decided an exploration/research theme deck would work as long as only a single copy of [Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eureka) existed in the deck (Plus, who owns more than one?!). The goal, I decided, would be to find the lone [Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eureka) and yell “EUREKA!!!!!” as loudly as possible when playing it. For added effect, I also decided to make the deck huge so discovering the [Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eureka) would feel like a real accomplishment.


Although [Hazezon Tamar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hazezon+Tamar); [Molimo, Maro-Sorcerer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molimo%2C+Maro-Sorcerer); and [Cognivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore) are not truly in theme, I couldn't find a better way to use [Eureka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eureka). [Cognivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore) makes some vague sense, and Hazezon is at least in *Legends*. Molimo, on the other hand, would get booed out of the theme party. If you have a better idea as a finisher that fits the theme, let me know.
