Melek, Commander Paragon
========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 30, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







It's no secret that Izzet is among the most popular of guilds to align with. While I'll never fathom why so many choose something other than the swift, fiery justice of the Boros, my duty is to adhere to the vision so many of you want to realize: a Commander deck led by the fashionably odd [Melek, Izzet Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melek%2C+Izzet+Paragon).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ct/ct246_melek.jpg)Based on the feedback of the cards you were most excited to see in *Dragon's Maze*, following up on Izzet didn't take genius intellect to determine. The theme of the deck, as gravitated to by most of you, was packing in plenty of instant and sorcery spells to key off of Melek. Unsurprisingly, one of the most suggested cards was [Possibility Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possibility+Storm), another card regarded well by you look at the whole set.


But crafting an entire Commander deck takes more than just a primordial idea and particular legendary creature. (And, speaking of legendary, [last week's rules update announcement](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/feature/248e) that bumped us out a week is important to keep in mind for today's article since we'll be including ways to copy permanents, and the handling multiples of legendary permanents in play will be changing soon!) It takes plenty of cards and play to make the most of the ninety-nine-high stack we take with us.


This is what you brewed up six cards at a time.


### Converging Divergent Forces


What makes [Melek, Izzet Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melek%2C+Izzet+Paragon) tick? It's all about his text box:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ct/ct249_1_6ges1qncob.jpg)Getting the most excitement out of Melek asks us to do a few things:


* Include enough instant and sorcery spells that they appear regularly on top of the library.
* Include instant and sorcery spells that we'd want to play and copy in a variety of battlefield situations.
* Ensure there's enough mana to cast our biggest spells quickly.

We'll get to all of these, thanks to your submissions. The first set is from Eamon, who cuts right to the first point:



> 
> * [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions)—Cantrips, then sets up your top card.
> * [Increasing Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Vengeance)—Doubled doubles with recursion.
> * [Epic Experiment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Epic+Experiment)—Ends games in a sudden burst of fire and lightning.
> * [Hypersonic Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypersonic+Dragon)—Gives you a response to anything.
> * [Spelltwine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spelltwine)—One of the most versatile cards ever when copied.
> * [Invoke the Firemind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invoke+the+Firemind)—Copied, you can get double the draw or double the damage.
> 


Eamon's pick of [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) is just one of several cards that can manipulate the top of the library, where the spells Melek cares about have to sit. [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) and [Mystic Speculation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Speculation) do a fine job of this too. Setting up an instant or sorcery on top is something Melek appreciates.

[Leyline of Anticipation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Anticipation), and its artifact ancestor [Vedalken Orrery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Orrery), lets you cast sorceries (and just about everything else) anytime. Given there are so many powerful sorceries that can really mess with opponents if they were instant, such as [Blatant Thievery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blatant+Thievery) and [Reality Strobe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Strobe), turning things on faster just makes sense. Kevin's other choices both round out ways to put an instant or sorcery on top of our library ([Scroll Rack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Rack)) or function as another way to double up on awesome things we cast ([Djinn Illuminatus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Djinn+Illuminatus)).

Scott's choices lead down different paths:



>

While we haven't gotten to many ways to make extra mana, [Braid of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) is one of those weird ways that requires a little thought. You have to use it during the upkeep, but if you have mana sinks and sorceries you can cast when you could cast an instant it works. We've also been light on spells you'd want to copy through Melek, and the two Scott chose here deliver. [Comet Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm) can slice and dice a battlefield, targeting creatures and players as you see fit, but [Rite of Replication](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication) could potentially make ten copies of the same creature if cast kicked from the top of the library.

[Index](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Index) is an innocuous spell that turns Melek on for potentially several turns in a row, but it's [Call the Skybreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+the+Skybreaker) and [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) that steal the show here. Digging through for a better four cards is nice, and so is throwing a little damage around. Creating creatures through spells isn't as easy for blue and red, but [Call the Skybreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+the+Skybreaker) is an ideal option here. Getting two pseudo-Dragons if it's off the top and transforming any extra lands we draw into more is a sweet deal.

[Possibility Storm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possibility+Storm) is an interesting addition to the mix here. While it certainly affects everything players cast from their hands, Melek changes the equation for us. Possibly Storm doesn't care if we cast spells from the top of our library, but it will still work if we try from our hand. This means we get the best of both words: using spells in hand, we can get the spell on top of our library instead, ideally at a discount, or simply cast it from the top if it's an instant or sorcery, where we still have the option to cast a cheaper spell in hand for the one on top. It's mindboggling to even think about!


Janne went looking for ways to make all this instant and sorcery action easier:



> 
> * [Mana Matrix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Matrix)—Awesome [Mark Tedin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Mark+Tedin%22%5D) art on a forgotten cost reducer.
> * [Arcane Melee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Melee)—Sure, it helps the entire table, but with sufficient planning, it should help Melek the most.
> * [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer)—More cost reducing!
> * [Dual Casting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dual+Casting)—More copies = more fun.
> * [Echo Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echo+Mage)—Even more copies = even more fun!
> * [Reiterate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reiterate)—Awesome card in Commander games.
> 


Discounting all these big sorceries can help in ways an extra mana source can't. Discounting affects all spells, so if you cast multiple in a turn you get the benefit of the discount multiple times. Janne's logical conclusion is that if you're discounting all these spells, now you'll want to copy them too.

### Things Got a Little Weird


Doesn't this deck look... strange? In hindsight, throwing together just the top-of-mind suggestions from all of you has led us to fulfill our plan of having spells, playing them as we'd please, and making enough mana to do it, but our deck is lacking coordination and cohesion.


There's plenty of obvious things you might want to change here, but that's exactly the point I wanted to make: the best of intentions do not naturally lead to the best execution. That's where you come in. I'd like to get the community scope on making changes to decks. One of the most frequent questions I see come through, that isn't attached to the feedback of the week, is how to "improve" a deck.


Many of you love the commander and themes you've chosen, but struggle to connect things in a way that makes the deck "work." Starting with our deck here, one that clearly has some potential but doesn't seem to work as we intended, I want to know what your next steps would be. **What simple changes would you make to this deck to improve it? (Alternatively, what's wrong with this deck and what would you do differently?)**



* Feedback via email
* 150 word limit to answer the question
* Name and email required (non-personal information to be used in column)

Some of you could write an entire article about what to change with this deck. I'm asking you to choose one paragraph that belongs in it, then share that thought. It isn't the specific cards you'd change, but the rationale behind changing them. Feel free to use examples, but make sure it's applicable to this Melek deck. The goal here is to encapsulate the approaches we all take to refining a deck from its raw, primordial state to something much more dangerous for opponents.


Join us next week when we look at what lies beneath. See you then!
