Magic Words, Part 1
===================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on September 29, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Welcome to the long-delayed, completely back-burnered, and ideally anticipated results for the Pick-a-Word Build-Around Contest! The raw details: I held a contest a little over month ago for a deck based flavorfully around a word, any word. The results: below!

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl101_water2.jpg)  
Out of 107 emails, I eliminated 66 on the first pass, 11 on the second, and finally a heart-wrenching 12 more to wind up with 18 decks, to be spread out between a couple articles. Thanks to all who submitted a deck; I absolutely enjoyed absorbing them, even if you didn't make the articles.

Let's get to it!

### Birds

Straightforward seems like a good starting approach for these results. AJ Grasso kicks things off with a Birds deck:


>  Birds, birds, and more birds! Like a scene from an Alfred Hitchcock movie, your opponent will be running in terror from the angry flock of birds you have cast upon them. The game plan is simple: play as many cheap birds as possible and swarm your opponent with your feathery friends. Use cards like [Soulcatchers' Aerie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcatchers%27+Aerie) & [Soraya the Falconer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soraya+the+Falconer) early to pump your birds to monstrous sizes. [Roc Egg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roc+Egg), [Seaside Haven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seaside+Haven), and [Impaler Shrike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impaler+Shrike) all combo nicely with [Soulcatchers' Aerie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulcatchers%27+Aerie), allowing you to gain card advantage while making your birds even bigger. [Stormscape Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormscape+Familiar) makes your white birds cheaper. Combo this with [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) and you'll be dropping 2-4 birds a turn. [Sage Owl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Owl) smooths your draws out while [Impaler Shrike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impaler+Shrike) draws you more birds. [Dovescape](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape) functions as protection from board sweepers but can be very risky because your Aerie will grow your opponents' birds. Finally, [Battle Screech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Screech) keeps the birds coming even when you ran out of birds to play.
> 
>

### Blitz

Next up is Matt Higgs with his mono-red Blitz deck:


>  In the blink of an eye, lightning can destroy nearly anything on earth. Its name has become synonymous with puissance, speed, and spontaneity. It instills awe, fear, and humility in the hardest of hearts; nothing so transient has ever had such an impact. This deck, BLITZ, comes from the German word for lightning. Within this deck, you will feel the clarifying rush of electricity course through the cards in your hand. Every creature has haste, and each is more fearsome than the last. Transforming pure red mana into blazing lightning is enormously effective. Lightning is effervescent, striking a player without warning and without defense. Many forms of lightning in the deck can ignore or overrun any meager defenses a slow-hearted mage might present.
> 
>

### Death

Alex Lamoureux built a fun and flavorful Death deck:


>  Early in the game, black magic summons [Deathspore Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathspore+Thallid)s and [Dregscape Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregscape+Zombie)s to provide sacrifices upon the altar. [Apprentice Necromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apprentice+Necromancer) can briefly unearth a single creature, perhaps brought to the graveyard by [Entomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb). [Hymn to Tourach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach), played on a flute made from the victim's bones, drives madness into the mind of the enemy planeswalker.
> 
>

### Peace

Devin Green chose the word "peace," and took it to strategic heart when deckbuilding:


> 
> [Atalya, Samite Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atalya%2C+Samite+Master) and I have set up a lovely little community here by the coast where our members can live in peace and harmony safely nestled within the towers and spires of this coastal sanctum. Here we strive towards [Purity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purity), towards peace of all sorts. Whether it be through the watchful gaze of the [Platinum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel)[Siblings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Emperion) and their protective herd of Pegasi or the occasional [Absorption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Absorb) of a stray [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), nonviolence is the key to solving our problems here.
> 
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Atalya, Samite Master | Celestial Force |


> 
> Most of the choices here are obvious; [Peace Talks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peace+Talks), [Palliation Accord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palliation+Accord), [Plumes of Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plumes+of+Peace), and [Peacekeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peacekeeper) were must-includes. Other choices, like [Celestial Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Force), [Elixir of Immortality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality), and [Pristine Talisman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman) are here to give us the resolve to endure violence without being forced to lash out to protect ourselves. The Elixir also helps us recur our counters and revive our fallen brethren. If things get hairy, though, I'm not afraid to step in and bear the cost of nonviolent resistance myself with my [Personal Incarnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Personal+Incarnation).
> 
> 
> Naturally, there are many ways to win with this deck, mostly in the delay-then-crush-them mode of game play.
> 
> 
> The real goal, however, is to get your [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) and [Abyssal Persecutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor) (he enrolled here in hopes of dealing with his violent tendencies. He's actually a pretty chill dude once you get to know him) to prove that violence really isn't the answer. At that point the game is unwinnable for everyone, and hopefully you've made yourself untouchable, so the only way to conclude is by giving your opponent a friendly hand shake and maybe getting an ice cream sundae to share.
>

### Clog

Joe H.'s deck clogs up your opponent's development, aiming for an ultimate decking:


>  The dictionary defines clog as something that hinders or to block off, and that is exactly what this deck does. This deck slowly clogs up the battlefield along with your opponent's hand, leaving your opponent helpless as they watch their library slowly creep away. This goal is achieved with multiple cards that stop your opponent from attacking you, and with multiple cards that limit your opponent's other actions.
> 
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Limited Resources | Chronomantic Escape |


> 
> The basic strategy is to just completely block off your opponent from being able to do anything. Start by using all your resources to stop your opponent from attacking you. Then when you have good board control, cast [Limited Resources](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Limited+Resources) to seal the deal. There are multiple cards to make your opponent have to pay to attack you; stack a few of these, and their five lands after [Limited Resources](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Limited+Resources) won't be enough to pay. Also, three spaced out [Chronomantic Escape](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomantic+Escape)s can also do this job nicely. Add this to [Leyline of Sanctity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) protecting you and [Cursed Totem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Totem) stopping creature's activated abilities, and your opponent has little to no options. Then to make sure that your opponent decks out before you do, force your opponent to draw more cards with [Careful Consideration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration). This deck will lead to slow games, the nature of a clog. 
> 
> 
>

### Götterdämmerung?

Leave it to Darth Parallax, a notorious inbox fiend, to defy the 3-5 letter rule:


>  GOTTERDAMMERUNG. It's a German word which means Twilight of the Gods. Armageddon. Judgement Day. The Apocalypse. Which is exactly what this deck does. It makes the End of the World happen. Most of the synonyms for this word have been turned into **Magic** cards, interestingly enough, and it was fun discovering just how many of them I could fit into this deck.
> 
> 
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Day of Judgment | Armageddon |


> 
> Everything in this deck either Destroyedicates everything on the field, or ... well, pretty much all the cards do that. With all the mass death and destruction going around, I figured I'd want [Undertaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undertaker) and [Twilight Shepherd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twilight+Shepherd) to run a recursion subtheme, [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) to be an indestructible survivor who laughs at [Near-Death Experience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Near-Death+Experience)s, and [Gaea's Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Herald) to make sure everyone else gets through. If you want the one-card answer to the question "What does this deck do?", ask [Avatar of Slaughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Slaughter). 
>
