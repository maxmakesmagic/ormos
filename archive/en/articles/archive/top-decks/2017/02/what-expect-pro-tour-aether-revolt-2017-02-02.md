What to Expect at Pro Tour Aether Revolt
========================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on February 2, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 






It's time for our quarterly check-in before the Pro Tour where I try to set the stage for what we might see during the tournament. Standard got extra shaken up this time, as in addition to the very impactful release of *Aether Revolt*, three of the biggest cards in Standard have exited stage left ([Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End); [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage); and [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter) were all banned in the [last announcement](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/news/january-9-2017-banned-and-restricted-announcement-2017-01-09)). That leaves the field wide open, and we are going to see a combination of new decks and the resurgence of some old favorites, plus twists on what was present shortly before the rotation. All of that adds up to a varied field, and we've got a bunch of different decks to look at today.

[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nissa%2C+Voice+of+Zendikar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Voice+of+Zendikar) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar)
The first deck I want to look at is a previously dominant archetype that has lain dormant for months: Green-White Tokens. The printing of [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) and [Rishkar, Peema Renegade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishkar%2C+Peema+Renegade) (as well as the departure of [Smuggler's Copter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smuggler%27s+Copter)) means that Gideon and Nissa can join forces once again.

This deck features many of the groupings of cards I'd expect to see in any Green-White Tokens list, though more copies of [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn) wouldn't surprise me.


**Game Plan**: Curve early creatures or [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran) into Nissa and Gideon, establishing a board presence of threats that are resilient to removal. Interact via [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) and [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare).


**Notable Interactions**:


* [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista)/[Lambholt Pacifist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lambholt+Pacifist)/any counter generation. This gives the deck free shots off Ballista and access to a two-mana 4/4 in Pacifist, both of which are quite powerful. Rishkar and Nissa really drive these combos, though [Verdurous Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdurous+Gearhulk) helps at the top of the curve.
* [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran)/[Nissa, Voice of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Voice+of+Zendikar)/[Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar). While this is an obvious combo, having this many high-loyalty planeswalkers is a real strength of the deck. Remember that vigilance comes into play in a big way here, and Heart is good on both sides of the court.
* [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn)/[Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista). While not present in this list, Avacyn shines with Ballista, as you can either play it for zero to flip her, or just remove all the counters.

This is nominally an aggressive deck, though it does lean more midrange, as decks in this format tend to do. There is a minor energy theme in this list, though not all lists will lean into that (and it does add a bit more depth to [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor), which is neat).


**Game Plan**: Amass an overwhelming amount of power and toughness thanks to Constrictor and all the counter-granting, while keeping the opponent's board clear with efficient removal. This deck also has a lot of creatures with baseline good stats, making it hard to beat in combat.


**Notable Interactions**:


* [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) and anything. The Constrictor really does everything in this deck, and most of the nonland cards (plus even [Aether Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Hub)!) work with it. It's the card the deck most wants to play on turn two.
* [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista)/[Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push). Ballista enables revolt at will, which is important to keep in mind when sequencing turns.
* [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner) plus other energy cards. Siphoner doesn't just have to be a two-drop beater, as saving it for later can also be profitable. Holding it back so you can generate energy via other means is a play worth keeping in mind.

This version goes pretty deep, with four colors worth of action making an appearance. It reminds me of the old (very old) Five-Color Green decks, where green was the base color and white, blue, black, and red were all splashes for various effects.


**Game Plan**: Assemble Saheeli and [Felidar Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Guardian) for the kill while using creatures with enters-the-battlefield effects to get value on the way.


**Notable Interactions**:


* [Saheeli Rai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saheeli+Rai)/[Felidar Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Guardian). Uh, yeah, this is a good one to know. Copy Guardian, target Saheeli, rinse, and repeat (though Cats generally don't like to get washed, in my experience).
* [Felidar Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Guardian)/ETB creatures. Gotta get that value. Blinking [Cloudblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer) or [Rogue Refiner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue+Refiner) is good times, and this deck has a solid backup plan even without Saheeli.
* [Oath of Nissa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Nissa)/[Saheeli Rai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saheeli+Rai). The underused ability of [Oath of Nissa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Nissa) lets you cast Saheeli off any set of lands, and that can and will come up.
