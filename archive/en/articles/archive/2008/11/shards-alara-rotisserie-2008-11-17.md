*Shards of Alara* Rotisserie
============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 17, 2008 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Gregory Marques












As we did with [*Lorwyn*](/en/articles/archive/one-delicious-draft-2007-11-26-0) and *Shadowmoor*, the Wizards Draft Club did a Rotisserie Draft with a full set of *Shards of Alara*. The set is a little smaller, so we only had six people in the draft instead of eight. It might just be me, but in hindsight I think it would have been better with eight. There would be have been more competition for the good cards, but I like Limited formats in which you are forced to play cards you might otherwise avoid putting in a deck.


I am not the best drafter in R&D. I know this, so I prepared for the draft by making a list. It's several lists really—I put all the cards in the set into categories, and within each category the cards are in order of how much I wanted to draft them.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/tcg/products/shardsofalara/ALA_expsym_200.jpg)Some of the categories are in approximate order, and you should be aware that I am not one of the people they allow to do pointing of card power level. I'll tell you briefly about some of the categories you can see in my lists. In *Shards* Rotisserie, I feel that the best way to win games is by using the most devastating creatures. The removal just isn't strong enough to contain them, and most of them win in one or two turns. This made me want to focus my early picks on them. There are sixteen on my list, meaning that almost everyone could get three of them if they so chose. Five of these game-winners cost eight mana, however and I wasn't sure our decks would be slow enough for them to come out to play. You have probably also noticed the \* after [Mayael the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayael+the+Anima). That's because while I think she could win games in the right deck she requires a lot of support from the rest of the cards you draft. There are a \*s next to [Master of Etherium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium) and [Where Ancients Tread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Where+Ancients+Tread) for the same reason.


On to the next most important category: removal. One of the best reasons to make a list like this before a Rotisserie Draft is to remind yourself of each and every one of the removal cards. I paid special attention to removal cards that could deal with the game-winners (as opposed to a card like [Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray), which is solid removal, but often useless against the bigger creatures). [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder) and [Where Ancients Tread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Where+Ancients+Tread) are two good examples of cards you might forget about when trying to find a removal card late in the draft. Even if you don't check your list every pick, just making the list increases the chance you will think about the less obvious cards.


One final thing I would like to note about my list before we get to the draft itself: the two-drop and three-drop categories. Knowing all the cheap creatures in whatever colors you end up in, and especially taking time to evaluate which ones are the best beforehand, will help you out a lot in the heat of the moment. It can also show you whether there is a solid aggro deck available and what colors you need to be drafting to play that deck.


### Draft Time!


The drafters for this event were Alexis Janson, Dave Guskin, Gregory Marques (that's me), Ken Nagle, Lee Sharpe, and Tom LaPille. While waiting for Ken and Lee to arrive, the rest of us talked a bit about what we might pick first, but being sly drafters we were also trying not to give away our plans to the enemy. We joked about the triple-lands and how there might be a run on them—as soon as one person picks one, the next four people have to grab the others. We decided they would last until about fourth pick. Our stragglers walked in, and we rolled d20s to decide the pick order. Ken was up first.

 I feel it's worth noting that while some others tried to hate-draft anything that might be a threat to them, I continued to look for sideboard cards or alternate cards for my deck. [Incurable Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incurable+Ogre), [Skeletal Kathari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Kathari), [Exuberant Firestoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exuberant+Firestoker), and [Goblin Mountaineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Mountaineer) are good examples of this. In fact, I immediately pulled out [Algae Gharial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial) for [Exuberant Firestoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exuberant+Firestoker) as it fit much better with my deck's overall strategy.


### Games


My first match was against Alexis. In the first game, [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) caught her perfectly, wiping out her small creatures. I played out a couple of big creatures after that and ended it quickly. In the next game she had a weak draw and didn't see enough smaller creatures. [Broodmate Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) made an appearance, along with his other brother, Darryl, to finish the job.


Against Dave I had a bit of a poor mana draw the first game and couldn't keep up with his exalted attacks. In Game 2 I made a strategic mistake on turn two. I had a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and a [Grixis Panorama](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grixis+Panorama) and a choice between playing [Elvish Visionary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) or fetching a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp). I played the Visionary, thinking I would have a chance to get the swamp later.



![Angelic Benediction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Angelic+Benediction)

Unfortunately, his [Qasali Ambusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Ambusher) and [Rhox War Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk) put on so much pressure I had to keep playing creatures just to stay in the game. When he played [Elspeth, Knight-Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant), I thought it was over, but I saw a very narrow out. If I fetched a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) with my Panorama and then top-decked any land after that, I would be able to play [Predator Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predator+Dragon) and hold off the flying 6/7 for a turn—after that, who knows, but at least it was one turn. I went for it and indeed, drew the land. The next turn got me [Broodmate Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) off the top. Dave sent in an alpha strike, and I was forced to block with every creature I had. He killed my other Dragon with [Resounding Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Roar), and I was again backed up against a wall. I might have had one more turn to draw something like [Resounding Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Silence), but Dave dropped [Angelic Benediction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Benediction) and tapped my [Court Archers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Archers) while attacking in the air for the win. Even with better draws, I was clearly not favored in the matchup.


My third match against Lee included this almost comical play: He had out [Blood Cultist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Cultist) and [Rockslide Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental). I had [Druid of the Anima](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid+of+the+Anima) and [Rakeclaw Gargantuan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakeclaw+Gargantuan). His creatures already had one counter each, so an attack with the Gargantuan wouldn't put me ahead. Instead I decided to play [Caldera Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caldera+Hellion). I sacrificed only one creature, not both. This was because his elemental would gain counters from my sacrifices before it took damage and two more counters would make it a 4/4. Fortunately, he didn't have any tricks to save his creatures and I wiped them out.


In the next game, Jund charm was just as effective as it had been against Alexis. I was only sad that I hadn't seen Sarkhan in a single game. I had a lot of fun and was very happy that my strategy was well planned and worked out well despite being in heavy competition for some of my cards.
