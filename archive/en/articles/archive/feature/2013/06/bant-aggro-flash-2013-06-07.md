Bant Aggro Flash
================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on June 7, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Gerry Thompson's really been helping me with my homework this week, and this is another sweet deck he directed my attention to (something he's always good for, really).

![Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Experiment+One)  
 This deck was piloted to a 4–0 finish by excellent deck builder Shouta Yasooka, yaya3 on **Magic Online**, so I assume he is at least largely responsible for the list, which takes Bant Flash in a much more aggressive direction than we'd seen so far. 


[Experiment One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) is great here, because despite the relatively high spell count, the creatures (and some of the spells) are particularly great at triggering evolve. 

 I like his decision not to play [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) or [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas), which allows him to just play the cards he wants rather than worrying about spell count. Instead, he prioritizes cards he can play at instant speed. 


[Spell Rupture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Rupture) is a great counterspell in this deck, but the choice to play exclusively [Spell Rupture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Rupture) and no [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate)s is significant just because it shows that he's not really concerned with exiling spells, which can be very significant against Reanimator. Still, with so many large, cheap creatures, [Spell Rupture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Rupture) is likely to be much more consistent as a counterspell and plays far better with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). 

 His sideboard offers Block Constructed powerhouse [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses), which I'm surprised he only has two of, as well as an assortment of lifegain, counterspells, and removal.
