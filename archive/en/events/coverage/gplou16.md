Black, Severa and Cohen make their case in the Bluegrass State
==============================================================




Friends, roommates and Madison natives Sam Black, Justin Cohen and Matt Severa went wire-to-wire as the favorites in Louisville and walk away the champions!

TOP 4 COVERAGE



More than 400 teams entered Grand Prix Louisville, but only one led from wire-to-wire. That would be 20th-ranked Sam Black and friends and teammates Matt Severa and 2014-15 Rookie of the Year Justin Cohen.


In a stacked Top 4, it was the Madison trio who outlasted the rest, knocking off Paul Rietzl, Jelger Wiegersma and Matt Sperling in the semifinals before moving onto another Hall of Famer in Ben Rubin and teammates Matt Boccio and Tillman Bragg, who had sent packing a pair of Pro Tour champions and a former Player of the Year in Steve Rubin, Alexander Hayne and Mike Sigrist.


In *Eldritch Moon* and *Shadows Over Innistrad’s* final Grand Prix, we saw everything from Sigardian Priest up to and including Emrakul, the Promised End, not to mention fan favorite Chris Pikula teaming up with Jamie Park and legend Jon Finkel to a Top 16 finish.


There was something for everyone at Grand Prix Louisville, but in the end it all belonged to the boys from Madison. Congrats to Black, Severa and Cohen!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



More than 400 teams entered Grand Prix Louisville, but only one emerged from the first day of action intact. No. 20 Sam Black teamed up with roommate (and the 2014-15 Rookie of the Year) Justin Cohen along with Matt Severa, and the trio stands atop the field after the first day of competition.


With a loaded tournament field it came down to one final match against Jon Finkel, Chris Pikula and Jamie Parke to decide which team would enter Sunday as the favorites to advance to the Top 4. Thanks to the power of Emrakul, the Promised End it was the Madison crew that was able to seal the deal.


While they may have the lead, there’s not much breathing room to be had. The team of No. 5 Steve Rubin, No. 10 Mike Sigrist and No. 24 Alexander Hayne sit right behind them on the leaderboard, along with Finkel’s team and the trio of No. 22 Paul Rietzl, Matt Sperling and Jelger Wiegersma.


With such a talented field in contention, who will rise to the final four?
