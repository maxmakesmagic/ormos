Magic 2012 Draft-Along
======================



 Posted in **Limited Information**
 on August 16, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_SteveSadin.jpg)
By Steve Sadin











It's Feedback Week here at DailyMTG.com, and that means that it's time for us to draft along with the [***Magic** 2012* Booster Draft Simulator](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/Resources.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/resources/boosterdraftsimulator)!

While the Booster Draft Simulator isn't as exciting as sitting down for a draft with your friends, at a tournament, or on **Magic Online**, it's still a good way to hone your skills, answer some interesting questions, and experiment with new ideas—all without having to purchase three booster packs, or set aside two or more hours to draft, build your deck and play out your matches.

In addition to being free, quick, and repeatable, the Booster Draft Simulator also allows you to interact with other players in a pretty unique way.

[![](http://archive.wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/activity/M12DraftViewer_ScreenShot.jpg)](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/Resources.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/resources/boosterdraftsimulator)  
If you're following along with someone else's prerecorded **Magic Online** draft, you can say that you would have made a pick differently... but after that pick is made you are forced to follow along as the draft has been irreversibly changed by the pick that the person who is physically drafting the deck.

If you would have taken a [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) where the person drafting the deck took [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate), you'll be able to see exactly what would have happened in the alternate reality that you created by making a different choice for the next seven picks... but then you are back to the [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) world where the person to your left got passed a juicy [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) plus all the other dominoes that consequently fell into place. There's just no way to figure out what would have happened after that.

By using the Booster Draft Simulator, you can see exactly what would have happened if you made a series of different picks than the person drafting the deck.

Then, once you're done with your draft, you can hop on over to the forums to talk about interesting picks, and share your final draft deck.

Have you done [this week's draft](http://archive.wizards.com/mtg/flash/draftsimulator/md8sj3ns0sk/magicdrafter.html) yet? Good—now you can see what I did!

Looks like we get to start the draft off with a very difficult pick!

If you have a strong preference for black, then [Gravedigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) is a very appealing pick. During the first week or two following the release of ***Magic** 2012*, I probably would have taken the [Gravedigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) as I was having a ton of success with Black-Red Bloodthirst (in large part because players were undervaluing the one-cost enablers like [Goblin Arsonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Arsonist) and [Tormented Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Soul)).

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gravedigger)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger)  
However, I think that Black-Red Bloodthirst is a bit overdrafted right now (which, admittedly, is not something that you need to think about when drafting with seven AI drafters... but I'm not going to let the fact that I'm drafting with algorithms rather than people skew my picks).

Given the fact that it's tough to put together a Red-Black Bloodthirst deck right now, I'm simply unwilling to sacrifice card quality early on in an attempt to put the deck together. Yes, I'm still willing to try to draft the deck, but for right now I'll only do so if I open, and/or get passed, a series of good cards for the archetype.

This leaves me with a choice between [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) and [Gideon's Lawkeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%27s+Lawkeeper).

I think that there's a ton of merit to both of these picks.

[Gideon's Lawkeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%27s+Lawkeeper) is an absolute steal of a deal at one mana. It lets you deal with the biggest and priciest of threats that your opponent presents, and it can even sneak in for a few points of damage if you draw it early.

Meanwhile, [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) is one of the best finishers in the format. If things are even remotely close when you cast [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun), you're probably going to win the game even if your opponent is holding a removal spell to deal with it.

Yes, there are plenty of expensive creatures that are great *if* your opponent doesn't have an answer, but the fact that you will be up several cards the moment you resolve [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) makes it very special.

**My Pick**

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun)  
There were a couple of factors that made me want to go with the [Gideon's Lawkeeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%27s+Lawkeeper) on this pick.

Normally when given the choice between two cards of comparable power level, I'll go for the cheaper option. I do this because you can usually find reasonable cards to top off your curve with, but it can be quite difficult to find cheap spells that have a big impact on the game.

Additionally, I don't particularly like drafting control decks in M12, as I think that the beatdown decks in this format are pretty fantastic. There are enough good cheap creatures to allow for some blazingly fast starts, and many of these cheap creatures are strong enough that the beatdown decks in this format can have late games that are comparable to those presented by dedicated control decks.

I will only try to draft a control deck if I have the right cards to build around—and [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) is devastating enough that I'm willing to build my deck around it.

After taking [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun), I want to pick up: a bunch of card drawing/library manipulation so I can reliably draw into my game-breaking cards and removal spells (like [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter), and [Divination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination)); dedicated control cards (all the while keeping my curve low so I can stay competitive against the beatdown decks of the format and buy enough time to cast my card-drawing spells); and/or tempo oriented cards like [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept), and [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) that will help me buy time and press any advantages that I might develop.

Yes, it's a bit unfortunate that we just passed a pack with three very strong white cards. It's also too bad that we have to pass [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) and [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) in this pack. [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) is not only a great card in the abstract, it's also a pretty perfect card to have when you're trying to build a control deck that wants to stall the game until it can cast [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun).

It's also worth noting that [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) and [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) are the second and third best cards in the pack, so the fact that I am passing a couple of good blue cards ultimately shouldn't be that big of a deal.

If things go according to plan and I do end up with a white-blue control deck highlighted by [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) and [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), then [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) will make a very good splash. If things don't go according to plan and I have to abandon blue or white (which could very easily happen as I never want to feel married to my first couple of picks), then I'm going to be very happy to have a [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade).

Because pretty much all of my damage sources have flying and I have two [Divination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination)s, I opted to run two [Pride Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pride+Guardian)s instead of my [Armored Warhorse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Warhorse) and/or my [Coral Merfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk).

I would usually fit a [Turn to Frog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+to+Frog) or two into my deck, but I only have a handful of creatures with more than 1 toughness, so I'm not going to be getting much value out of them.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Turn+to+Frog)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+to+Frog)  
While I'm not ecstatic about the way this deck turned out (especially given the high quality of cards in the draft), it has all the tools that I need to win: a good curve, good evasive creatures, some solid removal spells, several ways to gain card advantage, and a couple of absolutely game-breaking bombs at the top of my curve.
