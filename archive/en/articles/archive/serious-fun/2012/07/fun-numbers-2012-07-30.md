Fun by the Numbers
==================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on July 31, 2012 










Mathematics is awesome.

Seriously. While you might not prefer the field of digits, theory, and logic, it's thanks to millennia of development through countless cultures and individuals that our entire modern world exists as it does today.

As a mathematician I'm prone to bias here. But that doesn't make what I said entirely untrue.

Take, for example, **Magic**. It's a game driven by numbers, order, logic, and processes. Statistics and probability are game-defining pieces of what make it awesome. Every deck you build and every game you play are influenced by the mathematics underneath. While I can't explain or even completely define them, my appreciation for the subtle balances built into twenty years of refinement only grows.

*Boring!*

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf206_sleep.jpg)[Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) | Art by [Chris Rahn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=%5B%22Chris+Rahn%22%5D)

I know. Waxing philosophic about things I don't have to be concerned with maintaining isn't particularly interesting. But it does lay the groundwork for what we're doing today: basic statistics. Now, before you hit the back button in your browser hear me out. For two weeks I asked for [Krenko, Mob Boss](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%2C+Mob+Boss) Commander decks to be submitted. I carefully reviewed each submission. Out of the generosity of my heart I reformatted decklists that weren't in the requested format. (You're welcome!) Finally, I set about some fun that's a very different flavor from what you're used to.

Today, I'm going to show you a very different way to build decks. It isn't easy or fast, but that's part of the process of discovery it creates. Everything I discovered was driven by readers like you.

**CREATE TABLE KrenkoDecks**

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Krenko%2C+Mob+Boss)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%2C+Mob+Boss)Let's start with a handy link. [This spreadsheet](http://mtg.mn/Krenko) contains all of the data from all of the decks that each of you submitted. For reference:

* "Decks" is the breakdown of every submission sent in, as well as a reference lookup for information about each card from other tabs.
* "Data" is a group of summaries, including some basic statistical calculations around lands, creatures, and spells.
* "Type Map" is a reference where I classified every card with its type line as well as a grouping based on how I look at the card. (More on this later.)
* "DS Data" is a reference for the type line for every card.
* "Deck Pivot" is where all of the data from every deck can be manipulated to share information about the cards, namely the count of individual cards and their associated characteristics, across every deck.

That should help clarify what you're looking at, should you choose to dive in. However, I'm going to do a lot of that for us, starting with some surprising info:

* 72 unique deck submissions that resulted in 5,054 lines of card data.
* 861 unique Magic cards were included in aggregate, but...
* 5 of the cards provided were illegal choices (with Krenko as the commander)!
* The average (and median) number of basic lands was 30, with 32 creatures, 31 spells, and nonbasic lands at 6.
* This results in an average (and median) deck of 99 cards. Adding in Krenko tips the scale to exactly 100, meaning I didn't mess anything up when I rolled all the decks together.

The name or username submitted with the deck is included, so you can go look at each deck individually, if you want. What I will be covering today is what one deck looks like based on everyone's rolled together. So let's do that by answering some questions.

"What is the deck of strictly the most-often-included cards?"

* Spells that help us generate extra mana, such as [Caged Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caged+Sun) and [Brightstone Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brightstone+Ritual), fuel mass token generation ([Goblin Offensive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Offensive), [Goblin Warrens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warrens)) or powerful burn spells ([Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander), [Comet Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm)).
* There are also plenty of effects to fortify our Goblin army, including lords such as [Goblin Warchief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Warchief) and explosive boosts such as [Coat of Arms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coat+of+Arms) and [Fervor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fervor).
* The only Dragon that appears is [Voracious Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voracious+Dragon). Goblins must be delicious above all else.
* 34 lands is a little leaner that I personally prefer, but things like Sol Ring and [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power) help even that out.

The two decks above are, effectively, decks by popular votes: The most representative cards based on which were submitted more often. But there's always the next step. One thing that I've been working on is adding "classes" to all of the cards in my [Commander Box](/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/168).

A card's class isn't based on its type line or other characteristics. Classes are descriptive shorthand, telling me things about the card without having to know it's full text box. By adding these classes to every card I enter into the spreadsheet I can search for specific effects I want.

To give an example, [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) is a great card for Commander. Searching up a basic land of your choice helps us cast spells. This type of effect is what I shorthand as the "Fetch" class. If you take the relevant text "search your library for a land" and search [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/) you can find many similar cards. Within my spreadsheet I can find all of them by just filtering the column for Fetch.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf206_redbull.jpg)I'm not reinventing the wheel, and what I call cards might vary from how you'd choose to. The point is that I create a very quick way to find the types of things I want. Building a Commander deck is daunting, but using a class system makes it much faster for me. And since I've added the classes for every card, we can create some much more peculiar decks. (**ProTip:** Adding classes to more than 800 unique cards is tedious. Bring caffeine if you plan to try it.)

So what kind of deck would I want? I'm a fan of tokens, recursion, and [Glorious Anthem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Anthem)-type effects. The classes Tokens, Recursion, and Anthem can give us exactly that. I also want to ensure I have plenty of mana, so adding in the aptly named Card Draw and Fetch classes, as well as several nonbasic lands, should round out a typical deck.

This is quite a different blush from before! Our creature count is way down and our nonbasic land count is all the way up to 10. What gives? It's a result of our selection:

* Tokens and Card Draw tend to come in noncreature varieties. Things like [Goblin Offensive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Offensive) and [Mind's Eye](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind%27s+Eye) are typical. This deck took a step away from what can be called Tribalthe slew of Goblins that are Goblins for Goblin's sake.
* While I added some extra nonbasic lands, some also appeared in our selection. [Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) does, technically, fetch a land if desired. [Kher Keep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kher+Keep) does produce tokens, albeit not the Goblin variety. And since Scrying Sheet was picked up I changed out basic lands over to the Snow-Covered variety.

Our selection is, ultimately, pretty narrow. There are many other classes of cards that can be considered: Removal (things that kill things), Equipment (all things Equipment), Burn (for face-melting good times), and Dragon (because, hey, Dragons) are all great things to have in Commander.

The point of this deck is to show you what happens when you turn the blinders on completely. You can gather as many decks as you want, and classify every card you see, but it still takes a wider view of the cards you're selecting to make a deck happen. What classes can do is make that process *faster* by highlighting which cards appear most often. It also calls out cards that can fill a variety of roles. For example, [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) and [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) are popular cards in Commander and elsewhere since they each fulfill multiple roles:

* You get Tokens and Gain Life from [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), while packing Deathtouch to make combat a concern for blockers.
* [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) can Gain Life, Pump creatures, and serve as Removal, all assuming you get a few hits in with it equipped.

When I saw this I was floored. *What is he doing?* Fortunately, the email explained it all:


> The basic idea is to stall and draw/manipulate your library until you draw your combo pieces to win. [Krenko, Mob Boss](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%2C+Mob+Boss) with [Thornbite Staff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornbite+Staff) and [Altar of Dementia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar+of+Dementia) allow you to make a Goblin, sacrifice it to mill (put the top card of a library into the graveyard) someone for one, untap Krenko, then rinse and repeat. You are also able to make infinite mana with most of the other sacrifice outlets. This allows you to mill people with cards like [Sands of Delirium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sands+of+Delirium). You can also get infinite mana with [Umbral Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umbral+Mantle) equipped to Krenko, and have three or four Goblins with something like [Skirk Prospector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Prospector).
> 
> So there you have it, Mono-Red Krenko Mill.

I didn't even have a class for the Walls that came with this submission, and I would never had thought to try something like this. Blue is usually the domain of milling players, but it's in red that ways in Commander exist to pull it off quickly.

Ultimately, compiling decks and classifying cards are just tools to use. There's no replacement for exploring and trying out new things. But if you find there's *too* much to figure out, I hope the idea of pulling together decks you like is something you'll try yourself.
