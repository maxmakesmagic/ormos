==============================

 on August 29, 2012

With so many people on 18 points, there was a ton of math involved prior to the round as players scrambled to figure out if they were still live for the Top 4. Martin Juza and Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa were each among the cluster of players vying for one of those final spots.


Juza, however, was in the tougher spot. He had been on 18 points for the past two rounds, but had been unable to finish off either Brian Kibler or Shouta Yasooka to hit the important 21 point mark. He had gone 3-0 with his Modern Jund deck yesterday, but was struggling as we moved closer to the elimination rounds today.


Damo da Rosa also lost last round, but had been fighting his way back from the middle of the pack all day and was now relying on his menagerie of Zoo creatures to secure his place playing tomorrow



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/tpc12/r12_pv_vs_juza.jpg)  
*While the math was tough, the bottom line was clear: win and you're probably in. Lose and you were clearly out.*

### Game 1


Damo da Rosa didn't waste any time trying to march his way to the Top 4, curving [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) into [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) plus a [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) to put Juza to 8 life before he could even cast a spell. And when Damo da Rosa fired a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) directly at Juza's dome, even a [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) couldn't save him from a swift and merciless end. [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) for [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) sealed the deal.



![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/tpc12/r12_juza.jpg)  
*Martin Juza's had luckier days.*

"What a great day," Juza said. He had dropped his last two rounds to fall back to the pack when a win in either of them could have sent him to the Top 4.


Damo da Rosa 1, Juza 0


### Game 2


Juza was clearly frustrated as his opening seven had no lands of any kind in it. He had commented yesterday that sometimes your luck turns, and you often know it the instant it happens, as his did when he came back from an 0-2 start to finish the day 4-0 and move to second place. Unfortunately, it seemed as if his luck had turned back the other way today.


Damo da Rosa, however, was chugging right along, starting with a [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx). The [Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx) died to [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), but Damo da Rosa had both [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) and [Loam Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Lion) to follow up.


Juza started defending himself with [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), but Damo da Rosa attempted to sidestep that particular fight with a [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls).


[Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) gave Juza a glimpse of the future, and it didn't look good. [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix), and [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) were all extremely bad for Juza, and he clearly recognized the trouble he was in. He took [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) before casting [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) to clear out the Spirits.


As predicted, [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) cleared out the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) while Damo da Rosa's pair of 2/3s took him down to 12 life.



![](https://web.archive.org/web/20141031230440im_/http://archive.wizards.com/mtg/images/daily/events/tpc12/r12_pv.JPG)  
*Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa has his eyes on the prize, and a sliver of hope that he can make it in to the Top 4 playoffs.*

[Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), however, killed the [Loam Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Lion) before dying to [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) the following turn. Without black mana, Damo da Rosa couldn't flash back [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). Instead, he simply lost his [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) to a [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) and cast a [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx).


The [Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx) stayed a 0/1 for the time being, but Damo da Rosa found bigger game in a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) anyway.


[Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) cleared out the [Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx) and Juza found the [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) he had wanted for a few turns. But the cascade fates weren't kind.


"Can't even kill the 'Goyf," Juza said, staring at the [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) his Bloodbraid flipped and the 5/6 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) on Damo da Rosa's side of the board. "Wow, this sucks."


Instead, Juza simply fired the [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) at his teammate's head and began to try to figure out what the Brazilian pro had in hand.


"You don't have land, you don't have creatures, so you have..."Juza trailed off.


"Burn," Damo da Rosa finished.


But all of the calculations had only served to distract Juza, who then attacked with his [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) thinking the three damage would kill it, despite the fact that he had done the same math with [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) earlier in the turn.


"Yeah, perfect play," Juza said, shaking his head at the misplay and dumping the 3/2 into his graveyard while the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) lived on.


Damo da Rosa attacked Juza precipitously low before flashing back [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). When all Juza found on his turn was a [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) for [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), all Damo da Rosa had to do was flash him a pair of burn spells to convince Juza to pack it in.


Damo da Rosa 2, Juza 0
