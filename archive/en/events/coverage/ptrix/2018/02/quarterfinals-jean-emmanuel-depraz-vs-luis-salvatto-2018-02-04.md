Quarterfinals: Jean-Emmanuel Depraz vs. Luis Salvatto
=====================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 4, 2018 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











It might be shocking to learn that Hareruya Latin team member Luis Salvatto had only competed in seven Pro Tours before this weekend. Debuting at the 2011 World Championship, he had now made his second Pro Tour Top 8 in his eighth attempt. Quite frankly, that conversion rate is absurd. Salvatto also has five Grand Prix Top 8s, including a title from Grand Prix Rotterdam 2016, and he even won the 2015 Super Sunday Series Championship!


The Argentinian Gold pro's weapon of choice this weekend was Lantern Control, a prison strategy which served him well all weekend. After practicing with the deck for a few months, Salvatto's efforts paid off handsomely.


Salvatto aimed to empty his hand while finding [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) to shut down opposing attackers. After that, [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight) would enable him to see the top card of his opponent's deck. He could use either [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder) or [Pyxis of Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyxis+of+Pandemonium) to manipulate his opponent's draw step. By carefully selecting which cards his opponents would receive, he would eventually run his opponent out of cards by denying them anything relevant to draw for the rest of the game.


Jean-Emmanuel Depraz of France was playing in his third Pro Tour, and he was also a Grand Prix Champion himself. After winning Grand Prix Warsaw 2017, Depraz worked up a head of steam and charged into the Top 8.


[Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) variants have been a dominant archetype in Modern in recent years, and Depraz's build included Green for [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). It also allowed him to play [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse). Since Salvatto's deck was predominantly artifact-based, [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) could also seek out [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker) for additional artifact removal. These spells will play a crucial role should his offense get shut down by a backbreaking [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge).


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/ptrix/qf_depraz_salvatto.jpg)  
*Jean-Emmanuel Depraz has made a good season even better with his first Top 8 at only his third Pro Tour. He is facing off against Hareruya Latin's Luis Salvatto, Argentinian Gold pro in his second Pro Tour Sunday appearance.*


The Games
=========


Salvatto kickstarted his quarterfinal match as though he was playing Affinity, dropping five permanents on the board on turn one. The sequence of [Spire of Industry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+of+Industry), [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal), [Mishra's Bauble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Bauble), [Pyrite Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrite+Spellbomb), and [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) (naming [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith)) was an excellent start. Depraz's [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) took out [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), leaving Salvatto with just a single [Glimmervoid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmervoid) in his hand.


On his second turn, Depraz summoned a 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and Salvatto did not have the [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) yet. The first attack reduced Salvatto to 16 life, and Depraz added a second copy of [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).


Suddenly, Salvatto was facing a two-turn clock, and he would need to find a solution fast. Taking 8 more damage (going to 8 life), he untapped and drew what could have been his final card of this game. Luckily, it was [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings), which netted Salvatto the [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) he sorely needed.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/ptrix/qf_depraz.jpg)  
*Depraz brought a Traverse Shadow deck to this Pro Tour with some sweet targets for [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald), including relative Modern newcomer [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker).*


Things began to look up after that.


Within the next five turns, he peeled [Academy Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins), [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), and [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder). Sacrificing [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder) repeatedly to reuse [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings), Salvatto had the foundations of his prison almost done. With a timely [Whir of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whir+of+Invention), Salvatto completed the impenetrable lock of double [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), all four [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder)s, both [Pyxis of Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyxis+of+Pandemonium)s, a pair of [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge)s, and [Witchbane Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Witchbane+Orb)!


In Game 2, Depraz was first to play, but he was stuck on one land. He spent his first three turns firing off triple [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), binning Salvatto's [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal), [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), and [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge). Salvatto returned the favor, stripping Depraz of [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).


However, Depraz had a pair of fetch lands to knock his life total down to 11, allowing him to recruit a 2/2 [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow). A follow-up [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) reduced Depraz to 9 life while growing [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) into a 4/4.


Salvatto needed to draw something fast to get out of the fix, and he did. Evading death once more by locating [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) via [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings), Depraz helplessly watched on as Salvatto built his wall around him. After milling both [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay)s and [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)s from Depraz's deck, Salvatto knew that he would make a safe getaway.


The first two main deck games were not easy for Depraz, for he had few solutions to [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge). However, things were about to change after sideboarding, as he swapped out a bunch of useless creature kill spells for some artifact destruction instead. [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse), [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge)s, and [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker) were much better than, say, Fatal Pushes and [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) in this matchup.


In the third game, Depraz was on the play again, but Salvatto had the first move. Plopping [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) on the board, this was horrible news for Depraz, who had kept a hand with two [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)s. Nonetheless, this did not discourage Depraz, who tried his best to stay in the game.


He countered Salvatto's [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) with [Stubborn Denial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stubborn+Denial), but lost [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) to [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). To make matters worse, Depraz's next two draw steps were sheer mockery, as he found his third [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and then [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) right after that.


While Depraz was drawing those dead cards, Salvatto assembled [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder), and [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge). Not wanting to lose relevant cards from the top of his deck, Depraz fired off [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) at [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder).


And then, he drew his fourth [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize).


Not to say that they were totally useless, though. Along with some aggressive fetching, the [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)s helped Depraz lessen his life total down to 4. That made for a 9/9 [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow), and a timely [Temur Battle Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temur+Battle+Rage) killed Salvatto out of nowhere.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/ptrix/qf_salvatto.jpg)  
*Salvatto seeks to assemble his impenetrable wall while managing exactly what his opponent can draw each turn.*


"When I saw the Leyline, I was pretty worried, but I'm glad it worked out fine," Depraz smiled. Despite that "pseudo-quintuple-mulligan", Depraz managed to secure the win.


"I'll be on the play," Salvatto announced.


"So, are there matchups where you want to be on the draw?" Depraz asked.


"Usually, I like to be on the play, but sometimes I go on the draw against certain decks. For example, I choose to play second against Titan Shift, because I get one extra card and all they do is ramp, ramp, ramp," Salvatto explained.


In Game 4, Salvatto opened with [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity), [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar), and [Mishra's Bauble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Bauble), but that was much less exciting that Depraz's lines of play.


Thanks to two fetch lands and cycling [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith), Depraz took himself down to 12 life and summoned two 1/1 [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s on his second turn! Salvatto's best play was [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge), but he still had three cards in his hand.


Both of Depraz's [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s could attack, causing Salvatto to get a little worried. When the [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) player sinks into the think tank and starts doing some intense calculations, it is usually a sign of trouble. Eventually, Depraz got it all sorted out.


Fetching a basic land to go down to 11 life (making [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s 2/2), Depraz cast [Liliana, the Last Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope) and used her second ability to return [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith) to his hand. He attacked with both 2/2 [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s for 4 nonlethal damage, to the relief of Salvatto, who spent his next turn reducing his hand size down to one.


This would prevent the 2/2 [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s from attacking, but Depraz had a workaround. Activating [Liliana, the Last Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope)'s first ability, he shrunk one of his 2/2 [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s into a 0/1 and charged into the red zone. Before damage, he sacrificed another fetch land for an untapped [Blood Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt) to go down to 8 life, and then cycled two [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith)s to go down to 4![Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) ballooned into a 9/9 (7/8 due to Liliana), and brought Salvatto down to 8 life.


Then, Depraz summoned [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and wiped out the final card in Salvatto's hand, before using [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) to find [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker). Eating up [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) (bypassing [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar)), he was able to attack with both 9/9 [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s to equalize the score!


"[Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker) is amazing in so many situations," Depraz said, declaring his love for the Human Pirate. "I especially love it when I use [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker) to steal my opponents [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)s in mirrors."


Just before the rubber game, both players shook hands and wished each other good luck. The four previous games had been great so far, and we expect no less in the final game.


Salvatto's turn one [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) revealed Depraz's land-light hand.


* [![Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek)
* [![Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=STREET+WRAITH)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith)
* [![Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Death%27s+Shadow)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow)
* [![Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)
* [![Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thoughtseize)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize)
* [![Liliana, the Last Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%2C+the+Last+Hope)
* [![Marsh Flats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Marsh+Flats)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats)

That was quite a lot gas in Depraz's hand—even though he only had one land—and Salvatto had to mull over his options for a full minute.


"If I can take [Marsh Flats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats), it would be much easier," Salvatto admitted. The decision was not straightforward, but as he was prompted by a judge to make a selection, Salvatto decided upon [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow). He then sculpted his hand the next turn with two [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings) and also added [Ensnaring Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnaring+Bridge) to his board.


Meanwhile, Depraz was still stuck on one land, prompting Salvatto to switch gears. Instead of allowing him to draw lands, he began to restrict them. Through [Lantern of Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+of+Insight), Salvatto saw [Verdant Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) on the top of Depraz's deck, so he used [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) to jam it down. A second [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) shut down [Polluted Delta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Delta) later on. Despite Depraz having five lands in play, three of them were fetch lands, so he only had two mana at his disposal.


With that breathing room, Salvatto repeated the loop of [Whir of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whir+of+Invention), [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder), and [Academy Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins), eventually finding all four of his [Codex Shredder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Codex+Shredder)s and both his [Pyxis of Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyxis+of+Pandemonium)s.


Carefully denying Depraz of mana, Salvatto made sure that there was no way to break his fortress. Whirring for a second [Welding Jar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welding+Jar) to reinforce his walls, he eventually made it to the finish line.


"Usually, you want your opponents to draw lands, and you deny them the key spells. But you can also do it the other way and give your opponent all the expensive spells and prevent them from getting the mana they need. Lantern Control is amazing!"


"Good luck for your upcoming matches," said Depraz as he extended his hand.


"Thanks! Congratulations to you too, your first Top 8!"


Depraz didn't look too unhappy as he stepped away, for this weekend's results had exceeded his expectations. Coming into this weekend at 15 Pro Points, he will pick up at least 16 Pro Points to add to that.


Meanwhile, Salvatto advanced to the semifinals, hitting Gold level for the 2017-18 season. With more fortune to come, he could be a Platinum or a Pro Tour Champion by the end of the day!


**Luis Salvatto defeats Jean-Emmanuel Depraz 3-2 and advances to the semifinals!**
