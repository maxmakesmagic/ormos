Flavors of Delver
=================



 Posted in [GRAND PRIX KYŌTO 2015](/en/events/coverage/gpkyo15)
 on April 17, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman_icon_0.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











With almost all the cards in Magic's history at your disposal, Legacy is an extremely diverse format with more archetypes than you can imagine. Despite the power level being higher than Standard and Modern, it is comforting to know that no deck is particularly dominant and even the most represented ones occupy a small percentage of a typical tournament's metagame due to the large number of viable strategies.


Having said that, there are still fan favorites. The blue-based tempo deck.


The deck that was cited by many pros such as Hall of Famer Kenji Tsumura, Gold Pro Ken Yukuhiro and Pro Tour Top 8 Competitor Chikara Nakajima as the deck to watch this weekend.


The Delver deck.



![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?multiverseid=226749&type=card)![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?multiverseid=226755&type=card)


Ever since the days of Innistrad, [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) has quickly taken its place as one of the most important creatures in Legacy of all time. With the aid of [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) and [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), it almost always is a 3/2 flyer for a single mana and it is a quick clock when backed up counter magic and a sprinkle of support cards from other colors. For blue mages who enjoy playing with [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) and partners-in-crime [Daze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) and [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle), it is difficult to resist the allure.


Let us take a quick look at the many flavors of [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets)!

Staying in line with the theme of efficient and oversized creatures, Temur Delver features the green splash for [Nimble Mongoose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimble+Mongoose) and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). While the creature base deck may not have the best utility, they are worth every bang for their buck. A red splash for [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) is almost customary and sideboard options such as [Pyroblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroblast) complement the beatdown strategy and provide additional reach and support.

In particular, Tasigur is probably one of the best trumps in the attrition matchup should the game go longer than expected. Not only is it decently sized and stacks up against [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), it also costs half the price thanks to the myriad of cheap spells and fetchlands that are cornerstone of the entire Delver strategy.
