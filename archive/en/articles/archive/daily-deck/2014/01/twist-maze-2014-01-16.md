A Twist in the Maze
===================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 20, 2014 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Gerry Thompson











Lots of people asked, so I'm here to present it in all its glory—Jon Louck's Maze's End deck from the Ramp;D Challenge. Jon decided to spice it up and play his deck singleton style with the exception of [Maze's End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze%27s+End) and [Guildgates](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;name=+%5BGuildgate%5D). 

 There are some absurd situations that can come up when you're playing a singleton deck, as you never know what you're going to draw next. Will you be able to find that [Saruli Gatekeepers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saruli+Gatekeepers) to gain the life you need? Is [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) going to show up to answer that [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections)? Will our opponent walk into our [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)? Will we win with [Maze's End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze%27s+End), or will Elspeth show up and kill us before it comes to that? 

You never know!

 Jon's deck is perfect if you're looking for a good time, although I'd find room for a [Sylvan Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) and maybe an [Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian). Even if you're only playing one of each spell, there can be some important stuff you might miss.
