At Pro Tour–San Diego, the Tom Ross–designed "Boss Naya" deck lived up to its name, posting an undefeated Standard record in the hands of Luis Scott-Vargas and a near-perfect record piloted by Ross himself. The deck features an enormous ability to ramp up its mana, multiple search toolboxes, huge threats that start small, and the surprise technology of [Basilisk Collar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar)—whether searched up by [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), gaining massive life with [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), or shooting [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)s and [Broodmate Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon)s out of the sky with [Cunning Sparkmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage). Check out the [official event coverage](/en/events/coverage/g%C3%B6rtzen-goes-all-way-san-diego) for more on this deck and the unusual Jund deck that finally took it down in the Semifinals.
