Scratching the Surface of Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*
======================================================



 Posted in **The Week That Was**
 on October 26, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New York–based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 







What an amazing performance by Stanislav Cifka. He tore off a fifteen-match win streak to start [Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*](/en/events/coverage/ptrtr12) before losing Round 16 against Kelvin Chew. He then won his next three single-elimination matches to win the event in his first appearance on the Sunday stage. He was playing great **Magic** with the perfect deck for the weekend. Who could have seen this coming? 



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw218_stanislav_cifka.jpg)*Stanislav Cifka celebrating his victory in Seattle*

 Actually, Cifka would have been a pretty reasonable pick to do well at this event based on his short Pro Tour career. This was just his seventh Pro Tour appearance, but in that handful of events he already had a Top 16 finish at [Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored*](/en/events/coverage/ptars12) as well as an 18th place finish at [Worlds in 2011](/en/events/coverage/mwc11). He was a Platinum member of the Player's Club last season and looks like he will be in great shape to defend that status for next year. Historically, if a player is able to snag a Pro Tour Top 16 and elite status, he or she is more than capable of making it over the tiebreaker hump and into the spotlight. 



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw218_ochoa_2.jpg)*David Ochoa*

 Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* semifinalist David Ochoa is another player with a Top 16—back at [Worlds in 2009](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/coverage-2009-world-championships-2009-11-12)—on his resume and elite status in the Player's Club on the strength of three Grand Prix Top 8s last season. With this finish, he finally got himself off the list of the game's top players without a Pro Tour Top 8. One of the ways players get themselves onto that list is by virtue of close calls like Ochoa's performance at Worlds or Cifka's showing in Barcelona. 


 I like to pay some attention to those Top 8 near misses so when the players finally do come out on the right side of the tiebreakers it is not a total surprise. Let's take a look at the next tier of the standings from Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*. 



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw218_ari_lax_2.jpg)*Ari Lax*

 Ari Lax was rocking a throwback Team Unknown Stars jersey polo shirt this past weekend that saw him place 9th in the Pro Tour. Players who have worn that shirt include the likes of [Gavin Verhey](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Gavin%20Verhey), Matej Zatelkaj, and Christian Calcano. Going all the way back to his days on the Junior Super Series, Lax has been someone who has eloquently shared his **Magic** experience with others. He played a BUG Infect deck to within decimal points of the Top 8 and gave a detailed primer to his creation in a [Deck Tech](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MOTbjzIj6so) with [Zac Hill](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/archive.aspx?author=Zac%20Hill) that is a must watch if you plan to play at [Grand Prix Chicago](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/grandprix/chicago12) in just a couple of weeks. 


This was not the first such finish for Lax, who placed in the Top 16 at his [Pro Tour debut in Kyoto](/en/events/coverage/ptkyoto09). With three Grand Prix Top 8s on his resume and now two Top 16 finishes, you have to add Lax to the list of players waiting on their seemingly inevitable Sunday breakthrough.


There was a lot of bated breath as Scott Larabee read the names of the Top 8. Near the bottom of the bracket there was room for three players with four losses and seven players hoping their breakers held up to secure one of those spots. Pro Tour Hall of Famer Gabriel Nassif came within a hair's breadth of his tenth Pro Tour Top 8, which would have put a little daylight between him and the newly enshrined Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, with whom he is tied for lifetime Top 8s.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw218_nassif_darosa.jpg)*Gabriel Nassif, Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa*

Nassif's weapon of choice for the Modern rounds? Robots.

Nassif is a two-time Pro Tour champion and a nine-time Top 8 competitor. When you have that many Top 8s you lose track of the near misses, but there have been abundant Top 16s in the Frenchman's career. One thing was clear about his finish: The sun has not yet set on the career of one of the game's greatest players.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw218_maksym_gryn_2.jpg)*Maksym Gryn*

Maksym Gryn came into the two-weekend stretch of [Grand Prix San Jose](/en/events/coverage/sperling-williams-and-rietzl-win-san-jose) and the Pro Tour with just 2 lifetime Pro Points. After a 2nd-place finish at the team event and a Top 16 in Seattle, he has increased those points by an order of magnitude. In his profile for the Grand Prix, the 22-year-old student wrote: "I won a match at a Pro Tour, one time." He will have much more to write for his next Top 8 profile after an impressive stretch of eight days.


The weapon he chose to battle with was Jund.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw218_miguel_gatica_2.jpg)*Miguel Gatica*

 Costa Rican player Miguel Gatica followed up his 9th-place finish from his local Grand Prix with a tie for 8th place at the Pro Tour. Like Nassif, he played Robots in Modern. Gatica has been a member of his National Team four times and had 46 lifetime Pro Points coming into the weekend. He flew under the radar all weekend but rest assured he will find his way toward the feature match area more often once [Pro Tour *Gatecrash*](/en/node/640491) rolls around. 



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw218_dan_jordan_2.jpg)*Dan Jordan*

 I feel like every time I write one of these columns, Dan Jordan's name is going to come up. This is his third Top 16 in just seven Pro Tour appearances. His first came at [Pro Tour Amsterdam](/en/events/coverage/ptamd10) and then another last season at [Pro Tour *Dark Ascension*](/en/events/coverage/ptdas12)—his second Top 16 of the calendar year. Jordan played a RWU [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck that included a sideboard [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) package that allowed him to put [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and Elesh Norn into his graveyard by just searching for those two cards.

Costa lost a Round 15 mirror match to Wily Edel that likely cost him his second Pro Tour Top 8, but anyone not considering Costa for their Fantasy Pro Tour has not been paying attention to this impressive young player of the last two seasons. Check out the match [here](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/round-15-cascade-shopping-network-2012-10-20) (and while you are at it, you might as well read more of the excellent text coverage for the event). 


 Sebastian Denno is a Canadian player who worked with a second Mana [Deprive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deprive)d squad that included Noah Long, Mike Vasovski, and Jeremey Schofield. The first team, which featured the likes of Pascal Maynard and Alexander Hayne, recently joined forces with [World **Magic** Cup](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/welcome-world-magic-cup-2012-08-17-0) champion Tzu Ching Kuo to keep pace with other super teams. After Denno's great finish, expect him to get the call up to work with the A team. 


 Denno took a different route with his take on the Eggs deck—call it a Second Breakfast with a dash of Tabasco sauce—which used Krark-Clan Ironworks and [Banefire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Banefire). He also earned an approving nod from none other than [Aaron Forsythe](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Aaron%20Forsythe) when the head of **Magic** R&D witnessed the Canadian win a match with a sideboarded [Laboratory Maniac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Laboratory+Maniac).

 Despite the success of Shouta Yasooka with [Aether Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Vial) at the [**Magic** Players Championship](/en/events/coverage/watanabe-wins-magic-players-championship), we did not see a lot of it in action at the Modern Pro Tour.  If you are planning on attending Grand Prix Chicago in a couple of weeks you can expect to see decks like those featured in this article, as well as similar ones from [the Top 8](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/pro-tour-return-ravnica-top-8-modern-2012-10-20).
