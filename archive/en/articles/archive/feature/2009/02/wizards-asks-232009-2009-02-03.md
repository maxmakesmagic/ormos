Wizards Asks: 2/3/2009
======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 3, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











*Are there any cards you love that everyone else seems to hate?*


![Kenneth Nagle](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/authorpics/authorpic_kennethnagle.jpg)**Kenneth Nagle:** Well, there are cards that I play and greatly enjoy that others seem to completely dismiss (actively "hate" is too strong a term for **Magic** cards!).


**[Orzhova, the Church of Deals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhova%2C+the+Church+of+Deals)**


![Orzhova, the Church of Deals](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Orzhova%2C+the+Church+of+Deals)  
This card holds the record for the greatest jump from least respected to most respected land in the game for me.


Side-by-side with the Worlds-winning [Vhitu-Ghazi, the City-Tree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vhitu-Ghazi%2C+the+City-Tree), [Orzhova, the Church of Deals](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Orzhova%252C%2Bthe%2BChurch%2Bof%2BDeals) looks like a steaming pile of feces, and I declared the card a travesty. MORE mana? 1 life?! [Orzhova, the Church of Deals](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Orzhova%252C%2Bthe%2BChurch%2Bof%2BDeals) can't kill a player who is gaining 1 life per turn. Vhitu-Ghazi can kill someone who is gaining 200 life per turn, algorithmically speaking.


Within a month, I was playing Orzhov Control with the Church of Deals as my primary win condition.

The Church of Deals is a ponderous but unerring threat, yet nigh-immune to everything, including [Cranial Extraction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction), [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy), [Mortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify), [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), [Night of Souls' Betrayal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night+of+Souls%27+Betrayal), [Hinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hinder), [Time Stop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Stop), [Worship](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worship), and even [Faith's Fetters](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) (thanks, [Orzhov Basilica](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Basilica)!). While hard at work as a 20-turn clock, the Church of Deals ensures that your opponent's [Char](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char)s, [Kokusho, the Evening Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kokusho%252C%2Bthe%2BEvening%2BStar) triggers, and [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)es are only temporary, relentlessly tilting the life total balance back in your favor.


Around 75% of this deck wins come from a Church of Deals twenty-turn clock. Another 24% are won via [Neverending Torment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neverending+Torment), about a two-turn clock after some setup (their deck becomes completely benign on the second turn, though it takes five or so turns to kill). Once I had to kill my opponent with desperation [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) beatdown in extra turns.


Lesser men might put their faith into a [Kokusho, the Evening Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kokusho%252C%2Bthe%2BEvening%2BStar) or [Yosei, the Morning Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Yosei%252C%2Bthe%2BMorning%2BStar) to kill a [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute)d and [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Cranial%2BExtraction)ed opponent. Though Dragons they may be, immortal they are not. I am willing to kill my opponent by sinking a hundred mana into a land across twenty turns.


[Orzhova, the Church of Deals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhova%2C+the+Church+of+Deals) is a weapon for the dry accountant, the stoic monk, the patient sculptor, or the extreme griefer. It's my weapon, and I wield it proudly.
