James Buckingham's Esper Control
================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on April 6, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 





As I write this, I am on an airplane flying to Brussels for a 20-day stay in Europe. The purpose of my trip is obviously Pro Tour *Dragons of Tarkir*.



Pro Tour *Fate Reforged* was Modern, so it has actually been six months since we've had a top-level tournament in Standard. I'm arriving early to join the testing group and am very interested to see what decks await me. I can only hope that by the time this article is posted, I have decided on my deck and can sleep at ease.



This week I would like to take a look at some Standard decks which may be a hint to what the new format will look like; especially decks including new cards from *Dragons of Tarkir*.



I will start with James Buckingham's Esper control from the [StarCityGames Invitational](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/deckshow.php?event_ID=21&t[T1]=1&start_date=2015-03-27&end_date=2015-03-27&state=VA&city=Richmond&order_1=finish&limit=8&t_num=1&action=Show+Decks). This control deck makes use of several spells from *Dragons of Tarkir* that say "if you have a Dragon."



The backbone of the deck are [Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate), the cheap draw spell all blue mages have been waiting for, and [Dragonlord Ojutai](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai). Ojutai has gone through a 1000-year workout and can now use [Anticipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate) himself.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dragonlord+Ojutai)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Ojutai) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Anticipate)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anticipate)

He can be summoned with five mana and is a reliable blocker/finisher with hexproof. Unlike similar cards in the past, the cards from *Dragons of Tarkir* also trigger when you have a Dragon on the battlefield. Therefore, there's no need to hesitate playing him. The fact that he survives [Crux of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crux+of+Fate) also supports this, and attacking with Ojutai after resetting the board while keeping [Silumgar's Scorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%27s+Scorn) in your hand sounds like a fine plan.



Anyway, I'm sure we are going to see a lot more of this "look at the top three cards of your library and choose one" effect.
