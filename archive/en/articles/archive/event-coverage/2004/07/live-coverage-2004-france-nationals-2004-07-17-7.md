Live Coverage of 2004 France Nationals
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on July 17, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_92.png)
By Mark Wraith











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/frnat04/fm2_1.jpg)Thomas made top eight in the final European Championships in London last year. Bastien came very close to top eight in the Nationals of 2003, only losing in the last round to fall short. Thomas is playing Green-White with [Astral Slide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide)s and [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), and Bastien has a very strange red-green deck with a nice collection of morphs. ### Game 1

Bastien's mulligan provoked a fist pumping 'One step closer to victory', from the voluble Thomas. He started with a turn two [Slith Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Firewalker), turn three morph. Thomas cast [Astral Slide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide) on his turn three and then cleared the board with [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). This left Perez with only a [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) to attack. He summoned another morph which bizarrely was a [Skirk Marauder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Marauder). Thomas was visibly surprised when it unmorphed 'You're not supposed to play that'. The knowledge that his opponent had strange morphs would no doubt make his blocking decisions harder, after all the next morph could easily be a [Battering Craghorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battering+Craghorn).

Thomas stopped the Skirk hitting with his [Astral Slide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide), but he was taken even further aback when Bastien cast a [Hammer of Bogardan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer+of+Bogardan) at him. Regardless of the oddities coming at him, Thomas gained control when he drew an [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), allowing him to bring back [Renewed Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renewed+Faith) every turn via the [Astral Slide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide).

Bastien had drawn rather too many [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s so he had little to do but bring back the [Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer) and recast it. A second [Astral Slide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Slide) gave Thomas complete control, and on his next turn he drew a [Plow Under](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plow+Under). In combination with the Witness this meant that Bastien would spend the rest of the game drawing the same lands over and over. Rather than waste time he scooped.

Thomas Shaw 1 - Bastien Perez 0

### Game 2

Bastien [Magma Jet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet)ted on turn 2, and then cast a [Caller of the Claw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caller+of+the+Claw) inThomas end phase on turn three to continue the trend of unusual creatures. He [Electrostatic Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrostatic+Bolt)ed an [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and started to burn suggesting he had [Pulse of the Forge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Forge) in hand. On his fourth turn he dropped a [Specter's Shroud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Specter%27s+Shroud) and equipped the [Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call)er with it ! There was certainly no doubt that Bastien had gone to a real effort not to play a standard deck.

Shaw [Oxidize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oxidize)d the Shroud and then [Creeping Mold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Mold)ed Perez's [City of Brass](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Brass) as Bastien was short on lands, reducing him to only two [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s in play .In response Perez used a [Pulse of the Forge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Forge) to drop Shaw to nine. Shaw then cast [Plow Under](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plow+Under), Witnessed it and cast it again. The mana burn that Perez had taken came back to haunt him : Thomas drew [Duplicant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duplicant) to remove Bastien's remaining morph - a [Nantuko Vigilante](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Vigilante), and soon attacked for the win.

Thomas Shaw 2 - Bastien Perez 0
