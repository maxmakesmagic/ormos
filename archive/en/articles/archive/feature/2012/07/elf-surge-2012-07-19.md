Elf Surge
=========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 19, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











I don't know about you, but big spells excite me. Sure, they might not always be the most reliable of things, but when you finally get to cast them, big things happen. [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) might be the poster child for expensive tournament staples, but plenty of others have succeeded. [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) has seen moderate play in the past few years. [Enduring Ideal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Ideal) was a tournament-winning deck in old Extended. Even [Obliterate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obliterate) has seen its fair share of success. That said, none of those cost ten!

![Primal Surge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Primal+Surge)[Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) might be the most powerful effect in Standard, but at a price tag that is so far out there it is difficult to justify it as a legitimate card. Then, once you have conjured up a way to actually cast it, you need to refine your deck to eliminate as many instants and sorceries as possible! Sounds like a lot of work, and it is, but with the help of some pointed-eared friends, Josh Blackborow proposed the following list that TCGPlayer columnist Christopher Morris-Lent [wrote about recently](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=10588).

The list plays similar to the previous Elfball strategy that looked to fuel out a big [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) as quickly as possible. This one needs to get an additional mana, though, and the restrictions on your deck certainly hurt, but this is definitely the most viable home for [Primal Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Surge) I have seen yet. [Gilded Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus) and [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) pitch in from ***Magic** 2013*, but appearances by [Shrine of Boundless Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrine+of+Boundless+Growth) and [Palladium Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palladium+Myr) might be the most startling, as we haven't seen those guys since *Scars of Mirrodin* Limited! This deck looks like a ton of fun, though, for both the FNM crowd as well as the tournament hopeful!
