Simonot’s Simic Swarm Sweeps London
===================================




February 9-10, 2013












Grand Prix London 2013
----------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplon13/Trophy-Simonot.jpg)


In taking the crown here in London, Timothee Simonot completed the set for his small group of friends, who have all now featured in a Grand Prix Top 8, with two titles among them. Louis Deltour was the runner up at the last Grand Prix London, Pierre Dagen made Top 8 just a couple of months ago in Bochum, and Jeremy Dezani had won Grand Prix Lyon just a couple of weeks earlier! By any measure, that makes for an incredible few months of **Magic** for these four musketeers!


Drafting a quick, consistent, and crucially TRICKY Simic deck saw Simonot cruise past his Semi-Final and Final opponents. If the ever-Evolving [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor)s and [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One)s didn't get you, then he was more than happy to gain control of all your creatures with [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator). Both Manuel Hauck and Bartlomiej Tomiczek brought Boros to the table, but neither could contain Simonot, the Simic mastermind.


Simonot becomes the first champion of *Gatecrash*, defeating a mammoth 1969 other players who had arrived on Saturday with hopes of getting their hands on the trophy now heading back to France. One of the largest **Magic** tournaments of all time was a perfect way to welcome *Gatecrash* to the game and we saw some new stars born, both in Simonot's cool victory and in cardboard as well.


The coverage train now rolls out of London, bound for Pro Tour *Gatecrash* in Montreal before heading all across the globe for seven packed weeks of **Magic** action. If Grand Prix London is anything to go by, we're going to be talking *Gatecrash* cards an awful lot in the coming weeks. But for now it's congratulations to Timothee Simonot, and roll on Montreal!


Au revoir!

Sunday, 7:41 p.m. – Top 8 Draft: Andreas Nordahl
------------------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe*

 

Before the Top 8 draft began Norway's Andreas Nordahl shared with me the key component of his plan to win Grand Prix London – go Orzhov.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplon13/Top-8-Draft-Nordahl.jpg)
 

"When you look at the power level of the cards on their own it seems pretty obvious that red is the strongest color, and the good red cards go really deep. But it's very easy to clog up the ground in this format and then red doesn't get through. I've also got no experience of playing Simic so I'd prefer to play Orzhov – you have to really get the nuts cards to make it worthwhile as Dimir. There's a lot of value in being flexible, though, and not committing too early to a guild"


Orzhov was the plan, so let's see how it worked out for the Norwegian...

Quarterfinals – Round-Up
------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

The match between Andreas Nordahl and David Reitbauer was featured in all detail by our friends on video, leaving us with three quarterfinal matches, recounted here in not quite as much detail.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplon13/qf1.jpg)*Left to right: Jamie Ross (Orzhov) vs. Manuel Hauck (Boros)*
 

In game one, Ross kept a slow hand, while Hauck came out fast with [Truefire Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Truefire+Paladin) and [Boros Elite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite). He couldn't capitalize on that, though, when he failed to draw a fourth land. [Guardian of the Gateless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+of+the+Gateless), [Homing Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homing+Lightning), and [Knight Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Watch) were all stuck in Hauck's hand, and to make matters worse, he also lost his Truefire Paldin, when Ross cast [Aerial Maneuver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aerial+Maneuver) on his blocking [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards). Meanwhile, Ross himself didn't put up much of an offense. Various extort creatures managed to get Hauck down to 7, but when lands finally did arrive on Hauck's side, the sheer volume of spells he had accumulated quickly overwhelmed Ross.


For game two, Ross started with [Thrull Parasite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrull+Parasite), [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet), [Gutter Skulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gutter+Skulk), [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat), and more [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet). "That was just too much extort," as Hauck put it, when he picked up his cards for game three.


Here, Hauck opened on [Truefire Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Truefire+Paladin), Bomber Squad, and [Ordruun Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordruun+Veteran), whereas Ross got stuck on three [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), and remained there for the rest of the match. His single spell this game, [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards), couldn't quite compete with that.


**Jamie Ross 1-2 Manuel Hauck**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplon13/qf2.jpg)*Left to right: Timothée Simonot (Simic) vs. Per Carlsson (Simic splashing Rakdos)*

Carlsson's first creature was [Scab-Clan Charger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Charger) on turn four, and that just wasn't enough.


[Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) into [Frilled Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus) into [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis) gave Simonot a sizable early offense once again. Carlsson fought back valiantly, though, entering the race with his ground creatures. For a moment it looked as if Carlsson had actually turned the game around when he cast [Dinrova Horror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dinrova+Horror), but Simonot only redoubled with [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake). Simonot only had [Disciple of the Old Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Old+Ways) (without red mana), [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark), and [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) (unfortunately in this order). Lands and a [Scab-Clan Charger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scab-Clan+Charger) did arrive on Simonot's side of the board, just in time, as Carlsson sped up his clock with [Keymaster Rogue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keymaster+Rogue) (returning [Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn)). In a sudden turn of events, Simonot now turned his zero fliers into three via [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake), putting a second counter on both [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) and [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One). Soon he was the one attacking, while Carlsson's [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis) was held back on defense. Carlsson tried to ambush Simonot with [Totally Lost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Totally+Lost) on [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake) during combat, but that backfired spectacularly when Simonot had [Mystic Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Genesis): Not only was [Totally Lost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Totally+Lost) countered, Simonot also got a 5/5 token which turned [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) 5/4 and [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) 4/4. Carlsson was suitably impressed, then dead.


**Timothée Simonot 2-1 Per Carlsson**

Manuel Hauck won the die roll and put himself on the play, confidently keeping his opening seven cards while Timothee Simonot was forced to mulligan an unexciting opening hand of five lands, [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis) and [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake).

The threat of the Corps was real, but Simonot played a [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis) to Evolve both his creatures out of range of the [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps), and go onto the offensive. Hauck struck back with his two [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps) and Simonot decided it was important to prevent his opponent building a Battalion, throwing his [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis) at one of the Corps. Hauck seemed happy enough with the trade as well, replacing the lost [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps) with an Orduun Veteran.


Simonot's curve stumbled for a turn, but Hauck only passed the turn right back to his opponent as well, leaving Simonot the chance the Evolve both his creatures once again after playing a [Sage's Row Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage%27s+Row+Denizen). A 3/3 [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) and 2/3 [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) attacked to deal the first sizeable piece of damage of the match, leaving the scores 18-12 in Simonot's favour.


Could Hauck hit back? Simonot was defenceless, but apparently Hauck wasn't playing the typically aggressive Boros deck and spent the turn playing [Guardian of the Gateless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+of+the+Gateless) and holding both his [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps) and [Ordruun Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordruun+Veteran) back from the red zone!



![Guardian of the Gateless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Guardian+of+the+Gateless)

Play passed back to Simonot, but the Frenchman passed it right back to Hauck – clearly unwilling to tangle with the [Guardian of the Gateless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+of+the+Gateless). Hauck attempted to add a [Towering Thunderfist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Thunderfist) to his team but it was countered by [Spell Rupture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Rupture) and the players remained locked in a peculiar stalemate. It was Hauck who again attempted to break the deadlock, and this time his [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards) resolved unhindered.


Simonot revealed that he had drawn a [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake), and that gave both the [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) and [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) yet another +1/+1 boost, as well as Flying. Sending the [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) into the red zone Simonot decided to test the waters – the [Guardian of the Gateless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+of+the+Gateless) blocked and gained First Strike thanks to [Furious Resistance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furious+Resistance), but Simonot simply pulled two +1/+1 counters off the Experiment to regenerate it.


Unable to profitably attack against Simonot's oversized Simic creatures, Manuel Hauck turned to Plan B, or rather Plan E: Extort. The German summoned a [Knight of Obligation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Obligation), which sat alongside his [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards) as a real threat should the game drag on.


Simonot had been guarding his [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) in hand, safe from Hauck's [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps), but now saw an opportunity to gain an advantage and played the dangerous Simic wizard. On paper the Manipulator was vulnerable, but Simonot had drawn [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark), which could be played with Flash to give the Manipulator +1/+1. Hauck took the bait and attacked with his [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps), [Ordruun Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordruun+Veteran) and [Knight of Obligation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Obligation), and Simonot sprung the trap – playing the [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) in response to Battalion and placing his blocks!


It was a painful turn for Hauck, who lost both his [Ordruun Veteran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordruun+Veteran) and [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps), while only managing to kill a [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor), while [Massive Raid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massive+Raid) accounted for Simonot's [Sapphire Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sapphire+Drake).



![Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Simic+Manipulator)

Simonot's tricksy [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) had broken the deadlock and Hauck was vulnerable: the Frenchman played a [Deathcult Rogue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathcult+Rogue) to add a second +1/+1 counter to the [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) then took control of Hauck's [Knight of Obligation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Obligation), then added a [Frilled Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus) to his growing swarm. From a stalemate just a turn earlier it was now seven creatures vs two, and Hauck needed some serious help, but while the German still had [Guardian of the Gateless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+of+the+Gateless) it was too dangerous for Simonot to attack with all his might and he had to be content to swing the [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) for 4 instead.


Hauck drew a [Boros Elite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite), and Simonot drew a gamebreaker – a [Gridlock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gridlock) tapped Hauck's creatures and cleared the way for a massive alpha strike, taking the first game for Timothee Simonot!


Timothee Simonot 1 – 0 Manuel Hauck


For a Simic-Boros matchup that first game had been an unusual cat and mouse affair but you had to tip your hat to Timethee Simonot, whose rope-a-dope trick with [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) and [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) had ultimately made the difference.


A mulligan hadn't harmed Simonot's chances in the first game and he began the second in similar fashion. Across the table Manuel Hauck made a stuttering start, with a [High Priest of Penance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Priest+of+Penance) on the second turn but then missing his third and fourth land drops, to leave his Boros deck with only two land and no [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s!



![Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shambleshark)

Simonot must have smelt blood in the water – he played [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator), then a [Frilled Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus) to allow him to steal Hauck's [High Priest of Penance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=High+Priest+of+Penance). Things were rapidly going wrong for the German, and although he found a third land he was already 3-0 down on creatures, and with the real danger of the [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) stealing anything he could actually play. If the German had a silver lining it was that Simonot was hardly able to deliver convincing beatdown with his 1/1 Priest and 1/3 [Frilled Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus), meaning Hauck would have time to recover from his poor start.


Hauck played a [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards) but Simonot was waiting with the perfect answer, casting his [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) with Flash to give the Manipulator +1/+1, then immediately claiming the [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards) as his own! Things were rapidly going from "awkward" to "flat-out bad" for Hauck although he managed to play [Knight of Obligation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Obligation) on his next turn, while Simonot only had a [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis) to give his Manipulator a single +1/+1 counter, and couldn't immediately steal the Knight.


That was only a temporary reprieve for Hauck, and when Simonot played [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura) on his next turn the [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) was able to place the hoodoo on Hauck's [Knight of Obligation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Obligation) as well.


Six creatures played none and a glance at Simonot's hand revealed that he was holding a [Mystic Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Genesis) handy in case Hauck had any surprises up his sleeve. It wasn;t necessary, and the German offered his hand in congratulations – Timothee Simonot advanced to the Final of Grand Prix London!


**Timothee Simonot 2 – 0 Manuel Hauck**




Finals – Timothee Simonot vs. Bartlomiej Tomiczek
-------------------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe*

 

Simonot won the dice roll and wasted little time in getting his Simic offense rolling, with an [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) being joined by [Frilled Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus) – Tomiczek had no second turn play and was quickly taking damage as Simonot wasn't slow to start pumping his [Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oculus). [Arrows of Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrows+of+Justice) accounted for the [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) on the next attack, but Tomiczek was already down to 12 life and Simonot replaced the [Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oculus) with an unblockable [Keymaster Rogue](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keymaster+Rogue)!


Tomiczek played a [Zarichi Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zarichi+Tiger) but Simonot's offense kept coming, with the Keymaster dropping the Pole to 9 life before Simonot added a [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) and re-cast his [Frilled Oculus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frilled+Oculus) to put a +1/+1 counter on the killer rare. [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) had been the card that Manuel Hauck had been unable to stop in the Semi-Finals, and now Simonot was planning to use it once again to take a quick lead in the Final!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplon13/Final-Tomiczek.jpg)*Bartlomiej Tomiczek*
 

Tomiczek dipped into the tank searching for answers before emerging with an [Orzhov Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Keyrune) and a [Gift of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Orzhova) onto his [Zarichi Tiger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zarichi+Tiger). The Gift was a real gamble, because if Simonot could get three counters onto his Manipulator the Pole would quickly find the Tiger heading back in his direction!


Tomiczek attacked with the Tiger, climbing back to 12 life only to drop back down to 7 on Simonot's next turn. With the Frenchman's lands completely untapped Tomiczek had to act, but also had to fear any tricks from the Simic player's hand. Tomiczek had to try, though, but his [Knight Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Watch) was countered by [Mystic Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Genesis)! The 5/5 Ooze was enough convince Tomiczek the game was up, and Simonot eased into a 1-0 lead.


Timothee Simonot 1 – 0 Bartlomiej Tomiczek


Bartlomiej Tomiczek stared at his opening seven cards long and hard before choosing not to mulligan. It made for a brave call, choosing to keep a hand of red and white mana sources and black removal. If the Pole drew a [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) his hand would come alive, but fail to draw one and this could be one of the quickest final games of all time.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gplon13/Final-Simonot.jpg)*Timothee Simonot*
 

Simonot was determined that it would be the latter, and came roaring out of the blocks with a [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor) and [Disciple of the Old Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Old+Ways), then [Drakewing Krasis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drakewing+Krasis).


The pressure was undoubtedly on but skipping his third land drop left Tomiczek in trouble and missing the fourth drop as well meant the Pole had virtually no route out of trouble. The game was up and Simonot completed the rout in five unopposed turns to claim the title of champion of Grand Prix London and the first champion of *Gatecrash*!


### Timothee Simonot 2 – 0 Bartlomiej Tomiczek




Top 5 Cards
-----------



 

These were the cards that were most discussed, that led the charge, decided matches, and shaped this Grand Prix.


 


![Madcap Skills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Madcap+Skills)


 

### 5. [Madcap Skills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Madcap+Skills)


Several players, even in the Sealed portion of the tournament, ran as many as four one-drops in their decks, and this very aggressive approach to the format was by no means exclusive to Boros decks (though it certainly helped with Battalion)—Gruul decks were invited to the party as well. In either archetype, it often was [Madcap Skills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Madcap+Skills) which single-handedly turned a one-drop into a savage beating, that as early as turn two. Not to mention the smashing combination with [Armored Transport](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Transport). Kenny Öberg did mention it, saying: "If they don't block the Transport they're dead really quickly, and if they do block it ... it's like a double chump!"


  
  

 
 


![Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Slaughterhorn)


 

### 4. [Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn)


If he were allowed to pick one common to have five copies of in a *Gatecrash* sealed pool, Pro Tour *Dark Ascension* quarterfinalist Denniz Rachid would choose [Slaughterhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhorn). "I believe this may in fact be the correct answer here," he said, making note of the all-important versatility. A decent-sized creature or a [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) was just what was needed to break the ages old problem facing aggro decks, of wanting the maximum number of combat tricks to keep up the pressure but also having enough creatures to start the pressure in the first place.


  
  
  

 
 


![Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kingpin%27s+Pet)


 

### 3. [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet)


By general consensus, [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet) is the strongest of all the extort creatures, and a key player in giving the Orzhov deck the ability to deal early damage, to then finish opponents off via extort. A large number of [Basilica Guards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilica+Guards) will drain all life too, eventually, but [Kingpin's Pet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kingpin%27s+Pet) definitely gets there faster and more reliably.


  
  
  
  
  
  
  
  

 
 


![Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Simic+Manipulator)


 

### 2. [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator)


David Reitbauer called it the best card in his sealed deck and in his second draft deck, partly responsible for putting him in the Top 8. In the Top 8 itself it was then Timothée Simonot's turn to manipulate. He didn't need [Simic Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Manipulator) to win his quarterfinal, but the card dominated in the semi, especially when he surprised an attacking (and pinging) [Bomber Corps](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomber+Corps) with [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) to allow his Manipulator to survive at 1/2.


  
  
  
  
  

 
 


![Mystic Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mystic+Genesis)


 

### 1. [Mystic Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Genesis)


[Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) was a good card back in the day. Countering a spell, any spell, and getting a 2/2 creature for free? That's kind of unfair. Nowadays we have [Mystic Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Genesis) and that's reaching a whole new level of unfair. During the Top 8, we saw champion Timothée Simonot countering [Totally Lost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Totally+Lost) and later [Knight Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Watch). Countering a spell and getting a 5/5 creature for free? That's a beating!
