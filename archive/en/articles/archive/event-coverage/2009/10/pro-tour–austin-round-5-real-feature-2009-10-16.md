Pro Tour–Austin Round 5: The Real Feature
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 16, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett












Conley Woods (White-Black Martyr) vs. Shouta Yasooka (Green-White-Blue Gifts)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------


Two big names sat down at 3-0-1 in the last Constructed match of the day. On one side, 2006 Player of the Year Shouta Yasooka, playing White-Green-Blue Gifts, and on the other, PT–Honolulu Semifinalist and rogue deck designer Conley Woods.


“FAKE FEATURE!” –a nearby Brian Kibler, unhappy at being uncovered


“I hate fake features. They’re the worst. You’re all happy and then you get three words on Twitter.” –Conley Woods


### Game 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptaus09/r5_woods_yasooka.jpg)*Conley Woods and Shouta Yasooka make a colorful pair.*
Woods kicked things off with [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate), seeing a hand of [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment), [Pulse of the Fields](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Fields), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Rude Awakening](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rude+Awakening), and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). He took the [Rude Awakening](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rude+Awakening). Yasooka searched up a [Temple Garden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden) at end of turn with a fetch land and cast his Goyf. Woods passed on three mana and took 2, and Yasooka likewise made no play. Again Woods stood pat, but this time the charging [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) got hit by [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). Yasooka had a replacement ready.


Another [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate) caught Yasooka with a third [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) in hand. Woods took 3 from the Goyf on the table and watched as it was joined by [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks). He put down a [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) (inching towards enough [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) with Emeria already on the battlefield) and chump-blocked, gaining 3 by showing [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). He untapped and cast it, tidying the board a little.


Yasooka was on the back foot now. He swung for 2 but had no play. Woods, meanwhile, tapped six for [Sorin Markov](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin+Markov). The big vampire planeswalker took a bite out of the Finks, but Yasooka cashed in his [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) for more mana. His draw was a [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch). Woods dealt 2 to it with Sorin and then finished it off with [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant). Yasooka could do nothing but pass his turn.


Woods used Sorin’s [Mindslaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) ability and spent Yasooka’s turn getting rid of [Pulse of the Fields](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Fields) and [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). Emeria got him back his Martyr on his upkeep. Now Ajani was locking out Yasooka’s only blue mana source. Yasooka got back his Heirarch at end of turn with [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin) and swung in, trying to kill off Ajani, but Woods had [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) in hand. From there it was all over but the struggling.


Woods 1, Yasooka 0


### Game 2


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptaus09/r5_yasooka.jpg)*Yasooka lays the beats.*After a mulligan from Woods, they were off. Yasooka was able to get his [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) out ahead of [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate). He turned over a juicy hand of [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin), twe [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives), and [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter). Woods took the Mannequin and some damage, then put out a Martyr to chump and gain 6, showing [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos).


Next he tried a [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena). Yasooka Pathed his own Goyf to get to five mana he needed on his next turn to blow up the [Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena) with Explosives. Woods followed up with a Ranger for a pair of Martyrs, then hit and cast one of them when Yasooka did nothing on his turn.


Naturally, Yasooka was holding [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven). He presented [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam); [Emeria, the Sky Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria%2C+the+Sky+Ruin); [Miren, the Moaning Well](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Miren%2C+the+Moaning+Well); and a [Godless Shrine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godless+Shrine). He got to keep two of the lands, then dredged back the Loam to get the rest of them from his bin. [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) wiped the board and Woods gained 6 off his Martyr. He was, sadly, far from turning on his own Emeria.


Yasooka didn’t let up. [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) let him rebuy [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), offering Woods [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction), [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), and [Yosei, the Morning Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yosei%2C+the+Morning+Star). He got to keep [Yosei, the Morning Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Yosei%252C%2Bthe%2BMorning%2BStar) and [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). He wasted no time in pointing that at Woods, who turned over a hand of [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). He took [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and then got [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) with virtual buyback on [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin).


Woods couldn’t draw into action. Yasooka, meanwhile, had [Yosei, the Morning Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Yosei%252C%2Bthe%2BMorning%2BStar) and [Miren, the Moaning Well](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Miren%252C%2Bthe%2BMoaning%2BWell) to start locking him down, and enough [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) to keep [Yosei, the Morning Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Yosei%252C%2Bthe%2BMorning%2BStar) coming back with Emeria. Woods could not escape the vise.


Woods 1, Yasooka 1


### Game 3


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptaus09/r5_woods.jpg)Woods shook his head as he sent back his first seven. His next six were worth keeping, but Yasooka decided he needed to go down to five, then cast [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). Woods held [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter), [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena), [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos), and [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate). Woods played out his lands and waited.


Yasooka dropped a Witness on turn three, and Woods looked with dismay at his uncastable [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate). He untapped and Rangered up a pair of Martyrs, but Yasooka was firing on all cylinders. [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) got him Loxodon Heirarch and [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), and put [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction) and Witness into the graveyard. He untapped and cast the [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). Woods, finally with black mana, [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate)d all of Yasooka’s [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven)s, catching a second one in Yasooka’s hand and leaving him with [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Makeshift Mannequin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin), [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch), and [Emeria, the Sky Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria%2C+the+Sky+Ruin).


Then it was [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)’s turn to resolve. It took [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) and left Woods with Martyr and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile). Down came [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch). Woods had no play, and traded [Ranger of Eos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) and [Martyr of Sands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) for the Hierarch. Yasooka passed it back.


Woods [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate)d. Yasooka got a free Witness with Mannequin before losing it to Castigation. His hand was [Pulse of the Fields](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Fields), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize). This he used on his turn, and Woods responded with [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) on the non-mannequin Witness. Yasooka was slowly accumulating plains for his Emeria.


Woods topdecked an Emeria of his own, but had just five [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) and [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) on the battlefield. Yasooka’s came online first, and Witness meant it was bringing back spells. First was [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction) for [Sorin Markov](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin+Markov). Woods fought back gamely as the clock ran out, both players keeping a brisk pace, but Yasooka’s tide could not be pushed back. As they went into extra turns, Yasooka was ready with lethal on board.


Yasooka 2, Woods 1
