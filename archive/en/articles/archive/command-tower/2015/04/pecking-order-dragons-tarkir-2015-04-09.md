The Pecking Order of Dragons (of Tarkir)
========================================



 Posted in [Command Tower](/en/articles/columns/command-tower-archive)
 on April 9, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






Whether you're physically carrying a suitcase of decks or avidly cranking out drafts online, *Dragons of Tarkir* is finally here in all its incarnations.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/cardart_FateForgotten.jpg)



[Fate Forgotten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Forgotten) | Art by [Cliff Childs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5b%22Cliff%20Childs%22%5d)



While large tournaments and the Pro Tour this weekend look to define Standard for the next few months, it's the tables off to the sides everywhere else—homes, local game stores, and even Grand Prix—where the *rest* of the set will make its showing.



Commander, as an Eternal format, is more comparable to Legacy and Modern in how new cards shake things up: It's hard to dislodge the best and broken of yesterday from decks. But unlike other ways to play, with nearly every card in *Magic*, Commander encourages variety and experimentation with its restrictions.

Look at the Size of that Fella!
===============================



Going through my own decks leads to plenty of small changes, and it's been exciting to see the synergies and massaging the newest cards provide:



* [Damnable Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnable+Pact) and [Deathbringer Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Regent) play into the controlling, and damaging, themes of my [Mogis, God of Slaughter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogis%2C+God+of+Slaughter) deck.
* [Sidisi, Undead Vizier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sidisi%2C+Undead+Vizier) and [Vulturous Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vulturous+Aven) are easy to use with my [Pharika, God of Affliction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%2C+God+of+Affliction) deck popping out 1/1 Snakes regularly. And both [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) and [Foul Renewal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foul+Renewal) play into its graveyard-for-value theme.
* [Secure the Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Secure+the+Wastes) is as close to "a [Rhys the Redeemed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) card" as I can ever imagine making, and [Ainok Survivalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ainok+Survivalist) shores up another way for my deck to destroy annoying artifacts and enchantments.


Of course, these are just my modest tweaks. Some of you enjoyed cards far more, like Frank and [Foe-Razer Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foe-Razer+Regent):



Following the preview of *Dragons of Tarkir*, one of them stood out to me: [Foe-Razer Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foe-Razer+Regent). I had been toying with the idea of building a Gruul Fighting deck for a while, and this Dragon solidified it. I chose to go a path that allowed me several routes of victory, through commander damage, pure power or even infinite combo. Several other previews from *Dragons of Tarkir* turned the deck to have a small Dragon focus. Right now I have this list drafted up.



The deck is built around the standard green ramp into big things play style, with red providing me with draw power, grants haste and a few additional combat tricks. I chose to avoid one-of fight cards and instead focus on repeatable effects. In addition to fight effects, there are various other effects that involve entering the battlefield and damaging something. The commander was already set in stone; [Atarka, World Render](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka%2C+World+Render), both for it's high power and it's ability to come out and utterly destroy one opponent in one go.



So here I present: Atarka's Fight Circus.

After seeing what [Foe-Razer Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foe-Razer+Regent) could do in Limited, I'm not surprised to see it as part of a deck that takes the Dragons side of [Frontier Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontier+Siege) to its natural conclusion. Flying power and ways to clear the path are already solid routes to Commander victory, but doing it with red and green feels strange.



The Gruul and Atarka's brood aren't known for subtlety, but aerial death in red-green is still a bit startling. Fear is exactly what those Dragons feed on (aside from everything else that is).



Another draft deck-dependent Dragon that impressed me was [Enduring Scalelord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Scalelord), and Jim didn't miss the opportunity either:



When I first started building around [Ghave, Guru of Spores](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) with the initial Commander release, I knew I wanted to focus on one of his two abilities. I'd seen a lot of green-white token decks and adding black to the mix didn't seem interesting enough for me. I had also seen green decks with +1/+1 counters before… but mostly in an annoyingly Simic way. Using counters in green with black and white support was what I decided to take a shot on.



My focus on +1/+1 counters paid off when *Khans of Tarkir* brought the Abzan and their outlast ability. Getting cards in all three colors that cared about +1/+1 counters was the Christmas Ghave had been waiting for! You want flying? Try [Abzan Falconer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Falconer). You wanna untap all your creatures on everyone else's turn? [Ivorytusk Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivorytusk+Fortress). A little low on life? [Abzan Battle Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Battle+Priest) is there to pick you up. So many fun new toys!



*Dragons of Tarkir* brings one more +1/+1 counter creature in the mix I can't wait to try out: [Enduring Scalelord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Scalelord). Just like its uncommon kin, [Juniper Order Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juniper+Order+Ranger), [Enduring Scalelord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Scalelord) is a game changer with Ghave. A huge, flying, +1/+1 counter stockpile that can end games on its own or help to amass a Saproling army. Whether it's enchantments like [Abzan Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Ascendancy) and [Citadel Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Citadel+Siege) or creatures like [Vigor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigor) and [Necropolis Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropolis+Regent), boosting my other creatures only makes this Dragon nastier!

The Rest of the Roost
=====================



We're just beginning to plumb the depths of *Dragons of Tarkir*, and I wanted to take a moment to call out how rich the set is for Commander:



* The Monument cycle—[Atarka Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka+Monument), [Dromoka Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka+Monument), [Kolaghan Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan+Monument), [Ojutai Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai+Monument), and [Silumgar Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar+Monument)—are fantastic pieces of two-color mana rock. Having a bonus Dragon lurking in any deck is totally worth it.
* [Fate Forgotten](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Forgotten), [Hidden Dragonslayer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hidden+Dragonslayer), and [Radiant Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Purge) are cards any white player worth their salt will consider: In a format filled with powerful permanents and big, colorful creatures it's rare these answers won't have threats to battle.
* [Sunscorch Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunscorch+Regent) is a flying [Taurean Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taurean+Mauler). [Taurean Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taurean+Mauler) is a multiplayer powerhouse. [Sunscorch Regent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunscorch+Regent) just unseated it.
* [Profound Journey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profound+Journey) and [Myth Realized](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myth+Realized) are excellent late-game tools for a deck the needs them. Getting back two permanents for the price of one card, or creating an ever-growing monstrosity that dodges sorcery-speed removal is great.
* [Stratus Dancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stratus+Dancer) isn't [Willbender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender), but stopping an instant or sorcery is usually worth it when you're trying to keep "the deck full of [Crux of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crux+of+Fate)" in check. Similarly, [Blessed Reincarnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Reincarnation) shared the "two-for-one" effect of rebound, but instead answers one annoying creature an opponent controls for another. It's random and can backfire, but for mono-blue it's worth it.
* [Foul-Tongue Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foul-Tongue+Invocation) is a handy tool for opponents that go all-in on one commander, particularly useful since it's an instant and can respond to a second creature being cast.
* [Berserkers' Onslaught](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserkers%27+Onslaught) and [Impact Tremors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Impact+Tremors) work for different decks but for similar reasons: If you're playing [Purphoros, God of the Forge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purphoros%2C+God+of+the+Forge)—or tokens in general—the Tremors will add up damage quickly. And if you're on the hate-and-smash plan, double strike for just you is excellent.
* [Assault Formation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Formation): Be wary of the "second Doran" in [Doran, the Siege Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower) decks.
* The Command cycle—[Atarka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka%27s+Command), [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command), [Kolaghan's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kolaghan%27s+Command), [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command), and [Silumgar's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silumgar%27s+Command)—are all powerful, flexible tools in Commander. The one I'm going to cast most often is [Atarka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Atarka%27s+Command), putting a land into play on someone else's turn while doming everyone for 3 life (that's 9 damage total for just two mana in most of the games I play) will be good every time.
* [Swift Warkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swift+Warkite) is going to make [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) look even better in Jund decks, and [Ruthless Deathfang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruthless+Deathfang) will be an easy way for blue and black decks to crush creature decks.


There's even more packed into the set that I didn't need to touch on—you'll discover it soon enough!
