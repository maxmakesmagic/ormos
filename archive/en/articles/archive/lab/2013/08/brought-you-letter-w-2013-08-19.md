Brought to you by the Letter W
==============================



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on August 19, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikecannon.jpg)
By Mike Cannon




 Mike Cannon started writing From the Lab at the end of 2012 after two years with GatheringMagic. He is an ardent casual player and loves finding uses for bad cards. 







Welcome, minions! This week at the Lab, I decided to do something a bit differently. There are thousands of unique **Magic** cards in existence, with names starting with every letter of the alphabet. X has the fewest with ten, and even Q has several dozen. Therefore, I decided to choose a letter at random, scour through some of the best cards beginning with that letter, and build a pair of decks around them. As you've probably realized by this point, the letter chosen was W. 

### A Whole New World

 The first card that came to mind was [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World). I've always been a fan of the card, so finding ways to abuse it wasn't difficult. With the right list, you can cast [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) over and over again until your entire deck is on the battlefield. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl261_wholenewworld.jpg)  
 The key to going infinite with [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) is being able to return it from your graveyard to your hand every time you cast it. There are actually quite a few cards that can handle this task. [Anarchist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anarchist) can return a sorcery card form your graveyard to your hand when it enters the battlefield. If you hit it with [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World), it can return the card immediately, ready to cast again. [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) also works, and costs a bit less mana as well, although that won't usually matter much since you'll be putting it straight onto the battlefield. 

 The other thing you'll need to keep [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) going is some permanents that make more permanents. One of the best is [Saproling Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling+Burst). It can give you up to seven permanents for only five mana, depending on how long you have before casting [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World). That's a huge amount of value in this deck.

 The other token-producing cards come from *Rise of the Eldrazi*. [Nest Invader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nest+Invader) comes down early, giving you two creatures for two mana. A 2/2 is big enough to trade with an opposing creature if necessary, and Eldrazi Spawn tokens can be sacrificed for mana to cast [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) if you have more creatures than lands. Continuing up the chain, [Kozilek's Predator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Predator) gives you three creatures for four mana, and [Emrakul's Hatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%27s+Hatcher) gives you four for five. The Spawn tokens are great in this deck, giving you your choice of mana or permanents for [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World). [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) on the other hand, gives you both at once. Although it's only one permanent, it's a first-turn play that can help make sure you have the colors of mana you need, while also helping you reach eight mana more quickly. 


[Mystical Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Tutor) will help make sure you actually get [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) in your hand each game, since otherwise you're not doing much aside from assembling an army of 0/1 tokens. In fact, That's all you're getting from [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) at this point as well. [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) will make your 0/1 tokens into enormous trampling monsters for a turn. Although the Behemoth will be plenty big enough to kill your opponent by itself once your entire deck is on the battlefield, you don't want a timely [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to foil your plans. To that end, I've brought back [Saproling Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling+Burst)'s old partner in crime, [Fires of Yavimaya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Yavimaya). It gives all your creatures haste, allowing you to attack immediately with your absurdly gigantic army of various creatures and tokens. If I've done the math right, your final [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) will end with you attacking for 4,670 damage, minus any creatures that are stuck in your hand or graveyard.

### Deal 'Em

 My next idea was constructed from a number of cards. [Wheel and Deal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+and+Deal), [Wheel of Fortune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fortune), [Windfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windfall), and [Whispering Madness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whispering+Madness) all have fairly similar effects, and all start with the letter W. After considering a number of different ways to abuse this kind of effect, I settled on an old casual favorite: [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim). With [Liliana's Caress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Caress) from ***Magic** 2011*, we can now play eight copies of this effect, making anything that forces your opponent to discard his or her hand potentially deadly. 


[Wheel and Deal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+and+Deal) and [Windfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windfall) stayed in the deck, thanks to [Windfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windfall)'s efficiency and [Wheel and Deal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+and+Deal)'s ability to be cast at the end of your opponent's turn. The other cards were replaced by [Memory Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Jar) and [Magus of the Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Jar). These two nearly guarantee your opponent will be discarding seven cards, rather than using what's already in his or her hand. That's 14 damage with a single [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) on the battlefield, and lethal with anything more 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Wheel and Deal | Memory Jar |


[Dimir Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Signet) and [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone) accelerate your mana, allowing you to cast [Memory Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Jar) and [Magus of the Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Jar) on turn four. [Dimir Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Signet) fixes your mana if necessary, while [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone) can be sacrificed for a new card if you have extra mana. Finally, [Disperse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disperse) allows you to deal with any problem permanent, while dealing your opponent a bit of damage in the process. With all the various effects forcing your opponent to discard his or her hand, bouncing a permanent is just as good or better than killing it most of the time.

### One W Leaves

Although I did a brief stint in the WWWWWWWF (What in the World, Why are We Wresting, We're Writers! Federation), today I'll be taking a back seat as our two W decks battle it out for the Championship Welt. Let's taking things down to the arena and see which side proves victorious.


**Game 1**


 Both sides started off with a land, and Grimfall made the first move with a turn-two [Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone). Spawn World followed with a turn-two [Nest Invader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nest+Invader), and Grimfall cast [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) before passing the turn. Spawn World used a [Misty Rainforest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misty+Rainforest), then cast [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) to return the land to hand. Grimfall played a land and passed the turn, and Spawn World sacrificed a token to cast [Emrakul's Hatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%27s+Hatcher), making three more. Grimfall cast [Wheel and Deal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+and+Deal) at end of turn, making Spawn World lose 10 life from [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim). A [Windfall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windfall) then ended the game with another 14 damage.

### Your Number's Up

 It's contest time again here at From the Lab, and this is one long-time readers might be familiar with. During 2010 and 2011, [Noel](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Noel%20deCordova) held a series of votes allowing you all to select the theme for his next contest. For three votes in a row the loser, sometimes by a fairly narrow margin, was the collector-number contest. Despite losing to some of the other ideas, there was still a fair bit of interest in the idea, and now it will have its revenge. 

 Here's the concept. Every modern **Magic** card has a collector number on the bottom, below the artist credit. You can also find these numbers on the [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx). Selecting two of these numbers, (or only one for bonus points), build a deck using only cards that were printed with that number. For example, #154 would give you access to Forest from *Planechase* (2012 Edition), [Masked Admirers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masked+Admirers) from *Modern Masters*, and [Fertilid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertilid) from ***Magic: The Gathering**â€”Commander*, among many others. 

Design your deck and send me the list through the email link at the bottom of the page. Entries will be accepted through September 9, which should give you all plenty of time to scour through those pages of tiny numbers. I'll be highlighting my favorites during the following weeks, so check back then to see if your deck made it to the top. See ya!
