=======================================


1. Grand Prix Warsaw and Santiago

It was a tale of two decks this weekend, as Black-Green Delirium put the most copies of any deck into the Top 8s of both Warsaw and Santiago, but White-Blue Flash won each of the tournaments. But there was plenty more coming out of those tournaments to pay attention to. Here are a few items worth looking back on:


* [Grand Prix Santiago Top 5 Moments](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsan16/los-top-5-momentos-2016-10-30)
* [Grand Prix Warsaw Top 5 Moments](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpwar16/top-5-moments-2016-30-10)
* Top 8 lists for [Santiago](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsan16/top-8-decklists-2016-10-30) and [Warsaw](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpwar16/top-8-decks-2016-10-30)

I also want to talk about the deck that got second at Santiago, so we'll do that in the [Deck of the Day](#10) below. It was a gutsy metagame call that nearly paid off for one player.


1. [North American Eternal Weekend Top 8 Decks in Vintage and Legacy](http://www.cardtitan.com/coverage)

Somehow it was fitting—[Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End)—yes, the one currently legal in Standard—dealt the final points of damage in the Vintage Championship during Eternal Weekend as Joseph Bogaard won that particular crown. And, oddly enough, even though it didn't make the Top 8, [Paradoxical Outcome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradoxical+Outcome)—yes, the *Kaladesh* card—was the talk of the tournament. You can check out the Top 8 decks in both Legacy and Vintage on the link above.


You can check out the replays of Emrakul's Vintage takeover as well as everything else from [the Card Titan Twitch archive page](https://www.twitch.tv/cardtitan/v/98040029).


1. [Eric Deschamps Slideshow of Flaying Tendrils Promo Art Creation](http://www.ericdeschamps.com/blog/2016/10/26/eldritch-moon-art)

Over the weekend, Eric Deschamps showed off his process for the creation of some *Eldritch Moon* promotional art. It's spooky *and* gorgeous.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Jonathan Lobo Melamed's Blue-Red Fevered Visions](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsan16/top-8-decklists-2016-10-30)

[Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions) is a deck known for feasting on midrange decks just like Black-Green Delirium, and boy did Jonathan Lobo Melamed feast this weekend, finishing in second place at [Grand Prix Santiago](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsan16).


The deck is a holdover from pre-*Kaladesh* Standard, with a few important upgrades. First, [Spirebluff Canal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirebluff+Canal) is a great upgrade for the mana base in a deck that lacked enough strong dual lands until *Kaladesh*. Second, [Chandra, Torch of Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance) finally came out to play, acting as a key four-drop at the top of Melamed's curve. Chandra, along with the deck's namesake [Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions), makes sure the deck never lacks for things to do.


Speaking of things to do, [Thing in the Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thing+in+the+Ice) was nowhere to be seen. Instead, Melamed went aggressive with [Thermo-Alchemist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thermo-Alchemist) and [Stormchaser Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormchaser+Mage), choosing damage over any form of subtlety or trickery. From there, cheap removal and direct damage did the rest.


As black-green continues to re-rise, look for [Fevered Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fevered+Visions) to start keeping it in check, just as Melamed almost did all the way to a trophy. Almost.
