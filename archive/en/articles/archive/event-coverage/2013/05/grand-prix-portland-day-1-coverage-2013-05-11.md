Saturday, 2:36 p.m. – Maze's Modern Impact
------------------------------------------


*by Mike Rosenberg*

 

The release of *Dragon's Maze*, the third and final set of the *Return to Ravnica* Block, brought two major changes to the Modern format:


-It added 156 new cards to Modern, including some potentially powerful options to the larger formats such as [Beck // Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beck+%2F%2F+Call) and [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence)


-It brought changes to the Banned & Restricted list, as [Second Sunrise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Second+Sunrise) received the cut and Eggs has been left scrambled as a deck choice for Modern


With Eggs taking over the last Modern Grand Prix in San Diego, the format has shifted with the departure of one of the deck's key cards. The Grand Prix in San Diego also spotlighted some interesting new tech choices for the various green-black base decks, and also sported long-time contenders and combos.


Given the diversity in the last Modern Grand Prix, the Modern format should see some interesting new evolutions this weekend. Let's take a closer look at the remaining (legal) decks that were seen at the last event, and we'll also take a look at one deck that has recently been discussed as a potentially new option for the format.

Eric Froehlich and other players from his team sported some unique evolutions to the typical Jund deck with their choice from the last Grand Prix, known as [Ajani Goldmane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ajani%2BGoldmane)maw. This deck sports the familiar powerhouses of Jund, such as [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), and [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), but also includes a higher curve with [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) and the white mana to support [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and – more notably – [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant).


The red-white planeswalker did some real work in GP San Diego, locking down key permanents, threatening opponents from another non-creature angle, and sometimes just aiming a loyalty-charged [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) to the dome. The planeswalker proved itself to be a versatile and powerful new addition to Jund, and it wouldn't be surprising to see more of [Ajani Goldmane](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ajani%2BGoldmane) in the near future.


With its collection of very powerful, pro-active spells, the Jund, Junk, and other variations on these base archetypes still remain major contenders in Modern.

Not to be outdone by Jund's dominance in Modern, Sammy Tukeman was blasting [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman)s away all weekend with his white-blue-red control deck, which the San Diego native piloted all the way to the finals in March. The deck includes counterspells, [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and an assortment of burn spells such as [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and the super-efficient [Electrolyze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrolyze) which it can use to not only deal with planeswalkers, but also players as the game gets longer.


Did the deck pick up anything new in *Dragon's Maze*? Possibly, although it's difficult to be sure. [Renounce the Guilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) offers the deck a new answer to things like [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), while also being good against planeswalkers like [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant) and [Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade). However, the deck also gained new troubles, with [Notion Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Notion+Thief) being a potentially powerful sideboard option if a new deck is able to take advantage of blue-black creature this weekend.

With one combo deck making a potential exit from Modern due to the new Banned & Restricted list updates, players cannot forget about the other major threat still on the loose: [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift). David Sharfman has been playing the [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) combo deck for a while now, and has seen success with the archetype, most recently his Top 4 finish at GP San Diego. The deck aims to assemble a critical mass of lands before casting its namesake card, [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), to find a [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle) or two and a grip of Mountains. The resulting "eruption" from [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) triggers is usually enough to end the game.


While *Dragon's Maze* introduced some new cards for the color combination that [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) supports, it's hard to say whether they will be included. While [Ral Zarek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ral+Zarek) can ramp up a player's mana with its +1 ability, [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) seeks to add that extra mana into play with land searching, and not untapping trickery. We'll see in time whether anything new is adopted into the archetype.

Never a deck to be forgotten, Robots has had a way of reminding players far and wide that artifact creatures and [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) are still, in fact, a combination that can beat players quickly. Who knew?


The deck is simple in its plan, but powerful in its execution. Play artifact creatures, use powerful effects that work better with lots of artifact, and beat the opponent down. There are variations in how this is done, ranging from Steel Overseer beats with [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)s and [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter)s, [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) fueled attacks, or even the occasional "surprise, you're infected!' kills that result from an [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) and an equipped [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating).


With the combination of synergistic creatures, one of the most powerful aggressive cards of the format with [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating), and a whopping eight lands that can become artifact creatures, Robots attacks from a variety of angles and is never a deck that players should forget about when preparing for a Modern tournament.

Despite some of the elephants in the room, such as Jund, Robots, and [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), there is still room to build new decks based on what the expected metagame will look like. Hall of Famer Brian Kibler proved this to be a powerful plan, where he piloted his [Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) Naya deck to a Top 8 at Grand Prix San Diego two months ago. Domri in particular is a monster against opposing creature decks, giving Kibler a three-mana card advantage and removal machine, much like the role [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) serves for the Jund and Junk decks of the format. Its ultimate, by the way, is nearly unbeatable in the decks Domri is built around.


That said, designing decks to perform strongly against a certain sub-set of archetypes leaves you vulnerable in other matchups. Take, for example, Kibler's matchup against Holiday's Eggs deck, where Kibler performed a real-life "F6", referencing the feature in **Magic Online**, by going to the restroom while his opponent combo'd off. While losses against combos won't necessarily take that long with the departure of Eggs, it doesn't change the fact that Kibler's Naya deck has a weaker matchup against combos than many.


That said, the green-white base archetypes also gained new tools in *Dragon's Maze*. Will [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) be a creature that makes its way into Kibler's archetype, or will the archetype itself simply not be seen this weekend?

Round 4 Feature Match - Gerry Thompson vs. Carlos Pal
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin*

 

Pro Tour *Gatecrash* Top 8 alum Gerry Thompson came into this weekend with 38 Pro Points for the season.


While Thompson would need to put up an exceptionally good performance at Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* in order to secure an invitation to the World Championship – Thompson currently has his eyes set on an attainable (but still difficult) goal. Acquiring the 7 additional Pro Points that he needs to lock up Platinum level in the Pro Player's Club for next year.


Thompson is guaranteed 3 Pro Points just for showing up at Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* – but he still needs to pick up 4 additional points between this event, and the Pro Tour next weekend to propel himself to the highest level in the Pro Player's Club.


Thompson's opponent this round, Costa Rican **Magic Online** enthusiast Carlos Pal, is yet to achieve the Gold level in the Pro Player's Club that he would need to guarantee himself invitations to every Pro Tour. But given Pal's proficiency for winning Pro Tour Qualifiers (both in his home country, and on **Magic Online**), and the fact that he's now working closely with the increasingly successful Team ManaDeprived – it shouldn't be long before Pal becomes a Pro Tour mainstay.


### Game One


Thompson won the roll and played a [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) on his first turn – while Pal spent his first turn playing an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) taking an [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map), and leaving Thompson with [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter). [Azorius Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet), [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven), [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), and an [Urza's Power Plant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant).



![Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Celestial+Colonnade)![Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Unburial+Rites)

 

Had Thompson solely been interested in assembling the trifecta of Urza's lands as quickly as possible - which is what most Tron decks aim to do - then he could have played [Urza's Power Plant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant), plus [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) on the first turn. However, Thompson was actually interested in maximizing his ability to play [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) on turn three. (After the match, Thompson explained that he thought that Pal had made a mistake by taking the [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) instead of the [Azorius Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet) with [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek))


Pal got on the board with a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) – but a [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) for [Academy Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins), and [Crucible of Worlds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crucible+of+Worlds) (which Thompson was allowed to put into his hand) plus [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map), and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) put Thompson in a good spot.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gppor13/Gerry%20Thompson.JPG)*Gerry Thompson needs a good finish this weekend and/or next weekend to ascend to the Platinum level in the Pro Player's Club*
 

[Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), and [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) didn't do much to increase Pal's clock, or to disrupt Thompson – a fact that was made extremely clear when a second [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) gave Thompson all of the pieces that he needed to set up a [Mindslaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) lock.


A [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), taking a [Mindslaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver), gave Pal a short window to put Thompson away – however a [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) kept things just out of reach, allowing Thompson to set up his [Mindslaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver)/[Academy Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins) lock.


Gerry Thompson 1 – Carlos Pal 0


### Game Two

Modern is a constantly evolving format where players have access to very good mana. Consequently, players are able to tune their decks to take advantage of the strongest synergies that they can think of (across any of the five colors) without worrying too much about whether or not they will be able to find the colored mana they need for their spells.



![Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lightning+Angel)

 

A few months ago, Jund and Blue White Flash were two of the most popular decks in Modern. Flash forward to today, and the top performing Green Black decks have largely abandoned red in favor of white – while the successful Blue White control decks from today all have fairly heavy red splashes.


So if you're building a Modern deck, and you think of a card that you really want to have access to... only it isn't in your colors... then you should take a step back and figure out exactly what it would take for you to splash it. Odds are, you can find a way to make it work.
