Esper Control at StarCityGames Open
===================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on December 10, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






Whenever a new expansion is released and the environment changes, naturally the popular decks change, and that trend is even more pronounced when it's a new block. After all, when there's a rotation we lose about a third of the Standard card pool.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion)

With the exit of the second *Ravnica* block and the loss of all the strong Azorius cards, like [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), I figured it would be a while before we saw another White-Blue Control deck arise. But it turns out that day came sooner than anyone might have anticipated. While there was a version of it in the *Theros* block, Shaheen Soorani's Esper Control deck is pretty much the same as existed a year ago.



Twelve [scry lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&text=+%5bscry%5d&type=+%5b), with a Planeswalker as the finisher.



Counters, removal, hand destruction.



The concept is the same, even though "[Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)" now costs one more mana and can be countered, and even though "[Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)" only draws two cards.



Defend, reset, and then use draw spells to cast [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion).



The deck loses good—perhaps too good—ways to gain life; in compensation, it gains [Sorin, Solemn Visitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Solemn+Visitor). This is the only unknown. It looks like nothing more than a straight-up combo piece for Elspeth, but time will tell. If nothing else, at the end of the day it's still a Planeswalker.
