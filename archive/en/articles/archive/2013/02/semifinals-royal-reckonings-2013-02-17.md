Semifinals: Royal Reckonings
============================



 Posted in [ARTICLES](/en/articles)
 on February 16, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 





"I'm not taking this off until Jon pulls it off me with his own hands," Tom Martell said of the scarf he had worn through his improbable semifinals victory over the favored Melissa DeTora.


The scarf, naturally, belongs to Jonny *Magic* himself, Jon Finkel. And in winning what even Martell thought was an unwinnable matchup against [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) [Bant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant), surely something a little special had to be on Tom Martell's side. Why not a scarf imbued with the essence of possibly the greatest *Magic* player of all time?


Eric Froehlich had no such totem. What he did have was [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter), the 4/4 whose little-noticed discard clause helped run over Owen Turtenwald and his [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)s in the quarterfinals. His bevy of beasts were tough to handle for opponents all weekend and even tougher on their life totals. But against Martell's bevy of sacrificial [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler)s and [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens, they might have a hard time breaking through.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptgtc13/sf_froehlich_martell.jpg)  


### Game 1


Martell, on the play, hit the ground running early with [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) and [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat). But, as would be the theme of this match, Froehlich quickly outclassed Martell's creatures in size with a [Burning-Tree Emissary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) and [Flinthoof Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) on turn two.


Martell's response to Froehlich's beef wasn't bad. [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) entered the battlefield and paired with [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) to form a formidable blocking pair.


But while [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) was the old hotness in the world of three-drops, [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) was certainly the new hotness, and Froehlich cast two copies of the *Gatecrash* rare on turns three and four. The board became even more clogged when Martell added his fourth creature with another (unpaired) copy of [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin).


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptgtc13/sf_froehlich2.jpg)  


Martell had almost no good way to deal with the Reckoners through combat and only a very few ways to kill them outright, making Froehlich's next attack with both 3/3s tough to parse. All Martell could do at this juncture was stall for a little bit of time, but when he failed to find a fourth land and found himself unable to interact with the [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)s, he conceded and reached for his sideboard.


The sideboard did offer help with the Reckoner problem, supplying additional removal spells in the form of [Tragic Slip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip), plus a few gems that could help turn the tide in subsequent games.


Froehlich 1, Martell 0


### Game 2


Both players started on six cards for the second game.


Martell drew a [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) a turn too late, casting it on turn two before upgrading it to a 2/2 on turn three with a [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat).


The pair of 2/2s were quickly outclassed by a [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage) and a [Flinthoof Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar), though both were dispatched immediately with an [Orzhov Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Charm) and a now morbid [Tragic Slip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip).


Losing the Sage was a blow to Froehlich, who was now missing his third land with a hand full of three drops. The only play he could muster was a [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) on [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) as he fell to 4 life.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptgtc13/sf_martell2.jpg)  


He did finally get a [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) into play, but some tricks with an [Orzhov Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Charm), returning [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), and a morbid [Tragic Slip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip) let Martell swing for the win.


Froehlich 1, Martell 1


### Game 3


Just like last match, Martell was beaten with relative ease in the first game. Just like last match, he came back to win the second game on the back of his opponent's mana screw. And just like last match, he was still wearing Jon Finkel's scarf.


Martell led with [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) into [Skirsdag High Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest), but was quickly under the gun as a pair of [Flinthoof Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar)s crashed in on Froehlich's third turn, already putting Martell to just 12 life.


That 12 became 10 with a [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry), but Martell's third turn was a good one. [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) and [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) joined his board, giving him a bevy of options plus an easy way to make a 5/5 demon token immediately. Suddenly, Froehlich's formerly formidable attack force looked positively flaccid by comparison.


[Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) was still small by Demon standards, but it gave Froehlich incentive to attack. A 5/5 Demon jumped into play and [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat)s blocked a boar, letting Martell win combat, but he dropped to just 4 life in the process. He was well ahead on the table, but well behind on life totals.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptgtc13/sf_martell1.jpg)  
*Martell's [Skirsdag High Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest) goes to work, summoning monstrous demons.* 

And, it turned out, ahead on options. Froehlich had drawn poorly since his opener and had only lands in hand. While he could cagily hold them in hand to make Martell think, there was nothing he could do to stop himself from falling behind if Martell played his cards right.


When Jon's [Scar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scar)f...er, I mean, when Martell did in fact play his cards right, Martell took the lead in the best of three match.


Froehlich 1, Martell 2


### Game 4


Froehlich's hand had plenty of lands this time around, plus an excellent assortment of creatures. And by creatures I mean [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)s. That might be important.


Martell began with a [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), but had no follow-up. It was a pretty weak start that looked even weaker when Froehlich curved a [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage) into [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner).


Martell had his own copy of the 3/3 Reckoner, but a tech [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) kept it sitting idly off to the side while Froehlich swung right at Martell's face.


His response? [Clear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clear)ly, a second [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner).



![Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Boros%20Reckoner&options=)

Meanwhile, Froehlich had a hand full of action that gave him plenty of possibilities. Before casting anything, he chose to attack his [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) into Martell's with both players having access to white mana.


Martell, unsure of what to make of the attack, simply chump blocked with his [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish). That, however, turned on [Tragic Slip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip) and let Martell kill the follow-up [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), preventing [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage) from evolving.


After Martell passed with no play, Froehlich finally upgraded his [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage) to a 3/4 and attacked to trade off [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)s. That, again, enabled a [Tragic Slip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip) that let Martell deal with [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage).


But things just kept getting worse for Martell. Froehlich's third [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) was quickly joined by [Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade), and from that point, Martell's smaller creatures just couldn't match up.


Froehlich 2, Martell 2


### Game 5


Another mulligan for Martell was greeted by a reluctant keep by Froehlich. He had plenty of gas, but saw only two lands and no two drop creatures, plus an [Aurelia, the Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurelia%2C+the+Warleader) that wouldn't be cast any time soon. It was a risk, but any third mana source would be gas.


Plus, Martell was now going to just five cards.


"Oh *Magic*," Martell sighed, still wrapped in the scarf he had hoped would bring him luck.


Froehlich, however, looked to have all the luck. While he didn't draw any two drops, he did immediately find his third land. He just didn't cast anything until turn three, and even then it was a [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) on [Skirsdag High Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest). Froehlich had clearly learned he could never let the 1/2 live.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptgtc13/sf_froehlich1.jpg)  
*Froehlic has only a few forms of removal, but he knows what cards need to point them at.* 

And it was a good thing he did, as Martell had both [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) and [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) to follow up, as well as a [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) to make combat pretty miserably complicated for both players.


And, of course, Froehlich had his own Reckoner as well. As you do.


But what he also had was a [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage) and [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter), giving him the real possibility of upgrading his whole team with [Aurelia, the Warleader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aurelia%2C+the+Warleader) the next turn.


And, while this has gotten repetitive, we should note that Martell played a second [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). Again, as you do.


But the turning point of the game came on Froehlich's next turn when he drew the land for Aurelia. As Martell looked on and arched his eyebrows, acknowledging the Boros guild leader's power, Froehlich threw caution to the win and attacked with everything: Aurelia, [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), [Gyre Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gyre+Sage) and [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter).


The combat was complicated, to say the least. Martell had two [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)s of his own, and two combat phases meant intricate and interesting blocking scenarios.


When the dust cleared, Martell fell to 8, Froehlich lost his Reckoner, and Martell was reduced to two Spirit Tokens, a [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) and a [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat). It looked like it was pretty clearly the end for Tom Martell and Jon Finkel's scarf.


Never underestimate the power of Jon Finkel's scarf.


Untapping, with Froehlich seemingly safe on 14 life, Martell announced...[Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act). With Froehlich's own [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) out of the way, Martell could safely cast the sweeping sorcery and pave the way to victory. Giving his [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) protection from Red, Martell cleared the board, sent 13 damage from [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) to Froehlich's face, and attacked for the last few points.




![Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Blasphemous%20Act&options=)

  
*Never underestimate 13 damage to the face.* 

And seriously. Never underestimate Jon Finkel's [Scar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scar)f.


Tom Martell wins 3-2 and advances to the finals!
