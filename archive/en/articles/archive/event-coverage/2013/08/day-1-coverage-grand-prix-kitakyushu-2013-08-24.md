Day 1 Coverage of Grand Prix Kitakyushu
=======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 24, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_authorpic_larger.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast












Outside may be gray and rainy, but that hasn't stopped 1183 players from descending on Kitakyushu's West Japan General Exhibition Center for a weekend of close-quarter spell combat. The show is being put on by Shop-Fireball, owned by debonair archmage, part-time cowboy, and Hall of Fame hopeful Tsuyoshi Ikeda. All his preparation and hard work have paid off, with attendance far exceeding expectations, but he'll be on the sidelines this weekend. So much the better for those legends who are competing, including Hall of Famers Shuuhei Nakamura and Yuuya Watanabe, as well as former World Champion Makihito Mihara and former Player of the Year Shouta Yasooka.


As we wind down Standard heading into the big rotation, it's hard to be sure of anything. After the release of *M14* it seemed like the pros had agreed that Standard was solved. Jund was top dog, followed by Blue-White-Red control and Bant Hexproof. Success would come from tweaks within that framework and an accurate metagame prediction. Then Brian Kibler showed up with his take on Gruul and conventional wisdom was blown to smithereens. Nor was it a flash in the pan; Wenzel Krautmann steered it to a Grand Prix championship at Warsaw. Will its influence extend to the Japanese metagame? Will we see another unexpected breakout, like Big Green-Black, or Sacrificial Humans? Who will hoist the trophy Sunday night? Stay tuned this weekend to find out!

Saturday, 3:15 p.m. — Ooze Next: Deckbuilder Roundtable
-------------------------------------------------------


*by Ben Swartz*

 

With *Theros* on the horizon, Grand Prix Kitakyushu is the final Standard Grand Prix before *Innistrad* block and *M13* rotate out. With just over a month before *Theros* hits shelves, players are scrambling to find the best strategies in Standard. I got the chance to sit down with some of Japan's premier deckbuilders to get their thoughts on the current Standard format.


There was a clear consensus between the players as to what would be the most popular deck this weekend: Gruul. Popularized by Brian Kibler at the World Championships this year, the deck won Grand Prix Warsaw two weeks ago in the hands of Wenzel Krautmann. Armed with powerful creatures such as [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite), [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider), and [Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager), and backed up by [Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) to find reinforcements, the Red-Green deck is a force to be reckoned with. With a target on its back, nearly each one of the players cited [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) as the most important card in the deck.



![Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Scavenging+Ooze)

 

Pro Tour Kobe Champion, Masahiro Kuroda mentioned that while [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze)'s ability to remove cards from graveyards is certainly powerful, it is not enough to do away with the reanimator. Instead, Kuroda is piloting a reanimator deck that is powerful in the absence of a resolved [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). [Armed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armed) with powerful anti-aggressive creatures such as [Boros Reckoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) and haymakers like [Elderscale Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elderscale+Wurm), Kuroda welcomes the opportunity to face off against Gruul.


Former World Champion, Makihito Mihara, on the other hand, felt that the best course of action against [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) is to just ignore it. The advantage from Ooze comes from the ability to hamper graveyard-based strategies and grow to a size where [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) is unable to destroy it, Mihara explained. By playing a deck that is only marginally affected by [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze)'s activated ability and one with access to blanket removal cards such as [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) and [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), the Ooze's power can be contained.


Grand Prix Shizuoka and Grand Prix Kobe Champion, Yuuta Takahashi, is no stranger to pesky green creatures. Being a master of the *Lorwyn*-block-based Faeries deck, he understands that being able to side step and jump around powerful utility creatures is the most powerful vector of attack. His advice: use cards like [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) and [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) proactively to dispose of [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) and Gruul's other powerful creatures.


Tomoharu Saito was the only player that did not see [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) as a big problem. He felt that, well good, it is far from format defining. [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) and [Burning Earth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Earth), he mentioned, have a much bigger impact on the format. Along with using powerful removal spells such as [Blasphemous Act](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) and [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars), he suggested that players be more consistent and more aggressive than Gruul.


Players that have decided not to sleeve up red and green cards this weekend have come prepared to face off against raw power of Gruul and the versatility that [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) provides.

Round 5 Feature Match — Yuuta Takahashi vs. Hiroki Toyota
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Ben Swartz*

 

In Japan, Yuuta Takahashi is known far and wide as the Faeries Master. In 2008 he won back-to-back Japanese Grand Prix with the Blue-Black menace. It is a title that has followed him to this day. While Faeries is not legal in the current Standard format, Yuuta did his best to emulate his old aggro-control standby; Yuuta brought a Blue-White [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) deck that was reminiscent of those seen in Standard last summer. While many of the cards from that deck have rotated, Yuuta did his best to emulate them; in place of [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag), he used [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon); in place of [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), he used [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter).


His opponent, Hiroki Toyota, had his work cut out for him. The relative unknown from Okayama came prepared with an interesting Red-Black concoction. Featuring premium creatures such as [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot), [Lifebane Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie), and Thundemaw Hellkite and backed up by premium spells such as Liliana of the Veil and [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned), he hoped to burn the Faeries Master to a crisp.


Both players had earned their way to an undefeated record in Round Five: Yuuta Takahashi with three byes and Hiroki Toyota with two.


### The Games


Yuuta started things off with three [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s, each cast at the end of his turn. They padded his graveyard with powerful spells and allowed him to find and cast a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft).


The legendary hexproof creature was not long for this world, however, as Hiroki slammed down a [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and used her -2 ability to clear Yuuta's Board.


At what Yuuta would mention to be a crucial turning point in the game, Hiroki attempted to cast a [Lifebane Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie); Yuuta thought for a while and cast an [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) on it. After the match he mentioned that had he instead just cast his [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) he would have been able to take out the Liliana before it became too much of a problem.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/Yuuta_TakahashiR5.jpg)  
*Yuuta Takahashi* 
And that's exactly what Lilliana did. While it ticked up, Hiroki continued casting creatures. A [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) ate another [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), but Lilliana's discard ability proved to be too much as Hiroki's [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) resolved and attacked in.


It was at this point that Yuuta finally had an opening to play his [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), he untapped with it and took out Lilliana, but the threat of the Aristocrat still loomed.


With Yuuta looking to race the Falkerath Aristocrat with newly minted [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), it seemed like he was back on track.


But, in a flash, Hiroki turned over the top card of his library to reveal [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned). With one more land than Yuuta's life total, Hiroki burned the two-time Grand Prix Champion out of the game.


The two players remained quiet between the two games; Yuuta was clearly dejected that the game had ended the way it did.


Yuuta let out a sigh of relief when he looked at his opening hand. Finally he had not one, but two of his deck's namesake card: [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets).


Neither of them was able to transform, even with Yuuta using [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) to effectively get a fresh card for the second [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets). With Yuuta stuttering, Hiroki found time to hard cast a [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) for one to take out both flimsy [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)s.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/Hiroki_ToyotaR5.jpg)  
*Hiroki Toyota* 
Hiroki followed the Bonfire up with a pair of [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot)s and a [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat). Yuuta had multiple [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)s and a couple [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s to flash them back and keep Hiroki's board clean for a couple of turns, but nothing to permanently remove the creatures when they made their way back into play. After a few more attack steps Hiroki Toyota navigated his way to 5-0.


**Hiroki Toyota 2 – Yuuta Takahashi 0**

Round 7 Feature Match — Tzu-Ching Kuo (Bant Hexproof) vs. Kentarou Yamamoto (Big Black-Green)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Josh Bennett*

 

### The Players


Tzu-Ching Kuo is best known at the man who risked it all to become the Captain of Team Chinese Taipei, and then led them to victory in the 2012 World **Magic** Cup. He's been playing professionally for over a decade, and has a string of Grand Prix Top 8's extending back that far.


Kentarou Yamamoto has only a single GP Top 8 to his name, but he has one accolade that has thus far eluded Kuo: A Pro Tour Finals appearance. Yamamoto and teammate Yuuta Takahashi fought their way to Sunday at Pro Tour San Diego 2007, but ultimately fell to The Sliver Kids, Chris Lachmann and Jacob Van Lunen.


Both Kuo and Yamamoto have started the event 6-0 and are looking to close Day 1 strong to set up a Top 8 run.


### The Matchup


On paper it looks dismal for Bant Hexproof. Big Black-Green has a lot of tools that don't care about the namesake ability, such as [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate). After sideboarding they can ditch all their targetted removal and bring in even more weapons.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/d1_R7yamamotoVSkuo.jpg)
### The Games


Game one started with both players mulliganing. Kuo got early pressure of [Gladecover Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gladecover+Scout) with [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and added [Fiendslayer Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiendslayer+Paladin) to the table. Yamamoto brought down a giant stop sign named [Desecration Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon). Kuo made no play and Yamamoto summoned a second Demon. It looked like it would be over in a flash.


Kuo had been holding Etherial Armor and found a second on top of his deck. Suddenly his Fiendslayer was 8/8. He got in one hit, going back up to 20 life, and then the two stalemated. Yamamoto had a 3/3 [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) go to with his two Demons, keeping the Fiendslayer home. Kuo didn't have to wait long to find an [Unflinching Courage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage), and soon after he took the game.


Kuo 1 - Yamamoto 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/d1_R7yamamoto.jpg)  
*Kentarou Yamamoto* 
Again Yamamoto couldn't keep his opening seven. He stood on six. Kuo had the gangbusters open of [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) into Geist of St. Traft, but didn't have an enchantment to push it past a [Lifebane Zombie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie) from Yamamoto. Again Yamamoto had the double-Demon follow-up, and without a giant lifelinker on the other side of the field it was enough.


Kuo 1 - Yamamoto 1


Yamamoto mulliganed to six and watched as Kuo played out [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) into [Fiendslayer Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiendslayer+Paladin). He [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ed on the second turn and stole [Increasing Savagery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Savagery), leaving Kuo with just [Spell Rupture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Rupture), a second Fiendslayer, and land. Disaster averted.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/d1_R7kuo.jpg)  
*Tzu-Ching Kuo* 
Or so he thought. Kuo slowly slid off his top card, then flipped over a second [Increasing Savagery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Savagery), bashing in for seven points of lifelink damage. He even had the Pilgrim for Liliana insurance. From there it was a quick and brutal affair, as most Hexproof wins are.


**Tzu-Ching Kuo defeats Kentarou Yamamoto 2-1**


### The Postgame


I asked Kuo about the matchup and he smiled, admitting he'd dodged a bullet. In fact, he chose this deck specifically because he felt it was good against just about everything EXCEPT Big Black-Green. Since the match had turned on the Savagery topdeck, I asked him what his chances in the game would have been if he'd drawn a land instead that turn. He said that it would be very difficult, but not impossible.


Of course Yamamoto wasn't that sunny, having just picked up his first loss, but was keen to talk about his deck. He likes it for the current metagame, citing strong matchups against both Jund and Hexproof, and a "so-so" matchup against Kibler Gruul. I asked him if he thought the deck would survive rotation, and he said he couldn't say for sure.

I'm pretty sure I saw a single tear drip down his cheek as he tapped [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground)s to cast [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) on [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist). Round 9 Feature Match — Taiji Urasawa vs. Takahito Kobayashi
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ben Swartz*

 

It's a funny thing matching a name to a face. When the Japanese Coverage team saw the pairings for this round Tomohiro Kaji immediately circled the first table and underlined Takahito Kobayashi.


"Who is that?" another reporter asked.


"KBR," Kaji said softly.


The look on the reporter's face immediately changed to one of shock and awe.


Takahito Kobayashi, or kbr3 as he is known on **Magic Online** is famous in Japan for being one of the most dominant **Magic Online** PTQ players. After having won two **Magic Online** PTQs last year, he recently, he won a Pro Tour Qualifier for upcoming Pro Tour *Theros* in Dublin.


He hasn't had very much success in paper **Magic**, but sitting at 8-0 with his Red Green Black Zombie deck, he was hoping to change that.


His opponent, Taiji Urasawa, a local player from Osaka was playing the deck with a target on its back: Gruul. It's treated him well thus far, but will it be good enough to defeat the **Magic Online** Star?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/d1_R9UrasawaVSKobayashi.jpg)
### The Games


Game one started with creatures on both sides: a [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) and Loleth Troll for Kobayashi and a [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) for Urasawa.


On Urasawa's third turn he cast a [Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) and used it to fight his [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) against Kobayashi's Loleth Troll, destroying it before Kobayashi got a chance to untap with regeneration mana.


Kobayashi took this opening to take out Urasawa's [Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) with his Cackler and proceeded to add a [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) to his board.


Kobayashi played a [Dreg Mangler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) and attacked for 10 dropping Urasawa to 9, but Urasawa had a [Ghor-Clan Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) up his sleeve. That, paired with another [Flinthoof Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) took out the **Magic Online** player.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/d1_R9Urasawa.jpg)  
*Taiji Urasawa* 
The second game began with a turn two Loleth Troll for Kobayashi, which prompted a [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) from Urasawa. Kobayashi shrugged and discarded four creatures to his troll to prevent it from dying.


On his following turn, he untapped and drew, shrugged again, and dumped two more creatures into the graveyard, making the Loleth Troll an 8/8. Urasawa fell to 10, and Kobayashi picked up one of the discarded creatures, [Gleancrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gleancrawler), from his graveyard and put it back into play.


Urasawa attempted to stem the bleeding by playing [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) and a [Flinthoof Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar), but Trample from the gigantic troll was too much for him to deal with sending the match to a third and final game.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkit13/d1_R9Kobayashi.jpg)  
*Takahito Kobayashi* 
For the final game, Urasawa started things of with a [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), and then played a [Domri Rade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) to take out Kobayashi's turn two [Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy). Kobayashi did not have a haste creature, so Domri was allowed to live to see another day and netted Urasawa another [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist)


With Kobayashi already down to 10 life his back was against the wall to deal with both geists and an active planeswalker.


A [Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy), a [Dreadbore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore) to take out the planeswalker, and a pair of [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s to remove one of the [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist)s gave Kobayashi a foothold in the game. Kobayashi then cast a pair of [Diregraf Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul)s and slowly started to chip away at Urasawa's life total.


A [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) off the top dropped Kobayashi to four. Kobayashi played defense for a turn and braced for impact.


Urasawa looked at his newly drawn card, counted his lands, and overloaded [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars). With Kobayashi's board clear, [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) attacked in for the final four points of damage and advanced Urasawa to day two with an undefeated record.


**Taiji Urasawa 2 – Takahito Kobayashi 1**
