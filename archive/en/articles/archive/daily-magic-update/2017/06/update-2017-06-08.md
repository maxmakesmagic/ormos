After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [Is Black-Green Delirium outdated? Not at all.](http://www.hareruyamtg.com/article/en/category/detail/128) | Hareruya English | Lukas Blohon

Well, sure, you say that now that you won a Grand Prix last weekend, but what has it done lately?


Blohon goes deep on the deck, so if you're interested in it, everything you need is right here.


1. [*Commander Anthology* VS!](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/35256_Commander-Anthology-VS.html) | StarCityGames | Justin Parnell and Stephen Green

Want an early look at battling *Commander Anthology* right out of the box? Commander VS is on the case a day before the box set drops in stores.


1. [Adapting to a New Deck](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/35252_Adapting-To-A-New-Deck.html) | StarCityGames.com | Emma Handy

I bounce from deck to deck to deck (to deck) myself, and Handy has some useful advice for anyone looking to switch decks from one to another (to another).


1. [Improving at Your Own Pace](http://www.constructedcriticism.com/podcasts/constructed-criticism-188-improving-at-your-own-pace/) | Constructed Criticism | Spencer Howland and Michael Hinderaker

But I want to be better noooowwwww.


1. [This Week in Legacy: Ovinosping, Hareruya Legacy Challenge, and GP Vegas Preparation](https://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/this-week-in-legacy-ovinosping-hareruya-legacy-challenge-and-gp-vegas-preparation?src=wizards) | MTGGoldfish | Sean Brown

With Grand Prix Las Vegas just a week away, players are turning their attention to Legacy and Modern. With Miracles removed from the format, Legacy is still resetting its metagame, so pretty much anything can happen. A boy can dream.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Benjamin Luft's Blue-Red Control](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpams17/top-8-decklists-grand-prix-amsterdam-2017-06-04)

Luft came *thiiiiiiis* close to winning a Grand Prix last weekend with four [Torrential Gearhulk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk)s in his deck, but was halted by Lukas Blohon's Black-Green Delirium deck. Still, it's an impressive performance for this deck that has been on the rise as of late.


There were a few spicy bits that might have contributed to Luft's success. One is the main deck [Sphinx of the Final Word](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Final+Word), which gives the deck a leg up in other control matchups. The other is a single [Summary Dismissal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summary+Dismissal), which is, of course, excellent against [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger).


The rest of the deck is prototypical control deck stuff—cheap removal, counterspells, and card draw. Basically, all the good stuff.
