### GRAND PRIX STOCKHOLM 2014


DECK TECH: SIDISI-WHIP WITH THORALF SEVERIN
===========================================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)

Frank Karsten




October 25, 2014
 











 "The best card in the deck is [Hornet Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen), and it works best with [Whip of Erebos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) ... these are the two cards that make the deck," Thoralf Severin explained when the video coverage team recorded a deck tech with him at the beginning of the day.



Currently sitting at a 5-0 record, here is his decklist:

 The list is very close to Christian Seibold's deck from Pro Tour *Khans of Tarkir* and Eugene Hwang's deck from Grand Prix Los Angeles. If you if you want to learn more, you can find Seibold's deck tech [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m5j79EGUtS8), and Hwang's deck tech [here](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpla14/decktechsidisi). However, Severin has made a few small tweaks for this weekend.




 First, Severin has 1 [Sagu Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sagu+Mauler) in the maindeck. "The U/B Control list from Ivan Floch cannot beat it, except for double-blocking with [Prognostic Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prognostic+Sphinx)," Severin explained.




 Second, Severin has 4 [Drown in Sorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drown+in+Sorrow) in the sideboard. When asked about why he included the sweeper, he said that he expected more mono-red this weekend and needed a way to beat it.





*Thoralf Severin*
