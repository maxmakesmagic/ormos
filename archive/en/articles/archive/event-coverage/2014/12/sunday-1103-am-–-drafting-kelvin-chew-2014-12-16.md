Sunday, 11:03 a.m. – Drafting with Kelvin Chew
==============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on December 16, 2014 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170709072651im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel Neo










Kelvin Chew made his mark with a top 8 at Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica*, but has had a bit of a dry spell since then. Here at Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur, he is sitting on 8-1, very nicely placed for a run at the top 8. How would he fare?


Kelvin was passed a clear signal UG was open in pack 1. However, this also meant he had to pass some strong blue cards himself. This would come back to haunt him in his second pack.


Below lists the key cards Kelvin considered in each pack, with his picks depicted as the leftmost card.

By the end of the first booster, Kelvin had solidified his core colour as green, with a few strong blue cards as well as a smattering of red picks. He values [Nessian Courser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nessian+Courser)'s efficient costing more than [Nylea's Disciple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nylea%27s+Disciple) life gain.


In pack 2, blue was less forthcoming and his draft strategy somewhat derailed.

Kelvin did pick up a blue game ender in [Horizon Scholar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Scholar) and some mana ramp, but the second booster was otherwise unexciting for him.


He picked [Fabled Hero](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabled+Hero) over [Staunch-Hearted Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staunch-Hearted+Warrior) because he was still entertaining the possibility of switching secondary colours and he valued ramp over evasive creatures in his fourth pick.


The trio of colour fixers he picked up in the second booster also meant he was more open to possible splashes in pack 3.

Reflecting on the draft, Kelvin lamented passing [Hammer of Purphoros](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammer+of+Purphoros) in his booster one first pick. The draft would have gone very differently if he had – while red is widely viewed as underpowered in *Theros* draft, this also means players are prejudiced to the colour and it turned out under drafted. Some very strong red cards had tabled, including a pair of [Kragma Warcaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kragma+Warcaller) that would have been absolutely brutal in a minotaur deck.


**Deckbuilding**


The draft's done and dusted, however, and Kelvin was left with solid green, some powerful blue and white spells, a possible black splash for [Pharika's Mender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pharika%27s+Mender) and a smattering of red.


He chose to build his deck around a UG evasion core enhanced by a pair of powerful white creatures. He runs [Temple of Abandon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Abandon) simply for the scry ability and cut [Thassa's Bounty](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%27s+Bounty) as he already has sufficient late game spells.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/gpkl14/dr_kelvin.jpg)

For this strategy to work, his opponents need to be unable to close the game quickly so Kelvin's large evasion creatures can take chucks out of their life totals. He is possibly able to race with the help of [Hopeful Eidolon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hopeful+Eidolon).
