Round 2: "I've Felt More Awkward Before"
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 23, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_57.png)
By Dane Young 











Osyp Lebedowicz and Ruud Warmenhoven are familiar with each other. So familiar, in fact, that Osyp wasted no time in regaling the audience with a classic tale.

![Ruud Warmenhoven and Osyp Lebedowicz go way back.](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/pthol08/R1_Lebedowicz_Warmenhoven.jpg)*Ruud Warmenhoven and Osyp Lebedowicz go way back.*"One time I went out to a nightclub with Ruud. He says, 'American girls are so easy to pick up compared to Dutch girls.' So I'm like, 'Oh really?' And he says, 'Yeah, I have the perfect line. Wait for it.'"

"So Ruud goes up to this girl, and is doing fine without the line. She's into him, you can tell. Then he winks, and now I know it's coming."

"Have you ever made out with a guy with a tongue ring?"

"No."

"Would you like to?"

"No."

Ruud won the coin flip as both players shuffled. There was some confusion between decks as they both have the same **Magic: The Gathering** sleeves.

"I'm sure this is your deck."

Shrug.

They both laid out their opening hands, and Osyp, peeking first, shouted, "I TOLD you this was your deck! You're such an idiot! At least I know what you're playing. Do you want to switch?"

After another round of shuffling, both players again kept their opening hands, this time from the correct decks.

Warmenhoven kicked off with a Pendelhaven and a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), which was answered by a Snow-Covered [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and the same Elf from his opponent.

[Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) on turn two made things a little more interesting, as Lebedowicz had a [Civic Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder) off of a Snow-Covered [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), fetching another Snow-Covered [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) before passing the turn.

Warmenhoven attacked with [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), trading with [Civic Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civic+Wayfinder). [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) plus [Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon) ended the turn for Warmenhoven with a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) untapped.

[Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) came into play tapped, along with a second Wayfinder for Lebedowicz getting another snowy [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp). Warmenhoven's Perfect made a 3/3 trampler on end step.

Warmenhoven played a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and activated his [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), sending it, the Paragon and the Elf Warrior token into the red zone and connecting for 9.

Lebedowicz's fourth turn was a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) into [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic) and [Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher), discounted by revealing [Nameless Inversion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion). Warmenhoven shrugged and made another token.

Warmenhoven activated [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) again before sending everyone but the Perfect, threatening a lethal 12 damage. [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic) blocked the token, [Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher) blocked [Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon), allowing 6 damage through. Warmenhoven made a token with damage on the stack to get one last use out of his Paragon's +1/+1 counters.

Lebedowicz, hanging on at 5 life, taps out for [Profane Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profane+Command), giving the [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) -2/-2 and retrieving his [Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher) from the bin. Warmenhoven had a second [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) but passed the turn, as he had no good attacks.

Lebedowicz goes to 3 for [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), and Warmenhoven answered back with a lethal [Squall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall) Line.

**Warmenhoven 1, Lebedowicz 0**

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/pthol08/R1_Warmenhoven.jpg)*Ruud gets by in a pinch.*Lebedowicz led off the second game the same way he did the first: [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves). Warmenhoven's [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) allowed the Elf to sneak a point in, followed by a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and a pair of Boreal Druids.

Warmenhoven's had a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and a [Bramblewood Paragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblewood+Paragon) for turn two, but Lebedowicz trumped with a [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus). [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) set up the defenses for Warmenhoven, who went to 15 from the Colossus on Lebedowicz's turn. After combat, Lebedowicz played a [Gilt-Leaf Palace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilt-Leaf+Palace), revealing [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus), and tapped out to play the revealed Colossus along with a 0/1 Tarmogoyf.

Warmenhoven needed an answer to the opposing monsters, and he came up with a [Wren's Run Vanquisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wren%27s+Run+Vanquisher), revealing a second Perfect. He sent the turn back to Lebedowicz with a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) up.

[Primal Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command) from Lebedowicz put him up to 27 and tutored for a [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), getting a shrug from Warmenhoven as he made a token.

Warmenhoven went into the tank again, earning a stare-down from Lebedowicz. Warmenhoven emerged with a [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) and [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect), again passing with [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village) open.

The [Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) hit play for full price, killing the first [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect), which was replaced by a token. Tarmogoyf was now 2/3.

A third Perfect from Warmenhoven enabled both 4/4 tokens to crash in, taking Lebedowicz to 19. Lebedowicz flashed [Profane Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profane+Command) at the end of Warmenhoven's turn, threatening lethal with fear and damage. Warmenhoven just shrugged.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/pthol08/R1_Lebedowicz.jpg)*Osyp gets a Ruud awakening.*"I'm confused. I wonder what you have. I thought that was game..." Osyp said with a bewildered look. "MAN! I wish I knew my guys had pro black! Why couldn't you just scoop like a normal person?"

"Well, I played some games with that deck," Ruud replied.

Ruud had figured out a step ahead of Osyp that [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) couldn't reap the benefits of [Profane Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Profane+Command).

Pat Sullivan piped in from the gallery, laughing: "Osyp had that look where he thought he had it."

"I've felt more awkward before," Osyp said sheepishly.

Quick on the draw, Ruud asked, "Would you like to share that occasion with us?"

"No."

"I think we're even on the tongue ring story now."

[Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) bashed in, taking Warmenhoven to 12, but Ruud had an [Elvish Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Champion) to rumble in for approximately a billion.

**Warmenhoven 2, Lebedowicz 0**
