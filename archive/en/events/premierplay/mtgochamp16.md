General Information
-------------------


Sixteen top *Magic: The Gathering* players will play at Wizards of the Coast offices in the game’s digital platform, *Magic Online*, from March 3-5, 2017.


The event will feature Standard and *Aether Revolt/Kaladesh* draft, and has a total prize pool of $116,000. First place shall win $25,000 and earn the title of *Magic Online* Champion.

TOURNAMENT INFORMATION
----------------------



* [Overview](#-tabs-1)
* [Playoffs](#-tabs-2)

* Day One will consist of 3 Rounds of *Aether Revolt*/*Kaladesh* Booster Draft and 4 Rounds of Standard Constructed.
* Day Two will consist of 3 Rounds of *Aether Revolt*/*Kaladesh* Booster Draft and 4 Rounds of Standard Constructed.
* During the Swiss rounds, the *Magic Online* die roll will determine which player chooses whether or not to take the first turn of the first game of each match.
* During the Swiss rounds, each player will have 40 minutes of priority clock for a best-of-three-game match (80 minutes for both players combined). Three minutes of sideboarding between games will not count toward this total. Slow play will be enforced as needed, and a player who runs out of priority time loses the match, regardless of the game score or state of the current game.
* Players will use the same Standard decklist for all three days.

The top 4 competitors after Round 14 will be advanced to the single-elimination playoffs on Sunday.


In the event of a technical or administrative issue, Wizards of the Coast reserves the right to make alterations to the tournament as, in its sole discretion, it deems necessary to preserve event integrity. 




* The top 4 competitors after Round 14 will be advanced to the single-elimination playoffs on Sunday. DCI tiebreakers will be used as needed to break ties in the standings for both the cut to top 4 as well as prizes.
* Top 4 is Standard Constructed, best two-of-three matches.
* Matches can’t be drawn, intentionally or unintentionally.
* Players will use the same Standard decklist for all three days.

In the event of a technical or administrative issue, Wizards of the Coast reserves the right to make alterations to the tournament as, in its sole discretion, it deems necessary to preserve event integrity.
