Keep It Simple, Stoneforge
==========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 14, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











A week or two back, we discussed Legacy and specifically talked about [a mono-blue list that seemed pretty simple](/en/articles/archive/fishless-fish-2012-09-11) compared to most of the decks out there. The deck just wanted to play small creatures and counter a few spells en route to victory. As much as that deck was simple in a format of cool and complex things, today's list might be even more so, especially considering the list doesn't have access to [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will).

![Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Flickerwisp)Ben Nash took the following White Weenie list to a 1st-place finish in a recent StarCityGames Open in Portland, Oregon. No, you didn't read that wrong, I did in fact say White Weenie. The iconic archetype is often known as about as straightforward a deck as they come, but with enough disruption, Ben was able to punish the [Hive Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind)s, [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul)s, and [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)s of the format.

The deck plays a ton of really interesting small creatures that contain value beyond just their power and toughness. [Flickerwisp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp) is especially exciting, as that has been a card I have loved forever. Here, you can reset your [Phyrexian Revoker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Revoker) or [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) with it. You can even put the 3/1 into play off of [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), blinking [Omniscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) with it and stalling your opponent's win for a turn, allowing you precious time to come up with a plan of attack. [Æther Vial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) is the real engine of this deck, turning all of these creatures into uncounterable, instant-speed threats, even allowing your [Serra Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Avenger) to cheat into play a turn early!

Outside of the [Karaka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karaka)s and the [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s, this list is pretty friendly to anyone building on a budget, so if you have been contemplating trying out Legacy but don't want to spend a bunch, give this a shot!
