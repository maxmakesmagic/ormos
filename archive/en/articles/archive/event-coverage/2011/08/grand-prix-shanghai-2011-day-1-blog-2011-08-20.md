Saturday, 10:00 a.m. - GPT Winning Decklists
--------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim* 

Eleven Grand Prix Trials (affectionately christened Grinders) were held yesterday, rewarding each winner with three round byes for today's tournament. Red was clearly the most popular color since seven of the eleven winning decks contained fiery goodies like [Chandra's Phoenix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Phoenix), [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) and easily splashable burn spells like [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) and [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball). White, blue and black were pretty much represented in equal proportions, leaving green as the most under-appreciated of the day. Here are the sealed deck decklists for your viewing pleasure!

Saturday, 10:30 a.m. - Deckbuilding with Shuhei Nakamura
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim* 

The ballots have been tallied and the news is official. A Hall of Famer inductee is in the house today and he is none other than the legendary Shuhei Nakamura. With five Pro Tour Sunday appearances and a staggering seventeen Grand Prix Top 8s in his portfolio, it comes as no surprise that he received unparalleled unanimity during the Hall of Fame ballot.


I could go on about his achievements all day, but I shall give that a miss for now for three good reasons. Firstly, it would take more than one article to account his triumphs and secondly, you should already have heard plenty about his impressive resumé. Lastly, we're here to take a glimpse into his labyrinthian mind and discover how he tackles the brand-new ***Magic** 2012* Sealed Deck format.


Shuhei fanned through his card pool, smiling humbly and commenting it was not as good as he wanted it to be. "The colors are too separated. There is a lot of power but it's all over the place."


Despite preferring to not play with forests, he had a solidly deep green base and quickly decided it would be his core color. The two [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)s in his pool would become the cornerstone of his strategy today, and are always good with an assortment of green dudes.


Before he could even decide a second color, it seems to me that he had already determined that he was able (and needed) to play a third color, because of [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth). He also mentioned to me he would be going down to just sixteen lands today, thanks to these two mana fixers.


There were only nine black cards in his pool of eighty four, but he did not dismiss the possibility of playing with swamps. He had access to top-notch commons like [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) and [Gravedigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger), which are conveniently splashable options. He had set aside [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) as well, and I jokingly asked if he would ever play four colors in this format. He gently smiled, shook his head and joined me in a bout of hearty laughter.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha11/01_R2_Nakamura.jpg)*2011 Hall of Fame Inductee Shuhei Nakamura ponders his color choices*
Before deciding which cards to splash, the problem was deciding the second color. Between blue and red, he eventually decided on running with [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s. Saturday, 12.55 p.m. - The Impact of a Grand Prix in China
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim* 

"Since its introduction in 1993, **Magic**: the Gathering now has more than 12 million players in over 70 countries, printed in 11 different languages. I can fully understand why people call it "hobby of the world". **Magic** has had 74 sets and expansions, more than 11000 different and unique cards, which offer countless exciting possibilities and personalized customization."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha11/01_R1_Impact.jpg)*Opening Ceremony, left to right: Michael Cheng, General Manager Shanghai Ruika Trading Company; Elaine Wu, Business Development Manager Hasbro Trading China; Laura Ho, HAPM Wizards of the Coast Business Manager; Mr Xing Xiao Quan, China Sports Administration Casual Sports Center Director; Janet Chen, Hasbro Trading China Marketing Manager; Kelvin Liu, CFO Hasbro China; and HAPM Harland Chun, Vice President and General Manager.*
However, the main driving force behind the success and survival of this game aren't these staggering numbers alone. "What makes **Magic** such a strong game franchise game year after year is a highly developed and highly valued Organized Play system." commented Mr. Xing Xiao Quan, the Director of the China Sports Administration Casual Sports Center. The player community can play at Friday Night **Magic** and Prereleases if they desire a casual setting, and for those who desire more intense competition at a higher level, we have the Grand Prix Circuit, which is one of the primary ways to qualify for the Pro Tour.


At Grand Prix Beijing 2005, the world witnessed hometown hero Dong Zhong take home the Trophy, defeating a star-studded Top 8 which included Oliver Ruel, Kenji Tsumura, Tomoharu Saito, Masashi Oiso and Katsuhiro Mori. Just three years later at Pro Tour Kuala Lumpur 2008, Xu Ming made history by becoming the first Chinese player to make Top 8 of a Pro Tour, igniting hope in aspiring Chinese players and trailblazing their path to success. A testament to China's mastery is their triumph in 2009, where Zhang Zhiyang, Li Bo and Wu Tong took home the Worlds Team Champion in Rome.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha11/2009%20Worlds%20Team%20Champions.jpg)*2009 Worlds Team Champions*
Grand Prix Shanghai 2011 marks the second appearance of a Grand Prix in China, with many more to come in the future. Six years after Grand Prix Beijing 2005, the attendance has swelled fourfold, making today's event the largest **Magic** tournament in the history of China, with 631 attendees.


The Grand Prix occupies a different position in each player's hearts. For many, it is a means to getting qualified on the Pro Tour aside from the Pro Tour Qualifiers. For others, it is simply a place to make some prize money to fuel their favorite hobby or collect some Pro Points. For me, I think it's an excellent premise to meet friends, old and new. Round 6: Feature Match - Chikara Nakajima vs. Yuuya Watanabe
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw* 

When it comes to Grand Prix events held in the Asia Pacific region, these Japanese players have solid track record. Chikara Nakajima has often been seen in the top 8 of Grand Prix's, coming second in Singapore just a few months ago, while Yuuya Watanabe has been in more Grand Prix top 8's than I can count. At least not without maybe taking off my shoes.


Nakajima got started with a [Coral Merfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Merfolk), beefing it up with an [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton), prompting Watanabe to block the Merfolk with his [Alabaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alabaster+Mage). Watanabe took a few hits while he summoned a [Rusted Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rusted+Sentinel), and fetched back his Mage with a [Gravedigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger). Nakajima took the battle to the sky with an [Archon of Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archon+of+Justice), but Watanabe locked it down with a [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism). The [Chasm Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chasm+Drake) that followed didn't look nearly as fearsome.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha11/01_R6_Nakajima.jpg)*Chikara Nakajima's creatures are on lock-down.*
Watanabe was now in the driver's seat, attacking with his [Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel) and giving it lifelink with the Mage. Nakajima [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)ed and resummoned his [Archon of Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archon+of+Justice), but Watanabe used a [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) to find an [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) for it. Meanwhile, the relentless attacking [Rusted Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rusted+Sentinel), just big enough to attack past anything else Nakajima could play, brought game one to a close.


Watanabe 1 – Nakajima 0


This time it was Watanabe's turn to have a second turn 2/1, a [Child of Night](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Child+of+Night), followed by an [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton). With a pained look, Nakajima passed back his third turn without a non-land play. Not falling for it, Watanabe attacked Nakajima's hand with a [Distress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distress), revealing the [Archon of Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archon+of+Justice), a sandbagged [Griffin Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel), and surprise, surprise, a [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment).


His trap foiled, Nakajima finally began playing out his fliers. Without removal this time, Watanabe was beginning to take a pounding. A [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) caught his [Gravedigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) one mana short, and was followed up by a [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun). Watanabe could only make pained, keening noises as Nakajima revealed the top five cards of his library. Watanabe pushed four irrelevant cards to one side, and a [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus) to the other. Nakajima happily sent the pile of four to his graveyard.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha11/01_R6_Watanabe.jpg)*Yuuya Watanabe is just bluffing. He totally has the Day of Judgment.*
Things looked dire for Watanabe, until he cast a [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) of his own. It was now Nakajima's turn to make pained, keening noises, as he counted what was left of his library. An [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) took care of the [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus). Well, most of it, and a [Peregrine Griffin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peregrine+Griffin) started swinging the game back in Watanabe's favor. Watanabe eventually played a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and finished Nakajima with a splashed [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) to the face.


**Watanabe 2 – Nakajima 0**


Yuuya Watanabe defeats Chikara Nakajima 2 – 0

Round 8: Feature Match - Shuhei Nakamura vs. Naoki Nakada
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim* 

Shuhei Nakamura is a giant, at least in the world of **Magic**. It has been seven rounds and has only picked up only one loss so far with his three color concoction, previously featured in a deckbuilding report. Naoki Nakada may be a relatively less familiar name, but he is in fact the respectable Grand Prix Manila 2010 Champion and Top 4 Competitor of Pro Tour Paris earlier this year. Just like Yoshihiko Ikawa had demonstrated earlier, it IS possible to be greedy in this format by splashing more than one color. Nakada is armed with a four color monstrosity, loaded with goodies like [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun), [Mind Control](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control), double [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements), while still splashing for [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball), [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) and [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock).


Nakamura opened with [Merfolk Looter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) and [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel), ensuring that he would not be out of gas any time soon. Nakada could only watch Nakamura add three [Saproling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling)s and [Sacred Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Wolf) to his army, while all he had was a lowly [Griffin Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) with no other creatures on his side. Five turns later, Nakada dropped his second [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) onto the battlefield and resolved [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun), receiving [Timely Reinforcements](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) and [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism), eschewing [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) and [Stingerfling Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingerfling+Spider).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha11/01_R8_Nakada.jpg)*Naoki Nakada seems to be in control of the game.*
When Nakamura attached [Trollhide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trollhide) to his [Sacred Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Wolf), Nakada neutralized the usually terrifying threat by affixing [Spirit Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Mantle) on [Griffin Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel). [Mind Control](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) convinced Nakamura's [Skywinder Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skywinder+Drake) to switch sides and [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) dealt with a freshly summoned [Rusted Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rusted+Sentinel). Nakada seemed to have all the answers.


While Shuhei was in no immediate danger, he was sure to lose if he drew nothing relevant for the next three turns. His deck served up [Stingerfling Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingerfling+Spider) to deal with the 5/6 Sphinx, but that was [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel)led without delay. That served as excellent bait, since Nakamura was able to successfully resolve [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) the next turn, taking Nadaka from sixteen to three in one fell swoop, before dealing the final points of damage with his army of [Saproling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling)s.


Nakamura 1 – Nakada 0


### Game 2


Nakamura had no play until turn three, but at least it was a powerful one. [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel) made its appearance and promised to generate enormous advantage once again.


Nakada seemed to be rather action light and only managed to summon [Griffin Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) and [Aven Fleetwing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fleetwing) within his first six turns, applying relatively mild pressure. When Nakada tried to resolve his own [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel), Nakamura denied it with [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), before adding a pair of islandwalkers ([Lurking Crocodile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Crocodile) and [Harbor Serpent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Serpent)) to his board.


Nakada Pacified the gigantic snake and then tried to defend with [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan). When Nakamura attacked with [Stampeding Rhino](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stampeding+Rhino), Nakada blocked with the 6/6 trampler and [Aven Fleetwing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Fleetwing) (possibly fearing [Titanic Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Titanic+Growth)), but that was a plan gone awry. Nakamura destroyed the titan with [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade), killing the Aven Fleeting and causing two trample damage in the process.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsha11/01_R8_Nakamura.jpg)*Shuhei Nakamura – the Japanese Juggernaut.*
Nakada's [Mind Control](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) took over [Stampeding Rhino](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stampeding+Rhino), but Nakada still had no solution to the [Lurking Crocodile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lurking+Crocodile) or [Greater Basilisk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Basilisk). The one damage a turn from his [Griffin Sentinel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Sentinel) wasn't dealing damage quite as fast a [Druidic Satchel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druidic+Satchel) was gaining life for Nakamura. To make matters worse, Nakamura had just drawn [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) to destroy [Mind Control](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control). [Sphinx of Uthuun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) netted Nakada [Crown of Empires](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Empires) and [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock), but that was hardly enough to handle the incoming pressure.


**Shuhei Nakamura defeats Nakada Naoki, and is now at 7-1.**
