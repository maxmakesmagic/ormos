Gloria in Excelsis
==================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on July 24, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







I'm a kid of the 90s. This can mean a lot of things depending upon how you slice it, but for me it means I grew up watching cartoons on Saturday mornings. There are many cartoons that are definitive of the time, but the one I recall most is *Captain Planet and the Planeteers*.


The overarching, and often blunt, message of environmental contentiousness aside, the biggest takeaway I had wasn't about teamwork. It was the same point I found *Might Morphin' Power Rangers* and *Voltron: Defender of the Universe* leaving me with: if you pile up everyone's power you can *always* win.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_cap.jpg)*"Hey kids, lend me +1/+1!"*
And I don't mean teamwork.


Simply stack everyone up as one massive entity, then unleash it against your foes. I mean, The Planeteers called the Captain not because they wanted to have a group moment or could handle things as a team; they pulled him from the Earth *specifically* to take names and kick ass. It was awesome.


So what does 90s nostalgia have to do with **Magic**? This week is Exalted Week, where we're supposed to talk about the mechanic exalted. I have no idea why the idea of everyone pitching their power into one super-heroic being would be what I think of when I see exalted.


### By Your Powers Combined, I Am Freaking Huge!


Exalted is a pretty sweet mechanic for dueling in **Magic**. You send just one guy into the fray, collecting the bonus of those left behind, and usually force an opponent to take lots of damage while leaving your own turf defended or to throw away a creature in the face of overwhelming power. I'm more than certain others will be sharing how to make that happen.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_hound.jpg)[Hound of Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hound+of+Griselbrand) | Art by [Svetlin Velinov](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Svetlin+Velinov%22%5D)


Exalted *isn't* a mechanic I think of when I consider most multiplayer formats. Commander, free-for-all, Two-Headed Giant, and [Group Game Draft](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/72) are all ways to play that encourage attacking with multiple creatures, or attacking multiple players. The +1/+1 bonus is nice to have from a handful of sources, but trying to snuff out several players starting at 40 life, or the opposing team at 30 life, is still hard. It isn't that exalted isn't useful outside of duels, but it sure seems to shine in them.


The question I faced with exalted wasn't, "Is it something fun to play?" because it most obviously is fun. My question was, "Why would I want to have exalted in multiplayer?" The answer wasn't immediately apparent, but it came by asking something different.


*"Why would I want to attack with only one creature in multiplayer?"*


There are numerous ways to attack with only one creature yet dish out devastating damage. Here are a few:


* Poison—10 and done.
* Double strike—halfway there is good enough.
* Annihilator—may you find the blessed sleep.
* "When this creatures deals combat damage to a player"—a mouthful of awesome.
* Unblockable—stop trying to hit me and hit me.

All exalted does is add fuel to the flame. Except for riffs on tokens with [Sublime Archangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel), using exalted in multiplayer asks for a heavy commitment of creatures to the battlefield. Using exalted as an add-on for something else in multiplayer is much more fun with a much lower exalted requirement. Every mechanic can have its day. Exalted just happens to find its fame out of the table I sit at.


So it should be easy to just pile everything else and build an exalted deck, right?


* Poison is primarily in black with cameos in every other.
* Double strike lies under the purview of white and red.
* Annihilator calls without color, but makes steep mana demands.
* Combat damage triggers are all over the place, with some snazzy ones in green.
* Unblockable is blue, it's true! Well, mostly.

Every color in **Magic** plays nice with exalted, assuming we grab the right pieces. Let's go shopping.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_poison.jpg)Poison in multiplayer is a touchy topic. The potency of killing anyone, at any time, with only 10 "damage" is appealing. In turn, those you intend to infect tend to fend against you pretty hard. I've seen some hurt feelings by abruptly ending the game by 10 poison in the face, but some groups are more than willing to make Phyrexian dealings.


At the very least, we are.

* [Blackcleave Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blackcleave+Goblin) and [Putrefax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefax) have haste, making a surprise attack possible.
* [Skithiryx, the Blight Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skithiryx%2C+the+Blight+Dragon) is also happy to make one's acquaintance quickly.
* [Blighted Agent](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blighted+Agent) happens to be unblockable too.
* [Corrupted Conscience](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupted+Conscience), [Grafted Exoskeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafted+Exoskeleton), [Phyresis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyresis), and [Tainted Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Strike) ensure something nasty gets nastier.
* [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) hides until needed.
* [Pestilent Souleater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestilent+Souleater) is pretty powerful if you start with extra life.
* [Reaper of Sheoldred](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+of+Sheoldred)*won't* get gang-blocked very often.
* [Spinebiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spinebiter) is the honey badger of infect attackers.

Again, poison is mostly black but it's spread across every color in some way. These are the tools to turn a single attack into a one-hit knock out.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_doublestrike.jpg)Double strike is impressive and powerful, but it's certainly a little less scary than poison. Of course, there's nothing more terrifying than double strike *with* poison, but that's not the point here. What's important is that connecting directly with opposing players using double strike is another way to reduce the power you need to come up with in an attack.


Don't fear the reaper.

* [Ajani, Caller of the Pride](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Caller+of+the+Pride); [Assault Strobe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Strobe); [Avatar of Slaughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Slaughter); [Double Cleave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Double+Cleave); [Fireshrieker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireshrieker); [Runes of the Deus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runes+of+the+Deus); and [Savage Beating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Beating) grant it on command. [Rage Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Reflection) and [True Conviction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Conviction) stick around for good measure.
* [Crypt Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Champion) seems wonderfully fun. [Hound of Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hound+of+Griselbrand) does too.
* [Jodah's Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jodah%27s+Avenger) is a puzzle in a conundrum.
* [Rafiq of the Many](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rafiq+of+the+Many) is an exalted spin on double strike. [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) performs similar feats.
* [Rockshard Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockshard+Elemental) is always a surprise.

When your plan of attack is to plan to attack, you're on the right track. Double strike is a nice plan of attack.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_annihilator.jpg)The Eldrazi are the sole carriers of the annihilator keyword. While [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Infinite+Gyre) and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) might be the most famous, it's [Eldrazi Conscription](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Conscription), [It That Betrays](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=It+That+Betrays), and [Artisan of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artisan+of+Kozilek) I'm most interested in here. Annihilator isn't prevalent, thankfully, but these provide plenty of power to wield.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_combatdamage.jpg)Getting a bonus when dealing combat damage to a player is awesome. It's quite like icing on the cake in multiplayer. While cake is, ostensibly, the central component to our business, without icing it just doesn't feel the same. I even know a few friends who pick cake specifically for the icing.


I'm not sure where that analogy led to other than hunger.

* [Blazing Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Specter) has the handy haste to go with hand destruction.
* [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat)s; [Lightning Reaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Reaver); and [Skullbriar, the Walking Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullbriar%2C+the+Walking+Grave) mean more hasty attacks.

* [Breath of Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breath+of+Fury) lets you run down an entire line of your creatures that can attack unhindered.
* [Quietus Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quietus+Spike) seems nice with creatures with haste.
* [Riptide Entrancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Entrancer) is always a surprise as well. [Thada Adel, Acquisitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thada+Adel%2C+Acquisitor) isn't.
* The five swords are always worth the price of admission ([Sword of Body and Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Body+and+Mind), [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine), [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice), [Sword of Light and Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow), and [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace)).

Sometimes you just want to hit someone. These are all great way to feel good about that.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_unblockable.jpg)Unblockable is an interesting ability in that it isn't one. It's a characteristic about a creature, sure, but there are creatures which are unblockable without that word helping.


* [Azorius Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Herald), [Covert Operative](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Covert+Operative), [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker), [Jhessian Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhessian+Infiltrator), [Neurok Invisimancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Invisimancer), and [Trespassing Souleater](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trespassing+Souleater) are fine examples of unblockable creatures.
* [Hunted Phantasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunted+Phantasm) is less detrimental in a game full of opponents.
* [Cephalid Pathmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Pathmage) serves as both example and means.
* [Creeping Tar Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) is a land when you need it, a creature when you don't.
* [Deep-Sea Kraken](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep-Sea+Kraken) is a multiplayer favorite of my own, suspended or not.
* [Artful Dodge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artful+Dodge), [Distortion Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distortion+Strike), and [Jhessian Balmgiver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhessian+Balmgiver) make multiple passes for unblockable.
* [Dream Prowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Prowler) is exactly what we're looking for.
* [Akroma's Memorial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Memorial), [Beloved Chaplain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain), [Commander Eesha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commander+Eesha), [Spirit Mantle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Mantle), and [Unquestioned Authority](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unquestioned+Authority) all grant a different kind of unblockable.

"Can't touch this" sums this up nicely, I'd say.

### In Exaltation


So we have a ragtag group of cards to go with our exalted idea. As a quick refresher, these are the best means to get more from exalted.


* [Angelic Benediction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Benediction) gets rid of pesky blockers.
* [Battlegrace Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlegrace+Angel) adds lifelink to the mix.
* [Nefarox, Overlord of Grixis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nefarox%2C+Overlord+of+Grixis) and [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) help keep the path clear.
* [Duskmantle Prowler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Prowler) might be new to us, but I'm rather fond of haste.
* [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour) is the finest exalted trigger there is.
* [Sovereigns of Lost Alara](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sovereigns+of+Lost+Alara) beef themselves up quickly.

It's just a touch but what a punch it packs. Especially when you pile it all together like this.

Karona isn't a Commander for the soft at heart. Passing around a potent force for opponents to use too is just asking for trouble. But that's also why I like this type of deck so much. There isn't a theme of fine-tuned efficiency or demanding mana curves: It just wants to sit down and be played.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf205_karona.jpg)[Karona, False God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karona%2C+False+God) | Art by [Matthew Wilson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Matthew+Wilson%22%5D)


Karona can make the most of effects that grant poison, unblockability, and other abilities, including a one-shot kill with just [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour):


* Have [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour) in Play
* Attack with Karona, naming Avatar.
* Karona gets +4/+4 from the agreement.
* She hits the opponent for 9 damage.
* She untaps, thanks to [Finest Hour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finest+Hour).
* She attacks again, getting another +4/+4.
* Boom.

This isn't meant to be entirely multiplayer, but it works wonderfully for one way to play: **Attack Left**. *Attack Left means every player may only attack the player to his or her left.*


That's it.


It seems simple, but I've been giving it a try recently after having it pointed out as a way to make multiplayer games move. The goal here is to poison as quickly as possible. With plenty of cheap poison to pass around, you'll be racing to kill successive opponents to the left. And, if you've forgotten already, infect is a fine defensive ability next to regeneration.


Exalted might play a small role, but poison amplifies that small piece perfectly.


### The End of the Road


The process of diving through cards of one theme, and finding the tools for many others, is not unintentional. It's part of why **Magic** is awesome. Exalted is a tricky customer for the multiplayer table, but I hope these two starting points show you what's possible with a little ingenuity, faith, and exploration.
