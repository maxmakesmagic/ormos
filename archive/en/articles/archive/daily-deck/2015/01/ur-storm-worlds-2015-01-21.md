UR Storm at Worlds
==================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 21, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






*Editor’s Note: Shuhei submitted his Daily Decks before the* [*Banned and Restricted Announcement*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/banned-and-restricted-announcement-2015-01-19) *and, as such, did not know the contents of the announcement. We still thought it was worth revisiting the format prior to the announcement.*





---

Combo deck are always asking themselves two questions. One, how can I defeat my opponent even faster? Two, how can I resist my opponent's disruption?



One answer to these questions is to completely ignore the opponent and pursue speed alone.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Grapeshot)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot)



This was the answer Owen and his teammates brought to the table at [the World Championship](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/2014WC).



If the opponent doesn't interfere, the deck can play [Goblin Electromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Electromancer) and go off next turn. The deck plays mana acceleration (which are [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual)s, thanks to the Electromancer), cheap draw spells, and a combination of the two—[Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose)—to thread into [Past in Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Past+in+Flames) and finally round back with 20+ damage from [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot).



However, there are absolutely zero ways to disrupt the opponent.



With this cost, the deck has obtained the possibility of a three-turn kill—originally not thought possible in Modern.
