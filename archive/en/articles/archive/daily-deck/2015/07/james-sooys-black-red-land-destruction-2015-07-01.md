James Sooy's Black-Red Land Destruction
=======================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 1, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






Today we are continuing our origin stories with James Sooy. James is a Digital Designer in *Magic* R&D. He was born and raised in Dallas, Texas, and has been with Wizards since 2007. When James was growing up he played a lot of *Magic* with his twin brother, David. David only had one deck, and any time he got a new card he would immediately trade it away to make his deck more powerful.



The goal of this deck is to destroy all of your opponent's lands so they are unable to play spells. Eventually they will have a huge hand of spells they are unable to cast and they will lose to either [Black Vise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Vise) or [Underworld Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams). This deck utilizes one of the most frustrating combos to play against: [Wheel of Fortune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fortune) and [Underworld Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams). When you cast Wheel with [Underworld Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams) in play, you deal a whopping 7 points of damage to the opponent. Combined with [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s, [Chain Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+Lightning)s, and simply drawing a card and taking 1 every turn, it won't be long before your opponent is finished off.



[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wheel+of+Fortune)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fortune) [![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Underworld+Dreams)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Dreams)

To make things even more infuriating, four [Will-o'-the-Wisp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Will-o%27-the-Wisp)s—nearly all of the deck's creatures—were capable of stopping almost any creature thrown David's way. And [Will-o'-the-Wisp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Will-o%27-the-Wisp) can't even attack (well not effectively)!



Since this was David's only deck, James had to play against it *every* single time they played *Magic*! James was usually playing a Thallid tribal deck that splashed for the [Channel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Channel) and [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) combo, but these little creatures were no match for the powerful Land Destruction deck, especially when David was also running [Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake)! In James's words: "I played against it over and over. It taught me patience . . . and I hated it."
