Red-Green Karn Tron
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 21, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






Pro Tour Hollywood winner and 2009 US National champion Charles Gindy has played quite a few format-changing decks in his time, and his latest foray into Modern is already beginning to revolutionize the metagame! Looking to make a return to the Pro Tour stage, the Florida native won a PTQ last weekend using an innovative Red-Green Urzatron deck. His unique deck has quickly become the buzz of **Magic Online** and is on track to be a breakout performer in the last two weeks of Modern PTQs

Unlike other more controlling Urzatron decks that have done well in Modern, like [Luis Scott-Vargas's White-Blue Urzatron deck](/en/articles/archive/white-blue-urzatron-2012-02-22) from Grand Prix Lincoln, Gindy's list is full of threats. His no-frills take on the deck aims to assemble the Urzatron trifecta of [Urza's Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine), [Urza's Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower), and [Urza's Power Plant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant) as soon as possible by using [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings), [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map), [Sylvan Scrying](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Scrying), and a whopping fifteen cards that "cantrip"—or provide an effect and draw a card.

Once the Urzatron is in place, the deck chucks threat after threat at the opponent. Four copies of [Mindslaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) and four of [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) will give any Modern deck a truckload of trouble, and a singleton [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) can lay down the beats if necessary. A single [Academy Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins) in conjunction with mana filtering lets Gindy's deck recur [Mindslaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) turn after turn, essentially ending the game by stealing every one of the opponent's turns! And, of course, the deck also has the ability to use the "[Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) into [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn)" endgame that other Urzatron decks have established so well.

If you're playing in a PTQ this weekend, be prepared for this deck—or play it yourself! Check out the decklist below, and look at the [Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored*](/en/events/coverage/pro-tour-avacyn-restored-qualifier-season-top-8-modern-decklists) schedule to find a PTQ to play in.
