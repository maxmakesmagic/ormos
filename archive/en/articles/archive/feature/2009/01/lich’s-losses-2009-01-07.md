From the Lab is your column to find the most interesting, fun, and wacky combo decks the world has ever seen. Brought to you weekly by host Noel deCordova, the above decklist is one of Noel's greatest creations. It wasn't enough for Noel to just want to win with [Lich's Mirror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lich%27s+Mirror); no, he wanted his opponent's to *lose* infinite times! How does he do it? From the article itself:

*"I want to use* *[Puca's Mischief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puca%27s+Mischief)* *to give* *[Lich's Mirror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lich%27s+Mirror)* *to my opponent, kill him somehow, and watch him gain 20 and draw seven cards. Since my opponent doesn't own* *[Lich's Mirror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lich%27s+Mirror)**, it stays in play. The key card I have in play, however, is* *[Rain of Gore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rain+of+Gore)**. Instead of gaining enough life to get to 20, my opponent will lose that much life. Thus, he or she loses the game again—or would, if* *[Lich's Mirror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lich%27s+Mirror)* *weren't still in play. Instead, he or she shuffles up again, tries to gain enough life to get to 20 again, and loses even more life this time.*

*Yes, that's right. I want to kill my opponent an infinite number of times. Ultimate defeat!"*

You can read more about the deck in Noel's [original article](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/6).
