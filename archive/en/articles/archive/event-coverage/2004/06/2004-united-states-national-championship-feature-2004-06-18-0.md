2004 United States National Championship Feature
================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 18, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TedKnutson.jpg)
By Ted Knutson












![Mitchell Tamblyn](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/usnat04/mitch.jpg)


Mitchell Tamblyn is a seventeen-year-old Magic player from Phoenix, Arizona. He finished in the top 8 at Grand Prix: Oakland earlier this year, and came in 13th at Pro Tour: San Diego in May. He 3-0'd the first Constructed portion of Nationals this year, which, by his own estimation, is a miracle, since he considers himself a Limited Specialist.


Mitchell: "When I sat down, I planned to force Whiteâ€¦ if it had been before *Fifth Dawn*, I probably would have gone Green with the [Fangren Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fangren+Hunter), but I figured people have started drafting Green again, since Sunburst seems pretty decent in the last pack.


I feel like the table passed me some very good picks (like the early [Icy Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icy+Manipulator)), but I was having a hard time late because I was passing things like [Spire Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem) so as not to go too deep into a second color and keep my options. I ended up with three Blue cards and two Black cards, but I had to pass out some real power cards like [Devour in Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+in+Shadow) to keep my mana base in shape. Generally when a new set comes out, I like to play the cards that are more consistent, and avoid the rares or picks that might be confusing. A lot of people are attracted to cards with gold symbols on them, but unless they are really good or I don't have other good choices, I tend to avoid them.


I had a really tough choice in the first *Fifth Dawn* pack between [Acquire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acquire) and the new Looter ([Thought Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier)). Think I made the right call, because [Acquire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acquire) seems very good, but [Thought Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier) is probably a little more consistent.


Predictions: Probably 3-1. I think the deck is pretty good, but not ridiculous. It's just solid. I'm gonna run seventeen land, and that may be a mistake, but I can always sideboard it out."


These were the pick choices Mitchell ruminated over during the draft. The actual cards he chose are in **bold**.
