Providence Nine
===============



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 2, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






So this week we are going to recap the Legacy Top 8 from last weekend's [Grand Prix Providence](/en/events/coverage/legacy-champion-descends-jupiter), and finish up with—in the category of "good / winning decks that aren't Caw-Blade"—another great, different, and tournament-winning Standard deck that you can run, that you might not have seen before.

First up, Legacy:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td145_chart.jpg)  
So what do we have here?

Ultimately this Top 8 showcases the unpredictable norm for Legacy. Eight different decks including two different, oblique combo decks; two [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) decks; two [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) acceleration decks; one beatdown deck; and one aggro-control deck in Alex Majlaton's Merfolk (though you can make the argument that Turtenwald's Stoneforge [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) works much the same way ("You should have played around the [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze)").

Players have really embraced the diversity of the Legacy format, expressing their delight in quite disparate ways; to wit, Reid Duke's "Thirteen rounds and I didn't face any one deck three times," versus Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa's "People just play what they want, *even if it is bad*," ... to Majlaton's snarky "Nothing, *every* deck is really really bad" (emphasis mine, emphasis mine).

Well, let's take a look at some of these decks... Hopefully they aren't too bad.

We might as well start with our star, James Rynkiewicz's surprising Bant deck. There is a movement in Legacy of so-called NO Bant decks, indicating Bant aggro featuring the card [Natural Order](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Order) (the "NO" in NO Bant)... but James actually had no something else: no [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)!

[Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) is the pinnacle of Legacy Tier One, almost universally considered the best card in the format (or perhaps sharing that title with [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)), with the vast majority of Open Series event winners since the banning of [Survival of the Fittest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) playing [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), and generally both cards. So James eschewing it this time around was a statement... And people certainly noticed.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td145_noForce.jpg)  
This is not to say that James had no permission... He ran both [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) and [Mental Misstep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep), with some extra countermagic in the sideboard, but especially given his victory over a turn-two combo kill deck in the finals, playing no [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)—and winning with no [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)—is something people are going to talk about for years.

Enough about what it doesn't have. How does this deck actually work?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td145_gSun.jpg)  
Per the section header, this deck gets most of its edge from acceleration, both traditional in the form of [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) / [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) at the one, and from [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith). [Green Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) is "another" one-mana accelerator on the first turn, allowing James to procure [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) and set up for three mana on turn two. Of course, in addition to getting ready for a fast [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), we have some supplementary tricks. Lead off on [Tropical Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tropical+Island) to [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze) while tapped out, while still having sufficient mana for [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)... or the [Savannah](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah) in hand for [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) or [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) on the two, etc.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td145_3Cards1.jpg)  
The signature card of most Legacy Bant decks is [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary). Again, though James only listed three physical copies, his Zeniths make for mad redundancy. [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) is a Swiss Army knife... Huge attacker or blocker, [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) factory, procurer of the [Maze of Ith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith)... even fast [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul) defense!



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Knight of the Reliquary | Maze of Ith |

One of the principal strategies in Legacy is to just land a super-fast [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) (via [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), or however). [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) can search up [Karakas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) to invalidate that game plan.

As with many large-format Bant decks, James's doesn't over-specialize in any one area, but does many things passably or better. It is a fair but not the fastest beatdown deck (ONE [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)); it is a fair but not suffocating permission deck (NO [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)); it has some good card drawing in [Sylvan Library](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Library) plus [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor); and it captures some of the wonderment of [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) without being overly reliant on that, either. All-in-all, James can do lots of different things in lots of different games, and mold advantages almost like a hybrid deck.

Brian Eleyet played a cool combo deck that is kinda sorta two different combo decks. The first combo is "just" [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) + [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). We've seen lots of decks like this in the past. You run out an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) of some kind and either [Ancient Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb) or [City of Traitors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=City+of+Traitors), plop out [Show and Tell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) with cheatyface mana, thank your lucky stars you drew your two-card combo, and cross your fingers that the opponent doesn't have something interesting.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Show and Tell | Emrakul, the Aeons Torn |

In terms of what's "interesting," you really don't want to see [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation), [Karakas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas), or even [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) (which can go and get [Karakas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas)). But when the opponent doesn't have something interesting, it is ho hum, kill you with [Emrakul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul). Because really, on the second turn, who can withstand all that annihilator (not to mention 15 in the sky)?

Now the even more busted / exciting / humiliating combo is to cast [Hive Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind). Once [Hive Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) is on the battlefield, Eleyet could cast one of any number of Pacts—say, [Summoner's Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoner%27s+Pact). [Hive Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) will make a copy of the Pact for the opponent, and if he or she can't pay the cost when the upkeep rolls around... That's it! Second-turn kill (potentially).

| --- | --- |
| Hive Mind | Summoner's Pact |

Painter is a relatively straightforward combo, as far as Legacy combos go.

Get [Grindstone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone) onto the battlefield.

Get [Painter's Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) onto the battlefield at the same time; name a color.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Grindstone | Painter's Servant |

[Painter's Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) turbo-charges [Grindstone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone), so one shot will typically be game over, exhausting the opponent's entire library. Yes, there are some holes to a deck exhaustion strategy given available cards in the format, but Painter's raw speed makes up for a lot. You can easily go turn one [Grindstone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindstone), turn two [Painter's Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) + [Lion's Eye Diamond](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond). If opponents don't have something then and there, they never will.

For example, [Silvergill Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvergill+Adept) is pretty great in a Merfolk deck, unplayable in basically any other kind of deck.

![Silvergill Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Silvergill+Adept)  
Merfolk is a deck that thrives in a format of control and combo decks. You can get down a clock—maybe [Cursecatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher) on the first turn—and another clock, and your clocks all work together... and then you sit back on your countermagic while winning as quickly as possible.

On that subject, we have Owen Turtenwald's [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill+) deck. This deck can play a sit-there control game with the best of Legacy. It has [Mishra's Factory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Factory) and what passes for a high(-ish) land count. Turn-two [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill), get in there with [Mishra's Factory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Factory), and typically, the opponent will be obligated to destroy the Factory... free [Ancestral Recall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall)!



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Standstill | Mishra's Factory |

But in addition, Turtenwald can play [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) to shift the perspective of the deck. He can go aggro-control (much like Merfolk), and use his countermagic to protect that position. Even better, he can mesh the two halves of the deck together.

Consider...


> 1. [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) (fetching [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull))
> 2. [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) (with [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) out)
> 

Check out this position! If the opponent doesn't do something about [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) in response to [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill), Owen is able to drop the equivalent of a [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) onto the battlefield... *right through the [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill).* Pretty cool, huh?

Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa rounds out this Top 8 with a very different [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) deck.

This deck is a much more control-oriented look at [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). It is basically always going to win with either [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) or [Mishra's Factory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Factory) (no creatures). [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) offers a powerful synergy with both the deck's [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s and gives it the ability to get back fallen [Mishra's Factories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Factory) (you know, the little guys can be a bit fragile in a world full of both point removal and [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Jace, the Mind Sculptor | Life from the Loam |

[Pernicious Deed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed) offers a very versatile defensive card that can deal with almost anything (though not [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)). But the vast majority of the deck is instants. Sometimes you have to break your own [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill). Maybe you cast it on turn two but your opponent had more [Mishra's Factories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mishra%27s+Factory) than you. Maybe you didn't draw any lands and your opponent drew a ton. Maybe you actually used [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) to buy a ton of time and it did what you wanted... but you have to break it now.

![Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pernicious+Deed)  
Having a ton of instants can be productive there: You can break [Standstill](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill) at the end of your opponent's turn, and severely limit his or her ability to use the free cards. For example, an opponent who already has seven cards in hand and goes up to ten will often have to discard. It's a small edge, but it can definitely come up.

Larry beat Caw-Blade, DarkBlade, and then Caw-Blade again in the Top 8.

Some tips from our most recent PTQ winner:


>  The [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) will almost always continue with a [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) chain. The absolute worst thing you can do (although it seems like it would be a good idea at the time) is to discard [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) to get an [Oracle of Mul Daya](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya). If you do that you are down the creature in your hand and you have to wait upwards of two turns to start chaining [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine)s again, since you have to draw another creature, discard that creature for [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), then discard that [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) for [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine).
> 
> 
>

> Another losing play is on turn three playing [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) with an active [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) in play, landfall [to get a green mana] to discard [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), searching out a one- or two-drop, and playing it, triggering [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine). This may seem flashy but it puts you so far down on cards and tempo. It has the same problem as the Oracle plan. You have to restart the [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) chain and you are down DI cards in your hand.
> 
> 
> 

Seems like a pretty cool deck, especially with the combination of [Vengevine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) and [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) as a one-two punch against beloved [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) in Game 1, as well as its much-discussed Caw-Blade advantage.

So there you have it, another option for your battles this weekend, or throughout the PTQ season. I know I am going to give Larry's deck a go.
