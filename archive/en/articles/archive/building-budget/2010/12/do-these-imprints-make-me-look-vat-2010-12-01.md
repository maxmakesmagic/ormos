Do These Imprints Make Me Look Vat
==================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on December 1, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week I began exploring the applications of [Master Transmuter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Transmuter) in Extended. I've continued testing various lists and I've been more and more impressed with the deck's ability to win games against the best decks in the format. I've recently added [Scarecrone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scarecrone) and [Semblance Anvil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Semblance+Anvil) to the deck. I will continue working on the list and I'll be sure to have a tournament-ready version ready for you guys by the time Extended Pro Tour Qualifiers roll around. 

Today, I'd like to talk about another card that gains a lot of value when exposed to the Extended card pool. [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) is a card that begs to be abused. Its effect is powerful by itself, but it reaches a whole new level of absurdity when you combine it with cards like [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), [Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker), [Mournwhelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk), [Spitebellows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitebellows), [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), and [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab119_vat.jpg)  
Last week, I asked readers how they would like me to abuse my [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat). I've seen a lot of Elemental decks sporting cards like [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) and [Horde of Notions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horde+of+Notions). These decks are increasing in popularity on **Magic Online** and the optimal builds are becoming more and more streamlined as Extended tournament deck lists become available on the interwebs. 

I felt like the [Ancient Ziggurat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Ziggurat) versions of Elementals were too streamlined at this point for my column to greatly affect any builds. [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) is an obvious addition to the Elemental deck, but I felt like I could build a control deck that did a better job of abusing the powerful *Scars of Mirrodin* rare. The creatures that work well with [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) may still be Elementals, but I'd like to support these creatures with powerful spells. To me, it seems like the Elemental decks are just piles of creatures. Sure, the creatures draw cards and kill things, but I worry about any Extended deck that doesn't want to interact with its opponents. 

Grixis (blue, black, and red) seems like the best color combination for an Extended control deck. We get access to cards like [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout), [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate), [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), [Makeshift Mannequin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin), and all the Elementals that go well with [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) sans [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark). 

To start, I'll break the deck down into parts and explain the purpose of each card.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab119_theCardsHeader.jpg)  
[Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) makes a deck like this possible. I'm only going to play three copies, though, because it loses a lot of value in multiples. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab119_imprintHeader.jpg)  
**[Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)** is the obvious four-of here. [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) gives this deck an additional threat, a card advantage engine, and one of the sickest things you can possibly put on a [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat). 

**[Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker)** may not be a shoe-in yet, but it will be once people realize how powerful this type of effect is when combined with a [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat). A lot of time you will cast a [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) on the third turn, evoke a [Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker) on your fourth turn, and immediately induce a concession. 

**[Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage)** is an important tool against Jund and other control decks. It does a great job at giving you more time to set up your [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat), and it becomes a very nice imprint once you have your Vat on the battlefield. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab119_imprintCross.jpg)  
**[Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw)** is a surprisingly good threat that happens to provide a big advantage when you imprint it onto the [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat). 

**[Mournwhelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk)** is a closer. Once you've used a [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) to get your opponent off [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) mana you can safely evoke a [Mournwhelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk) and imprint it onto the [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat). [Mournwhelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk) is even better against decks that don't have [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). As soon as you have control of the board you can find your [Mournwhelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk) and keep your opponent off their draw step. 

**[Spitebellows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitebellows)** is a nice one-of. It kills [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan), Doran, and attacks for a lot of damage. An additional [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) initially occupied this spot, but that changed the first time my opponent cast a [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan). Killing Doran isn't quite as important as [Grave Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) because you can simply apply a [Nevermaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevermaker) lock.

**[Flamekin Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger)** can find the perfect piece for the perfect time. I see most Extended [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) lists play with four copies of this card, but the card disadvantage accrued by casting too many do-nothings can often be a huge hindrance. If the card let me search for any Elemental or [Mimic Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) then I'd be sure to play the full four. I'll only play a few copies while I wait for the errata on that, though. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab119_controlHeader.jpg)  
**[Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)** is the best spell in Extended and Standard. It takes a lot of arm-twisting to convince me to not play four of this card. It's incredible on offense and defense. It's good against Faeries and Aggro decks. 

**[Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)** has proven itself. It makes draws a lot more consistent and helps us find key cards for specific match ups. 

**[Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate)** is a classically awesome removal spell. A deck like this will be able to take advantage of the extra time it buys with these cheap removal spells. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab119_controlCross.jpg)  
**[Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout)** is a necessary evil. The Faeries match-up seems impossible without it. Fallout also does quite well against the White Weenie decks. 

**[Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren)** is great against other pure control decks. It preemptively deals with [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), it provides a steady stream of card advantage, and it encourages players to play more aggressively. (A huge boon for a deck like this.)

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab119_theCardsFooter.jpg)
