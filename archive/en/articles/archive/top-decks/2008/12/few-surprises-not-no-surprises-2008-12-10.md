Few Surprises ... But Not No Surprises
======================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on December 11, 2008 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











This past weekend was the Star City Games $5,000 Standard Open in Philadelphia, PA.

This week—and it will probably already be in progress once you are reading this article—is / will be the [2008 World Championships](/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/worlds).

Of the three formats that make up Worlds, Standard is the most important one (they are each one-third of the Swiss rounds, but it is Standard that will be played on Sunday in Memphis). So Standard has been fairly well defined by States and Champs, **Magic Online**, and the smoky back rooms of secretive playtest groups, but many major championships—particularly those with Standard components—have been up-ended at the last minute by tournaments occurring just beforehand. Will Worlds be another such example?

What did the Top 8 look like?



|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Vengeant Kithkin |  | 1 Winner |
| Faeries |  | 3 Top 8 |

| Esper Reveillark |  | 1 Top 8 |

So like the title of this article reads, there were few surprises making up this Top 8 ... but not *none*. We have seen decks like a lot of these before, but more than one have interesting tweaks, changes, and angles by which they attack the metagame. Plus, as a super added bonus, in addition to some cool videos to watch, we have some last-minute deck modification tips from the Top 8's local Pro Tour Champion! Let's begin!

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ajani+Vengeant)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant)Corey Mann won it all with this Kithkin beatdown deck splashing [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant). Note that with the exception of that awesome planeswalker (some very good players are calling it the best card in Standard), Corey's deck is four [Battlefield Forge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlefield+Forge)s shy of being 100% *Lorwyn* / *Shadowmoor* Block Contructed legal.

Now we have seen some red-white beatdown decks in Standard recently, including Marsh Usary's innovative [Painter's Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) combo deck, but Corey's deck is barely red at all. His *entire* red splash is to support [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant), four copies; *that's* how good the card is!

Besides splashing for the popular Planeswalker, Corey's deck ran 4 [Wilt-Leaf Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) over 3–4 [Thistledown Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thistledown+Liege), a "logical" yet fairly unexpected and unusual choice for the archetype.

[Wilt-Leaf Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) is just fundamentally a much stronger card than [Thistledown Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thistledown+Liege) (hence the logic). The reason [Thistledown Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thistledown+Liege) got / gets so much play in decks like this is that it is a Kithkin and plays all nice with [Rustic Clachan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rustic+Clachan) and [Wizened Cenn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wizened+Cenn). Plus, in [Mirrorweave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorweave) decks, [Thistledown Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thistledown+Liege) allowed Kithkin to start sequencing at the end of the opponent's turn or during combat (especially when a [Mirrorweave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrorweave) was involved). However in "modern" Standard, the Kithkin's greatest foe is Five-Color Control, a deck that typically tops up on [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum). While [Wilt-Leaf Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) doesn't turn Five-Color Control into a cakewalk, it does offer a little bit of insulation thanks to the creature's synergy with opposing discard. Plus—and as we've discussed with decks like the New Girl—[Wilt-Leaf Liege](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wilt-Leaf+Liege) is a stone cold show stopper against [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning) Beatdown. The black-red deck's defining sorcery goes from being baffling burn-bomb to ... well .... It's just depressing.

The highest performing Faeries deck was Jonathan Sukenik, at second place (two others in the Top 8, and more in the Top 16). Jonathan's deck ran a singleton [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) in the main, and was built with a much greater focus on destroying creatures and controlling the board than most Fae.

There are a number of ways one can go about building a Faeries deck. Will you play [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) for its offensive and pleasantly evasive capabilities, or switch to [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) to slow down hasted red creatures and keep your Warhammer a safer bet? What about card drawing? Will you go the planeswalker route with [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), splash white for [Esper Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm), or play off the top of your deck? Sowers or Cliques? [Remove Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) or [Broken Ambitions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broken+Ambitions)? Many of the Faeries decks *look* almost identical, but they can definitely be segregated into different camps.

For his part, Jonathan's was more anti-aggro than many a Fae deck. In the main, Sukenik played almost a full set of [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation)*and* the maximum number of [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp)s. Out of the sideboard, he rounded out the [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) count, played both [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) and [Eyeblight's Ending](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyeblight%27s+Ending), *and* ran all four copies of [Infest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest).

Why do we see a single [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) in so many Faeries decks? First off, [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) is most often used as a kind of proactive [Healing Salve](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Healing+Salve). Decks that run it would not necessarily do so but for the life gain. Fae is no exception; in fact, the life gain can be quite substantial in a deck that is vulnerable to the Red Deck and can fall behind, well, *anybody* thanks to its own [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom). Now speaking of [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom), [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) goes nicely with that card, turning individual 1/1 Faerie Rogue flyers into significant threats; in fact, a Faerie wearing a Warhammer will usually be able to trample over the other guy's Faerie (in a mirror). When this card comes online, it is obviously a useful tool for racing.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stigma+Lasher)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stigma+Lasher)David Kilmartin sped up the Red Deck Wins model by playing [Boggart Ram-Gang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang) at the three and replacing [Ashenmoor Gouger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashenmoor+Gouger) with [Tattermunge Maniac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattermunge+Maniac). With [Tattermunge Maniac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tattermunge+Maniac) in the mix, Kilmartin's red deck had three different high-quality one-drops to get damage in early.

His choice of two-drop was [Stigma Lasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stigma+Lasher). As we have seen in various deck lists, the Red Decks of the current Standard can play a variety of different drops at the two, with [Stigma Lasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stigma+Lasher) probably edging out [Vexing Shusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Shusher) as the most popular. [Stigma Lasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stigma+Lasher) on the play is actually quite devastating for decks planning to overwhelm Red with [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant)'s [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix), or [Primal Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Command). In fact, a strike can translate into a [Mind Twist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Twist).

[Magma Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Spray) is just more [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) hate. This card can't go to the opponent's face, but it really, truly, duly kills [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) (and can also disrupt a [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) deck's game plan).

That solitary [Reflecting Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflecting+Pool) (over the fourth [Ghitu Encampment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Encampment)?) .... I assume it was a head fake.

David's sideboard was actually quite interesting. The last time we saw [Dominus of Fealty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dominus+of+Fealty) was, well, never .... Kind of a persistent, singularly effective, [Unwilling Recruit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unwilling+Recruit) on wheels.

The quadrangle of [Chaotic Backlash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chaotic+Backlash)es is what really demands attention. Many red decks will roll over and die when threatened by enough [Spectral Procession](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Procession) tokens, but David's deck had a big burn spell to try to overwhelm the bad good guys, even without [Painter's Servant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painter%27s+Servant) to set it up.

Dave Irvine's deck is something that you may have seen before but we haven't really discussed in Top Decks ... until now!

This deck is an extremely synergistic collection of functional creatures. It's got disruption ([Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler) and [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage)), card advantage ([Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)), and [Control Magic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Control+Magic) ([Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation)). All of *these* creatures have 2 power or less, making them all [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)-eligible.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Irvine's deck can attack a particular opponent relentlessly and redundantly, knocking one of the three legs out from under his stool and never letting up. For instance, Dave could play third-turn [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage), pop it; return it with [Makeshift Mannequin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin); pop again, and eventually recoup it with [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) for [Stone Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain), [Stone Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain), [Stone Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain) action (hopefully all while blocking).

Or he could start on the first turn with a [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) (very quick), follow up with [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler), then round out the one-two-three with an offensive [Esper Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm) .... Probably not the average use of [Esper Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm) in this deck, but certainly effective against a hapless opponent who has already declared a mulligan or two.

Or Dave could play an attrition game against the opponent's creatures, play [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) to sweep, and clean up with a Sower of Tempation on whatever the opponent held back.

This deck can do a great many things with 2/x creatures, or sit behind [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) (against red) or go beatdown with them (against Faeries), or anything in-between.

Top Decks looked at this top deck a few weeks ago in the feature [Rogues Around the World](/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/td/13). Brian Kowal won a Game in the Gulf Cruise Qualifier with the red-white Vengeant [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) deck, albeit a less tuned one than Osyp used to make Top 8 of the Philadelphia tournament.

Brian collaborated with Osyp on this one, and they cut a [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) to bring the Boat-Brew down to the correct number of 60 cards.

The deck is extremely robust, and capable of playing multiple different game plans, all strong.

Osyp says that one of the key incentives to this deck is that it "plays the beatdown" role ... but still has more card advantage than most so-called "control" decks. "You are playing [Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) for a pair of Figures and tripping [Windbrisk Heights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights) and they are trying to assemble the colors to play [Esper Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm) .... It's kind of embarrassing for them. A control deck cannot win the attrition war against this deck."

The Boat-Brew is also a capable sit-there deck. As suggested by Osyp, it can sit there and accumulate card advantage with the best of them. It has fast beaters ([Mogg Fanatic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) and [Figure of Destiny](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Figure+of+Destiny)), mana acceleration ([Mind Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Stone)), two-for-ones ([Knight of the White Orchid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+White+Orchid) and [Murderous Redcap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap)), three-for-ones ([Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos)), four-for-ones ([Siege-Gang Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander)), and inevitability in [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)!

Plus, possibly the strongest card in the format, [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant) (that is Osyp's pick, anyway).

Many readers questioned the [Vithian Stinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vithian+Stinger)s in Kowal's sideboard the last time we talked about this deck, but the current version actually features a transformative sideboard. The "modern" Boat-Brew can run an eight-pack of protection from white against Kithkin .... [Voice of All](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+All) and [Stillmoon Cavalier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stillmoon+Cavalier) can prevent the opponent from attacking at all, and next to [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant), race at will.

Some suggestions from Osyp:

-1 [Burrenton Forge-Tender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burrenton+Forge-Tender)  
 +1 [Flamekin Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger)

Osyp thinks the red matchup is strong enough to move the Forge-Tender to the sideboard. Playing [Flamekin Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Harbinger) in the main allows the Vengeant [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) to use [Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) to actually tutor up [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark)! Fight with the Harbinger, get it into the graveyard, and you can recoup the little Flamekin for more and more [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) action.

Mike McGee's Top 8 Five-Color Control deck has a secret.

Did you see what it is?

It was actually 75/75 the same as Bryan Lynch's Rhode [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) State Championship-winning Five-Color Control deck!

This deck has a lot of unusual elements. Let's start with the creatures: two [Cloudthresher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher)s and two [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)s only .... [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) is almost always an automatic four-of in Five-Color Control ... but not this one.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace+Beleren)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren)To supplement card drawing, the Lynch / McGee deck played not just the stock quartet of [Esper Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Charm)s but also three copies of [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) ... strong card draw, better in the mirror. But wait! The planeswalkers don't end with Jace. Like many of the top decks, this one packs possibly the strongest of the strong, It Girl cat-bomb [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant).

This deck has an edge against other control decks thanks to the planeswalker action, but it is dismal against Faeries. As such, Bryan / Mike played a powerful sideboard against the First Among Equals with [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) for instant sweep potential and [Chameleon Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus) as an inexorable racer.

Mike took the time to do some Qamp;A for my beloved readers after his Top 8 appearance. Let's get to 'em!

**Top Decks:** Why did you play the deck?

**Mike McGee:** I played the deck because I wanted to play a deck that between my friend Eddy and I we could both play decks that ran blue. We only had enough cards for one copy of Faeries and one Five-Color Control. It's just very good against every deck that isn't Faeries and has a good matchup in the mirror due to Ajani and Jace.

The mana base is very stable with two filters of each. The only way—if you have seven or eight mana in play—you can't play [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) is to have three of the possible six lands that can't get you black or red *without* having one of the blue-red or blue-black filter lands.

**TD:** Would you play it again? What was your favorite card?

**MM:** The best main deck card is [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum)—it can turn around any game if it resolves—but my MVP was [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm). A lot of people just don't expect it and their sideboard plan with White Weenie and red-white is [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) anyway, so it can just blow them out with multiple options. You have "[Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm)" at instant speed if they try to make Figure 4/4 or you can remove the graveyard when their [Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) gets removed from play with [Condemn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn).

**TD:** What is the best matchup?

**MM:** Straight White Weenie.

**TD:** What is the bad matchup?

**MM:** Faeries. Game 1 against Faeries is almost 80/20 them, then after sideboard it becomes around 50/50. To win you need to draw your non-dead cards to have a shot and it's still tough. I should have lost all my matches against Faeries but I got very luck with their having terrible draws or making huge mistakes. It's auto-lose if they have [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom).

Thanks, Mike!
