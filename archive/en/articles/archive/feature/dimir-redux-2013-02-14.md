Dimir Redux
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 14, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







Two weeks ago, I wrote about [a Dimir mill deck](/en/articles/archive/dimir-2013-01-30) in Standard to use the new guild. It was just built to show off a new strategy, but after writing it, I became seriously curious about the deck's competitive potential. 


![Duskmantle Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Duskmantle+Guildmage)
 I physically put the deck together and tuned and updated it in early consideration for [Pro Tour *Gatecrash*](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/protour/gatecrash13). After Brian Bruan-Duin's success with [a graveyard-based deck](/en/articles/archive/infinite-humanity-2013-02-05) in the StarCityGames.com Open in Atlanta, I felt that I had to give up on the deck due to an extreme weakness to [Rest in Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace). It remains an exceptionally fun deck to play, so I thought I should share my latest version. 



[Mind Sculpt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sculpt), [Jace's Phantasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Phantasm), and [Wight of Precinct Six](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wight+of+Precinct+Six) have impressed me, but [Lazav, Dimir Mastermind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lazav%2C+Dimir+Mastermind) is too restrictive on the mana, because I want to play [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard). [Duskmantle Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskmantle+Guildmage) is pretty awesome, and I won some games by dealing 20 with [Jace, Memory Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept) or 14 with [Mind Sculpt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sculpt), but, ultimately, I think it's just too slow for competitive Standard. 


 Meanwhile, I realized that all the mill spells power [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman), which you can tap for mana, which is unusual and extremely powerful in Standard. [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) is an excellent way to buy time while your better creatures power up. 


If I were to play the deck in the future, it would look something like this:
