Pithy Title
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on December 20, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar











![A tree](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/house/cm102_tree.jpg)Welcome to Treefolk Week! With [Snow Week](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/cm28) and [Hoser Week](/en/articles/archive/making-magic/enemy-mine-2002-02-18) already in the books, I guess dedicating a whole week to trees is the only way we've got left to pander to the Canadians. And pander I shall!

I'm going to start with a little combo I came up with two seconds ago: [Tamanoa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamanoa) + [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack). It's like [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) has lifelink! The resulting deck will of course be named Tamarack, after the small, slender tree found primarily in northern half of North America. That's double north. A member of the larch family, the tamarack is easily recognizable (even from great distances) by its delicate deciduous foliage.

If you just enjoyed that fun tree fact, you're in luck: there's plenty more where that came from! It's called the Internet (or, I guess, the forest). Poke around in it for a while. You'll find all kinds of neat things. In the meantime, I'm going to play **Magic** cards.

### Good Things Come in Trees

Of all the new Treefolk in *Lorwyn*, [Doran, the Siege Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower) is clearly (and somewhat ironically) the most powerful. The 2007 **Magic** World Champion, Uri Peleg, took home the big trophy playing a Standard deck containing four copies of the sick son of a beech. Doran is just fine all by his lonesome, but he's just a little bit better when he's turning [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s into, uh, more powerful [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s, [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into [Scryb Sprites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Sprites), and [Ohran Viper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ohran+Viper)s into Elephidians. Of course, those creatures are also first-rate monsters, which explains how Peleg's deck could win a major tournament, and a deck that combined Doran with, say, [Kami of Old Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kami+of+Old+Stone) could not.

![Doran, the Siege Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower)Well, it's a good thing I'm not trying to win any tournaments, because there are plenty of fun and/or silly things you can do with [Doran, the Siege Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower) outside of such a setting. For instance, some people—nay, [some evil geniuses](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/429)—would have you kill your opponent on turn three by playing [Shuko](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shuko) (or Shaman *en*-Kor) then [Daru Spiritualist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Spiritualist), and following that up by playing Gnarls Barkley himself.Sure, turn-three kills sound both fun and silly, but I'd prefer to do something altogether more janky. It is a well-known fact that Doran brings out the worst in people, places, and things. He turns otherwise [Angelic Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Wall)s into hellacious ass-whompers, transforms [Forbidding Watchtower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidding+Watchtower)s into foreboding ones, and makes [Dancing Scimitar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dancing+Scimitar)s do the Electric Slide. I know, it's terrifying to think about. Worst of all, he makes the most peace-loving character in **Magic** into a killing machine. Poor [Karn, Silver Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn%2C+Silver+Golem). No more Mr. Passive Resistance, he's out to (reluctantly) kick some butt. With Doran at his side, Karn battles opposing critters as an 8/8. He'll still try to go easy on them, but that just makes things worse.

With this unlikely duo as the starting point, I started to look for artifacts that pump toughness. That meant armor: [Slagwurm Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Slagwurm%2BArmor), [Living Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Living%2BArmor), and [Belbe's Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Belbe%2527s%2BArmor), as well as [Scale of Chiss-Goria](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scale+of+Chiss-Goria). [Ensouled Scimitar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensouled+Scimitar) can attack as a 5/5 flyer if you control Doran, or it can simply make the Treefolk into a veritable 10/10. Of course, all of these artifacts can also be animated by Karn's touch if need be.

You can't play Doran without some mana-fixing, so I went with Birds, [Treefolk Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treefolk+Harbinger) (note that it can fetch dual lands), and the somewhat in-theme [Wanderer's Twig](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wanderer%27s+Twig). The "removal suite" is also apropos ([Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roots) and [Rootgrapple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootgrapple)), as is my pump spell of choice ([Twigwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twigwalker)).

During my search for cards to pair with Doran, I plugged "+0/+X" into a number of card databases. This led me to [Living Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Armor). It also turned up this trio, all, remarkably, from Legends: [Kry Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kry+Shield), [Subdue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Subdue), and [Great Defender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Defender). Boy, they don't make 'em like they used to. Thank God.

### Yew Willow Pine? Yew Butternut!

Wow, that was a stretch even for me. It doesn't even make much sense when translated. Unfortunately, I spent a frighteningly larch amount of time on TreeCanada looking fir potential tree-related puns. I've already used alder best ones, but I maple some of the less poplar ones out of my bag of tricks and yews them later. Cedar sycamore hemlock.

Here's another Doran deck, this time using toughness-pumping discard outlets like [Tireless Tribe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tribe) and, uh, [Krosan Archer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Archer) to fill your graveyard with, um, [Conclave's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conclave%27s+Blessing)s (and [Improvised Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Improvised+Armor) and [Fists of Ironwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fists+of+Ironwood)) before you play a game-affecting [Retether](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retether). I think I mentioned somewhere that this would be a little janky.

I included some other cards that work well with Doran, like [Silklash Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silklash+Spider), [Swooping Talon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swooping+Talon), and [Crowd Favorites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crowd+Favorites). I left out some others like [Spirit Weaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Weaver) and [Oathsworn Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oathsworn+Giant). The lone copy of [Solidarity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solidarity) gives the deck its name, but given the rest of the deck, that slot should probably go to [Aven Warcraft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Warcraft).

### Xylem for Murder

![Timber Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Timber+Protector)Ol' Doran isn't the only all-walkin', all-talkin', all singin' tree whose board-way melody we should all be paying some heed. No, not [Singing Tree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Singing+Tree). I'm talking about the original Tower of Wow-er: [Timber Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timber+Protector). Besides making your piddly 5/7s into 6/8 behemoths, T.P. makes them indestructible to boot. An unrepentant tree-hugger (What did you expect?), he keeps your [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s safe from harm as well. As long as he's around, you don't have to worry about [Orcish Settlers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orcish+Settlers), [Boggart Loggers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Loggers), [Goblin Gardener](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Gardener)s, or Akki Florists. Never mind that the Loggers can take out T.P. himself. That's called taking one for the team, and [Timber Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timber+Protector) is nothing if not a team player. Just look at how he'll stick up for his [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) friends, even if they've magically transformed into Elves.This deck is pretty straightforward. Fish a lot of [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s out of your deck with cards like [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) and [Hunting Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Wilds). These [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s will make your [Dauntless Dourbark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Dourbark)s huge and your [Ambush Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Commander)-led Elf army enormous. Once all of your lands are Elves, your [Elvish Branchbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Branchbender)s can then turn them into X/X Treefolk. Basically, they become slightly less dauntless Dourbarks. [Timber Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timber+Protector) makes everybody but your Elves indestructible, naturally.

T.P. is fine with any animated [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), whether it's because of the [Ambush Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Commander)s, [Elvish Branchbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Branchbender)s, [Hunting Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Wilds), or [Genju of the Cedars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genju+of+the+Cedars) and [Living Terrain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Terrain). Throw in an [Earth Surge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earth+Surge) and a [Strength of Cedars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+of+Cedars) for flavour and you're all set.

### I Will Not Lignify That With a Response

![Lignify and Vigean Hydropon](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/house/cm102_cardfan.jpg)The last Treefolk deck was inspired by a cute little combo sent to me by Efrén R. It consists of one part [Lignify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lignify) and one part [Vigean Hydropon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigean+Hydropon). You see, [Lignify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lignify) is a Treefolk Aura that turns the creature it enchants into a 0/4 Treefolk with no abilities. [Vigean Hydropon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigean+Hydropon), meanwhile, is an undercosted 5/5 with an "ability" that makes it so it can't attack or block. That sounds like a pretty poor ability to me, and Efrén agrees. To remedy this unfortunate situation, just play your [Lignify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lignify) on your Hydropon. Due to some crazy rule that somebody made up, you are not left with a 0/4 Treefolk with no abilities as expected, but rather a 0/4 Treefolk with no abilities and five +1/+1 counters! Your once combat-fearing "Wall of Hats" is now a 5/9 beatstick, ready to storm into battle (with appropriate headgear, presumably). You can pull off similar tricks with [Ovinize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinize) and [Cytoshape](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoshape).

Not content to merely beat down with an otherwise neutral Plant Mutant, I took note of the fact that its combined power and toughness was ten, the same as [Timber Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timber+Protector). Add some [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair)s and your Hydropon can search out T.P. and, once you drop the [Lignify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lignify), become an indestructible former Plant Mutant. That still wasn't enough.

I then added [Treefolk Harbinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treefolk+Harbinger) because it tutors for both [Lignify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lignify) and [Timber Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timber+Protector) (as well as [Rootgrapple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootgrapple), [Breeding Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pool), [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor), or just plain old [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)). With a [Wild Pair](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Pair) in play, the Harbinger's combined power and toughness of three lets you go looking for [Yavimaya Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Dryad), [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness), [Vedalken Mastermind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Mastermind) (I smell synergy!), or [Merfolk Thaumaturgist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Thaumaturgist). The Merfolk acts as a blue Doran, in a way, since it can make your [Timber Protector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timber+Protector)s deal 6 damage in combat and your Lignified Hydropons deal a whopping 9. You can also use it to wipe creatures off the board in conjunction with either [Lignify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lignify) or [Ovinize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ovinize). With [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and [Vedalken Mastermind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Mastermind) on the board, you can keep doing this, turn after turn. You're a dead yew. You're a dead ewe. Have fun with homophones!

### All Thematic for the People

[Fiery Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Fiery%2BJustice)With the holidays just around the corner, I thought I'd give away a bunch of bonus decklists. Often at this time of year, Magic columnists (Anthony Alongi is the only one I can think of right now, but I'm sure there are others) will build some holiday theme decks. The theme might be something as simple as adhering to a strict [red-white](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/santa-boros-champion-2005-12-06) (See: Claus, Santa)  
 or [red-green](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/few-favorite-things-2004-12-07) colour scheme.

Heck, you can include all three colours! Nothing says holiday spirit like some [Fiery Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Justice). Well, it just so happens that the three decks that I turn to most often when I'm playing on my own time and my own money are a mono-red, a mono-white, and a mono-green deck. They're pretty normal and don't require a ton of explanation. The first is a [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power) deck with a bunch four-for-a-ticket rares acting as finishers. [Torchling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torchling) is actually really good when it's a 6/6 and you have a million mana. The second deck is an all-commons White Weenie deck (which just got infinitely better with the addition of [Goldmeadow Harrier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goldmeadow+Harrier) and [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring)). The last deck is my favourite one to play, since it's full of exciting green cards. It's basically an updated version of the deck I sent JMS before he did [Going Rogue 2](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/395). It can do a bit of everything, but mostly what it does is kill people with [Squall Line](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall+Line).
