Sunday, 9:45 a.m. – Day 1 Undefeated Deck Lists
-----------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Now that was unexpected. And I'm not even talking about the [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler)/[Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) concoction built by a team from Hamburg (including PT Kobe 2006 champion Jan-Moritz Merkel). That's certainly interesting and unexpected; I never foresaw this one deck in particular going 9-0. But one innovative brew is hardly a trend.


What I find even more exciting are the others. Two (very similar) green-white beatdown decks and one deck that's essentially White Weenie (with a blue splash). No Reanimator and "only" four copies of [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk). Maybe the influx of the instant-based blue-white tempo decks kept [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) in check but were in turn bested by [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben)? That may be a new trend indeed.

Feature Match Round 11 - Martin Jůza (GWB Craterhoof Behemoth) vs. Daniel Antoniou (Naya)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Both of these players had already been in the feature match area yesterday, apparently with good reason, as they both went victorious then. Now, with both at 9-1, only won would walk away a winner.


### Game 1


Jůza opened on [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), while Antoniou just had a couple of lands (some entering the battlefield tapped) and made his first move on turn three: a rather unprepossessing Selesnya Keyrune.


Meanwhile, Jůza had [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch)ed into two extra lands and two copies of [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). He also had gotten in a couple of points of damage via [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens and now made a [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage), which immediately got stuck under Antoniou's Oblivion Ring.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm11_antoniou.jpg) *Daniel Antoniou* 
A second [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage) met a second [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), but all the while, Antoniou could never develop any sort of board presence of his own, and tokens plus [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) constantly nibbled away at his life total. To make matters worse, [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) found [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) which boosted Jůza's team and put Antoniou at 8 already.


[Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) made that 13, [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) plus tokens turned that to 7. Another [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) switched that back to 12 again, but now Jůza made four more tokens via [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and once again activated [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township). Jůza did hesitate about this line of play for a second. He was exposing himself to a topdecked [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) pretty badly, but there was nothing he could do about that anyway. Antoniou didn't have it, though, and that was that.


Martin Jůza 1-0 Daniel Antoniou


### Game 2


Both players started on acceleration, with [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) for Antoniou, [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) into [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) for Jůza. With four lands untapped on Antoniou's side, these tokens didn't dare to attack, and indeed Antoniou did have Restoration Angel at end of turn.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm11_juza.jpg) *Martin Jůza* 
Jůza summoned [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and lost it to [Selesnya Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm), but not without getting a 3/3 token as corollary. In total he now had two flying 1/1 tokens, a 3/3, and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) to Antoniou's lonely [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). Antoniou appeared to be heavily flooded on lands, whereas Jůza once again had [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township). Turn after turn, the mighty Township amassed more and more +1/+1 counters. Soon Antoniou was forced to chump-block and then he couldn't even do that anymore ...


Martin Jůza 2-0 Daniel Antoniou

*by Frank Karsten*

So that's 16% G/W Aggro, 11% Jund, 10% Zombies, 9% Bant Control, and 9% Reanimator together making up over half of the field.


Some of the archetype categories above are a little broad. For those of you interested in more fine-grand descriptions:


* The 31 G/W Aggro decks were comprised of 20 relatively standard versions, 7 Human-themed featuring [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) and/or [Mayor of Avabruck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck), and 4 slightly slower versions with [Armada Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armada+Wurm).
* 19 Zombies player almost all went for Black-Red, as only 3 Zombie players ran a Black-Green variant.
* The 17 Reanimator decks were comprised of 7 Green-White-Black builds, 6 versions with Red for [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), and 4 versions with Blue for [Tracker's Instincts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker%27s+Instincts).

Besides these more conventional archetypes, there were plenty of spicy brews around. You see the "R/U/G Soulbond" category above? Blue-White [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), the bane of last season's Standard format, is back! In recent weeks, UW Flash decks had already started to incorporate a lot of the cards responsible for its remarkable success before the rotation ([Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike)) and replaced those that didn't make it past the rotation (like [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) with [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)). And now, with less [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) in the top decks, even the namesake of old, [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), made its comeback.

In that environment, I figured [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) should still be viable," said Sutor. "And it turned out, [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) was indeed still viable. I showed the deck to Jonas, Lino, and Thomas, and they were able to duplicate my results too."

So we went looking for additional cantrips, and found [Fleeting Distraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleeting+Distraction). We also considered [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction), [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread), and at one point even [Crippling Chill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Chill). Holzinger still had [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction), and Müller was running [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread). All of these take out [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) temporarily and allow [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) to get in additional attacks. In the end, we went with two additional [Swift Justice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swift+Justice)s, which are obviously great with Geist, but also for example when [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) faces off against [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), or when Angel fights Angel," Sutor explained. "Also, the card is great against aggressive decks, thanks to lifelink and because it allows you to keep racing.

"[Spectral Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) may be the most important sideboard card. With that, Geist keeps being relevant, even a real menace, against creature-heavy midrange decks, which is really important because we're not set up for the long game.


"Overall, the deck has been the nuts, until Adam Prosak's UW Flash deck really caught on and people started to include as many copies of [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) as their manabase could make room for. We only have six counterspells, but being able to pinpoint one at [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) or not being able to do that sometimes makes all the difference."

The dream is to go turn 1 [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), turn 2 [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), turn 3 [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage), and kill on turn 4 with [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). "It happens a lot," Martin said. "[Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) can kill people out of nowhere, and it's easy to find with [Tracker's Instincts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker%27s+Instincts)."


A card that has raised many eyebrows is [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage). "People are picking it up, asking me what this card is, what it does, and whether it is even legal. In the end, they realize they have no idea what I am playing, and that has definitely been an huge advantage for me."


When asked how the deck came to be, Martin explained that he got it from Brad Nelson. Martin had helped tweaking the deck, talking to Brad every day, and changing some of the cards around, but it was Brad and the StarCityGames crew who invented it.



![Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)

Next, I asked Martin which decks he would like to be paired against. "Blue-White Flash is basically unlosable", he said. "You have so many good cards against them; [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) in particular are very hard for them to deal with. The Reanimator matchup is also very good. [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) can remove their threats, and with the mana boost provided by [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage) you can come over the top with [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) before they start casting relevant spells. You don't want to sideboard anything against Reanimator at all."


When asked which cards he did not want to face with his deck, Martin mentioned [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought); [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren); and [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). "But once we added [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township)s, we could beat those cards as well. Jace's -1 effect is negated entirely by [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township). The land also helps my creatures survive against [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) and forces my opponent to pull the trigger on [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) when facing a board of only 2 creatures."



![Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Loxodon+Smiter)

Next, I took a look at Martin's transformational-style sideboard, and I have to say that I'm thoroughly impressed. "Against Blue-White decks with [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), you morph into a [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) / [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) / [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) deck by taking some of the mana guys and the [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) (anticipating them to board [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace)). You also bring in the big cards against Jund to sidestep [Slaughter Games](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) and stuff like [Rolling Temblor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor). Finally, Mono-Red Aggro and Zombies won't be happy to face [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) after sideboard. And [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage) plus [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) is great against Green-White Aggro."


Could this deck play a major role in Standard going forward? It has been doing well in both Grand Prix tournaments this weekend---and it's fun to play, too!

Feature Match Round 14 - Martin Brenner (Green-White Aggro) vs. Guillaume Bourdinaud (Mono Red Aggro)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

Both Martin Brenner from Germany and Guillaume Bourdinaud from France are currently sitting at 11-2 records and have their sights set on their first Grand Prix Top 8. Brenner is playing Green-White with amazing creatures such as [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) and [Armada Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armada+Wurm). Bourdinaud is playing Mono Red Aggro with a ton of 1-drops.


### Game 1


Brenner started off with [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) into [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin), while Bourdinaud paired [Stromkirk Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble) with [Stonewright](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonewright) on his couple of turns. The creatures exchanged blows, with Bourdinaud unable to block [Stromkirk Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble) with any of his Humans.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/Guillaume-Bourdinaud.jpg) *Guillaume Bourdinaud* 
Bourdinaud looked to be winning the damage race, but that changed when [Armada Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armada+Wurm) and [Wolfir Silverheart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) came down on Brenner's side of the table. Brenner apparently loves his fatties, who were towering over Bourdinaud's tiny guys.


Bourdinaud tried to build up for a big [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf)-fueled attack, but it wasn't enough. Brenner used [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) turned [Wolfir Silverheart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) into a 10/8 trampler, and it closed out the game in short order.


Martin Brenner 1 - Guillaume Bourdinaud 0


### Game 2


Bourdinaud started with a nice curve of [Stromkirk Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble) and [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot), and tried to keep Brenner off white mana by burning [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) with [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame). A second Pilgrim for Brenner stuck around, however, and it allowed him to start casting big monsters again.


First up was [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer), which kept [Ash Zealot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ash+Zealot) and [Stromkirk Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble) at bay. Two [Gore-House Chainwalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker) from Bourdinaud tried to smash through [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer), but soon enough [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) came down to trump the 3/2s. And then [Wolfir Silverheart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) entered the battlefield as well. Despite a great start from Bourdinaud, his creatures were simply too small.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/Martin-Brenner.jpg) *Martin Brenner* 
Brenner made sure to play around a surprise [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) by always keeping enough creatures back to block. It was correct to play cautiously, but it wasn't necessary given Bourdinaud's draws: the Frenchman didn't draw anything that could help his tiny creatures get past the huge green monsters, and lost the game to the massive power of [Wolfir Silverheart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) once again.


Martin Brenner 2 - Guillaume Bourdinaud 0

I started off by asking how this deck came to be. "We were just looking at random decklists when our eye caught [an innovative one](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/deck/1102) from a recent Starcitygames Open," they explained. "This was the [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) / [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) deck that also featured the [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator) / [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) / [Gilded Lotus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus) infinite mana combo. We didn't get the numbers with all the 2-ofs and 3-ofs, and the [Gilded Lotus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus) combo seemed way too cute. But we started brewing, teching out the deck, and caught fire."


"We liked the idea of [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler), and started looking for additional pingers. We quickly found out about [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) and added it to the deck. Next, we added the [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) / [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) combo. It turned out to be very valuable against control decks like Esper with all their Planeswalkers, where the deathtouch angle isn't very effective."



![Tracker's Instincts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tracker%27s+Instincts)

"Afterwards, we added [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and [Tracker's Instincts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker%27s+Instincts) to make the deck more consistent," they continued. "[Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) is very good against Reanimator and Blue-White Flash. But [Tracker's Instincts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker%27s+Instincts) is what pushed our deck over the top. There are so many high-impact creatures and combos to find, and decks like Jund cannot keep up with the card generated card advantage unless they have [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return)."


Their deck seems very well positioned for the field, as it will eat the most popular deck in Day 2, Green-White Aggro, for breakfast. The Green-White players simply have no way to disrupt the soulbond combo.


When asked what cards pose problems for the deck, the two German players mentioned hexproof creatures such as [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and [Sigarda, Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons) (because they cannot be targeted with the pingers) and [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) (because it will take any soulbond combo apart). But they added [Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin) to the deck with that in mind, and it is a great answer to those problem cards.



![Appetite for Brains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Appetite+for+Brains)

No deck is complete without a sideboard. In the sideboard, we find some spot removal and life gain to keep up with aggro decks: [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) can exile [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), and [Mizzium Mortars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) can deal with [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter). The rest of sideboard has been created mainly with the control decks in mind: the discard spells and [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) can be brought in against, say, Esper control, by taking out [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster).


Going forward, this crazy concoction will be a force to be reckoned with. Merkel and Pritsch recommended to keep the 25 lands because the deck is mana hungry and runs so many colors. They said you could freely adjust the numbers on [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and on the [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) / [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) combo depending on whether you would expect more aggro decks or more control/midrange decks.


Between crazy combos, turn 3 [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), and a strong long game, Jan-Moritz Merkel and Max Pritsch have certainly assembled something a very impressive new Standard deck!

Despite being implementations of the same basic strategy, there are considerable differences between these two lists. Andreas Kontos's deck is trying to go big with mana acceleration and [Increasing Savagery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Savagery), whereas David Demmel's is much more aggressive with [Dryad Militant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Militant) and [Fencing Ace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace). The Ace is particularly interesting, theoretically allowing for an actual turn-three kill. There's definitely some potential here for further brewing ...

It seemed like the new [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) to me; it's a Delver that flips all the time if you want. Exiling one spell to make it into a 3/4 is enough. Although I had first cut [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) from the deck, I put them back when I saw that [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) was on the decline."


"With [Cathedral of War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+of+War), my deck can consistently attack for 4 on turn 2, and that gives a bunch of free wins," Brozek continued. "My best matchup is Mono Red Aggro. They cannot use burn to deal with [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) or [Nivmagus Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nivmagus+Elemental). My worst matchup is probably Green-White Aggro. They have so many cheap creatures, which means that I can't get big tempo boosts from [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) or [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm)."

And the more unusual cards, such as [Divine Deflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Deflection) and [Faith's Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Shield), have been very hard for my opponents to play around. [Faith's Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Shield) in particular can counter spells, [Fog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) if I'm at less than 5 life, and protect my [Nivmagus Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nivmagus+Elemental)--- it's been great."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/Petr-Brozek-showing-off-Sleep-and-Nivmagus-Elemental.jpg) *Petr Brozek showing off [Sleep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) and [Nivmagus Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nivmagus+Elemental).* 
Brozek is currently out of contention for the Top 8, battling in the last round with a 10-5 record. This suggests that his deck is not perfect yet and that it can be tweaked and improved further. For instance, Brozek mentioned that he would consider playing [Sleep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) maindeck as he has been boarding it in a lot and it has been instrumental in getting past [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk).


Still, his deck looks fun and powerful, and if you're looking for an innovative build to play for Friday Night **Magic**, then give this deck a spin!

Round 15 Feature Match – Benjamin Paulmeier (Red Black) vs. Martijn van der Vaart
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Oliver Gehrmann*

 

Only 2 more rounds until we'll know who'll advance to the Top 8 of Grand Prix Bochum. This is where it counts the most and where you'll need good nerves to pull through.


We invited Benjamin Paulmeier and Martijn van der Vaart on the Feature Match table; the winner will still have a chance to advance to the knock out portion of the tournament while the less fortunate player will have to walk away with some shattered dreams.


### Game 1


Both players kept their opening hands and Paulmeier wasted no time, casting an Unleashed [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler). It dealt 2 the following turn, another [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) came down and van der Vaart desperately tried to keep the pace with [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas), adding Sphinx's [Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revelation) to his hand.


[Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) dealt with [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas), clearing the way for attacks with both [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler)s. Since van der Vaart didn't perform any actions his following turn, he took another 4 from the attacks. Paulmeier tried adding more threats: One of his [Diregraf Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul)s met its master in [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) while the other one stuck.


Van der Vaart kept playing passively, only adding a fourth land. Paulmeier went in for it, but [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) came down, blocking one of the [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler)s. Paulmeier finally found a third land, allowing him to cast [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger).


The following turn, [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) stopped the attack of [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger), [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) blocked [Diregraf Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Ghoul), but [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) dealt 2, leaving van der Vaart on 2. Paulmeier passed with 3 lands ready, telegraphing an instant.


Still, the Dutch player kept his cool, added a sixth land his following turn and passed with only an untapped [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) in play.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm15-restoration-angel.jpg) *Would a lonely [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) be enough to stop Paulmeier from wrapping it up?* 
An instant [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) by Paulmeier got countered, the first [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night) the next turn met the same fate, however, a second copy meant that Paulmeier would come out on top the first game! "That's a lot of burn...", van der Vaart replied slightly shocked.


Benjamin Paulmeier 1 – 0 Martijn van der Vaart


"Against what deck did you draw?" a surprised van der Vaart questioned his opponent. "Esper Control," Paulmeier replied. "The second game took a lot longer than it should have..."


### Game 2


This time, it would be van der Vaart sitting in the driver's seat, however, Paulmeier felt confident he could steal the initiative when he opened with an Unbound [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler).


[Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) sent it right back, causing Paulmeier to play it again and also add a second copy.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm15-runechanters-pike.jpg) *Paulmeier didn't want to give van der Vaart time to ever equip the artifact!*

The [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler)s went in again, Paulmeier added [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) and a [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) from van der Vaart, sending [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) and [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to his graveyard, concluding the turn.

Cool Plays - The Many Uses of Rancor
------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

I overheard some pretty sweet plays this weekend. Two in particular stood out, and they both involved [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor).



![Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rancor)

The first involved a Green-Black-Red Zombie player who had enchanted [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) with [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor). His opponent, an Esper control player at 7 life, had the seemingly perfect answer in [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism). This is a card that the deck builders amongst you should definitely keep in mind when [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) is a thing in the format. But the story doesn't end here. On the subsequent turn, the Esper control player was wondering whether or not he should leave up any [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) mana or whether it would be safe to play [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for the full amount. At 7 life and facing an untapped [Blackcleave Cliffs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blackcleave+Cliffs), untapped [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb), and the 5/3 [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger), he figured there was nothing in the format that would be threatening enough to [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) and put all of his mana into [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). Well, he hadn't taken [Fling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fling) into consideration. [Geralf's Messenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) dealt 5 damage, and then came back from the grave for the final and lethal 2 points. So, even if [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) is taken out by [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism), its +2/+0 effect can still be the difference between a win and a loss!


The second play involved a Green-White Aggro player who was losing to his opponent's [Ravager of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravager+of+the+Fells). The Green-White player had [Selesnya Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm) in hand, but that wouldn't deal with a creature with 4 power or less. The solution was found in the fact that you can play [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) on your opponent's creatures as well. So he played [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) on [Ravager of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravager+of+the+Fells), subsequently exiled it with [Selesnya Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm), and moved on to win the match!
