In celebration of the impending release of *Shards of Alara,* Daily Decks looks at past creations that combined the colors of **Magic** into those of the shards found in Alara. The plane of Grixis would shiver the spines of most planeswalkers, just as the deck known as Trix chilled many a tournament player to the core. Arguably the most powerful combo deck of all time, this particular version was used by Tsuyoshi Ikeda to make the Top 8 of Grand Prixâ€“Kuala Lumpur in 2000. He forced [Necropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropotence) on the board with [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), then used the power of "the Skull" to fill his hand. To combo, he would simply play an [Illusions of Grandeur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusions+of+Grandeur), gaining 20 life in the process, then [Donate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate) it to his hapless opponent who would eventually fail to pay the cumulative upkeep and, conveniently, lose 20 life. [Firestorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firestorm) allowed Ikeda to keep the board clear of pesky creatures as well as do the final points of damage in the event his opponent tried to gain life to get out from underneath the Illusions.
