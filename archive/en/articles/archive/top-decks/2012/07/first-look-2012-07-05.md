By Jacob Van Lunen

Hello and welcome to Perilous Research, the newest column here on **DailyMTG.com**. Perilous Research is a column dedicated to the competitive world of **Magic Online**. Each week, I'm going to look at Daily Event results and explain how decks have changed from the week before. This column is an incredible tool for players looking to improve their results in competitive play. A quick read every week will give you a solid understanding of the metagame in all Constructed formats on **Magic Online**.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr202_angel.jpg)[Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) | Art by [Johannes Voss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Johannes%20Voss%22%5D)

The **Magic Online** metagame tends to be about one week ahead of the live metagame. Using **Magic Online**, you can correctly assess the expected metagame of the coming week and attack it accordingly. I plan to analyze the decks that go undefeated in **Magic Online** daily events each Saturday to give readers an expected metagame for the coming weekend's events. Decklists from all the most recent daily events can be found [here](/en/articles/archive/2009-06-29).

Today, I'm going to take a look at the decks that went 4–0 in daily events on Saturday, June 30, 2012.

### Standard

Two weeks ago, the Standard metagame was dominated by [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). The number of White-Blue Delver decks that had gone 4–0 was more than double that of the second most successful deck. Since then, White-Blue Delver has slowly loosened its grip on the format. Last week, red-green-white decks, both [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) versions and less complicated versions like the one played by Martin Juza at [Grand Prix Manila](/en/events/coverage/watanabe-snags-his-sixth-win), became very popular. Juza's creature brew punished Delver players and many started picking up more aggressive decks that were well positioned against the red-green-white strategies. Here's a breakdown of the decks that managed to go 4–0 in last Saturday's Daily Events.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr202_chart1.jpg)  
White-Blue Delver builds remain similar to the past few weeks. Many players have decided to include a second or third [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) to significantly improve their matchup against [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise)/[Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212)/[Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) strategies. Versions with [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) performed better a few weeks ago, but this week, Delver made a return to form. All but one Delver deck to go 4–0 played three or four [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel).

White-Blue Delver managed to make as many undefeated players as any other deck, but this week's results are significantly less lopsided than usual. Aggressive black-red decks are this week's big winner. The deck is growing in popularity because it offers players an excellent Delver matchup and its [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist)s let the pilot make aggressive trades favorably in creature matchups. Aggressive black-red decks have been performing decently in the past few weeks, but many of those implemented [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) as a powerful engine alongside [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler). This week, none of the undefeated black-red decks played any number of [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) in the main deck. [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) is excellent against the current field of red-green-based decks. There are very few players with [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and that creates an excellent opportunity for [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) to swoop in and pick up some free wins.

Red-Green Aggro performed exceptionally well when you take into consideration how few people actually play the deck. [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) is finally being recognized as a four-of in many decks that have previously only included one or two copies. [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) is significantly better in a deck that's good at creating a strong board presence. The versions of Naya that did very well last week were fully committed to board presence, playing four [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) and a pile of lands and creatures. The Red-Green Aggro decks function similarly to Juza's deck. However, these decks also include somewhere between two and five Equipment ([Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) and/or [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine)). The Equipment performs well against control and can often win Game 1 very handily against other red-green or Naya decks. The power of the Swords gets diminished greatly in Games 2 and 3, when these decks have access to [Ancient Grudge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge).

Standard is becoming more balanced. The success of aggressive black-red and red-green strategies might push people toward white-blue midrange decks in the coming weeks. Some white-blue midrange players have begun including some number of [Stonehorn Dignitary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonehorn+Dignitary) and [Venser, the Sojourner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+the+Sojourner). This combination will make it very difficult for aggressive decks to ever push through with an attack. However, post-board, the combination becomes a huge liability because [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) can steal [Venser, the Sojourner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+the+Sojourner), allowing that player to use the Planeswalker's ultimate.

### Pauper

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr202_chart4.jpg)  
It's always exciting when no storm decks have untarnished records. The latest iteration of the Blue Pauper deck is especially strong. It uses the tested and approved strategy of casting [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), flipping it, and following it up with strong disruption and tempo. The deck takes other [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) strategies into consideration with an excellent trump card, [Spire Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+Golem).
