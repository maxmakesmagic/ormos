“Here, I Ruel”
==============




August 8-9, 2009












Event Coverage
--------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri09/Grand-Prix-Brighton-Champion-Olivier-Ruel.jpg)


Playing by the numbers, Olivier Ruel has won his 5th Grand Prix, in his 26th Top 8, in his 107th Grand Prix. Having seemed destined to not even make it to the Top 8 after he lost the last round of the swiss to the undefeated Dutch champion Kevin Grove, the Frenchman drafted an aggressive Black/Red deck with a solid bank of removal spells and swept through the Top 8. Czech star Martin Juza was the gallant runner-up, but he lit up the second day of the tournament with his draft decks, only to fall at the final hurdle.


Having started the year by questioning his drive and motivation in Pro Magic, Olivier Ruel has bounced back in style here in Brighton. If you ever doubted his will to win then he showed you wrong here today, and if you ever doubted his enthusiastic love for the game then the sight of him jumping high in the air with glee when his name was announced as the final member of the Top 8 should set that concern to rest as well. With another glistening trophy in hand, Olivier Ruel is now only 9 Pro Tour Points behind Kai Budde in the lifetime standings, and seems certain to pass the German Juggernaut sooner rather than later.


So that was Grand Prix Brighton - a deserving showcase for the new M10 core set, and it has produced a deserving champion. Join us in Prague in September for more of the same!

Quarterfinals – Mark Dictus vs Kevin Grove
------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri09/QF-Grove.jpg)*Kevin Grove*Kevin Grove comes into the top eight of Grand Prix having done what few before have ever managed – a perfect run through the swiss. Few have got through the swiss unscathed, but none have ever converted that into a win at GP level. In a funny sort of way, the pressure was really on for the Dutch National Champion, who is clearly on a tear at the moment.


Grove, on the draw, took a mulligan, and found himself staring down a turn two [Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith). [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith) followed the very next turn, while all that Grove had was a Mountain and a Swamp.


By the time that Grove found his third land he was on 11, and Dictus had added a [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) to his side of the board. The third land enabled a [Goblin Artillery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Artillery), but given that Grove was soon on 5, it didn’t seem likely to be too effective.


Grove had a plan, with [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) to kill the [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage), and then a [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) to finish [Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith). This plan was halted by [Safe Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Passage) though, and Dictus’ attacks dropped Grove to 2, before [Razorfoot Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin) joined the team.


A [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) killed the Griffin, and kept Grove around for one more turn, but when post-combat Dictus cast a [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), that was plenty enough cause Grove to scoop up his cards.


Mark Dictus 1 – 0 Kevin Grove


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri09/QF-Dictus.jpg)*Mark Dictus*For the second game, Grove would be on the play, and seemed happy with his seven. This time it was Dictus turn to mulligan, which led Grove to sing a little Flemish mulligan song to the amusement of the crowd.


Dictus had a turn two [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) as the first play of the game, while Grove started with [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul). When Dictus tapped out for [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith), it allowed the Ghoul to get stuck in, and Grove followed up with [Canyon Minotaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Canyon+Minotaur). The Minotaur traded with [Veteran Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Swordsmith), and was soon returned by Grove using [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger).


Dictus’ ground plan was all defensive, with [Palace Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palace+Guard) joining the team, and [Razorfoot Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin) came along to allow for attacks in the air. Unlike the first game, attacks were less fast in coming. When [Warpath Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warpath+Ghoul) came in, [Palace Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palace+Guard) blocked, and Kevin made an uncharacteristic mistake in playing [Kindled Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kindled+Fury) to try to kill off the blocker. He had forgotten though that [Palace Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palace+Guard) is a soldier, and being helped by [Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith).


Grove was far from out of plays though. He had a [Magma Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Phoenix), which dominated the board. It was now number one target to be blinded each turn, and presented Dictus wit a bit of a problem. Worse still, after Dictus cast a [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon) and passed, he saw a [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) from his opponent. With 8 lands in play, there was every likelihood that the black sorcery would find him something pretty special. The next play from Grove was a [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) – quite the combo with [Magma Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Phoenix), though admittedly one that would leave each player pretty short on creatures.


When Dictus attacked with [Razorfoot Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin), Grove sacrificed his team to [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat), triggering [Magma Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Phoenix) to kill off much of Dictus’ board. He still had [Palace Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palace+Guard) and [Siege Mastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Mastodon), but wasn’t in as dominating a position as before. Dictus played [Disentomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) to get back his [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage), and cast it before the following turn casting [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel). Could this be it for Grove? He got back his [Magma Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Phoenix), but it wouldn’t even prove that effective a blocker, with [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) around. If sacrificed to [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat), the burn ability would hurt him more than Dictus.


Attacks from Dictus made the life totals 19 to 1 in Dictus favour. Grove drew his card for the turn, and dropped his hand on the table. For the first time in the tournament, and the last, he was defeated.


Mark Dictus wins 2 – 0, advancing to the semifinals!




Semifinals – Olivier Ruel (FRA) vs Mark Dictus (BEL)
----------------------------------------------------


*by David Sutcliffe*

Former Belgian National Champion, Mark Dictus, is no stranger to Top 8 situations, but for Olivier Ruel Brighton marked yet another stop in a monumental career. In fact, the French Hall of Famer suspected, Brighton could be his 100th ever Grand Prix! Sitting down with coverage supremo Richard Hagon, Olivier Ruel ticked off the events he had been to, trying to work out if he had hit his landmark 100th GP or not. In fact, he’d already passed it - and the pair came to the conclusion that Brighton was Olivier’s 107th Grand Prix!


Brighton isn’t Olivier’s hundredth Top 8, but it’s likely he’s not too flustered by the semifinal environment and seemed very relaxed - even pleased just to be there, given that he had only narrowly scraped into the Top 8 in the first place. His loss to kevin Grove in the final round seemed certain to have knocked Olivier out of the event, yet here he is - two matches away from another Grand Prix trophy!


“Attention Belgians. If you came on a bus, please leave - your bus is waiting outside and will leave without you.” boomed an announcement over the PA system, just as the players were about to begin.


“That’s my bus” said Dictus, “ah well - there goes all my supporters!”.


Then with a mighty high-five between the two players, this semifinal got underway.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbri09/SF-Dictus1.jpg)*Mark Dictus*Olivier Ruel’s [Goblin Piker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Piker)s were the first creatures to set foot on the battlefield, although they were immediately blocked down by a [Dread Warlock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Warlock). Ruel followed his Goblins with a [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) while Dictus began to settle his defences behind a [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) - and the [Wall of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Fire) that Ruel summoned up on his next turn seemed unlikely to cause the [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage) any more trouble.


As Dictus passed a couple of turns, holding onto his sole white mana source for the [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage), Ruel developed his position with a [Rod of Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rod+of+Ruin) and then a [Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) which stripped away... a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) and a [Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith). It looked as though Dictus was struggling badly for mana, but that Ruel was unable to capitalise on this fact as his only offense was still that sole [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat), and it as still being tied up by the [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage).


A Siege Gang Commander threatened to change things in Ruel’s favor, however. The Commander hurled one his explosive Goblins at the [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage), but Dictus immediately responded with a [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel)!


The Angel appeared majestic on the table. For about the three seconds it took Olivier Ruel to tap six lands and chargrill it with a [Fireball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball). The Commander, Aristocrat, and their Goblin minions headed into the red zone to put Dictus on 8 life and a turn away from defeat.


Mark Dictus had a backup plan. In fact he had a backup Angel - [Disentomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) returned the Baneslayer to hand, and to play, but it was too late. Attacking with his creatures for a second time, Olivier Ruel was able to ignore the [Lifelink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifelink) ability of the Baneslayer by feeding his [Vampire Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Aristocrat) with the creature that the Baneslayer blocked, and with a little help from another explosive Goblin, he took a 1-0 lead in the match.


Do you remember that [Mind Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) early in the game? And the [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) that was discarded to it, when it seemed like Olivier was an almost mono-black deck? That [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) would have solved Dictus’ problem with the Siege Gang Commander... on such small margins are Grand Prix Semi Finals decided.


**Olivier Ruel (FRA) 1 - 0 Mark Dictus (BEL)**


Given a ready supply of white mana in the second game, it was Mark Dictus who took to the offensive with a [Veteran Armorsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorsmith), [Blinding Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinding+Mage), and [Razorfoot Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorfoot+Griffin), while Ruel had a [Seismic Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Strike) for the Armorsmith and a set of trusty [Drudge Skeletons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons), but only a single Swamp to regenerate with.


Dictus added a [Palace Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palace+Guard) to his forces and attacked with all his creatures, dealing 4 damage to reduce Olivier Ruel to 11 in short order, but with a creature mix like that Dictus’ deck clearly wasn’t built for speed.
