The Evolution of Time Spiral Block Constructed
==============================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 31, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten












Welcome! Today I’m just going to quickly skim over the online Standard and Extended results and focus on Time Spiral Block Constructed (TSBC). Traditionally, Block Constructed with just one set is often not viewed as an interesting format, as the card pool was too small to make interesting decks yet, so you may wonder why I choose to feature it now. Well, TSBC is a special animal, because there are 121 extra “timeshifted” cards in the first set that act like almost a full extra expansion. Probably because of that, the format is reasonable popular online, and the bi-weekly Time Spiral Block Constructed Premier Event regularly attracts around 40 players. So it seems about time to dive into TSBC. 

But first, the weekly Standard update! We have a bunch of high-attendance Premier Events to add up, once again including a bunch of IPA Qualifiers.

U/b Pickles – or mono blue morphs, if you will – appears to be on its way out, and the Rewind enthusiasts seem to be taking up Dralnu du Louvre instead, which scored a whopping 20%. The most likely explanation for this trend is that U/b Pickles has a horrendous matchup against Mono Green Aggro, which also scored loads of Top 8s. Between the sea of Dralnu du Louvre and Mono Green Aggro, we have a brand new innovative rogue design that put up good numbers.

Bennie Smith would be proud. The goal of this deck is fairly obvious: use [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker) or Delirium Skeins to discard a card with dredge, then dredge your graveyard full of goodies and finish with a fat [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll) or [Svogthos, the Restless Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Svogthos%2C+the+Restless+Tomb). [Dread Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Return) on [Akroma, Angel of Wrath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Wrath) also works well. [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) have a dual purpose in this deck, acting as many accelerants and beefing up [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll) later on. 

 The card choices seem smart in the current metagame. [Stinkweed Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stinkweed+Imp) is not a blank with dredge 5; it is actually really good against Mono Green Aggro. Facing a [Silhana Ledgewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silhana+Ledgewalker) with [Moldervine Cloak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldervine+Cloak) and [Blanchwood Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blanchwood+Armor)? No problem for Big Stinks. [Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast)ing a [Scryb Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger) in response to a pump spell is likewise hot. Furthermore, Svogthos the Restless Tomb seems insane against Dralnu du Louvre. They can hold all the [Rewind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind)s in the world, but that won’t stop the land from attacking. Recurring [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void)s also tend to work well against counterspell strategies. 




![Svogthos the Restless Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Svogthos+the+Restless+Tomb)
  
  
 I have tried out wrapter’s version of the deck in the Casual Play rooms for a few games. Unfortunately I have to admit the deck felt rather clunky to me, so I’ll offer some suggestions for improvement. The whole “[Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) on [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) plus [Skarrg, the Rage Pits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skarrg%2C+the+Rage+Pits), so I can trample over with my [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll)” is cute, but it has never been relevant for me. I would like to see more [Svogthos, the Restless Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Svogthos%2C+the+Restless+Tomb) instead, as they seem absolutely awesome in this deck. Also, [Hypnotic Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypnotic+Specter) seems a bit out of place. I know that a turn 2 Specter is huge against Dralnu du Louvre, but it doesn’t truly fit in the deck’s strategy. **In the Beginning, There Was White Weenie and U/G Shifter**

White Weenie is a simple, obvious deck.  All the familiar elements are present, but an interesting inclusion in Aheana’s version is the maindeck [Angel’s Grace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%E2%80%99s+Grace). Such a defensive card is rarely seen in decks that are focused on beating down quickly. But TSBC White Weenie isn’t your average White Weenie. 

![Serrated Arrows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Serrated+Arrows)TBSC White Weenie runs more evasion creatures than ground creatures, so you will often find yourself in a damage race where your flyers are exchanging blows with opposing [Spectral Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force)s. A surprise “[Fog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog)” can buy you an extra pivotal turn in such a situation.  Other common choices for this deck include [Icatian Javelineers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Javelineers) and [Fortify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortify). Is White Weenie any good? I have my doubts. First of all, it doesn’t seem fast enough. It lacks good one-drops and has to fill out its creature curve with awkward cards like [Benalish Cavalry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benalish+Cavalry). Furthermore, a lot of the decks in the format play [Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows), with [Desert](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desert) and [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor) seen frequently as well. Piloting a deck full of [Soltari Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soltari+Priest)s and [Amrou Scout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amrou+Scout)s in such an environment is a recipe for disaster. White Weenie decreased in popularity over the weeks, and I can get behind that. There are simply stronger deck options available.

 Moving on. A couple weeks into the format a new deck archetype sprang up in large numbers, based around [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) (hence the name).

 This deck plays morphs like [Fathom Seer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), [Brine Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brine+Elemental), and [Thelonite Hermit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thelonite+Hermit) and runs four copies of [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) to abuse their unmorphing abilities. No matter what morph creature you’re copying, you are certainly having fun. This deck is very mana-hungry, so it runs [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) as well as [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). The lack of [Serrated Arrows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows) in the above version surprises me a bit, especially since it already runs a singleton [Academy Ruins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins) for recursion purposes. Furthermore, [Willbender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Willbender) looks like it would be a solid addition to the deck as well. 

 An important problem of this deck is that many other decks in the format have also caught on to the power of [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), as we will see soon enough. U/G Shifter dedicates a lot of slots on morphs that [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) can flip into for beneficial effects. But it hurts when you go through all of that effort to set it up your engine, only to lose to your opponent’s [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter). He can abuse your morphs just as well! When you basically hand your opponent a combo on a silver platter, it may be time to move on to another deck.

 The common factor of all Teferi Control decks is that they run some counters, some card draw, some creature kill, some bounce, some big fat flying win conditions, and of course [Teferi, Mage of Zhalfir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Mage+of+Zhalfir), which still works wonders in combination with [Mystical Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings). A sound balance between all those elements makes this deck one potent control deck. FFfreak’s deck dips in black for Mystical Teaching’s flashback cost and a duo of [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot)s. With [Prismatic Lens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens), [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) plus 1 [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), and 6 storage lands, finding a black mana source should not pose a problem. The above deck shows that [Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast) is the de facto [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) of TSBC. 

 I think that some version of Teferi Control is the best deck in the format, and it would be my choice for a tournament. But Scryb&Force is also not bad.

![Sporesower Thallid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sporesower+Thallid) This deck is a direct port of the Standard version, replacing Elves and Birds with [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and sometimes [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow). [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) are also not available, so the TSBC version just runs more guys: [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) and [Sporesower Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sporesower+Thallid). No, we’re not just trying to build a good draft deck here, [Sporesower Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sporesower+Thallid) is actually not that bad in TSBC. Take another look at the U/R Teferi Control deck we just passed by. What is its removal base? [Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast) and [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot)! A four-toughness creature therefore trumps most creature kill.  Not every Scryb&Force build plays blue. In fact, about half of the Scryb&Force players run red instead. I honestly wouldn’t know what the better choice is, but there certainly is some merit to the red version. Without [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), there is less of an incentive to add blue cards alongside a Scryb to the Force. And stuff like Assault//Battery, [Disintegrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disintegrate), [Stormbind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbind), [Thornscape Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornscape+Battlemage), and [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) are quite appealing as well.

 This deck is also directly based on an existing Standard deck, introduced by Shaheen Soorani at the World Championships. The deck is all about creatures with come-in-play abilities and [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink). A cute trick, for instance, is to put the echo of [Avalanche Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders) on the stack and respond with Blink, postponing the echo for a turn and nailing a second land. Playing all the colors of the American flag together provides access to [Lightning Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel), which is one of the best creatures in the set... if you can manage its casting cost, that is. 

 I would like to make room for [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) in this deck, perhaps instead of [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt). The morph has good synergy with all the 187 creatures. When you play [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) (unmorphed, that is), and copy a creature with a comes-in-play ability –say, [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) – then you get to bounce a permanent. That’s neat, but you’re not stuck with a 2/2 flyer for the remainder of the game. On your next upkeep, you just flip the Shapeshifter face down, then unmorph it and copy your opponent’s [Spectral Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Force) for good measure.

 Ah, you didn’t think I would end my discussion of the TSBC metagame without mentioning Slivers, did you? [Sedge Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedge+Sliver) is an improved [Glorious Anthem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Anthem) on a stick, [Bonesplitter Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonesplitter+Sliver) makes for a fast clock, and [Two-Headed Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Two-Headed+Sliver) laughs in the face of blockers. You are quickly building a self-reinforcing Sliver army with this deck, although it stands or falls with the survival of the essential Slivers. The major downside of this type of deck is that if your [Bonesplitter Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonesplitter+Sliver) is [Snapback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Snapback) during combat, your entire combat math is screwed. 

![Sedge Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sedge+Sliver) There have also been straight up red-black versions (without the green) around. I like the above one; I’d rather up my creature count with solid guys like [Might Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+Sliver) than with a meager [Basal Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basal+Sliver). Admittedly, the mana base of a full three color deck is not all that consistent in TSBC, as we don’t have the beautiful Ravnica duals. Two colors is the norm. This implied mana inconsistency is, by the way, one of the major problems of the 3-color BlinkRiders deck. The Slivers, however, have access to [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse), [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow), and [Gemhide Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemhide+Sliver). Still not perfect, and adding [Gemstone Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemstone+Mine) won’t hurt, but all these cards together go a long way towards ensuring you can withstand color screw. That concludes our journey through all major deck archetypes in Time Spiral Block Constructed so far. Hope that you enjoyed it and, join me next week.
