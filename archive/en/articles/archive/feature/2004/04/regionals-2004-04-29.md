### Regionals Primer

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/regionals/images/regionals.gif)This was a slow week for **Magic** events. While nothing much happened around the world on the previous weekend, there will be no shortage of news next week as American players are gearing up for the [Regional championships](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=events/magic/regionals) this coming Saturday.

Sixteen tournaments will be held across the continental United States, awarding eight invitations to Nationals each. Two more two-spot events will take place in Alaska and Puerto Rico. For most players, this is the largest and most competitive Standard tournament they will play in all year.

For those that need some catch-up, here's a very quick run-down of the top archetypes you are likely to face – or play – at Regionals.

**1) Affinity**

The most popular archetype of the day, you won't likely avoid facing this one in Regionals. Affinity decks are built almost exclusively out of *Mirrodin* and *Darksteel* cards and are set to abuse the artifact theme of these sets. Key cards for this deck are [Arcbound Ravager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager), [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) and [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault). Many deck lists have been adjusted to include anti-artifact hate in order to help defeat this archetype. While cards like [Shatter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shatter) and [Viridian Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Shaman) are helpful, often the most effective way to combat this deck is to utilize a global effect such as [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix).

**2) Tooth and Nail**

This may well be one of the top 2 or 3 decks in the field, though it is not often recognized as such. Tooth and Nail variants performed incredibly well at Japanese Regionals two weeks ago, and will definitely show up this weekend. This archetype is all about generating mana very quickly and staying alive long enough to cast [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) with Entwine. At this point you will be able to either put a pair of huge creatures into play (think [Darksteel Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus)), or drop a [Leonin Abunas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Abunas) / [Platinum Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Platinum+Angel) combo that most aggressive decks won't be able to deal with.

This deck cannot do much of anything unless it is able to generate a lot of mana, fast. Attacking its mana base with cards like [Stone Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain) or [Death Cloud](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud) is often the most effective way to thwart its plans.

**3) Goblin Bidding**

Goblin strategies used to have no late game. It was win quickly or lose the late game. Goblin Bidding variants come out almost as fast early on, but can also come back from a losing board position to win the game with a large [Patriarch's Bidding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Bidding). [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) is very good at helping set up an even larger Bidding in the mid-game. [Circle of Protection: Red](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circle+of+Protection%3A+Red) and other sideboard spoilers are effective against this deck though it can still often overcome quality sideboard strategies due to its speed, consistency, and power level.

Like most control decks, this one is far more vulnerable early in the game than later on.

### Play of the Week

Courtesy of Amy Derks

I have been playing **Magic** for about ten years. About nine years ago, I met a guy named Regi. One of our games went like this:  
 My first turn: lay Island  
 His first turn: lay Mountain, [Mox Ruby](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Ruby), [Mox Emerald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Emerald), [Black Lotus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Lotus). He sacked the Lotus for three red mana and summoned a [Ball Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning). He then tapped Mountain and [Mox Ruby](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Ruby) to play [Blood Lust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Lust) on the [Ball Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning) and [Mox Emerald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Emerald) to [Berserk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserk) it. He attacked for 20.  
 Me: tap Island, [Unsummon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon)[Ball Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ball+Lightning)!

Needless to say, I burst his bubble. We still got married three years later when he finally forgave me.

![Goblin Charbelcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Charbelcher)Courtesy of Scott SmithI was watching two friends play each other in a weekly draft. One player was playing b/w and had out 3-4 creatures about to attack for the win the next turn. The other player was playing a 3 color deck (g/b/r) and had cast three spells. [Sylvan Scying](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Scying), a [Journey of Discovery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+of+Discovery) and a [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher).

Needless to say, with lethal damage on the stack the guy had no other choice but to point the Charbelcher and pray. After the first 10 cards were turned over he only needed the mountain which was two cards away for the win. The funniest part was that he was splashing three mountains. One in play, one in his hand, and the other to double 12 damage to the dome =)

### Bad Play of the Week

Courtesy of Vasily

This happened in Moscow during a draft.   
 My teammate and member of Russian National Team Eugenius Idzikovsky played against someone in the 3rd round of swiss. He had [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) on the table and [Domineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domineer) in his hand. Opponent was at 1 life and played [Bottle Gnomes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottle+Gnomes) and [Frogmite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frogmite) on his turn. The Gnomes weren't very helpful because if he sacrificed them he would die from Disciple damage before gaining life.   
 Eugenius thought hard and ... played [Domineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domineer) on the Frogmite!
