Welcome to the Bash
===================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on December 11, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







This is the obvious: it's Rakdos Week and the "last week" of articles in 2012 for me. There are going to be a few surprises over the two-week break, and *Gatecrash* previews will begin firing on New Year's Eve. These are great reasons to throw a party. 

So let's talk Rakdos:


*"Members of Rakdos are driven by a desire to entertain themselves and they don't care who they hurt along the way."*

 I play a lot of Commander. It's the way to play that delights me the most. I get the power and randomness of Cube, the social action of multiplayer, and the pride of packing my coolest cards, all in one shot. I've written about "[the Social Contract](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/commander-laws-public-gatherings-2012-10-30)," [using all commons](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/cheap-second-date-2011-08-09), and [using *mostly* commons and uncommons](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/scavenging-more-2012-12-04). I even [created a database for around seventy submitted decks](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/fun-numbers-2012-07-31). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_slaughter_games.jpg)
[Slaughter Games](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) | Art by [Steve Prescott](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Steve%20Prescott%22%5D)


So in the spirit of Rakdos I'm going to do whatever the hell I want this week. It's my party, so sit tight and join in the fun.

###  Temporal Manipulation

 Commander, and multiplayer games in general, often take too damn long. While I love hanging with buddies, I want to play more than two games over the course of four hours. I can squeeze three games and a deck-building session into [Limited lunch](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/lunch-gathering-magic-2011-12-27). One game bleeding longer than an extended cut of *Lord of the Rings* is unacceptable to me. 

 There's a peculiar kind of Commander player I often avoid but actually enjoy more than I've admitted. This player packs the heat, brings the missiles to a gun fight, and detonates action faster than a Michael Bay film. He or she has decks build around [Kaervek the Merciless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaervek+the+Merciless); [Heartless Hidetsugu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Hidetsugu); and [Maga, Traitor to Mortals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maga%2C+Traitor+to+Mortals). This player channels the fury and passion of Rakdos, and whether you like it or not you're along for the ride. That ride is always quick, one way or another. 

I want to tap into that primal glee and just watch the world burn.

Speeding games up while doing what I've secretly wanted to do for a while is just the type of passionate exhibition Rakdos would approve. In fact, he'd demand it and provide suggestions for "improvement."

What do I mean?

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_combo1.jpg)
*[Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake), [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster), [Comet Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm), [Fault Line](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fault+Line), [Exsanguinate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exsanguinate)*
  
 I mean going big to send everybody home. [Earthquake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) is the classic example, but modern riffs like [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster) and [Comet Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Comet+Storm) are twists that I enjoy. [Exsanguinate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exsanguinate) is now a staple in "[Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth) plus [Cabal Coffers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Coffers)" decks, but you don't need only black mana to make it worth casting (unlike its distant cousins [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) and [Drain Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drain+Life)). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_combo2.jpg)
*[Caged Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caged+Sun), [Nirkana Revenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nirkana+Revenant), [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power), [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), [Rakdos Keyrune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune)*
  
 Making all that mana can be easy. While [Nirkana Revenant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nirkana+Revenant) and [Caged Sun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caged+Sun) let us double up on a color, [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum) and [Rakdos Keyrune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune) are the grease that makes the wheels turn easily. Making as much mana as possible will empower a lot of big things. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_combo3.jpg)
*[Liliana Vess](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess), [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), [Praetor's Grasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Praetor%27s+Grasp), [Demonic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Tutor), [Recoup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoup)*
  
 When we're throwing our own party it makes sense to have favors. Depending upon who shows up and what we come across during the game, going out to find something special is awfully generous. [Liliana Vess](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess) is an expert at getting what she wants, but [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) and [Demonic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Tutor) are the eBay and Amazon of our deck. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_combo4.jpg)
*[Decree of Pain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Pain), [Dregs of Sorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregs+of+Sorrow), [Incendiary Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incendiary+Command), [Reforge the Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reforge+the+Soul), [Greed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greed)*
  
 I enjoy having things to do at parties. As the host, including ways to keep our hand full of fun is a sharp move. That way, we'll always have something more to share. [Incendiary Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incendiary+Command) and [Reforge the Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reforge+the+Soul) has everyone join in on the fun, but a well-timed [Dregs of Sorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dregs+of+Sorrow) or [Decree of Pain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Decree+of+Pain) is more powerful. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_combo5.jpg)
*[Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold), [Leyline of Punishment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Punishment), [Furnace of Rath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+of+Rath), [Wound Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wound+Reflection), [Havoc Festival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havoc+Festival)*
  
 No party is complete without bowls of punch. We're going to serve real punches instead. [Havoc Festival](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havoc+Festival) and [Wound Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wound+Reflection) is a potent one-two, but [Stranglehold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stranglehold), [Furnace of Rath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+of+Rath), and [Leyline of Punishment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Punishment) are all equally painful for our friends. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_combo6.jpg)
*[Slum Reaper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slum+Reaper), [Killing Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Killing+Wave), [Dread Cacodemon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Cacodemon), [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder), [Massacre Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm)*
  
 Perhaps the most interesting party game is present in every game of **Magic**: [Red Rover](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Rover). The goal is simple: one team sends in somebody to break through the other team's line. For **Magic**, I like to shortcut this by taking out anyone who can be on an opposing team. [Slum Reaper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slum+Reaper) and [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder) are quick ways to take out one blocker, but [Killing Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Killing+Wave) and [Dread Cacodemon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Cacodemon)can make entire teams vanish. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf225_combo7.jpg)
*[Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play), [Steel Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Hellkite), [Anathemancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anathemancer), [Temporal Extortion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Extortion), [Stuffy Doll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll)*
  
 While inviting everyone to a party is fair game, there are times when someone special should show up. Cards like [Stuffy Doll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll) and [Anathemancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anathemancer) can highlight someone deserving. [Steel Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Hellkite) and [Devil's Play](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play) lay down a red carpet for the stars.

### Holidays are Never Long Enough

Why am I passionate enough about the speed of games to build a burn-based Commander deck? Pace of play is important to having fun. There are some players who are prone to "tank"â€”stop, think, and calculate their next actionsâ€”in every game they play.

I know this as I do it in Commander too often.

Whether I chalk it up as habit from my more competitive Cube-playing or an unfortunate side effect of handling the volume of information Commander games present, the fact remains that I find games take longer and I'm contributing to it. Players with powerful decks and plentiful choices are prone to taking time, and that's why I actively looks for ways to keep things moving.

### A Whole Lot of Not Caring

Just like we're all individuals with different perspectives, how we handle ourselves in multiplayer games is on a wide spectrum. It's easy to believe there's one, true way to engage other players, but it's more personal preference than textbook analysis.

The spirit of celebrating with Rakdos isn't about defining the right way to do something, but ensuring whatever you do is something you enjoyâ€”consequences be damned. Blow up the world, throw down the damage, and create chaos out of order if that's what you want. If everyone chose to play the same way games would be boring.

Just don't be surprised or offended if others avoid playing a role in your carnival.
