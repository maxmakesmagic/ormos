Top 5 Moments
=============



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on March 19, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











There were plenty of great moments, decks, players, cards, and stories over the course of the weekend here in Shizuoka. The following are my top five picks for things to remember from this Grand Prix.


**5. Mardu with five-drops establishes itself as the best deck in the format**


Going into the event, many people expected the metagame to be dominated by Mardu variants and Four-color Saheeli. That prediction largely came true, although in contrast to last weekend's Grand Prix events (which were won by Four-color Saheeli) the best-performing archetype in Shizuoka was Mardu.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_GPShizuoka2017_Moment-5_Metagame.jpg)


Even control masters like No. 2 Shota Yasooka chose the deck, and it put six players in the Top 8, including eventual winner Ryohei Kirino. A possible reason for Mardu's dominance this weekend might be that after many weeks of evolution, the perfect version may have been found.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Archangel+Avacyn)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Avacyn%2C+the+Purifier)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%2C+the+Purifier)Transform [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Painful+Truths)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painful+Truths)
[Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn) was included in the main deck of every single Mardu deck in the Top 8. She can pressure planeswalkers and profitably block [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran), and as a flash threat, she allows you to keep mana open to disrupt the Copy Cat combo. As Mike Sigrist wrote in [his article](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/building-the-perfect-vehicles-list-archangel-mardu/) earlier this week, "[Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn) is the single most important innovation for Mardu Vehicles moving forward."


In the sideboard, the planeswalker plan with [Oath of Liliana](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Liliana), [Nahiri, the Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nahiri%2C+the+Harbinger), and [Ob Nixilis Reignited](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ob+Nixilis+Reignited) is well-known by now, but the best-performing Mardu players in Shizuoka took the control plan even further. [Painful Truths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painful+Truths) was cast in both Semifinals; I saw multiple [Stasis Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stasis+Snare)s exiling [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar); [Fumigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumigate) and [Sorin, Grim Nemesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Grim+Nemesis) also showed up; and I spotted the resurgence of [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) as a way to improve against Saheeli decks.


When you play against Mardu in upcoming weeks, don't make the mistake of boarding against an aggro deck—In Game 2 and 3, your opponent will almost surely be closer to the control spectrum.

Combining the best of Ben Stark's Jund Aggro deck and Toru Inoue's Black-Red Eldrazi deck, Itou's deck is a delight. With so much of Standard coming down to planeswalker battles, [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran) and [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher) are perfectly positioned planeswalker killers.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Heart+of+Kiran)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Reality+Smasher)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher)
Meanwhile, he still has access to the powerful [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) / [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) synergy, while his plethora of artifacts enable [Unlicensed Disintegration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration) and [Spire of Industry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spire+of+Industry). The land helps cast [Reality Smasher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Smasher), which can even come out on turn four via [Catacomb Sifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Catacomb+Sifter). In the present format where you never want to give your opponent the opportunity to activate a given planeswalker twice, Inoue's deck with haste creatures and direct damage might have all the right tools.

Yukio Matsuda was at 9-1 at some point, and he got there with 23 basic Swamps. His deck can be classified as a Zombie deck: [Cryptbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptbreaker), [Relentless Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Dead), and [Haunted Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunted+Dead) all generate card advantage and offer graveyard synergies. In the late game, [Yahenni, Undying Partisan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yahenni%2C+Undying+Partisan) along with [Relentless Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Dead) is another powerful engine.


Matsuda's deck is a fun, yet competitive option for people who enjoy having access to their graveyard (and their opponent's decks, via [Gonti, Lord of Luxury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gonti%2C+Lord+of+Luxury)) as a resource.

**3. Grand Prix Shizuoka was a three-day celebration of the game**


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_GPShizuoka2017_Moment-3_Full-venue-from-above.jpg)


Over 2,700 players entered the main event, but the Grand Prix offered much more than that. As always, there were side events in all kinds of formats, artists who were happy to sign your cards, dealer booths to complete your deck, and much more.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_gpshizuoka2017_Moment3_a.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_gpshizuoka2017_Moment3_b.jpg)

**2. Two particularly sweet plays stand out**


A lot of great games and awesome plays occurred over the weekend. It's hard to pick highlights, but I have two.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_Ishkanah.jpeg)![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_player1_3.jpg)


First, Australia's Justin Robb, who played Ben Friedman's Delirium Saheeli deck, found a creative way to victory in Round 11: blink Ishkanah twice, then activate for 10 life loss!


He had to jump through some hoops to achieve it—such as finding his singleton Ishkanah in the first place, ultimating [Nahiri, the Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nahiri%2C+the+Harbinger) for [Felidar Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Guardian) to get another blink, and having [Servant of the Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+the+Conduit) for black mana—but it clearly showed that the deck can win without the Copy Cat combo.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_Distended%20Mindbender.jpeg)![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_player2_3.jpg)


The other highlight was selected by NicoNico commentator Tomohiro Kaji. He told me that in Round 9, Takehiro Fujimoto emerged [Distended Mindbender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distended+Mindbender) by sacrificing…Skysovereign, Consul Flagship. That has got to be one of the weirdest sacrifices ever. I'm not even sure how it's supposed to work—the consulate offers a flying ship, and suddenly an Eldrazi appears from the Blind Eternities?


Either way, Fujimoto used the cast trigger to take a [Felidar Guardian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Felidar+Guardian) and a [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning) from his opponent's hand, and that was enough for him to win the match and advance to 9-0.

**1. Ryohei Kirino took the trophy...in his very first Grand Prix ever!**


When Ryohei Kirino entered the venue earlier on Saturday morning, this is what he saw.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_GPShizuoka2017_Moment-1_Entrance.jpg)


The 27-year-old from Okayama, Japan had only picked up the game last year with the release of Eldritch Moon. Immediately fascinated by this game, he began to visit local game stores and won a Grand Prix Trial in less than half a year. This weekend marked the very first Grand Prix he ever played.


To select his deck, he put his trust in the wisdom of a Pro Tour Hall of Famer: No. 3 Owen Turtenwald.



> 
> For those curious about the list I played at [#GPNJ](https://twitter.com/hashtag/GPNJ?src=hash) to a t16 finish. The best version of the best deck in standard. [pic.twitter.com/OgfOQh9sTZ](https://t.co/OgfOQh9sTZ)
> 
> 
> — Owen Turtenwald (@OwenTweetenwald) [March 15, 2017](https://twitter.com/OwenTweetenwald/status/841832545851383808)


Equipped with the right deck, he finished Day 1 with a 9-0 record and acclimatized himself to the atmosphere at the top tables. After losing his first match, he was able to regain his calm and play as usual. Once he made the Top 8, he relaxed and focused on having fun. Nevertheless, he made strong gameplay decisions that impressed me when I watched him. For instance, in the [Finals](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpshi17/finals-ryohei-kirino-vs-qi-wentao-2017-03-19) he expertly used his removal spells to achieve board advantage in the key turns of the game.


Eventually, after two days of competition, the trophy that Kirino saw on Saturday morning belonged to him. "I cannot believe it," he said. "Everything I saw, everything I experienced this weekend was fresh. I'm very fortunate to have such a great Grand Prix debut."


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/gpshi17/m_GPShizuoka2017_Moment-1_Kirino.jpg)


Surely, all the events of today will be in Kirino's memory forever. It's the kind of Cinderella story that keeps us all dreaming. Congratulations again to Ryohei Kirino, your Grand Prix Shizuoka 2017 champion!
