Walk the Walk
=============



 Posted in **Limited Information**
 on April 3, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_256.png)
By Quentin Martin












There's nothing like analyzing how you draft to really dissect your Limited game. There are often lots of little errors in people's draft methods that distinguish between a good and a perfect (if at all achievable) draft. Upon reflection, there are often other mistakes made that are completely subjective to that draft, be it veering away from a draft order or whether to go with power or synergy.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/magic/images/cardart/in/mages_contest.jpg)


Most of my draft walkthroughs will be from an 8-4 queue on Magic Online. These are essentially packed with more difficult opponents and as such the quality of the games and the draft itself are better. This is actually better for you if you hope to improve your drafting skills as the signals will be more reliable, rare drafting will occur less (as the incentive is to make the final, as opposed to just winning the first round) and you will be better rewarded for making the correct picks.


However, most people draft in an environment much like that of the 4-3-2-2 queue, so for my first installment I will try to examine the minor nuances that this environment brings. These will also often be similar to most PTQ Top 8 drafts where there was a small field to start with or a general lack of high quality players. I've heard it said that drafting online is much like a Catch-22, whereby, in order to improve you should draft in the 8-4, but you seemingly have less chance of making the finals so will often lose more product in the effort to improve, and so, if on a low budget, will often not play enough Limited to improve.


This is not true. Most people reckon they can squeeze one win out of a draft deck, and hence play the 4-3-2-2 so that they get two boosters back and can keep drafting. However, it is likely that if your deck is capable of winning the first round, it has at least an okay shot of winning the next, so that you make the finals. If you are not that great a drafter, your decks will often be erratic – some very bad and others good, where you were in the right colors at the right time. If you get one of these good decks, you have a far greater chance of winning two than you do of winning one with the bad deck. In an 8-4, you only need to win two before you can intentionally draw the finals and receive six boosters. You will generally only need to reach the final of an 8-4, two wins necessary, once for every three wins you achieve in a 4-3-2-2! You need one extra win in a 4-3-2-2 to win the same amount...


Added to this is the fact that even if you get quite good and make the finals of a 4-3-2-2 quite regularly, and even win it – you only break even. Sure you get some rares, but they probably average around one tix a draft, meaning you have to win to actually make the slightest of profits. It is only the 8-4 queue that offers the chance to make a profit.


MTGO economics aside, to the Draft er.... Cave!

### Pack 1, Pick 1


  
[Viscerid Deepwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscerid+Deepwalker), [Ironclaw Buzzardiers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ironclaw+Buzzardiers), [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow), [Icatian Crier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Crier), [Dark Withering](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Withering), [Goblin Skycutter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Skycutter), [Cyclopean Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclopean+Giant), [Brass Gnat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Gnat), [Savage Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Thallid), [Screeching Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Sliver), [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor), [Phantom Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Wurm), [Plated Pegasus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Pegasus), [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor), [Avoid Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avoid+Fate)
Nothing awe-inspiring about this pack, and straight off the bat it leaves us with a decision to make. The three most powerful cards are [Dark Withering](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Withering), [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow), and [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor). There's not too much power difference between these cards, so it can often come down to color preference. You could run the black gambit and go with the Withering, but I feel that [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor) edges the others out slightly in power, and I like blue to boot.


Pick: [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor)


Aside: During the first few picks, once I have decided what card I will pick, I work out how many solid playables there are in the pack to see if I know what will wheel. It is often the late picks and the foreknowledge that something as simple as a [Basal Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basal+Sliver) or a [Jedit's Dragoons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jedit%27s+Dragoons) will wheel, that will determine which card you might pick earlier.


**Pack 1, Pick 2**  
[Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy), [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal), [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse), [Drudge Reavers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Reavers), [Psychotic Episode](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychotic+Episode), [Jedit's Dragoons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jedit%27s+Dragoons), [Sprout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprout), [Foriysian Interceptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foriysian+Interceptor), [Basalt Gargoyle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basalt+Gargoyle), [Celestial Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Crusader), [Saltcrusted Steppe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saltcrusted+Steppe), [Magus of the Candelabra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Candelabra), [Flying Men](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flying+Men), [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains)


A very poor booster. Here we have the option of [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) or [Celestial Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Crusader). Early on in a draft, I like to pick cards with potential. A powerful [Might Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+Sliver) or [Psionic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Sliver) in an empty pack will often shape the draft. This normally leads to my first few picks being in different colors, but it means that I have the highest chance for a high-power deck, whilst also getting the most out of the color signals of the drafter on the right. With this in consideration, the oft-overrated [Celestial Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Crusader) is our pick of choice.


Pick: [Celestial Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Crusader)


**Pack 1, Pick 3**  
[Aether Web](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Web), [Cloudchaser Kestrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudchaser+Kestrel), [Drudge Reavers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Reavers), [Goblin Skycutter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Skycutter), [Cancel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), [Drifter *il*-Dal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drifter+il-Dal), [Subterranean Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Subterranean+Shambler), [Mindlash Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindlash+Sliver), [Havenwood Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havenwood+Wurm), [Telekinetic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telekinetic+Sliver), [Paradise Plume](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradise+Plume), [Curse of the Cabal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+the+Cabal), [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm)


Another weak pack, but this time bringing a tight choice. The potential and synergy of our two options are both high. Either the [Cloudchaser Kestrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudchaser+Kestrel) to follow the heavy white theme of the [Crusade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade)r or the [Telekinetic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telekinetic+Sliver) to open the doors to the powerful white-blue Sliver archetype. This pick took me a lot of time and I eventually decided on the Kestrel. I haven't seen any solid blue cards since my [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor), and it is likely that it is being cut.


Pick: [Cloudchaser Kestrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudchaser+Kestrel)


**Pack 1, Pick 4**  
[Strength in Numbers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+in+Numbers), [Zealot il-Vec](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Zealot%2Bil-Vec), [Snapback](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapback), [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink), [Ancient Grudge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge), [Greenseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenseeker), [Foriysian Interceptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foriysian+Interceptor), [Basal Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basal+Sliver), [Voidmage Husher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Husher), [Firewake Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firewake+Sliver), [Hail Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hail+Storm), [Penumbra Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Penumbra+Spider)


Several options here. [Penumbra Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Penumbra+Spider) is a more powerful card than Hailstorm or [Strength in Numbers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+in+Numbers) and [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) is more powerful than [Snapback](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapback). This narrows it down a little, but leaves us once more with a conundrum. Blink and Spider are of similar power levels but the problem is that I haven't had any particularly strong signals for either color to use as a guideline. I have yet to take a green card, and the only signal so far was the [Telekinetic Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telekinetic+Sliver), so I take the Blink to try and bring my [Fledgling Mawcor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fledgling+Mawcor) back into focus.


Pick: [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink)


**Pack 1, Pick 5**  
[Ironclaw Buzzardiers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ironclaw+Buzzardiers), [Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy), [Mogg War Marshal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal), [Psychotic Episode](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychotic+Episode), [Sprout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprout), [Aetherflame Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherflame+Wall), [Pit Keeper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pit+Keeper), [Scryb Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger), [Spirit Loop](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Loop), [Restore Balance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restore+Balance), [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack)


Looks like green is open. [Scryb Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger) is a very powerful card and easily good enough to take over the far weaker [Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy). Success in this format seems to be determined by a good reading of the signals. Let's just hope this one is right.


Pick: [Scryb Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger)


**Pack 1, Pick 6**  
[Ashcoat Bear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashcoat+Bear), [Cloudchaser Kestrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudchaser+Kestrel), [Thallid Germinator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid+Germinator), [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr), [Divine Congregation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Congregation), [Mindlash Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindlash+Sliver), [Thallid Shell-Dweller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid+Shell-Dweller), [Jhoira's Timebug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhoira%27s+Timebug), [Tectonic Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Fiend), [Dralnu, Lich Lord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dralnu%2C+Lich+Lord)


This pack is a joyful sight but comes with mixed blessings. It shows that both of our colors, green and white, are open. The Kestrel is the strongest card here, especially given that we already have a [Crusade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade)r. The only problem is that we are passing three green cards, giving poor signals to the guy on our left. This is normally never a strong enough reason to not pick the best card for you. I do not expect to see much green from the left, having passed [Strength in Numbers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+in+Numbers), Hailstorm, and [Penumbra Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Penumbra+Spider) on top of the cards in this pack.


Pick: [Cloudchaser Kestrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudchaser+Kestrel)


**Pack 1, Pick 7**  
[Pentarch Ward](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentarch+Ward), [Thallid Germinator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid+Germinator), [Zealot il-Vec](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Zealot%2Bil-Vec), [Chameleon Blur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Blur), [Bewilder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bewilder), [Detainment Spell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detainment+Spell), [Spell Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst), [Honorable Passage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honorable+Passage), [Thrill of the Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrill+of+the+Hunt)


An easy pick as [Thrill of the Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrill+of+the+Hunt) is just so powerful, but it is yet again a pity to be shipping another green card so late.


Pick: [Thrill of the Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrill+of+the+Hunt)

[Errant Doomsayers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Doomsayers), [Corpulent Corpse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpulent+Corpse), [Aetherflame Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherflame+Wall), [Detainment Spell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detainment+Spell), [Thallid Shell-Dweller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid+Shell-Dweller), [Bewilder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bewilder), [Call to the Netherworld](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld), [Skittering Monstrosity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skittering+Monstrosity)


It should be noted that the [Corpulent Corpse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpulent+Corpse) indicates that black might be open, but as we saw no early strength in that color and are now happily entrenched in our colors, we can safely ignore it. Here we are offered a choice in two drops. White-green favors aggression over defense, and the [Errant Doomsayers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Doomsayers) is better in this department.


Pick: [Errant Doomsayers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Doomsayers)

[Icatian Crier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Crier), [Cyclopean Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclopean+Giant), [Brass Gnat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Gnat), [Savage Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Thallid), [Screeching Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Sliver), [Plated Pegasus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Pegasus), [Avoid Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avoid+Fate)


I'm normally never a fan of [Icatian Crier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Crier), but given that we already have a [Celestial Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Crusader), it is easy to pick over the potential sideboard card – [Avoid Fate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avoid+Fate).


Pick: [Icatian Crier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Crier)


**Pack 1, Pick 10**  
[Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy), [Drudge Reavers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Reavers), [Psychotic Episode](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychotic+Episode), [Sprout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprout), [Foriysian Interceptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foriysian+Interceptor), [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains)


It unlikely that [Sprout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprout) will make our deck given that we have shipped so many [Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid)s and have yet to pick up any [Herd Gnarr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herd+Gnarr)s or [Primal Forcemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Forcemage)s, so I pick the [Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy) as it can often ruin white-green's aggressive starts.


Pick: [Temporal Eddy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Eddy)


**Pack 1, Pick 11**  
[Aether Web](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Web), [Drudge Reavers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Reavers), [Drifter *il*-Dal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drifter+il-Dal), [Havenwood Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havenwood+Wurm), [Paradise Plume](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paradise+Plume)


This pick is close given our relative lack of combat tricks, but now that we have two [Cloudchaser Kestrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudchaser+Kestrel)s, the [Aether Web](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Web) seems weaker than the [Havenwood Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Havenwood+Wurm).

The quarters were rather uneventful as my opponent was quite bad and my deck was really good. The [Icatian Crier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Crier) won me one of the games on the back of [Scryb Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scryb+Ranger) – making rather more creatures than seemed fair. I overran him with my good quality fliers in the other whilst [Saltfield Recluse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saltfield+Recluse) held the ground.


I lost a near un-losable game in the semis to a [Fortune Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortune+Thief) when I had Gaea's Anthem and [Sacred Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Mesa) out against a red-black mage, but I destroyed him in the third game thanks to a sexy draw involving [Essence Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden), [Icatian Crier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icatian+Crier), [Uktabi Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uktabi+Drake) and two [Herd Gnarr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herd+Gnarr)s.


I lost the first game of the final to a 7/7 [Errant Ephemeron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Errant+Ephemeron) (thanks to a [Spike Tiller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Tiller)), and my slow draw couldn't race him.
