Hallelujah—It's Raining Men
===========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 17, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored* and its *Innistrad* Block Constructed format did what they were supposed to do. No one can say that we as readers and viewers, fans of the game, weren't given a rare treat, even for the Pro Tour. Some of the greatest in the game returned to the top tables on Day Three, we got to check out a variety of new and different decks...

... and the draw step was made more exciting than ever!

As such, the mandate of the Pro Tour (and excitement surrounding this fine last set of this fine block) were all fulfilled. Miracles happen... and they can happen in the finals of the Pro Tour, even (not to mention the deciding Game 5 of the quarterfinals)!

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td195_hay.jpg)"Insane" Hayne ran quite the crazy deck, didn't he?

Alex played a deck that was basically [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage); tons of lands... and miracles. He had a superb chance of topdecking a miracle as his first card many and most turns, just on account of playing sixteen of them.

Some of the cards are obviously useful, like [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus). It can clear any number of oncoming beaters as a late game six... or it can miracle away an annoying (and usually undying) [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) on the second turn.

Instant-speed card drawing is fantastically high value in Hayne's deck. With so many miracles, his [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)s were more than "just cheap" and his [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice)s were more than just an obvious route to card advantage. These inexpensive spells gave Alex the chance to score miracles on the other player's turn. And when he missed? Hey, nothing has kept these spells from being playable cards regardless (can there be a more insane end game than [Moon Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage) + [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)?).

Now... speaking of miracles?



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

[Entreat the Angels](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entreat+the+Angels)! Hayne took down the Pro Tour with a dramatic set of Angels... 20 power, all in the air, that hadn't been there a moment ago. At its worst, [Entreat the Angels](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entreat+the+Angels) is kind of an [Air Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Air+Elemental)/[Broodmate Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broodmate+Dragon) split card. And at its best? It can really turn the game on its end.

The card that differentiated Hayne's deck isn't actually a miracle at all. Alexander's Team ManaDeprived.com teammates have called it the best card in the deck... and it certainly is a different card that most teams would never have stumbled upon: [Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread).

*"[Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) is like a [Time Walk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) with flashback."*  
 —Alexander Hayne

So the deck can get lucky and exploit that luck, especially going very long. It can smash you for 20 in the air or take all the turns (even consecutively). But what about early? Rush decks—and we have spent the past few weeks talking about the various fast attack decks—can give control decks trouble in *Innistrad* Block.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td195_fee.jpg)[Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) | Art by [John Stanko](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22John+Stanko%22%5D)

[Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) steals these decks' value in the early game, buying time so Hayne's white-blue control can live long enough to "take all the turns," one of which includes having a critical number of 4/4 flyers on the battlefield. So that is how a non-miracle, seemingly unplayable draft common could be the difference that makes the difference and define an otherwise super-powerful miracle deck.

**Naya Variants**

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td195_cho.jpg)We can start with Joshua Cho's Naya deck, which, but for a couple of copies of [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned), was permanents-tacular.

The Naya strategy principally utilizes acceleration from green ([Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212) and [Scorned Villager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorned+Villager)) to jump to the high-quality four-drops across green, red, and white (everything from [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) to [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) to [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) + its many tricks).

The quality of drops in Naya is obviously strong, with many cards including natural card advantage: [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) can help fix your mana while drawing cards (notably you can cast a [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) off a [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) without being able to play, say, [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) for want of a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)); [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) does two different things when it hits the battlefield (and even more when left to its own devices); and [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) (in Cho's build) makes for a removal tool that can actually tangle with large creatures, like [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart).

All the Naya decks that made Top 8 played four copies of what many are calling the best card in *Avacyn Restored*. [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) can smash down an additional 12 power... and there isn't a whole heck of a lot most decks can do about it. Tap it with [Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread), maybe (if you are an innovative, tool-finding, genius)... but [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) can remove it only at great cost, and both [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) and [Brimstone Volley](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) need a little something-something (even if that is just the right set of dominoes falling) to finish the job.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td195_rac.jpg)Chopping down a color from the Naya team was Denniz Rachid and his green-white deck.

Going straight green-white allowed Denniz to increase the number of [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township)s he could use while still playing four copies of turn-one fixing [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls). While certain of the green-white deck's options look a bit restrictive ([Druid's Familiar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Druid%27s+Familiar) over [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), [Divine Deflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Deflection) over [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)), you can't argue with the explosiveness of [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) when backed up immediately by [Gather the Townsfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+the+Townsfolk); [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben); or even [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td195_yuk.jpg)Bouncing from the littlest beatdown to probably the biggest in the Top 8, we have Ken Yukuhiro's four-color Reanimator.

Of all the decks that performed, this one seems the "coolest" to me, with multiple awesome and exciting lines of play. Consider...


> * Straight fatties reanimator—Ken could fix his hand (and build his bin) with [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch)... and then [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) up a [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand). [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) is *naturally* about the biggest monster on the block, and in the unlikely event that it is dealt with, hey... you can still draw seven.
> * Humans combo reanimator—Ken could trade his [Cathedral Sanctifier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Sanctifier)s (kind of like Hayne's [Feeling of Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread)) early, play [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) to develop, then chump, find what he needed, build up, loot, [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), whatever... then bam! [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise)! His Humans subtheme could combine with [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) for extra damage (with built-in recoup) to gain extra enters-the-battlefield cards or life, [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) timing tricks, etc.
> * [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) to race—In a format where much of the fight is on the ground, don't underestimate the ability to go 4 high, hastily. [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) make for perfect ground-stall companions, blocking while building up the Aristocrat, whether or not an eventual [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) is in the cards.

Look back on the Naya decks various in the previous sections.

What colors was the ManaDeprived.com Hallelujah deck? Separately, what did we call that deck's defining "difference that made the difference?"

Yes, yes, the green-white, for all its merits, lacked a bit in the point-removal department... [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter)s, sure, but [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart)s in the sideboard only to keep pace.

God bless... err, *Angels* bless?... um, [Griselbrand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) curse Ken Yukuhiro and his [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) reanimator deck... just for putting up a little black representation at the top end of the *Innistrad* Block field. That said, [big Gris](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) and [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) don't exactly kill a creature. Thematically, the long night of Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored* seems decidedly *not* dark, nor full of Terrors of any kind.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td195_gei.jpg)[Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) | Art by [Igor Kieryluk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Igor+Kieryluk%22%5D)

What is great about the SCGBlack deck? This awareness of not just the power of [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart), but the conceptual bet that few were going to be able to deal with it. At least, not on time.

Sure, other squads figured out the Silverheart, some even played four despite the late curve eruption on five (most of the other green decks in this Top 8, but not all, and certainly not across the board in the rest of the meta). But what did Black do differently?



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |


> * Few of their guys or Geists presented particularly attractive removal targets. Sure, a miraculous [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) is going to sweep up [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) in a tragic two-for-one... but against the general absence of appropriate removal? [Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) gonna get ya. Plus, a well-placed bounce spell or balloon pants can make Saint Traft positively painful... even when you think you have a blocker. Is the potential relevance of the block's "[Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock)" on a Pilgrim going to stop an [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker), suited? You can stretch this question to the various hexproof and undying guys or Geists on the team; tempo, resilience, and the ability to best leverage the biggest Wolf by having a hard-to-hit/hard-to-kill/evasive soulbond partner in play make Bant an especial threat.
> * It wouldn't be a Finkel/Black Geist story without [Dungeon Geists](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists). The presence of this creature plus the inclusion of [Silent Departure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Departure) give Bant more and better opportunities to win the Wolfir war. No, neither are instants that can break up soulbond, but combined with Strangleroot's ability to jump to its death twice, Bant has tools to catch up when the opponent has the faster Silverheart draw; note this is particularly rewarding for a player like Finkel, who has an unusual facility for playing with his back against the wall. Compare this with some other Silverheart decks that might not even have [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter), ergo very little promise of getting out of jail (free or no). Bounce actually makes for some pretty good defensive margin against reanimation, where the fact that a big guy is in play isn't necessarily indicative of its being *played*.
> * Bant can play an essentially non-interactive combo game. In a world of mostly beatdown, slight control, and a minority Tinker-esque reanimator, the tool of Pilgrim into Stalker into [Increasing Savagery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Increasing+Savagery) is quite simply savage. The Bonfire window is brief, and once it is up, the opponent's only option is to face a race.
> 

... but when [Wolfir Silverheart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfir+Silverheart) shows up the following turn?

*You basically need a miracle.*
