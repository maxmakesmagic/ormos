The Ultimate Octopus
====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 16, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar













![Giant Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Giant+Octopus)

G[Giant Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Giant%2BOctopus) is not a terribly exciting card. A vanilla 3/3 Octopus for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) fits poorly into most deck strategies—other colors have beefier creatures for lower costs, and blue rarely wants its own [Hill Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hill+Giant).


If [Giant Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Octopus) happened to be a Merfolk, that would be something. Merfolk decks could definitely use a [Giant Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Octopus), especially when it could be a 4/4 islandwalker with [Lord of Atlantis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+Atlantis) on the table.


Or maybe if [Giant Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Octopus) were a blue 3/3 Elf that you could search onto the table with [Skyshroud Poacher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyshroud+Poacher) . . . now that would be pretty interesting.


Or wouldn't it be funny if [Giant Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Octopus) fit into your Ooze theme deck? Ooze decks definitely need a 3/3 for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif).


I imagine an Octopus Minion would come in handy with [Balthor the Defiled](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balthor+the+Defiled). Or a beefy Octopus Sliver could see use in Extended. An Octopus Kobold would be hilarious and would see play in lots of casual decks.


You probably think I'm going to tell you now that R&D threw a [Giant-Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant-Octopus)-like Illusion creature into the *Legions* set, much like [Mistform Shrieker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Shrieker). You would be right, and you would be very, very wrong.


Meet [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus), a decidedly new twist on Illusions.


 



![Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mistform+Ultimus)

Your first reaction is probably along the lines of "Ha! Cool!" followed by a lot of squinting and maybe a few "Uhhh . . . hmmm . . ."s. At least that was my initial reaction. (And if rule issues surrounding the card confuse you, read "[How It Works](#FAQ)" farther down in this article.) [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) obviously seems wacky enough to build decks around, but how exactly do you use a creature that is at once a Gypsy, an Island-Fish, Sand, a Mosquito, a Shyft, a Phelddagrif, a Yeti, and The-Biggest-Baddest-Nastiest-Scariest-Creature-You'll-Ever-See?


Below are five initial ideas I had about how to use this new fun-loving . . . thing. As you might expect, most of my initial thoughts involved interactions with *Onslaught* cards. [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus)is such a freaky creature, however, that I imagine many more uses will pop up if you put your mind to the task. I hope my own thinking will get you started.


### 1. Tribal . . . Ho!


As I have tried to point out already, probably the coolest thing about [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) is that it fits into every single "tribal" deck. The reason it always fits is that [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) is a Zombie, a Bird, and an Angel whether it's in your library, in your hand, in your graveyard, or in play. You can't alter it with [Artificial Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificial+Evolution) (except to change "Wall" in its text box to something else so the Ultimus can't attack) because its text will override any changes you make. [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) will always be every single legal creature type, ready for every single deck based around a creature type.


So, provided you have access to blue mana, if you're building a Soldier deck, an Orc deck, a thematic Sheep deck . . . you can always drop [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) into it. Always. Every time. [Giant Octopus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Octopus) might rarely get used as an Octopus, but plenty of other creature themes can celebrate a 3/3 for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif).


In particular, this means that creature types without much beef have access to a 3/3 (like Kobolds, Squirrels, and Saprolings). It also means that creature types that normally have few reasonably costed creatures (Dragons, Giants, Beasts, and so on) gain a four-mana option. In addition, [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) makes some creature theme decks (Starfish, Rabbits, Ferrets, and so on) possible where they weren't possible before.


I know Saprolings aren't currently en vogue, but I love those little guys, so here is a modest example. Note that [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) is 4/4 with [Verdeloth the Ancient](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdeloth+the+Ancient) in play, not 5/5. Verdeloth's effect looks for all Treefolk and all Saprolings in play and gives each of those creatures +1/+1; the effect can't apply twice to the same creature. (If you have [Aven Brigadier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Brigadier) in play, on the other hand, [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) is 5/5 because the Brigadier's bonuses are two separate abilities.)"

A final corollary on [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus)'s fit in tribal decks is that now any creature-themed deck has an excellent excuse to splash blue. Counter-Goblin sounds more plausible now that you have a monoblue Goblin to go along with your counterspells and card drawing. Counter-Elf? Counter-Cleric? Counter-Bear? Come on . . . you know you want to try it.

2. Endemic Plague

Here's a neat trick: When you sacrifice [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) as part of [Endemic Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endemic+Plague)'s cost, you automatically wipe the board clean of creatures (except for creatures like [Phyrexian Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Colossus) that don't have a creature type). It's easy to imagine the beginnings of a deck that uses this duo, other Mistform creatures, and some blue "bounce" to save your own critters.


[Patriarch's Bidding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patriarch%27s+Bidding) works well with [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus)—so does [Harsh Mercy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harsh+Mercy). It makes no difference what your opponent chooses, Mistform Ultimus will live to do battle with opposing critters. I imagine there's a deck here, although I can't visualize it quite yet.


Along similar lines, I can think of no better target for [Crown of Suspicion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion)—or any of the *Onslaught* Crowns—than [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus).


### 3. Tribal Golem


[Tribal Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Golem) on the table + [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) on the table = good times.


Even if your 3/3 Legend does nothing else to influence the game, it can single-handedly make your Golem a 4/4 flying, trampling, first-striking, regenerator with haste. In a world no longer populated by [Flametongue Kavu](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu), feel free to get excited about the possibilities here.

### 4. Riptide Shapeshifter


Interestingly enough, when you sacrifice [Riptide Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Shapeshifter) you can *always* put [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) into play. As long as your deck contains no other Nameless-Race, Wolverine-Pack, or Marid creatures, your Shapeshifter will find your Ultimus. This is a luxury if you can put your Ultimus to good use. Hold this idea because I will return to it momentarily.


### 5. Living Wish


Of course, like me, you may be overwhelmed by the possibilities that [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) presents. In that case, I say why not use [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) to ensure that [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus) will be the best creature for the best tribal theme at the best time? If you're playing by casual rules, you can conceivably Wish any creature—[Lord of the Undead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Undead) to fish your Ultimus out of the graveyard, [Aven Brigadier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Brigadier) to give it +2/+2, [Ravenous Baloth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth) to gain life, or [Goblin Wizard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Wizard) to gain protection from white. If you're limiting yourself to Wishing into a sideboard, there is still great opportunity for fun . . .

### How It Works



![Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mistform+Ultimus)

*(From the forthcoming* Legions *FAQ)*


The types "Illusion" and "Legend" are printed on the card's type line purely for flavor. The Ultimus has every other creature type as well.


It's a Legend, so the Legend rule applies to it. It's also a Wall, so the Wall rule applies to it (although it has an ability that allows it to ignore this rule). It's also a Sliver, and so will gain all Sliver abilities.


[Artificial Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificial+Evolution) doesn't affect this creature's types at all, although changing the word "Wall" in the second ability to another type will prevent the Ultimus from attacking because it will still be a Wall.


Like any creature, if the Ultimus "becomes" another type as the result of a spell or ability, it will lose all its other creature types. For example, if your opponent uses [Imagecrafter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) to turn your Ultimus into a Soldier, your Ultimus will be only a Soldier. All its other types will be lost for the rest of the turn.
