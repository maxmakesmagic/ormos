Token Opposition
================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 19, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







Now *this* is a brew. When looking at Legacy results, I first saw the creatures, and wasn't overly surprised. Four [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and four [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) is an interesting start, but not mind-blowing. I moved on over to the spells, and was instantly intrigued. [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition)? [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue)?? [Battle Screech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Screech)??? This is not your typical Legacy deck, and it has enough going on that it's definitely worth investigating. 

![15162](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=15162) At its core, this is a tokens-based deck, and even though it has a million little synergies, they all revolve around that. It's got [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) as the main ways to make tokens, with a miser's [Battle Screech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Screech) in there for [Quiet Speculation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation) value. Pyromancer and Souls are each engines in their own right, so playing four of each does a good job in ensuring that the deck always has tokens to utilize. 

 Once it has all the tokens, it's got plenty of uses for them. [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition) is the main one, and a well-fueled [Opposition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition) can easily lock out most opponents. [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue) is a more damage-based backup, and [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) turns tokens into disruption. 

 The card selection makes it all work, with [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) playing their normal role as smoothers, along with a sweet [Quiet Speculation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quiet+Speculation) that can fetch [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Cabal Therapy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy), and [Battle Screech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Screech). 

 Overall, this looks like quite the fantastical machine, and one that can do a lot of powerful things. The mana is likely its biggest weakness, as being four colors is certainly a cost, but that's something [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) is very good at dealing with.
