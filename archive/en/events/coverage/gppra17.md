Vefa Victorious in Prague
=========================




At the first Grand Prix to feature *Aether Revolt* in Limited, Yusuf Kemal Vefa took the trophy with a green-white deck, defeating Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa in the finals.

Top 8 Coverage



2,005 players travelled to Prague to take part in the first Grand Prix to feature *Aether Revolt* in Limited, making this the Czech Republic's biggest Magic tournament to date. After nine rounds of Sealed Deck, 629 players returned for the Booster Draft portion, and at the end of the Swiss, only eight players remained.


The most experienced players in the Top 8 were Christoffer Larsen, Ivan Floch, and (8) Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, but ultimately it was Yusuf Kemal Vefa, a 43-year-old entrepreneur from Istanbul, who took the trophy. His green-white deck, which was low on revolt synergies but heavy on fliers, triumphed 2-0 over Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa’s black-red deck in the finals.


Overall, the tournament showed *Aether Revolt* Limited to be filled with interesting combos and cool decks, and it ’s just getting started. With Pro Tour Aether Revolt right around the corner in Dublin, there’s plenty more action on the horizon.


Congratulations again to Yusuf Kemal Vefa, your Grand Prix Prague 2017 champion!

Day 2 Coverage



2,005 players had been in the running originally. Now, after the first day's nine rounds of Sealed Deck, this number was down to 629. 629 now sat down for the first *Aether Revolt*/*Kaladesh* Booster Draft at the Grand Prix level, among them many notable pro players who made the trip to Prague in preparation for next week's Pro Tour. Just looking at the people at 8-1 one could find (8) Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, (6) Lukas Blohon, Grzegorz Kowalski, Eric Froehlich, Christian Calcano, Peter Vieren, Ivan Floch, and Immanuel Gerschenson.

Day 1 Coverage



2,005 players travelled to Prague to enter the first Grand Prix to feature *Aether Revolt* in Limited, making this the Czech Republic's biggest *Magic* tournament to date. 629 of them qualified for the second day, including seven players at 9-0: Tamas Horvath, Eli Gammaitoni, Haokang Wang, Andrea Bignami, Michael Meier, Dominik Konieczny, and Ryan Sandrin.
