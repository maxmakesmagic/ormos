Grand Prix - Bologna
====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on April 29, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_authorpic_larger.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











The **Magic: The Gathering** Grand Prix tournament series is an international circuit of large-scale Qualifier tournaments featuring cash prizes and multiple invitations to Pro Tour events.

**Win a trip to Grand Prix Nottingham**  
 ...and many other **Magic** prizes!  
 At Grand Prix Bologna fill out the voucher\* that you'll find at the DCI booth and deposit it in the designated box before Saturday, June 11– 5 pm. A draw will be held on Saturday, June 11 at 6pm.

* 1st prize: Trip to Grand Prix Nottingham  

	+ Plane ticket to and from the GP
	+ 3 hotel nights (breakfast included)
	+ 1 free side event per day (2 in total)
* 2nd - 10th prize: An Grand Prix Sweater.
* 11nd - 20th prize: Free side event voucher (to be used at any European GP in 2005)

\* Only 1 entry per person.

### Pre-Registration

There is no pre-registration necessary for this event because it is open for everyone to enter.  


### Tournament Format And Schedule

The format for Grand Prix–Bologna is *Kamigawa* Limited; Sealed Deck (1 tournament pack, one booster *BoK* and one booster *SoK* ) with day 2 Booster Draft. All DCI Limited-format rules, **Magic** Floor Rules, and all subsequent updates will be enforced.

**Friday, June 10**: Registration from 4:00 – 8:00 PM.  
**Saturday, June 11**: Registration from 8:00 - 9:00 A.M.  
 Tournament begins at 9:00 A.M. The Top 64 players advance to competition on Sunday   
**Sunday, June 12**: The Top 64 players begin play at 8:00 A.M.

### Tournament

The main tournament at Grand Prix–Bologna is open to **Magic** players of all ages.  
*In the event that a player who wins an invitation to a Pro Tour stop at a Grand Prix has already qualified for an invitation, the invitation shall be extended to the next-best finisher in that Grand Prix. Previous qualification may be through a Qualifier tournament, through performance at a professional tournament, **or** by being an invited DCI player (valid upon the date of the official rank announcement for the appropriate professional tournament).*

* **Tournament Structure**  
 The structure for Grand Prix–Bologna will be modified Swiss-style (50-minute rounds), with the Top 64 players (or 128 if 800 or more players registered for the main event) advancing to the second day of competition for prizes and invitations. The top 8 previously uninvited Players will receive invitations to Pro Tour-London
* **Tournament Divisions and Prizes**  
 Grand Prix–Bologna will feature the standard “main-tournament” prize schedule as well as a special “Amateur" prize schedule. The Amateur prizes will be paid to the highest-finishing amateur players *in addition to any winnings those players receive from the main tournament prize schedule.* All prizes are in U.S. Dollars.

* **Amateur Status**  
 A player is considered to have amateur status if he or she has 0 lifetime pro points. If a player has ever been awarded ANY pro points from a Grand Prix, Pro Tour, or World Championship event, that player no longer has amateur status, may not participate in the Amateur Championship or Junior Super Series tournaments, and is not eligible for amateur awards at Grand Prix or other tournaments.

### How To Reach


**By car** Bologna is along the A1 motorway which connects it to Milan going north and Rome going south. To reach PalaMalaguti, exit at Casalecchio di Reno. Then take the 1bis exit and follow directions for “Maranello-Vignola-Palasport”. If you are not coming from the A1 motorway but from the orbital road, take exit number 1 instead of 1bis.   
  
**By train** PalaMalaguti is directly connected with Bologna Central Railway Station by the suburban railway “Bologna-Bazzano-Vignola” . Get off at Palasport. You should reach it in only 20 minutes. Trains leave from platform 7 at Piazzale Ovest.  
  
**By bus** You can reach Pala Malaguti by suburban bus from Bologna Central Railway Station. You can take bus 93 and 94 (only on weekday) and nr 671 (on weekdays and on holidays).
