Pride of Place
==============



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on July 17, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_theferrett.jpg)
By The Ferrett











You know, if I was going to hold a reader challenge, you'd think it would be for a card I could actually spell. But no; no matter how many times I've typed the words "[Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride)," I can't do it correctly to save my life. It's "Naclatl," or "Nacalt," or something else entirely. Only my faithful editor Kelly Digges saves me from looking far more foolish than I actually am. 

![Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nacatl+War-Pride)But thankfully, even though I can't spell the card, I can read your submissions. So let's take a look at the hundred or so [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) submissions that were resubmitted after the Great Laptop Crash of 2007 to find the winner. Before I start, though, let's talk about a couple of [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) misconceptions: 




1. *The tokens do not have haste*. They are put into play "tapped and attacking," but granting yourself another attack phase will *not* mean that your tokens are ready to go, since a permanent only loses summoning sickness (as us old-school folks like to call it) once it's started play on your side at the beginning of your turn. Assuming you don't get tricky with something like [Emblem of the Warmind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emblem+of+the+Warmind) or [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger) in the graveyard, the tokens are not ready for another go-round, even if you untap them.
2. *The tokens are removed from game at the end of the turn, and thus do not trigger any goes-to-the-graveyard abilities*. It's a shame, I know... But if you want them to trigger such abilities, you'll need to sacrifice 'em yourself.

The rules issues knocked out more than a few entries. But there was one problem that destroyed a lot more entries, some of them *very* cool submissions, and that was these words: 

"Before you enter your attack phase, I [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) your Nacatl." 

Unfortunately, I'd say a good 60% of the submissions I received (both before and after the Great Laptop Crash of 2007) *assumed* that you'd get to attack with your Nacatl, at which point all sorts of magical things would happen. And the decks, by and large, kind of fell apart if the Nacatl never got to that Great Attack Phase In The Sky. 

I should add that many of you did recognize the danger. Unfortunately, most of you went with two standardized ways of protecting your Nacatl, neither of which was consistent enough for me to feel comfortable with: 

*"I have [Shielding Plax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shielding+Plax)/[Avoid Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avoid+Fate)/[Stonewood Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonewood+Invocation)!"*   
 That might fly, if you're lucky, in a duel, but this was a *multiplayer* challenge—and you have to assume that opponents might gang up on you. Sure, you [Avoid Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avoid+Fate) once as James [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s your Nacatl – which doesn't help when Mara [Ichor Slick](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Slick)s it in response. Or you pass the turn and then Fred slams a [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) during his main phase, shuttling your Nacatl to the graveyard before you can attack. 

Plus, again, there's the whole "open mana" issue. If you can afford to wait to have nine mana open in a multiplayer game before you cast your game-winning creature, you're playing with *much* nicer people than I am. 

(Likewise, granting haste with an enchantment like [Emblem of the Warmind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emblem+of+the+Warmind) also gives you the same problems—hmm, you have the mana to play a six-mana creature and a two-mana enchantment in the same turn, *and* protect your dude? I'm not so sure I agree with you a hundred percent on your police work, there, Lou....) 

*"I have [Privileged Position](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Privileged+Position)!"*   
 That's nice. But unless you also have haste, you're still wide open to Fred's [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) (or Uthric's [Molten Disaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster)), which can get ugly. [Privileged Position](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Privileged+Position) is a good card, but it's also expensive and a large signal to experienced players that Something You'll Really Want to Target is About to Come On-Line. As such, it might well not be the panacea you're looking for. 

Actually, the interaction between [Camouflage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Camouflage) and [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) isn't much weirder than just [Camouflage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Camouflage) on its own. 

Ah, but if you did wrangle your War-Pride into the attack position successfully! Such *wondrous* things would happen! Just look at all the cool ideas that people have here!




* Patrick T. says that [Death Match](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Match) with Nacatl will cause a lot of havoc, giving your opponents' guys -3/-3 until the end of turn for every token. I tend to agree, even if one [Ashcoat Bear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashcoat+Bear) from your enemy before you attack can kind of ruin your day. (But hey! [Privileged Position](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Privileged+Position), amiright?)
* Wild Emilien liked playing an [Ixidron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixidron) after Nacatl attacks to turn his soon-to-be-sacrificed 3/3s into morphly 2/2s.
* Mark C. thinks that playing [Parallel Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallel+Evolution) on a War-Pride token to get more copies of that token that don't die at the end of turn is funny.
* Kory gets mad points for using [Dogpile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dogpile) as a finisher. Who uses [Dogpile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dogpile)?
* Both Joshua S. *and* Brian G. independently had the same really crazy idea; attack with Nacatl, use [Moment's Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment%27s+Peace) to ensure that you still have the most dudes on the board, then play [Standardize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standardize), then play [Peer Pressure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peer+Pressure). Voila! All creatures are the same type, you have more creatures than anyone else on the board, and you now own all of them—all for the cost of a successful attack phase, three spells in hand, and six mana! (All right, perhaps that's a bit much to ask.)
* Waldo enjoys taking a page from *Time Spiral* Limited and using [Herd Gnarr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herd+Gnarr) (and, stepping outside the Sealed environs, [Ancient Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Ooze)) to create a very large and not-easily-blockable guy.
* Apathy would like to give judges everywhere a heart attack by combining [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) with [Camouflage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Camouflage), then asking how people block.
* Chaz G. wanted a [Pollenbright Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pollenbright+Wings) on a Nacatl, which *would* be pretty darned amusing.
* Dr. Cat wanted a [Savage Beating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savage+Beating)-imprinted [Spellbinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellbinder) on his Nacatl. Whoof! Fun! But remember, the *tokens* will not be able to attack again without haste!
* Stephen M. says that [Confusion in the Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confusion+in+the+Ranks) combined with a [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) will give you all of your opponents' creatures. Funny, until your opponent play his own creature to steal your War-Pride... But as usual, [Privileged Position](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Privileged+Position) to the rescue!
* At least three separate folks suggested [Predatory Focus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predatory+Focus). Yowtch.
* Infinite people liked [Strength in Numbers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strength+in+Numbers).

There's even a little sub-genre of "the Nacatl gets through!" ideas called "Hi, I'm good with [Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggravated+Assault) to create a large number of attack phases": 




* Peter T. fiendishly combines [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) with [Thermopod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thermopod) and [Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggravated+Assault); just sacrifice the tokens for red mana to get yourself another attack phase! And another! And another! (He also uses [Gustcloak Savior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Savior) to yank the original out of combat, because—as I just mentioned—your token Nacatls won't be able to attack if you don't grant them haste.)
* In a similar vein, Jasper G. uses [Mana Echoes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Echoes) and [Skyshroud Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyshroud+Elf) to filter the mana and create infinite attack phases.
* ...and Paul Richard M. finds that [Nature's Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Will) and [Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aggravated+Assault) is the peanut butter in his chocolate.

[Aggravated Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Aggravated%2BAssault)But that said, once we start looking at the details of the submissions (and keep in mind, I *read* all of the pre–Laptop Crash submissions, I just didn't have them on hand when it came time to choose the contest winner), most of them broke down into a couple of clear categories:

Nacatl is a Cat that needs some help. In *Mirrodin* Block, cats were shown to be very good with equipment—not something that strikes me as being particularly logical, since I've owned cats and they never assembled death machines in their spare time, but whatever. And [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) is the perfect [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) enabler: haste *and* untargetability? What's not to love?

Well, the slowness is an issue. Equipping happens at sorcery speed, meaning that an instant can take your Nacatl straight from the Greaves to the graves. (Ah, the happy words "In response...") But what if you have [Leonin Shikari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Shikari)? Now you can equip anything at instant speed, making your Nactatl immune to targeted disruption that doesn't have the words "split second" on it. (Another spell hits the stack? Just re-equip it! It's free!) 

I like the Cat approach. Not only is Tribal usually a decent theme in multiplayer, but it also has some resiliency if you never draw the War-Pride or it falls apart. Witness.

> *You want to get [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) into play and protect it from removal so it can do it's thing, right? The first thing that sprang to mind was [Steely Resolve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steely+Resolve), which led me to a tribal deck featuring Cats (seemed better than Warriors). However, I discovered that [Raksha Golden Cub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raksha+Golden+Cub), [Taj-Nar Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taj-Nar+Swordsmith), and [Leonin Shikari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Shikari) were very good cats that made equipment desirable, and pitched the [Steely Resolve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steely+Resolve). This left me with [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) (great when you drop [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) with [Leonin Shikari](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Shikari) in play) for primary protection, as well as a tool box of equipment at various converted mana costs that you can use via [Taj-Nar Swordsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taj-Nar+Swordsmith). Also to protect your War-Pride from removal (targeted or global), [Fleetfoot Panther](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleetfoot+Panther) and [Whitemane Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion) (they also allow re-use of the Swordsmith). [Primal Rage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Rage) helps round out the enhancements to the War-Pride, while [Sword of Light and Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Light+and+Shadow) helps get it back when it's gone.*
> 
> 
> *The curve is a little high, so if your game needs to start faster you may need to add some early acceleration.*
> 
> 
> *It's been a while since my initial submission, and I've tested the deck since then. I may have made a few changes to the original list, but I can say it's done surprisingly well and I'm very happy with the result. At this point I'm tempted to drop Jedit for another Raksha, because Raksha is just that good here. I realize the deck doesn't deal with [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s, but I've found in practice that with [Lightning Greaves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) out, the deck has a lot of hasty threats and can afford to lose one or two that way.*

And with [Leonin Abunas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Abunas) (ANOTHER CAT!) to protect Altar and Greaves, and Demonic and [Enlightened Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor) to get what you're missing, how can you lose??*

### Hunted War-Prides

Strangely, many people found the biggest problem with [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) to be, "What happens if your opponents don't have creatures?" Me? I'm old-fashioned; if I'm in a multiplayer game and nobody has creatures, I'll cheerfully beat down with my overpriced 3/3. I'm not worried.

![Hunted Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hunted+Troll)Plus, it's multiplayer. If I'm really worried about Creatureless Phil over there, I'll attack someone else who *has* a lot of creatures and then put all my Nacatl tokens into play attacking Phil—provided, that is, I'm playing under multiplayer rules that allow dividing your attackers between multiple players. If your house rules allow only one defending player per attack, the War-Pride tokens all go that way, but if you can split attacks as desired, the Pride doesn't say who the tokens have to be attacking—heck, it could be anyone who's a valid target, so choose 'em right.(Also, War-Pride tokens get neatly around any [Ghostly Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison)-style effects, since they're already attacking, you don't pay the cost. You have to pay for the Pride itself, but the tokens are free, free, *free*.) 

The creature-free field, however, caused many to panic, and so they threw in the Hunted creatures from Ravnica to give their opponents guys for the Nacatl to work with. 

But this part always worries me. I do not, as a rule, like handing weapons to my opponents. They frequently find ugly ways to surprise me with them. Sure, those 2/2 Knight tokens are measly *now*, but one [Tromp the Domains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tromp+the+Domains) later and I'm dead. Hence, unless my whole deck involves immediately punishing my opponents for keeping ahold of the things I have given them, I'm not a fan. 

"But isn't [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) punishment enough, Ferrett?" you ask. And the answer is "no." As mentioned, the War-Pride is too fragile. If I give 'em these tokens and then it gets cacked, I'm not that far ahead. Sure, I have the Hunted creature itself, but usually they outnumber me.

### Speedy War-Pride

"How can I give my [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) haste *without* using all that extra mana?"

The classic answers are: 




* [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger) in the graveyard
* [Mass Hysteria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mass+Hysteria) on the table
* [Fires of Yavimaya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fires+of+Yavimaya)

![Lovisa Coldeyes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lovisa+Coldeyes)Any of those will do. But style points go to Steven James B., who reminded us that [Lovisa Coldeyes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lovisa+Coldeyes) makes Nacatl (and all his tokens) a heck of a lot bigger and speedier, while still allowing you to have a *non*-Cat Tribal deck. Also, if you're speeding up your War-Prides, a [Primal Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Forcemage) makes everything better. Just sayin'. 

### [Mass Hysteria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mass+Hysteria) / [Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium)

Many, *many* people liked this idea. Speedy War-Prides bring friends into play, and each of those friends does 3 damage to someone *before* it hits. 

The problem is that [Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) hurts you as well, and with all of the flash creatures and/or instant-speed removal around these days, there's a darned fine chance that you'll wind up with no War-Prides at all. And if you have no War-Prides, then you may wind up going under to your own enchantment.

Larsson Y. wins my heart with not a deck, but a way to use one of my favorite cards of all time: 


> 
> ![Marton Stromgald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Marton+Stromgald)*Marton Stromgald has always struck me as a very powerful effect stuck on an absolute wimp of a creature. The fact that you have to send his measly 1/1 self into battle to get that pump pretty much made sure that you'd only get one use out of him. Enter the War-Pride, and now, with careful stacking of the attack triggers, you get an army of n+1 attacking creatures, each of which is 4+n/4+n, where n is the number of creatures defending player controls. Add to this the line about how [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) must be blocked by exactly one creature if able, and there's a good chance that Marton will actually make it out of the combat step alive—possibly a first in the history of the game.*
> 

Fun fact: Way back in 1998, I used to play with Marton Stromgald in my Goblin decks. People hated him. And no, he never survived. So a way to help Marton is awesome. 

Ah, but we're approaching the end, and Ray H. has a fun idea: 


> 
> *The biggest problem that I have with [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) is that it's a creature and as a creature it is very fragile. With that in mind, I think the* sneakiest *way to get our beloved War-Pride into play and attacking is with [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack).*
> 
> 
> *"But now it dies at the end of the turn!" you say, so it looks like we need to save our War-Pride—or maybe they're just a bunch of* cowards*. That's right—[Cowardice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cowardice)! Now when your opponents start flinging spells at your War-Pride, they just come running back to your hand. Ah, but what happens when that silly green mage plays Fog? Now your War-Pride is in for a [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack)-induced heart attack...*
> 
> 
> *....Unless we do something a little unnatural. Like [Unnatural Selection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unnatural+Selection)!* 
> 
> 
> *Now for the mere cost of one colorless mana (while [Cowardice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cowardice) is in play) we can save the War-Pride! Not only that, but we can use any extra mana we have to bounce our opponent's creatures as well, letting all of those nice little tokens run through.* 
> 
> 
> *This deck as one major weak spot: enchantment hate. Now my play group doesn't use a lot of enchantment hate, or even artifact hate for that matter, it's either something silly, something "net-decky," or I have creatures and smash face with them, in my group. Every now and then someone pulls off a combo, but they usually reside in the "net-decky" group. Since the deck I built is Legacy legal, I threw in [Sterling Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sterling+Grove)s in case someone in my play group started main-decking [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant).* 
> 
>

> *The deck I am presenting here solves all the problems of using Nacatl War Pride. Most important of which is that, if the War Pride actually gets into play, it will almost certainly stay there. This deck also solves the problem of getting into play; the blue mage cannot just sit on a counterspell to stop your Nacatl army before it gets started. Lastly, this deck wins in a fun for multiplayer sort of way: smashing. First though, let me explain why this particular deck is so cool.*   
>   
> *The deck I have made here has a great deal of... synergy, if you will. There is obvious synergy between Scryb and Baru; flashy creatures and the Forcemage; Teferi and his "all my creatures are flashy" attitude – including Body Doubling a dead [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) to counter a spell...; and then all the goodness Nacatl brings to your table: Nacatl puts creatures into play and thus, the "already great in multiplayer" – essence warden becomes awesome. [Primal Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Forcemage) says: "you better block all these other cat warriors, cause otherwise they'll kill you." [Greater Good](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Good) gives the tokens an escape from the terrifying "removed from game" area, draws you cards (maybe a lot of cards, depending on the Forcemage and the number of creatures opponents control).* 
> 
> 
> *And then there's [Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape).* 
> 
> 
> ![Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dovescape)*[Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape) may as well scream "I WANT TO BE PLAYED WITH NACATL WAR PRIDE." Are you kidding me? This card wins you games. No one can kill [Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape) without a creature's ability, and therefore, no one can kill the War Pride without creature's abilities. Additionally, in a multiplayer game, someone will have to play a spell just to make blockers, and they make themselves an even easier target for your Pride of warriors – while giving you life through [Essence Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden). Also, when [Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape) gets into play (it will in multiplayer because most folks won't pack too much counter and often will have to use it for other scary effects like [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation). Plus, you're packing Teferi.) you are only playing seven total non-creature cards that would be countered so, you needn't worry about your own jank getting countered; also, you can still counter stuff ([Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake)) and save your War Pride from [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) type effects (see [Plaxmanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxmanta)).* 
> *Plus, [Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape) counters anyone's attempt to stop your second (or third... fourth?) War Pride from hitting the board.* 
> 
> 
> *So, there is obvious synergy, but can this deck actually win? Will people just announce "[Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) in response" and you'll cry?* 
> 
> 
> *Well, you are packing 5 counter spells as well as 4 [Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape)s and 3 [Body Double](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double)s, I think that terror is pretty well handled.* 
> 
> 
> *Will you just die before doing anything?* 
> 
> 
> *You have an average mana cost of 3.5 and with the mana acceleration in this deck, that's no problem. You also gain a lot of life from the Warden and ought to be able to flash in some 4/4s with the Forcemage's help. So, while the early game could be rough, this deck should handle it.* 
>

[Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape)? Oh, you had me at "Dove." 

All right, it's not a perfect deck—I'm not convinced that you couldn't remove the three Baru to make room for more Teferi (Teferae?), especially since you don't have a way to put a land into play at instant speed. (You can give the original Nacatl trample by laying a land before attacking, but the tokens it creates will have come into play after the land-laying and thus will be trample-free.) So if you want trample, maybe you could just run [Kamahl, Fist of Krosa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Fist+of+Krosa) or [Brawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brawn) for yoks. 

But still, this deck probably has the best chance of getting a [Nacatl War-Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) down and into the Red Zone before someone cacks it, giving you large guys and a large life total with the Warden. Ironically, it doesn't get around the "What if my opponents don't have creatures?" but that sort of ties into what I'm about to say next.

See, I must admit to disappointment. [Dovescape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dovescape)'s a neat way of doing this, but I was hoping someone would snap the Nacatl in *half*. Instead, what I got were a lot of entries that seemed, well.... 

...Fair.

I want a busted one. And as such, I can only assume that Nacatl is depressingly fair, and thus not the powerhouse it should be.
