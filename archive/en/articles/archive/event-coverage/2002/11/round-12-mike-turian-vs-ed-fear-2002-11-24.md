Round 12: Mike Turian vs. Ed Fear
=================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 24, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











Ed Fear sat down at the table, very unhappy about the way the draft went. “Alex Shvartsman ruined my draft!”, he angrily exclaimed. “Yeah, but he ended up with one of the best decks I’ve ever seen”, countered Chris Benafel, also at the feature match table. “Do you have a die?”, queried Mike Turian, settling for winning the eventual coin flip.

### Game One:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpla02/923.jpg) “Yup”, chirped Turian, keeping his opening hand. Ed, very dejected and somber, seemed completely intent on not even playing the game, as Mike dropped turn one [Goblin Sledder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder), turn two [Lightning Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Rift), and turn three morph guy. Ed stopped the beats with a [Mistform Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Wall), and all Turian could muster was a [Nosy Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nosy+Goblin). Ed, by contrast, cast [Sage Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Aven), setting up [Future Sight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight) on the top of his deck in an attempt to go absolutely berserk with card advantage.

Mike attacked with his [Nosy Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nosy+Goblin) and morph creature , prompting Ed to double block the morph creature. This allowed Turian to deal two to the wall, cast [Commando Raid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commando+Raid) on his goblin to deal two more to the illusion, and morph his double blocked creature to keep his now-revealed [Gravel Slinger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravel+Slinger) alive.

Ed, of course, drew and cast his broken enchantment the following turn, but took four to the face as Mike cast [Tribal Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Golem), which drove over for as a 4/4 haste first striker. Ed traded [Sage Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+Aven) and a morph [Ascending Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascending+Aven) for [Goblin Taskmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Taskmaster) and [Nosy Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nosy+Goblin), and proceeded to play Swamp, [Imagecrafter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter), [Riptide Biologist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Biologist) and [Frightshroud Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightshroud+Courier) off of [Future Sight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight).

On his own turn, Turian cast [Pinpoint Avalanche](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pinpoint+Avalanche) killing the Biologist. This allowed him to swing with [Nosy Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nosy+Goblin) #2 and the Golem. Since Ed had a horde of one toughness creatures, he had to trade two for one against the Goblin and the Slinger. This left Ed at two life, while Turian had no cards in hand and [Lightning Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Rift) on the board. 

Ed began casting huge creatures, starting with [Fallen Cleric](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Cleric) and following with [Anurid Murkdiver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anurid+Murkdiver). He used [Imagecrafter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) to shut down the Golem (turning the soldier into a leper), allowing it to safely trade with the swampwalking zombie. Ed began to turn the game around, but couldn’t get [Future Sight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Future+Sight) to give him anything other than lands and irrelevant cards. Mike finally drew [Erratic Explosion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erratic+Explosion), using it to reveal [Solar Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solar+Blast) and kill Ed.

Mike Turian 1 - Ed Fear 0

Ed: “[Lightning Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Rift) wasn’t stellar that game. You cast it and twenty-two turns later I was dead.”  
 Mike: “Actually, it cost me a point of damage because I couldn’t pump my Taskmaster that turn.”  
 Ed: “That’s a great card. Discard [Lightning Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Rift): your opponent gains one life and you mulligan.”

### Game Two:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpla02/920.jpg) Ed began the fun with a first turn [Imagecrafter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter), which Mike met with a first turn [Weathered Wayfarer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weathered+Wayfarer). Mike’s second turn [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) looked relatively neutered given the [Imagecrafter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imagecrafter) on the board, but powered up the shooter with a third turn [Nosy Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nosy+Goblin). Ed turned the Wayfarer into a goblin so that Mike would take an extra damage, and then dropped [Mistform Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Wall) followed by a morph creature. Both players were stuck on three lands however, and the next turn Mike traded his [Nosy Goblin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nosy+Goblin) for the Mistform creature (backed by [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith)) to bring the life totals down to 19-9 in favor of Ed, even though he lacked a board presence. 

Mike’s next turn brought down [Goblin Taskmaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Taskmaster) and [Glory Seeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory+Seeker), and Ed found time to play a fourth land and [Mistform Dreamer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Dreamer). The Dreamer died to [Sparksmith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), but not before making Mike take significant damage with his own shooter. Turian attacked with his horde, bringing Ed down to fourteen while being stuck at four himself. 

As Ed played more and more land, Mike started being able to activate his Land Tax machine, searching out [Daru Encampment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Encampment) and a cycling land from his deck in consecutive turns. He then cast [Akroma's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Blessing), attacking for eight and [Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock)ing Ed to death. 

Mike Turian 2 - Ed Fear 0
