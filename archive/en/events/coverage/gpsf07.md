GRAND PRIX SAN FRANCISCO 2007
-----------------------------


Scott-Vargas Repeats in Golden State!
=====================================




August 25-26











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/champ.jpg)Grand Prix San Francisco is over! Luis Scott-Vargas, with a feat accomplished only once before by Antonino De Rosa, has followed his Nationals win with a Grand Prix victory. Luis piloted his [Mystical Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings) deck through eighteen rounds, ending in the finals against his friend Jonathon Stocks. Stocks took it to three games, but it was Scott-Vargas' key sideboard [Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst) that vaulted the California native to the win and trophy. Congratulations to Luis Scott-Vargas, the 2007 champion of Grand Prix-San Francisco!

Sunday, Aug 26: 7:44 p.m. - Quarterfinals: Brett Blackman vs. Andrew Walden
---------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Noah Weil*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Tools.jpg)*Tools of the G/U mage*
It must be tough. You're forced to play the same deck all weekend, for fifteen rounds straight! Now that you're in the quarterfinals, you have play a near-perfect mirror! Brett Blackman and Andrew Walden came to GP San Francisco with a firm belief in the power of Green/Blue, and so far that faith has been vindicated. While Brett's popped up on the MSS circuit here and there, both are amateurs and already guaranteed a significant payday, as well as qualifying for Pro Tour Valencia. Yet there's still a trophy out there, and they both want it. Brett swore a victory, assuring the crowd his daily MTGO routines would continue to serve him well. Andrew just seemed happy to be there.


Game 1: A quick strategy lesson: there are two ways to win in **Magic**. You can play better spells, or you can play more spells (the best decks do both). Almost by definition it's impossible to play better spells than the opponent in a mirror match. Hence the way to win is overload. Walden took this theory to heart in the first game, which is to say, Brett didn't play enough lands. Brett did have three lands in the beginning, which doesn't sound bad when everything in the deck costs two and three mana. But with an opponent at five lands out, someone who's able to play two spells a turn, the advantage is overwhelming.


Brett did start well, negating Looters with his own, casting [Delay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delay)s then [Riftsweeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftsweeper) to counter the card for good. But that mana problem caught up, and it translated to Brett taking more damage than he could handle. A [Psionic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Blast) would take care of [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), but [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) would sneak through. [Thornweald Archer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thornweald+Archer) would take out the Cloudskate, but a Looter would still hit. Brett's life total was dwindling, and Andrew was still piling out the threats. Eventually Blackman found more mana to do more, and it looked like the game was slowly turning around. But Brett was just too low, and a topdecked Psi Blast from Walden gave Andrew the first.


Walden: 1  

Blackman: 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Walden.jpg)*This man is on his third mulligan*
Game 2: Things began in normal fashion, with Expanses and [Llanowar Reborn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Reborn) as appropriate. Andrew's [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) looked ok, but a +1/+1 counter made Brett's look better. That particular 'Goyf took on a permanent +2/+2 when Brett cast the MVP [Pongify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pongify), sweeping in for four quick points. Andrew's back was against the wall, and this time he was the one who was stymied at three lands to his opponent's five. Just like the first game, the defensive player would trade a card for a card, and take some damage. This game went even quicker than before. A last-ditch double block from Walden went south on a second [Pongify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pongify), and Walden immediately conceded.


Walden: 1  

Blackman: 1


Walden: "I'm going to first. Wait, should I? I do like extra cards…"  

Blackman: "You should go first."


Game 3: Walden, on the play, looked at his opener and sent it back. His next six were also unacceptable. His next five were *also* unacceptable. At four cards he found a keeper, retaining a smile through a rough batch of luck at the worst possible time. A mulligan from Blackman gave Andrew some hope, but Brett's next six were excellent. [Looter il-Kor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor), [Looter il-Kor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Looter+il-Kor) and all Andrew could do was play land and pass. Brett set up the perfect hand, dealing damage all the while. Andrew laughed about his impossible situation and extended the hand, wishing his opponent luck in the semifinals.


Brett Blackman defeats Andrew Walden and goes on to face Luis Scott-Vargas in the semifinals.

Luis managed to get a [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) to resolve, raising his life total to a healthier 13. This was the last major life total change for many turns, and the deep attrition war began.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Response.jpg)*Luis Scott-Vargas had the answers*
Blackman, jealous of Luis' [Triskelavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus), decided to make his own with a hardcast [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter), complete with its own +1/+1 counters. A flurry of [Triskelavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus) tokens and Factory Workers danced around the table, blocking, shooting, and trading left and right. Black-man cast creatures, Luis blocked with Factory Workers. The mood was tense, as it ap-peared Blackman was always on the verge of pushing through with [Psionic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Blast) or [Stonewood Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonewood+Invocation).


Blackman cast a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and sent a grafted Morph into the red zone, revealed to be a [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) copying [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), something able to get over the supply of Factory Workers. [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) number two hit the table, and it looked like Luis might have fallen behind.


That is, until [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption) decided to crash the party, apparently invited by Urborg. Such a long and exciting game ended so typically, with an Urborg-fueled [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption), and the extension of the hand.


Luis Scott-Vargas 2 - Brett Blackman 1


Luis advanced to the finals to face, not the Relic-toting Brazilian Antonino predicted, but more [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s. May the Tendrils flow.


  


Sunday, Aug 26: 8:59 p.m. - Semifinals: Paulo Vitor Dama Da Rosa vs. Jonathan Stocks
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Eric Reasoner*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Paulo.jpg)*Paulo Vitor Damo Da Rosa*
Imagine you're U/B teachings and your girlfriend is Mono U with [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). Now imagine you're having an argument about anything. You're there, you're fighting and you think you have valid points. You're both going back and forth, bickering at each other. All the while, you think maybe you're winning, or at least you're holding your own. But you aren't. You won't win this argument, and Teachings can't win this match-up.


### Game 1


Stocks started the match with a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and a couple of [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision)s. Da Rosa meanwhile was stock piling [Coalition Relic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic)s and cards in hand. [Triskelavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus) hit the table to defend the homestead and did a great job at it with a little help from a [Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void) for 2 which removed three (3!) [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s and a [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer) in the hand.


Undaunted, Stocks suspended an [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) for 1 to synch it up with an [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision)s which was about to fill up his hand. Five fresh cards later and the avatar was bashing in for 8. Da Rosa was on his heels now, looking across the board at a giant Chronicler and a mittful of cards. His hand consisted of [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation), [Haunting Hymn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Hymn), and [Tendrils of Corruption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption); he just had to wait for the right time to play them. An EOT Venser from Stocks gave Da Rosa the opportunity to hit with the [Haunting Hymn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Hymn). Still EOT, Stocks attempted to sacrifice [Horizon Canopy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Canopy) only to see his Chronicler shot down by Trike tokens.


Stocks just shook his head and muttered, "Man, where did I learn to count?"


But it wouldn't matter. Stocks had enough gas to get him to the finish line. After Da Rosa's recurred [Triskelavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus) was bounced before attackers on Stocks' turn, all that was left was the crying.


### Game 2


Da Rosa began the second game with a frustrating mulligan. Visibly upset about losing even an inch in what was a pretty difficult match.



![Serrated Arrows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Serrated+Arrows)

The early edge went to Stocks this game as he managed to stick a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and let it grow and grow and grow, all the while chipping away at Da Rosa's life total. A tussle over Teferi came at the end of Stocks' turn when he tried to [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) the completely ridiculous, almost unfair, wizard. The snake never saw play though as he was sent straight to the bin by a [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation). Next turn a [Slaughter Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact) on the [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) put Da Rosa in the driver's seat. Or so it appeared.  

Stocks was able to keep making guys and sticking damage where he could. A [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), he still had plenty of ways to make things difficult for Vitor.  

Da Rosa attempted a [Haunting Hymn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haunting+Hymn) on his turn, and could only shake his head when it was shot down by a [Draining Whelk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk). Fortunately, he didn't have to face down a 7/7 flier as he had a Trike token to bust the Whelk with the trigger on the stack.


A [Serrated Arrows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows) from Stocks elicited a loooooooooong think tank session for Da Rosa. He had a [Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst) to stop it, but had better things to do with it and his mana for the turn- namely putting his deceased [Triskelavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus) on his library. Finally the Arrows got the OK.


Attempting to crawl back into the game, Da Rosa drew the [Triskelavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus) and presented it for Stocks' [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel)ling pleasure. He did indeed have the [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), two in fact, and that was the end of GP San Francisco for Da Rosa.


Jonathan Stocks advances to the Finals where he'll face off against Luis Scott-Vargas


Stocks defeats Da Rosa 2-0


  


Sunday, Aug 26: 9:00 p.m. - Finals: Luis Scott-Vargas vs. Jonathon Stocks
-------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Noah Weil*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Unusual.jpg)*Unusual*
After eighteen grueling rounds, it came down to this final match. On one side, California native and current National champion Luis Scott-Vargas, known to his close friends as Omnipotence Incarnate. Opposing Luis was Nevada resident Jonathon Stocks, a friend and former testing buddy of Scott-Vargas. Luis was quick to point out he saw Jonathon's U/G list before the tournament but was "unimpressed". Luis may have jumped the gun on his scorn, but it remains to be seen if Stocks' list has what it takes to go all the way. With a hearty handshake and sincere wish of luck, the two competitors got underway for this final match of the tournament.


Game 1: Stocks won the roll and kicked things off with a suspended [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) and graft onto a morph. Scott-Vargas merely started doing what his deck best, which if you haven't heard by now, is making mana and waiting around for something better. Luis' first real play was a [Strangling Soot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot) on the facedown. While Stocks could have created some sick card advantage by flipping up [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), he elected to keep his lands where they were and placed his morph in the trash.


Stocks wasn't out of threats by a long shot as another morph stuck, his [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) landed on a land, and his Venser bounced another. Cheon was on the backpedal, stuck on only two mana for the turn. He played his third land for the second time and used it to cast [Shadowmage Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator), still having to pitch a card. In came the trio of 2/2s, dealing solid damage to Scott-Vargas. Jonathon managed to not only get triple threats on the table, keeping Finkel on defense, but have his mana ready to cast [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel) or [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) as needed. Luis understood he needed to restore the mana advantage, and spent his four mana on a [Mystical Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings) for [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation). Well and good, but the creatures used the free reign to keep up the attack. With [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel), Riftwing, and Chronicler in hand Stocks pondered his next move. Finally deciding to not even try to fight a counter war against Pact, Stocks suspended Chronicler for one and passed the turn. A fine move, as [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) had nothing on RFGed [Maro](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maro).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Finalist.jpg)*Grand Prix finalist Jonathon Stocks*
Scott-Vargas pondered and went for a [Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Void) for four, wiping out Venser but whiffing on the hand. Chronicler hit and immediately attacked, with Stocks declaring his attack "idiotic". Luis led with [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) on the next turn, then chuckled and called himself dumb for not having attacked with his Shadowmage first.


With an opponent at three life Stocks, cast [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), then burst out laughing when Scott-Vargas pointed out the prime [Llanowar Reborn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Reborn).


"I, uh, didn't want to get greedy."-Jonathon Stocks


Misplayed or not, Jonathon's Riftwing came over twice, handing the first game to the U/G mage.


Scott-Vargas: 0  

Stocks: 1


Game 2: Both players started with their special lands, [Terramorphic Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) and [Llanowar Reborn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Reborn). Stocks showed his sideboarded [Seal of Primordium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Primordium) against Luis' [Prismatic Lens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens), only to see Luis upgrade into [Coalition Relic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coalition+Relic). "Ah, you baited me".


Stocks began accumulating land, not wanting to commit anything without counter protection. Stocks was clearly looking forward to playing a morph with [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) backup, if he drew a land on his seventh turn. Stocks made an error here, when he passed with [Terramorphic Expanse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse) as his fresh land. Scott-Vargas had laid an Urborg the turn before, giving that Expanse the ability to tap for black. It was a trick the Urborg players well knew, but one Stocks had less experience with. Instead Stocks exchanged his land for another and got ready to start playing his hand, starting with a suspended Chronicler.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Buddies.jpg)*No hard feelings*
Scott-Vargas hadn't been idle, crafting his own strength with [Careful Consideration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration) and [Foresee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foresee). He had Pull From Eternity for the Chronicler and ended with [Shadowmage Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator), still with mana up. Stocks spent some time blowing up a Relic and playing with [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer), but it just gave Luis more cards via Shadowmage and even a token from Factory. A facedown from Stocks required a response and Luis had a good one: [Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst) with buyback. Stocks tried to Snake, but Luis tutored up a [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation). A brief incident occurred when the token disappeared to remind Luis of the Pact, but order was quickly restored. Stocks held on as best he could, [Serrated Arrows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serrated+Arrows) doing fine work against Finkels and assembly-worker tokens. Yet Luis kept taking cards, widening the gap. Stocks had one shot when he snuck a [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) onto the board, playing Nekretaal on a Factory token, then suspending [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision)s.


The Cloudskate hit a couple of times, but Luis turned it around with a Tendrils and Stocks was out of action. The final nail in the coffin? [Imp's Mischief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imp%27s+Mischief) on the [Ancestral Vision](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision). Jonathon Stocks picked up.


Scott-Vargas: 1  

Stocks: 1


The players were set to begin their final game when one of them made an interesting observation: they had never received each other's decklist for inspection. Typical GP policy has each top 8 competitor receiving time to study the decklist of the opponent, but that hadn't happened here. Head Judge John Carter was summoned, and determined he had made an error. As such, each player was given their opposite's decklist, and as much time as needed to study. The players looked at the sheets for about 30 seconds before tossing them away laughing, looking forward to finishing their exciting and final match.


Game 3: The crowd was hushed as the players shuffled and drew for their last game. Scott-Vargas led with a second turn [Prismatic Lens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Lens) ("Every time! So lucky!"), before casting a third turn [Foresee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foresee). Scott-Vargas kept three of the cards on top. Opponents *hate* seeing an opponent keep cards on top. Scott-Vargas also cast a[Careful Consideration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration), pitching a Tendrils. What in the world did he keep?!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpsf07/Carter.jpg)*John Carter announces the winner of Grand Prix San Francisco*
Stocks started with a facedown [Fathom Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fathom+Seer). It was targeted by [Strangling Soot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot), but Jonathon let his Venser feel like an Ertai and actually counter something for once, by bouncing the morph. The replayed morph was targeted by the flashed back Soot, countered by [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake). It looked good, but it just made Luis' [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) all the sweeter. Stocks started suspending spells, which seemed like a fine time for Scott-Vargas to cast Teferi. While it was bounced once, Luis had enough mana to easily replay it.


Stocks wasn't quite finished, despite a hand full of junky Snakes and [Cancel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cancel). Stocks had a plan. If he could get his Shapeshifter to stick, he could remove Teferi at his leisure. Jonathon went for the facedown, but forgot the key card in Luis' deck. Scott-Vargas cast his [Mystical Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings) for [Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst), casting the spell with buyback. Stocks berated himself for the error, for not casting the Shapeshifter face up. Still, there was one more chance. Riftwing was still entering the red zone. But with Scott-Vargas at four life down came [Triskelavus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus), the terror of the *Time Spiral* skies. Facing [Spell Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst) with no way out, Stocks extended the hand in congratulations, to the cheers and applause of the crowd..


Scott-Vargas: 2  

Stocks: 1


Congratulations to Luis Scott-Vargas, GP San Francisco champion!
