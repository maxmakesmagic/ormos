Oath is Not Dead
================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 8, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











Along with [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will), [Gaea's Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Blessing) was a card many players lamented leaving the Extended format, saying that it would take the [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) deck with it on its way out the door.

While it did take the traditional Maher-Oath toolbox version with it, the card [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) produces far too powerful of an effect to be ignored. In the words of YMG's Justin Gary, "It's a two-mana card that invalidates creature strategies."

Gary, a proponent of Oath in any form (he finished second in the Nice Masters with the last popular version of Oath), is currently near the top of the standings with a "Turbo Oath" deck that tries to Oath up one giant creature to win the game with quickly: [Cognivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore).

The "Turbo Oath" strategy was dabbled with before when [Thorn Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn+Elemental) was first released; the goal was to get the "unblockable" 7/7 into play and win in three attacks. But once the Gaea's Blessing/[Enlightened Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enlightened+Tutor)/Sylvan Library/Very Slow versions were perfected, Turbo Oath was abandoned.

Now it's back. The deck runs eleven permission spells and a bunch of card-drawing to find and protect the Oaths. It contains one [Krosan Reclamation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Reclamation) in case the [Cognivore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cognivore)s both die, but other than that, it can't reuse its cards, which was one of the hallmarks of old Oath.

Gary said the deck wasn't that great at first, but after playtesting a lot with his YMG teammates, they came up with fixes. The first was [Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed), which solves a lot of problem the deck has. The Deeds are the only black spells in the main deck, and the black mana allows for [Engineered Plague](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague)—great against [Aluren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren) and Sligh—in the sideboard. The second big addition to the deck was [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish), which gives the deck access to [Masticore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Masticore), [Gilded Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Drake), [Palinchron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palinchron), and [Dust Bowl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Bowl) from the board.

[Palinchron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palinchron) is not used in this deck as a combo piece, but rather as a replacement for [Morphling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morphling) as the unkillable creature of choice. With all the Edicts and Deeds running around, [Morphling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morphling)—considered the best creature ever—is actually a bad choice for this field. So [Palinchron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Palinchron), with its ability to return to its owners hand, is filling in.

The new Turbo Oath deck has trouble with super-aggressive decks like Sligh; Gary's one loss to this point was to an aggro red/green deck. But other than that, it has game against all the popular decks: Reanimator, the Rock, [Aluren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren), and [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog).

Oath is not dead, but is instead reborn.

Below are two other decks using [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids). One is an Enchantress Combo deck with [Turnabout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turnabout) and [Frantic Search](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frantic+Search), the other is a Turbo-Land variant that Oaths up [Battlefield Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlefield+Scrounger)s, which enable it to go infinite with [Time Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp).
