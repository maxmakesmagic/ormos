Arms Race
=========



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on February 18, 2010 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






Tomorrow a couple hundred of the greatest **Magic** players in the world are going to ram their [Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning)s into each other's [Kabira Crossroads](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kabira+Crossroads), their [Putrid Leech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech)es into each other's [Sprouting Thrinax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprouting+Thrinax)es, in Pro Tour battle. [Sphinx of Jwar Isle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Jwar+Isle) will shroud the skies beneath the shadows of its seemingly inviolate wings ... at least until that [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) shows up and shows it just who is the real king—or queen as the case may be—of the feathered 5/5 set. Landfall triggers will stack up as [Arid Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa)s pile down in the graveyard. Planeswalkers and [Scute Mob](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scute+Mob)s will warp the battlefield ... at least so long as they can weave and dodge around a pesky squad of [Vampire Hexmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage)s. [Eldrazi Monument](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument) will pave an invisible road across the sky, giving us a window into a future world where [Eye of Ugin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) might matter ... *a lot*; and, somehow, inexplicably, and unbelievably still, [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) will remain Standard legal.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/td/td78_cross.jpg)  
As usual, this PT is going to be a war. And like any good war, ultimately an arms race. 


The best deck designers in the world are going to be putting their shoulders to the boulders, too, pushing archetypes to the imaginary apex with a little help from *Worldwake*, hopefully, to the pinnacle of deck design possibilities.


Of course you should be checking the official coverage here on **dailymtg.com** nonstop tomorrow (and through the entirety of the weekend ... I suggest ctrl+r) to absorb any and all future Friday Night **Magic** technologies, but probably the most exciting part of the whole shebang will occur on Sunday as Brian David-Marshall welcomes Rich Hagon into the Top 8 webcast booth. [Worlds 2009](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/coverage-2009-world-championships-2009-11-12) was the grand send-off for my old webcast partner Randy Buehler, but I have a feeling that Brian and Rich are going to do a bang up job when Andre Coimbra repeats his Worlds performance this weekend (you know, just a guess).


You can check out the [Pro Tour webcast](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtgevent/webcast/home) on Sunday. Until then you can view the last of Randy's work; I for one am hoping for a repeat of the "haymaker" **Magic** that defined the Worlds Top 8.


[![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/td/td78_ptCta.jpg)](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtgevent/webcast/home)  
... But—haymakers or no—what can we expect from the Constructed portion of this, the first Pro Tour of 2010?


With its second-set-driven Standard format, you can expect this Pro Tour to feature a tall stack of known quantities; and as with any good arms race, the decks will be bolstered with some of the new toys *Worldwake* has whipped up.

Vampires has recently raised its fangs as one of the premiere decks in Standard. The tribe's combination of power (fifth-turn [Mind Sludge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sludge)), synergy (anything plus [Vampire Nocturnus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nocturnus)), and creature hostility—all while avoiding the distractions of a lot of other colors—make Vampires a contender.


But what could make the tribe just a little bit better?


What about a *lot* bit?


*Lookie What I Got:* 


![Dead Reckoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dead+Reckoning)  
True story: Unnamed Pro Tour champion, testing for tomorrow's Pro Tour. Uncharacteristically, with a creature deck. Opponent runs out a [Dead Reckoning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Reckoning); the target is something small, a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) perhaps. That's not the important part ... or the important creature in this exchange. *What are you getting?* queries the unnamed. His opponent isn't quite sure yet, shuffles through the available cards ... one of which is [Gatekeeper of Malakir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir).


*Oh, never mind. Next game!* He concedes.


**Red Decks**



Petr Brozek has got to be the most upright member of the Boros community since Tsuyoshi Fujita, or at least [Agrus Kos, Wojek Veteran](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agrus+Kos%2C+Wojek+Veteran). 


Brozek was already the grandpappy of the deck that came to be known—via the dubbing dexterity of one Brian David-Marshall—as Barely Boros (his X-1 standout from the World Championships).

William Cavaglieri shocked the world with his Top 8 appearace at Worlds—bereft of It Girl [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) no less—piloting his Mono-White creature deck.


Months have passed wince William's standout finish at Worlds, and individual players have further customized white creature decks, never missing a chance to play [Devout Lightcaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devout+Lightcaster) in the sideboard, and even [White Knight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=White+Knight) in the main! *Take that, black!*


But what is more hateful than a whole lot of anti-black cards is one superb anti-*red* option:


*Lookie What I Got:* 


![Kor Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kor+Firewalker)  
Will [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker) prove a game changer for white? Every color already had the option to play [Dragon's Claw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon%27s+Claw), and white creature decks in particular have the option for [Harm's Way](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harm%27s+Way) or [Refraction Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refraction+Trap); the difference that [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker) brings is simply 2 power. This is a card you can drop on the second turn, confident that it is burly enough to hide behind ... but one that can also *attack*, either "right back" or preemptively. 


**Control (or at least "Islands")**


One of the puzzling perceptions of Standard, both *Zendikar*-driven and now *Worldwake*-enabled, is the position of blue. Is it now "back" with certain card drawing options from the new set? *Was it ever actually the worst?*


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/TD/td78_treasure.jpg)  
Personally, I find blue's bad reputation unfounded.


Originally I was on the blue-bashing bandwagon, a big fan of unmatched threats [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel), and [Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos) ... but relatively late developments with proactive Grixis, Esper, and "American Control"-style card combinations had me wondering. 


In fact, blue might have already been the *best*!


I mean look at how blue can borrow an [Ajani Vengeant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant), for instance:

This is a Standard update that the incomparable Luis Scott-Vargas (high five!) used to win a Star City Games Standard Open just last month. 


Powered by maybe the most underrated two-mana spell in the format—[Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas)—this deck does a great job of negating an opposing Jund deck's removal while winning via traditional "blue" routes to victory: card advantage, (light) permission, and monolithic late-game threats.


So what did blue get?


*Lookie What I Got:* 


![Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)  
Already the darling of the pundits, [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) (along with [Treasure Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Hunt)) form the vanguard of *Worldwake*'s blue renaissance (though, again, I am not 100% sure that blue was in the market for a renaissance so much as a rediscovery).


[Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) has fallen at least somewhat in popularity over the recent months, alternating with [Divination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination) in many decks (if not being supplanted entirely by [Divination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divination)) ... so don't look for a lot of which-Jace competition. My guess is that, especially with [Treasure Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Hunt) acting as a [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) beneficiary, the Mind Sculptor will prove the more popular of the two Jaces.

This is a deck that has avoided the hype surrounding Jace and [Treasure Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Hunt), the flash of [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker), and the unexpected success of an Extended Boros deck. Given its persistent popularity, I would guess that while probably not a 30+% component of the metagame, Jund will remain one of the most popular decks in the Pro Tour room.


Already possessed of a fragile mana base, the addition of a new dual land may be a welcome one for Jund; I have found [Raging Ravine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raging+Ravine) to be useful primarily at the end of a long attrition fight, the last 3/3 necessary for deal-closing. I say "may" because the inclusion of the new *Worldwake* creature-lands is a challenge for balance. On the one hand they are dual lands, generally welcome in three-color decks; on the other, they come into play tapped and can make for slow and sometimes awkward opening draws.
