Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. Alright, folks... do you get it?


Here's how it works:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab138_wurm.jpg)You use ramp spells and card selection to set up your [Mass Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mass+Polymorph). You get at least three tokens onto the battlefield and cast [Mass Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mass+Polymorph), making it very likely that you'll get two [Massacre Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm)s and a [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon). You put the [Massacre Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm)s' triggers on the stack first so they resolve after the [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon)'s. The [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon)'s "enters the battlefield" ability destroys three of your opponent's permanents. (Maybe it's a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) and two lands, maybe it's just three lands.) The two [Massacre Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm)s' triggers then resolve, killing the Elephants and all your opponent's other creatures with 4 or less toughness. If your opponent had two creatures with less than 4 toughness, he or she loses 20 life because of the Wurms and dies. If not, well, your opponent just lost at least 12 life and three permanents (presumably keeping him or her off [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) mana), and you put 21 points of power onto the table.



![Mass Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mass+Polymorph)

 

Now, it's important to mulligan hands with [Mass Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mass+Polymorph) fatties if you don't have a [See Beyond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+Beyond). This deck can awkwardly draw its [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) and not be able to combo, but when the deck does what it's supposed to, it's a Massacre.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab138_2Cards1.jpg)I'd like to start by explaining my deck-building process here. I found the combo as posted by Neon Lights and was immediately intrigued. I wanted more Eldrazi Spawn token production, and the [Flayer Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flayer+Husk)s seemed like they were out of place. I was worried about [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) draws from [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) and Blue-Red-Green. I also didn't feel comfortable with games where my opponent had a [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) and a [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine). [Spawning Breath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Breath) solved every issue here. The Eldrazi Spawn it creatures useful for blocking equipped [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)s and helps us achieve our combo. The 1 damage gives us a great out against [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s getting equipped or early [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra)s.


I still wanted more spot removal, though. Losing to Sword + Hawk and [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) means I need more than four spells that let me power through that. Playing red gives me access to [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). A timely bolt can often buy you two full turns of survival. It only costs one mana, it can often be cast on the same turn as a ramp spell or card manipulation spell.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab138_see.jpg)[See Beyond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=See+Beyond) is an absolute must here. Only playing the four creatures makes my list a lot more susceptible to dud hands. In return, though, you become more resilient and more consistent when casting a [Mass Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mass+Polymorph).



![See Beyond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=See+Beyond)

 

[Growth Spasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Growth+Spasm) and [Awakening Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awakening+Zone) almost seem like they were tailor-made for this type of strategy. These spells, along with [Explore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore), allow you to "go off" as early as the fourth turn.

I want a sideboard that makes me better against countermagic. A lot of opponents may try to leave mana open for countermagic as soon as we have access to six mana. [Awakening Zone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Awakening+Zone) is very strong in these types of positions, but we probably want access to [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) or [Autumn's Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Autumn%27s+Veil) after sideboard. The rest of the sideboard can be filled with exciting cards to flip into off your [Mass Polymorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mass+Polymorph).
