Round 9: Terry Tsang vs. Kai Budde
==================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 4, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_aaronforsythe.jpg)
By Aaron Forsythe











![Kamahl, Pit Fighter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kamahl%2C+Pit+Fighter)Terry Tsang is painfully aware of Kai's two Kamahls and hopes they aren't a factor. Kai, however, is unaware of Tsang's [Bomb Squad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomb+Squad), but he should be able to deal with that a little easier.The two 7-1 competitors wished each other good luck and starting in on the beatings.

Kai came out blazing in Game 1 with [Diligent Farmhand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diligent+Farmhand) and [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel), while all Tsang had was two [Stern Judge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stern+Judge)s (and Kai wasn't even playing black). The Judges got a lot better when Tsang dropped [Divine Sacrament](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Sacrament) (Kai: "That got to you?!"), but Kai left the Mongrel on defense and everyone stopped attacking. When Kai played his second [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), Tsang held his breath in anticipation of Kamahl, but it was not to be. Kai merely played a [Krosan Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Avenger).

When Tsang got greedy and attacked with his Judges, Kai [Repel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repel)led one, and blocked the other with his Mongrel, pitching a card to make it white (and a beneficiary of Tsang's Sacrament – how unfair!).

Tsang: "That turn didn't go as well as I'd hoped."

It got worse for Tsang when [Deep Analysis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) allowed Kai to find Kamahl. All of Tsang's blockers were systematically removed, and the White Mongrel finished the job.

Tsang 0 – Budde 1

Tsang leads off Game 2 with a [Mad Dog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Dog), after which Kai dropped the [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel).

Kai: "Yay for the dog fights!"

Tsang gave his dog a [Crackling Club](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crackling+Club), however, and Kai decided the Mongrel was willing to trade straight up for the enchanted [Mad Dog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Dog).

An [Aven Cloudchaser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Cloudchaser) gave him back the Club, and he added a new [Mad Dog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Dog) and a [Stern Judge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stern+Judge) to the fray. Unfortunately, Kai had a [Temporary Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporary+Insanity) during Tsang's next attack, and the Canadian's creatures smashed into one another. Kai brought forth a [Springing Tiger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springing+Tiger), [Nantuko Disciple](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Disciple), and a [Pardic Arsonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pardic+Arsonist) (3 to the dome), and all Terry drew was a lone [Ember Beast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ember+Beast) and a handful of land.

**Final Result:** Tsang 0 – Budde 2

Tsang: "Those games weren't even close. How unsatisfying."

Budde moves to 8-1.
