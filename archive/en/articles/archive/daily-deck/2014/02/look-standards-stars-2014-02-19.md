A Look at Standard's Stars
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on February 20, 2014 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Gerry Thompson











Earlier this week, [I covered Makihito Mihara's Standard deck](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/deck/1432) with *Born of the Gods*. Today, I have Aaron Lewis's decklist, which is absolutely chocked full of *Born of the Gods* cards! 

 Aaron used [Hero of Iroas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Iroas) to bestow [Eidolon of Countless Battles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Countless+Battles) and [Hopeful Eidolon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hopeful+Eidolon) on the cheap. [Brimaz, King of Oreskos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos) unsurprisingly makes an appearance and works pretty well with [Eidolon of Countless Battles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eidolon+of+Countless+Battles). [Meletis Astronomer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meletis+Astronomer) keeps the gas flowing. Having a cheap way to trigger heroic, such as [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) or [Ordeal of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ordeal+of+Thassa), is incredibly important, as playing multiple spells per turn is the easiest way to get ahead. 

 If you enjoy building a giant monster, then you should check out Aaron's deck. Although Standard has a lot of removal, bestow mitigates the downside of creating a huge creature. I would like to see some of the sideboard [Gods Willing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gods+Willing)s make their way into the main deck, though. Being able to protect your creature or force through lethal damage seems really important for this type of deck.
