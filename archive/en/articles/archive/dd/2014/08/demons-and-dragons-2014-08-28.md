### DAILY DECK


Demons and Dragons
==================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)

Adam Prosak

From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer.


August 28, 2014
 










A Dragon, a Demon, and a Human walk onto the battlefield. The Human gets eaten right? Not in this deck! *Magic Online* user Goldo has created a deck where [Stormbreath Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbreath+Dragon), [Desecration Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon), and [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) work side by side to create quite a formidable deck!


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stormbreath Dragon)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormbreath Dragon)
This deck functions primarily as a board-control deck using lots of disruptive elements to keep the opponent down while winning with a singular threat. Any of the eleven creatures in this deck are more than capable of taking an opponent from 20 to 0 all by itself.


This leaves rooms for many instants and sorceries, which make [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) very powerful. [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) are potent with the individually powerful threats, keeping them safe from opposing removal. Finally, having access to both red and black give this deck potent removal options of its own, which makes life tough for opposing creature decks.
