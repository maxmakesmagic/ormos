Saturday, 10:53 a.m. – Grand Prix Trial Winning Decklists
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Event Coverage Staff*

 

Friday saw a rush of interest in the Grand Prix Trials as players fought to earn three byes in the main event. Here is a sample of their winning decklists. Interestingly, these winning lists contain more aggro decks (mostly black or red variants sporting [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler), [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), and/or [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear), as well as various [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) decks) than [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) decks.

Saturday, 10:55 a.m. – Metagame Musings with Emanuel Sutor
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Despite a couple of Pro Tour appearances and some moderate success at the Grand Prix level previously, outside of Germany Emanuel Sutor is mostly known for making the finals of Grand Prix Lyon two weeks ago. Within the German **Magic** community, he's also kind of famous for being one of the more talkative players. Coincidentally, he's very knowledgeable about what's going on in the world of **Magic** tech too, closely following results from **Magic Online** and offline tournaments, reading most of every article, and simply testing an awful lot.


Talkative and knowledgeable? Who better to talk to for a quick overview over the format, right?


"Recently, Blue-White Flash decks have taken the number-one spot in the metagame," said Sutor. "There's still some UWR Tempo around too, but the straight-up two-color version took the lead. Then there's Green-White Beatdown, with things like [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) with [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), accelerated by [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/blog_musings_emanuel_sutor.jpg) *Emanuel Sutor* 
"Close behind that or even tied for second place is Mono-Red. That deck is for real. Interestingly enough, it seems to have a favorable matchup against the slower [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) decks. [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) into [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) is an absolute beating," Sutor went on. "Of course, GW and Mono-Red will probably be played by a lot of people here, and might not make the best transition. The rest of the field is rounded out by Reanimator, Jund, and [Bant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant). Those six decks make up what one could call 'the establishment', but of course there are a lot of niche decks as well.


"One recent development to make note of is with Reanimator, where increasingly players have been cutting red. No more [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) but rather [Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker)'s Instinct and [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy), because the former is card disadvantage when opponents actually fight over the graveyard, with [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace), [Ground Seal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ground+Seal), or [Purify the Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purify+the+Grave).


"It seems Zombies are completely gone, or at least gone underground, biding their time. Consequently, [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) has been getting cut or at least reduced in numbers, which is a boon to the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) that's started to show up in some blue-white decks again," Sutor explained. "On the other hand, the Zombies have a good matchup against UW Flash, so there might be a comeback in the making there."

Saturday, 10:58 a.m. – What Would Frank Karsten (and the Rest of the Coverage Team) Play?
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

As I am writing this, 1731 players are waiting to hand in their decklists; the dealers are taking a momentary breath of relief; Richard Hagon, Marijn Lybaert, Matej Zatlkaj and Steven Leeming are juggling cameras and wires to set up the video stream with live commentary; the judges are taking care of the tournament logistics; and Tobi Henke and I are hunched over our laptops writing text coverage pieces. There's always a lot going on behind the scenes (in fact, Richard Hagon has been up until 5 am last night trying to fix some technical problems with the equipment ... hopefully everything will work today), and in general providing the event coverage is a lot of fun. But in the days leading up to this Grand Prix, I couldn't help myself thinking about which deck I would consider playing if I would be participating in the Grand Prix.


My weapon of choice, if I would compete, would be Blue-White Flash. As the current Standard format is littered with expensive 5-drops such as [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), the tempo that can be gained by Syncopating them for two mana is very appealing. I would trust the creator of the Blue-White Flash archetype, Adam Prosak, and play his latest version as streamed on his Twitch channel AProsak.

![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Moorland+Haunt)


















 

I simply love the way this deck plays. Except for [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) and [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike), each card in the maindeck can be cast on the opponent's turn. This brings back fond memories of the Faeries deck that I enjoyed playing in 2008-2009. Back then, my opponents had to go through all kind of hoops to simultaneously play around [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) and [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), only to fall into an [Agony Warp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) / [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) trap. Today, if Blue-White Flash passes the turn with four mana up, the opponent is in a similar bind: he will have to play around countermagic, [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon), and [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm). This is often rather tricky as the opponent doesn't know what the Blue-White Flash player has in hand; [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) has rotated out of Standard, after all.



![Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Restoration+Angel)

Another big draw of Blue-White Flash is its cards' flexibility and interactions. [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), for instance, is surprisingly versatile: you can not only mill yourself to turn on [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) but also your opponent to deal with a problem creature put there by [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm). Furthermore, casting [Rewind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rewind) plus [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) in a single turn is just filthy. And Prosak's build has some spicy one-offs in the sideboard: [Lyev Skyknight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lyev+Skyknight) has great synergy with [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), and [Misthollow Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misthollow+Griffin) plus [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) can help a lot in long, grindy games.


I am probably not the only player thinking about Blue-White Flash this weekend. Yesterday, I asked on Twitter what the most popular deck was going to be in Bochum, and Blue-White Flash was an oft-mentioned answer. It won the StarCityGames.com Standard Open in Dallas last weekend, has been prominently featured in various **Magic** strategy columns, and it has been winning a lot on **Magic Online**, as chronicled in [Jacob van Lunen's excellent overview of the format](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/pr/221). This exposure of the deck suggests that many players will try to beat it.



![Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cavern+of+Souls)

And the deck is certainly not unbeatable. Cards like [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) and [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) elegantly sidestep the counterspells. And cheap utility creatures such as [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) or [Ulvenwald Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Tracker) are difficult to deal with, as Blue-White Flash can only muster [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) as an answer. Now, if we look at Grand Prix Auckland, which was held two weeks ago, there were only two copies of [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) in the Top 8. For this weekend, I would expect players to add more [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) to their decks in anticipation of an uptick in countermagic. I already saw a couple lists containing 4 [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) amongst the Grand Prix Trial winning decks, and even the Blue-White Flash list above even contains several copies of the land for the mirror match. As everyone knows about Blue-White Flash now and will now be prepared, it might not even be the best deck for this tournament. Indeed, it may be a week too late for Blue-White Flash to shine.


Yet, I still like how the deck continually keeps the opponents guessing, and I believe that everyone should always try to play a deck that fits their personal style and that they enjoy battling with. Well, that's the perspective from a Pro Tour Hall of Famer. But there's a lot of talent on the rest of the coverage team as well, and I went around to ask them which deck they would play themselves.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/Coverage_team.jpg) *Your event coverage team this weekend. From left to right: Frank Karsten, Tobi Henke, Richard Hagon, Matej Zatlkaj, and Marijn Lybaert* 
**Four-time Pro Tour Top 8 competitor Marijn Lybaert:** "I would probably play White-Green-Black tokens because [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) plus [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) or quick Planeswalkers seem good against Blue-White Flash. If not that, I would run Blue Reanimator. With [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) and [Tracker's Instincts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker%27s+Instincts), that deck can let a [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) loose on the battlefield every turn starting from turn 4."


**Pro Tour Berlin 2008 finalist Matej Zatlkaj:** "I also expect a lot of [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls). I would still play a Blue-White deck, but not the Flash variant. Instead, I would go for a control build that focuses on powerful cards and Planeswalkers, not on small incremental advantages or counterspells."


**Grand Prix Brussels 2004 winner Tobias Henke:** "I would play Mono Red Aggro because I enjoy aggressive decks, and I want to smash opponents with [Stromkirk Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Noble) into [Lightning Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Mauler) into [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) into [Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider)."


**Pro Tour Statistician Richard Hagon:** "I would play Turbo-Fog ([as built by Gavin Verhey](http://www.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/rc/220)). I expect it to win the Grand Prix because it doesn't care about people that are trying to return [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) with [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) -- it just casts another [Fog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog) and ticks up [Otherworld Atlas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Otherworld+Atlas)."

Saturday, 11:23 a.m. – The Grand Prix from the Eyes of the Dealers
------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

The dealers always have some of the best insight into the latest developments. After all, what sells well before the tournament begins is a good indicator of what decks people will be playing. So, Marijn Lybaert and I asked some of the dealers which cards people were desperate to get their hands on.


The big story is [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) and [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls). No dealer could seem to keep them in stock. Players have only recently caught on to the power of [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), but it is certainly one of the most backbreaking late-game plays in the format. [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls), on the other hand, will make life difficult for the wizards slinging countermagic. Blue-White Control has definitely been on a rise these last few weeks, but it seems like people are gearing up this time around.

The sideboard cards that are selling well include [Righteous Blow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Blow), [Seraph of Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seraph+of+Dawn), and [Rhox Faithmender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender), which means that the Mono Red Aggro players will likely run into a bunch of life-gain cards and cheap removal this weekend. [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) was also in high demand, indicating that players are expecting to face [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) in Bochum.


Besides these cards, we also heard about a number of commons that were in high demand: [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame), [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter), [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread), and [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) -- a whole variety of different strategies being represented here.


Finally, [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) were selling very well. They may very well be the best creature and the best Planeswalker in the format right now. If the sale of these cards is any indication, Bant Control will be a force to be reckoned with in Bochum this weekend.

Saturday, 1:48 p.m. – Beating Thragtusk
---------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

[Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) is the consensus best card in Standard right now. Two weeks ago, there were 20 copies of [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) in Top 8 of Grand Prix Auckland. For this weekend, Hall of Famer Kai Budde put the over/under on the amount of [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) in Bochum's Top 8 at 16.5, indicating that he expects 4-5 decks sporting a full playset of [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) in the Top 8. I took the under because only 4 out of 12 Grand Prix Trial winning decklists were playing [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and I am expecting some innovation and funky non-[Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) decks. But it will likely be close, and the Twitterverse has been split on the issue. Feel free to join in the discussion with the #gpbochum hash tag.



![Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thragtusk)

But how does one go about beating [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)? I'd like to answer this with an analogy. Let us consider an imaginary format where one has to choose a number X and play the corresponding deck filled with only lands and X/X creatures for X mana. In such a format, a deck with only 5/5s for five mana will lose to a blazingly fast deck with only 1/1s for one mana. The 5/5 deck will also lose to a slightly slower deck with only 6/6s for six mana. But the 5/5 deck will beat a deck filled with 4/4s for four mana and a deck containing only 8/8s for eight mana.


So, you can beat [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) by going much faster ("go under"; the 1/1 deck) or slightly slower ("go big"; the 6/6 deck). You'll lose to it by going slightly faster (the 4/4 deck) or much slower (the 8/8 deck). With that analogy in mind, it becomes easier to classify the various strategies that are being played this weekend. I walked along the top tables during Round 3 to see how people were approaching the format.


### Decks that go under


I saw a surprisingly large amount players trying to win before a [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) comes down. First, there were a bunch of hyper-aggressive Mono Red Aggro decks with a ton of fast 1-drops that use [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf) and/or [Thundermaw Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) to evade [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk). There were also a few Zombies players aiming to use [Crippling Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Blight) to get their [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler)s past [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk). [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) was also spotted flying over [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk).


The aggro deck of choice, judging by the top tables that I saw, appears to be Green-White. Its mana curve starts fast and early, and a [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) enchanted with [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) can tear through [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk). In conjunction with [Sublime Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel), Green-White can pump out mind-blowing amounts of damage every turn that make the 5 life gained by [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) insignificant in comparison.


### Decks that go big


The necromancers were out in full force. Within the course of a few minutes, I saw several [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) targeting [Angel of Serenity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity). Besides the 5/6 Angel, I also saw players trying to go big with [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), [Nicol Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nicol+Bolas), [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth), and [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return). All these cards are the analogue of the 6/6 creatures for 6 mana.


So far I've only seen one guy trying to go completely over the top with [Epic Experiment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Epic+Experiment) into [Army of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Army+of+the+Damned), but we'll have to wait if that isn't a bit too ambitious. No sign of [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) yet... That card is a ton of fun, but it's similar to the 8/8 creatures for 8 mana: a bit too slow for this format.


From this cursory glance, Standard looks pretty diverse. All kinds of strategies, from big to small, are viable and competitive. But the pros with three byes haven't joined the fray to unveil their crazy brews, so stay tuned to find out how the tournament evolves!

Round 4 Feature Match – Stanislav Cifka (Blue-White Control) vs. Thomas Angelmahr (Jund)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

This round pitted together Platinum level Pro Stanislav Cifa from the Czech Republic, fresh off his win at Pro Tour *Return to Ravnica* with Eggs, and Thomas Angelmahr from Austria, whose relative lack of experience makes him the underdog in this matchup. Cifka is running an interesting Blue-White control deck without any win conditions except for [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and [Elixir of Immortality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality). Angelmahr is running a Jund deck featuring all the best cards in Red, Green, and Black.


### Game 1


The game began with Angelmahr casting threats and Cifka casting answers.


[Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk)? [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter).


[Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren)? [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict).


[Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells)? Another [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict).


[Rakdos Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune)? Put it on top with [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) and [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) it afterwards.


[Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) again? Another [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate).


[Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return)? [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate).



![Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought)

Cifa then pulled ahead with [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) and [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought), while Angelmahr was stuck with [Tribute to Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribute+to+Hunger) and [Ultimate Price](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ultimate+Price) in hand. As Cifka's deck didn't contain any creatures, these removal spells were useless.


A couple turns later, Cifka found [Elixir of Immortality](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elixir+of+Immortality) to increase the spell density of his deck and followed it up with a [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for 10. With Cifka in stern control of the game, Angelmahr was just waiting for Cifka to finish the game. The only threat Cifka had, however, was [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought), which was ominously ticking up every turn.


Eventually Jace reached 8 loyalty counters and its ultimate went down. After looking through Angelmahr's deck, Cifka chose to steal [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) and started making 3/3 tokens. Now, Angelmahr's creature removal spells were turned on, but they would not provide a permanent answer to Garruk.


Angelmahr despondently played on for a couple of turns, but after Cifka activated the second Planeswalker ultimate of the game (with [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) this time), Angelmahr conceded the game to save time.


Stanislav Cifka 1 - Thomas Angelmahr 0


### Game 2


Angelmahr, on the play, kept his 7-card opening hand, but Cifka had to think for a while. He looked at 3 [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains); [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation); [Jace, Memory Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept); and two [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate). He chose to mulligan because he lacked blue mana and, moreover, two of his blue cards required double blue. After the match, he mentioned that he possibly should've taken the gamble and have kept it after all. But as it ended up, Cifka went into the second game with a 6-card hand.


[Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) came down on turn 1 from Angelmahr, although he chose not to attack with it into Cifka's board of [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress) and [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) in fear of [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm). Next up, Angelmahr cast [Slaughter Games](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) and named [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate). He snatched one from Cifka's hand and removed a copy from Cifka's library.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/r4-Thomas-Angelmahr.jpg) *Thomas Angelmahr* 
[Slaughter Games](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games) revealed that Cifka was holding two [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought), which came down on the subsequent turns for brief mini [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) stints before being pecked off by [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) plus [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run).


Next up was [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) for Angelmahr, but Cifka destroyed it with [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). The 3/3 Beast token that was left behind and a freshly cast [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) then started assaulting Cifka, while the Czech Pro Tour winner started digging through his deck with [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), staying at a healthy life total while at it.


As the game progressed, we saw Cifka trying to get through with [Lone Revenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lone+Revenant), while Angelmahr was continually making chump blockers with [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) (on his side of the board for a change, in contrast to the preceding game). Cifka answered that by tapping down the 3/3 Beast tokens with [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage). The first Tamiyo fell to [Dreadbore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore), but a second copy stuck around and allowed the [Lone Revenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lone+Revenant) to take out [Garruk, Primal Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) for good.


After that, Cifka was in firm control of the game. To close out the game, Cifka chose to go with [Jace, Memory Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Memory+Adept) and started milling for 10 turn after turn. Angelmahr tried to fight back with [Rakdos's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) for 6, dealing the damage to Cifka rather than one of his Planeswalkers in the hope winning the game before time ran out.


But it was to no avail. Cifka was able to survive the discard spell with a huge [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) in hand, and Angelmahr's deck was reduced to 0 cards with just 1 minute left on the clock.


Stanislav Cifka 2 - Thomas Angelmahr 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/r4-Stanislav-Cifka.jpg) *Stanislav Cifka* 
After the match, I asked Angelmahr about his decision to name [Syncopate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) with the [Slaughter Games](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Games). He mentioned that he wanted to be able to resolve his spells, and he felt that he could deal with Planeswalkers by simply attacking.


I also asked Cifka why he chose to play a deck that is so light on win conditions. Wouldn't the clock be a problem? "I may very well end up with some draws, but I still felt that the deck is so good that I had to play it," Cifka answered. "If I didn't play it, I would blame myself because I think it's the best in the format." Well, his deck decision has worked out well so far, as Cifka moves to 4-0.

Saturday, 5:15 p.m. – Remember Your Triggers!
---------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

A month or two ago, a new Missed Trigger policy went into effect for events at Competitive Rules Enforcement Level and higher. The big thing you need to know is that all of your triggers must be announced now. It doesn't matter whether they are optional or not, good or not; you have to announce them, or at least acknowledge them (say, by clearly pointing at your cards), in order for them to happen.


This includes the triggers without a visual representation, such as [Sublime Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sublime+Archangel)'s Exalted, [Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyreheart+Wolf)'s attack trigger, or [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought)'s "Until your next turn, whenever a creature an opponent controls attacks, it gets -1/-0 until end of turn" ability. Previously, even if you didn't notice your trigger, it was assumed to have happened; this is no longer the case.



![Pyreheart Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pyreheart+Wolf)

Two weeks ago at the Modern Grand Prix in Lyon, this led to an awkward situation for a [Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) player who cast a [Pact of the Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+the+Titan) in the hope that his opponent would lose the game on the next upkeep. That did not happen because the [Hive Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hive+Mind) trigger wasn't explicitly announced. And today, there have been many stories of people eating Pyreheart Wolves because their opponents failed to mention the attack trigger.


I talked to several players to hear their thoughts on this new policy. Some are happy that they don't have to help their opponents beat them anymore by having to point of mandatory triggers. Others, including several Hall of Famers, believe that the announcements of mandatory obvious triggers is a waste of time, and that the cutthroat approach of calling people out on failure to announce their mandatory triggers takes away the fun in **Magic**.


I asked head judge Jurgen Baert what his take on the issue is. "The new policy works well in the majority of the cases, but it's a new policy and we still have to work on the remaining cases. The obvious triggers without a visual representation are indeed an issue. But once everyone gets more accustomed to clearly announcing everything, it will likely get better and not give any problems anymore. Also, the new policy is now easy to understand (for both judges and players) and accomplishes our goal of making remembering triggers a skill tester."


For now, just remember to remember your triggers, and remember to remember your opponent you remembered them.

Saturday, 5:40 p.m. – Sworn Enemies
-----------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

It's an ages old, iconic battle that keeps repeating itself again and again. From time immemorial, Mono-Red and Blue-White have faced off against each other in various forms and formats, and quite often game one favored the aggressive red deck, while the somewhat clunky control deck had to fight back with the help of sideboard cards. In the beginning it was [Circle of Protection: Red](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circle+of+Protection%3A+Red), more recently [Kor Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker), but white always offered some strong options.


Nowadays, the situation is much more dire for UW, but far from hopeless. Of course, there's the usual stuff: extra removal, more cheap spells. The most important sideboard card, however, seems to be [Seraph of Dawn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seraph+of+Dawn), a card which prompted the fire mages to in turn bring in [Thunderbolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderbolt).

Round 7 Feature Match - Yorick Pieters (Blue-White-Red) vs. Maria Artamanova (Green-White-Black)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*
I was particularly happy when this feature match came up. Remember [this story](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpmos12/day1#0) from Grand Prix Moscow a couple of months back? Maria Artamanova may have won big on the weekend back then, but unfortunately not in the actual Grand Prix. This time, however, she was indeed winning in the main event. Both she and her opponent had six wins so far, looking for a seventh.


### Game 1


Maria's [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) died to [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame), but her [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) at least prevented [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) from doing anything with said Pillar. Yorick's Mage got in for a couple of points of damage before Maria cast [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and put a stop to that.


Yorick had stumbled on lands, had in fact just found his fourth, whereas Maria appeared to be slightly flooded. However, one of her lands was [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township), which threatened to turn her [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens into a formidable force. Luckily, Yorick had [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) to take out all four of them.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm7_yorick.JPG)*Yorick Pieters*




Maria redoubled with [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), and when Yorick was forced to try to get rid of that with another [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere), [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) not only stopped that, but also provided yet another attacker. Yorick thought for a moment, then nodded and picked up his cards. "Yup. That's it."


Yorick Pieters 0-1 Maria Artamanova


### Game 2


"I know exactly which five cards I want to see now," Yorick said while taking his second mulligan. His deck had already been less than friendly to him in game one, and seemed determined to continue in that vein.


Interestingly though, Yorick started perfectly with [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) on turn three, whereas Maria had nothing but [Grisly Salvage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) and [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), developing no board presence at all. Her [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) went uncontested, but Yorick had [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) at the end of her turn and [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) on his, getting in another 8, putting Maria at 9.


On turn five, all Maria could do was to summon [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) and pass the turn. Yorick, on the other side, cast [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage), tapped down Maria's sole blocker, and had his team come crashing in again.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm7_maria.JPG)*Maria Artamanova*




But now things turned ugly for Yorick. [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) took out his [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere), returning Centaur Helaer to the battlefield, bringing Maria back to 12 and giving her a blocker for [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). She fell to 6, but suddenly Yorick was left with [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) as his only creature.


Tamiyo soon died to counter attacks, and Maria also had a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). Yorick had a replacement [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and tried to race Maria's three creatures plus [Vault of the Archangel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+of+the+Archangel). A tricky proposition, to be sure, but he might have actually gotten there with the help of [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) on his [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and flashback [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon). Maria, however, had another well-timed [Abrupt Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) for the Mage, and Yorick extended his hand in concession.


Yorick Pieters 0-2 Maria Artamanova



Round 7 Feature Match -Martin Jůza vs. Fabian Wachter
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Oliver Gehrmann*
Facing each other in round 7 are Pro Tour regular Martin Jůza from the Czech Republic and up-and-coming German sensation Fabian Wachter. Both haven't lost a single match so far, but only one of them will come out of this match with a perfect record. Let's see how this goes!


### Game 1


Jůza won the die roll and opted to go first. A look at his opening seven revealed four lands and he considered this for a minute before deciding to keep. [Temple Garden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden) came down, Wachter, who also kept his opening hand, matched it with a [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress) and play was back to Jůza.


He upped the ante with Woodland Cemetry and [Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker)'s Instincts, revealing three lands that all went to his graveyard and fetching Avacyn's Pilgrim. No play from Wachter, who was seemingly comfortable with interacting on the opposing turns.


Jůza cast [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and Avacyn's Pilgrim, but Wachter made short use of the former with the help of a [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere).


Grisly Salvage got Jůza [Somberwald Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Sage), he played it and play was back to Wachter. He passed with no actions.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm7_juza.JPG)*Martin Jůza*




Jůza now added [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb) and took 2, he then invited [Craterhoof Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) to the party and that meant a whopping 15 damage for Wachter! He had only 5 life remaining!


Wachter needed a miracle and apparently he found it! Unfortunately, he added it to his hand before casting it and the judge confirmed that he would no longer be able to play it under that circumstance. He did have a Planeswalker, but staring down Jůza's full field, that wouldn't be of much use.


Martin Jůza 1–0 Fabian Wachter


### Game 2


Both players took only little time accessing their sideboards. Wachter would have the advantage of going first the second game, but would it be enough for the underdog to overcome the highly favored Czech veteran?


Wachter opted to take a mulligan and when he asked Jůza whether he would keep his opening seven, he quickly replied, „Not sure." Eventually, he decided against taking the risk and kept.


Wachter had no plays on turn one, while Jůza took 2 from [Temple Garden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden) and followed it up with [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf).


Wachter started accelerating with [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), searching a [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain), he took the damage from the [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and Jůza added a second copy.


Just a fourth mana source for Wachter, while Jůza now invited [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) to the party. At the end of his opponent's turn, Wachter casted [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). He followed it up with [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage) on his own turn four (he was one land ahead courtesy of [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek)). He used its first ability on [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) and passed his turn.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/fm7_wachter.JPG)*Fabian Wachter*




Jůza had a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) of his own and after Wachter passed, only using the effect of Tamiyo, Jůza went in for an attack! Wachter assigned his own [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) as the blocker for Jůza's copy, but an activation of [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) made sure Jůza's Angel would get to see another turn while Wachter was left with only his Planeswalker! On top of that, Tamiyo took 2 damage that the Elves dealt.


Wachter used the Planeswalker's ability again, but he passed without performing any further actions. Jůza attacked once again, this time dealing with the Planeswalker once and for all. His follow-up play, [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), fell victim to [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate).


Wachter tried to sport a comeback with [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), but all it could do was buy him some more time as Jůza's board turned sideways once again. Wachter was left with a 3/3.


He attacked the following turn with it, tried to find an answer with Sphinx's [Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revelation), but his deck wouldn't answer the call, causing him to shuffle up his cards.


Martin Jůza 2–0 Fabian Wachter


Jůza remains undefeated!

Round 8 Feature Match - Lukas Tajak (Blue-White Delver) vs. Jan-Moritz Merkel (an amazing Nightshade Peddler brew)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*
This all-German feature match threw together two players at pristine 7-0 records: Lukas Tajak, who is fresh off a PTQ win from the preceding weekend, and Jan-Moritz Merkel, the winner of Pro Tour Kobe 2006. Tajak is a member of the large group of German players who are running Blue-White [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)---a list that looks very similar to the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck that was dominating Standard half a year ago. Merkel has something even more exciting up his sleeve: his deck features the mind-blowing combo of [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) and [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster). He also has [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) to soulbond with. Merkel certainly likes to make sure that every ping is lethal.


### Game 1


Tajak had a quick start in turn 2 [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and turn 3 [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), which put Merkel down to a low life total in short order. Merkel, in the meantime, ramped up his mana with [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek). His board of Woodland Cemetary, [Blood Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt), [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents), elicited a puzzled "okay" from Tajak, who didn't appear to understand what deck he would be facing.


Merkel didn't waste a lot of time showing what his deck was capable of. First, [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) took care of [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) Then, [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) followed and soulbonded with the 0/3 pinger. This allowed Merkel to keep [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) under control by blocking it and shooting down the 4/4 Angel token each turn.





![Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nightshade+Peddler)



[Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) made life more difficult for Merkel, turning [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) into a huge first striker (courtesy of [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour)). Merkel first threw his [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) in front and subsequently upgraded it with [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), which could soulbond with [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) now that [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) had died.


Next, Merkel used [Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker)'s Instinct to find several copies of [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), which started to chumpblock [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). This meant that Tajak was now unable to get through for additional damage. And all the while, [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) was ticking up and up and up.


Tajak did his best trying to get past Merkel's creatures, but the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) he mustered was no match for [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren). A couple turns later, Merkel actually started pinging his own creatures to grow Olivia, and moved in for the kill.


Jan-Moritz Merkel 1 - Lukas Tajak 0


While sideboarding, the players discussed how a single [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) or [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) for Tajak could have changed the entire game. But as it was, Tajak failed to draw any of them and simply could not deal with Merkel's utility creatures.


### Game 2


Tajak started with a turn 1 [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), but failed to flip it on his second or third turn. "Oh no... I have to flip it quickly before [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) comes out," he said. (Or at least, that's my best translation of the German table chatter.) [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) did indeed come out to take down [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), and was quickly joined by his friend [Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler) once again.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/Jan-Mortiz%20Merkel.JPG)*Jan-Mortiz Merkel*




Tajak, in the meantime, added pressure to the board in form of [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and [Runechanter's Pike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike), almost making this game seem like a copy of the first one. The big difference, however, was [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction). This innovative card from the German [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck detained [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster) for a turn, and then for another turn thanks to [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage). This allowed Tajak to sneak his [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and 4/4 Angel tokens to move past Merkel's defenses.


Jan-Moritz Merkel 1 - Lukas Tajak 1


While shuffling for game 3, Tajak gave Merkel some well-deserved compliments on his deck choice. "Schon'n sickes deck", I overheard.


### Game 3


Merkel, on the play for the final game, used [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) to ramp into a turn 3 [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren). Tajak, on the other side of the table, had an excellent start with [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) (which immediately transformed into [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration)) and [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) (which was promptly equipped with [Spectral Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight)).


[Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) fell to [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), however, and the game turned into an interesting damage race when Merkel added [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) to his board.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpboc12/Lukas%20Tajak.JPG)*Lukas Tajak*




At this point, time was called on the round. The various attacking and blocking decisions in the preceding games had apparently taken too many seconds off the clock. Merkel, at 6 life, had [Falkenrath Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) and a 5/5 [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) in play. Tajak, at 15 life, had a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) that was enchanted with [Spectral Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) in play. Could any player win in the five additional turns?


"To attack or to go on the defense? It's really close," Merkel remarked. "Ich mach defense," he eventually decided, passing the turn without attacking. Tajak, lacking any [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction) or other tricks, was unable to deal Merkel another point of damage, and the match concluded with a draw.


Jan-Moritz Merkel 1 - Lukas Tajak 1


Both players, who had already secured their Day 2 berths, move to 7-0-1!

Magic in Luxembourg
-------------------


*by Frank Karsten*
Luxembourg is a tiny European country bordered by Belgium, France, and Germany. It has just over 500,000 inhabitants. That's much less than, say, the city of San Diego! Still, there were a lot of players from Luxembourg in attendance today. Luxembourger Nigel Rowledge from the Troll And Toad store dropped by today to express his amazement at the recent uptick in Luxembourgian Grand Prix attendance.


"There is a double-digit number of players from Luxembourg in the Grand Prix this weekend," Nigel said. "I believe this weekend marks the highest Luxembourgian Grand Prix attendance ever. There are also several Southern Belgian players from our Friday Night **Magic** community in contention as well. We used to have only 1 or 2 players at Grand Prix tournaments, and we now have about 16 coming to an international event. And it's not just the Grand Prix: ten months ago, it was a struggle to get 8 players for an FNM, but now we have 25+."


When asked how that came to be, Nigel mentioned that everyone had worked together---with Steve Hatto and Daniel Alonzo in particular putting in a lot of effort---to aggressively go out and get the kitchen table **Magic** players back into the tournament scene. Discount tournaments had also helped to promote the player community. Additionally, Nigel said that it was nice that a brick-and-mortar store had started to stock **Magic** stuff.


One player from Luxembourg, Steve Hatto, has made Day 2 with a 7-1-1 record, so all of his compatriots will certainly be cheering for him and having a great weekend all around.
