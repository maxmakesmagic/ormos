*Champions* of IntoTheAether
============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 9, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar











I'm still suffering from *Champions of Kamigawa* fever. [Last week](/en/articles/archive/intotheaether-does-kamigawa-2004-11-02) I talked about the *Champions* Release Events, which was my awkward, bumbling attempt to learn the new cards through Sealed Deck and Draft (many people were quick to point out the awesomeness of the White in my first Sealed League that I somehow neglected). Very soon I'll jump into a four-week *Champions* Sealed League. For now, though, *Champions of Kamigawa* is flowing freely online so it's time to turn an eye towards Constructed...

Today I'm going to revisit the ***Magic** Online* Constructed formats I've described previously--Emperor, Prismatic, PDC, Tribal Wars, and Singleton--now that *Champions of Kamigawa* has arrived. In fact, I'm going to pick a deck I built for each and see how it might change with *Champions*. Why? Because many people approach new sets less by what new decks are possible, and more by how old decks are changed. I usually start with the new, but for whatever reason today I'm feeling rather nostalgic for the old. 

Also, as a caveat, I’m going to be covering a lot of ground today, so I'm going to take a break from giving actual game results (though I did play each deck at least once) and instead just focus on the decks. 

### No Love of Metal

![Glissa Sunseeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glissa+Sunseeker)My first Constructed stop was the weird and wild world of Emperor. Returning to Emperor was interesting for me because it was a chance to peek back at the Multiplayer room and see if any format besides 1-1-1 had stuck since my [Three Days of Emperor Fun](/en/articles/archive/intotheaether-has-no-clothes-2004-09-14). Sadly, the only games I saw running were 1-1-1 games, so I once again “went rogue” and advertised a 2-2-all Standard Emperor game for a couple of evenings. People gave me a little grief for choosing Standard as the format, but I figured I would stay true to my original experiment. Besides, the whole point was to retool one of my old Emp decks which were all built for Standard.If you recall from my Three Days of Fun, because of a misclick on my part I actually didn't have the chance to play one of my flanker decks, called Metal Haters. The idea behind the deck was to both take advantage of the fact that everyone was playing artifacts thanks to the *Mirrodin* block and, just in case, to turn *everything* into artifacts via [Mycosynth Lattice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Lattice). It was a fun idea, and I was disappointed not to see the deck in action.

Now that *Champions of Kamigawa* has arrived in Standard and kicked *Onslaught* Block out, I wasn't sure it made sense to stick with the Elf theme in the deck since I lost [Wirewood Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Herald). Dropping Elves as a design criteria allowed me to use the Lattice for things like [Tanglewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tanglewalker) and [Ouphe Vandals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ouphe+Vandals). I also focused more on [Glissa Sunseeker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glissa+Sunseeker) as an artifact killer now that I had access to [Time of Need](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+of+Need). A few notes on the changes: 

* It's monogreen now. This means I lose the whole Lattice-[Leonin Abunas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Abunas) interaction, but I had too many other things I wanted to try in Green. I thought about splashing Blue for [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate), but I think there are still enough artifacts running around that I don't need to rely wholly on [Mycosynth Lattice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Lattice).
* [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) has given way to the much-better [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) and [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach).
* Since I was already using [Time of Need](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+of+Need) and getting lots of land, I put in [Jugan, the Rising Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jugan%2C+the+Rising+Star) and [Myojin of Life's Web](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myojin+of+Life%27s+Web). Then I had a funny realization that in Emperor, Jugan dying is a huge boon for your team so I added a second copy. Can you imagine playing a second Jugan on purpose? Mu ha ha!

It's a flanker deck, but it also works reasonably well as an emperor. When I am surrounded by teammates, I can focus less on survival and more on getting the Lattice out to interfere with the opposing team. Expect me to continue to pop into Multiplayer over the next few weeks, and expect me to be packin' hate for all things metal.

[![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/global/images/mtgcom_feature_237_pic1_en.jpg)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/237)As always, I'm going to lean heavily on Doug Beyer's take on Prismatic, especially since last week he talked about [*Champions'* effect](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/feature/237) on his favorite format. Based on Doug's article and my own pondering, here are the changes I've made:

* Once again, [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) and [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) are grrreat. In the Door deck, I've dropped [Pentad Prism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentad+Prism) and [Explosive Vegetation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Vegetation) for them. The Prism always sort of bugged me in the deck because there was nothing to do with the artifact once it had used its counters and because it was useless with [Roar of Reclamation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+Reclamation). I'm a big fan of [Explosive Vegetation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Vegetation), but [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) simply pulls two lands faster.
* One of the cards that has bugged me in my most recent version is [Skeletal Scrying](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Scrying), largely because a) I *want* cards in my graveyard, and b) I'm trying desperately to survive, so the life-loss is often more than I can afford. *Champions* seemed like a good opportunity to ditch the Scrying for some other black card. My choices were: [Hideous Laughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+Laughter), which is like an Instant-speed [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) 5-8; [Nezumi Graverobber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Graverobber), which is like a [Withered Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch) and [Zombify](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify) rolled into one (both of which my deck could use); and [Rend Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rend+Flesh), which is just plain 'ole good creature removal. I decided on [Rend Flesh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rend+Flesh), mostly by process of elimination. I worried about [Hideous Laughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+Laughter)'s double-black cost and the Graverobber, while really cool, seems to fragment what my deck is trying to do (basically I worried about the temptation to beat on my opponents with their own creatures instead of get [Door to Nothingness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+to+Nothingness) into play).
* [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) has so much synergy with the idea of loading up my graveyard to return a [Door to Nothingness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+to+Nothingness) to play that it's practically cater-made for my deck. I decided to drop [Complicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Complicate) for it. My deck doesn't really have the ability to reliably counter anything of substance, and I had been using [Complicate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Complicate) mostly for its Cycling ability. [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) has a lot more card-drawing and tutoring horsepower than Cycling.

Other considerations included: [Heartbeat of Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartbeat+of+Spring), [Ghostly Prison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison), and [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), each of which helps the deck but which I couldn't find space to add. The toughest call for me was whether to keep [Renewed Faith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renewed+Faith) or add [Ghostly Prison](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison). This may still be a change I make down the line. For now, though, here's the deck:

*Champions of Kamigawa* dumps a whole new pack of Humans into the mix. I'm still a big fan of decks focused on “cogs,” so I want to keep the core of my Human deck as [Trinket Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) and [Auriok Salvagers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Salvagers). I think there are now enough Humans in blue to help out a deck like this and focus its color base. Gone are the cheap thrills of [Disciple of the Vault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) and [Moriok Rigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moriok+Rigger) (the Disciple always sort of bums me out in the Casual room, frankly), which have been replaced by the card-drawing goodness of [Azami, Lady of Scrolls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azami%2C+Lady+of+Scrolls) (remember that [Trinket Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) and [Thought Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier) are Wizards too) and [Jushi Apprentice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jushi+Apprentice). I have also found room for [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), which is by far the best cog to appear in *Champions*.

Almost a Wizards deck, but trickily Human. To me, the coolest thing about this deck, like any “toolbox” deck, is its versatility. Any deck where [Meekstone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meekstone) shines is cool in my book. Any deck with so many justifiable one-, two-, and three-copies of cards is also pretty neat.

### May I Please Have Another

Singleton is the only format where I didn't choose to retool a pet deck per se. I have [a pet Singleton deck](/en/articles/archive/intotheaether-keeps-it-simple-2004-10-26), but I'm actually rather happy with how it plays and I don't see a huge opportunity right now to change cards. I mean, oh sure... [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) fits into the deck, but you could have predicted me saying that, right?

Instead, I made a Singleton deck with a cool idea behind it that in practice was just, I don't know, boring. The deck was based on +1/+1 counters, listed first in [my initial Singleton article](/en/articles/archive/intotheaether-loves-counters-2004-10-19). This deck was an example of my “Embrace the One” strategy.

The reason I found the deck boring in practice was because it didn't have enough really spectacular things it did with its counters. A [Chlorophant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chlorophant) with threshold is always cool. A [Power Conduit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit), [Energy Chamber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Energy+Chamber), or [Dragon Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Blood) with things like [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) or [Pentavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pentavus) is spiffy. But a continually-2/2 [Clockwork Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockwork+Beetle)? Less exciting. 

*Champions of Kamigawa* brings two very fun counter-based cards: [Jugan, the Rising Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jugan%2C+the+Rising+Star) and [Orochi Hatchery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orochi+Hatchery) (man was I happy to see the Hatchery use charge counters and man was I disappointed that the Myojin use “divinity” counters). I added these to the deck, put in the requisite [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) and [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach), and added both [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and [Restock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restock) so that I can do my cool things more often. The result is a deck that has a much higher percentage of tricky tricks and splashy effects, and is overall a lot less boring to play:
