Grand Prix Seattle 2005
-----------------------


The Rock Shines in the Emerald City!
====================================











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpseat05/win.jpg)**Ernie Marchesano is the Grand Prix Seattle Champion!**


Several different players commented before the tournament that they thought anything was viable in this format, with the exception of maybe Rock or Tog. Those same players were watching the finals between Ernie Marchesano with Rock and Taylor Putnam playing his aggro Tog deck. Ernie took down the match in two games after [Cranial Extraction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Extraction) took out his opponent's [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)s and his [Volrath's Stronghold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath%27s+Stronghold) prevented Taylor from being able to implement his [Brain Freeze strategy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Freeze+strategy).


Before facing off against Taylor, Ernie dispatched the tournament's top-seeded player in Grant Struck and Goblin-wielding Max McCall. The Philly native was an amateur before he crossed the threshold of the Top 32 this weekend. In addition to his $2,400 payout for his victory this weekend, Marchesano will receive an additional $1,500 as the tournament's top amateur, bringing his payout just a hundred clams shy of $4,000.


Finalist Taylor Putnam was one of three non-amateur players in the Top 8 and his [Gush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush)-a-Tog deck served him well as he took down Tsuyoshi Fujita and Shuhei Nakamura -- each for the second time this weekend -- before falling to Ernie. Fujita's deck was one of the most buzzed-about decks of the tournament. It was a super-fast [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) deck that featured four [Blazing Shoal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Shoal)s to ensure [Dragon Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Tyrant) was legal every time it sneaked into play.


The Top 8 was a nice cross section of Extended decks with two Goblins, two Rock variants, [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack), Tog, [Aluren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren), and Cephalid Breakfast. In the end, nothing beat Rock -- or at least Ernie's version of the once-thought extinct Extended staple.
