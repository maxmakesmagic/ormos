Round 8: Neil Reeves vs. Mike Turian
====================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 28, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TobyWachter.jpg)
By Toby Wachter











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a820.jpg)Reeves wasn't too happy with how [his draft turned out](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/draft-2-neil-reeves-2003-06-28). He ended up sitting across from Gerard Fabiano, Mike Turian and Craig Krempels, TOGIT teammates who sat next to each other and essentially controlled and dominated the draft. As a result, Reeves was less than enthusiastic about his chances, and is certainly not very confident about his chances against Turian. It is worth pointing out that Reeves and Turian are two of the country's top Limited players.

### Game 1

Reeves had a turn two [Sunstrike Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunstrike+Legionnaire), but no morph creature. Turian played one, and Reeves summoned [Zealous Inquisitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Inquisitor) on turn four. The Legionnaire tried to tap Turian's morph, but it flipped over to reveal [Zombie Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Cutthroat). The Inquisitor attacked for two, and Reeves built up his defense with [Daunting Defender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daunting+Defender). Turian got a good evasion creature in [Sootfeather Flock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sootfeather+Flock), and Reeves brought out [Fallen Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallen+Cleric). Just as things started to go in Reeves' favor, Turian used [Slice and Dice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slice+and+Dice) to kill all the creatures on the board. Reeves came right back with [Twisted Abomination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Abomination), while Turian had a morph creature and [Smokespew Invoker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokespew+Invoker) with seven lands in play.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a817.jpg)The Abomination attacked for five, and Reeves brought out [Screeching Buzzard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard) and [Aven Farseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Farseer), leaving him with no cards in hand. Turian untapped, played his eighth land, and killed [Screeching Buzzard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Buzzard) with the Invoker. A morph ([Skittish Valesk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skittish+Valesk)) chump blocked the Abomination, and the Farseer got through to bring Turian to four. The Abomination was next to fall to the Invoker, and the Farseer dealt one more to bring Turian to three. At this point, Reeves need to keep playing threats to outrace Turian's one-sided Abyss, and brought out [Bladewing's Thrall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bladewing%27s+Thrall). The Invoker killed the Farseer (body language suggested this may have been a mistake by Turian), which forced the Invoker to block and trade with the Thrall. [Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon) Slayer represented lethal damage, but Turian had [Crested Craghorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crested+Craghorn) to take care of the problem. Both players started to rebuild, and Turian used [Cruel Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Revival) to break the game wide open, bringing back his Invoker.

Reeves- "You're quite a lucky man, Mike Turian."

He had enough mana to put it right back into play on the same turn, and soon the game was over.

Reeves- 0 Turian- 1

### Game 2

Reeves had a [Battlefield Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlefield+Medic), followed by a morph. Turian had no turn three play, and Reeves summoned [Akroma's Devoted](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Devoted). In response, [Scattershot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattershot) killed the Medic. Turian played [Goblin Clearcutter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Clearcutter).

Reeves- "I guess if you have your one [Cruel Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Revival), you're going to win the game."

A bunch of eggs went into one basket, as [Improvised Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Improvised+Armor) enchanted [Akroma's Devoted](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Devoted) to make it huge. Reeves kept improving his board with [Zealous Inquisitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Inquisitor) and [Aven Farseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Farseer), while Turian kept playing creatures in a morph and [Embalmed Brawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Embalmed+Brawler), and was missing his fifth land.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/natus03/a816.jpg)Reeves started mocking Turian a little, making fun of the fact that he surely had [Cruel Revival](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Revival) in hand, and just needed one land to kill the armored creature. [Skinthinner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinthinner) made things even more uneven, and Turian drew his fifth land, but merely played [Crested Craghorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crested+Craghorn) and ended his turn. The morph had to chump block the Devoted while the Craghorn traded with [Skinthinner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinthinner), and the game ended soon after.

Reeves- 1 Turian- 1

### Game 3

Turian mulliganed his opening hand. Reeves had a two-drop in [Aven Farseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Farseer), while Turian passed his third land drop without playing a creature. Land number four didn't show up for a turn, and when it did, Turian still had nothing to play, thanks to a hand filled with red cards. Reeves wasn't having nearly as much trouble, playing [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber) followed by [Vengeful Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengeful+Dead).

[Aven Redeemer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Redeemer) showed up soon after, and Turian played his first creature of the game in a very late [Death's-Head Buzzard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s-Head+Buzzard). That wasn't nearly enough and [Twisted Abomination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Abomination) was just insult to injury.

**Final Result:** Reeves- 2 Turian- 1
