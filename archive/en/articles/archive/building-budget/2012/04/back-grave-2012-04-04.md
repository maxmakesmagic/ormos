Back From the Grave
===================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on April 4, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week, I experimented with a [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) deck in Modern. You can find [last week's column here](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/grave-2012-03-28). A lot of readers were interested in the deck and asked if I would continue working on it for another week. I decided to put some more time into it to see if I could make it tournament ready.

Let's start by taking a look at where the deck was last week.

First, I'd like to talk about general improvements to the deck.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab189_pox.jpg)[Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) | Art by [Ryan Panacoast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Ryan+Panacoast%22%5D)

The biggest addition is [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox). To be honest, I have no idea how I didn't have this in the original list. It's especially strong against decks that play inexpensive creatures. Think about it: I cast [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) when my opponent has one creature in play and I have zero. I discard [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), or [Sedraxis Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter) and sacrifice a land. My opponent sacrifices a land, discards a card, and loses a creature. That's actually an absurd amount of card advantage for just two mana that can, at times, completely ruin your opponent's draw. (Destroying the only land of a certain color, for example.) [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) is especially effective when opponents mulligan.

[Rise // Fall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+%2F%2F+Fall) was the least impressive card in the original deck. I tried replacing it with [Wrench Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrench+Mind) and was immediately impressed. [Rise // Fall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+%2F%2F+Fall)'s mana cost was prohibitive, whereas you can cast [Wrench Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrench+Mind) whenever you have two mana available. It also feels a lot safer to cast [Wrench Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrench+Mind) on turn two.

![Wrench Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wrench+Mind)  
[Rotting Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Rats) is quite good here, but I was never happy to draw more than one copy in a given game. I went down to two copies and I've found that I'm usually much happier when I draw one and I don't have the awkward double-[Rotting Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Rats) draw nearly as often.

![Rotting Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rotting+Rats)  
[Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) probably should have been in this deck from the beginning. The card is easy to acquire these days and it synergizes very well with the rest of the deck.

Now I'd like to talk about the different matchups and how I've changed the deck to handle them.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab189_header1.jpg)  
I played against a few Red-White and Zoo decks. I expected these matches to be difficult, but after some tweaks I was winning easily. 

[Nyxathid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nyxathid) is really strong in these matches, and I wanted an extra copy. You're usually behind on board once you empty your opponent's hand, but [Nyxathid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nyxathid) changes that very quickly. 

I found that most games against Zoo and Red-White Aggro are won by triggering [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) and chump blocking with [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens, [Rotting Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Rats), and [Sedraxis Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter)s. I wanted extra copies of [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack), so I decided to try two copies of [Nezumi Shortfang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Shortfang). The *Champions of Kamigawa* rare worked really well in the new iteration of the deck. I had cut a lot of discard to make room for [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) and an extra [Nyxathid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nyxathid), and [Nezumi Shortfang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Shortfang) is very good at playing cleanup when your opponent only has one or two cards left in hand. Once it flips, [Stabwhisker the Odious](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stabwhisker+the+Odious) is a crazy threat that deals as much as 6 damage per turn. 

I wait to cast [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) against these decks. You want to make sure you're getting one of your opponent's creatures and not losing one of your own, unless you're sacrificing something with unearth or a [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast). [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) is your worst card here—these decks have [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben); [Plated Geopede](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede); and what seems like an endless stream of 2/3s.

Both WU and RG Tron decks turned out to be the biggest problem of all for me. The deck has trouble applying enough pressure to beat [Karn Liberated](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated); [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine); or [Iona, Shield of Emeria](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iona%2C+Shield+of+Emeria). The addition of [Smallpox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) definitely helped these matchups, but I still wasn't confident that I was in a good spot. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab189_3Cards1.jpg)  
I tried [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) in the sideboard, but the RG Tron deck has too much redundancy with [Expedition Map](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) and [Sylvan Scrying](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Scrying); it would just find the Tron piece I had destroyed and I'd lose anyway. 

I decided to try [Sowing Salt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt). There are a lot of games where [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) or [Karn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) is already on the table and I lose anyway, but sometimes I was able to sneak in the big sorcery and give myself enough time to steal the game. So I tried [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) over [Sowing Salt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt). [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) could be brought in for many matchups, as it's a much less narrow card. I believe [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) to actually be better against Tron anyway. It's difficult for a Tron player to have more than one big threat before it doesn't have any cards left in hand. Trying to interact with its land was difficult because so much of the deck is dedicated to forcing perfect Tron. I've been much happier just focusing on my discard plan and using [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) to remove the big permanents from the game. 

[Nezumi Shortfang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Shortfang) and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) aren't great here. They take too long to use beneficially and they make our deck more susceptible to [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm).

Aggro Loam is a nightmare matchup for the deck. [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) can be used to essentially draw three cards against us. The lands returned from the graveyard can be discarded to [Wrench Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrench+Mind), [Augur of Skulls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Skulls), etc. 

I found that [Nihil Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) is a great sideboard card here. [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) isn't good because it affects both players' graveyards and that can mess up our [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Sedraxis Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedraxis+Specter), [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), etc. 

![Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dark+Confidant)  
[Darkblast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) and [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) are necessary tools in the fight against [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). [Augur of Skulls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Skulls) is the worst card here. Aggro Loam has multiple ways to deal 1 damage to a creature at little to no cost.

[Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) can be difficult depending on its game plan and its draw. Sometimes it feels like you just get fishbowled, but a lot of the time you're able to empty out your opponent's hand before the deck combos. 

![Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Splinter+Twin)  
[Slaughter Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact) isn't as budget as I'd like it to be, so I chose not to include it in the deck. However, if you're having a lot of trouble with [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) then I strongly recommend finding some copies. [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) players often won't have [Dispel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) or [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) left to defend the combo; they're usually going off with their last card.

The Affinity match might be slightly in its favor for the first game, but after sideboarding it becomes one of the easiest matchups I've ever played. 

[Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) with [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) is pretty much impossible for Affinity to beat. [Ancient Grudge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) is obviously insane against it. [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) is a fine reset button. [Wrench Mind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrench+Mind) is bad against artifact aggro decks for obvious reasons. [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) and [Nezumi Shortfang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Shortfang) are especially strong here because this deck empties its hand for you. It's not unusual for Affinity to have two or three cards left in hand after the first turn. 

![Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tempered+Steel)  
[Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) tokens trade with a huge amount of the deck. Think about how bad [Signal Pest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) is against [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls). Some versions of Affinity are playing [Tempered Steel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) now. If you see a decent amount of white mana in the first game I'd recommend bringing in your [Ray of Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ray+of+Revelation)s.

One would expect Melira Pod decks to give me some trouble, but I've had a surprisingly easy time beating every copy I've played against.

| Melira, Sylvok Outcast | Birthing Pod |

You can usually empty a Melira Pod's hand before your opponent has an opportunity to cast [Ranger of Eos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger+of+Eos). If your opponent doesn't have the Ranger, it becomes really hard to race your [The Rack](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) and [Nezumi Shortfang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Shortfang). 

[Nihil Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb) can be used to prevent Melira Pod from comboing, so I like to sideboard those in and wait until my opponent has no cards in hand and no combo in play to cantrip with them. 

Melira Pod's fallback plan of being a green-based beatdown deck is terrible against you. [Bloodghast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) is your worst card against Melira Pod because of [Wall of Roots](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). It's also important to note that this is one of the matches where I'm really happy to have a pair of [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) in my sideboard.
