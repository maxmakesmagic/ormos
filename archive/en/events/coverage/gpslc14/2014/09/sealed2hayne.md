 First to take a stab was Pro Tour champion and No. 18â€“ranked Alex Hayne. He was quick to point out that overall in *Magic 2015* Sealed, power is more important that synergy. Cards can play well together, but it's much better to jam in the bombs where you can.




 After dispatching the cards he considered unplayable ([Grindclock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grindclock), [Glacial Crusher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Crusher), [Leeching Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leeching+Sliver), etc.), Hayne immediately pulled out the white. Noting the [Seraph of the Masses](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seraph+of+the+Masses), [Triplicate Spirits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triplicate+Spirits), and the gigantic beast [Soul of Theros](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+of+Theros), Hayne made it clear from the get-go that he would be playing some version of a white deck. [Sungrace Pegasus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sungrace+Pegasus) and [Pillar of Light](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Light) were happy additions. Though Hayne lamented that he only had [Marked by Honor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marked+by+Honor) to go with his two Heliod's Pilgrims (though he did consider the [Battle Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Mastery) repeatedly), he was happy to put all the white in, save for [Tireless Missionaries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Missionaries), [Boonweaver Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boonweaver+Giant), [Battle Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Mastery), and the [Wall of Essence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Essence). "If the Mastery is required by context, I'll play it. [Siege Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Wurm) with a [Battle Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+Mastery) is 'big game'." Now all he needed was a second color.





  

 "The blue could be good," he mused, looking at the [Military Intelligence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Military+Intelligence) and the multiple token makers, [Coral Barrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coral+Barrier). [Military Intelligence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Military+Intelligence) can create massive advantage if you've got the creature base. He laid out the cardsâ€”[Frost Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Lynx), [Nimbus of the Isles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nimbus+of+the+Isles) and othersâ€”and remarked, "Eh, it's all right." He quickly glanced over the other colors while the Blue was still in front of him, noting red's [Scrapyard Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapyard+Mongrel)s and the big [Cone of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cone+of+Flame), then dismissed black. "The only good cards are the two [Covenant of Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Covenant+of+Blood) and the [Accursed Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accursed+Spirit)." (As a preview, David Ochoa would decide otherwise.)




 His first full build was red and white. Throwing basically all the red cards into the mix, and added [Will-Forged Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Will-Forged+Golem), [Bronze Sable](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bronze+Sable) and [Hot Soup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hot+Soup) for the [Scrapyard Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapyard+Mongrel)s and to fill the curve. "[Hot Soup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hot+Soup) is real good." He continued, "Its funny because of how bad Prowler's Helm was in the last format. But here, evasion is so impactful; forcing through damage is key, especially in Sealed." He said Black-Green in particular makes good use of [Hot Soup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hot+Soup). "And the [Thundering Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundering+Giant)s are underrated. I like them a lot." Though in other formats three toughness can be an issue, Alex said "1" is the magic number for toughness here. If it's bigger than one, it can be useful. The Red-White had some holes, but the curve was good and the aggression was basically there.




 Though Red-White would be his final build, but Hayne had to work his way to it. He laid out potential builds in each of the other colors. Black-White was "too reactive." In a format so defined by the bombs, Hayne said you need a positive plan against them if you can't go bigger than they can. Blue-White enticed Hayne for a bit, and he liked the usually overlooked [Statute of Denial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Statute+of+Denial)s. "It's better in Sealed than people give it credit for. It's worse than [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) because of the extra mana, and because of the Souls [going to the graveyard], but it does a lot of work." But overall he decided it was too lackluster. Nothing really stood out and the Red build was just better. And lastly, Green-White looked all right, "but all it gives you is more top end." As example he said, "This deck doesn't need the [Ancient] Silverback."





  

 After dismissing the other three colors to go with the definitely-being-played White, Hayne returned to his initial Red-White build and refined it. He added in [Sacred Armory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Armory). "This thing's underrated too. It makes all your creatures more threatening." He pointed to the token makers, adding that when your trading tokens for [Invasive Species](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invasive+Species), you're doing pretty well. "It also works with the [Scrapyard Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapyard+Mongrel)s."
