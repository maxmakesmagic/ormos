Jarno Harkonen kicked off the draft with a [Nezumi Cutthroat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Cutthroat) and quickly moved into green-black, a combination that has been very popular at this tournament. The ace up his sleeve was a trifecta of Hondens that he quietly picked up and included in his final build. With two [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder)s to fix his mana and two [Nezumi Cutthroat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Cutthroat)s to bring the beats, he looks to have a decent shot at the title.

Shu Komuro started off with a premier green common, [Order of the Sacred Bell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Order+of+the+Sacred+Bell), and soon added blue to the mix. When a chance at a [Glacial Ray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Ray) came, he snapped it up, and finished with that plus a second Ray, two [Kodama's Might](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Might)s, and a [Consuming Vortex](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vortex) for lots of splice potential. Add to the mix three [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder)s and a [Kodama of the North Tree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama+of+the+North+Tree) and you get what looks to be one of the top decks at the table.

Three [Sokenzan Bruiser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sokenzan+Bruiser)s, two [Kitsune Riftwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitsune+Riftwalker)s, and *four* [Indomitable Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Indomitable+Will)s were liable to give his opponent fits. His main issue seems to be that he's lacking two and three drops.

Vasilis Fatouros began the draft in green-black, but almost switched to red when he opened up [Kumano, Master Yamabushi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kumano%2C+Master+Yamabushi). He did indeed take the legend (much to Jonsson's dismay), but Anton cut red hard enough in the second round of packs that he was forced to abandon the color entirely. Still, he ended up with two [Devouring Greed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Greed)s and *thirteen* Spirits to abuse them with. He also had [Dance of Shadows](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dance+of+Shadows) and [Tatsumasa, the Dragon's Fang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tatsumasa%2C+the+Dragon%27s+Fang) as additional finishers.

Anton Jonsson quickly settled into blue-red, a combination that's been amazing for him all weekend. He seems to be a little short on cards, having to play [Guardian of Solitude](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+of+Solitude) and [Battle-Mad Ronin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle-Mad+Ronin), but also has the amazing [Earthshaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthshaker), double [Teller of Tales](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teller+of+Tales) and triple [Yamabushi's Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yamabushi%27s+Flame). He spent his final first pick of the draft simply hating Kami of the Ancient Law from his quarterfinals opponent Kuroda, as the pack didn't have much for him and Kuroda was light on early drops.
