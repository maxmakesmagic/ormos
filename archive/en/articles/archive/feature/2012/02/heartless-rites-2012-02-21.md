Heartless Rites
===============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 21, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






At the StarCityGames.com Open Series in Charlotte this past weekend, rogue deck builder Ali Aintrazi unveiled his latest concoction. Pushing together parts from both the [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) deck and the French Reanimator deck, Ali added a little of his own deck-building seasoning and came up with a unique five-color [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) reanimator deck!

One game plan for this deck is the pure reanimation route. This deck quickly fills up its graveyard by using [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy), and [Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings). Then, thanks to a full set of [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and plenty of large creatures, this deck puts that stocked graveyard to good use. Sending a turn-four [Rune-Scarred Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune-Scarred+Demon); [Massacre Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm); or [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) lurching back onto the battlefield will be enough to devour most opponents.

However, the strength of Ali's deck lies in its variety of plans. If the pieces to reanimate don't come together—or if the opponent plays something like [Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage)—the deck can also just *cast* its large creatures! [Heartless Summoning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heartless+Summoning) creates a nice discount on the deck's most potent threats, allowing you to cheaply deploy some of the most powerful creatures in Standard. By using the same card-selection engines the reanimation half of the deck uses, Ali's deck has no problem finding its threats.

Could this mashup of two Standard decks begin to turn the Standard format on its head? Ali's 11th-place finish has pushed the deck out into the spotlight—now it's your turn to give it a try and see what you can do with it!
