How to Assemble Your Spirit Squad
=================================



 Posted in **How to Build**
 on July 19, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gabyspartz_0.jpg)
By Gaby Spartz




 By night, Gaby streams Magic on Twitch. By day, she's co-founder and VP of Content at Dose, the Chicago-based media company behind Dose.com and OMGFacts.com. 






*Eldritch Moon* has introduced some spicy new Spirits into Standard. Today, we're assembling the Spirit squad and exploring things to keep in mind when building a Spirits deck.


Synergy
=======


Spirits tend to be relatively small, evasive fliers. On their own, they're not much of a threat, but they get better in numbers. Let's take a look at [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit), for example:


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Selfless+Spirit)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit)
By itself, this is unassuming at best. Once you have a couple of Spirits out on the battlefield, it provides great protection against both targeted removal and board sweeps. [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) is also fantastic on defense. Board stalls are common in the current Standard format, but if you have [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) on your side of the battlefield, any attacks from your opponent are hopeless. They could lose multiple creatures on the attack, while you only lose a [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) in the exchange.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rattlechains)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains)
The value of [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) increases with each additional Spirit in your deck. The great thing about [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains)'s flash ability is that it makes any counterspells you put in your deck go up in value as well. Because you can cast it at the end of any turn, [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) gives you the option to hold up a counterspell during your opponent's turn, then flash in [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) if they didn't cast anything worth countering. You might even get a two-for-one if they try to remove one of your Spirits and you flash in [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) to protect it with hexproof.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thunderclap+Wyvern)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderclap+Wyvern)
Though not a Spirit itself, [Thunderclap Wyvern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderclap+Wyvern) is a nice way to power up your team, since they all have flying. Its flash ability gives you the same benefits as [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains).


Flexibility
===========


A big draw toward playing Spirits is the optionality that you get from them. Let's take a look at [Mausoleum Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Wanderer).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mausoleum+Wanderer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Wanderer)
The Spirits deck was itching to have a strong turn-one play, and [Mausoleum Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Wanderer) delivers. This card fulfills the same role that [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher) does for Merfolk decks. For as long as [Mausoleum Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Wanderer) is in play, your opponent will have to tread carefully because they don't want their instants or sorceries countered. Typically, when cards like [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher) or [Judge's Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar) are in play, your opponent is forced to play one turn behind to avoid being countered. [Mausoleum Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Wanderer) has the potential upside of being better than [Cursecatcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursecatcher) if you cast a Spirit at instant speed. It's true, these two are best friends.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/images/daily/HtB20160719_Ghost-love.png)


Potentially the most exciting new card for Spirits in *Eldritch Moon* is [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller). For the low price of three mana, you get a 2/3 flier *and* a counterspell. If you time things right, this card can be a huge blowout for your opponent.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spell+Queller)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)
One thing to be mindful of is keeping your [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) alive. If your opponent has ways to kill it, things can go south for you quickly. This is another situation where the second ability on [Rattlechains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattlechains) shines. You know who also combos with [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller)? [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) is ready to take one for the team.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nebelgast+Herald)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nebelgast+Herald)
[Nebelgast Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nebelgast+Herald) is great both on offense and defense. If you need to remove a blocker temporarily, flash in [Nebelgast Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nebelgast+Herald) and get rid of that pesky flier on the way. If you're being attacked and you need to buy yourself some time, you can flash in Negelgast Herald to tap down one of your opponent's attackers in a pinch. Once [Nebelgast Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nebelgast+Herald) is out on the battlefield, any additional Spirit will provide the same effect, so you can continue messing with your opponent's blockers while disrupting their attackers as well.

I like this quite a bit. One card I'm iffy about is [Anafenza, Kin-Tree Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anafenza%2C+Kin-Tree+Spirit). Curving [Mausoleum Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Wanderer) into Anafenza can be challenging, but she's a Spirit and her ability is good in this deck. She also a good target for [Ojutai's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ojutai%27s+Command).


Another thing worth trying with a Spirits deck—splashing green. [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) is much more impactful on our top end than [Thunderclap Wyvern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderclap+Wyvern) is.


Once we've added [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) into the Spirits deck, we no longer want any [Thunderclap Wyvern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunderclap+Wyvern)s or Avacyns. We want to make sure we can hit as many creatures as possible with each CoCo, which means [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage), despite not being a Spirit, gets a spot back on the team. Splashing green also gives us the option of playing [Tamiyo, Field Researcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+Field+Researcher). We don't want too many of her, since she's not a hit for [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), but her +1 ability lets us draw lots of cards.

If I were to take either of these decks to FNM, it would probably be Bant Spirits. This deck keeps your opponents guessing as they worry about playing into your counterspells, only to realize you were holding [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and now they're very far behind. You also have the ability to CoCo into [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) in response to what they're casting for maximum blowouts.
