Standard, with Flare
====================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on December 16, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week I had [a lot of fun](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/i-lost-my-alara-2009-12-09) suiting guys up with [Gigantiform](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gigantiform) thanks to [Sovereigns of Lost Alara](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sovereigns+of+Lost+Alara). I received a ton of positive feedback and strong suggestions for the deck. If you're looking to build the deck on a higher budget, I would update the mana base and find room for [Uril, the Miststalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uril%2C+the+Miststalker), [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), and [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab69_runeflare.jpg)  
This week I'd like to try out a new Standard deck. I was playing some games in the tournament practice room when a new idea killed me out of nowhere. I had taken 1 damage from a fetch land and was happily beating my opponent into oblivion. He had cast a [Font of Mythos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) a turn earlier, and I seemed to have solid control over the game. I drew my two cards from the Font and then drew for my turn. My opponent didn't let me get to my precombat main phase, thoughâ€”he cast [Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap), then cast [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast) targetting the big spell. I took 16, and a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) finished me off. I started asking him about the deck, and he gladly explained how it worked. This week I'd like to talk about this deck in today's Standard.

I'll start by explaining the card choices.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab69_theCardsHeader.jpg)  
**[Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap):** This is how we kill our opponent. Doming your opponent for 20 damage is not a difficult endeavor.

**[Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast):** Copying a [Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap) or a [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) is usually a game winner.

**[Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine):** We want our [Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap)s to be huge. This is a start.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab69_theCardsCross1.jpg)  
**[Font of Mythos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos):** [Font of Mythos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) is huge here. One Font satisfies the trap condition of [Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap) all by itself.

**[Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt):** I cannot think of a reasonable excuse to not play this in a deck with red mana.

**[Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning):** I really wish I could play eight [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), but *Zendikar* was kind enough to give us a really solid alternative.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab69_theCardsCross2.jpg)  
**[Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder):** [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) is a fine card selection tool when you need a [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) or [Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap) in a pinch.

**[Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp):** I want to make myself perfectly clear to those of you who have not had the chance to cast a [Time Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Warp) with a [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) and [Font of Mythos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) in play: This card is absolutely necessary to the archetype.

**[Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil):** Stunting an opponent's development, cantripping, and making [Runeflare Trap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runeflare+Trap) bigger are all good reasons to play [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil).
