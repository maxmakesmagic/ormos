Round 10 Feature Match - Antonino De Rosa vs. Martin Juza
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten* 
We don't always get to start off our Sunday feature match coverage immediately with a heavyweight battle, but we were lucky here in Moscow. With 7-2 records, both Martin Juza and Antonino de Rosa still have their sights set on another Grand Prix Top 8, but in order to make the cut they're (likely) going to need to go undefeated from this point forward.


Before the match started, the players exchanged some friendly banter, lamenting the fact that they were already paired against each other in the first round of the draft and chatting about their decks. "My deck is either really good or really bad," de Rosa mentioned. When Juza asked why, de Rosa explained that he simply hadn't drafted this format much. Well, let's see whether or not de Rosa's deck will perform...


### Game 1


Juza got to play first and didn't waste any time: a [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) powered him into [Talrand's Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%27s+Invocation) on his third turn. [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) from De Rosa quickly put a halt to the 2/2 flying tokens, though.


Then the board got cluttered with creatures: [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief), [Deadly Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse), and [Centaur Courser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser) for Juza; [Chronomaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton), [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin), and [Arms Dealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arms+Dealer) for de Rosa.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/fm%2010%20Board%20state.JPG)

*The board state in game 1.* 
So as we progressed into the mid-game, the board state was shown in the picture. Juza had just attacked with [Deadly Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse) and [Centaur Courser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser), putting de Rosa down to 13 life.


On his next turn, de Rosa enchanted his [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) with [Dark Favor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Favor) and attacked with the 5/4 lifelinking flyer. Juza had an answer in his hand: [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon). But the clever Czech player did not cast it right away. He first double blocked with his 2/2 Drake tokens, which prompted de Rosa to use [Arms Dealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arms+Dealer) (sacrificing [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin)) to shoot down one of the tokens. Only then did Juza play the [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon), and de Rosa was unable to replay his [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) because he had used his mana on the [Arms Dealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arms+Dealer) activation. In fact, de Rosa had tapped himself out completely, which gave Juza the window to take out [Arms Dealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arms+Dealer) with [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon). Immediately after, Juza cast [Tricks of the Trade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tricks+of+the+Trade) on [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) and attacked with all of his creatures. With that sequence of plays, Juza had completely turned around the game in his favor.


De Rosa was not to be outdone, however. He [Murder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder)ed the unblockable [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) and played a [Reckless Brute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Brute) in his second main phase to keep it back as a blocker. And when his [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) traded with [Deadly Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadly+Recluse), he had a second [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) ready as a replacement.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/FM%2010%20Martin%20Juza.JPG)

*Martin Juza* 
As the [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) and the 2/2 Drake token started exchanging blows, Juza appeared to be out of gas. De Rosa's life total was swinging between 3 and 5, while Juza's life was slowly dwindling.


Two [Chandra's Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Fury) from de Rosa later, Juza revealed a grip of three lands and the players went on to the second game.


Antonino de Rosa 1 - Martin Juza 0


While they shuffled, they talked about the draft. "So I opened Odric, Master Tactian and [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk); I took the Nighthawk because there also was a [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) in the pack and didn't want to fight for white," de Rosa said. "And then in the second pack I opened another [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk)." Juza just nodded, hoping he wouldn't face double [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) again in the next game.


### Game 2


Juza chose to play first, but it was de Rosa in the aggressor role, with [Chronomaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chronomaton), [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk), [Reckless Brute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Brute), and two [Tormented Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Soul) (one even carrying [Mark of the Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+the+Vampire)) that were putting a lot of pressure on Juza.


[Yeva's Forcemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yeva%27s+Forcemage) traded with [Reckless Brute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Brute), but Juza didn't play anything else on his fourth and fifth turn and could only muster a [Vastwood Gorger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vastwood+Gorger) when he hit 6 mana. That didn't really help much against the evasive black creatures, and it seemed that Juza would be picking up another loss.



![Elderscale Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elderscale+Wurm)

But then everything turned around when Juza played [Elderscale Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elderscale+Wurm). As long as it would stay in play, Juza simply couldn't lose!


The players asked a judge to confirm how the Wurm worked and also asked the judge about the interaction between lifelink and [Elderscale Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elderscale+Wurm). Apparently, lifelink still works even if the opponent is at 7 life.


So while the [Elderscale Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elderscale+Wurm) ensured that Juza wouldn't die to de Rosa's attacks, he still had to find a way to actually win the game. [Tricks of the Trade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tricks+of+the+Trade) on [Elderscale Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elderscale+Wurm) did the trick. Now, putting an enchantment on an already big creature may seem risky as you are putting all eggs in one basket. But, as Juza said, "If this guy dies, I die," so the Wurm was actually the perfect target for [Tricks of the Trade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tricks+of+the+Trade).


De Rosa had a couple of turns to draw an answer, but did not find any. He resignedly scooped up his ineffectual permanents.


Antonino de Rosa 1 - Martin Juza 1


### Game 3


Antonino showed his love for aggressive 1/1 creatures by starting off with [Tormented Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Soul) and two [Krenko's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command). [Augur of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) for Juza bricked, but provided a good blocker for the tokens nonetheless.


Various creatures then traded against removal spells: [Murder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder)s and [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon)s were flying everywhere. But de Rosa's [Tormented Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormented+Soul) still survived and kept on pressuring Juza, who was stuck on three lands.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/FM%2010%20de%20Rosa.JPG)

*Antonio de Rosa* 
De Rosa then added [Bloodhunter Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhunter+Bat) and [Mark of the Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+the+Vampire) to his board, while Juza looked at the [Primal Huntbeast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Huntbeast) and [Tricks of the Trade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tricks+of+the+Trade) (a nice combo, by the way) in his hand that he was unable to cast due to mana problems.


While sympathizing for Juza's bad luck, De Rosa was able to attack for lethal.


Antonino de Rosa 2 - Martin Juza 1



Sunday, 11:07 a.m. - Drafting with Raphaël Lévy
-----------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 
At the end of yesterday's Sealed portion, the French Hall of Famer and all-time Pro point leader Raphaël Lévy, having lost only one round, found himself in eighth place, so today he took his seat at draft table number one. I took up station directly behind him and watched his draft.


### Pack 1


Lévy opened a booster with all kinds of goodness, from the more expensive [Stormtide Leviathan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormtide+Leviathan) to the more aggressive [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader). However, the Muckwader was red like [Mogg Flunkies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Flunkies), and a [Harbor Bandit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Bandit) that was in consideration was just as black as [Servant of Nefarox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+Nefarox), [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood), and a number of other cards. Instead Lévy chose [Sentinel Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel+Spider), which at least was the only card of its color.


The best green card to follow it up with would have been a measly [Vastwood Gorger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vastwood+Gorger). That was quickly relegated to the back of the pack. Having just passed a host of black cards, Lévy also had no intention to move into that color for [Bloodhunter Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhunter+Bat). White on the other hand appeared to be wide open, with [Planar Cleansing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing), [Aven Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Squire), [Ring of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ring+of+Thune), and [Knight of Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Glory), which he took as his second pick.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/blog_drafting_levy.jpg)

*Levy working on his draft selections* 
Next up was a booster in which [Krenko's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command) and [Furnace Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Whelp) were arguably the strongest cards. Lévy switched back and forth between the latter and a [War Falcon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War+Falcon), in the end deciding against taking up another color just yet.


For his fourth pick, though, that simply wasn't an option anymore. The best white card was [Show of Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+of+Valor), the only green card [Fog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog). No, here Lévy took [Encrust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Encrust), passing both [Essence Scatter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Scatter) and [Dragon Hatchling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragon+Hatchling).


Next was [Captain's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain%27s+Call), then [Battleflight Eagle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battleflight+Eagle)—at least some white from admittedly weak packs. And indeed white continued to come his way, with a seventh-pick [Aven Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Squire), eighth-pick [Attended Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attended+Knight), and tenth-pick [Ring of Thune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ring+of+Thune). In between he picked up a [Trumpet Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trumpet+Blast), and the rest of the cards from the first round of boosters were just as unimpressive: [Disentomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb), [Merfolk of the Pearl Trident](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+of+the+Pearl+Trident), [Downpour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Downpour), [Warclamp Mastiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warclamp+Mastiff).


### Pack 2


So far Lévy had pretty much settled into white, dabbled the teensiest tiny bit in blue and green, less so in red, and had strictly avoided everything black. So obviously he opened a booster with lots of that, including the likes of [Cower in Fear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cower+in+Fear), [Murder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder), and [Nefarox, Overlord of Grixis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nefarox%2C+Overlord+of+Grixis). He sent an inaudible sigh skyward, then concentrated on the rest of the pack. [Timberpack Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberpack+Wolf) wasn't quite enough to sway him from taking another white card, so in the end he settled on [Griffin Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Protector).


For his second pick, he had to pass yet another [Murder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder), but at least got a bomb for himself: [Captain of the Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+of+the+Watch). Pick three was [Ajani's Sunstriker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Sunstriker) over [Healer of the Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Healer+of+the+Pride), pick four was [War Falcon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War+Falcon), and then white kind of dried up on him. [Rain of Blades](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rain+of+Blades) fifth, [Ravenous Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats) out of desperation sixth, and [Captain's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain%27s+Call) seventh wasn't exactly what the signals of the previous pass had promised.


He closed out pack two with [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders), [Safe Passage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Passage), [Mind Sculpt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sculpt), [Tricks of the Trade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tricks+of+the+Trade), [Plummet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plummet), [Ranger's Path](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger%27s+Path), and [Angel's Mercy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Mercy). Rather disappointing, with blue tentatively moving to secondary color being the silver lining.


### Pack 3


Blue? Well, how about [Sleep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) then? That was Lévy's first pick from a pack that held no white cards whatsoever save [Pillarfield Ox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillarfield+Ox).


Next was [Aven Squire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Squire) over [Fog Bank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) (passing yet another [Murder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder)), then [Serra Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel) over another [Sleep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep). [Arctic Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arctic+Aven) was a no-brainer, but pick five got interesting again, offering a choice between two-drops ([Silvercoat Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvercoat+Lion), [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern)), manafixing ([Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds)), or a second [Griffin Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Protector) to go with Lévy's two [Captain's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain%27s+Call)s. [Griffin Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Protector) it was.


Next up was [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) over [Captain's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain%27s+Call) and [Healer of the Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Healer+of+the+Pride), then [War Falcon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War+Falcon) over [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress), then [Healer of the Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Healer+of+the+Pride) over [Silvercoat Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvercoat+Lion). [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) wheeled, and most of the white cards Lévy had seen the first time around came around again too: first [Captain's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain%27s+Call) number three, then [Guardians of Akrasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardians+of+Akrasa), then [Glorious Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Charge).


After the draft, Lévy specifically mentioned these last two, his eleventh and twelfth pick respectively. "I knew nobody was white after pack one, but there just weren't any white cards in pack two. No [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) in the draft either." With all of his Soldier tokens [Glorious Charge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorious+Charge) certainly seemed nice.


Talking about his chances Lévy said, "Worried for a while, but pack three really saved me."



Round 11 Feature Match -Raphaël Lévy vs. Oliver Rauschy
-------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 
Raphaël Lévy simply needs no more introduction. His opponent this round was Oliver Rausch, an up-and-coming player who had been terrorizing the PTQ circuit in his native Germany for quite a while now. Maybe it finally was going to be his time for a breakout performance on the Grand Prix stage?


### Game 1


Lévy won the die-roll, but had no plays for the first couple of turns, starting off with [Guardians of Akrasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardians+of+Akrasa) on turn three. Meanwhile Rausch had cast [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader), attacked into the defender, then finished it off with [Crippling Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Blight) before adding yet more pressure with [Mogg Flunkies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Flunkies).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/fm11_levy.jpg)

*Raphaël Lévy* 
Lèvy summoned [Griffin Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Protector), Rausch summoned [Liliana's Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade), searching up a third [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp). Lévy held back with his flier and passed the turn with three [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s, two [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) untapped. [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders), anyone?


Rausch thought long and hard about it, then played his [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) and attacked with everything anyway. [Griffin Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Protector) blocked [Mogg Flunkies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Flunkies) and was indeed pumped via [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders). Still Lévy fell to 9. Another [Crippling Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Blight) took out the Griffin and [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse) made an appearance on the German side. Rausch's onslaught was relentless.



![Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Searing+Spear)![Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Crimson+Muckwader)

And it wasn't even going to be stopped by Lévy's [Knight of Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Glory). [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) dispatched that, and soon enough [Liliana's Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade) and [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader) got the job done.


Raphaël Lévy 0-1 Oliver Rausch


### Game 2


Lévy had [Ajani's Sunstriker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Sunstriker) followed by [Guardians of Akrasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardians+of+Akrasa), while Rausch summoned the distinct non-combo of [Mogg Flunkies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Flunkies) and [Servant of Nefarox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+Nefarox). Lévy's second attack with the exalted lifelinker turned the totals to 26 to 14 in his favor, and afterwards he cast [Attended Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attended+Knight) to also make any kind of retaliation quite impossible.


Rausch passed without play. Unable to attack himself, Rausch might have been much more inclined to double-block [Ajani's Sunstriker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Sunstriker), so Lévy just attacked with his Soldier token, 2/2 thanks to [Guardians of Akrasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardians+of+Akrasa). Lévy once again passed with five mana up. At end of turn, Rausch shot [Attended Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attended+Knight) with [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear), then attacked with [Servant of Nefarox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+Nefarox) which was blocked by [Ajani's Sunstriker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Sunstriker), then cast [Krenko's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command).


Lévy restocked his team with the inevitable [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) at end of turn and with [Knight of Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Glory) on his. The Invaders invaded for 5 damage to put Rausch at 7. A turn later that turned to 2 and soon the score stood at:


Raphaël Lévy 1-1 Oliver Rausch


### Game 3


Rausch opened on [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader), Lévy on [Knight of Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Glory). Rausch cast [Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy) pre-combat and attacked for 4. Since he had so few nonwhite creatures—a total of three in his deck—Lévy definitely had to enter the race now.


He tried to fight back with [Arctic Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arctic+Aven), but he lost that to [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) and a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) to [Ravenous Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats). Rausch's attack (with Knight and Muckwader) brought Lévy to 11. The latter's [Griffin Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Protector) didn't stop the beatdown either, at least not until [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) showed up a turn later. By then, Lévy was already down to 6 and was facing an attacking army of [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader), [Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy), and now two [Ravenous Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats). Also, his [Griffin Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Protector) was shot down with [Murder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) before blocks. [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) still ate [Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy), but Lévy fell to 1.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/fm11_rausch.jpg)

*Oliver Rausch* 
On his turn he summoned [Ajani's Sunstriker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Sunstriker) and attacked with [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders) which put Rausch at dangerously low life as well. However, now Lévy only had two blockers for Rausch's total of three attackers, relying on lifelink to survive the attack. When [Bloodthrone Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire) showed up pre-combat, allowing Rausch to simply sacrifice whichever creature would be blocked by [Ajani's Sunstriker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%27s+Sunstriker), there was no life to be gained. Soon no life left either.


Raphaël Lévy 1-2 Oliver Rausch

Sunday, 12:12 p.m. - Funky Decks in the First Draft
---------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*
Deck construction is a sweet time to take a peek at draft decks spread out on the table in all their glory. When walking around while players were building their decks, three in particular looked picture-worthy.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/Marcin%20Sciesinski.JPG)

The first deck that caught my eye was this masterpiece by Marcin Sciesinski. Double [Sphinx of Uthuun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Uthuun) and two [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) to rebuy them? Nice! You can easily go through your entire deck this way.


But wait ... do I see two Merfolk of the Peal Trident lurking over there on the left? What are they doing in the deck? Well, with two [Switcheroo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Switcheroo), a [Master of the Pearl Trident](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+the+Pearl+Trident), and a [Quirion Dryad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad), even a vanilla 1/1 for one blue mana becomes playable.


Although the deck looked like a blast to play, Marcin lost all three matches with it. "Raphael Levy overran me in the first round with a lots of tokens, and [Switcheroo](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Switcheroo) was no good against it," Marcin explained. "And then I lost to [Planar Cleansing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing) and [Harbor Bandit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harbor+Bandit) in rounds two and three."



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/Lukas%20Jaklovsky.JPG)

The largest number of Timberpack Wolves I've seen in a single deck so far today is three. This Blue-Green deck belongs to Lukas Jaklovsky, who seems to like his cards in multiples. Next to his three [Timberpack Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberpack+Wolf), he has as many as 4 [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon)s!


The dream start of turn 2 [Timberpack Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberpack+Wolf), turn 3 [Timberpack Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberpack+Wolf), turn 4 [Timberpack Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timberpack+Wolf) and double [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) is a definite possibility with this deck. Lukas managed to win all three matches with it.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/Sergey%20Stolyarov.JPG)

You know, I was hoping that someone would go the mill route today, so I was thrilled to see this deck by Sergey Stolyarov. Four [Mind Sculpt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sculpt) and a [Vedalken Entrancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Entrancer)? That's a great game plan if I ever saw one. And he also has [Jace's Phantasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Phantasm) to go with it.


Sergey posted a 1-2 with this deck, so maybe it doesn't always work (especially if opponents board into 50-60 card decks for games 2 and 3) but it's still fun to win in an alternative way!

Sunday, 2:12 p.m. - Drafting with Hannes Kerem
----------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*
Estonian Hannes Kerem is no stranger to the spotlight, having made previous experiences in this regard at Worlds 2008 where he finished in fourth place. Going into the second draft of the day, he was 11-1, well on his way to another Top 8, so this time a (comparatively smaller) spotlight was reserved for his draft process.


### Pack 1


[Essence Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain), [Serra Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Angel), or [Stuffy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stuffy+Doll)? Kerem went for the artifact creature as his first pick. He continued with [Fog Bank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog+Bank) over [Crippling Blight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Blight) and [Hellion Crucible](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellion+Crucible). Pick three gave him the most options yet, with a pack that still had [Faerie Invaders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Invaders), [Essence Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain), [Bloodhunter Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhunter+Bat), [Planar Cleansing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing), and [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader). He was willing to move into black and took [Essence Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain), then was visibly shaken when the next booster held a second [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader) as the by far best card in sight. Having just passed one, he clearly would have preferred something else, but there was nothing remotely close in power, so the Lizard it was.



![Mark of the Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mark+of+the+Vampire)![Fog Bank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fog+Bank)

Next up was [Bloodhunter Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodhunter+Bat), then [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin), then [Liliana's Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade). Not the most spectacular cards, but this gave him a solid base in black and red, to which he added [Mark of the Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+the+Vampire), [Bloodthrone Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire), and a couple of [Goblin Arsonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Arsonist)s. He scooped up a [Wild Guess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Guess) and a late [Fire Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental), and it was off to pack two.


### Pack 2


[Krenko's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command) over [Sign in Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sign+in+Blood) was not a particularly hard decision. Neither was [Akroma's Memorial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Memorial) over ... well, in fact Kerem didn't even look at the rest of the cards in the booster as soon as he had spotted the mighty artifact.


For his third pick, he was passed a peculiar pack all the good cards of which were green: [Roaring Primadox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roaring+Primadox), [Flinthoof Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar), and [Sentinel Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel+Spider). Kerem only got his second [Mark of the Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mark+of+the+Vampire).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/blog_drafting2_kerem.jpg)

*Hannes Kerem* 
And for some reason black and red continued to elude him this round. A fourth-pick [Chandra's Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Fury) was followed by a rare-drafted [Glacial Fortress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Fortress). A sixth-pick [Goblin Arsonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Arsonist) was followed by [Liliana's Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade) (passing more [Sentinel Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sentinel+Spider)s). Another [Liliana's Shade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Shade) and another [Bloodthrone Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire), along with [Disentomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disentomb) and [Smelt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smelt) rounded out this, overall rather disappointing, pack.


### Pack 3


First, Kerem picked up what could have been his third [Crimson Muckwader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crimson+Muckwader) but was actually "only" his second. Then he took [Public Execution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Public+Execution) out of a booster which also had: [Turn to Slag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+to+Slag), [Mogg Flunkies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Flunkies), and [Giant Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Scorpion)—too much at once!


His third pick was [Searing Spear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) over [Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy), then came [Fire Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental) over both [Rise from the Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rise+from+the+Grave) and [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse), then [Krenko's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command) over another [Fire Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental). A sixth-pick [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) beat out another [Krenko's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%27s+Command) and a [Goblin Battle Jester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Battle+Jester). Seventh was [Chandra's Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Fury), eighth [Fire Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental) number three, ninth was [Bloodthrone Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire).



![Knight of Infamy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Knight+of+Infamy)![Krenko's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Krenko%27s+Command)

Strangely, [Mogg Flunkies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Flunkies) and [Giant Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Scorpion) both made their way around the table. Kerem took the Goblin. The rest of his picks didn't add anything noteworthy to his draft pile, so that was that.


Overall, black and red certainly were either open, or there simply were more black and red cards in the packs than usual. Kerem's deck looked solid enough, although it exhibited a little "disconnect" between its separate parts. Small aggressive creatures like [Mogg Flunkies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Flunkies) and [Bloodthrone Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodthrone+Vampire) on the one hand, five, six, and even seven-mana spells on the other. But in the end, [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) is amazing at stitching things together (and Kerem had drafted two of those), so there was really nothing to worry about.



Round 14 Feature Match - Petr Kuznetsov vs. Eugene Idzikovsky
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*
At the beginning of this all-Russian feature match both players still only had two losses. A win here would almost certainly secure a Top 8 berth, a loss might eliminate from Top 8 contention. Petr Kuznetsov was on white-blue, Eugene Idzikovsky entered the fray with black-red.


### Game 1


Neither player seemed to be in a hurry, with no plays on either side for the first two turns. Both had 1/3 creatures on turn three, though: [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) for Kuznetsov, playing first, [Giant Scorpion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Scorpion) for Idzikovsky. The Scorpion was put under a spell of [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) and [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) netted its first extra card.


Idzikovsky didn't have a fourth land and had to content himself with casting [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin)—no good blocker for [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief), which provided its second extra card. Kuznetsov made two Drakes with [Talrand's Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talrand%27s+Invocation) and passed to Idzikovsky who made a 2/2 [Primal Clay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Clay). That and [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) blocked [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) while the Drakes got in for 4.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/fm14_Idzikovsky.jpg)

*Eugene Idzikovsky* 
Kuznetsov had a [Primal Clay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Clay) of his own, his a 3/3. But now Idzikovsky summoned [Fire Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental), rendering the groundbound lump of clay rather useless. He traded his flying lump of clay away for one of the Drake tokens, while the other Drake put him at 12.


Kuznetsov had another [Scroll Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) and an Odric's [Crusade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade)r which in turn traded with [Fire Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental). Idzikovsky was falling behind on cards and life, and soon even a freshly-cast [Xathrid Gorgon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Xathrid+Gorgon) wasn't enough anymore to help him out of his predicament. His [Essence Drain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Drain) might have helped, but when Idzikovsky tapped out for it, Kuznetsov showed [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), and that was that.


Petr Kuznetsov 1-0 Eugene Idzikovsky


### Game 2


The second game started much faster, had Goblin Arosnist trading with [War Falcon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=War+Falcon) as early as turn two. Over the next couple of turns, Kuznetsov first built a [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern), then built it up via [Ring of Evos Isle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ring+of+Evos+Isle). Idzikovsky's [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) went digging for answers or something to race with, but when his [Fire Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Elemental) came down on turn five, [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) was already 4/3 and growing ever larger still.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmos12/fm14_Kuznetsov.jpg)

*Petr Kuznetsov* 
Kuznetsov always kept two lands untapped to prevent any undue tampering, and churned out enough creatures to chump if needs be. Not that it was necessary; the monstrous 6/5 [Welkin Tern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Welkin+Tern) ended the game long before that became an issue.


Petr Kuznetsov 2-0 Eugene Idzikovsky

Still, this deck really looks like a blast to play.

"If I would have built the deck again, I would have cut [Furnace Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Whelp) and [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) for [Trumpet Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trumpet+Blast) and [Fungal Sprouting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Sprouting)", Antonino explained. "I can't cast [Furnace Whelp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+Whelp) reliably since I'm only playing 7 [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain), and [Rummaging Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rummaging+Goblin) is weak because it only provides one power for [Fungal Sprouting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Sprouting)."
