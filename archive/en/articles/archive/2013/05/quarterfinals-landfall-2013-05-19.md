Quarterfinals: Landfall
=======================



 Posted in [ARTICLES](/en/articles)
 on May 19, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author_pic_nate_price.jpg)
By Nate Price




 A longtime member of the Pro Tour and Grand Prix coverage staff, Nate Price now works making beautiful words for all of you lovely people as the community manager for organized play. When not covering events, he lords over the @MagicProTour Twitter account, ruling with an iron fist. 





The inherent drama of a crucial topdeck has created some of *Magic*'s most epicmoments, searing them into our collective memories. While being rewarded for having the heart of the cards in a moment of dire need can certainly result in an unparalleled rush of euphoria, being at the mercy of the deck also has its twisted dark side, as both Matej Zatlkej and Makihito Mihara found out in their thrilling, yet frustrating Quarterfinal match.


Makihito Mihara needed all five games to snag victory, but he was finally able to push through for the final points of damage in a very back-and-forth match that showed how powerful the top of the deck can actually be.


"Before we begin, I just want to say that I have a great amount of respect for you as a player, and no matter who wins, I will be happy," Zatlkaj offered, showing his reverence for Mihara's tremendous accomplishments.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/qf_mihara_zatlkaj.jpg)  


Regardless of how happy he was, it was clear that he was frustrated when his deck prevented him from playing *Magic* in the first game of his second Pro Tour Top 8. Land after land flew from the top of his deck into his hand as Mihara steadily built up his own mana base. Eventually, Mihara dropped an [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) into play, finding a use for his ever-growing stack of lands. Zatlkaj found a pair of [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)s to use his plethora of lands, but rather than providing him with some solace, they merely stoked his annoyance. [Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revelation)s of four and five left him no closer to being in the game than he was before he cast them.


"Both [Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revelation)s had only one spell," Zatlkaj said before admitting that Mihara was experiencing similar troubles. The one difference was that he was able to actually productively use his with the [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling). After two hits from the [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling), Zatlkaj conceded, laying his hand on the table, one land at a time. Once hit hit ten, both he and Mihara shared a nervous laugh. This was certainly not the way he wanted the match to begin.


While mana flood defined the first game, mana screw would decide another. Zatlkaj had fought through Games 2 and 3 with no mana troubles to take a 2-1 lead in the match, but he was derailed once again by his deck's fickleness. Failing to find a fourth land drop, he stumbled for a turn before he was able to land a [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought). Unfortunately, he was already facing a [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) and was forced to add a counter to Jace early. After Mihara added a second creature to the table on the following turn, Zatlkaj decided to make a move. He used Jace to draw some cards, but the piles Mihara split put an [Azorius Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildgate) in one pile and [Psychic Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychic+Strike) and [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) in the other. Zatlkaj sighed as he picked up the pile of spells and returned the land to his deck.


"The Guildgate did nothing for me there," he admitted after the game. "If it had been a land that came into play untapped, I would have taken it since I would be able to cast both [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) and another removal spell to keep Jace alive."


Instead, he was forced to stay on only four lands and try and make do. [Azorius Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) and [Devour Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh) combined to offer the hope of Jace surviving the turn. but Mihara had [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) to stop the [Devour Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devour+Flesh), keeping his [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) alive and attacking Jace. The next turns continued their downward trend, as Zatlkaj continued to be mana deprived. Mihara slowly pulled away, eventually putting Zatlkaj away.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/qf_zatlkaj.jpg)  


Interestingly, Mihara was experiencing mana troubles of his own that allowed the game to continue as long as it did. While Zatlkaj was unable to find a fifth land, Mihara managed to play the overwhelming majority of that game without a second blue source. This kept [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling), Sphnix's [Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revelation), and [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought), stranded in his hand until it was too late to matter. The fact that he was able to win the game considering his own significant handicap illustrates how behind Zatlkaj was.


The decks weren't always cruel. In Game 3, Zatlkaj found himself in a stall with Mihara. Mihara had just played a [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector), revealing that Zatlkaj was sitting on absolutely nothing in his hand. Imagine then Mihara's surprise when Zatlkaj drew his card, tapped six mana, and dropped a [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) onto the table.


"Hah, okay," was all Mihara could say as he watched Zatlkaj fill his hand up. It was the turning point in the game, giving Zatlkaj the [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) that he dropped onto the table two turns later, wininng him the game in short order.


![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/qf_mihara.jpg)  


Mihara was the recipient of a bit of fortune himself. In the final game of the match, Mihara was slightly ahead, having just resolved a Jace and drawn a couple cards when Zatlkaj fired off a [Reap Intellect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reap+Intellect) for two. Looking into Mihara's six card hand, Zatlkaj was a little dismayed to see that he wasn't able to hit an [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) or [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) with the lobotomizing effect, settling instead for a pair of [Sin Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector)s and [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel). After untapping, Mihara smiled as he tapped out to cast a [Sphinx's Revelation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for three, drawing into absolute business. In addition to finding protection for his Jace, he also found an [Obzedat, Ghost Council](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) to seal the game, and the match, two turns later.


"I thought my draw was pretty good in that last game," Zatlkaj told me after the match. "I had a pair of [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain)s, which are amazing on the play in this matchup, as well as an [Azorius Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Guildgate), but I didn't have a second white source."


Still, even with the many mana problems that plagued both players, there were some opporunities for great play. As is always present in high level control mirrors, both players took advatage of their [Watery Grave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave)s and [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain)s to posture, often taking two damage to put them into play untapped with no actual use for the mana in hand. Zatlkaj was able to correctly put Mihara on a counterspell and removal spell in his two card hand at one point, showing great discipline in waiting another turn before casting an [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) with mana for both [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) and protection available.


In the end, this matchup comes down to the tiniest of edges, and things happened to fall well for Mihara in this match. He played incredibly well, gave nothing away, and was rewarded for this with a victory over Matej Zatlkaj and a trip to the Semifinals.


**Match Notes:**


Mihara kept his [Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood) Barons in the deck after Game 1, an unorthodox sideboard strategy. This strategy gave him a heavier threat density than Zatlkaj expected, forcing him to alter his sideboarding strategy, brining in additional creature removal at the expense of permission.


While this matchup usually comes down to landing the first [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) or Jace, this time around, the player who played a turn-two [Precinct Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain) went on to win the game. This happened in four games, including Game 5, where Mihara won on the draw.
