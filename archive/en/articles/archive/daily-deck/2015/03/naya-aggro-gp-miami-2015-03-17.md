Naya Aggro at GP Miami
======================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on March 17, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






Zan Syed's Naya Aggro is similar to Andrew Boswell's Abzan Aggro in terms of its focus on speed. Its foundation is the red-green aggro [I talked about previously](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/daily-deck/red-green-beatdown-grand-prix-seville-2015-02-26).



The Naya version added [Chained to the Rocks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chained+to+the+Rocks) and [Valorous Stance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valorous+Stance), two of the cheapest white removal in the format. The deck aims to deploy cheap spells and creatures while refueling with [Outpost Siege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Outpost+Siege). It's very simple. By adding a color, usually you increase flexibility in card choice; the downside is a slight risk in mana problems, which leads to multicolor-producing taplands and a slower game plan. However, this Naya Aggro deck went the total opposite direction. The third color was rather to speed up the deck. That's why this deck ended up with all 24 lands entering the battlefield untapped and at the ready.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Valorous+Stance)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valorous+Stance)

Another surprise is the lands themselves.



Unlike Abzan aggro, the white splash isn't so demanding, with only spells having a single colored symbol. However, [Chained to the Rocks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chained+to+the+Rocks) requires a certain number of Mountains. There aren't any shock lands like in Modern and the deck refuses to use [Evolving Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) due to its enters-tapped effect. I made a quick count, and the result: 15 red sources, 16 green sources, and 10 white sources. Of course, this includes the full eight fetch lands and, you guessed it, four [Mana Confluence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Confluence)s. I'd say the mana base is a work of art.
