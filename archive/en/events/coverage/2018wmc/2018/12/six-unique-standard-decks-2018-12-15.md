Michel de Broux was playing arguably the most interesting deck in the room: Chromatic Black. It could generate massive amounts of mana with [Cabal Stronghold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Stronghold) and then combine [Chromatic Lantern](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern) and [Mastermind's Acquisition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mastermind%27s+Acquisition) to cast various off-color finishers from the sideboard. You don't get to see a mono-black deck cast [Zacama, Primal Calamity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zacama%2C+Primal+Calamity) every day.


Note that if you'd like to try this deck in an individual Standard tournament, then you may want to follow [Ali Aintrazi's Deck Guide](https://www.coolstuffinc.com/a/aliaintrazi-12142018-winning-with-chromatic-black) and replace [Carnage Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnage+Tyrant) with [Niv-Mizzet, Parun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Parun). Those legendary Dragons were not an option for de Broux as they were already taken by Pascal Vieren's deck. Team Belgium's Standard record was 0-3-1. (This was not an impressive record, but decks don't necessarily need to be winning to be unique and interesting.)

Oscar Ramirez registered Golgari [Undergrowth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undergrowth), which plans to fill its graveyard in the early turns with [Stitcher's Supplier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier) and [Glowspore Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glowspore+Shaman) and then exploits the undergrowth mechanic with cards like [Necrotic Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Wound) and [Molderhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molderhulk).


[Molderhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molderhulk) in particular has the ability to return [Memorial to Folly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memorial+to+Folly), which can in turn return another [Molderhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molderhulk). Or the same [Molderhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molderhulk) once it dies. Either way, it's a powerful value chain that gives the deck inevitability. Unfortunately, team Panama's Constructed record was 0-4.

Ken Yukuhiro, the Japanese captain, is known for his bubbly personality and bubbly deck creations. This time, the connoisseur of rogue decks brought his take on Selesnya to the tournament.


When I was doing the metagame breakdown, I first wanted to classify his deck as "Selesnya Tokens," but then I did another take and saw that his list eschewed March of Multitudes, [Saproling Migration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling+Migration), and [Trostani Discordant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani+Discordant). Instead, the list includes [Nullhide Ferox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nullhide+Ferox), [Dauntless Bodyguard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Bodyguard), and [Thorn Lieutenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn+Lieutenant), giving it a more aggressive bent. As a result, I named it "Selesnya Aggro." Team Japan's Standard record in the Swiss was 6-0.
