Zend of an Era
==============



 Posted in **From the Lab**
 on August 25, 2011 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Noel deCordova











Hello and welcome back to the Lab. On this last week before *Innistrad* previews, I feel like I'm teetering on the brink of a watershed of top-down **Magic** design. The set has successfully hummed with interest, although that could just be my supernatural hearing. I've always got [Leyline of Anticipation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Anticipation) on my personal battlefield.

Anyway, I realized a while ago that I would need a juicy theme to slide into this week every year. That's why, before the first preview week of each new fall release (or sometime around there), I like to dedicate a column to the block that rotates to make room. This year, it's *Zendikar* block. You can check out my previous farewells to [Alara block](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/105), [*Lorwyn* and *Shadowmoor*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/57), and [*Coldsnap*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/5) through the power of internet linkage if you wish.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl157_byZen.jpg)  
*Zendikar* may be rotating out of Standard, but I prefer not to equalize that statement to "forever banished from my mind." Although Standard intrigues me because of its inherent restriction, I ultimately choose to live in the infinite and far less stressful world of Casual.

But enough talk. Let's see what cards and strategies tickle my creative side from *Zendikar*, its follow-up *Worldwake*, and the plane-shattering *Rise of the Eldrazi*.

*Zendikar* Zaniness and *Worldwake* Wonderings
Zendikar came with the promise that lands would have an extra impact on game play, and this promise was delivered on via landfall, the flagship mechanic of the set. *Worldwake* continued the trend while riffing on it in subtle ways. While I admit I was hoping for some [Dryad Arbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Arbor) design space nuttiness during the block, landfall turned out to create some pretty wacky situations.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl157_baloth.jpg)  
The first landfall card I laid eyes on was [Rampaging Baloths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampaging+Baloths), which filled me with dreams of bazillions of 4/4 Beasts. [Khalni Heart Expedition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Heart+Expedition) is an exceptional mana acceleration card in dedicated landfall decks. [Ior Ruin Expedition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ior+Ruin+Expedition) fulfilled the card drawing role at first, but [Seer's Sundial](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seer%27s+Sundial) ultimately outclasses it.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Where am I going with all these landfall cards, though? Thought you'd never ask. There's an idea I've been toying with for a while, involving a Johnny stalwart that never seems to get any reminiscence: [Life and Limb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+and+Limb). Juggle that effect in your mind for a while until you understand it. Every [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) is now a 1/1 Saproling, which is kinda cool. More importantly for this deck, all your 1/1 Saproling tokens are now [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) lands. Meaning [Scatter the Seeds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scatter+the+Seeds) will trigger landfall thrice. Now that's an idea kernel.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

I sprinkled other Saproling generators into the budding list. [Jade Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jade+Mage) is an excellent new card that can slowly take over the game. [Utopia Mycon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Mycon) is pretty great as well, able to sacrifice excess Saprolings for mana. [Thelonite Hermit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thelonite+Hermit) makes four at once, which could mean four Beasts as well. [Sprout Swarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sprout+Swarm), I've come to decide, is a better singleton than a four-of in these sorts of decks. The convoke/buyback hybrid can generate an army by itself with enough prior tokens on the field.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The last piece is a splash of blue for [Artificial Evolution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificial+Evolution). The beloved creature type hacker can warp the deck sufficiently. Hack the Baloths to say Saproling instead of Beast. Not only will this result in an armada of 4/4 Saprolings (love weird moments like that) but if you have [Life and Limb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+and+Limb) on the battlefield, those Saprolings will also be [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) lands. Meaning, each 4/4 Saproling will trigger the Baloths ad infinitum. Since it's a "may" ability, you can stop the wheel after 117 rotations.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Artificial+Evolution)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificial+Evolution)  
Otherwise, [Artificial Evolution](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Artificial+Evolution) can hack [Master of the Wild Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+the+Wild+Hunt) to make your army of Saprolings a bit more ferocious. Land acceleration rounds out this [Life and Limb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+and+Limb) bent on landfall.

Another neat landfall combo revolves around the all silver-symboled *Worldwake* duo of [Ruin Ghost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruin+Ghost) and [Tideforce Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tideforce+Elemental). Both are decent build-around cards on their own. [Ruin Ghost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruin+Ghost) looks good alongside the "enters the battlefield" lands from these sets (particularly [Sejiri Steppe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sejiri+Steppe), which turns the Ghost into a pseudo–[Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) (Mother of Ruins?)), and [Tideforce Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tideforce+Elemental) serves as an untap-happy [Puppeteer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Together, with the landfall powers of [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) (to keep the engine going), you can assemble infinite landfall triggers. Tap a [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) and use it to activate [Ruin Ghost](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ruin+Ghost), targeting the [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). In response, spend ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) and use [Tideforce Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tideforce+Elemental) to untap the Ghost. When the [Plains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) comes back (untapped!), two important things happen: Tideforce untaps, and [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) makes a blue mana. In this way, accumulate infinite landfall triggers. Many avenues to victory exist; [Hedron Crab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab) for infinite milling, [Emeria Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) for infinite 1/1 Birds, [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu) for an infinitely powered attacker.

[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lotus+Cobra)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra)  
On the subject of [Tideforce Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tideforce+Elemental) and Bant-tinged decks, I recall a fun combo with another *Worldwake* card: [Selective Memory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selective+Memory), perhaps the telltale modern reject rare. Some wonder at this card's actual usefulness, but my thumbs were certainly sent atwiddling at this card. An interesting combo is to have [Tideforce Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tideforce+Elemental) out alongside [Skyward Eye Prophets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyward+Eye+Prophets). Then if you [Selective Memory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selective+Memory) for all your nonland cards, you can cycle through your deck with the Prophets, generating blue mana with [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) and untapping them each time.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

[Selective Memory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selective+Memory) makes me think of [Treasure Hunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Hunt), which is a decent one-two card advantage punch. I also went with the thematic [Mysteries of the Deep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mysteries+of+the+Deep) as some extra card-draw. In the end it's a green-white-blue hodgepodge of intersecting lands and landfall activations.

Before we leave *Worldwake*, there's another combo I'd like to revisit and update. During [Multikicker Week](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/ftl/79) I created a deck around [Marshal's Anthem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marshal%27s+Anthem). Its aim was to ramp up to twelve mana and return four creatures with an Anthem: [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), and three copies of either [Cloudgoat Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudgoat+Ranger) or [Sengir Autocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Autocrat). Target the Anthem itself with the Cloudskate, and then sacrifice everything to [Phyrexian Altar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Altar) for thirteen mana, gaining one mana for each iteration.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl157_anthem.jpg)  
Since I built that deck, that process has gotten easier to accomplish. This is thanks to [Myr Battlesphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Battlesphere) and [Hornet Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen), new innovations in token-making creatures, as they spawn four, not three. Only two of them and a [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate) are required now, and ramping to ten is easier than twelve. The infinite mana can be funneled into either a [Selesnya Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Guildmage) or a [Martial Coup](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup) (which doubles as mass removal in a pinch). Another method would be [Bramblesnap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramblesnap) (from *Rise of the Eldrazi*, of course) to tap all the creatures before sacrificing them to the Altar.

Last time I had [Buried Alive](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive) to set up my graveyard. This time I'll go with [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) to double as creature searching while ditching the token makers. [Compulsive Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) is a fine card draw and discard spell.

Rising to the Occasion
I'm a big fan of *Rise of the Eldrazi*. The set needed to deliver a unique experience, since it was positioned to be the end to the radical block design of *Zendikar* Block. Stuffed to burst with Eldrazi, Eldrazi Spawn, totem armor, rebound, defender matters, and level up, it was such an off-beat switch from the first two sets that I had to embrace it.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/ftl/ftl157_18G.jpg)  
I was prowling the set on [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) when I came across [Khalni Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Hydra) and thought, "Maybe I should showcase my actual green chroma deck?" Chroma, for those unaware, is an ability from *Eventide* that cares about the amount of colored symbols in mana costs. And [Khalni Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Hydra) delivers a whopping eight—meaning that just one other [Primalcrux](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux) on the field will be a 14/14 trampler. Fat beats a go-go! It's also fun to hoard [Khalni Hydra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Khalni+Hydra)s and [Primalcrux](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux)es in hand for a giant [Phosphorescent Feast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phosphorescent+Feast) (the *Future Sight* edition, since I like me some weird-looking **Magic** cards.)



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The rest of the deck is filled with green symbols from all kinds of mana costs. [Overbeing of Myth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overbeing+of+Myth) is a huge part of the deck, doubling my card flow and providing five symbols. I figured while initially building the deck that it was a great opportunity to use lots of random green singletons, as long as they had tons of green symbols. I like playing singleton-laden decks (why didn't I get into Commander sooner???). Among the one-ofs, [Garruk, Primal Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) can do all sorts of woodsman magic, [Nacatl War-Pride](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+War-Pride) is especially funny with [Primalcrux](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primalcrux) (yes, token copies have mana costs), [Cloudthresher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudthresher) provides four symbols and destroys flying decks, [Unyaro Bees](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unyaro+Bees) makes me laugh and cringe at the same time (a lifelong fear of bees will do that), and a timely [Nostalgic Dreams](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nostalgic+Dreams) can change the game.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

The deck needs lots of mana to work. [Joraga Treespeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker) (ring the theme bell!) is a superb one-mana Elf these days, and [Gaea's Touch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Touch) provides two symbols and a quick mana boost. When I'm in the right game-play environment, I swap out two Touches for two [Land Aid '04](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Land+Aid+%2704). *Unhinged* cards don't always have the green light in casual, but I carry two around just in case. Displaying my catscratch vocals to the world is fun as well.

All together, here's the list.
