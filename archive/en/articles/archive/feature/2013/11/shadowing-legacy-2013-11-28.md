Shadowing Legacy
================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 28, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







Death's Shadow decks keeping grabbing my attention, mainly because of how cool the namesake card is. Earlier, [I took a look at a Modern](/en/articles/archive/plunge-shadows-2013-08-20) [Death's Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) list, but today we've graduated to using the full twenty years of **Magic** cards, instead of the decade Modern spans. 

![Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Death%27s+Shadow) The main engine that drives the [Death's Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) train is still Phyrexian mana, with [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) and [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) offering a lot of life loss for a low amount of actual mana, and providing a good amount of utility while doing so. [Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe) is actually the perfect card for this deck, acting as a zero-mana +2/+2 that lets you know when the way is clear to move in on [Death's Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow). [Snuff Out](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snuff+Out) is an awesome new addition, and mimics Phyrexian mana quite effectively, while being efficient at killing [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), and [Mother of Runes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mother+of+Runes) alike. [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) round out the life-payment spells, and both disrupt the opponent heavily, which is quite necessary in Legacy. 

 As before, lands play a key part in growing the Shadow to monstrous proportions, and [Watery Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watery+Grave) makes a rare appearance in Legacy as a result. The mana base is one thing I like about this deck, as it's got basics, [fetches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&type=+%5B%22Land%22%5D&text=+%5Bsearch%5D+%5Byour%5D+%5Blibrary%5D&set=%7C%5B%22Onslaught%22%5D%7C%5B%22Mirage%22%5D%7C%5B%22Zendikar%22%5D), and only two colors, making it very consistent. 

 Even past the [Death's Shadow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) aspect, this still plays the role of a [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) deck, which is to say that it has cheap threats and cheap disruption. [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) and [Tombstalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tombstalker) are the backup kill mechanisms (or just the kill mechanisms, in [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)'s case), and [Baleful Strix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baleful+Strix), [Daze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daze), and Hymn all give you an advantage in either mana or pure cards. In light of recent events, such as [Grand Prix Washington DC](/en/events/coverage/turtenwald-finally-triumphant-dc), [True-Name Nemesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True-Name+Nemesis) is certainly a card this deck should take into consideration. It can play the card and needs to prepare to play against it, but Legacy being Legacy, there are no shortage of good options, and [Engineered Plague](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Plague) is a nice start if the deck doesn't end up playing any.
