Mines Smashes Melbourne!
========================




Leading the Eldrazi invasion, David Mines defeated Maitland Cameron in White-Blue Eldrazi mirror 2-1 to become the Grand Prix Melbourne Champion!

TOP 8 COVERAGE



The Eldrazi came, they saw, and they conquered, but Melbourne put up quite the fight. As the Top 8 was announced, it looked like the Gatewatch had the upper hand, taking the first 5 spots, while the Eldrazi snuck in with 6th and 7th place, sandbagging their greatest weapon, Brisbane’s David Mines, in 8th.


But the tables were flipped in the quarterfinals, with Louis Thomson-Gregg’s R/G Eldrazi taking down Kentaro Yamamoto’s Living End, Maitland Cameron’s W/U Eldrazi besting Lee Shi Tian’s Living End, and David Mines defeating Abzan Company in the hands of Chris Cousens. Yuuki Ichikawa was the only member of the Gatewatch to escape the quarters after he beat Jason Chung’s Dredge deck. However, Ichikawa soon lost to Mines, while Cameron triumphed over Thomson-Gregg.


A frequent Top 8 competitor in this region and on the play in the W/U Eldrazi mirror, Maitland Cameron was the epitome of the Final Boss for David Mines, but Mines wasted no time seizing the initiative playing an Eldrazi Mimic and an Endless One on his first turn. Cameron battled back to take Game 2, but Mines masterfully dodged Cameron’s Thought-Knot Seers in Game 3 to take the match, making David Mines the Grand Prix Melbourne Champion!


The highlight of the weekend for many though, was the Shadows over Innistrad celebration, featuring Stitcher’s Lab, a creepily atmospheric Escape Room, and several exciting Shadows over Innistrad preview cards. Be sure to check out the videos and photos of the experience across the internet, as we all look forward to the Shadows over Innistrad prerelease 2-3 April!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



The fierce battle continues here in Melbourne as those who have taken the Oath of the Gatewatch valiantly try to fend off the Eldrazi onslaught. Vast casualties on either side have reduced the original field size from 1,105 to 311 players.


So far, things are looking pretty even. Of our four undefeated players on Day 1, Kentaro Yamamoto, Maitland Cameron, Harshana Randeni, and Aidan Frisch, two were playing W/U Eldrazi, while the other two were on Jeskai and the Living End. We will bring you their decklists later today, as well as an archetype breakdown of the top 100 Modern decks in Day 2.


Right behind them are Tomoharu Saito, Jason Chung, Patty Robertson, and Yifan Wei, with Isaac Egan, Oliver Oks, Kelvin Chew, Shota Yasooka, Yuuki Ichikawa, and many more not far behind. Eldrazi appear to have the upper hand, but only just. Who will emerge triumphant by the end of the day? We shall see.

DAY 1 COVERAGE



They came over the horizon, the Eldrazi. While many of us cowered in terror, myself included, the brave and the righteous rode out to meet our foe. 1,105 players took to the battlefield in downtown Melbourne, some of whom sided with the Eldrazi, while others took up the *Oath of the Gatewatch* in an attempt to stop the towering monstrosities.


As Day 1 drew to a close, only 311 combatants remained, and leading the fray undefeated where Kentaro Yamamoto, Harshana Randeni, Maitland Cameron, and Aidan Frisch. Not far behind on 8-1 were Lee Shi Tian. Tomoharu Saito, Jason Chung, Patty Robertson, and Yifan Wei. Still in contention with a fighting chance: Isaac Egan, Oliver Oks, Kelvin Chew, Shota Yasooka, Yuuki Ichikawa, and many more.


Join us tomorrow as we break down the archetypes of the top 100 decks here at Grand Prix Melbourne, and see whether the Eldrazi menace can be stopped!
