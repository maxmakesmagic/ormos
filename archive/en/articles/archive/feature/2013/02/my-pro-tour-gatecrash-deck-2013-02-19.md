My Pro Tour *Gatecrash* Deck
============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 19, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_samblack.jpg)
By Sam Black




Sam Black is a Platinum Pro Player and longtime writer for StarCityGames.com. He is a respected deck builder and took over Daily Decks for the first half of 2013. 







I write all the decks for each week during the previous week. Right now, I'm on a plane on my way to [Pro Tour *Gatecrash*](/en/events/coverage/martell-calls-down-reckoning-pro-tour) in Montreal. 


 There's a good chance that Standard will look fairly different when you read this than it did when I wrote it, but this week I'm going to feature a variety of decks I considered playing for Pro Tour *Gatecrash*—or decks that teammates played. 


I'm not sure these lists will be exact, since there are still several hours before we actually have to register our decks and a lot can change at the last minute, but as of right now, I believe I have a pretty good idea of what I'll be playing.


 I've spent the last few weeks tweaking a variety of Orzhov-based decks. I've arrived at a deck I'm fairly happy with called The Aristocrats, because it's largely based on [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) and [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat). 


This is an aggressive deck that exploits a variety of creature-based synergies while using several of the best creatures in the format.


 The pressure starts early with [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), the heaviest-hitting one-mana creature in Standard, or [Doomed Traveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler), an all-star in this deck that fuels all the other cards. 


![Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cartel+Aristocrat)

[Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat), [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest), and another Human to be determined (I'm considering [Knight of Infamy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy); [Nearheath Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nearheath+Pilgrim); [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben); [Thrill-Kill Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrill-Kill+Assassin); and [Gather the Townsfolk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+the+Townsfolk)) follow up on turn two. 



[Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) is a deceptively good blocker against small creatures and a resilient threat, as well as a sacrifice outlet to allow [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest) to activate whenever I have two other creatures. 



[Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) provides those other creatures, which are excellent fuel to preserve my Aristocrats, or just flying attackers, and [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) is a nightmare for aggressive strategies. 



[Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) is at its best in this deck, with around twenty Humans to sacrifice to get +1/+1 counters. It presides over a far-more-nourishing public than we've usually seen in the Zombie cast it's supported in the past. 



[Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) deals a huge amount of damage, and, with eight creatures that let me sacrifice a creature at any time, my opponent is unlikely to ever get his or her conscripted creature back. 



[Orzhov Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Charm) is an amazing removal spell in a format where most removal is extremely limited, and this can double as a threat against control decks that don't have creatures I need to kill. I believe it's a criminally overlooked card at the moment.
