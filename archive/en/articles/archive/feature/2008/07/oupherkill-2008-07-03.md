Oupherkill
==========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 3, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar











Howdy, all! It's that time of year again. We're nearing the halfway point of the *Eventide* preview season and I have to say it's about time we previewed an Ouphe. The bar has been set pretty high after *Shadowmoor* brought us a pair of nice utility Ouphes, the card-drawing [Dusk Urchins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Urchins) and the life-gaining and pun-loving [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks). Joining [Spellwild Ouphe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellwild+Ouphe) as Ouphes who don't particularly care about the activated abilities of artifacts (ignoring for now the Brownie defectors), these new tribe members showed that just because your creature type sounds incredibly silly doesn't mean you can lay the beats and provide hours of entertainment for the whole family. (I guess [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) already proved that.) Like [Dusk Urchins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dusk+Urchins) and [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), my preview card expands our definition of what an Ouphe can do. I've never seen an Ouphe like this before:

![Gilder Bairn](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/house/cm130_7ho5fg.jpg)

Note that according to the card FAQ, "Gilder Bairn's ability will replicate each kind of counter on the targeted permanent, not just +1/+1 or -1/-1 counters." Any type of permanent and any type of counter is fair game. Some permanents can have more than one type of counter on them, and both types will be doubled by Gilder Bairn. For example, imagine you have an [Aboroth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aboroth) in play with one age counter and one -1/-1 counter on it. Use Gilder Bairn's ability targeting [Aboroth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aboroth), and you will end up with an [Aboroth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aboroth) that has two age counters and two -1/-1 counters on it. With cards like Frankenstein's Monster and Ebon Praetor that can accumulate a motley collection of +1/+0, +2/+0, +2/+2, +0/+2, and +1/+1 counters, Gilder Bairn will give them twice as many of each type of counter. Good luck keeping track!

### Doubling Season to Taste

Blue and green is probably my favourite colour combination. Of all time. Sure, ebony and ivory hold some appeal, as do silver and gold. Blue, green, orange has its moments, but, ultimately, I think the orange is unnecessary. With blue and green alone, you've got a mitt full of cards, a boatload of mana, a bushel basket full of beef, and a whole can of whoop-ass. Plus, you have the ability to just say "No!" at any time. "[Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) on my Primalcrux? No, sir: [Cryptic Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command)!" We all know it's bound to happen. What more could a grown mage want?

Besides their unique strengths, as you can see from this diagram, the areas where blue and green overlap are totally awesome! At least for the Johnny.

![This is a Venn Diagram](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/house/cm130_UGAwesome.jpg)

Monkeying with counters is one such area. Gilder Bairn comes from a long line of counter-monkeyers, such as [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season), [Evolution Vat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Vat), and [Clockspinning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning). Each one has their advantages and disadvantages. Gilder Bairn combines the power and versatility of [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) with the precision of [Clockspinning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning) and the offensive ferocity of a [Lumengrid Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumengrid+Warden). While it is doesn't have the global effect of [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season), Gilder Bairn has the ability to affect opposing permanents, and the best part is that it doesn't particularly matter if it comes into play long after the cards you wish to "gild." [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) doesn't provide you with this luxury. Furthermore, in many cases, [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) acts as a one-shot. Most of the synergistic cards come into play with their counters, and after they do so, [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) has done its job. Gilder Bairn, like [Clockspinning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning), can be used to further increase these initial counters or, in other cases, replenish them.

Just think: If you start with a single, solitary counter, and you take that counter and double it each turn, by the end of the month you'll have more than a million dollars!

### Reading the Q Cards

![Utopia Vow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Utopia+Vow)As with any of the creatures bearing the untap symbol, Gilder Bairn could use a little assistance if you want to get the most out of its ability. There are many ways to tap the Bairn. The first and most obvious method is to simply attack with it. It's got a big enough butt that it can probably survive its fair share of combat steps without any help, but giving it evasion somehow couldn't hurt.Then, of course, you've got the recent auras that grant the enchanted creature a tap ability. [Utopia Vow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Vow) seems to be a popular one, since it gives the creature a mana ability which effectively reduces the cost of the ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20210430014038im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/untap.gif) creature's ability. As Devin Low discussed last week, on certain creatures ([Leech Bonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leech+Bonder) or [Puresight Merrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puresight+Merrow)), this can allow you to use the untap ability as much as you want. [Presence of Gond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Presence+of+Gond), [Power of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire), and [Elemental Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Mastery) are other possibilities. I'm particularly excited about the idea of using Gilder Bairn, [Presence of Gond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Presence+of+Gond), and [Door of Destinies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+of+Destinies) together (with other Elves, naturally). Tap the Bairn to make an Elf, untap it to double the number of charge counters on [Door of Destinies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+of+Destinies), lather, rinse, and repeat. Pretty soon you'll have an army of enormous Elves (and very shiny hair).

Since Gilder Bairn is an Ouphe, the "tap an untapped [creature of a certain type]" cards won't be useful (i.e. [Drowner of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Secrets), [Master Apothecary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary), [Patron Wizard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+Wizard), etc.), but there are plenty of cards that only require that you tap a creature. Recent additions to this group include [Revelsong Horn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revelsong+Horn), [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum), and [Mothdust Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mothdust+Changeling), while [Aura of Dominion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+of+Dominion), [Glare of Subdual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glare+of+Subdual), [Opposition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition), [Kyren Negotiations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kyren+Negotiations), [Hair-Strung Koto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hair-Strung+Koto), and [Koskun Falls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koskun+Falls) (among others) fill out the back catalogue.

Now that we can assure that our Ouphes will get good and tapped, let's take a gander at some of the different kinds of counters we can be doubling.

### -1/-1 Counters!

Since these are the counters *du jour*, I figure we might as well start here.   
 One cool thing about Gilder Bairn is that it has a bit of mean streak. Sure, it likes to make things bigger and better, but it moonlights as an Assassin. Draw first blood with a   
 -1/-1 counter courtesy of [Leech Bonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leech+Bonder), [Gnarled Effigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gnarled+Effigy), or [Grim Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Poppet), and Gilder Bairn can swoop in to finish the job.

### +1/+1 Counters and Charge Counters!

Grafters! Spikes! Hydras! Triskeli-whatevers! Vampires! [Clockwork Beast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockwork+Beast)s! Heck, Clockwork Vampires! There is no shortage of creatures that enjoy being encumbered by +1/+1 counters. One of them ([Solarion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solarion)) can even go about doubling those counters by itself. With Gilder Bairn, [Evolution Vat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Vat), and [Doubling Season](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubling+Season) all easily fitting into the same deck, I foresee some very large [Solarion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solarion)s in the near future.

Charge counters are another "standard" counter, at least since *Mirrodin*. There is ample reason to double these counters as well. You might want to produce lots of mana ([Altar of Shadows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar+of+Shadows), [Black Market](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Market), all the Mana Batteries), gain tons of life ([Clearwater Goblet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clearwater+Goblet)), kill creatures ([Infused Arrows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infused+Arrows), [Heliophial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliophial)), make a whole bunch of Snakes ([Orochi Hatchery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orochi+Hatchery)), make a whole bunch of very big Snakes ([Riptide Replicator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Replicator)), or just do whatever it is that [Ventifact Bottle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ventifact+Bottle) does.

### Doom Counters!

Before Y2K, there was [Armageddon Clock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon+Clock). One of them has pretty much no effect at all and the other is a computer glitch. Counting down to Armageddon is fun and all, but the fact that any player can turn back the clock is a little disappointing. Add [Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub) to skip the upkeep, and your mutual destruction is practically assured. Heck, you could use Gilder Bairn's Ouphe-y brethren ([Brown Ouphe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brown+Ouphe)) to counter any attempts to remove the doom counters. [Armageddon Clock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armageddon+Clock) also happens to be the only magical clock that actually works with [Clockspinning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning) (except for [Mistform Ultimus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus), of course).

### Fuse Counters!

A fan of pyrotechnics? Try [Bomb Squad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomb+Squad), [Incendiary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incendiary), and [Goblin Bomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bomb). Just like setting things on fire? Why not use [Ghitu Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Fire), powered up by [Braid of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire), [Gemstone Array](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gemstone+Array), and [Fungal Reaches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Reaches)? Prefer to jump through some hoops for a more satisfying (if unlikelier) fiery cataclysm? Try [Molten Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Hydra), or [Spike Breeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Breeder) and [Kyren Negotiations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kyren+Negotiations). Do you like your arsonists to sport skin-tight leather suits and goggles? Call on [Chandra Nalaar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra+Nalaar) to set your blazes. Gilder Bairn just speeds up the process.

### Loyalty Counters!

Um, so, yeah. The planeswalkers. Gilder Bairn allows you to fast-forward right to their "ultimates." Sarkhan Vol works especially well with Gilder Bairn for all I know!

### Time and Fade Counters!

While Gilder Bairn can't do anything about the time counters on suspended cards the way [Clockspinning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning) can (they aren't permanents), it can keep your creatures with vanishing around to fight another day. The cards with fade counters work similarly, but the ones that allow you to "spend" fade counters to accomplish some goal (like [Parallax Wave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Wave), [Woodripper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodripper), [Saproling Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling+Burst), or [Ancient Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Hydra)) are especially good with Gilder Bairn. With some shrewd resource management, you can keep these cards around indefinitely while at the same time removing creatures from the game, destroying artifacts, creating Saprolings, or dealing damage virtually at will.

### Polyp Counters!

![Coral Reef](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Coral+Reef)If ever there was a card that was made for Gilder Bairn, it's Coral Reef. Not only is it a permanent that comes into play with counters and can redistribute those counters to other creatures, but it also provides a handy tap outlet for your Gilder Bairns. What can you possibly use polyp counters for, you ask? Well, for starters, you simply buff up the backsides of your creatures. Like I always say, a 1/4 Gilder Bairn is better than a 1/3 Gilder Bairn. Those +0/+1 counters can turn certain creatures into face-smashing monsters if you can somehow switch their power and toughness (Thanks, layer 6e!). Your polyp-enhanced [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba)s and [Turtleshell Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turtleshell+Changeling)s will become serious threats if they can get through unblocked.That's where [Kulrath Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kulrath+Knight) and [Horobi, Death's Wail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horobi%2C+Death%27s+Wail) come in. Since you can put polyp counters on opposing creatures as well as your own, you can use Coral Reef to slowly pacify your opponent's entire army with [Kulrath Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kulrath+Knight) or simply destroy everything in your path with [Horobi, Death's Wail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horobi%2C+Death%27s+Wail). Gilder Bairn itself can be used to kill creatures with [Horobi, Death's Wail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Horobi%252C%2BDeath%2527s%2BWail) on the board. ### Reactor? Darn near Gilder!

I used to like to play decks that pretty much do nothing. For a while, I had an oversized blue-white deck that did little more than stall the game with an arsenal of counterspells and recurring [Hesitation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hesitation)s and [Standstill](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Standstill)s (Thanks, Hanna!). Meanwhile, I would be playing cards like [Portent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Portent) and [Ransack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ransack) to ensure that my opponent drew nothing relevant (i.e. nothing but lands). It wasn't a particularly good deck, mind you, but it was fun to see exactly how long I could get away with doing nothing. Story of my life.

This deck is kind of like that.

![Darksteel Reactor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Darksteel+Reactor)With [Darksteel Reactor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Reactor), a win condition that, unassisted, takes twenty turns to do its thing, you know we're going to have to buy some time. Of course, Gilder Bairn speeds things up, so I made sure that my stalling techniques also worked with the Ouphe. [Spike Weaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Weaver) can keep creatures off your back indefinitely as long as you periodically double the number of counters on it. [Utopia Vow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Utopia+Vow) holds a single creature at bay, but doubles as a tap outlet for your Gilder Bairns. [Experiment Kraj](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+Kraj), in a roundabout way, provides another way to tap your Ouphe.Normally, [Parallax Tide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Tide) eats your opponent's lands and spits them back out, but with Gilder Bairn doubling the number of fade counters on it each turn, you can eventually ensure that you can remove every land your opponent can play. [Rune Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rune+Snag) and [Voidslime](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidslime) protect your key cards, while the latter can also be use to counter [Parallax Tide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Parallax+Tide)'s triggered ability—you know, the one that gives your opponent all those lands back. With enough verse-counter doubling, [Recantation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recantation) becomes a one-sided [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval).
