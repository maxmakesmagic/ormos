The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on May 12, 2016. Today's Must
============


1. [Game Analysis: Esper Dragons vs. Black-Green Sacrifice](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/game-analysis-esper-dragons-vs-bg-sacrifice/) | ChannelFireball | Reid Duke

When Reid Duke puts his head down and deeply analyzes any aspect of *Magic*, it's a must-read. His "[Thoughtseize You](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/26855_Thoughtseize-You.html)" article from a few years ago, for example, was one of the best single-card strategy articles ever written. And, of course, [his run on Level One](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/level-one/level-one-full-course-2015-10-05), where he taught the basics of *Magic*, is one of the best in-depth teaching tools available.


So it's no surprise that this article—which analyzes a match between Luis Scott-Vargas and Shota Yasooka—is incredibly detailed and enlightening. Get inside the head of one of the best players of the world while he tries to get inside the head of two more of the best players in the world. And then insert a lazy *Inception* joke here. But then go read this article.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Modern Dredge](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/modern-constructed-league-2016-05-11)

With Modern Weekend coming up as [Grand Prix Los Angeles](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/premierplay/grandprix/fact-sheet-gpla16) and [Grand Prix Charlotte](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/premierplay/grandprix/fact-sheet-gpcha16) take place in just over a week, we turn our attention to Modern and, specifically, to Dredge.


In this case, two new cards from *Shadows over Innistrad* seem to have given the archetype a kick in the pants, as Underground\_Brazil took the list below to a 5-0 finish in a *Magic Online* League.


[Insolent Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insolent+Neonate) gives the deck another discard outlet beyond [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Lotleth Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll), plus a way to dredge with a draw. In fact, because you get to discard before drawing, that means Neonate can hypothetically discard [Golgari Grave-Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll) and immediately dredge it.


[Prized Amalgam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prized+Amalgam), however, is the real, um, prize, as it returns from the graveyard easily and readily. Whether [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast), [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), [Rotting Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rotting+Rats), or [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine) is doing the returning, it doesn't matter—free 3/3s can beat all but the fastest of decks.


Graveyard hate is probably a pretty tough beat for this deck—beating [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) has got to be difficult—but it can do a reasonable impression of a beatdown deck, curving [Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) into [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast) or [Lotleth Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll), into [Prized Amalgam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prized+Amalgam). If you get that kind of start and your opponent is hoping to slow you down with [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace), well, they're going to have a bad time.
