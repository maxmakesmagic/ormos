Day 2 Coverage of Grand Prix Warsaw
===================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 11, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast












Yesterday, 984 players entered Day 1 of Grand Prix Warsaw 2013 to show off their skills in Standard Constructed. It was a day of intense competition, of great **Magic**, and exciting stories. We saw the rise of Gruul Aggro, the continued dominance of Jund Midrange and UWR Flash, and the surprising revitalization of Bant Hexproof. At the end of the day, 5 players were sitting on top of the standings with perfect 9-0 records: Sébastien Wilmotte from Belgium, Jens Brouwer from Germany, Niklas Ramquist from Sweden, Wenzel Krautmann from Germany, and Tomas Kuchta from the Czech Republic.


Today, they and 157 others have earned the right to compete for the title of Grand Prix Warsaw Champion. Join us here for more text and video coverage as we see who can successfully navigate through six more rounds of Standard to make it to the Top 8!

Sunday, 10:10 a.m. — The 9-0 Decks
----------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Five players escaped yesterday's competition with unblemished records of nine wins, zero losses. The big story Friday evening was the rise of Brian Kibler's Gruul deck, which took down half of all Grand Prix Trials. The big news Saturday evening, however, was the resurgence of Bant Hexproof . Of the five undefeated players, more than half got to their perfect 9-0 scores by enchanting [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) and its ilk.

Round 10 Feature Match — Niklas Ramquist vs. Wenzel Krautmann
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

So first Brian Kibler's Gruul surged in popularity, and now you find all of these Bant Hexproof decks at the top? How come? Could it be that Hexproof has a good matchup against Gruul? Gruul mage Wenzel Krautmann didn't think so. His opponent, Niklas Ramquist, playing Hexproof, claimed, "The matchup's about even," to which Krautmann replied, "Well, I won against two already."


However, the first game saw Krautmann stranded on three basic [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s, while Ramquist's deck at least delivered on the Aura part of the Hexproof/Aura package. He put [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) and [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) on one [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and another Courage on another Pilgrim. When Krautmann was about to stabilize by trading away his [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) and [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) and turning his [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze) 5/5, Ramquist redoubled with a third [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage), putting Krautmann at 3. Krautmann was forced to trade away his Ooze, but rebuild reasonably well with [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf). Meanwhile, Ramquist was left with no creature at all.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpwar13/fm10_ramquist.jpg)  
*Niklas Ramquist* 
Finally, red mana showed up on Krautmann's side and he began the process of diminishing Ramquist's considerable lifetotal, slow at first, then increasingly faster. [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager), [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze), and [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) joined the fray, while Ramquist was still without creature. [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) appeared just in time, was enchanted with [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), and then Ramquist's [Spell Rupture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Rupture) sealed the deal, countering a [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) that otherwise would have been lethal.


"That was a close one," commented Krautmann. "It didn't look that way at first, when you missed the red for so long," Ramquist replied. "But you almost turned it around."


For game two, Krautmann started on [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze), and [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager), while Ramquist opened on [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) as his first play, which Krautmann's [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) in hand couldn't really get rid off.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpwar13/fm10_krautmann.jpg)  
*Wenzel Krautmann* 
Ramquist followed it up with [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) and [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), attacking for 10, putting Krautmann dead next turn. Krautmann got in lethal first, though, with the help of a newly-cast [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider), dealing just enough damage to even the score.


[Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf); [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar), attack for 3; [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar), attack for 7. That was Krautmann's start into the third and final game of the match, a good one to be sure. Meanwhile, Ramquist had some trouble with too many of his lands entering the battlefield tapped, and could only muster a [Fiendslayer Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiendslayer+Paladin) with [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor). When he blocked with it and lost it to [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager), he never recovered.


**Wenzel Krautmann defeated Niklas Ramquist to advance to 10-0. "Now, I've beaten three Hexproof decks."**

Sunday, 12:15 p.m. — Deck Tech: Conjurer's Closet with Maciej Jampula
---------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

Maciej Jampula is a 45-year cake baker from Poland who showed up with one of the coolest decks at the tournament this weekend: he's battling [Conjurer's Closet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conjurer%27s+Closet) and [Tyrant of Discord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tyrant+of+Discord). ### How was the deck conceived and built?


"I was inspired by a [Conjurer's Closet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conjurer%27s+Closet) deck that I saw at a Starcitygames tournament a short while ago. That deck was Red-Green; I added blue for [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone). [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) is good with all the enters-the-battlefield effects."


### Can you walk me through the key cards and an ideal draw?


"An ideal draw includes [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern), or [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) to play [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) as fast as possible, and then a [Clone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone) to copy it. [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) is easily the MVP of the deck. Against Jund or UWR Flash, my game plan is to destroy their lands. [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) also has great synergy with [Tyrant of Discord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tyrant+of+Discord), which is very good when the opponent has few lands due to an early [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime).


But the engine behind the deck is [Conjurer's Closet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conjurer%27s+Closet). It's great with all the enters-the-battlefield effects. Not just [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime), but [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) as well."


### What is your most valuable sideboard card?


"I surprised many opponents with [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift). One match, I played against The Aristocrats, and my opponent got about 10 creatures out. He attacked, I didn't block, and he activated [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) to threaten a lot of damage. In response, I had a devastating [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift). He didn't expect that."


### Would you make any changes to the deck?


"[Tyrant of Discord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tyrant+of+Discord) is too slow. I think I prefer [Progenitor Mimic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Progenitor+Mimic) instead in the maindeck."


### Were you happy with your deck choice this weekend?


"Yeah, very happy. I got a good result with a rogue deck!"

Round 12 Feature Match — Rasmus Björklund vs. Frederico Bastos
--------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke*

 

Once again, Jund and Blue-White-Red Control clashed in the feature match area and once again the control deck prevailed. A true veteran of the game, Portugal's Frederico Bastos has two Pro Tour Top 8s to his name from more than ten years ago. Sweden's Rasmus Björklund on the other hand had just finished in his first Top 8 two weeks ago at Grand Prix Rimini. Going into the round both had two losses already and, for another Top 8 berth, desperately needed to avoid any more.


The first game went at a very sedate pace, clearly favoring Bastos's UWR. And indeed, after [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), Bastos seemed to have an answer for everything: [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate) for [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) for [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren), [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) for [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze). At one point, Björklund peeked at Bastos's hand with [Lifebane Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie) and saw [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm), another [Syncopate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Syncopate), [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling), [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate), and [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). This wasn't looking good for Björklund and continued to get worse later on with [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for five.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpwar13/fm12_bjrklund.jpg)  
*Rasmus Björklund* 
Bastos, always a calm player, played it very safe. Only after he had succesfully dealt with each and every threat Björklund could muster, did he finally summon [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling). The mighty [Shapeshifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shapeshifter), however, didn't take long to end things.


Game two went quite differently, starting with Bastos's two mulligans. Though, at first, it appeared he would have an answer for everything again. Burn took out [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) countered [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), [Warleader's Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix) killed [Scavenging Ooze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze). But all of this was only possible because Bastos was stalling on land. As such, he couldn't put [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) to any good use and soon ran out of answers. [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) cleared Bastos's hand, and once he had lost all of his spells, now it obviously was time for him to finally draw some lands. A Wolf token and a [Lifebane Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie) finished the job.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpwar13/fm12_bastos.jpg)  
*Frederico Bastos* 
[Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) were the first spells of game three. In fact, Bastos didn't cast a single spell before his end-of-turn [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) for two, even allowing Huntmaster to turn into [Ravager of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravager+of+the+Fells). [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) arrived just in time, but a 3/3 beast remained and put Bastos at 2.


[Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) targeting [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) brought Bastos back to 5 and really brought him back into the game too. [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion), [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought)—suddenly all the action was taking place on Bastos's side of the table. He even had a [Warleader's Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix) to return to a more healthy, if not entierly safe, lifetotal. Björklund, meanwhile, didn't have an answer to [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion) and was finally overrun by tokens just as time in the round was running out.

Round 14 Feature Match — Ivan Floch vs. Eliott Boussaudi
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Frank Karsten*

 

Wolves, Wurms, even an Octopus---this match was like a zoo.


### The Players and the Decks


Ivan Floch is an experienced player from the Slovak Republic. He has played over 500 matches at Pro Tours and Grands Prix, recently led his national team at the World **Magic** Cup, and was the champion at Grand Prix Lisbon 2012. Today, he is playing the deck that put the most players into Day 2: Jund midrange. "I actually wanted to play the red-green Domri Ooze deck that the Japanese ran yesterday," Floch mentioned. "But I made that decision only hours before the tournament, and I was unable to collect the cards in time. Jund was my backup choice, and it worked out for me. Still, I think the deck of Shuuhei and Yuuya is good, even if they didn't do well in the Grand Prix."


Eliott Boussaudi is a Grand Prix regular from Toulouse, France who hasn't had a breakout finish yet. He came to the table sporting a Norwegian Octopus, called Blekksprut. "This is the mascot of my house in Toulouse. When I go to a **Magic** tournament, I always bring it with me," Boussaudi explained. The deck he registered for the Grand Prix was Selesnya Aggro.


Both players were sitting at 11-2 records going into this round, so there was a lot on the line. A win would provide a good position to make it to the Top 8, whereas a loss would make it almost impossible to reach the Top 8.


### The Games


In game 1, Boussaudi had a fast draw featuring [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), and [Advent of the Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Advent+of+the+Wurm). Floch, in the meantime, ramped with [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) into [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return), which was unimpressive as it did not stop any of Boussaudi's attackers. A [Faith's Shield](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Shield) to counter [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) enabled Boussaudi to stay on the offence, and shortly after, he swung in for lethal damage.


In game 2, Boussaudi's deck did not cooperate, as he had to go down to 4 cards in search of a keepable hand. So, before the game even started, Boussaudi was already three cards down. Then Floch's first four plays were all creatures with enters-the-battlefield card advantage triggers: two [Lifebane Zombie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lifebane+Zombie), and two [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells). Seven cards down. Next up were two [Ravager of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravager+of+the+Fells) transformation triggers. Nine cards down. Yeah, this game wasn't really fair. Although Boussaudi fought valiantly with [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence), it was to no avail: he was already down too many cards, and quickly succumbed to Floch's card advantage creatures.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpwar13/R14_Ivan-Floch.jpg)  
*Ivan Floch puts [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) to good use.* 
Game 3 was a similarly lopsided affair. [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) cleared away two [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), leaving Boussaudi with only one land, and unable to cast his spells. [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) quickly rumbled in unopposed.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpwar13/R14_Eliott-Boussaudi.jpg)  
*Even though luck was not on Eliott Boussaudi's side, his Octopus kept on smiling.* 
"[Sorry](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorry) about those two games," Floch offered.


### Postgame on Sideboarding


After the games, the players discussed their sideboard choices for this matchup. Boussaudi showed that he sided out [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) and [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) (which are weak to instant speed creature removal) for [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless), [Sigarda, Host of Herons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons), and [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb). "I sided in 2 [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) for to destroy a transformed [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and a [Beast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast) token from [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk)," he explained. Floch wasn't sure: "I don't know. You're the aggressive deck. You don't want answers for my cards; you want to have as much pressure as possible."


Floch showed that he took out [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) for [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame). "They are really important to get rid of [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence)," he said. He also brought in a [Barter in Blood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barter+in+Blood) on the draw, but wasn't sure whether [Duress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) might have been better. This serves to show that sideboarding is one of the most difficult aspects of the game---even the best players in the world haven't figured it all out.


**Ivan Floch defeats Eliott Boussaudi 2-1**

And finally Mathieu Deloly's list, which is, well, mostly blue-white. Most notably, however, his red splash is supported not by the usual duals but by [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds) and a couple of actual [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s. A three-color deck which kills other three-color decks with [Burning Earth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Earth)? We like that.
