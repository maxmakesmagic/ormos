Sealed Deck with Martin Jůza
----------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*
Martin Jůza has certainly shown the World he's a Mage with which trifling is not advised. With 4 Grand Prix Top 8's in the last year, including a win in Bochum, you'd be hard pressed to pick a better player to give you advice on how to build a *Return to Ravnica* sealed deck.


When I approached the table, Martin had nothing but frowns for the cards in front of him.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/Sealed_Juza.jpg)Martin Jůza cannot see anything he likes.


"I don't have anything good," he lied, fanning out some great Rakdos removal and Golgari fixing. "This is good, but it needs a couple of [Gatecreeper Vine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) s before I'd want to play it all. Everything costs 3 of more, and if someone kills one of these [Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian) s, I'd be..." he faltered, unable to articulate the horrific idea of being just that far behind on the board.


"You want good synergy, rares, and removal," he explained, "and I don't really have that. Most people play Rakdos if they have rares like [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) or [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) , or Azorius splash Red, or Selesnya and Golgari if they have the nuts populate deck."


I asked Martin for some general advice on building sealed decks, and he advocated consistency. "People I playtest with are always asking why I don't splash this, or play that," he laughed, explaining that he'd rather have smooth draws than leave himself open to being stuck with off-color cards in his hand.


"I don't get greedy, that's just how I am."



Saturday, 1:30 p.m. – Quo Ejected from Grand Prix Sydney
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Event Coverage Staff*
Tzu-Ching Kuo was removed from the venue during Round 3 of Grand Prix Sydney for attempting to buy and sell cards from other players, an act prohibited by Wizards of the Coast and the tournament organizer. Kuo received a warning for buying and selling cards on Friday, and the second occasion during Saturday's Day One resulted in him being removed from the premises. This also ended his participation as a player in the Grand Prix.


Wizards of the Coast encourages players to trade cards with each other at events such as Grand Prix. However, only designated vendors are permitted to buy and sell cards or other game-related products on site. Players are also prohibited from soliciting to buy or sell cards with other players at an offsite location.

Round 4 Feature Match - Hao-Shan Huang vs. Wilfy Horig
------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
Straight off a Top 8 at Grand Prix: Singapore last weekend, Huang sat down to battle Horig, a respected Melbourne player. Huang led the action with a [Stealer of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets) , which Horig killed with an [Auger Spree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auger+Spree) . Huang rebuilt with a [Cobblebrute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobblebrute) , while Horig developed his board with a [Rakdos Keyrune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune) .


### Game 1


Huang's [Cobblebrute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobblebrute) attacked unblocked, and he followed up with a [Jace, Architect of Thought](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) . Horig had a [Mind Rot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) to knock a [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) out of Huang's hand, then finished off the Planeswalker with his Keyrune. Huang took the opportunity to summon a [Tower Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) . Horig summoned a [Hypersonic Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypersonic+Dragon) , but was unable to attack effectively, on a perilously low life total.



![Knightly Valor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Knightly+Valor)![Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tower+Drake)

 

Huang's [Knightly Valor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knightly+Valor) on his [Tower Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake) made the flier an intimidating threat. Horig tried a [Zanikev Locust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zanikev+Locust) , which blocked for a turn, but Horig didn't have any other ways to deal with the Drake.


Hao-Shan Huang 1 – Wilfy Horig 0


### Game 2


Horig led the second game with the eponymous rodent, [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) . Huang found himself a [Tavern Swindler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tavern+Swindler) as the Rats stayed at home for a turn.


Huang, keen to apply pressure before being overrun by Rodentus Horribilis, could only attack with his Swindler and summon an [Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian) . Horig declined to block and made another Rat...


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/01_R4_Horig.jpg)*A [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) s walks into a bar. Then another one. And then it all unfolds with a tedious inevitability...* 
 

We're sure that Huang had plays on his 4th and 5th turns, but they were, for all intents and purposes, irrelevant.


Wilfy Horig 1 – Hao-Shan Huang 1


### Game 3


Huang too a double mulligan into the decider, and had the first action with a [Sewer Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Shambler) . Horig kept pace with an unleashed [Gore-House Chainwalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gore-House+Chainwalker) . Huang followed up his Shambler's attack with a [Jarad, Golgari Lich Lord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jarad%2C+Golgari+Lich+Lord) , who Horig was quick to [Annihilating Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire) .


Huang, undeterred, kept attacking with his Shambler and followed with an [Ogre Jailbreaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Jailbreaker) . Horig, to an audible inbreath from the crowd, drew and summoned his [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) s with the vital 3 mana spare to protect it. Huang, already behind in resources, struggled to keep up over the next few turns, eventually Packing it in.


Wilfy Horig wins 2-1.

Round 6 Feature Match - Daniel Unwin vs. Simon Harnden
------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
### Game 1


"He's seen my sideboard, and knows it's better than my maindeck", Harnden confidently told me as he and Unwin sat down. Unwin sadly nodded and led with a [Centaur Courser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Courser) , while Harnden summoned a [Daggerdrome Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp) . Unwin cast a [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern) , while Harnden applied a slow clock with a [Lobber Crew](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew) , his Imp patiently attacking through the air to nibble away at Unwin's life total.


Unwin summoned a [New Prahv Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=New+Prahv+Guildmage) , while Harnden unleashed a [Spawn of Rix Maadi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawn+of+Rix+Maadi) onto the battlefield. Unwin sent it packing with a [Voidwielder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidwielder) . Harnden re-cast his Spawn and dropped an [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections) onto a land, which, coupled with the lifegain from the Imp, worked very well for him indeed.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/01_R6_Harnden.jpg)*Harnden's sideboard: better than your maindeck.*
 

Unwin tried to keep pace, casting an [Azorius Keyrune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Keyrune) and passing with a swathe of mana open for shenanigans. Harnden suited up his Imp with a [Deviant Glee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deviant+Glee) , and a [Traitorous Instinct](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitorous+Instinct) s let him attack fearlessly, his Spawn getting double-blocked and killed. Unwin summoned a [Vitu-Ghazi Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitu-Ghazi+Guildmage) and attacked with his creatures, but Harnden's [Lobber Crew](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lobber+Crew) and a freshly cast [Stab Wound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) s kept Unwin well clear of being able to race effectively.


An attack with his [Daggerdrome Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp) met a [Trostani's Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment) from Unwin. Post-combat, Harnden cast a [Stab Wound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) s to finish off Unwin's remaining life total.


"You didn't even play good cards", laughed Unwin as he riffled through his sideboard.


Simon Harnden 1 – Daniel Unwin 0


### Game 2


A [Thrill-Kill Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrill-Kill+Assassin) from Harnden was met with an [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) from Unwin. Harnden's follow-up [Splatter Thug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug) and [Viashino Racketeer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashino+Racketeer) were matched by a [Chromatic Lantern](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Lantern) and a [Golgari Decoy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Decoy) .


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/01_R6_Unwin.jpg)*Unwin finds the Golgari quite Charming.*
 

Unwin used a [Golgari Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Charm) to finish off Harnden's Racketeer. "See, I can kill a Pack Rat", Unwin pointed out. Harnden tapped two mana and summoned... A [Daggerdrome Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp) .


Not the Rat Unwin was looking for. He didn't seem to mind as he attacked with his [Golgari Decoy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Decoy) , forcing Harnden to throw his Imp into the graveyard in disgust. A post-combat [Risen Sanctuary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Risen+Sanctuary) drove them to a decider, Harnden shaking his head at his mistake.


Daniel Unwin 1 – Simon Harnden 1


### Game 3


While shuffling, Unwin and Harnden deliberated the best way Unwin's deck had of dealing with a Turn 2 [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat) . After listing their outs (the two are friends, and had discussed their decks before the match), this is exactly what happened. Harnden led with the Rat, and Unwin's [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) could only be played a turn too late thanks to a hindering [Transguild Promenade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transguild+Promenade) .


Unwin ramped into a [Martial Law](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Law) to try and stop the bleeding, but the swarm proved too much.


Simon Harnden 2 – Daniel Unwin 1

Saturday, 8:07 p.m. – Round 8 : On the Rebound
----------------------------------------------


*by Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw*
In the past, we've done a good job of bringing you all of the Main Event Action (at least, I like to think we've done a good job) but you'd be mistaken if you thought that was the only thing going on at a Grand Prix.


Not everyone can win every round, and we have plenty of other events to keep people entertained. The first event out of the gates was termed "The [Rebound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebound) Tournament," because if you were ready for that one, you really didn't do well in the main event.


After opening what he considered the worst pool possible and dropping out of the main event, 2008 Grand Prix Auckland Champion Dominic Lo borrowed a [Bant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant) Aura deck from 2012 Grand Prix Auckland Top 8 competitor Robert Liu with 5 minutes to spare, and easily crushed everyone else in his way to win the tournament, winning himself free entry to the rest of the Public Events this weekend.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/01_R8_Lo.jpg) *2008 GP Auckland Champion Dom Lo has still got it. At least sometimes.* 

Round 8 Feature Match - Robert Jackway vs. Tomoharu Saito
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
Jackway led the action in the final round of Day 1 with a [Rakdos Shred-Freak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Shred-Freak) . Saito had a [Trestle Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trestle+Troll) to slow Jackway down, but a [Sewer Shambler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sewer+Shambler) looked keen to get around the Troll.


Saito added to his board with a [Korozda Monitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Monitor) . Jackway grew his army with an unleashed [Rakdos Cackler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) and a [Rakdos Keyrune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune) . Saito attacked with his Monitor.


The key play of the game came a cycle of attacks later. Saito tried an [Assassin's Strike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassin%27s+Strike) on Jackway's Shambler, but Jackway had a [Launch Party](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Launch+Party) in response to take out Saito's [Trestle Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trestle+Troll) . Jackway untapped, used an [Annihilating Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annihilating+Fire) to take out the Monitor, and swung with his team.


Saito cast a [Horncaller's Chant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horncaller%27s+Chant) , restocking his board with a pair of 4/4's. A [Rogue's Passage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue%27s+Passage) on Jackway's Cackler let him get past the obstinate Rhinoceri long enough for an [Explosive Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact) to finish the job.


Robert Jackway 1 – Tomoharu Saito 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/01_R8_Jackway.jpg) *Robert Jackway* 
Jackway led the action in the second game with a [Rakdos Shred-Freak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Shred-Freak) . This time Saito had a [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) , who decided to hang around on Saito's side of the board rather than getting too aggressive.


Jackway had a [Rakdos Keyrune](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune) , while Saito's [Corpsejack Menace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpsejack+Menace) was a little more impressive. Jackway attacked anyway, and after Saito's cautious block, a [Stab Wound](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stab+Wound) ed the Menace to death.


Saito attacked with his Mangler and summoned a [Golgari Longlegs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Longlegs) , while Jackway had a [Daggerdrome Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp) to try and keep pace on his side of the board. The Mangler took bite-sized chunks out of Jackway, who raced back through the skies with his Imp.


Jackway piled on the pressure with a [Grim Roustabout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Roustabout) and a [Cobblebrute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobblebrute) . Saito's Longlegs and Mangler swung in regardless, and after no tricks from either player, the Longlegs traded with Jackway's [Cobblebrute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobblebrute) . Post-combat, Saito had a [Zanikev Locust](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zanikev+Locust) to put Jackway's Imp out of action.



![Explosive Impact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Explosive+Impact)

Jackway passed without any action. Saito cautiously sent in his Locust, which Jackway took without demur. Post-combat, Saito summoned a [Korozda Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage) , and in response, Jackway killed the Locust with an [Explosive Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Impact) . Saito scavenged his Locust onto his Mangler.


On Jackway's turn, he attacked with his Imp and then summoned a [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) . Saito passed, and Jackway attacked again with his Imp, nibbling away at Saito's life total. A [Splatter Thug](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splatter+Thug) joined Jackway's team, while Saito bolstered his with a Courser's Accord.


Jackway took out Saito's Guildmage with a [Street Spasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Spasm) , ruefully noting he was one mana short of killing his entire team. Saito grinned and cast a [Trostani's Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment) on Jackway's [Grim Roustabout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Roustabout) , then attacked with his 6/6 [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) .


Jackway took the damage, but when Saito attacked with his entire army the next turn, he was put in a bad position. After blockers, Saito was left with his Mangler and a Centaur – and bolstered his ranks with a [Trestle Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trestle+Troll) and a [Korozda Monitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Monitor) . Jackway's army had been decimated to a Keyrune and a lonely [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) .


A [Cyclonic Rift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cyclonic+Rift) from Jackway temporarily cleared the board, but Saito rebuilt too quickly for Jackway to muster an effective defense.


Tomoharu Saito 1 – Robert Jackway 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsyd13/01_R8_Saito.jpg) *Tomoharu Saito* 
### Game 3


Jackway started the decider by summoning a [Frostburn Weird](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostburn+Weird) , who mauled Saito until a [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) convinced him not to. Stymied, Jackway summoned a [Daggerdrome Imp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daggerdrome+Imp) before passing back to Saito, who had his [Korozda Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korozda+Guildmage) . Jackway and Saito both played creatures and smashed them into each other – but Saito had more tricks, with a pair of [Trostani's Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%27s+Judgment) s and an [Eyes in the Skies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyes+in+the+Skies) giving him token advantage over Jackway's R/B creatures.


Jackway was forced onto the defensive by the *Judgment*s, and over several turns, Saito showed that Selesnya's solid beaters have the advantage over Rakdos' aggressive, but fragile, armies.


Tomoharu Saito 2 – Robert Jackway 1
