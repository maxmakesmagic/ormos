Rallius chose to include green in his deck for two cards: [Squall Line](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall+Line) and [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing). Are these cards worth a second color? Let's find out through testing!


**Game 1: Wilke (Mono-White Control)**


I drop double [Fungal Reaches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Reaches) and build up counters until turn seven, when I go [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), [Beacon of Destruction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Destruction) (met with [Seht's Tiger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seht%27s+Tiger)) and [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) (in response) in one turn. He drops [Calciderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm), and I play a second [Beacon of Destruction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Destruction) and swing with my eight 3/1 guys. He blocks two and takes 18—I held back [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) in the case of a second [Seht's Tiger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seht%27s+Tiger) or some combat trick, as my hand was [Squall Line](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall+Line) and [Sudden Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Impact). He plays [Stormfront Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stormfront+Riders) and has one [Intervention Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intervention+Pact), but that is not enough to suvive the next attack phase.


Record: 1-0

**Game 2: manleypointer (White Weenie)**
He gets three [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden)s and a [True Believer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer) on the board. I get [Ashling the Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashling+the+Pilgrim), and hold all of his guys at bay. He attempts to land a [Mirror Entity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity), which I promptly [Lash Out](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lash+Out). To his second Entity, I blow up Ashling. I follow up with a second Ashling and a [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), and he has only a [True Believer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=True+Believer) and a [Serra Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Avenger). I quickly grow my Ashling to monstrous proportions (by pumping it twice on my turn and twice on his), winning me the game in a couple of swings despite his high point of 32 life.


Record: 2-0


**Game 3: Erathmedor (Blue-Black Control)**


He plays [Stonybrook Angler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonybrook+Angler) and [Shadowmage Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator). I get down Ashling, and [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) his 1/3 guy, so he can't draw extra cards. He kills my Ashling with [Sudden Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death), and repeats this chain of events when I play a second Ashling. To turn around good faith, I [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) a second [Shadowmage Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator). I get [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) and double [Ghitu Encampment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Encampment) on the board, and start riding the extra upkeep mana (and draw phase mana—remember, mana from [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) can be used on the card you draw that turn!) to activate my Encampments, and play [Lash Out](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lash+Out) and [Beacon of Destruction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Destruction) all at once. He succumbs on my fourth upkeep turn, while I still have [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster) and [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) in hand.


Record: 3-0


**Game 4: PDT1060 ([Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim))**


He gets [Wistful Thinking](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wistful+Thinking) with a [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim) out, knocking double [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock), [Lash Out](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lash+Out), and Ashling from my hand. I do get double [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) and have [Ghitu Encampment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Encampment)s (double) and a second Ashling. He kills Ashling with [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) and drops [Magus of the Jar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magus+of+the+Jar). I kill Magus with an [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) and start swinging for 4 a turn, keeping my hand size at zero so I can't be forced to discard too many cards (unless he forces me to draw first). I am able to avoid mana burn by putting counters on [Fungal Reaches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Reaches) or activating [Ghitu Encampment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Encampment)s multiple times. He drops a second [Megrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Megrim), and I drop [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing). He answers with [Forced Fruition](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forced+Fruition), and I kill him with a [Squall Line](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall+Line) for 8 during my upkeep thanks to seven free mana from my double [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire).


Record: 4-0


**Game 5: IAMQBA (Blue-Black "Comes into Play" Creatures)**


He gets [Faceless Butcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faceless+Butcher) x2 on my Ashling, but I kill them both with burn. He then evokes [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), brings it back with [Makeshift Mannequin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mannequin), and drops [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats) (getting [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) out of my hand). I lose a [Ghitu Encampment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Encampment) to [Nameless Inversion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion) but get another two, plus a second [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) (which gets in for 6). He uses [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw) to kill my [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), and then throws it in front of an Encampment while at 9 life. On IAMQBA's next upkeep, I play [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate), which he lets through. Two of the three remaining cards in my hand were [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock), which finish him off.


Record: 5-0


Part of the original plan from Rallius was to use [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) to accelerate mana and to keep the hand size constantly filled. Unfortunately, I haven't really drawn any of the Rites yet. In order to test the deck in that direction, I decide to add in [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine)s—not because the deck has shown any great weakness yet, but because I want to see how well the deck performs with a larger component of "draw-lots-of-cards."


To make room for the [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine)s in the budget, I drop a pair of [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster)s and a [Beacon of Destruction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Destruction). I consider adding in another [Sudden Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Impact) or two, but first I want to see what happens when I can reliably draw a [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) / [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) every game.


**Rules Note**The untap, upkeep, and draw steps are all parts of the same phase, the beginning phase. This means that mana generated during upkeep sticks around until your draw, and doesn't clear (or mana burn you) until you end your draw step. This also means that you can pay the cumulative upkeep of [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) for the turn, see what card you draw, and then decide what to do with that mana.

**Game 6: shade256r (White Weenie)**


I drop [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) x2 and forget that I need to turn off his [Knight of the Holy Nimbus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Holy+Nimbus)'s regeneration before hitting it with [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock), leaving me down a [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) and him up a [Knight of the Holy Nimbus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Holy+Nimbus). This forces me to use a turn and another burn spell to kill it again, but I get run over by his other creatures backed by [Mirror Entity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) in the meanwhile.


Record: 5-1


**Game 7: Quillboy (Blue-Black Control "Comes into Play" Creatures)**


After a very long, long drawn out game, I get down [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) and four [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing). I eventually get into a place where I have triple Fungal Reach, I'm at 2, he's at 4, and I have double [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire), one at six and one at two. I play [Beacon of Destruction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Destruction) three times in one draw phase, each time drawing out [Venser, Shaper Savant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant) or a [Momentary Blink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) for Venser. He taps an [Adarkar Wastes](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes) to bring himself down to 3, and I then finish him off with [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) and [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster) for one (I didn't [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) Venser because once he brought himself down to 3, I was virtually assured the win with my dual split second cards).


Record: 6-1


**Game 8: Grendel Nine ([Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World))**


We have a turn where he gets a hasted [Hearthcage Giant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hearthcage+Giant) (with [Inner-Flame Acolyte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inner-Flame+Acolyte)) after a [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) and is able to pump it to 22 power, but forgets to attack (while I am at 20). However, I also had a burn spell I could have used to kill one of the other Elementals in response to him playing the Giant, allowing him to swing for a maximum of 19 that turn, and opening him for a lethal counterattack. Either way, I end up winning the match a couple of turns later, as I have [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) and a buttload of burn spells.


Record: 7-1

**Game 9: meowth (White-Blue Damage Prevention)**
My nightmare match-up: [Purity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purity), [Sacred Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Mesa), [Story Circle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Story+Circle) (set to red). I almost pull this one to a draw by using [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) on his [Martyr of Sands](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martyr+of+Sands) (all four of them), and then using burn to kill [Purity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purity) and [Squall Line](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall+Line) (backed by all four [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire)s) to force a draw, but I was short the [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) on the last turn to make it happen.


Record: 7-2


**Game 10: SpooJones (Black-Red Madness)**


I tap my mana wrong, and end up dying instead of burning him out. Total brain fart. I should have won this game, but I lost because of a [Fungal Reaches](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Reaches) when I had double [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate), double [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) in hand, with him at 10 life. I tapped [Ghitu Encampment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghitu+Encampment) and [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) for the first burn spell, leaving me only a Reaches (no counters) and a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest). I lose because of this mistake.


Record: 7-3


With the extra [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine)s, I posted a 2-3 record. I probably could have lost the [Warp World](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) match up, and definitely would have won the Black-Red Madness match-up if I hadn't tapped my mana wrong, so these two are a wash. The [Purity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purity) match is my nightmare match ([Purity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purity) tends to trump [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), and I have huge problems getting past [Story Circle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Story+Circle)). Even so, the [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) / [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) configuration wasn't working well. This wasn't necessarily because of the card-drawing spells, but because my deck wasn't designed to take advantage of drawing cards.


Sure, I have lots of burn spells that I can draw, but in order to keep up with the number of cards in hand (especially if I start drawing 4-5 cards a turn), I need to aim my burn at an opponent's head (or it's simply easier to do so) versus going with a [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) plan. The focus becomes more on cards like [Sudden Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Impact) and board sweepers, versus dropping a 6/6 creature, throwing a burn spell or two, and swinging with 30 power worth of hasted creatures.


I had two choices at this point—focus the deck more on a [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) / [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) strategy (more [Sudden Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Impact)s, more board sweepers, less focus on [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) and burn), or remove the mutual card-drawing spells and focus more on [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) and [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility). In the end, I felt that the deck was more interesting as a [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) deck—one that could generate huge mana during upkeep and go nuts with [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility)—than as a retread of an Owling Mine or Turbo-Fog deck with burn.


**Out:** 3 [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) (-7.5), 4 [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) (-1.32), 2 [Sudden Impact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Impact) (-0.5)


So now I was going to go the [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) route. As suggested by reader Jareth, [Upwelling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upwelling) is a natural choice to pair with the Braid—all mana generated by the Braid would carry over past my beginning phase, allowing me to use it towards sorceries and [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) itself.


The four [Upwelling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upwelling) were a pure swap for the four Rites, since they cost the same for this contest. I have a little leg room left to work with, so I add in three [Harmonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize) for the three [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) (draw cards just for myself!), leaving me two slots and about six and a fifth tickets left to work with. I decide to add in a couple of [Chandra Nalaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra+Nalaar) to fill in those slots. Chandra does not work well with [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) (she is not a spell once she is on the table as a planeswalker), but she fills the role of mass removal—her second ability lets her kill a creature a turn for the cost of only a single card (Chandra herself), and her third ability just wins the game. The first ability (a point of damage a turn) is just gravy if it gets going.


**In:** 4 [Upwelling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upwelling) (1.32), 2 [Chandra Nalaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra+Nalaar) (6), 3 [Harmonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize) (3)

He drops [Gauntlet of Power](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gauntlet+of+Power) and [Yavimaya Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Dryad). With all the mana I've saved up, I drop [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility), [Squall Line](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squall+Line) for 6, play [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock), and swing for 30 damage.


Record: 8-3


**Game 12: nickallmighty (Mono-Green Treefolk)**

**Game 15: Ferrethater (Four-Color Elementals)**


He gets [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider), Elemental Harbinger, [Changeling Berserker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Changeling+Berserker) (drawing out a [Sudden Shock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) from me to kill his Harbinger, so he has to champion the [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) to keep the Berserker, sticking him at three mana) and [Incandescent Soulstoke](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incandescent+Soulstoke). I get Ashling to chump block, and then wipe his team with [Molten Disaster](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Disaster) for 3 (killing the Soulstoke, which drops the [Berserk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Berserk)er to 5/3, killing it). I then use [Chandra Nalaar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra+Nalaar) to kill off his returned [Smokebraider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider). From there, I control the game with a combination of Chandra and burn until I finish him off with Chandra and double [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate) some turns down the road.


Record: 12-3


I can honestly say that I didn't expect this deck to go 10-0 (not counting the foray into a [Howling Mine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) / [Rites of Flourishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rites+of+Flourishing) deck), but as a strict [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) / [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) deck, I went unbeaten testing for this article. [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) proved to be immensely useful, and it made me wish that I could have included more storage lands in this deck (I couldn't because I didn't have them on the list of cards available for this pool—if it had, I would have swapped in three [Molten Slagheap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Slagheap)s for three [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse)s).


If I were to keep evolving this deck, I would want to find a way to fit in either [Char-Rumbler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Char-Rumbler) or [Flamekin Brawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamekin+Brawler). Both are effectively X spells on creatures, and take advantage of the mana this deck can pump out. Chandra worked well as an addition, allowing me time to stop smaller creatures while dropping [Hostility](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostility) and a host of 3/1 tokens. This deck was also a blast to play, and it made me wonder why [Braid of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Braid+of+Fire) has never seen serious tournament play—it can generate insane amounts of mana quickly, works extremely well with the *Time Spiral* storage lands, and reminds me of playing with [Aether Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Aether%2BVial) when that card was legal.
