An Enchanting Dream
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 22, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I recently received a very cool reader submission for Pauper, thanks to a gentleman named Sebastian. He suggested taking a look at an innovative White-Blue Enchantment list that has done well in a couple Daily Events, and I liked what I saw. In a sea of monocolored aggro decks, this deck is doing something very different.


![Dream Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dream+Stalker)
 By using [Dream Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Stalker), [Kor Skyfisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Skyfisher), [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer), and [Drake Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drake+Familiar), this deck takes advantage of numerous Auras with "enter (and leaves) the battlefield" abilities, triggering them multiple times. With fourteen ways to bounce permanents and a pair of Perilous Researches, this deck does not lack ways to interact with its combo pieces. 


 Those pieces are mostly enchantments, and a surprisingly large number of commons have triggers that can be abused. [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), [Faith's Fetters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters), [Hobble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hobble), and [Journey to Nowhere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Nowhere) all handle creatures, with [Spreading Seas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) available to keep up card flow and mess with the odd [Cloudpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost). The most interesting and potentially powerful card in the mix is [Reality Acid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Acid), which triggers when it leaves, and leave it shall. Unlike the format, it isn't uncommon or rare that the same [Reality Acid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reality+Acid) will eat five or six permanents in a single game, and once it's started hitting lands, the odds that your opponents can stop you from locking them out is pretty low. 


 This deck plays a good suite of creatures with ETB effects as well, with [Lone Missionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lone+Missionary)s to stall the game and [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)s to keep the cards coming. [Dream Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dream+Stalker) and [Kor Skyfisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Skyfisher) can pick up any permanent, and bouncing [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer) is a good way to get additional uses out of destroyed or discarded enchantments. 


Once again, I highly recommend Pauper, and decks like this give me hope that the metagame has a good amount of variety available.
