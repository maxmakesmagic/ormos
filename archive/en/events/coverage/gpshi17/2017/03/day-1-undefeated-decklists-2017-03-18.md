Day 1 Undefeated Decklists
==========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on March 19, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











Ten players escaped yesterday's carnage with unblemished 9-0 records.


Two of them played Mardu Ballista. Ryouhei Kirino's version was relatively standard, but Takanori Watanabe had a list with an interesting sideboard: thanks to [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind), [Painful Truths](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Painful+Truths), [Fumigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumigate), and [Sorin, Grim Nemesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sorin%2C+Grim+Nemesis), he could slide even further on the control scale.


Two players were on Green-Black Constrictor. One of them, Makoto Higuchi, had [Dampening Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dampening+Pulse) in his sideboard, which he could cast via [Aether Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Hub) or [Servant of the Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Servant+of+the+Conduit)—it's sweet tech against the Copy Cat combo. Takehiro Fujimoto's version, in contrast, was more aggressive with [Scrapheap Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger) and could curve [Catacomb Sifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Catacomb+Sifter) into [Skysovereign, Consul Flagship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skysovereign%2C+Consul+Flagship).


Two more players, Yuki Matsumoto and Yoshifumi Karatsu, had found their success with Four-color Saheeli. Their main decks were fairly standard—no delirium package—but Karatsu's sideboard had some gems, such as [Dampening Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dampening+Pulse) for the mirror match and [Woodland Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Wanderer) to reinforce the mid-range plan.


The next two players both registered four copies of [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel), threatening an [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) as early as turn four. Chuan Sun combined the Marvel plan with the Copy Cat combo in his four-color list, while Takehiko Matsumura emphasized [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) in his Temur version.


The final two undefeated players were Masayasu Tanahashi (Temur Tower) and Kazuki Yamada (Red-Green Energy aggro with [Invigorated Rampage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invigorated+Rampage)).


You can check out all of their decklists below.
