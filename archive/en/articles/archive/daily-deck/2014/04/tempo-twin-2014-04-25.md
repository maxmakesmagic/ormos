Tempo Twin
==========



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on April 25, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson











At this point, Tempo Twin (a [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo deck with a backup plan) is mostly a known quantity. While your opponent stockpiles removal in an attempt to stop your combo, you end up nickel and diming him or her out with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), [Pestermite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), and [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt). Oddly enough, it's very effective.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Young+Pyromancer)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer)
Some, like Patrick Dickmann, have fully embraced the tempo aspect and added things like [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). For whatever reason, no one (that I've seen) has tried [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) in that spot. Baba123's deck looks like a budget deck with its lack of [fetch lands](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&type=+%5B%22Land%22%5D&text=+%5Bsearch%5D+%5Byour%5D+%5Blibrary%5D&set=%7C%5B%22Onslaught%22%5D%7C%5B%22Mirage%22%5D%7C%5B%22Zendikar%22%5D), but restrictions often breed creativity.


[Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) kills most people just as well as [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) does, while not requiring an additional splash, so it's not all downside. Additionally, [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) encourages you to run a lot of cheap card-drawing spells, which also allows you to lower your overall land count. In the end, your deck ends up running like a well-oiled machine. I doubt there are many turns where you have unused mana.


At this point, I'd advise anyone playing [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) to have answers to their opponents' answers. Sometimes, it's as simple as fighting them with a card they didn't expect, such as [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer). If you're playing Dickmann's RUG Twin variant, you should at least give Baba123's version a chance.
