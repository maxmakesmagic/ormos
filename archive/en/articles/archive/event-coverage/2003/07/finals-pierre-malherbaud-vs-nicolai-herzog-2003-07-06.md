Finals: Pierre Malherbaud vs. Nicolai Herzog
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on July 6, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Craig-Jones-Author-Photo-150x150_0.jpg)
By Craig Jones











And finally it all comes down to one match. Out of just under 270 players the field has been whittled down to two highly experienced players. Both Pierre Malherbaud and Nicolai Herzog have been here before way back in 1999. That year Herzog became the eventual champion. This year the Norwegian is looking to repeat the feat while Malherbaud is looking to win the title for the first time.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/euro03/a858.jpg)"You've already won it once, you can let me win it this time." – Malherbaud.

"That's really funny." – Herzog.

Malherbaud is running a Reanimator deck while Herzog is running blue-green. Both decks look to make very powerful monsters very quickly either through madness or getting them out of the graveyard cheaply. Malherbaud's disruption comes in the form of attacking the hand with discard while Herzog will be looking to either counter the reanimation spells or [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) the creatures if the spells get through. Malherbaud is confident about the matchup despite Herzog being the cause of his only loss in the tournament so far.

### Game 1

Malherbaud won the die roll and went first, laying a swamp before passed turn.

"First game in this Top 8 where I do not do [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) turn 1." – Malherbaud.

Herzog madnessed out a Rootwalla with [Careful Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Study) and Malherbaud took the opportunity to [Entomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entomb) Akroma end of turn. Herzog summoned a [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) for support on turn two. Malherbaud was building up to something nasty though, a [Doomed Necromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Necromancer) appeared on the table.

Herzog Studied for an answer, netting another free Rootwalla and added an [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) to put pressure on the Frenchman.

![Akroma, Angel of Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Wrath)Sure enough Necromancer became Akroma, Angel of many abilities and she rushed in to smash Herzog for 6. The Norwegian hadn't found an [Unsummon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unsummon) so his only strategy was to try and swamp the first-striking, non-tap to attack monstrosity. A [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel) got slaughtered by the blue-haired killing machine, but its death allowed the others through for 7 damage.Malherbaud bashed back for another 6 and tried to bring out a second Necromancer. [Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic) prevented that. Herzog held back the [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) in his turn, fearing a trick, and sent in the Walla's. Another creature got smashed to the graveyard but now Malherbaud was down to 5 life. The Angel flew over again, knocking Herzog to two. 

Malherbaud was crying out for support. A second [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic) denied him the [Zombify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify) that would have dug out another blocker. Akroma bravely swatted another nuisance but the other two creatures swarmed past and did the Frenchman in. 

Herzog 1-0 Malherbaud

Herzog brought in the [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost)s, [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) and [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) while Malherbaud, whose sideboard consisted mainly of Wish targets, made some minor alterations and took out some of the less useful cards.

During the interval Herzog took the opportunity to poke fun at Malherbaud's accent.

### Game 2

Both players mulliganed and Herzog came out the winner as he dropped a [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost) on the table on turn two before the Frenchman had cast anything. This gave Malherbaud pause for thought in his turn, especially as his game plan had been to Rites away Herzog's hand. It wasn't pretty. Lose four cards, to take three cards off your opponent (Centaur, [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost), [Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic)), but give them two in the process. Malherbaud didn't have much choice, his hand wasn't doing a great deal else.

Herzog started beating with [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) then a Rootwalla while Malherbaud wished for [Buried Alive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive), then stocked his graveyard with [Arcanis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcanis), Akroma and [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger). Malherbaud attempted to [Stitch Together](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitch+Together) Akroma. That was [Counter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counter)ed. He tried again. Herzog [Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic)ed. The [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) and Rootwalla came in for lethal damage on the following turn.

Herzog was now one game away from taking the title for a second time.

Herzog 2-0 Malherbaud

### Game 3

No turn one [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) for Malherbaud again as he dropped a [Barren Moor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barren+Moor) and then Wished for [Zombify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify) on his next turn. Herzog tapped out for [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) and then had his hand shredded by a [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites) that took out every spell and left him with nothing but land. A Rootwalla appeared off the top, leaving the Frenchman clear to [Zombify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombify) out [Phantom Nishoba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nishoba).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/euro03/a851.jpg)Herzog drew a timely [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) for a [Doomed Necromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Necromancer), but it still wasn't good for him as the Nishoba continued to take the game away in massive life swings. An [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) got trodden over for a turn to keep Herzog alive, but the top of his library had no help forthcoming and the Frenchman got to pull one back.

Herzog 2-1 Malherbaud

"That was a fairly devastating [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites)" – Herzog.

### Game 4

Game 4 started very precariously for the Frenchman as he had to mulligan down to five. Herzog wasn't in any mood to take pity and sent a [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel) in, pitching an [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) for an extra point of damage and then bringing out a back up [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel).

Meanwhile Malherbaud Wished for a [Buried Alive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Buried+Alive) and stocked up Nishoba, Akroma and [Anger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anger).

Herzog had stopped laying land and was relying on the [Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mongrel)s. He swung again, pitched an [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) and made [Compost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compost), leaving 1 blue open.

Malherbaud didn't have a reanimate spell unfortunately. He had to Wish for it first. That lost him a turn and put him one turn away from death to the mongrels. He tried it as a last resort but, as expected, Herzog was holding a [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic).

Malherbaud scooped and Nicolai Herzog became European Champion for a second time.

**Final Result:** Nicolai Herzog beat Pierre Malherbaud 3-1 and is European Champion 2003!
