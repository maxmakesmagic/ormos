UR Delver at GP New Jersey
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 6, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?multiverseid=386705&type=card)When I started writing this column, I decided on some rules to follow, promises made to myself about what I would and wouldn't do. But, I have a confession to make—this week, I'm brushing up against one of them. It was a pretty simple rule, a rather natural one: not to showcase the same deck until a new set was released. Anyway, I got excited by something I came across and decided to make it a feature for this week; as I started writing, I felt a strong sense of déjà vu, and after I did a quick search I realized I had already written about it.


That said, to ignore this deck or try to work around it would be extremely inelegant and undeserving…I feel I have no choice but to bend my own rule and post this deck for the second time in two months.


What deck am I talking about? UR [Treasure Cruise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) burn. This deck combines hyper-aggressive creatures with burn and fetch lands, while using [Treasure Cruise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) to refill its hand. It is an unusual deck in that it is viable and being played in all Constructed formats—Legacy, Modern, Vintage, and Standard—simultaneously.
