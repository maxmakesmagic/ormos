Share Your Devotion
===================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 26, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar











My favorite creature type is Lord, hands down. [Lord of Atlantis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+Atlantis), [Lord of the Undead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Undead), [Goblin King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+King), [Elvish Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Champion) . . . if a Lord exists in a set, I inevitably track down four copies and build decks around them.



|  |
| --- |
| *Looks like a Zombie, looks like a Kobold, looks like an Elf... but no. What gives?* |

Until recently, my only complaint with the Lord creature type is that if a card is a Lord it doesn't use any other creature type. For example, [Kobold Overlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kobold+Overlord) isn't a Kobold. As a creature type, Lord is actually kind of lame on its own and too often counterintuitive. Elves don't have Elves as champions? Goblins don't choose a Goblin to be king? These inconsistencies irk me. Thankfully, *Onslaught* has officially fixed the Lord creature type. For example, [Voice of the Woods](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+the+Woods) is a [Lord and an Elf](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/use-your-outside-voices-please-2002-09-24). Logic gods rejoice.

My reasons for liking the Lord creature type so much are twofold. First, I'm a fan of any card that promotes creatures in **Magic**. Basically, I like games that are won and lost with creature battles.

Second, the Lord creature type significantly blurs the line between theme decks and Constructed decks. To use [Goblin King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+King) effectively, you need a lot of Goblins. In fact, you better have a darn good reason for putting a creature into your deck that *isn't* a Goblin. Once you have enough Goblins, cards like [Goblin Grenade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Grenade) become more attractive. Before you know it . . . WHAM! You have yourself a pretty decent Goblin theme deck that also happens to scare people at Friday Night **Magic**.

Recent sets have included a lot of cards that promote creature themes without explicitly being Lords. [Balthor the Stout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balthor+the+Stout) and his evil twin [Balthor the Defiled](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balthor+the+Defiled) are good examples. So are [Dwarven Bloodboiler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dwarven+Bloodboiler) and [Squirrel Mob](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Mob). In case you were wondering: Yes, I love all of these cards, too.

![Doubtless One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Doubtless+One)The *Onslaught* expansion has more cards that blur the theme deck - Constructed deck line than almost all the other sets *combined.* For instance, each color receives a very Lord-like Avatar that promotes creature themes. White's Avatar card in this cycle is called [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One). 

What do you notice about [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One)? Cool art by Justin Sweet to be sure. But it also promotes a deck using a lot of Clerics. It's a Cleric, so it won't die when it's on the board alone. It isn't a Legend, which means there is nothing preventing you from having four in a deck (or in play). It has a reasonable casting cost, which makes it easier to use it as the centerpiece of a deck. With only one ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) in the cost, it can easily fit into a multicolor deck. It can deal any damage to gain you life rather than just combat damage, so it combos well with cards like [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) and [Spirit Flare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+Flare). And, to top it off, it doesn't look to have any built-in drawbacks. These are all points in [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One)'s favor.

All of these factors, however, lead us to the following question: Are there enough Clerics to make [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One) worth it? 

The answer to this question begins with [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary). [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary), which is also white and a non-Legend, and [Doubtless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless) One could have been Siamese twins separated at birth. The only downside to using [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary) is that it ties any deck using [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One) to a heavy white mana base. With cards like [Rotlung Reanimator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Rotlung%2BReanimator)[rearing their heads](/en/articles/archive/die-and-be-reborn-2002-09-25), sometimes you don't want a Cleric deck so focused on white. Still, the synergy between the two cards is undeniable. So for now, I'll assume that a deck with [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One) also wants [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary).

In Standard, most Cleric decks I have seen use [Beloved Chaplain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain). [Beloved Chaplain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain) can hold off a mean creature like [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) or [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) forever while slipping through superior defenses once you feel safe.

Sadly, cards like [Firebolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt) and [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) can quickly get rid of [Beloved Chaplain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain). For this reason, I personally like the addition of [Devoted Caretaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Caretaker). A 1/2 for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) is a good deal, and as long as your opponent is relying on targeted creature removal, [Devoted Caretaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Caretaker) will shine. Another solid Cleric for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) is [Benevolent Bodyguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benevolent+Bodyguard). Early in the game, [Benevolent Bodyguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benevolent+Bodyguard) can deal a few points of damage. Later in the game, there are no better bodies to guard than [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One) and [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary). With [Master Apothecary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Apothecary), [Beloved Chaplain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beloved+Chaplain), [Devoted Caretaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devoted+Caretaker), and [Benevolent Bodyguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benevolent+Bodyguard), you should feel fairly safe against everything except mass removal like [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) or [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate). But really, shouldn't Clerics always fear the wrath of their gods? 



|  |
| --- |
| *With cards like these in play, you'll forget what damage feels like.* |

That makes twenty Standard-legal Clerics in a deck, which is more than enough to fuel [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One). The Clerics I've described so far, though, are pretty dinky on their own. I would also consider adding something like [Teroh's Faithful](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful) as a solid blocker with a big butt. In fact, since [Teroh's Faithful](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teroh%27s+Faithful) and [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One) combine to net you a horde of extra life, it might be worth dropping a copy or two of [Test of Endurance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Test+of+Endurance) into your Clerics deck as a backup win condition (especially considering the Cleric Lord in *Onslaught*). Either that, or find some other cool use for all that life.

Standard-legal sets are filled with Clerics. [Ancestor's Chosen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestor%27s+Chosen), [Dedicated Martyr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dedicated+Martyr), and [Venerable Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venerable+Monk) add more life gain. [Blessed Orator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blessed+Orator), [Hallowed Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Healer), [Master Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+Healer), [Militant Monk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Militant+Monk), [Pilgrim of Justice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim+of+Justice), [Pilgrim of Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim+of+Virtue), [Samite Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Samite+Healer), and [Shieldmage Advocate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shieldmage+Advocate) fit into more defensive decks. [Confessor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confessor), [Pulsemage Advocate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulsemage+Advocate), and [Selfless Exorcist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Exorcist) can combat threshold or graveyard-happy decks. Meanwhile, [Stern Judge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stern+Judge) is a good card to play against black decks, and [Nomad Mythmaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomad+Mythmaker) fits into an enchantment-heavy deck. These are some of the Clerics outside the *Onslaught* set. And if [Doubtless One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doubtless+One) is anything to go by, it's clear that more Clerics are on the way.

Are there enough Clerics to make an interesting deck? I think so. In Standard, I might try something along these lines (again, avoiding any other new Clerics from *Onslaught* for now):
