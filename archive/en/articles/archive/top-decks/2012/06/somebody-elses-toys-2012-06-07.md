Somebody Else's Toys
====================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 7, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







I don't know if you've noticed this...


but if you're a regular reader of this column and stuff...


there's this card...


you might have noticed (not to be presumptuous or anything)...


there's this card that is pretty good in, you know, *formats—*Standard... Modern... (probably Extended)... and absolutely, *positively* Legacy—that we talk about... pretty much every time.


Delver


of


Secrets


You might have noticed that we rave about [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), well, more or less constantly on this column. I have called it the best card in Standard, and probably more than once. It's kind of unavoidable, seeing as it wins as a Legacy Burn deck (Blue-Red Delver), mid-range companion to [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) (RUG Delver), various tempo and aggro-control Modern decks (say with, with-with, and without [Isochron Scepter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) and even [Bloodchief Ascension](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodchief+Ascension)), and at least four distinct decks across at least three color combinations in Standard. We have classic White-Blue Delver with [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) and [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681), a heftier White-Blue Delver with Swords and now [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s, Esper Spirits where [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is joined by cards like [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) (sometimes with [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) and other times [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue)), and most recently Blue-Red Delver with [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)—fifteen one-ofs in the side and circa three [Sulfur Falls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Falls).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td198_elesh.jpg)[Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite) | Art by [Igor Kieryluk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Igor+Kieryluk%22%5D)


[Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) is the kind of card that is hard to hate, though, regardless of how annoyingly effective it can be. Some grumps a year ago might have complained about [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) being rare, and [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) being mythic rare, but what are you really going to say about today's masters? Are [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag), and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) really so hard to come by?


Blah BLAH *BLAH*.


We're starting on card previews soon so I said to myself, "Self, why don't we take a break from [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), for at least a week? Why not check out somebody else's toys?"


So that's what we're going to do today.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td198_star.jpg)  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td198_tcg.jpg)Interesting similarities between these two events. Half of each were composed of White-Blue Delver decks *that we aren't going to discuss at all*, but both events were won by Solar Flare. *Solar Flare!* Let's check out this recently super-successful alternative to He Who Shall Not Be Named.

The strategies of these two decks, despite their particular individual decisions, are substantially similar.


Both Solar Flare decks are white-blue-black board control decks that top up [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite).


Belfatto played a light counterspell suite of two [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)s, one [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate), and a solo [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate). Mullin played permission (three [Negate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate)s) only in his sideboard.

... but their core strategies both involved a little [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) action to set up fat men like [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) and [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite). Both decks used [Forbidden Alchemy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) to set up the thin-but-mighty [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and the four-of flashback (and A+ defensive spell) [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls).


What is important to note is how these decks can defend themselves early, in particular from aggressive and high-quality drops. Both Michael and Zack played [Go for the Throat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat), [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), and [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb) in various numbers. Fast removal spells are vital to a deck like Solar Flare, as they allow the deck to survive long enough against beatdown to get to the late-game lockdown position.


Now, speaking of which, these decks are built with powerful synergies, in particular exploiting [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan). Michael supplemented his removal with a solo [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight). [Dead Weight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) is of course essentially the modern era's answer to [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure)... and a [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) that can come back, turn after turn, via [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) to kill one small creature per turn. Many of the cards in the Solar Flare archetype have the same incentives... Zack played main-deck [Nihil Spellbomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihil+Spellbomb), and both had access to [Evolving Wilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolving+Wilds).

It looks a lot like a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) build! One [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) as a chain-up, one [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime), one [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts), and so on. But it is more a Red-Green Aggro deck adding white than a Pod deck sans Pods.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td198_thalia.jpg)[Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) | Art by [Jana Schirmer & Johannes Voss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Schirmer%22%5D+%5B%22Voss%22%5D)


Evan could get a quick aggro start with [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben). Thalia, of course, puts a hefty 100% tax on players digging for lands with [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) or [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) (lands, we should make mention, that they need to operate while Thalia is directing the battlefield); otherwise game-breaking cards like [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) start looking much more fair. Against a control opponent, Thalia undoes the play when it comes to dropping [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and is equally annoying on the [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) front... a 2-power drop on turn two, Thalia makes for quite a great start to a long line of effective offensive creatures.

**[Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned):** As fellow Naya enthusiast [Joshua Cho said in his Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored* Top 8 interview](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/video-deck-tech-naya-joshua-cho-2012-05-12-0), you can't miracle it to ruin your opponent if you don't have it in your deck. Wagstaff had this bomb in his sideboard, given the less mono-creatures outlook of Standard rather than Block.


**[Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger):** Primo fixer for a three-color creature deck and source of self-contained card advantage. [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) fills up the three nicely and can hit on two with the help of a first-turn [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212).

The major arcana of Blue-Black Zombies remains unchanged... [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) is the ace, and many of the most impressive sequences include utilizing [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) with [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) to burn the opponent out, or the blue-splashed [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) to double up the Messengers... (or both). [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) is a great mondo-combo with [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) because undying undoes the core vulnerability of the Image (dies to anything), and not only gives you the option of a larger re-bought (and burning) body, but the opportunity to switch it up if you wish. Like... 3/2 on the way down, why not a 7/7 [Explosive Vegetation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explosive+Vegetation) on the way back up?


Like many of our successful finishers, Michael utilized *Avacyn Restored*'s expanded toolset to make even more impressive the already-viable Zombies strategy.



![Blood Artist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blood+Artist)

 

Central to these is two-drop [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) obviously has a natural synergy with morbid- and undying-enablers like [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod), but in this deck it can make for a two-card [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) + [Fireball](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fireball) mondo-combo via [Killing Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Killing+Wave).


Kill... you?



![Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cavern+of+Souls)

 

The most controversial—probably best—card to come out of *Avacyn Restored* does some obviously good work in this list. One of the limitations of earlier Blue-Black Zombies lists was [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island). As in, "we have to play some." These days, between [Darkslick Shores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkslick+Shores), [Drowned Catacomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowned+Catacomb), and now [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls), the deck has a mana base friendly enough to drop [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) consistently... without compromising on the ability to hit [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger).


[Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), sure... but not an [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) in sight!

It's a Zombie deck. It can curve out and do aggressive Zombie-ish things.


It has some castable red cards.


Let's forget about that for a moment and focus on the [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)s.


[Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)s!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td198_grave.jpg)[Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) | Art by [Steven Belledin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Steven+Belledin%22%5D)


Let's imagine for a moment we have [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) in play and just examine the synergies. (Because the non-Pod games can easily play like a "regular" aggressive Zombies sort, although the one-of [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat)—especially with some of the Undying and [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) re-buys—can prove unusually and above-and-beyond brutal, given sufficient land.)


**[Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler):** This one-drop gives you a potentially never-ending stream of re-buys and two-drops with [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). The go-to two-drop will of course be...



![Gravecrawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gravecrawler)

 

**[Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist):** [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) has a quite obvious synergy with [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). Every time you sacrifice a creature you give the [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) a little more, you know, paint. Lethal, lethal paint. You can chain down in this deck by naturally re-buying [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), just to grab another [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). That will double up the efficacy of future [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) sacrifices. Or chain up, per normal, for a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck.


At the three, you have the option to go for [Manic Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal) or just do the obvious thing and get the Zombies ace of [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger).


[Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) has quite an obvious synergy with [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod). Like [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) in the more traditionally colored builds, it is the body that gives and gives again.

How about a [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph)? Three-to-four... back to three again? You can murder your [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) (triggering its undying) and then go and get a Metamorph to copy your pseudo-fresh (but nevertheless rotting [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger)). All the while, of course, you should be exploiting [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). Or, the aforementioned [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat)! She has haste, of course! So you can be going above and beyond—literally above in this case—at which point you can sacrifice your squad to her to make her bigger and bigger, probably triggering undying and [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) along the way. You might just kill the opponent with this.
