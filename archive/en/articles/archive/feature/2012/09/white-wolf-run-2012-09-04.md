The White Wolf-(run)
====================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 4, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











With the recent release of ***Magic** 2013*, Standard has seen a few key shifts in the metagame. Specifically, Wolf Run decks turned to blue for the power of [Temporal Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Mastery) and [Frost Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan). Traditional Wolf Run in its red-green shell still exists, of course, but outside of that, most other Wolf Run variants have lost popularity or faded out completely.

![Sigarda, Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons)Last weekend in Denver, though, Campbell rolled out a Wolf Run variant that not only contained no blue mana, but also had no red spells! Donald elected to try out a green-white version of the popular archetype with only a hint of red for the namesake land and a few sideboard all-stars in [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge).

By shifting the deck to white, Donald gained access to a ton of powerful effects. [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) is no stranger to success, of course, and it provides the deck with a catch-all sweeper that [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) nor [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) can't claim. [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) and [Sigarda, Host of Herons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigarda%2C+Host+of+Herons) both provide resilient threats in the five-drop slot to complement [Thragtusk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) nicely. White then allows the deck to add [Elesh Norn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn) to its host of threats, providing additional board control as well. It looks like now is the time to experiment with those [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)s before they rotate. I know I haven't searched up a [Slayers' Stronghold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slayers%27+Stronghold) with it yet...
