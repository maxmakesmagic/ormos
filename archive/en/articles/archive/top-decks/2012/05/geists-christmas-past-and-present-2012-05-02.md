The Geists of Christmas Past and Present
========================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on May 3, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











Up soon is [Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/event/protour/avacynrestored12), coming to you from beautiful Barcelona, Spain.

  
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td193_logo.jpg)*[Pro Tour Avacyn Restored](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtg/event/protour/avacynrestored12)*

Now, the Constructed portion of Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored* is *Innistrad* Block Constructed, a format that has graced these (virtual) pages not at all. As such, in prep for the upcoming sure-to-be-Earth-shattering professional event, crack editor Trick Jarrett asked yours truly to devote a couple of episodes of Top Decks to *Innistrad* Block, so as to cozy ye olde audience into Barcelona's heretofore unfamiliar waters.

For this week's article, I ask you to consider these three points to begin with:


> * This is about the Geists of Christmas past and present... not future. We are going to look at the Innistrad Block format from the standpoint of where the format has been so far... kind of where we are coming from.
> * [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) is banned in Innistrad Block Constructed.
> * So is [Intangible Virtue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intangible+Virtue).
>

The advantage of the red-green deck over other beatdown decks in the format is, of course, in the area of mana acceleration. The deck has [Scorned Villager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorned+Villager), which allows it to move from two to four on turn three; lots of the best cards are four mana ones, like [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless), [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider), and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td193_sco.jpg)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/sun_arttransformbutton_active.png)![](https://media.wizards.com/legacy/mtg/images/daily/ftl/moon_arttransformbutton_static.png)[Scorned Villager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorned+Villager) and [Moonscarred Werewolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonscarred+Werewolf) | Art by [Cynthia Sheppard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;artist=+%5B%22Cynthia+Sheppard%22%5D)

[Dawntreader Elk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawntreader+Elk) doesn't precisely jump you to four *quickly*, but its ability to absorb an attack while moving up your position on the battlefield is nothing to sneeze at... especially when so many of the relevant decks are also aggressive.

I think it is important to understand the capacity of some of these red-white decks if you want to familiarize yourself with *Innistrad* Block. [Rally the Peasants](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Peasants) can set up a massive amount of damage, kind of like a mini-[Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun) (or "more than [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun)" with one more mana)... all at instant speed.

Not all the red-white decks have [Rally the Peasants](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Peasants), but you should probably know it *might* be there, as it is the kind of card that can unexpectedly win games when it catches you napping.

Plus, red-white in general seems to be one of the most popular decks in Top 8s. If it were me? I'd have solid plans whether or not my opponent is threatening the off-color [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun).

Most of the decks we have looked at so far can be categorized as some kind of beatdown with some burn spells thrown in to either get blockers out of the way or to finish games. The rest of these decks represent a spectrum including midrange control decks, heavy removal decks, and self-mill/fatty builds.

Decks like these are about what a control deck looks like for *Innistrad* block. RUG has [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) for permission... and expands out in several different directions to show off various different control capabilities.

[Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and [Desperate Ravings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) are the card advantage and card drawing, [Garruk Relentless](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) is the powerhouse, and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) even provides a bit of life gain. Because the deck has some RG to go with its U, [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) is even there to help close out games!
