After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [*Modern Masters 2017* Art Review](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/mikelinnemann-03082017-modern-masters-2017-art-review/) | Gathering Magic | Mike Linnemann

Linnemann goes deep on the art for *Modern Masters 2017 Edition*. The article is long, but well worth it if you're an art fan and just want to bask in all the new art.


1. [Commander VS: Odds and Evens](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/34680_Commander-VS-Odds-and-Evens.html) | StarCityGames.com | Justin Parnell and Stephen Green

It's [Surrak Dragonclaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surrak+Dragonclaw) vs. [Rhys the Redeemed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhys+the+Redeemed) vs. [Volrath the Fallen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath+the+Fallen) vs. [Sygg, River Cutthroat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sygg%2C+River+Cutthroat) in this week's edition of Commander VS.


1. [The Creative Process with Rob Prior (Live Painting!)](https://collected.company/2017/03/07/creative-process-w-rob-prior-live-painting-149/) | The Command Zone | Jimmy Wong and Josh Lee Kwai

This is a very different kind of Command Zone podcast, as the pair gets creative with Rob Prior. More art and creativity than Commander, this podcast is still worth a listen/watch if that's your jam.


1. [Brewed Force: Colorless Eldrazi](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/brewed-force-colorless-eldrazi/) | ChannelFireball | Neal Oliver

Remember Eldrazi? Remember how powerful they are? Remember that they're still Standard-legal? Neal Oliver remembers the titans and brews up a powerful colorless Eldrazi ramp deck looking to annihilate opponents.


1. [Jiachen Tao Shares Platinum Pro Tips](https://kitchentablemagic.org/2017/03/07/jiachen-tao/) | Kitchen Table Magic | Sam Tang

It's safe to say you've moved past the kitchen table when you're talking to a Platinum pro and Pro Tour winner. Tao shares what it's like to play on the big stage and what it takes to get to that level. And also a delicious recipe for chicken soup. Maybe. That last part might not be correct.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Weldfast Black-Red](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2017-03-07)

*Magic Online* player BOB-ICHI recently went 5-0 with a black-red artifact aggro deck that has its roots in [Pro Tour *Aether Revolt*](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptaer), where a few players played similar decks.


Much of the deck has all the aggressive components we've come to expect from Mardu Vehicles—[Heart of Kiran](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran), [Scrapheap Scrounger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger), and [Unlicensed Disintegration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unlicensed+Disintegration). But the deck ducks white cards in favor of an artifact-heavy theme that lets it take advantage of [Weldfast Engineer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weldfast+Engineer) and [Forerunner of Slaughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forerunner+of+Slaughter), making its [Walking Ballista](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista)s, Thopters, [Heart of Kiran](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran)s, and [Scrapheap Scrounger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger)s all the more deadly.


It's fast, it's aggressive, and it's got consistent mana, and it certainly deserves a look for any aggressive-minded players.
