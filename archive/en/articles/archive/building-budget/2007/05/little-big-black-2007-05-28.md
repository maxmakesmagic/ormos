If you would like to help me playtest, I am taking volunteers to spend a couple of hours playing ten straight games of Magic (one against each of the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks) using **Magic Online**! Here's my requirements:


* You must have one of the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks built, unchanged, as per the version at the end of an article. Sorry, you have to provide your own cards for this one!

This ended up being a really efficient deck, with many built-in synergies. The most among them—the interaction between [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) and [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor). With the two of them working in tandem, [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) could give any creature -2/-2 for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)—sacrifice the [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor), then sacrifice another creature to bring back the [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor). In this way, you ended up only losing one guy, but giving an opposing creature the ol' [Enfeeblement](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enfeeblement) treatment.


Even without the Rusalka/Traitor interaction, the deck is set up for some quick beats. Many of the creatures have evasion ([Dauthi Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauthi+Slayer), [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor), [Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader)), and all of the creatures are fast, efficient weenies. There is one problem for building this deck on a budget:


This deck ain't budget.


[![Bad_Moon](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/2e/Bad_Moon.jpg)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Bad%2BMoon)Here's a breakdown of the prices for each of the cards in the deck, using **Magic Online**'s seller room as a gauge:


[Bad Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon): 1.5 each  
[Dauthi Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauthi+Slayer): 1 each  
[Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor): 1 each  
[Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena): 4 each  
[Snow-Covered Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Swamp): 3 for 1 ticket  
[Soul Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Spike): 2 for 1 ticket  
[Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader): 2 for 1 ticket  
[Withered Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch): 2 for 1 ticket


Cost: 6 ([Bad Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon)) + 4 ([Dauthi Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauthi+Slayer)) + 4 ([Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor)) + 16 ([Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena)) + 7.66 ([Snow-Covered Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Swamp)) + 2 ([Soul Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Spike)) + 2 ([Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader)) + 2 ([Withered Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch)) = 43.66 tickets


Given that our budget for this column is a strict high of 30 tickets, this just won't do. In order to make this deck fit our budget, some cuts are going to have to be made.


The first cut is to remove the [Snow-Covered Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Swamp)s. They are there solely to support [Rimebound Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rimebound+Dead), and seven and two-thirds tickets is a lot of money to pay to support a one/one regenerating common creature. The [Snow-Covered Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Swamp)s are easily replaced by regular [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s, and so the [Rimebound Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rimebound+Dead)/[Snow-Covered Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Swamp)s are out.


Out: 23 [Snow-Covered Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Swamp) (-7.66 tickets), 4 [Rimebound Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rimebound+Dead) (36 tickets total)  

In: 23 [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)


This still puts us above budget. There are two distinct ways to go with this deck, at this point:


 


1. There are a lot of cards that cost 1-1.5 tickets each, such as [Bad Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon), or [Dauthi Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauthi+Slayer). If I start yanking out playsets of those, I can replace them with commons/uncommons that aren't necessarily as good but that can fill the role of attackers.
2. I can take out the [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena)s and come in well under budget.

This is a hard decision, because [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) is a really powerful card—the ability to draw twice as many cards as your opponent equals more opportunities to kill them early-on in the game, since Little Big Black is the deck serving up all the threats. Likely your opponent is going to be scrambling to play catch-up with you, so the loss of a little life in exchange for the drawing of a lot of cards is a small trade-off.


In the end, I decided that the [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena)s should go. While I would love to have them in the deck, I can't justify gutting the creature base of the deck for a card that costs a little over half of the budget of the entire deck. I also took out [Phyrexian Etchings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Etchings), since it is kind of slow and cumbersome.


![Phyrexian Arena and a big red X](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/building/bb113_ArenaX.jpg)In exchange for the Arenas coming out, I decided to try a bunch of higher-end cards that would have a game-swinging effect. If I couldn't overload in the beginning with weenies, I would drop my bombs at the end of the game and try to outright win with one or two different cards. My choices for these slots were all cards that I'd just wanted to play around with:


[Roiling Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roiling+Horror): After my success with [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth) and [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) during the 10 Decks in 10 Weeks project, I was itching to get my hands on this guy. Best suited for a mono-black deck such as this one due to a triple-black suspend cost, I figured that [Roiling Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roiling+Horror) would make a great top-of-the-curve creature. If I'm ahead by 5 life, [Roiling Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roiling+Horror) comes down as a 5/5 haste creature that turns into 10/10 almost immediately. In addition, I can suspend him to drain those last few points of life if I hit a creature stalemate situation.


[Enslave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enslave): It's [Confiscate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confiscate) for black, except better and worse. Worse, in that it can only grab creatures. Better, in that the point of damage it does is to the *creature's owner.* Yes, I misread it the first time I had it played on me as well—I thought that [Enslave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enslave) did a point to me. This is not the case—[Enslave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enslave) does a point to whoever physically (or electronically) owns the card, so it adds, as they say, the insult to the injury.


[Twisted Abomination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Abomination): A common out of Scourge—pick up that version rather than the "timeshifted" *Time Spiral* version. Good both for fetching lands in the early game and for coming down on turn seven (don't play it unless you have regeneration mana up!) as a huge regenerating beater in the late game.


Out: 4 [Phyrexian Arena](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) (-16 tickets), 1 [Phyrexian Etchings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Etchings) (-.5 tickets) — (19.5 tickets total)  

In: 4 [Roiling Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roiling+Horror) (.5 tickets), 3 [Enslave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enslave), 2 [Twisted Abomination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Abomination) — (21.5 tickets total)

Game 1: Rupes (White Weenie)  

I get turn-one [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) and turn-two [Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader). The Crusader ends up going all the way. I do get [Plagued Rusalka](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plagued+Rusalka) and [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) to kill a [Knight of the Holy Nimbus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Holy+Nimbus), to make sure I can keep racing against a [Serra Avenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serra+Avenger) and [Tivadar of Thorn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tivadar+of+Thorn).  

Record: 1-0


Game 2: Vicious Beastie (Mono-Black Control)  

Vicious Beastie gets [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) (hitting nothing) back-to-back with [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) (getting my board) and then drops double [Skeletal Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Vampire) for the win.  

Record: 1-1


Game 3: NirokSyn (Black/White Weenie)  

He gets [Garza's Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garza%27s+Assassin), and I get [Withered Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch). He drops [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) and puts [Loxodon Warhammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) on it, and I drop [Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader) and take his [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) with [Enslave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enslave). He drops [Rathi Trapper](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rathi+Trapper) and moves the Warhammer onto it, but I drop [Twisted Abomination](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twisted+Abomination), and then [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) and double [Bad Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon) for the win.  

Record: 2-1

I get double [Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader), [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor), and [Bad Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon). He gets double [Soul Warden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Warden), [Stonecloaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonecloaker), [Jötun Grunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=J%C3%B6tun+Grunt). I run him over with pro-white and shadow guys and finish it with a [Soul Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Spike).  

Record: 3-1


Game 5: varianalos (Black/Red Tokens)  

I get a great draw:  

Turn two: [Dauthi Slayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauthi+Slayer)  

Turn three: [Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader) (he drops [Kher Keep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kher+Keep)) (18)  

Turn four: [Bad Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bad+Moon) (12) (he drops [Sengir Autocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Autocrat))  

Turn six: [Nether Traitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nether+Traitor) (4), [Soul Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Spike), win.  

Record: 4-1


So far, so good. There were definitely weaknesses in the deck. For one, I have no way to affect my opponent's hand. This allows my opponent to hoard removal spells or late-game bombs, making my race an uphill battle. Since black is the color of discard spells, this is easily solved. In the end, from a list of [Blackmail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blackmail), [Coercion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coercion), [Delirium Skeins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins), [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void), [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) and [Stupor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupor), I end up choosing [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void) as my discard spell of choice. The reason for this is simple—I want to be able to hit, with 100% accuracy, the exact spell I want out of an opponent's hand. I don't care if I get [Skeletal Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Vampire) and a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) with [Stupor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupor) when it's that [Damnation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) I need right here and now. The same goes for [Blackmail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blackmail) and [Delirium Skeins](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins). [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) is too expensive (ticket price) to fit this deck.



![Nightmare Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nightmare+Void)

[Coercion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coercion) is a real possibility over [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void)—even though I can recur [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void) repeatedly, I lose a race to [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) if I'm on the draw. [Coercion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coercion) lets me take out that Wrath on my turn three, before my opponent can untap and Wrath. Still, I decide to give [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void) a shot, and see how it works.

I also have found that [Enslave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enslave) hasn't been a great addition to the deck. Sure, I can grab a creature now and then, but if my opponent doesn't have something large on the board, it's a huge wasted slot that could be another fatty. In addition, [Roiling Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roiling+Horror) hasn't worked out quite like I'd hoped. It seems better suited for a deck with a lot more [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit)/[Drain Life](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drain+Life) effects, as a lot of my games have come down to races.


In the end, I take out two [Enslave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enslave)s (leaving one in the deck for fun), a [Roiling Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roiling+Horror), and a [Soul Spike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Spike), and add in three [Nightmare Void](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void) and a [Helldozer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helldozer). [Helldozer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helldozer) is a huge, massive beast who can both swing for a lot of damage and set my opponent back from playing their huge bombs.

This deck ended up being pretty strong, and one of the best things is how customizable it can be at the top end. Don't want to run [Roiling Horror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roiling+Horror)? Add in more [Helldozer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helldozer)s—or maybe [Korlash, Heir to Blackblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Korlash%2C+Heir+to+Blackblade) (once *Future Sight* is legal) or [Mortivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortivore) or [Liege of the Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liege+of+the+Pit). The MVP for this deck was definitely [Stromgald Crusader](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromgald+Crusader). Left unchecked, the damage output on this jump knight is just insane, and he shut down virtually every white deck I faced.
