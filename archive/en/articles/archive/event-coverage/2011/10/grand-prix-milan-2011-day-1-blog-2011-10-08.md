Saturday, 11:03 a.m.: Statistics from the GPT Winning Deck Lists
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 

Yesterday saw about twenty Grand Prix Trials, the last chance for players to earn three byes for today's main event. The decks whose players actually finished in first place at one of those tournaments and walked away with the coveted prize – what did they have in common? Can you guess what the most-played card among those decks was?



![Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brimstone+Volley)
![Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bonds+of+Faith)

It's probably a removal spell, right? Something like [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) or [Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonds+of+Faith). In fact, both of these were among the most-played cards in the winning deck lists, but they only shared fourth place with [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto) and [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice).



![Dead Weight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dead+Weight)
![Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elder+Cathar)

There were even more copies of [Dead Weight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) and [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar), tied for third place. [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) and, interestingly, [Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuke) put up higher numbers still. But by far the most-played card among the winning decks, even more than the seemingly ubiquitous [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch), was [Chapel Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chapel+Geist). Surprised? So was I. Apparently, white-based aggressive decks were out in full force yesterday. It will be interesting to see whether this trend continues today.



![Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Forbidden+Alchemy)
![Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Unburial+Rites)

Apropos, the color combination that put most players in first place was red-white. Close behind was some combination of Esper (white-blue-black), largely white-blue splashing black for the flashback cost of [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) or blue-black splashing for the flashback cost of [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), although white and black removal also made a splash appearance. Straight white-blue came in third, but after that there was a variety of color combinations.



![Instigator Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Instigator+Gang)
![Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Devil%27s+Play)

Bombs did play a role, albeit a rather small one. The winning decks included: [Instigator Gang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Instigator+Gang) and [Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play), tied for most-played rare, followed by [Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk). [Angelic Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Overseer) was the most-played mythic rare.



![Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Geist-Honored+Monk)
![Angelic Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Angelic+Overseer)

So, in conclusion, red and especially white started the weekend as the clear frontrunners, with blue and black trailing close behind. Now, we're off to see how conclusive that conclusion actually is.




Saturday, 12:23 p.m.: Saturday Morning Swap Shop
------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 

Limited GPs are always kind of fun, and all the more so when a set is still quite new, as there are still all sorts of card interactions to get used to. I'm sure there are plenty of players that are here this weekend who are as excited about the ability to trade for a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), or the last card they need for their Commander deck. There is one kind of swap though, that only happens once on the weekend.


At the very start of the day, each player registers one deck (notes down all the cards in the card pool), and then it gets passed on to another player to play with. That way the judges can be sure that everyone is working with random cards from boosters, and nobody is sneakily cheatily playing with cards they brought themselves.


The moment where *that* swap happens is kind of fun, as every arm in the room raises in some magical Mexican wave, as if the head judge had just asked everyone if they wanted to win here in Milan.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/featureSwapShop.jpg)
*Put your left hand in, your left hand out, do the hokey pokey and turn yourself around...*



Saturday, 12:44 p.m.: Sealed Deck Building with a Mystery Pro
-------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 

It's back to Sealed Deck Building with the Mystery Pro. We'll give you all the details of his Sealed pool and his deck building process, as well as show you his final deck. But we won't tell you his name, as to not harm his chances in the competition. Let's go ahead then, shall we?


While sorting his cards, was there a twitch on his face when he came across [Mentor of the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek)? Why, yes I think there was. He began by laying out some green cards, looked unhappy with the result and quickly put them away. Next, he went through his artifacts, which yielded one [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut).


A quick sift through red revealed [Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play) as well as a couple of [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils), [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre), and [Scourge of Geier Reach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+of+Geier+Reach). White showed promise with [Elite Inquisitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Inquisitor), [Mentor of the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek), and two copies of [Avacynian Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacynian+Priest). The mystery pro nodded his approval. He added the white cards to his artifact creature, sorted by mana cost.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/featureMysteryPro.jpg)
*Our featured mystery pro lays out his cards and makes the hard choices...*

He laid out black, blue, and red in a similar fashion. Black didn't offer much, but it did include the flashback of two [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy), and the possibility to play [Grimgrin, Corpse-Born](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimgrin%2C+Corpse-Born). Blue had [Silent Departure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Departure), [Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms), and [Claustrophobia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claustrophobia) among other things, but was dangerously light on creatures. Red helped with that, with a total of eight creatures, and even a [Rally the Peasants](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Peasants) for additional offense. He was not satisfied with white-red, though, and tried to make a blue-white deck with a splash of red for [Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play), [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre), and the two [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils). Would his one [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto) be enough to support red as well as the flashback cost of [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy)? He went back to blue-white with black for Grimgrin, [Dead Weight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight), [Tribute to Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribute+to+Hunger), and the aforementioned flashback costs.


While counting creatures, and working on his mana, he constantly muttered under his breath. His brow furrowed and he sighed repeatedly. This pool, clearly, was no easy task to build correctly. All of it was only further complicated by [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) and [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard), which would certainly put even more strain on a shaky mana base. He removed the black splash and went back to red, then changed his mind and took another look at black. Rinse and repeat. He even tried a four-color version with two splashes, going for a maximum of power. He went through the pile he had previously set aside and got all of his red cards out, then put them away again.


He pondered the four-color option, took out [Spectral Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Rider), and put it back in. Finally, he settled on blue-white with a splash of back. The last two cards he cut from his deck were [Silverchase Fox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverchase+Fox) and [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler), despite possible interactions with [Mentor of the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mentor+of+the+Meek) and [Grimgrin, Corpse-Born](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grimgrin%2C+Corpse-Born). He started to register his deck ...


Then, with only three minutes left on the clock, he frantically went through his pile of cards one last time, and decided to go the four-color route after all! Just as the clock ticked down to zero he managed to hand in his deck list. A real thriller from start to finish, it's not often that deck building is this close and this exciting. His comment: "That was way too complicated. I'm not at all sure, whether this is correct. Possibly not."

Saturday, 1:00 p.m. - Zombie Machinations
-----------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 

With a whole new sealed deck format, it would just be wrong for us not to show you a deck build or two. However, it would be just as wrong for us to give you a complete decklist for a player on the internet, only to then have them lose to players with internet access and the wherewithal to use it. As such, I sat down with Zombie, the pseudonym of a mystery pro player who for now shall remain nameless.


"I asked the guy on my left if his deck was any good... he said not really. This could get a little ugly"



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/Building%20with%20Zombie.jpg)
*"A little ugly," indeed.*

"That's a good start" A [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) stared Zombie in the face. He was disappointed to find though, that he didn't have quite the suite of white cards to go with it.


The first run through for Zombie was all about stripping out the cards that were definitely not going to get played. This included [Bump in the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bump+in+the+Night), [Sensory Deprivation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensory+Deprivation), and a variety of curses. Having spotted a [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) right at the top of his pile, Human was quick to pick Green as an opening colour. With a good curve of creatures, including a couple of [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), it would allow him to accelerate nicely, and activate his Township even if white didn't work out deep enough to play as a main colour.


As a first pass, it was only a [Slayer of the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slayer+of+the+Wicked), [Smite the Monstrous](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite+the+Monstrous), Ghostly Warden and the flashback on [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations) that would require any white at all.


"My only good colour is green" lamented Zombie, having piled out a solid selection of green cards, supported by small amounts of blue and white. "I don't have a single [Caravan Vigil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caravan+Vigil). This might be too greedy."


Looking at [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake), Saab Ruinator, [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) and [Skaab Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Goliath), it did seem that Zombie's graveyard might be gobbled up pretty fast, which isn't the greatest of combos with a [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) that sat at his five drop slot. Between [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and a pair of copies of [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant), there seemed little doubt that he could power into his higher drops fast though, and there was a [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows) to make sure that he would be able to draw interesting spells to cast.


Human checked his array of werewolves carefully. A [Gatstaf Shepherd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatstaf+Shepherd), [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) and [Ulvenwald Mystics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Mystics) would each allow him to beat down, and even in un-flipped state they can provide a reasonable clock, with some help from a [Hamlet Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamlet+Captain). The [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) would be particularly good with his werewolves, as it would give him ample things to do even while not casting spells.


Of the zombies in Zombie's pool, [Skaab Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Goliath) was the one he tagged as not being worth the investment in dead bodies. [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) isn't too costly in dead creatures, and [Skaab Ruinator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Ruinator) is simply colossal, making the six drop look a little pricy for his deck.


In terms of spells, things were a little quiet. Just five non-creatures were making the cut; [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch), [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon), [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations), [Claustrophobia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claustrophobia) and [Frightful Delusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightful+Delusion). The counterspell, which has the potential for a nice little two-for-one, was a concession to the fact that there would be bombs in the format. Given all the Skaab creatures Zombie was running, having lots of non-creatures seemed to be a bad idea, so he just stuck with the best.


"Wow... I'm so stupid... my deck just got better."


It took Zombie a couple of passes through his cuts to realise that there was a [Battleground Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battleground+Geist) in there. It swiftly took the place of [Hamlet Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamlet+Captain), and left him looking at splashing [Gallows Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gallows+Warden), in spite of having cut back his white commitment to just [Slayer of the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slayer+of+the+Wicked), [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) and flashing back his [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations).


"I think I'm just going to be casting big guys and swinging." Zombie declared ruefully. He split his deck by colour and started listing it on his sheet, ready to think about how to build his mana base, which only required a single [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains).


Predictions? "I'm going to say an easy 10-0" smiled Zombie. "I just hope I don't flip [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) too many times with my [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant)."

Round 2: Racing De Rosa - Antonino de Rosa vs. Marco Pandino
------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 

Normally we don't do feature matches in round 2, but for Antonino de Rosa, now living in Palermo and making the most of a local Grand Prix, we were more than happy to make an exception. De Rosa is an old time pro who, having lived in such far flung corners of the world as Curacao and the Philippines, but he is now much closer to home. To see the long-time pro with just one bye is a testament to quite how long he's been out of the Pro Tour spotlight. He still knows what's what though, being one of the principle forces behind the [Puresteel Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puresteel+Paladin) deck, and having got back on the tour for Philadephia.


### Game One


Marco won the roll, and led with a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), while Ant had a turn one [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger). A [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse) from Marco was soon outclassed when De Rosa cast a [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth), who flipped out and became an [Unholy Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unholy+Fiend) at the first opportunity. The 3/3 didn't get equipped, as de Rosa was wary that Marco had not played a spell on his third turn. His instincts were correct. An [Ambush Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Viper) jumped in the way and left Ant to cast an [Ashmouth Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashmouth+Hound) and a [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch).


Marco had a big game ready, in [Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless), who swiftly became Veil-Cursed as he fought with de Rosa's only creature.


"What am I going to do about that one?" De Rosa asked a substantial crowd who were checking out his match. He tried another creature, but soon lost it to a [Corpse Lunge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Lunge). Things were looking ugly for Ant. A [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame), plus its flashback dealt with Garruk, but when [Olivia Voldaren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) replaced it, he declared that he wasn't quite sure how he was meant to win.


"I think those might be the best two rares in the set. At least that's what I've been told." Ant was not about to drag out a game where he was unlikely to come back. He scooped up his cards, and went to his sideboard for game two.


Marco Pandino 1 – 0 Antonino De Rosa



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/fm2%20Smilin%20Ant%20De%20Rosa.jpg)
*Antonino De Rosa*

### Game Two


Still smiling, De Rosa wasn't going to let the game one beating bring him down. A rocky run at Pro Tour Philadelphia had left De Rosa with just a single bye, and he seemed happy to play.


  

"I got to play my rare too, right? My white/red land?" It was fair to say that De Rosa's rare had created less impact on the game than either of his opponent's.


A [Bloodcrazed Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodcrazed+Neonate) from De Rosa was the first play of game two, and the Slith-alike was able to get in for two uncontested before being joined by an [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar). Marco was stuck on two lands, and forced to discard a [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) on his third turn, looking on as Ant continued to get stuck in. [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils) was his next discard, and when the following turn didn't bring Marco a land either, he was soon scooping up his cards.


"Oh **Magic**, how we love you. Some unbeatable rares, some land light draws, we've got it all here." One of the top commenters on the game, Mr Antonino De Rosa.


Marco Pandino 1 – 1 Antonino De Rosa



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/fm2%20Marco%20Pandino.jpg)
*Marco Pandino*

### Game Three


For game three, Ant again had a turn two [Bloodcrazed Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodcrazed+Neonate), but this time Marco was not short on lands. He was short on a blocker for the 2/1 though, meaning it got to grow at least once. Ant had an Elder Cahar for the third turn, mirroring the second game exactly.


A [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) killed off the Neonate before it got too big, but Ant kept rumbling in, and followed with an [Ashmouth Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashmouth+Hound). A [Woodland Sleuth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Sleuth) came from Marco, who used [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) to kill Ant's hound. De Rosa didn't seem impressed, and finished off the Sleuth with [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame).


Ant had a [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils), while Marco was on [Somberwald Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Spider). This looked a fairly large roadblock until Ant played his sixth land and a [Butcher's Cleaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher%27s+Cleaver), before equipping it to [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar). He was now swinging for quite a bit, and gaining life into the bargain.


Marco simply equipped his spiders with [Mask of Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+Avacyn) and passed. His road-block was now quite a bit better. A second [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar) showed up for Ant, whose creatures more or less lived in the red zone. Marco was on just 9 life, and had to be careful. He flashed in [Ambush Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Viper), and blocked both creatures. When the devils died, Marco was on 6, and [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar) #2 became a 4/4. With a cleaver, that 4/4 became a lethal 7/4 lifelinker.


Marco might have had the rares, but he was in a very rough spot. Ant cast another [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils), and attacked, forcing a block from Marco. The following turn he had a [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse) to block, but still went to 3, with De Rosa at 44. Being at 3 life with [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils) in play is a tough proposition, and Marco scooped up his cards.


**Antonino de Rosa wins 2-1!**


After the match, Antonino let us in on his new mission in **Magic**.


"Now that I'm living somewhere with a lot of opportunities to play, I'm going to take them. They announced Planeswalker Points, and I'm in 12th place of anyone in lifetime points. Kai [Budde] is 13th. I'm level 48 now, and there are only five level 50's."


"Gerard Fabaino is ranked 6th, at level 49. If Gerard gets to level 50, then I have to get to level 50. Right now I'm not sure who's in 11th on Planeswalker Points, but let it be known, I'm out to get you!"


It turns out that it's Hall of Fame member Frank Karsten who is above Antonino on the list in 11th place. There's only 1,266 points between them. The race is on.

Saturday, 4:30 p.m. - The Scale of the Beast
--------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 

Previously, the largest Grand Prix ever to be held in Italy had 1,291 players. That was in Florence, a little less than a year ago. Now there's a new record, and 1,790 is definitely not mucking about. That's close to 500 players more than ever showed up for an Italian Grand Prix before.


1,790 ... That's a whoppin' 10,740 booster packs which were opened at the beginning of deck construction. Can you imagine the sound of 10,740 booster packs being opened at about the same time? The rustle is not exactly deafening but, wow, it *is* impressive.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/blogScale.jpg)
*"...a whoppin' 10,740 booster packs..."*

Now, 10,740 booster packs ... All in all, including checklist cards, basic lands, and tokens, that's 171,840 cards. Can you imagine an amount like that? Well, of course you can't. It's pretty hard to really grasp the idea of such large numbers.


Let's look at it a different way. [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Lunarch) is, along with its fellow mythic rares, one of the rarest cards in *Innistrad*. After all, [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mikaeus%252C%2Bthe%2BLunarch) leads the Church of Avacyn and, by definition, there can be only one leader. Of course he's legendary as well as mythic. He's not one of the common folk, but a pretty elusive person. But in a field of 1,790 players with 10,740 booster packs openend, we estimate that there might be as many of 90 copies in the tournament. This Grand Prix has really reached [Mythic Proportions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mythic+Proportions)!

The Belgian had [Frightful Delusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frightful+Delusion) to stop this though, and force Rosada to discard a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest).

Saturday, 5:47 p.m. – Juuudge!
------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 

It's always worthwile to check with the judge team for insight into card interactions and tricky rules questions. They're busy people here in Milan, but still took the time to help out with this piece to educate all of you readers at home. Thanks to them, here are the three most interesting interactions we've seen so far.


[Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin) enters the battlefield as a copy of a creature. Now what will it be when it copies a double-faced card? The answer is, it will be a copy of the one side that was face-up at the time. And since the [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Splinter%2BTwin) isn't double-faced itself, it will never transform. Interestingly enough, [Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin)'s activated ability will only be able to destroy the original creature when it has the same side face-up as it had when it was copied.



![Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Evil+Twin)
![Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Villagers+of+Estwald)

Let's go with an example on that one. Say, you copy your opponent's [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald) with your [Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin). Your [Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin) will remain a copy of [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald), even when your opponent casts two spells in one turn. His [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald), however, will transform back to being [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) and then can't be destroyed by [Evil Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evil+Twin)'s (a.k.a. [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald)'s) ability. When it transforms again, though, it will be a valid target for the activated ability once more.


Another question came up a couple of times already this weekend: Can a Zombie equipped with [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) use [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) to shoot [Elite Inquisitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Inquisitor)? And if it can, will the damage be prevented?



![Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blazing+Torch)
![Elite Inquisitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Elite+Inquisitor)

The answer to both questions is no. [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) grants an ability to the creature it is attached to. Basically, the equipped Zombie has the ability to sacrifice [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) to have it deal 2 damage to target creature. Since [Elite Inquisitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Inquisitor) has protection from Zombies it can't be the target of a Zombie's ability. If it could, the damage wouldn't be prevented because it's actually [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) dealing the damage. But a Zombie can't torch [Elite Inquisitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elite+Inquisitor) anyway, so that doesn't really matter.


[Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Lunarch) is a very very rare creature, mythically rare even. But in a field of 1,790 players it already came to pass that one [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mikaeus%252C%2Bthe%2BLunarch) met another [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mikaeus%252C%2Bthe%2BLunarch). Now, player B wanted to know what would happen if he were to cast [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mikaeus%252C%2Bthe%2BLunarch) for just one mana (with X=0, that is). Obviously, it dies by way of being 0/0. However, would his opponent's [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mikaeus%252C%2Bthe%2BLunarch) also die because of the legend rule?



![Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Lunarch)
![Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Lunarch)

The answer is yes. There are two state-based effects which would kill the second [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mikaeus%252C%2Bthe%2BLunarch) (having zero toughness and being one of two legendary permanents with the same name), while there's only one to kill the first [Mikaeus, the Lunarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Mikaeus%252C%2Bthe%2BLunarch) (being one of two legendary permanents with the same name). But all state-based effects are checked at the same time, so both are put into their owners' graveyards.


However, the biggest "troublemaker" so far was [Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonds+of+Faith). Quite a lot of people forgot to retrieve the card after a match when they had enchanted an opposing creature. The judges try to return every card to their rightful owner. Still, their collection of [Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonds+of+Faith) is ever-growing.

Their poster child is [Undead Alchemist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undead+Alchemist). What could be more flavourful than sending your team after all the cards left in an opponent's deck rather than just going for damage. Milling is a fairly dangerous strategy for sealed deck in this format, as there are simply so many ways that it can backfire. Flashback is just the start, while opposing Skaab creatures, along with green creatures counting graveyards make things even worse.



![Dawn of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dawn+of+the+Dead)

Remember all those bad things that can happen to you if you mill your opponent? He'd much rather make the bad things happen to his opponent by playing mill effects on himself.



![Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Armored+Skaab)

Subtly synergistic, and more powerful than some have given credit. Zombies.

Saturday, 7:14 p.m.: - Tribal Spotlight: Werewolves

*by Tim Willoughby* 

Here at Grand Prix Milan there are a whole new set of incentives to play low cost cards in the format; namely werewolves. Already we are seeing that this is a format where aggressive starts are definitely possible, and few are quite as aggressive as those coming from werewolves. ![Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kessig+Wolf+Run)

![Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Travel+Preparations)

Tomorrow in draft though, will be a different story. Not only will there be plenty of wolves to choose from, this will also be the draft strategy with the clearest signals of all, as every player in the draft will be able to see who is picking a lot of double-faced cards highly. This is a strategy that favours the bold, but given the raw power of some of the transformed werewolves, especially the larger green ones, it could be a winner.

*by Tim Willoughby* 

Plenty of tribes will be getting the spotlight over the course of the weekend, but one of them that I am rather fond of has sneakily dodged the spotlight in *Innistrad* thus far. ![Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pitchburn+Devils)

There are the creatures that don't mind too much about being sacrificed, like [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils) or [Mausoleum Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mausoleum+Guard). There are creatures that like to see other creatures die, like [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows). There are those that like to see graveyards filling, like [Skaab Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Goliath) or [Boneyard Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boneyard+Wurm).



![Mausoleum Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mausoleum+Guard)
![Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Murder+of+Crows)

[Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) single-handedly means that morbid will always trigger when you want it to, making [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) a consistent five point burn spell, and [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) a consistent removal spell.



![Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice)
![Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Morkrut+Banshee)

I think that my favourite thing about [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) though, is what he does to the board while active. If your opponent has a removal spell, then the best it's going to do is change that spell's target into a 2/2. If your opponent is attacking with a human holding a [Butcher's Cleaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher%27s+Cleaver), then the lifelink can be stopped by blocking and then sacrificing the blocker (who would likely have died anyway) before damage. There's even a 2/2 left behind to block with (and sacrifice) the following turn.


While he might seem unassuming, the homunculus argument is a strong one when it comes for working out which cards to play in *Innistrad* sealed.

Saturday, 11:40 p.m. - Breaking Through the Bubble
--------------------------------------------------


*by Tim Willoughby* 

Going into round 9, there were a whole host of big names whose tournament life was in their hands. While Shuhei Nakamura had raced off to a fantastic start in the tournament, he had faltered for a couple of rounds, causing him to be walking the room repeating, 'please, just one more' to anyone who would care to listen. His deck, chock full of quality black removal, and a pair of demons, it would seem was listening to him, and after a worrying couple of rounds, Nakamura locked up his day two place.


Christian Calcano was another player who had travelled a long way to not make day two, but found himself perilously close to just that series of events. His blue/green deck had some powerful cards, but he had ultimately found [Skaab Ruinator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Ruinator) a little too greedy and difficult to cast, siding it out in most matches. In spite of this, he too saw fortune smile on him, winning fast, and declaring this an 'easy game' now that a day of drafting on Sunday was locked up.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/Christophe%20Gregoir%20and%20Yves%20Sele.jpg)
*Christophe Gregoir and Yves Sele*

For Christophe Gregoir, the red/white route was his chosen path, and in spite of a little scare in one game, he was able to get the win he needed. Both players were tired, but it was his opponent who blinked first, with a crucial [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) on a [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) token failing due to the on-board [Gallows Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gallows+Warden) being the final straw that left the game in Gregoir's hands.


Then, we had Yves Sele. He took the path less travelled to day two, by winning game one with white creatures, backed up by [Garruk Relentless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Relentless) (who ultimately fetched an [Elder of Laurels](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+of+Laurels) to close out the game), and then completely changing up for game two. In the second game, facing down [Ludevic's Test Subject](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ludevic%27s+Test+Subject) and a host of other blue/white threats, Sele was a blue/white/black deck himself. He didn't even need the Grimgrin, Corpse Born in his deck to win the second game, instead winning a tempo war to make day two.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/Kenny%20Oberg.jpg)
*Kenny Oberg*

Things didn't go so well for Kenny Oberg. His dream was crushed in a very close game three, as his blue/white deck fell to a blue/black deck splashing for [Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devil%27s+Play) and [Heretic's Punishment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heretic%27s+Punishment). It was the latter that finally killed off the Swede, who on three life was in fine shape to be shot down by the powerful enchantment.


There is still a round of play here in Milan, but for those still playing the pressure is off – everyone still battling has qualified for draft day.

Round 10: Feature Match - Nico Bohny vs. Florian Krauer
-------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobi Henke* 

And this was it, the final round of the day, deciding who would enter tomorrow's draft portion on an undefeated record. "Actually, the first game I played today with my deck was also against his deck," Krauer said, indicating Bohny on the other side of the table, "fancy us ending up here!"


The two Swiss players had played a couple of test games during their bye rounds. So who's winning? "Either [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) or he is," Bohny joked.


### Game One


Bohny played first, and came out of the gate blisteringly fast with [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), enchanted the 1-drop with [Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonds+of+Faith), then had [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth) on turn three, already attacking for 4 with his Champion.


Meanwhile, Krauer had made [Village Ironsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Ironsmith) on turn two and transformed it on turn three. Bohny didn't have a third land, but didn't let that stop him. [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth), with a little help from [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch), killed [Ironfang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ironfang), and he continued the beatdown. At end of turn, Krauer used [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) to get rid of [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth), then summoned [Screeching Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Bat). Bohny cast [Sharpened Pitchfork](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharpened+Pitchfork) and used it to turn his Champion into a 5/5 first striker. [Screeching Bat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screeching+Bat) chump-blocked and allowed Krauer to cast [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) for 5 damage to get rid of [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish). Bohny just had another [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) and was completely out of creatures, while Krauer found [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto) and summoned a [Slayer of the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slayer+of+the+Wicked). Soon, though, Bohny found [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer), then [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar), and then ... [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker).


[Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar) picked up [Sharpened Pitchfork](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sharpened+Pitchfork) and was blocked by a freshly-summoned [Geistcatcher's Rig](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistcatcher%27s+Rig). This put to +1/+1 counters on [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker). Also, [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) killed off the battered [Geistcatcher's Rig](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistcatcher%27s+Rig). On Bohny's next turn, [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) went from 3/3 to 4/4 with the help of the Pitchfork, Krauer went from 6 to 2. He drew his next card, then shuffled up his cards for game two.


Nico Bohny 1 – 0 Florian Krauer


Bohny: "Didn't I say? [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) is the winner!"



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/fm10_bohny.jpg)
*Nico Bohny*

### Game Two


"Here he comes again," Bohny commented as he cast [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) on turn two. Krauer had already committed [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) to the board, but cast [Rolling Temblor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) anyway. Bohny, however, had [Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment+of+Heroism), so [Rolling Temblor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor) only killed Krauer's own [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats). Both players just played lands for a couple of turns, while [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) brought Krauer to 17. Bohny summoned [Thraben Purebloods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Purebloods), Krauer cast [Curse of Death's Hold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold), killing [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker). Bohny had [Urgent Exorcism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urgent+Exorcism) to destroy the Curse, and attacked for 3.


[Victim of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) killed [Thraben Purebloods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Purebloods), which Bohny replaced with [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler). Krauer summoned Geistcatcher Rig. When the Rig attacked, Bohny killed it with [Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuke), which in turn allowed Krauer to summon a morbid [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) to kill [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler), netting him a 1/1 flier. This one didn't live long either, thanks to [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame).


Bohny restocked on creatures with [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) and [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar), while Krauer added [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils) and [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut) to his board. Bohny made [Manor Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manor+Gargoyle), Krauer made [Skirsdag Cultist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+Cultist). Bohny stopped that, before it could cause any actual harm by way of [Claustrophobia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claustrophobia), but lost his own [Manor Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manor+Gargoyle) to [Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuke). Bohny made [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) and [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant), Krauer called upon [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul) and a second copy of [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils). The board was getting crowded.


Bohny sacrificed [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar) to make his [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) 5/6, simultaneously forcing Krauer's [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut) to attack into its demise. Bohny had no more play, Krauer flashed back his [Rolling Temblor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Temblor), killing all of Bohny's creatures except for the [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler). He attacked with his two [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils) putting Bohny at 8. When Krauer revealed [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) and enough cards in his graveyard to kill the 5/6, Bohny conceded.


Nico Bohny 1 – 1 Florian Krauer



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmil11/fm10_krauer.jpg)
*Florian Krauer*

### Game Three


Bohny had the insanely aggressive start once again, this time with [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler), [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker), and [Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonds+of+Faith) (enchanting the Stalker) on his first three turns, while Krauer went without play for his own first three turns. Bohny added [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar) to his team, Krauer cast his first spell of the game in [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut), but was already at 11 by then.


When Bohny attacked with everything, the [Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juggernaut) blocked [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) and Krauer fell to 6. [Curse of Death's Hold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold) killed the newly-minted token and untapped [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut), and for a moment it looked as if Krauer might make his comeback. The next moment, however, was [Moment of Heroism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moment+of+Heroism) which Bohny cast to win the game.


**Nico Bohny 2 – 1 Florian Krauer**
