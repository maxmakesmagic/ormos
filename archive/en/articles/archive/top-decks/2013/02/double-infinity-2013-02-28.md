Double Infinity
===============



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on February 28, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







T his past weekend set yet another huge record breaker for **Magic**: The Gathering. In Charlotte, NC, we had not only the biggest North American Grand Prix of all time, not just the biggest Grand Prix,... but the biggest **Magic** event ever. [Grand Prix Charlotte](/en/events/coverage/skarren%E2%80%99s-skarrg-rampage-charlotte)—some might say the Grandest Prix—was... Not Constructed.


Luckily—and this is truly amazing—at the same time that the largest **Magic** event of all time was raging, a pretty significant Standard Grand Prix was going on in [Quebec City](/en/events/coverage/turn-three-take-fourteen-christiansen-mauls-quebec-city), up north. The Top 8 there was pretty exciting... and also pretty surprising.


Grand Prix Quebec City; or, " [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) was so last week."

Nico Christiansen played a blisteringly fast Naya beatdown deck that mixed up many new cards with some substantial surprises.


On the one hand, we have tons and tons of new quick drops— [Boros Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite) and [Experiment One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) on one; [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) (and virtual new card [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) ) on two; [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) on three; and even a solo [Ghor-Clan Rampager](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Rampager) on four... Naya Blitz is here-a-*Gatecrash*, there-a-*Gatecrash*... everywhere-a-*Gatecrash*!

But the strange thing?


Over and over we see new Boros cards... a white one-drop into eventual [Frontline Medic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic) .


We see fast green beats that stretch from a displaced Simic ( [Experiment One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) as a redundancy on [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) ) to [Flinthoof Boar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flinthoof+Boar) (a virtual Gruul spell)...


But where is [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) ?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td236_missingreckoner.jpg)Apparently, the dominant new card of [Pro Tour *Gatecrash*](/en/events/coverage/ptgtc13) is just *so last week*.


Other big surprise? [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) !


This deck is built for speed, and [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) helps out two ways. It can pull a creature past a red point-removal card and, of course, it can just rip 3 from a defender's desperate life total... in a pinch you can get both at the same time! If there is one thing players at home should take note of it is that if you have the choice... burn the Naya Blitz player on your own turn, or maybe at the end of his or her turn; otherwise, you risk a [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) smackdown.


Ditto, potentially, on [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) .

Wenzel Krautmann also posted an impressive finish with a similar Naya deck featuring mostly creatures and a couple of [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) s. Krautmann's deck is substantially different, though. Wenzel played [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) as a one-drop, as well as twenty-three lands. This allowed him to go bigger on drops—the traditional four-drop pairing of [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) . Overall, it's a more synergistic, potentially higher-powered look at a Naya attack, rather than going for purely quick beatdown.

You know how, the first time you saw The Aristocrats play out, you expected something spectacular to happen? Not that The Aristocrats is bad or anything—in addition to a Grand Prix Top 8 we will discuss in a bit, it produced the best possible result for a deck designer ([it won](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/finals-triumphant-aristocracy-2013-02-17) the Pro Tour it was built for). Just... you expect some kind of crazy infinite combo, having been primed by previous [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) -driven Human Reanimator decks.


[World **Magic** Cup](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/welcome-world-magic-cup-2012-08-17-0) standout Tzu Ching Kuo did not disappoint with this one. Totally different *Gatecrash* sacrifice outlet, maybe, but even more spectacular than even the previous Human Reanimator decks (which could produce infinite life and Wolves).


Here's the combo...


Set up a position where you can somehow get [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) onto the battlefield in concert with [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) , [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) , and [Undercity Informer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Informer) . You can, in theory, chain out the Emissary and Informer, cast the Angel later, and then cover the Angel up with a [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) by hand... but that's not really what we are talking about.

The dream draw with this deck more involves dumping all the relevant tools into the graveyard using [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) , [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) , and [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) .


You then use [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) to get back [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) , which then returns the trio (at least). When [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) comes into play, it produces two mana, and [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) 's job is to make the Angel disappear.


Now you use the two mana to sacrifice first the [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) , then the [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) , to the [Undercity Informer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Informer) . For sake of simplicity, the [Undercity Informer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Informer) is pointing at the opponent's library.

The [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) goes to the graveyard, followed by the [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) ... and when the [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) leaves the battlefield, the Angel reappears, returning the [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) and [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) back to the battlefield (again giving you two mana and again disappearing the Angel). You can grind the opponent with the [Undercity Informer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Informer) repeatedly this way, with the [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) giving you exactly enough mana to activate the Informer twice, looping these two pivotal Humans.


If you are rich (say you have an extra [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary) and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) access) you can gain lots of life and make lots of Wolves, but the real goal here is to just grind out the opponent's library. There are numerous advantages here over the previous build that "just" made Wolves and gained life. You don't need to get to your next attack to win, you can go completely over even an opposing "infinite life" combo (whether Humans Reanimator or RWU Lucky Charms Control). Also, you can jazz your own graveyard with [Undercity Informer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undercity+Informer) , taking advantage of either just a bigger graveyard for more [Angel of Glory's Rise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Glory%27s+Rise) action or to gain access to tools like [Purify the Grave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Purify+the+Grave) .

A few weeks ago, it was the most laudable of three-drops. Great in Zombies; great in other Zombies; so good it was included as a difficult [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) "target" in Modern Jund lists.


And then...?


[Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) !


Well, what seems like an off week for the Minotaur Wizard, combined with some Zombies wizardry by players like Becerra, have returned [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) to prominence—here in a Jund Zombies list.


Being Jund instead of Rakdos, Becerra got to take advantage of more great cards; he could start on [Deathrite Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) and activate all abilities. In addition to [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) , he got to take advantage of the mighty [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) on three. And, of course, on two, yet another massively powerful Zombie in [Lotleth Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll) : the deck can put together a hellacious offense, draw on the resilience of not only [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and [Geralf's Messenger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geralf%27s+Messenger) but the removal-resistance of [Lotleth Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll) (regeneration), [Dreg Mangler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreg+Mangler) (re-buy), and the four-drop bomb [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) .

Punch *and* staying power.


Becerra's spell base is designed, nigh explicitly, to be able to handle a [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) . [Brimstone Volley](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) might make for a painful exchange, but at least all main-deck spells can take out the beatdown-hating Minotaur Wizard (although [Tragic Slip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tragic+Slip) , perhaps tragically, takes a little setup via combat or [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) ).

Speaking of [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) , Thomas Holzinger re-bought the success of the Pro Tour winner at the follow-up Grand Prix.


The Aristocrats is a tough deck to put into a simple box. It is essentially a Humans beatdown deck that can start on [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) and then buff immediately—if surprisingly—with [Knight of Infamy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy) , then buff and pair with [Silverblade Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) .

But in addition to a straightforward Humans beatdown strategy, The Aristocrats plays off of multiple synergies based on sacrificing its own creatures. [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) , going long, can ensure damage by forcing through an unblocked 2/2. [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) is near the top of its always lofty game here. Haste, resilience to [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) ,... even some extra buffing (and fuel) via [Doomed Traveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) !

All that self-sacrifice lets you set up a powerhouse long game via [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest) , plus massive extra value from [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) . The old "steal your beast thing, smash you with it, then throw it into the graveyard instead of giving it back" trick... might I suggest [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) ?


### Jund Midrange Variants


Speaking of [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) , nearly half the [Grand Prix Quebec City Top 8](/en/events/coverage/turn-three-take-fourteen-christiansen-mauls-quebec-city) was composed of Jund Midrange decks. Both the styles of Jund Midrange from the Top 8 of Pro Tour *Gatecrash* were represented.


Any version of Jund Midrange is going to run a combination of the best black, red, and green cards in the format, combining flexible removal, great threats, and blowouts... all set up with [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) . Jund Midrange decks will typically play at least two copies of [Rakdos's Return](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos%27s+Return) main deck to punish permission-poor control decks, and can turn even a lowly [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) into a murderer via an endgame [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) .

This style of Jund Midrange features more offensive utility creatures main, notably [Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk) . Cantin used all four copies of [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) main, the so-called [Searing Spear](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Spear) / [Mutilate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate) split card.

### "Grandest" SCG Tournament


Not to be outdone by Quebec City, [Grand Prix Charlotte](/en/events/coverage/skarren%E2%80%99s-skarrg-rampage-charlotte) put out a Sunday tournament that rivaled the size and difficulty of a Grand Prix itself. Given the epic enrollment of Grand Prix Charlotte, tournament organizers StarCityGames wanted to give something back. They had already scheduled a tournament for Sunday, but they not only doubled the prize payout... but gave away free entrances to everyone who was eliminated in the first pre-draft round of Day Two!


The tournament, whose winner would also get a free flight and hotel room for the upcoming Grand Prix Miami, gave us even more looks to the viable decks of Standard after Pro Tour *Gatecrash*.

Straight Junk Reanimator seems like an important look back at the pre-*Gatecrash* version of the Standard format. Unlike many of the decks we have looked at—Naya Blitz, The Aristocrats, &amp;amp;c.—this deck is not rich with *Gatecrash* cards (although it does play some copies of [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) in the sideboard).


The deck simply plays a great game against aggro, combining both a powerful combo element with some of the best "fair" tools in Standard.


On the one side, Reanimator can just be a good creature deck, *a la* Selesnya Ramp. You can start on [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) or [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and find your way to the Achievement Unlocked of [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) + [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) . [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) is great not just with [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) but [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) , [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) , and [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) .

Then again, you can go nutso with [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) and [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) , put a bunch of tools into the graveyard, and start grinding out discounted—and repeating— [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) s. Or you can start on your one-drop accelerators... and then suddenly make them huge (and lethal in one turn) with a single [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth) thanks to [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) .


One underrated feature of this style of Reanimator deck is the ability to grind out a very long game against [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&amp;amp;name=Nephalia%2BDrownyard) decks via [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) and [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) . Over time, you can get an Angel in the graveyard (not hard against a [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&amp;amp;name=Nephalia%2BDrownyard) deck) and either play an uncounterable [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) or use [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) to hide the *next* Angel (and hopefully some other tools) under the one on the battlefield. In this manner, the opponent is forced to deal with each Angel... while giving you the next Angel to potentially set up further and further snowballs of card advantage.

Because Angels can kill many times faster than [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&amp;amp;name=Nephalia%2BDrownyard) (and because the opponent will often be in a position where he or she has to find an answer for each Angel) this can prove a successful strategy, especially when you can net two more creatures (ideally, removal-resistant [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) s) with each Angel loop.

Esper Control decks exist—like many common color combinations—along a continuum. Bigford played a permanents-heavy version with both little and big creatures, with primarily Planeswalkers to kill.


Like many Esper decks, he played just the two [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) s for main-deck permission.


* Only two copies of [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) and three copies of [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) main (many Esper decks play four and four).
* A return of [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) ! After months of languishing in the zero-to-two range, the cross-format all-star returns in Bigford's deck as a four-of.
* No copies of [Planar Cleansing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing) ... likely an accommodation to Bigford's own Planeswalker-defined strategy to win.
