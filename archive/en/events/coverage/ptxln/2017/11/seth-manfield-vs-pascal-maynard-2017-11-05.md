Finals: (11) Seth Manfield vs. Pascal Maynard
=============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 5, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











As the players shuffled up for the finals of Pro Tour *Ixalan*, a Top 8 that began as a blend of established pros and first timers—including Samuel Ihlenfeldt in his first-ever Pro Tour appearance—came down to two players no one was surprised to see.


Pascal Maynard, veteran of thirteen Grand Prix Top 8 appearances and one previous Pro Tour Top 8, squared off against No. 11–ranked Seth Manfield, who was making what could be a career-defining fourth Pro Tour Top 8.


With 24 Grand Prix Top 8s and four previous Pro Tour Top 8 appearances between them, the finals was a matchup between greats, with each experienced in high-pressure moments of *Magic*. With $50,000 on the line—not to mention automatic Platinum status, the lead in the Player of the Year race, and a trophy to boot—the finals of Pro Tour *Ixalan* was one for the ages.


The decks were both surprises as well. The most-played archetypes at the event were Temur Energy, Ramunap Red, and Four-Color Energy. All three had made it to the Top 8, but neither in the hands of the two finalists.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/110517_6389.jpg)  
*Seth Manfield and Pascal Maynard faced off for the title of Pro Tour* Ixalan *Champion!*


Instead, Manfield was piloting a Sultai deck focused on [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) that also utilized some energy cards to get extra value out of [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner), [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista), and [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker). Maynard was on a lightning-fast "combo" version of [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift), which aimed to place the artifact into the graveyard and reanimate it with [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) as early as the third turn and consistently on the fourth. From there, Maynard would reanimate [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) and [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) to grind opponents out with a parade of big creatures.


### **The Games**


Manfield mulliganned to six, but both players had very good draws in the first game. While Manfield curved [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) into [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner) into double [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) to take out Maynard's board of a [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) and [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits), Maynard built it right back up and more with [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish) on [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) on the fifth turn.


But without [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) to help buoy his board, Maynard was susceptible to the [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista), and one of the Constructs went down to trade itself for [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat). That enabled revolt for [Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push) to take out the [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits), and Manfield crashed in with his Snake and Siphoner.


But the Angel arrived on the next turn, courtesy of a [Strategic Planning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strategic+Planning) and [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift). It ate [Vraska's Contempt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vraska%27s+Contempt), but left behind Servos on the board while [Cast Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cast+Out) took care of Manfield's [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor). He tried to rebuild with [Rogue Refiner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue+Refiner), but another [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) came back for Maynard on the next turn to apply even more pressure. When Manfield's draw step failed to find an answer, the finals moved on to Game 2.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/110517_6363.jpg)  
*Pascal Maynard took down Game 1 to jump to an early lead.*


It was Maynard's turn to mulligan in the second game, while Manfield curved [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) into [Rogue Refiner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue+Refiner). A [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat) for Maynard slowed the American down, but a second Walking Constrictor into [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) cleared out a [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) that hadn't found a [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) to discard. Faced with the fast clock Manfield was presenting, Maynard could only helplessly play out chump blockers as the 2015 World Champion's team crashed in to knock him down to 5 life. One draw later Maynard scooped up his cards and the players moved to the sideboarded games knotted at one win apiece.


Manfield started Game 3 with a mulligan, while Maynard kept a one-lander thanks to a pair of Opts and a [Search for Azcanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Azcanta), which would serve to set up his draws if he could find a crucial second land. [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) revealed that hand to Manfield, and the [Search for Azcanta](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Azcanta) bit the dust. It took two Opts, but Maynard did find his second land, while Manfield finally got on the board on the third turn with [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner). [Minister of Inquiries](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minister+of+Inquiries) put [Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions) and [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) into the graveyard for Maynard, who then deployed [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) to take [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner) off the table.


Unfortunately for Maynard, that didn't last long as [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker) took the Warden out of commission and returned the Siphoner to play. [Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions) from Maynard threatened to turn the tables yet again, but a timely [Blossoming Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Defense) from Manfield kept his board intact. The odd-looking board got even more so as Manfield cast Maynard's own [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) out from under [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker) and removed the [Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions) from play. A post-combat [Deathgorge Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathgorge+Scavenger) removed the other one from the graveyard, and Maynard found his back squarely up against the wall. [Cast Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cast+Out) was the perfect answer, exiling his own [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) and getting back [Angel of Sanctions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Sanctions) to remove [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner) from the game.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/110517_6352.jpg)  
*Seth Manfield established a strong board state, but Pascal Maynard's [Cast Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cast+Out) was always a threat.*


Another [Hostage Taker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hostage+Taker) swung things right back around, and thanks to a huge [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista) and an all-out attack, Manfield knocked Maynard to 3 and ended the game on the next turn, taking a 2-1 lead as the finals moved onto the fourth game.


[Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) led things off for Manfield in what could be the deciding game of the Pro Tour, but a [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) from Maynard took out the Snake. [Deathgorge Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathgorge+Scavenger) allowed Manfield to interact with Maynard's graveyard while also getting in damage, and a [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) took potential gamebreaker [Skysovereign, Consul Flagship](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skysovereign%2C+Consul+Flagship) out of Maynard's hand. Manfield's next attack knocked Maynard down to 6 life, but the Canadian finally found his fifth land for [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention), which quickly turned his otherwise unimpressive board into a formidable fighting force.


But he wasn't the only one with anthem effects. Manfield played [Rishkar, Peema Renegade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishkar%2C+Peema+Renegade) to place a pair of counters on both the Walking Constrictor and the [Deathgorge Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathgorge+Scavenger), though he was forced to simply pass the turn afterward as Maynard had eight creatures in play and an [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) to grow his team.


With the board stalled, both players were looking for ways to break things open. It was Maynard who found it first, milling a [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) into his graveyard and returning it with [Refurbish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Refurbish). The final card in Manfield's hand turned out to be [Appetite for the Unnatural](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Appetite+for+the+Unnatural), which cleanly answered the namesake card of Maynard's deck. But a second copy of [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) was waiting on top of Maynard's deck, and he tapped seven mana for it, returning another [Angel of Invention](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Invention) and taking down the game in short order.


That sent things to a fifth and deciding game—though Maynard made sure to check first.


"It is 2-2, right?" he asked sheepishly.


It was. And it would all come down to one final game. Two Pro Tour regulars, two repeat Top 8 competitors. A high-five between the two and the deciding game was off to the races.


[Attune with Aether](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attune+with+Aether) into [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner) was the start for Manfield, who began drawing extra cards on the third turn. A second one arrived, and the beatdown began for the former World Champion. He knocked Maynard down to 14 life, but came up short of a fourth land drop. [Deathgorge Scavenger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathgorge+Scavenger) followed up and exiled [Sacred Cat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Cat), leaving Maynard trying to play catch-up.


Cast out on Scavenger was a start, but [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) took out the [Fumigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fumigate) Maynard was hoping to deploy on the next turn. The attack knocked Maynard to 10 life, and a [Fetid Pools](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fetid+Pools) meant Manfield would have access to more mana on the next turn. That turned into [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor) plus [Walking Ballista](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Ballista), which took out [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) and allowed the menacing [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner)s to crash in again.


Maynard was in serious need of an answer as his life total dipped perilously low. [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits) found [Fairgrounds Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fairgrounds+Warden) to remove the [Winding Constrictor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winding+Constrictor), but an all-out attack from Manfield plus a [Blossoming Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Defense) was enough to provide exactly lethal.


Not that he got a chance to finish things that way. As soon as he saw the [Blossoming Defense](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blossoming+Defense) in Manfield's hand, Maynard was already taking off his headset. He stood up from his seat and walked around the table to embrace Manfield in a huge hug, congratulating him on becoming the Champion of Pro Tour *Ixalan*.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2017/events/ptxln/PTIxalan-20171105-3162.jpg)


**Seth Manfield defeated Pascal Maynard 3-2 to become Pro Tour *Ixalan* Champion!**
