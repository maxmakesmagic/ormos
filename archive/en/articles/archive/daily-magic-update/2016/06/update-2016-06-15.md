Today's Must
============


1. [*Magic* Math—Should You Play Two Chandra with Four Oath of Nissa?](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/magic-math-should-you-play-2-chandra-with-4-oath-of-nissa/) | ChannelFireball | Frank Karsten

It's the article we've all been waiting for since Raphaël Lévy [won a Grand Prix](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpman16) with two [Chandra, Flamecaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Flamecaller)s that could only be cast via [Oath of Nissa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Nissa). Frank Karsten is basically the community's math teacher, and in this article Karsten uses such phrases as "using a simple hypergeometric distribution," and "conditional probability with land counts and card selection." But don't feel daunted—read on anyway and feel all the smarter for it.

1. [Playing Pauper: Rebels](http://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/playing-pauper-rebels) | MTGGoldfish | Jake Stiles

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Standard Temur Traverse](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2016-06-14)

I'll admit, looking through Standard lists for innovation is a little daunting right now, as the metagame is pretty settled while we wait for *Eldritch Moon* to release whatever horrors it has in store for us.


But that doesn't mean innovation is dead. *Magic Online* user GBRUNO recently went 5-0 in a Competitive Standard League with a Temur [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) deck that plays a bunch of interesting cards.


First, there are some of the more obvious choices if you're going to build a Temur deck. [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) is a format staple, [Roast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roast) is great removal, [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy) is a powerful card that helps get delirium, and [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) might be the most-played card in Standard.


But then there's [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe) with no madness cards. Main deck [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) and a pair of [Brutal Expulsion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brutal+Expulsion)s, which you don't really see anywhere. Plus, the deck actually goes as deep as playing [Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wastes) just to pack three copies of [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer).


I have no idea if this deck is good, medium, or poised to break out. All I know is that one player managed to go undefeated with the list, so there has to be something to it.
