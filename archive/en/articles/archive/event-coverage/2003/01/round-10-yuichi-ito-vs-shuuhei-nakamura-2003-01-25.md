Round 10: Yuichi Ito vs. Shuuhei Nakamura
=========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 25, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gphir03/a959.jpg)Shuuhei Nakamura is no stranger to Grand Prix greatness. Whether alone or with his cronies in S.S.D., he's made a habit of winning and he isn't likely to break it soon. Today he's playing what many believe to be Extended's best deck: [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog). He's managed to avoid falling to mono-red so far, but he'll need a little luck to escape it entirely. This round he faces an easier matchup: The Rock. Its pilot, Yuichi Ito, is very much an unknown quantity.

### Game 1

Ito led with [Llanowar Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes) and [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), seeing Nakamura's hand of two land, [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother), [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) and [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). He took the counter. Nakamura drew another, but what he really needed was more land. Ito took advantage fetching [Dust Bowl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Bowl) with [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish). If Nakamura didn't find lands soon, he'd be sunk. He drew [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval), played swamp, and gave it back.

Ito [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ed away the second Leak and waited. Nakamura drew and played [Lonely Sandbar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lonely+Sandbar). This was the moment of truth. Ito drew, played [Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed), and was done. He had no fourth land. From there, everything changed. Nakamura was free to play his [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), enabling a pitch of [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis). Ito held off Deeding for a turn, but still he couldn't draw an extra land. Nakamura's Analysis provided all the lands he needed.

Finally Ito blew the Deed during Nakamura's attack. Nakamura was ready to save with [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse), and he played out [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar). Ito Tutored for a fourth land and played another Deed, but by now Nakamura was running away with it. [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) stocked his hand and graveyard, and though Ito resolved [Gigapede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gigapede), he had nothing to stop Nakamura's [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). He conceded as soon as he saw the spell.

### Game 2

Again, Ito's early game was [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), this time on the second turn. He stripped [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) and left Nakamura with just a pair of [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)es and [Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic). He hoped for a masterstroke when he came down with [Phyrexian Negator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Negator), but Nakamura laughed and showed off the [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) he'd just drawn. Ito was understandably disappointed.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gphir03/a958.jpg)That meant that Nakamura was free to Wish for [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) instead of [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother). Ito was again stuck on three land. Another [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) from Nakamura kept him from Tutoring up a fourth. Ito [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)ed again, forcing out the [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). Two [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s went to the graveyard while he stocked up on lands and [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar). The [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) took his second [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction).

Nakamura got his Familiar into play, ready to dig for more action. [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) stopped a [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother). [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) took his [Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic), but he still had Analysis and [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish). An Edict from Ito finally got rid of the Familiar. Nakamura Analyzed twice, tapping out and giving Ito the go-ahead. All he could manage was [Yavimaya Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Elder).

With Nakamura drawing so many cards, Ito was fast running out of action. He had another [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) for Nakamura's [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), but Nakamura was fine with that. He removed his [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction)s from the game, and then Wished for one of them after Ito played his last card, another Elder. Nakamura displayed no fear. His [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) gave him another Familiar and some lands.

Ito activated both his [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village)s and attacked with all his creatures. Nakamura dismissed the effort with [Smother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother) and [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) on the Treetops. He continued to search with [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and [Cephalid Coliseum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Coliseum), but turned up blanks. Another [Fact or Fiction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) sent two of his remaining [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog)s to the graveyard in favor of more card drawing. Of course, the fourth turned up on his next draw step. Nakamura just shrugged. Ito put up little enough resistance that Nakamura didn't even need to dig for the [Upheaval](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval). He just removed Ito's blockers and swung for the win.

**Final Result:** Shuuhei Nakamura defeats Yuichi Ito 2-0
