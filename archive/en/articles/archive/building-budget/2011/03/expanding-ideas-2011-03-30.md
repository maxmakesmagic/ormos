===============



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on March 30, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week I worked on a rareless deck that had the ability to compete with some of the most established decks in the metagame. Since that column, a lot of you have sent me emails asking what rares I would add to the deck if I was not overly concerned with keeping the rareless theme.

This week, I'm going to take the rareless deck [from last week](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/rareless-and-careless-2011-03-23) and upgrade it with a few different rare cards. We'll start by taking a look at the deck from last week:

The original version of "Rareless and Careless" did very well in games where it drew [Neurok Commando](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Commando). It stole other games with [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend), but the [Neurok Commando](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Commando) draws seemed like the games where the deck functioned the way we wanted it to. The deck had trouble with [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) and [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle). The deck did very well against players whose decks were full of non-Titan creatures.

I set out to find another [Neurok Commando](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Commando) in the format. [Scroll Thief](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scroll+Thief) and [Surrakar Spellblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surrakar+Spellblade) seem like the obvious choices. [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) is also a nice stand-in. [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) will help us find the key uncommon with a greater level of consistency.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Scroll Thief | Surrakar Spellblade |

Cheap burn is very well positioned right now, especially if you can back it up with a card advantage engine. [Searing Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) is a powerful spell and works very well with [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend), but it suffers a bunch of problems in the deck as it's currently configured. You see, there aren't any red-blue dual lands easily available to us right now. If I cast a [Searing Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze) on turn two, then there's absolutely no way for me to cast a [Neurok Commando](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Commando) on the third turn, my ideal play. I've been shaving the numbers on this card as it was. I'd much rather have additional copies of [Burst Lightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burst+Lightning) or [Staggershock](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staggershock).



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Searing Blaze | Kiln Fiend |

I found that the [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend)s in the main deck were a bit of a mistake. Even if Caw-Blade doesn't pack much removal, most people have anywhere from four to eight spot removal spells. [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend) gives people a target for their [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s, [Condemn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn)s, [Go for the Throat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat)s, and [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s. If I cut the [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend), then [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) is the only targetable creature in the main. To all the [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend) lovers: Don't worry! The [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend)s will still be in the sideboard, as they become a lot better when your opponents don't have any removal spells left after sideboarding.

Once the [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend)s are gone, it seems safe to take it a step further. [Æther Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Adept) is good, but it may be better as another card.

Now we're doing work! We've got some extra slots to work with and we know what we want our deck to do. Now that we've cut all the targetable creatures it's safe to say that our extra copies of [Neurok Commando](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Neurok+Commando) (numbers five through seven) should be [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren)s. [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) has been printed in a few sets now and shouldn't be too difficult to acquire three copies of.

![Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jace+Beleren)  
Here's the new main deck. It may not look special, but it does a great job fixing the leaks in the original rareless version of the deck.

The new sideboard is where the real magic happens. Your opponents won't see any creatures for their removal spells to hit, which gives you a great opportunity to side in the absurdly powerful—*if they take out their removal*—[Surrakar Spellblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surrakar+Spellblade).

Think about it: You've just sideboarded out all your spot removal. Your opponent casts a [Surrakar Spellblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surrakar+Spellblade) or [Kiln Fiend](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiln+Fiend). You just lost the game as long as your opponent has a few burn spells to force it through. And this deck has no shortage of burn spells.

The rest of the sideboard is consistent with last week's. I still want [Perilous Myr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Myr) and [Narcolepsy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Narcolepsy) as answers to [Kor Firewalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker), and I still need a playset of [Flashfreeze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flashfreeze)s to help the [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) matchup. Here's the final sideboard.
