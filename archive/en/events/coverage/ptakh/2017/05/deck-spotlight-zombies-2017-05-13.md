Deck Spotlight: Zombies
=======================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 14, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











They've been lurking for months, just under the surface. (Un)living on the fringes, never fully coming into play. That is, until *Amonkhet* opened the pyramids, and the graves.


Zombies have arrived in Standard.


With a new Standard metagame at Pro Tour *Amonkhet,* a Zombie from the plane has been largely responsible for marking the rise (from the grave) of the Zombie tribe: [Dread Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Wanderer). The now-ubiquitous one-drop is the biggest reason why Zombies are back in the metagame, but it's not just *Amonkhet* that's on display.


The core of the Zombies deck has actually been around since *Eldritch Moon*. The archetype took a backseat to artifact-centric strategies over the past six months, but the printing of Wanderer and [Liliana's Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Mastery) have reinvigorated the deck.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dread+Wanderer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Wanderer) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Liliana%27s+Mastery)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Mastery)
The core of the deck stays the same regardless of whether it's the more popular mono-black version, or the white-black build team [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) showed up with that midway through Day Two was well on its way to taking Chris Fennell to the Top 8.


The eight one-drops are the key. Wanderer is joined by [Cryptbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptbreaker), which is both a tempo first-turn play when needed but more importantly a source of late-game card advantage. It can either tap an army of Zombies to cash in a point of life for a card, or it can turn an unneeded card into a Zombie to maintain pressure. One of the common mistakes players make is believing the deck to be solely an aggressive deck, when in reality [Cryptbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptbreaker) nearly by itself gives the deck a solid late-game plan. If both players are looking to the top of the deck for help, the ability to draw even a single extra card can be backbreaking, and even if [Cryptbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptbreaker) is alone on the battlefield it can turn excess lands into an undead horde. Likewise, [Dread Wanderer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dread+Wanderer) keeps coming back, providing a constant stream of pressure.

There is some debate at the two-drop spot, and players are finding success across the spectrum. While most players chose to utilize [Relentless Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Dead) and honorary Zombie [Metallic Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallic+Mimic), there are plenty finding success without it (see Fennell's deck). Present in nearly every sideboard was [Scrapheap Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger), which is a powerful recursive threat to bring back against control decks or [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) decks that boarded into sweepers.


Next comes the lord. [Lord of the Accursed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Accursed), that is. A powerful pump spell for the team, it also can help break a board stall by giving all Zombies menace if needed. Alongside it is [Diregraf Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diregraf+Colossus), a card that many people forgot about for a long time but one that is extremely powerful in this shell. It is often a 4/4 as soon as it is cast, and only grows the Zombie board state after that.


At the top end of the curve sits [Liliana's Mastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana%27s+Mastery), a new addition that players are jamming anywhere from two to four copies of. Not only does it refill a board immediately following a sweeper, but it pumps the team for a lethal attack or gives the deck an edge in the mirror if the opponent has an army of 2/2 Zombies facing what are now 3/3s.


The removal the deck has access to is also top-notch. [Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push) and [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness) deal with most of the major threats in Standard, while [Dark Salvation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Salvation) is a beating all on its own.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dark+Salvation)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Salvation)
Truly a unique card, [Dark Salvation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Salvation) does it all. It can serve as a three-mana spell that creates a Zombie token while removing an opponent's creature, but it also scales up as the game goes on, and it hasn't been uncommon to see [Dark Salvation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Salvation) create two or even three Zombie tokens in Nashville.


Here we come to the major divergence between the mono-black and white-black variants. While the mono-black lists are able to play [Westvale Abbey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Westvale+Abbey) or [Cradle of the Accursed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cradle+of+the+Accursed) to gain value from their lands, that versatility must be sacrificed by the white version of the deck. In return, it gains access to [Wayward Servant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wayward+Servant)—a verifiable machine gun of life drain—or even [Binding Mummy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Binding+Mummy), a key card in the mirror.

White also offers access to what are defining cards in the Pro Tour metagame filled with [Aetherworks Marvel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) decks: [Declaration in Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Declaration+in+Stone) and [Anguished Unmaking](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anguished+Unmaking) to deal with the inevitable [Ulamog, the Ceaseless Hunger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulamog%2C+the+Ceaseless+Hunger) that the Marvel decks will produce. Not being dead to the deck's most powerful card allowed the white versions of the Zombie decks to successfully fight through a field of Marvels. In addition, having [Gideon, Ally of Zendikar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon%2C+Ally+of+Zendikar) in the sideboard allowed the deck to work its way through the Marvel decks playing [Chandra, Flamecaller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Flamecaller) that were heavier on control elements. Alongside [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind) and [Scrapheap Scrounger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrapheap+Scrounger), Zombies is able to match up well against Marvel and control decks.

All in all, Zombies is the breakout deck of *Amonkhet.* Its combination of early aggression, late-game inevitability, and ability to adapt to the metagame around it has defined Standard at the Pro Tour, and it's clear the Zombies aren't going away anytime soon.
