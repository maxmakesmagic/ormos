Hey everyone, and welcome to the last week of 10 Decks in 10 Weeks! It's been two and a half months since I started this experiment, and I think that overall it's been pretty successful. Some deck transitions were smoother than others, and some decks performed better than others, but there were a lot of good ideas, some pretty killer decks, and a whole lot of budgeting going on!

The most requested follow-up to this series was definitely a Battle Royale in which all ten decks squared off against one another to see which would come out on top. I'm not averse to figuring out how to do this if it's something you, the reader, really want to see—so if you wow my socks off on these poll results, I'll see what could be arranged!

This week's article focuses around [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth), a rare from *Planar Chaos*. [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth) is part of the same cycle of creatures as [Aeon Chronicler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler). Functionally, I could describe [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth) as the mismatched offspring of [Hunting Moa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunting+Moa) and [Emperor Crocodile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emperor+Crocodile). Like the former, it gives +1/+1 counters to other creatures. Like the latter, if you control no other creatures, it dies.


![10 Decks in 10 Weeks](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/features/bb_10decks_logo.jpg)That's the rub against [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth)—in almost all situations, it cannot be your only creature on the board. If you don't either suspend it put a counter on other creatures, or have other creatures in play with +1/+1 counters, it dies. In order to build a successful [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth) deck, you need creatures with +1/+1 counters—lots of them.


In order to get started, let's take out the black from last week's deck.


Out: 4 [Chromatic Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star), 1 [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit), 1 [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), 4 [Null Profusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Null+Profusion), 10 [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), 1 [Urborg, Tomb of Yawgmoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg%2C+Tomb+of+Yawgmoth), 1 [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)

I've taken out a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) in order to make room for [Simic Growth Chamber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Growth+Chamber)s. In this deck, we're definitely going to need the color fixing, because the focus of this deck is creatures with +1/+1 counters. Which creatures have the most +1/+1 counters? The green spikes and the Simic graft creatures from *Dissension*!

In: 8 [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), 4 [Simic Growth Chamber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Growth+Chamber)


I'm not going to lie—a lot of the green combo pieces from last week's deck aren't going to end up making the next cut—but I'm leaving them in to see what happens just in case. To start the deck off, I added in 4 [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu). This is one of the earliest drops that can get +1/+1 counters, and it works extremely well with [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) and [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow). Naturally, 4 copies of [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth) need to be in the deck, and I've returned [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk) from [A Blink In Time](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/10-decks-10-weeks-blink-time-2007-04-09) to make a repeat appearance, four weeks later. I don't expect this version to play very well (there aren't enough creatures to reliably be able to play [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth)), but I want to see how the cards interact.


In: 4 [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu), 4 [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth), 2 [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk)

I get an early [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu) and follow it with [Overgrowth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrowth). He tries to [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang) it, and I play [Early Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Early+Harvest), untap, and [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk). I then get double [Harmonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize), [Reclaim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclaim), two more [Overgrowth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrowth)s, and another [Early Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Early+Harvest). He counters a couple of spells with [Muddle the Mixture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture) and [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang)s my Kudzu, but the Whelk goes all the way.  

Record: 1-0


Yeah, pretty much what I thought—I had lots of mana generation and barely any creatures. To fix the deck from this point, I took out a lot of the [Null Profusion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Null+Profusion) engine pieces, and added in some good blue, green, and artifact creatures that deal in +1/+1 counters. [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling) is a 3/3 for three and [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Root-Kin) is a 4/4 for four, and both give great benefits to other creatures with +1/+1 counters—they are auto-includes in this type of deck. I also added one each of four utility creatures—[Triskelavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus) (a large flyer and tim), [Spike Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder) (lifegain, and the ability to move around +1/+1 counters), [Spike Tiller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Tiller) (another Spike, and one that can make three more creatures with +1/+1 counters), and [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Manipulator) (a limited Control Magic).


Out: 4 [Early Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Early+Harvest), 3 [Summer Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summer+Bloom), 1 [Mirari](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirari), 4 [Overgrowth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrowth)

Game 2: Joysynthesis (U/B Control)  

I get stuck on three mana, and he plays [Dimir Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildmage) and [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) on my [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu). I then get [Reclaim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclaim) into [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth) for a fourth land and play [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Manipulator) and [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling). They die, one to blocking his [Dimir Guildmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Guildmage) and one to a [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit). When I try to play another Frogling, he answers with [Remove Soul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) and I lose.  

Record: 1-1

Game 3: Countrythug (Mono-White [Rescue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rescue))  

I get [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu), it hits once as a 4/4, and he plays [Devouring Light](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Light) to kill it. [Sunlance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunlance) kills [Spike Feeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Feeder), but I land another Kudzu, follow it with [Triskelavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus), and watch him block with [Aven Riftwatcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Riftwatcher) and [Dust Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Elemental) to gain 4 life and stop a creature. He then does that again, except with [Whitemane Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whitemane+Lion). When he tries to put [Griffin Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griffin+Guide) on his Lion the next turn, I kill it with [Triskelavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus) tokens in response. I get [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling) to the board, and it protects me the rest of the way for the win.  

Record: 2-1
Game 4: Garthug (U/W Urzatron)  

Mulligan a one-land hand into some offense. After a long game, he plays double [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) and double [Tidings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidings), gets double Urzatron and triple [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), and beats me while at 4 life. I have a ton of offense and he's almost dead, but he locks me down with [Spell Burst](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Burst). I nearly beat him three times, once with [Triskelavus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelavus), once with [Spike Tiller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Tiller) plus [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth), and once with [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Root-Kin) and [Draining Whelk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draining+Whelk).  

Record: 2-2


Game 5: hirohito\_0 (Slivers)  

I get turn-two [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu), turn-three [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), turn-four [Plaxcaster Frogling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling), turn five suspend [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth), turn six unsuspend, play [Cytoplast Root-Kin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Root-Kin), attack with an 8/8 Kudzu and a 15/15 Behemoth.  

Record: 3-2


I'll say this—when the deck works, it works fast. [Fungal Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Behemoth) virtually doubles the power I have on the board at the time, since almost all of my creatures are 0/0 dudes with +1/+1 counters.


To make the deck more explosive, I took out more engine pieces ([Reclaim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclaim) was nice, but hardly necessary to smash down on an opponent), and added in [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse)s (there are great with [Vinelasher Kudzu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vinelasher+Kudzu)—I play the Expanse, the Kudzu gets +1/+1, and I sacrifice the Expanse to get another land and another +1/+1 counter), [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) (to speed up the deck a turn, and to give me another creature for the deck), and a second [Spike Tiller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Tiller)—so far, I've been happy whenever I drew the first.


Out: 3 [Reclaim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reclaim), 1 [Gaea's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaea%27s+Blessing), 1 [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), 1 [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), 3 Search for Tommorrow  

In: 4 [Terramorphic Expanse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terramorphic+Expanse), 4 [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), 1 [Spike Tiller](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Tiller)

This deck would probably be improved somewhat by adding in countermagic ([Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)/[Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)) to stop a key spell around turn four or five at a key moment, and I'd probably take out the [Whirling Dervish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirling+Dervish)es to make room for that—but I've added in the 4-8 counterspells before, so you know how that works if you want to make that change!
