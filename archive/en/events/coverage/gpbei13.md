Zhou Brings Back Beijing!
=========================




May 10-12, 2014












Grand Prix Beijing 2013
-----------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/Champion-Shot.jpg)


Congratulations to Zhou Quan, your Grand Prix Beijing 2013 Champion. Steamrolling through the entire field of 894 with only one match loss all weekend, Zhou was able to retain the trophy on local soil by defeating Thailand's Kositaporn Nonthakorn in the finals.


Three other Chinese players also made the cut, but all fell in the quarterfinals, including World Team Champ and Grand Prix Shanghai Champ Li Bo, Nandi Zhang, and Shuo Li. Rounding off the Top 8 are Toshiya Kanegawa of Japan, Wong Wei Quan from Singapore, and Daniel Godfrey from Wales.


The ten guilds' history is a web of wars, intrigue, and political machinations as they have vied for control of Ravnica, and players will need to deftly maneuver past the challenges and perils of this brand new Sealed format. Will the ten guilds of Ravnica destroy each other or is there a chance of restoring the peace? Only time will tell!

Toshiya Kanegawa has been on a tear recently. After achieving Top 8s in Shanghai and Singapore, he has now come to Beijing in search for greater glory. Kanegawa flooded the board with [Goblin Rally](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Rally) tokens, but luckily Li had [Experiment One](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experiment+One) and [Gatecreeper Vine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine) to keep the tokens at home for a short moment.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/QF-LiBo.jpg)  
*Li Bo*

One more attack put Li at a life total low enough for Kanegawa to end things with a [Warleader's Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warleader%27s+Helix).


Kanegawa 1 – Li 0

*by Chapman Sim and Jim Jen*

 

Both players were playing in their very first Grand Prix Top 8 and were clearly very excited to be among the last 8 men standing. Wong Wei Quan hails from Singapore, who dropped by Beijing en route to attending the Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze* in San Diego. The first game was pretty lopsided as Wong kept an iffy hand with little action, possibly mana-screwed. Despite starting with [Foundry Street Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Street+Denizen), Wong soon found himself being overwhelmed by [Disciple of the Old Ways](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Old+Ways), [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark), [Opal Lake Gatekeepers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opal+Lake+Gatekeepers) (netting Godfrey a card, seeing that he laid Guildgates on turns one and three).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/QF2-Daniel-Godfrey.jpg)  
*Daniel Godfrey* 
When Wong finally reached five mana, he tried to create a blockade with [Towering Thunderfist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Thunderfist) but that plan was put on hold by [Inaction Injunction](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inaction+Injunction). He added two [Knight Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Watch) tokens to the board, hoping not to get crushed on the next turn.


However, [Lavinia of the Tenth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lavinia+of+the+Tenth) made an appearance and detained everything except the 4/4 Thunderfist, allowing Godfrey to reduce Wong down to one single life point and finishing him off the next turn with an all-out attack.


Godfrey 1 – Wong 0

![Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Stealer+of+Secrets)

Wong started poorly once again and found himself mana screwed. The situation looked dire, as Godfrey resolved [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets) and then coming in for a card.


Eventually, Wong managed to assemble a team of [Skyknight Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire), [Knight Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+Watch) and [Wojek Halberdiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wojek+Halberdiers), only to enter a brief stalemate against Godfrey's [Crocanura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crocanura) and [Opal Lake Gatekeepers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opal+Lake+Gatekeepers).

A [Foundry Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Champion) from Wong picked off the final creature and winning was merely a formality from that point.


Godfrey 1 – Wong 1

[Wojek Halberdiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wojek+Halberdiers) stared down at Godfrey's [Stealer of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stealer+of+Secrets), while Wong added a pair of [Nivix Cyclops](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nivix+Cyclops) and a very dangerous-looking [Court Street Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Street+Denizen).


Godfrey had [Opal Lake Gatekeepers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opal+Lake+Gatekeepers) but seemed to be in grave danger, since any instant or sorcery could transform Wong's board into a formidable force. True enough, Wong used [Martial Glory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Glory) as a "sorcery speed" [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) on [Wojek Halberdiers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wojek+Halberdiers), transforming both cyclopes into 4/4s before crashing into the red zone.

An [Azorius Arrester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Arrester) and [Dragonshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonshift) later, Wong Weiquan was off to the semifinals and Daniel Godfrey would have to be content with his great day thus far.

![Foundry Street Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Foundry+Street+Denizen)

### Game 3


Evasion is important. That has been the one undeniable truth of limited play for the past two decades. After Non's [Shambleshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambleshark) got zapped by [Electrickery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Electrickery), [Vassal Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vassal+Soul) and [Soulsworn Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulsworn+Spirit) started to wreck havoc on Wong's life total.


Despite having [Court Street Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Street+Denizen), [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) and [Rubblebelt Maaka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rubblebelt+Maaka), the Singaporean player was unable to race effectively. Non had [Pyrewild Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrewild+Shaman) to deter Wong's "[Hill Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hill+Giant)" and [Hands of Binding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hands+of+Binding) to paralyze [Bloodfray Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodfray+Giant) for good.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/SF-Non.jpg)  
*Kositaporn Nonthakorn* 
Wong tried to get back in the game with [Towering Thunderfist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Towering+Thunderfist) but what he really needed was a removal spell to end the ciphering madness. However, that was not to be and Wong eventually gave in to the pair of relentless spirits, ending his great run there and then. Good job regardless! Let's head to Pro Tour Theros together!


Nonthakorn 2 – Wong 1


**Kositaporn Nonthakorn defeats Wong Wei Quan to become (possibly) the first Thai player to reach the finals of a Grand Prix!**

Finals – Zhou Quan vs. Nonthakorn Kositaporn
--------------------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim and Jim Jen*

 

Yesterday, 894 hopefuls converged at Beijing, in the hopes of being crowned the Grand Prix Beijing Champion. Now, only 2 players remain and they are none other than local hero Zhou Quan as well as Thailand's Nonthakorn Kositaporn.


After a short break, both players starting shuffling up each others deck while rolling the dice, actions that will dictate the outcome of this final match.


### Game 1


Non had the fortune to begin first, but was cursed with a mulligan. Zhou quickly capitalized with [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) and [Stonefare Crocodile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonefare+Crocodile). Non used [Paralyzing Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paralyzing+Grasp) on [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) then using [Turn & Burn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn) to rid himself of the lifelinking croc.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/Finals-Non.jpg)  
*Nonthakorn Kositaporn* 
Zhou was not out of attackers though, and quickly refilled with [Haazda Snare Squad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Haazda+Snare+Squad) and a centaur token via [Alive & Well](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alive+%2F%2F+Well), but that was no match for Non's rare bomb, a [Molten Primordial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Primordial), which forced Kenning Apparition to sacrifice itself.


Non's next move was to cast and return [Krasis Incubation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krasis+Incubation) on [Molten Primordial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Primordial), turning it into an 8/6. Zhou shrugged it off with [Smite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite). Now that the meanest monster was out of the way, Zhou had no problems winning with the rest of his smaller dudes, including freshly engaged [Fencing Ace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace) and [Judge's Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Judge%27s+Familiar).


Zhou 1 - Nonthakorn 0


### Game 2


Zhou attempted to zone in upon Non with [Boros Mastiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Mastiff) and [Keening Apparition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Apparition) but Non's [Beetleform Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage) held them back when he passed turn four with no play. Zhou was happy to advance his board with [Selesnya Cluestone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Cluestone) and [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior), and was perfectly okay with Non having nothing to do.


Non suddenly had a change of heart and decided to race. He attacked with [Beetleform Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage) and used [Krasis Incubation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krasis+Incubation) on Zhou's [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior), temporarily a mere Grizzly Bear. However, he had Kenning Apparition to undo that and then enchanted the [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior) with [Gift of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Orzhova).


Trying to find an answer, Non dug with [Urban Evolution](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urban+Evolution), tapping out thereafter. Zhou pumped [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior), and attacked for a twelve point life swing, leaving Non at 5 life and himself at 27. Non was not at the end of the road though, as he managed clog up the skies (a rare feat!) with [Maze Glider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+Glider), [Runewing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runewing) and [Tower Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Drake), stopping the potentially 6/6 [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior) in its tracks.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpbei13/Finals-Zhou.jpg)  
*Zhou Quan* 
The back-breaker turned out to be [Alpha Authority](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alpha+Authority), which prevented the [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior) from being double or triple blocked. At the end of his line, Non tried one of the last tricks in his almost exhausted arsenal. Using [Catch & Release](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Catch+%2F%2F+Release), he inquired if he could gain control of [Alpha Authority](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alpha+Authority) in order to point [Turn & Burn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn) at the monster that was causing him so much anguish. Judges informed him that it was not possible because the [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior) still has hexproof, even though control of [Alpha Authority](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alpha+Authority) had switched control.


Zhou 2 – Kanegawa 1


**The writing was on the wall and Zhou Quan took a moment for the truth to sink in. He had just won Grand Prix Beijing.**

Top 5 Cards of Grand Prix Beijing
---------------------------------


*by Chapman Sim* 

 

 


![Boros Guildgate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Boros+Guildgate)


 

### 5. [Guildgates](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Guildgate)


How do you splash your best cards without clogging up your deck with "occasionally-clunky" Cluestones and Keyrunes? Playing with Guildgates rather than devoting precious slots in your deck means that you get to squeeze more goodies into your deck, while maintaining your land count at the average of 17. They also enhance cards like [Ogre Jailbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ogre+Jailbreaker), [Greenside Watcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenside+Watcher) and the entire cycle of Gatekeepers. Guildgates have been and will continue to be a vital cornerstone in the world of *Return to Ravnica* Limited, all day, all weekend and possibly all season. Until the arrival of Theros, of course.


  
  

 
 


![Turn // Burn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn)


 

### 4. [Turn & Burn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn)


Heralded as the most insane split card of the set, it is not hard to understand why. This card wins games and also assisted Thailand's Nonthakorn Kositaporn in securing a couple of games! Both halves serve as decent removal individually, but when fused, the advantage is overwhelming. Totally throwing off combat math by taking down two of your opponent's creatures, [Turn & Burn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+%2F%2F+Burn) also grants immense tempo while generating card advantage all at the same time! Great in a blue deck, great in a red deck, and totally awesome if you're the Izzet Mage.


  
  
  

 
 


![Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Putrefy)


 

### 3. [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy)


Historically speaking, cards the with text "destroy target creature" have always been prized for its sheer versatility. The ability to pick off an opposing bomb with no questions asked is a privilege that players were willing to spend their first picks on. Heck, even [Grisly Spectacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Spectacle) deserves an honorable mentions. Today, this [Murder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder) upgrade is better than ever, disallowing regeneration and provides the option to occasionally smash an opposing Cluestone or Keyrune.


  
  
  
  

 
 


![Beetleform Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Beetleform+Mage)


 

### 2. [Beetleform Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage)


The common consensus is that the format has become slower. The Simic player would love you to think that way as they ravage upon your life total with [Beetleform Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage), alongside [Cloudfin Raptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudfin+Raptor)s and other flyers. Left unchecked, the [Beetleform Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage) threatens to end the game in five turns, usually less in conjunction bloodrush pumps or direct damage. Difficult to kill and almost impossible to block profitably, [Beetleform Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beetleform+Mage) is one of the best bargains you can get in the world of *Dragon's Maze* limited.


  
  
  

 
 


![Gift of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gift+of+Orzhova)


 

### 1. [Gift of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Orzhova)


For the low low price of three mana, this nifty aura transforms an ordinary Grizzly Bear into an extraordinary racing machine, let alone when it's being stuck onto [Kraul Warrior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kraul+Warrior). [Gift of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Orzhova) played an instrumental role in helping Zhou Quan achieve victory and remains one of the best cards in the entire block. Regardless whether you're a beatdown player at heart trying to close the game quickly or a control player requiring a potent win condition, you won't be disappointed with possibly the best "Flight" ever printed.
