Round 4:  Three "Games"
=======================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 17, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 







[Gabriel Nassif (Rakdos) vs. Martin Jůza (Boros)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdgm13/fm2) 


Ben Stark (Boros Blitz) vs. Gaudenis Vidugiris (Bant Control)
-------------------------------------------------------------


[Samuele Estratti (UWR Control) vs. Uri Peleg (Azorius Control)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdgm13/fm5)




---

Records will indicate that Ben Stark beat Gaudenis Vidugiris in the fourth round of Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*. What they won't really show is that there wasn't much **Magic** actually being played.


A mulligan to five, a blisteringly fast start, and mana screw made for three equally quick and equally lopsided games as Stark's Boros Blitz deck easily bested Vidugiris' [Bant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant) Control list in three games.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/r4_stark_vidugiris.jpg)  
*Both Ben Stark and Gaudenis Vidugiris played through three very quick and very lopsided games of **Magic** in Round 4.* 
Well, three "games."


"Zero games of **Magic** in three. I guess I signed up for that when I played this deck," said Stark, who was battling this weekend with a nearly Mono-Red beatdown deck that prominently featured such Constructed all-stars as [Foundry Street Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Foundry+Street+Denizen), [Firefist Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firefist+Striker), and Gore-house Chainwalker.


The first game featured Stark sending one-land and zero-land hands back before settling on one that had two lands and two [Burn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burn)ing-Tree Emissaries. All it took for Vidugiris to clean that mess up was a [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and a [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). From there, it was mostly academic when [Ætherling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling) hit the table.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/r4_stark.jpg)  
*Stark agonizes over a first game that was completely out of his control.* 
The second game was a bit more exciting...for Stark.


Foundary Street Denizen into [Firefist Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firefist+Striker) into [Rakdos Cackler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Cackler) and a second Striker, all backed up by a [Legion's Initiative](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion%27s+Initiative), meant even [Gatecreeper Vine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatecreeper+Vine), [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter), and [Supreme Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) couldn't keep pace.


But at least he got to play his third land. In the third game? Not so much.


The Team SCG player made a show of it with three [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) to try and stymie another blazing fast start from Stark, but two [Legion Loyalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Loyalist)s blanked the tokens and made blocking virtually impossible. Though the ending took several turns for Stark to set up, Vidugiris never stood a chance with [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) and [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) stranded in his hand.


"You mull to five, I don't draw land three," Vidugiris said. "It happens."


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdgm13/r4_vidugiris.jpg)  
*While fortune favored Vidugiris in the first game, the same could not be said for the second or the third game.* 
Stark said after the match that, while he had no idea how the match should play out should actual games of Magic happen, as Team ChannelFireball hadn't really paid much attention to [Bant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bant),, there were some scary cards in Vidugiris' deck. [Loxodon Smiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) and [Centaur Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) are both bigger than the red deck can typically handle and, without [Firefist Striker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firefist+Striker), Stark said he basically can't attack into 3/3s or bigger.


That, at least, is part of the reason why Stark and others chose to splash [Boros Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) and [Frontline Medic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frontline+Medic), giving them more ways to get through giant blockers. Still, he estimated the matchup verses Selesnya decks was no better than 50/50.


The matchup he did like, however, was Esper, which is certainly out in force this weekend. In fact, Stark chose the deck precisely because he knew Esper would be the most played deck on the weekend (and it is), and the Boros Blitz deck is very well favored against it, despite how Stark feels about some of the individual cards in the deck.


"Our creatures are some of the worst I've ever played in a Constructed tournament," Stark said. "I'm just dumping a bunch of stuff on the board and just hoping."


**Match notes:** There were three ties on the dice rolls to start the match. Stark eventually won. Then he was forced to mulligan to five...before the match, Stark said the following:


"Every time we finish the draft, I'm just so mad. I just feel so advantaged in Draft, then I have to play a stupid Constructed deck. I just want to draft again."


Vidugiris' response?


"I have to say, I feel just the opposite."


Ben Stark's lifetime Limited winning percentage is slightly higher than his Constructed win percentage but significantly higher than his Block Constructed lifetime win percentage. He wins roughly 67 percent of his professional-level Booster Draft matches. Vidugiris, on the other hand, is much better in Constructed versus Limited, and significantly better in Block, where he, amusingly enough, wins roughly 67 percent of his matches.
