Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Jason Simard's Mono-White Eldrazi and Taxes](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpvan17/top-8-decklists-2017-02-19)

I have multiple reasons for showing off this Modern list from [Grand Prix Vancouver](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpvan17) last month. And they're all fantastic reasons. Just the best reasons.


For one, the Masters of Modern just revealed this morning that [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor), a cog in this hateful wheel, is being reprinted in *Amonkhet*. Simard only played one in the main and one in the side, but the "gotcha" feeling of [Aven Mindcensor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Mindcensor) is really high, especially in conjunction with [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter).


For two, we have a [Team Unified Modern Grand Prix this weekend in San Antonio](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/premierplay/grandprix/fact-sheet-gpsa17), and it's worth noting that this deck is both powerful and steals very few cards other decks might want. [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) is played in many places, [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) gets used a bit, [Eldrazi Temple](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Temple) goes in other Eldrazi decks, and [Mutavault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) is played by Merfolk—but that's about it. It sucks up some powerful sideboard cards ([Stony Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence) and [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace)), but any white deck would do that.


Finally, also note how many of these cards are in *Modern Masters 2017 Edition*. [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), [Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp), and even the sideboarded [Stony Silence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stony+Silence) and [Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage).


Given all of this, I wouldn't be surprised to see this deck pop up now and then in San Antonio.
