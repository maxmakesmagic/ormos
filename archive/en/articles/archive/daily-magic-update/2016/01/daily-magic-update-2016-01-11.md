The Jan. 11, 2016 Update
========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on January 11, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know for today, January 11, 2016. Today's Update is brought to you by [the number 64](#number9).


Today's Must See
================


1. [SCGCharlotte](http://www.starcitygames.com/events/090116_charlotte.html) | The Charlotte Open | StarCityGames.com

While no one in the Top 8 was playing 64 cards, there was plenty of Modern action ahead of Pro Tour *Oath of the Gatewatch* at the Charlotte Open. Brian Huffman won with Jund, but the Heartless Eldrazi deck shown in this video deck tech was certainly the talk of the tournament.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/czkYqzv_ftg>


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [Decks Inspired by *Oath of the Gatewatch*](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/abesargent-011116-decks-inspired-by-oath-of-the-gatewatch/)  | Gathering Magic | Abe Sargent

If ever a brewer could brew, how many brews could a brew brewer brew? Abe Sargent runs through three decks inspired by new *Oath of the Gatewatch* cards. Mostly casual, all a blast (one, literally).


1. [*Oath of the Gatewatch* Spoilers](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLGsoeNcK9QA-OZL-1MrzFo72t0SSQPi7D)  | The Mana Source | Wedge

The Mana Source is going all out for Oath of the Gatewatch now that the full set is live. Follow along on the [preview playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLGsoeNcK9QA-OZL-1MrzFo72t0SSQPi7D), as Wedge is uploading a new video about the new set roughly every three minutes or so (note: actual times not remotely close to every three minutes).


1. [*Magic: The Gathering*—*Zendikar Expeditions*](http://muddycolors.blogspot.com/2016/01/magic-gathering-zendikar-expeditions.html)  | Muddy Colors | Howard Lyon

This art-focused feature popped up on Friday, but the Daily *Magic* Update didn't exist then. Plus we were all either busy pouring over the *Oath of the Gatewatch* [Card Image Gallery](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/card-image-gallery/oathofthegatewatch) anyway or mentally preparing ourselves for the Minnesota Vikings [to suffer yet another heartbreaking playoff loss](http://espn.go.com/nfl/story/_/id/14543646/tearful-blair-walsh-fg-miss-ends-vikings-season-my-fault), so I can't blame you for missing this. I can blame [Blair Walsh](http://espn.go.com/blog/nflnation/post/_/id/195511/blair-walsh-is-human-so-lets-treat-him-that-way) for missing that field goal, however. Whatever, I'm bitter.


Still, Lyon goes through his process for creating three of the *Expeditions* released with *Oath of the Gatewatch*: [Mana Confluence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Confluence), [Strip Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strip+Mine), and [Ancient Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Tomb). They're pretty.


1. [Todd VS. The Boss: Esper Control VS. The Gatewatch](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/32189_Todd-VS-The-Boss-Esper-Control-VS-The-Gatewatch.html) |StarCityGames.com | Todd Anderson and Tom Ross (PREMIUM SUBSCRIPTION REQUIRED)

Todd is playing Esper Con—blah, blah, blah. The real reason you're watching this is to check out Tom Ross' Oath-fueled Naya deck with not one, not two, but *three* Oaths featured, plus a quartet of planeswalkers (a fifth in Nissa, Vastwood Seer even). This is a premium article, but it's worth a view if you're a subscriber.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. Ben Rubin's 64-card Abzan Blue

Reid Duke might have won [Grand Prix Oakland](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpoak16) by expertly piloting a Four-Color Rally the Ancestors deck, but the talk of the tournament was definitely Hall of Fame Pro Ben Rubin playing 64 cards...because? Whatever Rubin's reason, he finished second with Abzan Blue plus a few. Now that's old school.
