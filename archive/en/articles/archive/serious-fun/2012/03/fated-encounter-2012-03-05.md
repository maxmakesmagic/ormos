The Fated Encounter
===================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on March 6, 2012 










It wasn't long ago that I was at [Grand Prix Baltimore](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpbal12/welcome) and felt like Ke$ha: I was heading out for a good night and boys were blowing up my phone. Technology I only understood as science fiction in episodes of *Star Trek: The Next Generation* is today an everyday convenience, and my "smart phone" lets me do very dumb things, like put out a general call for Commander games at a Grand Prix with attendance in excess of 1,500 players (not counting those who didn't register for the main event).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_tweet.jpg)  
It turns out there are quite a few players who enjoy Commander at these things. Who knew!

I apologize to everyone who rolled over to get in a game but found I was already full; keep heading to Grand Prix and I promise you'll have a another chance to smash me. I know I'm planning to go!

It must have been fate, or clever Twitter monitoring, that brought two players to me. It was time for [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores), and the cumulative effort of the readers to Serious Fun, to take Commander to task.

Battle!

Through the Fires and Flames
As you recalled, the [final winner in voting](http://staging.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/sf/182) was a [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) deck with a strong sacrifice theme layered onto a powerful mana base built from nonbasic lands.

This deck has plenty of mana, plenty of token producers, and plenty of ways to make those extra creatures into everything from extra cards to [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade)s. I love it, as I should: I built the pieces that were merged together to form it!

While decks are awesome, Commander for me is more often about the players I'm enjoying the game with.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_amanda.jpg)  
Amanda, the [@sagegnosis](https://twitter.com/#!/sagegnosis) of Twitter, was packing a very focused blue deck, powered up by Commander [Teferi, Mage of Zhalfir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Mage+of+Zhalfir). When asked if the deck could counter spells, Amanda merely shrugged, giggled, and feigned ignorance.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_jorge.jpg)  
Jorge, of the less imaginative [@jorke550](https://twitter.com/#!/jorke550) of Twitter, brought one of my favorite legendary creatures to the party: [Edric, Spymaster of Trest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edric%2C+Spymaster+of+Trest). I don't know where Trest is or why Edric is the spymaster, but the prospect of producing a bucket of tokens to pin-prick opponents on my way to drawing lots of cards was appealing.

**Aside**I have a brief aside. When I created the initial Commander voting I assumed I had a spare copy of both [Ghave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) and [Vish Kal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vish+Kal%2C+Blood+Arbiter). Of course, this proved untrue. Thanks to everyone at the convention center Friday and Saturday who helped me find both!

Our game started like so many Commander games: Amanda and I hit all our early land drops as Jorge, starting strong with a [Leyline of Anticipation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Anticipation), sputtered on two for a few turns, although he had a [Lightning Greaves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) to show for his effort. At five lands or so, I decided to start easy by casting [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores). After a moment of consideration, Amanda played the old-school original [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell).

Amanda had some justification for the all-stop on Ghave. "I kinda feel bad but I'm trying to remember what's in your deck. I'm pretty sure he's bad for me."

"He probably is," I admitted with a shrug.

Jorge hit a third land and didn't wait to use it by snapping down his [Edric, Spymaster of Trest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edric%2C+Spymaster+of+Trest), slapping [Lightning Greaves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves) onto it, and poking Amanda for 2 to draw a card. Amanda and I both didn't play anything on our turns, so Jorge got to repeat it again immediately before he cast a [Rofellos, Llanowar Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rofellos%2C+Llanowar+Emissary). Amanda tried to [Hinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hinder) it, but Jorge busted out an unexpected [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_stop.jpg)  
Just like that, mana was no longer a problem for Jorge!

"Oh, it's on now!" Amanda warned Jorge.

Amanda continued to just play a land and pass to me, but with my seventh land in seven turns I tried for Ghave again. Success!

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_mana.jpg)  
Apparently, lots of mana is a good thing.

At the end of my turn, Jorge added a [Trygon Predator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator) to his team, then used it to take a flying stab at me. Amanda, having been burned losing the counterspell war, leapt in with a [Venser, Shaper Savant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venser%2C+Shaper+Savant) to bounce the Trygon, slowing down Jorge.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

Amanda wheeled to me quickly, so I just kept on the path of the first part of our plan and made some tokens via [Hornet Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen). At the end of my turn again, Jorge popped out a [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), then tried for a [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction).

And it was on.

With Jorge tapped out, Amanda's response of [Withdraw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withdraw) seemed surreptitiously timely—sending Solemn and Edric to hand—and the follow-up [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) for one of the counterspells in the graveyard sent the [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) packing. Jorge just passed his turn to Amanda, who only added a [Nevinyrral's Disk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk) to the battlefield. After poking both of them with my evasive tokens and their flying [Queen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet+Queen), I cast a [Woodfall Primus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodfall+Primus) that resolved to snuff out Jorge's Lighting Greaves.

At the end of my turn, Jorge dropped down the three bounced creatures: [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum); [Trygon Predator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator); and [Edric, Spymaster of Trest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edric%2C+Spymaster+of+Trest). His main phase featured a newfound [Rhystic Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Study), then a quick attack at Amanda. While the [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) traded with Solemn, the Trygon hit home and took out the [Disk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disk).

| --- | --- |

But when Jorge went to end his turn I had a surprise: thanks to an abundance of mana I was able to sacrifice [Woodfall Primus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodfall+Primus) twice (by taking a +1/+1 counter off Ghave to create a Saproling, only to immediately sacrifice the Saproling to put a +1/+1 counter on [Woodfall Primus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodfall+Primus) to cancel out the –1/–1 counter from persist), powering through Jorge's [Stifle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle) to take out his [Leyline of Anticipation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Anticipation).

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_iWillAlways.jpg)  
At this point, having enough mana and tokens to power it through, I could have used [Woodfall Primus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodfall+Primus) to chew through lots of my opponents' lands. I chose not to, and instead selectively picked apart things I found annoying (although I will *always* pay for an opponent's [Rhystic Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Study)). Having the power and capability to do something doesn't mean you always should, even if it seems like it would win you the game.

I wanted to play an awesome game, and I set the tone by leaving their resources relatively untouched; I only demolished little engines that pulled players ahead. "With great power comes great responsibility," Uncle Ben once told Peter Parker, and it's worked out pretty well for our friendly neighborhood web crawler. 

Amanda added only an innocuous [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist) and let me take a turn to poke them both again—Jorge to 25 and Amanda to 26. And with Edric in play I got to draw cards too (Thanks, Spymaster!) before closing the turn by recycling the [Woodfall Primus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodfall+Primus) to take out [Rhystic Study](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Study).

Jorge had some other tricks in mind, and at the end of my turn he cast [Worldly Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Worldly+Tutor) for [Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx). It was the obvious and immediate play on his turn.

Passing to Amanda, a devious plot was set into motion. With a tap of [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist), everyone drew seven cards—the number in Amanda's hand—followed by Jorge choosing to draw a full twenty-eight *more* thanks to the Sphinx.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_hand1.jpg)  
But Amanda had also discarded a [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) to [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist). One [Argivian Restoration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argivian+Restoration) later and Jorge was under a [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver) for his following turn. I had drawn into [Crop Rotation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crop+Rotation), so I transformed something into [Volrath's Stronghold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath%27s+Stronghold), which let me put my discarded [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) on top of my library.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_restore.jpg)  
Not surprisingly, [Prime Time](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) drew out a [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) from the hand-flush Jorge, which also returned [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) to my hand. By tapping out, though, I knew I could swing through without worry, and Jorge was left perched at a precarious 15 life. I added a [Spawning Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit) before letting the turn go.

Amanda then took over for the turn [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver)ed (It's a verb, really!) from Jorge. With an eye on naughty and nice things, Amanda did quite a job of making the most of the "extra" turn:


> * Played [Reliquary Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reliquary+Tower) (nice)
> * Cast [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) to copy the [Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) (nice)
> * Cast [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) (nice), and equipped Rofellos, Llanowar Emissary (naughty)
> * Cast Mystical Tutor to put [Bribery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bribery) on top of Jorge's library (nice)
> * Played [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) (nice), equipped [Trygon Predator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator) (nice), and struck at Adam (nice... for Jorge)
> * After combat, cast [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) (naughty) and [Mystic Snake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) (naughty), as well as Yavimaya Elder (nice) that was immediately equipped by [Skullclamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullclamp) (naughty and nice)
> * Final Naughty to Nice Count: four to nine
> 

The turn then went to Amanda, who finally brought in [Teferi, Mage of Zhalfir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Mage+of+Zhalfir), followed by [Knowledge Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knowledge+Pool) and an activation of [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist). I responded by cycling the [Woodfall Primus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodfall+Primus) through the [Spawning Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit) to ensure the [Knowledge Pool](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knowledge+Pool) was left unknown.

Now, if you recall, there were more than thirty cards still in Jorge's hand, and [Jace's Archivist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%27s+Archivist)'s ability to discard then draw the highest number is mandatory, unlike triggers from the Sphinx. While Amanda and Jorge had huge hands...

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_hand2.jpg)  
![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_hand3.jpg)  
Jorge actually had to draw more cards than there were left in his library. *Boom*! Jorge was down! At the end of Amanda's turn I used my Stronghold to recycle [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) to the top of my library—and therefore to my hand on my turn.

But the game was about to end expeditiously.

With a furrowed brow and evil grin, Amanda declared I would lose on the next turn, but I had my turn left to figure out a way for my own win. With about thirty-odd cards in hand to look through, I found two of the most powerful I could have in the situation: [Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed) and [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites).

As Amanda had said earlier, "It was on."

I cast [Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed). Amanda responded with [Gush](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush), then responded to that with [Mystical Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Tutor) to put [Force of Will](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) on top of Amanda's library. Mikaeus was countered.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
|  |  |

I then cast [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), targeting the countered Mikaeus. Amanda rifled through thirty or so cards in hand and introduced me to [Thwart](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thwart). [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) was countered.

Do you remember how I said I was hitting my mana every turn and had an abundance to work with? That didn't stop happening, so I had enough to use the flashback on [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites). Amanda looked at a bunch of tapped lands, shifted through the massive hand looking for another free counter spell ([Pact of Negation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation), perhaps?), then frowned.

[Mikaeus, the Unhallowed](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mikaeus%2C+the+Unhallowed) entered the battlefield.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_power.jpg)  
With the threat of doom looming overhead, I decided to pull the trigger on a Mikaeus-based combo I shared a few weeks ago. Cycling [Woodfall Primus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodfall+Primus) through [Spawning Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Pit), with Mikaeus in play, I chose to meet assured doom with overpowering force, and wiped away all of Amanda's noncreature permanents.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/sf/sf185_end.jpg)  
Unable to capitalize on the massive hand without any mana, Amanda knew the jig was up. My follow-through attack forced Amanda to block with what few creatures were left, and my next turn would have sealed the deal. *Victory!*

The Pick of Destiny
If you're wondering whether [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) is an unbeatable machine, have no fear—the obligatory follow-up game the next day featured Amanda again, as well as Andrew ([@a\_magrini](http://twitter.com/#!/a_magrini)) and Omar ([@itomarhernandez](http://twitter.com/itomarhernandez)). Despite a first-turn [Burgeoning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burgeoning) for Andrew, and plenty of token making on my own part, Omar crafted an incredible battlefield before wringing our lands out of play through [Keldon Firebombers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Firebombers).

Nice job, Omar!

All said, I really enjoy this deck we've made together, and I'm pleased to have seen several (okay, many) suggestions and adaptations from readers who also enjoy putting Ghave in command. Thanks for making this awesome!
