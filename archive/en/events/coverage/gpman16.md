Manchester Makes Six for Lévy
=============================




Playing Green-White Tokens in Standard, Hall of Famer Raphaël Lévy defeated all comers, including fellow Frenchman Julien Henry and his Bant Company in the finals, to become champion of Grand Prix Manchester 2016: his sixth GP win!

TOP 8 COVERAGE



When the 1,697 players sat down for their first matches this weekend, Raphaël Lévy was arguably the most accomplished of them already. In a career spanning multiple decades, he had previously reached the Top 8 of 20 Grand Prix events and won five. By the end of the tournament, both of these numbers had increased by one.


Lévy's monumental achievement was but one of the great stories to come out of the weekend. Standard, for instance, continued to impress with the variety of viable decks: (7) Martin Müller made the Top 32 with a brand new Jace's Sanctum deck, while Matthew Hunt even earned a Top 8 berth with Blue-Red Ulamog. And Lévy, the champion himself, surprised lots of people with Chandra, Flamecaller in a deck with zero sources of red mana but four copies of Oath of Nissa.


Congratulations to Raphaël Lévy, champion of Grand Prix Manchester 2016!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Welcome back to Grand Prix Manchester! The tournament had started with a massive 1,697 players yesterday, but barely 30 percent of them made it through the first day's nine grueling rounds with the requisite record of 6-3 or better to qualify for the second. In fact, just 482 returned today to fight for a spot in the Top 8, including luminaries such as Mattia Rizzi and Lukas Blohon at 9-0, Raphaël Lévy at 8-0-1, Antonio Del Moral León, Magnus Lantto, Florian Koch, Pierre Dagen, and Christoffer Larsen at 8-1.


Today would see another six rounds of Standard, followed by the Top 8 playoffs. Also, with regards to our ongoing coverage: more deck features, the Top 100 metagame breakdown, and many, many decklists. So check back throughout the day for text updates straight from the battlefield of Grand Prix Manchester 2016!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



After nine rounds of Standard, an original field of 1,697 had been reduced to a mere 489. Everyone with a record of 6-3 or better qualified for the second day of competition and would return in the morning, for another six rounds of Standard, for a shot at the Top 8, for points and prizes, fame and fortune.


Overnight, six players stood atop the standings with perfect records of 9-0: Lewis McLeod, Yuri Stroilov, Lauri Vuorela, Luke May, as well as Mattia Rizzi and Lukas Blohon, who had previously reached the Top 8 at three and five Grand Prix events, respectively. These six would have a head start over the competition tomorrow. However, the likes of Hall of Famer Raphaël Lévy, Pro Tour champion Antonio Del Moral León, and MOCS champion Magnus Lantto were close behind (all within the current Top 16, in fact) and would certainly do their best to catch up with or surpass them on the hunt for a Top 8 berth.


Meanwhile, Standard once again demonstrated why it's such a popular Constructed format. Just to mention the truly unusual, we saw seasoned pros summon Dragonlord Ojutai off of Shaman of Forgotten Ways and cast Part the Waterveil with the help of Jace's Sanctum. One can only imagine what we might find tomorrow!


So check back then for more Standard tech, for all the news straight from the battlefield, and for the epic conclusion of Grand Prix Manchester 2016!
