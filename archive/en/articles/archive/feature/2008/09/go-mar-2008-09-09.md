In celebration of the impending release of *Shards of Alara,* Daily Decks looks at past creations that combined the colors of **Magic** into those of the shards found in Alara. An early champion of Esper, Pro Tour Hall of Fame member Mike Turian played this blue-black-white Go Mar list to the Top 8 of the 2001 Barcelona Masters Series. Using familiars of the Sun and Nightscape persuasion to make counterspells and removal like [Recoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recoil) and [Exclude](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude) cost less, Mike attempted to stall his opponent long enough to deliver the coup de grace: [Dromar, the Banisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromar%2C+the+Banisher). The gigantic 6/6 not only flew over his opponent's defenses to bash for a third of their life total, but also allowed him to pay ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161225105850im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless2.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) in order to bounce all creatures of a chosen color, potentially nullifying his opponent's entire team! Check out a *Shards of Alara* preview card from Esper [here](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/riddle-sphinx-2008-09-09) and make sure to visit the *Shards of Alara* [product section](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/products/shardsofalara).
