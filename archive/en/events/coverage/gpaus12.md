Levy's Tempo Delivers the Beats in Austin
=========================================




January 7-8












Grand Prix Austin 2012
----------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/Levy%20Trophy%20Shot.JPG)Raphael Levy is the Grand Prix Austin Champion!


There must be something in the water in Austin that invigorates old-school pros. Like Brian Kibler at Pro Tour Austin, the Live Music Capital of the World was good to Raphael Levy.


Levy has been chasing after the lifetime Pro Points record and now finds himself 8 points closer, up to 494. He's only five points behind Olivier Ruel for second and a mere six points behind Kai Budde himself, the only player to hit 500 lifetime points so far. And he did it all clawing his way back from a 7-2 Day 1 performance.


It was a wild weekend as a number of named pros made late runs at the Top 8, only to fall in the last few rounds. It will be remembered for another dominant performance from a ChannelFireball team member – this time David Ochoa – and *Innistrad* sealed's Swan Song.


But more than anything we very well could look back on this tournament and Levy's performance as the start of his run to the very top of the Pro Points mountain.

Quarterfinal - Pat Cox vs. Jason Howden
---------------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen*

 

This Top 8 draft resulted in a number of very powerful decks across a variety of archetypes. GW Aggro, BR Revmoval, UW Tempo, UB Control, Red-based aggro and BW were all represented, plus a GU good-stuffs kind of deck that eschews milling for a slew of powerful cards. It seems everyone in the Top 8 got the memo that good cards are good (\*cough\* [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper), [Curse of Death's Hold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+Death%27s+Hold), [Bloodgift Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodgift+Demon)\*cough\*), but synergy might just be better.


The Pat Cox versus Jason Howden match pitted Cox's traditional UB self-mill, Skaab deck against Jason Howden's nontraditional GUr deck that packed number of interesting – and dangerous – cards.


**Game 1** 


Cox tried to build an early advantage with a [Curiosity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curiosity) on a [Stromkirk Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Patrol), but a timely [Spidery Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidery+Grasp) stopped it from going too far. After that Cox started attacking with a bunch of 1/x creatures – [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab)s and [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab) – before the board stalled out. With Cox at 6 and Howden at 7, Cox attacked with nearly everything, including an [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul).


When a splashed [Pitchburn Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pitchburn+Devils) blocked the Ghoul, the three damage to Cox stacked on top of the Ghoul's lifegain trigger and Howden used that window of opportunity to fire a Morbid [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) at Cox for the win.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/QF%20Jason%20H..JPG)
*Jason Howden* 

**Game 2**


Game two was a battle of undercosted zombies as Howden attempted to race Cox's [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) with a [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler). However, Cox was able to triple-up on the decedent of Serendib Effreet with a [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart). While [Spidery Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidery+Grasp) was enough to kill one of the Drakes, Howden couldn't deal with the other two and couldn't quite race thanks to [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) holding down the ground.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/QF%20Pat%20Cox.JPG)
*Pat Cox* 

**Game 3** 


Howden sat on two lands for several turns, even forced to discard from his three-color deck.


But hold on, it wasn't that simple.


Cox had very little pressure, as he could only attack with a [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse) and the least imposing [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab) he could muster. Howden even used his discards to fuel a [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) and kill the [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse).


"It's a 10 turn clock!" Cox said, attacking again with the Crab.


But now that the Corpse was, well, a Corpse, he was able to use the spare parts to animate a [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake). And when an [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) flipped over a ton of business, Howden finally packed it in.


Pat Cox defeats Jason Howden 2-1


Craig Edwards defeats Austin Bursavich 2-0



 

Quarterfinal - Austin Bursavich vs. Craig Edwards
-------------------------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen*
On paper, this match looked pretty lopsided. Bursavich was on the mill plan – not milling himself, but milling his opponent with multiple [Curse of the Bloody Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Curse+of+the+Bloody+Tome)s and such – and only some moderate removal. Edwards, meanwhile, had a strong green white deck with a bunch of tricks and undercosted creatures.


**Game 1** 



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/QF%20Austin%20Bursavich.JPG)
*Austin Bursavich* 

Craig Edwards has a pretty nasty green white build that can curve right out to put more pressure on control decks than they can rightfully deal with. That was exactly what happened as he played [Selfless Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Cathar) into [Hamlet Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamlet+Captain) into Avacyn Priest and followed with an [Ambush Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Viper).


Bursavich fought back with some powerful cards like [Sturmgeist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sturmgeist) and [Battleground Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battleground+Geist), but a [Smite the Monstrous](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite+the+Monstrous) let Avacyn Priest dominate the table. Eventually a [Festerhide Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festerhide+Boar) started taking large bites out of Bursavich and led Edwards to victory.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/QF%20Craig%20Edwards.JPG)
*Craig Edwards* 

**Game 2**


Game two went much the same. Edwards jumped out to a fast start with Avacyn Priest, [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) and [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut). Bursavich was on the back foot right away, and even though a [Claustrophobia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claustrophobia) locked out the Avacyn's Priest, [Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Juggernaut) eventually became too much for Bursavich to handle, and he was run over by a bevy of creatures from Edwards' menagerie.



Quarterfinal - Eric Downing vs. David Ochoa
-------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*
"So how many Top 8s does this make for you," Downing asked as he presented his Draft deck to Ochoa.


"Um," Ochoa paused as he cocked his head in thought. "I think this makes three."


"That equals the number of Grand Prix I've played in," Downing casually grinned.


**Game 1**


Downing got on the board first with a [Traveler's Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveler%27s+Amulet) followed by an [Ashmouth Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashmouth+Hound). Not wanting to fall too far behind, Ochoa added a [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth) to his side. Downing had a double-faced card of his own on the third turn, and his [Village Ironsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Ironsmith) came down in his human form, just waiting for a lull in the action to transform. Ochoa chose to turn his little girl into a little monster, electing to keep his gaga horror at home. *by Blake Rasmussen*
With the new Organized Play changes, making the Top 4 of a Grand Prix automatically qualifies you for the next Pro Tour. So Raphael Levy and Eric Downing had to be pretty happy to be here, right?


The answer is of course they're thrilled to make it to these rarified heights in a more than 1,000-man tournament, but each had already punched their ticket to Hawaii, Downing through Planeswalker Points and Levy through, well, being Raphael Levy and being in the Hall of Fame. His performance this weekend was opening the door for him not only to cross 500 lifetime Pro Points this season (he needed only 14 before the tournament started), but to take over the all-time lead in Pro Points from none other than Kai Budde.


Just as impressively, Levy started the day 7-2 and hadn't dropped a match all day.


Downing didn't like his deck much, but he had put together a very fast, very aggressive mostly red deck that had already finished off David Ochoa's BombRare.dec in the quarterfinals.


Levy, as was his style this weekend, was in white and aggressive. He was using two [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) and [Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk) to pace his UW tempo deck through the Top 8.


**Game 1**


Levy started the game with an [Unruly Mob](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unruly+Mob) that was quickly overshadowed by Downing's [Ashmouth Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashmouth+Hound). The Frenchman played an [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar) that could get out of hand if allowed to buttress the Mob and followed it up with an [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin). Griffin had been much maligned as being mediocre, but they were serving Levy well this weekend.


Downing's aggressive UR deck started spitting out creatures too. [Feral Ridgewolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Ridgewolf) joined his side of the field just before [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) dug deep. He binned a [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice) among the cards, digging for the [Traveler's Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveler%27s+Amulet) he played.


Levy, however, upped the ante with a [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler)s and a [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice). The Apprentice left unchecked could turn the Mob into a monster (somewhat ironically).


Aware of that fact, Downing used [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) to kill the Mob before the Apprentice became active. However, it still had both a [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) and an [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar) to work with. Levy tried another [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin), but lost it to [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley).


[Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) was another haymaker for Downing, as it made using the Apprentice difficult at best.


Still, Downing respected the little apprentice enough to destroy it with [Into the Maw of Hell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Maw+of+Hell). Levy turned it into a Homunculus and kept his advantage on the board, even if he lost one of his tricks.


[Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms) cleared out the [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) and Levy took the opportunity to attack with the Griffin, Homunculus and [Elder Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Cathar).


Downing recast the [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) and used the opportunity to attack Levy down. But that sinking feeling Downing got when he went to declare his attackers was the [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) the Hall of Famer flashed back.


[Silent Departure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Departure) made a go of it with Edwards at two life, but a second [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) shut that plan down.


Levy 1 – Downing 0


**Game 2**


This time, it was Downing's turn to be the aggressor. [Ashmouth Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashmouth+Hound) and [Rakish Heir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakish+Heir) came down early, but had to face Levy's [Chapel Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chapel+Geist).


Levy refused to trade the Geist for just the Hound and Downing continued to curve out, using his four mana to play both a second Hound and a [Village Ironsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Ironsmith). With four creatures on the board, all of which could be dangerous attackers, Edwards was putting some serious pressure on Levy, and an [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin) was not going to change that.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/SF%20Eric%20Downing.JPG)  
*Downing got aggressive to take down Game 2* 
It was so ineffectual, in fact, Downing actually just attacked with all four of his creatures the next turn, including his still 1/1 [Village Ironsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Ironsmith). He was either representing a trick or trying to get in as much damage as possible while he could. Neither option was very good for Levy.


[Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) on both ends allowed Downing to kill the [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin) and [Chapel Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chapel+Geist) at the expense of his [Rakish Heir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakish+Heir), all while getting in four damage.


Levy, still on the back foot, could only play [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) to trade with a hound.


[Unruly Mob](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unruly+Mob) quickly died to a [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame), and once Downing was done with his turn, Levy only had an [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) facing down a [Village Ironsmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Ironsmith), [Ashmouth Hound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ashmouth+Hound) and a [Skirsdag Cultist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+Cultist), all while sitting at 8 life. He just happen to also be stuck on four lands with a Geist Honored Monk in hand. When he didn't see any roads to victory, he chose to move on to game three, with a number of cards languishing in his hand.


Levy 1 – Downing 1


**Game 3**


Downing played an early [Bloodcrazed Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodcrazed+Neonate), looking to get aggressive again, but Levy made it look pretty silly with a [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit).


The [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit) continued to rule the roost, as it flew past a [Lantern Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Spirit) that Downing used to trade with a [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) and keep Levy off any crazy combos.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/SF%20Levy.JPG)  
*Levy has been dominant today, storming back from a 7-2 Day 1 to make the finals.* 
[Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) revealed both [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) and [Think Twice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice), but Levy trumped either of those possible plays with [Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk).


[Silent Departure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Departure) and Geistfame cut down on Levy's creature count, but he reloaded with [Unruly Mob](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unruly+Mob) and a [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger). The attack for 10 dropped Downy all the way to four, and when he couldn't muster a defense the following turn, he extended his hand and wished Levy good luck in the finals.


Raphael Levy defeats Eric Downing 2-1 and moves on to the finals




Semifinals Pat Cox vs. Craig Edwards
------------------------------------


*by Marc Calderaro*
Craig Edwards was very excited to hear that David Ochoa lost his Quarterfinal match-up. "He was my only loss on the weekend. I lost to him twice." Edwards had before shown signs of fatigue, but since hearing about Ochoa, he'd perked up.


"I was his only loss," Pat Cox countered. He wore a huge grin.


**Game 1**


Pat Cox drew first blood with [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse), and following the carnage trail was an [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul). Craig Edwards evened the score at 18 a piece, then cast [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) on his opponent's end-step.


"Saw that one coming," Cox quipped.


"Why didn't you just blindly attack into it like all my other opponents?" Edwards took out Cox's fresh [Stromkirk Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Patrol) with a [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter). He then used a [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) plus a few extra points of combat damage to take out the Ghoul. A [Festerhide Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festerhide+Boar) feasted on the corpse and then fed on the Walking variety in the red zone, further dwindling Cox's creature base.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/SF%20Craig%20Edwards.JPG)  
*Craig Edwards is off to an early lead* 
Edwards's Boar, [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit), Bell-Ringer, [Darkthicket Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkthicket+Wolf) and [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) stared down a [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant), but they all backed off as soon as the monstrous, grotesque [Skaab Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Goliath) showed up. All, that is, but the flyer, who made the totals 7-16 in Edwards' favor.


Cox was fighting hard, but he was drawing too much land. He wasn't going to be able to hold off the alpha strike for long. A [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) was added so the Goliath couldn't block, and Cox could no longer hold off. It was very sad. All the Goliath wanted to do was play. Instead, he was solemnly picked up off the battlefield along with Cox's other cards as he prepared for the second game.


Craig Edwards 1 – 0 Pat Cox


**Game 2**


Cox started off again, this time with a [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse) and an [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab), binning an [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler), a [Dead Weight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) and a [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab). [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) joined them on turn four as a [Corpse Lunge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Lunge) killed off Edwards's [Darkthicket Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkthicket+Wolf). Edwards had a [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) and a 1/1 [Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot). It was a defensive early game with Cox ahead 18-17.


"You're bleeding me, bro," Edwards said as he took one per turn. He dropped a [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) and passed it back.


[Sensory Deprivation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensory+Deprivation), [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart) and Ghoulcaller's Chant were part of a stacked hand for Cox. He cast an end-of-turn [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to recur the [Corpse Lunge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Lunge), but Edwards was waiting for it, playing [Spidery Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidery+Grasp) to save his Villagers. The 2/3 soon flipped into its bigger form, but then hit the bin to more removal.


The scores were still high and both players were jockeying for position. The [Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) had slowly grown to 3/3 and Cox's board had slowly grown, thanks in part to [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart), into a bevy of Blue and Black dorks that couldn't attack, but made for a great defense. Excluding a mountain of land, Edwards had just a [Smite the Monstrous](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite+the+Monstrous) in his hand and it had a grand total of zero targets out of six of Cox's creatures.


[Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) and [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab)s had been seriously milling Cox. His deck was at most half the size of Edwards's. Cox was pinging successfully each turn and the totals were 18-13. [Creepy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creepy+Doll) kept coming as Edwards kept drawing land after land after land.


Finally the game threatened to move faster than a [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) mirror match when Cox cast an un-smite-able [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake). It elicited some action from Edwards who used his [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) to kill a creature and grow his [Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot), then used a new [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon) to kill the Drake and grow the 'knot further to a 5/5. Attacks started to even the totals at 13-12. Edwards lamented the [Creepy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creepy+Doll) forcing his [Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) to stay back on defense.


Ghoulcaller's Chant brought back the Drake and [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul) and Cox swung with his little guys to sink Edwards to 7. Edwards had six cards in his hand, but most were land. He maintained enough removal though so that every time a big creature swung (read: [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake)), he had a response (read: [Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rebuke), this time). But when Cox made a [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) for the third time (with the flashed back [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart)), it proved to outlast the removal Edwards had drawn.


Cox had eight creatures and the only thing standing in his way was a [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit). He tried to put it on top of the library with a [Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms), but [Ranger's Guile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ranger%27s+Guile) prevented it. So instead, Cox just swung with the entire team.


[Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) killed the [Abattoir Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abattoir+Ghoul), [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) stopped the [Walking Corpse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Walking+Corpse), [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit) jumped in front of the Drake. A [Smite the Monstrous](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smite+the+Monstrous) took out a [Stromkirk Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Patrol); after first-strike damage on the Drake, the Bell-Ringer used a [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) to finish off the flyer and Edwards went to 1.


It was a valiant effort, but it didn't stop him from drawing land. A draw step later, it was over.


Craig Edwards 1 – 1 Pat Cox


"I thought about hate-drafting that stupid [Creepy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creepy+Doll). I hate it so much." Edwards, apparently doesn't like creepy automata with scissors. I can't imagine why. "I had a big [Lumberknot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) ... Stupid [Creepy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creepy+Doll)."


**Game 3** 


Edwards's early [Selfless Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Cathar) and [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) were blunted by the 1/4 [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab). He, along with his [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) friend, had milled a [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart) into the 'yard for later use.


The Villagers flipped and took Cox to 15, then Preyed Upon the minty fresh [Battleground Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battleground+Geist) as Edwards looked to control the tempo of the game. He had a [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter), [Darkthicket Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkthicket+Wolf) and a Ranger's [Guile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guile) in the grip and he made the Cathar useful again when he gave it a [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) to play with.


Cox's endless parade of low-powered creatures continued, but this time he played both the [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) and a [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) to complement them. The transformation of the [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald) back into Villagers signified the shifting tempo of the game. Edwards ganged up to take down the Mauler, and was left with only a constantly transforming Estwald with the totals 15-17, then 15-14.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/SF%20Pat%20Cox.JPG)  
*Pat Cox comes from behind to take the last 2 games*
Cox again used a Ghoulcaller's Chant to get a two-for-one, and consistently maintained a creature lead on Edwards. Edwards let out a large sigh as he shifted his land around in play. [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) removed the [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) from the game and if it weren't for a [Battleground Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battleground+Geist) – soon copied with the [Cackling Counterpart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cackling+Counterpart) in the graveyard – there'd be very little game movement. But as it was, the four-powered flyers put Edwards on the back foot. In fact, both back feet. He was on 11, and a mere two turns away from death.


Edwards attacked with his Villagers and Cox had no fear throwing the Skaab in front of it. The two creatures bounced off one another, and soon after Edwards fell to 3 from the mutually pumping flyers.


The natural progression after falling to three from big flyers is to, well, die. And that's what Craig Edwards did; he died.


Pat Cox 2 -1 Craig Edwards


Pat Cox advances to the Finals!




Finals Pat Cox vs. Raphael Levy
-------------------------------


*by Blake Rasmussen*
Could there be something about Austin that favors old-school pros and Hall of Famers? Three years after Brian Kibler won Pro Tour Austin, Raphael Levy, one of the longest-playing Pro Tour players – his first Grand Prix win came in 1997 – is in position to win his fourth Grand Prix and continue his now seemingly inevitable march toward 500 Pro Points and first on the lifetime list.


Standing in his way is Pat Cox. While it's hard to match Levy's lifetime achievements, Cox is an accomplished player in his own right. The American made it through the Top 8 on the back of a defensive UB Skaab-based deck that could turtle-up behind [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab)s and [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab)s.


**Game 1**


For the final match of Grand Prix Austin, Levy chose both to play and to keep. His aggressive start of [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) and Invisible stalker had Cox on the back foot almost immediately. It didn't help that the America was stuck on just [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s early, unable to cast much more than a [Dead Weight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+Weight) on a [Selfless Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Cathar) in the early turns.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpaus12/Finals%20Pat%20Cox.JPG)  
*Pat Cox in the finals*
An [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin) threatened to fly over any of the many [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab)s in Cox's deck once he found an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), and Cox was quickly down to 8 life when Levy cast [Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk) to flood the board with evasive creatures.


[Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) and [Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms) gave him a small amount of breathing room, but the Monk was still a 5/5 and Cox was still well behind on the board. He stayed that way when Levy countered his [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) and bounced a [Stromkirk Patrol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stromkirk+Patrol) with [Lost in the Mist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+the+Mist).


"That was not much of a game," Cox said before choosing to play first in game two.


Levy 1 – Cox 0


**Game 2**


Levy once again got off to a quick start with a Selless Cathar and a [Spectral Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Rider), enhanced by a [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger).


Cox flashed in a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to block a [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger) enhanced Cathar, which in turn allowed him to cast a [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) to gum up the board. But the [Spectral Rider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Rider) picked up the Dagger and continued to ride in on Cox's life total, dropping him to 13 before Levy played a [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice).


[Victim of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) on the Rider let Cox suddenly perk up slightly, suddenly finding himself ahead on the battlefield.


But the feeling didn't last long. [Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms) took care of the [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler), as Cox had no other creatures in his graveyard to feed it. Levy even had another [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) to help carry the load.


The [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice)s picked up the Dagger and attacked Cox down to five, the Frenchman zeroing in on a trophy he's been chasing for the last year. Cox tried a last-gasp [Creepy Doll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creepy+Doll), but a timely [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) kept Levy firmly in control.


Two attacks and 8 damage from [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice)s later and Hall of Famer Raphael Levy was the Grand Prix Austin Champion!


Raphael Levy defeats Pat Cox 2-0 and is the GP Austin Champion

Top 5 Cards
-----------



![Charmbreaker Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Charmbreaker+Devils)

This is the second time this red rare has graced this list. And for good reason. Recursion is always a key to breaking the symmetry of the Sealed format, and Devils does that in style. It was the marquee card of many of the top-finishing Sealed decks. It returns your [Midnight Haunting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Haunting), your [Into the Maw of Hell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Maw+of+Hell), your [Lost in the Mist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+the+Mist). You name it, as long as it's an Instant or Sorcery, [Charmbreaker Devils](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charmbreaker+Devils) has probably recurred it to win a game.



![Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Feeling+of+Dread)

[Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) is a perfect representative of the tempo base of this format. Raphael Levy's two copies in his Top 8 deck pretty much gave him the Quarter- and Semi-Final wins. Like [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations) and [Desperate Ravings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ravings) it is a powerful card if you are in both colors, but you probably want to stay away from it if you can't pay its Flashback cost. Mostly.



![Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Geist-Honored+Monk)

It would be a big mistake not to highlight this card. Not only is it powerful on its own, but add in a modicum of synergy available in the format, say, a [Silent Departure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Departure) or even just a simple [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) and this friend of the ghosts becomes a force with which to be reckoned. Another integral card in Levy's winning deck it came out early and often and frequently won the game on the spot.



![Spider Spawning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spider+Spawning)

A sign of the true depth of the limited format, the true power of this card hadn't even been fully utilized until a few short weeks ago. But don't let its late appearance on the scene mislead you into misjudging its power. Add in a dash of self-mill (or your opponent milling you) and this card can explode in the late game (or even the mid-game) and completely overwhelm an opponent before they can say, "Eww! Spiders!"



![Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Prey+Upon)

I can think of no card to define the versatility of the format better than [Prey Upon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prey+Upon). This humble piece of green removal, over the evolution of the format, became more and more important and more and more of a centerpiece in any deck involving green, especially Green-White. Historically the worst archetype in tournament **Magic**, adding a single piece of efficiently costed, color-pie friendly removal made Green-White arguably the most consistent and powerful archetype of a consistent and powerful format.
