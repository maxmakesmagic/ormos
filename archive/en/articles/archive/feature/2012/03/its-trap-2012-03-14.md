IT'S A TRAP!
============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 14, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






As the Modern PTQ scene continues to develop, players are finding exciting new ways to merge eras of **Magic**. In a recent **Magic Online** PTQ, HotGoblin1 provided a new spin on a deck that briefly showed up in Extended last year: Green-White [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap). Merging the best elements of that deck with the power of [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach), HotGoblin1 added extra consistency to a deck that was already quite explosive!


How can [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) and [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) fit together in the same deck? With this deck, the name of the game is finding ways to sneak an [Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul) onto the battlefield, and as you'll soon find out there are plenty of ways to cheat out the 15/15 Eldrazi.


The two most obvious ways to achieve an Eldrazi attack in this deck is to use [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) or [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach). Full of plenty of acceleration like [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), this deck can hardcast one of those two cards as early as turn three. Either of those cards make it easy for an [Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul) or "merely" a [Terastodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terastodon) to come out to play.


While obvious, those ways aren't even the most deadly. This deck makes use of the *Lorwyn* hideaway lands [Windbrisk Heights](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights) and [Mosswort Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mosswort+Bridge) as another method to get out its fatties. Between 0/1 attackers and the quickly-growing [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary), the hideaway conditions are easy for this deck to meet. Best of all, when you play a card off of a hideaway land you get to cast it—thanks for the extra turn, [Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul)!


In case your opponent comes packing hate, this deck isn't a one-trick pony either. It's full of viable beatdown cards, so if your opponents spend too much time stopping your combo, [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) and [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) might just run them over!


If putting [Emrakul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul) onto the battlefield on turn three sounds fun, this might be the deck for you. The Modern world continues to grow—give it a try at a [Pro Tour *Avacyn Restored* PTQ](/en/node/640181) near you!
