Top 8 Coverage



What had started with 1,510 players originally had first been reduced to 466, now it was down to eight. Only eight players remained in the running for fame and fortune, points and prizes, title and trophy.


The first two people to secure a spot in the Top 8, with an intentional draw in Round 15, were four-time Grand Prix champion Martin Jůza and Pro Tour *Battle for Zendikar* finalist Ryoichi Tamada.


They were joined by Giuseppe Reale, fresh off a Top 8 at Grand Prix Bologna just two weeks ago, by Grand Prix Lille quarterfinalist Petr Sochůrek, and most notably by Hall of Famer Ben Stark. Rounding out the Top 8 were first-timers Fabian Friedrich, Arne Huschenbeth, and Paolo Magnani.


Who would come out on top? Stay tuned to find out!

Day 2 Coverage



Welcome back to Paris, France, where this weekend players from all over the world have gathered for the final hurrah of *Khans of Tarkir* and *Fate Reforged* in Standard!


For one last Grand Prix, rhinoceroses lay siege to outposts, hordelings burst out, scales are hardened, ancestors are rallied, time is dug through, and players can rely on fetch lands to make their four- or even five-color mana bases work with relative ease.


What had started with 1,510 participants had been reduced to 466 by the end of the first day of the tournament. Now 466 players were back for six more rounds of Standard, to compete for a spot in the Top 8. Many familiar faces could be found at or near the top of the standings overnight, including Ryoichi Tamada and Martin Jůza at 9-0 as well as Ondřej Stráský, Ben Stark, Marcio Carvalho, Sebastian Thaler, Pierre Dagen, Yuta Takahashi, Gabriel Nassif, and Fabrizio Anteri all within the Top 32. These ten could boast a combined 84 Grand Prix Top 8s, 15 wins before the weekend, and one might reasonably expect the numbers to have increased by its end.


Stay tuned for text updates straight from the battlefield to find out who ends up on top here at Grand Prix Paris 2016!

Day 1 Coverage



It was a great first day full of Magic here in Paris. 1,510 players from all over the world convened in the French capital for one final chance to summon Siege Rhinos and cast Dig Through Time, to attack with Mantis Riders and play Rally the Ancestors before the upcoming Standard rotation.


Through nine grueling rounds the original field was whittled down to a mere 466. All of them would return in the morning for another six rounds of Standard, on the hunt for prizes and points, for fame and fortune, and eventually a shot at the trophy and title. Overnight, four players remained undefeated with perfect records of 9-0: Steve Bains, Ryoichi Tamada, Martin Jůza, and Olivier Gheysen.


Interestingly enough, none of them had chosen the supposedly best deck. In fact no Rally pilot was even playing for 9-0 in the last round of the day. Instead, the 9-0 crowd featured an illustrous line-up of Abzan, Grixis Control, Bant Company, and Dark Jeskai.


Join us tomorrow for a breakdown of the top decks, for deck lists, and for further news straight from the battlefield of Grand Prix Paris 2016!
