Round 4: The Goyfs Clash
========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 29, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_SteveSadin.jpg)
By Steve Sadin












[Alexander Hayne vs. Samuele Estratti (Cube Draft)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/tpc12/fm3)


Shuuhei Nakamura (Jund) vs. David Ochoa (Zoo)
---------------------------------------------


[Jon Finkel (W/U Aggro) vs. Shouta Yasooka (R/G/U Control)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/tpc12/fm5)




---

Friends and long time teammates Shuuhei Nakamura and David Ochoa decided to prepare with different groups for the Players Championship. Ochoa tested alongside Luis Scott-Vargas, Josh Utter-Leyton, and Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, while Nakamura collaborated with fellow globetrotters Martin Juza and Yuuya Watanabe.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/tpc12/fm4.jpg)

*Friends and teammates. And despite being rivals in this event, they certainly weren't bitter about it.* 
The groups ultimately settled on two very different decks for this event. Ochoa and his collaborators went with a lightning fast Domain Zoo deck, while Nakamura opted to pilot a Jund deck that is chock full of removal and two for ones.


### Game 1


Ochoa won the roll and got off to a fast start with [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) and [Qasali Pridemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage), while Nakamura spent his early turns tearing apart his opponent's hand with two copies of [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek).


After picking apart Ochoa's hand, Nakamura decided that it was time to take over the board. First Nakamura played a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), which stopped any attacks from happening, and then he followed it up with a [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) that left Ochoa without a single creature.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/tpc12/r4_nakamura.jpg)

*Shuuhei Nakamura clears the path with his powerful Jund deck.* 
Ochoa bought himself some time by using [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to flashback [Tribal Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames), and take out the 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), but without anything left in reserve, he could do nothing to stop Nakamura from taking the first game with [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), [Dark Confidant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), and [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks).


Nakamura 1, Ochoa 0


### Game 2


Ochoa got off to another quick start in the second game with a [Kird Ape](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) and a [Loam Lion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Lion). Fully aware of how much pressure he was under, Nakamura hoped that he could stem the bleeding with a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) followed by a [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), but [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) and [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) left the Japanese pro without any defenses.



![Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pyroclasm)![Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tarmogoyf)

Ochoa further pressed his advantage with a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), putting Nakamura into a spot where he had to hope that his [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) cascaded into something great so that he could even have a chance of crawling back into the game.


The [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) found a [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm). However, all of Ochoa's creatures had at least three toughness! Consequently, the sometimes-devastating board sweeper failed to do anything.


An attack and a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) later, and the players were off to a deciding third game.


Nakamura 1, Ochoa 1


After seeing [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) and [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) out of his opponent's deck, Ochoa decided that he needed to make some significant changes to his deck for the third game. In order to make himself a bit more resilient against his opponent's sweepers, Ochoa took out his [Steppe Lynx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx)es and replaced them with bigger threats.


### Game 3


Ochoa took a mulligan and began the game without a one drop or a two drop, making Nakamura's turn-three [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) the first play of the game. Ochoa finally got something on the board when he cast a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), but that wasn't long for the world as a [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf) cascaded into a [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), which promptly ate Ochoa's only creature.


Ochoa then hoped to get himself into the lead with a [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) backed up by a hand full of removal spells. However, an Inquisiton of Kozilek, a [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), and a [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) activation left Ochoa in a particularly dire situation.


But when Ochoa's draw step yielded him an [Elspeth, Knight-Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant), suddenly it was Nakamura who found himself back-pedaling.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/tpc12/r4_ochoa.jpg)

*David Ochoa's Zoo deck packs plenty of fire power, and when staring down multiple sweepers, it can also pack a little planeswalker power as well.* 
Nakamura began working his way back into a good position by using a [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) to take out a Soldier token and a [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) activation to kill off Ochoa's [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), but Ochoa found himself a replacement [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) on his very next turn.


At this point, Ochoa's deck stopped yielding him anything of note, giving Nakamura the time that he needed to take over the game with a pair of Bloodbraid Elves (finding [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil)).


When Nakamura used a [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) to take out Ochoa's own [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), it was only a matter of time before the Japanese pro was able to triumph over his mustachioed foe.


Nakamura 2, Ochoa 1
