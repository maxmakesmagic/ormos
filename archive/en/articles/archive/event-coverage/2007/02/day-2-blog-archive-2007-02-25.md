BLOG
----



 

Diversity of the format was the secret word yesterday and that has continued into the Day 2 field, where 25 different archetypes are on display among the 64 competitors.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Archetype** | **Number** |
| Loam | 11 |
| Tron | 8 |
| TrinkeTog | 6 |
| TEPS | 4 |
| Boros | 4 |
| Affinity | 4 |
| Elvish Opposition | 3 |
| Goblins | 3 |
| DC Rock | 2 |
| King in the Castle | 2 |
| Pet Cemetery | 2 |
| Old School Tog | 2 |
| Other different 1-ofs | 13 |

As you can see, Loam put the most players into Day 2, which makes sense because it was probably the most played deck in yesterday's field. After that come the various Tron and Tog decks (most of the Tron decks chose not to play [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage)s, while the vast majority of Tog decks did). After that you get the usual suspects we've been seeing from the PTQ circuit with a couple of interesting decks that I had not seen before.



![Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trinket+Mage)

DC Rock are Rock decks that are running 2-3 [Death Cloud](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Cloud)s - a hit to the mana base, but one made more palatable by [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and *Ravnica* dual lands. We'll have a deck tech on King in the Castle a bit later, so I won't spoil that except to say that it's a multi-color green midrange aggro deck (that makes it sooo much clearer, right?). Pet Cemetery is what I'm calling the black-white animal decks with creatures like [Spectral Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Lynx), [Savannah Lions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lions), [Isamaru, Hound of Konda](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Isamaru%252C%2BHound%2Bof%2BKonda) and [Jotun Grunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jotun+Grunt)s. They also run everyone's favorite card drawing Invitational winner ([Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)), [Vindicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vindicate)s, [Gerrard's Verdict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gerrard%27s+Verdict), discard, and the very spicy [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate) out of the sideboard.


Then you get a mess of one-of decks that make this format basically impossible to metagame directly. Extended is alive and well, and as you can see, you can play almost anything and win.


As the day progresses, we'll type up some of the more interesting decklists in the Day 2 field, as well as do a couple of deck techs and bring you the usual Top 8 goodness, so stay tuned.


  


Sunday, Feb 25: 11:33 a.m. - The King is Back
---------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*

 

Its name once conjured fear into the minds of players around the globe. Its mere existence was the driving force behind the restriction of many cards in Vintage. It was the most dominant creature in **Magic** for over two years. And then [Sudden Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) came off the printing reels. It was official-[Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) was dead.


Or not. Tog players have managed to find a way around the two-mana instant with the help of the [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) control engine common to the blue-based [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) decks circulating around the format. In fact, the newest Tog evolution seems more like a modified version of the [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) control deck than the mass card-drawing, burst kill [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) of old.

With the addition of [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) and [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) to the aggressive creature base in these decks, players are able to get a decent board presence and then lock the game down with careful manipulation of their [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance). If some thing goes wrong, the marquee card of *Planar Chaos*, [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation), shows up to set this back to normal. In addition, the profusion of shuffling effects in this deck allows the pilot to always have a fresh Top if desired.


Even in the face of this new tech, some players have stuck to their guns and are rocking an older, more recognizable version of [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog).

The formula is as simple as it ever was. [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) plus some permission and removal alongside a large amount of card drawing usually equals one dead opponent.


These Tog decks both have the same concerns with the field, though. While most of the aggressive match-ups are a relative easy time, they have some troubles with the other control decks in the format. U/W Tron has a decent advantage in the first game, but with [Dwarven Blastminer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dwarven+Blastminer), [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), and a decent amount of artifact removal, the Tog player has a relatively easy go of things for the remaining games. The one real problem that the newer breed of Tog has is Gifts Rock. [Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed), discard, and recursion of large creatures causes some problems for the Tog player. Even still, [Tormod's Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt) recursion still offers a chance to put the Tog player back into the driver's seat.

*by Julien Nuijten*

 

So here I am, doing coverage again at the second day of the GP, which I'm probably better at than playing nowadays, since I rarely win matches of **Magic** anymore. Forgive me for my slight whining, it's what we **Magic** pro's do best. I'll stop that now.


Personally I'm not a big fan of the Extended format in general, but particulaly the current environment. There are too many decks available and most of them are equally good, but they all just have a few unsolvable problems. For instance, Aggro CAL can rarely beat a deck with [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) and the UW Tron deck has too many difficulties dealing with one-drops and [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)s. In a format with fewer decks to play, this could be resolved with good metagaming, but in an Extended format like this one, it's really hard to say which decks you're going to play since the difference between a popular deck and a less popular deck is 15 and 5 percent. On the other hand, the format does allow for a lot of deckbuilding creativity. Check out Alex Ledbetter's variant of the No-Stick deck called Beat Stick. It's a much more aggressive version of the deck, with a lot of cards in it that are new to the archetype. He is currently 9-2-1 with 2 byes.

***JN:** Did you build this deck yourself? What inspired you to pick up the No-Stick deck and turn it into a more aggressive deck?*  

I did build it myself. I liked the old No-Stick deck, but it seemed like nowadays people are too prepared for the Scepter-Chant lock. That's why I put in the [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance)s and Tops; it really helps against cards like [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) whereas the old version with [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)s needed a lot more mana and more cards to deal with that card. [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) of course also helps here. I put in the creatures because most players are very comfortable in taking a lot of points off their lands when they play against Scepter/Chant, and if you draw something like a [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel) and Helix you can just punish them for that.


***JN:** How do the aggressive elements in the deck combine with the more controlling elements?*  

It's only a little bit aggressive, it's not really focused around the creatures. It's just a way to win the game if your draw happens to come out that way. It's also good to have a couple of four-mana spells for [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), and [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel) is the best four-mana creature for the deck. I considered just playing Teferi, but people know how to play around it, and I think most of the time the lock is too slow anyway.


***JN:** What would you say is the biggest problem of the deck?*  

Its worst matchup is the UW Tron deck because there are no more hard counters in the deck, and [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) doesn't do so much against their business spells. That's why there are four Dwarven Blastminers in the sideboard, just to try and fix that matchup. I'm not too confidant in the Agro CAL matchup either, since it's hard for the deck to deal with their threats if they come out early. You still have [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance)/Top though, so that always gives you a decent shot.


***JN:** What about its biggest strength?*  

It's very hard to lose against TEPS and other combo decks, since the deck plays 4 maindeck [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) and the Scepter/Chant lock. It's quite good against aggressive decks too, like Boros and Affinity, because of the [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)es and Angels.


***JN:** Would you play it again? If so, would you make any changes?*  

I'm not sure I'd play it again, Trinket Tog might be just better. But I do like playing my own decks, so that might cause me to play it again. The sideboard is pretty tight because of the [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)es, but I do think you want something against the Loam deck, perhaps splashing [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate) as a Wish target is good enough. But it's hard to say what you want to focus your deck on, because of the large variety of decks you could encounter.


  


Sunday, Feb 25: 12:55 p.m. - Round 11: Mark Herberholz vs. Gerard Fabiano
-------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*

 

These two players are mainstays on the Pro Tour and are long time friends. Herbie had a pretty decent last year, including a win at PT-Honolulu. In fact, he's playing an Extended version of the Zoo deck he used to take down the title on the beach. Gerard is playing an old-school version of Psychatog featuring some green control bombs, such as [Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed) and [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam). As expected, the banter started as soon as the two hit the table.

Sunday, Feb 25: 2:48 p.m. - Deck Tech: King in the Castle
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*

 

Extended is filled to the brim with decks that use a veritable toolbox of cards to adapt to any situation they could face. Consequently, it makes sense that this Extended season has seen the newest incarnations of the toolbox deck, and there are many different avenues from which to choose, including Gifts Rock, which uses a [Gifts Ungiven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) engine powered by [Eternal Witness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness) and [Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis); the new Trinket [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) deck, which uses [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage)s and an arsenal of cheap artifacts to handle problems; and King in the Castle.


King in the Castle at first glance seems like a typical beatdown deck. It has the cheap efficient creatures like [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) and [Troll Ascetic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Troll+Ascetic). It has the [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte)s. It even has [Armadillo Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak). But that's where the similarities end. Besides the beatdown element, the deck has enough disruption to solidify the matchup against the control decks, and enough control to pave the way against the aggressive decks.


Kyle Sanchez, the deck's designer, says that the deck can go any way you need it to go. Its [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) engine gives the deck the utility it needs to disrupt any opposing deck just enough that the beaters are able to get their job done. [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) stops [Pernicious Deed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pernicious+Deed) and [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault). [Tormod's Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt) ruins [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and [Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis). [Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) and [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) just wreak havoc on most aggressive decks.


According to Kyle, the deck has favorable matchups against the most popular decks in today's field. "Tog is almost impossible to lose. U/W Tron is a little harder. You can wreck their hand early, but they have so many big cards that it may not matter in the long run. Loam is dependant on the player. If they're good it's close, but if their not that great, this deck crushes them." The deck's versatility allows it to handle most situations, but the glue that holds it all together is the aggressive core. You can have all the fancy tricks in the world, but sometimes, you just win games by turning men sideways.







#### Kyle SanchezKing in the Castle

Sunday, Feb 25: 3:29 p.m. - Round 13: Kenji Tsumura vs. John Pelcak
-------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Julien Nuijten*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpdal07/pelcak.jpg)*John Pelcak*
John Pelcak's on a roll. He hasn't dropped any points yet, and is paired down against Kenji, who's got five fewer points in the standings. Kenji brought the popular Aggro CAL deck while John is playing Affinity, so it should be pretty good for Kenji, as he's got access to 3 [Shattering Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Spree) in the board and 4 [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish)es to get them in Game 1.


### Game 1


Kenji won the roll, always a big advantage in this format. He started off with a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and missed his [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) on [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) after John had played an [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker). John did reveal [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) and two [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer)s, so there was no doubt that Kenji's second Therapy would be good. John played out his Plating but the Worker couldn't get in yet because Kenji had just played [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). On his next turn, Kenji played a 2/2 [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) and flashed back Therapy sacrificing the Wall to take out the Enforcers. John revealed two lands and [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus), and traded the Worker for the [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore), putting the modular counter on the Nexus. Kenji had another [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore), but it couldn't stop the Nexus with a Plating, now putting Kenji at 7. John had found another [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) as well, and things were looking very bad for Kenji, who didn't have an answer to either of John's creatures.


Pelcak 1 - Tsumura 0


Both players boarded in a few very good cards; Kenji's got 2 [Krosan Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip), 2 [Shattering Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Spree) leaving one in the sideboard for [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish), [Hull Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hull+Breach), [Darkblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) and another [Devastating Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devastating+Dreams). He took out all four [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) and three [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy).


Apparently John didn't really know what to sideboard, and started laughing when I picked up his deckbox. He'd taken out 2 [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) and one each of [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer), [Thoughtcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast), [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating). He put in 4 [Tormod's Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt) and 2 [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle).


### Game 2


Kenji played first and had to mulligan a slow hand of [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), [Devastating Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devastating+Dreams), [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore), [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots), [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) and a red and green cycle land. John mulliganned as well, and started off with [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) on [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault), two [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter)s, [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) and a [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus), but he didn't really have a lot of power. When he activated his Nexus on the next turn, Kenji responded with a [Krosan Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip) on the Ravager putting the modular counter on an [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter), and he could only attack for two with his fliers. Kenji played [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) on his fourth turn, getting ready to play [Devastating Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devastating+Dreams) on his next turn, but John killed it and Kenji's graveyard with [Tormod's Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt). John again didn't have anything, and lost his entire board except for a 1/3 [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) and a [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) to the [Devastating Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devastating+Dreams). Kenji only had three lands and no cards in hand though, but his next two draw steps were a [Shattering Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Spree) and [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam), providing him with plenty of gas while the [Shattering Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Spree) took out John's board once again. John came up with [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) and [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating), but Kenji had found another [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) which was just too big to stop at this point.


Pelcak 1 - Tsumura 1


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpdal07/tsumura.jpg)*Kenji Tsumura*
### Game 3


John made a few more sideboard changes, as he put in Cabal Therapies instead of [Stifle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stifle)s. He played first and both players kept their hands, and John started off with [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus), [Arcbound Worker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Worker), [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) on [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) and [Chromatic Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star). Kenji had a [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant), and John attacked with his Nexus and Worker and made a [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer). Bob revealed a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), but much more relevant was Kenji's third turn [Shattering Spree](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shattering+Spree) for [Myr Enforcer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Enforcer) and two of John's three lands. The Worker and Nexus got in there again, but that's all John could do as Kenji was setting up [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore)/[Devastating Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devastating+Dreams) for his next turn. The Confidant kept working on Kenji's life total though, as he'd now revealed a [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore) and [Seismic Assault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault), dropping him down to four. John played a [Tormod's Crypt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tormod%27s+Crypt) which caused some confusion when Kenji went for [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore)/[Devastating Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devastating+Dreams), but the end result was that John had a [Chromatic Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star) and a [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle) while Kenji had a 6/6 [Terravore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terravore), which quickly sealed it.


Tsumura 2 - Pelcak 1

Sunday, Feb 25: 5:03 p.m. - Round 15: Alex Ledbetter vs. Tim Cozine
-------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Julien Nuijten*

 

Alex is playing the Beatstick deck that we talked about in the coverage earlier, while Tim brought a more conservative deck to this event; Boros. Alex told me that he thinks he's a favorite in this matchup, but it's still a close one, because red decks sometimes just do win.


### Game 1


Tim won the roll, making all of his spells almost twice as good. He mulliganned and played the first spell of this match for top 8: [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight). It got in for two and Tim surprisingly passed the turn with a land and a [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage) in hand, against Alex' board of [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain). Tim [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)ed Alex when he played a [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), and Helixed him again after he tapped out for Ice on the [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight), again choosing not to play the [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage). [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant) with kicker bought Alex some time and he used that to lay down [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), and now things weren't looking so good for Tim although he put his opponent down to nine in the next attack step. Tim tried to lure Alex to put the Top on top of the deck by playing [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt), but he just spinned it first and found another Top on top of the deck. Tim didn't try the [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) he had in his hand and just passed the turn. [Meddling Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) came down for Alex, naming [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt), and it seemed like Alex was ready to race Tim when he end-of-turn [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix)ed him. While Alex had all of Tim's spells under control with Top/[Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), he didn't have a solution to the [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) yet, but surely he would be able to find something for it in one of the next four turns he had. He did, as he [Cunning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Wish)ed for a [Condemn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn) which took care of Tim's last threat on the board, but somehow [Pillage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillage) on the Top, putting the Top on top, was able to force through another [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) as Alex still hadn't found anything. [Lightning Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Angel) for Alex came down soon after that though, putting Tim at 8 while Alex was still as 6. A [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) from Alex put him even further ahead, and the lone [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) couldn't get the job done.


### Game 2


Tim played first again and both players kept their seven. Tim had a rather weak start for a Boros deck: no turn one drop and just a Jitte on the second turn. A third-turn [Jotun Grunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jotun+Grunt) seemed like a good creature but when Alex played [Orim's Chant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orim%27s+Chant) without kicker in Tim's next upkeep, Tim put his [Jotun Grunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jotun+Grunt) in the graveyard for some reason, despite having two fetch lands in his own graveyard. In the meanwhile, Alex was setting up Top/[Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) again, but couldn't find a two mana spell on top when Tim [Disenchant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disenchant)ed the Balance. Another [Jotun Grunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jotun+Grunt) came down to carry the Jitte, but it was Tim's only creature so Alex was able to solve it with [Porphyry Nodes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Porphyry+Nodes). [Thirst for Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thirst+for+Knowledge) refilled Alex' hand and he played another [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) while still being at a very healthy 15. Things got even worse for Tim when Alex played [Isochron Scepter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) and imprinted [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix), and at this point there was simply nothing Tim could do.


Alex Ledbetter won 2-0 and takes his rogue reroll deck to the Top 8!


  


Sunday, Feb 25: 4:57 p.m. - Round 14: Analynn Bustamante vs. Jesse Hawkins
--------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpdal07/hawkins.jpg)*Jesse Hawkins*
Jesse Hawkins is an up and coming **Magic** player who, after his performance yesterday, was able to shrug off the amateur label despite being only 15. He comes into this game playing the incredibly powerful Gifts Rock deck. His opponent this round is none other than the Analynn Bustamante, who you should remember from the blog coverage from yesterday. She is playing a hyper-aggressive three-color deck that straddles the line between Boros and Zoo.


Analynn started the game fairly strong with a turn 1 Isamaru followed by a turn 2 [Kataki, War's Wage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kataki%2C+War%27s+Wage). Jesse whiffed on his turn 1 [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) naming [Boros Swiftblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Swiftblade). However, he found out that beyond the Kataki and a [Jotun Grunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jotun+Grunt), Analynn didn't have too much he'd have to be worried about. Since this was Jesse's first trip to the feature match area, he was a little confused by the playmat's setup. After casting his Therapy, he remarked, "Which of these is the discard zone. . .er, graveyard. . .whatever it's called in this game." "What do you usually play," Analynn wanted to know. After a long pause, Jesse managed out a resigned "**Magic**."


When he managed a turn 2 [Sakura-Tribe Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder), it seemed he would gum up the board fairly well. Analynn pressed her advantage and cast [Molten Rain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Rain) on Jesse's [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb). This brought him into single digits, always scary when facing off against a Zoo deck. You never know when things could take a quick turn for the worse. However, on his next turn, Jesse played a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to flash his Therapy back and crippled Analynn by hitting two [Jotun Grunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jotun+Grunt)s from her hand. Regardless of the hit to her hand, Analynn kept drawing men and kept putting them into play. Jesse couldn't find more than a fourth land and a couple of spot removal spells. After a turn or two, that left him at a very scary three life facing down an angry Isamaru.


Luckily for him, Analynn didn't have the [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) to finish him off, and he was able to hold the line down with a [Genesis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis) he finally was able to cast. Analynn really put it to him, though with a [Sudden Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock) straight to the face. That left Jesse at one. Any burn, and especially a [Sudden Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Shock), would seal the deal. Jesse decided to see where he was and played a Therapy naming [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). He whiffed and saw her hand of a land and two more Isamarus. Yay, legends rule! While she wasn't able to get the litter of puppies onto the table, Analynn did have the fortuitous rip of the final points of burn required for the kill.


Analynn Bustamante 1 - Jesse Hawkins 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpdal07/bustamante.jpg)*Analynn Bustamante*
Jesse got to start the fight for game 2 and lead off with a first turn [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). It knocked a [Krosan Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip) out of Analynn's hand, and revealed a grip of five animals and a single land. When Jesse played a turn 2 [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) and was able to know both of the Lavamancers out of Analynn's hand, things were looking good for him. She hadn't managed to draw a second land yet, and a slowly developing board definitely favored Jesse. Jesse then reset the board with a quick [Engineered Explosives](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Engineered+Explosives) for one. Analynn's second land finally arrived in the mail, and she flipped a [Silver Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Knight) onto the board. Jesse kept his advantage by playing [Living Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) twice on the next turn for some really heavy hitters in Meloku, The Clouded Mirror and [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch).


Analynn managed to pick up an [Umezawa's Jitte](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) for her knight and began putting counters on it the turn after. Despite the Hierarch and Meloku coming down on Jesse's side, Analynn still managed some cool tricks with Jitte and Helix to clear Jesse's side of the board. After that dominating performance by Jitte-one of many-Jesse was going to find it hard to come back. When his deck provided nothing but land over the next two turns, Jesse conceded the match.


Analynn Bustamante 2 - Jesse Hawkins 0
