Pro Tour Battle for Zendikar Photo Essay
========================================



 Posted in **Arcana**
 on October 21, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






Pro Tour *Battle for Zendikar* was an amazing show, filled with moments and *Magic* that made memories to last a lifetime. From the 367 players through to the winner of the Top 8, these are a few snapshots of what happened in Milwaukee.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_01.jpg)


With the chill of fall settling upon Milwaukee, things began with the warmth of the game's greatest being honored.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_02.jpg)


Willy Edel, Shota Yasooka, and Eric Froehlich each appeared for their induction into the Pro Tour Hall of Fame.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_03.jpg)


Nostalgic stories were shared, each reflecting on the game they had spent their lives playing.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_04.jpg)


While the elected three were given rings of their own...


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_05.jpg)


...one touchingly gave the gift of a ring himself.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_06.jpg)


With festivities out of the way, the first day began with *Battle for Zendikar* Draft, a difficult format filled with powerful cards.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_09.jpg)


Although some draft choices are more obvious than others...


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_10.jpg)


...everyone found something they enjoyed to put into their deck.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_11.jpg)


The wide field moved on from drafting to Standard...


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_13.jpg)


...filled with surprises and discussion across the players that remained.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_14.jpg)


Viewers at home enjoyed following along...


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_15.jpg)


...as friends and family found their favorites fighting on-site.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_16.jpg)


Some just visited for the fun of being a fan...


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_17.jpg)


...while others fought to the end for their chance to advance.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_18.jpg)


Gideon himself watched on in the end...


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_19.jpg)


...when competitors pushed through to the Top 8 at hand.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_20.jpg)


National stalwarts and Hall of Fame members filled the ranks of the Sunday eight...


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_21.jpg)


...but after it all, it was Kazuyuki Takimura who claimed the fame.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/ARC20151021_22.jpg)


Congratulations to Kazuyuki Takimura, champion of Pro Tour *Battle for Zendikar*!
