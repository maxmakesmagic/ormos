Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG.com's exclusive **Magic Online** column.  Two weeks ago, Red-White-Blue Control strategies were, without any doubt, the best control decks. Since then, the rise of classic White-Blue Flash strategies has dramatically affected the power of these decks. Red-White-Blue decks used to be able to fill their deck with [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion) and, in some cases, [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) to easily overpower enemy control strategies. However, the White-Blue Flash deck has access to a few copies of [Renounce the Guilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) in addition to a suite of permission spells that outperforms the countermagic of the less-dedicated control decks. The Red-White-Blue deck has adapted to look more like its White-Blue cousin. We're not seeing [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) anymore, the deck is packing more countermagic, and multicolor permanents have made an exit from the main deck entirely in favor of stronger control elements. Control matchups are no longer a time to jam [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion)s. Instead, the new breed of Red-White-Blue decks looks to win control matchups with [Ætherling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86therling)/[Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) or well-timed burn following up [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) aggression. This new iteration of Red-White-Blue Control looks like it's well positioned to be the new control deck of choice.

 White-Blue Flash may seem like a dinosaur. The deck was a dominant force in Standard early last November, but its viability waned when everyone started playing [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) and it received a few nails in the coffin out of *Gatecrash* in the form of [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) and [Assemble the Legion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assemble+the+Legion). [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) became a format staple once Act 2 became popular and White-Blue Flash was the furthest thing from what I'd expect a new tier-one deck to look like. Fortunately, [Renounce the Guilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) goes a long way to solving all of these problems. The deck is, once again, well positioned against a field of control decks that don't play the control game as hard and aggressive decks that eschew [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) for two-drops that are easily cast off of [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary). This deck's success has sparked a rise in power for [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) decks. The deck seems like a very dedicated control deck but it has the ability to close games quickly once it gets control, especially if it's able to find the [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike). [Renounce the Guilds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Renounce+the+Guilds) gives the deck access to inexpensive removal that wouldn't be found in blue or white otherwise. I've been playing a great deal of this deck online over the last week. I'm confident in its current metagame positioning, but I fear it may not be in the best place once [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) is being played by more decks.

 Esper decks prey on the other control strategies using the inevitability of [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard). The deck pressures Planeswalkers with [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), but the low creature count lets the deck pack a full four copies of [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict), so it can repeatedly wipe the board against the heavy creature decks. Aggressive strategies have been slowing down in favor of power and it seems like the Esper deck is well positioned again, given the current state of affairs. The deck still has trouble with most [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) decks, but those have been waning a bit in popularity. I expect the [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) decks to have a bit of a resurgence in the coming weeks, which could hurt the Esper deck, but, at least for the coming week, this is one of the better decks in Standard.

 Junk Tokens is another dinosaur of a deck that's beginning to make a comeback. The format seems to be in a similar place to where it was last November, when White-Blue Flash and Junk Tokens first started to make waves. The deck easily overpowers other creature decks and forces control strategies to chain [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s or [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus). This deck's viability hinges heavily on the fact that no one seems to be playing [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) or [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) in the current environment. Jund has seen a significant decline over the last week and the Naya decks seem to be jamming more charms and fewer [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) and [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars). I could see a shift happening where these decks adapt. [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) is very strong, but he's not the best against the [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) decks, and it seems like the Naya strategies could easily sideboard out their [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) in favor of [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned).

[Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) decks are finally starting to wane a bit in popularity. The deck still performs well and we're beginning to see the deck adapt to a more control-based metagame. [Sin Collector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sin+Collector) makes its way back into the main deck and [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) has become a staple. [Fiend Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) can target [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) against the control decks, making their [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) come at a cost. [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) and [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf) allow the deck to start chaining [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime)s early, neutering control decks. The deck can still be a powerhouse against aggressive decks after sideboarding, but without lingering souls in the main deck the Junk Rites deck can get easily ran over by the most aggressive decks in game one.

 Midrange Naya strategies have made a huge splash over the last two weeks. The deck has tremendous board presence against the decks without a board sweeper and [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) and [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) give the deck a lot of resilience against the control decks. Versions of the deck with main-deck [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) over some number of [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) play much differently than their counterparts. This deck is likely to be a major player in the coming weeks and it will be important to test against multiple versions of this deck, especially if you're playing a midrange or aggressive deck.

 Jund is getting cornered as of late. The deck can adapt and change for any format, but the current diversity the format is presenting to the deck makes it hard to address all the problems. It's very hard to build a Jund deck that has a strongly favorable matchup against the control strategies and the aggressive strategies. [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) is an extremely powerful card, but the amount of [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and [Doomed Traveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler)s in the format definitely take some away a lot of her strength as a main-deck consideration. The deck lacks the [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)–type card to find precisely the iteration of cards it need for a situation. Each matchup is very draw dependent. For example, a hand full of cheap spot removal with an [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) at the top end is sure to crush the creature decks, but stands very little chance of beating Red-White-Blue control.

 Aggressive Gruul decks continue to be the top-performing aggro decks in Standard. [Hellrider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hellrider) is strong against Planeswalkers and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and is one of the best positioned aggressive cards right now. The decks that don't pack enough removal find themselves dead before they've had a chance to make any notable board presence. 


![Hellrider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hellrider)
  
 Be prepared to deal with a lot of [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s and not a lot of [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)s or [Mizzium Mortars](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Mortars) this coming weekend. Don't bring too much spot removal to the table if you're not playing [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). The next week is going to be a big one for Standard even though there aren't many major events.
