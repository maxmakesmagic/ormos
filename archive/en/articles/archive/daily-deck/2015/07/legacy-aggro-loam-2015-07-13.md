Legacy Aggro Loam
=================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 13, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






This week, Daily Decks is back to our regular schedule of showcasing top tournament decks. At [Grand Prix Lille](http://magic.wizards.com/en/content/fact-sheet-grand-prix-lille-2015), the competitors battled the challenging Legacy Format. Selecting a deck from *Magic*'s deep history of cards can be a puzzle, and many players gear toward playing blue cards. [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), and [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) are your staples, and most Legacy players use those cards as the backbone for their decks.



I am always intrigued when players choose to play non-blue cards in Legacy, and Top 4 competitor Christoph Alsheimer did just that. In fact, Christoph played every color *but* blue, choosing to play a four-color Loam deck.



Aggro Loam is built around the engine of [Life from the Loam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and utility lands. Usually those utility lands are [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s and fetch lands to help you find the right colors, but also present in this list are [Maze of Ith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith), [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas), and [The Tabernacle at Pendrell Vale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Tabernacle+at+Pendrell+Vale). These lands are useful in different situationsâ€”and when you are dredging three cards every turn, you are more likely to find them than if you are taking a normal draw step.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chalice+of+the+Void)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void)

[Chalice of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chalice+of+the+Void) is a great card to play in any non-blue Legacy deck. With one charge counter, it shuts down so many different cards. Of course the aforementioned [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) and [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) are your big targets, but there's also [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), and countless others. With [Mox Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Diamond), a Chalice for one can be cast on as early as the first turn.



Aggro Loam is just full of value. [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) can help you find your various one-of hate cards, such as [Gaddock Teeg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gaddock+Teeg) or [Scavenging Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenging+Ooze). [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) is a great card against the most popular deck of the tournament, Miracles, and the combo of [Punishing Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Punishing+Fire) and [Grove of the Burnwillows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grove+of+the+Burnwillows) can stop any deck with small creatures like Elves or Goblins. Aggro Loam has always been a fun deck to play, and it's pretty exciting to see it in the Top 8 of a Grand Prix.
