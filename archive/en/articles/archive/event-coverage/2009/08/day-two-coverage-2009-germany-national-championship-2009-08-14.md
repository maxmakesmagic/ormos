Day Two Coverage -- 2009 Germany National Championship
======================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 14, 2009 










![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/blurb2.jpg)A fresh Saturday has broken over Aschaffenburg, and the participants of German Nationals are primed and ready. Which is a fancy way of saying that day 2 of German Nats has begun. Three rounds of draft, four rounds of Standard and we will have a Top 8 and a line-up for Sunday.


It’s not just the main event, though, that draws players into Aschaffenburg’s town hall. Germany has a long tradition of big Legacy tournaments at Nationals, and today will be no exception. Play The Game, See The World is what the Legacy players are fighting for today: An invitation to Rome and the chance to compete for travels to the entire next Pro Tour season.


Today, we will bring you the PTQ results, continuing coverage of the main event, a look at what’s happening on the sidelines, and as a special treat the German coverage over at PlanetMTG ([www.planetmtg.de](http://www.planetmtg.de)) will have a complete draft viewer from table one that we here on dailymtg.com will link you to. Stay with Tobias Henke and Hanno Terbuyken as they bring you all that’s bringable from German Nationals 2009!

Feature Match Round 8: Florian Pils vs Thomas Steeger
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/Unfried-Pils-Steeger.jpg) Yes, we’ve had Thomas Steeger in yesterday’s last feature match. But it is traditional to kick off day two with the undefeated players, and Steeger has done exactly that. His draft had kicked off with [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum) and offered such goodies as third-pick [Thraximundar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraximundar) in the Alara Reborn pack. The picks he wanted didn’t come in packs one and two, but Reborn was his saving grace: [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate), [Thraximundar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraximundar), and ten other cards that made it into his maindeck. His opponent is no slouch, either. Florian Pils sports a 6-1 record coming into Saturday and belongs to the cream of Germany’s crop of better known players.


And he has never lost a game here in Aschaffenburg where he won the die roll.


Game 1:


Pils won the die roll...


...and shunted the duty of playing first in Steeger’s direction. Both players mulliganed once. Steeger started with [Crumbling Necropolis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis), showing off his Grixis tendencies, while Florian Pils went for whatever with [Armillary Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armillary+Sphere). Five lands into the game, Pils put down [Putrid Leech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) as his first play and had [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate) for Steeger’s answer, [Cloudheath Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudheath+Drake).


[Yoke of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yoke+of+the+Damned) from Steeger handled [Gorger Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorger+Wurm), but Pils had [Slave of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slave+of+Bolas). He took Steeger’s fresh [Grixis Sojourners](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grixis+Sojourners), and after a brief debate about who would get to remove something at end of turn (the controller of the Sojourners, e.g. Pils), Steeger scooped up his cards, especially since an earlier [Demonic Dread](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Dread) yielded nothing useful for Steeger and his deck had run out of steam.


Florian Pils 1 – 0 Thomas Steeger


Game 2:


[Drag Down](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drag+Down) from Steeger handled Pils’ first threat, Sewn-Exe Drake. Not missing a beat, or a land drop for that matter, Pils tapped five to play [Putrid Leech](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrid+Leech) and [Hissing Iguanar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hissing+Iguanar). Steeger, with [Scourge Devil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+Devil) in play, had just [Yoke of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yoke+of+the+Damned) for the Leech to force a trade with his Devil.


Pils found the trade advantageous. One of his cards for two of Steeger’s? Good play! And Pils had a follow-up in [Nacatl Outlander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nacatl+Outlander). Steeger wanted to even out the board by Unearthing [Scourge Devil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scourge+Devil) and sacrificing it to [Fleshbag Marauder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleshbag+Marauder), forcing Pils to give up his Outlander.


But the semi-pro put up unrelenting pressure, with just the right mix of removal in the form of [Resounding Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Resounding+Thunder), and army bigger by two creatures. Steeger had no way of holding off Pils with just [Shambling Remains](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shambling+Remains). He dropped his irrelevant hand on the table and extended the other one, disappointed in his deck’s performance especially in the first game.


Florian Pils 2 – 0 Thomas Steeger


“I drew the worse half of the deck”, Steeger said about the first game, but also admitted that his deck wasn’t that consistent on the mana front. “Maybe I got too lucky yesterday so today balances out?” Steeger wondered. We’ll see.




Saturday, 1:15 p.m. -- Competitive Lounging
-------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

Video screens have a terrible attraction. Magic players simply cannot stay away, especially when they see shiny rectangles with Magic artwork. [Duels of the Planeswalkers](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Digital/DuelsOfThePlaneswalkers.aspx), Magic’s Xbox Live debut, is on display here, and players flock to it. Unsurprisingly, the success DotP enjoys on the Xbox Live Marketplace is mirrored here. But it is not just the four screens with 360s behind them. The blue-green lounge, with sofas and chairs and comfortable cushions, is just as much reason to chill out there as the videgame is.


Magic players wouldn’t be Magic players, though, if they weren’t competitive. So when Klaus Jöns and Raul Porojan got hold of the controllers, they went at it like digital fiends. A crowd gathered to watch the two of them take the place of Planeswalkers for a duel. Laughter and good-natured banter abounded as those two fought themselves through the digital interface.


Who won? Nobody cared. The game felt more like a Planeswalker office prank, or Planeswalker beach party, or Planeswalker after-work cocktail round – or a combination of all of them. So if you find yourself near an extravagantly decorated sofa lounge with four 26” flatscreen TVs and a handful of Xboxes: Try Duels of the Planeswalkers, it’s fun! ![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/lounge.jpg)




Saturday, 1:42 p.m. -- Probably Incidental: Talking to Jan Ruess
----------------------------------------------------------------


*by Florian Koch*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/Jan-Ruess.jpg)*Jan Ruess***You finished day one with a 4-3 record. Before the tournament, you told us that you really desired to become national champion. This must be disappointing for you. What went wrong?**


Yes, it is definitely a disappointment. The constructed portion went really bad. I played Jund control and the deck did not deliver at all. Dennis Johannsen plays the same deck and he is 3-1, so the deck can obviously do a lot better. If I had to pick again I would choose the same deck. Afterwards I swept my draft pod with a bad deck.


**How was your second draft this morning?**


My deck is much better than yesterday, but I lost to the first round anyway. Just now I won against Alex Fanghänel. The deck definitely should deliver a 2-1, so I expect to win the last round, too.


**German nationals seem to be very hard for the most accomplished players. In recent years the finals saw solid players, but not the names you’d expect to see there, like Sebastian Thaler or Simon Görtzen. Do you have an explanation for that?**


It is probably mostly incidental. But then the German nationals are extremely late in the season. So even unprepared players come with highly tuned decks and there is not much tech to discover any more, either. Differences in skill and effort of deck selection and building thus don’t have the effect they might otherwise have.




Round 10 Feature Match: Simon Görtzen vs. Benedikt Sonnenschein
---------------------------------------------------------------


*by Tobias Henke*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/fm10_benedikt.jpg)*Benedikt Sonnenschein* Both players currently have three losses and need to accumulate four straight wins to secure a top 8 berth. Benedikt picked up his losses in the first three rounds of the tourney, thus is still undefeated in draft, while Simon started 2-2 in Standard and finished yesterday’s play at 4-3.


Benedikt had to mulligan, then went into the think tank for his six-card hand. He re-emerged from that with a brave decision to keep. Unluckily, he missed a land drop, had to discard, and when finally he had deployed his first creature ([Kathari Screecher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kathari+Screecher)), Simon already had a board chock-full of [Monstrous Carabid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monstrous+Carabid), [Carrion Thrash](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Thrash), with [Necrogenesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis) tugging along, just in case the heavy hitters would somehow fail to deliver. But of course they did.


**Simon Görtzen 1 - 0 Benedikt Sonnenschein**


This time around, it was Simon’s turn to mulligan (down to six), while Benedikt started with a full grip of seven and went to business: [Armillary Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armillary+Sphere), [Aven Trailblazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Trailblazer), and on turn four [Esper Stormblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Esper+Stormblade) plus the activation of said Sphere saw him use his mana perfectly every turn.


Meanwhile Simon had [Necrogenesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis), but not much else, him being stuck with lots of [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s, [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s on the battlefield and [Caldera Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caldera+Hellion) in his hand. He was not completely out of the game yet, though. At the end of Benedikt’s next turn (Benedikt had cast [Lorescale Coatl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lorescale+Coatl)) Simon cycled [Deadshot Minotaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadshot+Minotaur), used that to build a token with [Necrogenesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis), and finally drew a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) in the process.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/fm10_simon.jpg)*Simon Görtzen*Benedikt tried to increase the pressure and tighten his grip on the game with [Flurry of Wings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flurry+of+Wings), but walked (rather: flew) straight into Simon’s trap: Next turn, another red source enabled [Caldera Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caldera+Hellion): that got rid of Benedikt’s air force, while being 4/4 (and surviving) itself.


Benedikt was visibly shocked by this sudden turn of events. [Rhox Bodyguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Bodyguard) was all his deck could muster and now the game was firmly in Simon’s hand. [Scarland Thrinax](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scarland+Thrinax) entered the battlefield and [Necrogenesis](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrogenesis) went to work on the densely populated garveyards. [Deathbringer Thoctar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathbringer+Thoctar) might not even have been necessary to carry this game home, but Simon had it and was prepared to use it. It didn’t take long...


**Simon Görtzen 1 - 0 Benedikt Sonnenschein**

Round 11 Feature Match: Eugen Libkin vs. Sebastian Thaler
---------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

Thaler is one of the Germans who warrants the phrase “he needs no introduction”. At 22 points, he needed to win out from here, just like his opponent: Libkin had seven wins going in and needed four more to make Top 8.


**Game 1:**


Thaler, with Merfolk, won the die roll and put pressure on the board, as Merfolk do. Libnik, with Faeries, had Doomblade to kill [Merrow Reejerey](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Reejerey), but missed his fourth land drop, and Thaler promptly revealed another Reejerey. [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) from Libnik solved that problem, [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) flashed in as a blocker, and Thaler was boardless.


But Merfolk is a deck that has (and always had) the ability to bring out little men with little trouble. It’s the same in this format, and [Stonybrook Banneret](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonybrook+Banneret) joined [Sygg, River Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sygg%2C+River+Guide). Thaler attacked with his two [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)walkers and a [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault). Libnik blocked what he could: [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) stopped [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault). Libnik also tried [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) in that combat, but Thaler couldn’t be having with that. [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) countered the Clique and drew him a card.


[Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) from Libnik revealed just a [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Mystic Gate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Gate) in Thaler’s hand. But at 17 life, Thaler could easily afford to race with his on-board [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)walkers, taking Libnik to 5. He tried [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) just to have something, but Thaler calmly revealed Command #2, and that was that.


Eugen Libkin 0 – 1 Sebastian Thaler


**Game 2:**


Libnik decided to go first, crucial for Faeries to establish enough mana for early control actions. He skimped on a turn-two [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and used [Broken Ambitions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broken+Ambitions) instead to counter Thaler’s [Silvergill Adept](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvergill+Adept). The Clash revealed [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher) for Thaler and [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) for Libkin, which he put under his library. Land would not be his problem in this game, then.


But that [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher)? That could quite possibly become a problem. The Thrasher can eat a Faerie with every attack, so that [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) essentially becomes a [Pacifism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) with an upkeep of one life. Of course, Faeries has numerous other ways to stop a Thrasher, but stop it it must, eventually.


The [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) Libkin had declined to play on turn two came a turn later, and its first offspring traded with Thaler’s Silverglil Adept in combat. Thaler refilled the empty board with Glen-Elendra Archmage, and Libnik decided on [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) in Thaler’s end of turn.


“You don’t also have [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation), do you?” Thaler presumed.


Libkin shrugged, untapped and played [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation), taking Thaler’s Archmage. Thaler had [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) to immediately remove the Scion and its back-breaking bonuses and then brought [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher) to the battlefield. Libjkin attacked Thaler down to 14 and played [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) #2. A second [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) from Thaler targeted [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation), because the former Rookie wanted his Glen-Elendra Archmage back. Libkin sacrificed the Archmage to counter the Path and keep his Sower, but of course Persist gave the Archmage back to his owner, viz: Thaler.


Libkin tried [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique). He wanted to know what Thaler was up to, and get more power on the board to fly over Thaler’s two-creature ground force. Thaler had [Sage’s Dousing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage%E2%80%99s+Dousing), which left Libkin with just two cards in hand.


The untap gave Thaler five [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher) triggers on the stack. In response, Libkin showed [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp), with just one card left in his hand. Thaler looked at his hand, then at Libkin. He had [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), but what about Libkin?


Eventually, Thaler called the bluff, saw Libkin’s [Agony Warp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agony+Warp) and raised him a [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command). Libkin shrugged, and the Command resolved.


From that point on in the match, Libnik had essentially lost. He was able to get Thaler down to 7 life, then 2 with his Faeries and the [Sower of Temptation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation). What he did not have was an answer to [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher), and his two [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s ticked down his life total at a rate of two a turn.

The former Rookie also had [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) naming [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique), sealing the deal while his [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher) decimated Libnik’s defense. The end came when Thaler, on 2 life, used his [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command) in Libnik’s upkeep to tap all his Faeries and attacked with [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault), Glen-Elendra Archmage, [Meddling Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meddling+Mage) and a 9/9 [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher). All Libnik had was [Gargoyle Castle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gargoyle+Castle) to chump-block the [Wake Thrasher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wake+Thrasher), but the remaining team took Libnik to 2.


Thaler said go, and Libnik’s pair of [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s preserved Thaler’s chance for a Sunday appearance.

We all know stories of horrible mana screw, horrible mana flood, or hideous misplays costing games. But then there are also those games, where something so completely unexpected happens that you simply want to exclaim: “There has to be an error in the matrix!”

Saturday, 7:42 p.m. – Running Sushi and A Timely Round
------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/last%20matches.JPG)For the first time in history, or at least as far as all concerned can remember, a round at German Nationals finished before time ran out. It took the judges by total surprise when head judge Michael Wiese announced: “Round 13 is over” with five minutes left on the clock. The black shirts scrambled to heed the announcement and gazed amazed at the clock still running.


After round 13, players down to 12th place in the standing had a chance to grab a slot in Sunday’s Top 8 competition. Four of them secured their spot with an intentional draw: Tobias Gräfensteiner, Andreas Schraut, Thomas Steeger and Andre Luff, who had qualified for his first Nationals just the night before it started via one of the grinders. Besides those four, the top seed – Florian Pils – chose to play and win the last round to cement his place at the top of Swiss standings and to ensure a Top 8 appearance for Sebastian Thaler. Had Pils lost his match against Jan-Philipp Budde (no relation), Thaler’s 31 points would not have been enough.


Speaking of Thaler, he has found the best name for a deck we have seen all weekend. His Merfolk deck is named “Running Sushi”. By the way: Just like Andre Luff, Thaler’s last-round opponent Simon Leigh also had qualified by winning a grinder and was just a hair’s breadth away from the Top 8, which is rounded out by Sebastian Kuchenbecker and GP Hanover winner Lino Burgold.


We will see tomorrow who of those will ultimately carry the title of German National Champion 2009. For now, the Top 8 competitors will spend the night poring over each other’s decklists. The rest of the assorted Magic players will try their hand at bowling, beer, drafting and a trivia contest at the Player’s Party tonight.

Saturday, 9:12 p.m. – Play The Game, See The World with Legacy
--------------------------------------------------------------


*by Hanno Terbuyken*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gernat09/Stefes%20and%20Boness.JPG)*Martin Stefes and Michael-Andrej Boness, the two PTGSTW winners.*There is a new tournament series going on all around the world: Play The Game, See The World.
