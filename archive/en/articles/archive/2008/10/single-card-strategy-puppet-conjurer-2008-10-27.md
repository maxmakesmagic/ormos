Single Card Strategy: Puppet Conjurer
=====================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 27, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar












![Single Card Strategy](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/global/images/mtgcom_feature_93_pic1_en.jpg)
![Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Puppet+Conjurer)

Whether you're the Timmiest of Timmies or the Spikiest of Spikes, a normal, everyday Melvin or an Über-Melvin, or even if you're someone who just plain loves just plain [Grizzly Bears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears), *Shards of Alara* has some choice bits reserved especially for you. I could be (am) wrong, but I think [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) was designed specifically to make me happy (Elf + "Draw a card" = Win!). I'm going to start calling it Solipsistic Elf.


For those unfamiliar with the SCS MO, my job today will be to take a single card—in this case, [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer)—and discuss some of the strategies (and tactics!) that it can be a part of in your future games, hopefully spurring some fun deckbuilding of your own. I guess all that was obvious from the title. Less obviously, the chosen card has traditionally been an interesting uncommon, making a playset more accessible to more players than a rare while still maintaining that build-around-me potential. Selecting a suitable card from *Shards* was a little tricky, since so many of the exciting uncommons are "French vanilla" creatures like [Rhox War Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+War+Monk), "French vanilla" creatures with a double-shot of espresso like [Fire-Field Ogre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Field+Ogre), or Swiss Army-style utility cards like the Battlemage and Charm cycles. All fine cards, but my strategy advice wouldn't be much more than "Play 'em!" After narrowing the field down to a few finalists, I opted for [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) for one major reason:


Those Homunculus tokens are creepy as [expletive deleted]! Pediophobes, avert your eyes!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/arcana/1688_token2.jpg)Good [Godsire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Godsire), just look at that thing! It's like a cross between a neonatal [Uatu the Watcher](http://www.marvel.com/universe/Uatu_The_Watcher) and a Russian nesting doll. It is a truly unsettling image. What makes it off-the-charts disturbing for me is that, in context, one of the primary tasks of these dapper "little men" is to hurl their fragile little bodies in front of things like barreling [Rakeclaw Gargantuan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakeclaw+Gargantuan)s and otherwise unstoppable [Spearbreaker Behemoth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spearbreaker+Behemoth)s. It's a tough job, but somebody's gotta do it. It might as well be a sinister-looking child, if you ask me. Homunculi: where ancients tread. If they get gobbled up by something called a [Skullmulcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullmulcher), they can consider themselves lucky. Otherwise, they just get euthanized by their creator a turn later. What a life! Now, we still haven't seen the words "sacrifice a human" on a card, but it seems like we're inching mighty close to that point.


### Homunculus, Sweet Homunculus


Besides birthing the creepiest token creatures in **Magic** history, [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) is a fine card to build around because it has so many "moving parts." Those parts, in turn, have properties that link them to a web of other cards. Together, they form a constellation of interactions; call it Urza Major. [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) itself is black, an artifact, a creature, a Human, and a Wizard, allowing you to play it profitably in decks with cards that care about those things. It also has an ability that, with one activation, allows you to create a token that is a blue permanent, an artifact, a creature, and a Homunculus. During your subsequent upkeep, you get to send a permanent with (probably) those same qualities to the graveyard.


That's a lot to consider, and there are many angles of approach we can take, so why don't we look at these parts and properties (i.e. read the friendly card) a little more closely.



> ***![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif), ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20170424152623im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/tap.gif): Put a 0/1 blue Homunculus artifact creature token into play.***



![Thousand-Year Elixir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thousand-Year+Elixir)

First off, we can see that it has a tap symbol in the activation cost. In **Magic**, this means two things. One, you can only use the ability once each turn; and two, with help from other cards, you can use the ability many times per turn. While it's probably painfully obvious, that simple fact does open up for consideration a whole class of cards and it doesn't hurt to remind oneself that nothing is at seems in this game, and the rules are made to be broken.


Some of the cards that you might want to include in your [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) deck care specifically about creatures with tap abilities—cards like [Magewright's Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magewright%27s+Stone) and [Thousand-Year Elixir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thousand-Year+Elixir)—and allow you to double up on the activations. Others have more general utility, allowing you to untap creatures (the on-theme [Puppeteer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer) and [Puppet Strings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Strings)), artifacts ([Voltaic Key](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Key), [Tezzeret the Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret+the+Seeker), and [Filigree Sages](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Filigree+Sages), among others), artifact creatures ([Voltaic Construct](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voltaic+Construct)), artifacts or creatures ([Aphetto Alchemist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aphetto+Alchemist)), or simply permanents of any kind ([Tidewater Minion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidewater+Minion) and [Fatestitcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatestitcher)). Because [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer)'s ability is an activated one, another card you might want to consider is *Lorwyn*'s [Rings of Brighthearth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rings+of+Brighthearth), which, as it happens, also works nicely with all the aforementioned untappers.


Once you've gone through the hassle of activating your [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer)'s ability, you are rewarded with a new permanent that, as I said, has a whole bunch of relevant attributes. For starters, it's a 0/1 creature. Admittedly, that's not terribly exciting. You won't be giving it trample with your [Mosstodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mosstodon) any time soon. Of course, this being **Magic**, you can change this sad reality to a happier one in any number of ways. Since it has no abilities, you can try [Muraganda Petroglyphs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muraganda+Petroglyphs). Since it's blue, you can boost its power with any of the blue Lieges from *Shadowmoor* block ([Glen Elendra Liege](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Liege) will even pump [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) itself!) or you can turn to [Sunken City](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunken+City), the old friend of [Homarid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homarid)s, [Homarid Spawning Bed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Homarid%2BSpawning%2BBed), and [Merfolk of the Pearl Trident](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Merfolk%2Bof%2Bthe%2BPearl%2BTrident) alike. Since it's an artifact, you can take advantage of [Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium), the new "artifact lord." And finally, since it's a Homunculus, you can... well, here your options are limited. There's [Brass Herald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Brass%2BHerald), [Coat of Arms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coat+of+Arms), and, with a little work, [Door of Destinies](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Door+of+Destinies) (cue the [Nameless Inversion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion)s!). Beyond that, there isn't much. The one sad thing about [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) is that it doesn't work that well in a Homunculus theme deck, what with its penchant for "[scowling] at his imperfect creation, then [crushing] it to scrap." That goes for you, too, [Chameleon Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chameleon+Colossus).

But wait, there's more! Many, many cards in **Magic** reward you for putting into play a permanent of a certain quality. [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) allows you to put a permanent with many qualities into play every single turn. It's a blue creature, you say? Why not experiment with [Dire Undercurrents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dire+Undercurrents)? It's an artifact? How about tapping down a permanent with [Lumengrid Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumengrid+Sentinel), charging up your [Serum Tank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Tank), or pumping up [Glaze Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glaze+Fiend) and its predecessor, the mighty [Vermiculos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vermiculos)? It's a Homunculus? Well, why not petition R&D to print a card that triggers whenever a Homunculus comes into play? Because that's probably your best option at this point.


### Atog Bites (Little) Man


Okay, so you're well on your way to making one or more Homunculi each turn. You've got an army of evil, robed doll-people lined up like bowling pins across from a first-striking Gargantuan. What now? Well, your options are many, but most of them involve your little Homunculi having to cancel their retirement plans.


Half of the Atogs and close to a third of all the black creatures in **Magic** would like nothing more than to dine on puppet-based dishes. While these percentages might not be true now (ever), I've got to consider future readers (and alternate-future readers) in my calculations. And once word gets out, Zombies and Horrors and Golems and even Bird Skeletons will be lining up for some good old fashioned Homunculus-style cooking. As of now, you can feed your tokens to [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) to make it stronger; to [Sadistic Hypnotist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sadistic+Hypnotist), [Tar Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tar+Fiend), or [Nezumi Bone-Reader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Bone-Reader) to make your opponent discard cards; to [Skullmulcher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skullmulcher) to draw cards; to [Gutless Ghoul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gutless+Ghoul) to gain life; to [Ertai, the Corrupted](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ertai%2C+the+Corrupted) to counter spells; to [Stronghold Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin) to destroy a nonblack creature; to such insatiable monsters as [Yawgmoth Demon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth+Demon), [Rust Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rust+Elemental), [Devouring Strossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Devouring+Strossus), [Lord of the Pit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Pit), [Doomgape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomgape), or [Archdemon of Unx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archdemon+of+Unx), simply to keep them around and smashing face for another turn. And that's just some of the *creatures*!

[Bone Splinters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bone+Splinters), [Abjure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abjure), [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast), [Goblin Bombardment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Bombardment), [Attrition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attrition), [Recurring Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recurring+Nightmare), [Skull Catapult](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+Catapult), and many other cards allow you to trade your 0/1 tokens for an increase in some desirable resource or a decrease in such resources for your opponent. That's one way of putting it. Another would be to simply proclaim them to be "cannon fodder" and, quite literally, launch them out of a cannon. Less literally, you can use [Doom Cannon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Cannon) or [Fodder Cannon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fodder+Cannon) for you Homunculus-launching purposes.

But you'd better do all this stuff pretty quick, because of the second line of game text on [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer):



> ***At the beginning of your upkeep, sacrifice a Homunculus.***



![Hissing Iguanar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hissing+Iguanar)

These babies are temporary. The good news is twofold. First of all, you can get around this "drawback" by doing things like skipping your upkeep step entirely ([Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub)) or by changing all of your creatures into some non-Homunculus creature type ([Conspiracy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conspiracy)). Better yet, you can turn this mandated man sacrifice into a bonus. A handful of creatures from Jund's dragonscape come with a built-in [Sadistic Glee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sadistic+Glee), so the [Scavenger Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenger+Drake)s, [Rockslide Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rockslide+Elemental)s, and [Algae Gharial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Algae+Gharial)s of that plane will no doubt appreciate the clockwork binning of little men. [Hissing Iguanar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hissing+Iguanar), [Vicious Shadows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vicious+Shadows), and the banhammered [Disciple of the Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disciple+of+the+Vault) punish your opponent whether or not they had anything to do with the timely demise of your Homunculi. Equipping your short-lived tokens with [Deathrender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrender) also seems pretty exciting to me, especially if you're also playing with some of the big black monsters I mentioned earlier and can drop them into play at a significant discount. As with the sacrifice outlets I discussed, the cards that trigger whenever a creature (or artifact) is put into a graveyard can lead to all sorts of adventures in resource management. And isn't that why we're all here? You know, besides the Elves?


### It's Time to Play the Music!


To give you a better idea of the kinds of decks that make puppet conjury worthwhile, I've put together a couple of simple ones that use mostly recent commons and uncommons. The first one uses [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) to hold down the fort while your flying creatures whittle away at your opponent's life total, or, in the case of [Tower Gargoyle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tower+Gargoyle), chops away at it like a lumberjack. Due to the inclusion of [Glaze Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glaze+Fiend), I made sure that almost all of the other creatures were artifacts, and without much of a stretch, did the same with the spells.

[Etherium Astrolabe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etherium+Astrolabe), as Mark Rosewater mentioned recently, is pretty awesome with [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer). I'm not sure you would ever want more than two in a deck because its value depends so heavily on the presence of [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer), but one seems like a must, especially considering it's basically a combat trick if you have a [Glaze Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glaze+Fiend) in play. ### It's Time to Light the Lights!



![Phyrexian Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian+Vault)

The next deck shifts the focus from artifacts and moves it to creatures. With [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) Edict-ing your Homunculi each turn and sacrifice outlets like [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) and [Phyrexian Vault](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Vault) eating all of your cheap "comes into play" creatures like [Ravenous Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats), [Blister Beetle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blister+Beetle), and *Lorwyn*'s blue and black evoke creatures, your [Scavenger Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scavenger+Drake)s will become monsters in no time. There are so many creatures in the deck not long for this world that it seemed wise to splurge on a pair of [Dire Undercurrents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dire+Undercurrents) and a pair of [Grave Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Pact)s. Once both enchantments are in play, a single activation of [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) will result in one drawn card for you and one sacrificed creature for your opponent. As of now, the deck has no use for the red mana provided by the set of [Crumbling Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crumbling+Necropolis)es, but the version I've been fooling around with online made room for two copies of [Sedris, the Traitor King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sedris%2C+the+Traitor+King), the Grixis necromancer who allows you to reuse all of your creatures' "comes into play" abilities. Again, this deck is ripe for customization.

### It's Time to Meet the Puppets!



![Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Intruder+Alarm)

Finally, here's an example of the kind of combo deck you can build using [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer). For long-time combo enthusiasts, any new creature that taps to put another creature into play should set off warning bells. Bells that sound like, oh, an [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm). Most [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm) decks that I've seen have had to use green, either for [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) or some land animation spell like, uh, [Animate Land](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animate+Land). Not anymore, now that [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) is on the scene. Because [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm) untaps all creatures whenever a creature comes into play, all you need is a creature like [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) and a creature that produces enough mana to activate the creature-making ability, and you can make infinite creatures. In this case, either [Silver Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver+Myr) or [Scuttlemutt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scuttlemutt) will cover the one blue mana needed to make your arbitrarily large amount of Homunculi.


Once you've got those three cards in play, you can do pretty much whatever you like. [Sage of Lat-Nam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Lat-Nam) will allow you to draw your deck. [Arcum Dagsson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcum+Dagsson) can pull every noncreature artifact out of your library and put all of them directly into play. From there, you can win by milling your opponent with [Grinding Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grinding+Station), or you can cause a death of a thousand pings with [Blasting Station](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasting+Station), or you can simply play [Coat of Arms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coat+of+Arms) and earn an arbitrarily large number of style points for dealing lethal damage with Homunculus tokens. You can adapt this deck, too, even by cutting the [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm)s entirely, since [Puppet Conjurer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppet+Conjurer) forms a perfectly serviceable combo with [Sage of Lat-Nam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Lat-Nam) and [Arcum Dagsson](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcum+Dagsson) even if you don't drag infinity into it.
