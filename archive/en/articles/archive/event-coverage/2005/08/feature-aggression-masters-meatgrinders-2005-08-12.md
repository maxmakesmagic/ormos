Feature: Aggression Masters the Meatgrinders
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 12, 2005 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







The U.S. Nationals Grinders have historically been the pinnacle of watching the metagame as a spectator sport. In years past, we have glimpsed the first appearance of Kjeldoran Outpost, the rise to dominance of Oath of Druids, and in the hands of young David Williams, even the hairline fracture Survival of the Fittest before its big *break* at Worlds 1998. Legendary teams have been forged in the Nationals Grinders with their $42 Tickets and long bus rides, and these tournaments have historically set the metagame for Nationals itself, and for the rest of the Championship season.


But who could have predicted this?

Agro Red has been a staple of the format since French Regionals months ago. Since then, it has evolved from a base-land destruction deck to a more focused beatdown; of the nine "Red Deck Wins" variants that graduated the meatgrinders, seven started [Sword of Fire and Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Fire+and+Ice), with the other two running Swords and/or [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte) in the board. [Zo-Zu the Punisher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zo-Zu+the+Punisher) and [Jinxed Choker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jinxed+Choker) have become common cards as well… The new batch of Red Decks are all about damage… even if they have to deal some to themselves.


Notice how Mike McGee can get an aggressive start with [Frostling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frostling)*and* has incorporated more burn elements - such as long game [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) - into an archetype that once started not just [Stone Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain) and [Demolish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demolish) in addition to [Molten Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Rain).

Red we had on the radar… But White Weenie? The surprise of the Grinders is that the little white men are *really good.* Sporting similar aggressive strategies to Red Deck Wins, White Weenie gets in there early and forces the opponent to play on the defensive. Though white traditionally lacks the late-game finishers of a [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) or [Arc-Slogger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc-Slogger), [Jinxed Choker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jinxed+Choker) has given some versions new life. [Shining Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shining+Shoal) is another card that can get those last two points of damage in, and [Hokori, Dust Drinker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hokori%2C+Dust+Drinker) is present to punish slower decks. Hokori not only slows down big spell decks in general, but even Green decks - that are now so reliant on comes into play tapped acceleration via [Sakura-Tribe Elder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) and sometimes [Kodama's Reach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) - fail to generate any advantage from having more physical lands *in play.*


"White Weenie is simply the best deck in the format," says Sam Gomersall, visiting member of the English National Team, Magic Invitational participant, and international superstar. "It won English Nationals… What more do you want?"


The key to White Weenie is that [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix) is superb in the format. Each of the six qualifying White Weenie decks started *at least* three copies of the *Mirrodin* Block hoser, with five of the six running all four main.


"White Weenie is the only deck that can exploit [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix)," Sam continues. "Any other deck gets destroyed by it, so they can't play it… whereas White Weenie can just stick it in and use it to beat all the other decks."



![Culling Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Culling+Scales)

Just think about it: [Mindslaver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindslaver), Kiki-Jiki, [Arc-Slogger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc-Slogger), [Vedalken Shackles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles), [Oblivion Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Stone), even Aether Vial…the key threats in many of the format's best decks are all shut down by [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix). The White Weenie deck, on the other hand, can go about its [Lantern Kami](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lantern+Kami) and [Suntail Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suntail+Hawk) business without adverse interaction. The versions that run [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox) can even keep tapping that accelerator while "locked" under their own Matrixes because the Matrix is kind enough to look the other way where mana abilities from artifacts are concerned.


At the same time, White Weenie's proactive cards are highly effective at filling particular niches. Look at a card like [Auriok Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Champion). As a pure beatdown creature, [Auriok Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Champion) is rather poor… a 1/1 for two mana. But in a format with so much red, it is a superb blocker. Incidentally, [Auriok Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Champion) generates a Soul Warden-like life increase that is doubly helpful against Agro Red Decks and all their fire. While [Leonin Skyhunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Skyhunter) and [Suntail Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suntail+Hawk) tear the skies, less flashy cards like [Auriok Champion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Champion) and [Raise the Alarm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+the+Alarm) help White Weenie win the race despite being far behind in the reach category: they block.


I consider it a point of pride that Kuroda-style (i.e. "Flores") Red took home two Grinders slots as well… *twice as many at [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail)*, thank you very much. Kuroda-style Red, while considerably slower to kill than Red Deck Wins, has far better tools for fighting a deck like White Weenie. Admittedly, a deck designed to abuse [Sensei's Divining Top](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) and [Arc-Slogger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc-Slogger) is hit hard by [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix), but [Culling Scales](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Culling+Scales) was chosen specifically as an "[Oblivion Stone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Stone)" that operated even when the opponent had Matrix in play! There is a fierce dance between Champions, Scales, and [Terashi's Grasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terashi%27s+Grasp)s to be sure, but Kuroda-style Red has the most powerful reach in the format as well as strong answers in [Sowing Salt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sowing+Salt) and [Unforge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unforge) for other predicted opponents and their specialty lands or Swords.

The big story after White Weenie has to be the disappointing performance of [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail). Considered by most to be the best deck in the format, [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) produced only one Grinder invitation out of a possible 24. Metagame pundits blamed it on a bad day and a lot of hate.


"People know how to beat it now," said Teddy Cardgame, blogger extraordinaire, Magic editor, and metagame fanboy. "Tooth was probably under-played considering how good a deck it is."


Considering the fact that one stray [Molten Rain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Molten+Rain) or a [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix) and a Hokori can give a beatdown deck *just enough* time to take down the Deck to Beat, perhaps the rest of the format has just caught up. Maybe the singularly dominant archetype from U.S. Regionals and all of post-Affinity Standard has finally been de-throned…but check back on Sunday afternoon for this one. I'm not willing to call it a day for the big green guys just yet.


My pick for the most inventive deck of the Grinders has to be Teddy Wyly's Proteus Belcher:

Combining many of the best elements of a true Control Blue deck with that "icky combo feeling" (tm Chris Pikula), Wyly's deck can attack an opponent on multiple fronts. Against a straightforward creature deck, its weapons are many: [Vedalken Shackles](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Shackles) and [Threads of Disloyalty](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Threads+of+Disloyalty) are the all stars of anti-beatdown in today's blue arsenal. But in Wyly's deck, they have a second motive. Wyly can steal a guy and then aim his [Proteus Staff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proteus+Staff) at it. Check that deck list: there is only one creature. He really is going to get that [Darksteel Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/usnat05/feat4_fan.jpg)[Proteus Staff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Proteus+Staff) also allows Teddy to stack his deck for purposes of [Goblin Charbelcher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Charbelcher). With nothing more than a [Fabricate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fabricate) and a [Blinkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus), Wyly can set up a lethal Charbelcher as well. This is particularly good in games where Wyly accidentally draws his [Darksteel Colossus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Colossus).


Lastly, against [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail), Wyly's sideboard shares the ascendant anti-Green suite. For all of you wondering how he can cast [Mephidross Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire)…he can't. Wyly uses [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast) to piggyback the opponent's [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) in order to set up his sideboard creatures. [Twincast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twincast)'s [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) fetches Uyo and [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion), and with [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) still on the stack, Uyo copies it yet again for a pair of Vampires. Before [Tooth and Nail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tooth+and+Nail) ever finishes resolving, the two Vampires and the [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) set up infinite damage with the [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) "machine gun" technique; every +1/+1 counter [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) uses to shoot is replaced by two more thanks to the power-up provided by *two* [Mephidross Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mephidross+Vampire)s.


Tricky? Yes. Powerful? Indubitably. Wyly's deck, like many of the others, though, is completely shut down by [Damping Matrix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damping+Matrix). White Weenie has historically been a great deck at U.S. Nationals. In a year dominated by [Necro](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necro)potence, Bill Macey ground in back in 1996… and did it again with a different White Weenie come 1999. Matt Linde showed Mike Long the Abeyance in 1998, and Kyle Rose won it all with his Waylays the next year. Even when held down by powerhouses like Napster and Angry Hermit, Elliot Fung was able to battle to the Top 8 of U.S. Nationals in Orlando.


Don't be surprised if history repeats itself.
