=================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on October 14, 2009 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week I [wrote about](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/great-white-weenie-2009-10-07-0) my second favorite card from *Zendikar*, [Conqueror's Pledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conqueror%27s+Pledge). After another week of testing the White Weenie archetype I found that my list was missing a key card from the new set. [Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) is a powerful answer to cards like [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) and [Volcanic Fallout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout). [Brave the Elements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brave+the+Elements) also serves as an excellent finisher or combat blowout against decks like Vampires. Here's the updated White Weenie list:

White Weenie is a quick beatdown deck. This week I would like to look at the other end of the spectrum. My favorite card from *Zendikar* is, without any doubt, [Luminarch Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension). I've gotten to play with the card a few times, and I am sure that it is one of the best Turbo-Fog finishers ever printed (if not the best outright). I scoured [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) and put together a very powerful budget archetype.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/boab/boab60_luminarchSplash.jpg)  
I have always been a big fan of Turbo-Fog. This type of deck punishes opponents who want to win a traditional game of attacking and blocking. The deck has an inherent weakness to decks with a lot of burn, but makes up for its bad matchups with nearly unloseable creature matchups. A format without Faeries seems like a very ripe place to unleash Turbo-Fog.


The first piece of any successful Turbo-Fog deck is [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine). I want to supplement the card-drawing artifact with an additional two-sided card draw effect. [Font of Mythos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) seemed like an obvious inclusion in this spot. Many people may be confused by these cards. [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) and [Font of Mythos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) are, in the abstract, not good cards. Your opponent gets to draw the extra cards first, and you spent a card to start the insanity. For either of these cards to warrant a place in your deck, you need to make sure you're abusing the effect more than your opponent. Decks that try to play more powerful spells than their opponent are not the answer here. If your deck devotes slots to [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) and spends a turn to cast it, then you are already far behind. I do not care how powerful your spells areâ€”you will not win most games under those conditions. For a card like [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) or [Font of Mythos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) to be an asset, your deck needs to attempt to change the way a game of **Magic** is played entirely. Turbo-Fog isn't looking to trump its opponent's cards; it seeks to completely invalidate them. Your opponent can draw and play as many creatures as he or she wishes, but no amount of creatures will be enough to kill you if you have a Fog effect waiting for them every turn.

After deciding on four [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine) and some number of [Font of Mythos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos), I decided to check Gatherer for Fog effects in Standard. I was given the following list.


**[Angelsong](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelsong):** This seems like a very solid choice. If I'm playing against some type of control deck, I can probably just cycle this and try to get closer to my [Luminarch Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension). Two-mana Fogs are always welcome inclusions in this type of deck.


[Fog:](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fog%3A) Plain-Jane, old-fashioned Fog.


[Safe Passage:](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Safe+Passage%3A) Three mana is a lot to pay for a Fog, but given our options, we may need to play a card like this.


**[Tanglesap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tanglesap):** I cannot think of many playable Standard creatures that trample right now. This is, essentially, another two-mana Fog.


After looking at those cards it becomes pretty clear that this deck is going to be green-white. I will round out the deck with some spot removal for the early game and [Martial Coup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup) for late-game defense.

The Turbo-Fog deck may not have performed as well as I had hoped, but I still feel it has a good amount of power. In the Jund matchup, they really need to draw more than one [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse) in the early turns of the game. The Burn and [Time Sieve](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Sieve) matchups will be extremely hard no matter what we do. This still seems like a very solid choice if your local shop has a lot of White Weenie, Jund, and Vampire players. I'm happy with the way the deck turned out. I don't particularly like the [Journey to Nowhere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Nowhere). and I would probably replace any copies I could with [Day of Judgement](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgement). I think this deck may benefit from a blue splash for a play set of [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) also. Perhaps the deck can eschew [Luminarch Ascension](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Luminarch+Ascension) altogether in favor of a plan to mill the opponent with [Font of Mythos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Font+of+Mythos) and [Howling Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Mine).
