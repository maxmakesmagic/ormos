Draft Recap: Hoaen's Pimp Hand is Way Strong
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 5, 2006 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TedKnutson.jpg)
By Ted Knutson











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptpra06/draft1_hoaen.jpg)*Hoaen entered Prague with a draft reputation matched by few other competitors.*The Ho is a pimp when it comes to Limited formats. Underrated for his Constructed skills, Rich Hoaen is widely regarded as one of the very best Limited players in the game by virtually every Magician on the planet. The Japanese speak of him in hushed, reverent tones, and people up and leave ***Magic Online*** draft queues in droves when rhoaen deigns to speak in one. Therefore the choice to follow his first draft in this unique and fascinating full block was an obvious one.


Talking to Richie before the draft, he said he'd been going four colors in a lot of the practice drafts he had done, whether he was drafting green or not. Here in this draft, he opened a relatively weak *Ravnica* pack with [Brainspoil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainspoil), [Thundersong Trumpeter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundersong+Trumpeter), [Bramble Elemental](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bramble+Elemental), and [Veteran Armorer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Veteran+Armorer) as the only notable picks, and immediately went green, picking the Bramble. Former World Champion Julien Nuijten was seated to Rich's right, and immediately cooperated by shipping the big man a [Siege Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Wurm) to go along with [Oathsworn Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oathsworn+Giant), [Skyknight Legionnaire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyknight+Legionnaire), and [Golgari Rot Farm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm). High quality cards quickly petered out, however, as Rich had to take a [Flight of Fancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flight+of+Fancy) in pack 4, [Spectral Searchlight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Searchlight) in pack 5, and [Elves of Deep Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elves+of+Deep+Shadow) in pack 6 for his playable cards, before he wheeled the Rot Farm tenth, shooting a pair of raised eyebrows at the table in the process.


At the end of pack 1, Hoaen had drafted exclusively green and blue cards along with some decent artifact mana fixing, but some thin packs in the middle left him with fewer quality *Ravnica* cards than he probably would have liked.


[Stratozeppelid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stratozeppelid) flashed Rich a wink as he opened his *Guildpact* booster, going immediately into his pile with only cursory glances at the [Pillory of the Sleepless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillory+of+the+Sleepless) and [Douse in Gloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Douse+in+Gloom) also present. [Izzet Chronarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Chronarch), [Dryad Sophisticate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Sophisticate), and [Orzhov Basilica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Basilica) surrounded [Burning-Tree Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Shaman) in pack 2, strongly suggesting that Gruul cards were available for the taking. That's exactly what Hoaen did for the rest of the pack, adding a [Stomping Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground), [Gruul Turf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Turf), [Gruul Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gruul+Signet), [Burning-Tree Bloodscale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Bloodscale), and [Ghor-Clan Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghor-Clan+Savage) to his growing pile, as well as a single [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) that came to him around pick 6.



![Assault Zeppelid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Assault+Zeppelid)

After two packs, Rich was now clearly entrenched in Simic/Gruul colors and would be looking to clean up blue-green cards in Dissension. That's exactly what happened, as it appeared the entire table was now more than willing to pass him what many feel are the strongest cards in *Dissension*. Hoaen didn't end up with any [Assault Zeppelid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Zeppelid)s, but he seemed to get almost every other solid playable in the guild including two very late [Coiling Oracle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coiling+Oracle)s, two even later [Vigean Hydropon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigean+Hydropon)s (or what Mike Turian calls the Wall of Hats), [Helium Squirter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Helium+Squirter), [Trygon Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trygon+Predator), [Silkwing Scout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silkwing+Scout), [Cytospawn Shambler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytospawn+Shambler), and the extremely effective [Plaxcaster Frogling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plaxcaster+Frogling).


When building his deck, Hoaen wasn't sure he liked it that much because it had very few spells (he added a [Leap of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leap+of+Flame) late in construction to give himself more tricks), but his mana was rock solid, and all of his creatures were solid or better. Having done quite a few drafts in this format myself, it wasn't the strongest blue-green-red deck I've seen, but it looked pretty good, and in Hoaen's hands would probably lead to a 2-1 record or better.
