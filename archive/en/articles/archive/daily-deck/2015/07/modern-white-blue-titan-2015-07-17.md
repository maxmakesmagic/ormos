Modern White-Blue Titan
=======================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 17, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Today's Daily Deck is a really cool take on white-blue. On the surface, this deck looks like a cross between White-Blue Control and Death and Taxes. There's some creature control with [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and great utility creatures such as [Wall of Omens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) and [Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp). But if you take a closer look, what this deck is actually trying to do is gain tons of value with [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan).



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sun+Titan)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)

Every non-land permanent in the deck does something cool when it enters or leaves the battlefield, so they're all desirable targets for [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)'s enters-the-battlefield and attack triggers. If you need to remove a creature, get a [Detention Sphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) or [Æther Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Spellbomb). If you want a land, grab a [Pilgrim's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim%27s+Eye). If you're low on life, return a [Lone Missionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lone+Missionary). Even some of the lands have utility. [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) and [Tectonic Edge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) can destroy your opponent's lands and then return to play to be used again.



One great thing about this deck is it just never runs out of [Sun Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)s. Not only are there four in the deck, but there are lots of ways to retrigger them. [Flickerwisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flickerwisp) can exile it and return it to the battlefield, and if things go wrong and your Titan dies, [Emeria, the Sky Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria%2C+the+Sky+Ruin) can bring it right back. It may seem like a long shot that [Emeria, the Sky Ruin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria%2C+the+Sky+Ruin) will trigger, but with four [Pilgrim's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pilgrim%27s+Eye)s and tons of control, games will go pretty long and you will hit your land drops. This deck brings a lot of inevitability.



Overall, White-Blue Titan has lots of interesting choices to make at all points of the game. Most of those choices revolve around which permanent to return to the battlefield with Sun Titan—but regardless, the choices will definitely test your skill. As someone who's seen the deck in action, I can tell you that the deck is tons of fun to play.
