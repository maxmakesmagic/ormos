Commanding Modern Horizons
==========================



 Posted in **Ways to Play**
 on June 13, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






It's fair to say that *Modern Horizons* is a *Magic* set like few others. Packed with both all-new cards and some of the most powerful options we've seen in years, *Modern Horizons* is poised to push the Modern format in exciting new directions.


But it isn't the only format that stands to benefit.


Packed with updated takes on classic effects and exciting fresh effects to play around with, *Modern Horizons* will make its mark on Commander as well. Here's how.

Awesome New Staples
===================


One of the most exciting features of *Modern Horizons* is how many cards for *multiple* Commander decks there are in the set. Even the basic lands!


* [![Snow-Covered Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Snow-Covered+Forest)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snow-Covered+Forest)

Snow-Covered basic lands aren't new, but being both full art and available in every pack means it's a great time to collect a pile of them for that snowy Commander deck you'll make someday. Even if you're not looking to go all-in on a snow theme, snow basics work well with almost any card that cares about you having snow mana or permanents, like [Arcum's Astrolabe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcum%27s+Astrolabe) and the aforementioned [Dead of Winter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dead+of+Winter).

Need tools like removal or [tutors](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/play-design/m-files-hour-devastation-part-1-2017-07-14#tutor)? Perfect New Tools (for Your Favorite Decks)
===========================================


A cursory look over the complete *Modern Horizons* set reveals more than splashy cards and powerful options for decks. There's plenty for the very specific ways to play some of the decks you enjoy best.


* [![Archmage's Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Archmage%27s+Charm)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage%27s+Charm)
* [![Mirrodin Besieged](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mirrodin+Besieged)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrodin+Besieged)
* [![Graveshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Graveshifter)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graveshifter)
* [![Sling-Gang Lieutenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sling-Gang+Lieutenant)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sling-Gang+Lieutenant)
* [![Goblin Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Engineer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Engineer)
* [![Deep Forest Hermit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deep+Forest+Hermit)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Forest+Hermit)
* [![Hall of Heliod's Generosity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hall+of+Heliod%27s+Generosity)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hall+of+Heliod%27s+Generosity)

I have a confession: I absolutely love my [Kefnet the Mindful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kefnet+the+Mindful) deck. I typically enjoy aggressive decks, and my commanders of choice often reflect that. But every now and then sitting down with a classic mono-blue control deck, kitted out with card drawing, artifact-based battlefield destructions, and literal [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) is just what I needed. [Archmage's Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archmage%27s+Charm) hits the sweet spot of being an answer ([Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)!), more cards, or even a sneaky way to steal something with low converted mana cost ([Sol Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring) and friends).


[Mirrodin Besieged](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrodin+Besieged) is another powerful blue card, but it doesn't go anywhere near Kefnet. If you play [Jhoira, Weatherlight Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhoira%2C+Weatherlight+Captain) or [Saheeli, the Gifted](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saheeli%2C+the+Gifted), then you know what a deck packed full of artifacts can do. Playing [Mirrodin Besieged](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirrodin+Besieged) with a graveyard full of them will be lights out for one opponent, but the constructive mode of making Servos can cascade with [Goblin Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Engineer) (another new card!) or [Daretti, Scrap Savant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daretti%2C+Scrap+Savant).


[Graveshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graveshifter) is [Gravedigger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger), but for any tribal deck running black. Have a life gain and Cleric deck with [Ayli, Eternal Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ayli%2C+Eternal+Pilgrim)? Need another Zombie to get back Zombies with [Varina, Lich Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varina%2C+Lich+Queen)? Want a Vampire to get back a Vampire and make a Vampire all at the same time with [Edgar Markov](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edgar+Markov)? [Graveshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Graveshifter) is an amazing tool as a changeling.


[Sling-Gang Lieutenant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sling-Gang+Lieutenant) is perfect for a [Wort, Boggart Auntie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+Boggart+Auntie) deck. It's a Goblin that gets back a Goblin, which you can get back with Wort! [Deep Forest Hermit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Forest+Hermit) is essential tech to anyone who loves playing around with Squirrels. And [Hall of Heliod's Generosity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hall+of+Heliod%27s+Generosity) is for enchantment decks (hello [Estrid, the Masked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Estrid%2C+the+Masked) and [Kestia, the Cultivator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kestia%2C+the+Cultivator)!) what [Academy Ruins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Ruins) already is for artifact decks.


Shiny New Legends (to Be Your Next Commander)
=============================================


Anyone playing Commander will spend a lot of time sifting through cards and finding the right mix of spells and lands, but at the end of the day, it's still all about the commanders. *Modern Horizons* is loaded with them.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ayula%2C+Queen+Among+Bears)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ayula%2C+Queen+Among+Bears)
If you ever wanted [Grizzly Bears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzly+Bears) to be a shining example of an awesome card in Commander, [Ayula, Queen Among Bears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ayula%2C+Queen+Among+Bears) is exactly what you need.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/98dVqC1U9T8>


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis)
[Hogaak, Arisen Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis) cannot only be cast again from the command zone, but also your graveyard. This means after the first time you play it, you can let it go to the graveyard instead and avoid paying the commander tax every time you replay it. Just don't forget you need piles of creatures in play and maybe a sprinkling of cards to exile from the graveyard to make it work.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Kess%2C+Dissident+Mage)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kess%2C+Dissident+Mage)
[Kess, Dissident Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kess%2C+Dissident+Mage) isn't a new-to-Commander card, but as a powerful Wizard that can get back spells for you, Kess is a wonderful way to lead a powerful deck. While the Command Zone's breakdown is from when Kess first appeared, the strategies to use her remain timeless.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/dER3XEMDt58>


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Morophon%2C+the+Boundless)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morophon%2C+the+Boundless)
Don't have a way to lead your favorite creature type in a Commander deck? You do now, thanks to [Morophon, the Boundless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morophon%2C+the+Boundless). Pumping your favorite creature type is fine, but it's the "make your favorite type of creature easier to cast" ability that's the real gem. Given we have [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest) entering Modern and the new [Deep Forest Hermit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Forest+Hermit) to play with, [Jason Alt's five-color Squirrel tribal deck around Morophon](https://www.coolstuffinc.com/a/jasonalt-05302019-morophon-lord-of-squirrels) seems a good place to start.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pashalik+Mons)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pashalik+Mons)
[Pashalik Mons](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pashalik+Mons) is the kind of commander that might be best served sitting "in the 99" cards of your deck. Pashalik turns every Goblin into either another two Goblins, a ping of damage, or even both. Adding him to the likes of [Krenko, Mob Boss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krenko%2C+Mob+Boss) is a surefire way to dish out damage even if your Goblin horde is annihilated. A good Goblin Commander deck primer is a great way to get started making your green army come to life.


<https://www.youtube.com/embed/gbsPnbwZYQg>


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sisay%2C+Weatherlight+Captain)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sisay%2C+Weatherlight+Captain)
Another commander that commands using all five colors, [Sisay, Weatherlight Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sisay%2C+Weatherlight+Captain) is your go-to way to put all your favorite legendary permanents in the same place. Getting her power high enough to pull those permanents you want is much easier with cards like [Champion's Helm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion%27s+Helm), [Hero's Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Blade), or, my favorite, [Blackblade Reforged](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blackblade+Reforged).


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=The+First+Sliver)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+First+Sliver)
[The First Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+First+Sliver) is hardly the first Sliver we've seen, but it's certainly among the most powerful ever printed. When it *and every Sliver to cast afterward* nets you more Slivers, it isn't hard to get out of hand. Waiting another turn cycle to attack invites an opening for opponents to undo all your hard work cascading, so be sure to include Slivers like [Blur Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blur+Sliver), [Heart Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+Sliver), [Firewake Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firewake+Sliver), and the brand-new [Cloudshredder Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudshredder+Sliver) to take the initiative right after.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Urza%2C+Lord+High+Artificer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%2C+Lord+High+Artificer)
[Urza, Lord High Artificer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%2C+Lord+High+Artificer) is a card asking to be broken. It didn't take long for some experts to step up to the plate showing us how it's done.

Yawgmoth is one of *Magic*'s oldest villains, and now we get to see why in [Yawgmoth, Thran Physician](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yawgmoth%2C+Thran+Physician). There are so many ways to use all of Yawgmoth's abilities, it makes sense that [Eric Levine went all-in on a primer for using The Ineffable in Commander](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/just-thran-this-out-there-yawgmoth-thran-physician-in-commander/).
