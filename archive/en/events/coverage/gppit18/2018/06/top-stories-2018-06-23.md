### Black-Red Midrange with Corey Burkhart


If you're going to join the [Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler) army, you can still make it better. 25th-ranked Corey Burkhart knew exactly what to do.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gppit18/gppit18-burkhart.jpg)


"I played red-black at the Pro Tour and went 4-6." he said. "I found I didn't like the cheap cardsâ€”[Bomat Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier), [Soul-Scar Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul-Scar+Mage) and [Heart of Kiran](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heart+of+Kiran). By now, people have figured out the answers to red decks and the format's adapted but not enough. People are still playing 1 toughness creatures in decks."


"In testing I saw [Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler) still kills enough things and buys you enough time, so rather than play it in an aggro shell I put it in midrange where it answers threats. As long as players are beating down with 1 toughness creatures, Chainwhirler is going to be good."


"It's a good card, with a target on its back, but players aren't overcompensating enough yet." Burkhart noted. "I feel like you shouldn't be playing things like [Champion of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+Wits)â€”except maybe [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) since it's so powerful for green decks. Everyone's ready for red so I don't want to be top decking [Soul-Scar Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul-Scar+Mage) on turn six; I want [Angrath, the Flame-Chained](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angrath%2C+the+Flame-Chained) or [Glorybringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer) or [Chandra, Torch of Defiance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%2C+Torch+of+Defiance) or [Vraska's Contempt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vraska%27s+Contempt). I want to void my deck of low mana, high variance cards."


"I'm comfortable playing those grindy games against blue decks. With enough [Doomfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomfall)s and [Vraska's Contempt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vraska%27s+Contempt)s you're not afraid of their mythic haymakers." he said. "I'm going to make you play [Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk) when you don't want to: I'm going to tax their answers and be the last one with a threat on the battlefield."

"The only 1 toughness creatures in the deck are [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves)," Wu said. "If you have a first turn Elf you're going to be able to deploy your three-drop before their Chainwhirler hits. When we were testing for the Pro Tour we assumed it was going to be a bad matchup. It *is* bad if they have [Soul-Scar Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul-Scar+Mage) and Chainwhirler and can just nerf a bunch of your guysâ€”but if they don't have that combo we found we were winning a lot of games."


"Outside of Llanowar Elf, [Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler) doesn't do anything to our deck. "We have a bunch of 3/4 creaturessâ€”[Thrashing Brontodon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrashing+Brontodon), [Greenbelt Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenbelt+Rampager)â€”which are a nice combo with Heartn of Kiran: You play the Rampager, then play the Heart, then play Rampager again, crew the Heart with the return trigger on the stack, Rampager goes back to your hand, attack with the Heart, then finally play the Rampager again and it sticks, all by your third turn."


"[Greenbelt Rampager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greenbelt+Rampager) smooths things out. ### White-Blue God-Pharaoh's Gift with Corey Baumeister


Some decks claim a good matchup again the Standard frontrunner red decks. When an elite Standard player like Corey Baumeister claims a deck is "supposed" to it, ears perk up.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gppit18/gppit18-baumeister.jpg)


"Leading up to Pittsburgh, I played Zac Elsik's [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) list he made Top 8 with at the SCG Invitational." he said. "When I played it online I played against five red decks in a rowâ€”the deck it's supposed to beat. After testing some control matchups with [my brother] Brad Nelson, it felt fine against everything but Blue-Black Midrange. I felt confidentâ€”and Brad found some tech to put in too."


What makes Baumeister's deck so good against red decks? "[God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=God-Pharaoh%27s+Gift) is the only deck that goes so far over the top red's removal that it doesn't affect it." Baumeister explained. "You still have to deal with [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade), but now you've got [Settle the Wreckage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Settle+the+Wreckage) and [Sunscourge Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunscourge+Champion) to dance around them with. On a turn they're holding up [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) you force them to use it before you play your Gift. [The Scarab God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Scarab+God) is a little tough since [Glint-Sleeve Siphoner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint-Sleeve+Siphoner) on turn 2 is rough. Blue-black has card draw and counterspellsâ€”every one of their cards does something against you."


Of course, even in the face of a tough matchup Baumeister seemed undaunted for one good reason: "It's a freaking boatload of fun to play."
