Fabrizio's Five
===============




Five. Five Grand Prix titles. Fabrizio Anteri turned his tenth Grand Prix Top 8 into his fifth win and took his place among the elite of the game, both as Platinum pro and as one of the most succesful Grand Prix players of all time.

TOP 8 COVERAGE



Kai Budde, Yuuya Watanabe, Shuhei Nakamura, Fabrizio Anteri … What's this? This is the list of players with the most Grand Prix wins in their *Magic* career. To be fair, Fabrizio Anteri was now tied for fourth place with Luis Scott-Vargas, Olivier Ruel, and Raphaël Lévy. But the fact that most of the aforementioned are members of the Hall of Fame only shows what a monumental achievement Anteri's fifth victory—his third this season!—really was.


But let's start in the beginning. In fact, this weekend marked the first time *Shadows over Innistrad* was played at the Grand Prix level. 2,084 players gathered in Barcelona for the occasion, especially legions of pro players on their way to the Pro Tour in Madrid next week. 633 returned for the draft rounds on Day 2, eight entered the playoffs.


And Anteri was by no means the most accomplished among those eight. But while players like (6) Reid Duke and Pro Tour champion Shaun McLaren lost their quarterfinals, Anteri pressed on. He himself dispatched Hall of Famer William Jensen in the semifinals and eventually won the finals against Mehdi Saadi with the combination of Rush of Adrenaline and Uncaged Fury.


Congratulations to Fabrizio Anteri, newly-minted Platinum player, new leader in the race for the Grand Prix Master slot at the World Championship, and champion of Grand Prix Barcelona 2016!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



Welcome back to Barcelona, where on this sunny morning 633 players have returned to the shadows, the *Shadows over Innistrad*.


Today's order of business was Booster Draft. A draft would be followed by three rounds, another draft, another three rounds, then cut to Top 8 and playoffs. With Pro Tour *Shadows over Innistrad* less than a week and but a few hundred miles away, the pros were out in full force this weekend. Just within the top two draft pods at the beginning of the day one could find multiple Hall of Famers, Platinum and Gold players, and three of the current Top 25. Most notably William Jensen, Marcio Carvalho, and Brian Braun-Duin had ended the first day on 9-0.


Stay tuned throughout the day for live updates, draft coverage, decklists, and more, for all the action and all the news straight from the battlefield of Grand Prix Barcelona 2016!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



What had started with 2084 players was reduced to a mere 636. After nine grueling rounds of *Shadows over Innistrad* Sealed Deck, Day 1 of Grand Prix Barcelona 2016 was at an end.


In another regard, however, this triple-GP weekend was only the beginning. Here in Spain as well as in Beijing and Albuquerque, people had their first chance to play the new set at the Grand Prix level. Especially pro players made good use of the opportunity to prepare for next week's Pro Tour in Madrid. And its proximity made Barcelona the prime destination for them. 18 of the Top 25 ranked had come here, as well as a host of Hall of Famers, pros of various levels, and Europe's finest.


At the end of the day, seven remained undefeated with perfect records of 9-0: Platinum pros Marcio Carvalho and William Jensen, GP champions Damien Bouillot and Brian Braun-Duin, as well as Geoffrey Martinelle, Ferran Vila, and Francisco Rubio.


For another impression of the amazing line-up of famous names in this tournament one only needed to look at places 9 through 16 though. In what would become the second Draft Pod tomorrow sat Platinum pro Steve Rubin, Hall of Famer Raphaël Lévy, (7) Alexander Hayne, Gold-level pro Pierre Dagen, (10) Fabrizio Anteri, and (20) Brad Nelson.


Join us tomorrow and follow along as we follow some of the game's best and brightest on their forays into the world of *Shadows over Innistrad* Booster Draft!
