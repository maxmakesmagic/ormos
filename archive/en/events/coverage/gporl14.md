TOP 8 COVERAGE
--------------


At the start of this globe-spanning *Khans of Tarkir* Limited doubleheader, the Grand Prix trial winning decklists from Shanghai showed a marked lean towards the Mardu clan. While plenty of players found success with adding green to their black and white spells, the Abzan were still playing second fiddle to the Raid-loving, face-smashing Mardu. 


Fast forward to the end of the weekend, and a Top 8 here in Orlando that was dominated by Eugene Hwang – and the Mardu. Harry Corvese, in his fourth GP Top 8, fell to the Hwang horde in the quarterfinals. Brazil's Artur Villela was next in the firing line. Why wasn't his 9/10 Tuskguard Captain good enough? Because Hwang used Jeering Instigator to turn it right around and aim it at Villela's face. Ouch indeed. That set up a final against...well, that's the next part of our story.


Both Frank Lepore and Melissa DeTora are well-known figures in the *Magic* community. The affable Lepore is community manager at tcgplayer.com, while DeTora has long been a welcome part of the Pro family. Apart, they've carved themselves a reputation. As a couple, together they were almost unstoppable this weekend. Both at 8-1 overnight. Both at 10-2 at the end of the first draft. Both on table one down the stretch. Win. Win. Win. Win. Suddenly it was round 15, and an incredible moment of Significant Other-ness, as they took an Intentional Draw to secure their seats at the final draft of the weekend.


Lepore fell at the first hurdle, but DeTora monstered her way to the final, with multiple Snowhorn Riders, and Tusked Colossodon. Once there, she forced a game three, and briefly looked as though she would add yet another first to her career, as the first woman to win a Grand Prix. Briefly? Briefly, because Eugene Hwang had allied himself with the Mardu, and when it comes to the beatdown, they, and he, just weren't going to be beaten.

DAY 2 COVERAGE
--------------


The Top 8 is set here at Grand Prix Orlando, and one more draft separates someone from a *Khans of Tarkir* Limited title. There are some great stories waiting to be told amongst the eight. Harry Corvese is in his fourth GP Top 8. The same is true for Pierre-Christophe Mondon, looking for a second title following victory at Oklahoma City last year. Sol Malka is another Grand Prix champion. He won Grand Prix Tampa. In 2002. Ian Farnung travelled all the way to Sao Paulo to claim his first GP Top 8 earlier this year, matching the achievement of Eugene Hwang, who made his GP Top 8 debut in Vancouver. Then there's Artur Villela of Brazil, last seen in Top 8 action in the final of GP Rio de Janeiro last year.


But with respect, there is a storyline this weekend that rises above mere *Magic* achievement. Although we all want victory for ourselves, being able to root for our nearest and dearest friends is pretty special. Now imagine that you plan with your Significant Other to attend the GP. Obviously you both intend to win. Now imagine that you get to 13-1 – that's BOTH of you – and in the final round you declare an Intentional Draw that sees you both into the Top 8 as the only players to reach 40 points on the weekend.


Step forward, Melissa DeTora and Frank Lepore. Many of you will know Frank as the Community Manager for TCGPlayer.com, while Melissa has both Grand Prix and Pro Tour Top 8s to her name. Now, with only three rounds to go, they're only a couple of wins away from history – or possibly a very stressful drive home....


Elsewhere, the second day showcased how very, very different Draft and Sealed Deck can be. There were eight undefeated players at the end of day one – only two of them managed positive records on day two, and only the 9-0 Ian Farnung managed to parlay that stellar Sealed into a seat in the Top 8. Even Tom 'The Boss' Ross fell short, his day one draw ultimately costing him his chance to draft one last time. 


So, the stage is set for the fairytale ending – and for six others to spoil the party and claim the title for themselves.

DAY 1 COVERAGE
--------------


It’s All Land in Orlando – with nine rounds of Sealed Deck action over for the day, there’s no doubt what really matters, and it’s the part of the battlefield that most people just don’t have the bandwidth to pay attention to – the manabase. Coming into the weekend, we knew mana was going to be important. After all, how were you going to successfully pilot your three color Clan deck if you couldn’t reliably get hold of all three colors of mana? In the event, completing the trifecta was barely breaking sweat. Then you decided how far your mana could take you beyond the normally-exacting requirements of three colors on turn three. Four on turn four, five on turn five, some of the pools today were capable of extraordinary manarial (not a word) gymnastics.


Five players will return tomorrow with unblemished records: Jason Southworth, Ian Farnung, Daniel Reyes, Dominic Ceresani, and Gabriel Rogasner played approximately all of the colors all of the time, and dominated the day. Right behind them, and also undefeated (but with a draw to spoil the symmetry), are Kit Chinlund, Hugo Terra, and the star of recent StarCityGames Invitationals, Tom ‘The Boss’ Ross. 


Many of the biggest names in the game are already in Honolulu, preparing themselves for the challenge of the Pro Tour next weekend. Some were here, though, and Ari Lax, Melissa DeTora, and Hall of Famer Ben Stark are all poised well within striking distance at 8-1 overnight. Also en route for the Pro Tour were an assortment of European Pros, with Matej Zatlkaj, Stanislav Cifka, and Frank Karsten all coming back tomorrow at 7-2.


But, as always, the nine rounds of Sealed have done their work. From a starting field of 2277 – a number that only two or three years ago would have been cause for sharp intakes of breath, but now seems, incredibly, almost normal – only 233 will return to tackle the twists and turns of the new Draft format. Join Rich Hagon and Adam Styborski for all the action, as the quest for the Grand Prix Orlando champion continues!
