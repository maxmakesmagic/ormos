Recent Additions to Legacy
==========================


Now that the Legacy Grand Prix concluded, we have a good idea of how recent releases have impacted an eternal format such as Legacy. Yesterday in the [Day 1 Highlights](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpshi18/day-1-highlights-2018-11-30), we spoke about how [Arcane Artisan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Artisan) shook up Sneak and Show considerably.


Due to the high power level of Legacy, cards need to be stellar in their role or be extremely powerful to break into the format.What other new cards did we see added to Legacy in the last six months?


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Arena+Rector)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena+Rector)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Archon+of+Valor%27s+Reach)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archon+of+Valor%27s+Reach)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brightling)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brightling)
Battlebond, released in June, added several key cards to the metagame other than [Arcane Artisan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Artisan). Nic Fit, a deck built entirely around [Arena Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena+Rector), was seen at Shizuoka. [Arena Rector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arena+Rector) allowed several overcosted planeswalkers such as [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated), [Kaya, Ghost Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaya%2C+Ghost+Assassin), and [Ugin, the Spirit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ugin%2C+the+Spirit+Dragon) to find a home. From the sideboard, Nic Fit also plays a singleton Archon of Valor's Reach to lock certain decks out wholly.


[Brightling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brightling) is also worth a mention because it saw light play in both Death & Taxes this weekend and even showed up in a couple of White-Blue Control lists. Three mana is a lot to pay, but Death & Taxes players have [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial), while White-Blue Miracles players have plenty of lands and simply want an unkillable win condition which can also race well.


* [![Goblin Trashmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Trashmaster)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trashmaster)
* [![Remorseful Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Remorseful+Cleric)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remorseful+Cleric)
* [![Runic Armasaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Runic+Armasaur)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runic+Armasaur)
* [![Sai, Master Thopterist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sai%2C+Master+Thopterist)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sai%2C+Master+Thopterist)

Then, in July, Core Set 2019 hit the shelves, adding a few new toys!


[Goblin Trashmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trashmaster) is a critical addition and has made its way to almost all Goblin decks. Not only could you cheat it onto the battlefield with [Goblin Lackey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey), but you can also search for it with [Goblin Matron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Matron). Against artifact-based decks, [Goblin Trashmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Trashmaster) will singlehanded shut them down.


[Remorseful Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remorseful+Cleric) is a massive addition to Death & Taxes, giving the white weenie player a main deck graveyard hate option which also soars through the skies. It can be searched out with [Recruiter of the Guard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Recruiter+of+the+Guard) and can surprise unwitting opponents by swooping onto the battlefield at the crucial juncture via [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial).


It's also interesting that we saw some [Runic Armasaur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runic+Armasaur)s around the hall, seeing how it's one of the best cards against Lands! Crack a fetch land? Draw a card. [Maze of Ith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maze+of+Ith) your attacker. Draw a card. Are you using [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rishadan+Port) to tap down my land? Ok, I'll draw a card! Did you just [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) me? I'll draw a card for that too!


Last but not least, we spotted someone playing Auriok Salvager Combo featuring a pair of Sai Master Thopterist! Don't you love the diversity of Legacy?


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Assassin%27s+Trophy)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassin%27s+Trophy)
Then, in October, Guilds of Ravnica contributed the biggest card of the year, Assassin's Trophy! If you thought it was only good in Standard and Modern, think again! Its sheer versatility allowed it to be included in a wide range of Legacy decks, such as Jund, Sultai, Nic Fit, Elves, Loam, and any deck which can support the mana cost. Killing [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) never felt so smooth!


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Legion+Warboss)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Warboss)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Goblin+Cratermaker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Cratermaker)
[Legion Warboss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Legion+Warboss) also showed up in huge numbers, with many players running them alongside [Goblin Rabblemaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Rabblemaster), providing the deck with positive redundancy. [Experimental Frenzy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Experimental+Frenzy) also showed up in some sideboards, as a tool to combat attrition wars. That's a lot of new cards in one Legacy deck.


[Goblin Cratermaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Cratermaker) was an excellent addition for a bunch of red decks, most notably in Goblin decks, Red Prison decks, and even Painter Servant decks. Perhaps you haven't noticed, but it can kill [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn), as well as [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull), [Thought-Knot Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought-Knot+Seer), Umezawa's Jitte, while still having the ability to zap a small creature such as [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets).


What this also meant is that Dragon Stompy is no more (because the Dragons have given way to a bunch of Goblins) and the deck is now known as Red Prison. Five-time Grand Prix Top 8 competitor Shota Takao made Day 2 with this list below, and his list is an excellent example of how the deck evolved!

6 (16) Years In The Making
==========================


When Pro Tour Hall of Famer Kenji Tsumura locked up his spot in the Top 8, the first thing which left his lips were the words, “six years!”. It’s been a long time, he murmured, since he made the Top 8 at a Premier Event.


However, that was not all. Tsumura's old friend and teammate, Yuuta Hirosawa, also made the Top 8 with him, and Hirosawa’s previous (and only) Grand Prix Top 8 was 16 years ago, at Grand Prix Sapporo 2002!  
  

Before this weekend, both players have never played each other in a sanctioned match, until Round 13 today. They ended up running out of time to force a draw, but both players made the Top 8 cut regardless. If they had both won their quarterfinals, they would have been paired against each other in the semifinals. However, both seasoned veterans fell in the quarterfinals!


Both Tsumura and Hirosawa have been friends for over a decade, having first participated in Pro Tour San Diego together. It was a Two-Headed Giant Pro Tour, and that collaboration eventually flourished into a lifelong friendship. Since then, they’ve always teamed with each other, and playtested with each other. Now, they’ll finally be back to attend the next Pro Tour together just like the good old days.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpshi18/gpShizuoka2018_L_Day2_TsumuraHirosawa.jpg)


For additional reading, check out [this piece](https://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpkyo16/longtime-friends-2016-09-11) about this pair of Lifelong Friends and be inspired by this tale of a lifetime.


Teruya’s Third Trophy
=====================


1429 players took part in this weekend’s Legacy Grand Prix here in Shizuoka. After eighteen rounds, it was Teruya Kakumae who triumphed above all.


This marked his third Grand Prix title in his fourth trip to the Top 8 playoffs. His first title at Grand Prix Kobe 2014 utilized Modern Constructed, while his second title at Grand Prix Auckland 2015 featured Limited. His third Grand Prix Top 8 in Grand Prix Kuala Lumpur 2016 was a Standard event. Now, like a true master, he had taken down a Legacy Constructed tournament, and let’s not forget that he’s also a Pro Tour Team Series Champion!


What’s a genuinely all-rounded player Kakumae was, to be adept is any format he touches!


Piloting Eldrazi Stompy, he had to defeat Akihiko Shiota in the quarterfinals, and then Takahiro Ishikawa in the semifinals, followed by Hiroyuki Kaga in the finals. After stomping through the competition, all he had left to do was lift his trophy and smile for the camera once more.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpshi18/gpShizuoka2018_L_TrophyShot2.jpg)


Congratulations to Teruya Kakumae, for winning Grand Prix Shizuoka (Legacy) 2018!
