Wrecking With Rakdos (Part 3 of 3) - Rakdos Control!
====================================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on September 11, 2006 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











Welcome back to Building on a Budget! You voted for Hellbent, and in my last column, you got [Hellbent Aggro](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/wrecking-rakdos-part-2-3-2006-08-28). In this week's column, I'm going to take Hellbent in the other direction, and build towards Rakdos Hellbent Control.


Before I go on, I'd like to address a couple of topics that were raised in the forums of my previous article. Don't worry – we'll get to the deck soon enough! (See, I don't get a card preview for *Time Spiral*, but I want to build some sort of suspense in my column.) 


*Questions:* Why aren't you playing more Hellbent cards in your deck? How can you call it a Hellbent deck if the only Hellbent cards you're playing are [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) and [Gobhobbler Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gobhobbler+Rats)? 


*Answer*: As I stated in my last column, many of the Hellbent cards are woefully underpowered, even when Hellbent is enabled (see [Slaughterhouse Bouncer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughterhouse+Bouncer)). If there were more Hellbent cards that were worth playing, by George, I would play them! [Remember rule #3](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/deckbuilding-101-five-tips-better-deckbuilding-2006-06-05): 



> 
> “Focus your goals, but don't be a slave to your theme.”
> 
> 
> 


Building a Hellbent deck isn't about throwing in as many cards that say Hellbent as possible, and then going to town. Everyone has different goals as a deck builder – but everyone should be trying to build decks that are cohesive. Why play cards that will make your deck more likely to lose? 


As far as calling the deck a Hellbent deck with only two Hellbent cards ([Gobhobbler Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gobhobbler+Rats) and [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet)), this is perfectly fine. The entire focus of the deck is to enable Hellbent on these cards, from playing removal spells that double as permanents (to get them out of my hand) to playing creatures which enable discarding of cards ([Rakdos Augermage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Augermage) and [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage)). The point of the deck is to viably win with only what's on the board – a.k.a. Hellbent.


There's a long precedent in **Magic** for naming/making decks around a central theme, even if that theme is only a couple of cards. For instance, [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) decks are built around [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), but that's only a single card! U/G Madness decks regularly run only three madness cards ([Circular Logic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circular+Logic), [Basking Rootwalla](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basking+Rootwalla), and [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm)), but are built around that theme ([Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) and [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba) to enable madness, [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis), [Wonder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wonder), and [Roar of the Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar+of+the+Wurm) to take advantage of discard effects). I'm very comfortable calling the last deck Hellbent Aggro, because it is 100% built to be both aggressive, and to work around the Hellbent mechanic.

As you'll notice, there ended up being only twelve total creatures in the deck – four copies of [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon), [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), and [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet). I ended up with eight acceleration cards (four [Coldsteel Heart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coldsteel+Heart) and four [Rakdos Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Signet)) and twenty-two lands. Normally this would be a low count for a control deck, but most of the cards in this deck have a low mana cost. 


![Rakdos_Pit_Dragon](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/DIS/Rakdos_Pit_Dragon.jpg)Any good control deck worth its salt runs control elements, and this one is no exception. In addition to [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), I ended with three each of [Seal of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire) and [Seal of Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Doom), and three copies of [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run). [Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottled+Cloister) allows me to enable Hellbent on my opponent's turn (for [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon)), and allows me to block safely with [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) without worrying about losing cards out of my hand (if there are any left). In addition, [Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottled+Cloister) allows me to draw multiple cards a turn, and unless I draw two lands at once, I can go right back to enabling Hellbent with a spell/creature plus a land.


The last two card choices are [Delirium Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins) and [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer). The point of Rakdos Control is to get as many permanents to the board as possible ([Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) is the only non-permanent spell in the deck), enable Hellbent, and either strip my opponent's hand with [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet)/[Delirium Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins), or kill them in one/two swings with [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon). The problem I found with this strategy is that both of these creatures could be chump blocked, allowing my opponent time to get back into the game/draw more threats. [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) gives these creatures trample (and a healthy power boost!), allowing the Poppet to force a discard even when blocked, and allowing [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon) to double strike/trample over for a ton of damage. 


**Game 16: LMBYAG (U/R Control)**


I play first, and get down [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), and he does not have any removal spells in hand. I begin hitting him for four a turn with the make-a-goblin ability of the Guildmage, while he begins [Annex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annex)ing and [Stone Rain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone+Rain)ing my lands. Eventually, I get a [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) on the board, and he [Confiscate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Confiscate)s it. I play a second Poppet, and use my Guildmage to make his attacking/blocking situation impossible. My Poppet strips his hand, and I ride my two creatures to victory.


*Record: 10-6*


**Game 15: etai15 (B/W Flyers)**


![Delirium Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Delirium+Skeins)On the play, I drop a turn 2 [Coldsteel Heart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coldsteel+Heart) (naming red – I always want more mana for [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon), unless I'm short on black mana entirely), and follow it with a turn 3 [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon). Etai15 drops a couple of flyers, but I strip out three of his five cards, and all of my remaining cards with a fourth turn [Delirium Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins). [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon) instantly becomes a lethal threat (I can pump him to fourteen points of double strike damage a turn), and etai15 is forced into chump-blocking mode on turn 5. After a couple of turns of this, I draw [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) off the top of my deck, clear his only blocker, and come in for lethal damage.
 *Record: 9-6*


**Game 14: r0369 (Ghost Dad)**


R0369 gets two quick [Tallowisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tallowisp)s, and I do not have [Seal of Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Doom) to kill either one. This allows him to get multiple [Pillory of the Sleepless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillory+of+the+Sleepless), and I draw only [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet)s and [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage)s. I don't get a [Seal of Fire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire) to combine with the Guildmage to kill his [Tallowisp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tallowisp)s, nor do I get [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) – so basically I'm forced to make 2/1 haste goblins to stay alive while eating 3+ damage a turn, one at a time. I don't draw solutions, and I lose.


*Record: 8-6*


**Game 13: Chilleo (Haakon Knights)**


I get [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) on turn 2, and he answers with Haakon. Fortunately, I have multiple [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) in hand, and he ends up taking seven damage twice (three from [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run), two from Haakon dying, and two from my Guildmage attacking). Even with this quick reduction in life total, Chilleo stabilizes with Haakon, [Garza's Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garza%27s+Assassin), and multiple recover spells – including [Grim Harvest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Harvest) and [Krovikan Rot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krovikan+Rot). The Rot kills my Guildmage, but I play a second one, and use it to immediately kill his Assassin, removing both of his recover spells from the game (he has only two mana open). I then draw [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer), and use it on the Guildmage to trample over for enough damage to win.


*Record: 8-5*


**Game 12: Reylance ([Warp World](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World))**


I get a third-turn [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon) thanks to a Signet, throw down one of each Seal on turn 4, put [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) on my Dragon turn 5, and enable Hellbent on turn 6, killing Reylance on a single swing. Reylance lets me know he's playing a [Warp World](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) deck, because I killed him too quickly for him to get set up.


*Record: 7-5*


**Game 11: J Player (U/W Precon)**


I run him over with [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) and [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage). He can't block because the Guildmage kills all of his early creatures, and [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) strips all of the higher-cost cards out of his hand.


*Record: 6-5*


**Game 10: Stipp75 (Elves)**


He gets two [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), two [Dryad Sophisticate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dryad+Sophisticate), and a [Silhana Ledgewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silhana+Ledgewalker). I drop a [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage) and a [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet), and begin to stabilize the board – my Guildmage is able to take out an elf a turn, the Poppet can block any of his smaller creatures, and I have [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) in hand to kill the Ledgewalker. Unfortunately, Stripp75 has [Giant Growth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) and [Might of Oaks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Oaks), and slaps me around for eleven damage in a single turn, killing me. It didn't help that I drew five of my eight mana artifacts over the course of the game. Wherefore art thou, Seals of Fire and Doom?


*Record: 5-5*


Games 10-16 were played with the final version of my Rakdos Control deck. As you'll notice, three of the seven games were decided by [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer). Unlike creature enchantments, equipment allows you to enhance your creatures without running the risk of losing two-for-one card disadvantage. My choices for this slot were either [O-Naginata](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=O-Naginata) or [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) – both give the creatures I care about trample ([Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) and [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon)). In the end, I decided that the swing in life totals with the Warhammer was more important than dropping a first-turn piece of equipment, thus helping the Hellbent cause out.

Three Warhammers is the right number, because I'm already running a low creature count, and the last thing I want is to draw multiple Warhammers in a game, and not have a creature to equip!

**Game 9: BringerofWins (Rakdos Mirror)**


I get an insane draw – turn 2 [Coldsteel Heart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coldsteel+Heart), turn 3 [Rakdos Signet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Signet) and [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet), followed by turn 4 [Delirium Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins). This enabled Hellbent on my Poppet, allowing me to strip BringerofWins' entire hand of six cards on turn 4. I draw [Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottled+Cloister) off of the top of my deck, and the card advantage I generated was insurmountable. 


*Record: 5-4*


In this game, I encountered a bug in ***Magic** Online*. It's pretty simple – if [Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottled+Cloister) removes a split card from your hand, that split card stays removed from the game permanently. In my game against BringerofWins, I drew [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) off of the Cloister, allowed it to be removed from the game at the end of my turn, and the [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) was gone for good. This is why I considered removing all copies of [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) from my deck at my upgrade to Rakdos Control version 4.0.


**Game 8: MrJet (W/R *Legends*)**


I kill his guys with multiple Seals of Doom and Fire, drop double [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) and [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon), enable Hellbent, and steamroll him. 


*Record: 4-4*


It was about this game that I realized that regardless of whether you build Hellbent to be Aggro or Control, your deck is going to be fully aggressive once Hellbent is enabled. [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon), when Hellbent, can kill on a single swing. [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) wants to take out as many cards from your opponent's hand as possible. The difference between the two strategies is how you go about obtaining Hellbent, and which creatures you want to run. In aggressive Hellbent, your focus is around smaller creatures, and killing as quickly as possible. In control Hellbent, your focus is around larger creatures, and setting up the board so that your guys can swing through unmolested, ending the game in just a couple of swings.


**Game 7: ArdyJay (U/R Izzet)**

I drop [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon) down, but he has a couple of chump blockers, which allow him the time to cast [Tidespout Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidespout+Tyrant). I don't draw a [Seal of Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Doom) or a [Hit & Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hit+%2F%2F+Run) to kill the Tyrant/force a block, and he grabs control of the game.


*Record: 3-4*


I had multiple games where my [Rakdos Pit Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Pit+Dragon) and/or [Jagged Poppet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jagged+Poppet) were chump blocked, causing me to lose. ![Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bottled+Cloister)I wanted to bring myself up to a full complement of Signets, and I wanted to try out [Bottled Cloister](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bottled+Cloister) – several forum members reported great success with Cloisters in their Hellbent decks, and I'm never one to shy away from card advantage. After all, if you're playing a deck where you want to unload your entire hand, chances are the Cloister is the last card you're going to play to the board. If it gets bounced/destroyed, you've lost nothing – and if it doesn't, you're seeing twice as many cards a turn as your opponent.
 [Twinstrike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Twinstrike) was just slow and cumbersome, and [Wrecking Ball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrecking+Ball) often sat in my hand – four mana is a lot to ask, considering my Seals cost one/three mana, and can just sit on the board. Every time I ended up wanting to cast [Wrecking Ball](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrecking+Ball), it was a turn that I couldn't cast other cards out of my hand, disabling Hellbent.


Lyzolda has a place in a deck with more creatures than this one. Nothing else to see here, please move along!
