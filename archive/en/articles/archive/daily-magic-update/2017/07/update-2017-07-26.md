The July 26, 2017 Update
========================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on July 26, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_Nicholas_Wolfram.jpg)
By Nicholas Wolfram




 Nicholas Wolfram fell promptly in love with tabletop and video games when he was only two years old, and he has been writing about them since he figured out how words worked. Now he puts together words about Magic, which he started playing way back in 2000. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on July 26, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by Standard metagame speculation and my own [Fraying Sanity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fraying+Sanity).


Today's Must-Read
=================


1. [God-Pharaoh's Gift](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=14067&writer=Seth+Manfield&articledate=7-26-2017) | TCGPlayer | Seth Manfield

This deck came out of seemingly nowhere to win a *Magic Online* Pro Tour Qualifier. While it does have some weakness against aggro (which has gained strong support from *Hour of Devastation*), this deck obviously has something to it based on its performance. I guess we'll see this weekend at the Pro Tour whether this is going to be a surprise contender!


After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [Azorius Fraying Sanity](http://www.mtgmintcard.com/articles/writers/soh-weng-heng/azorius-fraying-sanity) | MTG Mint Card | Soh Weng Heng

With [Startled Awake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Startled+Awake) and [Fraying Sanity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fraying+Sanity) in the same Standard, it was only a matter of time before brewers started trying to crack that formula open. Soh attempts to make this into a [Persistent Nightmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persistent+Nightmare) for their opponents.


1. [Standard Mono-Red Is Back](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/standard-mono-red-is-back/) | ChannelFireball | Corey Burkhart

If there's one things that's clear from the release of *Hour of Devastation*, it's that [Earthshaker Khenra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthshaker+Khenra) has shaken up the metagame enough to bring back Red Deck Wins. Corey joins the ongoing conversation surrounding this deck with his own take on the deck.

Amonkhet as a lot of animal-people on it. Bull-people, bird-people, snake-people, jackal-people, human-people . . . who knows how much longer the list can go? My vote goes to bird-people, by the way. I've never met a bird-person I didn't like.


Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Robert Anderson's 1st-Place Grand Prix Toronto Deck](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gptor17/top-8-decklists-2017-07-23)

Robert took two of *Hour of Devastation*'s most powerful tribal cards and showed just how powerful they can be . . . without any other cards from those tribes. Honestly, it shouldn't be a huge surprise that [Crested Sunmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crested+Sunmare) and [Pride Sovereign](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pride+Sovereign) are powerful in their own right. I'm sure Robert would have loved to open a [Regal Caracal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Regal+Caracal) in pack three, but it's pretty obvious now that it would have been overkill. Well done!
