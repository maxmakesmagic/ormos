### DAILY DECK


Midrange Abzan from Hareru-ya
=============================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)

Shuhei Nakamura

Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points.


October 8, 2014
 










[Yesterday](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/dd/esper-control-hareru-ya-2014-10-07), we looked at a deck from Hareru-ya, and today I'd like to look at another one from that shop, [a deck that went 3–0](http://www.happymtg.com/decks/view/D070303) in matches from the 20:00 FNM: Tooru Usui's midrange Abzan deck.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nissa, Worldwaker)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa, Worldwaker)
This deck's strength lies in raw card power. It's chock full of removal, with cards that dominated the Block Constructed format: [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize); [Sylvan Caryatid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid); [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix); and [Elspeth, Sun's Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Sun%27s+Champion). In addition to all that, Usui has included what is probably the best Planeswalker in the current format, [Nissa, Worldwaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%2C+Worldwaker). It's definitely an all-star deck.


From *Khans of Tarkir*, we have [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino), [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm), and two different multicolor lands. [Siege Rhino](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege+Rhino) not only offers protection from beatdown strategies; at 3 points its life drain ability is enough to pull one back from the brink if necessary. [Abzan Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Charm) offers great versatility in that, depending on circumstances, it's either removal or an extra draw. Having that kind of flexibility in only one slot is a wonderful thing.


I expect we'll be seeing decks like this a lot from here out.
