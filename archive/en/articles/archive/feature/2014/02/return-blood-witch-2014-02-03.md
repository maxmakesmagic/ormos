Return of the Blood Witch
=========================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on February 3, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson











 Despite Rakdos Aggro winning [a Standard Grand Prix](/en/events/coverage/chilean-navas-shows-madcap-skills) in this format, it's received very little press. Some updated versions have been popping up on **Magic Online** lately and they look pretty good. 

![Exava, Rakdos Blood Witch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Exava%2C+Rakdos+Blood+Witch) Of course, some of that has to do with the deck incorporating a couple of Mono-Black Devotion's tricks, such as [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat). Considering how easy it is to win games with an uncontested turn-two [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat), you'd think that it would start showing up in a more aggressive deck, but so far that hasn't been the case. 

 Ironclawish decided to do just that, although sadly could only fit two [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault)s in a mana base that is also trying to cast [Spike Jester](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Jester) and [Dreadbore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreadbore). That makes the [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat)s less threatening than Mono-Black's, but they are still very powerful. 

 Decks like Zombies used to top off their aggressive curve with a plethora of haste creatures, and Ironclawish does the same here. That sort of hard-hitting top end is super difficult to deal with, no matter what deck you're playing. It wouldn't surprise me if some finely tuned Rakdos Aggro decks started making a comeback, even if it is just on the back of [Pack Rat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat).
