Big Red
=======



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on May 30, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






Last week, I made a budget alternative to the powerful Dunkpod deck that went undefeated on the first day of [Grand Prix Minneapolis](/en/events/coverage/calcano-triumphant-aberrant-delver-deck). This powerful new archetype, designed by Leif Torgerson, approaches the Standard metagame from a unique angle. The deck has a lot of powerful synergy and it's likely to produce more impressive results in the coming weeks. Torgerson gave the deck to a few of his friends, one of whom won a qualifier on Friday night and another, Chris Schafer, managed to [post a 9–0 finish](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/grand-prix-minneapolis-day-2-coverage-2012-05-19) in the first day of competition.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab197_tum.jpg)[Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) | Art by [Drew Baker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Drew+Baker%22%5D)

The more things change, the more things seem to stay the same. Two weekends ago, some crazy stuff happened. Christian Calcano managed to win Grand Prix Minneapolis with an innovative blue-red deck. Brad Nelson made the finals of the same tournament piloting a [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) brew designed by Nick Spagnolo. This week, it seems like Standard is, once again, being put into a chokehold by White-Blue Delver decks. Luckily, [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) gives decks like Humans and Ramp the necessary tools to punish Delver. I feel Delver no longer has the same unfair advantage that led to its dominance before the release of *Avacyn Restored*.

I suspect red-green decks—of the ramping and aggressive varieties—to enjoy great success this coming weekend. This will put [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect) into a good position again, but a lot of Architects might be scared off by Delver's success last weekend.

I decided to brainstorm a bit in hopes of finding forgotten cards that happen to be well-positioned in the current Standard metagame. [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) jumped to mind immediately. Here are the things I'm scared of in the current Standard metagame: [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace), [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan), and [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan). [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) is very well positioned against all these cards.

| Tumble Magnet | Tezzeret's Gambit |

[Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) needs some degree of proliferate in order to be successful. Unfortunately, cards like [Contagion Clasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp) aren't quite as good as they once were. We're going to have to use cards like [Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit) in order to maintain counters on our [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet). This type of proliferation isn't something we can maintain forever. This leaves us in an interesting spot: If an opponent ever untaps when we have no counters on a [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) we're going to be at a huge disadvantage—we will have thrown away a card and our opponent's most impressive threat will be ready to take over the game. It's important that we don't use our [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) counters frivolously. When deciding whether or not you're going to tap something with [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) you should ask yourself a few questions:


> 
> * Will this attack put me at a life total that will force me to make suboptimal plays for the sake of playing safe? The "safe" life total is different against every deck. Against Ramp, for example, you can safely go to 6 and you won't have to worry about it affecting your game plan. However, against a deck like Red-Green Aggro, you'll probably want to keep your life total around 9. You want to afford yourself the luxury of attacking and casting spells most profitably.
> * Will this attack increase the number of turns it will take to kill my opponent? The most common problem here is lifelink creatures. It's almost always correct to tap a Germ token or any other creature that carries a [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) or [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace). It's absolutely correct to tap a [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) whenever given the opportunity.
> * Will this attack potentially provide my opponent with card advantage or a significant tempo advantage? [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) is the scariest threat in this department, but cards like [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold) and those with [Ophidian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ophidian) effects are similarly worth tapping in most spots.
> 

We need to be frugal with our charge counters, but we still only have so many we're able to use. We're going to combat this problem by putting our opponent on a reasonable clock. It's okay if our [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) runs out of counters if that pivotal point coincides with our opponent's life total being at or below 0.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/boab/boab197_koth.jpg)[Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) | Art by [Jason Chan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Jason+Chan%22%5D)

I'm already going to play [Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit), so I'd like to see what cards benefit the greatest from a single instance of proliferate. The single best threat to proliferate once is [Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer). Koth has become very easy to acquire these days. [*Duel Decks Venser vs. Koth*](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/productarticle.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/venservskoth/productinfo) put a lot of extra copies of the mythic rare into circulation. Koth fits particularly well into a red deck with a lot of sweepers and [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet)s, and [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) can be used to protect [Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer). We can use Koth's +1 ability the turn it enters the battlefield and bash our opponent for 4 damage. The next turn, we can use [Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit) and activate Koth's ultimate. [Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit) is an interesting kind of [Time Walk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Walk) when we have the opportunity to use it this way.

| 212238 | Kuldotha Phoenix |

I need a reliable win condition that has a strong damage output, evasion, and inevitability. I can't play enough threats to be sure I'm going to continue drawing threats if my opponent deals with the first one. [Kuldotha Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Phoenix) works perfectly. It attacks for 20% of our opponent's life total per swing and it has haste. Flying is basically unblockable against Red-Green Aggro, which will probably be the most successful deck next week. Perhaps the most important part of [Kuldotha Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Phoenix) is its metalcraft ability. Once the game starts to go long you will eventually reach metalcraft. Any opponent will have trouble dealing with [Kuldotha Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Phoenix) once we're able to reanimate it every turn without spending a card. This is a card I've thought about a lot recently. I want to work on a deck that uses [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting) and [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) to discard [Kuldotha Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Phoenix) in the early stages of the game. Once I've achieved metalcraft I can start returning it to play. Hit the forums or shoot me an email if you would like to see this deck in the coming weeks.

[Sphere of the Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) is going to be really good in our opening hands. [Sphere of the Suns](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns) lets us cast [Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) on the third turn. If we're lucky enough to have [Tezzeret's Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%27s+Gambit) as well, we might find ourselves with a Koth Emblem as early as the fourth turn.

One of the most attractive parts about a red deck with no small creatures is access to extremely inexpensive board sweepers like [Whipflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) and [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm). [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) is a wildly popular threat that requires a board sweeper or 3-toughness creatures and a solid curve. We're going to use [Whipflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) and [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) to deal with the *Dark Ascension* mythic rare.



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Sphere of the Suns | Slagstorm |

[Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) is an excellent answer to [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist), a card that punishes red haphazardly. Board sweepers aren't very good when they make our opponent's threats bigger. It's nice to have a mix of board-sweeping effects and spot removal. We want Pillar or Flame for [Strangleroot Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and [Whipflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) or [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) against [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells).

I want [Pristine Talisman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman) because it does everything I want to do in a deck like this. I can take some hits without having to use my [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) counters, because I can always clean up with board sweepers and recoup lost life with [Pristine Talisman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman). [Pristine Talisman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman) is also nice when you're bringing back [Kuldotha Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Phoenix).

Sometimes, I like to include cards that are absurd in Limited just to see whether or not their power translates well to Constructed. I've never really had a chance to sleeve up [Hoard-Smelter Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoard-Smelter+Dragon). I've only drawn it a few times, but I can't say I've been disappointed. In fact, the ability to kill Swords, Wurmcoils, Inkmoths, and Spheres is actually pretty good. [Hoard-Smelter Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoard-Smelter+Dragon) is a nice one-of to have when a deck makes a lot of mana.

[Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=247419) is another great mana sink. When you've attacked, it's almost always a one-turn clock when you're casting [Devil's Play](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=247419) and attacking with [Kuldotha Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Phoenix) on the same turn.

[Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine) seems like another great one-of. If a game goes long enough where I have trouble keeping counters on a [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet), then [Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine) can surely lock up the game.

### Against White-Blue Delver

This matchup is weird. They want to be aggressive, but you can often take the wind out of their sails with an early [Whipflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare), [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm), or well-timed [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame). [Pristine Talisman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pristine+Talisman) does a pretty good job of offsetting their poke. ### Against Ramp

They've got the best endgame of anybody, so that can cause some real problems. Early Koth puts a lot of pressure on them and our ideal endgame in Games 2 and 3 involves [Hoard-Smelter Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoard-Smelter+Dragon). They're going to take out [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm), so we can bring in [Precursor Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precursor+Golem) and apply tremendous pressure.

### Against Architect

They will copy our Phoenix or Dragon, so it's important we have a plan for that before it happens. We have [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) for [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine), but they often make an army of Wurmcoils. You can try to control this with Hoard-Smelter, but the best plan is just to race—they're pretty slow if you can kill [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect).

A deck like this has a ton of potential to do well. [Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) is extremely easy to acquire these days. The different angles available with this main deck and sideboard make it a nightmare for any opponent. A lot of people might lose to you if they sideboard cards like [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) because they're playing against red.

There are a lot of people who like a deck like this. It's quite strong and I'm sure you won't be disappointed if you sleeve this one up for FNM this weekend.

There are still a lot of directions we can go in the new Standard format. From [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) to [Necrotic Ooze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necrotic+Ooze), with [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) all over. Be sure to hit the forums to talk about your favorite cards in Standard.

Happy Brewing!
