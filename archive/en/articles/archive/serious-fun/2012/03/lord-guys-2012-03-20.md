Lord of the Guys
================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on March 20, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







Let's get medieval today: lords are *en vogue* this year, and you're invited to party with the creature elite. It's a formal affair, so you're going to need to brush up on style for the occasion. Lords are particular about who they admit, but the rewards are fantastic.


Being a creature among your elite comes with prestige well worth the effort.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf187_zom.jpg)[Zombie Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Master) | Art by [Jeff A. Menges](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Jeff+A.+Menges%22%5D)


"Lord" in **Magic** is an anachronistic term inherited from the beginning of the game. [Goblin King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+King) was not a Goblin but a *Lord* who granted all Goblins +1/+1 and mountainwalk. [Zombie Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zombie+Master) was similarly a Lord that granted regeneration and swampwalk to all Zombies.


While the creature type Lord is long gone, the feeling of tribal royalty is still strong. Are you ready to play courtier on the battlefield?


### Like a Marionetteâ€”The House of Skeletons


If you want to dance with bones, you need to know the long history of the House of Skeletons:



> 
> * [Drudge Skeletons](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drudge+Skeletons) first appeared in *Limited Edition* (Alpha), and remained a staple of core sets throughout the years.
> * Some functional reprints were created: [Restless Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restless+Dead) from *Mirage* and [Unworthy Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unworthy+Dead) from *Urza's Saga*.
> * Several adaptations were printed: [Kjeldoran Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Dead) from *Ice Age*, [Urborg Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Skeleton) from *Invasion*, [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) from Ravnica, [Skeletal Changeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Changeling) from Lorwyn, [Reassembling Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton) from ***Magic** 2012*, and [Manor Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manor+Skeleton) from *Dark Ascension*.
> * A lord for Skeletons, the fearsome [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron), first appeared in *Shards of Alara*.
> 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf187_death.jpg)[Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) | Art by [Nils Hamm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Nils+Hamm%22%5D)


As far as tribes go, the Skeleton tribe has many members but only the aforementioned [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) as a lord. (Note: [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton), and to a similar extent [Brass Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Herald), is the "[Mistform Ultimus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistform+Ultimus)" of lords, and is just generally assumed.) Fortunately, their long history grants them a variety of interesting options.

The Skeletons rise! While regenerating, efficient, aggressive Skeletons are fine alone, it's only with [Death Baron](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) that they get truly terrifying. When every blow is lethal and they never stop attacking, you can count on an opponent to resort to drastic measures. [Reassembling Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton), [Oversold Cemetery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversold+Cemetery), and [Volrath's Stronghold](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath%27s+Stronghold) ensure that even if you break the magic backbone behind the Skeletons, they'll *still* clatter onward.

With a [Reassembling Skeleton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reassembling+Skeleton) or [Oversold Cemetery](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oversold+Cemetery) online there isn't any drawback at all!


The House of Skeletons is not built of cards!


### The Howl Pact


While Skeletons have been rooted in **Magic** since the earliest days of the Multiverse, Werewolves are relative newcomers. Sure, you can point to [the few that existed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&subtype=+%5B&quot;Werewolf&quot;%5D&set=+!%5B&quot;Innistrad&quot;%5D+!%5B&quot;Dark+Ascension&quot;%5D) before *Innistrad* and *Dark Ascension*, but they aren't the Werewolves that will be remembered.

If this were the real world, we'd have a machine that would eat Florence. Storming the gates with a slew of early creatures, backed up by a duo of lords in the [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) and [Immerwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immerwolf), the idea is to get in as many attacks as possible. There are two ways this will play out:



> 
> * Your opponent will play spells and blockers, trying to prevent the howlers from transforming. In this case, your burn spells can clear blockers or be used after a spell kills one of your own ([Brimstone Volley](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley)).
> * Your opponent will falter for a turn or two, and in that time you will be able to ravage his or her life total sufficiently to turn your blocker-clearing burn spells into a game-ending burst.

While Werewolves and Wolves are scary without any lords, adding the leaders turns the deck away from an average aggressive deck into a salty powerhouse. Remember: If you use [Moonmist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist) to force a transformation, your Werewolves won't turn back if [Immerwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immerwolf) is out! If your [Immerwolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immerwolf) is out the transformed [Mayor of Avabruck](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) ([Howlpack Alpha](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+Alpha)) is even more powerful.


Of course, nailing an opponent with a mid-combat [Moonmist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonmist) is just about the greatest reason there is in **Magic** to let loose a verbal howl. Try it!

The Alliance of Allies is a ragtag group of fighters, led nominally by a [Kazuul Warlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazuul+Warlord) or other temporary prime minister. Unlike other tribes that strictly require their lords to do the heavy lifting, every one of the Allies contributes small pieces along the way. Individually, each would be a difficult argument to call a "lord," but when you begin adding them up the mass effect of rallying races as one can overpower any threat.


There are a few individuals who can stand up as the leader for a time, each bringing something different to the table:



> 
> * [Kabira Evangel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kabira+Evangel) grants protection to all Allies, letting them swiftly strike the enemy without worry.
> * [Talus Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Talus+Paladin) grants lifelink to Allies, bringing to bear the ability to rapidly replenish the resource of life even as they devastate an opponent.
> * [Kazuul Warlord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazuul+Warlord) amplifies every Ally, increasing everyone by +1/+1 increments for each individual who joins.
> * [Murasa Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murasa+Pyromancer) is the tactical nuclear weapon of the Allies, entering the battlefield and focusing all the power of the Alliance into a precise point of destruction.

I'd share much more interesting things about the elves, but they asked that I stay brief on their behalf. Conservation is a skill hard learned, and elvish trust is hard earned. I think you can understand.


It's impossible to discuss lords in **Magic** without focusing on the related tribal themes. But it's important to note that while many creatures can stand on their own, lords are one of the few kinds that *demand* support. [Elvish Archdruid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Archdruid) in a deck without Elves is a slightly spruced-up [Gray Ogre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Ogre). [Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) without any Spirits would just be a harder-to-cast average creature with flying. Even [Adaptive Automaton](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adaptive+Automaton), the most varied and practical of lords, would be lost without a few of the same creatures to sign up for.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf187_drog.jpg)[Drogskol Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drogskol+Captain) | Art by [Peter Mohrbacher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5B%22Peter+Mohrbacher%22%5D)


Lords are awesome and they command adoration because they form a highly desirable glue for tribal decks. Piling up any number of the same types of creatures is great, but it's adding a lord or two that turns a small gathering into a block-busting house party. It's a symbiotic relationship, and I'm always proud to see the new faces of lords unifying tribes all over again.
