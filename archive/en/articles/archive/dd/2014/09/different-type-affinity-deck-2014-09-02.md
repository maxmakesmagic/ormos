### DAILY DECK


A Different Type of Affinity Deck
=================================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)

Adam Prosak

From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer.


September 2, 2014
 










The basic Affinity shell is very powerful in Modern. Play as many cheap artifacts as you can, relying on a few powerful cards such as [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating), [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer), or [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) to do much of the heavy lifting. Yuusei Gotou felt that the deck was a little too reliant on these cards but would benefit from pulling back on the heavy artifact theme for some other hard-hitting cards.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ensoul Artifact)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul Artifact)
Instead of playing cards like [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager) or [Steel Overseer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Overseer) that require many artifacts, Yuusei's deck focused on cards that only need a small number of artifacts to be effective. This led Yuusei to [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) and [Shrapnel Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast). While [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) is still around, it is still a very effective card with only three or four artifacts in play.


With less burden on artifacts, Yuusei gains the ability to play nonartifact cards more liberally. [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) are his main-deck choices, with [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Whipflare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) in the sideboard. These cards provide a degree of resilience against cards that are normally potent against a classic Affinity deck. After all, no amount of [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge)s will stop a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) from attacking you.
