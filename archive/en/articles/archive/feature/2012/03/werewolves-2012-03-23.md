Werewolves!
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 23, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)
By Gavin Verhey




 When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he dreamt of a job making Magic cards—and now as a Magic designer, he's living his dream! Gavin has been writing about Magic since 2005. 






Several of the tribes powered by *Innistrad* block have shown up at the top levels of tournaments, and it looks like it's finally time for Werewolves to get their day in the ~~sun~~ moon! In a recent **Magic Online** Daily Event, Paulito\_Mang went 4–0 with his take on the double-sided creatures.


This aggressive Werewolf deck tries to push out of the gates quickly, sporting eight one-drop Werewolves to begin the beatdown. [Reckless Waif](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Waif) and [Wolfbitten Captive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wolfbitten+Captive) punish any deck that doesn't have a turn-one play. [Gatstaf Shepherd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatstaf+Shepherd) and [Mayor of Avabruck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) provide some solid turn-two options, and then on turn three, both [Daybreak Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daybreak+Ranger) and the Werewolf lord [Immerwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immerwolf) add even more to your fanged force. Even if your opponent finds a way to deal with your early plays, [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) sits on the top of the curve and threatens to be an army all on his own.


A huge part of this deck's success likely lies in [Full Moon's Rise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Full+Moon%27s+Rise). Many Werewolf decks in the past have sideboarded this card, but Paulito\_Mang plays a full set, main deck! The enchantment ensures your creatures can shrug off opposing board sweepers; perhaps more importantly, though, is the added trample. With all of the tokens around Standard, [Full Moon's Rise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Full+Moon%27s+Rise) lets your Werewolves chomp right through Spirit and Human tokens alike!


If you've been looking for a fun, budget-friendly deck for Friday Night **Magic** tonight, this is right up your alley. It's time for Werewolves to get some showtime on Standard's center stage!
