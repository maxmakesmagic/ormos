Today's Musts
=============


1. [Magic Math: Deploy the Gatewatch](http://www.channelfireball.com/articles/magic-math-deploy-the-gatewatch/) | ChannelFireball | Frank Karsten

I love a good Frank Karsten math article. If you aren't aware, the Hall of Fame pro literally has a Ph.D. in this kind of thing, so when he throws weird looking equations up on the screen, just trust that they're correct. Today he tackles Superfriends and just how likely you are to get the most out of [Deploy the Gatewatch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deploy+the+Gatewatch).


1. [Superfriends—EDH Archetypes](https://rocketjump.com/listen/superfriends-edh-archetypes-114) | The Command Zone | Jimmy Wong and Josh Lee Kwai

[Deploy the Gatewatch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deploy+the+Gatewatch) gets printed, and suddenly The Command Zone looks at the Superfriends archetype? *And* it's on the same day Frank Karsten does the math for it? Coincidence? Probably, but it does make it a banner day for [Deploy the Gatewatch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deploy+the+Gatewatch).


Superfriends is an archetype that uses as many planeswalkers as possible, so Jimmy and Josh walk through building a deck jam-packed with planeswalkers for Commander.


After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [Fact or Fiction: *Eldritch Moon* Release Week Edition!](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33317_Fact-Or-Fiction-Eldritch-Moon-Release-Week-Edition.html) | StarCityGames.com | Ross Merriam and Todd Stevens

There's another one of these (Fact or Fictions, that is) on StarCityGames.com Premium as well ([featuring Brad Nelson and Todd Anderson](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33313_Fact-Or-Fiction-Eldritch-Moon-Release-Week-Edition.html)) if you have access, but if not, this one asks the same questions of some pretty game gamers as well. Asking questions like "Will emerge win this weekend?" or "Will aggressive decks rule the start of the season?" or "Where are my keys?" the crew gets to the heart of what's going on in Standard.


1. [Living Splendidly in Standard](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13404&writer=Conley+Woods&articledate=7-20-2016) | TCGPlayer | Conley Woods

There's a lot of talk about [Splendid Reclamation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splendid+Reclamation) as being potentially busted—but most people are eyeing it for Modern or other older formats. Standard is a tougher format to break. But Conley Woods is going to try anyway! He's got three different Standard brews using [Splendid Reclamation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splendid+Reclamation) to great effect. But busted? Guess we'll see.


1. [Spike Pit—Stay Mad, Bro](http://puremtgo.com/articles/spike-pit-stay-mad-bro) | PureMTGO | Kyle Lewis

Kyle Lewis has fond memories of madness. And of Vampires. So, obviously, when those two joined forces in *Shadows over Innistrad*, he was rarin' to go. And then the deck didn't quite get there. But now, with *Eldritch Moon*, Lewis thinks it just might, and he's got the standouts, the synergies, and, of course, a deck list for your perusal.


1. [Holistic Wisdom: Top 10 Constructed Cards in *EMN*](http://www.mtgmintcard.com/articles/writers/freyalise/holistic-wisdom-top-10-constructed-cards-in-emn) | MTGMintCard | Freyalise

Let's see, Top X list? Check. Set review–like? Check. Controversial choices? Check. I think we might have the perfect release week article here!

Decks of the Day
================


1. [A Match to Remember](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jpVVsso-oSM)

Last night Eric Froehlich and Luis Scott-Vargas, two of the best players on the planet, played one of the [best matches in the history of the Vintage Super League](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jpVVsso-oSM). Both players were playing similar Jeskai Mentor decks ([Monastery Mentor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery+Mentor) and [Young Pyromancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) serving as the key win conditions), and the matches were ones worth playing again and again.


For one, both players put on a clinic in both taking strong lines of play, and just playing fast. But that wasn't the highlight—and there were several highlights. There was an insane comeback in the first game where the announcers basically were calling the game before fortunes reversed. Then there was the absurd second game where there were approximately a million tokens on the board and a billion triggers any time anyone played a spell.


It was an epic match, and certainly one worth reliving.
