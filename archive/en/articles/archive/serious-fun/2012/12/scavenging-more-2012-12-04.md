Scavenging for More
===================



 Posted in **Serious Fun**
 on December 4, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/astybs_authorpic.png)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







Making the most with the least available is an admirable goal. Thousands of stories, both fiction and non, revolve around the success of ingenuity borne from missing resources. It's a well-worn trope that is personified by the titular character of the  [1980s television series *MacGyver*](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MacGyver). 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf224_ghave_guru_of_spores.jpg)
[Ghave Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave+Guru+of+Spores) | Art by [James Paick](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22James%20Paick%22%5D)



 That's what [last week's common and uncommon Commander blowout](/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/dredged-deck-2012-11-27) was about. After I ran through the entire list of Standard cards eligible for a [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) deck I shared a deck:

 I'll be honest: I simply didn't make the cleanest connection between undying critters and Ghave. For those who missed it, too, let's look at [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf): 



* Cast [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf). ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20220123123600im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/green.gif) is as cheap as undying gets.

* [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf)'s undying trigger resolves, returning it to play with a +1/+1 counter.
* Use Ghave's first ability to remove the +1/+1 counter from [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf), netting a [Saproling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling) creature token.

[Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) works the same way, and [Lumberknot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumberknot) is a perfect fit for this in addition to being a hexproof place to put +1/+1 counters. I'm not MacGyver, but the mind-at-large of the community never fails to see what I don't.

### Gold Standards


 As I emphasized last week, there is no correct or incorrect choice for cards. What you *want* is always more important than any idea of *need*. With only four rares in our example deck it's easy to see that building a Commander deck without any rares (for those of you willing to reach into *Legends* and *Chronicles* for a commander) is possible. 


Like many of you, I don't like cutting away rares from Commander. In fact, part of why I enjoy Commander so much is because I can pile together lots of splashy, sweet cards that I don't have a home for anywhere else. Balancing that desire against our restrictions is the tension at the heart of newer Commander players' barrier to entry.


Here are the restrictions we're working under:



* How can we use more commons and uncommons to full effect?
* How can we show newer players, using newer cards from newer sets, how to get in?

Put another way, they will limit what players can do, and how they can do it:



* "Commons and uncommons" I took to mean without the splashy, powerful rares that are harder to find and acquire. I used the decklists and games featured in [recent Grand Prix event coverage](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx) as a guide for what's hot and desirable as rares to avoid. I also looked at preconstructed decks and promotional cards from Prerelease events to see what may be more abundant.
* "Newer players" I took to mean those who only have access to a small, recently released pool of cardsâ€”or, [Standard legal](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=judge/resources/sfrstandard).

 Circumstances vary by player. Commons, uncommons, and rares that aren't featured in Standard tournaments are still daunting if you just picked up **Magic** with *Return to Ravnica*. It's impossible to cover everything, but the idea of looking through what you have to identify what you want to use is applicable everywhere. 


 I've [already done that](https://www.dropbox.com/s/v2o46sw3dnnss3q/CMDR.txt) with rares in Standard, so it's just a matter to making swaps where one card does something I want in a way I like even better. It's not about defining one card as "better" than another, but that sometimes I like how a card gets the job done even more. Rares often fit the bill, but they don't have to.

### Dashing Through Dining


 The first game I played featured both a mono-blue [Thada Adel, Acquisitor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thada+Adel%2C+Acquisitor) and blue-red [Jhoira of the Ghitu](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhoira+of+the+Ghitu) deck. Both of these Commanders are among those I consider strong, and I knew both would bring powerful stuff to bear. The game unfolded in a strange way: 




|  |  |

* Thada came down early and began stealing everything in sight. While [Mask of Avacyn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+Avacyn) was the only item taken from me, it was [Sol Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sol+Ring), [Mana Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Crypt), [Umezawa's Jitte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Umezawa%27s+Jitte), and more that the Jhoira player lost. While I tried a well-timed [Crushing Vines](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crushing+Vines) to take out the Jitte in an attack, it was countered.
* Just as Thada took complete control, the player had to quit. While all the artifacts that were stolen stayed out, the Jhoira player was primed to level the battlefield with [Slagstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm). I had [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores) out and ripped [Young Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Wolf) off the top of the library.

Through it all I kept plugging away by making [Saproling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling)s and ever-increasing the size of [Ghave, Guru of Spores](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghave%2C+Guru+of+Spores). 



[Maelstrom Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Wanderer) eventually got to [Body Double](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Body+Double) his [Avenger of Zendikar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avenger+of+Zendikar), making a metric ton of Plant tokens. Following that up with several ramp spells, including my old-school favorite [Quirion Trailblazer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Trailblazer), the kingmaker position firmly switched. However, my 10/10 Ghave and seven [Saproling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Saproling)s, with room to grow, weren't far behind. 


 One of [The Mimeoplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Mimeoplasm) player had enough and cast [Go for the Throat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) on Ghave. While it's hard to say why, as the player didn't share, I'd suspect it was due to how long it took me to iterate through the actions. Even with auto-yields it takes a lot of time to remove counters and sacrifice creatures. 


 Soon, one player's [Butcher of Malakir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher+of+Malakir) faced down another's [Grave Pact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Pact), like a powder keg primed. [Maelstrom Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Wanderer) fired for [Roil Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roil+Elemental) and [Rite of Replication](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Replication), which naturally copied the [Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental). A [Mosswort Bridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mosswort+Bridge) yielded [Creeping Renaissance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Renaissance) for creatures. Some landfall later and lots of things were stolen.... but there wasn't an attack. 


I pounced.


 With [Golgari Decoy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Decoy), [Ring of Valkas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ring+of+Valkas), and [Phantom General](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+General) in hand, and the mana to play them all, I scored an exact 24 damage alpha strike against the [Maelstrom Wanderer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Wanderer) player. Just as I had been planning for a few turns, saving things for a one-shot wonder worked. 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/sf/sf224_pounce.jpg)  
 Unfortunately, time really was against me: I was forced into concession by running out of time as [Extractor Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extractor+Demon) milling let one of the [The Mimeoplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Mimeoplasm) players combo out the game. 


### Fungus Among Us


Did our deck of "mostly commons and uncommons" let us play some good Commander? I'd say we've succeeded. Our deck won't become a model for absolute power, or an oppressive feature our friends may hate. It's straightforward, aggressive, and gives us just a few ways to power out a battlefield full of critters and keep others honest.


 It's also as weak as other decks to [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) effects, as well as any deck that's brimming with the most powerful cards Commander can offer. Again, we can't ask a handful of rares to replace a well-tuned and battle-forged deck of power, but keeping things going and giving us ways to get in a few good licks is enough. 


I'd love to hear what else you see for the deck.
