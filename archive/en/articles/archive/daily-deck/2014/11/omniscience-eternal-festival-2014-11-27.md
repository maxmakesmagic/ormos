There was one other player in the Eternal Festival beside [Kageyama](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/daily-deck/jund-eternal-festival-2014-11-13) and [Kikuchi](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/dd/abzan-eternal-festival-2014-11-12) who posted a 23-point top finish in the Swiss: Hisayuki Hōseki, with his [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience) deck.



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Omniscience)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience)


I don't think much needs to be said about [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience). Basically, if it hits the table, you basically win. The trick, then, is getting it out.


How does one support a ten-mana card and build in a win condition? It's truly a head-scratcher that makes you sit down and think. At least, if one is in regular *Magic* thinking mode, as I was when I first saw this card.


But in Legacy thinking mode, the answer to the first part of the question is easy: you can ignore the mana cost with [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell). So now it's just about the win condition. Ahhh, Legacy.


Looking closer, we can see this deck is built around [Burning Wish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning+Wish) as the axis. Since the Wish is used to get [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell), there are effectively seven copies of that card. Then, with [Omniscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omniscience), the Wish can be used to pull in [Enter the Infinite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enter+the+Infinite), which lets you go into super-combo mode with infinite [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). Free Eldrazi? Sure, why not?
