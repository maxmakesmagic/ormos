Modern Cats
===========



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on May 20, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 Melissa is a former Magic pro player and strategy writer who is now working in R&D on the Play Design team. 






When I started playing *Magic*, eighteen years ago, I discovered a deck called "Zoo." This deck was popular in the now-retired Extended format and got its name because its creatures are usually found in a zoo. The deck played lions ([Savannah Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lion)s), apes ([Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape)), and sometimes even dogs ([Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup)). Over time, *Magic*'s creatures have gotten better and the deck received many upgrades. [Savannah Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Savannah+Lion)s became [Loam Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Lion) and [Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup) became severely outclassed by creatures like [Watchwolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Watchwolf), [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl), and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).



Today's deck is a unique take on Zoo. If you look at the creature base, it's safe to say that you can find them all in a zoo. What's interesting about this deck is that every creature in the deck has one thing in common: they are all cats! FredSavage16 piloted his cat deck to a 3-1 finish in a Daily Event on *Magic Online*.



I never realized this before, but there are a lot of great cats in Modern. [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) was once one of the most powerful creatures when Modern debuted as a format and was even banned for a short time. [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) and [Fleecemane Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleecemane+Lion) dominated their respective Standard formats. The deck even plays Brimaz, a card that I used to call "Brimaz, God of Cats."



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Steppe+Lynx)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fleecemane+Lion)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fleecemane+Lion) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimaz%2C+King+of+Oreskos)


Rounding out the deck is [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant), the cat Planeswalker. There have been many versions of Ajani printed, but I agree with FredSavage16 that [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant) is the best fit for this deck. Dealing 3 damage can be backbreaking in a deck that also plays [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and [Lightning Helix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix). Zoo decks historically have struggled in dealing with large creatures, so the plus ability is also beneficial here. I can see the argument for playing [Ajani, Caller of the Pride](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani%2C+Caller+of+the+Pride) instead of [Ajani Vengeant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ajani+Vengeant). Sometimes it's hard reaching four mana in a deck with only 20 lands and giving a [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) double strike can go a long way.
