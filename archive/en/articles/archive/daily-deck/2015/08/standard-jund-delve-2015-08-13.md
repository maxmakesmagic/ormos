Standard Jund Delve
===================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on August 13, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Graveyard-based decks have been increasing in popularity since the release of *Magic Origins*. Most of these decks use [Rally the Ancestors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors) and take advantage of the new sacrifice outlet, [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk). The deck we're looking at today is also a graveyard deck, but it uses a different suite of cards than what we're used to seeing.


This Jund deck was piloted by Luke Mulcahey to a 33rd-place finish at Pro Tour *Magic Origins*. I called this deck "Delve" because it's trying to go all-in with self-mill to utilize powerful delve spells. [Commune with the Gods](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Commune+with+the+Gods), [Gather the Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gather+the+Pack), and [Satyr Wayfinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Satyr+Wayfinder) are the spells Luke used to fill up his graveyard. These cards allowed him to cast an early delve creature such as [Gurmag Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gurmag+Angler) or [Tasigur, the Golden Fang](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang). [Flamewake Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamewake+Phoenix) is a card that hasn't seen much play in Standard, but it definitely fits well in this deck. With all the milling that Luke is doing, [Flamewake Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamewake+Phoenix) won't be cast for his mana cost very often. Luke also has a reasonable Plan B of playing a more midrange game. Larger creatures like Dragons and [Reaper of the Wilds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reaper+of+the+Wilds) can help close out a game if the early creatures have been dealt with.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tasigur%2C+the+Golden+Fang)
[Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) is a very important card here. This deck uses the early game to set up, so you'll need [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) if you're ever going to survive an early assault from a red deck. Every creature in the deck can survive [Languish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Languish) except for [Flamewake Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamewake+Phoenix), which can easily be returned to play if it's swept away.


This is a very cool take on self-mill, because while it wants to get a lot of cards in its graveyard, it's not doing any actual reanimating. Instead, it's using the cards for mana to fuel delve spells. It's pretty clear that delve is a very strong ability, and this deck proves just how powerful the strategy can be.
