Personalizing Commander 2018: Adaptive Enchantment
==================================================



 Posted in **How to Build**
 on August 7, 2018 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 






***Commander (2018 Edition)* decks come out August 10!**


Head to a [store near you](http://locator.wizards.com/) to pick up the newest *Commander* decks, then start planning how you can personalize your deck and perfect your play style.


[Yesterday](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/how-build/personalizing-commander-2018-exquisite-invention-2018-08-06) I showed you how to personalize "Exquisite Invention," the blue-red artifacts deck from *Commander (2018 Edition)*. In that article I covered a few of the reasons why you might want to swap out cards from your brand-new preconstructed deck, so lets just jump right into how I'd go about making "Adaptive Enchantment," the green-white-blue (Bant) enchantments deck, my own.



![Arixmethes, Slumbering Isle](https://media.wizards.com/2018/images/daily/cardart_C18_Arixmethes-Slumbering-Isle.jpg)[Arixmethes, Slumbering Isle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arixmethes%2C+Slumbering+Isle) | Art by: [Dimitar Marinski](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?action=advanced&output=spoiler&method=visual&artist=+%5BDimitar%5D+%5BMarinski%5D)
Adapting "Adaptive Enchantment"
===============================


Adaptive Enchantment is the Bant enchantments deck in *Commander 2018*, combining green, white, and blue into a creative and defense-oriented deck.

Again, we don't have a baseline of experience to draw from with updates, so we'll focus instead on enhancing what the deck does well already—make enchantment happen. (It's not not going to happen.)


**Upsides**:


* Plenty of powerful enchantments, supported by the new "enchantress" [Tuvasa the Sunlit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tuvasa+the+Sunlit).
* Enchantment bonuses and synergy with cards like [Herald of the Pantheon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herald+of+the+Pantheon), [Sigil of the Empty Throne](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne), and [Estrid's Invocation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Estrid%27s+Invocation).
* Strong control cards like [Winds of Rath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winds+of+Rath) keep the battlefield under control.
* There are plenty of powerful enchantments to draw from and try out.

**Downsides**:


* Auras are a liability—if the creature is destroyed, you lose the Aura too.
* Going wide to close a game out takes time and works against our battlefield-clearing spells.

In this deck, we're pulled in two directions: slow down to take over a game and pile up Auras on creatures to attack. The former wants ways to gain advantages and win without committing too many resources. The latter wants to go fast and quick with one or two superpowered creatures.


In this case, I feel slowing down and leaning into constant incremental value is the biggest value in the deck. While going big with Auras is always exciting, there are many more cards like [Sigil of the Empty Throne](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne) than [Bear Umbra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bear+Umbra).

**Low-Synergy Cards**:


* [Righteous Authority](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Authority), [Epic Proportions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Epic+Proportions), and [Spawning Grounds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spawning+Grounds) are big, high-risk commitments in a game of Commander.

* There are much better removal options for us than [Dismantling Blow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismantling+Blow).
* [Heavenly Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heavenly+Blademaster) and [Bruna, Light of Alabaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bruna%2C+Light+of+Alabaster) are all-in on Auras, which we're moving away from.
* [Loyal Unicorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Unicorn) and [Loyal Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Drake) aren't doing anything extra with enchantments. Neither is [Daxos of Meletis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daxos+of+Meletis).
* [Dawn's Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawn%27s+Reflection) is better suited with other forms of mana acceleration.

As we'll see in the "high synergy" section, there's a ton of room to amp up the power of our deck with enchantments that do even heavier lifting. Additionally, we can use low-risk ways to take over the game without having to go all-in on one or two creatures.

**High-Synergy Cards**:


* [Ghostly Prison](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Prison), [Propaganda](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Propaganda), and (especially) [Sphere of Safety](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+Safety) do a ton of work slowing opponents from attacking *you*. They can still hit others, but time is what we're interested in.
* [Rhystic Study](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhystic+Study) is a potent enchantment for either slowing opponents down or drawing a bunch of extra cards.
* While we're at it, "Enchantress" creatures like [Mesa Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mesa+Enchantress), [Verduran Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verduran+Enchantress), and [Satyr Enchanter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Satyr+Enchanter) keep the cards flowing as we play out our enchantments.
* [Courser of Kruphix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Courser+of+Kruphix) helps us play lands out of our library while padding our life total—and is an enchantment itself!
* [Grasp of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Fate) and [Aura Shards](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aura+Shards) are powerful removal spells that beef up our enchantment options.
* [Starfield of Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starfield+of+Nyx) is among the most powerful enchantments we can add—letting us both make our enchantments a late-game victory option but also get back our best enchantment that was destroyed.*Every. Turn.* (It's very good.)
* Gods from *Theros* block like [Thassa, God of the Sea](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa%2C+God+of+the+Sea) and [Heliod, God of the Sun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heliod%2C+God+of+the+Sun) give us indestructible enchantments we can sink extra mana into and easily turn on as creatures later. They're win conditions we can count on.
* [Sterling Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sterling+Grove) and [Greater Auramancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Auramancy) are two more enchantments that can do incredible things for us. Protecting our enchantments from targeted removal makes them even harder to destroy, but searching up an enchantment on demand with [Sterling Grove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sterling+Grove) can change the game outright.

There are many more enchantment options—we didn't even look at [Control Magic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Control+Magic) and other ways to just take things from opponents!—that can give us a cascade of value, but starting with some of the strongest and playing the deck to see how it feels has to happen next.


And then it's time to update the deck again.
