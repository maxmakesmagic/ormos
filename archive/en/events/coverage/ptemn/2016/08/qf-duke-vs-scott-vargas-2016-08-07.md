Quarterfinals: (15) Reid Duke (Red-Green Delirium Ramp) Vs. (5) Luis Scott-Vargas (Bant Company)
================================================================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on August 7, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Walkinshaw-Profile.jpg)
By Ray “blisterguy” Walkinshaw











Coming into his third Pro Tour Top 8 in a row, No. 5-ranked Luis Scott-Vargas is clearly a man with his head screwed on right. He's a man who knows what he's doing and knows how to play *Magic* better than most. I asked him to go into detail about his game plan for this match with his Bant Company deck:


"I'm gonna Bant him."


Across the table from Scott-Vargas is teen heartthrob No. 15-ranked Reid Duke. A man who also knows his way away around the *Magic* table and likes long walks on the beach. Duke strikes me as an elegant and well-spoken man, now in his second Pro Tour Top 8 and playing a Red-Green Ramp deck that makes the most of [Grapple with the Past](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapple+with+the+Past) and [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End). I'm sure he has more to say on this matchup:


"I'm going to win. I'm going to take control of his turns, and beat him." Both players laughed. I assume at my expense.


The Games
=========


Scott-Vargas began festivities with a [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter) while Duke played a [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency). Reid cracked the Vessel on turn two, taking a [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) over a couple of lands. Unfortunately for Duke, his failure to play a spell meant he was suddenly facing down a [Krallenhorde Howler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krallenhorde+Howler), which was quickly joined by a [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit). Duke played a third land but could only pass back the turn. Scott-Vargas also played a third land without play, but at least attacked Duke down to 12.


Duke tried to [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald), but Scott-Vargas had a [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) for it. Duke then played [Kozilek's Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek%27s+Return) to kill the [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit), but it wasn't enough to take out the 2/3 flier or 3/3 Werewolf. Scott-Vargas attacked Duke to 7 and passed the turn back, this time with four mana open.


[Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow) entered the battlefield for Duke, joined by a trio of Spider tokens, but Scott-Vargas played [Dromoka's Command](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dromoka%27s+Command) on his [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) to take down one of the Spiders, then untapped and played a [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) on Ishkanah. He attacked with his 3/3 and 3/4, and Duke's remaining Spiders dutifully soaked up the attack.


Duke played [Ulvenwald Hydra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Hydra), but Scott-Vargas showed him a second [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) and they were off to Game 2.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/ptemn/ptemn_qf_lsv.jpg)  
*Luis Scott-Vargas is here to make puns and Bant people, and he's all out of puns.*


As they waited to start Game 2, Duke reflected on Game 1. "Thinking about that hand, knowing you had Recruiter I wonder if it would have been better to play the Vessel on turn two instead of turn one, but I guess if you'd had [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) I would rather have played it the way I did."


Scott-Vargas again led with [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter), but this time Duke had a [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) for it. When Scott-Vargas passed back his third turn without play, Duke was not in the least bit surprised to have his [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage) encounter a [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller). Scott-Vargas followed it up with a [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) and a fourth land, before attacking Duke to 18.


Duke played [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive) and passed, falling to 13 when Scott-Vargas attacked with the Tracker and Queller. Scott-Vargas considered his follow-up for a moment, before dropping a [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) and a [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit) into play. Duke was now facing a sizeable army and would need to do something promptly, lest he get Banted.


Duke untapped and immediately played and cashed in a [Vessel of Nascency](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vessel+of+Nascency), finding a [Dragonlord Atarka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Atarka) over three lands. He chased it up with [Nissa's Pilgrimage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nissa%27s+Pilgrimage) and passed back the turn.


Scott-Vargas drew and played his sixth land. Between his [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker), [Selfless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Spirit), [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller), a [Lumbering Falls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumbering+Falls), and his now 4/5 [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate), he had lethal.


Both players now had access to their sideboards, so they tucked in to tailor their strategies.


In Game 3, Scott Vargas played an early [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) and a [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter), but this time Duke met them with a [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker) of his own. Duke followed up with a land to get a Clue, and a [Hedron Archive](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Archive), which allowed him to both develop mana *and* cash in his Clue. Scott-Vargas simply passed through his turn, allowing his Recruiter to transform.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/ptemn/ptemn_qf_table.jpg)  
*Okay, what I said about Scott-Vargas being out of puns is clearly a lie.*


Duke played his fifth land to give himself seven mana, and played [Ishkanah, Grafwidow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ishkanah%2C+Grafwidow). Scott-Vargas tapped his four lands to counter the Spider with a [Clash of Wills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clash+of+Wills) for three. Duke then played [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald) for [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End). At this point, he had five different card types in his graveyard.


Scott-Vargas played land number five and immediately played a [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), finding [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) and a [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) to bounce Duke's Tracker. Scott-Vargas attacked Duke down to 14.


Duke untapped and again played [Traverse the Ulvenwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traverse+the+Ulvenwald), this time for [Dragonlord Atarka](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonlord+Atarka). He then played a Forest and the Dragonlord bathed the [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) and the [Duskwatch Recruiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duskwatch+Recruiter) in flame, leaving the [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) and [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) to fight on.


Scott-Vargas exhaled loudly as he began his turn, and again slammed a main-phase [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), this time turning over [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) and a second [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage), sending Atarka back from whence it came. Scott-Vargas continued his attack, dropping Duke to 10.


Emrakul descended upon the battlefield. Scott-Vargas's eyes glazed over as he fanned his hand out on the table. Clearly out of his mind, Scott-Vargas sent his [Sylvan Advocate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Advocate) head-first into the towering Eldrazi, and then played his third [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) of the game, somehow failing to find any of the creatures that were so clearly there to anyone watching. He then left the remaining cards in his hand face-up on the table: [Archangel Avacyn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archangel+Avacyn), [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller), and [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage).


Scott-Vargas shook his head as if trying to dislodge a fly and untapped for a turn of his own. He played the [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage) on Emrakul and attacked for 6, dropping Duke to 4 before passing back the turn with three mana open and a [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) openly in hand.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/ptemn/ptemn_qf_duke.jpg)  
*Reid Duke puts on a brave face as he gets Banted.*


Duke considered his turn carefully. He cracked his Clue and replayed his [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker). Scott-Vargas accepted the bait and Quelled the Spell. Duke played [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) on the Queller to get back his Tracker, but it appeared the writing was on the wall.


"I'm at 4, right?" he asked, before extending his hand, "good games."


Luis Scott-Vargas defeats Reid Duke 3–0 and will move on to the semifinals, where he'll face either Owen Turtenwald or Yuta Takahashi.
