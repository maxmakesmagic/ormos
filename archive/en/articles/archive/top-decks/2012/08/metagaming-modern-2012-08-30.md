Metagaming Modern
=================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on August 30, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td210_mtg_pc.jpg)This week is the inaugural **Magic: The Gathering** Players Championship. Sixteen of the Pro Tour's brightest stars—from Rookie of the Year Alexander Hayne; to maybe the greatest player of our era, Luis Scott-Vargas; to maybe the greatest player *of all time*, Jon Finkel—will battle in a sixteen-man brawl to determine 2012's Player of the Year.


Of the various formats where these titans of **Magic** will test their mettle, only one involves seventy-five-card decklists that so deeply concern Top Decks: and that format is Modern. Now, lucky for us fans of the game, we just had a different mega-tournament featuring Modern marvels (the [World **Magic** Cup](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/welcome-world-magic-cup-2012-08-17-0)) that might give us a window into what we might see in the Constructed portion. By the time you are reading this, the **Magic** Players Championship will be underway (and truth be told, the first round of Modern will already be in the history books)... but the playoffs will (also) be Modern. What are some of the possibilities we can borrow from the World **Magic** Cup?

Jund was the darling of Block Constructed at the World **Magic** Cup and remains a pillar of the Modern format. This deck is the quintessence of card quality... [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) and [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) on turn one; [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) as arguably the best one-mana support spell, ever; and scads of removal riding up the curve... [Terminate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate), [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil), [Maelstrom Pulse](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse). Creatures will die when facing this deck, whether by the virtue of its interaction, grabbing a freebie on the back of a [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf), or some kind of great combat creature (e.g., [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks)).

Between [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), and Liliana, Jund can interact with and disrupt essentially any kind of deck in Modern. Once you've softened up the opponent a bit, it doesn't take too long to finish the job with [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf).


The sideboard has tools for all different kinds of opponents... [Ancient Grudge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge) for Affinity, [Grafdigger's Cage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) and [Jund Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jund+Charm) for graveyard decks, and [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) for [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) or [Kiki-Jiki](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki) infinite combos. Jund can take on all comers.

Staying within Jund colors, Assault Loam is a powerful midrange deck that leans heavily on [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam). [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) and a single [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) can help you hit repeated land drops; and [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) can take the edge off of [Ghost Quarter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter)'s card economy issues while disrupting an opponent's mana base. Of course, dredging [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) over time will make [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) bigger and bigger!

The real powerhouse moves that this deck can accomplish are [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) + [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) (or [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) to a lesser extent), where you can discard a land to trade for a real card; or [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) + [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault), which can make for a reasonably quick clock. Once you have [Seismic Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seismic+Assault) on the battlefield, especially if you have Loam access, the game is a different one; you can kill almost any small creature at a whim, and it takes maybe six mana worth of [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam) re-buys to kill the opponent outright, just by aiming for the head.

Midrange decks are notoriously thin against combo, but when you factor in the [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) setup of this deck, all you will see are empty hands on the other side of the table. One of the really cool things about dredging [Life from the Loam](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Life+from+the+Loam)? You can eventually uncover your [Raven's Crime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raven%27s+Crime) or [Flame Jab](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Jab) in the graveyard.

This midrange aggro deck is built on some impressive three-drop creatures: [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) and [Doran, the Siege Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower). First-turn [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) makes hitting one of these on turn two easy, and once it is down...? You have a serious threat!

[Doran, the Siege Tower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doran%2C+the+Siege+Tower) makes [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) (usually \*/1+\*) marginally bigger and [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) is itself a positive power threat. Oh, and Doran is essentially a 5/5 for three, which is [Phyrexian Negator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Negator) territory, but with no fundamental drawback.


[Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) is a surgeon with a potentially very big scalpel. It acts as kind of a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) (you can tap a land for mana, then sacrifice it, getting another land for mana that turn); chaining through lands like [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) and [Marsh Flats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats) make [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) bigger and bigger as you cycle through your deck. At the same time, you can eventually find threats like [Stirring Wildwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stirring+Wildwood) and [Treetop Village](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village).

Doran is a reasonably disruptive deck; between [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), [Tidehollow Sculler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidehollow+Sculler), and various creature removal or graveyard hate, you can put the opponent off a turn, hopefully mopping up with your powerhouse threats before he or she can come back.

The Modern Affinity deck at this point runs relatively little actual "affinity"-mechanic spells (I chose Peleira's for its inclusion of [Thoughtcast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtcast)). The deck rides the synergies and offensive impact of the *Mirrodin* and *Scars of Mirrodin* street gangs to win very quickly. Just on the straight creature cards, you can play a ton of [Memnite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)s and a [Signal Pest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest), get in, play another guy or so, then clean up with a [Master of Etherium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium) buff. [Steelshaper's Gift](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steelshaper%27s+Gift) for [Cranial Plating](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating) makes for a huge impact piece of offensive Equipment, and [Galvanic Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Blast) (plus [Shrapnel Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shrapnel+Blast) in some versions) offers insane finishing power.

Pretty cool hybrid deck here...


On the one hand, you can go [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) into [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) and march up the value chain, drawing a card with [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens), getting back 2 life with [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) (while giving yourself a renewable body for [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod)), blowing up one of the opponent's lands with [Avalanche Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders), and finally stealing the opponent's best thing with [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) (which ideally you could further steal with another [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) or something).

Or, you can play a brutal combo deck.


Either [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) (which can, by the way, untap [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) if need be) or [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) will combine famously with [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). The Legendary Goblin can double a [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) (which will then untap him for another tap, again and again) or a [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) (which can then blink him back untapped, for another go 'round, and another after that). You will then give yourself nigh-infinite 1/4 or 3/4 creatures. Presumably with "infinite" guys, you can overwhelm the opponent with a single, combo-licious attack step.

As a [Birthing Pod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) deck, this one has the ability to draw on all different kinds of specialty haters—from [Ethersworn Canonist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethersworn+Canonist) against decks that want to do a lot of stuff in a single turn (sorry, [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf)); to the cantrip no-no [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) (how much does that [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) cost again?); to redundant mana denial via [Fulminator Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fulminator+Mage) and [Avalanche Riders](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avalanche+Riders); to insurance in the mirror via [Linvala, Keeper of Silence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+Keeper+of+Silence).


This is one of the most flexible strategies in Modern, acting as a progressive creature-card-advantage deck on one end, but also as a brutal and relatively quick combo deck as its primary route to victory.

So... the stones on this guy!


Kuo actually (co-)*won* the World **Magic** Cup with this deck in front of him, knowing that Modern was going to be the sole Constructed format at the most prestigious sixteen-man, invitation-only tournament of the year just two weeks later.


There used to be a saying among Regional Champions-feeding-into-Nationals aspirants: "dance with the girl what brung ya." Will Kuo repeat his Modern selection?


Kuo's white-blue deck is a bit different from other much more typical Modern Delver decks. His curve is higher, with [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) and [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command)—both four-mana spells—on top of [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks), and [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) on three. This deck has more expensive cards, but they are certainly quality ones that can have a solid impact on the game (especially against aggressive opponents or those trying to compete at one-for-one).

Kuo, unlike the RUG (relatively typical) or RWU (most typical) Delver players in Modern, ran only two colors (not counting [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) as a fake black one)... but quite a number of lands at twenty-six. The higher land count afforded him access to [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) (the old Standard favorite) and [Elspeth, Knight-Errant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant) as a solo table-snapper. By cutting what most consider the trademark offensive creature for a strategy like this, he also freed up the [Serum Visions](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) slots that are used to smooth out lower land counts and more reliably flip up [Insectile Aberration](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration).

And while on the topic of "typical" Delver decks, I decided to end on a somewhat atypical one.


The structure of Binek's RWU main deck will have most Modern aficionados' heads nodding... twenty-one lands; fast creatures headlined by [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets); super-fast support spells (primarily burn) with [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix), and so on.


... and then there is [Tribal Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames)!



![Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tribal+Flames)

 

[Tribal Flames](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) is a two-mana burn spell that, in concert with Binek's [Stomping Ground](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stomping+Ground) or [Blood Crypt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Crypt), can hit for as many as ***5*** damage. Quite the potential pairing with [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), no?

The other player has got to put both [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and [Sphinx of the Steel Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Steel+Wind) in your graveyard, and you know what happens next, when you untap.


Or, greedy-side, you can go for [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites); [Sphinx of the Steel Wind](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Steel+Wind); [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting); and [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite). If you have sufficient time, there is no good split for the opponent (you can make sure the huge guys are in your graveyard by playing or flashing back [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting)). Imagine how surprised an opponent who thought you were playing a super-quick, essentially two-dimensional Star Spangled Slaughter deck might be finding him- or herself pinned by 5-damage burn spells and a reanimator transformation!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td210_sphinx_of_the_steel_wind.jpg)Modern is a format of many combos... blue-red storm decks as well as more traditional combo-[Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) decks. It is a format of Tron putting together the kinds of [Gifts Ungiven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gifts+Ungiven) packages we just discussed, or simply hard-casting [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn). Modern gives us scads and scads of [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)-centric offensive or midrange creature assaults, and more than one head-scratcher of a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) play.


...but the World **Magic** Cup, for the most part, showcased offensive decks (if even midrange or multi-dimensional ones)... which is why we focused on those kinds today.
