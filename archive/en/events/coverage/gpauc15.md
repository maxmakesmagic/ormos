Kakumae Conquers Auckland
=========================




A new Dragonlord has emerged, and his name is Teruya Kakumae. Crushing Maitland Cameron in two quick games, Kakumae became the Grand Prix Auckland Champion in less time than it took to write this.

TOP 8 COVERAGE



Teruya Kakumae quietly entered the Grand Prix Auckland Top 8 amid the celebration and fanfare often afforded local players, but by the time the booster packs had been cleared away and everyone was building their decks, it was clear Kakumae had amassed a powerful Mardu Horde under the leadership of Kolaghan, the Storm’s Fury, and the locals were in for a rough ride.


Kakumae first battled his way through Jacques van Eeden’s Abzan deck in three games, before advancing to face John Brugman’s Savage Knuckleblade and chums. Big Knucks gave the first game to Brugman, but Kolaghan soon showed up to show Brugman who was the boss, and Kakumae advanced to the finals to face Tasmania’s Maitland Cameron.


Cameron’s Abzan deck had repelled Jason Chung’s aggressive Red-Green deck, if only just, before moving on to dispatch Fabian Dickmann’s Tempo-focused Jeskai deck. Unfortunately, what stood between Cameron and the trophy was Kakumae’s Bellowing Saddlebrute, and it hit back and hit back *hard.* Kakumae demolished Cameron in two straight games in less than ten minutes.


Accompanying Kolaghan in Kakumae’s deck were a Noxious Dragon and a Wardscale Dragon, with a Shockmaw Dragon cheering from the sideboard. It’s as if the Fate of Tarkir had been Reforged, and Teruya Kakumae was here to tell us that they’re coming. The Dragons are coming...

Day 1 Coverage



We started the day with 383 players, the largest ever turnout in New Zealand by a fair margin, but then we gave them all Fate Reforged sealed decks and made them fight it out, and now we’re left with just 64 players advancing to Day 2. I’m sure the rest of the field will be able to content themselves with the multitude of public events on offer tomorrow.


We had three undefeated players going into Round 8, but Jason Chung was paired down and lost, and Luke Mulcahy lost to remaining undefeated player, Richard Tasker from Hamilton, just an hour or so south of Auckland.


Also playing tomorrow at 8-1 will be Nam Sung Wook, Michael Maurici, Wilfy Horig, and Maitland Cameron. At 7-2 are Ken Yukihiro, Yifan Wei, Ivan Schroder, Sam Loy, Paul Jackson, Patty Robertson, Joseph Sclauzero, and Don van Ravenswaaij. Sneaking though at less than that are Hao-Shan Huang, Allen Zhang, Gene Brumby, Isaac Egan, Zen Takahashi, and some guy called Hall of Famer Shuhei Nakamura (I believe that’s what it says in his passport now).


Join us in the morning while we watch the Top 64 draft their way to the Top 8 playoffs, and see if we can’t send off *Fate Reforged* Limited in style!
