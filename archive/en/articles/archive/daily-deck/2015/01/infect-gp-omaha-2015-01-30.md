Infect at GP Omaha
==================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 30, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






Damage is not the only way to win at *Magic*.



Some decks win by emptying their opponents' libraries. Others win by resolving cards that say "win the game."



However, the deck I'd like to introduce today is neither—it does not attack life total nor deck.


[![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Groundswell)](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Groundswell)

Do you remember the last way to win a game of *Magic*? Yes, poison.



The concept of the poison deck is not far off from a mono-green beatdown deck: Grow your creatures with spells like [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) and break through.



Keep in mind that poison wins with only ten damage compared to the regular 20, so that [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth) turns into a [Become Immense](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Become+Immense) if it's cast on a infect creature.



I almost forgot, the Modern [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth)s are +4/+4. Add this to the creature's original power times two: 1+1+4+4=10 poison counters and your opponent is dead.



Just remember that infect creatures are weak little 1/1s. Don't take any risks. Protect your precious creatures with [Apostle's Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Apostle%27s+Blessing) and [Vines of Vastwood](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vines+of+Vastwood).
