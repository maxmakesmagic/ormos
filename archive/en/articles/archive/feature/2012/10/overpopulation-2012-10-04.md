Overpopulation
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on October 4, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











 It's Selesyna Week here on DailyMTG.com, and around the **Magic** universe, many authors and websites have taken on the theme. So, despite my reputation of going against the crowd, I figured a few nods to Selesyna were appropriate. Luckily, with the release of *Return to Ravnica* comes the release of a whole new batch of legendary creatures. More legendary creatures means more opportunities for more Commander decks! So, lets get into the swing of things here at Daily Decks with a look into the [Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice). 

![Trostani, Selesnya's Voice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Trostani%2C+Selesnya%27s+Voice) This week's list comes to us courtesy of Bennie Smith over at StarCityGames.com. Bennie decided to stay on theme this week and tackled his take on a Selesyna Commander list with Trostani, Selesyna's Voice at the helm. Green-white naturally has a lot of Commander mainstays you get to show off with any Selesnya deck you might be interested in. Cards like [Harmonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize) are just good cards to have access to. Other cards in this department include things like [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime), [Path to Exile](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile), and [Eternal Witness](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Witness). But beyond just the good cards, finding a theme for your Commander deck is where much of the fun lies. Luckily, with a general like Trostani, we can probably figure out what direction we need to go in. 

 Bennie knows and jammed a bunch of awesome token makers into the deck as a result. If you want one-shot token making, we've got it. [Armada Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armada+Wurm), [Bestial Menace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bestial+Menace), and [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm) all give you the instant gratification of a token in play, ready to be populated. Or you can turn to a more sustained token generating approach. Cards like [Jade Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jade+Mage), [Hornet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hornet) Queen, and [Thraben Doomsayer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Doomsayer) all help to ensure you have enough tokens to turn on all of the tokens-matter cards still in your deck. And where would tokens be without a way to get them angry and more powerful? [Collective Blessing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Blessing), [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township), and [Eldrazi Monument](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Monument), among others, turn all of your 1/1 Insects, Squirrels, and Saprolings into Beasts with an attitude. Selesnya definitely believes in strength in numbers and this list delivers. For a full breakdown of Bennie's list, check out his article on the subject [here](http://www.starcitygames.com/magic/commander/24937-Give-Commander-A-Try-With-Trostani-Selesnyas-Voice.html).
