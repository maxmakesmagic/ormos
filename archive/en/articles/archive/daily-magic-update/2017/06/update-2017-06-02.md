The June 2, 2017 Update
=======================



 Posted in **Daily Magic Update**
 on June 2, 2017 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_BlakeRasmussen.jpg)
By Blake Rasmussen




Blake is the content manager for DailyMTG.com, making him the one you should email if you have thoughts on the website, good or less good (or not good). He's a longtime coverage reporter and hasn't turned down a game of Magic in any format ever. 






The Daily *Magic* Update is a roundup of everything *Magic* you should know on June 2, 2017. Today's Update is brought to you by the D&D [Stream of Annihilation](#9).


What to Watch This Weekend
==========================


1. [Grand Prix Amsterdam](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpams17) | [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic) | 12 a.m. PT/3 a.m. ET/7 a.m. UTC
2. [Grand Prix Omaha](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpoma17) | [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic) | After the Grand Prix Amsterdam broadcast

It's a Grand Prixtrabonanza!


Yes, I'm going to stick with that.


We have three Grand Prix on the weekend and two broadcasting on [twitch.tv/magic](https://www.twitch.tv/magic). Amsterdam goes first and Omaha's broadcast will follow immediately on its tail, providing hours upon hours of Standard coverage this weekend. So load up on snacks, cancel all of your plans, and settle in for a long weekend of *Magic* from around the globe.


And if you want the full experience, you can also follow [Grand Prix Manilla via text coverage on the website](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpman17).


Today's Must
============


1. [No Longer a One-Deck Metagame](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/no-longer-a-one-deck-metagame/) | Channel Fireball | Ondřej Stráský

Marvel is the deck everyone will be aiming for at our Grand Three this weekend, but Stráský sees a shift in the metagame that might even things out a bit.


After Those, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
============================================


1. [So You Want to Beat Aetherworks Marvel?](http://articles.mtgcardmarket.com/new-developments-in-standard/) | MTG Card Market | Dylan Donegan

Don't we all? (Including Ondřej Stráský.)


Donegan has some actual advice for doing just that. I just have pithy comments. His way is far more useful.


1. [*Amonkhet* in Modern](http://www.toplevelpodcast.com/amonkhet-in-modern/) | Top Level Podcast | Mike Flores and Patrick Chapin

[Glory-Bound Initiate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory-Bound+Initiate)? Who knew. But the exerting 3/1 isn't the only *Amonkhet* card making its presence felt in Modern. And it's only a matter of time before [Slither Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slither+Blade) gets added to that list as well.


1. [Modern Jund Deck Guide](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/modern-jund-deck-guide/) | Channel Fireball | Reid Duke

Grand Prix Las Vegas isn't *that* far away, and Reid Duke *did* just take this deck to a Top 4 at a StarCityGames.com Open. And he *is* [one of the best teaching writers](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/level-one/level-one-full-course-2015-10-05) in *Magic*. And I *am* using all of these italics to emphasize things. How can you say no to any of that? *How*?


1. [Blue-Black Control](http://manadeprived.com/ub-control-semulin/) | Mana Deprived | Semulin

Back to Standard. Looking for something different? Shaheen Soorani piloted this deck at [Grand Prix Montreal](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpmon17) a few weeks back, and Semulin takes it for a spin and explains his thinking along the way.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Four-Color Planeswalkers](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2017-06-01)

You know what the most powerful part of the [Aetherworks Marvel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aetherworks+Marvel) deck is? The fact that the rest of the deck surrounding it—the Temur Energy shell—is so good it's basically a deck on its own.


*Magic Online* player SpringRoll85 took that shell—the [Rogue Refiner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rogue+Refiner)s, the [Whirler Virtuoso](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirler+Virtuoso)s, [Attune with Aether](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Attune+with+Aether), and more—and added Oaths and a slew of planeswalkers and calmly went 5-0 in a Competitive Standard League.


Actually, I have no idea if they were calm or not, but they were certainly 5-0.
