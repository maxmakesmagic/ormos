The Final Five: Black!
======================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on July 9, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












Hello everyone, and welcome to Building on a Budget, the column dedicated to building decks that cost 30 tickets or less using **Magic Online**! In the past, we've usually started with a deck list and evolved it over a series of playtesting games, tweaking a card here and a card there. But I thought to myself, "Self, if you were confronted with a random pile of cards, how would you evolve *that* deck?" And from that thought, we have today's column: black!


Black, as a color in **Magic**, has many facets. One facet is dedication to the accumulation of power through any means possible. This is played out through sacrifice, self-discard, and loss of life. Black also deals in the supernatural horrific (skeletons, zombies, and the undead), reanimation (tied in to the theme of the dead), discard (again, playing on the theme of horror, except more mental than physical), and the draining of life ([Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt) / [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit) type effects that are vampiric in nature). Black also likes directly killing creatures. Whereas the domain of red is dealing direct damage, the domain of Black is outright removing creatures from the board, with cards like [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) or [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw). Direct damage in black is usually tied to the vampirism theme.


Now let's talk about rarity. Rarity does not necessarily denote level of power, which is important! Compare [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) (common) to [Urza's Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Rage) (rare). [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) is a much more efficient spell than [Urza's Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Rage): it costs two mana less and deals the same amount of damage. [Urza's Rage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Rage) has some extra perks—can't be countered, can be kickered for a lot of mana—but if you're looking to play the "objectively" better card, you'd probably go with [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt).


Thankfully, **Magic** isn't a game of purely objectively better cards; if that were the case, there would be one deck that always won, and no other decks would stand a chance. Instead, **Magic** is a game of interaction and strategy—some cards interact better amongst themselves than other cards, and some cards interact against your opponent's cards rather than interacting with your own cards.


Take a handful of creature removal spells: [Tendrils of Corruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption), [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt), [Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit), [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror), [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp), [Sudden Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death), [Eyeblight's Ending](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyeblight%27s+Ending), and [Nameless Inversion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion). Is any one of these objectively better than the others? No. Why not? Because while each of these cards can kill a creature, none of them function identically.


[Consume Spirit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit): Sorcery speed. Scales with mana invested. Can deal damage to opponents.


[Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt): Sorcery speed. Scales with [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s in play, but always costs six. Can deal damage to opponents.


[Eyeblight's Ending](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eyeblight%27s+Ending): Instant speed. Can't kill Elves (including changelings). Tribal.


[Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp): Instant speed. Can't kill creatures with toughness  3. Can kill indestructible creatures. 


[Nameless Inversion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion): Instant speed. Can't kill creatures with toughness  3. Can kill indestructible creatures. Tribal.


[Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw): Sorcery speed. Can be played at two or five mana. Can't kill black or artifact creatures. Can attack each turn if played for five.


[Sudden Death](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death): Instant speed. Can't kill creatures with toughness  4. Generally uncounterable.


[Tendrils of Corruption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Corruption): Instant speed. Scales with [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s in play, but always costs four. Can't deal damage to opponents.


[Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror): Instant speed. Costs two mana. Can't kill black or artifact creatures.


Which is better, [Nameless Inversion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion) or [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp)? [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp) will allow you to shrink a creature more easily to chump block it, if you can't kill it outright... but [Nameless Inversion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion) can pump your own creature with toughness  4 to deal an extra 3 damage, interacts with tribal cards ([Wort, Boggart Auntie](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+Boggart+Auntie) anyone?), and has two card types for [Tarmogoyf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). Though these two cards are extremely similar (both are black two-mana instants that shrink a creature's toughness by 3 for a turn), they can have wildly different uses and interactions in-game!

So let's tie together the essence of black with the essence of rarity and the interaction of convergent cards. Rare cards tend to be the biggest, splashiest effects in a set. Again, this does not mean objectively more powerful, but it does usually mean more complicated (more lines of text, more rules involvement). Rares tend to be more flavorful than commons, because often commons are doing the grunt work—be a creature with one keyword, destroy an artifact or enchantment, no frills asked. If you're stuck for a place to start a deck, usually you'll look to your rares (or uncommons, in some cases—there are a lot of build-around-me uncommons in **Magic**) more than your commons for inspiration.


The reason I bring this up? I went through all of the Extended-legal rares available on **Magic Online**, and made a list of every monocolored one that cost half a ticket or less. I left all artifacts, lands, and gold cards off these lists, but included hybrid cards, because they can be played in a monocolored deck. My goal? Start a deck with twenty-five basic lands and thirty-five completely random low-cost rares (remember, these all cost half a ticket or less, so the total cost of this deck will be 17.5 tickets at most—and many of these rares trade at three for a ticket, four for a ticket, or five for a ticket!).

So you may ask "Ben, this is a completely random deck lacking in any cohesion, and it's filled 100% with rares, aside from the basic lands! How is this either *Building* or *on a Budget?*" And my answer is this: 30 tickets or less, folks—and the above deck, we're looking at about 10-12 tickets worth of cards. Moreoever, this is Building on a Budget at probably it's purest form of all! I am taking what is essentially a highlander theme deck of 35 black budget rares, seeing what works and what doesn't, and evolving the deck from there. After the initial 35 rares? All bets are off. I can include any commons, uncommons, or rares that make the cards in the deck work, as long as they stay within the budget. But the catch is that I really want to find which cards interact well with one another, and focus the deck on these cards! Will [Nihilistic Glee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihilistic+Glee) feed [Phyrexian Delver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Delver)? Will I ever get threshold on [Repentant Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repentant+Vampire)? Is there anything that can bounce permanents for [Warped Devotion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warped+Devotion) (well, probably not—expect this to get cut)? The only way to find out? Shuffle up and let er' rip!


**Game 1: Bluevictorious (Golems)**


 



![Horobi, Death's Wail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Horobi%2C+Death%27s+Wail)

I get down Chainer and [Horobi, Death's Wail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Horobi%252C%2BDeath%2527s%2BWail). Bluevictorious gets down a bunch of Golems, drops [Mycosynth Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycosynth+Golem), plays double [Brass Herald](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brass+Herald) for free, puts down four more Golems (including [Bosh, Iron Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bosh%2C+Iron+Golem)) and swings for a zillion on the following turn. If only I had a way to target creatures!

Record: 0-1


**Game 2: maisonnucleaire (Red-White Elemental Blink)**


Maisonnucleaire starts with three [Inner-Flame Acolyte](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inner-Flame+Acolyte)s in a row, and puts [Squee's Embrace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squee%27s+Embrace) on two of them. I am thrown almost immediately into chump-blocking mode, with [Western Paladin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Western+Paladin), [Strongarm Tactics](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strongarm+Tactics) (discarding [Patron of the Nezumi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Nezumi)), and [Midnight Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Ritual). I drop down [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare) to act as a more permanent blocker, and get knocked down to one life...only to draw and drop [Chainer, Dementia Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%2C+Dementia+Master). He pumps [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare) to 8/8....but since I am in a chump blocking-to-stay-alive situation, I also get [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare) removed from the game when Chainer dies! Talk about strange card interactions!


Record: 0-2


**Game 3: lemmywinks007 (White-Red Aggro)**


Lemmywinks007 starts with [Boros Swiftblade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Swiftblade), and I get down [Toshiro Umezawa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toshiro+Umezawa), which quickly dies to [Lash Out](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lash+Out). He then plays [Fists of the Anvil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fists+of+the+Anvil) on his Swiftblade and knocks me for 10 damage! I get stuck on three lands and start discarding cards, and he continues to hit me for 2 a turn. At 4 life, I get [Hollow Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+Specter), and he suspends [Arc Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Blade). I trade creatures, and he drops [Slith Ascendant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slith+Ascendant). I can't get a fourth land still, and he attacks for 1, plays [Relentless Assault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relentless+Assault), and attacks for 2, and I concede.


Record: 0-3


In a deck with a lot of high-cost black cards, I'm finding that 25 lands just aren't enough. For my first cut, I simply take out five cards that don't have much use in this card pool and add in five [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s. I'd rather get land flooded (especially since I have cards that can take advantage of extra lands, such as [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare)) than not be able to play any spells at all.


**Out:** [Death Pits of Rath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Pits+of+Rath), [Mortal Combat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortal+Combat), [Sins of the Past](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sins+of+the+Past), [Strongarm Tactics](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strongarm+Tactics), [Warped Devotion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warped+Devotion)  
**In:** 5x [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)

**Game 4: dragonbear ([Wildfire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wildfire))**


Dragonbear casts double [Riftwing Cloudskate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate), taking my [Lethal Vapors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lethal+Vapors) and my [Murderous Betrayal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Betrayal) off the board with each one. Dragonbear also casts multiple [Compulsive Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research)es and [Sleight of Hand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleight+of+Hand)s, putting [Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm)s in the graveyard, allowing him to play a 7/7 [Magnivore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magnivore). I use [Murderous Betrayal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Betrayal) multiple times until I am at 1 life, at which point he plays [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) to kill me.


Record: 0-4


**Game 5: dragonnw (Elf Warriors)**


I get [Toshiro Umezawa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toshiro+Umezawa), and follow him with [Lethal Vapors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lethal+Vapors) (which gets hit by [Spring Cleaning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spring+Cleaning)), [Hollow Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+Specter), and [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare). Dragonnw drops [Obsidian Battle-Axe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obsidian+Battle-Axe) and [Cloudcrown Oak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudcrown+Oak), but my 6/6 is larger than his 5/5. He then drops [Ambush Commander](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Commander), and I drop [Cemetery Puca](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Puca). I swing in with both of my flyers, make him sacrifice a [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) to let his Oak kill my [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare), but turn my Puca into another [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare), followed by [Sengir Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Vampire). This leaves him at 8 life—and on my next turn, I draw a [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), and drop [Patron of the Nezumi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patron+of+the+Nezumi). This lets me attack with everything, and force him to die through both flying damage, and damage from his guys blocking.


Record: 1-4


 



![Cemetery Puca](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cemetery+Puca)

All right, that's my first win! So far, my deck still has a lot of big, clunky creatures, but some of the flyers are quite decent—just a lot to play. I make my next cut of rares that aren't exactly synergistic in this deck and add in four [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror)s and a [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt) as removal.

**Out:** [Empty the Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Catacombs), [Endemic Plague](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Endemic+Plague), [Last Laugh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Laugh), [Lethal Vapors](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lethal+Vapors), [Nihilistic Glee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nihilistic+Glee)  
**In:** 4 [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror), 1 [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt)


**Game 6: QuantumFoam278 (White-Green-Blue Aggro)**


I draw fifteen [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s, [Terror](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror), [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt), and [Midnight Banshee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Banshee). QuantumFoam278 kills [Midnight Banshee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Banshee) with [Crib Swap](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crib+Swap), plays [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), blinks it twice (drawing six extra cards), puts down [Lightning Greaves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Greaves), and starts pumping it with [Immaculate Magistrate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immaculate+Magistrate). I play face-down and then flip up [Silent Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter) right in time, killing [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) as a 3/3 just in time. He plays [Windbrisk Heights](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Windbrisk+Heights), but I start knocking cards out of his hand two at a time. Finally, he plays [Austere Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Austere+Command) and wipes the board clear.


I drop [Colfenor's Plans](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colfenor%27s+Plans), removing [Sengir Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Vampire), [Hollow Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+Specter), [Toshiro Umezawa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toshiro+Umezawa) and more [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)s from the game. He plays three quick creatures, and then flips over [Mirror Entity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirror+Entity) with the Heights, killing me in two attacks.


Record: 1-5


**Game 7: ApparitionKG (Legendary Cards!)**


I get [Stronghold Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin), [Toshiro Umezawa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Toshiro+Umezawa), and [Cemetery Puca](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Puca), and he gets [Captain Sisay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+Sisay). I attack with [Stronghold Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin), and then sacrifice it to make [Cemetery Puca](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Puca) into [Captain Sisay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Captain+Sisay), which would allow me to get some of the legendary creatures from my deck! However, he uses [Lightning Helix](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Helix) to kill my Puca / Sisay, and then drops Honden of the Infinite Rage. I drop [Silent Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter) onto the board and start taking out his hand, then follow it with [Wound Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wound+Reflection), so I can deal 8 damage a turn. The game is over shortly thereafter, despite a [Kodama of the North Tree](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama+of+the+North+Tree) on ApparitionKG's side.


Record: 2-5


Time for another round of cuts. [Silent Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter) has been an absolute beating, and [Stronghold Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin) has been a great source of cheap, reusable removal for the deck. I take out some of the Rares in favor of three Assassins and two Specters.


**Out:** [Gravespawn Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravespawn+Sovereign), [Necroplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necroplasm), [Colfenor's Plans](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Colfenor%27s+Plans), [Polluted Bonds](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Polluted+Bonds), [Murderous Betrayal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Betrayal), [Death Match](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Match), [Midnight Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Ritual)  
**In:** 3 [Stronghold Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin), 2 [Silent Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter)

**Game 8: Maestro11234 (Black-Green Husk)**


 



![Wound Reflection](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wound+Reflection)

I get an early start with [Cemetery Puca](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Puca), but it dies to [Cruel Edict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Edict). On my next turn, I [Persecute](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Persecute) naming black, getting [Sisters of Stone Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Sisters%2Bof%2BStone%2BDeath) of the Stone Death, and two other black-green creatures. He drops [Penumbra Bobcat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Penumbra+Bobcat) and [Nantuko Husk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk), and I get [Silent Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Specter), [Stronghold Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin), and another Puca. My Specter knocks out several five-drops from his hand while he's at four lands, and a [Repentant Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repentant+Vampire) lets my air force outrun him before he can get into the game.

Record: 3-5


**Game 9: Qizx (Mono-Black Control)**


Qizx gets stuck at three mana, but puts up a fight with [Last Gasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Gasp), Darkness, and [Sudden Spoiling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Spoiling). However, no mana is as no mana does, so though I lose my [Cemetery Puca](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cemetery+Puca), I do get [Sengir Vampire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sengir+Vampire), [Hollow Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+Specter) and [Stronghold Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stronghold+Assassin), and follow them with [Wound Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wound+Reflection) for a quick kill.


Record: 4-5


Now that I've got a little more of a curve, I can probably go back down to a more normal amount of lands. I do not want to lose my [Nightmare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare) to Chainer ever again, so Chainer comes out. [Horobi, Death's Wail](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Horobi%252C%2BDeath%2527s%2BWail) doesn't have enough friends to help him target opposing creatures, so he goes too. In come two more [Wound Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wound+Reflection)s (which is like a one-sided [Furnace of Rath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Furnace+of+Rath) for players), three [Corrupt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt)s (works well with [Wound Reflection](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wound+Reflection)!), a [Midnight Banshee](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Banshee), and two [Hollow Specter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hollow+Specter)s. It's an all-ghoulish feast!


**Out:** 4 [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp), [Gangrenous Goliath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gangrenous+Goliath), [Horobi, Death's Wail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horobi%2C+Death%27s+Wail), [Chainer, Dementia Master](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainer%2C+Dementia+Master)
