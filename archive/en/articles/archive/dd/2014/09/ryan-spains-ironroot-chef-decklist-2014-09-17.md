Ryan Spain's Ironroot Chef Decklist
===================================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on September 17, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamprosak.jpg)
By Adam Prosak




From Friday Night Magic to the Pro Tour, Adam Prosak loves all types of tournament Magic. Currently, Adam is working in R&D as a developer. 






[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Yisan,%20the%20Wanderer%20Bard)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yisan,%20the%20Wanderer%20Bard)
Would you like to sing a song?


At this past weekend's [Community Cup](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/occ14), the players were asked to build decks based on the flavor of a few cards. *Magic Online* Design Manager Ryan Spain decided to build his deck around [Yisan, the Wandering Bard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yisan%2C+the+Wandering+Bard), featuring musically inclined cards from across Standard.


Ryan got the whole ensemble together. When the [(Glaring) Spotlight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=366334) was on, the orchestra was ready. The violins were playing front and center with their [Bow (of Nylea)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=373603), but the rest of the string instruments were hidden. The [(Springleaf) Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=378534) was providing the percussion, while the [(Shipwreck) Singer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=373702) provided the melody. Of course, the [Chorus (of the Tides)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=378405) was providing ample backup. It was apparent that their performance had won the [Crowd's Favor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crowd%27s+Favor). They loved the [Soul (of Innistrad)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=383387) music and asked for an encore, but there would be none. They had sung their hearts out, and were out of [(Last) breath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=373680).
