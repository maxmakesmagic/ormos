The Gift of Devotion
====================



 Posted in [ARTICLES](/en/articles)
 on February 4, 2014 









Most people assume that Standard is "solved," but Paul Dunn begs to differ. While he uses the most powerful cards in Mono-Black Devotion ([Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Pack Rat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Rat), [Underworld Connections](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), [Hero's Downfall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero%27s+Downfall)), his overall strategy is much different.



![Gift of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Gift%20of%20Orzhova&options=)And, dare I say, better.

Mono-Black Devotion loses to Mono-Blue when it falls behind on board but cards like [Gift of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Orzhova), [Nighthowler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nighthowler), [Agent of the Fates](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Agent+of+the+Fates), and [Wring Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wring+Flesh) all help out in that department. Mono-Black mirrors end similarly and [Nighthowler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nighthowler) gives you a powerful, cheaper threat for the matchup.


Paul's deck might struggle against random decks or WU Control more than a typical Mono-Black Devotion deck, but with some fine-tuning, his Mono-Blue and Mono-Black matchups should be pretty good. I would want more sideboard cards for the difficult-looking white-blue matchup, but I'd like my spot against everything else.
