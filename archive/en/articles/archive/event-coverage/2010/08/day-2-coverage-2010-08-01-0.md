Round 10 Feature Match – Ari Lax vs. Matt Sperling
--------------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*

"I guess the train had to derail sometime. Only two of my opponents played [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) during day one…zero [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance). I came into this tournament knowing that if I played this matchup, I was just dead."


"Man, you're really setting this up for the upset."


Lax won the die roll and chose to go first. After Sperling resolved a mulligan, Lax stripped him of yet another card with a [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) hit the bin out of a hand that contained a [Predict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predict), [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), and three land.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol10/lax.jpg)*Ari Lax doesn't like letting his opponent get two permanents into play.*
On the following turn, Lax went for it. Yes, that's right, turn two. [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain) set up a combination that resulted in two [Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal)s, a [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual), and [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual) leaving him with a storm count of six, six black mana in pool, and two cards in his hand. Those last cards proved to be a [Lion's Eye Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) and a now-hellbent [Infernal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor). In response to his own Tutor, Lax cracked his [Lion's Eye Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond) for black mana, making sure he would get the mana but not have to discard the card the card he was tutoring for. His tutor fetched up a second copy of [Infernal Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Tutor), raising the storm count to nine. The last card he got was the [Tendrils of Agony](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tendrils+of+Agony), which stormed off for exactly lethal loss of life.


On turn two.


"I guess that's the one I get to steal."


Ari Lax 1 – Matt Sperling 0


This time, it was Lax's time to mulligan. His six card hand gave him a bit of disruption in the form of a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), but Sperling had a [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) to hide away the goods. All Lax got to choose from was a second [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Predict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predict), and a [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare). Just as last game, Sperling's [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) went away.


Unlike last game, this one made it to turn four. Lax only had a single land in play, and Sperling was steadily growing his mana base. With each turn that passed, Lax was going to find it harder to win. He eventually made his way up to three lands and used a pair of [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain)s and a [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) to help him set up the eventual flurry, but Sperling had managed to stick a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) by then.


With all of his digging done, Lax went for it. First came [Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal). Then [Dark Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Ritual), putting the storm count at four. After the Ritual resolved, Lax used the mana to clear the way with a [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). Sperling thought for a minute before giving the go ahead and revealing a hand of [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell), [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare), and [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). Philip Yam and his Goblins started out the day with an unblemished record after a 9-0 Day One, but had hit a few speed bumps on day two, including losing the die roll against one Eli Kassis.

"Are you considering conceding to not show me what you're playing?"


"Yeah."


Instead, Yam had a [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) for Kassis' only green source, and a [Goblin Lackey](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Lackey) to follow up.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol10/Philip%20Yam%20Round%2013.jpg)*Philip Yam*

This opened to door for Yam's fourth [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland) of the match, again on Kassis' only green source again, followed by an Æther vial. After shuffling and looking again, Martell still found no one drop, and was forced to flip the Top to keep the [Chain Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+Lightning) from resolving. This gave Hatfield the room he needed to resolve a 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).


Martell fought back with a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) of his own, but declined to replay the [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), leaving [Tundra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tundra) open for the [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) that wrecked Hatfield when he attacked with his animals the following turn. [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) ate the [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape), and then ate a Lightning Bot.


Without even checking again, [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) jumped straight from the top of Martell's library to the battlefield, and a [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) allowed [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) to stop a [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), the last card in Hatfield's hand. Martell had a second [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), and quickly took Hatfield – and his hat – to the cleaners.


Tom Martell – 1 Alix Hatfield 0


**Game 2**


Once again Hatfield led with [Taiga](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Taiga)/Ape, which was met by Martell with a [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top). The [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) that followed was [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)ed, and Hatfield tipped his hand a bit when he missed his land drops for the next two turns with no plays.


Meanwhile Martell made his Top work for him…er, TopS work for him, as he found and played a second and eventually a third one of the ubiquitous Champions of Kamigawa artifact.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol10/fm15Hatfield.jpg)*Hatfield and his hat*


Hatfield's second land allowed him to fetch a [Plateau](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plateau) for a [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) that, based on the last several missed land drops, was going to be pretty tame in the meantime. Meanwhile, the [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) continued to do what it has done since about the beginning of time: beat for two.


A fetchland off the top made the [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) scary, and Hatfield cracked the [Wooded Foothills](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wooded+Foothills) to try and grow it. Martell had the [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) (courtesy of one spun [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top)).


"So [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) gets in for a very satisfying two damage?" asked Hatfield.


The [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) did indeed get in, putting Martell to 11 while he worked his Tops. Eventually all that topping produced a [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), only to watch helplessly as it fell to [Krosan Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip).


Equally scary was Hatfield's [Choke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choke) (oh, and the [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) that sent Martell to 7 after two more [Flooded Strand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flooded+Strand) activations) while Martell had only [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s in play. [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) tried to stop it, but [Pyroblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroblast) robbed it of its glory.


However, the last Top on the table still had work to do, and blindly drew the top card to reveal…[Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)! Martells [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s would indeed be untapping.


The Top again went to work, taking out the [Kird Ape](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kird+Ape) that had been so much trouble with a found [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares). Unfortunately for Martell, a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) and a [Krosan Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip) on the last of his three Tops was enough for Hatfield to grab game two.


Tom Martell – 1 Alix Hatfield 1


**Game 3**


A turn one [Plateau](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plateau) signaled game three would be slightly different for Hatfield, who led not with an Ape, but with a Cat, as [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) looked to go Goyf sized early. A [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm) in response allowed Martell to [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) the offending kitty and follow up with a [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance).


[Pyroblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroblast) eliminated the [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance), but Hatfield again had no second land while Martell made a Tarmogoy, which pretty quickly ate a [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol10/fm15Martell.jpg)*Tom Martell knows a thing or two about fighting Zoos animals*


A second [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) was Hatfield's follow-up, and Martell held serve with his second [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance). When Hatfield went to play a third [Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx), [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) blindly revealed [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), prompting an audible sigh from Hatfield, who also cheekily attacked with his 0/1 [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx).


A forest pumped up the [Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lynx) and allowed a [Qasali Pridemage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Qasali+Pridemage) to resolve, only to die the next turn to a [Firespout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout), emptying the battlefield of creatures.


An attempted [Krosan Grip](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip) was stopped by [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) revealing [Firespout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout), and a [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare) caught one [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and a hard cast [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) stopped a second 'Goyf. That allowed Hatfield to resolve a [Sylvan Library](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Library), which, unfortunately for the hat, could not attack.


The 5/6 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) from Martell, however, certainly could. [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) (with some help from [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) and [Firespout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout)) countered [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) and [Knight of the Reliquary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary) as both players sped up their play pace to try to finish as the round neared its end. When [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) revealed another three for Hatfield's [Choke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choke), Hatfield conceded, putting Martell within shouting distance of a Top 8 appearance.


**Tom Martell – 2 Alix Hatfield 1**

Feature Match Round 16 -Ari Lax vs. Tom Martell
-----------------------------------------------


*by Nate Price*

"Step one if this matchup is to win the die roll," Ari Lax said. "Step two is having them mulligan to five."


As we learned his previous turn in the Feature Match area, Ari Lax is not very pleased at his chances of defeating Tom Martell's [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance) deck with his ANT deck. He considered himself lucky to have stolen a game from Matt Sperling earlier with a sick turn two kill.


This game, he started off ripping a [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) out of Martell's hand, leaving him with a pair of [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top)s and a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). A [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) allowed him to search through his deck. It also freed him up to use a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) to strip Martel of his freshly drawn [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance). Instead of dropping the powerful enchantment, Martell was forced to resort to a 3/4 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) in its stead. Lax merely played a land and passed the turn.


During his upkeep, Martell used his Top to arrange his draw. Unhappy with his find, Martell used a [Scalding Tarn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn) on his main phase to wash the cards away. He then plopped down a twin [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and attacked Lax to thirteen. Yet again, Lax just drew and passed. He was holding a trio of [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual)s, but nothing to do with all of that mana. Martel took advantage of Lax's apparent weakness, attacking him down to five. On his turn, Lax cracked his two fetch lands, trying desperately to thin his deck and raise his odds of hitting something, even if it was only a small amount.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol10/fm16Lax.jpg)*Lax gave new meaning to the phrase Ad Nauseam, dying to the second spell it revealed*
Those odds paid off as he drew an [Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) for his turn. It was time to begin. First came a non-threshold [Cabal Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual). It resolved. With a black in his pool, and three untapped lands, he tried another. This time, Martell stopped it. Lax just plowed on through with another. With five mana in his pool, he cast [Ad Nauseam](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ad+Nauseam) with audible wretch. The past couple of turns, as well as sacrificing his lands, left him with a mere three life to play with. The first card revealed was a [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), dropping him to two. The next must have cost two or more, because Lax scooped it under his cards so fast that I wasn't even able to see what it was.


"I did have outs," he admitted.


"LED, LED, LED, [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)?" Martell asked.


"There were a few ways it could have gone involving one casting cost cards."


Ari Lax 0 – Tom Martell 1


Lax started game two with a [Lotus Petal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Petal), which he immediately sacrificed for a [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm). Martell thought for a second or two before declaring it ok. When Lax tried to follow that with a [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), Martell Forced it. [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) stole the second [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress). With two mana available, Lax ventured a guess at Martell's play.


"[Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance)?"


"There's a chance I am playing that spell."


"Well, considering you exiled one for [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)…"


"There is a higher chance I am playing that spell."


Indeed he did, and the powerful enchantment hit play. When Lax went for a third [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress) on the following turn, Martell went to a blind [Counterbalance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterbalance).


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration//mtg/images/daily/events/gpcol10/fm16Martell.jpg)*Martell played the control deck to a T…with a little help from lady luck*
"Oh my God," Lax moaned. Sitting face up on the top of Martell's library was a [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top), countering the [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress), as well as providing an ominous glance into a bleak future for Lax. The Top hit play, along with a [Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) to keep Lax out of threshold. The nail in the coffin would be a [Vendilion Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) backed up with [Karakas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karakas) to maintain strict control over Lax's hand. After a few turns of futility, Lax decided to pack it in. The only spell he managed to land during the time after the blind Top was a [Lion's Eye Diamond](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lion%27s+Eye+Diamond).


Ari Lax 0 – Tom Martell 2
