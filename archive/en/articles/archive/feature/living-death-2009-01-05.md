Patrick Chapin played this version of Living Death at the 2000 Invitational. The deck sought to play an early [Survival of the Fittest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest). It would then filter through the creatures in the deck, stocking the graveyard with utility threat after utility threat, until the big turn. For ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif), Patrick would wipe the board clear of his opponent's creatures, then return all of his to play in one fell swoop. Living Death decks were very popular throughout the time *Tempest* was legal.
