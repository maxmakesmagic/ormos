By Invitation Only
==================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on January 3, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar











*Before the Storyteller ballot was even a twinkle in someone's eye, I was recapping my experiences at the **Magic** Invitational. Sure, it was for "What If?" Week, meaning it didn't actually happen, but writing this report was still great fun. I'm especially proud of the fact that my made-up MoK Standard format is a similar but narrower version of the Choose Your Own Standard format devised by Mark Rosewater, and that my speculative Invitational roster was fairly close to the actual roster. No one will believe me, of course, but I was this close to choosing Guillaume Wafo-Tapa as the Resident Genius, instead opting for what seemed like a safer choice. On top of all that, I got to design some sweet green fatties, and that's something that no Invitationalist has ever really done. Thanks for reading and enjoy the holidays!*

*This article originally ran February 28, 2007*

I'm just as shocked as you are. 

Never in a million years would I think that I could be playing in the **Magic** Invitational. In the past, the Writers' Pick was a slot determined *by* writers. For whatever reason, the event organizers decided that this year, the Writers' Pick would be given *to* a writer. While I qualify as a writer, I never expected to win. It was, as they say, an honour just to be nominated. Luckily for me, it seems that the fans of Jamie Wakefield, John Rizzo, JMS, and Mark Gottlieb (among others) were gone fishing the week of the voting. 



|  |  |  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |  |  |

If you said that my Pro Tour resume was slender, you would win the Understatement of the Year Award. Most of the other invitees probably have more Pro Tour Top 8s than I have kitchen table tournament Top 8s (and we usually only get 5-6 guys to show up). I'm probably not even the highest rated player in my apartment (and my roommate doesn't even play **Magic**). I might be the only person on the planet who has covered more sanctioned matches than he's played. The ratio is something like 2:1. 

But what was I gonna do? Turn down the invitation? Yeah, right. I was playing to win and/or not embarrass myself too badly.

### The Stakes

![Juntu_Stakes](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/IN/Juntu_Stakes.jpg)As you know, when you win the Invitational, you get to see your likeness on a card of your own design. Now, I'm not much of a card designer. At least, I'm not much of a "design" designer. I was playing along with the Great Designer Search until the Un-set challenge, at which point, my brain promptly exploded. I don't have the grand visions that are required to design cohesive **Magic** sets, but if you asked me to do something extraordinarily narrow, like make 100 green fatties that cost seven mana, I could probably come up with some halfway decent stuff. 

If I could've worked on any **Magic** set ever made, I would've picked *Time Spiral*. I love all the references to past cards, and just about any fantasy card that I've designed has been loaded with references to other cards. Here's a short list of my favourite things in **Magic**:

1. Creatures with comes-into-play abilities.  
 2. Green creatures.  
 3. Fetching a lot of land with cards like [Skyshroud Claim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyshroud+Claim). 

Put it all together and you get green creatures with comes-into-play abilities that get better (or finally become playable) when you have a ton of land in play. Basically, I'm going to submit a, uh, seven-mana green fatty.

I did a bit of research, and while several people have submitted green cards, they are usually things like [Survival of the Fittest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Survival+of+the+Fittest) or [Holistic Wisdom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Holistic+Wisdom) (or both) tacked on to the body of a [Grizzly Bears](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Grizzly%2BBears). From what I could gather, only two people in the history of the Invitational have submitted green fatties (say, creatures with a combined power and toughness of 10 or greater) and neither of those creatures is particularly "green." First, there was Gerardo Godinez's submission in 2000 (the year Jon Finkel won):

GG, Master of the Shadow  
 2GG  
 Creature - Beast  
 5/5  
 Flying, protection from black  
 Fading 4 (This creature comes into play with four fade counters on it. At the beginning of your upkeep, remove a fade counter from it. If you can't, sacrifice it.)

The second one was Brian Kibler's submission in 2001 (the year Kai Budde won): 

Apocalyptic Dragon-Kin  
 3RGW  
 Creature - Dragon Angel  
 4/4  
 Flying  
 When Apocalyptic Dragon-Kin comes into play, discard your hand and destroy all other permanents in play.

![Nature's Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Nature%27s+Resurgence)Most of the Invitational submissions I've seen have the phrase "draw a card" on them somewhere, often in multiple locations. I just so happen to like card-drawing, too. Especially in green. A couple cards that I've tried to use, with little success, are [Nature's Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Resurgence) and [Collective Unconscious](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collective+Unconscious). What if you turned them into a creature's comes-into-play ability?

Modal Citizen  
 4GGG  
 Creature - Elf Druid Mutant  
 When CARDNAME comes into play, choose one - Draw a card for each creature you control; or each player draws a card for each creature card in his or her graveyard.  
*It wasn't big on Jungian analysis or Gaia theory. It much preferred Jugan analysis and Child of Gaea theory.*  
 5/5

Okay, that body (or any body, really) with *either one* of those two abilities would probably be ridiculous. If I had to choose one, though, it'd be the [Nature's Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Resurgence) ability. This card wasn't exciting enough for me, despite being an Elf that will often draw you six cards. So, keeping in mind what Devin Low wrote about making good green fatties, I came up with this, a tweak on [Krakilin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krakilin) and/or [Ivy Elemental](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ivy+Elemental):

Crackling Ivy XGG  
 Creature - Plant Fungus  
 Trample  
 CARDNAME comes into play with X +1/+1 counters on it.  
 When CARDNAME comes into play, put X 1/1 green Saproling creature tokens into play.  
*"The specimen's dish seems to be broken." - [Mycologist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mycologist), last words*  
 0/0

It's like a weed that grows out of control. Not bad, but I can probably do better. One of my favourite cards of all time is [Kamahl, Fist of Krosa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kamahl%2C+Fist+of+Krosa). He's big and he can make your whole team bigger. The cool part, though, is his ability to animate lands. This allows you do fun things in concert with [Triskelion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Triskelion) or [Goblin Sharpshooter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sharpshooter), but it also gives you some measure of protection against board sweepers like [Wrath of God](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). As long as you have some green mana available, your opponent knows that a Wrath will also take out many of his lands. With that in mind, I came up with this:

Revolting Force  
 5GGG  
 Creature - Fungus Elemental  
 2G: Until end of turn, all lands become 2/2 creatures that are still lands.  
*"Ugh." - Ugg, goblin goon*  
 7/7

![Uktabi Kong](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Uktabi+Kong)Now, I like those cards. They are definitely cards I would put in my decks. But this is the Invitational. You (or, at least, I) only get one shot. Why not go for broke and tack as many abilities as possible on to a single card? Here's what you'd get if you combined three of my favourite cards:

Charming Gorilla  
 6GG  
 Creature - Giant Ape  
 When CARDNAME comes into play, if you played it from your hand, choose one - Destroy all artifacts; or creatures you control get +3/+3 and gain trample until end of turn; or put a 1/1 green Insect creature token into play for each [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) you control.  
*Bolts of lightning were no match for its peels of thunder.*  
 8/8

For reference, that's [Overrun](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overrun), [Beacon of Creation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beacon+of+Creation), and the card that I'm still hoping will become the first Un-card to make it into a "real" **Magic** set, [Uktabi Kong](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Uktabi+Kong). The question of how my ugly mug will get into the art of this card is something I will leave up to the Creative guys.

### Masques of Kamigawa

![Volrath the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Volrath+the+Fallen)Wow, the flattery continues. Several months ago, on this very site, in [this very column](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/cm27), I introduced this very format to this very planet. It was received with such widespread acclaim that Mark Rosewater chose it as one of the formats for this year's Invitational.

Basically, the format is a sort of "What ifâ€¦?" Standard, made up of less-than-beloved Masques Block (*Mercadian Masques*, *Nemesis*, and *Prophecy*), Kamigawa Block (Champions, Betrayers, and *Saviors of Kamigawa*), and the latest Core Set (in this case, *Ninth Edition*).

Instead of resting on what few laurels I had, I decided to scrap all of my previous MoK decks (even Kiki-Pirates) and start fresh. I didn't want to do something lame, like play Rebels with [Shining Shoal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shining+Shoal). Since the main focus of Kamigawa Block was "Legendary Creatures," I decided to see how this aspect of the block intersected with Masques Block.

I quickly hit on [Volrath the Fallen](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volrath+the+Fallen) as the card that I wanted to build around. As a discard outlet, you can use him alongside [Slumbering Tora](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slumbering+Tora) to set up a [Footsteps of the Goryo](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Footsteps+of+the+Goryo) or [Goryo's Vengeance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) on [Kokusho, the Evening Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kokusho%2C+the+Evening+Star) or [Iname as One](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iname+as+One). If you pitch Iname to Volrath, you'll have an 18/16 creature! Of course, you'll have a huge guy that can be chump-blocked all day long, which is why I included a pair of Shizos.

The deck seeks to combine the *Saviors of Kamigawa* "wisdom" cards (in particular, the "[Maro](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maro)" cycle) with [Howling Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Wolf), [Nesting Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nesting+Wurm), and [Ensnare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnare) from Nemesis. [Spontaneous Generation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spontaneous+Generation) is a "wisdom" card that came before its time and it works well with all the hand fillers. The deck can win out of nowhere with the combination of Masumaro and Ensnare. At the end of your opponent's turn, play [Ensnare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensnare) by returning two [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)s to your hand. This will tap all creatures and give Masumaro +4/+4. On your turn, you can play [Gush](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gush) or [Seek the Horizon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seek+the+Horizon) or [Howling Wolf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Wolf) and pump Masumaro even more. Since all of your opponent's creatures will be tapped at this point, you can swing freely, just like Vladimir Guerrero. 

### Reject Rare Draft

Green is notoriously bad in Reject Rare Draft. Compared to normal draft formats, green retains all of its weaknesses (no removal, little evasion), but has none of its usual strengths (there are very few mana-fixers or combat tricks in the rare slot). Last time I tried to draft green in this format, I ended up with a handful of [Vexing Beetle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vexing+Beetle)s and not much else. Take that, [Last Word](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Word)!

![Rysorian_Badger](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/HM/Rysorian_Badger.jpg)Borrowing a page from Zvi Mowshowitz's playbook, I decided to attempt the "green gambit" and force the colour. To let everybody know that I was planning to do this, I was outfitted with a homemade green t-shirt bearing the image of a certain Badger from Rysoria. Badgers? Badgers? We'll certainly be needing some of those.

I first-picked a [Golgari Grave-Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Grave-Troll) (What was *that* doing in there?), and it gave me something to focus on for the rest of the draft. I started picking cards that wanted a full graveyard ([Bearscape](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bearscape), [Chlorophant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chlorophant), [Nature's Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Resurgence), and the very splashable [Death or Glory](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+or+Glory)) and cards that could fill it up ([Wood Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wood+Sage), [Cephalid Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cephalid+Vandal), [False Memories](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=False+Memories), and [Animal Magnetism](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Animal+Magnetism)). Unsurprisingly, I got a late-pick [Pedantic Learning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pedantic+Learning), which goes so well with all of my self-milling cards that I decided to run it. I also picked up a [Words of Wilding](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wilding), whose ability to allow me to skip my draw will come in handy since there's a good chance that I will deck myself otherwise.

After the first two packs, I still didn't have any removal, or, for that matter, any way to deal with creatures whatsoever. Luckily, the third pack served up a [Cytoplast Manipulator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cytoplast+Manipulator) (to go with my [Spike Breeder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spike+Breeder), [Evolution Vat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Evolution+Vat), and Erithizon), a [Suncrusher](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Suncrusher) (to go with, among other things, the Vat), and a very late [Stone-Tongue Basilisk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stone-Tongue+Basilisk). I managed to snap up a few more goodies before the draft was done, including [Omnibian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Omnibian), [Anthroplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anthroplasm), [Sekki, Seasons' Guide](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sekki%2C+Seasons%27+Guide), and [Novijen Sages](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Novijen+Sages) (which is amazing with both [Chlorophant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chlorophant) and [Anthroplasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anthroplasm)). With so many good Simic cards coming in the third pack, you'd think I engineered the card pool myself. Last and probably least, I quite happily snapped up a fifteenth-pick R. Badger to round out the deck.

### Auction of the Elder Dragons

The bidding process becomes even more interesting when you consider that you will now have to fend off 15 opponents with your reduced life total. With that in mind, I opted to wait things out and took a less powerful deck at a lower price. Mihara, playing with [Vaevictis Asmadi](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vaevictis+Asmadi), played [Rite of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame), [Mana Geyser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Geyser), [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song), and [Cabal Ritual](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Ritual) before playing [Dragonstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dragonstorm) for five dragons. He fetched [Bogardan Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite), Brimstone Dragon, [Rorix Bladewing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rorix+Bladewing), [Fire Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Dragon), and [Kokusho, the Evening Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kokusho%2C+the+Evening+Star). The Hellkite domed me for 5, the [Fire Dragon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire+Dragon)[Spitting Earth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Earth)'d Kokusho, which drained me for 5 more, and the hasty dragons crashed in for the final 12 points.
