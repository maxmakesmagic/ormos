Glen Jones's Enchantress
========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 2, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 






Today's deck is from one of my favorite Constructed formats, from a time when the original Alpha dual lands were legal in the format—Extended. Powerful combo decks like Illusions-[Donate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Donate), Full English Breakfast, and [Oath of Druids](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oath+of+Druids) ran rampant during that time, but Glenn Jones, a Florida native and now a member of R&D's editing team, chose to play Enchantress.




> 
> "I played this deck (or something really close!) into my first competitive Top 8 finish. And in many ways that experience hooked me on *Magic* for life. I mostly played Mono-Green Enchantress and did poorly; the popular decks in Florida were very powerful combo decks. Before driving to Orlando for a larger event, my friend Jeremiah and I went looking through his "Enchantments" box for new ideas—I stumbled upon [Back to Basics](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Back+to+Basics). My mana accelerants let me threaten to cast a hoser as early as turn two. And with no mana and no blockers, most of my opponents couldn't fend off [Yavimaya Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Enchantress)…or anything wearing an [Ancestral Mask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask)."
> 
> 
> 



[Ancestral Mask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask) and [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) are great *Magic* cards that are mediocre on their own but very powerful in the right deck. Furthermore, creatures were *much* worse back then and creature removal was pretty scarce. Once Enchantress gets going, it becomes surprisingly difficult to deal with a 42/40 trampling [Yavimaya Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Enchantress).



[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Yavimaya+Enchantress)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Enchantress) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Ancestral+Mask)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Rancor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor)


These days, Enchantress still exists in Legacy but it's a much different deck. In Legacy, creatures rule the format and removal is played in every deck. [Yavimaya Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Yavimaya+Enchantress) and [Ancestral Mask](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Mask) are just not viable options for winning, so Legacy Enchantress is more like a slower combo deck with win conditions like [Sigil of the Empty Throne](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sigil+of+the+Empty+Throne) or [Words of Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Words+of+Wind). Most Legacy decks have just enough disruption to stop Enchantress from getting off the ground, and [Argothian Enchantress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Argothian+Enchantress) is much easier to deal with than it was fifteen years ago. The deck is still a blast to play, and I still get pretty excited whenever I see an Enchantress deck at the top of the standings.
