House of Commons Blowout Spectacular 2!!!
=========================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 10, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_chrismillar.jpg)
By Chris Millar












Welcome, all! With *Eventide* nearly upon us and *Shadowmoor* about to be swept away, I think it's about time that I revisited one of my favourite things that I've ever done on the site. The last [House of Commons Blowout Spectacular](/en/articles/archive/house-commons-blowout-spectacular-2008-01-10) generated a lot of mail from people wanting to know if (and hoping that) I would do the same thing again once *Shadowmoor* came out. Well, this is it. 


In case you missed it last time around, I took four of every *Lorwyn* common (no more, no less) and used those cards to make thirteen roughly equal decks. Once those four copies were used up, I couldn't put that card in another deck. Sorry, [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)! The goal was to find a home for every card and to make the most playable decks possible. We're looking for the greatest good for the greatest number here. It's like a cross between doing a Sudoku puzzle and [Building on a Budget](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/columnarchive&column=BuildingOnABudget).


What can you do with the decks? Well, at first I thought it would be fun to just duel or play multiplayer free-for-alls with the decks, but the folks at pdcmagic.com had an even better suggestion: auction them off and then have a tournament! It was a blast, and you can read about it [here](/en/articles/archive/auction-pauple-2008-02-07).


The first time around was very satisfying and very fun, so I had to do it again. I didn't know what I was getting into.


### *Shadowmoor* Money, *Shadowmoor* Problems


[![Juvenile_Gloomwidow](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/cardart/SHM/Juvenile_Gloomwidow.jpg)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Juvenile%2BGloomwidow)Wow, were things trickier this time around. For starters, as Monty Ashley explained in [a recent Ask Wizards](/en/articles/archive/ask-wizards-may-2008-2008-05-01), the cards in *Shadowmoor* have an average converted mana cost that is higher than *Lorwyn*'s. The same is true when you look at just the commons. Off the top of my head, I'll say that the difference is one-tenth of a mana. It doesn't sound like much, but note that you have to pay full price for all of the expensive spells in *Shadowmoor*. *Lorwyn*'s evoke Elementals had high mana costs but could be played for much less. Cards with affinity, landcyclers, and morph creatures are other ways we've had to mitigate a glut of expensive spells. *Shadowmoor* has no such luxuries. As a result, I couldn't skimp on land in any of the decks the way I could with Kithkin, Elves, and Merfolk.


The other main issue is that *Shadowmoor* has a lot of cards that require a lot of assistance to maximize. For instance, the cycle of Initiates want to be in monocoloured decks. The cycle of Duos, "hybrid-aligned" Scarecrows, and "Avatauras" (like [Shield of the Oversoul](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shield+of+the+Oversoul)) all want to be in specific two-colour, hybrid-heavy decks. At the same time, the conspire spells need creatures of the relevant colours, and what about all the -1/-1 counter shenanigans? [*Shadowmoor*'s mechanical "web"](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/daily/dl34) is giving me a headache.


Ultimately, though, I found a solution.


### Attack of the 61-Card Decks!


We'll start things off by talking about the monocoloured decks. Of course, "monocoloured" takes on a whole new meaning when we're talking about *Shadowmoor*. With the high number of spells using hybrid mana in the set, it's easy to build a deck with two or three different colours of spells but only one type of basic land to support them. The next several decks all fit this particular mould.

![Gravelgill Axeshark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Gravelgill+Axeshark)

First off, we've got a mono-black control deck boasting many of the usual weapons used by such decks. It's got some of the best black removal spells available to us ([Torture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torture) and [Gloomlance](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gloomlance)), the best discard spells ([Splitting Headache](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splitting+Headache) and [Cinderhaze Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cinderhaze+Wretch)), a pumpable "Shade" ([Loch Korrigan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loch+Korrigan)) to act as a finisher and/or chump-block inducer, and a Drain Life-style effect ([Rite of Consumption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Consumption)) which just happens to combo nicely with the "Shade." But, wait, there's more! The deck is packed to the gills with -1/-1 counter tricks, starting, appropriately enough, with [Gravelgill Axeshark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravelgill+Axeshark). Because it has persist, it won't stay dead the first time it sleeps with the fishes. Combined with [Chainbreaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainbreaker), it can rise from the grave(l) every single time it gets whacked. Besides doing its usual job of healing your withered creatures and slowly growing itself, [Chainbreaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainbreaker) also combos with [Cinderhaze Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cinderhaze+Wretch) to allow you to effectively [Mind Rot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Rot) your opponent once each turn or [Fugue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fugue) your opponent once in a while. A full set of [Fate Transfer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Transfer)s, meanwhile, can turn your persistent Axesharks into [Cultbrand Cinder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cultbrand+Cinder)s, transform your [Chainbreaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chainbreaker)s into Watchwolves (while offing a hapless X/2 creature in the process), allow you to get a [Scar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scar) and bonus discard out of your [Cinderhaze Wretch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cinderhaze+Wretch)es, and, perhaps best of all, let you to use [Torture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torture) as a sort of "bank of pain." The service charges are a real killer.

The next deck, perhaps the weakest, is basically the home of all the green cards that wouldn't fit anywhere else. It has a somewhat unfortunate 1-2-3-4-5 creature curve that can be interrupted by pump effects ([Nurturer Initiate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nurturer+Initiate), [Viridescent Wisps](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridescent+Wisps), and [Blight Sickle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blight+Sickle)), some arachnoid fine-dining, and the [Gleeful Sabotage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gleeful+Sabotage) of various Scarecrows and "Avatauras." I'm not sure this is the best place for [Blight Sickle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blight+Sickle), because forty percent of the creatures already have wither (perhaps the last deck would be a better fit). Still, it does provide a +1/+0 boost, so even if the wither is redundant, it makes your guys into better attackers. And that's about all they are going to do anyway.

Of all the Initiates, the blue one is the one that needs the most support, at least if you want to try to win the game by decking. That's a pretty tall order, but at least we've got some help with [Memory Sluice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Sluice), a miniature version of [Glimpse the Unthinkable](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimpse+the+Unthinkable). If you manage to play all four of Sluices with conspire, your opponent will be down 32 cards. Take away the seven cards in your opponent's starting hand, and that leaves 21 cards for you to mill away (assuming a 60-card deck). That's eleven activations of [Drowner Initiate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+Initiate), minus one for every two cards your opponent draws. It can be done (especially with multiple [Drowner Initiate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+Initiate)s and [Memory Sluice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memory+Sluice)s), but it will take time.

While the creatures in the deck aren't all that intimidating, as with the mono-red deck above, you do have some late-game reach once it becomes suicidal to send your creatures into the red zone. [Poison the Well](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Poison+the+Well), [Rite of Consumption](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Consumption), and [Smolder Initiate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smolder+Initiate) (all of your non-Scarecrow spells are black) help in this regard, while [Traitor's Roar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traitor%27s+Roar) allows you to use your opponents' monsters against them, judo-style.

I called this a control deck, but that's mostly because it's slow. Finally we get to the first ever House of Commons Blowout Spectacular!!! combo deck. It's not a fresh new combo by any means, but it gets the job done. The three key cards are [Morselhoarder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder), [Sinking Feeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sinking+Feeling), and [Power of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire). With both Auras on the [Morselhoarder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder), you can tap it to deal one damage to a creature or player because of [Power of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire), pay 1 to untap it with [Sinking Feeling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sinking+Feeling) (which also puts a -1/-1 counter on it), and use [Morselhoarder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morselhoarder)'s ability to remove the counter and add one mana to your mana pool. End result: You've dealt 1 damage, and you're all set to repeat the process as much as you like. That's only part of the deck, though. If you don't get the whole combo going, you can put [Power of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+of+Fire) on [Pili-Pala](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pili-Pala) or [Merrow Wavebreakers](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Wavebreakers) and use their untap abilities in conjunction with the Tim ability to mow down your opponent's creatures.

### House of Cards at U.S. Nationals


In case you missed the announcement in the latest issue of [*The Gazetteer*](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=magic/images/tournamentcenter/2008/usnats_gaz23.pdf) (301KB PDF download), I will be in Chicago attending [U.S. Nationals](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=events/nationals/us08-events) from August 1st to 3rd. Mostly I will spend my days gunslinging. What this means is that you can come up and play me (for free!) with a chance to win some exciting prizes. The roster of gunslingers is pretty awesome. R&D members Matt Place, Erik Lauer, and Kenneth Nagle will be taking part, as will Pro Tour Hall of Famer Alan Comer and future Pro Tour Hall of Famer Kenji Tsumura. The last gunslinger is a guy named Richard Garfield, the creator of *RoboRally* and *The Great Dalmuti*. I'm sure he's done some other things as well.


There are tons of other [fun public events](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=events/nationals/us08-events) taking place that weekend, including league play, multiplayer free-for-alls, and, most importantly, Elder Dragon Highlander. I will have a bunch of EDH decks with me, so if you're looking to have your dreams crushed by [Hanna, Ship's Navigator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hanna%2C+Ship%27s+Navigator) (or crush my dreams), feel free to join in.


I'm not sure what decks I will be playing at the gunslinging tables, so I thought I'd let you have a say. I've selected five fun-looking, borderline tournament-playable cards for you to choose from. I will be writing a report of some kind when I return, so even if you can't make it to Chicago, your vote will still help determine what you get to read about in future articles. Feel free to post ideas in the forums or drop me a line via the email link at the bottom of the page.
