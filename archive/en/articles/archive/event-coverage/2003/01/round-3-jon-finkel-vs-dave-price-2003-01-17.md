Round 3: Jon Finkel vs. Dave Price
==================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 17, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a932.jpg)Read about their draft [here](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/draft-1-finkel-williams-and-price-2003-01-17).

Pro Tour Los Angeles Champion Dave Price faced down his real life gaming buddy and former uber-player Jon Finkel. Both are extremely popular players who have numerous invitational visits to their names.

"I'm shuffling Jon Finkel's deck—I don't trust this guy!" quipped Dave Price.  
 "I'm shuffling your deck too. I don't trust you," replied Jon Finkel.

### Game 1

Dave drew his hand. "Mulligan? Mulligan? Mulligan?" asked Johnny Magic, getting all in Price's face. Price kept, and started playing Sligh as usual with a turn two [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). He continued with a morph creature ([Blistering Firecat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blistering+Firecat) perhaps?), to which Finkel responded by cycling [Krosan Tusker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Tusker) to grab a much needed [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) to go with his three [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s. 

Price threw his guys into the breach, as Finkel cycled a second Tusker to grab another [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain). "Look at all this card advantage I'm getting", he muttered. As he untapped and fiddled with his lands, Price stared off into space, at the spectators, and seemly singularly bored in waiting for Jon to make his move. When it was a morph creature, Dave finally mosied his hand slowly across the table, and lethargically picked up his hand. After a couple of frowns and grimaces, he drew his card for the turn. 

With [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) and his morph on the table, Price attempted to shoot Finkel's creature for one. Jon responded by casting [Chain of Plasma](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+of+Plasma), which would kill the tim and keep it from dealing damage on resolution. Dave thought long and hard, and finally made the play of [Sunfire Balm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunfire+Balm) to save his guy, and followed with [Glory Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory+Seeker).

Jon untapped, and shipped his creature across the table. Dave declined to block, and Finkel tapped six mana to reveal [Snapping Thragg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Thragg), the mega-[Skirk Commando](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirk+Commando). Not wanting to keep things a mystery, he chose to gig the last face down creature on the table, which turned out to be [Daru Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Healer).

Dave nodded at this turn of events, untapped, and came across for another three to drop Finkel to eleven life. [Solar Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solar+Blast) took care of the Thragg, though Finkel had seven mana and [Venomspout Brackus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venomspout+Brackus) lying in wait. This creature met with [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism), and the offensively oriented [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) and [Glory Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory+Seeker) were joined by [Battering Craghorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battering+Craghorn). Finally, Johnny Magic mounted a serious defense with [Symbiotic Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Symbiotic+Wurm) and [Wirewood Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Elf). On the next turn, the Craghorn plus the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) traded on a double block with the Wurm and Elf, allowing Dave to play a rather large [Daunting Defender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daunting+Defender).

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a933.jpg)On the next turn, Jon smashed the cleric with [Pinpoint Avalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pinpoint+Avalanche) and went on the O with all five of his one power weenies. [Glory Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory+Seeker) took one out, but Jon quickly played a morph guy after combat. 

Price coaxed a trade of [Glory Seeker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glory+Seeker) for face-down [Treespring Lorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treespring+Lorian) on the following turn, but then emptied his hand to play one last morph guy. Jon stood at three cards, and used one to cast a second [Wirewood Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Elf). None of his guys blocked Dave's creature, so Price tapped the requisite four mana to turn the 2/2 into a ¾ first striking [Daru Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Lancer).  
[Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) took out an insect token, and Dave looked to be in a good position with the life totals at nine to five in his favor.

It was not to last though, as Jon drew [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize), bringing the forgotten Brackus into the fray. He swung with seven power worth of creatures, and then sent Price deep into the negatives with [Searing Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Flesh).

Jon Finkel 1 – Dave Price 0

### Game 2

Dave went into full Sligh mode again with both a first and second turn [Goblin Taskmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Taskmaster). Finkel slacked none, casting a second turn mana elf. The Wirewood traded for two mana and a Taskmaster, allowing Jon a breather. This gave him the leg up on the morph game, as he safely played a morph guy the following turn. It too blocked a Taskmaster, so the board cleared of all non-land permanents while the graveyard filled with [Charging Slateback](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Charging+Slateback)s and [Goblin Taskmaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Taskmaster)s

Dave started the game over with [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber), which died quickly to [Chain of Plasma](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chain+of+Plasma). "Ship it back?" asked Jon, Dave choosing not to dignify the question with an answer. Finally, Finkel started asking questions of his own, playing [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage), [Snapping Thragg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapping+Thragg) and [Pinpoint Avalanche](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pinpoint+Avalanche) (on a freshly summoned [Gustcloak Savior](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Savior)) on consecutive turns. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/ptchi03/a934.jpg)The King of Beatdown scrambled to steady his board against the Thragg with [Daunting Defender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daunting+Defender), which seemed to work for a turn as Finkel simply cycled [Krosan Tusker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Tusker) to grab a second [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) to go with his first and his [Grand Coliseum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Coliseum). This gave him enough mana to play a face down dude, and once again Dave emptied his hand, this time to cast [Aven Soulgazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Soulgazer) and to peek at Jon's face down creature.

"[Roar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Roar)!" yelled Jon, revealing [Krosan Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Colossus), with only six mana on the board. "That takes eight to morph, right?" "Yeah". Dave sent his Defender across the table, and Jon doubled it up with his Thragg (which died) and his not-quite-sneaky Colossus (which did not die). The Snapper was quickly replaced with [Venomspout Brackus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venomspout+Brackus), netting Jon another card and a 5/5 body. 

Dave debated sending his Soulgazer into the breach, and every time he would put his hand on the flyer, Jon would laugh in glee at Dave's predicament. With the life totals at fifteen even, Dave didn't seem to be in a great position. All three of Finkel's guys went ballistic the next turn , the Brackus a target of Commando raid after no blockers to kill the Soulgazer. Even though Dave managed to first strike the [Wirewood Savage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage) with a newly cast [Daru Lancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Lancer), Finkel had [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) for a top-decked [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism), and kept his [Searing Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Flesh) face up on the table.

"You...have..beaten...me", Dave spoke in robotic tones.

**Final Result:** Jon Finkel 2 – Dave Price 0.
