After That, Read, Watch, or Listen to These
===========================================


1. [Modern White-Blue Lark](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13359&writer=Ali+Aintrazi&articledate=6-28-2016) | TCGPlayer | Ali Aintrazi

I'll admit, there were other deserving articles for this slot. Alex Ullman has a great look at the new [Pauper metagame on Gathering Magic](http://www.gatheringmagic.com/alexullman-06282016-the-eternal-drake/). Ross Merriam [grades *Shadows over Innistrad* on StarCityGames.com](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33172_A-Critique-Of-Shadows-Over-Innistrad-Standard.html). And SaffronOlive [explores the Possibility Storm, um, possibilities, on MTGGoldfish](http://www.mtggoldfish.com/articles/budget-magic-94-20-tix-modern-possibility-storm).


But [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter).


[Reveillark](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reveillark) decks were some of the most powerful Standard decks in their day, competing directly with (and often losing to) the Faerie decks that saw [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) and [Ancestral Vision](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Vision) banned for a time in Modern. So why not explore 'Lark in a Modern metagame that seems wide open? It's a value deck with a combo finish that gets to play a lot of the best control cards in the format. Color me interested.


1. [MtA Episode 179: A Melding of Minds](https://www.patreon.com/posts/5852230) | Magic the Amateuring | Maria Bartholdi and Meghan Wolf

Deck of the Day
===============


1. Pauper Elves

Did I miss a memo somewhere? Did the meeting of the great Pauper minds forget to include me on their mailing list? I've been playing Elves in Pauper for quite a while (it's one of three decks I have built), and suddenly they're popping up all over the place. Pauper Constructed League churned out no fewer than four lists at 5-0 in [yesterday's decklist update](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/pauper-constructed-league-2016-06-27). Is [Elvish Vanguard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Vanguard) the reason for the resurgence? Or some other metagame factor? Or is it just a coincidence that we happened to catch a bunch of these on the same day?


For those who don't know, the deck is almost straightforward. Almost. Nominally, you play a bunch of Elves and they make each other stronger. But then there's [Distant Melody](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distant+Melody). By splashing blue, the deck can play this spell that can completely restock your hand for just four mana. And with all the mana Elves the deck plays, you can also get away with an absurdly low land count.


The list below is from JudahBenHur on *Magic Online*—only because I like the singleton [Viridian Longbow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow)—but all of the lists from that 5-0 update seem like solid bets.
