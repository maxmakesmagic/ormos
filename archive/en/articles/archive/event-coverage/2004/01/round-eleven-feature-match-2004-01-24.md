Round Eleven Feature Match:
===========================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 24, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_TobyWachter.jpg)
By Toby Wachter











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpoka04/r11fm2ja1.jpg)Masami Ibamoto had some experience on the Pro Tour, but left competitive Magic to work for Hobby Japan. He quit, and waited out the six months before he could play in sanctioned events again. He's back, and in a big way, making waves in this Grand Prix with his Loop Junktion infinite life combo deck. 

### Game 1

Ibamoto started off with [Nomads en-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nomads+en-Kor) and [Daru Spiritualist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Spiritualist), one card away from the infinite life combo. Mihara knew this, and stayed back with counterspell mana. He allowed Ibamoto's [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) to resolve, and was very surprised to see him fetch [Starlit Sanctum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Starlit+Sanctum), which gave Ibamoto an uncounterable final combo piece. He gained infinite life, and Mihara started counting cards to see if he could win via decking.

Mihara- "GG"  
 Ibamoto- "GG!"  
 Me- "Even I can understand that."

Mihara- 0 Ibamoto- 1

### Game 2

Mihara started this game far better, opening with a first turn [Isochron Scepter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isochron+Scepter) with [Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire)/[Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice) thanks to [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox). A few turns later, a second Scepter with [Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire)/[Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice) came out, crippling Ibamoto's mana while Mihara gained some insane card advantage. Ibamoto used Eldamri's [Call](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call) to get [Daru Spiritualist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Spiritualist), a combo piece that wouldn't die to [Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire). Once he drew enough land to get past the [Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice) Scepters, the Spiritualist came out along with [Shaman en-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaman+en-Kor). Mihara wasn't in any real danger, however, as he cast [Upheaval](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Upheaval) and brought out [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) with the help of two [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox)en. 

Mihara- 1 Ibamoto- 1

### Game 3

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpoka04/r11fm2ja2.jpg)Mihara played a first turn [Powder Keg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg) with the help of [Chrome Mox](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chrome+Mox), and Ibamoto absolutely annihilated the early board with [Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serenity). It destroyed the Keg and Mox on the following upkeep, and [Task Force](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Task+Force) was [Counterspell](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell)ed. [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) searched up a Sanctum, and Ibamoto summoned [Shaman en-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaman+en-Kor). Mihara dug into his library for cards with a pair of [Accumulated Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge), and Ibamoto cast [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish), getting a [Daru Spiritualist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daru+Spiritualist) that was [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed. Mihara now played [Dust Bowl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Bowl), which could take care of the Sanctum combo piece. The en-Kor attacked, and Ibamoto played [Academy Rector](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Academy+Rector). Mihara cast [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction) on end step, and Ibamoto made the piles [Bloodstained Mire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodstained+Mire), [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) and [Accumulated Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge), [Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire)/[Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice)- Mihara took the two card pile. He then untapped, and used [Dust Bowl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Bowl) to destroy the Sanctum. [Living Wish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+Wish) searched up [Wasteland](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland), which destroyed the [Dust Bowl](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dust+Bowl) that would have eventually destroyed all of Ibamoto's land. The Rector and Shaman continued to attack, and Ibamoto played a second Rector followed by a Sanctum. [Accumulated Knowledge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge) put Mihara ahead on cards, as did another [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction). The piles were [Fact or Fiction](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fact+or+Fiction), two land and [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), [Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire)/[Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice)- Mihara took the [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) pile. He played a [Powder Keg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg) to eventually kill the Shaman, and brought out the 'Tog. Ibamoto attacked with his 1/2 team, bringing Mihara down to four. Interestingly, he played a second [Shaman en-Kor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaman+en-Kor) with the [Powder Keg](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg) out, and when it got a second counter on Mihara's upkeep, both died. [Ice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice) tapped down [Kor Haven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Haven), and the [Psychatog](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) came through for lethal damage. 

Mihara- 2 Ibamoto- 1
