The Highlight Reel
==================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on April 21, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






So... [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) count.


At the time of this writing I had access to deck lists from nine of the twenty-one National Qualifier Top 8s, but we are tracking at 168 of a possible 288 Jaces. So the best card in the format is still present in more than half the decks in the [National Qualifiers Top 8s](/en/events/coverage/2011-national-qualifiers-top-8-standard-deck-lists), but quite a bit shy of the 32/32 that we saw in the Top 8 of the recent [Grand Prix Dallas-Fort Worth](/en/events/coverage/shiels-stands-alone-dallas).


Of the Top 8s that I *do* (again, at the time of this writing) have access to, this is how the National Qualifiers various shook out:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td139_chart.jpg)  
Looking at these results, my opinion is that the conversation is a bit off-center. I don't really think it's a question about [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) so much as it should be a conversation about Caw-Blade.


Caw-Blade is a better-performing deck in Standard than... well... almost any deck that has ever operated at scale. It's just a wonderful deck that great players use to great success. I think that is a big difference between the current Standard (which many great players think is a great format, and I agree) and the more draw-dependent predecessors that hovered around [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom) or [Bloodbraid Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodbraid+Elf).


Caw-Blade is a highly interactive, skill-intensive deck that consistently rewards great players. Case in point, Joshua Ravitz easily cruised to a 6-0 finish in New York this weekend past, before double-drawing into his US Nationals invitation. Josh has played [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) five times now. Of those five, he made Top 8 of consecutive StarCityGames.com Open Series events, won a PTQ (albeit in Extended), and now went undefeated at his Nationals Qualifier. So he didn't Top 8 the Grand Prix... You can't win 'em all!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td139_hawk.jpg)  
If it were *really* about [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), wouldn't we have had some greater indication before *Mirrodin Besieged*?


Previously the conversation was all around [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan); at the beginning of the season, we saw Mono-Green Eldrazi and Red-Green [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) decks as dominant, and there was chatter (albeit misguided, we can probably all agree) about [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle) being an all-time boogeyman a la Faeries and Jund. [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Valakut%252C%2Bthe%2BMolten%2BPinnacle), however seemingly effective, was just a deck. A good deck maybe, but not a boogeyman. Caw-Blade is something else... It's not a boogeyman because it isn't soul-crushing or monotonous. Caw-Blade doesn't cast the trademark "The Fix"-like shadow that has made players from the Deadguy Red to Fires of Yavimaya to, of course, Faeries and Jund eras hate playing against the Deck to Beat. Caw-Blade is simply a statistical outlier; a truly great deck.

It benefits from having not only the best card in the format—[Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)—but also an intersection of the *two* best creatures in the format, [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) and [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk), all under the same roof. [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) was available during the Caw-Go era (but no one was complaining about the deck back then); it was the appearance of [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) that elevated [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)'s game (and gave [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) the path to realize its place in the Standard pantheon) that granted this deck in particular the seemingly unassailable title of best of breed.


We have been talking about Caw-Blade seemingly since its first appearance at [Pro Tour Paris](/en/events/coverage/ptparis11) (where it, of course, won in the hands of Ben Stark)... yet the deck continues to evolve, improve, and reward highly skilled pilots.

Josh's deck is reasonably representative of the modern Caw-Blade.


Most players have taken their cues from primarily Joshua Utter-Leyton's Grand Prix build.


So look for these decks to run one or even more likely *two* copies of [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) as a Sword-carrier... You will also often see one main-deck [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan), which as a Sword-bearer is big enough to beat up most everyone, plus can buy back many lost resources (Swords, Mystics, even [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren)).




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Inkmoth Nexus | Sun Titan |

You are going to see a [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod)*somewhere*... Typically main deck, but at least in the sideboard, as players like Gavin Verhey in Washington chose to go. The wholesale adoption of [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) in the environment has essentially extincted the Red- and Black-splashing Caw-Blade variants. Of the decks I looked at, there was but one copy of Angry Birds, and no DarkBlade decks at all.


As I said before, I think [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)'s position in this deck (and by extension the metagame) is a bit exaggerated; don't confuse that for Jace not being the best card in the format, because he is... He just isn't the be-all and end-all. Case in point, in Josh's win-and-in round, he played his umpteenth Caw-Blade mirror. In the deciding game, his opponent controlled [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) essentially from turn four through the rest of the game, ultimately out-drawing Josh three Jaces to none.


To an observer, it never looked like Josh was out of the driver's seat. Regardless of what Jace was trying to do, Josh could advance his board with [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s, keep his opponent's mana tapped, and move around [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod)s and Swords to prevent profitable defensive blocking. I found this refreshing as the conventional wisdom says that in these kinds of mirrors, the player with Jace is advantaged... but at least in this one game, it didn't seem that way.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Mortarpod | Sword of Feast and Famine |

Ravitz later pointed out that at that point he had played close to 40 tournament rounds with essentially the same deck list (if you include his Extended PTQ win, where skills still transfer), and that familiarity with the deck lends an edge in the mirror. Caw-Blade is a deck that rewards practice, as unlike the [Bitterblossom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bitterblossom)s and [Bloodbraid Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Bloodbraid%2BElf)-into-[Blightning](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightning)s of the past, its powerhouse card "god draws" actually give you more and more decisions to make, rather than *just* more card advantage.


For example, what Equipment you get with a second-turn [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) will color your entire game... yet [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) is often the right answer. That said, what you *do* with this [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) gives you a mana dump for the rest of the game, as well as a not-insignificant point of toughness to play around with.


Previously the red removal, viz. [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage), was useful in sniping down [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s, but [Mortarpod](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortarpod) allows a Caw-Blade deck to take down the birds in response to the opponent's equipping [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine). This functionality has given straight White-Blue Caw-Blade the new lease on life... which it expresses with more consistent mana and access to four copies of [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge).


For a minority (or at least now-minority) take on the same, consider a red-white-blue version of Caw-Blade:

When this style of deck came out, it was considered the answer to the newly anointed best deck in the format.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td139_cunning.jpg)  
The red version retains most of the non-[Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge) functionality of Caw-Blade, and adds more efficient removal. Because of the presence of [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage) and [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan), Angry Birds will usually opt for [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) as the secondary (that is, non-[Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine)) piece of Equipment. [Basilisk Collar](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Basilisk+Collar) can gain life like a [Sylvok Lifestaff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Lifestaff), but essentially makes [Cunning Sparkmage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cunning+Sparkmage) into an even better [Royal Assassin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin), and transforms [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) into something else entirely. Something awesome!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td139_2Cards1.jpg)  
Without leaving white and blue we have a variety of different strategies we can explore. Luke's deck has a bunch of creatures that do interesting things when they hit the battlefield. [Sea Gate Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) and [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) help regulate your draws and hit your land drops. [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) works nicely with basically everything in the deck (including [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren), which is arguably a superior role player than [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) in this deck... at least when you consider the presence of the Titan), and can make for relentless disruption alongside [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge).




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Sea Gate Oracle | Wall of Omens |

The real gem, though, is [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp).


![Contagion Clasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Contagion+Clasp)
  
The Clasp makes any and all [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet)s infinite and has significant synergy with all the deck's many planeswalkers. You can go Ultimate with [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) while [Brainstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm)ing every turn, or similarly buoy Gideon or Venser (especially when the former is aggressively and defensively sticking his neck out). Venser the Sojourner has a special place in a deck with so many counters... You can reset a permanent on zero (for example, a spent [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet)), returning it to functionality.

Kind of in the neighborhood of White-Blue Control is Max Goldstein's Bant-colored control deck. This deck wants to win turn nine or later in all likelihood (what, with those 5/5 white and blue flyers), but can still get the jump on the opponent with [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra). Turn-two [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) into [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) can deposit quite the [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) the next turn!




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Lotus Cobra | Baneslayer Angel |

Max's deck is like a Bant-colored answer to Red-Green-Blue: tons of planeswalkers and powerful finishers, with [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) and [Explore](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Explore) as the two drops that get him there. [Baneslayer Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) on five is kind of like the Bant Control's [Precursor Golem](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precursor+Golem), with [Sphinx of Jwar Isle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+Jwar+Isle) playing the role of [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan).


[Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura), and of course [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) are so good that you can build all different details around a solid framework of powerful cards and end up in a number of interesting places.

Hall ran the Green-Blue [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) deck, largely as described in my friend Brian David-Marshall's [column](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/twtw/133) after the StarCityGames.com Open Series event in Edison, New Jersey.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td139_wave.jpg)  
[Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) is arguably the most powerful deck in the metagame, kind of like the next level evolution of Mono-Green Eldrazi... Sorta that deck, if it had [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor).


Green-Blue can play an overpowering game that can come as quickly as a turn-three [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) (first-turn [Joraga Treespeaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Joraga+Treespeaker), second-turn [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), third-turn [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan)), which can then set up a turn-four [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) ([Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) gets [Halimar Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths) and another land, probably [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs) for [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra)). [Halimar Depths](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Halimar+Depths) helps you set up your [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) + land drop... I'm sure you see where this is going.




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Genesis Wave | Joraga Treespeaker |

The deck is entirely optimized around six-mana permanents or cheaper. The only non-permanent card in the deck is [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave) itself. Everything else hits the battlefield off a Wave, with a Wave for six or more fearing only multiple copies of Jace or too many [Genesis Wave](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Genesis+Wave)s in the top six (or however many more than six).


[Wall of Tanglecord](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Tanglecord) and [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) are the deck's one-two punch against [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine), with the underappreciated 0/6 a truly annoying answer to an equipped [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk).




|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Wall of Tanglecord | Tumble Magnet |

In addition to the power game, the green-blue deck has a superb tempo game. As Lukasz played it, the deck has main-deck [Spreading Seas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spreading+Seas) to slow down mana development, which can be supplemented via [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) (often on turn three after sideboarding), [Frost Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Titan) keeping mana tapped, or [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), which can go and get two copies of [Tectonic Edge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tectonic+Edge). The mana control aspect keeps Green-Blue Wave multi-dimensional, and just gives it (with [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime)) even more game against [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine).

Sukenik is an up-and-coming eighteen-year-old from New Jersey. He has played in one TCGPlayer.com event and three StarCityGames.com Open Series, and made Top 8 of all four; as Watchwolf92 on **Magic Online**, Jonathan is currently in 17th place in the Player of the Year race, but for our purposes, he may have a key to unlocking this metagame.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td139_tez.jpg)  
The Grixis Tezzeret deck will be sending Jonathan to US Nationals via wins over Caw-Blade and RUG and other decks, for a 6-0-2 finish.


"The deck has a lot of game and synergies that involve a lot of practice. For example, you really want to have a [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) in play on turn two. The reason for this is [Koth of the Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer). Many people questioned Koth, but let's just say that it kills both Jaces, indirectly gains life against aggro, and if you ever get an emblem, you're in good shape. You want to use your [Scalding Tarn](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scalding+Tarn)s to get [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s as often as possible.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td139_3Cards2.jpg)  
"Another sleeper in the deck is [Contagion Clasp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp), with all of the Planeswalkers. I beat Black-Red Vampires in Game 3 by Jace-ing into Tezzeret, playing it, proliferating, and draining him for 10... Only to play another Tezzeret next turn, and doing the same thing to take the match.


"Another example of an interesting play was against RDW. I knew my opponent had [Searing Blaze](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Searing+Blaze), and was behind 7-16 (with 4 damage coming from my Koth). I only saw one course of action due to his [Arid Mesa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arid+Mesa) in play. I animated his [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s with Koth every turn until I could Ultimate my Koth and kill him with my [Mountain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain)s, pinging for 1. I skillfully ended that game at 3."


![Searing Blaze](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Searing+Blaze)
  
The message is actually pretty interesting here. Jonathan subtly tells us why some players—armed with planeswalkers various, if most obviously [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)—are consistently doing well in this format: These powerful cards give them options, and most specifically, options that they can take without spending incremental mana. The Tezzeret kill over Vampires might not be obvious, but it's at least straightforward... but Koth animating the opponent's lands to get to the emblem? Pretty clever!


In the spirit of [Koth of the Hammer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer), let's close with a deck by Tommy Luke in Colorado... Which didn't have any kind of Jace at all!
