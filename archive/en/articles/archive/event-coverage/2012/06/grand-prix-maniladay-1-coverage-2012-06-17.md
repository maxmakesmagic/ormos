--------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
Benson Cohun won 3 byes last night, taking down a Grand Prix Trial. Who was his first opponent of the weekend? Only one of the End Bosses of **Magic**, Shuhei Nakamura. Cohun shook his head in disbelief. "This is my 'Welcome to the GP'?"


### Game 1


Cohun blasted out of the gates with a trio of [Sphere of the Suns](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphere+of+the+Suns). Nakamura used a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to accelerate into a [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) and a [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger). An [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) from Cohun killed the [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), the Splicer, and pinged Nakamura. Nakamura had no further action, playing a [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) and passing.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R4_Nakamura_Cohun.jpg)

*Shuhei Nakamura and Benson Cohun*
A [Whipflare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare) from Cohun was met with a [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), Nakamura eking extra value out of his Ranger. Cohun kept up the pressure with a [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), while Nakamura tried for some defence in a [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist). After a turn or two of getting pumelled, Nakamura gave up and headed for his sideboard.


Benson Cohun 1 â€“ Shuhei Nakamura 0


### Game 2


Nakamura's early [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) was met with a [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) from Cohun. Nakamura continued to lay threats, with a [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) and another [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist). Cohun answered the assault with a [Beast Within](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Beast+Within), and then a [Slagstorm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) to clear the board.


Nakamura followed up with a [Borderland Ranger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger) and a [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben), while Cohun had only a [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth). Nakamura attacked with his duo and summoned an [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim). Cohun continued to accelerate with a [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum). Both players chipped away at each other's life totals. Cohen found a [Pillar of Flame](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) for Thalia, and Nakamura cast an [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) on the [Solemn Simulacrum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Solemn+Simulacrum), leaving a lonely [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) nibbling away.



![Solemn Simulacrum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Solemn+Simulacrum)![Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Oblivion+Ring)

Cohun found the first significant threat with a [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells). He followed up with a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), fetching that now-familiar pair, [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) and an [Inkmoth Nexus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus). Nakamura had little on defence and took beats from the Titan for a turn before Cohun added *even more* pressure with an [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan). Nakamura flashed in a [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), but even the tricksy flier could avail him naught against Cohen's incoming army.

Round 5 Feature Match - Cynic Kim vs. Gene Brumby
-------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw* 
Gene Brumby, a long time and respected member of the New Zealand **Magic** community, has elected to skip a World **Magic** Cup Qualifier this weekend to fly out to Manila, and then on to Yokohama next weekend. Brumby's been on his fair share of New Zealand National Teams in the past, and figured it was time to let others have a go.


Cynic Kim was on the South Korean National Team last year, and will be leading them again this year, as the highest Pro Points player.


### Game 1


Kim got up in Brumby's face early with a flipped [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), stunting the New Zealander's mana with a [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) on a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). Brumby stumbled on his land drops, losing a [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) to a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and a [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) when a [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) brought the counter spell back for an encore. A [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) only sufficed to keep Brumby alive a few turns longer, before Kim put him down with a [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel).


Kim 1 â€“ Brumby 0



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R5_Kim.jpg)

*Cynic Kim [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)s his play. Get it? Because he, you know, cast... [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)... I'll shaddup now.* 
### Game 2


This time it was Brumby's turn to come screaming out of the gates, with an [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim), a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), and a [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), discarding an [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) and a [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). Kim [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)ed on his first turn, but failed to play anything but land after that.


Brumby punched a [Thrun, the Last Troll](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) past the [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) he assumed Kim was holding, which joined the Pilgrim in knocking Kim down to 13. Brumby continued to fill up his graveyard with a [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) and a [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), before summoning a second Pilgrim.


Kim played his fourth land, and finally made a [Phantasmal Image](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) of [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Thrun%252C%2Bthe%2BLast%2BTroll), causing them both to wink out of existence with a tiny popping sound. He followed up with a [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) on one of Brumby's Pilgrims, carefully continuing to represent a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) for the [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) in Brumby's graveyard.


Brumby flashed back a [Faithless Looting](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting), but Kim [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)ed the [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) that followed it. Brumby still had an [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) in his graveyard, and now had a [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run) in play, to make his small mana critters considerably more dangerous. Kim again passed the turn back without play. Brumby flashed back his second Looting, discarding a pair of lands, before playing the last card in hand, a [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), finding two more land, and binning a second [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth). Kim summoned a [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) at the end of Brumby's turn, and finally attacked.


Brumby untapped, and drew a [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) off the top of his deck, fetching two [Gavony Township](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township)s. Kim pointed a [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) at the Titan at the end of Brumby's turn, and summoned a [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to flash back the [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) at the remaining Pilgrim, leaving him with just a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to Kim's Angel and [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Snapcaster%2BMage).


Kim attacked for 5, dropping Brumby to 11 to his 9. Brumby again summoned his Titan, this time just finding a pair of innocuous mana-tapping lands. He then flashed back [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) targeting Elesh Norn, but Kim had another [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) it. Brumby had one card left in hand, and all of his non-Titan permanents were tapped. Kim went over the possibilities in his head, before summoning [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage), and tapping down the Titan. The [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Snapcaster%2BMage)s attacked for 4, putting Brumby to 7, but the Angel stayed back.


Brumby counted his lands, before slamming down another [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites), bringing a pair of [Craterhoof Behemoth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Craterhoof+Behemoth)s back from the dead. The huge beasts High-Fived each other in Buddy Cop Film fashion as they stormed into the attack phase, along with their wise-crackin' token comic relief character, the [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), for a total of 28 damage.

### Game 3


Both players mulliganed to four in the deciding game. Brumby had the first play with a [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch), sending an [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) to the bin, but no tasty reanimation targets to match it. Kim summoned a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Snapcaster%2BMage) at the end of Brumby's second turn, attacked for 2, and played a [Sword of War and Peace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace). Brumby played a third land, and passed. The [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Snapcaster%2BMage) took up the blade, viciously cutting Brumby for a total of 8 damage, dropping him to 10. He then [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)ed, and ended without a fourth land.


Brumby did have a fourth land, and cast an [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim). Kim attacked again, and Brumby fell to 3. Brumby untapped, and drew his card and tanked. He had an [Acidic Slime](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Acidic+Slime) in hand, but didn't have the second Green mana he needed to cast it. He considered flashing back [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) on the [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), but instead passed back the turn. Kim played a fifth land and summoned Tamiyo. The Planeswalker tapped down the Pilgrim, and the Mage delivered the killing blow.


Cynic Kim defeats Gene Brumby 2-1



Round 6 Feature Match - Martin Juza vs. Hironbu Sugaya
------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker* 
Hironobu Sugaya and Martin Juza sat down at the feature match table and reminisced about old times. The last time the two played was in Japan, at Nagoya. One of the best things about doing coverage is seeing old competitors try and puzzle out just *which* corner of the world they last hung out in. Sugaya has had a good track record in Manila, winning the Block Constructed GP in 2008. Juza's resume is longer than space allows, so, instead, we turn our attention to the match at hand...


### Game 1


Juza led with a [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). Sugaya made the unusual play of casting two spells before playing a mana source, a [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) to take out the Birds and a [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) to scope out the shape of the game ahead. He followed with a [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). Juza had a [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), which died to a [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) but left the Golem sitting on the battlefield.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R6_Juza.jpg)

*Juza's off to an early lead.* 
Juza cast [Bonfire of the Damned](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) on the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), which was countered by a [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak). Juza summoned a [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), while Sugaya had a [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), which was happy to block the incoming Huntmaster. Juza had a second to follow up, and Sugaya was out of blockers and options.

Round 8 Feature Match - Terry Soh vs. Gerald Camangon
-----------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker* 
Terry Soh, Invitational winner, Malaysian team member and all-round Mage of Augury sat down to battle Gerald Camangon, who made Top 8 of GP: Manila in 2010 and is on this year's Philippines nationals team.


### Game 1


Camangon led the first game with a [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) and followed it with a [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). The [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler) attacked into [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Snapcaster%2BMage), who obligingly blocked.
