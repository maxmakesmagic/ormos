Right in the Gut
================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on August 9, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 






M***agic** 2013*'s release events are in full swing on **Magic Online**. The new Standard metagame is beginning to take shape. This week, I'm going to focus on the changes we're beginning to see in popular Standard archetypes and highlight some of the most interesting decks that went undefeated in last Saturday's daily events.

Surprisingly, the card that seems to have gained the most from ***Magic** 2013*'s release is [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot). [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) was commonly seen as a two-of in most White-Blue Delver lists previous to ***Magic** 2013*'s arrival. Now, it seems like almost every Delver list is playing three or four copies in the main. [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)'s newfound strength is far-reaching. The card is being played in some number by most decks that play [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) and it's even being included in most Naya Pod and Black-Red Aggro decks.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr207_gut_shot.jpg)[Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) | Art by [Greg Staples](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Greg+Staples%22%5D)

Why [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)? Gut shot is obviously good at dealing with turn-one [Delver of Secrets](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), but it's true strength is against the non-ramp green decks. Decks like Naya, Elves, Red-Green Aggro, and Mono-Green are very dependent on their [Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=243212), or [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves). The current Standard environment doesn't provide these decks with many strong two-mana creatures besides [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist). [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) costs zero mana and manages to put a lot of decks behind by a full turn. [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) was often sideboarded out in Delver mirrors, but [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681) is getting a lot of love recently, making [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) far less embarrassing in the midgame.

Delver's inclusion of four [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s has done a serious number on the green decks playing one-mana acceleration creatures. Red-Green Aggro and Naya were tier-one decks just one week ago, but now it's very clear that newer versions of Delver that include [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) and four [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s are pushing these green decks further down the ladder in terms of viability.

The kinks are still being worked out in many of the newer archetypes and the latest versions of Delver had a dominating week in **Magic Online** daily events. Here's the breakdown of decks that went 4–0 in last Saturday's daily events:

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr207_chart.jpg)  
Delver has consistently done well recently, but these results go above and beyond the status quo. I've already touched on some of the changes we've seen in Delver decks, but I'd like to take a look at one of the 4–0 lists from last Saturday and break down the changes we're seeing in the most dominant Standard archetype:

This list is an excellent example of what you need to expect when playing against Delver in the coming week. [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=244681) has, once again, become the Equipment of choice for Delver players. [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) gives the deck added strength against [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist) and Zombies, while providing a nice boost of card advantage. [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) is a card I've seen in a few White-Blue Delver sideboards. [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) is especially strong in versions of the deck that play [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) (for obvious reasons); casting [Sleep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sleep) with a [Vapor Snag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) in hand is almost always going to end the game if you have any sort of board presence.

It's not fair to call this Zombies, but the deck does play a lot of Zombies. This deck has a lot going for it. It can be incredibly aggressive with cards like [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), but it becomes strongest in the midend game. It's incredibly difficult to beat this deck with any green-based creature deck; cards like [Skinrender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skinrender), [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), and [Skaab Ruinator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Ruinator) are too much to handle. [Massacre Wurm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Massacre+Wurm) functions similarly to [Elesh Norn, Grand Cenobite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elesh+Norn%2C+Grand+Cenobite), but the Wurm can often outright kill opponents if it's coupled with a [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). I wouldn't be surprised if this archetype became a major player in the coming weeks.

This deck is incredibly strong in a Delver-dominated metagame. This list hasn't gone all-in on beating Delver, but this many [Whipflare](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whipflare)s and [Black Sun's Zenith](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith)s will definitely do some work. The deck reminds me of the deck that Patrick Chapin piloted to the finals of [Grand Prix Orlando](/en/events/coverage/its-brewers-world-grand-prix-orlando). The deck's endgame is far and away the strongest in the format. I'm happy to see that Saul22 included four copies of [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post) here. Last week, we saw a lot of lists that played two or three copies of the ***Magic** 2013* artifact. It's becoming clear now that having multiple copies of [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post) in play makes the game incredibly difficult for any opponent. I expect [Trading Post](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trading+Post) decks to be a huge part of the new Standard metagame in the coming weeks.

I haven't cast a [Quirion Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad) in years, so I was pretty excited to see sr4fim's Dryad deck. The deck plays very similarly to Delver. [Quirion Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad) is incredible in this type of deck, all the Phyrexian mana spells are huge for obvious reasons, and it's not unusual to attack with a 4/4 [Quirion Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad) on the third turn. I decided to take this deck for a test drive and I played it in a few heads-up queues on **Magic Online**. A lot of you have asked for some match reports from some of the more interesting decks each week, so I'd like to share a match I played with this against Black-Red Aggro.

Sample Match:

### Gro! vs Black-Red Pod

Game 1:

I won the roll and kept [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), [Hinterland Harbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hinterland+Harbor), [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), [Gut Shot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot), [Mental Misstep](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mental+Misstep), [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), and [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder). 

I played my [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and cast [Ponder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder), leaving [Quirion Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad), [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and another [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) on the top of my deck. My opponent led things off with a [Gravecrawler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravecrawler), which I wasn't happy to see because I had just lost a match to Mono-Black Zombies.

I drew [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), played [Hinterland Harbor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hinterland+Harbor), cast [Quirion Dryad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Dryad), cast [Gitaxian Probe](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), drew [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak), and passed the turn.
