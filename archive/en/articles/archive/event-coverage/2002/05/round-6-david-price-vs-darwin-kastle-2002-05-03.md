Round 6: David Price vs. Darwin Kastle
======================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 3, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Headshot%209-2014_3.jpg)
By Randy Buehler











Dave Price and Darwin Kastle met in a round six feature match that featured more Pro Tour experience than any match in the history of the Tour – neither player has ever missed an event. These two PT champs have already lost twice each so the loser would be eliminated from Day 2. Price drafted a red-green deck while Kastle went blue-black – it was a battle of the format's classic archetypes.

![Shower of Coals](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shower+of+Coals)Game 1 was a pure race with neither player even seriously considering blocking. Kastle served with his small blue and black fliers while Price brought the beats with [Cartographer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartographer) and [Rabid Elephant](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rabid+Elephant). Price got ahead in the race by killing two of Kastle's fliers with [Howling Gale](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howling+Gale), but Kastle followed up with [Balshan Collaborator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balshan+Collaborator) and then a [Repel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repel) on Price's elephant. Kastle seemed poised to win, but Price turned things around with a timely [Shower of Coals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shower+of+Coals), killing all Kastle's creatures and Kastle. All Kastle could do was cock his head and ask "That got to *you*?" "Yeah – fourth pick!"Game 2 was another race. Price took the early lead with his [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel), but he stumbled on lands and Kastle was able to get out [Balshan Collaborator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balshan+Collaborator) with two swamps untapped before Price could play another creature. Kastle then blundered – targeting [Wild Mongrel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) (not [Springing Tiger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springing+Tiger)) with his [Slithery Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slithery+Stalker). Price just discarded a card to change its color. Kastle was then forced to use [Crippling Fatigue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crippling+Fatigue) twice to kill the Tiger and then watch helplessly as Price discarded to Wild Mongel to drop Kastle to four and then finished him off with [Shower of Coals](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shower+of+Coals) again.

**Final Result:** Price 2- Kastle 0
