Deck Construction: Illuminati
=============================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 29, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/933.jpg)Illuminati consists of Grand Prix aficionado and store owner Alex Shvartsman, former US National Champion Justin Gary, and PT Champion Zvi Mowshowitz.

J(ustin) G(ary): Oooh, two Chatters. (Opening cards). Come on, give me two drops. Woohoo! Two drops! (Sorts [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear)s).  
 A(lex) S(hvartsman): Uh oh. Decklist problems. They registered two [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel)s, not two [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear)s.  
 Z(vi) M(owshowitz): Judge!

Head judge Rune Horvik went over and quickly corrected the mistake. Illuminati had clearly opened [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear)s and not Mongrels, much to their chagrin.

JG: It would have been nice to have had Mongrels instead.  
 ZM: Red/green doesn't look good, and white has a ton of good two drops. Let's try red/white.  
 JG: Is red/white good?  
 ZM & AS: I have no experience with it.  
 ZM: Let's at least try it though.

Zvi took all the red cards and added them to the white ones. This left Alex with a pile of blue and green cards, and Justin with a deck of black ones.

ZM: That's thirty (cards)!  
 AM: Right now if I play seventeen lands, [Crush of Wurms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Wurms) would not be unreasonable.  
 JG: Black only has eight creatures right now that I'd want to run, so let's try it with some of the blue. (Takes two [Lost in Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+Thought), [Thought Eater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Eater), [Aquamoeba](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquamoeba), [Aven Windreader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aven+Windreader), and [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) from Alex's deck. He then cuts [Malevolent Awakening](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malevolent+Awakening)). I want one of your bounce spells. I don't have many fliers either, so let me take the (Ghostly) Wings. AS: Do you really want [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood) in that deck?  
 ZM: They'll just sac guys with [Lost in Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lost+in+Thought) on them.  
 AS: Plus I don't like [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites).  
 ZM: I'm going to run sixteen lands.  
 AS: I'd play seventeen lands, you have a ton of four and five drops.  
 ZM: No I don't, I only have five, and I have nothing that needs mana to activate, except the [Hypochondria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypochondria). I wonder if I can pay one white mana? (laughs sarcastically)  
 AS: Six if you want to flashback the [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt), though.  
 JG: Do you want to run (Blazing) Salvo over [Demoralize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demoralize)? [Demoralize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demoralize) can do more damage potentially.  
 AS: I'd still run a seventeenth land over either!

Alex, Justin and Zvi switched seats at this point to look over one another's builds.

ZM: My card pool isn't great (looking at blue/green deck). Red and white are overstuffed.  
 AS: I don't like these decks at all. Let's try green/black and blue/white.  
 ZM: You can easily take away all my high end white cards like Zealot. I'll just add more burn to my deck instead.  
 JG: This may not work, but let's take a look at it.  
 AS: (After the switch) Actually, I like the way these decks look a lot better now.  
 JG: But who gets the [Patchwork Gnomes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Patchwork+Gnomes)?  
 ZM: You need this for threshold in the red/white deck.  
 JG: Rarrrrrrrrrr! (Grabs the Gnomes and windmills them onto the red/white deck). [Innocent Blood](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Innocent+Blood) is a lot better in the two Chatter deck.  
 AS: I was just eyeing that. I was eyeing the [Malevolent Awakening](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Malevolent+Awakening) also.   
 JG: Should I run [Scorching Missile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scorching+Missile) or [Flaming Gambit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flaming+Gambit)?  
 ZM: The Missile, definitely. You have [Anarchist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Anarchist) to go with it.  
 JG: Good call. I don't know much about this archetype, but I remember what red/white looked like in *Tempest* and this looks a lot like that deck.  
 AS: You mean jank?  
 JG: I don't know, but it looks good!  
 ZM: Let's trade again.

This time Alex moves to the black/green deck, Zvi back to the red/white, and Justin to the white/blue.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/934.jpg)JG: Chamber is cute with the (Wormfang) Drake, but I'm not a big fan of it.  
 AS: My deck isn't taking advantage of the [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear)s. None of my four drops are going to get cast on turn four.  
 JG: I want [Auramancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auramancer)s.  
 AS: If you do, run Wings.  
 JG: Then what should I cut?  
 AS: Cut either [Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) or [Predict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predict).  
 JG: [Predict](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predict) can go, but [Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek) is [Peek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peek)!  
 ZM: I think I'd rather run burn to the head (instead of [Demoralize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demoralize)). Eventually people will get total board control against me, unless I play stupid healer games, but I'd rather just rely on burn.  
 JG: So do you want to trade your [Vigilant Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vigilant+Sentry) for my [Militant Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Militant+Monk)?  
 ZM: YES! (Swaps the two cards quickly) MUCH better!  
 JG: I like the Sentry a lot better in my deck. He's going to be amazing.  
 AS: Do you think [Keep Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keep+Watch) would be better than [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough)?  
 ZM: No way! [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) is threshold on a stick.  
 AS: These two decks are solid (black/green and white/blue), but that other deck isn't. But then again, it's not supposed to be solid.  
 JG: Your little guys are going to keep swinging, Alex. You're just going to keep getting through with them because of the Butchers and the [Sudden Strength](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Strength)s.

Zvi asked his teammates about the rules interactions of damage prevention shields and [Phantom Nomad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantom+Nomad). They were divided on how the Nomad's ability would interact with damage prevention from the Healer and [Hypochondria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypochondria), so they called over head judge Rune Horvik yet again.

ZM: So Rune, if you had a protection from red creature and an [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake), how is damage prevented?  
 Rune: The damage is reduced to zero.  
 ZM: Well, what about having two damage prevention shields around my Nomad?  
 Rune: If they were both up when the creature was damaged, then you would choose which takes effect.  
 JG: So if someone [Firebolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firebolt)ed my Nomad and I used [Hypochondria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypochondria) on it, I could have the [Hypochondria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hypochondria) prevent the damage and then not remove a counter from the Nomad?  
 Rune: Correct.  
 ZM: Oooh, that makes my deck a lot better now! (Rune goes away to adjudicate other situations wisely). Do you think that you two (Alex and Justin) should switch decks for the metagame?  
 JG: We could switch if you'd like. I like to play the white/blue deck better, but I'm comfortable with the black/green.  
 ZM: You HAVE to switch after how nuts you went when you opened the Chatters and two drops! (cackles)

Alex and Justin traded seats yet again.

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpnj02/932.jpg)AS: [Keep Watch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keep+Watch) beckons me!  
 JG: No! It's like an [Inspiration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inspiration) that costs one less mana but makes you wait to draw the cards. [Deep Analysis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deep+Analysis) and [Breakthrough](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breakthrough) are much better.  
 AS: Should I play the two sac lands ([Seafloor Debris](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seafloor+Debris)) to help get to threshold?  
 ZM: I've seen more people get screwed by those lately than helped by them.  
 JG: I like them.  
 ZM: But if you don't draw them in your opening hand they can really screw up your mana development. They are surprisingly bad.  
 AS: At least I only have one one-toughness guy in my deck. No losing to tims for me!  
 ZM: And I can shield up my Nomads.  
 JG: I'm happy with our deck choices. We got some goods cards.  
 ZM & AS: Our card pool was ok.  
 JG: I think I want to play sixteen lands though.  
 AS: Hell no!  
 ZM: Why not? He's got two [Werebear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Werebear)s.  
 JG: I'll choke on land with this deck at seventeen. I'm really tempted to run the [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites).  
 ZM: I think you'd rather run [Leaf Dancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leaf+Dancer), if you have sixteen lands you won't have extra lands to pitch to [Last Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Last+Rites).  
 AS: Or you could add the [Mossfire Egg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mossfire+Egg), it's like running sixteen and a half lands.

The three players turned in their deck lists, at which point I asked Justin and Zvi about the land counts in the decks.

BB: I've noticed that Alex is very strict to making sure all decks have at least seventeen lands, no matter what.  
 JG: He's really too uptight about that. Sometimes you want to run sixteen lands.  
 ZM: In fact, I always look at a deck to see which lands I can get away with not running!  
 JG: Zvi is really good at figuring out mana in the decks.
