Undefeated Decks List
---------------------


*by Steve Sadin*

In most Sealed Deck formats, pro players will gravitate towards black, red, and/or blue as their main colors in large part because that's where the best removal, and evasion creatures can be found. But in *Innistrad* Sealed, white seems to have established itself as the color to play (and consequently, the color to beat). Four of the six undefeated Sealed Decks from [Grand Prix Milan](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/grand-prix-milan-2011-day-1-blog-2011-10-08) played white as one of their main colors (a 5th deck splashed white), and all four of the undefeated Sealed Decks here at Grand Prix Santiago took advantage of the quality, and depth that white has to offer.


Why is this?


Well, this time around black, and red, don't have that many more top-notch removals than white does â€“ and white's evasion creatures are on par with anything you can cast with blue mana.


Will white continue to dominate during today's drafts?


Stay tuned to find out!

Round 10 Feature Match - Rodrigo Soto vs. Martin Lecce
------------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

Despite the above-the-title star power that has headlined Grand Prix Santiago it was Argentinean Martin Lecce and local player Rodrigo Soto who were the only remaining 9-0 players at the start of the draft rounds. Rodrigo, who was playing in his first real tournament, was passing to Martin in the draft and sent a murmur around the table when he opened [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) in the first pack. Martin had opened [Ludevic's Test Subject](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ludevic%27s+Test+Subject) and both players took their double-faced goodies.


There was even mure murmuring and harumphing when Lecce opened his [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) in the second pack and took it rather than passing it to Soto. Lecce ended up drafting a 40-card version of Solar Flare with [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) , various fatties to bring back, and even a pair of [Isolated Chapel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Isolated+Chapel) s to make it all hum.


**Game one**


"You will play first," said Soto upon winning the die roll. Both players kept their openers and Soto made the first play with [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) . An [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) from Lecce flipped three lands and [Ludevic's Test Subject](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ludevic%27s+Test+Subject) . Soto played [Ghoulraiser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulraiser) and attacked for one with his deathtouch creature.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/round%2010%20Soto.JPG)
*Rodrigo Soto*
Martin passed the fourth turn with no play and cast [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) after Soto tapped out for [Moan of the Unhallowed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moan+of+the+Unhallowed) . The instant turned out about as well as Lecce could have drawn up the play in the locker room. He pitched [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) , a land, and [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) and kept [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) . He played [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows) on his turn.


Soto sent everyone in on the attack and Lecce ate a token, stymied the [Ghoulraiser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulraiser) with [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) , and took three. He looted with the Crows and pitched [Thraben Sentry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Sentry) . He untapped to play [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) and [Avacynian Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacynian+Priest) . Rodrigo drew his next card and promptly scooped. He was holding a fist full of green cards and only [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) s in play.


Lecce - 1 Soto - 0


**Game two**


Once again Soto chose to be on the draw. Lecce led off with [Traveler's Amulet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Traveler%27s+Amulet) while the Chilean player had [Typhoid Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Typhoid+Rats) . A [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) and [Caravan Vigil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Caravan+Vigil) revelaed the missing color from game one for Soto, who fetched a second [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp) . Martin cracked his Amulet for an [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) . The third turn saw another slackerly [Ghoulraiser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghoulraiser) followed by [Moan of the Unhallowed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moan+of+the+Unhallowed) . Martin, who could see the game slipping away from him, bought a turn with [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) . He was holding [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) and [Skaab Goliath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skaab+Goliath) but had no graveyard fodder to fuel them.


He played [Morkrut Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Morkrut+Banshee) without turning on morbid and passed the turn only to find himself staring across the table at [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) .


Lecce drew and played his own [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) but without any other vampires to help him race to five vampires so he could transform the bomb rare he was behind in the race with Soto's threatening to come online a turn earlier. At that point he would be forced to chump block the army of 4/4 fliers and would never be able to catch up -- or he would just be dead. Soto kept mounting his board presence and played another Moan and merely attacked with his Rats.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/round%2010%20Lecce.JPG)
*Martin Lecce*
Lecce needed his deck to help him and could only say go. He fell to 7 as Soto started to send the team and flashed back his first Moan. Lecce played [Avacynian Priest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacynian+Priest) and passed. Rodrigo flashed back his second copy and announced attackers. Martin tapped a zombie and vampire in response with a flashed back [Feeling of Dread](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feeling+of+Dread) . Soto simply attacked with his Rat and Martin was happy to put his Priest in the way to power out [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) next turn.


When Soto attacked with everything a turn later Lecce reached for his sideboard for the rubber game.


Lecce - 1 Soto - 1


**Game Three**


Lecce put Soto on the play in the rubber game and the Chilean player promptly mulliganed down to five cards. He was still scrappy though and had Typoid Rats wielding a [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger) to sneak in some damage. Lecce played [Markov Patrician](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Markov+Patrician) and traded with the Rat to gain back some life. Lecce deployed [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) on turn four. Soto was still scuffling while Lecce was able to add [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) and [Murder of Crows](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murder+of+Crows) alongside his one-man airforce.


Final result: Martin Lecce was the only 10-0 player remaining in the event after defeating Rodrigo Soto in three games.

"My red, and my black removal spells were about the same in my deck thanks to the fact that I have two [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) s, a [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) , and two [Mindshrieker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindshrieker) sâ€“ and while red would have given me some slightly better individual cards, the black gave me [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) , the ability to flashback [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) , and access to [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) ."


Even with a [Sulfur Falls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfur+Falls) in his pool, Turtenwald decided against splashing his red.


"I figured that I had a lot more to lose than I had to gain by splashing the red. My deck is already pretty good, and I have several double blue spells, a double black spell â€“ so I didn't think it was worth it to risk having color problems. Not only that, but I wouldn't have been able to play [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) if I splashed the red -- and I think that [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) is really good in my deck because of all of my removal spells, and (potentially) defensive creatures."


When asked if the speed of the format affected his deck building decisions, Turtenwald explained that his pool didn't really give him the option.


"Once I decided to play Blue-Black, pretty much every card in my deck was an auto include â€“ the only real choice that I made was to leave a second [Dissipate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) in my sideboard. I'm not going to do something crazy like leave [Makeshift Mauler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Makeshift+Mauler) in my sideboard because I'm worried that the format is too fast."


Owen felt optimistic about his chances with the deck "I would certainly take this over a random pool."


Nine rounds later...


After he finished Day One with an 8-1 Record, I had a chance to talk to Owen again now that he had a full day's worth of play with the deck under his belt.


Before I could even ask him any questions, Owen explained that he had misbuilt his pool.


"I wound up boarding in my red every match. My two [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) s were better than all of my black cards put together.


My game ones (with the Blue-Black version of the deck) were really difficult for me â€“ I think I want 3-3 in games ones. When I won with the Blue-Black version of the deck, it was because I drew better than my opponent, or because the game went long, and I got to deck him with [Nephalia Drownyard](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) . But once I switched to red, I was able to just run my opponents over.


Plus, a disproportionate number of my opponents had Inquisitor Elite, so that made boarding out my black even better"


At the beginning of the day, Owen had likened his two [Corpse Lunge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Lunge) s to his [Brimstone Volley](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brimstone+Volley) , and his [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) , while dismissing his [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) s as being marginal. By the end of the day, he came to the conclusion that he had completely misevaluated his removal spells.


" [Corpse Lunge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Lunge) wound up being really mediocre for me. Even though I had a bunch of ways to put cards in my graveyard, my creatures were so small that it was usually only able to do one or two damage."


" [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) is way better than I gave it credit for when I was building my deck. It kills all the same things that [Corpse Lunge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corpse+Lunge) kills, and it can deal with early [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) , [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth) s, and [Vampire Interloper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Interloper) s without setting you way back."

Sunday, 11:44 a.m. - Drafting with Martin Juza
----------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall* 

Martin Juza was one of four players yesterday to go undefeated in the Sealed Deck rounds. The Czech player went 8-0-1 playing a blue-white tempo build with three copies of [Chapel Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chapel+Geist) . As he sat down for the first draft of the Grand Prix he was looking forward to drafting something with more card selection. He was hoping to get cards like [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) and [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) and exerting some control over his games.


One of the new wrinkles of this draft format has been double-faced cards -- not just mechanically in how they play but in how you draft them as well. Since there is no card back, players can see a card that every other player opens and when they are picked that information is out in the public as well. That information is out there but how players will deal with that information remains to be seen.


As Martin sat down at his table he groaned, "Dude, I have no idea how to draft. I have only done like 10 drafts. Hopefully going 4-2 should not be that hard."


Here was the table order for the draft:


* 1 Martin Lecce
* 2 Martin Juza
* 3 Rodrigo Lopez
* 4 Ronald Lunar
* 5 Melissa Detora
* 6 Alessandro Gozzo
* 7 Dan Brunello
* 8 Rodrigo Soto

As the players sifted through pack one both Lecce and Soto quickly took their double faced cards. Lecce, immediately to Juza's right took [Ludevic's Test Subject](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ludevic%27s+Test+Subject) , while Soto slammed [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) . Suddenly plans to be blue-black looked less clear for Juza than before the packs were opened. He flipped through his own pack and saw [Essence of the Wild](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+of+the+Wild) , [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) , [Hanweir Watchkeep](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hanweir+Watchkeep) , and [Butcher's Cleaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher%27s+Cleaver) . Juza decided to play it safe and take the Cleaver and wait and see what was open.


Pack two came with [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) and Juza took that over another [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) and [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) -- the only cards he singled out for consideration in that pack. [Hamlet Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamlet+Captain) was next over the impossibly expensive [Moldgraf Monstrosity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moldgraf+Monstrosity) . He stayed mono-green with [Orchard Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orchard+Spirit) next before veering into red for a late Rolling Tremblor over an [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) that he briefly flirted with. There was also a late [Rage Thrower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rage+Thrower) that came around but he opted for the cheaper -- a definitely on-color -- [Ambush Viper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ambush+Viper) . He was solidly green with his complimentary color still to be determined at the end of pack one.


There was a murmur -- no talking, just a murmur -- as Lecce opened [Bloodline Keeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodline+Keeper) and chose not to pass it to Soto, who had taken that very card in pack one. Juza, meanwhile was looking through a pack with [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) , [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) , [Cloistered Youth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloistered+Youth) , [Forbidden Alchemy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forbidden+Alchemy) , and [Moan of the Unhallowed](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moan+of+the+Unhallowed) . He quickly took the Cagebreakers. He toyed with choosing [Splinterfright](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinterfright) in pack two but went with [Galvanic Juggernaut](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Galvanic+Juggernaut) instead -- there was also a [Travel Preparations](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Travel+Preparations) in the pack. [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) and [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit) were his subsequent picks -- his first real move toward white cards. He took [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) next with [Victim of Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Victim+of+Night) and [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) notably still in the pack. It looked like both green and white had dried up but then a late surge yielded [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) as well as a tabled [Orchard Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orchard+Spirit) and [Splinterfright](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinterfright) .


At the end of pack two Juza had sixteen cards earmarked for play in his deck and some sitting on his bench. He grinned and flashed the two [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) with [Splinterfright](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinterfright) . Juza immediately regretted his first pick of the third pack when he took [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) over cards like [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger) , [Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) , [Falkenrath Noble](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Noble) , [Unburial Rites](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) , [Spidery Grasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spidery+Grasp) , and [Festerhide Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festerhide+Boar) . His next pick forced him to choose between [Bonds of Faith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonds+of+Faith) -- he had no removal if he was going green-white -- or the game breaking [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) . He also considered [Avacyn's Pilgrim](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) but chose the land. [Darkthicket Wolf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkthicket+Wolf) was next and then a Pilgrim. He rounded out the pack with a smattering of white fliers.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/drafting%20with%20martin%20juza.JPG)
*Martin Juza's Best White/Green Spread*

"I have too many three drops," moaned Juza as he began to lay out the deck to sort the wheat from the chaff. He looked at the Villagers that he had first picked in the third pack and shook his head. "This pick was such a mistake. I should have taken [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger) there."


He laid out 22 cards and a [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) : "I am going to play 17 lands because I have the two Pilgrims."



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/drafting%20with%20martin%20juza%202.JPG)
*Juza decides on a 23rd card.*

The cards that were on the sideline were [Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) , [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) , and Demonmaul Hauberk. He wanted to be able to get creatures into his graveyard for his two Cagebreakers and the latter two could help in that regard. On the other hand, he liked the fact that the Bellringer was a combat trick and added to his Human count for the [Hamlet Captain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hamlet+Captain) . He would eventually add that card to his pile but later realized he could have gone with 16 lands and played the [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) there.

Round 12: Feature Match - Melissa Detora vs. Martin Juza
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Steve Sadin* 

Melissa Detora came into this match with a 10-1 record, while Martin Juza entered the round as the sole remaining undefeated player in the tournament at 10-0-1.


### Game One


Juza won the die roll and chose to play first, but had to begin the match with a mulligan.


Detora started things off with a [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) , and a [Civilized Scholar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civilized+Scholar) while


Juza opened with four [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) s and a [Villagers of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Villagers+of+Estwald) (which quickly transformed into the more menacing [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald) because Juza couldn't cast any of the other spells in his hand with only four [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest) s).


[Civilized Scholar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civilized+Scholar) turned into a [Homicidal Brute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Homicidal+Brute) , which promptly attacked Juza down to 15 and a [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab) promised to keep [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald) at bay.


Juza's [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin) got trumped by a [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) , and the European pro's [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit) died at the hands of a [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) .


Detora built up her air force with a [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) , and Juza cast a [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) which would allow him to win the game in a matter of turns, if he could live through Detora's evasive attacks.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/fm12%20Detora.JPG)
*Melissa Detora*

Detora cast a second [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) , and flashed it back to take out [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin) , before attacking with both of her fliers to knock Juza down to 6.


Juza put a [Butcher's Cleaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher%27s+Cleaver) on his [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) before attacking (making two wolves), gaining six life to put himself back up to a relatively healthy 12, and knocking Detora down to 8.


Detora looted with her [Civilized Scholar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Civilized+Scholar) , and thought for a moment as she decided between attacking in the air to knock Juza to 6 (at which point she would sacrifice whatever creature blocked the [Butcher's Cleaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher%27s+Cleaver) ed [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) to her [Stitcher's Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Apprentice) so that he wouldn't gain any life) â€“ or leaving her creatures back, and mugging Juza's [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) if it attacked. After weighing her options, Detora decided to pass the turn.


Juza attacked with [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) , [Howlpack of Estwald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Howlpack+of+Estwald) , and (what became a total of) 4 2/2 Wolf Tokens. Detora chose to block [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) with a [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) , before killing it at the end of Juza's turn with her [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) â€“ however, Juza had another Kessig Cagebreaker to replace it.


Detora had no answer for the second [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) and consequently died to Juza's army of wolves a turn later.


Martin Juza 1 â€“ Melissa Detora 0


### Game Two


Detora chose to play first, and got off to a (relatively) quick start with [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) , [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) , and [Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab) before Juza had played a creature.


Juza's turn four [Voiceless Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voiceless+Spirit) picked up a [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) , and took out [Moon Heron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moon+Heron) a turn later, and an [Orchard Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orchard+Spirit) gave the European pro a real clock to work with.


[Fortress Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fortress+Crab) attacked in, and Juza blocked with his [Orchard Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orchard+Spirit) â€“ allowing Detora to decimated his board with her freshly drawn [Geistflame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geistflame) . With a couple of creatures in his graveyard, Juza was able to cast his [Splinterfright](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinterfright) â€“ while Detora went to the air with a [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) .


Juza cast a [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) , which is particularly deadly alongside [Splinterfright](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinterfright) , and [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) , then followed it up with a second [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) a turn later.


Juza seemed to have the game in hand, at least unless Detora drew something fantastic.


When Detora drew only a land for her turn, it looked like Juza had the match...



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/drafting%20with%20juza.JPG)
*Martin Juza*

### Game Three


Juza again had a slow start with [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) , [Butcher's Cleaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher%27s+Cleaver) and [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin) as his first three plays â€“ allowing Detora to get off to an early lead with [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) , Selfless Occulist, [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) , and a [Blazing Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blazing+Torch) to take out Juza's [Abbey Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abbey+Griffin) .


When Juza cast a [Kessig Cagebreakers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Cagebreakers) with four creatures in his graveyard (thanks to [Mulch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) ) Detora suddenly found herself in a spot where she needed to topdeck something quickly to get back into the game.

*by Luis Scott-Vargas* 

With one draft left to go, the players in Pod 2 were competing for two slots in the Top 8, with possibly more based on tiebreakers. The two players in the finals of the draft could presumably draw in at 12-2-1, leaving not much of a margin for error. I covered Paulo Vitor's draft, which was actually only his fourth draft ever in the format. Despite not having many drafts under his belt, PV discussed the format at length with the rest of the team, particularly Ben Stark, with 50 drafts under his belt.


Ben basically recommended that PV draft either Blue-Black mill yourself or Green-White Aggro, a fact which clearly weighed on PV as he looked at his first pack. After the transform cards were revealed, none of which would go on to be first-picked (they were all common werewolves and the like), PV had basically three real options: [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) , [Battleground Geist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battleground+Geist) , or [Midnight Haunting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Haunting) . He could take the Skaab and look to go blue-black, or take the Haunting and aim for green-white, since either of those would be better to build around than the Geist.


He took the Haunting, likely because it was the best of the three cards, and possibly because it left the pack with just two good blue cards, presenting a better signal. Luckily for him, he was then passed a [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) , which was a windmill slam. Not only was it a great signal, it is one of the best possible cards for the GW deck, particularly with [Midnight Haunting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Haunting) . The only downside was that (unknown to PV) the player to his right took [Slayer of the Wicked](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slayer+of+the+Wicked) over the Township, so the way wasn't completely clear.

*by Steve Sadin* 

**In what ways did your work illustrating cards for** **Innistrad** **differ from your work on previous **Magic** expansions?**


Steve Prescott: Jeremy Jarvis (the lead art director for **Magi**c), made it a point for me to infuse gothic horror themes into all of the pieces that I did for **Innistrad**. My stuff isn't normally that dark, so it was a bit of an adjustment for me, but I had a lot of fun illustrating cards for this set.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/Prescott.jpg)
*Steve Prescott showing off the original art for [Daybreak Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daybreak+Ranger) , and [Nightfall Predator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightfall+Predator) .*

In sets like **Mirrodin**, or *Ravnica*, that have louder, more animated feels to them, I can kind of go crazy with the lighting, and use splashy colors whenever I want to. But in **Innistrad** I needed to work with actual atmospheric light sources, like full moons, gaslamps, and torches.


Why do the colors that you use matter so much when you're doing an illustration?


The short answer is that color has a huge impact on mood.


Take Berserk for instance â€“ for that, I wanted to use a bunch of insane colors to drive home the idea that the character was really going ballistic and hitting you in the face â€“ but I couldn't just throw in splashes of blue and magenta since loud colors don't exactly have a gothic horror feel to them.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/Image.Berserk.jpg)

In order to develop the gothic horror feel that I wanted, I had to use more earth tones, and include more natural light sources, than I usually do. That said, I was still able to work some loud colors into the art for Parallel lives.


When you sit down to illustrate a new card, what do you feel you need to get right in order for the piece to be a success?


Most of the stuff I've done for **Magic** relies heavily on facial expressions. I think that the face sets the mood as much as the physical environment that the characters are in, so I like to make sure that the face accurately depicts the right emotion. Be it pensiveness, fear, or unbridled rage, if you get the face right â€“ the rest really tends to fall into place.


**Which** **Innistrad** **illustration did you have the most fun working on?**


Outside of the concept pieces? Ranger's [Guile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guile) .


The more painterly a piece is, the better. I like anything where I get to play with big brushstrokes â€“ and Ranger's [Guile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guile) really gave me an opportunity to go wild, and explore new themes.


Do you work exclusively with paints, or do you do any of your work digitally?


I work almost entirely with acrylic paints, except for a brief experimental stage where I upload my completed sketch and try out various color schemes on my computer.


My favorite piece of concept art for the set -- a chilling illustration depicting a standoff between a werewolf, and an angry mob of villagers â€“ does a great job of capturing the mood of **Innistrad**. What goes into making a piece of concept art?



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsan11/stf157_coverImage.jpg)

I love doing concepting â€“ all that you're required to do is draw cool stuff, and have fun with other artists, and at that end of the process you've created the foundation for a new world.


There are several stages of concept art. At first, you're just creating characters, and figuring out what things like Zombie Horrors, and [Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit) Knights should look like. But at that point, we needed to start setting the mood for the characters we had designed.


The piece that you're referring to is what is known as a Concept Scene â€“ our first opportunity to show our characters in action.


Are there any **Magic** illustrators whose work you really look forward to seeing?


There are a lot of people whose work I really enjoy, but the paintings that Mark Zug, Chris Moeller, Jesper Ejsing, Chris Rahn, and Steve Belledin are probably my favorites.


It's not that I have a bias against digital artists, but it's just so cool for me to see what other people who work within the same medium as me can do.

*by Brian David-Marshall* 

Shuhei Nakamura had an outside shot at making the Top 8 with a win this round but events were conspiring against him off stage to make that a near impossibility. Chilean National Champion David Kaliski was on the verge of a bye into the Top 8 due to a DQ in his pod that had given him a match win against the offending player at the start of the last three rounds. Kaliski -- who actually lost the only round he actually played in draft two -- was one spot higher than Shuhei and had better breakers.


Had Shuhei known this he could have asked for a draw, which would have locked Chilean player Carlos Iturra into the Top 8. Thinking he had a shot at the Top 8 though, Shuhei had to play. Perhaps "thinking" is too strong a word for the mental state that Shuhei was in at this point. Despite being an elect member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, Shuhei has not slowed his travel pace at all and has been in Milan and Brisbane on the previous two weekends -- and he is scheduled to hit Hiroshima this coming weekend.


He arrived at the tournament site this morning feeling as bad as I have seen any players since Frank Karsten and his bucket in Yokohama several years ago. To make matters worse Nakamura was handed all sorts of treatments this morning by his teammates many of which seemed to have exacerbated his condition instead of improved it. He had sloughed through the day and with a win could still manage to Top 8. Carlos Iturra just wanted to lock up his Top 8 and the invite to Pro Tour Dark Ascension.

### Game One


Shuhei led off with [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) and suited it up with [Spectral Flight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) on turn two to beat in for three. His air strike was short lived as Iturra played [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter) . Nakamura had no follow up and Iturra equipped his Hunbter with [Cobbled Wings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cobbled+Wings) and flew over for one.


Nakamura shrugged and played [Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms) to [Time Ebb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Time+Ebb) the Hunter. Iturra was happy to tuck the Hunter in his back pocket for later and played [Somberwald Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Spider) . Shuhei passed with no play. Iturra unsheathed his [Silver-Inlaid Dagger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silver-Inlaid+Dagger) and dropped his opponent to 15 with an attack in the air.


Nakamura scooped a couple of turns of nothing later when Iturra added [Grizzled Outcasts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grizzled+Outcasts) .


Nakamura - 0 Iturra - 1


### Game Two


Nakamura played [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) on turn three -- milling [Selfless Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selfless+Cathar) with his [Deranged Assistant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deranged+Assistant) . Fiend HUnter could only stalk the Assistant, and Nakamura was able to smash in for four while bouncing the Geist off of the Hunter. He played [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) after combat.


[Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms) made it impossible for Iturra to interact successfully with Nakamura's hexproof legend or his [Stitched Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitched+Drake) .

### Game Three


Shuhei was really showing the effects of his illness this round as he kept a hand with five lands, [Butcher's Cleaver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Butcher%27s+Cleaver) , and [Grasp of Phantoms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Phantoms) . He actually did nothing the entire game other than play those two spells, lots of land, [Armored Skaab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armored+Skaab) , Thraban Sentry, and [Woodland Sleuth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Sleuth) while Iturra played big green creatures ranging from [Festerhide Boar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festerhide+Boar) and [Somberwald Spider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Somberwald+Spider) to [Essence of the Wild](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+of+the+Wild) .


Shuhei inquired about the draw as the match was winding down but Iturra just shook his head. Nakamura nodded, extended the hand and rested his head on the table.


**Final result: Carlos Iturra won the final round of the Swiss two games to one and ended up in 5th place in the standings. As it turns out the draw would have still left Nakamura in the same exact spot in the standings -- 17th place with two Pro Points.**
