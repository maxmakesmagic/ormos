There were lots of big Standard events this past weekend... a StarCityGames.com Open, the StarCityGames.com Invitational, and of course [the Grand Prix in Singapore](/en/events/coverage/da-rosa-reigns-supreme-singapore). There was also a lot of Legacy played, but given our last couple of weeks' foci, I thought I would just talk (sing, really) about Standard.


And despite the continued success of Caw-Blade variants, I decided this week (in addition to writing that short and silly ditty about [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb)) to focus on all *the other* interesting stuff that performed. So let's start with [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb), shall we?

Former Player of the Year Shouta Yasooka leads us off this week with a very different Blue-Black Control deck. It's not quite like the removal / control decks or disruption / control decks that we've seen with their varying [Abyssal Persecutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abyssal+Persecutor)s, [Sea Gate Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle)s, or even [Liliana Vess](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess) that we have see over the past few months... and his was *also* not really like any of the Tezzeret decks we've seen since Pro Tour Paris.


 


With...



> 
> * 1 [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine),
> * 4 [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice),
> * 4 [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb), and
> * 3 [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet)
> 


Shouta was kind of scraping the mathematical bottom of the barrel in terms of how many artifacts you can reasonably play in a Tezzeret deck. Twelve artifacts is about one card in five (and you get to look at five cards with Tezzeret's "plus" ability).


...but what artifacts they were!

[Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) is a big finisher. At this stage, even the "regular" Blue-Black Control uses this finisher. [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice) allows the deck to play turn-three [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) or [Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas): It buoys the relatively low land count, and gets big in the late game. [Tumble Magnet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet) is one of the best solutions in the format to a resolved [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine). None of this is new.

...but what about [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb)?


[Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) is so cool some lunatic might write a song about it!


So what makes [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) so special?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td146_torpor.jpg)


 


From the "obvious" standpoint, it is a relatively cheap card that turns off what makes essentially every other deck in the metagame special. A [Primeval Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) will no longer search up two lands. A [Squadron Hawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)... no longer flies into battle as a Squadron but alone. [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) has to deal with [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) somehow—probably [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil)—before it can complete the [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combination. And our girl [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)? She isn't looking for anything.


 



![Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Torpor+Orb)

 


There are a number of reasons why [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) might be cool. One of them is that it is an artifact, and [Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas) needs ample artifacts around to be effective to begin with. Another is that [Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas) can make an otherwise blank (but not really) [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) into basically a Dragon. But subtly, [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb)*just isn't a creature.*


 


Many other decks looking for this kind of functionality (that is, turning off [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)) might try to accomplish it via [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite). But being a noncreature artifact makes [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) immune to being cleared by an opposing Jace, the Mind Sculpror, halving a Deceiver Twin deck's usual set of outs against a hateful two.


In case you didn't see the Grand Prix Top 8, it looked like this:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td146_chart.jpg)


 


Most of the Caw-Blade decks now look to be playing [Dismember](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember). This gives them a potentially one-mana answer to [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) + [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), and also a mana-efficient answer to an opponent's equipping an attacker.

"Vampires" has strayed a bit from the original tribe and team. For example Mispagel ran [Hero of Oxid Ridge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) as an additional beater. [Manic Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal) is going to be present in the sideboard if not the main deck—primarily as an answer to [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) / [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), but when you are facing off against decks with [Spellskite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) or [Phyrexian Metamorph](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) (or even [Everflowing Chalice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice)), [Manic Vandal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manic+Vandal) is just going to prove a useful removal card... at least before the dreaded [Torpor Orb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) strikes.

So why Vampires, and why now?


The short answer is that with Caw-Blade taking up so much of the top tables, and with Caw-Blade so concerned with beating itself—all the card choices revolving around stealing mana from a Sword activation, or getting ahead of an opposing [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) with a [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren)—there is less space for cards like [Day of Judgment](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) or even [Gideon Jura](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) that have traditionally plagued creature decks.


Remember that back around Pro Tour Paris, Caw-Blade ran fast life gain like [Sylvok Lifestaff](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvok+Lifestaff), whereas today you need to actually untap with [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) in order to get the [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) down before turn five. The light spot removal in Vampires can help prevent that. Vampires, packed with [Go for the Throat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat), [Arc Trail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail), [Gatekeeper of Malakir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir), and of course [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) has roughly a billion ways to break up Equipment. You can [Arc Trail](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arc+Trail) a [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) the turn the opponent passes, or Gatekeeper a [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) token even after "the worst" has happened. [Sword of Feast and Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) will produce a creature you can't block, but you have every opportunity to [Go for the Throat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat) or burn a potential protection from black creature before everybody gets set up, and [Gatekeeper of Malakir](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir) can slay the Swordsman regardless of protection.

Well, maybe we aren't actually breaking any promises... Jacob's deck played a [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) package, but there was no attached "Caw," as it were.


 


MJ's deck is essentially a [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) deck splashing white for the best two-drop in Standard. He is taking advantage of the significant crossover in cards between these top strategies—[Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor); [Preordain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain); [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak); [Spell Pierce](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce); [Into the Roil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Roil); and so on can be played in either deck—to produce a mash-up capable of winning with either draw.

Against some decks he can play for second-turn [Stoneforge Mystic](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) / third-turn [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) or Sword, and against others the [Deceiver Exarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) + [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) win. Or better yet, especially when his opponent isn't certain of what's going on, Jacob can hassle with the more aggressive white splash, get the opponent to tap a bunch of mana in order to avoid being beaten by [Batterskull](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull), and then snap back the other direction for his Exarch kill.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td146_exarch.jpg)


 


Hybrid decks have basically been my favorite for as long as I can remember, and even the straight Exarch decks have been leaning back on [Consecrated Sphinx](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) or [Inferno Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) anyway as an alternate kill... Why not the actual next-best thing you can do in Standard as Plan B? My guess is that—at least until we see a shakeup, potentially later this month—"Twin Blade" may be a hot strategy online and/or for PTQ play.
