Mythic Championship IV Day 1 Metagame Breakdown
===============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on July 26, 2019 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











It's been only a few months since Eli Loveman took Humans to victory at Mythic Championship II, but the Modern metagame has gone through substantial changes since then.


*War of the Spark* and *Modern Horizons* rank among the two most impactful set releases in the entire history of the format, and *Core Set 2020* has added several relevant cards as well. What's more, [Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below) was banned just a few weeks ago. With all these shakeups, where did players' Modern deck choices end up in Barcelona?

Hogaak Is the Most-Played Deck by Far
=====================================


* [![Hogaak, Arisen Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis)

Even without [Bridge from Below](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bridge+from+Below), [Hogaak, Arisen Necropolis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hogaak%2C+Arisen+Necropolis) remains a very powerful card that is well worth building around. Consider the following start:


* Turn 1: [Bloodstained Mire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodstained+Mire) and [Carrion Feeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carrion+Feeder).
* Turn 2 [Stitcher's Supplier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier) and [Faithless Looting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faithless+Looting). Cast Hogaak from the graveyard, trigger [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), and pass with 14 power on the battlefield.
* Turn 3: Attack for lethal.

While this is an exceptionally good start, it illustrates why so many players chose the deck. A metagame share of 21.4% is absurdly high for Modern. And this doesn't even include the Dredge decks (which sometimes had one copy of Hogaak) and the Hogaak Dredge decks (which run 3 Hogaak and 4 [Stitcher's Supplier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stitcher%27s+Supplier)). Hogaak is certainly the card of the tournament.

While most players agreed on the core of four-ofs, there was no consensus on the final slots to fill out the deck. The above list runs [Insolent Neonate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insolent+Neonate), [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe), and [Golgari Thug](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Thug) in those last few slots, whereas other versions had graveyard setup cards like [Cryptbreaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptbreaker), [Lotleth Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotleth+Troll), [Shriekhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekhorn), [Altar Of Dementia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar+Of+Dementia), and/or [Hedron Crab](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hedron+Crab) or interactive cards [Fatal Push](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fatal+Push), [Assassin's Trophy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassin%27s+Trophy), and/or [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) instead. And that's far from an exhaustive list of card options.


But while a consensus on the optimal build is lacking, a large part of the field agreed that returning Hogaak, [Vengevine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vengevine), and [Bloodghast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloodghast)s for free is a powerful strategy.

The deck got several upgrades from the last few sets. [Aria of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aria+of+Flame) is an alternative win condition that circumvents both creature removal and anti-graveyard cards. [Finale of Promise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Finale+of+Promise) is also a strong addition because it returns [Arclight Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arclight+Phoenix) by itself. And one-of additions like [Lava Dart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Dart) or [Magmatic Sinkhole](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magmatic+Sinkhole) are easy to find with all the card draw spells.


These card draw spells, by the way, still form the core of the deck. They help find [Thing in the Ice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thing+in+the+Ice) and [Arclight Phoenix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arclight+Phoenix), enable them, and keep you churning through your deck. Some things don't change.

The eight most-played non-land cards among all main decks and sideboards break down as follows.

With [Leyline of the Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+the+Void) as the most-played card overall, it does seem like the Mythic Championship competitors came prepared for the Hogaak menace. The non-negligible number of maindeck Leylines is a further testament to that. And while the full card breakdown will be posted later, I can already tell you that [Rest in Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rest+in+Peace) and [Grafdigger's Cage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grafdigger%27s+Cage) are among the most-played sideboard cards.


The question is: Will it be enough? How many anti-graveyard cards are necessary to beat Hogaak? How well can the deck perform in a field that is at least somewhat prepared for it? And which decks from the other 78.6% of the Modern metagame will over-perform?
