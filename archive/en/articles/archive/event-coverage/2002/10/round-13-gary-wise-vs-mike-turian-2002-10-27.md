Round 13: Gary Wise vs. Mike Turian
===================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on October 27, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_602.png)
By Matt Urban











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a877.jpg)Gary Wise and Mike Turian are both looking to make top 8, but because they have 28 points each, they need to win out to do so. Both players have great resumes to their credit. CMU regular Mike Turian has a Grand Prix win at Montreal and also a Pro Tour top 8. To go along with this he has many other high finishes. The blue/black deck he is armed with is very good, sporting fast creatures and a [Voidmage Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voidmage+Prodigy) (can't keep Kai out of the feature match area). 

Gary Wise has what may be an even greater list of accomplishments. He recently placed in the top 4 of Pro Tour Boston as a member of Courtney's Boys and a Worlds top 8 to go with the usual laundry list of high Pro Tour and Grand Prix finishes. Gary's deck is a high-powered red/white deck featuring multiple [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith)s and good removal. 

Oh, yeah, they also won Pro Tour New York 2000 together as Potato Nation.

The match began with some friendly banter. Gary stole a pepperoni from Mike's slice of pizza, and wished Mike good luck. Both hoped that the winner would reach top 8 no matter who came out ahead.

Gary flipped a quarter to see who would go first. Mike lost the flip, but stole the quarter after being advised to check for double heads.

### Game 1

Gary led off with a turn two [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), and Mike countered with a turn two [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid). The [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) is one of Mike's power cards for the matchup, as it is a cheap creature that can hold the ground while his fliers take the game. Gary had [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) for it, though, and began attacking. 

Beyond the [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid), Mike's draw was not good. He had to play out [Barren Moor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barren+Moor) instead of cycling it, indicating a land light draw or a hand full of high casting cost spells. He did have a turn four [Ascending Aven](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ascending+Aven), but that was shot down by [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) after Gary summoned a [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) to aid his [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). 

Gary summoned a [Dive Bomber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dive+Bomber), further torturing Mike through his [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid)'s drawback. All Mike could do was summon an [Entrails Feaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entrails+Feaster). It looked like Mike was trying to kill Gary through [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) damage, but then Mike showed his trick. In response to [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) shooting the [Entrails Feaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entrails+Feaster), Mike cast [Read the Runes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Read+the+Runes) for three, sacrificing the treacherous Anurid and the [Entrails Feaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entrails+Feaster) that would die anyway. 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a878.jpg)The [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) dominated play for a few turns while Gary attacked with the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder), and a recently summoned [Gustcloak Harrier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gustcloak+Harrier), bringing Mike to 12 until Mike managed a [Crown of Suspicion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crown+of+Suspicion) to finish the [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) shenanigans. 

Gary had another. 

Mike: "Another one? How lucky!"

Wise: "You gave them to me."

Mike put up a valiant defense with a [Screaming Seahawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Screaming+Seahawk) and fetched its brother, but it just wasn't enough as the second [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) controlled the board for long enough for Gary to win.

Wise – 1, Turian – 0

### Game 2

Turian was a bit frustrated by Gary's second [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith), but he had to get over it. He chose to play and again had a turn two [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid). Gary, though, had turn one [Goblin Sledder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Sledder) and turn two [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith). 

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpphi02/a879.jpg)"How lucky!"

"My draw is pretty good."

Gary also had [Sandskin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sandskin) to stop the [Wretched Anurid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wretched+Anurid) beats and keep his life total at a lofty 17. This game, Turian had a [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) to deal with the treacherous Zombie. The [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith) took a shot at the [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk), and Mike predictably sacrificed the Anurid in response in an attempt to save it. Gary responded with [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock), finishing off the [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) and most of Mike's dreams. 

Gary kept attacking, bringing Mike down to seven, and Mike finally came up with a creature big enough to survive [Sparksmith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparksmith): [Tribal Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Golem). Even with no special abilities, it is still a 4/4 body.

Gary had a [Pacifism](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pacifism) lined up and his next attack brought Mike down to a precarious four life. Mike tried for a last ditch [Festering Goblin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Festering+Goblin), but Gary had [Akroma's Blessing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%27s+Blessing) to give all his creatures protection from black and attacked for the win.

**Final Result:** Gary Wise defeated Mike Turian 2 – 0, eliminating Turian from Top 8 contention.
