Day 1 Top 100 Metagame Breakdown
================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on December 4, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











As many as 441 players had made Day 2. Because finding a single decklist in the big pile of all 1,456 lists which had entered the tournament takes almost a minute each, we had to settle, as usual, for a breakdown of the Top 100 players at the end of Day 1. At Grand Prix Madrid, this top-table metagame looked like this:

 White-Blue Flash and Black-Green Delirium form exactly 50% of the metagame at the top tables. Red-Green Aetherworks Marvel and Mardu Vehicles claim the next 32%. All of this is roughly in line with the metagame analysis, matchup matrix, and equilibria that I provided in my ["Setting the Stage" piece](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpmad16/setting-scene-standard-grand-prix-madrid-2016-12-03) earlier on Saturday, so there are no big surprises here.



For these "Big Four", the following things stood out to me after glancing over the Top 100 decklists.


* **Black-Green Delirium**—There was not a lot of innovation; most lists looked very much alike and largely similar to last month.
* **White-Blue Flash**—There were some minor tweaks: I saw several lists with [Spell Shrivel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Shrivel) main deck (to complement [Revolutionary Rebuff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revolutionary+Rebuff)) and also I spotted [Key to the City](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Key+to+the+City), [Essence Flux](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Flux), or [Elder Deep-Fiend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elder+Deep-Fiend) in certain main decks.
* **Red-Green Aetherworks Marvel**—The last few flex slots in the main deck sometimes featured innovative choices, such as [Tireless Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tireless+Tracker), [Nahiri, the Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nahiri%2C+the+Harbinger), and [Linvala, the Preserver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Linvala%2C+the+Preserver). In the sideboard, multiple players were splashing a color, either blue for [Negate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Negate) or black for [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind).
* **Mardu Vehicles**—I didn't notice a ton of changes compared to last month's aggregate list, although several players wisely opted for [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) and/or [Key to the City](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Key+to+the+City) instead of, say, [Harnessed Lightning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harnessed+Lightning).

Beyond the "Big Four", there are plenty of alternative deck choices available. Out of the ones that are new and that we weren't already featuring in a Deck Tech piece on the Grand Prix Madrid coverage page, I was most impressed with the following two brews.
