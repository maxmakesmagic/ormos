I, Sage
=======



 Posted in **Feature**
 on August 5, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_240.png)
By Mark L. Gottlieb











Greetings from the Arctic hinterlands! To really get into the spirit of *Ice Age* Week, Scott Johns sent me trekking through the merciless icescape at the top of the world. Thanks, Scott! This really is a refreshing change from all that pesky August warmth and not-freezing-off-my-toes that was, frankly, getting a bit monotonous. I've been not freezing off my toes all my life, so I guess it was time for a change. While I'm up here, I've been inventing games like Polar Ball (bop the polar bear in the nose with a snowball = +5 points, get your arm mauled off = -15 points) and What Does the Stuff in the Thermometer Taste Like? But my real purpose in being here was to find the three *Ice Age* Sages, and I'm happy to report that my mission has been accomplished. Stay tuned as we rap about three prisony, lockish decks based around combos that hinge on *Ice Age* cards.

### Do You Feel a Draft?

The first Sage I found, chilling out in his igloo, was O'Shea Jackson. I beseeched him… er, besought him… well, I asked him some questions.

**Mark**: Hi!  
**O'Shea**: That's not a question.  
**Mark**: Oh. So… What wisdom can you share with me, O wise one?  
**O'Shea**: Don't bother with [Krovikan Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krovikan+Vampire).  
**Mark**: I was kinda looking in its direction for a deck. Back in the day, it was always way too difficult to net creatures with it. But now I can slap [Viridian Longbow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viridian+Longbow) on it while [Death Pits of Rath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Pits+of+Rath) is in play. Bingo, bango, I steal my opponent's creatures. [Escape Routes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Escape+Routes), or some other sort of bounce, can rescue my Vampire in case any damage is sent towards it. Even better, if I bounce my Vampire after it exsanguinates a creature but before that creature comes back from the dead at the end of the turn, the “lose control” clause won't be in effect and I can keep my new undead minion forever. What's wrong with that?  
**O'Shea**: Sure, that's a good plan. But don't use that Vampire. As will happen over an 11-year evolution, costs and power curves have been adjusted and redefined. [Soul Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Collector) is strictly better than [Krovikan Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krovikan+Vampire) in five different ways.  
**Mark**: Five!   
**O'Shea**: …  
**Mark**: Oh, wait. Let me try that again. Five?  
**O'Shea**: They're both ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) Vampires with nearly identical reanimation abilities. But [Soul Collector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Collector) has +0/+1, it has flying, it has morph, the dead creature returns immediately, and you keep that creature permanently.  
**Mark**: Maybe I don't want the creature to return immediately. Maybe I want the creature to return later so I can [Tremor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tremor) or Wrath during that window of time while it's not on the table.  
**O'Shea**: Uh, yeah. Sure.  
**Mark**: What else is strictly worse in *Ice Age* than in later sets?  
**O'Shea**: Lots of stuff. Plenty of the early cantrips, like [Flare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flare). [Altar of Bone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Altar+of+Bone) is painful compared to [Eladamri's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eladamri%27s+Call). [Soldevi Machinist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soldevi+Machinist) is no [Vedalken Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Engineer), though the creature types are different so it's not a strict upgrade.  
**Mark**: Is anything better in *Ice Age*?  
**O'Shea**: Sure. Costs were still new, so fluctuations from what we now consider the norm happened on both sides. Just look at [Rime Dryad](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rime+Dryad) next to [Norwood Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Norwood+Ranger) or [Woodland Druid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Druid). Crazy!  
**Mark**: Do you have a deck for me?  
**O'Shea**: You know it. It's Total War. And when I'm finished, it's gonna be a bloodbath.

![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mirrodin%27s+Core)
















Parts of this deck have existed for years, and some elements even predate *Ice Age*. Back in Alpha, pairing [Icy Manipulator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Icy+Manipulator) with [Nettling Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettling+Imp) was almost as good a way to ice a creature as pairing it with [Royal Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Royal+Assassin). Force your opponent's creature to attack, then tap it so it couldn't attack. It would die of shame at the end of the turn. *Ice Age* reintroduced Icy and gave us a new [Nettling Imp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nettling+Imp)—one that could untap [Zuran Spellcaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Spellcaster) if it had nothing better to do.

![Total War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Total+War)*Ice Age* also gave us [Total War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Total+War). This red enchantment basically said that if any of your creatures attacked, all the rest of them were automatically Nettled: They better attack too, or they'll croak. That could combo with either pesky Imp to force all of your opponent's creatures to attack… which doesn't necessarily help you. Enter [Silent Arbiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silent+Arbiter). (You could also use [Dueling Grounds](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dueling+Grounds), but if you're going to go 5-color, I can think of better plans.) [Norritt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Norritt) forces one of your opponent's creatures to foolishly charge into battle. The Arbiter forbids the rest of his creatures to attack as well. (Trust me. Or at least trust Rule 500.2.) Then [Total War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Total+War) kills the rest of his creatures. Talk about crowd control. Note that these two combos don't work together; you have method A for destroying one creature and method B for destroying all but one creature. If you have [Total War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Total+War), [Norritt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Norritt)ize something, then tap that something, your opponent is off the hook for that combat phase… though you still take out whatever you poked with [Norritt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Norritt).[Varchild's War-Riders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Varchild%27s+War-Riders) are in the deck to make sure your opponent has some creatures you can send to their doom this way. Also, giving him some 1/1 tokens makes the [Total War](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Total+War) combo much better than if he would otherwise only have, say, a horde of 6/6 creatures. And the War-Riders and the Arbiter are good buddies. There are various alternates and redundancies built into the deck; you can try to build it with whichever pieces you have. Its main weakness is that almost all of its creatures are tiny. [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) is not your friend. [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) is not your friend. [Tremor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tremor) stole your girlfriend and ran over your dog.

**Mark**: Was anything *not* moved to blue?  
**Tracy**: Green's direct damage and permanent destruction didn't wind up in blue, but Sage #3 will have more about that. Oh, wait, those cards are from *Alliances*, not *Ice Age*. Never mind.  
**Mark**: Any other oddities besides [Temporal Distortion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temporal+Distortion), [Annex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Annex), [Splintering Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splintering+Wind), and [Tornado](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tornado)?

This is a lock deck, and although it's blue-white, it relies on the white part for the countering! [Order of the Sacred Torch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Order+of+the+Sacred+Torch) + [Vodalian Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vodalian+Mystic) means you can counter any one spell a turn for the cost of 1 life. Throw in [Rule of Law](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rule+of+Law) (or [Arcane Laboratory](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcane+Laboratory)), and your opponent only gets to attempt one spell a turn!

Replace the Mystic with [Blind Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Seer) and you can do the same thing for ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) and 1 life. It's more expensive, but [Blind Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind+Seer) is better in the long game than the Mystic. It's more versatile: It can hit permanents, which interacts well with your *Ice Age*-era Circle of Protection, your [Light of Day](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Light+of+Day), your [Hibernation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hibernation), and your [Hydroblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hydroblast). It's bigger: Beating down with its 3/3 bulk is your victory condition… after you remove all your opponent's permanents from the board. And you can activate it multiple times: This is key if you need to counter a spell on your turn, then untap your Order and counter a spell on your opponent's turn. Untapping one permanent is doable thanks to [Puppeteer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Puppeteer) and [Staff of Domination](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staff+of+Domination), but untapping both the Order and the Mystic is a tall order.

It's not a total lock yet, though, because countering all your opponent's spells will still chew away at your life total. But the Staff can replenish that life, as can [Jasmine Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jasmine+Seer) or [Convalescence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Convalescence). [Jasmine Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jasmine+Seer), like many cards in this deck, can benefit from the loving touch of [Mind Bend](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Bend). And yes, in an earlier iteration of this deck, [Balduvian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balduvian+Shaman) was in there. If you've got the moxie to include it, I tip my cap to you.

Why does the deck include snow-covered lands? Because it's still *Ice Age* Week, dangit!

### Into the Chasm

Finally, after trudging around the icy deathscape for so long that I was seeing the art for [Touch of Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Touch+of+Death) and [Dance of the Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dance+of+the+Dead) trailing me in anticipation (I probably should have brought some mittens), I stumbled upon the third and wisest Sage. No, not Santa Claus—that guy wouldn't know a combo if it bit him on his nose like a cherry—Rob Van Winkle.

**Mark**: Quick, before I freeze—do you have any *Ice Age* combos?  
**Rob**: Hmm… that set has [Necropotence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necropotence), [Illusions of Grandeur](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Illusions+of+Grandeur), [Enduring Renewal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Enduring+Renewal)… nope, no combos to speak of.  
**Mark**: What are the best cards in the set?  
**Rob**: You can't go wrong with [Ice Cauldron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ice+Cauldron). Or [Balduvian Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Balduvian+Shaman). They were created thanks to a licensing deal with LensCrafters, If you like to squint and say “huh?” you can't go wrong with those two.  
**Mark**: Why are you tormenting me?  
**Rob**: 'Cause my style's like a chemical spill.  
**Mark**: For a sage, you don't seem very wise.  
**Rob**: Ever gotten frostbite on your temporal lobe? I don't recommend it.  
**Mark**: That explains a lot. Look, can you help me out with anything?  
**Rob**: How about a long-standing game mechanic introduced in *Ice Age*?  
**Mark**: Yeah! That sounds perfect!  
**Rob**: Great. Cumulative upkeep it is.  
**Mark**: Uuuhhhhrrrggg…  
**Rob**: Hey, cumulative upkeep isn't as bad as it once was. It got a lot better when [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit) came out because now you can remove age counters from your permanents. But that's nothing compared to [Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub). A lot of the cards with cumulative upkeep are really powerful, and the only thing that keeps them in check—or even keeps them bad—is that they're going to quickly become too expensive to keep around. Erase the words “cumulative upkeep” from them, and you can do some nasty things. You want a near lock?  
**Mark**: Always.  
**Rob**: [Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub) and [Glacial Chasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Chasm).  
**Mark**: Yikes.  
**Rob**: You want another?  
**Mark**: Yes, sir, Mr. Wise Sage, sir.  
**Rob**: [Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub) and [Ritual of Subdual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ritual+of+Subdual).

[Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub) + [Glacial Chasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Chasm) means that although you never get another combat phase, you can't ever be damaged again. [Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub) + [Ritual of Subdual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ritual+of+Subdual) means that no player can produce colored mana for the rest of the game unless he's got some non-land mana sources. You do. Does your opponent? Your opponent can still play artifacts (which I hear are pretty popular these days), so the game isn't over yet, but it'll be hard to break your lock without access to [Naturalize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize), [Vindicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vindicate), or any other colored spell.

If this deck doesn't find a way to manage your cumulative upkeep costs, it will roll over. That's why [Ancestral Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Knowledge) is in there; that'll get you to an [Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub) or, as a backup plan, a [Power Conduit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Power+Conduit). Note that you want *both* of those artifacts to get best use out of [Tornado](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tornado), which will annihilate your opponent's side of the table. Yeah. Green does that. With a [Glacial Chasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Chasm) in play, you don't need to worry about pain from Talismans or pain lands… or [Squallmonger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squallmonger), which is you primary road to victory. It's perfect: You can't attack, remember, so you need direct damage. In green or blue. That mainly costs colorless mana because you've got a [Ritual of Subdual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ritual+of+Subdual) out. The [Squallmonger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squallmonger) way is just flat-out faster than the [Zuran Spellcaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zuran+Spellcaster) way. You can splash another color (I've got a [Shivan Gorge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Gorge) and the *Ice Age*tastic, [Eon Hub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eon+Hub)-loving [Infernal Denizen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infernal+Denizen) in there), but [Squallmonger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squallmonger) + [Glacial Chasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Chasm) is just way too much fun.

The alternate victory condition is, when you're ready, to blow up your own Chasm with [Tornado](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tornado) and attack with fat, cumulative upkeepless creatures. If you want to do that, definitely throw in some [Aboroth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aboroth)s.

I trust that today we all learned a little something about **Magic**, a little something about *Ice Age,* a little something about life, and a little something about setting your own foot on fire for warmth. I know I did. Until next week, have fun with *Ice Age*.
