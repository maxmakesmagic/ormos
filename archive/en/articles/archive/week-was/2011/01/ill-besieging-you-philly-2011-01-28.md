I'll Besieging You in Philly
============================



 Posted in [The Week That Was](/en/articles/columns/the-week-that-was-archive)
 on January 28, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/author-photo_BDM.jpg)
By Brian David-Marshall




 Brian David-Marshall is a New York–based game designer who has been involved with Magic since 1994, when he started organizing tournaments and ran a Manhattan game store. Since then, he has been a judge, a player, and one of the longest-tenured columnists on DailyMTG.com, as he enters his second decade writing for the site. He is also the Pro Tour Historian and one of the commentators for the Pro Tour. 







Buried under almost twenty inches of snow it would be easy to succumb to cabin (co-op?) fever. Fortunately I have ample food supplies, a wife who looks nothing like Shelley Duval, and the full *Mirrodin Besieged* [Visual Spoiler](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/article.aspx?x=mtg/tcg/mirrodinbesieged/spoiler) to keep me sane. I am going to be in Philadelphia this Saturday as a guest of my old business partners at [Gray Matter Conventions](http://www.graymatterconventions.com/MirrodinBesieged/pa.html) for the *Mirrodin Besieged* [Prerelease](http://www.wizards.com/magic/tcg/events.aspx?x=mtgcom/events/prerelease-facts). I am going to be the gunslinger and if you beat me with your Sealed Deck—or a Standard, Extended, or Commander deck—you can win a booster pack. (It also means I will be having a pastrami sandwich from Reading Market Terminal—something you can sample yourself when you come to **Magic** Weekend in Philly this fall!)


So I have been listening to the sounds of snow shovels and thinking about what cards from *Mirrodin Besieged* I am hoping to open, I can add to my Constructed decks, and what might be able to earn a precious slot in my Commander deck.


And before you ask—Phyrexian.


I am going to be expecting to see a lot of turn-one [Ardent Recruit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ardent+Recruit)s in people's sealed decks. What percentage of them will manage to get fully 'crafted? That is the big question about this card and I suspect that it will not be too often. With [Auriok Sunchaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Sunchaser)—its closest comparison from *Scars of Mirrodin*—you find yourself using your first turn on a Spellbomb or a piece of Equipment so you can get your 'craft on ASAP. You will get a fully grown Recruit on turn two once in a [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)'s birthday. Even on turn three it is going to be tough to do since it will require playing two artifacts in one turn on one of those turns.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw127_metalcraft.jpg)Of course with the release of this set there is some added tension to metalcraft since you can get there mid combat. In the *Scars*-only format if someone attacked their [Auriok Sunchaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auriok+Sunchaser) into your mana Myr on turn two it meant either [Seize the Initiative](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seize+the+Initiative) or—more likely—a willingness to trade. I am going to be very leery of that trade now that [Master's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master%27s+Call) is in the mix ... although the actual master's call is when you block and they don't have the instant-speed supply of artifact creatures to push them over the top. [Master's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master%27s+Call) also means you will occasionally need to make some otherwise bad blocks or get killed by instant-speed metalcraft.



![Master's Call](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Master%27s+Call)

 

Your opponent does not even need to show you white mana to threaten to boost their artifact count. With four, or five, mana a [Shimmer Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmer+Myr) could come down and then instant-speed Spellbombs could rain down after it. A lot of attention is being paid to the Phyrexian side of Limited at the moment but it feels like metalcrafty players will have plenty of new resources at their disposal.



![Thrun, the last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Thrun%2C+the+last+Troll)

 

Ever since [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) has been previewed I have been looking for ways to cope with him in whatever formats I happen to play. I have already been looking at -1/-1 counters and proliferate as one way to whittle him down to size (and before you go creating a forum account, proliferate does not target) but [Contagion Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Engine) might be showing up late for the party and how many times will I be able to get Thrun to attack into my infect creatures? [Choking Fumes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choking+Fumes) looks like a fine way to get my proliferate started and I could easily see it becoming a Standard sideboard card for white-blue control decks.

Cards like [Sandstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sandstorm) and [Simoon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simoon) have always found a place in sideboards against weenie decks but [Choking Fumes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choking+Fumes) does something unique that allows it to multitask against bigger creatures, weenies, and, in the case of Thrun, shrouded enemies. Mike Flores [recently wrote about](/en/articles/archive/top-decks/focus-white-blue-control-2010-11-18) a White-Blue Control deck by Kurt Spiess that abused proliferate. With [Contagion Clasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp) already a part of the deck it is not insane to think that a deck like this could play [Choking Fumes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Choking+Fumes) out of its sideboard. Even landfall creatures like [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) and [Plated Geopede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plated+Geopede) will die to this once their bonuses expire at the end of the turn and [Kuldotha Rebirth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kuldotha+Rebirth)s will end tragically often leaving only a 1/1 [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide) in their wake.


Another card I have been looking out for answers to is [Blightsteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightsteel+Colossus)—[Leonin Relic-Warder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder) is a cheap response to that question. This card is essentially a legged [Journey to Nowhere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Journey+to+Nowhere) that can only target enchantments and artifacts. I fully expect this guy to see play in formats ranging from forty cards all the way up to one hundred cards. It deals with indestructibles, springs creatures from under [Arrest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrest)s, and recalculates the math on metalcraft. Plus it attacks for 2! What more could you want from a creature? Well they don't call me Greedy-M for nothing. Anytime I see a permanent that has an ability to remove something upon entering the battlefield and returns it upon leaving, I look for ways to bounce that permanent in response to the first trigger.

Imagine for a moment that you have a [Blightsteel Colossus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blightsteel+Colossus) in play and I have no creatures, but four mana. I can play my [Leonin Relic-Warder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder) and, with the "enters the battlefield" trigger on the stack, play [Disperse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disperse) to return the Relic-Warder to my hand. Now the "leaves the battlefield" trigger goes on the stack only there is nothing to return to the battlefield. When the "enters the battlefield" trigger finally resolves I can remove the Colossus and it will not be able to come back for the remainder of the game. And I still have the Relic-Warder in hand. Don't worry, you still win the game with your twelve mana to my four—but I have the moral victory to comfort me.



![Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Consecrated+Sphinx)

 

[Mind's Eye](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind%27s+Eye) is already a Commander staple card and I can't imagine that blue mages won't be scrambling this weekend to get their hands on a shiny copy of [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx) for their decks. In a four-player game—assuming it is not insta-killed—you can draw at least six cards by the time the turn comes back around to you once you play the Sphinx. I can even see this guy getting played in sixty-card decks with [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren). "We'll both draw" will become a much more commonly uttered phrase in the wake of this card. You will draw three cards to one with the plus ability of Jace and then another two cards on their turn. Who cares if they kill it at that point when you have just drawn five cards to two?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw127_sphinx.jpg)Unless you were looking to sneak in an extra 2 points of damage with your [Kor Spiritdancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Spiritdancer) it is highly unlikely that you would ever [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) your own creature. I imagine that happening a lot more frequently with [Corrupted Conscience](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupted+Conscience). Imagine a game against a player piloting Soul Sisters—the life gain deck that was created by Conley Woods prior to U.S. Nationals. It is quite conceivable that the only way you can beat the deck is by poisoning them with your [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) over two turns. Or in a proliferate deck getting in for one hit with your own creature and then finishing them off with your [Contagion Clasp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contagion+Clasp).


If you are a traditionalist you can also just use it to steal their best guy. If that is your kind of thing. Personally, I can't wait to steal an [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan). Once you deal them three poison counters with an attack they are in full blown [The Abyss](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=The%2BAbyss) mode for the next few turns although it is unlikely that the game is going to last that long. [Mind Control](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Control) has already seen sideboard action in some high profile decks and I expect that this card will make an appearance at some point as well.



![Steel Sabotage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Steel+Sabotage)

 

[Steel Sabotage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Sabotage) is one of my favorites—and one of the best named—commons in this set. I talked earlier about the mind games you can play with metalcraft now that you can get it as an instant but this card gives the player across the table a chance to get inside your head as well. Imagine that you play your third artifact and your opponent, with only one blue mana available, does nothing and you get your metalcraft on. Do you attack since they did not show you a [Steel Sabotage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steel+Sabotage)? Or are they sitting back on the Sabotage to bounce something mid-combat and essentially -2/-2 your team? There is a lot of play in this card and I look forward to seeing how the Pros use it come the Limited rounds of Pro Tour Paris.



![Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith)

 

[Black Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Sun%27s+Zenith): See Thrun, see Thrun die. Die, Thrun, die!! Hmmm ... perhaps the snow and thinking about **Magic** cards all day is getting to me after all. All play and no work, etc. ... Or maybe it is just the ferocity of the black cards.



![Go For the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Go+For+the+Throat)

 

How good is [Go for the Throat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Go+for+the+Throat)? This card kills pretty much everything short of a [Wurmcoil Engine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) that you want to kill. It is a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) that kills black creatures! Drives a stake in the heart of [Vampire Nighthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk), returns a [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) to whence it came, and still kills the vast majority of blue, white, red, and green creatures to boot. [Grasp of Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grasp+of+Darkness) saw main-deck play in Faeries this past weekend in Atlanta but I have to imagine that it will be quickly displaced by this card. This is one of the first uncommons I want to lock up my playset of on the weekend and as such will be first-picking it every draft I can. You know, just for my collection.



![Virulent Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Virulent+Wound)

 

A more subtle removal spell that I love in this set is [Virulent Wound](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Virulent+Wound). It is ideal for killing turn two mana Myr and a great way to deal with pesky [Ichorclaw Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr). I also like that the -1/-1 counter sticks around and is not just a debuff until the end of the turn. It also gives you a non-combat way to get the poison/proliferate engine going.


Flores and I have talked about a Standard black proliferate deck that poisons people to death with only an [Ichor Rats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Rats) to get things started. With the ability for this card to kill [Arbor Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf), Llaonwar Elf, or Bird of Paradise while advancing a victory condition this just became a hypothetical sideboard card in our hypothetical deck.


The *Mirrodin Besieged* Bingo board would almost certainly have "Killing your opponent with [Burn the Impure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burn+the+Impure) when they have no infect creatures" as a square if:



> 
> 1. It would fit in a square.
> 2. The Bingo board existed.
> 


I am sure just mentioning this is going to cost me at least one match during spellslinging but keep in mind that you can sneak in those last 3 points of damage with [Burn the Impure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burn+the+Impure) if you use [Tainted Strike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tainted+Strike) or [Phyresis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyresis) on your opponent's creature.



![Into the Core](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Into+the+Core)

 

I cannot figure out how good [Into the Core](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Into+the+Core) is for Limited. I mean it is obviously quite good but is it insane, first pick-run-around-the-room-and-get-an-unsportsmanlike-conduct-warning good? Probably somewhere in between and it is for sure something you need to plan for on both sides of the spell. Metalcraft decks will have little choice but to get wrecked by this but other decks will have to think about this much the same way they do [Turn to Slag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turn+to+Slag). The difference with this card is that there is no way to protect your artifacts other than to only give them one target. Since the card needs to have two targets to be cast that is your only way to steer your opponent. And not even indestructible artifacts are safe!


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw127_vig.jpg)I have already carved out slots in my [Momir Vig, Simic Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Momir%2BVig%252C%2BSimic%2BVisionary) Commander deck for a handful of cards from *Mirrodin Besieged* and most of them are green. [Blue Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blue+Sun%27s+Zenith) (so long [Stroke of Genius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stroke+of+Genius)), [Consecrated Sphinx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consecrated+Sphinx), and possibly [Treasure Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Mage) are the blue cards I am looking at but the first card to go into the deck is going to be [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith). So many of the creatures in my deck draw a card, steal a creature, become a copy of a creature, or counter a spell when they enter the battlefield that [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) will truly shine here. (Again, no need to register for the forums, I am fully expecting to have my [Vedalken Orrery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Orrery) in play when I cast this to find my [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake).) One of the most frustrating cards in my deck for opposing Commanders has been [Ixidron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixidron) but my brief fantasy of being able to fetch it with [Green's Suns Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green%27s+Suns+Zenith) was cut short by actually reading the card. I do have a [Dyrad Arbor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dyrad+Arbor) in the deck which I can now fetch on turn one and that seems awesome.



![Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)

 

Mark my words. Despite his vows to not play green decks anymore, [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith) and [Lead the Stampede](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lead+the+Stampede) will push a certain [Top Decks](http://www.wizards.com/magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?tag=TopDecks&description=Swimming%20With%20Sharks) author to build a [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)-powered sixty in the near future. At last count there were thirty-three creatures in my Commander deck and the underwhelming [Seer's Sundial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seer%27s+Sundial) may be replaced by this green draw spell. More likely though is that I will be replacing the Sundial with [Praetor's Counsel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Praetor%27s+Counsel) since creature-heavy decks will often find their graveyards very well stocked in Commander with so many board-sweeping effects running around.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/stf/stf127_tez.jpg)[Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas) is one of my favorite cards in the new set and is sure to be the focus of multiple deck brews for me over the next few weeks. I have been looking at [Throne of Geth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Throne+of+Geth) as a potential Constructed playable card for the past few weeks and Tezzeret is one of the reasons why. Throne is one of the only ways to proliferate for free and lets you get an ultimate-sized Tezzeret on the turn you play him. I can even just sacrifice the Throne but I would much prefer to do it to an [Ichor Wellspring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichor+Wellspring) and draw a card.



![Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas)

 

One of the interesting aspects of Tezzeret is that while he can let you attack with your noncreature artifacts he can also turn existing artifact creatures into bigger versions. It is not as easy to sit back and take a hit from an [Ichorclaw Myr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ichorclaw+Myr) when it is becoming a 5/5 infect creature. Another thing about Tezz is that he does not necessarily need to do it to your artifacts. Imagine playing some as-of-yet nonexistent Blue-Black Vampires list. Your opponent is threatening you with [Mimic Vat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) and can untap and kill your creatures and then copy your best one—maybe even your [Vampire Hexmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Hexmage) which will kill your planeswalker. Orrr ... you could animate that [Mimic Vat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mimic+Vat) with the Agent of Bolas and then use [Consuming Vapors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consuming+Vapors) or [Gatekeeper of Malakir](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gatekeeper+of+Malakir) to make your opponent sacrifice it. Unlikely, but something to keep in mind when playing with Tezz.



![Shriekhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Shriekhorn)

 

[Shriekhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekhorn) at common means that decking will become a real way of winning drafts assuming you can find ways to proliferate. Otherwise you are looking at six cards per [Shriekhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekhorn) without the aid of [Glinthawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glinthawk)s and [Glimmerpoint Stag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmerpoint+Stag)s to reset them. The number of [Shriekhorn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekhorn)s I expect to have played against me this weekend is well into the double digits. How good is the strategy? I will let you know when I tally up the remaining packs in my box at the end of Saturday.


If you happen to find yourself in Philly on Saturday be sure to come by and say hello and maybe play a game of **Magic** with me!


### Five Cards from Atlanta


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/twtw/twtw127_card01.jpg)When we look back at [Grand Prix Atlanta](/en/articles/archive/event-coverage/grand-prix-atlanta-2011-white-round-1-pairings-2010-12-09) it will be impossible to do so without talking about the card [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift)—and it has become impossible to talk about it without mentioning it's partner in crime [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen). They were in three of the Top 8 decks and all three of those decks advanced to the semifinals. The Finals were ultimately decided in a showdown between two different takes on how you can build the deck with Ben Stark opting for a more traditional red-green version while first-time champion Jason Ford played a version with blue that included [Oracle of Mul Daya](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oracle+of+Mul+Daya) and was able to [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) key ramp spells in the [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) mirror.
