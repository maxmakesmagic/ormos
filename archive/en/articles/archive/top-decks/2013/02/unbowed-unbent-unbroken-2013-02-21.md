Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken
=========================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on February 21, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







Pro Tour *Gatecrash* is in the books and Tom Martell, innovator of [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) and iconic multiple superteam veteran, is [our Pro Tour champion](/en/events/coverage/ptgtc13). Tom didn't disappoint; once again, he packed four [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) in his seventy-five. And the Top 8 as a whole didn't disappoint, either. Pro Tour *Gatecrash* showed us all manner of [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)s. We saw the resurgent return of Esper Control and another take at Bant Control with an off-color utility land... and Bant's pilot was herself a history-maker. Pro Tour *Gatecrash*—thanks to Melissa DeTora—gave us our first Top 8 appearance in a Pro Tour by a female player.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td235_protour.jpg)Just wow.


Wows all around.


Here's how Pro Tour *Gatecrash*'s Top 8 lined up:

The Aristocrats is a difficult deck to categorize. It has no single plan and little coherent redundancy; instead, this is a deck that can mold its plan to different stages of the game and against each individual opponent. If there is one overarching concept, it is—as Sam Black reported to [Brian David-Marshall](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Archive.aspx?author=Brian%20David-Marshall)—the abuse of **Magic**'s most powerful ability: the ability to sacrifice your own creatures.


This format is becoming in large part defined by the *Gatecrash* threat [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). The ability to sacrifice your own creatures allows you to block a [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) but get your blocker out of the way before damage is dealt (allowing you to avoid the Reckoner's fearsome damage-sharing ability). [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) can sacrifice creatures to stay in play against the sweep spells of control deck, and [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) can do the same to sneak past blockers to close damage later in the game.

Many of the creatures in The Aristocrats do double duty or provide multiple bodies for the self-sacrifice strategy. [Doomed Traveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) and [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) both give you multiple 1/1 creatures; [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest), multiple 5/5s. Again, the ability to sacrifice your own creatures comes into play with [Skirsdag High Priest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skirsdag+High+Priest)... morbid requires a death, but in this case the payback can be huge and flying.

The Aristocrats is first and foremost an aggressive deck. It can start on [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) like a conventional Humans deck... and follow up with [Knight of Infamy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy). I wonder if this is the first time those two highly played cards have ever met in a single deck. [Knight of Infamy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Infamy) buffs [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) with its +1/+1 counters ability... and when the Champion rumbles into the red zone, the Knight can give the Champion an additional exalted +1/+1.

The same [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) that set up your first-turn ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227125855im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/white.gif) and second-turn ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif) can give you a late-game ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130159im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/red.gif) for [Zealous Conscripts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts)... possibly a game-ending one against a powerful blocker or near-ultimate Planeswalker. New card [Orzhov Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Charm) makes the strongest possible debut (a Pro Tour win)... and can showcase many different parts of the veritable buffalo. Larsson's deck has WU Flash elements—including piggybacking the creature base, in large part—but, above and beyond the inclusion of [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) and [Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act), it has a potentially even more powerful combination: lucky charms.


If you can assemble [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm), [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm), and [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), any damage catalyst can potentially end the game... sort of.


First, use [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm) to give your creatures lifelink.


Next, use [Boros Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Charm) to make your creatures indestructible.




|  |  |

Now, find a way to deal damage to your [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). An [Izzet Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) would work... whatever damage source. The first two Charm catalysts are instants, so you can just rumble in and maybe draw a block, then apply both Charms before anything dies.


Your [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) will have lifelink, meaning every time it deals damage, you can gain that much life.


Your [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) will be indestructible, meaning that it doesn't matter how much damage it takes, it will not die.


So say your opponent blocked with an [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf). That puts a point on the Reckoner. The Reckoner now gets to put a point somewhere else. *What about to itself?*

Boros Reckoner
 | 
Arbor Elf
 |

Reckoner deals a point to Reckoner, you gain a life... and the Reckoner now has *another* point to distribute. Rinse and repeat until you are tired of gaining life.


To infinity and beyond!

Gerry Thompson continued his long string of successful play with RWU Flash decks with this *Gatecrash* update. Most interesting to me is that Gerry elected to eschew the [Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) combo... and the only Charm he played was the stock-flash [Azorius Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Charm). No lucky charms combo, either.


Instead, Gerry played a single [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre).



![Harvest Pyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Harvest+Pyre)

 

Gerry could put twenty or more cards into his graveyard and then send the [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) at his [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), killing the opponent with the damage replication.


[Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) takes the place of both [Runechanter's Pike](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Runechanter%27s+Pike) and [Moorland Haunt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) in this deck, giving the deck a path to inevitability that can also be used early on as a regular point-removal spell. This version of RWU plays four copies of [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour). [Thought Scour](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour), of course, fuels the [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre)... it doesn't matter if you accidentally flip the [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) into your graveyard (or use it for defense early); [Snapcaster Mage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) can bring it back when you need it.

### Jund Decks


Jund produced two Top 8 performances at Pro Tour *Gatecrash*, presenting any number of unambiguously good cards, tons of flexible removal, and few if any nightmare matchups. On balance, the sometimes-overwhelming concentration of creature removal in Standard Jund decks will often put a creature-reliant opponent into a spot where he or she has serious problems winning.

Owen Turtenwald added quite the feather to his already quite-decorated hat with this innovative look at Jund. You might think there isn't much to say about the archetype... but Owen managed to play three copies of [Arbor Elf](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arbor+Elf)!


The additional jump in mana, especially when combined with [Overgrown Tomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb), can give him a turn-two [Liliana of the Veil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil).

Bant Control decks splashing a fourth color for a land-based finisher are not new to this Standard. For months, Bant + [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard) was one of the premier decks in the format. With [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kessig%2BWolf%2BRun) Bant, we have a version that instead touches red to activate [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run), in a shell that plays substantially more creatures than we have seen in Bant colors.


What should be obvious is that this deck really wants to gain life. In addition to [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk), we see main-deck [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) (and a fourth [Centaur Healer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Centaur+Healer) in the sideboard). Together, these various X/3 creatures can gain a fair amount of life and combine beautifully with the blink ETB of [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel).

Having lots of creatures in general is effective with [Kessig Wolf Run](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kessig+Wolf+Run). Any of the creatures can potentially go lethal long, even the tiny 1/3 [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas).


[Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) is actually at its weakest in this build. There are only twenty dance partners in this deck, meaning that your [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) will miss a fair amount of the time; that said, it is necessary to slow down the various cheap drops in opposing creature decks. The goal here is to use [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) to gain an advantage, maybe draw two to four extra cards, gain some life, live long enough to draw another [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), and then really blow out the game with a second big X-spell.

[Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kessig%2BWolf%2BRun) Bant does many of the really powerful, desirable things you want to do in this format. It might not have any kind of [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) (and therefore none of the shiny new [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) combinations) but it can unlock the [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) + [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) achievement, chain [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation) into [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation), and gain tons of life doing so over time. After boards you can take over a game with powerhouses like [Gisela, Blade of Goldnight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gisela%2C+Blade+of+Goldnight) or make all that lifegain even better with [Rhox Faithmender](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rhox+Faithmender).

This is certainly not the first Esper Control deck we have seen in post-*Return to Ravnica* Standard play, but there is some amazing new technology here that can be very helpful for control players moving forward.


First of all, like with [Kessig Wolf Run](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Kessig%2BWolf%2BRun) Bant, we see a higher concentration of creatures than you might be used to seeing in an Esper Control deck. Many such decks have played no creatures at all (winning with just their quartet of [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard)s). All nine of Stark's creatures gain an advantage when hitting the table, from the two-drop Wizards and the extra instants and sorceries they bring along with them, to [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), who gives you a Wizard-rebuy or can at least mug an attacker mid-combat—like a [Simian Grunts](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Grunts) of old.

Having all those creatures allowed Stark to get an advantage with [Dramatic Rescue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dramatic+Rescue): lifegain on top of the ETB card advantage (allowing for further card advantage).


Most of the rest of the deck includes defensive instants—permission, creature removal—and card draw. Ben played the full four [Think Twice](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Think+Twice)s and the full four [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)s. Between having four [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)s and four copies of [Nephalia Drownyard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nephalia+Drownyard), this deck potentially starts with a huge natural advantage against other control decks.


And then there is this:



![Planar Cleansing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Planar+Cleansing)

 

It is no secret that control decks need a sweeper; we are not surprised at seeing a quartet of [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict)s, for instance. Most such decks have been supplementing with six-drop [Terminus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus)... [Planar Cleansing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing) is something else altogether, though.


[Planar Cleansing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Planar+Cleansing) deals with all the problem permanents that opposing midrange decks use to try to get past the relatively thin permission of Standard control decks. It can take out every [Garruk, Primal Hunter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk%2C+Primal+Hunter) and [Staff of Nin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Staff+of+Nin) intended to keep pace with [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation). Every one, and all at the same time.


Pro Tour *Gatecrash* has certainly done its job ushering in a new set of different, powerful, and interesting decks that showcase familiar angles like Jund or control... but with new twists and tools. The beatdown decks like The Aristocrats offer shifting and complex lines of play, and [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) challenges us with a card that can be played either in beatdown or control... that looks like it will be even more format-defining than [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) before it.


With these changes and exciting new cards and decks, we are left with lots of questions...


What is going to happen next?


What kinds of decks will be successful?


How will players learn to cope with (or further adopt) [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner)?


What are you going to play at [FNM](http://www.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/fnm) tomorrow night?
