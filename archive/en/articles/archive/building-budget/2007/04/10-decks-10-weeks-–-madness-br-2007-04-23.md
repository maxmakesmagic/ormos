Since this deck is going to center around the madness mechanic, we need to include two different types of cards: madness cards and madness enablers. All of the madness cards in *Time Spiral* and *Planar Chaos* have been black or red. They include:


[Big Game Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Big+Game+Hunter)  
[Brain Gorgers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Gorgers)  
[Call to the Netherworld](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld)  
[Dark Withering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Withering)  
[Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper)  
[Gorgon Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorgon+Recluse)  
[Muck Drubb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muck+Drubb)  
[Nightshade Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Assassin)  
[Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm)  
[Psychotic Episode](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychotic+Episode)


The first rule I came up with for this deck—I wouldn't play any madness card that I wouldn't be comfortable playing without being able to discard it. That's right—if I wouldn't play the card if it were missing the madness ability entirely, I wouldn't play the madness version. Why is this? Mainly, because there aren't a lot of good madness outlets in Standard right now, at least for red and black (I'll expound on this in the next section). This means that I run a good chance of having to hard-cast any given madness card over the course of a game. The main advantage of madness is that it reduces the mana cost of a card. If that card wasn't worth playing to begin with, why bother?


This immediately eliminated [Big Game Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Big+Game+Hunter), [Brain Gorgers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brain+Gorgers), [Call to the Netherworld](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+to+the+Netherworld), [Dark Withering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Withering), and [Gorgon Recluse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gorgon+Recluse) from contention. I wouldn't play a one-shot [Intrepid Hero](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intrepid+Hero), [Giant Cockroach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Cockroach), [Raise Dead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Raise+Dead), a [Dark Banishing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing)/[Seal of Doom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Doom) that costs double the mana, or [Thicket Basilisk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thicket+Basilisk) in a regular deck. This left [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper), [Muck Drubb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muck+Drubb), [Nightshade Assassin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Assassin), [Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) and [Psychotic Episode](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychotic+Episode). I have played [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt), see the Drubb as unique, love [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) and [Keening Banshee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keening+Banshee), am a huge fan of [Arrogant Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arrogant+Wurm), and wouldn't be above playing [Coercion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coercion) if [Distress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Distress) wasn't around. 


So now that I've gotten a pool of five different madness cards to choose from, I need to find ways to discard cards from my hand. The most advantageous madness enablers are ones which have an activation/other cost of discarding a card, so you can turn that disadvantage around to an advantage. Thankfully, there were plenty of these to be found in Standard right now. I culled down the list with the same rule that I made for selecting madness cards—I would not play any madness enablers that I wouldn't otherwise want to have in a deck.


This left me with a decently-sized list of contenders, including [Avatar of Discord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Discord), [Conflagrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conflagrate), [Delirium Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins), [Demonic Collusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Collusion), [Drekavac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drekavac), [Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Jaya%2BBallard%252C%2BTask%2BMage), [Kindle the Carnage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kindle+the+Carnage), [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe), [Mindless Automaton](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mindless+Automaton), [Phantasmagorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmagorian), [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), [Slate of Ancestry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slate+of+Ancestry), [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), [Trespasser il-Vec](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trespasser+il-Vec), and [Undertaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undertaker). I cut this list down further with more criterion:





1. The spell/effect that causes the discard can't cost more than three. I want to be able to play my madness cards using the madness cost reasonably early. If I buyback [Demonic Collusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Collusion) (five mana plus discarding two cards), I'd need to be at eight mana to Madness [Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) on that same turn. Why not just play [Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) to begin with at that point?


Eliminated: [Demonic Collusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demonic+Collusion), [Rakdos Guildmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Guildmage), [Slate of Ancestry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slate+of+Ancestry)
2. No fragile creatures. By fragile, I mean [Avatar of Discord](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Discord) and [Trespasser il-Vec](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trespasser+il-Vec). Look, I've played hundreds of games for this column now. Every time I've faced down one of these guys, my opponent ended up with a dead creature, and (in the case of the Avatar) down two cards. I've seen these cards be bad on the other side of the table from me, and so I'm very disinclined to play with them.

![Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Jaya+Ballard%2C+Task+Mage)This leaves [Conflagrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conflagrate), [Delirium Skeins](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delirium+Skeins), [Drekavac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drekavac), [Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaya+Ballard%2C+Task+Mage), [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe), Mindless Automation, [Phantasmagorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmagorian), [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), and [Undertaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Undertaker) as my choices for initial Madness outlets. You all know that I love Jaya Ballard, and Jaya is the most independently powerful card on this list—an Incinerate-a-turn machine that randomly hoses blue and randomly Infernos the board is a decent card. 
 In: 3 [Jaya Ballard, Task Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jaya+Ballard%2C+Task+Mage)


Sheer [Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) was an aggro deck through and through, and so to compliment the remains of that deck, I decide to go with burn and offense with this first build of B/R Madness. [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) and [Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) are the most offensively oriented of the remaining Madness cards, so I add four copies of each.


In: 4 [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper), 4 [Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm)


I also want to try out [Muck Drubb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muck+Drubb), so I put in a singleton copy. In addition, I throw in a singleton copy of [Phantasmagorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmagorian) (which might well be the hardest **Magic** card name to type in consistently, and made me want to disregard it just for the sake of having to type it over and over for this article! [Phantasmagorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmagorian)! [Phantasmagorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmagorian)!).


In: 1 [Phantasmagorian](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmagorian), 1 [Muck Drubb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muck+Drubb)


For the last few slots of the deck, I decide to try out [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox). [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) combines well with [Stingscourger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger) (I can remove a smaller creature and then kill their big guy) and [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders) (they are going away anyhow, right), and provides me an outlet for madness. I also throw in the compliment of black lands to replace the white lands in the deck.


In: 4 [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), 4 [Rakdos Carnarium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Carnarium), 8 [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).


This leaves me with seven slots left to build with. In previous builds I've run whatever signet is appropriate for the color combination. In this build, I'm going with my old favorite, [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem), in that slot. [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem) works well with [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) (it doesn't get hit), and provides me a pretty big body to hit with. I also rotate [Conflagrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conflagrate) back into the deck—it was last seen in week 3 (Aeon in Flux). It acts as early removal, and then as a late madness outlet.


In: 4 [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem), 3 [Conflagrate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Conflagrate)

Game 1: Aquacadet92 (U/B [Leyline of Singularity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Singularity)/Hunted Creatures)  
 He gets stuck on two mana, and I beat him down with [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders) and [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem). I didn't really get an opportunity to try out any of the madness cards.  
 Record: 1-0


Game 2: enema\_of\_chaos (Orzhov)  
 This game was a lot more of a test for the deck. I get out a couple of quick [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem)s. He [Faith's Fetters](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faith%27s+Fetters) one, and then drops Teysa with [Shadow Lance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadow+Lance), [Ghost Council of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Council+of+Orzhova), and [Mourning Thrull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mourning+Thrull). I [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) away the Thrull, force a chump block of the Spirit Token, [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) away the Teysa, and then come in with exactly lethal damage with [Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) and [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem) trampling over his Ghost Council.  
 Record: 2-0


[Muck Drubb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muck+Drubb) Count: 1  
[Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) Count: 1


Game 3: Sage\_GT (U/W Snow)  
 He gets a quick three creatures. I [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) and [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) two on turn three, then swing with [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem) past his one remaining creature – a [Drift of Phantasms](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drift+of+Phantasms). He drops [Rimewind Cryomancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rimewind+Cryomancer), and I [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox) again, killing his defender and allowing me to drop [Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm). [Stingscourger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stingscourger) clears the way for nine points of trample damage the following turn, finishing business.  
 Record: 3-0


Game 4: yakyak (U/W Suspend)  
 I get [Keldon Marauders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Keldon+Marauders), hit for five, and then drop a face-down Akroma. Jaya Ballard deals 6 a turn with [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) (discard the Temper to the Incinerate ability), and this allows me to untap and win with Akroma on turn six.  
 Record: 4-0


[Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) Count: 2 (remember, I only have one in the deck!)


Game 5: PTrenkle (Slivers with Duals)  
 He drops down [Spinneret Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spinneret+Sliver), and I answer on turn four with Jaya Ballard. He casts [Psionic Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Sliver), and uses his Spinneret to kill Jaya Ballard. He then proceeds to drop multiple other Slivers, and I draw an overabundance of lands and [Phyrexian Totem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Totem)s.   
 Record: 4-1


At this point, I'm leaning towards needing more removal. [Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium) isn't great in this deck, because I don't have as many large creatures as I did in the previous deck, nor creatures that could recur themselves. [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice) is also not great removal when it can't be played and replayed multiple times.


In their place, I add in [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe) (which was initially considered as a madness outlet at the beginning) and [Strangling Soot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot). [Strangling Soot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot) is reusable removal which can stop early creatures, and it can be discarded to other effects and used from the graveyard thanks to Flashback—making it synergistic with Jaya Ballard, [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), and [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe).


Out: 3 [Pandemonium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), 3 [Sparkmage Apprentice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sparkmage+Apprentice)  
 In: 3 [Lightning Axe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Axe), 3 [Strangling Soot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot)

 Record: 10-6

My opponents can outdraw me two-to-one cards a turn, which leaves me playing catch-up the entire game to their removal. I want something that can help me keep up with the Joneses, but [Phyrexian Arena](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Arena) itself is quite expensive online.


Since I didn't get to be a part of *Future Sight* preview week here on **magicthegathering.com**, I decide to go into the future to get my card advantage. This isn't an arbitrary choice—with the discount on madness cards, it's possible for me to drop several cards a turn with the Profusion.  Jaya Ballard keeps the board clear and drops [Reckless Wurm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reckless+Wurm) to the table. [Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) stays back as a wall against [Calciderm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Calciderm) for a turn and then goes on the offense with [Fiery Temper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Temper) to win the game.

I definitely had a lot of fun with this deck and ended up with quite a little pile of synergy. I'm not sure this is the most absolutely mind-blowingly powerful madness deck that can be built in Standard right now, but the interactions between the madness cards, Jaya, Haakon, [Null Profusion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Null+Profusion), [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox), and other cards made it a really tight package. There were combos within combos, and my opponents didn't quite know what to expect or to play around in most of the games.
