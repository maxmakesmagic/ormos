Red-Green Tron at GP Omaha
==========================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 26, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/shuhei_authorpic.jpg)
By Shuhei Nakamura




Shuhei Nakamura is a member of the Pro Tour Hall of Fame, former Player of the Year, and is one of just six players with more than 400 lifetime pro points. 






It seems like the World Championship was just yesterday but Pro Tour *Fate Reforged* in Washington, DC, is already next week.



The format is *Khans of Tarkir*/*Fate Reforged* Booster Draft and Modern.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Karn+Liberated)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated)

[Last year's winner](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/ptbng14) was Shaun McLaren with his Jeskai control deck. Who will take the trophy this year?



This week, I would like to introduce some Top 8 decks from [Grand Prix Omaha](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpoma15), the Modern tournament prior to the Pro Tour.



First off, Andrew Elenbogen's Red-Green Tron.



Once [Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Mine), [Power Plant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Power+Plant), and [Tower](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Tower) are in play, the power is yours. Usually, this starts with something like [Karn Liberated](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Karn+Liberated) destroying lands.



If your opponent begins flooding the board, [All Is Dust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=All+Is+Dust) comes in handy. Although not possible on your third turn, [Oblivion Stone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Stone) is another choice.



The deck finishes off with utilizing its overflowing mana to call [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) from the [Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin).



Urza Tron has always been around, since it begins produce seven mana on turn three with green cards like [Sylvan Scrying](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Scrying) and [Ancient Stirrings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Stirrings) played via [Chromatic Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star).



[Relic of Progenitus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus) is a third cheap artifact that doubles as a cantrip. It matched well with the field full of [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise)s at the time. Mainboard [Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) clearly illustrates what matchups the deck was prepared for.



Now might be the time to switch to [Firespout](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Firespout) for potential [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl)s.
