Commander Legendary
===================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on July 23, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Melissa_DeTora.jpg)
By Melissa DeTora




 A former Magic Pro player and strategy writer, Melissa's career Top 8s include PT Gatecrash and 2 GPs. She's currently a development intern at WotC. 







Today on Daily Decks, we're going to take a look at Ethan Fleischer's [Reki, the History of Kamigawa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reki%2C+the+History+of+Kamigawa) Commander deck. Ethan is a Game Designer in R&D; he started at Wizards about five years ago after winning [The Great Designer Search 2](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/great-designer-search-2-2011-03-07).



*Magic* players have a bittersweet relationship with legendary permanents. Some players love them because they represent exceptional characters in *Magic*'s history. Other players hate them because you can only have one copy on the battlefield at a time. If legendary permanents are so cool and we are only allowed to have one in play at a time, it seems like Commander is the perfect format for them!



Ethan's calls his Legendary deck "completely adorable," as it's a very unusual strategy in Commander, and the commander himself is a very weak card on his own. The deck works by playing mana creatures such as [Elvish Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Mystic) and [Fyndhorn Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fyndhorn+Elves) in the early turns, and then playing [Reki, the History of Kamigawa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reki%2C+the+History+of+Kamigawa) as early as turn two. Once he's on the battlefield, the card advantage just keeps flowing. Reki lets you draw a card every time you play a legendary spellâ€”and practically every spell in this deck is legendary! One thing that's really cool about Reki is his trigger condition happens when you *cast* the spell, not when it resolves. So even if your stuff gets countered, you still get to draw a card.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/DD20150723_GetinLoser.png)


*"Get in, loser."*



Another interesting thing about this deck is that the card choices are extremely unique. I've been playing *Magic* for eighteen years, and I still had to look most of these cards up on [Gatherer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Default.aspx) to see what they did. I mean, there weren't really a lot of [Hua Tuo, Honored Physician](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hua+Tuo%2C+Honored+Physician)s or [Meng Huo, Barbarian King](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Meng+Huo%2C+Barbarian+King)s being played in tournaments. I also hadn't considered how many legendary artifacts exist in *Magic*.



Ethan enjoys building decks that encourage him to run cards he might otherwise overlook. There are just so many obscure cards in *Magic*, and Ethan's Commander deck is proof that every *Magic* card has a home. Ethan is still hoping to fit more sweet legendary creatures in his deck. He's is looking forward to trying out [Azusa, Lost but Seeking](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azusa%2C+Lost+but+Seeking), [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll), and maybe even [Lady Zhurong, Warrior Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lady+Zhurong%2C+Warrior+Queen)!
