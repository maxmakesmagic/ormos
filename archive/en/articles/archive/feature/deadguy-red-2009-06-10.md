The modern red deck wouldn't be what it is today without Dave Price's innovative take on the aggressive strategy. In the late 90's, a deck called "Sligh" had come into existence to counter the pump-Knight weenie decks of the era (featuring cards like [Knight of Stromgald](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+Stromgald) in the black versions and [Order of the White Shield](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Order+of+the+White+Shield) in white). Price, known as "The King of Beatdown" and "The King of Qualifiers" for his propensity for playing beatdown decks and for manually qualifying the first dozen+ of his Pro Tour appearances, offered a new spin on the deck with a hyper aggressive strategy that was soon dominating U.S. Nationals. The effect of Dave's deck is still felt to this day, whenever a player drops a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and says "[Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) you!"
