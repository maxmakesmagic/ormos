From Marseilles to Montreal—A Look at WMCQs
===========================================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on June 6, 2013 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Mike Flores











World **Magic** Cup Qualifiers various have been raging in cities around the globe, off and on, for months now. Top performing players in the various WMCQs will have the chance to represent their parent nations in the upcoming  [World **Magic** Cup](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=mtg/event/worldmagiccup13) . Although more-or-less open events, many players consider their WMCQs as a stand-in for their National Championships and approach them with a similar level of severity; here in the United States, it is not unheard of for strong players or even Hall of Famers to fly from one end of the country to the other to try their hands at a WMCQ. Players at all levels can appreciate the performing builds, adopt them for [FNM](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/TCG/Events.aspx?x=events/magic/fnm) or other Standard tournaments, or use them as starting points to their own further explorations. 

Today's Top Decks will examine recent WMCQ Top 8 decklists from Montreal, Canada, and Marseilles, France.

![](https://media.wizards.com/images/magic/daily/td/td250_chart.jpg)  
Bant Hexproof
 Bant Hexproof is a deck that does what it sounds like: its colors are green-white-blue (the "Bant" trio from *Shards of Alara*) and it plays (among other creatures) a hexproof eight-pack of [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). Those eight hexproof creatures tend to be set in stone, with [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) being the best option against most decks capable of blocking, and [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) going from "arguably the strongest offensive creature in Standard" to "completely bonkers" when paired with a card like [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight); [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim) can give the deck some speed (setting up, say, a second-turn [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)) with most decks choosing between [Fencing Ace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace) or [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter) as the next level of offense.

 Bant Hexproof plays nearly as many offensive Auras as it does creatures themselves. Hard-to-stop threats like [Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker) can give this aggro deck a very "combo" feeling of non-interactivity; evasion in the form of flying or trampling makes an already hard-to-stop [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) even harder, even for decks that can typically put a stop sign in front of a 2/2 creature; [Fencing Ace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace) can potentially kill the opponent on the third turn when suited up with a lucky trio of [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor)s. 

  
 Bant Hexproof was a rogue strategy that rose to a reasonable level of popularity culminating in a Grand Prix finals mirror match back in [Atlantic City](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpatl13/welcome) in January; that was before *Gatecrash* and before *Dragon's Maze*. 

 Recent sets gave the deck tools: first [Gift of Orzhova](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gift+of+Orzhova) in *Gatecrash*, recently upgraded to [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) via *Dragon's Maze*. [Unflinching Courage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage), [Rancor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), and [Spectral Flight](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spectral+Flight) are all awesome ways to enhance your creatures; and what [Ethereal Armor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) may lack in single-card efficiency, it is undeniably unbelievable in the context of playing with lots of enchantments (which this deck does). The other notable addition from *Dragon's Maze* is [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence); Voice has grown up to be essentially a go-to four-of here. If Bant Hexproof has a weakness it is getting its creatures all killed (ergo, no target for all those enchantments) and the extra bodies afforded by this new two-drop therefore help the deck keep going even in the face of relevant removal resistance.

 The Marseilles WMCQ actually saw a Bant Hexproof mirror match in the finals, with F. falling to P. in a duel of Geists and Auras. F. played a more streamlined deck with mostly four-ofs. Rather than any [Fencing Ace](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fencing+Ace)s, F. played three [Strangleroot Geist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangleroot+Geist)s main (with a fourth in the sideboard). 

  
 An important addition to this strategy in recent weeks has been [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses). One of the cards that Bant Hexproof is somewhat soft to is [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict). Many of its key threats—[Invisible Stalker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker), [Geist of Saint Traft](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft)—can't be targeted but can be swept; unfortunately, [Supreme Verdict](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Verdict) can't be stopped by any kind of permission spells but can kind of be "countered" by a [Rootborn Defenses](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rootborn+Defenses); there is sometimes an additional tokens bonus when [Voice of Resurgence](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) has already spawned an Elemental.

 If you haven't seen this strategy yet...*this deck is sweet*. 

Eschewing many of the regular old midrange elements of Junk Reanimator, Four-Color Reanimator swings only for the fences. Absolutely nothing but boom booms in this deck.

 Instead of just going for a regular old threat, this one can go [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) + [Sire of Insanity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sire+of+Insanity) to ensure an uncounterable [Mind Twist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Twist) on legs against control. Did you foolishly tap for an [Augur of Bolas](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Bolas) or [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek)? Four-Color Reanimator can turbo out a Sire with [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) flashback. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |

 That [Cavern of Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) works hard in this deck... it can go [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity) loop with two or more Angels to create a long-game hell situation for removal-based control defense (the Cavern making the Angel loop impossible to stop with [Dissipate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dissipate) over the course of seven or more turns). 

 But the super special side is [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner). [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) is Standard's finest stop sign for Blitz decks (one of the notable foils to Reanimator). In addition to keeping the odd Lightning Mauler off your back, [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) is a mad combo enabler in this deck. [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch) and [Grisly Salvage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grisly+Salvage) are already filling up your graveyard... providing plenty of fuel here for the [Harvest Pyre](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harvest+Pyre) combo kill.

Naya Zoo
 Red-green-white (the "Naya" colors from *Shards of Alara*) previously represented the fastest offensive shard in Standard with Naya Blitz... all battalion and bloodrush and the odd [Giant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Growth). Games could *de facto* end on the second turn if the opponent hit too many [Burning-Tree Emissary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Burning-Tree+Emissary)s, with mad [Champion of the Parish](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) buffs or turning on all the 3/3 [Boros Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Elite)s. 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| [card]Burning-Tree Emissary[/card] | [card]Champion of the Parish[/card] |

  
However, recent trends have slowed Naya down.

 The performing Naya decks from these WMCQs have taken their cues from recent Grand Prix or the continuing [social media work of one Tomoharu Saito](https://twitter.com/TomoharuSaito), going with [Loxodon Smiter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Smiter)s (at least in part to slow down opposing rush decks) and [Domri Rade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Domri+Rade) at the three; both often come down on the second turn via [Avacyn's Pilgrim](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avacyn%27s+Pilgrim). 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| [card]Domri Rade[/card] | [card]Avacyn's Pilgrim[/card] |

  
 Today's Naya Zoo decks are a proud promenade of creature quality... Voice of Resurgence, [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)... all the hits; they tend to finish super strong and essentially unstoppable with [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) as the big finisher. Although they tend to play [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) (and [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) + [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)), their devotion to [Thundermaw Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundermaw+Hellkite) at the five has largely squeezed [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) out of the curve.

[Vampire Nighthawk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vampire+Nighthawk), [Huntmaster of the Fells](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), all the way to [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk). Junk Reanimator
 Junk Reanimator remains one of the most popular strategies in Standard. It mixes progressive midrange capability with a combo-esque [Mulch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulch)/[Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) game. Essentially, Junk Reanimator can go bigger than most other midrange decks with [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity), but [Unburial Rites](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unburial+Rites) allows it to set up faster than those competitors by under-paying for cards like [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) (or frustrating attackers by turning one [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) into three).

 B. went big with his Aristocrats deck, adding three copies of [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) to the aggressive, White Weenie-esque strategy. 

 The Aristocrats has always been an offensive-minded deck. It can start on [Doomed Traveler](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler), then lead into [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) or [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist). The ability to sacrifice its own creatures with [Cartel Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) and [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat) in concert with [Blood Artist](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Artist) gives the deck the ability to win outside just the red zone. [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner) + [Blasphemous Act](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blasphemous+Act) allows it to brain the opponent for 13, combo-style.

 The Aristocrats is basically always attacking, never gives the opponent triggers, and fields a super high level of quality between [Boros Reckoner](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boros+Reckoner), [Lingering Souls](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls), [Falkenrath Aristocrat](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Falkenrath+Aristocrat), and so on. 

Prime Speaker Bant
 From the first time [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek) laid eyes on [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and [Angel of Serenity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Serenity), they all decided they wanted to make a big, happy (did I mention "big") family of ramping and expensive spells. [Thragtusk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thragtusk) and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) made a little 3/3 achievement together. [Selesnya Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Selesnya+Charm) could defend all these big guys from [Olivia Voldaren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Olivia+Voldaren) (what's the point of making awesome big guys if some vampire queen was just going to steal them?). [Sphinx's Revelation](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Revelation)? That's big, too! 

  
Big big big.
