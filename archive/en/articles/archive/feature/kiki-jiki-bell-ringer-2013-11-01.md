Kiki-Jiki, Bell-Ringer
======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 1, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







If you were wondering why Martin Juza and Stanislav Cifka bought a box of *Innistrad* boosters the morning of [GP Antwerp](/en/events/coverage/dickmann-unleashes-twins-antwerp), this deck is the answer. They needed [Village Bell-Ringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer)s, and it turned out that dealers had neglected to bring such a *powerful* Modern staple. Their deck is an interesting take on [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), completely eschewing blue for white, using [Village Bell-Ringer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Village+Bell-Ringer) and [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) as combo pieces instead. 


![Village Bell-Ringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Village+Bell-Ringer) The actual combo works slightly differently, so don't just go tossing [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) on [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) blindly. Blinking the enchanted Angel will of course make the [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Splinter%2BTwin) fall off, so if you have Angel you really need to find Kiki-Jiki in order to go off. There are legitimate reasons to [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Splinter%2BTwin) up an Angel, like blinking [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) or [Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) each turn, but the combo is not one of those reasons. 



[Blade Splicer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) and [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) are a big part of why this deck plays differently than normal [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Splinter%2BTwin) decks. They give the deck much more of a board presence and let it play a much more midrange role than the fully combo-oriented UR versions. Add to that the ten removal spells and four [Restoration Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel)s, and you can imagine how aggro decks would have trouble breaking through the waves of Golems, Walls, Angels, Bolts, Paths, and [Magma Jet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magma+Jet)s. 


 There's a little bit of hate in the deck too, with two [Blood Moon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blood+Moon)s and two [Relic of Progenitus](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Relic+of+Progenitus)es able to randomly hose all sorts of linear decks, like Tron, Melira, [Living End](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Living+End), and all sorts of three-color decks. 


 Ultimately, a tempo version of [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) *did* win the tournament, but this offers another interesting alternative to the normal [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Splinter%2BTwin) decks, and is especially resistant to [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and early pressure both.
