Beast Cubed
===========



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 21, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_jaymoldenhauer-salazar.jpg)
By Jay Moldenhauer-Salazar












Until recently, I actually wasn't conscious of Beast as a creature type. After all, there's no Lord for Beasts like [Goblin King](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+King) is to Goblins, and no card gave particular benefits for using Beasts over other creature types. To me, Beast was just a generic name given to a critter that looked burly and ran on four or more legs.


Then came *Onslaught.* [Ravenous Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth), [Aether Charge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Charge), [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage), [Snarling Undorak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snarling+Undorak), [Shaleskin Bruiser](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaleskin+Bruiser), [Contested Cliffs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Contested+Cliffs), [Vitality Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vitality+Charm), [Krosan Groundshaker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Groundshaker), [Spurred Wolverine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spurred+Wolverine), and [Thunder of Hooves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thunder+of+Hooves) charged onto my dining room table and planted their beastly fists on their beastly hips. Now, like a lot of people, I find it pretty hard to ignore Beasts as a genus (or phylum, or class, or whatever it is that **Magic** creature types are).


What I hadn't realized is that a lot of my favorite creatures in previous expansions are, in fact, Beasts. I hope you'll indulge me in a day of nostalgia as I look back at ten Beasts that hold a special place in my heart. They may not be the most *powerful* Beasts \*cough\*[Blastoderm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blastoderm)\*cough\*[Spiritmonger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiritmonger)\*cough\* but they're sure fun to build decks around . . .


### 10. [Fylamarid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fylamarid)



![Fylamarid](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Fylamarid)

[Fylamarid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fylamarid)always struck me as an amazingly freakish creature. It's a flying blue antiblue-Beast that can turn creatures blue for blue mana. Freaky.


It may be obvious, but [Fylamarid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fylamarid)just screams to have a deck built around it in which having blue creatures is a detriment to your opponent and/or a benefit to you. [Circle of Protection: Blue](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Circle+of+Protection%3A+Blue), along with protection from blue creatures like [Voice of Reason](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Reason) and [Bloated Toad](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bloated+Toad), come to mind as possible components of the deck. [Possessed Aven](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Possessed+Aven) and [Llawan, Cephalid Empress](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llawan%2C+Cephalid+Empress) are perfectly appropriate, too. Or for a rareless “lite” deck try the following:

### 9. [Crater Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crater+Hellion)



![Crater Hellion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Crater+Hellion)

Nothing says BOOM! quite like [Crater Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crater+Hellion). This is probably the most tournament-used Beast on this list (with the possible exception of #2), but I think most casual groups have giggled with glee when [Crater Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crater+Hellion)drops onto the table. With [Aether Charge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aether+Charge), [Crater Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crater+Hellion) might as well be named “Four to you! And you! And you! And you over there!” Or at least that's what I like to call him.


There's nothing particularly tricky about building a deck around [Crater Hellion](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crater+Hellion), unless you decide to use it with something like [Nature's Revolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nature%27s+Revolt). Oh sure, paying the echo will be difficult when you've blown up all the land on the table, but if you happen to have another creature whose toughness is greater than 4 . . . who cares?


### 8. [Glowing Anemone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glowing+Anemone)



![Glowing Anemone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Glowing+Anemone)

For a very long time, I lugged a monoblue deck around with me that used [Glowing Anemone](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glowing+Anemone), [Ankh of Mishra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ankh+of+Mishra), [Viseling](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viseling), [Iron Maiden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Maiden), and a lot of land-targeting bounce like [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang) and [Hoodwink](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hoodwink). What was great about the Anemone was that it a) provided defense against cheap little creatures, and b) let me say “anemone,” which is right behind “discombobulate” and “waffle” as my favorite word to say over and over.


If I were going to recreate the deck today, it might look something like this. And don't forget, if your opponent is using the new *Onslaught* fetch lands, [Ankh of Mishra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ankh+of+Mishra) hits twice as hard.

### 7. [Lumbering Satyr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumbering+Satyr)



![Lumbering Satyr](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Lumbering+Satyr)

If playing with [Alter Reality](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alter+Reality) and [Fylamarid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fylamarid) is fun, playing with [Crystal Spray](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crystal+Spray) (or [Whim of Volrath](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whim+of+Volrath)) and [Lumbering Satyr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumbering+Satyr) is equally fun. Having a bunch of green, unblockable fatties on the table is great. When you get to draw a card (thanks to [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage)) or sacrifice your Satyr at opportune times for life (thanks to [Ravenous Baloth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Baloth)), things start to get a little ridiculous.


I should point out that it's relatively easy to make a monogreen Extended deck using [Lumbering Satyr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lumbering+Satyr) and not a single forest. Sort of silly, as giving an opponent forestwalk is irrelevant if they're already dead. But what the heck, it sounds like fun.

### 6. [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy)



![Cavern Harpy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cavern+Harpy)

To my mind, [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) is the least beastly of the Beasts. It seems like [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) should at least be a “Bird Beast,” which would differentiate it from those hulking, nonflying critters (and would add a lot of interesting possibilities to Bird decks). But, alas, at the time of *Planeshift,* R&D wasn't comfortable with multiple creature types. As a result, [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) is the only blue-black Beast and also one of the smallest Beasts around (though we can thank [Sacred Prey](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Prey) for that particular distinction).


Why is [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) cool? Mostly because of all the fun you can have with comes-into-play creature abilities like [Ravenous Rats](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ravenous+Rats), [Man-o'-War](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Man-o%27-War), and [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal). Also, think about the combination of [Wirewood Savage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wirewood+Savage) and [Cavern Harpy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+Harpy) for a moment. Got it? Now add [Aluren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aluren). Ooooooh . . . You're ready for the Pro Tour!

. . . but not quite as big a fan as I am of [Jackalope Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackalope+Herd).


True story: When my wife and I moved from Michigan to the San Francisco Bay area, we drove two different vehicles. Sarah got the moving van and the big dog, while I got the Honda Civic and the little dog. We communicated via walkie-talkies to coordinate meals and restroom stops, all the while having fun with “breaker breaker that's a ten-four Charlie” talk. Anyway, with all due respect to Midwesterners, the middle of the United States has some incredibly boring scenery, so I needed something to keep me awake. I bought a pack of *Exodus* on the drive, and decided that each day I would pick a few cards and ruminate on deck possibilities using that card (no, I haven't changed much).


I never got past my first common—[Jackalope Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackalope+Herd). I spent the *entire drive* with a [Jackalope Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackalope+Herd) taped to my steering wheel. I even learned—and don't try this at home kiddies—to write on a pad of paper while driving, making somewhere in the neighborhood of a dozen Jackalope decks.


Alas, none of my [Jackalope Herd](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackalope+Herd) decks ever won me a tournament. But I always loved the near-creatureless, kind of controllish green-red deck that used a horde of instants. The idea was that anyone trying to kill my Herd would instead find it bounced to my hand, ready to play again. It made for some fun combat tricks too.
