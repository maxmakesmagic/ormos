Plenty of Temples
=================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on January 31, 2014 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211024110319im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/themes/wiz_mtg/images/global/generic-avatar-150.png)
By Gerry Thompson











 Last week, I showed off [Ali Aintrazi's Five-Color](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/deck/1415)[Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian). Today, I have a similar deck, although King Feeble's version is a more modest four-color deck featuring cheaper spells instead of [Axebane Guardian](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Axebane+Guardian)s and bigger spells. 

 This deck takes a lot of what makes Mono-Black Devotion so powerful, like [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize), [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), and [Gray Merchant of Asphodel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gray+Merchant+of+Asphodel), and adds [Sylvan Caryatid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Caryatid) for acceleration, early blocking, and mana fixing. King Feeble also has [Abrupt Decay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrupt+Decay) and [Detention Sphere](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Detention+Sphere) to deal with nonland permanents, which is a big step from Mono-Black, which can't remove an [Underworld Connections](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underworld+Connections), [Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thassa), or [Bident of Thassa](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bident+of+Thassa) once they are in play. 

[Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council) is the finisher of choice, instead of the sometimes unreliable [Desecration Demon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desecration+Demon). The inclusion of Obzedat makes [Whip of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) a fantastic choice 

 My first thought on the mana base was, "Wow! Sixteen [Temples](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoileramp;method=visualamp;action=advancedamp;name=+%5BTemple%5Damp;type=+%5B%22Land%22%5Damp;format=+%5B%22Standard%22%5D)? That's going to go a long way toward fixing its draw steps." At closer inspection, I realized that [Temple Garden](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+Garden) doesn't actually scry. Still, twelve Temples is pretty impressive, and I imagine it does wonders for the consistency.
