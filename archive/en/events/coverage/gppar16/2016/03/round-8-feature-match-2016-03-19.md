Round 8 Feature Match: Antonio Del Moral León (Jeskai Ascendancy Combo) vs. Bill Chronopoulos (Four-Color Rally)
================================================================================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on March 19, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_FrankKarsten.jpg)
By Frank Karsten











This match pitted together two players who were no strangers to high-level play. Antonio Del Moral León won Pro Tour Fate Reforged, while Bill Chronopoulos had been part of the Greek 2014 World Magic Cup team and the Grand Prix London 2015 Top 8.


Both were playing four-color combo decks, but with completely different game plans. Chronopoulos had registered with Four-Color Rally, the consensus best deck in the format, while Del Moral León had opted for a rogue [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) brew. Given that this was his last chance to try a crazy Standard deck with cards from both Khans of Tarkir and Oath of the Gatewatch at a major event, he made the most of it by combining [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) with [Expedite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedite), [Slip Through Space](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slip+Through+Space), and [Elemental Uprising](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Uprising). It's always a pleasure to see an exciting brew endorsed by a well-known player.


Game 1
======


Del Moral León, on the draw, had [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic) on turn two, but it was immediately bounced by [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage). Normally that's not a big problem because you can just play it face-down as a morph to get around [Reflector Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reflector+Mage)'s ability, but there was a problem: Del Moral León didn't have a third land.


So he effectively had to skip his third turn, and one extra turn was all that Chronopoulos needed. He quickly chained [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) into [Rally the Ancestors](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rally+the+Ancestors), which allowed him to set up lethal amount of [Zulaport Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zulaport+Cutthroat) triggers before Antonio Del Moral León had a chance to set up his combo.


Antonio Del Moral León 0 - Bill Chronopoulos 1


Game 2
======


In the early turns of the game, it looked like Del Moral León was playing a textbook blue-red control deck featuring [Magmatic Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magmatic+Insight), [Slip Through Space](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slip+Through+Space), [Expedite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedite), and [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) as card draw spells and [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) and [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames) as removal spells.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gppar16/gppar16_r8fm_del-moral-leon-chronopoulos.jpg)  
*Antonio Del Moral León vs. Bill Chronopoulos*



Eventually, he found [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy), and the fun began for Del Moral León. After first casting [Radiant Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Radiant+Flames) to sweep Chronopoulos's board (well, except for [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk)) while looting in the process, his next turn was a big one.


* [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic).
* [Expedite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedite) my [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic), loot and pump.
* [Elemental Uprising](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Uprising) my [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery), loot, pump, and untap my [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic).

If, at that point, Del Moral León would have had a string of cantrip in hand, then he could have easily set up a lethal attack on that turn. But without additional card-draw spells, he merely ate an Eldrazi Scion token with his animated [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery) and passed the turn with [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic) as his sole untapped permanent.



![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gppar16/gppar16_r8fm_del-moral-leon.jpg)  
*Antonio Del Moral León*



When Chronopoulos, on his turn, cast [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), Del Moral Leon tapped his [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic) for [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) and triggered [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy). The turn was passed before it was discovered that [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic) didn't actually have haste on Chronopoulos' turn anymore, so it couldn't produce the mana for [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel). After a lengthy ruling and appeal process, the head judge rolled back to the point where the [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel) was cast incorrectly. [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) resolved, and Chronopoulos increased his clock to the point where Del Moral León was forced to chump block with his [Rattleclaw Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rattleclaw+Mystic).


But eventually, Del Moral León found the tools he needed to win the game. He got out a second [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) and activated [Wandering Fumarole](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wandering+Fumarole). A [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) got him two untap and two pump triggers. [Magmatic Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magmatic+Insight) did the same. Soon enough, the Fumarole had grown to a 7/11 and was made unblockable by [Slip Through Space](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slip+Through+Space). Two [Elemental Uprising](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elemental+Uprising) sent a [Mystic Monastery](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Monastery) and a [Cinder Glade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cinder+Glade) into the fray as well, and the resulting attack was more than lethal.


Antonio Del Moral León 1 - Bill Chronopoulos 1


Game 3
======


At the beginning of the third game, the two linear combo decks spent a surprising amount of time disrupting each other. Del Moral León had a [Fiery Impulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiery+Impulse) for Chronopoulos' [Jace, Vryn's Prodigy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Vryn%27s+Prodigy), while Chronopoulos in turn exiled [Jeskai Ascendancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jeskai+Ascendancy) with [Transgress the Mind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Transgress+the+Mind).



![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gppar16/gppar16_r8fm_chronopoulos.jpg)  
*Bill Chronopoulos*



However, Del Moral León had another copy of the enchantment in hand, along with [Expedite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedite) and [Magmatic Insight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Magmatic+Insight) to dig deeper into his deck. He quickly found a [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) as well, while Chronopoulos merely added an [Elvish Visionary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Visionary) and a [Nantuko Husk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Husk) to his board—not the most impressive clock.


Nevertheless, Del Moral León's deck fizzled and failed to cough up any additional action. Chronopoulos added [Grim Haruspex](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Haruspex) and [Zulaport Cutthroat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zulaport+Cutthroat) to the battlefield, and Del Moral León life total dwindled down to zero shortly after.


Antonio Del Moral León 1 - Bill Chronopoulos 2


Del Moral León falls to 5-2-1 and has his work cut out for him. Chronopoulos is guaranteed a spot in Day 2 with his 6-1-1 record and is still in the running for Top 8.
