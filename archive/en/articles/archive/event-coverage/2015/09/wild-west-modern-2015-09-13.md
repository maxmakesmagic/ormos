The Wild West of Modern
=======================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on September 13, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/hosler.jpg)
By Corbin Hosler











We opened Grand Prix Oklahoma City coverage by running down some of the [incredible diversity in the Modern format](/node/660311). One of the big questions entering this event was if the “wild west” state of Modern would continue, or if some of the more traditional decks would rise up to dominate the format.


Entering the opening round Sunday, it can be confirmed that Oklahoma remains the wild west.


At this point, there can be little doubt that Modern is a deckbuilder’s paradise. Not only did some of the usual suspects like [Patrick Chapin perform well](/node/660361) with innovative decks, but he was far from the only player to find success, and not all of them are names you may have expected.


Enter Stephen Perigo’s Naya Allies deck.


“When I heard *Battle for Zendikar* was coming out with Allies, I wanted to herald them in,” he explained. “I used to play [Warp World](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warp+World) Allies when they were in Standard, and I wanted to try them out in Modern. I didn’t realize there were other people testing with it, so I worked on it myself. Other people are playing [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) and [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial), but I wanted to play with [Hardened Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales) and [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) so I went with my own list. There’s still a lot of refining that needs to be done, but I liked what I had.”


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpokc15/gpokc15_perigo.jpg)**  
*Stephen Perigo began battling for Zendikar a little early, advancing to Day Two of Grand Prix Oklahoma City with his Allies deck.*


What Perigo has is a unique take on the Modern format. The basis of the deck is one-drop [Hada Freeblade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hada+Freeblade) and two-drops [Kazandu Blademaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kazandu+Blademaster), [Oran-Rief Survivalist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oran-Rief+Survivalist) and the most threatening of them all: [Akoum Battlesinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Battlesinger). Perigo’s army can generate a ton of triggers from allies entering the battlefield, and it can be blisteringly fast, especially when combined with [Hardened Scales](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hardened+Scales) or [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company).


“I’ve had some crazy wins,” Perigo said. “One player wiped my board, and the next turn I cast [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company) and got two [Akoum Battlesinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akoum+Battlesinger)s. They both triggered twice and I attacked him for 10 haste damage and won. There was another game where I beat Affinity by gaining 23 life on one turn with [Ondu Cleric](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ondu+Cleric), and even one where I beat a Zoo deck that left back blockers by drawing [Abzan Falconer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abzan+Falconer) and making all my guys fly.”


With *Battle for Zendikar* on the way, Perigo is most excited to add Lantern Scout to his deck, while Munda, Ambush Leader and March From the Tomb will likely find their way somewhere into his 75.


While Perigo has the most interesting deck to advance to Sunday, he’s far from the only player finding success going rogue. Of the five decks that went undefeated on the first day of competition, three of them — Lantern Control, Elves and [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) — aren’t decks you typically find near the top of the standings.


Further down the of Day Two competitors, the innovation continues. Forty-one different archetypes advanced to Sunday, and no deck comprised more than 14 percent of the field. Those decks included Soul Sisters, Faeries, Ad Nauseam, a unique Jund-colored take on [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift), a slower, [Hangarback Walker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hangarback+Walker)-focused version of Affinity, and even a wild [Return to the Ranks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Return+to+the+Ranks) deck.


Chris Lopez arrived in Oklahoma accompanied by an army of little green men, and his elvish army carried him to an undefeated record on Saturday. After the success of the Elf deck that won Grand Prix Charlotte in the hands of Michael Malone, the deck fell off the radar somewhat, a development that surprised Lopez.


But, armed with *Magic Origins* additions Dwynen’s Elite and especially [Shaman of the Pack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shaman+of+the+Pack), Lopez has one of *Magic’s* treasured tribes back to the limelight.


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpokc15/gpokc15_lopez.jpg)**  
*Chris Lopez has made use of a few key Magic Origins cards to propel Elves back into the spotlight after finishing Day One undefeated.*


“Shaman provides so much reach for this deck,” he said. “It helps you win the game even if the ground clogs up. I think Elves is a great deck. The removal-based decks are huge right now, and they’re really poor against this deck. If they’re killing your mana creatures they don’t have removal for your threats, and If they save them for the threats you’re going to have the velocity to kill them anyway.”


There’s no question that a huge variety of decks can find success in the “wild west” of the Modern metagame. In Oklahoma City, the only question that remains is which of them will be left standing when the dust settles.
