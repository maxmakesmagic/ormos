Round 10 Feature Match - Hironobu Sugaya vs. Raynand Rivas
----------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*
Four years ago, Hironobu Sugaya defeated Shouta Yasooka to become the 2008 GP Manila champion, and is showing similar promise this weekend, going undefeated on day one. Local player Raynand Rivas was also undefeated yesterday, and is looking to stop Sugaya in his tracks. Both players had only two byes, too!


### Game 1


Rivas won the die roll, and played a [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls), and then a [Loyal Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Cathar). When it attacked a turn later, Sugaya opted to [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) it during combat, on the grounds he probably wouldn't be able to [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) anything that followed, anyway. What followed was a [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader). Sugaya answered with a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft), while Rivas replayed his [Loyal Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Cathar), and an [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure), and attacked for 6.


Sugaya [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)d the Filipino, seeing [Fiend Hunter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fiend+Hunter), and [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish). He considered his options, before finally summoning a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to flashback the [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) on the [Loyal Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Cathar), to force his Geist through, to even the scores at 14 apiece. The [Crusade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crusade)r attacked back, and the [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Snapcaster%2BMage) valiantly soaked up the damage. Rivas summoned his [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), and once again, replayed his Cathar.


Sugaya [Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe)d again, this time seeing the Hunter, and a [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge). Rivas lacked the second Red mana for the Hero, but the future still looked bleak for the Japanese player. He attacked with his Geist, trading it and a [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) for Rivas' Champion, before replacing the Geist with a new one. Rivas drew the Red Mana he needed, summoned the Hero and crashed into the attack phase with a great deal more damage than Sugaya was prepared to take.


Rivas 1 – Sugaya 0



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R10_Sugaya.jpg)

*Hironobu Sugaya contemplates taking more combat damage in one sitting than any sane doctor would recommend as safe.* 
### Game 2


Sugaya passed back his second turn with mana open, while Rivas summoned a [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben). Sugaya traded it for a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image) in a puff of logic, before summoning a [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer). Rivas played a replacement Thalia, but was missing a third land. Sugaya was missing his fourth land, but wasn't having any trouble getting a board presence going, with a second Image, this time of the [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer).


Rivas payed a good part of the Iron Price for a [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) on one of the Golems, but took 3 from the remaining one before he could take care of it with a [Leonin Relic-Warder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder). Sugaya tried to [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) his [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), but Rivas responded with a [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) to keep those shenanigans out of the picture. A second Angel inadvertently ate the Image'd Splicer, but the pair of fliers was enough for Rivas to give up on this game, and try his luck in the decider.


Rivas 1 – Sugaya 1


### Game 3


Sugaya ditched his 7 cards for 6 better ones, while Rivas blazed out of the gates with [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish), and Thalia. Sugaya threw a [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) into the mix to keep the Champion off his mark, and again neutralised Thalia with a [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image), but missed his third land drop. Rivas resummoned his Champion, and bolstered it with a [Cathedral Sanctifier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cathedral+Sanctifier).



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R10_Rivas.jpg)

*Raynand Rivas is building an army.* 
Sugaya found his third land, and used it to bring himself some [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements), one of which traded for the Sanctifier. A [Doomed Traveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doomed+Traveler) pumped the Champion up to 3/3, while Sugaya summoned a [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft). The remaining Soldiers traded with the Traveler and chump blocked the Champion, but Rivas passed back the turn without further play, no doubt due to the fact he had five [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains) in play, and no Red mana. Sugaya attacked with his Geist, and then [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)ed. He ended the turn with a [Moorland Haunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moorland+Haunt) open.


Rivas finally drew a [Clifftop Retreat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clifftop+Retreat), but passed the turn without attacking. Sugaya cashed in his dead [Phantasmal Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phantasmal+Image), and gave some thought to Rivas' now defensive position. He attacked with the Geist, and when Rivas agreed to block with his 3/3 Champion, Sugaya [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag)ged it. Rivas summoned some [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements) of his own, and then played an [Honor of the Pure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Honor+of+the+Pure), his board suddenly much more formidable. He attacked with his Spirit Token, but Sugaya dropped a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) on it. Sugaya untapped and tapped out to play and equip a [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace) to his Geist, and attacked Rivas down to 4 life.


Rivas played a [Leonin Relic-Warder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Relic-Warder) on the Sword, and an [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) on the [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), but it wasn't enough to stop the Geist sending an Angel Token in to finish him off.


Hironobu Sugaya defeats Raynand Rivas 2 – 1



Round 11 Feature Match - Andreas Ganz vs. Shi Tian Lee
------------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
### Game 1


Both players are on their respective national teams – Lee on the Hong Kong team, Ganz captaining the Swiss. Lee led off with a turn 1 [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets). Ganz, after a mulligan, paid 2 life to summon a [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge). "[Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel)?", asked Lee, bemused. Lee used a [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) on the Skirge to clear the path for his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets). Ganz used his second turn to re-summon the Skirge and a [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest), then cast a [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) and [Dispatch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispatch)ed the [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets).


Lee rebuilt with a [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer). Ganz made himself a free [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk) by bouncing and replaying his [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) before dropping a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) and attacking. Lee summoned a [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) to blink his [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer) in and out of the battlefield, netting himself an extra Golem token. Both swung in at Ganz, who declined to block, leaving his beasties out of harm's way.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R11_Ganz.jpg)

*Andreas' robot army breaks out the Ganz* 
Ganz summoned a second [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest) and tried attacking with a [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol), but Lee had a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to flash back his [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) and stop the troublesome attacker. Lee's combat phase brought an all-out attack with his team. Two Golems, a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and the [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel) all charged in. Ganz threw his [Vault Skirge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vault+Skirge) in front of the [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), killed the Mage with a [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest), and fell to 5 life.


Ganz found a second copy of [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel). With his opponent on 11 life, Ganz had exactly lethal, as long as the one remaining card in Lee's hand wasn't a trick.... It wasn't.


Andreas Ganz 1 – Shi Tian Lee 0


### Game 2


Ganz: "I have some small men!"  

Lee: "I have some [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s!"


Ganz: "Oh. I see."


Lee: "I also have these [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer)s!"


Ganz: "Hmm."  

Lee: "And these [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag)s!"


Ganz: "....."


Shi Tian Lee 1 – Andreas Ganz 1


### Game 3


Ganz led play with an [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) and an [Origin Spellbomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Origin+Spellbomb), and then followed up with a[Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol), a [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal) and a [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest). Lee passed his second turn without any action. Ganz activated his Spellbomb and attacked with his Idol and the Pest to drop Lee to 17, choosing not to play any spells.



![Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Blade+Splicer)

Lee tapped out for a [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), giving Ganz an opportunity to play a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) and attacking for 7, bring Lee to 10. Ganz summoned an [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) and attacked, this time with only a solo [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol). Lee took the damage, and post-combat, Ganz cast another Idol. At the end of Ganz's turn, Lee flashed in his telegraphed [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel), blinking out the [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer).


[Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) came down on Lee's mainphase, [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder)ed, and joined the army sitting patiently on the battlefield. Ganz summoned another [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion) and attacked with both of his [Glint Hawk Idol](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk+Idol)s, forcing Lee to block with his Angel. A [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) slowed down the other Idol temporarily, but Ganz just replayed it and passed. Lee attacked his [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) into Ganz's [Etched Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etched+Champion), dropping his creature count low enough to maximise advantage from his [Timely Reinforcements](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Timely+Reinforcements). The reprieve was only temporary, though, as Ganz's Idols punched through for victory.


Andreas Ganz 2 – Shi Tian Lee 1

Round 13 Feature Match - Jun Young Park vs. Ding Yuan Leong
-----------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*
Jun Young Park battled through to a top 8 finish back at GP Kitakyushu back in 2007, while Ding Yuan Leong thrashed a Jund-packed top 8 at GP Kuala Lumpur in 2009.


### Game 1


Park won the roll, but threw away the first 7 cards he saw. His next hand was pretty good, however, with [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) into a [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer). Leong had a fine hand, too, with [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) into [Dungrove Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungrove+Elder). Park peeled a [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) off the top of his deck, and played it for two to wipe out Leong's team and attack for 4. Leong recovered with another [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and an [Ulvenwald Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Tracker), while Park summoned a [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), and attacked again, knocking Leong to 10.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R13_Park.jpg)

*Jun Young Park found a miracle, and played it for 2.* 
Leong [Green Sun's Zenith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Green+Sun%27s+Zenith)'d up a new [Dungrove Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungrove+Elder) to go with his 3 [Forest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s, and passed back the turn, Park eying up the combination of [Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tracker) and Elder with some concern.


Park untapped with a 2/2 Wolf token, the 2/2 Huntmaster, 1/1 [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), 3/3 Golem Token, and a [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). He mulled over his options, before finally attacking with everything. Leong blocked the Huntmaster with his Avenger, and fell to 4 life. Park played a second [Blade Splicer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blade+Splicer), and passed the turn back with a [Gavony Township](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gavony+Township) in play.


Leong drew his card for the turn and considered his options. He had a 3/3 [Dungrove Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungrove+Elder), a [Llanowar Elves](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves), and the [Ulvenwald Tracker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ulvenwald+Tracker), but decided that was not going to be good enough, and packed up his cards.


Park 1 – Leong 0


### Game 2

*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R15_Ganz.jpg)

*Andreas Ganz explains his [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck.* 
Andreas Ganz will be leading the Swiss National Team at the World **Magic** Cup this year, and frequently lands some very respectable finishes at Pro Level **Magic** events. He chose an unorthodox deck for this weekend, however.


"[Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) has a good matchup against [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)," he explained. "It's a metagame choice, really."


I will admit, it has been some time since we've seem a [Tempered Steel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tempered+Steel) deck do well, and correspondingly, the many [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge)s that used to fill out peoples sideboards have been dwindling.


"There's not a lot you can change about the deck, really," Ganz lamented on the lack of new cards he's added to the deck. "I looked through all of the new cards, but there wasn't really anything that would fit. I removed a [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk) from the main for a [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), to give me a fifth way to kill [Restoration Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Restoration+Angel). [Glint Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glint+Hawk) is also a bit of a risk when people are playing [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot). I played one earlier with two [Signal Pest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Signal+Pest)s in play, but they had the [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot) and a [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) anyway."

*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinshaw*

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R15_Juza.jpg)

*Martin Juza's variation of a Naya deck.* 
Martin Juza came to this event with a slightly unusual concoction this weekend. He's taken a Naya deck, and stripped out almost all of the spells that aren't [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned).


"All the spells are bad against [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets). I had two [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring), a [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember), and a [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame), but they just weren't doing anything, and Bonfire was, so I added the two more Bonfire, a third [Geist-Honored Monk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist-Honored+Monk), and the fourth [Borderland Ranger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Borderland+Ranger)."


So that's pretty good against [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), apparently, but what about everything else?


"It can't beat decks with [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment)," Juza admitted. "If I'm completely honest, [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) is the best deck, I just wanted to play something different."


Interestingly enough, the deck appears to have an advantage after sideboarding, at least for now. "People assume I'm playing [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod), so they board in [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) and [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge). I'm not playing Pod because it's too much messing around, and in all of those games, I'd rather cast Bonfire on turn five than Pod on turn three and activating it on turn four."

Sunday, 3:45 p.m. - Deck Tech: U/G/R Delver
-------------------------------------------


*by Pip Foweraker*
Doing coverage, you get to read a lot of decklists. Doing a metagame breakdown, you get to read a *whole lot* of decklists. There's a lot of sorting, and it's easy to fall into a sort of mindless trance.  *"[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)"* , your brain mutters,  *"[Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), Ramp, [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets), Zombies, [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets)... Wait, [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells)? "* 


Curiousity piqued? So was mine. I headed over to the tables to find out what was going on. This interesting build was being ably piloted by Min-Chih Sun (Dell Sun), fresh out from Taipei, sitting on an X-0-4 record. When I pulled him aside after his match, the plot thickened.


Responsible for designing the bulk of the deck was none other than Tzu Ching Kuo, 3 time Taiwanese National Champion and multiple GP Top 8'er. We talked a little about the deck, and it had already accumulated an impressive pedigree.


Kuo had brewed the deck in time for last weekend's PTQ / WMCQ weekend in Taipei, where it had won *both* events, piloted by two different players, Han Yu Bei and Cheng Tang Yi, with Hao-Shan Huang also piloting the deck to an impressive finish. Min-Chih Sun hadn't played a game of tournament magic in nearly a decade before this weekend's admirable result, so the potency of the deck speaks for itself.


The deck is aiming to beat a very specific metagame opponent – [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) – while not giving up ground against the other matchups. [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) and [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) give the deck an advantage in countermagic wars against other [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) decks, and [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) is being hailed as a near-universal panacea and slayer of multitudes. Nobody I've talked to this weekend who's running it can name a matchup where it can be sided out – and that's impressive.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpmla12/R15_Bei_Decktech.jpg)

*Who have 20 copies of [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells), [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), and [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage)? These guys!* 
Opening up red in the sideboard gives the deck a huge amount of flexibility in answering threats post-board. [Zealous Conscripts](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zealous+Conscripts) and [Hero of Oxid Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Oxid+Ridge) are both Humans, maximising value from [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls). [Combust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Combust) and [Dungeon Geists](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dungeon+Geists) are both powerful role players, and there's even flashback value added for [Ancient Grudge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancient+Grudge).


What changes could be made? The lack of 2-mana creatures have hurt the curve a little, and one possibility might be to replace the [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned)s with [Invisible Stalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Invisible+Stalker)s. The sideboard, of course, should be fine-tuned to your local metagame, but the players have been very happy with it so far.


If you're looking for a refreshing variant on the powerful [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Delver%2Bof%2BSecrets) engine for your next Standard tournament, this might be it!
