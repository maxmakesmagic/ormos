Today's Must
============


1. [*Eldritch Moon* Set Guide: Multicolor, Meld, Artifact, Colorless, and Land](http://magic.tcgplayer.com/db/article.asp?ID=13397&writer=TCGplayer+Staff&articledate=7-18-2016) | TCGPlayer | Pretty much everyone

So, if you missed the first installment of TCGPlayer's new format for set reviews, you're in for something fairly epic in the world of set reviews. Here, six of TCGPlayer's content producers *all* chime in to give scores to cards, which are then averaged to give you a more holistic view of how good a card might be. It's extensive and thorough, probably the longest of any online. And, as a bonus, it comes with two decklists from TCGPlayer's brew factory to get you started.


While we're on the topic of set reviews, Patrick Chapin is starting [his popular review series on StarCityGames.com today](http://www.starcitygames.com/article/33304_Eldritch-Moon-Set-Review-Black.html). One drawback—it's Premium, so you have to have paid for it. But if you have, it's well worth the read.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Pauper Blue-Red Drake Combo](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/pauper-constructed-league-2016-07-17)

If you follow any of the chatter on today's Banned and Restricted announcement, there wasn't much expected to be banned—with one exception.


There was some chatter that [Peregrine Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peregrine+Drake), newly made common by *Eternal Masters*, would get the axe due to its rising popularity and power in Pauper. However, the rise of the Drake is relatively recent, so it survived this time. Whether it survives the next one will be a function of what happens in Pauper in the meantime.


If you're not exactly sure what I'm talking about, the deck list below will give you some clue. With [Peregrine Drake](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Peregrine+Drake), [Ghostly Flicker](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghostly+Flicker), and either [Archaeomancer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Archaeomancer) or [Mnemonic Wall](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mnemonic+Wall), the deck can repeatedly flicker the pair, untapping their lands and getting the flicker effect back. They end up with unlimited mana and, if you throw a [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) or [Sea Gate Oracle](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sea+Gate+Oracle) in there, unlimited draws. Once they draw their whole deck, they can dump all of the mana into a [Rolling Thunder](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Thunder) and simply win.


The rest of the deck is a blue-red control shell, packing cheap removal, card draw, and counterspells, in that way reminding many people of the old [Splinter Twin](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) deck in Modern. That kind of power in Pauper certainly gives some people pause.


But for now, Drake survives to untap another land.
