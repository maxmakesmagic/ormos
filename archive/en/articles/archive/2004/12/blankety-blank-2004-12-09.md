Blankety-Blank
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on December 9, 2004 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_markgottlieb.jpg)
By Mark Gottlieb












Scott Johns: “But this column doesn't *do* anything!”  

Mark: “No—It *does* nothing.”


That's right, kids. In the immortal tradition of *Seinfeld*, today's column is about nothing. Well, sometimes it's about [Kjeldoran Phalanx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Phalanx). And sometimes it's about [Form of the Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Form+of+the+Dragon). And sometimes it's about [Accumulated Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge). And sometimes it's about the mating dance of the common wombat (no, wait, that was last month). Occasionally Aaron Forsythe will burst through my front door in a goofy manner and mooch something from my fridge. Fun times.


Nothing gets a bad rap. What's so awful about the absence of all things? Are you telling me you wouldn't enjoy a leisurely vacation in the murky depths of space? Are you??? I imagine it would be quite relaxing—No deadlines, no email, no oxygen. Aaahhh. The concept of nothing more commonly denotes not the absence of everything—just the absence of whatever it is you're looking for at the time. For example, I may complain that my refrigerator has nothing in it, but it's not empty by any means. And if you don't believe me, just ask the sentient clump of mold that lives behind the black goop and the thing that used to be a tomato.


### Nothing to Worry About


 



![74252](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&multiverseid=74252)

What all this is getting to is the card sound effect of very loud silence. Um, the card [One of the most obvious uses for \_\_\_\_\_](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) is to combo it with one of its *Unhinged* comrades, [Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s). Put these two together and you're just about halfway to anywhere you want to go.

Paul Barclay, all rumors to the contrary, actually does know his ABC's, so he spent some time coming up with winning board conditions. Some of his suggestions:


[Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s) + [Kjeldoran Phalanx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Phalanx) + [Venomous Dragonfly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Venomous+Dragonfly) + [Quirion Trailblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Quirion+Trailblazer)


[Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s) + [Kjeldoran Outpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Outpost) + [Vizzerdrix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizzerdrix) + [Questing Phelddagrif](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Questing+Phelddagrif)


[Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s) + [Teferi's Puzzle Box](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%27s+Puzzle+Box) + [Jhovall Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhovall+Queen) + [Erg Raiders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erg+Raiders) (or any of over 600 other cards which include a D and G)


Neat. He also noted the tremendous alphabetical usefulness of [Alexi, Zephyr Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Alexi%2C+Zephyr+Mage). The first thing to note here is that in order to win with [Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s), [Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s) must be in play, and that automatically knocks out some tricky letters like K and W. The second thing to note is that in order to win with [Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s), you're playing with *Unhinged* cards, so [Titleless Joe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) is legal in your format. It's the same color as [Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s), it only costs two mana (as opposed to [Vizzerdrix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vizzerdrix), [Kjeldoran Phalanx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Phalanx), and [Jhovall Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhovall+Queen)), and it's extremely flexible.


What to do with the rest of the deck? My first solution was to throw all of Paul's suggestions together. Except for the easily replaceable [Erg Raiders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erg+Raiders), all of the cards were white, blue, or green. But too many cards were too expensive, and I began cutting. I also started taking the lands into account. My first move was to use lands that included D and G to replace [Erg Raiders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Erg+Raiders). That way, [Now I Know My ABC's](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Now+I+Know+My+ABC%27s), [Teferi's Puzzle Box](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%27s+Puzzle+Box), [Jhovall Queen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jhovall+Queen), and a land would give me a win. I eventually refined that strategy: What if every land in the deck contained the same specific reasonably obscure letter? If every single land had a V in it, I could ignore V completely in my nonland permanents! Thanks to the snow-covered lands, it wasn't that hard to do.

### Apropos of Nothing


Crusader wrote to me to suggest two interactions for [Volrath's Nameshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252). One was to combo it with [Retraced Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retraced+Image). Think about it: On turn 2, you play [Blank Slate Boy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252). On turn 3, you change its name to [Akroma, Angel of Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Wrath), play [Retraced Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retraced+Image), and put an [Akroma, Angel of Wrath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Wrath) into play from your hand for free. (Note that the “legend rule” doesn't take effect because only the real Akroma is legendary.)




|  |
| --- |
| “Rules” corner: You can choose any card name for [Blanky](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252)'s ability, not just the name of a creature. |

I took this ability and ran with it. [Bifurcate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bifurcate) goes a step farther than [Retraced Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retraced+Image)—you snatch a giant creature from out of your *deck*, not your *hand*, and put it into play. [Mask of the Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+the+Mimic) is cheaper than [Bifurcate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bifurcate), it's an instant while [Bifurcate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bifurcate) is a sorcery, and it's the same color as the [Master of Dis Guys](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252)… but it requires you to sacrifice another creature to play it.


I soon realized that there was no reason for me to limit myself to only fetching giant creatures. And when I say “realize,” I mean that Crusader flat-out suggested this as a method to get [Gleemax](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gleemax) into play. Although [Bifurcate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bifurcate) will let me fetch only a creature, [Retraced Image](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Retraced+Image) isn't picky, and [Mask of the Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+the+Mimic) has a loophole. It will only target a creature, but it will fetch any kind of card.




|  |
| --- |
| “Rules” corner: The “anywhere” note means you can use [What's My Name Again?](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252)'s ability while it's in your graveyard, hand, or library. Or even out of the game. If you're playing an *Unhinged* format, it's the best mana sink ever made. You'll never take mana burn again. Except for ½ mana burn. |

Of course, the deck completely fails if I don't find an [Amnesiac](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252). [Myr Servitor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Servitor) will let me return any of them in my graveyard to play, and it serves as excellent [Mask of the Mimic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mask+of+the+Mimic) food. [Skyshroud Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyshroud+Sentinel) will let me fetch them from my library (after I spend the mana to change their names to [Skyshroud Sentinel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skyshroud+Sentinel), of course).


The only thing left is to fill the deck with ridiculous fatties and broken enchantments that I'd like to play for free as early as turn 3.

### Nothing to Wear


[Identity Thief](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) has too many uses to cram into decks in this humble little column, but I'll throw some more out there anyway.


* Use it with the *Odyssey* Shrines (not the good Shrines)! Change its name while it's in your graveyard.
* Similar but different—Use it in your graveyard to pump up your [Kindle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kindle)s and [Flame Burst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Burst)s.
* [Winnow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Winnow) + [Dr. Underscore](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) = Cantrip monowhite [Vindicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vindicate) that can't hit lands.
* Supplement your [Spirit of the Night](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spirit+of+the+Night) or [Viashivan Dragon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viashivan+Dragon) engines. You still need the [Urborg Panther](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urborg+Panther) or the [Kyscu Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kyscu+Drake), but this can sub in for the [Breathstealer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breathstealer), [Feral Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feral+Shadow), or [Spitting Drake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spitting+Drake).
* The deck I nearly made but didn't: [Your Title Here](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) + [Pack Hunt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pack+Hunt) + [Pyromancy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancy). Fetch three [Draco](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Draco)s at once.

### Nothing Lasts Forever


 



![Bazaar of Wonders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bazaar+of+Wonders)

Crusader's other suggestion was to pair [The Sound of One Hand Clapping](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) with [Bazaar of Wonders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+of+Wonders). Ouch. Let me be clear about this: O!U!C!H! That is harsh beyond belief. So, naturally, I love it. With this combo, you have the ability ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif): Counter target spell. (How? When your opponent plays a spell, [Bazaar of Wonders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+of+Wonders) triggers. In response, find a [Bob from Accounting](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) in play or in your graveyard and change its name to the name of that spell. When the Bazaar's ability resolves, it will counter the spell.) Um, that should win you the game. Sure, [Blinkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blinkmoth+Nexus) or other man-lands can still get you, and you need a way to deal with permanents already on the board. But seriously: Ouch.

I decided that this deck wanted card drawing. [Accumulated Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge) is happy to see [I'm Running Out of Clever Ways to Say Blank](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) in the graveyard. [Intuition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intuition) is happy to see [Accumulated Knowledge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Accumulated+Knowledge), but you may often be better off fetching three [Bazaar of Wonders](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bazaar+of+Wonders). [Urza's Hot Tub](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Urza%27s+Hot+Tub) turns [You Know the Card I Mean](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) into a [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor). Let's be clear now—don't change its name to “[Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor)”; change its name (while it's in your hand) to the card you want to fetch, then discard it to the Hot Tub. You can get whatever card in your deck you want for 3 colorless mana.

| “Rules” corner: Despite the “anytime” comment, you can't use [Ooflotak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252)'s ability while another spell or ability is in the midst of resolving. |

My favorite card-drawing trick, though, is to pair [Enough Already](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) with [My First Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=My+First+Tome). You need to be holding [It Has Malleable Flavor Text Too](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) and any other card that has flavor text (such as an artifact land, which is why they're in the deck) while you have [My First Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=My+First+Tome) on the table. Change [Mr. Wigglesbottom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252)'s flavor text into exactly what the other card's flavor text is. (Yes, the flavor text ability works, but you might want to have a printout of the [*Unhinged* FAQTIWDAWCC](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=magic/faq/unhinged) handy when you spring this for the first time on somebody.) Then activate [My First Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=My+First+Tome) and read that flavor text. If your opponent guesses the blank card, reveal the one that really has that flavor text. If your opponent guesses the one that really has that flavor text, reveal the blank one. It's a 2-mana [Jayemdae Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jayemdae+Tome)! Of course, this is probably vastly illegal and the powers that be (otherwise known as “Mark Rosewater”), having never noticed this loophole before, may swiftly close it. There might be some talk about how you're supposed to play fair, and rigorous techniques for using [My First Tome](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=My+First+Tome) may be invented, including placing the card whose flavor text you read face down on the table so you can't pull a switcheroo. I don't know what he'll rule—so get away with this while you can!


My last trick is to combo [Nameless One (Not the *Onslaught* One)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) with [Grim Reminder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Reminder). Once your opponent casts a spell—any spell—you can change the card's name in your library to the name of that spell, then play [Grim Reminder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Reminder) to find and reveal it. Goodbye, 6 life. [Undercover Brother](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=74252) is shuffled back into your library, and you can fish [Grim Reminder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Reminder) out of your graveyard to do this over and over. So the basic idea of this deck is that when your opponent casts a spell, you counter it, or make him lose 6 life, or both. And if he holds his spells, [Iron Maiden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Maiden) will get him. (By the way: There are [Iron Maiden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Maiden)s in the deck.) And to clean up any pre-Bazaar messes, to supplement the [Iron Maiden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iron+Maiden)s, and to take advantage of your card drawing, the deck naturally has [Turbulent Dreams](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Turbulent+Dreams). What better way to use your post-Bazaar dead cards?
