Chaining Through Legacy
=======================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on March 20, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_gerrythompson.jpg)
By Gerry Thompson












Legacy is a great format with a breadth of options, depending on what you're trying to do. If you're looking to play a combo deck, there are seemingly endless options, ranging from the suicidal "Oops! All Spells" to something a little more resilient, like today's deck.


![Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Food+Chain)
 With [Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain) and [Misthollow Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misthollow+Griffin) (which is searchable via [Manipulate Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manipulate+Fate)), you can create as much mana as you'd like, although you can only cast creature spells with it. As it turns out, there are some powerful creatures available in Legacy! [Tidespout Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidespout+Tyrant) can bounce all of your opponent's permanents, whereas [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) might kill your opponent outright. 


 Some people run a heavier combo version with cards like [Fierce Empath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fierce+Empath), but I like what Phil3579 has done here. Granted, I've got a soft spot for [Shardless Agent](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shardless+Agent), but it gives the deck a reasonable backup plan of beating down with value creatures if they stop you from assembling your combo. 



[Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain) is always something I wanted to try in Legacy and Phil3579 isn't making it any easier on me. Sometimes you durdle around for a bit and sometimes you kill opponents on turn twoâ€”I'm fine with either. Plus, you get to pitch [Misthollow Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misthollow+Griffin) to [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) for maximum value.
