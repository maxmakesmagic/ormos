Deck Tech: Food Chain with Koki Kageyama
========================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on November 27, 2016 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/chapman.jpg)
By Chapman Sim











At the end of Round 11, there were only two players at 11-0 and one of them was Kageyama Koki. Currently at the top of the Swiss standings, he looks like he knows what he's doing.


Kageyama is a relatively-new player with less than two years of experience. Picking up his first *Magic* card only during Khans of Tarkir block, most might expect him to be mediocre at best. However, if he has proven anything, it is that he has quickly caught up with twenty three years worth of *Magic* cards within a short time span and is now competing in Legacy with high levels of mastery!


Clearly, *Magic* is never too difficult to learn!


**![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/events/gpchi16/a_gpChiba16_D1_6kageyama_koki.jpg)**  
*Koki Kageyama, Top in Swiss after Round 11!*


While doing his research in preparation for Grand Prix Chiba, which is his third Grand Prix, he came across something similar and found the engine of [Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain) to be very abusable. Not only that, the deck looked like a lot of fun and he quickly put together the deck and started practicing. Relentlessly putting in games for the past three months, he's now very familiar with all the interactions in his deck and understands what to do in various matchups.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Food+Chain)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain)
“When I found the deck online, it is a little different from what I have today. However, the concept is the same and that it is a deck that works well against all fair decks. Shardless Sultai, Delver, Miracles, for example, are all examples of fair decks and [Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain) has a good matchup against them.”


Just these two cards alone generate infinite mana that you may spend on creatures. Exiling [Misthollow Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misthollow+Griffin) gets you five mana and you recast it back for four mana. And then you repeat it over and over. The same trick works with [Eternal Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Scourge) as well, because both these creatures are special in a way that they can be cast from exile.


In order to find these cards, [Manipulate Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manipulate+Fate) is perfect.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Manipulate+Fate)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manipulate+Fate)
“This is the most important card in beating Miracles. They must counter it. If it resolves, I'm basically drawing four cards for the price of one. [Misthollow Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misthollow+Griffin) and [Eternal Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Scourge) cannot be killed by 'exile' effects, so [Swords to Plowshares](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares) is useless against them. And if they use [Terminus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminus) to put it at the bottom of my library, I can cast another [Manipulate Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manipulate+Fate) to get all of them into exile again. If they end up in the graveyard somehow, I have also [Gurmag Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gurmag+Angler) to delve them away and also [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) to exile it.”


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Misthollow+Griffin)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misthollow+Griffin) [![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Force+of+Will)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will)
By the way, you can also exile Misthollow Raven to [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will). Nifty! It is bizarre that a powerful deck like Miracles could get beaten up by a card as odd as [Manipulate Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manipulate+Fate) but in this shell it is probably one of the best tools.


Once Kageyama has infinite mana, he can use [Fierce Empath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fierce+Empath) to search for [Emrakul, the Promised End](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Promised+End). Alternatively, he can also go for [Tidespout Tyrant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tidespout+Tyrant) to bounce his opponent's entire board of permanents and also bounce [Baleful Strix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baleful+Strix) over and over again to draw his entire library. Once you have unlimited mana at your disposal, it's not all that difficult to win.


But what if you don't always find [Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain) or if it gets destroyed or countered? Kageyama says that Plan B is actually very solid as well.


“Misthollow Griffin and Owl beatdown is the way to go. You'll be surprised how fast the damage adds up, and I also have [Deathrite Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathrite+Shaman) to make them lose life. I don't always rely on the combo and actually I won Round 10 by attacking with two [Misthollow Griffin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Misthollow+Griffin) that my opponent had no solution to.”


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Baleful+Strix)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baleful+Strix)[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Sylvan+Library)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Library)
Kageyama also points out that the deck is actually “very grindy” too. For example, [Baleful Strix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baleful+Strix) is usually a two-for-one, Misthollow Raven and [Eternal Scourge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eternal+Scourge) can't ever be killed, and [Manipulate Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manipulate+Fate) is bonkers (never thought I'll ever say that in my lifetime). There's also [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), [Ponder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ponder) and [Sylvan Library](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sylvan+Library) which makes it possible to stack up against decks like Shardless Sultai who also share common elements.


“Against unfair decks, Game 1 is tough but at least we have [Force of Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+of+Will) and [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) in the maindeck so we have a chance. My bad matchups are Sneak and Show, Reanimator and Storm. If you understand the weaknesses of [Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain), we can tune the sideboard to deal with those archetypes.”


Most players certainly did not expect [Food Chain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Food+Chain) this weekend and Kageyama has claimed a lot of victims. Will [Eternal Scourge win yet another Grand Prix](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpdfw16)? Let's see if Kageyama can win a couple more matches to lock up his spot in the Top 8!
