The Tireless Tribes of *Lorwyn*
===============================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 15, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 







By now you probably know that *Lorwyn* Constructed play was designed with very definite, linear, *tribal* themes in mind. Each of the several tribes has a unique flavor, building power via synergies and raw numbers, each deck truly a more significant object than the sum of its component cards. While the past couple of weeks we have looked at a variety of largely non-thematic builds—from black-green midrange [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker), to big green-red [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) + [Fertile Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertile+Ground), to the latest blue counterspell deck from Resident Genius control specialist Guillaume Wafo-Tapa—to date, Swimming With Sharks has touched only lightly on the tribal decks made possible by this great new set. This week, we correct that, and focus on the new set's local heroes, the tireless tribes of *Lorwyn*!

I think Josh Beck's Top 8 deck from Alaska States is a good example of a modern *Lorwyn* Goblins deck. Unlike most of the main Goblins decks of years past, *Lorwyn* Goblins are both black *and* red. Red has always been the main Goblins color, but the black splash becomes very thematically relevant as the tribe moves into graveyard-based card advantage, gaining bonuses for watching one's fellows fall into the abyss, &c.


 



![Wort, Boggart Auntie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Wort%2C+Boggart+Auntie)

The main source of raw card advantage is of course new card [Wort, Boggart Auntie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wort%2C+Boggart+Auntie); this big 3/3 is more than a polychromatic [Hill Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hill+Giant). Under her watchful eye, the Goblins deck is capable of recycling in-theme cards, both Goblin creature cards and [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)'s new best friend, [Tarfire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarfire) (both a [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) [and tribal instant] and a Goblin!). [Nameless Inversion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion) is a nice addition to this deck... Cards with changeling are Goblins, so [Nameless Inversion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion) has a natural synergy with Wort, [Auntie's Hovel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Auntie%27s+Hovel), or in a pinch, [Boggart Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Harbinger).

Some of the best Goblins in Standard are not from *Lorwyn*, so not surprisingly, this deck plays the classics... [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) (one of the best one-mana creatures of all time, Goblin or no) and [Mogg War Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal) (best friend to non-Goblin [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon)). On the other hand, the tribe has a lot of punch... maybe not as much as during *Onslaught* Block, but a lot nonetheless. [Knucklebone Witch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knucklebone+Witch) is extremely scary in the early game, kind of the Goblins equivalent to [Arcbound Ravager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Arcbound+Ravager); effective in many or even most non–lost cause situations, in an attrition fight, [Knucklebone Witch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knucklebone+Witch) is pure gold.


The Goblins deck play very well in-tribe, and extends its friendliness to [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon). [Mogg War Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal) is just too good with [Greater Gargadon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon) (if you suspend the Gargadon on turn one and follow up with [Mogg War Marshal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+War+Marshal)l you can still attack with two 1/1 Goblins on turn three if you are clever... you just get a bonus of one turn)... This deck has a couple of additional ways to generate tokens, one being the incomparable [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander). Both [Siege-Gang Commander](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Siege-Gang+Commander) and [Mad Auntie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Auntie) are absolute murderers with a large number of tiny Goblins in play.

![Aquitect's Will, Lord of Atlantis, and Riptide Pilferer](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/swimming/mf172_cardfan.jpg)Eight "pump" Merfolk really improve the offense to this deck, but to be honest, most of the current Merfolk already have fine bodies for their prices; you can't really argue with 1/1 for one, 2/1 for two with a special ability, &c.


Special mention *does* have to go to the deadly [Riptide Pilferer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Pilferer)... This is usually a sideboard card, and a scary one for most blue decks. Greg has three main... and thanks to [Lord of Atlantis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+Atlantis), [Riptide Pilferer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Pilferer) ain't never gettin' blocked. [Aquitect's Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquitect%27s+Will) is the perfect cantrip complement for this deck due to the Merfolk synergy with [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island)walk, making [Riptide Pilferer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riptide+Pilferer) or any of the Merfolk that much more deadly in non-blue matchups.


The only thing I find strange about Wilder's listing is that [Sygg, River Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sygg%2C+River+Guide) is missing (especially as he has the white splash with [Adarkar Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Adarkar+Wastes) and [Tideshaper Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tideshaper+Mystic)). Sygg seems like one of the most exciting Merfolk available in Standard, an awesome beater with [Aquitect's Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquitect%27s+Will) and an [Eight-and-a-Half-Tails](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eight-and-a-Half-Tails) at the same time. All of Wilder's white is out of the sideboard: defensive hosers against opposing creatures in [Condemn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Condemn) and [Teferi's Moat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%27s+Moat), and [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) as catch-all defense and of course planeswalker suppression.

For more information, visit [www.wizards.com/tribalwars](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtgcom/events/tribalwars07) and get ready to battle!


I was not originally very gung-ho about Faeries, but a chat with Zvi Mowshowitz set me straight on this tribe. Faeries might be the most tempo-oriented linear squad in *Lorwyn*, with (up to, potentially) the eight-pack of 1/1 flying creatures on turn one. Faeries are hard to kill: their [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) has flash and operates as a two-for-one [Counterspell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterspell) (with bite!) against removal; they can therefore hold a lead with the best of beatdown decks. Faeries are highly customizable: In these three decks alone, we have main deck [Sower of Temptation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sower+of+Temptation) and [Wydwen, the Biting Gale](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wydwen%2C+the+Biting+Gale); non-Faerie [Shadowmage Infiltrator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowmage+Infiltrator); or even [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror) (and that's not even considering a whole different color combination like we saw last week!). Silly as it seems to say out loud (or type), [Faerie Conclave](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faerie+Conclave)*is* a Faerie (or can at least become one)! I think you'd be surprised how many close games you win because the opponent didn't count on Conclave + [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona), probably setting up a [Psionic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Blast) to the head.


Like the Merfolk, Faeries seem like poor combat creatures but, practically speaking, nothing could be further from the truth; it's not that Faeries don't fight well... they *refuse* to fight. The entire tribe comes down at instant speed, flies, and (largely) just refuses to block. [Mistbind Clique](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique) does wonders in terms of grabbing a game the other guy thought he was going go win, suddenly a green-style body with a blue-style position on (or rather hovering above) the board. With so many flash spells (including [Scion of Oona](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona)), Faeries is extremely hard to race.


Hopefully this article was helpful in introducing some of the tribes of *Lorwyn*, what they can do, and what a winning deck from the recent States and Champs looks like (at least as a starting point).
