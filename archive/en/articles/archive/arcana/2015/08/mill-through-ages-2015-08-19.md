Mill Through the Ages
=====================



 Posted in **Arcana**
 on August 19, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/Bio_Yichao.jpg)
By Michael Yichao




 Yichao is a writer of words for plays, television, theme parks, and—most recently—Magic: The Gathering. He loves Cube Draft and corgis. 






*"More than one mage was driven insane by the sound of the [Millstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millstone) relentlessly grinding away."*


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/images/daily/cardart_ANT_Millstone.jpg)


[Millstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?multiverseid=1012) | Art by [Kaja Foglio](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Kaja+Foglio%22%5D)


Recently, Michael Majors took home the trophy at [Grand Prix San Diego 2015](http://magic.wizards.com/en/events/coverage/gpsd15) with a deck that relied on milling an opponent to death—that is, removing all the cards from an opponent's library. Majors's weapon of choice? [Sphinx's Tutelage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Tutelage), a powerful little uncommon enchantment.

By running a flurry of draw spells and 27(!) lands, the deck proved surprisingly consistent, digging for a copy of [Sphinx's Tutelage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx%27s+Tutelage) and ruthlessly grinding opponents out—sometimes in dramatically quick fashion.


In *Magic*'s history, mill decks have always occupied a special place in players' minds. Most players either love them (yours truly) or hate them (people who like "having fun" and "casting their spells"). Today, we take a look at a few mill decks from the past, in homage of Majors's victory.


ORIGINAL MILLSTONE
==================


At the very first Pro Tour in 1996, not one by *two* decks packing the original [Millstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millstone) made it to the Top 8, with [Michael Loconto taking home the top prize](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Events.aspx?x=mtgevent/ptny96/results)!

MILLSTONE: COMEBACK EDITION
===========================


We now jump forward in time to [US Nationals 2001](http://archive.wizards.com/sideboard/event.asp?event=USNAT01). Zvi Mowshowitz showed up with a blue-white deck featuring [Millstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millstone). The two-mana kill condition was great at sneaking into play before opponents could have counterspells ready, and the transformational sideboard would surprise beatdown players with some sweet giant fliers. Grind, grind, grind goes the [Millstone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Millstone)!
