Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget. Today's column is a continuation of the mega-series of articles dealing with [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). Yes, [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits)—a card so powerful that they had to ban it in Prismatic!

(Okay, granted, it would be problematic in a format that mandates 250 card decks—but I'll take my moral victories where I can get them!)


Before we get started, let me remind everyone of the ground rules for building a budget [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck:


1. [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) is the only rare allowed in the deck. This is a budget deck, and 240–250 cards already pushes the boundaries of many budgets.
2. The deck can't contain more than forty uncommons at any given time. When possible, I try to pick uncommons that aren't super-pricy, i.e., not [Remand](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) or [Harmonize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Harmonize).
3. This build of [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) is Standard-legal. In this article, that means *Ninth Edition*, *Ravnica*, *Guildpact*, *Dissension*, *Coldsnap*, *Time Spiral*, and *Planar Chaos* are allowed. You already knew this because you read the sidebar at the top of the article, right? *Right?* Sigh. Nobody reads that sidebar, do they? Poor, poor sidebar.
4. The intent is to win with [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). It's fine if it comes down to alternate win conditions every now and then, but we're not just going to take [Aeon in Flux](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/10-decks-10-weeks-aeon-flux-ur-2007-03-19), [Sheer Pandemonium](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/10-decks-10-weeks-sheer-pandemonium-wr-2007-04-16), [Profusion Confusion](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/10-decks-10-weeks-%E2%80%93-profusion-confusion-bg-2007-04-30), and [The Two Ladies](/en/articles/archive/building-budget/10-decks-10-weeks-two-ladies-gw-2007-03-05), mash them up in a cement truck, and see what messy Macedonian architecture spews forth.

To be honest, there weren't many *Planar Chaos* cards that would be good additions to the [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck—most of the black and blue uncommons and commons in the set are not silver bullets or card drawing / tutoring spells. Rather than make this a pretty boring article in which I add a single copy of [Piracy Charm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Piracy+Charm) to the deck, I decided to revamp the deck by adding green.


This is a project I've been toying with for a couple of revisions, but now seems to be the best time to move forward. Adding green to the deck covers a couple of glaring weaknesses I've found with the deck in the past, namely removing enchantments, artifacts, and flyers.


Green also allows for a more stable acceleration base. Part of the goal of the deck is to drop a Signet on the second turn, but often the color of the Signet is pretty irrelevant to the deck—for instance, here's the version we left off with from *Time Spiral*:

![](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dimir+Aqueduct)


















The deck doesn't run any white cards, yet there's [Azorius Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet), as a generic "accelerate blue mana" card. Green has plenty of early accelerators that can get the specific color of mana you need, while also providing a shuffle effect to things such as [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr) or [Telling Time](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telling+Time).


First, let's get a new mana base in the deck:


Out: 4 [Azorius Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Azorius+Signet), 4 [Izzet Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Signet), 4 [Rakdos Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Signet), 4 [Frost Marsh](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Marsh), 14 [Island](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), 14 [Swamp](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp)


In: 1 [Golgari Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Signet), 4 [Golgari Rot Farm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Rot+Farm), 4 [Simic Growth Chamber](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simic+Growth+Chamber), 28 [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest), 4 [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), 4 [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow), 4 [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek)


First, I added the [Golgari Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Signet) because it's on-color for both ends. Then, I put in copies of each of the green/X bouncelands from Ravnica block. Last, I put in [Forest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forest)s and the three best green mana-acceleration sorceries in Stanard.


 



![Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Putrefy)

Adding green also allows me to up the quality of certain removal spells. For instance, [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) is almost universally better than [Dark Banishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing), so I want to add in a card which can remove both artifacts and creatures, rather than just creatures.

Out: 3 [Dark Banishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing)  

In: 3 [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy)


Speaking of silver bullets, this deck runs on the strategy that, if you're running a 240-250 card deck with a ton of tutoring and transmute effects, you should be able to have at least one answer to any given situation somewhere in your deck. This time around, I've added [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) already (to answer artifacts). In addition, I'm putting in copies of [Krosan Grip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip) and [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) (for [Muddle the Mixture](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture) / [Dimir Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Infiltrator) and [Drift of Phantasms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drift+of+Phantasms) to tutor for), plus [Needle Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Needle+Storm) (in case I face down a bunch of flyers, which has happened in the past) and a single [Ana Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ana+Battlemage), as a tutorable body / discard spell.


To make room for them in the deck, I took out four [Perilous Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research) (the weakest card-drawing spell), three [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)s, and a [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang). The bounce spells were there to half-cover the deficiency in removing non-creature threats, and now that I'm packing both [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize) and [Krosan Grip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip), we should be set there.


In: 1 [Krosan Grip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip), 1 [Ana Battlemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ana+Battlemage), 1 [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize), 1 [Needle Storm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Needle+Storm)  

Out: 4 [Perilous Research](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research), 1 [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang), 3 [Repeal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)

Game 1: Vantere (R/G [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium))  

He gets a couple of early creatures and drops [Pandemonium](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pandemonium), and then [Primal Forcemage](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primal+Forcemage). I [Slay](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slay) the Forcemage and use [Mystical Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings) to get [Naturalize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Naturalize). Then, I get double [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) going with [Grim Harvest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Harvest), tutor up a hand full of bounce for a suspended [Greater Gargadon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Greater+Gargadon), and slowly build my mana with [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) and Signets. Finally, with a hand full of removal and bounce, I get [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) and win.  

Record: 1-0


Game 2: Snort71 ([Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits))  

He gets stuck on three mana and [Boomerang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boomerang)s one of my bounce lands. I just keep dropping lands, and when he taps out to play [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot) on my [Sage of Epityr](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sage+of+Epityr), I drop [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) and win.  

Record: 2-0


Game 3: Razzorrat (R/W [Pyrohemia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrohemia))  

He drops down [Guardian of the Guildpact](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Guardian+of+the+Guildpact) and [Seal of Fire](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seal+of+Fire). I get [Farseek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), [Rampant Growth](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rampant+Growth), [Golgari Signet](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Signet) and [Search for Tomorrow](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) by turn four. I also drop [Dimir Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Infiltrator) to block his 2/3 guy, but he uses [Seething Song](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seething+Song) to get out [Pyrohemia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrohemia) and uses that to kill my board. I [Mystical Teachings](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings) for [Putrefy](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) to kill his Guardian, which, in turn, kills his [Pyrohemia](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyrohemia). He passes the turn, and I flash back the Teachings to get [Clutch of the Undercity](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clutch+of+the+Undercity) and transmute that into [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), which fetches [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) to finish the game.  

Record: 3-0


![Clutch of the Undercity and Diabolic Tutor](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/building/bb112_cardfan.jpg)Game 4: Coccobill (G/B Aggro)  

He gets an early [Penumbra Spider](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Penumbra+Spider) and Putrefies a Looter *il*-Kor. I get another Looter, and drop both [Dimir House Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+House+Guard) and [Dimir Infiltrator](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dimir+Infiltrator) to stop his guy. He plays [Llanowar Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Elves) and I strip the rest of his hand with [Consult the Necrosages](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consult+the+Necrosages), including a [Krosan Grip](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Krosan+Grip). He plays [Gravedigger](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gravedigger) and [Golgari Brownscale](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Golgari+Brownscale), and I set up my board to get [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) via the transmute / [Diabolic Tutor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) route, with [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot) in hand just in case.  

Record: 4-0


Game 5: Nitz007 (R/W Slivers)  

He gets triple [Two-Headed Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Two-Headed+Sliver) with [Sinew Sliver](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sinew+Sliver). I nearly stabilize, but I die to a second [Rift Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt) at 2 life after locking up the board with [Thought Courier](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier), [Nekrataal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal), and [Drift of Phantasms](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drift+of+Phantasms).  

Record: 4-1


All right, it's time for me to grouse again—there really needs to be an uncommon black board sweeper somewhere in Standard. Right now, you need to play red in order to remove weenies en masse in anything less than the rare slot ([Pyroclasm](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm), [Sulfurous Blast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sulfurous+Blast)), so this deck still has a weakness against a rush of smaller creatures. I miss the days of [Infest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) and [Hideous Laughter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+Laughter).


Well, since we're playing green, we have one sort-of removal spell ([Rolling Spoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Spoil), which also gives the deck a way to kill a land, if need be). I can also add in life gain, so that I can buy a turn or two if I'm low on life. The cards I want for this slot are [Natural Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Spring) (a great one-shot lifegain effect) and [Sophic Centaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sophic+Centaur). I often have a lot of cards in hand, and [Sophic Centaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sophic+Centaur) seems like it can make a huge difference if left unchecked.


In: 1 [Rolling Spoil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rolling+Spoil), 1 [Natural Spring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Natural+Spring), 1 [Sophic Centaur](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sophic+Centaur)  

Out: [Strangling Soot](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strangling+Soot), 4 [Chromatic Star](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chromatic+Star)

Once again, a very respectable record pulled with this deck. I really recommend building this (or some version) of [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) if you play **Magic Online**. [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) really takes advantage of the online format, since you don't have to sit there shuffling a monstrous deck after every game or tutoring effect. [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) is a great test of deckbuilding. Unlike an ordinary deck, where you're tuning to get the tightest draw possible within a sixty-card limit, [Battle of Wits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) makes you fit in a ton of extra cards for the benefit of playing a card that says "![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227130050im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless3.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif): You win the game." This gives you an opportunity to play niche cards, fringe cards, pet cards, and fun cards, ones that you normally wouldn't be able to jam into a tuned deck.
