Soul Searching
==============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on November 13, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods












So, one thing we all like to do, at one level or another, is "go deep." This bit of terminology, which can technically mean just about anything, is generally characterized by someone doing something out of the ordinary—and sometimes even convolutedly. Instead of winning with [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) in Storm, maybe you try to win with [Dingus Egg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dingus+Egg) plus [Volcanic Awakening](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Awakening). You, my friend, have just gone deep. A few weeks ago, we featured a deck that I thought did a lot of digging to come up with its concept—[one featuring](/en/articles/archive/blinking-deadeye-2012-10-25) [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator). But, as we discover today, there was an entirely different level of deep to be had! 

![Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Deadeye+Navigator) Josh Hendricks showed up to the StarCityGames Open in St. Louis also featuring [Deadeye Navigator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deadeye+Navigator), but this time, the six-drop only appeared as a two-of despite being exactly what the deck is all about. Why? Well, Josh had to fit about a thousand other little synergies and combos within the deck. Sure, [Jace, Architect of Thought](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+Architect+of+Thought) and  [Tamiyo, the Moon Sage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=+Tamiyo%2C+the+Moon+Sage) are both just powerful cards, but look a little deeper. 


[Nightshade Peddler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightshade+Peddler)? Ever heard of it? In this list, if the Peddler gets bonded up with say, an [Izzet Staticaster](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Staticaster), you end up with a [Maelstrom Pulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maelstrom+Pulse)/[Visara the Dreadful](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Visara+the+Dreadful) hybrid. What if "just" a [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) or [Niv-Mizzet, Dracogenius](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Niv-Mizzet%2C+Dracogenius) hooks up with the Peddler? Yea, that [Shock](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shock) is now [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal) and that ping is now [Terminate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terminate). Plenty of other awesome cards, like [Gilded Lotus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gilded+Lotus), all make the deck stand out even more. It finished with a solid Top 16 badge, so give this deck a shot and see how deep you can go!
