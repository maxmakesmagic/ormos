2007 Magic World Championships Tournament Format
================================================



 Posted in **Feature**
 on July 13, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/wizards_author.jpg)
By Wizards of the Coast











*All Universal Tournament Rules and Magic: the Gathering Floor Rules are in effect for this event unless specifically overruled by information in this Format Document*

### Schedule

Play will begin each day at 9:00 AM. Players should be in the tournament hall by 8:45 AM each day.

### Format

- Individual formats: Standard, Booster Draft, and Legacy
- National Team format: Two-Headed Giant Booster Draft
- Check [www.thedci.com](http://www.thedci.com) for all DCI format rules
### Daily Information

**Day 1 (Thursday, December 6)** 
- Standard format - 5 Swiss rounds
- Booster Draft format – 1 Booster draft followed by 3 Swiss rounds
- 3 *Lorwyn*™ booster packs will be used for drafting
**Day 2 (Friday, December 7)** 
- Booster Draft format – 1 Booster draft followed by 3 Swiss rounds
- 3 *Lorwyn* booster packs will be used for drafting
- Legacy format - 5 Swiss rounds
**Day 3 (Saturday, December 8)** 
- Two-Headed Giant Booster Draft Format
- 6 *Lorwyn* booster packs will be used for drafting
- Each National Team will consist of 2 “sub-teams” *(example: USA 1 and USA 2)*
- Each sub-team will draft separate from each other
* Each sub-team will be drafting with the same National Teams  *(example: if USA 1, France 1, Germany 1 and Italy 1 draft together in a pod, then USA 2, France 2, Germany 2, and Italy 2 will draft together in a pod)*
* Each sub-team will play the same National Team each round  *(example: if USA 1 plays France 1, then USA 2 plays France 2)*
* The composition of the sub-teams will change before each draft  *(example: Players A, B, C and D are on a National Team. During draft 1, the sub-teams will consist of players A &B and C & D. During draft 2, the sub-teams will consist of players A & C and B & D. During draft 3, the sub-teams will consist of players A & D and B & C.)*

- 6 Swiss rounds will be played (3 drafts with 2 Swiss rounds per draft.)
**Day 4 (Sunday, December 9)** 
- The Top 8 individual players and the Top 2 National Teams will compete on Sunday
- The Top 8 individual matches will be best 3 of 5, the Top 2 Team matches will be single game matches
- Individual players will use the same Standard decks that they used on Thursday
- National Teams will play Two-Headed Giant Booster Draft.
* The composition of each sub-team will be chosen by the members of the National Team.
* The two sub-teams from each National Team will sit across from each other at the draft table.
* Each sub-team will play randomly play a sub-team from the other National Team.
* If both sub-teams from a National Team win their matches, they will be the National Team Champion
* If one sub-team wins and one sub-team loses, the winning sub-teams will play another match to determine the National Team Champion.

### Tournament Length

**Individual formats**
- 16 Swiss Rounds total, 60 minutes per round (Constructed) or 55 minutes per round (Booster Draft)
- Booster Draft deck construction time limit will be 30 minutes
- Top 8 single-elimination finals have no time limit; however, players are expected to play at a reasonable pace and finish their matches within 90 minutes.
**National Team format**
- 6 Swiss Rounds total, 50 minutes per round
- Two-Headed Giant Booster Draft deck construction time limit will be 40 minutes
- Top 2 single-elimination finals have no time limit; however, players are expected to play at a reasonable pace and finish their matches within 60 minutes.
### Advancement (Individual formats)

- All players may participate in all Individual-format Swiss rounds
- After round 16 the top 8 players advance to Sunday’s top 8 single-elimination finals
### Advancement (National Team format)

- The national team members’ match points during the first 2 days will count towards a National Team’s accumulated score
- Each team win during the National Team day will add an additional 9 points to a team’s accumulated score
* If both sub-teams win during a round, the team receives 9 points
* If both sub-teams lose during a round, the team receives 0 points
* If one sub-team wins and the other loses, the team receives 3 points

- Members of individual National Teams will not be paired against each other at any time during the Swiss portions of the tournament
- After round 6 the top 2 National teams advance to Sunday’s top 2 single-elimination finals
### Notes

- 48K, REL 5
- Players are required to use the same Standard deck for day 1 and Top 8.
- Players are responsible for knowing all DCI rules and high-level tournament rules
- Players must bring pen, paper, card sleeves and appropriate counters
- Players will be drafting from boosters that have had the foils cards replaced. Foils from *Lorwyn* boosters are replaced as follows: Common, uncommon, and rare foils are replaced by a random, unduplicated *Lorwyn* common card.
