Fighting the Good Battle (of Wits)
==================================



 Posted in **Building on a Budget**
 on January 30, 2008 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












Hello everyone, and welcome back to Building on a Budget! In this week's article, we've got a lot going on—the results of the "What is Budget?" poll and forum discussion, the evolution of one of my personal favorite decks, and the first round of playtesting for the Extended budget-competitive Intruder Alert deck! Let's dive right in, shall we?


A couple of weeks ago, I asked you, the reader, two questions in the form of a poll. The first question:




| **How should Budget be determined for this column?** |
| --- |
| By a strict ticket count. | 3855 | 82.0% |
| By a strict limit on number of rares (under a certain ticket amount) and uncommons per deck. | 844 | 18.0% |
| **Total** | **4699** | **100.0%** |

The results of this poll are pretty conclusive and definite—BoaB should continue to be defined by a strict ticket limit from **Magic Online**. This leads to the second question: what should that ticket limit be?




| **What is the maximum cost, in tickets, of a budget deck?**  |
| --- |
| 30 Tickets | 2432 | 55.2% |
| 25 Tickets | 466 | 10.6% |
| 20 Tickets | 845 | 19.2% |
| 15 Tickets | 290 | 6.6% |
| 10 Tickets | 188 | 4.3% |
| Less than 10 Tickets | 185 | 4.2% |
| **Total** | **4406** | **100.0%** |

While a majority voted that they were happy with a 30 ticket limit, quite a few people (approximately 45%) wanted a lower ticket limit. Many expressed their thoughts in the forums as follows: even if 30 tickets is fine as a limit, I shouldn't strive to hit 30 tickets each week if I don't need to—just fill out decks with the right cards, and if cheaper and viable alternatives are available, go with them. Fair enough! Look for me to start shifting in this direction for future columns - 30 tickets as a limit, but less consciously trying to hit that 30 ticket limit unless absolutely necessary as called for by that deck.


One of my favorite decks to evolve in the Building on a Budget column is the [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) decks. With the release of each new set, I would change up the deck with the newest cards, until the unthinkable happened: [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) was rotated out of the base set with *Tenth Edition*, and [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) was Standard legal... no more!


 



![Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Battle+of+Wits)

A good number of people wrote over the past few months to ask me when I was going to evolve [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) in Extended. The time is now—I love being able to instantly win the game with a pile of huge cards, and playing [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) online is ideal, since you don't have to deal with shuffling a ridiculously large stack of cardboard.

For those who are witnessing the total awesome power of [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) for the first time, note—this is [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), budget style! You have to be especially budget-conscious when making a deck that has over 200 cards, since costs of even the commons can add up. Here's a brief rundown of the official (tm) Building on a Budget [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) Building rules!


1) The deck shalt be between 240-250 cards. This alloweth yon Bleiweiss to draw cards with spells, yet not put himself below 200 cards for his win condition, [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits).  

2) No rares will be allowed in the deck other than [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). No more than 40 uncommons at one time, and their expense shall be kept down.  

3) [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) is legal in Extended now, so Extended shall be the format. Cards from *Seventh*, *Eighth*, *Ninth*, and *Tenth Edition* will be allowed, in addition to *Coldsnap*, *Invasion* block, *Odyssey* block, *Onslaught* block, *Mirrodin* block, Kamigawa block, *Ravnica* block, *Time Spiral* block, and *Lorwyn* (whew—that's a lot of sets!)


I had moved the [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) deck to a three-color build as of *Planar Chaos*, but I decided to revert it back to a two-color build (black and blue) since Extended has a much larger card pool. Here's a list from the last time I played [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) as a two-color deck.

Since we have a much larger card pool than was available in Standard at the last update, I figured I'd need to go through the list of all the cards available in blue, black, artifact, and land for this deck. I pulled up a spoiler generator, and first went through commons, then uncommons, and then cross-referenced ticket prices to see which cards were affordable for the deck.


Just as a reminder—the main strategy of this deck is to drop a [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), and as quickly as possible. The deck achieves this by having a ton of card-drawing and card-filtering spells, along with multiple tutoring devices ([Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) and transmute cards from *Ravnica*). Because the deck is between 240 and 250 cards at any given time, there are many silver bullets in the deck—cards that are a one or two-of that are good against one specific strategy and can be tutored for, but that you might not draw in any given game due to the size of the deck.

In order to fix up the deck, it was time to take cards out and put cards in. The first swap I made was removing [Counsel of the Soratami](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counsel+of+the+Soratami) for [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)—[Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) is almost always the superior card choice, as it can be played as a creature or more like a sorcery.


[Frost Marsh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Marsh) has gone up a lot in price due to a snow-based deck in Standard, so it was no longer a viable budget option. However, the cycling lands from *Onslaught* are perfect for this deck—when I don't need them they are a new card, and when I do need them, they are a land. I put in 4 [Lonely Sandbar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lonely+Sandbar) and 4 Barron Moor in place of the 4 [Frost Marsh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Frost+Marsh), 2 [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island), and 2 [Swamp](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swamp).


Instead of having both [Execute](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Execute) and [Slay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slay), I took them both out and added in [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark).


There weren't any good mass-removal spells available in standard last I used it, but [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) and [Hideous Laughter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hideous+Laughter) are now both available. I added one of each in (to be able to transmute for either/or depending on the transmute card in hand). I also added [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror), [Rend Flesh](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rend+Flesh) and [Eradicate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eradicate), and took out [Dark Banishing](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing)s.


The deck wasn't great on graveyard hate, but Extended allowed me to add [Nezumi Graverobber](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nezumi+Graverobber), [Withered Wretch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Withered+Wretch), and [Coffin Purge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Coffin+Purge), and take out [Cremate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cremate).


Extended has a lot more silver bullets than Standard (due to a large card pool) and I already had a large card-drawing pool. [Treasure Trove](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Trove), [Sift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sift), [Consult the Necrosages](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consult+the+Necrosages), [Perilous Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research), and [Telling Time](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Telling+Time) all got the axe, and were replaced with more situationally useful cards. These cards include:


[Echoing Truth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Truth) / [Echoing Decay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Echoing+Decay): To deal with lots of token creatures, such as from an [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) or from an [Intruder Alarm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Intruder+Alarm) deck.


[Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe): Discard and card drawing.


[Death Denied](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Denied): Originally in the early builds of the deck as a good one-of to return most of your creatures in one shot.


[Rancid Earth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancid+Earth): The deck is lacking in land destruction. Now it has a spell to kill a troublesome land.


[Wash Out](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wash+Out): As a one-of, since it is slightly more expensive. In the right situation, capable of taking out the opponent's entire board.


[Shriekmaw](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw): Also as a one-of, and a strict one-for-one swap with a [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal). This gives a five-drop tutor target to go along with multiple two, three and four drops.


[Mind Sludge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mind+Sludge): Just for the off-chance I can take out my opponent's entire hand.


[Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar): With so many blue spells in this deck, [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar) will likely shave several mana off costs over the course of the game. Also, it's an early defense against ground creatures, if need be.

Game 1: dxpong (Blue-White ?)


I get a couple of card-drawing spells and cycling lands, and he is stuck on two mana. I play the one [Rancid Earth](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancid+Earth) in my deck on his [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), and he concedes.


**Record: 1-0**


Game 2: Ninja24601 (Red-Black Mill)


He gets a second-turn suspended [Wheel of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fate), and hits me with a turn-four [Barbed Shocker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Barbed+Shocker), knocking out a set-up of [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) into [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). I recover with [Nekrataal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nekrataal), and draw a [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) off of the [Wheel of Fate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wheel+of+Fate), winning me the game.


**Record: 2-0**


 



![Pull from Eternity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Pull+from+Eternity)

Notes: The deck doesn't have a way to deal with suspended cards. [Riftsweeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftsweeper) and [Pull from Eternity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pull+from+Eternity) (off-color) would be ways to work around this. Also, I could add in a single [Clockspinning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clockspinning).

Game 3: cyberbobbo (Red-White Burn)


Over the course of the game, he plays [Flamebreak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flamebreak), double [Lava Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lava+Spike), double [Pulse of the Forge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Forge), and [Rift Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rift+Bolt). Thankfully, I have a [Muddle the Mixture](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Muddle+the+Mixture) in hand for one of the [Pulse of the Forge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+the+Forge)s, and this allows me to [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor) for [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits), allowing me to win the game at four life.


**Record: 3-0**


Notes: Black has ways to gain life, but there aren't a whole lot in this deck. If I'm about to get burned out, it might be good to have an out or two ([Consume Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+Spirit), [Soul Feast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soul+Feast), [Corrupt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Corrupt)) to gain that life in a pinch.


Game 4: numsixof1 (Red-Green Aggro)


I get a second-turn [Thought Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier) and use it to dig for a fifth land to play [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). In the meanwhile, he plays [Imperious Perfect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imperious+Perfect) ([Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)ed) and dies with a [Prodigal Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prodigal+Pyromancer) on the board.


**Record: 4-0**


Game 5: Tetmon ([Nantuko Shrine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nantuko+Shrine) / [Scrabbling Claws](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scrabbling+Claws)) (Full Match)


Game 1: I get [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits) on turn five, and I win. I had to tutor up my fifth land on turn four with [Diabolic Tutor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Diabolic+Tutor), though, but I had seen his hand on turn three with [Nightmare Void](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightmare+Void) (thanks to a Signet), so I knew he had no answers in hand.


Game 2: I get another turn-five [Battle of Wits](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battle+of+Wits). He has [Rending Vines](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rending+Vines) in hand. Unfortunately for him, he had played double [Sakura-Tribe Elder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sakura-Tribe+Elder) and [Kodama's Reach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kodama%27s+Reach) and played a land a turn, so he was short a card in hand to be able to destroy it.


**Record: 5-0**


Game 6: Velvet\_Devil (Red-White Gargadon)


I get a set-up with some guys, and ways to kill his Gargadon. He drops [Yosei, the Morning Star](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Yosei%252C%2Bthe%2BMorning%2BStar), and I [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark) it. He then drops a [Belfry Spirit](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Belfry+Spirit) when I'm tapped out, and uses the next turn to [Boom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Boom). I have two signets and a [Thought Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Courier) out, with an [Infest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) in hand. I don't find my first land until three turns later, and by then he's found a red for another Gargadon, which seals the game.


**Record: 5-1**


Notes: Need instant-speed card drawing—I had [Mystical Teachings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystical+Teachings) in hand while we were stalemated, and I wanted to get a card that would draw me several more cards (cards are options!), but there was nothing good in the deck. Also, [Dizzy Spell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dizzy+Spell) really can't get much of note, aside from [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) and [Deathmark](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deathmark), neither of which are probably worth the [Dizzy Spell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dizzy+Spell) Slots.


With the previous games in mind, I took out the [Dizzy Spell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dizzy+Spell)s, a [Sudden Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death), and a [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) and added in [Careful Consideration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Careful+Consideration), [Opportunity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opportunity), two [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse), and two [Exclude](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude). [Exclude](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exclude) gives me a [Remove Soul](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remove+Soul) that draws a card, and [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse) gives me a three-drop bounce spell to replace [Wipe Away](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wipe+Away).


**Out:** 4 [Dizzy Spell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dizzy+Spell), 1 [Sudden Death](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sudden+Death), 1 [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)
