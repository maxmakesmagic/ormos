Why Walk When You Can Fly?
==========================



 Posted in **Limited Information**
 on November 13, 2007 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/generic-avatar-150_256.png)
By Quentin Martin












This article doesn't need much of an introduction. It's the eagerly awaited first *Lorwyn* draft walkthrough. As the set is still new, picks are still uncertain, and people have yet to form clear and concise (and accurate) opinions about cards, there's more detail than normal. Note how most of the picks juggle tribal worth. At the beginning, tribal cards present diametrically opposed reactions. Some are powerful enough so that when you see them early, you dive into that archetype, whilst some just aren't powerful enough to warrant picking early because they only bloom in the right environment. It is a tricky line to walk between when picking a card is justified and when it is correct to pass it by. Let's crack some packs.


### Pack 1


**Pack 1, Pick 1**


![Pack 1 -- Click to enlarge](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/magic/images/mtgcom/fcpics/limited/qm35_Pack1_Small.jpg)[Surge of Thoughtweft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surge+of+Thoughtweft), [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter), [Weed Strangle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Weed+Strangle), [Mudbutton Torchrunner](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mudbutton+Torchrunner), [Woodland Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Changeling), [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum), [Kithkin Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kithkin+Healer), [Springjack Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springjack+Knight), [Heal the Scars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heal+the+Scars), [Inkfathom Divers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkfathom+Divers), [Boggart Birth Rite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Birth+Rite), [Hunter of Eyeblights](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunter+of+Eyeblights), [Woodland Guidance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Guidance), [Glarewielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glarewielder), [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess)


A reasonably strong pack to kick start with. Were it not for the rare, we would slam the [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter). I want to talk a little about [Hunter of Eyeblights](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunter+of+Eyeblights). I think it is an incredibly powerful card. Sure, he's a little slow, but he will often get the job done and in the process he'll leave you with a 3/3 body. He's kind of like an expensive [Assassinate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assassinate) that you take an additional point of damage from. At the end of the day though, he is a removal spell that two-for-ones.


Pick: [Liliana Vess](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+Vess)


**Pack 1, Pick 2**


[Kithkin Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kithkin+Healer), [Deeptread Merrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deeptread+Merrow), [Kithkin Daggerdare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kithkin+Daggerdare), [Herbal Poultice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herbal+Poultice), [Avian Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avian+Changeling), [Warren Pilferers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warren+Pilferers), [Aquitect's Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquitect%27s+Will), [Kinsbaile Skirmisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kinsbaile+Skirmisher), [Spring Cleaning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spring+Cleaning), [Blind-Spot Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Blind-Spot+Giant), [Giant Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Harbinger), [Scattering Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattering+Stroke), [Silvergill Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvergill+Adept), [Mad Auntie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Auntie)


Lots of options here. [Mad Auntie](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mad+Auntie) and [Warren Pilferers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warren+Pilferers) are certainly lead contenders and are black to boot. I don't like taking a [Giant Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant+Harbinger) high unless I already have a [Nameless Inversion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nameless+Inversion) or a [Thundercloud Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thundercloud+Shaman). [Avian Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avian+Changeling) is great but not a contender with the Pilferers. [Silvergill Adept](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvergill+Adept) is a strong card; I like Merfolk and I am a fan of blue-black, even though I prefer Faeries in that colour combination. This leaves us toying between the two black cards. The Auntie is certainly a cute card, but much like [Wizened Cenn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wizened+Cenn), the tribe it makes you fill your deck with really is not that strong. This means we can take the more flexible, arguably more powerful Goblin.


Pick: [Warren Pilferers](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warren+Pilferers)


**Pack 1, Pick 3**


[Moonglove Extract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonglove+Extract), [Boggart Sprite-Chaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Sprite-Chaser), [Oaken Brawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oaken+Brawler), [Smokebraider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider), [Stonybrook Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonybrook+Angler), [Fertile Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertile+Ground), [Dawnfluke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawnfluke), [Broken Ambitions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Broken+Ambitions), [Nath's Buffoon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nath%27s+Buffoon), [Faultgrinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faultgrinder), [Drowner of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Secrets), [Treefolk Harbinger](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treefolk+Harbinger), [Spinerock Knoll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spinerock+Knoll)


Another phenomenal pack. Before I go into the actual pick decision too much, I want to talk about the hideaway land. These cards are great and currently underrated. If you're about to pick a mediocre card for you deck instead—don't; it will probably be cut any way. These land are almost always good. They get activated in most games, giving you a free spell. Sure, several of their trigger conditions tend to require you to be winning (not [Shelldock Isle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shelldock+Isle), which is by far the best), but that's still fine. For example, [Mosswort Bridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mosswort+Bridge) often gives you a free card when you are entrenched in a [Silvergill Douser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silvergill+Douser)–induced stall. Pick these higher.


[Moonglove Extract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonglove+Extract) is always going to be a contender to be picked so we'll see what else there is. I don't want to really take the [Smokebraider](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smokebraider) and commit to elementals at this point, although I do love to if I open a [Mulldrifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter) or [Aethersnipe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Aethersnipe). This leaves us choosing between the two merfolk.


Last week, I said I would take [Stonybrook Angler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stonybrook+Angler) over [Drowner of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drowner+of+Secrets), and corrected myself in the forums. I'm still not too sure. I prefer to pick up the Drowner over worse cards, when I already know that I'm Merfolk, because otherwise it is a fairly unexciting 1/3, like a [Vedalken Entrancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vedalken+Entrancer) outside of a Dimir deck. Don't get me wrong, the card is great, but this early on, before I know I'm Merfolk, I prefer the Angler. This is because firstly, in blue-black, you tend not to be Merfolk-heavy and secondly, the Angler is still amazing within a Merfolk deck where it can untap a [Douse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Douse)r or a Streambed Aquitect to shut off combat more effectively than tapping might achieve.


Having said all of that, we're not necessarily blue yet and I think the [Extract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extract) is better than both Merfolk either way (unless you are already deep, in which case the Drowner can be better if you are light on win conditions).


Pick: [Moonglove Extract](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Moonglove+Extract)


**Pack 1, Pick 4**


[Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite), [Boggart Sprite-Chaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Sprite-Chaser), [Thieving Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Sprite), [Kinsbaile Balloonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kinsbaile+Balloonist), [Whirlpool Whelm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirlpool+Whelm), [Black Poplar Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Black+Poplar+Shaman), [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto), [Elvish Eulogist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Eulogist), [Giant's Ire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant%27s+Ire), [Lairwatch Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lairwatch+Giant), [Seedguide Ash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seedguide+Ash), [Prowess of the Fair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prowess+of+the+Fair)


Neither [Thieving Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Sprite) nor [Shimmering Grotto](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shimmering+Grotto) are powerful enough for us to stay with the black plan, so it's time to look elsewhere. [Seedguide Ash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seedguide+Ash) is just a wrapped-up Durkwood Boars. For why I won't be picking the [Kinsbaile Balloonist](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kinsbaile+Balloonist), read [last week's article](/en/articles/archive/limited-information/so-youve-first-picked-oblivion-ring-now-what-2007-11-06), but on top of this both blue cards are more powerful. [Whirlpool Whelm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirlpool+Whelm) is a great trick but [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) is one of the better commons in the set as it does so much for so little. Untapping blockers, tapping attackers, [Rishadan Port](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Rishadan%2BPort)ing land in upkeeps, untapping Merfolk mid-combat to use their abilities again, triggering [Dreamspoiler Witches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamspoiler+Witches), and then it attacks to boot.


Pick: [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite)


**Pack 1, Pick 5**


[Wispmare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wispmare), [Thieving Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Sprite), [Hillcomber Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hillcomber+Giant), [Warren-Scourge Elf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Warren-Scourge+Elf), [Lairwatch Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lairwatch+Giant), [Spiderwig Boggart](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spiderwig+Boggart), [Boggart Forager](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Forager), [Pollen Lullaby](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pollen+Lullaby), [Vivid Creek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Creek), [Merrow Commerce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Commerce), [Thorn of Amethyst](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn+of+Amethyst)


Unexciting, with the best playable being both in black and promoting any future Faerie theme.


Pick: [Thieving Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thieving+Sprite)


**Pack 1, Pick 6**


[Battlewand Oak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Battlewand+Oak), [Hurly-Burly](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hurly-Burly), [Plover Knights](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plover+Knights), [Paperfin Rascal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paperfin+Rascal), [Facevaulter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Facevaulter), [Hunt Down](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunt+Down), [Giant's Ire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Giant%27s+Ire), [Dawnfluke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawnfluke), [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite), [Knucklebone Witch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knucklebone+Witch)


The best card in this pack is the late Plover Knight. Normally I would snap up such a late colour signal as this, but I won't for several reasons. Firstly, I'm not a big fan of white, but that isn't the deciding factor here. I already have two five-drops and am likely to pick up more, and I don't want the deck to become too heavy. On top of all of this, white and black have no tribal synergy between them, meaning I won't be able to capitalize on any tribal theme. Added to all of this, there is a [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) to push us into Faeries and to combo with any [Dreamspoiler Witches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamspoiler+Witches) down the line. The [Paperfin Rascal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Paperfin+Rascal) never gets a shoe in as he's just pretty bad unless you really need a Merfolk.


Pick: [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite)


**Pack 1, Pick 7**


[Deeptread Merrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deeptread+Merrow), [Fistful of Force](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fistful+of+Force), [Soulbright Flamekin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soulbright+Flamekin), [Shields of Velis Vel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shields+of+Velis+Vel), [Fire-Belly Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fire-Belly+Changeling), [Elvish Branchbender](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elvish+Branchbender), [Amoeboid Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amoeboid+Changeling), [Entangling Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Entangling+Trap), [Vivid Crag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vivid+Crag)


Pick: [Amoeboid Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amoeboid+Changeling)


**Pack 1, Pick 8**


[Surge of Thoughtweft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Surge+of+Thoughtweft), [Wellgabber Apothecary](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wellgabber+Apothecary), [Aquitect's Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquitect%27s+Will), [Mournwhelk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk), [Zephyr Net](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Zephyr+Net), [Scarred Vinebreeder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scarred+Vinebreeder), [Hearthcage Giant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hearthcage+Giant), [Merrow Commerce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merrow+Commerce)


Pick: [Mournwhelk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk)


**Pack 1, Pick 9**


[Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum), [Kithkin Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kithkin+Healer), [Springjack Knight](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springjack+Knight), [Heal the Scars](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Heal+the+Scars), [Boggart Birth Rite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Birth+Rite), [Woodland Guidance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Woodland+Guidance), [Glarewielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glarewielder)


I was very tempted to cut the [Glarewielder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glarewielder) here as he is a very powerful card that generates a massive tempo swing and will often win you the game right there. However, the [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) means I have more options to splash something if it comes my way, or to tap a [Fallowsage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallowsage) if I'm short on playables. It keeps my options open, which is far better than cutting a card from someone who probably won't be one of your opponents.


Pick: [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum)


**Pack 1, Pick 10**


[Kithkin Healer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kithkin+Healer), [Herbal Poultice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Herbal+Poultice), [Aquitect's Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aquitect%27s+Will), [Kinsbaile Skirmisher](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kinsbaile+Skirmisher), [Spring Cleaning](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spring+Cleaning), [Scattering Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattering+Stroke)


[Scattering Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattering+Stroke) may not be the best card in the world, and when compared to the other counters in the format, it certainly pales in comparison, but sometimes you just need a counterspell, or another instant to trigger [Dreamspoiler Witches](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dreamspoiler+Witches). It probably won't make the final deck, but it's not the end of the world in a Faerie deck, where you often leave four mana up for a flash creature. I also sideboard it in a lot against slow decks and decks with big bombs I have no answer to.


Pick: [Scattering Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattering+Stroke)


**Pack 1, Pick 11**


[Boggart Sprite-Chaser](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Sprite-Chaser), [Oaken Brawler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oaken+Brawler), [Dawnfluke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dawnfluke), [Nath's Buffoon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nath%27s+Buffoon), [Faultgrinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faultgrinder)


I was again very tempted to cut here. Lots of games in this format can drag on into the late game, meaning expensive cards seem to be the way to go. This means that [Faultgrinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faultgrinder), a card that I see lining people's sideboards, is often good enough to maindeck. I've had lots of green decks featuring [Fertile Ground](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fertile+Ground)s that just need a fattie to get there and [Faultgrinder](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Faultgrinder) does that job. Having said all this, [Nath's Buffoon](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nath%27s+Buffoon) gives me sideboarding options and, again, if there's something that's good for me, I take it rather than hurt someone else.

### Deck Building


I've thirty-one playables to choose from, but some of them can be quickly discarded. Firstly, the [Oblivion Ring](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring) is not going to be splashed and there are few early guys to use the [Springleaf Drum](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Springleaf+Drum) with. This puts [Fallowsage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fallowsage) in the board, which is no problem as the deck has plenty of things to do on turn four. This also helps set the [Mournwhelk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mournwhelk) and [Scattering Stroke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scattering+Stroke) aside as this deck has plenty of late game.


The last three contenders for the final slot were the second [Glimmerdust Nap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmerdust+Nap), the [Amoeboid Changeling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Amoeboid+Changeling), and the [Hunter of Eyeblights](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hunter+of+Eyeblights). The Hunter was the first to be discarded as the deck already has four fine five-drops and, as I mentioned earlier, it is a little too slow for this deck. The Changeling doesn't do very much for the deck. It makes the [Spellstutter Sprite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite)s better, but they are already pretty fine, and the little guy doesn't fizzle enough spells or effects as some of its predecessors have done. It is better in a Merfolk deck where it lets your [Streambed Aquitects](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Streambed+Aquitects) pump everything. This leaves the [Glimmerdust Nap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmerdust+Nap) as the default inclusion, which suits me fine as it helps to shut off cards like Imperious Perferct and big men that the deck will struggle with. The deck will definitely play eighteen land as it has so many expensive spells that it must get there and cannot afford to stumble.

I couldn't draw an answer to the Perfect in time, but the deck has a hard time dealing with the Huntmaster anyway. Not the best matchup. I drew seven land in a row after I kept my opening hand in the second game and lost to double Huntmaster and a Pilferered Perfect. He flashed me the [Garruk Wildspeaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Garruk+Wildspeaker) in his grip at the end of the match. A first round loss is disappointing, but the mana screw combined with the awesome power of his deck doesn't make me worry much. He went on to win the draft without dropping a game to boot.
