Heavenly Children
=================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on February 13, 2014 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




 Stybs has played Magic the world over, writing and drafting as part of the event coverage team and slinging Commander everywhere his decks will fit. 







The power of the gods of Theros are closer than ever to the mortal realm.


  
  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/ct/2014/ct_wk06_286_cardart_nykthosshrinetonyx.jpg)[Nykthos, Shrine to Nyx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nykthos%2C+Shrine+to+Nyx) | Art by [Jung Park](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Jung%20Park%22%5D)


*Born of the Gods* has arrived and there're plenty of new blessings to try from the pantheon of Theros. Whatever the favors you choose, finding new choices is among the most exciting features of a new set. Since Commander relies so heavily on individual cards, the odds of finding something new is almost a guaranteed success.


At least that's what I've found in my own experience.

What did I change over? Not that much, but the net effect is significant. First and foremost, [Kaervek the Merciless](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kaervek+the+Merciless) is gone, replaced by an easier-to-cast and indestructible way to apply pressure to opponents. Backed by a suite of battlefield-clearing removal, the odds that opponents may not be able to choose to sacrifice a creature is good.


Other changes include new ways to chip away at opponents' life totals ([Fate Unraveler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fate+Unraveler) replacing [Nevinyrral's Disk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nevinyrral%27s+Disk)), applying different pressure to opponents' creatures ([Defiler of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Defiler+of+Souls) replacing [Mutilate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutilate)), and creating an indestructible monster from the graveyard of an unsuspecting enemy ([Fated Return](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fated+Return) replacing [Army of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Army+of+the+Damned)). There were also two "side" upgrades, with [Astral Cornucopia](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Astral+Cornucopia) replacing [Rakdos Keyrune](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rakdos+Keyrune) (so we can go even bigger with mana for [Earthquake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthquake) or [Drain Life](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Drain+Life)) and [Temple of Malice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Temple+of+Malice) replacing [Shizo, Death's Storehouse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shizo%2C+Death%27s+Storehouse) (since a little massaging and mana fixing is better than granting fear for this deck).
