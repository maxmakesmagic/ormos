TOP 8 COVERAGE



Pascal Maynard has won Grand Prix Buenos Aires 2015! The man made famous by a Tarmogoyf has reminded everyone that he's not just an indelible *Magic*moment, but he's also very, *very* good at the game. Piloting his Abzan Megamorph Control deck, he defeated Marcos Paulo De Jesus Freitas of Brazil in the finals through three tough, long games to take home the trophy.


This amazing weekend in Argentina has come to close. Brazil, Uruguay, and Argentina all had impressive showings, and some of the Pros chasing the final few points going into Pro Tour *Magic Origins* did what they needed to do. (18) Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa has clinched Platinum before Vancouver, and Maynard has now tied (22) Alex Hayne for the Grand Prix slot of the World Championship.


It was a great time in Buenos Aires, but we cannot rest on our laurels yet. The last-ever core set is coming out soon, and the Pro Tour is just around the corner! There's so much more to do.


Adios from Buenos Aires!

DAY 2 COVERAGE



It might be rainy and cold outside, but inside the Buenos Aires convention center, the fires are lit brightly. Proverbial fires of course. The fires of competition, the fires of desire—just not physical fires. Yesterday was a day of intense battles, but there’s one more day of fighting before a winner can be crowned Grand Prix Buenos Aires 2015 champion.


At the top the standings are Renzo Chiappa at 9-0, and Eduardo Castro Saa and Martin Quiroga at 8-0-1. If you’ve heard Quiroga’s name recently, it’s because he’s won a Super Sunday Series qualifier and will be joining returning champion Luis Salvatto at the Wizards of the Coast headquarters next year. Adding a Grand Prix Top 8 to his resume could help out his cred going into that awesome event.


Though it is two Argentines and a Chilean at the top of the standings, there are some international pros stalking them. Names like (24) Willy Edel, Pascal Maynard, (21) Craig Wescoe, and (18) Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa are all in the running, and flew here with one goal in mind—to get those final Pro points they need.


Will the big-name pros fight it out and claim some spots in the Top 8? Or will the ever-increasing competition from the South American scene push the interlopers out and let the home continent reign supreme? By this evening we’ll find out.


Good luck everyone, and welcome to Day 2 of Grand Prix Buenos Aires!

DAY 1 COVERAGE



700 planeswalkers descended upon The Golden Center in Buenos Aires this morning with dreams of autonomously hoisting up a trophy at the end of the weekend.  After nine rounds of Standard competition only 74 are still in contention. Of those, only three remain undefeated: Eduardo Castro Saa, Martin Quiroga, and Renzo Chiappa will enter the second day of competition without a single loss on their records. Standard continued to evolve here on Day 1. Sidisi Whip is back, and more popular than it has been since since the release of Dragons of Tarkir. Some of the continent’s biggest Magic names showed up hand in hand with Sidisi, Brood Tyrant. Competition resumes at 9 a.m. tomorrow morning. Will the zombies of the brood tyrant rise to dominance once more, or will a new king of Standard be crowned? Stay tuned to all the action as it unfolds here at Grand Prix Buenos Aires.
