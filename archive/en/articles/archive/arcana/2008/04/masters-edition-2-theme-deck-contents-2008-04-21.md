The*Masters Edition 2* Theme Deck contents are now available for viewing! Interested in knowing what new wonders await you? Read on to find out!


### Orc Attack!


They’re back! One of **Magic**’s oldest tribes returns to throw down one more time! With the “Orc Attack!” deck you’re the one in control of a horde of Ironclaw and [Brassclaw Orcs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brassclaw+Orcs). Of course, it helps to have subordinates like the [Orc General](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orc+General), [Orc Veteran](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orc+Veteran), or [Orcish Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orcish+Captain) to carry out your orders. Opponent getting in the way? A heavy dose of firepower should handle things! [Lightning Bolt](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Incinerate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Incinerate), and [Skull Catapult](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+Catapult) will pave the way for your team to finish the job against all comers!


Wave after wave of Orcs will rush to do your bidding with direct damage clearing the way or clearing your opponent! When you’re done playing, “Orc Attack!” won’t just be your deck; it will be a phrase that sends your opponent running!

### Haze of Spores


“Haze of Spores” is exactly that: your opponent won’t know what hit them! Creatures like [Fyndhorn Elves](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fyndhorn+Elves) power out ancient beasties like [Thallid Devourer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thallid+Devourer) and [Thorn Thallid](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thorn+Thallid) which in turn spore out their own Saprolings to annihilate your opponent. [Fungal Bloom](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fungal+Bloom) hastens the Saproling goodness, and if your opponent does somehow manage to kill your creatures? [Night Soil](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Night+Soil) lets you put those creatures back to use by generating more Saprolings.


 Of course, you may want to do something outside the combat step with your 1/1s. [Skull Catapult](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skull+Catapult) lets you send them straight at your opponent’s head. And if that pesky opponent keeps trying to attack you back? [Whirling Catapult](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whirling+Catapult) and [Spore Flower](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spore+Flower) will make sure you have the advantage! “Haze of Spores” brings a whole new spin to the phrase “fog of war”!

### Band Together


One of **Magic**’s most cherished abilities returns: banding! With the “Band Together” deck you’ll lead a team of warriors working together to defeat a common enemy. Call on the [Benalish Hero](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Benalish+Hero) to join with a [Shield Bearer](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shield+Bearer). Attack with the [Righteous Fury](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Righteous+Fury) of the [Angel of Light](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Light), all while protecting yourself with the [Kjeldoran Elite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Elite) and [Home Guard](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Home+Guard)s. Naturally your opponent won’t make it easy, but you’ll be ready with [Swords to Plowshares](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Swords+to+Plowshares), [Reprisal](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Reprisal), and even [Exile!](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Exile%21+) 


When playing “Band Together” you command a team dedicated to your cause. Attack with your creatures working together, protect them with ancient magics, and march ever onward to VICTORY!

### Deathbreed


Deep within the dankest bogs, mires, and swamps lurks something powerful. When you play the “Deathbreed” theme deck, you’ll be the planeswalker who controls it! Head into battle by calling upon the forces of the Thrulls: Mindstab, Armor, and Basal. Send a horde of undead [Gangrenous Zombies](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gangrenous+Zombies) or [Kjeldoran Dead](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kjeldoran+Dead) to do your darkest bidding. For the truly evil, you’ll call upon the powers of the [Ebon Praetor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ebon+Praetor) and [Minion of Leshrac](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Minion+of+Leshrac).


Of course, those powers don’t come cheap. They’ll call upon a sacrifice even as they obey your command. Fortunately you’ve come prepared with [Breeding Pits](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pits). Meanwhile [Hymn to Tourach](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hymn+to+Tourach), [Dark Banishing](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Banishing), and [Feast or Famine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Feast+or+Famine) will keep your opponent and your opponent’s creatures occupied. With “Deathbreed” you’ll teach your opponent new meanings for the word “dead”!
