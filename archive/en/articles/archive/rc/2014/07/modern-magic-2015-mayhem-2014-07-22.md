Modern Magic 2015 Mayhem
========================



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorPic_GavinVerhey.jpg)

Gavin Verhey

When Gavin Verhey was eleven, he wanted a job making Magic cards. Ten years later, his dream was realized as his combined success as a professional player, deck builder, and writer brought him into Wizards R&D during 2011. He's been writing Magic articles since 2005 and has no plans to stop.


July 22, 2014
 







The challenge: build Modern decks that include a new *Magic 2015* card.


The results: awesome.


Every set adds new elements to Modern, but the nature of *Magic 2015* as a slightly wackier core set lends itself particularly well to Modern deck building. There are all kinds of intriguing Modern options nestled within the set's many layers.


Today, instead of diving deep into one particular deck, I want to run through a smorgasbord of decks that people sent in and spend a little bit of time highlighting each. There's plenty to get through today, so without any further preamble, let's hop right into it!


Slivers


Ahâ€”where better to start than this classic archetype? *Magic 2015* adds a new batch of Slivers to the mix. In Standard, that's to combine with the *Magic 2014* Slivers...but in Modern, you get to draw upon the 45 Slivers in *Time Spiral* block as well! (Plus any Changelings you feel like throwing into the mix.)

The mana base for Slivers in Modern is absolutely perfect, allowing for you to play numerous five-color lands that have very little drawback in your deck. Only one of these does damage to you, and [Cavern of Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cavern+of+Souls) actually helps make your Slivers even better by being uncounterable!


So, what can we do with that?


Well, I would want to try and make Slivers as aggressive as possible. The baseline I use to compare aggressive decks in Modern is to Zoo; I feel like it's important to ask the question, "Why is this better than playing [Loam Lion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loam+Lion) or [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl) on the first turn?"


Here, the answer is because of how much larger and evasive your creatures can get. Sure, Nacatl will outmatch you on the first turnâ€”but it doesn't take long for your creatures to become larger and significantly harder to deal with.


This is a deck that wants to be aggressive, and I want to play as many two-mana Slivers that up my damage output as I can. Sinew, Predatory, and Frenzy are all good starts, and I want to add [Leeching Sliver](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leeching+Sliver) to that mix.




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Predatory Sliver)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Predatory Sliver)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Leeching Sliver)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leeching Sliver)


The Slivers I'm actually not as excited about are, surprisingly, the mana-producing Slivers. While normally these are the backbone of a Slivers strategy in something like Standard, here there isn't time: I want to be as aggressive as possible. Those can go in favor of something that is better for my clock.

Although decidedly not a Sliver, for one mana it lets you pump out extra Slivers at little extra cost for the rest of the game and can lend itself to some of your brutal draws.

You can do all kinds of crazy things with [Chief Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+Engineer). I received some MUD-esque decks with him, using the Engineer as an engine and [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)s as moxes to power out cards like [Trinisphere](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinisphere) (which now interacts favorably with convoke!) and [Lodestone Golem](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lodestone+Golem). I also received some affinity decks with him, letting that deck drop its hand even faster than usual.


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Chief Engineer)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief Engineer)
But the list I wanted to take a look at is something a bit more interesting. It's sort of like a larger robots deck. It's still capable of some crazy drawsâ€”but it also has some longer-game elements as well.


One of the big attractions here is [Myr Superion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr+Superion). Hello there! With [Grand Architect](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grand+Architect), [Chief Engineer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chief+Engineer), [Etherium Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etherium+Sculptor), and [Mox Opal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox+Opal), cheating this creature's drawback becomes surprisingly easy. That can add to your army's power fast!




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Myr Superion)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Myr Superion)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Etherium Sculptor)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Etherium Sculptor)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Mox Opal)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mox Opal)


Speaking of adding power fast, [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) adds some major punch. It's pretty easy to be attacking in for 5 on the second turn with the likes of [Ensoul Artifact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ensoul+Artifact) on a [Memnite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Memnite)â€”or, perhaps even better, putting it on a [Darksteel Citadel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darksteel+Citadel) for an indestructible attacker!


In general, while I don't mind curving a little higher than a robots deck, I don't want a ton of reactive cards here. I'm going to be tapping out a lot to cast spells early, and in a proactive deck like this one I don't want to have cards stuck in my hand. [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) can go. (Path leaving also makes the mana a lot easier.) At that point, I want to up the artifact aggro tools a bit: [Ornithopter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ornithopter) contributes to some of your nuttiest draws with the Engineer, and [Master of Etherium](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Master+of+Etherium) gets huge quickly. And, as much as I dislike playing against the card, I would certainly be remiss to not include the completely absurd [Cranial Plating](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cranial+Plating).

In fact, I would just eschew [Lord of the Undead](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lord+of+the+Undead) and [Death Baron](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death+Baron) entirely in favor of more creatures that you can play from the graveyard to make sure your [Necromancer's Stockpile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromancer%27s+Stockpile) is working at maximum efficiency.


Make them discard cards while all the while setting up your own incredible endgame engine. It's going to be seriously difficult for many decks to overcome a [Necromancer's Stockpile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Necromancer%27s+Stockpile) in the long game.

Imagine this.


Turn one, you play a [Wild Nacatl](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Nacatl). Your opponent plays a Mountain and says goâ€”nothing to worry about. You attack with Nacatl and cast a [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf), thinking you're in awesome shape.


Then, you die.


*Land, [Pyretic Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyretic+Ritual), [Desperate Ritual](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Desperate+Ritual), [Immolating Souleater](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating+Souleater), pump it eight times, [Assault Strobe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault+Strobe), [Hammerhand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hammerhand) making your [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) not block. Attack for 20.* 




[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Immolating Souleater)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Immolating Souleater)


[![](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Assault Strobe)](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Assault Strobe)


Well then. Fair enough.


Now, this deck certainly isn't consistent by any stretch of the imagination. Unless you find some solution to not drawing your combo pieces, I wouldn't really recommend it for any tournament. But if you want to play something flashy that can win on turn two with some not unreasonable draws (or turn one, after adding [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide)!) then this is the deck for you.


Normally, I would want to add in [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe)s and [Street Wraith](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Street+Wraith)sâ€”but you can't really afford to pay any life because of how it weakens your Souleater. Similarly, your mana base is restricted because you don't want to take any damage. So, there's not a lot more you can really do. [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) is one addition, and [Manamorphose](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Manamorphose) is a way to cantrip and fix your mana without paying life. After that, you're on your own here!
