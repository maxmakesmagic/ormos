2007 JAPAN NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS
---------------------------------


Champs Fall, Rookies Rise in Japan!
===================================




August 31-September 2, 2007












[日本語の取材へ](/en/node/577946)


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/champ.jpg)Next year's Nationals are coming to Kanagawa! Masaya Kitayama, a 22-year old student and former finalist in the High School Championships, took the title of National Champion with his black-green discard deck. His opponent was also a 22-year old Kanagawan student. Ren Ishikawa was playing MoriKatsu Storm, a variant on the Aussie Storm deck, designed by last year's winner Katsuhiro Mori. Ren had dispatched a pair of Solar Flare decks to reach the finals but he could not deal with the increasingly popular GoyfRack deck.


Joining them in New York City as members of the National team were Takahashi Akiyama, a beautician from Chiba playing Solar Flare, and Makato Nagashima, a 'salaryman' from Yamanashi playing Project X. While this team is not well known they proved their mettle by defeating a trio of players with impressive Pro Tour resumes that included two different Pro Tour finalists and one winner.


**Congratulations to Masaya Kitayama, the 2007 Japanese National Champion!**

### Yuuta Takahashi


**Please tell us about yourself.**  

I'm a 21-year-old university student in Tokyo.


**What have been some of your significant Magic achievements before now?**  

I've played in most Pro Tours since LA 2006. I got lucky at San Diego and managed to get second place.


**How did you qualify for Nationals this weekend?**  

Pro Players' Club.


**What were your results this weekend? What colors did you play in the draft?**  

I went 5-1-1 in Standard and 5-2 in the draft. In the first draft, I went 4-0 with a white-blue deck that had good tempo and card advantage. I used a black-green deck in the second draft that wasn't so good.


**What deck did you play in the Standard portion of the tournament?**  

A blue-black Pickles deck I designed myself. It's the same deck I've been playing since GP Kyoto.


**How did you prepare for Nationals?**  

I playtested with friends and people at Ikebukuro Ogre.


**Any special memories from this year's Nationals?**  

I had three different games where I managed to pull out a win at 1 life.

### Shuu Komuro


**Please tell us about yourself.**

Pro Players' Club.

**Any special memories from this year's Nationals?**

My opponent played [Psionic Blast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psionic+Blast) to kill it, but luckily I had a [Simian Spirit Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Simian+Spirit+Guide) in my hand I was able to burn to deflect the Blast, and I went on to win the game.

Sunday, Sept 2: 11:36 a.m. - Quarterfinals: Ren Ishikawa vs. Kazuya Mitamura
----------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/QFMitamura.jpg)*Mitamura*
Kazuya Mitamura appears to be poised on the brink of greatness. He already has two Pro Tour Top 8s to his credit - including a recent finals finish at PT Yokohama - and if he could weather a Morikatsu Storm he would be representing Japan at Worlds. Fittingly Mitamura was relying on the sun for victory over Storm with Solar Flare as his weapon of choice.


Ren Ishikawa, on the other hand was relying on a fallen star. The deck he was playing was designed by last year's National Champion Katsuhiro Mori, currently suspended from the game for accumulated warnings. Despite the suspension, Morikatsu remains one of the most revered **Magic** players in this country. It had looked like Mori was going to quit the game for awhile but while you can keep the game away from the boy it is very hard to keep the boy out of the game.


Katsu noticed the AussieStorm deck played by Kuan Tian at Australian Nationals and it sparked his creative juices. He began working on the deck online, perfecting the mana base, massaging the sideboard so that it could have a game against the popular Gruul decks with Martyr of Ash, and generally refining the deck in 8-man queues and PEs on MTGO. Katsu's deck designs were instrumental in the ascent of Kenji Tsumura during his Player of the Year run and Kenji - ever the loyal friend - dearly hoped that he could have piloted the deck to the Top 8 of this year's Nationals in Mori's stead as did Masahiko Morita but both players fell short of their goal.


The only player to successfully navigate the Swiss rounds with MoriKatsu Storm was Ren Ishikawa. Ishikawa has had some success as a professional game player here in Japan with some other games but was still waiting for his breakout performance with the forefather of all card games. While preparing for this event he noticed Mori playing the deck online and asked last year's Champion for the list and, of course some advice.


Japanese coverage reporter Keitia Mori contacted the former Champion yesterday evening to assess the match-up of the storm deck vs. Solar Flare. Katsu felt that in the hands of a skilled player - at least as good as the two-time PT Top 8 competitor Mitamura - that his creation was actually advantaged against Solar Flare. If he was playing the deck, or Kenji, or Morita then he would pick his deck but the skill level of Ishikawa has yet to be determined. Perhaps we would have a better idea of what Ren Ishikawa was made of after a five game set.


**Game 1**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/QFMitamura2.jpg)Neither player did anything on the early turns. Ishikawa considered [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) for Mitamura's third turn [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) but ultimately preferred to put a counter on his storage land instead.


A turn later he was [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate)d and revealed a hand of [Perilous Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research), [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), two copies of [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal), [Rite of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) and a [Steam Vents](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steam+Vents). Another [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate) a turn later saw Swath added to the mix and Mitamura stripped the [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand). With nothing appearing eminent from Ishikawa's side of the table Mitamura tapped out for [Skeletal Vampire](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Skeletal+Vampire). Ren [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)ed a token with the obvious sacrifice in response.


The Vampire started to bang away at Ishikawa's life total while he rebuilt his hand and continued to accumulate counters his two storage lands. [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) was suspended for one and came into play, beating Ishikawa down to eight. He added a [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar). Ishikawa was dead on board next turn and he had to go for it. His draws had been pretty solid including the essential [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) but he wasn't sure if he could get there or not…but what other options were there?


He removed all the counters from his two storage lands. He attempted [Rite of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) but it was [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed - he only had two red mana sources in play - and tried to figure out the right way to play it. He let it resolve and attempted it again. Mitamura tapped out for another [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) AND had [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) as a back up. Ishikawa still had some life in him though. He played [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix) and [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)ed it hoping for a red source. He found one but did not have the luxury of waiting around for [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) to resolve and he scooped - he didn't even wait until Mitamura's upkeep to see if he would remember to pay.


**Game 2**


Ishikawa led off the action with [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans). He almost passed the turn without playing [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix) but memories of [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate) urged him to do otherwise. Mitamura slowly built up his resources playing lands into Signets. Ishikawa had another [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) and suspended [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom). Mitamura saw dark clouds massing on the horizon and clucked his tongue. He tapped out to suspend Chronicler while the [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) was still a turn away.


Ishikawa had all the key ingredients to make it rain; just not enough mana to take advantage of his tapped out opponent. He had to wait and suspended another Lotus and deployed another [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans). He [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) and hoped the Yokohama finalist would tap out again or at least commit some spells and mana to defending it. Mitamura stroked his chin and decided to play [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) instead and pass the turn with three mana open.


Ishikawa got his [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) but chose not to attempt any shenanigans. He was holding Teferi and obviously planned to see what kind of tussle they could get into over that. Mitamura played [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) and then tapped two for a Signet. That was the window Ishikawa wanted and before the Signet resolved he flashed out the legend. Mitamura had [Mortify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify) for the legend but did not have any untapped mana to fight over anything. Was he holding [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation)?


Ishikawa offered up [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath) to find out and the answer was no. From there it went Claws, Claws, [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal), Claws, play a land and fire off two [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot)s for a whopping total of 39 damage. Luckilly for Mitamura the damage doesn't care over between games.


**Game 3**



![Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Remand)

Ishikawa led off with [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) while Mitamura had an on-schedule Signet. His [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) was [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed and as a result he missed a land drop. Ishikawa suspended another [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom). [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) resolved a turn later and Mitamura found his land. Ishikawa got his [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) to stick but could not make a move on his turn - he was holding three [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath) and [Rite of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) and had nothing to do with them. Mitamura dug for land with [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) and pitched a pair of [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation). Ishikawa had actually sided out his entire suite of [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) but Mitamura had no way of knowing which direction Ishikawa would go after boarding.


[Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) came in and the deck delivered [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) just in time. Ishikawa led off with [Rite of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) and parlayed it into Swath which was countered by [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation). Mitamura could not do anything about the next two copies of the red enchantment and this time he took 35.


**Game 4**


Ishikawa did not have a [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) opening this game and found the wall pressing into his back rather quickly as Mitamura suspended [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) on turn four off of a Signet and another a turn later.


Ishikawa tried to make something happen in that window with [Rite of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Flame) into [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath) but Mitamura had Pact. Ishikawa could have [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed his own Swath but he would not have enough mana to pull off the combo. He chose to let the Pact resolve and [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed the second Chronicler. A well-timed [Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate) from Mitamura pulled the [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) and the game was over within two swings of the blue [Maro](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Maro).


**Game 5**


Both players reached for their sideboards. If Mitamura had tipped to the fact that the [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) were in his sideboard, he wanted to keep him honest and at least make him think they were coming back in, although they were not. Was Mitamura taking his [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation)s out or performing a similar dance of in and out with the same cards?


I say that when you play seven Signets you will almost always have one on turn two and Mitamura showed no reason for me to dispute my own theory. Ishikawa tried to slow the game down a bit with [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) for [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) and untapped to play [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) and [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix). Mitamura decided to commit a body to the board while digging for cards and cast [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar) instead of [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research).



![Angel of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Angel+of+Despair)

The two players settled into draw-go mode as they both tried to craft their end games. Mitamura had his [Angel of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair) ultimately [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed and Ishikawa went three cards deeper at the end of the turn. He also cleared a land by sacrificing Flagstones for a [Sacred Foundry](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sacred+Foundry).


Ishikawa seemed to think that maybe this could be a winning turn and sacrificed another Flagstones to [Perilous Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research). He had the window it just didn't appear that he had the critical mass of spells in hand unless he could bait out a fight over [Pyromancer's Swath](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyromancer%27s+Swath) but if Mitamura had no action, or chose not to fight over it, Ishikawa could only get him mostly dead - he would have loved some carry over damage from either of his two wins this game - and then have to discard his hand at the end of every turn. Ishikawa decided that he was in no hurry and could refine his hand with [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) - conveniently nestled under his [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix) for easy access.


Mitamura used the [Compulsive Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Compulsive+Research) from all those turns ago and added a Signet to his board. When Ishikawa tapped out to put a third counter on his storage land at the end of the turn, Mitamura considered using his [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) on it and finally went ahead and did it. The drawback was that Ishikawa got an untapped [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) and used it to activate his Claws and draw three cards.


[Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) beget [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) and Ishikawa suspended [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) and played a freshly drawn copy of the blue enchantment. Mitamura decided that was worth a [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation) and remembered to pay for it. Ishikawa was content to play the waiting game; he was under no pressure from Mitamura. Despite being attacked for a handful of turns by the Hussar he was still close to 20 from his card drawing via [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix).


Ishikawa [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed [Angel of Despair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel+of+Despair) for the second time in the game and untapped to play his own [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar). Ishikawa had a Lotus due to arrive on the following turn and he took his time deciding between another Hussar and [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) ultimately choosing the better storm enabler. He suspended another Lotus and passed the turn with the two Hussars looking at each other awkwardly across the red zone.


[Castigate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Castigate) may have been too little too late for Mitamura who saw a hand containing two Swaths, two [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot), [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix), [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal), and [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation). He held his head in his hands and attempted to puzzle his way out of impending disaster. Then suddenly he saw it. He sat up straight and confidently sent the Pact out of the game and steeled himself for a fight.


[Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) hit the table but Ishikawa decided that if Mitamura was up for a fight he would rather spend some more time in the gym. He just played a land and said, 'go' - at least that is what I assume he said. Signets from Mitamura were the only plays on each of the next two turns. He was holding five cards. Ishikawa peered at them intently trying to will his vision past the purple sleeves - he knew one card was the Angel but what were the remaining cards in his hand?


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/QFIshikawa.jpg)*Ishikawa*
[Perilous Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research) ate a redundant Claws at the end of Mitamura's turn as he looked for a way to defend on of his two Swaths in his one big turn - whenever that would eventually be. For now he just toyed with his mana before suspending a Lotus and passing the turn.


Finally the Angel came down and zeroed in on a Lotus. Ishikawa sacrificed the Lotus to his [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix) and proceeded to [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal) his own [Court Hussar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Court+Hussar), which yielded Teferi. Ishikawa attempted to flash out the legend. It was returned to his hand with [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) but it had done its job. Mitamura only had four mana left when the dust settled.


Claws. Swath…Swath…Swath…[Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot). Mitamura slowly shook his head and began to pack up his cards. He displayed a hand that contained only two copies of [Mortify](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mortify) and one of those damned [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation)s.


Final result: Ren Ishikawa won the match in five games and advanced to the semifinal round with a spot on the Japanese National team tucked away in one pocket and reigning Champion Katsuhiro Mori's deck in the other.

Sunday, Sept 2: 1:07 p.m. - Quarterfinals: Shu Komuro v. Makoto Nagashima
-------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Eli Kaplan*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/QFKomuro.jpg)*Komuro*
Japan's second Pro Tour winner Shu Komuro looked around at the microphone and camera trained on the table's Red Zone playmat. "I get to lose here", he joked. "This match is going to end 0-3."


He wasn't just kidding around. After consulting with spectators Shuhei Nakamura and Yuuya Watanabe, they placed Komuro's chances at 20 percent. Komuro's Blink deck was packed with bounce and time management tricks like [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), but the combo of Makoto Nagashima's Project X deck used cheap creatures in conjunction with mana acceleration to get endless 1/1 spirit token generation or lifegain combos fast. Nagashima's trump card was [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling), *Ravnica*'s instant speed creature tutor. With up to four games played with sideboards, Komuro's [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God)s had to work around an instant-speed [Ghost Council of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Council+of+Orzhova).


Komuro already had his name on New York City's invitation list. Nagashima needed to win to get his name onto that piece of paper.


**Game 1**


Nagashima's aggressive approach with life and mana coupled with a helping of bad luck cost him the first game. He won the die roll and used a [Horizon Canopy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Canopy) to play [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise). He followed with [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) on the next turn. Komuro suspended two [Riftwing Cloudskate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftwing+Cloudskate)s and used the threat of [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village)s to keep Nagashima from putting any pressure on. But Nagashima simply couldn't wean himself from the [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant)'s extra draws, and a second [Horizon Canopy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Canopy) pinged away. Komuro milked two [Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink)s, bouncing Nagashima's [Orzhov Basilica](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Basilica) again and again. In a fit of aggravation, two unlucky 'Bob' triggers knocked him from an ostensibly safe 11 to 3, and [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ing a [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch) gave Shu just enough edge to take home the win.



![Orzhov Pontiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Orzhov+Pontiff)

Shu Komuro 1-0 Makoto Nagashima


Shu boarded out most of his underpowered creatures for hard hitters. [Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury), [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), and [Loxodon Warhammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Warhammer) dramatically increased his deck's power quotient while decreasing its operational efficiency. Nagashima went for his [Slaughter Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact)s, [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate), Auger of Skulls, and [Teneb, the Harvester](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teneb%2C+the+Harvester). [Orzhov Pontiff](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Orzhov+Pontiff) and a few combo pieces hit the bench.


**Game 2**


Nagashima took the field first with an explosive opening. Two [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) occupied his side of the table at the end of his second turn. Komuro took a small stab at the acceleration game, playing [Edge of Autumn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn) on his second turn to fetch [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains). Nagashima's early investment paid off; [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and a third turn [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch) knocked Shu down to 12. Shu was drawing [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village)s aplenty, but didn't have enough green to double block the big dumb elephant. The mana flood quickly pulled Komuro under.


Both players received warnings from the judge for slow play during this game.


**Game 3**


Komuro took advantage of a slow Nagashima opening. Nagashima made two copies of [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) in the early game, but had nothing to use the mana on. [Slaughter Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact) sat in the younger player's hand, but Nagashima had the fear of [Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) weighing down on his mind like a ton of bricks. The Cloudskate came in and kept hitting Nagashima until he was at six. Nagashima tried to jump back in with [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling), but it met [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) two times.


When Komuro suspended an [Aeon Chronicler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Aeon+Chronicler) for two, Nagashima finally relented and aimed the [Slaughter Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact) at the 6/6 when it hit the table. Komuro wasn't willing to save it with Blink, suspecting an [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate). He saved the [Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) for flipping over Red Akroma. The Akroma attacked three times for the win.


Nagashima was shocked, "Man, I can't believe how badly that game went!" Yes, his draws were poor but he wasn't aware of how true his words were. When Nagashima blocked Akroma with [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), Komuro failed to notice that Akroma's red incarnation had trample. He assigned all the damage to the Bird. Komuro could have shaved a turn off the kill if he had assigned damage properly. From his reactions, Nagashima seemed oblivious as well. This mistake would prove to be costly in the future.


Shu Komuro 2-1 Makoto Nagashima


**Game 4**



![Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury)

When Komuro shuffled and presented his deck, the Japanese coverage reporter/and Level 2 judge immediately jumped in to point out that Komuro's sideboard had 16 cards in it. Komuro was aghast. The judges quickly conferred and decided that with such a significant title on the line and since neither player had drawn cards yet, Komuro would not receive a game loss and have an opportunity to correct his error.


True to form, Nagashima started building his mana base with [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village), [Llanowar Wastes](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Llanowar+Wastes), and [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots). Komuro went down to 18, playing an untapped [Breeding Pool](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Breeding+Pool). Nagashima made yet another [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) and [Augur of Skulls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Skulls). Komuro's [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) kept his hand safe just a little while longer. [Edge of Autumn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn) fetched Komuro's fifth land, a [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains).


When Saffi Eriksdottir showed up for Nagashima, Komuro took the initiative back with [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God). The first decision Nagashima made was sounding a [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) for four, but [Mystic Snake](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mystic+Snake) shut that down. The next decision was Saffi's Choice; Nagashima had to choose between saving a [Wall of Roots](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Roots) for mana or hitting Komuro's hand with Auger of Skulls. He went down the trail of spite.


Komuro had four cards in hand after this exchange, so Nagashima kept the Auger around. Nagashima ripped Saffi off the top. If he could keep both on the table until next upkeep, Komuro's entire hand would be decimated. Komuro came up with a morphed [Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury) and had a [Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink) ready to in the graveyard. But Nagashima ripped a [Slaughter Pact](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slaughter+Pact) off the top to axe the timeshifted Angel.


Komuro's deck stopped producing spells. Nagashima drew Teysa and [Ghost Council of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Council+of+Orzhova) to finish Komuro off.


Shu Komuro 2-2 Makoto Nagashima


**Game 5**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/QFNagashima.jpg)*Nagashima*
Komuro's opening hand of [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God), [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), [Momentary Blink](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Momentary+Blink), Akroma, Flagstones, [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village), and [Island](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Island) was just what the doctor ordered. He burned through two [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)s persistently keeping Nagashima's Saffi off the table. But Nagashima's continual work paid off, playing [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), Saffi, and [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) in one turn. [Wrath of God](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wrath+of+God) left Bob Maher as the only creature standing, but Nagashima dropped two more pieces of the Project X combo onto the table, Teysa and Saffi. Komuro was under the gun and decided to go on the attack. He played Akroma face down.


With his opponent on the ropes, Shu's composure broke. His head wasn't in the match anymore. He cycled [Edge of Autumn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn) sacrificing Flagstones, fetching a [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain). He placed a freshly drawn [Edge of Autumn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Edge+of+Autumn) in his graveyard, sacrificing his [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain). But Komuro's brain wasn't in the right area code. His picked up his library, as if he was going to search for [Plains](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plains), and started shuffling his library. [Hallowed Fountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Fountain) doesn't replace itself like [Flagstones of Trokair](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flagstones+of+Trokair), though. Komuro didn't realize his mistake and literally banged his head against the playmat when the judges walked away to confer. Since the game play error was in an entirely different catagory of mistake and because the event was in the Top 8, the judges assigned Komuro a second warning and told the players to continue.


Komuro connected with Akroma two times, knocking Nagashima down to 11 who made some of it back with [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch). Komuro attacked again, knocking Nagashima down to 5 while Komuro was at 8. The Hierarch struck back and Nagashima played [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to put it in the path of the furious angel. The Angel went out of the Red Zone with Nagashima still drawing breath. Then the situation got sticky. Some spectators in the crowed were loudly chattering about the rules error. Sunday, Sept 2: 1:18 p.m. - Statement Regarding the Quarterfinals Ruling
------------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Yoshiya Shindo (DCI Level 3 Judge)*

 

In Game 5 of his Quarterfinal match with Makoto Nagashima, Shuu Komuro received a game loss penalty. I would like to take this opportunity to explain how this came about.


During the match, Komuro had already received two warnings for play-related offenses, and had been advised that any other warnings might be upgraded as a result.


Toward the end of the fifth game, with Nagashima at 4 life, Komuro attacked with [Akroma, Angel of Fury](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Akroma%2C+Angel+of+Fury). Nagashima blocked with [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise).


It is important at this juncture to mention something that occurred during Game 3 of the same match. Komuro had Akroma out in this game as well, and through his playing had made it clear that he did not realize the creature had trample. (A floor judge had verified this.)


Returning to the fifth game, after the block Nagashima placed the Birds in his graveyard, and removed the Bitter Harvest that was there. Komuro confirmed this, then passed the turn.


At this point, believing game and match to be over, a number of spectators began clapping, while others shouted out "Trample!". Upon realizing that Akroma has trample, Nagashima conceded and began scooping up his cards, followed by Komuro.


The problem here is that Komuro did not declare that any trample damage would be assigned to Nagashima and continued the game. Afterward, both players arbitrarily rewound the game to the point where damage would have been assigned and changed the game state. This behavior is illegal. When either or both players realized there was a problem with the game state, they should have called for a judge immediately and ask for adjudication. (Note: Current DCI policy for premier events no longer calls for each match in the finals to have a table judge present.) Instead, Komuro and Nagashima-even if unwittingly-created a situation where the result of the game was artificially decided.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/headjudge.jpg)As a result, both Komuro and Nagashima were issued warnings for "Game Rule Error-Game Rule Violation". However, since Komuro had already received multiple warnings during the match, I determined that it was appropriate to upgrade his warning to a game loss.


There are two lessons to be taken from this. One is that players should always ask for assistance from a judge if they become aware of a problem during a game, no matter how insignificant it may seem. We judges are here to help players, not punish them. Second, spectators should refrain from making any comments or reactions about a game until it is complete, as doing so may affect the outcome of the game. No matter how insignificant the comment, it will change how the game is played, which could lead to another problem (penalties for receiving outside assistance).

Taking a point of damage from his second land, [Horizon Canopy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Canopy), Nagashima brought out Saffi Eriksdottir. Kitayama let out a [Cry of Contrition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cry+of+Contrition) haunting the Legendary Human Rogue.


Nagashima played his third land, another [Horizon Canopy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Horizon+Canopy). Ouch. Kitayama found a second [Cry of Contrition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cry+of+Contrition) haunting Saffi, then [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror)ed the ever-fleeing lass. I'm sure there's a good horror movie analogy out there somewhere, but I can't find it, so I'll settle for mentioning that forcing your opponent to discard four cards by turn three is scary stuff.


[Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) could have put Nagashima back in the game, but [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy) immediately shut it down. Kitayama followed with two copies of [The Rack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack). Kitayama sealed the game with [Stupor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupor).


Masaya Kitayama 2-0 Makoto Nagashima


**Game 3**


Nagashima's Project X rapidly kicked into gear this game. Turn one Bird of Paradise, turn two Bird of Paradise, and Saffi Eriksdottir is exactly the early game his deck needs. Nagashima took a slight detour on turn four for a [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch), but got back on track with an [Essence Warden](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Essence+Warden) and [Crypt Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Champion) on the next two turns. Kitayama never saw any of his disruption.  

When Nagashima's life started shooting for the heavens, Kitayama packed it in.


Masaya Kitayama 2-1 Makoto Nagashima


**Game 4**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/SFNagashima.jpg)*Nagashima*
Kitayama started off the hand attack with an Auger of Skulls. Nagashima's lands kept coming into play tapped and he was perpetually one step behind. The first Auger forced [Crypt Champion](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crypt+Champion) and [Ghost Council of Orzhova](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Council+of+Orzhova) to hit the bin. Nagashima made a [Loxodon Hierarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loxodon+Hierarch), but it fell prey to [Terror](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Terror). A second Auger and [Stupor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupor) stripped Nagashima down to one card, and Kitayama added insult to injury with two Racks.


With one card in hand, five lands including a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village), and ten life against Kitayama's six lands (two [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village)s) and two Racks, Nagashima took a desperate gamble. He played [Chord of Calling](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chord+of+Calling) for two, fetching out [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant). It didn't matter. Kitayama's [Darkblast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Darkblast) killed the 2/1. Death soon followed.


Final result: Masaya Kitayama defeated Makoto Nagashima 3-1 to advance to the finals.


  


Sunday, Sept 2: 3:55 p.m. - Finals: Ren Ishikawa vs. Masaya Kitayama
--------------------------------------------------------------------


*by Brian David-Marshall*

  
![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/Final_Ishikawa.jpg)*Ishikawa*
Next year's National Championship will most likely be held in Kanagawa as both players in the finals hailed from the nearby prefecture. 22-year old student Ren Ishikawa was one match away from winning Japanese Nationals with a deck designed by reigning champion Katsuhiro Mori. His last two matches had been against Solar Flare decks, a match he turns out to be a pretty big favorite. For the finals he would be facing off against a different animal altogether.


Masaya Kitayama is also 22 and from nearby Kanagawa. He won the High School Championship several years back and was looking to notch another win with his GoyfRack deck.


**Game 1**


Ishikawa shipped back his opening hand while Kitayama roleplayed out his first few turns should he keep his initial seven, which he decided to do. [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) was the turn two play from Ishikawa which was met with [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) across the table. Ishikawa aimed [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) at the Invitational card, which was replaced by [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf).


Ishikawa drew five cards but was suddenly looking down the barrel of a 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf). He suspended two Loti and added [Claws of Gix](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Claws+of+Gix) to the Board. Kitayama asked him to discard one of the remaining cards with [Cry of Contrition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cry+of+Contrition) and attacked for seven, with a Treeptop Village joining the fray.


Ishikawa restocked with [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) but it looked like he was going to have to win before his suspends resolved. He [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)ed his Claws and replayed it, and two Rites of Flame forged ten goblins. Kitayama wasn't sure if he was prepared to send a pair of 4/5s to potentially be eaten by goblins and instead attacked Ishikawa's hand with a [Stupor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stupor) in the hopes of hitting a land or artifact. He did manage to hit a Lotus but still declined to enter the red zone fearing the counter attack - Ishikawa was down to two cards.


The pair of [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom)s arrived. Ishikawa [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)ed the haunted [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) and measured his attack. He felt it came up short and declined to make a move. Kitayama sent one of his Villages into battle and took down three goblins on its way out. It also maxed out his [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s at 6/7, which came barreling into the red zone alongside a [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village). Ishikawa's goblins devoured the Village and two more were run over by [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf)s. It was only a temporary measure and Ishikawa scooped up his cards a turn later.


**Game 2**


Sideboarding:


Kitayama: + 4 [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate), +1 [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation), +2 [Putrefy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Putrefy), +2 [Riftsweeper](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Riftsweeper)  

-1 Call, - 4 terror, - 4 [Smallpox](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smallpox)


Ishikawa: + 2 [Logic Knot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Logic+Knot), +1 [Pact of Negation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pact+of+Negation), +1 [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens), +2 Shapeshifters - 2 [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), - 3 Swath, -1 [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom)


Kitayama held onto [The Rack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) until turn two and returned [Overgrown Tomb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Overgrown+Tomb) with a Rot Farm. His turn three [Augur of Skulls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Skulls) was [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)ed. Ishikawa transmuted for [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) and suspended it.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/Final_Kitayama.jpg)*Kitayama*
Kitayama replayed the Augur with the mana to regenerate and looked rather alarmed when Ishikawa played a morph. He sacrificed the Augur and Putrefied the face down Shapeshifter. After Ishikawa drew for the turn, Kitayama attempted to [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate)[Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand)s only to learn that there was only one more left in the deck after sideboarding. On the brighter side he also learned how his opponent sideboarded. He saw a hand with [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans), Rite, Claws, and [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot). Ishikawa played the enchantment and the Claws and passed the turn without enacting his Plans.


[Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) arrived for Kitayama. Ishikawa thinned his deck of land, shredding his Flagstones through his Claws, and drew three cards to stay above the Rack. Kitayama had another [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate) loaded and ready. He took the [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) - there was another in Ishikawa's hand, who began to talk to himself as one of the crucial card drawing components of his deck was removed from the game. He used his [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot) on the [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) and threw a few pseudo-Shots at Kitayama for good measure.


Kitayama played a fresh [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) with a 4/5 [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) by his side. Kitayama could have sworn it was a 5/6 but he was reminded that all the [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) had been removed from the game. [Vesuvan Shapeshifter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuvan+Shapeshifter) copied [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) before it was killed with [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot).


Kitayama wasn't willing to let the storm deck get it's Bob on and cleared the board with [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation). He played two separate [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) only to have one of his tokens [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal)ed -- but there were two more where that one came from and Ishikawa was soon down two games to none.


**Game 3**


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/HandsShake.jpg)Ishikawa opted to swap out the suite of counter **Magic** he had brought in for Game 2 and returned the Swaths. He led off the third game with [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) while his opponent had [Augur of Skulls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Augur+of+Skulls) and a [Cry of Contrition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cry+of+Contrition) to haunt it a turn later. Ishikawa drew five in response and discarded [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal).


With the Augur threatening to strip three cards from his hand on Kitayama's upkeep, Ishikawa just played [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) and passed the turn with two mana open. I think we all knew what was coming and he got to look at five fresh cards before deciding what three to discard - [Perilous Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research), [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) and a land.


Kitayama hoped to draw some more disruption with [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) but Ishikawa had more than enough gas in hand to combo him out on his next turn.


**Game 4**


Two [Lotus Bloom](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Bloom) came out on turn one for the storm player while [Dark Confidant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dark+Confidant) drew a Grapehsot on turn two. Kitayama was hurting for a land. He played [Tarmogoyf](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tarmogoyf) but did not have any mana left for the [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate) in hand to clear the [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot)s.


He attacked with his Goyf, played [Treetop Village](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treetop+Village), laid [The Rack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=The+Rack) and passed the turn. Ishikawa paused during his draw step to see if there was an [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate) coming but Kitayama wanted to see where this turn was going.


Lotus, Lotus. Claws, [Repeal](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repeal), Claws, [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans), Rites, [Empty the Warrens](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Empty+the+Warrens) for sixteen tokens. Kitayama [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate)d the [Grapeshot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grapeshot)s - he saw only [Tolaria West](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tolaria+West) in hand -- at the end of the turn and had a little more than one turn to draw a land for the [Damnation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Damnation) he was holding. He found the land but Ishikawa cracked his Lotus and used the mana to break his [Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans). He hit the [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and was able to attack for fifteen.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/jpnat07/The_Winner.jpg)Ishikawa grinned as he transmuted for Pact and hoped that Kitayama would not realize he could not pay the upkeep cost of it and possibly just scoop. That did not happen and Kitayama cleared the board.


[Hatching Plans](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hatching+Plans) draw three cards and [Perilous Research](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Perilous+Research) drew another two while Kitayama was attacking with a pair of Villages. When Kitayama showed the storm player another [Extirpate](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Extirpate) we had a champion.


Final result: In a four game set, Masaya Kitayama became the 2007 Japanese National Champion over Ren Ishikawa. Joining the two of them at Worlds on the team are Takashi Akiyama and Makato Nagashima.
