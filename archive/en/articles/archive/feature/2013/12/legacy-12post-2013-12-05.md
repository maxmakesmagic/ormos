Legacy 12Post
=============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on December 5, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_2016_LSV.jpg)
By Luis Scott-Vargas 




Luis Scott-Vargas plays, writes, and makes videos about Magic. He has played on the Pro Tour for almost a decade, and between that and producing content for ChannelFireball, often has his hands full (of cards). 







I can't say that I've ever seen a [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned) cast in Legacy, much less in a deck with [all three elder Eldrazi](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&type=+%5B%22Creature%22%5D&subtype=+%5B%22Eldrazi%22%5D&rarity=+%5BM%5D), [Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage), and [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), but here we are. 12post is a deck that takes on the unique challenge of assembling [Cloudpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost)s in a format of [Wasteland](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wasteland)s, but thanks to many strong enablers, it actually works. Once it starts generating absurd amounts of mana, it hard-casts Eldrazi, and apparently Bonfires. 


![Cloudpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Cloudpost)

[Cloudpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudpost), [Glimmerpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmerpost), and [Vesuva](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vesuva) form the core of the deck, and the rest of the cards are just there to enable and maximize them. [Sensei's Divining Top](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sensei%27s+Divining+Top) is the top card for the cantrip slot, mainly because it only costs colorless mana to start spinning. This deck can sometimes need to keep an all-colorless hand, and Top fits right in to those. Top also sets up [Bonfire of the Damned](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bonfire+of+the+Damned), with a little help from [Brainstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Brainstorm), giving the deck a relevant removal spell that's decent early and can end the game late. 



[Trinket Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Trinket+Mage) is mainly there to find Top, although it can pick up a [Pithing Needle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pithing+Needle), [Candelabra of Tawnos](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Candelabra+of+Tawnos), or [Expedition Map](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Expedition+Map) if the circumstances demand it. [Crop Rotation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crop+Rotation) is another tutor, one that comes with quite a bit of risk but gives the deck plenty of explosiveness as a reward. After Rotation, [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) is actually the next tutoring option, all the way at six mana. Titan is in a strange spot; to cast it, the deck has to be working to some degree, but once cast, Titan still provides a ton of valuable ramping. You can also fetch multiple [Glimmerpost](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glimmerpost)s with [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), making it a relevant play at any point in the game. 


 All this mana has to go somewhere, and that somewhere is the Eldrazi. [Ulamog, the Infinite Gyre](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Ulamog%252C%2Bthe%2BInfinite%2BGyre), [Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kozilek), and [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?&name=Emrakul%252C%2Bthe%2BAeons%2BTorn) all make an appearance, and thanks to [Eye of Ugin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eye+of+Ugin) (which can be fetched any number of ways), you often get to choose which Eldrazi you need. [Show and Tell](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Show+and+Tell) is a way to cheat in the Eldrazi as well, although putting in a [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan) is honestly not the worst. 


 The sideboard for the deck is pretty sweet, too, with the full twelve counterspells against combo, as well as [Sneak Attack](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sneak+Attack) against control and a lone [Glacial Chasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glacial+Chasm) against aggro. 


I've never played this deck in a tournament, but I imagine that landing a fast Eldrazi is very satisfying, and there's nothing better than Bonfireing someone out (or worse than getting Bonfired out, as I can also attest to).
