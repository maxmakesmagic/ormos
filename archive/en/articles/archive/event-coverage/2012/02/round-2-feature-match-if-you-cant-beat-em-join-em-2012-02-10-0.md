Round 2 Feature Match:
======================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on February 10, 2012 






![](https://web.archive.org/web/20180108123623im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/mtg/files/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_SteveSadin.jpg?itok=5-cQx5h-)
By Steve Sadin












[Christian Calcano (White-Blue Humans) vs. David Williams (Three-Color Pod)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_1)


Samuele Estratti (White-Blue Delver) vs. Ben Stark (Wolf Run Ramp)
------------------------------------------------------------------


[Willy Edel (White-Blue Delver) vs. Conley Woods (Three-Color Wolf Run)](http://archive.wizards.com/Magic/Magazine/Article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdka12/fm_3)


Ben Stark and Samuele Estratti both won Pro Tours last season – with Stark flying to victory at Pro Tour Paris with the first iteration of the Caw-Blade deck that would ultimately terrorize Standard for months to follow, and Estratti taking the trophy at Pro Tour Philadelphia with his format defining Modern [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) deck.


There was a lot of talk this week about what new deck(s) ChannelFireball might come up with for this event, but in the end, Stark decided to pilot an update to the Red-Green Ramp deck that Jun’ya Iyanaga won Worlds with.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r2_estratti_vs_stark.jpg)
*Two Pro Tour champions from 2011 clash in the feature match area.* 

Meanwhile Estratti, a player who isn’t afraid to play the best deck even if much of the field has their sights set on it, decided to go with White-Blue Delver this weekend.


### Game 1


Stark won the roll, but had to start things off with a mulligan into a hand that he seemed a bit hesitant to keep. Estratti’s [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) let him see why the Pro Tour Paris Champion was unhappy with his opening hand, as Stark revealed four lands and a [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells).


Estratti was ready to punish Stark’s weak draw with a Delver of Secrets, a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) to counter Huntmaster of Fells, and a [Snap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snap)caster Mage (flashing back [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak)) to counter Stark’s follow up play of Green Sun’s Zenith, and Midnight Haunting to crank up the pressure.


Another [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) dealt with an [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan), and while Estratti had no counterspell for [Slagstorm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slagstorm) the next turn, he had a [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) to reset his [Snap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snap)caster Mage, and a Geist of Saint Traft to finish off the job that his fliers had started.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r2_stark.jpg)
*Well then...next game?* 

Samuele Estratti 1, Ben Stark 0


“Do you like our strategy for this tournament?” Asked Ben Stark after the first game. “Iyanaga destroyed all of us at Worlds with Red-Green Ramp, so we figured if you can’t beat ‘em, join ‘em.”


### Game 2


The second game started off much like the first as Estratti played an early Delver of Secrets, and countered Stark’s fourth turn [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells) with a [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak).


But when Estratti missed his fourth land drop, that gave Stark the opening that he needed to resolve a Green Sun’s Zenith, fetching up a [Huntmaster of the Fells](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Huntmaster+of+the+Fells).


[Midnight Hauntings](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Midnight+Hauntings) gave Estratti more pressure, and a [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) kept the Huntmaster from flipping without forcing the Italian pro to waste any mana on his own turn.


Another Midnight Haunting and a barrage of [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag)s and [Gut Shot](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gut+Shot)s later, and Estratti had taken the match.



![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/ptdka12/r2_estratti.jpg)
*The PT Philly champion presses on, despite stumbling on mana early.* 

Samuele Estratti 2, Ben Stark 0
