Modern Vogue
============



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on August 15, 2013 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/080214_1291.jpg)
By Jacob Van Lunen




Jacob Van Lunen began playing Magic in 1995. He has participated in organized play at every level of competition and was a member of the winning team at Pro Tour San Diego in 2007, thanks to an innovative draft strategy. As a writer, Van Lunen has had more than three hundred Magic strategy pieces published 







Welcome back to Perilous Research, DailyMTG's exclusive [**Magic Online**](/en/articles/archive/2011-10-17) column. Last week, the Standard Pro Tour Qualifier season came to an end. On **Magic Online**, this heralded a huge boost in popularity for the game's other major formats, especially Modern. The [**Magic** World Championship](/en/events/coverage/youth-served-flash) showcased Modern and offered up a taste of the format's mainstream. The decks, while powerful, weren't as wacky or innovative as most people expected. After all, Shouta Yasooka's innovative Eternal Command deck [from last year](/en/events/coverage/watanabe-wins-magic-players-championship) completely dominated the tournament. It seems that the masses on **Magic Online** have taken it upon themselves to be the innovators. The format has undergone some major changes and a swath of innovative archetypes have been birthed in just the last two weeks. 


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/pr/pr260_thrun.jpg)
[Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll) | Art by [Jason Chan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=%5B%22Jason%20Chan%22%5D)



 Today, we'll be taking a look at the current state of Modern. The very popular Red-White-Blue decks we saw at the World Championship have faded to obscurity in the blink of an eye as [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft); [Thrun, the Last Troll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thrun%2C+the+Last+Troll); and [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) have made major waves in the last few days. We'll take a look at the known entities of the format and discuss their reactions to the format's recent changes, and we'll look at new archetypes and discuss their capabilities and potential longevity. Let's jump right into things with some decklists:

[Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) strategies have been a major part of the Modern metagame since Sam Pardee used the strategy to secure himself [a Grand Prix victory](/en/events/coverage/its-pardee-time) earlier this year. The deck uses [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) alongside creatures with persist like [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap) and [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks). With [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) in play, the persist creatures return to the battlefield from the graveyard without -1/-1 counters. This lets the deck repeatedly sacrifice one of the persist creatures to achieve infinite "enter the battlefield" triggers; thus netting infinity×2 life with [Kitchen Finks](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) or dealing infinity×2 damage with [Murderous Redcap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murderous+Redcap). [Melira, Sylvok Outcast](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Melira%2C+Sylvok+Outcast) serves double duty against the Infect decks that you might occasionally see on **Magic Online**. Most of the cards in the deck are very powerful on their own. The deck plays like a midrange creature deck, but it has the ability to punish an opponent for tapping too much mana, by going infinite. [Voice of Resurgence](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Voice+of+Resurgence) lets this deck play a strong game against the tempo-based blue decks that have been doing well recently. [Cartel Aristocrat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cartel+Aristocrat) augments [Viscera Seer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Viscera+Seer) by providing the deck with a sacrifice outlet at a different mana cost that has the ability to passively protect itself from all matters of trickery.

 The [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) combo deck is fast and resilient. The deck uses [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) or [Pestermite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pestermite) in conjunction with [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) or [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker) to copy the creature; each copy of the creature will untap the creature enchanted with [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin) or the [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker). This process allows the deck to create infinite tokens and attack for lethal damage as early as the fourth turn. The deck uses cards like [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Flame Slash](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flame+Slash), [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand), and [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) to buy time for its combo. Against control decks, the deck can sideboard out its removal in favor of more interactive spells. [Dispel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dispel), [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite), [Mizzium Skin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mizzium+Skin), [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm), and [Counterflux](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Counterflux) work to protect the combo.

 The less-popular version of [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) is still one of the best decks in Modern. The deck often uses the [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker)/[Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) combo as its finishing blow, but, like the other [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) strategies, it plays a very powerful game up to that point. Main-deck [Glen Elendra Archmage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glen+Elendra+Archmage) is tutorable with [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod); it can be sacrificed to search up [Kiki-Jiki, Mirror Breaker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kiki-Jiki%2C+Mirror+Breaker); and it protects the combo all by itself. [Birthing Pod](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birthing+Pod) also dramatically increases the value of each sideboard slot, allowing the deck to tutor for specific cards in the matchups where they have the ability to take over or reestablish footing in a game.

 This is one of the more interesting decks I've seen in a while. The deck protects itself from direct damage with [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity) and uses [Avatar of Hope](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Avatar+of+Hope) and [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) with [Plunge into Darkness](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Plunge+into+Darkness) to create very difficult, if not impossible, board states for the opponent. [Rite of Consumption](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rite+of+Consumption) can be used with [Death's Shadow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Death%27s+Shadow) to finish off opponents out of nowhere and the deck passively protects itself from disruption with the [Leyline of Sanctity](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leyline+of+Sanctity). The deck has a lot of ways to prevent its own death and, while its consistency looks like it might be questionable at times, it's certainly not a deck I would like to be paired against if I didn't have a control deck. [Angel's Grace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angel%27s+Grace) is surprisingly strong on its own against aggro and combo decks.

 The Hexproof deck used by Reid Duke at the World Championship two weeks ago has been the boogeyman of Modern for a few months. The deck is blazingly fast with its [Ethereal Armor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ethereal+Armor) draws and tremendously difficult to race with [Daybreak Coronet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Daybreak+Coronet). [Unflinching Courage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Unflinching+Courage) is the latest addition here, providing a much welcomed [Armadillo Cloak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Armadillo+Cloak) effect to the mix. The deck is weak to things like [Liliana of the Veil](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Liliana+of+the+Veil) and [Hallowed Burial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hallowed+Burial), but it's difficult to manufacture a strategy that doesn't lose to this deck's best draws. We can expect this deck to continue showing up in Modern events for the foreseeable future, but I expect at least a few [Back to Nature](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Back+to+Nature)s in people's sideboards if it becomes an epidemic.

 One of the coolest, and certainly the fastest, combo strategies in Modern is the [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand)/[Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) combo deck. The deck uses [Goryo's Vengeance](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goryo%27s+Vengeance) or [Through the Breach](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Through+the+Breach) to cheat [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn) or [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) into play. If it's an [Emrakul, the Aeons Torn](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emrakul%2C+the+Aeons+Torn), then it simply attacks, annihilates, and moves on most of the time. However, when it makes a hasty [Griselbrand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Griselbrand) it can attack, then pay 7 life until it finds [Fury of the Horde](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fury+of+the+Horde), then attack again, then pay 7 more life, then attack again, and that's all it takes to win the game. The deck achieves third-turn kills reasonably often, but it more consistently operates as a turn-four combo strategy.

[Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) is the most powerful one-card combo in Modern. Casting [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) with seven lands in play allows a player to deal 18 points of damage divided how he or she chooses in increments of 3. With all the fetch lands and shocklands in Modern, 18 is usually good enough. Once the deck gets to eight lands it can fire off a massive 36-point blast. The deck uses cards like [Primeval Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Primeval+Titan), [Farseek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Farseek), and [Search for Tomorrow](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Search+for+Tomorrow) to accelerate its mana and to passively win the game by playing lands alongside [Valakut, the Molten Pinnacle](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Valakut%2C+the+Molten+Pinnacle). [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) and [Izzet Charm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Izzet+Charm) protect the combo and buy time. [Prismatic Omen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Prismatic+Omen) makes the deck worlds faster by turning a six-land [Scapeshift](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scapeshift) into 72 points of damage.

This is an obviously powerful endgame that's coupled with a very strong early game.

The deck has a tremendous removal suite to back up the [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) and uses [Elspeth, Knight-Errant](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Elspeth%2C+Knight-Errant) and [Might of Alara](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Might+of+Alara) to further guarantee that [Geist of Saint Traft](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Geist+of+Saint+Traft) can charge into the red zone. [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) does a whole lot of damage when you're casting it for the full 5. [Tribal Flames](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tribal+Flames) in conjunction with [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) is actually just absurd and its ability to close games out of nowhere is unbelievable. [Lingering Souls](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lingering+Souls) proves itself to be one of the most powerful cards in the game by making an appearance as a four-of in this aggressive brew. This deck is very impressive and it could be easily adapted to include better disruption like [Thoughtseize](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) in the sideboard.

 White Weenie, despite all the power available in Modern, is still putting up very reasonable numbers. [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) forces Jund players to have a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and [Thalia, Guardian of Thraben](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thalia%2C+Guardian+of+Thraben) harshly punishes combo and control players. [Æther Vial](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=%C3%86ther+Vial) helps the deck play around countermagic and lets it develop fast. [Leonin Arbiter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Leonin+Arbiter) lets [Ghost Quarter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ghost+Quarter) become a [Strip Mine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Strip+Mine). The deck has a lot of passive interaction and it has specific draws that dominate most matchups. There's certainly some consistency issues with the card makeup of these strategies, but playing only one color helps combat the number of draws that are mulliganed this way by reducing the variance in other places.

 Modern is diverse and evolves every day. **Magic Online** is an excellent place to acquire competency in one of **Magic**'s most complex formats. Preparing for a Modern Grand Prix can often be difficult without **Magic Online**. Modern is a format where a player can become attached to a deck and play it indefinitely. There's nothing stopping you from building one of these decks and playing it for the next three years, learning every line of play and tweaking it as new sets spawn new archetypes.
