Hunted Humans
=============



 Posted in **Feature**
 on September 18, 2012 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_conleywoods.jpg)
By Conley Woods











In **Magic**, we all grow familiar with certain color combinations and what they typically do. For example, if I told you we were going to discuss blue-black today, your mind would probably go straight to some type of control deck. Black has provided removal while blue has provided card advantage and counterspells for years. Similarly, red-white would evoke some sort of aggro deck. Well, for green-white, while the exact confines of the colors have changed over time, we still always think of some sort of creature-based strategy. Recently, GW Tokens was the offering from the color pairing, but Brian Emerald decided to move away from this a bit.

![Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Champion+of+the+Parish)At a recent TCQ in Massachusetts, Brian decided to try out GW Humans. While the Tokens lists that did well at [Worlds last year](/en/events/coverage/japan-stands-tall-worlds-again) had some Human synergies in them, this take on the list is all about the tribal factor. [Champion of the Parish](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Champion+of+the+Parish) and [Mayor of Avabruck](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mayor+of+Avabruck) both want a mass of Humans in the list. Once you get past that synergy, though, you get to a who's who of green and white creatures, most of which just happen to be Humans. [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader), [Hero of Bladehold](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hero+of+Bladehold), and [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) are just a few of the powerful Humans the list has access to.

Moving down to the spells, though, there are a few exciting things to talk about. [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor) should come as a surprise to no one, as it has been winning matches for over a decade. [Angelic Destiny](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Angelic+Destiny) is interesting, though, as it replaces the last two [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor)s and gives the deck a better finisher than [Rancor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Rancor), albeit at four times the price. [Revenge of the Hunted](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Revenge+of+the+Hunted) is really awesome in here, though. While we have seen the miracle make small appearances before, imagine throwing it on to your [Silverblade Paladin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Silverblade+Paladin) or [Loyal Cathar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Loyal+Cathar). The [Lure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lure) is almost a free roll at that point! Unfortunately, you cannot target [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader), but I don't think the deck minds that much!
