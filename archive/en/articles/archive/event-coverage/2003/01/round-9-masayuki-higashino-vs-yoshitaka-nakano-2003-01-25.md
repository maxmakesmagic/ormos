Round 9: Masayuki Higashino vs. Yoshitaka Nakano
================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on January 25, 2003 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_joshbennett.jpg)
By Josh Bennett











![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gphir03/a963.jpg)Masayuki Higashino might not look menacing, with his gentle rounded features and bundled up in his great coat, but don't be fooled. Higashino has earned his reputation as Japan's thug extraordinaire. At the last Grand Prix – Hiroshima, it was Higashino who walked away with the title. Today he's playing against type, red beatdown instead of black. Yoshitaka Nakano is a friend of Higashino's, and both hail from Osaka, and has taken up the black cards in Higashino's stead. He's no slouch, either, appearing in the Top 8 of Japanese Nationals in 2002.

### Game 1

Higashino was at a distinct advantage in the matchup, and he knew it. Nakano spent his early turns making 2/2 monsters while Higashino burned them out of the way. Things looked dire when he resolved a pair of [Cursed Scroll](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cursed+Scroll)s. Nakano's only hope was [Phyrexian Negator](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Negator), hardly comforting against red. Higashino, out of gas, drew [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer). He dropped Nakano to just Negator, [Slay](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Slay)er and two swamps. Nakano bashed in and crossed his fingers.

Higashino drew yet another [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer) for the Negator. Nakano decided to leave himself with the Negator, bringing Higashino down to one. It was the last damage he would do. Higashino's Scroll left him permanentless, and soon he had both of them online. Nakano played on, hoping for some screw-up, but none came.

Higashino 1 - Nakano 0

### Game 2

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gphir03/a964.jpg)Nakano had no illusions about his chances for Game 2. His hand was a middling affair with four land and few beats. He hoped for the best. Higashino matched his [Carnophage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnophage) with [Jackal Pup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jackal+Pup). Nakano shrugged and paid the life to keep his zombie active. He dropped Dauthi slayer and handed it over.

Higashino swung in, trading his Pup for the [Carnophage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Carnophage), then dropped [Mogg Fanatic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mogg+Fanatic) and [Grim Lavamancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grim+Lavamancer), whistling innocently. Nakano drew and found he had no more creatures to play. He tried [Cabal Therapy](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cabal+Therapy) for [Volcanic Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Hammer), and saw instead [Powder Keg](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Powder+Keg) and [Flametongue Kavu](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Flametongue+Kavu). 

Rather than continue the farce, he scooped up his cards and went to get a coffee.

**Final Result:** Masayuki Higashino defeats Yoshitaka Nakano 2-0
