Announcing New Booster Battle Packs
===================================


1. Now, with *Shadows over Innistrad*...

Coming with the *Shadows over Innistrad* release is another way to experience *Magic*—Booster Battle Packs.


Available in North American mass market stores, Booster Battle Packs contain a random pairing of two 30-card decks ready to battle against each other, as well as two *Shadows over Innistrad* booster packs to spice things up. These ready-to-play decks are flavored to feel like playing Sealed, with just a dash of the excitement that comes from opening up a booster pack or two.


Grab a Battle Pack, grab a friend, and you've got everything you need.


We're offering these in mass market stores as a way to help out players who don't have access to one of our awesome hobby retailers. The decks are the same five monocolored decks available for free at Wizards Play Network locations around the world, and they offer a quick and easy way to learn and improve your *Magic* game—all while battling your friends.


They look like this:


![](https://media.wizards.com/2016/images/daily/zpC0aoHbbK.png)


Look for Booster Battle Packs in mass market stores starting May 13 with an MSRP of $9.99.


Before we go, some people [have *already* noticed](https://www.reddit.com/r/magicTCG/comments/4bt0o1/my_lgs_got_its_soi_release_kit_today_included/) that there are cards in these decks that have [a unique expansion symbol](http://imgur.com/b9dKJOO). Just so we're all aware, these cards are Standard-legal for as long as *Shadows over Innistrad* cards are Standard-legal.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Green-Blue](http://sales.starcitygames.com/deckdatabase/displaydeck.php?DeckID=100220) [Crush](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush)

Here's something truly new, out of a StarCityGames.com Invitational Qualifier in Hatboro, Pennsylvania (where all true innovation comes from). Cory Dissenger finished in 7th place with a new list that almost entirely survives rotation.


Eschewing white and [Collected Company](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Collected+Company), Dissenger took parts of the Bant Company Deck deck, included some blue instants, and tacked on a heaping helping of [Crush of Tentacles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Tentacles). That, by the way, is what the [Briber's Purse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Briber%27s+Purse) is for—a semi-useful zero-cost spell that can turn on surge for [Crush of Tentacles](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crush+of+Tentacles) when necessary.


Also note the liberal use of [Pulse of Murasa](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pulse+of+Murasa), which can form a pretty strong loop with [Den Protector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Den+Protector) as the game goes long.


The deck loses only a few cards with the rotation, and could potentially gain a few new cards from *Shadows over Innistrad*. Oh, have I not mentioned? The [full Card Image Gallery](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/card-image-gallery/shadowsoverinnistrad) is live. Maybe you can pick up some ideas over there.
