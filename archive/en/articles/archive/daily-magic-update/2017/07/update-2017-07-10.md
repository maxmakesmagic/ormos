1. [2017 Chesley Award Winners](http://file770.com/?p=36192)

In particular I want to draw your attention to Best Gaming Related Illustration, where [Ryan Yee](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Search/Default.aspx?output=spoiler&method=visual&action=advanced&artist=+%5b%22Ryan+Yee%22%5d) won for [Die Young](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Die+Young) in *Kaladesh*. The card [Yee has called his favorite *Magic* piece](https://www.instagram.com/p/BRvwGcuDUfA/) is beautiful, haunting, and now a Chesley Award winner. If you haven't heard me say it before, Chesley Awards are a massive deal in the science fiction/fantasy art world. Congratulations to Ryan Yee!

It's certainly nothing official, but it's fun fodder for discussion. Which, I suppose is the point.

Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Blue-Red Control](https://www.channelfireball.com/articles/hour-of-devastation-is-exactly-what-ur-control-needs/) | Channel Fireball | Joel Larsson

Larsson thinks *Hour of Devastation* is bringing Blue-Red Control to the next level—and he means that in two different ways. One, the new set brings some new toys, particularly [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade) and [Supreme Will](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Supreme+Will). Two, the actual card [Hour of Devastation](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Hour+of+Devastation) gives the deck a sweeper unlike any the deck has seen before. It might even be the best sweeper in Standard.


So expect this deck to not only continue kicking around in new Standard, but devastating opponents left and right.
