Deck of the Day
===============


1. [Uniq's Blue Oketra's Tokens](http://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/mtgo-standings/competitive-standard-constructed-league-2017-06-28)

It was only a matter of time before someone added a color to the otherwise mono-white [Oketra's Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oketra%27s+Monument) deck, and *Magic Online* player Uniq did just that all the way to 5-0.


The list had me at "[Cloudblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer)," but the real trick to this deck is that [Metallic Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallic+Rebuke) and [Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) turn the deck from a (somewhat) straightforward white swarm deck into a hybrid with former format monster White-Blue Flash, giving the deck a ton of interaction it didn't have previously.


[Spell Queller](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Queller) is obviously an all-star, and [Cloudblazer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cloudblazer) is obviously one of the best cards every printed in the history of ever. However, the real genius here is [Metallic Rebuke](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Metallic+Rebuke), which gets help from a surprising number of artifacts available in this deck, mostly in the form of Clues from [Thraben Inspector](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thraben+Inspector) and [Bygone Bishop](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bygone+Bishop), plus the eponymous [Oketra's Monument](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oketra%27s+Monument).
