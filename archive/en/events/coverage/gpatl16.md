TOP 8 COVERAGE



Atlanta makes it two trophies in six weeks for Brazilian Carlos Romão. And "2" isn't the only big number for the former World Champion. "5" is also important, as Romão now has *five* Grand Prix titles! Californian Cash Turner had an amazing undefeated run into the finals that was one for the books, but Romão slammed those tomes shut, and is pressing forward to Pro Tour *Kaladesh*next week.


Grand Prix Atlanta was part of the first weekend of *Kaladesh*and it's thoroughly proven to be full of fun, challenges, masterpieces, and excitement. Stories abound about multiple two-copy Panharmonican decks, Modules gone crazy, and Skyships turning changing the course of games.


The Top 8 was filled to the brim with strong players both old and new—Chris Fennell, Geddes Cooper, Noah Walker, Thien Nguyen, Ray Perez, Jr., and Matt Frank filled out the bunch with Turner and Romão. It was jam-packed from start to finish.


And *Kaladesh* is just getting started. With Pro Tour *Kaladesh*right around the corner in Hawaii, there's plenty more action on the horizon.


Congratulations again to Carlos Romão, and see you all in Honolulu!

Day 2 Coverage



Yesterday, players took six packs of the brand new *Kaladesh* set and built the best deck they could from the cards. Today, it’s time to do something a little different with those packs.


It’s drafting time. And I’m not talking getting-yourself-behind-a-vehicle-to-shade-from-the-wind–type drafting. The cards of *Kaladesh* are quite powerful, and we’ll certainly see some amazing draft decks today. Decks full of exciting build-arounds like Dynavolt Tower, Whirler Virtuoso, and maybe a Panharmonicon or two.


After the first day, Chris Fennell, Cash Turner, Mason King, Geddes Cooper, and Quinn Griffin are the ones with the picture-perfect 9-0. Below them are Evan Nelson and No. 16-ranked Sam Black at 8-0-1, and some stellar names at 8-1. Of particular note, Chris Pikula, and basically the entire Team East-West Bowl.


We’ll be streaming everything over at [twitch.tv/magic](http://www.twitch.tv/magic), including tons of pros’ drafts and all the action from the top of the tables.


It’s gonna be a great Sunday on *Kaladesh*. See you there!

Day 1 Coverage



*Kaladesh* has completed its first lap. Day 1 of Grand Prix Atlanta, on the inaugural *Kaladesh* GP weekend, is wrapped. Sitting right at the top of the standings are five players with the glistening 9-0. Lead by vet Chris Fennell, Cash Turner, Mason King, Geddes Cooper, and Quinn Griffin have everything to lose tomorrow, as they'll be the ones to beat.


Right below them are Evan Nelson and No. 16-ranked Sam Black at 8-0-1, and a slew of great names behind them at 8-1. Just to list a few: Chris Pikula, Matthias Hunt, Neal Oliver, Matt Severa, Luis Salvatto, Scott Lipp, Craig Wescoe, Josh Cho, Thien Nguyen, Joe Demestrio, and Frank Skarren.


It's going to be an exciting day tomorrow. Not just because of all these great names, but because, race fans, it's draft—and drafting can change everything.


“That's right. Sunday, Sunday, Sunday! Come see the best battle in out in a race for *Kaladesh* Limited supremacy! Come see Truckasaurus [Renegade Freighter], battle the Grraaaaaavedigger [Ballista Charger] at Atlanta Motor Speedway! That's Sunday, Sunday, Sunday!”



 Tune it to [twitch.tv/magic](http://twitch.tv/magic) to catch all these vehicular wrecks as they happen. *Kaladesh* is here, and it's mayhem at the raceway. See you all tomorrow!
