Round 8 Feature Match: Matt Sperling vs. Neal Oliver
====================================================



 Posted in [COVERAGE ARCHIVE](/en/events/coverage)
 on January 10, 2015 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_adamstyborski.jpg)
By Adam Styborski




Adam "Stybs" Styborski joined DailyMTG.com in 2009 to take over Serious Fun, before switching over to begin Command Tower in 2013. With his passion for Commander and community inclusion, you'll find plenty of opportunity each week to share your thoughts about everyone's favorite casual format. 





Matt Sperling was coming off a great weekend, ending an excellent run of finalist at Grand Prix Denver the weekend prior. Adding a Top 8 with Pro Tour *Magic 2015* as well as another at Grand Prix Vancouver, Sperling was again a rising player on the Premier play circuit. (And with friends like Pro Tour Hall of Fame member Paul Rietzl is no surprise at all.) Winning this match would ensure he’d have his chance to do it again on Day 2 here.


Neal Oliver, champion of the mammoth Grand Prix Law Vegas in 2013, had recently finished his world tour as one of the members of the US World Magic Cup team. Assisting in their rise to fourth, alongside captain and first-ranked Owen Turtenwald, Oliver was a formidable player in his own right: His only other Grand Prix Top 8s were *both* finalist finishes, at Oakland in 2013 and Ottowa in 2014.


In fact, Oliver was a player that commanded plenty of respect from Sperling.


“I spend every day telling everyone you’re the most under-rated player,” Sperling said, “and now I have to beat you.”


“Like at Sacramento where I went 6-0 then 0-3?” Oliver laughed. “Let’s just try and both make Day 2.”


Sperling wasn’t done however. “Excited to defend your title in Vegas?” he asked. Here, Oliver couldn’t help but grin ear to ear.


“I’m preregistered and everything.”


**The Decks**


Both players were using the powerful Blue-Red Delver deck. As one of the newer pillars of Modern, The Blue-Red Delver deck is built to handle games in multiple ways. On the surface, it’s a tempo deck that uses its namesake [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets), [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage), and [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) to make the most of a bevy of instants. While [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt), [Spell Snare](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Snare), and [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) are typical of how the deck stays ahead of opponents, [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe), [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions), and [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) support quickly building up to [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise)-as-[Ancestral Recall](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ancestral+Recall) to refill the options.


In longer games, the ability to stymie combos and fight back against control means it can bide its time and set up an unanswerable sequence of plays. With so many cheap spells and threats, it’s a deck with surprising resilience and power over time. Playing the mirror, as in this case, amplified the depth of what the deck could do.


**The Games**


Sperling was on the board before Oliver even took his first turn, thanks to Steam vents and [Monastery Swiftspear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery+Swiftspear). Though Oliver was quick to kill it with [Pillar of Flame](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pillar+of+Flame) on his turn, Sperling tred [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) soon thereafter. [Remand](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Remand) was waiting for Oliver, and Sperling’s [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) revealed even more removal and countermagic on the other side.


Oliver returned the [Monastery Swiftspear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery+Swiftspear) favor, and Sperling had [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) in kind. [Gitaxian Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gitaxian+Probe) from Oliver revealed a similar hand for Sperling, and the two went back to facing off in relative parity.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpoma15/20150110-gpomaha-sperling.jpg)  



Things finally broke when Sperling drew a [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) for his turn and it resolved, refilling his hand.


“That’s pretty good,” Oliver said.


[Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions) helped Oliver find his own copy of Cruise, but Sperling won the resulting counter war over that. Sperling killed Oliver’s lonely [Snapcaster Mage](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Snapcaster+Mage) before casting another [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise). With a full grip Sperling took control of the game.


[Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) was Sperling’s first move, and they traded instants over it. The net result was Sperling casting multiple [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s to put Oliver at 6 life with three 1/1 Elemental creature tokens in play to continue battling with.


Oliver’s [Vapor Snag](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vapor+Snag) answered one of the tokens, but that wasn’t enough. Two turns later and they moved to the second game.


“You got it.” Oliver said.


The second game initially looked to favor Oliver. While Sperling was the first to land [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) and a few Elementals, it was Oliver that flipped his [Delver of Secrets](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Delver+of+Secrets) into [Insectile Aberration](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Insectile+Aberration) and made even more tokens with his own Pyromancer.


But it wasn’t to be.


Then Sperling unleashed an entire hand of removal to wipe away Oliver’s side and leave five Elemental tokens behind. [Monastery Swiftspear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery+Swiftspear) and [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) joined the fray as Oliver defended his second [Young Pyromancer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Young+Pyromancer) of the match. The attack still dropped Oliver to just 6 life.


![](https://media.wizards.com/2015/events/gpoma15/20150110-gpomaha-oliver.jpg)  



There was a moment in between the exchanges, however, when Sperling knew Oliver had [Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) but still left [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise) on top of his library with [Serum Visions](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Serum+Visions). Oliver didn’t know what it was, but Sperling grimaced when the Scour milled it away.


[Forked Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Forked+Bolt) cleared away the remaining defenses Oliver had.


“It stings,” Oliver said as he fell to 1 life.


[Pyroclasm](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Pyroclasm) was Oliver’s last effort to stay alive but Sperling had the natural follow up: [Monastery Swiftspear](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Monastery+Swiftspear) for the last point needed. Oliver extended his hand.


“I really misplayed in this match,” Sperling said.


“Why do you think that?” Oliver asked.


Sperling related the [Treasure Cruise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Treasure+Cruise)-[Thought Scour](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thought+Scour) situation above. “I was warned about the Scour games specifically,” Sperling said. “I just forgot.”
