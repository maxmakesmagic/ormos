![Iona, Shield of Emeria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Iona%2C+Shield+of+Emeria)Did I ever tell you about the time I had turn-three [Iona, Shield of Emeria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Iona%2C+Shield+of+Emeria) against mono red? Good news: I won that game!

My deck from the Prerelease is trying to maximize the power of [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) and [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap). The deck is a deck of dreams, and the key to many of these dreams is a turn two [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra). Casting turn-three [Baneslayer Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Baneslayer+Angel) is a play that comes up relatively often. To do this you need a turn-two Cobra followed by a turn-three fetch land, giving you five mana for the Angel on turn three. The much bigger dream is the turn-three Iona. This one involves turn-one [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise), turn-two [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), and then a turn 3 fetch land. This gives you six mana on turn three to cast [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap) and hopefully find one of your three copies of Iona in your top seven cards.

This deck is heavy green-white but includes black for a few reasons, the main being that you want fetch lands to combo with the [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra). Once you decide to put in the black fetch lands, you might as well add some strong black cards, and [Ob Nixilis, the Fallen](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ob+Nixilis%2C+the+Fallen) is perfect for this deck. With fetch lands and [Path to Exile](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Path+to+Exile) (cast on your own little creatures) you can quickly drain your opponent for 6 and create a 9/9 Ob Nixilis (often much larger). 

A couple cards to think about to improve the deck: [Sphinx of the Steel Wind](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sphinx+of+the+Steel+Wind) is *very* strong against Jund decks. They have few ways to win the game once it hits the table due to its lifelink and its protection from red and from green. You would usually pull her with a [Summoning Trap](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Summoning+Trap), but in some games [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) or [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) will give you the ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161115194122im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/blue.gif) you need to actually hardcast her. Another card to think about is [Martial Coup](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Martial+Coup). This deck generates a lot of mana in the late game, so big ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20160829100442im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/x.gif) spells can be game-changers.
