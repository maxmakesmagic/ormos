Anybody Who's Anybody
=====================



 Posted in **Top Decks**
 on January 6, 2011 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_mikeflores.jpg)
By Mike Flores




Michael Flores is the author of Deckade and The Official Miser's Guide; the designer of numerous State, Regional, Grand Prix, National, and Pro Tour–winning decks; and the onetime editor-in-chief of The Magic Dojo. He'd claim allegiance to Dimir (if such a Guild existed)… but instead will just shrug "Simic." 






"Anybody who's *anybody* plays [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) in Faeries," Josh says. "Some play it main deck."

"Yeah," responds Pat. "It's way better than it is in Standard. Funny ... [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine) might be the best Titan in Extended."

![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/images/magic/daily/td/td124_wurmcoil.jpg)  
I am two-thirds of the way through an 0-3 draft performance at Jonny Magic's. Patrick Chapin is in town (New York, that is, "the City") for New Year's celebrations ... and where else were we going to go? The pair of us were bumming around the corner of Finkel's apartment when the Man ("the Machine") himself, and his mom, happened by.

"Are you guys going to head on up, or what?"

Typical Sunday afternoon.

The draft prior to ours features two U.S. Nationals Top 8 competitors ... *from the same year* (Josh Ravitz is one of them, Chris Manning the other). I am gearing up to play Jamie Parke. Meaning—counting Chapin—that the draft we are *in* features two American World Championships finalists.

*Typical.*

Back to the action: 

"The only question to ask," says Josh, "is whether your deck can beat Faeries. It's really Faeries or other."

"I like Four-Color Control!" 

News flash: *Patrick Chapin likes Four-Color Control.*

"Four-Color Control loses to Faeries," retorts Ravitz.

"Does it?" The Innovator is quizzical. "I mean ... Five weeks from now, eight weeks from now ... who can even say what Four-Color Control looks like?"

That was twenty-four hours ago; *and that is how this article started.*

At the time, the first **Magic Online** Pro Tour Qualifier for this winter's Extended PTQ season was just revving up ... and surprise, surprise: Faeries was top dawg!

In this article we will go over not only the Top 8 deck looks at that top dawg, but a brief analysis of Pat's comment. What are some different looks at Four- (and Five-) Color Control? By its very nature, polychromatic control decks are about the most customizable decks in the format. They are—by their many, many colors—able to play essentially any cards, allowing them to obtain "good match-ups" in essentially any match-up ... but always at a cost. How have different players approached this puzzle so far?

Aside: Just minutes before working on the first draft of this article, I lost to TeamCKgetthere's Faerie deck.

Due to the pedigree of picking up the first PTQ win, we can probably assume that TeamCKgetthere's list will be the default PTQ Faeries deck ... or thereabouts. In any case, it should make for a fine gauntlet deck, being, as it is, The Deck to Beat.

Key Qualities:


> 
> * 25 lands, 7 creature-lands: I can see a full eight creature-lands in this archetype, just because [Creeping Tar Pit](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Creeping+Tar+Pit) is so good at killing planeswalkers. to death. [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) has long been a three-of (at least) in Faeries; full four-of in most cases due to the synergies with [Scion of Oona](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Scion+of+Oona) and potential with [Mistbind Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mistbind+Clique). Don't be surprised if [Mutavault](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mutavault) ups the Faeries count to set up an embarassing [Spellstutter Sprite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellstutter+Sprite) two-for-one.
> * Heavy disruption: [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) has floated in and out of main-deck staple status over the years; this version plays not only a full four copies of [Thoughtseize](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Thoughtseize) (expect all four in essentially all Faeries opponents), but one [Inquisition of Kozilek](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) and two [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique)s ... with more of each coming out of the side.  
>  A mix of removal: TeamCKgetthere played [Disfigure](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure), [Smother](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Smother), [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb), and [Infest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest); [Doom Blade](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade) will also be a common include, and you should anticipate (or play!) more copies of [Infest](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Infest) should tokens or other small creature strategies grow common.
> * [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine): Because anybody who's anybody plays it, sideboard or otherwise.

How does a "modern" Extended Jund differ from the Standard deck that so plagued the world for months and months last year?

The defining feature of this style is the inclusion of [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman); [Fauna Shaman](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) can find singleton bullets like [Shriekmaw](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shriekmaw), or line up multiple copies of [Demigod of Revenge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge). [Demigod of Revenge](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Demigod+of+Revenge) is especially special in a format where countermagic-based defenses such as those played by Faeries, Four-Color Control, and [Wargate](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wargate) are so popular.

Mythic Conscription in Extended works much the same way that it did in Standard. Drawing on Zvi Mowshowitz's Mythic mana base—[Birds of Paradise](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) and [Noble Hierarch](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Noble+Hierarch) with [Lotus Cobra](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra) + [Knight of the Reliquary](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Knight+of+the+Reliquary)—the deck forces down powerful threats ranging from [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) to [Sovereigns of Lost Alara](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sovereigns+of+Lost+Alara). When Sovereigns hits, you can attack with whatever creature is still untapped, search up [Eldrazi Conscription](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Eldrazi+Conscription), and smack for 11 or more.

With [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and six planeswalkers, Mythic Conscription can operate as an accelerated White-Blue deck, or fall back on creature-lands. The card quality, overall, is among the highest card-for-card in the business.

Celldweller's take on last summer's (arguably) most successful archetype features a fair number of improvements (or at least alterations) with the move to Extended's wider card pool:


> 
> * Mana base: [Razorverge Thicket](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Razorverge+Thicket) and [Seachrome Coast](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Seachrome+Coast) represent expansions towards the future, but [Murmuring Bosk](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Murmuring+Bosk)—drafting on the availability of [Verdant Catacombs](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Verdant+Catacombs)—gives the deck an old school Extended edge.
> * Card choices: [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) broadens the available tools; [Dauntless Escort](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dauntless+Escort) main deck is more of a metagame call.
> 

My early game prediction? Mythic Conscription may prove the most successful attack deck of the season.

I got turned onto this deck by Joshua Ravitz (apparently before his—perhaps correct— tunnel vision in the Faeries direction), and it seems like a fine performer. The [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)s in particular are savage threats in the format. [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan) + [Kitchen Finks](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kitchen+Finks) can laugh off a [Cruel Ultimatum](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cruel+Ultimatum). In this deck, again thanks to that sunniest of Titans, [Jace Beleren](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) is actually better than [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor)! But everything—from the [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) to the [Oblivion Ring](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Oblivion+Ring)s to the singleton [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) and [Ratchet Bomb](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ratchet+Bomb)—have fine synergy with the [Sun Titan](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sun+Titan)s, and as parts of the whole.

### Part 2: Four-Color Anybody Who's Anybody

Now is the part of the article we talk about trending and possibilities in Four- and Five-Color Control decks. 

It is hard to say what is "typical" in the archetype, but there are certain things that we can see across many different deck lists. For example:


> 
> * About 26 lands
> * Generally two copies of [Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) main deck; another one or two copies in the sideboard
> * Most common creature package being some combination of [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens) and [Wurmcoil Engine](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wurmcoil+Engine)
> 



|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| Wurmcoil Engine | Wall of Omens |

The finisher featured here is [Bogardan Hellkite](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bogardan+Hellkite). While it looks like a creature-sweeper, I choose to see this as a cool tool against other control decks, as it allows a blue deck to pick a fight with another Blue deck at the end of turn, rather than waiting for a mana disadvantage on one's own turn. And while a good man is not too good to trade a [Vendilion Clique](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Vendilion+Clique) for a [Boggart Ram-Gang](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Boggart+Ram-Gang), the same can't be said for the proactive 3/1 Faeries over typically defensive [Wall of Omens](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wall+of+Omens).

![Bogardan Hellkite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Bogardan+Hellkite)  
But the structure of the deck itself is actually much more interesting than the creature details.

We see eleven Vivid lands, thirteen tapped lands overall... Up from the previous model.

Why play a slower sequence of lands?

How else are you going to make sure you can cast [Great Sable Stag](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Great+Sable+Stag)?

Along with a 3 + 1 split on [Volcanic Fallout](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Volcanic+Fallout) (over 2 + 1), we see a Five-Color Control deck that is much more aware of other decks with [Mana Leak](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and [Cryptic Command](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Cryptic+Command), among those Faeries.

I mean [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)?

[Sunblast Angel](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sunblast+Angel) might get you oohs and ahs (and angry looks from beatdown victims), but [Mulldrifter](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mulldrifter)—once a staple—is just cool.
