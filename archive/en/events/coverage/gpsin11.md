da Rosa Reigns Supreme in Singapore
===================================




June 4-5, 2011












Grand Prix Singapore 2011
-------------------------


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsin11/champion620.jpg)


Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa certainly earned his victory at Grand Prix: Singapore. He had to master the notoriously complex and difficult CawBlade mirror match, slaying opposing [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)s and [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s repeatedly over the weekend. 623 players turned up hopeful, and over two days of slinging spells the field was reduced to a Top 8.


We entered the single elimination rounds with a field stuffed with CawBlade. Owen Turtenwald, Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa, Chatchai Saethang and Soh Weng Heng all chose to play the powerful deck, and Marios Angeloupolos and Hao-Shan Hang piloted Darkblade in the Top 8. Standing out from the crowd were Chakira Nakajima piloting Boros and Shouta Yasooka with an innovative Blue/Black Control list.


Paulo proved too powerful for the other CawBlade players to slow down, as he won the quarters and semis in straight games. Tearing up the opposing bracket was Japan's Chakira Nakkajima, who entered the finals boasting an 7-0 record against CawBlade over the weekend. The finals themselves showed two players at their finest, the decks throwing everything they had at each other. In the end, though, there could only be one winner, and Paulo Vitor Damo da Rosa is your Grand Prix: Singapore champion!

Quarterfinals – Chikara Nakajima vs. Marios Angelopoulos
--------------------------------------------------------


*by Ray "blisterguy" Walkinsha* 

### Game 1


Nakajima won the die roll, and immediately summoned and attacked with a [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide). He [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember)ed Angelopoulos' [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), summoned a second Guide and attacked Angelopoulos down to 12. An [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) from Angelopoulos took a [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader) from Nakajima's hand, but could do nothing about the [Koth of the Hammer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Koth+of+the+Hammer) beside it. "I think I'm dead if you get a land," Angelopoulos stated with a shrug, summoning a [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk). Nakajima drew the land, and sent his Goblins and a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) in eat the Hawk and drop Angelopoulos to 8.


Angelopoulos summoned two more Hawks, throwing one of them in front of the [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and falling to 4, as Nakajima summoned a [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull). Angelopoulos lapsed into thought, before attacking with his remaining Hawk, summoning [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) and +2'ing him. Nakajima's army quickly crushed the Planeswalker, a Guide revealing an [Emeria Angel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Emeria+Angel) on top of Angelopoulos' library. The Angel couldn't stave off the attacks for long, and they were quickly off to game two.


Nakajima 1 – Angelopoulos 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsin11/qfNak.jpg)*Chikara Nakajima has a response.*
### Game 2


Nakajima again had a pair of [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)s, but this time Angelopoulos had a [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) soaking up some of the damage. Angelopoulos followed the [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) with a [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), fetching a [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull). The Guides dropped Angelopoulos to 14, but Nakajima was lacking a third land. Angelopoulos wasted no time flashing the [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) into play, and set to repairing the early damage inflicted by the [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide)s. Nakajima could only summon a lowly [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx) on his turn. Angelopoulos' board was filling with [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s, and his [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) was making short work of the Japanese player. Angelopoulos then summoned Elspeth Tirel and gained life, putting himself on 23. "Do you like my sideboard?" Angelopoulos joked, pointing to the Planeswalker.


Nakajima could only smile meekly as Angelopoulos attacked with 3 Hawks and 2 Creeping Tar-Pits to take the match to the decider.


Nakajima 1 – Angelopoulos 1


### Game 3


Both players kept, but Nakajima didn't look thrilled with his hand, summoning a [Steppe Lynx](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Steppe+Lynx). Angelopoulos cracked a [Marsh Flats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats) to cast a Kozilek's Inquisition. Nakajima revealed [Marsh Flats](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Marsh+Flats), [Goblin Guide](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Guide), [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering), [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) and [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk). "It's not so bad, but I don't see any red mana," Angelopolous mused as he took the Hawk.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsin11/qfAng.jpg)*Marios Angelopoulos is calmly in control of the game.*
Nakajima again missed his next land drop, while Angelopoulos played a Creeping Tar-Pit, killing the Lynx with a [Doom Blade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Doom+Blade). Nakajima drew and summoned a [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), fetching a [Sword of War and Peace](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+War+and+Peace), "that was a nice draw!" Angelopoulos admitted.


Angelopoulos cast [Preordain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Preordain), then a Mystic of his own, fetching a [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull). Nakajima summoned [Mirran Crusader](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mirran+Crusader), and on the following turn, equipped it with the Sword. With no answer for the Crusader, Angelopoulos was forced to throw an [Inkmoth Nexus](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inkmoth+Nexus) in front of it for the turn.


"Now, I need a miracle," Angelopoulos announced, flashing me his cards. He had one white source in play, and a [Gideon Jura](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Gideon+Jura) in hand. "Not today," he bemoaned as he drew for the turn. He shuffled through his hand, gesticulating silently at various permanents, counting, and going over the possible plays in his head, before finally passing the turn.


Nakajima attacked again with his Crusader, Angelopolous flashing a second [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) into play so that only took 16 from the Sword'd Crusader, dropping the Greek player to 3. Nakajima tapped a [Mountain](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mountain) and placed a [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt) on the table.


"I'm dead," Angelopoulos replied, revealing the Gideon and a [Day of Judgment](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Day+of+Judgment) in his hand, and pointing to his lone white source of mana.


**Chikara Nakajima defeats Marios Angelopoulos 2 – 1** 




Quarterfinals – Owen Turtenwald vs. Shouta Yasooka
--------------------------------------------------


*by Pip Hunn* 

Both players are used to the spotlight, having had a bevy Grand Prix and Pro Tour wins and finishes between them. Yasooka is piloting a home brewed U/B control deck featuring a playset of metagame-targeting [Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb)s. Turtenwald is piloting CawBlade, a deck that has yet again proven its dominance over Standard this weekend.


### Game 1


Both players opened up with lands for the first few turns. Yasooka cast a [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) which gained him a few cards. He followed up with a [Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) and passed, but a [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering) from Turtenwald removed the offending artifact. Turtenwald began the offensive with a [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), which resolved.


Yasooka developed his board with another [Torpor Orb](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torpor+Orb) and an [Everflowing Chalice](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Everflowing+Chalice), but Turtenwald suited up his Mystic with a [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) and began attacking. Yasooka stemmed the bleeding with a [Tumble Magnet](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tumble+Magnet).


Turtenwald activated a [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade) and began attacking. Yasooka reversed the game's fortunes by demolishing the board with his singleton [Consume the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+the+Meek), which killed Turtenwald's animated Colonnade and [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic)s.


Yasooka followed up with a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor), to which Turtenwald could only play another relatively innocuous [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic) and equip it. Yasooka followed up with a [Tezzeret, Agent of Bolas](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Tezzeret%2C+Agent+of+Bolas). Turtenwald looked at his empty board. Against the combined might of two Planeswalkers, it didn't look like he was going to last long.


He didn't.


Shouta Yasooka 1 – Owen Turtenwald 0


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsin11/qfYa.jpg)*Shouta Yasooka*
### Game 2


Game two began with an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek) from Yasooka, which netted him a [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce). Turtenwald played a pair of [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s, and Yasooka [Disfigure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure)d one before casting a [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren).


Turtenwald played the rest of his fliers and attacked Jace repeatedly, but Yasooka had more [Disfigure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure)s to keep the Planeswalker alive. Turtenwald summoned a [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), which fetched out [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull). A third [Disfigure](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Disfigure) from Yasooka killed the Mystic, but Turtenwald just hard cast the equipment. Yasooka took advantage of the opening to summon a [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan). The ground clogged up, Turtenwald equipped the [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) to a Hawk and swung through the air.


The [Grave Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Grave+Titan) tried to race Turtenwald's pair of fliers, but barely kept pace with the life gain from the Hawk. At a precarious 1 life, Yasooka attacked with his Titan and an army of Zombie tokens before trying a [Consume the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+the+Meek). Turtenwald had a [Spell Pierce](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spell+Pierce) to stop it, and the players moved on to the deciding game.


Owen Turtenwald 1 – Shouta Yasooka 1


### Game 3


Turtenwald mulliganed to 6 for the deciding game. Yasooka cast an [Inquisition of Kozilek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inquisition+of+Kozilek), revealed a hand of lands, [Mana Leak](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Mana+Leak) and a [Divine Offering](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Divine+Offering). Another Inquisition left Turtenwald with only lands in his hand. Luckily, he topdecked a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor) at the perfect moment and summoned it unopposed.


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/mtg/images/daily/events/gpsin11/qfTu.jpg)*Owen Turtenwald*
Yasooka summoned a [Jace Beleren](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace+Beleren) to kill off the Planeswalker's bigger brother. Turtenwald hardcast a [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull), leaving him with a decent board position but an empty hand. Turtenwald climbed to 30 life and knocked Yasooka down to 2 life over several swings, the [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull) accompanied by a [Celestial Colonnade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Celestial+Colonnade).


Yasooka had held onto a [Consume the Meek](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Consume+the+Meek) for several turns and, at the last possible moment, drew the land to play it. Both players looked to the top of their libraries for a threat. Turtenwald got there first with a [Jace, the Mind Sculptor](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Jace%2C+the+Mind+Sculptor). He fatesealed Yasooka into oblivion while a singleton [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk) flew Turtenwald to victory.

This little guy looks rather innocuous on the surface, but when suited up with a [Splinter Twin](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Splinter+Twin), can make about as many copies of itself as necessary (and there's not usually any incentive to stop at "just what's necessary"). There have been many ways to build around this combo sighted here this weekend, ranging from straight Blue/Red, to Blue/Red and any one of the remaining three colors. The rise of [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch) has caused an increase in sideboarded [Combust](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Combust)s.



![Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Phyrexian+Metamorph)

### Phyrexian Metamorph


One of the most versatile [Clone](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Clone)-style creatures we've seen in a long time, and at a very competitive cost, to boot. Copying anything from [Inferno Titan](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Inferno+Titan) to [Sword of Feast and Famine](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Sword+of+Feast+and+Famine) to [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) (things weren't going well for Matt Marr that game). Rumors have been circling that [Phyrexian Metamorph](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Phyrexian+Metamorph) might also make an appearance or two at Pro Tour Nagoya next weekend.



![Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Spellskite)

### Spellskite


This harmless looking 0/4 flew under the radar for about as long as anything can be expected to do so these days (about 2 minutes and 45 seconds.) [Spellskite](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Spellskite) has been summoned to protect a wide range of Very Important Persons, allowing [Deceiver Exarch](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Deceiver+Exarch)s to suit up uninterrupted (and incidentally, also interrupting opposing Exarchs), letting equipped [Squadron Hawk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squadron+Hawk)s to attack unimpeded, and even leaping in front of stray [Lightning Bolt](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lightning+Bolt)s that may have been intended for Planeswalkers.



![Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Dismember)

### Dismember


As far as removal spells go, [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) has definitely set the bar high. I imagine that it has occasionally been cast for its normal mana cost of ![](https://web.archive.org/web/20211201201921im_/https://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/colorless1.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif)![](https://web.archive.org/web/20161227195556im_/http://magic.wizards.com/sites/all/modules/custom/wiz_autocard/wiz_manacost/manasymbols/black.gif), but the majority of the time, people are paying one mana of any color, and marking their life total 4 points lower. [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) has closed the loop on several vulnerabilities decks once had (CawBlade vs [Fauna Shaman](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Fauna+Shaman) or [Lotus Cobra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Lotus+Cobra), Mono Red and Boros vs [Kor Firewalker](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Kor+Firewalker)) while being a very cheap and effective way to disrupt anyone looking to equip or enchant a creature.



![Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?type=card&name=Batterskull)

### Batterskull


Allow me to wax alliteratively for a moment, but [Batterskull](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Batterskull)'s a beating. Combined with [Stoneforge Mystic](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Stoneforge+Mystic), people can expect to see it entering the battlefield as early as turn three. At any point in the game, a 4/4 with lifelink and vigilance is an imposing line of defense, and being able to attack early and often will make (for instance) the life cost of [Dismember](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Dismember) negligible, and ensure an aggressive deck will be facing a very steep, uphill battle.


So it's safe to say the New Phyrexia definitely turned the power in Standard up a notch! No wonder the Mirran's didn't stand a chance.
