Round 10 Feature Match - Mike Turian vs. Neil Reeves
====================================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on May 12, 2002 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/authorpic_benbleiweiss.jpg)
By Ben Bleiweiss












![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/919.jpg)The centerpieces of two respective **Magic** Online teams squared off this round (Mike from Team CMU and Neil from the board-leading Southeast Locals), each having fought to a 7-2-1 record. Both players were in joking moods, as evidenced by the first exchange after they sat down:


Neil: You hate me.  

Mike: I don't hate you.  

Neil: But you've said it four times!  

Mike: But I didn't really mean it.


At the next table, the other feature match had gathered quite a crowd. Our table didn't seem to have as many spectators initially. Pro Tour player and spades afficianado Matt Linde wandered over just as the game began.


Matt: Your crowd is just me and Bleiweiss.  

Neil: That's because we're a has-been and a never-will-be.  

Mike: Last time someone called me a has-been, I won the Pro Tour.


### Game One


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/920.jpg)Mike won the die roll, and chose to play first. Mike also chose to mulligan first, keeping a slow hand with lands and high casting cost spells. Neil stayed with a risky hand, playing two pain lands and a [Crosis's Catacombs](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Crosis%27s+Catacombs) by turn three. "This is going to be really embarrassing", Neil proclaimed, returning a land to his hand to play the dragon's lair. The Potato threw some steam in the pressure cooker with a [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) and [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest).


Finally, NeilR began drawing more consistent lands, sowing an Island, [Underground River](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Underground+River), [Shivan Reef](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shivan+Reef) and [Shadowblood Ridge](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Shadowblood+Ridge) in successive turns. These were used to bring out a [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar), a [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog) and to Repulse the token elephant out of the game. Spurred on by the toothsome visage of everyone's favorite 1/2 creature, Neil sang this song:


"I love cookie.  

Cookie is for me."  

-Neil Reeves, GP Milwaukee 2002. Turn Five, Round Ten. All rights reserved. TM.


Although he tapped down to a single open mana in order to drop these spells, Mike failed to capitalize. His fifth turn [Opposition](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Opposition) met with a [Force Spike](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Force+Spike) (Mike stalled on four lands), and the game went downhill very quickly from there. Neil kept chanting his battle cry.


"I love cookie.  

Cookie is for me!"  

-Neil Reeves, GP Milwaukee 2002. Turns six, seven, eight, nine, and ten.


"I like cookies too! I'm all about the cookies!"  

-Mike Turian, somewhere between the cookie chorus.


The CMU player kept trying to stay in the game, but each of his spells was dealt with, starting with a [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) (counterspelled), then his hand ([Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe)), and lastly a second [Call of the Herd](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Call+of+the+Herd) (countered). Eventually Neil dropped a second [Psychatog](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Psychatog), and cleared away all the squirrels over the course of a few turns for the win.


Neil Reeves 1-Mike Turian 0.


### Game Two


Mike Turian again took a mulligan. Neil pumped his fist, and exclaimed:


![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/image_legacy_migration/sideboard/images/gpmil02/921.jpg)Neil: You're lucky I'm not a judge, you'd get a warning for being so ugly.  

Mike: You mean sexy.  

Neil: That was the next thing I was going to say.


The game was even less close this time. No matter what Mike would play, Neil had an answer. An early [Wild Mongrel](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Wild+Mongrel) was [Repulse](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Repulse)d and then FTKed. A [Merfolk Looter](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Merfolk+Looter) was FTKed. [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) were FTKed. The only non-land permanent Mike could keep in play was a [Squirrel Nest](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Squirrel+Nest), and Neil simply kept swinging with his army of 4/2 creatures backed with a [Nightscape Familiar](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Nightscape+Familiar). A [Probe](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Probe) stripped Mike's hand of all spells, and the game ended quickly and brutally on turn nine, after Turian drew consecutive [Birds of Paradise](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Birds+of+Paradise) to lose the game.


Neil Reeves 2-Mike Turian 0.
