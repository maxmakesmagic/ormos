Top 8 Profiles of Grand Prix Copenhagen 2018
============================================



 Posted in **Event Coverage**
 on June 10, 2018 






![](https://media.magic.wizards.com/styles/auth_small/public/images/person/henke_author.jpg)
By Tobi Henke











After fifteen rounds of Standard, with a total of 745 others eliminated, only eight players remained in the running for the fame and the fortune, the title and the trophy. Find out more about these people here!




---

![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpcop18/t8_dominguez.jpg)


**Name:** Javier Dominguez


**Age:** 31


**Occupation:** *Magic*


**Hometown:** Palau de Plemagans, Spain


**Previous *Magic* accomplishments:**


One Pro Tour Top 8, 2nd at Worlds 2017, two Grand Prix wins.


**What deck did you play this weekend, and why did you choose it?**


White-Blue Control. I was trying to beat [Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler).


**What was the best card in your main deck, and why?**


[Approach of the Second Sun](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Approach+of+the+Second+Sun). It's an easy way to win games.


**What was the best card out of your sideboard, and why?**


[Torrential Gearhulk](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Torrential+Gearhulk). [Duress](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Duress)-proof!




---

![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpcop18/t8_dang.jpg)


**Name:** Martin Dang


**Age:** 36


**Occupation:** Pro dad for Thea


**Hometown:** Aarhus, Denmark


**Previous *Magic* accomplishments:**


Sponsored by MTGMintCard and Dragon Shields.


**What deck did you play this weekend, and why did you choose it?**


White-Blue. I tuned it for the Red-Black matchup. It's also what I played at the Pro Tour.


**What was the best card in your main deck, and why?**


[Teferi, Hero of Dominaria](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Teferi%2C+Hero+of+Dominaria). Because Teferi.


**What was the best card out of your sideboard, and why?**


[Ixalan's Binding](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Ixalan%27s+Binding) was very good versus Red-Black.




---

![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpcop18/t8_blohon.jpg)


**Name:** Lukas Blohon


**Age:** 28


**Occupation:** Tourist and content producer for tcgplayer.com


**Hometown:** Prague, Czech Republic


**Previous *Magic* accomplishments:**


PT *Eldritch Moon* champion.


**What deck did you play this weekend, and why did you choose it?**


Red-Black Aggro. It is too good not to play it and Thomas Hendriks gave me a great list and sideboard plans for every matchup.


**What was the best card in your main deck, and why?**


[Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler). It's just too powerful.


**What was the best card out of your sideboard, and why?**


[Chandra's Defeat](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Chandra%27s+Defeat). It is one of the ways to get back into the game when you are on the draw in the red mirror.

**Name:** Tobias Maurer


**Age:** 31


**Occupation:** Project coordinator


**Hometown:** Mannheim, Germany


**Previous *Magic* accomplishments:**


Played Pro Tour *Hour of Devastation*.


**What deck did you play this weekend, and why did you choose it?**


Mono-Red. I didn't have time to test, and I played the deck before. It's the best!


**What was the best card in your main deck, and why?**


[Goblin Chainwhirler](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Goblin+Chainwhirler) is insane.


**What was the best card out of your sideboard, and why?**


[Glorybringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer) won me a lot of games.

![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpcop18/t8_kernke.jpg)


**Name:** Tim Kernke


**Age:** 25


**Occupation:** Retail


**Hometown:** Magdeburg, Germany


**Previous *Magic* accomplishments:**


Nothing. This is my first GP. Started 3-2, then won the rest of the matches.


**What deck did you play this weekend, and why did you choose it?**


Mono-Red Aggro. I didn't expect much from the event and wanted to play a deck that follows a straight line.


**What was the best card in your main deck, and why?**


[Earthshaker Khenra](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Earthshaker+Khenra), early pressure with late-game potential!


**What was the best card out of your sideboard, and why?**


[Glorybringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer), awesome value machine!

![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpcop18/t8_sanchez.jpg)


**Name:** Francisco Sanchez


**Age:** 25


**Occupation:** Chef


**Hometown:** Malaga, Spain, currently living in London, UK


**Previous *Magic* accomplishments:**


A few 12-3 finishes at GPs and qualified for a couple of PTs.


**What deck did you play this weekend, and why did you choose it?**


Red-Black Aggro. It had the best results at the Pro Tour.


**What was the best card in your main deck, and why?**


[Glorybringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer). Despite only playing one, I was lucky enough to draw it many times.


**What was the best card out of your sideboard, and why?**


More [Glorybringer](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Glorybringer)s. Card is just so good.

![](https://media.wizards.com/2018/events/gpcop18/t8_stahle-nilsson.jpg)


**Name:** Joakim Stahle-Nilsson


**Age:** 23


**Occupation:** Lecturer


**Hometown:** Karlshamn, Sweden


**Previous *Magic* accomplishments:**


None.


**What deck did you play this weekend, and why did you choose it?**


A very low-end mono-red deck. Because most removal in the current metagame costs 2+.


**What was the best card in your main deck, and why?**


[Bomat Courier](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Bomat+Courier), because it hits turn one and can replenish my hand later.


**What was the best card out of your sideboard, and why?**


Probably [Abrade](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Abrade). It works well against so many decks in the current metagame.
