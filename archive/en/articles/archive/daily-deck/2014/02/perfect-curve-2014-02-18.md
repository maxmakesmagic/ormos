The Perfect Curve
=================



 Posted in **Daily Deck**
 on February 18, 2014 










The [Super Sunday Series Finals](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/sss14/welcome) showcased *Born of the Gods*, and once the Standard rounds started, all eyes were on two people—Makihito Mihara and Aaron Lewis. I'm looking at Mihara's deck today, but Aaron's will show up later this week.



![Ephara, God of the Polis](http://gatherer.wizards.com/Handlers/Image.ashx?size=small&type=card&name=Ephara,%20God%20of%20the%20Polis&options=)Mihara, Hall of Fame hopeful and former world champion, has been doing incredible work as of late. He made Top 4 of  [Pro Tour *Dragon's Maze*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptdgm13/welcome) with Esper Control then followed it up by making Top 8 at  [Pro Tour *Theros*](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/ptths13/welcome) with RG Devotion. He demonstrated his range even further at the Super Sunday Series by playing a difficult-to-categorize Esper deck.

His deck, similar to the deck that won [Grand Prix Shizuoka](http://archive.wizards.com/magic/magazine/article.aspx?x=mtg/daily/eventcoverage/gpshi13/welcome), is a white-based deck with some aggressive elements like [Soldier of the Pantheon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soldier+of+the+Pantheon), [Precinct Captain](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Precinct+Captain), and [Imposing Sovereign](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Imposing+Sovereign). However, Mihara's deck also features the [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council)/[Whip of Erebos](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Whip+of+Erebos) combo, plus Ephara and Heliod for even more late-game power.


Is it an aggro Human deck? Is it an aggro-control deck? I'm guessing neither or both, depending on your draw. When you curve out from [Soldier of the Pantheon](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Soldier+of+the+Pantheon) into [Obzedat, Ghost Council](https://gatherer.wizards.com/Pages/Card/Details.aspx?name=Obzedat%2C+Ghost+Council), it won't matter what you classify your deck as—you're going to win.
